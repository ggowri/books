Osborne's mind buzzed and his heart thundered.

Him, the Godfather's heir?

There had to be a catch.

The Godfather's empire was vast and colossal, spanning and dominating every major industry in Rosmandi. He commanded thousands. His connections were intricate.

Osborne doubted he could handle it.

If he took over now, it might all fall apart. Surely the Godfather knew that.

"You think I can do it?" he asked, frowning.

The Godfather smiled. "Kid, I admire your composure. Yes, I think you can do it. But you're not ready. To be my heir, you'll need to pass a series of tests. You'll need to show me you possess the necessary courage, wisdom, cunning, and charm."

Osborne sighed.

"How many tests will I have to pass?"

"Ten in total," the Godfather continued. "For each test you pass, you'll gain a portion of my wealth and power. Pass them all, and you'll be the new Godfather—only better. You'll become the Super Godfather."

Super Godfather?

"But, a word of caution," the Godfather sternly warned. "The tests are dangerous. Once you start, there's no going back. Fail, and you won't just lose the opportunity—you may perish."

Osborne swallowed hard.

"Of course, if you don't want to risk it, I can give you one billion dollars in cash and send you somewhere safe. You could live rich, kid. It's the least I could do for my only grandson."

"One billion in cash?"

Osborne was shocked. The Godfather offered the money so casually, as if the sum was pocket change. Even the Caldwells could only dream of such immense wealth.

Osborne considered his options. On one hand, a billion dollars in cash and a safe place to live was a tempting offer.

On the other, a billion was a mere fraction of the Godfather's fortune.

Why settle when he could inherit it all? Not just the money, but the power and respect that came with such a formidable title.

It was risky, but it was also the best way to make his now-ex-wife Ophelia regret how she treated him.

It was also the only way he could confront the Godfather and ask why he was abandoned. He didn't think he could go on not knowing the answer to a question that had haunted him his entire life.

"I want to be your heir!" Osborne declared, his eyes burning with determination. "No matter the danger, no matter the cost, I will pass your tests."

The Godfather gently smiled, as if relieved.

"You're greedier than I thought," he said, chuckling. "So be it. No special favors. You will earn what you seek—the power, the money, the influence, the women. They're all part of the trials that await you."

Osborne took a deep breath, trying to steady his pulse.

"I will say this one last time," the Godfather said. "To be clear, you understand that should anything go wrong during the course of these trials, I won't intervene. This is your last chance to rethink your decision. As the saying goes, greed leads to the abyss."

Osborne shook his head.

"No need," he replied. "I've decided. This is about more than greed. It's about a new world filled with opportunity and prosperity. Let's get to it. What's the first trial?"

The Godfather's eyes lit up. "Good! Then it's settled. Your first trial is simple: go to the Caldwell family and demand a million dollars in cash for your divorce."

"A million-dollar divorce?" Osborne asked. "That's simple?"

The Godfather grew stern. "I watched you become nothing more than the Caldwells' lowly son-in-law. As an Augustine, you are so much more than that. No one should leave you with nothing. It's an insult for which no Augustine should settle. So go on. Claim your settlement from the Caldwells."

Osborne felt his pulse quicken.

"And remember. If you fail, I won't recognize you as my grandson. You won't inherit a thing from me."

The Godfather's harsh reminder made Osborne's blood run cold. Demanding a million dollars from the Caldwells could easily end in disaster.

Madness flickered in Osborne's eyes. He had countless questions, but there was no point in delaying his reply any longer. With a sigh, he nodded.

"I'll do it."

The Godfather smiled. "Go, then. Show your courage and cunning. Prove you're worthy to be my heir. Remember, an Augustine can be killed—but never humiliated. You have one day to secure your settlement."

Osborne nodded as the screen went black, then stepped out of the car without a word.

Securing a million-dollar divorce settlement from the Caldwells in one day? Even for a wealthy family like them, a million dollars is a huge ask. They'd likely rather kill me than pay up.

As he walked down the street, he considered his options.

How should I go about this? he wondered. To them, he was nothing more than an undeserving peasant.

He couldn't imagine this would end well.

As Osborne continued down the street, his mind raced, searching for an answer, a tactic, a path—anything that could help him figure out how to complete his first trial.

He spotted a general store and got a crazy, but perhaps brilliant, idea.

Maybe, just maybe, this could work…
