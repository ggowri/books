"You should learn to talk about your frustrations. Find someone to vent to, and you'll feel better. Meanwhile, I'll hear your tragic tale, realize my life isn't so bad after all, and feel great.

"Isn't that a win-win?

"So don't go! You've got nothing better to do anyway. It's just the two of us here, so talk to me!"

She clung to Osborne, determined to get the truth out of him.

"And, well... I lost the argument."

Osborne couldn't help but laugh.

He knew she was just trying to cheer him up.

He thought, "Alfreda really is such a kind and sweet girl.

If only all Elf women are like her..."

Unfortunately, Peggy's icy, scornful face flashed through his mind.

He waved a hand dismissively. "Alright, I feel a lot better now. Thanks for cheering me up! You should head back, Alfreda. I have some things to think about."

"Alright! Rest well. And don't worry. As long as you're staying here, I won't let anyone mess with you!"

She waved, watching Osborne head into the wooden lodge before skipping off.

Ugh, I'm a princess of the Nature Elf Royal Family, and I still have to dig for information on my own. What a disgrace!

Maybe I should just leave Elf City..."

The idea popped into her head suddenly. But in the end, she shook it off.

"The outside world is too dangerous. I'd better stay put," she thought.

Humming an old Elf song, she made her way back to her quarters.

Just then, a mocking laugh echoed through the air.

"Well, well, Princess Alfreda. Seems like you're enjoying yourself. You got punished and sent to guard this shabby little inn, yet you still have the mood to sing?"

"What are you doing here?

"If you lay a hand on me, the Fire Elf Royal Family will retaliate. And your dear father, the king, will have to punish you to appease us.

Monica laughed triumphantly.

"So? Do you dare hit me? Come on, do it!"

"Tell me, Princess Alfreda, don't you think your life is pathetic?

"We're both princesses, yet you're living worse than a common Elf. If I were you, I'd be too ashamed to even step outside.

She taunted Alfreda again.

Seeing her pause, Monica grew bolder, laughing even louder as she continued her insults.

Inside the wooden lodge, Osborne sensed Alfreda's rising fury and the turbulent energy outside. Unable to ignore it, he stepped out to see what was happening.

The moment he appeared, Monica spotted him.

Her smugness vanished. Her expression turned ice-cold. "Human? You're actually staying here? Damn it! Who allowed you to live here?"

He hadn't expected to run into Monica.

It was obvious she was here to give Alfreda trouble.

He ignored her and turned to Alfreda instead. He could see she was shaking with rage, her face flushed with frustration.

"Are you alright?" he asked.

Alfreda didn't respond.

Monica, seeing that Osborne had completely disregarded her, flew into a rage.

"A filthy Human like you dares ignore me?! You lowly human, I'm talking to you! Can't you hear me? How dare you rent a place here? Are you looking for death?

Before she could finish, a sharp slap echoed through the air.

Monica was suddenly sent flying, crashing hard against a massive Elf tree.

With a loud thud, she tumbled to the ground.

The air fell into silence. Her arrogant voice was finally cut off.
