Donna intercepted the lightning with her body.

With a sudden crack, as the lightning struck, a thick earthen wall materialized in front of her, miraculously blocking its path.

Osborne swallowed.

This elderly woman is also a metahuman. Her strength surpasses Milo’s by far. Is her superpower to control the earth?

Osborne was extremely shocked.

“L-Let go of me.”

At that moment, a shy voice suddenly sounded from Osborne’s arms.

He glanced down and discovered Clarissa staring at him with her captivating eyes. She, like a startled bird, was held tightly in his arms, cheeks flushed red.

He coughed and quickly released her.

He said solemnly, “Ms. Radcliffe, we need to leave this place immediately and find somewhere to hide. You’re being attacked by more than one person, and gunmen are closing in.”

As soon as Osborne finished speaking, gunshots rang out in the distance.

His face darkened. He instantly picked up Clarissa and ducked behind the cover.

The next moment, countless bullets hit the cover, kicking up clouds of dust. The barrage was so dense that Osborne couldn’t lift his head.

Fortunately, his Satan’s Eye could see through the cover and into the distance.

Two hundred yards away, dozens of fully armed, masked assailants in black were advancing rapidly, each wielding an automatic rifle and firing as they approached.

Paul and the other bodyguards with Clarissa were forced to duck behind cover, returning fire cautiously and not daring to lift their heads.

If this persisted, within half a minute at most, dozens of killers would reach them.

“Damn it,” Osborne couldn’t help but curse under his breath. He should have expected this long ago. How could the test of heirship that the Godfather gave to his heir be devoid of danger?

It was simply unrealistic to complete the test effortlessly.

Now, his top priority was ensuring Clarissa’s safety. Her death would signify his failure.

We can’t confront them directly, he thought. Our only option is to fight while falling back. I need weapons!

Osborne made up his mind and looked suddenly into the distance.

There lay one of Clarissa’s bodyguards, struck by lightning and unconscious. His riot shield was discarded beside him, and there was a pistol in his holster.

Osborne measured the distance, gritted his teeth, and prepared to dash out from cover. Though highly perilous, with the riot shield and pistol, he could mount a defense.

“W-What are you going to do?”

When he was about to rush out, Clarissa abruptly grabbed his arm.

He was stunned and quickly explained, “I need weapons and a riot shield. Without them, I can’t fight back or ensure your safety.”

“But it’s too dangerous. Bullets are flying everywhere out there,” Clarissa said, her concern evident.

Osborne shook his head and smiled, “I have to go. Otherwise, you will die.”

Then, he pounced out swiftly like a cheetah.

He activated his Satan’s Eye to its utmost, calculating the position and speed of every incoming bullet, evading them with precision.

Yet, even so, he still couldn’t avoid the bullets completely.

The ceaseless bullets were like raindrops, impossible to dodge entirely.

Unexpectedly, Osborne felt a pain in his left shoulder, and blood spurted out.

Fortunately, it was not a vital part. Enduring the pain, he hurriedly picked up the riot shield and positioned it in front of himself.

The next moment, endless bullets hit the riot shield with bangs.

Osborne was grateful for the top-notch riot shields provided by the Radcliffe family for their bodyguards, crafted by the Draco Group, which could easily withstand even dense rifle bullets.

He promptly pulled out the pistol from the unconscious bodyguard’s waist.

“Now, it’s my turn to fight back.”

Osborne braced the riot shield with his injured left hand and fired fiercely with his right.

The downpour limited visibility for all but him. With Satan’s Eye, he effortlessly pinpointed the location of every enemy.

With each shot he fired, an enemy fell a hundred yards away.

By the time he had emptied the last of the ten bullets in his magazine, ten enemies lay knocked down in the distance, and the dense gunfire abruptly thinned out.

He seized the opportunity and returned to Clarissa’s side.

“Ms. Radcliffe, let’s go.”

Osborne helped Clarissa up and covered her with the riot shield, ready to leave.

Meanwhile, a tall and robust figure obstructed their path.

It was Paul.

Osborne subdued the enemy’s gunfire, prompting Paul to emerge from cover. Instead of launching an immediate counterattack, he swiftly rushed to Osborne.

“Hey, let go of Ms. Radcliffe! I am the captain of her bodyguards. From now on, I will take care of her.”

With that, Paul pushed Osborne away violently.
