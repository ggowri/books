"Diana, hold on a second!" Osborne called out to her as she stepped further ahead.

Diana turned around, confusion etched on her face.

"Osborne, what's wrong? If you're feeling scared, you can just let me go ahead alone!"

With a smile, Osborne shook his head.

"I'm not scared! I just have a feeling... there's a Deathborn lying in wait right around the next bend."

Diana stared at him in disbelief.

"Are you serious? You can sense it? Do you have some kind of power to predict danger?"

Osborne smiled and nodded.

"Something like that."

Diana's eyes lit up with excitement. "You really have a danger-sensing power! That's fantastic! With that ability, your safety is much more assured. At least we don't have to worry about being ambushed by a Deathborn!"

Osborne couldn't share in her enthusiasm; instead, he was pondering how to leverage this opportunity to give Riley and Emmett a run for their money.

The Deathborn hidden ahead, lying in wait, was a Deity Overlord—smart and powerful, even more formidable than any Osborne had encountered before.

Though he was confident he could handle it, he had no desire to expose his strength just yet.

If he revealed his capabilities, Riley and Emmett would be on high alert around him.

It would be more strategic to avoid confrontation.

Could he go around it?

Osborne considered that option. Even though the rocks and soil surrounding Divine Peak Nine had specialized energy barriers, making them incredibly tough—almost impenetrable even to earth elemental power—his spatial power was relatively unaffected.

He could easily use it to maneuver quietly past the concealed Deathborn, leaving Riley and Emmett to deal with whatever awaited them.

But revealing that spatial power was a risk he was reluctant to take.

"What to do?" Osborne thought.

Riley and Emmett were only a little over half a mile behind them. If he couldn't come up with a plan soon, his chance to prank them might slip away.

He turned to Diana again. "Do you have any idea how we could bypass that Deathborn and avoid a fight?"

Diana's eyes brightened.

"Oh! I get it! You want Riley and Emmett to face the Deathborn instead. You're planning to set a trap for them, right?"

Osborne smirked. "They're out to get me, so if I get the chance, I'm definitely going to strike back. But to pull off this little trick, we need to sneak by that Deathborn without alerting it."

Diana's eyes sparkled with excitement.

Clearly, she had long been dissatisfied with Riley and Emmett and was eager for a chance to turn the tables.

"I have an idea!" she exclaimed, pulling out a small orb that instantly captured Osborne's attention.

"What's this?"

Diana quickly explained, "This is a Deathborn Relic! It's an eye left behind by a Deity Overlord-level Deathborn after it died, and it's a mutated one! It contains a unique essence of Deathborn power. If we activate this essence and envelop ourselves in it, other Deathborn will mistake us for one of their own and won't attack!

"Deathborn Relics are rare, and the power inside is limited. Using even a little bit means it's gone for good, which is why we don't use it lightly—only in life-or-death situations.

"But now that you've confirmed there's a Deathborn hiding ahead, we can use this Relic to disguise our presence.

"The ambushing Deathborn will think we are allies and leave us alone! It will only strike once Riley and Emmett pass by!"

As Diana finished speaking, Osborne's eyes sparkled with excitement.

"A Deathborn Relic? That's a fantastic find! With this, there's no way we won't set them up!"

Without wasting another moment, they sprang into action.

Diana channeled her divine power into the relic, which suddenly radiated an unusual energy that wrapped around both her and Osborne.

"Let's go!" she urged, and they moved deeper into the tunnel.

Osborne remained alert. Although Diana was confident the Deathborn Relic would mask their presence and keep them safe, he couldn't shake the anxiety creeping in.

After all, they were close to a Deity Overlord-level Deathborn—one with cunning intelligence.

Soon enough, they approached the spot where the Deathborn was hiding.

When Diana finally saw the creature, who was camouflaged and poised to strike, shock washed over her face.

"Osborne was right! He really can sense danger!" she thought, but she kept silent, focusing on staying hidden.

Under the protection of the Deathborn Relic, she and Osborne slipped right past the looming threat without triggering any response.

They moved swiftly away from the danger, the tension easing as they got further from the ambush.

Not far behind them, Riley and Emmett were steadily making their way into the darkness, oblivious to the peril that lay ahead.
