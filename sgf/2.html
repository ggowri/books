As Osborne stared at them, he once again questioned if he was dreaming. Perhaps he was still on the floor, either dead or beaten unconscious. A sense of shock overwhelmed him.

"The Godfather wants to see me?" he asked, confused. "Which one?"

"Mr. Augustine, with respect, there's only one Godfather in Rosmandi," the leader replied.

Osborne's eyes widened.

The Godfather? The ruler of Rosmandi's underworld? Why would someone so powerful want to see me? And why are they kneeling?

His mind raced with questions, but before he could inquire further, the man simply opened the car door.

"Mr. Augustine, please, get in. All will be explained in time. The Godfather is waiting for you."

Osborne hesitated for a moment, but figuring he had nothing to lose and nowhere else to go, got in the car.

The moment he took his seat, a screen turned on, showing the shadowy silhouette of a burly man, sitting in a large chair with a black cat in his lap.

Osborne squinted through bruised and battered eyes, trying to get a better look at the imposing man before him. From what Osborne could discern, the man had slicked-back hair, a black suit, and a bowtie. He exuded wealth and prestige.

Osborne squinted harder, but he still couldn't make out the man's face. All he could see were two piercing eyes cutting through the shadows.

Is he the Godfather?

Even through the screen, his power was obvious.

Suddenly, the man shooed the black cat from his lap. He stood up, leaning on a cane as he approached the camera.

Osborne recoiled as the man came into focus.

"You... how?" As hard as he tried, he couldn't break eye contact with the shadowy figure. His gaze… It couldn't be.

The man smiled, pointing to his left eye as he leaned closer to the camera. "You're wondering why my left eye has double pupils like yours, aren't you? Well, to most I'm the Godfather. But you can call me Grandfather. "

"What?" Osborne's eyes widened, revealing double pupils in his reflection.

He was stunned into silence.

An orphan all his life, he never knew his family.

Now, out of nowhere, the Godfather showed up and claimed to be his grandfather?

Was it a prank arranged by Ophelia? Was it brain damage from the beating he took?

"Kid, I know it's hard to believe, but it's true. There's a DNA test on your right, middle seat. Take a look," the Godfather said.

Osborne picked up the sealed manilla envelope and tore it open, a sense of bewilderment washing over him as he leafed through the report.

There, in bold print, were the results confirming their relationship.

Could this man really be his grandfather? With the Godfather's status, faking it seemed unnecessary. Still, despite the report, Osborne had his doubts. After the morning he'd had, he wasn't about to believe a damn thing without definitive proof.

"We share The Eye of Satan, a signature trait of the Augustine family's bloodline. It activates the first time you sleep with a woman after coming of age."

Hearing this, Osborne was almost convinced. It seemed too specific to be fake. His eye mutation was his secret, unknown even to his ex-wife Ophelia—and the timing of the mutation's first occurrence aligned perfectly with what the Godfather described.

"The Eye of Satan, huh?" Osborne whispered, seeking further confirmation. "Anything special about it? Can it do anything?"

"It's powerful beyond imagination. From the looks of it, yours is still level one, which means it enhances daytime vision, grants night vision, and slows your perception of time," the Godfather explained.

Osborne stifled a gasp. His vision had become incredibly sharp, even at night, and he could see fast-moving objects in slow motion.

Everything matched.

Tears welled in his eyes.

"Have you always known about me? Why wait until now? And why did my parents abandon me?"

He remembered living under bridges with his sister, fighting for scraps, thinking he was nothing more than a street kid.

Now, he had a legacy. He had a family. His grandfather was the Godfather—a billionaire several times over.

"Sorry, kid. You may be my grandson, but you're not getting answers to those questions just yet. Not unless you become my official heir," the Godfather said coolly.

"Heir?"

The Godfather nodded. "What can I say? I'm not getting any younger. I need to preserve my legacy. As my grandson, you have the right to inherit everything—assuming you want it. So, what's it going to be? Do you want to become the new Godfather?"
