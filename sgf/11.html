Justin stared at Osborne’s grin, a chilling fear creeping up his spine.

It made Justin doubt himself.

Could Osborne’s bomb be real?

Justin turned to the shopkeeper, his face twisted with rage, and started beating him.

“Tell me, is the bomb real or not?”

“Fake! It’s all fake! The powder inside? It’s just sand. I delivered it myself.” The boss, unable to take the beating, cried out. “Stop hitting me.”

Osborne smirked, “Justin, don’t think I’m underestimating you. You’re a coward. Even if you beat him to death and he wasn’t lying, you still wouldn’t dare face me.”

Justin halted, his face twisted with rage.

Ignoring him, Osborne turned to Ophelia, “Do you remember three months ago when I flipped the dining table?”

Ophelia was taken aback but nodded.

Three months ago, she and her stepmother, Evelyn Caldwell, were about to have dinner, however, Osborne went mad and flipped the table. Furious, they gave Osborne a harsh lesson.

She remembered it vividly.

“Osborne, why bring this up? You’ve done plenty of dumb things over the years; this one’s no big deal.”

Osborne sneered, “You think I’m being dumb? Ha! If it weren’t for me, you would have been dead by now, you fool. If I hadn’t flipped your table, Justin would have poisoned you.

“Justin bribed your maid to lace your food with poison. With you out of the way, he’d have no competition for the inheritance.

“Isn’t that right, Justin?”

Ophelia frowned deeply, unable to believe it.

Justin’s eyes widened, teeth clenched.

So, his poisoning plan from three months ago had been foiled by Osborne.

He loathed Osborne but dared not show it. He shouted, “Slander! Osborne, you worthless scum, how dare you accuse me? No proof, just lies. Do you think Ophelia is an idiot? She’s my cousin; why would I want to poison her?

“Ophelia, this loser is trying to drive a wedge between us. You won’t fall for it, will you?”

Ophelia’s face darkened, remaining silent.

Osborne gave a faint smile and continued, “If you think I’m lying, Ophelia, just investigate. Start with the nanny.”

Justin’s face turned pale at Osborne’s words.

Osborne continued, “And a month ago, Justin sent his men to kill you. Luckily, I found out in time.

“We fought in the dark, and you blamed me for making noise and disturbing your rest.”

Ophelia’s expression shifted again, staring at Osborne in disbelief.

“Do you have any proof? And why didn’t you explain back then?”

Osborne shrugged. “I said I didn’t want you to worry. Would you have believed me?”

Ophelia paused, biting her lip.

“Proof? That night, I injured their arms. Check it out. A month ago, did one of Justin’s men have an arm injury? You’ll find your answer.”

Justin’s face darkened instantly.

Furious, he spat, “You scoundrel! Trying to sow discord? It was a fight in the dark. How can you be sure they were my bodyguards? They couldn’t see you, so how could you see them?”

Osborne looked at Justin with disdain, offering no explanation.

What, should I tell him I have Satan’s Eye and can see in the dark?

He turned to Ophelia with a mocking grin. “Do you really trust Justin? Are you willing to bet the explosives on me are fake?

“If we go down together, who do you think benefits the most?”

Ophelia clenched her fists, glaring at Osborne, but her mind was already made up.
