Osborne was rendered speechless, realizing he had underestimated the Williamson family's cunning.

The Honor Quest Ceremony for Cuthbert was not just a celebration but a well-laid trap. They had prepared for the possibility of losing the divine artifact, the Volt Hammer, by ensuring they could summon it back with the Thundergod Gloves.

Their plan was nearly flawless!

Had it not been for Isa's insistence on compensation with a top-grade divine artifact and the fact that the Thundergod Gloves were the Williamson family's only such artifact, Osborne would have walked right into their snare.

Upon returning to Rosmandi and finding the Volt Hammer missing, Osborne would have been utterly bewildered, never suspecting the Williamson family's hand in its disappearance.

Peter looked somewhat embarrassed and quickly changed the subject. "The Thundergod Gloves are no ordinary top-grade divine artifact. They're said to be deeply connected to the most powerful legendary artifact, the Hammer of Thor.

"It's rumored that by wearing the Thundergod Glovess, one can sense the location of Hammer of Thor and potentially find it."

Osborne was visibly shaken by this revelation.

"Through the Thundergod Gloves, I can sense Hammer of Thor's location and find Hammer of Thor? Have you sensed its location?"

Peter's embarrassment persisted.

"No," he admitted.

Osborne's excitement waned.

Even if Hammer of Thor remained lost, Thundergod Gloves stood as a top-grade divine artifact, capable of amplifying the Volt Hammer's power, transforming the already formidable weapon into something truly terrifying.

Even wielded alone, without the Volt Hammer, the gloves could transform into the ultimate boxing gauntlet, infusing the wearer's fists with a surge of strength.

Without hesitation, Osborne claimed the Thundergod Gloves.

Peter's eyes brimmed with reluctance, a silent storm of hesitation. Yet, as his gaze flickered towards the icy demeanor of Isa, his lips sealed shut.

"Ms. Night, we've paid our compensation. May we leave now?" Peter inquired cautiously.

Isa smiled as she turned to Osborne.

"Do you have any other requests, Osborne? Speak freely; they won't refuse."

Osborne hesitated.

With nervous tension, Peter stared at Osborne and dreaded the thought that Osborne might, once again, propose another unreasonable demand.

"I want nothing more, but I have questions that need honest answers."

Relief washed over Peter.

His heart lightened with the realization that Osborne sought nothing further.

"Ask away. We'll answer truthfully if we know."

Osborne pressed on. "Your Williamson family has a good relationship with the Aberrants, right?"

Peter paused but nodded.

"We do. There's been no conflict for decades, but we're not allies. We simply coexist and jointly maintain the structure of Tethynia."

Osborne nodded and asked with a hint of anxiety, "Have you noticed any unusual movements from the King of the Aberrants recently?"

His concern was palpable.

It had been some time since Sebastian had ventured alone into the Aberrants. With no news and no way to contact him, Osborne feared the worst.

Yet, he held his tongue. What if Sebastian's cover remained intact? To ask outright could unwittingly unravel the secrecy shrouding his location.

Peter's features were etched with clear lines of hesitation.

"You're asking about Sebastian Augustine from Rosmandi, right?"

Osborne's body tensed. "You know him?"

Peter nodded, stating, "I do. Just three days ago, I sensed a battle between him and the King of the Aberrants.

"Both are top S-class warriors, ranked on the Quasi-Divine Rankings. I was curious to witness such a clash.

"But by the time I arrived, Sebastian was gone. Only the King of the Aberrants remained. I asked if he had killed Sebastian, and he denied it, saying he had only inflicted severe injuries and let Sebastian escape.

"Then, the King of the Aberrants left without further explanation."

As Peter's words hung in the air, a visible shiver ran through Osborne.

He savored what Peter had said and pondered, "Sebastian had a fight with the King of the Aberrants?

Sebastian was seriously injured?"

"You're not lying to me?" Osborne demanded and clenched his fists in worry.

Peter hastened to affirm, uttering, "I swear on my honor that every word I've spoken is the truth! I witnessed the aftermath at the battleground. The ground was stained with copious amounts of Sebastian's blood.

"His injuries must be grave."

Osborne took a deep breath and looked solemn.

Peter looked to Isa, who dismissed them with a wave of her hand. They fled as if pardoned, leaving Osborne and Isa alone amidst the weight of the news.

"Osborne, don't let it consume you," Isa said gently.

Her hand caressed his cheek. "I, too, felt the ripples of that battle, but I was preoccupied with my search and didn't investigate further. Sebastian must be alive; I can vouch for that. However, he is indeed gravely injured and likely seeking a place to recover.

"With his strength, as long as he's alive, he will heal in time. Once he recovers, he'll return."

Osborne nodded vigorously, clinging to this hope.

"Yes, as long as he's not dead!"

His gaze then shifted to Isa. Determination burned in his eyes.

"Isa, I want to grow stronger!"
