As the number of Celestial Deity-level Deathborn dwindled, the battle became increasingly manageable. Both Osborne and Hannah inevitably began to let their guard down.

Hannah, in particular, was caught up in the excitement. Having never experienced such a chaotic battle before, she felt a rush of exhilaration at the prospect of victory. The thought of wiping out all the remaining Celestial Deity-level Deathborns filled her with joy, even more so than triumphing over the top talents of the other races.

In this moment of triumph, she couldn't help but relax.

After all, Osborne was right there with her.

With his strength, even if she held back, he could easily finish off the remaining Celestial Deity-level Deathborns on his own.

"Our teamwork is absolutely flawless!" Hannah declared, a proud smile spreading across her face.

But just as she reveled in her success, disaster struck.

Neither she nor Osborne noticed that while they were engaged in combat with the Celestial Deity-level Deathborns, two even more powerful Deathborns had been stealthily traversing through the solid rock in a spectral form, now positioned directly behind them.

At the moment Hannah was basking in her accomplishments and Osborne was focused on attacking another Celestial Deity-level Deathborn, the two Deathborns suddenly emerged from the rock. In an instant, they shifted from their ethereal state to their physical forms and launched a simultaneous attack on both Hannah and Osborne.

The speed and force of the assault were overwhelming.

By the time Hannah sensed something was off, it was already too late to dodge or counterattack.

With a startled cry, she barely managed to summon her divine power for defense.

In the next second, the attack struck her with terrifying force, shattering her defenses and slamming into her body.

She was sent flying, blood spraying from her mouth as she hurtled through the air.

Simultaneously, Osborne faced his own assault. However, his reflexes were quicker than Hannah's. As the dreadful attack approached from behind, he instinctively reacted, channeling his power to unleash a Thunderform Flash.

Brilliant arcs of electricity enveloped him, propelling him forward at lightning speed while also providing a protective barrier.

Although Osborne couldn't completely evade the attack, he managed to avoid taking a direct hit.

The blow was powerful enough to shatter his lightning defense, but it left his Divine Body unscathed.

Everything happened in the blink of an eye.

One moment, Osborne and Hannah believed victory was within reach, and the next, they were both sent sprawling.

The difference was that Osborne had actively dodged while Hannah was violently thrown back.

Seeing Hannah spitting blood and turning pale sent a wave of dread through Osborne.

"Deity Overlord-level Deathborn! Two of them!" he exclaimed, his expression shifting to one of grave concern.

He forced himself to regain control, quickly turning to the two Deity Overlord-level Deathborns that were now racing toward the injured Hannah.

Panic surged within him. Hannah was severely hurt.

The sheer power of the Deity Overlord-level Deathborn's surprise attack was devastating; even with her formidable strength, she had been unable to withstand it unprepared.

That Hannah hadn't been outright killed was a testament to her resilience.

But now, in her weakened state, if those two Deity Overlord-level Deathborns struck her again, it would be the end.

At that moment, Hannah, still conscious but barely so, looked up in horror as the two Deity Overlord-level Deathborns charged toward her. Despair filled her eyes.

Even if she hadn't been injured, facing off against two of them would have been a nearly impossible task.

"Am I really going to die here?"

A cold wave of despair washed over Hannah as she helplessly watched the two Deity Overlord-level Deathborns charging toward her at breakneck speed. She felt utterly defenseless, unable to react or dodge.

All she could do was wait for her fate to unfold.

Osborne was still far away. Even if he used Thunderform Flash, he wouldn't be able to reach her in time.

"Is this how it ends? I can't believe I, Hannah, am going to die like this. I haven't even gotten married yet."

The scent of death hung thick in the air, and she couldn't shake the feeling that her time was up.

"All those times I kept my identity hidden from the top talents of the other races, and now no one knows my true potential. I was finally ready to make my mark, and now... it's all going to be for nothing."

With the two Deity Overlord-level Deathborns closing in, their grotesque forms and overwhelming power were clearer than ever.

Their killing intent radiated from them like a palpable force, leaving Hannah engulfed in despair.

She couldn't take it anymore. With her eyes shut, Hannah braced for the inevitable.

Meanwhile, Osborne's face was a mix of hesitation and turmoil. Even using Thunderform Flash wouldn't be enough to save her at this point.

He felt trapped, knowing there was only one way to truly rescue Hannah.

Osborne had to use spatial power.

But using it would reveal his identity, and that could lead to dire consequences.

"Do I really want to save Hannah?"

The question loomed heavily in his mind as he watched in horror, realizing Hannah was on the brink of death at the hands of the Deity Overlord-level Deathborns. With time running out and Hannah's life hanging by a thread, Osborne made his decision.
