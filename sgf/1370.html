Osborne continued to draw energy from the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity, urging his space elemental essence to absorb its powerful core energy.

In this deep space, time had become an abstract concept.

Osborne had no idea how long he had been here, but it felt like an eternity. Yet, there was no point in fretting; impatience wouldn't help him escape.

All he could do was wait patiently.

As his space elemental essence rapidly absorbed the core energy, he divided his focus, also working to enhance his dark elemental essence.

The progress was slow, but any improvement was better than none.

Time slipped away, and before he knew it, all three core energies from the Primordial Crimson Lotus had been consumed.

Just as he had anticipated, his space elemental essence had only reached the late Level Six, still a significant distance from its peak.

"Looks like I'll need at least three or four more core energies from the Lotus to break through to Level Seven," he thought, biting his lip in frustration.

With his essence upgraded to late Level Six, the deep space around him no longer felt like an endless void. It resembled a moonless night—still dim, but he could see a bit more clearly now.

Unfortunately, there was nothing around him.

At this point, Osborne felt no threat from the spatial tide, not because his Divine Body had grown stronger but because his spatial power had intensified.

He could even influence the flow of the tide with a mere thought.

His spatial power had skyrocketed beyond what it once was.

Yet, despite this newfound strength, he still had no clue how to escape this deep space.

He muttered, determination etched on his face, "Looks like I'll have to keep extracting core energy from the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity.

"If I don't push my space elemental essence to Level Seven, I won't be able to leave!

"Sorry, Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity! I know how crucial those core energies are to you, but I'm in a tight spot and have to keep borrowing from you!"

With that resolve, Osborne activated the Breath of Celestial.

Just like before, he harnessed the Breath of Celestial to draw core energy from the Lotus, pulling one energy after another from its body.

One wasn't enough, so he continued until he had extracted five core energies.

But when he took the fifth core energy, the Primordial Crimson Lotus suddenly trembled violently.

It seemed to weaken and become somewhat translucent from the loss of so much energy.

In that instant, its blooming petals snapped shut.

"What the...?!" Osborne gasped, panic rising in his chest.

He quickly halted his extraction, staring in horror at the closed petals of the Lotus.

His expression darkened as he realized that his Dimensional Microcosm had previously been resting on those very petals.

Now, with the petals closed, the Microcosm was completely enveloped, severing his connection to it.

"What do I do now? Is the Dimensional Microcosm going to be okay? What about Grandpa, Anastasia, and the others in there? Damn it! If I had known this would happen, I wouldn't have extracted so much energy!"

Frantic, Osborne felt utterly helpless.

Even if he tried using the Breath of Celestial again, he couldn't re-establish contact with the Lotus to release the Dimensional Microcosm.

Osborne took a deep breath, trying to calm his racing thoughts. "It'll be okay.

The Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity is just low on core energy. Once it replenishes, it'll bloom again. Its petals are enveloping the Dimensional Microcosm, making it even safer.

I just need to focus on leveling up my space elemental essence to Level Seven and get the hell out of here."

With that resolve, Osborne dove back into his meditation.

After absorbing another surge of the Lotus' core energy, his space elemental essence finally reached the peak of Level Six, teetering on the brink of a breakthrough.

Meanwhile, his dark elemental essence, through natural cultivation, had quietly advanced to Level Six mid-stage.

"Phew, almost there," he murmured, his voice tinged with relief.

Just as he prepared to push forward, a jolt of anxiety shot through him.

His eyes snapped open, scanning the dim recesses of deep spatial. Osborne felt a prickling sensation, as if something—or someone—was watching him.

Osborne's heart pounded.

"What the hell? Something's watching me? That can't be right. This is deep space; nothing but raging spatial tides. What could be out there?"

His expression grew somber, but the shock was just beginning.

The Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity within him began to tremble violently, as if gripped by fear.

Osborne's eyes widened in disbelief.

"What? What could possibly be spooking the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity?

This is a Cosmic Treasure we're talking about!"
