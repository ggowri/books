"I'm using martial art at Class S from Eityreia Organization, Stealth Arts! I've already mastered it, so how can you possibly see me?"

Roger's voice dripped with anger, but there was a hint of panic behind it.

He wasn't particularly powerful, barely on par with Stuart, having only just broken into Class AAA. But with the Stealth Arts at Class S exclusive to Eityreia Organization and his dagger, he could easily take down opponents at Class AAA.

He'd even managed to assassinate experts from the Godson Rankings.

Yet here he was, facing Osborne, and his supposedly unbeatable stealth was completely useless.

Once might be a coincidence, but twice?

Osborne had definitely figured out his technique.

It was as if Osborne knew exactly where and when Roger would strike, dodging effortlessly and countering without hesitation.

This threw Roger off balance.

"Stealth Arts?" Osborne muttered, a calm smile tugging at his lips.

The truth was, Roger's Stealth Arts relied on merging his body with the surrounding space. When he disappeared, he was actually hiding in Dimensional Space.

So, no matter how sharp someone's eyesight was, they couldn't see Roger because he wasn't even in the same space.

Plus, Roger was excellent at masking his energy, making it nearly impossible for most people to detect him before he struck.

But Osborne was a spatial power user.

For him, Roger's stealth wasn't much of a challenge.

Roger, now clearly frustrated, snapped, "How the hell are you seeing through my Stealth Arts?"

Osborne just smiled and said, "Call it a strong sixth sense."

"Sixth sense?" Roger scoffed, narrowing his eyes.

"You really expect me to believe that? Even if you're that lucky, you won't always dodge me. I can keep trying, but you? You only need to mess up once, and you're dead.

"Let's see you dodge this!"

Roger vanished again, slipping into the dimensional space as he silently moved toward Osborne.

Osborne could feel exactly where Roger was but decided to let him get closer, waiting for the right moment while quietly gathering his strength.

Roger was biding his time, waiting for Osborne to make a mistake.

Sensing this, Osborne decided to play along, deliberately letting his guard down just a bit.

"Got you!"

Roger took the bait, emerging from the dimensional space with his dagger aimed right at Osborne's heart.

Osborne's eyes flashed with cold determination.

Roger's attacks were all lethal, clearly aiming to kill him in one strike, which only fueled Osborne's anger.

"Get lost!" Osborne roared, unleashing a punch he had been holding back.

His fist smashed into Roger's shoulder with crushing force.

Roger hadn't expected the opening to be a trap. The blow shattered his shoulder, and he dropped his dagger as he was sent flying across the arena, blood spraying as he crashed hard onto the ground.

Osborne caught the dagger mid-air and calmly pocketed it.

He looked down at Roger, his voice cold, "You lost. Is this over now?"

Struggling to get up, Roger saw his dagger in Osborne's hand and spat out blood.

"Give... give me back my dagger!"

Osborne smirked. "You tried to kill me, got your ass kicked, and now you want your dagger back? Why would I do that?"

"Because I'm with Eityreia Organization!" Roger's voice dripped with anger, his eyes full of threat.

"Hand over the dagger, and we're done here. Otherwise, you'll never know peace. We will hunt you down endlessly!

"You'll die, and so will everyone you care about! Everyone connected to you will pay the price!"

His eyes blazed with a mixture of rage and madness.

Osborne's expression tightened, and a flash of deadly intent crossed his face.

Roger was clearly not bluffing.

After a moment's thought, Osborne reined in his anger, pulled out the dagger, and tossed it back to Roger.

"This ends now."

Roger snatched the ancient dagger, his face twisted in contempt and cold satisfaction.

Without saying another word, he shot Osborne one last icy glare before quickly making his exit.
