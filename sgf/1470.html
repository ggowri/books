Tiana spoke candidly, "But if a Deity Overlord warrior comes after you, we might not be able to save you in time."

"Their power is far beyond yours—you're merely at the Transcendent Deity level, and you'd struggle to withstand even a single blow."

"Even if you're a Super VIP, we can't assign a Deity Overlord warrior to protect you around the clock."

"So, if the worst happens, even if we want to help, we might just be too late."

Her words drew scoffs from the crowd.

"Even if he spends 100 million, he can't get a Deity Overlord warrior's protection! This guy thinks that scoring some ancient deity's treasure and a pile of Essence Crystals makes him untouchable now that he's a super VIP."

"That's rich! No wonder he dared to provoke Fabian."

"Now he probably regrets ever stepping up. I bet he'll be begging for mercy soon enough!"

"He must definitely be regretting it now."

Osborne chose to ignore their jeers and pressed on with his questioning. "So if a Deity Overlord warrior targets me, you all just stand by? That Super VIP status sounds pretty useless, doesn't it?"

Tiana shook her head, her tone firm. "Of course, we won't just sit idly by."

"As a super VIP, you'll receive our protection. Generally, people will think twice before messing with you, but there are always those who are reckless—like Fabian! If he really comes after you, we won't let him get away unscathed. We'll make him pay a hefty price for his actions."

"That being said, don't expect us to help you kill Fabian or seek revenge; that's simply not possible."

Osborne nodded thoughtfully. "Got it! So, becoming a Super VIP is mostly a deterrent. If a Deity Overlord warrior decides to take me out, they'll just face a few consequences and move on."

He shrugged, a hint of determination shining in his eyes.

"Looks like I really need to rely on myself after all. Trusting others is a fool's game! But I still want that Super VIP status. Are there any more items up for auction? Let's keep this going."

With that, Osborne settled back, ready for the next round of bidding.

Soon enough, the auction resumed its usual pace, and this time, they showcased slaves from various other clans. It seemed the world wasn't just about alliances—there were debts to be settled and vendettas to be avenged.

Some had fallen victim to betrayal, while others were driven to sell themselves into servitude to escape disaster.

However, the number of slaves from different clans was significantly lower.

Osborne wasn't interested in any of the other clans' slaves that came up for bidding.

Without Osborne's involvement, the auction regained its normal pace, allowing the various factions in attendance to finally make some gains.

As he awaited the next item, the auctioneer's voice rang out once more. "Next up, we have a very special female slave on the block. Her background is quite unique—she's a hybrid!"

"I can't disclose her specifics or abilities, but I assure you, no matter how much you pay, she will be worth every crystal!"

As soon as he finished, the crowd erupted into a frenzy of chatter and speculation.

"What? All her details are a secret, and you expect us to bid just because she's a female slave? And you seriously think she'll be worth whatever we pay?"

"I'm not buying that!"

"At least tell us what two clans she's a hybrid of!"

The auctioneer chuckled in response. "I'm afraid I can't reveal everything due to the sensitive nature of her case. But I can tell you this—she has half human blood in her!"

At that revelation, a new wave of excitement surged through the crowd.

"One half human blood? And you can't even disclose the other half? It's not some superior clan, is it? Otherwise, why would you keep it under wraps?"

"Superior clan? What a joke! There's no way a superior bloodline would mix with a lowly one like the Human Community. That would just tarnish their lineage!"

"Exactly! It's impossible for there to be a hybrid of the Human Community and a superior clan. With such pathetic details, do you expect us to throw our coins at this auction? You think we're fools?"

"I know I'm out!"

Osborne furrowed his brow, intrigued by the murmurs. "A Human Community hybrid with another clan? Half human blood, and no revelation about the other half? What's the deal there?"

He found himself pondering the implications.

But given the Human Community connection, Osborne mulled it over before making a decision; he couldn't let this opportunity slip away.

Lifting his paddle, he stated confidently, "I bid one million!"

It was a hefty sum for an unknown hybrid slave, even with her Human Community lineage. He figured it would be enough to keep others at bay.

However, just as he settled in, the auctioneer spoke again, cutting through the chatter, "Apologies, but this hybrid girl is under a commission from someone else!"

"The starting bid is 30 million!"

"If it doesn't reach that, she will be destroyed on the spot!"

A stunned silence fell over the crowd at the auctioneer's announcement.

Thirty million?

That was an outrageous starting price, even higher than the opening bids for three top-tier Human Community female slaves who were at the Celestial Deity level!

It felt astronomical, completely off the charts.
