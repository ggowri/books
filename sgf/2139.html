The ancient voice echoed through the cave, reverberating ominously. At the spot where Uriel had fallen, a translucent figure suddenly materialized.

Towering and imposing, this spectral elder gazed intently at the Sacred Artifact-level palace, his excitement palpable.

Despite the recent explosion of Sacred Power, the palace remained unscathed.

"Ascending Sacred Sanctum! It's mine now!"

The translucent figure of the elder moved eagerly toward the Sacred Artifact-level palace.

But then, his expression hardened dramatically.

He narrowed his gaze at a shadowy corner of the palace and bellowed, "Who's hiding over there?"

"Show yourself! If you don't come out, don't blame me for what happens next!"

His cold, menacing voice sent chills through the cave.

In the next moment, two weary figures emerged slowly from the shadows—Osborne and Alfreda.

Their faces were pale, reflecting the tension of the moment as they regarded the spectral elder.

The elder's surprise was evident as he took in the sight of the two.

"What? There are still survivors? I sealed a portion of Sacred Power within Uriel's body! If anything happened to him, that power would unleash, annihilating everything within 60 miles!

"You two... you actually survived?"

Osborne bit his lip, choosing silence.

He had felt the terrifying surge of Sacred Power within Uriel, which was unstable and ready to explode. In that critical moment, he activated interdimensional travel, hoping to escape the inevitable.

But he knew that even with interdimensional travel, evading the blast would be nearly impossible.

The Sacred Power within Uriel was far too potent.

It was a true force of Sacred Power, unlike anything he or Alfreda had encountered before.

That power, once unleashed, could easily crush their bodies to dust.

Osborne had to find the only place that might save them—the Sacred Artifact-level Palace.

He and Alfreda dove into the palace just in time, praying it would shield them from the impending explosion.

Miraculously, his gamble paid off.

Though the shockwave from the blast still left them injured and bloodied, they had survived.

The same could not be said for the Umbra World beings, who were obliterated without a trace.

"Wait... you're the head of the Ethereal Elven Temple!"

"One of the three Empyrean Sacredness warriors of Elf City—Isidore Rosedale!" Alfreda exclaimed, her voice filled with shock.

The elder turned his gaze to her, surprise etched on his face, which quickly morphed into recognition.

"Ah, so it's you, Princess Alfreda of the Nature Elf Clan."

A glint of menace flashed in his eyes as he regarded her.

Alfreda felt a wave of fear wash over her, her body tensing as she trembled slightly. It wasn't until Osborne grasped her arm reassuringly that she felt a bit of relief, her complexion regaining some color.

Osborne was equally stunned.

The figure before them was none other than the master of the Ethereal Elven Temple, a true Empyrean Sacredness warrior.

His goal was the Sacred Artifact-level palace, and that complicated matters significantly.

The only silver lining for Osborne was that Isidore was merely a specter, not his true self.

The rules of the Umbra Earth continent dictated that only those under three hundred years of age could enter; anyone older would be struck down by the fearsome lightning from the sky.

This rule applied even to Empyrean Sacredness warriors, meaning Isidore could not step foot into the Umbra Earth in his true form.

Osborne considered for a moment, realizing that Isidore had likely hidden a fragment of his soul power within Uriel's body, using it to manifest here.

A single strand of soul power shouldn't be too strong, Osborne thought, and with that realization, he felt a wave of relief wash over him.

Just then, Isidore spoke again, his tone surprisingly amiable, "Princess Alfreda, you two should leave. While this Sacred Artifact-level palace may be damaged, it's far beyond the reach of younglings like yourselves. If you attempt to seize it, you'll invite endless troubles. So, I suggest you make a hasty exit!"

Alfreda's brows knitted in confusion.

She exchanged a glance with Osborne, seeking guidance in his eyes.

Keeping his demeanor steady, Osborne replied, "He's right, Alfreda. The Sacred Artifact-level palace isn't ours to claim. Only warriors like Mr. Isidore Rosedale are worthy of obtaining its power! Let's head out quickly."

With that, he gently took Alfreda's hand, guiding her step by step toward the cave's entrance.

Soon, they found themselves standing next to Isidore's ghostly figure.

Osborne nodded toward Isidore, who returned the gesture with a warm smile, his demeanor surprisingly gracious, laced with admiration.

But just as Osborne and Alfreda attempted to pass by, Isidore made a sudden move.

His hand came crashing down toward them with astonishing speed. In a normal situation, there would have been no time to react.

But just then, the two figures vanished into thin air.

Isidore's palm struck the ground with a deafening impact, creating a massive crater but leaving Alfreda and Osborne utterly unharmed.

They had used interdimensional travel and reappeared over a thousand yards away, racing for the cave's exit with all their might.

Osborne growled, his expression gloomy as he pulled Alfreda along, "Damn it! I knew that old coot wasn't up to any good!"

"Even Uriel understood the necessity of silencing us to keep the existence of the Sacred Artifact-level Palace a secret! Isidore would certainly do the same!"

"That old fox is devious!"

As they fled, Osborne desperately cast interdimensional travel again, but the power was heavily suppressed in the Umbra Earth, limiting him to a jump of a mile at most.

Behind them, a chilling voice snickered, saying, "You're quick on your feet!"

"Seems like you've been preparing all along!"

"I haven't even attacked yet, and you're already scheming your escape! Spatial power, huh? Too bad you can't outrun me!"

"Since you've learned of the Ascending Sacred Sanctum, it's better for you to just die—I can't allow anyone to spill the secrets of the Sanctum!"

With that, Osborne suddenly sensed a terrifying force barreling toward his back.

His eyes widened in shock. "Why is he so fast?"

Gritting his teeth, Osborne activated the Eclipse War Armor and donned the Bloodwrought Armor, pulling Alfreda tightly into his embrace.

The next moment, Isidore's devastating attack slammed into Osborne's back. The Eclipse War Armor shattered like fragile glass, splintering under the brutal force.

Then came the Bloodwrought Armor, an Apex divine artifact that offered little resistance against Isidore's ferocity, crumbling effortlessly and losing all its divine power.

Osborne's own divine power defense and physical strength followed suit under Isidore's onslaught.

The overwhelming power penetrated every layer of his defenses, striking him with crushing force. Blood spewed from Osborne's mouth as he felt his body nearing a breaking point.
