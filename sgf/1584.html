A grand scheme, involving a whole lot of people, began!

According to Osborne's plan, today was Donald's first official day on the job. Guy gave him a warm welcome, showed him some goodwill, and officially made Donald a centurion under his command, letting him pick his team freely.

So, after choosing his team, Donald decided to take them out to celebrate, and the place he picked just happened to be on the west side of town—very close to Laird Borden's mistress' house.

This gave Donald and Murray a legitimate reason to be there.

Most of the members Donald chose were talented elites from various clans and factions—perfect witnesses for what was about to happen.

In fact, they'd also end up "injured" by "Laird," drawing even more groups into the mess.

So, after nightfall, Donald and Murray, along with their chosen one hundred and ten soldiers, swaggered out of the base.

Before heading out to "celebrate," Donald even called Herman Saxton, letting him know where he'd be.

Naturally, Herman had no objections.

He even praised "Guy" for being considerate, since according to the rules of the Wild Flame Army, Donald wasn't supposed to leave the base without permission.

The fact that Donald could take his men out showed that Guy was extending an olive branch.

Herman also wasn't worried about Donald's safety.

Given Donald's status, no one in Wild Flame City would dare mess with him.

And so, Donald led his group, marching confidently to the west side of town.

They celebrated and drank in a tavern.

Meanwhile, Osborne, having learned from Guy that Laird was already heading in their direction, began to move. He approached the courtyard where Laird's mistress lived.

Osborne quickly scoped out the place.

There was a divine lock on the courtyard gate, and without the key, it would be impossible to enter quietly without alerting everyone inside.

But for Osborne, that was no problem.

He activated his Satan's Eye to check out the courtyard. Seeing only Laird's mistress and about seven or eight servants, none stronger than a low-level Celestial Deity, Osborne used his spatial power to slip inside without setting off the divine lock.

By now, Osborne had completely altered his appearance.

He looked exactly like Donald.

He quickly made his way deeper into the courtyard.

Using his power, he knocked out the servants in seconds.

"Who's there?!"

A stunning, sultry woman stared at Osborne, her face going pale, eyes full of alarm.

Osborne mimicked Donald's voice, sneering, "What a beautiful woman! Just what I need tonight to blow off some steam!"

With that, Osborne lunged at her.

The woman gasped in shock and tried to fight back. She was a low-level Celestial Deity, but against Osborne, she stood no chance. After a brief struggle, she was knocked out cold.

"Got a good look at my face, didn't she?"

Osborne muttered, stripping the unconscious woman and tossing her onto the ground. He even made sure to make the scene look like she'd been assaulted—though he found it disgusting, it was part of the plan.

He left behind a few traces, including an identification tag...

One belonging to Donald.

After finishing, Osborne smirked.

"Laird, this is your favorite woman. I don't believe for a second you won't lose it when you see this. You're definitely going to think Donald did this."

Mumbling to himself, Osborne quickly left.

Time for the next phase!

By now, Donald and his group had finished their feast and were just leaving the tavern, heading down a street near the courtyard on their way back to the base.

And right there, on that street, they ran into Osborne.

At this point, Osborne had taken on Laird's appearance, even wearing clothes that Guy had secretly swiped from Laird's wardrobe.

The clothes even carried Laird's scent.

No matter who saw him, they'd be convinced he was Laird.

"Laird," having been bumped into by Donald, flew into a rage, not giving Donald or his men a chance to explain. He attacked immediately.

After a brief but intense fight, the peak Deity Overlord "Laird" easily left Donald, Murray, and the others badly injured, nearly destroying their divine essence.

Even the soldiers Donald had brought along were hurt by "Laird."

After that, Osborne, still in disguise, used Laird's voice to threaten Donald, glaring down at him before turning and heading back to the courtyard...

And all of it was recorded.

"Laird, let's see how you get out of this one!"

Osborne muttered, ordering Donald and the others to leave quickly before they ran into the real Laird.

Now, all that was left was to let the situation escalate!
