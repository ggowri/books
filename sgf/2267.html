As Osborne stepped out of his lodgings, he hooked up with Bethan and Jamar, heading for the arena.

Today was the grand finale of the city duel—the day when the champion and the top ten contenders would finally be crowned.

As they made their way, Osborne couldn't help but notice how deserted the streets around Azure Haven Academy were.

It was like the whole town had stampeded to the arena, jostling for the best seats to catch the crowning of the city-duel champ.

"Osborne!" A familiar voice rang out, and he whipped around to see Angela striding towards him.

"Go on without me," he said, motioning for Bethan and Jamar to keep moving.

Then he turned back to Angela, his curiosity piqued.

Angela flashed him a warm smile. "It's the last day of the city duel, and you're one of only two undefeated guys left in the running. Honestly, you've blown all my expectations out of the water. Since you've come this far, go out there and grab that championship!

"If you manage to clinch the victory, I've got a real treat in store for you!"

"A treat?" Osborne arched an eyebrow, hooked by her mysterious vibe.

But Angela just shot him a grin, leaving him scratching his head about what she had up her sleeve.

"I'll give it my all," Osborne said.

She nodded, her expression turning dead serious. "Today's bouts are gonna be fierce. Azure Haven Academy has been pulling some shady moves with the match pairings. All your opponents are from there, and they'll surely try to wear you out."

Osborne let out a confident chuckle. "No problem! When you have absolute strength, all their little tricks are just a joke!"

Angela stopped for a second, caught off guard by his rock-solid confidence.

After a moment, she nodded. "Okay then, as long as you're ready! I'll be in the stands, rooting for you, waiting to see you claim that title!"

With that, she waved goodbye and headed off.

Osborne continued on his way to the arena, but he suddenly felt a pair of eyes on him.

He turned to see Clara emerging from the Fantasia Isle quarters, her gaze fixed on him.

"Osborne!" she called out, quickening her pace to catch up. "Hey there!"

"Hi, Clara!" Osborne replied, matching her friendly tone.

He appreciated her politeness; after all, she was a formidable opponent, having mastered Sacred Power at such a young age.

They were both set to be in the top ten of the city duel and would soon compete together in the state duel in Zephyr State.

"Do you think you can clinch the championship today?" Clara asked, her eyes sparkling with curiosity.

Osborne hesitated for a moment before responding, "You really think I have a shot at it?"

Clara said, her confidence unwavering, "Absolutely! You've already beaten me! Others might doubt you, but I know you have the best chance of winning. Of course, Leroy is also a tough contender. I can sense she's not to be underestimated. If I face her, I can't say for sure I'd win.

"So, it's clear that the championship will be fought between you and Leroy! But I'm rooting for you!"

Osborne was taken aback. "Why's that?"

Clara smiled, "No reason, really. I just don't like Leroy! Plus, when we get to the state duel, we'll be teammates. You've got to help me out and get me into the top ten!"

"Wait, help you? Get you into the top ten? What do you mean? How can I help you in the arena?"

Clara looked equally surprised. "You don't know?"

"Know what?" Osborne asked, puzzled.

Clara let out an exasperated sigh. "The state duel isn't just a bunch of one-on-one scraps! Seriously, don't you know? There are a hundred cities in Zephyr State, and each city picks its top ten fighters for the state duel. That's a whopping thousand contestants in total!

"There are so many competitors that the usual arena showdowns just won't cut it. So starting from the state duel, we'll be switching to wilderness battles! Team-ups will be the norm.

"You didn't know?"

Osborne rubbed the back of his neck, looking a bit sheepish. He'd just assumed that the upcoming state and zone duels would follow the same arena-style format.

But wilderness battles? That was a whole new kettle of fish.

"Can you spell it out for me?" he asked, genuinely intrigued.

Clara nodded enthusiastically. "In the state duel, they usually pick a remote spot where all the thousand contestants go in to hunt for specific items. The more you snag, the higher your score! But just finding them ain't enough; you gotta be able to keep them!

"You can swipe from each other, too! Even if you find a boatload of items, if you're not tough enough to defend them, it's all for nothing—you'll end up with zero points.

"The rankings in the state duel are based on how many points you rack up! So, a whole bunch of people team up to find and safeguard the items. If you try to go it alone, you're gonna have a hard time getting a good score!

"After all, the competition in the state duel is cut-throat!

"We'll be up against opponents from First-rate Powers! If we don't team up, forget about cracking the top ten; it'll be a struggle just to make it into the top hundred."

Osborne listened, his eyes wide in disbelief.

It seemed that standing out in the state duel was going to be a whole lot tougher than in the city one.

In Tempest Fortress City, there were only three Second-rate Powers, and he could count the real threats on one hand. But in Zephyr State, there were a hundred cities, each with several Second-rate Powers.

Every single one of those powers would be a force to be reckoned with, and many would be just as tough as the Mystic Sirius Sect. Just one strong contestant from each Second-rate Power could pile on the pressure for him.

And then there were the disciples from First-rate Powers to worry about!

The mere thought of going up against entire teams made his head spin.

Going solo seemed like a near-impossible feat.

Osborne asked Clara, "Are you thinking of teaming up with me for the state duel? Why not hook up with Leroy or Marreese?"

Clara kept a poker face. "Like I said, I'm not into them! Besides, they wouldn't go for it anyway! The folks from Azure Haven Academy will stick together and won't even give us a second look."

Osborne nodded, mulling over her words. "Alright! If push comes to shove, let's team up!"

Clara's face broke into a beaming smile. "It's a done deal! And I'm already crossing my fingers for you to take down Leroy and grab the city duel championship!"

With a wave, Clara took off, leaving Osborne to make his way to the arena.

The place was charged with energy, filled with spectators champing at the bit for the final showdown.

As the battles got underway, Osborne waited for his turn.

But first up were Marreese and Tyson. They went at it hammer and tongs for half an hour, and in the end, Marreese came out on top once again.

Next up was Leroy versus Tyson.

It was obvious to all that Leroy was barely breaking a sweat.

She took out Tyson with ease, displaying a might that left the crowd in utter awe.

The cheers for Leroy's likely victory reached a deafening crescendo.

At last, Osborne heard the referee call his name.

"Mystic Sirius Sect's Osborne, up against Azure Haven Academy's Leona Acosta!"

Osborne's eyes lit up.

He sprang to his feet, his veins pumping with adrenaline. "It's finally my turn!"
