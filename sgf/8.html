Ophelia frowned, speechless.

Just hours ago, she thought she knew Osborne well.

But now, seeing the crazed Osborne and the bodyguards on the ground, she realized he wasn’t the same pathetic husband she once knew.

Why has he gone mad?

Could it be that he loves me so much? Did our divorce push him over the edge?

Ophelia suddenly thought she could use this to her advantage.

Softly, she said, “Osborne, I know you’ve been wronged. The Wise Order made the decision to leave you with nothing, not me. I still love you.

“How about this: I’ll ask the hospital staff to leave now. You put down the bomb remote, and we can talk.

“Maybe we can find a better solution.

“I… I never really wanted to divorce you.”

Ophelia’s face was soft with emotion, but to Osborne, it looked fake and disgusting.

“Cut the act, Ophelia,” Osborne sneered. “You don’t know me, but I know you too well. Your acting sucks. Get the hospital staff out of here and give me a fair divorce settlement, or we’ll both go down together.

“You’re the heir to a billion-dollar empire. I’m just an orphan with nothing. If you go down with me, it’s an even bigger win.”

“You!” Ophelia gritted her teeth, furious and frustrated.

She felt trapped. She thought she could control the situation easily, but Osborne had been in control all along.

The man she once saw as worthless now had the upper hand.

Osborne was standing alone against the entire Caldwell family.

Commoner versus elite?

Ophelia was stunned.

Was she really going to pay Osborne a divorce settlement?

She felt powerless.

She couldn’t risk Osborne detonating the bomb.

She didn’t want to die.

She had just become the Caldwell heiress and hadn’t even enjoyed the wealth and power yet.

With that thought, Ophelia finally gave in.

“Fine, I agree. I’ll give you the divorce settlement,” she spat. “We can draft a new agreement. On behalf of the Caldwell family, I’ll give you two million dollars.”

Osborne sighed in relief but sneered, “Two million? Are you kidding me? I’ve endured three years of humiliation and abuse from the Caldwell family. Two million to make me go away? Ha! I’d rather take us all down.”

With that, he raised the detonator, ready to press the button.

“Stop!” Ophelia turned pale and quickly intervened. “How much do you want? A hundred million is out of the question. Even as the Caldwell heiress, I can’t authorize that. The Wise Order oversees everything.”

She stared at him, trying to show confidence she didn’t feel.

“If you insist on a hundred million, then go ahead and blow us up.”

Osborne paused, pretending to think it over.

He knew the Caldwell family’s situation well. Ophelia couldn’t possibly give him a hundred million in cash. He was just aiming high to negotiate.

Osborne wasn’t worried at all; he had the upper hand now.

After some back-and-forth, they finally struck a deal.

The Caldwell family agreed to compensate Osborne with twenty million.

Osborne was pleased but pretended to be at a loss, demanding Ophelia draft a new divorce settlement.

Soon after, the twenty million hit his account, and Osborne finally relaxed.

He actually pulled it off.

He passed the Godfather’s heir test.

Osborne’s eyes were a bit red.

If not for the Godfather’s test, he would never have dared to storm the Caldwell Manor alone. And getting the Caldwell family to cough up twenty million? That was a damn miracle.

At this moment, Osborne was overwhelmed by triumphant emotion.

Just hours ago, he was beaten by Ophelia’s bodyguards, forced to sign a divorce agreement that left him with nothing.

But now, he got the compensation he deserved.

Most importantly, he passed the Godfather’s first test.

What would the Godfather reward him with?

What would his reward be? Wealth? Power? Or women?

The renowned Godfather, his granddad, wouldn’t be stingy, right?

Now, Osborne just needed to get out of the Caldwell Manor and claim his reward.

Osborne straightened up and walked towards the door. Ophelia and the others, despite their frustration, didn’t dare to stop him.

But then, everything changed.

A woman in the crowd glanced at her phone and suddenly gasped.

“Ophelia, we can’t let him leave!

“We’ve been tricked! That loser, the bombs on him…

“Are fake!”
