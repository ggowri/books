Osborne's gaze was fixed on a spot six miles beneath the surface, where the waters lay calm.

There was no sign of battle, which only confirmed his worst fears: Bryce had been captured.

Bryce was alive but barely clinging to consciousness, his body battered and bruised.

Clearly, the Aberrants intended to take him alive for some sinister purpose.

Otherwise, with sixty Class S clones surrounding him, there would be no way Bryce could've escaped—he'd have been dead long before now.

Although Bryce was a Class S warrior, Osborne was confident that he could take Bryce down swiftly in a one-on-one fight.

However, even with that certainty, Osborne had no illusions about facing sixty Class S clones at once. He knew that not even the Trident of Poseidon would give Bryce an edge in that kind of overwhelming situation.

"At least it's only sixty, not the hundred I was fearing," Osborne thought, his expression serious as tension gripped his chest.

"What's going on, Osborne? How's my brother? Is he okay?" Kendra's anxious voice broke through his thoughts, her eyes filled with concern.

Osborne took a deep breath, trying to maintain calm. "Listen, don't freak out. The Aberrants hasn't killed Bryce yet. He's alive but... they're planning to take him away alive. There are sixty Class S clones with him right now. They should be moving him soon."

"Sixty Class S!"

Kendra nearly stumbled back, her face turning pale with despair.

"What are we going to do? They're going to torture my brother! How could this happen? We have to do something!"

Osborne said firmly, gripping her shoulders to steady her, "Hey, take a breath!

"We still have a chance! I think they don't know we're here yet. That gives us an edge!

"But I can't take you with me.

"You need to find a safe spot while I figure out how to rescue him. I can't risk your safety.

"If I'm gone for more than a day and still not back, head home without me.

"No heroics, understood?"

Kendra bit her lip, wrestling with the overwhelming fear churning inside her.

"Okay, I'll do what you say! But Osborne... you have to promise me you'll be careful. If... if you can't get Bryce back, don't put yourself in danger trying. I don't want to lose you, too."

Her pain was clear, concerns flooding her eyes.

She desperately needed her brother saved, yet she didn't want to see Osborne jeopardize his life for a mission that might be hopeless.

"Got it, I'll be cautious," Osborne replied, determined.

He quickly grabbed her hand and activated another interdimensional travel jump, taking them back 90 miles to a small island.

"This should be safe enough for you to hide," Osborne said as they landed.

Given Kendra's Class AAA strength, she could stay safe as long as she didn't encounter the Aberrants.

"Whatever you do, stay put and don't draw attention to yourself."

With a last reassuring look, Osborne activated interdimensional travel again and shot 90 miles away.

He emerged just 6 miles from Bryce's location.

He quickly cast his Satan's Eye ability, sharpening his focus on the sixty Class S clones, vigilant for any sign of weakness and waiting for the right moment to strike.

Osborne felt his heart race; he knew he had to tread carefully.

This was no small task—sixty Class S clones were no joke, and if they closed in on him, it would be game over. He needed a plan, and he needed it fast.

Osborne mused, "I either need to take out over ten Class S clones at one time, dropping their numbers below fifty, or I have to rescue Bryce first.

With Bryce's strength, he should be able to hold off ten or even twenty Class S clones for a short time. That would relieve some of the pressure on me."

The thought of taking down ten Class S clones in an instant felt daunting.

He recalled his last spar with his grandfather, where he had managed to eliminate only six before being forced to retreat.

He thought, "Compared to that, saving Bryce seems like an easier choice.

His injuries are severe, but with the Light of Redemption, I can get him back to peak condition in no time."

However, the challenge loomed large. Bryce was surrounded by sixty Class S clones, and getting in close enough to use the Light of Redemption wouldn't be easy.

Osborne pondered, "Maybe I can tackle both at once!

If I can take out several Class S clones quickly, I could break their circle and create a path to Bryce.

But if I get swarmed while trying to reach him, and Bryce can't hold off the remaining clones, we're both done!"

Osborne clenched his jaw, deep in thought, as he calculated the best approach.

"Which Class S clones should I target first to maximize my chances of success and disrupt their circle?"

His gaze landed on eight Class S clones huddled together, their proximity offering a glimmer of hope.

"Those are the ones! If I can take them down, I'll have a clear shot at Bryce. Plus, they're all Vampire Kings—my specialty!"

Osborne felt a surge of confidence.

He knew he could dispatch them more easily than the others.

"This is it! I'm going for it!"
