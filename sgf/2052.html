Osborne dodged the whip of the elven guard once again, his expression growing colder by the moment.

The guard's attacks were becoming increasingly fierce.

If it weren't for Osborne's formidable strength, any ordinary Ascension One Supernal Deity King would have been severely injured, perhaps even left for dead, under such relentless strikes.

This only fueled Osborne's anger.

His gaze fixed on the nine elves, rage simmering within him, threatening to boil over.

"Still think you can hide?"

The guard, now completely enraged, launched four consecutive strikes, each one failing to even graze a hair on Osborne.

He evaded them all with ease, and the humiliation was palpable.

As an Ascension Five Supernal Deity King and the youngest captain of the Fire Elf Clan's royal guard, he felt deeply shamed to be outmaneuvered by a mere human of Ascension One.

In front of the Royal Family, this embarrassment was unbearable.

His fury ignited, and the wooden whip in his hand burst into flames, signaling that he was ready to unleash his full power. A strike from an Ascension Five Supernal Deity King, if it landed, could easily maim even an Ascension Four Supernal Deity King, let alone someone of lower rank.

"Get down!" he roared.

With a thunderous crack, the whip transformed into a massive, fiery serpent, sweeping down toward Osborne like a divine dragon, filling the limited space of the Elf City's dimensional portal.

Osborne, no matter how swift he was, found it impossible to evade the impending strike.

At that moment, Osborne's fury reached its peak.

"So you want me dead? Fine! Then let's settle this! Kill!"

A murderous glint flashed in Osborne's eyes as he prepared to unleash his full power, ready to take down this group of elves and make his escape.

He'd just have to find another city, after all; there were plenty of other places beyond Elf City.

Just as Osborne was about to explode into action, a commanding voice cut through the tension.

"Stop!"

The voice was melodious yet authoritative, causing the Ascension Five guard to shudder and halt his attack without a moment's hesitation.

The immense, fiery whip vanished in an instant.

Osborne, who had been ready to retaliate, also came to a stop, turning to see the source of the voice.

It was one of the Royal Family members, a female elf.

Though she appeared disheveled, resembling a refugee, her beauty was undeniable, with delicate skin and striking features.

Her brow was furrowed as she glared at the male elf who had initially reprimanded Osborne.

She said coldly, "Desmond, have you had your fun yet?

"This expedition hasn't gone well, and we've lost several elite guards. I know you're frustrated, but don't take it out on innocent people!

"If you keep this up, we might as well go our separate ways and you should stop bragging that you're my friend!"

As she finished speaking, Desmond Garza's expression shifted dramatically.

Anger flared in his eyes, but he quickly backtracked, trying to appease her. "Joanna, please don't be angry! I was just following the protocol to discipline him, not venting my frustrations!

"He did break the rules of Elf City, didn't he?

"We're members of the Elf Race's Royal Family, and he's been staring at us for too long. By the rules, he deserves ten lashes!"

The elven princess, Joanna Edmund, scoffed disdainfully.

"That rule is utterly tyrannical! Our Sylvan Elf Clan has always opposed such nonsense! It was your Fire Elf Clan that insisted on implementing it!

"Besides, he is obviously a newcomer to Elf City. He hasn't even set foot in the city yet and clearly knows nothing about your ridiculous regulations!

"Are your guards trying to kill him?

"You're going too far!"

Desmond's face shifted through a spectrum of emotions, but quickly he plastered on a smile.

"Joanna, please don't be angry! I see I was wrong! I shouldn't have been so arrogant and overbearing. I apologize!"

Turning to Osborne, Desmond added, "Look, my friend from the Human Community, this has all been a misunderstanding. I've been under some pressure lately, and unfortunately, you were in the line of fire. Thanks to Princess Joanna's reminder, I now realize my mistake. I genuinely apologize; I hope you can forgive me."

Handing over a VIP card, he continued, "Consider this a peace offering!

"With this VIP card, you'll get a ten percent discount at any establishment within the Fire Elf Clan. I truly hope you can forgive my earlier behavior."

As the ornate card, engraved with a flame totem, fluttered toward Osborne, he furrowed his brow, contemplating the situation.

It was clear that Desmond wasn't sincerely apologizing; his actions were merely a ploy to curry favor with Joanna.

Still, after a moment's thought, Osborne accepted the VIP card.

Desmond visibly relaxed, turning to Joanna. "See? I've apologized sincerely, and I truly regret my actions! Please don't stay angry."

He gestured to another male elf nearby. "Bernie, you say something too."

Bernie Grantham quickly chimed in, "Princess Joanna, I believe Desmond has genuinely learned his mistakes.

"He is indeed remorseful; can't you give him another chance?"

Joanna's icy demeanor softened somewhat.

She frowned, saying, "Let's hope we don't have a repeat of this. I dislike it immensely!"

She then turned to Osborne.

After a moment of hesitation, Joanna didn't utter another word but gave him an apologetic look, nodding slightly before walking deeper into Elf City.

Bernie and Desmond hurried to catch up with her.

Before departing, Desmond shot Osborne a cold, piercing glance filled with silent malice and barely contained rage.

It seemed to say, "You lowly human, consider this your warning."

Without awaiting a reaction from Osborne, Desmond quickly turned and left.

Osborne rubbed his temples, sensing trouble brewing.

He thought, "I hoped to keep a low profile in Elf City and avoid unnecessary conflict, but that's clearly wishful thinking! I don't want to stir up trouble, yet trouble seems to find me!

As soon as I discover a way to quickly enhance my powers and abilities, I'll be out of here! This is the territory of the Elf Race, and I've now made an enemy of a member of the Fire Elf Clan's Royal Family.

Things are likely to become a lot more difficult."

He sighed, hoping for a smoother experience moving forward, and nodded gratefully at Letitia for her earlier intervention.

Then, he stepped into Elf City.
