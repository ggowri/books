As Osborne watched the crowd scatter like spooked deer, a soft chuckle escaped his lips.

It was all thanks to Lincoln's astonishing might.

His effortless victory over Lincoln had sent a crystal-clear message to everyone—if they couldn't hold a candle to Lincoln, there was no way they could take on Osborne.

In this arena, the slow-footed were easy pickings.

One moment they were the hunter, and the next, they could end up being the hunted.

Osborne waved off the fleeing competitors. Sure, they were all in the top thirty, each with a fair share of points.

But he had no interest in giving chase.

After downing Lincoln, he'd snagged a tenth of Lincoln's points—350 thousand.

That bumped his total up to a whopping 7.95 million, enough to leapfrog Horace and land him the second spot on the leaderboard, just trailing behind Agatha, who'd broken the ten-million mark.

Osborne muttered, plotting his next moves, "That oughta do the trick!

"If I can take out Benson, Broderick, Horace, and Agatha one after another in a flash, I'll push my score past 11 million! And when I beat Agatha, her points will take a nosedive.

"Then, I'll be taking home the championship!"

Leroy suddenly spoke up, "Shame I won't be around to see that showdown.

"This is the clash of the titans from the power rankings! It's gonna be the most epic battle of this state duel, no doubt about it!"

"If I can't knock out Broderick and Benson, there's no way I can go toe-to-toe with Agatha."

His eyes blazed with determination.

Meanwhile, on the far side of the Arctic Maw Chasm, Agatha and Horace finally squared off.

Horace's eyes were like lasers, and his whole demeanor was oozing with fighting spirit. "Agatha, you finally decided to show up!

"I've been itching for this fight for ages!

Horace frowned and said, "Is that so? Maybe there are some dark-horse masters in Zephyr State, but I don't think they can hold a torch to us.

"In my book, if I beat you, I'll grab the title of the strongest under the Empyrean Sacredness. And if you beat me, I'll tip my hat to you as the best."

This was the famous Frost-invoking Canvas.

Rumor had it that in the city duel, Horace had unleashed the Frost-invoking Canvas with such brute force that it had frozen the entire arena solid in an instant. Even the high-ranking Empyrean Sacredness observers up on the podium were left gobsmacked.

This was one hell of a Sacred Artifact.

According to the officials in the Zephyr State, it was on the same level as Agatha's Phoenix Cry Bone Flute, and miles ahead of the rest in its league.

That was exactly why Agatha and Horace were at the top of the power rankings, holding the first and second spots respectively.

"Agatha, let's see if you can stand up to my Frost-invoking Canvas!" Horace declared, full of swagger.

Agatha gave a faint smile. "The Frost-invoking Canvas, huh? It's a real beast, no doubt. A while back, it might've given me the jitters. But not anymore."

She casually tucked her hands behind her back, oozing confidence. "Come on, take your best shot."

Agatha's main motivation for venturing down to the seventh layer of the Arctic Maw Chasm was to hunt for the Frost-kissed Heart. She aimed to soak up its powers to fend off Horace's freezing attacks.

The chilling energy unleashed by the Frost-invoking Canvas was rumored to be utterly destructive.

Her mentor had warned her that even with the Phoenix Skeletal Shield, she'd probably end up as a mere ice sculpture. So, her best bet was to harness the energy of the Frost-kissed Heart and steel herself against the bone-numbing cold.

But things didn't go as she'd hoped.

Even though she hadn't fully kindled the Flames of the Dark Phoenix, she was now tough and confident enough to withstand run-of-the-mill ice powers.

I've won!

No one below the Empyrean Sacredness can get out of my Frost-invoking Canvas unharmed.

Once frozen, it's all over. Even those who've just ascended to the Empyrean Sacredness can't pull through.

Agatha, you've already bitten the dust!
