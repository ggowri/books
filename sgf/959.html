"Flame!" Osborne shouted again, unleashing a torrent of fire that seared Gerardo's blood, igniting a fresh wave of agony within him.

This time, however, Gerardo didn't scream.

He had grown accustomed to the pain.

But the toll on his body was becoming alarmingly apparent as his wounds worsened. With Osborne's relentless support, Sebastian hammered Gerardo again and again, each strike echoing like thunder.

Gerardo's breaths were growing shallow and labored.

Once more, Sebastian sent Gerardo crashing to the ground, blood pouring from his mouth as he struggled to remain standing. Yet amid the chaos, Gerardo's eyes glinted a deep crimson, a fierce rage bubbling within him.

Gerardo exclaimed, "Ha! Look at you!

"I didn't expect to be pushed this far! A Class AAA little punk causing me so much trouble? I underestimated you!"

Gerardo trembled, but there was a palpable surge of terrifying power radiating from him.

"Don't let him talk! Kill him!" Osborne yelled in alarm.

Before Osborne could utter another word, Sebastian unleashed his next attack, sensing the shift in Gerardo's energy.

In an instant, an explosive force erupted from Gerardo, sending Sebastian hurtling backward, blood spewing from his mouth.

Osborne's expression shifted to one of shock as he swiftly caught Sebastian in mid-air.

Their eyes widened in disbelief as they stared at Gerardo, who was now emanating an overwhelming presence.

"What the hell is happening?" Osborne muttered, furrowing his brow.

Gerardo was on the brink of defeat, yet now he seemed more dangerous than ever.

Sebastian shook his head, clearly at a loss.

"Osborne, be careful! If things go south, you need to run. Don't worry about me!"

Osborne frowned, stubbornness igniting within him. "No way! We're in this together!"

Sebastian's body tensed.

He continued to watch Gerardo with an unwavering focus. The energy surrounding Gerardo intensified, and even Sebastian found it difficult to approach.

Gerardo cackled suddenly, a chilling sound that sent shivers down their spines.

"Sebastian, this was meant for you! A little surprise I've been saving. But now? Now it's time for you both to die!"

With that, Gerardo's form twisted violently, morphing almost inhumanly before their eyes.

Osborne gasped.

"Medusa?!"

Before their astonished gazes, Gerardo transformed into something monstrous—a reflection of Medusa herself.

It was clear to Osborne that this was no ordinary shape-shifting power; he could feel an aura of monstrous strength radiating from Gerardo.

It was divine power!

Sebastian shouted in horror, "Oh no, that's the Medusa deity! He's fused his body with her remains, temporarily wielding divine power!

"Osborne, we need to run! Gerardo can manipulate divine power now; we don't stand a chance!

"Let's go!"

Sebastian grabbed Osborne by the shoulder, bolting away as fast as they could.

"You think you can escape?" Gerardo taunted, his voice twisting into something unearthly, sharp, and feminine.

He didn't immediately launch an attack; instead, Gerardo seemed to revel in his newfound form, admiring his body as if it were a flawless piece of art.

"For twenty long years, I dedicated myself to merging this Medusa deity with my own body, and just recently, I finally succeeded!

"Though it's not a perfect union yet, and there are still some issues, it's more than enough to take you down! I am now a true god!

"Any Class S who stands before me is nothing but worthless trash!

"Sebastian, Osborne, you two are the first to witness this power of mine. Even in death, you'll have something to be proud of!"

As his words echoed, a chilling light flickered in Gerardo's eyes.

"Petrify!"

With a sudden pulse, the energy surged from his gaze, reaching out across the distance where Osborne and Sebastian had taken refuge.

In an instant, they froze in place; their bodies halted as the stone began to creep over them. It only took a breath for the stone to envelop them completely, leaving them immobile, not even a finger able to twitch.

Fear etched itself across their expressions.

They thought, "How could this be? At such a distance, Gerardo has managed to petrify us with a mere glance?"

Sebastian was among the elite of Class S, not much weaker than Gerardo. Yet, despite his formidable strength, he had been effortlessly turned to stone by Gerardo.

What kind of power was this?

It seemed only a true deity could wield such terrifying abilities.

Their minds raced in shock, but their bodies were powerless to react.

Gerardo laughed, his voice dripping with mockery as he seamlessly appeared in front of both of them.

He regarded the statues of Sebastian and Osborne as if they were corpses, a grim satisfaction shining in his eyes.

"Now, it's time to send you both off to hell!"
