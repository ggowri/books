“There’s actually a powerhouse?”

Osborne snorted coldly. Being enveloped by this tornado, he immediately felt that there were countless wind blades around him, which were viciously cutting his body.

Luckily, a force emerged from the Class B Defensive Soft Armor to withstand the power of the wind blade for him.

But Osborne could clearly feel the Defensive Soft Armor’s energy being rapidly depleted.

In half a minute at the most, the Defensive Soft Armor would run out of energy and turn into regular bulletproof armor.

Paul laughed out loud and said coldly, “Why don’t you continue to be arrogant? So what if you have the Class B Defensive Soft Armor from the Draco Group? Under Ms. Reed’s Phoenix Tornado attack, you’ll soon be cut into a pile of broken meat.

“Even Ms. Radcliffe’s Holy Light Healing was too late to save you. You’re doomed.”

Paul then looked icily at Clarissa.

“Ms. Radcliffe, it’s your turn now. Ms. Donna gets tangled up in Raymond. Your bodyguards are stuck with a bunch of werewolves. And Osborne, you can’t count on him.

“Now, you can die in peace.”

Paul walked quickly toward Clarissa.

Clarissa bit her lip. There was no fear in her eyes, only concern for Osborne.

Osborne was somewhat powerless at this point.

He thought, I’m not strong enough after all. I have too few means. When I run into a Class C+ metahuman, I won’t get much of a handle.

Osborne was even more anxious as he watched Paul rush toward Clarissa.

“Damn. Isa, why haven’t you arrived yet? You’re not going to break your promise, are you?” Osborne shouted.

And just then, the roar of a motorcycle sounded.

A motorcycle then rushed into the battle, drifted handsomely, and came to a stop next to Clarissa’s car.

Isa removed her helmet, revealing her long, curly hair. A faint smile was on her delicate, charming face.

She teasingly looked at Osborne and said, “I’m here, aren’t I? Aren’t you still alive? A wind-controlling metahuman. Not bad.”

Isa finished and got off the motorcycle.

Osborne couldn’t help but roll his eyes. “I won’t pay you if you still don’t come.”

Isa glared at him.

She looked at Paul and Pamela and said, “Go ahead. Which one do you want me to deal with?”

Osborne pointed at Pamela. “That woman is yours. I’ll take care of him myself.”

At that, Paul sneered, “I didn’t think you had help. But she’s just a petite woman. What can she change?”

Pamela didn’t say anything but had the same look of disdain on her face.

Isa smiled, “I didn’t expect me to be underestimated.”

With that, Isa looked at Osborne and said, “You must get close when you fight an elemental metahuman. Their bodies are brittle like glass. Get as close as you can, and they’re screwed.”

Isa stopped smiling after that.

She then crouched down and then stomped on the ground. Her body then shot up into the sky like a cannonball, rushing towards Pamela, who was one hundred yards away, at a terrifying speed.

Pamela, however, didn’t panic and quickly waved her hand.

“You want to get close to me? Stupid. Phoenix Tornado, strangle her.”

Instantly, tornadoes appeared in front of Isa, preventing Isa from getting close. The tornadoes were like blades, making it difficult for her to get close.

But Isa acted as if she didn’t see them.

She clenched her fists and threw a punch toward the Phoenix Tornado, which was blocking her way.

Boom!

In an instant, all the tornadoes were blown away by her as if they had never existed.

“What?”

Pamela was horrified to see this.

Osborne was also incredibly shocked. He knew very well how terrifying Pamela’s tornado was. It would be difficult for him to break free on his own, at least for now.

But Isa broke it up with a single punch.

Isa didn’t slow down and was in front of Pamela in an instant.

“A wind-controlling metahuman at Class C+. You’ll come back to the Institute of Human Research with me. I’ll study your body.”

With that, Isa threw another punch.

Pamela looked incredibly grave. She gathered several tornadoes in front of her body, trying to fend off Isa. However, they were still shattered by Isa’s punch.

Isa easily punched Pamela in the chest.

Pamela spat blood and flew backward, passing out straight away.

Isa turned to look at Osborne and smiled, “See? I told you elemental metahuman’s body is brittle as glass.”

She picked up Pamela’s body.

Osborne was dumbfounded.

So were Clarissa and Paul.

No one expected Pamela, the mighty Class C+ elemental metahuman, to be knocked out by a single move from Isa.

“It’s impossible.” Paul was terrified. Looking at Isa, he trembled violently.

Isa, however, ignored him.

“Osborne, I’ll go back first. Settle the matter here on your own.

“By the way, do remember to pay me on the final payment.”

Osborne nodded. “I will.”

With that, Osborne looked at Paul with a sneer. “Paul, now you’re doomed.”
