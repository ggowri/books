Osborne stared in shock at the pristine white orb cradled in Mila's tiny hands.

She held it out toward him, her expression earnest.

"You saved me from the Deschanel Clan, so I want you to have this!"

Osborne hesitated, a flicker of doubt crossing his mind.

"This gem looks anything but ordinary. Is she really just giving it to me? Doesn't that seem a bit reckless?"

"What does it do?" Osborne asked cautiously, trying to gauge its significance.

Mila tilted her head, a look of confusion washing over her face. "Oh, right! What does it do? I think... I might have forgotten? All I remember is that my mom and dad called it a Sanctuary Gem, but the details are all fuzzy."

She gave her head a little shake, a pained expression crossing her features.

"But if I can't remember, it's not a big deal. It's the only thing I have left, and I don't have anything else to thank you for.

"So, I guess you'll have to figure out what it does on your own."

With that, she tossed the gem toward Osborne casually.

He caught it, but to his surprise, it felt incredibly light as if it had no weight at all.

The moment eased his apprehension; after all, carrying something that weighed over a million pounds would be a burden for anyone.

Osborne examined the gem briefly, finding nothing amiss or particularly remarkable about it. He decided to pocket it for now, planning to investigate its properties later.

He turned his attention back to Mila. "Now that you're free, what are you going to do next?"

Mila's brow furrowed deeply.

"I don't know! So much is missing from my memory. It feels like I don't have any family left! I want to find my mom and dad, but I can't even remember who they are! I also want to find Ms. Divine Shadow, but where could she be?"

"Ms. Divine Shadow?" Osborne murmured, intrigued.

It seemed like Mila's memory was incomplete—though it was hard to tell if she was faking it or not. Still, the name "Ms. Divine Shadow" that slipped from her lips caught Osborne's attention.

As far-fetched as his own thoughts seemed, he decided to probe a little further.

"Is she a person?" Osborne asked, gazing at her.

Mila's eyes widened in surprise.

"Huh? How did you know Ms. Divine Shadow isn't human? She's a divine beast! A beautiful tiger! Have you seen Ms. Divine Shadow before?"

Her hopeful gaze bore into him, and Osborne felt a pang of guilt for what he was about to say.

"Honestly, if the Divine Shadow you're talking about is a tiger, then I haven't seen her. The only creature I've encountered named Divine Shadow was a cat..."

As the words left his mouth, he realized he might have been mistaken.

Osborne thought to himself, "The mysterious cat at the Augustine Estate—Divine Shadow—how could it possibly be related to Mila?

She's from the Age of Gods!

At her young age, she possesses the power of a Celestial Deity and a Level Six Divine Body. Even in the Age of Gods, her status must be incredibly high.

Moreover, she mentioned that her parents predicted Lord Malevolence's invasion before it even happened.

They dared to confront him to change the course of the future!

That means Mila's parents must be formidable figures—perhaps even Deity King warriors!

So how could that lazy cat at the Augustine Estate be the same as the Ms. Divine Shadow she's talking about?"

"Ah!" Mila suddenly gasped, her excitement palpable as her cheeks flushed bright red.

She grabbed Osborne's arm tightly, her eyes sparkling.

"Mister, how do you know that Ms. Divine Shadow likes to shrink her form down to look like a cat?

"You've seen her, haven't you?

"Where is she? She's still alive?

"Please, take me to Ms. Divine Shadow! I miss her so much!"

Mila's enthusiasm was overwhelming, her grip firm on his arm. Even if she hadn't regained her full strength, she was still an Intermediate Deity.

And that was saying something since she had a Level Six Divine Body!

She might look like a little girl, but her power wasn't far off from Osborne's.

As Mila's grip tightened, he sucked in a sharp breath, bracing against the pain shooting through his arm.

At the same time, his mind raced with shock.

"Could that mysterious cat from the Augustine Estate—Divine Shadow—really be the Ms. Divine Shadow she's talking about?

Either way, if I can get Mila to meet this Divine Shadow, everything will fall into place!"

Osborne quickly replied, "Yes, I've seen a cat named Divine Shadow. But I can't be certain it's your Ms. Divine Shadow! I can take you to meet her, but you need to let go of my arm. If you don't, my arm might just get mangled!"

As he finished speaking, Mila's face flushed even more, a look of guilt passing over her features.

"I'm so sorry! I didn't mean to!

"But... your arm seems fine!

"You have a Waste Divine Body, don't you? How can you be this strong?"

Mila gazed at him with awe, her eyes wide with disbelief.

Not wanting to get into the specifics, Osborne waved it off. "Let's go! I'll take you to see Divine Shadow right now!"
