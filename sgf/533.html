"Mr. John Augustine!"

"Are you alright, Mr. John Augustine?"

A group of people from the Clifford Augustine family rushed to John's side, their voices filled with concern.

John slowly regained consciousness.

His body ached all over, and it took him a moment to fully grasp the reality of his defeat—he had lost utterly, failing to even touch Osborne.

His face darkened with frustration and anger.

John glared at Osborne, his eyes burning with resentment.

He had been overly confident in his Satan's Eye's Illusion Projection, believing it would surely disorient Osborne. Because of this, he hadn't bothered to dodge Osborne's attack.

If he had, he was certain the outcome would have been different.

John still had many tricks up his sleeve that he hadn't had a chance to use, so he wasn't ready to accept this defeat.

"I refuse to accept this! I want a rematch—I wasn't ready just now!" John demanded angrily.

Osborne gave him a sidelong glance and replied coolly, "A rematch? That can be arranged, but if you lose again, you'll owe me another ten percent of your resources."

John's expression hardened abruptly.

He had already lost ten percent of the Clifford branch bloodline's resources. Another loss would be a significant blow. Even as an heir to the Clifford branch bloodline, explaining this to his family would be challenging.

Yet, the thought of not fighting again left him restless.

"John, just sit this one out. Let me handle him. Even if you want to fight again, you'll have to wait until I'm done," Henry said, his face set in a stern expression as he approached.

John considered this and, after a moment, huffed in resignation.

"Henry, be careful. He's tricky. My Illusion Projection didn't affect him at all—his mental defenses are exceptionally strong."

Henry simply smiled, his gaze fixed intently on Osborne.

"Don't worry. I'll be careful. And Osborne, I'm not John. I won't be careless. You won't find any chance to win against me!"

With that, a powerful aura emanated from Henry.

Another Class A+ fighter!

Henry's power felt even denser than John's, though he still fell short of Class AA.

As long as Henry wasn't Class AA, Osborne remained unruffled.

Calmly, Osborne remarked, "Is that so? I think fighting you might be even easier than fighting him."

Osborne then casually retrieved an energy pistol from his Dimensional Space.

He twirled it smoothly in his hand, a mocking smile on his face as he regarded Henry.

Henry's face turned icy. "Osborne, are you out of your mind? Do you really think a toy like that will have any effect on someone at our level? Are you seriously planning to use that pathetic water gun against me?"

Osborne's smile remained cool and unfazed. "Water gun? Just so you know, it's not a water gun but a highly effective energy weapon. It should be more than enough to take you down.

"Don't say I didn't warn you if you let your guard down and end up getting shot."

"Ridiculous!" Henry's face flushed with anger.

He saw Osborne's words not as a warning but an insult.

"You're clearly too arrogant. Let me teach you a lesson!" Henry roared, activating his Satan's Eye.

Five white star points gleamed, signaling that his Satan's Eye had reached level five.

Ocular energy flickered around him, and Osborne felt a light mind control pressure on him. However, it didn't seem particularly strong.

In the next moment, orange level-two Devil's Light appeared around Henry, forming a protective shield.

Henry's fingers moved swiftly. It seemed that he was preparing some sort of devastating technique.

Five seconds had ticked by, and Henry's energy was raging uncontrollably, yet his ultimate move was still unprepared, his attack still on hold.

Osborne's patience was wearing thin.

"What a waste of time!"

With that, Osborne raised his energy pistol, a pale yellow energy surging into it. He aimed directly at Henry's thigh and pulled the trigger.

Henry didn't even flinch.

He smirked. "A laughable toy like this? You think it can hurt me? Wait until I unleash my..."

He didn't get to finish his sentence before a blood-curdling scream tore from his throat. Osborne's energy pistol had effortlessly pierced his level two Devil's Light defense, the shot puncturing his thigh.

A gaping blood hole appeared on his leg, blood spurting out in torrents.

Osborne didn't hesitate; he fired four more shots, each one targeting a different limb of Henry's.

In an instant, all four limbs were riddled with holes, blood gushing everywhere. The power Henry had been building for so long shattered, and he was struck by a sudden backlash. His face went ashen, blood trickling from his mouth, clearly suffering from the recoil.

With a thunderous crash, Henry collapsed, his eyes wide with shock and frustration.

He couldn't fathom how a mere pistol could shatter his Devil's Light defense and penetrate his body.

It made no sense!

But no matter how unwilling he was, he had lost.

Osborne was already standing over him, his energy pistol pressed against Henry's head.

With a smile, Osborne said, "Henry, you've lost.

"Time to face the music.

"From now on, one-tenth of the resources from both the Narciso's Augustine family and the Clifford's Augustine family will be redirected to the Dampston's Augustine family.

"That's the price you pay for overstepping your bounds."
