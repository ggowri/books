"Screw it, let's take these beasts down!" the eldest man, probably in his forties, growled through gritted teeth.

His eyes blazed with fierce determination. He was prepared to make these monsters pay dearly even at the cost of his own life.

The others, however, were less resolute.

They were all relatively young, the youngest being around twenty-two or twenty-three. Had it been just three months ago, a twenty-something Class A Master would've been hailed as a prodigy and would've commanded respect in Rosmandi at the very least.

Even in superpower factions, they would've been seen as future pillars of strength.

But times had changed. The surge in star power had infused the world with unprecedented energy, accelerating growth even without formal training.

Nowadays, Class A warriors in their twenties were popping up in Ancient Families and even ordinary elite families.

For the super factions, only Class AAA warriors were considered elite, and only those reaching Class S were pillars.

These three, fresh off their Class A breakthrough and not from any super faction, had little battle experience. Now, confronted with nine ferocious Class A Dark Exotic Beasts, they were paralyzed with fear.

Their bodies tremble, faces drenched in cold sweat.

In their panic, they could barely muster 30% of their true strength.

If it weren't for the elder man shielding them in front, they'd likely have collapsed by now. These beasts would've been upon them without resistance, ending in a gruesome feast.

The elder man, seeing their dire situation, burst out the cry in hopes of igniting their fighting spirit. But the effect was minimal.

Now, the nine Dark Exotic Beasts moved in with precision. They seemed more cunning predators than mere animals driven by instinct, coordinated and cautious, forming an attack formation.

The older man's hope dimmed.

Meanwhile, Osborne and Anastasia and Zoe had arrived just a few hundred yards away.

They kept their presence concealed, unnoticed by both the beasts and the besieged four.

Anastasia broke the silence, her voice tense. "Should... should we help them?

"These big tigers are just gross! They're nothing like I imagined. I hate how they make me feel."

Her brows furrowed tighter.

Osborne arched a brow. "We'll rescue them, but not yet. These fools underestimated the danger, venturing deep into the Lostridge Mountains, which was swarmed with Beast Tide, with merely Class A strength.

"They need a harsh lesson first."

Anastasia and Zoe nodded in agreement, ready to act.

At this moment, the beasts made their move.

They avoided the older man, the only real threat, and zeroed in on the trio.

Their massive bat-like wings flared, propelling them with explosive speed.

The foul odor of their breath clouded the air, sharp claws and fangs gleaming as they dove, set to tear through the unarmed trio.

"Ah!"

The three youngsters shrieked, eyes squeezed shut in terror, lashing out wildly.

Such frantic strikes, however, were no match for the Dark Exotic Beasts. These creatures boasted forms of immense strength and speed, effortlessly dodging the attacks and closing in.

The middle-aged man's face paled.

"Watch out!" he bellowed, desperate to rush to their aid.

But three beasts had blocked him from reaching them.

A thunderous crash echoed as all three were toppled in an instant.

Against the colossal beasts—resembling elephants—their forms seemed pitifully small.

Claws slashed, and they were wounded, but their Class A resilience held, preventing a gory disembowelment.

Yet their end was nigh.

With heavy paws, the beasts pinned them to the ground.

Those foul, monstrous jaws snapped shut, intent on devouring them.

"Ah!" Panic-stricken squeals erupted again.

With the beasts looming for the kill, they wet themselves in terror.

"Zoe, it's time to act!"

Osborne knew they couldn't delay any longer. These fellows had clearly learned their lesson.

Zoe sprang into motion.

Her lips parted, emitting three melodious syllables.

And the Dark Exotic Beasts within several hundred yards froze suddenly in place, with prey in reach.
