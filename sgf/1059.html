"Mr. Wesson Augustine, the Aberrants' forces have entered the territory of Newport City! They're closing in fast on the Augustine Estate!"

Upon receiving the news, Wesson shot up from his seat, his expression filled with seriousness.

"It’s time! Keep scouting!

"Get everyone ready! The battle is about to begin! Anyone below Class A needs to take refuge in Challenge Peak! It's heavily fortified and can protect you!

"Battle formations, get ready!"

As Wesson gave orders, a swarm of drones gathered overhead, monitoring every movement around the Augustine Estate, locking onto anyone who dared to approach.

It wasn't long before a throng of several hundred figures appeared at the entrance of the Augustine Estate. They all dressed in uniform garments, their faces masked by blood-red fabric, leaving only their cold, lethal eyes exposed.

The air around them pulsed with a palpable, overwhelming power.

Among them, hardly anyone was classified as Class A; the weakest were Class AA.

From a vantage point, Wesson stood with the leaders of several major factions, their faces grim as they looked at the approaching Aberrants.

"Why are they masking their faces?" Greyson from the Lambert family couldn't hold back his curiosity.

Wesson shook his head.

"I'm not sure! Our intel suggests that there are 3,000 of these masks in total. Most of them are Class AAA, with a few Class AA thrown in. They don't talk—they just kill! This is just a small part of them."

Just as he spoke, another wave of masked figures arrived, joining those already at the entrance of the Augustine Estate.

They didn't launch an attack—just stood there, fixated on the Augustine Estate.

Their numbers continued to add up from 1,000 to 3,000.

Before long, all those 3,000 masked figures were gathered at the entrance, their eerie silence weighing heavily on the atmosphere, as if they didn’t know how to speak.

The tension was palpable, and those inside the Augustine Estate could hardly breathe.

If Osborne were present, he would recognize that all 3,000 of these individuals were Gerardo's Human Slaves!

They masked because they couldn’t expose their true identities.

These Human Slaves were a force to be reckoned with!

Like Osborne, they had been thrust into Human Slave incubators, absorbing energy from the cultivation fluid to enhance their physical strength.

Meanwhile, their bodies and minds had been enslaved, now solely loyal to Gerardo, only responsive to his commands.

As 3,000 Human Slaves stood gathered, their immense presence was terrifying!

And this was just a fraction of the Aberrants' strength.

Soon enough, more forces arrived, this time a squad of identical figures.

They were clones, including Medusas, Werewolves, Vampires, Taurens, Titans, and so on.

The ranks of clones seemed endless as they converged at the entrance of the Augustine Estate.

Each of them hailed Class A and higher, maintaining the same eerie silence as they coldly looked at the Estate.

Their numbers were skyrocketing.

Eventually, their numbers swelled to 40,000 strong and stopped increasing.

Still, the faces of those inside the Augustine Estate darkened, anxiety settling in.

Just the sheer number and strength of these clones were enough to rival their four battle formations! They were only lacking in Class S warriors. But the Aberrants had plenty of those!

As they braced themselves for the storm ahead, the weight of the encroaching threat loomed larger than ever.

In a matter of moments, Class S clones began to emerge, one after another.

The numbers of Class S clones were skyrocketing.

It was like watching a snowball roll down a hill—growing bigger and faster with each moment.

Before long, the count of Class S clones skyrocketed to 500—but it didn’t stop, as more began to materialize.

Finally, the tally of Class S clones reached 800, and then, at last, it came to a halt.

Wesson, Greyson, and the others couldn't suppress their gasps as they stared at the daunting assembly of 800 Class S clones. Fear and despair flickered in their eyes.

800 of them!

They only had 50 Class S fighters.

How could they possibly stand a chance?

At that moment, even Wesson felt desperate. Gripping Godfather's scepter tightly, he could feel his hand quiver.

He thought, "The former Godfather is severely injured and still in a coma!

The new Godfather is still breaking through. Who knows when he'll be back!

Emma's strength isn't enough to unleash the potential of the scepter! That's why she handed it over to me, to take charge!

But even with Godfather's scepter, how on earth can we fend off 800 Class S clones?

It's like fate has turned against the Augustine family, against the Draco Group!"

Wesson clenched his jaw, feeling his resolve starting to crumble when he gazed out at the ever-growing might of the Aberrants' forces.

Fortunately, the Aberrants didn't seem in a hurry to attack. They remained stationary, poised outside the gates of the Augustine Estate. Looked like they were waiting for someone.

And who they were waiting for was none other than Gerardo.
