Lillian looked at Osborne with a face full of scorn. "Lachlan, stop pretending! You think you can bluff your way into being a big client and threaten us with complaints? Do you really think you qualify as a big client? Every time you came here to buy slaves, you haggled for ages and only picked the worst ones.

"If someone like you could ever be a big client, our slave market would have gone under ages ago!

"I remember you couldn't even afford a decent carriage!"

As Lillian finished her taunt, Yannick, the member of the Rainbow Viper Clan beside her, chuckled, clearly relieved.

"So, Lachlan, are you going to apologize or what?

"Do you want me to make a move against you?"

Yannick took a step toward Osborne, his eyes filled with menace. As a Transcendent Deity warrior, he was at a similar level as Lachlan, and while he wasn't entirely sure he could take Osborne in a fight, he felt confident in his territory.

After all, this was the slave market, his domain, and Yannick doubted that a lowly member of the Human Community would dare to stir up trouble here.

Plus, he had a dozen backup enforcers behind him.

With a threatening glare, he continued, "If you don't apologize to Lillian and get out of here, I won't hesitate to make my move. You'll have to deal with the consequences."

Osborne narrowed his eyes at Yannick's words. "Go ahead, give it a shot."

If it had been Lachlan standing there, he would have likely caved under the pressure, maybe even offered up some Essence Crystals to appease Yannick.

The Swift family, while powerful in Wild Flame City, couldn't compare to the Rainbow Viper Clan, let alone the true masters of the slave market—the Demonic Dragon Clan and the Soaring Lion Clan.

Those were forces the Swift family couldn't hope to challenge.

Even as a member of the core leadership of the Swift family, Lachlan had no choice but to submit under such circumstances.

But standing there was Osborne.

From the get-go, he had resolved not to let anyone humiliate him. Osborne wasn't there to be disrespected. No matter who it was, if they tried to bully him, he'd fight back.

If it came down to it, he could always start fresh with a new identity.

After all, he possessed the Level Six peak space elemental essence, and if he wanted to escape, even a Deity Overlord warrior wouldn't be able to stop him.

With that in mind, how could Osborne possibly back down?

He mused, "You want me to apologize?

Dream on!

You think you can hit me?

Bring it on!"

His eyes were cold, filled with defiance and even a hint of provocation.

Lillian was taken aback.

She thought, "How can Lachlan be so bold?

He's just a Human Community member!

I've never seen anyone from the younger generation here in Wild Flame City stand up to a warrior from another race like this, let alone provoke them.

Has Lachlan lost his mind?"

She glanced at Yannick, who wore an expression of fluctuating anger.

Yannick had never encountered a Human Community member who dared to speak to him this way, and it infuriated him.

"You pathetic human scum, you've really crossed the line! Since you want to play tough, I'll personally make you pay. I'll break your bones and make you submit!"

With that, Yannick lunged at Osborne.

He unleashed his formidable divine power in a single, powerful punch aimed straight at Osborne's head.

But in the blink of an eye, Yannick's massive form was sent flying backward, his chest caving in as he crashed to the ground, blood spilling from his mouth in a violent torrent.

Silence fell.

An overwhelming hush enveloped the scene, leaving everyone in stunned disbelief.

Lillian stared at Osborne in shock, slowly retracting her foot and swallowing hard.

"Lachlan actually dared to make a move!" she thought, incredulous.

Not only had "Lachlan" struck first, but he had also kicked Yannick, sending Yannick flying with a single blow.

One kick, and he had severely injured Yannick!

She mused, "The guy is a Transcendent Deity warrior, for crying out loud!"

"Damn it!"

At that moment, Yannick's entourage finally snapped to attention, their faces twisted in anger as they glared at Osborne. In unison, they charged at him.

"You're asking for trouble by causing a scene in the slave market!

"Apologize right now! Go get him!"

A dozen warriors surged forward, a mix of Transcendent Deities and Superior Deities, their various attacks converging on Osborne like a storm.

But in the blink of an eye, Osborne burst through the onslaught, completely unscathed, not even a wrinkle in his clothes.

He laughed coldly, and then it was his turn to strike back.

Screams erupted as chaos ensued.

Lillian could barely keep up with the whirlwind of movement.

In mere moments, Osborne had plunged into the crowd, moving like a tiger among terrified rabbits. Within just a few breaths, everyone around him was down on the ground, writhing in pain and crying out in anguish.

There stood Osborne, untouched, his expression as calm as ever.

Lillian was dumbfounded.

Just then, Osborne turned his gaze to her, a mocking smile appearing on his face. "Is this what you think gives you the upper hand? Pathetic!"

Before she could react, he was suddenly in front of her, gripping her neck tightly.

"Stop!"
