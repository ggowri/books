"You... how dare you!"

Armstrong struggled to speak, his voice trembling as he looked down in disbelief at the massive hole in his chest.

Unlike Gerardo, he didn't possess such formidable regenerative abilities; Osborne's punch had obliterated his heart and shattered his insides.

This kind of injury was more than enough to be fatal.

Blood began to seep from the corners of Armstrong's mouth, a steady stream he couldn't seem to stop. Regret and hatred twisted his features.

He shouted, "My father won't let this slide!

"The Thornberry family won't forget this!

"The League of Divine Descent will come for you!

"I'll be waiting for you in hell! And Gerardo, you dared to betray me, I swear..."

Armstrong's voice broke as he coughed up a mouthful of blood, his body collapsing to the ground with a heavy thud.

He was dead.

Osborne's cold gaze lingered on the lifeless body of the top-tier Class S warrior. He didn't take Armstrong's threats seriously.

He thought, "The Thornberry family won't let this go?

The League of Divine Descent won't forget me?

Fine by me! I wasn't planning on letting them off the hook anyway. Helios? The so-called strongest on Blue Planet?

They all deserve to die!

Anyone involved in the siege against the Augustine family will pay the price.

And as for Gerardo, he will die in the most painful way possible. No one will save him!"

But Osborne hadn't chased Gerardo just yet.

First, he knew he couldn't catch Gerardo. Gerardo's mastery of spatial power was still superior; with every interdimensional travel, he could cover at least 500 miles. By now, Gerardo was probably back in his stronghold.

And within that stronghold lay a multitude of powerful warriors. Even if Osborne managed to catch up, he might not be able to take Gerardo down alone.

Secondly, there were more pressing matters at hand.

The members of the Augustine family were still under siege!

The Aberrants had over three hundred Class S clones and thousands of ordinary clones relentlessly hunting down the Augustine family.

Simultaneously, Class S warriors from the League of Divine Descent and the Eityreia Organization were on a mad killing spree.

Osborne had to act fast to save them.

He could always deal with Gerardo later; the members of the Augustine family, if killed, wouldn't get a second chance.

With a frosty expression, Osborne muttered, "Gerardo, consider yourself lucky. I'll let you live a little longer. Once I sort this out, I'll pay you a visit and send you straight to hell!"

With that, he sprang into action.

Finally, he had a moment to send a message to Wesson. While waiting for a response, he quickly absorbed the residual blood energy in the area.

The blood energy was astonishingly dense!

It surpassed anything Osborne had ever encountered before. Not long ago, this very spot had been a battlefield for nearly 100 thousand Class A and above warriors.

In the end, fewer than 50 thousand survived.

At least six hundred Class S warriors had perished, most of them Aberrants' clones, but several normal Class S warriors had also fallen.

And now, with Armstrong—a top-tier Class S—dead at his hands, the blood energy here was overwhelming!

Fortunately, after breaking through to Class S, the Blood Cross' capacity to absorb blood energy had increased significantly. In the past, Osborne would have struggled to absorb even a fraction of this energy; most would have gone to waste.

But now, he could easily take it all in!

With his Class S status, the barriers that had once held back the Blood Demon members from reaching this level had finally shattered.

He thought, "With enough blood energy, they can all ascend! With this much blood energy concentrated here, it's enough for at least a hundred members of the Blood Demon Vanguard to make the leap to Class S!

And there's even some leftovers!

We could potentially elevate two hundred Blood Demon members to Class S!"

But as Osborne glanced at his Draco Star Wristlet, his heart sank. Wesson still hadn't replied.

His expression darkened.

"I hope nothing's gone wrong..."

Osborne speculated that Wesson was likely engaged in a fight, which was why he hadn't responded. Without wasting another moment, he began sending messages to everyone he knew, checking in on their locations.

He couldn't afford to wait around; Osborne had to follow the trail of the fleeing Augustine family. Activating Satan's Eye, he set off in pursuit, scanning for any signs of their passage.

Minutes ticked by, and despite his efforts, he hadn't found any trace of the Augustine family. Just as frustration began to creep in, his Draco Star Wristlet vibrated.

He glanced down, and his face paled.

"Damn the Eityreia Organization! You're all going to pay for this! Not a single one of you will leave here alive today!"

With a chilling resolve, he ripped open a dimensional rift.

Yulissa had provided him with a coordinate just 60 miles away. In a heartbeat, he executed his interdimensional travel, arriving almost instantaneously at the new location.
