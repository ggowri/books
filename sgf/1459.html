Osborne had made up his mind.

For him, Essence Crystals were a mere trifle; twelve large Essence Crystal veins alone lay in the East Zone, not to mention the numerous smaller and medium-sized veins controlled by the various factions.

Should Osborne so desire, he could pay those veins a visit at any time.

With his prowess, the number of Essence Crystals he could obtain was virtually limitless.

The only matter he needed to ponder was what to do with these precious resources to achieve the greatest impact.

At present, rescuing the enslaved members of the Human Community was his imminent task.

Although Osborne was aware that purchasing more slaves might inadvertently signal a demand, encouraging the slave market to further prey on humans, he could not afford to think too far ahead.

For now, all he aimed to do was to prevent as many enslaved humans as possible from falling into the hands of other powerful factions and from being subjected to torment.

As for shutting down the slave market and ensuring that the other clans would never dare to capture humans as slaves again, those were matters he would consider in the future.

Tiana murmured, "The slave auction begins in five minutes."

Osborne nodded and surveyed the other attendees.

Each appeared formidable, hailing from the major factions—only they could afford to amass a significant number of slaves, especially at this top-tier auction, where each slave was a gem and the prices exorbitant.

Ordinary slaves could be bought for as little as 10 thousand or even just a few thousand Essence Crystals, but they were often weak in strength and mediocre in aptitude.

The real prized slaves, however, were either extraordinarily powerful, exceptionally talented, or stunningly beautiful.

These special attributes ensured they would grace the auction block with a starting price of no less than 50 thousand Essence Crystals.

Moreover, they were frequently sold in groups.

"Time's up!"

At last, an auctioneer appeared on the main auction stage, smiling as he bowed to the crowd.

"Welcome, esteemed guests, to the Wild Flame City slave market for this most prestigious slave auction. I won't waste your valuable time with trivialities; our auction begins now!

"I assure you, every guest present today will leave thoroughly satisfied! For this batch of slaves up for auction is nothing short of exceptional!

"Behold, the first lot!"

With that, a group of twenty slaves—all beautiful women—appeared on the stage.

They were clad in revealing attire, their limbs bound with special divine artifacts, faces etched with despair.

Their arrival instantly electrified the audience, their eyes blazing with fervor as they ogled the twenty stunning female slaves.

The auctioneer smiled as he introduced, "The first batch of slaves consists entirely of female Human Community members, handpicked for their exceptional physique, appearance, talent, and strength. These aren't mere decorative pieces; each boasts at least Intermediate Deity-level power!

"Purchasing them isn't just about utility but also about indulgence—having them around not only serves your needs but also pleases the eye.

"Such quality among Human Community female slaves is rare, let alone in a group of twenty.

"The starting bid is one million low-grade Essence Crystals, with each subsequent bid no less than 50 thousand. The auction—begins now!"

As soon as the auctioneer finished, eager hands shot up.

"These twenty exquisite Human Community female slaves resonate perfectly with the aesthetic of the Aether Hawks Clan, so the Anderson family claims them! One million and 50 thousand!"

"Pfft! One million and 50 thousand? Dream on! The Crimson Ironhoof Clan's Earwood family also fancies them—one million and 100 thousand!"

"The Spirit Turtle Clan's Ulysses family bids one million and 200 thousand!"

"Rainbow Viper Clan's Margery family bids one million and 300 thousand!"

"One million and 400 thousand!"

The auction hall erupted into a frenzy as representatives from various factions fiercely competed for these twenty top-tier female slaves from the Human Community.

Their appeal lay not only in their beauty but also in their formidable strength—each boasting at least Intermediate Deity-level power. Acquiring such assets could serve myriad purposes.

The bidding grew increasingly intense.

However, not everyone was captivated by the allure of these twenty elite human slaves.

Differences in aesthetic preferences among the clans meant the majority refrained from participating.

Osborne, too, held back. As he listened to the rising bids and gazed at the twenty terrified, despairing Human Community females on stage, his face darkened.

He understood all too well the fate awaiting these women if they fell into the wrong hands.
