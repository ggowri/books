Osborne lunged forward, his body radiating with a brilliant martial art aura as energy surged in all directions.

Bang!

The drone hovering above Osborne's head exploded instantly.

Seeing this, Osborne paused.

His wounds appeared severe, but in reality, they weren't life-threatening. The dagger hadn't pierced his heart, and his body possessed remarkable healing abilities that would soon restore him to full health.

He had feigned serious injury to create an opportunity to destroy the drone.

With the drone taken out, his next moves would remain unseen.

With this in mind, Osborne gripped the black dagger embedded in his back and violently yanked it out.

Blood spurted out, but Osborne didn't flinch.

His gaze remained cold and fixed on David in the distance.

"David, you're done for!"

With that, Osborne charged towards David.

David's injuries were far more severe than Osborne's.

He had never anticipated that Osborne, in such a dire situation, would detect his assassination attempt. Even more surprising was that Osborne had been biding his time, waiting for David to reveal himself.

Caught off guard by Osborne's attack, David had little time to defend himself.

Fortunately for David, Osborne's backward kick was awkward and lacked full force; otherwise, it would have turned him into a bloody pulp.

But even though David wasn't dead, he was gravely injured, lying on the ground and coughing up blood.

As Osborne approached and held the black dagger with murderous intent, David's face turned pale.

Despite the searing pain, he tried to activate his power, hoping to blend into the darkness once more.

But it was too late.

As David's body began to merge with the shadows, Osborne hurled the black dagger with immense force. It zipped through the air like a bullet, crossing several thirty feet in an instant and burying itself deep in David's throat.

David's eyes widened in shock as his powers were interrupted, leaving him unable to dissolve into the shadows.

Osborne didn't hesitate—he unleashed a powerful punch.

"No!"

David's desperate scream was cut short as Osborne's fist connected with his head, smashing it like a watermelon.

Osborne finally exhaled in relief, but he didn't linger.

He quickly opened a dimensional space and tossed David's lifeless body inside.

Then, he stepped into a dimensional rift.

In the next moment, he reappeared miles away. After several quick jumps, there wasn't another soul within dozens of miles.

Even then, Osborne didn't stop. He activated his earth elemental ability and burrowed into the mountainside, disappearing from sight.

It wasn't until a minute after he vanished that a drone finally arrived, scanning the mountain surface but failing to detect Osborne's presence within.

Osborne activated the Satan's Eye to confirm his surroundings were safe before finally relaxing.

He retrieved David's body from the dimensional space.

"Dark elemental power!"

Osborne's eyes sparkled with excitement. The reason he had been so determined to kill David was twofold: first, because David had tried to assassinate him.

Second, because of David's dark elemental power.

Such power was exceedingly rare, rivaling even spatial power.

Now that he had encountered it, Osborne wasn't about to let the opportunity slip away.

"You wanted to kill me, so now you're the one who's dead. As for your dark elemental power, don't worry—I won't let it go to waste. From now on, it's mine!"

With that, Osborne used the Satan's Eye to begin absorbing the dark elemental essence from David's body.

It was his eighth elemental essence, and the process went smoothly.

Osborne soon felt a deep understanding of the dark element, realizing that he could now blend into the shadows just as David had.

"What a powerful ability!

It's perfect for assassination—almost impossible to defend against! If my Satan's Eye were any weaker, or if it hadn't mutated, I never would have detected David hiding in the darkness."

Osborne was thrilled.

He waved his hand, summoning flames that reduced David's body to ashes, erasing any trace that the man had ever existed.

"I doubt I can keep the fact that I killed David a secret, but as long as I insist it was an accident due to the intensity of his assassination attempt, no one can really fault me.

At worst, I might face some minor punishment.

Considering the value of this dark elemental power, a little slap on the wrist is a small price to pay.

The only downside is that I can't openly use the dark element power for a while, or everyone will know I absorbed David's elemental essence."

With that thought, Osborne felt a tinge of regret.

If he could use the dark element freely, he was confident he could stand out in the upcoming Branch Bloodline Competition.

He checked the time and saw that the next emblem signal was almost due.

Without hesitation, he erased any traces of his presence, retrieved an energy pistol, and emerged from the mountain.

Although he had killed David, eliminating a formidable enemy, the competition was far from over, and he couldn't afford to relax just yet.

Unbeknownst to him, news of David's death had already sparked an uproar within the Augustine family.
