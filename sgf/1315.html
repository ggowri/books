"Terrence, you've lost," Osborne declared, staring icily at the defeated figure sprawled on the ground.

Defeating Terrence in just three moves was surprising even to Osborne, who now felt just how vast the gulf was between them. As a Superior Deity, he surpassed Terrence in every aspect, making the victory seem like an adult overpowering a child.

Terrence was dazed, unable to utter a single word.

It wasn't the defeat that frightened him but the sheer devastation of it all.

Osborne wasn't even a Celestial Deity; he was a Superior Deity, yet he crushed Terrence so thoroughly.

This battle had shattered every ounce of confidence Terrence had painstakingly built over the centuries of his existence.

Osborne shook his head and turned to Divine Shadow.

"Grandma Divine Shadow, you said you wanted to handle him personally. He's all yours now."

Divine Shadow nodded, glancing at the defeated Terrence.

She could empathize with his broken spirit. Had she been in his place, she might have been equally crushed, feeling life wasn't worth living.

Knowing Osborne's overwhelming prowess, she understood Terrence's despair.

Divine Shadow spoke. "Terrence, you were once a formidable leader. Even in death, I'll offer you dignity. End it yourself."

Terrence trembled at her words, finally snapping back to reality.

A wry smile spread across his face.

"End it myself? What dignity is that? If I must die, it will be in battle."

He turned his gaze to Osborne, a resigned calm replacing his earlier rage and defiance.

"I want to die by his hand.

"You called me a formidable leader, and I've faced countless geniuses, but I've never encountered anyone as terrifying and hopelessly brilliant as him.

"If he doesn't fall, he'll become a Deity King at the very least, perhaps even surpassing the likes of the God of Night.

"I believe he could become stronger than any Deity King and even transcend that realm."

Terrence's eyes flickered with envy.

He continued, "This was my youthful ambition, a dream I soon realized was unattainable. No one could achieve it—or so I thought.

"But now, I believe he can.

"So I choose to die by his hand. It's the greatest respect you can offer to me!"

With determination, Terrence struggled to his feet, divine power flaring around him one last time.

"Osborne, come and finish me!"

Divine Shadow was taken aback by Terrence's words but found herself agreeing internally with the sentiment.

She also thought Osborne was destined for greatness.

She urged, "Osborne, grant his wish. Though I yearn to end him myself out of hatred, I believe he should fall by your hand.

"That way, when you become the world's strongest warrior someday, his death will have some honor."

Osborne looked at them both in surprise but nodded.

"Terrence, since you wish to die by my hand, I will oblige.

"Don't fault me for destroying the Deschanel Clan—it was your clan that ignited this conflict.

"In war, there's no right or wrong, only life or death."

With that, Osborne vanished, appearing instantly before Terrence.

Terrence met Osborne's gaze and murmured, "In war, there's no right or wrong. You're right; this is fate."

With those final words, Terrence launched a feeble punch at Osborne.

Weakened by his grave injuries, the attack lacked any real force—a mere Junior Deity could have easily dodged it. Yet, it was Terrence's last effort, his final act of defiance, and he gave it everything he had left.

Osborne, out of respect, chose not to evade.

Terrence's fist made a soft thud against Osborne, and a bittersweet smile formed on his lips.

"Forgive me, my kin of the Deschanel Clan. I'm coming to join you now..."

With those words, he closed his eyes.

At the same moment, Osborne's own fist struck Terrence with decisive force.

Terrence's Divine Body shattered, his soul obliterated, leaving no trace behind.

The once-great patriarch of the Deschanel Clan, a warrior who had reached the pinnacle of Deity Overlord, fell at Osborne's hands. Without a Cosmic Treasure like the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity, there was no hope of revival.

Thus ended the reign of a formidable ruler.

The Deschanel Clan, an illustrious divine clan from the Age of Gods, vanished into the annals of history with Terrence's passing.

Osborne let out a slow breath, an inexplicable heaviness settling on his shoulders.

It was as though an unseen burden weighed on his heart.

"Will my Augustine family meet the same fate one day?" he wondered.
