With Osborne's interference ruining their ambush plan, Vance and his five companions had no choice but to confront Harry and Amelia head-on, and the battle erupted with ferocity from the very start.

Harry was stronger than Vance, but only slightly so. They knew each other well; even with Harry's edge, it would take time to decisively defeat Vance.

In this scenario, Amelia was in grave danger.

As Vance kept Harry occupied, he commanded his remaining five men to focus their attacks on Amelia. They all understood how much Harry cared for his sister; if Amelia was in peril, it would throw Harry into a frenzy.

Capturing Amelia could very well force Harry to surrender.

"Get her!

"You five are all Junior Deities, just like her! Even if her Divine Body is a bit stronger, she can't possibly handle all of you! If you can't even catch one woman, you might as well not be alive—just a waste of space!" Vance shouted, his voice echoing through the mine.

Harry's heart raced with panic. He attacked Vance with reckless abandon, desperate to finish him off and rush to Amelia's aid.

"Amelia! Be careful! Hold on a little longer! I'll take care of Vance and come to save you!"

But Amelia was in no position to respond.

She was surrounded by five opponents, all Junior Deities like herself. Despite her slightly superior Divine Body, she quickly found herself on the defensive, unable to mount any counterattacks.

The situation was dire.

If this continued, she wouldn't last much longer. Soon, she would be overwhelmed and captured, leaving Harry vulnerable.

At the sight of this, Harry's anxiety mounted.

The more frantic he became, the more mistakes he made. He nearly fell victim to Vance's attacks several times while trying to reach Amelia.

"Ha! Harry, you're as good as dead today!"

Vance laughed, relishing the advantage.

He continued to taunt Harry, hoping to drive Harry further into a panic.

And it was working.

Harry repeatedly attempted to ignore Vance and save Amelia, but each time he failed, Vance seized the opportunity to inflict more injuries.

Amelia, too, was starting to show signs of injury.

The odds were stacked heavily against them.

Osborne, hidden behind a nearby rock, watched with growing fury.

Yet, he felt powerless to intervene.

With his current strength, he couldn't take on any of the Junior Deities directly.

All he could do was warn Harry and Amelia to watch out for ambushes.

Strength!

It all came down to strength!

It all came down to strength! Without regaining his power, Osborne was left feeling helpless.

Osborne clenched his jaw, realizing he couldn't wait any longer. He had to find a way to turn the tide.

"If I jump in directly, I won't just fail to help Harry and Amelia—I'll put myself in danger too! If that's the case, I'll have to resort to this."

With determination, Osborne quickly retracted the Eclipsing Mantra and unleashed his blood energy.

The thick blood energy began to spread around him.

For ordinary cultivators, this wouldn't raise any alarms; in the heat of battle, Vance, Harry, and the others were oblivious to the shift.

But for the Deathborn lurking deep within the mine, Osborne's sudden surge of blood energy shone like a beacon.

From a distance, they caught a whiff of the life force emanating from him and wasted no time rushing toward his direction.

They were hungry.

They craved blood and flesh.

Before long, Osborne sensed the presence of several Deathborns approaching from miles away. However, these were just Class S Deathborns; their numbers wouldn't significantly alter the battle involving Harry, Vance, and the others.

"Not good enough!

I need to draw in at least a few Junior Deity Deathborns to really shake things up!" Osborne urged himself, intensifying the flow of blood energy within him.

With each heartbeat, his blood energy surged further, spreading through the air like a vibrant signal.

More and more Deathborns began to catch a whiff of life's essence, their instincts driving them rapidly toward him.

The horde was growing.

But just as Osborne realized something was amiss, it was already too late. A rush of Deathborn surrounded him, their numbers far beyond what he had anticipated.

His brow furrowed in alarm. "Damn it! I summoned too many!

One Intermediate Deity Deathborn, eight Junior Deity Deathborns, and over thirty Class S Deathborns!

They won't wipe out Vance, Harry, and the others, will they?

No. It doesn't matter! If this keeps up, Harry and Amelia won't last! I have to stir things up if there's any hope!"

Determined to act, Osborne activated the Eclipsing Mantra, effectively cloaking all traces of his presence.

In an instant, the Deathborns lost their ability to sense him.

But that was no longer the issue; Harry, Vance, and the others were fully engaged in battle, releasing their own life energy in fierce waves.

The Deathborn, drawn by the living essence radiating from the fighters, charged forward with unrelenting ferocity.
