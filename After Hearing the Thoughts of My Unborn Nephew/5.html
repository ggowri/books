Chapter 5

Fiona watched me floating in the air, her eyes red with rage!

She struggled excitedly, staring intently at the helicopter circling in the sky.

A person came down from the helicopter, untied me, and safely brought me to the edge of the cliff.

The fierce wind howled, kicking up a cloud of dust. As the helicopter departed, the dust gradually settled.

Fiona stared at me, her voice trembling. "How did you survive?"

I chuckled lightly and said, "Did you really think that if I actually died, you could just claim it was because we argued, and I impulsively jumped? With no evidence, the police wouldn't be able to do anything to you?"

"Too bad, I had already set up a safety net below just to catch myself."

"I told you, if you let me go, you'd still have a chance. But you let money blind you, completely bewitched by Oliver."

She struggled in vain, then stopped moving, her eyes fixed on me, filled with shock.

I walked up to her and whispered so only the two of us could hear, "Oliver was right, I really was reborn."

She widened her eyes, her gaze filled with hatred toward me. If the police weren’t holding her, she would’ve strangled me on the spot.

Too bad, she no longer had a chance.

I sneered, "But apart from the lottery thing, everything else he said was wrong.

"In my previous life, I listened to you and persuaded Grace to have an abortion. She did, and then the lottery won. You all blamed me for losing the money. I was slapped dozens of times by you and hacked to death by Grace with a kitchen knife. And you just coldly said I ruined your chance to get rich.

"It was clearly you who made me persuade Grace, yet in the end, I was the only one who died. Fiona, I was so unwilling."

"This time, I won't let you live comfortably."

Fiona finally panicked and screamed, "What are you trying to do?"

I didn't say anything more.

The police took Fiona away.

I hugged my parents, tears of excitement streaming down.

After being reborn, I immediately found a chance to message them, asking them to pay attention to my situation.

They were very alert, immediately reported to the police secretly, and cooperated with the rescue operation.

When Fiona knocked me out and took me to the mountain, the protective net at the foot of the mountain was already in place.

So, when I saw the helicopter flying overhead, I knew I wouldn't die.

As for Fiona, I couldn't really let her spend the rest of her life in prison.

Days like that would be too easy for her.

I want to make her life worse than death.

She was charged with attempted murder, with solid evidence, but not enough for the death penalty. She was ultimately sentenced to life imprisonment.

After Benjamin and Victoria found out about this, they came to me.

Benjamin said, "Liam, I know Fiona did something wrong, but you’re husband and wife. How could a husband send his wife to prison himself? It’s such a joke if people hear about it."

Victoria continued to persuade, "Hurry up and bail her out. If you're this heartless, who would dare marry you in the future? You can't stay single forever, can you?"

"We are your elders, after all. You should listen to us."

"As long as you listen, even if you and Fiona don’t have kids in this lifetime, we won’t mind. We can raise Oliver together."

After they finished speaking, I said, "Got it. Go back and wait for news."

They thought they had convinced me, their expressions softened a lot, and before leaving, they even patted my shoulder politely.

My dad angrily muttered curses at their backs.

My mom looked at me with concern. "Why didn’t you let us speak just now? If you hadn’t stopped him, your dad would’ve thrown a punch."

I patted her hand and noticed it was much rougher than I remembered.

The wrinkles on her face were also increasing.

Thinking about how I was brutally killed in my past life, how heartbroken would they be if they knew?
