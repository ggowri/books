Chapter 4

Seeing Benjamin and Victoria being swayed again, I narrowed my eyes and sneered, "Oliver, you claim to be reborn. How do you know about what happened after the abortion in your past life?"

Oliver is still just a child. Seeing he couldn't outtalk me, he started throwing a tantrum and shouting, "You must die! If you don't die, I'll die in the womb!"

Grace panicked, gently stroking her belly. "You can't have anything happen to you. Of course, I'll listen to you, but Liam is right too. If we end up with a murder case, your future won't be easy."

Oliver snorted coldly, "Then take him to a deserted place and kill him, or push him off a cliff and say he accidentally fell while playing. Either way, he must die."

I shouted angrily, "Why do you have to kill me? Do you feel better only if the whole family carries a murder case?"

"Do you even care about your family?"

Oliver just kept insisting that everyone kill me, or he wouldn't live.

Grace looked at me awkwardly. "Sorry, my son said you must die. He's the lucky star, so please just endure it and jump off the cliff."

I laughed in anger. "Just because he won you a $500,000 lottery, you believe him unconditionally?"

"Even if you erase the traces of killing me, flaws will eventually show!"

Oliver suddenly dropped a bombshell, "Aunt Fiona, you bought insurance for Liam back then, right?"

One sentence changed the expression on everyone's face.

Especially Fiona, she stared at me intently.

The accidental insurance coverage is three million dollars.

Fiona pressed me step by step, saying, "Don't blame me for being ruthless, blame your bad luck."

I turned and ran.

I had just rushed out the door when I suddenly tripped!

Benjamin tripped me with his leg, and I fell to the ground, hitting the back of my head hard.

Everything went black before my eyes, and I completely lost consciousness.

I woke up from the pain.

I opened my eyes and found Fiona dragging me up the mountain.

My clothes were torn full of holes, and my exposed skin was scraped, oozing blood.

I gritted my teeth and struggled. "Fiona, you'd better let me go, or you won't have a chance later."

Fiona frowned, and seeing me resisting, she casually picked up a stick and hit me on the head.

A searing pain tore through my scalp, my vision darkened, and the sting spread through my body like wildfire.

I don't know how long it took, but we finally reached the mountaintop.

This place is remote, almost no one comes here.

It seems they chose it carefully.

My eyes were red as I stared at her intently. "Fiona, this is your last chance. If you really push me down, no one will be able to save you."

She said nothing, just kept a cold face, dragged me to the edge of the cliff, and suddenly pushed me off!

I shouted, "Fiona, you're finished!"

Fiona stood at the edge of the cliff, looking at me coldly.

The next second, the police pinned her down at the edge of the cliff.

My parents leaned over the edge of the cliff and shouted, "Liam!"

Seeing this, I laughed.
