Chapter 1

My name is Liam Hayes. After my wife Fiona Harrison's sister, Grace Harrison, became pregnant with her son Oliver, who has supermale syndrome, the whole family could hear his thoughts.

Oliver said, "The doctor is just jealous that I'm a prodigy, that's why they advised you to abort! You absolutely can't abort me!"

"I'm a genius, I can predict lottery numbers and help you win big!"

After I earnestly explained the dangers of supermale syndrome, Grace finally had an abortion.

But two days later, the lottery numbers Oliver predicted actually won.

The whole family instantly turned against me, angrily accusing me of being selfish and jealous that Grace was carrying a genius son.

Grace completely lost her mind and grabbed a kitchen knife to kill me.

When I opened my eyes again, I was reborn on the day Grace was found to be carrying a fetus with supermale syndrome.

This time, I decided not to interfere.

"Mom, you can't abort me, I'm a prodigy.

"I can predict lottery winning numbers. If you don't believe me, buy a ticket first. If it wins in two days, you'll know if it's true."

Hearing Oliver's inner voice, the whole family was stunned.

Grace hesitated for a moment and cautiously looked at her parents, Benjamin Harrison and Victoria Harrison. "Mom, Dad, did you hear that?"

The two nodded stiffly.

Victoria looked conflicted and turned to me. "Liam, do you have any suggestions?"

I gently smiled at her. "You're the elder, it's up to you."

In my previous life, it was Benjamin and Victoria who asked me to persuade Grace to have an abortion.

As Grace's biological parents, they found it inconvenient to say such things, but I, as an outsider working in a hospital, knew more.

But after winning the lottery, they all blamed me.

Benjamin and Victoria accused me of being jealous of Grace because Fiona and I couldn't have children.

Fiona slapped me dozens of times, leaving my face swollen.

Grace, driven to madness, grabbed a kitchen knife and killed me.

I begged them for help, but all I saw were their cold and disgusted looks.

In this life, I won't do such things again.

Once a woman conceives a fetus with supermale syndrome, her body secretes a special hormone that strongly suppresses any thoughts of terminating the pregnancy.

Grace was already unwilling to have an abortion. After hearing Oliver's words, she said, "Why don't we buy a lottery ticket first and see? What if we win?"

Oliver immediately got excited and said, "Mom, trust me, I'm so happy. You must stay away from Liam, he doesn't mean well."

Grace was half-skeptical, but her gaze toward me became wary. She stepped back two steps, distancing herself from me.

I ignored her, turned around, went back to the room, and started packing, preparing to file for divorce.

Fiona followed me in, grabbed my wrist, and said, "Even though we can hear the child's thoughts, this is too dangerous. You should still persuade Grace to have an abortion."

I calmly opened the suitcase. "What's dangerous?"

She got anxious. "You're a doctor, don't you know? Children with super-male syndrome are highly likely to develop severe violent tendencies as they grow up, even to the point of killing their parents.

"Grace is my sister, are you really going to stand by and do nothing?"

I looked at her anxious face and just found it ridiculous.

In my past life, she made me persuade, but in the end, I bore all the blame alone.

When Grace attacked me with a kitchen knife, I desperately begged them for help.

They only looked on coldly, thinking I cost them the chance to win the jackpot.

With my luggage packed, I picked up the suitcase and walked out.

I said, "I am your husband, but when it comes to Grace, I'm ultimately just an outsider. It's not my place to interfere.

"Besides, didn't Oliver say it himself? He's a prodigy, maybe he really can bring you wealth."

Fiona then noticed the suitcase in my hand, grabbed my wrist, and frowned. "Where are you going?"

Worried she might stop me, I casually said, "My mom said she's not feeling well, so I'm going home to take care of her for a few days."

When Oliver heard I was leaving, his voice turned sharp and piercing. "We can't let Liam go! He'll definitely find someone to force Mom to the hospital for an abortion."

"Quick, lock him up, don't let him leave."

Grace immediately locked the door. "Liam, wait until the lottery results are out in two days. If it's fake, I'll let you go."
