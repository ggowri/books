Chapter 6

I pressed my lips together and said softly, "Don't worry, I know what I'm doing. I won't let myself be wronged."

I don't dare tell them about my rebirth, afraid they'll feel guilty.

Since I've been given a second chance, I will make up for all those regrets.

Hearing me say this, they were worried but still trusted me and didn't ask further.

I started preparing the materials.

Taking advantage of Fiona's imprisonment for her crimes, I decisively filed for divorce in court.

Coming out of the court, looking at the divorce certificate in my hand, I heaved a sigh of relief.

Half a month passed, and Benjamin and Victoria, seeing that Fiona hadn't been bailed out, came to my house to question me.

I casually said, "Bail isn't that simple. If you keep pressuring me, I won't bail her out."

Benjamin and Victoria had no choice. Though unhappy, they didn't dare provoke me.

After settling the ties between Fiona and me, I personally wrote the bail letter, forgiving her actions and agreeing to let her out.

The police took the documents but hesitated to speak.

After all, what Fiona did was very dangerous. If it happens again, they might not be able to save me in time.

I understood what he meant.

But I was ready.

Seeing my firm attitude, the police stopped persuading and took the documents to process the bail.

The moment Fiona saw me, her eyes turned red. "I didn't expect you to love me so much."

"I'll absolutely listen to you from now on, okay? Let's go home."

She endured so much suffering in prison, and seeing me afterward filled her heart with joy.

I took two steps back. "Fiona, we were already divorced when you went to prison. This is the official divorce certificate from the court. I’m here today just to hand it over to you."

"You'll need it if you get married again in the future."

She froze, looking at me in disbelief.

My task was done, and I got in the car and left.

The plane's about to take off. If I miss it, I won't be able to fly with my parents for the trip.

I've been handling the paperwork for going abroad these past two weeks.

As for Fiona, with her abilities, she can't possibly find me.

Why did I let her out? Because staying in prison was too easy for her.

As long as Oliver is alive, their days won't be easy.

I boarded the plane with my parents, completely leaving all the troubles behind.

After Fiona got home, she was still dazed, not fully reacting yet.

Benjamin and Victoria were overjoyed to see her return.

Victoria said, "Looks like we misunderstood Liam. I didn't expect him to actually bail you out."

"Fiona, you must have suffered in prison, right? I'll cook you a good meal right away. By the way, where's Liam? He hasn't been home for half a month, and Grace has lost her appetite. Hurry and get him back to cook; Oliver loves his cooking."

Fiona finally came to her senses and said, "Liam divorced me."

Victoria didn't believe it. "How is that possible? He loves you so much. If you two are divorced, who would marry him?"

"My mom threatened him back then, saying if he didn't bail you out, you'd leave him. He was terrified at the time."

They still think I'm the old Liam, unable to leave Fiona.

However, I didn't know any of this. At that time, I was traveling abroad with my parents.

Grace stroked her flat belly as she walked over and said, "Fiona, it's just a man. Once we have money, we can have as many as we want."

"Here, these are the new winning numbers Oliver just predicted."

Oliver snorted lightly, "That's right, even though I didn't kill him, it's fine if he stays far away and doesn't cause trouble. Mom, you have to take good care of me. I'm a lucky star, and once I'm out, I'll help you make big money."

"By then, you can find as many men as you want."
