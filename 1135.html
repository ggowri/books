"Understood, Godfather!" Matthew responded quickly.

He began to explain the situation regarding the Draco Group's energy guns.

These weapons had been in production for quite some time, but only in small quantities. Most of them were used on the Draco Group's combat drones and robots.

While their power was decent and stronger, but not much, than standard kinetic weapons. They were still only capable of inflicting damage on Class B metahumans at best.

Due to their high production costs, few factions could afford to splurge on energy guns.

Most believed it was more practical to invest in a larger stock of kinetic weapons and equip more personnel, achieving a similar effect.

However, energy guns became a hit three months ago when the Aberrants launched an attack on the Augustine family.

The Blood Demon members, armed with modified energy guns, wreaked havoc, turning the tide of battle.

Thanks to breakthroughs in materials, their power had increased dramatically; even Class A warriors found it hard to withstand their fire. In a coordinated attack, they could take down Class AA and even Class AAA opponents!

The super energy guns posed an even greater threat, capable of challenging Class S warriors.

Word spread fast, and suddenly, energy guns were the talk of the town.

Not long after, when cities faced Beast Tide attacks and sought help from the Augustine family, the Draco Armed Services Branch was dispatched with energy guns to help. Their effectiveness against the Beast Tide was unparalleled, far surpassing that of ordinary kinetic weapons.

Various factions began to take notice and were eager to purchase energy guns.

Sebastian had yet to decide whether to sell these weapons.

He was concerned that if these factions became too powerful, they might one day turn their weapons against the Augustine family and the Draco Group.

As Matthew finished his explanation, Osborne fell deep into thought.

After a moment, he declared, "Energy guns can be sold, but we need to keep the price reasonable. Also, they are not only available to the Ancient Families and superpowers. Even ordinary folks can have access to energy guns!"

Matthew's eyes widened in disbelief.

"Godfather, you want to sell energy guns to regular people? Aren't you worried about the chaos that could cause? Handing weapons to civilians is like giving a baby a loaded gun. There are bound to be unpredictable risks!"

Osborne shook his head seriously.

"Matthew, the world has changed, so has the times; the entire Blue Planet is in turmoil. The Beast Tide threatens human cities and everyone's safety. Some even say the doom day is coming!

"Are you still worried about the risks of giving ordinary people energy guns? They need to defend themselves! They need weapons to protect their homes!"

"Besides, the energy guns we sell can be customized. We can build in hidden functions for remote control, allowing us to limit their firepower whenever necessary.

"If they misuse the guns to create chaos or harm others, we can instantly disable the guns, turning them into useless scrap metal!

"And when they face a Beast Tide attack, we can help them unlock higher power levels to better fend off the beasts and safeguard their communities.

"This isn't just feasible; it's our responsibility! Hidden functions. Not difficult, right?"

Matthew's eyes lit up immediately at Osborne's decision.

"That should be no problem! Leaving a hidden function in the energy guns will ensure they won't turn guns on us someday. Since you've made the call, Godfather, we'll follow this plan!"

Osborne nodded with a smile. "Just remember, the super energy guns are off the table. We don't have enough for ourselves as it is."

"Understood!" Matthew affirmed, though he later frowned.

"Once we start the energy gun sales, we're going to be swamped with orders. With those sales alone, the Draco Group could solidify its position as the top conglomerate in Rosmandi.

"But mass-producing energy guns requires rare metals, and we'll need to send more people to the rare metal mines. And those mines are out in the wilderness, and most of them are now controlled by beasts..."

At this, Osborne's eyes flashed with cold determination.

"Then we'll take them back! I'll have the Blood Demon squad assist you."

Matthew relaxed.

"With the Blood Demons backing us, it'll be a piece of cake. They're our anchor in these turbulent times. But while you were in slumber, no one could command the Blood Demon, not even the former Godfather."

Osborne offered a faint smile but said nothing.

The Blood Demons answered only to him; they were under his control and his alone.

Shortly after, Matthew left.

Finally relaxed, Osborne glanced down at his sister Anastasia, who still clung tightly to his arm, and couldn't help but grin.

He thought, "It's good to have my family safe and together.

But where is Clarissa?"

Osborne had been awake for quite some time now, yet Clarissa hadn't shown up. A growing sense of worry began to rise.

He immediately dialed Clarissa's number.
