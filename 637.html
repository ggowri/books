Osborne gripped the Goddess of Grain's scythe tightly. It was a divine artifact, not particularly powerful, but its standout features were its durability and sharpness. It was terrifyingly sharp.

For Osborne, this razor-sharp scythe was the perfect weapon for assassination. As long as he could get close to an enemy, the Goddess of Grain's scythe would easily slice through their throat.

No enemy could withstand its cutting edge.

"Let's start with you," Osborne whispered as he targeted a lone enemy rummaging through the warehouse, seemingly searching for something.

There were no other enemies within one hundred yards.

Osborne picked the enemy and moved in quickly.

At this moment, his body was just a faint black shadow moving through the darkness. It was impossible to notice him unless one specifically looked for him.

Just like David had once approached Osborne, Osborne's Satan's Eye, at full power, barely detected David's shadow.

His enemy did not have Osborne's keen eyesight. He also never expected that there would be an enemy daring enough to sneak in.

Osborne remained hidden behind him, concealed in their shadow. He did not notice Osborne's approach and continued searching for things.

Osborne's lips curled into a cold smile.

He controlled his killing intent, knowing that true experts could sense it easily. If he revealed his intent, the enemy would instantly detect something wrong.

He tightened his grip on the Goddess of Grain's scythe.

In an instant, Osborne moved. "Die."

The scythe, only a few feet long, looked like a curved dagger and was very easy to handle.

It sliced through the enemy's throat in a swift arc.

At the critical moment, the enemy finally sensed something was wrong, but it was too late. He couldn't escape in time and tried to use his strength to block Osborne's attack.

Unfortunately, his defenses were as flimsy as paper against the sharp scythe.

The scythe cut a fine line across the enemy's throat.

His eyes widened, and in the next moment, his head fell to the ground with a soft thud.

The slight noise of the head hitting the floor went unnoticed. The enemy's wide eyes remained frozen in shock until he died, unaware of what had happened.

Blood spurted from his neck, and his body slowly slumped to the ground.

He was dead. A Class A enemy had been silently taken out in the warehouse.

Osborne sighed in relief.

Killing a Class A enemy with his current strength was straightforward. The challenge was doing so without alerting more enemies.

With the enemy's body now on the ground, Osborne's plan was confirmed viable.

Using the dark power to hide, approach the enemy, and then use the Goddess of Grain's scythe for a quick kill.

"On to the next." Osborne didn't linger and immediately locked onto the second enemy.

The same method of assassination succeeded swiftly again. Then came the third and fourth enemies.

Osborne continued to take out heads.

Soon, he had silently eliminated several isolated enemies.

There were still nine enemies left in the mine.

They were divided into two places. One place had three enemies who seemed quite formidable.

The other place had six enemies.

Osborne hesitated for a moment, then decided to take out the three stronger enemies first. They were clearly the leaders of the group.

Eliminating them would make dealing with the remaining six easier.

"One more time, and we're done," Osborne murmured, blending back into the shadows and swiftly approaching the trio.

He had become increasingly adept at controlling the dark elemental.

Despite the three being stronger, they still didn't notice Osborne's approach.

Soon, Osborne was behind one of them.

Without hesitation, he used the Goddess of Grain's scythe to slice through the enemy's throat. He quickly moved behind the second enemy to strike again.

At that moment, the first person's head suddenly flew into the air.

The other two, upon seeing this, were immediately stunned and horrified.

They immediately unleashed powerful defenses around themselves.

Osborne's expression changed.

These three were all Class AA.

He didn't have time to think. The scythe slashed through the second enemy's throat effortlessly, cutting through his defenses with ease.

With a sharp sound, the second enemy's head was severed.

At that moment, the third enemy finally spotted Osborne's shadow. His anger and fear were evident as he launched a fierce attack.

The terrifying force struck Osborne's shadow, causing him to revert to his true form. But Osborne remained unperturbed.

He had defeated many Class AA talents at the Class A+ level, including David, Sam, and Lance. He had even beaten four Godsons of Yalynch.

Now that he had become a Class AA himself, he had no fear of just one more Class AA enemy.

He whispered, "Die."
