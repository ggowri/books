"What? She is Maia?"

Gasps filled the arena as everyone turned to stare at Emma in disbelief.

Most of the spectators had participated in Cuthbert's Honor Quest Ceremony and had witnessed firsthand his formidable strength. Typically, challengers barely lasted a single round against him, their efforts futile, with many unable to even touch him.

Yet, somehow, Cuthbert had failed to achieve fifty consecutive victories, a feat well within his grasp.

The reason? He had encountered not one but two extraordinary opponents.

The first of those was Maia.

Back then, Maia hadn't even made it onto the Godson Rankings. Everyone assumed she would be easily defeated by Cuthbert, perhaps even humiliated in the process.

However, Maia turned the tables—her Death Realm was shockingly powerful, inflicting real damage on Cuthbert and forcing him to tap into his Strength Realm, exhausting a significant amount of his energy.

Due to that unexpected outcome, Cuthbert, who aimed for a hundred consecutive wins, ended up losing in his fiftieth match to another anomaly—Axell.

Because of that battle, Maia secured a place on the Godson Rankings, where the Prophet Alliance placed her at 299.

And now, they were discovering that the talented Maia was actually Emma in disguise!

Here she stood, looking no more than twenty-five years old, and she was the one who had previously made Cuthbert look so helpless!

The audience was stunned; jaws dropped as they processed the revelation.

Clarissa's eyes widened with shock as she regarded Emma. She had spent time studying the Godson Rankings and hadn't realized Emma had already made it into the top 300.

Moreover, Emma had been to Tethynia!

Emma had masqueraded as Maia, while Osborne had disguised himself as Axell; they were siblings!

That was something Osborne had never mentioned to Clarissa.

Clarissa frowned slightly, pondering, "Why has Osborne kept quiet about Emma being with him in Tethynia? Do they share some kind of secret?"

A pang of unease hit her as the realization settled in.

Meanwhile, Emma caught Clarissa's gaze and smiled gently, seemingly unaffected by the murmurs around her.

"Hey, Clarissa, what do you say we have a little friendly competition?"

Clarissa's thoughts were a whirlwind.

She thought, "Does Emma really want to go against me?"

"Sure!" Clarissa replied, feeling a spark of excitement.

Though she acknowledged Emma's strength, she wasn't intimidated. Clarissa was ready to step into the arena.

Emma said, raising a hand to stop her, "Just a second, Clarissa. I'm not talking about a fight to the finish. I meant a friendly showdown. Let's each take a platform and face off against whoever dares to challenge us. We'll see who can last longer and defeat more challengers!"

A gleam of determination lit up Emma's eyes as she finished her proposal.

Clarissa felt her brows knit together.

Deep down, she was more interested in a direct battle with Emma to see who was stronger, but if Emma preferred to avoid a clash, she wouldn't push it.

After all, she had come here to hone her skills against formidable opponents, and this arrangement aligned perfectly with her aim.

"Alright then, let's do it!" Clarissa agreed, feeling invigorated.

"Great! But let's put something on the line to make it interesting. What do you think?" Emma added with a playful smile.

Clarissa nodded. "You tell me."

Emma leaned in closer, lowering her voice to a private whisper just for Clarissa's ears. "Remember when I told you that I like Osborne?"

Clarissa's expression turned icy.

She nodded, recalling their conversation well.

"What's your point?"

Emma took a deep breath and said, "I want you to know that I think I've finally figured out what it means to love someone. So, I want to compete with you fairly! If you lose, I want you to give me a chance to pursue Osborne."

Clarissa furrowed her brow. "And if you lose?"

"If I lose, I'll bless you both and promise never to interfere with your relationship," Emma replied earnestly.

But inside, she thought, "If I lose, I'll just be Osborne's secret lover for life and keep you in the dark."

Without hesitation, Clarissa nodded. "Alright, I accept!"

A spark of excitement lit up Emma's eyes as she smiled, "Then let's do this! Clarissa, my level is higher than yours, so I'll have a bit of an advantage. But then again, pursuing love has never been fair, has it?"

Clarissa nodded again. "That's fine by me.

"Whoever gets knocked off the stage first loses!" Clarissa declared coldly, stepping toward the arena.

Emma followed, climbing onto the platform once more.

She scanned the crowd below and called out, "Ladies and gentlemen, we're here to welcome any challengers! If you've got the guts and the strength, step up and fight us!"

With that, determination surged through Emma, and her fighting spirit ignited.

She thought, "I have to win this match!

It's the only way I can be with Osborne openly.

I must defeat Clarissa!"
