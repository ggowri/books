Time crawled by. Up on the colossal, snow-capped mountain, Osborne was laser-focused on finding the Frost-kissed Heart.

He'd completely lost track of the state duel—honestly, he couldn't give a damn about it.

All that mattered was making every second count in his relentless search.

What really bugged Osborne, though, was Agatha's continued absence.

She'd obviously made it up the mountain, but their paths hadn't crossed once.

Could she have already bailed?

Maybe the mountain's freezing cold was too much for her, and she hightailed it back to the sixth layer from the seventh. Yeah, that's gotta be it! I've got the Ascending Sacred Sanctum to keep me going on the seventh layer. But for Agatha to stick around like I have? That'd be a tough slog.

With that thought, Osborne shrugged it off and pressed on with his search.

As days blurred into nights, the first phase of the state duel wrapped up on the third day.

From then on, it was a free-for-all. Everyone was fair game and could be targeted for their tokens or extra points.

The real carnage had begun, set to rage on for four long days.

On the final day, all participants had to haul their asses back to the first layer of the Arctic Maw Chasm for a final, brutal showdown to determine the duel's rankings.

Meanwhile, back in Frost Peak City, Angela was a mess.

Her lips were clamped shut so tight, as if she was fighting back a tidal wave of emotions, and worry clouded her face.

Osborne still hadn't shown.

And on the scoreboard, his score was still stuck at 300 thousand points. He'd dropped out of the top ten and was now hanging around in twentieth place.

His score hadn't budged an inch in three days.

Everyone was convinced Osborne had bit the dust on the sixth layer—no question about it.

But Angela just couldn't bring herself to believe it.

Deep down, she still clung to a glimmer of hope that Osborne would suddenly turn up, safe and sound.

But as the clock ticked, that hope dwindled.

She knew in her heart that Osborne's chances of survival were slim to none.

In a last-ditch effort, Angela pulled some strings through the Fairy Moon Sect and got in touch with Rupert, the head referee of the state duel.

She pressed him for Osborne's whereabouts, and Rupert's answer was a real gut punch.

Rupert had checked with all the referees inside the Arctic Maw Chasm, and none of them had seen hide nor hair of Osborne.

Osborne had vanished into thin air.

At this moment, Danilo chimed in, trying to offer some comfort, "Hey, Ms. Olsen, don't beat yourself up too much.

"Osborne was one of the best disciples our Mystic Sirius Sect has seen in a century. I'm bummed about his loss, too. But what's done is done. No use crying over spilt milk.

"State duels are a rough game. Someone's bound to draw the short straw. Osborne just got unlucky."

Angela bit her lip hard enough to draw blood without saying a word.

Danilo wasn't about to drop it. "I know this is tough to swallow, Ms. Olsen. But Osborne's gone. Otherwise..."

"Enough!" Angela interrupted him abruptly.

She drew in a deep breath, glanced up at the scoreboard, and all of a sudden, a sense of indifference washed over her. The state duel? It just didn't seem like a good use of her time anymore.

But eventually, Angela gathered her resolve and decided to stay put.

It doesn't matter. I'll tough it out until the end. As long as the state duel isn't over, I can't lose hope.

With that thought, she locked her eyes on the scoreboard.

The situation had completely changed. Over the past three days, the rankings had been shifting like crazy, faster than the weather could change. The top-scoring contestants were neck and neck, constantly stepping up their game and piling on the points.

Who was at the top of the leaderboard? It wasn't Agatha anymore.

On the scoreboard, Horace was in the first place with a score of 2.2 million.

Right behind him, Broderick secured the second position with 1.8 million points.

Benson took the third spot, having amassed 1.7 million points.

Lucinda Turlington landed in the fourth place with a score of 1.3 million.

Jalen Tatum occupied the fifth position, scoring 1.25 million.

Skipping a few ranks, Conrad stood at the ninth place, his score reaching 1.08 million.

Agatha was in the tenth position, having scored one million.

Moving further down, Jared held the twelfth place with 650 thousand points.

Gustave was in the fifteenth place, with a score of 450 thousand.

Finally, Osborne was at the 20th place on the scoreboard, with a relatively lower score of 300 thousand.

Looking at the scoreboard, Angela couldn't help but heave a sigh.

Osborne used to be on top! It's such a pity he decided to take on the sixth layer.

If he'd just stuck to the fifth layer looking for tokens, he'd probably have a whole lot more points by now.

"Osborne's teammate, that little girl Clara, is in a bind!" Danilo suddenly chimed in.

Angela quickly scanned the numerous projections, easily spotting Clara's. Clara was currently on the third layer of the Arctic Maw Chasm, running for her life.

Bad news—she was being chased by a whole team.

And a seriously tough one at that, led by none other than Conrad.

In a heartbeat, Conrad and his four teammates had Clara cornered.

Malcolm chuckled, saying, "Hahaha!

"Keep running! What'd you stop for, you little brat? Think you can outrun us?" Conrad sneered at Clara, his face dripping with contempt.

He turned to Conrad and continued, "Mr. Maurice, this chick's got a boatload of tokens on her! Mostly from layers four and three, worth close to a hundred thousand points!

"She didn't even register these tokens under her name! Talk about hitting the jackpot. If she had, we'd only get a tenth of her points, even if we creamed her.

"But now, these unregistered tokens are up for grabs! Once we take her down, they're all ours!

"That's almost a hundred thousand points' worth of tokens!

"Mr. Maurice, with those extra points, your spot on the scoreboard will be even more secure. You might even climb up to eighth place!"

After Malcolm finished, Conrad's eyes lit up.

He glared at Clara and said, "Hand over all the tokens you're carrying! You're no match for me, so don't even bother putting up a fight."

Clara's face was ashen.

Convinced that Osborne wasn't easily killed, she scavenged tokens for three days, planning to give them to him upon his return.

Thus, Clara stashed the unregistered tokens away.

The initial three days of the state duel unfolded seamlessly.

Yet, when the fourth day marked the onset of the second phase, the situation took a turn for the worse. The badges acquired a new capability—detecting nearby individuals with a substantial number of tokens.

Despite her stealth efforts, she was discovered.

Once word spread about her abundant tokens, the temptation proved irresistible, prompting others to covet and attempt to seize them.

Previously, Clara had managed to elude her pursuers on several occasions.

Given her presence on the third layer, where the other contestants were relatively less formidable, escape was feasible.

Regrettably, this time fortune deserted her as Conrad's team targeted her.

In this team of five, even Malcolm, the least powerful member, held the eightieth position on the strength leaderboard. It was an insurmountable challenge for Clara to fend them off.
