Chapter 1881 I'll teach him a lesson

When Paul arrived, the surgery had already begun, and Andrew and Peter stood straight outside the operating room, waiting.

Amelia sat on a chair outside, her fair fingers caressing her abdomen, one hand on her belly and the other gripping the armrest.

The back of her hand gripping the armrest was a bit forceful, and upon closer inspection, one could see protruding bones.

Her complexion wasn't very good either, having not eaten or slept well during the days she was confined.

On top of that, she finally got to see James, only for him to get injured, so naturally her emotions were tense.

Fortunately, the doctor said that the bullet had hit James in the shoulder and hadn't caused any vital damage, otherwise she wouldn't have been able to hold on.

As pregnant women, Eva had gained several pounds while Amelia had become particularly thin, clearly having endured a lot during her pregnancy.

Paul wasn't very sympathetic towards other women, but seeing Amelia looking so exhausted, he couldn't help but furrow his brow.

"Amelia."

Paul walked up to Amelia and advised her to go back to the hotel to rest.

"You look exhausted, go back and rest."

Amelia shifted her gaze away from the operating room and looked up at Paul standing in front of her.

"Don't worry, I'm fine."

Even if she was exhausted, she had to wait until James' surgery was over and he was safe before she could relax.

She became stubborn, just like James. Paul tried to persuade her for a few moments, but seeing that he couldn't change her mind, he stopped trying.

"Then I'll have someone arrange a rest area for you."

After saying that, he didn't care whether Amelia agreed or not, he simply waved his hand and had someone take care of it.

As Paul was persuading Amelia to go to the rest area, the door to the operating room opened and the doctor walked out.

"How is it?" Paul immediately approached and asked.

Amelia also hurriedly leaned on the armrest and walked over, anxiously looking at the doctor.

"Was the surgery successful?"

The doctor took off his mask, revealing a confident smile. "The surgery was very successful, he has been transferred to the ward, you can go see him now."

The four people outside the operating room finally breathed a sigh of relief.

When Amelia wanted to go to the ward with them, she suddenly felt the baby move.

Amelia, who was feeling some pain in her stomach, could only lean against the wall for support and rest.

The men didn't notice and rushed straight to the ward. James, lying on the hospital bed, opened his eyes and didn't see Amelia.

His face was even paler than when he had the surgery. He thought something had happened to Amelia and completely disregarded the injury on his shoulder.

He tried to sit up, scaring Andrew, Peter, and Paul, who quickly held him down. "James, you just had surgery, don't move around."

Because he exerted force, James' shoulder started bleeding again, quickly staining the white gauze red, but he didn't notice.

"Where's Amelia?"

At this moment, he was still weak from the anesthesia and couldn't push away the hands of the others, so he could only grab Paul's arm from behind.

"Did Matty take her away again?"

Paul thought he was going to the bathroom, but who knew he was in such a hurry because he didn't see his wife at first glance.

"James..."

Paul didn't have a chance to speak yet when Amelia, who had already entered the room, supported herself against the wall and slowly walked to the door.

Seeing that frail figure, James' worried heart suddenly relaxed. "Are you feeling unwell?"

"No." Amelia shook her head and pointed to her own belly.

"The baby heard that your surgery was successful and is dancing inside me."

Hearing that it was discomfort caused by the baby's movements, James' cold gaze swept over Amelia's belly.

"After he's born, I'll teach him a lesson."

Amelia, who had already entered the room, heard this and glanced at him coldly.

"The baby hasn't even been born yet, and you're already thinking about teaching him a lesson. You're really something."

After murmuring these words, Amelia shifted her gaze back to his shoulder. "Why is it bleeding again?"

She furrowed her brow, bent down, and touched the bloodstains on the gauze. "I'll go call the doctor."

James grabbed Amelia's hand and held it in his own palm. "It's nothing, you don't need to do it."

Upon hearing this, Andrew and Peter instantly understood and exchanged glances.

"I'll go call the doctor."

"Then I'll go buy some food and drinks."

Andrew and Peter walked away, leaving Paul alone.

"Paul."

"Yes?"

Paul thought James was going to say something special to him, so he leaned over, sat next to the hospital bed, and looked at him expectantly.

"James, go back and take care of Eva."
