Chapter 1700 When I’m not by your side

Amelia walked up to Herb.

"Mr. Clark, you've tried to borrow the game twice now, not only embarrassing me but also showing a lack of respect. Don't call me sister anymore."

What she meant was that brothers wouldn't treat their sisters like this, but Herb didn't think about the concept of "respect or disrespect."

He just wanted to take advantage of the opportunity to mess with James, without considering that it would embarrass Amelia. Now he realizes it, a little too late.

"Sis, I don't know who will draw the Joker. Even if it's me in the end, I would suggest kissing James just to prank him."

However, he didn't expect Amelia to be the one who drew the card. Caught up in the excitement of the game and thinking about his previous loss, he didn't consider gender or marriage.

He definitely didn't mean to disrespect Amelia, but who cares what goes on in his mind? By saying those words, he showed a lack of respect.

Herb didn't intend to say much more. He leaned his face closer, "Let's fight, but once we're done, let me go home..."

James slapped his swollen face hard and then slowly pulled back his hand. "If there's a next time, I'll break your legs."

Herb, who had been subdued, looked at James and couldn't help but think that the way he spoke was similar to how Johnny scolded him.

And why did he feel like... James' eyes resembled a little girl's? Although his memories were fuzzy, this feeling seemed familiar.

As he stared at James, the man took off his gloves and coldly ordered, "Throw him into the sea."

Those three cold words instantly interrupted Herb's wandering thoughts. "James, why don't you keep your word?!"

James put down his gloves, took out a wet wipe, and calmly wiped his fingers before looking up at him.

"Forget that the rules of winning and losing are determined by me."

In other words, he could do whatever he wanted. Herb's face turned red with anger.

"Evans, you're ruthless!"

James threw away the wet wipe and held Amelia's hand, leaving the top deck of the open-air cabin.

Amelia trembled a bit and looked at Herb being carried by several bodyguards onto the deck.

"Honey, are you really going to throw your cousin off the ship from such a high place?"

James suppressed the coldness in his eyes and replaced it with a tender look as he lowered his gaze to Amelia.

"Just scaring him."

Throwing someone from the top of the ship would undoubtedly result in certain death, with internal organs shattered.

Hearing his words, Amelia breathed a sigh of relief. "He won't dare to do it again."

James suddenly remembered something and gradually slowed his pace until he stopped, turning to gaze at Amelia.

"If next time, I'm not by your side and he treats you like this again, be more ruthless and just slap him."

Amelia looked up at him with a puzzled expression, examining him.

"Where are you going when you're not by my side?"

James felt a sudden pain in his heart at the thought of their upcoming separation. Unable to control himself, he raised his hand and embraced her.

"What I mean is when I'm not at home."

Amelia, who had been holding her breath, slowly relaxed and reached out to hug James around the waist.

"Don't worry, if Herb dares to do something like that next time, I'll beat him with a baseball bat."

Amelia's voice, filled with gritted teeth and a grinding of her little tiger teeth, made James couldn't help but curl his lips into a smile.

After laughing, he raised his slender hand and stroked her soft hair, expressing all his reluctance in the touch of his fingers.

Eva's wedding night was quite unique. For example, when Paul held Eva and was about to kiss her, a howl came from the top floor.

His movement paused, and the screaming from upstairs stopped. He lowered his head again, just as his lips were about to touch Eva's, a pig-like scream came from above.

Paul felt that James wasn't torturing Herb, but torturing him.

Frustrated, Paul decided to ignore it and held Eva close, listening to the screams from above while kissing her.

After being scared all night, Herb was kicked off the ship by the bodyguards.

Johnny, who had been following the ship, caught the miserable Herb and turned in the opposite direction.

Zack, standing on the deck, looked at the massive ship and narrowed his eyes slightly. He hadn't found an opportunity to talk to James alone yet.

He didn't know when he would go to Erebus. He could only go back and ask Holden for information, then quietly follow when the time was right.
