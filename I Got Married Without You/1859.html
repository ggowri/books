Chapter 1859: Matty's Last Stand

Upon hearing Matty's words, a pang of anxiety seized everyone.

"So soon?"

"If it were me, I'd rush back to seek revenge, free from tracking and bombing threats," Matty nodded.

Considering James' protective nature, he'd not sit idle. Their task now was to prevent the enemy hacker from changing the bomb's program. With that thought, Matty took over from a programmer, swiftly disabling the complex encryption system and shortening the bomb countdown.

Paul, monitoring the computer, was furious as his hard-earned delay was shortened. He resumed typing with cramped fingers.

In a flash, the delay was extended again. Matty grinned with admiration, "This kid's good, better than any of you."

The operator, uninterested in hacking prowess, asked, "Big bro, James is coming tomorrow. We only have external explosives. Will it work?"

They could monitor S's chopper, detonate the bomb when it lands, and retreat. But they feared the explosives wouldn't kill everyone.

Compared to the operator's anxiety, Matty was much calmer.

"Eason, tell Carson to wait outside with his crew. They're on cleanup duty post-blast," Matty instructed.

"But if we bail, leaving Carson to handle S, isn't that throwing him under the bus?" Eason, close to Carson, objected.

"Don't forget. Without Carson, James wouldn't have breezed into the upper zone. A traitor's sacrifice is due course," Matty snapped back.

Eason fell silent, outnumbered by Matty's supporters.

S's unity contrasted sharply with Erebus' survival instinct. Eason felt a pang, "Easy for you to say. You're not the ones on the chopping block."

"We're here to take out S, who hurt your family. Not to debate sacrifices," Matty soothed, patting his shoulder.

"I won't spill to Carson. But send more backup," Eason begrudgingly conceded.

Despite his friendship, Eason prioritized revenge. All operators were here for vengeance, self-preservation over friendship.

Matty promised more support, then addressed others, "Explosives, defense, my granddaughter, all are to restrain James. Any concerns?"

With the extra assurance, operators shook their heads, "Let's see how James and S walk into our trap."

They could leave, but stayed to witness S's downfall.

Carson understood Matty's plan when he got Eason's call.

He felt a chill, but decided to help James.

"Eason, can you sneak Amelia out of Erebus?"

"Sorry, Amelia is our last card. I can't let her go."

After Eason's refusal, Carson said nothing, just forced a smile.

"Don't you think using a woman as a shield is dishonorable?"

"It is, but Carson, in a life-or-death situation, who cares about honor?"

Eason advised Carson to prioritize Erebus, then hung up and blocked signals again.
