Chapter 1863 Reaping the benefits

James interrupted Master Davies, who was deeply immersed in his memories.

He lifted his gaze and stared into the eyes of the man opposite him, eyes that seemed to see through everything.

"That's right, at that time, I thought that using you as a weapon to eliminate the Clark family, the Leinster family, and the Evans family would be more satisfying than doing it myself, especially since they are all your relatives."

If Matty was ruthless, then Master Davies was venomous. He patiently strategized for years just to set up this chessboard.

"At the age of seven, when I was beaten to the brink of death, you extended a helping hand and pulled me out. I thought it was redemption, but it turned out to be all part of your plan."

Even though James had already suspected being used, he still felt hurt, and his disappointment was evident in his eyes.

Throughout his life, his father didn't love him, his mother died early, and his stepmother abused him. Even his grandfather only saw him as useful...

So, at the age of seven, when Master Davies held him, covered in blood, he whispered in his ear, "Don't be afraid, I'll be here for you, and everything will be alright."

At that time, he truly believed that Master Davies was his redemption, his lifeline on the path of growth, and a caring godfather.

But now, in this moment, before winter arrived, the former Master Davies, like a ghost, turned into frost, forever frozen in his heart.

Master Davies noticed the change in James's eyes, but he seemed to have already anticipated this outcome and did not feel regretful.

"To be honest, seeing you destroy the Leinster family and lead the Evans family against the Clark family is quite satisfying. However..."

Master Davies paused and looked at James, who appeared lonely and desolate on the other side.

"The game I set up may have eliminated the Leinster family, but the Clark family and the Evans family are still there..."

"There's no need to hide anymore when it has come to this point," James interrupted, his chin raised, urging Master Davies to continue.

"You're right, even if I didn't say anything, you would have found out. So, it's better for me to tell you the answer."

James suppressed his feelings of disappointment and leaned back on the sofa, waiting for Master Davies to continue.

"The Evans family, as we both know, is exceptional. I was thinking, without you, they wouldn't last long, would they?"

"Coincidentally, that's when Matty intervened."

"I thought, if you died in Erebus, I wouldn't be implicated, right?"

Master Davies's voice sounded as if he was discussing something ordinary, indifferent.

But to James, it felt chilling in his ears.

"So, you've known all along who the founder of Erebus was, and you were willing to sacrifice so many members just to send me to my death."

Master Davies waved his hand, dismissing this claim.

"At first, I truly believed that Erebus was an organization formed by the elimination of the cancerous elements within S, united together."

"But when they started massacring members of S, I remembered darkness, and I guessed that Matty was the mastermind."

"I also considered directly confronting Matty with S, but..."

Master Davies couldn't bring himself to say it, but James didn't give him any face or room to hide.

"But your father participated in the gang rape, and your cousin was the mastermind behind Matty's hatred."

"You were afraid that if Matty found out, he would seek revenge on you and the Davies family, so you didn't dare reveal your identity and confront Matty directly."

James had figured out everything, and Master Davies no longer concealed it, simply nodding in acknowledgment.

"Actually, if you hadn't mentioned leaving S, I wouldn't have used your trust in me to send you to Erebus."

Master Davies knew that with James's personality, once he entered Erebus and discovered the mastermind, he would undoubtedly kill them.

By using James's hand to secretly eliminate Matty, he could forever hide in the shadows without being implicated.

If James failed to kill Matty and perished in Erebus, the Evans family, without James, would be defeated.

Whichever way it went, it was advantageous for him. How could Master Davies not sit back and watch, reaping the benefits?
