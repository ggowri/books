Chapter 1832: A Beacon

James felt fate was mocking him, laughing bitterly.

However, Matty was unfazed by his wild appearance.

"You got ten minutes," he said, exiting the lab, followed by his men in black.

Dylan stayed back, advising, "Just agree for now. You can explain to Amelia once you're free."

"Dylan! I'm not deaf," Matty's chilling voice stopped him.

Startled, Dylan quickly shut up and left.

Outside, Matty unmasked, showing his icy eyes.

"Twenty lashes for you," he ordered Dylan, who complied fearfully.

Matty then gave out tasks, "Dylan, start the program in the control room. Others, transfer the chips, then destroy the chip room."

After, Matty warned everyone not to talk about the chip or James' ordeal in Erebus.

He specifically mentioned Carson, who was puzzled by Matty's irrational orders far away in Chiang Rai.

Once everything was set, Matty led the remaining men to the bio-lab, resuming his position of dominance overlooking James.

"Made up your mind yet?"

James' eyes quivered slightly but he gave no response.

Matty wasn't in a hurry, instead, he sat back on the couch, patiently waiting for James' answer.

James knew that to remove the chip's control, 1-1 had to contradict his conditions of not limiting James' freedom.

This condition seemed beneficial for James, but the real winner would be 1-1. He was smart, advantageous to himself yet holding James' Achilles heel.

Having been a business tycoon for years, James knew he had met a formidable adversary this time. Yet, he merely curled his lips into a hollow smile.

"You're a curse to Amelia," he said.

"Perhaps. But without S, she wouldn't be in this mess."

Sophia had been killed by S. If she hadn't been murdered, then Amelia's mother wouldn't have married Brandon, and Amelia wouldn't be suffering like she was now.

James eyed the masked man. He briefly wondered about the link between Amelia's woes and 'S', but resentment for Matty took over. Anyway, his brothers' deaths wouldn't go unpunished.

With that thought, James' icy gaze hardened even more, his eyes burning with hatred towards 1-1. Though he was in a vulnerable position, he hid his rage well.

"Remove your mask if you want my compliance."

"Tomorrow, Amelia will come and she'll spill everything."

Matty knew his secret role as Erebus's founder wouldn't stay hidden but revealing it too early would tip off Amelia.

Self-protective, Matty swapped identities with James in a negotiation, keeping his own a secret.

He suspected Amelia knew who 'S's founder was, planning to use James to subtly test her. He controlled everything, not caring about James' fate.

James' piercing eyes stared back, predator-like.

"If you release me, I'll leave her, but won't be cold," James offered.

Aware of Amelia's fear of his frosty manner, he vowed not to repeat it, promising to treat her kindly.

This was James' compromise.

If Matty overstepped, he risked awakening a sleeping lion, causing unforeseen trouble.

"Deal," Matty agreed, blocking James, "But if you try to reconcile or explain, I'll trigger the end - terminate her pregnancy, even kill the baby if born!"

With a stern warning, Matty bolted from the couch to the control room, aiming to tweak James' brain chip for better control.

Once he was gone, James tilted his head, his thick lashes casting a large, lonely shadow under the light.

Dominic once said his love was his Achilles heel; he was right. Amelia and their child were his weak points.

But as a husband, he couldn't stand Amelia's slightest suffering. So, his weakness also became his strength, a beacon guiding him through the darkness.
