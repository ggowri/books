"Mom, take care of yourself."

Amelia was actively embraced by Alexander. She was going to cry. Then, Amelia lifted her fingers and placed them on Alexander's head, reluctant to part. She gently caressed him and said, "Alexander, your father and I are waiting for you coming back."

Alexander nestled in her arms, nodded, then hugged her tightly before releasing her and turning sideways toward James. "Father, as I finish my studies and come back, you will definitely not be able to beat me."

James smiled and didn't say anything. He just lifted his big palm and patted his shoulder. "Remember, conceited people are easily stabbed. You need to learn to be compatible with others."

Alexander remembered James' advice in his heart. "I will."

James withdrew his palm and took out a golden knife with an "S" engraved on it from his waist, handing it to him. "At the birthday party, you caught this knife, and now I'm officially giving it to you."

Alexander had heard of James' story from Peter and had long known that Wesley Shawn, who led thousands of members, had subdued many people with a golden knife.

Now, James handing the knife to Alexander was a sign of trust in him and a hope that he had the ability to protect himself. Alexander thanked James for his unspoken love and respectfully took it with both hands. "Thank you, Father."

James didn't say much anymore. Then, he headed toward the car and raised his chin. "Let's go. Don't turn back."

Children who grew up had to leave their homes, and even if parents were reluctant to part ways, they must learn to let go.

Alexander held the knife and stepped forward, awkwardly hugging James before turning around and getting into the car.

He was probably shy, closing the door and windows very quickly. Even Amelia didn't have time to give him any more advice. Alexander let Peter drive the car.

Amelia followed behind for a few steps until she couldn't see the car, then covered her face and cried. If possible, she really didn't want Alexander to be smart so he wouldn't leave her.

But there was no such hypothesis. Alexander was a genius, not belonging to his family but to the school, laboratory, and research institution that needed him. Her son was destined not to be an ordinary person.

James grabbed her shoulder, silently providing comfort and strength. Feeling supported, Amelia leaned on his shoulder, and her tears blurred as she gazed at the empty road from afar.

James taught Alexander all the techniques of marksmanship, swordsmanship, and skill, but he still felt uneasy, so he asked Peter to follow Alexander to study abroad.

James' fingers slid down from her eyebrows and eyes to her red lips. "Two more times, I'll take you tomorrow."

Amelia shook her head while poking away his fingers. "No, I can't keep up with my physical strength. If we made it again, I could not get out of bed tomorrow."
