Chapter 1849: I am on Your Side

After Dylan left, Amelia stayed put, deliberately waiting for Matty.

As expected, he came running.

Just as he arrived, he gave a few instructions to his men in black.

When he turned around, Amelia had disappeared.

Now he found her leaning against the corridor, lost in thought.

Then, Matty slowed down his pace.

"Amelia, are you scared?" He asked.

"It's my father. It feels a bit cruel." Amelia admitted, her head down.

"That man abandoned you and your mother. He's no father of yours," Matty scoffed.

"I think it's because I'm pregnant. All this doesn't sit well with me," Amelia said, not arguing back.

"If you're uncomfortable, go rest." Matty suggested without noticing anything odd.

"Where should I go?" Amelia asked.

"As the leader of Erebus, the control room is yours from now on. Rest there."

"Good idea. There's a lounge in there. Perfect for a pregnant woman like me."

Matty glanced at her belly. He didn't want Amelia to have James's baby, but...

He had no choice but to accept it. As long as Amelia was part of The Lawrence Family, he could find a way to guide the child, no matter who the father was.

Matty told Amelia to go to the control room and she went without hesitation. He had to leave for a while but told her to rest well. He would return to grant her full access once he finished his business.

Once he left, Amelia's face darkened, her expression unreadable.

Before long, Dylan arrived with a bunch of snacks.

"Take your pick, they're all spicy," he said.

"I just wanted to talk to you," Amelia replied as she accepted the snacks and closed the door. She turned to face him.

"What do you want to know?" Dylan asked, taking a deep breath.

"I want to know what you know." Amelia replied after putting the snacks aside.

"All I know is that James is our partner in Erebus."

Dylan didn't want to say more and Amelia didn't push him. She just lowered her gaze to her belly. "Dylan, I've been an orphan all my life. I don't want my child to grow up without a father like I did."

Dylan felt a pang in his heart. He sensed the hardships Amelia must have faced. But he had been nurtured by Matty since childhood. Was he supposed to betray him now?

"Dylan, you don't have to tell me the truth. But I hope we can be honest siblings, without secrets or false pretenses," she said calmly after a pause.

Dylan clenched his fists but remained silent, not daring to look at her.

"I felt good coming back to this family when I found out you were part of it." Amelia added, looking at Dylan who remained silent.

Dylan was polite, graceful, respectful towards women. Who wouldn't want a brother like him? She liked all three brothers of The Lawrence Family. Regardless of the truth, she saw them as her family because they provided the warmth she craved for.

Dylan couldn't look at Amelia's earnest eyes without feeling guilty. But he managed to look away.

Amelia knew the truth from Dylan's reaction. She didn't know why but it was enough for now. She could find out the rest later.

With that, Amelia dropped the subject.

"Dylan, thanks for the snacks. I'll eat and rest for a while. You can leave now."

She let go of the issue and Dylan thought he'd be relieved. But instead, he felt shackled.

Dylan wasn't normally indecisive. He could make decisions quickly depending on where his heart leaned.

Then, he turned to look at Amelia, her face calm and sweet. "I'm going to the surveillance room to find Matty. Hide and listen. Whatever you decide to do afterwards, I am on your side."
