Chapter 1834: Sneaky Messages

If Matty wasn't the founder of Erebus, Amelia might've found his actions a bit touching.

But right now, she felt nothing.

She just picked up her pace to keep up with him.

Dylan was the one driving, with no one else in the car.

Amelia knew her hunch was right; none of the Lawrence Family knew about Erebus, except Dylan.

Amelia sarcastically praised Matty's acting skills, while he tried to paint a picture of how cruel 'S' was and how much Erebus had suffered under him.

But Amelia couldn't care less about his attempts to manipulate her. She just turned her head, looking out the window and memorizing their route.

The journey was long, involving a car ride, a helicopter, a ferry, and more helicopter rides.

After a whole day and night, they finally arrived at an unknown mountain top.

Amelia suspected Matty had intentionally taken a longer route to confuse her, but seeing how familiar they were with the way, she doubted it.

Meanwhile, Andrew was livid, hitting the steering wheel, "That sly old dog! Despite our chopper, he slipped us!"

"He did start Erebus. He's hard to pin down. Let's wait for Mrs. Evans' location update," Peter tried to soothe him.

As they waited, Amelia shadowed Matty and Dylan to a villa.

Matty casually pointed out the mountainside villas, defining the zones by compass directions.

He explained to Amelia she needed to learn the Ace layout for the 1-1, briefing her on the zones, rules, and gameplay.

It dawned on Amelia that Erebus was concealed underground, the villas serving as entrances.

She was stunned that such a secret place could harbour Erebus, and ordinary villas could house such malevolence.

After the Ace rundown, they entered a Zone A villa, home to a respectful elderly couple.

Amelia learned they were long-serving Lawrence Family servants, well-treated by Matty, who had generously gifted them assets and supported their children.

Despite admiring Matty's leadership skills, Amelia found his use of them for murderous purposes deeply disturbing.

Then, Matty led her through the basement, twisting and turning like a maze, before finally stopping at a hefty metal door.

A series of security checks - facial scan, iris recognition, palm verification, voice check - and the door finally swung open.

Getting inside Ace wasn't just about stepping through the door. An elevator ride and several corridors later, they reached Section A.

Their destination was the upper area, specifically the control room - Matty's hub of operations.

A black-masked henchman brought a mask, black with a bluebird design.

Matty explained the pattern was her grandmother's favorite, a constant reminder of his vow for vengeance.

The continuous infusion of hate was unsettling for Amelia, but she had her reasons for being there, discomfort be damned.

After Matty helped her don the mask, he had Dylan summon the operators for a meeting in the control room, planning to reveal her identity.

Amelia's real intention was to see James and deliver some information, but after Matty promised they would meet post-announcement, she didn't argue.

As Dylan began tapping on the console, a mechanical voice echoed across areas A, B, and C.

Operators in the middle of tasks quickly made their way to the control room.

When Amelia asked how many operators were there, Matty explained that each area had two groups of nine - all his partners, not counting the numerous henchmen in black.

Once she had a rough idea of the layout and personnel, Amelia took advantage of the gathering in the control room to sneak a peek at her phone's signal.

Realizing it wasn't blocked, she quickly made an excuse to go to the bathroom, hiding in a stall to send a quick message and location to Andrew.

Matty, ever observant, knew something was up the moment Amelia excused herself.

As she left, he nodded subtly, and the henchman at the console started typing, intercepting the message.

However, Amelia, unaware, thought her message had been sent and returned to the control room, now filled with masked figures in black protective gear.

As she entered, the operators lifted their heads, their gazes intense even behind their masks.

Amelia steadied herself, ignored the burning stares, and took her seat next to Matty at his beckoning.
