Chapter 1705 Old pictures

Amelia noticed Dylan disappearing into the study and quickly whispered to Neil, "Neil, Dylan has been testing us. Be careful."

Rubbing his wrist, Neil shrugged it off, "People like him enjoy doing this. The more cautious you are, the more suspicious he becomes. Relax."

Neil had more experience with the aristocratic families he encountered during his project surveys than Amelia did.

Amelia followed his lead, letting go of her tension and started examining the house.

The architecture was simple, as if it belonged to an ordinary wealthy family.

The only thing that seemed unique were the vintage photos on the walls.

Those photos looked old and each one was only half intact.

It was as if back then, she misunderstood Ryan and cut off the half that belonged to him, leaving only her and Eva.

The man in those photos resembled Dylan, and judging by their age, that man should be Matty.

So the owner of this house was Matty's first love, and the one who cut the photos must be Dylan's first love.

Amelia didn't understand why only Matty's photos were left in her own house, while hers were cut off.

Confused, she had a puzzled look on her face when Neil whispered in her ear, "Let me tell you a secret, Dylan just said his grandmother passed away, but he lied to you."

Amelia raised her eyebrows in surprise, "His grandmother didn't pass away?"

Neil shook his head, "His grandmother did pass away, but she's not his real grandmother. His grandmother is someone else, but I don't know who."

Amelia: "...So you're basically saying nothing."

Neil shrugged, "Isn't that shocking enough?"

Amelia rolled her eyes at him, "You don't even know who Dylan's real grandmother is, and you dare say it's shocking."

Neil scratched his head, "I vaguely remember my master mentioning it, but I was playing a game at that time, so I didn't pay much attention..."

Skender always reviewed every project Aaron took on, so he thoroughly investigated every party involved in the project.

Aaron was skilled at digging up information. He could uncover not only family secrets but also trivial old stories.

At that time, Aaron used to tell Neil the stories behind each party, but Neil was too focused on gaming.

Footsteps came from upstairs.

"Miss Clark, could you come up? My grandfather said he only wants to see you alone."

Amelia and Neil exchanged glances.

"I usually need an assistant to take notes during style discussions with the project parties."

Dylan rested one hand on the railing of the escalator, slightly tilting his head to look at Amelia, who was looking up at him.

"Miss Clark, it's best to follow my grandfather's request."

It was a gentle statement, but there was a hint of authority in his words.

That was the inherent pressure that came from a scion of an aristocratic family.

After hesitating for two seconds, Amelia sat on the couch, opened her toolbox, took out her notebook, and walked upstairs.

Watching Amelia go upstairs alone to meet the project party, Neil felt anxious. If something went wrong, would James sacrifice him?
