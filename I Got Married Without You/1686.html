Chapter 1686 Sharing my good luck with you

Amelia took Grace and planned to find Eva, but Eva arrived at the door first. The two hadn't seen each other for over a month, but now they coincidentally met at the door and couldn't help but smile at each other.

Back inside the castle, after Frank prepared some food for pregnant women, Amelia took Eva up and down for a few rounds, then extended her hand and gently touched Eva's flat belly.

"Eva, do you remember what you promised me before?"

"Of course, I remember."

Standing, Eva gently touched Amelia's hair.

"When I give birth, I'll let you be the godmother."

Upon hearing this, Amelia raised her head and smiled sweetly at Eva.

"I'm starting to look forward to it."

The joy and delight in her eyes were all for Eva, without a trace of pain for her own inability to conceive.

She acted well, always hiding her worries in her heart, but Eva could still sense her emotions.

"Amelia, your child will come soon too."

Eva sat down, held her hand, and gently patted her hand.

"When your child is born, I'll also be the godmother."

Eva amused Amelia, and the heaviness in her heart quickly disappeared.

"Then I'll take your good words, and let you be the godmother of my child as soon as possible."

Eva extended her fair fingers and tapped Amelia's nose.

"Sharing my good luck with you."

Amelia leaned in closer, her high nose bridge almost touching.

"Do it a few more times, so I can have more children."

Eva couldn't help but laugh, tapping Amelia's nose ten times in a row.

"Let's see if you can have ten."

"Two is enough, preferably one boy and one girl. Then my life will be complete."

This was a loving couple's most common hope, and Eva shared the same sentiment, but she didn't force it.

After chatting with Eva for a while, Eva felt tired and Amelia quickly asked her to rest in the guest room.

She originally wanted Eva to have a good sleep, but little did she know that Paul couldn't find his wife and rushed to Blue Bay Island.

"Amelia, where's my wife?"

When Paul entered, Amelia looked around, but he didn't even give her a glance, which made her find it amusing.

"I've locked up your wife. If you want to see her, pay me fifty thousand."

Without hesitation, Paul took out his phone and prepared to transfer the money, but Amelia stopped him by pressing the screen.

"So you're actually transferring."

Amelia smiled.

"I was just joking. Eva is sleeping in the guest room. Maybe you can wait for her to wake up and take her home."

Paul glanced at the sleeping Eva in the guest room, then went to the kitchen.

"Amelia, how about tonight Eva and I have dinner at your place?"

Amelia had already wanted to invite them for dinner, so when Paul showed interest in their chef, she smiled and nodded.

"As long as you don't mind having dinner with James."

"I'm not afraid."

"I am!"

Just as Eva reached the living room, she quickly interjected upon hearing such a terrifying topic.

Amelia put down her phone, propped her forehead with one hand, and looked Eva up and down.

"Why are you so afraid of my husband? He's not a ferocious beast."

Thinking Eva would give a reason, they both said in unison:

"He is a ferocious beast!"

"He is a ferocious beast!"

Amelia: ...

"I always thought my husband was gentle."

Eva walked over, picked up her bag from the sofa, and responded to Amelia's words.

"That's only towards you."

Paul nodded in agreement, lowered his head, and kissed Eva's hand.

"Eva, come home with me. I'll hire a good chef to make you a delicious meal."
