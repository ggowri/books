Chapter 1839: Can We Part Ways?

His eyes held stars, like a fog, masking the profound and dark depths of his stare.

Amelia couldn't tell what James was thinking. But his words, they were suffocating. Amelia couldn't bear to hear them. Her nose stung, her eyes reddened, beyond her control.

"Is it because of Emily's threats again...?"

Just like before, Emily used her life to threaten James. He had no choice but to push Amelia away. But this time was different.

"I'll handle Emily as the leader of Erebus. Don't be scared."

Her voice, firm in her resolve to protect him, was warm in James's ears. It brought warmth but also shame.

"Amelia, this time... Emily didn't threaten me. I... I want a divorce."

Amelia stood still, her heart heavy, the sour sting in her nose, tears welling up in her eyes, and like pearls off a string, they fell.

"Why...?"

Seeing her cry, James felt a pang of panic. He raised his slender fingers, nervously wiping away her tears.

"Amelia, being with me... It's hard, isn't it?"

He gently wiped her tears, softly called her name, and even looked at her tenderly. But his next words were far from tender.

"Zack died here because of me. Many members of S died here too. The burden I carry... it's too much. If you stay with me, you'll suffer. So..."

He paused, his voice trembling when he spoke again. It felt like something was slicing at his throat, cutting inch by inch, painfully.

"Can we... part ways?"

Through the blurry veil of tears, Amelia looked at the man before her, almost unable to believe that the words 'part ways' had come from his mouth.

The first time he pulled out a breakup agreement, Amelia walked away with grace, no fuss.

She figured she could let go and forget about James when he stopped seeking her, but he kept coming back.

When he returned from abroad, he pleaded with her, claiming he'd die without her. Amelia etched these words into her heart and bones and loved him just as intensely.

So, she too would die without him. But he'd rather see her die than part ways, just because he was afraid she'd get tired?

Taking a deep breath to suppress the dull pain, Amelia reached out again to hold his hand, gripping it tightly as if offering strength.

"I've managed through all the fatigue before. What matters now is not whether I'm tired, but that I can join you in seeking revenge and face everything together."

Feeling her strength, James lowered his eyes, hiding the bitterness within.

"Since Matty declared you as 1-1, you and I have been on opposing sides."

Only then did Amelia realize this, her heart skipping a beat. She quickly grasped his hand and explained frantically, "I only used this as an excuse to enter Erebus because Matty wouldn't let me see you. I didn't mean to oppose you."

The moment she saw the golden leaf, she thought he was restrained. Her first instinct was to find him without considering the implications.

"I'm sorry. I should have thought more before making hasty decisions."

Her regret mirrored in James' eyes seemed particularly cruel.

Knowing it had nothing to do with her, yet pushing her away with such an excuse, he felt utterly weak.

James, you can't even protect your own life, your wife.

If you're so weak, why not just die?

But he had to protect the child and avenge Zack. He had to stay alive!

The viciousness in his eyes was both defeated and defiant.

He concealed all his grief, reaching out his other hand to gently stroke her hair.

"Even if you're not against me, you're still Matty's granddaughter, and you took his place. Both of these roles are in opposition to me."

His words, spoken so lightly, drew a line between them, making them instant enemies with different stances.
