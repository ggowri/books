Chapter 1841: Talk to the Baby

"Amelia."

As Amelia was about to walk away, James suddenly called out to her. She turned around and found him staring at her belly.

"Can I... touch the baby?"

He hadn't been around since she got pregnant and hadn't had the chance to feel the baby.

Following his gaze, Amelia looked down at her protruding belly and gave a slight nod of approval.

James then reached out and touched her belly, feeling the roundness beneath his fingers. His heart, which felt like it was tearing apart, instantly softened.

Seeing the softness in James' eyes, Amelia was reminded of Paul reading a children's book to the baby in Eva's belly. She wanted the baby's father to have a chance to talk to the baby too, so she asked James.

"Do you... want to talk to the baby?"

Caught off guard, James didn't know what to say but he cherished the moment, gently touched Amelia's belly, and softly said, "Hello..."

After a moment, he looked up at Amelia with a nervous expression, "Can he hear me?"

Amelia gently caressed her belly and nodded.

"He's almost six months old now. He moves around a lot, so sometimes when I talk, he responds by moving his arms and legs."

James, who was about to become a father for the first time, had never heard about these things before.

Hearing that their baby was moving around in her belly made his eyes well up with tears.

"But why... why isn't he moving now?"

He had been touching carefully, but he couldn't feel the baby moving as Amelia had described.

"Maybe... you didn't say enough."

Just saying hello wasn't enough. Their baby wasn't familiar with his voice yet and probably couldn't be bothered to respond.

James stared at the growing belly, lost in thought.

Rising from his chair, he knelt in front of her, his gaze level with the unseen life within.

He spoke again, sincerity ringing in his voice.

"Your mother has gone through a lot to have you. We just hope you'll be born safe and healthy." He continued, "As your father, I feel guilty for not being there for you, for not telling you stories or taking care of your mother."

"But kid, I don't ask for much in life. If your mother is okay and you're born safe, I can handle everything else."

At his words, the baby inside responded with a movement. Amelia's belly stirred.

"He moved," she said.

James placed his ear against her belly, feeling the tiny movements intimately.

Three kicks, each one clear and powerful, as if the baby was reaching out to him.

Overwhelmed with emotion, James let out a silent cry. He held her tighter, hiding his tears.

Amelia couldn't see his face, but she let him hold her until the baby's movement ceased.

Then, James slowly recomposed himself, gently releasing her.

"Take care of yourself," he said.

Amelia nodded and they shared a silent moment before she broke it.

"I... I'll go first," she said, turning quickly and leaving the room.

James watched her go, a bitter smile on his face, the pain of separation etching onto his heart.

As the door closed behind her, he mocked himself.

The weak deserve the pain, so why fear it when you've already pushed it away?
