Chapter 1682 Having a child was more urgent

Hearing the distinctive footsteps, Neil, lying on the couch, immediately sat up straight. "I'm feeling a bit dizzy, so I'll head back home first."

Amelia: "Neil, didn't you say that if I couldn't finish all the design drawings today, you would stay at my place?"

Neil waved his hand while getting up. "Oh, I have my own place. Why would I stay at your place? I'll come back tomorrow. See you later..."

But just as he got up, a slender and fair hand suddenly rested on his shoulder and gently pressed him back into place.

"Whose leg are you planning to break?"

Neil glanced at James, who had a blank expression, and quickly forced a stiff smile, revealing a perfectly symmetrical smile.

"Of course, it would be my own leg that I would break!"

A playful smile appeared in James' cold eyes.

"I just heard that you were planning to break my leg."

"Hehe."

Neil foolishly smiled.

"I definitely didn't say that."

 

Neil, like Paul, was able to adapt easily. He automatically fetched a wet wipe and handed it to James.

"Mr. Evans, wipe your hands."

Amelia had never seen Neil being so tactful before and couldn't help but raise her hand, propping up her chin to watch him perform.

James stared at the bizarre face in front of him for a few seconds before taking the wet wipe, wiping his hands, and then looking away.

"If you talk about me behind my back again, I'll saw off your leg."

After staring at Amelia for a while, James walked around the desk and picked her up.

Amelia, who was suddenly lifted up, let out a scream and instinctively hooked her arms around the back of his neck.

"Husband, where are you taking me?"

James didn't answer her, he just lowered his eyes and looked at her with deep affection.

With just one look, Amelia knew what he wanted to do.

"How about waiting until I finish the drawings?"

"Forget about the drawings for now."

Having a child was more urgent.

Originally, James didn't want to have a child, but...

If he were to face any danger, having a child would give Amelia something to hold onto, and maybe she wouldn't be so devastated.

Of course, this was only a possibility. His own plan and determination were to survive in Erebus.

Amelia couldn't lose him, and he couldn't lose Amelia. For each other's sake, they had to come back alive.

Watching her uncle's figure, Grace, who was crouching on the ground, asked with a hint of curiosity:

"Every time my uncle carries my aunt upstairs, it takes a long time for them to come back down. What does he do with my aunt?"

"Do you want to know?"

After James carried Amelia away, Neil sprawled back on the couch.

"Yes, I want to know."

Curious Grace eagerly nodded, like a puppy.

"If you want to know, give me $5,000."

Grace: ...

On the top floor, in the romantic room, Amelia leaned against the wall, panting, with her hands resting on James' broad shoulders.

"What's gotten into you?"

Not only did he leave work early today, but as soon as he came back, he wanted to make love. Did something happen to him?

James, who was holding Amelia, lifted his chin slightly, revealing a distinct jawline, and looked at her flushed face...

"I'll tell you after we're finished."
