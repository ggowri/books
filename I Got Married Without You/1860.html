Chapter 1860: Master Davies Doesn't Mind

Carson stood by the window, phone in hand.

After a moment of hesitation, he dialed James's number.

"Just a heads up, Matty's sending me to the front. I'll be fighting against you."

James didn't seem surprised by Matty's tactics. His expression didn't change much.

"You could just switch sides and watch the show from the sidelines."

A carefree smile played on Carson's lips.

"I'm 1-4, the first puppeteer to join Erebus. There's no way I'd just stand by and watch, at most I won't go after you."

There were many brothers in Erebus, besides Matty. For their sake, for the Erebus they built together, he had to follow orders, complete the mission.

"I won't attack you, but S's people have killed my family. I might have to take a few of them down."

The word 'kid' filled James with a strange sense of warmth, as if he had found a loving elder in that moment.

"See you tomorrow."

Given the circumstances, Carson wouldn't defect easily. James knew better than to try and convince him. After saying those three words, he added two more.

"Uncle."

James had called him 'uncle' before, but this time it felt different. It felt like he was actually family.

Carson wanted to say something, but James hung up. As he stared at the screen, the bedroom door swung open. Johnny stood there, arms crossed.

"Uncle, you're 1-4, aren't you?"

Caught red-handed, Carson was shocked, but he didn't show it. He brushed past Johnny and left the room.

"You knew I wanted to go to Erebus, you gave me a golden leaf and a stupid codename to mock me. But when I was in danger, you reached out to help."

Ignoring Johnny, Carson opened a bottle of wine and filled a glass to the brim. Just as he was about to drink, Johnny's hand covered the glass.

"Uncle, since you're a partner in Erebus, Herb and I never got an invitation. You're afraid we'd go and never come back, right?"

Carson, unable to find an excuse or hide the truth, looked at Johnny.

"Without me, you would have died in Erebus. Do you think I'd have golden leaves sent to you two idiots?"

Johnny hated Carson's mouth, but had to admit he was right. Without him, he would indeed be dead.

"I just didn't expect you to be the puppet master behind Erebus, or that Amelia's grandfather would be its founder."

Mentioning Amelia made Johnny nervous.

"I just heard you say Amelia is trapped in Erebus. Is she in danger?"

Carson looked thoughtful as he observed the anxious Johnny.

"Why do you seem more worried about Amelia than James?"

Johnny, feeling exposed, avoided Carson's gaze and loosened his grip on the wine glass.

"Of course not."

"Really?"

Carson sipped his wine, studying Johnny.

"If you weren't, you wouldn't have risked your life to save Amelia."

Johnny looked uncomfortable, unwilling to admit his past actions.

"I would extend a helping hand to any woman or child in danger."

Carson didn't press further, just sipped his wine, thinking about tomorrow's events.

"I heard you on the phone with James. Are you two planning a fight to the death tomorrow?"

"Hardly a death match. I'm just buying time. Once I've done that, I'll pull out."

He wasn't foolish enough to send his men to their deaths.

"I'll go with you tomorrow."

Carson, who was drinking his wine, looked surprised.

"What can you do?"

The kid who was always sick, who couldn't fight or carry a load, was going to be a sentry?

Johnny saw the contempt in his uncle's eyes and his face darkened.

"If I didn't know, I wouldn't go. But now that I do know, I have to help James."

Carson thought Johnny was crazy.

"I'm helping Erebus, you're helping James, and you want to go with me? Are you kidding?"

"I can't just stand by and do nothing."

Johnny left after dropping that line.

"Let me know before you leave tomorrow. I'll inform my men."

Carson almost had a heart attack. Not helping him, but helping James, and still reminding him to call. He had never seen such a turncoat!

Also, Johnny was helping James for Amelia, wasn't he? Why does this kid always get himself into troubling situations?

Carson was left scratching his head, while James, having sent the S members to the Cloud territory, waited for Andrew.

Along with Andrew, Master Davies, who had never shown his face, stepped in. "James, your men traced their investigations to me. Impressive."

James, standing by the floor-to-ceiling window, turned slightly and looked at Master Davies. "If I can't find your people, this is the only way to invite you over. I hope you don't mind, Master Davies?"

Master Davies, leaning on his cane, took off his black hat and slowly walked in. "Even if you hadn't investigated darkness, I would have come to see you anyway. Of course I don't mind."
