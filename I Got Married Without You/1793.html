Chapter 1793 Eva had been missing Amelia

She had a fever and was sweating, her seaweed-like hair was soaked, she looked as if she had just been pulled out of the sea.

Seeing Amelia in this state, Eva felt extremely sorry for her, she lifted the hair from her forehead and used a towel to wipe the sweat away.

It had been over two months since Amelia left a letter and disappeared overnight, breaking all her promises, and Eva was both angry and worried.

She had been through these two months either lying in bed or crying.

Eva had even prepared for the worst, but still refused to believe it.

Fortunately, Johnny came back and told her that Amelia was still alive, as was James, but they were in danger.

This allowed her to finally get a good night's sleep.

She knew Johnny's words were cautious, but for Eva, who had been waiting anxiously, it was enough.

After all, as long as Amelia was alive, everything was fine.

Eva was extremely gentle, repeatedly wiping Amelia's forehead, face, neck, and palms. She continued until Amelia's body temperature gradually went down, and only then did Eva breathe a sigh of relief.

Amelia was still having a nightmare, but this time, she felt a gentle hand lift her from the sea.

Her tightly furrowed brow gradually relaxed, and under the cool touch, she slept for a while, and soon slowly opened her eyes again.

This time, she didn't see Johnny, but Eva's pretty face.

"Amelia, are you awake?"

Eva saw Amelia open her eyes and quickly put down the towel in her hand to check on her.

"How are you feeling now?"

After gently shaking her head, Amelia moistened her dry lips.

"Eva, I'm sorry for making you worry."

Hearing that, Eva felt a lot less angry.

She raised her hand and deliberately pushed Amelia's shoulder.

"You're pregnant, but you went to such a dangerous place. You didn't consider your own safety, or our worries!"

Hearing Eva scold her like an older sister, Amelia curved her lips into a gentle smile.

"Eva, have you missed me?"

Eva had intended to pretend to be angry, but upon hearing this, she couldn't help but cry.

"I missed you much."

Amelia also couldn't help but shed tears. "Eva, you are the best to me."

"We grew up together, who could compare to our childhood bond? How could I not be the best to you?"

Amelia held back her tears and then burst into tears.

"Don't cry, you look ugly when you cry."

"You don't look good when you cry either."

After they finished speaking, they cried and laughed. Then Eva approached her and hugged Amelia.

"In any case, you're back, and that's what matters."

After a moment of silence, Amelia took a deep breath. "James divorced me."

"Divorced?" Eva was incredulous when she heard this news.

She felt that even if Paul divorced her, James would not divorce Amelia.

After all, James's love for Amelia was incomparable, so how could he divorce her?

But the emotions in Amelia's eyes told her that they had indeed divorced.

"What... what happened?"

Amelia turned over and lay on her side, placing her hands under her cheek.

"Eva, should I not have gone to find James?"

Eva, who knew nothing, was a little confused. "There's nothing you should or shouldn't do. How could you not go to find someone you love who has disappeared? "

Eva was now with Paul, building a home together, and although it hadn’t been long, she felt quite dependent on him.

She thought that if Paul were to disappear one day, she would also go to find him without hesitation.

He was her beloved person and her husband, so how could she do nothing when he was in danger?
