Chapter 1921 She must be in so much pain

The staggering man grabbed Amelia's cold hand and pressed it against his own face.

Her fingertips were covered in blood, quickly staining James' pale face red.

James lowered his gaze, slowly taking in her bloody body. She must be in so much pain, he thought.

For the first time, he realized that caring for someone meant more than just feeling sorry for them.

It meant wanting to take their pain and suffering upon oneself.

If it were possible, he would take on all of her burdens.

He was willing to endure any punishment as long as it meant sparing Amelia from torment. He would do anything, even die for her.

James lowered his heavy and exhausted face, holding onto Amelia's hand as he leaned over the operating table.

His tall and imposing figure bent down in a posture resembling a penitent worshipper.

Even from outside the operating room, one could see the trembling of the man towering above.

No one could guess if he was crying at that moment, but everyone knew that he was suffering beyond words.

To be alive yet wishing for death, that was the greatest agony.

At the same time, James had experienced the life and death struggles of his wife and child.

No one knew how he had endured these past twenty hours, but they knew that someone like him must be in immense pain.

Eva cried out in anguish, her heart torn apart. If it weren't for Paul supporting her, she would have collapsed on the ground.

Rebecca, hearing Eva's cries, reached out and tightly held her hand, silently giving her strength.

The Evans family sympathized with Amelia's ordeal and felt for James' helplessness.

After learning about their own grandfather's actions, they felt a chill in their hearts.

Knowing that Amelia was James' life, Dominic still went ahead with his plan. Such a grandfather was rare in the world, and how could his grandchildren not feel chilled by his actions?

Compared to the Evans family's concern for James, the Lawrence family's concern was solely for Amelia, especially Dylan.

Just a few days ago, they had met at the airport, embraced each other, and Amelia had even asked him to name their child.

How could it be that in such a short time, seeing his sister again would be in such a bloody state?

Dylan, with a gunshot wound in his chest, could hardly believe his eyes.

He grabbed Owen and Eugene's hands, asking them to support him as he walked into the operating room. Being closer now, he could see even more clearly.

When he saw that Amelia's hair, which used to cascade down to her waist like seaweed, had been shaved off, Dylan's eyes turned red.

He knew that his sister loved her appearance, but now, her once beautiful hair was gone.

Her whole body was covered in wounds, with no good place left, just blood everywhere.

Moreover, the all-capable old doctor by their grandfather's side said that Amelia was in a deep coma and essentially beyond saving.

She was his sister, whom he had only recently recognized, how could she just be gone like that?

Matty felt heartbroken, leaning on his cane, turning away and not wanting to stay here.

The smell of his granddaughter's blood and the sight of her being neither alive nor dead were unbearable.

Outside the door, Carson saw Matty leave and his gaze shifted back to Amelia. Seeing her condition, he couldn't help but wonder about Sumasa's daughter.

She truly had a tumultuous fate. He just didn't know if she would be as lucky this time as she was when she survived the sea incident.

As he thought this, Carson noticed Johnny, a young man of similar height to James, who had followed him into the operating room when James entered.

But it seemed that Johnny didn't know how to approach Amelia, so he could only stand there like a statue, not far from the operating table.

Carson didn't go in, so he couldn't see Johnny's expression, but he could see his trembling figure swaying several times.

If it weren't for Herb supporting him, Johnny might have been scared unconscious.

Just from that swaying figure, Carson could sense Johnny's thoughts, but it seemed that these thoughts would forever remain hidden in his heart.
