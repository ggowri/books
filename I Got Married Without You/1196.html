Chapter 1196 Amelia, you are really lucky to have escaped (1)

James didn't want to talk about it, and Amelia didn't ask any further. She took the coat handed to her by the servant and helped him put it on. She then considerately tied his tie for him.

When they were living in No.8 Mansion, Amelia used to help him put on his suit jacket and tie before he left, just like a wife would.

At that time, James would fantasize about how great it would be if she were his wife. However, he suppressed the urge to stay and constantly warned himself.

The person she loved was only Ryan, and he was afraid of Gloria finding out and couldn't marry her. Their relationship was destined to be a short-lived marriage where they had each other for a brief moment.

Who would have thought that his unwillingness to give up would eventually make her his wife? Now, everything she does is so beautiful and heartwarming.

"Alright."

After finishing adjusting his shirt collar, Amelia looked up and smiled at him, "Go quickly, I'll be waiting for you at home."

James didn't want her to know about his dirty past, but yesterday he only went to the company and Dominic hurt her, leaving her alone at home. He was worried, so he grabbed her hand and said to her, "Come with me."

Compared to the ugly and bloody past, Amelia's safety was much more important.

Amelia didn't refuse when he wanted to take her with him. She nodded and put her hand in his palm.

She followed James to the prison.

James didn't let her wait in the car, but took her directly to see Gloria.

When she saw Gloria through the thick glass for the first time, Amelia was stunned.

She had never seen Gloria in person, only seen her in the news.

The former chairman and head of the Leinster family is sitting in a wheelchair, holding a book in her hands.

Her surroundings are pleasant, with green trees shading the area. Sunlight filters through the branches, casting dappled shadows on her face.

Her face was not masked or covered in any way, but rather burned and twisted with lines resembling the shape of insects, covering her skin densely.

Even in the sunlight, her appearance was still enough to make one's heart skip a beat...

As if sensing someone looking at her from outside, Gloria's gaze moved away from the book and slowly raised.

When she saw James standing outside the door with his hands in his pockets, her eyes were suddenly filled with hatred.

While Amelia may feel empathy for the burned skin on her face, it was impossible to ignore the deep-seated sinfulness in her eyes.

Her eyes are like those of a murderer hiding in the corner...
