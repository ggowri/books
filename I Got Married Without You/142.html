Chapter 142 The People of Weil Family Is Really Good at

Amelia was still shocked that Jackson would come forward to seek justice for her when she heard Lena put the blame on her.

Amelia didn't want to pursue the matter initially, but Miss Weil's accusations made her angry.

Just as she was about to ask Lena what she had said to her, a flat voice suddenly came from behind.

"Miss Weil, have you been learning to confuse right and wrong in the past few years?"

The man behind her was dressed in a black suit. Standing under the light, he looked a little bright.

When Amelia heard his voice, she knew who he was. She was grateful he stood up for her, but she dared not look back.

James glanced at Jackson, who was holding onto Amelia's hand, with a gloomy expression.

James slowly walked down the stairs and stood in front of Lena,

"I just happened to pass by when Miss Weil humiliated her."

James didn't show her any respect and exposed her lie, causing Lena to pale.

Lena still wanted to argue, but James glanced at her with highly indifferent amorous eyes.

Lena had never seen such a terrifying look before. Even though his eyes were beautiful, the look was enough to afraid her.

Lena was so scared that she immediately shut her mouth. She stood rooted to the spot, not daring to move.

Only then did Harry realize that something had happened to his sister. He quickly pushed through the crowd and rushed over.

Before Harry could ask what had happened to Lena, James gave him an indifferent look.

"The Weil family are really well-educated."

After saying that, James left with his bodyguards.

Watching the show from the side, Paul looked thoughtfully at James's back.

James was indifferent and would never speak up for unimportant people.

This time, he actually stood up to teach Miss Weil a lesson. It was really incredible.

Paul glanced at Amelia, who was standing intimately with Jackson, and his expression darkened slightly.

It must be because of her that James made an exception for her.

He didn't expect this woman to be so capable. Not only could she get Jackson to protect her, but she could also get James to help her.

Paul wanted to go over and teach her a lesson, but after thinking for a while, he gave up.

Even if James didn't want her, he shouldn't be the one to teach her a lesson.

Paul turned around, took his assistant, and left with James.
