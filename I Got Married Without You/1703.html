Chapter 1703 The Lawrence family

When Neil visited the Lawrence family's construction site last time, it was Dylan who received him, so they naturally knew each other and shook hands easily.

"Sorry to keep you waiting."

"No problem."

Dylan replied casually and then looked at Amelia standing next to Neil.

"I didn't expect Miss Clark to look even better in person than in the photos."

A hint of admiration flashed in his eyes, but it quickly disappeared.

This was Amelia's first time meeting Dylan in person and formally assessing him.

Dylan looked really good, with outstanding features that complemented his perfect face shape. His fair skin made him look regal and dignified.

Apart from his looks, he had a graceful and elegant demeanor. Every movement he made exuded charm, like a heir from a well-bred family.

"You have more charisma in person too."

Amelia complimented him, and Dylan smiled, extending his hand towards her.

"Let's formally introduce ourselves. I'm Dylan."

Amelia also shook his hand.

"I'm Skender."

Dylan nodded politely, released her hand, and invited her and Neil to get in the car.

Normally, a family like his would have a driver, but Dylan personally drove without any airs.

"My grandfather is strict with us. He taught us from a young age to be low-key and not too extravagant. So, for things within our capability, we usually do them ourselves."

After explaining this, Dylan glanced at Amelia and smiled. "Although I didn't hire a driver, it doesn't mean the Lawrence family can't afford the design fees."

His relaxed and humorous tone eased the tension in Amelia, who was pretending to be Skender.

"You're joking. I know very well about your family's wealth and status."

Dylan continued, "I assume Miss Clark has already researched my family before coming, so I won't introduce myself further."

Amelia nodded lightly and didn't respond. Dylan then asked, "Is this your first time in England?"

"Yes, it is. I've never been here before."

Dylan looked at her and then opened the car windows and sunroof.

"Feel the breeze and enjoy the scenery. We'll be at my house soon."

His attentive care towards women seemed to be something he had grown up with. Dylan's grandfather, Matty, must have been a kind old gentleman as well.

Dylan's car moved slowly on the wide road, surrounded by lush trees, with the refreshing scent of nature filling the air.

Amelia couldn't help but turn her head to look at the flowers and trees outside the window. Occasionally, she glanced at the rearview mirror, following their black luxury car closely.

Dylan noticed and asked, "Why didn't you bring your friend with you?"

He looked at Amelia teasingly, "Are you afraid the Lawrence family will eat people?"

Amelia felt a bit embarrassed that he had found out, but she quickly responded, "You know I rarely go out to survey construction sites myself. Occasionally, when I do, it's natural to be cautious about dangers."

She didn't mention who was following them but only implied that there was a risk, showing her prudence.

But Dylan didn't mind, "For a renowned architect like Miss Clark, it's indeed necessary to hire bodyguards."

Amelia smiled and replied, "Safety first, right?"

Dylan returned the smile and didn't say anything more.

Soon, the car passed through several European-style towns and stopped in front of a European-style estate.

It was a bit secluded, but the scenery was beautiful. There was a stream in front of the estate, and on the opposite side, there was a lush green hill.

The bright sun shone down, casting a golden light. The blue sky and white clouds, purified by the light, added a touch of freshness to the scenery.

The estate, nestled in such a picturesque setting, was neither big nor small, just as Dylan had described, low-key yet not overly luxurious.

After Amelia got out of the car and looked around, Dylan introduced, "This is where my grandfather's first love used to live. After retiring, he moved here."

Amelia's gaze shifted to Dylan, who was leading them towards the lawn. "Your grandfather's first love, is she your grandmother?"

Dylan shook his head. "My grandmother passed away a long time ago."

Amelia was briefly stunned. That meant the person Matty cherished wasn't his wife, but his former first love.
