Chapter 1861: Daley Parsons

"Did you figure I'd make it back alive?" James' dark eyes were icy, his voice low.

"With your skills, surviving wasn't a long shot," Master Davies replied, handing his cane to Andrew and settling on the sofa.

"So why are you here?"

"Since you know about Darkness, might as well tell you."

"And if I didn't know it existed, were you planning on keeping it from me forever?"

"Darkness isn't exactly a charity," he dodged the question, his tone indifferent.

The setting sun painted James in a golden hue, but the man bathed in light felt no warmth.

"So you're not just keeping secrets, you're lying. Think I'm that easy to manipulate?" James questioned coldly.

"I've invested more in you than in Holden and Emelyn, there's no deception in that. Why would I manipulate you?" Master Davies denied.

"Really?" James scoffed.

Master Davies didn't respond. James strode forward, looking down at him, "S existed long before, but you erased all evidence, claiming it was created for me. Isn't that deception?"

Faced with the man bathed in light, like a judge, Master Davies hesitated, "Yes, I did deceive you. But I thought it didn't concern you, so I didn't tell you."

James thought he'd be unaffected by Master Davies' confession, but hearing it made him disappointed, "Is it irrelevant to me, or am I just not useful enough for you to tell me the truth?"

Master Davies fell silent, "James, Darkness was established by my father's generation. It's history, so there's no need to use you."

"But you knew Darkness killed Sophia, leading Matty to form Erebus and hunt S. Isn't that using me?" James retorted coldly.

"You're definitely my protégé, piecing together the whole picture from scraps of info. You're ready to stand on your own," Master Davies didn't deny, he merely stated.

"Thanks for the training, but I'm not quite there yet. After all, I only know part of the story. I still need you to fill in the details," James' voice was icy.

"Even in a war of words, you don't give an inch. Impressive," Master Davies chuckled.

After his praise, Master Davies shifted his gaze from James' face to the couch across from him.

"Let's sit and chat."

The deep, cool man stood still for a few seconds before taking a step and sitting across from Master Davies.

As he sat down, a ray of sunshine separated the two. One sat in the dark, the other bathed in sunlight.

The man in the sunlight looked as if he was edged with gold, making Master Davies seem even more bleak.

Master Davies looked at James across from him, slowly touching his black hat brim, and began in a faint voice.

"Before Sophia met Matty, she had a first love, Daley Parsons, the rising star of the Parsons family of North America."

"My father said Daley was renowned handsome."

"Unfortunately, a rising star is no match for the overshadowing Lawrence Family. Daley was crushed in the mud when he met Matty."

"Matty's father always told him to take what he wanted, so Matty, being greedy by nature, took Sophia from Daley."

"Daley couldn't bear it, harassed Sophia a few times, even shot at Matty. Matty, who had never been shot before, had a killing intent after getting shot."

"With a swipe of his knife, the beautiful young Daley died in a sewage ditch. One of the witnesses was my aunt, Zelene Davies."

"She had to work hard to marry Matty, like leaving Sophia's belongings to create the illusion that Sophia killed Daley. With just a little push, Sophia was hit with a disaster."

"Daley's father, Bernard Parsons, never liked Sophia. He thought she abandoned Daley to climb the Lawrence Family ladder, even killed Daley, and blamed the pain of losing his son on Sophia."

"Bernard, the so-called rising star, was actually just a thug on the streets of North America. The aristocratic families there looked down on him for gaining status through violence."

"Even though he was despised, Bernard was quite capable of gathering thugs. After finding out his son was 'killed by Sophia,' he formed the Darkness to hunt down their family."
