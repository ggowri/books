Chapter 1679 Brother-in-law

James glanced at him indifferently as he walked towards the company, coldly saying, "I'm not interested in your good news."

Paul, accustomed to this, caught up with his pace and said nonchalantly, "James, guess what I did yesterday?"

The man exuding a cold aura climbed the stairs and replied, "I don't know, but I do know that you didn't even care about the company in order to win back Ms. Miller."

Paul, feeling somewhat guilty for not having come to the company during this time, reassured him, "James, I promise to come to the company on time from now on, never absent."

After making his promise, Paul leaned closer to James and whispered in his ear, "Do you know why?"

Feeling annoyed by his chatter, James replied coldly, "Did you win Ms. Miller back?"

Paul's face lit up with joy, "Not only did I win her back, she won't ever leave again."

Having already guessed the good news, James played along and asked, "So, you didn't bring her home and she won't leave? How is that possible?"

When the topic was thrown back at him, Paul's smile became even more cheerful, "I married her. We just got our marriage certificate yesterday. Surprised?"

To his disappointment, James didn't react at all. He didn't even look at him. Paul was puzzled.

"James, don't you feel surprised at all?"

"Surprised."

"I knew it."

Suddenly, James turned his head and looked at Paul.

"What's that look on your face, James? Are you looking down on me?"

James didn't say anything. He just withdrew his gaze and walked towards the elevator. Paul, feeling scorned, quickly caught up with him.

"I married Eva. Shouldn't you call me brother-in-law?"

This remark finally got a reaction from James. He paused his steps.

He turned his tall and straight body, lowered his gaze, and stared at Paul.

"What did you just call me?"

"Brother-in-law. Did I say it wrong?"

In the face of Paul's provocation, James gave him a deep look.

"Do you want to die, or do you want to be removed from the Evans Family?"

"Can I choose neither?"

James tilted his head slightly, his eyes like stars, locked onto Paul in a strange and intimidating way.

"I take back what I just said. Pretend you didn't hear anything."

Paul, who could be flexible and adaptable, acted dumb and pressed the elevator button, then made a welcoming gesture with a smile.

"James, didn't you say we were going to have a meeting? Come in."

Only then did James avert his gaze from staring at him, step into the elevator.

There was one more piece of news that Paul hadn't told James, and it was weighing on his mind.

He secretly glanced at the man standing in the elevator, resembling a sculpture made of ice.

Between being frozen to death and being suffocated by secrets, Paul chose the former without hesitation.

"Oh, by the way, James, let me tell you another piece of good news. Eva is pregnant. I'm going to be a father!"

If the news of their marriage was something James had already anticipated, then the news of the pregnancy was a shock to him.

James' exquisite and ethereal face gradually turned grim.

Andrew, who got married after him, would become a father before him.

Paul, who got married after Andrew, would also become a father before him.

Would it be Zack's turn next?

When James had a gloomy expression on his face, Paul, who was overly happy, nudged his arm with his elbow.

"James, if it weren't for you convincing my mom to apologize to Eva, I would still be tearing down the house right now. I wouldn't have known about the child."

James glanced at him and ignored him.

Paul didn't notice and continued talking to himself.

"Here's the thing, to show my gratitude to you, I'll let you choose the name for my child."

James rolled his eyes and said indifferently, "The child hasn't been born yet, we don't even know the gender. How can we choose a name?"

Paul didn't mind at all, "Then let's choose one for a boy and one for a girl. That should be enough, right?"

James, visibly annoyed, glanced at him, "Do you really want me to choose?"

"Of course!"

Without thinking, James blurted out, "For a boy, Ken. For a girl, Barbie."

Paul: ... That's too random of a name!
