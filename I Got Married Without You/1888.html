Chapter 1888 Ryan is quite nice

Ryan, wearing a white shirt, stood outside like a breath of fresh air, looking clean and refreshed.

Compared to his previous appearance in a wheelchair, he was now standing and very attractive.

Amelia's gentle gaze moved from his serene and elegant face to his legs.

Seeing his legs, able to stand straight and even walk steadily, the guilt that had been weighing on her heart gradually dissipated.

Ryan had finally stood up and no longer had to spend his life in a wheelchair.

From now on, he could live a normal life.

Amelia's eyes filled with a contented smile, but she refrained from looking at Ryan too much in the presence of James.

She simply nodded at him and then averted her gaze.

\Ryan also avoided looking at Amelia too much.

If he couldn't let go of the missed feelings, he would have to suppress them deep inside.

After all, she was now married and soon to be a mother.

Even though she had been tormented by countless nightmares, Ryan knew he had to restrain himself.

He followed Edith and approached Eva.

Amelia stood up to give them space, holding the baby in her arms.

James, sitting on the couch, glanced at the passing pair without turning his head, showing a hint of indifference.

Because Amelia had come to his side and sat down, tugging at his sleeve to show him how cute the sleeping baby was.

At that moment, Amelia was completely focused on the baby, with no trace of Ryan in her eyes.

It was as if Ryan's presence no longer stirred any emotions within her.

James, with his eyes fixed on Amelia's face, instinctively curved his lips and, for the first time, reached out his finger to touch Ken's little mouth.

As his finger touched the soft lips of the baby, the ice in James' eyes gradually melted.

At the same time, he heard Amelia's gentle voice saying, "Isn't he adorable?"

James raised an eyebrow and lightly tapped his forehead. "Adorably ugly."

Amelia playfully scolded him, "Don't call other people's children ugly, or our child might turn out ugly too."

James arrogantly sneered, "How could our child be ugly when both you and I are so good-looking?"

Although their conversation was quiet, Ryan overheard every word.

He could sense how happy Amelia was without even looking at her, just by her presence.

Ryan's gaze fell to the baby girl in Eva's arms. "Can I hold the baby?"

"Of course." Eva handed the baby to him.

As Ryan held the baby, the suppressed emotions in his eyes gradually dissipated.

He turned around and happened to see Amelia holding the baby boy.

She had lowered her head and didn't look at him, but when he caught a glimpse of her profile bathed in sunlight, he suddenly remembered what Amelia had said before.

She had said, "Ryan, when we get married, let's have a pair of twins. You can hold the girl, and I'll hold the boy. Then, as we grow old, we can watch them grow up together. How does that sound?"

With the baby in his arms, Ryan snapped back to reality.

He coincidentally met James' distant and indifferent gaze, and quickly averted his eyes as if feeling guilty.

For that moment, they had fulfilled their past promise, and that was enough, even if they were holding each other's children...

Ryan collected his thoughts and, after holding the baby for a while, asked about the names of the two children.

They chatted for a while before he left, sending many supplements as a gift.

After Ryan left, Amelia looked at him discreetly.

Although he could now walk, he still suffered from severe depression, and she didn't know if her deliberate distance was right or wrong.

She couldn't find an answer, but Edith provided one: "Ryan's legs may have healed, but his depression is still quite severe. There was a time when the pain caused by his leg treatment almost drove him to suicide, but he said..."

To spare Amelia any psychological burden, Edith assured her that he would keep on living.

However, even saying that could be a burden.

Edith omitted the rest and simply said, "Don't worry, he will keep on living."

Amelia nodded gently and said to Edith, "Please help him as much as you can."

Edith looked thoughtful and said to Amelia, "If I were to say that after spending time with Ryan, I feel that he's quite nice, would you find that strange?"

As Edith said this, Anthony, who had come to congratulate them, happened to be at the entrance of the delivery room.

He heard Edith's words loud and clear, and his eyes suddenly lost their color.
