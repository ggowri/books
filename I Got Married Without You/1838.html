Chapter 1838 Divorce

"Can't talk 'cause of cameras?" Amelia asked, hinting at Matty to turn them off. But James pulled her back.

"No cameras in Matty's private room, Amelia."

The only camera-free space in Ace. Matty kept it pry-free.

"Soundproof? Afraid he might overhear..." she glanced at the metallic surroundings.

James smirked at her paranoia. "It's soundproof. And he doesn't know me, why eavesdrop?"

Amelia thought Matty knew James from Emily, hence the restrictions. But James denied it, comforting her.

"If he knew me, would I be safe here?"

"But..."

"Amelia, I'm hiding from Emily who's using my identity. She's keeping me in Erebus, cut off from everyone. I'm trying to find Erebus' creator to return to you."

He said this to clear Amelia's doubts so they could part in peace.

James knew hints or guesses wouldn't help. Matty needed them apart, or he'd be trapped and their unborn child would be at risk.

His safety wasn't important, but the child Amelia fought to conceive was.

The sight of James threatened by Emily saddened Amelia. She hugged him tight.

"James, knowing Erebus' founder, you can leave. I'll deal with Emily."

As she leaned into him, melting into his arms, James felt his icy heart thaw. He held her tighter, unable to resist.

She tried to protect him, but he felt guilty. He was pushing her away in his way.

"Amelia."

"I'm here."

Her eyes sparkled with joy; she was glad he called her by her name again.

Gazing at Amelia, James struggled to say the harsh truth.

But now, he had to change the situation. Even if it hurt, he had to be cruel.

"I know Erebus' founder now, I'll find a way out, don't worry."

"Mmm."

Amelia hugged James tighter, relieved that he was okay.

"There's something else I need to tell you."

At this, Amelia looked up at him, her eyes twinkling with anticipation.

"Go ahead."

"The divorce papers from before... they're legit," he steeled himself.

"What?" her smile faded to a sad grimace.

His heart raced and fear of losing her gripped him. He hugged her tighter.

She stared, then pushed him off, meeting his gaze.

"Explain."

His heart sank as she pushed him away, the finality of it all ringing in his ears.

Once the words were said, they couldn't be unsaid. He was left with the pain, watching her from afar, divided by a sea of hurdles.

"We're officially divorced."
