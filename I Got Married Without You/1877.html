Chapter 1877 I’ll shoot myself

James quickly shot and killed one of the black-clad individuals.

As he was about to adjust his aim towards the other one, Amelia's body was covered in red dots.

At the same time, the gun held by the other black-clad person was pressed against Amelia's temple.

"Mr. Evans," one of the operators spoke, holding a long gun, as they emerged slowly from the escape pod inside the room.

"If you comply with 2-5's demand, we will release your wife. Otherwise..."

As the operator's words fell, the gun pressed against Amelia's head was forcefully tightened.

"Don't hurt her!" James exclaimed, his heart racing with fear. Clearly, his concern for Amelia had surpassed his own life.

Even if Amelia were to suffer the slightest injury, he would be extremely anxious.

"Come at me, but don't touch her," James said, his eyes lacking the decisiveness he had shown when he shot 2-5.

"We can spare her, but you must commit suicide in front of us," one of the operators stated.

These words struck Amelia like a fatal blow. She gazed at James's handsome face and shook her head.

"Don't do something foolish," she pleaded. He had already been injured many times for her sake.

She didn't want him to be so reckless. It would only cause her pain.

Amelia's clear eyes revealed her love and concern, warming James's heart.

"Don't worry, I'll be fine," he reassured her, slowly shifting his gaze away from her affectionate eyes to the three operators.

"I can apologize, but I cannot commit suicide," James declared.

During the process of eliminating the cancerous elements in the business world, S organization has indeed done some questionable things.

For example, like how Emelyn dealt with Jessica, sending her off to Myanmar to face her own demise.

Another example is how he handled Kevin, sending him to prison for a lesson.

However, since taking over, he has never allowed his members to engage in acts of torture or killing.

James admitted that S Organization was guilty, but it did not warrant a death sentence.

However, as the leader of S, he knew he had to pay a price no matter what.

He was willing to apologize and atone for the sins accumulated by S over the years.

After all, he had learned from his experiences that actions have consequences.

"I'll fire a shot as an apology, and you release her," James said, raising the gun in his hand and aiming it at his own shoulder.

The tall and upright man didn't even blink his eyes as he pulled the trigger.

Bang! The deafening sound of the gunshot echoed through the entire corridor and the entire escape pod.

It shattered Amelia's eardrums and her heart. She looked at the man who had been shot but still stood tall.

"Don't be afraid," he said, his voice reaching Amelia's ears after a long delay.

In her blurred vision, all she could see was blood.

The dark red blood flowed from James's shoulder, staining his white shirt beneath his suit jacket.

Each drop of blood that fell from his shirt hammered against Amelia's heart.

Amelia's tears gushed out along with the falling blood.

"James!" she cried out, struggling desperately against the grip of the black-clad person restraining her.

But she was completely immobilized.

Amelia realized at that moment that her existence was James's fatal weakness.

James saw the self-blame in her eyes, but he only shook his head to comfort her.

He quickly changed the direction of his gun and, taking advantage of the moment when the three operators were distracted, shot and killed one of them with a single bullet to the head.

He then swiftly pulled out a golden knife and threw it at one of the remaining operators, aiming for their forehead.

Within a span of five seconds, James killed two people.

The remaining two operators quickly regained their composure and aimed their guns at Amelia.

But before they could pull the triggers, James had already crossed the bulletproof glass door and embraced Amelia, shielding her from harm.

The two bullets missed their mark and hit the wall instead. The two operators' faces turned pale, but they attempted to shoot again.

However, Matty had already killed them both.

As the two operators fell to the ground, Matty holstered his gun and turned to look at the tightly embraced couple.

When his gaze fell upon James's shoulder, his brows furrowed slightly.

He hadn't expected James to be so daring.

With his marksmanship, he could have killed those individuals without firing that shot. But he did it as an apology.

Matty's initial disdainful gaze gradually transformed into admiration. It was clear that a man like James was deserving of Amelia's love.
