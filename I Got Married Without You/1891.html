Chapter 1891 Amelia treated Johnny differently

When Zack was mentioned, James's cold eyes were tinged with a haze of regret that seemed endless.

He remained still for a long time, until he managed to hide the pain in his eyes.

Then he looked up and met Johnny's serious gaze.

"What's the name of the person who looks like Zack?" Johnny asked.

"Zenith," James nodded lightly.

"I happened to have some business in my home country, so I thought I'd let you know," Johnny said.

In fact, a phone call would have sufficed to inform Johnny about this matter, but Johnny seemed to have his own reasons for making the trip.

James, dressed in a dark coat and exuding a faint aura of melancholy, could easily see through Johnny's intentions.

It was just that Johnny was not good at controlling his emotions and always acted on impulse.

During this period, Johnny had a strange dream.

He dreamt of Amelia stumbling and staggering towards him, and he caught her firmly, saying, "Alright, I'll take you away."

After this dream, Johnny sat by the bedside, staring at the desolate scenery outside the window, unable to forget it.

Because of this dream, the heart that should have been put down was once again stirred.

Now, he lowered his gaze and looked at the intertwined fingers of the two people, which reminded him that it was just a dream, a dream that he had longed for.

"Thank you," James said, his heavy heart slowly retracting from the images in the snake pit.

When he looked at Johnny again, he sensed that Johnny was thinking about something.

Following Johnny's gaze, he looked at his own hand holding Amelia's, and for a moment, he felt a hint of covetousness.

"Mr. Brook," a cold voice as icy as snow interrupted Johnny's thoughts.

He immediately snapped back to reality.

He looked up and met James's indifferent gaze.

"Thank you for saving my wife and for coming all the way back to inform me," Johnny said.

"It was nothing, just a small favor," James replied.

After Johnny finished speaking, he walked around the front of the car and opened the passenger door, intending to get in.

But James's indifferent voice slowly sounded in his ears.

"Aren't you staying for dinner?"

Johnny, who was holding onto the car door with one hand, gave him a disdainful look.

"Do I look like someone who doesn't have food to eat? Do I need to stay at your house for dinner?"

"Well then, let me give you a big gift as a token of gratitude," Amelia said.

Johnny's temper improved a bit, but he was still not very happy.

"When I saved you, I must have been out of my mind. I won't save you again if it happens another time. So stop with all the thanks, it's annoying."

Looking at Johnny's impatient expression, Amelia was taken aback.

He seemed indifferent, but what she wanted to thank him for was not just saving her, but also the drawer of food he had left for her.

In that difficult environment, every apple and every glass of milk Johnny gave her was unforgettable.

Amelia cherished this kindness in her heart.

Before Johnny got into the car, she sincerely said, "Thank you, Johnny."

The man who had already sat in the car paused for a moment.

In the next second, he pretended as if nothing had happened, fastened his seatbelt, and quickly started the car.

When the car turned around and drove away, Johnny finally dared to openly look through the rearview mirror at the gradually shrinking figure...

He couldn't describe the feeling, but he felt empty inside.

Did he want to have her? Yes, he did. But she didn't belong to him, not even a glance.

In the end, he could only describe it as helplessness.

Johnny kept his feelings for Amelia hidden deep inside, restraining himself from crossing any boundaries.

James followed his lead and didn't mention anything.

But he could also see that Amelia treated Johnny differently, and this difference began with the drawer of food Johnny had left for her.

James saw on the surveillance footage that Amelia cried all night for Johnny and even held onto the apple he had left behind while sitting on the bed.

At that moment, James knew that in Amelia's mind, there was only Johnny, the man who was willing to die for her.

Regardless of whether it was out of gratitude or not, at that moment, Johnny had become someone special to Amelia.

This difference made James feel uncomfortable.

He didn't know how he managed to get through those moments behind the surveillance footage, but fortunately, it was all in the past now.

James squeezed Amelia's hand and brought her back to reality.

"Wife, tomorrow, I'm going to Barcelona," James said, his eyes filled with a heavy emotion.

"You're going to see Zenith?" Amelia asked, meeting James's gaze that was bathed in the light of dusk.

"I'm going to see if Zack has come back," James replied.

Zack was already dead and wouldn't come back, but if someone who looked exactly like him appeared, who wouldn't mistake him for Zack?

Amelia didn't shatter James's hope in his heart.

She simply said calmly, "I'll go with you."

James's lips slightly parted, wanting to stop her, but Amelia continued, "If possible, the three of us should have another meal together, the one you cook, on the ship..."
