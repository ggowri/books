Chapter 1929 James collapsed in the snow

He prayed to the gods and Buddhas, offering his life in exchange for Amelia's.

But now, instead of taking his life, the gods and Buddhas had taken away Amelia's memories, waking her up. It was a profitable deal.

Although he tried to console himself with this thought, James couldn't help but laugh.

It felt like everything he had experienced in the past decade was nothing but a futile dream...

Looking at Amelia's thin and exhausted face, her bloodshot eyes, and her bitter smile, Amelia's heart tightened uncomfortably.

She thought it was her heart causing the discomfort and pressed her hand against it. The strange pain quickly dissipated.

At that moment, Ryan took the opportunity to release her other hand.

"Amelia, your husband is back. Talk to him. I'll leave now and come back to see you another day."

Anxious, Amelia called out to Ryan, stopping him in his tracks. "Ryan, don't leave. I don't know him. Leave me alone, I'm scared."

James, still maintaining his position on the hospital bed, remained silent for a long time.

He lowered his thick eyelashes, looking at the woman beneath him, filled with fear. "Are you scared of me?"

Amelia wasn't scared of him, but rather the unfamiliar feeling.

However, she didn't know how to express it, so she chose to remain silent and even directed her pleading eyes towards Ryan.

James had never witnessed how much she loved Ryan before. He had only heard her call his name in her sleep.

Now, seeing it with his own eyes, James suddenly lost all his faith.

He clenched his fingers on the bedsheet, digging his nails into his skin until they drew blood.

Suppressing the pain, he slowly straightened his body...

Feeling embarrassed and hopeless, James looked at Ryan. "Since she's scared of me, you should stay and accompany her. I... will leave."

As he said these words, his eyes suddenly became moist.

Perhaps afraid of being seen, James forced his aching knees to support his unsteady body, leaned against the wall, and turned to leave.

Watching his faltering figure, Amelia's heart ached once again. This time, she could deeply feel that it was hurting for James. But...

She looked up at Ryan, who had a heavy expression, and felt that her intuition was correct.

She could be sure that she loved Ryan, and she couldn't regress along with her memories, right?

Amelia couldn't understand. Her nerves were splitting apart. "Ryan, can you tell me what exactly happened between me and him?"

Ryan withdrew his gaze and looked at Amelia, who was full of confusion. "The things between you and him can't be explained in just a few words. But you love him very much. You're willing to risk your life for him."

Although Amelia couldn't believe it, she didn't refute Ryan's words.

She only said, "I don't feel love for him now. I only feel that I love you. So, Ryan, no matter what the reason is for our separation, as long as you haven't let go of me, we still have a chance to start over."

The man leaning against the wall outside the hospital room heard these words. His exhausted body, worn out from days of hard work, trembled uncontrollably.

James had rushed into the hospital without even putting on his coat, braving the wind and snow, only to find out...

Amelia had forgotten about him. She only remembered Ryan and loved Ryan.

But what about their Mr. Evans? Would their years of love be reduced to nothing because of a bout of amnesia?

Inside the room, Ryan pulled out a chair in front of the hospital bed, seemingly wanting to say something to Amelia.

But James no longer had the mood to listen. He lifted his painful and unsteady steps, swaying as he walked out of the hospital.

Andrew and Peter wanted to follow, but James raised his hand, and they understood that he wanted some time alone. They stopped in their tracks.

James, enduring the pain that was about to shatter his body, leaned against the wall and walked away step by step.

He stumbled out of the hospital. The snow outside was heavier than before, covering the cold ground in an instant...

Standing at the hospital entrance, he looked up at the snowflakes falling from the sky and suddenly felt an overwhelming sense of loneliness.

This loneliness emanated from within, making it unbearable for him. His vision went black...

His tall and upright figure collapsed onto the snowy ground.

The pure white snow, like reed flowers, fluttered and fell, melting bit by bit on the man's flawless face.

Even though he was only wearing a thin suit and shirt, he couldn't feel the slightest coldness.

Perhaps his heart was already cold enough, so the physical coldness was insignificant.

Before his consciousness completely dissipated, he stared intently at the falling snow and said in his heart, "Amelia, I can't hold on anymore. If you remember me and I'm no longer here, please don't blame me..."
