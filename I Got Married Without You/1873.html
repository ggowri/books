Chapter 1873 Amelia, I'm here to take you home

James seemed to be able to read Matty's mind, his deep eyes scanning the people behind him.

Holden and Emelyn's footsteps gradually slowed down, and the others also paused, not taking another step forward.

Matty, who was now completely defeated, had no strategies left even if he used Amelia as a threat.

They would wait for James to enter first, then destroy the surveillance cameras and follow him.

They had ways to deal with Matty, so it didn't matter if James went in alone.

After entering the password, the square white wall quickly opened from both sides.

James took elegant steps and entered, and the automatic door quickly returned to its original state.

The dark corridor lit up with lights, leading to the elevator.

James walked along the cement path, crossed the corridor, and approached the elevator step by step.

After descending five floors in the elevator, a faint light shone through the glass.

It was the incandescent light refracted on the elevator glass from the emergency room, slightly dazzling.

James faced the light and looked at Matty standing in the corridor.

There were numerous infrared beams between the two, and if James took one step forward, the hidden bullets would pierce him.

"Sorry, since we are enemies, I naturally have to be cautious," said Matty with false politeness.

Facing Matty's false politeness, James ignored him and looked around indifferently, beyond the infrared beams. "Where is Amelia?"

Matty raised his hand and pressed the control panel to open Amelia's room.

Amelia, sitting on the bed, heard the noise at the door and suddenly looked up.

The door was open, but unfortunately, at the moment the door opened, a bulletproof glass door quickly descended.

Amelia was still locked inside, but at this moment, she could see the outside environment and James standing in the corridor.

When the door opened, he also looked up and saw her.

Their eyes met, and they didn't move.

Amelia stared at James for a while, then forced herself to stand up and walked to the door.

She didn't say anything, just raised her teary eyes and quietly looked at the man whose steps were blocked not far away.

At this moment, James seemed to see her longing for him in her eyes, and warmth surged in his heart.

"Amelia, I'm here to take you home."

Hearing James' familiar and pleasant voice, Amelia curved her pale lips and showed a gentle and serene smile.

"Okay."

Her agreement meant that she was willing to turn back.

So this time, no matter what, he would take her away.

James, with a hint of a smile in his dark eyes, looked at Amelia for a long time before reluctantly shifting his gaze to Matty across from him.

"Release her, and I will tell you everything I know."

When James looked at Matty, the tenderness in his eyes evaporated, replaced by a chilling gaze.

"From your tone, it seems that you have more information than just the founder of S organization?"

"I know who killed your wife."

Matty's heart suddenly tightened. "How do you know?"

This time, James didn't reply, just looked at the woman standing behind the glass door.

"Release her first."

Seeing the handsome and noble face in front of him, Matty suppressed his eagerness to know the truth and continued to restrain James.

"At this point, it's difficult for me and her to repair our relationship. I will definitely give her back to you. Just say it."

From Matty's words, it seemed that he had made up his mind to perish together with the founder of S organization, which was why he said such resolute words.

Now that he had completely fallen and was defeated, the only way left for Matty was to perish together.

James spoke in a low and cold voice, stating unrelated facts. "The person who cruelly killed Sophia is Daley's father, Bernard, the organizer of darkness."
