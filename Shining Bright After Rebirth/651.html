Chapter 651 Let Diana Go

Diana smiled as she declined Paula's request. "Sorry, but there's nothing left for us to talk about. Our ties are severed. Let's each live our own lives from now on."

She had already said everything that needed to be said and done everything that needed to be done.

After cutting ties, she informed Paula that Jeff had another woman and that Queenie was Jeff's biological daughter, thus repaying the debt of being brought into this world.

Paula knew Diana would never forgive her, but she still wanted to express her gratitude. In a low voice, she said, "Thank you for telling me about Jeff."

Diana shook her head. "There's no need to thank me. I should be thanking you for giving birth to me. I told you the truth back then to repay the debt of you carried me."

Paula cried quietly, realizing the cruel reality.

If it hadn't been for that debt, she would have remained in the dark.

In the previous life, it wasn't until Queenie devoured the family business and her four sons were murdered that she finally learned the truth.

Watching Paula cry her heart out stirred nothing in Diana. Whatever love once existed had been squandered in their previous lives.

Paula suddenly blocked Diana's path, asking in a trembling voice, "Diana, can't you truly forgive me? I know I was wrong."

Diana scoffed, staring at Paula's tearful face. Diana looked somewhat similar to Paula, and she had inherited Paula's beautiful eyes. "But tell me, can anyone genuinely forgive a mother who once wanted to kill her own child?

"Ms. Bishop, you've already killed me once, and I've repaid my debt. I will never forgive you.

"Remember that, and please stop interfering in my life."

Her words were firm.

In her previous life, Paula had been in a position to stop Steven from killing her, but as Diana's mother, she chose the family's interests, her four sons, and her precious adopted daughter over Diana's life.

"What?" Paula was in utter disbelief, shaking her head in pain. "Diana, no! I didn't... Diana, you must have misunderstood something!"

"Misunderstanding?" Diana chuckled bitterly. "There's no misunderstanding. Without my warnings, you would've eventually chosen to kill me for the sake of your beloved adopted daughter.

"I warned you because I wanted to live. So, please, stop disrupting my life."

Her voice was as cold as ice atop a snowy peak.

Paula froze, realizing the harm she had inflicted on Diana in favor of Queenie.

She thought, "In the end, things would have spiraled to me personally killing my own biological daughter, only to be destroyed by Queenie in the end."

Queenie's schemes were vicious, but why had Paula been so bewitched by her?

She had always doted on Queenie and treated Diana like she was invisible.

Queenie was talented, sweet-talking, and knew exactly how to win Paula's favor.

Meanwhile, Diana had always been seen as a bully to Queenie, and Paula had, without question, sided with Queenie every time.

Diana sneered, "Do you get it now? Parental favoritism is like a venomous snake. Your bias fueled Queenie's cruelty towards me because she always knew you'd unconditionally protect her and blindly blame me.

"You raised a viper with a naturally poisonous nature. Isn't that just poetic?"

Her words cut deeper than any knife.

Paula staggered back a few steps, almost collapsing.

Steven, who had quickly rushed over, caught Paula just in time.

Diana didn't look at Steven, turning to leave with Cecil by her side.

Steven watched Diana's retreating figure, lips pressed into a thin line. He let out a cold chuckle. He couldn't help but wonder, "Between Cecil and Alexander, whom will Diana choose?

Will she really rather be with them than want me?"

Paula helplessly watched as Diana walked away.

Noticing it was Steven who was holding her up, she felt a surge of anger. "Steven, is this what you and Queenie wanted? My Barnes family was completely ruined. I beg you, let Diana go."
