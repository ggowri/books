"Shadow Terror Fiend!" Diana murmured, taking a deep breath to steady herself.

She continued, "What exactly is a Shadow Terror Fiend? I can't say for sure. I've never seen one myself. In fact, aside from Lord Malevolence, no one has truly laid eyes on a Shadow Terror Fiend—everyone else who has encountered one is dead!"

"Even Lord Malevolence was gravely injured by it!"

"The legends about the Shadow Terror Fiend come from those close to him, and it's said that using the Malevolence Airship above Divine Peak could attract its attention."

"The Shadow Terror Fiend has no physical form, just a terrifying shadow. Even the weakest among them could effortlessly annihilate an Ultimate Malevolence Airship. The powerful defenses of a Supreme Malevolence Airship can barely withstand a few of its attacks."

"And if you're up against a strong Shadow Terror Fiend? Forget it—no Supreme Malevolence Airship can survive that! A regular Deity King warrior wouldn't stand a chance and would be obliterated in an instant. To this day, aside from Lord Malevolence, no one has survived an attack from a Shadow Terror Fiend."

"Well, there might be one exception—the God of Night from the Human Community!"

"Rumor has it he also survived an encounter, but he was injured worse than Lord Malevolence!"

As Diana finished speaking, Osborne's eyes widened in disbelief.

"I knew that Lord Malevolence and the God of Night had been injured on Divine Peak, but I thought they were overwhelmed by the Deathborn army!"

"So it was the Shadow Terror Fiend?"

"What on earth is that thing? Even top-tier warriors like Lord Malevolence and the God of Night couldn't handle it and ended up severely wounded."

Osborne's mind raced, his respect for Divine Peak Nine deepening.

He couldn't shake the feeling that something unspeakably terrifying lurked within its shadows.

Diana added, "The Shadow Terror Fiend isn't exclusive to Divine Peak Nine; every divine peak has its own legends about them. It's said that each peak's Shadow Terror Fiend is unique!"

"Because of these tales, explorers of the divine peaks are extremely cautious; unless absolutely necessary, they won't use the Malevolence Airship."

"As for why using the Malevolence Airship increases the chances of attracting a Shadow Terror Fiend, no one can say for certain. The bottom line is, we should avoid using it unless we have no other choice."

Osborne nodded, absorbing the weight of her words.

"Let's keep moving! After Angelo shook off the Deathborn army with the Malevolence Airship, he immediately stowed it away. It seems he's wary of the Shadow Terror Fiend, too."

With that, Osborne took Diana's hand, and they pressed on, confident that Riley, who was embedded within Angelo's team, would ensure they wouldn't lose their way.

Before long, they caught up with Angelo's group, who were taking a much-needed break.

Though the earlier battle had been brief, they had pushed themselves to the limit to dispatch the Deathborn quickly, leaving them drained.

In a place as perilous as Divine Peak Nine, it was crucial to be at their best, ready for whatever threats might arise.

After a short rest, Angelo rallied the team to continue their journey.

Soon, they approached the vicinity of the second city-level Deathborn nest.

After some deliberation, Angelo decided to take a longer route, steering the team from a distance around the nest, which meant they would traverse an entirely unknown area.

The unknown held infinite possibilities.

Perhaps this region posed no danger at all, with only a few insignificant village-level or town-level Deathborn nests that wouldn't threaten them.

Or they might accidentally stumble into another city-level Deathborn nest—or even the Deathborn Kingdom itself.

Originally, Angelo hadn't planned on taking this route, but the previous encounter with the city-level Deathborn left him puzzled about how they had been discovered and pursued.

So, he resolved to play it safe and detour.

"Let's go! Divine Peak Nine is vast, and not every corner is fraught with danger! Besides, entering an unknown area could lead to unforeseen rewards!"

"Who knows, we might stumble upon the Miracle Elixir or even a massive Celestial Crystal vein, which means we won't need to push deeper!" Angelo encouraged the anxious group.

His words sparked a glimmer of hope in their eyes.

The prospect of finding the Miracle Elixir or Celestial Crystals was tantalizing.

Both were treasures they could only dream of, and only in uncharted territories could they hope to find such riches.

Watching Angelo lead the way into the unknown, Osborne rubbed his chin thoughtfully.

"Should I stick with them or cut ahead and wait? After all, unknown areas bring unknown dangers; there's no need for me to take that risk."

After a moment's contemplation, Osborne shook his head.

"Forget it, I'll follow along. If there's a chance we actually find the Miracle Elixir, it would be foolish to pass up that opportunity!"

With that resolution, Osborne took Diana's hand and pressed on, determined to stick with the group.

But as they entered the unfamiliar territory, he felt a sense of heaviness settle over him.

Even in his parallel space, he had to remain vigilant; the presence of the Shadow Terror Fiend loomed ominously, a reminder that there could be forces lurking about that might sense his concealed existence.

Before long, they arrived at the foot of a towering mountain.
