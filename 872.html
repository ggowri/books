Osborne was momentarily taken aback by Emma's words.

She quickly filled him in on the situation, her eyes darting nervously toward him.

Emma was worried that Osborne might be angry with her.

After all, it was she who had provoked Clarissa, who had insisted on competing with Clarissa, and who had been the one to reveal their relationship to Clarissa.

Emma had once told Osborne that she didn't care about status, about whether he would marry her, or how it might affect his relationship with Clarissa.

But now, it seemed she had broken that promise.

The thought of Osborne being upset, or worse, choosing to distance himself from her, gnawed at Emma.

She knew all too well that in Osborne's heart, her position could never compare to Clarissa's. If it weren't for her forceful method, they probably wouldn't even be together.

Emma's anxiety was evident.

Taking a deep breath, Osborne looked at Emma, who appeared fearful, and couldn't help but smile.

"Don't worry; I'm not mad at you.

"This was bound to happen sooner or later! If anyone's at fault here, it's me for not controlling my own desires," Osborne added, giving her a gentle pat on the head.

"Why don't you head back for now? I need to talk to Clarissa. Since she's already aware of everything, it's time to clear the air. I'll face the consequences of my own mistakes."

Emma's expression softened as she gazed at him, relief washing over her.

"Are you really okay with this?"

Osborne nodded. "Yeah, I'll be fine."

Emma said, "Just make sure you talk things through with Clarissa. If... and I mean if... she can't handle it, you can always choose to let me go!

"I won't cling to you!"

With that, she turned and left, leaving Osborne to watch her retreating figure with a shake of his head.

He tore open a dimensional rift and stepped through.

In the blink of an eye, he found himself in the luxurious mansion where Clarissa lived.

"Osborne! You're finally out!" Clarissa exclaimed, her eyes lighting up as she sensed his presence.

She rushed toward Osborne, throwing herself into his arms and wrapping her arms tightly around his waist.

Osborne froze for a moment. From Clarissa's radiant face, he saw no signs of distress.

Clarissa looked just as she always did, her typically icy demeanor melting into a warm smile.

It was as if she was completely unaware of his situation with Emma, which he knew was impossible.

He gently cupped her face, his expression softening. "Yeah, I'm out! How's life treating you here, Clarissa?"

She nodded enthusiastically. "It's been good! But how was your time away? Did you achieve what you wanted? Are you feeling confident about the Honor Quest Ceremony?"

Clarissa started to say more, but Osborne interrupted her gently, "Clarissa, you know about Emma, right? I'm sorry."

Clarissa's smile suddenly froze, her body stiffening in place.

"Clarissa, I'm sorry, I didn't..." Osborne began, but Clarissa pressed a finger to his lips, silencing him.

She shook her head gently.

"Osborne, there's no need to apologize or explain. I understand everything!"

Without another word, she tugged on his hand, guiding him toward the living room.

Osborne was puzzled.

He thought, "What's going on with her? Is she upset?" Her demeanor left him feeling a bit lost.

Osborne followed her into the spacious living room, where Dylan, Eugen, Donna, and Barlyn were all gathered.

Their faces brightened when they saw the two of them enter hand in hand.

"Osborne! You made it!"

"We heard about the Honor Quest Ceremony. We may not be able to help you much, but we believe in you!"

"Yeah, you've got this!"

Osborne smiled back, still unsure why Clarissa had brought him here.

Clarissa smiled at Eugen, "Grandpa, can you tell Osborne how many wives you've had in your life?"

Eugen was momentarily taken aback. "Why do you want to know that? Don't you already know how many grandmothers you have?"

"I do, but I want to hear it from you," Clarissa insisted.

With a faint smile, Eugen replied, "I've married six wives in my lifetime. I loved them all sincerely, and they treated me well in return. It's a shame, though—I couldn't protect them. They've all left this world now."

A glimmer of nostalgia and sorrow filled his eyes as he spoke.

Osborne listened, struck by the weight of Eugen's words.

Clarissa then turned to Dylan. "Dad, how many wives have you had?"

Dylan rubbed his nose, looking slightly sheepish. "I've had four. Your mother is the fourth. You already know this, right? Why are you asking?"

Clarissa nodded.

She shifted her gaze to Donna. "Aunt Donna, how many wives does Uncle have besides you?"

Donna glanced between Clarissa and Osborne, a knowing look crossing her face.

Yet, she replied, "Your uncle has three wives besides me."

Clarissa nodded again, then turned to Barlyn with a playful grin. "Barlyn, how many wives do you plan to have in the future?"

Barlyn's eyes lit up with excitement. "Well, obviously, the more the merrier!"
