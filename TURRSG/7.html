Chapter 7 Bankrupt

"I want all these assets."

Evander set the list down, his gaze locking onto Gideon. "I'll buy them at double the loan value."

"Double?"

Gideon's eyes lit up, though a flicker of hesitation followed. "But, Mr. Bexley, the Paul family's loan isn't due yet..."

"I'll make it due early."

Evander cut him off, pulling a file from his briefcase and sliding it across the table. "This is additional evidence of Paul Group's tax evasion. It was enough to get the IRS to freeze all their accounts. The bank can then legally repossess the collateral, and I'll buy it from you. Perfectly above board."

Gideon scanned the file, its pages dense with incriminating financial details, and sweat beaded on his forehead.

This evidence was even more damning than what the IRS already had. They were clearly prepared with ruthless precision.

He realized then that Evander wasn't here to negotiate. He was here to drag the Paul family into the abyss.

"But..." Gideon swallowed hard. "If the Paul family collapses, those villas and the tower could lose value..."

"I'm not after their value."

Evander lifted his coffee cup, taking a slow sip. "I want the Paul family to know what it feels like to lose everything, to have nowhere to turn."

His voice was soft, but its steely resolve sent a shiver down Gideon's spine.

Gideon shuddered but nodded quickly.

"Absolutely, I'll get it done!

"First thing tomorrow, I'll send Zak a notice that his loan's due early. If he can't pay, we'll seize the properties!"

"Not enough." Evander set the cup down, his gaze sharp as a blade. "I want it done now.

"And tell Zak that every penny of the interest and penalty fees he owes the bank will be collected."

Gideon froze for a moment, then grasped the full weight of Evander's intent.

This wasn't just about bankrupting the Paul family—it was about burying them under a mountain of debt.

"Yes, yes, of course!"

Gideon scrambled to agree, reaching for his phone to issue orders.

"Wait." Evander stopped him, adding, "When you seize the properties, make a spectacle of it. Get the press there. I want the entire city to watch the Paul family fall from grace."

Gideon's blood ran cold, but he didn't dare refuse. He simply nodded in obedience.

As he watched Evander's retreating figure, a chill crept up his neck.

This man, with his mild demeanor, wielded tactics as ruthless as the legendary underworld kingpin.

Evander stepped out of the bank.

The sunset painted the sky in fiery oranges.

He stood on the steps, gazing at the massive screen across the street broadcasting financial news.

Paul Group's stock had hit rock bottom, and a news ticker beneath it flashed the words: [Paul Group Suspected of Severe Tax Evasion.]

His phone rang. It was Norman's assistant.

"Mr. Bexley, Zak's personal accounts are frozen, and his hidden funds in the Swiss bank have been transferred. We also found he owes fifty million dollars to underground loan sharks."

"Good." Evander's voice was ice-cold. "Tell the loan sharks that they should go collect their debt from the Paul family tomorrow."

Hanging up, he looked up at the deepening twilight.

Norman wanted every asset the Paul family had. Evander intended to not only deliver on that promise but to do so with even greater brutality.

He wanted to turn Zak from a billionaire into a debt-ridden pauper. He wanted to see Naomi, the pampered princess, become a homeless stray.

He felt this was the only fitting payment for what they had done to him.

"To Summit Group," Evander told Jaiden.

The neon lights outside the car windows flickered on, outlining the city's prosperous silhouette.

Evander leaned back, closing his eyes.

The wounds on his back throbbed faintly, but his heart was calm and resolute.

A fire of vengeance blazed in his chest, illuminating the shadows of his once-dark life.

He knew that from this day forward, he was no longer the Evander who let others trample him.

As night deepened, chaos erupted in the Paul family's villa.

Zak smashed everything within reach, roaring, "Who did this to me?" Naomi cowered in a corner, trembling, while Kimberly had long since fled with an excuse to go home.

When the loan sharks' collection notice arrived via text, Zak's vision blurred, and he collapsed, limp as a rag doll.

*****

When Zak collapsed, Evander was having dinner with Iris at an upscale restaurant.

Iris noticed his gaze drift toward the window. "What are you looking at?"

Evander's attention snapped back to her, and he slid the perfectly cut steak across the table. "Nothing. Try this."

He wore a charcoal gray suit that accentuated his broad shoulders and slim waist. The once-melancholic humility in his expression had been replaced by confident determination. His every movement now carried the grace of someone born to wealth and power.

The memory of yesterday's passion sent heat creeping up Iris's neck.

"Thank you!" She picked up her fork, stabbing a piece of steak and shoving it into her mouth to hide her flustered expression.

"I can't believe you're the founder of Summit Group. My boss this whole time..."

The seared beef's smoky flavor bloomed on her tongue, but it couldn't suppress the strange flutter in her chest.

"It doesn't matter. You've done exceptional work these past years. Everyone says you're one of the top female CEOs in America. I wasn't wrong about you back then."

Iris shook her head: "It was your brilliant decision-making. I've always wondered what kind of person could have such genius ideas and such meticulous business thinking."

"Now that you've met me, am I very different from what you imagined?"

"Completely different..."

Evander caught the flush spreading across her ears, amusement flickering in his eyes.

"Oh, did you hear? The Paul family went bankrupt." He mentioned it casually, as if discussing the weather. "Their mansion's already been sealed off. The press got plenty of photos—it'll probably make tomorrow's business headlines."

Iris had no idea what had happened. "How did the Paul family go bankrupt? What about Naomi..."

"That woman?" Evander took a sip of his wine, letting the tannins roll across his palate. A cold smile curved his lips. "Word is, before she collapsed sobbing outside the police station, she was still screaming for Nadine to help her."

At Nadine's name, Iris looked up, searching Evander's face for any reaction. But she found only calm indifference.

It seemed he'd truly cut all ties with the Ellery family.
