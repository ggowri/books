Chapter 31 The Return

In the president's office of Nelson Capital Tower, Oliver was beside himself with worry.

His beloved youngest daughter, Kimberly, had been missing for seven days.

His eldest daughter, Vivian Astley, had called in tears, explaining that Kimberly had taken Sophia to the wetland park for a day out. On a whim, she'd hopped into a man's helicopter, saying they were headed to Vieques Island to see wild gorillas.

That afternoon, a thunderstorm had rolled in. The helicopter's signal vanished from radar just as the storm hit its peak.

"Who the hell was that man?"

Oliver hurled a million-dollar crystal tumbler to the floor, shards scattering across the plush carpet. "Vivian, think hard. Did Sophia say anything else?"

Vivian's voice trembled over the phone.

"Dad, I've asked her a hundred times. She doesn't know his name; she just said he looked 'super cool' flying the helicopter. I've already called the police. The search team's been out there for seven days, and they've found nothing."

Oliver hung up, pacing the office, hands clasped behind his back.

Beyond the floor-to-ceiling windows, skyscrapers gleamed in orderly rows. That sight was great, but all he could feel now was a gnawing panic.

Kimberly was his baby girl, cherished since birth. If anything happened to her, how could he face his late wife?

Naomi, standing nearby, handed him a tissue, dabbing at her eyes with exaggerated concern.

"Oliver, don't stress yourself out. Kimberly's sharp—she'll be fine. Maybe the helicopter made an emergency landing on some island, and she's just waiting out the storm."

Her words dripped with false sympathy, but a glint of malice flickered in her eyes.

After all, if Kimberly truly never came back, there would be a sizable share of the Nelson fortune for Naomi to claim.

"An island?"

Oliver stopped dead, his graying temples stark under the office lights. "Landing on an island in that storm? That's as good as a death sentence."

Just then, the heavy oak door creaked open.

Kimberly stood in the doorway, wearing an oversized men's shirt, sleeves rolled to her elbows, revealing faint scratches on her forearms. Her cheeks were flushed from the sea breeze, her expression tentative.

"Dad..."

"Kimberly!"

Oliver stared, hardly trusting his own eyes. He rushed forward, enveloping her in a tight embrace, his voice shaking.

"You're okay? Thank God! I've been worried sick."

Naomi hurried over, clutching Kimberly's hand with feigned warmth.

"Thank goodness you're back. You don't know how much weight your dad's lost worrying about you..."

Her words trailed off as her gaze shifted past Kimberly, landing on the man at the door.

Her voice spiked. "What's he doing here?"

Oliver followed her stare, spotting Evander there.

Evander stood in rumpled cargo pants and a grass-stained T-shirt, but he held himself straight, like he was ready for a fight. His eyes were steady as he looked at them, with a mysterious half-smile on his face.

"You two... came back together?"

Oliver's brow furrowed, his tone icing over.

He knew Evander—Nadine's deadbeat husband. The man who trailed after his wife like a shadow, too weak to manage his own company.

How could someone like that end up with his daughter?

Kimberly caught the chill in her father's voice and quickly looped her arm through his, flashing a playful smile.

"Dad, it's Evander. You met him at Nad's birthday party, remember?"

Her face lit up, eyes sparkling with excitement.

"We were stranded on an island for seven days. It was wild!

"He even took down a boar to protect me."

"An island?" Oliver's expression darkened. "You two were alone on an island for seven days?"

"What?" Naomi let out a theatrical gasp, hand flying to her mouth.

"Kimberly, have you lost your mind? You spent a week on an island with this loser?

"Did he kidnap you?

"I knew he was trouble. All that nice-guy act, but he's full of schemes."

"Naomi, stop it!"

Kimberly's brows knitted, and she instinctively stepped closer to Evander.

"He's not a loser. He's incredible! If it wasn't for him, I'd probably be some animal's lunch."

Evander stayed silent, watching the scene unfold with quiet detachment.

He noticed Kimberly's trembling shoulders as she stood in front of him, yet her stance was firm, like a sunflower bracing against a storm.

Oliver shook off his daughter's arm, jabbing a finger at Evander.

"Evander, I'm warning you—stay away from my daughter. You're Nadine's husband. Spending seven days alone with Kimberly? What will people say?"

"Dad!" Kimberly's eyes brimmed with tears, her voice cracking. "It was an accident. The helicopter crashed, and we had no choice but to land on the island."

"No choice, so you slept together?"

Naomi's voice dripped with venom, her eyes lingering on the oversized shirt clinging to Kimberly's frame.

"Kimberly, I'm disappointed. Nad treated you like a sister. How could you do this to her?"

The words pierced Kimberly like a needle, draining the color from her face.

Memories of the island flooded back—the heat of Evander's breath under the moonlight, their shadows entwined on the sand, the reckless gasps in the cave...

Shame washed over her, yet she couldn't shake the warmth those moments held.

Evander's hand brushed her back, a subtle signal to stay calm.

He stepped forward, meeting Oliver's fury head-on.

"Mr. Nelson, what happened on the island was an accident. As for Nadine and me, our marriage has been over for a while. I've already signed the divorce papers."

"Divorce?" Oliver froze, then sneered, "You think leaving Nadine makes you worthy of my daughter? Evander, know your place. A man who lives off women has no right to be with Kimberly."

"It's not up to you to decide if I'm worthy," Evander replied, his voice steady, his eyes sharp as a blade.

Kimberly's tears threatened to spill. She sniffled, stepping closer to Evander and gripping his arm tightly.

"Dad, I love Evander. I don't care who he was or what he becomes. I love him."

"You're out of your mind!" Oliver's voice shook with rage.

"Do you even know who he is? He's a cast-off from the Ellery family. If he's close to you, it's for the Nelson fortune!"

"No!" Kimberly shook her head, tears streaming down her face. "He's good to me—better than anyone."

When Oliver saw that look of pure determination in his daughter's eyes, his heart felt like someone had grabbed it and squeezed.

He'd always seen her as his little princess, needing his protection. Now, she stood firm for a man he despised.

Naomi stoked the fire.

"Oliver, look at how she's been fooled! If Nad finds out, the Nelson-Ellery partnership is done."

At the mention of Nadine, Kimberly stiffened, guilt flickering in her eyes.

But she lifted her chin, facing her father.

"I'll explain things to Nad. Dad, please, don't tear us apart."

Evander gave her hand a gentle squeeze, offering silent support.

He knew Oliver was just the first hurdle. Nadine, Denny, and his grandfather's test loomed ahead.

Yet, watching Kimberly stand up to the world for him, something uncertain started to stir in his heart.

Maybe this entanglement, born of calculation, was veering off its intended course.

Oliver looked at his daughter's red-rimmed eyes, then at Evander's unreadable gaze, and felt a bone-deep weariness.

He waved a hand, his voice hoarse.

"Go home, both of you. I need time to think."

Naomi wanted to protest, but a cold glance from Oliver silenced her.

Kimberly clutched Evander's hand, cautiously backing out of the office.

At the elevator, she glanced back at the closed door and let out a soft sigh.

"Don't worry," Evander murmured, his voice carrying a gentleness even he didn't notice.

"It'll all work out."

The elevator chimed softly as it came to a stop.

As the metal doors slid open, Kimberly's and Evander's breaths caught in their throats.

Nadine stood inside.
