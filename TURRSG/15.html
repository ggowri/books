Chapter 16 The Alliance Strikes

The next morning, in the top-floor lounge of Nelson Capital Tower, Kimberly's father, Oliver Nelson, sat on a leather sofa, a cigar between his fingers. His eyes scanned the proposal before him.

There were hints of gray at his temples, but his eyes remained sharp. As the biggest investor in Nogales, he had seen too many flashy projects, and his gut told him that this proposal seemed oddly familiar.

"Stock prediction app, brain-computer interface..."

Oliver spoke slowly, smoke curling around him.

"Mr. Westbrook, since when did Westbrook Group dabble in medical tech?"

Denny's smile faltered, but he quickly recovered.

"It's a newly launched project. Core tech imported from overseas—cutting-edge, way ahead of the curve."

Nadine added, "Mr. Nelson, Summit Group's brain-computer interface infringes on our patents. We're prepping a lawsuit. Once they lose, Westbrook Group's project will dominate the market, with returns at least tripled."

Oliver said nothing, turning to his daughter.

"Kimmy, what do you think?"

Kimberly, in a professional suit, sat up straight, pulling out her tablet to display a market analysis.

"The stock prediction app's user base is mainly the middle class. They have strong investment needs but lack expertise. That's our opening."

She swiped to the brain-computer interface page.

"As for this project, Summit Group started earlier, but their cash flow's tight. With Ellery Group's backing, Westbrook Group can overtake them."

Her voice was clear and melodious, with sharp analysis that hit the mark. Her eyes sparkled with an optimistic outlook on the market.

Oliver watched Kimberly with a flicker of fondness in his eyes.

He knew she and Nadine were best friends, and she had a soft spot for Denny. Her analysis might carry some bias, but the data held up.

"Eighty-five billion dollars isn't pocket change."

Oliver stubbed out his cigar, his tone grave. "Failure could shake Nelson Capital's foundation."

"It won't fail." Nadine's voice was certain.

"Ellery Group will pour every resource into supporting Westbrook Group, from patent lawsuits to marketing. A full package.

"Mr. Nelson, all you need to do is invest the funds and wait for the dividends."

Denny jumped in. "We've secured deals with European companies. Once funding's in, we're ready to launch."

Oliver was silent for several minutes, his eyes moving between the three of them.

Nadine's confidence, Denny's eagerness, and Kimberly's anticipation ultimately led him to nod.

"Alright, I'll trust you this time."

Oliver picked up his pen and signed the investment letter.

"But let's be clear. If anything goes wrong, don't expect me to go easy."

"Thank you for your trust, Mr. Nelson!"

Denny nearly leapt from his seat in excitement.

Nadine exhaled, a victorious smile curling her lips.

Eighty-five billion dollars—enough to crush Summit Group completely.

She could already picture Evander, bankrupt, groveling at her feet for forgiveness.

Kimberly linked arms with Oliver affectionately.

"Dad, I knew you had the best judgment!"

Leaving Nelson Capital Tower, Denny rubbed his hands eagerly.

"Nad, we did it!

"With this funding, forget Summit Group, there is no one in the country who can touch us!"

Nadine glanced at him coolly.

"Don't celebrate yet. Get the patent suit filed immediately, and push to freeze Summit Group's accounts so they can't fight back."

"Got it!" Denny nodded. "I'll set it up now."

Watching Denny hurry off, Nadine pulled out her phone, thumbing Evander's number. Her finger hovered over the call button, then fell away.

Not yet. She'd wait until Evander hit rock bottom to make him crawl home.

Kimberly approached, offering a coffee.

"What's on your mind?"

Nadine took the cup, staring at her reflection in it.

"I'm wondering what Evander's face will look like when he sees our alliance."

"He'll be scared stiff," Kimberly laughed. "Someone like him has no business trying to go up against us."

Evander dialed Iris' number. It rang for a while before she answered, the clatter of a keyboard in the background.

"Iris, you okay?"

"I'm fine."

Iris' voice was light. "Patent office folks came for a follow-up check, but they're gone now. Why the sudden call?"

Evander hesitated, not mentioning the photo.

"I just missed you."

A brief silence, then Iris' soft laugh came through.

"I miss you too. Oh, Professor Hartley just said the summit added a live debate round. You might have to face Denny onstage..."

"I'll be there."

Evander cut in, "Whatever the format, I can handle it."

Just then, the villa's doorbell rang.

The maid announced William's arrival.

In the living room, William was studying a seascape painting, hands behind his back. He turned at their footsteps, his face grave.

"There's trouble."

William removed his glasses, pulling a newspaper from his briefcase. The headline screamed: Summit Group Accused of Data Fraud, AI Ethics Review Board Under Fire.

Evander's fingers tightened on the paper's edge.

The article pinned Denny's model issues on Summit Group, citing "anonymous experts" claiming the brain-computer interface endangered privacy.
