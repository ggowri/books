Chapter 96 Freya's Necklace

High up in the air, Freya nestled snugly in Evander's arms, her voice thick with sobs. "Od, you gotta come back for me, okay? Promise me."

Evander let out a soft grunt of assent, his heart heavy with guilt.

He was all too aware that this might be a farewell for good.

Before long, the darkened streets of London came into sight.

Freya landed softly on the same street where she'd first taken Evander.

As she unwrapped her arms from around him, her eyes lingered on his face, filled with longing she couldn't hide.

"I'll head back and wait for you," she murmured.

Evander nodded and turned to leave, but Freya grabbed his hand.

She took off the Brisingamen necklace from her neck and carefully placed it around his.

"Take this," she said softly. "It'll protect you, and I'll always know where you are. Just crush it if you're in trouble, and I'll be there in a flash."

Evander looked at the dazzling necklace around his neck, touched.

He was about to refuse, but seeing Freya's unwavering expression, he simply nodded.

"Thanks, Freya," he said.

Freya managed a smile, then transformed into a silver streak and vanished into the night sky.

Evander's fingers traced the Brisingamen necklace. His chest churned with emotions he couldn't name.

Then he turned and made his way towards Royal One Estate.

After walking just a few steps, he spotted a familiar figure running towards him.

"Mr. Bexley!" Lois' voice was laced with sobs as she threw herself into Evander's arms. "You're finally back! I thought that darn wind had taken you away forever!"

Evander gently patted her back, soothing her. "I'm here. Don't cry."

Lois looked up and noticed the necklace around Evander's neck. A flicker of curiosity crossed her eyes.

"What's this...?"

"A gift from a friend," he said with a small smile. "Keeps me safe."

He left out the part about Freya. He knew Lois wouldn't buy it if he told the truth.

Lois nodded and didn't press further. Instead, she tightly clasped his hand.

"Come on. Let's go home. I'll open a bottle of red. We'll celebrate."

Evander smiled and nodded, then walked hand-in-hand with Lois towards Royal One Estate.

The night breeze blew, and the Brisingamen necklace around his neck glowed faintly.

Like a whisper, it reminded him—a goddess was waiting in Sessrumnir Palace.

What he didn't know was that this necklace would not only protect him but also drag him into a storm far worse.

Lois finally caved to her curiosity. She turned to look at him, her eyes full of concern.

"Mr. Bexley... who did you just meet? That wind was so freaking weird. When you got swept away, I was scared out of my wits."

Evander stopped and reached up to ruffle her hair.

"I'm fine," he said softly. "The wind just tossed me into a sycamore tree on the roadside."

He gave a crooked smile, almost teasing.

"Lucky for me, a passerby spotted me stuck in the branches, grabbed a ladder, and pulled me down."

"Stuck in the branches?" Lois' eyes widened in shock and a bit of annoyance. She playfully swatted his arm.

"Come on, stop kidding! Are you hurt? Did the branches scratch you?"

As she spoke, she reached for his sleeve to check, clearly worried.

"Really, I'm okay. Look." Evander held up his arm and did a little twirl to show he was unharmed.

"She was really nice. After she got me out of the tree, she sat with me for a while and gave me this necklace, said it'd protect me."

He reached into his collar and pulled out the necklace, gently swaying the gemstone with his fingertips.

Lois leaned in for a closer look. The gemstone glowed softly under the streetlights, its delicate cut making it clear this wasn't some cheap trinket.

She reached out, brushing her fingertips against it. A coolness seeped through her fingertips.

"Seems like a good guy," she murmured. "Anyway, let's just hope you don't run into any more freak winds."

Then she grabbed Evander's hand again, holding it even firmer than before.

"If something happened to you, I—"

"Hey." Evander squeezed her fingers, cutting her off. "Nothing's gonna happen. I'll be careful, okay? You won't have to worry about me again."

Hand in hand, they walked toward the estate, the night quiet around them.

******

Upon their return to the manor, dinner was already laid out in the kitchen.

The perfectly cooked Wellington steak sizzled enticingly, its crust crispy and the inside tender. The creamy mushroom soup filled the dining room with its rich, inviting aroma, and the fruit salad was adorned with fresh blueberries and strawberries.

Lois tugged Evander to sit down and sliced a piece of steak for him herself.

"Try it! The chef cooked it medium-rare just the way you like. He said it'd be so tender the juice will burst out," she said.

Evander took a bite. The meat was juicy, and the flavor of the black-pepper sauce danced on his tongue. It tasted even better than the steak he'd had at that Michelin-starred restaurant last time.

He smiled at Lois, "This is amazing. We don't need to eat out anymore."

"Of course," Lois said, puffing out her chest proudly.

Then she speared a piece of salad for herself. "I learned how to make this from the chef. I'll cook it for you next time I'm free."

After dinner, they settled on the sofa in the living room to go over the materials for the Royal Council meeting the next day.

Lois handed a thick document to Evander and traced her fingers over the words on the paper.

"Sir Gary is chairing tomorrow's council. He's big on the practical use of technology, especially in medicine and the military.

"You should tell him more about Vanguard Tech's AI in emergency robots and intelligent defense systems. He'll definitely be interested."

Evander took the document and carefully leafed through the technical parameters and case reports, marking the key points now and then.

"I've prepared a presentation with real-time simulation videos to show the AI's response speed. It should win them over," he said.

Then he paused and looked at Lois, his voice softening.

"By the way, about Felix..."

Lois' fingers stilled for a moment. She nodded, a complicated look flashing across her eyes.

"Even though he did a lot of bad stuff before, I still feel... kinda sad." She sighed. "But something's off. Felix was in good shape. How could he just..."

Evander hesitated for a second but decided to tell her part of the truth.

"Actually, he's not really gone..." he said. "He'll be back in two days..."

He left out Hela and Helheim, just giving her the result so she wouldn't be sad.

Lois' eyes widened in surprise. "What? He'll come back in two days? For real?"

Evander nodded. "Yeah, really. He'll be alive again in two days."

Lois nodded and didn't press further. She refocused on the materials.

"Then let's double-check the process for tomorrow. Can't afford any slip-ups."

Once they'd finished reviewing the materials, it was well into the night.

Lois yawned and rested her head on Evander's shoulder, her voice slurred with sleep. "I'm beat. Let's hit the hay and get some rest for tomorrow's meeting."

"Sure!" Evander kissed Lois on her blushing cheek, helped her up, and they headed towards the bedroom.

Just as they reached the bedroom door, the Brisingamen necklace around Evander's neck started to warm up slightly, and a faint silver glow flashed across the gemstone.

His heart skipped a beat.

Freya's necklace. Has something happened to her?
