Chapter 6 First Trial

Evander's voice was filled with determination.

Only with absolute power could he protect himself and make those who hurt him pay dearly for their actions.

Norman raised his hand, signaling the men in black behind him.

One approached quickly, carrying a silver tray with an encrypted phone and documents.

"Your first trial," Norman's voice was ice-cold. "Make the Paul family bankrupt within three days.

"After what Naomi did to you in that warehouse, they deserve this punishment.

"Complete this, and I'll give you a reward that will change your life forever."

Evander suppressed his rising emotions and picked up the document, reading it carefully.

The Paul family's empire lay exposed before him: three public corporations, seven international estates, and most tellingly, their hidden Swiss accounts. The level of detail was unsettling—this intelligence had been meticulously gathered long ago.

"Zak Paul's been in business for over thirty years. He has started with loan sharking, and his hands are stained with blood."

Norman lifted his cup, steam veiling his expression. "He thought aligning with the Ellery family would keep him safe forever, but he forgot: no one touches my grandson and gets away with it."

"Now, Evander, you can tap into HexCore's resources." Norman pointed at the phone. "It has contacts for the world's top hackers, backdoor access to all the main banks in the world, and even evidence of Zak's embezzlement."

Seeing the spark ignite in Evander's eyes, Norman added, "But I don't want a mere bankruptcy. I want them plummeting from the heights to the gutter, trapped in a living hell."

Evander drew a deep breath and tucked the phone into his pocket.

He thought of Summit Group's servers, of firewalls that had crumbled under his touch, and an icy smile tugged at his lips.

When it came to dismantling someone's foundation, no one was better suited than him.

"Do you need manpower?" Norman inquired.

"No."

Despite the blood still seeping from his wounds, Evander's gaze burned with fierce determination.

"Taking down the Paul family? I can handle that alone."

Norman studied his resolute profile, a flicker of pride warming his eyes.

"Good!"

He waved a hand, and two doctors in white coats hurried forward.

"Get those wounds treated first. I'll be waiting in the study."

Evander followed the doctors out.

As antiseptic-soaked gauze pressed against his skin, he gritted his teeth, silent through the sting.

His mind was mapping out a strategy to assault the Paul family's financial fortress.

Few people knew that before marrying Nadine, he was a genius in scientific research. Even after their marriage, he had built Summit Group into a rising star company through his strategic vision and remote guidance alone.

The codes he'd once crafted for AI breakthroughs would now become his deadliest arsenal.

Once bandaged, he slipped into a sleek black suit. He looked pale, but the gleam in his eyes was like a blade.

Evander patted the phone against his chest.

He felt there was a dormant beast harboring in his chest, ready to be unleashed.

Entering the study, he found Norman tinkering with an antique clock.

The gilded pendulum swung in a slow, measured rhythm, each tick-tock like a countdown to the Paul family's doom.

"Ready?" Norman asked without looking up.

"Ready."

Evander flipped open his laptop. The moment his fingers struck the keys, it was as if he had become a different person, focused and formidable.

Complex financial data unraveled before him like a map, the Paul family's seemingly ironclad cash flow riddled with vulnerabilities.

"Zak's weakness lies in his offshore accounts."

Norman brought over a glass of whiskey. "He's stashed most of his dirty money in the Cayman Islands, under shell companies."

Evander ignored the drink, pulling up a sequence of code.

"I know."

He hit enter, and the screen bloomed with the internal dashboard of a Cayman bank. "Their firewalls are even weaker than I expected."

Norman observed his fluid maneuvers, his smile deepening with satisfaction.

Norman was certain that this young man, who had endured so much humiliation at home, had carried the same blood in him as himself.

When the first chunk of funds vanished from the Paul family's accounts, Evander's expression remained impassive.

He recalled Naomi's heels splashing in that filthy puddle, her venomous words—"A worthless nobody like you doesn't deserve to breathe"—and his keystrokes grew heavier, more deliberate.

"Not bad! I know my grandson can do it!" Norman exclaimed with delight. "I want all of the Paul family's assets within three days!"

*****

The black Bentley glided away from the estate.

Evander rolled down his window, letting the cool breeze wash over him, clearing his troubled thoughts.

His fingers tapped lightly on the leather seat, falling into rhythm with his heartbeat.

"Head to Citibank headquarters."

Evander's voice was flat, betraying no emotion as he spoke to the driver, Jaiden Ortiz.

Jaiden nodded, turning the wheel to merge into the main road's flow.

Evander closed his eyes, and Naomi's cruel face flashed through his mind.

His hand tightened. He picked up his phone and dialed a number.

Twenty minutes later, Evander arrived.

The Citibank headquarters gleamed under the afternoon sun, its glass curtain wall throwing back a cold, metallic light.

As he stepped through the revolving doors, the lobby manager spotted him immediately. The man's polite, well-practiced smile froze for a split second, then shifted into a respectful expression.

"Mr. Bexley, you're here. The president is waiting in his office."

Clearly, the phone call he'd made earlier had done its job.

The elevator glided upward, its mirrored walls reflecting Evander's tall, unyielding figure.

His black suit was pristine, not a wrinkle in sight, concealing the scars beneath but not the fire in his eyes.

The door to the president's office stood slightly ajar, the rustle of papers drifting out.

Evander pushed it open, and the middle-aged man hunched over his desk leapt to his feet, his face breaking into an eager grin. "Mr. Bexley, it really is you! Please, have a seat!"

The president, Gideon Watts, was an old hand who had navigated the treacherous waters of finance for thirty years. Now, looking at Evander, his eyes gleamed as if staring at a walking goldmine.

He personally brewed a cup of coffee for Evander, his hand trembling slightly as he set it down.

"I've compiled all the documents you requested."

Financial documents littered the coffee table, dominated by a detailed list of the Paul family's mortgaged properties.

Three prime city villas and their riverfront headquarters building—assets valued at nearly 800 million dollars—all hastily leveraged by Zak that morning in a desperate attempt to save his crumbling company.

"Zak finalized the paperwork just this morning, hoping the loan would bail out his company."

Gideon rubbed his hands together, his tone tinged with schadenfreude. "But this afternoon, news broke about his tax evasion scandal. Now the whole bank's sweating over this bad debt."

Evander picked up the list, his fingertip tracing the words "Tidemark Tower."

That thirty-story office building was the Paul family's pride. Years ago, Zak had thrown a lavish opening gala there. At that party, he had gone out of his way to publicly humiliate Evander, who was there as Nadine's plus-one.

The mocking laughter still echoed in his ears. Evander closed his eyes briefly, and when he opened them again, they were sharp with cold determination.
