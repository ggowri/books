Chapter 70 A Fond Farewell

The evening sun painted the sky in shades of warm orange.

Evander drove his SUV toward Ellery Villa, the window rolled down to let in the cool evening breeze. His fingers brushed over the Arcane Ring as he thought about the gifts he had prepared for Delilah and Ruth.

A faint smile tugged at his lips.

Half an hour later, the car stopped at Ellery Villa's entrance.

Half an hour later, he pulled up at the villa. Carrying a light jacket, he stepped out and walked inside.

Laughter greeted him the moment he entered the living room.

Ruth was sitting on the oversized sofa, Delilah nestled in her lap. The coffee table in front of them was scattered with colorful blocks, a half-built castle standing proudly among them.

"Evander, you’re here." Ruth’s face lit up as she patted the empty spot beside her. "Come sit. Lilah’s been asking when you’d be back. You’ve both been so busy lately—it’s rare to have time together. You should spend more with her."

At the sound of his name, Delilah looked up. Her eyes sparkled the moment she saw him. She leapt off the sofa and ran straight into his arms, hugging his waist tightly.

"Daddy! You’re finally back! Did you bring me a gift?"

Evander scooped her up, pinched her cheek, and grinned. "Of course I did."

He opened his hands to show his empty palms, even turning them over for effect.

"See? It’s right here."

Delilah blinked at his empty hands, then pouted. "Daddy, you’re lying! There’s no gift!"

Ruth chuckled, shaking her head. "If you didn’t bring one, that’s fine. Just don’t tease her like—"

Before she could finish, a bracelet studded with five flawless diamonds shimmered into Evander’s palm. The gold chain, etched with delicate rose patterns, glinted luxuriously beneath the chandelier. It was clearly worth a fortune.

"Wow! What a beautiful bracelet!" Delilah gasped, her little hands hovering as if afraid to touch it. "Daddy, is this for me?"

"Of course it is."

He fastened the bracelet gently onto her wrist. The diamonds glittered brilliantly as she moved, dazzling under the light.

Ruth leaned in, amazed. "Your hands were empty a second ago… how did this appear? Are you a magician now? You really do have hidden talents."

The sound of heels clicking on the floor drew their attention.

Nadine had returned from work.

She walked in with her briefcase, her expression darkening as soon as she saw Evander. "What are you doing here?" Her tone carried clear displeasure.

But then her gaze landed on Delilah’s wrist. The bracelet’s five perfect diamonds caught the light, each one easily worth a fortune. The piece had to be worth at least fifty million dollars, far beyond anything found in stores.

"Look, Mommy!" Delilah waved her arm proudly. "Daddy gave me this! He can do magic!"

Nadine frowned and started to head upstairs, but Ruth caught her hand.

"Nad, you’re back just in time. Come sit. Evander said he has a gift for me too—you should see it."

Reluctantly, Nadine sat down at the edge of the sofa, still wary.

Evander settled back beside them with Delilah on his lap. Once again, he held out his empty palms.

"Grandma, this one’s for you."

As he focused, a royal ruby bracelet appeared in his hand. The gem’s deep red glow was warm and vivid, its texture flawless. It was the kind of treasure that had clearly been polished by time.

"This… this is for me?" Ruth gasped, covering her mouth in disbelief.

Born into a wealthy family, she had seen countless jewels, but never a ruby of this quality.

"You’re so thoughtful! But don’t spend so much next time. Family happiness matters more than anything. And really—you two should get serious about giving Lilah a sibling. I’m still waiting for another grandchild."

Her words hung awkwardly in the air. Nadine’s face flushed, and she opened her mouth to object, but Evander cut her off.

"We’ll keep that in mind."

He looked down at Delilah. "Lilah, Daddy’s here to say goodbye. I’ll be away on a business trip starting next Thursday—gone for about a month. I’ll bring you an even bigger gift when I return. Be good while I’m gone, listen to your teachers, and don’t get into any trouble, alright?"

Delilah nodded reluctantly, wrapping her arms around his neck. "Okay, Daddy. Eat well, don’t catch a cold, and come back soon."

Her fondness for this gift-giving, magic-performing father grew stronger, melting away the distance she’d once felt.

Evander patted her head, then glanced at Ruth. "Is Emma not home? No one’s cooking tonight?"

"I’ll do it," Nadine said stiffly. "Lilah hasn’t had much of an appetite for greasy food lately."

"No need." Evander walked over to the dining table, smiling.

With a wave of his hand, two perfectly cooked filet mignon steaks, two bowls of creamy mushroom soup, a fresh fruit salad, and a bottle of 1982 Lafite appeared neatly on the table. Steam curled into the air, filling the room with a rich aroma.

Ruth and Delilah stared in shock. Nadine froze, frowning deeper.

"Are those even safe to eat? What if they make Grandma or Lilah sick?"

Ruth and Delilah looked hesitant as well. Food appearing out of thin air hardly inspired confidence.

"You’ll know it’s not a trick once you taste it." Evander pulled out a chair and helped Delilah into her seat. "Go on, Lilah. Try it."

She stabbed a piece of steak with her fork and popped it into her mouth. Her eyes lit up instantly.

"Wow! This is better than the restaurant Mommy took me to! Grandma, Mommy, you have to try it!"

Ruth took a cautious sip of soup, then her eyes widened. The creamy texture and deep mushroom flavor were genuine—no trace of artificiality.

She nodded approvingly. "Evander’s not only good at making money—he’s thoughtful too. Nad, you really should cherish him and stop being so stubborn."

Nadine’s stomach growled despite herself. The smell alone was enough to make her mouth water. She hesitated, but watching Ruth and Delilah dig in finally broke her resolve. With a huff, she sat down, speared a piece of steak, and tasted it.

The meat was tender and juicy, perfectly seasoned, better than any she’d had at a steakhouse. Her cheeks warmed—her earlier words suddenly felt hollow. But hunger won out, and with no gift for her, she couldn't pass up the food, so she kept eating despite herself.

Evander joined them at the table. He had picked up the meal from a restaurant earlier, never expecting the Inventory Cube to preserve it so perfectly.

After dinner, Delilah clung to his arm, playing with blocks at his side.

Meanwhile, Ruth was already scheming again—this time about how to persuade them to give her another grandchild.
