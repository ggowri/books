Chapter 115 The First Girl

The main venue of Paris Fashion Week was packed, every seat taken.

The lights dimmed to black in an instant. A single spotlight cut through the darkness, casting its beam on the entrance of the runway.

And then she stepped out. Sarah Grant—top-tier runway model—emerged in four-inch stiletto heels, her first step landing in perfect sync with the beat.

A hush fell over the room.

She wore a wine-red velvet mermaid gown that hugged every curve of her silhouette. With each graceful step, the hem swayed gently, revealing a glimpse of her long, fair legs.

Her neckline was poised like a swan's, elegant and smooth, with tiny diamonds scattered along her collarbones, glinting beneath the lights. They mirrored the subtle shimmer of her pearl earrings.

Her hair was swept into a soft, vintage-style low bun, with loose tendrils framing her face like something out of an old film.

Her eyes lifted slightly at the corners. Her lips, painted in muted rose, were pressed together in a way that carried a hint of cool detachment, yet the glance she gave the audience had a quiet, effortless allure.

Every step was deliberate. Her waist shifted with the rhythm, feminine yet powerful, never performative but perfectly captivating.

She belonged in the spotlight. That much was clear.

At the center of the runway, Sarah spun on her heel, her gown sweeping out in an elegant arc. The velvet glistened under the lights, the rich red catching every flicker of gold and silver in the air.

Applause thundered from the audience.

Fashion editors in the front row raised their cameras all at once, the sound of shutters echoing like waves.

Sarah merely offered a subtle nod, her expression serene, unaffected by the storm of praise around her.

As the final look came to a close, she raised her hand and let her fingers glide over the gown's skirt.

The silver ring on her finger caught the light, flashing alongside the diamonds at her collar. A faint smile played at the corners of her lips, elusive and almost imperceptible.

The spotlight slowly dimmed. She turned and walked offstage, her tall, graceful figure retreating into the darkness, every step as composed and regal as her entrance.

"You were absolutely stunning!" her assistant exclaimed, rushing toward her with a water bottle the moment she returned backstage.

"Designers are sending requests left and right. They all want to know your availability."

Sarah took the bottle, her fingertips still tingling from the velvet's touch. She adjusted a loose strand of hair in the mirror, her voice calm.

"Have them send the proposals to the studio."

Her eyes drifted to the ivory handbag resting beside the vanity. Inside was a photo that her dad, Lawrence Grant, had given her himself. Scrawled on the back were the words: [By the fountain in the gardens of the Palace of Versailles.]

It was the meeting place he'd arranged for her blind date.

"By the way," the assistant added quickly, "your father called again. He said the man from North America is... someone significant. He wants you to make sure you go straight there after the show—no delays, no excuses."

Sarah paused for a beat, then reached into the handbag and pulled out the photo.

The man in the picture wore a dark gray suit, standing near London's Tower Bridge. His features were sharp, with a strong jawline, striking eyes, and a composed expression that felt utterly out of place amid the city's bustle.

She stared at it for a few seconds before tossing it back into her bag.

"Help me change out of the gown. I'm heading to Versailles now."

Half an hour later, a sleek black sports car cruised along the tree-lined road leading to the Palace of Versailles.

Sarah leaned against the window, watching the rows of sycamore trees blur past. Despite the golden afternoon light, her mind remained detached. She still wasn't expecting much from this meeting.

Over the years, Lawrence had introduced her to countless "prospects"—noble heirs, business elites, politicians' sons.

They were either arrogant beyond reason or too eager to please. Not a single one had ever made a real impression.

She wouldn't have agreed to this one either if Lawrence hadn't emphasized that it "concerned the family's future".

The car arrived at Versailles just as the sun began casting amber hues across the ornate façades, bathing the palace walls and sculptures in a warm, dignified glow.

She stepped out, heels clicking against the stone path, and made her way into the gardens.

The fountain sprayed crystal arcs into the twilight air, droplets scattering across the cobblestones with a soft hiss.

The lawns were manicured to perfection, and the marble statues shimmered in the fading light, regal and still.

Sarah pulled out the photo and glanced around, scanning the scene for a familiar face.

A few couples lingered near the fountain, but none resembled the man in the picture.

Her brows pinched slightly in frustration.

The lack of punctuality only confirmed what she feared. This was going to be another waste of time.

"Guess he's not coming," she muttered under her breath, turning on her heel to leave.

She didn't see the man rushing up behind her.

In an instant, her elbow struck something solid, which later turned out to be a chest, and she lost her balance.

Before she could hit the ground, a warm hand wrapped around her waist, steadying her effortlessly.

The strength in that grip grounded her.

Startled, she looked up and met a pair of deep, calm eyes.

His lashes cast a faint shadow beneath his brow. His nose was clean and angular, lips set in a firm line. It was him, the man from the photo.

"It's you..."

Evander's voice came out low and a little out of breath. He just came from the airport.

He'd recognized her too. Their eyes locked, and for a moment, neither moved.

Sarah's heart skipped a beat. A flush crept up her cheeks.

The resistance she'd felt toward this whole meeting began to fade—not completely, but enough to surprise her.

Up close, he was even more magnetic than in the photograph. There was something in the way he carried himself—a quiet control, a kind of gravity—that made her feel as if the world had narrowed to just the two of them.

And the way his hand still rested on her waist, his touch burning through the fabric, made her pulse jump.

She'd seen handsome men. But none of them had the mystery and the restraint.

She found herself staring at his lips, wondering what they might taste like.

Then she heard it—a quiet, teasing laugh.

"Ms. Grant," Evander said, releasing his hold on her waist, his tone light with just the faintest trace of mockery.

"Didn't think I'd be catching you this early in the date. Guess I'm just that charming."
