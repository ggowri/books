Chapter 94 Question

In the evening in London, the setting sun cast a warm orange-red glow over the streets.

Evander and Lois walked hand in hand along the sidewalk in a leisurely manner.

They had just stepped out of the Michelin-starred restaurant on the corner, and the lingering aroma of truffle foie gras and Burgundy red wine from the meal still seemed to play around their noses.

Lois glanced at Evander, a beaming smile lighting up her face as her fingertips lightly brushed his palm.

"The crème brûlée we had was just the right level of sweetness. Even better than what our estate chef makes," she said, her eyes sparkling. "Once the deal is successful, can we come back here again?"

Evander grinned, tightening his grip on her hand. "Absolutely. Next time, we'll order the whole menu. I want you to be fully satisfied."

As they chatted and laughed, they made their way down the street towards Royal One Estate.

There weren't many people on the street, and every now and then, a vehicle would pass by, leaving a soft purr of the engine in its wake.

Suddenly, the previously clear sky darkened, and a powerful gust of wind came out of nowhere.

The sycamore trees by the roadside swayed violently, their branches and leaves thrashing about. Dead leaves and bits of paper were immediately swept up into the air.

"What a sudden gust!"

Lois instinctively leaned closer to Evander, but the strong wind was so forceful that it made her stumble and fall to the ground.

With a startled scream, she tried to get up, but then she saw something that made her heart skip a beat — Evander was lifted right off the ground by the strange wind and was floating in the air like a feather!

"Mr. Bexley!" Lois cried out, her voice choked with sobs.

She reached out to grab him, but all she managed to catch was a wind-carried leaf.

The passers-by on the street were also shocked by this strange sight.

They stopped in their tracks and looked up at Evander hovering in the air. Some took out their phones to snap pictures, while others let out gasps of amazement.

In the air, Evander remained composed.

He steadied himself, frowned, and instinctively activated his X-ray Function.

The next moment, what he saw made him catch his breath—in the middle of the gale, there stood a woman with an elegant figure.

She was wearing an "Eagle Feather Robe" that glinted silver. The feathers fluttered gently in the wind, highlighting her curvy figure.

Her long golden hair cascaded down like a waterfall, with tiny sparkles at the ends.

Her skin was as smooth as silk, and her features were so perfectly formed that she seemed like a divine creation. Especially those lavender eyes, which seemed to hold a whole universe, were incredibly beautiful.

It was a goddess! Evander realized right away.

Rather than struggling, he went along with the wind and slowly drifted towards the woman.

When he landed in her arms, a faint, cool floral fragrance filled his nostrils.

Her body, though cold, was incredibly soft and smooth, like fine silk wrapped around a cloud. Evander felt as if he were in a dream.

"Stay still," the goddess said in a voice as gentle as a spring breeze rippling a lake, yet with a hint of unwavering authority.

She put her arm around Evander's waist.

The Eagle Feather Robe fluttered open, and the two of them turned into a streak of silver light, pierced through the clouds, and disappeared beyond the London skyline.

On the ground, Lois stared blankly in the direction where Evander had vanished, tears streaming down her face. She remained frozen, helpless, not knowing what to do next.

After what felt like forever, Evander felt a sense of lightness as his feet firmly touched the ground.

He opened his eyes and found himself in a magnificent palace.

The palace walls were made of crystal, refracting all sorts of colorful lights. Countless glowing vines hung from the ceiling, and the cool floral scent still lingered in the air.

In the center of the palace, on a raised platform, stood a golden throne inlaid with precious stones.

And the goddess was sitting on the throne, quietly looking at him.

Only then did Evander notice the necklace around the woman's neck.

The necklace was strung together with countless dazzling gemstones. Each one emitted a brilliant light, especially the pigeon-blood ruby in the center, which resembled a dancing flame. Its splendor was so captivating that one couldn't tear their eyes away.

Suddenly, Evander recalled the legendary Brisingamen necklace.

It was the most precious treasure of the Goddess Freya, crafted by four dwarves for her, symbolizing ultimate beauty and power.

"Are you the Goddess Freya?" Evander asked, his voice tinged with surprise.

Freya nodded gently. Her lavender eyes fixed intently on Evander, filled with a complex mix of emotions.

"So, you're Evander?" Her gaze lingered on his face for a long time as she absent-mindedly stroked the armrest of the throne.

The man before her bore an uncanny resemblance to her long-lost husband, Od.

Especially those eyes, with a hint of languidness and sharpness, were almost identical.

Evander knew that Freya must have brought him here because of Felix.

He didn't bring it up first, but calmly replied, "Yes, I'm Evander. May I ask what you want from me, Goddess Freya?"

Freya took a deep breath, suppressing the flutter in her heart, and regained her divine authority in her tone.

"Kelly came to me, saying that you sabotaged Felix and Lois' relationship, swindled all of Felix's money, and even joined forces with Hela to kill him. Is that true?"

Evander let out a soft chuckle, completely unfazed. "Goddess Freya, it's rather hasty to judge based solely on Kelly's words."

Evander recounted everything that had happened on the cruise ship, detailing the events with precise clarity.

"Felix was the one who provoked me first. After losing a fortune in the casino, he held a grudge against me and teamed up with the Scientific Paranormal Research. They even planned a brain dissection on me. Later, Kelly called on Hela."

He paused, his eyes narrowing slightly as he continued, "Hela only kept Felix in Helheim for three days as I asked. She didn't kill him. His 'death' was just a misunderstanding. He's still alive, and he'll be back to normal in three days."

Evander leaned in slightly, his tone steady but assertive, "And as for Lois, she chose me. It wasn't because I forced her. Felix's arrogance and malice made her completely disillusioned with him. It was her own decision."

His gaze softened, and he looked up as he spoke directly to the goddess, "Goddess, you're the one who governs love. Don't you know that true love isn't bound by a marriage contract? It's defined by genuine affection, by sincerity."

Freya fell silent for a moment.

As the goddess of love, she understood that love couldn't be forced. She could also tell from Evander's eyes that he wasn't lying.

Moreover, Hela's act of imprisoning Felix instead of killing him was in line with what she knew about Hela.

Hela, though in charge of the dead, never casually deprived the living of their lives.

She looked at Evander again, and the authority in her eyes gradually faded, replaced by an uncontrollable excitement.

This man not only spoke eloquently but also resembled Od so much in his mannerisms.

Unable to resist, she rose from the throne and slowly approached Evander. Her Eagle Feather Robe fluttered gently, and the Brisingamen necklace glowed even more brightly under the refraction of the crystal.

"Don't be scared..." Freya's voice trembled a little as she reached out to touch Evander's cheek.

Evander instinctively took a step back, avoiding her hand.

Sensing the strange look in Freya's eyes, he was reluctant to get overly involved with the gods.

He spoke with a cool, detached tone, "Goddess Freya, I don't know why you're treating me differently, but you're a goddess, and I'm just a mortal. There shouldn't be anything inappropriate between us.

"You're so noble and sacred. As a goddess, please have some respect for yourself."

Freya's hand froze in mid-air, and for a moment, surprise flashed across her face. Then, her expression shifted back to one of calm composure.

Looking into Evander's eyes, she recognized the unwavering determination there and realized her actions had been a little too forward.

She took a deep breath, stepping back to regain her divine poise.

"I apologize. I was lost in thought, reminiscing about someone. You remind me so much of my husband. Would it be alright if I gave you a hug?"

Evander was caught off guard.

A hug from the goddess of beauty herself? It was an offer he could hardly refuse. The honor was unimaginable.
