Chapter 21 Helicopter Trip

A breeze laced with the scent of fresh grass brushed across Evander's face.

He held the kite spool, feeling the gentle tug of the string as the eagle-patterned kite soared higher, spiraling toward the clouds.

"Wow, it's flying so high!"

Sophia clapped her little hands in delight, the pink bow on her braids bobbing with each movement.

Her little face tilted upward, sunlight dancing on her thick lashes, casting delicate shadows. She looked like a joyful butterfly herself, fluttering with excitement.

Kimberly stood nearby, her white chiffon blouse billowing slightly in the wind, revealing a glimpse of her slender waist.

Today she had on pale blue shorts, her long legs glinting in the sunlight—enough to make any guy's head spin. Every move she made, her curves shifted softly, drawing every man's eye right to them.

Evander's gaze lingered on her for a moment, his throat tightening.

He couldn't believe he had never noticed just how dangerously alluring Kimberly could be.

Compared to Nadine's icy elegance or Naomi's sultry charm, Kimberly had a unique allure.

She possessed both the grace of a socialite and a dangerously seductive edge.

"You're amazing, sir!" Sophia tugged at Evander's sleeve, her eyes sparkling with joy.

"You got it way higher than Aunt Kimberly ever could!"

Kimberly shot Sophia a playful glare. "Oh, you little sweet-talker."

She turned to Evander, a faint smile curving her lips. "Didn't know you had this trick up your sleeve."

"I've flown kites with Lilah a few times," Evander replied.

He looked away, winding the spool as the kite quivered and climbed higher.

The mention of Lilah brought a quick sting to his chest. However, that bitterness was quickly overtaken by another thought.

The second test… maybe it's not as impossible as I once believed.

He pushed the thought away at once, shaking his head as if to clear it.

But when his gaze landed on Kimberly again, his eyes lingered on her chest a moment too long.

The sunlight filtered through the chiffon, casting a faint, shifting pattern of light and shadow on her skin. At that moment, she looked like a painting in motion.

"Sophia," Evander said suddenly, his voice carrying a subtle coaxing tone, "would you like to fly as high as that kite?"

Sophia blinked, then nodded eagerly. "Yes! But... I don't have wings."

Evander crouched to meet her gaze, his eyes level with hers. "That's fine. I have a helicopter.

"How about I take you and your aunt to Vieques Island?

"It's got the cleanest beaches and tons of island birds, and if we're lucky, we might even see some gorillas."

"Gorillas!" Sophia's eyes lit up instantly.

She began to shake Kimberly's arm. "Aunt Kimberly, let's go! I wanna see the gorillas!"

Kimberly's brow furrowed slightly.

"Vieques Island? That's a bit far, isn't it? I heard there aren't many people on that island either..."

"It's not far at all. Just a two-hour flight by helicopter."

Evander met her eyes, his tone earnest. "It'll be a nice break. You've both been swamped lately."

Kimberly hesitated.

She wanted to glean information from Evander, but going so far alone with him felt... risky.

Yet Sophia's pleading eyes, combined with Oliver's instructions, tipped the scales. She nodded. "Alright, but I need to check with my sister first."

"Yay!" Sophia bounced up, planting a kiss on Kimberly's cheek.

"You're the best, Aunt Kimberly!"

Evander watched their exchange, a sly smile tugging at his lips.

Step one of his plan was a success.

At two in the afternoon, Evander landed his helicopter on a nearby airstrip.

He'd earned his pilot's license back in his junior year of college.

The silver-gray machine gleamed coldly in the sunlight, its rotors humming as they spun.

Sophia was all giggles at first, peppering Kimberly with questions as they approached.

But the moment she stepped into the cabin and the rotors' roar grew louder, her face paled.

As soon as the helicopter lifted off, she let out a wail and clung desperately to Kimberly's arm, her face wet with tears and snot.

"I'm scared... Aunt Kimberly, I wanna get down..."

Kimberly, sweating with worry, patted her back.

"It's okay, Sophia. It'll be over soon..."

But Sophia couldn't stop. She cried harder, her little face flushed crimson until she could barely catch her breath.

Evander frowned, quickly maneuvering to descend.

Moments later, the helicopter touched down.

As soon as it stopped, Sophia scrambled to get out.

Kimberly had no choice but to carry her down, soothing her while calling her sister.

"You take Sophia back," she said, glancing at Evander. "I'll stay with Mr. Bexley..."

"No, Aunt Kimberly, you should come with me!" Sophia clung to Kimberly, sobbing, "I don't wanna go alone!"

Kimberly looked at Evander, her eyes apologetic. "Sorry, maybe next time..."

"No worries," Evander cut her off, opening his cabin door. "Want me to drive you back?"

Kimberly shook her head. "Oh, it's fine. My sister's on her way."

Then she added, "How about... you wait for me? I'll hand Sophia off to my sister and come right back. We can still go to Vieques Island as planned."

Evander paused, then grinned. "Sounds wonderful."

Half an hour later, Kimberly's sister arrived and took Sophia away.

Kimberly returned to the helicopter, a trace of embarrassment on her face.

"Sorry to keep you waiting."

"It's nothing," Evander smiled warmly. "Hop in."

Kimberly took a deep breath and climbed aboard.

Without Sophia's cries, the cabin was quieter, filled only with the steady thrum of the rotors.

Kimberly buckled her seatbelt and gazed out the window as the city shrank below, unfurling like a map.

"Do you fly this thing often?" Kimberly asked, trying to make conversation.

"Sometimes."

Evander piloted with ease, stealing a glance at her striking profile.

Sunlight streamed through the window, illuminating the fine hairs on her face and her long, fluttering lashes.

"How come I never saw you fly before?" she asked, turning toward him.

"Well, I didn't have one before," Evander said with a little self-mockery in his voice. "This Bell 825 is new. Cost me 300 million dollars..."

"300 million dollars?!" Kimberly's mouth fell open, her voice shooting up in total shock.

In that instant, she found herself warming to him.

She remembered Nadine saying Evander had ten billion dollars to his name.

Is that actually true?
