Chapter 73 Reconciling With Iris

The morning sun bathed the glass facade of Summit Group's towering headquarters, setting the building aglow with a radiant shimmer.

As the workday loomed, employees hurried through the revolving doors.

Evander pulled his SUV to a stop in front of the building.

The moment he entered the lobby, a ripple of deference swept through the crowd. Employees straightened in their seats and nodded politely as he passed.

"Morning, Mr. Bexley!"

"Good morning, Mr. Bexley!"

Evander nodded back, his voice calm and warm. "Morning, everyone."

His bold performance at the recent auction, coupled with his proven prowess within the company, had erased any lingering doubts among the staff. Where there had once been doubt and condescension, there was now only respect.

He strode to the elevator and pressed the button for the top floor.

Iris' office was up there.

As the elevator hummed upward, Evander's fingers brushed against the Arcane Ring on his middle finger. He'd already mapped out his approach about how to request a month's leave and sway Iris with the promise of "core technology".

With a soft ding, the elevator doors slid open.

Iris' secretary stood poised at the office entrance, her face lighting up with a polite smile when she saw him.

"Mr. Bexley, you're here. Ms. Lennox is inside. Please, go right in."

Evander flashed a quick grin and pushed open the door.

Iris sat behind her expansive desk, her eyes fixed on a cascade of data flickering across her computer screen.

She was a vision of poised authority in a sleek black business suit, her hair neatly swept back, her makeup subtle yet striking. The air around her crackled with the commanding presence of a seasoned executive.

At the sound of his entrance, she glanced up.

The moment her eyes met his, a faint flush crept across her cheeks, and a flicker of unease danced in her gaze.

She still remembered vividly that day outside Nelson Capital Tower, when she had seen him acting cozy with Kimberly. She had been so furious she turned and left on the spot. They hadn't spoken since.

Yet, at the auction, Evander had stunned her by dropping 5 billion dollars to secure a royal emerald jewel for her.

The extravagant gesture had left her heart tangled in a storm of frustration and confusion, unsure how to face him now.

But Evander strolled in as if nothing had happened, his smile easy and disarming as he approached her desk.

"Morning, Iris. You're looking more stunning than ever—that suit suits you perfectly."

Her blush deepened, and she ducked her gaze back to the screen. After a beat, she looked up, her expression settling into its usual cool professionalism.

"You've been AWOL for days, Evander. Per company policy, your salary's docked for the month, and don't even think about a year-end bonus."

"Iris, you're running a tight ship, and I respect that," Evander said, his grin unwavering, not a trace of resentment in his tone.

"But I'm not here about my paycheck. I came to ask for a month's leave."

"No way," Iris shot back without missing a beat, her eyes narrowing with a hint of playful defiance.

"The company's swamped right now. You're the tech director—how can you just vanish for a month?"

Evander was ready. He leaned forward slightly, his voice dropping to a conspiratorial whisper, laced with just a touch of allure.

"Iris, I've got my hands on Vanguard Tech's core AI tech. In two days, I'm heading to London to negotiate a tech swap with Royal Tech for their humanoid robotics breakthroughs."

He paused, letting the weight of his words settle. "When I'm back, I can bring both companies' cutting-edge tech to Summit Group. You really going to say no to that?"

Iris' head snapped up, her eyes wide with disbelief.

As Summit Group's president, she knew the stakes. Vanguard's AI was the gold standard worldwide, and Royal Tech's robotics were unmatched.

If Evander could deliver their secrets, Summit wouldn't just climb out of its current rut. It would leap to the forefront of global tech giants.

Her resolve softened, a sheepish smile tugging at her lips.

"You're serious? You can really pull this off?"

"When have I ever lied to you?" Evander replied, cocking an eyebrow with easy confidence.

Iris' brow furrowed, a spark of suspicion flaring.

"What about that day at Nelson Capital Tower? You and Kimberly, all cozy and handsy outside her place. You call that not lying to me?"

The memory had lingered like a thorn in her chest, still unhealed.

Evander's lips curved into a wry smile as he took a step closer, his tone tinged with mock grievance. "That? That was because of you."

"Me?" Iris blinked, utterly thrown. "What does any of this have to do with me?"

"Everything," Evander said, leaning in closer, his voice a low, intimate murmur.

"I want us to go public, Iris, but Nadine keeps delaying the divorce papers.

"You know Kimberly's close to her, so I asked for her help—hoping she could convince Nadine to move things forward.

"That day you saw us? That's what we were hashing out. It got messy, and you caught it at the worst moment. I didn't get a chance to explain, and you thought the worst."

Iris stared at him, her surprise softening the edges of her lingering hurt.

She'd never imagined the scene tied back to her. The knot in her chest began to unravel, though a trace of skepticism lingered.

"You're telling me the truth? No games?"

Evander leaned closer, his breath warm against her ear, carrying a teasing, almost electric intimacy.

"Iris, you're my woman now. Why would I play games with you? We've been liking each other since our college days, head over heels in our own way. I'd never dream of betraying that."

"Who's head over heels for you?" Iris scoffed, her cheeks flaming as she shoved him back playfully. "Don't flatter yourself. You're not even on my radar."

But her racing heart told a different story.

Evander saw right through her, his grin softening. "Oh, I know. I'm not on your radar—you just keep me right here." He tapped her chest, right over her heart.

"You..."

Iris huffed, glaring at him, though her lips twitched with suppressed amusement.

After a month away, he'd returned with words smooth enough to charm the stars from the sky.

"How about I make it up to you?" Evander offered, his voice warm and coaxing. "Dinner tonight, my treat."

Iris hesitated, her guard still half-raised.

Then, in a flash, Evander extended his hand.

A bouquet of vibrant red roses appeared as if by magic, their petals dewy and fragrant, glowing under the office lights.

"What..." Iris gasped, her eyes wide in disbelief.

"How... Where did those come from? Your hands were empty a second ago!"

Evander smiled as he handed her the roses. "For you," he said softly. "May you always bloom as beautifully as these."

Her hands trembled slightly as she accepted the roses, bringing them close to breathe in their rich, velvety scent.

The fragrance lifted her spirits, chasing away the last of her doubts.

She met his earnest gaze, her tone still playfully grudging.

"Fine. I'll let it slide this time. Your leave is approved."

"But," she added, pointing a finger at him, "if I catch you tangled up with another woman again, we're done."

"Got it, Iris," Evander said with a relieved laugh, nodding.

His plan had worked—leave secured, and the tension with Iris smoothed over. It was a huge victory.

"Eight o'clock tonight, Cloudscape Dining," he said, his voice brimming with anticipation. "Don't stand me up."

Iris finally let a small, genuine smile break through.

"Deal."

Evander shot her a warm grin and turned to leave her office.

He rode the elevator back down, stepping out of Summit Group's gleaming doors with a lightness in his step.
