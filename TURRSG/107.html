Chapter 108 Crown Orders

The next morning, Evander woke and gently brushed his hand through Lois' tousled hair, his expression filled with tenderness.

Watching her sleep in his arms, he couldn't resist pressing a soft kiss to her forehead, careful not to wake her.

When the sunlight crept across the foot of the bed, Lois finally opened her eyes.

A flush spread across her cheeks as she met Evander's gentle gaze, and she wrapped her arms around his neck.

Still sounding a little sleepy, she whispered, "Mr. Bexley, how long have you been awake? Why didn't you wake me up?"

"I couldn't bring myself to," Evander replied with a smile, giving her nose a playful pinch. "Come on, we need to get going. We're meeting Walter today, so we should head out early."

Lois nodded and slowly sat up with his help.

After they both washed up, they went downstairs for breakfast.

The dining room table was already laid out with a delicate spread—golden toast, perfectly fried soft-boiled eggs, and a fresh fruit salad.

As they ate, Lois chatted with Evander about what he planned to discuss with Walter.

She didn't understand the technical details, but she listened with earnest focus and occasionally offered naive yet amusing suggestions that made Evander laugh more than once.

When breakfast was finished, they gathered their things and walked out of the estate side by side.

Lois slipped her arm through Evander's and rested her head lightly on his shoulder, a bright smile of contentment on her face.

She didn't notice the black sedan parked at the corner of the street. Inside, Felix pointed his phone at them, taking shots of every moment they were close.

Felix stared at their picture together, gripping his phone so hard his knuckles turned white. His eyes burned with jealousy and hate.

Frigg's words from last night still rang in his head, but watching Lois lean so close to Evander made his blood boil all over again.

Felix lowered his phone, started the car, and drove toward the federal bank. He intended to find his father, Mourid, and then meet with Edward.

I'll make Evander pay and take Lois back!

*****

Meanwhile, Evander and Lois were heading to the Institute for Esoteric Studies.

On the way, Evander turned on his tablet and reviewed the details of the Decision Matrix Model once more.

Lois sat back quietly in her seat, watching him concentrate, her chest filling with pride.

She knew Evander would nail this breakthrough research soon. And when he did, everyone would finally see just how amazing he was.

More than half an hour later, the two of them arrived. Walter was already waiting in his office, and when he saw them enter, he greeted them warmly.

He smiled, "Hello, Mr. Bexley, Princess Lois. I went over some more material last night on the transformative logic of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life. Today, we can dig deeper into how it might be integrated into a robot's autonomous consciousness system."

Evander nodded, set his tablet on the desk, and pulled up the model.

He said, "I also made some revisions to the model last night, Professor Harlow. Look here.

"I integrated the Crown's logic of strength and initiative into the robot's goal-setting module, so that when it carries out tasks, it can adapt its strategies more proactively instead of waiting for instructions."

Walter bent over for a closer look, nodding in approval from time to time.

Lois sat nearby with the stack of notes Walter had prepared. She didn't understand much of the technical content, but she still leafed through them with careful attention.

*****

The atmosphere in the royal palace was oppressively heavy.

Edward sat on the throne, his expression dark and severe.

Felix stood before him, holding up his phone. The screen showed a picture he'd snapped that morning of Evander and Lois together.

Mourid stood at the side, worry etched across his face. "King Edward, please look at this."

Felix handed the phone to Edward, his voice laced with calculated grievance as he said, "Lois teamed up with that commoner, Evander, to poison me with a slow-acting toxin and force me to sign away the engagement.

"And now they flaunt their relationship openly, with no regard for the royal family's dignity.

"Evander is nothing but a fraud. He isn't worthy of Lois. He only wants to use her status to strike a deal with Royal Tech!"

Edward glared at the picture on his phone while Felix kept talking trash, his blood boiling. It was only natural, since he always cared deeply about the royal family's reputation.

Lois, the royal princess, had gotten way too friendly with some random nobody and even broke things off with the Gosse family. There was no way Edward would let that slide.

"Unacceptable!" Edward slammed the armrest of his throne, his voice heavy with fury.

He continued, "Lois has gone too far. Who is this Evander? How dare he behave so brazenly? Don't worry, Mourid. Your family will get the explanation they deserve."

Then Edward grabbed his phone, called the Royal Guard, and gave them this ice-cold order.

He said, "Go to the Institute for Esoteric Studies and get Lois back to Royal Three Estate. Keep her there until I say otherwise.

"And dig up everything you can about some guy called Evander Bexley. I want to know exactly what gives him the nerve to approach my daughter."

After Edward hung up, he looked at Felix, his tone softening, "Felix, you have my word, I will make this right for you.

"Lois is a princess. Only the crown can choose who she marries. Evander won't ever get to marry her. Once she comes to her senses, I will make sure she honors her engagement with you again."

Hearing that, Felix broke into a pleased smile. He bowed quickly. "Thank you, King Edward. I knew you'd have my back. I'll take good care of Lois and keep that commoner away from her."

Mourid jumped in, "Thank you, King Edward, for understanding everything. You're doing our family such a huge favor."

Edward waved his hand in dismissal. "That's all for now. Go on, and I'll notify you when there is news."

Felix and Mourid bowed and left the palace.

Once inside the car, Felix couldn't hold back a laugh. "Did you see that, Dad? King Edward is on our side. This time, Evander is finished, and Lois will be back with me soon."

Mourid finally eased a breath and nodded. "Smart move getting King Edward involved. But you gotta keep yourself in check from now on. If you keep acting wild like before, even King Edward's help won't make Lois truly stay with you."

"Yeah, I get it." Felix nodded without really caring, his head still full of hate for Evander.

He didn't just want Lois back. He wanted Evander disgraced and ruined, forced out of London for good.

*****

At the Institute for Esoteric Studies, Evander and Walter were still caught up in a heated discussion over the finer details of the Decision Matrix Model.

Just then, Lois received a call from an unfamiliar number. Believing it to be someone from the royal staff, she answered right away.
