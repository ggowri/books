Chapter 17 Cornered by Conspiracy

"It's Denny's doing."

Iris' voice came from the doorway. She stood there, travel-worn, eyes red.

"Our servers were hacked. We lost some experimental data."

Evander hurried over, grasping her cold hands.

"Don't panic. We'll figure this out."

"How can I not panic?"

William paced, agitated. "Tomorrow's the summit debate. This news is timed to humiliate you publicly."

Ciotti spoke up, "I have a way."

She faced Iris. "Give me the list of lost data and those 'anonymous experts' names."

Iris blinked. "You..."

"Trust her." Evander met Ciotti's eyes, resolute. "She can help."

A warmth flickered in Ciotti's blue eyes. She pulled out her phone.

"Give me an hour."

At nightfall, Ciotti slapped a file on the coffee table.

It detailed Denny's payments to hackers, evidence of the "anonymous experts" taking bribes, and a recording.

In it, Denny gloated about ruining Evander.

"How'd you pull this off?" Iris gaped.

"Trade secret."

Ciotti winked, turning to Evander. "Tomorrow's debate is your stage."

Evander gripped the file, his fingers trembling with force.

Looking at Iris and William beside him, and Ciotti sipping her drink by the railing, he suddenly felt that they were sure to win this fight.

The next morning, at nine, the AI Summit's debate hall was packed.

Denny sat across, smirking with smug assurance.

Evander presented his evidence, stepping to the podium.

Halfway through, a tall, sharp-featured blond man suddenly rose from Denny's side.

He wore a perfectly tailored charcoal suit, a silver badge gleaming on his chest.

The badge marked him as part of the European Bar Association.

"Objection."

His voice carried a faint German accent, each word crisp. "Before discussing Mr. Westbrook's character, shouldn't we first address patent ownership?"

He adjusted his gold-rimmed glasses, scanning the room.

"Based on our documents, the brain-computer interface's core principles were proposed by Germany's Noah Lab in 2018 and exclusively licensed to Westbrook Group in 2020."

The big screen flashed two files.

One was Noah Lab's patent certificate; the other, a licensing agreement with Westbrook Group's red seal, dated three years ago.

"That's impossible!"

Iris shot up, her voice shaking. "Our core algorithm was independently developed, with full experimental records!"

"Records can be faked, but international patent filings don't lie."

The blond lawyer produced a USB drive. "Here is a comparison report between Noah Lab's original code and Summit Group's published paper. The similarity is as high as 91.7%."

He paused, his tone commanding authority. "I believe anyone who respects intellectual property can make a fair judgment."

Ciotti's lawyer stood to counter but frowned at the encrypted German documents.

"These files need time to verify..."

"Debate time is limited, sir."

The blond lawyer cut in, "My client has provided notarized licensing agreements, while your side lacks any pre-2020 research evidence, correct?"

Evander froze, completely at a loss.

Staring at the unfamiliar code on the screen, he suddenly understood why Denny had been so arrogant.

This wasn't some hastily thrown-together trap—it was a carefully laid hunt, planned long in advance.

He had never even heard the name Noah Lab. None of Ciotti's friends in Europe had ever mentioned it either, which meant it had been deliberately kept in the shadows.

In the audience, William was pounding the table in frustration, but the staff held him back.

Iris' face had gone white as paper. She clutched the corner of Evander's jacket so tightly her nails nearly dug into his skin.

Denny lounged back in his chair, a victor's smirk tugging at his lips.

He cast a glance toward Nadine, who was calmly touching up her lipstick, her eyes cold and distant.

"The voting results are in."

The host's voice cut through the silence, carrying a subtle trace of awkwardness.

"After urgent discussions between the panel and patent experts..."

He paused, clearly reluctant, before announcing, "This debate's winner is Westbrook Group. Summit Group is suspected of patent infringement and will be handed over to the relevant authorities for investigation."

The room erupted in shock.

Camera flashes rained down on Evander like a storm. Reporters' questions came sharp and fast.

"Mr. Bexley, how do you respond to the plagiarism accusation?"

"Will Summit Group abandon the brain–computer interface project?"

"Was your so-called AI ethics initiative nothing but a smokescreen?"

Evander stood there, unaware of how much time had passed.

The evidence in his hands now felt like nothing more than scraps of useless paper.

If Denny was willing to stoop to this kind of trick, then he clearly intended to crush Summit Group once and for all.

"Let's go."

Ciotti suddenly took his arm, her voice calm in a way that felt almost out of place. "This isn't the place to talk."

Iris bit her lip until it bled, barely keeping her steps steady.

William followed, sighing heavily.

"Too despicable! This is straight-up robbery logic!"

As they left the debate hall, they ran head-on into Denny and Nadine.

"Evander, I told you you couldn't win."

Denny's voice dripped with unchecked arrogance. "Be smart and disband Summit Group now. I might even let you manage a department at Westbrook Group."

Nadine stayed silent, her striking eyes raking over Evander like he was trash left on the street.

Evander's gaze burned with a fury that could've scorched the air.

"Denny, a win built on these low tactics won't last."

"Yet I won, didn't I?"

Denny scoffed, "In business, it's the result that count, not how you get there."

He leaned in, his voice a whisper only Evander could hear.

"Don't forget, Oliver's 85 billion dollars is about to hit Westbrook Group's accounts. Summit Group's end is coming."

With that, he slung an arm around Nadine's waist and strode off amidst the flashing lights.
