Chapter 1 Cheated

"Evander, Denny and his fiancée Iris are coming over for dinner tonight. Make sure you cook something special."

Evander Bexley stopped sweeping and looked down at the nasty burn marks covering the back of his hand.

"Nad, can we just eat out tonight? The doctor said I shouldn't get my hand wet yet..."

"Just do what I tell you! It's just a small injury—wrap it up and you'll be fine! You're just being lazy!" Nadine looked at her husband with ice-cold disgust. "Oh, and I left my limited edition lipstick somewhere in the bedroom. Go find it.

"And if you can't find it, don't even think about coming to bed tonight."

Evander pressed his lips together and nodded quietly. "Got it."

His words came out in a submissive whisper.

Six years of marriage. He had given up his own career to support their home life, dedicating himself to helping Nadine's career grow. Thanks to his support, Nadine became a well-known CEO, while he earned a reputation as nothing more than a house husband living off his wife's success.

But he'd always thought that as long as they had love, what other people said didn't matter.

He headed upstairs and pushed open their bedroom door.

Crouching down, he reached for the nightstand drawer when he heard a buzzing sound. Then came the sharp ding of a text.

He glanced up—

Nadine's latest iPhone was lying on the bed, the phone she never let out of her sight.

Evander was about to grab it and take it downstairs when he noticed the screen was still lit up.

A text preview flashed across the display, and the words hit him like a punch to the gut:

"Baby, after dinner tonight, let's drive out for some car fun..."

Evander's eyes went wide.

The screen suddenly went dark, showing only Nadine's wallpaper photo—she was standing on the red carpet in a designer gown, with an elegant yet distant smile.

His heart felt like someone was squeezing it in a vice, making it hard to breathe.

Did he read that wrong?

He grabbed the phone, his knuckles going white from gripping it so hard.

The text preview was gone, leaving just the cold lock screen.

The message preview was gone, leaving only the cold lock screen.

No, he wasn't seeing things. Even with his eyes closed, the message was still crystal clear in his mind.

But who was it?

Who the hell was texting his wife stuff like that?

Maybe it was some kind of prank?

"No way. I'm just overthinking this."

He muttered to himself, but his hands moved on their own, lighting up the screen with shaking fingers hovering over the keypad.

He tried Nadine's birthday: "Incorrect"

Their anniversary: "Incorrect"

Their daughter Lilah's birthday: "Incorrect"

Each error notification felt like a slap in the face.

His breathing grew heavy as beads of sweat rolled down his temples, dripping onto the phone screen and blurring into small wet spots.

Suspicion burned through his chest like fire. He almost threw the phone at the wall, but that message kept pulling at his mind. He had to know who was texting his wife.

Suddenly, his thumb stopped moving.

With trembling fingers, he typed in Denny's birthday.

The phone unlocked with a soft click.

Evander's temples throbbed.

Denny was his half-brother—his dad's affair baby. When Denny's mom finally managed to steal their father away, she'd kicked Evander and his mom to the curb.

Lately, because Denny was dating Nadine's friend Iris Lennox—the mayor's daughter—he'd been finding excuses to come over for dinner all the time.

Had they been hooking up this whole time?

Thinking about the barely hidden disgust in Denny's eyes, remembering how he'd made soup for Nadine when she came home late last night...

The message screen opened, showing the sender as "Denny."

The full text was like a knife straight through his heart:

"Baby, after dinner tonight, let's drive out for some car fun. Get your husband to cook something that'll get us in the mood—like those oysters from last time. I'll need the energy to fuck you."

Evander's hands wouldn't stop shaking.

He opened "Denny" chat history and found a bunch of explicit messages:

"Didn't get enough in your guest room last time, next time let's do it in my living room..."

"Tomorrow we won't need protection, I can come inside you..."

At the bottom was a video file.

Evander could barely breathe as he tapped it—

The video showed their guest room, the sheets he'd just changed yesterday looked all messed up and wrinkled.

Nadine was naked on the bed, her hair stuck to her sweaty neck.

And the guy on top of her—he knew that face way too well.

It really was Denny!

"Ah... Denny, harder..." Nadine's moans came through the phone speaker, full of desire. "You're so much better than your pathetic brother... When I tell him not to touch me, he doesn't even try..."

"Glad you know it, Nadine." Denny's laugh was sleazy and smug. "He has no clue his innocent wife is actually a total slut in bed, does he? You suck me so well every time! And your little hole feels just as good!"

"Stop talking about that loser..." Nadine's voice was full of disgust. "If I didn't need him to babysit Lilah, I would've dumped him years ago!"

The video continued to play, each sound and image piercing Evander like knives until his vision began to blur.

He gripped the phone so hard the case started cracking.

Six years of devotion, six years of putting up with everything, six years of real love—and this is what he got back. Complete betrayal, right under his own roof.

"Denny! Nadine!"

Evander's eyes turned blood-red. "You cheating pieces of shit! I'll make you both pay for this! I'll make you wish you were never born!"

He stared at the phone screen, his jaw tightened.

On the nightstand sat a bottle of 1982 Lafite he'd just opened—a surprise for their sixth anniversary.

Now it felt like the universe's cruelest joke.

Taking a deep breath, Evander forced down the rage burning inside him. The red faded from his mind, leaving only ice-cold calm.

He put the phone into his pocket and turned toward the kitchen, each step steady and purposeful, like he was crushing six years of love under his feet.

When he put on his apron, his hands were perfectly steady.

He reached into the back of the storage cabinet and pulled out a small brown bottle.

The Lafite's rich, fruity aroma filled the air as he opened it.

Evander tilted the wine bottle, watching the deep red liquid pour into the decanter like blood.

He unscrewed the small brown bottle and added several drops of the clear liquid, swirling the wine until it mixed.

The red wine remained pristine—no sign that anything had been added.
