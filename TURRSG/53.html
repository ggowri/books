Chapter 54 Volleyball Game

Saturday morning, the private beach in Nogales shimmered beneath a flawless sky. The sun painted the fine sand a warm, golden-orange, while the sea breeze whipped whitecaps against the rocks, each crash echoing like a drumbeat.

At center stage, a volleyball net stretched taut across the court. Tiny colored flags danced along the ropes in the wind, adding a festive touch to the otherwise charged air.

At exactly ten o'clock, Jack's military SUV rolled to a stop.

He jumped out first, bare-chested, his bronzed torso gleaming in the sunlight. Rigid pecs, carved abs, and battle-hardened arms all spoke of years of disciplined training. In nothing but black shorts, a volleyball tucked casually under one arm, he exuded raw strength—and a silent challenge aimed straight at Evander.

Aileen and Emily emerged behind him, drawing their own share of stares.

Aileen's deep-red bikini clung to her curves, the shade amplifying her mature allure—long legs, a slim waist, and a neckline that teased with effortless elegance. Emily, in contrast, radiated youth. Her pale-blue swimsuit highlighted her freshness, every bounce of her step brimming with unrestrained vitality.

"Jack, looking buff as ever!" Emily teased, giving his arm a playful slap before skipping toward the net.

She shot him a cheeky grin. "But I'm on Mr. Bexley's team today. Don't cry if you lose!"

Jack smirked, confidence oozing. "Don't worry about me. Just make sure you don't regret it."

Then he turned to Aileen, tucking a strand of hair behind her ear in a tender, proprietary gesture. "Stick close to me. We'll win this easily."

Aileen forced a smile, but her gaze drifted to the road. Where was Evander?

Her answer came with the deep, throaty roar of an engine.

A sleek Rolls-Royce Phantom glided into view, its polished black surface reflecting the sunlight like a mirror. Norman's car.

The passenger door opened, and Evander stepped out.

He wore loose white beach shorts and a blue short-sleeved shirt with the top buttons undone, revealing just enough collarbone and chest to hint at the lean muscle beneath. The sun kissed his skin, giving him a clean, radiant glow that contrasted sharply with Jack's heavy, battle-forged presence.

As Evander approached, his eyes lingered—just briefly—on the two sisters.

Emily's playful youth and Aileen's poised elegance made a striking tableau. For a heartbeat, his expression flickered, but he quickly masked it with an easy smile.

"Mr. Bexley, finally!" Emily dashed to him, latching onto his arm, her body brushing softly against his. "We've been waiting forever!"

Evander felt the warmth of her touch but smoothly freed his arm with a polite smile. "Sorry I'm late—traffic was a nightmare."

He met Jack's stare squarely, utterly unruffled by the other man's sculpted physique.

Jack's lips curled into a thin sneer. He tossed the volleyball into Evander's hands.

"Best of three. Loser buys dinner."

The disdain in his voice was unmistakable.

Evander caught the volleyball, savoring the firm weight beneath his fingertips. He smiled faintly, saying nothing, and followed Emily across to their side of the net.

On the opposite side, Aileen took her place beside Jack. Her brows pinched slightly; worry clouded her expression. Jack had been a volleyball ace since his academy days. Evander, no matter how charming, didn't look like the type who could hold his own in a match like this. If he lost badly, wouldn't it be humiliating?

The whistle blew. Game on.

Jack was first to serve. He tossed the ball high, his muscles flexing as he slammed down with ruthless force. The ball shot forward like a cannonball, a white blur screaming straight for Evander's face.

A cocky grin spread across Jack's face. He wasn't aiming for points—he was aiming for humiliation.

"Evander, look out!" Aileen blurted, her voice tight.

Emily clasped her fists nervously, her heart leaping to her throat.

But Evander stood cool and unshaken.

After Norman's vitality serum and a month of intensive karate training, his reflexes were razor-sharp. He shifted lightly to the side, letting the ball whistle past his cheek, and then—at the last instant—snapped his arm out to snatch it from midair.

A sharp thwack! echoed as his palm closed around the ball.

Gasps rippled across the court.

Evander bounced the ball lazily in his hand, his smile easy, his voice smooth. "Thanks for the 'serve,' Mr. Dawson. But… the rules say it's supposed to go over the net, right? Or were you just handing it to us?"

With a casual flick, he lobbed the ball gently across the net. It arced perfectly and dropped just within reach of Aileen—too easy to miss, like he was offering her a gift rather than a challenge.

The move wasn't brute strength. It was poise. Confidence. Grace.

"Nice and steady," Evander added softly, as though in contrast to Jack's brute force.

Emily whooped and clapped, her face glowing with excitement. She bounced on her toes and patted Evander's shoulder. "Mr. Bexley, that was so cool! I thought that ball was gonna smash you in the face!"

Evander only gave her a small, knowing smile.

Back in college, Evander had been the star of the volleyball team. His form, his power, his precision—none of it had dulled.

With the extra month of brutal physical training under Norman's watch, handling Jack was child's play.

As the game wore on, Evander began to show his true colors.

Every thunderous smash Jack launched across the net was defused with effortless ease.

His sets dropped neatly into Emily's waiting hands, timed to perfection.

And his spikes—fast, angled, unpredictable—repeatedly left Jack and Aileen scrambling in the sand.

The crowd of onlookers—families lounging on the private beach, curious attendants, even a few joggers who'd paused to watch—started to murmur in excitement.

The mysterious billionaire wasn't just a wallet; he had skills.

Then came the moment.

Evander leapt high into the air, his body arching like a pro, and with one brutal swing slammed the ball down. It streaked across the court like black lightning.

Jack's eyes widened. He couldn't react in time.

Smack!

The ball nailed him square in the cheek, knocking him backwards into the sand. A plume of dust exploded around him.

The court went dead silent for a heartbeat.

Then Emily squealed, clapping her hands. "Mr. Bexley, you're amazing!"

Aileen pressed her fingers to her lips, a laugh slipping out despite herself.

There was a glint of triumph in her eyes—Jack had finally been knocked down a peg.

Red-faced, Jack staggered to his feet. His cheek throbbed, his pride stung. He glared daggers at Evander but swallowed his rage.

He'd started the match with arrogance; now he'd have to choke down the humiliation.

But no matter how hard Jack fought, momentum was gone.

The first set ended with Evander and Emily in the lead.

The second, their coordination was flawless, Emily's serves feeding perfectly into Evander's devastating spikes. Another victory.

By the third, Jack's energy was shot. Evander and Emily crushed him without mercy.

When the final point hit the sand, Emily squealed and launched herself at Evander. Laughing, she wrapped her arms around his neck and her legs around his waist, clinging to him like an excited child.

"Mr. Bexley, we won! You're incredible!"

Caught off guard, Evander froze for a split second.

Then he steadied her with gentle hands at her waist. Her perfume was faint and sweet, drifting up as he bent to press a soft kiss to her forehead.

"It's all about teamwork," he said with a warm smile.

Emily's cheeks bloomed pink. Her eyes, shy but bold, lifted to his lips. She rose onto her toes, ready to bridge the gap—

But Aileen was there in an instant, tugging her sister back with a firm hand.

"That's enough, Emily." Her voice was calm, but the flash of jealousy in her eyes betrayed her. "We're all covered in sand. Go shower and change. Dinner's on me tonight."

Emily pouted, but relented, sliding down from Evander's arms.

She shot him one last wistful glance. "Don't forget, Mr. Bexley—we're having dinner together later!"

"I'll be there," Evander promised, his smile warm, his tone steady.

On the sidelines, Jack looked like he'd bitten into a lemon.

His fiancée and her sister were both drawn to the same man, and he'd been humiliated on the sand. But making a scene now, after such a loss, would only worsen his shame.

Jaw tight, he grabbed his towel and muttered, "I'm going to shower. Meet you in the lot."

Aileen lingered a moment, giving Evander a look that mixed gratitude, curiosity, and something softer she couldn't name. "You too, Mr. Bexley. Get changed—we'll wait."

Evander nodded. He strolled into the waves, letting the seawater wash the sand from his skin, then returned to his Rolls-Royce.

Inside, he shut the tinted windows, stripped out of his beach clothes, and slid into a perfectly tailored suit.
