Chapter 91 Die?

Dawn broke, casting a warm glow over a luxurious villa in central London. The early-morning sunlight filtered through the slats of the curtains, spilling across the room.

Fred, the head of the Scientific Paranormal Research department, sat uneasily on the living room sofa, tapping his fingers restlessly on his knee. His face was stormy, full of frustration.

Two special forces soldiers stood by, their assault rifles at the ready, eyes alert and fixed on the door.

They'd been waiting all morning for news about Evander's death.

"What's taking so long?" Fred muttered, irritation evident in his voice.

The previous day, Felix had called, boasting that Evander's death would be confirmed by now. But it was nearly noon, and there was still no word. Felix was unreachable.

Just then, the door to the villa burst open.

A butler in uniform rushed in, his face pale and strained. "Mr. White! Terrible news! Mr. Gosse is in trouble!"

Fred shot up from his seat, heart racing. "Trouble? What do you mean?"

"Is Evander dead?" Fred asked quickly, dreading the answer.

"No..." The butler's voice trembled. "It's Mr. Gosse. He... he died in his sleep. Mrs. Maria Gosse went to wake him up and found he had stopped breathing."

Fred froze, his mind racing. "What?!" He staggered back in disbelief. "How could Felix be dead? He was fine just yesterday!"

Fred quickly jumped into his car, accompanied by the two soldiers, and sped towards Felix's family estate.

Half an hour later, Fred reached Felix's bedroom.

The room was in disarray.

Felix's mother, Maria Gosse, was slumped over the bedside, wailing.

His father, Mourid Gosse, stood nearby, his face ashen, fists clenched, eyes filled with grief and rage.

On the bed lay Felix, pale as a sheet, eyes shut, lips purple. There was no doubt he was gone.

Fred stepped forward to check his carotid artery. The coldness and lack of a pulse confirmed his worst fears.

Frowning deeply, Fred was thoroughly puzzled.

Felix had always been healthy, with no underlying conditions. How could he just drop dead?

And there were no signs of a struggle; it was all so creepy.

"Was there anything unusual last night?" Fred asked Mourid seriously.

Mourid took a deep breath to compose himself. "Last night, after Felix got home, he said he wanted to rest early and asked us not to disturb him.

"We thought he was just tired, so we didn't think much of it. It wasn't until this morning when Maria went to wake him that we found... he was gone."

Fred's expression grew even more solemn.

Last night was when Felix went to see his aunt, Kelly.

Could Felix's death be related to her?

But Felix's plan was to get rid of Evander. Why was he the one who ended up dead in his own bed?

"Could it be Evander?" Fred murmured.

Evander had supernatural abilities and had escaped from the Scientific Paranormal Research's lab. Maybe he'd turned the tables on Felix.

Hearing the name "Evander", Maria looked up, her eyes full of hatred.

"It's that damn lowlife! It's gotta be him! Felix was talking about getting rid of him yesterday. He must've fought back and killed Felix! Mr. White, you have to avenge him!"

Fred kept silent.

He walked over to the window, looking out at the street, his mind racing.

If Evander was behind this, his powers were truly terrifying.

Killing someone without a trace or a struggle? It was unthinkable.

Fred suddenly spoke, his voice firm, "Mr. Mourid Gosse, hold off on cremating and burying Felix for now. I've got a feeling there's more to his death than we're seeing. "There might even be a way to bring him back. Keep his body intact until I can figure this out."

"Bring him back?" Mourid and Maria were both shocked.

Maria grabbed Fred's hand eagerly. "Mr. White, are you serious? Can Felix really be revived?"

Fred nodded, "I can't make any promises, but there's a chance. Remember, both Felix and I are part of the Scientific Paranormal Research. Something doesn't add up here."

He was being deliberately cagey, actually planning to wait until he found Evander and uncovered the truth before making any decisions.

Mourid nodded. It sounded crazy, but he was willing to trust Fred. "Okay, I'll do as you say. I won't bury Felix until you've sorted this out."

Without another word, Fred turned and left the villa with his two special-forces soldiers.

He hopped into the car, his face grim, and told the driver, "Take me to Royal Tech!"

He knew Evander was probably with Lois at the moment. To solve this mystery, he had to find Evander.

Meanwhile, at the headquarters of Royal Tech, Evander and Lois were sitting in the reception room, facing an elderly gentleman in a suit with graying hair—Duke Chad Cavendish, the senior advisor of Royal Tech and Lois' uncle.

The reception room was sumptuously and stately decorated. A British royal insignia hung on the wall, and delicate coffee sets were laid out on the table.

Lois picked up her cup, took a sip, and smiled at Chad. "Uncle, this is Mr. Bexley, the one I mentioned. He's a senior technical advisor at Vanguard Tech. He's here to talk about a potential core-technology exchange with us."

Chad gave Evander a once-over. "Mr. Bexley, I've heard of Vanguard Tech. You've really made a name for yourselves in AI.

"But you should know, Royal Tech's core technologies, especially in humanoid robots, are the result of years of hard work. We've never shared them with anyone outside."

Evander remained calm, pulling a document from his briefcase and sliding it across the table to Chad.

"Your Grace, I understand your concerns. This document outlines Vanguard Tech's core AI technologies—autonomous learning algorithms, voice interaction systems, and emotion-recognition tech.

"If applied to Royal Tech's humanoid robots, they could triple in intelligence. These robots might even think for themselves and show emotions."

Chad picked up the document, flipping through it with increasing interest. The more he read, the more impressed he became.

The technologies outlined in the document were groundbreaking—exactly the kind of advancements Royal Tech had been struggling to achieve.

"This... these are really Vanguard Tech's core technologies?" Chad asked, his voice tinged with surprise.
