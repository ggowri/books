Chapter 100 Lois' Mom

A gentle, moist breeze drifted through the woods, caressing Kelly's pale face and making her shiver involuntarily.

Evander held her up, moving at a calm, unhurried pace.

Avoiding the paths that the Scientific Paranormal Research might search, they made their way towards Ragnarok Psychiatric Hospital.

Leaning on his arm, Kelly no longer had that wild look in her eyes. Instead, there was just a touch of blankness and exhaustion. Every so often, she'd murmur, "Will Felix really wake up tomorrow?"

Evander always nodded patiently to reassure her.

Over half an hour later, the outline of Ragnarok Psychiatric Hospital finally appeared in the distance.

Evander let out a sigh of relief and quickened their steps. Quietly, he led Kelly through the back door of the hospital.

Martha was arranging medications in the corridor. When she spotted them, her eyes popped in surprise.

"Mr. Bexley? Ms. Kelly Gosse? What are you two doing back? Where are the folks from the Scientific Paranormal Research?"

"Don't worry about them," Evander cut in seriously. "We've got to keep Kelly safe. We can't let the Scientific Paranormal Research get their hands on her again.

"They don't want to protect her; they just want to use her psychic powers for bad stuff."

Martha's expression turned serious, and she nodded quickly.

"Got it! No worries. We've got an underground ward. It was for patients with serious violent tendencies. It's well-hidden, and the entrance is behind the pharmacy's storage room. Even if the Scientific Paranormal Research people come, they won't find it. I'll take Ms. Kelly Gosse there right away."

With that, Martha grabbed a thick coat for Kelly and called over two orderlies. They carefully supported Kelly.

Kelly looked back at Evander, her lips moving, but in the end, she said nothing and followed Martha towards the pharmacy.

Watching their figures fade at the end of the corridor, Evander finally felt at ease.

The underground ward was hidden and had medical staff guarding it. Kelly would be safe for now.

It wouldn't be easy for Fred to reach her.

Just as Evander turned to leave the hospital, his phone in his pocket rang. The screen showed "Lois".

He quickly answered, "Hey, Lois. I just got Kelly back to the hospital. She's safe now."

"Great!" Lois' voice had a smile and a bit of nervousness. "Mr. Bexley, are you free now? My mom wants to see you. She's inviting you to dinner at the Royal One Estate tonight and said you gotta come."

"Your mom?" Evander was caught off guard, and his nerves tensed up right away.

Charlotte Campanella was Lois' mother and a member of the royal family. She was high-class and rarely showed up in public.

Her sudden dinner invitation clearly meant more than just a casual get-together.

"Yeah." Lois softened her voice with a hint of coaxing. "Mom said she wants to meet the guy I've been talking about.

"Dress nice, not too casual. Mr. Bexley, don't embarrass me."

Evander couldn't help but smile, and his nervousness eased a bit, but he also felt a new sense of seriousness. "Don't worry. I'll get ready and won't disappoint you. What time's dinner?"

"Seven. Be there at six-thirty. I'll wait for you at the manor gate. Okay, I won't bug you anymore. Remember to wear a suit!" Lois said.

After hanging up, Evander stood at the hospital entrance, looking at the distant streets, already planning in his head.

To meet Charlotte, he had to dress formally and bring a proper gift.

Royal family folks were used to fancy things, so an ordinary gift wouldn't cut it. It had to be something meaningful and valuable.

Then he thought of the "Inventory Cube", which held lots of rare treasures.

With a mere thought, a translucent virtual image of a Rubik's Cube materialized right before Evander.

He reached out and gave it a gentle tap.

Instantly, the cube unfolded, presenting a neatly-arranged display of items, much like a mini-museum.

"Suits..." Evander started rummaging through the "Clothing" section.

Before long, he spotted a pure-white handmade suit.

The fabric was top-notch Italian wool, soft yet with a nice stiffness. The collar and cuffs were embroidered with delicate, understated patterns, oozing an air of luxury.

Right next to it was a dark-blue silk tie with tiny pearl embroidery, a perfect match for the suit.

"Now, for a gift..."

His eyes landed on a rosewood box.

He opened it, and there lay a brooch.

The centerpiece of the brooch was a golden rose, with small rubies inlaid on the petals and two pearls on the stem.

It was so exquisitely crafted that it was impossible to look away.

At the bottom of the box, there was an engraved line:

[Custom-made for the French royal family in the 17th century.]

Clearly, it was a historical treasure, just right for Charlotte.

After making his selections of the suit and the gift, Evander retrieved his black Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost.

He got into the car and, in the spacious back seat, changed into the suit and tied the tie.

Then, he carefully placed the brooch in a velvet box and slipped it into his suit pocket.

All set, he started the car and headed towards Royal One Estate.

The car glided smoothly through the London streets.

The evening sun streamed through the window, casting a soft glow on his white suit.

As he watched the passing scenery outside, Evander still felt a bit jittery.

Was Charlotte seeing him because of his connection with Lois?

Or was it about the failed cooperation with Royal Tech?

No matter the reason, he had to handle it well and avoid any blunders.

Over half an hour later, the car pulled up at the entrance of Royal One Estate.

Lois was already waiting there.

She was wearing a light-blue dress, her long hair elegantly pulled up in a chignon, a gentle smile on her face.

As Lois saw Evander's car, her eyes brightened, and she quickly walked over.

Evander stopped the car, opened the door, and stepped out.

Lois looked at him, her eyes full of admiration.

"Mr. Bexley, you look amazing today! This suit really suits you," she said.

Evander smiled and reached for her hand. "You're gorgeous too. Is... is your mom in the estate?"

"Yeah, she's in the living room having coffee," Lois said, squeezing his hand reassuringly.

"Don't be nervous. Mom is nice, just a bit serious. Just be yourself, and she won't give you a hard time."

Evander nodded and followed Lois into the estate.

The garden was beautifully maintained, and the evening breeze carried the sweet fragrance of flowers.

They strolled through the garden and into the main-house living room.

The living room was sumptuously decorated, with valuable paintings on the walls and a white piano in the corner.

A lady in a purple dress was sitting on the sofa, sipping coffee.

She resembled Lois, but with a more stately and elegant aura. It was Charlotte.

Hearing the footsteps, Charlotte looked up and fixed her calm yet piercing gaze on Evander.

Her look made his nervousness shoot up a bit more.

With a quick step forward, he bowed slightly.

"Hello, I'm Evander Bexley. Thanks for having me."

Charlotte nodded, gesturing for him to take a seat.

"Relax, Mr. Bexley. Lois won't stop going on about you. Says you're a sharp young guy, started Vanguard Tech, and gave her a big hand. I just had to meet you myself."

Sitting beside Charlotte, Lois flashed Evander a little wink to calm his nerves.

Evander smiled, pulled a velvet box from his inside pocket, and presented it to Charlotte.

"Mrs. Charlotte Cavendish, it's great to finally meet you. Not much of a gift, but here's a brooch made for the French royals in the 1700s. Hope you like it."

Caught off guard, Charlotte took the box and popped it open.

The brooch made her eyes light up.

Picking it up to inspect it, she exclaimed in surprise, "I saw one like this in the royal museum. Didn't think there'd be another in such mint condition.

"You're a real gentleman, Mr. Bexley. Love it!"

Seeing Charlotte pleased, Evander let out a quiet sigh of relief. Lois also smiled to herself.
