Chapter 81 Poker

Lois finally lifted her head, her cheeks flushing slightly. The lingering trace of Evander's release between her thighs left a faint, sticky sensation, making her shift uncomfortably.

"Why didn't you think of that earlier? Would've saved us all this trouble." Her voice had softened considerably. "Mr. Bexley is cultured, well-read, and he does magic tricks. Spending time with him would be good for you. You'd actually learn something worthwhile instead of wasting your life gambling."

Felix nodded eagerly. "You're absolutely right. I should learn from Mr. Bexley."

Just then, Evander appeared with Anya and Andrea in tow.

The moment Anya spotted Lois, she rushed forward and grabbed her arm. "Lois! You really are here! We were so worried! We thought that magician might have done something to you!"

Lois shook her head with a gentle smile. "Don't worry about me. I'm fine. Mr. Bexley is a good man."

She glanced at Evander, and something soft flickered in her eyes.

Felix stood up and extended his hand to Evander. Gone was the hostility from before, replaced by something that looked almost like genuine respect.

"Look, Bexley, my bad about earlier. Lois says you're a man of many talents. How about we play some poker? No-limit Texas Hold'em—you do know how to play, right?"

Evander's pulse quickened. He definitely knew poker. Felix had just hit on his favorite game.

He shook Felix's hand, grinning. "Absolutely. I love Hold'em. Though let's keep it friendly, yeah? Poker should be fun, not a second mortgage."

Felix blinked, then laughed. "Of course! 10 thousand pounds to start. Win or lose, we keep it clean."

This was exactly what Felix needed. Poker wasn't just luck. There was real skill involved. He won way more than he lost, and this was his chance to get some respect back.

When Anya and Andrea heard "Texas Hold'em", their eyes lit up immediately.

Anya stepped closer, her pale yellow dress swaying with the movement. "I know how to play too! Can I deal for you guys?"

Lois frowned, clearly not thrilled about the gambling, but since both Felix and Evander had already agreed, she didn't object. Instead, she just gave them a warning look. "Don't get carried away. Remember, we're royalty. We have reputations to maintain."

Evander nodded and turned to Felix. "Should we head to the game room now?"

"Sure! Let's go right now." Felix jumped at the chance, worried Evander might change his mind.

He turned to Lois. "You want to come with us?"

Lois hesitated for a moment, then nodded. "Fine, I'll come with you. Someone has to keep you two from fighting."

Felix led the group through the main casino floor, straight to the back, where the high-roller suites were. He'd booked the most expensive room they had that morning.

The place was pure luxury porn. Plush carpets you could sink into, oil paintings that probably cost more than most people's cars, and a poker table made from solid mahogany. Even the chips looked like they were dipped in real gold.

"This is where we'll play. Nobody will bother us." Felix opened the door, chin up, making sure Lois saw he had money to burn.

Lois stepped behind him, her gold dress swaying. Her hands were folded in front of her, and she looked like she didn't want to be there.

Anya and Andrea looked at each other, then hurried to stand behind Evander. They flanked him like a pair of eager groupies, all blonde curls and wide eyes, hanging on his every move.

Evander approached the table and sat down, leaving his cards untouched. "Mr. Gosse, I prefer playing by instinct in Hold'em. But please, take your time with your cards."

The comment landed like a punch to Felix's gut.

Behind him stood Lois, and behind Evander were Anya and Andrea, three royal women watching his every move. If Felix lost to some commoner who wouldn't even look at his cards, he'd never live it down.

"Fine! We'll do it your way!" Felix slammed his hand on the table, making the silver-edged chips rattle.

"10 thousand pounds to start. If you're going to play blind the whole time, I'm looking at my cards and doubling every raise."

"Sounds good." Evander sat down and rested his hands on his knees. True to his word, he didn't even glance at his hole cards.

They agreed to let Anya deal. She cracked open a fresh deck, her fingers dancing as she shuffled and cut the cards like she was putting on a show.

She dealt two cards face-down to each player, then flipped the first three community cards—two, seven, and a king.

Felix's hand was already moving toward his cards. He lifted the corners just enough to peek, and a smile spread across his face. King-queen in his hand, and there was a king on the board.

A pair of kings wasn't the nuts, but it should be plenty to beat someone playing blind.

"Mr. Bexley, your bet," Felix spoke with deliberate condescension, stroking his cards slowly to emphasize that he could see them while Evander couldn't.

"You won't even look at your cards? You sure you want to call?"

Evander's brows furrowed slightly as his X-ray vision kicked in. He could see his hole cards clearly—a three and a five. Combined with the community cards, he had absolutely nothing. Felix's pair of kings had him beat by miles.

His expression smoothed out. "I fold this hand."

"What's wrong? Getting scared?" Felix let out a mocking laugh, making sure everyone could hear. "I thought you magicians were supposed to be fearless."

Evander ignored the jab and watched Anya deal the next round.

The flop came six, six, and a jack. Felix snuck another peek at his cards—ace and six. A full house, aces over sixes.

His chest puffed up with confidence. He was about to start gloating when Evander spoke first.

"I'm calling this one. 20 thousand pounds."

Felix blinked, then broke into a grin. "You're betting blind for 20 grand? Fine, I'll raise you to 40. Let's see what kind of miracle hand you think you've got."

He took his sweet time stacking his chips, even glancing back at Lois with a smug look before pushing them forward.

"I call. Let's see them." Evander's voice stayed completely calm.

Felix flipped his cards with a flourish. "Ace-six, full house!"

Evander just smiled. He tilted his chin slightly, and Anya reached over to flip his cards.

"Pocket aces!" A full house, but aces over aces crushed Felix's aces over sixes.

Felix's grin froze on his face. His fingers gripped the corner of his cards so tight his knuckles went pale.

From that point on, things went from bad to worse for Felix.

Evander played like he could see the future. When he got dealt trash, he folded immediately—zero losses.

When he spotted cards that could make a straight or flush, he'd bet blind for two or three hands, then get out clean. But whenever he had just enough to beat Felix's hand, he'd go all in without hesitation.

50 thousand, a hundred thousand pounds—the numbers kept climbing while his voice stayed completely calm.

Felix was spiraling. He could see his cards, calculate the odds, do everything right, but somehow, Evander kept beating him, even when he was playing blind the whole time.

Behind him, Lois was getting more disappointed by the minute.

Meanwhile, Anya and Andrea couldn't stop gasping and squealing behind Evander, looking at him like he was some kind of god.

"This hand, I'm betting blind. One hundred thousand pounds," Evander spoke casually, like he was ordering coffee. He didn't even touch his chips, just called out the number.

Felix checked his hole cards. He'd been getting garbage hands for several rounds now, and it was starting to piss him off. He slowly lifted the corners of his cards.

Ace-king. With the community cards showing King, Six, Ace, Two, Three, he had two pair. Finally—this was the best hand he'd seen all night.

He took a deep breath and deliberately slowed his voice. "Mr. Bexley, you sure you want to bet a hundred thousand blind? What if I caught something big this time?"

He was trying to intimidate Evander into folding, but all he got back was, "That's up to you."

"I call! And I'm raising to 200 thousand pounds!" Felix shoved his chips forward, silently praying that Evander was just throwing money away on pure luck.

Evander made another blind bet. Felix raised another 200 thousand pounds.

"Mr. Gosse, I call. Let's see what we've got. Anya, would you flip my cards for me?"

"Sure thing," Anya said, reaching for the two cards in front of Evander.

The moment she turned them over, her jaw dropped again.

Evander's hole cards were pocket deuces. Combined with the community board of King, Six, Ace, Two, Three, he had three of a kind. It beat Felix's two pair by a whole rank.

Felix's teeth ground together. "How the hell... You don't even look at your cards. How do you keep winning?"

His voice was shaking. He couldn't wrap his head around it. He had every advantage—he could see his cards, analyze the odds, and make informed decisions. And yet he was getting destroyed.

Evander smiled at him. "Mr. Gosse, I just got lucky today. That's all it was."

Anya's eyes lit up behind Evander, and she started clapping softly. Andrea couldn't help herself. "Mr. Bexley, that was incredible! You're playing blind and winning every hand. You're better than the pros in here."
