Chapter 50 Let's Get Started

Inside the auction house, waiters carefully presented the treasures to their new owners.

Kimberly cradled the Royal Diamond Bracelet in its velvet box. Her fingertips skimmed the cool diamonds, their glittering light reflecting in her eyes. A faint blush colored her cheeks, and a shy smile tugged at her lips.

Oliver, standing proudly at her side, caught the expression and couldn't help but beam himself.

Evander's very public gift had elevated not only Kimberly but the entire Nelson family. His opinion of the young man had shifted entirely.

He patted Kimberly's shoulder, eyes full of approval.

"Kimberly, that Evander's got a good heart. If you get the chance, you should get to know him better."

Naomi stood nearby, her gaze locked on the bracelet. Envy coiled in her chest like a venomous snake, making her breath come short.

Across the room, Iris held the royal emerald set.

The smooth, cool weight of the gems in her hand stirred memories she'd tried to bury—Evander's reckless moment in the elevator, and the calm authority in his eyes when he'd claimed the jewels for her just minutes ago.

William, watching her conflicted expression, sighed quietly.

"Iris, don't get too caught up in emotions. Evander… might not be as simple as he looks."

Iris didn't answer. She only clutched the jewelry box tighter.

Five billion dollars was dazzling, yes—but no trinket could erase the scars he'd left.

And yet, against her will, a flicker of something unsettled her heart. Like a pebble dropped in still water, the ripples refused to fade.

The most radiant of all was Aileen.

She held the crown hairpin delicately, the pigeon-blood ruby gleaming under the chandelier's glow as though whispering royal secrets from another age.

Carefully, she pinned it in her hair, then twirled in front of the mirror. Her blue eyes sparkled with unrestrained delight.

This wasn't just jewelry. It was a symbol—of glory, of status. And it had been given to her by Evander.

Outside, Evander was about to slip into Juno's car when hurried footsteps rang behind him.

Aileen, in her silver mermaid gown, came rushing across the pavement, high heels clicking like a staccato beat. The shimmering fabric trailed after her like the tail of a fish breaking through water.

Breathless, her cheeks flushed, her eyes alight, she called out, "Mr. Bexley! I'll prepare everything. Please—come to the western suburb castle at eight tonight."

Evander glanced at her as he settled into the car. The sunlight caught her silver dress, making it glow. The crown hairpin in her hair blazed like fire.

He gave a simple nod, his voice even. "Sure."

Juno started the engine, and the black sedan melted into traffic.

Aileen stood watching until it disappeared, a bright smile tugging at her lips. She pulled out her phone and fired off a quick text to her assistant: [Cancel today's manuscript appointments. I'm leaving work early.]

But her moment of giddy joy shattered when a voice, low and cold, sounded behind her.

"You know this Mr. Bexley?"

She spun around. Jack was there, his eyes hard, his presence heavy. Her smile faltered.

Feigning casualness, she waved her phone. "He's just a contributor for LUXERA. I asked him to meet about revisions. Don't overthink it."

Jack's scoff was sharp. His gaze raked her from head to toe.

"Really? A contributor who drops ten billion on a hairpin?"

He stepped closer, towering over her, menace curling in his tone.

"Aileen, you're my fiancée. Don't get too cozy with the wrong kind of man."

Her face paled, but she drew herself up, stubbornness flashing in her eyes.

"Mr. Bexley isn't the 'wrong kind.' He's… talented."

Jack's sneer deepened. "Talent? In Nogales, talent doesn't put food on the table."

His hand shot out, fingers clamping her chin with bruising force.

"And trust me—he wouldn't dare touch you. If he does, I'll make sure he vanishes from this city.

"Take care of yourself. I'm off to work."

Without another glance, Jack released her and strode toward the waiting military SUV.

The driver snapped the door open, and Jack climbed in. With a roar, the vehicle sped away, leaving Aileen standing alone on the curb.

Her fingers brushed the spot on her chin where his grip had reddened her skin. A flicker of grievance and resentment flashed in her blue eyes.

Drawing a steadying breath, she turned toward her sports car.

All the way home, her thoughts tangled like storm clouds.

Jack's suffocating possessiveness weighed on her chest, while Evander's mystery—and his breathtaking generosity—pulled at her like a magnet.

Her mind drifted to the article Perfect Sex and Love. Its explicit yet strangely philosophical words had made her pulse race, and now they returned, echoing in her head.

Back at her villa—a European-style mansion with a rose garden and a living room lined with medieval antiques—Aileen wasted no time. She wrapped up the last of her work, left early, and headed upstairs.

In her bedroom, she opened the wardrobe, eyes skimming the rows of dresses before settling on a black silk nightgown trimmed with delicate lace. It skimmed her mid-thigh, elegant yet daring.

She slipped into it, applied a light touch of makeup, and smoothed on nude lipstick. With care, she removed the crown hairpin from her hair, setting it on the dressing table like a holy relic.

Downstairs, she uncorked a bottle of 1998 Burgundy, poured two glasses, and placed them neatly on the coffee table.

The wall clock read 6:30. An hour and a half until eight.

Time crawled. She perched on the sofa, the printed copy of Perfect Sex and Love in her lap, telling herself she was only preparing to discuss revisions. But her restless heartbeat betrayed her.

Then—the doorbell rang.

Her heart lurched. She hurried to the door and peeked through the peephole.

Evander stood there.

Tall, composed, wrapped in a black windbreaker. His handsome features were calm, unreadable, a black gift box in hand.

Aileen drew a breath, smoothed her expression, and opened the door with a gentle smile.

"Mr. Bexley. You're here."

He gave a small nod and stepped inside.

The chandelier glowed softly overhead, the scent of Burgundy drifting through the room. Evander set the gift box on the coffee table, his gaze sweeping over the antiques before landing on her.

She stood in her silk nightgown, the fabric catching the light against her calves and collarbone. Her skin glowed faintly under the chandelier's warmth.

"Have a seat," Aileen offered, handing him a glass of wine.

Her fingers brushed his by accident, a jolt of heat shooting through her. She pulled back quickly, flustered.

Evander accepted the glass and sank into the sofa. His eyes fell on the printed copy of Perfect Sex and Love waiting on the table.

He raised a brow, lips curving in the faintest smile.

"Shall we begin, Ms. Burton?"
