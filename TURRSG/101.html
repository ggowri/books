Chapter 102 Paired Forces

Lois sat to the side, and when she heard Charlotte's words, she felt happy for Evander as well.

"Don't worry, Mom. Mr. Bexley will definitely succeed. He has solved so many problems before, and he will manage this one too."

Charlotte reached over and ruffled her daughter's hair, her look soft and warm.

"Of course, I believe in him. But research takes time. Make sure you remind him to rest, or he'll end up exhausting himself."

With that, she rose to her feet and said, "It's getting late. I'll leave you two to it and head back to the estate. If anything comes up, call me."

Hearing that, both Evander and Lois quickly stood to walk Charlotte out.

By the time they reached the manor's entrance, Charlotte's driver had already pulled the car around.

She turned back to Evander and reminded him again, "The Kabbalistic Tree of Life research isn't a race. Think in terms of implementation, not just theory.

"If there's something you don't understand, you can consult with the people at the Institute for Esoteric Studies. I'll let them know."

"Thank you for your concern, Mrs. Charlotte Cavendish. I'll keep that in mind," Evander replied as he gave a polite nod.

Charlotte gave him a brief nod in return and then got into the car and left.

Watching the car disappear into the night, Lois slipped her arm through Evander's and said with excitement, "My mom is so good to you, Mr. Bexley. She even gave you guidance on your research. This could really secure our partnership with Royal Tech."

Evander nodded with a smile, feeling a surge of motivation inside. "Yes, I never expected Mrs. Charlotte Cavendish to support me this much. She's not trying to set me up to marry her daughter, is she?"

His teasing instantly brought color to Lois' cheeks. She gave his arm a gentle tap, though a smile tugged at her lips. "Quit it. My mom just thinks you're sincere, so she's willing to help. But..."

She paused, looked up at Evander, and gave him a mischievous look. "If you really did have that thought, I wouldn't mind."

Evander took in the flush on her ears and the anticipation in her expression, and warmth spread through him.

He drew Lois into his arms, resting his chin lightly on her hair. His voice carried a trace of laughter as he said, "Then I'd better work hard and win Mrs. Charlotte Cavendish's approval soon. But for now, I still need to settle the matter with Royal Tech."

The two of them stood together at the manor entrance, held close as the night air carried the scent of flowers and brushed gently across their faces.

Lois leaned against Evander, listening to the steady beat of his heart, and felt completely at peace.

It took her a long moment before she finally pulled away from Evander's arms. She then tugged on his hand and led him toward the living room, saying, "Come on, don't stay out here in the wind. You'll catch a chill.

"You wanted to look up information on the Kabbalistic Tree of Life, right? I'll get you a glass of milk and keep you company while you read."

Back in the living room, Lois went to the kitchen for the milk while Evander turned on his laptop and began searching for detailed material on the Kabbalistic Tree of Life.

Before long, the screen filled with dense texts, and he started with the original Kabbalah interpretations.

"Here's the milk." Lois carried a glass over and set it beside Evander.

Then, she sat down next to him and craned her neck toward the screen curiously. "These look so complicated. Can you even make sense of them?"

Evander took a sip of the milk, the warmth slipping down his throat into his stomach. He pointed at the Tree of Life diagram on the screen and replied, "It's actually not that hard.

"See, the Kabbalistic Tree of Life is built on paired forces. Each pattern contains multiple nodes, divided into active and passive.

"Active stands for strength, while passive stands for flexibility. Their combinations work like binary code in a computer, creating different layers of meaning."

Lois gave a knowing nod, though most of the jargon went over her head. Still, seeing the concentration on Evander's face, she couldn't help but take it seriously as well.

She asked, "Then where do you think Royal Tech went wrong in their research?"

Evander answered, "I suspect they focused too much on appearances and missed the essence."

With that, he closed the page of references, pulled up a blank document, and began sketching a preliminary model combining patterns with algorithms.

Evander continued, "They might have simply matched active with '1' and passive with '0', without considering the logic behind the patterns.

"For example, two actives in different nodes don't mean the same thing. It's more like weights in an AI algorithm, which have to be adjusted according to context."

Lois leaned in to look at the model Evander was drawing. She couldn't decipher the complex formulas, yet she could sense the beauty in their logic.

She said, "That sounds incredible. So, when do you think you can finish a complete plan?"

Evander said, "If things move quickly, we could see some initial results in about a week."

He then saved the document, turned off his laptop, and rubbed his tired eyes.

Evander added, "But first, I need to get in touch with scholars at the Institute for Esoteric Studies to confirm some of the pattern interpretations. I want to make sure there aren't any mistakes."

They discussed a little longer before Lois, worried he was exhausting himself, urged him to rest. But he told her to go to bed first. He still wanted to study a bit more.

By three in the morning, Evander could no longer keep going and finally went to sleep.

The next morning, Lois helped him reach out to the Institute for Esoteric Studies.

The call was answered by an elderly professor named Walter Harlow, a specialist in the intersection of Kabbalah and modern technology.

When Walter learned that Evander wanted to apply the Kabbalistic Tree of Life to research on robotic consciousness, he grew very interested and immediately invited him to the research center for a meeting.

Lois had planned to go with Evander, but she needed to check whether Felix had woken up. So, she asked Evander to drive over by himself.

Before Evander left, she reminded him again and again, "Be careful on the road. Call me if anything comes up. And if Walter shares anything important, make sure to ask him more about it."

"Don't worry, I will," Evander said with a smile. He pressed a gentle kiss to her forehead before getting into the car and driving off.

The Institute for Esoteric Studies was located near University College London in an old, distinguished-looking building.

By the time Evander arrived, Walter was already waiting outside. Dressed in a gray suit, his hair was streaked with white, but he looked energetic and sharp.

When he spotted Evander, he came forward warmly. "Oh, Mr. Bexley, it's an honor to finally meet you. Your work in AI has gained international recognition. I didn't expect you to also take an interest in metaphysics."

Evander replied politely, "You're too kind, Professor Harlow. I only know the basics. Well, I came today hoping to learn from you."

After his respectful greeting, he followed Walter inside the research center.
