Chapter 30 The Journey's Beginning

Seven days later.

The helicopter's engines roared to life, slicing through the clouds with a steady hum.

Below, the island's outline shrank, a crumpled swath of green fading into the distance.

The dense coconut groves blurred into patches of emerald, and the shoreline glittered like a silver ribbon, curling around the land they had called home for the past week.

Kimberly put her face against the window, drawing random patterns with her fingers on the cold glass, her eyes showing she didn't want to leave.

"What are you thinking about?"

Evander's voice cut through the quiet. He kept his hands on the controls, his face looking sharp in the sun, his jaw tense.

Kimberly looked at him, her eyes full of love she couldn't say out loud.

"I'm thinking about this whole week."

Her voice came out like a sweet sigh. "These were the best days of my life."

Evander stopped for a moment, then a small, perfectly timed smile crossed his face.

"Mine too."

The words were half-true.

For the past week, he felt totally free.

He didn't have to act like the good little doormat everyone wanted him to be, and he didn't have to watch out for Denny's tricks. He just did whatever his gut told him to do.

But happy? That was a stretch. Efficient was more like it.

Thirty-seven times in seven days. According to Ciotti's probability charts, the odds of conception were at least eighty percent.

His grandfather's second test was likely in the bag.

Kimberly didn't catch the detachment in his tone. She reached out, her hand covering his on the control stick, her fingers brushing the veins on his knuckles with a tender warmth.

"When we get back... can we still be like this?"

Her voice trembled with cautious hope, like a child afraid of being turned away.

These past seven days, Evander had been impossibly gentle.

Mornings, he'd greet her with the best breakfast he could muster. When she wandered off to collect shells, he'd trail quietly behind, ready to catch her if she tripped. At night, under a blanket of stars, he'd hold her close, whispering that her eyes outshone it all.

His sweet words had pulled her under, drowning out the world.

She forgot he was Nadine's husband. And forgot her own place as the Nelson family heiress.

Evander felt the softness of her touch, but his mind stayed clear.

He turned to her, his eyes softening with practiced warmth.

"Of course."

At least until the hospital report confirmed her pregnancy, he had to maintain the illusion.

Kimberly's eyes lit up, sparkling like stars.

She leaned in, pressing a quick kiss to his cheek.

"I knew you wouldn't lie to me."

Evander smiled faintly, saying nothing, and subtly adjusted the flight path.

He could feel Kimberly staring at him. Her look felt heavy, like vines squeezing his arm, his chest, pulling at him in a way that made him want to break loose.

Evander looked at the woman next to him, her face all bright and innocent, and made himself smile. Her fingers were still drawing little circles on his hand, like she was planning their future.

"Want some food?"

Evander changed the subject, nodding toward the buckwheat sandwiches he'd made on the island.

"A little." Kimberly's attention shifted.

She picked up a sandwich but brought it to his lips first, feeding him like a pet. "You first."

Evander took a bite. The bread's sweetness mingling with the savory tang of ham enveloped him.

Kimberly watched him chew, her eyes crinkling with delight, as if he were savoring a delicacy instead of a rough sandwich.

These seven days had felt like a honeymoon.

By day, they'd venture into the jungle for red berries.

Kimberly would skip ahead, stepping on his shadow, giggling when she tripped over vines and fell into his arms.

They'd comb the beach for shells, and she'd press the prettiest conch into his hands, saying it held the ocean's voice, hoping he'd always remember this island.

At night, they'd lie side by side on the sand, gazing at the stars. Kimberly would point to Orion, spinning tales about the constellation carved into Nelson Manor's beams. She told him it was the monster that freaked her out when she was little.

Evander would listen, his arm slipping around her waist, whispering, "I'll protect you from now on."

In his arms, Kimberly's cheeks would flush pink, like a teenager on her very first date.

And then there were the moments of passion.

From the soft grass in the cave to the moonlit sand, from the shade of coconut trees to the shallow waves, they left no corner untouched.

They made love with reckless abandon.

Kimberly grew bolder. She'd wrap her arms around him from behind while he grilled fish, pressing against him through her damp swimsuit.

Under the stars, she'd unbuttoned his shorts during their stargazing, her fingertips carrying the chill of seawater.

She'd gasp in his ear, "Evander, deeper." Her body opened up like a flower in the heat of passion.

Evander played along eagerly.

He knew her surrender was key to ensuring conception.

He matched her rhythm, whispering sweet nothings, driving harder as she climaxed, enjoying how her body shook, the quick grip and heat.

He'd finished inside her thirty-seven times.

He counted each time in his head, making sure to go deep.

Kimberly's ovulation window was still open. The odds were in his favor.

"What are you thinking about?"

Kimberly's voice pulled him back.

She'd finished her sandwich, wiping her lips with a napkin.

A flicker of worry flashed across her eyes. "Are you thinking about how to explain this to Nad?"

Evander's heart tightened, but he forced a casual smile.

"Don't worry about it. I'll handle it."

He already had the whole story planned out.

They would tell everyone their helicopter went down, they got stuck on some empty island, and somehow made it through alive.

As for their reckless week, as long as he and Kimberly kept quiet, Nadine might never know.

And if she did? With the leverage he held now, he wasn't afraid.

In fact, it might be better if she found out.

It could push her to sign the divorce papers earlier.

Kimberly frowned.

"Nad... She's sensitive. If she finds out..."

"She won't blame you," Evander cut in, his tone firm, as if stating a fact.

"And if she does, I'll have your back."

Kimberly's lips curved into a smile, and she nodded.

She trusted Evander and believed their bond could weather any storm, just as she believed the sun would rise each morning.

The helicopter descended, the island now a mere speck, no bigger than a fingernail.

Kimberly gazed at the clouds outside, a pang of loss settling in her chest.

Part of her didn't want to return and longed to stay on that island forever—just her and Evander. There was no Nadine, no family feuds, only the sea, the sky, and the warmth of each other's skin.

"We..." She started to speak, but Evander's voice cut through.

"We're almost there." He pointed to a gap in the clouds ahead.

"Through that, you'll see the city."

Kimberly swallowed her words, the ache in her chest deepening.

She looked at Evander's focused profile and felt a strange distance.

He was slipping back into the cool, controlled Evander, no longer the man who'd teased her with sweet words on the island.

"Evander."

Her voice was soft, a faint tremble betraying her nerves.

"You won't... ignore me when we get back, will you?"

Evander turned, his eyes softening with feigned warmth.

"Why would I?"

He reached out, ruffling her hair with a natural gesture.

At least until the pregnancy was confirmed, he'd play the devoted lover.

Kimberly let out a relieved breath and leaned back, watching the city's outline grow closer, a smile tugging at her lips.

She trusted him, believing their love could conquer all, just like she trusted the stars to appear when night fell.

The helicopter glided through the final layer of clouds, the city sprawling below like an open map, alive with weaving traffic and towering buildings.

Evander guided the helicopter toward the grassy wetland park where they'd taken off, the propellers slowing to a soft hum.

Soon, they had returned to the journey's starting point.

Evander and Kimberly stepped down from the helicopter.
