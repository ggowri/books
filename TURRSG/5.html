Chapter 5 Grandfather?

The wrinkles on that old man's face held the stories of time. The curve of the nose, the shape of the brow, and even the faint hint of detachment in his eyes perfectly matched the blurry face from the depths of Evander's memory.

It was his grandfather, Norman Westbrook, the man who, at Evander's age of three, stormed out after a fight with his grandmother and vanished into the world.

Back then, Norman had disappeared without a trace. His grandmother had waited for him every day at the front door, but he never came back.

A year later, she passed away from a broken heart.

The person who saved me... is my grandfather?

Evander stared at Norman, his throat tight as if choked by an invisible weight.

Twenty years had passed, and Norman's absence had left a bitter resentment in the family. Even after his grandmother's death, Norman hadn't shown his face. No one knew where he had been all these years.

A storm of questions raged inside Evander, but he opened his mouth and found that he couldn't make a sound.

Norman extended a hand toward him. His palm was calloused, but it carried a reassuring warmth.

"Evan, are you still willing to call me your grandfather?"

The hall was so silent you could hear a speck of dust settle.

Evander stared at the outstretched hand and suddenly remembered his third birthday. On that day, that same hand had held his as they walked along the cobblestone alley, and Norman had bought him an ice cream that melted all too quickly.

His eyes reddened.

Norman's hand settled on Evander's arm, steady and grounded with the strength of a life weathered by time.

With a gentle tug, he lifted Evander from the frigid marble floor.

The old man's fingers traced the scabs on his wrist, his brow furrowing briefly before smoothing out again.

"Stand up straight."

Norman's voice carried undeniable authority, yet held a subtle hint of tenderness.

He turned and walked toward the massive chair, his leather shoes stirring up small clouds of dust.

Evander stood frozen, his wounds screaming with every movement, but the pain paled against the shock reverberating through him.

Watching Norman's back as he sat, Evander suddenly realized his grandfather, who had been gone for over two decades, was far more than he appeared.

The men in black suits lining the hall stood with unwavering composure, their hands resting on their waists.

He knew they must have had weapons concealed beneath their jackets.

"Do you know where you are?"

Norman's voice echoed through the cavernous hall, resonant as struck metal.

His fingers tapped the armrest, the dark gold cloud patterns on his robe glinting under the light. "This is HexCore, the nerve center of the global underground order."

"And I," he declared, "am the man who controls it!"

Evander was stunned.

He'd come across the name "HexCore" while researching the dark web. It was a shadowy organization rumored to control over half the world's legitimate and illicit trade. He'd never imagined his grandfather was its mastermind.

"I'm getting old."

Norman raised a weathered hand, studying the calluses etched into his palm. "I've spent a lifetime fighting and killing. It's time to step back."

His gaze snapped to Evander, sharp as a hawk's.

"But this empire needs an heir."

Evander's heart pounded, his blood roaring like a furnace in his veins.

He thought of the humiliation of being crushed under Denny's boot, Nadine's cold, dismissive stare, and Naomi's twisted sneer as she swung the whip.

The pain they'd inflicted now fueled a blazing fire, scorching his reason.

"I'm giving you ten trials."

Norman's voice rose, commanding. "Pass them all..."

He paused, his finger pointing to the throne-like chair. "And the title of the world's richest man, the position of the underworld kingpin, will be yours."

A cold smile curved Norman's lips. "From that throne, your word becomes law. Women will beg for your attention, treasures will flood your vaults, and men will die for the honor of serving you.

"You'll be untouchable in this world!

"Everyone will bow at your feet, begging to be your stepping stone."

Evander's breathing grew heavy as blood rushed to his head.

He remembered Nadine's words at their wedding: "You're not even worthy to shine my shoes."

He recalled the searing pain of Denny's club and Naomi's whip in that warehouse.

"Evander, do you want to be my heir?" Norman's piercing gaze seemed to look straight into his soul.

The question shook Evander to his core.

He needed revenge. He needed power. Everyone who had looked down on him, who had betrayed him—they would all pay dearly!

Taking a deep breath, Evander said, "Yes. I'll do it!"
