Chapter 109 Lois Being Taken Away

A cold voice came through the phone.

"Princess Lois, by order of King Edward, we are to escort you back to the Royal Three Estate. Please come with us immediately."

Lois froze, confusion flickering in her eyes.

"Why? I'm discussing something important with Mr. Bexley and Professor Harlow now. I can't just leave."

"This is a direct order from King Edward. We're just following protocol," the guard replied flatly.

"If you refuse to comply, we will have to use force."

Panic rose in Lois' chest. She quickly handed the phone to Evander.

"It's the royal guards. They said King Edward wants me back at the Royal Three Estate immediately. And if I don't go, they'll take me by force."

Evander took the phone, his expression darkening instantly. His tone turned sharp.

"Why is King Edward having her taken away? What has she done?"

"We weren't given the reason, sir. We're simply carrying out orders," the guard said, his voice still icy.

"If Princess Lois doesn't come out now, we'll be forced to enter."

Evander ended the call, his jaw tight as he looked at Lois' anxious eyes. Fury was already rising in his chest.

He didn't need to guess. Felix had clearly said something to Edward behind their backs.

He took Lois' hand and said firmly, "Don't be afraid. I'm here. I won't let them take you."

Walter sensed something was wrong and quickly offered, "Should I go talk to them? I know one of King Edward's advisors. Maybe I can help smooth things over."

Evander shook his head.

"That won't be necessary, Professor Harlow. This is between me and the royal family. I'll handle it."

He walked over to the window and saw several black sedans parked outside the institute. Men in black suits and sunglasses stood at the entrance—clearly here for Lois.

Lois clutched Evander's hand tighter, her voice trembling.

"What do we do now? Will my father make things difficult for you?"

"He won't," Evander said, gently patting the back of her hand.

"I'm calling Duke Chad. He can talk to the king. Don't worry. I'll keep you safe."

He pulled out his phone and dialed Chad.

The call connected quickly, and Chad's warm voice came through.

"Mr. Bexley, what can I do for you?"

"There's a situation," Evander said calmly.

"The royal guards are outside the Institute for Esoteric Studies. They say King Edward wants to take Lois back, and they're threatening to use force."

He paused and then added, "I'm pretty sure Felix put something in the king's ear. Could you speak with him on our behalf?"

There was a beat of silence.

Chad's tone grew serious. "That's... concerning. All right, stay calm and don't engage with the guards. I'll call King Edward now and find out what's going on. I'll get back to you as soon as I can."

After hanging up, Evander let out a slow breath.

"Take it easy," he told Lois. "Duke Chad's on it. He has the king's ear. If anyone can get through to him, it's Duke Chad."

Lois nodded and leaned closer to Evander, but a shadow of unease still lingered in her eyes.

She knew how much Edward valued the royal family's reputation, and Felix had always known how to exploit that. This wasn't going to be easy.

Outside the room, the guards were starting to lose patience. The lead officer stepped forward and knocked on the office door, his voice clipped and cold.

"Princess Lois, this is your final warning. Are you coming out, or are we coming in?"

Evander strode to the door and pulled it open, his eyes like ice as he stared down the guard.

"I've already contacted Duke Chad. He's speaking to King Edward right now. Until he gets back to us, none of you is coming in."

The guard hesitated, frowning at the mention of Chad. He knew the duke carried weight with the king. After a moment, he gave a reluctant nod.

"Fine. We'll wait ten minutes. But if we don't hear anything by then, we'll be forced to proceed."

Evander nodded, shut the door, and returned to Lois' side.

Lois gripped his hand, her voice shaking again.

"Ten minutes... Is that enough time? Will Duke Chad really get through to him?"

"He will," Evander replied, squeezing her hand.

But even he felt a flicker of doubt.

He knew this wasn't just about their relationship anymore. This might derail everything he'd been working toward, including his partnership with Royal Tech.

If Edward truly disapproved of their relationship, the obstacles ahead would only multiply.

Minutes ticked by in tense silence. The only sound in the room was the quiet thud of their heartbeats.

Lois kept glancing at the clock on the wall, anxiety rising with every passing second.

Just as the ten-minute mark approached, Evander's phone finally rang. It was Chad.

He answered immediately, his voice tight with urgency. "Well? Did he listen? Has he changed his mind?"

Chad sighed on the other end, his voice laced with regret.

"I tried. But King Edward's furious right now. He says Lois has disrespected the royal family and insists she return to the Royal Three Estate for reflection. But he promised not to punish her. She's just to stay at the estate for a few days to cool off."

Chad went on, "He did ask about you, though. I told him about your talent and your work on the AetherMind Chip project. But... he's still not impressed. He's not convinced you're right for Lois."

Evander's heart sank. He knew there was no fighting this—not without making things worse.

He drew in a breath and said quietly, "Thank you. I understand. I'll let her go with them. But please... keep an eye on her for me. Make sure she's treated well."

"I will," Chad said.

"And don't lose hope. Once the king cools down, I'll talk to him again. Your research really is groundbreaking. Once he sees that for himself, he might just come around."

After hanging up, Evander looked at Lois and saw her eyes all red, holding back tears. Guilt washed over him like a wave.

Out of nowhere, he grabbed the necklace he was wearing.

It was Freya's pendant—made of moonstone with this shiny silver chain, and seven small diamonds that sparkled in the light.

He carefully put it on Lois, his fingers barely touching her collarbone.

His voice dropped, steady and sincere.

"Lois, this used to belong to Freya. It's more than just jewelry. It senses danger, and it can summon divine aid. If anything goes wrong at the Royal Three Estate, whether King Edward loses it or Felix tries something dirty... just crush one of those diamonds."

He looked her in the eyes. "Freya will sense the call. And she'll come to you, no matter what."
