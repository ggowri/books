Chapter 24 Setting the Plan in Motion

Evander slipped back into the dense jungle, his heart pounding as he sprinted toward the helicopter.

The cabin door still hung open, swaying slightly in the breeze. Evander rummaged through the co-pilot's storage compartment and pulled out a small brown vial, its label scrawled with foreign script.

It was the potion, innocuous as a vitamin drink at first glance.

"Kimberly, don't blame me," he murmured, clutching the vial tightly, his eyes shadowed with a tangle of guilt and resolve.

"Blame Denny, blame your dad, and blame how damn irresistible you are."

He sealed the cabin door, then turned and plunged deeper into the dense, thick jungle of the deserted island.

The forest here was lush and tangled, thick vines twisting around the trunks like serpents. Wildflowers, vivid and nameless, bloomed in the shadowy undergrowth.

Evander recalled passing a patch of berry bushes earlier. The berries had looked like cherries, plump and ripe, surely edible.

After about ten minutes of trekking, he found them.

The berries hung in clustered chains, glistening like drops of ruby glass in the sun.

Evander pulled out his pocketknife, slicing a tiny nick into a berry, then unscrewed the potion vial, letting a few drops seep into the flesh.

The liquid was odorless and tasteless, melting seamlessly into the fruit, leaving no trace.

He doctored the ripest, most enticing clusters, then plucked a few untreated ones for himself.

When he was done, he tossed the empty vial into a swampy patch deep in the jungle. He then brushed his hands and headed back toward the beach.

Kimberly, at the same time, was disappointed to see that her clothes were still damp from the wash.

She gathered broad coconut leaves, clumsily weaving them into a makeshift skirt and top.

Her leafy skirt, its hem ragged and uneven, was barely held together. A vine tied behind her neck secured the top, offering just enough cover, yet with every movement, her waist and long legs came into view.

Evander returned to the cave's edge just as Kimberly emerged from the trees.

Sunlight spilled over her, the dappled shadows of leaves playing across her delicate skin. The rough garb should have made her look disheveled, yet somehow it lent her a kind of raw, untamed beauty.

Her bare feet, dusted with wet sand, sank softly into the earth, and the leafy top rose and fell with her breath, tracing the curves of her body.

Evander's breath caught, the berries nearly slipping from his hands.

He'd never seen her like this.

Gone was the polished heiress; in her place stood a fierce, untamed beauty. Now, she was like a wildflower blooming in the wilderness, thorned but dazzling.

"Where'd you go?" Kimberly asked, spotting him and taking a wary step back.

Her fingers tightened on the edge of her leafy skirt, her eyes sharp with suspicion.

The memory of his earlier touch was still a sting beneath her skin.

Evander held up the berries, flashing a disarming smile.

"Found us some food. These look fresh."

He offered them forward, but his gaze kept straying toward her chest, where the edge of a leaf had left a faint red mark against her skin.

Kimberly glanced at the berries and shook her head.

"No thanks. Could be poisonous."

Her voice was icy, laced with distrust.

She had thought it through. Anything unfamiliar on this island could be a trap—especially something offered by him.

Evander’s smile faded slightly, but he didn’t push. He popped a berry into his mouth.

"Sweet as candy," he said, chewing deliberately. "Guess you're overthinking it."

He chewed slowly, letting his eyes linger on her lips, imagining how they would feel beneath his own.

Kimberly turned away, her throat parched.

Washing clothes in the sea had left her drained and thirsty.

After a beat, she asked, "Is there any water in the chopper? I'm thirsty."

Evander's heart quickened, but he kept his tone casual.

"Yeah, hang on."

He sauntered back to the helicopter, his mind racing with calculations.

This was his moment.

Inside the cabin, he found an unopened case of bottled water in the storage compartment.

He grabbed one, twisted off the cap, and pretended to take a sip. Out of the corner of his eye, he saw Kimberly standing with her back to him, gazing toward the glittering sea.

Seizing his chance, Evander quickly grabbed an empty bottle and ran into the dense forest. He searched through the waist-high grass and soon found the brown vial under a rock.

He'd thrown it there in a hurry and hadn't noticed that a little bit of the potion was still at the bottom. It wasn't much, but it was enough.

Evander unscrewed the cap of the water bottle and tilted his wrist, letting the transparent liquid flow silently into the bottle. No one would ever know it was there.

He chucked the empty vial back into the grass, gave the water a shake to mix it, and headed back.

Sunlight filtered through the leaves, painting his face half in light, half in shadow, mirroring the duality of his intentions.

"Here you go."

He passed her the water, the bottle still carrying the heat of his palm.

Kimberly took it, her fingers brushing the cool plastic.

She barely hesitated, then twisted the cap. Instead of drinking right away, she poured a little into her hand. Then, she sniffed it and even tasted a drop cautiously.

It tasted like water, clean and ordinary.

"Thanks," she said, tilting her head back and taking a long gulp.

Water trickled down her chin, soaking the leaves of her top, deepening their green hue.

She didn't notice Evander's eyes locked on her throat as it moved, his gaze burning with a hunger he barely concealed.

"Your clothes still not dry?" he asked, his voice catching a husky edge.

"Yeah," Kimberly replied, glancing down at her leafy outfit, her cheeks warming.

"They'll need more time."

The potion was already stirring, its effects masked by her thirst. Kimberly felt nothing unusual, just a growing dryness in her throat.

Evander nodded, saying nothing more, and munched on a few berries, his eyes never straying far from her.

Kimberly stood by a reef, clutching the water bottle, and took another sip.

Sunlight gleamed on her bare shoulders, sweat beading along her neck and slipping into the gap of her leafy top, like a winding stream.

Suddenly, Kimberly frowned, wiping her brow.

"Why's it so damn hot?" she muttered.

Kimberly untied the vine from her neck, loosening her leaf top to expose more skin, hoping the sea breeze would carry away some of the heat.
