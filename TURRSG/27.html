Chapter 28 A Night on the Beach

"Get up."

Evander strode over, bending down and extending a hand.

His palm was broad and warm, still carrying the heat from the fight.

Kimberly hesitated for a moment before placing her hand in his.

With a gentle tug, Evander pulled her to her feet. She stumbled, crashing into his chest.

That familiar sweaty smell was now mixed with a hint of blood. Kimberly felt her heart quicken.

"You..." her voice trembled slightly. "Since when did you get so good at fighting?"

Evander released her, his tone calm and matter-of-fact. "I've had some practice."

"Practice?"

Kimberly arched a brow, clearly not buying it.

Whatever he said, she didn't believe a month of training could turn him into someone who could kill a wild boar with just his hands.

Evander offered no further explanation.

He glanced at the dead boar on the ground, then up at the darkening sky.

"We need to deal with this before nightfall, or it'll attract other animals."

Kimberly's eyes darted to the boar, her stomach churning.

She turned away, yet her eyes betrayed her, stealing a glance at Evander again.

The last rays of sunset bathed him in gold. That dangerous look hadn't left his face, and his eyes had this edge she'd never seen before.

That's when it hit Kimberly—this wasn't the same guy she thought she knew at all.

"What are you standing around for?"

Evander kicked some dry branches. "Go get some wood. We're cooking this boar tonight."

Kimberly mumbled "okay" and started picking up sticks.

As she bent down, her gaze lingered on Evander's back. He was skinning the boar with a pocket knife like a pro—definitely not what she'd expect from some useless live-in son-in-law.

Something changed inside her, like a tight knot slowly coming loose.

She thought about how the boar came at her, and how Evander just jumped right in to protect her.

She remembered how he threw those wild punches at the boar and how warm his hand felt when he helped her up.

He'd saved her life.

The realization sent a wave of unease through her, tinged with an inexplicable thrill.

She quickly looked down and kept herself busy, grabbing way more sticks than they needed.

Evander worked swiftly, slicing the boar's meat into thick chunks and skewering them on branches.

By the time Kimberly returned with an armful of firewood, he was ready.

"Light it."

He tossed her a lighter.

Kimberly caught it and crouched, fumbling to spark a flame.

The fire caught, its flickering glow dancing across her face.

Evander propped the skewers over the flames. Fat dripped into the fire, sizzling and filling the air with a mouthwatering aroma.

"Have you... gone hunting a lot before?" Kimberly blurted out, unable to hold back her curiosity.

"Nope," Evander turned the skewers. "Just started practicing recently."

The conversation ended there, replaced by the hiss of cooking meat and the whisper of the waves.

The salty breeze swept over the sand, and for a moment, there was an unexpected sense of peace.

When the meat was done, the sky had turned inky black.

Evander handed Kimberly a skewer.

"Try it."

She hesitated, then took it.

The meat was crispy outside, tender within, and infused with a smoky flavor that surprised her.

"Not bad," she mumbled around a mouthful. "Didn't know you had this in you."

Evander gave a short, dry laugh but said nothing.

Kimberly nibbled at the meat, the firelight casting her sharp features in a soft, radiant glow.

Evander watched her quietly.

If it weren't for his grandfather's second test, he'd never be sitting here, sharing a meal with this aloof, unpredictable heiress.

Norman had ordered him to get Kimberly pregnant within two weeks. Their earlier encounters might not have been enough.

He'd need to keep at it.

Evander had made up his mind. For the next few days, he'd keep her on this island, savoring the primal thrill of their game.

But he couldn't rush it.

He'd play it cool and let Kimberly come to him.

With that thought, Evander stood and headed to the helicopter.

He grabbed two bottles of water and flicked on the cabin's interior light.

"Here," He handed her a bottle. "When you're done, I'll take you back to the helicopter to sleep."

Kimberly twisted off the cap and took a few greedy gulps.

Sated, she stood and followed him to the helicopter.

Evander reclined the seat, spreading a blanket over it.

"You take the inside. It's more comfortable than the sand," he said.

Kimberly watched him work, a strange pang tugging at her chest.

The image of him taking down the boar was still vivid in her mind. Yet here he was, this powerful man, carefully tucking in the edges of her blanket like a thoughtful attendant.

"And you?" she asked.

"I'll sleep outside."

Evander nodded toward a patch of sand beside the helicopter, where he'd laid out some dry grass.

"I'll keep watch. You don't need to worry about those wild animals."

Kimberly's heart skipped a beat.

She wanted to tell him to sleep inside with her, but the words caught in her throat.

"Alright... Get some rest."

Evander flashed a faint smile and said nothing more.

He grabbed a thicker blanket, spread it on the sand, and lay down with his back to the helicopter. His movements were calm and unhurried, as if this were any ordinary night.

Kimberly settled into her "bed", gazing out the window at the moonlight.

The waves sounded like a gentle lullaby, but sleep wouldn't come.

Her mind replayed the day's chaos—the humiliation of being tossed into the sea, the terror of the boar's charge, and Evander's unyielding figure shielding her.

He's not that spineless "loser" anymore...

Kimberly pushed open the cabin door.

Under the moonlight, Evander lay still, his breathing steady, seemingly asleep.

The sea breeze tugged at his shirt, revealing the taut lines of his waist.

Kimberly's cheeks warmed. On impulse, she slipped out of the helicopter and sat beside him on the sand.

The grains dug into her skin, but the open air felt freer than the stuffy cabin.

She studied Evander's sleeping face. His long lashes cast faint shadows over his cheeks, his high nose and sharp jawline striking in the moonlight. He was, unexpectedly, handsome.

"Nadine, are you really going to divorce this guy?

"If you don't want him... I just might."

Her whispered words hung in the air, her heart swelling with a strange warmth.

Suddenly, Evander stirred, mumbling sleepily, "Kimberly...

"Why are you so distant..."

Kimberly froze, holding her breath.

"You're so gorgeous..."

Kimberly's heart slammed against her ribs, the words hitting her like a sudden wave.
