Chapter 101 Tip

A servant wheeled in a trolley laden with coffee and pastries and neatly arranged them on the coffee table.

Charlotte gracefully picked up her cup, took a dainty sip, and then turned her gaze to Evander.

"Mr. Bexley, I heard your cooperation talk with Royal Tech hit a snag in the parliament today, right?"

Evander felt a jolt inside. He hadn't anticipated Charlotte bringing it up so bluntly.

Quickly gathering his thoughts, he replied sincerely, "Yeah, Sir Gary believes Royal Tech's tech is self-sufficient and doesn't need external assistance. So, we couldn't reach an agreement for now.

"But I'm not throwing in the towel. Vanguard Tech's AI can truly bring benefits to Royal Tech. I'll look for another opportunity to talk to Gary and try to change his mind."

Charlotte nodded, and there was a hint more of approval in her eyes.

"Your persistence is commendable. But Gary is quite stubborn and values Royal Tech's 'independence'. Just flaunting the tech won't do the trick to convince him.

"You've got to make him see that cooperation is a 'win-win' situation, not about 'dependency'."

These words sparked a light in Evander's eyes.

It was as if Charlotte had just shown him the right path.

He'd been so focused on showing off the tech that he'd overlooked Gary's concern about "independence".

He needed a win-win angle. Make Gary see that working together would improve the tech, not hand over control.

Could that be enough to sway him?

Evander said earnestly, "Thanks for the tip. I'll mull it over and tweak the plan to persuade Sir Gary next time."

Charlotte smiled and tenderly glanced at Lois.

"Lois has been a handful since she was a kid, but she has an eye for people.

"Mr. Bexley, I hope you and Lois will get along well."

This was like a reassuring pat on the back for Evander, warming his heart.

He nodded seriously. "Don't worry, I'll take good care of Lois."

Sitting beside him, Lois blushed slightly and quietly slipped her hand into Evander's.

Seeing their intertwined hands, Charlotte showed a relieved smile.

The atmosphere in the living room gradually relaxed.

The three of them chatted about everything from the scenery in London to the development of Vanguard Tech.

Eventually, a servant came over to remind them that dinner was ready.

"Alright, let's head to the dining room," Charlotte said, getting up and leading the way.

Evander and Lois followed suit.

Lois whispered to Evander, "See, I told you my mom's a nice lady. You were so antsy before."

Evander smiled, giving her hand a gentle squeeze, "I overreacted. I'm really glad to have your mom's approval today."

In the grand dining room, a sumptuous dinner was elegantly laid out on the long table. Candles flickered, casting a warm glow over the scene.

The three took their seats, and the servants began serving the dishes.

Evander picked up his knife and fork and started having dinner with Lois and Charlotte.

Charlotte deftly cut a small piece of foie gras with her silver fork, popped it into her mouth, and savored it for a moment.

Then she looked at Evander and said approvingly, "Mr. Bexley, Lois says you're not only a tech whiz but also have a knack for food?"

Evander put down his utensils and smiled.

"I just like to explore it in my free time. I'm no professional gourmet.

"But this foie gras… cooked perfectly. Crispy outside, tender inside. The sweet-and-sour fig jam cuts the richness. Honestly, it's amazing."

Charlotte's admiration grew even more.

"Most young people these days are so eager for instant success. Rare to find someone who pays attention to details like this."

She paused, looking Evander up and down, from his upright posture to his calm way of speaking and his humble attitude when dealing with royalty.

The more she observed, the more she found him special.

"Real class isn't about fancy clothes or titles. It's about who you are inside. Half these noble kids with their fancy titles can't match your grace and smarts."

Hearing her mom praise Evander, Lois blushed and gently tugged at Evander's sleeve, her eyes beaming with pride.

As he felt her gesture, Evander's heart melted.

He said to Charlotte, "You're being too kind. I just like to do things carefully. I don't think I deserve the word 'class'."

"Don't be so humble." Charlotte waved her hand and signaled the servant to refill the wine.

"After all these years, I can read people pretty well. Lois is really lucky to have met you."

During the following dinner, the atmosphere got even more laid-back.

After the meal, when the servant brought in coffee and desserts, they all moved to the living-room sofa.

Taking a sip of coffee, Charlotte fixed her eyes on Evander, and her expression gradually turned solemn.

"Mr. Bexley, I've got something to tell you about the cooperation with Royal Tech."

Evander sat up straight, a bit tense. "Sure, please go ahead. I'm listening."

Charlotte said slowly, lowering her voice a bit, "Sir Gary's refusal to cooperate isn't just because he thinks Royal Tech doesn't need outside help. The real reason is that there are still major flaws in Royal Tech's core technology for humanoid robots.

"They've been trying to make humanoid robots develop self-awareness, enabling them to walk, rest, and even think like humans without manual control.

"But after years of research, they still haven't overcome the technological bottleneck."

Evander was quite surprised.

He'd only seen Royal Tech's surface-level achievements in the field of humanoid robots and never thought their core technology was still underdeveloped.

Charlotte went on, with a touch of helplessness in her voice.

"To solve this problem, Royal Tech has been secretly collaborating with brain research centers and the Scientific Paranormal Research.

"They're attempting to use the AetherMind Chip to simulate human brain signals and give robots self-awareness.

"Over the years, they've also drawn inspiration from the Kabbalistic Tree of Life, believing its changing laws might hold the key to the mystery of consciousness."

"Kabbalistic Tree of Life?" Evander exclaimed.

With a bit of knowledge of Esoteric Studies, he knew the Kabbalistic Tree of Life was part of Kabbalah, full of complex philosophical ideas and mathematical logic.

He never imagined Royal Tech would use it to study humanoid robots' self-awareness.

"Exactly." Charlotte nodded. "They've discovered that the concept of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life is similar to the binary system in computers, but it's more complex and flexible.

"Binary only has '0' and '1', while each path of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life can generate countless variations, similar to the diversity of human consciousness.

"Unfortunately, after nearly a decade of research, they still haven't found the key to combining them."

Evander's eyes lit up right away.

He'd been racking his brains on how to break through Royal Tech's technological barrier, and unexpectedly, here was the breakthrough point.

Although he wasn't an expert on the Kabbalistic Tree of Life, with his understanding of Kabbalah and in-depth research on AI technology, he might just find the clues Royal Tech had overlooked.

Evander said, his voice filled with excitement, "Thanks a lot for telling me all this.

"Your trust in sharing such important info means the world to me.

"I'll search for more information online tonight and study the theory and mathematical principles of the Kabbalistic Tree of Life. Maybe I can really find a way to break through the bottleneck."

Charlotte said, smiling with relief as she saw the sparkle in his eyes, "I'm sure you can.

"Once you break through this technological bottleneck, combining Vanguard Tech's AI technology with self-aware robots will be a game-changer.

"Then, Sir Gary will come to you for cooperation on his own; no need for you to reach out to him."
