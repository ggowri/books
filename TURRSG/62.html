Chapter 63 Taking Down the Night Owls

Aileen's voice was choked with relief, on the verge of tears. But before she could continue, Elsa took over, her tone steady and commanding.

"Mr. Bexley, where are you? Are you hurt?" Elsa's words were urgent, yet her calm never faltered.

Evander took a deep breath. "I'm at the grove by the old steel plant on the city's edge. I'm alright—just a hit to the neck, nothing major. But listen, I found their hideout. It's under the plant. They've got drug labs, weapons, and tons of armed guys."

He quickly recited the directions he'd memorized. "From the city, go past the red water tower, drive about three kilometers, then take a left after the broken stone bridge. That'll get you to the plant. The entrance is through the main factory elevator. Hit negative three to get to their main base. And hey, I've already tied their leader up in an office. She's a woman in black with short hair and a scar on her face. Secure her first."

Elsa's voice was filled with determination and a hint of excitement. "Stay put. Don't move. I'm getting the whole security team on this. We'll be there in thirty minutes. Tonight, we end the Night Owls for good."

As the call ended, Evander leaned back against a tree, watching the distant headlights. His pulse finally began to slow down. He rubbed his neck, wincing at the soreness. He was exhausted, but the thought of victory—his survival and discovery—kept him steady.

*****

Meanwhile, the Nogales Security Bureau was buzzing with energy. Elsa marched into the command center, her presence commanding the room.

"Activate Level One operation protocol now," she ordered. "Chief Hillary, you're in charge. Get twenty patrol cars and fifty SWAT officers ready. Arm them with body armor and rifles. Meet at the poplar grove in thirty minutes."

She pointed at the map on the wall, her finger landing on the steel plant. "The target's the Night Owls' underground facility. Expect heavy resistance. Move quietly—no sirens. We strike fast and hard."

"Got it!" Hillary saluted and got to work.

Officers sprang into action, checking weapons and putting on vests. The command center was alive with controlled urgency—every step bringing them closer to the Night Owls' downfall.

Elsa studied the map, her eyes sharp with resolve. The Night Owls had tormented Nogales for years, dodging police raids time and again. Now, with Evander's intel, they finally had a shot at ending it all!

Half an hour later, a convoy of police cars silently slipped out of the bureau, heading for the outskirts of the city. In the night, they looked like a black serpent gliding along the empty highway, headlights dimmed and engines muffled.

When they reached the grove, Hillary was the first to step out. He spotted Evander by the roadside and hurried over. "Mr. Bexley, you've done enough. Shall we move now?"

Evander nodded, pointing to the silhouette of the abandoned steel plant. "Follow me. The entrance is in the main factory's elevator. Most of their guys are in the drug lab and armory. The office area has only a few guards."

He led the way, with Hillary and the SWAT officers close behind. Under the moonlight, the rundown plant loomed like a beast in the dark. The lamps by the gate flickered, casting shaky shadows across the rusting walls.

Evander pushed open the heavy door, hinges groaning, and guided the team through the factory floor to the elevator. "This is it. Press negative three."

He hit the button, and the doors creaked open, releasing the stale scent of rust and damp air. One by one, the officers stepped inside. Hillary signaled for two of them to stay at the entrance, blocking any escape routes.

The elevator descended slowly, its metallic groans echoing loudly. When the doors opened, a sharp chemical odor hit them. Evander signaled for silence and stepped out first, gesturing left. It was the lab.

Through the windowed wall, they saw machines humming and workers in blue uniforms, unaware of the incoming danger.

"Move," Hillary commanded quietly.

SWAT officers burst into the room, weapons raised. "Freeze! Police!"

Panic ensued. Some workers dropped their tools and ran, while others reached for guns hidden under tables. But the officers were faster, forcing them to the ground. "Hands on your head! Down, now!"

In minutes, over a dozen suspects were subdued. Then gunfire erupted from the armory.

"They know we're here!" Hillary snapped, charging toward the noise with part of the team.

Evander followed. Though unarmed, his martial arts training helped him navigate the chaos. A gunman lunged at him; Evander sidestepped and punched him hard in the jaw, dropping him. The attacker still clutched a pistol, finger twitching on the trigger. Evander stomped on his wrist, pried the weapon free, and fired at another assailant rushing toward them.

"Mr. Bexley, watch yourself!" Hillary shouted, taking down another hostile with a precise shot.

Evander nodded quickly, joining the unit as they advanced. He knew the underground layout better than anyone and pointed out hidden corridors and ambush points, steering the team clear of traps.

Elsewhere, another squad reached the office. Following Evander's directions, they found the hidden compartment under the desk and pulled out the bound Karla. Half-conscious, Karla's eyes widened in fury and fear at the sight of the uniforms. She struggled against her restraints, but the officers pinned her down and cuffed her.

The battle raged on for nearly an hour. By the end, every armed man in the base was either captured or killed while resisting arrest. No one escaped.

Hillary surveyed the aftermath—suspects in cuffs, piles of seized drugs, weapons, and counterfeit goods stacked high—and a rare smile spread across his face. He laughed, "We did it. The Night Owls are finished."

SWAT officers spread through the underground complex, photographing evidence, logging seized contraband, and escorting prisoners to waiting vans.

Moments later, Elsa arrived on the scene. She walked into the lab, her heels clicking against the stained concrete. The sight of the dismantled operation filled her with grim satisfaction. Her gaze settled on Evander—dusty-faced and exhausted, but his eyes were still bright.

"Mr. Bexley," she said sincerely, stepping closer, "thank you. Without your intel and cooperation, we couldn't have taken down the Night Owls so smoothly. You've earned a lot of credit tonight."

Evander gave a weary smile, letting the fatigue show a bit. "It was the least I could do. Helping you was an honor. Besides..." He paused, his voice dropping with a sense of personal resolve. "I was their victim. Being here, helping to end them—it feels like justice."

He met her eyes with honesty. "And I have to say, seeing how you led tonight—calm under pressure, commanding the whole operation—you've earned my respect."

A faint, rare smile touched Elsa's lips, almost shy despite her usual toughness. She smiled, "Enough of that. It's past two in the morning. You're hurt and need rest. Let me take you back."

"Alright," Evander agreed softly, following her into the waiting car.
