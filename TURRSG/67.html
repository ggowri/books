Chapter 68 Magic and Ambition

Evander noticed Elsa's delighted reaction and quietly breathed a sigh of relief. Thank goodness she just thought it was a magic trick.

He wrapped his arm around her waist and kissed her forehead softly. "Just a little trick. Glad you like it. This agate's for you."

"Really?" Elsa squealed, sounding just like an excited schoolgirl. She held the red agate close, her cheeks turning a sweet shade of pink. "Thank you, Evander. This is the most special gift I've ever gotten."

She handled the agate with care, like it was a rare treasure, her eyes shining with even more admiration for him. Evander seemed to be everything she dreamed of in a guy—brave, clever, and now pulling off amazing magic.

Evander smiled, playfully ruffling her hair, his gaze drifting to the Arcane Ring on his finger. Its red glow had faded, turning back to a dull black, but he could feel it connecting with him more, almost like it was part of him.

Inventory Cube... Evander silently repeated the name in his mind, attempting to store the agate back in the space. As he focused, the agate disappeared from Elsa's hand.

"Hey! Where'd the agate go?" Elsa gasped, frantically looking at her hand and around the area, her face full of confusion. "It's gone!"

"Don't worry," Evander said, taking her hand, concentrating, and making the agate reappear in her palm.

"Wow!" Elsa was completely stunned. She stared at the agate, then back at Evander, eyes wide with awe. "Evander, you're amazing! How do you do that? Can you teach me?"

"It's a secret," Evander smirked, playfully pinching her cheek. "Maybe after a few more... intimate conversations, I'll tell you."

Elsa blushed deeper at his suggestive tone but nodded eagerly. "Deal! You make me happier than I've ever been, Evander. Thank you!"

She hugged him tightly, pressing her face against his chest, listening to his steady heartbeat. Sunlight warmed their skin as the pool's mist mixed with the floral scent, filling the air with a sweet sense of closeness.

Evander and Elsa strolled into the living room, heading to the dressing room to change.

Elsa slipped back into her cream-colored dress, which made her skin look even fairer, while Evander donned light gray loungewear that couldn't hide his strong frame. He then led Elsa to the large sofa.

Earlier, he'd gone to the dining room's wine cabinet, eyeing the neatly arranged bottles, including two 1982 Château Lafite Rothschilds left by his grandfather. Evander closed his eyes, focused, and thought, Store.

Instantly, the wine bottles and a set of crystal goblets from a nearby cabinet vanished without a trace.

Elsa sat on the sofa, curiously studying the bookshelf in the living room. Evander's voice snapped her back. "Elsa, check this out."

She turned to see him sitting beside her, his hands empty. A second later, a bottle of red wine appeared in his right hand, and two sparkling crystal goblets materialized in his left.

"Oh my gosh!" Elsa gasped, covering her face with her hands before reaching out to touch the wine bottle and goblets. They felt cool and solid, undeniably real, with the bottle's label clearly reading "1982 Château Lafite Rothschild."

She picked up the Lafite, her fingers tracing its intricate design, then looked at Evander, eyes wide in disbelief. "Is this... real? Not just a magic prop?"

"Of course it's real," Evander grinned, conjuring a corkscrew and opening the bottle. He poured the wine into the goblets, the deep red liquid swirling and leaving delicate legs on the glass, irresistibly tempting.

Evander handed one goblet to Elsa, his tone playful. "Give it a taste. See if it's familiar."

Elsa took the goblet with both hands, sipping carefully. The rich fruit aroma bloomed on her tongue, with smooth tannins and a lingering finish—the unmistakable taste of 1982 Lafite!

Her eyes lit up, staring at Evander with amazement. "It's real! It's really 1982 Lafite! Evander, how do you do it? Your magic is so convincing it could fool anyone!"

Having navigated Nogales' political scene for years and seen countless oddities, she'd never witnessed someone conjure wine and glasses from thin air, perfectly replicating the taste of top-tier Lafite. Gone was her deputy mayor's composure; she was like a starry-eyed fangirl, totally smitten with Evander.

She marveled at the wine. "This is unbelievable... absolutely magical..."

Evander watched her excitement, a satisfied smile on his lips. This was exactly the reaction he wanted. He wrapped his arm around her waist, fingers lightly brushing her dress, enjoying the softness.

Sunlight, red wine, and a beautiful woman in his arms—he felt completely content.

Just then, Elsa's phone on the coffee table rang, the screen flashing "Office Call." She frowned, reluctantly pulling away from Evander to answer."What's up?"

Evander faintly heard the assistant mention an "urgent meeting."

Elsa nodded. "Got it. I'll be at the office in thirty minutes."

She hung up and turned to Evander with apologetic eyes. "Sorry, Evander, I have to attend an urgent meeting. We'll have to continue our conversation another day. I need to go."

She grabbed her bag and headed out. Evander walked her to the villa's entrance. Elsa turned back, giving him a wistful glance. "Don't forget to miss me!"

With that, she got into her car and drove off.

Evander watched her white sedan disappear, a relieved smile spreading across his face.

Elsa's visit had activated the Arcane Ring's first magic, fulfilling his goal.

Next, he planned to use her connections to meet more nobles, further evolving the ring.

Perhaps one day, he'd grow so strong he'd no longer need to pass his grandfather's trial. He would surpass him!
