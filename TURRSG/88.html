Chapter 89 Helheim

Felix's heart nearly exploded with joy. He gave Kelly a deep bow. "Thank you, Aunt Kelly! Once Evander's gone, I promise I'll visit you all the time."

He spun around and rushed out of the hospital room. The special forces guards at the door immediately snapped to attention when they saw him.

Felix waved them toward the car. "Come on, let's get out of here. Tomorrow, we'll have some good news to celebrate."

The black sedan pulled away from the psychiatric hospital and disappeared into the night.

Back in the room, Kelly placed the photo on her bedside table.

She moved to the center of the room, sat cross-legged on the floor, and closed her eyes. Her hands pressed together in an intricate gesture.

She began chanting in a language that felt ancient and incomprehensible.

Each word seemed to summon Hela, and with every syllable, the warmth in the room vanished. The temperature dropped several degrees.

The dim bedside lamp flickered a few times, then died completely. Darkness swallowed the room except for thin streams of moonlight sneaking through the curtain gaps.

Kelly's body began to shake. Her long hair lifted and swayed in the still air like she was underwater.

When her eyes snapped open, they were completely bloodshot. Her voice turned raspy and unfamiliar, like someone else was speaking through her.

"Kelly Gosse. You called me. What do you want?"

"Your Highness Hela." Kelly's body collapsed forward onto the floor in a deep bow. Her voice was filled with absolute reverence.

"My nephew Felix has been humiliated by some mortal named Evander Bexley. This human doesn't know his place. He even has some kind of strange powers.

"I'm begging you, drag his soul into Helheim. Let him rot in the realm of the dead as punishment for his arrogance."

In the darkness, an invisible presence seemed to focus on the photo lying on the table.

After a moment, that raspy voice spoke again, cold amusement threading through every word.

"Evander... What a fascinating little soul." Hela seemed to let out a sneer. "If that's what you desire, then so be it.

"I will see this human for myself. If his soul is... delectable, he shall be brought to my realm and bound to my service."

"Thank you, Your Highness Hela!" Kelly pressed her face to the floor, her whole body shaking with excitement.

The harsh voice faded away. The room's temperature slowly returned to normal, and the desk lamp flickered back to life.

Kelly rose from the floor, a twisted smile curling on her lips. She stared at the photo on the table, muttering to herself.

"Evander... you won't see another sunrise."

******

Meanwhile, back at Royal One Estate, Evander had pulled some gourmet food from his Inventory Cube to share with Lois.

Now they were curled up on the big couch in his bedroom, going over the details for tomorrow's Royal Council meeting.

Lois leaned against Evander's chest, files on the Council members spread in her lap. She spoke quietly.

"Tomorrow I'll start with the chairman, Sir Gary Tomlinson. He was one of my father's oldest friends, so if we can win him over, the rest of the board should fall in line.

"Just know that Sir Gary's pretty cautious when it comes to tech partnerships. You'll need to bring your A-game."

Evander nodded, running his fingers gently through her hair. "Don't worry. I've got all of Vanguard Tech's core technology specs ready to go. Once they hear what I'm offering, they'll be convinced."

He had no idea that disaster was already closing in on him.

Outside, the night grew darker. Clouds swallowed the moonlight whole, and shadows seemed to creep across the entire estate like something wicked this way comes.

Lois yawned and settled against his chest, her eyes drifting shut. "It's getting late. We should get some sleep. Tomorrow's going to be a long day."

Evander scooped her up and carried her to the bed, tucking the covers around her before sliding in beside her. He watched her peaceful face in the dim light, a soft smile tugging at his lips.

He couldn't know that this quiet night would be his last taste of peace on earth. Hela, goddess of death, had already marked his soul. The gates of Helheim were creaking open, waiting for him.

Deep in the night, an icy gust swept through out of nowhere.

It slipped through the window cracks, carrying the stench of decay that only came from the underworld, and crept silently into the bedroom.

Evander's face twisted in his sleep, his breathing growing shallow. Something invisible seemed to grab his soul and yank it right out of his body, pulling him toward the darkness outside his window.

He felt like he was floating in zero gravity. Wind roared in his ears while shifting shadows swirled around him. He had no idea how long he drifted through that void before his feet finally hit solid ground.

What he saw made his blood freeze.

A murky black river stretched out in front of him.

The water had this sick greenish tint to it and reeked like rotting fish. White bones bobbed on the surface here and there.

This was the Gjöll, the legendary black river that separated the world of the living from Helheim.

A narrow bridge stretched across the water. The structure was built from some dark yellow material that, on closer inspection, looked disturbingly like countless fingernails from the dead, all fitted together.

Twisted runes crawled along the railings, radiating pure menace. It was Gjallarbrú, the Bridge of Wailing Souls.

At the far end crouched a massive hound. Its eyes burned red like hot coals, its fur was pitch black, and thick drool dripped from its jaws.

It was Garmr, the hellhound that guarded the entrance to the Underworld.

The moment Garmr spotted Evander, it sprang to its feet and let out a deafening howl. Then it lunged forward, ready to tear him apart.

Evander instinctively stepped back, but his body felt weightless, completely drained of strength. That was when it hit him. He was in soul form now. He couldn't fight this hellhound even if he tried.

A cold voice echoed from the other end of the bridge. "Garmr, back off. He's with me."

The massive dog heard the command and immediately backed down. It let out a few frustrated growls before settling back into a crouch, but those blood-red eyes stayed locked on Evander like he was dinner.

Evander's eyes tracked toward the voice. There, at the bridge's end, stood a robed figure draped in black. He couldn't make out the face, but death seemed to pour off the stranger like fog.

"Follow me."

The figure turned and walked into the darkness ahead. Evander didn't have much choice but to follow.

Once he stepped into Helheim, the real horror hit him.

Endless darkness swallowed everything whole. No trace of sunlight existed here. Only scattered dead fires flickered in the distance, their eerie blue-green glow casting ghostly shadows that made the landscape look even more twisted.

Bones littered the ground everywhere he looked. Lost souls drifted past him, letting out bone-chilling wails that echoed through the air. The smell of decay and cold bit into him so deep that even his soul started shivering.

After walking for about 15 minutes, a massive palace loomed ahead of them. The sight was both magnificent and terrifying.

The roof was covered in dead snakes, their scales gleaming cold and dull in the flickering light. The walls were built from enormous bones stacked high, and two skeleton guards flanked the entrance, gripping rusted swords in their bony hands. This was Hela's palace—Éljúðnir, the House of Misery.

Evander felt awed but not afraid. He followed the figure through the palace doors.

Inside, no torches burned. Only the dead flames embedded in the walls gave off any light.

In the center of the great hall, a throne made of white bones sat on a raised platform. A woman occupied the throne.

When Evander got a clear look at her, his breath caught in his throat.

The woman's body was split perfectly down the middle.

Her right side had skin as white as fresh snow, with golden hair cascading like a waterfall and blue eyes as deep as the ocean. She wore a dark purple robe with black runes embroidered along the collar and sleeves.

A necklace of tiny bones hung around her neck, and a cloak woven from raven feathers draped over her shoulders. Each feather caught the dim light, radiating the kind of divine authority that made mortals tremble.

But her left side was a completely different story.

The skin was gray and rotted like dead wood. The muscle had wasted away, exposing patches of stark white bone underneath. Maggots actually crawled through the festering wounds, and the stench of decay was so strong it made him want to gag.

The contrast with her beautiful right side was absolutely horrifying.

This had to be Hela, the goddess of death who ruled Helheim.

Evander steadied himself and walked to the center of the hall. He looked up at Hela on her throne, his voice carrying a hint of curiosity.

"Your Highness, am I dead? Why did you bring me here? Please don't tell me this is your way of asking me out."
