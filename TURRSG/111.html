Chapter 112 Felix's Fury

After discussing the final details of the signing ceremony, Evander and Walter left the Royal Tech building together.

As soon as they got into the car, Evander pulled out his phone and called Norman.

The call connected after a few rings, and Norman's steady voice came through.

"Evan, how's the research coming along? Any progress with Royal Tech?"

"Grandpa!"

Evander's voice brimmed with excitement.

"We did it! Sir Gary agreed to the core tech exchange. The signing ceremony is set for tomorrow at 2 PM, but they need you there in person to sign. Where are you right now? Can you make it?"

There was a pause, and then Norman's voice returned, this time with a hint of amusement.

"Haha, Evan, well done. I'm already at the airport, about to board."

Evander blinked in disbelief.

"Wait—what? Did I hear you right? You're boarding? Boarding for where?"

"London, of course," Norman said, teasing him a little.

"I knew you'd close the deal, so I booked a flight in advance. Was going to surprise you. But since the ceremony's set for tomorrow at 2, perfect timing."

He continued, his tone shifting into business mode, "I'll land at Heathrow around midnight. Tell them to move the signing to 9 AM. Let's get it done early, so we have time to align on the tech specifics with Royal Tech."

Evander could hardly believe what he was hearing. Norman had planned this all along.

"Grandpa, why didn't you say anything earlier? I'll call Duke Chad right now and ask him to shift the ceremony to 9 AM."

"No rush. First, arrange for someone to pick me up from the airport. Keep it low-key. Just a driver is fine," Norman instructed. "And how's Lois?"

"She's still at the Royal Three Estate. As soon as the ceremony's done, I'm going to get her."

"Good," Norman said, clearly satisfied.

"All right then. I'm about to board. I'll call you once I land in London. Just make sure everything goes smoothly tomorrow. No hiccups."

After the call ended, Evander was still beaming, unable to hide the smile tugging at his lips.

Walter, watching him light up, chuckled.

"Sir Westbrook's coming?"

"He's already on his way. He'll be in London by midnight!"

Evander could barely contain himself. "He even suggested moving the signing to nine in the morning."

Walter smiled warmly.

"That's fantastic news. With Sir Westbrook there in person, the signing will go off without a hitch. Go on, call Duke Chad and let him know."

Evander nodded and dialed Chad immediately.

When Chad heard Norman was landing that very night and that he'd offered to move the ceremony earlier, he was pleasantly surprised.

"That's amazing! I'll notify Gary and the Royal Office right away. We'll move the signing to 9 AM sharp. Mr. Bexley, your grandfather doesn't waste time, does he?"

Meanwhile, in the attic of the Royal Three Estate...

Lois sat by the window, reading and rereading Evander's message.

Evander: [The partnership's sealed. The ceremony's tomorrow.]

Her fingers grazed across the screen, a radiant smile blooming on her face.

She stepped closer to the window, gazing out at the garden of full-bloom roses.

Her hand instinctively touched the pendant around her neck, and she whispered softly, "Evander... come to me. I miss you so much."

One of the maids brought up a tray of fruit and noticed her glowing expression. She couldn't help but grin.

"Your Highness, you're in such a good mood today. Is it because of Mr. Bexley?"

Lois blushed and gave a small nod.

"Mm-hmm. Once the signing ceremony's over tomorrow, he's coming to get me."

The maid exclaimed. "That's wonderful! You've been cooped up in this attic for so long. It's about time."

Elsewhere, Felix was pacing furiously in his study.

Moments earlier, Mourid had informed him that Royal Tech would be signing a contract with Vanguard Tech the next day. And worse, even Edward stopped fighting against Evander dating Lois.

His rage ignited instantly, like gas catching fire.

"How is this happening?" Felix growled, slamming his fist against the table.

"Evander's just a nobody! How does a damn commoner get everyone's approval? Lois is my fiancée. She belongs with me!"

Mourid watched Felix spiral and let out a helpless sigh.

"Felix, at this point, being angry won't change anything. But... I did overhear something interesting from the captain of the royal guard. Apparently, King Edward has other plans. There still might be a window for you."

Felix's eyes lit up.

"What plans? Tell me!"

"I'm not sure of the details," Mourid admitted.

"But I heard King Edward wants Queen Charlotte to take Lois abroad for school. If you can go with her... you might still have a shot."

Hope surged in Felix's chest.

"Studying abroad? Perfect! As long as I can stay close to Lois, I don't care where we go. I'm going to see King Edward right now!"

Midnight, Heathrow Airport...

Norman dressed in a dark suit, stepped out of the terminal looking sharp and alert despite the hour.

The driver Evander had sent was already waiting at the exit. The moment he spotted Norman, he hurried over.

"Sir Westbrook, I'm your driver. Mr. Bexley sent me."

Norman gave a polite nod and followed him to the car.

As the vehicle made its way toward the Royal Horseguards Hotel, city lights flickered by outside the window. A faint smile played on Norman's lips.

So, Evander made it through the fifth trial after all. Looks like it's time to raise the bar.
