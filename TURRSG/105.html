Chapter 106 Vow of Bitter Revenge

In the late hours, London was veiled in mist, while the walls of Ragnarok Psychiatric Hospital caught the moonlight, their gray-white surface looking bleak and unyielding.

Felix pulled a black cap low over his face, keeping to the shadows of the rear wall as he slipped past the patrolling guards.

He clenched a crumpled note in his hand, the one he had secretly obtained earlier from Mourid and Maria with details of the underground ward's entrance.

Ever since Evander had drugged him and forced him to sign the annulment agreement, hatred had been spreading through Felix like wild vines, choking the breath out of him.

After turning it over again and again, Felix realized that only his aunt, Kelly, could help him.

The entrance to the underground ward was hidden behind a concealed door in the pharmacy storeroom. Taking advantage of the nurse on duty dozing off, Felix slipped inside, feeling along the wall until his fingers found the switch.

With a faint click, the hidden door creaked open, releasing a wave of damp, moldy air. He drew in a steadying breath, lifted the light on his phone, and started down the steep staircase.

The basement corridor was lit only by emergency lamps, their dim yellow glow stretching Felix's shadow until it loomed grotesque across the walls.

Following the markings on his note, he quickly reached Kelly's room. He knocked softly, and a guarded voice called from inside. "Who is it?"

"Aunt Kelly, it's me, Felix," Felix whispered. "I need to talk to you. It's about Evander that damn commoner."

The door then opened just a sliver. Kelly stuck her head out, and only after recognizing him did she move aside to allow him inside.

The room was bare, furnished with only a bed, a table, and a chair. Kelly wore a hospital gown, her face pale, her expression still clouded with traces of confusion.

She closed the door, her voice tight with worry. "Felix, why are you here? Hasn't Scientific Paranormal Research given you trouble?"

Felix sat down at the table, his fists clenched so tightly his knuckles turned white. His voice seethed with hatred as he said, "Aunt Kelly, I came because I need your help to take revenge.

"Evander has gone too far. He not only let Hela throw me into Helheim's dungeon, but he also stole Lois from me and drugged me into signing the annulment."

The more Felix spoke, the more his anger surged, his bloodshot eyes burning with rage.

He snarled, "I nearly died in that dungeon. That crushing, dark, and freezing cold will haunt me forever. Lois was supposed to be my fiancée, yet he stole her from me with his filthy tricks. I won't rest until he's dead."

As Felix grew worked up, Kelly looked at him with pity. She herself had been outmaneuvered by Evander before, so she understood that mix of helplessness and rage.

She stepped closer to Felix and spoke gently, "I understand how you feel. Evander really did go too far.

"He used my abilities as a medium and then had me thrown into this place. The problem is... my strength isn't what it used to be. Summoning Hela drained me so much that I don't even know if I can call on another god again."

Felix grabbed her hand, his eyes full of expectation, and said, "You're the one who can make it happen, Aunt Kelly.

"Come on, you're the most gifted medium. If you could summon Hela, you could definitely reach an even greater god. As long as it means that damn commoner Evander dies, I'm willing to give up anything."

Kelly hesitated, her own memories of being used by Evander stirring up a wave of bitterness.

Finally, she nodded. "Alright, I'll help you. But I can't summon Hela again. She seems to have some sort of tie to Evander, and she might not side with us. Let me think... Maybe I could try calling on the Goddess Frigg instead."

"Frigg?" Felix froze for a moment. He didn't know much about the Norse gods, only that Frigg was the Allfather, Odin's wife, and held a position of great honor.

"That's right." Kelly walked to the center of the room, closed her eyes, and drew in a deep breath.

She added, "She is the goddess of the sky and of marriage. If she agrees to help, not only will Evander pay the price, but there's even a chance you and Lois could be reunited."

As Kelly spoke, she pulled a small bronze pendant from her pocket. It was the medium she had once used for summoning, its surface etched with intricate runes.

She set the pendant on the table, formed a seal with her hands, and began chanting an ancient incantation.

Almost immediately, the air in the room grew colder. The lights flickered, and shadows along the walls twisted as though something was trying to force its way out of the darkness.

Felix watched Kelly anxiously, his palms slick with sweat. He held his breath, waiting for Frigg to appear.

But Kelly went on chanting for a long time, her voice growing weaker as the strange signs around them faded. The bronze pendant remained on the table, unchanged and silent.

At last, she opened her eyes, her face even paler than before. Gasping for air, she whispered, "It's no use... My power still isn't enough. Frigg's divinity is too strong. I can't summon her."

Felix's expression dimmed, and he spoke with quiet defiance, "Let's try again. It has to work. Maybe if we change the spot, the result will be different?"

He glanced around, and suddenly his face lit up. "The rooftop! It's wide open and is closer to the sky. It might be easier to call Frigg there."

Hearing that, Kelly hesitated. She knew leaving the ward so late at night was dangerous, but when she saw the hope in Felix's face and thought of everything they had been through, she finally nodded.

She replied, "Alright, we'll try the rooftop. But we have to be careful. We can't let the staff or security see us."

With that, the two slipped quietly out of the ward and started up the stairs toward the rooftop. The hospital was eerily silent at that hour, their footsteps echoing down the empty corridors.

Felix kept a cautious eye on their surroundings. Once he was sure no guard was nearby, he led Kelly to the rooftop entrance.

He pushed open the heavy iron door, and a gust of cold wind rushed in, carrying the dampness of the night.
