Chapter 95 Are You Leaving Tonight

Evander couldn't tear his eyes away from Freya.

Her vulnerability was clear, the years of searching, the loneliness—and, in this moment, it was as if she wasn't a goddess at all, but just a woman desperate for the one she loved.

Her tears shimmered as they rolled down her face, and the ache in her eyes made his chest tighten.

With a soft nod, his voice barely above a whisper, he said, "If you don't mind, just a hug."

As soon as the words left his mouth, Freya stepped forward quickly, wrapping her arms around him tightly.

Her embrace was so strong that it seemed as if she wanted to meld Evander into her very being.

The feathers of the Eagle Feather Robe brushed against Evander's cheek, bringing a faint coolness, but they couldn't conceal the delicate yet rich floral scent on her body.

"Od... sweetie..." Her voice cracked with emotion as she held him close, her sobs making his chest tighten in sympathy.

Her tears fell onto his shoulder, soaking into his clothes as she poured out her pain. "Where have you been all these years?" she asked, her voice trembling.

"Do you know how hard I've searched for you? I've looked everywhere... even Hela has helped me. And yet, you disappeared without a trace..."

Her sobs carried the pent-up grievances of years, and every word was filled with heart-rending longing.

Evander stood stiffly in her embrace, his heart in turmoil.

He knew he wasn't Od, yet he couldn't bear to shatter this brief comfort.

Raising his hand, Evander gently wiped away the tears on Freya's face.

When his fingertips touched her skin, it felt smooth, and there was even a faint warmth, no longer the coldness he'd felt before.

"I..." Just as Evander was about to explain, his gaze landed on Freya's lips.

Her lips were tinged with a soft pink, like cherry blossoms blooming after the rain. With the tears on them, they were even more alluring.

Whether influenced by the atmosphere or driven by his own desires, Evander lowered his head slightly and planted a gentle kiss on her lips.

In that instant, it was as if an electric current had shot through his body.

To his surprise, Freya's lips were incredibly soft and carried a sweet fragrance, like the morning dew on flower petals, making one yearn for more.

Freya was stunned for a moment, and then intense love blazed in her eyes.

She deepened the kiss on her own initiative, gently prying open Evander's teeth with her tongue and entangling it with his.

"Od..." Freya's voice became even softer.

She wrapped her arms around Evander's waist and tiptoed.

The two slowly floated off the ground, embracing and kissing in mid-air in the palace.

The light refracted by the crystal walls shone on them, the glowing vines swayed gently around, and the floral fragrance in the air became even more intense.

The whole palace seemed to be enveloped in a romantic halo.

Evander received Freya's kiss passively, not daring to make any improper moves.

He could clearly feel the softness of Freya's bosom pressing against his chest. The skin under the Eagle Feather Robe was incredibly smooth, and every breath she took carried an intoxicating fragrance that quickened his heartbeat.

But he always remembered that he wasn't Od.

Evander was well aware of the insurmountable gap between mortals and gods.

If he truly crossed the line, he might not only anger the other gods but also invite even more terrifying punishments.

He gently pushed Freya away, his eyes filled with apology. "Goddess Freya, I'm sorry, I..."

"Don't call me Goddess. Just call me Freya," Freya interrupted him, pressing her forehead against his.

Her lavender eyes were full of infatuation.

"You're Od. I know it. Even if you've forgotten me, I can feel that you're the husband I've been waiting for all these years."

Looking at the obsession in her eyes, Evander couldn't help but feel a twinge of helplessness.

He was aware that any attempt at explanation at this moment would be in vain. Freya was firmly convinced that he was Od.

Instead of forcefully refuting and hurting her feelings, it was wiser to go along with her for the time being and find an opportunity to clear up the misunderstanding later.

"Freya..." Evander called her name softly, reaching up to tenderly stroke her long, golden hair.

The strands slipped through his fingers like liquid sunlight, warm and soft.

Hearing her name called, Freya broke into a brilliant smile. It was like the first sign of melting ice and snow, instantly brightening up the entire palace.

She wrapped her arms around Evander again and slowly twirled with him in mid-air. The Eagle Feather Robe fluttered open, resembling a pair of huge silver wings as they soared through the crystal palace.

"Od, I'll take you to my garden," Freya said, tugging at Evander's hand and flying towards an archway at the rear of the palace.

As the door swung open, a riot of vividly-colored flowers came into sight.

Countless rare and beautiful blossoms were in full bloom. Some petals glowed faintly, and some gave off a gentle, ethereal halo. Butterflies flitted among the flowers, and the air was filled with a refreshing, sweet fragrance.

"This is my garden. I planted every flower with my own hands. Each one is a symbol of my yearning for you," Freya said, leading Evander by the hand through the flower field.

Her fingertips lightly grazed the petals. "I thought you'd never return. Little did I know... I'd see you again."

As Evander looked at the happiness shining in her eyes, his heart grew even heavier.

He knew this happiness was based on Freya's infatuation. Once the truth came out, how much of a shock would Freya have to endure?

But he couldn't bear to shatter this idyllic moment.

So, he simply accompanied her on a stroll through the flower field, listening to her tales about the past she shared with Od.

"You used to love watching the sunset with me here. You said the sunset here was even more enchanting than that in Asgard."

Freya pointed at a gazebo in the distance. "We brewed mead here together. You said you'd save the finest jar for our child..." Her voice faded away.

Her eyes were filled with a blend of longing and sorrow.

Evander listened quietly, saying nothing, just gently squeezing her hand.

He could feel the warmth gradually seeping back into Freya's palm.

It was the warmth of a god, yet it held the profound affection of a mortal.

Before he realized it, the sky started to darken.

The flowers in the garden began to emit a more intense glow, illuminating the whole garden as brightly as day.

Freya stopped and turned to look at Evander, with a touch of reluctance in her eyes.

"Od, are you leaving tonight? Can't you stay with me here in Sessrumnir?"

Evander's heart ached.

He knew he couldn't stay. Lois was waiting for him.

There were also matters to finalize regarding his cooperation with Royal Tech and Felix's situation to deal with.

Taking a steady breath, Evander met Freya's gaze, his expression firm.

"Freya, I'm sorry. I can't stay. There are things I need to take care of. Once it's done, I'll come back to see you."

Her face went pale, the spark of hope in her eyes dimming. "You're leaving again? Just like before... leaving me all alone?"

Evander shook his head quickly. "No. I'm not abandoning you. I just have to finish this first. I swear—I'll come back for you when it's over, alright?"

He knew it was an empty promise, but it was all he could offer to ease her pain.

Freya stayed quiet for a long moment before finally nodding, her eyes shimmering with hurt. "Fine. I'll wait. But this time, don't disappear for so long."

"I promise," Evander murmured, pulling her into a gentle embrace.

"Now... can you send me back?"

Freya nodded silently, then wrapped her arms around his waist.

The Eagle Feather Robe spread wide, catching the air, and in the next instant they became a streak of silver light—soaring out of the garden, past Sessrumnir Palace, and racing toward London.
