Chapter 62 Night Owls

Inside the getaway car, Evander was awake but kept his eyes shut, pretending to be out cold. His head throbbed, but he could hear the three masked men talking around him. Their voices were rough and unpolished, like they were from the streets.

"Are you sure we should hand this guy over to the Night Owls? Look at this guy—soft hands, no scars. He's built like some pampered bookworm," the youngest thug scoffed, his street-hardened voice dripping with skepticism.

"Shut your trap!" the other one snapped harshly. "Young Master gave us orders. You questioning them now, huh?"

"This guy knows the Young Master. We can't just let him meet the boss- he might recognize him. We'll have to wait for Ms. Heath's orders. Whether he gets beaten up or killed depends on her mood."

"And who the heck is this Young Master, anyway? Why's he got such a beef with him?"

"Questions like that will get you killed. Just focus on driving. If the cops spot us, we're done for."

Evander's chest tightened at the mention of the Young Master. Considering Jack's earlier attack, he figured the Master they talked about had to be him.

And the Night Owls... he'd heard about them before. One of the top criminal gangs in Nogales, dealing in drugs, weapons, and fake medicine. Elsa had tried to bring them down plenty of times, but they always slipped away thanks to their hidden backers.

Now Jack had teamed up with them just for revenge. Evander's mind raced. If he could use this situation to find the Night Owls' hideout, gather hard evidence, and help Elsa bust them, he might not only turn things around but also earn her trust.

That could give him the perfect chance to get closer to her and complete the third nourishment for the Arcane Ring.

He steeled himself for the risk. No guts, no glory. Keeping his body limp, he peeked out the window, taking in the passing scenery. The car was heading to the city's outskirts—old factories, empty fields, and rundown farmhouses.

Evander mentally marked key spots—a red water tower, a crumbling stone bridge, and a dense patch of woods. About an hour later, they arrived at an abandoned steel plant. The place looked like a ghost town, with cracked walls and shattered windows, just two flickering streetlamps at the entrance.

The masked men dragged Evander out and carried him through the rusty gates. Inside, it was a whole different world. The air reeked of chemicals mixed with gunpowder. They took him into a building, down a narrow stairway, and into a creaky old elevator. It groaned all the way down.

When the doors opened, a wave of acrid stench hit him. Below was a massive underground complex, buzzing with activity. Workers in blue uniforms moved between machines, stirring vats of liquid—likely drugs. Others assembled guns or packed pills into boxes.

Evander's pulse quickened; this was solid evidence of the Night Owls' crimes.

He kept his face blank, memorizing the layout, until they finally brought him into a surprisingly nice office. The room was all black carpet, with an abstract painting on the wall.

Behind the desk sat a woman in her thirties, short-haired, dressed in a black bodysuit showing off her toned arms. A thin scar marked her cheek, and her eyes were sharp and predatory.

So this was the infamous leader of the Night Owls, Karla Heath.

"Ms. Heath, we've brought the guy the Young Master wants dealt with," one of the men announced.

Karla looked him over, noting his sharp features and tall build. A flicker of interest crossed her face. She gestured for the men to leave, her voice rough. "Leave us. Close the door."

The men did as told, leaving Evander alone with her. Karla walked over, grabbing his jaw and lifting his head. "Not bad-looking. Shame you crossed the wrong guy."

Her hand drifted down his cheek, then tugged at his collar before ripping his shirt open. His chest muscles gleamed in the light.

"In the meantime," she murmured, eyes darkening, "might as well have some fun before deciding what to do with you."

Evander forced himself not to react. There were at least a dozen guys with guns outside. If he made a move now, he'd never make it out alive. So he stayed limp, pretending to be out cold.

Karla's hand roamed over his chest, her touch rough and possessive. She unfastened his belt, her fingers moving swiftly, igniting an instinctual response in his body.

Evander gritted his teeth, his mind racing with plans. He needed to wait for her to drop her guard before making his escape.

Karla's lips curled into a wicked smile. She straddled him, guiding him into her with fierce intensity, moving with a fervent rhythm. The office filled with the sound of her breaths and fabric rustling against skin.

Evander closed his eyes, feeling every sensation. His fingers crept toward a metal pen holder nearby, gripping it tightly as a silent promise of action.

After several intense minutes, Karla's pace quickened, her breath growing ragged. Suddenly, she shuddered, letting out a satisfied moan as her body trembled with release.

Now was the moment!

Evander's eyes snapped open as he lunged, grabbing Karla's throat with both hands. She froze, trying to scream, but only a strangled rasp came out as his grip tightened.

Her face flushed red, muscles straining. Evander held on, refusing to let go. Only when her gaze glazed over and her body went limp did he release her. Moving quickly, he dragged her behind the desk.

He tied her wrists and ankles with a necktie and belt, then gagged her with a strip of cloth before hiding her in a compartment under the desk.

Wasting no time, Evander fixed his clothes and searched the office for an exit. A crime boss like Karla would definitely have a secret escape route.

He found a safe behind the bookshelf sticking out slightly. Pushing hard, it slid aside, revealing a narrow, pitch-black tunnel.

Without hesitation, he crawled in. The passage was tight and dusty, but he kept moving, driven by one thought: Get out.

After several meters, a faint glow appeared. He quickened his pace.

When he finally emerged, fresh night air filled his lungs. Above, stars stretched across the sky, and nearby stood the poplar grove he'd memorized.

He was free.

Collapsing on the ground, Evander gasped for breath. His neck throbbed, but relief washed over him. Reaching into his pocket, he pulled out his phone. Miraculously, it hadn't been taken, just dead.

He swapped in a spare battery and turned it on.

"Aileen, it's me. I'm safe. I got out," he croaked. "Put Elsa on. I found the Night Owls' underground base."
