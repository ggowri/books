Chapter 86 Anything I Can Do

Evander burst out of the white building. He didn't call Anya right away.

At the corner, he paused, eyes sweeping the street. The black sedan was still parked outside. A few SPR agents leaned against it, chatting, clueless he was already gone.

He couldn't risk the Imperial Hotel. Felix knew his schedule. If he showed up there, they'd pin him in minutes.

I need somewhere safe. And I need to move on Royal Tech—fast.

One name came to mind. Lois.

Lois was the British royal family's eldest princess. Royal One Estate was her home—a fortress with security so tight even Felix wouldn't dare make a move there.

More importantly, Lois had real pull at Royal Tech. If Evander wanted access to the people who actually mattered, she was his best shot.

Decision made, he flagged down a taxi and gave the driver her address.

The man blinked, taken aback, then gave a low whistle. Not many people dared to name that destination out loud. Still, he put the car in gear.

The ride felt like forever. Evander kept his eyes on the rearview, scanning the windows, tense until he was certain no one from SPR was trailing them. Only then did he let his shoulders ease.

Thirty minutes later, the taxi slowed near Royal One Estate. The sprawling property loomed in the distance, half-shrouded by tall hedges and iron gates.

Evander paid the fare, waited for the driver to pull away, then slipped into the roadside bushes, vanishing from sight.

The estate was ringed with tall iron fencing crowned with live wires, security guards pacing steadily at the front gate. No way he was walking in that way.

Evander kept low, moving fast through the undergrowth. Every so often, he scaled a tree, using the height to study the mansion—its lighting, the rhythm of the patrols, the positions of the balconies.

His X-ray vision flickered on now and then, cutting through walls, tracing the people inside.

And then he saw it: a lit window on the second floor. Behind a sliver in the curtains lay a familiar figure on the bed. Lois.

He scanned the grounds one last time. No guards in sight. With a steady breath, he dropped from the tree, landing soundlessly on the balcony outside her room.

The glass door wasn't locked. He raised his hand and tapped gently against the frame, the knock barely audible.

Inside, Lois tossed and turned, sleep nowhere in sight. Worry for Evander had her wide awake.

A sudden knock—soft, urgent—came from the balcony. She froze, heart in her throat. Slowly, she padded toward the glass door and peeked through the curtain.

A tall silhouette stood outside. Evander.

Her pulse spiked. She yanked the door open, and there he was—clothes dusty, but his eyes sharp and alive.

Relief surged so hard it nearly broke her. "Mr. Bexley!" Her voice trembled. "How did you—never mind. Come inside, quick."

He stepped in, but before he could speak, Lois wrapped her arms around him from behind, clinging to his shirt like he might vanish if she let go. She pressed her cheek against his back, voice thick with emotion.

"Thank God... you're okay. When SPR took you, I couldn't sleep. I kept imagining what they might do. Tell me—did they hurt you?"

Evander turned to her, giving her shoulder a reassuring squeeze, and told her everything—how they'd darted him in the neck, what he'd seen in the lab, the spirits, and how his Arcane Ring had flared to life, helping him escape.

"Spirits?" Lois' eyes widened. "You mean those things are real?"

Her shock quickly hardened into fury.

"Felix again! He's so obsessed with getting revenge on you he'd actually team up with the SPR? That's not just reckless—that's evil."

Evander watched her trembling fists, her outrage burning hotter by the second. This was the moment.

He lowered his voice. "Lois, there's something important I need your help with."

"Tell me," she said instantly, nodding. "If I can do it, I will."

He drew a steady breath. "I need to be honest about who I really am. I'm a senior tech consultant for Vanguard Tech, back in the States. That's why I'm here in Britain—to negotiate overseas partnerships.

"My company wants to work with Royal Tech on a technology exchange. Vanguard has world-class AI. Royal Tech has humanoid robotics.

"We'd trade—each side gets what it needs. You'd keep Europe. We'd focus on the Americas and Asia."

The smile faded from Lois' face. Her brows knit, her tone turning careful.

"A core tech exchange? Mr. Bexley, that's... nearly impossible. Royal Tech has never shared its core technology. Not once.

"Everything's tightly controlled by the royal family. Even I don't have the authority. Something like that would need approval from the Royal Council."

Evander had expected as much. His gaze held hers, steady and sincere.

"I know it's a long shot. But if you can help me get a meeting with the Council, I believe we can prove it's worth it. Vanguard's tech could bring real benefits to Britain—economic, and strategic."

Lois was silent for a beat. Then a sly smile tugged at her lips, her eyes sparkling with mischief. "Mr. Bexley, if I pull strings and make this happen... how exactly are you going to repay me?"

Evander blinked, thrown for half a second—then he grinned. "Whatever you want. Name it."

Lois's cheeks warmed with a faint blush. She slipped her hand into his and gave it a tug, pulling him toward the bathroom.

"Well then... you can start by helping me clean up," she teased, her voice low and lilting. "Do a good enough job, and I'll pull every string I have. Even the Royal Council won't be able to turn me down."

Evander's pulse quickened at the sight of her flushed face and eager eyes. Without a second thought, he scooped her up in his arms and strode into the bathroom.

The room glowed with soft light, a massive tub filled with warm water and scattered rose petals, the air thick with a faint floral scent.

He set Lois gently on the tub's edge, his hands moving to slip off her nightgown.

Her skin shimmered under the light, her full, soft breasts rising and falling with each breath. A hint of shyness flickered in her eyes, but she didn't pull away.

Evander's fingers grazed her skin, feeling her slight tremble. He leaned in, pressing a slow kiss to her shoulder.

Lois simply closed her eyes, letting the moment take her.
