Chapter 111 The Decision Matrix Model

Chad was thrilled by the news.

"Excellent! I'll get everything arranged immediately. I'll notify Gary and the core scientists at Royal Tech to attend. This is your best shot. Make it count."

After hanging up, Evander walked over to the window.

The morning sun was just beginning to rise, casting a golden glow over the skyline. His heart was full of anticipation.

He pulled out his phone and sent a message to Lois.

Evander: [Lois, I'll be presenting the research in three days. Once I succeed, I'm coming to get you.]

At that very moment, Lois was sitting by the window. As soon as she saw his message, tears welled up in her eyes.

Her fingers trembled as she typed, the phone shaking in her hands.

Lois: [I believe in you! I'll wait. No matter how long it takes, I'll wait for you!]

For the next three days, Evander didn't rest for a second.

He spent every waking hour at the research center, running final tests and fine-tuning every detail of the presentation. Nothing could be left to chance.

Walter was right there with him, helping prepare all the supporting materials and even running mock Q&A sessions to anticipate questions Royal Tech's scientists might ask.

The night before the presentation, Evander finally got a full night's sleep.

The following morning, he dressed in a sharp, tailored suit and carefully packed the demonstration equipment for the Decision Matrix Model.

Then, with Walter by his side, he headed to the Royal Tech building.

The building stood tall in the heart of London—a gleaming modern skyscraper. Staff were already waiting at the entrance to receive them.

Chad had arrived early as well. When he saw Evander, he approached quickly.

"Ready? Sir Gary and the other scientists are already in the conference room. King Edward couldn't make it in person, but he's asked me to relay everything to him in real time."

Evander took a deep breath and nodded.

"I'm ready."

Inside the conference room, every seat was filled.

Gary sat at the head of the table, his expression serious. The rest of the scientists wore guarded looks, sizing Evander up with critical eyes.

But Evander showed no trace of nervousness. He walked calmly to the front, connected the equipment, and then turned to face the room.

"Ladies and gentlemen," he began, his tone steady and confident, "today I'll be demonstrating the Decision Matrix Model—a research breakthrough that merges the Kabbalistic Tree of Life with advanced AI algorithms."

He paused briefly, letting the words settle before continuing, "This system allows humanoid robots to make decisions in a way that mirrors human cognition, while also generating emotional feedback. It has the potential to break through the current limitations of Royal Tech's existing frameworks."

With that, the demonstration began.

The data for the humanoid robot was displayed clearly on the screen. From autonomous obstacle avoidance to emotionally intelligent responses, each function drew astonished reactions from the room.

One moment especially stood out.

During a simulated "fire emergency", the robot not only located and rescued a "trapped individual", but also adjusted its approach based on the victim's condition. Its movements were so precise and gentle, it moved like a trained human rescuer.

Gasps and praise filled the room.

"This is a major leap in technology!"

"Remarkable. This young man is something else."

"He's made a groundbreaking contribution to humanoid robotics."

Gary's stern expression gradually softened, replaced by visible surprise and approval.

When the demonstration ended, he was the first to applaud.

"Incredible! Mr. Bexley, your research has far exceeded our expectations."

Evander looked directly at him, speaking with sincerity.

"Sir Gary, this isn't just a personal achievement. It's a result of bridging cultural philosophy and technological innovation. If Royal Tech is open to collaborating with Vanguard Tech, I'm confident we can push humanoid robotics even further and lead global innovation together."

Gary's applause echoed through the conference room. One by one, the other scientists joined in, their expressions now filled with admiration and respect.

The skepticism toward Vanguard Tech, the doubt surrounding Evander—all of it faded, replaced by genuine acknowledgment.

From his seat at the side, Chad smiled, clearly pleased.

He'd trusted his instincts, and Evander had proven him right. The man wasn't just talented. He could turn that talent into real, tangible results.

"Mr. Bexley," Gary said, rising from his seat and walking over, "on behalf of Royal Tech, I officially agree to form a core tech exchange partnership with Vanguard Tech."

He extended a hand, his voice steady with conviction. "Your Decision Matrix Model has resolved our greatest challenge in developing autonomous awareness in humanoid robotics. This fusion of symbolic intelligence and technological innovation is unprecedented."

Evander shook his hand, his chest tight with emotion.

This wasn't just a professional victory. It was the validation he needed to stand beside Lois without compromise.

"Thank you for your recognition," he said genuinely.

"Vanguard Tech will bring our most advanced AI technologies to the table. Together, we'll shape the future. We can finalize the partnership details soon, but I'd like the signing ceremony to happen as quickly as possible."

Gary considered for a moment before nodding.

"How about tomorrow at 2 PM? We'll host it in Royal Tech's grand hall. We'll invite members of the royal family and the media. Everyone should witness this moment."

Chad added, "I'll notify the Royal Office right away and arrange all the formalities. But as per royal protocol, both companies' legal representatives must be present to sign the agreement. Vanguard Tech's legal signatory is..."

"My grandfather, Norman Westbrook," Evander answered immediately.

A flicker of worry crossed his mind.

Norman was all the way in North America. Could he really make it in time for a ceremony less than 24 hours away?

Gary nodded.

"Then please make sure Sir Westbrook is present. This partnership isn't just a corporate milestone. It's a symbol of technological diplomacy. His presence would send a strong message."

"Understood," Evander said, steadying his voice. "I'll contact him right away and make sure he's there on time."

Even though uncertainty lingered, he didn't let it show.

He knew this final step couldn't go wrong. Not when everything was on the line.
