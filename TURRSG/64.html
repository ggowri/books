Chapter 65 Seizing the Moment

Evander set his phone down and leaned back on the couch. The commendation ceremony played on the TV, but he was already over it. Elsa's speech, the reporters' questions, the crowd's cheers—they were just background noise.

His mind was set on activating the Arcane Ring as soon as possible.

"Oh, right, Ciotti's elixir," he suddenly remembered the small black vial upstairs—the Focus Elixir.

Ciotti's grandfather once claimed, deep inside the stone chamber of Mortis Gorge, that a single pill could keep someone going without food for ten days. Evander hadn't bought it at the time. A miracle like that seemed impossible.

But now the ring on his middle finger felt different after what Aileen and Emily had done—warm and alive, with a faint pulse.

Could the elixir really be something special?

Evander got up and headed to the second floor, opening the hidden compartment in his wardrobe. The black bottle lay quietly in its velvet box. He picked it up and pulled out the stopper. A rich, refreshing herbal scent hit him.

He tipped a single pill into his palm. It was no bigger than a coffee bean, smooth and black, with an oddly appealing aroma.

After staring at it for a moment, he popped it into his mouth and swallowed. The pill melted away instantly, leaving no bitterness, just a faint, sweet aftertaste.

Soon, a warm sensation spread from his stomach, flowing through his veins like soothing water. He squinted in comfort, touching the scrapes on his arm. The wounds, once stinging, now felt painless. His skin had grown unusually sensitive, each touch sending clear signals to his brain.

Even more surprising, his body, tired from last night, was now buzzing with energy. His mind felt clear, the earlier fog lifted.

"This elixir really does work!" Evander exclaimed, thrilled. He finally understood Ciotti's grandfather's words. For the elderly, it prolonged life; for the young, it boosted strength and heightened senses.

He quickly poured out another pill, put it back in the bottle, sealed it tight, and returned it to its hiding spot.

Just then, a car engine rumbled downstairs, followed by a door closing. Evander's heart skipped a beat. He rushed to the window, parted the curtains, and looked down.

A white sedan was parked at the villa's entrance. Elsa stepped out in a cream-colored dress, holding a paper bag, clearly here to thank him.

"This is my chance!" Evander's pulse quickened. Elsa's visit was a golden opportunity to grow closer to her. He needed this moment alone to secure the Arcane Ring's third nourishment.

Taking a deep breath, he smoothed his casual clothes to look presentable, then hurried downstairs. As he reached the living room, the doorbell rang.

Evander opened the door, managing to look just the right amount of surprised. "Mayor Burton? What brings you here?"

"Just came to check on you and drop off something," Elsa said, holding up the paper bag with a smile as she stepped inside. "After the ceremony, I stopped by a fruit shop. Got you some fresh fruit—good for your recovery."

"You're too kind, coming all this way," Evander said, taking the bag and leading her to the couch. "Here, sit. I'll make you some coffee."

"No need to bother. I'll just stay a bit," Elsa waved a hand, her eyes scanning the living room. The decor was simple, the floor spotless. The bookshelf, packed with history and philosophy books, matched Evander's polished demeanor.

Evander insisted and headed to the kitchen. He grabbed two coffee cups, milk, and sugar from the fridge. While the coffee was brewing, he slipped the elixir into one cup. Moments later, he returned with the coffee, handing her the one with the elixir.

He smiled, "Mayor Burton, try this. I'm not sure if it's to your taste."

Elsa took the cup, sniffed it, and sipped. The rich coffee aroma, mixed with a faint herbal note, surprised her. "This is great—much better than the instant stuff at the office."

Evander sat across from her, holding his own cup. His heart raced, but he kept his cool, chatting casually. "No work for you today? There must be a lot to handle after the ceremony, right?"

"It's Sunday. No work today." Elsa set her cup down, her eyes bright with relief. "With the Night Owls gone, I can finally relax a little." She paused, looking at Evander sincerely. "I really have to thank you. Without you, the Night Owls might've lingered for years, hurting who knows how many people."

"Just doing what I had to do," Evander smiled, his gaze resting on Elsa's face. He saw her cheeks flush faintly, her eyes softening. The elixir was clearly working.

Elsa felt it too. A warm current flowed through her, loosening her tense muscles. Her senses sharpened. She could feel Evander's gaze on her, making her heart race for reasons she couldn't quite pinpoint.

"Your... wounds—how are they?" Elsa shifted the topic instinctively, only to realize her voice sounded softer than usual.

"Much better, thanks to your ointment," Evander said, touching his neck deliberately to reveal a patch of skin. "They don't hurt anymore."

Elsa's eyes fell on his neck. The bruise had faded, though faint marks lingered. For a moment, she wanted to touch it, to check if it was truly healed. The thought startled her. She quickly looked away and sipped her coffee to hide her fluster.

Evander noted her reaction, quietly pleased. "By the way, Mayor Burton," he said, his tone curious. "You're always so busy. Do you ever get time to unwind? Any hobbies?"

Elsa paused, surprised by the question. She thought for a moment, then smiled. "Sometimes I do yoga or walk by the beach, but work usually takes over."

"Yoga's perfect for relaxing. It suits you," Evander nodded, his eyes warm with appreciation. "I practice some karate, but I dabble in yoga too. You know, I've got this great deck by the pool upstairs—it'd be perfect for practice. Maybe you could show me some moves?"
