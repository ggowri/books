Chapter 22 A Storm Unleashed

The helicopter sliced through the clouds. Evander stole a glance at the instrument panel.

The navigation said they were less than an hour out from Vieques Island.

Outside, the sky had been a brilliant cobalt, shimmering with promise. Now, it churned with menace, dark clouds roiling up from the horizon like a tidal wave of ink.

"Looks like rain's coming," Kimberly murmured, pressing her face against the window.

Her white chiffon blouse fluttered in the draft, clinging to her frame and tracing the gentle curves of her body.

The shock of hearing "300 million dollars" still buzzed in her veins. She stole a glance at Evander, her eyes full of curiosity.

Now she felt the man was a puzzle, layered with secrets she couldn't quite unravel.

Evander's fingers tapped lightly on the control stick, a faint and almost imperceptible curl playing at his lips.

"Out here the weather can turn on you fast," he said, his voice low and measured. "We might hit a thunderstorm."

He flicked on the weather radar, and a glaring red lightning bolt flashed across the screen, sharp and foreboding.

"Should we head back?" he asked.

"No!"

Kimberly blurted it out before she could think, her mind flashing to her father's hopeful eyes.

She bit her lip and said, "We're almost there. This chopper can handle a storm, can’t it?"

Evander's brow furrowed, his tone grave.

"Hard to say. The Bell 825's tough, but flying through a thunderstorm's a gamble."

He paused, as if wrestling with a heavy decision, then added, "But if you're set on going, I'll do my damnedest to keep us safe."

Kimberly, too caught up in her own resolve, missed the calculating glint in his eyes.

"Sorry," she stammered, guilt creeping in for pushing him. "I didn't mean to..."

Before she could finish, the cabin lurched violently.

A blinding bolt of lightning slashed through the clouds, followed by an ear-splitting crack of thunder. Sheets of rain pounded the windows, blurring the world outside into a chaotic smear.

"Hold on!" Evander shouted, yanking the control stick upward.

The helicopter pitched and swayed like a leaf caught in a hurricane. Kimberly screamed, clutching the armrest, her stomach churning.

"What's happening?!" she cried.

"Engine's acting up!"

Evander's voice was tight, his hands wrestling with the controls as they shuddered.

"Lightning might've fried the circuits. I've got to find a place to land!"

Kimberly's face drained of color, her eyes squeezed shut.

The howl of the wind, the hammering rain, and Evander's urgent shouts surrounded her—though his urgency was nothing but an act.

After what felt like an eternity, the chopper slammed into the ground with a bone-jarring thud. The impact threw them both forward, their bodies straining against the seatbelts.

Kimberly's vision went black for a moment, and she nearly passed out.

"You okay?" Evander’s voice was rough with breath, his hand fumbling at the buckle before reaching to steady her shoulder.

Kimberly sat frozen for a beat, then gave a shaky shake of her head. "Where… where are we?"

"No clue." Evander peered out the window.

Rain lashed down in sheets, but through the haze, he could make out a stretch of coconut palms and a sandy shore. "Looks like some deserted island."

"A deserted island?"

Kimberly's voice spiked with panic. "You mean we’re stranded?"

"We should get out," Evander replied, popping the cabin door.

Cold rain poured in, laced with a gust of salty wind.

"The storm’s still on us," he warned. "Stay here and we’ll be sitting ducks for lightning."

Kimberly hesitated, then followed him out.

The moment she stepped from the chopper, the downpour soaked her to the bone.

Her chiffon blouse turned sheer, clinging to her skin and revealing the faint outline of her nude bra. Her blue shorts, drenched and heavy, hugged her thighs, accentuating their taut lines.

Evander's gaze flicked over her, his throat bobbing at the sight in front of him.

His own black T-shirt was plastered to his chest, the rain tracing the hard lines of his muscles, his pecs faintly visible beneath the soaked fabric.

"This way—looks like there’s a cave." Evander said, pointing toward a shadowy rock formation half-hidden by the palm grove.

Kimberly lowered her head and walked behind him, trying not to look at his body.

However, from the corner of her eye, Kimberly caught a glimpse of his bulging chest and the toned lines of his waist. Her heart inexplicably quickened.

She yanked her gaze away, cursing herself.

Pull it together, Kimberly. Now’s not the time for this crap.

He's your best friend's husband, for God’s sake!

They trudged through the wet sand and into the coconut grove.

The dense leaves offered some relief from the rain, but Kimberly's clothes were already soaked and clung to her body.

Rushing to keep up, she tripped over a tree root and cried out, stumbling forward.

"Careful!"

Evander's hand shot out, catching her by the waist.

His palm pressed against her, the warmth of her skin seeping through the drenched fabric. His fingers tightened, just for a moment, as if reluctant to let go.

Kimberly jerked away, her cheeks flaming despite the chill.

"Thanks," she muttered, her voice barely audible.

Evander nodded toward the cave ahead. "We're here."

It was a shallow cave, its entrance obscured by thick vines.

As Evander pushed the foliage aside, a deafening flurry erupted—hundreds of seabirds exploded from the cave, their wings whipping up a gust that nearly knocked them over.

Kimberly cried out, spinning and flinging herself into his arms.

Evander staggered back, his hands instinctively wrapping around her.

In the chaos, his palm landed squarely on her full breast.

The soft warmth of her body pressed against his fingers through the sodden chiffon.

He knew he should have let go, but before he could stop himself, he gave the faintest squeeze.

"What the hell?!"

Kimberly shoved him away, her face a storm of embarrassment and anger.

Without thinking, she raised her hand and slapped him.

The crisp sound echoed clearly in the downpour.

Evander's cheek flared red, his eyes narrowing with a mix of surprise and something darker.

Kimberly regretted it instantly, but pride held her firm.

She glared at him, her eyes stinging with unshed tears.

"You grabbed me! You’re such a creep!"

Before Evander could respond, Kimberly spun on her heel and bolted into the rain, the cold water mingling with the tears she refused to acknowledge.

Evander stood frozen, watching her disappear into the palm grove, a cold smile playing on his lips.

He'd meant to unsettle her, and he'd succeeded. But that slap... It stirred something restless in him, an irritation he couldn't quite name.

Evander's gaze dropped to his hand. He could almost still feel her warmth lingering there.

His throat tightened, and he took off after her.

"Ms. Nelson! Come back! The rain's too heavy!" he called, his voice cutting through the downpour.

Kimberly ignored him, stumbling through the slick sand, her mind a tangle of fury and shame.

She despised Evander's brazenness, but she hated the way her heart had fluttered even more.

For a brief, dizzying moment, she felt strangely disoriented in his arms.

"Wait!"

Evander caught up with her and grabbed her wrist.

Her skin was icy, her wrist so slender it felt like it could snap with a single pinch.
