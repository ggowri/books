Chapter 72 Preparing to Leave

The next morning, a crisp Monday, Evander slipped out of the villa before dawn, leaving Nadine, Ruth, and Delilah still nestled in their dreams.

His SUV hummed softly as it rolled out of the community.

Evander dialed Emily's number.

They'd made plans the day before—he'd pick her up for school and say his goodbyes.

The phone rang barely twice before her drowsy, lazy voice spilled through the speaker.

"Mr. Bexley! You're here already? Gimme a sec, I'm coming down!"

Twenty minutes later, his rugged SUV pulled up in front of Aileen's castle.

Emily bounded out, her light blue school uniform skirt swishing, a backpack slung over her shoulders. She was a burst of energy, like a sparrow darting through a spring morning. She yanked open the passenger door and slid in with a grin.

"Mr. Bexley, you're so early today!" she chirped, buckling up.

Her eyes quickly landed on the suitcase in the back seat, and her brow furrowed with curiosity.

"Are you going on a trip or something?"

"I'm going to London," Evander replied, his tone measured. "I'll be gone about a month."

He eased the car into gear, trying to keep his voice light. "That's why I wanted to drive you to school today—to say a proper goodbye."

"London? A whole month?"

Emily's bright smile vanished, her lips pulling into a pout. She grabbed his arm, her voice turning playfully petulant.

"No way! Leave the car here. I'm skipping school, and you're taking me with you!

"You have to finish what you started the last time."

Evander chuckled, shaking his head. He reached over, ruffling her hair gently. "Look, I never use protection. What if you get pregnant? Your whole college plans would be ruined. I can't do that to you."

"I don't care!"

Emily huffed, wrapping her arms around his bicep, her cheek pressing against his sleeve. Her voice softened, tinged with a sulky edge.

"But I haven't even experienced real sex yet. You can't leave without giving me that!"

Her eyes glistened, a mix of defiance and vulnerability, and Evander felt a tug at his heart.

He slowed the car, glancing at her with a patient, steady gaze.

"Emily, come on. Be good." His voice was warm but firm.

"When I'm back from London, I'll make time for you, I promise.

"But right now, you're a student. School comes first. I won't let this mess up your future."

She pouted, her silence stretching for a long moment before she gave a reluctant nod.

"Fine. But the second you're back, you're taking me out. No excuses!"

"Deal," Evander said with a grin, easing his foot back on the gas.

The SUV picked up speed, gliding toward the University of Arizona.

Forty minutes later, they pulled up to the campus gates.

Emily unbuckled her seatbelt but didn't move to get out. Instead, she leaned over and planted a quick, soft kiss on his cheek. "You better think of me in London," she said, her tone half-teasing, half-serious. "And don't you dare go messing around with other girls!"

"Only got eyes for you," Evander teased back, his smile easy as he watched her hop out.

She lingered, glancing back at him every few steps until she disappeared into the academic building.

Only then did he pull away, the hum of the engine filling the quiet.

*****

At noon, Evander parked outside the sleek office building of LUXERA magazine and shot Aileen a quick message: [You busy? I'm at the bistro downstairs. Have time for a goodbye lunch?]

Moments later, Aileen emerged from the glass doors, her cream-colored suit pristine, her hair swept into an elegant low bun.

Her makeup was flawless, but faint shadows under her eyes betrayed her exhaustion.

"Why this sudden goodbye?"

Aileen asked, settling into her seat across from Evander. She accepted the menu from the server, her eyes questioning.

"I'm headed to London for a month," Evander explained, signaling the waiter to order her favorite—filet mignon—and a plate of pasta for himself.

"Business, technical collaboration stuff. I didn't want to leave without letting you know. Didn't want you to worry."

"A whole month?" Aileen's hand froze mid-air, her fork hovering above the table. A flicker of disappointment crossed her face.

She'd just shaken off the weight of Jack's drama, hoping for more time with Evander, and now this. Her chest tightened, a hollow ache settling in.

"Is it... complicated? Any chance you'll be back sooner?"

"Can't say for sure," he said. "Depends on how things progress."

Evander caught sight of her eyes rimming with red and felt a pang of regret.

Aileen looked up, her voice soft but heavy with unspoken longing.

"I finally cut things off with Jack for good. I thought maybe you and I could..."

She trailed off, the words too raw to finish.

Evander knew exactly what she meant, but deliberately changed the subject.

"I might need to get a UK phone number while I'm there. I'll send you the new one when I do. Don't worry if you can't reach me right away—I'll make sure to check in whenever I can."

Aileen nodded, her knife slicing through the steak with mechanical precision, though she barely touched her food.

Her eyes flicked to him, brimming with quiet hope.

"You won't... forget about me over there, will you?"

"Never," Evander said, his voice steady and sincere.

"When I'm back, we'll grab dinner and catch up. Promise."

Lunch passed in heavy silence.

Aileen stole glances at Evander, her eyes full of unspoken words, wanting him to stay but not knowing how to ask.

When it was time to go, she lingered outside the entrance, suddenly stepping forward to wrap her arms around him.

Her voice was a whisper against his ear. "Stay safe. Come back soon."

He gave her back a reassuring pat, watching as she disappeared into the building before turning to leave.

*****

As dusk painted the sky in deep purples, Evander pulled up to Kimberly's sprawling estate.

She was already waiting at the gate, her pale pink dress catching the evening light. The moment she saw his car, she hurried over, slipping into the passenger seat with a worried frown.

"You're really going to London?" she asked, her voice small.

"Yeah, I leave Thursday," Evander said, reaching for her hand. He knew she was worried for him.

"That pregnancy you were worried about? We'll sort it out when I'm back.

"I'll probably switch to a local number over there, but I'll get in touch as soon as I can. Don't worry if you can't reach me right away."

Kimberly nodded, her expression soft but trusting. She leaned her head against his shoulder, her voice barely above a murmur.

"Just... be careful, okay?"

They dined at a Michelin-starred restaurant.

After that, Kimberly tugged Evander back to her estate, the grand house quiet and empty under the night sky.

She led him to her bedroom.

The room was a soft haven—pink curtains swaying gently, white lace draped over the bed, and her sketches scattered across a desk.

She turned to him, wrapping her arms around his waist from behind, her cheek pressed against his back.

"I don't want you to go," she whispered, her voice catching.

Evander turned, pulling her into his arms, his lips finding hers in a slow, tender kiss.

Kimberly melted into him, her hands clutching his shirt as the kiss deepened, a hungry edge to it.

She slipped off her dress, the fabric pooling at her feet. Her body glowed in the soft light, curves illuminated with a quiet allure.

She took his hand, guiding it to her chest, her eyes shimmering with need and trust.

"One more time," she pleaded softly. "Please."

Evander didn't pull away.

He knew this was their last moment together before he left, a fleeting chance to hold her close.

His clothes joined hers on the floor, and he lifted her gently onto the bed, its softness cradling them both.

This time, his touch was gentle and deliberate, every movement laced with care.

It was a stark contrast to the wild, untamed passion they'd shared on that distant island.

Kimberly bit her lip, stifling her gasps, her body trembling beneath him.

In that quiet room, they both ached for the reckless freedom of the wilderness, where no walls held them back.

The safety of home was a comfort, but their hearts yearned for the untamed air of the open world.
