Chapter 10 Coach Ciotti

Norman clapped his hands three times.

Immediately, two servants stepped forward carrying silver trays.

The first tray held a gold-embossed property deed and a set of keys. The deed bore the name "Azure Cove Residence" in elegant script—the most expensive property in the city, worth three hundred million dollars.

"Your first reward," Norman announced. "Azure Cove Residence, plus a Gulfstream G700 helicopter parked on the villa's rooftop. It's yours to use whenever you need."

The second servant lifted a velvet cloth, revealing a crystal vial nestled in the tray.

"Vitality Serum—upgraded formula." Norman's tone carried deeper meaning. "It'll double your physical capabilities instantly."

Evander didn't spare the vial a glance. "Grandfather, what's the second test?"

Norman's smile slowly faded. He rose and approached Evander, his gaze sharp as a blade.

"Listen carefully. Your second test is this—I want you to get Kimberly pregnant. Within two weeks."

"Get Kimberly pregnant?"

Evander's voice was hoarse with disbelief. He stared at Norman, pupils dilating with shock.

Kimberly—Nadine's closest friend, daughter of investment mogul Oliver Nelson, the proud woman who graced every gala in champagne-colored gowns.

Get her pregnant?

This wasn't just an insult to the Nelson family—it was throwing him into the fire.

"Grandfather, this is insane." Evander's fists clenched until his knuckles went white. "Kimberly is Nadine's best friend. I—"

"I'm not asking for your opinion." Norman cut him off, his cane striking the floor with a resounding thud. "You simply execute."

Evander's throat worked as he swallowed hard. He wanted to refuse, to say this violated every moral boundary, but the words died under Norman's piercing stare.

He knew his grandfather's temperament—once decided, never changed.

"Must I... do this myself?" Evander's voice dropped to barely a whisper, a last desperate struggle.

Norman nodded without hesitation. "Yes."

"Why?" Evander finally raised his voice.

Norman's expression turned cold, calculating.

"Getting Kimberly pregnant serves three purposes: First, it destroys the friendship between Nadine and Kimberly, breaking the alliance between their families. Second, it avenges the humiliation she inflicted on you. Third, it allows us to absorb the Nelson family assets."

Evander listened, his expression shifting to grudging admiration. He'd never realized how strategically his grandfather's mind worked.

"But making a woman pregnant in two weeks might be—" Doubt crept into his voice.

"You can, and you will!" Norman's tone brooked no argument.

Evander could only nod.

Norman's mouth curved into a slight smile. "Return to Azure Cove first. I have another surprise for you."

...

After bidding his grandfather farewell, Evander drove toward Azure Cove Residence.

As he entered, he saw a young woman in a white karate uniform walking toward him.

She had striking blonde hair and sun-kissed skin. Though tall and graceful, her black belt revealed she was no ordinary beauty. She was clearly a high-ranking martial artist.

"Coach Ciotti Karen? It's really you."

"Evander, long time no see. Six years, hasn't it been?"

Evander smiled bitterly. He'd given up so much to marry Nadine.

They embraced warmly, but Ciotti suddenly aimed a surprise kick at Evander's legs. Evander blocked it reflexively, absorbing the impact.

"Impressive. You haven't lost your touch—still my best student."

Evander's lip curled. "Grandfather didn't send you just to see if I can still fight, did he?"

"Of course not." Ciotti produced two VIP passes for the AI Summit. "Tomorrow's AI conference—I'm your bodyguard."

...

The next morning, the AI Summit venue was draped in crimson carpet, media cameras flashing like stars.

Iris stood at the entrance checking her watch repeatedly, her cream-colored suit making her look both sharp and elegant. She clutched two printed speech drafts in her hands.

Inside, the host's enthusiastic voice echoed through the hall.

Denny was on stage presenting his "latest breakthrough"—an AI model that claimed to predict stock market trends.

Thunderous applause erupted from the audience.

But Iris frowned. The model's core algorithm bore striking similarities to a paper Summit Group had published last year.

In the parking garage, Evander held the door for Ciotti.

She'd traded her training clothes for a silver slip dress, her blonde hair elegantly pinned up.

"Never thought you owned formal wear like this," Evander teased, adjusting his charcoal suit.

"Events like this require a certain level of formality." Ciotti smiled, smoothing her dress. Suddenly, her expression sharpened.

"Watch out."

Before the words fully left her lips, three men in black jackets emerged from behind concrete pillars, blocking their path.

The leader, sporting a prominent scar across his face, casually swung a collapsible baton while his mouth twisted into a malicious grin.

"Mr. Bexley, we need to have a word."
