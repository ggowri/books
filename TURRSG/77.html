Chapter 78 A Jaw-Dropping Magic Trick

Lois waved toward her boyfriend, Felix Gosse, her voice tinged with a hint of urgency.

"Felix, come here for a sec. I want you to meet someone."

Felix had been joking with a few companions while rolling chips between his fingers. At the sound of Lois' voice, his smile vanished instantly, replaced by a look of irritation.

He shoved the chips at a friend and sauntered over, his swagger dripping with arrogance.

He sized Evander up, his gaze lingering on the tailored suit with a sneer.

Though he could tell Evander's suit was custom, the brand eluded him, so he mentally pegged Evander as some nobody chasing aristocratic clout.

"This is Mr. Evander Bexley. We had a great chat about Jane Eyre this morning," Lois said, her smile warm as she tried to lighten the mood.

"Evander, this is my boyfriend, Felix Gosse."

Evander extended his hand politely, his manner impeccably courteous.

Felix ignored the gesture, his eyes cold as he drawled, "Bexley, isn't it?"

"Tell me," he continued, voice dripping with disdain, "which noble family claims you? How many estates do you own in London or New York?"

His tone oozed superiority, as if lineage were the only measure of a man's worth.

Evander's expression remained unruffled, his voice steady.

"Sorry, I'm just a regular guy. Currently a senior technical advisor at Vanguard Tech."

"A commoner?" Felix barked a laugh, loud enough to turn heads. "Ha! That's rich!"

He pointed at Lois, his voice thick with mockery.

"You really don't know who you're talking to, do you? She's King Edward's eldest daughter—a princess with both beauty and brilliance. And you? You think someone like you deserves to even breathe the same air as her?"

"Felix, that's enough!" Lois snapped, her brow furrowing with irritation.

She hadn't expected him to be so crass.

Evander's mind stirred with a quiet ripple of surprise.

Lois is a princess of the royal family?

It explained her refined grace, her eloquent speech, and the way she connected so deeply with Jane Eyre's quest for equality.

He tucked away his shock, his gaze sharpening as he turned the tables on Felix.

"And you, Mr. Gosse? What makes you qualified to be by Princess Lois' side?"

Felix's face darkened, his tone growing brasher.

"Me? My father's a banker with twelve banks across Europe. Of course I'm worthy."

"Oh, really?" Evander pressed, his voice calm but cutting.

"Those twelve banks, that wealth—how much of it was earned by you? You're just riding his coattails. What gives you the right to look down on anyone?"

"You're asking for it!" Felix snarled, his face flushing with rage.

He thrust a hand toward his friends. "Daniel! Bring me the cash I won earlier!"

A man in a gray suit hurried over with a black briefcase and handed it to Felix.

Felix snatched a thick stack of pounds and flung them at Evander's face.

"Here's half a million pounds. Take it and get lost, or you'll regret crossing me!"

Evander let out a cold chuckle, his eyes flicking to the empty space before him.

With a casual wave of his hand, something astonishing happened.

Stacks of pounds materialized in midair, cascading down like a waterfall, piling into a towering mound of cash. Gasps erupted around them.

"Holy—look at all that money!"

"That's got to be a billion pounds! Where did it come from?"

"Is this some kind of magic trick? Cash just appearing out of nowhere?"

The murmur of the crowd erupted into a roar. Guests abandoned their conversations, waiters halted in mid-step, and every eye fixed wide on the spectacle.

Lois clapped a hand over her mouth, her eyes wide as saucers.

She stepped closer, carefully plucked a bundle from the heap, and ran her fingertips across the crisp notes. Bringing it to her nose, she inhaled deeply.

The scent was unmistakable. No chemical ink, no counterfeit odor—only the fresh aroma of genuine currency.

She looked up at Evander, her voice trembling with awe.

"Mr. Bexley, these... these are real pounds? How did you do this?"

Felix stood frozen, his bravado crumbling.

He stumbled to the pile, grabbing a stack and inspecting it frantically, holding it to the light to check the watermark. His expression morphed from arrogance to shock, then to sheer disbelief.

"No way... This can't be real. Who the hell are you?"

Evander's smile was icy, his voice steady but commanding.

"Who I am doesn't matter.

"What matters is this is just pocket change to me.

"Mr. Gosse, here's a billion pounds. Take it and walk away from Princess Lois."

The crowd gasped, whispers rippling through the room.

"Did he just call a billion pounds pocket change?"

Even royalty rarely had such liquid wealth at their fingertips, and here was this supposed commoner, wielding it like it was nothing.

Lois' cheeks flushed, her heart swelling with unexpected warmth.

She knew Felix was drawn to her royal status, and Evander—a man she'd only just met—was willing to toss a billion pounds to keep Felix at bay.

The gesture stirred something deep within her.

Her gaze softened, the curiosity from earlier giving way to genuine affection and trust.

Felix, however, hurled the stack of pounds back onto the pile, his face contorted with fury.

"You think a billion pounds can make me leave Lois? In your dream!

"My father's worth a trillion pounds, and it'll all be mine one day!

"You're just some con artist pulling tricks. Who knows where this dirty money came from?

"Get your cash out of here, or I'm calling security!"

Evander's lips curved into a sly grin.

"Fine. I'll go."

With another wave of his hand, the mountain of cash vanished as if it had never existed.

In its place, a sleek silver Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost materialized on the spot.

Its elegant curves gleamed under the lights, the golden Spirit of Ecstasy hood ornament catching the light with a brilliant shimmer.

The crowd erupted again.

"That's a Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost—the world's most expensive car!"

"Is he really just a magician? This is unreal!"

Gasps turned into cries of astonishment.

Lois' hands flew to her mouth, her eyes wide with disbelief. She felt like she was dreaming.

First, the cash appeared and vanished, and now a Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost? It was beyond comprehension.

Evander strolled to the car and opened the driver's door.

He turned to Lois, extending a hand, his voice warm and magnetic.

"Princess Lois, the air's a bit heavy in here. Shall we get some fresh air on deck?"

Lois caught his gaze, then glanced at Felix's seething expression beside her.

The stark difference between the two men made her choice clear.

She followed her heart.

Nodding, she placed her hand in his, her voice soft but certain.

"Yes, Mr. Bexley, I'd love to."

She slid into the passenger seat, a radiant smile lighting her face.

Evander started the engine, its refined purr cutting through the crowd's murmurs as he drove past them, gliding smoothly toward the open deck.
