Chapter 88 Goddess of Death

Felix blinked awake in the lab as the sun started to dip below the horizon. Fred was still out cold on the floor, and the operating table was completely empty.

The moment Felix saw that, his anger for Evander reached a boiling point.

That nobody, Evander, had wrecked his engagement, taken his money, and totally ruined his shot at superpowers.

And now, the bastard had somehow managed to escape right under SPR's noses. It was the final slap in the face.

"Get to the Imperial Hotel. Now!" Felix snapped, storming out of the lab and barking orders at his driver. His voice was sharp, barely holding back the fury bubbling inside.

He had two heavily armed special forces guys with him. His father had pulled some strings to get them from a private security firm.

They were packing assault rifles, their expressions cold and sharp—like they were just waiting for someone to make a wrong move. The kind of look that made everyone else feel uncomfortable.

Felix was sure Evander would show up at the Imperial Hotel, where Anya had booked their room. All he had to do was wait, and he'd have him.

The black sedan rolled up to the hotel entrance, and the doorman took one look at the armed soldiers getting out and practically bolted for cover.

Felix walked into the lobby with his two men flanking him, heading straight for the front desk. His voice was icy as he asked, "Has a Mr. Evander Bexley checked in? Mixed race, wearing a custom black suit?"

The desk clerk quickly typed into the system, then looked up, shaking his head. "Sorry, Mr. Gosse. No record of a Mr. Evander Bexley. He hasn't checked in."

Felix's eyes darkened. "What? That can't be right! His friend made the reservation—Anya."

The clerk nervously shuffled through his papers. "We tried calling the person who booked the room, but her phone goes straight to voicemail..."

The clerk looked up, clearly uncomfortable. "I'm sorry, sir. But we've got no record of him being here."

Felix slammed his hand down on the marble counter, the sound echoing across the lobby. "That's impossible! You're telling me Evander didn't even check in?!"

"No, sir. I'm really sorry," the clerk stammered, sweat beading on his forehead. "I can try again, if you want?"

Felix's jaw tightened. Evander hadn't come to the Imperial Hotel at all. The sneaky bastard had figured out his plan.

Taking a slow breath, Felix forced himself to calm down. "He's hiding somewhere... but he can't stay hidden forever. There's nowhere left for him to run."

Felix turned on his heel and headed back to the car, his fingers tapping an impatient rhythm on his knee. His mind raced.

Evander doesn't know anyone else in London, aside from Lois and the Stuart sisters. Where the hell could he have gone?

Lois lived at Royal One Estate, with tight security that Felix couldn't get through. No way would he be able to grab anyone there.

Anya and Andrea were nowhere to be found either.

Felix clenched his teeth. Was he really going to let Evander slip through his fingers like this?

Then, out of nowhere, a name hit him—Kelly.

His aunt Kelly had been SPR's most famous psychic back in the day. She could talk to the dead, and people even called her "the voice of Hela, the Nordic goddess of death".

Five years ago, she'd pushed her abilities too far and had a complete mental breakdown.

They locked her up in Ragnarok Psychiatric Hospital, but her powers never went away. If anything, they got stronger and more twisted.

Maybe Kelly was the only one who could help him take down Evander.

"Change of plans. We're going to Ragnarok Psychiatric Hospital." Felix's voice was sharp with determination, and something dangerous flickered in his eyes.

He knew this was his last shot. Even if it meant calling on the dead for help, he was going to make Evander pay.

Ragnarok Psychiatric Hospital sat at the base of a small mountain just outside London.

From a distance, the gray building looked like some massive beast crouching in the shadows, giving off seriously creepy vibes in the evening light.

The walls were high and topped with razor wire. When the security guards at the gate saw Felix's car coming, they immediately opened up. They'd obviously been expecting him.

They'd clearly been expecting him.

The black sedan pulled up in front of the psychiatric hospital's main building. Felix stepped out with two armed guards and gave them their orders.

"I want you stationed outside her room. Nobody gets in—not doctors, not nurses, nobody. Don't let anyone near this place until I come back out."

"Yes, Mr. Gosse!"

Both men snapped to attention and took their positions by the door, gripping their assault rifles as they scanned the hallway.

Felix took a deep breath and pushed open the door to Kelly's private room.

The room was dim, lit only by a single yellowed lamp. The air reeked of disinfectant mixed with something else... something that smelled like decay.

A woman sat in a rocking chair by the window, her back to the door. Her long white hair hung loose and tangled over her shoulders, covering most of her face. She didn't look 43. She looked ancient.

"Aunt Kelly, it's me, Felix. I came to see you." His voice dropped automatically, carrying a hint of fear he couldn't quite hide.

The rocking chair slowly came to a stop. The woman turned around.

Her face was carved with deep wrinkles, but her eyes still blazed bright, though there was something hollow about them, like they could see straight through to people's souls.

She stared at Felix, her gaze sharp enough to make his skin crawl, then her mouth twisted into a strange smile. "Felix, you're in trouble, and now you're here asking for my help, huh?"

Felix froze for a second, then quickly stepped forward and nodded. "Aunt Kelly, you're amazing! How did you know?"

Kelly didn't answer. Instead, she raised one gnarled hand and pointed to his pocket. "Just give me a picture. I need to see who it is."

Felix quickly pulled out a photo from his jacket, a shot he'd secretly taken of Evander's face earlier.

In the picture, Evander was standing on the cruise ship's deck, a slight smile on his lips, his eyes calm and confident.

Felix shoved the photo toward Kelly, his voice thick with hatred.

"This is him, Aunt Kelly. His name's Evander Bexley, and he's just some nobody.

"He stole my fiancée, took my money, ruined everything I've worked for, and almost got me killed! I want him gone. I want him out of my life before tomorrow morning!"

Kelly took the photo and stared hard at Evander's face. Her hollow eyes slowly started to glow with a hint of red.

A terrifying smile spread across her face, revealing yellowed teeth that made her look absolutely savage.

"Someone wants to hurt my nephew?

"Evander, you're asking for a death wish..." She ran her fingers over Evander's face in the photo, her touch as cold as ice. "The gates of Helheim haven't welcomed such an... interesting soul in quite some time.

"Felix, you should head back now. I need to speak with Her Highness Hela directly. I'm going to ask her to personally drag this arrogant little fool down to the realm of the dead, where he'll stay forever."

"Helheim?" Felix's eyes lit up immediately.

Of course he knew that name—the realm of the dead in Norse mythology, ruled by Hela, the goddess who was half beautiful, half corpse.

Once Hela marked someone's soul, they could never return to the world of the living.

"Aunt Kelly, you can actually contact Her Highness Hela?"

"I'm her chosen representative. She hears every word I speak." Kelly's voice dropped to something dark and otherworldly, like it was echoing up from the depths of hell itself.

"Go now. Don't interrupt my communion with Her Highness. By tomorrow morning, you'll hear news of Evander's death," Kelly told Felix before closing her eyes.
