Chapter 74 A Romantic Date With Iris

The last traces of the sunset melted into the horizon, leaving only a faint orange glow in the sky.

At seven-thirty, the warm amber lights of Cloudscape Dining, perched atop a suburban hill, began to flicker on, casting a cozy glow.

Evander had arrived early, standing by the railing of the open-air garden, gazing out at the twinkling city lights below. His heart thrummed with anticipation.

He'd come three hours ahead, not just to reserve the entire rooftop restaurant but to pour his heart into every detail of the evening.

The long table was draped in rich crimson velvet, flanked by white roses and lavender flown in from the Netherlands. Every few feet, slender white candles flickered in the breeze, their flames dancing, making the flowers glow even more vividly.

Nearby, a violinist tuned her strings, the delicate notes floating intermittently, weaving an elegant charm into the romantic atmosphere.

Evander glanced at his watch. Thirty minutes until eight.

He strolled to the table, double-checking every detail.

The 1982 Lafite was decanted, breathing perfectly; the steaks waited in a warming cart; the fruit platter was arranged with artistic precision. Everything was ready.

*****

At eight on the dot, a sleek white Porsche glided into the restaurant's parking lot.

Evander hurried to the entrance to greet her.

Iris stepped out, radiant in a champagne-colored evening gown, its hem sparkling with tiny diamonds that caught the light.

Her hair was pulled back in an elegant low bun, and the emerald necklace Evander had given her sparkled at her throat, giving her a graceful, classic look.

"Iris." Evander flashed a warm smile, reaching out to take her clutch.

Iris looked at him, then at the rooftop garden before her, surprise flickering in her eyes.

The entire restaurant was empty but for them.

Seeing the candles and flowers, she knew instantly that this was no ordinary dinner.

It was a carefully orchestrated date.

She hesitated for a moment before handing him her clutch, her voice tinged with a shy softness.

"You... you reserved the whole place? This must've cost a fortune."

"Only the best for you," Evander said with a grin, guiding her to the table.

"Come on, sit. Let's see if my dinner lives up to your standards."

Iris settled at one end of the table, her gaze settling on Evander across from her.

He looked dashing in a tailored black suit, a diamond cufflink glinting at his collar.

The candlelight softened the sharp edges of his features, casting a gentle warmth over his face.

She picked up her knife and fork but paused, her eyes tracing the table's elegant setup. A ripple of sweetness stirred in her chest.

The violinist began to play Salut d'Amour, its tender melody filling the rooftop garden.

Evander poured her a glass of wine. The dark ruby liquid swirled in the crystal glass, leaving fine traces along the rim.

"Iris, try this. A vintage I picked just for you."

Iris lifted the glass, taking a slow sip.

The wine's rich, fruity depth bloomed on her tongue, its finish lingering like a warm memory.

She looked up at him, her eyes shimmering with gratitude.

"Thank you, Evander. This is the most thoughtful dinner I've ever had."

"Glad you think so," he replied with a relaxed smile, cutting into his steak.

They ate and talked, their conversation flowing effortlessly from work to life, the atmosphere light and intimate.

The candles flickered, the violin sang, and every so often, a breeze carried the scent of flowers, making the moment feel almost enchanted.

Halfway through the meal, Evander raised a hand, signaling a nearby waiter.

The waiter nodded and slipped into the control room.

A sharp pop pierced the air, and the first firework burst across the night sky.

Golden sparks showered over the garden.

More fireworks shot into the sky, one after another.

Red, blue, purple, and pink bursts painted the dark canvas above, their brilliant patterns lighting up the night like day.

Iris looked up, her eyes sparkling brighter than the display above. A radiant smile spread across her face.

Evander had spent 5 million dollars on this custom show, each burst designed with care, just for her.

Her lips curved into a radiant smile, her eyes glittering like starlight.

It was her first time seeing such a romantic spectacle, and the thought that it was all for her made her heart race.

"Like it?" Evander asked, stepping to her side, his voice soft.

Iris nodded, her eyes glistening, her voice catching slightly.

"I love it. So much."

She turned to him, her emotions spilling over, and reached out. "Dance with me?"

"Absolutely," he said, taking her hand and leading her to the open space at the garden's center.

The violinist shifted to a slower, more tender waltz.

Evander's arm circled her waist, and Iris rested her hand on his shoulder.

They swayed gently to the music.

Iris leaned into him, her body brushing against his, feeling the steady rhythm of his heartbeat, the faint scent of his cologne mingling with the flowers around them.

The moment felt like a warm embrace, safe and serene.

She closed her eyes, a soft smile playing on her lips, savoring every second of this bliss.

To Evander, Iris was the woman he cherished most.

His feelings for her had taken root back in college, and now, slowly, he was becoming her anchor.

His love for her was less about possession and more about adoration, a desire to protect and cherish her.

He held her gently, his touch tender, almost cautious, as if afraid to disrupt the delicate magic of the night.

They danced through one song, then another, lost in the music's graceful rhythm.

The romantic evening seemed to stretch on forever.

Iris relaxed fully, her cheek resting against his chest. She whispered, her voice barely audible, "Evander, will you kiss me?"

His body tensed for a moment.

He looked down at her flushed cheeks, her eyes brimming with quiet longing, and leaned in, pressing a soft, fleeting kiss to her lips.

The kiss was light, like a feather's touch, filled with a careful reverence.

Iris leaned in, wanting more, but Evander gently pulled back.

He whispered against her ear, his voice low and restrained, "Iris, if we keep going, I might lose myself and ruin this perfect night.

"You're the woman I love most. I want to give you joy, not take it from you."

Iris' heart swelled, a tingling warmth spreading through her.

Looking into his sincere eyes, she felt something deeper than desire.

She was moved by his restraint, his respect for her.

To her, his love was purer and more intoxicating than any passion.

In that moment, she felt like the luckiest woman in the world.

They danced a little longer before returning to the table, hand in hand.

Evander refilled their wine glasses. They raised their glasses and clinked them together, the sound clear and delicate.

"Remember that first time you helped me with my studies in college?" Iris said suddenly, her eyes soft with nostalgia.

"You were so nervous sitting across from me, you could barely hold your pen. You even got ink all over my notebook."

Evander chuckled, the memory sparking a grin.

"How could I forget? I was head over heels for you, stumbling over my words every time you looked at me. And you didn't even tease me—you just patiently walked me through the problems."

"I thought you were kind of adorable back then," Iris said, shaking her head with a fond smile.

"You were so quiet, but so earnest. I knew you were someone I could count on."

They fell into reminiscing about their college days.

Every detail came flooding back—their first meeting, the club activities they joined, forming the campus AI research team, and the bittersweet goodbye at graduation. Each moment was etched in their memories.

Time slipped away, and soon it was midnight.

"It's getting late. We should head out," Evander said, glancing at his watch.

He stood, picking up Iris' clutch for her.

Iris nodded, rising. Then, unexpectedly, she wrapped her arms around his waist and pressed a quick kiss to his cheek.

"Evander, thank you. Tonight was incredible."

He froze for a second, then smiled, giving her back a gentle pat.

"I had an amazing time too. Drive safe, and text me when you're home."

"Will do," she said, stepping back and heading to her car.

Evander watched her Porsche disappear down the winding hill, her taillights fading into the night.

A quiet, contented smile curved his lips as he turned back to the restaurant.

Tonight, they didn't make love, but the moment touched his heart and stayed with him more than any passionate encounter ever could.
