Chapter 116 Whispers in the Garden

Only then did Sarah realize she was still in his arms.

She quickly straightened, smoothed down her skirt, and composed herself as best she could.

"Apologies. I didn't realize someone was behind me... Thank you, Mr. Bexley," she added, her voice softening as she spoke his name, one she'd already known.

Evander noticed the faint blush tinting the tips of her ears, and some of the distance in his gaze began to ease.

He'd just arrived in France, still carrying the emotional weight of leaving Lois behind. He hadn't expected much from this blind date. In fact, he hadn't expected anything at all.

But Sarah was... different.

She didn't have Lois' soft innocence. Instead, she radiated a sharp, poised confidence. And that moment of flustered panic when she turned, followed by the unmistakable desire in her eyes when he caught her, it had caught him off guard, in the best way.

"No worries," Evander said, glancing down at his watch.

"I just got off the plane. Traffic was heavier than I thought. Sorry, I kept you waiting."

He nodded toward a bench by the fountain. "The view's pretty nice here. Want to sit down?"

Sarah nodded, and the two of them walked over to the bench.

The fountain misted gently before them, droplets catching the fading light. A breeze carried the scent of fresh grass and lavender, lifting a few loose strands of her hair as they sat.

She glanced sideways at Evander. He was staring off toward the palace, lost in thought. The golden light cut across his profile, softening the angles of his face.

Unlike the male models she worked with, there was nothing forced or practiced about his looks. He wasn't posing for effect. He was simply... striking. Like stone sculpted not to impress, but to endure.

"Is this your first time at the Palace of Versailles?" she asked, breaking the silence with a casual tone.

Evander turned to her.

"Yeah. I've read about it, of course, but seeing it in person is something else."

He paused for a beat and then added, "I'm no expert in architecture or royal history, but I have to say, the gardens are beautifully designed."

"They are," Sarah said, her eyes lighting up with a touch of warmth. "Actually, a lot of the fountains were built according to the original blueprints from Louis XIV's reign. The water systems have barely changed in centuries."

She smiled faintly.

"When I was little, my father used to bring me here all the time. I knew every path by heart. I'd run around the fountains like a little wild thing. He always said I was more tomboy than noble."

Evander listened quietly, not interrupting.

He realized that when Sarah spoke about things she truly cared about, the reserved model exterior fell away. Her features softened. Her voice gained color.

There was something real, something alive in her that the runway lights could never capture.

"You sound like you've got a lot of history here," he said.

"Yeah, I guess I do."

She gave a small smile. "But after I started modeling, I didn't really have time for places like this anymore. If it weren't for this date, I doubt I'd have come back."

She didn't bother sugarcoating it.

"To be honest, I'm just not really looking for a relationship right now," she said, her tone calm but unapologetic.

"If my father hadn't insisted, I wouldn't have come today either. I'm far more used to standing on a runway under spotlights than sitting on a bench talking to a stranger about things that don't matter."

Evander raised an eyebrow. Her bluntness was oddly refreshing.

"Funny. I'm in the same boat. I only came to France to get some space. The whole blind date setup was my grandfather's idea. I didn't expect anything from it."

He paused, eyes glinting slightly. "And for the record, I've never cared much for the whole 'noble heiress' stereotype either."

Sarah blinked and then laughed—a light, crystalline sound that scattered the tension between them.

"Well, aren't we a pair of unwilling participants?"

Her bright laughter somehow shifted the entire energy between them. What had felt stiff and formal began to give way to something looser, more honest.

The last rays of the sun filtered through the leaves, casting golden flecks across her face, outlining the curve of her cheek, her jaw, her lips. Evander caught himself staring.

"Mr. Bexley?" Sarah asked gently, noticing the shift in his expression.

"Something wrong?"

Evander tucked his phone away and shook his head.

"Nothing. Just thinking about work, that's all."

Then, he deftly redirected the conversation. "I heard you're one of France's top models. You're always on the international circuit?"

A flicker of pride lit in Sarah's eyes.

"Yeah. It's been five years now. The early days were rough. You know, running between shows until dawn, walking through blisters and fatigue."

Her voice steadied. "I kept telling myself that if I pushed through, I'd eventually make it to the big stages. And I did. These days, I get to choose who I work with. I've even started saying no to designers I don't believe in."

Her voice carried quiet conviction.

"I love the runway. That moment when the spotlight hits, and every eye in the room turns to you... It makes me feel whole. Like I matter."

Evander gave a small nod. "You turned something you love into a career. That takes guts. You've earned it."

They kept talking.

From fashion weeks to French cuisine, from AI systems to Renaissance art, the conversation flowed easily.

The sky darkened by degrees.

Garden lights flickered on around them, soft and scattered like fallen stars.

The fountains shimmered silver beneath the night lamps. The breeze carried a trace of roses. The entire garden felt wrapped in velvet.

And at that moment, Sarah leaned in slightly, her voice a whisper. "Evander, since this is your first night in Paris... let me take you to dinner."
