Chapter 8 Dinner

In the distance, an ambulance raced down the highway, sirens wailing as it sped toward the Paul family estate.

Evander knew exactly what that meant—Zak was being rushed to the hospital.

His grandfather's assistant had just sent word that Zak had suffered a sudden heart attack at home. Whether he'd survive was now in God's hands.

Evander's eyes turned cold for a moment before softening back to their usual warmth.

He pulled his gaze away from the window, only to meet Iris's curious stare.

"What did you see?" she asked.

"Nothing."

Evander cut a piece of steak and brought it to her lips, his tone as natural as if he were feeding a child. "Try this."

Iris froze, then instinctively parted her lips to take the bite.

The tender beef mixed with the warmth from his fingertips made her heart skip a beat.

Watching her flustered reaction, Evander's smile deepened.

"Oh, by the way," Iris said after swallowing, as if suddenly remembering something, "there's an AI industry summit the day after tomorrow. Professor William Hartley is giving the keynote. I thought you might be interested—want to go together?"

"Of course."

Soft violin music began playing throughout the restaurant—a classic waltz.

Evander set down his cutlery and reached out to her.

"Care to dance, Ms. Lennox?"

Iris stared at his hand with elegant fingers, hesitating.

Her evening dress was a bit short for dancing, and besides... every time she looked at him, she remembered yesterday—how he'd moved inside her, claiming her first time...

"Don't be nervous," Evander said, as if reading her thoughts. His smile was gentle. "Just one dance."

Hesitating, Iris placed her hand in his palm.

His hand was warm and dry, gripping hers with just the right pressure as he helped her to her feet.

They moved to the center of the restaurant's dance floor, swaying slowly to the music's rhythm.

Evander's steps were flawless—clearly he'd had professional training.

His hand rested lightly on her waist, and through the velvet fabric, he could feel the soft warmth of her breasts.

Iris kept her head down, her long hair falling like a curtain to hide most of her face, leaving only her delicate chin visible.

"Iris."

Evander suddenly stopped dancing and gently lifted her chin.

Forced to look up, Iris found herself drowning in his deep eyes.

They reflected the crystal chandelier's light like a sky full of stars, leaving her momentarily dazed.

"About yesterday..."

Iris's heart began racing as she started to speak.

Just then, Evander's phone rang.

The intrusive ringtone shattered the intimate atmosphere.

Evander frowned, glancing at the caller ID—his grandfather's assistant.

He released Iris and stepped aside to answer.

"Sir, Zak Paul didn't make it. He's been pronounced dead."
