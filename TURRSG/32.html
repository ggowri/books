Chapter 33 Iris Finds Out

"Nad..."

Kimberly reached out to help Nadine, but her hand was slapped away.

"Don't touch me!"

Nadine's voice was sharp as ice, her bloodshot eyes webbing with fury. "Kimberly, you make me sick."

The employees turned their heads, phone cameras flashing in the dim light, whispers swelling like a tide.

Kimberly's face flushed crimson, her fingers twisting the hem of her shirt as she stood frozen, at a loss for words.

Evander, unfazed by the stares, smoothed his wrinkled shirt and slipped an arm around Kimberly's waist, his tone cool and detached.

"Leave her be. Let's go."

"Get your hands off her!"

Nadine scrambled to her feet, ignoring the throbbing pain in her wrist, her eyes blazing at Evander.

"You're in such a rush to whisk her away? Off to celebrate your little fling?"

Her voice was low but carried, slicing through the air for all to hear.

A few employees who recognized them gasped, their murmurs growing louder.

Kimberly's cheeks burned hotter, and she pulled free from Evander's grip, turning to Nadine.

"Nad, it's not what you think. We..."

"Not what I think?"

Nadine's laugh was sharp, laced with bitter anguish. "I saw it with my own eyes, and you're still trying to play me for a fool?

"Kimberly, tell me—what's our twenty years of friendship worth to you?"

Kimberly's words caught in her throat, and she turned her face away.

Nothing she could say would make Nadine forget what she just saw.

Evander's brow furrowed, his patience wearing thin.

He tugged Kimberly toward the exit. "We're done talking to her."

"Stop right there!"

Nadine chased after them, her heels clacking sharply on the marble floor, each step a jolt to everyone's nerves.

She blocked their path at the building's entrance, her chest heaving with barely contained rage.

"You're just going to walk away? Don't I deserve an explanation? Doing that in front of me—do you think I'll just let it slide?"

"An explanation?" Evander scoffed, his gaze icy.

"I thought what happened in the elevator was clear enough. Do we need to spell it out?"

Nadine's eyes reddened, her jaw clenched as she shouted. "I'll never forgive you!"

Evander smirked, his voice dripping with disdain.

"I don't need your forgiveness. Just sign the divorce papers. Whatever we had is long gone. Let's both move on."

"You..." Nadine trembled with fury, tears brimming but refusing to fall.

"Evander, I was blind to marry a heartless snake like you. To think you'd stoop so low, doing this just to force me out!"

She took a deep breath, as if steeling herself for a monumental decision.

"Fine. I'll sign the papers.

"But don't get cocky. I can't count on the Nelson family anymore, but Ellery Group will throw its full weight behind Westbrook Group. And when Summit Group is swallowed whole, you'll be the one regretting it."

With that, she spun on her heel, her footsteps fading into the distance, her silhouette resolute, unyielding.

Kimberly watched her go, her heart a tangled mess of guilt and sorrow.

She knew Nadine meant every word. With Ellery Group's power and Denny's ambition, Summit Group was headed for trouble.

Evander, sensing her unease, gave her shoulder a reassuring pat. "Don't worry, Summit Group won't go down that easily."

Kimberly nodded, but her lips stayed sealed.

Her real fear wasn't Summit Group—it was her and Evander's future. Could it really be as smooth as he promised?

Just then, a cool voice called out from behind.

"Evander."

Evander's body stiffened. He turned slowly, the cold detachment on his face giving way to a flicker of panic.

Iris stood a few steps away, dressed simply in a white T-shirt and jeans, her hair pulled back carelessly. Fatigue shadowed her face, but her eyes held a faint, unmistakable trace of hurt.

She gripped her car keys, her knuckles white.

Evander quickly dropped Kimberly's hand, forcing a nervous smile.

"Iris, what are you doing here?"

Iris didn't even look at him. She just stared at Kimberly with a face that gave nothing away.

She'd spent days freaking out, trying to reach Evander. And Summit Group was falling apart.

This morning, she'd learned he was safe and at Nelson Capital Tower. So she rushed over, just to walk right into this.

There was Kimberly, hanging all over Evander's arm, both of them acting like they were on some romantic getaway.

"If I hadn't shown up now," Iris said quietly, her voice dripping with sarcasm, "would you be sending me a wedding invite instead?"

"Iris, it's not what it looks like," Evander said quickly.

Iris finally turned to him, her eyes sharp, as if trying to see through his soul.

Without a word, she walked to her car.

"Iris!"

Evander hurried after her, and after a moment's hesitation, Kimberly followed.

Iris slid into the driver's seat but didn't start the engine. She stared straight ahead, her thoughts a tangled mess.

Evander pulled Kimberly into the back seat, still pleading.

"Iris, there's nothing going on between me and Ms. Nelson..."

Iris turned, her gaze settling on Kimberly, her tone eerily calm.

"Is that true?"

The air in the car turned heavy, almost suffocating.

Evander glanced at Kimberly, his eyes pleading silently.

Kimberly met Iris' stare, a faint, defiant smile curling her lips.

She shook her head slowly, her voice clear and deliberate.

"No. It's not."
