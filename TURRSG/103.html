Chapter 104 Apologize or No Antidote

As soon as the call connected, Lois' voice caught as she said, "Mr. Bexley... Felix already woke up, but he refused to sign the papers, and he even put his hands on me."

At that moment, Evander was in an office at the Institute for Esoteric Studies, standing with Walter over a Tree of Life diagram as they delved into the details of merging the Kabbalistic Tree of Life with AI algorithms.

The sound of Lois' shaken voice darkened Evander's expression at once. He murmured an apology to Walter and then strode quickly to the window.

Evander softened his tone as much as he could. "It's okay, Lois. Just stay calm. I already knew Felix would refuse to sign. That's why the elixir I gave you wasn't just a life-saving potion. It was a slow-acting poison."

"Poison?" Lois froze, tears forgotten. "What do you mean?"

"This poison doesn't kill instantly," Evander explained. "But if he doesn't take the antidote within three days, the illness will strike suddenly, and his death will be painful."

His voice cooled as he continued, "I never wanted to resort to this, but Felix has no sense of gratitude. He even dared to hit you. That leaves me no choice.

"Tell him this—if he doesn't sign the annulment agreement, he will be dead in three days. And make sure he apologizes to you for that slap.

"I already put the antidote in your bag this morning. It's a single white pill."

The mention of the poison made Lois uneasy, but the thought of Felix being forced to apologize gave her a grim sense of satisfaction.

She drew a deep breath, wiped away her tears, and answered with steady resolve, "I got it, Mr. Bexley. I'll tell him exactly what you said right now."

After ending the call, Lois returned to the room.

At that moment, Felix was still arguing with his parents. When he saw Lois come back in, he gave a sharp, mocking laugh. "What now? Ran crying to Evander? I'm warning you—even if he shows up, I'll kill him with my own hands."

Lois stepped up to him, her look like ice, and said, "Felix, the elixir I gave you did more than wake you. It's also a slow-acting poison.

"If you don't sign the annulment papers within three days, and if you don't apologize to me, I won't give you the antidote. You'll die in agony. Those are Mr. Bexley's words, and he never bluffs."

"Poison?" Felix's face drained of color, but then he let out a sneer. "You really think I'd believe that? How could a commoner like Evander possibly have something like that? Don't bother trying to fool me."

"Whatever." Lois pulled out her phone and pulled up a picture. It showed a bottle filled with black liquid.

She said, "This is the antidote. If you sign the papers and apologize to me, I'll give it to you. Otherwise, in three days, you'll suffer until it finishes you off.

"Don't forget, you've just crawled back from the brink of death. I doubt you want to go through it again."

Staring at the picture, Felix was reminded of the torment in Helheim's dungeon, and his resolve began to waver.

He did not fear Evander, but he feared death. He absolutely feared the suffocating cold and endless darkness swallowing him whole.

Mourid and Maria panicked as well. They then rushed to persuade Felix, "Just sign it. Staying alive is what counts. Even without Lois, you can always find someone better down the road."

Felix stayed silent for a moment. He looked at Lois' hard expression, thought about the possibility of dying again, and finally gritted his teeth. "Fine, I'll sign. But I won't apologize."

"You have to apologize as well." Lois stood her ground. "You slapped me, and you owe me that. If you don't, even with your signature, you won't get the antidote."

At those words, Felix's fists clenched so tightly his nails dug into his palms.

He stared at Lois' unwavering expression, thought again of the poison slowly eating away at him, and at last lowered his head. His voice was muffled as he said, "I'm sorry. I shouldn't have hit you."

Lois gave a slight nod and handed over the agreement to dissolve the engagement. "Sign it. Once you do, I'll give you the antidote."

Felix took the pen, his hand trembling slightly as he signed his name on the document.

When Mourid and Maria saw him finish, they finally breathed in relief. They quickly took the agreement, checked it, and handed it to Lois.

Lois accepted it, carefully tucking it away. Then, she took a white pill from her bag and set it on the table.

She said, "This is the antidote. Take it right away. And remember, if you ever come after me or Mr. Bexley again, there won't be an antidote next time."

With that, Lois spared Felix no further glance and walked out of his house.

Stepping past the doorway, she lifted her head to the sky. Sunlight touched her face, and at last the resentment and anger weighing on her heart faded away.

Lois pulled out her phone and dialed Evander. Her tone carried a light ease as she said, "Mr. Bexley, Felix signed the papers and even apologized to me. I also gave him the antidote."

On the other end, Evander let out a breath of relief, his voice softening. "That's wonderful. Does your face still hurt? I'll come to the estate soon to keep you company."

"I'm fine. It doesn't hurt anymore," Lois replied.

Evander chuckled over the line, "Lois, leave Felix's house right away. He was never poisoned. The antidote you gave him was nothing more than a regular cold pill."

"What?" Lois gasped, her steps quickening. "Mr. Bexley, you tricked me, you..."

Evander said quietly, "I'm sorry, Lois. I had no choice. If I had told you Felix wasn't poisoned, and that the pill I slipped into your bag was just an ordinary cold tablet, you would have ruined the act and never gotten that agreement to dissolve the engagement.

"It was the only way."

Lois hurried out through Felix's front gates, but a smile tugged at her lips. "Oh, I don't blame you. If anything, I admire you more. So, will you sleep with me tonight?"
