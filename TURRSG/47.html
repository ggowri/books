Chapter 48 Secret

Dusk crept over Azure Cove Residence, shadows stretching long across the marble floors.

Mary's footsteps echoed through the stairwell, carrying with them the mouthwatering aroma of dinner.

"Mr. Bexley, Ms. Karen, dinner is ready," she called.

Evander pushed back from his desk, the chill of the keyboard still clinging to his fingertips.

As he descended the stairs, the crystal chandelier above bathed the living room in golden light.

The rosewood dining table gleamed beneath its glow. Silver cutlery lay in perfect order. Steam curled from creamy mushroom soup, while the savory scent of pan-seared foie gras mingled with the velvety fragrance of red wine.

Ciotti was already seated, swirling a bottle of 1982 Lafite. She tilted the neck, pouring the ruby liquid into her glass. The wine glimmered like a jewel under the light.

"What are you waiting for?" she teased, chin tilted playfully. "Sit. Mary made your favorite—black truffle steak."

Evander slid into the seat across from her. Mary set down the last dish, bowed politely, and withdrew, leaving only the two of them.

The gentle clink of cutlery against porcelain blended with the rhythm of waves crashing beyond the windows.

Ciotti kept raising her glass, her voice bright as she chattered about the auction. But Evander only murmured half-responses. His eyes stayed fixed on the dark swirl of wine in his goblet.

In its reflection, Norman's stern face kept flashing in his mind.

What if Norman discovered he hadn't brought back the elixir? Would all trust be lost? Would the Godfather's seat—the future he'd been fighting for—slip forever out of reach?

"What's wrong with you today?" Ciotti put down her cutlery and propped her chin on her hands, looking at him.

"You've been in a bad mood since this morning."

She swayed the wine glass in her hand. The wine trickled down the inner wall of the glass, leaving sinuous trails like delicate serpents slithering.

"Are you worried about the auction or... about Sir Westbrook?"

Evander paused for a moment and unconsciously rubbed the ring on his finger.

He didn't want to hide it from Ciotti, but he didn't know how to start.

It was embarrassing to admit his failure.

Ciotti looked at his tense jawline and suddenly smiled. She leaned down under the table and rummaged around for a while.

When she sat up again, there was a small black bottle in her hand, about the size of a palm.

The bottle was pitch-black, sealed with a dark-red cork. It was the same kind of elixir that had been lined up on her grandfather's bedside in the Mortis Gorge chamber.

Evander's eyes widened sharply, and his cutlery clattered onto the plate.

"What's this..."

"Don't you recognize it?" Ciotti deliberately waved the bottle, and there was a faint rattling sound inside.

The smile on her face grew broader. "In the chamber, when my grandfather turned to look at the hot spring pool, I secretly took it from the wooden box. I planned to tell you in two days, but seeing you so worried that you can't even eat..."

"Ciotti..." Evander's voice trembled.

He reached out to take the bottle, but his hand stopped in mid-air, as if he was afraid that all this was just an illusion.

Ciotti had already brought the elixir his grandfather wanted out secretly?

"Here, take it. This is the elixir your grandfather wants." Ciotti pushed the bottle towards him.

The cork glowed with an old-fashioned sheen under the light.

"My grandfather said it's the 'Focus Elixir'. One pill can last for ten days of energy. It's not the most precious 'Longevity Elixir' he's hiding, but it should be enough to pass your grandfather's test, right?"

Evander picked up the bottle. It felt heavy in his hand, and the cold touch of the bottle spread from his fingertips to his heart. The anxiety that had been weighing on him for days instantly vanished.

He looked up at Ciotti.

She was resting her chin on her hand, looking at him with a gentle smile. The stray hairs by her temples fell on her cheeks, overlapping with the image of her sorting the jewels in Mortis Gorge.

"Thank you," Evander said hoarsely, clutching the bottle tightly. "If it weren't for you..."

"Come on, what are you thanking me for?" Ciotti interrupted him, picked up her glass, and clinked it gently with his.

"Aren't we partners? Once you pass the test, I've done my job as your 'treasure-hunting assistant'."

She paused for a moment, then leaned in closer to him, lowering her voice a bit.

"Besides, I'm waiting for you to inherit the Godfather position. Don't forget to take me along for the ride when you make it big."

Evander was tickled by her words and finally relaxed his tense shoulders.

He picked up his wine glass and took a big swig of the red wine. The mellow taste spread across his tongue, and the warmth slid down his throat into his stomach.

All the pent-up stress, anxiety, and self-doubt from the past three days were instantly cast aside.

With this bottle of elixir, he could complete his grandfather's third test and get one step closer to the Godfather position.

"However, I can't give this elixir to my grandfather just yet," Evander said as he placed the bottle inside his suit pocket. He could feel the outline of the bottle through the fabric with his fingertips.

"I'm not ready for his fourth test. I just want to take a break for a while."

Ciotti raised an eyebrow but didn't object.

"I'll follow your lead. But you'd better hide it well. If we lose this thing, we'll have to make another trip to Mortis Gorge."

She picked up her knife and fork again, cut a piece of foie gras, and held it up to Evander's mouth.

"Try this. Mary cooked it to perfection today."

Evander opened his mouth and took the bite. The rich, buttery flavor of the foie gras melted in his mouth, mixed with the lingering taste of red wine on Ciotti's fingertips. It was the most delicious he'd ever had.

He looked at Ciotti, who was smiling sweetly across the table, and for a moment, he was in a daze.

Why was this woman so good to him? She was so selfless that she was willing to risk her life for him.

When the cork of the second bottle of Lafite popped out again, the crystal chandelier in the dining room in their eyes had taken on a hazy glow.

Evander poured Ciotti another half-glass of wine.

The deep-red liquid sloshed in the goblet, making the smile on her face look even more tipsy.

He asked, "Another glass?"

Ciotti didn't say a word. Instead, she tilted her head back and downed the wine in one go. The curve of her neck was clearly visible under the light.

When she put the glass down, her fingertips accidentally touched Evander's hand. She flinched slightly, as if burned, and then nonchalantly picked up the empty glass again.

"Fill it up," she said.

They'd polished off their second bottle of Lafite, and the alcohol was steadily coursing through their veins.

Ciotti's cheeks were rosy, and a few strands of hair at her temples clung to her damp skin, lending her a more alluring and languid air than usual.

As Evander watched her fingertips trace circles around the rim of her glass, he was suddenly reminded of how resolute she'd been when she shielded him in the cave at Mortis Gorge.

This woman had a knack for effortlessly toggling between being composed and being endearing.

"Alright, that's enough. You can't have any more," Evander said, placing his hand on hers.

The warmth of her skin against his palm made them both freeze for an instant. "You'll get plastered."

"I'm not drunk..." Ciotti slurred, though her eyes were already unfocused.

She tried to stand up, propping herself on the table, but her body swayed. Thankfully, Evander quickly steadied her by the waist.

The softness beneath his palm made them both pause.

Ciotti's breathing hitched, and she instinctively nestled into his embrace. Her warm breath tickled his neck.

Evander helped her settle onto the large sofa in the living room.

All of a sudden, Ciotti grabbed his tie and tugged him down so that he was bent over. She stared at him with a tipsy look in her eyes.

"Evander... Stay with me for a bit," she murmured.

Evander nodded and took a seat.

Ciotti turned to face him, and the drunken haze in her eyes gradually gave way to a more complex emotion.

"Evander... I've got a secret. Wanna hear it?" she said, her voice a bit lower and with a faint quiver.

Evander met her gaze.

"Sure," he said, feeling his heart skip a beat.

Ciotti wet her lips and swallowed hard. Then, she suddenly shook her head and flashed a coy smile.

"Not gonna tell you," she said, her fingertips brushing across Evander's hand, sending a little tickle through him. "It won't be a secret if I blab."

"What's this secret that's so hush-hush?" Evander pressed, noticing the brief flicker of unease in her eyes, which only piqued his curiosity more.

He'd known Ciotti for ages, and she'd always been calm and collected. But seeing her all shy and charming like this was a new sight.

Ciotti's cheeks flushed even deeper. She glanced down at her fingertips, where the peach-colored nail polish was a bit chipped.

In fact, just a moment ago, she'd almost let it slip.

Ciotti wanted to tell him about that night when she'd seen Iris follow him into the villa, heard the intimate sounds from the bedroom, and then sneaked into his room in the middle of the night, climbing into his bed while tipsy.

She wanted to share how that stolen moment of intimacy had been so exhilarating, both nerve-wracking and addictive.

Ciotti wanted to confess that they'd once had sex.

"Come on, spill the beans. If you don't, I'm gonna head upstairs and sleep," Evander said with a laugh, gently nudging her shoulder.

Ciotti's eyes widened with a brief panic, quickly masked by the drunkenness.

She leaned forward suddenly and planted a quick peck on Evander's lips.

The soft touch was like a feather, carrying the rich aroma of red wine and the sweetness of a young girl.

Before Evander could react, she said, "The secret is... true love doesn't need words."

Evander was taken aback for a moment, not quite getting it.

Suddenly, Ciotti got up from the sofa on her own.

"Forget it, not talking about it anymore. I'm going upstairs to sleep," she declared.

Evander accompanied her upstairs and into the room.

He laid her on the bed, switched off the light, and then left.
