Chapter 93 Goddess Freya

In the afternoon at Ragnarok Psychiatric Hospital, the pungent smell of disinfectant lingered in the corridors.

Martha Winslet, a nurse, tiptoed along with a tray in her hands.

She was on her way to Kelly's private ward.

Kelly, a former psychic from the Scientific Paranormal Research, was now a crazy woman. Her mood was always unpredictable, and one wrong move could set off a huge tantrum.

The ward door was ajar. Martha gently pushed it open and saw Kelly huddled in the corner. Her white hair was in a mess, sticking to her face, and her eyes stared blankly at the floor.

"Ms. Kelly Gosse, it's time for your meal," Martha placed the tray on the small table beside the bed, speaking as softly as she could.

Kelly didn't move, as if she hadn't heard a thing.

Martha hesitated for a moment and then said, "There's something else... I think you should know. This morning, your nephew, Mr. Gosse, he... passed away."

The words hit Kelly like a thunderbolt.

Suddenly, she looked up, her vacant eyes instantly ablaze with a manic gleam. "What'd you say? Felix is dead?!"

"Yes." Martha nodded, her voice full of sympathy. "The police found his body this morning. They're still investigating the cause. As his aunt, you had to be informed."

"Impossible!" Kelly shrieked.

She lunged at the bedside and kicked the tray onto the floor.

The food scattered everywhere, and the plate shattered into pieces.

She started rolling on the floor like a madwoman. Her white hair was smeared with grease as she roared, "It's Evander! That devil! He killed my nephew! I'll make him pay! I'll send him to hell!"

Her shrill screams echoed through the ward door and into the corridor, making the passing nurses stop in their tracks.

But Kelly didn't care. She got up, sat cross-legged on the floor, formed familiar hand gestures, and began chanting the incantation to summon Hela.

However, the connection that used to respond immediately was now gone. No matter how devoutly she chanted, there was no sign of Hela.

"Your Highness Hela! Where are you?!" Kelly tugged at her hair in desperation. "Didn't you promise to deal with Evander? Why is Felix dead? Come out!"

There was no answer.

Kelly's eyes grew increasingly desperate. All of a sudden, she shot to her feet and bashed her head against the wall.

There was a dull thud.

Blood oozed from her forehead, but she seemed oblivious to the pain. Instead, she began chanting a different spell.

This time, she was trying to summon Goddess Freya, the deity in charge of love, beauty, and fertility, who could peer into people's hearts and tell good from evil.

On her knees on the floor, the blood from her forehead trickling down her cheeks, Kelly sobbed as she pleaded, "Goddess Freya! Please, hear me out! That devil Evander stole Felix's fiancée, Lois. He swindled all of Felix's money and then, with Hela's help, killed him! Punish him! Make him pay!"

The incantation reverberated through the empty ward, but there was still no sign of a response.

Kelly lost it completely.

She shoved aside the nurses who rushed in to calm her down and charged out of the ward like a mad bull.

The medical staff in the corridor quickly closed in on her, trying to stop her.

"Don't you touch me! I gotta find Goddess Freya!" Kelly flailed her arms around wildly, scratching a doctor's arm with her nails.

Taking advantage of the chaos, she staggered towards the stairwell and made a beeline for the rooftop on the fifth floor.

The wind on the rooftop was strong, making her white hair fly everywhere.

The medical staff chased her to the rooftop door but dared not get any closer.

Kelly was standing on the edge of the rooftop, with a drop of dozens of feet below. One wrong step and she'd fall.

"Ms. Kelly Gosse! Calm down! Let's talk this through!" the doctor called out from a safe distance, her voice full of urgency.

Undeterred, Kelly took a seat, cross-legged, right on the rooftop's edge. Against the gusting wind, she shut her eyes and started chanting the spell to call Freya once again.

This time around, her voice oozed a kind of piety that was off the charts, along with a "do or die" determination. It was like she'd already burned all her bridges.

The wind roared past, sounding like a wild beast. Out in the distance, clouds started to clump together, and the sky began to turn an eerie shade of dark.

Just then, a soft but clear voice popped right into Kelly's ear.

"Kelly, I've heard your call."

Kelly's eyes flew open, and tears welled up in an instant.

"Goddess Freya! Is it really you? Did you really hear me?"

"Yeah, it's me." The voice stayed gentle but had this no-nonsense authority.

"I know what Evander has done. Don't worry. I will go grab him for a chat. If he's as bad as you say, he'll definitely get what's coming to him."

Only Kelly could catch these words.

All excited and shaky, she started bowing her head to the ground again and again.

"Thanks, Goddess Freya! Thanks!"

Over at the rooftop door, the medical staff were giving each other these confused looks. They were totally lost about who Kelly was yakking to. All they saw was her going all hyper and bowing nonstop.

It looked like her condition was taking a turn for the worse.

The doctor didn't want to rile her up, taking a slow step back and saying, "Ms. Kelly Gosse, check it out. Your prayer's been answered. How about coming back to the ward with us?"

Suddenly, Kelly raised her head, her eyes ablaze with a wild and fanatical gleam.

"No need for that! Goddess Freya will avenge me! Evander is going to bite the dust soon. Just you wait and see!"

As she spoke, she inched even closer to the edge of the rooftop.

The medical staff were so terrified that they stood stock-still, not daring to do anything that might further enrage her.

The wind picked up even more force. Sitting there on the brink, Kelly wore a strange, twisted smile on her face, as if she could already envision Evander getting his just deserts.

"Hahaha!"

Kelly's eerie laughter sent shivers down everyone's spines.
