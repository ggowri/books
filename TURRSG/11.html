Chapter 11 AI Ethics

Evander pulled Ciotti behind him, thumb pressing the panic button on his car key.

"Denny send you?"

His voice was calm, but his mind was spinning fast.

The booze on their breath, the pulled-down caps—all bad news.

"Cut the crap!"

The scar-faced thug swung his baton and rushed in—reckless, but ruthless.

Evander dodged smooth as silk, landing a kick right to the guy's knee.

Crack.

The guy hit the ground howling.

His buddies froze for a beat, then rushed in.

Just then, Ciotti stepped out from behind him, silver dress flashing like a blade.

She caught the one on the left, twisted his wrist.

Pop.

His scream echoed through the parking lot.

Evander didn't hesitate, facing down the last thug head-on.

A punch flew at him, but he didn't even flinch.

With his left hand, he locked the thug's elbow, and his right hit the armpit like lightning. The guy's arm went limp.

Evander spun, flipped him clean over his shoulder, and slammed him to the ground.

Two minutes flat—three muscleheads down, all groaning in pain.

Ciotti brushed the dust off her dress, elegant as ever.

"Maybe get that ninth dan re-certified," she smirked.

Evander glanced at his watch, sweat rolling down his jawline.

"Time's up. I'm on next."

******

The elevator doors opened just as the host's voice boomed.

"Next, welcome to Summit Group's founder, Mr. Bexley!"

All eyes snapped to the entrance.

Evander took a deep breath, straightened his slightly rumpled collar, exchanged a quick look with Ciotti, and strode onto the stage.

"Mr. Westbrook's presentation was impressive," he said, voice calm, carrying through the hall.

"But I'm not here to talk about AI and quick profits. I'm here to map out AI's next five years."

Then, he clicked. A complex neural network lit up the screen behind him.

"First, medical breakthroughs."

His finger traced patterns in the air as the diagram shifted.

"Our brain-computer interface isn't just helping ALS patients communicate—we're talking memory storage and disease prediction."

The crowd gasped.

In the front row, William stroked his beard, eyes warm with pride.

Evander had been his best student, and this was the vision they'd discussed years ago.

"Second, environmental control."

Another slide appeared—AI climate models.

"We can predict extreme weather 72 hours ahead, and optimize emission reduction by combining global carbon data with satellite imagery."

Iris felt tears welling up.

"And finally, AI ethics." Evander's tone darkened, sharp and intense.

"Tech isn't good or evil. Those who use it need limits."

"That's why we're partnering with twenty universities to build the first AI Ethics Review Board. Innovation must always serve humanity."

Three seconds of dead silence.

Then the room exploded with applause.

William sprang up, clapping like crazy.

Iris's hands were red from all the applause.

Even the professors in the front row were nodding, clearly impressed.

Denny's face drained of color.

All his "achievements" suddenly looked like a bad joke next to Evander's vision.

As Evander stepped off the stage, Ciotti handed him a bottle of water.

"Who knew? Karate champ and motivational speaker," she teased.

Not far away, Iris walked toward him, a soft smile blooming across her face.

But the moment she saw Ciotti, that smile faltered.

A gorgeous blonde bombshell, acting way too cozy with Evander.

She rocked a silver slip dress that hugged every curve. Her features could've been carved by Michelangelo himself, and those blue eyes? Deep as the ocean, sparkling like the night sky.

Something pricked at Iris's chest, stirring up threads of jealousy.

She straightened her spine, fingers curling slightly at her sides.

"Iris, meet Ciotti—my old karate instructor," Evander said with a grin.

"Heard so much about you, Ms. Lennox."

Their handshake was brief.

Still, Iris couldn't miss those toned fingers. Definitely a fighter's hands.

Meanwhile, in a far corner, Denny's phone rang.

"Sorry, Mr. Westbrook. We screwed up."

"Idiots. Can't even handle one loser and a woman." Denny's voice was ice-cold.

"But sir, they were too strong—"

Click. He ended the call.

He watched Evander surrounded by admirers, a flash of pure malice flickered.
