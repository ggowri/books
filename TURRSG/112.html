Chapter 113 King Edward's Arrangement

At 8 AM, the grand hall at Royal Tech had already been refreshed and beautifully set up for the ceremony.

A red carpet stretched from the main entrance to the signing table, flanked by tall arrangements of white roses. Logos from both Vanguard Tech and Royal Tech were prominently displayed on the stage. Cameras were already set up, and journalists stood ready, anticipating the momentous event.

Chad and Gary arrived early.

As soon as they saw Norman and Evander walk in, they stepped forward to greet them.

"Sir Westbrook, welcome," Chad said warmly.

"I've heard a lot about you. It's an honor to finally meet you in person."

"You're too kind," Norman replied with a smile.

"It's Vanguard Tech's privilege to partner with Royal Tech."

At exactly 9 AM, the signing ceremony officially began.

The host stepped onstage to introduce the representatives and outline the scope of the collaboration. Applause erupted from the audience.

Then Norman and Gary each signed the agreement. As they exchanged documents and posed for photos, a flurry of camera flashes lit up the ballroom, capturing the historic moment.

Afterward, Norman addressed the press.

"Vanguard Tech will be working hand-in-hand with Royal Tech to incorporate the Decision Matrix Model into humanoid robotics. Our goal is to advance global technological innovation.

"But beyond sharing technical expertise, we're also committed to fostering cultural exchange and achieving mutual growth."

The moment the interview ended, Evander could no longer hold back.

He turned to Norman eagerly. "Grandpa, let's go to the Royal Three Estate."

Norman gave a nod. "Let's head over now."

But just as they reached the exit, they saw Charlotte approaching with a group of royal guards.

Her expression was unusually guarded as she addressed them. "Gentlemen, King Edward requests your presence at the Royal Three Estate. He has something to announce."

A strange unease stirred in Evander's chest, but he still nodded.

"Understood. We'll head there now."

Thirty minutes later, they arrived at the Royal Three Estate.

Edward was already seated in the main drawing room, with Charlotte beside him, her expression solemn.

Lois was there too. As soon as she saw Evander, she rushed toward him, but Edward cut her off sharply.

"Lois, come stand over here."

She paused mid-step and then quietly walked over to stand beside Charlotte.

Edward turned his gaze on Evander and Norman, his tone firm and resolute.

"Mr. Bexley, Sir Westbrook, congratulations on your successful partnership with Royal Tech. However, I have a new arrangement regarding Lois."

Evander felt a chill creep through his chest. "Your Majesty, what arrangement?"

"I've asked Charlotte to enroll Lois in a prestigious aristocratic school abroad," Edward announced.

"She'll leave for Switzerland next week and remain there for at least two years. She's a princess, and it's time she received the education befitting her status. This infatuation with romance must come to an end."

"Switzerland?" Evander's voice faltered as he turned to Lois.

"Your Majesty, she doesn't want to go abroad. She wants to stay in London—"

"This isn't about what she wants," Edward interrupted coldly.

"It's about what the royal family has deemed necessary. Felix will accompany her. He'll continue to pursue her there. And if Lois is willing, their engagement will remain intact."

"Felix is going too?" Evander's expression darkened. "Have you even considered how Lois feels about all this?"

"I'm sending her abroad for her own good," Edward said sharply. "That's what a father does."

Tears welled in Lois' eyes. "Father, I don't want to go to Switzerland. I want to stay here, in London..."

Charlotte gently took her hand. "The school is excellent, Lois. This is good for the future of the monarchy."

Just then, Felix strolled into the room, a smug smile on his face.

"Don't worry, Lois. I'll take care of you in Switzerland. Once you come around, we can return and finally have our wedding."

Lois recoiled from him, her gaze filled with disdain. She turned to Evander, eyes full of desperation.

"What do I do..."

Evander stepped toward her and took her hand, his voice steady.

"Don't be afraid, Lois. Two years will pass quickly. Take care of yourself while you're there."

Norman stood to the side, quietly observing.

Edward had laid out his plan with unwavering resolve. But Evander still held Lois' hand tightly, reluctant to let go.

A flicker of knowing passed through his gaze.

He had suspected all along that Lois' royal background would eventually become a liability.

As heir to the Godfather, Evander couldn't afford to be tied down by emotional entanglements.

Edward's decision to send Lois to Switzerland fit perfectly with Norman's own thinking.

Seeing how unwilling the young couple was to part, Norman stepped forward.

He placed a firm hand on Evander's shoulder and spoke in a calm, commanding tone, "His Majesty is doing what he believes is best for his daughter. Let's go."

Evander froze, wanting to speak again, but Norman gently pulled him away.

He turned his head for one last look. Lois' red-rimmed eyes and heartbroken expression nearly broke him.

Once outside, they got into the car.

Norman reached into the inner pocket of his suit and pulled out an envelope. He removed three photographs and handed them to Evander, his tone calm but resolute.

"Evan, congratulations. You've passed the fifth trial. You secured a major partnership despite royal interference, and more importantly, you stayed true to yourself. I'm proud of that."

Evander looked down at the photos, confused.

The first showed a tall, elegant girl in haute couture on the runway at Paris Fashion Week.

The second featured a radiant woman at a movie premiere, holding a trophy that read "Best New Actress".

The third captured an athletic girl mid-air during a volleyball match, her jersey emblazoned with the French national team's logo.

"All three are descendants of noble French bloodlines," Norman explained.

"The first is one of France's top runway models. The second is a rising star in Hollywood. The third plays for France's national volleyball team. I've arranged for you to meet them.

"Consider this the second part of your reward for completing the fifth trial. The first part is 30% ownership of Vanguard Tech."

Evander's hands froze around the photos. Shock filled his eyes.

"Grandpa... I'm not ready to give up on Lois."

Norman let out a cold chuckle.

His gaze sharpened. "Evan, understand this—everything in this world is a stepping stone on your path to power. That includes women. But once you reach the summit, all of it will be within your grasp. Everything. Including love."

Evander sat stunned, conflicted and overwhelmed.

But deep down, he knew Norman wasn't wrong.

If he truly wanted to build something lasting, something that could change the world, he couldn't be ruled by emotion.

Norman clapped him on the shoulder again.

"Enough. Go to France for a while. Clear your head. One of these girls will help you forget Lois. When you return, I'll give you the sixth trial."
