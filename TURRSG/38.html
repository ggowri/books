Chapter 39 The Strange Guardians

"It can't run this time," Ciotti said, raising her submachine gun at the lion's head.

"Wait." Evander pressed her barrel down, eyes narrowing on the lion's swollen belly.

"It's a female. She's pregnant."

Ciotti froze, squinting at the faint bulge of its abdomen. Her finger eased off the trigger, and she lowered the gun with a tight breath.

"Treasure first. We'll deal with her later."

They turned to the Crimson Coffin. Up close, the gem-studded lid revealed intricate etchings—thousands of tiny skulls woven together into a grotesque tapestry.

Ciotti wedged her dagger into the seam, but the lid refused to budge.

"It's locked," she muttered, nodding at the brass clasp fixed into the side.

"We need a key."

Evander scanned the chamber. His gaze fell on the skeleton with the silver bracelet. Its bony hand was clenched tight, like it had guarded something even in death.

He crouched, prying open the brittle fingers. Inside lay a brass key engraved with the same skull motif as the coffin.

"Got it."

He handed it to Ciotti. She slid it into the lock and twisted. A sharp click rang out, echoing against stone.

The lid cracked open an inch, releasing a strange, intoxicating scent—exotic, spiced, almost jeweled in its richness.

But before they could peer inside, the lioness roared. She staggered upright, limping toward them with desperate fury.

Ciotti whipped her gun up—

"Behind you!" Evander tackled her to the ground.

A spear hissed over their heads, piercing the lion's chest. The beast gave one last, ragged cry before collapsing, still.

They looked up.

Three burly men stepped from the shadows, draped in animal pelts, faces streaked with crimson paint. Each carried a gold-bladed axe, their eyes sharp and unyielding.

"This is sacred ground," the leader growled, voice rough as gravel yet startlingly clear. "Outsiders aren't welcome."

Evander and Ciotti swung their guns up, barrels fixed on the trio. They hadn't expected anyone alive down here.

The men spread into a triangle, silently encircling them. Their stance was practiced, deliberate—like trained guards.

The leader advanced, his axe glinting cold in the glow.

"You came for the Crimson Coffin, didn't you?"

Ciotti ignored the question. "Who are you?"

The man gestured first to the fallen lion, then to the skeleton with the bracelet.

"We guard this place—his skeleton, and his last wish. Fifty years ago, he came with a team. They triggered a trap. Only he survived. Before he died, he warned us: the treasure is cursed. A harbinger of disaster. It must never leave."

Evander and Ciotti exchanged a sharp glance.

The sudden appearance of these so-called guardians made an already dangerous mission more precarious. Fighting them now would be suicide.

"Hand over the key," one of the men snarled, hefting his axe. "Or we take it."

The cave fell taut with silence. Evander's grip whitened on his gun. Doubt crept in. Was it worth spilling blood here—for his grandfather's test? And what if the curse was real?

The leader's lips curved into a grin, red paint cracking to reveal yellowed teeth. He tapped his axe against his palm.

"Or… we settle this another way."

Evander's eyes narrowed.

"One of you fights me. One-on-one." The man thumped a fist against his chest, muscles rolling beneath the pelts. "If you win, the key's yours. If you lose… you leave and forget this coffin ever existed."

Ciotti stepped forward, but Evander caught her arm. Slinging his gun over his shoulder, he stepped up.

"I've got this."

"Evander!" Ciotti hissed, eyes flashing with worry.

The man looked carved from stone, years in the wild hardening every muscle. This wouldn't be easy.

"Don't worry," Evander said with a grin, voice light, almost cocky. "I'm a ninth-dan karate master, remember?"

The leader raised a brow at the word, intrigued. He tossed his axe to one of his men, then rolled his neck until vertebrae cracked.

"My name is Madden McCarthy."

"Evander Bexley."

Before his name had fully left his lips, Madden charged like a boulder in freefall.

His fist swung at Evander's face, raw and brutal.

Evander slipped aside, catching the wrist and twisting hard. Madden grunted, pain flashing, but his other fist came fast, arcing toward Evander's ribs.

Evander let go, ducked under, and drove his elbow into Madden's side.

Madden staggered back, surprise flickering in his eyes. He hadn't expected such speed from the lean young man.

"Come on!" Madden roared, lunging again.

He swept a low kick, aiming to shatter Evander's knee.

Evander vaulted, spinning mid-air, and slammed a kick into Madden's shoulder.

The blow sent Madden crashing to the stone, dust swirling around him. He struggled, breath ragged, trying to rise.

Evander stepped in, foot pressing lightly against his chest. His eyes stayed cold, steady.

"Still want to fight?"

Madden stared up at him for three long seconds—then broke into booming laughter.

"Not bad. You've got fire." He tapped Evander's ankle. "I'm out."

Evander hauled him up. Madden rubbed his shoulder, calling something to his companions. The men lowered their axes, their eyes no longer hostile.

"We're not guardians," Madden admitted with a rough chuckle. "We have nothing to do with that expedition fifty years ago."

Evander and Ciotti exchanged another startled look.

"We came here six months ago. A research team." Madden's voice grew heavy. "We've been surviving ever since. Hunting. Waiting for someone else to show up."

"Then why block us?" Ciotti demanded.

"Not blocking." His smile was bitter. "Testing."

He nodded at the Crimson Coffin.

"That's just the surface. Below this cave lies an entire world—an underground jungle out of Journey to the Center of the Earth. Glowing plants. Flying lizards. Things worse than lions."

His tone hardened.

"We've gone down three times. We started as twenty-four. Now there are three of us left."
