Chapter 77 A Kindred Spirit in Books

Evander's heart clicked with understanding.

So, Lois was here, lost in her book, because her boyfriend had left her to her own devices.

He didn't pry further, instead keeping the conversation light and natural.

"It's not every day you meet someone who loves these books as much as I do. I'm Evander."

"Lois," she smiled, offering her hand. "It's such a treat to run into someone who gets this book. I was feeling a bit lonely reading by myself."

Evander clasped her hand briefly, a polite touch before letting go.

Then, with a subtle flourish, he extended his empty right hand, palm up.

Lois' brow furrowed in confusion, but before she could speak, a vibrant red rose materialized in his palm.

Its petals were dewy and flawless, as if plucked moments ago from a secret garden.

"Oh my goodness..." Lois gasped, her hand flying to her mouth, eyes wide with astonishment.

"Where did that come from? Your hand was empty!"

She studied his fingers, then scanned around, searching for some hint of a trick, but found nothing.

"Just a little something," Evander smiled, holding out the rose. "It made me think of you, same as the book."

Lois took the rose gingerly, bringing it to her nose. The rich, velvety scent drew a wide smile across her face, a flush creeping up her cheeks.

Her gaze softened, curiosity and warmth flickering in her eyes as she looked at him.

This man wasn't just a literary soul—he could conjure magic, or so it seemed. He was worlds apart from her boyfriend, who only cared about gambling.

They steered clear of personal details, diving back into Jane Eyre instead.

Evander spoke of Jane's struggles at Thornfield Hall, tracing her evolving feelings for Rochester, from reliance to fierce independence.

Lois shared the scene that broke her heart—Jane's resolute departure after discovering Rochester's hidden wife.

"I think when Jane left, her heart must've been shattering," Lois said, cradling the rose, her voice steady with conviction. "But she knew her dignity mattered more than love. If it were me, I'd do the same. I'd never sacrifice my principles for love."

"You're absolutely right," Evander said, his tone warm with admiration.

"True love isn't about one side giving in—it's about equality and respect.

"Jane only returned to Rochester when they stood as equals, not because of his wealth, but because their souls aligned."

Lois leaned in, captivated, her eyes sparkling with growing fascination.

Unlike the aristocratic men who only talked about jewelry and racing, Evander showed genuine depth in his understanding of literature.

This brief half-hour meant more to her than all the time she'd spent with her boyfriend.

Time slipped away, and soon the setting sun cast golden rays through the ship's glass walls, bathing Lois' lavender gown in a warm, amber glow.

She glanced at her watch, a flicker of reluctance crossing her face.

"I... I should probably go find Felix. He's been gambling all afternoon, probably losing a fortune."

Evander caught the hesitance in her voice and smiled gently.

"No worries. If you want to keep talking about the book, I'll be here tomorrow, same time."

Her face lit up, and she nodded eagerly.

"Yes! I'll definitely come!"

Clutching the rose and her copy of Jane Eyre, she gave him a little wave.

"See you tomorrow, Evander!"

He watched her walk away, a sly smile tugging at his lips as he sipped his whiskey.

Lois hadn't revealed much, but her style, her poise, and her casual mention of her gambling-addicted boyfriend screamed high society.

She was already warming to him. If he played his cards right, her connections could open doors to elite circles—and maybe even spark something more.

A soft announcement crackled over the poolside speakers.

A melodic female voice invited passengers to a welcome banquet at seven in the top-deck ballroom, urging formal attire.

Evander checked his watch and found it was six-thirty.

He headed back to his cabin.

Inside, he pulled a pristine white suit from his Inventory Cube.

Crafted from Italian wool with intricate embroidery at the collar, it was tailored for the occasion.

He slipped it on, adjusting his tie in the mirror, then added a sapphire-encrusted cufflink, elevating the look with a touch of regal flair.

At seven sharp, he stepped into the ballroom.

The hall buzzed with energy, crystal chandeliers scattering light like stars, and a lilting waltz floated through the air.

Men and women in resplendent gowns and tuxedos mingled, champagne flutes in hand, their laughter and murmurs a symphony of high-society elegance.

Evander's gaze swept the crowd, quickly landing on Lois.

She stood in a corner, radiant in a pale yellow gown, her hair swept into an elegant updo, though her posture betrayed a hint of unease as she waited for her boyfriend.

Not far away, a man in a black suit with slicked-back hair laughed loudly among his friends. He clutched a stack of chips in one hand, the stench of liquor wafting even across the room. That had to be Felix, Evander figured.

Lois spotted Evander, her eyes brightening instantly.

She flashed him a warm smile, giving a discreet wave.

Evander inclined his head politely in response but did not approach her just yet.

He knew this was not the moment to intrude.

The banquet was just beginning, and he had plenty of time to connect.

Evander grabbed a flute of champagne and drifted to the floor-to-ceiling windows, gazing at the inky sea beyond.

Waves churned under the ship's lights, their white foam glinting faintly. A sea breeze slipped through a crack, its salty tang sharpening his senses.

"Mr. Bexley?" a familiar voice called softly.

He turned to find Lois behind him, her silk gown shimmering under the chandeliers, a glass of champagne in her hand.

"What a surprise to see you here," she said, smiling. "Here for the banquet?"

"Yes, I am," Evander replied with a slight smile. "Are you here with your friend?"

Lois nodded. "Let me introduce you."
