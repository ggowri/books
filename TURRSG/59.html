Chapter 60 Aunt Elsa

Emily, who had been quietly sitting to the side, brightened up at his agreement. Her smile was impossible to hide. Just moments ago, she'd come so close to something forbidden, and now the anticipation still buzzed inside her.

If she could catch him alone at the party, maybe they could finally finish what had been left undone. Her cheeks flushed as she snuck a glance at him, her eyes filled with hope.

"Oh, don't worry about that," Aileen said with a gentle smile. "Aunt Elsa doesn't care for fancy gifts. She just wants to know you care."

But Emily jumped in, "Come on, Aileen! Mr. Bexley is meeting Aunt Elsa for the first time. He's got to be ready." She turned to him, her tone light and teasing. "Don't sweat it, Mr. Bexley. Just grab something nice. We'll wait for you at home, and when we meet Aunt Elsa, I'll make sure to say all the right things on your behalf."

Evander nodded, already considering his options. Ciotti had just come back from Mortis Gorge with some rare treasures from the royal collection. Any one of them would be impressive enough for Elsa without going overboard.

A simple gift wouldn't catch her interest, but something with royal history and elegance? That would be just right.

He stood up, flashing them a smooth smile. "I'll go get ready then and be back to pick you up later."

The three left the dance studio together. Evander drove Aileen and Emily back home. When they stopped outside, Emily lingered a moment, grabbing his arm before slipping out.

Her voice was low and sweet, full of unspoken promises. "Come early, Mr. Bexley. I'll be waiting." Her fingers brushed lightly across his wrist, sending a subtle spark through him, her eyes saying everything she couldn't.

Evander's lips curled into a knowing smile. He watched until the two women disappeared into the villa before starting the engine and heading toward Azure Cove Residence.

The drive stretched on, but his mind was anything but calm. Elsa's image flashed in his mind again and again—the way she commanded meetings in those sharp suits, her confident speeches at international forums, and the tender way she listened to people at charity events.

Every side of her only made him more eager for the night ahead. If he could get Elsa's essence, the Arcane Ring would finally be one step closer to its full power, awakening the Inventory Cube, a power that allowed him to store and summon items at will.

With that ability, everything would become easier—whether finding the remaining royal women or surviving Norman's trials.

Forty minutes later, the Rolls-Royce pulled up in front of Azure Cove Residence. Evander stepped out and entered the villa. The living room was quiet; Ciotti was nowhere to be seen.

Evander frowned, calling out, but there was no answer. Had something come up, making her leave without saying goodbye?

Evander went upstairs and pushed open his bedroom door. The sight made him pause. The desk was covered with pearls, gemstones, and royal trinkets—treasures Ciotti had brought back from Mortis Gorge.

Among them was a sapphire-studded royal brooch, a necklace with deep green emeralds, and several ancient coins with the royal crest.

He stepped closer and saw a pink note on top, written in Ciotti's neat handwriting: "Evander,

something urgent came up at home. I'll be away for a few days. Don't worry about me. I've split the Mortis Gorge treasures—half for each of us. This set is yours. Guard them well.

-Ciotti"

The short message stirred something in him. Ciotti was always like this—quietly giving, always there when he needed her most. These had been her grandfather's heirlooms, yet she still split them for him. A woman like her was rare.

Evander picked up the sapphire brooch. Its cool surface glinted under the light, the faintly etched royal insignia on the back proof of its exquisite craftsmanship and undeniable worth.

This was perfect for Elsa. Elegant enough to suit her aristocratic bloodline, but not so flashy it seemed calculated. It would show thoughtfulness, not extravagance. He placed the brooch into a velvet box. From the pile, he also chose a pearl bracelet—something to give Emily as a token of thanks.

When the gifts were ready, he checked the clock. It was already half past five.

He changed into a tailored black suit, fastened the diamond cufflinks Ciotti had left behind, and adjusted his tie in front of the mirror.

The Arcane Ring on his middle finger gleamed under the lights, its spiral pattern looking sharper than before.

He took a deep breath, his eyes steady. Tonight's birthday party wasn't just about giving gifts. He needed to find a way to get close to Elsa, earn her trust, and secure a private moment. Only then could the Arcane Ring receive its third nourishment.

His phone buzzed. A message from Emily lit up the screen: [Mr. Bexley, are you ready? We're leaving at six-thirty! I'm wearing my new lavender gown tonight. You'll love it, I promise.]

A playful smiley and a heart emoji followed.

Evander typed back: [On my way. Can't wait to see your new dress.]

Pocketing his phone, he picked up the gift boxes and left the room. Soon, the Rolls-Royce was gliding away from Azure Cove Residence toward Aileen's family estate.

Half an hour later, the car stopped in front of Governor Kenny's villa. Emily came running out, her lavender gown shimmering with tiny crystals that caught the light with every step. She spun toward him like a fairy tale princess come to life.

"Mr. Bexley, you're here! Do you like it?" Her eyes sparkled. "I chose lavender on purpose. Aileen said it suits me best!" She twirled gracefully, her skirt flaring as it lifted just enough to reveal her ankles and the curve of her calves.

"You look beautiful. It's perfect on you," Evander said with a warm smile.

He held out the velvet box with the pearl bracelet. "Here—your gift. I promised you something from the royal collection."

Emily's eyes widened in delight. She slipped the bracelet onto her wrist immediately, then turned to show Aileen. "Aileen, look! Mr. Bexley gave me a royal pearl bracelet. Isn't it gorgeous?"

Aileen, elegant in a crimson gown that made her skin glow, gave Emily an approving nod.

With practiced grace, she stepped forward, her gaze finding Evander's. "Shall we go? Aunt Elsa is already waiting at the villa. Tonight you'll also meet a lot of leaders from politics and business. It's a great chance to build connections."

Evander nodded, opening the door for them. "After you." The Rolls-Royce slipped smoothly into the evening traffic, heading toward Elsa's villa.

In the car, Emily chattered excitedly about the birthday decorations—how every year Elsa personally chose the roses, filling the villa with blooms. Aileen occasionally added reminders about etiquette, suggesting how Evander should greet various guests.

Evander leaned back in his seat, absently rubbing the Arcane Ring with his thumb. His mind, however, was already elsewhere—plotting the night ahead.

First, he needed to make a strong impression with the brooch. Then, he would use Aileen and Emily's introductions to draw Elsa into conversation. Finally, he wanted to wait for the right moment to create a private opportunity—long enough for the Arcane Ring to receive its third nourishment.

The sun was setting, painting the sky in brilliant shades of orange and red. Golden light spilled across the Rolls-Royce, giving its sleek body a warm sheen.

Evander gazed out the window, a faint, almost unnoticeable smile playing on his lips.

Everything was going smoothly. It was almost as if fate itself had favored him.
