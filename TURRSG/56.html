Chapter 57 Carry Me to the Room

The Rolls-Royce glided smoothly through the streets of downtown.

Inside the car, Emily was still chattering excitedly with Evander about the fight they had just witnessed.

Suddenly, Aileen's phone rang.

She glanced at the caller ID, and her brows furrowed immediately. When she answered, her voice carried a sharp seriousness. "Hello, Anna. What happened?"

On the other end, her colleague Anna Gray spoke in a rush.

Fragments of conversation cut through the static: publishing issue... rejected review... urgent...

Aileen's expression darkened with each word. She nodded silently, then drew a deep breath. "Got it. I'm on my way to the office. Just keep things together until I get there. Don't panic.

When she hung up, she turned to Evander with an apologetic look.

She said, "Mr. Bexley, I apologize, but there's an urgent situation at the company. Your manuscript, 'Perfect Sex and Love,' has run into problems with the review board. I need to handle this right away."

She paused, looking over at Emily in the passenger seat. "Emily, why don't you show Mr. Bexley to your dance studio? I'll join you both after work."

"Okay!" Emily nodded quickly, her eyes shining with anticipation. "Mr. Bexley, I'll take you to my studio. You can watch me dance!"

Evander smiled and agreed, "Of course. Let's get Aileen to the office first."

He steered the car toward the headquarters of LUXERA magazine.

Ten minutes later, the car rolled to a stop in front of a sleek glass building.

Aileen stepped out and turned back to them. "I'll make this as quick as I can. Wait for me at the studio."

Then she bent slightly toward Emily, her tone more pointed. "And behave yourself. Show Mr. Bexley a proper dance."

"I know!" Emily waved her off, watching as Aileen disappeared into the building.

Then she spun back toward Evander, almost bouncing in her seat. "Let's go, Mr. Bexley! Aileen rented this private studio just for me. It has everything. You're going to love it."

Evander started the car and let Emily guide the way.

She could barely contain her excitement as she told him about it. "It's so cool—there's tons of space to dance, plus a shower and even a place to hang out. Aileen says it's perfect. I can do everything there! Isn't that awesome?"

It was clear she loved the studio.

Twenty minutes later, they pulled up to an arts center. Emily led Evander inside, and they rode the elevator to the fifth floor. At the end of the hallway was a frosted glass door marked: [Private Dance Studio. Do Not Enter Without Permission.]

Emily unlocked it, and a faint lavender scent greeted them. The space was larger than Evander imagined. Light gray flooring stretched wall to wall. One side had towering mirrors; the other was filled with props—barres, yoga mats, ballet shoes. A door in the corner led to the bathroom and lounge.

"What do you think, Mr. Bexley? Isn't it amazing?" Emily twirled across the floor, her pale-yellow skirt fluttering like butterfly wings.

"It's beautiful and very professional," Evander said, walking to the mirror, catching his reflection before turning back to her with a smile. "So, what are you going to dance for me?"

Emily tilted her head, eyes sparkling with curiosity. "What do you want to see? I can do ballet, contemporary, even a little jazz."

"How about a bit of each? I'd love to see all your styles," Evander said, settling onto a small sofa in the corner, one leg crossed over the other. "I'm looking forward to it."

Beaming, Emily darted into the lounge to change. Moments later, she returned in a pure white ballet dress that brushed her knees, revealing the delicate lines of her legs. Pink slippers hugged her feet.

She stepped gracefully to the center of the room, bowed to Evander, then took a steady breath. With slow precision, she began a classic ballet sequence. Soft music floated from the speakers. Emily rose onto her toes, spinning and leaping lightly across the floor.

Her movements were elegant and refined, her arms fluid, her neck long and poised, like a swan in flight.

Yet Evander noticed something beneath the beauty. Every time she went en pointe, her brows knitted slightly, a shadow of pain flickering across her face—subtle but impossible to miss.

"Take a break for a moment," Evander said suddenly, concern in his voice. "Your toes look like they're hurting. Don't push yourself too hard."

Emily halted mid-twirl, her breath slightly uneven. "Really, Mr. Bexley, it's fine," she said softly, managing a small smile. "Pain is just part of ballet—we all learn to live with it."

She stepped closer, lifting one foot to show the worn, faded pink of her ballet slippers. She chuckled, "See? I've gone through several pairs already. The ache is real when I'm on my toes, but once I'm dancing, it fades away."

Evander's gaze lingered on her reddened toes, a pang of empathy stirring in his chest. He shook his head gently. "Why not take a break from ballet? Try something less demanding, like a lively modern dance."

Emily's eyes sparkled with excitement, and she nodded eagerly. "Sounds fun. Let me show you a modern dance routine."

She darted to the changing room and returned in a sleek, beige leotard that hugged her every curve. The silky fabric accentuated her waist, full chest, and perfectly rounded hips, each movement highlighting her captivating figure.

The music shifted to an upbeat, pulsing rhythm. Emily began to dance, her movements full of energy. She swayed her hips, tossed her hair, and leaped with youthful exuberance and undeniable allure.

Each jump sent a subtle ripple through her chest, unbound by any bra, drawing Evander's attention. His throat tightened, and a flush of heat stirred within him. Yet Evander maintained his composure, the picture of a gentleman.

His eyes followed her every move, but beneath his calm exterior, his heart raced. His fingers absently traced the black ring on his middle finger.

Emily was the second "nourishment" needed to activate the Arcane Ring, and he knew he had to seize this moment.

Emily danced with fierce passion, sweat beading on her forehead, strands of hair clinging to her flushed cheeks, adding a touch of sultry charm. Her glances toward Evander were filled with hope and admiration, craving his approval.

After about half an hour, Emily spun and leaped, but her foot slipped. She lost her balance and crashed to the floor with a heavy thud.

"Emily!" Evander bolted forward, catching her before she hit the floor. He lifted her in his arms, his voice tense. "Tell me where it hurts. Are you alright?"

Emily's face had gone white, her teeth clenched against the pain. "My calf," she managed through tight lips. "I twisted it—it's bad."

Evander carried her to the sofa in the changing room and gently set her down. He carefully lifted her leg, noting the graceful lines of her calf, now marred by slight swelling at the ankle.

"Let me massage it. It'll help ease the pain," he said softly, his eyes reflecting concern. He knew this was his chance.

Emily nodded, her cheeks flushing as she looked at him with trust. "Thank you, Mr. Bexley."

His hands settled on her calf, the warmth of her skin sending a thrill through his fingertips. He massaged the swollen area with gentle strokes, occasionally brushing the sensitive skin on the inside of her leg. A faint tremor ran through her, and a subtle smile curved his lips.

As he continued, his hands gradually moved upward, gliding past her knee to the soft skin of her inner thigh. Emily's breathing quickened, her cheeks glowing a deep crimson. She instinctively tried to close her legs, but Evander gently pressed her knee, keeping her still.

"Does it hurt?" he asked, looking up at her, his eyes carrying a hint of seduction. "Tell me if it does."

Emily shook her head, her gaze hazy as she leaned toward him. His touch, warm and teasing, sent her pulse racing, a tingling warmth spreading through her. Her body responded, a soft dampness growing between her thighs.

Evander sensed her reaction, his smile deepening. His fingers continued their slow exploration, reaching the tender skin of her inner thigh, warm and impossibly soft. He traced gentle circles, relishing her subtle shivers.

"Mr. Bexley..." Emily's voice trembled, a mix of anticipation and shyness in her eyes. She rose slightly on her toes, leaning closer to him. "I need to rest. Could you carry me to the room?"

"Alright," Evander said with a nod, scooping Emily into his arms and carrying her toward the room.
