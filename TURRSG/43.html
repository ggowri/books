Chapter 44 Blood-sucking Vine

"Blood-sucking Vine!" Ciotti shrieked, panic twisting her face.

She hacked at the nearest tendril, but her dagger rang off it with a metallic clang, like striking steel.

"Hit them with light!" she shouted.

Evander didn't hesitate. He cranked his flashlight to full and swept the beam across the writhing vines.

The effect was immediate. They recoiled, oozing dark green sap that hissed as it ate into the chains like acid.

"They're afraid of it!" Evander yelled. He scurried a few feet forward, angling the beam toward Ciotti. "Move!"

Ciotti pushed herself faster, dagger slashing through the air. A spine raked across her arm—venom spread black over her skin—but she kept going, sweat soaking her uniform.

She was almost at the far side when a thick vine shot up, coiling tight around her ankle.

"Evander!" she screamed, pitching backward toward the abyss.

He lunged, seizing her safety rope. The chain rattled under their combined weight as they battled the vine's pull.

"Cut it off!" Evander roared, muscles straining.

Ciotti wrenched one hand free and stabbed down hard. Her blade pierced the base of the vine—sap burst out, splattering her face with a foul stench. The tendril recoiled, sliding back into the mist.

Ciotti crawled onto the rocks, collapsing, gasping for breath. Her ankle was swollen and black.

Evander let out a ragged sigh, his back soaked in cold sweat.

"You okay?" His voice cracked.

She waved him off, digging through her pack. She pulled out an antidote syringe and jammed it into her thigh with a grimace.

"Not dying today. But hurry—the vines will be back."

Evander swallowed hard and stepped onto the chains. Every nerve screamed at him to be careful. He kept his flashlight sweeping the sides, dagger clenched tight.

The vines slithered out again, but he was ready this time. Each blast of light drove them back into the mist.

Halfway across, he froze. Etched into the chain were faint markings.

Dates. Dozens of them, worn but legible—the last from fifty years ago. Beside it, a crude skull, identical to the one on the treasure map.

"That's my grandpa's mark," Ciotti's voice rang out from the far side. She'd seen it too.

"He crossed this bridge fifty years ago."

Evander's heart lurched.

Fifty years ago, Ciotti's grandfather had stood here. Had he reached the treasure? Why leave these marks behind?

In that split-second of distraction, a vine whipped from the shadows and coiled around his wrist.

"Ahh—!" Evander cried out as the spines pierced his skin. His flashlight slipped, clattering down the chain before vanishing into the green mist below.

Darkness swallowed him. More vines closed in, their tips scraping, hunting. The pain shot up his arm, making it almost impossible to grip his dagger.

"Evander!" Ciotti's voice rang out, desperate. The chains rattled—she was stepping back onto the bridge.

"Stay back!" he roared, summoning every ounce of strength. He drove his dagger into the vine strangling his wrist. Sap burst, foul and hissing. The tendril recoiled, and Evander crawled forward with what strength he had left.

Ciotti's beam slashed across the dark, forcing the vines to retreat.

At last, Evander rolled onto solid ground, chest heaving. Ciotti darted forward, slicing the last vine free. His wrist was already swollen and black, just like her ankle.

"Serum—now!" Her hands shook as she dug through her pack and pulled out the final syringe. She jabbed it into his arm.

The venom ebbed, pain fading little by little. Evander slumped against the rock wall, sweat soaking him. He looked back at the trembling chains swallowed by mist, a chill racing down his spine.

One slip, and he would've been another body lost to the abyss—like Madden and the others.

"Catch your breath," Ciotti said. She pulled the last half of a compressed biscuit from her pack, snapped it in two, and handed him a piece. "Eat. You'll need it."

Evander chewed slowly, eyes drifting to her bandaged ankle, then his wrist. Guilt churned in his gut. She wouldn't be hurt if she hadn't come to save him.

"Sorry," he muttered, voice low.

Ciotti blinked, then cracked a lopsided smile. "What are you apologizing for? We're partners. Ride or die, remember?"

Her smile faded, tone hardening. "But we've got to be sharper. My grandpa's diary was clear—the closer you get to the treasure, the deadlier it gets."

Evander nodded. She was right. Since entering Mortis Gorge, they'd been toeing the line of death—bridges collapsing, birds diving, vines poisoning. And all of it for one thing: the elixir his grandfather demanded.

"Do you even know what it is?" he asked suddenly. "What kind of elixir could be worth all this?"

Ciotti shook her head. "Not exactly. But according to my grandfather's diary, it doesn't grant immortality—only prolongs life."
