Chapter 27 Thrown to the Waves

Kimberly went totally limp. Evander scooped her up and carried her toward the sea.

Looked like he was seriously going to throw her in the water.

Without thinking, Kimberly wrapped her arms around his neck.

Their bare skin pressed together, and she could feel every taut line of muscle in his chest, the lingering heat radiating from him.

Her cheeks flushed hot. "What are you doing?"

"Feeding you to the fish."

Evander's tone gave nothing away, neither jest nor threat.

He carried her out of the cave with steady, unhurried steps, as if she weighed no more than a feather.

Kimberly froze, then let out a cold laugh.

"You wouldn't dare."

She was certain he was bluffing. What man would toss a woman he'd just been intimate with into the sea?

Besides, the lingering hunger in his eyes betrayed him.

Relaxing, she leaned into his shoulder, closing her eyes to rest.

The setting sun filtered through the swaying leaves above, dappling her face in warm gold. The scent of salt and sea breeze clung to the air.

Kimberly's muscles ached, and her thoughts were a tangled mess. An absurd feeling settled over her.

In just one day, she'd fallen from an untouchable heiress to a stranded woman entangled with a man on this forsaken island.

If Nadine found out, she'd never forgive her.

The thought stung, but Kimberly shoved it down.

What was done was done. It was too late for guilt now.

Evander carried her through a grove of coconut trees. The soft crunch of sand beneath his feet mingled with the whisper of the wind.

The sea breeze lifted his hair, tangling it with Kimberly's loose strands.

"Hey." Kimberly opened her eyes, her voice hoarse.

"Are you really going to feed me to the fish?"

Evander glanced down at her. "What else?"

The sun cast a golden outline along the sharp cut of his jaw.

"Why would I keep you around? I surely won't want you to run back to Nadine and tell her I had you on this island."

Kimberly's heart clenched. She wanted to promise she would keep silent, but even she knew how hollow that would sound.

They'd been besties for over twenty years. Even if she said nothing, Nadine would find out, one way or another.

"Then... go ahead."

Kimberly turned her face away, staring at the churning waves in the distance, her voice soft as a sigh. "I couldn't face her anyway."

She was still certain Evander wouldn't do it.

Evander said nothing and just kept walking.

As they went closer to the beach, the scent of the sea grew stronger, laced with the briny tang of shells.

Kimberly closed her eyes, her mind drifting back to their earlier passion.

"We're here."

Evander's voice cut through her thoughts.

Kimberly opened her eyes and found they were already at the water's edge.

The sun dipped into the horizon, painting the sky a brilliant orange, the shimmering waves glittering like scattered gold.

"It's beautiful," she murmured, awestruck.

Then, without warning, Evander let go.

With a loud splash, Kimberly plunged into the sea.

The icy water surged over her chest, its salty bite flooding her nose. She coughed violently, flailing to stay afloat.

"Evander, you jerk!"

She shoved the water from her face, eyes burning. Glaring at him as he stood on the shore, she screamed, "Are you trying to kill me?"

Evander waded into the shallows, his pant legs soaked by the waves. He held a bundle of clothes, smirking faintly.

"Kill you? That'd just pollute the ocean."

Kimberly spotted the clothes he was holding.

It was her white chiffon blouse and light blue shorts. She'd left them out to dry on the rocks, and now they were all stiff from sitting in the sun.

"We can't let you wear leaves all day, can we?"

Evander tossed the clothes to her.

"Put these on. We're heading to the helicopter. It's getting dark."

Kimberly caught the clothes, stunned.

The water swirled around her knees, its chill clearing the haze in her mind.

She watched Evander turn toward the helicopter, his silhouette stretched long by the setting sun. A bitter laugh escaped her as she shook her head.

This man's utterly unpredictable.

Kimberly bit her lip as she put on the clothes.

The chiffon stuck to her wet skin, showing the light marks Evander had left. At least she looked more normal now than when she was covered in leaves.

"Hey! Wait up!"

Kimberly hurried after him, her steps still unsteady.

Evander paused but didn't turn around.

"You..."

Kimberly hesitated, then forced the words out. "Why didn't you just drown me back there?"

Evander turned, the sunset igniting his eyes like embers. "Because I'm not done with you yet."

Kimberly's face flushed hot.

She glared at him, ready to spit something sharp, but the intensity in his eyes stopped her.

There was no mockery there, only a deep, unreadable current, like the undertow of the sea.

Suddenly, a rustling came from the coconut grove.

"What's that?"

Kimberly grabbed Evander's arm, her fingers whitening with fear.

Evander frowned, scanning the swaying trees.

Out of nowhere, a skinny wild boar charged out of the bushes. Its rough black hair stood on end, and its sharp tusks gleamed in the dimming light. Its small dark eyes fixed on Kimberly as it let out a deep, threatening growl.

"Run!"

Evander shoved Kimberly aside, but she just stood there like a statue.

The boar charged right at her, its feet pounding the sand, tusks pointed straight at her body.

She could smell its nasty breath hitting her face. But her legs wouldn't move at all.

Kimberly couldn't even scream as the boar closed in, its tusks almost touching her stomach.

In a flash, Evander lunged forward.

Using his momentum, he drove his knee into the boar's flank with a brutal kick.

The boar let out a piercing squeal, its massive body flung sideways, crashing into the sand in a cloud of dust.

Kimberly collapsed where she stood, her heart hammering wildly.

She stared at Evander's broad back as he stood between her and the beast, his silhouette steady and unyielding. His muscles, taut under the golden light, gave her an unexpected sense of safety.

The boar staggered up, shaking its head in fury before locking eyes on Evander and charging again.

"Watch out!" Kimberly cried.

Evander didn't flinch.

He crouched slightly, eyes sharp as a blade, ready to strike.

As the boar's tusks neared, he sidestepped, grabbing its bristly mane with one hand and its neck with the other. With a fluid twist, he swung himself onto its back.

His fists hammered the boar's skull, each blow ringing through the air like a crack of thunder.

Kimberly sat paralyzed on the sand, watching Evander's muscles bulge with effort. The boar wailed beneath him, its hooves clawing uselessly at the ground.

Her mind went blank.

Was this really the Evander Nadine called a "useless coward"?

He rode a raging boar like a beast unleashed, his eyes fierce, his fists iron.

The boar let out a final, pitiful cry, its massive body collapsing. Its legs twitched briefly, blood foaming at its mouth, then it went still.

Evander slid off, panting, sweat dripping from his jaw.

He turned to Kimberly and frowned when he saw her still sprawled on the sand.

"What, you are too scared to stand up?"

Kimberly snapped back to reality.

Evander's chest glistened with sweat, his fists smeared with blood.

She stared at him, opened her mouth, but couldn't get the words out.
