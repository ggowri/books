Chapter 85 The Brain Dissection Experiment

Three days later, the luxury cruise ship Royal Glory docked in London Harbor.

Morning light filtered through the mist, the damp sea air clinging to the decks.

Evander descended the gangway first, suitcase in hand, every inch the picture of effortless power in his tailored suit.

Behind him, Anya and Andrea followed side by side, dressed in nearly identical cream-colored dresses.

They whispered to each other, stealing glances at him when they thought he wasn't paying attention, excitement written all over their faces.

"London air beats being trapped on that ship," Anya said with a bright smile, breathing deeply.

She turned to Evander. "Should we head to the hotel with you, Mr. Bexley? I booked us rooms at the Imperial—right near Buckingham Palace."

Evander gave a slight nod. "Fine. We'll drop the bags first, then decide on the rest."

Andrea piped up softly, "I also found a few places for afternoon tea. There's this famous spot that—"

Her words cut off. A voice rang out behind them, all too familiar.

They turned. Lois was approaching, sharp in a pale blue suit.

Beside her walked Felix. His complexion was pale, his suit pressed to perfection, but his eyes carried something darker—something unreadable.

"Mr. Bexley," Lois greeted, her voice cool, polite to a fault. "Just getting off the ship as well?"

Her tone was distant, edged with ice. She hadn't forgiven Felix, but courtesy wouldn't let her ignore him outright.

Felix forced a smile, but his eyes kept drifting to Evander, studying him like a puzzle he was desperate to solve.

Anya and Andrea immediately moved in front of Evander, their gazes sharp as knives.

"What do you want now? Haven't you caused enough trouble on the cruise?"

Felix opened his mouth, but before he could answer, several men stepped out of a black sedan idling nearby. They moved with purpose, their boots hitting the pavement in unison.

The man in front wore a slate-gray uniform, a silver badge gleaming on his chest. He flipped open a black credential wallet. His voice was clipped, official.

"Mr. Bexley? We're with Scientific Paranormal Research. I'm Agent Fred White. You need to come with us for a study."

"Scientific Paranormal Research?" Anya's face drained of color. "The group that investigates supernatural phenomena? What do you want with him?"

Evander's stomach dropped. He knew exactly who they were. SPR's reputation wasn't about studying anything. They hunted people with abilities and used them as lab rats.

He stepped back, voice firm. "I don't have abilities. I'm not part of your research. Leave."

Fred only smiled coldly, then jerked his chin at the men behind him.

Evander tensed, ready for a fight, but a sting hit the back of his neck. Cold fire spread instantly through his veins. His knees buckled. Darkness closed in.

"Mr. Bexley!" Anya and Andrea screamed, lunging for him.

The agents shoved them back.

Lois went pale. She pushed forward, furious. "What the hell are you doing? Let him go!"

But Felix blocked her, his voice suddenly calm, soothing—too smooth.

"Relax. Mr. Bexley's just helping with a small experiment. He'll be back. Nothing to worry about."

Even as he spoke, his eyes flicked to Fred, a silent order: Get him out. Now.

Fred gave a sharp signal. Two agents hauled Evander's limp body up and shoved him into the waiting sedan. The car peeled away, swallowed by harbor traffic.

"Evander!" Anya tried to run after it, but Felix clamped down on her arm.

"Don't!" His grip was iron. "You can't go against SPR. They'll crush you."

Lois stared after the car until it vanished, realization dawning like a knife in the gut. She spun on Felix, her fury exploding.

"This was you. You tipped them off about Mr. Bexley, didn't you?"

Felix's eyes darted away, but he wouldn't admit it. "I just... I thought his abilities were special. He could contribute to science. Look, don't overthink this. He'll be fine."

Lois stared at him in disgust. She was completely done with him. "Felix, you make me sick. If anything happens to Mr. Bexley, I swear I'll make you pay."

She turned and stormed off. Anya and Andrea followed her, heading in the direction the sedan had gone.

Felix stood there alone, a cold smile creeping across his face.

Inside the black sedan, Evander was slowly coming to.

The anesthesia hadn't worn off completely. His whole body felt like dead weight, and he could barely keep his eyes open. Through the car window, he watched the city blur past until they stopped in front of some kind of hidden white building.

"Get him inside. The lab's ready for him." That was Fred's voice. Two guys in scrubs grabbed Evander under the arms and dragged him through the entrance.

The hallways were dimly lit, with weird charts plastered all over the walls. The whole place reeked of disinfectant and formaldehyde, the kind of smell that made people's skin crawl.

They hauled him into this massive lab filled with high-tech equipment. Several people in white coats and surgical masks were clustered around an operating table, like they were getting ready for something big.

They strapped Evander down tight. His wrists and ankles were bound with thick rope so he couldn't move an inch.

"We're starting the brain dissection. First, run a scan on his neural energy."

A man in glasses raised a strange device, lowering it toward Evander's head.

Evander strained against the restraints, muscles burning, but his body still wouldn't obey.

Then a voice cut through the sterile air, muffled by a surgical mask.

"Mr. Bexley. Stop struggling."

Evander's blood froze. He knew that voice.

"...Felix?"

The man chuckled darkly, tugging down his mask. "Sharp ears. Guess you really do have powers."

Evander's eyes blazed. "You! You sold me out to SPR? You want them to crack open my skull?"

Felix's laugh was wild, unhinged.

"Superpowers—that's what you have! Winning without looking at your cards, bending Lois and those twins around your finger? Don't play dumb."

His expression twisted with greed. "Once I extract that power source from your brain, I'll be the first noble with abilities. Lois will crawl back to me. Everyone will fear me. And you?"

He grinned, teeth bared. "You'll be remembered as a noble sacrifice for science."

Rage tore through Evander. No reasoning with him. He needed a way out.

Then instinct took over. His vision sharpened—X-ray mode flickered on.

And what he saw nearly made his heart stop.

Two shapes stood in the corner of the lab. Shrouded in white mist, their silhouettes were human... but wrong. Their eyes were hollow, empty.

"Ghosts?" Evander whispered.

He'd never believed in spirits. Yet here they were—and no one else seemed to notice.

Only him.

Heat flared at his finger. The Arcane Ring pulsed, glowing faintly. Almost without thinking, Evander lifted his hand and aimed at the apparitions.

A thin beam of light streaked out, striking both figures.

The ghosts convulsed violently. Their blank eyes snapped into focus—now filled with pure terror.

They locked eyes for a beat—then the two spirits slowly sank to their knees before Evander, bowing like worshippers.

His pulse spiked. He forced his thoughts into words.

Help me. Take them down. Untie me.

The spirits shot forward.

What followed was pure nightmare fuel.

Fred stepped closer with a device in hand—then froze. His face turned scarlet, eyes bulging as if invisible fingers were squeezing his throat. The device hit the floor with a clatter.

His assistants jerked the same way, clawing at their necks until their bodies convulsed and collapsed in a heap.

Felix's blood drained. He spun to run—only to be slammed against the wall by something unseen. He crumpled, groaning in pain.

Then his gaze snapped to Evander. The ropes around him were untying themselves, loops loosening one by one like ghostly hands at work.

"What the hell—?" His voice cracked. "You... you're doing this with your powers?"

He couldn't see the spirits. To him, it was Evander, some terrifying hidden ability. And it made him want that "power source" even more.

Evander ripped free, landing hard on his feet. He stalked straight to Felix.

Felix tried crawling back, but Evander's boot connected with his temple. Felix went limp, knocked cold.

The spirits hovered at his side like loyal sentinels.

Evander glanced at the sprawled bodies. His voice was low, commanding. "Get me out of here."

They obeyed, gliding ahead.

Down the pitch-dark corridors, the SPR guards never stood a chance.

Each time they appeared, the spirits struck first—silent, invisible, dropping men to the floor without a sound.
