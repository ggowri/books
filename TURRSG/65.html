Chapter 66 A Spark in the Sunlight

Elsa hesitated for a moment at Evander's invitation. Doing yoga by the pool, just the two of them, felt kind of personal.

But then she remembered how Evander had helped bring down the Night Owls, and she felt like she owed him. Turning him down might come off as unfriendly.

Plus, it was Sunday, her rare day off. Some relaxation could be nice. She met Evander's sincere gaze and smiled.

"Sounds like a plan! But," she teased, "you've got to stop calling me Mayor."

She paused, a playful twinkle in her eye. "We're friends now. I'm only a little older than you, so let's stick to first names. It's way more chill."

Evander felt his spirits lift. Dropping the formalities meant Elsa was opening up, which made him happy.

"Got it, Elsa. Just call me Evander, then."

"Deal."

Elsa smiled and followed him upstairs. The soft light in the stairwell caught her cream dress, making it shimmer slightly. Her steps were light, free from the usual seriousness of her official role.

On the second floor, Evander ducked into a guest room and came back with a blue yoga outfit. It was Ciotti's, never worn, and seemed like it would fit Elsa perfectly.

"The changing room's through there. Fresh towels and everything else you need are inside."

Elsa took the outfit, the soft fabric brushing her fingers, giving her a little thrill. It had been forever since she let herself relax like this, buried in endless paperwork and meetings, rarely even wearing casual clothes. She smiled, stepping into the dressing room to change.

When Elsa stepped out of the changing room, Evander's breath caught in his throat. At thirty-two, her skin was fair and flawless. Years of yoga had kept her in perfect shape—her tight yoga pants showed off her long, slender legs, and her fitted top revealed a glimpse of her slim waist and delicate navel.

Her chest rose and fell with each breath, radiating a mature beauty that was both elegant and alluring.

"Is something up? Does it not fit?" Elsa asked, a bit shy under his gaze, tugging at the hem of her top.

"You look great. It suits you," Evander said, quickly looking away to hide his reaction. He slipped into another room to change into his own black yoga outfit, his athletic build accentuated by the tight fabric.

"Let's head to the balcony," he said, leading the way and avoiding eye contact to keep his cool.

The second-floor balcony was spacious, with an open-air pool on one side. The water sparkled in the sunlight, surrounded by potted plants that added a faint floral scent to the air.

Evander grabbed two yoga mats from a storage area and set them up near the pool. The sunlight warmed the mats invitingly.

"Let's start with some basic poses. Take it easy, no rush." Elsa stepped onto her mat, took a deep breath, and began stretching gracefully. She raised her arms, her back arching slightly, her silhouette striking in the sunlight.

Evander joined her, trying to focus but unable to stop glancing at Elsa. The memory of her stepping out in that yoga outfit was hard to shake.

Soon, Elsa fell into a rhythm, closing her eyes and fully immersing herself in the stretch. The effects of the Focus Elixir lingered, and she was acutely aware of Evander's gaze, its warmth making her cheeks flush and her heart race. She chalked it up to not being close to a guy in ages, never suspecting the coffee.

They practiced for an hour. Sweat beaded on Elsa's forehead, strands of hair clinging to her cheeks, adding a relaxed beauty. She paused, catching her breath, her thighs aching from a high-intensity pose. It felt like a slight muscle strain.

Evander noticed and handed her a clean towel. "Take a break. You must be tired."

Elsa's fingers brushed against his as she took the towel, sending an electric shock through her body. Her heart raced and she jerked back, trying to convince herself it was just because she hadn't been close to a guy in forever. But that spark... she couldn't deny she wanted to feel it again.

"Evander, could you help me out with a leg massage? I pushed too hard, and my thighs are sore."

Evander's eyes widened—this was what he'd been hoping for.

He nodded quickly. "Sure, sit down. I'll help."

Elsa sat on the yoga mat, stretching one leg out and lifting it slightly. Evander knelt beside her, gently placing his hands on her calf. Elsa let out a soft gasp. The intense tingle returned, spreading through her body, leaving her limbs weak and her eyes fluttering shut in comfort.

She felt the warmth of Evander's fingertips and the strength in his palms, each knead hitting her sore spots perfectly, melting her tension.

"Evander, could you move up a bit?" Her voice was soft, her eyes hazy as she looked at him. "The inside of my thigh hurts, too. Could you massage there?"

Evander's pulse surged.

He hesitated, playing it cool. "Are you sure? I mean, you're..."

"It's fine. We're friends, and it's just a leg massage." Elsa's cheeks flushed, but she continued, "It really hurts. Please help."

Evander hid a grin, pretending to be reluctant. "Alright, but it might sting a bit."

His hands moved slowly upward, past her knee, nearing her inner thigh. The skin there was smoother and warmer, and as he massaged, Elsa's breathing grew rapid, her body trembling slightly. She felt the root of her thighs getting wet.

His fingertips were grazing her inner thigh through the thin yoga pants. The tingling grew overwhelming, her body quivering uncontrollably.
