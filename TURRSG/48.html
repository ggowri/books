Chapter 49 Auction

Three days later, a line of sleek black limousines stretched in front of the prestigious Platinum Grace Auction House in Nogales.

Uniformed attendants in tailored tuxedos stood at perfect attention, opening car doors with polished precision as one guest after another stepped into the limelight.

Word of the "Royal Secret Collection" auction had exploded across the city days earlier, drawing elite collectors and magnates even from neighboring towns.

On the top floor, the grand banquet hall blazed with brilliance, crystal chandeliers scattering light like diamonds across the crowd. Laughter and murmurs mingled in the air, every face tinged with anticipation.

When Evander and his friend Juno entered side by side, heads turned instantly.

Evander wore a tailored Italian black suit, understated save for a sapphire cufflink at his collar. But what caught eyes was the black ring gleaming on his middle finger.

Juno gave Evander a sly nudge, his eyes sweeping the hall.

"Look—Oliver's here. And Nadine and Denny, too. Things are about to get interesting."

Evander didn't answer, his gaze drifting over familiar figures.

Kimberly, elegant in lavender, clung to Oliver's arm.

Naomi followed close, her smile brittle and strained.

Nadine stood pale in a black dress, Denny leaning in to whisper something at her ear.

Iris wore a cream-colored suit, her teacher William at her side.

But it was Aileen who commanded the room from the corner.

She shimmered in a silver mermaid gown, the same diamond necklace glinting at her throat.

At her side stood a man in crisp military uniform, the star on his epaulets blazing under the light—Major General Jack Dawson, her boyfriend.

Juno lowered his voice. "Jack's family's no joke. His father's a general, serious influence. Word is he's gunning for that crown hairpin Aileen's been obsessed with. Looks like tonight's her shot."

Evander stayed silent, eyes flicking toward the backstage area.

Ciotti wasn't here yet, but she'd already messaged him—she was behind the curtains, keeping watch to make sure nothing went wrong.

At exactly nine a.m., the auction began.

The auctioneer strode onto the stage, velvet tray in hand. Resting on it was the first item—the Royal Diamond Bracelet.

"Lot one," he announced grandly. "An eighteenth-century bracelet custom-made for the French royal family, set with thirty-six pear-shaped diamonds totaling fifty-eight carats. Opening bid: fifty million dollars. Increments: no less than one million."

Before the echo of his voice had faded, Denny shot his paddle up.

"Fifty million!" he declared, flashing Nadine a fawning grin. "Nad, this bracelet would look perfect on you."

Nadine's lips curved into the faintest smile, but she said nothing.

From across the room, Evander raised his paddle.

"One hundred million."

The hall gasped. He'd doubled the bid without blinking. Heads swiveled toward him, whispers rising like a wave.

Denny's smirk faltered. He clenched his jaw, then barked, "Two hundred million!"

Evander's reply was smooth, unshaken: "One billion."

The room fell silent. Even the auctioneer froze, eyes wide.

To leap from fifty million to a billion wasn't just a bid—it was dominance, plain and absolute.

Denny's face flushed crimson. He darted a glance at Nadine, then Oliver.

But in the end, he lowered his paddle, too cowed to press further.

"One billion, going once… going twice… sold! Congratulations, sir!" The auctioneer slammed the gavel, his voice ringing with disbelief.

Evander rose calmly to his feet, turning toward Kimberly.

"This Royal Diamond Bracelet is for Ms. Nelson."

The hall erupted again—gasps, applause, murmurs.

Kimberly's eyes widened, her cheeks flushing pink as she cast him a shy look.

Oliver's lips curled into a satisfied smile. He inclined his head at Evander, clearly pleased.

Naomi's expression curdled, envy flashing as she glared at Kimberly. Nadine went ghostly pale, nails biting into the fabric of her skirt.

Beside Evander, Juno let out a low whistle and laughed.

"Bold move. Oliver's probably seeing you in a whole new light now."

Evander didn't answer. He only sat back down, his face calm, though his thoughts churned.

This was only the beginning.

The second item was unveiled—a set of royal emerald jewelry.

The necklace's stones gleamed crystal-clear, glowing with a deep green radiance. Matching earrings and a ring completed the dazzling set.

"Lot two," the auctioneer boomed, his voice echoing through the hall. "A nineteenth-century British royal emerald ensemble. The main stone weighs eighty-two carats. Starting bid: eighty million dollars!"

Andrew Sterling, Nogales' wealthiest tycoon, wasted no time. He lifted his paddle.

"One hundred million."

Evander raised his own paddle, his tone calm, almost casual.

"One billion."

A ripple of shock coursed through the room.

Andrew arched a brow, then chuckled. "Young man, don't get carried away. Two billion."

"Five billion," Evander said evenly, his voice cutting through the hush like a blade.

The hall fell dead silent. Women gasped, hands flying to their mouths.

Andrew's smile stiffened, his eyes narrowing as he studied Evander. Then, with a slow shake of his head, he lowered his paddle.

"Five billion, going once… going twice… sold!" The auctioneer's hammer cracked down, his voice brimming with awe.

Evander rose to his feet, gaze locking on Iris.

"This royal emerald set is for Ms. Lennox of Summit Group."

Gasps rippled through the audience. All eyes turned to Iris.

She froze, staring at Evander. Her eyes flickered with a storm of emotions—anger, disbelief, something softer hidden beneath.

William leaned in, whispering at her ear. She blinked, pulled back to herself, and gave Evander the barest nod.

For a fleeting second, gratitude sparked in her eyes before she looked away.

She was still furious with him. But five billion spent in her name? That hit its mark, whether she wanted it to or not. Her pulse stuttered, betraying her resolve.

At Evander's side, Juno let out a low whistle and clapped his shoulder.

"Really pulling out all the stops to make amends, huh?"

Evander didn't answer. His eyes stayed on Iris, guilt pressing heavy on his chest.

He knew jewelry couldn't erase the hurt he'd caused her. But maybe—just maybe—it could show that he'd never meant to wound her in the first place.

Finally, the last and most anticipated item was unveiled—the crown hairpin.

The auctioneer advanced with reverent care, a velvet tray balanced in his hands. Under the chandeliers, the crown hairpin shimmered with a golden glow, the pigeon-blood ruby at its center blazing like captured fire.

"Lot three," the auctioneer declared. "A crown hairpin created for Queen Victoria's Diamond Jubilee in 1897, featuring a twelve-carat pigeon-blood ruby and twenty-eight white diamonds. A singular piece in the world. Starting bid: three hundred million dollars, with increments no less than five million."

No sooner had he finished than Jack Dawson raised his paddle.

"Three hundred million," he said, turning to Aileen with a smile meant only for her. "Aileen, this belongs to you."

Her answering smile brimmed with expectation.

But Evander lifted his paddle, his voice cutting through the room.

"Three billion."

A collective gasp rippled through the hall. Jack's eyes widened, and even Aileen froze. Three billion was far beyond his reach.

Jack scowled, forcing out through gritted teeth, "Three-point-one billion!"

Evander didn't flinch. His tone was light, almost casual.

"Ten billion."

The hall went utterly still. You could hear the sound of breath being held.

Jack's face blanched. His jaw tightened, but in the end, he lowered his paddle. He didn't dare push further.

"Ten billion, going once… going twice… sold!" The auctioneer's voice cracked as the gavel came down, disbelief written across his face.

Evander rose. His gaze found Aileen, steady and unwavering.

"This crown hairpin belongs to one person here—and that's Ms. Burton, editor-in-chief of LUXERA magazine."

The room buzzed with shock.

Aileen's hand flew to her mouth, eyes shining with tears. She stood and bowed deeply.

"Thank you, sir. May I know your name?"

"Evander Bexley," he said with a faint smile.

Aileen stiffened. The name struck her like lightning.

Three days ago, she'd received an email—subject line: Perfect Sex and Love—signed by Evander Bexley. The man she was meant to meet at the western suburb castle tonight… was him.

Her heart thudded, heat rising to her cheeks. She dipped her head again.

"Thank you, Mr. Bexley."

Beside her, Jack looked ready to explode. His fiancée had just accepted a ten-billion-dollar gift from another man—in front of the entire city's elite. Humiliation burned through him like acid, but he forced his hands still.

Evander had just thrown down nearly twenty billion like pocket change. Picking a fight now would be suicide.

Across the room, Nadine's pain cut deep.

She was his wife. His wife—and yet Evander had showered priceless treasures on other women while not sparing her so much as a glance. The ache was unbearable.

She clenched her fists so hard her nails bit into her palms, fighting back tears.

When the auction finally drew to a close, Juno fell in beside Evander as they exited into the night air.

"You really threw the gauntlet tonight," he said with a low whistle. "Jack won't take this lying down, and Denny's bound to stab you in the back first chance he gets."

Evander's reply was firm, his eyes like steel.

"Let them try. If they want to play, I'll play."
