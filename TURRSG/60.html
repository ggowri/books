Chapter 61 Mr. Bexley Has Been Kidnapped

The car moved smoothly through the evening light. Outside, the bustling city slowly transformed into the serene beauty of the villa district.

Emily leaned against the window, tapping it excitedly as they passed by. "Look, Mr. Bexley! That's the rose garden at Aunt Elsa's place. She trims the roses into heart shapes every year around this time. They're stunning."

Evander glanced where she pointed. Sure enough, clusters of red roses bloomed vibrantly in the fading sunlight, climbing gracefully along the wrought-iron fence with an almost deliberate romantic flair.

He nodded slightly, but his attention returned to the velvet gift box on the console. Inside was a sapphire brooch, a piece he'd meticulously verified. The crest on the back matched the unique style of the Norwegian royal family from the eighteenth century. It was perfect for Elsa.

Aileen, sitting next to her sister, gave Emily a knowing smile before turning to Evander. "Aunt Elsa can be pretty serious, especially during formal events. Don't try too hard—just be yourself. I'll introduce you first, so you won't feel too much pressure."

"Thanks for the heads-up." Evander met her eyes briefly. "I'll remember that."

The car slowed, stopping in front of a grand villa. The building, with its pale beige stone, exuded a quiet authority. Two white marble statues stood guard at the entrance, and freshly cut roses lined the stairs on either side.

At the door, attendants in black tailcoats stepped forward, opening the car doors with practiced elegance.

As they stepped out, the soft sound of a violin floated from inside the villa, blending with the gentle hum of conversation. It was lively, but not overwhelming.

"Aunt Elsa!" Emily called out, rushing ahead, her lavender gown fluttering like butterfly wings as she bounded up the steps.

Evander and Aileen followed more calmly. Once inside, Evander spotted Elsa near the fireplace, engaged in conversation with two guests.

She was striking—more so than on TV. Her features were refined, her white suit tailored perfectly to emphasize her straight shoulders. Her hair was styled in a low chignon, and the only jewelry she wore was a simple pearl necklace. Her presence alone filled the room.

Noticing the commotion, Elsa turned. Her gaze briefly landed on Emily before shifting to Evander, assessing him with a sharp but not unfriendly look.

"Aunt Elsa, this is Mr. Evander Bexley, the one I told you about," Aileen said, stepping forward with a warm smile. "He brought you a birthday gift."

Elsa's eyes moved to the velvet box in Evander's hands. She nodded slightly. "You're too kind. Your presence is gift enough."

Her voice was lower than it sounded on television, carrying the calm authority of someone used to leadership. She added, "Mr. Bexley, you're quite young and already so successful. I've heard about the auction."

Evander's heart skipped a beat. She knew.

He quickly offered the gift box with both hands. "It's just a small token. This brooch was crafted for the Norwegian royal family in the eighteenth century. I thought you might appreciate it."

Elsa accepted the box, and when she opened it, a flicker of surprise crossed her eyes. Her fingers traced the cool sapphire before she examined the crest on the back. When her gaze returned to Evander, there was a hint of warmth in her eyes.

She smiled, "I didn't expect you to know so much about royal artifacts. This crest is indeed from an old branch of my family. Very thoughtful of you."

"Just something I stumbled upon in my studies," Evander replied modestly, though he silently exhaled in relief. The first step had gone well.

Emily eagerly leaned in, showing her wrist. "Aunt Elsa, look! Mr. Bexley gave me this too—a royal pearl bracelet! Isn't it beautiful?"

Elsa's eyes flicked to the bracelet, then to Emily's flushed cheeks. Her expression was unreadable, but she didn't comment, simply signaling an attendant nearby.

She instructed, "Take Mr. Bexley's gift for safekeeping. And bring him a glass of champagne."

The party was in full swing. Around them, politicians and business elites mingled in groups. A few recognized Evander and approached him, their questions often circling back to the auction and his background.

Evander handled himself with calm precision, never revealing Norman's true identity while subtly showcasing his own strengths. Several guests nodded in approval, impressed by his poise.

Aileen occasionally chimed in with perfectly timed comments, and together they appeared like longtime allies.

Meanwhile, Emily stayed close to Evander, pulling him along to admire the villa's décor—sharing stories about oil paintings one moment, then dragging him to the terrace to enjoy the night view the next. Her anticipation was clear.

Evander knew what she was hoping for. But tonight wasn't the right time.

As darkness settled over the estate, warm golden lights illuminated the garden. Buffet tables were set up among the roses, silver cutlery gleaming under the soft glow. Waiters moved through the guests, offering food as violins played a gentle, elegant tune.

Evander was holding a porcelain plate, about to enjoy two pieces of foie gras, when a waiter in a black tailcoat approached him quietly.

The man whispered, "Mr. Bexley, someone is asking for you at the entrance. They said it's urgent."

Evander frowned, suspicion stirring. At this hour, who could it possibly be? Ciotti had already left, and Juno hadn't mentioned coming.

Setting down his fork, he nodded toward Emily and Aileen across the room. "I'll step out to meet someone. I'll be back soon."

Emily, mid-bite with a macaron, looked up instantly. "Want me to come with you?"

"No need. It's probably nothing." Evander gave her a reassuring smile before following the waiter outside.

Crossing the lively hall, he stepped into the evening air. A cool breeze carried a hint of chill.

He looked around, but the entrance was empty. Only the two marble statues at the gate stood silently in the shadows.

"Where is he?" Evander turned to question the waiter.

But before he could finish, a sharp blow struck the back of his neck.

Pain flared, his vision went black, and he crumpled to the ground.

"Mr. Bexley!" Emily's panicked shout echoed as she rushed forward, with Aileen right behind her.

By the time they reached the door, Evander was being dragged into a black, unmarked sedan by two masked men. The car door slammed shut, and in the next instant, the vehicle sped away like a shot arrow.

"Mr. Bexley!" Tears filled Emily's eyes as she started to chase after it, but Aileen grabbed her arm.

"Don't! It's too dangerous!" Aileen's voice was firm, though her face was pale.

She took a deep breath. "We need to get Aunt Elsa. She's in charge of the city's security. If anyone can help, it's her."

They hurried back inside.

Elsa was conversing with several political colleagues, but the alarm on their faces immediately caught her attention. "What's going on?"

"Aunt Elsa!" Aileen burst out, panting. "They took Mr. Bexley—right outside the building! Two men in black just knocked him unconscious and dragged him into an unmarked black car!"

Elsa's expression hardened, her presence suddenly sharp as steel.

For something like this to happen during her birthday gathering, at her villa, and to a guest under her protection—it was a blatant challenge. As Deputy Mayor in charge of security, this was a direct affront to her authority.

She quickly pulled out her phone and dialed, her voice authoritative and commanding.

"This is Elsa. Initiate emergency response protocols now. Track a black, unmarked sedan that just left my villa. My guest is inside. I want every camera in the city checked until it's found, and his safety ensured. Also, dispatch patrol units nearby. Lock down all major exits from the district."

Ending the call, she placed a reassuring hand on Emily's trembling shoulder.

"Don't worry. Security's already on it—we'll find Mr. Bexley soon."

Her gaze shifted toward the gates, eyes narrowed with determination.

Whoever dared to pull this stunt had to be either desperate or part of something bigger and more dangerous.
