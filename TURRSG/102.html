Chapter 103 Between Helheim and Home

Helheim's dungeon held no trace of light, only a suffocating blackness and a cold that bit as sharp as winter steel.

Within that gloom, Felix crouched on the frozen ground. His suit was caked with dirt, and the hair that had once been neatly combed now hung in complete disarray.

For three days, it felt as if the world had forgotten him. All he heard was his own breathing and the steady beat of his heart, while a crushing silence washed over him like a tide.

Felix thought he would remain trapped forever, condemned to become one of the dead. Then came a long creak as the iron door opened. A faint beam of light pierced the darkness, forcing him to squint.

When he looked up, he saw Hela in a deep violet robe, moving slowly toward him, her presence carrying the frigid air of the Underworld.

Instinctively, Felix stepped back, fear written across his face. Three days of captivity had left him with a profound sense of dread for the goddess of death.

"Your three days are up. You are free to go now," Hela said, her voice cold and steady, untouched by emotion.

She continued, "But before you go, I must warn you. Evander isn't someone you can afford to cross. It was his choice to lock you up, and that was his way of putting you in your place."

"What?" Felix's head shot up, fear burning into fury. "That commoner was the one who had you lock me in here?"

Hela gave a slight nod. "Yes." Then, with a hint of warning in her tone, she said, "Once you are free, show him the respect he deserves. Do not set yourself against him again.

"Otherwise, the punishment you face next time will be a hundred times worse. You might never leave Helheim but remain here forever as one of the dead."

With that, Hela turned toward the dungeon door. "Come with me. I will take you back."

Silently, Felix followed her out. When he stepped into the open and saw the ghostly flames of Helheim once more, there was no gratitude in his heart. Rage consumed him.

He was livid that Evander had dared to throw him into a place like this to suffer. He even vowed that Evander would pay. He would tear him apart and make him taste a torment far worse than death.

On the surface, Felix appeared obedient as he walked behind Hela, but hatred had already taken root deep inside him. Her warning was cast aside without a thought.

Hela led him out of the dungeon, while far away in his own home, a heavy tension lingered in the air.

Lois stood at the bedside, her brow furrowed as she looked at Felix lying motionless on the bed. Nearby, Mourid and Maria paced back and forth, their faces etched with worry.

"Princess Lois, we already gave him the elixir with water, so why isn't he waking up?" Maria's voice broke as she spoke, and she reached out to touch Felix's cheek.

"He still isn't breathing, and his body is ice cold. You wouldn't have tricked us... right?" Mourid echoed her, his tone carrying a faint edge of threat.

He added, "We already agreed to break off the engagement, Princess Lois. But if you gave us a fake elixir and Felix dies, we'll make sure Evander, that damn commoner, pays with his life."

Seeing their panic only made Lois more anxious inside, though she forced herself to stay calm. "That's impossible. The elixir Mr. Bexley gave me will work. Give it time. Felix might wake up any moment."

Before she even finished speaking, Felix's eyelids twitched. Mourid and Maria rushed forward, and Lois leaned in, tense with anticipation.

Felix's eyes opened slowly, bloodshot and wild, like someone who had just clawed his way back from hell. He shot upright on the bed, scanning the room in a frenzy.

The moment he spotted Lois, the madness in his look twisted into pure hatred. He roared, "Where is that fucking commoner? Where is he? I'll kill him! I'll make him pay for locking me in the dungeons of Helheim!"

The force of Felix's voice startled Lois.

She instinctively stepped back before steadying herself and telling Mourid and Maria, "There you go. Felix is awake now. It's time you honored your word. Have him sign the agreement to end the engagement."

Seeing that Felix had truly woken up, Mourid and Maria were overjoyed and hurried to his side.

Maria urged, "Oh, Felix, you're finally awake. Quickly, sign this agreement to break the engagement. Princess Lois saved your life. We can't go back on our promise."

"What? Break the engagement?" Felix sneered, his gaze locked firmly on Lois.

He snapped, "Absolutely not! Lois is my fiancée. Why should the engagement be broken? This is all Evander's doing. I'll never sign this. He won't get to marry her, and I'll see to it he pays with his life!"

Lois had not expected Felix to stay this stubborn even after waking up. Her tone turned cold. "Felix, whatever feelings we once had are long gone. Forcing us to stay tied together makes no sense. And after everything you've done, there's no way I could ever be with you again."

"Shut the fuck up!" Felix suddenly pushed to his feet. He grabbed her wrist and slapped her across the face, the sharp crack echoing through the room.

Lois' cheek flushed red in an instant, and tears welled in her eyes.

Mourid and Maria were startled, rushing to restrain Felix. "Felix, have you lost your damn mind? How could you strike Princess Lois?"

Lois pressed a hand to her burning cheek, fighting back the tears. She pulled out her phone, hurried out of the room, and dialed Evander.
