Chapter12 A Dirty Trick

Night was falling.

The restaurant Evander had booked glowed softly under lantern light.

A tinkling bell sounded as they pushed the door open.

William took the window seat, watching the darkening sky, casually rolling a cup in his hands.

"The coffee here's pretty good. I'll have the owner pack some beans for you to take home."

"You really know your stuff," Evander smiled as he sat down.

Iris settled beside him, still holding the summit materials.

"I think those ethics guidelines should cover cross-cultural stuff too..." she started.

"No rush." William raised a hand to stop her, his eyes gently sweeping over them. "Tonight, no work talk. Think of this as my way of celebrating your success."

Just then, the waiter brought over three bottles of Lafite red wine.

Evander poured a full glass for William and half a glass for Iris.

William's eyes flicked between them, nodding in approval.

They chatted casually until William's phone buzzed on the table.

He glanced at the screen, frowning slightly.

"The president of the Academy of Sciences."

He answered, listened for a moment, then stood up.

"Emergency at the Academy. I need to head back."

"I'll drive you," Evander offered, starting to get up.

"No, no." William waved him off, grabbing his briefcase. "You two enjoy your evening. The bill's on me."

At the door, he turned back and pointed at the table.

"That seafood dish is excellent. Eat up."

The door closed softly, leaving just Evander and Iris in the room.

The atmosphere suddenly felt a bit off. They looked at each other and laughed.

"He's always like that," Iris said, picking up her fork and placing a piece of meat in his plate.

Halfway through the meal, she suddenly unbuttoned her jacket, tiny beads of sweat appearing on her nose.

"Is the AC off? It's so hot in here."

Evander sensed something was wrong too.

The room was comfortably cool, yet inside, his body felt like a slow-burning flame spreading from his stomach to every limb.

He looked at Iris. Her cheeks were as red as ripe cherries, her gaze unfocused, and she was biting her lower lip.

"Something's wrong." Evander shot up, knocking over his chair. "There's something in the food!"

Just as he said it, Iris swayed, steadying herself with the table.

"I feel so dizzy..."

Evander rushed to support her. When his fingers touched her skin, she was burning hot.

"We're leaving!" He swept her up in his arms.

She buried her head in his neck, her breath scorching.

As he pushed open the door, he caught a glimpse of a shadow at the end of the hallway—the same server who'd brought their food.

Seeing Evander emerge, the guy turned and ran toward the stairs.

"Stop!" Evander shouted, carrying Iris as he gave chase.

By the time they reached the first floor, the server had vanished.

With no time to waste, he carried Iris out of the restaurant and flagged down a taxi.

"Azure Cove Residence! Hurry!"

The driver glanced in the rearview mirror at Iris's flushed face and Evander's sweating forehead. Wisely, he kept quiet and merged into traffic.

"Evander..." Iris murmured, shifting in his arms, her voice thick with heat. "So hot..."

"Hang in there, we're almost there," he reassured her, holding her wandering hands still, his heart pounding like a drum.

He knew Denny wouldn't let this go easily, but he hadn't expected such a dirty trick. Thank God William had left early, or it would've been even more humiliating.

As the taxi approached Azure Cove Residence, Evander felt a surge of relief.

The security system here was set up by his grandfather—three layers of invisible electric fences, with alarms triggered by any approaching vehicles.

Sure enough, as the car reached the gate, a security guard came over to check IDs.

Evander gave his name and showed the property deed before the gate slowly lifted.

In the rearview mirror, he noticed a black sedan had stopped about a hundred yards away, clearly blocked from entering.

"Stop here," he said.

He paid the driver, then carried Iris out of the car.

The sea breeze was cool, helping him clear his head a bit.

Iris was barely conscious. She clutched his shirt and murmured his name.

He strode into the villa and carried her straight to his bedroom.

The AC was running full blast.

He placed her on the bed and pressed a damp towel to her forehead.

Iris whimpered softly, grabbing his wrist, her eyes hazy as she looked at him.

"Don't leave... please..."

"I won't leave." he reassured.

He sat on the edge of the bed, his other hand gently patting her back.

"Just sleep a bit. You'll feel better once the meds wear off."

The AC seemed to have lost its cooling power entirely.

Evander watched Iris toss and turn, her hair damp with sweat, lips a vivid red, each breath radiating heat.

His mind started to drift, temples pounding, feeling like a fire inside him was burning even hotter.
