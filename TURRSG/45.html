Chapter 46 Ring

Ciotti froze, her breath catching in her throat.

Before her rose three walls stacked with gold bars, a mountain of coins scraping the ceiling, and heaps of pearls and agates blazing so brightly they hurt the eyes.

Her fingertips trembled as though the sheer weight of the fortune pressed down on her chest.

This was enough to buy half a country.

And her grandfather… wanted to leave it all to her?

Her voice broke, tears streaking her cheeks. "Grandpa, this is too much. I don't want all this—I just want you to come home with me."

The old man sighed, rough fingers brushing her tears away with surprising warmth.

"Silly girl. This was always meant for you. Your father always said I was money-mad. He never understood—I only wanted enough to keep you safe for life." His gaze drifted to the stone altar in the hot spring's center, his voice trailing.

"Who'd have thought I'd spend twenty years waiting for this moment."

While they were lost in the tide of reunion, Evander drifted toward the bookshelf in the corner.

Pearl light spilled across rows of ancient tomes, their yellowed pages glowing faintly, their gilded titles gleaming in the dim chamber.

He pulled a few free. Mystery of Metaphysics revealed intricate formation diagrams that twisted and tangled like mazes. The Martial Arts Secret Book bore bold, forceful annotations in thick black ink, the script brimming with power.

And then his eyes caught on a worn leather spine at the very bottom: The Tesseract Chronicle.

The moment his hand touched it, a faint shiver crawled up his arm.

Evander opened the book.

On the first page was an illustration of a pitch-black ring. A spiral pattern coiled across its face like a serpent sleeping in the dark. The ink was blurred with age, but the words below remained clear.

The Arcane Ring.

The text named it a lost heirloom of a medieval duke, a relic said to tear through time itself, powerful enough to topple dynasties.

Evander's heart skipped a beat as he traced the faded descriptions.

It spoke of nine powers bound within the ring.

The Inventory Cube—a pocket space that could swallow anything in sight, from a pebble to a mountain.

X-ray Function—vision that could cut through walls and armor, sharper than any blade.

Magic Light Release—a force deadlier than cannons, blasting through steel with ease.

Time and Space Control—to slow, freeze, even reverse the flow of time.

Mind Control—to bend wills like puppets on a string.

Illusion Projection—phantoms so real they could fool every sense.

Damage Transfer—to hurl an attacker's strike back at them.

Evander flipped further, breath catching.

The final notes were written in darker ink, almost a warning: After the great explosion of magic power, the magic core shattered. It needs to be nourished by the vaginal juices of twenty-seven royal noblewomen to regain its magic...

Every time the ring is nourished by three women, it releases one type of magic.

"Evander, what are you looking at?" Ciotti's voice suddenly came from behind.

Evander quickly put the book back on the shelf.

But his finger accidentally knocked off a copy of Star Chart. The pages scattered on the floor.

As he crouched down to pick up the book, he noticed the golden bedside.

A black ring lay there quietly, with the spiral pattern on the ring face exactly the same as the one in the ancient book's illustration.

The old man ambled over, his eyes following Evander's to the ring. His Adam's apple bobbed quietly.

He'd been rubbing this ring for three long decades. His fingertips were so familiar with every groove.

But until now, he'd never thought there was anything special about it.

"What? You like it?" the old man chuckled. "It's long since lost its power. But since you're my granddaughter's fiancé, consider it a welcome gift."

He strolled over, plucked the ring up as if it were nothing more than scrap metal, and tossed it to Evander like a worthless trinket.

The moment Evander's fingers touched the ring, a cold shiver ran up his hand. Its surface was as smooth as polished marble, as if it had been caressed repeatedly.

He slipped it onto his middle finger, and to his surprise, it fit perfectly, like it was custom-made for him. The ring face pressed against his palm, sending a faint quiver, as if there was a tiny creature stirring inside.

"Grandpa, we really gotta leave now," Ciotti said, her backpack bulging with carefully-selected agates, carvings, and some ancient royal ornaments that had witnessed centuries of history.

"Come with us, please. Just pay a visit to Grandma's grave, just this once," she pleaded, her eyes brimming with hope.

The old man shook his head and pointed at the wooden crates beside the bed. "I'll stay here and watch these things."

Inside those dozen crates, bottles were neatly arranged, with labels like "Focus Elixir" and "Force-aggregating Powder".

"These elixirs are something else. One pill can keep me full for ten days and make me as strong as a bull. I'm living like a king here. And when I'm feeling lonesome, I can always read a book," the old man explained, a contented smile on his face.

Ciotti was about to argue more, but Evander gave her a gentle nudge.

He bowed slightly to the old man and said, "Grandpa, we're leaving now. If we get the chance, we'll come back to see you."

The old man waved his hand and led them to the back room.

There, a clear underground river flowed tranquilly.

"Hop on the wooden boat and follow the river. The current will take you to the exit.

"And remember, don't let Norman catch a whiff of this place. That old guy is as greedy as a pig; he'd gobble up the whole Mortis Gorge if he could."

Suddenly, as if he'd just had a brainwave, he added, "Oh, and if that ring gives you any trouble, just chuck it. It's no big deal."

Evander gently rubbed the ring with his thumb but kept his mouth shut.

Ciotti sniffled and gave her grandfather a tight hug.

The back room was narrower than the front one. At the far end, there was a dim expanse of water with gentle ripples. A wooden boat was tied to the shore. Despite its water-darkened hull, the boat looked as sturdy as an oak.

The old man said, giving Ciotti a gentle push, "Get on board. Once you're out of the cave, head south. You'll hit the road in a day."

"Take care of yourself, Grandpa," Ciotti said, her voice thick with emotion.

Then she turned and stepped onto the boat.

Evander followed right behind. Once they were settled, the old man used a pole to give the boat a gentle shove, and it slowly drifted into the darkness with the current.

"Little Cici, take care of yourself!"

The old man's voice echoed in the tunnel.

Ciotti lay on the side of the boat, waving until her grandfather's figure disappeared into the tunnel's shadow. Then she sat down across from Evander.

As the boat glided along the underground river, they had to lie down and hug each other when passing through narrow spots.

After about an hour, a faint light appeared in the distance.

Ciotti looked at Evander excitedly. "We're almost there!"

Just then, as Evander lay on top of Ciotti, the ring on his finger suddenly started to heat up.

Words from The Tesseract Chronicle flashed through his mind.

The ring needed to be nourished by the vaginal juices of twenty-seven royal noblewomen to regain its magic.

Before long, the boat carried them out of the cave.
