Chapter 34 A Meeting With Norman

Evander hadn't expected Kimberly to be so blunt.

Iris' eyes dimmed, but she showed no surprise, as if she'd anticipated this all along.

She turned to gaze out the car window, her voice cold as frost.

"Evander, please get out."

Evander opened his mouth to explain, but Kimberly tugged at his arm.

Her resolute gaze met his, and with a reluctant clench of his jaw, he pushed the door open and stepped out.

The moment the door slammed shut, Iris started the engine, tires screeching against the pavement. Soon, her car vanished into the stream of traffic.

Evander knew he might have lost Iris for good this time.

Kimberly stepped closer, gently taking his hand.

"Don't be upset. You still have me, don't you?" Her voice carried a soft warmth, meant to soothe.

Evander didn't respond, only drew a deep breath.

He wasn't sure if he'd made the right choice.

But there was no turning back now.

The sun glared down, bathing Evander in light, yet it couldn't dispel the shadows clouding his heart.

This entanglement, born of desire and calculation, was veering toward a future he couldn't predict.

Just then, a sleek black Rolls-Royce pulled up beside them.

Before Evander could react, the door swung open. Aldred Sullivan, his grandfather's assistant, stepped out with two bodyguards, his eyes icy behind dark sunglasses.

"Mr. Bexley, Sir Westbrook requests your presence."

Aldred's voice was flat, as if delivering a routine message.

The bodyguards didn't wait for a response.

They flanked Evander and gripped his arms with iron strength, pinning him in place.

"What are you doing?"

Kimberly instinctively moved to intervene, but Aldred blocked her with a subtle step, his demeanor unyielding.

"Ms. Nelson, this is the Westbrook family's business. Please stay out of it."

His tone was polite but carried an unmistakable edge.

Evander glanced at Kimberly, her worried eyes tightening his chest.

But beneath that, a wave of relief washed over him.

This sudden "abduction" gave him a perfect excuse to slip away.

"I'm fine. Go home," Evander said to Kimberly, his voice rough.

He tugged against the bodyguards' grip, signaling them to ease up.

"Drop this. I can walk."

The guards glanced at Aldred, who nodded, and they released him.

Evander ducked into the back seat of the car. Through the tinted window, he saw Kimberly still standing there, her white shirt dazzling in the sunlight, like a flower abandoned by the roadside.

The car glided smoothly out of the city, heading toward the suburbs.

Evander leaned back, closing his eyes.

The day felt like a feverish, surreal dream.

Images flashed through his mind—Oliver's rage, Nadine's breakdown, Iris' resolute departure, and Kimberly's trusting eyes.

Each memory churned in his head, throbbing at his temples.

"Mr. Bexley, Sir Westbrook has been in high spirits lately," Aldred said, breaking the silence in the car.

"Especially after hearing about your time with Ms. Nelson on the island."

Evander opened his eyes, staring at the trees blurring past the window, but said nothing.

He knew what his grandfather meant—those thirty-seven times, the eighty percent chance of conception.

To Norman, Evander's connection with Kimberly was nothing more than a task.

The car rolled through the familiar gates of the estate, down a manicured tree-lined drive, and stopped in the center of a sprawling lawn.

Norman stood with his back to them, leaning on his cane amid a cluster of roses, his camel-colored suit glowing softly in the sunset.

"Grandfather," Evander called as he stepped out.

Norman turned slowly, his weathered face creasing with a smile, his cloudy eyes scanning Evander from head to toe.

"Evan, well done."

He paused, his tone brimming with certainty. "That Nelson girl's definitely pregnant."

Evander's heart lurched; the tension he'd carried for days finally eased.

He lowered his head, a trace of exhaustion in his voice. "You knew about that?"

"Congratulations. You've passed my second test."

Norman extended a hand, and Aldred promptly handed him a black card.

Norman pressed it into Evander's palm, patting the back of his hand. "Ten billion dollars. Your reward for completing the second test."

Evander gripped the thin card, its metallic chill seeping into his fingertips.

He'd earned it, but no joy came with it.

"Another reward awaits at Azure Cove Residence," Norman added, a cryptic smile curling his lips.

"You'll see when you get there."

Azure Cove Residence?

Evander froze, then remembered—Ciotti was waiting for him there.

Could it be...

A thought sparked, his breath quickening.

"Grandfather," he said, looking up with anticipation in his eyes.

"Can you give me the third test now?"

He expected it to be the key to inheriting the patriarch's role.

Norman waved a hand, his smile fading slightly.

"I've told Ciotti about it."

He pointed to the car at the gate. "Go ask her."

With that, Norman turned toward the black Rolls-Royce, his cane tapping the ground sharply, as if urgent business awaited.

"Grandfather!"

Evander moved to follow, but Aldred stepped in his way.

"Mr. Bexley, Sir Westbrook has matters to attend to," Aldred said, his tone leaving no room for argument. "Please leave."

The Rolls-Royce's engine roared to life, and it vanished down the tree-lined drive.

Evander stood there, clutching the ten-billion-dollar card, his heart heavy with unease.

The third test was with Ciotti?

That wasn't like something his grandfather would do.

Something felt off. Norman's smile had held more than approval—there was a hidden edge, like a reef shrouded in fog, impossible to discern.

"Mr. Bexley, shall I take you to Azure Cove Residence?"

Aldred's voice pulled him back.

Evander nodded and slipped into another car.
