Chapter 37 The Eerie Forest

The campfire burned down to a heap of glowing ash, its faint light barely pushing back the dark.

Evander and Ciotti retreated to their tents, but sleep refused to come.

Flat on his back, Evander listened to the wind howling through the trees.

His mind wouldn't quiet—Kimberly's warm smile, Nadine's jealous glare, Iris's steely resolve, Ciotti's teasing glance from earlier—all flickered through his head like a restless slideshow.

His nights with Kimberly had been equal parts lust and strategy, but where that path led was a blur. Right now, none of it mattered. He couldn't afford distractions. Tomorrow, Mortis Gorge would demand all of him.

The night deepened, a biting chill seeping through the canvas. Evander burrowed deeper into his sleeping bag, drifting at last into uneasy dreams.

At four a.m., the forest lay heavy and still.

A rustle outside snapped Evander awake. He bolted upright, hand already closing around the dagger beside his pillow.

He unzipped the tent flap, icy air rushing in, sharp with dew. Ciotti stood by the campfire's dying embers, a massive pack on her shoulders, adjusting the beam of a military flashlight.

"You're up?" she called, not even glancing over as she flicked a compressed biscuit his way. "Five minutes. We need to clear the outer forest and reach Mortis Gorge before dawn."

Evander caught it and bit down—the chalky grit stuck to his tongue. He pulled on his camo gear, tucked a pistol at his waist, and zipped his pack shut with a rasp that seemed too loud.

"Why the rush?" he asked, stepping up beside her.

"Patrols sweep the outer forest." Ciotti's voice was calm as she strapped a machine gun across her pack.

She nodded northwest, the flashlight slicing a clean line through the dark. "Upgraded my gear last month. Infrared scanner picks up heat signatures a hundred feet out."

She clapped his shoulder, steady and warm through the fabric.

"Step where I step. Don't snap a twig."

They dropped low, slipping into the trees. Mist curled thick around the trunks, dew dripping with every brush of leaves overhead.

Ciotti moved like a shadow, camo blending into the undergrowth, her steps silent. Evander followed, boots falling into her tracks like echoes.

She froze suddenly, her hand pressing him down by the neck, shoving him into a bed of ferns.

A flashlight beam swept overhead. Muffled voices crackled on radios. Boots crunched no more than thirty feet away, then faded into the fog.

"Hold your breath," Ciotti whispered, her words hot against his ear, mixing with the scent of damp leaves. Evander flushed at the closeness, but didn't move.

When silence finally swallowed the forest again, Ciotti seized his wrist and pulled him through a gap in a barbed-wire fence, smooth and silent as smoke.

Dawn brushed the sky as they broke free of the trees.

Mortis Gorge loomed ahead—its jagged ridges like the teeth of some ancient beast, moss clinging to stone like old scars.

"Soulrend Cliff. That's our first marker."

Ciotti spread the treasure map on a waterproof tarp, pinning the corners with stones. Her finger tapped a section marked with a red skull.

"We'll need gas masks to cross the miasma zone."

Evander traced the dotted line, noting the words circled over and over: Miasma Zone. A warning was scrawled beside it in rough pencil.

"Who wrote this?" he asked.

Ciotti's eyes darkened.

"My father."

She folded the map with a snap and tossed him a mask.

"No more questions. Move."

The miasma zone stretched across the valley like a sickly fog, its stench thick even through their masks.

Ciotti led the way, slicing through vines with her combat knife. Each cut sprayed milky sap that smeared across her visor in ghostly streaks.

Suddenly, Evander's topographic device shrieked.

The radiation index spiked, its red warning light pulsing like a frantic heartbeat.

"What the hell?" Evander grabbed Ciotti's pack strap, shoving the screen in front of her.

She paused, then tugged off her mask for a long breath, brow furrowing.

"Radioactive elements."

Sweeping her metal detector across the ground, the wail spiked near a cluster of small purple flowers.

"Radiation flowers. Touch the sap, and your skin will rot."

She sliced one at the base. Milky fluid oozed from the stem, hissing as it hit a rock. The surface blackened instantly.

Ciotti's jaw tightened. An old memory flickered—her father's study, a yellowed newspaper about explorers who'd died from sudden skin decay.

They pushed through. By the time the sun crested the ridge, they stumbled clear of the fog. Ciotti slumped against a rock wall, sweat dripping down her temple, bangs plastered to her forehead.

Evander handed her a water bottle. Watching the curve of her neck as she drank sent an unwanted flush to his ears.

"What are you staring at?" Ciotti wiped her mouth, eyes sparking with mischief. "Think I'm tougher than Ms. Nelson?"

Evander choked on his own drink, coughing as he turned away.

"Leave her out of this. Focus."

"Alright, all business." Her tone leveled out. She pulled out a compass—its needle jittered wildly.

"Magnetic field's shot here. Electronics won't last."

She spread the map across her knee and, with a quick swipe of lipstick, marked a triangle.

"My father noted a cave somewhere in this zone, ten years back. A karst system. That's our next lead."

As they entered the marked zone, the sky dimmed. Clouds hadn't gathered, but the sun still dulled, casting a sickly yellow glow.

Then Ciotti yanked Evander down.

A thunderous flapping ripped the air as a flock of black birds dove overhead, claws scraping stone in a scream that rattled his bones.

"Soul-devouring birds," Ciotti said coldly. "Their saliva eats through anything. Get caught, you're done."

Evander's eyes followed their flight. The flock skirted wide around a dense patch of ferns. He tugged Ciotti's sleeve, nodding.

"Hide there."

They dove into the ferns, pressed close as the birds shrieked and wheeled above, then finally drifted off.

Panting, they lay side by side in the dirt. Evander's hand brushed against hers—her fingers cold, trembling.

"You? Scared?" he teased, remembering her brag about crossing the Amazon at ten.

Ciotti snatched her hand back, ears flushing.

"Scared? Please."

She stood, brushing herself off, and deliberately stomped on his boot.

"Get up. The cave's just ahead."
