Chapter 9 Reward

The assistant's voice crackled through the phone. "Naomi just caused a scene at the hospital. The police took her away."

Evander's eyes turned cold. "Got it."

He hung up and turned around to find Iris standing there, her cheeks still flushed with a lingering rosy glow and a hint of confusion in her eyes.

"What's wrong?" she asked.

"Nothing."

Evander walked over and took her hand again, his smile warm and gentle. "Let's keep dancing."

After dinner, Evander drove Iris home.

The car moved smoothly through the quiet streets, the atmosphere comfortable between them.

"I'll pick you up for the AI Summit on Thursday," Evander said.

"Thanks," Iris said. "Don't worry about coming early though—I can make my own way there."

"Absolutely not," Evander insisted. "This is my first time attending as your date. I need to make it special."

Iris laughed, her heart fluttering with sweetness.

The car stopped outside Iris's building. She unbuckled her seatbelt but didn't get out.

She looked at Evander, hesitated for a moment, then gathered her courage and leaned over to press a gentle kiss to his cheek.

"Good night."

Before he could react, she pushed open the door and darted out like a startled rabbit.

Evander touched the spot where she'd kissed him, watching her disappear into the building entrance, the smile on his lips lingering long after she was gone.

He sat there for a while before finally starting the car and driving away.

Back at his modest apartment, Evander didn't go straight to bed. He opened his laptop to check on the Paul family situation.

Naomi had been detained for making a scene at the hospital. The rest of the Paul family had scattered like rats from a sinking ship—no one wanted to deal with their mess anymore.

Evander closed the laptop and went to bed.

*****

The next morning, Evander drove to Summit Group.

The moment he walked into the office, he noticed his employees looking at him strangely.

Puzzled, he waited until his assistant rushed over with a newspaper.

The front page featured a crystal-clear photo of him and Iris dancing at the restaurant the night before. The headline read: "Mayor's Daughter Spotted Dancing with Mystery Man Just Days After Broken Engagement—New Romance?"

Evander frowned. The photo was remarkably clear.

His phone rang. Nadine.

After a moment's hesitation, he answered.

"Evander, did you ask for a divorce because you were already sleeping with Iris?" Nadine's voice was shrill, with an almost imperceptible tremor.

"So you got the divorce papers," Evander said coolly. "Sign them as soon as possible."

Nadine's heart sank as she remembered the divorce papers in her mailbox that morning.

"Evander, I never thought you'd do this to me!"

"Nadine, before you say that, maybe think about the disgusting things you've done. Denny's already had you in every position imaginable, hasn't he?"

"I—"

Before Nadine could make excuses, Denny burst through the office door carrying steaming takeout boxes.

"Nad, try this! I waited in line forever to get it."

Guilt washed over Nadine as Evander's cold laugh echoed through the phone before he hung up.

Seeing Nadine's lost expression, Denny asked with concern, "Nad, what's wrong? Who was that?"

Nadine took the spoon he offered. "Nothing."

"You're eating like a little kitten." Denny pointed to the sauce at the corner of her mouth, reaching out affectionately to wipe it away.

Nadine instinctively backed away from his touch.

Denny froze, then casually withdrew his hand, though his mind raced with worry.

Could this mean...

She still had feelings for Evander?

*****

Evander sat in his luxury car as it entered a heavily guarded villa district.

The roadblocks and hidden sentries were more numerous than during his last visit—clearly, his grandfather was taking his security increasingly seriously.

When the grand mansion came into view, Evander couldn't help but catch his breath -

The colonial-style estate was built into the hillside, with marble railings encircling a massive fountain plaza. The manicured lawns spread out like green velvet, making even the White House seem modest in comparison.

As soon as the car stopped, two rows of black-suited bodyguards and servants bowed in unison, their voices ringing out: "Welcome home, Young Master!"

The synchronized greeting echoed through the courtyard.

Evander stepped out of the car and walked along the red carpet. He could feel countless eyes on him—curious, awed, but mostly showing an instinctive deference.

The feeling was foreign yet strangely fitting for his current state of mind.

Passing through the carved corridor, he entered the soaring main hall. Sunlight streamed through stained glass windows, casting brilliant patterns across the marble floor.

On the platform ahead, Norman stood with his hands behind his back, facing the window. His silver hair caught the light with a soft luminescence.

"You're here."

Hearing footsteps, Norman turned around. Gone was his usual stern expression, replaced by something almost boyishly excited.

He hurried down the steps and clapped Evander on the shoulder with enough force to make him sway slightly.

"Evan, congratulations on passing my first test!

"Are you ready for your reward?"
