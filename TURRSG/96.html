Chapter 97 Sorry

"What's going on?" Lois asked, noticing Evander had suddenly stopped. There was a tinge of confusion in her voice.

"No, it's nothing. This necklace is kinda cold, and it's making my skin itch," Evander replied, trying to play it cool.

He led Lois into the bedroom and said, "You go ahead and get some sleep. I'll just use the restroom."

Lois nodded and nestled into the bed. Before long, she was fast asleep, her long lashes casting a soft shadow on her cheeks.

Then, Evander tiptoed out of the bedroom and stepped onto the balcony.

The night breeze carried a sharp chill. He ran his fingers over the necklace around his neck and murmured, "Freya, is that you?"

As soon as the words escaped his lips, the necklace grew warm.

A silver light shot out from the gemstone, and a faint silhouette of Freya materialized in the air.

She was still wearing her glistening Eagle Feather Robe. Her golden hair fluttered gently in the night wind, but her face was clouded with obvious anxiety.

"Od, bad news! Something happened to Kelly!" Freya exclaimed.

"Kelly?" Evander frowned, his tone turning serious in an instant. "What happened to her?"

Freya said, her voice laden with worry, "She jumped off the rooftop of the mental hospital. Thankfully, there was an air cushion below, so she didn't die, but she broke her leg.

"Right now, she's in a real mess. She keeps cussing you out and saying she'll find someone 'who can put you in your place' to take you down."

Evander's eyes turned cold.

Kelly just wouldn't quit and still wanted to stir up more trouble.

He asked, "Who could she find?"

Freya said, looking at him with concern, "She used to be a psychic at Scientific Paranormal Research and could communicate with some gods.

"And she's off her rocker now. She's capable of anything. I'm worried she'll summon some powerful gods that'll be hard to deal with and pose a threat to you."

Evander fell silent for a moment, fingers brushing over the necklace's gemstone without thinking.

Then he made up his mind. "Got it. After the council meeting tomorrow, I'll head to the hospital to see her. This can't keep going on. I have to put an end to this once and for all."

"No way!" Freya immediately objected. "Kelly's lost it. It's way too dangerous for you to go. She hates you so much that she might even try to kill you."

Evander said firmly, "I've got the necklace you gave me and some other tricks up my sleeve. I'll be fine.

"This needs to stop. If I don't deal with her, she'll keep causing headaches for us and might even drag Lois into it."

Seeing his determined look, Freya knew she couldn't talk him out of it.

She sighed and said, "Then you must be extra careful. If you're in danger, just crush one of the necklace's gemstones, and I'll be there in a flash."

"Okay." Evander nodded. "Thanks for letting me know. It's late. Just get some rest."

Freya wanted to say more, but just nodded instead. Her ghostly figure gradually faded into the night.

The necklace cooled down and returned to its previous chill.

Standing on the balcony, Evander stared at the glittering night view of London in the distance. Beneath the bright lights lurked unseen dangers.

Kelly, Felix, Scientific Paranormal Research, and all those unknown troubles were waiting for him.

But he wouldn't back down. He was determined to seal the cooperation with Royal Tech and protect Lois, making sure those who wanted to mess with him would pay the price.

When he returned to the bedroom, Lois was still sound asleep, a faint smile on her face.

Evander gently lay down beside her, carefully pulling her into his arms before closing his eyes.

******

The next morning, as dawn began to break, Lois woke up.

She eyed the necklace around Evander's neck curiously and gently touched it, careful not to wake him.

It wasn't until breakfast was ready that she gently prodded Evander.

"Time to get up. You'll be late if you don't hustle."

Evander opened his eyes, ruffled her hair, and smiled as he sat up.

"I'm up. I'll be down for breakfast after I freshen up."

After breakfast, they climbed into a chauffeur-driven car and headed to the Royal Council building.

The building loomed ahead—solemn, imposing.

At the entrance, guards in red uniforms stood at attention, golden epaulets gleaming in the sunlight.

A cluster of reporters had already gathered. The moment Lois and Evander stepped out, cameras started clicking.

Lois looped her arm through Evander's. With a poised smile, she walked gracefully into the building.

The council hall was already teeming with people.

Chad spotted them and waved them over right away.

At the head of the table sat Gary. His hair was gray, and his eyes were sharp. He was poring over the documents in his hands.

As Evander came into view, Gary looked up and sized him up for a few seconds.

Gary said in a steady, powerful voice, "Mr. Bexley, Princess Lois.

"We've got the materials Chad submitted about the cooperation between Vanguard Tech and Royal Tech.

"But we need to dig deeper into Vanguard Tech's technical muscle.

"That's why we've invited you here today. We want you to give us the lowdown on your core technologies and tell us what real-world value they can bring to Royal Tech."

Evander nodded, strode up to the podium, and fired up his laptop to pull up the prepared PPT.

Taking a deep breath, he launched into an in-depth talk about Vanguard Tech's artificial intelligence technology.

He covered the iteration speed of the autonomous learning algorithm, the accuracy of the voice-interaction system, and the application of emotion-recognition technology in elderly-care robots.

Every bit of data was crystal clear, and each case came with real-time videos, vividly showing off the AI's capabilities.

The council members listened intently and threw in a few questions now and then.

"Is the AI's security-protection system up to snuff?"

"What's the ballpark cost of rolling out the technology?"

Evander patiently fielded each question.

With a clear head and a confident tone, he gradually won Gary over. The initial skepticism on Gary's face gave way to approval, and he even nodded from time to time.

After wrapping up the tech talk, Evander looked at the council members and declared firmly, "Vanguard Tech's AI can not only turn Royal Tech's humanoid robots from mere tools into companions but also create value in fields like emergency rescue, defense, and education.

"By teaming up with us, Royal Tech can not only solidify its lead in Europe but also break into the global market. It's a win-win deal. I'm dead-set on us creating a better future together."

As soon as he finished, the hall erupted in enthusiastic applause.

Gary stood up, smiling, "Mr. Bexley, your presentation is really persuasive. But Royal Tech won't be partnering with Vanguard Tech.

"You see, we think we can get our hands on your core AI tech in three years.

"On the flip side, it'll take you at least ten years to catch up with Royal Tech's core tech for humanoid robots. Sorry, but we can't reach an agreement on this core-tech exchange."

With that, Gary turned on his heel and walked off.
