Chapter 45 Old Man

After half an hour's rest, they pushed forward again.

The tunnel widened as they went, the faint azure glow on the rock walls growing brighter. Up ahead, a massive cave mouth yawned open, like the jaws of some ancient beast.

Carved into the stone above the entrance was a full skull symbol—the same as on the treasure map.

"We're here," Ciotti breathed, excitement quickening her steps.

Behind her, Evander's heart hammered. Whatever lay beyond—elixir, danger, or both—there was no turning back.

As they neared the entrance, Ciotti's flashlight caught an engraving on the wall:

Immortality is but a fool's dream.

Evander froze. The script matched the other carvings. Ciotti's grandfather had left this, too.

So he hadn't sought immortality after all. Just… more time.

"Let's go in and find out," Ciotti whispered, drawing a deep breath before stepping into the cave.

Evander followed, hesitating only a moment.

Inside stretched a vast cavern. In the center rose a stone platform. Piles of gold and jewels lay scattered around it, thick with dust, untouched for ages.

Evander frowned. "This is it? The treasure?" He couldn't hide the disappointment. He'd expected something greater.

Ciotti climbed the platform, eyes on a small black box resting in the middle. She lifted the lid with careful hands.

No elixir. No jewels. Only a yellowed diary and a brass key—identical to the one in Evander's pocket.

"My grandpa's diary…" Ciotti's voice shook.

She opened it, the faded handwriting still clear enough to read. Evander leaned in beside her. Page after page detailed her grandfather's long, brutal search for the treasure.

Then—

"Who's pawing through my junk?"

The voice was rough, harsh as gravel, echoing like thunder through the cavern.

Evander snapped his head up. His flashlight cut across the far wall—just in time to see stone grinding open, revealing a hidden door.

Out stepped an old man with a mane of white hair. He wore a camel-colored robe embroidered in dark gold, a black pendant glinting at his waist. His skin was ruddy, his eyes startlingly clear, bright as stars.

The diary slipped from Ciotti's hands, thudding onto the stone.

She stumbled forward, voice breaking. "Grandpa? You're alive!"

The old man frowned, tapping his staff against the floor. Chips of stone scattered from the impact.

"Girl, where did you crawl out from? Don't go spouting nonsense about being related."

But then his gaze fell on the necklace at her throat—a half-crescent pendant, the word Karen carved tiny along its edge.

His expression shifted. His grip on the staff eased, though caution lingered in his eyes. "That necklace… where did you get it?"

"Grandma gave it to me!" Ciotti clutched the pendant tight.

"I'm Ciotti—Joshua Karen's daughter. You left when I was five. Grandma said you went to find a cure for her cough and never came back…"

"Joshua…" the old man whispered. His son's name trembled off his lips. Tears welled suddenly, bright in the cavern's cold blue glow.

Trembling, the old man pulled a pocket watch from his robe. Inside, nestled against the glass, was the other half of the crescent necklace.

When he held it up to Ciotti's pendant, the two pieces clicked together perfectly, forming a full moon. The carved edges aligned seamlessly.

"Really… it's you. My little Cici…"

His voice broke. The walking stick slipped from his grip and clattered onto the stone. He lifted a hand toward her, fingers trembling, then froze—drawing back as though one touch might shatter the fragile moment into dust.

Evander let out a breath, relief softening his chest—until the old man suddenly whirled on him.

The tenderness vanished, replaced by icy steel.

Like a predator, he lunged. His bony fingers shot for Evander's forehead with startling speed.

"Where'd you come from, boy? How dare you trespass in my territory!"

"Grandpa, stop!" Ciotti cried, throwing herself between them.

The old man's hand halted a hair's breadth from her nose. The force of it stung her cheek.

"He can't die! He's my fiancé!"

"Fiancé?" The word snapped from his lips like a whip. His brows knit as he turned his sharp gaze on Evander.

"You're her fiancé?"

He sized Evander up from head to toe, eyes narrowing, appraising him like goods in a market.

"Strong enough. But those eyes… too shifty. Not the look of an honest man."

Heat burned in Evander's chest under that piercing scrutiny. He opened his mouth to speak, but the old man had already turned, striding for the stone door.

"Fine. Come in. The traps outside are about to reset. Stay any longer, and the Soul-devouring Birds will make a meal of you."

Ciotti grabbed Evander's hand and pulled him along.

The stone door rumbled shut behind them.

What lay beyond stole their breath.

The chamber stretched wide, its ceiling studded with countless pearls that glowed soft and silvery, bathing the cavern in moonlight bright as day.

Along three walls towered open crates—tens of thousands—brimming with gold bars stacked in perfect rows.

Near the entrance, a mountain of gold coins rose toward the ceiling, rubies the size of pigeon eggs wedged in the cracks, scattering bloody light across the room.

At the center bubbled a natural hot spring, steam drifting in ghostly veils. Beside it sat a golden bed, draped with the snow-white pelt of a great beast. The fur shimmered faintly silver, like the legendary hide of a snow lion.

On either side of the bed, chests overflowed with pearls and agates, the largest pearl the size of a fist, glowing with shifting rainbow hues.

And in the far corner stood a towering bookshelf—tens of thousands of ancient tomes, spines gilded with bright golden characters. Some were even bound in gold thread, treasures of knowledge beyond price.

Ciotti's eyes went wide, her voice a breathless whisper. "Are these… real? Am I dreaming?"

She picked up a coin from the heap, bit down. A neat tooth mark dented the gold.

Her gaze snapped to her grandfather. "Grandpa… all these years, you've been guarding this?"

The old man lowered himself onto a stool carved from glittering ore beside the hot spring. He dipped a ladle of the same material into the steaming pool and sipped, his expression tinged with pride.

"Back then, I quarreled with your grandma," he said. "I swore I'd find the legendary Longevity Spring to cure her cough. Who would've thought I'd stumble on this place instead?

"This water truly strengthens the body. Look at me—I'm in my seventies, and I can still drop a black bear with one punch."

Then his tone shifted. His sharp gaze fixed on Evander.

"You're Norman's grandson, aren't you?"

Evander stiffened, instinctively about to deny it, but the old man cut him off.

"Don't bother pretending. You look just like him when he was young. Forty years ago, your grandfather brought men here to steal these treasures. I broke three of his ribs myself. I never expected that even after all these years, he'd still be sending someone back."

Evander hesitated, then slowly pulled the brass key from his pocket.

"Grandpa isn't after the treasure. He sent me to find the elixir."

At the sight of the key, the old man threw his head back and let out a booming laugh, eyes watering from the force of it.

"Elixir? What elixir? One to cure his greed?"

He jabbed his finger toward the stone altar at the center of the hot spring.

"Is that what you mean?"

Evander turned. Sure enough, on the stone slab sat a bottle etched with the same skull pattern as the treasure map.

He moved instinctively to step forward, but the old man's staff slammed down, blocking him.

"What's in that bottle is arsenic. Back then, Norman's men stole one. When they returned, they used it to poison their own people."

Ciotti's eyes went wide. "But the diary said—"

"I wrote the diary on purpose," the old man interrupted with a weary sigh. His gaze clouded.

"I didn't want your grandma to worry, so I made it sound like danger kept me away. I never thought she'd…"

His words trailed off. He only reached out and patted Ciotti's head gently.

"All these years, I've been here, guarding these treasures. Waiting for the day you grew up—so I could hand them to you myself."

"What?"

Ciotti froze, her mind blank. Her grandfather… was going to give all of this to her?
