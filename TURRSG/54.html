Chapter 55 Emily's Innocence

Emily called out, "Mr. Bexley, please give me a ride!"

At her voice, Evander slowly lowered the window of the Rolls-Royce.

Emily's smiling face came into view. She wore a pale yellow dress that ended mid-thigh, her long legs gleaming under the sun.

"Mr. Bexley, Aileen and Jack already booked the restaurant," she said brightly. "I thought you might have trouble finding it, so I came to be your guide!"

Her voice was crisp and cheerful, like a bird in the woods. She tapped her fingers lightly against the window frame in rhythm.

Evander's gaze lingered briefly on her delicate collarbone and smooth legs before he smiled and nodded. "Alright then, I'll take you up on that."

He opened the door, placing his hand on the roof like a gentleman as Emily bent down to slip into the passenger seat.

The motion tugged her skirt a little higher, revealing the curve of her waist. His eyes paused for a beat before he looked away.

Once seated, Emily buckled her seatbelt and turned to him with sparkling curiosity. "This car is amazing! Did you buy it yourself?"

"It's my grandfather's," Evander said, steering the car smoothly out of the lot. "I borrow it sometimes."

As he drove, his eyes flicked once to her legs.

They shifted restlessly with her chatter—graceful but full of youthful energy. It reminded him, unbidden, of Aileen's sultry allure the night before.

Two sisters. Two very different temptations.

"Your grandfather must be incredible!" Emily leaned closer, her floral scent mingling with the salty breeze. "Anyone driving a Rolls-Royce can't be ordinary."

"Not really. Just some small businesses," Evander deflected, not ready to reveal Norman's true background.

He steered the topic away. "What about you? What's your major?"

"Dance," she replied eagerly, her eyes lighting up. "Ballet's my favorite, though I love modern dance too. When we were on the beach earlier, I even snuck in a few spins."

She lifted her arm in a graceful pose, fingers long and fluid. "Last week I won first place in our school's competition. One day, I want to be like Margot Fonteyn."

"That's impressive," Evander said sincerely. "Dance takes discipline. Winning means serious effort. Back in college, I couldn't even handle basic morning drills."

Emily burst out laughing. "No way!"

Their conversation flowed easily—her training, favorite performances, dreams of one day stepping onto the world's grandest stages.

Her liveliness was infectious, her admiration clear.

But suddenly her tone softened, her eyes wistful.

"It's a shame, though. None of the guys at school understand dance. They either think ballet girls are fragile or just say things like, 'You spin really well.' They don't see the beauty in it… and they're not exactly manly either."

She turned to him, gaze warm and unguarded. "If I could meet someone like you, it'd be different. You're a gentleman, capable… and you really see things."

A faint blush crept over her cheeks. "I don't even have a boyfriend. I just… haven't met anyone I like."

Evander's hand paused briefly on the wheel.

The anticipation in her eyes shone like starlight, tugging at him in ways he hadn't expected.

With a teasing smile, he said, "Then maybe you should wait for me. I'm about to be single again."

The words hit her like a stone tossed into still water. Emily froze, her heart racing.

Sunlight poured through the window, sharpening his profile—the strong jaw, the faint curl of his lips.

"Mr. Bexley… are you joking?" Her voice trembled.

If he meant it, did that mean someone might finally understand the 5 a.m. practices, the blisters, the devotion to her stage?

Evander didn't answer directly. Instead, he pointed ahead with a smile.

"We're almost at the restaurant. Don't think too much about it. After dinner, if you're willing, maybe you can dance for me. Let me see for myself."

"Gladly!" Emily said at once, her face glowing with excitement.

Evander chuckled softly, catching the spark of delight in her eyes.

It seemed the second "nourishment" would be easier to claim than he expected.

The Rolls-Royce eased to a stop outside a Michelin-starred restaurant.

At the entrance, Jack and Aileen were already waiting.

Aileen had changed into a sleek black gown that hugged every curve, her hair swept elegantly over one shoulder. She looked radiant, magnetic.

Jack stood beside her in a crisp white shirt, but his face was still stormy, the sting of his volleyball loss simmering beneath the surface.

When Emily stepped out with her hand looped casually through Evander's arm, Jack's expression darkened further. A sharp glint flashed in his eyes.

"You're late," he said flatly, irritation threading every syllable.

Emily leaned closer to Evander with an impish grin. "We lost track of time."

She swung his arm lightly, adding with a laugh, "Mr. Bexley even said he wants to see me dance. At least he cares. You never once asked if I was exhausted after rehearsals."

Jack's jaw flexed. He didn't answer. Instead, he turned abruptly toward the restaurant doors. "Let's go. I already ordered." His voice was clipped, controlled—just barely.

Inside, the four of them were seated by the wide ocean-facing window.

The silverware gleamed under the chandelier, the faint scent of truffles and wine hanging in the air. Waves crashed against the rocks outside, the perfect view at odds with the tension at the table.

Jack sat opposite Evander, his expression thunderous. He barely spoke, tearing into his steak with a knife and fork that scraped too harshly against porcelain. Each movement was precise, but fueled with restraint that threatened to snap.

Beside him, Aileen's eyes occasionally strayed toward Evander. A playful glimmer lingered there, subtle yet dangerous, like sparks catching in dry grass.

Halfway through the meal, Evander was savoring a forkful of foie gras when a sudden warmth brushed against his thigh. His muscles stiffened.

Aileen's toes—barely veiled by the slit in her gown—were tracing slowly up his leg. The pressure was deliberate, teasing.

His hand twitched. The stem of his wineglass tipped, and in an instant, red liquid spilled across the pristine white cloth… then onto Jack's lap.

The stain spread dark and unmistakable.

Jack shot to his feet, fury blazing in his eyes. "What the hell do you think you're doing?" His voice cut through the hum of the restaurant like a blade.

For one raw second, it looked like he might strike Evander across the table.

But the clink of glasses quieted, diners turned to stare, and several phones lifted discreetly to record.

Jack froze.

As a major, he knew better. A public scene here wouldn't just shame him—it would stain his father's name as well.

"I'm so sorry—really sorry," Evander said quickly, sliding a stack of napkins across the table with an apologetic look.

Inside, though, he was laughing.

"I must've caught the chair leg and lost my balance. Clumsy of me."

Emily and Aileen both shook with suppressed laughter, their shoulders trembling.

Jack snatched the napkins, dabbing furiously at his pants, but the dark stain only spread.

His glare at Evander was murderous before he spun on his heel and stormed toward the restroom.

Behind the locked door, Jack pulled out his phone, his voice low and venomous.

"It's me. Get your men here. Michelin restaurant, downstairs. I want you dressed like street thugs, waiting at the door. Bring plenty of guys. Don't let him walk away."

A respectful voice crackled on the other end. "Who do you want us to deal with? Do we need weapons?"

"No. Just fists." Jack's knuckles whitened around the phone. "The man I'm dining with—the tall one in the blue shirt. Break his legs. Make sure he remembers what happens to anyone who dares steal my woman in Nogales."

The image of Emily's bright smile turned toward Evander—and Aileen's knowing eyes—made Jack want to rip him apart on the spot.

He hung up, straightened his soiled trousers in front of the mirror, and drew a slow breath. His reflection stared back, calm on the surface, seething beneath.

Evander, you won't crawl out of here.

Back at the table, Emily leaned in close, her laugh soft and teasing.

"Mr. Bexley, you were way too clumsy just now. But Jack's face—oh my god, priceless. He didn't look cool at all."

Her breath feathered against his ear, tickling, and she gave his arm a playful nudge, the gesture as natural as it was affectionate.

Evander's lips curved in quiet amusement. "Maybe he's still tired from the game earlier. Guess he hasn't recovered."

Then, smoothly, he shifted the conversation. "So, tell me more about that dance competition you mentioned. I'm curious what daily life looks like in your program."

Even as he spoke to Emily, his gaze flicked across the table.

Aileen met his eyes, her brows arched, her smile sly and knowing.

The touch of her toes earlier had been no accident.

Evander understood at once. His lips curved ever so slightly.
