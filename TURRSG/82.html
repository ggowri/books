Chapter 83 Won't Regret It

The cruise ship's top-floor restaurant was pure elegance.

Crystal chandeliers cast soft light across the room, and through the floor-to-ceiling windows, Evander could see nothing but dark ocean stretching endlessly. Every now and then, waves crashed against the hull, and somewhere in the background, a violin played something romantic.

Evander had claimed a table by the window. Steam rose from his Blue Mountain coffee, filling the air with that rich, expensive smell.

He stirred it absently, gazing at the ocean beyond. The night waters stretched out like dark velvet, endless and mysterious.

A waiter hovered nearby. "Mr. Bexley, can I get you anything else?"

Evander shook his head. "I'm good. I'll order when my friends get here."

The waiter nodded and disappeared.

Evander sipped his coffee. It was perfect—smooth and rich, with just the right balance of bitter and sweet.

He leaned back in his chair, thinking about what had just gone down in the card room.

Felix's face when he'd lost... Lois walking away without looking back... The way Anya and Andrea's eyes had lit up when he'd handed them their cut... He couldn't help but smile.

30 minutes later, he heard footsteps clicking across the marble floor.

Evander looked up to see Anya and Andrea walking in side by side. Both had changed into pure white dresses with tiny pearls dotting the hem. Everything about the way they moved screamed elegance and grace.

Anya walked ahead with a bright smile on her face, while Andrea followed behind, looking a little shy and stealing glances at Evander every few steps.

"Mr. Bexley, sorry we're late!" Anya hurried over to the table and pulled out a chair. "We got held up counting all those chips and dealing with the black cards."

Andrea sat down too, her voice barely above a whisper. "Good evening, Mr. Bexley."

"Don't worry about it," Evander said with a smile. "I only just arrived. The steaks and seafood here are exceptional—any preferences?"

Anya grabbed the menu and rattled off their order like a pro—two filet mignons, escargot, and a bottle of '82 Lafite. Then she turned to Andrea. "What about you? Want anything else?"

Andrea shrugged quietly. "Whatever you're having is fine."

The food arrived quickly. The steaks were perfectly brown and sizzling, the escargot smelled amazing with all that garlic butter, and the wine left elegant traces down the sides of their glasses.

Anya picked up her knife and fork, cutting into her steak. "Mr. Bexley, what you did back there was incredible! Playing blind the whole time and still winning? Especially that last hand when your straight flush beat his four of a kind. That was insane!"

She looked up at him, eyes wide with curiosity. "How did you pull that off? Was it really just luck?"

Evander set down his silverware and picked up his wine glass, swirling it gently before taking a sip. His voice was casual when he answered.

"It's not all luck. I'm good at reading people. Every time Felix looked at his cards, his face gave him away.

"When he had a good hand, the corners of his mouth would turn up, and his eyes got excited. Bad cards made him frown and tap his fingers on the table. I could tell from those little tells whether his hand was strong or weak."

"Oh my God, Mr. Bexley, that's incredible!" Anya's mouth fell open. "Can you read my sister Andrea right now? She's been quiet this whole time. What do you think she's thinking?"

Evander's gaze shifted to Andrea.

The moment Andrea felt his eyes on her, she looked down shyly. Her long lashes fluttered like butterfly wings, a soft blush spread across her cheeks, and her fingers twisted nervously in her napkin.

Evander watched her reaction, a slow smile spreading across his face. He winked and said, "Andrea... She wants to sleep with me."

"What?" Anya nearly dropped her fork, her voice jumping an octave. "That's impossible! Andrea, is that really what you're thinking?"

Andrea's face flushed crimson.

She looked up, her eyes showing a mix of panic and determination. She turned toward the window with an awkward smile, trying to compose herself.

Anya wasn't letting this go. She grabbed Andrea's arm. "Andrea, is what Mr. Bexley said true? Come on, tell me!"

Andrea took a deep breath and turned back to face them. She nodded slowly, her voice quiet but clear. "Yes, it's true. I do like Mr. Bexley."

Evander's heart jumped. He'd been completely guessing, and somehow he'd nailed it.

Anya shook her head in disbelief, looking back and forth between Andrea and Evander. "You guys just met yesterday! How can you already like him?"

Evander stepped in with a smile. "Love doesn't make sense. Sometimes you just click with someone in a split second."

He turned to Andrea, lifting his glass with a gentle look in his eyes.

"Andrea, shall we finish these drinks and continue our chat upstairs?"

Andrea's eyes lit up instantly. She didn't hesitate for a second—just downed the rest of her wine in one go.

Her cheeks flushed pink, and her gaze went a little dreamy, but she kept her eyes locked on Evander. "Mr. Bexley, I... I finished it."

Evander stood up and nodded to Anya. "Anya, thanks for dinner. I'm going to take Andrea back now."

Anya looked at Andrea's unsteady state and let out a resigned sigh. "Alright, you two be careful. Andrea... take care of yourself."

Evander wrapped his arm around Andrea, who was swaying slightly, and headed toward the restaurant exit.

She leaned into him, breathing in his warmth and the subtle scent of his cologne. Something sweet and nervous fluttered in her chest.

She tilted her head up to study his profile, a happy smile tugging at her lips without her realizing it.

They stepped into the elevator together. Evander hit the button for the top floor. With just the two of them in the small space, the air felt charged.

Andrea stayed pressed against his side. "Mr. Bexley, I'm... I'm kind of nervous."

He rubbed her back gently. "Don't be. Just stay with me, okay?"

When the elevator reached the penthouse level, Evander guided her down the hall to his first-class suite. He unlocked the door and helped her inside.

The room was all soft lighting and luxury. A king-size round bed dominated the space, covered in plush bedding. Through the balcony doors, she could see the ocean stretching out under the night sky.

Evander settled her onto the leather sofa and went to pour her a glass of water. "Drink something. It'll help with the alcohol."

Andrea took the glass of water, sipping it slowly, but her eyes never left Evander.

She set the glass down, stood up, and stepped close to him, her hands gently wrapping around his waist. "Mr. Bexley," she whispered, her voice soft but steady, "I'm ready."

Evander looked down at her, cradled in his arms, her cheeks flushed a deep pink, her gaze hazy yet shimmering with determination.

He brushed his fingers gently through her hair, his tone warm and careful. "Andrea, you're sure about this? Once we start, there's no turning back."

Andrea nodded firmly, burying her face against his chest, her voice catching slightly. "I'm sure. I won't regret it."

Evander didn't hesitate any longer. He leaned down, capturing her lips in a kiss.

Andrea responded eagerly, her arms tightening around his neck, her body trembling with a mix of nerves and passion.
