Chapter 82 Scientific Paranormal Research

Lois walked over and put her hand on Felix's shoulder. "Felix, just stop. Mr. Bexley knows what he's doing. There's no shame in losing to someone like that."

Felix saw the disappointment in Lois' eyes, then looked at her two cousins practically swooning over Evander. Something bitter twisted in his chest.

He yanked his black card out of his wallet and slammed it on the table. "Two more hands! If I win, I'm back in the game. If I lose, I'm done."

Evander watched him make his desperate play, still smiling, "Alright. Two more hands it is."

He turned to Anya. "Anya, would you deal for us?"

Anya nodded and smiled, shuffling the cards again.

After the flop, the community cards showed Nine, Ten, and Jack. Felix checked his hole cards—eight and seven. With the board, he could make a straight.

His heart started racing. He was about to speak when Evander beat him to it.

"This hand, I'm betting a million pounds blind. You can either call and raise it to two million, or fold."

Felix's jaw dropped. A million pounds without even looking at his cards? Evander was basically telling him he didn't matter.

Felix looked down at his straight, then back at Evander's untouched hole cards. His teeth clenched. "Fine. I call and raise to two million pounds. Let's do this."

The turn and river cards hit the table. The final board showed Five, Two, Nine, Ten, Jack. Both players flipped their cards.

Felix's grin died on his face. Evander's hole cards were a queen and king—a king-high straight that crushed Felix's jack-high straight.

Felix went pale as a sheet. But he still had one more chance to win it all back.

The final hand, Anya's hands shook slightly as she dealt the cards. The hole cards landed in front of both players.

Felix's fingers shot toward his cards instantly. The second he peeked at the corners, his pulse quickened, and his head snapped up. A massive grin spread across his face.

He got pocket aces. If another ace hit the flop, he'd have three of a kind, trip aces. He would have a huge chance to win everything back.

The cards hit the table—Ace, Ace, King!

When Felix saw those two aces, he nearly lost his mind. Four of a kind! In Texas Hold'em, only a straight flush could beat this hand.

He fought to keep his excitement in check, slowly arranging his chips like he was bored. "I'll open with a hundred thousand pounds."

Evander didn't even blink. "I'm in for 500 thousand pounds. Blind."

Felix smirked inside and shoved another stack forward. "I'm raising to five million pounds!"

"So, Mr. Bexley, you still feeling lucky with that blind bet?" His voice dripped with mockery.

A small smile tugged at Evander's lips. "I'll see your five million pounds and raise you back. Your move, Felix. If you want to keep going, we're talking 10 million pounds now."

Anya and Andrea exchanged shocked looks. Five million on a blind bet was insane.

Behind Felix, Lois gripped her dress so tight her knuckles went white. She was terrified for Evander. She could see Felix's cards—four aces were basically unbeatable.

The turn card came up as a Two, then the river showed a Four. The final board read Ace, Ace, King, Two, Four.

Felix let out a harsh laugh.

He slammed his black card down on the table, his voice shaking with manic excitement. "I'm raising another 40 million pounds! 50 million pounds total!

"Come on, Mr. Bexley. I thought you wanted to show me up? Let's see that blind confidence now!"

Even Anya froze mid-reach. Andrea tugged at Evander's sleeve, her voice barely a whisper.

"Mr. Bexley, 50 million pounds is way too much. Maybe we should—"

Evander held up a hand, cutting her off. His eyes stayed completely calm. "Don't worry. I'll match the 50 million pounds."

Felix lost his damn mind. Because his account didn't have anywhere close to that much left.

But there was no way in hell he was backing down from this hand.

He stared at Evander's face-down cards like he could burn holes through them with his eyes. "50 million pounds it is, Mr. Bexley. Show your cards!"

He reached across the table to flip Evander's cards himself, but Evander caught his wrist. "Easy there. Let's have Princess Lois do the honors for this final hand."

Lois blinked in surprise, then walked over on shaky legs. Her fingers trembled slightly as she reached for Felix's cards first.

Two aces to go with the pair of aces on the board—four of a kind.

Felix jumped up from his chair, pointing at Evander's hidden cards and laughing like a maniac. "You see that? Four aces! You're finished! You're just a nobody, and you think you can take me on? You—"

His words died as Lois moved to Evander's cards and flipped them over.

Three of spades. Five of spades.

Combined with the ace, two, and four of spades on the board, they made a straight flush. Ace through five.

The room was so quiet it felt like time had stopped.

Andrea slapped both hands over her mouth, afraid she might scream out loud.

Anya's eyes went wide. "Oh my God! Mr. Bexley, that's insane! A straight flush!"

Felix's laugh died in his throat. His face went from flushed red to ghost pale in seconds.

He lunged forward and grabbed Evander's cards, flipping them over and over like he was searching for some sign of cheating. "No way! This is impossible! I had four aces. How the hell did I lose?"

Evander smiled calmly. "I just got lucky, Mr. Gosse."

"No! This can't be real! You cheated!" Felix whipped out a gun and pointed it straight at Evander, his eyes bloodshot with rage.

Evander sat up straighter, that same calm, gentlemanly smile never leaving his face. "I never touched a single card. Never even looked at them once. So tell me. How exactly did I cheat?"

"Felix, put the gun down!" Lois stood frozen, her body trembling beneath that golden dress. But it wasn't fear. She was done with Felix's bullshit.

She stepped forward, placing herself between the gun and Evander. Her voice turned ice cold.

"Felix, you're the one who suggested this bet. You're the one who kept doubling the stakes. Mr. Bexley played blind the entire time. He was already going easy on you.

"When you can't handle losing, you pull a gun and scream about cheating? Is that what you call having class? Felix, I'm done. Don't you dare mention gambling to me again. And don't bring up our engagement either!"

She spun around and stormed out, her golden dress sweeping across the poker table and knocking chips to the floor. The sharp clatter sounded like Felix's dignity shattering into pieces.

Felix stood frozen, still clutching those two aces, his face white as a sheet. He watched Lois disappear through the doorway, then looked at Anya, Andrea, and Evander. Slowly, he lowered the gun.

Because now, he had nothing left. Even Lois was gone.

If he did anything else stupid, he'd be looking at jail time next.

That was when Evander finally stood up. He turned to Anya and Andrea. "Take the money. Consider it payment for dealing tonight."

Anya and Andrea just stared, their eyes wide with shock as they looked at the massive pile of chips on the table.

"Mr. Bexley, this is... this is way too much! That's over a hundred million pounds! We can't possibly—"

Evander just smiled. "If you don't want it, I'll have to give it back to Mr. Gosse."

Anya froze, then quickly spoke up, "No way, house rules—no refunds at the poker table. We're keeping it!"

Andrea rushed over to help Anya collect the chips, along with Felix's black card.

"Mr. Bexley!" Anya called out as she saw Evander heading for the door. Her voice carried a hint of hope. "It's getting late. Why don't we take you to dinner?"

Evander stopped and turned back. "Sure. I'll wait for you at the fine dining restaurant on the top deck."

With that, he left the gaming room.

Felix was still slumped in his chair, looking completely defeated. Then, it hit him—Evander wasn't just some magician. The guy had actual superpowers.

He shot upright, his expression turning dark. He pulled out his phone and dialed the number for Scientific Paranormal Research.

SPR was the world's first academic institution dedicated to studying supernatural phenomena using scientific methods. Felix had gotten involved with them through his aunt, Kelly Goose.

"Fred, I'm on the Royal Cruise and I've found a man with real superpowers. His name is Evander Bexley. We dock in London in two days—perfect timing to start that brain dissection project we talked about. "

He let out a sneer. "I'll send you his photo tomorrow. Bring extra men. We can't let this one get away."
