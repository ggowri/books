Chapter 53 Emily

Aileen let out a soft laugh, trailing her fingertips along Evander's chin.

"As the old adage goes, what's truly terrifying isn't just a brute… but an educated one."

Her voice was light, teasing, but her body betrayed her. Her knees still felt weak, her skin still tingled with the memory of his touch.

Then, the sudden growl of an engine shattered the moment. Doors slammed outside, voices echoed faintly through the stone walls.

Aileen's smile vanished. Color drained from her face as she shot upright, fumbling for the clothes scattered across the floor.

"It's Jack!" she whispered, panic spiking in her tone. "And—oh no—it's Emily. She wasn't supposed to be home this week. What is she doing back?"

Evander was already on his feet, moving with cool efficiency. He adjusted his shirt and collar, scanning the room with quick, practiced eyes.

In one smooth motion, he snatched the discarded manuscript from the coffee table, crumpled it, and tossed it into the trash as if erasing a crime scene.

"Relax," he said calmly, straightening his cuffs. "Just tell them we were talking about the magazine layout."

He crossed the room, smoothing the wrinkles from Aileen's nightgown.

His fingertips brushed her earlobe—light, fleeting—and she shivered despite the urgency of the moment.

"Breathe," he murmured. "Act natural. Don't blow our cover."

Aileen pressed a hand to her chest, forcing herself to draw in a deep breath, to calm the racing of her heart.

Just as Aileen finished straightening herself, the living-room door swung open.

Jack strode in first, crisp in his military uniform, every step radiating authority. Trailing behind him was a young girl in a white spaghetti-strap dress that showed off her delicate collarbones and long, slender legs.

It was Emily.

At nineteen, with her golden hair tied in a playful ponytail and the faintest dimple flashing when she smiled, she was the very picture of youthful charm.

She shared Aileen's striking blue eyes and elegant nose, but where Aileen carried a mature poise, Emily's liveliness made her glow.

"Aileen, I'm home!" Emily chirped, bounding in with the energy of a whirlwind.

Her gaze landed on her sister first, then immediately shifted—caught by the tall figure on the sofa. Recognition dawned, and her eyes lit up like stars.

The mysterious man from the televised auction—the one who had stunned everyone with his outrageous bids—was standing right in her house.

"What on earth brought you back so suddenly?" Aileen asked, forcing a casual tone as she reached for Emily's backpack. Her eyes, however, kept flicking nervously toward Evander, as if terrified the truth might slip.

"It's all because of this!" Emily waved her phone, the auction broadcast frozen on the screen with Evander's face and the glittering crown hairpin.

"I saw it live. I just had to come back—I couldn't believe my eyes! And then… Mr. Bexley himself, here in person!"

She hurried forward, beaming as she extended a hand. "Hi, Mr. Bexley. I'm Emily Burton, Aileen's sister. Freshman at the University of Arizona. Thank you for the crown hairpin—you should've seen how excited Aileen was!"

Evander rose to greet her, his expression composed. He clasped her hand politely. Emily's palm was warm, her youthful energy radiating in a way so different from Aileen's cultivated elegance.

As she smiled up at him, rosy-cheeked and guileless, a flicker of thought stirred in Evander's mind. Emily was Kenny's youngest daughter, carrying the same royal lineage. Could she be the "second nourishment" the Arcane Ring craved?

He released her hand with a faint smile, his tone smooth. "You're welcome, Ms. Emily Burton. Your sister certainly deserves it."

Aileen moved quickly to the wine cabinet and poured another glass of red for Jack.

"How come you two came in together?" she asked, forcing her voice into something casual.

"I was heading home from work and gave Emily a lift," Jack replied, taking the glass from her.

He sipped, his eyes scanning the room.

Something in the air felt… charged. Aileen's cheeks were too flushed, the atmosphere a little too warm.

Still, he dismissed it as nothing more than an intense discussion carried over from the auction.

"Aileen, pour me one too!" Emily chirped.

She bounded to the wine cabinet, snatching an empty glass before her sister could stop her.

"You're still in school," Aileen scolded lightly. "Go easy."

"I'm nineteen. That's an adult," Emily countered with a grin, filling half a glass. "One sip won't kill me."

She plopped down beside Evander, glass in hand. "Mr. Bexley, let me toast you! Thanks for what you did for Aileen."

Evander raised his own glass, and the crisp clink echoed in the quiet room. They drank.

Emily's blue eyes sparkled with admiration. "You were incredible at the auction today! Dropping billions like it was pocket change—I've never seen anyone like you."

Evander gave a modest smile. "Just lucky enough to have the means."

Then his gaze drifted between the two sisters.

His voice was smooth, almost offhand, but his words carried weight.

"You're both extraordinary. Ms. Aileen Burton—poised and elegant. Ms. Emily Burton—bright and full of life. Two of the most remarkable women I've ever met."

The compliment landed perfectly. Emily's grin widened, delighted, while Aileen's blush deepened, her mind flashing back to the moment on the sofa just minutes ago.

Jack's expression darkened.

Evander had the audacity to praise his fiancée and her sister right in front of him. Was he mocking him, or simply ignoring him altogether?

He swallowed his irritation with another long drink and leaned forward. "Mr. Bexley, a man who spends like you must come from an aristocratic family, no?"

Evander set down his glass, calm as ever, and replied with a confident smile.

"My grandfather and father were ordinary men. But I'll make sure my sons and grandsons are nobles."

The words hung in the air—steady, assured, without a trace of arrogance.

Aileen and Emily exchanged a glance, their admiration only deepening.

To Emily, Evander seemed worlds above the shallow braggarts she knew at school. To Aileen, he was dangerously captivating—more so than ever.

"Aileen, let me see that crown hairpin!" Emily tugged on her sister's arm, her eyes glittering with anticipation.

Aileen sighed, helpless, and slipped into the bedroom. She returned moments later with the velvet box.

Emily snatched it eagerly, flipping it open. The pigeon-blood ruby blazed under the chandelier, throwing sparks of crimson across the room.

"Wow! It's even more gorgeous in person than on TV!" she gasped.

Holding the hairpin against her golden hair, she turned to Evander, her eyes bright with mischief. "Mr. Bexley, will you bid on something royal for me next time?"

Evander's smile was easy, but inside, satisfaction stirred. The fish was nibbling the bait.

"Of course," he said smoothly. "If you see something you like, I'll make sure it's yours."

"Really?" Emily squealed, practically bouncing on her toes.

Then, in a rush of girlish gratitude, she leaned forward and planted a quick kiss on his cheek, leaving the faintest mark of lipstick behind.

"Thanks a million, Mr. Bexley! Give me your number—I want updates on any cool treasures!"

Evander obliged, exchanging contacts with her.

Watching the excitement in her eyes, his smile deepened. The second "nourishment" was already within reach.

Across the room, Jack's expression soured.

To him, Emily's kiss was a dagger between the ribs.

Still, with the two sisters watching, he swallowed his anger.

Drawing a slow breath, he forced a genial tone. "Emily, you're still into beach volleyball, right? It's Saturday tomorrow. Why don't the four of us team up—make a day of it?"

His gaze slid toward Evander, the friendly suggestion edged with challenge. "Just for fun. What do you say?"

Emily lit up, immediately turning to Evander. "I'm in! And I'm teaming with Mr. Bexley. You and Aileen can be the other pair. Losers buy dinner!"

"Deal," Jack said instantly, his jaw tight.

Volleyball was his arena. Tomorrow, he'd wipe that calm smile off Evander's face.

Aileen looked at Evander uncertainly. "Mr. Bexley, are you free tomorrow?"

"Of course," he said lightly. "I'd be glad to play with you two ladies."

Aileen only gave him a polite smile, missing the subtle undertone.

After a little more small talk, Jack rose. "It's late. I'll pick you up at the beach at ten tomorrow."

Evander stood as well. "Same here. See you then."

They left the castle side by side, each climbing into his own car.

Jack's military SUV pulled out first, its headlights slicing through the night.

Glancing into the rearview mirror, he caught sight of Evander's sedan rolling behind. His lips curled into a cold, bitter smile.

Evander wasn't just after his fiancée—he was winning over her sister, too.

Tomorrow, Jack promised himself, he'd make him regret ever setting foot in their world.
