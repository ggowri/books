Chapter 107 Goddess of Marriage Speaks

The rooftop was bare, with nothing but a few lightning rods standing tall against the night sky. Moonlight spread across the ground, casting a faint silver glow.

Kelly walked to the center of the rooftop, took out the bronze pendant once more, and closed her eyes as she drew in a steady breath.

This time, she didn't rush to recite the incantation. Instead, she let herself feel the energy between heaven and earth, trying to forge a connection with Frigg.

Felix stood nearby, watching Kelly anxiously, his fists clenched tight.

After a long while, Kelly finally opened her eyes. Her expression had hardened with resolve. She began to chant, her voice louder than before and filled with reverence.

As the words spilled from her lips, the wind on the rooftop began to stir, sweeping the fallen leaves into spirals through the air.

The moonlight seemed to shine brighter, illuminating the bronze pendant in Kelly's hand as the runes etched into it flickered with a faint glow.

Suddenly, a soft white light descended from the sky, enveloping her.

Felix's eyes went wide with shock. He tried to make out what lay within the radiance, but there was nothing to see.

Only a gentle yet commanding voice echoed through the air. "Kelly, I have heard your summons and your plea."

When Felix realized that it was none other than the Goddess Frigg, a fierce thudding rose in his chest, his heartbeat quickening uncontrollably.

He nearly cried out in excitement right then and there. But he quickly covered his mouth, terrified of disturbing the goddess.

The moment Kelly heard the voice, she dropped to her knees and spoke with deep respect, "Honored Goddess Frigg, my nephew Felix and I have suffered injustice. I beg you to help us.

"Evander, that commoner, not only tormented my nephew but also stole his fiancée, Lois. We ask that you punish him and make him pay for what he's done."

Frigg's voice remained gentle, yet her authority left no room for doubt as she said, "Kelly, I know what you have endured, but fate has its own course.

"Evander is not the vile man you imagine. Everything he has done has been to protect himself and Lois."

Hearing that, Felix couldn't hold back and snapped, "No, you've got it wrong. Evander, that peasant, is nothing more than a cheat!

"He threatened me with slow-acting poison, forced me to sign away my engagement, and locked me in the dungeons of Helheim. He's nothing short of a monster!"

Frigg's tone softened, but carried steady authority as she replied, "Felix, calm yourself. I can see what lies ahead. I am not mistaken.

"Lois will not remain with Evander in the end, not because of your vengeance, but because her father forbids it. Evander is of mixed blood. To the royal family, he is unworthy to wed into royalty, and even less worthy to inherit royal power."

Felix froze in shock. He never expected the one opposing the relationship to be Lois' father.

He quickly asked, "Are you saying that as long as Lois' father refuses, I still have a chance to be with her?"

Frigg's voice remained gentle as she answered, "That is correct. Your engagement with Lois was originally recognized by both families.

"As long as you let go of your resentment, stop clinging to revenge against Evander, and instead pursue Lois with sincerity, your families will help bring you back together.

"Evander and Lois were only ever meant to share a brief connection. It was never destined to last."

Kelly let out a quiet breath of relief. "So, you mean, even without revenge against Evander, Felix can still be with Lois?"

"Yes." Frigg's voice grew fainter. "The gears of fate have already begun to turn. All you need to do is follow your heart and do what must be done."

She added, "Remember, do not let hatred blind you. If you do, you will only bring yourself more pain. My power cannot linger in the mortal world for long. This is farewell."

As Frigg's voice faded, the white light surrounding Kelly slowly disappeared. The rooftop returned to its earlier stillness, with only the wind brushing softly past.

Kelly stood, looking at Felix with a relieved smile. "Felix, did you hear? Frigg said you still have a chance with Lois. You don't need to keep chasing revenge against Evander."

But Felix didn't share her joy. His brow furrowed, and bitterness still weighed heavy in his chest.

"Aunt Kelly," he said through gritted teeth, "that commoner tortured me and even slept with Lois. How can I just let him go? Even if I can be with Lois again, I want to make him pay."

Kelly tried to reason with him, saying, "Felix, Frigg already warned you not to let hatred take over.

"If you insist on revenge, you might provoke Frigg's displeasure. Then not only would you lose Lois, but you would also bring more trouble on yourself. Let's listen to her and set aside the hatred. Focus on winning Lois back."

Felix stayed silent for a moment, thinking about what Frigg had said and about his feelings for Lois. In the end, he gave a firm nod.

He said, "Alright, I'll do just that, Aunt Kelly. But I'm not about to forgive Evander for what he's done to me. If he dares to go after Lois again, I won't let it slide."

Kelly let out a quiet sigh of relief when he agreed and said, "Good. It's late now. We need to get back to the basement before the guards notice anything. I'll find a way to reach out to you again later, and we can figure out how to help you win Lois over."

With everything settled, the two of them quietly left the rooftop and retraced their steps back to the basement.

Felix returned to Kelly's ward, where they talked a while longer about plans to pursue Lois. Only after that did he reluctantly take his leave.

When he stepped out of the psychiatric hospital and got into the car, he watched the night passing by outside the window. The bitterness inside him slowly gave way to a growing anticipation for Lois.
