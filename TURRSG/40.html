Chapter 41 Two's Safer

"Check it out!" Ciotti's voice echoed as she spotted Evander. Her eyes sparkled with excitement. "Isn't this amazing? It's just like the scenes in Voyage au centre de la Terre."

Evander joined her, his chest tightening with awe. A hundred feet below the surface, and they'd stumbled onto a hidden world he never could've imagined.

"Let's wait for the others before we move forward," he said.

Ciotti nodded, already scanning the shadows. "Fine. I'll scout ahead, make sure it's clear."

Her figure slipped into the dim blue glow, boots clicking on the rocks like the toll of a distant bell.

Leaning against the wall, Evander glanced back at the rope swaying gently at the well's mouth. Madden and the others descended one by one.

Alex's boots scraped the wall, gravel tumbling softly.

"Hold on tight!" Madden barked from below.

Then Dylan's foot slipped. His body swung wildly, knuckles white on the rope.

Evander lunged, grabbing the line and steadying the fall. He guided Dylan down until his boots hit stone. Dylan collapsed to his knees, gasping, cold sweat glittering under the strange blue light.

"Stay close," Evander said, clapping his shoulder.

He scooped up a flashlight and swept it forward. Through the swirling green mist, faint glow-stick markers dotted the rocks—Ciotti's trail.

They caught up to her soon after. Ciotti was crouched by a protruding stone, fingers tracing strange engravings. Unlike the others, this one bore an arrow pointing right.

"My grandfather's mark," she said, standing and brushing dirt from her knees. "He mapped a safe route. Hug the wall."

The path narrowed to a single file. On their left, the damp wall dripped with cold beads of water. On their right yawned an abyss, green-blue light churning like some restless sea.

Evander edged sideways, shoulder scraping the stone. Madden's breath was hot at his back, earthy and sharp.

"Watch it!" Madden suddenly shouted.

Ciotti yanked Alex away from a loose rock just as it tumbled into the chasm. It took a full half-minute before the echo returned, deep and hollow.

"The ground's weathered," Ciotti muttered, prying open another stone with her dagger. Beneath, the rock was riddled with holes.

"Test before you step. Don't put your full weight down."

She pulled a rope from her pack, tied one end to her waist, and handed the other to Evander.

"Hold hands. If one of us slips, the rest can pull them back."

They linked together, moving slow and steady. Evander's hand closed around Ciotti's. In the pale light, her ponytail swayed, her camo uniform scuffed and scratched. He remembered her shielding him before the coffin, and his grip tightened unconsciously.

"There's a fork," Ciotti called, her flashlight sweeping across two passages.

The left was narrow, walls scraped by fingernails. The right was wider, but a fishy stench wafted from it.

Madden's nose wrinkled, face going pale.

"Miasma. One of my guys smelled it last time. Didn't even make it thirty feet before he collapsed, twitching like a puppet."

He pointed at the scratches.

"Our leader left these. Red rock dust was still under his nails."

Ciotti scraped the wall with her dagger. Red powder smeared her gloves.

"Sulfur. Nasty stuff. Wear these." She handed out anti-slip gloves, then turned sideways into the left passage.

"Follow my steps. Avoid the frost on the rocks."

The blue glow dimmed as the tunnel pressed in. Their flashlights painted narrow cones of light across slick walls. Evander's shoulders bumped stone again and again, each impact sending a dull ache through him.

Half an hour later, the passage opened wide.

Ciotti's beam landed on an iron suspension bridge stretched over a black abyss. Chains groaned in the draft, and the rotten planks swayed, dark as old blood.

"This is the only way," Ciotti said, kicking a board. Splinters broke off and fell, vanishing into the dark. "Chains are solid. Planks are trash."

Evander crouched to inspect the anchor. Rust peeled under his glove, revealing engraved numbers: 1947.

He scraped deeper—manganese steel. Strong enough for a ton of weight.

He turned to Madden. "You ever cross it?"

Madden shook his head. His excitement dulled to a grim frown.

"No. The miasma turned us back every time. Without you, we wouldn't have made it this far."

Dylan pointed across the gap. "Our leader's marks end there. We think… he might've fallen."

Ciotti shook the chains. The bridge rattled, groaning into the abyss. She clipped a safety lock onto the chain.

"I'll go first. Scout."

"I'm with you," Evander said, snapping on his own lock. The chain burned cold through his gloves, rust crumbling against the fabric.

"Two's safer."

Ciotti didn't argue. She clipped her flashlight to her chest and stepped onto the chain. Arms spread wide, balancing like a tightrope walker, her boots clinked with each step, the bridge swaying under her weight.

Evander followed, close enough to catch her if she fell. Her back tensed with every sway. The rope around her waist slid against his gloves, taut with his grip.

Midway across, a gust blasted upward from the abyss. Ciotti stumbled, nearly pitching forward. Evander yanked her back, holding them steady as the wind howled past.

For half a minute they clung to the chain until the gale eased. Then they pressed forward, inch by inch.

"Look—down there," Ciotti whispered.

Her flashlight pierced the dark. Far below, countless green lights churned, swirling upward. Eyes. Dozens—hundreds—of them.

"They're Soul-devouring Birds," Madden called from the cliff, voice tight. "They fear bright light. Blast them!"

Ciotti switched her flashlight to high-beam. Evander followed. The green eyes scattered instantly, shrieks cutting through the abyss.
