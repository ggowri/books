Chapter 98 Kelly Was Taken Away

Gary's words hit the hall like a bucket of ice-cold water, instantly dousing the once-enthusiastic atmosphere.

The applause died down abruptly, and the council members exchanged bewildered glances.

Chad, too, frowned, clearly taken aback by the sudden turn of events.

Lois stood there, utterly stunned. She instinctively tightened her hold on Evander's hand, and her fingertips turned as cold as ice.

"Sir Gary, you... you seemed to be on board with Vanguard Tech's tech earlier. What gives?"

Gary paused, throwing a haughty glance back at her.

"Approval doesn't equal cooperation.

"Royal Tech has decades of experience in humanoid robots. A startup like Vanguard Tech can't hold a candle to us.

"Their AI has its good points, but our R&D team reckons they can crack it in three years. No need to trade our core tech for it."

With that, he didn't waste any more time and strode out of the council hall.

One by one, the council members followed suit.

Some even shot sympathetic looks at Evander as they passed.

Chad walked up to Evander, looking apologetic.

"Sorry about this, Mr. Bexley. Never thought Sir Gary would flip like that," he said. "He's always been stuck on local companies, thinking foreign tech won't pose a threat to Royal Tech."

Evander said calmly as he closed his laptop, "It's all good. Cooperation is a two-way street. Royal Tech has its own reasons, and I get it."

He then turned to Lois. Seeing her still in a daze, he gently patted the back of her hand.

"Don't stress. We've got time.

"I've got a month to seal this deal, and less than a week's gone by. We'll figure it out."

Lois looked into Evander's compos time."

Before they knew it, the car pulleded eyes, and her anxiety started to melt away.

She knew Evander was no quitter. If he said they'd find a way, he must have a plan.

She nodded and took his hand again. "Okay, I'm with you. No matter what comes our way, I'm right here by your side."

Chad watched their intertwined hands, a mix of relief and worry flashing across his eyes.

"Even though this negotiation didn't pan out, I'll have another go at Sir Gary and see if I can change his mind. Take it easy."

"Thanks, Your Grace," Evander said, and then he and Lois left the council building.

Once in the car, Lois couldn't help but let out a sigh. "Sir Gary is so stubborn. Vanguard Tech's tech could really give Royal Tech a leg up, but he's too biased to see it."

"His bias is part of it, but there could be other factors." Evander leaned back in his seat, his fingers gently drumming on his knee. "Royal Tech might have its own tech roadmap or be worried about leaking its core tech.

"Anyway, let's handle Kelly's situation first and then focus on making this cooperation happen. One thing at a up in front of Ragnarok Psychiatric Hospital.

Evander and Lois got out and headed inside.

When they reached Kelly's ward, they found it empty, with only some food scraps on the floor.

Just then, the nurse, Martha, passed by and greeted them.

"Princess Lois, here to see Ms. Kelly Gosse?"

"Where is she?" Evander asked, his eyes scanning the empty room.

Martha sighed, "She was picked up by Fred from the Scientific Paranormal Research yesterday afternoon.

"They said Ms. Kelly Gosse used to work there and they needed to take her back to their headquarters for protection. And they've banned all visits. Even us nurses aren't allowed to ask about where she is."

"Fred?" Evander frowned, immediately catching on.

Fred was clearly trying to use Kelly's psychic powers to cause him more headaches.

He hadn't expected Fred to move so fast. It seemed the SPR was dead-set on being his enemy.

"What should we do now? The headquarters of the SPR is heavily guarded. There's no way we can get in," Lois asked worriedly.

Evander was silent for a moment. He took out a small bottle from his pocket, poured out a pill the size of a coffee bean, and handed it to Lois.

"Right now, we have to deal with another matter first.

"Take this and go to Felix's place."

"What's this?" Lois took the pill.

A gentle warmth spread from her fingertips, and a faint fragrance lingered around the pill.

Evander explained, "It's a pill that can wake Felix up tomorrow.

"Even though he's stopped breathing, this pill can activate his body's functions, and he'll come to tomorrow.

"Go to Felix's home and give the pill to his parents. Tell them this medicine can save Felix, but the condition is that they must annul your engagement with Felix."

Lois was stunned.

"Annul the engagement? But Felix is still..."

"Precisely because he's 'in a coma' now, his parents are desperate." Evander looked at her seriously. "Felix is their only son. They'll surely agree to any condition to save him.

"Besides, your engagement with Felix has long been a formality. Take this chance to end it, and he won't pester you anymore."

Lois thought for a while and nodded. "Okay, I'll go. But what about you? Aren't you coming with me?"

Evander said, "I'm going to check out the area around the SPR headquarters and try to find Kelly.

"Don't worry. I'll be careful and call you right away if anything happens."

Lois carefully slipped the pill into her bag and said, "You gotta be careful, okay? Don't go picking fights with those guys from the SPR."

"Sure thing," Evander replied with a smile and a nod.

He watched as Lois hopped into a taxi and drove off.

Then, he set his sights on the direction of the SPR headquarters.

The headquarters was housed in a massive factory on the outskirts of London. It was like a fortress, with security cameras perched everywhere and guards on constant patrol, making it a real tough nut to crack.

Evander didn't charge in blindly. Instead, he found a quiet nook nearby and activated his X-ray Function to scope out the situation inside the factory.

The factory was fortified to the hilt. Everywhere he looked, there were members of the SPR in black uniforms, clutching assault rifles.

Evander scanned each room meticulously with his X-ray Function.

Finally, he spotted Kelly in a basement room.

She was tied to a chair, looking as pale as a ghost. There was still a bandage on the wound on her forehead, and her eyes were wild and crazy.

Fred was standing in front of her, holding some strange contraption and doing goodness-knows-what.

Evander frowned. Just as he was about to take a closer look, his phone rang.

It was Lois.

He quickly answered, "Hey, Lois. How'd it go?"

"They agreed!" Lois exclaimed, her voice brimming with excitement. "I gave the pill to them. At first, they were skeptical. But when I let them feel the warmth of the pill and told them your deal, they talked it over and agreed.

"Now they've drawn up the annulment agreement. I've signed it, and they said they'll get Felix to sign it once he wakes up tomorrow."
