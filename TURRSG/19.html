Chapter 20 A Chance Delivered

Sophia shyly glanced at him, then lowered her head, taking small licks of her cotton candy.

Evander wanted to say something, but he was afraid of scaring the little girl, so he just ate in silence.

"Thank you... for before, sir," After a moment, Sophia spoke up softly, her voice barely louder than a mosquito's hum.

Evander was taken aback for a second, then smiled.

"You're welcome. Just stay away from deep pools next time. They're dangerous."

Sophia nodded and then asked, "Sir, do you know my aunt?"

"I do."

"Then do you know why she's sad?" Sophia asked, blinking her big eyes. "When she was helping me change, she kept shaking her head."

Evander paused for a moment, about to answer, when Kimberly returned with two plates of food.

She placed a plate of fruit in front of Sophia, then glanced at Evander, saying casually, "Thank you for earlier.

"If anything had happened to Sophia, I'd have no idea how to explain it to my sister."

"It was nothing," Evander said, his tone polite but cool.

He sped up his eating, eager to end the conversation.

"I heard the Summit Group has been through the wringer lately," Kimberly said, ignoring his distance.

"My father mentioned it to me the other day. He said your brain-computer interface project has a lot of potential, but luck hasn't been on your side.

"Sometimes, bending a little can take you further.

"Don't you think so, Mr. Bexley?"

The suggestion in her words was as clear as day.

A cold laugh echoed in Evander's heart, but his face remained impassive.

"Ms. Nelson, are you suggesting that I should bow to Denny?"

"That's not what I meant," she said, avoiding his eyes as she dabbed at Sophia's mouth with a napkin.

"I just think there's no need to butt heads.

"Even if the crisis is over, Summit Group must've taken a hit, right?"

Evander set down his utensils and wiped his mouth with the napkin.

"Thank you for your concern, Ms. Nelson, but the Summit Group will be fine."

He stood up. "I have something to do. Please excuse me."

Kimberly watched him leave, a complex expression in her eyes.

Sophia tugged on her hand. "Aunt Kimberly, he doesn't seem to like you."

"You're just a child; what do you know?" Kimberly said, stroking her head, her gaze hardening with a new resolve.

By five in the afternoon, after wrapping up the group activities, Evander prepared to leave early.

Iris needed to stay with others. She walked him to the parking lot and reminded him, "Drive safe."

"Got it," Evander replied, his tone soft.

As he spoke, a car rolled slowly toward them.

Evander didn't pay it much mind. However, when it drove close, its back window rolled down, and Sophia's little head popped out. She waved at him. "Goodbye, sir!"

A faint smile touched Evander's lips. "Goodbye, Sophia."

As he spoke, he noticed Kimberly's gaze fixed on him.

He paused, then gave her a slight nod in acknowledgment.

Kimberly nodded back, and after a brief glance at Iris beside him, she turned away.

As her car drove off, Iris rubbed her chin. "Did you see that? The way Kimberly looked at me... It felt like she was sizing me up."

Evander didn't think much about it. "Really? Why would she do that?"

Iris couldn't quite put her finger on it.

It was just a feeling.

She hesitated for a moment before suggesting, "Maybe she thinks I'm too close to you and got suspicious for Nadine's sake?"

Evander's smile faded, and he shook his head. "No. Trust me, it's definitely not that."

He knew Kimberly and her friends were close to Denny.

It was obvious that, between him and Denny, they'd rather see Nadine with Denny.

In fact, Kimberly and the other girls in their circle would probably be thrilled if he were to be with another woman. After all, that relationship could get him away from Nadine sooner.

So, the idea that Kimberly would be worried about him betraying Nadine was laughable.

Evander got into his car and drove off.

An hour later, he arrived at the Aromind Wellness Retreat.

Evander stood in the courtyard, looking at the gaunt, empty-eyed woman sitting in a chair nearby. That woman was his mother, Maleah Bexley. However, the person he saw now was so different from the mother he remembered.

Although he had grown used to seeing her this way, the sight still tore at his heart.

The director of the retreat, Killian Hayes, stood beside him and spoke softly, "I'm sorry, your mother's condition hasn't improved."

Maleah couldn't be around people from her past, as it could trigger another manic episode.

She was in a rare state of peace now, and Evander didn't want to disturb that fragile peace.

After standing there for a while, he moved away from the courtyard, worried that she might notice him.

Once he was at a safe distance, he spoke to Killian and the staff who cared for Maleah, "Please... take good care of her."

"Of course, Mr. Bexley. It's our duty."

Evander watched his mother through a window for a few more moments. Then, after leaving the books and supplies he had brought for her, he left the wellness retreat.

His heart remained heavy as he left.

On the drive back, he passed that wetland park again. Kites dotted the sky, vibrant against the blue. On impulse, he turned the car around and pulled into the park.

A gentle breeze swept through the park, the sun was shining, and the scenery was beautiful.

However, everyone else was there with a partner or family. Evander, on the other hand, was there alone, and he felt a little out of place.

He stood in front of a small stall, wondering whether to buy a kite for himself. Just then, a little hand grabbed his finger and gave it a shake.

"Sir."

Evander looked down in surprise. "Sophia?"

Sophia nodded slightly, her small hand still holding on to his.

Evander watched his mother through a window for a few more moments. Then, after leaving the books and supplies he had brought for her, he left the wellness retreat.

His heart remained heavy as he left.

"Sir."

Evander looked down in surprise. "Sophia?"

Sophia nodded slightly, her small hand still holding on to his.

"Sophia..." Kimberly came over, her voice trailing off when she saw Evander.

She paused for a moment before walking toward them. "Are you here to have fun, too?"

"Yeah."

Evander was about to leave, thinking he wasn't really familiar with Kimberly, when Sophia suddenly looked up at him and said, "Sir, let's fly a kite together!"

Evander froze.

He realized that this might just be his chance to get closer to Kimberly.

A genuine smile spread across his face as he looked at Sophia.

"Sounds good!"
