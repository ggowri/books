Chapter 18 Accepting the Mission

In the afternoon, Iris was summoned for questioning by the authorities.

Evander received a call from Norman.

"Evan, I've already sent people to handle things with Summit Group," Norman continued.

"The patent office investigation will be resolved soon. You don't need to worry about Iris."

Evander's head snapped up. "You intervened for Summit Group?"

Norman answered on the phone, his voice steady, "Yes."

Just then, Evander's phone buzzed to life.

The name "Lilah" flashed on the screen. It was his and Nadine’s five-year-old daughter, Delilah Ellery, who must have just returned from her grandmother’s house.

"Daddy!"

Delilah's soft, nasal voice came through, brimming with childish sweetness.

"I miss you! When are you coming home?"

Evander's heart melted, the weight of his anger and turmoil dissolving into a bittersweet helplessness.

He stepped to the window, gazing at the distant horizon where the sea met the sky, his voice softening to a tender murmur.

"Soon, Lilah. I'll be back soon. Be a good girl, okay?"

"Really?" Her voice sparkled with hope.

"Will you bring me a strawberry cake? The biggest one, please?"

"Absolutely, I'll get you the biggest," Evander promised, his eyes welling up.

After he hung up, he turned to face Ciotti.

She stood with her back to him, her shoulders shaking slightly.

Evander approached and gently patted her back. "Ciotti, could you keep an eye on Azure Cove Residence for me? I need to go to Ellery Villa..."

"You're really going through with this?"

Evander’s voice was low and devoid of strength. "I need to see Lilah."

"Aren't you afraid you'll get in too deep? Nadine and Kimberly... They'll eat you alive."

"I'll be careful."

Evander handed her a key. "Stay here and wait for me to come back."

*****

Meanwhile, Nadine and Kimberly strode into Nelson Capital Tower.

They pushed open the door to the chairman's office and immediately saw Naomi sitting on Oliver's lap.

Naomi was wearing a revealing spaghetti-strap dress, her red-manicured fingers massaging Oliver's shoulders in a way that was intimately provocative.

"Naomi?"

Nadine and Kimberly frowned instantly.

Naomi had been their childhood friend, but after the Paul Group went bankrupt, they hadn't seen her since. Neither imagined their reunion would be like this.

Kimberly's expression darkened as she turned to Oliver.

"Dad, what is this about?"

Oliver patted Naomi's backside, signaling her to stand.

"Naomi's now my personal assistant. She's here to help me with some business," he said calmly.

Naomi smoothed her dress, flashing Nadine and Kimberly a smug, provocative smile.

"Nadine, Kimberly, it's been a while."

Nadine ignored her and walked straight to the desk.

"Mr. Nelson, we're here to ask about the 85 billion dollars. When can it be transferred to Westbrook Group's account?"

Oliver leaned back in his chair, his fingers tapping on the desk rhythmically.

"Money isn't the problem, but money alone won't be enough," he said, his gaze turning sharp.

"Summit Group's success hinges on William's tech. You need to smooth things over with Evander and get William's core technology from him."

"Smooth things over with that loser?" Kimberly scoffed, her voice dripping with disdain. "He's a nobody now, not worth our time."

"You're wrong," Oliver said, shaking his head. "Evander's no ordinary man.

"He's William's most valued student. Find his weakness, and the technology will be ours."

Oliver's eyes glinted with ruthless intent.

"Once we have it, destroying Summit Group will just be a matter of time."

Something like hesitation flickered across Nadine's gaze.

Get Evander to hand over the technology?

It is almost an impossible task.

*****

The black Bentley glided through the gates of Ellery Villa.

Evander's grip on the steering wheel was so tight that angry red marks dug into his skin.

As he stepped out of the car, a joyful cry pierced the air.

"Daddy!"

Delilah, dressed in a fluffy pink princess dress, flew toward him like a little bird. She was still clutching the plush rabbit he had given her last time.

"I got you the biggest strawberry cake," Evander said, his voice softening as he talked to her.

He reached into the passenger seat and took out a beautifully packaged cake box.

Delilah's eyes sparkled with joy. She threw her arms around his neck, clinging tight.

"You're the best, Daddy! I don't ever want you to leave again."

Evander's heart stung. He stroked her silky hair, his throat tightening. "I'm not going anywhere. I'll stay with you."

Mary, the housekeeper, stood at the doorway. When she saw him, she froze for a beat before bowing politely.

"Mr. Bexley, you're back."

There was a flicker of surprise in her gaze. It was obvious that she hadn't expected to see him here again.

Evander nodded, taking Delilah's hand as they stepped into the living room.

The familiar crystal chandelier gleamed overhead, casting light across the expensive Persian rug.

On the wall hung the Ellery family portrait, where Evander stood at the edge, his expression stiff. In fact, he almost looked like an outsider in that photo.

"Daddy, will you read me a story?"

Delilah tugged him toward the stairs, her little shoes clattering against the floor.

He let her pull him upstairs into her bedroom.

The pink walls were plastered with cartoon stickers, and the bookshelf was crammed with picture books.

Evander sank onto the carpet, and Delilah nestled in his arms as he read The Little Prince.

Her breathing soon grew soft and even, and before long, she was asleep against his chest, her long lashes fanning out like delicate wings.

Carefully, Evander carried her to bed, tucking the covers around her.

Looking at her peaceful face, a mix of emotions churned in his chest.

For the chance to bring her back, maybe he could put his pride aside, at least for now.
