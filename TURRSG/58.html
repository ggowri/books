Chapter 59 A Virgin

Emily's voice was shaky. "Mr. Bexley... please..."

Hearing her, Evander kicked off his pants and moved closer. He gently positioned himself at her entrance, easing in slowly.

Emily let out a sharp cry, her body tensing as her nails dug into his back. The intense pain made her eyes well up with tears as they rolled down her cheeks.

Evander stopped, noticing the fresh blood. It was unmistakable—she was a virgin. Her blood bloomed like a red blossom against her pale skin.

A wave of tenderness washed over him, calming his initial urge. He softly kissed her tears away, his voice low and soothing. "Hang in there. It'll get better. I'll be gentle."

Emily nodded, biting her lip. She buried her face in his neck, breathing shakily. She could feel him inside her, a mix of pain and something else she couldn't quite describe.

Evander placed a ringed hand over the scene, and the ring glowed brighter, absorbing the mixed fluids. It seemed to react more intensely to the virgin blood, almost humming with satisfaction.

Soon, the pain on Emily's face faded. Just as Evander was about to pick up the pace, the doorbell rang, breaking the silence.

"Aileen?" Emily whispered, panic in her eyes. "Why's she back already?"

Evander froze, pulling away carefully. He glanced at the evidence of their encounter—the blood, the crumpled sheets—and his heart raced. If Aileen saw this, they'd be caught.

"Get dressed," he urged, tossing Emily a towel and quickly fixing his own clothes.

Emily wrapped herself up, temporarily forgetting her injured leg. She rushed to the wardrobe, grabbing a white ballet dress, her fingers fumbling with the buttons in her haste.

Evander, now composed, casually sat on the sofa with a dance magazine, pretending nothing had happened.

"I'm coming!" Emily called out, taking a deep breath before opening the door.

Aileen stood there with a bag of desserts. She raised an eyebrow, noticing Emily's flushed cheeks. "Why'd you take so long? I rang a bunch of times."

Emily quickly ducked her head. "Oh, Mr. Bexley was just helping with my leg. I twisted it, and he was massaging it. Guess we didn't hear the bell."

Aileen seemed satisfied with the explanation, lifting her bag. "I brought tiramisu for you and Black Forest cake for Mr. Bexley. Let's dig in!"

She walked in, her gaze landing on Evander, who sat coolly, flipping through the magazine.

"Mr. Bexley," Aileen began, "the editorial issue's sorted. 'Perfect Sex and Love' is good to go next week. I'll get you a signed copy."

"Thanks for your hard work," Evander replied, setting down the magazine. His smile was polite, hiding any tension.

Emily, trying to calm her nerves, grabbed a slice of tiramisu. "Aileen, you're incredible! It's gonna be huge once it's out."

Aileen chuckled, offering Evander some cake. "Try this. It's amazing."

"Thanks." He took a bite, but noticed Aileen's eyes flicker to his ring. His heart skipped, but he stayed composed. "Anything else you need help with now that the publishing thing's sorted?"

"Not right now," Aileen said, smiling. "But it's my Aunt Elsa's birthday tonight. Wanna come with me?"

"Elsa?" Evander was caught off guard. He knew who Elsa was—the powerhouse who became Deputy Mayor at just thirty-two.

She was always on TV, wearing perfectly tailored suits, speaking confidently at ribbon-cutting ceremonies and political meetings.

Her eyes held the same sharp intelligence as any man, yet she was strikingly beautiful, with gentle almond eyes that softened when she smiled.

She was the perfect blend of beauty and brains.

What struck Evander most was her story—her husband, a narcotics officer, died during an international drug bust five years ago. Since then, she'd thrown herself into her work, never discussing her personal life publicly. Her dedication became legendary in political circles.

To Evander, she had always been a woman far beyond his reach—a goddess on a pedestal.

He never imagined she would turn out to be Aileen and Emily's aunt.

The Arcane Ring required the essence of twenty-four women of royal blood to awaken.

Elsa, descended from the Norwegian royal family and wielding real power, would be an unparalleled offering. If he could obtain her essence, the ring would be nourished enough to grant him its first ability—the Inventory Cube.

He couldn't miss this opportunity.

"Mr. Bexley?" Aileen asked, noticing his silence.

"Yeah, of course," he replied, setting the cake down.

A look of delight crossed his face. "I'm just surprised. That formidable woman I've admired from afar is actually your aunt.

"I see her on television often and have always respected her decisiveness. I never thought I'd have the chance to attend her birthday celebration."

He paused deliberately, feigning hesitation. "But I should really prepare a proper gift before I meet her."
