Chapter 43 Do We Have Any Other Choices

Ciotti dug through her pack and cracked a line of glow sticks, laying them along the crevice floor until the path glowed faint green. Then she pulled out a coil of rope and tied it tight around Evander's waist.

"These rocks are brittle. Stick close, and don't touch the walls," she ordered, voice firm.

They crawled deeper, at times flat on their stomachs, the tunnel closing in like a throat. Slimy moss squelched under their boots. The air stank of sulfur, heavier even than the miasma zone.

"Light ahead," Ciotti whispered suddenly. She motioned for him to kill his flashlight.

A faint red glow flickered in the darkness—like the smolder of hot coals. It grew brighter as they squeezed through the last stretch and stumbled into a cavern.

Evander froze.

The cave was massive, its center dominated by a raised platform glowing a bloody red. Thousands of clay pots lined it, each exhaling wisps of scarlet mist. Ores embedded in the rock walls glowed faintly, bathing everything in a twilight haze.

"The Crimson Ruby Lode," Ciotti gasped, running her hand along the edge of the platform. "The ruby that nourishes elixirs—it's real."

Evander's gaze locked on a larger clay pot. Dense mist spilled from its rim, Latin inscriptions curling around its surface in the same skull pattern from their map. He reached for the lid—

"Don't touch it!" Ciotti shouted.

A bolt hissed from the ceiling, grazing his ear before burying itself in the platform. Venom dripped from its tip, sizzling in the red mist.

Evander snapped his head up. Dozens of dark shapes clung to the rock walls. Green eyes glowed, hungry and unblinking.

Soul-devouring Birds. More of them than they'd ever seen.

"They're guarding the pots," Ciotti hissed, yanking him behind her as she chambered her gun. "My grandpa wrote that the ruby mist makes them insane."

The birds shrieked and dove, a black tide sweeping down.

Ciotti's machine gun roared, muzzle flashes strobing red against the cavern walls. Feathers and foul liquid splattered the stone. Evander drew his pistol, aiming for wings, but even crippled, the beasts still clawed forward.

The cave became a slaughterhouse, red mist swirling in the gunfire like blood turned to smoke.

"Passage on the left!" Ciotti shouted, reloading. Blood spread across her sleeve where a claw had torn through.

Evander grabbed her wrist, dragging her toward the narrow tunnel. He hurled his empty pistol behind them, the clang slowing the birds' charge for an instant.

They slipped into the crevice single file. Evander hacked down vines to block the entrance, muffling the shrieks behind them. Their footsteps thundered in the cramped passage until they collapsed at the far end, gasping.

Ciotti tore open a med kit and bound her arm while Evander leaned against the wall, chest heaving. Then his flashlight beam cut forward—and froze.

Another bridge.

Rust ate through its chains, darker and thicker than before. Across the gap, etched in stone, was a full skull pattern.

Ciotti's lips curved into a tired smile. "Right track."

But her eyes lingered on the bridge, clouded. "I'm not sure this one will hold."

Evander's hand brushed the brass key in his pocket, cold and heavy. The elixir his grandfather had chased for decades—it had to be beyond this bridge. Madden's fall, the deaths, the cryptic carvings—all of it pressed down on him like fate.

Ciotti's beam swept the chains. Rust clung in clumps like scabs, flakes crumbling under her touch. "At least fifty years old," she muttered. "This is worse than before."

Evander stepped to the edge, picked up a rock, and dropped it into the abyss.

Seconds stretched. No echo returned.

Below, the green mist churned thick, jagged rocks jutting through it like lurking beasts.

The memory of Madden and the others plummeting into the dark slammed into him. His stomach lurched, and he staggered back a half-step, bile rising in his throat.

"Any other choices?" Ciotti scoffed. She pulled two safety locks from her pack and tossed one to him. "We're low on rations. Two days, maybe less."

Evander's fingers shook as he clipped the lock to his belt. His compass spun wildly. His arm throbbed from a bird's claws. They were burning through strength they didn't have.

"I'll go first," Ciotti said, snapping her safety lock onto the chain. She tested it with her boots; the steel groaned, but held. "You stay here. If it gives, shout."

Evander tightened the buckle at her waist, his jaw set.

"Turn back if it's too much."

She flashed a grin and stepped onto the chain. Her movements were slower this time, hugging the steel as she inched across. Rust smeared dark streaks under her boots.

The lock clicked rhythmically, echoing into the abyss.

Halfway across, the bridge convulsed. Not from wind. From below.

Ciotti's flashlight stabbed downward.

Through the mist, gray-white vines writhed up the chains. Dozens of them. Their tips tapered to spiked maws, like fanged serpents climbing for the kill.
