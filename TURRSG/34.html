Chapter 35 The third trail

The car rolled through the gates of Azure Cove just as twilight bled along the coastline, washing the sky in soft shades of pink and gold.

Evander stepped out, greeted by a salty breeze laced with the sweet perfume of gardenias. The tight coil in his chest eased a little.

Ciotti stood in the glow of the porch light, her burgundy silk gown spilling around her like a crimson tide. Her golden hair fell in loose waves over her shoulders, and a sly smile curved her lips.

She strolled toward him, trailing a fingertip across his chest.

"Well, look at you," she teased, her voice dripping with mischief. "Survived Sir Westbrook's Second trail, did you?"

Her eyes flicked down deliberately, lingering on his midsection.

"Hope you didn't burn yourself out completely."

Heat rushed to Evander's ears. Those thirty-seven fevered nights on that cursed island were supposed to stay buried. Hearing Ciotti drag it into the open felt like she'd ripped the cover off his most private secret.

He looked away, swallowing hard. "Did my grandfather tell you that?"

Her smile widened. "Sir Westbrook only said you were... efficient."

She hooked her arm through his, brushing his wrist with fleeting, electric touches as she guided him toward the house.

"But honestly, with someone like Ms. Nelson in your arms... who could blame you for slipping a little?"

Her voice dropped, the teasing edge fading.

"But go easy, Evander. The third trail's coming, and it'll take everything you've got—body and soul."

The warning slipped through like a quiet shot, concern breaking past her usual playfulness.

Evander pulled his arm free and headed inside.

"Let's take this inside."

For a second, surprise flickered in Ciotti's eyes, but she smoothed it over with her usual smile and followed.

The chandelier lit every corner of the living room, but it couldn't touch the shadow in Evander's eyes.

"Have a seat," Ciotti said, motioning toward the couch. Her heels clicked across the hardwood as she poured wine, her gown swaying, leaving a faint floral scent in her wake.

Evander's gaze landed on the coffee table. A yellowed parchment lay pinned under a glass paperweight, its edges scorched, strange symbols and winding lines twisting across it like an ancient map.

"What's this?"

He reached for it, but Ciotti's hand slid over his, stopping him.

"Easy there, boy." She handed him a glass, red wine swirling like molten rubies.

"That's Sir Westbrook's third trail."

Evander's pulse kicked up.

He slid the paperweight aside and snatched the parchment.

It was brittle, smelling of dust and time, the ink faded but the twisting lines still sharp. At the top, a crude skull leered above jagged mountain ranges. A circle in red ink marked one spot, next to a line of Latin script.

"A treasure map?" His voice shook with a mix of awe and disbelief.

He'd seen plenty of the world, but a map that old? That was a first.

Ciotti sipped her wine, her smile unreadable.

"Sir Westbrook wants you to find what it leads to within two weeks. The gold and jewels are yours—he only wants the elixir hidden there."

"Elixir?" Evander frowned.

"What kind?"

Ciotti shook her head.

"No idea. He just said it's worth more to him than his life."

Evander's grip on the parchment tightened, the paper crackling. His mind flashed back to his grandfather—the urgent tap of his cane, the shadow in his eyes.

Could this elixir be the key to everything?

"Oh—one more thing," Evander said, dragging himself back to the moment. "My grandfather mentioned another reward here. What is it?"

Ciotti set her glass down and stepped closer, her eyes glinting like starlight on restless water.

"Sir Westbrook's pairing you with a seasoned treasure hunter for this mission."

"Who?" Evander leaned in, curiosity sparking.

Ciotti rose on her toes, her breath warm against his ear.

"You're looking at her."

"You?" Evander blinked, pulling back. "You're a treasure hunter?"

To him, she was just the karate coach his grandfather had hired—graceful, sure, but a treasure hunter? That didn't track.

Reading his doubt, Ciotti laughed softly, amused.

"What, you don't believe me?"

She kicked off her heels, leaving just black ankle socks, and in one fluid motion snapped out a side kick. One heel flew through the air, smashing into a vase in the corner.

The vase exploded with a sharp crack, shards scattering across the floor.

Evander froze, stunned. This wasn't the Ciotti he thought he knew. Her eyes were razor sharp, her movements precise and deadly.

"I'm not just a karate champ," she said, stepping closer, flexing her wrists as her knuckles cracked.

"I'm also the heir to a line of treasure hunters."

Evander's eyes widened.

Ciotti's voice was steady, threaded with authority.

"Since I was a kid, I trained in more than just kicks and punches. Martial arts were just the basics. My real skills are geological surveying, wilderness survival, and cracking ancient codes.

"At ten, I trekked the Amazon. At fifteen, I dug up a seventeenth-century pirate wreck. By twenty…" She let the silence hang, letting the weight of it sink in.

"You're that good?" Evander breathed, awe replacing his doubt. "I believe you."

Now it made sense—why his grandfather had hired her, why he'd trusted her so deeply. Ciotti wasn't just charm and mischief; beneath it was steel.

With a satisfied smile, Ciotti plucked the map from the table and pointed to the jagged mountain symbols.

"This is three hundred miles from Nogales, deep in primeval forest. They call it Mortis Gorge."

Her tone darkened.

"It's not just predators you'll be up against. Strange, unexplainable things happen there. Records say thousands went into Mortis Gorge. Not a single one ever came back. That's why it's forbidden—no person or organization's allowed inside."

Evander's frown deepened.

"A forbidden zone? How do we even get in?"

"That's my puzzle to crack."

Ciotti folded the map, eyes sparking with a thrill that bordered on reckless.

"The deadlier the place, the bigger the secret it hides, right?"

Evander studied her, seeing the shift. The polished, playful Ciotti was gone. At the mention of Mortis Gorge, she lit up like a kid tearing into a Christmas gift.

"When do we leave?" he asked.

"Tomorrow," she said without hesitation.

"Gear's ready. I've already decoded the Latin. All that's left is pinpointing the site."

She paused, her gaze sharp.

"Are you going to tell Ms. Nelson? You'll be gone a while."

"No need," Evander said firmly.

"This doesn't concern her."

His tangled feelings for Kimberly, Nadine, and Iris were knots he wasn't ready to pull apart. Distance felt right. Time to breathe.

For now, his focus narrowed to a single point: grow stronger, face the trial, find the treasure.
