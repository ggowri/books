Chapter 64 The Unsung Hero

Elsa's black sedan cruised through the quiet early morning streets. The car was silent, except for the soft hum of the AC. Elsa leaned back in the passenger seat, occasionally glancing at the streetlights flashing by. Their glow softened her sharp daytime demeanor, revealing a hint of exhaustion.

Evander sat in the back, absentmindedly fiddling with the ring on his finger.

"How's your wound?" Elsa's sudden question broke the silence, her voice tinged with concern.

Evander paused, then shook his head. "Nah, it's just a few scratches. No big deal."

He hesitated before adding, "You were the mastermind in taking down the Night Owls, Mayor Burton. I just did what anyone would."

Elsa turned to him, admiration in her eyes. "Don't sell yourself short. Without your intel, we wouldn't have found their hideout so fast. You were brave and smart. Most people would panic in that kind of situation, but you stayed calm, mapped everything out, and remembered the routes. Not everyone can do that."

Evander gave a faint smile but didn't reply. He knew being too humble would seem fake. Keeping it low-key was the best way to stay on Elsa's good side.

The car went quiet again, but this time it felt more comfortable, like a shared understanding.

Around 3:30 a.m., they pulled up to Azure Cove Residence. Evander opened the door and turned to Elsa. "Thanks for the ride, Mayor Burton. Get some rest."

"Wait," Elsa said, rummaging through her bag and pulling out a small tube of ointment. "This is from overseas—great for disinfecting and pain relief. Use it on your scrapes."

She paused, then added, "And if anything swells or starts hurting bad, you need to hit the hospital. Don't try to tough it out."

"Thanks, I will," Evander replied, taking the ointment. Their fingers brushed for a moment, surprising them both.

Elsa quickly pulled her hand back, a faint blush on her cheeks, though she maintained her calm smile. "I'm off then. Call if you need anything."

"Will do."

Evander watched the car's taillights disappear into the night before heading inside. Back in his room, he took a shower, letting the hot water rinse away the night's fatigue. He looked in the mirror at the scrapes on his arm and the faint bruise on his neck, applying Elsa's ointment. The cool sensation was an instant relief.

Lying in bed, sleep didn't come quickly. His mind replayed the night's events—the adrenaline from the kidnapping, the tension of outsmarting the Night Owls, the thrill of escape, and the subtle connection with Elsa. This mission had definitely strengthened her trust in him.

The third dose of nourishment for the Arcane Ring seemed closer than ever.

Eventually, drowsiness took over. When he woke again, sunlight was streaming through the curtains, scattering light across the floor.

He got up, washed up, and made a sandwich for breakfast. Just as he settled with his phone, it rang. The caller ID read "Deputy Mayor Elsa."

He'd saved her number last night in the car.

"Mr. Bexley, are you up?" Elsa's voice was steady, with a touch of warmth.

"At three this afternoon, we're having a commendation ceremony at City Hall to honor those who stood out in the Night Owls operation. I want to give you a first-class merit award. You're Nogales' hero—you deserve it."

Evander's heart skipped a beat, but he frowned. If he appeared on that stage, every underground group in Nogales would zero in on him. He hadn't unlocked the Arcane Ring yet, hadn't built the strength to handle those threats. Exposure now could mess up his grandfather's test.

"I'm honored, Mayor Burton, but I have to decline," Evander said earnestly. "I'm just an ordinary guy. I don't want to be in the spotlight. If I'm up there and other underground groups target me, it'll bring trouble to me, you, and the police. I'd rather stay out of it."

There was a pause. Then Elsa replied, her voice understanding. "I get it. I didn't think it through. Okay, I won't push it. But the ceremony will go on. We'll tell your story and call you our 'nameless hero.' It's the least we can do to honor you."

"Thanks for understanding, Mayor Burton."

Evander exhaled in relief. "If you need me again, I'll do what I can to help."

After hanging up, he turned on the TV. At exactly 3:00 p.m., the ceremony began, broadcast live from City Hall. Elsa, in a sharp white suit, stood on the stage, radiant and confident. Hillary and several SWAT officers received their awards to loud applause, cameras flashing.

Soon, it was Elsa's turn to speak. Her voice carried through the TV into Evander's living room.

"In this operation against the Night Owls, one unsung hero stood out. Kidnapped, he stayed calm, mapped their hideout, gathered crucial evidence, and helped the police complete the mission. He wishes to remain anonymous and declined this honor, but we'll never forget his contribution. He is Nogales' pride—our true Unnamed Hero!"

Watching Elsa's sincere expression and hearing the crowd's applause, Evander's lips curved into a faint, calm smile. He lifted a glass of water, took a slow sip, feeling no particular surge of pride. To him, the "Unnamed Hero" title was just a step in his bigger plan. His real goal was to get closer to Elsa.

His phone buzzed with messages from Aileen and Emily.

[Mr. Bexley, did you see the ceremony? Aunt Elsa mentioned you! You're the Unnamed Hero!] Emily's message was full of exclamation marks and excited emojis.

[Mr. Bexley, well done! We'll celebrate once you've recovered.] Aileen's message radiated pride.

Evander replied: [Watching it at home. Thanks, guys. I need to recover, but I'll hang out once I'm better.]
