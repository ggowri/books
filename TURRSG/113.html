Chapter 114 The Airport Goodbye

"Everything's arranged in France," Norman said casually. "Go see a few runway shows, drop by the film festival, and maybe catch a volleyball match. Consider it a break. As for the sixth trial, come find me when you're ready."

Evander felt the weight in his chest ease just a little.

"Thanks, Grandpa. I understand."

The car pulled up to the Royal Horseguards Hotel.

Evander stepped out, slipping the three photos into the inside pocket of his coat without a second glance.

Back in his room, he pulled out his phone and opened his chat history with Lois.

From the first message to the last, the warmth was still there, but now it burned.

They were soulmates. Their bodies fit as though made for each other. But the truth was, they weren't meant to be.

Edward's decision had drawn a chasm between them that Evander couldn't cross, not yet. And rather than drag each other down, maybe it was time to cut the cord completely.

He took a deep breath and opened his contacts. His finger hovered over Lois' name.

For a long moment, he hesitated.

Then, gritting his teeth, he hit delete.

Her name vanished from the list.

He felt lighter, as if he'd set down a burden, but it also left a hollow ache in his chest, like something vital had been carved out of him.

He went a step further. With a few taps, he erased every word they'd ever shared. Just like that, it was all gone.

No more loose threads. No way back.

The next morning, sunlight broke through the London skyline.

Beams filtered in through the curtains, casting fractured shadows on the hotel floor.

Evander rose early and dressed in a crisp, casual suit before heading downstairs.

Norman was already seated in the lounge, with a coffee and a sandwich laid out in front of him.

"Morning, Evan. Breakfast's ready. After you eat, I'll have the driver take you to the airport. My flight to Nogales is this afternoon. You take your time in France. No need to rush back."

"Okay," Evander said, sitting down.

He picked up the sandwich, but his appetite eluded him. He took one symbolic bite and set it down.

Norman noticed.

"Don't dwell on it," he said calmly. "A change of scenery can do wonders for the heart."

Evander gave a quiet nod, saying nothing more.

After breakfast, the driver took him to the airport.

As the car moved through familiar London streets, Evander stared out the window. These were places he'd known, walked through, and shared with Lois. And yet, he felt no pull to stay.

He was starting a new chapter, one without her.

Heathrow Airport was bustling with travelers and noise.

Evander stood near his gate, boarding pass in hand, waiting for his flight to be called.

That was when he saw her. Across the terminal, Lois appeared with Charlotte and Felix walking beside her.

She wore a soft ivory dress, and the pendant still glinted around her neck, catching the sunlight. She looked just as beautiful, just as luminous, just as unreachable.

Evander's heart skipped a beat. He instinctively turned away, hoping she hadn't noticed. But she had.

"Evander!" Her voice broke through the crowd, bright and full of hope.

She pulled away from Charlotte's grasp and ran straight toward him.

Evander stood still as she reached him, her breath slightly uneven.

He wanted to tell her, "Take care of yourself." But the words twisted in his throat, and what came out instead was cold.

"You're leaving today?"

Lois nodded, still catching her breath.

"Yeah. To Switzerland. What about you? Where are you going? When will you come visit?"

Evander avoided her gaze.

"I'm headed to France," he said quietly.

"And, Lois, I'm not going to visit."

The smile vanished from her face. Her eyes widened in disbelief.

"What? What did you say? Why? We made a promise—"

"That was before," Evander replied, his tone flat.

"You're a princess. You'll never be free of King Edward's decisions. None of us can change that. It's better for both of us to move on. You focus on your studies in Switzerland. Find someone who can actually make you happy. And I'll... I'll let go."

"Let go?"

Tears filled Lois' eyes. Her voice trembled. "Is that how easy it is for you? Can you really forget everything we've shared?"

"It's not about whether I can," Evander said, meeting her gaze, "it's that I have to."

He paused and then added, "Lois, we're not right for each other. The gap between royalty and someone like me... It's too wide. We were always going to fall apart. Better now than later, before we destroy each other."

Just then, Felix strolled up and pulled Lois away from him. His smug grin was unbearable.

"See, Lois? He never really loved you. I'm the one who's always been there. I'm going to Switzerland with you. I'll show you what real love looks like."

Evander ignored him completely. His eyes stayed on Lois, and his voice remained calm.

"I wish you all the best in Switzerland. My flight's boarding. Goodbye, Lois."

Without another word, he turned and walked away. He didn't look back.

But behind him, he could hear her muffled sobs, and it twisted something deep inside him. Still, he didn't stop.

He knew this was what he had to do.

Only by cutting cleanly could they both move forward.

On the plane, he settled into his seat and leaned back, eyes closed.

He felt no regret, neither for deleting her number nor for the words he'd said.

Better to break now than to bleed later. Better to end it completely than to pretend there's still hope.

Maybe—just maybe—one of those three women waiting in France could unlock the Arcane Ring's third enchantment.

After all, they each carried royal blood.

The plane lifted off, heading toward France.

And far across the sky, another plane carried Lois toward Switzerland. Their paths had split.

From this moment on, their fates would no longer intertwine.

That afternoon, Norman boarded his own flight, bound for Nogales.

Seated in first class, he looked out over the clouds, a faint smile playing on his lips.

Evander had learned what it meant to choose and what it meant to sacrifice. He had ambition, and he had discipline. Now, he finally carried the heart of a man ready to take the Godfather's place.
