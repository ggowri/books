Chapter 69 A New Quest Begins

Evander stood at the villa's entrance, watching Elsa's white sedan disappear down the street before turning back to the living room.

His fingertips brushed the Arcane Ring.

The items in the Inventory Cube seemed to pulse in sync with his thoughts, transmitting a steady, comforting sensation.

He approached the coffee table, eyeing the half-full bottle of 1982 Lafite. Focusing his mind, he thought, "Store," and the wine and goblets vanished instantly, neatly tucked into the Inventory Cube.

With this ability, he felt more confident facing his grandfather's tests.

"I should deliver the elixir to Grandpa."

Evander thought of the black bottle of Focus Elixir Ciotti had left for him.

He hurried upstairs to his room and unlocked the wardrobe's hidden compartment.

Inside lay the bottle. He pulled out the stopper, tipped it over, and let the pills spill onto the table. After a quick count, there were one hundred ninety-eight.

He divided the pile in two—ninety-nine in a small porcelain bottle, the rest back in the original.

With a thought, he sent one into the Inventory Cube and tucked the other into his pocket.

Then, Evander headed downstairs to the garage.

He drove out in his Rolls-Royce, heading toward his grandfather's private estate.

Forty minutes later, the car reached the estate's entrance.

The gate guard, recognizing Evander, let him pass.

He parked the Rolls-Royce in the private garage and stepped out, only to be greeted by the butler, Aldred.

"Mr. Bexley, what brings you here?"

"Sir Westbrook isn't at home. If you're looking for him, he's at the Vanguard Tech building, handling important matters these days," Aldred said, bowing respectfully with a cautious tone.

"Vanguard Tech?"

Evander froze, caught off guard.

He knew his grandfather controlled vast underground operations but had never heard of "Vanguard Tech," a tech company. "Vanguard Tech is Grandpa's business?"

"Indeed."

Aldred nodded, a hint of pride in his eyes.

"Vanguard Tech specializes in artificial intelligence and humanoid robotics, with core technology at least a decade ahead of Summit Group and Westbrook Group.

"While Sir Westbrook isn't the president or chairman, everyone in the company knows his authority surpasses all management. He's the true power."

Evander's shock deepened.

His grandfather's influence was far greater than he'd imagined.

Suppressing his surprise, he nodded. "Thank you, Aldred."

He didn't ask more, instead driving his SUV from the garage toward the Vanguard Tech building in the tech park.

The Vanguard Tech building towered at the heart of Nogales' tech park.

Its fifty-story glass facade gleamed with a cold, metallic sheen in the sunlight.

The "Vanguard Tech" logo at the entrance radiated both innovation and authority.

Evander parked in the underground lot and stepped out, spotting a group of suited executives escorting a familiar figure from the building's main entrance.

It was his grandfather, Norman.

Norman, in a gray suit with neatly combed hair, looked vibrant despite being nearly seventy.

His presence commanded such respect that the business tycoons and tech elites around him instinctively bowed slightly.

His gaze swept the parking lot and landed on Evander.

"That's all for today's meeting. Refine the IPO plan and give me the final version by tomorrow morning."

"Yes, Sir Westbrook," the group replied, respectfully watching Norman approach Evander.

"Grandpa." Evander stepped out of the car and walked forward.

He pulled the original porcelain bottle from his pocket and handed it to Norman with both hands.

"I found the elixir. Please check if it's the Focus Elixir you wanted."

Norman opened the bottle. A rich herbal fragrance drifted out, subtle but potent. He studied it carefully, then his stern expression eased and a faint smile appeared.

"Only half a bottle, but yes—it's the Focus Elixir. Evan, congratulations on passing the third test."

Evander exhaled in relief, a trace of ease crossing his face.

"I'm honored to have completed it, Grandpa."

He didn't mention keeping the other half of the elixir.

Some things, he felt, were best left unsaid.

"Since you've passed, you deserve a reward."

Norman turned toward his black Maybach. "Get in. We'll talk details somewhere private."

Evander followed, and the driver smoothly headed toward the estate.

Norman pulled a document from his briefcase and handed it to Evander.

"This is a transfer agreement for 10% of Vanguard Tech's shares, your first reward.

"The company's IPO process starts next month. At the current valuation, these shares are worth at least ten billion dollars, and they'll double after the listing."

Evander took the document, his fingers brushing the heavy paper, the share amount and official seal clearly visible.

Though surprised, he kept his composure and nodded calmly.

"Thank you, Grandpa."

"Here's the second reward."

Norman pulled a gold-embossed ticket from his inner pocket.

It featured a detailed image of a luxury cruise ship, marked with a destination of "London, UK," and a royal crest in the bottom right corner.

"A first-class ticket for the Royal Glory cruise, departing next Thursday, with private butler service, direct to London's port."

Evander took the ticket, his fingers tracing its intricate design, his mind swirling with questions.

"Grandpa, is this ticket for the fourth test?"

"Exactly."

Norman leaned back, his gaze sharp and deep.

"The fourth test is to infiltrate the British royal family and secure a core technology exchange in humanoid robotics. You have one month."

"The British royal family?"

Evander's heart jolted.

He needed a pure royal noblewoman's vaginal juice to activate the Arcane Ring's second magic.

The British royal family was the perfect target.

This was a golden opportunity!

Suppressing his excitement, he feigned contemplation. "Royal Tech's core technology has high barriers, and the royal family is cautious. How can I gain their trust?"

"That's for you to figure out."

Norman smiled, pulling a file from his briefcase.

"Vanguard Tech has had three indirect collaborations with the British royal family, mainly in medical robotics. I've prepared an identity for you—Vanguard Tech's senior technical advisor for overseas tech exchanges.

"Several royals and nobles will be aboard the Royal Glory. Prove your worth, win their approval, and connect with Royal Tech's leadership."

His tone hardened.

"This test is all or nothing.

"Succeed, and Vanguard Tech expands into the Americas and Asia. You'll also begin taking over some of my authority.

"Fail, and not only will your shares be revoked—you can forget about inheriting anything."

Evander nodded solemnly. "I understand. Within one month, I'll secure the technology exchange with Royal Tech."

Norman nodded approvingly, patting Evander's shoulder. "Get going. You have three days to prepare."

Evander stepped out, watching Norman's black Maybach drive away.

Three days left—he could say goodbye to Iris, Kimberly, Aileen, Emily, and Elsa.

After all, he'd be abroad for a month.

He worried they'd call constantly, missing him, which could disrupt his focus.

But first, before anyone else, he needed to see his daughter, Delilah.
