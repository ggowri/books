Chapter 90 Heir of the Devilish Overlord

In an instant, Hela's expression turned ice-cold.

Who would've thought this mere mortal would dare flirt with her?

With a swift flick of her slender hand, a cold, fierce gust of wind shot straight at Evander.

The air carried the eerie wails of countless undead, as if they were eager to rip his soul apart.

"You arrogant mortal! How dare you disrespect me like that!" Hela snapped, her voice laced with cold, murderous intent.

Evander's face went pale. Instinctively, he raised his right hand.

Suddenly, the Arcane Ring on his middle finger glowed with golden magic, forming an invisible shield that blocked the wind. The gust hissed as it hit the shield, then vanished into nothing.

Hela shot up from her throne, her bright blue eyes locking onto the Arcane Ring on Evander's finger. Shock flickered across her face.

"Is... Is that the Arcane Ring?"

She stepped down from the dais, her gaze fixated on the ring from a distance.

The runes on its surface and the magic it emitted matched the legendary Arcane Ring of the Devilish Overlord.

Her voice wavered slightly. "What's your connection to the Devilish Overlord? How did you get his Arcane Ring?"

Evander's mind raced. He had no idea who the Devilish Overlord was, but he knew he couldn't show any weakness.

He smirked arrogantly. "Your Highness, who else could I be? If I'm wearing the Devilish Overlord's ring, I'm clearly his heir."

"What?!" Hela's eyes widened in shock.

That quickly turned into awe.

As the ruler of Helheim, she knew the Devilish Overlord's name struck fear into even the gods.

If this guy was truly his heir, he couldn't be just anyone.

Hela immediately backed off, her killing intent melting away as she became much more composed and respectful.

She stepped toward Evander, clearly eager to take a closer look at the Arcane Ring, to confirm if it was really what she thought it was.

But Evander raised his hand to stop her.

"Your Highness, don't come any closer," he said, his voice deadpan. "The left side of your face... kinda freaks me out."

Hela froze, her brow furrowing for a second.

Then it hit her—she was still in her half-beautiful, half-decayed form.

Quickly, she closed her eyes. When she opened them again, the decayed side of her body had vanished, leaving her looking like a flawless, ice-cold beauty.

With smooth skin, blonde hair, and piercing blue eyes, Hela's deep-purple robe clung to her graceful figure, the crow-feather cloak trailing gently behind her. She looked every bit the noble, cold goddess.

She flashed a captivating smile as she walked up to Evander, the bone-chilling cold around her only adding to her allure.

Evander's gaze naturally drifted to her chest. It was full, firm, and swayed with every step, giving off a dangerous yet seductive energy that made his heart race.

Without thinking, he smirked and teased, "Your Highness Hela, you're stunning. If you take one more step, I won't be able to keep my hands to myself."

Hela's smile faltered, her eyes flashing with a moment of surprise. But she quickly regained her composure.

She hadn't expected the Devilish Overlord's heir to be so bold—or so unrestrained.

Despite the surprise, she kept her cool. "Mr. Bexley, this is Helheim, the realm of the dead. It's not a place for flirting with goddesses."

Evander didn't seem bothered at all. Instead, he closed the distance between them, now standing only a foot away from Hela.

The coldness radiating off her was palpable, and he couldn't help but glance at the skin partially hidden by her robe.

With a mischievous grin, he said, "I'm just telling the truth, Your Highness. You're so gorgeous, has no one ever fallen for you?"

Hela's expression darkened, her voice becoming icy. "Mr. Bexley, you need to watch your tone. I'm the ruler of Helheim, not some woman you can flirt with on a whim."

She stepped back slightly, her eyes narrowing with warning. "Keep acting disrespectfully, and even though you're the heir of the Devilish Overlord, I won't hesitate to put you in your place."

But Evander wasn't intimidated. He knew the Arcane Ring would protect him, and Hela's fear of the Devilish Overlord worked in his favor.

He reached out to touch her cheek, his voice full of playful charm.

"Your Highness, relax a little. Now that we're face to face, how about we talk? Like, why bring me to Helheim? Who's really behind all this?"

Hela instinctively pulled back, dodging his touch. She knew she had to tread carefully around the heir of the Devilish Overlord, so she spoke with more patience.

"Kelly begged me to bring you here. She's one of my followers, a former psychic from Scientific Paranormal Research. She wants me to take your soul to Helheim and keep you here forever, to get back at you for what you did to her nephew, Felix."

"Felix, again?" Evander's eyes narrowed, his expression hardening. "So he's the one behind this. Guess I went easy on him last time."

Seeing the killing intent in Evander's eyes, Hela was a bit taken aback.

This heir of the Devilish Overlord was just as ruthless as the Devilish Overlord himself.

She quickly said, "Mr. Bexley, you're just a soul now and can't leave Helheim without my say-so."

Evander looked at Hela, his tone menacing. "So, whether I can go back depends on your mood?"

Hela quickly shook her head.

"No way. You're the heir of the Devilish Overlord. I wouldn't dare to keep you here. As long as you promise to behave, I'll send you back to the mortal world right away. And I'll help you deal with Kelly and Felix as an apology."

Evander was tempted. He knew he needed Hela's help.

Kelly's ability to summon Hela was a real threat, and Felix was always looking for a chance to get even.

If he could get Hela on his side, his troubles in London would be a lot less.

He dropped the teasing tone and said seriously, "Okay, I promise to be respectful. I can forgive you for bringing me here, but you gotta do two things for me."

Relieved, Hela nodded quickly. "Tell me, Mr. Bexley. I'll do what I can."

Evander said with a cold look in his eyes, "First, cut off your connection with Kelly. Her ability to reach you is a threat to me, and it's gotta go.

"Second, bring Felix here and lock him up for three days. Let the people at Scientific Paranormal Research know it's not me who's going down tomorrow, but him—Felix. But don't kill him. I'm not done with him yet."

Hearing Evander's laughter, Hela saw a glimpse of the Devilish Overlord in him.

She hesitated for a moment.

Kelly was a bit... eccentric, but she was her follower. The psychic who could talk to her. But an order from the Devilish Overlord's heir came first.

Finally, she nodded. "Fine, I'll do what you ask. I'll cut ties with Kelly and bring Felix here for three days."

Evander gave a satisfied nod.

"Good. Now send me back. I've got things to handle."

Without hesitation, Hela waved her hand. A dark glow surrounded Evander's soul.

He felt himself floating again. The scene blurred before his eyes. The cold, dark feeling of Helheim slowly faded away, replaced by familiar warmth.

When he opened his eyes again, he found himself in the bedroom of the Royal One Estate.

The first light of dawn filtered through the window.

Lois was still in his arms, fast asleep.

Evander touched the Arcane Ring on his finger. The faint golden glow still lingered. The whole experience had felt like a dream, but it was too vivid to be one.

Sitting up, he looked at Lois. A smile spread across his face.

He was back.

If he'd died in Lois' bed... it would've left her with a lifetime of grief and unease.
