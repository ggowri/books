Chapter 110 The Princess in the Attic

Lois clutched the cool pendant around her neck, and at last, the tears she'd been holding back began to fall.

She wrapped her arms around Evander's waist, pressing her face to his chest.

Her voice trembled as she choked out, "I don't want to go... I'm scared that if I leave, I'll never see you again."

"You will," Evander whispered, holding her tightly. His hand moved gently along her back, as if calming a frightened kitten.

"I'm going to finish the Decision Matrix Model and integrate it into the AetherMind Chip. If the research impresses King Edward, he'll recognize my value," he said quietly, his eyes steady on hers.

"Once that happens, I'll come to the Royal Three Estate and bring you back. We won't be apart again. Just promise me, don't argue with him. If anything happens, tell me. Promise me."

Lois nodded firmly, wiping the tears from her face. Her gaze slowly steadied.

She understood now. This parting was just the price they had to pay for a future together. She wanted to be his peace, not his burden.

Rising on her toes, she brushed a soft kiss against Evander's lips. Her voice was still a little shaky, but full of resolve.

"I'll wait for you. Just don't take too long. Please."

Evander gave a slight nod and gently tucked her hair behind her ear. Then he watched as she opened the door and walked out with the guards.

He stood by the window, eyes locked on the black sedan outside. He didn't look away until it disappeared at the far end of the street.

Walter stepped up beside him and placed a comforting hand on his shoulder.

"Don't be too discouraged. Princess Lois is King Edward's daughter. She'll be safe. What we need to focus on now is finishing the model. It's the key not only to your partnership with Royal Tech, but also to standing beside Lois with your head held high."

Evander nodded hard. "Right."

Suppressing the storm of emotions inside, he returned to the desk and powered on his tablet.

The Decision Matrix Model lit up the screen.

Every algorithmic module, mapped to a node on the Tree of Life, and every line of logic hidden in the code was the only thing keeping him grounded.

Over the next ten days, Evander buried himself in work at the Institute for Esoteric Studies.

His desk became a mountain of ancient texts on the Kabbalistic Tree of Life, AI research manuals, and scrap paper filled with equations.

He barely paused to eat, grabbing a quick takeout meal before going right back to the screen.

Walter watched him throw himself into the work, both heartbroken and deeply impressed.

Every day, he'd prepare a summary of key points for discussion and have his assistant deliver fresh coffee and pastries.

"Research is important," he reminded gently, "but you won't last long if you run yourself into the ground like this."

Evander's hand paused on the mouse. His bloodshot eyes didn't waver.

"I can't afford to slow down. Lois is still stuck at the Royal Three Estate. The faster I deliver results, the sooner she can come back to me."

He pointed to the updated interface on the screen.

"I've integrated the logic of the Tree of Life with the signal transmission system in the AetherMind Chip. The robot can now adjust its decision-making in real time based on different scenarios.

"When it encounters obstacles, it weighs the risks of going around versus climbing over. It doesn't just follow orders anymore."

Walter leaned in for a better look.

The simulation showed a humanoid robot moving smoothly, navigating around obstacles, and adjusting its lighting system according to the brightness of the environment. Its motions were almost indistinguishable from a person's.

"This is incredible!"

Walter clapped his hands in excitement. "This goes beyond anything Royal Tech has. Sir Gary is going to be blown away!"

Evander nodded, already tweaking lines of code again.

"It's still not enough. I want it to have something closer to emotional feedback. For example, when it detects danger, I want it to react with more than just avoidance. I want it to show signs of alertness, the way a person would.

"That's how we break through the limits of Royal Tech's current framework."

And so, the two of them plunged deeper into their research, refining the model piece by piece.

Evander would occasionally text Lois with updates, hoping to reassure her.

Meanwhile, in the attic of the Royal Three Estate, Lois lived for the faint buzz of her phone.

The attic was small, with a single window overlooking a vast rose garden.

She would sit there every day, clutching the pendant around her neck, staring out at the flowers, silently wishing for a glimpse of Evander.

Two maids took turns keeping watch outside her door, and two large bodyguards stood downstairs. She wasn't even allowed to leave the floor, let alone step outside.

Edward stopped by occasionally. And every time, he tried to change her mind.

"Lois, you are the Crown Princess. You carry the dignity of our bloodline. You cannot lower yourself for some commoner. The Gosse family has been close to ours for generations. Felix is your rightful match."

Every time, Lois held her ground.

"Father, I don't care if Evander's a commoner. He's brilliant. He's capable. And I know you'll see that one day. I will never choose Felix. I love Evander."

Edward, frustrated by her stubbornness, left in a huff. After that, he stopped visiting altogether, merely instructing the staff to keep an eye on her.

Felix had come once.

He brought an armful of expensive gifts and wore that fake smile she hated so much.

"Lois, I know you're waiting for Evander, but he's a fraud. He's not going to invent anything groundbreaking. Just give up already. Come back to me. I'll be really sweet."

Lois didn't even blink. She grabbed his gifts and threw them out the door.

"Felix, wake up. Evander will come for me. You should be the one giving up."

Humiliated, Felix stormed off with a dark look on his face. But with Edward's warning still in effect, he didn't dare retaliate.

Day after day passed. Before long, two weeks had gone by.

Then, one early morning...

Evander finished debugging the final block of code. When the words "Model Test Successful" appeared on the screen, he let out a deep, shaky breath and nearly shot out of his chair.

Without wasting a second, he called Chad, his voice brimming with excitement.

"Duke Chad, the Decision Matrix Model is complete! I want to present a live demonstration in three days. I want the Royal Tech scientists and you to be there. This isn't just about the partnership anymore. This is the only way I can bring Lois back."
