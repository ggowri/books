Chapter 99 Save

"Great news!" Evander heaved a sigh of relief. "Where are you at the moment? Do you need me to come and pick you up?"

"I've just left Felix's and I'm going to take a cab back to the manor. Any progress in finding Kelly?" Lois responded.

Evander informed her, "Yeah, she's in the basement of the Scientific Paranormal Research headquarters, under Fred's control.

"But the place is swarming with guards. I can't get in right now. Once you're back at the manor, stay there. I'll head back after I deal with this."

"Sure. Just be careful, all right?" With those words, Lois ended the call.

Evander put his phone in his pocket and continued to keep an eye on the factory.

He saw Fred waving some gadget in front of Kelly.

Suddenly, Kelly's body began to shake violently, and she kept muttering something.

A self-satisfied smile appeared on Fred's face. It seemed that he was trying to force Kelly to summon a deity.

Evander's eyes glinted with a frigid coldness.

Fred's madness knew no bounds as he was trying to force Kelly to summon a deity just to defeat Evander. The thought of it made Evander's blood boil.

He was well aware that time was of the essence. Every second he delayed was a ticking time bomb; if he didn't rescue Kelly swiftly, the consequences would spiral out of control.

Moving with the stealth of a shadow, Evander made his way towards the factory's back door.

As expected, it was lightly guarded, with only two soldiers standing watch.

Reaching into his pocket, Evander pulled out a compact tranquilizer gun.

It was a well-thought-out preparation, a tool he'd stashed away precisely for moments like this.

Holding his breath, he waited for the guards to turn around. Then he pulled the trigger, and two tranquilizer darts flew straight into the guards' necks.

The guards dropped to the ground silently.

Evander rushed over, dragged them into a hidden corner, then put on one of the guards' uniforms, pulled down the cap, and walked into the factory.

Most of the members of the SPR inside were on patrol. No one paid any attention to this "new guard".

Following the route he had seen with his X-ray Function, Evander made his way to the basement.

The entrance to the basement was guarded by four soldiers with heavy weapons. It was even more heavily defended than other areas.

A direct assault would be fruitless. He glanced around and spotted a fire hydrant close by. An inspiration struck him.

He stealthily approached the fire hydrant, feigning an inspection of the equipment. Then, when the guards were off guard, he abruptly turned the valve.

A powerful stream of water gushed out and surged towards the guards.

Caught by surprise by the sudden deluge, the guards scattered in all directions.

Seizing the opportunity presented by the chaos, Evander dashed forward and delivered a punch to the back of each guard's head. They instantly slumped unconscious.

He swiftly opened the basement door and stepped inside.

The basement was dimly illuminated, with only a few emergency lights flickering.

As Evander made his way down the corridor, he soon heard Kelly's shrill screams.

He quickened his steps and reached the room.

There, he saw Fred pointing a device at Kelly's temple. Kelly's face had turned a sickly bluish-purple, and she appeared on the verge of collapse.

"Fred, stop!" Evander thundered as he charged into the room.

Fred was startled to see someone burst in. When he realized it was Evander, his face contorted with rage.

"Evander! How dare you show up here!"

He pressed a button on the device, and Kelly's body convulsed even more violently. Foam began to dribble from her mouth.

Narrowing his eyes, Evander took out another tranquilizer gun and shot at Fred.

Fred was quick on the draw. He dodged to the side, and the dart hit the wall.

He tossed the device aside, pulled out a pistol from his waist, and aimed it at Evander.

"Your time's up!"

Evander remained calm, ducking behind a table to avoid the gunfire.

Bullets pelted the table, sending wood chips flying everywhere.

Evander took advantage of the moment to pull out another tranquilizer gun from his pocket and fired at Fred's shoulder.

This time, Fred couldn't dodge. The tranquilizer dart hit him square on, eliciting a pained groan from his lips.

The pistol slipped from his fingers, clattering onto the floor as his body began to sway unsteadily.

Evander sprang forward, delivering a swift kick that sent Fred crashing to the ground. Without hesitation, he moved to untie the ropes that bound Kelly.

Kelly was in a pitiful state, so weak that her legs couldn't support her weight. She leaned against the wall, gasping for air like a drowning woman grasping at straws. The wild, deranged look in her eyes had vanished, replaced by sheer, unadulterated fear.

"Come with me," Evander said as he supported Kelly and was about to lead her away.

Just then, the sound of hurried footsteps echoed from outside.

The other members of the Scientific Paranormal Research and the special-forces soldiers had heard the gunshots and were rushing towards the basement.

Evander knew there was no time to waste. Supporting Kelly, he sprinted towards another exit of the basement.

Beyond the exit was a thick forest. Once they got in, they could shake off the pursuit of the Scientific Paranormal Research.

The members of the Scientific Paranormal Research and the special forces soon caught up and opened fire.

Bullets whistled past them. Evander and Kelly ran for their lives and finally made it into the forest.

The dense foliage in the forest made the members of the Scientific Paranormal Research hesitant to enter. They could only shout from outside.

Evander supported Kelly and found a secluded cave to take shelter in.

Leaning against the cave wall, Kelly was still gasping for breath. She looked at Evander with a complex mix of emotions in her eyes. "Why did you save me? I even wanted Hela to kill you before."

Evander replied calmly, "I'm not doing this out of the goodness of my heart. I just don't want to see Fred exploiting your abilities for his idiotic plans.

"Moreover, your nephew Felix isn't dead. He'll come to his senses tomorrow.

"And you've been out of your mind. If Fred keeps controlling you, you're doomed."

Kelly fell silent.

Looking down at her hands still marked red by the ropes, she realized Evander was right.

Fred didn't care about protecting her; he just wanted to use her.

If Evander hadn't saved her, she might have been forced by Fred to summon some unknown deity, and the consequences could have been disastrous.

"Felix... Will he really wake up?" Kelly suddenly asked, her voice quivering.

Evander said, "He'll wake up tomorrow. If you still want to see him, follow me quietly and stop causing me problems. Otherwise, even if Felix recovers, you won't get to see him."

Kelly looked into Evander's determined eyes and finally nodded. "Okay, I'll come with you. But you have to promise me I'll see Felix wake up."

"I promise," Evander said.

Then he helped Kelly out of the cave and headed in the direction of the Ragnarok Psychiatric Hospital.

He knew that after dealing with Kelly's situation, his next task was to figure out how to repair the cooperation with Royal Tech.

Even though Gary was stubborn, Evander believed that as long as he found the right approach, he could surely get Royal Tech to change its stance.
