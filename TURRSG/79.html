Chapter 80 Penetrating Gaze

Felix stormed into the cabin.

A Vanguard MP5 submachine gun met him, its muzzle pressed firmly under his chin.

Evander pushed upward, forcing Felix's head back.

Lois, now dressed, had slipped out in the chaos.

"Felix." Evander's voice was ice-cold, devoid of warmth.

"You're breaking into my cabin armed. Under maritime law, I have every right to defend myself."

Felix froze, his hand trembling around his pistol.

He hadn't expected Evander to be armed with a submachine gun—or to react so swiftly.

"I'm here for Lois!" Felix growled, shoving the MP5 aside and barging deeper into the cabin.

He tore through the room like a man possessed, ripping back the bedsheets, yanking open the wardrobe, and even dropping to his knees to check under the bed.

The cabin was small—there was no way to hide anyone. He rushed to the glass balcony, but Lois was nowhere to be found.

Panting heavily, his eyes bloodshot, Felix glared at Evander like a cornered beast.

"You damn magician! Where'd you hide her?"

Evander stared at his frantic display, then raised a hand.

A sharp slap landed across Felix's face, leaving his cheek red and a trickle of blood at his lip.

Felix staggered, stunned.

He hadn't expected Evander to strike him.

"You hit me?" he sputtered, raising his pistol.

But Evander was faster; the MP5's cold barrel was now pressed against Felix's forehead.

The chilling touch made Felix shudder.

"Banker's son," Evander sneered, his voice dripping with disdain.

"Lois went to the bow deck. All she wanted was for us to be civil, but you had to escalate things. So much for being a gentleman."

Felix's fingers quaked, and he lowered his pistol.

He knew Evander wasn't bluffing after that slap. He believed a man bold enough to strike him wouldn't hesitate to pull the trigger.

After all, he was in Evander's cabin now.

Just then, hurried footsteps echoed from the corridor.

Daniel burst in, breathless, grabbing Felix's arm.

"Felix, cool it! We found Lois! She's at the bow deck, playing with a kid at the sand table!"

"What?"

Felix spun around, disbelief etched on his face.

He holstered his gun, his rage cooling into reluctant embarrassment.

After a tense silence, he extended a stiff hand to Evander.

"Look... I lost my temper. Let's put this behind us. Maybe we can work toward being friends. I'll get someone to repair your door."

Without another word, he turned and strode toward the bow deck, Daniel trailing close behind.

Evander stowed the MP5, a cold smirk tugging at his lips.

Suddenly, a searing heat pulsed from the Arcane Ring on his middle finger.

He lifted his hand, studying the ring.

Its dark surface was transforming, now shimmering with a deep, molten gold, as if liquid metal flowed across it.

A voice echoed in his mind.

"Royal essence absorbed. Secondary ability unlocked: Enhanced Vision. Two more power sources needed for full stabilization. Current capacity: 30-foot penetrative sight through solid obstacles—wood, metal, glass, fabric, etc."

As the voice faded, a surge of warmth flooded from the ring, racing up his arm and pooling at the base of his skull.

His eyes glowed faintly gold.

A strange sensation rippled through him, like an invisible lens sharpening his vision.

Evander frowned slightly, testing the new power.

Instantly, a miraculous sight unfolded.

The cabin's thick wooden wall turned transparent, revealing the scene in the next room.

He saw a man in pajamas lounging on a sofa, phone in one hand, a glass of red wine in the other.

When Evander relaxed his brow, the wall snapped back to normal.

"It actually works!" he exclaimed, his heart racing with exhilaration.

He furrowed his brow again, this time aiming his gaze at the wardrobe.

The wooden panels vanished, exposing neatly hung clothes, a spare pistol tucked in a corner, and even the man next door, his clothing now invisible.

"Holy hell!

"That's crazy!"

Evander's grin was almost manic.

Felix loves gambling, doesn't he?

With this new ability, I could clean him out at the tables.

Excited by the thought, he made his way swiftly toward the bow.

But as he turned a corner in the corridor, two figures blocked his path.

They were tall, striking blondes—twins, by the look of them.

The resemblance was uncanny, as though carved from the same mold.

Both wore pale yellow sundresses, their hems skimming their thighs, revealing long, sexy legs. Golden curls cascaded over their shoulders, their doll-like features striking, though their eyes held a spark of suspicion.

"You're that magician from earlier, right?" The one on the left said, her voice crisp and bright.

"We're Lois' cousins. I'm Anya Stuart, and this is my sister, Andrea Stuart. Where'd you take our cousin? Everyone saw you pull her away!"

Evander's gaze flicked to them, and instinctively, he furrowed his brow.

He still wasn't used to toggling the ability on and off.

The effect kicked in instantly, and his breath caught.

Their dresses vanished, revealing two breathtakingly sensual figures.

Their skin was flawless, their full breasts tipped with delicate pink, their waists impossibly slender, and their subtle curves framed by faint golden hair. Every inch of them radiated raw allure.

Evander's body reacted, his throat tightening as he swallowed hard.

Forcing his brow to relax, he broke the spell. Their dresses shimmered back into existence, covering what he had seen.

"You've got it wrong. Lois isn't with me," he said calmly, pointing toward the bow deck.

"She's up ahead. I was just heading there myself."

Anya and Andrea exchanged skeptical glances.

But Evander's calm expression gave them pause. They said nothing more and only followed behind him.

"We're coming with you," Anya said, her tone wary. "If you're lying, we're calling security."

Evander said nothing, leading the twins toward the deck.

As they rounded the corner, he spotted Felix crouched by a sandpile, his expression softened as he spoke to Lois.

Lois, radiant in a golden gown, knelt at the sand table, guiding a four-year-old girl's hand as they traced letters with a tiny shovel.

The deck's lights bathed her in a regal glow.

"Lois, I got carried away earlier," Felix said, his voice laced with regret.

"That Mr. Bexley's got some serious skills. You were right. I shouldn't have been such a jerk. Maybe he and I could actually be friends."
