Chapter 19 Team-Building Trip

The next morning, Evander drove to the Summit Group offices.

The glass doors of the building shimmered in the soft morning light.

He got out of the car and stood at the entrance, his gaze sweeping over the employees' faces, which were once again filled with long-lost smiles.

The tension that had held him captive finally began to ease.

The receptionist spotted him, her eyes lighting up as she bowed with a grin.

"Mr. Bexley, you're back!"

Evander nodded and strode into the office.

The clatter of keyboards filled the air, a symphony of productivity. The meeting room door stood ajar, where the product team hashed out the latest iteration plans. On the wall, a performance chart was marked with red arrows pointing upward.

Everything signaled that Summit Group's crisis had passed.

"Evander!"

Iris, dressed in a crisp suit, rushed out of her office with a stack of files in her arms and a smile she couldn't hide lighting up her face.

"You're finally here! The patent office folks left this morning. They said the investigation was a misunderstanding and even apologized!"

"Thank you for everything," Evander said, taking the files from her.

Their fingers brushed, and they both flinched away as if from an electric shock, a blush rising on their cheeks.

"It was nothing," Iris said softly, looking down.

"To celebrate the end of the crisis, I thought we could do a team-building trip. Everyone's been so stressed lately," she said in a low voice.

"That's a great idea," Evander smiled. "You pick the spot."

"I want to go to a spa resort!" Iris' eyes sparkled like a kid unwrapping a gift. "There's a new resort just outside the city. It's got everything—hot springs, a barbecue pit, and even a camping area!"

Evander nodded. "Alright, let's go there."

The team-building trip was set for that afternoon. About thirty people piled into a large bus and headed out of the city.

The bus was filled with laughter and cheerful chatter. Ashton from the planning department strummed his guitar and sang, while the marketing girls passed around snacks, giggling.

Iris sat next to Evander, showing him pictures of the spa resort on her phone.

"Look at this infinity pool. You can see the stars at night," she said, pointing to a nighttime photo, her eyes gleaming with anticipation.

Evander's gaze lingered on the pool's edge in the photo, a faint smile tugging at his lips.

"It's beautiful."

The resort was even grander than the pictures, its classic architecture nestled among lush green trees, the air tinged with a faint sulfur scent.

After checking in, everyone went to their rooms to change into their robes. They all agreed to meet in the hot springs area in half an hour.

Evander slipped into a navy-blue bathrobe and headed to the men's bathing area. It was empty when he arrived.

He chose a pool by the window and sank in, the warm water enveloping his chest, easing his taut muscles.

Sunlight streamed through the glass ceiling, casting dancing reflections on the water's surface. For a moment, he felt a rare sense of peace.

Less than a minute later, he caught a glimpse of a small figure crouching at the edge of the pool.

It was a little girl in a pink robe, about the same age as Delilah. She was standing on her tiptoes, curiously reaching her hand out toward the water.

The pool was four feet deep, far too deep for her.

Evander was about to call out a warning when he heard a muffled splash.

The girl vanished beneath the surface, sending up a spray of water.

"My goodness!"

Evander leapt up instinctively, crossing the pool in a few strides and pulling the girl out of the water.

The little girl had swallowed a few mouthfuls of water and was crying uncontrollably, her arms wrapped tightly around his neck. Her face was flushed, and tears streamed down her face, mixing with the pool water.

"It's okay. You're safe now," Evander said gently, patting her on the back.

"What's your name? Where are your parents?"

However, the little girl just continued to cry, her words a jumbled mess.

With no other choice, Evander carried her toward the changing room, intending to ask the staff to broadcast a search for her family.

Just as he stepped out of the men's bathing area, he saw a familiar figure standing not far away.

Kimberly, in a cream-colored robe, was tall and stunning. She was frantically looking around. When her gaze fell on the girl in Evander's arms, her face changed, and she hurried toward them.

"Sophia!" Kimberly cried.

The little girl in Evander's arms immediately reached for her, crying out, "Aunt Kimberly..."

Kimberly took Sophia into her arms. Seeing the little girl's soaking wet robe and red-rimmed eyes, her eyebrows knitted together in a tight knot. That little girl turned out to be Kimberly's niece, Sophia Astley.

After a moment, she looked at Evander, her voice filled with a complex mix of emotions.

"It's you..."

"She fell into the pool by accident. I just pulled her out," Evander explained, putting their past grievances aside. "You should get her changed so she doesn't catch a cold."

Kimberly said nothing more, turning away with Sophia in her arms.

But Sophia's crying didn't stop, and Kimberly patted her back softly to soothe her.

Evander watched them disappear before returning to the hot springs pool.

The warm water was still comforting, but the easy relaxation he had felt before was gone. The encounter felt far too coincidental to him.

After his dip, Evander changed into a casual outfit and went to the buffet restaurant.

He had just sat down with a plate of seafood when he saw Kimberly walking toward him, holding Sophia's hand.

Sophia was now in a yellow dress, her hair in two little pigtails. With a cotton candy in her hand, she looked much livelier than before.

"Sir, can we sit here?" Sophia pointed to the empty seat across from Evander.

Evander was reluctant but couldn't really say no, so he simply nodded.

"Please, have a seat."

"Sophia, sit nicely. I'm going to get some food," Kimberly said, settling the child down.

Then she looked at Evander. "Could you watch her for me?"

"Sure," Evander replied.

As he watched Kimberly walk to the buffet, a heavy feeling settled in his heart.

His grandfather had given him a deadline of two weeks to get Kimberly pregnant.

Now, there were only ten days left.
