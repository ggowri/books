Chapter 76 A Chance Encounter

Thursday morning, Ciotti drove Evander to the bustling dock.

By the time they arrived, the place was already swarming with people.

A massive luxury cruise liner loomed at the water's edge, its hull emblazoned with the words "Royal Glory" in gleaming gold, catching the sunrise's glow.

It was a floating palace, majestic and awe-inspiring.

"This is the ship?" Ciotti asked, craning her neck to take in its grandeur, her eyes wide with wonder.

She'd sailed on luxury liners before, but this one dwarfed them all—its dozen decks rising like a floating city before her.

"Yeah, I'll be on board for about three days before we reach London," Evander said, grabbing his suitcase and turning toward the gangway.

Ciotti caught his wrist, her head bowed, her voice trembling with emotion.

"Evander, promise me you'll stay safe in London. Don't push yourself too hard."

"I'll be fine, Ciotti. See you soon," he replied, offering a reassuring smile.

Ciotti watched him stride toward the Royal Glory.

Her hand drifted to her stomach, and she whispered softly, "Sweetheart, your daddy's off to London. Let's pray he has a safe journey and comes back to us soon."

At the boarding gate, staff verified Evander's ticket and credentials.

He was listed as a senior technical advisor for Vanguard Tech, tasked with international tech exchanges.

Once cleared, a staff member ushered him aboard with a respectful nod.

Evander ascended the gangway, glancing back at the dock.

Ciotti's slender figure was already fading into the crowd, her silhouette swallowed by the throng.

It was his first time on such a lavish cruise.

The ship radiated extravagance.

The grand atrium was carpeted in rich crimson, a colossal crystal chandelier overhead scattering prisms of light.

Ornate vases brimming with fresh white roses lined the halls, their delicate fragrance mingling with the air.

Attendants in crisp black tuxedos stood at attention, bowing to guests with polished courtesy.

"Welcome aboard the Royal Glory."

Guided to the first-class cabin area, Evander found the decor even more opulent.

The corridors were adorned with priceless oil paintings.

Each cabin door bore a custom nameplate.

His suite was on the top deck.

Opening the door, Evander was struck by the sheer elegance.

The spacious living area boasted leather sofas and a marble coffee table.

The bedroom featured a massive circular bed.

Beyond the floor-to-ceiling windows stretched an endless expanse of ocean, and a private balcony housed a steaming hot tub.

"Mr. Bexley, your luggage has been placed inside. Please ring the call bell if you need anything," the attendant said, bowing before slipping out.

Evander tossed his suitcase into a corner—it was just for show. All his valuables were safely tucked in his Inventory Cube.

He stepped onto the balcony, watching the dock shrink as the ship glided toward the open sea, the salty breeze kissing his face.

A faint smile curved his lips.

This was his first taste of such decadence.

For the first time, he felt the pulse of high society thrumming around him.

After settling in, Evander decided to explore the ship.

He left his cabin, strolling down the corridor.

It didn't take long to realize the Royal Glory was a floating luxury resort.

The first floor housed the grand atrium and dining halls, the second a sparkling pool and fitness center, the third a theater and arcade, and the fourth a ballroom and card room.

There were even a wine-tasting lounge and a cigar bar.

Every corner was meticulously designed and staffed by professionals exuding refinement. The ship was a testament to wealth and taste.

The passengers were a who's-who of the elite, men and women in bespoke suits and dazzling gowns, their every gesture polished and poised.

Young men stood tall, their tailored suits accentuating their charm, their conversation erudite.

The women were visions of elegance, their dresses flowing, makeup flawless, and exuding the confidence of the upper echelon.

Evander, in a sleek black suit with a diamond cufflink glinting at his collar, cut a striking figure. His calm, commanding presence turned heads.

Curious glances followed him—some speculative, others admiring, and a few women letting their eyes linger with unabashed interest.

He wandered to the second-floor pool deck, where guests were already splashing and lounging.

White loungers ringed the pool, where several bikini-clad women basked under the sun, their bodies bronzed and enticing.

One, a blonde with striking blue eyes, caught his gaze and flashed a sultry smile, raising her champagne flute in a playful toast.

Evander nodded politely, his mind already turning.

As Vanguard Tech's senior advisor, his credentials carried weight in elite circles.

The first-class deck bustled with wealthy women, each a potential romantic encounter.

A brief affair would not only make the voyage more interesting but might also activate the next level of his Arcane Ring's power.

He approached the poolside bar, ordering a whiskey. As he turned, a soft voice chimed behind him. "Excuse me, is this seat taken?"

He glanced back to see a woman in a flowing lavender gown.

Her chestnut curls cascaded over her shoulders, her features delicate, her eyes warm and inviting, like a blooming violet radiating quiet elegance.

She clutched a thick book, clearly looking for a spot to read.

"No, go ahead," Evander said, stepping aside to offer her the barstool next to him.

She thanked him and settled in, ordering a juice before diving into her book.

Evander's eyes flicked to the cover. It was Jane Eyre, its title embossed in gold, a delicate bookmark tucked between the pages.

His interest piqued.

He'd read the novel twice, captivated by Brontë's fierce, independent heroine.

Seeing her engrossed, he didn't interrupt, sipping his whiskey and gazing at the pool's shimmering ripples.

About ten minutes later, that woman closed the book with a soft sigh, her eyes distant, still caught in the story's spell.

She took a sip of her juice, her expression wistful.

Seizing the moment, Evander spoke, his tone warm with genuine curiosity.

"I've read that book twice. Jane Eyre's resilience always hits me hard."

Her head snapped up, her face lighting with delight, as if she'd found a kindred spirit.

"You've read it too?

"I thought nobody read classics like this anymore," she said, setting her glass down, her voice brimming with excitement.

"Jane's courage, her fight for equality—it's so inspiring. I don't just admire her; I'm in awe," Evander said, his sincerity shining through.

"She's born with nothing, yet she never compromises her dignity or her pursuit of love, even when faced with Rochester's wealth and status. She holds her own, always. That idea of 'soul equality'—it's timeless."

The woman, Lois Cavendish, nodded eagerly, her eyes sparkling.

"Exactly! I love when she declares, 'It is my spirit that addresses your spirit, just as if both had passed through the grave, and we stood at God's feet, equal, as we are!'"

"Every time I read that, it's like a jolt of strength," she added, then her voice softened, tinged with melancholy.

"My boyfriend, Felix, thinks these books are 'sappy'. He'd rather blow his money in the card room than sit with me and read."
