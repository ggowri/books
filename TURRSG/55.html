Chapter 56 I Will Never Marry You

After the meal, the four of them stepped out of the Michelin restaurant together.

The afternoon sun blazed overhead, harsh against the polished glass of the Rolls-Royce waiting at the curb.

Evander lifted a hand to shield his eyes as he reached for the door—then froze.

Heavy footsteps thundered from both sides.

Five men spilled out of the alleys flanking the restaurant.

Their hair was dyed in gaudy shades, their jeans torn, steel pipes gleaming in their hands. But their cold, disciplined stares didn't belong to street punks.

The leader, a blond with a sneer carved across his face, spun his pipe lazily in one hand.

"Come with us," he drawled. "Someone's got a score to settle with you."

Evander's expression cooled. Out of the corner of his eye, he caught Jack tugging Aileen and Emily behind him, his lips twitching with the faintest, smug curl.

So. Jack's handiwork.

"What's happening?" Aileen gasped, clutching her gown tight. Panic flickered across her face. "Why are they after Mr. Bexley?"

Jack put on his mask of concern, voice smooth but laced with false sympathy.

"No idea. Maybe one of his enemies caught up."

He gripped her arm firmly. "Stay out of this. It's got nothing to do with us."

"How can it not?" Emily snapped, trying to shove past him. Jack's hand clamped down on her shoulders, holding her in place.

"Mr. Bexley is our friend!" Emily cried, tears brimming in her eyes as she looked at Evander, who now stood alone in the ring of steel. "We can't just watch them gang up on him!"

Evander barely heard her. His gaze locked on the so-called thugs.

Their snarls and bravado were nothing but a mask—their balance was steady, their grips controlled, their eyes flicking with silent signals.

Beneath the cheap wigs, he caught glimpses of tactical head covers.

Not gangsters. Soldiers.

Jack hadn't just hired thugs. He had sent in his own men.

"Don't just stand there!" the blond barked, swinging his steel pipe in a vicious arc straight for Evander's skull.

The air whistled with the blow.

But Evander moved like lightning. His body twisted, the strike missing by inches, and the pipe smashed into the pavement with a crack that sent sparks flying.

Before the blond could recover, Evander's hand shot out, clamping down on his wrist like an iron vise. With a sharp twist, a sickening crack split the air.

The man screamed, the steel pipe clattering to the pavement.

Evander didn't hesitate. His boot slammed into the thug's gut, launching him backward into the wall.

The impact rattled the bricks, and the blond crumpled to the ground, gasping and clutching his stomach.

The other four rushed him at once, pipes swinging, fists cutting the air.

Evander flowed between them like water—calm, precise, untouchable. Every strike they threw met only empty space, every counter of his landed with brutal efficiency.

His fist shattered one man's nose in a spray of blood.

A whip-like kick buckled another's knee with a sickening crunch, dropping him howling to the ground.

He spun and drove his elbow straight into the third man's chest—thud—the air left his lungs in a choking groan as he collapsed.

In less than two minutes, the five who had swaggered in were sprawled across the street, writhing in agony, sweat-soaked and broken.

Gasps rippled through the bystanders. Phones were out, cameras flashing.

"Holy crap, did you see that?"

"He doesn't just look sharp—he fights like a machine!"

"Is this real life? He's like a movie hero!"

At the restaurant entrance, Aileen and Emily stood frozen, wide-eyed, hearts pounding.

For Aileen, images blurred together—the tenderness of his touch last night, the ruthless precision she'd just witnessed now.

A gentleman and a protector, gentle yet deadly. Her chest tightened as she realized she was sinking deeper, unable to stop herself.

Emily's cheeks burned red with excitement.

She yanked free from Jack's grip and rushed to Evander's side. "Mr. Bexley! Are you okay? Did they hurt you?"

Her gaze swept over him, both worried and starstruck.

Every movement of his was still vivid in her mind—fast, sharp, devastating. He hadn't just defended himself; he'd owned the fight.

Jack stood frozen, his face drained of color.

Those men weren't random street thugs. They were elite soldiers—his soldiers—handpicked, trained to perfection. Yet Evander had dismantled them in minutes, tossing them aside like children in a schoolyard scuffle.

Who is this man? Jack's thoughts reeled.

Evander looked like any polished civilian, but his strength was something else entirely. Terrifying.

Emily's voice cracked through the silence, sharp with fury. "Who are you people? Why did you attack Mr. Bexley?"

The men exchanged uneasy glances, none daring to speak. Their eyes flicked once more to Evander—warily, almost reverently—before stumbling away.

The blond ringleader cast Jack a pleading look, but when Jack stayed rooted in place, he bolted. The others followed, vanishing into the shadows.

Aileen turned on Jack, her eyes blazing with disappointment. "Jack… why didn't you help? Were those men sent by you?"

The way they had ignored Jack—no aggression, only deference—was damning enough. The truth was written all over it.

Jack's face flushed crimson, humiliation hardening into brittle arrogance. His voice cracked with scorn as he snapped, "Why should I help him? He's just a lowlife. If he were beaten to death, who would care? He's not worth a shred of pity!"

Squaring his shoulders, he lifted his chin in defiance.

"Don't forget—I'm a major general. Why would I ever stoop so low as to save a gutter rat like him?"

Aileen recoiled as if he'd slapped her. Her voice trembled, but her words cut clean. "That's how you see the world? That civilians deserve to be bullied? Jack, I can't believe I didn't see it sooner. You're selfish. Cruel. There's nothing just about you. You're terrifying. We're finished. I will never marry you."

Jack's composure cracked. "What? No—you can't! You can't do this!" His voice rose, frantic. He reached for her arm—

But before he could touch her, Evander's hand clamped around his wrist.

Jack froze. The grip was like iron. His bones screamed under the pressure, and for a heartbeat he couldn't breathe. The strength behind it was inhuman.

Aileen gave Evander a grateful glance before tugging Emily close. "Come on."

Emily threw Jack one last glare—sharp with disgust—before sliding into the passenger seat of the Rolls-Royce.

Aileen opened the back door and met Evander's eyes. Her voice softened. "Mr. Bexley… could you take us home?"

Evander simply nodded. His gaze lingered on Jack—cold, warning, merciless—before he released his wrist and slid behind the wheel.

The Rolls-Royce purred to life and glided down the street, carrying the sisters away.

Jack stood alone, the shadows of the alley stretching over him. His fists clenched so hard his knuckles popped. His jaw ached from how tightly he ground his teeth.

"Evander!" he roared, his voice raw and feral. He kicked a trash can so hard it clanged against the wall. "You filthy lowlife! I'll kill you—I swear I'll kill you!"
