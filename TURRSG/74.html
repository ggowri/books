Chapter 75 Ciotti's Secret

Wednesday morning dawned, and Evander began his preparations for London.

He steered his SUV toward the city's elite private bank, where the city's wealthiest did their business.

The bank's grand hall gleamed with opulence—crystal chandeliers sparkled overhead, and staff in pristine suits moved with practiced deference.

"Mr. Bexley, your one billion pounds in cash is ready, secured in the vault," the client manager said, striding forward and offering a glass of champagne with both hands.

"Would you like to inspect it now?"

"No need to count it. Just take me to the vault," Evander replied, taking a sip of the champagne, his tone as casual as if he were discussing the weather.

The manager blinked, caught off guard.

One billion pounds could fill half a room, and most clients would pore over every detail.

Though surprised, he remained deferential and gestured for Evander to follow.

"This way, sir. The vault's on the third basement level."

They descended in a private elevator, and the heavy alloy doors parted to reveal a cavernous vault.

The cash sat in neat stacks, a breathtaking mountain of wealth in the room's center.

Two security guards stood watch, their eyes scanning vigilantly.

"Mr. Bexley, it's all here. Shall we arrange transport?" the manager asked, already mentally coordinating armored vans.

Evander shook his head, raising his hand, the Arcane Ring glinting faintly.

Then, something surreal unfolded.

The stacks of cash vanished, one by one, as if swallowed by an invisible void.

In moments, the vault was empty, save for the bare floor.

The manager and guards froze, jaws slack, eyes wide as saucers, their breaths caught in disbelief.

"What... what just happened?" The manager stammered, staring at Evander.

Evander flashed a cryptic smile. "I stored it in another space. Thanks for your help."

The manager and guards exchanged bewildered glances.

Too stunned to react, they only noticed Evander's departure when he was already gone.

Snapping out of his daze, the manager bolted to the security room with the guards in tow.

He pulled up the surveillance footage from the vault, which clearly showed the cash disappearing into nothingness, with no visible light or trace of how it was done.

His hands trembled as he clutched the monitor.

Who was this man? This was beyond human!

Leaving the bank, Evander drove to a luxury car customization center.

The world's only Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost awaited him—a 250-million-dollar masterpiece.

The staff member bowed slightly as he led Evander to the private garage.

There it was: the silver beauty, its classic lines catching the light. The Spirit of Ecstasy—crafted in pure gold—crowned the hood, gleaming like a beacon in the afternoon sun.

The interior was a study in extravagance—supple leather seats, a diamond-encrusted dashboard, and plush wool floor mats, every detail screaming unparalleled craftsmanship.

"Mr. Bexley, the vehicle's fully prepped, and all paperwork is complete. It's ready for you," the staff member said, handing over the keys with pure respect.

Evander slid into the driver's seat, the leather embracing him like a second skin. He nodded, satisfied.

He started the engine, its low, refined purr filling the air. The car glided through the garage, smooth as silk.

"Impressive," he murmured.

After receiving the paperwork, Evander drove his new Rolls-Royce Silver Ghost back to Azure Cove Residence.

This car outshone even his grandfather's prized model. At that thought, a smug grin tugged at his lips.

Back at the villa, he stowed the Rolls-Royce in his Inventory Cube, where it joined the one billion pounds, filling most of the spatial storage.

Then, he turned to packing other essentials.

In the basement storage room, he cleaned the short-barreled pistol and submachine gun from his Mortis Gorge trip, checking their magazines.

Then came a dozen tailored suits, casual outfits, an umbrella, a high-powered flashlight, a Swiss Army knife, several bottles of 1982 Lafite, vacuum-sealed beef jerky, and bottled water.

He even tucked in a few bouquets of fresh red and white roses.

All of it went into the Inventory Cube.

These were his essentials and, as he thought with a faint grin, his arsenal of "charm weapons" for London.

By noon, Evander had finished his preparations.

Stepping into the living room, he froze at the sight of a familiar figure on the sofa—Ciotti.

She wore a loose gray tracksuit, her hair cascading carelessly over her shoulders. Her face was pale, her eyes shadowed with exhaustion and a quiet sadness, a stark contrast to her usual vibrant energy.

"Ciotti? When did you get back?" Evander asked, surprised.

He'd thought she was still tied up with family matters.

Ciotti looked up slowly, her eyes flickering with a mix of emotions—joy, hesitation, and a trace of panic.

She forced a faint smile.

"Just got in. I wanted to call, but didn't want to disturb you in case you were busy."

Evander sat beside her, noticing her bloodshot eyes and a hospital report on the table. Concern creased his brow.

"Are you okay? You look worn out. Is something going on with your family?"

Ciotti's fingers twisted the hem of her shirt, and she stayed silent for a moment before murmuring, "It's nothing. I just... haven't been feeling great. Went back home for a checkup."

Her eyes avoided his, her voice laced with evasion.

She couldn't bring herself to tell him the truth—her period was late, and tests confirmed she was pregnant. It was Evander's child, conceived in a moment he didn't even recall.

For days, she'd wrestled with whether to tell him.

He was still entangled with Nadine's divorce, oblivious to their night together.

She didn't know if he would believe her or accept the baby.

Her heart ached with turmoil. She had been unable to sleep, but finally, she had made a decision.

She'd have the baby, whether Evander knew or not, whether they could be together or not.

She loved him with every fiber of her being.

Even if he rejected the child, she'd be content just to live with him and their baby.

"Just not feeling great?" Evander pressed, unconvinced by her pale complexion. "Let me see that report."

Ciotti's heart skipped, and she quickly tucked the papers behind her, forcing another smile.

"It's really nothing. Just a bit of anemia. The doctor said rest will fix it."

Sensing his concern, she pivoted.

"Anyway, you're getting ready for London, right?"

Evander saw she didn't want to talk and let it go with a nod.

"Yes, I'm taking a cruise to London tomorrow. There are some tech deals I need to handle there. I'll be gone for about a month."

He paused, studying her.

"You don't look well. Take it easy, okay? Call me if you need anything."

"Will do," Ciotti said, her emotions a tangled mess.

She looked at him, yearning to spill the truth—that he was going to be a father—but the words stuck in her throat.

She feared shattering the fragile peace between them and dreaded his rejection.

Evander didn't notice her inner turmoil. He stood up and went to the kitchen to get her a glass of water.

"Let me get you some water. Rest up. I'm leaving tomorrow, so take care of yourself while I'm gone."

"I will," Ciotti replied, taking the glass he offered.

Her fingers brushed his, and she pulled back quickly, a flush creeping up her cheeks.

"Take care of yourself in London," she said quietly. "Don't work too hard, and remember to eat properly."

"Promise," Evander said with a warm smile, pulling her into a gentle hug and sitting with her on the sofa for a while.

*****

At three in the afternoon, Evander visited Norman's estate to confirm his cruise tickets and travel documents.

Norman, seeing his meticulous preparations, nodded with satisfaction.

"Evan, this London trip is critical. Secure Royal Tech's core technology. If you hit any snags, reach out to Aldred—he'll have your back."

"Got it, Grandpa," Evander said, his mind already running through a flawless plan.

By evening, he returned to Azure Cove Residence.

Ciotti had prepared a lavish dinner, all his favorite dishes, spread across the table.

They ate in relative silence, the air heavy with unspoken words.

Evander occasionally asked about London, and Ciotti answered patiently, her voice steady despite the storm in her heart.
