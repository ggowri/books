Chapter 47 Start

Two days later, Ciotti's SUV rolled along the gravel road in front of Azure Cove Residence, the tires crunching and kicking up a fine dust cloud.

She killed the engine, flung open the door, and dashed into the house.

The straps of her backpack dug painfully into her shoulders, and the jewels inside made a soft chiming sound, like gentle wind chimes.

Evander followed behind, lugging the remaining luggage. His fingers absent-mindedly toyed with the ring on his middle finger. These past two days had been oddly quiet, as if the heat the ring radiated in the underground river was just a wild dream.

"Evander, get in here fast!" Ciotti's voice rang out from the living room.

Evander stepped inside to find Ciotti emptying the contents of her backpack onto the rosewood table.

Pearls and agates scattered everywhere.

A crown hairpin with a pigeon-blood ruby stood out, its gilded edges engraved with Victorian-era symbols.

Ciotti snatched up the hairpin, stuck it in her hair, and twirled in front of the mirror.

"How do I look? This was once sported by the Queen of England."

Suddenly, she seemed to remember something. She rummaged through the pile of jewels and pulled out three necklaces.

"Hey, what about Iris, Kimberly, and Nadine? You should give them something, right? I mean..."

"No, it's all good." Evander cut in.

He walked over to the window and stared out at the ocean. The coolness of the ring against his skin made him think of the records in The Tesseract Chronicle.

He hadn't brought back the elixir Norman wanted, and since he failed the third test, he didn't have the nerve to call Norman.

Evander said, "If you really wanna help me deal with them, how about we hold an auction?"

Ciotti paused for a second, then her eyes lit up.

"You mean, to show them how valuable these things are?"

She leaned in close to him, her nose almost touching his chin. "Or maybe to let them know you're a different guy now?"

Evander didn't reply. He just stared at the crown hairpin in her hair.

The sunlight streaming through the French windows hit the ruby, which sent out a seductive glow, kinda like the juice of the Blood-sucking Vine in Mortis Gorge.

He suddenly realized that Ciotti was better at understanding people than he thought.

This auction was both a flex and a challenge.

But it didn't matter to him. If he couldn't pass Norman's tests, he was a loser and would never take over Norman's spot as Godfather.

"I'll take care of it tomorrow," Ciotti said, patting his shoulder.

She started sorting the jewels into different groups.

"I know some people at auction houses. These treasures are gonna cause a stir in the city."

Bright and early the next morning, Ciotti drove off from Azure Cove Residence.

Evander locked the villa door behind him and holed up in the study.

As soon as the computer screen came to life, the ring on his middle finger started to warm up a bit.

He took a deep breath and opened the contacts list. Norman's number was right at the top, but he still couldn't bring himself to hit the call button.

He logged into Nogales' government intranet. His fingers flew over the keyboard. The permission verification box popped up and then disappeared, and finally, he landed on an encrypted list.

"Royal nobility..." Evander mumbled as he scrolled through the list slowly.

Most of the names were of old lords and ladies with titles.

But then, the name "Aileen Burton" caught his eye.

The profile showed that Aileen was Governor Kenny Burton's daughter.

At 25, she was the editor-in-chief of the fashion magazine "LUXERA". A Cambridge University art grad, she owned three castles, and her grandfather was a distant relative of the Norwegian royal family.

In the photo, she was wearing a silver spaghetti-strap dress, showing off her tall and sexy figure.

Standing on the Cannes red carpet, she was all smiles. Her blue eyes were like the Nordic lakes, showing tranquility, and the diamond necklace around her neck made her collarbones look even more delicate.

"She'll be the first one on my list," Evander said to himself.

Evander closed the profile page and launched a new document.

Tapping out "Perfect Sex and Love" in the title bar, he paused, with Ciotti's face flashing across his mind.

He recalled how, when she sorted the jewels, stray hairs at her temples were stuck with pearl fragments, and she looked as earnest as a child.

But there was no time to dwell on such thoughts. Activating the Arcane Ring was his top priority.

He shook his head to clear his mind and forced himself to focus.

In the article, he masked his explicit desires with obscure philosophical theories, ranging from Botticelli's "The Birth of Venus" to Freud's sexual instinct, and finally concluded that "perfect love demands the dual harmony of soul and body".

The words subtly issued a secret invitation.

As the email-sent prompt popped up, the warmth in the ring faded.

Evander leaned back in his chair, watching seagulls skim the sea outside the window. He felt like a sly hunter setting a well-crafted trap.

But he had no other option. Norman's authority loomed over him like a shadow, and he dared not contemplate the consequences of failing the test.

Only by growing stronger could he break free from Norman's shadow.

Meanwhile, in the city's most exclusive auction house, Ciotti was chatting animatedly with the manager.

She placed the crown hairpin on a velvet tray. Sunlight filtered through the skylight, casting the ruby's glow as dancing specks on the wall.

The manager said, "The heraldry on this was specially commissioned for Queen Victoria's Diamond Jubilee in 1897. There's only one like it in the world."

The manager donned white gloves and examined it closely. Every engraving was crystal-clear under the magnifying glass.

"Ms. Karen, if this is real, it could easily sell for over a hundred million dollars at auction."

Suddenly, he added in a hushed tone, "But are you sure you want a public auction? Won't it attract trouble?"

"Trouble?" Ciotti laughed, picking up a sapphire bracelet. "People who can afford these treasures aren't scared of trouble," she said, leaning in closer.

"Oh, and help me reach out to all the major media. Tell them there's a 'Royal Secret Collection' auction. It'll make their front-pages a hit."

The news spread like wildfire across Nogales.

That afternoon, all mainstream media headlines were dominated by the auction.

LUXERA magazine even dedicated three full pages to analyzing the jewels' origin.

In a note penned by Editor-in-Chief Aileen herself, her curiosity about the mysterious contributor all but bled through the lines.

That evening, Evander received her reply.

It was short, almost curt: [Your article is interesting. Maybe we can have a chat. Meet me at the western suburb castle, 8 p.m., three days from now.]

Evander's lips curled into a cold sneer as his eyes fell on the address.

He pulled up the map. The castle was one of Aileen's private estates—an old stronghold said to be filled with medieval relics and secrets that no outsider had ever seen.

He shut down the computer and headed to the living room.

On the table, the auction date on the calendar was circled in red—three days later.

But his mind was still on Norman's third test.

No matter how grand the auction, it couldn't mask his potential failure.

Just then, Ciotti pushed the door open, humming a tune.

She held an auction catalog with the crown hairpin on the cover.

"See, it's a hit! Aileen's magazine is all over it," she said, then noticed Evander's expression.

"What's up? You look like you've seen a ghost."

"Nothing," Evander replied, taking the catalog and tracing Aileen's name with his finger.

"Just wondering if there will be any disruptions at the auction," he said, though in fact, he was dreading Norman's disappointment.

Ciotti raised an eyebrow. "You mean Iris and the others?"

She sat down beside Evander, snatched the auction catalog, and flipped to a certain page.

"Don't worry. I've got it covered. I'll show them what real wealth looks like," she said.

Evander said nothing, just rubbed the ring on his finger.

He knew the auction was just the start.

The real game was yet to come, and Aileen was just the first pawn.
