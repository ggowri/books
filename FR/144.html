Only the Hawkes understood what Julian meant by those words.
But that wasn't the point.

Lizeth and Camille turned their heads almost simultaneously.

The moment their eyes landed on Bertha, their gazes practically burned with fury.

In stark contrast, Joshua—seeing his beloved daughter released from detention—finally relaxed, exhaling deeply in relief.

So, my influence as Chairman of Elvador Group still holds weight.

Even Julian wouldn't dare cross me lightly.

Joshua's eyes filled with paternal warmth—until he saw Bertha's shaved head.

Fury exploded through him.

"Who did this?" he thundered, gripping Bertha's shoulders.

"Tell me, and I'll destroy them. No one touches my daughter and walks away."

The moment Bertha saw Camille, it was as if a spell had been cast on her.

She stood frozen, her eyes filled with terror.

But when Bertha met Wilson, Lizeth, and Camille's hate-filled stares, her heart sank.

Camille must have revealed the truth from five years ago.

Words failed her, trapped in her throat by an invisible vise.

"Bertha, don't be afraid. Tell me who did this to you," Joshua demanded protectively.

Before Bertha could respond, Julian's icy voice cut through the air, "I did. What exactly do you plan to do about it, Mr. Joshua Elvador?"

Joshua's body stiffened, and he looked at Julian in disbelief.

Julian was lazily leaning against the sofa, his long legs casually crossed.

He seemed nonchalant, yet there was an air of natural elegance about him.

He looked like a predator toying with his prey, high up and untouchable, exuding an aura of superiority and calm detachment, as if looking down on all life from above.

His gaze was as if he were looking at trash.

With that, no matter how self-assured Joshua had been, doubt suddenly crept into his mind.
Did Julian really release Bertha out of respect for me?

In that moment, he remembered Julian's words earlier, "The show is about to begin."

A show?

His gut told him that it's certainly not going to be good for him.

Just as Joshua was drowning in self-doubt, Lizeth could no longer contain her fury.

Her eyes were bloodshot, and like an enraged beast, she stormed over to Bertha and slapped her across the face with a loud, resounding smack.

The slap was forceful, sending Bertha's face turning to the side.

A clear five-finger mark appeared on her pale cheek.

Lizeth shouted, "My daughter was pushed down the stairs by you! And you dare frame Aurora? You're not human! You're a devil!"

Her voice trembled with rage, and the veins in her eyes stood out as her fury boiled over.

The Sherwoods had been played for fools for five agonizing years.

While Aurora wasted her college years in prison and Camille languished in a hospital bed, Bertha and Joshua had likely laughed at their stupidity.

Camille was only fifteen when she became a vegetable, just after passing the entrance exam for Orley High School.

Orley High School was the most prestigious school in Orley, and only two people from the upper society's children got in—one was Aurora, and the other was Camille.

Unfortunately, Aurora's college years had been spent in prison, while Camille's high school and college years were spent in a hospital bed.

Facing the person who ruined her daughter's life, how could Lizeth not hate her?

She wished she could tear Bertha apart right now.

She raised her hand again, but before the slap could land, her arm was grabbed firmly by Adolph.

"Mom, how can you slap Bertha without asking what happened first?"

Lizeth looked at him, her heart twisting with pain.

"Because Camille was pushed down the stairs by her and turned into a vegetable. With that reason, what's wrong with me slapping her?"

"No way!"

Adolph immediately denied, "Mom, you've seen Bertha grow up. You know how kind she is. She wouldn't push Camille down the stairs. There must be some misunderstanding.

"I think it's possible that Camille lost her balance and..."

"Adolph!"

Before Adolph could finish speaking, Camille's sharp voice suddenly cut through the air.

Her eyes were icy and sharp, looking at Adolph as though he were a sworn enemy.

She couldn't believe it—after all this time, Adolph was still defending Bertha and trying to clear her name.

It was him who had claimed to like Aurora, and it was him who had hurt her so badly.

Back then, he wanted to break off the engagement with Bertha, and now, with the truth exposed, he was desperately trying to whitewash her.

Camille suddenly felt intense disgust toward Adolph.

To be honest, Adolph's behavior hurt her more than Bertha ever did.

"Adolph, let me make it clear to you right now. Five years ago, Bertha was the one who pushed me down the stairs. Aurora was wrongfully accused!" Camille shouted, her voice full of anger and grievance.

"No, no, it wasn't me. It really wasn't me," Bertha shook her head desperately, tears welling in her eyes.

With the bright red slap mark on her face, she looked as if she had suffered a great injustice.

"Adolph, you have to believe me," she said, her voice choking with tears.

Adolph could not bear to see Bertha cry.

He immediately gave Camille a stern warning with his eyes.

"Camille, you can't just say things like that. This concerns Bertha's reputation. And I believe Bertha would never do something like that."

"So her reputation matters, but Aurora's doesn't?" Camille was furious.

If she could stand, she would've already stormed over and slapped Adolph herself.

Her face twisted with fury, resentment burning in her eyes as she spat, "Do you want to know why Bertha pushed me down the stairs?

"Because Bertha and Joshua, these two despicable bastards, conspired to get rid of Aurora. They didn't care whether she lived or died.

"As long as they put her in the hospital, they planned to declare her brain dead immediately, transplant her kidneys to Bertha, and donate all her other organs.

"They even wanted to donate Aurora's body as a teaching specimen.

"I overheard their plan, and that's why they tried to kill me—to silence me. And then, they pinned their crime on Aurora, framing her for murder."

Camille yelled out the truth from five years ago at Adolph.

Perhaps because she was so enraged, after shouting, she began to pant heavily, her face turning red from the fury.

Adolph was struck as though by lightning upon hearing the truth.

The truth was too vile, too incomprehensible—it completely shattered everything he thought he knew.

In all his twenty-something years, he had never encountered people this ruthless and monstrous.

Adolph shook his head, disbelief written all over his face.

"No. That's impossible. You're making this up. Even if Bertha and Joshua really wanted to harm Aurora, there's no way the hospital would go along with it."

It seemed he found a flaw in Camille's words, and his eyes lit up.

"Right! The hospital wouldn't cooperate. Clearly, you're just making this all up. I think you've been a vegetable for five years, and your brain's not working properly. It was Aurora who hurt you, but you got confused and thought it was Bertha."

With that, Adolph turned to Wilson and Lizeth.

"Mom, Dad, don't get caught up in Camille's nonsense. This is clearly all a misunderstanding. Take her home, okay? Let's not have her—"

Before Adolph could finish his sentence, Lizeth forcefully shook off his hand and slapped him across the face.

"I can't believe I gave birth to such an idiot who can't tell right from wrong," she said bitterly, her voice shaking with both anger and sorrow.

He slowly turned his head back, his bloodshot eyes filled with fury.

"Why don't you believe the obvious flaw? The hospital wouldn't have helped Bertha and Joshua harm anyone."

With that, he suddenly glared at Aurora, who had been coldly observing from the sofa, gritting his teeth.

"Is this what you wanted? To turn me against my family? Are you satisfied now?"
