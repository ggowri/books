Ethan immediately realized he had misspoken. After what nearly happened last time, she probably wanted to avoid him at all costs. There was no way she would willingly go home with him.

He quickly dropped his teasing tone and said, "I'll take you to a hotel."

Lily did not refuse.

Inside the car, the atmosphere felt heavy.

She gazed out the window, her face emotionless. The streetlights flickered past, casting fragmented shadows on her features.

Ethan glanced at her through the rearview mirror. Seeing her cold and distant demeanor, he sighed helplessly.

It seems that my behavior last time really scared her.

What he didn't realize was that if Lily hadn't mistaken him for being gay, she would have already made a move on him.

"I was wrong before," Ethan said, his voice low and sincere, laced with guilt. He added, "I truly want to apologize. This card is a token of my sincerity. I hope you'll accept it."

He tried to keep his tone gentle, forcing the card into Lily's hand.

Lily was momentarily stunned, her gaze falling on the card.

She hadn't expected him to still give it to her.

"I... I don't need this," she said slowly, her voice still cool. But only she knew how much she longed for the money.

Still, she had to keep up appearances. Her image had to stay intact.

When it was time to act, she had to commit.

"Take it back." She handed the card back to him.

Ethan had expected her actions. She was stubborn and disobedient even, she still had the innocence of a university student.

She was turning down a million dollars without hesitation. Most people wouldn't be able to do that.

Ethan's impression of Lily improved even more.

At least all his efforts tonight weren't in vain.

His tone left no room for argument, his gaze carrying a hint of dominance.

I must have looked incredibly cool. Maybe I even managed to make her swoon a little.

She smiled slightly, a teasing glint in her eyes.

Her evaluation of him was simple.
He is dumb, silly, and ridiculously fun to mess with.

Just because he was being so generous, she would temporarily forgive him for being friends with scumbags like Barnett and Darren.

As long as he didn't provoke Aurora, she would let him be.

Feeling much better, Lily tucked the bank card away carefully.

Tomorrow, she would find Aurora and discuss leaving the country.

Even though her mother said the Hawke family treated Aurora well, she knew that relying on others was never a good idea.

Especially men, one day, they might adore you and treat you like a queen, but the moment they lose interest, they can turn cold and take everything back without hesitation.

She was sure that after everything Aurora had been through, she had long since given up on men.

Yesterday, she had already gotten their address from her mother.

Her bond with Aurora wasn't just a coincidence.

For Aurora, in the entire Elvador family, only Daisy had ever truly cared about her.

And Lily? Back when Aurora was about to take her Scholastic Assessment Test, Lily had braved a downpour to deliver her exam admission ticket, only to fall and break her right hand.

Because of that injury, she struggled during her Secondary School Admission Test, writing slowly that she missed several major questions. She could have gotten into a prestigious high school, but she was only admitted to an average one.

Neither Daisy nor Lily owed her anything, yet they had always treated her sincerely.

That was why, after getting out of prison, when the Elvador family used Daisy and Lily to threaten her, she endured it all in silence.

She didn't want them to suffer because of her.

Thanks to Aurora’s tutoring, she could become the top student in her grade, for consecutive three years.

During the Secondary School Admission Test, her injured hand slowed her down, missing out on a top school. But even when Aurora was in prison, she hadn't forgotten about Lily.

During her mother's visits, Aurora had asked her to bring all her high school notes to Lily.

That was why Lily had managed to get into Orley University of Technology, one of the most prestigious universities.

A woman as kind and brilliant as Aurora shouldn't have to endure mistreatment.

Lily was determined to take her away, far from those who sought to hurt her.

Ever since coming to the Hawke Estate, she had been relying on him too much.

Aurora felt a little guilty.

She wanted to do something for Julian, but he lacked nothing.

At the same time, Lily had just arrived at the Hawke Estate.

Towering columns supported the grand entrance, their wrought-iron designs finely detailed.

The front yard was filled with blooming flowers, their fragrance thick in the air. Each petal was flawless, layered like a meticulously crafted masterpiece.

Looking at all this, Lily couldn't help but wonder.
In such an extravagant place, is Aurora living well?

Taking a deep breath, she stepped forward and rang the doorbell.

But even after a long wait, no one answered. Frowning, she wondered.
Is everyone still asleep?

She hesitated for a moment but still walked in. As she entered the living room, a rich aroma filled the air.

Walking toward the kitchen, she saw Aurora busy at the stove.

Her eyes welled up with tears as she softly called out, "Aurora."
