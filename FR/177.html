Stella's face got all smug, like she'd just won the lottery. "Oh, do thank your manager for me," she purred, laying it on thick like butter.

The waitress flashed a polished, professional smile. "Sure. It's our pleasure."

With that, she wheeled the empty cart out of the room.

Once the door clicked shut, the crowd turned their envious eyes on Stella, rushing over to shower her with compliments.

Meanwhile, Gavin, humiliated from being slapped, was fuming. His chubby face twisted in rage as he grabbed a plate from the table and hurled it straight at Aurora's head, cursing furiously.

"You ungrateful little wretch! How dare you hit me? I'll kill you!"

Aurora reacted fast. She snatched a fork from the table, ready to defend herself.

But before she could strike, Daisy threw herself in front of her, shielding her.

A sharp "crack" rang out as the plate smashed against Daisy's head, shattering into pieces.

"Daisy!" Aurora cried out in panic.

"Ms. Elvador, I'm fine. Go get Mr. Hawke," Daisy said, wincing through the pain.

Her words sparked another round of mocking laughter from the group.

"You two seriously think you know Mr. Hawke? Do you even know who he is? The guy's Peloland royalty. He's been in Orley six months, dropped tens of billions of dollars on that eastside land, and now he's the richest man around. And you idiots think you're on his radar?" one of them jeered.

"If you're dying to meet him, stick around. When James gets here, kiss his boots real nice. Maybe if he's in a good mood, he'll let you catch a glimpse of Mr. Hawke from a mile away," another added.

"James is a big deal now. No way he'd waste his time on trash like her," someone else sneered.

"Trash like who?" a voice cut in.

The door swung open again, and a guy in a white plaid suit strolled in.

James Miller was the golden boy of their high school class. He was undeniably handsome, more so than any other man in the room.

But that pretty face was all he had.

Five years was a long time since those glory days.

He'd been a cocky one back then, but at least his eyes were clean—hadn't been tainted by all the world's BS yet.

Now? After getting knocked around by the real world, those eyes were razor-sharp with naked ambition. He carried himself like someone who'd finally gotten their first hit of power and couldn't get enough of the high.

"James! You're finally here!" the crowd cheered.

They swarmed him, piling on the flattery like eager puppies.

James waved them off with a casual, "Oh, you guys are too much," but his smug grin and sky-high chin told a different story.

"What took you so long, James? We've been waiting forever!" someone whined.

He smirked, oozing confidence. "Just hashing out some business with Mr. Hawke."

That was half-true.

He was with Julian earlier, but "business" was a stretch. He'd mostly been fetching coffee and hovering like a lackey.

When he heard Julian was at Grand Hotel, he shamelessly tagged along to see him.

Julian had been gearing up to discuss the East Suburb project with someone and brushed him off without a second thought.

James was heading to Room 107 when he spotted a waitress with an empty cart.

He tossed out a casual question. "Are my guests all here?"

She'd replied politely, "Sir, are your friends a group of young people in their twenties, including a Ms. Walker?"

James nodded eagerly. "Yep, that's them."

"They're in Room 101, sir."

James blinked, thrown off.

I'd booked 107, not some 101.

What's with this?

"Not 107?" he asked.

The waitress clarified, "No, sir. Mr. Hawke reserved 101. Your friends are already there."

James was riding high, feeling like he'd hit the jackpot.

He figured Julian must think pretty highly of him to book the Grand Hotel's VIP suite for his reunion, just to make him look good.

He'd thought Julian didn't even know he existed, but now he felt like he mattered.

Without a shred of doubt, James strutted into Room 101, beaming as he spotted his old classmates.

Their praise sent him soaring, his ego ballooning by the second.

"Alright, alright, since we're all here, let's sit," he said, waving a hand.

Everyone scrambled to their seats.

Then James' eyes landed on Aurora.

She was his high school crush, the goddess he'd pursued relentlessly but never won over. He'd tried everything back then, but nothing swayed her.

The one you couldn't have always shone the brightest.

He was married now, but that didn't stop him from keeping his options open.

In fact, he was already plotting his divorce. He wanted to bleed his wife dry, leave her with nothing, and walk away with every penny.

He had made up his mind that once he got her money, he would indulge himself in luxury, drowning in pleasure every single day.

And now, looking at Aurora, he found himself drawn to her even more than before. She was even more beautiful than she had been in high school.

Guess my taste hasn't changed a bit. Still hooked on girls like her.

For a quick rise, he'd married a rich heiress, who was the only child in her family, but he despised her. Sleeping with her felt like cuddling a hog.

Everyone could see the raw hunger in James' stare.

"Aurora, you showed up uninvited. So admit it. You're here for James. Now that he's here, go say hi already," someone prodded.

Aurora hated the way James looked at her. It was like he was stripping her bare with his eyes, and it made her skin crawl.

She frowned, "James, are you sure you booked Room 101?"

He raised an eyebrow, brimming with swagger. "Of course I did. You think I'd forget my own reservation?"

Aurora went quiet.

Now she wondered if Julian had mixed things up.

Either way, she had to get out of there fast and find Julian to sort this mess out.

She grabbed Daisy's arm and headed for the door.

But the crowd blocked the exit, refusing to budge.

"You're here now, so don't run off."

"What's this? Playing innocent again? I can't stand that fake act of yours anymore."

"Cut it out already. It's just pathetic."

Someone shoved Aurora hard from behind.

Caught off guard, she stumbled forward, tumbling straight into James' arms.

"See? Told you she came here to throw herself at James," a voice crowed.

"Tsk, tsk, all of us watching, and she's not even embarrassed. Shameless, huh?"

"She wouldn't give James the time of day in high school. Now that he's loaded, she's all over him. How disgusting."

"Ease up. If James likes her, he can have some fun with her. She's practically begging for it, so why let that go to waste?"

"Fair point. With a criminal record, finding a job must be tough. Maybe selling her body is the only option left."

Their words cut deep, vicious, and crude.

Daisy trembled with fury. "Shut up! Ms. Elvador's nothing like that!"

"Ms. Elvador? Ha! More like a call girl," someone shot back.

Aurora squirmed, trying to break free from James' grip, but he held her tight, his arms like a vice.

It was his first time holding her, and she felt so soft against him.

Her scent hit him like a drug—intoxicating and irresistible—sparking an instant reaction.

Aurora noticed the change in him and almost gagged, her stomach twisting with revulsion.
