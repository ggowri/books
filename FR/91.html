Aurora got into the car and settled in the back seat.

She froze when she took in the car's interior decorations.

Everything she saw was covered in fluffy pink material.

The passenger seat especially caught her eye. Its plush pink cover featured bold letters reading: [Aurora's Special Seat].

Hanging from the rearview mirror was a photo of her lounging in the sunlight on the couch at the Hawke Estate.

In the photo, her face was serene. Though her expression was neutral, the image radiated contentment and happiness.

Aurora felt a wave of emotion as she looked at all the personal touches meant just for her.

Her eyes grew misty as she ran her fingers over the soft, plush material of the seat cover, feeling the warmth and thoughtfulness behind every detail.

She remembered her first ride in Barnett's car after exiting prison.

His car had been filled with traces of Bertha everywhere.

Back then, she had worn cheap jeans and a T-shirt. Her entire outfit probably cost less than Bertha's designer dress bag handle.

She had felt so worthless then, never imagining a day someone would treasure her this much.

Ethan seemed to notice her emotional turmoil and said warmly, "Madam Hawke set all this up herself. Do you like it, Ms. Elvador?"

Aurora was surprised but quickly grabbed the whole situation. While Julian was good to her, he would never deck out his car in such a girly pink style.

Still, it was unbelievable that Julian let Beatrice have her way with his car and even allowed her to claim the passenger seat.

Aurora gently touched her gown and softly said, "I love it."

It was the first time she had worn a formal gown.

It was also her first time wearing something expensive and beautiful.

She would never again feel out of place like before, wearing cheap clothes in luxury cars.

The contrast couldn't be more obvious. The Hawke family cared so much about her. In contrast, the Elvador family couldn't care less.

But it didn't matter anymore since she had already left the Elvador family behind.

Ethan looked at Simon, standing motionless at the mansion's entrance. "Simon, get in the car already."

Simon pouted with reluctance. "I don't want to watch Julian get all lovey-dovey with some girl."

Ethan rolled his eyes but still tried to reason with him.

"You're Ms. Elvador's stylist today. What if her makeup needs touching up? Mr. Hawke specifically had you brought over from Putnam because you're the only one he trusts."

Simon's face lit up instantly.

With a proud attitude, he declared, "Well, if it's Julian's wish, I suppose I'll have to agree."

As he spoke, he strutted to the car on his long legs, swaying his hips dramatically as he opened the passenger door.

Before he could take the seat, Daniel interrupted him.

"I'm sorry, but this seat is reserved for Ms. Elvador. Please sit in the back."

If Aurora hadn't been wearing an evening gown and looking so stunning, Daniel would have already invited her to take her designated seat.

Simon froze momentarily, shot Daniel a dirty look, reluctantly slammed the passenger door, and plopped down in the back seat in a huff.

As the car pulled away, an awkward tension filled the air.

Simon was still sulking, stealing glances at Aurora from the corner of his eye.

Aurora could only respond with an awkward smile at his behavior.

Ethan couldn't help but chime in, "Come on, Simon. Quit pouting. Remember, your job is to ensure Ms. Elvador's makeup stays perfect."

Simon crossed his arms and declared, "Of course! When I'm on the job, I guarantee our girl here will look flawless until the party's over!"

Aurora couldn't help but laugh at Simon's dramatic attitude, feeling the tension melt away.

She said softly, "Thank you so much for all your help today, Simon. I would've been lost without you."

Simon's stern expression softened at her words, and a slight smile tugged at his lips though he tried to maintain his aloof facade.

"Hmph, at least you know talent when you see it. Don't worry, your pretty head! Just follow my lead, and you'll be the belle of the ball."

Aurora gazed out the window at the passing scenery, her heart filled with anticipation as the car cruised smoothly along the road.

A black Rolls-Royce approached like an elegant panther, gliding closer until it stopped at the hotel entrance.

The car's sleek body reflected the surrounding lights, radiating luxury.

Before the doors opened, curious onlookers had already gathered around.

Daniel stepped out first, walking confidently to the car's rear to open the back door with practiced efficiency.

Simon was the first to emerge, swaying his hips as he stepped out dramatically.

He put on an aloof expression, but the excitement in his eyes was impossible to hide.

While Simon's appearance had already drawn attention, it was merely the opening act.

Then Ethan came around from the other side, extending his arm with gentlemanly grace.

A delicate, porcelain-like hand emerged slowly from the car, gently resting on Ethan's offered arm.

The crowd held their breath at the sight of that hand, eagerly waiting to see the owner.

All eyes were on Aurora as she gracefully stepped out of the car.

The sunlight bathed her in a golden glow, creating an ethereal aura around her.

A few loose strands of her elegantly styled hair fell around her smooth neck, dancing in the breeze and adding a playful touch to her appearance.

She wore a pink floor-length gown that hugged her curves perfectly. The sheer fabric offered tantalizing glimpses of her silhouette, and the hem swayed with each step like flowing clouds.

Her features were stunning, perfectly arched eyebrows, bright, clear eyes, a straight nose, and lips as delicate as rose petals.

Her face was a masterpiece, as if sculpted by a master artist.

The onlookers were rooted, their eyes wide and jaws dropped in amazement.

The usually bustling hotel entrance fell completely silent as everyone focused on Aurora.

Time seemed to have stopped.

Daniel carefully lifted the train of her dress, taking care not to create a single wrinkle.

The group slowly made their way into the hotel.

All eyes followed their movement.

Joshua and Celeste, standing among the crowd, couldn't believe they'd run into Aurora at an event like this.

Joshua glared at her with murderous rage.

He should have known she would never stay low-key.

When they forced her to marry Julian, she verbally agreed. Still, her face showed reluctance, and she acted like a pure, innocent maiden.

Looking at her now, she was nothing but trashy!

She just couldn't wait to throw herself at him, dressed up like that. She was obviously trying to seduce Julian at the party!

No, perhaps Julian wasn't her only target. She was probably after every wealthy heir at the party!

This shameless woman had some nerve.

Celeste, however, had a different take on the situation than Joshua.

While Celeste hadn't thought that far ahead, seeing Aurora didn't bring her joy.

Her brows remained tightly furrowed.

Ever since Aurora ran away from home, Barnett had drowned his sorrows in alcohol until he developed a stomach ulcer.

Celeste herself had spent every day worried sick about Aurora's wellbeing.

While they had been sick with worry, here she was, not only living her best life but showing up at Beatrice's birthday party dressed to the nines.

Enraged, Celeste stormed over without a word and slapped Aurora hard.

She screamed, "Aurora, you ungrateful brat! You're living it up while we've been worried sick about you!"
