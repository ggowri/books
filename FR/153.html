Celeste's heart brimmed with regret as memories of her harsh treatment of Aurora flooded back, each one stabbing her with guilt.

Even so, she couldn't help but think Aurora's choice to send her to a mental hospital was brutally cold.

If Aurora had wanted, there would have been countless crueler ways to deal with her.

However, Aurora had only sent her to a mental hospital, which, in a way, was an act of mercy.

Otherwise, Celeste might have ended up like Bertha, subjected to being toyed with by countless men in front of Joshua.

To stop her from running again, they even cruelly broke both her legs and locked her in a pigsty like an animal.

From that day on, she became nothing more than a tool for their lust.

Before long, she got pregnant.

No one knew whose child it was.

Even then, the men showed no restraint, satisfying their urges on her day after day until she miscarried.

Her life became a cycle of beatings and violations.

She became pregnant repeatedly, only to miscarry each time, trapped in an endless cycle.

Eventually, even when the men stopped beating her, her body, ravaged by frequent miscarriages, developed a condition that made her prone to losing pregnancies.

Within a year, pregnancy and loss wore her down, her health crumbling before everyone's eyes.

Bertha had always been proud, dreaming of a glamorous life, and vowing to marry the richest man she could find.

Now, she lived worse than a dog, violated by a pack of old, ugly, filthy men.

The damage to her body and mind was unbearable.

In less than a year of hellish life, her spirit broke, and she descended into complete madness.

Of course, that was a story for later.

In Orley's prison, Barnett had long shed the air of a domineering CEO.

Now, dressed in a prison uniform with a shaved head, he wore heavy shackles on his hands and feet.

He approached forward slowly from a distance, the clinking of his chains echoing through the silent prison corridor, growing louder as he neared.

He figured his visitors would be his parents or Bertha, but the last person he expected was Camille.

When he saw her icy face, his heart raced, and his eyes darted away, avoiding hers.

The day she had woken up, he had already realized Aurora didn't harm her.

But he refused to admit it.

After all, it was he who had confidently testified back then, accusing Aurora of attempted murder.

He had ruined his own sister's life, leaving her disabled, and he couldn't bear to face the truth.

Barnett wanted to escape, but when he met Camille's cold stare, he found himself walking toward her as if compelled.

They sat across from each other, separated by thick glass, each picking up a phone.

Silence hung heavy, neither daring to speak first.

Camille broke the quiet.

"Surprised to see me, huh? Too shocked to talk?"

Barnett dropped his gaze, his eyes unfocused.

His guilty, defeated look didn't stir pity in her.

Instead, it only confirmed her view of him as an utter coward.

"Don't you have anything to ask me?" she continued.

He mustered the courage to meet her eyes, his lips trembling as he pieced together words.

"I've known everything," he finally said.

Camille let out a sharp, bitter laugh.

"You know nothing."

He frowned, sensing there was more behind her words.

Then, she laid out the truth from five years ago, sparing no detail.

She explained how Isabella had deceived Joshua, how Joshua had abandoned Aurora to an orphanage, and why, fifteen years later, he brought her back.

Every detail spilled out, unfiltered.

Barnett listened, his fists clenching until they slammed against the glass.

"Liar! You're lying! You're trying to trick me!" he roared.

Camille scoffed, "Tricking you? You're already in prison. Why would I bother?

"I came here to make sure you know every rotten thing you did to Aurora. I have to tell you the truth. You hurt your own sister for Bertha's sake. Do you regret it now?"

Regret didn't even begin to cover it for Barnett.

His heart twisted like it was being carved open, the pain choking him.

He thought that Bertha might have accidentally pushed Camille down the stairs five years ago and was too scared to admit it.

However, he never expected that the truth was that Bertha and his father had plotted to take Aurora's kidney, harvest her organs, and donate her body as a medical cadaver.

If Camille hadn't overheard their plan by chance, Aurora would have died five years ago.

Even so, Aurora hadn't escaped unscathed. She had been framed, beaten in prison for five years, and left broken.

"How could this be?" he stammered.

"No way! I don't believe it. You're just saying this to torture me, right?"

He lost it, pounding the glass, eyes bloodshot, roaring like a crazed lion.

Camille sat still in her wheelchair, watching him lose his mind with calm detachment.

With a furious yell, Barnett slammed his head against the glass.

Two prison guards rushed in and subdued him.

He still struggled and yelled, "Let me go!

"Find Aurora! I need to see her. I'll ask her myself!" He glared at Camille as he demanded.

Camille smirked coldly.

"Barnett, Aurora won't see you. It's time you felt what she went through in here."

She hung up the phone and wheeled away.

His eyes widened in panic.

"Camille, don't go! I need to see Aurora!"

But she never looked back, not even as her figure disappeared from his sight.

Barnett crumpled to the floor, muttering, "No way... I don't believe it. You're lying."

Tears streamed down his face as he whispered, "Aurora, I was wrong. Please, give me one more chance. When I get out, I'll spend my life making it right."

Barnett was confessing his pain, but he had no idea that by the time he got out of prison, Aurora would have already disappeared from his life.
