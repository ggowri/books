The next morning brought perfect weather. Sunlight streamed through the curtains, filling the room with a cozy warmth.

Aurora got herself ready and made her way downstairs, where she spotted Julian sitting on the couch, completely absorbed in his newspaper.

Hearing her footsteps, he looked up, his eyes softening as they met hers.

Aurora froze, caught off guard. "Mr. Hawke? Aren't you supposed to be at work?"

Julian looked at her intently, his tone calm yet gentle. "You're going to see Joshua and Bertha today?"

"Daniel told you?"

"Yeah." Julian set down his newspaper, his eyes never leaving Aurora.

Aurora fidgeted with the hem of her shirt. "I really need to go. Some things just need closure, you know?"

She paused before asking, "Mr. Hawke, will it mess up your schedule if you come with me?"

Julian just shook his head. "Don't worry about it. I've already passed everything off to Ethan."

Aurora nodded, falling silent for a moment. She knew that once Julian decided to accompany her, there was no changing his mind.

Daniel had already pulled the car up to the front. Once Julian and Aurora were in, he smoothly started the engine.

Aurora sat in the back, watching the familiar city pass by her window, a deep sense of reluctance settling in her chest.

It wasn't the city she'd miss, but Julian and Beatrice.

She knew once she left, she might never come back.

The ride passed in silence until they finally reached the remote mountains.

The mountain road was brutal—narrow, winding, and riddled with potholes.

Aurora limped painfully down the path, each step clearly a struggle. She gritted her teeth, trying hard to look unbothered, but Julian saw right through her act.

Julian crouched down in front of her, his voice gentle and low. "Hop on. I'll carry you."

"No need." Aurora shook her head. "The trail's rough enough already. Having me on your back will just make it worse. I can manage."

Julian wasn't having it. "Get on. If something happens to you, I'll never forgive myself." His tone left no room for argument.

Aurora looked at him, feeling a rush of emotion she couldn't quite name. She bit her lip, hesitated for a moment, then slowly climbed onto Julian's back.

Julian stood up smoothly, adjusting her to a more comfortable position as he secured her legs.

Daniel walked ahead, scouting the path and glancing back at them every so often.

The mountain trail was a mess, with jagged rocks everywhere and potholes that seemed to appear out of nowhere.

Julian's steps were heavy but careful. He watched every move he made, terrified of jostling her.

Clinging to his back, Aurora couldn't shake the guilt building inside her. "Mr. Hawke," she whispered near his ear, "seriously, let me down. I can walk on my own."

Julian just shook his head. "Stay still. We're almost there."

Something in his voice was so final that Aurora gave up arguing. She settled against his broad back, silently taking in the warmth radiating from him.

Julian was drenched in sweat after their endless trek, his shirt sticking to him like a second skin.

Aurora reached over, her fingers gentle as she wiped the sweat from his brow.

Feeling her touch, Julian broke into a smile. Suddenly, he wasn't so tired anymore.

Finally, they spotted a few run-down houses in the distance, looking completely out of place against the backdrop of lush green mountains and clear waters.

That's where Joshua and Bertha were staying.

Julian set Aurora down, and the three of them headed toward the houses.

The closer they got, the faster Aurora's heart raced.

All those painful memories came rushing back like a tidal wave, and she clenched her fists without even realizing it.

Julian sensed her tension and gave her hand a reassuring squeeze.

Daniel had personally escorted Joshua and Bertha here, so he knew exactly where they lived. With his guidance, Julian and Aurora entered the remote, rundown village.

The villagers looked completely down on their luck, wearing clothes that were patched over and over—filthy and falling apart.

The moment these villagers spotted Aurora, Julian, and Daniel, their eyes lit up with curiosity. Their stares locked onto the three of them, following their every move as they made their way through the village.

Under Daniel's lead, they finally arrived at Joshua's old stomping grounds—the house where he'd grown up.

It was a run-down mud brick shack, squat and bare-bones. The walls were cracked, and the roof tiles were half-missing, like the whole thing might cave in any minute.

The yard was overrun with chickens and ducks, giving off a rank stench.

They'd barely reached the doorway when an old man's voice came booming from inside, spewing insults.

"Worthless piece of shit! Why the fuck did I even bother raising you? You ghosted me for thirty goddamn years, living the high life while pretending I didn't exist. What, afraid I'd ask for a fucking handout in my old age?

"Now you're all crippled up and crawling back here? Never saw a cent when you were rolling in it, but now I'm supposed to wipe your ass for the rest of my life?

"You're just like your dead whore of a mother. I dropped two grand on that cheap bitch, and she had the balls to act too good for me, wouldn't even put out.

"Ha! If I didn't need a son, I would've offed her ass years ago. Ungrateful cunt. All that shit I went through for a son, and you turn out to be a backstabbing little prick. Should've killed you both right then - you and that bitch - and let the dogs have a feast. You want food? Go eat shit."

With those words, a man was thrown out the door. He hit the ground with a heavy thud, sending dust flying everywhere.

The man struggled on the ground but couldn't get up or speak. All that came from his throat were painful groans.

This man was Joshua.

Once the proud CEO of Elvador Group, he now wore tattered clothes that reeked of filth. He was so skinny he barely looked human, with sunken cheeks and protruding cheekbones.

His exposed skin was covered in scars—obvious signs he'd been beaten regularly.

Right after, a skinny old man in his seventies walked out of the house.

Without a word, he started kicking and hitting Joshua. "Fucking waste of space. Just die already, you piece of shit!"

"Uh..." Joshua tried to speak, but his stroke had left him paralyzed. When he opened his mouth, nasty drool just dripped out. He couldn't form a single word.

After kicking the young man a few times, the old man finally noticed Aurora, Julian, and Daniel arriving.

He recognized Daniel's face—he was the one who'd dumped Joshua and Bertha's sorry asses here. The old man went stiff as a board.

"You people... what are you doing at my house?"

Daniel barely opened his mouth when some woman's blood-curdling scream ripped through the air from the pig pen.
