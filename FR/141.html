Bertha and Joshua never expected their secret plot to be uncovered by Camille.

Thus, the decision to silence Camille was made on the spur of the moment.

However, when Camille tumbled down the stairs, bloodied and injured, both Bertha and Joshua panicked.

Murder was a crime, and Bertha had no intention of going to prison. The moment she saw Aurora, she immediately pinned the blame for the intentional killing on her.

Bertha disrupted their plan.

Aurora was arrested by the police and sent to prison, which, ironically, had saved her from the horrific fate of forced brain death and vivisection.

Otherwise, she would have been conscious while her organs were brutally harvested, and aher body would have been donated for dissection, all to bolster Joshua's reputation.

From this perspective, Camille saved her life.

Even though Aurora didn't die as planned, her time in prison was filled with inhumane suffering.

In this orchestrated conspiracy, Aurora had no way out.

She faced two grim outcomes: either her body would be worn down, allowing Joshua and Bertha to succeed, or she'd be imprisoned and tormented by Camille's parents, unaware of the truth, along with Bertha and Joshua.

In comparison, going to prison seemed like her only stroke of "luck."

At least she survived and walked out of prison, giving her the chance to witness the truth today.

Yet, this was also what filled Aurora with the most despair and pain.

Because five years ago, she only had two outcomes.

During her time in prison, she was endlessly humiliated, beaten, and told that it was all orchestrated by the Sherwood family.

She had harbored hatred for them for five years.

But now, hearing the truth, she found she couldn't even bring herself to hate them anymore.

Without Camille's intervention, she wouldn't be alive today.

She would have died five years ago.

Her body would be hollowed out.

Her remains would be donated as a cadaver.

And her sacrifice would be used to glorify Joshua's legacy.

How could she hate the Sherwood family?

Without Camille, she wouldn't be here today.

In the end, Camille suffered in her place and saved her life.

She couldn't even bring herself to hate Camille's parents.

Aurora's heart ached as if pierced by needles, and her vision blurred.

She thought that after enduring so much suffering, she had grown accustomed to being unloved.

But when the truth was laid bare, she realized she couldn't accept it.

How could my father so cruel to hollow out my body?

She was abandoned by in an orphanage, left to fend for herself. When she finally returned, she was still treated with cold indifference. For 18 years, Joshua turned a blind eye to her suffering, and now he wanted her life.

She was his own daughter.

How could a father like Joshua exist in this world?

What kind of darkness in his upbringing could have made him so vicious?

What had she done to deserve becoming the sacrificial lamb in this conspiracy?

At this moment, Aurora even felt a sense of relief that she had been abandoned by Joshua.

If she had been raised in the Elvador family, would she have turned out as cruel as Bertha or as foolish as Barnett?

Neither cruelty nor foolishness was what she wanted.

All she desired was a simple life. Why was that so hard to achieve?

This conspiracy not only ruined her life but also brought undeserved suffering to Camille and plunged the Sherwood family into endless pain.

At that moment, Aurora no longer resented the Sherwood family. Instead, she felt indebted to them.

Once a happy and prosperous family, their daughter had become a vegetative state, though she had now awakened, her bodily functions had deteriorated, and her lifespan might be shortened. Their son, once full of promise, had been manipulated by Bertha and was now in custody.

Aurora wiped away her tears and stood up, intending to help Wilson and Lizeth.

But the moment she rose, a searing pain shot through her heart, and her head spun. Her legs gave way, and she collapsed to the ground.

"Aurora!" Beatrice, Ashley, and Daisy cried out in alarm.

The three elderly women, having heard Camille's story, trembled with anger, their eyes red with fury.

They all reached out to catch Aurora, but Julian was quicker, wrapping his arms around her limp body.

Aurora looked up at him, but her vision was blurred by tears. The harder she tried to make out his face, the more the tears fell.

Her voice, when she spoke, was hoarse, as if it didn't belong to her.

"Mr. Hawke..."

"I'm here."

"My heart... it hurts."

Those four simple words struck Julian like a hammer.

This was the first time Aurora told him she was in pain.

Even when she had been hospitalized for drinking, she had never said she was in pain.

Even when Daniel had uncovered the truth, she hadn't expressed such agony.

Julian gently wiped her tears away and gazed at her intently. "Don't be afraid. Whatever you want to do, I'll stand by you. Don’t worry about anything."

Aurora understood his meaning, and her tears flowed even more freely.

For the first time, she didn't want to suppress her emotions.

She threw her arms around his waist, burying her face in his broad, warm chest, murmuring, "Thank you."

Without Julian, she truly didn't know what she would have done.

Perhaps, before the truth came to light, she would have been driven to death by the repeated harm inflicted by the Elvador family.

The living room was filled with Aurora's sobs.

Everyone present was moved by her emotions, and tears fell from their eyes.

Wilson and Lizeth's hearts were in turmoil.

Their daughter had taken the blow meant for Aurora, which was why she had become a vegetable.

They should have hated Aurora, but they had already taken their revenge. Aurora's life had been too tragic, and they couldn't bring themselves to hate her.

After a while, Aurora's sobs subsided.

Just then, Joshua's voice rang out from the courtyard.

"Is Mr. Hawke home? I'm Joshua, Bertha's father. I've come to compensate for the embroidery."

Joshua, now confined to a wheelchair, was pushed by Frank.

The money he held was from his property.

Originally, Joshua didn't need to sell his assets because he had transferred all his money to Isabella, who could have used some to help Bertha.

But Isabella was cunning, once the money was in her hands, she refused to part with a single cent.

Thinking of the properties still under Joshua's name, she said to him, "Joshua, once Bertha is out, we can go abroad with our son. No use for your properties here. Why not sell them all now? It'll help Bertha, and no ties left in Orley. The day Bertha is released, we'll fly abroad. How does that sound?"

Joshua agreed without hesitation.

When Isabella heard this, she was so thrilled that she and Frank had a quick romp in the car.

Joshua's last remaining value had been squeezed dry.

He had no money, no property.

Once they went abroad, he would be left to wander the streets, fending for himself. What a fool.

Now, this fool, thoroughly manipulated, was being driven by Frank to the Hawke Estate.

Joshua intended to compensate for The Garden embroidery, valued at 20 million dollars, which Bertha had destroyed, and plead with Julian for her release.
