Celeste shook her head. "Aurora, this is way too risky."

Aurora waved it off like it was nothing. "That's exactly why I need your help. All I'm asking is that you give the cops a ring if I get snatched. That's not rocket science, right?"

Sure, it wasn't exactly a tall order, but Celeste wasn't willing to roll the dice.

From her past run-ins, she knew just how ruthless Joshua and Isabella could be.

She was afraid that this time, they'd manage to take one of Aurora's kidney.

"Aurora, I—" Celeste started.

"What? You're backing out?"

Celeste quickly shook her head. "I'm not backing out. I just don't want you to end up getting hurt again."

Aurora let out a sneering laugh. "It isn't about whether you're on board or not, is it?"

Celeste, looking utterly perplexed, asked, "What do you mean?"

"Your precious son sees me back in the picture. You really think he won't spill the beans to Joshua and Bertha?"

Celeste's eyes widened in sudden dread, and she vehemently shook her head. "No way. Barnett wouldn't do that."

Even though Barnett had been rough on Aurora, Celeste still had faith that he wouldn't stab his own sister in the back, especially since they were well aware that Bertha was the fruit of an affair.

"Think I'm lying?" Aurora sneered. "Well, why don't you go outside and see what Barnett's up to?"

Celeste still hung back, dead-set on the idea that Barnett wouldn't do anything to harm Aurora.

But her heart was pounding like a drum.

Quietly, she cracked open the study door a smidge. She didn't even have to step out; Barnett's voice from the living room came through loud and clear.

"Dad, have you found a kidney for Bertha yet? Not yet? What gives? How hard can it be to find a match?"

"I gotta tell you something. Aurora, the one who went missing for eight years, is back. She's right here in our house, jawing with Mom in the study."

"What? You think Aurora's kidney might be a good fit for Bertha? You want me to haul her to the hospital for a donation?"

"But knowing her, I know she'd never go for it. She can't stand our family. Alright. Don't sweat it, Dad. I'll see to it that she gets to the hospital."

After hanging up the phone, Barnett just stood there in the living room, deep in thought for what seemed an eternity.

Finally, he mumbled to himself, "You can get by with one kidney. Bertha's health is on the fritz. If she doesn't get that transplant soon, she's a goner. Aurora would just be out of a kidney, but Bertha would lose her life."

"Besides, while Dad was wrong to put Aurora in an orphanage, if he hadn't, she wouldn't have been adopted by the Barron family and lived like a princess. So, he deserves some credit for that."

Though Barnett clearly favored Bertha and wanted to take Aurora's kidney, he didn't want to be seen as the villain, so he concocted all these justifications to harm Aurora.

Celeste heard it all, and for a moment, she nearly lost control, almost charging out to confront Barnett.

How could I give birth to such a brainless son?

Aurora ended up in an orphanage, and you think that's good luck for her?

In her previous life, Aurora had suffered terribly, supposed to be cherished and nurtured like a delicate flower, but had instead withered away far too soon due to Joshua's cruelty.

Celeste finally cottoned on to what Aurora had meant earlier.

Aurora was spot-on. Whether she was out for revenge or not, Barnett was bound to spill the beans about her situation to Joshua.

No matter how they'd lived their lives, it seemed her kidneys were always earmarked for Isabella.

Given Joshua's cut-throat nature, he'd managed to take Aurora's kidney in the past. There was no question he'd cook up something just as dirty this time.

A wave of shame washed over Celeste, and she dropped her gaze. "Aurora, I'm really sorry. It's my bad for not keeping your brother in line."

Aurora fell quiet.

Barnett had always been two-faced, in this life and the last.

Every time he hurt her, he'd put on a sad act afterward, making it look like he really cared.

To the outside world, it seemed he was heartbroken for his sister.

But in reality, he'd never shown her an ounce of kindness, just endless humiliation, mockery, and disdain.

"Ms. Jerome, will you give me a hand?"

Celeste nodded stiffly. "Alright, you have my word..."

She hesitated for a second, locking eyes with Aurora, her own eyes brimming with hope. "Aurora, if I help you out this time, will you think about coming back home and living with me?"

"I'll make sure you get the biggest and swankiest room. You won't ever have to stay in that storage closet again."

Aurora let out a mocking laugh. "Ms. Jerome, you're really pushing it. You think I'm just gonna brush off all the crap you've pulled on me over the years out of the goodness of my heart? And then you want to keep me trapped in this god-awful Elvador Villa?"

"Do you really think this place hasn't done enough damage to me?"

"Let me set you straight. Every single brick, every beam, every blade of grass in this house is like a slap in the face. I've never felt even a smidge of family love here, just your never-ending, crazy-making humiliation."

As Celeste listened, memories of the past came flooding back.

She could clearly see all the times Aurora had been tortured, and each memory felt like a dagger to her heart.

"Hold on," Aurora said all of a sudden, her expression softening.

"I shouldn't say I never felt any family love. At least Daisy really cared about me."

Just thinking about Daisy made Aurora's heart warm.

"In my past life, I always dreamed of growing up and making it big so I could buy Daisy a huge house and let her live out her golden years in comfort. But it never happened. This time, I think I can make that happen."

Celeste clenched her fists tightly.

It hurt her to see her own daughter preoccupied with the thoughts of a servant.

The feeling of being overlooked was excruciating—she longed to have Aurora look her way.

Celeste couldn't help but wonder if Aurora had felt the same way in her past life.

Only through personal experience could she grasp the depth of that pain.

"Aurora, I'm so sorry."

Aurora merely wore a smirk, leaving it unclear whether it was a sign of gratitude or mockery.

"I've said what I needed to say. I should be on my way now."

"Are you really leaving?" Celeste's heart sank at the thought.

Since awakening all those years ago, she had been preoccupied with thoughts of Aurora.

And seeing her now, looking just like she did in their previous life, stirred a longing in Celeste to shower her with love and make amends for the debts of the past.
