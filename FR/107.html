Night had fallen, and the darkness enveloping the city.

Neon lights reflected off the sleek black surface of the Rolls-Royce.

Daniel spotted Julian approaching and promptly opened the car door, bowing slightly with utmost respect as he waited for Julian to enter.

Julian cradled Aurora in his arms, his crystal cufflinks gleaming in the darkness.

She curled against him like a tired cat, seeking comfort.

In the blink of an eye, a dark figure burst through the champagne-colored glow of the streetlights.

The attacker's fist cut through the air, accompanied by a sharp whistle, only to be intercepted by Ethan three inches from Julian's face.

The crisp sound of bones dislocating elicited a groan of pain from the man as Ethan restrained him completely.

"Julian!" the man roared, struggling against Ethan's grip.

"The Elvador family owns you nothing! Why did you have someone break my parents' legs? And why did you send my sister to prison? What do you want from us?"

Julian narrowed his eyes, examining the disheveled man before recognizing him as Barnett.

Barnett's once-pristine suit hung wrinkled on his frame. His hair fell messily across his forehead, and stubble covered his face, giving him a haggard appearance.

The dark circles beneath his bloodshot eyes were so pronounced they resembled bruises.

Aurora was startled at the sudden commotion. She stirred in Julian's arms, her long eyelashes fluttering as she slowly opened her eyes, still hazy with sleep.

When she registered the tense scene and realized Julian was holding her tightly, a blush crept across her cheeks.

"Mr. Hawke," she said with embarrassment, "please put me down."

Julian's arms suddenly felt empty. He frowned slightly with displeasure, throwing a colder look at Barnett, who had awakened Aurora.

When Barnett saw Aurora, surprise flashed in his eyes before quickly giving way to anger.

He recalled drowning his sorrows in alcohol daily after Aurora's disappearance, drinking until he developed a perforated stomach that landed him in the hospital.

After his discharge, he'd wandered like a walking dead.

Whenever he thought of Aurora's award certificates and Vertar University admission letters stashed in the utility room, he hated himself for destroying her life.

Over the past month, Barnett had tortured himself to the point of self-destruction, attempting to atone for his sins through self-punishment.

Yet he never imagined Aurora would have hooked up with Julian.

Compared to a month ago, Aurora looked remarkably better.

Her face had filled out with a healthy glow. She wore haute couture and had been held intimately in Julian's arms. The sight stung Barnett deeply.

He realized that while she'd been missing, she must have been living comfortably. Meanwhile, he'd been a fool, kept in the dark, worrying about her needlessly.

"Aurora!" Barnett shouted, his voice hoarse with rage. "Mom and Dad's legs were broken by Julian, and they're hospitalized. How could you stay with him? Don’t you care about your family?"

“What family? I don’t think I have one,” Aurora replied, her eyes were as calm as ever.

Her soft voice landed like a bombshell, carrying a power that pierced hearts, echoing in the silent night.

Barnett faltered, guilt flickering across his face.

He opened his mouth but found his throat constricted.

After a long moment, he managed to say, "Aurora, blood is thicker than water. If you have any problem with us, come home. We can resolve them together."

Aurora remained unfazed, her tone icy. "All I get when I return to the Elvador family is beatings. When have you ever felt sorry for me? When have you ever tried to solve problems peacefully?"

Her eyes revealed profound disgust as if she were looking at something repulsive.

She went on, "You're always talking about morality and justice, judging me from your high horse without looking at your own faults."

"You lied to me, not just once or twice, but for three whole years. I kept trusting you, giving you endless chances, and what did I get? I ended up in prison. I paid with broken legs and lost a kidney. That should be more than enough to repay your parents for bringing me into this world."

The painful memories no longer stirred any feelings within her, as if the pain had completely frozen her heart.

Barnett knew Aurora spoke the truth, but she was his parents' biological daughter, and he didn't think she could be so heartless.

He pointed accusingly at Julian and asked Aurora. "Seriously? Do you have to be so cruel? You still wanna be with him even he’s the reason why your parents got hurt that badly?"

Aurora fell silent.

Julian was extraordinary—he was basically the very perfect man that every woman dreamed of. But Aurora knew she was scarred and unworthy of him.

Barnett interpreted her silence as confirmation.

He lost control, shouting, "You're an adult! How can you not tell right from wrong? Our parents' legs were broken. Do you know how painful it is to have both legs broken?"

Aurora suddenly laughed—a laugh so bitter and ironic it cut through Barnett.

His eyes widened as his gaze fell on Aurora's injured leg. Instantly, the fight drained from him. He deflated, looking even more haggard and miserable.

No one understood the pain of broken legs better than Aurora, who had experienced it firsthand.

"I believe your parents' injuries have nothing to do with Mr. Hawke," Aurora stated firmly, looking toward Julian.

Julian's eyes softened with a smile, his manner indulgent toward her. "It has nothing to do with me."

Ethan quickly added, "I can testify that Mr. Hawke hasn't seen Mr. Joshua Elvador or Mrs. Celeste Elvador today."

What Julian and Ethan claimed was indeed true.

Aurora had no reason to doubt this—she had seen Celeste and Joshua earlier when she arrived at the hotel.

At that time, both of them had been perfectly fine. Only Daniel knew what happened afterward.

She turned to Daniel, as did Julian and Ethan.

Daniel, as Julian's employee, remained perfectly composed under their gaze.

"Mr. Joshua Elvador and Mrs. Celeste Elvador fell down the stairs and broke their legs," he stated calmly.

Aurora, Julian, and Ethan remained silent.

"Bullshit!" Barnett yelled at Daniel.

The hotel entrance had fewer than 10 steps. Even if they had genuinely fallen, they would have sustained minor scrapes at most. How could both people break their legs simultaneously?

"You can ask the security guard at the door if you don’t believe it," Daniel replied evenly.

The security guards had been watching. When he was mentioned, his heart began to race.

He hurried over, smiling nervously. "Mr. Elvador, your parents did fall down the stairs accidentally and broke their legs. We all witnessed it."

The other security guards at the hotel entrance chimed in unison, "Yes, we all saw it happen."

Barnett's eyes widened in disbelief. He knew it was impossible, yet everyone corroborated the story.

Aurora's lips curved into a knowing smile.

Barnett must be familiar with this feeling. After all, five years ago, Joshua, Celeste, Barnett, Bertha, Hubery, and Adolph had treated her precisely this way.

When Barnett caught Aurora's half-smile, he felt so much pain that he could barely breathe.

Agony spread through his body as he suffocated under the weight of knowing the truth but lacking evidence.

He looked up sharply, searching for surveillance cameras.

Julian's cold voice reached his ears. "My people always clean up the traces thoroughly."

The meaning was clear—even if surveillance footage existed, it would have been deleted, just as Celeste had deleted footage proving Aurora's innocence five years ago.

Fighting fire with fire had always been Julian's favorite.

Suddenly, the fountain lights about 30 feet away illuminated, startling the fish and shattering the reflection of stars and moon on the water's surface.

In the flickering light and shadow, Julian casually fiddled with his pinky ring and said to Daniel, "Well done. You'll get a doubled year-end bonus."

Finally relieved, Daniel replied with a smile, "Thank you, Mr. Hawke."

After a pause, he turned to Aurora. "Thank you, Ms. Elvador."

This blatant provocation nearly made Barnett faint with rage. "You... You..."
