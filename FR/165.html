"Look clearly, I'm not Aurora, but I can tell you very clearly that Aurora will never forgive you for the rest of her life.

"If you truly feel sorry for Aurora, then just die."

Adolph was still heavily drunk, his consciousness muddled.

He reached out, gripping Lily's ankle tightly, pleading, "Aurora, don't be so heartless. I really want to make up for it."

"Make up for it? Do you think a few words of apology and some tears can make up for the wrongs you've done?"

Lily's voice trembled with anger.

"Aurora is such a good person.

"If she hadn't met the Elvador family, the Sherwood family, and Hubery, her life wouldn't have ended up like this.

"She was so kind, so innocent. How could you all do this to her!"

Her heart felt as though it was being stabbed by countless needles.

"What's the use of saying all this now? If you really know you were wrong, then go die. Only by dying will you truly atone for your sins."

With that, Lily raised her foot and kicked Adolph one more time, then turned and decisively walked away.

Samuel stood from the sofa and walked over to Adolph, casting him a cold glance before saying, "If you loved her so much, why didn't you cherish her? You deserve this!"

"Ms. Walker, where are you going?"

It was Adolph's mother, Lizeth.

She was Zoey.

Now out of prison, she had come to find the Sherwood family.

"Reduced my sentence? Do you think that's enough? I'm out now, penniless.

For the Sherwood family in its prime, one million dollars would have been pocket change.

How could he possibly come up with one million in one go?

"This is blackmail! We'll report you to the police!"

Wilson's face turned even paler.

If this came to light, he wouldn't just be ruined.

He could end up behind bars himself.

"Fine. I'll give you the money. But you have to keep your word and leave Orley."

Aurora's future was destroyed, and now the people responsible want to live a good life?

Did they ever ask if she'd allow it?
