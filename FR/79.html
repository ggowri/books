Celeste comforted Bertha for a while before Bertha finally calmed down.

Bertha gave Celeste a sweet smile. "Mom, I'm sorry. You’re still wounded on your belly, yet you're comforting me instead. Please go back to your room and rest, don't tire yourself out."

Seeing Bertha so thoughtful, Celeste felt greatly comforted, and her original fatigue dissipated a lot.

She gently patted Bertha's back and said softly, "Silly child, I'm fine, as long as you're okay."

Celeste turned to Joshua with a serious look in her eyes. "Joshua, send more people to look for Aurora. After all, she is also your daughter. No matter what she has done, we cannot abandon her."

Although Joshua was reluctant, he still nodded.

After speaking, Celeste took Bertha and left the ward.

At the door of the ward, she met Isabella, who had a kind expression on her face.

Celeste smiled, her tone polite and gentle, "Hello, Dr. Steele."

Isabella smiled in response, "I was going to your ward to find you, but you weren't there. So I came to see Mr. Elvador. Mrs. Elvador, you look well. It seems you're already fine."

Celeste said gratefully, "Thanks to your help."

Isabella's tone was somewhat serious. "It's okay. But you still need more rest."

Bertha cooed to Celeste, "Mom, you should listen to the doctor. I'll take you back to your room."

Celeste nodded and let Bertha support her as they slowly walked towards her ward.

Isabella watched the departing figures of Celeste and Bertha, holding her head high with pride.

After they had gone far, Isabella pushed open the door to Joshua's ward and then locked it.

Joshua had been impatient for a long time.

To avoid being discovered, he pulled Isabella into the bathroom. Then he tore her black stockings and eagerly pressed her against the wall.

Isabella also actively responded to Joshua's movements, her eyes blurred.

The bathroom was filled with seductive moans.

Five minutes later, it was over.

As soon as Isabella left the ward, the smile on her face disappeared without a trace.

She cursed, "Useless old jerk!"

For the next three days, Aurora stayed in Orley Caring Hospital to recuperate.

Darren appeared at the door of her ward every day on time, holding a large bouquet of flowers.

Aurora actually didn't want to see him. She was worried that the Elvador family would notice and come looking.

Early on the fourth day, Ethan handled the discharge procedures for Aurora.

Less than half an hour after she left, Darren appeared with flowers.

Upon learning the news of Aurora's discharge, he was so angry that he cursed without regard for his image, "Aurora, you have guts! I come to see you every day, yet you didn't tell me in advance."

What made him even more angry was that he didn't even have Aurora's contact information.

Darren angrily threw the flowers into the trash can and left the hospital in a huff.

Meanwhile, in the car, Aurora sat in the back seat.

Ethan started the car, and it slowly moved through the streets of Orley.

After a while, the car stopped at a red light.

Aurora slowly rolled down the window, a breeze gently brushed her face, bringing a hint of coolness, but also blowing away the gloom that had harbored in her heart these days.

She closed her eyes, feeling the gentle caress of the wind, and showed a long-lost ease.

The green light came on, and the car slowly moved forward.

At that moment, a black Maybach came from the opposite lane, just brushing past Aurora's car.

The driver inadvertently turned his head and happened to see Aurora closing the car window.

Although Aurora only showed her profile, he recognized her at a glance.

Hubery's heart skipped a beat.

He shouted, "Aurora!"

His voice stood out abruptly in the traffic, causing passersby to look sideways.

Hubery didn't even care about traffic violations. He just wanted to keep Aurora and never let her leave again.

He ignored the surrounding traffic conditions and jerked the steering wheel.

Hubery's sudden lane change caused the surrounding vehicles to instantly fall into chaos, with honking sounds rising one after another.

After a loud bang, his car collided with another car. The car shook violently, but Hubery didn't notice at all.

Hubery didn't bother to check his injuries, he just opened the car door and ran in the direction Aurora had left.

He shouted while running, "Aurora, don’t go!"

The owner of the car that was hit immediately got out of the car and chased after Hubery, angrily cursing, "Stop! Are you trying to flee?"

He grabbed Hubery's clothes, but Hubery mercilessly shook him off.

Hubery's mind was filled with Aurora. Without thinking, he punched the man to the ground and continued to chase after Aurora.

Aurora, sitting in the car, had just rolled up the window when she heard a loud bang from behind.

She looked back, and Ethan also glanced in the rearview mirror, frowning. "There seems to be a car accident behind."

Aurora's heart tightened, she faintly heard someone calling her name.

A sense of unease welled up in her heart.

"Ethan, speed up," Aurora urged.

It wasn't until they safely returned to Hawke Estate that Aurora felt relieved.

Beatrice, Ashley, and Daisy had been waiting at the gate, like mother hens waiting for their beloved child to return home. As soon as they saw Aurora, they hurriedly surrounded her.

But when they saw Aurora's pale face, their hearts tightened at the same time.

"What's wrong?" Beatrice's voice was filled with concern and worry.

"Are you feeling unwell somewhere?" Daisy also came up and reached out to touch Aurora's forehead.

Ashley also looked anxious. "Aurora, why do you look so bad?"

Facing the concerned gazes of the three, Aurora was deeply moved.

She shook her head, wanting to explain, but before she could speak, Ethan took the lead. "Ms. Elvador was frightened by the car accident just now."

The three were startled.

Beatrice quickly checked Aurora's body. "Aurora, are you hurt?"

Aurora quickly held Beatrice's hand and comforted her, "Madam Hawke, I'm fine. It was someone else who had a car accident."

Only then did they breathe a sigh of relief, and the expressions on their faces relaxed a lot.

"That's good."

At this moment, Hubery had been subdued by the police.

He was still looking around, searching for Aurora's figure.

It wasn't until the police put him in the police car that he realized he had caused big trouble.

He was facing charges of hit-and-run.
