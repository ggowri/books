Bertha's face was filled with terror, her body writhing as she struggled desperately, her hands waving in the air.

"My father is the chairman of Elvador Group! You can't treat me like this!" Her voice shrieked with sharp desperation, echoing through the banquet hall.

The bodyguards remained unmoved. Their iron-like grips held Bertha's arms firmly, pressing her down to her knees, forcing her to bow at Aurora's feet.

Bertha abruptly turned her head, catching Aurora's gaze, which was as calm and distant as the night sky.

At that moment, she felt like a worthless insect, crushed beneath someone's boot.

Images of all the times she had stood tall, mocking and tormenting Aurora, flooded her mind.

Back then, she had always looked down on Aurora, treating her as someone beneath her, mercilessly trampling on her dignity.

But now, the tables had turned.

Bertha fought to hold back the overwhelming humiliation threatening to drown her. She tried to put on a facade of fragile vulnerability.

Tears welled up in her eyes, quickly pooling and shimmering in the corners.

Her voice trembled with feigned sorrow. "Aurora, why are you letting Mr. Hawke do this to me?"

Tears spilled down her pale cheeks, each drop sliding like a silent confession of her helplessness.

She looked like a delicate flower caught in a violent storm, on the verge of withering, invoking pity in anyone who saw her.

But Aurora remained silent, standing still, her gaze mocking and cold.

When Bertha saw that Aurora wasn't moved, anger flared inside her.

She quickly turned her tearful gaze toward Julian, her eyes full of pleading hope. She hoped her pitiful appearance could soften his heart.

But what she didn't know was that Julian's heart was as cold as stone.

No matter how much she cried, no matter how innocent she seemed, to him, it was all a pathetic charade, a joke that didn't stir even the slightest emotion.

Without the slightest hesitation, he commanded, "Do it."

The shaving blade came down, and Bertha felt a chill shoot through her scalp, a bone-deep coldness that seemed to freeze her from the inside out.

Her body began to tremble uncontrollably, and a desperate, guttural scream tore from her throat. "No! Please, no!"

As the razor moved, her shiny hair was slowly shaved off, falling to the cold, hard floor in disarray.

Bertha thrashed wildly, her body twisting and writhing in panic.

The sharp blade nicked her scalp, and blood trickled down her cheek, staining her flawless skin.

Bertha let out a heart-wrenching scream, a wail that echoed through the banquet hall and pierced the hearts of everyone who heard it.

The onlookers, stunned by the brutal spectacle, went pale.

Especially the women who had tormented Aurora, their faces now drained of color, their legs shaking. It was as though they could see their own fate reflected in Bertha's suffering.

Shaving a woman's head was a profound insult.

Now, with the blade cutting into Bertha's scalp, her hair follicles were likely destroyed.

For a woman who valued her beauty above all else, this was nothing short of a catastrophe, a fate more cruel than death.

Judy, witnessing this, collapsed to the ground. Her eyes were vacant as if her soul had already left her body, leaving only an empty shell.

Adolph, seeing Bertha's suffering, was consumed by panic. Rage surged through him like a firestorm.

Without thinking, he rushed toward her, shouting desperately, "Stop! Stop it!"

But before he could even get close, Ethan kicked him in the chest, sending him flying backward, crashing heavily to the ground.

Two bodyguards immediately stepped forward, pinning him down, and keeping him on the floor.

Adolph thrashed violently, his voice dripping with fury. "Julian, how dare you do this to Bertha? I will never let this go!"

His eyes were bloodshot, his face contorted in rage.

But to Julian, his threats were nothing more than the pitiful squeaks of an ant. He wouldn’t even be bothered to spare him another glance.

With no response from Julian, Adolph turned his fury toward Aurora.

"Aurora, Bertha is your sister! How can you just stand there and watch her suffer like this? Are you a monster?"

Aurora sneered, her voice sharp and biting, "It's been over a month. Camille should be able to speak by now, right? Did she not tell you who the real culprit is, or did you stop her from saying it?"

Adolph's face drained of color in an instant.

The reason he had sent Camille abroad was to prevent her from revealing the truths he didn't want to hear.

He tried to act like nothing had happened.

With cowardly thoughts running through his head, he convinced himself that as long as Camille didn't speak, nothing wrong had happened, and he could still justify everything he had done to Aurora.

He refused to discuss Camille any further, quickly changing the topic. "I'm your fiancé, yet you hooked up with another man. Shame on you! Aurora, you better tell them to stop, or I will never forgive you!"

Aurora only looked at him silently. His accusations and insults sounded to her like a clown's pathetic performance, making her feel nothing but ridicule.

Julian frowned, his voice cold and cutting. "Too noisy."

Ethan understood immediately. He quickly pulled off his socks and without hesitation, stuffed them into Adolph's mouth.

Adolph's eyes went wide in horror.

He writhed, trying to break free from the bodyguards' hold, but it was useless.

He desperately tried to spit out the socks, but no matter how hard he tried, it remained lodged in his mouth.

The disgusting smell filled his mouth, almost making him gag.

All he could manage were muffled, incoherent noises, helplessly watching as Bertha's hair was shaved away.

Bertha lay on the floor, her eyes empty.

A strange, eerie silence filled the banquet hall.

Julian's gaze shifted slightly, coldly sweeping over the four wealthy women who had once helped Judy torment Aurora.

His stare, as chilling as a winter storm, caused them, and their husbands, to tremble uncontrollably.

"Mr. Hawke, we didn't know she was with you. If we had known, we never would have laid a hand on her," one of the women spoke first, her voice shaking, her eyes filled with both fear and ingratiation.

"Exactly! We heard she was trying to seduce Mr. Anderson Hawke, and we acted in a moment of anger. We thought we were doing the right thing. Please, forgive us. We didn't know!" Another woman quickly joined in, her face a mask of pleading.

Their husbands, too, chimed in, their voices dripping with false politeness. "Mr. Hawke, we've already taught them a lesson. They know they were wrong."

Julian smirked slightly, his voice laced with cold sarcasm. "But you seemed to be too easy on your wives. You've torn my woman's clothes off and exposed her to the public, practically naked in front of you."

"You think this is enough to make things right?"

The four women went deathly pale. They realized then that Julian would never let them off the hook so easily.

Their husbands began to panic, desperately trying to explain, "Mr. Hawke, it's all a misunderstanding..."

Julian showed a cruel smirk. "I'm not unreasonable. Since you admit your mistake, then accept the punishment."

"You like to hit people so much, how about each of you leave behind a finger? Then, I'll have someone strip you naked and throw you out of the hotel for everyone to see."

At the chilling suggestion, the four women's faces turned ghostly white, drained of all color.
