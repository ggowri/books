Lily felt utterly helpless, but she kept her cool. She tried to negotiate with Julian. "Mr. Hawke, I can just grab a cab. Let Ethan drive you all home. It would be easier for me to go back alone."

Before Julian could even respond, Aurora jumped in, her tone soft but insistent. "Lily, you haven't heard? There was a brutal attack near a bar last night. It's all over the news now.

"The poor victim got his eyes ripped out, tongue cut off, bones crushed to dust. He was found at dawn and rushed to the hospital, but despite the doctors' best efforts, he didn't make it."

Aurora's eyes went all puppy-dog. "Streets ain't safe at night. Please, let Ethan drive you. We'd feel better."

Daisy chimed in, nodding vigorously, "Yeah, Lily, I get it. You hate being a burden. But this is life or death. Couldn't live with myself if some freak got you in a cab!"

Lily held back an eye-roll.

To Aurora and Daisy, she was just a sweet, innocent girl.

But actually, that vicious "attacker" they were fretting over was none other than Lily herself. They would never imagine this.

A heavy darkness settled in her chest. Last night... yeah, she'd definitely crossed a line.

She could've just ended him right there, but that would've been too easy. Too merciful.

No, she'd wanted him to suffer. To wake up every morning wishing he were dead.

Funny how quickly he'd given up anyway.

What a waste.

Lily plastered on a fake smile and pushed her food around her plate, her mind a million miles away.

Under the table, her fingers worked lightning-fast, shooting off a quick text to Samuel.

Fifteen minutes later, his reply came: [I'm outside.]

Lily set down her fork with a soft clink. "Mom, Aurora, I'm done. You guys take your time. Gotta get back before the dorm locks up."

Aurora smiled warmly, "Alright, go ahead. See you tomorrow after class—we're throwing you a nice birthday party at the Grand Hotel. Noon sharp."

Lily flashed a quick grin in return, then stood and made a beeline for the door.

Ethan shot to his feet almost instantly, trailing close behind.

But by the time he stepped out of the room, Lily was already darting out of the restaurant. In one fluid motion, she hopped onto Samuel's motorcycle.

Ethan's face darkened, a storm brewing in his eyes. "Lily, stop right there!" he bellowed.

She didn't even spare him a glance. "Go, now," she snapped at Samuel.

Ethan wasn't worth her time.

Samuel shot Ethan a smug look, flipped him the double birds, and cranked the throttle. The motorcycle roared to life, peeling out of the lot like a shot arrow, vanishing into the night.

A cold laugh ripped from Ethan's throat. "Go ahead, run. Makes the hunt more fun."

The wind whipped past as Samuel sped through the darkness, Lily clinging tightly to his waist, her body leaning into his. The rush of air howled in her ears.

"Hey, feeling any better tonight?" Samuel shouted over the noise. "If you're up for it, how about we hit a bar?"

Lily's reply was clipped, icy. "Not interested."

"Then where to?"

"Campus."

Samuel wavered, his voice going soft. "It's not even late. Maybe we could-"

Before he could finish, a blinding beam of light sliced through the night behind them, followed by the deep, rumbling growl of an engine.

A car was gaining on them at an alarming speed. In a heartbeat, it pulled level with Samuel's motorcycle.

Lily glanced over, and her gaze locked with Ethan's.

Her stomach dropped.
Damn it! How the hell is he keeping up? Why won't this asshole back off?!

"Speed up," she urged Samuel, her voice sharp and frantic. "Don't let him catch us. I don't care where we end up—just lose him!"

Samuel's face split into a wild grin as he cranked the throttle. The motorcycle surged forward, tearing down the road.

Ethan's Maybach kept pace, its sleek frame a relentless shadow. He wasn't about to let them slip away.

His knuckles went white on the wheel, jaw clenched, eyes locked on his prey.

The Maybach's high beams blazed, eating up the distance between them.

Then, in a flash, Ethan seized his moment. He yanked the wheel hard, tires screeching against the asphalt as the car carved a sharp arc. Sparks flew, lighting up the road like fireworks.

With a jolt, the Maybach swung in front of the motorcycle, forcing it to a grinding halt by the roadside.

Samuel ripped off his helmet, fury blazing in his eyes. He stormed toward the car, fists clenched.

"What the hell is wrong with you, you psycho?" he roared, ready to throw down.

Ethan didn't miss a beat. He stepped up and threw a haymaker that sent Samuel eating dirt.

Before Samuel could get his bearings, Ethan's boot found his ribs, leaving him writhing on the ground.

Ethan stalked past him, zeroing in on Lily like a heat-seeking missile.

She stood her ground, face twisted with rage and panic. "What's your damage?" she snapped, voice rising. "I've spelled it out for you like you're five. Last night was a drunk mistake. Can we just bury it and move on?

"I'm not even pissed about it, so why are you being such a drama queen? Jesus, are you really this pathetic?"

Her words tumbled out in a rush, but Ethan seemed deaf to them.

He stalked up to her like a predator, all power and menace. Then, he grabbed her head and crushed his mouth against hers.

Lily's eyes widened in shock, her body freezing for a split second.

When her brain kicked back in, she lost it. She reared back and slapped him hard across the face. "You done yet?" she yelled.

Ethan's head snapped sideways, but instead of anger, a faint smirk tugged at his lips. He tongued his cheek as if savoring the sting.

In one swift move, he bent down, hoisted Lily over his shoulder, and started walking.

She shrieked, beating his back like a drum, legs going wild. "Put me down, you psycho!"

Ethan didn't flinch. He carried her toward the Maybach with steady, unshakable steps.

The car's headlights bathed them in a harsh glow, illuminating Ethan's crisp suit, its fabric glinting with a steely sheen, and Lily's skirt, fluttering wildly in the breeze, adding a touch of chaotic beauty to the scene.

At that moment, the rest of the world seemed to fade into the background, leaving only the two of them.

At the car, Ethan yanked open the back door and tossed Lily inside.

She barely had time to right herself before he climbed in after her, pinning her down and slamming the door shut.

In the tight confines of the car, Lily squirmed beneath him, her breaths coming fast and ragged. "What do you want from me?" she stammered, nerves fraying.

Ethan's gaze bored into her, dark and intense. "Can't you tell what I want?"

His voice was low, rough with barely restrained hunger.

Lily turned her face away, refusing to meet his eyes.

He chuckled darkly, "Still so stubborn. Guess I'll just have to make you listen."

His hands started exploring every inch of her, raw and hungry. Lily thrashed wildly, fighting to break loose, but he had her locked down tight.

The air in the car turned electric, heavy with tension and raw, dangerous heat.
