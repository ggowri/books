Lily finally breathed a sigh of relief inside the kitchen.

She walked out with her pasta, about to sit next to Aurora.

But then she remembered Aurora couldn't hear, so she changed direction and sat across from her instead.

This way, Aurora could easily read her lips.

As she ate, Lily casually asked, "Aurora, who was that man?"

"That's Mr. Hawke's assistant, Ethan."

Lily's heart skipped a beat, and her fork froze mid-air.

So he really is Mr. Hawke's subordinate?!

Then why was he hanging out with Darren?

The thought of the one million dollars she had scammed from Ethan weighed heavily on her.

She hated arrogant rich people, so scamming them had never bothered her conscience.

But she didn't want to deceive good people, especially those who protected Aurora.

Lily suddenly lost her appetite.

That one million dollars felt like a hot potato, and she just wanted to return it to Ethan as soon as possible.

If Aurora finds out I scammed Ethan, she'll be so disappointed in me.

Lily hung her head low, her emotions in turmoil.

She mechanically shoveled food into her mouth, but it tasted like nothing.

After the meal, Lily helped Aurora clean up the dishes.

Preoccupied with her thoughts, she spoke very little.

Aurora, who couldn't hear, was naturally quiet as well.

The kitchen was filled only with the sound of running water and the faint clinking of dishes.

Once everything was tidied up, Aurora took Lily into her studio.

These days, Aurora spent almost all her time in the studio embroidering, except for eating and sleeping.

Lily looked around at the colorful threads filling the room and then at Aurora, who was completely absorbed in her embroidery. Her heart ached with pity and admiration for Aurora.

Aurora excels at everything she does.

She even managed to learn such a complex skill while in prison.

And in just five years, she's reached a master level.

But if Aurora hadn't gone to prison, her life after graduating from university would have been so much more brilliant.

Lily sat quietly, watching Aurora embroider without blinking.

Aurora sat by the window at her embroidery frame, the morning sunlight streaming through and casting a golden glow over her.

Aurora looks so serene and beautiful while embroidering. If she were dressed in ancient attire, she'd look like a noble lady straight out of a painting.

Lily couldn't help but take out her phone and capture a photo of Aurora embroidering.

After taking the photo, she continued to watch Aurora quietly, but her thoughts drifted far away.

She was busy figuring out how to return that one million dollars to Ethan.

At the same time, she was also thinking about other things.
Aurora said she wants to finish this piece within a year. How am I supposed to save enough money to go abroad in just one year?

Time flew by, and soon it was lunchtime.

After having lunch at Hawke Estate, Lily bid farewell to Aurora and headed straight back to school.

In the afternoon, Aurora continued her embroidery, while Lily attended her classes.

As the sun set, the sky turned a warm orange-red.

After her classes, Lily received a phone call.

The man on the other end spoke in an intimate tone.

"Lily, did you miss me?"

Lily had been irritable all day since seeing Ethan, so her tone was impatient.

"Spit it out."

Samuel was caught off guard by Lily's coldness.

Samuel had a reason for putting the call on speaker.

Earlier that day, their family had held a meeting with one main agenda—hoping that one of the two brothers would go into business and take over the family company soon.

Darren showed little interest.
"I'm a doctor. I have no interest in taking over the family business."
Samuel shrugged.
"I'm still young and want to enjoy life for a few more years. Dad, you're still young too. As long as you're in charge, everything's fine."
Kelson's face turned red with anger.
"A useless doctor and a good-for-nothing slacker. When I'm gone, who's going to take over the business? Don't you need some time to learn the ropes?"
Samuel smirked.

"Darren, just quit being a doctor. It's exhausting. Being a CEO is way more comfortable. My future wealth depends on you."
Darren shot Samuel a glare.

"Being a doctor is my calling. I love saving lives, and I know nothing about running a company. Samuel, you should step up. When you're in charge, build a few more hospitals."
"No way. My youth is precious. I'm not wasting it dealing with businessmen."
"Then I'm not doing it either."
The two brothers avoided the topic of business like the plague, neither willing to take charge.
This infuriated Kelson.

A massive company, and yet no one wanted to inherit it.
"If neither of you wants to take over, then get married. Have kids right away. If I can't count on you, I'll train my grandchildren."
At this, Samuel immediately imagined Lily's beautiful yet fierce face.
He couldn't help but swallow hard.
Winning over Ms. Walker is going to be a long and difficult journey.

So he said, "Why go through all that trouble? Dad, you're only fifty. You and Mom can have another kid. Right, Darren?"

"Samuel's right. Dad, instead of relying on us, you and Mom should get to it."

"None of you are taking me seriously. What do you even want?

Kelson's eyes lit up. "Really?"

Darren, however, poured cold water on his enthusiasm.

"If you really have a girlfriend, then make a call and let us hear it."
"Fine, I'm not afraid."

Lily's number was at the top of his contacts, labeled "Goddess Lily."

If this girl is Samuel's goddess, she must be someone he likes.

Without hesitation, Darren dialed Lily's number, flashing a mischievous grin as he put the call on speaker.
Samuel's heart nearly stopped.
He wanted to hang up, but the call had already connected.
Reluctantly, he decided to brazen it out, pretending to be close to Lily.

"Lily, did you miss me?"

Samuel's face turned bright red.
