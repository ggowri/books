Aurora watched eagerly, but Celeste's expression didn't show the doting affection she had expected.

Instead, when Celeste saw the unconscious Megan and Bertha, along with Barnett writhing in pain on the ground, her head throbbed violently.

Flashbacks hit her hard.

"Mom, you knew Bertha pushed Camille down the stairs. Why cover for her?

"You're my birth mom! Yet you erased the footage that could've cleared my name. How could you be so cruel to me?

"Officer, it wasn't me. You've got the wrong person—it was Bertha!

"Was I just a pawn for you? Fine, I'll repay your 'kindness.' You only birthed me but never raised me. That debt isn't worth much.

"You're all murderers. You drove me to my death. I don't care about my innocence anymore—I just want you dead!

"Celeste, your beloved husband took your kidney for his mistress, and the daughter you coddled was that woman's child. Karma's finally catching up with you."

The scene in the stairwell was a mirror of the past, flooding Celeste with memories of her former life.

Those brutal images tore through her mind, making her head feel like it was splitting, her heart aching as if stabbed.

The daughter I carried for ten months was abandoned by that bastard Joshua to suffer in an orphanage, while he forced me to raise Bertha, that illegitimate child!

My kidney was taken and given to Isabella.

My daughter's kidney was taken and given to Bertha.

When I learned the truth and tried to take revenge on Joshua, he stabbed me in the eye.

The memories tortured her until she collapsed, her body convulsing.

Aurora frowned at the sight.

Is Celeste so heartbroken seeing Barnett and Bertha covered in blood that she can't even stand?

But in my old life, when I was covered in scars, Celeste was living her life like nothing happened.

Guess she can't even be bothered to pretend.

Aurora shot Celeste a cold glance.

Seeing the total lack of hope in Aurora's eyes felt like a thousand arrows hitting Celeste's heart. It hurt so much she could barely breathe.

Gritting through the agony, she stretched a trembling hand toward Aurora. Her gaze was filled with endless remorse and anguish.

I know how stupid I was.

Aurora, please believe in me one more time.

This time, I'll give you all my love and cherish you. You're my true treasure.

"Aurora, I'm sorry..."

Celeste's voice was so faint that Aurora didn't even catch what she said.

Not that it matters. Whatever she's saying, it's definitely nothing good.

It's the same old insults—calling me a monster, ungrateful, wicked.

I've heard it all before.

"Julian, let's go. My mom will worry if she can't find me."

"Okay."

Julian took Aurora's hand, and the two walked away.

Celeste tried to stand and chase after them, but her grief had paralyzed her.

All she could do was let tears of regret stream down her face.

"Oh, my sweetheart, you scared me to death! Let me check—are you hurt anywhere?"

Laura's expression darkened with fury.

"How dare they lay a finger on my princess? They've got a death wish."

William added angrily, "Mom, it was that same brother and sister from the children's store yesterday. They got into Celestia Palace, barged into the lounge, and tried to hit Aurora and me!"

"Where are they?"

"They're over there. They even tried to frame me for pushing Megan down the stairs."

Celeste's eyes locked onto Aurora. "The child in your arms is my daughter.

Celeste's behavior was off.

She shouldn't even recognize me at this point. Yesterday at the store, she didn't know who I was and even helped Bertha bully me.

Did Celeste also get a second chance?

Celeste hurt me so deeply in my old life. Just because we've gone back in time, does that mean I should forgive her?

Let her keep dreaming!

I have a better mother now, who truly loves me. I don't need Celeste's affection.

"Mommy, that woman looks scary. I'm afraid."

The phrase "that woman" hit Celeste like a hammer, making her head pound.

I'm not some stranger.

I'm her mother. Her real mother!

Why is she in Putnam? And adopted by someone else?

She has a new mother now. From yesterday, it's clear this woman gives her all the love she needs. She's no longer starved for affection.

That makes me, her birth mother, completely unnecessary.

Celeste couldn't accept it.

I haven't even atoned yet. I haven't repaid the debt I owe her.

How can Aurora already not want me?

"I'm your real mother. Aurora, please, come to me."

Now you remember you're my mother? Too late!

She lifted her head from Laura's shoulder, feigning childish innocence.

"If you're my birth mother, why did you abandon me in an orphanage the moment I was born? Did you hate me that much?"

The question struck Celeste like a fatal blow.
