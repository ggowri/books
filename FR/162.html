Ethan's eyes followed the motorcycle closely, trying to get a better look at the girl's face.
But the motorcycle was speeding so fast that it vanished from view in the blink of an eye.
Ethan pressed down on the accelerator, but just then, the deafening roar of multiple motorcycle engines came from behind.
Six or seven motorcycles zoomed past his car like lightning, taking over the once-empty road.
Ethan had no choice but to ease off the accelerator.
As he watched the rebellious youths riding ahead, he realized he must have been overthinking things.
She's Daisy's daughter and has a good relationship with Ms. Elvador.
How could she possibly be hanging out with these delinquents?
Maybe it's just a coincidence that the girl is wearing the same clothes as Lily.
So, Ethan let go of the chase, slowed down, and allowed the group of motorcycles to fade into the distance.

At the front of the pack, Samuel was riding his motorcycle at breakneck speed.

His posture was agile, leaning slightly forward, his hands gripping the handlebars steadily.

Only his bright eyes were visible through the helmet, shining with excitement.

In front of Lily, he was giving it his all, showcasing his riding skills to the fullest.

He thought he must look incredibly cool right now.

Samuel couldn't help but smile smugly.

He shouted, "Ms. Walker, how's my riding?"

He was eagerly anticipating Lily's praise, imagining how she would respond in an admiring tone.

But what he got was Lily's cold, emotionless voice.

"There's a curve ahead. If you don't want to die, focus."

Samuel's enthusiasm was doused.

Ms. Walker is still so cold.

But strangely, her coldness is damn attractive to me.

Samuel had been born with a silver spoon in his mouth, surrounded by people who flattered him. Every woman he had met before had tried their hardest to please him.

No one had ever dared to yell at him.

But Lily was like a unique light that had burst into his otherwise mundane world, making him fall for her.

He had never thought he would like a cold, ruthless, and violent woman.

But Lily's violence impressed him, and her scolding inexplicably excited him. He was falling deeper and deeper, even wondering if he had a masochistic streak.

Lily was completely unaware of Samuel's thoughts.

The motorcycle sped through the curve, and Lily held tightly to Samuel's strong, muscular waist.

She looked back and didn't see Ethan's car, which finally eased her tension.

Good, he didn't catch up.

She was afraid Ethan would recognize her and tell Aurora about this side of her.

Aurora was so gentle and obedient.

Lily didn't dare to reveal this side of herself in front of her, fearing it would scare her.

That's why she pretended to be well-behaved in front of Aurora.

Actually, it wasn't entirely an act.

When she was with Aurora, she would calm down and not be as cold and ruthless as she was with others.

The motorcycle shot through the empty road like a black lightning bolt.

Without a doubt, Samuel and Lily were the first to reach the finish line.

Samuel took off his helmet with flair, his hair flying wildly in the wind. He raised an eyebrow at Lily, looking smug. "Ms. Walker, how was it? Am I cool? Do you want to date me?"

Lily remained calm and unexcited.

She leisurely pulled out her phone, opened the payment app, and said, "Transfer the money."

Samuel's smile froze.

"Ms. Walker, you're so heartless! I was so cool, and you didn't even praise me. You're really breaking my heart."

Lily's gaze was cold, staring at him without blinking.

Under her gaze, Samuel's heart raced, and his face turned slightly red.

He quickly looked down, pretending to search for his phone, but it was really to hide his inner turmoil and excitement.

At that moment, a voice in his head screamed.
Damn, Ms. Walker's look of disdain is so thrilling!

I love it!

Ms. Walker, keep looking at me like that, only at me.

He fumbled for his phone and, without thinking, transferred a million dollars to Lily.

Seeing the sudden increase in her account balance, Lily was slightly stunned.

"What do you mean by this?"

Samuel chuckled, scratching his head, and explained, "Didn't you agree to be my girlfriend for a day for 100 thousand dollars? I'm giving you a million dollars, so be my girlfriend for ten days, okay?"

A faint smile tugged at Lily's lips, and a hint of amusement flashed in her eyes.

She found Samuel's behavior somewhat endearing.

She stepped closer to Samuel, grabbed his collar, and forced him to lower his head to meet her gaze.

Samuel's heart skipped a beat at her sudden move.

His face turned completely red, all the way to his ears.

"Ms. Walker, what are you doing?" He stammered, his mind involuntarily conjuring images of a kiss.
Is Ms. Walker going to kiss me? No, I'm not ready for a kiss yet!

But since Ms. Walker is taking the initiative, bring it on.

Samuel slowly closed his eyes, puckered his lips nervously, and waited for Lily's kiss.

Lily looked at his silly expression and barely suppressed a laugh.

"If you still need a girlfriend after ten days, as long as you pay, I'm at your service."

With that, she let go of him and pushed him away.

Samuel's fantasy shattered, leaving him feeling empty.

He looked at Lily with a pitiful, resentful gaze.

I spent a million dollars to have her for ten days, and she didn't even give me a kiss.

Just then, the others began arriving at the finish line.

Samuel saw his rival and flashed a mocking smile, raising an eyebrow. "You lost."

Edie snorted and pushed Cynthia toward Samuel.

"Here, take her."

Cynthia stumbled into Samuel's arms.

Samuel acted like he had touched something dirty and pushed her away in disgust.

"I never said I wanted her."

Edie sneered, "Didn't we agree? If I lost, I'd give Cynthia back to you. If you lost, you'd give me your woman."

As he spoke, his gaze landed on Lily. He looked her up and down shamelessly and let out a flirtatious whistle. "Hey, beauty, what's your name?"

Lily acted as if she hadn't heard him, not even glancing his way.

Edie's face darkened, and his brows furrowed. He said in a hostile tone, "I'm talking to you. Are you deaf?"

Ever since discovering Aurora's hearing loss, Lily had become very sensitive to that word.

She turned around, her gaze like icy daggers, and said coldly, "I have no obligation to tell you that."

Edie was taken aback, then a playful smile reappeared on his face.

"Mr. Samuel Lowell, this girl's got some attitude. Why don't you let me have her? Don't you like Cynthia? We can swap."

"I don't like Cynthia. I'm not a garbage collector. I don't take trash." Samuel's face turned red with anger as he lashed out.

Then he pulled Lily behind him, shielding her completely.

Next, he pushed Cynthia back toward the man.

"You take care of your own woman. I'm not interested. My new girlfriend is a thousand times better than her."

Cynthia was humiliated, tears welling up in her eyes.

She said pitifully, "Samuel, how could you say that to me?"

Samuel shot her a look.

"You don't seriously think I agreed to the race for you, do you?"
"Isn't that why?"
"Not at all! I just wanted you to see how much better my current girlfriend is. Stop flattering yourself!"

"You..." Cynthia stomped her foot in frustration.

She bit her lip, tears streaming down her face, and said with grievance, "Edie, he's humiliating me."

Edie clicked his tongue, glanced at Samuel, and said in a harsh tone, "Mr. Samuel Lowell, that's not how it works. We had an agreement. If I lost, I'd give Cynthia to you. You turn her down, and it's like slapping my face."
"I never said I wanted her. Are you crazy? Let's go."
Samuel couldn't be bothered to argue, grabbing Lily and the other two friends as he made to leave.
"Did I give you permission to leave?" Edie narrowed his eyes, warning them.
"Do I need your permission?" Samuel shot back defiantly.
