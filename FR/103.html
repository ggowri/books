The four women, used to living the high life and feeling superior, now looked like hunted prey.

Fear washed over them like a tidal wave. They shook, their once proud heads hanging low in pure terror.

One of the women shook violently, her muscles spasming in tension.

With a tearful plea, she begged, "Mr. Hawke, please punish me in another way. We really know what we did wrong!"

Her husband, usually confident and assertive in the business world, was now desperate, his demeanor ingratiating as he approached.

He begged, "Mr. Hawke, please forgive us. My wife doesn't know better. Spare her. We're willing to pay any price!"

The other three women, already reduced to a sobbing mess, had their meticulously applied makeup running down their faces, turning them into a pitiful sight.

They cried out, "No! Please, don't strip us of our clothes. This is too humiliating. Mr. Hawke, have mercy!"

Julian's smile held an eerie, knowing quality as his voice rumbled low, rich with intent. "You're really willing to pay any price?"

They thought they saw a flicker of leniency in his eyes, a faint hope sparked within them.

However, what came out of Julian’s mouth next crushed their hopes completely.

"Would you be willing to go bankrupt for it?"

In that instant, the atmosphere in the banquet hall grew tense and suffocating. Everyone stood motionless, the smiles they had yet to express immediately vanishing.

A wife could be replaced, but the collapse of a company meant years of work gone in an instant. The chance of rising again was slimmer than a miracle.

The husbands' eyes flickered between greed and inner conflict. After a brief moment of hesitation, the balance tipped heavily toward protecting their companies.

They could only watch as Julian signaled to the bodyguards to proceed.

The bodyguards seized the women's arms, dragging them aside and forcing them to lay their hands flat on the table.

"Let me go!" the women screamed and fought desperately.

Julian, with an air of elegance, picked up a fruit knife. The blade gleamed coldly under the light, reflecting his steely expression.

He handed the knife to one of the women's husbands.

The man, his face twisted with fear, stammered, "Mr. Hawke, what do you mean?"

Julian sneered coldly, his words deliberate, "You failed to discipline your wife, which is why she's wreaking havoc out here. Now, I'm giving you a chance to discipline her."

The man's hand shook uncontrollably as he grasped the fruit knife.

His gaze shifted between Julian's piercing eyes and his wife, who was now sobbing uncontrollably and on the brink of collapse.

The man's dilemma was palpable.

He wanted to beg for mercy, his lips trembling, but his throat felt as though an invisible hand was choking the words out of him.

"Don't listen to him," his wife cried out.

The man glanced at his wife, a flicker of pain and reluctance crossing his face.

He wanted to lower the knife, but when he locked eyes with Julian's cold, unrelenting gaze, his hand froze as if paralyzed.

"Mr. Hawke, please. Is there any other punishment? I... I really can't do this," the man pleaded.

Julian remained silent and unwavering.

At the same time, Aurora stood to the side, quietly observing, her eyes devoid of any pity.

These were the same people who had once humiliated her, beating her without a second thought.

Of course, they never imagined they would find themselves trapped in such a brutal predicament back then.

Watching them squirm in terror brought Aurora a strange sense of satisfaction.

She turned her gaze to Julian.

The light fell on him, casting his figure in sharp relief.

Aurora felt a deep sense of security in his presence.

Her eyes slightly welled up, and she was filled with a mix of emotions of gratitude, dependence, and an unfamiliar sense of safety.

The room was deathly silent, the air thick with tension. No one dared to make a sound, fearful of attracting Julian's attention.

Not a sound could be heard, save for the man's labored breathing and his wife's desperate sobs echoing through the tense space.

"I'll count to three. If you're not moving by the time I reach three, you won't get another chance." Julian's voice shattered the silence.

"Three..." His voice was as cold as ice, carrying an edge of finality.

The man flinched, his body trembling as his face contorted in agony.

"Two..."

The man gritted his teeth, his features twisted in pain as his eyes, bloodshot, darted around, like a cornered animal.

"One..."

At the final count, he steeled himself.

With a sharp motion, he brought the knife down, severing his wife's thumb with brutal precision.

"Ah!!!" she screamed, her body jerking violently, before she collapsed backward, losing consciousness from the pain.

The bodyguards wasted no time. They grabbed a bucket of ice-cold water, splashing it on the woman's face.

The shock of the cold water revived her, and she awoke with a start, her eyes locking onto her severed bloody thumb.

She screamed again, her wails a heart-wrenching sound that filled the room.

The man dropped the bloodied knife with a loud clatter, trembling uncontrollably.

He fell to his knees, tears flooding from his eyes.

The onlookers were frozen in shock. Some women covered their mouths, unable to make a sound. Some men turned their heads, unwilling to witness the cruelty.

The people who had once been close to this couple now recoiled, afraid of being swept into this dreadful storm.

The remaining three couples were now thoroughly terrified.

Julian's icy gaze swept over the kneeling man. His voice was devoid of any sympathy. "Next."

The men stumbled backward, their faces ashen. They could barely able to stand, while their wives pleaded.

Julian remained unmoved, and the women, desperate, turned to Aurora for mercy.

"Ms. Elvador, we're sorry. We were wrong. Please, spare us."

"We'll do anything. Just let us go, please."

"Please, we beg you!"

Aurora looked at their terrified faces, and not a shred of sympathy stirred in her heart.

The only reason she could stand here and seek revenge was because Julian was protecting her.

Without him, she would've had to endure the beatings and humiliations.

If that had been the case, she would've been humiliated again the next time she crossed paths with these women.

So, she didn't feel the need to forgive them just because they begged. She wouldn't be a saint for the ones who had hurt her.

Aurora bent down and picked up the bloodstained knife. The blood from the blade dripped onto the floor, splattering like red flowers on the polished surface.

She handed the knife to one of the men, a small, cryptic smile playing at the corner of her lips.

"Your wife has to pay for what she did. Don't you agree?"

The man's eyes widened in fear.

The girl in front of him appeared delicate, but her eyes held a chilling resolve that made his blood run cold.

It was clear that she wouldn't let this go.

When he hesitated to take the knife, Aurora's smile faded. "I don't want to waste any more time talking."

Her words were like frost, sending a shiver down his spine.

With trembling hands, he grabbed the knife and, in a final act of desperation, like the first man, he cut off his wife's thumb.

One by one, the screams echoed through the hall.

The four women had their thumbs severed, and their clothes were ripped off, leaving them in nothing but their underwear.

Their bodies, exposed to all, bore the marks of humiliation and pain, amplifying their desperate cries.

The bodyguards dragged them out of the banquet hall, their wails still ringing in the air.

It was six o'clock in the afternoon, peak rush hour.

The streets were bustling with cars, with people coming and going.

The four women were thrown out of the hotel, causing an immediate stir.

Pedestrians stopped in their tracks, staring with a mix of shock, curiosity, and pity.

Their dignity shattered, their hands crippled.

No family with wealth and status could tolerate such a ruined lady of the house. Their futures were now forever ruined...

Back in the banquet hall, the air was filled with an almost suffocating tension.

Bertha and Adolph, having witnessed Julian's cruelty, dared not make a sound.

They shrank back, doing everything they could to avoid drawing attention, fearing they might be the next victims.

Julian turned toward Judy, his voice low and cold. "Next, it's your turn."
