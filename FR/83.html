Someone like him would never apologize, even after a fight and he was in the wrong.

His assistant's presence in the room couldn't bode well.

He approached the bed with deliberate calm and emptied the bag, spilling ten neat stacks of crisp bills onto Adolph's bed.

"This is 'medical compensation' from Mr. Hawke. He said 100 thousand dollars should be more than enough to cover your expenses. Feel free to use the rest to buy some supplements. Maybe something to boost your brainpower."

Adolph's face twisted into a shade of livid fury. His eyes blazed with indignation.

One hundred thousand dollars might be a considerable sum to some, but to Adolph, the scion of the Sherwood family, it was just a small amout, not even enough to buy the shoes on his feet.

To think his injury was deemed worth a mere one hundred thousand dollars by Julian was a slap in the face. And that remark about "boosting his brainpower" was a blatant jab at his intelligence.

Rage surged through Adolph, his neck veins bulging as his face contorted with anger.

He seized the money from the bed and hurled it at Ethan.

"Fuck off!"

Ethan, agile and composed, dodged effortlessly, the flying bills missing him entirely.

Watching Adolph's meltdown, Ethan felt a quiet satisfaction, though his expression remained impassive.

Ethan said, "Mr. Sherwood, there's no need for this. Mr. Hawke means well."

His calm demeanor only highlighted Adolph's frenzied state.

Overcome by fury, Adolph attempted to lunge at Ethan, disregarding the pain from his wounds, but he stumbled and fell to the floor.

Ethan couldn't help but chuckle at the sight.

"Seeing you so spirited, I'd say you're on the mend. Rest up. I'll be sure to let Mr. Hawke know you're not on death's door."

Just before it shut, he shot Adolph a sarcastic grin. "See ya, Mr. Sherwood!"

That infuriating gesture nearly sent Adolph over the edge.

His insides churned, threatening to expel everything within him.

Aurora? What's so special about that girl? She can't hold a candle to Bertha. If it hadn't been for our need of her, I never would have brought her back from the orphanage eight years ago.
