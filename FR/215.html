Celestia Palace was the only seven-star hotel in Putnam.

To celebrate Aurora's birthday, the Barron family had reserved the entire hotel.

Laura had dressed Aurora in a custom-made pink princess puff dress.

With her flawless skin and cute, soft look, Aurora was like a living doll.

When the four of them arrived, Benjamin crouched down to Aurora's level, gently patting her head.

"Sweetheart, there are many guests outside. I have to play the host, so I can't stay with you."

All the guests were big shots in Putnam, and as the head of the family, it was his duty to greet them.

Though Aurora had never had her own birthday party before, she'd seen the Elvadors throw grand ones for Bertha many times, so she knew how it worked.

She nodded obediently. "Okay, Daddy. You can go."

Seeing how mature she was for her age, Benjamin felt proud but a little heartbroken.

The usually tough and cold Benjamin now turned into putty in his daughter's hands.

With reluctance written all over his face, he said, "Not even a little sad to see me go, sweetheart? I don't want to leave my baby girl for even a minute."

He even pretended to cry, putting on an exaggerated display of sorrow.

Aurora was speechless.

Fatherly love is just too overwhelming.

She cupped his face and played along. "Daddy, you're a grown-up. Don't cry like a kid, or you won't look handsome anymore."

Benjamin sniffled. "Then I want a big hug to make me feel better."

At his words, Aurora felt helpless.

Ever since joining the Barron family, her days had been filled with hugs, kisses, and over-the-top affection.

It was sweet… but kind of overwhelming.

Still, she gave him a tight hug and even threw in an extra kiss as a bonus.

"Daddy, hurry and greet the guests."

Now with Aurora's kiss and hug, Benjamin instantly revived and then handed Aurora over to Laura.

After he left, Laura pointed to the grand door in front of them and said to Aurora, "When the birthday party starts, I'll walk you through this door. You will be the shining star of this celebration, my dear."

To the old Aurora, this door had seemed like an insurmountable chasm.

She'd dreamed so many times that the Elvadors would throw her a party, walk her through this door, and proudly introduce her as their daughter.

Yet, it was just a fantasy.

The Elvadors had never given her an ounce of love.

Now here were the Barrons, showering her with everything she'd craved.

Each kind gesture made her hate her old self more.

If she hadn't stayed in the orphanage for Hubery and had just agreed to be adopted by the Barrons in her old life, she would have had all this much sooner.

No manipulation by the Elvadors. No disability. No drinking poison in despair.

Worse still, she'd dragged poor Lily down with her, turning her into a murderer.

This time, Lily is far away from my miserable past!

I'm sure she'll live a normal life.

Aurora silently vowed that when she grew up, she would visit Daisy and Lily and make up for everything she owed them.

Just as Aurora was lost in thought, Laura spoke again.

"Hey, sweetie, we've got time before the party. Why don't we chill in the lounge for a bit?"

"Okay, Mommy."

Then, Aurora, Laura, and William headed toward the lounge together.

Once they arrived, Laura turned to Aurora with a serious expression. "I need your opinion on something, Aurora."

Aurora blinked in confusion. "What is it?"
