"Your daughter?" Laura sneered, her voice dripping with disdain. "You let her end up in an orphanage. What right do you have to call Aurora your daughter?"

Celeste's fists clenched, her voice shaky but firm. "I'm her real mom. Blood ties can't be broken."

Aurora felt sick. Celeste talked about making things right, but did she forget the pain she caused before? Didn't she remember how much Aurora hated the Elvador family?

If she truly wanted to make amends, she should've stayed far away, like she was gone for good.

Celeste looked at Aurora with what seemed like real warmth.

"Aurora," she pleaded, "I'm your birth mother. Come home with me."

But Aurora's face stayed ice-cold, saving her warmth for the Barrons.

With a tone sharper than frost, she replied, "This is my home."

The rejection pierced through Celeste like shards of glass.

"Aurora, you have to believe that I love you. You're the child I carried for nine months. How could I ever abandon you to an orphanage? It wasn't me; it was your father, that despicable man who left you there. I didn't know anything about it!

"Now that I've finally found you, won't you come back with me? Please?

"Don't you love pretty dresses? I will buy you as many as you want. Anything at all. You name it."

Inside, Aurora scoffed silently.
Dresses? I don't care about dresses. Not really.

She treasured everything the Barrons gave her, even if it was just a leaf or a pebble.

To her, anything from them was priceless.

As for Celeste? Even if she brought down the moon from the sky, Aurora wouldn't spare it a second glance.

Instead, Aurora nestled deeper into Laura's arms.

"Mommy, I'm so tired."

Laura stroked her hair soothingly, her eyes brimming with affection.

"Then sleep, sweetheart. Today must've been exhausting with all those reporters around."

Without hesitation, she scooped up Aurora and headed upstairs.

Seeing this, Celeste lunged forward, panic overtaking her features.

"Where are you taking my daughter? Give her back to me right now!"

Benjamin stepped in swiftly. "You're not welcome here. Leave."

"Leave?" Celeste spat incredulously. "Why should I leave? If you wanted a daughter so badly, why didn't you have one yourselves instead of stealing mine? How dare you!"

Frustration was etched across Benjamin's face. He had zero tolerance for further theatrics from this lunatic.

"Guards!" he barked sharply. "Escort this woman off the premises. Now."

Two big guys in black showed up in seconds, one on each side of Celeste.

Before she could protest, they dragged her out forcefully, tossing her onto the driveway outside.

She pounded on the door, screaming at the top of her lungs.

Yet, no one came.

As dusk settled into night, her cries eventually faded into weary silence.

Defeated, she stared longingly at Barron Estate before reluctantly walking away.

In a rundown rental apartment, Celeste arrived home only to hear Joshua's angry shouts echoing from inside.

"Barnett! What kind of nonsense is this? While your mother and I were locked up these past few days, you let your sister live in this dump?"

His voice dripped with disdain. "You're a boy, so you can rough it out. But your sister grew up surrounded by luxury! How could you subject her to such hardship?

"You're doing this on purpose, aren't you? Just like your mother. Both of you—nothing but thorns in my side. Just being around you pisses me off."

Celeste's veins throbbed visibly beneath her skin.

This was how Joshua treated her children when she wasn't around.

If he was this awful to his own son, what did he do to Aurora before?

With a thunderous kick, the door flew open, startling everyone inside.

Joshua spun around, fury blazing in his eyes.

"What the hell is wrong with you?"

Celeste's glare was murderous.

Without a word, she grabbed a broom leaning against the wall and began beating him mercilessly.

Blow after blow landed, driving him to cower and retreat.

"You crazy bitch! Stop it!" he yelled, shielding his head desperately.

But Celeste showed no mercy. Each strike fueled her rage until Joshua was battered and bruised beyond recognition.

In the corner, Bertha huddled silently, terrified.

Though still a child in years, Bertha wasn't naive.

She knew her mother, the woman who had once showered her with love, had turned into a stranger.

It started at the birthday gala of Aurora. Ever since, her mother's gaze had morphed into a look of pure hatred, as if Bertha had become a festering eyesore.

Barnett stood frozen, watching his father get pummeled without uttering a word.

Finally, when exhaustion overtook her, Celeste stopped.

Pointing accusingly at Joshua, she roared, "What gives you the right to lecture my son, you bastard?"

Joshua clenched his teeth, still defiant. "I scolded him for his good! And you attack me over something so trivial? Celeste, when did you become such an unreasonable shrew?"

Her laughter erupted, wild and bitter, tears streaming down her face.

"Unreasonable? Shrew?

"You complain that Bertha is too delicate to endure hardship, but apparently, my biological son deserves suffering, and my daughter, abandoned in an orphanage, deserved even worse. Is that it?"

Joshua's heart skipped a beat.

Celeste has found out? Impossible. Don't be ridiculous. There's no way she could've pieced it together.

"What nonsense are you spouting? I don't understand a word," he stammered nervously.

Celeste slapped him hard across the face.

"You killed my father, dumped my newborn daughter in an orphanage, and handed me that bastard child Bertha to raise. You heartless monster."

Shock widened Joshua's eyes as he stared at Celeste.

She really does know. How?

He smirked bitterly, "So what if I did? Your father forced me to marry into his family, and he always looked down on me. That old fool deserved to die.

"Once he was gone, dealing with you was easy. You dumbass!

"But I never expected you'd find out so soon. Tell me how?"

"You vile creature! I'll kill you!"
Enraged, Celeste lunged again, swinging the broom wildly.

Overwhelmed, Joshua fled, dragging Bertha along behind him.

Celeste lunged forward, her legs pumping desperately as she chased after him, but the distance between them grew.

The broom slipped from her fingers, clattering to the ground.

Breathless and defeated, she collapsed to her knees.

Wrapping her arms around herself, she wept uncontrollably.

Her voice broke, raw with longing.

"Aurora, my daughter, when will you return to me? I miss you so much. I want to make up for everything I've done wrong."

Memories flooded her mind, the reporters crowding around Aurora at Barron Estate earlier that day.

This version of Aurora seemed even more extraordinary than the last.

In their previous life, Aurora had been the top scorer on the SAT at eighteen.

But in this timeline, at merely five years old, she was already a university student, captivating journalists with her brilliance.

Such a remarkable child was her daughter. But she lost her dear daughter.

Is fate mocking me, giving me a second chance only to rip it all away again?

Standing by the doorway, Barnett watched his mother sobbing.

His chest tightened with pain, every sob slicing through him like a knife.
