A cold, calculating smile spread across her face, one that others might miss if they weren't paying attention.

She had no qualms when it came to dealing with arrogant people.

The moment she saw Aurora being betrayed by the Elvador family, Lily understood one crucial thing.

You're better off relying on yourself than anyone else.

Only by growing stronger could you stand tall in this world, untouched and unbowed, never at the mercy of others.

Aurora, wait for me. Once I've saved up enough, I'll take you and Mom far away from Orley, to a place where no one can find us. We'll start fresh, a whole new life.

"She just left. She won't be back so soon. Let's hurry up and get this over with before that crazy woman comes back."

Lily's eyes narrowed dangerously, cold as a predator stalking its prey.

After the rumors about her being a rich man's mistress started spreading, her reputation at Orley University of Technology was destroyed.

If it weren't for Barnett stepping in, she'd have been expelled by now.

But Lily didn't feel the need to thank him.

She wasn't an idiot. From the moment the rumors started, it was clear Barnett was behind it, probably to pressure Aurora.

It was despicable.

Since then, the people who once pretended to be friendly with her had turned into wolves, using their family's status to bully her without a second thought.

She'd always kept to herself, too quiet and too soft for anyone to think she'd fight back.

But seeing Aurora go through all that made Lily realize just how dangerous it was to rely on others.

Since high school, she had become a porcupine, prickly, defensive, and unafraid to fight back when threatened.

Anyone who dared to push her would find out just how far she'd go to defend herself.

She leaned back against the cold wall, arms crossed, her expression unreadable.

After about ten minutes, she could hear the rustling of her roommates cleaning up.

She knew what they'd been doing. Her toothbrush had been used to scrub the toilet. Her towel was dirty from their feet. Toilet water had been poured into her shampoo and shower gel.

"Alright, let's clean up and head to the cafeteria," one of her roommates said, the sound of things being packed following.

Lily pushed the door open slowly, her lips curling into a twisted smile as she leaned against the doorframe. "Having fun, are we?"

As she walked in, Lily locked the door behind her with a soft click.

The sound made the three girls' blood run cold.

One of the girls, the first to recover from the shock, nervously stammered, "You... you came back so early? We were just cleaning up for you..."

Lily's smile deepened, but it was anything but comforting. It sent chills down their spines, like the snap of ice in the dead of winter.

"You really like my stuff that much? If you do, just say the word. I'll give them to you," she said, her voice soft, but with an unyielding pressure that made it clear there was no room for argument.

"N-No, we'll use our own stuff."

Lily's smile vanished in an instant, replaced by an icy expression. "I'm not asking for your opinion. I'm telling you to do so."

"Y-You... don't do this. We’re sorry, okay? Please!"

Lily's eyes flicked to the knife, and with a casual flick of her wrist, she grinned. "You’re sorry? You think an apology will make it all go away? If so, I’ll be damned."

"Remember what I said two months ago? You can bully me all you want, but I can take your lives. One life for three. How does that sound?"

The memory of that cold, ruthless threat hit them hard, and they began to sob in terror.

Under Lily's cold stare, they had no choice but to comply, like animals led to slaughter.

Step by step, they walked into the bathroom, using her toothbrush to brush their teeth, her shampoo to wash their hair, and her towel to wipe off the dirt.

Lily stood by the door, watching them the whole time with a smirk.

"This is how it should be," She huffed.

Sometimes, you couldn’t put an end to bullying simply by dodging it. You had to strike first.

Lily knew it by heart. And it looked like she had to hit the bar tonight and find that guy with the Maybach.

She needed money, and the easiest way to get it was from men like him.

And she felt no guilt about it.
