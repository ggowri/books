Celeste was well aware that her plea was pushing it, but she plowed ahead anyway.

"Come on! He's your own brother! Now that you're back on your feet, can you really have the heart to let him rot in jail over something that isn't even that big of a deal?"

"You're the youngest whiz-kid scientist in Peloland. I bet if you put in a good word with the court, they'd go easy on him. Just cut him some slack for the sake of family, please?"

Aurora suddenly roared, her eyes blazing with fury, "Enough already! Cut him some slack? Who gave a damn about me when I was going through hell?"

"Yeah, I know he's my brother. But what kind of brother teams up with others to steal my kidney? That's just messed up!"

"In my past life, he framed me, and I ended up in jail for five years. Before that, he gave me grief for three whole years in the Elvador family. Even after I got out, he just kept on putting me down."

"The fact that I haven't come after him for payback in this life should be seen as me being lenient. If he hadn't stirred the pot, I wouldn't even acknowledge he exists."

"But what did he do? He tried to take my kidney and leave me a wreck, and you still expect me to just forgive and forget?"

"Celeste, you've got to know how much I loathe the thought of being disabled. In my past life, your family reduced me to a shadow of who I used to be. Do you really think I can just roll over and take it again?"

Tears welled up in Celeste's eyes and streamed down her face as she shook her head vigorously. "No. That's not what I'm saying at all!"

Aurora let out a scornful scoff. "Barnett's not a little kid anymore. He's a grown-up, and he's gotta face the outcome for what he's done."

"But don't you fret. He's an accomplice, so he won't get the death penalty for this. He'll just do a few years behind bars. It's not the end of the world."

Celeste's eyes popped wide open in shock.

She knew that line all too well.

In her past life, Barnett had said the exact same thing to Aurora.

"What's five years in prison? You're just sitting in jail, but Camille's a vegetable."

Celeste couldn't bring herself to meet Aurora's gaze.

Aurora continued, "Since Barnett thinks prison's no biggie, let him have a taste of it for himself. I'm sure he'll see things differently once he's in there."

"Don't you worry. I'm not going to pull any strings to torture him. All he'll lose is his freedom. I lost a leg and a kidney in my last life. He's got it a whole lot better than I did."

Celeste just stood there, dumbfounded, her mind a complete blank.

Aurora shouldered past her and walked out of the room.

As she stepped out of the room, she bumped into a tall figure.

Aurora looked up in shock.

She never in a million years thought she'd run into Adolph at the hospital.

This was the guy who'd hurt her in her past life, but compared to the hell the Elvador family and Hubery had put her through, his actions were just a drop in the bucket.

Aurora was caught off guard and didn't know what to do at first.

Adolph, sharp as a tack in his sleek black suit, couldn't tear his eyes away from her the moment he saw her.

Most folks wouldn't give scientists a second look, but Adolph was a cut above the rest.

He'd heard about Aurora's reputation since his school days.

He'd always been proud of his top-notch grades, but next to her genius, he felt like a fish out of water.

Even though they were the same age, while Aurora was already hitting the books at college, he was still playing with toys in kindergarten.

In more ways than one, Aurora was his idol, and he was a die-hard fan.

Suddenly finding himself wrapped in his idol's embrace, Adolph was so stoked that his whole face turned as red as a beet.

Standing right beside him, Camille let out a gasp of surprise.

"Wow! Adolph, is this really your idol, Aurora? I just saw a news report about her yesterday, and now here she is! She's even more stunning in person than on TV!" Camille gazed at Aurora with shining eyes.

Aurora shook herself from her thoughts, her expression neutral, and her tone cool.

"Could you let go of me now?"

Adolph snapped back to reality, realizing he was still holding Aurora's slender waist, their bodies pressed together.

Her waist was so tiny and soft, and the scent of her shampoo mixed with body wash was intoxicating.

For a moment, he didn't want to let go.

Before Adolph could gather his wits, a firm hand suddenly pushed him aside, pulling Aurora into a strong embrace.

The voice that followed was cold and unyielding. "What were you planning to do with my fiancée?"

With Aurora gone, a sense of emptiness washed over Adolph, and his heart plummeted like a stone.

He turned to face the newcomer and froze.

It was Julian, the heir of the prestigious Hawke family in Putnam.

Adolph didn't like him at all.

He'd seen online that Aurora was engaged to this very prince of the Hawke family.

Adolph knew he could never measure up to Aurora; to him, she was like the brightest moon—untouchable and distant.

Julian may be impressive, but he's just a businessman. What gives him the right to pursue a genius like Aurora?

Besides, they were just childhood friends who haven't seen each other in eight years.

The whole 'fiancée' thing is child's play, just a game we played as kids.

With that in mind, Adolph eyed Aurora with a worried look. He was on high alert, ready to jump in and protect her if Julian even thought about giving her a hard time.

But then, as he stole a glance at her, he was floored to see her eyes light up like a Christmas tree at the sight of Julian.

Julian, now a sprightly twenty-two, still had that boyish charm that could make heads turn, but there was also a newfound calm and poise that came with maturity.

In her past life, Aurora had been through the wringer, and Julian had been her knight in shining armor, a ray of hope in the pitch-black darkness.

The little boy she once knew had finally come into his own.

And yet, he recognized her right off the bat.

The coldness in Julian's eyes melted away, replaced by a warm glow as he made his way towards Aurora.

"Aurora, you're finally back," he said, his voice soft as silk.

He reached out, his fingers grazing her face ever so gently, tracing the contours of her young features.

"I've missed you like crazy all these years."

Aurora gave him a tender smile. "Me, too. I've missed you loads."

"Then..." Julian's eyes were swimming with emotion. "Let's head back to Putnam and get engaged."

Just as Aurora was about to give the okay, a tall figure came charging toward her.

He swooped her up in a bear hug.

"Aurora? Is it really you? My sweet little sis is finally back! I've been missing you like crazy!" William was blubbering, completely overcome with feelings.

The once chubby and adorable little sister had blossomed into a young woman.

In no time, Laura came dashing over too. "My daughter! My precious angel!" She squeezed Aurora tight.

Even Benjamin couldn't keep his emotions in check. He opened his arms wide, pulling Laura, William, and Aurora into a big family embrace.

"Aurora, after you left the research institute, you came to Orley and got yourself kidnapped! Do you have any idea how worried sick I was? What if something bad had happened to you?"

The air was filled with a mix of laughter and tears as the family hugged it out, over the moon with happiness.

Celeste stepped out of the ward, watching this heart-warming reunion unfold. In an instant, she felt like a fifth wheel.

Her own daughter had pushed her to the sidelines.
