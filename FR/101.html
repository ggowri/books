Beatrice was shaking with anger after hearing Anderson's words. She raised her cane and struck him hard.

"Shut up! If it weren't for you marrying that good-for-nothing, none of this would have happened today! Look what Judy did to Aurora, and you're still trying to play peacemaker here!"

Anderson, humiliated by his mother's public blow, gritted his teeth but said nothing.

He marched toward the door of the banquet hall, trying to leave.

But the door was locked from the other side and wouldn't budge.

"Ethan, open the door!" Anderson's voice carried a commanding tone.

Ethan smiled, but his words were firm. "Mr. Anderson Hawke, if you don't want to make Mr. Hawke angry, please stay put."

Fury surged in Anderson's chest, and he clenched his fists. "You dare defy me? Do you want me to fire you?"

Ethan continued smiling, unaffected. "I believe you can, Mr. Anderson Hawke, but you might as well wait here."

Anderson's veins popped in anger, but he had no choice but to stay.

The guests in the hall watched in stunned silence. Ethan's disregard for even the chairman's orders left them breathless in fear.

The atmosphere was thick with tension, almost suffocating.

Judy collapsed on the floor, her eyes vacant.

Meanwhile, her accomplices were all bruised and battered by their husbands. None of them dare to complain about it.

They only hoped that when Julian returned and saw them that miserable, he might show them mercy.

*****

Julian walked down the long hotel corridor, cradling Aurora in his arms.

Aurora leaned against his broad chest, listening to his steady heartbeat. The rhythmic thump calmed her like never before.

As they rounded the corner, an unwelcome voice cut through the air. "Mr. Hawke, where are you going?"

Adolph stood not far away, wearing a flattering smile.

Normally proud, Adolph had to lower himself in front of Julian.

Beside him, Bertha stared at Julian, her gaze frozen.

Julian's eyes scanned the two, then stopped at the torn embroidery in Bertha's hands. His eyes narrowed.

He could sense Aurora's tension.

Though he didn't know the full story, the fragments he had pieced together made it clear that Adolph and Bertha were undoubtedly involved in whatever had happened to her.

He stopped and, for once, his expression remained relatively calm. "Mr. Sherwood, shouldn't you be heading to the banquet hall?"

Adolph, taken aback by the sudden attention, nodded eagerly. "We'll go right away. Mr. Hawke, you're..."

He tried to get a better look at the person Julian was holding, but Aurora was so tightly wrapped in Julian's arms that Adolph couldn't see her face.

However, the delicate feet exposed under the fabric told him it was a woman.

Bertha shot a hostile look at Aurora, but her expression quickly returned to one of innocent curiosity, her big, clear eyes blinking at Julian.

Unfortunately, Julian didn't spare her a glance.

Without answering Adolph's question, Julian simply said, "You two go ahead. I'll be there soon."

Adolph and Bertha didn't think twice. After briefly bidding Julian farewell, they hurried toward the banquet hall.

Julian watched them leave, a slight smile tugging at his lips. He spoke into his collar mic, "Ethan, let those two in."

He planned to lock up everyone who had hurt Aurora and take them down one by one.

"Don't worry," he murmured, offering her reassurance.

Aurora's stiff body slowly relaxed in his arms.

Once back in their private room, Aurora went straight into the bathroom while Julian had his men buy clothes and medical supplies for her.

He also ordered them to retrieve the hotel's security footage.

His men worked quickly, and soon they had everything ready.

Julian sat down, watching the footage unfold, his brows furrowed in deepening anger.

He saw Bertha provoking Aurora, tearing at her hair.

In the footage, Adolph sided with Bertha without question, even dragging Aurora into a room. He saw Bertha destroy the embroidery.

Not only that, he watched as Judy and others beat Aurora.

Adolph saw it all.

The more Julian watched the clip, the angrier he became.

His fingers tightened around his phone, his knuckles whitening when he saw the blood on Adolph's lip.

At that moment, the bathroom door opened.

Julian set his phone down and looked up. Aurora walked out of the bathroom in a white bathrobe.

Her wet hair cascaded down her shoulders, and her eyes, bright as ever, seemed even clearer. Beads of water still clung to her long lashes, glittering as they fell.

The soft bathrobe clung to her, revealing her slender neck and collarbones in smooth, graceful lines.

Her lips were slightly pink, exuding both allure and a trace of vulnerability.

But the sight of her lips reminded him of what Adolph had done, and it angered him.

His brow furrowed, though only for a moment. He quickly masked any emotion.

"Come here."

His voice was softer, lower than usual.

Aurora hesitated, feeling nervous. She didn't dare refuse, so she quietly walked over and sat on the bed.

Julian dipped a cotton swab in ointment and was about to apply it when he caught a whiff of her fragrance.

For a moment, his hand froze, hovering just above the swollen bone of her cheek.

The steam from the bathroom still clung to her lashes, sparkling like tiny crystals, reflecting the light as she trembled slightly.

Aurora looked up, surprised. "Mr. Hawke, what's wrong?"

"It's going to hurt," he said, his voice hoarse.

His Adam's apple bobbed "Hang in there."

He leaned in slightly, his face close to hers.

The cool ointment made contact with her skin, soothing the pain.

His serious expression was mere inches from her face. Aurora could see every detail of him.

Her heart raced, her breath catching in her throat. She nervously clutched the edge of her robe, staring at his strong nose, too afraid to move.

The faint scent of his shampoo, mixed with tobacco, hovered near her nose. His warm breath brushed against her neck. His temperature made her face flush involuntarily.

It seemed Julian noticed her discomfort. He raised an eyebrow and leaned in closer, his breath now brushing over her neck more deliberately.

The tickling sensation made her shiver involuntarily.

Julian, noticing her discomfort, lifted his eyes to meet hers. "Does it hurt?"

"No." Aurora quickly averted her gaze, feeling like she might suffocate.

"I'll be gentler. Bear with me. It'll be over soon."

Aurora waited quietly.

After the ointment was applied, Aurora thought it was over.

But Julian surprised her by starting to dry her hair himself.

At the age of twenty-three, Aurora had never experienced such thorough attention and care.

She felt that both Julian and Beatrice had been so kind to her. If she ever had the chance, she promised herself she would repay them.

Before she knew it, she was already lying in bed.

Turned out that Julian had just gently pressed her down.

Aurora's eyes widened in shock. "Mr. Hawke... this isn't right, is it? I... I'm not ready..."

Julian tucked her in, his voice cool but reassuring. "Ready for what?"

Aurora froze, her face turning crimson.

She quickly turned her head away. "No... Nothing."

Julian chuckled softly, "It's still early. We'll go to the party after you get some rest."

Aurora wasn't sure what Julian intended, but given his demeanor in the banquet hall, it was clear he wouldn't let things go easily.

She said gratefully, "Thank you, Mr. Hawke."

Julian placed his large hand over her eyes. "Sleep."

With his calming words, Aurora slowly drifted into a peaceful sleep.

Julian quietly watched her, a faint smile on his face, but it quickly disappeared as he looked at the clock.

***

Time passed slowly, and the guests in the banquet hall endured a long, tense three hours of fear and anxiety.

The atmosphere inside the hall felt as if a storm were about to break.

Everyone was terrified, speculating on how Julian would handle them.

As time passed, their fear slowly turned into despair.

They started to argue, accusations flying, as the whole room descended into chaos.

Finally, the doors to the banquet hall creaked open.

Julian and Aurora entered together.

Aurora wore a soft blue dress, her hair flowing gracefully, and her face had recovered somewhat from the swelling. She looked refreshing.

Julian's gaze swept over the crowd like a blade, finally landing on Bertha.

Thinking of how she had grabbed Aurora's hair, his voice turned cold. "Ms. Bertha Elvador, your hair is a mess."

Bertha froze, thinking for a moment that Julian might be concerned for her. A small smile crept across her face as her cheeks flushed.

However, Julian's next words shattered her smile, freezing her in place.

"Someone, shave her head."

His voice was soft, but in the silent hall, it rang out with chilling clarity.

Every woman in the room gasped in horror.

It took years to grow long, beautiful hair, and even longer to maintain it.

Though shaving didn't hurt, the psychological impact was profound.

"No! Please, no!" Bertha screamed in terror.

Several bodyguards quickly stepped forward, seizing her arms and dragging her from the crowd.
