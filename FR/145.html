Aurora knew Adolph's stupidity all too well.

The truth was, fools were often more detestable than outright villains.

She had tried to explain, but he'd turned a deaf ear.

Even with Camille's testimony, he'd clung to his doubts.

The way he carried himself, so convinced that he's the only one seeing the truth while everyone else was blind, was downright ridiculous.

Aurora couldn't help but laugh out loud, her smile laced with mockery.

"Since you're so sure hospitals wouldn't help them," she said coolly, "let me show you exactly how they did."

As Aurora spoke, she pulled open the drawer of the table and took out a document.

She slowly stood up, dragging her injured leg, and walked toward Adolph.

With each step she took, Adolph's heart began to beat wildly.

Aurora's gaze was so determined, her expression so calm, that a sick dread pooled in Adolph's gut.

Even though he was still stubbornly denying it with words, deep down, he couldn't help but believe.

Stopping before him, Aurora met his eyes unflinchingly and extended the document.

"Read. Then you'll understand."

Adolph instinctively avoided Aurora's gaze.

To cover up his inner panic, he forced himself to remain calm and snorted, "Fine. Let's see what trick you're playing this time."

With that, he quickly snatched the document from Aurora's hand, flipping through it page by page.

As he read, his hands began to tremble uncontrollably, his breathing grew heavier.

It turned out that the head of Orley Central Hospital was Joshua's mistress and also Bertha's biological mother.

No wonder Bertha and Joshua were so sure the hospital would cooperate with them.

Bertha was standing right beside Adolph, and as he flipped through the document, she saw everything clearly.

In an instant, her pupils dilated, and terror filled her heart.

How... How had they uncovered this?

Real fear gripped her for the first time.

Julian's reach was terrifying—if he could unearth decades-old secrets, what else did he know?

One thought consumed her—run, now!

She then spun around abruptly and bolted, ready to flee.

But before she could take a step, a powerful hand clamped down on her shoulder with an iron grip, so strong it felt as if her shoulder blade might shatter under the pressure.

Adolph's voice came through clenched teeth, "Bertha, you still haven't explained yourself. Where are you going?"

Bertha winced in pain, tears immediately flooding her eyes.

"Adolph, you're hurting me!"

She shouldn't have said that.

The moment the words left her mouth, all the emotions Adolph had been suppressing for so long burst forth like a dam breaking.

He snapped, "Hurt? Now you know what pain feels like?

"Did you think about that when you pushed Camille down the stairs?

"When you sent Aurora to jail and bribed the inmates to torture her, did you ever think Aurora would hurt?"

Adolph's voice grew harsher, his fury boiling over.

"You, Joshua, and her!" He pointed at Isabella, who was standing nearby.

"When you all conspired to steal Aurora's organs, did you ever think about how much pain she would be in?"

Adolph was furious.

He slammed the document into Bertha's face, yelling, "Bitch, you've been lying to me all this time!"

Tears streamed uncontrollably from Adolph's eyes.

He couldn't even tell whether they were for his own stupidity or for the regret of hurting Aurora after being deceived by Bertha.

He raised his hand and slapped Bertha across the face.

The slap was so forceful that Bertha fell to the ground.

"Bertha!"

At almost the same moment, Isabella, Frank, and Joshua all exclaimed in unison.

They rushed to Bertha's side.

Adolph no longer cared about them.

He turned around and looked at Aurora with deep regret in his eyes.

He wanted to say something—anything—but how could he even begin to make up for the damage he'd done?

Just moments ago, he had still been blindly defending Bertha.

But it wasn't my fault.

It's all Bertha's fault!

I've been deceived—I'm the victim too.

Adolph raised his hand, wanting to hold Aurora tightly, to let her truly feel his remorse.

Aurora frowned.

She didn't want Adolph touching her.

She felt nothing but disgust for him.

Aurora tried to move away, but her legs were in too much pain, and she couldn't escape fast enough.

Just as Adolph was about to pull her into his arms, a strong steady hand reached out and wrapped around her waist.

With one swift pull, Aurora was placed in the embrace of someone who smelled of cedarwood.

The next second, Julian mercilessly kicked Adolph, sending him flying.

"Do you really think you have the right to touch her?" Julian's voice was low and icy.

Adolph lay on the ground, slowly lifting his head.

He looked at Aurora in pain, pleading, "Aurora, I didn't mean to. Please forgive me."

Aurora remained nestled in Julian's arms, her face buried against his chest, surrounded by the comforting blend of cedarwood and faint tobacco.

She was so lost in it that she barely even registered Adolph's desperate words.

Her silence plunged Adolph into absolute despair.

Aurora would never forgive him.

He could never get the woman he loved back.

Watching Aurora and Julian in such a close embrace, Adolph felt a sharp pain in his chest.

He struggled to get up from the ground and screamed in a hoarse voice, "Let go of Aurora!"

Julian didn't even glance at him, simply holding Aurora as they walked away.

Adolph's vision went red as he raised his fist, rushing forward, "I said, let go of Aurora!"

At that moment, Wilson couldn't take it anymore.

He stepped forward and slapped Adolph across the face, shouting, "You brat, how long are you going to make a scene? Don't you feel ashamed?"

Lizeth also looked at him with deep disappointment.

"Adolph, you've really let us down."

Camille sneered, "You're such an embarrassment. I wish we weren't even related!"

Adolph shook his head frantically, scrambling to defend himself.

"No, no, it's not like that! It's not my fault! Bertha used me!"

Aurora would never forgive him, and that alone had already broken him.

Why couldn't his parents and sister understand him?

Adolph's mind was buzzing, his eyes bloodshot.

He was seething, yet he couldn't lash out at Aurora or his family, so all that fury was aimed at Bertha, Joshua, and the others.

"It's all your fault!"

Adolph punched Joshua, knocking him to the ground.

He then started beating Bertha, yelling, "Bitch, go to hell!"

Bertha screamed and cried, writhing in pain.

Frank, seeing Bertha being beaten, was consumed by fury and rushed forward, grappling with Adolph.

But Adolph, strong and fueled by rage, overpowered Frank, nearly slamming him to the ground in a brutal assault.

Isabella, seeing this, was heartbroken.

She tried to help Frank, but Wilson kicked her away.

Whatever, Adolph was still his son, and as a father, he couldn't just watch him being beaten.

The scene completely spiraled out of control, turning into chaos.

Isabella cried as she looked at Joshua, "Joshua, how can you still focus on those useless papers? Get up and help Frank!"

But Joshua's mind had gone blank.

When Adolph had knocked him to the ground earlier, he'd caught a glimpse of the documents scattered around him.

Now, holding those papers in his trembling hands, Joshua felt like the ground had just collapsed beneath him.

The truth was laid out in black and white—Bertha wasn't his and Isabella's daughter, and the son overseas wasn't his biological son.

Both of them were actually Isabella and Frank's.

But that wasn't even the worst part.

What shocked him even more was that Frank wasn't Isabella's brother, but her husband.

They even had a marriage certificate.

Suddenly, he remembered what Darren had said outside the Hawke Estate.

It wasn't Celeste who had cheated on him—it was Isabella.

Joshua, with red eyes and shaking hands, stepped in front of Isabella, his voice trembling as he asked, "Isabella, give me an explanation!"
