Aurora's indifference made Hubery feel like he was punching a pillow—his anger just vanished against her calmness, leaving him frustrated.

Seeing Hubery stunned, Bertha's eyes instantly welled up with tears. "Hubery, are you okay?" she asked, her voice quivering with faux concern.

He's been so concerned that he even came to the hospital with me. Is this how you treat people who care about you?"

Her words were laced with pity, her voice breaking just enough to sound convincingly upset. She looked every bit the innocent victim, while Aurora, in her eyes, was the unreasonable villain.

Aurora couldn't hide her disgust for Bertha's fake sympathy. The theatrics made her stomach churn.

She knew exactly what Bertha was up to—using Hubery as a pawn to take aim at her.

Once, she would have been devastated by Hubery's misunderstanding. Her heart would have shattered.

But not anymore. Hubery meant nothing to her now.

With a casual flick of her wrist, Aurora raised her hand as though she might strike again.

Bertha shrank back, her face draining of color as she hurried behind Hubery, peeking out from behind him like a frightened rabbit.

"Aurora, you're going too far," she muttered, her voice shaky, though her eyes gleamed with a hint of triumph.

Hubery was left dazed, his eyes wide with disbelief as he stared at her, struggling to process what had just happened. "Aurora Elvador!"

The trust she had once had in him, that bond they had shared, was now replaced with pure disgust.

"Aurora, you... you hit me..." His voice cracked, heavy with unshed tears.

He was her Hubery, wasn't he? How could he now be nothing in her life?

Did he really think she shouldn't strike back?

Before she died, Bertha was going to pay.

Aurora's heart skipped a beat, her pulse quickening in panic.

It was Adolph.
