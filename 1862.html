Osborne took a deep breath, watching Seth struggle to sit up in his excitement. "Uncle Seth! Hold on a second! She's not my mother, Vivienne. She's just disguised as her!

"I brought her out to show you guys to confirm if your Vivienne is the same as my mother. And it looks like she is!"

With that, he shot Anastasia a glance.

She quickly reverted to her original appearance.

Seth and Tristan stared at her, momentarily lost in disappointment at the revelation.

But soon, their excitement reignited.

Seth locked his gaze on Osborne, his voice brimming with enthusiasm. "So, you really are my little sister's child? My nephew? How old are you?"

Osborne felt a bit awkward. "If we're going by the time on my home planet, I'm nearly fifty!"

Seth's expression froze.

He thought, "Fifty years old? By the time Vivienne left the Hudson family, she hadn't even turned fifty. How could Osborne possibly be her child?

Realizing the confusion, Osborne quickly explained the situation on Blue Planet.

"Blue Planet! My sister left Celestara for a Dimensional Microcosm!"

To Seth, Blue Planet was just that—a Dimensional Microcosm.

He continued, "No wonder we've been searching high and low for her without a trace! Turns out she wasn't even in Celestara..."

Seth began to piece it together.

Osborne shook his head, frowning. "No! My mother returned to Celestara over twenty years ago! The reason you couldn't find her isn't that she was on Blue Planet!"

Concern washed over Osborne as he spoke.

Although he had discovered his mother's family, the absence of news about her was troubling.

He mused, "She has family here, a renowned family within the Human Community! Why hasn't she returned to the Hudson family? Why is there no word from her at all?

Where could she be?

Has something happened to her?"

Osborne's worry was palpable, and Seth's face turned pale as he absorbed the implications.

Vivienne had only spent a few years on Blue Planet.

Shortly after giving birth to Osborne, she and Theodore had fled back to Celestara.

In terms of Celestara's timeline, she hadn't been gone long at all.

Yet, she had been missing for over twenty years!

Even with the Hudson family's relentless efforts, they had found no trace of her.

It was a grim realization.

Seth clenched his jaw. "So, you're Osborne, right?

"No matter what, you are indeed my sister's child! You coming back to the Hudson family is a monumental deal for us! A great blessing!

"You're my sister's child, which makes you my child too!

"From now on, this is your home!"

Tristan frowned, hesitating. "Dad, Grandpa just went to the Sanctum of Light and hasn't returned yet."

"Oh?!" Seth's brow furrowed, and he gritted his teeth. "Then inform all your uncles and aunts! Get them all here! My sister's child is back, and they'll be thrilled!"

Tristan nodded but hesitated, glancing at Seth's frail form, concern etched on his face.

"Dad, your health..." Tristan began, concern etched on his face.

"Don't worry about me! Just get everyone here. That's what matters most!" Seth insisted, his voice firm despite his frailty.

"Alright! I'm on it!" Tristan bit his lip, determination setting in as he dashed out of the room.

Osborne watched, puzzled.

"Can't he just use the terminal wristband to reach out? Why does he have to run around to contact everyone?"

But before he could dwell on it, a loud, enthusiastic voice rang out from outside the villa.

It was Tristan, calling out with urgency that echoed across Hudson Valley.

"Everyone! I need you all here!

"It's super important! Really, really important! Hurry up!"

His voice reverberated through the valley, drawing attention from every corner.

Almost instantly, powerful figures emerged from various villas, converging around Tristan, their expressions a mix of confusion and concern.

A middle-aged man, his brow furrowed with worry, asked, "Tristan, what's going on? Is it Seth? Is he... is he alright?

"It's all my fault! As the older brother, I failed!

"I couldn't save Seth! And now he's had to use the Miracle Elixir on my son! It's all on me!"

Frustration and guilt washed over the man as he slapped his own face, anguish evident in his eyes.
