Feeling the surge of his newly upgraded Devil's Light, Osborne couldn't help but grin.

The next step was to integrate this power into his martial art aura, which would significantly enhance his defenses.

After all, as his strength grew, so too did the caliber of his opponents.

Lately, he had faced a string of formidable foes and had come to recognize a glaring weakness in his arsenal—his defensive capabilities.

Osborne's previous martial art aura, infused with Level Eight Devil's Light, was already quite powerful. Most opponents at the same level would struggle to breach it unless they wielded a potent divine artifact.

But the unfortunate truth was that Osborne's opponents were rarely of the same caliber.

Only Class S warriors could truly be considered his equals.

In those encounters, his martial art aura often fell short, leaving him vulnerable and frequently injured.

Even though he had the Light of Redemption to heal him, the sting of injury was never pleasant.

Plus, the Light of Redemption wasn't something he could use on a whim; it had a cooldown of half an hour.

More often than not, Osborne found himself battered and bruised long before he could call upon its restorative powers.

Thus, boosting his defensive strength had become a priority.

If Osborne could improve his defenses, he could engage Class S opponents more comfortably.

He had considered seeking a defensive divine artifact, similar to Emma's Swallow Shield, but ultimately decided against it. Such artifacts were rare and hard to come by, and relying on external forces could be a double-edged sword.

So, enhancing the strength of his martial art aura became the most viable option.

Despite already being far stronger than others at his level, Osborne felt there was still room for improvement.

Now, with the Level Nine Devil's Light at his disposal, he was ready to take that leap.

"Let's find out just how strong it can get," Osborne thought, focusing intently as he began to merge the Level Nine Devil's Light with his martial art aura.

The moment the dark, shimmering energy made contact with his aura, it reacted violently, like ice meeting boiling water.

Osborne's eyes widened in surprise.

"This is new... I've never experienced anything like this before. What's going on?"

Furrowing his brow, he pressed on, determined to maintain control as he merged the energies.

The air around him hummed with energy. Most practitioners found it challenging to blend martial arts with elemental powers, as they were two distinct paths of cultivation.

But Osborne had an advantage.

When he broke through to Class A, he had fused the soul of powers with his body, allowing him to combine his martial art aura with Devil's Light.

But this time, with the Devil's Light reaching Level Nine, something unprecedented was occurring.

He wasn't sure if this change was a blessing or a curse.

With a firm grip on the Level Nine Devil's Light, Osborne forced it deeper into his martial art aura. Instantly, the once vibrant red aura was tinged with a shadowy black, creating a mesmerizing swirl of colors.

Yet, the fusion was far from complete.

The red and black energies began to pulse and swirl violently, like a tempest brewing in the sky, spinning and twisting beyond his control.

"Complete the fusion! Transform!"

A sudden spark ignited within Osborne's mind.

"My Level Nine Devil's Light is undergoing the most profound fusion with my martial art aura.

Previously, while my Devil's Light could merge with my martial art aura, it was superficial, more of a mixing than a true integration.

They complemented each other, becoming stronger, but only in a 1+1=2 manner.

Now, they are fusing completely!"

Level Nine Devil's Light seemed to reach a zenith, merging with an equally potent martial art aura, catalyzing a miraculous metamorphosis.

This integration was inherently greater than 1+1.

Moreover, they were evolving!

A hum reverberated through the air as ten minutes passed.

The profound fusion and transformation finally concluded, enveloping Osborne in a crimson glow.

This signified his new martial art aura.

Feeling the intensity of his aura, Osborne couldn't help but be exhilarated. Yet, he retrieved the Goddess of Grain's scythe and tested his aura.

The scythe penetrated his aura, but Osborne felt an intense resistance akin to slicing through tough hide.

With the Goddess of Grain's scythe, Osborne could effortlessly breach the defenses of Class S powerhouses as easily as slicing paper.

This meant his defense now far surpassed ordinary Class S powerhouses!

Osborne's excitement surged.

"Is this the ultimate state of martial art aura? Its defensive capability has at least increased tenfold compared to before! Ordinary Class S powerhouses wielding divine artifacts won't easily penetrate my aura again!"
