"Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity—it's mine now!"

Osborne couldn't shake the feeling that luck was on his side. This Cosmic Treasure was leagues beyond any ordinary divine artifact.

Divine artifacts were categorized into tiers: Ordinary, Premium, Top-Grade, and the rarest of all—Ultimate.

Top-grade divine artifacts were already quite scarce, each with a significant legacy. In the Age of Gods, even the average deity wouldn't have the privilege to wield one.

As for Ultimate divine artifacts?

Only the most powerful deities could claim them.

But above Ultimate artifacts lay something even more formidable: Cosmic Treasures.

While divine artifacts were crafted by deities, Cosmic Treasures were born from the very fabric of the universe, possessing unimaginable power.

Even a Deity King might not be worthy of such a treasure.

Just like the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity before him!

This wasn't just any plant; it was said that its seeds existed long before the Big Bang.

After the universe erupted into existence, it sprouted and grew for billions of years, eventually becoming what it was now—eternally preserved in its current form.

Even after countless eons, when the universe itself might crumble, it would remain unchanged.

Its most astounding trait? Immortality.

No force could destroy it, and no power could obliterate it.

If Osborne could somehow transplant it into his own body, he could achieve the same immortality.

"It's mine now!"

Osborne took a deep breath, forcing himself to calm down. He knew that transplanting the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity into his body wouldn't be a walk in the park.

He didn't quite understand how he knew all this, but a nagging feeling told him that the process would be perilous.

Thankfully, Osborne had the Blood Cross on his side.

The Blood Cross was a derivative of the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity, and after being fused with his body for so long, it had left an imprint of its essence within him.

In fact, the incredible recovery abilities Osborne possessed were a direct result of that essence.

So, while the transplant would be dangerous, he had a fighting chance.

If anyone else were to attempt it, they'd likely meet a grim fate—exploding from the sheer force of the Lotus' power!

That power was terrifying, even more so than that wielded by a Deity King.

Although it wouldn't just explode without warning, if even a fraction of that energy escaped during the transplant, it could obliterate a planet.

If his body wasn't a good fit, the Lotus would resist, and the slightest slip could mean disaster for Osborne.

Even a Deity King wouldn't dare force a Cosmic Treasure into his own body.

To do so would spell the demise.

"Still, I think I can pull this off! The Blood Cross is already integrated with me; my body should resonate well with the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity. It shouldn't fight back!"

Osborne's breath quickened as determination surged within him.

This was a Cosmic Treasure, and now that he had the chance to claim it, how could he not at least try?

Even if it meant risking his life, he had to go for it!

But Osborne wasn't naive; he prepared for the worst. He quickly scribbled a will, just in case he didn't make it out alive. He didn't want anyone rushing in to save him and putting themselves in danger if things went south.

The mental strength disturbance was undeniably a manifestation of the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity's power.

Even the gods themselves couldn't withstand its proximity.

Any attempt to rescue Osborne would only end in their death—a grim certainty.

With this in mind, Osborne penned his will, setting it to auto-send to all his loved ones should he not cancel it within two days.

Then, with resolute determination, he set forth.

"Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity! The gift of immortality! Here I come!"

Osborne trod cautiously toward the Lotus, each step a tentative probe, an exploration of whether the Lotus would repel him.

If it did, he'd turn tail without a second thought.

For all its allure, the Lotus couldn't outweigh his life.

If it shunned him, transplanting it would be a death sentence—an absolute dead end, which Osborne, a pragmatist, refused to pursue.

His audacity to try stemmed from a high likelihood of success!

His gamble had paid off. As anticipated, the Lotus remained indifferent to his approach. He reached its side, and still, it did nothing to resist him.

Another might have been repelled instantly, but not Osborne.

"I can do this," he said, buoyed by newfound confidence.

Osborne inhaled deeply, preparing for the critical transplant.

But then, a chill ran down his spine.

A voice, icy and forbidding, rang in his mind. "You think you can transplant the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity? You're asking for death!"
