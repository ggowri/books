Chapter 226 The Breakout Studio

What was the most explosive headline of the Christmas holiday season?

Without a doubt, it was the stunning breakout of If There's a Next Second, the debut film from a newly founded studio called Feather Films.

By the fourth day of the new year, Before the Music Fades had already lost momentum. Its early dominance faded fast as word of mouth turned sour, and for the first time since its release, its daily box office dropped below 100 million.

In contrast, Yves' film If There's a Next Second was soaring. Rave reviews poured in from every direction, and moviegoers began spreading the word on their own. More and more people were heading to the theaters out of sheer curiosity.

Some even came back for second and third viewings. The film had officially gone viral.

And it wasn't the only Feather Films production taking off. The Adventures of Elle the Little Fairy, a completely different genre with a very different audience, was also climbing the charts.

It didn't take long for someone to point out that both films, despite being so different, were produced by Feather Films.

And both were written by Alistair—or, in the case of The Adventures of Elle the Little Fairy, co-written. The other credited writer? Eloise.

Stellar Films could no longer stay quiet.

Before the Music Fades had been a major investment. Unless it hit the 1 billion mark, it wasn't even going to break even. And now, with total earnings not even reaching 500 million and theaters drastically reducing its screenings, even crossing the 1 billion line was starting to look like a fantasy. It had once been projected to bring in five times that.

Adding to the sting was the fact that If There's a Next Second had originally been one of their own projects. But when Alistair became the target of a smear campaign, Stellar Films had cut ties and publicly canceled production. In response, Alistair had funded the project himself. Director Yves stood by him. Thanks to a powerhouse crew and passionate performances from the cast, they delivered a film that dazzled audiences.

And now, Stellar Films had become the industry's biggest cautionary tale.

Giselle, humiliated, attempted to fight back by hiring bots to smear If There's a Next Second.

But before her plan even gained traction, an old video surfaced online—one showing her bullying a fellow student back in school.

The glamorous, picture-perfect Giselle was suddenly public enemy number one.

She sat on the floor in shock, watching servants frantically pack her things.

She had never imagined her life would spiral like this.

When Paige went down, Giselle had spent weeks panicking, convinced that Caroline had something to do with it.

Caroline, after all, was a vengeful queen.

Giselle had been the one who hired people to spread malicious rumors about Alistair. And if Caroline ever found out, there was no way she'd let it slide.

But nothing happened. Time passed, and eventually, Giselle let her guard down.

She wasn't just anyone—she was Giselle Reeves. So what if Caroline was Gordon's daughter? She was untouchable, and Caroline knew it.

Or maybe, just maybe, Caroline never even realized she had been behind it all.

Now she understood. It wasn't that Caroline hadn't known. And it definitely wasn't because she was afraid. The time simply hadn't been right.

Now, the scandal was too big to ignore. Giselle had become a national disgrace. Her own family, desperate to protect their name, made the painful decision to cut her off and send her overseas.

The once-flawless Giselle, who used to travel with an entourage, now walked into the airport all alone.

In the departure lounge, a television screen was playing an interview on one of America's most prominent entertainment channels. Director Yves and several cast members from If There's a Next Second were being featured.

Yves was recounting the many setbacks during filming. He said that following his gut and sticking with the project had been the best decision of his life.

Giselle silently crushed the soda can in her hand.

Heaven and hell really could be just one step apart.

She had all the bitterness in the world, but no way to fight back.

Now, as the family's disgrace, even returning home would be nothing but a dream.

The runaway success of If There's a Next Second lifted everyone involved in the film.

Even minor supporting actors saw their value skyrocket.

As for Janice, who played a major role, her career was suddenly on fire.

Feather Films moved quickly, signing several cast members, including Janice.

Meanwhile, the actors who had originally been cast but later dropped out could only wallow in regret. But there are no do-overs in life. The wrong choices often don't come with second chances.

Feather Films, barely out of the gate, had managed to deliver two major hits in a single season, instantly establishing itself as a rising force in the industry.

Like a storm breaking into a clear sky, the company swept through the industry with a force no one could ignore.

Alistair's name was no longer followed by terms like loser or gold digger. Bit by bit, it began to carry weight.

"Bye, Mommy! I'm off to preschool!"

"Bye, sweetheart. I'll drive Eloise today."

"Drive safe."

Just as Eloise ran to open the door, Alistair spun around and stole a quick kiss from Caroline, cupping her face in his hands.

Caroline laughed and gave him a playful nudge. "Go on already."

After dropping Eloise off, Alistair returned home and stepped into the conservatory, a warm, light-filled space where vines curled up the walls and blossoms soaked in the morning sun.

The moment he stepped inside, someone pressed up against him.

He turned, surprised.
Caroline?

At this time of day, she was usually already at the office.

She hadn't said anything about taking the morning off.

Caroline leaned her cheek against his back. Her voice was flat, unreadable. "I'm two weeks late this month."

It took a moment for that to register. "What's two weeks late?"

She didn't answer.

And then it hit him like a freight train.
She means... her period?
"You mean your period is late?" he asked, voice low.

She gave a quiet hum of confirmation.

They had always taken precautions, but nothing was foolproof.

Alistair slowly turned to face her, eyes dropping to her stomach.

She was wearing a loose home outfit. Her frame was slim as always, her sweatshirt hanging loosely over her flat belly.

He instinctively reached out but caught himself, for his hands were still cold from outside. He quickly pulled back.

Caroline grabbed his hand and gently pressed it against her abdomen through the fabric.

Alistair stood there, silent, letting his palm rest against her stomach.

There might be a little life growing in there. Our child.

His heart began to race. "We're going to the hospital. Now," he said quickly.

Caroline hesitated. "What if it's just a delay?"

She had seen how much he wanted a child. The last thing she wanted was for him to get his hopes up for nothing.

"If it's just a delay, then that still means something's off. We'll check, and if you need it, we'll get you back in balance."

He led her into the bedroom to change, handling her as if she were already nine months pregnant. She couldn't decide whether to roll her eyes or kiss him.

This is just a maybe. What'll he be like if it's confirmed?

Alistair pulled out a thick, long down coat from the wardrobe. Before they stepped out, he made sure she had on her hat, wrapped a scarf around her neck, and slipped gloves onto her hands. Only then did he take her hand in his and lead her out the door.
