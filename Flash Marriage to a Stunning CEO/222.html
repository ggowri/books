Chapter 222 Nothing Left Unsaid

"Honey, where's Eloise? With Grandma?"

"I was building a snowman with her. Then Leo came by, so I asked him to help look after her. He's been staying at Ashbourne Manor lately, so I'm guessing he took her back there."

Caroline paused for a long moment before speaking softly, "My father got involved in the matter. As long as the result is what I wanted, I don't care who takes over. I handed it off to him."

What she wanted was for Paige to be locked up in that psychiatric hospital for the rest of her life.

Alistair let out a quiet sigh. "Don't do that again. I was really worried."

Caroline responded with a soft, "Okay."

The streetlights along both sides of the road began to glow one by one, winding ahead like a necklace strung with dazzling pearls by a giant hand.

Alistair gave Leo a call.

He was right. Eloise had indeed been taken to Mabel by Leo.

Caroline turned and wrapped her arms around his arm, resting her chin on his shoulder. "Let her stay at Ashbourne Manor tonight. We can bring her home tomorrow evening."

It was Saturday tomorrow. Eloise didn't have school, and Caroline was clearly giving her and Leo a chance to bond.

Alistair couldn't help but feel moved. His outwardly cold but inwardly tender wife might appear indifferent toward Leo, but deep down, she did care about her cousin.

The truth was, there had never been any real animosity between Caroline and Leo. Their strained relationship had always been Paige's doing. Now that she was no longer there to sow discord, the tension between them seemed to melt away like a spring breeze sweeping across a frozen lake. The change was visible.

"I think that's a good idea," Alistair said with a nod.

The roads were unexpectedly clear, and they arrived thirty minutes earlier than Caroline had expected. It was only ten thirty when they got home.

As soon as they stepped inside, Caroline instinctively leaned into Alistair's embrace.

She needed a moment to recharge.

They held each other close for a while, cheek to cheek. Then Caroline murmured, "Honey, I actually knew you a long time ago."

Alistair's heart skipped a beat.

Is she finally ready to tell me everything?

Caroline wrapped her arms around his neck, her voice laced with guilt. "I never dared to tell you before. I was afraid it would change how you saw me."

Alistair gazed at her tenderly. "How could it ever?"

Caroline had made up her mind. She wasn't going to keep it from him anymore. "We used to be neighbors. Bet you didn't see that coming, right? You never noticed me, but I used to see you almost every day."

Alistair, of course, already knew. He had known everything.

But hearing Caroline tell the story herself carried a weight no one else could understand.

Their thoughts drifted to twelve years ago.

"I saw her today," Caroline said quietly. "She called me trash—filthy, worthless, and too stubborn to take a hint."

She had sat at the window in a daze, staring blankly outside, and that was when she saw the boy standing there—looking up, smiling at her.

So even something as ugly and unwanted as me... didn't make him look away?

After that, she began to observe the boy next door—casually at first and then more deliberately.

But she wasn't discreet enough. He noticed her quickly.

He had lifted his head, flashing that clean, handsome smile, and waved at her.

She hadn't dared to wave back. Back then, she had thought she was too broken, too hideous to be seen by anyone.

If he really saw me, he'd probably be terrified.

That night, the stagnant pool of emotions inside her had finally started to ripple.

She was afraid he'd be annoyed by her presence. She was afraid that the next time he looked up at her window, his eyes would be filled with disgust.

But it turned out she had been wrong.

Although he never tried to talk to her again, she could feel it in the way he moved and acted—he was being considerate of her.

He had started spending more time outside, clearly on purpose.

His house was a plain, slightly worn two-story building. He used to live downstairs with his grandfather but later moved upstairs. And when he was home, unless it was nighttime, his curtains were always open, the windows wide, showing his room to the world.

It might have seemed meaningless to others, but to her, it was clear—this was his way of being there for her.

Alistair felt a wave of emotion swell in his chest.

Caroline was incredible.

She had actually picked up on what he had been trying to do back then.

She was right. When he realized someone was watching him, he hadn't been creeped out. On the contrary, with a heart still untouched by the cruelty of the world, he had tried his best to offer her a tiny spark of joy—just by existing.

"Thank you," Caroline whispered.

The memories stirred up a deep, relentless tide in Alistair's heart. He cupped her face, unable to hold back any longer. "Sweetheart, I have something for you. I was planning to give it to you on your birthday, but I just can't wait. Christmas is coming, so let's call it an early Christmas gift."

Caroline's eyes lit up. "What is it?"

Alistair gave her a mischievous smile. "That's a secret."

He placed his hands gently on her shoulders and guided her to sit down on the couch. Then he turned and walked into the storage room.

Before stepping out again, he made a request. "Close your eyes, sweetheart."

"Okay."

He returned to the living room.

Caroline sat upright, her back perfectly straight, hands resting primly on her lap. She looked more proper than a schoolgirl waiting for roll call.

Alistair tiptoed up to her and couldn't resist leaning in to kiss her lightly on the lips.

Caroline didn't open her eyes, but a smile tugged at her lips. "Is that my gift? Well, it's a little lazy, but I still like it."

Alistair chuckled, the warmth of his breath brushing her skin. "Don't underestimate me. My gifts are never half-hearted."

She felt his presence draw back and grew anxious. "Can I open my eyes now?"

"Just one more second... okay, now you can."

Caroline slowly opened her eyes. The first thing she saw was Alistair's glowing, eager smile. Her gaze dropped and then froze in place.

Alistair spoke gently, "Open it and see if you like it."

Caroline stared at him in shock, as if trying to confirm something.

He gave her a look of encouragement. She leaned forward, slowly reached for the item on the table, and placed her hand on the wooden box.

With one firm push, she opened it.

Time seemed to stop.

She sat there, stunned, staring at the neatly arranged contents. Tears fell without warning.

She didn't bother wiping them away. Her hands trembled as she picked up the topmost item—a photo album. Inside were pictures of her from infancy up to the age of twelve.

Silently, she set the album aside and picked up a notebook. The first page greeted her with a line in a familiar, delicate hand: [To my dearest Caroline, my precious girl.]
