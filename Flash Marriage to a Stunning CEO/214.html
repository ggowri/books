Chapter 214 The Truth Was Finally Revealed

The first night after Eloise left, both Alistair and Caroline embarrassingly found themselves wide awake, unable to sleep.

After a while, lying there restless just felt pointless.

Alistair glanced at Caroline with a meaningful look. "I heard working out helps with sleep."

Caroline caught on instantly.

They'd been together for a while now, but she still got shy when it came to this.

"You already... I mean... didn't we already..."

They had already "worked out", hadn't they?

But faced with Alistair's renewed interest, what could Caroline do? She let him have his way, as always.

After a long round of exertion, they finally managed to fall asleep.

Days passed steadily and uneventfully.

The film was progressing smoothly and would be wrapping soon.

As for the case, Theresa's crimes were indisputable. Execution was inevitable.

Alistair found time to visit the psychiatric facility—to "check in" on Paige.

The moment Paige saw him, the gleam of excitement on her face disappeared completely. She sneered in disgust, "Why is it you?"

Alistair sat down calmly, gazing at the windows reinforced with thick safety bars. He couldn't help thinking of prison.

Being in here was worse than jail.

He could see it in her face—hollowed out and drained. Paige was falling apart.

To be honest, if it weren't for that same detestable look in her eyes, Alistair wouldn't have even recognized her at first glance.

She hadn't been inside long, but the once-composed and elegant Professor Paige Ashbourne now looked like she'd aged twenty years. Her face was haggard, her spirit dull, and her hair had gone streaky with white.

When Alistair rose to leave, Paige's eyes locked onto him. "Where do you think you're going?"

He just got here and now he was leaving? What kind of game was that?

Alistair offered her a polite smile. "Didn't you say you didn't want to see me? Truth is, I don't care much to see you either. But I was curious how you were holding up in here. Now that I see you're miserable, I can rest easy. That's all. Bye now."

"You bastard!" Paige exploded. "You little punk, don't get cocky. One day I'll get out of here, and when I do, I'll make sure you regret ever crossing me!"

Alistair arched an eyebrow, his tone infuriatingly casual. "You really think that day's coming? Keep dreaming."

He stood there for a moment, watching as several nurses rushed in and pinned Paige to the bed while she kept screaming. Only then did he turn and walk away.

After leaving the hospital, Alistair didn't go home. He headed straight for the New York branch of Ashbourne Group.

Not just anyone could walk in and request a meeting with the country's wealthiest man.

The moment he stepped through the front door, the receptionist recognized him and looked conflicted. "I'm sorry, Mr. Harlow. Mr. Gordon Ashbourne is fully booked today. He might not have time to see you."

Alistair didn't respond. He simply pulled out his phone and made a call.

A few minutes later, Gordon's assistant came down in person and respectfully escorted him upstairs.

Not long after that, the receptionist received a call from Gordon's office. "If Mr. Harlow visits in the future, he doesn't need an appointment. Let him go right in."

Only two other people had that privilege—Mabel and Caroline.

Caroline had never used it, though.

The receptionists exchanged wide-eyed glances.

Did this mean Gordon had accepted Alistair?

Alistair was led straight into Gordon's office.

Gordon was still in a meeting. He was busy, yes, but not so busy that he couldn't spare a few minutes to see someone.

Alistair made himself comfortable on the couch, sipping the coffee the assistant brought him.

Before long, Gordon walked in.

He looked genuinely surprised to see Alistair. Maybe even—though he wouldn't admit it—quietly pleased.

He sat across from Alistair, expression mild. "What brings you here? Go ahead."

Alistair didn't waste time with small talk. "Mr. Gordon Ashbourne, may I ask how long you plan to keep Professor Paige Ashbourne in that psychiatric facility?"

Whatever flicker of delight Gordon had felt was gone. His expression tightened. He frowned slightly, clearly irritated by Alistair's formality.
Did he seriously just call me "Mr. Gordon Ashbourne"?
Then the words sank in.

"What's that supposed to mean?" he asked, suddenly serious.

Alistair gave him a faint smile. His features were clean-cut and confident, the picture of a wholesome young man.

He knew Gordon was a busy man, so he didn't bother being diplomatic. "I believe someone with a condition like Professor Paige Ashbourne should remain in the right place and receive proper treatment."

Gordon was silent for a long moment and then spoke softly, "Convince me."

After committing Paige, he hadn't visited her, but he'd received reports.

And they were grim.

As her brother, Gordon didn't want to treat her this way.

But pretending nothing happened would be an insult—to himself, to his late wife, and to his daughter.

As for how long she should stay, he genuinely hadn't decided.

Alistair's expression turned serious. "Do you know what she did to Caroline?"

Gordon flinched slightly and then let out a sigh. "Caroline had some unpleasant history with Paige..."

He stopped mid-sentence as a terrible thought hit him.

He had trusted Paige completely. No matter how strained her relationship with Caroline had been, she was still her aunt. He had always believed or wanted to believe that whatever Paige did was ultimately for Caroline's benefit.

But after seeing how far Paige was willing to go for her own selfish interests...

Gordon didn't want to finish the thought.

But Alistair wasn't going to stop there.

He watched Gordon's reaction closely, a tightness building in his chest.

So Gordon really had no idea what Caroline had been through that year. Brilliant in business, no doubt. But as a father? He'd failed her.

"Back then, the reason Caroline had issues with Professor Paige Ashbourne was because she allowed others to ransack Caroline's room and destroy the only things her mother left her."

Gordon said nothing.

"And the version you were told was that Professor Paige Ashbourne was worried Caroline would become rebellious, so she sent her to some special institution for discipline."

Gordon nodded slowly. He looked like he wanted to cut Alistair off but realized that doing so would be useless. Even if he refused to hear it, that wouldn't erase what Caroline had been through.

Alistair continued, cold and steady like a machine playing a horror story Gordon wished he could shut off.

"Caroline tried to run away. She failed. After that, she gave up. She became a puppet on strings, doing whatever she was told. She wasn't allowed to go outside. She couldn't even go downstairs."

He paused, his voice tightening. "She's a living, breathing human being, Mr. Gordon Ashbourne, and she wasn't even allowed to feel the sun on her face. Tell me, how could she ever be close to someone like Professor Paige Ashbourne?"

His tone sharpened.

"The pain was so severe that she eventually forgot everything that happened that year. But the hatred she felt toward Paige? She never forgot. It's carved into her bones."

The titan of industry sat there, trembling.

That was the year Caroline came back cold, distant, and unwilling to even call him Dad. And all this time, he had chalked it up to Caroline being stubborn, a failed rehabilitation effort...
