"Shut up and die!" With those icy words, Osborne unleashed an unrelenting storm of thunder that enveloped everyone on the battlefield.

The late-stage lightning elemental essence he commanded was terrifying beyond measure.

Even as the guards of the Fire Elf Clan Royal Family tried desperately to defend themselves, they stood no chance.

As their cultivation levels had already been suppressed to Ascension Two, their efforts were futile against the overwhelming power of Osborne.

When the lightning finally subsided, the ground lay littered with over twenty charred corpses, each face frozen in fear.

Yet one soul remained standing, clutching her head and screaming at the top of her lungs.

It was Princess Monica.

Osborne hadn't forgotten the deal he made with Alfreda. Monica had been a persistent thorn in Alfreda's side, and it was her actions that had forced Alfreda to leave Elf City and venture into the peril of Umbra Earth.

Alfreda wanted nothing more than to kill Monica herself.

Thus, Osborne had spared Monica's life, but her shrill, wailing screams had driven him to kick her away with little more than a flick of his foot.

"Stop screaming! You're still breathing!"

His tone was dismissive as he turned to Alfreda, whose gaze was fixed on him in shock.

Alfreda stood within the borders of the absolute forbidden zone, her own cultivation level severely diminished to Ascension Two, just like the others.

Witnessing how effortlessly Osborne dispatched those at her same level filled her with dread.

A chill ran through her as she took in the carnage.

Alfreda realized fully that she was no match for Osborne in her current state.

If she had been at her peak—still at Ascension Five—she might have had a fighting chance, even if she couldn't claim victory outright.

But in this reduced state, there was no way she could emerge victorious.

This meant that if Alfreda really fought against Osborne, as long as Osborne used the absolute forbidden zone, she would be easily defeated by Osborne!

At that moment, Alfreda couldn't spare a thought for her greatest rival, Princess Monica.

Instead, she stepped closer to Osborne, curiosity and awe swirling in her eyes.

"You...."

Alfreda opened her mouth, struggling to find the right words. The power he had just displayed was overwhelming, almost beyond comprehension, leaving her wondering how to articulate her thoughts.

She mused, "Is Osborne truly from the Human Community?

No way he's just human!

He's got to be part of an Apex Clan like the Sacred Dragon Clan, the Sacred Tiger Clan, or the Angel Clan, right?

Only a prodigy from one of those Apex Clans could wield such ferocity at Ascension Two."

Unable to contain her curiosity, she finally blurted out her question, her gaze earnest.

"Osborne, just tell me the truth—what's your real race? You're not from the Human Community, are you?"

At her inquiry, Osborne paused, his expression a mix of surprise and humor.

"What else could I possibly be?"

Alfreda tilted her head, testing her luck. "Come on, just tell me! I promise I won't spill a word! You're actually from the Sacred Dragon Clan, right? You're disguised as Human to keep your identity hidden while you train, aren't you?"

Osborne chuckled, gently patting her head.

"Don't jump to conclusions! I'm not from the Sacred Dragon Clan. In fact, I've never even met one! I'm just your average, run-of-the-mill Human!"

At that revelation, frustration twisted Alfreda's brow.

She narrowed her eyes, contemplating his words. After a long pause, Alfreda finally spoke up, "Once we get back to Elf City, I'm taking you to the Astral Testing Arch for a proper test. If you really are just an ordinary Human, I suspect your total score might just hit Class SSS! You could be one of the rare Celestial Progeny in this cosmos!"

"Celestial Progeny? Me?" Osborne chuckled, pointing at himself in disbelief.

Yet, the thought wasn't entirely far-fetched. Alfreda had reached a Class SS score at the Astral Testing Arch, and if they fought at the same cultivation level, he was confident she wouldn't stand a chance against him.

It was obvious from this that his talent far outshines Alfreda's.

"Maybe I really do meet the criteria for being called a Celestial Progeny," he mused, intrigued by the idea.

So Osborne said, "I guess I'll find out once we hit the Arch.

"First, though, we need to deal with her." Osborne gestured toward Princess Monica, who lay on the ground, her eyes wide with terror.

Alfreda's expression turned icy as she looked at Monica.

Beneath her calm exterior, a storm of hatred and anger simmered, a faint glimmer of murderous intent flickering in her eyes.

Monica cried out, her voice trembling, "No! Don't kill me!

"Alfreda, I know I was wrong! Please, spare me! I promise I'll never bother you again! I'll be your servant, I'll do anything you want!

"Please, don't kill me!

"Alfreda, we're both from the Elf Race! Have mercy!"

Her pleas grew more desperate, tears streaming down her cheeks.

Monica begged for her life, the weight of her noble status crashing down on her.

As a princess of the Fire Elf Clan, she had always held a high position in Elf City, and now she found herself on the brink of death, regretting every decision that had led her here.

At that moment, all she could think was how she wished she had never set foot in Umbra Earth.

If she had stayed in Elf City, she would never have faced such peril.

Alfreda would never have had the chance to end her life.

Slowly, she crawled toward Alfreda, clutching her legs and sobbing loudly, hoping for mercy.

But Alfreda merely stared down at her, her expression cold, filled with disdain and mockery.

With a swift kick, she sent Monica sprawling across the ground.

"Monica, for your own dignity, you should just accept your fate! I will kill you! You've provoked me for years, and I swore I would end you one day!

"Especially when I saw your father pressuring mine at the Nature Kingdom, forcing my father to bow to him to protect me.

"I've sworn it, and now the time has come!

"No matter how much you beg, I will not spare you! So stop crying; it only makes me look down on you more!"

Alfreda's voice dripped with contempt as she continued, "I'll send you off to reunite with your brother!

"Remember, in your next life, don't bully others just because you think you're powerful...

"Next time, be a better person!"

With a flick of her wrist, a vibrant green vine shot forth, piercing Monica's chest.

"No!"

Monica's terrified scream echoed in the air, but it was too late.

She could only watch in horror as the vine plunged deep into her heart.

Gurgling, blood spilled from her lips as she looked at Alfreda with disbelief and fury, life slipping away from her eyes.

"Alfreda, you'll regret this!

"The Fire Elf Clan will avenge me... just wait!"

With a final, defiant glare, Monica collapsed to the ground, her body going limp as the last flicker of life faded from her.
