After several Class S Deathborns were taken down, the high-grade death scent they left behind barely made a dent in restoring the wilting Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity.

It was clear that the essence from Class S Deathborn was far too scarce to make a significant impact.

Osborne sighed, frustration creeping into his thoughts. "Even if I took down thousands or even hundreds of thousands of Class S Deathborns, it wouldn't be enough to revive the Lotus to a point where it can heal me. This is just not going to cut it."

He knew he had to take a risk.

"If I want the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity to recover, I'll need to hunt down higher-tier Deathborns. The more powerful the Deathborn I take down, the more death scent I'll get, and the better it will be for the Lotus. But going after Junior Deity Deathborn in my current state? That's a tall order."

His thoughts were interrupted as he spotted two Junior Deity Deathborns still oblivious to his presence, wandering aimlessly, clearly confused.

He mused, "I gotta take them out, no doubt about it!

Sure, dealing with a Junior Deity right now is a bit of a gamble, but if I don't step up, that Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity will never recover, and neither will my injuries or my power.

I'm the Blood Clan's progenitor, I got that natural edge over the Deathborn. They can't bring their full game when they're up against me. If I'm too chicken to mess with a Junior Deity Deathborn, what am I gonna do when I face other Junior Deities?

Just roll over and die? That is not the way to live!

In Celestara, Junior Deities are just scraping by at the bottom of the food chain. I gotta get back on my feet, even stronger if I wanna survive in this joint. So yeah, I gotta roll the dice, gotta take the risk."

With a surge of determination, Osborne made his decision.

"My Divine Body is cracked and heavily injured, so I can't use too much power without risking further damage. My space elemental essence is also in rough shape, but the dark elemental essence seems to be holding up well. As long as I manage my strength, I should be able to handle a Junior Deity Deathborn."

With a plan in mind, he slipped behind one of the Junior Deity Deathborn.

Thanks to his full use of the Eclipsing Mantra, the creature had no idea he was there, mistaking him for just another rock in the terrain.

It wasn't until he launched his attack that the Deathborn finally reacted.

But by then, it was far too late.

Osborne's fist, imbued with dark energy, slammed into the Deathborn's back, piercing straight through its body and releasing a cloud of death scent into the air.

The Deathborn howled in pain, enraged but still standing, turning to engage Osborne in a fierce battle.

If he had been at full strength, taking down a Junior Deity Deathborn would have been a walk in the park. But now, every moment was a struggle, and he found himself in tight spots more than once.

Thankfully, the aura of the Blood Clan founder instilled enough fear in the Deathborn that they held back at crucial moments, giving Osborne just the openings he needed.

After a grueling exchange, he finally managed to bring the Junior Deity Deathborn down, panting heavily from the exertion.

But just as he caught his breath, the other Junior Deity Deathborn, sensing the commotion, charged toward him. There was no time to rest; he had to prepare for another fight.

After what felt like an eternity, Osborne staggered to a halt, bloodied and exhausted.

Both Junior Deity Deathborn lay defeated at his feet, but the battle had taken its toll. He had pushed himself too hard, worsening the injuries to his already battered Divine Body.

"At least I finally took them down," Osborne said, a mix of relief and pain washing over him.

The death scent left behind by these Junior Deity Deathborns was far more potent than that of Class S Deathborn. As the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity absorbed the rich essence, its withered appearance began to soften.

Osborne could feel a stirring within the roots, as if part of the Lotus was awakening from a long slumber. A gentle energy radiated from the roots, gradually mending his wounds.

Joy surged through him.

"This really works! Just two Junior Deity Deathborn, and the Lotus' roots are already coming back to life, helping me heal. If this keeps up, it won't be long before the Lotus is fully restored! At the very least, my injuries will be healed."

His eyes sparkled with hope and eagerness.

The thought of hunting down more Deathborn was tantalizing, but he knew he had to exercise caution.

Defeating two Junior Deity Deathborns had taken a toll on him, and he needed to rest. "I should head back and let the Lotus work its magic.

No need to rush! I've figured out how to recover so I can afford to take a day off.

With the Lotus' help, I'll definitely regain some strength and won't struggle as much against the Junior Deity Deathborn next time.

Tomorrow, I'll take down even more of them!"

With that thought in mind, Osborne turned to make his way back to Rodriguez Village.

As he approached, he noticed a crowd gathered around the stone house where Harry and his sister lived. The group looked menacing, their expressions twisted with malice.

Harry and his sister stood at the edge, their faces pale with fear.
