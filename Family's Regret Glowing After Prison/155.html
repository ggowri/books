Chapter 155 A Full Body Examination

At the hospital, Aurora underwent a full battery of tests, with Daisy by her side.

She handed the results to the doctor, a seasoned professional with over thirty years of experience.

His expression grew more serious as he examined the papers, his brow furrowing deeper the longer he studied them.

After what felt like an eternity, Aurora stepped out of the office.

Her eyes were hollow and lifeless, as though her soul had been drained, leaving her devoid of all energy.

The doctor's words echoed in her mind, each one a sharp blade piercing her heart.

"How does someone so young end up with a body in this condition?

"You've been malnourished for years, your blood and energy levels dangerously low. Combined with old injuries, your body has been pushed far beyond its limits.

"Also, you're missing a kidney. That's left you weak, and it's dragging down your other organs as well," the doctor said, shaking his head.
Daisy spoke up, her voice tight with worry.
"Doctor, if we replace the missing kidney and take good care of her, can she improve?"
He sighed deeply.

"It's not that simple. Years of weakness and hidden damage have already caused permanent harm to her organs.
"As for her ears, if it were just a ruptured eardrum, we could fix it. But her auditory nerves are irreparably damaged. Even a cochlear implant won't help. And with prolonged hearing loss, it could begin affecting her speech.
"Regarding her leg, even if we reset it, she'll never move like a normal person. At best, she might walk without a limp, but she'll never be able to move quickly."
The doctor continued, each word feeling like a blow to her already battered spirit.
Aurora understood now.

Cooked meat can never be fresh again.
No matter how hard she tried, her broken body would never heal.
She couldn't marry, have children, or even experience intimacy like a normal woman.
"Doctor, how long do I have with my body like this?"
The question weighed heavily on her, but she had to know.

"If you're well taken care of, maybe four or five years," he replied.
Four or five years?
That meant she might not even make it to thirty.
Aurora stumbled forward, her body feeling as though it carried the weight of a mountain.

Each breath stabbed her chest.
Daisy followed closely behind, her eyes welling up with tears from heartache.

"Ms. Elvador, don't give up. Medical science has advanced so much. If hospitals in Orley can't help, we'll try elsewhere," Daisy pleaded.
Aurora kept walking, limping with each step, unresponsive.
Then it hit Daisy.

Aurora could no longer hear any sound.
Tears spilled instantly.
When had she lost her hearing?

If Daisy hadn't come today, she wouldn't have even noticed.
Watching Aurora's fragile, thin frame, Daisy clamped a hand over her mouth, trying to stifle her sobs.

But the tears wouldn't stop.
Just yesterday, they had struck back at those who had hurt Aurora.
Yet now, Daisy felt no satisfaction from that revenge.

No amount of revenge could bring the girl's health back.

Anger and sorrow overwhelmed Daisy, but she was powerless to help Aurora.

A deep sense of helplessness filled her heart.
"Aurora!" A deep male voice suddenly called out.
Daisy quickly wiped her tears and looked up, seeing Adolph in a hospital gown, his face pale, blocking Aurora's path.

Adolph was there because the night before, after learning the full truth, he had been hit harder than ever.

Back home, he drowned himself in liquor until alcohol poisoning set in, and his parents rushed him to the hospital.

He never imagined he would run into Aurora at the hospital.

Reaching out, he grabbed Aurora's frail shoulders, his eyes red as he asked, "Aurora, are you here to see me?"

Aurora's hollow gaze slowly focused.

When she recognized Adolph, unmasked disgust flashed across her face.

"I can't even stand the sight of you," she said coldly.

Adolph's face turned ashen, his lips trembling. "Do you hate me that much?"

"Yes! I hate you! I wish you'd just die!" she suddenly shouted at him.

If it weren't for them, she wouldn't be this crippled shell of herself.

Her heart felt like it was being ripped apart, bleeding out.

If she hadn't gone to prison back then, she would have gotten into Vertar University.

Five years later, she would have graduated smoothly and landed a decent job.

By the time she met Julian, she wouldn't have to feel so inferior.

She had finally found someone she liked, but she wasn't worthy of him, nor could she be with him for the long haul.

Tears streamed down Aurora's face.

Why is my life so miserable?
She almost wished she had never met Julian.

That way, she wouldn't ache so much over losing him.

What was the point of revenge if her life was still a wreck?

"Adolph, don't ever show up in front of me again. I really despise you."

Her words stabbed Adolph like a knife.

The phrase "I despise you" looped in his mind like a curse.

He lunged forward, pulling her into a fierce hug as if he could meld her into himself.

"Aurora, don't do this to me, please.

"I really like you. Hit me, yell at me, anything; just give me one last chance, I beg you."

She shoved against him, but he only held tighter.

"Aurora, you got your revenge yesterday. My debt to you is paid. Let's start over. I swear I'll spend the rest of my life making it up to you. Believe me, please."

His voice cracked, thick with sincerity, as if he would rip his heart out to prove it.

However, Aurora didn't care for his remorse.

It was too late.

What's spilled can't be scooped back up.

A shattered mirror never fits back together.

Her life was ruined, beyond repair.

"Let me go, Adolph," she said, her tone icy.

"No, I won't." Adolph clung to her stubbornly, as if holding on meant there was still hope.

A bitter smirk flickered in her eyes.

"I've got something to show you. Let go."

Adolph hesitated, then slowly released her.

"Aurora, what is it?" he asked, hope flickering in his voice.

Without a word, she slapped the thick stack of test results against his chest.

Adolph frowned in confusion but took them, flipping through page by page.

His eyes widened as he scanned through the results. His breathing grew ragged, and his hands shook violently.

"No way! This can't be real. I don't believe it," he muttered, but tears betrayed him, falling freely.

His legs gave out, and he dropped to his knees before her.

He reached up, gripped her slender waist tightly, and sobbed uncontrollably, "Aurora, I'm sorry, I'm so sorry..."

She didn't look down at him or catch his words.

But even without looking, she could guess he was apologizing again.

Her unfocused gaze drifted to the end of the hallway.

People passed by, staring curiously at them, but she didn't care anymore.

I'm dying soon.

What's there to care about?

Adolph, seeing no reaction from Aurora, looked up and saw her lifeless, hollow expression.

The sight nearly broke him.

Suddenly, he grabbed her hand and struck it against his face.

"Aurora, hit me," he pleaded.

"If it makes you feel better, hit me as hard as you want."
