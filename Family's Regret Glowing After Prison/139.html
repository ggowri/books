Chapter 139 Repentance

On the second floor, the embroidery room overlooked the garden with an unobstructed view when the window was opened.

Julian had positioned the room to face the garden so that when Aurora needed a break, she could stand by the window, breathe in the floral-scented air, and relax.

At this moment, Julian leaned lazily against the window, his gaze following Aurora and Lily's every move in the garden.

As he listened to their conversation, his deep eyes narrowed slightly, and a flicker of intrigue crossed his face.

Daisy really raised a sharp-minded daughter.

He had expected unscrupulous men to pursue Aurora, but instead, it was Lily who was trying to take her away.

Yet, he showed no signs of anger. He remained calm and composed, listening intently to their conversation.

Aurora hesitated for a moment, "I... agree."

"Really?" Lily's face lit up with joy. She stepped forward and tightly hugged Aurora, her voice brimming with excitement. "Aurora, when should we leave?

"How about tomorrow? I'll book the tickets later."

Aurora couldn't see Lily's lips or hear her words, but she could tell that Lily had spoken at length.

She gently pushed Lily away and continued, "I agree to leave with you, but..."

"But?" Lily's smile froze, and a hint of nervousness and confusion flashed in her eyes. "But what?"

Aurora bit her lower lip, looking troubled. "I promised Mr. Hawke that I would finish embroidering The Garden for Madam Hawke. At her birthday party, Mr. Hawke spent 20 million dollars on that piece, but I was careless, and Bertha took the chance to tear it apart."

As she spoke, guilt and self-reproach filled her eyes.

"Moreover, Mr. Hawke has been kind to me. When I was at my lowest, he appeared and pulled me back. If it weren't for him, I would have jumped off the bridge the night Camille woke up."

That night, she had also drunk two bottles of wine worth a million dollars, which her body couldn't metabolize, leading to her hospitalization.

Julian had also paid for her medical expenses.

She kept all these things in her heart.

She owed Julian so much and had yet to repay him.

Lily clenched her fists, never imagining that Aurora had once harbored such despair.

At this moment, she was immensely grateful that Aurora had met Julian, or, she would never have seen Aurora.

Lily cautiously asked, "Then, after you've repaid Mr. Hawke, can we go abroad?"

Aurora smiled gently, "Okay."

Now that she had mastered the art of embroidery, she could support herself even abroad, ensuring she wouldn't fall into dire straits.

Julian listened quietly, the glint in his eyes deepening, like an unfathomable lake.

Aurora, I never agreed to let you escape from my side.

Grandma has already decided you'll be my wife.

I've even placed my mother's ring on your finger.

You're part of the Hawke family. How can you leave so easily and abandon us?

However, he didn't plan to stop her now. What he wanted was for Aurora to willingly return to him, even if she did try to escape.

*****

The next day, Aurora woke up to the sound of light rain tapping rhythmically against the window.

She followed her usual routine, washing up and then heading downstairs for breakfast.

After finishing her meal, she prepared to go upstairs to continue her unfinished embroidery work.

But just as she turned, a warm and strong hand suddenly grasped her wrist.

The touch sent a surge of electricity through her body, making her heart race uncontrollably.

Her eyes widened in surprise and confusion as she looked at the man in front of her.

Julian's lips parted, and his deep, magnetic voice flowed through the air. "Aurora, sit."

The way he said her name made her flush and heart race.

She didn't know when he had stopped calling her Ms. Elvador, but every time he spoke to her, he would pull her close, making her face him directly.

"Mr. Hawke, is…something wrong?" Aurora's voice was soft and slightly trembling.

"Yes, Adolph's parents have brought Camille here," Julian said calmly, as if it were the most ordinary thing. But Aurora could feel the tension in the air.

She was stunned and didn't know how to react.

The moment she had been waiting for, day and night, for five years had finally arrived.

Although the truth had already been uncovered by Daniel, Camille held a special significance for her.

Only she can prove my innocence.

Only Camille's testimony could completely clear the five years of wrongful accusations Aurora had endured.

Following Julian's pull, she slowly sat down beside him.

Julian turned slightly and instructed Ashley, "Ashley, open the door and let them in."

Wilson and Lizeth had been waiting outside for a long time.

After learning the truth from Camille, their faces had turned ashen, filled with guilt and remorse. They could hardly face Aurora.

For over an hour, while Aurora had been waking up, washing, and eating breakfast, the couple had stood outside the Hawke Estate, letting the cold rain soak them.

They didn't use umbrellas, as if punishing themselves for their past mistakes, seeking redemption through this self-inflicted suffering.

The rain mercilessly drenched their clothes, their hair clinging to their pale faces, making them look disheveled and pitiful.

Ashley opened the villa's door and said to Wilson, Lizeth, and Camille standing outside the iron gate, "Come in."

At these words, Wilson and Lizeth shuddered.

For a moment, their legs felt as if they were too heavy to move.

Just yesterday, they had been determined to teach Aurora a lesson and humiliate her. But in one day, everything changed.

Five years ago, Aurora had never harmed Camille.

Yet, Camille's injury had become the beginning of Aurora's nightmare: five years of wrongful imprisonment and endless suffering.

Camille glanced at her depressed parents, and felt a wave of sorrow.

"Go in. We must face what's coming. No one can escape the need for atonement. This is what we owe Aurora."

Her voice was calm and resolute as she pushed her wheelchair into the villa, her parents trailing behind like lifeless figures.

When they saw Aurora sitting on the sofa in the living room, it was as if an invisible force had drained all their strength. Their legs gave way, and with a thud, both of them knelt before Aurora.

"Aurora, we're sorry." Wilson's voice was hoarse and trembling, filled with deep remorse.

"We didn't mean to. We thought you harmed Camille, so we sent you to prison. We're truly sorry." Lizeth sobbed and said, tears streaming down her face.

In just a few days, they had been crushed by one harsh truth after another, as if they had aged 10 years in an instant.

They sincerely apologized, but Aurora would not easily accept this belated repentance.

"Do you think a simple 'sorry' can make up for the suffering?" Aurora's voice was calm, but it carried an undeniable anger and resentment.

"In prison, I was slapped every day, forced to kneel, drank toilet water, pricked with needles, and my leg was broken..."

As Aurora recounted each detail, Wilson and Lizeth's faces grew paler.

"Isn't this all your doing?"

"And it's not just you. Joshua, Bertha, and the others even bribed prisoners to torment me. They even took one of my kidneys."

"I was once a healthy person with a bright future, but now I'm disabled. Everyone is responsible for this."

Aurora's voice trembled slightly. "You've ruined my life. How can you think a simple sorry can erase the harm you've caused me?"
