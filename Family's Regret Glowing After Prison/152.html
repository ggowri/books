Chapter 152 Adolph Went Crazy

Adolph's words were utterly ridiculous.

Sure, Aurora was the true heiress of the Elvador family, but from the start, his engagement had always been with Bertha.

Back when the engagement was made, the Sherwood family knew Bertha wasn't the biological daughter of the Elvador family.

Even so, they pushed Adolph into it.

After all, Bertha was the apple of everyone's eye then, basking in the spotlight.

What Adolph's parents never saw coming was how vicious Bertha's true nature would turn out to be.

But for Adolph, only the real Elvador heiress was worthy of him.

The moment he learned Aurora was the real daughter, his heart decided she was his fiancée.

However, the chilling part was that, despite claiming her as his own in his mind, he never once stood by her side, never offered the support or trust she deserved.

There was only doubt and harsh words.

Now, seeing Aurora wrap her arms around Julian's waist, leaning on him with complete trust, Adolph was consumed by jealousy.

In his madness, he blurted out those absurd words without thinking.

Upon hearing this, Wilson, Lizeth, and Camille were so embarrassed that they wished the floor would swallow them whole.

Julian, who had been gently comforting Aurora, slowly lifted his eyes.

His gaze cut toward Adolph like a blade.

A mocking smirk appeared on his face.

Then, with a smooth motion, he raised his well-defined hand and cradled the back of Aurora's head.

Under everyone's stunned stares, he leaned down without warning and pressed his lips to hers.

Aurora had been lost in her insecurities, quietly grieving over the thought of never being with Julian forever.

This sudden kiss froze her in place, her mind blank, unable to process it.

Julian's kiss was fierce and commanding, paying no mind to the crowd watching.

His lips moved against hers, lingering and claiming, while his eyes stayed locked on Adolph, brimming with provocation.

It was as if he was saying, "Aurora is mine. You don't stand a chance."

Adolph met that challenging stare, his heart pounding uncontrollably.

He trembled with rage.

"You... you bastard! I told you not to touch Aurora!" he shouted, charging forward with a raised fist.

Before he could reach Julian, his father intercepted him with a swift grab.

Furious, Wilson slapped him across the face and roared, "You idiot! Haven't you humiliated us enough?"

Adolph clenched his fists, tears welling in his eyes as he yelled in grievance, "Dad, he is stealing my woman!"

"Your woman?" Wilson snapped through gritted teeth. "Aurora accepted Mr. Hawke's proposal. She's his fiancée now. As for your woman..."

At this, his face flushed with embarrassment.

Although he hated to admit it, the one engaged to Adolph was Bertha, that venomous woman.

"Your woman is Bertha. Stop making a fool of yourself!"

Those words hit Adolph like a bucket of cold water.

He deflated, staring daggers at Julian.

Meanwhile, Julian pulled a blushing Aurora firmly into his chest, then bent down and scooped her up into his arms.

Standing tall, he strode toward the stairs with steady steps.

Through it all, Aurora didn't spare Adolph a single glance.

"Aurora!" Adolph shouted her name, his voice raw and desperate.

But Aurora couldn't hear him.

"Aurora Elvador!"

He lost it completely, lunging forward to chase after her, only to be held back by his father's iron grip.

Julian carried Aurora into a room, and the closing door cut off Adolph's view.

His heart plunged into an icy abyss, sinking to rock bottom as every shred of hope shattered.

He didn't even dare imagine what might happen between Aurora and Julian alone in there.

If Julian kissed her in front of everyone, what's stopping him from going further out of sight?

The thought drove him mad, his vision darkening with rage. He stumbled, nearly collapsing, but his father caught him just in time.

Adolph's pain hit its peak.

He sank to the floor, hands covering his face, muttering through choked sobs, "I was wrong, Aurora. Please, look at me again..."

The living room echoed with his mournful cries.

Watching their son break down, Wilson and Lizeth felt their hearts ache.

But what could they do?

They had wronged Aurora too and had no right to face her.

That she hadn't come after them was already an immense mercy.

Wilson hardened his resolve and bellowed, "Shut up! You have no right to cry. Get up! We're going home."

With that, he yanked the sobbing Adolph off the ground and dragged him toward the door.

Lizeth pushed Camille's wheelchair behind them.

Camille glanced up at the second floor, whispering in her heart.
Aurora, you have to be happy.

Outside, Adolph refused to leave, insisting on staying outside the villa to beg for Aurora's forgiveness.

Furious, Wilson slapped him a few times across the face before forcibly dragging him away.

Back at the Sherwood Estate, Wilson learned that the Hawke Group had crushed the Sherwood Group to the brink of bankruptcy.

Although it hadn't fully collapsed, their current state was barely a step away from ruin.

Wilson, Lizeth, and Adolph fell into a heavy silence, the atmosphere so oppressive it was hard to breathe.

Camille, however, let out a cold laugh and said, "This is all karma."

Ever since she woke up and saw Adolph blindly defend Bertha, she had sensed this day would come.

If she hadn't accidentally saved Aurora's life, Sherwood Group would've followed the Elvador family into ruin.

The fact that her parents and brother could return home intact was only because Aurora had spared them out of consideration for her.

Anyone hearing those ugly secrets would've made the same choice I did.

Before knowing Bertha's true colors, hadn't she, like Adolph, hurt Aurora over and over for Bertha's sake?

Everyone who harmed Aurora deserves this.

"Dad, lend me your driver," Camille said.

"Where are you going?"

A chill flickered in her eyes. "To the prison. Everyone knows the truth now, but Barnett doesn't.

"As one of the accomplices who framed Aurora, I won't let him live with a clear conscience."

With that, she wheeled herself out, resolute.

In Orley's mental hospital, Celeste thrashed wildly, screaming, "I'm not sick! Let me out!"

Two nurses exerted great effort to pin her down, while another tied her securely to the bed with a rope.

Terror twisted her face as she shouted, trembling, "What are you doing? Do you know who I am? I'm..."

But halfway through her words, she faltered, at a loss for words.

Before meeting Joshua, she had been the esteemed eldest daughter of Jerome Group, a name known to all in Orley.

After marrying him, she became Mrs. Elvador.

But now?

Her father was dead.

Joshua, that scum, had deceived her all along, and she wanted nothing to do with being his wife anymore.

Now, with no father or husband to rely on, her son in prison, and her daughter filled with hatred toward her, she was nothing.

For the first time, Celeste felt utterly helpless.

"Please, let me go. I'm not sick," she begged through tears.

The doctor ignored her pleas, replying coldly, "Everyone who comes here says they're fine. If you weren't sick, why would you be sent in?"

"I'm not sick," she repeated, over and over, but no one believed her.

"Don't worry, you'll be sick soon enough."

With that, a needle pierced her skin.

As the drug from the syringe entered her body, Celeste's mind grew hazy.

Despair swallowed her whole.

Now, she too tasted what Aurora had endured, being misunderstood, with no one believing her no matter how she explained.

Did Aurora feel the same back then?

So helpless and despairing...

As her eyes fluttered shut, a single tear of regret slid down her cheek.
