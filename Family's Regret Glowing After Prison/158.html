Chapter 158 A Familiar Silhouette

It was not until 4 a.m. that Julian finally fell asleep.

But less than three hours later, he was awakened by the ringing of his phone.

Julian fumbled for his phone, his hoarse voice still laced with drowsiness. "Hello?"

Ethan's voice came through the phone.

"Mr. Hawke, there's a crucial matter in the East Suburb project that requires your personal attention. I'll pick you up in a bit."

"Alright," Julian responded, taking a deep breath.

Ethan noticed that his voice sounded different from usual as if he was weak.

"Mr. Hawke, are you okay?"

"I'm fine," Julian replied curtly before hanging up.

He forced himself to sit up, but his vision blurred due to low blood sugar.

Julian gritted his teeth and swore to himself, "I will definitely eat properly from now on, no exceptions!"

After washing up, Julian, dressed in a black suit, opened his bedroom door.

As soon as he opened the door, the familiar scent of cilantro hit him.

Julian had a bad feeling.

Aurora stood there with her usual gentle smile, "Mr. Hawke, good morning. Perfect timing. I made pasta. Would you have some?"

Julian's gaze fell on the pasta on the dining table.

Perhaps because he had eaten too quickly yesterday, Aurora mistakenly thought he hadn't had enough. Today, the portion was even larger than before.

Aurora's smile deepened. "You woke up early today, so you don't have to rush like yesterday. Take your time. If it's not enough, there's more in the kitchen."

Julian's mouth twitched involuntarily.

He felt the need to explain to Aurora that he was allergic to cilantro, but seeing the expectant look in her eyes, he couldn't bring himself to say it.

It's just an allergy. I'll take some medicine later.

Though he thought this, his steps toward the dining table were still hesitant.

Just as he sat down, the doorbell rang.

Julian's eyes lit up.
Ethan's here already?

Then I can pretend I'm in a hurry and take this plate of pasta with me.

I'll give it to Ethan in the car.

That way, Aurora's kindness won't go to waste.

So he said, "I'll get the door."

Aurora couldn't hear the doorbell, but she understood Julian's words.

She quickly said, "Mr. Hawke, I'll get it."

With that, she hurried over and opened the door.

Julian sat at the dining table, not touching his food.

He stared intently at the door, eagerly anticipating Ethan's arrival.

However, when he saw who it was, his expectations were instantly shattered.

It wasn't Ethan at the door—it was Lily.

As soon as Aurora opened the door, Lily, like a cheerful little bird, spread her arms and gave her a big hug.

"Aurora, I missed you so much," Lily said, taking a deep breath near Aurora's neck. "You smell so good!"

Julian felt as if something of his had been taken over.

Aurora asked in surprise, "It's not the weekend today. Why are you here?"

Lily let go of her, her face beaming with a smile. "I don't have classes this morning, so I came to see you.

"Can I come to see you whenever I don't have classes?"

Lily's cheerful and playful demeanor made her seem like a carefree little girl.

But the look in Lily's eyes as she gazed at Aurora was filled with tenderness and concern.

Lily just wanted to make Aurora happy.

Aurora's expression softened, and she patted Lily's head as if soothing a child.

"Of course, I'd love to see you every day."

How could she not like someone as cute as Lily?
Just looking at Lily brightened her mood considerably.

Aurora took Lily's hand and led her inside.

"Lily, have a seat. There's more pasta in the pot. I'll get you some."

Lily giggled, "Aurora, you're so nice. If I were a man, I'd definitely marry you."

Aurora gave her a helpless look. "You and your sweet talk."

Lily stuck out her tongue playfully.

The intimate bond between Lily and Aurora made Julian feel uneasy.

As Lily passed by him, she merely nodded in his direction as a form of greeting.

Then she happily followed Aurora into the kitchen.

From the kitchen, Aurora's voice could be heard. "Why are you following me in here?"

Lily replied in a coquettish tone, "I finally get to see you. I just want to spend more time with you."

Aurora was amused by Lily's words and laughed.

The two chatted and laughed in the kitchen, making Julian feel somewhat out of place.

Just then, Ethan walked in from outside.

"Mr. Hawke, are you ready?"

Lily, who was about to serve herself some pasta, froze at the sound of his voice.

Why does this voice sound so familiar?

She turned her head to look toward the dining area and saw a man in a silver-gray suit, wearing gold-rimmed glasses, walking over. He looked refined and handsome.

When she saw him, Lily's eyes widened.

Isn't that the man who gave me a million dollars?

Isn't he friends with those scumbags Darren and Barnett?

Why is he here?

And it looks like he's Mr. Hawke's subordinate.

Lily's mind was in complete chaos.

Suddenly, Ethan seemed to sense something and glanced toward the kitchen.

Lily was so startled that she quickly turned her head away, stiffening in place.

Ethan's gaze lingered on Lily's silhouette. "That person..."

Why does her back look so familiar?

As Ethan pondered, Aurora walked out with a plate of pasta and introduced. "This is Lily, Daisy's daughter."

Daisy's daughter?

She looks about the same age as Aurora.

Lily?

He couldn't help but think of the Lily he knew, feeling that their silhouettes were even more alike.

Ethan stared intently at Lily as if waiting for her to turn around.

Lily was so nervous that she broke out in a cold sweat, feeling utterly panicked.

At that moment, Aurora asked, "Ethan, have you eaten? Would you like some too?"

Ethan withdrew his gaze and smiled at Aurora.

Just as he was about to take the pasta from her hands, Julian's deep voice interrupted, "He's already had breakfast."

With that, Julian picked up the plate of pasta in front of him.

"I have some urgent matters to attend to. I'll take this with me and eat on the way. Ethan, let's go."

Julian turned and strode out.

Ethan reluctantly glanced at the pasta in Aurora's hands and then took one last deep look at Lily's back in the kitchen before quickly following Julian out.

Once outside, Julian shoved the plate of pasta into Ethan's hands. "Here, eat it."

Ethan was both surprised and flattered.

But when he looked down and saw the cilantro on the plate, he immediately understood.

He chuckled teasingly, "Cilantro is so delicious, yet you can't eat it. What a shame."

Julian opened the car door and got into the driver's seat.

"Seems your salary is still too high."

Ethan paused mid-bite. "Why?"

"You talk too much."

Ethan then had to keep his mouth shut.
