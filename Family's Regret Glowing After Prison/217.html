Chapter 217 Epilogue: Aurora XI

The scene was all too familiar to Aurora, just like the false accusations she faced in her past life.

Once again, Bertha was framing her for a crime she didn't commit.

But this time, it wasn't Joshua standing beside Bertha; it was Barnett.

Aurora's heart clenched. She balled her fists tightly, her gaze fixed on Barnett.

In my last life, Barnett fell for Bertha's lies and testified against me, landing me in jail.

Now, I want to see what he'll do after witnessing Bertha's crime himself.

Barnett stood frozen in place.

In his mind, Bertha had always been sweet, well-behaved, and lovable.

He never expected her to push Megan down the stairs and then pin the blame on someone else.

When Barnett met Aurora's icy, probing stare, an inexplicable wave of guilt surged through him as if he had somehow wronged her before.

But we've only met twice.

Just as Barnett was at a loss, Bertha grabbed his arm in panic. "Barnett, help me! I didn't mean to!"

"How am I supposed to help you?"

"There are only three of us here. If you back me up and say Aurora pushed Megan, everyone will believe it!"

Barnett shuddered, wondering if he had misheard.

He stared at Bertha as if seeing her for the first time.

Since when did my sweet little sister become like this?

A flicker of irritation rose in him, but Bertha was still his little sister, the treasured jewel of the Elvador family.

No way he'd side with an outsider over her.

His expression hardened as he turned to Aurora. "I saw you pushed her."

The stairwell fell into dead silence.

After speaking, Barnett felt as if all his strength had drained away.

Barnett felt drained, unable to meet Aurora's eyes, fearing the hatred he might see.

But he was wrong.

There was no hatred in Aurora's gaze. No surprise, either.

She knew Barnett always favored Bertha and how little she mattered to the Elvadors.

Since she had already accepted it, there was no disappointment left.

A mocking smile tugged at Aurora's lips.

She faced this situation differently now.

In her previous life, she had sobbed and screamed, desperately pleading, "It wasn't me. It was Bertha!" But no one had believed her.

This time, Aurora didn't struggle or argue. She knew the Barron family would stand by her.

I'm not that unwanted, bullied Aurora anymore!

Now, I have a family!

She stared at Barnett and Bertha, and suddenly, she laughed.

The sound echoed sharply in the stairwell.

"You two are truly rotten and pathetically stupid," she said, pointing up. "The security camera caught everything. Did you think you could frame me?"

Their heads snapped up, and their faces paled at the sight of the camera.

Aurora smirked.

In her past life, Celeste had erased the footage, leaving her defenseless against the Elvadors' lies.

But now, she had a loving family and solid proof.

Celestia Palace was the only seven-star hotel in Putnam, with serious power backing it.

She doubted the Elvadors had the influence to tamper with their security feeds.

This time, I hold all the cards.

Her voice dripped with scorn. "My parents adore me. What do you think will happen to the Elvador family when they find out you tried to frame me?"

Bertha, though panicked, refused to back down. "You're just nouveau riche trash. We aren't afraid of you!"

Aurora's smile turned even colder. "You still don't know my name, do you? I'm a Barron—one of the four great families of Putnam."

Horror flashed across Barnett and Bertha's faces.

Yet, Aurora had no interest in wasting more time on them.

I need to find Mom. She'll worry if she comes back and can't find me.

Just as she turned to leave, footsteps echoed from below.

They grew closer, then stopped beside her.

Aurora looked back, meeting Julian's bright eyes as he took her hand.

She flushed slightly. "Julian... Did you hear everything just now?"

He gave a small nod. "Aurora, letting them off so easily is too merciful. They tried to frame you. They need to be punished."

Still holding her hand, he began ascending the steps, one by one.

Barnett and Bertha trembled under Julian's icy, impassive gaze.

Even at just ten years old, Julian carried an intimidating aura, his eyes sharp with lethal intent.

When they reached the top, Julian faced the two and said calmly, "I'm ten years old—below the age of full criminal responsibility."

Barnett's eyes widened. "W-What do you mean?"

Julian smirked, but his eyes remained cold.

In one swift motion, he seized Barnett and shoved him down the stairs.

Barnett tumbled violently, landing in a crumpled heap beside the unconscious Megan, his body twitching in pain.

Then, Julian kicked Bertha down after him.

The pampered girl shrieked the whole way down before crashing at the bottom, blood streaming from her head as she passed out.

Once it was done, Julian spoke coolly. "Neither of them has reached the age of full criminal responsibility either. Since the law can't touch them, I'll avenge my Aurora myself."

His tone was light, but every word carried a bone-chilling edge.

Aurora stared at Julian in awe.

She wasn't afraid. She didn't find him cruel. She only admired his courage to fight back.

If I had been like him in my past life, the Elvadors wouldn't have hurt me.

Suddenly, she threw her arms around Julian, hugging him tightly.

"Julian... Thank you."

Julian stiffened, unused to such sudden closeness. He had always been solitary.

But he didn't resist. He loved it.

Just then, footsteps echoed from the stairwell.

Aurora released Julian and looked down, straight into Celeste's eyes.

Her gaze narrowed.
Celeste's timing is as perfect as it was in the past.

Back then, after Bertha pushed Camille, Aurora and Celeste had arrived at the scene one after another.

Even though Celeste knew Aurora couldn't have pushed Camille from below, she still protected Bertha and deleted the footage.

Now, seeing her precious son and adopted daughter lying bloodied at the bottom of the stairs... I wonder how she'll react.
