Chapter 143 Celeste Learning the Truth

What? Why was Camille here? Wasn't she have been sent abroad?

Joshua panicked.

He had planned to buy a gun and eliminate Camille once his family settled overseas to tie up loose ends. But before he could even leave, there she was, alive and right in front of him.

Hadn't that scoundrel Adolph sworn that Camille was under strict house arrest in a remote estate with no chance of escape?

Joshua's eyes darted around, and when they landed on Wilson and Lizeth, it all clicked. They must have brought her back.

The Sherwood couple was glaring at him with unmistakable hatred, eyes burning with fury.

Joshua's heart sank. Camille must have told them everything.

Beads of sweat formed on his forehead, and he couldn't bring himself to meet their gaze, quickly looking away.

But when his eyes fell on Beatrice and Ashley, he felt as if the ground had been ripped out from under him.

What the hell were these two old hags doing here?

He remembered vividly how they had stood by Aurora's side the day Camille woke up.

At the time, he had dismissed them as harmless, but now it was clear. Things were far more complicated.

The woman standing there, dressed like Daisy, was obviously a servant, but the elegant woman seated on the sofa? That had to be Beatrice, Julian's grandmother.

The realization slammed into him like a sledgehammer.

He had unwittingly offended Beatrice.

He could only hope she didn't recognize him, or else Bertha's release would be impossible.

When his gaze finally landed on Aurora and Julian, his back went cold with sweat.

Aurora was here too, seated proudly beside Julian.

How had this happened?

Had she seduced Julian in advance, knowing he had once considered arranging a marriage between them?

Joshua thought to himself.
Yes, that has to be it.

Aurora is nothing but a gold-digging, manipulative woman.

Joshua's mind spun, but he quickly masked his panic, forcing a smile.

"Aurora, you're here too," he said, his tone dripping with false concern. "You've been gone so long without a word. I've been so worried."

Aurora, the illegitimate child he had always despised, had always craved family affection.

He could use that to manipulate her into convincing Julian to release Bertha.

But instead of the softness he expected, Aurora met his gaze with pure hatred.

After reading Daniel's findings, she initially wanted to tear him apart. Now, she wished for him to suffer a fate worse than death.

Today, he would learn the full truth.

She couldn't wait to see his reaction. How did he feel when he discovered his beloved daughter wasn't his own and that all his scheming had only served others?

Aurora smirked. "You worried about me?"

"Of course! I'm your father," Joshua insisted, his shamelessness on full display.

The word "father" drew sneers from everyone in the room.

Wilson looked at him as if he were filth. "Planned to carve up your own daughter, harvest her organs for your fucking mistress' child, and dare call yourself a father?"

Lizeth chimed in, her voice icy, "Strip your daughter of her heart, her liver, everything. You father?"

Daisy added, "Using her body for your damn reputation? You're a total fucking disgrace."

One by one, they laid out his crimes.

Joshua felt as if he'd been struck by lightning, his face pale and his mind reeling.

His eyes darted around the room, panic setting in.

He had always known keeping Camille alive was a mistake.

He should have killed her when she was still in a vegetative state.

Who could have predicted she'd wake up? Damn it!

His face twitched with unease, but he forced himself to stay composed. "I don't know what you're talking about," he said weakly.

Camille let out a cold laugh. "Isn't this exactly what you and Bertha were plotting five years ago?

"So, you wanted to drive your wife insane, looking like the 'loving husband.' Once you controlled Elvador Group, you'd kill her and marry Isabella. And Barnett? You'd have him work for your mistress and her kids, making sure he gets nothing."

"No! You're lying! This is all a setup!" Joshua protested, his voice shaking.

"A setup?" Camille's eyes widened, her emaciated face making her look almost monstrous.

"You pushed me down the stairs, I heard your plan, but I survived."

"I ruined your plans, so you framed Aurora for murder."

She turned to Celeste, her gaze filled with scorn.

"Wow, Mrs. Elvador, you're really something as the wife and mother. You deleted the surveillance footage that could’ve cleared Aurora, ensuring she took the fall for Bertha. Five years in prison—impressive job ensuring your own daughter suffered beyond belief."

Celeste felt as if she'd been plunged into an icy abyss, her entire body trembling.

Learning that Joshua had abandoned Aurora, killed her father, and raised his mistress' child as her own had already shattered her.

But the truth of what had happened five years ago was a hundred times more devastating.

Joshua and Bertha had tried to kill Aurora, and when that failed, they framed her for attempted murder.

And she, Celeste, had helped destroy the evidence and convinced Aurora to take the blame.

Her breathing grew ragged, her heart feeling as if it were being torn apart.

A metallic taste filled her mouth, and before she could stop it, she spat out a mouthful of blood.

Her body swayed, but her remaining eye fixed on Aurora, filled with remorse.

She reached out a trembling hand, as if to touch her daughter's face, to express her regret.

But her vision darkened, and she collapsed to the floor.

Before losing consciousness, she saw Aurora sitting motionless on the sofa, her indifference cutting deeper than any blade.

Aurora wasn't like this before.

It was Celeste who had destroyed their bond.

"Aurora, I'm sorry," she whispered before darkness claimed her.

Aurora didn't even flinch, leaving Celeste lying cold on the floor.

Just then, the door to the living room opened.

Ethan walked in, followed by Bertha, Isabella, and Adolph.

"Mr. Hawke, I've brought everyone you asked for," Ethan said.Julian nodded, a cold smirk curling his lips. "Now that everyone's here, begin the show."
