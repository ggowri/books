Chapter 223 Epilogue: Aurora XVII

Celeste stumbled back to her room, collapsing onto the bed in a haze of despair.

She murmured, "No way. I can't let the Barron family steal Aurora away from me."

Even though they showered Aurora with love now, Celeste remembered the past when their so-called love turned to neglect, leaving Aurora back in the orphanage.

Aurora had suffered enough in that lifetime.

This time, as her biological mother, Celeste swore she would never let history repeat itself.

Yes! I need to get Aurora back. She's still young and impressionable.

If I can just show her love and build our bond, she'll eventually see how much I care.

And then it hit her.

Aurora, the top scorer, would have a big college admission party thrown by the Barrons.

It would be the perfect stage. Crowds of people, influential and curious, would gather in one place.

Celeste planned to step up and announce she was Aurora's real mom.

The Barrons, under all that public pressure, would have to give her daughter back.

With her plan set, Celeste's heart calmed, and she drifted into a restless sleep.

The days flew by, getting closer to the start of the school year.

Three days before the term began, the Barron family hosted a lavish college admission banquet for Aurora.

This time, they didn't choose Celestia Palace as the venue.

Instead, the celebration unfolded within the grandeur of their estate.

Inside the banquet hall, guests mingled in clusters, their laughter and chatter filling the air with a festive buzz.

But the moment Benjamin and Laura stepped forward, leading Aurora into the spotlight, a hush fell over the room.

All eyes turned to the girl at their side.

Aurora, breathtaking in her elegance, commanded the room without effort.

Some gazes held adoration, others tenderness, and a few glinted with calculated curiosity.

At just five years old, Aurora had already secured a place at the prestigious MIT.

What a prodigy! Her future was bright!

The guests, mostly savvy business folks already tight with the Barrons, saw Aurora as an even bigger reason to cozy up. She was a golden opportunity.

Benjamin faced the crowd, his smile radiant with pride. "Today is my daughter's..."

But before he could finish, chaos erupted.

"You stole my daughter and drove my family to bankruptcy! Shame on you!"

The furious roar sliced through the room as Joshua stormed into the banquet hall, his face flushed with rage.

Behind him, a swarm of security guards surged forward, ready to seize him and drag him out.

But Joshua fought like a man possessed, his strength almost inhuman as he wrestled against their grip, refusing to be subdued.

His eyes locked onto Aurora, glinting with a feral greed.

Unbelievable! Aurora, this little bastard, scores top honors in the entire state at just five years old?

She's got my brains. My flesh and blood. Brilliant. A genius.

If he had known this little brat was a child prodigy, he never would've dumped her at the orphanage all those years ago.

A prodigy like Aurora could've brought unimaginable glory to the Elvador family.

When she was five years old, Aurora enrolled in MIT as the top scorer.

The credentials stacked up like a towering monument, rendering her worth virtually priceless.

She was a walking goldmine, her fame already rivaling that of A-list celebrities. Her name was splashed across headlines and trending online.

Now, with the Elvador family bankrupt and desperate, Joshua saw his ticket to a comeback.

All he had to do was reclaim Aurora and squeeze every ounce of worth from her.

And if, down the line, Isabella's kidney disease worsened, they could always harvest Aurora's kidney to save her.

Isabella had kidney disease, and what if Bertha inherited it too?

The solution was to keep Aurora under his thumb as a living organ donor for Isabella and Bertha.

With these vile thoughts churning in his mind, Joshua had stormed into the banquet hall, heedless of the occasion.

Aurora's big day? He couldn't care less.

Selfish to the core, he only ever thought of himself.

Breaking free from the security guards, Joshua fixed his gaze on Aurora, tears welling in his eyes as he painted the picture of a heartbroken father.

He choked on a sob with feigned anguish.

"Aurora, my sweet girl, I finally find you! Do you have any idea how much my heart has ached since you were stolen by traffickers the day you were born?

"Your mom was devastated. She couldn't eat or sleep. I had to adopt Bertha just to keep your mother from falling apart.

"But I never gave up on you, Aurora. For five years, I've searched high and low, and now, seeing you here, I'm just so happy."

As he spoke, Joshua inched closer to Aurora, outstretching his arms.

"Aurora, honey, can I give you a hug? Please?"

He was a master of deception, his sniffling and tear-streaked face fooling many of the unsuspecting guests.

After all, most of them only knew Aurora as the girl the Barron family had adopted from the orphanage in Orley.

They had assumed her biological parents had abandoned her, perhaps favoring a son over a daughter.

But now, this dramatic tale of traffickers and a grieving father turned their assumptions upside down.

"Poor thing," one guest whispered. "So she wasn't abandoned. She was stolen by traffickers."

"Yeah, poor little thing," another agreed. "From the moment she took her first breath, her life was hijacked by cruelty."

"But honestly, I think her biological parents are the real victims here. They waited nine whole months, hearts full of hope, only to have their precious daughter ripped away by those heartless traffickers."

"Can you imagine the agony? It'd shatter any parent's soul."

"They should give her back," someone else cut in. "The Barron family have only had her for a month. The bond can't be that deep. Let her go back to her real family."

The crowd, swept up in Joshua's performance, began to turn against the Barron family.

Some even pulled out their phones, recording the spectacle.

Joshua watched the scene unfold, his heart swelling with joy.

The mighty Barron family has been brought to their knees by public opinion!

As long as these gullible fools believe my sob story, the Barron family will have no choice but to surrender Aurora.

Desperate to revive his fallen family, Joshua was willing to do anything.

He turned to Benjamin and Laura, dropping to his knees with a dramatic thud.

"Mr. and Mrs. Barron, I'm begging you! Give me back my daughter!

"She's my everything. You might not know this, but my wife and I always dreamed of having a girl. I'm a total girl dad, through and through.

"When my baby was stolen, I spiraled into depression. For five years, I've been on meds, barely hanging on.

"But when I found out Aurora was my daughter, I stopped taking the pills. She's my cure and my reason to live.

"Please, I'll do anything for you. Just give me back my little girl."

His words were sweeter than a love song, his acting so convincing he could've won an Oscar.

The crowd ate it up, completely deceived.

But Aurora, who knew the depths of his cruelty, watched him with icy detachment.

Benjamin and Laura, meanwhile, exchanged a knowing look. Their smiles looked sharp and unyielding.

Benjamin sneered. "So, you're saying Aurora was stolen by traffickers at birth?"

Joshua nodded eagerly, emboldened by his own lies. "Yes, absolutely."

After all, no one had seen him abandon the child. He was untouchable.

"And you claim to love your daughter dearly?"

"Of course!" Joshua exclaimed, his voice dripping with faux sincerity. "She's my flesh and blood. How could I not love her?"

Benjamin's smile deepened, like a predator toying with its prey.

Then, he raised a hand and snapped his fingers.

"Well, then, let's take a look at just how much Mr. Elvador loves his daughter."

At his signal, the massive screen behind them flickered to life.
