Chapter 112 The Tedious Performance

Barnett fell to his knees in the mud with a thud, but the sound was swallowed by the thunder.

The howling wind, carrying raindrops, pounded heavily on him, causing him pain. Yet, this pain was nothing compared to the agony in his heart.

Rainwater streamed down his forehead and cheeks continuously. For a moment, he couldn’t tell if it was raindrops or tears on his face.

His screams were torn apart by the wind, and a metallic taste of blood rose in his throat.

He recalled all the harm he had inflicted on Aurora in the past.

The scenes of him humiliating Aurora for Bertha's sake were like needles in his eyes, stinging him with every blink.

Each scene magnified and looped in his mind, tormenting him unbearably.

"I'm such an asshole! How could I treat her like that? Aurora—"

Barnett's heartfelt repentance moved even Darren, who stood beside him.

Darren frowned, feeling both sympathy and a sense that Barnett deserved it.

He thought, If he had known this day would come, why did he act that way? Barnett is miserable, but compared to the suffering Aurora endured, his misery is less than a fraction of hers.

Aurora was about to enter a top university but was wrongfully imprisoned and left with multiple disabilities.

I can't imagine what she had gone through in prison. She was beaten deaf, had a leg broken, and a kidney removed.

How much torment did Aurora endure in those dark days?

Seeing Barnett's pitiful state, Darren couldn't bear it. He advised, "Barnett, Aurora can't hear you. You should go back."

But Barnett, as if out of his mind, knelt on the ground, facing Aurora's room, and continuously slapped himself.

The sound of slaps echoed in the rain.

Soon, Barnett's cheeks became swollen and red.

Darren was shocked and quickly intervened, "Barnett, what are you doing?"

Barnett pushed him away and roared, "Aurora can't hear, but she can see!"

The pouring rain couldn't mask his torrential tears.

"I ruined Aurora, so I must atone. I will wait until she forgives me."

With a face full of sorrow, he continued to slap himself.

Darren stood by, watching the seemingly insane Barnett, unsure of what to do.

Aurora saw all of this.

She wasn't moved at all, only felt that Barnett was putting on a show.

It wasn’t the first time he had done it, but after each repentance, he would still humiliate her for Bertha.

She was already tired of his performances.

The only person Aurora cared about was Julian.

Because of her, Julian had fought with Barnett.

Aurora turned quickly, dragging her heavy leg, and jogged out of the room.

She rushed downstairs, desperate to make sure Julian wasn't hurt.

At the same time, Julian walked in from outside.

His black shirt clung tightly to his body, outlining his chest muscle and his eight packs.

His hair was wet, with droplets continuously falling. Even soaked, he couldn't hide his innate elegance. The rain made him even more sexy and charming.

"Mr. Hawke—" Aurora saw him and quickened her pace, but accidentally missed a step.

Julian's heart clenched.

At that moment, everything around him blurred, only Aurora's falling figure was crystal clear.

He shot like an arrow, breaking through the air, striding swiftly towards her.

Rainwater slid from his hair, across his cheeks, and gathered into droplets at his chin, splashing away with his movements.

Just as Aurora was about to hit the floor, Julian reached out and firmly caught Aurora's waist.

Aurora was lifted by a pair of gentle yet strong hands and sat on Julian's lap.

Julian's other hand protected her back, holding her securely in his arms.

At this moment, time seemed to stand still.

Outside, the wind still howled, and the rain poured, but inside the living room, only their breaths could be heard.

Julian panted heavily, his deep eyes filled with concern and fear. He locked his gaze on Aurora's eyes as if confirming her well-being.

Aurora stared blankly at Julian, so close, feeling his strong heartbeat and the warmth from his body. She gripped Julian's arms tightly as if she never wanted to let go.

Julian keenly caught the worry in Aurora's eyes. And he was deeply moved by it.

His gaze was like a hot iron, firmly locked on Aurora. His hand holding her arm involuntarily tightened.

Aurora, feeling the pain, struggled slightly.

Julian, immersed in complex emotions, snapped out of it and immediately loosened his grip.

Taking a deep breath, he managed to calm the turmoil inside. Then, he slowly took Aurora's hand.

Under Aurora's astonished gaze, he removed his pinky ring, a simple yet exquisite ring that sparkled with a cold light on his fingertip.

Julian slid the ring onto Aurora's middle finger, the action slow and devout as if performing a sacred ritual.

The ring slid over Aurora's knuckles and finally rested steadily on her middle finger.

Aurora's heart felt as if gripped by an invisible hand.

She looked up, her eyes filled with confusion and shock.

"Mr. Hawke..." Aurora's voice trembled slightly. She seemed shocked by Julian's sudden action, yet also suppressing the complex emotions rising from deep within.

Julian gazed at her, his voice deep and powerful. "Wear it, and you are mine. I will always protect you."

His voice echoed in the quiet living room, carrying a power that reassured Aurora.

Outside the window, Barnett was still slapping himself amidst the storm.

But Aurora had no time to care about Barnett. She was overwhelmed by the sudden happiness.

Aurora's eyes moistened. Her heart was a mix of emotions—touched, surprised, but mostly filled with anticipation for the future.

She had noticed the ring on Julian's hand the first time she saw him.

He always wore it, never took it off.

So, Aurora guessed he liked the ring very much.

But now, he had taken it off and placed it on her hand himself.

Aurora wanted to ask Julian what it meant, was it what she thought?

But she didn't dare. She feared it was her own wishful thinking, yet also feared it was exactly what she thought.

Aurora lowered her eyes, feeling that her mood was on a roller coaster. She was happy, but then sad.

Before she knew it, she found herself being lifted.

Julian effortlessly picked her up and step by step, carried her up the stairs.
