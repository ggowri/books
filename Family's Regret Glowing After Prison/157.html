Chapter 157 Food Poisoning

Hubery huddled in the corner by the trash can.

Aurora, I've let you down.
I've gotten what I deserved.

The pain of his legs being crushed, the humiliation of everyone's scorn, and the gnawing hunger from empty days all hit him now.

So this is what you went through.

Back then, to support my college education, you worked so hard during the busiest time of high school, participating in competitions, pushing yourself to maintain the top spot, and winning scholarships.

You gave me all your money.

Now, you're still the same, giving most of your money to me.
Aurora, have you already forgiven me?

Hubery's mind flooded with memories of their time at the orphanage.

At that time, Aurora looked at him with eyes full of trust.

She always followed behind him like a little shadow, and he had vowed to protect her for life.

Tears slipped down his cheeks.

"If I'd known it'd come to this, I never would've studied law.

"If I hadn't become a lawyer, would things between us have turned out differently?"

He rambled to himself for a while, then tore into the bread, shoving it into his mouth.

Aurora, for you, I've got to pull myself together.

*****

Soon, Aurora and Daisy returned to the Hawke Estate.

Beatrice was sitting on the sofa arranging flowers.

Upon seeing Aurora, she quickly set her things aside and smiled warmly, "Aurora, you're back."

Aurora had adjusted her emotions the moment she stepped through the door.

She wore a bright smile, looking genuinely happy.

"Madam Hawke." She greeted cheerfully.

But the moment Beatrice saw her, her smile faltered, worry clouding her eyes.

"Aurora, why are your eyes so red?"

She hurried over, examining her closely.

"Have you been crying? Did someone hurt you? Tell me. I'll take care of it."

A wave of warmth washed over Aurora, nearly bringing tears again.

The ones who care about you notice everything, while the ones who don't never see a thing.

Back then at the Elvador Villa, under Adolph's pressure, she had knelt before Camille's hospital bed, banging her head until blood ran down her face.

Her forehead was wrapped in thick white gauze, so obvious, yet the Elvadors never batted an eye.

Now, with just slightly red and swollen eyes from crying, Beatrice, despite her age, noticed the difference immediately.

Aurora took a quiet, deep breath, pushing down the tears threatening to spill.

She flashed a smile. "A gust of wind blew sand in my eyes earlier. I rubbed them for a while."

Beatrice's tense expression softened.

Aurora's health was fragile, and she always worried some careless fool outside might hurt her.

Still, sand in the eyes wasn't trivial.

"Ashley, grab the eye drops," she ordered.

"Madam Hawke, I'm really fine."

Beatrice wouldn't hear it. She tugged Aurora to the sofa, sitting her down and gently pulling her to lie back, head resting on her lap.

She cradled nearly half of Aurora's body, treating her like her own granddaughter.

Aurora's nose stung, and her emotions swelled again.

Just as a tear threatened to fall, Beatrice dripped the eye drops in.

The cool liquid hit, and Aurora's tears broke free, disguised by the drops.

"What's wrong?" Beatrice asked, alarmed.

Ashley and Daisy crowded around, three pairs of eyes fixed on her.

Aurora brushed it off.

"The drops are cold."

Beatrice exhaled in relief.

"Hang in there, sweetie. I will finish it soon, and you'll feel better."

Aurora smiled up at her, eyes locked on Beatrice's kind face.

Beatrice applied the drops twice, but each time, the eye drops mixed with tears streamed down from the corners of Aurora's eyes.

Puzzled, Beatrice asked, "Why does it keep running out?"

"Maybe my eyes don't hold water," Aurora teased.

Beatrice chuckled, "You silly girl, always teasing me."

Aurora closed her eyes, hiding the storm inside, and wrapped her arms around the old lady's waist.

"You're so good to me, Madam Hawke. I want you to be happy every day."
Even if I'm not here later, don't be sad.

Everyone moves on.

Mr. Hawke is so wonderful.

He'll find someone better, someone right for him.

I'm just a fleeting guest in his life.

She nuzzled into Beatrice's embrace, breathing in her comforting scent, feeling utterly safe.

Beatrice's heart melted at the closeness.

She patted Aurora's back gently.

"I want you to be happy every day too."

"Mm," Aurora mumbled, her voice muffled.

"Did running around all day tire you out?"

"A little."

"Then rest here," Beatrice said, showing no intention of letting her go.

Aurora stayed nestled against her, half her body cradled.

Her frail frame couldn't handle much. Just a few extra steps left her weak.

Under Beatrice's soft coaxing, Aurora drifted into a drowsy sleep.

Once she was fully asleep, Beatrice carefully laid her down, placing a cushion under her head.

Daisy had already fetched a thin blanket, draping it gently over Aurora as Beatrice stood up.

Unbeknownst to the sleeping Aurora, the three women stood by the sofa, quietly gazing at her peaceful face for a long while before tiptoeing away.

"Madam Hawke, what should we make for dinner? Mr. Hawke will be back soon," Ashley asked.

Beatrice glanced at Aurora on the sofa and whispered, "Cooking might wake her. Let's order takeout for Julian."

Ashley blinked.

Takeout?

Has Mr. Hawke ever eaten that?

"What about Ms. Elvador?" Ashley inquired.

Beatrice turned to Daisy. "Aurora loves your oatmeal the most. Add some millet as it's more gentle on the stomach. Daisy, just make some oatmeal. It won't make too much noise."

Beatrice's doting on Aurora hit a new level, leaving Ashley and Daisy stunned.

Although it seemed a tad biased, they didn't mind one bit.

When Julian got home, the family gathered at the table.

Before Aurora sat a bowl of thick, fragrant oatmeal, paired with an egg custard Beatrice had insisted on.

In contrast, Julian's meal consisted of four greasy, salty, calorie-laden dishes, paired with dull, dry pasta.

His eyes widened for a split second. "Did Ashley make this?"

Ashley shook her head.

"Then Daisy?"

Daisy shook her head too.

Julian's eyebrow twitched. "It can't be Grandma, can it?"

Beatrice grinned at him. "I specially ordered takeout for you. These four dishes have great reviews. Try them!"

Julian stared, speechless.

Why order takeout for me all of a sudden?

He glanced at Aurora's gentle meal, and then at the oily dishes in front of him.

He opened his mouth for a moment, then shut it and ate.

Surprisingly, it's not bad. Grandma didn't lie.

*****

Late at night, silence blanketed the house as everyone fell into a deep sleep, except for Julian, who paced back and forth between his bed and the bathroom.

Six trips later, his face was ashen, steps shaky. He plopped on the bed, fuming.

This morning, he had eaten Aurora's pasta and had an allergic reaction to the cilantro.

At night, he had eaten Beatrice's takeout and was hit with vomiting and diarrhea.

Gritting his teeth, he muttered, "Grandma, what on earth did you order for me?"

Growing up, Julian had always had the finest food and clothing, with a touch of germaphobia, practically insulated from bacteria.

That takeout was fine for seasoned eaters.

But for him, it threw his gut flora into chaos.

The fact that it hadn't dehydrated him completely was a testament to his strong physical constitution.
