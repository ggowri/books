Chapter 100 Pay for That

Judy's face turned ghostly pale in an instant.

Just moments ago, in her heated rage, she had only wanted to humiliate Aurora.

But now, facing Julian, it felt like someone had dumped a bucket of ice water over her head. Her feverish mind snapped back to reality, and all her previous arrogance vanished in an instant.

Thoughts raced through her mind as she tried to think of excuses, but her throat felt gripped by an invisible hand, leaving her unable to make a sound.

When her eyes met Julian's razor-sharp gaze, a chill ran from her toes to the top of her head. His look alone made her feel like she was about to be torn.

Judy forced a weak smile and stammered, "Mr. Hawke..." Her voice came out strained and shaky through gritted teeth. "Today is Beatrice's day. I was trying to liven things up."

The women who had helped Judy hurt Aurora finally realized the gravity of the situation, and they smiled nervously.

"Mr. Hawke, we were just joking around."

"Yeah, that's right..."

Julian didn't even spare them a glance. His gaze moved past them to the battered figure lying on the ground.

Aurora's clothes were practically in shreds, with bruises covering her exposed skin. Her face was so swollen it was barely recognizable.

Her hair was spread out messily on the floor, matted with blood and tears, creating a heartbreaking sight.

Despite her condition, Julian recognized her instantly.

His eyes narrowed as his breath caught in his throat. The shock on his face quickly transformed into blazing fury, his gaze intense enough to burn everything in sight.

He swiftly removed his expensive tailored suit jacket, draping it over Aurora's battered body with urgent yet gentle care.

Julian scooped Aurora into his arms without hesitation and instinctively tightened his grip to hold her securely against his chest.

"Mr. Hawke!" Someone gasped, taking a step back.

Everyone stood there with their jaws dropped, watching the normally germophobic and unapproachable Julian wrap his clothes around a blood-covered woman.

Julian's clenched jaw cast a cold shadow under the pearl chandelier's glow. As he bent his long legs, still clad in his suit pants, Aurora's dark red blood immediately began seeping into the expensive fabric.

Amid the collective gasps filling the ballroom, Aurora suddenly twitched in his arms.

Her blood-matted eyelashes fluttered open with effort, and her unfocused eyes found his grim face. She managed a broken smile and whispered, "Mr. Hawke... have you come to save me?"

Her words pierced his heart like an ice pick.

Julian's arms tensed instantly as Adam's apple bobbed twice. He pulled her even closer to his chest.

"Yes," he answered softly, as if afraid his voice might startle her.

Through his sweat-soaked shirt, he could feel the prominence of her shoulder blades, which made her seem even more fragile and pitiful.

Julian's icy gaze swept across the room like a sharp blade, finally pinning Judy down like a nail.

Judy's head started spinning, and her vision went dark as her legs turned to jelly, nearly collapsing.

"You... She..." She stammered, her lips trembling, unable to form a coherent sentence.

"Lock down the premises," Julian commanded, his voice as cold as ice. "No one leaves until I return.

"Everyone involved should consider if their body parts are worth the price of hurting someone under my protection."

As Ethan reached for his radio, Judy lunged forward with a piercing scream.

She had finally realized the gravity of the situation and was all too familiar with Julian's methods.

"Mr. Hawke, please listen! That bitch seduced your father first! That's why I..."

"Seduced?" Julian let out a chilling laugh. "It seems you need another lesson..."

He advanced toward her, forcing her to back up against the dining table. "About distinguishing truth from lies.

"Just like when I taught you the difference between a miscarriage and a hysterectomy."

Judy's perfectly styled curls were now plastered to her forehead with cold sweat. Animal-like whimpers escaped her throat as her manicured nails tore through the tablecloth.

Julian carried his companion toward the side door, leaving drops of blood scattered across the black marble floor. Each step seemed to fray everyone's nerves even further.

His thick eyelashes cast dark shadows as he glanced down, then suddenly turned to Beatrice, who sat pale-faced in the main seat. "Grandmother, I'll need to borrow your silk shawl."

Beatrice's heart ached as she watched the girl she had considered family being oppressed.

She quickly removed her cloak and handed it to Julian, saying, "With Grandma here, anyone who dares to bully Aurora won't get away with it."

"Hmm," Julian grunted in response.

He tightly wrapped the dark red silk shawl around Aurora, completely covering her.

Against the crimson silk, her exposed ankle looked startlingly pale.

In the deathly silence, Julian carried Aurora straight out of the banquet hall.

As the door slowly closed, the last ray of light cast a harsh shadow across Judy's distorted face.

Her ears were ringing, and her face had grown paler.

She finally realized she had misunderstood something, and this misunderstanding might cost her more than she could bear.

She looked at Anderson with pleading eyes and shrieked, "Anderson, that woman is your mistress!"

Anderson's face darkened as he frowned. "What nonsense are you spouting?"

Judy screamed at him like a mad woman, "How is it nonsense? I saw the photos your mother sent you of her, and you said you'd agree to anything as long as your mother approved!

"Your family just thinks I'm barren, so you found someone younger!

"Is it my fault I can't have children? It's clearly—"

"SLAM!"

Beatrice slapped her hand on the table, deliberately cutting off Judy's words.

She had never liked Judy—not one bit.

Her hands trembled angrily as she spat, "I sent it to Anderson because I wanted him to see Julian's future wife!"

Her words hit like a thunderbolt, leaving Judy completely stunned.

The socialites who had helped Judy hurt Aurora were now shaking in their designer shoes.

They were screwed!

Absolutely screwed!

Everyone knew that Julian now ran the Hawke Group with an iron fist.

It was common knowledge that Julian showed no mercy. Anyone who crossed him met a terrible fate!

They finally understood the chilling meaning behind Julian's warning about "considering the value of their body parts."

The ballroom erupted into chaos.

The socialites who had participated in the assault turned pale as sheets.

They begged Beatrice to let them leave, but she wouldn't budge.

Did they think they could walk away after attacking Aurora?

Not a chance in hell!

When their husbands learned what had happened, they ragefully turned on their wives with raised fists. The scene descended into mayhem.

Julian hadn't specified their punishment yet.

But this waiting game only amplified their terror to unbearable levels.

Judy trembled uncontrollably like a leaf in the wind, her mind filled with images of Julian's cruel methods of torture.

Anderson's heart softened at the sight of her distress.

No matter how grave her mistakes were, she was still his wife.

Besides, she had only struck someone younger than her. While it wasn't right for an elder to hit her daughter, it wasn't such a serious offense that required severe punishment.

Anderson sighed and approached Beatrice, saying, "Mom, today is your 80th birthday. We should keep the peace. You know how Julian can be hot-headed sometimes. Let's not get caught up in his drama."
