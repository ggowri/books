Chapter 146 DNA Test

The development of the situation far exceeded Isabella's expectations.

Today, she had eagerly gone to the country jail, hoping that Joshua would bring good news.

She thought that as long as Joshua could convince Julian to let Bertha go, she would be able to joyfully bring Bertha back home.

But instead of receiving a call from Joshua, Isabella was met by Julian's personal assistant, Ethan.

Ethan bailed both Bertha and Adolph out of the county jail, and Isabella happily thought that Joshua had convinced Julian.

The three of them then followed Ethan to the Hawke Estate.

At that moment, Isabella belatedly realized that everything had been carefully planned by Julian.

The purpose was to gather them all together and publicly expose everything that had happened all those years ago.

Isabella's heart pounded in her chest.

The truth was a ticking time bomb, and she knew the second it was confirmed that Bertha and their son weren't Joshua's, their entire family would be doomed.

But Isabella wasn't the type of woman to crumble under pressure.

No matter how chaotic her thoughts were, she kept her composure, her expression betraying nothing.

With a natural expression, she took the document from Joshua's hand and casually flipped through it.

Then, suddenly, she let out a soft laugh, before tossing the document carelessly to the ground.

"Joshua, do you trust me, or do you trust Aurora?" Isabella asked softly, locking her gaze onto Joshua.

Joshua, whose face was filled with anger and on the brink of exploding, was stunned by her words.

He stared at Isabella.

In her eyes, he saw sincerity and what seemed like an unwavering love for him.

Shaking her head helplessly, Isabella sighed, "Come on, haven't you figured it out yet? This whole thing is a trap set by Aurora and the Hawke family.

"Aurora hates us. She resents Bertha for ruining her life. That's why she forged these documents—to drive a wedge between us.

"You can't be deceived by this. If we turn against each other, it will play right into Aurora's hands.

"All these years, you know better than anyone how I feel about you. If I didn't love you, why would I still be single?

"I'm now the head of Orley Central Hospital, and countless successful men have pursued me, even young men in their twenties were lining up, hoping I'd take them in. But I never once wavered.

"Doesn't this prove how sincere my feelings for you are?"

It was undeniable that Isabella's quick thinking and eloquence were extraordinary.

Otherwise, she wouldn't have been able to win Joshua over in just half a month.

She reached for Joshua's hands, holding them gently, her eyes filled with warmth as she continued, "Joshua, I fell for you at first sight, and all these years, my heart has only belonged to you.

"Even though your health isn't what it used to be, I've never thought of leaving you. I truly want to spend my life with you."

Joshua looked at Isabella's beautiful face, listening to her gentle words, and all his doubts vanished instantly.

As his anger faded, the tension in his body relaxed, and only then did he feel the sharp pain in his legs, reminding him that his broken bones hadn't fully healed.

He swayed, and Isabella immediately supported him, helping him into a wheelchair.

Joshua felt her concern for him and gently patted her hand, comforting, "Isabella, I trust you."

Hearing those words, Isabella's heart finally settled.

What a fool!

Only a woman like Celeste, who was obsessed with love, could be so easily deceived by him.

If it weren't for Celeste being born into a rich family and having a father who adored her, she would have been used and discarded long ago.

It was her fault for being so naive.

Whoever was born as her child was just unlucky.

Aurora's miserable life was all because of Celeste's foolishness.

At this thought, Isabella subtly gave Frank a reassuring look.

Seeing Joshua dispel all doubts, Frank couldn't help but smile slightly, an almost imperceptible smile forming on his lips.

Joshua, now looking at Aurora with a look of extreme disgust, coldly laughed,"Trying to drive a wedge between me and Isabella with this little stunt, huh? You've got the wrong guy.

"I've been in business for nearly thirty years. Your little tricks don't even register in my eyes.

"You really are a Jerome through and through—vile to the core. That arrogant face of yours makes me sick."

Joshua thought Aurora would be embarrassed and ashamed by his humiliating words, but instead, she was looking at him with pity.

Her eyes seemed to look at him as though he were a fool.

Joshua couldn't stand that kind of look, as though he were worthless, something beneath her notice.

He came from a poor mountain village, and his family was the poorest in that village.

In that small village, anyone could bully him.

He had had enough of people looking down on him.

Joshua's expression twisted into a snarl.

He shouted, "What's with that look?"

Aurora sneered, "Joshua, you really are beyond redemption. The evidence is right in front of you, but you refuse to see it, blindly trusting Isabella's one-sided story.

"You don't believe the information on the document? Your eyes can't be that blind, can they? Don't you see how Bertha looks nothing like you, but resembles your driver perfectly?"

As she finished, all eyes in the room turned to Frank.

Frank, being just a driver who usually tried to stay out of the spotlight, was rarely noticed by anyone.

But now, with Aurora's reminder, everyone realized that Bertha's nose and mouth were strikingly similar to Frank's.

Joshua froze.

It was Isabella who reacted first, quickly saying, "People always say nephews take after their uncles. What's so strange about Bertha looking like her own uncle?"

Joshua let out a slow breath, his tension easing—only to scoff the next second, "Funny how the guilty always think everyone else is just as rotten."

Aurora's mocking smile deepened.

"Since that's the case, let's do a DNA test."

The moment those words left her mouth, it felt like a spell had been cast, freezing Isabella, Bertha, and Frank in place.

Aurora fixed her gaze on Joshua and said, "You're so sure Bertha is your biological daughter, right? Then why don't you take a DNA test with her?"

At that, Joshua's hands clenched tightly around the armrests of his wheelchair, turning his knuckles white.

His face turned ashen, but he said nothing.

"What's wrong? Are you afraid?" Aurora continued to mock him.

"Or how about we do a test between Bertha and Frank?"

Joshua's heart sank instantly.

Aurora was too confident, so confident that it terrified him.

Once it was proven that Bertha wasn't his daughter, all his actions over the years—hurting his wife, son, and daughter for Bertha—would become a joke.

It wasn't that he didn't want to do the test; he simply couldn't bring himself to do it.

Right then, Julian finally spoke up, "Dr. Lowell is here. Ethan, go pull some hair samples from Joshua, Bertha, and Frank and give them to Dr. Lowell."

At his command, Ethan stepped forward immediately.

Frank's heart sank.
If the truth is exposed, the three of us will never make it out of Hawke Estate unscathed.

No, no...

I have to grab Isabella and Bertha and get the hell out of here.

With this in mind, he clenched his fist and swung it at Ethan.

However, before his fist could land, Ethan grabbed it, twisting Frank's arm with such force that Frank was brought to his knees.

With a crack, Frank's shoulder dislocated, and he screamed in agony, instantly losing the ability to resist.
