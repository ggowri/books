Chapter 221 Epilogue: Aurora XV

Anderson was so shocked that his eyes nearly popped out of his skull.

He knew Julian was a little devil, but he never expected the boy to be cunning enough to frame him for a crime.

Anderson glared at Julian with pure hatred. "You vile bastard—I hope you rot in hell!"

Julian smirked coldly, "I'm a bastard? And what does that make you? Anderson, you should worry about yourself first."

"Yo-you!" Anderson was so furious he nearly passed out.

"If I'd known you'd grow up this wicked," he spat, voice trembling, "I'd have drowned you at birth."

Julian remained unfazed by his outburst. After all, the man was just flailing in his death throes.

That night, the Hawke household was in chaos.

Anderson was arrested for intentional assault, while Judy was rushed to the ER.

Julian lay in bed, staring out the window, completely at peace.

I can't wait for tomorrow. Then I'll see Aurora again.

*****

Half a month flew by, and a lot went down.

First, Anderson was sentenced to five years in prison for intentional assault.

Judy, having lost her tongue, suffered a massive psychological blow and became emotionally unstable.

Julian, ever the "thoughtful" child, even paid her a visit at the hospital.

The moment Judy saw him, she flew into a frenzy, practically foaming at the mouth with rage.

Seizing the opportunity, Julian had her committed to a psychiatric facility.

No one questioned it. They thought she'd gone nuts after Anderson cut out her tongue.

No one suspected that a ten-year-old boy had orchestrated it all.

Half a month later, the Barron household was buzzing with excitement.

Aurora's acceptance letter had arrived.

With her outstanding academic record, she had been admitted to MIT.

Benjamin and Laura clutched the acceptance letter, tears of joy streaming down their faces.

"Aurora really got the highest SAT score?"

"Aurora really got into MIT?"

"God, Julian is a genius!"

The Barrons looked at Aurora as if she were a rare treasure.

But what truly caught them off guard was the swarm of reporters who descended upon their home upon hearing the news.

Cameras flashed incessantly, their blinding lights nearly overwhelming.

Microphones were practically shoved into Aurora's face.

Sure, a perfect SAT score was wild.

But at five years old?

It was insane. A full-blown miracle.

The reporters' eyes burned with fervor as they stared at her.

They could already see the headlines: Prodigy Genius! Future Nobel Winner?

They scrambled over each other for the exclusive.

The Barron residence was packed, the atmosphere electric.

By evening, Benjamin and Laura were exhausted, their throats dry from nonstop talking.

But when they looked at Aurora, their eyes were pure adoration.

They couldn't help but feel grateful for the Elvador family's stupidity.
How could they have abandoned such a brilliant child in an orphanage?

Laura cradled Aurora in her arms, gently stroking her hair.

Look at how brilliant this little mind is.

She said softly, "Aurora, how about we throw you a grand celebration party for your admission?"

Aurora smiled sweetly, playing the perfect little angel.

Finally, I'll have my celebration.

In her previous life, both she and Bertha had gotten into college.

Bertha had only made it into a mediocre school, while Aurora had been accepted into the prestigious Vertar University.

Yet, the Elvadors had only thrown a celebration for Bertha.

During that very party, Aurora had been handcuffed by the police.

From that moment on, their lives had diverged completely.

Bertha became the Elvador family's heir, the darling of Orley's high society.

Aurora, overnight, became a murderer.

This time, not only did she have loving parents and a big brother, but she also had friends—and now, her celebration.

Her greatest regret had been missing out on college, forced to spend those years behind bars instead.

Now, everything was different. She would finally experience university life for herself.

Overcome with emotion, tears spilled uncontrollably down Aurora's cheeks.

Laura panicked. "Sweetheart, why are you crying?"

Aurora hugged her neck tightly. "Mommy, thank you for bringing me home from the orphanage. Otherwise, I'd never have known what happiness feels like."

Laura's heart softened further.

"Don't cry, my love. Whatever others have, you'll have them, too—no, even better."

Benjamin wrapped his long arms around both Laura and Aurora, and William joined in.

The family of four embraced, and the scene was heartwarming.

Yet, once again, the moment was shattered.

"Aurora, I've come to take you home. Come with me."

Celeste's voice rang out from outside the villa.

"You can't go in!" a servant protested.

"Move! Don't stop me! My daughter is in there!"

After a brief commotion, Celeste barged in—only to see Aurora nestled happily in the Barrons' arms.

Her heart lurched. The sight was like a knife to her chest.

Aurora is my daughter. How dare they hold her like this?

In my old life, when I brought Aurora home, she was still in the orphanage.

Why the hell is she in Putnam now?

Celeste's eyes flicked around—then lit up.

So that's what happened.

In her past life, the Barrons must've taken Aurora in and dumped her later.

That's how she ended up back in the orphanage.

If they were just gonna toss her away again, then fine. Celeste would take her back first.

I know the truth now. This time, I won't hurt Aurora like before.

I'll give her all my love, spoil her like a princess, and make Bertha pay for everything she did. Ten times over. No, a hundred.

Celeste convinced herself she was absolutely right.

Then, she shot the Barrons a disdainful look. "You already have a son of your own. Why must you steal my daughter?"
