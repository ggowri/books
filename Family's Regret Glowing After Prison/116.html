Chapter 116 Let Daniel Handle This

Ethan abruptly stood up, the leather sofa creaking beneath him.

His eyes locked on the yellowing medical file on the coffee table. "I'll look into this."

Aurora's suffering struck him deeply. Just hearing her story made him feel like his chest was being squeezed, as if an invisible hand was tightening around his heart.

He couldn't fathom how any parents could be so cold-hearted. He had always known people could be cruel, but this was unimaginable.

If Joshua and Celeste were truly behind this, bankrupting their family would hardly be enough punishment.

Just as he was about to leave, Julian's voice cut through the room, low and commanding. "You go rest. Let Daniel handle this."

Ethan froze for a moment, instinctively turning his head.

Julian was still sitting on the leather sofa, leaning slightly forward, his hands clasped tightly together.

There was a chill in his gaze, a steely determination that made the air in the room feel heavier.

Daniel Jenkins was a man of action, known for taking down a dangerous drug lord's stronghold.

After his military service, Julian, with his sharp eye for talent, recruited him as his personal driver.

On the surface, he was quiet and reserved, but behind the scenes, his skills had proven invaluable in handling some of the most dangerous situations for Julian.

Ethan knew Julian's temperament well. If Julian was letting Daniel take over, then he was truly angry.

With him on the case, the Elvador family's secrets, no matter how deep they were buried, would all be dragged into the light.

Reluctantly, Ethan sank back into his seat.

Aurora felt a suffocating weight pressing down on her chest. She glanced around at the people in the room, each face full of sympathy and concern.

It was too much to bear. Her heart felt like it was being crushed, and each beat brought a sharp pang of pain.

The ache inside her was overwhelming, like a tidal wave crashing over her, threatening to drown her.

She wanted to break down, to let it all out, but she couldn't.

Not in front of them. She didn't want to make them worry.

So, she forced herself to appear calm, to hide the turmoil inside.

Seconds later, she forced a small smile, though it barely reached her eyes. "I... I'm going to my room."

She stood, but dizziness immediately hit her, the world around her spinning. Teetering, her body swayed as if she might collapse.

Julian, Ethan, and Darren were all on their feet in an instant, reaching out to catch her.

Even Beatrice, Ashley, and Daisy instinctively moved forward, their faces full of worry.

But Aurora, with sheer determination, steadied herself.

She looked up and forced a faint smile. "Don't worry. I'm fine. I just got up too quickly."

The smile was fragile, and her body still trembled, but she managed to push past the sofa and headed toward the stairs.

"Ms. Elvador, breakfast is ready. Please eat something," Daisy said softly, concern clear in her voice.

Aurora looked at her, her lips barely moving as she shook her head. "I'm not hungry, Daisy."

Her steps were slow and heavy, like she was walking through thick fog. Every movement felt weighed down, as if the ground itself was trying to drag her back.

Daisy watched her limp away, her heart breaking with each step Aurora took.

It was unbearable to think of the years she had spent in that cold, unloving household, enduring so much pain. Daisy could hardly hold back her tears.

The Elvador family's cruelty had no bounds. To think they were even after her kidneys was unimaginable.

But Aurora didn't go to her bedroom. Instead, she walked straight to the embroidery room.

She stood by the window, the sunlight warming her pale face, but it did nothing to lift the cold emptiness inside her.

Outside, the flowers in the garden were in full bloom, fiery red, soft pink, and pure white. The air was thick with the scent of blossoms, a beautiful contrast to the heaviness she felt inside.

This room had been specially chosen by Julian for her.

He had said, "When you're tired, stand here. Look at the view. Breathe in the fresh air. It'll help clear your mind."

Usually, standing by this window would bring her some peace, the simple beauty of the flowers and the fresh air lightening her mood.

But today, nothing could chase away the storm brewing inside her.

Aurora slowly made her way to the embroidery frame, her eyes resting on the colorful threads. But to her, they seemed dull and lifeless, devoid of any vibrancy.

Her fingers involuntarily grazed the fabric, trembling slightly as memories of the years she had spent in the Elvador family swirled in her mind.

The cold stares, the cruel words, and now, the horrific realization that her own parents might have been plotting to steal her organ...

Each thought was like a blade cutting deeper into her heart.

She picked up the needle, her fingers shaking as she forced herself to focus.

She tried to distract herself by continuing the "The Garden" embroidery, a piece she had just started.

But as the needle pierced the fabric for the third time, a tear slipped from her eye and fell onto the silk.

She wiped at it quickly, but more tears followed, each one uncontrollable, soaking the fabric in a growing patch of dampness.

Then, in her daze, the needle pricked her finger.

Blood welled up, a stark red against the pale silk, mixing with her tears. Then a twisted, painful flower bloomed on the fabric, mirrored the chaos inside her.

Everything she had been holding in, all the pain, all the frustration, all the heartache, finally broke through.

She collapsed over the table, sobbing, as the years of suffering poured out of her in one agonizing release.

Julian had quietly approached and was now standing behind her, watching.

His heart sank at the sight of her shaking shoulders, but when he reached out as though to comfort her, his hand froze midair.

He could feel her pain acutely, yet words failed him, and all he could do was stand there, silently bearing witness to her torment.

Aurora's sobs spilled out, desperate and broken.

"What did I do to deserve this? Why does it have to be me? Why do they hurt me when they know how badly I want love... Am I really that worthless?"

Julian's hand, still clenched by his side, tightened further, his knuckles white with the effort.

He spoke softly, but his words were firm and unwavering. "You're not worthless. You're not the problem. We all care about you. It's them. Everything they did is on them."

Aurora didn't hear him. She was too lost in her grief, crying uncontrollably.

Her sobs echoed through the room, a heart-wrenching sound that seemed to penetrate everyone's soul.

Outside the door, Beatrice, Ashley, and Daisy were standing, helplessly watching the scene with tears in their eyes. Their hearts ached, seeing her like this, knowing all the pain she had endured.

Even in the living room, Ethan and Darren could hear her crying.

Ethan's fists were clenched tight, his body tense with barely contained fury.

"How could the Elvador family treat her like this?" His mind seethed with anger. "Those bastards... they'll pay for what they've done!"

His thoughts were a whirlwind of rage, and with barely a second thought, he stormed out of the villa and into the sleek black Maybach parked outside.

The engine roared to life as the car sped off, leaving a trail of dust behind it.

Meanwhile, Darren stood frozen in the living room, the weight of guilt pressing down on him.

The room felt suffocating, every corner filled with the heavy realization of his past mistakes.

His mind replayed every cruel word, every harsh action he had ever taken against Aurora, and now, it felt like a thousand knives stabbing at his chest.

He couldn't stand being in the room any longer. He turned and left quickly, his footsteps quick and heavy.

*****

At the school gates, Lily stood with an umbrella in hand, her eyes bright with anticipation.

The black Maybach came speeding toward her, its tires splashing through the water on the pavement.

Lily's face lit up, and she waved the umbrella enthusiastically.

But the car didn't slow down. It zipped past her like a streak of lightning, the gust of wind blowing her hair into a mess.

She looked at the license plate, knowing for certain that this was the same car Ethan had driven last night.

He had promised to come by this morning to pick up her umbrella.

Yet, barely a day had passed, and he had already forgotten about her. He didn't even think to slow down as he drove right past her school.

Her smile vanished in an instant, replaced by a cold, steely look.

"So typical," she muttered bitterly. "Rich people are just as arrogant as the Elvador family."
