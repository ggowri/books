He hadn't forgotten another reason for his journey to the West Zone.

He wanted to find Isa, along with Clarissa, Emma, and Austin.

More importantly, he needed to understand Isa's connection to the God of Night.

Rumors swirled that Isa was the God of Night's woman, and even the God of Light had confirmed this.

But deep down, Osborne refused to believe that Isa would betray him.

He was determined to find her and clear the air, but he wasn't in a hurry. He knew he was still too weak.

Twenty years ago, when Isa first left the Blue Planet for Celestara, she had fought to a standstill against one of Lord Malevolence's Nine Dark Sovereigns, Icy Six.

Isa's power was undoubtedly far superior to Jethro's and perhaps even rivaled that of the God of Light—she was certainly one of the strongest warriors in the Human Community.

And then there was the God of Night, the most powerful warrior recognized by the entire Human Community.

Osborne didn't realize that his desire to find Isa wasn't as strong as it should be. The truth was that he had lost confidence in himself.

Faced with the formidable Isa and the even more powerful God of Night, he felt a wave of insecurity wash over him. He wanted to grow stronger before confronting them.

Jethro frowned at this. "Running low on Celestial Crystals? That's indeed a significant problem!"

With this, he reached into his pocket and pulled out the Dimensional Bag that Osborne had just gifted him.
