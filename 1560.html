A flash of determination crossed Osborne's eyes.

"Guy, you'd better think twice before you come after me. If you do, I won't hesitate to turn you into one of my Blood Clan descendants!"

With that thought in mind, he turned and walked away.

The Wild Flame Army boasted a true strength of 500 thousand, but only about 300 thousand were currently stationed around Wild Flame City.

Of those, 100 thousand had been dispatched to the West Zone to support the battlefield, while another 100 thousand had gone to Divine Peak Nine, where they had recently suffered a heavy defeat, losing over 40 thousand troops.

Despite these losses, the number of recruits eager to join the Wild Flame Army was high, and within days, they had filled the ranks again. As a newly appointed centurion, Osborne would likely find that most of his future members, apart from a few squad leaders, would be fresh recruits.

However, the arrangement of his new team would depend on his direct superior, Bruno Borden, who was also from the Fero Tiger Clan and the Borden family.

Bruno was a late-stage Celestial Deity and currently held the rank of chiliarch.

According to Wild Flame Army regulations, every leader of ten thousand had their own designated training ground for their subordinates.

Osborne made his way to Guy's base, which was located in the southern part of Wild Flame City.

He arrived quickly, and after showing his centurion badge, he was allowed to enter the compound.

The base was expansive, capable of accommodating thousands of soldiers for training.

At that moment, several thousand recruits were practicing in the main training area. Most of them were newly joined soldiers, primarily at the Superior Deity level, with a few at the Transcendent Deity level.

Osborne ignored them and headed straight for Guy's office to report in.

Upon his arrival, he found the office buzzing with activity.

A divine power enveloped the entire space, preventing any prying eyes from getting a glimpse inside.

Osborne felt a spark of curiosity and discreetly activated his Satan's Eye, using spatial energy to create an incredibly small dimensional portal between the office and the outside.

This minuscule portal emitted such weak energy fluctuations that it would go unnoticed by anyone without a keen sense.

Previously, Osborne would have found this impossible.

However, after spending considerable time using spatial energy to gather Essence Crystals, his control over space had improved significantly, allowing him to manage this feat.

With the tiny dimensional portal established, Osborne could not only see what was happening in the office through his Satan's Eye but also eavesdrop on the conversation taking place within.

Inside the office, he saw two men sitting and sipping coffee.

One was a middle-aged man radiating an air of authority—Osborne recognized him as Guy.

He bore a striking resemblance to Laird, the general of the City Guard, about sixty percent alike.

The younger man seated across from Guy was Bruno Borden, a chiliarch and Guy's nephew.

Guy spoke casually, "Bruno, has that Lachlan—no, he should be called Osborne now—reported for duty yet? Has he been assigned to my command?"

Bruno smiled, "I just got word that Osborne has reported in! I rushed over to tell you because of it. Thanks to your advance notice, he's been smoothly placed under your command, and I'll be directly overseeing him!"

Guy nodded in satisfaction. "Excellent! Now that he's under my command, I'll have plenty of chances to deal with him.

"Rumor has it he's acquired an ancient god's treasure vault, filled with endless Essence Crystals! We need to find a way to get everything from that ancient vault out of him!"

Bruno's eyes lit up with excitement at Guy's words.

"Uncle Guy, leave this to me! Since he's under my direct command, I can handle him easily!"

Guy shook his head resolutely. "No, Bruno. This Osborne is no ordinary opponent.

"He may have just broken through to the Celestial Deity realm, but he managed to hold his own against Fabian for twenty minutes without letting Fabian land a single hit! Even if you're a late-stage Celestial Deity and have the upper hand in strength, if he wants to escape, there's little you can do to stop him.

"If we fail and he gets wind of our intentions, it will become a real problem. We need to take him down in one go!"

As he spoke, a flicker of ruthlessness flashed in Guy's eyes.

"That's why I've decided to handle this myself. Later, you need to find a chance to send him out on a task in the wilderness. I'll lie in wait here. Given my strength, he might be fast, but he won't stand a chance of getting away."

The two of them continued to discuss their plan, completely oblivious to the fact that Osborne was listening in on every word.

A cold smile crept onto Osborne's face as he processed what he had overheard.

He mused, "Oh, Guy! I wasn't planning to deal with you this quickly. After all, you're a Deity Overlord, and I wasn't entirely sure I could take you down without rousing suspicion.

But if you're looking for trouble and planning to ambush me out in the wild, then don't say I didn't warn you!"
