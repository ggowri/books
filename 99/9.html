Chapter 9

I said, "Within three years, Mark transferred marital assets totaling fifteen million dollars. Additionally, he's suspected of tax evasion and embezzlement amounting to twenty-three million dollars. Combined with his extramarital affair, for which I have solid evidence, I formally request a divorce."

Mark's face flushed red as he glared at me furiously.

"Just how much do you know!" he finally roared.

I didn't answer, simply presenting piece after piece of evidence calmly, along with a thousand high-definition intimate photographs.

Emily kept covering her face and crying, looking like a pitiful little white flower.

She said, "I don't know anything about this. Mark did all of it. He deliberately deceived me. I'm just a twenty-two-year-old college student—what could I do? Besides, I'm pregnant. Arresting me would be abuse of a pregnant woman. I have the right to apply for bail!"

Her defense was pathetically laughable, her excuses so clumsy that even the gallery couldn't help but boo.

But surprisingly, Mark remained silent throughout.

Was he really going to take responsibility for everything?

Seeing him look at Emily's belly, I finally understood. He wanted to protect that child, so he chose silence.

I looked at him and slowly smiled, saying word by word, "Mark, you don't really think Emily is carrying your child, do you?"

Emily's head snapped up, her face instantly turning deathly pale.

Mark hadn't caught on yet and instinctively retorted, "Nonsense! That's my child!"

I sneered mockingly, "You're that confident? Think every woman in the world can't resist you? But you don't know that Emily has been using your money to support her boyfriend behind your back. They've checked into hotels more times than you two combined."

I threw out the evidence—hotel records and photos, crystal clear.

Emily screamed, "Fake! This is fake! I didn't!"

No one believed her anymore. Mark stared at those photos with bloodshot eyes, looking ready to devour them alive.

He roared, "Emily! How have I wronged you? You actually cheated on me!"

A cheating man accusing his mistress of cheating—truly laughable.

Seeing she couldn't hide it anymore, Emily simply admitted it, "So what? I'm just an accessory! I'm still pregnant and can get bail! What can you do to me!"

I spoke up at the right moment, "Is that so? Then how do you explain orchestrating the car accident that caused my miscarriage?"

Her expression changed, "What are you talking about? You were careless and got hit by a car. What does that have to do with me?"

I directly produced the evidence, "This is the record of you paying the hit-and-run driver, these are your chat records with him, and the driver himself can testify."

As soon as I finished speaking, the driver was summoned forward, looking deeply remorseful.

He said, "My son was sick and I had no money. Emily said if I hit you with my car, she'd help pay for my son's treatment. I'm sorry, I really didn't know you were pregnant. I thought no one would die, so I was possessed and agreed."

I stood there expressionless.

But Mark had already broken down, "So it was you! That was Elena and my only child! You murderer!"

Emily wasn't backing down either, "Didn't you only want my child? I was just helping you remove the obstacle to divorce!"

Mark tried to rush forward but was restrained, and the scene descended into chaos again.

In the end, Mark received multiple charges and was sentenced to ten years in prison. Emily, being pregnant, applied for bail.

As we were leaving, Mark suddenly broke free from his restraints and charged out frantically. He pushed Emily hard to the ground. She screamed and fell, blood pooling beneath her.

Now she couldn't escape prison either.

After the verdict, I successfully divorced Mark and, taking my rightful assets, rebuilt my company.

It turned out that Mark's absence didn't have a decisive impact. I continued focusing on technical research and development, applying for countless patents.

During this time, Mark frequently sent me letters from prison, desperately repenting and begging for my forgiveness. At first, I opened a few, but later I didn't even look at them, throwing them straight into the trash.

When invited to give a speech at my alma mater, I stood on stage, looking at the young faces below.

I smiled and said, "Today, I'm establishing a scholarship in my personal capacity to support outstanding students at my alma mater. I hope every young person can shine through their own abilities and forge their own path. When others introduce you, may they respectfully call you by your name—as yourself, not as someone's wife or husband."
