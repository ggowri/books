Chapter 5

The unclosed microphone crashed to the ground, and a piercing screech instantly echoed throughout the venue. Everyone's eyes turned toward the sound.

But Mark couldn't care less about that.

He stared intently at the big screen, then whipped around to face me, roaring: "Elena, what the hell are you doing? What does this photo mean?"

I shot back: "What does it mean? You tell me."

We locked eyes for several seconds. Suddenly, cold sweat began beading on his forehead, and his gaze started darting away.

I didn't give him another chance to speak and redirected everyone's attention back to me.

I announced: "Ladies and gentlemen, I'm here to report Emily for academic misconduct. Her graduation project plagiarized my unpublished research. The people in this photo are the parties involved."

The big screen displayed a couple's photo—fresh and pleasing to the eye, a handsome man and beautiful woman intimately embracing.

The only problem was that the woman was Emily, the outstanding graduate representative who had just received the scholarship, and the man was Mark, a married company executive for many years.

I didn't address their overly intimate relationship but immediately pulled up a document.

I continued: "This is Emily's graduation project that I copied from Mark's computer. The biggest highlight of this design is the code simplification, reducing the bloated architecture by one-third. This approach appeared in my research two years ago."

I then displayed my original files—32 versions of code iterations and 15 versions of research articles, all neatly arranged on screen.

The modification times of both files were clearly visible at a glance.

I said: "Not only that, but my name still appears in the code comments. Emily, when you plagiarized, you couldn't even clean up the watermarks properly. Did you not notice, or could you simply not tell?"

I hit enter, and the filter command quickly compiled all instances of my name in Emily's graduation project—105 in total.

Emily's face turned deathly pale, her lips trembling as she could barely stand.

She stammered: "You're lying! This is fake. I didn't plagiarize."

I smiled faintly and pulled up her graduation thesis interface again.

I said: "In that case, could you please explain what the Fell Theory mentioned on page 21 of your thesis refers to?"

She looked completely bewildered, her mouth opening and closing without producing any words.

Mark snapped angrily: "Enough, Elena! Emily has already graduated. Who remembers specific theories?"

I replied: "Is that so? But the Fell Theory hasn't been published yet. How did Emily know about it and use it in her graduation thesis?"

Emily panicked: "If you know about it, why can't I?"

I sneered: "Because the theory's author is my friend. He showed me his research article before publication. As of now, that article still hasn't been published. What, do you know him too? Have you seen that article?"

Hundreds of eyes instantly turned toward Emily.

Emily's face went completely ashen, swaying as if she might faint at any moment.

She suddenly collapsed into Mark's arms, covering her face and crying: "Mark, I didn't plagiarize. I really don't know why Elena wants to hurt me."

Mark held her protectively, grinding his teeth as he growled at me: "She's just a kid. Do you have to drive her to her death to be happy? It's just a thesis—who cares!"

I walked over and without hesitation delivered a crisp slap across his face.

I said: "Don't care? Then tell me, when would it be worth caring about? When my research results get published by you two in major journals and become Emily's stepping stone to success—would that be worth my concern?"

I then forcefully yanked the necklace from Emily's neck, saying: "This is my mother's keepsake. Wearing it defiles it."

Emily cried out in pain, and Mark's eyes filled with even more hatred as he looked at me: "You psycho! You'll pay for everything you've done today!"

I stared at him coldly: "Is that so? I'll make you pay first!"

As soon as I finished speaking, the academic files on the big screen instantly switched to couple photos.

Every photo of Mark and Emily began cycling through on the screen.

From holding hands to kissing, including deliberately enlarged wedding photos and matching rings.

Each image testified to the fact that Mark had cheated on his wife with Emily.
