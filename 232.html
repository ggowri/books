Osborne's gaze fell upon the pile of shattered statues. To his surprise, he spotted what looked like a staff among the debris.

Clarissa noticed it, too.

Her eyes widened in shock when she recognized the staff. "The Fate Staff!"

"The Fate Staff?" Osborne echoed, his curiosity piqued.

With a flick of his ocular energy, a rod approximately a foot long floated up from the fragments. It had a rustic, ancient appearance, half black and half white as if it had been forged by time itself.

At the top, an intricate figure of a winged deity stood—its features were gone, yet Osborne felt an unmistakable resemblance to Clarissa.

"Clarissa, do you know this?"

Clarissa nodded vigorously. "In my sudden influx of memories, there is some information about it, but it's sparse. All I know is that it's called the Fate Staff, a divine artifact said to have once belonged to the God of Destiny. Beyond that, I'm in the dark."

"Divine artifacts?"

Osborne's eyes went wide. Those were rarer and far more powerful than ancient magic weapons, crafted by no ordinary being—shrouded in mystery.

Some whispered that divine artifacts were formed from the very essence of the earth, while others claimed they were remnants of long-lost deities.

One thing was for sure. Divine artifacts were immensely powerful.

Until now, Osborne had only heard of one—Cain's Blood Cross. Legend had it that Cain was the progenitor of Vampires, heralded as the God of Darkness.

The Blood Cross was his weapon.

The one Osborne possessed, now inked as a tattoo on his chest, was perhaps a divine artifact in its own right, but he wasn't certain.

Yet here lay the Fate Staff—an authentic divine artifact.

A greedy desire welled up inside him; who wouldn't want to possess something so powerful? But Osborne reigned in his ambition, channeling his ocular energy to present the Fate Staff to Clarissa instead.

"Here, take it. It feels like it belongs in your hands. Only you can truly unlock its potential."

Clarissa was taken aback.

"You're giving this to me? It's a divine artifact!"

Osborne's expression softened. "I believe it suits you better. You're the Fatebearer of the Radcliffe family, after all. Perhaps this is its rightful place."

Overwhelmed, Clarissa felt emotions swell within her.

The statues had been Osborne's conquest, and he was the one who discovered the Fate Staff. Everything rightfully belonged to him. If he wanted it, she would have no objections.

Yet, there was an undeniable attraction pulling her toward the staff.

Despite her feelings, she would never have dared to ask for it, fully aware of the value of divine artifacts—especially one as significant as the Fate Staff.

But here was Osborne, willingly offering it to her!

Tears welled up in her eyes as gratitude washed over her. Unable to contain herself, Clarissa stepped closer and enveloped him in a heartfelt embrace, the kind that spoke volumes of her feelings.

She rose up on her toes, leaning in to place a sweet kiss on his lips.

Caught off guard but pleasantly surprised, Osborne responded, their lips entwining as his tongue danced with hers, sending blushes blooming on Clarissa's cheeks.

But she didn't pull away; instead, she melted into the kiss, eyes fluttering shut.

After what felt like an eternity, they finally parted, strands of shimmering saliva threading between them.

With her cheeks still flushed, Clarissa lowered her gaze, unable to meet Osborne's eyes.

"Osborne, we should let everyone know about us. I don't want to hide anymore. I want to shout from the rooftops that I like you—I want the world to know I'm your girlfriend!"

She looked up, her eyes shimmering with hope, but Osborne stood frozen in surprise.

Announcing their relationship to everyone was a topic Osborne had been mulling over himself.

The fourth trial from the Godfather required him to pursue Clarissa and make her his woman.

But the specifics of what constituted "completion" were quite vague.

On the surface, Clarissa was already his girlfriend—he had won her heart. Yet, the fourth trial seemed far from complete.

Osborne even wondered if the trial was only considered finished after they had consummated their relationship.

However, he quickly dismissed this notion.

He reasoned that the reason his trial wasn't completed was that their relationship had not been publicly declared.

To truly finish the task, Osborne needed to make their relationship known to everyone.

Osborne was in a dilemma, unsure of how to proceed, when Clarissa took the initiative to suggest announcing their relationship.

"Clarissa, have you thought this through? Announcing our relationship will bring a lot of pressure. You know as well as I do that your family won't exactly be thrilled about this."

Clarissa bit her lip, determination in her eyes. "Osborne, I've made up my mind. I don't care what others think or say. I want to be with you, no matter what.

"We should announce it! I'm ready to face whatever comes our way. In the past, I didn't have the strength to support you, so I hesitated to push for a public declaration. But now, I'm capable of standing on my own. I can share the burden with you.

"So let's go ahead and make it official!"
