Sebastian's face paled, and his body trembled uncontrollably.

Alexander's piercing gaze seemed to penetrate his thoughts, leaving him feeling exposed and vulnerable.

"I dare not stall, Mr. Hawthorne. I'm truly in awe of your powers.

"Please, just spare me and my son. I beg of you, Mr. Hawthorne.

"Here are the last few locations Guzman visited. I'll send them to you immediately. If I get any concrete information about him, I'll report it right away," Sebastian said, forcing himself to speak.

Alexander grunted, a hint of a smile on his face.

"Fine, I'll spare you this time, considering your cooperation. As for your son, we'll see about that after we find Guzman."

With that, he pressed a palm to Sebastian's forehead.

The Force, which had been lurking and causing turmoil within Sebastian's mind, instantly calmed down.

Sebastian felt a wave of relief wash over him as the previous agonizing pain vanished into thin air.

"I... I'm really healed? It's miraculous!" Sebastian exclaimed, his voice filled with excitement.

He stood up abruptly and shook his head vigorously, marveling at the absence of pain.

Before, even the slightest movement had caused him excruciating headaches.

"Sebastian, I've only temporarily eased your pain. If we don't find Guzman within ten days, you'll suffer double the agony. I mean what I say," Alexander warned, his tone icy.

Sebastian shuddered at the thought, realizing that double the pain would be unbearable.

His excitement and relief were instantly quenched by the realization that he only had ten days.

"Mr. Hawthorne, please rest assured. I will do everything in my power to find Guzman. I will cooperate fully..." Sebastian assured, bowing and scraping to Alexander like a servant.

Alexander dismissed him with a wave of his hand and turned to leave.

He sent the locations Sebastian had provided to Anne, instructing her to have them checked out carefully.

As soon as Alexander was out of sight, Sebastian's smile vanished, replaced by a look of pure malice.

"Damn you, Alexander. You think you're so smart? Just wait until the black robe wizard arrives. I'll make you pay!"

Just then, one of his men came rushing in.

"Mr. Sebastian Tate, big news. There's been a change in the magic powder control. Billy has disappeared, and now Betty, who's been missing for a while, is in charge of magic powder sales.

"Moreover, Betty has publicly announced that she will hold a magic powder exchange meeting in three days to determine the various sales agents for magic powder."

Sebastian was taken aback.

"What! Betty, that traitor. There must be someone behind her!"

Whether they could obtain the magic powder formula during this trip to Loscity would determine the future of the Brilliant Group.

Putting Alexander's matter aside for the moment, he immediately called Guzman to report the situation.

After a half-hour phone call, Sebastian hung up with a very unsightly expression.

Guzman had given two clear orders.

First, Alexander must die.

Second, they must obtain the magic powder formula.

Sebastian immediately issued orders. "Find Betty at all costs. I need to see her."

Then, he returned to the hospital to visit his son, Theodore.

"Dad, are you just going to let Alexander go?" Theodore asked, a hint of unwillingness in his voice.

Sebastian sneered, his face darkening.

"How could I just let him go? But I'm cursed, so I have to make some concessions. Once your uncle's black robe wizard arrives in Loscity, that brat will be finished.

"Don't worry, he doesn't have long to strut about."

Theodore nodded vigorously.

"He must suffer beyond imagination. We need to make it clear that crossing us means a death sentence."

Sebastian's agreement was palpable.

"A mere Alexander, even with his bit of dark magic, is hardly a major threat. Our real enemy now is the magic powder and those who control it.

"Alexander's demise is a given, and the magic powder will be ours."

His eyes gleamed with ruthless determination.
