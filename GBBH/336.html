"Get out of my way. I'm looking for someone!" Alexander's tone was calm yet firm.

The leader of the group was momentarily taken aback, then anger flared in his eyes. "Do you know where you are? This is the Miranda Hotel. You must have a death wish coming here and causing trouble."

Time was running short, and Alexander didn't have the patience for a confrontation. Every minute wasted could mean danger for Madelyn. "Move aside!"

With a powerful shout, he charged forward like a freight train. Several burly men were knocked to the ground, bruised and dazed by the impact.

The leader, wiping blood from his face, looked horrified. "Mr. Lewis, we've got a situation! We need backup now!" he urgently radioed for support, alerting a middle-aged man inside.

Zachary Lewis, taking charge, ordered, "Get all security to the lobby immediately!"

Dozens of uniformed security guards quickly assembled. Alexander walked into the hall, unfazed.

"Stop right there!" Several guards, eager to impress Zachary, surrounded Alexander aggressively.

Alexander glanced at them coolly. "If you don't want to leave on your own, you'll end up on the floor."

Before the guards could react, Alexander struck them with swift, precise blows, leaving them unconscious.

Zachary's expression shifted. He recognized that this intruder was highly skilled. Turning to the older man beside him, Brian Turner, he asked, "Mr. Turner, what's your assessment of his strength?"

Though in his fifties or sixties with graying hair, Brian stood straight, his eyes sharp and his presence commanding—a clear indication of his mastery.

"He's just a fool who thinks he can throw his weight around here. He won't get far," Brian replied, dismissing Alexander with a contemptuous smile.

"I'm here to find someone. Just tell me which room Marcos is in, and I won't cause any trouble," Alexander said, growing impatient.

Brian stood his ground, sneering, "We protect our guests at the Miranda Hotel. You can't just waltz in here and demand answers."

Alexander's eyes flashed with determination. Without another word, he moved forward.

"Stop! Are you deaf?" Brian shouted, charging at Alexander with a powerful punch.

To Alexander, Brian's attack was no more than a child's play. He was in a hurry to find Madelyn and had no interest in a drawn-out fight.

"I told you to move!" Alexander kicked Brian in the chest with lightning speed.

Brian, unable to avoid the attack, flew backward and crashed into the wall with a loud thud.

The onlookers were stunned. Brian was Zachary's personal bodyguard, a champion in multiple fighting tournaments, yet he was easily defeated.

Alexander continued on his path, undeterred. Zachary, now sweating, ordered, "All of you, get him! A million dollars to whoever takes him down!"

The security guards, initially intimidated, were spurred by the promise of a reward. Driven by greed, they charged at Alexander, hoping to overwhelm him with sheer numbers.
