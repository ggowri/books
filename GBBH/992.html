Oliver sneered, unwilling to waste any more words with Asher. With a swift kick, he sent Ryder, still clutching a shovel, sprawling onto the ground.

"Get lost!" he barked. Turning to his officers, he commanded, "Protect this grave! No one touches it!"

Dozens of officers surged forward, forming a protective perimeter around the grave, shielding Lily and the others. The tide of the confrontation shifted in an instant. Ryder's group, once so aggressive, seemed to deflate, their morale crumbling. Even Asher's bodyguards exchanged uneasy glances, their loyalty faltering under the weight of the situation.

Lily and Ann exchanged relieved looks, their despair giving way to cautious hope. What seemed like a dire situation was now leaning in their favor.

"Hmph!" Axel's cold laugh cut through the air, his expression a storm of anger and defiance. "Very well. A mere police commissioner of Loscity dares to challenge the Vance family? Let me remind you—West Cemetery belongs to us now. Even if God Himself descends, this grave will be dug up today!

Axel's brazen confidence sent a chill through the air, leaving Ava and Oliver frowning deeply. It was clear the Vance family was prepared to escalate this conflict to any extent.

Some carried advanced tracking equipment capable of pinpointing sniper positions from bullet trajectories. Mounted grenade launchers and heavy machine guns loomed ominously, ready to unleash devastation.

A reinforced company of more than a hundred heavily armed soldiers formed up, their precision and power undeniable.

Axel's triumphant laugh echoed across the cemetery. "Hahaha! Oliver, did you really think you could stop the Vance family today? This grave will be dug up, and you? You'll be stripped of your title as commissioner! Mark my words—your career ends here!"

Oliver's grip on his weapon tightened, but his hand trembled as he aimed. Sweat dripped from his brow. He was outgunned, outnumbered, and painfully aware of the vast influence the Vance family wielded.

Ashley's small voice broke the tension. "Planes! Look, planes!"

Axel and Asher froze. These helicopters were not theirs, which could only mean one thing: reinforcements had arrived—and they weren't on their side.
