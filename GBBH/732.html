Yinson gave Blaze the order to capture a patient infected with poison and send the patient to the Sakra Society.

There were infected people all over Loscity, and Blaze could have captured one and shipped the person out through a special channel.

However, Blaze's target was Jeff, who was going to be infected with plague and sent to Risun's research center.

Blaze smiled cheerfully at the thought of the tragic fate Jeff was about to face, looking quite smug and excited.

Melin would definitely be heartbroken when Jeff disappeared. At that time, Blaze was going to take advantage of the situation and play his tricks to get Melin for sure.

"Melin, even though you cruelly rejected me, how can I stand you being with another man?

"Especially Jeff. He's just an ordinary police officer. How is he worthy of you? How does he deserve such a pretty and noble woman like you?

"Jeff, you have to disappear!"

Blaze whispered like a madman as he followed Jeff closely.

He was nervous because he was about to do it, his body trembling uncontrollably.

He had a syringe hidden in his pocket with the blood he had taken from his patient. As soon as he pumped the blood into Jeff, Jeff could instantly be infected with plague.

After that, he would send Jeff to the designated place, and he would never come back.

"Mr. Beck, I have a little problem. Can you help me?"

Blaze caught up with Jeff to ask.

He gave a gentle smile, looking innocent.

Jeff smiled as soon as he saw it was Blaze, "Dr. Bates, never mind. Just tell me if there's anything, and I'll definitely help you out."

"That's great!"

Blaze patted Jeff's shoulder. It did help that he'd purposely gotten on Jeff's good side during this time.

He thought, "Jeff's an idiot!"

Blaze despised Jeff in his mind, but his smile was cordial.

"Mr. Beck, we got a batch of specific medicine in our hospital, and I heard it's very useful for the patients, but I can't move it all by myself. Can you help me carry it?"

"Specific medicine? That's great!"

Jeff was instantly excited.

"Dr. Bates, wait for me. I'll hurry up and call for help."

Blaze hurriedly tugged at him and spoke helplessly.

"We can't tell anyone. After all, this batch of specific medicine isn't much, and if everyone knew about it, it would definitely cause looting and lead to a riot.

"So it's just the two of us going to carry it. Only you and me will know about this."

Jeff nodded repeatedly.

"Right. Dr. Bates, you're right, let's go move it."

Jeff couldn't wait to rush Blaze as he wanted to settle this plague sooner rather than later.

"Don't rush. Take it easy."

Blaze was in no hurry. If he hadn't worn a protective mask, his eerie and creepy expression would have freaked Jeff out for sure.

The two went to the underground parking lot of the hospital after getting on the elevator.

"Dr. Bates, where's the medicine?"

"Mr. Beck, take it easy. We can never let anyone see it, or we'll cause a ruckus."

After exiting the elevator, Blaze looked around cautiously.

The underground parking lot was already full of cars. It was silent without a soul in sight.

Blaze had planned for today's action for a long time, and he had chosen a blind side where no one would ever know.

"That's the car, over here!"

Blaze pointed to the white pickup truck in the corner.

He opened the trunk, which was dark and stacked with several large cardboard boxes.

"Mr. Beck, please carry it. It's a little heavy."

Blaze smiled and pointed to the cardboard boxes.

Jeff got excited, and he hurriedly leaned over to hug the cardboard box.

"Hey!"

He exclaimed in surprise as the cardboard box was so light that he picked it up in one go.

Jeff was puzzled as he opened the cardboard box to see that it was actually empty with nothing inside.

"Dr. Bates, what's going on? Where's the specific medicine?"

Jeff asked Blaze when he turned around in surprise. A man dressed in black made a fist towards him.

As a result, a black shadow accompanied by a strong wind came crashing down heavily.

"Ah!"

Jeff covered his head in severe pain, and his eyes glazed over.

Immediately afterward, Jeff felt another sharp pain in his neck, and he could no longer stay awake and felt very dizzy.

As he fell heavily, Blaze opened his mask, looking hideous and terrifying.
