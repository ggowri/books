A few security guards eyed Alexander with ill intent, skepticism written all over their faces.

None of them could accept that this ordinary-looking guy could possibly be a resident—especially since he didn't even have a car.

They had already reported his identity to their supervisor and were now waiting for a response.

Moments later, Ottilio Sinclair, the manager of Grand Hyatt Estates, came rushing up, visibly out of breath.

"Mr. Sinclair, what brings you here personally?" one of the guards stammered.

The head guard felt a knot tighten in his stomach. He thought, "This guy's really a trouble! Otherwise, Mr. Sinclair wouldn't have come personally!"

"Surround him—don't let him get away!" the head guard barked.

Several others moved in, some brandishing tasers while others had their hands resting on their holsters.

Alexander stood his ground, unfazed.

Ottilio shouted as he sprinted over, delivering a swift kick to the leading guard.

"Stop! You all back off! Can't you see this guy's a respectable owner? Ready to throw away your jobs?"

The security team froze in shock.

"Get out of the way! This is Mr. Hawthorne, the most esteemed resident at Grand Hyatt Estates," Ottilio scolded, his voice laced with authority.

Amid the shocked, fearful glares of the guards, Ottilio ushered Alexander inside.

Alexander didn't give the fiasco a second thought; to him, those guards were no more significant than ants.

Meanwhile, Xavier had taken the stunning Jennifer Minogue back to his place. The moment they stepped through the door, they wasted no time getting cozy.

In seconds, Xavier had Jennifer wrapped in his arms, their lips moving wildly together. Clothes were shed, and soon, they were entangled on the floor in a passionate heap.

Just a few minutes later, Xavier pulled back, still breathless, and the two cuddled up, chatting away.

"Darling, do you know that guy from earlier?" Jennifer asked softly, her eyes sparkling with curiosity.

Xavier gave a mischievous grin, puffing up with pride.

"He's just a high school classmate of mine—total loser. I just had to mess with him."

Jennifer giggled. "Mr. Ulysses, you're such a bad boy!

"That guy really cracked me up thinking he could claim to be a resident here. With how he dressed, he'd fit right in as the janitor!"

Xavier roared with laughter, relishing his moment of superiority.

"Speaking of which, I heard there's a billion-dollar mansion around here—something called the Grand Hyatt Estates' Majestic Manor. Is that true?"

Jennifer's eyes sparkled with envy.

The villas at Grand Hyatt Estates had been categorized into three tiers: high, medium, and low. The Ulysses family's villa had fallen into the lower mid-range, valued at tens of millions.

The high-end villas had, on the other hand, started in the hundreds of millions. Among them, the crown jewel had been a mansion known as "Grand Hyatt Estates' Majestic Manor," worth one billion dollars. This property had been an asset of the Saint Group.

Xavier's expression shifted slightly. His family's villa was nowhere near that level.

"Sure, the Grand Hyatt Estates' Majestic Manor exists, but a billion dollars is pure folly. Who in their right mind would buy that?" he scoffed.

"Mr. Ulysses, I'd love to see the Grand Hyatt Estates' Majestic Manor. Could you take me?" Jennifer begged, eyes wide with eagerness.

"What's so special about it? It's just a house; how good can it really be?"

Xavier frowned, reluctant to agree.

The truth was he couldn't take her there. Grand Hyatt Estates had rules against trespassing into private territories.

The Grand Hyatt Estates' Majestic Manor was perched on the mountain, taking up half the picturesque hill that surrounded the estate. That area was strictly off-limits.

There were harsh penalties for invading private property, including being shot on sight by patrols.

Security at Grand Hyatt Estates was split into outer and inner circles.

The outer circle of security was made up of regular guards, the kind Xavier could easily boss around.

But the inner circle? That was a different story. Comprised of retired military personnel and mercenaries, they were heavily armed and didn't hesitate to use their firepower.

The owner of the Grand Hyatt Estates' Majestic Manor was someone far beyond Xavier's reach.

If he crossed the line, it could spell disaster for the entire Ulysses family.

"Come on, Mr. Ulysses! I just want to broaden my horizons a little.

"Take me to see it, will you? I promise I'll make it worth your while. Whatever you want, I'll be all yours when we get back," Jennifer purred, snuggling closer to Xavier and batting her lashes.

Her playful promise sent a thrill through him, and he felt his resolve start to crumble.

Xavier mused, "Well, I've heard that the Grand Hyatt Estates' Majestic Manor has been empty for ages. Maybe it wouldn't hurt to take a peek. Plus, it could score me some serious points with Jennifer."
