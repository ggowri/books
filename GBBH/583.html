This sleazy idiot wasn't worth her time at all.

Even though her relationship with Nancy wasn't great, she genuinely felt for her half-sister.

"By the way, Clay, why didn't you come back with Nancy?" Richard asked, concerned.

"Well..."

Clay's smile vanished instantly.

"Nancy's been a bit busy... She asked me to come ahead and keep you company. She should be here soon..."

"Heh, but I saw her driving back with a guy. I wonder what their relationship is," Sophie chimed in, a playful smile on her face.

"What!?"

Clay's expression dropped in shock.

His heart raced. Nancy wouldn't actually bring Alexander back, would she?

That would be a total slap in the face!

Richard frowned. "Nancy came back with a guy? Sophie, are you sure?"

"I'm positive. I even followed their car. I caught a quick glimpse—they looked pretty cozy. The guy's definitely better looking and more charming than Clay," Sophie said, pointing at Clay without holding back.

"I'm so mad..."

Clay's face turned red, utterly humiliated in front of everyone. He felt like he could explode.

Although he'd been pushed around by Alexander a few days ago, he had trained hard since then and was confident he could take him on now.

Anyone who dared to steal his woman had to pay!

Luther thought for a moment before chuckling,

"Clay, don't worry. I know Nancy. She's always been proud and never pays attention to guys. Even a young stud like you struggles to get her attention, let alone anyone else.

"I think maybe Nancy did this on purpose to test you."

"Yeah, right. It's definitely a test," Clay agreed, though his expression grew more grim.

The other day when he called Nancy, she was with Alexander.

Just thinking about Nancy's beautiful body being with him made Clay's heart ache.

"Where are my daughter and her man?"

A striking middle-aged woman, supported by a couple of aides, hurried over.

She was Nancy's mother, Gill. Since Luther had a love child, she had been a bit off her rocker.

Gill often had episodes where she wouldn't recognize anyone except for Nancy.

"Slow down, darling. Be careful not to trip," Luther said, quickly rising to help her.

After all, they were married, and her mental health issues were partly his fault, so he felt guilty and always tried to accommodate her.

"Mom, I'm Nancy's man, Clay," he said, moving closer.

"You? Really my daughter's man?" Gill scrutinized Clay from head to toe.

"Yes, yes! I'm your daughter's man, Clay..." Just then, Gill slapped him hard across the face.

The slap left not only Clay but everyone else frozen in shock.

"You liar! You're just a country bumpkin with no looks and no charm. How could you be my daughter's man?" Gill shouted angrily.

Clay was speechless, unable to retaliate. He could only take a deep breath to suppress his rage.

He forced a smile. "Mom, I really am Nancy's fiancé. We're getting engaged today."

"Pfft!"

Gill spat on his face in disgust. "I don't agree with this marriage."

"Haha!"

Sophie burst out laughing, unable to hold it in.

Clay's face turned green, but he didn't dare to fight back.

"Huff!"

Clay took another deep breath, reminding himself this was a test from above.

He absolutely couldn't lose his cool—he had to keep a gentleman's smile.

"I really am your daughter's future husband..."

"Shut up!"

Another hard slap landed on his face.

"Hehe..."

Sophie couldn't help but giggle, covering her mouth.

Clara quickly pulled her daughter aside, trying to hush her, but she struggled to hold back her own laughter.

Luther's face turned red with embarrassment as he said,

"Clay, I'm really sorry. Please don't take it personally. You know how my wife's illness is—it's hard to treat."
