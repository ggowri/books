The injury from the war had lingered for decades.

He had visited countless renowned hospitals and specialists, but none could help.

Now, as he aged, the injury worsened, and he was running out of time.

Worse, the pain flared up daily, making life unbearable.

"Sir, the root of your problem is in your brain, an old injury. With current medical technology, the pain can only be alleviated, not cured.

"Recently, your condition has worsened. It's splitting headaches, sleepless nights, and declining health. Am I right?"

Alexander spoke calmly.

"How do you know that?! Did Lara tell you?"

Ralph looked at him, utterly shocked.

Lara quickly shook her head. "Grandpa, I didn't say a word, really!"

Her expression was serious and determined, clearly not lying.

Ralph stared gravely at Alexander. His condition was known only to his closest confidants, not even most of the Hansen family.

Could Alexander really be that skilled?

Alexander turned to Quentin and said confidently, "Mr. Thompson, I can ease Sir Hansen's pain and extend his life. Can you keep your word?"

Quentin's eyes lit up, and he answered firmly, "Rest assured, as Commander of the Northern Command and a four-star general, I always keep my word!"

Ralph gave a self-deprecating smile.

"Are you two using this old bag of bones for an experiment?"

Alexander placed his hand seriously on Ralph's shoulder, slowly channeling the Force.

Through Force Sense, he detected a tiny fragment lodged in Ralph's right brain, impossible to remove with current medical technology.

It was a miracle Ralph had survived for decades despite it.

Although Alexander couldn't remove the fragment, he could encase it with the Force, reducing Ralph's pain and prolonging his life for some time.

Alexander didn't plan to relieve all the pain at once. People rarely cherish what comes too easily.

So, Alexander deliberately stimulated Ralph's brain tissue with the Force, causing mild pain to keep him cautious.

Lara stood by, wide-eyed, not daring to even breathe.

Quentin also watched Alexander's actions intently. If he could really help Ralph, there was hope for his bedridden grandson too.

Before long, Ralph felt a warm current fill his head. It's a strange sensation, both hot and itchy.

It felt like a swarm of ants crawling inside his head, but the itchiness also eased the pain considerably.

The discomfort gradually faded, replaced by an increasing sense of comfort and lightness, as if he were floating.

Eventually, Ralph fell asleep from the comfort, even letting out a soft snore.

Lara's face lit up with joy. Her grandfather hadn't slept well in a long time; it was clear that asking Alexander to treat him was the right decision.

After a while, Alexander withdrew his hand, stopping the flow of the Force.

Ralph felt the warm current recede like a tide, abruptly waking him.

He touched his head, deeply moved.

"Thank you, Mr. Hawthorne. I feel much better. You truly have a gift."

There was a trace of reluctance in Ralph's eyes. The comfort he'd just experienced was addictive, making him feel much lighter.

"Sir, with monthly treatments, living a few more years won't be a problem."

Alexander spoke confidently, exuding an aura of authority.

Quentin's eyes narrowed. This young man was truly skilled and unfathomable.

"Mr. Hawthorne, my grandson is bedridden. If you could help ease his suffering, I promise the military will buy the magic powder for no less than half the market price!"

Quentin promised earnestly.

"Alright, but I expect you to keep your word, Mr. Thompson. Also, I demand severe punishment for Schroder!"

Alexander said expressionlessly.

Seeing Alexander's cold gaze, Quentin, a battle-hardened general, felt a sudden chill.

He was a four-star general, forged in the fires of war, yet here he was, intimidated by a man in his twenties. It was simply unbelievable.

Quentin wasn't mistaken; if Alexander wished, he could take his life at any moment!
