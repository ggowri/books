Alexander was still on his way to the trading center.

Xavier said with a serious expression, "Alexander, those mafias will definitely retaliate against us. We must be careful."

The Mafia was already vindictive in the first place, let alone being humiliated in front of so many people.

"Whatever. Come as they may, and I'll slay them all!"

Alexander smiled confidently and continued to stride forward while admiring the exotic scenery on both sides of the road.

Suddenly, Alexander stopped and stretched out his arms to stop Xavier and Alice behind him.

A dozen people rushed over from all directions to them, like a pack of wolves sieging their prey.

Those ferocious men in uniform camouflage combat uniforms each held a submachine gun that aimed at Alexander and the other two.

They were simply so outrageous.

"Let's go!"

Hugging Alice with one hand and grabbing Xavier's arm with the other, Alexander turned around and rushed into an alley.

Their enemies outnumbered them and were armed with automatic weapons.

Although this was not even an intimidation to Alexander, if a fight really broke out, Alice and Xavier would definitely be in danger, especially Xavier, who was mediocre at fighting.

As he reacted to the threats so swiftly, those armed men were stunned.

Their original plan was to encircle them and shoot them all; it was a very secure plan.

But Alexander anticipated their tactics and ran away with his companions in advance.

By the time the dozen or so Mafia shooters realized his escape, Alexander had already led the two men to run dozens of meters away and still kept speeding.

"Now, shoot them!"

Someone anxiously gave orders.

Soon, the sound of gunshots broke out.

More than a dozen submachine guns were pointed at Alexander, and they put the whole street in the shower of bullets regardless of the innocent people's safety.

To avoid the bullets, the traffic on the street was in chaos.

It offered Alexander a good chance to cover them in the flood of the cars.

In the face of the indiscriminate fire from fully automatic weapons, traffic was clearly the best cover.

"Mr. Hawthorne, you're so swift."

Alice was surprised. Being held in Alexander's arms, she felt like she was flying as she moved flexibly among the traffic.

Things might not be so pleasant for Xavier. After all, Alexander only dragged him to shuttle back and forth between the cars.

He was keen on drag racing with his pals since the unlimited speed could bring him delight. But never did he experience such horrifying speed and swerve.

In the chaos, Alexander was both fast and nimble, just like a fish in the water, and easily dodged the bullets.

More than a dozen submachine guns were still firing intensively.

The terrifying shower of bullets was so dense that they forced a black Rolls-Royce to stop on the side of the road.

"Don't kill me! I'm just a driver! Please don't kill me, please..."

The driver opened the car door and rushed out, shouting for mercy as he ran away crawling.

The Rolls-Royce was left there, and there seemed to be a woman sitting in the back seat of the vehicle.

Without any hesitation, Alexander stuffed Alice and Xavier into the car and then sat in the driver's seat himself.

"Who are you?" The woman in the backseat asked seriously.

Turning around his head, Alexander saw a stunning woman in her late twenties who was dressed sexy.

Alexander said calmly, "Excuse me, can I borrow your car for a while?"

Despite her slight nervousness, she didn't yell for help but fixed her eyes on him, looking surprisingly calm.

Alexander squinted his eyes and could tell she was not an ordinary person.

But now was not the time to probe into her identity. Alexander shifted gears and stepped on the accelerator. As the engine roared, the car rushed out like a steel beast.

Those Mafia shooters failed to catch up with them and could only watch their targets disappear.

"Idiot! How can you let them get away?"

Paxton hysterically yelled at his subordinates.

He looked somewhat similar to Ian but was like a fiercer version of him. Anyone could tell his cruelty at first sight of him.
