The crowd exchanged glances, incredulous that anyone would consider a mere rock worth 10 million dollars.

Even among the wealthy attendees, no one was eager to drop that kind of cash on something they wouldn't use.

"You don't have to buy it; I've got it covered..." Alexander began, but Ava interrupted him with a playful smile.

"Well, if you're so determined to have it, I'll buy it for you as a gift. You can't refuse my generosity; it's the thought that counts!"

A warmth spread through Alexander as he felt her kindness wash over him.

Guests around them shot him envious glances, whispering about how lucky he was to have a woman willing to spend money on him. After all, ladies like Ava were a rare find.

David's gaze turned icy as he watched Ava.

The auctioneer, clearly excited, called out, "Ten million!

"We have a bid of ten million from this lovely lady. Is there anyone willing to raise the stakes?"

David hesitated, weighing his options.

Houtte, seated beside him, leaned in and whispered, "Mr. Federer, if you drop ten million on a rock, some folks from the Federer family will definitely question your judgment. Don't worry; once the auction wraps up, I'll make sure Alexander knows who's boss."

Finally, David took a deep breath, suppressing his frustration, and nodded.

"Ten million, first call, second call, third call... sold! Congratulations to this lady for securing the meteorite at 10 million dollars!"

The auctioneer's voice rang out with enthusiasm.

Originally estimated to sell for six or seven million, the meteorite had fetched a staggering price. The auctioneer's commission would be quite the bonus.

Alexander glanced at Houtte, thinking, "This guy's been cozying up to Orion, and now he's whispering to David. He's definitely up to no good."

As the auction continued, Alexander found little interest in the subsequent items and refrained from bidding.

Just when impatience began to creep in, the auctioneer presented a red tray.

At the center of the tray lay a yellowish gemstone, roughly the size of a ping-pong ball.

Its rich, pure luster was interwoven with shimmering golden threads, a true masterpiece.

Alexander felt a surge of excitement, sensing the earth's energy radiating from the gem.

This was the kind of stone worthy of crafting a Lightsaber.

"Next up, we have our most important item: a natural, top-quality yellow gemstone. The starting bid is set at 30 million dollars, with a minimum increment of 500 thousand!" the auctioneer announced, his enthusiasm infectious.

The atmosphere in the room shifted, excitement palpable as the gemstone made its appearance.

Such rare treasures were seldom seen and were often regarded as family heirlooms.

The wealthy attendees were eager to bid, driven by both pride and desire.

"Thirty-one million!"

"Thirty-two million!"

"Forty million!"

In the blink of an eye, the price had skyrocketed by 10 million.

Yet, Alexander remained calm, watching the proceedings without raising his paddle.

Ava leaned in, puzzled. "Aren't you going to bid? You've been eyeing this for a while."

Alexander smiled serenely, glancing over at David's side of the room.

"David and Houtte are in the mix. If I jump in now, it'll only fuel their fire. No need to rush; let's see how this plays out."

Before long, the price climbed to 60 million.

Many bidders began to drop out; even for the wealthy, 60 million was a hefty sum.

David turned to Alexander with a mocking tone. "I heard you've had your eye on this gem for ages.

"Well, I'm not letting you have it. Sure, you've got that magic powder, but with such a short sales history, you're probably looking at a profit of just over 100 million. You can't outbid me!"

In David's eyes, if the price got too steep, even Ava wouldn't back Alexander.

Alexander watched him with a cold, detached gaze, a sneer of contempt appearing on his face.

He finally raised his paddle, his voice calm but carrying an unmistakable weight. "One hundred million."

The jump of 40 million in one go left the room gasping—everyone sucked in a sharp breath. A collective murmur rippled through the crowd. They thought, "Is this some kind of joke? Who would casually throw around that kind of money like it is nothing?"

But what they didn't understand was that this gem wasn't just another shiny rock. To Alexander, it was the key to unlocking a massive power boost. Once he had enough to forge the Lightsaber, his strength would soar to unimaginable heights.

This gem was priceless in Alexander's eyes, a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity. If he missed out on it, who knew how long he'd have to wait?
