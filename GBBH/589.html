Alexander grabbed Clay by the neck, lifting him effortlessly like an eagle snatching a chick.

"Speak! How did you hurt Gill just now? If you don't, you're toast!"

Clay kept quiet.

Alexander tightened his grip, and Clay's face started to turn blue as he gasped for air.

"I'll count to three. If you don't spill, it's game over for you."

"One!"

"Two!"

As Alexander counted, his gaze grew colder.

Clay trembled uncontrollably, knowing Alexander wasn't joking; he could snap his neck in an instant.

"I... I used dark magic and cast a curse..."

Gasps filled the room!

The Reynolds family was stunned, realizing Clay was indeed the real culprit.

"Clay! How could you do something so heartless? I seriously misjudged you!"

Luther was furious.

He even slapped himself, nearly mistaking this scheming guy for his daughter's husband.

Clay's attack on Gill was beyond vicious.

Luther and Nancy were seething, but Richard was even more enraged and felt utterly ashamed.

"Clay, you deserve to die! You really do!"

The old man shouted angrily.

Alexander held Clay up, still gripping his neck.

"Please don't kill me... I... I was wrong. I'll leave Kingsdon right away..."

Clay flailed helplessly, struggling in vain.

He was gasping for air, like a fish out of water, growing weaker by the second, his face darkening from lack of oxygen.

"Sigh..."

Richard sighed deeply and cautiously addressed Alexander, "You can't kill him here; otherwise, the City of Sorcery will hold a grudge against us. He's not just Rock Rossetti's apprentice, but also his illegitimate son. If he dies, Juan won't let it go."

Alexander shot him a cold glance and scoffed, "Are you saying the Kaylon is afraid of the City? Being bullied like this and still acting so weak—clearly, you're getting old."

Richard's face flushed with embarrassment.

"Right, right, I'm getting senile. I don't want the Kaylon to make a powerful enemy!"

Alexander remained unfazed, tightening his grip further.

"Ugh!"

Poor Clay's eyes bulged; he was on the brink of passing out.

Richard could only fret, unsure of what to do.

Luther quickly said to Nancy, "Nancy, your engagement to Clay is definitely off, but starting a feud with the City of Sorcery isn't necessary."

After a moment of contemplation, Nancy finally spoke, "Fine, let him go, but he must leave Kingsdon for good and never bother me again."

Alexander grinned, "What's this? Feeling sorry for him?"

"What? What nonsense are you talking about?"

Nancy tugged at Alexander's arm in frustration.

Then she leaned close to his ear, blushing as she whispered, "If it helps, I'll serve you with Rosalia tonight. Whatever you want to try, I'm game."

Alexander licked his lips, feeling a bit tighter in his pants.

Nancy was undeniably an enticing woman; even if she wasn't as accommodating as Rosalia, she was more than enough to leave any man wanting more.

If she was all in... Alexander felt a stir of interest.

"Thud!"

Alexander slammed Clay down to the ground like tossing out trash.

"Huff! Huff!"

Clay lay on the ground, gasping for air, still shaken.

Moments ago, he thought he was about to die, his vision fading and even losing the ability to feel pain. He never expected to still be alive.

"Hahaha!"

Once he caught his breath, Clay suddenly erupted into manic laughter, a smug smirk on his face.

"You all are still afraid of the City of Sorcery! Just wait and see, this isn't over. I won't let you off the hook."

Clay felt emboldened.

Before Alexander could move, Nancy delivered a powerful kick to Clay's face.

The heel of her high-heeled shoe dug into his flesh, causing blood to gush as he howled in pain.

"Bitch, you dare to mess with my looks?"

In response, Nancy pulled out a gun and fired two shots.

"Ah!"

A piercing scream echoed as Clay's knees were punctured, rendering his legs useless.
