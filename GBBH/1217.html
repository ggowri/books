"With you on the team, I'm confident we'll be unstoppable! We'll secure the raw Immortality Serum and develop a plan after studying it."

Quentin clenched his fists in excitement.

"Let's go. See you during the operation."

Alexander left the room, with Quentin hurrying to follow behind him.

After leaving the room, Alexander arrived at the gathering spot where the team for the night's operation, including Ilona and other advanced metahumans, had assembled.

"Alexander!" Brittany's presence at the gathering was unexpected.

"Stay back at the base and wait for us. We'll return victorious."

Alexander smiled and patted Brittany's head. Without a word, she threw herself into his arms.

The rest of the team watched the scene, their faces filled with envy toward Alexander.

Ilona, too, looked at Brittany with a trace of envy. She had always admired strength, and Alexander was the strongest person she had ever encountered.

The power Alexander had shown was already terrifying, and Ilona believed it was just the tip of the iceberg.

Because of this, Ilona not only admired Alexander but also felt a growing affection for him.

Alexander glanced at everyone, and the team immediately stood at attention, not daring to upset him.

A few of the metahumans offered awkward smiles, clearly nervous.

"Alexander, there's something I need to tell you," Brittany said.

Still hugging him, Brittany leaned in and whispered in Alexander's ear.

"What is it?" Alexander asked, puzzled.

Brittany said seriously, "Alexander, there might still be trouble from Gael's side!"

"Oh? What trouble? Didn't their leader, Ilona, already admit defeat? Could it be that she's still unwilling to let it go?"

Alexander hadn't expected the matter to remain unresolved.

"Gael has an older cousin named Jonah Jimenez, and he's said to be even stronger than Ilona.

"Unlike Gael, Jonah is ruthless, extremely protective, and values his pride. I heard he was furious when he found out you humiliated Gael in public.

"So, you can bet Jonah will come looking for trouble."

Brittany frowned, looking at Alexander with concern.

After all, with the mission about to start, any additional complications could spell disaster.

But after hearing her out, Alexander simply chuckled, brushing it off.

Seeing Alexander's nonchalance, Brittany grew anxious.

"Alexander, you really need to be careful. Don't let something small trip you up."

Alexander smiled and patted Brittany on the back, reassuring her.

"Don't worry. I'm not someone who's easy to deal with."

"Are you seriously not worried at all? Not even a little? Jonah is even stronger than Ilona!"

Brittany was speechless.

"There's no need to worry. No matter how strong he is, he's no match for me."

Alexander spoke with full confidence—not out of arrogance, but because he trusted his own abilities.

"But..."

Brittany wanted to say more, but Alexander cut her off.

"Trust me, it'll be fine. If Jonah tries to provoke me, I'll make sure he regrets it for the rest of his life."

Alexander smiled calmly, showing no sign of feeling pressured.

"Alright, I trust you," Brittany said, finally giving in.

At that moment, the sharp sound of boots striking the floor echoed through the air.

A man in his forties appeared, staring coldly at Alexander.

In his hand, he carried a wide-bladed sword over three feet long. Its sharp, gleaming edge radiated an ominous killing intent.

"Jonah, what do you think you're doing? The mission is about to start. Are you seriously trying to stir up infighting now?"

Seeing the man clearly looking for trouble, Quentin questioned him sharply.

"General Thompson, my brother Gael was injured. I'm here to ask you to seek justice for him."

Jonah spoke with his head lowered, while a cold, sharp aura radiated from his body.

"I was there when it happened... and honestly, I think Gael brought it on himself. You can't blame anyone else, and I suggest you stop listening to baseless rumors that undermine our unity."

Quentin warned him sternly.

Even though Jonah was stronger than Ilona, Quentin wasn't going to allow him to stir up trouble at such a critical moment.

The mission was about to begin, and infighting would greatly harm their team's strength and morale.
