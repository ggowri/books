Cheers and applause erupted from the crowd below, leaving Jalen's throat hoarse from shouting.

Logan covered his ears, scoffing, "This is bullshit. There's a hidden door under the cabinet that leads to the stage. It's so obvious."

Alexander smiled, knowing that for most people, this was all new. It wasn't like him and Logan, who were used to such tricks.

As the finale wrapped up, the show came to an end. Alexander herded Logan and Jalen towards the gate of the yard to wait.

Logan looked at Alexander with sympathy. "If you really like him, I support you."

Alexander rolled his eyes. "I'm just grateful. The pills he gave me worked wonders and helped me break through to level two as a warrior. Am I not allowed to thank him?"

Soon, only a few people remained, and the young man in white was still nowhere to be found.

Alexander furrowed his brow and returned to the yard, where he spotted the maid crying in a corner not far away.

He approached her, bowing slightly. "Excuse me, where is your master?"

The maid, Lana, looked up, her face streaked with tears, and suddenly clutched Alexander's sleeve, exclaiming, "It's you! You and Julius... Please help me find Her... my master."

Alexander frowned, asking, "Is he missing?"

Lana sobbed, "It's all my fault. I shouldn't have let her go to the stage. Actually, she's a woman."

Alexander scanned the empty yard; the audience had departed, and the stage was deserted, sending a chill down his spine.

At that moment, a man emerged from behind the stage curtain. It was the older Risunese who performed the Disappearing Act.

As Alexander took a closer look, he was taken aback; this man was no ordinary performer.

The Risunese had changed drastically. His hair was gray, with a bald patch on top.

His forehead was lined with deep wrinkles, and crow's feet etched the corners of his eyes, giving him a sinister look akin to a serpent ready to strike. His stride was steady and powerful, and his large hands hinted at martial prowess.

Lana moved to approach the Risunese, but Alexander pulled her back, deliberately raising his voice, "Your master must have left early. Let's go back and look for him."

He tugged on Lana's sleeve and headed toward the courtyard gate.

Noticing only Lana was left, Logan asked in surprise, "That sissy... Oh, sorry! Where's that young man?"

Alexander's face darkened. "It's a woman disguised as a man, and now she's gone."

Jalen shouted, "Could it be that those Risunese really have magical powers?"

Alexander glanced around, confirming they were alone. He instructed the two, "Logan, let's go inside and check later tonight. You see if there's a back door and keep an eye on it. Call me if anything happens, but don't act recklessly.

"Jalen, take Lana back to Emerald Manor to settle in first."

Turning to Lana, Alexander bowed earnestly. "Lana, don't worry. Your lady has helped me, and I will definitely find her and keep her safe."

"Thank you," Lana replied, reluctantly departing with Jalen.

As Logan moved towards the back of the yard, Alexander stared at the beautiful circus banner of Risun Circus, his fists tightening hard.

He muttered to himself, "How dare those Risun bastards stir up trouble here? But I can't act rashly, or I'll end up dead..."

Behind the courtyard curtain, several shadowy Risunese whispered among themselves. Reece Perumal, a short Risunese with a noticeable black mole on his face, chuckled lewdly, "Mr. Burkart, that woman was truly stunning. Thankfully, I have a keen eye and realized at once she was masquerading as a man."

"Reece, that woman is not simple. We can't act against her just yet. We need to uncover her true identity first," replied Arturo Burkart.

Arturo fiddled with a gold plate in his hand, which displayed a grand Five Phoenix Tower on one side and a line of small text on the other, reading: [The Thirteenth...]

In the blink of an eye, Alexander had been guarding the entrance to the alley leading to the compound for four hours. It was getting late, and his anxiety was rising.

Suddenly, someone called out, "Alexander! Alexander!"

Looking up, he saw Logan waving at him from a corner.
