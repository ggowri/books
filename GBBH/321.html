He clawed at Alexander's fingers with all his might, hoping to take in even a breath of air, but it was useless.

Alexander coldly warned. "If you harass us again, I guarantee you'll regret being born."

The deadly intent sent a shiver through Marcos' entire body.

"Hel... help me..." Marcos stammered.

His two bodyguards finally snapped to their senses, charging forward with angry shouts.

But Alexander simply hurled Marcos at them.

With a whoosh, Marcos was thrown like a cannonball, crashing into his bodyguards.

The three of them collapsed, rolling over each other in pain, unable to even get up.

All the passengers fell silent, watching Alexander with fear; not one dared to intervene.

Alexander glanced around, and they quickly averted their gazes, pretending to see nothing.

"Huff... huff..." Marcos gasped, his vision dark, feeling as if his neck was about to snap.

When Alexander grabbed him earlier, it was as if Death's scythe had been placed at his throat, threatening his life.

One guard clutched his chest while the other held his head, both in extreme pain, clearly suffering from the earlier impact.

At that moment, hurried footsteps and shouting could be heard.

Alexander turned to see flight officers and crew members rushing over, alarmed by the commotion.

After taking a few deep breaths, Marcos finally regained his composure, genuinely thinking he was about to die moments before.

"You bastard. You dare hit me! Just wait until we get to Tijuana—I'll make you pay!"

Marcos swore vengefully.

Alexander sneered dismissively, not even bothering to respond.

"Forget him. Don't let this maniac ruin your mood," Madelyn said, tugging Alexander's arm.

"Who told you to start a fight here? Anyone who causes trouble again will be removed from the plane immediately," the air marshal sternly warned.

Marcos opened his mouth as if to argue but held back.

As a celebrity and Caleb's son, if people found out he got beaten on a plane, he'd be humiliated.

But he would definitely get revenge for this.

Once they reached Tijuana, his territory, he'd make sure this kid wouldn't leave in one piece.

Madelyn smirked. "You're a celebrity—don't want to see headlines about you getting beaten on a plane, right?"

Marcos gritted his teeth, remaining silent.

Alexander kept smiling, but to Marcos, that radiant smile looked like the face of a devil.

At the same time, Marcos felt a chilling murderous intent, making him freeze in place.

He looked at Alexander with fear; this guy must have killed before—his aura was even deadlier than his father's subordinates.

Marcos didn't dare say anything, knowing that if he spoke, this guy might actually finish him off.

He forced a grim expression and said, "This is a misunderstanding; we were just messing around."

Alexander patted Marcos on the shoulder, making him shudder with fright.

The air marshal watched Alexander coldly, aware that something was off, but since no one was pursuing it, he decided to let it go.

"You all remember this—if you cause trouble again, you'll be arrested for endangering public safety!"

After sternly warning them, the air marshal left.

Walter looked at Alexander with a complex expression. He thought he'd have to step in to calm things down, but Alexander managed to make Marcos suffer without even getting physical.

Throughout the rest of the flight, Alexander and Madelyn chatted and laughed together.

Meanwhile, Marcos sat there, face pale, completely silent.

Finally, the flight landed at Tijuanea Airport.

Marcos immediately stood up and darted off the plane like a rabbit.

He had already messaged his dad, arranging for dozens of capable men to wait outside—he couldn't let this grudge go unavenged.

"Think you can mess with me? Just you wait!"

Marcos sneered darkly, determined to avenge this insult or die trying.

But as soon as he got off the plane, he was surrounded by a swarm of reporters.

"Mr. Brooks, someone tipped us off that you were harassing a woman on the plane and got beaten up by her husband. Care to explain?"

"Could you clarify the situation? Was it true you got hit for flirting with a woman?"

Reporters crowded around Marcos, bombarding him with questions.
