"Mr. Mathison, I need to see Mr. Burkart urgently!"

"No! Mr. Burkart is in a confidential meeting."

"Riggs and Misael are dead, and that envoy is a fake! He took off with the woman and Zamir!"

The door swung open, and Arturo stumbled out, looking disheveled and pale. He seized the Risunese man by the neck. "Say that again!"

"The envoy killed Riggs and Misael..."

"Damn it!" Arturo hurled the Risunese man aside.

He quickly rallied dozens of Risunese soldiers, torches in hand, and started his pursuit down the mountain with Reece and Raylan.

Meanwhile, Alexander was carrying Zamir on his back while holding Isolde's hand as they hurried down Walgus Hill. Glancing back, he spotted a line of torches rapidly approaching.

Anxiety gripped him. The sky was pitch black. Despite the moonlight, the jungle was dense and treacherous.

They couldn't afford to light torches, relying instead on Zamir to navigate. However, Zamir, being just a child, struggled to see in the dark, so Alexander had to carry the two himself.

Fortunately, Isolde had surprisingly reached the first level of martial arts, which meant she could at least keep up.

Hiding in the shadows wasn't a viable option; they'd be trapped by dawn.

Moreover, Arturo, a level-four warrior with keen senses, could detect even the faintest sound from a distance. Their only hope was to run as fast as they could.

Two hours later, a figure burst from the forest, carrying Zamir and a young beauty under his arm.

Although a level-three warrior would typically exhibit steady breathing, Alexander's handsome face was drenched in sweat, his chest heaving as exhaustion set in.

As he looked back at the encroaching torches, a fierce determination ignited in his eyes. He gritted his teeth, summoned all the Force, and surged forward.

Isolde felt her face flush and her heart race as she clung to him.

She wasn't sure if it was intentional or not, but she felt a tingling numbness coursing through her body, rendering her almost powerless.

She chastised herself for not being stronger; after a short while, she couldn't keep up.

Isolde tilted her head slightly to gaze at Alexander, feeling inexplicably drawn to him.

At the front of the group was Arturo, with Reece and Raylan trailing a hundred feet behind, while dozens of Risunese followed.

Arturo let out a cold snort, his expression grim, and shouted back, "I see him. He's not far ahead!"

The Risunese responded with shouts of surprise and quickened their pace.

Fifteen minutes later, Alexander darted past a stone wall, only to suddenly backtrack.

There, he spotted a high cave in the wall, accompanied by a small stone tablet engraved with the words "Crematory Ground." It was likely a burial site for the priests of Walgus Church.

He turned to see Arturo leaping towards him, clearly about to catch up.

Faced with no other choice, he jumped into the cave, set the two down, and instructed firmly, "Go check for an escape route. I'll hold them off here!"

With that, he pulled out Finn's Life-Saving Pills, uncorked the bottle, and swallowed them all, keeping them tucked under his tongue.

"Okay," Zamir responded and stepped into the cave.

Once Isolde was on the ground, she felt her entire body go limp, needing a moment to gather herself. Looking at Alexander's figure blocking the cave's entrance, she felt an unexplainable sense of calm.

Not far from the cave, Zain suddenly halted, peering into the distance. He spotted a flicker of light moving in the dark ahead and could faintly hear shouting—it seemed to be the Risunese!

His eyes lit up, and he yelled, "Come on! I'm going in first!"

Logan, hearing the commotion, looked up just in time to see Zain dart away, racing toward the flickering flames!

"Isolde, why are you still here?" Alexander turned around and noticed she hadn't moved, prompting him to shout in alarm.

Isolde snapped back to reality, her cheeks flushing as she responded and started to retreat deeper into the cave.

In mere seconds, Arturo reached the cave's entrance.

He strolled toward Alexander, hands clasped behind his back, his gaze fixed on him. His wrinkled face contorted into a frown, and his hoarse voice sent shivers down one's spine.

"Mr. Hawthorne, why did you suddenly leave without a word?" he asked.
