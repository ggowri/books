"You're apologizing now? It might be a bit too late for that!"

Alexander looked down at Frederick with a cold, detached gaze.

Frederick's heart had been pierced, yet he clung to life, his formidable strength keeping him alive.

Unless Alexander chose to heal him, which was unlikely, Frederick's chances of survival were slim.

"Kill them all! Leave no one standing!" Alexander commanded with a wave of his hand.

A wicked grin spread across Augusta's face as he relayed Alexander's orders through his communicator.

The sound of gunfire echoed ominously, each shot marking the end of another Morris family member, their lives snuffed out in an instant.

Augusta was relentless, swinging his machete as he pursued the remaining Morris family members with fierce determination.

Meanwhile, Alexander prowled the estate, searching for any sign of Felix.

"Felix really took off, and he's quick about it," Alexander muttered to himself, noticing the absence of Felix's presence.

It was clear he had made a hasty retreat.

"Boss, we couldn't find Felix. What's the plan now?" Augusta asked.

Calm and collected, Alexander analyzed the situation. "Hmph, he's nothing but a stray dog now. The news of the Morris family's downfall will spread like wildfire—no one will dare ally with him again.

"The military won't risk a conflict with me just to back him up. Felix is smart enough not to return to the Red Umbrella Group to wait for death.

"He'll head to Treasure Island, trying to recruit the Brilliant Group to bolster his strength since he doesn't know Cloe is under my wings."

Alexander declared, striding away with Augusta in tow, "Let's move out and wait for Felix to walk right into our trap."

Behind them, the grand Morris Estate erupted in flames, the inferno painting the sky a vivid crimson.

Yet not a single Morris family member fled the scene; their bodies lay strewn about, lifeless and still.

For the foreseeable future, Felix would keep his head down.

Alexander decided to return to Loscity with his daughter, while Augusta would lead a team back to Treasure Island to strengthen their defenses and bide their time.

As Alexander's figure faded into the horizon, a figure darted into the burning Morris Estate—it was Felix.

The mansion was engulfed in flames, and the sight of over a hundred fallen Morris family members was gruesome.

Even the cold-hearted Felix felt a chill run down his spine.

"Mr. Frederick Morris, how are you holding up?"

Felix finally found Frederick sprawled on the ground, fortunate to have collapsed in an open area, or he would have been consumed by the flames by now.

"Who..."

Frederick trembled as he raised his head, his face ashen and devoid of color.

"It's me, it's me! Are you alright?" Felix called out, scanning Frederick's injuries to assess their severity and determine if Frederick could still fight back.

"Felix, is that you!?" Frederick exclaimed, a rush of relief flooding him.

He hadn't expected Felix to risk everything to save him.

"Felix... you have to help me... please, stop the bleeding. I can only hold on for a little while longer. If you don't come, I'm done for..." Frederick pleaded desperately.

Felix narrowed his eyes, a menacing glint flickering within.

"Mr. Frederick Morris, are you really that badly hurt?"

He focused intently on Frederick, his right hand slowly gripping the syringe.

Frederick gasped for air, clutching Felix's arm tightly. "Felix, save me!"

"Alright, I'll save you!!!"

Felix's cheerful demeanor shifted in an instant. With a sudden, swift motion, he plunged the syringe deep into Frederick's chest, injecting the crimson serum directly into his body.

The two were so close, and Felix moved like lightning. Frederick, gravely injured and on the brink of death, had no strength left to resist.

"Ah! Felix, what are you doing?" Frederick gasped.

He was horrified as he watched the eerie red light flicker from his chest, a horrific gaping hole melting through him.

"You bastard, are you trying to kill me?" Frederick roared, summoning the last of his strength to claw at Felix's throat.

"You old fool, you still want to fight back?" Felix chuckled, twisting Frederick's arm until a sickening crack echoed in the air.

Frederick's right arm snapped like a twig.

As he attempted to retaliate, he found himself utterly drained of energy, trembling and convulsing, his chest cavity already ravaged by the serum.

"Frederick, consider yourself lucky—you get to be my stepping stone to greater power! Don't worry, I'll make sure to avenge the Morris family by taking down Alexander!"

Felix gloated, looking down at Frederick with a sneer of triumph.
