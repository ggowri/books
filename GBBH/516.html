Felix and about a dozen of his men stepped off the plane, where dozens of people were already waiting on the tarmac.

Leading them was an elderly man with a deeply wrinkled face and tired, yellowing eyes. Dressed in a tailcoat, he looked like a butler.

Despite his age and white hair, he stood tall, without the slightest hint of a stoop or hunch.

His eyes, in particular, lacked the dullness of old age; instead, they were sharp and alert, glinting with life.

Clearly, the old man was a martial arts expert.

With a wave of his hand, the old man signaled the crowd behind him to part, and out stepped a middle-aged man, Barry Morris.

Barry's sharp gaze was fixed intensely on Felix.

The old man followed him obediently, while a young woman in her twenties stood to his side.

All eyes were on Felix, watching him with scrutiny, caution, and even a hint of disdain.

For once, Felix didn't wear his usual stern expression; instead, he plastered on a broad smile and, with a straight-faced Danielle beside him, strode toward Barry.

His eyes flicked over Barry, then settled on the old man and the young woman, making him tense up slightly as he murmured in a low voice, "Everyone, be on high alert. They're powerful, especially the old man. His strength runs deep, and I can't even gauge it."

At Felix's warning, his subordinates immediately curbed their arrogance and took on a more cautious demeanor.

They continued to stare at the old man, mentally sizing up his strength.

Barry was already a level-three metahuman with formidable martial skills. Could the old man possibly surpass him?

But what truly caught their attention was the young woman beside him, dressed in a sky-blue high-slit qipao, her alluring figure accentuated by long, slender legs.

Unlike Danielle's mature allure, this woman exuded refined elegance, like a polished gem glowing with allure.

The two sides sized each other up, and in a few strides, Felix and his entourage were standing before Barry.

"Heh..."

Felix's smile held a trace of obsequiousness, shedding the demeanor of a cold CEO and more like someone begging a favor.

Barry raised a sharp brow and asked coldly, "Mr. Vanderbilt, was my son, Raven, really killed by Alexander? Did you fail to protect him?"

His voice was loud, laced with anger and arrogance.

Felix's brows furrowed. Barry was throwing down the gauntlet right from the start, and a flicker of hostility crossed his gaze.

But he quickly lowered his gaze, hiding his reaction.

When Felix raised his head again, his smile had returned.

"Mr. Morris, I swear on my life, Raven was killed by Alexander! We've done all we could for revenge. Many lives have been lost on his behalf."

Felix put on a regretful expression.

In truth, even Raven's genetic backup had been extracted and used by Felix to enhance his own power.

Felix extended his hand, but Barry ignored it, remaining cold and aloof.

"No need for formalities. This is my secretary, Joyce, and my butler, Diego Morris.

"Your family and ours share strong ties, and Red Umbrella Group has supplied a potent power elixir. Our patriarch wants me to join you in taking down Alexander.

"My son's death will be avenged!"

Barry locked eyes with Felix, scrutinizing his reaction.

Felix was overjoyed, about to express his gratitude.

Just then, Barry's tone shifted, and he spoke with haughty confidence. "Of course, there's a condition. You'll need to hand over half of your shares in Red Umbrella Group to the Morris family, free of charge."

"What?! You want half of my shares?"

Felix's face turned tense immediately.

He had been prepared to offer five percent, but with the Morris family demanding half, that would amount to a full twenty percent.

The problem was he'd already gifted five percent to Francis; now, with the Morris family taking such a massive cut, it was as if they were bleeding him dry.
