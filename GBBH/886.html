"Bang!"

After a bloodcurdling scream, the area fell into a sudden silence. Cyrus made no further sound.

Edmund swallowed hard, shocked at how quickly his brother had fallen.

They were masters of ghostcraft, capable of absorbing the souls left behind by the dead and converting them into their own strength.

This ability not only increased their power and speed but also significantly boosted their defense.

Even when Alexander's kukri dagger struck earlier, it hadn't breached their defenses. But Alexander was simply too strong.

"Ahhh!"

Still stunned, Edmund suddenly felt searing pain in his arm. Instinctively, he tried to pull back, but it was already too late.

Alexander passed by him, the kukri dripping with blood.

Crimson drops of blood dripped from the blade.

Infusing the Force into his kukri, Alexander sliced through the ghostcraft defense, nearly severing Edmund's right arm.

"Swish!"

Another flash of the blade came down, aiming for Edmund's right arm again.

Edmund didn't have time to think and dodged as quickly as he could.

But Alexander was too fast, and with a single strike, he severed Edmund's right arm.

Blood gushed like a fountain, pooling on the ground.

What terrified Edmund more was the presence of an overwhelming power that suppressed his ghostcraft, preventing the wound from sealing.

The strength he had painstakingly gained through ghostcraft was vanishing rapidly, like snow melting in flames.

The Force burned through him, causing excruciating, soul-searing pain that was impossible to endure.

Though he had only lost an arm, it felt as if his entire body was splitting apart.

"Spare me! Please... don't kill me..."

Trembling uncontrollably, Edmund could only beg for mercy.

Alexander didn't hesitate, slashing his kukri horizontally.

Edmund's head separated from his neck and flew through the air.

In that instant, Edmund's eyes widened, his face frozen in terror.

His body fell heavily to the ground like a felled tree.

In just two or three minutes, Alexander had ended the fight with the two ghostcraft masters.

They had been assassins hired by the Mafia to hunt Alexander, only to be killed when he turned the tables and came for them.

Meanwhile, Marvin and Shadow stood back-to-back, their expressions grim.

After entering, they hadn't expected to find an expansive forest inside.

Dense trees blocked out the sky, plunging the area into darkness and eerie silence.

"Watch out!"

Shadow hissed a warning.

In an instant, Marvin and Shadow rolled in opposite directions.

"Boom!"

A rocket flew in, exploding where they had just stood, sending a searing fireball into the air.

Splintered tree trunks and leaves mixed with dirt flew everywhere.

"Since you're here, there's no escape!"

A cold, mocking voice echoed from the forest.

Marvin managed to dodge quickly, but the explosion's force still hit him.

A chunk of flesh was torn from his arm, and blood flowed freely.

He quickly pulled out a bandage, wrapping the wound efficiently to stop the bleeding.

"They must have at least second-level firearms abilities, or that rocket wouldn't have been this powerful!"

Shadow issued a warning while grabbing his ankle, which had dislocated during the roll. He yanked it sharply and stomped it back into place.

"Crack!"

Pain surged through him, but his dislocated ankle was back in place.

Aside from a dull ache, his ability to move remained intact.

"Who are you working for? How dare you cause trouble for the Mafia?"

A sinister voice rang out.

Marvin and Shadow turned to see three figures emerging slowly from the dense forest.

The most striking figure was a ninja, his face concealed by a black veil. He wore a black samurai uniform, with two katanas strapped diagonally across his back.

His footsteps were so light they resembled a hunting lynx, silent and calculated.

If he hadn't revealed himself, Marvin and Shadow might never have noticed him.

A ninja like this in a forest was lethal!

Marvin and Shadow took deep breaths and tightened their grips on their weapons.

The forest was thick with an ominous sense of danger.

"Rumble!"

Thunder rumbled in the sky as dark clouds rolled in, signaling an impending storm.
