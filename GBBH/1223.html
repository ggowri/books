Alexander gave a slight nod. "If you're telling the truth, I might consider letting you live."

"Thank you, sir. Thank you for your mercy." Kylian bowed repeatedly, his forehead touching the ground.

"You get to keep your life," Alexander said coolly, "but I can't have you running around telling tales."

Before Kylian could react, Alexander pressed his palm against his forehead. Horror filled Kylian's eyes as he felt the Force seep into his mind.

The blow didn't kill Kylian. It just wiped out his recent memories. When he'd wake up later, he wouldn't remember a thing about what went down.

Kylian's vision went black, and he crumpled to the floor like a rag doll.

Alexander ordered his men to drag Kylian out of there, then went back to searching the place.

He made a beeline for Fernando's bedroom, figuring that's where he would keep his most valuable stuff.

Right then, Quentin came rushing in; all worked up after hearing Alexander had squeezed a security code out of Fernando's assistant.

"We need to find Fernando's secret room," Quentin blurted out, barely containing his excitement. "That's got to be where he's stashing the pure Immortality Serum."

But when Alexander reached Fernando's bedroom door, he found Jonah waiting for him.

Jonah was standing there like some kind of human roadblock; arms spread wide across the hallway.

Alexander stopped dead in his tracks. The two men locked eyes, and one could practically see the sparks flying between them.

"Get out of my way," Alexander growled, his voice cold as ice.

"Now that we've taken over the base," Jonah sneered, "it's time we settled our little score."

"I'm looking for the pure Immortality Serum. Get out of my way, or you won't like what happens next." Alexander's patience was wearing thin.

"Oh really?" Jonah's laugh echoed through the hall. "You're threatening me? Let's see who ends up regretting this little encounter.

"I know the pure Immortality Serum is in Fernando's room, and I'm not about to let you steal my glory."

Jonah's arrogance was practically radiating off him as he sneered.

Ilona frowned from the back of the group, wanting to step in. But with Jonah being so aggressive and Quentin staying silent, she swallowed her words.

Quentin knew these two would have to settle their score one way or another, so he chose to stay out of it.

"You're just like Gael, Jonah—all talk, no bite," Alexander said with a dismissive smirk.

That lit Jonah's fuse. "You think you're hot stuff, Alexander? Then shut up and prove it! Let's go right now. We'll see who's all talk!" His body tensed, ready to strike at any moment.

"You're digging your own grave, Jonah." Alexander's eyes narrowed to slits, a deadly glint flashing behind them.

Ilona stepped forward hesitantly, trying to play peacemaker. "Look, maybe we can all take a step back here. There's no point in holding onto old grudges. It never ends well."

"Shut your mouth, Ilona," Jonah snapped. "The only way is with Alexander on his knees begging for forgiveness. Otherwise, no deal." He stormed toward Alexander, his face twisted with rage.

"Get the hell away from me," Alexander growled, swinging his arm to deliver a hard slap to Jonah's face.

"You dare try to slap me? You've got some nerve." Jonah's fury exploded. Slapping someone's face was the ultimate insult. Alexander going straight for a face slap just made his blood boil even more.

Jonah was a level four metahuman with both super speed and special abilities. When those powers combined, they packed way more punch than one would expect.

His combat skills could even rival a level-five metahuman.

In Jonah's mind, he was just one small step away from officially reaching level five, and when that happened, he'd be throwing down with the level six crowd.

"I'm gonna slap you into next week!" Jonah roared, swinging right back at Alexander.

Their hands collided with such force that it sent out a shock wave, the energy blast forcing everyone to scramble backward.

Ilona, Quentin, and the others practically jumped out of their skin as they retreated, not wanting to get caught in the crossfire between these two powerhouses.

When the dust settled, Alexander hadn't budged an inch.

Jonah, on the other hand, wasn't nearly as cool about it. He stumbled back several steps, glaring at Alexander with murder in his eyes. But instead of getting angry, a grin spread across his face.

"You're as tough as they say," Jonah said, looking genuinely pleased. It had been ages since he'd found someone who could push him back like that.
