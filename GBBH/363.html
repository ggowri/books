Cloe's specially selected bodyguards, though strong and skilled fighters, now stood frozen like statues, completely unresponsive.

Hidden in the shadows, Alexander silently approached the mysterious Henry.

None of them, including Henry, noticed that someone else was nearby.

"Ms. Bryant, you are simply captivating!"

Henry gave a sly, seductive smile.

His smile was intensely alluring, holding a strange appeal for Cloe.

At that moment, Cloe felt as though she had never met a man so charming, so handsome.

Alexander let out a quiet scoff.

Through his eyes, he saw a wisp of dark energy flash across Henry's face.

Cloe was clearly influenced by this dark energy and unconsciously moved closer.

Just as Alexander suspected, Henry knew corpse-driving sorcery and had other tricks up his sleeve.

This dark energy was powerful, capable of hypnotizing and influencing people's minds.

"Mr. Rogers, I think you're quite wonderful!"

After saying this, Cloe felt something was off but couldn't quite pinpoint what.

Henry chuckled and said, "Ms. Bryant, I'd like you to stay for dinner. How about a nice chat together?"

He reached out to wrap his arm around her waist, his intentions crystal clear.

Suddenly, something crossed Cloe's mind, and she frowned, stepping back.

Henry's hand landed on empty air, and his expression soured.

"Ms. Bryant, are you really going to refuse me?"

"Well..."

Cloe clutched her forehead, her mind conflicted. One voice urged her to agree, while another reminded her that she belonged to Alexander.

Henry chuckled darkly.

"Relax, Ms. Bryant. It's just dinner and some conversation, that's all. I'll personally see you home afterward."

Cloe was stunned.

She hesitated further, frowning as she struggled with what to do.

Just then, a cold glint flashed across Henry's face as he waved his hand toward Cloe's bodyguards, sending streaks of dark energy into them.

The bodyguards stumbled slightly, then silently turned and walked away, their movements stiff like poorly oiled robots.

As Henry dealt with the bodyguards, Alexander felt a chill run down his spine. He could sense the terrifying nature of this dark energy.

This guy must use this dark energy to control corpses; if it could manipulate the dead, it could certainly control the living.

With a wave of his hand, Henry had sent Cloe's bodyguards away and taken control of her.

He took Cloe by the hand and, smiling, led her toward a back room.

Alexander, his expression cold, followed them, a quiet intent to kill rising within him.

Treasure Island was his critical base, even his hidden stronghold, and he couldn't allow a corpse-witch like this to remain.

Following them, he quietly released a stream of the Force, directing it at Cloe.

Cloe shuddered and immediately snapped back to her senses.

She yanked her hand from Henry's grip and demanded angrily, "What did you do to me? Are you asking for trouble?"

Henry froze, shocked that his seemingly unstoppable sorcery had failed.

His handsome face took on a greedy expression.

"You snapped out of it on your own? That's unusual... Do you have some kind of protective charm on you?"

Cloe tried to turn and run, but Henry slammed her against the wall, pinning her.

He revealed his true face, viciously saying, "Ms. Bryant, if I don't help you, the Brilliant Group will lose control of Treasure Island soon, and the Red Umbrella Group will swallow it whole."

Struggling against him, Cloe demanded, "Is it you causing the disappearances at the trading center? The Red Umbrella Group wants you to take us down, don't they?"

"Smart!"

Henry grinned wickedly.

"The Red Umbrella Group offered a billion dollars. I couldn't say no. And long before Guzman arrived on Treasure Island, my family had lived here for centuries.

"So Treasure Island belongs to me, and I'm going to bring down the Brilliant Group and the Red Umbrella Group, too. You're all going to hell!"
