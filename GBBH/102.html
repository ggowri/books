Samuel forcefully shoved Nick aside.

"You absolute fool! You're fired effective immediately! If Mr. Hawthorne decides to pursue legal action for your public discrimination and personal insults, our bank will fully cooperate."

Nick was left completely flabbergasted, at a loss for what to do next.

He mused, "What gives Alexander the right to be a diamond VIP? How on earth did that happen?

Is that old bat hiding something? Could she really be a billionaire and left everything to him? Over 100 million dollars—damn it!

Not only did Alexander get me fired, but he also swiped the fortune that should've been mine!"

Alexander didn't feel the need to waste any more breath on Nick. To him, Nick was nothing but a cockroach scuttling on the ground.

If it weren't for Lily, he could've made one phone call to have Nick dealt with.

With a broad smile plastered across his face, Samuel turned to Alexander.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I sincerely apologize for this incident. We'll get to the bottom of it. You came to withdraw money, right? Allow me to assist you personally."

Samuel ushered Alexander into a private VIP room.

Nick watched in utter disbelief, his expression darkening further by the second.

"Just wait, Alexander. I'll find a way to reclaim what's rightfully mine."

"Hey!" Nick was suddenly jolted by a hefty pat on the shoulder.

He jumped and glared at the security guard. "What do you want?"

The guard shoved him again.

"You're no longer an employee here. Please exit the premises and stop interrupting our business."

Nick felt his vision blur with rage.

He was used to barking orders at security, never expecting to be treated like this himself.

"Mr. Portman, take care of yourself. I know jobs are tough to come by, but with your skills, you'll land something better," one colleague chimed in.

"Yeah, Mr. Portman, want us to throw you a farewell party?" another added, the glee barely concealed in his voice.

Nick, who had always been harsh and demanding with his colleagues, could see their delight. The happiness in their eyes only fueled his fury.

"Damn you, Alexander! I'll make sure I get back what's mine!" he roared, dumping all the blame on Alexander.

An hour later, Alexander arrived at the retirement home, carrying a bag filled with cash.

As he entered James' room, everything remained untouched, just as it had been the day James departed.

Alexander slowly sank into the couch, gazing at the rocking chair James used to love.

For a moment, it felt as if the gentle old man was still there, sitting in the chair with his eyes closed and a faint smile on his face.

Sunlight poured through the window, casting warm light on the scene, while the rocking chair swayed gently as if beckoning him to remember.

Unconsciously, Alexander lost track of time, spending hours in the quiet comfort of that room.

When he finally stepped out of the retirement home, he felt different—somehow renewed, as if he had undergone a cleansing. A refreshing sense of clarity washed over Alexander, and he sensed that his connection to the Force had deepened, too.

A smile broke across his face. The trials of the heart were as crucial to a Warrior's strength as any battle fought; they were an essential part of growth.

...

At the entrance of the villa, Ava was waiting for Alexander to return home.

Emma couldn't help but ask, "Ms. Blackburn, when you are negotiating with the Hansen family, why do you insist that Alexander attend?"

Ava flashed a knowing smile.

"Tomorrow marks the day of the magic powder exchange conference. Sebastian will undoubtedly pull out all the stops to snag the formula for the magic powder.

"While the Saint Group might not be in the magic powder business, we absolutely cannot let Sebastian get his hands on it!

"That's why we're teaming up with the Kaylon and the Hansen family and bringing Alexander into the mix. Even if Sebastian manages to strike a deal with the Cripples and the Blood Gang, he still won't stand a chance at securing the formula.

"Alexander is an indispensable asset; his strength is unmatched in the Saint Group, and honestly, I'd say he's among the best in all of Loscity."

Emma raised an eyebrow, skeptical.

"Is he really that tough in a fight? I can't see it."

Ava let out a dismissive huff.

"You came in too late to witness what he's capable of. As long as Alexander is on our side, we can keep Sebastian in check."

Emma nodded thoughtfully, then shook her head.

She hadn't seen Alexander in action, so it was hard for her to believe. It felt like Ava was seeing him through rose-colored glasses, overestimating his abilities.

When Alexander finally returned to the villa, Ava had been waiting for quite a while.
