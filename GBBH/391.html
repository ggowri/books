Early the next morning, Alexander took Ashley to preschool.

At the preschool entrance, Alexander gently ruffled his daughter's hair.

"Ashley, if anything happens, ask your teacher to call me."

Ashley nodded vigorously.

"Ms. Purnell, I'm back for class!" Ashley shouted.

Ashley ran toward a woman standing nearby.

Ella was poised and graceful, with long hair cascading over her shoulders. She wore a fitted white dress accentuating her figure, and her long legs caught the eye as she walked.

Ashley had already run into Ella's arms, who laughed softly as she hugged her, a gentle, inviting fragrance lingering in the air.

"Ms. Purnell, this is my dad..." Ashley introduced Alexander, sounding very grown-up.

Before she could finish, Ella pointed at Alexander in surprise. "Alexander, is that you?"

Alexander blinked, recognizing her as his childhood neighbor, Ella Purnell. He hadn't seen her in over a decade. It was fate.

"Ella, so you're teaching here!"

Ella patted Ashley's head with a complicated expression and said, "Alexander, so Ashley is your daughter. I'll take good care of her, I promise."

"Thanks, Ella. Let me take you out for a meal sometime."

Alexander thanked her and then turned to leave.

Suddenly, a Mercedes came speeding toward them, getting faster.

It drove straight at Ella and Ashley, alarming the people nearby, who screamed in panic.

Ella froze for a moment, and by the time she tried to grab Ashley and move, it was too late.

Ella's face paled, and she immediately stepped in front of Ashley.

In the blink of an eye, a figure rushed over, grabbing Ella and Ashley, leaping several miles away.

"Bang!"

The Mercedes sped past where Ella and Ashley had been standing and crashed into a flower bed.

That was close!

Ella was pale with fright, her mind blank.

Alexander asked, concerned, "Are you alright?"

Ella snapped back to her senses, realizing she was pressed tightly against Alexander's chest, her face reddening as she noticed the closeness.

"Thank you, Alexander. I'm fine... this is the second time you've saved me."

Ella remembered how she had almost drowned as a child, and Alexander had saved her. Now he had saved her again.

Just then, a heavyset woman with a smell of alcohol stumbled out of the car.

"Who wrecked my car? Was it you? You're paying for it!" the woman shouted at Alexander, her face quivering with anger.

Ella was furious. "Oh, it's you, Mrs. Powell! How could you drive drunk? You nearly hit us!"

The woman looked Alexander up and down with disdain and said, "Ms. Purnell, you're exaggerating! It was obviously your fault, blocking my way and causing me to crash. This is a fifty-thousand-dollar Mercedes. You're paying up!"

Seeing her attitude, Alexander sized her up instantly. It's a woman with a little money who didn't know her limits.

"Why should we pay? Wait right here while I call the cops. We'll see what they say about your drunk driving and trying to shift blame."

Alexander pulled out his phone immediately.

The woman instantly deflated; drunk driving was illegal, and if the cops came, she'd end up in court.

She scoffed. "Look at you, broke and pathetic. Fine, I'll let it slide. Consider yourself lucky I'm so generous.

"Ms. Purnell, I'll cover the repair costs myself, for your sake."

Alexander replied icily, "I was scared out of my mind. Maybe you should take me to the hospital for a thorough check."

The woman shrieked immediately.

"You're trying to shake me down? You must not know who I am! I'm Mandy Summers!"

Alexander began dialing on his phone.

"Alright then, I'll let the cops handle this."

Mandy instantly backed down again, hurriedly pulling out a wad of cash from her purse and throwing it at Alexander.

"Loser, isn't this what you wanted? Take the money and get lost!"

Alexander fixed her with a hard, piercing stare.

"For your kid's sake, since they're in class with my daughter, I'll let it go this time."

Turning around, he stuffed the cash into the nearby charity donation box.
