Cleveland let out a cold snort and gathered his energy. His massive body swelled as his muscles tensed. In an instant, he lunged forward, covering several yards in a single step. His fist shot out toward Alexander's face with terrifying speed.

"Arhat Fist!"

Alexander's brows furrowed. He steadied his breathing, keeping his mind calm. His martial core pulsed with power, sending energy surging through his body. His muscles tightened and expanded.

At the critical moment, he stepped back, ducking low. Using a maneuver from Arhat Fist, he twisted his body and slipped beneath Cleveland's outstretched arm, narrowly avoiding the strike.

Seizing the opening, he pivoted sharply. His waist twisted, spine coiling like a spring before releasing with explosive force. His arm swung around, fist cutting through the air with a sharp whistle, aiming for Cleveland's back.

Cleveland's heart pounded. Sensing danger, he hastily turned his body. His shoulders jerked, barely dodging the blow. Without missing a beat, he surged forward, arms twisting and locking around Alexander in an iron grip.

With a swift hook of his foot, Cleveland threw his full weight into the move, attempting to hurl Alexander to the ground.

Alexander felt his balance shift. He inwardly admired Cleveland's skill.

Instead of resisting, he let his body go limp, allowing himself to be thrown.

Then, with a sudden roar, he tightened his grip, latching onto Cleveland's arms with all his strength.

Cleveland tried again to slam Alexander down, but this time, it didn't work.

Alexander used the momentum, stabilizing himself just before impact. His feet planted firmly on the ground.

He drove his legs forward, stepping between Cleveland's stance, and locking himself in place. His energy surged.

With a powerful thrust, he slammed his forehead straight into Cleveland's chest.

A sickening crack rang out. Cleveland's ribs shattered. Blood spurted from his mouth as his body crumpled to the ground.

Alexander strode forward and yanked off Cleveland's face covering. "I knew it was you."

The fight had ended in mere seconds. Rory stood frozen. Their movements had been too fast, a blur of motion he could barely follow. By the time he grasped what had happened, Cleveland was already lying on the ground, coughing up blood.

Panic surged through him. He thought, "Mr. Wheeler ate the Verdant Crown Melon. I heard he became a level-one warrior. How could he lose?"

He dropped his iron spear and bolted.

But Logan was faster. He dashed forward, catching up in a few strides. With a fierce shout, he struck Rory down with a single punch. Picking up the fallen spear, Logan pressed its tip against Rory's throat.

"Alexander, should we just kill him?" He made a slicing motion across his neck.

Rory's face turned ashen. "No... please, spare me! It wasn't my idea! Cleveland forced me! I have a family..."

Alexander chuckled. "Rory, take Cleveland and get out of here."

"Alexander..." Logan shot him a displeased look, clearly reluctant to let them go. But Alexander remained firm. Seeing no other choice, Logan lowered the spear.

Rory, terrified they might change their minds, scrambled to his feet. He hoisted Cleveland onto his back and staggered toward the manor without looking back.

"Alexander, if you let them go, what if they come back for revenge?" Logan asked frustration in his voice.

"Cleveland is finished." Alexander's gaze darkened. "And don't forget, we're in Dominion Church territory. If we kill someone, the consequences will be severe."

His eyes drifted to the distant silhouette of Dominion Church's grand tower. He clenched his fists.

"Am I really from this world? What are Warriors and Ava? I will find out."

Rory carried Cleveland back to the manor.

It was a long time before Cleveland regained consciousness. Weakly, he fumbled for a small bottle and downed half of its contents.

It was "Mending Powder," a rare life-saving elixir from the Hall of Lives within Dominion Church.

He had only managed to obtain a single bottle thanks to Donald. Its effects were nothing short of miraculous.

Within minutes, some color returned to his face. He coughed up a few mouthfuls of clotted blood and felt slightly better. "Rory," he rasped, voice weak. "Take my identity token... go to Mr. Wachtell."

"Yes, Mr. Wheeler! Just rest!"

Rory grabbed the token and sprinted toward Dominion Church's Top Court.

Before long, Donald arrived at Emerald Manor and entered Cleveland's quarters.

Placing a hand on Cleveland's chest, he began chanting in a deep, rhythmic tone.

His voice carried an eerie calm, spreading a strange sense of peace through the room.

For over an hour, Cleveland remained motionless, absorbing the healing energy. When Donald finally stopped, Cleveland took a long, shuddering breath.

"You'll live," Donald said grimly. "But your ribs are shattered. Even if you recover, your days of training are over."

His tone grew sharp. "What happened? I gave you a Verdant Crown Melon! You were supposed to become a level-one warrior!"
