Devil Island lay just over 100 nautical miles from Treasure Island and covered about two-thirds of its size, making it a sizable piece of land.

Last night, a large cargo ship had approached the island.

By dawn, more than a dozen helicopters descended.

Alexander led the advance team, setting up a magic circle to dampen the storms surrounding the island. He then guided the cargo ship closer, offloading weapons, ammunition, and supplies onto Devil Island.

Only Marvin, Charlize, and Rosalia stayed behind in Loscity.

Even Augusta, who had just arrived in Loscity with elite forces, came to Devil Island with William and Logan.

While flying over the island in the helicopter, Alexander had spotted a clear spring that flowed across the island, providing a vital fresh water source.

This water abundance made Devil Island highly valuable for development.

Verdant trees thrived under the island's lush conditions, and its scenic beauty was striking.

Should the island be developed, it would easily rival Treasure Island.

And with the two islands supporting each other, their defensive capabilities would be significantly enhanced.

"Unload quickly and conceal everything. According to intelligence, the Dragon Gang's ship will arrive within a few hours," Alexander commanded.

Over a thousand people began moving with urgency.

Late that night, under the moonlight, ripples spread across the sea as a large cargo ship neared Devil Island.

Spotlights illuminated the scene as the ship deployed numerous modified high-speed motorboats.

Soon, over a dozen boats, each carrying dozens of people, sped across the water toward Devil Island.

The men on board were dressed in black uniforms. Though they were not particularly tall, they exuded a fierce and murderous air.

They carried automatic rifles, and some had katanas strapped to their backs.

Several captains, identifiable by their uniforms reinforced with protective padding at the chest and joints, commanded the Dragon Gang's landing force.

The engines roared, propelling the motorboats like arrows shot from a bow, leaving trails in their wake.

"Hurry, guide the cargo ship closer so we can bring the heavy equipment and supplies ashore," a burly, scar-faced man barked.

He carried a new M16 with a katana strapped to his waist.

"Bro, didn't the boss spend billions of dollars on buying Devil Island? Isn't that a bit of a waste?" asked a short man beside him.

Though their builds were vastly different, they shared a striking resemblance.

The scar-faced man glared at his younger brother. "You've no idea. Our boss is securing a fallback plan. Our conflicts with the Sakura Society are escalating, and now, they're allied with the Mafia and have military support."

The short man sighed, "That spells trouble for us. I even heard some people wanted to rebel, saying our boss was just a woman and should step down."

"Shut up! That's not something we should talk about. Just focus on your job," the scar-faced man admonished, cutting the conversation short.

His gaze swept over the dark woods nearby.

Suddenly, his brother chuckled, "Actually, taking over Devil Island is a good move. It's only 100 miles from Treasure Island, and now, we've driven a stake right in the shipping route to the mainland.

"If we want to sink their ships, we can, and they'll have no choice but to pay us protection fees."

The scar-faced man nodded. "You're right. Once we set up defenses, I'll find a chance to sink a few of their vessels and force Treasure Island to supply us regularly."

Just then, the short man took a call.

He grinned, "Good news, bro. Aidan's reinforcements will be here soon. With our abilities, we'll take Treasure Island with no trouble."

"Good, we'll take Treasure Island," Casper laughed with anticipation.
