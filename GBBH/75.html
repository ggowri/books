A wave of shock rippled through the crowd as mocking voices directed their scorn at Theodore.

The Brilliant Group, notorious for its drug trafficking and merciless crimes, was built by the infamous Godfather known as "Reaper."

The organization's chairman, Guzman, was wanted in over a dozen countries, living like a ghost between Sandgo and Mesco to avoid extradition.

Yet here was Theodore, the only son of Sebastian, president of the Brilliant Group, commanding thousands of loyal followers, now reduced to publicly seeking legal protection. It was utterly laughable.

Faced with Theodore's threats, Alexander let out a hearty laugh. "Ryan was the first to lose his legs to me. You'll be the second."

In a flash, Alexander tapped each of Theodore's shoulders, his movements so swift that no one could comprehend what had just happened.

The Force sliced through Theodore's arm nerves like a blade.

As Alexander turned and strolled away, the room stood in stunned silence.

They thought, "Is he really just walking away? He was so adamant about crippling Theodore, but now it seems he's afraid of the Brilliant Group's retaliation. What a blowhard!"

Theodore, filled with despair, watched Alexander leave, and it was as if he had been thrown a lifeline.

He laughed triumphantly, his confidence surging.

"A waste of space is a waste of space! If you're so tough, why don't you stick around? You said you'd break my hands, didn't you? Come on, you coward!"

But suddenly, his laughter came to an abrupt halt.

Theodore's eyes widened in disbelief as he looked at his arms.

"My hands... why can't I move them? What did he do to me?"

Panic set in as he tried to wave his arms, but they remained completely still, devoid of sensation.

"Ah! What's wrong with my hands?!"

The onlookers gaped, caught off guard by the rapid turn of events.

David, Mary, and Houtte, seasoned in their experiences, had never witnessed anything so bizarre.

Theodore was genuinely incapacitated, and Alexander hadn't even laid a weapon on him!

Oscar muttered to himself, "This is unbelievable. Could that guy be practicing some kind of sorcery?"

Mary's eyes lit up with realization. "Absolutely! It must be some mysterious magic. Who knew he was not only skilled but also a formidable sorcerer!"

The mention of "sorcery" sent a chill down everyone's spine.

Those who had mocked Alexander felt a shiver run down their backs, goosebumps prickling their skin.

They thought, "What if Alexander has indeed cast some sort of spell on us?"

Andrew stammered, turning to Jane, "Sweetheart, do you think Alexander could curse us with magic?"

Jane was a whirlwind of emotions, grappling with the realization that the man she had shared her life with for five years possessed such abilities she had never noticed.

As Alexander approached his battered Toyota, ready to drive away, a familiar voice called out, breathless and urgent.

"Wait!"

He frowned, turning to see Jane rushing toward him.

"Are you coming to defend Theodore? I'd advise against it; you really don't want to mess with me right now, or else..."

He didn't finish his sentence, but the threat hung in the air, palpable enough to send chills down Jane's spine.

"Alexander, you're being reckless! If the Brilliant Group decides to retaliate, Ava won't be able to save you!"

Alexander raised an eyebrow, taken aback by her words.

"Jane, we're divorced. You don't get to dictate my actions."

Fury ignited within her. She thought, "I've set aside my pride to come and warn him, only to be met with indifference?"

"Alexander, you need to lift that curse off Mr. Tate, or you're going to regret it!" Jane shouted, her voice a mix of desperation and anger.

Alexander shot her a cold glance before turning on his heel and getting into the car.

Jane was seething, stomping her foot, and unleashing a torrent of curses.

"Jerk! Just because you've got Ava, that little vixen, you think he can walk all over me. Just wait till Mr. Tate gets his hands on you!"

That evening, Theodore was rushed to the finest private hospital in town. A team of top-notch surgeons and neurologists gathered to consult on his case.

Despite their collective expertise, they were at a complete loss for a viable treatment plan.

What baffled them even more was that Theodore's arm showed no signs of response, yet astonishingly, the rest of his body was functioning perfectly. It defied all medical logic.
