"Before I leave, I can protect you for a while and teach you how to spot danger, take down zombies, and deal with mutated beasts."

"Me, a warrior? Is that really possible?" Sally asked, her voice trembling with a mix of fear and uncertainty.

Alexander chuckled. "You've got some serious agility when dodging zombies, and your speed is impressive. Sure, you might need to work on your strength a bit.

"Honestly, any ordinary person can take down regular zombies with a weapon and a little courage.

"Unless you're facing evolved speedsters or powerhouses, or those massive mutated beasts, you should be okay..."

As Alexander shared his insights, Sally gradually began to feel a spark of confidence.

"Alexander, you really will teach me?" she asked, her expression serious.

Sally was a woman who understood the importance of timing; relying on others was ultimately not a solution. Gaining her own strength was the key to survival.

"Don't worry, I'll teach you," Alexander assured her.

He started to show Sally some basic combat techniques and knife skills.

Suddenly, a massive black figure loomed several hundred yards away, reminiscent of a primordial beast.

Alexander's eyes narrowed as he recognized what it was—a mutated bison.

This creature was enormous, dwarfing even a heavy-duty truck, with a massive head crowned by two thick, sharp horns that glinted menacingly in the light.

Those horns looked like they could pierce the very sky.

Its four colossal hooves were terrifyingly thick; a single kick could send a person flying.

"What kind of monster is that?" Sally gasped, trembling.

This beast was nothing like the zombies; not even hundreds or thousands of them could take down this mutated bison.

Alexander remained calm, watching the creature with keen interest.

The bison seemed curious about them, approaching at a leisurely pace, not charging but moving steadily.

"Don't move, and don't be afraid," Alexander reassured Sally, studying the bison closely.

A bold idea suddenly sparked in his mind.

"What if I could tame this bison? Riding it could let me charge through hordes of zombies!"

As the mutated bison drew closer, Alexander could see it more clearly.

It stood taller than a person, its sleek black hide shimmering with an otherworldly glow. It felt as if even bullets would bounce off its tough exterior.

It loomed like a massive wall, exuding an intimidating presence, while Sally felt her knees weaken, paralyzed by fear.

As the bison approached, Alexander narrowed his eyes, locking gazes with the beast.

The bison seemed equally intrigued, and they stared at each other, heavy breaths escaping its nostrils.

But as the creature inched closer, Sally, terrified, stumbled backward and accidentally knocked over a metal lunchbox, the clang echoing in the tense air.

Startled, the bison's eyes turned crimson, and it lowered its head, charging at Alexander with terrifying speed.

Its sharp horns resembled deadly blades, ready to impale him.

"Watch out!" Sally screamed in horror.

Alexander had hoped to tame the beast, but this charge was too ferocious, as if it would bring about the end of the world.

What a shame!

With a resigned sigh, he sidestepped with lightning speed, swinging his sword in a fluid motion.

The long-dormant Lightsaber materialized, slicing smoothly through the bison's massive head.

The severed head rolled away, and the enormous body crashed to the ground, blood gushing out like a fountain.

"Ah!"

Sally covered her mouth, completely stunned.

She had braced herself for a dangerous showdown, fully expecting Alexander to struggle against the mutated bison, if he even managed to defeat it at all.

But Sally never anticipated that he would take down the terrifying beast in a single strike.

After the initial shock faded, exhilaration surged through her. Sally had always thought of Alexander as incredibly skilled; after all, he had willingly stepped up to offer her protection.

But witnessing his true power made her realize he was far beyond what she had imagined.

The bison's massive hooves twitched erratically, and its wide eyes were filled with terror, bloodshot and wild, as if the creature couldn't comprehend its own fate in that fleeting moment.
