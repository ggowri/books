Eamonn stammered, "Ms. Hawkins, I have signed an alliance with Mr. Welsh. You have to help me become the family head and gang leader. How can you go back on your word?"

Vivian snorted contemptuously and said coldly, "Locke is already dead. Are you going to hell and reason with him?"

Eamonn was stunned for a moment, and the people standing on his side all had bitter faces and deep regret.

Norma didn't know why Vivian came to support her, but she knew Alexander must have arranged it.

"Eamonn, do you still want to compete with me for the position of family head?"

Norma's tone was full of murderous intent, and it was apparent that she was preparing to deal with him to frighten the others.

Eamonn knew that if he continued to confront Norma, he would surely die miserably.

He gritted his teeth, bent down deeply, and whispered, "I'm sorry... Please... Please forgive me, Ms. Walsh..."

The dozens of Walsh family executives behind him also bowed and apologized, and some even knelt to beg for mercy.

These people were all dejected, and their expressions were as gloomy as if their family had died.

Alexander pointed at Eamonn and said, "Everyone else can be forgiven, but not this guy."

What bad news!

Eamonn's face turned pale, and he immediately shouted, "This is our family issue. The family lead has the final say. Who are you to speak?"

"He has every right!"

Norma answered it immediately, then waved her hand coldly. "Drag him away. Tie him up and feed him to the sharks."

Eamonn was so frightened that he staggered back a few steps. He felt as if all the bones in his body were being pulled out, and he had no strength left.

Norma glared at him. "What are you all standing there for? Do it!"

Everyone came to their senses immediately, and someone rushed forward, twisted Eamonn's arms behind his back, and pushed him out.

No matter how Eamonn begged for mercy or how much he cried, Norma had no intention of showing any mercy.

Alexander was right. Now that her foundation was unstable, this was not the time to be soft-hearted. They must make an example of him.

Just then, Vivian came over and took the initiative to extend her hand to Norma. "Ms. Walsh, wish it a happy cooperation!"

Alexander saw Norma in a daze and couldn't help but call her.

"Norma..."

"Oh, okay, okay. From now on, we will be friends."

Norma woke up in shock and quickly shook hands with Vivian warmly.

Then, Alexander took out a list and said coldly, "Beyond Eamonn, your family harbors traitors linked to both the Mafia and the Sakra Society.

"Thanks to Ms. Hawkins who provided the list. Now I will read out the names one by one..."

With his words, several guys looked highly panicked.

They retreated quietly, trying to take the opportunity to slip away.

If their identities were revealed, they would be doomed.

"Want to run? Where to?"

Alexander sneered contemptuously and waved his hand.

The cigarette butt in his hand flew over with sparks and immediately pierced through an old man's thigh.

The old man fell to the ground, covering his bloody thigh and rolling over, howling like a pig being slaughtered, with blood flowing all over the ground.

Alexander said indifferently, "Whoever dares to step out of this door before I finish reading the list will die!"

His cold, murderous intent shocked everyone. Several traitors trembled violently but did not dare to get closer to the door.

"Casey Walsh, Joe Walsh..."

Every time Alexander called out a person's name, Norma ordered people to arrest them.

The traitors were discouraged and had no choice but to surrender.

Everyone was shocked beyond words. They didn't expect to see a good show of catching traitors.

It was scary to think about it. With so many traitors hiding within the family, any decision would be transparent to the Sakra Society, which was simply unimaginable.

The senior figures of the Walsh family had changed their attitude towards Alexander. They were not just grateful but also had a hint of awe to him.

"Drag all the traitors out and feed them to the sharks! Take away all their property and distribute it to other members of the family."

Norma acted decisively and efficiently, demonstrating the skills and momentum of a strong woman.

"Thank you, Ms. Walsh! I am willing to serve you at my utmost!"

The members of the Walsh family were thrilled with their decision, reaping substantial property gains and embracing Norma as their true leader.

Meanwhile, Norma leaned close to Alexander's ear and whispered anxiously, "Master, my sister Hazel hasn't come back for a whole night. Something must have happened!"
