Alexander shook his head. Timothy was under his control through the Force—there was no way he'd betray him unless he wanted to face a fate worse than death.

Something had clearly gone wrong with Timothy.

At that moment, headlights appeared at the end of the road, the sound of engines rumbling steadily growing louder.

Yvonne quickly gave orders to her subordinates to prepare for combat.

The screech of tires echoed through the night as enemy vehicles came into view. Alexander's sharp eyes caught sight of at least three armored vehicles in the convoy.

The Mafia had really gone all out—armed helicopters, armored vehicles. This was no small-scale operation.

In one of the armored vehicles, Arthur grabbed Timothy by the collar and snarled, "Why did our helicopter crash? Tell me, did you warn Alexander?"

Timothy let out a manic laugh, his eyes wild. "Arthur, you traitor! It's one thing to betray me, but you're going against Alexander? You're as good as dead!"

Enraged, Arthur slapped Timothy across the face with a brutal force. "Timothy, I've been wanting to get rid of you for a long time. Since you've betrayed the Godfather, I'll be taking your place. Alexander's as good as dead today."

With that, Arthur kicked Timothy to the ground and barked at his men, "Check if they have any heavy weaponry. Fast!"

"Yes, Boss!"

His men immediately deployed thermal imaging and night vision to scan the area.

A week earlier, Arthur had betrayed Timothy to the Mafia's higher-ups.

The organization had sent a team to Tijuanea to capture Timothy and install Arthur as the new boss of the Mafia's local operations.

Upon hearing that Alexander was back in Tijuanea, Arthur had been ecstatic. He immediately mobilized his forces and, using his new position, rented a helicopter and three armored vehicles from the local military.

Arthur had assumed that such overwhelming firepower would make short work of Alexander. But he never expected the helicopter to be brought down so easily.

Timothy, his hands bound and his face swollen and bruised, grinned maliciously. "Arthur, you're finished."

Fuming with rage, Arthur pulled out a gun and aimed it at Timothy's head. "Keep talking, and I'll blow your brains out right here!"

Just then, one of his men reported, "Boss, we've checked. They don't have any heavy weapons, and their numbers are only about half ours."

Arthur's brow furrowed in suspicion. There was a gnawing feeling in his gut, like a prickling sensation down his spine.

He clenched his teeth. "Surround them. Open fire. Leave no survivors."

His convoy immediately sprang into action.

Three armored vehicles and a dozen off-road trucks, each armed with several mounted machine guns, formed a formidable assault force.

Over thirty fully armed soldiers disembarked from the vehicles, quickly setting up a battle formation to close in on Alexander.

The machine guns on the armored vehicles locked onto him.

"Master, what do we do now?" Yvonne's voice held a hint of panic.

Her subordinates were armed with only pistols, and in a direct fire exchange, they would be wiped out in no time.

Even with her witchcraft, she could only hope for a mutual destruction in the face of such overwhelming firepower.

Alexander, however, waved his hand dismissively. "Just stay hidden. I'll take care of the rest."

With that, he vanished into the night, his form dissolving into the darkness like a ghost.

Crash!

A blinding beam of light shot out, effortlessly slicing through one of the armored vehicles, splitting it in half.

Inside, the gunner barely had time to react before he was decapitated by the force of the strike.

In the next instant, a scorching white fire ignited within the vehicle, and with a deafening explosion, the vehicle's ammunition went up in flames, sending debris flying.

The shockwave took down a large portion of the soldiers nearby, their bodies crumpling like ragdolls.

"Fire! Kill him!"

Arthur stood frozen for a moment, his mind scrambling to catch up, before he bellowed orders.

The air erupted with gunfire, hundreds of bullets tearing through the night, chasing after Alexander's elusive form.

But no matter how fierce the gunfire, they couldn't touch him—not even a trace.

Boom!

The second armored vehicle exploded in a burst of flames, as Alexander destroyed it effortlessly. Its ammunition also detonated, creating another massive fireball.

The two destroyed armored vehicles now burned like massive bonfires, casting enough light to make the night feel like day.

And there, standing in front of the wreckage, was Alexander, like some kind of demonic figure. A smile tugged at his lips as he stared down the last armored vehicle in the convoy.
