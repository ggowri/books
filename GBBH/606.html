"Kill!" Weston shouted, his cunning mind spinning into action as he pivoted to charge at Alexander.

He knew the strategy—capture the king before taking out the pawns.

Weston had deliberately let Alexander slip by earlier, focusing instead on Augusta. Now, he aimed to catch Alexander off-guard from behind.

Felix had warned him about Alexander's strength, but Weston couldn't fathom that a mere twenty-something could pose a real threat. Surely, he thought, there must be someone backing Alexander up.

With his level-two strength power surging, Weston felt his speed increase significantly.

While he might not match Augusta's agility, he was like a gust of wind as he closed in on Alexander.

But to his surprise, the formidable opponent he had been chasing didn't even flinch; instead, Augusta turned to him with a sinister grin.

A chill ran down Weston's spine, but he pressed on, determined to seize the moment.

"Get down!" he shouted, bringing his palm down with all his might toward Alexander's back.

The force of his strike sliced through the air, and a vicious smile crept across Weston's face. He couldn't wait to make this punk pay.

But suddenly, a swift gust of wind rushed past him as Alexander spun around, mirroring his attack with a palm strike of his own.

The impact was thunderous, a deafening crack that reverberated through the air as their palms met.

Weston's expression shifted from confidence to sheer shock as he felt the force of Alexander's blow.

Weston was sent flying backward as if he had been hit by a cannonball, his body exploding in a shower of blood and flesh.

Gore splattered everywhere, but in a remarkable twist, an invisible barrier stopped the gruesome remnants before they could touch Alexander, leaving him untouched.

Alexander scoffed, a cruel smirk appearing on his face. "Hmph, you brought this on yourself."

The Morris family members, who had once thought of Weston as one of their strongest fighters, now stood dumbfounded as they witnessed him reduced to nothing more than a bloody pulp by Alexander's single strike.

"You'll pay for this!" Rumen cried out, clutching his severed arm and glaring at Alexander with venomous hatred.

Alexander shot him a cold glance, hardly interested. Rumen was losing too much blood to last much longer, and Alexander couldn't be bothered to waste words on him.

"You're going to die! You will suffer a terrible fate!" Rumen spat, his face contorted with rage.

Alexander regarded him with disdain like a grim reaper in human form.

"Your doom was sealed the moment the Morris family allied with Felix."

His tone was chilling, devoid of warmth.

"You can't do this! Mr. Frederick Morris will kill you! You'll die here, and I'll hang your corpse from a tree for the vultures to pick at, let the wolves gnaw on your bones..." Rumen cursed, his voice dripping with malice.

But before he could finish, Alexander waved his hand.

A flash of white light sliced through the air, and in an instant, Rumen was cleaved in half, crashing to the ground with a look of sheer horror frozen on his face.

The remaining members of the Morris family recoiled in terror, their fear of Alexander growing as they witnessed the slaughter. They scattered like leaves in a storm, with Augusta ruthlessly hunting them down from behind.

From a distance, Frederick and Felix observed the chaos, their expressions grave.

Even Frederick, usually brimming with confidence, felt a knot of doubt twist in his gut as he watched Weston explode into a bloody mess.

Frederick wondered, "This is a formidable opponent! Who could possibly stand against him?

And he's at such a young age. How powerful Alexander will become in a few decades. Will he be invincible?"

As Frederick watched his family members fall, one by one, into pools of their own blood, anger and a thirst for vengeance surged within him.

Yet, he found himself weighing the balance of power between himself and Alexander, struggling to determine where he stood.

If he didn't have a solid plan, Frederick wouldn't risk making a move.

Felix, on the other hand, glared at Alexander, his eyes ablaze with fury.

"Frederick, I'm giving you one last chance—hand over Felix, and maybe I'll spare a few lives from the Morris family!" Alexander's voice rang out coldly as if he were a king issuing commands to his subjects.

Frederick's teeth ground together, his grip tightening around his glass until it shattered in his hand.
