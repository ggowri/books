Lara and Ava each got into their cars and disappeared into the night.

Ava rubbed her temples and took a few sips of water, feeling her dizziness ease a bit.

Remembering Lara's words, she couldn't help but smile wryly, unsure if what Lara said was true or not.

She sighed softly—what difference did it make if it was true or false?

Alexander was the fiancé that the Godfather had chosen for her, and they had feelings for each other...

Suddenly, Ava felt something was off; this wasn't the way home, nor was it heading to Alexander's place.

Ava was completely awake now and quickly shouted,

"Dominic, where are you going? Did you take a wrong turn?"

Silence.

Ava's heart raced, and she coldly asked, "Dominic, what do you think you're doing? This isn't the way home!"

But the driver ignored her, as if he were deaf.

Ava's back was already soaked with cold sweat, and the effects of the alcohol had been replaced by shock.

"You're not Dominic! Stop the car now, or I'll call the police!"

Ava quickly took out her phone, ready to call.

Suddenly, the driver slammed on the brakes. Ava crashed hard against the backrest, losing her phone somewhere.

The car had stopped; they were clearly in a remote area with no one around, surrounded only by eerie, dark woods and shrubs.

"Get out!"

A cold voice rang out as Ava was dragged out of the car by the driver.

"You're not Dominic. Who are you? I'm the president of the Saint Group, and I advise you not to mess around."

Despite her messy appearance, Ava didn't lose her cool; instead, she focused on protecting herself.

This driver looked just like Dominic but was definitely not him.

In her fear, Ava suddenly thought of Alexander, and tears rolled down her cheeks.

"I know you're Ava; and I'm looking for you."

The driver chuckled.

"What do you want?"

Ava slowly backed away, looking for a chance to escape.

The sudden turn left her feeling helpless, but it was remarkable that she could still keep calm.

"Hmph! Ava, can't you recognize my voice?"

The driver sneered, feeling his neck a few times, then surprisingly rubbed off a layer of skin to reveal a thin, translucent mask of a human face, tossing it in front of Ava.

"Gary?!"

Ava stared at him in shock.

She never expected that the head of the Federer family would come to kidnap her himself.

"I heard your family developed a new generation of facial masks that look like real human faces; I didn't think it was true. Seems like I was wrong."

Ava knew Gary was targeting Alexander and seeking revenge for David, and her palms and soles were now sweating.

Her curvy, sensual body trembled slightly, and the fear in her expression added a different kind of beauty that made one feel pity.

Gary approached slowly, like a cat playing with a mouse.

"Alexander killed David and even dared to go against me publicly. I'll start by punishing you since you're involved with him.

"Ms. Blackburn, you dress so seductively every day; you must be good in bed, right? Come here and serve me well, and maybe I'll spare your life."

He grinned wickedly, reaching out to hug Ava.

"Get away!"

Ava cursed angrily as she quickly backed away.

She didn't expect Gary, who usually dressed like a decent man, to be such a pervert; his eyes filled with desire and hatred made him an evil.

"Gary, you're trash! If you have the guts, go take it up with Alexander instead of sneakily impersonating a driver to kidnap me. Are you even a man?"

Ava aimed to provoke Gary to create an opportunity for escape.

"Hahaha..."

Gary laughed wildly.

"Ms. Blackburn, you're clever; you think you can anger me to find an opportunity, huh? But let me tell you, I won't fall for that.

"I want Alexander to pain, starting with you, and then his half-dead daughter in the hospital!"

"You beast, you even go after little girls!"

Ava scolded as she took steps backward.

Gary remained calm; he loved the feeling of being in control, washing away the humiliation Alexander had caused him before.
