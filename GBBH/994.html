His expression twisted with rage as he glared at Gavin and the others.

"Gentlemen, I ask for your understanding. If you give me this opportunity, I will be deeply grateful. This grave will definitely be dug up today!"

Gavin remained stoic, letting out a disdainful snort.

The white-haired elders standing beside him, however, were infuriated. Axel's daring to be disrespectful in public was practically a death wish.

They were determined to support Alexander today because he had generously shared half the profits from the magic powder deal with them.

After all, money moves mountains!

Gavin said calmly, "Alex, do whatever you need to. We'll all support you."

With just one simple sentence, Oliver and his men's morale surged.

Alexander took a deep breath, suppressing his rage. It had been a long time since he had felt this furious, not since becoming a Warrior.

"If you bow in front of the grave and apologize, I might spare your lives. But since the Vance family is so obstinate, don't blame me for what happens next."

With that, Alexander waved his hand.

In an instant, a dozen of the Vance family's bodyguards collapsed, each taken down with a precise shot to the head.

Before anyone could react, another wave of bodyguards fell into pools of blood. By now, every bodyguard standing beside Asher and Axel had been sniped.

Axel quickly waved his hand, shouting, "Kill him!"

One of the white-haired elders next to Gavin bellowed, "Who dares move? I'll see you court-martialed!"

"It's Mr. Ford Vito!"

The more than one hundred soldiers from the Vance family froze in shock. Ford was a general, the supreme commander of the military.

"Shoot! Fire!"

Axel shouted angrily, but none of the soldiers obeyed his command.

While the Vance family had produced generals in the past, the highest-ranking officer they currently had was only a lieutenant general—nowhere near the status of Ford.

Instead of firing, the soldiers began retreating, putting as much distance as possible between themselves and the conflict.

Axel was so furious that his vision blurred, and he nearly fainted.

Alexander's lips curled into a mocking smile as he waved his hand.

A group of men in black suits appeared, marching in precise formation.

Each of them had a solemn expression and carried a bouquet of chrysanthemums.

Their movements were synchronized, exuding an aura of professionalism and menace.

Asher's face turned pale with fear. As someone with a sharp eye, he could immediately tell these men were no ordinary individuals.

They had all experienced battle and bloodshed, and their presence radiated lethal intent.

He thought, "These are members of the Ultimate Alliance. How could Alexander, a supposed gang leader, command such a formidable force?"

Cold sweat soaked Asher's palms and feet.

Ryder and his dozen men were even more terrified. Their faces drained of color, and they trembled uncontrollably.

They all hid far away like sheep, confronted by a pack of wolves.

At the head of the formation, Augusta approached the grave, gently placing his bouquet in front of the tombstone.

The rest of the black-clad men followed suit, one by one, each laying their chrysanthemums in an orderly fashion.

Bunches of chrysanthemums surrounded the tombstone and the cemetery.

Soon, the entire grave was surrounded by chrysanthemums, their sweet fragrance wafting through the air.

Such a grand memorial service was typically reserved for the passing of truly influential figures, yet here it was before a humble grave.

At this time, Alexander began walking toward Asher.

His footsteps echoed like heavy blows pounding Asher's heart.

Asher, Axel. Bow and apologize!"

Alexander roared.

At the same time, countless men in black shouted in unison, "Bow! Apologize!"

The deafening command shook Axel and Asher to their core, leaving them trembling uncontrollably.

Ryder and his men were so frightened they collapsed to the ground, their courage shattered.

The scene was suffocating, brimming with murderous intent.

"Bow!"

Alexander ordered again.

"Swish!"

Countless gun barrels turned toward Axel and Asher.

The two men, their faces livid, were on the verge of spitting blood. They were among Kingsdon's elite, yet here in Loscity, they were being humiliated by a gang leader.

"You won't bow? Do you want to die?"

Alexander's voice was ice-cold.

Asher's face turned an ugly shade of purple as he trembled.

Grinding his teeth, he tried to stand firm.

He said, "Alexander, stop trying to scare me! Mr. Gavin Lawson and others are watching. Would you really dare kill us in front of them?

"Besides, West Cemetery belongs to the Vance family now. I'll dig up this grave no matter what!"
