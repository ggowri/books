"Daddy, I can't... please, no more. I've already come three times, I almost couldn't keep quiet... just finish, please!"

Cloe was crying, practically begging—she just couldn't handle any more.

Alexander was close too. He gripped her hips hard, pushing for one last burst of intensity.

With a final surge, Alexander let go, and Cloe let out a high-pitched whimper before collapsing entirely.

If Alexander hadn't been holding her, she would've crumpled onto the floor.

After a few minutes of rest, Cloe managed to pull herself together, regaining her composed, elegant CEO demeanor.

The two of them headed into the auction hall.

The room was already packed with over a hundred people, and more guests were constantly arriving.

Alexander and Cloe quietly took seats in the back, staying low-key to observe the room.

A young woman in the front rows turned her head, her eyes widening in surprise when she saw Alexander.

It was the same woman from earlier—the one Alexander had saved. One of the Ghost.

The woman looked excited as she approached Alexander, giving him a slight bow,

"Thank you for saving me this morning. My name is Cynthia Bridges, it's great to see you again!"

At the same time, she discreetly waved her hand, releasing a fine, colorless powder onto Alexander's leg.

"Yeah, sure."

Alexander gave a cold smile, watching her every move.

Whatever that powder was, probably some drug or poison, it wouldn't work on him—not with his Force shielding him.

Seeing Alexander's indifferent response, Cynthia awkwardly returned to her seat.

Two seats over from her sat a stern-faced middle-aged man.

He had a square jaw and an expression like the whole world owed him money.

The man exchanged a quick glance with Cynthia before turning to look at Alexander, pretending it was casual.

Alexander could sense the man's murderous intent—definitely one of Cynthia's associates, clearly a seasoned killer.

The middle-aged man smirked, pulling out his phone to type a message.

Cynthia was also busy on her phone—it was obvious they were plotting something.

"Heh..."

Alexander couldn't help but smile, his eyes clear and amused. To him, they were nothing more than ants.

The higher-ups in the Ghost must really underestimate him if they thought these two could take him.

At that moment, an auction staff member approached them.

He froze for a second when he saw Cloe, clearly shocked to see the CEO attending the auction.

Cloe leaned in and whispered a few words to him, and he quickly handed over an auction catalog.

"Go do your job. Pretend you don't know me."

The staff member nodded quickly and walked away.

More and more people were entering the auction hall, and the room, which could fit five hundred, was now more than half full.

Meanwhile, the middle-aged man next to Cynthia kept glaring at Alexander like a hawk.

Cloe frowned slightly, a hint of concern in her eyes. The Ghost was no joke—it represented the interests of some of the most powerful people in the world.

If they were targeting Alexander, there was no way they'd send just two small-time operatives.

"Sir, should I call in some backup?"

Cloe looked to Alexander, waiting for his decision.

Alexander smiled. "No need to worry. I've got this."

He took Cloe's hand and led her forward a few rows, sitting right next to Cynthia.

Both Cloe, Cynthia, and the middle-aged man were stunned, clearly unsure of what Alexander was planning.

"Ms. Bridges, you keep trying to get close to me, even tried to poison me. What exactly does the Ghost want from me?"

Alexander suddenly asked, his voice calm and direct.

Cynthia nearly jumped out of her seat, her face a mix of shock and confusion. She hadn't expected her cover to be blown so easily.

The middle-aged man's eyes went wide, his face full of hostility, ready to make a move at any moment.

Cynthia took a deep breath, trying to stay composed, then sneered,

"Alexander, I'll admit you're impressive. But let me give you some advice—play smart. Hand over the formula for the magic powder and beauty ointments, and give up Treasure Island and Devil Island."
