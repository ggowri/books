"So itchy! Help! Someone, please help me!"

Cedric wailed, his face contorted in agony as he scratched his body with a ferocity that tore at his skin.

Blood began to trickle from the fresh wounds, but Cedric was beyond caring.

The itch was all-consuming, gnawing at his very bones, driving him to the brink of madness.

"What's happening to him?"

"Is it contagious? Everyone, stay back! Someone call an ambulance, quick!"

Bystanders watched in horror as Cedric, acting like a man possessed, tore off his clothes and scratched his body into a bloody mess. His flesh was raw and bleeding, but still, he clawed at himself without mercy.

It was a scene out of a horror movie. Cedric, covered in blood, looked more like a monster than a man. The crowd recoiled in fear, keeping their distance.

"Itchy, so itchy! Please, someone help me! Save me! Scratch this itch! Quick, please!"

His cries for help went unanswered as his blood-stained clothes lay discarded on the ground.

Just as Cedric was nearing the brink of insanity, the ambulance finally arrived.

By then, his face and body were a grotesque mess of blood and torn flesh.

To make matters worse, the skin he had scratched open now burned like fire as if roasted over hot coals.

"So Itchy and painful! Just kill me!"

Cedric wished he could find a rock to bash his head in—anything to end the torment. The pain was worse than any torture he could imagine.

Looking around, he realized with horror that many people were filming him with their phones.

Stripped bare, he was a bloody mess. Shock and curiosity gleamed in the eyes of the onlookers, some even live-streaming his humiliation.

Cedric was so enraged that his vision swam, and the world around him blurred. The last thing Cedric felt was the hard ground beneath him before everything went dark.

"Quick! Get him to the hospital!"

The paramedics rushed in, loading Cedric onto a stretcher and whisking him away from his public nightmare.

"Huh, what's going on over there?"

Olivia noticed a crowd gathering, sirens blaring, police cars, and an ambulance on the scene.

Alexander chuckled. He knew exactly what was happening.

Using the Force to scramble Cedric's nervous system, Alexander had given him a taste of illusion—one that would render him a broken man for the rest of his life.

"Let's not bother with that. Getting the exchange back is more important."

"Alright, follow me."

Olivia nodded, and together, they made their way toward the market.

The massive sports arena was a buzzing hive of activity, with thousands of stalls and a sea of people milling about.

Olivia led Alexander to a locker room protected by a keypad lock.

She punched in the code, and the door clicked open.

Inside, Olivia rummaged through the lockers for a moment. Then, with a soft "click", one of the lockers shifted. A large ventilation duct was revealed behind it.

"This is my father's secret passage. It leads to the underground exchange. The Mafia doesn't know about it."

Hand in hand, they slipped into the duct.

A few minutes later, they emerged into a vast underground hall.

Alexander never would have imagined that beneath Rose Market lay a hidden exchange like this.

The vast, open floor was meticulously arranged, with rows upon rows of display cases showcasing a stunning array of jewels, antiques, and valuable memorabilia.

But what really caught Alexander's eye were the piles of rough gemstone ore scattered around the area.

In front of each mound of ore was a small sign providing details about the origin of the stones and what kind of gems might be found within.

Dozens of staff in sleek black suits moved among the guests attending the trade show, offering their assistance.

Most of the gemstone ore had been shipped all the way from Gatheolia and places like Soutaxia.

A number of jewel merchants and a few guests with a keen interest in stone speculation were already examining the rough stones.

Stone speculation was simple, really. One relied on one's eye for detail and one's experience to pick a stone. If it contained a gem, one hit the jackpot. If not, well, one was out of luck.

But for Alexander, this was a piece of cake. His All-seeing Eyes allowed him to see straight through the ore.

He could instantly tell whether a stone held a gem or was worthless.

To everyone else, these oddly shaped rocks were nothing more than ordinary stones. But to Alexander, they were something else entirely.
