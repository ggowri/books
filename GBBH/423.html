Bang! Bang! Bang!

A series of loud crashes echoed throughout the bar.

Kelvin and his men were thrown to the ground in a chaotic pile.

"Ouch! My hand's broken!"

"My back... I can't move..."

Alexander had intended to teach them a harsh lesson, then throw them out of the Alliance, prompting Betty to tighten the rules for future members.

Crash!

The bar doors burst open with a thunderous slam.

A tall, imposing man in black strode inside, followed by a legion of men dressed in identical dark suits, moving in unison with an air of unstoppable force.

Seeing this, Kelvin's eyes brightened with relief.

"Mr. Wells is here!"

He scrambled over, clutching Maurice's leg and pleading loudly.

"Mr. Wells... you're finally here. You've got to stand up for us!"

The others followed suit, presenting their wounds as if auditioning for sympathy.

Maurice fell silent.

His expression was steely, ignoring them as he fixed his gaze on Alexander and Betty.

"I'm screwed," thought Maurice.

He gave a slight tremor, realizing that these idiots had actually messed with boss Alexander and Betty.

Maurice was merely a lieutenant selected by Logan, managing only a handful of men.

"Mr. Wells, please avenge us!"

Kelvin continued his wails, oblivious to the storm brewing.

"Damn it!"

Maurice roared with fury.

"Yeah! Damn it, these scumbags—agh!"

But before he could finish, Maurice punched him square in the nose, worsening his already bloody face and knocking him out cold.

Maurice marched up to Alexander and Betty and knelt abruptly.

"Mr. Hawthorne, Miss Rowe, my deepest apologies for my lack of discipline. Please, punish me as you see fit!"

He dared not reveal that Alexander was the true leader of the Ultimate Alliance.

"Please, Mr. Hawthorne and Miss Rowe, punish me!"

His men followed suit, dropping to their knees before the two, filling the bar with a chilling tension that made it hard to breathe.

Kelvin and his friends froze, dumbfounded. Who was this "Miss Rowe" they were all terrified of?

Then, a flash of realization struck Kelvin—Betty Rowe?

His head spun as the pieces fell into place.

With an icy tone, Betty issued her orders. "Our Ultimate Alliance has no place for trash like this. This fool deserves to keep his life but not his dignity—take one hand and throw them all out of the Alliance.

"You failed in your duties. One month's pay docked."

"Thank you, Mr. Hawthorne and Miss Rowe!"

Relieved, Maurice gave a respectful nod.

He turned to Kelvin and the others with a menacing grin.

"No! I'm sorry... I didn't know she was Miss Rowe. Please, forgive me...

"It's a misunderstanding! A complete misunderstanding!"

They fell to their knees, begging.

Alexander ignored the scene, wrapping his arm around Betty's waist as they left the bar.

Maurice and his men closed in, and the bar filled with screams and pleas for mercy.

Outside, Alexander steadied Betty, who leaned into him as they made their way to the parking lot.

Betty, slightly tipsy, nestled against Alexander, one hand clutching him, the other wandering, sparking his desire.

Betty looked absolutely stunning in her lightly intoxicated state!

Her flushed cheeks, alluring curves, and plunging neckline revealed her fair, voluptuous figure, swaying with each step, enticing beyond words.

Alexander couldn't resist slipping his hand beneath her neckline.

"Mm..."

Betty moaned softly, instinctively pressing closer.

But Alexander suddenly paused, his sharp gaze snapping to the nearby street corner.

There was a murderous intent in the air.

"Well, look who we have here!"

A white-haired old man leaning on a cane limped toward them.

His wrinkled face gleamed with excitement as if he'd found a hidden treasure.

Behind him was a sharply dressed middle-aged man who locked eyes on Alexander like a predator.

"Mr. Toynbee, it's him. He's the bounty target—Alexander Hawthorne!"

The man stepped forward, positioning himself alongside Antony Toynbee to block Alexander's path.

Antony licked his lips, sneering darkly.

"So it is him. This walking jackpot of a hundred million dollars."

A sinister aura surrounded them as the tension mounted, snapping Betty out of her drunken haze.
