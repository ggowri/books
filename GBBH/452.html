Dozens of bodies lay scattered across the corridor, their twisted forms telling of the violence that had just transpired.

The once pristine floor was now a crimson canvas, painted with the blood of the fallen and littered with bloody footprints, the air thick with the pungent smell of blood.

The remaining individuals dared not obstruct Alexander any longer, their faces etched with terror as they retreated.

With a cold expression, Alexander sheathed his Lightsaber, the blade remarkably free of any bloodstains.

Having gleaned no useful information, Alexander strode out of the Pegasus Club with a heavy gait.

Ava, growing impatient and anxious, inquired, "Are you alright? Did you find Ashley?"

Alexander gestured sharply. "Search the place!"

Alice immediately led a hundred individuals into the Pegasus Club, scouring every nook and cranny, but to no avail.

Alexander's disappointment was palpable. He suppressed the rage simmering within and departed.

The massive convoy headed towards the Red Umbrella Group's headquarters, disappearing into the night.

In the meantime, the news of Kyle's demise reached Felix with haste.

"What? Kyle is dead? Impossible! He was a level-one metahuman with pyrokinetic abilities. How could he be killed by Alexander?"

Felix rose from his seat in a fit of rage, slamming his hand on the table.

"Mr. Vanderbilt, Alexander is leading dozens of vehicles towards us, estimated to be two to three hundred people. What do we do now?" his subordinate urgently reported.

Felix gritted his teeth and asked, "How many men can we gather right now?"

"We don't have enough men in Loscity. At most, we can gather about a hundred, and many have never seen combat."

The subordinate glanced at Felix fearfully.

"A bunch of trash!"

Felix slapped him, sending him reeling from the impact.

"Get Raven involved immediately. I want Alexander dead at any cost!"

"Yes, sir, right away!"

The subordinate, clutching his stinging cheek, hurried off.

Alexander's convoy arrived at the heavily fortified estate that served as the Red Umbrella Group's headquarters.

It was late at night. The faint moonlight cast long shadows, and an eerie silence enveloped the area, broken only by the glow of a few street lamps.

Using his All-seeing Eyes, Alexander scanned the surroundings. Dozens of eyes were watching within a radius of three hundred feet.

Some belonged to the Red Umbrella Group's hidden sentries, but many more were bounty hunters eager to claim the half-a-billion bounty.

Ava's hefty reward had proven effective. Although Ashley had not been found, the manor was effectively under siege.

"What do we do now? Should we storm in together?" Ava asked, her concern for Alexander's safety evident.

Alexander, however, stared coldly into a shadowy corner and bellowed, "Come out. There's no use hiding. I've already seen you!"

"You actually spotted me? It seems you do have some real skills."

A raspy male voice echoed, and a lean middle-aged man emerged from the shadows.

Alexander appraised him. The man had brown hair and pale skin, wore a black garment, and carried a slender rapier at his waist, his feet bare against the ground.

His piercing blue eyes radiated a chilling coldness.

Alexander asked coldly, "Are you with the Red Umbrella Group?"

The man's eyes narrowed as he scrutinized Alexander before chuckling.

"I am Raven Morris, Deputy Head of Security for the Red Umbrella Group."

Alexander waved his hand impatiently.

"Cut the chatter. Hand over my daughter immediately, or I'll slaughter all of you!"

Raven sneered, his eyes narrowing with murderous intent.

He glanced at Alexander and said, "Alexander, don't think that defeating Kyle makes you invincible. If you want to charge in, you'll have to ask my sword first."

He gripped the hilt with his right hand and slowly drew out the long sword.

It was an extremely slender rapier, its sharp tip gleaming with an intimidating silver light, giving the impression that it could pierce through steel plates.

Rosalia warned quickly, "Master, be careful. He is a swordmaster!"

"Swordmaster?"

Alexander's gaze turned more serious.
