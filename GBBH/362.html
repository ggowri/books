Alexander nodded, saying, "You go in first. I'll watch over you from the shadows."

Cloe perked up and instructed her bodyguard to open the iron gate before calmly walking inside.

A strange glint flashed in Alexander's eyes as he looked through Cloe's fitted skirt, catching a glimpse of her full figure.

Her shapely figure and long legs swayed elegantly, captivating his gaze entirely.

Alexander chuckled to himself; this vision was perfect for appreciating beauty.

After waiting a moment for Cloe to enter, Alexander walked over as well.

This time, several men in black stood at the entrance, staring coldly and expressionlessly at him.

"Sir, this is private property. Entry isn't allowed."

Alexander was stopped at the gate.

"I'm your most esteemed guest. You should be welcoming me, not blocking me."

A strange glint appeared in Alexander's eyes as he looked at the lead guard.

The man's mind fogged over, and he stared at Alexander blankly, unable to speak.

"Is this how you treat guests? Move aside!" Alexander commanded.

Alexander's tone turned especially cold and unyielding.

The men shook their heads, a look of confusion crossing their faces as they stammered, "Welcome. Please, come in..."

Alexander ignored them and walked inside.

Using his All-seeing Eyes and Force Control together, Alexander easily subdued them, feeling quite pleased with himself.

As he walked, he took in the decor and layout of the castle-like building.

It felt like stepping into a medieval art gallery. It's an odd mix of Gothic and Baroque styles, creating an unsettling atmosphere.

Alexander circled the ground-floor hall but didn't spot Cloe, and with a glint in his eyes, he realized she was on the second floor.

He made his way upstairs quietly, staying alert and scanning his surroundings.

Alexander was confident he'd detect anyone approaching, but facing a legendary sorcery lich, he couldn't afford to be careless.

As he moved further along the second floor, the decor shifted drastically.

The windows grew smaller, intricately carved doors remained closed, and the dim lighting added a dark and eerie vibe.

The walls were covered in various paintings, most of which were disturbing and macabre.

Twisted faces, crying children, bloodstained weapons... the paintings were shockingly vivid, even using 3D techniques for a powerful impact.

Alexander paused at the hallway corner, his X-ray vision allowing him to see the scene at the other end without turning.

At that moment, Cloe was staring blankly at the paintings on the wall while her loyal guards had similarly dazed expressions.

"Mr. Rogers, do you think real demons exist in this world?" Cloe asked.

Her voice wavered with fear, and her curvy figure trembled slightly, stirring certain desires in men.

"Heh heh, you're perceptive, Ms. Bryant. You see the true meaning of this painting!"

Henry was a refined, middle-aged man.

He had a square face, thick eyebrows, and large eyes, with a tall, well-built frame in a sharp, expensive suit, exuding a noble aura.

His eyes, in particular, were bright and intense, with an eerie glow much like Alexander's.

The first time Alexander saw Henry, his heart tightened.

He was sure now. This man was the sorcery lich who controlled corpses!

At the same time, Alexander felt a growing sense of danger.

Since becoming a Warrior, it had been a long time since he felt this kind of threat. Now, as an Intermediate Warrior with All-seeing Eyes, he was stronger than ever.

This was a Warrior's sixth sense, only triggered in the presence of formidable foes.

Alexander controlled his breathing and merged into the shadows, making himself nearly invisible.

"Henry is no ordinary foe. Better be cautious!"

Liches were exceedingly rare, and Alexander had no idea what dark magic a sorcery lich might wield.
