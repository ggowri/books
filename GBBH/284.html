Lara hesitated, unsure of how to respond. Alexander's displeasure was immediate.

"What kind of attitude is that? Ms. Hansen is your boss, and she needs permission to see her own grandfather?" he demanded.

Mateo gave Alexander a cold look.

"Alexander, this is a private Hansen family matter. It doesn't concern you. I've told you, Mr. Hansen is meeting an important guest, and not just anyone can interrupt."

It was clear Mateo had no intention of respecting Lara. The two men in black behind him moved aggressively towards Alexander.

"Step aside!"

In a swift motion, Alexander grabbed one by the throat and tossed him aside. Fortunately, he held back, and the men only stumbled.

Mateo quickly drew his gun, pointing it at Alexander, escalating the tension.

"What are you doing? Put the gun away and let them in," Ralph's weak voice called from inside the room.

Reluctantly, Mateo shot Alexander another fierce glare but holstered his gun and stepped aside. Alexander walked forward, deliberately bumping Mateo, causing him to stagger and glare even more.

Inside, Alexander saw Ralph seated with a stern-looking man who radiated authority. A bodyguard in a crisp military uniform stood behind him. All eyes were on Alexander as he entered.

Lara quickly said, "Grandpa, I invited Alexander to help you."

Ralph pulled his granddaughter close, smiling bitterly.

"Lara, I appreciate your concern, but my time is running out. I really don't have much longer..."

The man beside Ralph studied Alexander and then smiled.

"Seeing is believing. I didn't expect the developer of the magic powder to be so young."

Ralph introduced him.

"This is Mr. Thompson, the Commander of Northern Command!"

Alexander and Lara were taken aback. A general had arrived, and he outranked Schroder, yet they seemed to be on opposing sides.

Quentin chuckled.

"Schroder doesn't know I'm in Loscity. His greed has clouded his judgment. His actions are his own, not the military's."

Alexander felt relieved; direct confrontation with the military was unwise. While he could defend himself, his family and friends might suffer.

Since Schroder was acting independently, Alexander saw a chance to set things right.

Quentin's smile faded as he spoke seriously.

"My visit is related to the magic powder. The military wants to collaborate with you and purchase it as a strategic reserve at a fair price. Its benefits for alertness, pain relief, and potential enhancement are remarkable. I hope you won't refuse, Alexander."

Alexander asked calmly, "What do you consider a fair price?"

Quentin thought for a moment.

"Half the market price. But there are additional benefits. You'll become a special military supplier and receive the rank of Major."

"Half?!" Alexander felt conflicted, but the role of special supplier and Major was appealing.

"Mr. Thompson, isn't half too low? It should be at least two-thirds; otherwise, I'd rather pass on being a supplier or a Major."

In truth, the cost of magic powder was about one-fifth, so even at half the market price, Alexander would profit greatly.

The bodyguard behind Quentin shouted angrily, "How dare you speak to Mr. Thompson like that?"

Quentin raised a hand to silence him, then spoke with interest.

"I hear your medical skills are exceptional and you know some sorcery. If you can cure Ralph, I might have another task for you. The price is negotiable if you can help."

"Alright, it's a deal!" Alexander agreed without hesitation and approached Ralph. He began by visually examining Ralph, then used Force Sense to assess his condition. Ralph watched with a frown, still skeptical of Alexander's abilities.
