The hall fell silent; clearly, the Federer family was still in shock.

As the head of the Federer family, Gary had experienced plenty of accidents and was the first to regain his composure.

"Alexander, how dare you cause trouble in the Federer family!" he yelled while reaching for his gun.

Edward's state and Alexander's sudden intrusion explained everything.

Gary's eyes twitched with fear and unease.

Several bodyguards quickly drew their guns and aimed at Alexander.

Seeing this, Gary felt a bit courage coming back.

"Alexander, are you stupid? You think you can just break into the Federer family on your own? I'm still alive, and neither I nor my family is finished yet!"

"Ha, do you really think a dozen of them can do anything to me?"

Alexander laughed.

"Gary, you kidnapped of Ava, so you are dead to me. I'll give you a chance—kill yourself to spare the rest of the Federer family."

Gary was taken aback for a moment, then burst into laughter.

"That's a load of nonsense, haha!

"Alexander, you're under my control right now. You'll be shot into death with just a word from me! You want me to kill myself? Are you insane?"

Not only did Gary think Alexander was insane, but the rest of the Federer family joined in mockery.

Alexander sneered. "You really don't understand the situation here!"

He strode toward Gary, completely ignoring the armed guards.

"What are you doing? Looking for death? Before you die, hand over the magic powder formula, or I'll send your sick daughter to hell!"

Gary quickly backed away while shouting angrily.

Hearing Gary threatened him with his daughter infuriated Alexander.

His daughter was his bottom line, and anyone who dared to target his daughter would die.

"Stop right there!"

One bodyguard fired a shot at Alexander's feet, trying to stop him.

Alexander vanished from sight the moment they heard the gunshot.

The guard who fired screamed in agony as Alexander twisted his right hand into a grotesque claw-like shape.

It was incredibly fast.

Everyone was stunned; no one could follow Alexander's movements.

The guard had shot first, yet the shot and his scream occurred almost at the same time—it was unbelievable how fast Alexander was.

Gary felt a chill down his spine, drenched in cold sweat.

He was also skilled, but he had never seen speed like Alexander's.

"Edward, what happened to that my 100 people?"

Gary turned to Edward, demanding answers.

Edward hung his head, looking defeated.

"They're all dead; guns are useless against him... he's not human; he's a Death from hell!"

The hall fell into strange silence, the atmosphere thick with tension.

Gary's eyes widened in disbelief as he gritted his teeth and said,

"This is unacceptable, absolutely impossible!"

Edward sighed. "Master, you shouldn't go against Alexander, and you definitely shouldn't have kidnapped Ava. If you don't want to drag the Federer family down with you, just take the easy way out."

Gary was furious, glaring at him menacingly.

"You want me to kill myself for the sake of the Federer family? Edward, you idiot, do you even know what you're saying? I am the head of the Federer family, representing its dignity and prestige!"

The hall was filled with murmurs.

"Over a hundred people are missing. We're really in danger this time; Gary shouldn't have provoked this guy."

Some began to voice their opposition to Gary.

"We could have made a lot of money as agents for magic powder. Why did we have to steal the formula? Now we've run into a hard nut."

"Yeah, the master has been acting reckless this time; it was poorly considered. We need to find a way out."

"Why not talk with Mr. Hawthorne? Let's both take a step back."

Those opposing Gary seized the opportunity to chime in.

"Rebellion! Rebellion! You traitors!"

Gary angrily cursed, making him resolute in his decision.

"Kill him! Kill him!"

With a command from Gary, gunfire erupted as over a dozen bodyguards opened fire on Alexander.
