The laboratory housed dozens of high-tech glass pods.

Inside one of them, Alexander lay motionless, his body enveloped in a silver liquid.

Suddenly, the pod trembled violently.

A nearby researcher jumped in fright and immediately sounded the alarm.

The door slammed open. Yinson strode in, kicked the researcher to the ground, and snapped, "Useless idiot!"

Behind him stood over a dozen elderly men, all impeccably dressed and exuding an air of authority. Each one was a figure of immense power.

Yinson smirked. "Impressive. Alexander broke through the first scenario so quickly. Fine, let's raise the stakes. Initiate the second scenario!"

He pressed a red button and barked, "Draw his blood! Gather every data point possible! If we want the Immortality Serum to be perfect, we must uncover the secrets hidden within Alexander."

One of the elders hesitated. "Mr. White, are you sure he won't wake up?"

Yinson sneered. "Relax. I injected him with ten times the normal dose of the Immortality Serum. The moment he shows signs of waking up, I'll increase the dosage and plunge him into a new illusion.

As long as he keeps pushing his limits, we'll eventually extract every last secret from him."

The elders nodded eagerly. Their eyes gleamed with a predatory hunger like wolves eyeing a defenseless lamb.

*****

"Anyone home? Delivery for you!"

Still half-asleep, Alexander opened the door to find an elderly man in his sixties or seventies standing outside. The old man had kind eyes and a warm smile, holding up a package.

Alexander tore it open and found two movie tickets inside.

A strange musk-like scent wafted up from the package. He glanced down at his right hand and noticed a droplet of golden liquid on the back of it.

Before he could react, the droplet vanished. He found it odd but didn't dwell on it.

What he didn't know was that on the left side of his chest, just over his heart, a faint, palm-sized golden silhouette had begun to form beneath his skin.

The shadowy figure seemed to be holding something, pulsing ever so slightly with a dim golden glow in sync with his heartbeat.

Alexander cupped his hands around his mouth and shouted, "Logan! Get up! We're going to the movies!"

"Alexander, it's way too early..."

A chubby man emerged from the back room. His face was identical to Logan's in Alexander's memories.

They arrived at the theater and followed the crowd into the screening room.

The seats were state-of-the-art, resembling astronaut chairs. Each had a circular viewing helmet attached. They settled in and put on their helmets.

The movie began. On the massive screen, bold letters flashed: [Warrior of Triumph over Demons]

A smaller line followed: [Heaven's Rule Film & TV Production.] Then came the credits: [Director: The Best Director. Producer: Top Producer. Starring: Leading Actor Award Winner...]

"What the hell is this?" Logan exclaimed.

Alexander chuckled. This was going to be interesting.

A triumphant, soaring soundtrack filled the theater. A massive blue planet came into view. Two enormous landmasses floated upon an endless ocean, their northern tips barely touching.

To the east lay the Sahasia, shaped like a mighty rooster, ruled by the dominant Hrodvik Dynasty.

To the west was Noah Continent, a vast inverted triangle, where the Noah Empire reigned supreme.

Between the two continents lay countless islands, led by the nations of Risun and Xhantao, which together formed the Oceanic Federation.

The beat of war drums thundered through the theater.

Alexander and Logan were completely absorbed in the film when suddenly, the screen flickered. The voltage was unstable. Alexander's helmet began flashing on and off.

"Shit! It's going to catch fire!" Alexander yanked at the helmet. Logan rushed to help.

As soon as Alexander pulled the helmet off, a thick plume of smoke poured out. A faint golden glyph shot out from inside and struck Alexander directly in the left side of his chest.

"Shit!"

Agonizing pain ripped through him. Both he and Logan screamed at the top of their lungs, "Fuck!"

Everything went black. A crack in space itself split open before them. In the blink of an eye, the void swallowed them whole.

Alexander had no idea how much time had passed. Somewhere in the darkness, he vaguely heard someone calling his name.
