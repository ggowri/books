Just as Alexander was about to act, a deep, authoritative voice suddenly rang out.

"Howard, who are you arguing with?"

A middle-aged man, impeccably dressed in a sharp suit, stepped out from inside.

"Dad, come here quickly! Grace has been tricked by this fraud. He promised to treat Gavin, but it's all a lie!"

Howard, seeing the middle-aged man, instantly brightened, rushing forward to be the first to report the "wrongdoings."

Grace quickly introduced, "Jaden, this is Alexander. I brought him here to treat Gavin."

"Are you Alexander?"

Jaden's brow furrowed, his sharp gaze scanning Alexander from head to toe.

Alexander met his stare without flinching, and the intensity in his eyes caused Jaden to pause, a brief flicker of surprise crossing his face.

Jaden's voice was cold as he addressed Alexander, "Mr. Hawthorne, I remember you claiming to have healed Gavin's old injury, is that right?"

Alexander nodded, "That's correct. And I believe this time—"

Before he could finish, Jaden cut him off with a swift wave of his hand.

"Mr. Hawthorne, the Lawson family has already given you a chance. The truth is, not only did you fail to heal Gavin, but you've made his condition worse. Now, he's hanging on by a thread.

"This time, it's over. Please leave," Jaden added firmly.

Grace, frustrated and unwilling to give in, quickly interjected, "Jaden, after Alexander treated Gavin last time, didn't Gavin's health improve significantly? Alexander believes that someone's tampering with his treatment this time.

"We have to let Alexander try again. He's the only one who can save Gavin!"

She grabbed Alexander's arm, pulling him toward the room.

Jaden stepped forward, blocking their path, "I'm sorry, but I've already hired a renowned expert to treat him. You can't go in and disturb the treatment."

"A renowned expert?" Grace paused, clearly surprised.

Jaden's face showed a smug satisfaction as he continued, "Yes, I've hired Professor Matt, a Bell Medical Award recipient. He's a top expert in cardiovascular and neurological fields.

"Just before I came out, Professor Matt was treating Gavin, and his condition has already shown significant improvement!"

Grace's heart raced at the news, her face lighting up with hope. "Jaden, is this true? Has Gavin really improved?"

Grace became visibly agitated, her sole concern being Gavin's health. Whether or not Alexander had truly healed him was of little importance to her.

Alexander let out a quiet sigh of relief. It seemed that Gavin hadn't been sabotaged—his condition must have been due to a sudden illness after all.

Howard, seeing that Alexander had fallen silent, couldn't resist sneering, "Well, well. Earlier, you were so cocky, but now? We've got Dr. Matt here, a giant in the medical world. He's a real heavyweight—someone whose reputation is known worldwide. You're no match for him."

Jaden shot a cold glance at Alexander before turning back to Grace. "Grace, now that Gavin's condition has improved, we don't need outsiders causing a scene. Let's not disturb him."

Before Grace could respond, a cry of alarm rang out from inside the room.

"Disaster! Disaster! Gavin's deteriorating fast... he's not going to make it!"

"What?!"

Everyone froze in shock, particularly Jaden, who staggered back in horror.

His face went pale, and he quickly abandoned any thoughts of Grace or Alexander, rushing inside with frantic urgency.

"Dad, please hang on!" he shouted, panic seizing him as he entered the room.

Inside, chaos had erupted. The room was in disarray, with people shouting and crying, the sound of panic filling the air.

Gale, Grace's father, was there too, his eyes bloodshot, his fists clenched tightly.

Unlike his incompetent brother Jaden, Gale knew the true weight of the Lawson family's position. It wasn't just about wealth or power—it was about Gavin.

Gavin was the pillar of the Lawson family.

As long as Gavin stood strong, the family could thrive.

But if something happened to him, their influence, their status—it would all come crashing down.

Their enemies would waste no time seizing the opportunity, ready to tear the Lawson family apart.

Grace's face turned ashen as she grabbed Alexander's hand, pulling him into the room with her.

Alexander's eyes immediately found the center of the chaos—a bed where Gavin lay, motionless. His face was a sickly gray, his body completely still.

A team of medical staff surrounded him, working frantically with advanced equipment, but there was no response.

His eyes were shut tight, and the air around him was thick with the smell of death.

A fresh, bright red stain on the pillow beside him was a clear sign that he had coughed up blood just moments ago.
