"Mr. Cooper..."

At Toby's level, he usually wouldn't take on ordinary cases, and his fees were extremely high; it was surprising he'd get involved in such a minor case.

Just as Rowan was feeling uneasy, an even more shocking scene unfolded.

Yeats Porter from Loscity Court arrived.

Toby quickly greeted him warmly, saying, "Mr. Porter, I appreciate your help with this case; our client, Mr. Alexander, is innocent."

"No problem, no problem."

Yeats didn't chat with Toby for long; he bypassed him and walked straight to Alexander.

"Mr. Hawthorne, hello, I'm Oscar's nephew. It's an honor to meet you."

Yeats extended his hand to Alexander with great respect, shocking all the lawyers present.

A judge can significantly influence a verdict, and judges wield the power of decision, standing high above others.

Alexander hadn't realized this judge was from the Porter family, so they chatted happily.

Toby's feelings were quite complicated; he initially thought Yeats came because of him, but it turned out it was mainly due to Alexander—this client was truly extraordinary.

Rowan felt a chill in his heart; how could he win this lawsuit now?

Jane, however, scoffed in defiance.

"Ha, Alexander, don't think you can do anything just because Ava pulled a few strings for you. Mr. Poole is a very well-known barrister, and I will definitely win."

Jane still confidently asked Rowan, "Mr. Poole, am I wrong?"

Rowan ignored her, his expression shifting, clearly weighing the pros and cons.

Jane noticed his silence and asked curiously, "Mr. Poole, what do you mean? Why aren't you saying anything?"

Toby burst out laughing.

"Hahaha, since when did Rowan become a barrister? I remember he's not even a full-fledged Advanced lawyer yet, two tiers below a barrister."

Yeats also showed a hint of mockery.

Rowan felt extremely awkward; he wanted to retort but couldn't find the words since Toby's statement was factual.

Jane finally noticed something was off; the Mr. Poole who had been so confident suddenly seemed like a different person.

Rowan sighed and said, "Ms. Fairfax, I didn't expect Toby to intervene in this case and even bring a judge with him.

"So, we have no chance of winning this case... I advise you to back off. As for the lawyer fees, I'll return them to you right away..."

Rowan looked incredibly awkward.

"Mr. Poole, do you even know what you're saying? I have to win this lawsuit!"

Jane was fuming with rage.

She knew Alexander had money now, and while she regretted it, she still viewed him through old eyes.

Rowan sighed in frustration. "Look at how eager Toby and Mr. Porter are with Alexander; it's clearly flattery. Ms. Fairfax, you've really gotten me into a losing case!"

"That bastard Alexander is just relying on his magic powder, not a real prestigious family."

Jane muttered stubbornly.

If she hadn't forced Alexander to divorce, what kind of glory would she have enjoyed?

Thinking of this made Jane's heart ache as if it were being cut by a knife.

"Ms. Fairfax, I advise you to withdraw your lawsuit quickly; otherwise, you'll face our charges. If you're found guilty of defamation, you'll have to pay a hefty sum and could even go to jail."

Madelyn fixed her intense gaze on Jane.

"Shut up! It's clearly Alexander who scammed our family out of millions. Why should I withdraw the suit? He should apologize and compensate me double, or I won't back down."

Jane shouted like a furious shrew, beside herself with anger.

"What a ridiculous joke!"

"Mr. Poole, what do we do now? Is there really no way out? You're my lawyer; you have to help me..."
