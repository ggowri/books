Grace pouted, clearly annoyed.

"You all forced me into this. I don't like him! He's just a petty guy and a pervert, ugh!" she muttered under her breath.

Gavin sighed, helpless. He had no way of dealing with his granddaughter's stubbornness.

When Alexander got home, he found Lily sitting with Ava, chatting.

"Lily, what are you doing here? You should've just called me! I would've come over to see you," Alexander greeted her with a smile.

Lily wiped the tears from her eyes, then took his hand.

"Alexander, I know you're busy now, but your mother's death anniversary is coming up in two days. We should go pay our respects."

Alexander froze for a second, completely forgetting about it.

"You're right. Let's go now."

*****

Alexander's mother was buried at a cemetery at the foot of a hill on the western outskirts of Loscity.

It was the largest cemetery in the city, with rows of tombstones stretching far into the distance, creating a solemn and heavy atmosphere.

Alexander carried Ashley, and with Lily and Ava, they arrived at the cemetery gate.

Since his mother's anniversary was in two days, he had come early to clean her grave and prepare for the memorial.

As he looked at the cemetery, a place that felt both familiar and foreign to him, Alexander's face grew somber.

His mother had passed away before he could ever give her the life she deserved.

A deep ache in his chest made it hard to breathe.

"Don't be sad," Ava said softly, trying to comfort him. "Your mother would be so proud of everything you've achieved. She'd be happy for you."

Ava wasn't sure how to console him, so she changed the subject, mentioning the deal with Gavin.

"Giving exclusive rights to a few powerful countries is a long-term win. It'll also keep the Ghost from causing us trouble."

Ava was fully on board with the idea.

They might earn a little less, but to Alexander, money was no longer the priority. He had bigger things to worry about.

He had bigger things to worry about. After all, if a real crisis ever hit, money would be useless. The exclusive rights could be traded for food, weapons, and other supplies.

Alexander nodded quietly. "Yeah, I get it."

They continued toward his mother's grave. With every step, Alexander felt the weight of the grief pressing harder on his chest, suffocating him with every breath.

The grave was tucked away in a quiet corner, far from the main paths.

The area was overgrown with weeds, and dead branches scattered around. Alexander hurriedly began clearing the area.

Lily grabbed a broom to sweep the leaves, and Ava used a cloth to clean bird droppings from the tombstone.

None of them spoke. They simply worked in silence.

Alexander crouched down in front of the tombstone, his tears flowing freely.

He had never known his father. His mother was all he had from childhood.

Her face, so familiar yet fading with time, was growing harder to remember. But he tried, and with effort, he could still bring her image to life in his mind.

"Mom, I brought Ashley, Lily, and Ava to see you," he said softly, his hand gently brushing over the tombstone as if he could feel her smile.

Even someone as strong-willed as Alexander couldn't stop the tears from flowing. The deep, aching longing surged within him, and the pain was almost too much to bear.

Ava felt her heart twist with empathy. She could sense the loneliness and sorrow Alexander had buried deep inside.

Her eyes grew misty, and she whispered something in Ashley's ear.

"Grandma, I'm here to see you!"

Ashley, wise beyond her years, spoke softly, "Rest well, Grandma."

Alexander smiled through his tears, feeling a sense of peace wash over him as he patted his daughter's head. The sadness in his heart, though still present, felt a little lighter.

Lily was crying too, holding onto Ava's hand, letting the tears flow freely.

Just as Alexander finished arranging the flowers he brought, he heard footsteps approaching.

A group of people was coming their way, talking loudly.

"That perfect burial plot is just ahead. We're almost there!"

"Didn't know Sir Vance was from Loscity. This spot is a real find. Once we get it, Sir Vance will reward us big time!"

"Once we get our hands on it, Sir Vance promised I'll become the cemetery manager."

"Congratulations, Ryder!"

Their laughter and congratulations grew louder as they drew nearer.
