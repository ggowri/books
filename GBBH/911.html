Alexander acted swiftly. The rabbit barely had time to lift a claw before he snapped its neck with a sharp twist.

There was a sharp crack as the rabbit's head flopped down, but it still didn't die. Instead, it became even more vicious, clawing at Alexander with renewed fury.

"Damn it. This rabbit must have been bitten by a living dead!"

Alexander pieced together the rabbit's strange behavior with the bite marks on its leg. He was now certain that it was a zombie rabbit.

If the living dead could turn animals into monsters like this, then humans bitten by them were doomed to the same fate.

Alexander recalled the recent wave of disappearances in Loscityg, and it all pointed to Sakra Society's involvement.

The rabbit twisted violently, trying to sink its teeth into his hand.

"Stay dead, will you?" Alexander growled.

With a swift motion, he smashed the rabbit against a nearby rock.

Its body crumpled under the impact, bones snapping like dry twigs. At last, it went limp, motionless.

Alexander glanced at the lifeless creature and shook his head. Any thought of eating it was gone. Without wasting time, he took off in the direction of Base One.

This mission wasn't just about rescuing Hazel. It might also uncover the Sakra Society's dark experiments with the living dead.

The forest grew thicker, the shadows deeper. The mountains loomed high above, and not a soul was in sight.

But the closer Alexander got to the base, the more twisted wildlife he encountered.

Rabbits, wolves, even wild boars and snakes... all turned into fearless zombie beasts.

There was no mistaking it now. The base was experimenting on animals, and some had clearly escaped, unleashing chaos into the wild.

Finally, Alexander spotted the entrance to Base One.

A group of wild boars was nearby, frantically digging into the dirt with their snouts. They were tearing at the ground with such force it was clear they'd found something.

These weren't normal boars. They were massive, their tusks unnaturally long, gleaming white under the moonlight.

Alexander crept closer, his steps silent, trying to see what they were after.

But the boars noticed him almost immediately. One by one, their bloodshot eyes locked onto him.

With enraged grunts, they charged, their bulk shaking the ground as they closed the distance.

Alexander didn't flinch.

A flash of silver lit the air as he activated his lightsaber. The boars didn't even have time to scream before they were split cleanly in two.

He approached their kill to see what had them so ravenous, but his stomach turned at the sight. It was a rotting corpse.

The body was bloated and blackened, far from anything resembling a normal human.

Scattered nearby were broken bones, remnants of previous victims.

It wasn't hard to piece it together. The boars had unearthed the body from shallow soil and feasted on its decaying flesh.

These corpses weren't random. They were failed experiments from the base, dumped and buried here like trash.

Alexander's expression hardened. This was undoubtedly one of the reasons the animals had mutated.

Base One was close now. He clenched his fists, a cold smile tugging at his lips. Hazel would be rescued, and this wretched base would be destroyed.

Now, he just needed to find a way in. The mountain's shadow hid everything in darkness, but Alexander had his x-ray vision.

He quickly scanned the area, relying on the intel provided by Kellen, who had spilled his secrets in exchange for his life.

There, at the base of the mountain, a dense patch of shrubs concealed something unusual.

To anyone else, it looked like an ordinary thicket. But Alexander could see it clearly, a cave hidden behind the foliage, its mouth dark and foreboding.

Inside, the base's steel blast door lay sealed tight, surrounded by electric fences and infrared surveillance.

Charging in through the main entrance would only alert the enemy.

If this was an underground research facility, there had to be a ventilation system.

After a quick search, Alexander found a narrow ventilation shaft hidden under thick underbrush.

The shaft was barely wide enough for a person to squeeze through. A steel grate covered the opening, and the drop inside was pitch-black and endless.

Alexander crouched by the entrance, sizing it up. It was tight but manageable. He took a deep breath, channeling his energy to adjust his body.

With a series of sharp pops, his joints shifted, and his tall frame, normally over six feet, compressed by several inches. His broad shoulders slimmed, his build shrinking just enough to fit.
