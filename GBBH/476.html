"Ten million and five hundred thousand dollars."

The words had just left his mouth when someone immediately raised their paddle.

"Eleven million dollars."

"Eleven million and three hundred thousand dollars."

"Eleven million and five hundred thousand dollars."

Right from the start, the bidding got heated, voices calling out one after another.

"Thirteen million dollars."

Alexander raised his paddle, bumping the price up by over a million dollars without hesitation.

To upgrade the power core of his Level One light sword to Level Two, Alexander needed seven high-quality gemstones in seven different colors.

So, in his eyes, spending tens of millions on this top-grade sapphire would be worth every penny.

Seeing Alexander's aggressive bid, several people in the room became cautious—it was clear he wouldn't back down.

"Thirteen million and five hundred thousand dollars!"

Suddenly, a furious, raspy voice came through.

Alexander turned to look and saw it was Nate Sullivan.

Nate's face was swollen and bruised, and he was staggering back into the room.

He glared at Alexander with pure venom.

"Alexander, you want that gemstone? As long as I'm here, forget about it! My family has money, and today I'm going to make sure you don't get what you want!"

Nate had made up his mind. No matter what, he was determined to mess with Alexander, to try and put him down.

"Fifteen million dollars!"

Alexander smiled at Nate and raised the bid by another million. To him, it was just a drop in the bucket.

As the battle between the two heated up, most others in the room decided to sit back and watch the show, eager to see how it would unfold.

"Think you can scare me? Sixteen million dollars!"

Nate shouted confidently, raising the bid by another million.

"Eighteen million dollars."

Alexander casually upped the ante by two million dollars.

"Eighteen million and one hundred thousand dollars."

Nate's face turned pale. He only raised it by a hundred thousand this time—clearly starting to feel the pinch.

Although the gemstone was indeed top quality, it was small, and its market value was at most seventeen million. Now, the price had already surpassed that by over a million.

"Twenty million dollars."

Alexander spoke lightly, raising the price by nearly two million dollars, utterly calm and composed.

"Damn bastard! This guy's insane!"

Nate cursed through gritted teeth, clearly hesitating.

"Twenty million and one hundred thousand dollars."

He eventually raised it by just one hundred thousand.

But everyone could tell—this was his limit. He couldn't go much higher.

"Alexander, are you an idiot? This gemstone is worth at most eighteen million, and you're willing to pay twenty million dollars? Are you nuts?"

Nate taunted Alexander, trying to rile him up.

Alexander smirked, "You can't afford it, so why act tough? Get lost! I've got money to burn. You don't like it? Too bad. Do something about it!"

"Twenty-one million dollars!"

Alexander added another nine hundred thousand without blinking.

The room erupted in murmurs of shock, everyone buzzing in disbelief as they tried to figure out who Alexander was.

No one understood the importance of the gemstone to Alexander. Even if it cost him two hundred million, he wouldn't hesitate—he needed it.

Seeing Alexander's domineering attitude, Nate knew he'd lost again.

While Nate's family was indeed wealthy, overpaying by millions for a gemstone wasn't smart.

"Fine, you win this time!"

Nate stormed off, frustrated and humiliated.

Ava couldn't hide her delight at seeing Nate put in his place.

As for the twenty million dollars, she didn't give it a second thought—just like Alexander.

"Twenty-one million dollars, first call!"

Leo Jenkins raised the auction gavel, his voice full of excitement.

"Twenty-one million dollars, second call!"

No one else made a sound.

"Third call—sold!"

Leo brought the gavel down hard. The rare blue sapphire was officially Alexander's.

Now, with the rare red and blue gemstones in his possession, Alexander only needed five more—orange, yellow, green, indigo, and violet.

"Ladies and gentlemen, our next item is a rare piece—a bracelet retrieved by an archaeologist from an ancient thousand-year-old tomb. Its material is still unidentified."

Leo swept his gaze across the audience, his tone lifting a few notches.

Hearing Leo's introduction, many people started whispering among themselves.

Their eyes were glued to the stage—it was clear that quite a few attendees had come specifically for this mysterious bracelet.

"A bracelet from an ancient tomb? Made of an unknown material?"

Alexander's interest was piqued.
