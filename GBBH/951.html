The young woman had a stunning appearance and a tall, seductive figure.

Her white training outfit fluttered in the breeze as she moved, each gesture radiating a quiet elegance and martial grace.

Her punches were fast and precise, and her body seemed to flow like water, each movement a fluid expression of power and agility.

As the morning breeze swept through, her outfit clung to her body, revealing her curves in a way that was impossible to ignore.

Alexander wasn't staring at her for the reasons most would. His attention was fixed on the strange energy flowing within her.

This was internal energy, a fundamental force in martial arts, like the Force in some ways.

It was about tapping into one's hidden potential, achieving harmony with nature, and accessing incredible power.

After studying her for a moment, Alexander shook his head.

"The flow of her internal energy is a bit chaotic. But she could easily handle a few regular opponents," he muttered to himself, but the young woman heard it clearly.

Her gaze snapped to him, eyes flashing with irritation.

"What did you just say?" she demanded, stepping into his path.

"You think you can talk down to me? How about we have a little sparring match, huh?"

Alexander smiled lightly, his tone calm and unruffled.

"Sorry, that was a mistake on my part."

But his casual tone only seemed to make her angrier.

"You!" Her face flushed with anger, her voice rising. "How dare you mock me like that?"

She was used to respect, always treated with deference. Never had someone dared to be so rude to her, especially not in front of her grandfather.

"You're asking for it!"

She lunged to grab his wrist, trying to catch him off guard with a quick move.

"Grace, stop!" The old man, who had been watching quietly, spoke up firmly, stopping her in her tracks.

"Grandpa, didn't you see him disrespect me?"

"Quiet!" he snapped, his voice leaving no room for argument.

With a huff, the young woman relented. Her grandfather was the only one who could reign her in like this.

She shot Alexander one last glare, then moved back to stand beside her grandfather.

Alexander glanced at the old man, a faint smile tugging at his lips. There was something vaguely familiar about him, like he'd seen him somewhere before.

The old man, who was calm despite the tension, finally spoke up.

"Mr. Hawthorne, it's a pleasure to meet you. I'm Gavin Lawson, and this is my granddaughter, Grace. We've come to Loscity specifically to find you."

Alexander's demeanor shifted at once, his face becoming serious.

Gavin was a powerful figure, second in command in the Avalora's government.

Grace couldn't help but roll her eyes. "Grandpa, why don't we just grab him and interrogate him? Why bother with the pleasantries?"

Gavin gave her a sharp look, clearly not pleased with her suggestion.

"Look carefully at Mr. Hawthorne. Do you notice anything... unusual about him?"

Grace squinted at Alexander, her eyes narrowing with skepticism.

"What could be so special about him? He's just some guy who's rude and doesn't know his place."

She gave him a once-over, and though he wasn't bad-looking, there didn't seem to be anything extraordinary about him.

Gavin sighed in exasperation.

"After all your years of training, you still don't see it? His breathing. Look at the way he breathes. It's completely different from ours."

"His breathing?" Grace blinked, surprised by the observation.

"Yes, Mr. Hawthorne's breath is steady, controlled, much slower than ours. This means his martial arts are at an incredibly advanced level. Far more advanced than yours.

"I can't believe it... In my lifetime, I never expected to meet someone as skilled as Mr. Hawthorne. No wonder he's able to handle top-tier fighters without breaking a sweat."

Gavin's tone was filled with admiration.

Grace, now noticing the way Alexander's chest barely moved as he breathed, realized her grandfather was right.

His physical conditioning was off the charts, far beyond hers.

"Could he really be stronger than me?" she thought, both amazed and a little frustrated by the realization.

Alexander, however, remained silent. The truth was, calling him a martial artist was an understatement. He was a Warrior.

"So, what brings you to Loscity to find me?" Alexander asked, cutting straight to the heart of the matter.

Grace, seeing his indifference, couldn't hold back her frustration.

"You really are something else. Aren't you going to apologize to my grandfather for your rudeness?"

Alexander just smiled coldly, showing no intention of backing down.
