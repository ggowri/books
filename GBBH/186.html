"Don't rush it, guys. Here, let's have a drink first," Alexander said with a grin, raising his glass.

The three clinked their glasses together and downed their drinks in one go.

As they sipped and chatted, the hours crept closer to midnight, yet there was still no sign of Ian.

Just when Alexander was beginning to think Ian wouldn't show, an excited voice crackled in his earpiece.

"Boss, Ian's here!"

Instantly alert, Alexander turned his gaze toward the entrance of the bar.

The door swung open, and a group of people surged in, surrounding a balding middle-aged man—none other than Ian himself.

Dressed in a gray suit and adorned with a chunky gold chain and a flashy watch, Ian strutted in, his arm draped around a stunning woman in revealing attire.

Ten of his henchmen flanked him, their watchful eyes scanning the crowd for any threats.

Ian's sharp gaze darted around, and after a quick sweep of the bar, he relaxed slightly, a smile creeping onto his face.

He playfully groped the woman, and together, they sauntered down the corridor that led to the back office.

"Boss, you were right! Ian really showed up," Augusta said, his impatience palpable.

Marvin was practically itching to spring into action. If it weren't for Alexander holding him back, he would have charged in already.

Those lackeys didn't stand a chance against him.

But Alexander's attention was fixed on the woman.

A frown creased his brow as he recognized her. He mused, "That's Lillian Fairfax, Jane's cousin. Not exactly a good girl; she's in the nightlife scene at this age? I can't believe she's mixed up with Ian."

"Boss, can we make our move now?" Augusta asked, his excitement bubbling over.

"Not just yet. We've got them hooked; there's no way they'll slip away. Let's wait until they're all inside," Alexander replied with a cold smile.

He watched Ian and Lillian disappear into the corridor, followed closely by Ian's thugs.

Alexander remained calm, biding his time for a few more minutes to ensure no more of Ian's men were entering.

Finally, he exchanged glances with Augusta and Marvin, giving them the signal.

With a shared thrill, they sprang to their feet and made their way toward the back of the bar.

As they approached the corridor, two burly bouncers blocked their path.

One of them, a bald man with a fierce glare, barked, "Are you blind? No loitering allowed here!"

"Tell Ian someone's here to settle a score," Alexander replied, his tone steady and unfazed.

The bald man hesitated, sizing Alexander up. His right hand instinctively moved toward the gun hidden at his waist.

Alexander caught the movement and couldn't help but smirk.

In a flash, Augusta and Marvin lunged forward, each grabbing a bouncer by the neck and twisting.

With a sickening crack, both men collapsed, their bodies discarded into the stairwell.

"This is what they call Loscity's fourth largest gang? They think their lair is a lion's den, but really, they're just trash." Alexander scoffed, stepping past the fallen bodies with confidence.

Augusta and Marvin quickly fell in line behind him.

At the end of the corridor was a lavish private room that looked like an office but held more secrets within.

The interior was as opulent as a five-star hotel, decked out with expensive carpets and handcrafted wooden furniture.

Ian lounged on a plush sofa, Lillian nestled against him.

Her ample boobs were barely contained by her outfit, her short skirt revealing long, flawless legs—half of her backside was visible.

Ian slid his hand beneath Lillian's skirt, eliciting a burst of giggles from her.

Standing nearby were Ian's henchmen, their expressions stone-cold, as if they didn't care about the debauchery unfolding before them.

These were the elite of the Fraternity; Ian wouldn't be so relaxed if they weren't.

Lillian leaned in close, whispering something in Ian's ear that made him laugh heartily, completely unaware of the storm brewing just outside his door.

With a resounding crash, the door burst open, and in strode Alexander and his two companions.

Ian's initial shock quickly subsided when he saw it was just the three of them. He remained unfazed, even keeping his hand inside Lillian's skirt.

"You're Alexander, huh? What do you want with me?"

Ian recognized him and immediately adopted an air of smug superiority.
