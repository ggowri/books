After the ambush, the team's morale didn't falter despite the casualties. In fact, the loss only fueled their determination to fight harder in the future.

Marvin pulled out his phone. "I'll notify Boss and have someone sent for backup."

William nodded. "Good. These supplies are crucial. We can't afford any mistakes."

Alexander was stunned when he received Marvin's call.

Louvian Port wasn't far from Loscity, and the ambush happened so close. Sakra Society was getting too bold.

He immediately called for Augusta to lead a team of elite soldiers, splitting them across several helicopters to provide support.

At the same time, Alexander ordered Betty to deploy every available agent to search for Sakra Society's hideouts in Loscity.

They were known for poison and ambushes, and Alexander was certain they had a secret base in the city.

Alexander also called on Kaylon, the four prominent families of Loscity, and the police to coordinate in tracking down any clues.

After hours of activity, Betty arrived and reported, "Sir, we haven't found any sign of Sakra Society members. What should we do?"

Alexander frowned slightly, pacing the room.

He was certain Sakra Society had set up a base in Loscity, but finding it was like looking for a needle in a haystack.

Suddenly, his eyes lit up. "I'll contact Brittany. She can help us check the city's water bills. That's how we'll find them!"

Betty's eyes widened. "I can't believe I didn't think of that. Sakra Society couldn't have been here long, and with so many people, their water usage must be through the roof.

"We just need to find a place where the water consumption has spiked recently. That's where they'll be!"

Soon, through Brittany's connections in the military, Alexander got his hands on Loscity's water data.

Betty scanned the massive sheets of data, her eyes starting to blur. "I'll gather a team to go through this and pinpoint the suspicious areas."

Alexander waved her off. "No need. I've already found it."

He pointed to a specific address on the report. It was a food processing plant in a small town south of the city.

Betty's mouth hung open in shock. She had no idea what to say.

With so much data, even dozens of people working together would've struggled to spot something this quickly.

But Alexander had glanced at the sheet and found the anomaly almost immediately.

"This factory's water usage has tripled in the last half month, and it's not far from where the convoy was ambushed. It's definitely there!" Alexander's eyes gleamed with cold fury.

Once he had a location, he immediately ordered Betty to prepare a team while he himself got into his black BMW Seven Series. Like a dark stallion, he disappeared into the night.

The highway stretched out before him as the BMW raced through the night, the world outside blurring by.

The pale moon above seemed to sense Alexander's murderous intent, slipping behind a cloud to hide.

At that very moment, three black SUVs were parked at the entrance of the food processing plant.

"Dax, it's time to go. We've lost contact with everyone. Things don't look good. I bet it won't be long before Ultimate Alliance tracks us down," a burly, brown-haired man said from the driver's seat, eyes cold as he watched Dax standing outside.

The muscular man's shirt was half-unbuttoned, his powerful physique on full display. His square jaw was set in a grim expression, with no hint of a smile on his face.

His brown eyes were filled with unmasked hostility as he glared at Dax.

"Mr. Malone, do you really think they'll come? Is that even possible?" Dax wiped the sweat from his brow, still in disbelief.

Tyler Malone snorted derisively, "We underestimated Ultimate Alliance. Alexander is beyond dangerous. We came with forty men, and now we're down to half.

"If we don't leave now, they'll find us, and they'll kill us one by one!"

Dax's face went pale, his voice shaky as he stammered, "Then... what about the one million dollars you promised me? Shouldn't I get that?"

Tyler's temper flared. He pulled out his gun and pressed it to Dax's forehead, sneering, "You greedy bastard! We only stayed in your warehouse for a short time, and we already gave you 200 thousand dollars. Now, you want a million dollars?"

Dax opened his mouth to scream, but before he could, the gunshot rang out.

The sharp sound shattered the stillness of the night, sending birds flying in all directions.

Dax's head exploded, and his lifeless body collapsed to the ground with a heavy thud.

Tyler didn't even spare the corpse a second glance. He slammed his foot down on the gas, and the SUV roared to life.

The other two vehicles followed closely behind, speeding off into the night, headed east.
