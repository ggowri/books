The sound of bones being chewed echoed through the forest, casting an eerie and terrifying atmosphere in the silent mountains.

Alexander pursued the noise. The living dead were gone, leaving behind the mangled remains of Bruno's body on the ground.

Bruno's usually resolute face was twisted in a look of sheer terror as if he had witnessed something utterly horrifying.

A helicopter lifted off from not too far away.

Alexander spotted three living dead hanging from a net beneath the helicopter, swiftly flying away.

He recognized one of the passengers on the helicopter: Jake!

He remembered this guy. He was an expert who had come to Loscity Hospital with Elaine.

It seemed the appearance of these living dead was connected to the Sakra Society.

"The Mafia, City of Sorcery, Sakra Society. I won't spare any of you."

Alexander clenched Lightsaber tightly, his eyes fierce.

On the helicopter, Jake wiped the cold sweat from his face, muttering to himself, "I underestimated him! Luckily, I got out fast. I didn't expect Alexander to be so formidable, not even the living dead could stand against him."

The pilot, Landon, asked, "Mr. Deleon, we didn't complete the mission Mr. White gave us, and we lost three living dead people. What do we do now?"

Jake rolled his eyes, annoyed. "Don't we still have three living dead? You don't get it. As long as we have one living dead, I can make it spawn an army."

Landon looked at him curiously. "How?"

Jake grinned smugly.

"Landon, if you get bitten by a living dead, within a few hours, you'll turn into a low-level zombie, fearless of death, without pain, and with only an instinct for blood.

"The only downside of a low-level zombie is that they can't obey my commands like the living dead. They can only be controlled through the command of the living dead."

Landon was shocked.

He stammered, "So... isn't that just like the Resident Evil? We'll be eaten by zombies!"

Jake chuckled evilly, "It's not the same. We can control the living dead to command the zombies, so the future of the world belongs to us, Sakra Society.

"When Mr. White truly masters the method of immortality, he's bound to initiate a great purge. There are too many humans on this planet, just wasting resources."

Landon's face turned ashen, unable to control his trembling.

"Mr. Deleon, what do we do now?"

Jake pondered for a moment, then said, "Since Alexander is so powerful, a few living dead definitely won't be enough to eliminate him. Find me a remote village. I'll create an army of fearless soldiers there, then lure Alexander in and make sure he dies without even a trace of bones left."

Landon's face grew even worse. He understood that the army Jake referred to would undoubtedly involve using live people to create zombies.

"Are you deaf? Did you hear what I said?"

Jake glared at him unhappily.

Landon quickly nodded. "Yes, I heard you..."

The helicopter flew away from Loscity, heading towards a small village at the foot of the mountains.

Alexander returned to the Ultimate Alliance with a heavy heart.

Everyone cheered when they learned that Bruno was dead.

Alexander couldn't bring himself to smile. He immediately called an emergency meeting with the top brass of the Ultimate Alliance.

"Betty, distribute the weapons. Everyone, stay alert and be prepared for any crisis at any time.

"Marvin, contact Augusta immediately and have him bring a thousand elite troops to Loscity. Speed is crucial.

"Charlize, from now on, you and Rosalia will protect Ava and Ashley around the clock. Without my orders, you are not to leave their sides for a single moment."

"Yes, Boss!" Everyone agreed solemnly.

Although Alexander didn't specify what would happen, the situation was definitely not going to be simple or easy.

After the meeting, Alexander still didn't crack a smile.

The terrifying faces of the living dead occasionally flashed through his mind.

Alexander had a premonition that the Sakra Society was about to unleash a disaster more terrifying than a plague.

Trouble seemed to follow trouble.

"Oh, you're finally back!"

Alexander had just returned home when a beautiful figure threw herself at him, hugging him tightly.

Recognizing the familiar feminine scent, Alexander finally smiled. It was Rebecca.
