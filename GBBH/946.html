Alexander had made up his mind. There was no way he was going to let this grain fall into anyone else's hands.

Yinson would eventually set off a crisis, and when that happened, money wouldn't be enough to get the grain.

At that moment, Cynthia stood up and declared loudly, "We're taking this batch of grain. Five billion and ten million!

"I hope everyone will show some respect for the Ghost and not compete with us. If something goes wrong, don't say we didn't warn you."

It was a blatant threat, and the guests at the auction immediately tensed up.

This was the legendary Ghost. They couldn't afford to cross them.

The room went silent. No one dared to raise their paddle to bid against Cynthia.

Cynthia wore a smug, almost arrogant smile, as if she were above everyone else.

"Five point one billion!"

Alexander raised his paddle, upping the bid.

Cynthia's face darkened with fury. She was about to bid again, but a middle-aged man beside her stopped her.

"No need to rush. Since Alexander wants to go head-to-head with us, I'll take care of him later. He'll buy it, and then it'll all be ours."

Cynthia sneered, her eyes burning with resentment as she glared at Alexander.

The middle-aged man's eyes were equally cold and dangerous.

In the end, Alexander managed to secure the grain for himself.

Next, the auctioneer introduced the second item, a production line from an international arms supplier, capable of producing bullets and grenades of various calibers.

"Ladies and gentlemen, the starting price is one hundred million, with each bid going up by no less than one million."

"One hundred and ten million!"

Alexander made the first bid, his gaze challenging Cynthia.

Strategic materials like this were essential to his plans. If he wanted to turn Treasure Island and Devil Island into doomsday bases, weapons and ammunition were a must.

Cynthia wasn't interested in this outdated production line, but Alexander's challenge had her boiling with anger. Her eyes flashed with murderous intent.

After several rounds of bidding, Alexander won the production line for one hundred and fifty million.

The next items up for auction were valuable antiques, jewelry, and so on. Alexander wasn't interested in any of them and let them all go without a second thought.

The auctioneer's voice trembled as he announced the final item.

"Ladies and gentlemen, the last item is a set of documents related to the immortality serum of the Sakra Society. The starting price is five hundred million, with each bid increasing by no less than ten million."

A murmur spread through the room, and many faces lit up with greed and excitement.

Clearly, everyone was here for the immortality serum.

Such a thing could only be auctioned off on an extralegal island like Treasure Island. Anywhere else, and it would have been seized by authorities.

Cynthia stood up again, her voice dripping with arrogance.

"We came to Treasure Island for two things, and one of them is to buy these documents. Anyone who tries to bid against me is an enemy of the Ghost!"

The room fell silent. Many people were already backing down, fear evident in their eyes.

Others, however, were furious, glaring at Cynthia, unwilling to just let her take it.

The immortality serum was an irresistible temptation for anyone, and those who had come to the island had some power themselves. They weren't going to give up without a fight.

Even the auctioneer had had enough.

He glared at Cynthia and sternly said, "Madam, you've repeatedly broken the rules of this auction. I'm asking you to leave!"

Cynthia was furious. She pointed at the auctioneer and spat, "You're nothing but a small-time nobody, and you dare to expel me? Jaxon, take care of him!"

Suddenly, everything went haywire.

A few barely visible black rays shot out from the middle-aged man's sleeves, faster than lightning, and struck the auctioneer in the face.

"Ah!"

The auctioneer screamed in pain, his hands clutching his face as he collapsed to the floor.

Black poison needles were embedded in his face, which had already turned a dark shade. Blisters formed and rapidly rotted, as if his flesh was dissolving.

The room erupted into chaos as people scrambled to get away.

"Stop! How dare you make trouble on Treasure Island? Guards, seize them!"

Cloe was livid, slamming her hand down on the table.

Dozens of guards rushed in, surrounding Cynthia and the middle-aged man.

"Think you can capture me? You're not even close."

The middle-aged man sneered, his movements a blur as he swung his arms.

Countless black poison needles shot out from his sleeves, like a storm, aimed straight at Alexander and Cloe.
