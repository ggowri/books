The name "Alexander" was now a thorn in Guzman's side, and the Red Umbrella Group was lurking nearby, ready to pounce.

"Guards, arrest Cloe and strip her of her rights!" he ordered without hesitation.

The men in black hesitated briefly before moving to restrain Cloe. Guzman sighed deeply. "Cloe, I've raised you for over twenty years. It pains me that you've made a mistake. I have no choice but to use you as a sacrifice..."

At the mention of "sacrifice," Cloe's face turned pale with horror. Guzman practiced witchcraft, and every month, a woman was sacrificed for his rituals—a gruesome reality that haunted Cloe.

"Dad, why are you doing this to me? Is this really happening?" Cloe's voice shook with fear.

Guzman showed no emotion, his eyes a mix of complex feelings before hardening. He had adopted Cloe because she was perfect for the ritual. He had kept her isolated, forbidding her from relationships to ensure she remained a virgin for the sacrifice.

With Alexander and the Red Umbrella Group closing in, Guzman couldn't delay any longer. "Cloe, this is final. Your sacrifice is your way to repay me for raising you."

His tone was cold, devoid of any fatherly warmth. The room was tense, and while some felt sorry for Cloe, no one dared to speak against Guzman.

He addressed the group, "You are all part of the Bryant family. We're facing a minor challenge now, but once I become a white wizard, the City of Sorcery will support us, and the Brilliant Group will thrive even more!"

The tension eased, and the sympathy for Cloe vanished. Some even began to persuade her. "Cloe, if Mr. Bryant hadn't taken you in, you might be living a life of hardship. You owe him everything."

"Yes, it's time to give back. Your sacrifice will be honored."

"Cloe, thank you for your sacrifice. We'll ensure you're remembered properly."

"With your help, our family will reach new heights!"

The Bryant family members chimed in, their smiles betraying their excitement.

"You beasts! You're all monsters. You should be the ones sacrificed!" Cloe yelled, struggling in vain.

The group ignored her, turning to congratulate Guzman. "Mr. Bryant, congratulations on your upcoming advancement!"

Guzman raised his hand for silence. "Prepare the altar! The ritual begins now!"

With Guzman's command, everyone grew solemn. Guzman stood tall, his gaze fixed on Cloe with a fierce intensity.

A steel wall in the room slid open with a series of clicks, revealing a black scepter taller than a man. At its top sat a real human skull, not just a model.

As the scepter appeared, the room dimmed, as if the light itself was being absorbed. A faint smell of blood and an ominous energy emanated from the scepter, filling the room.

Even Alexander was taken aback, and the expressions of those present turned pale as they bowed their heads deeply.

Guzman gestured, and two burly men in black dragged Cloe to kneel before the scepter.
