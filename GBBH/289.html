Of course, he skipped over the intimate parts with Lara.

"So that's what happened. I can't believe so much has gone on," Ava said.

Alexander replied, "His grandson is in Tijuanea, and it seems like sorcery is needed to stabilize his condition. It's probably more than just a simple illness. I can't be completely sure until I see him myself.

"Regardless, I won't let Schroder's scheme succeed!"

Ava nodded and then sneered.

"Schroder dared to impersonate the military in issuing orders. That's a serious offense in the army, and Quentin will definitely teach him a lesson."

Alexander let out a faint snort.

"I want more than just to teach him a lesson. Since he dared to challenge me, I'll make sure he's ruined for good!"

Ava was startled. Schroder was a lieutenant general and a key figure in Northern Command. Quentin might reprimand him, but he wouldn't go so far as to destroy him completely.

She said, "If you can cure Quentin's grandson, Schroder might not be ruined forever, but he certainly wouldn't dare mess with us again."

Alexander smiled calmly, knowing Ava was underestimating his abilities.

If not for the sake of his loved ones and friends, Alexander wouldn't have hesitated to finish Schroder off tonight.

Ava warned, "Jane has gotten close to Schroder, so you'd better be careful!"

Alexander shook his head, giving a disdainful smile.

"The Fairfax family is trying to ally with a tiger. It won't end well for them!"

Frank was very anxious.

Andrew, Belinda, Jane, and several other key Fairfax family members had gone to the banquet and had since lost contact.

It was now past 2 AM, and still no news.

Frank lamented with regret, "I told them not to stir things up and just be honest. Now look, something's happened again!"

Everyone was silent, unsure of what to say.

"Bang!"

"Well, well, did you all gather here waiting for me to arrest you? Makes things easier for me," a burly, fully armed man sneered as he stepped inside.

It was Rosen Gross, Schroder's personal guard.

Behind Rosen, dozens of armed men barged in, looking vicious.

Frank glared at them.

The Fairfax family might have fallen, but Frank still wanted to preserve their dignity.

"Heh, you old geezer got guts. I like that," Rosen sneered.

Slap!

At Rosen's command, the dozens of men surged forward.

The Fairfax family cowered, trembling, not daring to resist.

Rosen's gaze was filled with malice as he sneered.

"We're General Flores' men. If you want to blame someone, blame Alexander for once being your daughter's husband."
