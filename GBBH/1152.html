Stephen sheathed his sabers, exhaling deeply.

Defeating three experts in succession had consumed a lot of his energy.

"You bastard, there aren't so many 'ifs' in this world!" he sneered, looking down at Cesar with contempt.

Karson shouted, "Come out and face him!"

Four gunmen burst into the room each wielding submachine guns trained on Stephen.

Laser lights flickered on Stephen, and gunfire erupted, the bullets raining down like a violent storm.

Cesar hadn't expected Karson to have such a final gambit, but hope surged within him. Perhaps this surprise attack could actually bring Stephen down.

Karson believed that no matter how strong Stephen was, he wouldn't be able to withstand such a barrage.

These four gunmen were elite; if they could seriously wound or even die with Stephen, it would be worth it.

However, as the gunfire erupted, Stephen unleashed his powers to their fullest.

He moved with the grace of a feline, his body more agile than a monkey and faster than a cheetah. He twisted and turned through the bullets, expertly slicing at one of the gunmen.

With a swift motion, he severed the gunman's head, blood spraying everywhere. Before the body even hit the floor, Stephen dispatched the second gunman.

In mere moments, the last two gunmen met the same fate, their bodies cleaved in two along with their weapons.

Before Karson could even contemplate escape, his last resort crumbled, leaving him in utter defeat.

Alexander remained seated, observing the chaos as if he were watching a play unfold.

Stephen's cold gaze locked onto Alexander. He found Alexander strange; he showed no fear and seemed to dismiss him completely.

"Mr. Chapman, you squandered the two chances I gave you. Do you regret it now?" Stephen taunted, advancing toward Karson step by step like a cat savoring its captured prey.

"I will never surrender! I will never be your slave!" Karson shot back defiantly, slowly retreating.

The shadow of death loomed larger, and escape felt impossible.

Stephen chuckled darkly, "I heard you sent your family away. It won't save them. I'll hunt them down one by one and kill them.

"You have a daughter about to start high school, right? I like chicks. I'll make sure she knows you asked me to look out for her."

Stephen laughed lewdly, relishing the power he held, playing with his prey before delivering the final blow.

Suddenly, Cesar lunged at Stephen from behind.

Karson's eyes lit with hope, and he charged at Stephen with renewed vigor.

Without even glancing back, Stephen simply threw his knife and sheath behind him.

With a cry, Cesar was swatted away.

Karson found his fist caught in Stephen's grip. In the struggle, Karson felt his wrist twist painfully.

With a single kick, Stephen sent Karson sprawling.

He coughed up blood and fought to rise.

Though he stood in a position of power, he was afraid of death. Yet, faced with certain death, he found a surge of courage and resolve.

"You can kill me, but you can never humiliate me!" Karson glared defiantly at Stephen.

His body trembled uncontrollably, but he steadied himself, refusing to back down in the face of Stephen's murderous intent.

Stephen clapped slowly, a smirk on his face. "Well done. No wonder you're renowned in Louvian. But it's a shame that you've chosen to go against Sakra Society. You're sealing your own fate."

In a swift motion, Stephen drew his saber.

The act was deliberate and slow, and his expression was gleefully mocking.

Just as Karson was about to be killed, Alexander's calm voice cut through the tension. "Stop, Stephen. Enough with your performance."
