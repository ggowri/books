Betty drew her pistol and aimed it directly at one of Sebastian's henchmen.

"This is the Ultimate Alliance, not some free-for-all. You can't just waltz in and out whenever you please. One of you has to stay behind!"

Sebastian's face flushed with rage.

"What the hell is this Ultimate Alliance crap? I've never even heard of it! You lay a finger on my people, and I'll..."

Before he could finish, Betty fired a shot, making Sebastian flinch.

The man with the bloodied chest fell to the ground, terror, and shock etched on his face.

"Now, you can get lost," Betty said with a sweet smile, waving them off.

Sebastian's face cycled through a range of emotions before he finally, grudgingly, decided it wasn't worth the risk. Reluctantly, he turned to leave with his men.

"Stop right there!" Betty's voice rang out again, halting them.

Sebastian was furious, ready to go down swinging.

But Betty tossed a golden-embossed invitation in front of him and said coolly, "Here's an invitation to the symposium. It's at 10 a.m. the day after tomorrow, and Mr. Sebastian Tate, you're expected."

Sebastian studied the invitation, then asked, "Will the person behind you be attending?"

"Of course," Betty replied without hesitation.

Sebastian said nothing more and walked away in silence, his anger simmering as he got into his car.

He spat on the ground in frustration once he was out of the bar.

"Damn it, you'll pay for this. I swear, if I don't make you suffer!"

He left, thoroughly disgruntled.

Meanwhile, inside the building across the street, Augusta, Marvin, and Logan packed away their sniper rifles with satisfied smiles.

News of Sebastian, the president of the Brilliant Group, attending the "Magic Powder Symposium" quickly spread throughout Loscity.

The mysterious figure behind the magic powder formula was drawing even more attention.

Though the event was still days away, it had already become the city's hottest topic!

In the evening, as the city lights began to twinkle, Ava hummed cheerfully while busy in the kitchen.

Rebecca, looking worried and exasperated, couldn't believe Ava wasn't more concerned about being deceived.

The secretary chimed in, "Ms. Blackburn, why are you doing all this housework? You should let someone else handle it. Men don't like overly domesticated women; they prefer something new."

Ava, maintaining her serene smile, didn't respond.

She was discovering for the first time how much joy she found in preparing a meal for someone she loved.

Rebecca was persistent.

"Ava, I'm not lying. I saw them together with my own eyes. They were even hugging."

"Stop being so naive. Get back to work. Alexander will be here soon, and I still have one dish left to finish."

"Ava, you'll regret this. I'm going to expose Alexander for who he really is!"

Ava shook her head with a wry smile. Her friend's prejudice against Alexander was far too deep.

As dusk fell and the city lights came alive, Ava had set a beautiful table for a sumptuous dinner.

The living room was aglow with warm orange candlelight, creating an atmosphere of indescribable coziness and warmth.

The secretary had been sent away to wait outside, leaving just Alexander and Ava alone in the spacious room.

"Are you actually cooking? Did you make all this for me?"

Alexander could hardly believe his eyes.

It was astounding to him that someone as prominent as Ava would go out of her way to cook and invite him over.

Thinking back to the five years he spent with Jane, where he was always the one buying groceries and cooking, Alexander couldn't help but feel a bit of regret.

Ava gently placed a piece of meat onto Alexander's plate and flashed him a warm, inviting smile.

"Try my cooking and let me know how it is. I hope we have a wonderful evening together."

Her flirtatious tone stirred something inside Alexander, filling him with a mix of excitement and anticipation.
