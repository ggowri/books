In the outskirts of Loscity, there was a scenic manor.

It was known as the estate of the Federer family, the top of Loscity's four great families.

Important members of the Federer family lived here, and whenever there were significant meetings, family members gathered in the manor.

A tall wall surrounded the estate, with watchtowers at intervals.

Each watchtower was manned by guards armed with weapons, and with the modern surveillance and security systems, it was practically impregnable.

However, today was different; most of the personnel at the watchtowers had left for the secret, failed mission.

Key figures from the Federer family were gathered in the main hall, waiting news from Edward.

The hall was very big, over 10,000 square feet, lavishly decorated with antiques, artifacts, and intricately carved furniture, exuding opulence.

Gary sat on a leather sofa in the center of the hall, looking serious, with a hint of pain on his face.

Ava's kick had nearly incapacitated him, and he still hadn't fully recovered.

Next to him were five middle-aged men, all well-dressed in luxury brands, exuding an air of wealth and authority, clearly senior members of the Federer family.

Gary checked his watch from time to time; it was nearly 4 o'clock, and he still hadn't heard from Edward.

He couldn't help but glance at the row of bodyguards nearby, all fully armed and imposing, which gave him a little reassurance.

"Mr. Federer, relax; everything will be fine."

A seductive young beauty sauntered over, sitting beside Gary, pressing her ample chest against his arm.

Her smooth, fair legs were fully exposed beneath her ultra-short skirt, radiating a sultry charm—she was clearly a temptress.

Gary forced a smile and placed his hand on her thigh, stroking it as he muttered to himself.

"I can't relax until I see Alexander captured."

One of the senior members of the Federer family laughed.

"Master, Edward is our best fighter; even the old butler can't beat him. Besides, it's not just Edward; our best forces are out, so capturing Alexander shouldn't be a problem."

Gary frowned. "That's true, but Edward has been gone a long time without a call. It's hard to feel at ease."

"Master, please don't worry! We're ambushing them; Ava surely isn't prepared and won't mobilize the Saint Group, so capturing them will be a piece of cake."

"Exactly! Facing Edward and over 100 armed elites, how high-and-mighty can Alexander be?"

"Yeah, this is like using a sledgehammer to crack a nut!"

The senior members of the Federer family were confident and chatting merrily, convinced the plan would succeed.

"Hmm, you all have a point."

Gary could only smile, thinking it was time to ease the tension; Edward would definitely bring back good news.

If they could capture Alexander, they could force him to hand out the magic powder formula, and the Federer family would rise to new heights.

Plus, they could teach that vile Ava a lesson, allowing Gary to vent his frustrations.

Imagining a bright future, Gary breathed a sigh of relief, feeling that the pain in his private area had lessened considerably.

Just then, the clock chimed four times; it was already 4 o'clock.

The heavy rain has stopped, the dark clouds have dispersed, and a hint of dawn is starting to appear on the horizon.

"Why hasn't there been any news yet? Edward has always been reliable; what's wrong to him today?"

Gary started to feel anxious again, quickly pulling out his phone to call Edward.

"Bang!"

Suddenly, a loud crash echoed as the hall doors burst open.

The massive carved wooden door detached from the frame and fell heavily to the ground, shaking the entire hall.

Edward stumbled in, covered in blood, and collapsed to his knees.

The Federer family members were all stunned and quickly stood up.

Gary shouted in shock, "Edward, what's going on? Have you lost your mind?"

In the next moment, a cold and authoritative voice rang out.

"Gary, your time is up!"

Alexander calmly walked into the hall, his deep gaze sweeping around before landing on the terrified face of Gary.
