By the time Alexander got back to the master bedroom, it was already the middle of the night.

Ava lay face down on the bed, completely still, as if she were asleep.

But Alexander knew better—she'd been watching earlier, her face flushed with embarrassment, clearly affected by what she saw between him and Rosalia.

He smirked to himself, pretending nothing had happened as he got into bed and pulled Ava close, letting sleep take over.

.....

The next morning, Ava's phone started blaring before dawn broke.

She rubbed her eyes, reaching over to grab it.

After a short conversation, she hung up, her brow furrowed in worry.

"Alex, Charlize is in critical condition—what do we do?"

Alexander's expression turned serious right away. Charlize Collins had gotten those burns protecting Ashley.

"What are we waiting for? Let's go to the hospital."

Twenty minutes later, they arrived at the hospital.

Charlize lay silently in the hospital bed. The beautiful face she once had was now only half intact—the other side burned black.

Her body was covered in burn ointments, the dark paste making her look like charred wood.

Dr. Sherwood, the best doctor in Loscity, was overseeing her treatment.

A group of nurses and doctors bustled around, all of them visibly tense.

Dr. Neal, the hospital director, stood by, eyes wide with concern.

He knew that if Charlize died here, the whole hospital would be in deep trouble.

He anxiously asked, "Dr. Sherwood, how is the patient now?"

Dr. Peter Sherwood shook his head, looking disheartened.

"The burns are too extensive. There's nothing more we can do. It's time to inform the family and make final arrangements."

Dr. Neal felt his temper flare, about to lose it, when Alexander's voice cut through.

"Who says Charlize can't be saved? I'll take care of it!"

Everyone turned to see Alexander and Ava rushing in.

Dr. Sherwood frowned.

"And just who are you? You think you can question my medical expertise?"

Alexander replied, his tone even.

"Dr. Sherwood, you might be the best doctor in the city, but if you can't save Charlize, I sure can."

Dr. Sherwood snorted.

"If you can actually save her, I'll become your student."

Alexander shrugged. "If you want to be my student, we'll see if I'm in the mood to take you."

Dr. Sherwood looked like he couldn't decide whether to laugh or scream—this kid was unbelievably arrogant.

Dr. Neal recognized Alexander and Ava, his face paling.

Rumor had it even top military officials showed Alexander respect, and the Red Umbrella Group had lost to him. There was no way Dr. Neal would risk offending him.

Dr. Neal grabbed Dr. Sherwood's arm, pulling him back.

"Dr. Sherwood, keep quiet, now's not the time for this."

Dr. Sherwood fumed. "Dr. Neal, why are you stopping me? He's questioning our hospital's treatment, my skills, and I can't say a word?"

"I said zip it! I'm trying to help you here."

Dr. Neal's patience was thinning—he'd never dealt with someone so oblivious.

"Mr. Hawthorne, if you'd like to help with the treatment, of course, you're welcome to. But Charlize's burns are extremely severe. Might I ask which medical school you graduated from?"

Dr. Neal asked cautiously.

Alexander replied calmly, "I never went to medical school, and I've never worked in a hospital. I developed my skills on my own."

Dr. Neal was left speechless while Dr. Sherwood sneered, his expression filled with disdain.

"If you can't help, get out of the way. Don't waste my time."

Alexander waved his hand, and an invisible force shoved Dr. Sherwood and his team backward.

Dr. Sherwood tried to step forward, but Dr. Neal grabbed him, practically dragging him out.

Ava's eyes filled with tears as she looked at Charlize's condition.

Alexander motioned for Ava to leave as well.

In moments, it was just him and Charlize in the room.

Alexander looked at Charlize's burned body, and he couldn't help but think back to her once soft, beautiful form. A bitter feeling tightened his chest.

"Damn you, Felix Vanderbilt. I swear, you'll pay for this."

Alexander muttered a few curses before focusing, channeling the Force into his hands, preparing to heal. He placed his hands gently on Charlize's body, beginning the treatment.

This was the first time Alexander had used his full Force to heal since becoming an Intermediate Warrior.
