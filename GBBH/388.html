Alexander laughed out loud.

"I'm almost afraid of ending up in jail. Maybe I should let you go back. Once you're there, pass on a message to Felix at the Red Umbrella Group for me."

"Tell him to get ready, because I'll show him what real fear feels like. I'll make him experience the slow crawl of death."

Alexander finished and placed his hand on Bernard's forehead.

A thin stream of Force slipped silently into Bernard's mind. It's a skill Alexander had mastered since advancing to Intermediate Warrior.

Force Annihilation!

Force Annihilation not only inflicted immediate, intense damage but also silently eroded the body, destroying cells and causing excruciating pain until it brought a slow, agonizing death.

Bernard was now like meat on a butcher's block; if Alexander wanted him dead, it would take only a thought.

"What...what did you do to me?" Bernard asked, terrified.

Bernard's heart pounded as an intense chill spread through his body after Alexander's touch.

Alexander's smile, his actions, and the sheer menace radiating from him filled Bernard with pure terror.

He couldn't help but deeply regret ever crossing such a dangerous man.

"Get out!"

Alexander commanded, looking at Bernard as if he were already dead.

Bernard froze, stammering, "What...what did you say?"

"I said get out!"

Alexander kicked him down.

Bernard's face turned pale, and without a word, he turned and ran.

He now realized that Alexander was no weakling living off of women. He was truly powerful.

Bernard scrambled out of the estate, driving away at breakneck speed while frantically calling the police...

Soon, a large police force arrived at the estate, personally led by Oliver.

However, the estate was empty. Not only was Eddie's body gone, but even the bloodstains had vanished.

There wasn't a soul at The Cripples' headquarters; the entire estate had an eerie, unexplainable atmosphere.

Oliver scowled as he asked, "Mr. Clifford, are you lying? There's nobody here, not even any blood!"

"What..."

Bernard was speechless.

Standing nearby, Melin kept silent, feeling a rush of warmth as she recalled the time Alexander had helped her with the detox.

Unfortunately, she hadn't seen Alexander since their goodbye at the hospital. He was always busy, and he had no shortage of women around him.

Melin had looked into Alexander's life and found that women like Ava, Rebecca, and Betty were all close to him. The thought left her feeling a bit jealous.

"Hmph!"

Melin glared at Bernard, scolding, "Bernard, you're wasting public resources and slandering Mr. Hawthorne. That's grounds for legal action."

"I'm not! You...you're all in on this with Alexander. This is infuriating..."

Furious, Bernard turned and stormed off.

Melin glared at Bernard's back, muttering, "Alexander, you jerk. Just wait until I catch you; I'll give you a good lesson!"

Oliver looked thoughtfully at Melin, giving a knowing smile without saying a word.

Alexander was at the height of his power, and Oliver wasn't about to risk crossing him without solid proof.

A few hours later, Bernard stumbled into the Loscity Grand Hotel.

Inside the hotel's top-floor presidential suite, Bernard entered and dropped to his knees in front of Felix on the sofa.

"Welcome to Loscity, Mr. Vanderbilt. Please, help us get revenge!"

Felix, reclining with one leg crossed over the other, looked at Bernard with displeasure.

"And why would I help you?"

Bernard's face fell, and he gritted his teeth, saying, "Because Alexander is our common enemy!"

"You mentioned that Alexander had a message for me. What do you mean?"

A murderous glint and a hint of confusion flashed in Felix's deep eyes.

Bernard nodded repeatedly. "He said that. He wants you to experience true fear, to feel the approach of death..."

He repeated Alexander's message word for word.
