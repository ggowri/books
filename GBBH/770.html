Although Alyssa had already seen Alexander's abilities, his strength was now even more terrifyingly evident.

She only snapped back to reality when Alexander took her hand again.

Clinging to him, it seemed as though she wanted to melt entirely into his embrace.

As they exited the old mansion, they found around twenty enemies waiting outside.

"Don't... don't come any closer!"

Faces pale with terror, they shrank back as Alexander stepped forward.

The sounds of gunfire and agonized screams from inside the mansion had led them to believe their leader could handle everything.

Yet, to their horror, it was Alexander and Alyssa who emerged unharmed, while the fate of their dozens of comrades was all too clear.

"Fire!" someone yelled in desperation.

Automatic rifles roared, spitting a hailstorm of bullets toward Alexander.

Smoke filled the air, the heavy gunfire like the very summons of death.

Yet every single bullet missed its mark, leaving only a trail of wasted ammunition and holes scattered across the ground.

Quickly, Alexander moved Alyssa behind a rock and then darted to circle around behind his attackers.

"Where is he?!"

As Alexander vanished, a suffocating fear took hold of each of them.

Pale faces, eyes wide with terror, trembling hands and bodies—they were all gripped by their own helplessness.

"You're too slow!"

Alexander dived into their midst, beginning a brutal assault.

Rat-a-tat-tat!

Bullets flew wildly as someone fired in panic, hitting several of his own men instead of Alexander.

Alexander's All-seeing Eyes glowed with a mysterious light, like a supercomputer processing every detail of the environment, reading each expression and movement down to the smallest twitch.

Even their next moves were laid bare before him, his mind conjuring countless strategies to dispatch them in seconds.

With the memory and computational power far beyond an ordinary mind, Alexander—an Intermediate Warrior—could predict every action and track every bullet's trajectory.

From a scientific perspective, his brain development far surpassed that of the smartest scientist by dozens of times.

Like an angel of judgment, he moved effortlessly, sending them all to their doom.

Within moments, the last of the Mafia lay lifeless in a pool of blood.

The Mafia and the City of Sorcery's finest had been utterly annihilated.

With a calm expression, Alexander stowed his Lightsaber, seeming entirely untroubled.

Alyssa, still in shock, could hardly find words.

Alexander's gaze turned toward a nearby thicket. "Come out," he said icily. "You can't hide, and you won't escape."

Alyssa gasped—she hadn't realized someone was still there.

The silence was heavy, broken only by the occasional bird call.

She looked questioningly at Alexander, wondering if he'd somehow mistaken.

But his gaze only grew colder.

"Come out, or I'll make you."

Finally, a rustling sound came from the bushes.

A woman emerged, her figure draped in a striking red cloak.

The bold color flared like fire in the wind, casting her as a volcano waiting to erupt with deadly energy.

Alyssa felt an uneasy thrill—the woman exuded an aura of seething power.

The woman's face was flawless, her pale skin setting off her exquisitely delicate features.

Her long hair was swept into a high ponytail, secured by a red ribbon that matched her cloak.

Though cloaked, her seductive figure was unmistakable, with curves as alluring as they were deadly.

Alexander couldn't help but give her a second glance. She was indeed stunning, a beauty on par with someone like Ava.

Yet he could sense the darkness within her—this was a sorceress.

"Why would you kill so many? Are you a demon?" she demanded, her piercing gaze full of anger.

Alexander laughed coldly. "How many have you killed here in Mandale Town? You're the real monsters!"

Her face flushed with indignation, momentarily struck silent by his retort.

Alexander had encountered plenty of beauties before; her attempts to feign innocence didn't faze him.

He saw her for what she was—a venomous viper in a captivating disguise.

Surveying the bodies scattered around, the woman sneered, "All worthless! A bunch of useless fools!"
