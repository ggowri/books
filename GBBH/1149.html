Stephen didn't even spare Alexander a glance. To him, Alexander seemed utterly ordinary, and his calm demeanor was mistaken for sheer fright.

Without releasing his internal energy, Alexander looked just like anyone else.

Karson gritted his teeth and shouted, "Stephen, stop being so arrogant. Let's see who's stronger after a fight!"

Stephen grinned mockingly. "You want to take me on? You're all just a bunch of losers!"

"Who the hell are you calling a loser?" Dariel snapped, his temper flaring.

Unable to hold back, he pointed at Stephen and declared, "I've heard you're the top expert in Sakra Society. I'll take you down right now and show you what I'm made of!"

With that, Dariel launched a punch.

As expected from a boxing champion's disciple, his movements were exceptionally slick as he charged at Stephen.

He threw a powerful punch that created a fierce gust of wind, his fist flying toward Stephen's cheek with lethal speed.

Having knocked out dozens of opponents and faced countless life-and-death situations in underground fights, Dariel was confident in his boxing skills.

He launched a surprise attack, disregarding any sense of fair play, simply aiming to eliminate Stephen quickly and claim the promised 30-million-dollar bonus from Karson.

In a fight, the winner took all, and morality or rules didn't matter—victory was everything.

Against Dariel's sudden assault, Stephen remained unfazed, a sinister smile creeping across his face, completely dismissing Dariel's threat.

"Loser," he said coldly.

Before Dariel could even process Stephen's words, Stephen unsheathed a saber in a flash.

The blade gleamed ominously, slicing through the air with a chilling whistle, and a wave of icy menace sent chills down everyone's spines.

Except for Alexander, no one saw how quickly Stephen drew his blade.

In the blink of an eye, Dariel screamed in agony and staggered back, blood spraying like a fountain.

As everyone looked closer, they realized Dariel's right hand had been severed, lying on the ground while his fingers quivered slowly.

His face contorted in horror, his eyes wide as he grasped his right wrist with his left hand, staring at Stephen in disbelief.

If he hadn't retreated fast enough, the blade would have taken not just his hand but his entire arm.

Dariel stumbled back several steps, his face ashen, utterly robbed of any fighting spirit.

"So fast..." he stammered, fear stripping him of strength as blood poured from his wound.

"Loser. I could kill you like squashing an insect!" Stephen sneered, pointing the saber toward the ground. Drops of crimson blood dripped from the blade, pooling on the floor.

The metallic scent of blood filled the hall, both pungent and terrifying.

Travis furrowed his brow and asked, "What level of metahuman are you? Do you possess speed power? Otherwise, you couldn't have moved that fast!"

Stephen wasn't just a metahuman with speed; his swordsmanship was fierce and precise, a renowned martial art emphasizing quick draws and immediate strikes.

The technique he employed was said to be capable of severing a bull's head in one strike—an incredibly powerful sword move.

Dariel hadn't anticipated Stephen's prowess. He had cut through Dariel's hand with the same ease a chef uses to chop a chicken wing.

"What's wrong? Scared? Losers!" Stephen scoffed.

Cesar and Travis fumed at being insulted as losers.

Karson glanced at them and declared firmly, "I'll double the reward if you can get rid of Stephen!"

Cesar and Travis exchanged excited glances.

But they knew that facing Stephen alone was a fool's errand.

Dariel had just attempted a surprise attack and had lost an arm for his troubles; neither of them had the strength to match Stephen.

Cesar licked his lips, his heart racing.

Travis' small eyes were now wide open as he scrutinized Stephen.

The two men radiated an intimidating, murderous presence as they scrutinized Stephen, searching for any weaknesses, ready to change the tide of battle.
