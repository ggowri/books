Marvin was about to rush in to help but was pinned down by Locke and the woman, leaving him unable to act recklessly.

Facing two opponents alone, sweat had soaked Marvin's back.

"Boom!"

Thunder rumbled as a torrential downpour began.

The dense rain pierced through the leaves, pelting Marvin's body and dripping down from his head.

But Marvin didn't dare blink, keeping his eyes locked on Locke and the woman.

"Boss, get here now!"

For the first time, Marvin, who had always been fearless, felt unsure of himself.

He wasn't afraid of dying; he was afraid of dying without taking them down, leaving Shadow in danger.

Meanwhile, Shadow had lured Flame deep into the forest.

The area was already pitch black, and with the heavy rain, visibility was even worse.

There wasn't a glimmer of light, and even reaching out, you couldn't see your own hand.

Shadow and Flame, two elite assassins, seemed to have merged into the oppressive darkness.

Even their breathing became imperceptibly light, with only the sound of rain pattering around them.

"With your limited skills, you still want to play hide-and-seek with me? Heh, you'll bleed out in less than twenty minutes.

"Plus, you've been poisoned. Your body must be paralyzed by now, right? "

Flame was truly unhinged, yelling and screaming in the darkness, openly revealing his location. He clearly didn't take the injured Shadow seriously.

His laughter was like the howl of a hyena, sending chills down the spine.

"Do you know how your master died? I stabbed that old man over a dozen times, and he still wasn't dead.

"Then I tied up his daughter—your first love—chopped off her hands and feet, and made them watch each other die.

"I have to admit, your woman had a great figure. I couldn't help myself and had some fun.

"Even without her hands and feet, it didn't stop me. I bet she was thinking of you when she died, wasn't she?"

Flame kept taunting Shadow while moving quickly in the darkness.

He wanted to provoke Shadow into attacking. In his wounded and poisoned state, Shadow would surely lose.

Yet, everything remained eerily quiet, with no signs of movement.

Flame started to panic. Could that bastard Shadow have escaped in the downpour?

He shouted again, "Do you know how horribly she died? After I was done, I cut the flesh off her backside and fed it to dogs.

"Heh heh heh, let me tell you, those wild dogs enjoyed it.

"Your so-called noble assassin family? They looked down on me, giving you the best techniques and not me. Why?

"And yet, the truth is I'm stronger than you! You're nothing but trash, hiding behind a woman's protection and cheating your way into inheriting the family legacy!

"They were poisoned the same way you are—neurotoxin. Soon, you won't be able to move and will die like a pig at my hands.

"What a pity. Eighteen top-tier assassins, and I slaughtered them like pigs, all by myself. You're the nineteenth and the last... "

Flame sounded completely deranged, laughing maniacally.

He thought he could hear Shadow's heavy breathing and faint footsteps.

Pretending to notice nothing, he kept laughing wildly while stealthily moving toward the sounds.

When he was just a few feet away from the breathing, Flame let out a roar and, using a flash of lightning to guide him, lunged toward Shadow.

"Ahhh!"

A shrill scream pierced the air, and Flame froze in shock.

He realized that half his body had been severed by a katana.

Shadow had embedded his katana between two thick trees. As Flame charged forward, his right side collided with the sharp blade.

It was just a small trap, but at this moment, it became the final, deadly move.

Flame collapsed to the ground, howling in pain, and quickly begged, "Don't kill me! I'll give you the antidote..."

His words were abruptly cut off!

Shadow slit his throat with a dagger. Scalding blood sprayed out, mingling with the rain as it flowed onto the ground.

Flame's eyes widened in disbelief as the light faded from them. His body slumped to the ground, motionless.
