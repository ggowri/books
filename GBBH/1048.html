When they arrived at Aetheris Street, Rosalia frowned deeply. The area had developed too quickly in recent years, and everything had changed.

She had been here before on missions and had contacted Ariella, but now, everything seemed different.

She couldn't find anyone who could contact Ariella.

After Alexander inquired about the situation of the Obsidian Gang by paying for information, they received unexpected news.

They were told that the leader of the Obsidian Gang was dead. And Ariella, as the heiress, was being hunted, her life hanging by a thread.

Alexander and Rosalia hadn't expected this at all.

They tried to use the Obsidian Gang's power to find Hazel, but now it looked like they had to help Ariella first.

After all, Ariella was Rosalia's friend, and Alexander couldn't just stand by and do nothing.

Besides, if Ariella took control of the Obsidian Gang, it would also be beneficial for him.

Rosalia felt uneasy upon learning that Ariella was forced to work as a barmaid in a pub.

"Mr. Hawthorne, you have to help Ariella. Please, I'm begging you!" she pleaded.

"Don't worry, I'll help her," Alexander reassured her.

After hearing his answer, Rosalia finally relaxed.

They checked into the best hotel on Aetheris Street and enjoyed a hearty meal.

After dinner, Rosalia took Alexander to meet a relative.

"You know quite a few people in Lumiria, aside from Ariella. I didn't expect your uncle to be here, too."

On the way, Alexander and Rosalia chatted.

Soon, they arrived at a tidy neighborhood. It wasn't rich people's zone, but the environment was not bad, with lush greenery and beautiful scenery.

Rosalia explained her uncle Malcom Sugarman's situation. Malcom and his wife were her only living relatives.

And because of her connection to Ariella, she had helped them settle here, where they could be protected by the Obsidian Gang.

Now that Ariella was in trouble, Rosalia wanted to check on how Malcom was doing. Alexander, of course, had no objection.

Having earned a lot of money as a killer, Rosalia had sent some of it to Malcom. From the neighborhood's appearance, it was clear that Malcom and his family were living comfortably.

Rosalia rang the doorbell.

The door opened quickly, and a white-haired old man appeared. He stared at Rosalia in shock before breaking into a wide grin.

He grabbed her arm tightly and burst into tears.

"Rosalia, you've really come back! I haven't seen you for so many years! Why didn't you let me know you were visiting me? Come in, come in!"

Rosalia's eyes welled up as she hugged him.

"This is... my boyfriend, Alexander Hawthorne..." she introduced.

"Ah! That's great!" Malcom exclaimed, enthusiastically shaking Alexander's hands and pulling both of them inside.

"Come out and see! Rosalia is back! And she brought a handsome boyfriend!" he called.

"Rosalia's back?!" A woman rushed out of the kitchen, and upon seeing Rosalia, she embraced her and started crying.

Alexander, watching the family cry tears of joy, felt deeply moved.

"Come on, sit down!"

"I'll make you some coffee. What would you like to eat?"

Malcom and his wife busied themselves, showing a great deal of warmth.

As Alexander smiled, he glanced over at the door, noticing a shotgun and a semi-automatic rifle leaning against the wall.

Noticing that both of the guns were loaded, he couldn't help but think, "Is there a danger here?"

He remained alert but, after using his enhanced vision, saw nothing unusual. He felt that perhaps it was just for self-defense.

Suddenly, the sound of a baby crying echoed from upstairs.

Rosalia froze for a moment. She knew Malcom didn't have any children.

She curiously asked, "Uncle Malcom, is someone else staying upstairs?"

Malcom sighed deeply and plopped onto the sofa, his expression complicated as he began explaining.

"This is a complicated situation. It has to do with infighting within the Obsidian Gang. Some people from Risun arrived on Aetheris Street..."

"People from Risun? Could they be from the Sakra Society?"

With that thought, Alexander and Rosalia exchanged a glance and felt their excitement rise.

They hadn't found Ariella, but this was an unexpected lead.

"Three days ago, a woman holding a baby was being chased and happened to flee to my house. I couldn't just let her die, so I saved her," Malcom continued.

"I thought about calling the police, but she wouldn't let me do that. She said there were traitors within the police, and if I called the police, both she and the baby would die."

Malcom explained the details of the situation.
