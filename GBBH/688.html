The man appeared with a head full of silver hair, his face deeply wrinkled like ancient tree bark, and his figure slightly stooped yet still exuding a commanding presence.

Just as Levi and the others found themselves in desperate straits, the Godfather finally arrived, immediately pleading for their lives.

After all, these men were pillars of the organization and losing them to Alexander's wrath would be a severe blow.

"My son, your power now... Even I, known as the Reaper, would think twice before underestimating you!"

In Heinrich Hawthorne's old, wise eyes was a glint of a mixture of astonishment and admiration as he gazed at Alexander.

Moments earlier, Alexander's display of power had seemed almost divine, a boundless strength that had utterly surpassed Heinrich's own, filling him with both amazement and joy.

In the end, Alexander chose not to press further, sparing Levi and his group.

With a great battle on the horizon, they could still prove useful.

Killing his own people just before such a fight would indeed bode ill. But while he'd spared their lives, punishment was still due.

Heinrich rose slowly from his wheelchair, leaning on his cane as he made his way over to Levi and the others.

Levi fell to his knees, profusely apologizing, sobbing, and repenting all at once.

"Godfather, I didn't know he was your son! I was wrong. I accept any punishment!"

He cried, slapping his own face repeatedly.

Lucas and the others also knelt, following Levi's lead, slapping their own faces and pleading for mercy.

The air was filled with the sounds of their wails, and the onlookers broke into a cold sweat.

It was a good thing they hadn't attacked earlier, for none had guessed that this figure was Godfather's own son.

All eyes turned to Alexander, now filled with awe and reverence.

"My son, you decide how to handle them."

Heinrich gestured to Alexander and left the decision in his hands. He wanted to see what judgment Alexander would ultimately render.

Alexander strode forward to Levi and his men, towering over them.

"I should have killed all of you. But my father has pleaded on your behalf, and with the battle against Felix imminent, I'll spare your lives for now.

"When the fighting begins, you will be the vanguard. Earn your redemption on the battlefield!"

These men could indeed prove useful in the coming battle. If any tried to flee, Alexander could snuff them out in an instant.

Both Heinrich and Shadow nodded in approval. Alexander's decision was prudent, asserting his authority and dignity while also showing mercy.

"Mr. Hawthorne is truly merciful!"

"So this is the famous Mr. Hawthorne! They say seeing is believing, and indeed it's true."

"Incredible! I'm convinced!"

"Levi and his crew lacked vision. Mr. Hawthorne has been exceptionally forgiving, but if they don't prove their worth in battle, even I won't show them mercy!"

Murmurs of approval spread through the crowd as everyone watched Alexander with admiration.

These were fighters who respected strength, and it was clear that Alexander's power surpassed even that of the Godfather and Shadow.

"Thank you, Mr. Hawthorne, for sparing us!"

"Rest assured, Mr. Hawthorne, we'll fight bravely and redeem ourselves in battle."

Levi, Lucas, and the others now stood humbly, their former arrogance completely erased.

"My son, all these years, I couldn't find you. It was my failing, and I'm sorry," Heinrich said sincerely, apologizing to Alexander.

Everyone was stunned. The Godfather, known for his ruthless decisiveness, was bowing his head in front of everyone.

Alexander felt a mix of emotions; he had once believed his father was dead and had never held any resentment toward him.

After hearing Heinrich's apology, any lingering discomfort in his heart dissolved.

As the tension from this encounter faded, the banquet atmosphere revived, buzzing with energy once more.

They were all battle-hardened individuals, unperturbed by the sight of blood.

Shadow raised his glass to Alexander in a toast, his admiration genuine.

"Mr. Hawthorne, your strength has advanced so quickly! Since the last time we met, you've more than doubled in power! I'm truly envious!"

Alexander gave a slight smile as they clinked glasses.

Unable to resist, Heinrich asked, "Son, what level of metahuman do you consider yourself now?"

His voice held a faint tremor, his anticipation clear.

According to data from the development of the power elixir, metahumans theoretically had nine levels. However, the highest known level so far was only three.

Alexander's power was sufficient to contend with a level-six metahuman, and with a lightsaber, he could even defeat a level-seven metahuman.
