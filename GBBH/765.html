"Oh, I just love a woman with a fiery temper. Makes it all the more fun."

Woodrow sneered, wiping the spit off his face, then sticking his fingers in his mouth to lick it, grinning lecherously.

"Get away, you sick freak!"

Alyssa's disgust was clear; she felt ready to vomit.

Kerwin smirked, adding, "Woodrow, stop wasting time. If you're not going for it, I will."

"Animals! If either of you lays a hand on me, I'll kill you!"

Alyssa thrashed against her restraints in vain.

Woodrow chuckled, "Oh yeah? Then go on, kill me—with that big ass of yours!"

"Look at her acting all innocent," he sneered. "Women are meant to please men, especially ones like you who are just begging for it.

"Come on, scream for me! The louder, the better!"

Woodrow took a small vial of red pill, forced Alyssa's mouth open, and tried to stuff it in.

"Go to hell!"

Alyssa shot her leg out and landed a swift kick straight to Woodrow's groin.

As a normal wizard, Woodrow wasn't quick enough to dodge.

He never expected that Alyssa, already bound to the pillar, could suddenly launch an attack.

Alyssa, even restrained, had used all her strength, and her blow struck true.

Woodrow let out a wail, clutching himself and collapsing on the ground as the vial clattered down, spilling red pills across the floor.

"Ah... hurts like hell!"

Woodrow gasped, breaking out in a sweat as pain wracked his body.

It took Kerwin a moment to snap out of his shock, but when he did, his face twisted with rage.

"You dare to pull a stunt like that?"

Grabbing Alyssa by her collar, he slapped her hard across the face, splitting her lip and making her vision blur.

"You kick me, huh? I'll make you pay!"

Woodrow, still grimacing in pain, picked himself up and shakily brandished a black rod. His face twisted as he pointed it at Alyssa, trembling with fury.

Before Kerwin could stop him, Woodrow unleashed a spell.

Sizzle!

A bolt of black energy shot toward Alyssa like an arrow, but a bright flash intercepted it, forming a protective barrier that absorbed the spell.

Alyssa glanced down and realized the source of the light: a gleaming amulet around her neck. It was the amulet Alexander had given her, crafted from high-grade gems and engraved with a powerful magic circle to guard against witchcraft.

"What the fuck?"

Woodrow stared, eyes wide with shock. He raised the rod again, firing two more bolts toward her face.

Bang!

Both spells vanished the moment they touched the shield of light.

They fell silent.

Alyssa herself was stunned, looking down at the amulet. It was Alexander's gift, and now it was protecting her.

Kerwin's shock turned to a greedy grin as he realized what she had.

"An amulet that blocks magic! Who gave you this? Tell me!"

Woodrow's eyes glittered with desire.

A protective amulet of this kind was a treasure that any sorcerer would covet, an item so rare that only the most skilled dark wizards in the City of Sorcery could create even one.

It would be impossible for ordinary sorcerers like Woodrow and Kerwin to possess something like that.

Owning such an item would elevate any sorcerer's status, giving them access to rare resources and advanced spell-books.

For Alyssa, an ordinary person, to possess something so precious was a complete waste.

Woodrow reached for the amulet, gripping its chain tightly. "Where did this come from? Are there more of them? Tell me now, and maybe I'll spare you."

Kerwin seized Woodrow's arm, eyes blazing with intensity. "We both found it, remember? This belongs to both of us!"

Woodrow shot him a cold glare.

"Patience. Let me get some answers first. She might have more than one."

Kerwin, equally eager, barked at Alyssa, "Come on, Alyssa! If you have another amulet, hand it over, and we might let you go."

The two of them started shoving each other, each wanting the amulet for himself.
