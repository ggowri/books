Rosen's entire right leg—his toes, ankle, and knee—were all dislocated.

"Ah! I really don't know anything..."

Rosen's anguished screams echoed in the interrogation room.

Meanwhile, Oliver and several officers watching from the control room looked terrified.

"Alexander is ruthless!"

"What kind of ability does he have to do this?"

In the interrogation room, Rosen lay on the floor, glaring at Alexander with malice.

"You're so vicious. The general will not let you go unpunished..."

"Still not cooperating?"

Alexander grabbed his left leg and shook it again.

"Ah!"

Rosen screamed again, all the joints in his limbs dislocated, pain rendering him immobile on the ground.

"I'll ask you one last time—where is Schroder's hideout in Loscity?"

Alexander loomed over him, his gaze icy.

Rosen trembled but remained tight-lipped, clenching his teeth.

"Very well, you are tough," Alexander said with a smile.

He then opened a black leather bag in front of Rosen, pulling out items one by one.

A sharp scalpel, a set of needles, pliers, a hammer... along with bandages, anesthetics, and various other medical supplies.

Alexander smiled as he introduced the purposes of each item to Rosen.

The most terrifying of all was the large glass jars he revealed last.

One contained several black snakes, another was filled with army ants, and the last housed dozens of hornets.

"This is torture! You're so cruel... I'd rather die than tell you anything..."

Rosen trembled uncontrollably.

"Ha, I didn't say you'd die. You won't die. How about trying a hornet's sting? They love honey."

Alexander took out a small jar of honey, grinning as he said, "If we slather this on you, the hornets will be quite pleased..."

Rosen felt Alexander's gaze lingering near his crotch, nearly scaring him into submission.

"I'll talk! I'll talk! Just don't do that to me!"

Half an hour later, Rosen's signed confession and a video of his admissions lay on Oliver's desk.

Bang!

Oliver slammed his hand down, knocking over his coffee cup.

"Schroder, a lieutenant general, actually ordered his subordinates to commit such a heinous kidnapping! This is outrageous."

Alexander chuckled. "He even impersonated the military to issue orders. General Thompson already knows about this and has promised to punish Schroder severely."

"Really? General Thompson knows?"

Oliver's face lit up with joy.

"Of course. When Schroder attended the Hansen family's gala, General Thompson was there too—though Schroder had no idea."

Alexander was cleverly leveraging Quentin's authority.

While Quentin opposed Schroder's actions, he hadn't fully sided with Alexander, unless Alexander could save his ailing grandson.

Oliver, visibly frustrated, asked, "What do we do now, Mr. Hawthorne? Should we surround Schroder and rescue Jane?"

Alexander answered calmly, "Director, there's no rush. Let me rescue Jane and the others first, then we can bring him to a military tribunal.

"Plus, I need to consult with General Thompson. After all, Schroder is one of his key officers, and we need his full support.

"If we capture Schroder without his backing, it won't just be a slap in General Thompson's face, but also a disgrace to the Northern Command. Don't you agree?"

Oliver nodded vigorously. "Yes, yes, you are right. Do you need my assistance in rescuing Jane?"

Alexander shook his head. "No need to worry, Director. I'll have concrete news for you within three days."

"Great! I'll follow your lead!"

Oliver was overjoyed.

It was best if Alexander handled it himself. That way, if anything went wrong, it wouldn't fall back on Oliver.

However, Oliver couldn't help but admire Alexander's exceptional skills and strategic mind.

"By the way, Mr. Hawthorne, Melin asked me to tell you she's waiting for you to pick her up from the hospital."

Oliver looked at Alexander with a mix of envy and subtle jealousy.

Melin had always been blunt with men. Countless distinguished suitors had pursued her, but none had succeeded.
