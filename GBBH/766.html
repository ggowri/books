"Hahaha!"

Alyssa burst into laughter. "Let me go? Do you think I'm a fool? If you have the guts, kill me. I'm not telling you a thing about the amulet's origin."

Woodrow and Kerwin erupted in anger, their faces contorted with rage.

"Bitch! Think carefully. If you don't talk, I'll make sure you die in agony," Woodrow threatened, unwilling to give up.

Alyssa shot them a look of utter disdain.

"Cut the nonsense. Do what you want."

Kerwin sneered, "Enough talking. Give her a tenfold dose of the stimulant! When she's begging for a man, we'll get our answers."

Woodrow agreed immediately, "Yes, let's do it."

He picked up a handful of red pills, at least a dozen of them, and, with Kerwin's help, grabbed Alyssa to force them into her mouth.

"Stop!"

A powerful shout suddenly thundered through the room.

An invisible wave of sound crashed over them like a raging tide. Woodrow and Kerwin clutched their ears in pain, screaming as their eardrums felt like they were being torn apart.

The soundwave reverberated through the room with such force that the windows shattered into shards.

Yet Alyssa seemed unaffected; the attack was directed solely at the two wizards.

"Who's there?!"

Woodrow and Kerwin were stunned.

This old castle was a hidden stronghold; how could someone suddenly break in?

They had traps set up and guards stationed outside—how had this happened without any warning?

In truth, Alexander had only just stepped into the hallway.

Using his All-seeing Eyes, he could see through the walls, witnessing Woodrow trying to force-feed Alyssa.

With a roar as fierce as a tiger, he charged forward, caring nothing for the guards.

"Stop right there! Or I'll shoot!" a guard yelled, but before he could finish, Alexander bulldozed through them like a runaway train, sending bodies flying.

His pace never faltered as he stormed ahead.

Someone shot at Alexander.

Machine gun fire erupted, bullets pouring down like rain as they tore through the corridor, aiming to tear him apart.

But it was all for nothing—each bullet passed harmlessly through the afterimage Alexander left in his wake.

In the blink of an eye, he was upon the shooter, delivering a single blow that sent the guard crashing lifelessly to the floor.

In seconds, half the guards were dead, sprawled across the hall.

They were skilled, elite Mafia enforcers, yet before Alexander, they were powerless, like mice before a lion.

The remaining guards regrouped, creating a crossfire at the doorway and unleashing another barrage of bullets.

The gunfire continued nonstop as they took turns firing, expertly swapping out empty magazines.

The fierce gunfire locked down the corridor, making it impossible for anyone to advance.

But Alexander remained calm, grabbing two bodies as shields and charging forward.

The bullets thudded into the corpses, and any stray shot was deflected by an unseen Force, unable to even brush his clothes.

Screams filled the corridor as guards were flung aside, landing with sickening thuds as they coughed up blood.

These trained fighters were no match for Alexander's unstoppable force.

"Pathetic tricks," Alexander muttered, pausing at the door.

He gave a cold snort, noticing the trap set just inside. With his All-seeing Eyes, it was a child's game to avoid.

Bang!

With one powerful kick, he sent the door—and the trap—flying, the heavy wood crashing to the floor in a cloud of dust.

"It's you? Alexander!"

Woodrow and Kerwin had just recovered from the searing pain in their ears.

They weren't weak, but Alexander's speed was something they had never anticipated.

Alexander sneered, "Your City of Sorcery has been spreading sickness through Loscity, and I haven't even dealt with you for that yet. Now you dare stir up trouble in Mandale Town? You're audacious beyond belief."

Woodrow's face twisted in fear, and Kerwin's turned ghostly pale. Neither of them could have expected Alexander to appear out of nowhere, descending upon them like a vengeful storm.

This was the same man who had once made the elder Rock disappear without a trace—a terrifying legend come to life right before their eyes.
