Following the stream, Alexander began digging. In no time, he had carved out a large pit several yards wide and deep.

Next, he lined the hole with plastic sheeting, layering it four or five times. He covered the bottom with stones, then added several more layers of plastic on top.

Rainwater quickly began to fill the pit.

He laid a dozen logs across the top and spread another sheet of plastic over them, forming a makeshift cover.

Then, using some pipes he had found in a grocery store, he inserted a drainage outlet at the lower end of the pit.

Water flowed smoothly through the pipe. He attached a spigot, and their simple reservoir was complete.

Alexander also fashioned a basic filtration system using mesh screens from the store. Rainwater, once filtered, became noticeably clearer.

He dropped purification tablets into the bucket. Before long, the murky rainwater transformed into clean, usable water.

It wasn't drinkable yet, but once boiled, it would be safe for bathing, washing clothes, and even drinking.

"Don't throw away the empty water bottles or jugs," Alexander said. "With this reservoir, as long as it rains, you can keep boiling water and refilling them.

"This way, you won't run out of water for a long time."

He turned to Sally with a smile.

She froze for a moment, feeling both grateful and heartbroken.

She knew she couldn't make him stay. No matter how much she wished otherwise, she understood that he would leave her sooner or later.

He had done so much for her, and she had no idea how to repay him.

The rain continued for an entire week.

Luckily, the air-raid shelter was on high ground. Otherwise, it would have been completely flooded.

Watching the relentless downpour, Alexander couldn't help but worry. This wasn't the rainy season, yet the weather was behaving strangely. He had no idea what was causing the anomaly.

He pondered it for a long time but found no answers. With a heavy heart, he turned back into the air-raid shelter.

In one corner, the glow of electric lights pushed back the darkness.

Sally was busy working on a set of leather armor, cutting and shaping the thick hide Alexander had prepared.

But mutant bison hide was incredibly tough. Ordinary needles couldn't pierce it. She had to use a hammer and a metal punch, striking the same spot a dozen times just to make a single hole.

Then, she threaded the pieces together with electrical wire. The finished armor looked crude and misshapen, but it was incredibly durable.

During the rainy week, Alexander had made several more supply runs. He had raided nearly half the stock from a local grocery store.

Now, one corner of the air-raid shelter was piled high with tools and materials. He had also found a temporary shelter at a construction site and hauled it back.

After setting it up outside the air-raid shelter, he dug a simple septic tank and installed a squat toilet inside. At least the sanitation problem was solved.

"This rain has been going on for a full week..."

Standing at the entrance, Alexander sighed as he gazed at the endless downpour.

Sally's cheeks turned slightly red. She secretly prayed that the rain would never stop.

This past week, being with Alexander had been the happiest time of her life. She had found comfort, both emotionally and physically.

"What do you think? Doesn't look too great, does it?" Sally said, slipping on the leather armor.

The oversized armor hung loosely on her, making her look like a child wearing a grown-up's clothes.

It was not only too big but also stitched together in a crooked, clumsy fashion.

Alexander chuckled, "Looking good isn't the point. Protection is what matters."

With that, he swung his sword at her.

Sally let out a terrified scream. She had no time to dodge. The blade struck her squarely in the chest.

The sword left a faint white mark on the armor but didn't even scratch the surface.

"Not bad!"

Alexander nodded in satisfaction. He silently praised the mutant bison. Not only had it provided an abundance of meat, but even its hide was proving invaluable.

Of course, he had held back. If he had used the Force, he would have cut through both the armor and Sally in one swing.

"That scared me... Scared me to death!"

Sally clutched her chest, breathing heavily, her ample curves rising and falling.

Then, she grinned, running her hands over the armor. With this protective gear, her chances of surviving the apocalypse had just increased significantly.

Even a mutant beast would struggle to claw through this hide.
