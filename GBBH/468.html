Kirk Harris, the owner of Dragonstone Gem Company, paused for a moment, then burst into laughter.

"Haha, Ms. Blackburn, you must be joking! You've got every gemstone imaginable at Astral Bistro. Why would you need anything from me?"

He gave Alexander a skeptical once-over. High-quality gemstones weren't cheap—they easily cost millions of dollars.

With Alexander dressed so casually, Harris doubted he could afford such a purchase.

Just then, Alexander's All-seeing Eyes picked up something unusual—a dark mass of negative energy concentrated around Harris' left chest.

It was a sign of an impending heart attack, a very dangerous situation.

But Alexander didn't mention it, instead smiling as he said,

"Mr. Harris, I need a batch of gemstones—the higher the quality, the better. Price isn't an issue. I'm willing to pay ten percent above market rate."

Harris' eyes lit up. This kid was serious.

He immediately turned on the charm, "Alex, don't worry. If you're Ms. Blackburn's future son-in-law, you're family. I promise you'll get the best deal. Want to see the goods?"

"Sure, let's take a look!" Alexander was genuinely excited.

Harris led them into the warehouse, which wasn't much smaller than Astral Bistro's.

Alexander searched the whole place, but nothing caught his eye—just some decent-quality stones, but nothing top-tier.

Feeling disappointed, Alexander was about to leave.

As for Harris' health, it wasn't his problem—no need to get involved with someone he had no connection to.

Suddenly, something caught his attention.

His All-seeing Eyes detected a faint glow coming from an inconspicuous corner.

There was a pile of discarded, low-quality gemstones—dusty and dull, clearly leftover rejects that hadn't been worth keeping. Who knew how long they'd been sitting there?

But within that pile, a faint red glimmer stood out to Alexander.

"This is a top-tier ruby. It's small, but it's definitely a high-quality piece!"

Alexander's heart skipped a beat.

To upgrade his level-one lightsaber core to level two, he needed seven high-quality gemstones of different colors—red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet.

The ruby was tiny, about the size of a peanut, hidden inside a piece of low-grade stone. That's why it had been overlooked.

Buried under the other discarded stones, Alexander almost missed it.

He moved quickly, digging through the pile.

The sound of stones clattering echoed in the warehouse.

Soon, he pulled out the gem.

It was covered in a dull red layer, barely recognizable as a gemstone—more like some worthless rock.

Harris watched Alexander, puzzled. He'd seen Alexander comb through the warehouse, looking unimpressed, which had annoyed him.

Now Alexander was pulling a random piece of junk from the pile and seemed thrilled. Harris couldn't figure it out.

"Alexander, you must be mistaken. That's not a gemstone—it's just trash." Harris said, clearly confused.

Alexander's eyes locked on Harris with an intense stare.

"Mr. Harris, I know you're a smart man, so let's not beat around the bush. How much do you want for this stone?"

Harris' heart skipped a beat—he suddenly felt like Alexander could see right through him, and it made him uneasy.

There was something about Alexander's eyes, the way they seemed to shine, making Harris feel exposed.

Harris was thinking, if this really was a top-tier gem, he needed to find a way to get it back.

"Mr. Harris, I'll be blunt—this is a high-quality ruby. Your people missed it, but now it's in my hands. Taking it back isn't an option."

Alexander spoke with calm confidence.

"Alexander, let me be honest too. If this really is a top-tier gemstone, I'd rather put it up for auction. I won't let it go that easily."

Harris' tone grew more serious.

Lynn's expression darkened, her voice sharp as she spoke up.

"Mr. Harris, isn't this a bit much? Alex was the one who found it. If he hadn't noticed it, you would've tossed it as junk!"
