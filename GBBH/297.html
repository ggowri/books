In Rosen's view, Alexander was on the brink of defeat.

He sneered, "I thought you were tough, but you're just a weakling."

In a fight between experts, the first move was crucial.

Alexander had nowhere to escape. Even if he dodged, he would face a storm of attacks.

Mike and Tony were excited, sure that Alexander would be beaten.

"Be careful!" Melin cried out, fearing that Alexander couldn't withstand such a fierce attack.

She tried to pull Alexander back, but he stood his ground.

Alexander smiled mockingly.

To him, their attacks were child's play.

"These are Schroder's elite men? Their punches are slow," he taunted.

His fist shot out to attack Mike.

His move seemed slow and light, but it struck Mike's chest like lightning.

A crack echoed as Mike was thrown back, crashing to the ground and spitting blood.

Tony's knife missed, and Alexander kicked him in the face.

Tony screamed, holding his face as blood flowed from his fingers.

In a blink, the two men who had the upper hand were now defeated, writhing on the ground.

The room fell silent.

Rosen was the first to react. "Kill him! Fire..."

Before he could finish, Alexander grabbed his throat and held him as a shield.

The others raised their guns.

But they dared not shoot with Rosen in the way.

Alexander smiled, "Rosen, if you don't want to die, let me take Sir Fairfax and Melin away."

Rosen was irritated, but he didn't want to die.

If he refused, Alexander would surely kill him.

Alexander's grip tightened, and Rosen felt like he was being choked by an iron collar.

"I'll let them go," he gasped.

Alexander shouted at the others, "Didn't you hear your boss? Let Sir Fairfax go!"

Rosen waved his hand weakly. "Let him go."

His men had to obey.

They only had to release an old man, not everyone. Schroder would not punish them for it.

The Fairfax family members shouted, "Why not save us? Please, save us!"

"Yes, why only save that old man? We are the future of the Fairfax family!"

Some were pleading, while the others were either doubting or cursing.

If they were not tied up, they might have confronted Frank.

Alexander kept hold of Rosen and told Melin to help Frank leave.

Melin refused, "No! I won't leave you behind. We'll leave together."

Alexander was speechless.

He looked at Melin closely.

She was kind and brave.

Melin blushed and said, "What are you looking at? Don't get the wrong idea. I just want to leave with you."

Everyone was stunned.

The tough woman was acting like a shy girl in front of Alexander.

She was filled with admiration, respect, and shyness.

Alexander almost laughed, wondering if she had a crush on him.

It was possible. Proud women often saw their rescuers as princes.

With Alexander's skills and handsome looks, he certainly fit the role of a prince.
