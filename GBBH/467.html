Alexander quickly said, "Oh, I couldn't possibly trouble you like that, Ms. Blackburn."

"It's no trouble at all. Thank you for bringing my daughter back. Besides, I'd love to take a walk, and spending time with Ava makes me happy."

Lynn's words were so sincere that Ava's eyes filled with tears.

"Silly girl, why are you crying? I know I made mistakes back then. At that time, I was struggling to make ends meet...

"I didn't think about how it affected you, and I should apologize."

Lynn's eyes misted over as well.

Ava hugged her tightly, and the two cried on each other's shoulders.

Alexander watched, smiling softly, glad to see the mother and daughter finally making peace. It lifted a weight off his shoulders, too.

He couldn't help but think of his own mother, who had passed away, and the thought stung more than he expected.

Lynn hugged Ava one last time, then smiled at Alexander.

"My daughter's beautiful and kind, so don't you dare bully her, got it?"

Alexander bowed his head slightly, smiling.

"Don't worry, Ms. Blackburn. I care about her too much to ever hurt her."

Lynn noted how respectful and grounded Alexander remained, and she nodded approvingly. Her daughter had chosen well.

With Lynn leading the way, the three of them headed into Astral Bistro's gemstone warehouse.

"Alex, feel free to pick whatever you like. Consider it my gift to you."

Lynn spoke with confidence, as if generosity came naturally to her.

From a distance, Alexander's All-seeing Eyes had already picked up the glow of gemstones, and his spirits lifted.

Once the security guard opened the heavy iron door, they stepped inside.

The warehouse was massive, thousands of square meters, filled with rows of display cases showcasing an array of gemstones, emeralds, as well as gold and silver.

"Alex, our higher-quality gems are over on..." Lynn began.

But before she finished speaking, Alexander had already made his way to one of the central displays, closely examining the contents.

Lynn was taken aback—Alexander hadn't even asked any questions and yet went straight for the best gems.

Was he a gem expert? It seemed unlikely—he was only in his twenties.

Even if he was, no expert could pinpoint the best display that easily.

Alexander caught the curiosity in Lynn's eyes and smiled.

"I've always had a good eye for these things, especially gemstones."

"Impressive! Alex, I wouldn't have guessed you knew so much about gems at your age."

Lynn was getting more and more satisfied with her prospective son-in-law.

"You flatter me, Ms. Blackburn. I just know a little bit."

The truth was, Alexander wasn't a gem expert, but his All-seeing Eyes made it easy to judge quality.

"This one's top-grade red agate... This sapphire is impressive, easily worth a million dollars... This citrine, though, is a pity—too many impurities. It could've been a premium piece."

Alexander murmured to himself as he inspected each gemstone, his commentary revealing his skill.

Lynn was surprised again—he didn't use any tools, nor did he check the tags, yet he spoke about each gem as he knew them intimately.

Soon, Alexander picked out a dozen high-quality gemstones, though none were quite top tier.

High-quality gems could still be useful—they could be crafted into protective charms or used for enhancing Spellweave Patterns.

Despite the size of Astral Bistro's warehouse, truly high-grade gems were in short supply.

Before long, Alexander had browsed the entire inventory, finding nothing else noteworthy, and he shook his head in mild disappointment.

Lynn, eager to help, said, "If you're looking for top-tier gemstones, you should attend tomorrow's auction. There'll be some incredible pieces up for bid."

"That sounds perfect—I'll definitely be there!"

Alexander's eyes lit up, excited at the unexpected opportunity.

"Alex, I'll take you to a friend of mine now. Besides here, he has the most extensive collection. He might have what you need."

Lynn was enthusiastic, leading Alexander across the street to Dragonstone Gem Company.

"Ms. Blackburn! What a surprise seeing you here in person. You could've just called, you know?"

A middle-aged man in a tailored suit with a square jawline greeted them with a wide smile.

Lynn returned his smile warmly.

"Mr. Harris, this is Alexander, my daughter's boyfriend. He's looking for top-tier gemstones."
