Keith told Alexander about the entire process of acquiring the Sakra Society members' information.

Alexander was surprised.

"No wonder Norma couldn't find her sister. That girl already left Risun. Keith, you've done a great job this time!

"With this information on Sakra Society members, I can track them all down and eliminate them, including Yinson!

"Well done, Keith. Thanks for your hard work!"

After praising him, Alexander suddenly frowned and quickly warned him.

"Keith, oh no! You've underestimated them. You need to leave your place immediately."

"What's going on, Mr. Hawthorne? I was careful, and all traces were wiped out. They won't find anything on me."

Keith still didn't quite trust Alexander's judgment, thinking he had done it perfectly.

"Keith, think about it. Since Decker loved secretly filming so much, with all those videos of female teachers and students, he must have installed cameras in his office, too.

"And all the surveillance footage may have already been uploaded to the cloud. Soon, Sakra Society will hunt you!

"Get out of there, head to the Dragon Gang, and find Norma! She'll protect you!"

Before Alexander could finish his words, there was a loud crash from outside.

Then, Paula's shrill scream echoed.

"Ah! What do you want...? Master, escape! They're here!

"Keith, run!"

Paula's desperate shouts were followed by the sound of a struggle.

"You bitch, you killed Mr. Payne! You're dead!"

A man's furious shout rang out, followed by Paula's blood-curdling scream, then... silence.

Keith quickly ended the call, shoved the USB drive into his pocket, locked the door.

And then he used heavy furniture, his desk, chair, and other items to block the door.

It wouldn't hold for long, but it would slow them down.

He knew that Sakra Society's killers were ruthless. If they caught him, he would totally die.

But if he could make it to Dragon Gang's turf, he'd be safe.

Right now, with the Sakra Society under pressure, their power was nothing compared to the Dragon Gang's.

Meanwhile, Alexander had already contacted Norma and sent someone to help Keith.

Outside, Keith could hear hurried footsteps getting closer. He was panicking, unsure where to run or where to hide.

Someone kicked the door from outside.

"Fuck! Open the door! You can't escape!"

The violent pounding on the door and the shouts made Keith even more anxious and frantic.

He could hear that there were several people outside. And he knew, if they broke in, it would be over.

Those men kicked the door hard again.

Thankfully, the door was solid wood, sturdy enough to resist for a little while, especially with the desk and cabinets behind it.

Keith panicked.

This was the sixth floor. If he jumped off, he'd definitely die.

He had nowhere to run, only able to stare at the door, waiting for his death.

The men from outside kicked the door again and again.

The door was cracking now, the desk and cabinets shaking, barely holding.

A few more hits, and they'd be in.

Keith imagined the gruesome outcome. He felt he would probably end in a way worse than Decker.

"I can't die! I just started enjoying my life!"

Grinding his teeth, Keith made up his mind.

He felt that, no matter what, he couldn't stay in the room. Even if he fell, there was a small chance of survival.

Keith quickly opened the window and, shivering, climbed onto the ledge.

The ledge was only wide enough for a palm to fit. It was just enough for a person to stand if they pressed tightly against the wall.

However, it was his only chance at survival.

He spread his arms out, his body sticking to the wall, moving slowly, making sure he wouldn't fall.

Sweat poured down his face, the wind making his body feel light like he might slip at any moment.

He reminded himself not to look down, moving with stiff, awkward motions.

It was only five or six steps to the next window, but to Keith, it felt like an eternity.

Finally, he was just one step away, but his body felt like it was no longer responding to his commands. Every movement was a struggle for him.

And now, he had to step onto the next ledge, but without his hand gripping the wall, the friction would lessen, and he could easily fall.
