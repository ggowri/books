The other item was a red jade box, about half a palm in length and width. Rhodes carefully lifted the lid, and at once, a rich fragrance filled the room. A brilliant crimson light shot out, revealing the faint outline of a golden serpent-dragon, coiling and soaring within the glow, accompanied by the faint sound of a dragon's roar.

Inside the box lay a single crimson pill—the legendary martial arts elixir, Golden Serpent Pill!

If a level-four warrior at their peak consumed this pill, it would greatly enhance their chances of condensing their Force into a core, significantly increasing their odds of advancing to a level-five warrior.

Jaryl, an inner disciple ranked 46th on the martial virtue leaderboard, had sent someone to deliver this pill—on one condition: Alexander had to die in the upcoming grand tournament.

Rhodes let out a bitter chuckle. He was already on the verge of reaching level-five warrior. The sting of his previous tournament defeat had pushed him to shatter his mental barriers, ensuring his inevitable breakthrough. Now, with Madden's Thunderstrike Manual, it would be nearly impossible for him notto reach level five.

He didn't need the Golden Serpent Pill for now, but there was no denying its value. He had seen one before in the Treasure House, priced at five million silver coins or one million martial virtue points.

That Alexander sure was a troublemaker!

But I like that. The humiliation still burned in my heart. Alexander must die.

Rhodes' expression shifted unpredictably as he picked up the Thunderstrike Manual and began flipping through it...

Meanwhile, in the Marshal's Mansion, an old steward bowed and stepped away. Madden sat calmly in a grand armchair, his face expressionless as he muttered to himself, "Rhodes... Alexander..."

In the Dominion Church's Inner Court, Jaryl asked sharply, "Did he take it?"

A middle-aged servant bowed and answered, "Rhodes accepted it."

Three days later, Alexander handed Logan a banknote worth one hundred thousand silver coins. Combined with the one hundred thousand they had won from the last tournament, they now had two hundred thousand silver coins—the startup capital for Serene Medical.

Logan, holding the banknote tightly, excitedly set off for the Hall of Lives, bringing Nova and a few estate workers along.

Liora had once mentioned that the sacred land of the Hall of Lives had a special blessing—herbs sprouted faster and grew with exceptional quality. In fact, most of the herbal seedlings supplied to all of Darwin came from there.

After Logan left, Alexander sat in deep thought. October was approaching fast.

In just a few months, the New Year's Grand Tournament would take place. The Whetherall family and Rhodes would surely come after him, and now Jaryl had joined the mix. Alexander had to push himself harder, break past his limits—only then could he survive. He was just about to plan to spar with Vegeta.

Dong! Dong! Dong!

Alexander leaped to his feet, his face instantly changing. The Great Brahma Bell of Dominion Church had tolled—something big had happened.

Without hesitation, Alexander activated his Force, his feet propelling him forward like a soaring bird as he raced toward the relay station.

Fifteen minutes later, Alexander returned to the Outer Court, heading straight for the training grounds.

The training grounds were packed with Outer Court disciples. The atmosphere buzzed with excitement—whenever the Great Brahma Bell rang, it meant something monumental had occurred.

After a short wait, Declan and Huxley, accompanied by several young disciples, stepped onto the assessment platform. The crowd immediately fell silent.

Declan shook his bible, his expression grave. His voice boomed like thunder.

"Ever since Xhantao surrendered after the first failed invasion by the Oceanic Coalition, it has been a vassal state of the Hrodvik Empire for thirty years—paying tribute every year and sending envoys annually.

"We have just received an urgent report from the imperial court. Today, an envoy from Xhantao arrived in Darwin, pleading for assistance. The Risun Empire has mobilized an army of one million troops in a sudden assault, advancing at a rate of one hundred miles per day. They are now less than five thousand miles from Hamish, the capital of Xhantao. The Risunese are ruthless and insatiable. If they successfully conquer Xhantao, their power will grow exponentially. From then on, our Hrodvik Empire will never know peace.

"Each of you will soon receive a summons from the imperial court. Of course, the battlefield is a place where death is nearly certain! As Outer Court disciples of Dominion Church, you have the right to refuse—or to step forward and enlist."

As Declan spoke, his eyes flashed with a deep, knowing light. Alexander felt as if the master was looking directly at him—just like back during the Outer Court selection, when Declan had secretly hoped he wouldn't rush into battle before reaching level-five warrior.

Huxley stepped forward, taking over the announcement.

"For those who choose to enlist, all costs in the Manual House, Treasure House, Beast Lodge, and Gracehaven Hall—including silver and martial virtue points—will be halved. Additionally, three days from now, the Shaha Trade Fair will be held at the training grounds, jointly hosted by the imperial court and Dominion Church. All enlisting disciples are eligible to participate!"

With that, the two masters turned and left for the meditation hall.

Some in the crowd fell into deep thought, while others turned and left without looking back. Small groups of three or five huddled together, whispering among themselves, while a few spoke with passionate intensity...
