The kidnappers weren't just ordinary criminals; they were seasoned soldiers who had seen the worst of battlefields.

As Melin approached the back door, she heard screams from inside and ducked into the dense bushes, using her binoculars to peer inside.

Alexander was perched in a large tree, hidden by the thick foliage. From his vantage point, he spotted Melin.

Her uniform and gear marked her as a police officer. Her actions seemed reckless, but he admired her bravery.

The abandoned factory was filthy, covered in thick dust. Members of the Fairfax family were tied to concrete pillars, with seven or eight men in black patrolling with assault rifles.

Rosen was punishing a young man, Dexter Fairfax, whom Alexander recognized. Dexter had given him dirty looks before, and now Rosen was whipping him mercilessly.

Nearby, Frank was tied up separately, seething with rage.

"Come on, if you're so tough, beat me instead! Bullying them isn't a skill," Frank shouted.

"You old fool, you're almost dead. I'm not wasting my energy on you," Rosen laughed, striking Dexter again. Dexter's cries echoed through the factory.

Frank trembled with anger, powerless to help. Meanwhile, Alexander moved silently to the factory roof.

Two guards were stationed there, heavily armed, but they were no match for him. He swiftly neutralized them, donned a guard's hat, and took a weapon.

From his new position, he noticed Melin sneaking along the wall. She crouched under a window, awkwardly sticking out as she used her phone to capture images inside. Alexander couldn't help but smile at her daring.

Without his intervention, she might have been caught by the roof guards.

Melin was thrilled to have recorded Rosen's brutality. This evidence would force Oliver to act, and it could make headlines, exposing the crimes of kidnapping and beating over 30 people.

Her determination to bring criminals to justice was unwavering.

However, Melin had underestimated Schroder's influence. Her presence was already detected by another hidden guard. She put away her phone, checked her gun's magazine, and attempted to call for backup.

"Is anyone there? Please respond," she whispered into her radio.

But the signal was weak, and no reply came. Frustrated and uneasy, Melin felt a bad premonition, but her determination drove her forward.

She planned to take down the leaders first, hoping to capture Rosen and free the others. If the Fairfax members could arm themselves, they might stand a chance.

But this was wishful thinking. Even with weapons, they were unlikely to fight back against Rosen's battle-hardened men.

Unbeknownst to her, two armed guards had quietly trailed her, waiting for an opportunity to capture her.

Melin's athletic training had given her a fit physique, and her uniform accentuated her curves. The guards watched her with interest, struggling to stay focused on their task.
