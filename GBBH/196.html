In less than half an hour, the Federer family, once over a hundred strong, was reduced to just over thirty.

Each of them was terrified, completely lost.

What scared them even more was that many people in black were pouring in from all directions, armed with various weapons, closing in menacingly.

Clearly, they were ambushed, and they still had to face the terrifying Death in such a stormy night.

The elite team of the Federer family had completely lost their fighting spirit, turning into lambs for slaughter.

The intense battle that Augusta had imagined never happened; it only took them about ten minutes cleaning up the rest of them.

The rain continued to fall, and the screams gradually lowered down.

The ground was littered with bodies, and blood flowed everywhere.

Alexander wore a calm, gentle smile as he wiped the bloody dagger.

At that moment, only one person stood before him: Edward.

Besides this so-called top fighter of the Federer family, everyone else had fallen.

"Alexander, I had no idea you were hiding so deeply. After killing so many people, won't you have nightmares tonight? You're just a devil!"

Edward trembled uncontrollably.

If he had any strength left, he would have turned and run away immediately.

As Edward watched Alexander came closer slowly, he felt an overwhelming sense of dread, backing away while starting to plead for mercy.

"Please don't kill me. I don't want to die. Just tell me what you want me to do..."

He was truly scared out of his wits, lacking any will to resist.

Alexander chuckled. "Since you're so perspective, I won't kill you; you might be useful to me."

With that, he was already in front of Edward.

Edward staggered back two steps, gripping his knife tightly but lacking the courage to strike at Alexander.

His hands trembled, rain streaming down his face, and he didn't dare to blink.

This elite group was the Federer family's trump card, but in less than half an hour, over half of them had been killed by Alexander.

Although Edward was slightly stronger than the old butler, his mental defenses had completely collapsed.

He took a step back as Alexander took a step forward.

"Clang!"

Finally, Edward's knife fell to the ground, and he knelt before Alexander with a thud.

Alexander smiled slightly, "It seems you really are afraid of me."

Just then, a flash of lightning illuminated Alexander's brilliant smile. Edward almost wet his pants at this.

"Please don't kill me... I'll do whatever you ask..."

"Smack!"

Alexander slapped him hard across the face.

Edward spat blood but didn't dare to move.

"Smack!"

Alexander slapped again.

Blood sprayed from Edward's mouth and nose, and several of his teeth were knocked out; his swollen cheeks felt completely numb.

"Where's Gary?"

Alexander looked at him coldly, ready to slap him again if he hesitated.

"Gary is at the home waiting for me to report back; he told me to bring you and Ava back within two hours..."

Edward didn't hold back; he told Alexander immediately.

"How much security does the Federer family have?"

Alexander's question made Gary shiver; he knew what Alexander was getting at.

"Speak!" Alexander shouted sharply.

"I'll tell you... we have a dozen bodyguards at the Federer family estate..."

"Good, take me to the estate. I want to see Gary. As long as you listen to me, not only will I spare your life, but I can also offer you wealth."

Alexander's expression was hard to read.

"What? Wealth?"

Edward was stunned.

Just then, a bolt of lightning split the night sky; to Edward, Alexander's amused expression looked more terrifying than a reaper from hell.

"Let's go." Alexander pushed Edward into the car.

He checked the clock; it was already 4 AM, and although it was still raining, it had lightened up considerably.

Alexander looked out the window at the hellish scene outside.

He ordered Augusta to drive while the others quickly cleaned up the scene.

With hundreds injured or dead, if not handled properly, it would cause a nationwide stir.

Fortunately, the heavy rain had washed away most of the evidence, and once they finished cleaning, there would be no traces left.

Augusta drove as fast as she could toward the Federer family estate.
