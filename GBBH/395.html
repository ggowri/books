Eugene smiled in agreement.

"Yes, yes, three drinks, and everything's forgiven. I believe Mary is loyal to us. She'd never betray us over someone like Alexander."

Mary frowned.

"I'm sorry, but I can't drink anymore. How about I apologize with water instead?" Mary suggested.

As she spoke, Mary raised the cup.

"Crash!"

Lawrence swung his hand forcefully, knocking the cup from her hand and smashing it to the floor.

"Enough with the excuses! If you won't drink, then come upstairs with me. If you could sleep with Alexander, you can sleep with us!" Lawrence snarled.

Lawrence growled menacingly.

Eugene smirked silently, clearly having planned everything with Lawrence.

"Heh heh..."

The other Blood Gang members looked on with glee, enjoying the scene.

At this, Eugene urged, "Mary, Lawrence here represents the Godfather. Showing your respect with a toast, and you still won't drink three glasses?

"Now that the Blood Gang serves the Mafia, your refusal is disrespectful to the Godfather. If he gets angry, you won't be able to handle it."

The others quickly joined in, agreeing.

"Mary, if you keep refusing, it's on you. We won't side with you if Lawrence loses his temper."

"Exactly. Lawrence has already spoken. It's just three drinks. It'll settle things between you two nicely!"

"Right, it's just a bit of alcohol. What's the hesitation? Or would you prefer to make amends in a room upstairs? Ha ha!"

"Maybe Mary's been lonely and wants to join us all upstairs!"

Their eyes roamed over Mary with leering, malicious delight.

Mary, as the branch leader in Loscity, had once commanded their respect, though they were envious.

But with Eugene and Lawrence backing them now, they were each taking their chance to corner her.

Mary's once-cheerful face turned ashen as she realized she was trapped.

She knew she couldn't drink these three glasses. She'd never make it out of the hotel. Luckily, she had sent Alexander a text for help.

In all of Loscity, he was probably her only hope.

To buy time, Mary smiled and said to Eugene, "Mr. Griffin, as a member of the Blood Gang, if I'm forced to drink and word gets out, won't it damage the gang's reputation?"

Eugene laughed mockingly, ignoring her completely.

Seeing her refusal, Lawrence slammed the bottle down in front of her.

"You think you're someone important? Even your gang boss would show me respect. You'll drink whether you like it or not!"

Mary trembled with anger, Alexander's face flashing through her mind. He was her last hope.

"Now it's not just three drinks. You'll drink this entire bottle, or I'll pour it down your throat!" Lawrence threatened viciously.

Tears welled in Mary's eyes; without the Blood Gang's protection, she had fallen from her position of power to a mere nobody.

Eugene watched, amused, a cold smile on his face, already plotting his next move.

Just as Lawrence grabbed Mary and was about to force the bottle into her mouth, a chilling voice echoed.

"Stop! You dare force my woman to drink?"

In an instant, the air seemed to freeze as the room fell silent.

Everyone turned, wide-eyed, to see Alexander stride into the room.

They all recognized Alexander, but none of them had expected him to show up here.

Lawrence sneered, saying, "Mary, looks like you really are involved with Alexander. You tipped him off, didn't you?"

Eugene laughed as well. "Brilliant as always, Lawrence. Using this harlot, Mary, to draw Alexander out. It worked perfectly!"

"Bang!"

Lawrence slammed the table and pointed at Alexander, shouting, "You think you're fit to play hero? I've been waiting to trap you all along!"

As he spoke, a group of burly men flooded into the room, surrounding Alexander.
