He slowly stood up, coldly staring at Stephen, locking him in with a terrifying gaze.

"Um?" Stephen's face changed as he felt the air around him grow heavy, as if a terrifying thunderstorm was about to descend.

He shot a fierce glare at Alexander, his expression filled with confusion and disbelief.

Alexander stood with his hands clasped behind his back, observing Stephen with a completely relaxed posture and a deep, unfathomable gaze.

Stephen couldn't help but shiver. In that instant, it felt as if all of his secrets were laid bare, exposed by him. There was no hiding anything.

It was a chilling feeling.

Stephen finally took this seriously, gripping the hilt of his sword tightly, his brows deeply furrowed.

He hadn't paid attention to Alexander when he entered because Alexander had seemed too ordinary, not like a real expert at all.

Now, Stephen realized how badly he had underestimated him. Among everyone here, only this young man was truly his match.

"Who the hell are you?" Stephen demanded gravely.

Alexander gave a faint smile. "Stephen, I heard you're the newly crowned number one expert of Sakra Society, and you've used enhancement drugs, right?"

"That's right. What are you trying to say?"

Stephen gripped the hilt, ready to strike at any moment.

"Then you must know where Yinson is. If you tell me everything about Yinson, I might consider sparing your life." Alexander spoke as if it were a simple request.

"You want to find Mr. White? Who the hell are you?"

Stephen's eyes swept over Alexander. He suddenly thought about someone. His face immediately changed drastically.

"You're Alexander!"

He shouted in anger, pulling out two sabers in an instant, a murderous aura radiating from him.

Alexander smiled faintly, "Yes, that's right, it's me."

At first, Stephen was shocked, but then he grew excited, his expression turning even more vicious.

"Alexander, you dared to make our Sakra Society suffer, nearly putting Mr. White in a position where he couldn't recover. I will make you pay for that!

"Tyler and the others must have died at your hands. Today, I'm going to chop off your arms and legs, turn you into a cripple, leaving you to suffer huge pain."

Stephen gritted his teeth, wishing he could tear Alexander apart immediately.

Stephen's murderous intent grew more intense. Karson and Cesar were both shocked. From Stephen's words, it seemed that Alexander had once dealt a heavy blow to Sakra Society.

Karson's face revealed an expression of hope and excitement. Perhaps this time, there was still a chance. He saw a glimmer of hope for survival.

"You were so arrogant earlier. Now, why are you talking so much?"

Alexander's Enhanced Vision locked onto Stephen, taking in all of his physical condition. What part of him was strong, where his weaknesses were, it was all clear, laid bare before him.

In Alexander's powerful mind, he instantly simulated dozens, even hundreds, of Stephen's movements.

Each version of Stephen launched a different attack at him, including subsequent combos. He knew exactly how to respond, how to defeat Stephen.

This was the power of an Advanced Warrior, completely surpassing human limits.

Alexander disdainfully beckoned to Stephen with a finger. "Come on, I'll give you three moves without fighting back."

Karson immediately warned him in fear, "Alex, don't be careless. This guy is the top expert of Sakra Society. Earlier, even Mr. Dussel and the others were no match for him. You need to be cautious!"

But Alexander just smiled dismissively.

Stephen was furious. This was the greatest insult he had ever received.

"You want to give me three moves without fighting back? Fine, very well. Today, I'll cripple you, and then you'll know how wrong you were."

Stephen stopped holding back and drew both of his sabers.

The blade power was twice as vast as before. The massive sword light, like two new moons, slashed toward Alexander, aiming to cut him into pieces.

Alexander kept his promise, not fighting back. He dodged and retreated effortlessly.

For a moment, Stephen held the advantage, fiercely chasing after Alexander.

But something strange happened. No matter how fast Stephen swung his blades, no matter how he surged forward, he couldn't even touch Alexander.

Alexander seemed like a leaf caught in the wind, effortlessly drifting away from the blade's wind, his movements graceful and light.

Every time it seemed like he was about to be struck, he would narrowly avoid it by mere millimeters.
