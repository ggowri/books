The atmosphere was thick with tension as Emma nervously glanced between Alexander and the check lying temptingly on the floor.

Thirty million dollars!

That was a sum most people could only dream of making in a lifetime.

Like Rebecca, Emma was convinced Alexander wouldn't pass up this golden opportunity.

After all, with that kind of money, he could have his pick of beautiful women—or even juggle a few at once.

Sure enough, Alexander bent down and picked up the check, much to Rebecca's delight.

Her disdainful smile spread wider. Rebecca was convinced that she had finally broken him under the allure of wealth.

Emma's eyes narrowed with contempt as she watched Alexander, her expression dripping with scorn.

But then, the surprise came.

Rebecca's smug grin faltered as she watched Alexander tear the check into pieces, flinging them out the window.

The pieces fluttered away like confetti, startling the fly perched on the glass, which immediately darted off into the air.

Alexander's voice was calm as he addressed Rebecca.

"Did you see that fly just now? It flew away."

"What do you mean?" Rebecca frowned, confusion etched on her face.

With piercing clarity, Alexander locked eyes with her. "You look down on it because it's just a fly, but it can soar. Can you?"

Rebecca was momentarily speechless.

He continued, his tone gaining strength, "You only see it as a fly, but it can fly higher than any of us.

"It has seen skies you've never glimpsed, weathered storms you've never faced.

"There are countless flies in this world, living their own lives, just like us common folk.

"I bet when the Hansen family first arrived in Loscity two centuries ago, they might have been seen as less than a fly by those big shots.

"Even now, I see the Hansen family as just a slightly stronger fly."

Rebecca's face turned crimson with rage.

"Shut your mouth! Who do you think you are to judge the Hansen family?"

Ignoring her fury, Alexander seemed to be lost in thought.

"You, as part of the Hansen family, aren't superior; you're just standing on the hard work of your ancestors. What's there to be proud of?

"To me, you and Orion are just flies."

Alexander looked at Rebecca with an expression full of pride.

"You compare us to flies? You must be out of your mind!" Rebecca shot back, her voice rising.

She didn't want to argue with him any longer.

In her mind, Alexander had a way with words that twisted and turned, making it hard for her to counter him.

"Even if you make valid points, you have no right to be involved in negotiations between the Hansen family and the Saint Group. That's the gap in our statuses—a chasm that can't be crossed."

With that, she strutted into the office, leaving Emma at the door, her posture defensive, like a guard protecting treasure.

Alexander didn't bother to argue. Instead, he walked around the corner and called Anne.

Before long, Ava and the two from the Hansen family emerged from the office, their expressions a mix of confusion and frustration.

Ava held her phone, indignation spilling over.

"Ms. Hollister, what's going on? Why did the chairman refuse to let me negotiate with the Hansen family? What's the reasoning behind it?"

Anne explained the situation, and Ava's eyes widened. "What? You want me to wait until tomorrow's meeting? What does that even mean?"

Before she could process it further, Anne hung up.

A sly smile crept onto Alexander's face. He had instructed Anne to relay the chairman's orders. no negotiations with the Hansen family.

Tomorrow, the Saint Group will undoubtedly become the exclusive distributor of magic powder. Forget the Hansen family; everyone would be begging to partner with the Saint Group.

Orion glared at Alexander; his fist raised threateningly.

"What's so funny, Alexander? You better remember our wager. If you back out, I'll make you wish you hadn't."

With that, he and Rebecca left, their expressions cold, not even bothering to acknowledge Ava.

Rejection from the chairman of the Saint Group stung deeply, leaving Ava feeling humiliated.

Feeling wronged, she retreated into her office, ignoring Alexander completely.

Word spread quickly about Ava's decision to break things off with Alexander, and soon, the news reached every corner of the Saint Group.

The story quickly spread like wildfire, eventually landing on the front page of an online news outlet.
