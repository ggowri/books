With all the Cripples sprawled out on the ground, Houtte stood there, utterly alone.

Alexander brushed a few stray strands of hair from his forehead, straightened his suit, and walked toward the stunned Houtte with the grace of a dancer.

Houtte felt numb, thinking, "I brought over fifty of my best men!

And they all fell before Alexander in just a matter of minutes? Is he even human?"

Houtte stammered, panic rising in his chest, "Alexander, this is a misunderstanding! Please, don't come any closer! It was David who made me do this... it's all a mistake!"

As he spoke, Houtte pulled out his handgun, aiming it at Alexander.

But his hands trembled, betraying his fear.

Alexander didn't seem to notice the gun; he continued to advance calmly.

"Stop right there! I swear, if you keep coming, I'll take you down!" Houtte shouted, his voice rising in desperation.

Yet Alexander ignored him, closing the distance until he was just about ten feet away.

With gritted teeth, Houtte squeezed the trigger.

Three shots rang out.

Ava gasped, thinking, "Why is Alexander charging toward a barrel of a gun?"

In a flash, everyone witnessed a breathtaking scene.

Alexander twisted and turned with lightning speed, effortlessly evading every bullet that was fired at him.

Houtte panicked and emptied his magazine, each shot echoing like thunder.

Yet, not a single bullet found its mark, not even brushing against Alexander's suit.

"Oh my God! You're not human! You're a demon—a creature straight out of hell!" Houtte squealed in horror.

In a fit of terror, Houtte tossed his gun aside and scrambled away.

Alexander casually kicked a stone on the ground.

The rock whizzed through the air like a bullet and struck Houtte's right leg.

It created a gruesome wound that bled profusely.

"I'm sorry! I didn't mean it! This is all a misunderstanding—David made me do this! Please, spare me!" Houtte cried, collapsing to the ground in a pitiful display.

With tears mixing with snot, he begged for his life, completely abandoning his previous bravado.

In the distance, sirens began to wail; the cops were on their way, drawn by the chaos.

Alexander turned briefly to the driver of the Rolls-Royce. "You keep Ms. Blackburn safe until the police arrive."

With that, he scooped up Houtte like a hawk with its prey and sprinted away with surprising speed.

Houtte, towering at six foot three and weighing over two hundred pounds, felt as light as a feather in Alexander's grip.

Meanwhile, inside the luxurious vehicle, David was frantically urging the driver.

"What's taking so long? Hurry up, or he's going to catch us!"

The driver slammed on the gas, tires screeching as they tore down the street.

David sighed in relief, confident that Alexander wouldn't outrun the car's speed.

Once they turned the corner ahead, they'd lose him completely.

But suddenly, a deafening crash echoed through the air.

In the blink of an eye, a body propelled through the sky like a projectile, smashing through the windshield and landing violently in the driver's seat, sending shards of glass flying everywhere.

The driver screamed, his right eye punctured by a glass shard, and the car turned into a wild beast, crashing hard into a lamppost.

Airbags deployed with a loud pop, and though David escaped unscathed, he was dazed, his senses foggy.

Through the haze, he caught a glimpse of the figure who had shattered the windshield: it was Houtte, bloodied and covered in glass shards.

His eyes were wide with terror and disbelief, frozen in place—he was clearly dead.

Before David could regain his composure, Alexander tore the deformed car door open, seizing him by the neck and yanking him out.

"Alexander, you dare lay a hand on me? The Federer family..." David began, his bravado masking his fear.

"Shut up!"

Alexander roared, delivering a series of fierce slaps that left David spitting blood and his cheeks swollen and red.

The sound of sirens grew increasingly closer, echoing through the tension-filled air.

David's eyes lit up with triumph.

"The cops are here, and you really think you can get away with killing me? I'm a state senator, not some low-life scum like you.. Ah!"

His taunts were abruptly cut off.

With a swift motion, Alexander shoved him against a broken streetlight pole, impaling his body upon the jagged metal. Blood gushed forth, staining the surroundings.

Just as the approaching police cars were about to round the corner, Alexander stood up, darted off, and vanished into the dense thicket of the nearby woods.
