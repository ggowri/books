Ten days later, a group of soldiers delivered three white jade bottles to Alexander, containing a total of nine Docpirin pills.

The autumn air was crisp, and the scenery was breathtaking. A hundred miles from Kean Manor, Alexander and a dozen companions galloped across the open plains on tall, powerful horses. The sound of hooves thundered, mingling with their laughter and shouts.

When the horses grew tired, they found a forest clearing to rest. The estate guards laid out mats and unpacked large food containers, revealing an array of delicacies—roast chicken, braised beef, steamed fish, and roast pig.

Alexander, Logan, the three Valdez brothers, Vegeta, and Nova sat together, eating, drinking, and chatting. Spirits were high, and laughter filled the air.

But Alexander noticed that Logan's brows were tightly furrowed as if something was on his mind. Leaning in, Alexander chuckled and asked, "What's on your mind? Something bothering you?"

Logan grinned. "Alexander, the other day I was talking with Father Liora and learned that the sacred herbs at Hall of Lives grow in blessed land, enhanced by spiritual energy. That's how they have thousand-year-old and even ten-thousand-year-old medicinal plants.

"But Sahasia is vast, with billions of people. Hall of Lives's herbs are excellent, but they can't possibly supply everyone.

"Even with Quinton's great leadership and Hrodvik flourishing, it's still difficult for the common folk to access quality medicine. And if a plague or war breaks out, getting medicine would be nearly impossible."

Alexander sighed and nodded.

"Yeah... It's always been hard to get medical treatment, and medicine is expensive. You have an idea?"

"Alexander, you know I have experience in cultivating medicinal herbs. We don't have plastic greenhouses here, but we do have transparent glass tiles. There's no electricity, but we have water power. We have manpower. We have land..."

Logan spoke passionately, and Alexander's eyes lit up as he listened.

"We need our own brand. We establish large-scale herbal plantations, then set up chain pharmacies. Not only would we be saving lives, but we'd also expand our influence across the world..."

By the end, Alexander was breaking into a cold sweat—Logan had practically brought a modern business model into this world.

He had only ever heard about these ideas from TV and the internet, but they sounded promising.

Logan finally stopped talking and took a big gulp of liquor to quench his thirst.

Sellers and his brothers exchanged glances and nodded.

Logan's eyes reddened. He thought of his parents. "By the time they grow old and gray... will I ever be able to go back?"

The Thunderstrike Manual!
