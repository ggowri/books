Alexander was just fuming about not getting closer to Ava, but now any desire he had vanished, replaced by an overwhelming anger.

"Anthony, you're such a coward! If you've got guts, come at me directly! Why resort to such vile methods?"

Compared to Guzman, Alexander now wanted to deal with this second brother of his even more.

Unfortunately, even Alice knew little about Anthony. All she knew was that Anthony was an eccentric scientist, who controlled the multi-billion-dollar "Red Umbrella Group" from behind the scenes.

Red Umbrella Group was a tech giant involved in biotech, defense products, and other advanced industries.

Rumor had it that Red Umbrella Group had close ties with the government, working on many secret biochemical drugs and biological weapons projects.

Alexander reviewed the information on Red Umbrella Group in his mind and soon arrived at the restaurant.

He found the large dining hall floor covered with over a hundred people sprawled out, scattered everywhere.

They were all wearing Saint Group uniforms, each in agonizing pain, curled up and convulsing, some even with blood at the corners of their mouths.

About twenty medical personnel were already there but seemed completely helpless.

Ava quickly asked the restaurant manager, "What's going on here, Ron?"

Visibly shaken, Ron stammered, "The doctor... just said the drinks in the restaurant are poisoned, Ms. Blackburn. I swear I didn't know... it wasn't me..."

"Useless!" Ava pushed him aside and hurried over to the lead doctor.

"Wait, it's you?" She was taken aback to find that it was Vincent, causing her to pause in surprise.

Vincent blushed with embarrassment, avoiding Alexander's gaze, and quickly began explaining the situation.

Based on our initial tests, it's a neurotoxin. While we've managed to stabilize some, over fifty are in critical condition, and their chances look grim.

Max grabbed Ron, scolding him. "Tell me the truth! Did you poison the drinks? Say it, or I'll deal with you myself!"

Ron was on the verge of tears. "Mr. Blake, Ms. Blackburn... I swear I didn't do it. I've managed the company's restaurant for ten years. It couldn't have been me!"

Ava scolded. "Max, stop it. Ron likely had nothing to do with this. If he wanted to poison us, he'd have had plenty of chances before today."

"Hmph! Even if it wasn't you, Ron, you're still responsible for poor management. We'll settle this once it's over." Max angrily pushed Ron aside.

Vincent frowned, saying, "We don't have enough ambulances to take everyone to the hospital. And even if we did, they don't have enough doctors to save them all..."

"Ah, it hurts so much!"

"Please, someone save me!"

More employees began crying out in agony, rolling on the ground in sheer torment, creating a hellish scene.

Witnessing this, even Vincent and the usually composed Ava appeared at a loss.

"Let me try!" Suddenly, Alexander stepped forward.

Ava, Vincent, and others looked at him in shock.

"Honey, do you have a way?" Ava hugged Alexander, her face full of excitement and hope.

Alexander gave a wry smile. "There are so many people. Let me assess their condition first."

Vincent mentioned it was some kind of neurotoxin, and Alexander wasn't sure if the Force would help in neutralizing it. Even if it did, there were too many critical patients.

Vincent looked at Alexander with a peculiar expression, feeling low about the vast gap between them.

Alexander's smile faded as he used his Force Sense to examine the patients.

In that moment, he emitted an aura of calm authority that commanded respect.

Ava looked at him with admiration, her eyes sparkling.

Vincent, Max, and others felt a sense of awe and even submission as they watched him.

Alexander was unaware that as he tapped further into the Force, his strength and the energy he radiated would far surpass that of ordinary people.
