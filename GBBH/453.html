Masters of the sword were categorized into five levels—swordsman, swordmaster, great swordmaster, sword emperor, and sword sage. If Raven was a swordmaster, he was undoubtedly a master with the sword.

Raven stood with one foot slightly ahead of the other, his body center swaying slightly, maintaining an extremely delicate balance.

At the same time, the rapier in his hand pointed slightly downward, a faint pale light flowing along its length.

Everyone present felt an immense pressure, intermingled with a bone-chilling cold.

Alexander, being at the forefront, felt it even more intensely. He hadn't expected Raven to be not only a skilled swordmaster but also a metahuman with freezing power.

Alexander snorted lightly, unimpressed.

Ice and fire were natural enemies. Kyle had fire power, so Alexander used the Force to simulate freezing power to counter him, leading Kyle to believe he was also a metahuman.

Faced with Raven's freezing power, Alexander would, of course, use the Force to simulate fire power.

Moreover, the Force Burning technique was already overbearing.

"Die!"

Raven struck three rapid thrusts.

Three points of sword light, forming a triangle, stabbed toward Alexander's chest while the freezing power wreaked havoc, making Alexander feel as if he was being frozen solid, his body rigid.

A swordmaster with freezing power was indeed formidable.

Alexander snorted coldly and unleashed the Force. His frozen body immediately returned to normal.

At the same time, a scorching energy erupted, dispersing Raven's three attacks.

"Not bad! So you, like Kyle, are a metahuman with fire power. I underestimated you."

Raven roared, his sword striking like lightning.

This time, he thrust five times, targeting Alexander's throat and chest.

"I am a metahuman? You're insulting me!"

Alexander laughed loudly.

He was absolutely right. The Force of an Intermediate Warrior far surpassed low-level powers.

Only metahumans of level five and above could possibly engage in combat with Alexander as he was now.

Raven's five attacks were easily dodged by Alexander, not even grazing the hem of his clothes.

"It's my turn to strike!"

Alexander's aura surged, the Force erupting.

With a dazzling sword light, the Lightsaber fell like a meteor from the sky, instantly severing Raven's rapier.

"Damn!"

Raven's face paled, and he retreated hastily.

But it was too late. The white beam had already brushed across his chest.

Even though Raven retreated quickly, he still felt a sharp pain in his chest. Blood gushed out like a spring.

Raven looked down to see a sword wound as thick as a finger and as long as half an arm across his chest.

"Alexander, you also wield a sword! What kind of sword is this?"

Raven stared at Alexander in shock, finding it hard to believe.

Alexander's figure flickered, and he appeared behind Raven like a specter before Raven could recover from his astonishment.

He felt a flash of white light pass by him, and a sharp pain shot through his right arm.

"Ah!"

In his scream, Raven's right arm fell to the ground like a chicken wing severed by a kitchen knife, his blood splattering.

"You dare to maim me? I am a swordmaster and a level-one metahuman. How could you possibly maim me! I will kill you!"

Badly injured, Raven had fallen into madness, howling like a wounded beast.

An icy frost suddenly erupted. Frost even formed on the ground and spread rapidly.

Blood flowed from Raven's eyes, nose, and even ears. He had become a grotesque and terrifying figure of blood.

Raven was willing to damage his body in exchange for a short burst of power.

The coldness of the frost doubled, forcing Ava and others to retreat in horror, but Alexander remained calm.

Raven was fighting desperately. If he couldn't kill Alexander, he would die from exhaustion.

"The Force, burn!"

Alexander swung his Lightsaber.

Two dazzling sword lights crossed to form a cross, darting across Raven's body.

Raven's grotesque expression solidified, and his body bore a cross-shaped crack.

Then, his entire body shattered into countless pieces of flesh and blood that fell to the ground, igniting small white flames.

The cold frost disappeared without a trace, burned away by these flames.
