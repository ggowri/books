Silvia turned around, grabbed her designer handbag, and reached inside.

She pulled out a thick stack of cash and flung it at Alexander.

"The best male models in Kylaville charge five thousand. I'll double that."

"Here's ten grand. Take the money and get out."

She crossed her arms over her chest, exuding arrogance.

Alexander didn't even glance at the money. Instead, he smirked and said, "If you want me gone, at least let me put my pants on first."

"You... you shameless bastard!"

Silvia nearly choked on her anger. She grabbed an ashtray and hurled it at him.

Alexander reacted quickly, catching it midair.

As he got dressed, he was secretly amazed at how his body had changed. Ever since regaining a trace of the Force, his strength and reflexes had clearly improved.

But he was still full of questions. He thought, "Why am I here? What happened before this?

Where did the zombies and mutated beasts go?"

"Move faster and get the hell out, you useless man!"

Silvia's sudden ridicule snapped Alexander back to reality.

His pride as a man felt challenged, and he immediately shot back, "Whether I'm useless or not, the one who experienced it should know best. Who was begging for more last night?"

Silvia's face turned crimson. She wanted nothing more than to throw him out.

Her voice rose in shame and anger. "Take your payment and get lost. I slept with you! I don't need you to take responsibility!"

Alexander sneered. "Consider it a free trial. Next time, you'll have to pay. See you around."

With that, he grabbed the unconscious Maxwell off the floor and strode out of the villa.

Just as he reached the door, a sudden gust of wind rushed toward him from behind.

His instincts kicked in, and he dodged to the side. Half a wine bottle whizzed past his head, barely missing his hair before shattering it against the ground.

Silvia had thrown it with full force.

If his reflexes hadn't improved, he would have been hit. At best, he'd be bleeding. At worst, he'd be knocked out with a concussion.

He turned around and glared at Silvia. "What the hell do you want?"

"You pathetic man! In a month, I'm getting engaged to Lonnie. He's from the Atkinson family, one of Kylaville's top elite families. You can't afford to cross him."

"You better pray no one ever finds out about today. Because even if Lonnie doesn't kill you, I will!"

Her voice trembled, filled with despair and rage.

Alexander chuckled. "Relax. As long as you're not Ava, I won't cling to you."

"You're right. Let's just pretend this was a dream. It's better for both of us."

With that, he walked away without looking back.

Silvia curled up in the corner of the couch, covering her face as tears streamed down her cheeks.

"Why... why did this have to happen..."

Betrayed by a friend, her purity, which she had guarded for over twenty years, was suddenly taken away. The loss and pain were unbearable.

She cried for a long time.

Suddenly, a familiar soft cough sounded behind her.

Silvia shuddered in fear. She looked up and saw that Alexander had returned.

"You! Why are you back?! What do you want now? I'll call the police!"

Terror flashed across her face. Her whole body trembled.

Alexander let out a sigh. "I wrote you a prescription. It's your first time going through this. The pain will last at least a week. Crush these herbs into powder and apply them. You'll feel better in half a day."

"Get out!"

Silvia snapped. She grabbed her phone and hurled it straight at Alexander's nose.

He tried to dodge at full speed.

But they were too close, and Silvia moved too fast. The phone grazed his ear, sending a sharp sting of pain through him.

"You really don't know what's good for you!"

Alexander clutched his ear, furious.

Silvia buried her face in her knees and sobbed uncontrollably.

"Fine, fine! Stop crying. I'm leaving!"

Alexander sighed and stepped out of the villa, shutting the door behind him.

Silence filled the room. Silvia sat on the floor, drained and broken.

Her eyes drifted to the red stain on the couch. The memory of last night flashed through her mind. Her pale face flushed crimson.

She forced herself to stand, determined to check the security cameras and confirm that Alexander was truly gone.

"Ow..."

But the moment she moved, pain shot through her. She nearly collapsed.

She couldn't even walk anymore.

Going to a hospital was out of the question. She was a woman of status. If news spread that she had been so wild in bed that she had to see a doctor, she would become a laughingstock.
