Theodore's laughter echoed through the room as he saw Alexander lose his composure.

"You're nothing but a waste of space, Alexander! Even your wife and daughter aren't really yours. Ava will bow to me, and you'll always be the lowest of the low."

"Mr. Tate, please don't keep rubbing it in. You're going to give him a heart attack."

"Yeah, Mr. Tate, why don't you just knock Jane up again? Show this loser how far apart you really are!"

Theodore's henchmen joined in, their taunts flying thick and fast.

The guests murmured among themselves, laughter and mockery rippling through the crowd.

But Alexander didn't rise to the bait. Instead, he took a swift step forward and swung his hand.

The sound of the slap rang out like a gunshot, and Theodore staggered back, blood spilling from his mouth.

Everyone froze, including the tattooed man, who couldn't keep up with Alexander's speed.

Theodore clutched his swollen cheek, fury bubbling over. "You... you slapped me?"

"That was for my daughter," Alexander replied coolly, and before Theodore could react, he delivered another powerful slap that sent Theodore crashing to the ground.

"And this one's for myself."

Fuming, Theodore shouted, "You're dead, Alexander! I swear I'll kill you!"

In all his life, apart from his uncle and father, no one had ever dared to strike him like that.

Alexander's gaze was cold. "I believe in repaying kindness with kindness, and vengeance with vengeance. You insult my daughter today, and I'll break both your hands."

Theodore turned pale as a ghost.

Silence fell over the crowd; no one expected Alexander to not only slap Theodore in public but also threaten to cripple him.

"Enough!"

The tattooed man snapped out of his shock and charged at Alexander, throwing a punch that seemed to cut through the air like a freight train.

"You're gonna regret this!"

Alexander didn't flinch. Instead, he met the punch head-on.

The two fists collided with a loud crack, and the tattooed man's right hand hung limply at his side, broken.

Pain surged through him, and he clutched his shattered wrist, eyes wide with disbelief.

"My... my hand!"

He had thrown a punch that could shatter stone, yet it had been effortlessly countered.

Alexander raised an eyebrow, surprised to find his own wrist tingling. The tattooed man's punch had contained some mysterious inner strength—an internal force he hadn't anticipated.

"This guy is the toughest opponent I've faced so far," Alexander thought, impressed.

"I told you I'd break your hands, and now there's no one to save you," Alexander declared, striding toward Theodore.

"Everyone, attack! Get him!"

Panic set in as Theodore shouted for his men to swarm Alexander.

A dozen black-clad bodyguards surged forward, but they couldn't stop Alexander's advance.

One by one, they fell to the ground, unable to withstand even a single punch from him.

"You're dead meat!"

The tattooed man roared, channeling his rage as he lunged at Alexander again.

Without even looking back, Alexander delivered a powerful kick.

The tattooed man flew across the room, crashing into the wall with a sickening thud.

He groaned in pain, unable to rise.

The remaining henchmen were either knocked out cold or too terrified to make a move against Alexander.

The crowd, once filled with jeers and laughter, now stood in stunned silence, slowly backing away, afraid of being caught in the crossfire.

Some had witnessed Alexander's fight with Joseph before, but they never imagined he could unleash such ferocity.

Now they understood why Ava had chosen him.

A powerhouse like Alexander was someone any faction would want to recruit.

Andrew stammered in shock, "How is this possible? He... he used to let us beat him up and belittle him, didn't he?"

Jane glanced at Theodore, now a pitiable mess on the floor, and then at Alexander, who stood tall and commanding, exuding an aura of power. An overwhelming sense of regret washed over her.

"Have I really made a mistake?"

Jane's emotions were in turmoil, torn between guilt and despair.

Theodore, scrambling and desperate, tried to make his escape, but Alexander was already right in front of Theodore, his gaze dripping with disdain.

"Alexander, think this through. Maiming me is a serious crime. I'll call the police, and with so many witnesses, you'll end up behind bars for a long time!"

Theodore's attempt to leverage legal threats against Alexander was nothing short of absurd.
