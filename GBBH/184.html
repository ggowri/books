Logan lay there, his arm soaked in blood, bandages wrapped around his shoulder, with crimson still oozing through.

A nasty, swollen bruise marred the back of his head, festering a dark hue, the contusion pushing alarmingly against his scalp—it was a horrified sight.

He was completely unconscious, eyes tightly shut, unaware of the chaos around him.

Augusta, Marvin, and Betty, alongside a handful of other subordinates, surrounded anxiously at his bedside.

"What happened? Why did the Fraternity attack us?" Alexander questioned, his tone serious, scanning their faces for answers.

Betty and the others exchanged uneasy glances, their expressions turning awkward as they hesitated.

"Spit it out! What's going on?" Alexander pressed.

Betty leaned closer, whispering urgently into Alexander's ear, "It's like this... the guy who tried to kidnap Mary, Mark, is tied to the Fraternity.

"David was pulling the strings behind him.

"Now that David's dead, the Fraternity couldn't get paid, so they're out for revenge.

"They attacked our headquarters with over a hundred guys, trying to snatch our magic powder."

Alexander felt a pang of guilt—this mess was partly his fault. Thankfully, Marvin and Logan had two teams with them, totaling just over twenty men.

They managed to push back the Fraternity's assault, but at a cost: dozens of their enemies were either dead or injured, and half of his crew had been hurt.

Logan had bravely defended the warehouse storing the magic powder, taking down several of the attackers but taking a gunshot wound to his arm and a blow to the back of his head.

"Boss, the doctor said Logan's in critical condition. He might not make it, so you really need to do something!" Augusta seized Alexander's arm with desperation.

Betty frowned, adding solemnly, "The main issue was that blow to his head.

"The doctor mentioned he's suffering from a severe brain hemorrhage. Even if we manage to save him, he might end up a vegetable."

Alexander waved them off, his face impassive. "I get it. Just give me a moment."

He stepped closer to the bed, channeling the Force Sense to probe Logan's body.

The air was thick with tension as everyone watched him intently in silence.

Moments later, a smile broke through Alexander's steely expression.

"No need to panic, I can heal him. It's serious, sure, but he's not going to die or end up a vegetable."

A sigh of relief spread through the group.

"Everyone except Augusta, Marvin, and Betty—get out. I need privacy to work. No interruptions," Alexander commanded, and his subordinates quickly exited the room.

Alexander's eyes sharpened with determination. He placed his hands on either side of Logan's head, summoning the Force deep within.

A stream of Force flowed into Logan's skull, dissolving the mass of bruised blood slowly but surely.

Before long, beads of sweat began to dot Alexander's forehead. The severity of Logan's injuries had drained the Force rapidly.

Twenty long minutes ticked by, and finally, Alexander withdrew his hands with a sense of relief.

Logan's suffering expression had vanished; he lay relaxed, drifting deep into slumber.

"The Fraternity is asking for death," Alexander muttered coldly.

His anger simmered just beneath the surface. Initially, he thought the magic powder would earn him respect from the power players in Loscity.

Instead, it seemed they viewed him as prey, first the Cripples and now the Fraternity. Alexander realized it was time to give a proper warning.

"Boss, the Fraternity is one of the four major gangs in Loscity, right behind the Cripples and the Blood Gang, and directly under the Kaylon. If we're going to hit back, we need a solid plan," Betty cautioned, clearly concerned about Alexander's impulsive actions.

With a cold chuckle, Alexander's once fiery gaze had now returned to its usual steely calm.

"Just a minor gang, the Fraternity, you can trust me—I don't take risks."

"This mess is my fault, and I've dragged you all into it. I'll be settling the score with the Fraternity and avenge Logan, don’t worry about it."

He scoffed at the notion of the so-called fourth largest gang in Loscity.

Guzman himself didn't intimidate Alexander, let alone these nobodies.

Augusta quickly stepped in, trying to stop him.

"Boss, I know you're tough, but you can't go after them alone. We're coming with you!"
