"Who do you think you are, kid? Sticking your nose where it doesn't belong? You're asking for trouble!"

A big guy yelled as he charged at Alexander, swinging a heavy punch at his face.

"Pathetic."

Alexander scoffed, kicking out without much effort.

The big guy might've swung first, but Alexander was faster.

His kick sent the man flying, and he landed right next to another thug, who was already groaning in pain, clutching his stomach.

The two remaining thugs were terrified, realizing they were way out of their depth.

One kick, and their buddy was down—this guy was definitely no ordinary guy.

Shing!

They both pulled knives from their belts, the blades glinting as they unsheathed them.

They waved them around, trying to look tough while threatening Alexander.

"Kid, you better back off if you know what's good for you. Get outta here, or you're not gonna live long!"

"Yeah, beat it while you still can! Otherwise, this knife's going in clean, coming out bloody!"

Alexander just smirked at the two who were shouting, and instead of backing off, he started walking toward them, step by step.

One step. Two steps. Three steps—getting closer and closer.

The tension was thick, pressing down on them like a weight.

The two thugs watched in horror as Alexander kept advancing, their faces paling and their hearts pounding.

"Stay back! Don't come any closer!"

One of them couldn't take the pressure any longer. He yelled and lunged forward, stabbing at Alexander's chest.

Alexander, with a look of disdain, grabbed his wrist and twisted it hard.

A loud crack echoed down the alley, followed by the thug's scream as his hand twisted into an unnatural shape, looking like a claw.

The last thug turned and ran for it.

Alexander couldn't be bothered to chase him. Instead, he kicked a knife lying on the ground.

"Wham!"

The knife flew through the air like an arrow, piercing the thug's chest.

He let out a scream, collapsing to the ground with a gaping wound, blood pouring out like a fountain.

In no time, Alexander had taken care of all four thugs. Only then did he turn his attention to the young woman.

She was attractive, but compared to the women Alexander had been around, she didn't quite measure up.

The woman's coat had already been ripped off by the thugs, and she was left in just a tight-fitting undershirt that had been pulled halfway up, her pale stomach exposed.

Her face was flushed with fear and embarrassment.

Alexander asked, "You okay?"

The woman stammered, "I... I'm alright. Thank you for saving me..."

Alexander asked her what had happened, and she explained.

It turned out this was her first time on Treasure Island, here for an auction. She'd had a bit too much to drink last night at a bar and ended up running into some trouble.

"When's this auction happening?"

Alexander asked, his interest piqued.

"It's today," she replied quickly. "I heard the Brilliant Group is looking for a pharmaceutical production line, and my factory recently went under. The production line is still top-notch, so I came here to try and auction it off."

Alexander nodded and then turned to leave.

"Wait! Please!"

The woman called out to him, her voice anxious. "Can I hire you as my bodyguard?"

"Bodyguard?"

Alexander chuckled, waving her off.

"Treasure Island's got strict laws. Just don't wander around alone, and you'll be fine."

As he spoke, the sound of hurried footsteps grew louder, getting closer.

A heavily armed patrol unit rushed over, guns drawn the moment they saw the men on the ground.

"Hands in the air!

"This is Treasure Island. You can't just kill people here like it's nothing."

Alexander looked at the patrol officers with interest.

They were all big, well-built, radiating a hardened aura—clearly battle-tested.

The captain, in particular, had something unusual about him—he was a metahuman.

Alexander's All-seeing Eyes could detect a strange energy circulating in his body.

"Hands in the air!"

The captain shouted at Alexander.

The woman beside him was terrified, trembling as she had no idea what to do.
