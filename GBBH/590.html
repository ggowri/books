"Damn you, bitch, you're dead meat..."

Clay still refused to back down.

"Bang! Bang!"

Two more shots rang out, shattering Clay's hands.

Nancy sneered, "I won't kill you, but I didn't say I wouldn't punish you. I'll take your hands and feet, leaving you just enough to breathe."

Clay was in so much pain he passed out.

What had started as annoyance for Alexander now turned into satisfaction; you can't show weakness to your enemies.

"Get him out of here!" Luther commanded.

Clay was dragged out like a dead dog by the bodyguards.

Richard felt a bit awkward as he turned to Alexander, "Sorry about earlier. I didn't expect Clay to be such a despicable guy."

The Reynolds family gathered around Alexander, chatting him up excitedly.

He carried himself with the grace of a true gentleman, a far cry from Clay.

Once the family learned that Alexander was the mastermind behind the magic powder, their excitement skyrocketed, praising Nancy's keen eye.

Suddenly, Alexander and Nancy became the center of attention, leaving Sophie feeling sidelined and a bit forlorn.

Clara looked on with envy.

"Oh, darling, if you could find a guy like Alexander, I'd be more than satisfied," Clara said.

Sophie rolled her eyes, "How about we both marry him?"

"You! What a silly girl, that's just shameless."

Clara reached to swat her.

"Mom, I was just joking. No need to get so worked up."

Sophie laughed, dodging, but her gaze lingered on Alexander with a glint of something more.

Two hours later, Alexander left the Reynolds mansion, where Nancy stayed to celebrate her grandfather's birthday.

As he arrived at the hotel, a man in a black robe, flanked by several others, surrounded him.

Then they wheeled out a chair and seated in it was none other than Clay, now incapacitated.

"Heh..."

Alexander smirked; he had suspected the City of Sorcery would have more men in Kingsdon than just Clay.

Clay was wrapped in bandages, stained with blood, and reeking of medication.

Alexander knew Clay wouldn't let it go after Nancy took him down, but he felt no fear—in fact, he welcomed their arrival.

The man in the black robe strode towards Alexander, thin and imposing, leaning on a dark cane. His veins bulged as he gripped it, fingers like claws.

A fierce aura radiated from him, his cold gaze capable of making even the bravest shiver.

Clay growled, "Mr. Williams, it's him, Alexander!"

Alexander smiled indifferently.

"Clay, you're a nobody now, and yet you still want to drag others into your mess. I don't even know how to respond."

Clay gritted his teeth, "Heh, don't think you're invincible. Mr. Williams is our contact in Kingsdon; he'll help me get revenge!

"I'm not only going to make you suffer; I want Nancy and her whole family gone. I'll hang you up and make Nancy kneel and lick my toes while you watch..."

Clay raved, his madness evident from the shock of his condition.

Alexander scoffed, "You don't know that I've already set a trap, do you?"

"What trap? Don't try to scare me, I'm not afraid of you."

Clay's face turned pale.

"Don't believe me? Let's test it!"

Alexander snapped his fingers.

"Ow! Ah... what... my head hurts..."

Clay suddenly cried out, twitching violently in his chair.

"What's happening? Mr. Williams... help me... it hurts!"

Everyone stood in shock; with just a snap of Alexander's fingers, Clay was writhing in agony—too bizarre to comprehend.

In truth, Alexander had left a trace of the Force within Clay when he dealt with him, ready to make his life a nightmare at any moment.

Patrick was taken aback, rushing to examine Clay.

He muttered incantations and pulled out several odd items, using five or six different spells before he could relieve Clay's pain, albeit slightly.

Clay was pale as a ghost, weakly pleading, "Mr. Williams, kill him! If you kill him, I swear I'll be fine."
