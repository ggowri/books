At the same moment, Alexander heard the gunfire.

Though he was still several miles away from the food factory, his sharp instincts immediately kicked in.

He slammed on the brakes. The car screeched to a halt, the tires burning against the road as a faint smell of burnt rubber filled the air.

Alexander sat still for a moment, channeling the Force to sharpen his senses.

Suddenly, his ears twitched. He caught the faint sound of engines, several cars speeding eastward.

Without wasting a second, he shifted into gear and sped off, taking a shortcut to cut them off.

Within minutes, he spotted three black SUVs racing down the road.

He floored the pedal, weaving through the shortcut to block their path.

He got out of the car, casually scooping up a handful of small stones from the roadside. He held them in his hand, weighing them carefully.

Each stone was coated with the Force. The moment they were launched, the force behind them would be as devastating as a sniper bullet.

Alexander smirked. "You think you can escape, Sakra Society? Not a chance."

The roar of the engines grew louder as the SUVs neared.

Alexander stood in the middle of the road his gaze laser-focused on the first SUV.

Inside, Tyler was jamming to loud rock music while slamming the pedal to the floor. The engine roared to life, pushing the vehicle forward at over 100 miles per hour like a dragon tearing through the dark.

Tyler had the window down, letting the cool night air rush in as he reveled in the speed.

But beneath the thrill, his frustration simmered. His time in Loscity had been nothing but a series of failures. The poison he'd slipped into Gavin's drink had seemed like a success at first, but in the end, it didn't work.

Then, he'd tried to get close to Xavier, hoping to control him, but that mission had fallen apart, too.

His final plan had been to ambush Ultimate Alliance's convoy and destroy it. But once again, things had gone terribly wrong.

Not only had they failed to harm Ultimate Alliance, but they had lost half their men. Now, he was down to just a handful of survivors.

For someone like Tyler, that was a huge blow to his pride.

"Damn it!" He slammed his fist on the steering wheel, cursing.

"Sir, are we really just leaving?" a big man sitting in the passenger seat asked, his voice laced with frustration.

Tyler glanced at him with a cold look and snorted, "What do you want to do? Stay here and wait to be caught? Don't underestimate Ultimate Alliance. If we stay at the factory, they'll track us down in no time. And we won't be able to get away."

The big man hesitated, stammering, "But we didn't complete the mission... What if Mr. White punishes us when we get back?"

"Don't worry. I'll take the blame. What we're really short on in Sakra Society is talent. He won't kill us," Tyler said with a dismissive sneer.

But it wasn't just the big man who was nervous. The others in the back seats and the two SUVs trailing behind were all on edge.

The drive continued smoothly, and slowly, Tyler began to feel a sense of relief.

With one hand on the wheel, he lit a cigarette with the other, taking a deep drag. The smoke filled his lungs, and he let out a satisfied exhale.

"There is someone ahead?" the big man suddenly shouted.

Tyler squinted ahead. There were headlights visible at the intersection.

"Stop freaking out. It's probably just a passing car," Tyler scoffed, taking another drag of his cigarette.

As they rounded the bend, they saw Alexander standing in the middle of the road, a calm smile on his face as he waved at them.

"Shit! It's Alexander! How did he get here?!" Tyler's face went pale, his heart skipping a beat. It was as if he'd been struck by lightning. In an instant, he flicked the cigarette out the window.

Before he could react, Alexander raised his hand.

With a sharp crack, the window shattered, and Tyler felt a searing pain in his chest. He couldn't hold onto the steering wheel anymore.

The car veered off the road, crashing down from several meters in the air before tumbling down a slope.

Tyler felt weightless as he was thrown around like a ragdoll, the vehicle spinning uncontrollably.

Alexander raised his hand again. A stone whizzed through the air, striking the second SUV's driver in the head. The vehicle swerved and crashed head-on into the third SUV.

Inside, screams erupted as people scrambled to escape, but they were met with a rain of stones, each one exploding their skulls with brutal precision.
