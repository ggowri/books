"Hey there! I'm Sophie Pepys. Wishing you both all the happiness!"

Sophie stepped up next, officially recognizing Alexander.

Gill wasn't exactly in her right mind, so everyone ignored her ramblings.

But Sophie was different; even though she was younger, she carried some weight.

"Thanks," Nancy replied, clearly surprised. Their relationship hadn't always been great.

Who would have thought her sister would openly support her today?

In truth, Sophie's change of heart was also about her own future.

If even the favored daughter had to make sacrifices, then she, the less favored second daughter, might end up marrying someone important for the family.

Every woman dreams of her Prince Charming and a happy marriage, so accepting family plans can be tough.

Plus, Sophie had always felt resentment toward her father, which made her stand firmly by Nancy today.

Alexander glanced at Sophie, then quickly looked away, showing no interest at all.

Sophie was taken aback; she didn't expect this handsome man to be so decent, not ogling her like other men.

Compared to that creepy Clay, Alexander's gaze was refreshingly clear.

Sophie felt a mix of emotions; such men were rare, and she suddenly envied Nancy even more.

Just then, Luther erupted, "Nancy, what are you bringing this guy home for? I only recognize Clay; I won't acknowledge this punk!

"Have I spoiled you too much? You've become so bold, causing a scene at Grandpa's birthday party. How could you do this to us?"

Nancy huffed back, "If anyone wants to marry Clay, go ahead; I'll support it with both hands."

Clay shook with anger, fists clenched tight.

Richard tried to calm him. "Don't worry, Clay. We won't acknowledge anyone but you."

Clay gritted his teeth, feeling utterly humiliated.

"Alexander, I want you dead!"

He truly wanted to lash out first, to strike hard.

But Clay held back; the more intense the moment, the more he needed to keep his cool.

No matter what, he had to secure Nancy first. Once he did, even the Kaylon would be his. A mere Alexander wouldn't pose a threat then.

Clay put on a sorrowful face, wiping his eyes as he said, "Grandpa Richard, I can't believe Nancy would say that. This guy must be threatening her; someone needs to grab him now.

"If you let this slide, how can I hold my head high? The reputation of the City of Sorcery is at stake! Mr. Rossetti will be furious, and the Reynolds family will face the wrath!"

Clay hid his malice behind a smile, making Richard's expression grow even darker.

"Pfft!"

Gill suddenly spat at Clay. "You think you can marry my Nancy? Dream on!"

"Shut up!"

Richard couldn't hold back his anger any longer.

Nancy stood her ground, shouting, "Grandpa, don't push me. You'd be breaking the law. If you insist, I might just resign as the master of the Kaylon and take you to court."

Richard was so furious he nearly stumbled, speechless.

"Are you two already together?" Luther couldn't help but ask.

Knowing his daughter well, he was aware that Nancy had never been keen on men and had never had a proper boyfriend.

But now, seeing Nancy and Alexander so close, it was clear their relationship had crossed into deeper territory.

Nancy felt her face flush, recalling that night in the bar, how she had leaned into Alexander's embrace, feeling electrified.

"Yeah, we're together now. I really love him," Nancy declared firmly, and everyone could tell she wasn't lying.

The Reynolds family began murmuring among themselves, exchanging glances.

"You! You're the eldest daughter of the Reynolds, the boss of the Kaylon! How can you act so casually?"

Luther pointed a shaking finger at his daughter, fuming with rage.

At that moment, someone suggested, "Since Nancy really doesn't want Clay, why not let Sophie marry him? She doesn't have a boyfriend yet."
