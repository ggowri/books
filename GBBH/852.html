"It's simple. Sell me all the iridium the Dragon Gang has collected, and I'll solve this problem for you right now," Alexander said casually, as if handling Benjamin was a piece of cake.

Norma was taken aback. Although iridium was valuable, and the Dragon Gang had a few hundred pounds of it, what did Alexander need so much for?

Alexander asked calmly, "So, what do you think? This shouldn't be too difficult, right?"

The others scoffed, their eyes on Alexander turning scornful and mocking, as if he were insane.

"Is this guy here just for attention? How can he deal with Benjamin?"

"Exactly, he's probably a fraud!"

"Iridium is expensive; he must have ulterior motives."

The room buzzed with murmurs; no one had faith in Alexander.

"Everyone, shut up!" Norma barked.

At her shout, the room fell silent.

"Mr. Hawthorne, if you can win back what we lost, not only will the money be yours, but I'll also agree to your condition," Norma said, her eyes carrying a hint of intrigue.

She looked at Alexander with significant intent.

"Good, you better keep your word."

Alexander said no more and left the surveillance room with Marvin, heading toward the VIP lounge.

At that moment, the three casino experts were already slinking away, preparing to leave.

As they passed the door, they cast skeptical glances at Alexander, clearly not trusting him.

Inside the luxurious lounge, only Benjamin and the female dealer remained.

Benjamin sat with his legs crossed, leisurely sizing up Alexander with a critical eye.

Click!

Alexander closed the lounge door.

Benjamin's eyes were filled with disdain. He had never seen this young man before and assumed he wasn't one of the top-ranked gambling experts.

He even turned away, not bothering to acknowledge him.

Alexander sat confidently across from Benjamin, a bright smile on his face, with a sharp gleam in his deep eyes.

A long silence followed as neither of them spoke.

"Heh..."

Benjamin sneered. "Are you here to play cards with me? If not, get out. I don't have time to waste on you.

"If Norma doesn't have the guts to send someone to play against me, she might as well shut this casino down!"

His tone was arrogant, treating Alexander as if he were insignificant.

As the gambling king ranked second globally, having only lost once in his life, he had the right to be cocky.

"Are you looking to die?" Marvin snapped, his fierce aura making Benjamin flinch.

Bang!

Benjamin slammed his hand on the table.

"What's this? Is that all the guts the Dragon Gang has? Are you planning to kill me? If so, consider your casino finished."

Alexander waved his hand, signaling Marvin to step aside.

"Benjamin, I think your gambling skills are mediocre. You swapped cards to win earlier. If you're brave enough, play against me. Let's see who's better," Alexander said, a sharp gleam crossing his eyes.

"Ah!"

Suddenly, Benjamin cried out and clutched his eyes, his face contorted in pain.

The surveillance room erupted into a commotion, with everyone unsure of what had just happened, speculating wildly.

"What's going on with Benjamin? Is he sick?"

"Does Mr. Hawthorne have some sort of special ability?"

Norma narrowed her eyes. She wasn't sure what Alexander had done, but she was certain it was connected to him.

"If you're too scared to bet, then get lost!" Alexander taunted, full of contempt.

"Damn it! You're just using some hypnosis ability, aren't you? Didn't expect you've used power elixir too," Benjamin snapped.

"You think a little hypnotic power can help you beat me? You're dreaming!" Benjamin shouted, though he avoided direct eye contact.

Alexander smiled and gestured. "Deal the cards!"

The female dealer snapped out of her daze and began dealing the cards.

The tension in the surveillance room was palpable. Everyone stood up instinctively, staring at the screen.

Over half of the wall-mounted LCD screens were displaying close-ups of Alexander and Benjamin.

They held their breath, still filled with doubt about Alexander.
