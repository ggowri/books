Alexander spent the entire night meticulously preparing the magic powder.

Ava came looking for him. She knocked on his door, but there was no answer. When he didn't come to find her either, Ava stormed off in a huff.

With the upcoming conference, tension gripped Loscity. Factions from all over had gathered here, each waiting for the right moment to make their move.

Betty spared no expense by renting out Loscity's convention center as the venue for the gathering.

The event hadn't even started, yet crowds had already gathered around the venue, and luxury cars filled the streets.

Two hours before the event began, the parking lot was packed with high-end cars like supercars, Rolls-Royces, and Bentleys, all signaling that the guests in attendance were far from ordinary.

When Alexander arrived in his old, battered Toyota, it drew scoffs and contemptuous glances.

In the midst of all the luxury vehicles, that beat-up Toyota looked wildly out of place.

Near the entrance, two groups were locked in a standoff.

On one side stood men from the Brilliant Group. Each of them was burly, clad in matching black suits and sunglasses, towering like sentinels.

Opposite them was a line of Hansen family men, just as large and intimidating in their matching attire, exuding a tense, aggressive energy.

In total, there were hundreds on both sides, and the atmosphere was charged with tension.

"What's going on? The conference hasn't even started, and the Brilliant Group is already at odds with the Hansen family?" someone murmured.

"Sebastian and Orion are both claiming exclusive distribution rights for the magic powder. They each say they have Mr. Gellar's authorization," another replied.

"This is going to be interesting. Is that even possible?" someone asked, intrigued.

"Where is Mr. Gellar anyway? This could turn into a fight. We should keep our distance," someone cautioned.

The crowd murmured excitedly, watching the spectacle unfold.

Orion snapped angrily, "Sebastian, what is the meaning of this?"

Sebastian calmly pulled out a contract.

"Orion, I signed a deal with Mr. Gellar and even paid a forty-million-dollar deposit. What about you? Do you have any proof?"

Orion froze. He had given Jackson a twenty-million-dollar deposit but hadn't signed a contract. Panic spread across his face.

Seeing Orion's reaction, Sebastian let out a triumphant laugh.

Then, his expression turned cold as he waved his hand.

"For anyone who dares obstruct the Brilliant Group's operations, we will kill them on the spot!"

Over a hundred men in black roared in unison. Their cries sent chills through everyone present.

The crowd knew well that the chairman of the Brilliant Group, Guzman, was a ruthless drug lord.

And behind Guzman was a Godfather known as "Reaper," a figure who had instilled terror in countless people. No one dared to cross him.

With the support of the magic powder creator, Sebastian's power was formidable, and now, as the exclusive distributor, the Brilliant Group's influence would only grow stronger.

The resounding roar of his men, like the terrifying howls of wild beasts, made Orion's courage falter.

"That damned Jackson...he dared to cheat me!"

Cursing, Orion finally backed down.

Without the exclusive rights to magic powder, the Hansen family had no way of standing up to the Brilliant Group.

With a smug grin, Sebastian announced, "Anyone interested in becoming a magic powder distributor, line up here and place your deposit."

Immediately, a long line formed before him. People pushed and jostled, desperate to get closer.

Someone questioned, "Mr. Tate, wasn't Betty in charge of selling the magic powder? When did the Brilliant Group take over as the exclusive distributor?"

Sebastian scoffed disdainfully.

"What do you know? Betty is just a sales rep under Mr. Gellar. Now that he's granted me the exclusive rights, the business is mine to handle."

This statement only deepened the crowd's belief. As the Brilliant Group's president, Sebastian wouldn't lie.

Everyone understood that even the smallest role as a magic powder distributor could yield massive profits.

The Cripples, the Blood Gang, the Kaylon, various powerful families, and even Orion hesitated. The prospect of joining the line was tempting, and they, too, were considering stepping forward.
