Felix ignored him, surprised that someone dared bring the fight directly to the Morris family.

Anyone bold enough to confront the Morris family directly was certainly not an ordinary man.

Felix followed Weston into the inner courtyard, occasionally noticing bodies sprawled out among the plants or on the pathways.

Just as Felix's worry deepened, the scene before him opened up to a large clearing.

In the vast courtyard stood a well-built, expressionless man in his middle age.

His skin was softer and smoother than most women's, gleaming like flawless ivory.

The man stood there casually, hands behind his back, yet he radiated a shocking, intimidating presence.

"Mr. Morris!"

Weston bowed his proud head in reverence.

This was the Morris family patriarch, Frederick Morris.

Frederick gave a brief huff, waving his hand, and Weston quickly stepped aside to await instructions.

Felix, usually full of confidence, now felt a growing unease, his heart racing. Frederick was nothing like he'd expected.

He'd assumed the patriarch would be a bloated, corpulent man.

But Frederick looked unassuming and radiated such an intense aura that even Felix, a level-three metahuman, felt intimidated.

Felix reined in his energy and obediently lowered his head.

His greatest asset was his sense of timing. He knew when to be flexible and adapt.

Facing someone as terrifyingly powerful as Frederick, Felix knew he'd need to absorb many more elite warriors to match him.

Though Felix behaved meekly, he still held himself with composed dignity.

Frederick narrowed his eyes, a little surprised. Felix's calm under his oppressive aura suggested he was no ordinary man, at least on par with Diego and Barry.

Felix's strength was indeed beyond Frederick's expectations.

The Red Umbrella Group's chairman was tougher than Frederick had anticipated.

Felix took the opportunity to apologize, "Mr. Morris, I don't know something significant happened in the Morris family. I'm sorry. Please forgive me."

"Heh..."

Frederick chuckled coldly, neither agreeing nor disagreeing.

Felix seemed unaffected by Frederick's disdain and instead asked warmly, "Mr. Morris, who are these people, daring to provoke the Morris family?"

Frederick gave Felix a long, hard look and slowly replied, "A bunch of clowns who won't be causing much trouble."

Felix smiled, curious about what Frederick had in mind.

After a pause, Frederick's expression grew solemn as he asked, "You said that Barry and Diego are both dead?"

Felix nodded. "Yes, killed by Alexander and the Kaylon working together."

Frederick's eyes flashed with a deadly glint.

"Felix, how dare you lie to me! If they're dead, how did you make it back alone? You must have been involved!"

Felix's body shook, his face showing shock.

"Mr. Morris, my hatred for Alexander runs deep. I'd love nothing more than for him to be dead. Why would I help him kill your family?

"Barry was set up in the casino and lost Joyce to the Kaylon. He and Diego tried a midnight rescue, only to fall into a trap.

"I was their getaway driver and barely escaped. If you don't believe me, send someone to check with the Kaylon."

Frederick remained silent, scrutinizing Felix's expressions and mulling over every word he'd said.

The silence was thick with tension, and Felix was ready to bolt at any moment.

Finally, Frederick let out a cold laugh.

"That Alexander dares to join the Kaylon to harm my family! Anyone who dares to oppose the Morris family is signing their death warrant!"

"Mighty as always, Mr. Morris!"

Felix lowered his head respectfully.

Frederick detected nothing suspicious about Felix. In fact, he seemed somewhat impressed by Felix's abilities.

"Since Alexander has managed to kill even Diego and Barry, it seems I'll have to handle this personally."

A sudden surge of murderous intent filled the air, making Felix's back go cold. His body tensed like a spring, ready to flee at the slightest indication of danger.
