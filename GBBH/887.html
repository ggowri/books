Marvin and Shadow remained on guard, ready to strike at any moment.

Aside from the tension brought by the ninja, there was also a middle-aged man in a gray suit and a young woman.

The man and woman had an aura just as intimidating as the ninja.

Both sides stopped at a moderate distance, staring each other down.

Even from several meters apart, Marvin and Shadow could feel the cold, merciless killing intent radiating from their opponents.

"Cyrus and Edmund are keeping one occupied outside. Let's take care of these two first," the man in the suit said with a sinister chuckle.

The man in the suit stepped forward slowly, his chilling words making Marvin and Shadow's eyes widen.

Before arriving, Alexander had shown them photos of the Mafia branch leader. It was him—Locke!

This man was incredibly powerful, no wonder the Godfather trusted him to establish a branch in Risun.

As for the woman, she was cloaked in a black robe, standing among the dense shadows of the trees, her figure almost completely obscured.

"Be careful!" Shadow warned Marvin.

After cautioning Marvin, Shadow ignored Locke and the woman, focusing intently on the ninja in black.

The two locked eyes and slowly moved closer.

Their movements were eerily similar, even their breathing matched in rhythm.

Both moved silently, blending seamlessly into their surroundings.

The ninja unsheathed two katanas, their gleaming blades cutting through the darkness in a chilling display.

Shadow held a dagger in his left hand and a compact submachine gun in his right, glaring at the ninja with pure hatred.

The ninja returned the glare, his eyes filled with a predatory intensity.

Locke showed no intention of attacking Marvin right away. Instead, he watched the fight between the ninja and Shadow with a smug smile.

Clearly, he believed victory was already in his grasp and had nothing to fear.

Marvin didn't let his guard down for a second, keeping an eye on Shadow while watching Locke and the strange woman out of the corner of his eye.

Shadow and the ninja were both extremely skilled assassins.

Their breathing, movements, and even the look in their eyes indicated they were cut from the same cloth.

These were unique techniques only top-tier assassins could master, allowing them to conceal their killing intent and blend into their surroundings completely.

Even experienced fighters would struggle to detect them.

"Bang, bang, bang!"

Shadow was the first to attack, his submachine gun spitting fire as he aimed at the ninja.

Even with just one gun, his controlled bursts created a web of bullets that seemed inescapable.

The ninja's front, back, and sides were completely covered by the gunfire.

But as soon as Shadow pulled the trigger, the ninja moved like lightning.

The spot where he had been standing was riddled with bullet holes, each spaced so evenly it looked meticulously planned.

"Bang, bang, bang!"

Shadow kept firing.

The bullets chased after the ninja until he slipped behind a large banyan tree, where Shadow finally ceased fire.

Two drops of blood stained the ground, proof that the ninja had been grazed by a bullet.

As skilled as he was, he couldn't completely evade Shadow's precise calculations.

"I know who you are—you're Shadow!"

Locke, who had been watching from the side, was stunned.

Only now did he realize Shadow's identity.

"Well, what a surprise. I was planning to take you out, but you came straight to me," Locke said with a smirk.

After his initial shock, Locke's expression shifted to one of eager anticipation.

"Should I step in and help Flame?" the woman asked.

Her voice was mechanical and ice-cold, sounding more like a machine than a living person.

"No need. Flame is the Mafia's best assassin. He and Shadow are archrivals—it's fitting for them to settle this once and for all," Locke replied with a grin.

The ninja in black seemed offended by her offer to help and shouted angrily,

"None of you interfere! Today, only one of us will walk away alive!"

As soon as he finished speaking, he vanished.

"Clang!"

Shadow swung his dagger behind him, blocking the attack with a piercing clash of metal.

The katana in the flames was only four inches away from the back of his head, but it was firmly blocked by the dagger.
