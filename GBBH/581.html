As soon as Nancy parked, several men in black rushed over.

They watched the car door intently, making sure it was Nancy stepping out.

"Boss!"

When they saw it was her, everyone bowed in unison.

They respectfully escorted Nancy and Alexander into the estate.

"Hey! Who's that guy next to the Boss? Is she dating him?" One of the bodyguards asked curiously.

"Looks like it. That guy must have hit the jackpot."

"I think the Boss is prettier than the Second Miss, but the Second Miss is a huge online star—she dances like a pro."

They chatted among themselves, secretly envying Alexander.

"Haha, isn't Clay Burch the one who's engaged to the Boss? And he's here. This is going to be interesting."

"Clay Burch thinks he's all that with the City of Sorcery backing him up. He never takes us seriously."

"Exactly. I hope someone teaches him a lesson. That guy looks like he could hold his own."

Though Alexander was walking away, he could still hear their conversation clearly.

The Ferrari they'd passed earlier was parked nearby, along with a Rolls Royce, a Bentley, and other luxury cars.

The most eye-catching was a bright red sports car.

Nancy spotted the red car and scoffed, saying, "That's Clay Burch's ride. He's here!"

Alexander smiled slightly. "Don't worry; this time, I won't let him off easy."

"Great!"

Nancy took a deep breath to calm her nerves.

"Just don't mess up. Be careful with what you say and follow my lead!"

"Got it, I'll follow your lead."

Nancy still felt uneasy, letting out a quiet sigh.

She hoped nothing would go wrong and that they could deal with Clay Burch smoothly.

The two walked to the entrance, and Nancy gave Alexander a knowing look, then playfully linked her arm through his, leaning against him.

Alexander wrapped his arm around her waist, and they made their way into the living room, looking quite cozy.

All the bodyguards and staff were stunned.

They couldn't believe their eyes—the usually aloof and proud Boss was openly cuddling with a man. It was unprecedented.

Alexander didn't care about their stares, taking in the grandeur of the massive estate.

The Kaylon had a rich history; this place had probably stood for over a century and cost a fortune to maintain.

The air was filled with a subtle fragrance that instantly cleared his mind.

Alexander recognized it as a rare spice known for its invigorating properties.

This spice was more valuable than gold, and to have that scent wafting through the estate required millions spent on sachets placed in discreet corners.

Plus, those sachets lost their fragrance after a while and needed to be replaced.

Every piece of furniture was made of precious wood, and with the craftsmanship involved, it was worth a pretty penny.

A mansion this size, just the furniture alone must be worth hundreds of millions.

"The Kaylon really knows how to flaunt their wealth," Alexander remarked casually.

Nancy smiled, her pride evident. She'd heard plenty of compliments before, but Alexander's made her feel accomplished.

"Now do you see how great I am?" she teased, unable to help herself.

Alexander chuckled and shook his head.

"Miss Nancy, you're back!"

An older man in a sharply tailored suit approached, his shoes gleaming. He beamed at Nancy.

Then his expression changed dramatically when he noticed Alexander.

"This is Marlon, the head steward who has worked here his whole life," Nancy introduced Marlon Blackwell to Alexander.

Marlon scanned Alexander from head to toe, quickly concluding that although he was handsome and had a certain charm, he came from a humble background. His clean attire simply couldn't compare to Nancy's.

Did this guy really think he could ride Nancy's coattails?

The elders had already decided that Clay Burch was to be Nancy's fiancé.

Marlon felt a surge of disdain for Alexander.
