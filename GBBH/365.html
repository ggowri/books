"Hahaha!"

Henry laughed smugly, quickly ducking behind the headless corpses.

These headless bodies weren't fully trained yet and were basically ordinary corpses, but they felt no fear or pain.

With dozens of headless corpses rushing at once, Henry was sure that even a skilled fighter would be torn apart.

Furthermore, the nails of these headless corpses were coated in corpse poison. One scratch would ensure infection.

But the next second, Henry's smile froze on his face.

Alexander moved like an agile monkey through the headless corpses, attacking repeatedly.

"Bang!"

Alexander kicked one of the corpses hard in the knee.

Although it felt no pain, its entire leg twisted and broke, causing it to collapse to the ground.

Dodging two more corpses, Alexander grabbed a steel bar from a hole in the wall, yanking it out with a fierce shout.

"Kill!"

Alexander swung the steel bar, channeling the Force into it, causing it to emit a faint white glow.

With a single swing, he easily sent two headless corpses flying.

He charged back and forth, breaking the bones and tendons of each corpse, leaving them crumpled at his feet.

The number of corpses between Henry and Alexander dwindled, and Henry began to back away in fear.

"Whoosh!"

Alexander suddenly threw the steel bar, which struck Henry squarely in the chest with force.

"Ahh!"

Accompanied by a horrific scream, Henry flew through the air like a ragdoll, spitting blood before landing heavily on the ground.

All the corpses in front of him had been taken down.

More corpses came rushing through the hole in the wall, pouncing toward Alexander.

"Bang!"

Another corpse was sent flying, slamming into the wall and making it tremble.

The corpse couldn't withstand such force and broke apart.

Alexander leaped into the air and stomped hard on two incoming corpses.

"Crack! Crack!"

A series of bone-cracking sounds echoed as the two corpses were flattened like trash under a compactor, collapsing to the ground.

Alexander moved quickly, taking down over a dozen more corpses in the blink of an eye.

"Out of my way!"

With a powerful roundhouse kick, he struck the last corpse, splitting it in two on the spot.

"Cough... cough..."

At this point, Henry lay on the ground, coughing up blood uncontrollably.

The steel bar had broken several of his ribs, piercing his lungs and making each breath excruciatingly painful.

"You bastard... I'll kill you..."

Henry muttered curses at Alexander, seething with anger.

He never imagined someone this powerful could exist, able to handle dozens of fearsome, unfeeling corpses.

Alexander ignored him, glancing coldly at the corpses scattered on the ground, his expression grim.

The headless corpses, despite their broken limbs, continued to twitch and struggle on the ground with a frightening persistence.

Cloe looked at the writhing severed limbs on the ground and felt nauseous, almost throwing up.

Alexander walked over to Henry, looking down at him from above.

In that instant, Henry felt an intense, chilling killing intent.

His chest throbbed with pain, making it impossible to breathe, and he couldn't even lift a finger—he was left to wait for death.

"Don't kill me... I can tell you the secrets of corpse-controlling magic... if I die, the necromancer's legacy dies with me, and that would be a great loss!"

Desperate to save his life, Henry had no choice but to beg.

"Corpse magic? Ha, I'm not interested!" Alexander sneered.

Alexander sneered, pressing his foot down on Henry's chest as a surge of Force exploded from him.

Henry's entire chest caved in, and he didn't even have time to scream before he died instantly.

Alexander searched Henry's body, finding a few ancient book pages in his inner pocket.

The pages contained fragmented records of corpse-controlling magic, along with some other necromancer spells.

"Hmph, harmful things like this shouldn't be kept around."

Alexander waved his hand, and white flames ignited on the pages, turning them to ash instantly.

With the Ultimate Legacy, he had no need for such dark magic, and he didn't want to become something neither human nor ghostly.

Cloe spoke up, concerned, "Boss, the Red Umbrella Group must know that Guzman's gone, or they wouldn't have hired Henry to attack our trading center."
