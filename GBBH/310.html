Schroder was suddenly overjoyed, arrogantly threatening. "The police are already here. If you lay a finger on me, you'll regret it!"

Alexander looked at him with disdain.

"Rosen already revealed that you're the mastermind behind the kidnapping. You've been caught red-handed. What else is there to say? Just wait for your military tribunal!"

Schroder refused to back down. "This is slander! Northern Command won't allow me to be defamed. You won't get away with this!"

"You really think Mr. Thompson will help you? Besides, do you think I'll let you escape easily? If I don't eliminate the root of the problem, it'll just come back to haunt me!"

With that, Alexander slapped Schroder on the forehead.

A surge of the Force rushed in, twisting like a knife in his head.

"Ah!"

Schroder collapsed to the ground, clutching his head and convulsing.

Alexander watched him expressionlessly.

"This is your fate—worse than death."

"Let’s go!"

Alexander ignored Jane and turned to leave with his men.

He didn't care what Jane and Andrew would tell the police. The priority was to help Quentin's grandson recover and gain his support.

"Alexander..."

Jane cried as she rushed after him.

He stopped, frowning at her.

"I'm sorry. It took me until today to realize how wrong I was. Please forgive me..."

Jane sobbed, apologizing to Alexander with all her heart.

He looked at his ex-wife in astonishment, surprised she had come to apologize. It seemed she had been bullied by Schroder and had woken up to reality.

"Alexander, for Ashley's sake, is there any chance we could go back to how things were?"

Jane asked with hopeful eyes.

Alexander sighed.

"It's too late. But you will always be Ashley's mother. I hope you can come when she gets out of the hospital."

With that, he quickened his pace and left.

Behind him, Jane's desperate cries echoed, but Alexander's heart had turned to stone. They couldn't go back anymore.

He could let go but couldn't forget the betrayal and humiliation Jane had inflicted upon him, like a shattered mirror with cracks that would never heal.

With tear-filled eyes, Jane watched Alexander walk away, feeling utterly defeated.

She knew she had lost this man forever...

Outside the estate, Alexander was surprised to find that not only the police had arrived, but also a group of fully armed soldiers.

Oliver was grinning as he tried to appease Quentin.

"Respected General Thompson, Schroder is fine. He just had a headache and was sent to the hospital."

Quentin let out a long sigh of relief.

"It's good that he's alive. Whether General Flores is guilty or not will be determined by a military tribunal. Everything hinges on the evidence."

As he said "evidence," he looked seriously at Alexander, his meaning clear.

Alexander replied confidently, "Mr. Thompson, I'm ready. We can head to Tijuanea to help your grandson anytime."

Quentin smiled and nodded with satisfaction.

"Very good. I knew Mr. Hawthorne was a great man. If Schroder really was behind the kidnapping, he must be severely punished!

"Make sure to investigate thoroughly. We need to get to the bottom of this." He commanded earnestly.

Oliver eagerly agreed, nodding knowingly.

Alexander merely smiled, noncommittal.

The Force could save lives but also take them. The Force tormenting Schroder would leave him worse than dead, and he wouldn't last much longer.

At that moment, Oliver leaned in and whispered, "Mr. Hawthorne, did you know that Schroder has close ties with the Red Umbrella Group?"

Alexander frowned. "Not really. What intel do you have?"

Oliver hurriedly leaned closer, saying, "Schroder came to Loscity pretending to convey orders from the military, mainly due to the Red Umbrella Group stirring the pot. They're also helping Orion become the head of the Hansen family."

"Although Orion is under house arrest for the murder of Mr. Ralph Hansen, we must be cautious of the Red Umbrella Group's power. Mr. Hawthorne, you have a good relationship with Miss Hansen, right? "

He flashed a knowing smile that men understood, clearly envious of Alexander's luck with women.
