Jane felt as if a cold snake was crawling on her, sending chills down her spine.

"You won't get away with this! Even if you avoid the military tribunal, Alexander will kill you one day!"

Jane cursed, struggling desperately, but her struggles were futile.

"Are you really trying to scare me with Alexander? Haven't you two already divorced?"

Schroder was furious.

"I've changed my mind. Everyone, go!"

With his command, the others were instantly excited.

Jane felt faint at the thought of being humiliated like an animal in a zoo.

She thought of Alexander and deeply regretted everything that had led to this moment.

If she hadn't forced him into a divorce, would she be in this situation now?

Jane felt the pain in her heart.

"General, something happened! Otis... They are...!"

Suddenly, someone rushed in, flustered and unable to articulate.

Schroder sensed something was wrong and snapped. "What's happening? What about Otis?"

"He said he found Alexander and led a team to confront him, but... they were wiped out! Alexander has broken through!"

"What! Alexander is here? Impossible..."

Schroder was stunned.

Bang!

A sudden explosion echoed from outside, reverberating into the basement.

From the surveillance footage, they could see the heavy villa door burst from its frame and crashed to the ground.

As dust rose in the chaos, Alexander charged in, followed by Augusta and Marvin, along with a dozen loyal subordinates.

Bang!

Gunfire erupted. Alexander squinted, instinctively dodging as a bullet grazed his cheek, leaving a deep dent in the floor.

For a seasoned Warrior, avoiding bullets was second nature.

It was only when he underestimated his opponent that he was caught off guard and might be shot by a sniper.

Turning to the sniper on the rooftop, he flicked his wrist.

Whoosh!

A gleaming dagger sliced through the air, piercing the sniper's chest and sending him flying.

The alarms blared loudly in the villa, but Schroder's team had been reduced to just one group, with half of them still in the basement with him.

As Alexander stormed in like a force of nature, panic erupted among them. Some were taken down, while others threw up their hands.

The basement was filled with fear, and some even contemplated fleeing.

Schroeder roared. "Calm down, you idiots! They won't be able to leave alive!"

His subordinates steadied slightly, forgetting about Jane as they quickly drew their weapons and formed a combat line.

With a cold smirk, Schroder continued, "That guy thinks he can show off just because he's skilled. They think we don't have any experts on our side? If you don't act soon, I won't be able to ally with the Red Umbrella Group!"

The others looked around in surprise, wondering if there were indeed hidden Warriors from the Red Umbrella Group nearby.

But aside from their own, not a single shadow could be seen.

Suddenly, several figures dropped from the ceiling, startling everyone.

They were a group of Soutaxia men dressed in tight combat gear. Their bronzed skin was taut over powerful muscles.

They cast a deep glance at Schroder and then blocked the entrance to the basement.

Schroder excitedly shouted, "Everyone, don't be afraid! These are the Shadow Warriors from the Red Umbrella Group, all enhanced by power elixir!"

His subordinates were instantly thrilled. They had heard of this power elixir, and the name "Shadow Warriors" sounded impressive.

At that moment, Alexander arrived at the basement entrance.

Without a sound, two Shadow Warriors charged toward him like phantoms.

They brandished two gleaming Kukri daggers, their blades sharp and glinting ominously, capable of severing a bull's head with a single strike.

Their speed was astonishing, and the force of their attack was overwhelming.

For the first time, Alexander chose to retreat rather than engage head-on.
