"Oh? What's so special about it?" Alexander asked, his curiosity piqued.

The clerk was happy to spill everything he knew. "Man, you can trade them for all sorts of stuff. Hit up the Treasure Hall for weapons and armor, swap them for martial arts manuals in the Manual House, or even get yourself some sweet spiritual mounts from the Beast Lodge. If you're just after cash, the Finance Office will give you twenty coins per point..."

"How do I earn these points?" Alexander asked.

"Oh, there's tons of ways," the clerk replied. "Breaking through to new martial arts levels, helping people out like a hero, defending the nation, conquering the Thirty-Three Stage, ranking high in tournaments... Heck, you can even straight-up buy them with enough money or treasures."

As they walked through the Martial Virtue Hall, the clerk pointed out a massive stone tablet that stretched several stories high along one wall.

"See that? That's the Martial Arts Ranking in Outer Court," he explained, gesturing enthusiastically. "Lists the top hundred disciples by power level. The higher up your name is, the more powerful you are!"

Alexander figured it was getting late anyway. The welcome goodies could wait until another day—no rush there.

Right now, he needed to head back to Emerald Manor before Logan started worrying.

He gave the clerk a quick, respectful bow and made his way home.

Meanwhile, at the examination hall in the Dominion Church's Outer Court, things were getting interesting.

A young man showed up with an elderly attendant in fancy clothes. The young man was wearing this expensive silk robe, but he looked rough—pale as a ghost, with beady eyes, droopy eyebrows, and a flat nose. It was Julius Wetherall, the precious young sir of the Marshal's Mansion.

"Finn, hand over our golden pass," Julius ordered his well-dressed attendant.

"Right away, sir."

Finn immediately pulled out a palm-sized golden token from his robes and presented it to Declan.

Declan checked it out. The front had this incredibly detailed engraving of soldiers marching to battle, thousands of troops looking all majestic and stuff. On the back, there was just one character carved deep into the metal—Exempt.

After looking it over, Declan passed it to Huxley and Fermin.

Declan and Huxley shared a knowing look, then glanced at the sickly-looking rich young man. Both of them let out heavy sighs before standing up and heading for the door without another word.

Fermin let out a heavy sigh. "Man, the things parents do for their kids..."

The whole mess started when Julius nearly got his head blown off, followed by that humiliating beating from Jeffrey, the governor of Darwin Court.

Sure, his family had enough medicines to heal him up in a day, but the incident left some serious emotional scars.

Something about that experience must have flipped a switch in him. Now he was suddenly all about proving himself and bringing honor to the family name.

When the Dominion Church's Outer Court announced they were recruiting lay disciples, Julius jumped at the chance to join the selection process.

After much deliberation, Madden went all in, pulling out the world's only golden pass to secure his son a spot in the Dominion Church's Outer Court.

Leading the pack were the three supervisors—Nathan, Logan, and Jalen—with a swarm of workers buzzing behind them.

Logan rushed forward and wrapped Alexander in a bear hug. "Dude, you're back! Word is you crushed that selection process!"
