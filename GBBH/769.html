Alexander moved with blinding speed, leaving the man no time to dodge. All he could do was let out a furious roar and brace himself, hoping to withstand the blow.

But this punch, though only half of Alexander's strength, was devastating.

The Force surged through Alexander's fist like an unstoppable drill, shattering the man's entire arm and ripping apart half his torso.

Flesh and bone fragments scattered, painting the walls with a gruesome spray.

The man in black didn't even have a chance to summon his witchcraft; he was reduced to a mutilated corpse before he could cast a spell.

The onlookers froze, wide-eyed with disbelief. Alexander's two strikes had happened in an instant—too quickly for anyone to react.

With a smug grin, Alexander glanced at the mangled body. "There aren't many left who can survive one punch from me. You took two. That's not bad."

The room fell silent.

The thug with the shaved head took an involuntary step back, his face pale.

"Everyone, get him! Kill him!"

The thug gave the order viciously, and everyone launched a fierce attack on Alexander.

A barrage of gunfire erupted as bullets swarmed toward Alexander like angry hornets.

"Watch out!"

Alyssa gasped, instinctively dropping to the floor and rolling to take cover.

Alexander, however, didn't flinch. Alyssa was behind him, and he had no intention of letting a single bullet reach her.

Sizzle!

With a swift motion, he ignited his Lightsaber, a brilliant beam of light bursting into existence.

As he slashed it through the air, a radiant shield formed, blocking the entire hallway.

Bullets shattered against the light, falling harmlessly to the ground in pieces.

To Alexander, thanks to his All-seeing Eyes, even bullets appeared sluggish, hardly faster than snails.

"What is this guy? A monster?"

The thug leader stammered, retreating in terror.

Alexander's gaze sharpened. "Trying to run now? You think you can get away?"

With a single step, Alexander launched himself forward like a shot from a crossbow, closing the gap in a blink.

The man closest to him didn't even have a chance to react as Alexander's Lightsaber cut through him and his rifle in one clean stroke.

Woosh!

The corridor echoed with the sharp, explosive sound of the Lightsaber slicing through the air.

Several gunmen were sliced in half, blood and limbs scattering across the floor.

"He's a demon! A demon!"

One of the men screamed, trying to flee, while others fired at Alexander in a panic.

But their bullets were useless, every one deflected by Alexander's impenetrable defense.

The more they resisted, the faster they fell, mercilessly cut down into pieces.

Alexander tore through them like a tiger in a flock of sheep, leaving devastation in his wake.

Within moments, the hallway had transformed into a nightmarish scene, littered with dismembered bodies.

Blood coated the floor and walls, making every step slick and treacherous.

The bald leader stumbled as he tried to escape, tripping over a severed head and crashing face-first into a pile of remains.

The arrogance he'd shown before was now replaced by sheer terror and desperation.

Alexander approached him leisurely, holding his Lightsaber close.

The heat alone scorched the man's eyebrows and singed his hair.

"Please... don't kill me," he sobbed, tears streaming down his face. "It was the Godfather—Brandon Hawkins! He forced me into this! I swear it!"

He broke down completely, pleading, "I have a family—a wife, kids, and an old mother. Please, have mercy..."

Alexander's face remained emotionless, untouched by the man's cries. There wasn't an ounce of sympathy in his eyes.

Somewhere along the line, he had become this unyielding, ruthless force.

A living enemy would one day take his revenge.

Mercy to one's enemy, after all, was the ultimate cruelty to oneself.

"I'm sorry," he said quietly, "but I have people to protect, too."

And with that, Alexander swung his Lightsaber.

The thug fell silent, frozen as a thin line of blood appeared at his throat.

A moment later, his head tumbled to the ground.

Alyssa stood there, wondering if she was dreaming.

The narrow corridor was strewn with bodies, blood soaking the floor. Within mere minutes, Alexander had wiped out the entire group, leaving nothing but death in his path.

The stench of blood was overwhelming, the evidence all too real that this nightmare was no dream.
