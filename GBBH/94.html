Sebastian shuddered in fear, thinking, "If Alexander dies, who will lift the curse off me?

Guzman mentioned bringing in a black-robed sorcerer, but I'm not taking that chance."

"Alexander, are you deaf? Apologize now, or I'll order the men to shoot!"

Andrew's voice was brimming with arrogance.

Sebastian, enraged, wanted to kill Andrew on the spot. He shouted, "Damn you! Are you trying to get me killed?"

Andrew, immediately sensing the danger, turned to Alexander like a whipped dog.

"Mr. Sebastian Tate is asking you—are you trying to kill Mr. Sebastian Tate?"

"Fuck you, Andrew! I'm talking about you!" Sebastian roared, his anger boiling over.

With his last ounce of strength, he kicked Andrew hard in the groin.

"Ah!" Andrew shrieked in agony, clutching his groin.

Jane and the others stood in shock, unable to comprehend why Sebastian had attacked Andrew.

"Mr. Sebastian Tate, what are you doing? Why are you hitting me?" Andrew groaned, barely able to speak.

"Why? Because you dared to point a gun at Mr. Hawthorne. Not only do I want to hit you, I want to see you dead!" Sebastian's voice was laced with fury.

The Fairfax family members were stunned into silence, feeling as though they were trapped in a nightmare.

They thought, "How can Sebastian, who has watched Theodore's hands being ruined, still be aiding Alexander?

It's beyond comprehension."

In the midst of their bewilderment, Sebastian bowed deeply to Alexander, his tone now respectful and pleading.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I'm willing to tell you Guzman's whereabouts, but he's very cautious, hardly staying in one place for more than half a day. So, if you could ease my suffering first, I'll cooperate with you."

Sebastian looked at Alexander with hopeful eyes.

Alexander studied him for a moment before smiling, "I didn't expect you to be so cooperative. I thought you'd hold out for at least a couple of days. But how do I know the information you give me is genuine?"

The place fell into eerie silence, broken only by Sebastian's desperate pleas.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I swear I won't lie to you. Please, please spare me... I apologize on behalf of my son. We were wrong, we were wrong..."

His face was streaked with tears, his sincerity palpable.

Alexander, however, turned to Andrew and the others, a cold smile on his face. "They say you have a bounty on me. Is that true?"

Sebastian's anger reached a boiling point.

He pointed at Andrew, seething.

"Grab him, break his legs, and make him apologize to Mr. Hawthorne."

Andrew, writhing in pain, begged Alexander, "Alexander, I'm sorry, I'm sorry... For the sake of being your ex-wife's father, please don't let them break my legs... Please..."

Alexander remained indifferent. "That wasn't your attitude a moment ago, was it? You were just aiming a gun at me."

Sebastian snapped his fingers sharply. "What are you all standing around for? Drag him aside and teach this worthless scum a lesson. Break his legs!"

Several men moved quickly, pinning Andrew down and beating him mercilessly, making him writhe and cry out on the ground.

Jane and the rest of the Fairfax family, terrified, turned and fled.

Andrew, bloodied and battered, lay helpless on the ground, no longer able to resist.

Just as someone was about to bring out a baseball bat to break his legs, Alexander finally spoke up, "Enough. Let him go."

The Brilliant Group members stood frozen, unsure whether to follow Alexander's command.

Sebastian, enraged, shouted, "Are you deaf? Did you not hear Mr. Hawthorne's orders? Get him out of here and throw him in the trash!"

Andrew was quickly dragged away and unceremoniously tossed into a dumpster.

Alexander gazed at Andrew's predicament with a deep sense of ruefulness. "This is what you call reaping what you sow," Alexander murmured.

Sebastian, desperate and pleading, implored, "Mr. Hawthorne, I'll do whatever you ask, just please, let me off the hook."

Alexander, with a flat, almost indifferent tone, asked, "I've crippled your son's hands. Aren't you going to seek revenge on me?"

Sebastian shook his head vigorously. "No, no! That was his own fault for crossing Mr. Hawthorne. He brought this upon himself."

Alexander continued, "You really will give me Guzman's whereabouts?"

Sebastian nodded eagerly, like a chicken pecking at grain.

"I'm a man of my word. Honestly, I've never liked him anyway. I'll cooperate one hundred percent."

Alexander grinned, his sharp gaze fixed on Sebastian. "I think you're just stalling, trying to get someone else to handle me, aren't you?"
