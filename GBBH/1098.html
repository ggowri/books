"Impossible! This is fake! It's all fake, you liars! You're all liars!"

Silas screamed at the top of his lungs, panic evident in his expression.

He knew Atlas was a well-known lawyer in Newbrook, someone who wouldn't make a move unless he had full confidence in his position.

Atlas shoved the copy of the agreement into Silas' hand with an icy glare.

"You better take a good look. We're taking over Silver Shield Security now."

Silas' head spun. His hands trembled as he glanced at the document, and the realization hit him like a freight train. His face contorted with disbelief and panic, his eyes widening in shock.

"Liars! You're all liars! Don't think you can fool me!"

With a growl, Silas tore the document into shreds.

"You think you can just take over Silver Shield? I'll kill you!"

Like a madman, he lunged at Alexander, claws outstretched.

"Get lost!"

Alexander casually kicked Silas away, sending him flying across the floor.

"You liars... you'll never get Silver Shield Security!" Silas spat blood, struggling to get up but failing miserably.

"We don't need to waste time on this idiot. Let's go."

Alexander dismissed Silas with a shrug, stepping over him and leading the way into the building.

"Spit!"

As they passed by Silas, Alvin spat on him in contempt, and Silas nearly fainted from rage and humiliation.

"Good deeds bring good rewards, bad deeds bring bad ones," Emma muttered, her eyes filled with disdain as she gave Silas one last glare.

He lay there like a deflated balloon, unmoving.

Maxine, her face expressionless, ignored Silas entirely. Her gaze, however, was fixed intently on Alexander's retreating back.

The previous night, she had mustered the courage to knock on his door, only to be shut out. That rejection had left her with a lingering bitterness.

With Atlas' help, Alexander quickly took control of Silver Shield Security, assuming the role of chairman with full control.

Alvin was appointed as CEO.

Silas, upon hearing the news, felt his heart drop. Blood sprayed from his mouth in a violent gush.

He refused to accept it. Silver Shield Security had been the pride of the Nicholson family, and now it was being stolen by outsiders.

"Liars! Get out here, all of you!" Silas screamed, attempting to storm into the building, but was stopped by a group of security guards.

"Stop! What do you think you're doing? You're no longer vice president. The chairman has ordered that no one from the Nicholson family is allowed inside."

The guards, showing no mercy, shoved Silas back.

In the past, when the Nicholson family had been at the peak of their power, they treated the guards like mere servants—calling on them as if they were nothing more than lapdogs.

Now, however, those same guards had no respect left for them.

"You bastard! Let me in, or I'll fire you all... you damn dogs! You're nothing but dogs!"

"Get out!"

The head of security shouted, shoving Silas harshly to the ground.

"What... how dare you? You're just a security guard! How dare you lay a hand on me?" Silas stammered in disbelief, and his pride was shattered.

"Ha! You think you're still vice president? Your family's done for. You're lower than a dog now, so get lost. If you don't, we'll throw you out ourselves."

The head of security's words were sharp, dripping with scorn.

Silas, now livid, felt his blood boil. The humiliation was too much.

In his fury, everything went black, and he collapsed unconscious on the spot.

Meanwhile, Alvin and Alexander entered the research and development lab.

Everywhere they walked, employees of Silver Shield Security showed deep respect, bowing their heads and stepping aside.

"Silver Shield Security mainly manufactures and sells surveillance cameras, alarm systems, night-vision equipment, protective helmets, tactical gear, bulletproof vests, long-range communication devices, and stun guns," Alvin proudly explained as he led Alexander through the lab. "All of our products have passed military-grade tests. Our clients range from the military to civilian sectors, and our quality is unmatched."

Alexander's heart leaped.

This was exactly the kind of acquisition he had been looking for.

Earlier, he had stockpiled weapons, ammunition, and military-grade equipment worth hundreds of billions of dollars on Treasure Island and Devil Island.

However, without the ability to maintain and produce more, it was a hollow investment.

He needed the production capacity to sustain his strength in the long term, especially if the world was heading toward disaster.

Alexander's eyes gleamed as he turned to Alvin.

"I'd like to see a sample of your All-purpose Bullet-resistant Jacket. I might have a few suggestions for improvements."

Alvin paused, taken aback. Was this mysterious Alexander not only skilled in combat and medicine, but also an expert in developing security products?
