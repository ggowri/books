Augusta's blade moved with deadly speed, slicing through the air as it severed two heads in one swift motion.

Gunfire erupted from the enemy, fierce and indiscriminate, as the Tiger Strike Squad unleashed a torrent of bullets without a care for their own comrades.

The hail of gunfire enveloped Augusta, who hadn't anticipated such a rapid and ruthless response.

The sheer brutality of their assault left him on the brink of disaster.

In the blink of an eye, Alexander seized Augusta, lifting him off the ground like an eagle soaring into the sky.

Bullets whizzed beneath them, finding their mark on several members of the Morris family.

With a graceful descent, Alexander landed unscathed.

With a flick of his wrist, shards of glass, shimmering with a faint white light, shot out like flying daggers, piercing through multiple bodies of the Morris family.

He had crushed a cup, infusing it with the Force.

The sudden onslaught, combined with the earlier barrage of gunfire, decimated half of the Morris family members present.

Inside the tent, chaos reigned, screams echoing as bodies crumpled to the ground, blood pooling like dark flowers blooming on the earth.

Faced with Alexander, the Tiger Strike Squad was like mice cornered by a cat—utterly helpless, with no chance of escape.

In a matter of moments, over twenty members of the Morris family lay lifeless.

Augusta stood frozen in shock, words escaping him.

He had spent recent weeks on Treasure Island, convinced he had grown stronger, thinking he was closing the gap between himself and his boss.

But now, it was painfully clear—he was falling further behind Alexander.

Alexander picked up a backpack from the ground, revealing two fully loaded submachine guns inside.

After a quick inspection, he tossed it to Augusta.

He commanded, "Let's move. Anyone who dares to attack, take them down!"

"Yes, Boss!"

Augusta nodded vigorously.

Just as they stepped out of the tent, two women carrying two plates of roasted beef approached, heads bowed and oblivious to the carnage that had just unfolded.

They moved closer, seemingly unaware of the danger.

But as one man brushed against the edge of their clothing, his expression shifted in an instant. Clutching his throat, he fell to the ground, frothing at the mouth, his skin turning a horrific shade of black in mere seconds.

What a deadly poison!

Alexander's gaze locked onto the two women with a cold smirk.

As they closed the distance—about twenty-five feet away—they dropped the pretense and shouted, "Kill!"

Two dark shadows sprang from the two plates of beef, arrows flying toward Alexander with lightning speed.

Hidden within the two plates of roasted beef were crossbows, their tips coated with a lethal toxin designed to silence any who bled.

After releasing the bolts, the women surged forward, relentless as hunting lions, their intent clear.

The chilling aura of death loomed over them, sending a shiver down Augusta's spine, causing him to break into a cold sweat.

Though these women weren't metahumans and lacked any supernatural powers, their assassination techniques were honed to perfection.

Even a level-three metahuman would struggle against such an onslaught.

Augusta felt trapped, with no way to evade the incoming threat.

In an instant, Alexander activated Force Confinement, bending time and space around him.

Everything slowed as if the world had been thrown into slow motion.

He casually stepped back.

The sound of air being sliced apart echoed as an arrow zipped past him, missing its mark.

One of the women, moving without a sound, accelerated again, her violet-tipped nails glinting like blades as they aimed for Alexander's throat.

Yet to him, their movements appeared as if they were playing out in slow motion.

With a slight shift of his body, Alexander evaded the attack effortlessly.

"Damn it!"

Frustration surged in the women as they unleashed a flurry of strikes, their four hands moving like a whirlwind, intent on tearing Alexander apart.

Their speed was astonishing, pushing the limits of human perception.

Even Augusta, who possessed level-two speed power, felt a surge of fear; these martial artists were a force to be reckoned with.
