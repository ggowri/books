The Shadow Warriors were desperate. Facing Alexander Hawthorne's strength, they had no choice but to fight with everything they had.

The spiked hammer swung by the bald man moved at lightning speed, leaving a blurred trail in the air, menacing as ever!

To Alexander, however, it was nothing.

"You're doomed!"

With a roar, Alexander unleashed the Force, a thunderous sound reverberating in the air.

The bald man shuddered, his speed faltering immediately.

Taking advantage of the moment, Alexander struck, grabbing the bald man's wrist and twisting it violently.

Crack!

The bald man's right hand twisted into a terrible shape, and the hammer fell heavily.

Before it could hit the floor, Alexander seized it, bringing it down hard onto the bald man's forehead.

Crack!

The bald man's skull caved in, collapsing like a lifeless snake onto the ground.

Alexander's speed with the hammer was unfathomable. The other Shadow Warriors could barely register his movements.

"Ah!"

One of the Shadow Warriors screamed, clutching his bloody face. His nose had met the spiked hammer and was now ruined.

Another shriek followed, accompanied by the sickening sound of shattered kneecaps.

Two Shadow Warriors lay on the ground, writhing in agony, their legs obliterated.

Only one Shadow Warrior remained, trembling with a heavy iron chain in hand, standing frozen in fear.

Alexander smirked and advanced like a lightning bolt, kicking the Shadow Warrior into the air.

Blood spurted from every orifice as his chest caved in, bones shattered to pieces.

The basement fell silent.

Both Schroder's men and the remaining Shadow Warriors were like straw before Alexander was effortlessly dispatched.

In the end, only Schroder cowered in the corner, shivering.

Though he had faced the horrors of the battlefield, his fighting spirit was completely crushed by Alexander's terrifying power.

As Alexander approached, Schroder was paralyzed with fear.

Not only was Alexander glaring coldly at him, but Augusta and the others also looked at him with the utmost disdain.

"What a worthless general!"

Marvin spat contemptuously at Schroder.

"You insulted me? I'll kill you!"

Unable to withstand the pressure, Schroder frantically pulled out a gun to shoot Alexander.

Snap!

The sound of breaking bones echoed as Augusta disarmed him, snapping his hand with the gun.

Schroder screamed in agony, his face pale as a ghost.

At that moment, Andrew, who had just been rescued, shouted desperately. "Stop! Alexander, what do you think you're doing? You dare lay a hand on the general? Let him go!"

As he scolded Alexander, he turned to plead with Schroder.

"General, Alexander isn't one of us. You can punish him, but please don't cause trouble for the Fairfax family."

"Jane likes you a lot. Please let us go, and I assure you that Jane will serve you wholeheartedly."

Everyone was left speechless. The old fool was not only incredibly foolish but also utterly shameless.

Jane intervened. "Dad, shut up and stop embarrassing us."

Andrew shot her a furious glare. "What do you know? Alexander is disrespecting the general. This is a huge offense!"

Alexander could only shake his head, finding it best to avoid such shameless and profoundly stupid people.

Augusta and the others were equally dumbfounded, having never seen such a ridiculous old man.

Alexander waved his hand. "If you won't appreciate it, then into the cell you go."

Marvin, unable to contain himself any longer, grabbed Andrew by the throat, shoving him and Belinda into the prison.

At this moment, Augusta whispered to Alexander. "Boss, how should we handle Schroder? If we don't deal with him properly, it could lead to big trouble."

Upon hearing this, Schroder burst into arrogant laughter. "You're too afraid to lay a finger on me! Smart ones will let me go, or if I raise a fuss, you'll all be in deep trouble."

"If you don't get rid of the root, the trouble will just sprout up again!"

Alexander waved his hand, turning to leave with determined strides.

Schroder trembled in fear. "What do you mean? Do you dare to kill me in public?"

At that moment, distant sirens began to wail, growing closer.
