John spat out another mouthful of blood, collapsing onto the ground, gasping for air.

He had taken too many hits. It seemed unlikely he would survive. Blood gushed from his mouth continuously.

"Transfer all your savings to this account," Alexander commanded John.

As a key executive of the Brilliant Group, he must have amassed a considerable personal fortune.

John's facial muscles twitched, his expression one of intense struggle. Money had always been his lifeblood. He was reluctant to part with it.

"John, hurry up! Once the transfer is done, you can leave. Guzman will generously reward you, offering you Sebastian's position, making you the CEO of the Brilliant Group..."

Alexander's voice, modulated by the Force, carried a peculiar frequency and was filled with allure.

"Come on! Transfer the funds, and you'll soon be the CEO of the Brilliant Group, wielding immense power!"

John became agitated and took out his phone to transfer.

His body trembled uncontrollably as he muttered to himself in excitement, "I'm going to be the CEO. I'm going to be the CEO..."

He entered the password, and the transfer was successful!

Alexander's phone immediately vibrated with an SMS notification of the incoming billions of dollars. He couldn't help but chuckle, thinking, "John is indeed a moneybag."

He had previously spent a billion at the trading center to purchase potential potions. Now, he had not only recouped that amount from John but had also made an additional few hundred million dollars.

At this point, John was motionless on the ground, silent.

Alexander checked the time. It was nearing two in the morning.

With a casual flick of his hand, pale flames engulfed John's body. Soon, under the influence of the Force, he turned to ashes.

The Neptune was quietly docked at the pier.

This colossal cruise ship could accommodate over three thousand people, with rooms more luxurious than those in a five-star hotel.

"Stop right there! Who are you?"

"Who said you could come here? This is not a place for the likes of you."

Several guards intercepted Alexander as he approached the cruise ship.

They surrounded him aggressively, ready to act at the slightest wrong word.

Alexander smiled amiably, "Thank you for your hard work. Mr. Bryant sent me to express his regards."

The guards were momentarily stunned. They were not ordinary members but Guzman's personal bodyguards.

They thought skeptically, "Could this guy really be sent by Mr. Bryant to console us? Otherwise, how would he know Mr. Bryant is here?"

As they hesitated, Alexander struck.

The leading man didn't have time to react before Alexander snapped his neck. His head twisted completely around, his face filled with horror, now facing his back.

"Ah! Captain!"

"Captain!"

The guards, their faces pale with fear, drew their guns to shoot at Alexander.

"Sleep now!"

Alexander slapped their chests swiftly.

They fell to the ground, screaming, blood spurting from their mouths.

That single palm strike had not only broken their breastbones and ribs but also sent the ribs piercing through their lungs. They couldn't move now even if they wanted to.

Alexander tossed them all into the sea to feed the fish and then silently infiltrated the cruise ship.

A few minutes later, he stood in front of a door made of reinforced steel.

Two guards with blank expressions stood by the door, already under the influence of Alexander's Force Control.

In their eyes, Alexander was as unassailable as their chairman, Guzman.

"Open the door!"

At Alexander's command, one of the guards quickly entered the door's opening password on the control panel and pressed his fingerprint.

The thick security door slowly opened.

After knocking out the two guards, Alexander slipped in like a cat, silent and stealthy.

He had managed to avoid the patrolling guards either by his agility or by influencing their minds with Force Control, stealthily making his way here.

Alexander felt a surge of excitement, sensing he was getting closer to Guzman.

The key was not to alert Guzman. If Guzman escaped this time, Alexander wasn't sure he could find him again.

Beyond the door was a deep corridor, the floor covered in thick, luxurious carpets.

The rooms were beautifully decorated, the walls adorned with exquisite murals.

Footsteps approached from ahead. A group of people was coming, about to corner Alexander in the corridor.
