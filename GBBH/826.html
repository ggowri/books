Bobby was sly and devious, always forming cliques back in college, bullying classmates and causing all sorts of trouble.

At the time, Alexander was naive and often fell victim to Bobby's schemes.

They had fought a few times in the past, and both had come out worse for it. The rivalry had lingered ever since.

Craig kindly warned him.

"Don't get into it with that guy. Bobby's petty and has a small mind. Now that you're here, he might try to pull something. Just ignore him."

"What's he going to try?"

Alexander sneered, dismissing the idea.

"Don't worry. As long as he doesn't start anything, I'm fine. Since he's treating us to dinner, we'll enjoy the meal. Might as well take advantage of it."

Craig burst out laughing. "You're right. We're here to eat, not to deal with him!"

Craig then gave Alexander a curious glance, noticing how different he seemed, more confident, with the vibe of someone important.

The three of them followed the waiter to a large hall inside.

There were already ten tables set up, almost full.

Not just their classmates but also their spouses or plus-ones.

It seemed Bobby's popularity was still strong. People were eager to attend and show him respect.

"Hey, look who's here!"

As Alexander walked in, Bobby, who was sitting at the head of the table, immediately stood up.

Bobby was around the same age as Alexander, but he had put on some weight and had a big gut. He wore a sharp suit, a thick gold chain around his neck, several gold rings on his fingers, and a flashy gold Rolex on his wrist that sparkled under the lights, almost as if he wanted to make sure everyone knew how wealthy he was.

Alice let out a derisive snort upon seeing Bobby's appearance.

"What a country bumpkin."

Alexander smiled. Bobby certainly looked tacky.

But since they were classmates, and Bobby seemed genuinely happy to see him, Alexander gave him a handshake.

As their hands met, Bobby suddenly tightened his grip, trying to make Alexander look bad by forcing a show of strength.

No matter how hard Bobby tried, Alexander didn't flinch.

Alexander was annoyed by this childish attempt.

He responded with a slight increase in pressure, and Bobby immediately yelped in pain.

"Ow! Let go... Let go of me..."

Everyone stared, thinking that Alexander had intentionally humiliated Bobby.

Someone quickly stepped in, reprimanding, "Alexander, what are you doing? Let go!"

Alexander shrugged and released Bobby.

Bobby, sweating from the pain, gritted his teeth and said.

"I'm trying to do a good thing by hosting this reunion, and you actually pull this sneak attack. You're so despicable."

Alexander raised an eyebrow and shrugged. "Wasn't it you who squeezed first?"

Craig and some of the other classmates quickly intervened to smooth things over.

"Come on. Just forget it. We're all classmates here."

"It was just a misunderstanding. It's a reunion. Let's have fun."

Bobby, realizing he wasn't a match for Alexander, shot him a few sideways glances, then turned his attention to Alice. His eyes widened in surprise.

He gave a dismissive humph and sarcastically said.

"Alexander, I've met your wife Jane before. This isn't her. Looks like you're a real scumbag, cheating on your wife with someone else."

Craig quickly explained, "You're mistaken. This is Alexander's secretary."

Everyone froze for a moment.

"Hahaha!"

Bobby burst out laughing.

"A kept man like him can afford a secretary? This is too much! Maybe she's just some prostitute he hired to play along, trying to look like a big shot in front of us."

Alexander's eyes darkened, and his voice turned icy.

"Bobby, apologize to my secretary right now, or I'll knock all your teeth out."

Alice, who had been about to lose her temper, couldn't help but feel a sweet sense of satisfaction hearing Alexander defend her.

Bobby sneered and turned to the others.

"Do you really believe this is his secretary? Look at him, a poor guy who can barely make ends meet, and he's got the money to hire a secretary?

"If everyone really thinks she's his secretary, then I'll apologize."
