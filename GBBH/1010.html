This time, Windwolf did not strike empty-handed. Instead, he drew a sharp dagger.

The gleaming blade swept towards Betty. The sharp wind from the blade was already lifting her long hair and was reaching her neck.

"Be careful!"

The members of the Ultimate Alliance shouted in unison.

Tears welled up in their eyes. They wanted to save Betty, but they couldn't match Windwolf's speed.

Betty was frozen in fear, unable to dodge in time. She could only shut her eyes in despair.

Windwolf showed a cold and triumphant smile.

The knife howled as it swiftly approached Betty's neck.

In the next second, Betty would surely lose her head.

At the life-or-death moment, suddenly, a sharp sound rang out.

Windwolf's dagger snapped in two, one piece falling to the ground, missing its target.

A stunned silence fell over the room, and everyone was frozen in place.

Windwolf's face turned pale as he tried to figure out what happened.

His hand trembled. The dagger had broken from a forceful attack, but he couldn't see the enemy.

A small stone, no bigger than a soybean, rolled across the ground.

Someone had used this tiny, bean-sized stone to break Windwolf's dagger.

Not only was Windwolf dumbfounded, but Betty was too.

In the next moment, a look of joy spread across her face. She knew it had to be Alexander coming to her rescue.

She knew that no one else could possess such strong power except Alexander.

Asher narrowed his eyes with dark expression. His skills were not bad, even stronger than Windwolf's in some ways.

Just now, Windwolf's dagger had been broken by a stone, yet Asher hadn't seen who did it or how it was done.

Even if Asher had given his all, it would have been hard to break a knife with just a stone.

"Humph! You failed at causing trouble at the Brilliant Group, so now you've come to stir things up with us at Ultimate Alliance. Asher, you've chosen the wrong path."

A cold and angry voice echoed from outside.

Everyone's hearts clenched at once. The people from Ultimate Alliance felt a surge of hope and excitement while Asher's side turned grim and tense.

Everyone turned in unison, staring at the door.

A bloodied figure crawled in, groaning in pain. He reached out to Asher for help, but before he could say a word, someone yanked him away, and a shrill scream followed.

Asher had stationed dozens of men by the door, but now they were nowhere to be seen. It was clear they were done for.

Alexander's figure appeared at the door, his cold gaze locking onto Asher.

Asher couldn't help but shudder before revealing a savage grin on his face.

He had come prepared. He had anticipated Alexander's appearance, knowing that Alexander was skilled.

Asher had made sure to prepare himself for this moment.

He had already predicted that, so besides Windwolf, there were two other level-two metahumans hidden in the crowd.

He felt that with Windwolf, the three powerful metahumans, plus himself, they could overwhelm Alexander. There was no way Alexander could resist.

"Alexander, you finally showed up!"

Betty cried out in joy, rushing into Alexander's arms.

Alexander patted her cheek, gently wiping away the tears from her eyes, and grinned.

"Silly girl, why are you crying? Just wait and see how I get you your revenge!"

Betty laughed through tears and her voice was filled with relief.

"Yeah, Asher is such a jerk! He forced me to surrender and wanted me to sleep with him..."

A cold glint flashed in Alexander's eyes, his voice low and menacing.

"You are my woman, Betty. Whoever dares to make you sleep with him, I will make him die."

His domineering words made everyone shudder.

Asher's face darkened further.

"Alexander, don't get too smug. I've been waiting for you, and now you've delivered yourself right to my doorstep.

"Hand over the magic powder formula and beg me for forgiveness, now!"

Asher's voice grew more and more furious as he recalled everything Alexander had done at West Cemetery. He was itching to kill Alexander right then and there.

He had come prepared. With three metahumans on his side, plus himself, he was certain they could handle Alexander. So, Asher was confident in his success.
