The young women serving the dishes all looked over, their expressions filled with dissatisfaction and disdain, believing that Alexander had never seen the world.

"He actually said Mr. Clement's food wasn't that great?"

"Mr. Clement is a descendant of royal chefs. He must be deliberately slandering him out of jealousy."

"If it weren't for our rules, I would really want to kick him out."

A few women whispered among themselves, extremely displeased with Alexander's earlier evaluation.

"What are you saying? What do you mean?" Cloe's expression changed as she glared at them in anger.

The manager sneered and said, "Ms. Cloe Bryant, I'm sorry. We can understand that someone may not like the dishes. Perhaps he hasn't had good food before. But it's indeed a pity to waste the hard work Mr. Clement put into making these meals."

Cloe slammed her hand on the table. "Enough! Is this how you treat guests? Watch your attitude."

The hall manager retorted, "Ms. Cloe Bryant, it was this gentleman who insulted Mr. Clement first. You can't just twist the facts."

"Shut up. I see you need a lesson," William shouted, ready to slap the manager, but was stopped by Alexander.

"Forget it. We came for food."

William huffed, "My boss thinks it doesn't taste good, and I think it doesn't taste good either. Can't we express our opinions?"

Logan chimed in sarcastically, "You are too arrogant. Whether the food tastes good or not is the guest's business. Do you think you can control that? Forcing us to give a positive review, is that it?"

If Alexander hadn't intervened, the clueless hall manager would have definitely ended up on the floor.

"What are you arguing about? Why aren't you properly serving the guests?"

An angry voice came from a middle-aged man as a gray-haired man in a chef's outfit walked out from inside.

He was dressed like a chef and carried a faint smell of cooking oil.

"Mr. Clement, this guest says your dishes aren't good," the manager reported quickly.

Dulles frowned slightly and calmly said, "Dear guests, I welcome you to dine here, but I don't understand why there's a quarrel. If you are dissatisfied with the dishes, you can provide constructive feedback. There's no need to trouble others.

"I consider my cooking skills to be decent, but I'm also willing to learn."

Dulles appeared polite, but he was actually questioning Alexander.

Alexander completely ignored Dulles. With his current status, this so-called descendant of a royal chef was nothing more than an insignificant insect to him.

Dulles, feeling indignant, stepped closer to Alexander and asked, "Sir, may I know your name?"

William glared fiercely at Dulles. "This is my boss. What do you want?"

Dulles' expression changed instantly. He knew William was one of the senior managers of Treasure Island. Therefore, Alexander's status must be even more exalted.

If Alexander wanted to make trouble for him, his restaurant wouldn't be able to operate on Treasure Island.

Dulles had come to Treasure Island to open a restaurant because he had offended enemies and couldn't survive in his own country.

The residents of Treasure Island had strong purchasing power, allowing Dulles to recoup his costs and make a significant profit in a short time.

Dulles forced a smile and stretched out his hand to Alexander. "So, you're Mr. Hawthorne. It's a pleasure to meet you. May I ask what shortcomings my dishes have? I will certainly improve. I'm truly sorry if I didn't meet your expectations."

The expressions of the hall manager and the servers changed. Dulles was someone they regarded as unafraid of power, yet his attitude had shifted so quickly this time.

Alexander glanced at Dulles and smiled slightly, "Mr. Clement, your dishes aren't really that bad. They're okay."

Dulles' mouth twitched in frustration.

"Mr. Hawthorne, please be direct. I will make improvements."

Dulles' attitude seemed very humble, but in reality, it was a tactical retreat, wanting Alexander to realize that his cooking skills were not something everyone could question.

After pondering for a moment, Alexander said, "If full marks are a hundred, I would give you eighty. I don't mean to undermine you."

But the more Alexander talked like this, the more Dulles felt it grated on his ears.

Finally unable to contain himself, Dulles smiled and said, "Mr. Hawthorne, it seems you are a gourmet. Then I suppose your cooking must be better than mine. How about you show us your skills?"
