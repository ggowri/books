Marvin shot him a glare and asked cautiously, "Boss, should we let Norma know we've arrived?"

"Not yet. I want to take a look around first. I heard those films were all shot in this building."

Alexander glanced around, curiosity evident on his face.

Keith muttered to himself.

"Norma... I feel like I've heard that name somewhere... Oh, right! She's the leader of the Dragon Gang, isn't she?"

Keith's whole body shook with excitement.

The Dragon Gang often appeared in TV news and online stories, and someone had even made a documentary about Norma.

She was a top figure in the Walsh family, the head of the Dragon Gang.

"Could this be my chance to make my wish come true?"

Keith's face flushed red with excitement as a deep sense of satisfaction washed over him.

"Stop! Who are you?"

At the entrance, Alexander was blocked by a group of fierce-looking security guards.

They looked intimidating, their expressions far from welcoming.

Alexander asked calmly, "Can't I just go in for a look?"

The guards burst into mocking laughter.

"Kid, here for the actor job? This isn't the way; go around to the other side."

One guard pointed the way with a sneer.

In these parts, men applying for acting roles often faced ridicule and scorn.

"You're asking for trouble!"

Marvin was about to lash out but was stopped by Alexander's hand.

He paid no attention to the guards' attitude and turned to head toward the back of the building.

"Boss, why didn't you let me deal with them?"

Marvin asked, puzzled.

"Quiet!"

Alexander cut him off.

"What do you know besides fighting?"

"Forgive me, Boss. I was wrong..."

The normally hot-tempered Marvin lowered his head obediently, admitting his mistake.

Keith, now even more terrified, stayed silent and followed Alexander obediently.

Seeing his cousin's submissive demeanor made him fear Alexander even more.

The small plaza behind the building was chaotic.

A large group of men was crowded in line, jostling each other like it was a busy market.

There were men of all ages—gray-haired old men, fresh-faced teenagers, and mostly middle-aged men.

There were at least four or five hundred of them—tall, short, heavy, thin—a wide array of people.

No matter their age or appearance, they all shared one thing, not a single one had an honest gaze.

Some of them could even be described as sleazy.

Their demeanor was sleazy, suggesting they had seen too many questionable films and had skewed minds.

"Why are you shoving? What gives you the right to cut in front of me?"

"Stop yelling! I was here first, so I'm ahead of you."

"Why argue? There are too many people today. The chances of passing the audition are slim. If I can even get a background role, I'll be happy."

"Heh, I'm getting old. Can you give me a break?"

The plaza was a mess.

A woman in business attire held a megaphone and shouted, "Quiet! Anyone who cuts in line or talks loudly will be kicked out and banned from applying ever again!"

Dozens of security guards stood behind her, maintaining order.

After shouting, the woman pulled out a black baton, twirled it, and walked through the crowd, striking anyone who displeased her, making them yelp in pain.

"Hmph!"

She glared at the sleazy men, annoyed. They were of such poor quality that it was impossible to find decent actors—just trash.

Just then, Alexander approached with Marvin and Keith.

"Quite the crowd, huh? Packed in like sardines."

Alexander was only there to watch the commotion and use the opportunity to discreetly insert Keith into the Dragon Gang.

Nearby, a job notice was posted clearly.

"Dragon Gang hiring actors! High pay. Requirements as follows..."

After reading it, Alexander commanded Keith, "Get in line and join the Dragon Group as my informant."

Keith froze, his face turning red before going pale.

He had been here before but failed to pass the first round and was immediately eliminated.
