"Earlier, I only temporarily relieved your pain to see how you'd behave. You did alright, so for the next year, you won't feel any pain."

Alexander's tone was calm as he reached out and tapped both of them on the head, suppressing the Force he'd left in their bodies.

"Thanks, boss! It doesn't hurt anymore!"

Arthur and Timothy were ecstatic.

Alexander's eyes flashed with a sly glint as he smiled, "It won't happen often now, but remember, it'll flare up once a year at the end of December. Come find me then, and I'll help suppress it temporarily."

Arthur and Timothy had been overjoyed, but their happiness faded as fast as a bucket of ice water on a winter day.

Once a year? That meant they'd be Alexander's lapdogs for life.

Their faces went from joyful to utterly dismal in seconds.

"What's wrong? Not happy about it? Think once a year is too much? Would you rather deal with random, constant pain? How about that instead?"

Alexander's smile held a hint of coldness.

Arthur and Timothy couldn't force a smile, their heads shaking quickly.

"No, no! Please, not that. Boss, we'll do whatever you say. Once a year sounds great!"

Alexander already knew these two hated his guts, but they were too weak for him to bother using his Force to fully control them.

He didn't want to waste his power on them, so he left a little "insurance" to keep them obedient.

In the slums on the outskirts of Tijuanea, low buildings and shabby tents were packed together in disarray.

Trash was scattered everywhere, dirty water pooled along the streets, and homeless people could be seen huddled in corners.

Luckily, with the widespread use of the magic powder, there weren't many addicts left. Otherwise, this place would've been in even worse shape.

"Hey, boss Timothy! Hey, Arthur!"

Seven or eight men saw Timothy and Arthur and immediately greeted them with enthusiasm.

Timothy barked, "Are you all blind? Greet Mr. Hawthorne!"

The men froze, then quickly bowed to Alexander in unison.

"Good day, Mr. Hawthorne!"

Timothy bent over, smiling obsequiously at Alexander. "Boss, that scammer Victor lives here."

Alexander eyed the shabby shack with disbelief. This guy always acted like some bigshot in front of Lynn, boasting about being a successful businessman, but he lived in a dump like this.

"Grab him!" Alexander ordered casually.

"On it, Boss! No need for you to dirty your hands."

Timothy rolled up his sleeves and charged forward, kicking the rickety wooden door with all his might.

The door, already barely hanging on, didn't stand a chance.

BANG!

With a loud bang, it flew off its hinges and crashed to the ground.

The group barged in like a pack of wolves, and soon, Victor's terrified cries echoed from inside.

It wasn't long before he was dragged out, his arms twisted behind him like some stray dog.

Victor had just taken a dose of magic powder, still in a blissful high, so it took a moment for reality to sink in.

Timothy grabbed a bottle of water and splashed it in Victor's face.

Victor shivered, blinking at the group.

All he saw were fierce expressions and a knife at his throat, the blade already nicking his skin.

"Please, don't kill me! Please!"

Victor's face turned ghostly pale as he pleaded for his life.

Alexander smirked, "Be honest. Why did you try to scam Lynn?"

"You?! It's you? You son of a—"

Victor's shout was cut off by Timothy's hard slap across his face.

"Watch your mouth, old man! How dare you speak to our boss like that? You got a death wish?"

Timothy barked while Arthur kicked Victor in the stomach, sending him sprawling to the ground.

"You heard him. Answer the boss' question, or I'll skin you alive."

Arthur twirled the knife, grinning wickedly.

Victor finally realized how screwed he was, almost wetting himself in fear.

"Why did you scam Lynn?"

Alexander's voice was icy, cold enough to freeze anyone in their tracks.
