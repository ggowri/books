Soon, the ambulance and police cars arrived.

"What happened? Have you found any clues about the killer? Where did he go?

"A bunch of useless idiots, not a single lead!"

Oliver barked at his subordinates.

The audacity of the killer was beyond belief—he not only attempted to assassinate the sheriff but caused a massive explosion that humiliated Oliver, the head of the police station. There was no way to save face now.

The officers stood silent, too afraid to speak.

"Forget it, calm down..." Alexander intervened with a calm smile. "It's not really their fault... After all, even I couldn't catch the killer."

Oliver sighed, exasperated, and waved his men off.

The officers shot Alexander a grateful look before quickly scattering.

"Mr. Hawthorne, is this killer really from the Sakura Society? I can't believe after all the international crackdowns, they still have this kind of power left."

Oliver mused, shaken.

Alexander sneered, "Don't worry. That killer won't last much longer."

Oliver nodded, a bit relieved. "We're lucky to have you on our side, Mr. Hawthorne. Without your help, we'd never be able to handle someone as ruthless as him."

When Alexander returned to the police station, Melin was waiting anxiously for him.

"Alexander, are you okay?"

She rushed to him as soon as she saw him, scanning him carefully for any signs of injury.

When she found none, her breath finally relaxed.

"I'm fine, but that old bastard got away."

Alexander smiled faintly, trying to lighten the mood.

"You've been at it all night. You must be starving. Come sit down. I'll make you some spaghetti."

Melin quickly went to work, moving with a natural tenderness—like a loving wife.

Alexander glanced at the clock; it was already past midnight. He was hungry.

Melin not only made spaghetti but also two small side dishes.

"You've had a long night too. Come, let's eat together."

She urged him to sit down, and they shared the meal quietly, the only sound in the room being the clink of utensils.

There was a warmth in the air, unspoken, yet tangible.

Alexander ate without looking up, but he could feel Melin's gaze on him.

"What are you staring at? Is there something on my face? Come on, eat!"

He chuckled, picking up a piece of meat and putting it in her bowl.

"Thanks."

Melin smiled back at him, their eyes meeting in a silent exchange that said more than words could.

After finishing his meal, Alexander stood up to leave.

Suddenly, Melin grabbed his arm and whispered, "Will you stay tonight?"

For a moment, Alexander hesitated, a trace of guilt flickering across his face.

"Don't worry. That old bastard's seriously wounded. He won't be back. I just need to check on my family... in case something's happened."

The moment he mentioned his family, Melin's expression fell. She knew exactly who he meant—Ava.

"Of course... You'll be gone when the sun comes up, won't you? Just like all those fleeting moments..." Melin thought to herself, a pang of sadness in her chest.

Alexander had already taken a step toward the door but paused. He turned back, giving her a reassuring smile.

"I'll find him, Melin. I promise."

"Thank you."

Her voice barely above a whisper, Melin nodded, her eyes heavy with emotion. She softly closed the door behind him.

As Alexander left, he could see the sorrow in her eyes, and a sigh escaped him. His mind was far from clear.

The faces of several women swirled in his thoughts, each pulling him in a different direction.

In the cab on his way home, Alexander couldn't shake the feeling that something wasn't right. The driver was acting strangely, glancing back at him through the rearview mirror a little too often.

That sudden, unsettling sense of danger washed over him.

Real experts, those who had faced death time and again, could feel these shifts in the air—the danger that was about to unfold.

As a seasoned Warrior, Alexander had honed this instinct.

The driver was definitely suspicious.

Alexander caught the driver's gaze in the mirror. Their eyes locked.

"What are you looking at?"

A cold chill ran through Alexander's voice as he asked, his tone laced with warning.

"I... I wasn't looking at anything."

The driver's voice cracked, and his grip tightened on the steering wheel. His expression twisted into something unnatural.

"Heh. You're with the Sakra Society, aren't you?"

Alexander's voice was calm but laced with deadly intent.

The driver's face turned pale. In an instant, he jerked the wheel sharply.

"Bang!"

The taxi slammed into a roadside flower bed, the violent force sending the car into a spin. It flipped once in the air before crashing violently back onto the road.
