Timothy and his group were all drunk, the table a mess of cups and plates, with several empty bottles rolling around the deck.

*****

By the time Alexander and his team arrived at Treasure Island, dawn was nearly upon them.

The convoy sped through the dim streetlights, and as they reached an intersection, Alexander suddenly signaled for the driver to stop.

The driver slammed on the brakes, the tires screeching as the car came to a halt on the side of the road.

"Get lost, you thugs! Or I'll call for help!" A woman's voice drifted faintly from somewhere in the distance.

Though the voice was faint, the air was still enough that Alexander's keen senses could pick up every word, even from hundreds of meters away.

He opened the car door and stepped out, striding toward the source of the voice.

The area was dimly lit, but his exceptional vision allowed him to see clearly.

Around a corner a hundred meters away, a young woman was stumbling, clearly intoxicated. She wore a black trench coat over a tight-fitting top that accentuated her graceful figure, and her short skirt revealed long, slender legs.

She wobbled unsteadily, struggling to keep her balance.

There were four men around her, grinning lecherously, making inappropriate advances. They were clearly trouble.

"Hey, beautiful, what's the rush? Come on, let's grab another drink together," one of the men, a greasy-looking fat guy, said as he reached for her waist.

"Get off me! If you keep bothering me, I'll call the patrol team. You know the laws of Treasure Island, don't you?" the woman snapped, struggling to break free.

Hearing her mention the patrol team and the law, the men froze, glancing around nervously, but finding no one else in sight.

The fat man's anger flared. "Damn bitch! How dare you try to scare us? Grab her! Take her back and have some fun!"

The men rushed forward, one covering her mouth, another pinning her hands, while the fat one wrapped his arms around her waist and lifted her off her feet.

Clearly, this wasn't their first time; they subdued her with ease and dragged her into a dark alley nearby.

The woman was too drunk to fight back effectively, and despite her desperate struggles, she couldn't break free.

They shoved her against a wall, covering her mouth to muffle her cries, and began groping her.

The fat man, the leader of the group, grinned with excitement, lowering his head to bury his face in her chest and began shamelessly biting.

The woman's muffled cries were all she could manage, her fear evident in her wide eyes as she tried to scream, but only weak, pitiful sounds escaped.

"Haha, lucky us! We've got a real gem here tonight!" the fat man laughed. "Bob, hurry up and take your turn, or I'll start without you!"

"Look at this figure! Soft and big. I'm gonna make her regret this," one of the men sneered.

"Hold off for a minute. If the patrol team shows up, we're done for," another man warned, holding back.

Bob grinned wickedly and pulled out some rope to bind the woman's hands.

Her eyes were filled with terror, two tears of despair rolling down her cheeks. With her mouth covered, she couldn't scream louder than a desperate whimper.

"Such a slut, wearing a thong, huh?" one of the men leered, while they roughly tied her up with the rope.

At that moment, a calm, cold voice pierced through the air. "What do you think you're doing?"

The men froze, startled, their bodies shaking with fear.

Before they could react, a fierce gust of wind whipped through the alley, and then—bang! Bob was sent flying with a single kick from Alexander.

Weighing at least 200 pounds, he was tossed like a ragdoll, his body crashing to the ground with a sickening thud.

His head slammed into the pavement, and his eyes rolled back, knocked unconscious.

"Bob! What happened to you?" one of the men screamed, rushing to help Bob.

Without a sound, Alexander appeared before them, his deep eyes cold as ice, as if he were looking at dead men.

"According to the laws of Treasure Island, you all deserve to die."

A murderous intent flickered in his eyes.

Treasure Island was his home, and no one was allowed to break its rules.
