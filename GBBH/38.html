Joseph's right hand throbbed with excruciating pain, and he struggled to catch his breath under Alexander's crushing weight.

He looked up at Alexander with terror in his eyes. He knew this guy wasn't joking around; he was truly capable of murder.

"All... all of you... get out... now... "

Joseph struggled to command his underlings.

Alexander's foot pressed down harder, making it increasingly difficult for Joseph to breathe.

The Cripples' men retreated, leaving without a trace.

Alexander slowly lifted his right foot.

Joseph gasped for air, his body drenched in sweat from the ordeal.

After catching his breath, he defiantly threatened.

"You dare cripple my hand? Are you trying to start a war between the Saint Group and The Cripples?"

Alexander looked at him with contempt.

"The fact that you're still breathing is the greatest mercy I'll show you."

Joseph was struck speechless.

"Get on your knees and apologize, then get lost!" Alexander barked.

"You! Don't push your luck!" Joseph sputtered.

Alexander grabbed him by the throat and hoisted him into the air.

"You gonna submit? You gonna kneel?"

Joseph's face turned even darker from lack of oxygen.

His eyeballs began to bulge as he desperately clawed at Alexander's hand.

"I... I submit. I'll kneel..."

Faced with the threat of death, Joseph finally caved.

Alexander flung him aside, his burly frame crumpling to the ground like a sack of trash.

Trembling, he got on his knees and stammered.

"I... I apologize. I'm sorry..."

"Get lost!"

Alexander waved him off.

Joseph scurried away with his lackeys, not even daring to look back.

Only then did everyone else snap out of their shock.

"I gotta say, Mr. Hawthorne's got some serious moves. No wonder Ms. Blackburn's taken a shine to him."

"Don't forget, Joseph trained under Mason, the boxing champ. Mason's known for being overprotective. Think he might step in himself?"

"Big deal! He's just another meathead who knows how to throw a punch."

"Yeah, if the Saint Group's guys hadn't been packing heat, Joseph's crew would've turned that punk into Swiss cheese by now."

The crowd buzzed with chatter. Some praised Alexander's skills, others fretted about Mason's potential retaliation, but most still looked down their noses at Alexander.

In their eyes, no matter how skilled he was, Alexander wasn't part of their elite circle. At best, he was just a capable bodyguard or hired muscle.

Meanwhile, Ava was utterly astonished.

She never imagined Alexander possessed not only miraculous medical skills but also such impressive fighting abilities.

Yet this guy had endured five whole years of bullying from the Fairfax family.

It seemed he was hiding something. She'd have to dig deeper and get to the bottom of this!

Ava mulled over these thoughts, her curiosity piqued.

Despite Godfather's matchmaking intentions, Ava remained reluctant to marry someone she hardly knew.

Billy gushed, tears welling in his eyes.

"Thank you so much, Mr. Hawthorne! From now on, if you tell me to jump, I'll ask how high!"

Alexander was quite pleased with his attitude.

"Come see me tomorrow. I have some business for you to take care of."

"Of course, of course! I'll be there bright and early!"

Billy nodded vigorously, like a woodpecker on caffeine.

The entire confrontation was captured on camera, with Anne watching intently from the security room.

She promptly sent the video to Godfather.

The silver-haired old man watched the footage, musing to himself.

"He's definitely my flesh and blood, my good son. To think he managed to lay low in the Fairfax family for five years.

"I thought he was done for, but he's proven to be quite capable. He's no less impressive than his older brothers."

Overcome with emotion, the old man began coughing violently, specks of blood visible in his phlegm.

The medical staff, ever-vigilant, rushed to his side...

Ava cautiously inquired, "If you're so capable, how come you let the Fairfax family push you around before?"

Alexander scratched his head sheepishly. "Well... I believe in killing them with kindness."

Ava burst into laughter, unable to contain herself.

She shot Alexander a withering glare.

"If you don't wanna spill, fine. But don't feed me a bunch of bull. You can't keep secrets forever, you know. I'll find out sooner or later."

Alexander just chuckled, neither confirming nor denying anything.

After all that drama with the Cripples, the ball was winding down.

Everyone started saying their goodbyes to Ava.

Oscar limped over, leaning on his cane, and spoke with a complicated expression.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I misjudged you. While you're not quite on par with my grandson, you've got skills of your own, I'll give you that."
