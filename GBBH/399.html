Still, Wyatt had to admit Alexander was really strong.

Eugene urged impatiently, "Dad, hurry up and teach him a lesson!"

He had seen his dad punch right through a wall before.

That kind of terrifying power wasn't something a regular person could match. The Blood Gang's success today was largely thanks to Wyatt.

Wyatt took a few steps forward, cracking his knuckles as he approached Alexander.

"Kid, don't think a little skill makes you untouchable. To me, you're just a joke."

Seeing their boss about to act, the crowd got all hyped up, as if pumped with adrenaline.

"Dad, let me slap him hard!" Eugene shouted excitedly.

Wyatt nodded. "You'll get to slap him a hundred times soon enough."

Alexander was speechless.

"You guys talk too much. If you're going to fight, get on with it. If not, get out of Loscity!"

Mary couldn't help but laugh. Alexander was aggravating.

Wyatt's expression grew darker.

"Arrogant!"

He suddenly leaped forward, appearing right in front of Alexander, swinging a heavy punch directly at his head.

The punch was powerful, and the speed was at its peak.

"Is that it? Get lost!"

Alexander threw his own punch, meeting Wyatt's fist head-on.

"Bang!"

What happened next shocked everyone.

Wyatt was sent flying backward, spitting out blood mid-air.

Alexander had defeated Wyatt with just one move.

Silence fell; only the sound of heavy breathing could be heard as everyone stared at Alexander in disbelief.

Eugene was stunned, frozen in place.

He had always thought his dad was unbeatable, yet he'd been defeated in a single move. What was going on?

Wyatt himself was the most shocked. When his fist collided with Alexander's, no one could have known the turmoil it caused inside him.

He had put all his power into that punch, strength surpassing a champion boxer's, yet it had been shattered so easily.

Clutching his broken right hand, Wyatt managed to choke out, "How... how did you do that?"

A confident smile spread across Alexander's face, exuding a commanding, powerful aura.

"The Blood Gang never should have sided with the mafia, and you shouldn't have messed with me. But now it's too late!"

Wyatt, both anxious and terrified, spat out another mouthful of blood and passed out.

Alexander's cold gaze fell on Eugene.

"You set up that trap for Mary, didn't you?"

Eugene's face went pale, and he turned to run.

"Ouch!"

With a cry of pain, Alexander kicked him to the ground.

Eugene quickly began begging for mercy.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I'm sorry. I didn't know you and Mary were... involved. It wasn't intentional. I was pushed into it by that jerk Lawrence..."

He crawled on his knees toward Mary, pleading.

"Mary, I swear I didn't know... really! This is all a misunderstanding... the Blood Gang has always treated you well...

"I honestly didn't mean it, I didn't know anything. If I had meant to hurt you, may I die a horrible death and go to hell."

Eugene's acting was convincing; he was begging, crying, and looked truly desperate.

Seeing Eugene grovel like this, especially compared to his previous arrogance, Mary felt a wave of nausea, nearly gagging from disgust.

Seeing that neither Mary nor Alexander had spoken, Eugene's eyes lit up, and he quickly got up and tried to run.

Alexander's cold voice rang out.

"Did I say you could leave?"

Eugene froze, slowly turning around, his face looking as if someone in his family had died.

"Everyone, get down on your knees. If you don't, you'll die!" Alexander ordered sternly.

Not only Eugene but the other Blood Gang leaders also felt overwhelming pressure.

"Thud! Thud!"

Everyone's knees went weak, and they dropped hard to the ground.

Instantly, everyone was on edge, but they were secretly relieved that someone had managed to send a message to the police earlier.

A top-tier gang reduced to relying on the police for protection. This was the biggest joke of all.
