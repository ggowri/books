Ava thought for a moment and said, "I'll reach out to Mr. Wallace and Mr. Thompson. They should be able to acquire a batch of military-grade weapons."

"Very well. Be cautious and keep in touch. Rest assured, I'll return soon."

Alexander replied as he got out of bed, leaving the bedroom to arrange for a private jet to head to Mandale Town at the fastest speed possible.

This time, Alexander was traveling alone, without any of his subordinates.

He had absolute confidence in his own strength, and he wasn't truly alone. Mandale Town was still under the control of the Axe and Beverly.

After a long flight, Alexander finally arrived in Mandale Town.

"Alexander, you're finally here!" Xavier and Beverly exclaimed with excitement as they saw Alexander step out of the vehicle.

Their faces were filled with joy and excitement, and their followers looked relieved.

Alexander had become a legendary figure in Mandale Town. With his arrival, everyone felt a surge of confidence.

"Good day, Mr. Hawthorne!"

Over a hundred people bowed to Alexander in unison, drawing the shocked and envious gazes of everyone on the street.

"Who is this young man with such importance?" someone whispered.

"Keep your voice down. This is a member of the Axe and the McLean family. They're at war with the Mafia. Do you want to die?" another person warned.

"I've heard too. The Mafia has the support of wizards. The Axe has suffered greatly, even their leader, Alyssa, has been captured," someone else added.

"Could this young man be the reinforcements the Axe called for? It can't be. He's so young and alone; what ability could he have?"

Many speculated, not optimistic about Alexander and the Axe.

Alexander went straight to the headquarters of the Axe.

"Have you found any clues?" he asked.

Xavier quickly reported, "Alexander, we haven't found any clues yet. What should we do? The longer we wait, the more dangerous it is for Alyssa."

Beverly looked at Alexander with affection, bowing her head deeply.

"We will assist with everything we have, with just one word from you!"

Her attitude was extremely respectful.

She could never forget the last time Alexander had left her in a state of agony and ecstasy.

Beverly trembled at the memory of those painful and pleasurable events.

"Well, your rescue of the Axe this time was commendable. I will reward you," Alexander complimented her in a flat tone.

Beverly's face lit up with joy, her cheeks flushing with excitement.

"No clues are troublesome. But, it's not entirely hopeless." Alexander commanded, "Take me to Alyssa's room immediately."

"Yes, sir!"

Although the members of the Axe were puzzled about why Alexander wanted to go to Alyssa's room, they dared not ask and did not dare to delay.

They quickly took Alexander to Alyssa's bedroom.

"Everyone out, guard the door. Without my command, no one is allowed to enter!"

Alexander issued the order seriously.

No one hesitated, immediately retreating and looking at Alexander with some unease.

Alexander activated the Force, using his All-seeing Eyes to scan the room.

With the All-seeing Eyes, the entire room took on a different appearance, as if it were a layered high-definition image.

The base color of the room was black and white, while the footprints, fingerprints, and even the hairs Alyssa had left behind were clearly visible in red through the All-seeing Eyes.

Alexander immediately went over, picked up the fallen hairs, and took out a few sets of lingerie from the wardrobe, sniffing them carefully.

Having reached the peak of an Intermediate Warrior, his sense of smell was extremely keen, and he had firmly memorized Alyssa's scent.

The shape of Alyssa's footprints was also imprinted in Alexander's mind.

Next, Alexander went to the location where Alyssa was last seen and began a meticulous search.

Everyone was puzzled about what Alexander was doing, but no one dared to disturb him, all waiting quietly for his next move.

"Found it!" Alexander exclaimed in delight.

He sniffed vigorously. Using his All-seeing Eyes to scan the ground, he indeed saw a series of intermittent footprints left by Alyssa.

Next to Alyssa's footprints, there were several rows of men's footprints. Those must have belonged to the kidnappers.
