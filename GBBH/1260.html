Alexander kept his eyes locked on the figure. He took a deep breath, channeled the Force, and hurled his Lightsabers with explosive power.

The glowing blades streaked through the air like lightning, instantly appearing behind the stranger.

Though the figure stood nearly 220 yards away, Alexander's Lightsabers closed the distance in a heartbeat.

Only then did the Lightsabers make its signature sound, tearing through the air with a piercing shriek.

The stranger was agile, but Alexander's attack came with such ferocity that by the time he noticed, it was too late to dodge completely.

He managed to twist away, barely avoiding a fatal blow. The Lightsabers sliced through one of his arms, sending blood spraying.

Alexander rushed forward and, with a gesture, called his Lightsabers back to his hand through the Force.

Before the man in black could scramble to his feet, Alexander was already there, pinning him down with a boot to the chest.

The beast tide had fallen into chaos. Though some beasts still attacked Louvian Port, many had fled.

Karson and his people, who had nearly lost all hope, were overcome with joy. They knew Alexander must have turned the tide somehow.

Despite their lingering anxiety, they could finally see the light of victory ahead.

"Tell me," Alexander demanded in an icy voice. "Was Yinson behind this?"

The mysterious figure's cloak fell away, revealing a face as gaunt as a skeleton.

"Heh." He fixed Alexander with a grotesque smile.

Alexander stomped down hard, instantly breaking two of the man's ribs.

But instead of screaming, he just let out an eerie laugh. Even with broken ribs piercing his chest cavity and blood pouring from his mouth, he showed no sign of pain.

Alexander's brow furrowed. This had to be one of the Sakra Society's test subjects—probably engineered to control mutant beasts. These subjects had lost their ability to feel pain, and likely any human emotions at all.

The man suddenly let out a bestial howl, lunging like a wolf to bite Alexander's leg.

Alexander reacted with lightning speed. He unleashed the Force in a violent burst, hitting the man like a sledgehammer to the chest and sending him flying backward.

But the man wouldn't give up. He struggled to his feet and charged again like a rabid dog.

Alexander frowned. This clearly wasn't a rational human anymore. Yinson had turned him into a monster.

With a swift motion, Alexander swung his Lightsaber and severed the man's head.

The head rolled away, but that twisted smile still frozen on its face. The headless body collapsed, blood gushing like a fountain from the neck.

The moment he fell dead, all the mutant beasts turned tail and fled, scattering into the darkness. Their earlier bloodthirsty rage had vanished completely.

Then came the thundering sound of engines.

Dozens of military helicopters appeared on the horizon, racing toward Louvian Port.

Headlights pierced the darkness in the distance as at least a hundred military vehicles approached the port.

A heavy silence fell over the survivors.

For a moment, all they could hear was each other's heavy breathing.

Then, someone broke into a cheer. "We've got rescue coming! We're gonna live!"

Karson grabbed Norah's hand, tears of joy streaming down his face.

"We made it! We actually won!"

"Hell yeah!"

People erupted in celebration, waving their arms and jumping up and down.

They hugged each other, almost delirious with relief.

But as their cheers died down and they took in the devastating battlefield before them, silence fell once again. Some quietly wiped away tears.

Sure, the rescue had finally arrived. But the cost was unbearable. Only half of their original thousand remained.

They'd be lucky if a few thousand residents survived in Louvian. That was the price paid in blood.

Fiona dropped her gun, her face blank, all traces of joy vanishing from her expression.

Her heart felt like it was being crushed by a boulder as she stumbled toward the crevice where Oswald had disappeared.

The crack was still there, impossibly deep and dark, but there wasn't even a shadow of Oswald to be seen.

Fiona stood there silently, tears streaming down her face uncontrollably.

She looked like a shell of herself. Her devastation and despair were a stark contrast to everyone else's excitement and joy.

"Oswald..." she whispered.

Looking at the blood spattered on the ground and the broken rope, Fiona finally broke down into gut-wrenching sobs.

Her cries cut through the celebration, drawing everyone's attention. The joyful atmosphere quickly died down as tears welled up in their eyes.
