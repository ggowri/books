Meanwhile, in some regions of Loscity, mainly the slums, a terrifying disease had begun to spread.

This infectious disease was ruthless. Even masks and gloves weren't enough protection. Once infected, victims suffered from violent coughing fits, followed by pulmonary fibrosis, before dying from multiple organ failure.

What most people didn't know was that Monica, the ruler of the City of Sorcery, unleashed this plague. It was a mysterious poison of her making.

This deadly poison had been excavated from an ancient tomb by Monica and Rock.

In their expedition, Monica claimed the poison while Rock took possession of the Ancient Grimoire.

The poison's potency had weakened considerably over the millennia. Otherwise, the exposure would have instantly melted victims into pools of black liquid.

Even though the poison's toxicity had weakened, it was still terrifyingly potent. It was incredibly contagious, the symptoms hit you like a freight train, and once they did, you were in serious trouble.

Within a week, the infected suffer total organ failure. Their bodies start hemorrhaging; their skin erupts in ulcers... it was a gruesome death, and there was nothing anyone could do to stop it.

Standing in her office at the Saint Group headquarters, Ava watched ambulances racing through the streets below.

"What in the world is happening? God help Loscity," Ava whispered, her beautiful face etched with concern.

Her phone rang twice, and Ava's face lit up when she saw Alexander's reply.

With Alexander by her side, Ava felt utterly safe. She knew that even if the sky came crashing down, she'd have nothing to worry about.

...

The wailing of sirens pierced the air as ambulances rushed patients into hospitals across the city.

Crowds of people were flooding into hospitals, too, supporting sick friends and family members who needed urgent care.

Loscity Hospital, being the largest in the city, had the most wards and beds available.

Melin stood at the entrance, her stunning figure accentuated by her fitted police uniform.

But right now, her stunning face was set in an icy mask. The Chief had assigned her to this beat, responsible for keeping the peace at Loscity Hospital, and it was proving to be one hell of a job.

Though it was her first time dealing with such a bizarre situation, Melin showed no sign of backing down.

"Melin! We've got trouble! All the wards are full, and people are getting aggressive because we can't admit any more patients!"

A young nurse with chubby cheeks rushed over, her round face filled with anxiety.

"Stay calm. I'll handle this!"

Melin gestured to her officers and strode quickly toward the emergency room.

The scene in the spacious lobby was chaotic. Dozens of stretchers were scattered haphazardly across the floor, each holding a patient.

The air was filled with agonized moans as patients writhed in obvious pain.

The scene was horrific. Patients lost control of their bodily functions and vomited non-stop. The stench was enough to make anyone's stomach turn.

It was like a scene straight out of hell. Melin's frown deepened as she felt her hands and feet begin to shake.

"Please, I'm begging you! Save my wife!"

"Help my daughter, please, hurry!"

"If you don't save my son, I'll kill you all!"

A mob of desperate family members surrounded the doctors and nurses. Some were pleading, others making threats. The whole place was in complete chaos.

"Please understand! We're at full capacity! You need to go to other hospitals! Please, try somewhere else!"

"Stand back! We need to treat our patients!"

The medical staff was drenched in sweat, completely overwhelmed. None of them had ever seen anything this terrifying. It felt like the end of the world.

Loscity Hospital was packed to the brim, severely understaffed, and couldn't possibly take any more patients.

Yet ambulances and families kept bringing in more sick people. The crowd was so thick you couldn't see through it, completely blocking the hospital entrance.

"Oh God! My throat suddenly hurts so bad... My heart's racing... Why am I coughing?... Am I infected?"

New infections were popping up everywhere, with people coughing so hard they could barely breathe.

"Everyone clear the area! Now! Sound the alarm! It is a severe infectious disease outbreak! Everyone needs to evacuate immediately!"

Melin shouted through the megaphone.

The emergency sirens blared throughout the hospital, their piercing wail continuing without pause.

Everyone froze in shock! It was the hospital's epidemic alarm!
