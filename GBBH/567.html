Ten million dollars a month was astronomical for an average person.

Even a typical wealthy person couldn't afford that kind of spending.

But for the extravagantly lavish lifestyle of Barry, he was practically broke.

The Morris family's rules were strict. If Barry exceeded his spending limit again, he'd face severe punishment and could even be expelled from the family.

So, Barry was already broke, deeply in debt, a complete pauper.

Felix had made it clear he was out of money, but Barry was still fixated on winning. He was desperate to recoup his losses, caught up in a gambler's frenzy.

Seeing Barry hesitating and not exchanging more chips, Jaylon and Finley launched into another round of cutting remarks.

Finley and Jaylon spat out taunting insults, each one like a knife stabbing into Barry's pride.

"Barry, you're done already? Out of cash? Can't believe you're giving up after just a few hundred million," Jaylon mocked, his eyes full of contempt.

"Come on, leave him alone, Barry's out of money," Finley jeered, "He should just get lost if he's broke!"

Even the attractive dealer asked in a seemingly caring voice, "Sir, would you like to continue playing? If not, please step aside, or kindly exchange for more chips if you want to keep playing."

Barry's face turned pale, and he gritted his teeth.

"Haven't you heard of the Morris family? Who dares to say I'm broke? I'm playing, and I'll win it all back today!"

"Mr. Morris..."

Diego leaned close and gently advised him, "It's getting late. Maybe we should head back and rest."

Joyce also said cautiously, "Mr. Morris, how about we go back? I'll help you with a bath so you can relax..."

Barry cut her off sharply before she could finish.

"Shut up! Both of you, just shut up!

"Diego, Joyce, give me all the cash you have and exchange it for chips!"

Desperate, Barry gave direct orders to Diego and Joyce.

Diego frowned but still patiently advised in a low voice, "Mr. Morris, maybe it's best to call it quits...if you keep going, no amount of money will be enough..."

"Exactly, Mr. Morris, at this rate, even hundreds of billions wouldn't be enough."

Joyce grew anxious; she'd finally saved a bit of her own money and didn't want Barry gambling it away.

She knew he wouldn't repay her if he lost, and she didn't dare ask him for the debt.

If he kept gambling, he'd only get deeper into this losing streak.

But Barry's arrogance and his gambling addiction wouldn't let him give up so easily.

"Mr. Morris, let's just go," Joyce urged.

Let it go?

How could he let it go!

The more they tried to dissuade him, the more Barry clung on, his expression shifting with indecision.

Just then, Alexander and Nancy's helicopter landed on the rooftop helipad.

"Welcome, Ms. Tucker!"

Cole led a group of black-suited men, bowing respectfully to Nancy.

Seeing Alexander holding Nancy around the waist, everyone froze in surprise.

Nancy was known to avoid getting close to men, let alone showing such public affection.

Cole was stunned, wondering about Alexander's background.

Nancy, her face expressionless, said, "This is my boyfriend, Alexander. What he says is what I say."

"Yes, Ms. Tucker!"

Everyone bowed their heads obediently.

"What's the situation with Felix and Barry?" Alexander asked, his voice edged with concern.

Cole quickly filled him in, and Alexander nodded, a slight smile forming as he pointed at Joyce on the surveillance screen.

"Make sure they win her over. Then we can use her as a way in to see what kind of plot Felix and Barry are hatching together."

"Understood!"

Cole agreed immediately, relaying Alexander's orders to Finley and Jaylon via radio.
