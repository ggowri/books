"Since yesterday, people have come looking for that woman and the baby. I thought about calling the police, but I'm afraid there really are traitors in the force.

"To be safe, I've kept the guns there. If things get out of hand, I can protect them."

Malcom frowned deeply, clearly worried.

Alexander quickly offered, "Malcom, if you don't mind, I'd like to meet that woman. I have a few questions for her.

"We came to Lumiria looking for a woman, someone from Risun."

"Of course, of course," Malcom said eagerly. "The woman is quite beautiful. The baby isn't hers; she rescued him."

Alexander followed Malcom upstairs.

When Malcom opened the door, Alexander's heart soared. That woman, to his surprise, was Hazel.

She was sitting, cradling a few-month-old baby, feeding him from a bottle while gently patting his back, humming a lullaby. She looked every bit like a tender, loving mother.

However, Alexander knew she couldn't have given birth to the baby. After all, she hadn't been in Lumiria from Risun for even half a month.

In such a short time, there was no way she could have been pregnant and given birth.

"Who's there?"

Hazel's voice startled when she saw Alexander.

"Did Cyclops send you to capture me?" she asked in fear.

She held the baby tightly, tears welling up in her eyes as she begged, "Please, don't kill Riley! If you let him live, I'll go with you. I promised Lorna I'd protect this child.

"Please, please, spare us..."

Hazel wept, her voice trembling.

Alexander smiled, relieved. He had feared that Hazel might resist returning with him, but now it seemed easier than expected.

It seemed the baby had changed Hazel's thoughts.

The baby cried loudly, and Hazel, in a panic, soothed him, her eyes brimming with tears.

"Riley, don't cry. I'll protect you."

She continued to plead with Alexander, "Please, spare us. If you do, I'll do anything you want. Anything! Just let us go!"

Alexander was taken aback. He hadn't expected Hazel, who had once been captivated by Cyclops, to be so caring.

She walked back and forth, softly singing and coaxing the baby in her arms.

Gradually, the baby stopped crying, and he fell asleep.

Malcom quickly explained, "Don't worry. This gentleman isn't a bad guy. He's my niece's boyfriend. He came to Aetheris Street to find someone."

Hazel let out a relieved sigh and gently laid the baby down, covering him with a blanket.

As she bent over, her ample chest fell, causing Alexander to stare in surprise.

Hazel was young, but her body had certainly been shaped by Cyclops' twisted influence.

"Well, you talk. I'll go help with dinner," Malcom said, understanding the situation and heading downstairs.

"Ms. Walsh, my name is Alexander. I was sent by your sister Norma to bring you back.

"It took me a long time to track you down after Cyclops brought you here. I'm so glad I found you."

Alexander explained his purpose, and Hazel's eyes widened in disbelief.

"You're... you're here to save me, sent by Norma?" she whispered, trembling.

Tears filled her eyes as she clutched her mouth and began to cry.

"I'm so regretful. I shouldn't have trusted Cyclops... Norma warned me, but I didn't listen.

"Cyclops is a beast. He takes women, but he doesn't stop there. He even harms babies. They like to torture and kill, even eat people. They're monsters!"

Hazel sobbed, pressing her hand to her mouth to stifle her cries, fearing the baby would wake.

"What? They eat people?"

Alexander's face paled, a shiver running down his spine. Even with his formidable strength, he couldn't help but shudder.

Hazel wiped away her tears and continued, "If I weren't part of the Walsh family and have some value to them, they would have killed me long ago. Riley's mother, Lorna, was killed right in front of me...

"I barely managed to escape with Riley. Thankfully, these two kind people saved me. Otherwise, I'd be dead too..."
