"The sales of the magic powder have been good lately, but why has the cash flow dropped by ten percent?"

Billy thought as long as he kept things under wraps, no one would ever know.

"Are you trying to deceive me?" Alexander growled, grabbing Billy by the throat and lifting him off the ground.

The grip tightened, and the sheer force made Billy's eyes bulge in terror.

"You have made enough as the middleman. People can't be too greedy."

Billy nodded vigorously. "Yes, yes! I owe everything to you, boss. I was just too greedy. I won't let it happen again!"

"Get lost!" Alexander waved him off.

Billy didn't dare utter another word and hurriedly left the room.

He thought, "It's all Betty's fault. If it weren't for her, Alexander wouldn't have discovered it."

"You did well."

Alexander slammed his hand on the table again, frustration boiling over. "He dares to touch my people? He's got a death wish."

Her curvy figure pressed against his chest, and the softness of her body was intoxicating.

"Betty, set up a networking event and notify all the companies and families in Loscity, big and small. Anyone interested in becoming a distributor of magic powder should come.

"At the event, I'll appoint the Saint Group as the main distributor and form an alliance with the four major families of Loscity—the Cripples, the Blood Gang, and the Kaylon—to create a nationwide sales network.
