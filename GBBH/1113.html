Oliver cautiously continued.

"Mr. Hawthorne, no offence, but... I think Ms. Ward... might be possessed.

"Ahem... I'm sorry. This is just my guess. I could be talking nonsense."

"Possessed? Alright, thank you, Mr. Benton. I see."

Alexander furrowed his brow. According to Oliver, this situation was becoming more complicated.

Oliver paused and fell silent.

"Okay, I'm on my way to see Melin."

Alexander agreed and then turned and rushed toward the police station.

Melin lived in a building behind the police station. Alexander had been there once before.

At that moment, Melin was lying in bed, looking utterly drained.

Lately, her mind had been in turmoil, occasionally haunted by memories of the terrifying battle to eliminate the living dead.

Maybe she was just exhausted from all the stress, Melin thought, trying to comfort herself, and closed her eyes, quickly drifting off to sleep.

She didn't know how long she slept, but when she woke, it was already evening.

The room was dim, and the streetlights outside had just flickered on. Melin suddenly felt bloated and needed to use the bathroom. She found the toilet was broken.

"Damn it! I forgot to have it fixed."

Shaking her head, she decided to use the public bathroom outside.

She rubbed her temples, her head heavy and foggy, as if it had been stuffed with glue.

Melin hurried downstairs and headed toward the shared restroom.

This was the police dormitory building, with the police station just ahead, so Melin felt completely at ease. Aside from feeling exhausted, there was nothing that seemed out of place.

Melin rubbed her temples as she walked into the women's restroom.

There were several stalls, all with their doors closed, and no sounds coming from inside.

Melin chose the outermost stall and gently pushed the door open.

Not long after, the soft sound of water could be heard.

After finishing, Melin felt much lighter. Suddenly, faint footsteps echoed from outside.

Tap, tap...

Two quiet steps, and then silence again.

At first, Melin thought it was someone else coming in, but soon, she realized something was off. Everything was too quiet.

"Who's out there?"

Melin asked, puzzled.

But no one responded. Seconds passed, and the restroom remained eerily silent.

Tap, tap...

The footsteps came again.

"This is Melin. Who's out there?"

Melin raised her voice.

Still, there was no response. The silence stretched on.

Melin's face changed. She wondered if it could have been a hallucination or if she had imagined the sound.

"Who's out there? This is Melin!"

She called out again, not willing to give up.

Again, there was no reply. Melin suddenly felt a chill down her spine and quickly stood up to fasten her pants.

Just as she was about to open the door, a pale hand reached under the partition, holding a sharp black trident dagger.

Without thinking, Melin jumped up onto the toilet.

Bang!

A flash of cold light and the toilet was sliced into pieces by the sharp dagger.

Melin froze in shock. If she hadn't moved quickly, her legs might have been gone.

Fuming with anger, she kicked the stall door with all her strength. It flew off its hinges and slammed to the ground.

But outside, there was no one. It was as if everything that had just happened was a mere illusion.

Melin rushed out, shouting angrily, "Stop hiding! Come out now!"

It was dead silent.

Determined, Melin kicked the door of the stall next to her.

Crash!

The door flew open and slammed against the wall. The stall was empty, not even a shadow.

Melin kicked open stall after stall, relentlessly searching for any clues.

When she reached the last stall, the door was ajar, but once again, it was completely empty. There was no sign of the attacker.

She wondered if it could be a ghost.

Suddenly, Melin felt her throat tighten, and a cold chill ran down her spine.
