Meanwhile, Barry, Diego, and Felix returned to the hotel room, looking battered and worn.

Felix was about to leave, but Barry stopped him.

"Felix, let's have a proper chat," Barry said.

Barry's tone was unusually warm and friendly, which Felix found odd.

Felix wondered, what did Barry want to talk about?

However, Felix, always the sly fox, didn't show his surprise. He simply nodded politely.

"Alright, I'll make some coffee, and we can talk over drinks."

After they entered, Barry plopped down on the couch.

Diego stood by with a grim look, saying nothing.

Felix leisurely prepared the coffee.

Throughout the process, Barry and Diego didn't say a word, both staring intently at Felix.

The room was silent, the atmosphere tense.

As the smell of coffee filled the room, Felix poured cups for Barry and Diego, finally easing the tension on their faces.

A faint, forced smile appeared on Barry's face.

"Felix, thank you for all the funding. You're certainly a friend worth knowing," Barry said.

Felix smirked internally but kept his expression calm.

"No need to thank me; it was just a small amount. Too bad the Red Umbrella Group is under fire. I can't free up much more. Otherwise, you'd still have a chance to turn things around."

Barry laughed. "Haha! Felix, don't worry. Regardless, you have my gratitude."

Since leaving the gaming table, Barry, an infamous gambler, suddenly seemed rational again.

"Joyce isn't just my secretary and lover; we've been close since childhood. She's also tied to the Morris family's honor.

"I'm worried about her. This isn't over. I can't let Joyce spend the night with those two scumbags."

Hearing this, Felix's face shifted slightly.

Barry's words made it clear. He wanted Felix to find a way to bring Joyce back.

This was the Kaylon's territory, and any trouble here would surely bring an onslaught from them.

Felix pretended not to understand, giving an awkward smile without saying much.

Barry laughed. "Hahaha, Felix, don't worry. I'm not asking you to act. Diego and I will handle it. I trust you won't just stand by, right?"

Felix gave Barry a pointed look.

"This is the Kaylon's territory. They're not the ones to mess with.

"You both saw it just now. They have plenty of top fighters and likely some hidden trump cards too. I think going against them here, over one woman, isn't the smartest move."

Barry waved dismissively, looking annoyed.

"Relax. Diego and I will handle it. But you need to drive us out of here. Once we have her, we're heading straight to Kingsdon."

Diego gave Felix a cold stare.

"If I'm not mistaken, you've gained powers and reached level-two metahuman, right?

"So arranging a getaway car should be simple for you. Unless, of course, you don't want to help."

Diego's eyes flashed with menace as he glared at Felix.

Barry dropped his smile, watching Felix with a blank expression.

Felix stayed silent. He wasn't foolish. Barry and Diego's attempt to rescue Joyce would likely stir up the Kaylon, potentially risking lives.

Getting involved could spell disaster for him!

Seeing his hesitation, Barry and Diego exchanged a look, realizing he was reluctant to help.

Narrowing his eyes, Barry asked, "Don't you want our help against Alexander?"

Felix weighed his options carefully, finally nodding in agreement.

"Fine. I'll be the getaway driver, but I'm not fighting."

"Excellent!"

Barry slapped his thigh and pulled out a black sphere about the size of a thumb.

"This is our family's secret weapon. Inside is a swarm of killer bees we've spent countless resources to cultivate! Once the shell cracks, they'll kill whoever it hits.

"Don't worry. The killer bees only attack once."
