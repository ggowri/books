Oswald thumped his chest confidently. "Fiona, I'm all in for you. If those monsters come crashing in, you just stay behind me. I swear, I'd rather die defending you!"

"Shut it! Don't talk like that!"

Fiona's anger flared, her machete once again pressed against Oswald's neck.

Her voice quivered. "You can't die. If anyone's going, it's gotta be me first. You need to keep living..."

Yet, Oswald showed no sign of fear. He snatched the machete from her hand and tossed it to the ground.

The orange glow of the fire illuminated his face, revealing an unprecedented determination.

"Fiona, I'm a man. Hiding behind a woman? That's not how it works!"

Fiona fell silent as tears welled up in her eyes and slipped down her cheeks.

Oswald instinctively pulled her into an embrace.

The commotion caught the attention of those nearby.

"Look at that! Fiona and Oswald are getting cozy!"

"Of course they are; they grew up together. What's so surprising about that?"

"When did you two get together? We had no idea!"

A crowd quickly gathered, laughter and chatter filling the air.

Fiona felt a flutter of shyness, snuggling into Oswald's embrace, resting her head against his chest.

Oswald chuckled, "Well, now the whole world knows, huh? We agreed that nobody gets to die. And after this, we're getting married! You'll give me two—no, three sons!"

"Ugh, who said I'd bear your kids?"

Fiona playfully swatted Oswald's cheek, but her heart melted at his words.

"Ha! You totally will!"

Laughter erupted around them, but suddenly, someone atop the wall shouted, "The beast horde is stirring!"

Panic broke the jovial moment; everyone rushed to the walls, leaving only Fiona and Oswald behind.

"They're coming back!" Fiona clung tightly to Oswald.

Whispering in her ear, he urged, "You can't die! You have to remember our promise—to get married and have kids together!"

Fiona's eyes sparkled with a fierce light as she shyly nodded. "Okay, but you can't die either. We're in this together!"

"Let's go!"

Hand in hand, they marched toward the makeshift wall, weapons ready, both wearing expressions of happiness, their movements steady and calm.

A low growl echoed from the darkness.

Suddenly, a series of terrifying roars erupted, followed by a cacophony of rustling noises.

Under the heavy cloak of night, the sound of chaos drew near, even if the beast horde remained hidden.

Gradually, the noises grew louder and more pronounced.

The rest of the group, aside from Alexander and a few others, felt the tension rising; they could sense the approaching beasts but not see them.

The monsters were drawing closer through the shadows, like an unstoppable tide of terror.

With a quick flick of his wrist, Alexander ordered his men to fire off some flares into the night sky.

Bright trails of light burst into existence, illuminating the darkness for a fleeting moment.

The workers at Louvian Port stood frozen in shock, trembling as the beast horde became apparent—much more terrifying than during the day.

While the previous beast wave consisted mostly of small feral mutations, now they faced an army of established predators.

Tigers, lions, and bears had transformed, their imposing forms swelling to two or three times their original size, as if ancient giants had returned to wreak havoc upon the world.

The chorus of roars from these mutated beasts surged violently, a wave of sound crashing toward the port like a roaring ocean.

Except for Alexander, everyone else, including Norah and Augusta, turned ashen, terror written across their faces.

Some were visibly shaking, unable to control their fear, and a few even found themselves too frightened to hold it together.

"Open fire!"

At Alexander's command, the most dangerous moment had arrived. It was no longer the time to conserve ammunition.

Instantly, everyone began to fire wildly, a barrage of bullets and shells tearing through the air, unleashing a torrent of metal into the horde of beasts.

Phosphorus and incendiary rounds rained down, creating a wall of fire several hundred yards from the city walls.

Mutated beasts were engulfed in flames, burning fiercely. The intense heat illuminated the battlefield, revealing a scene of relentless chaos.

The wave of beasts was far more ferocious than during the day, with countless mutated creatures charging toward the inferno.

Even as their bodies burned, they continued to surge forward, driven by some unseen terror behind them, madly rushing toward Louvian Port.
