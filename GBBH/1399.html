Not long after, Alexander's faint chanting could be heard outside the tent. "We stand at the threshold of battle, defending our homeland, our families, and everything we hold dear..."

Inside the tent, Fermin and Sellers were trembling, their eyes filled with tears.

In the deep mountains on both sides of Hero Gorge, 600 thousand Risunese ran in the valley and swarmed towards the Hrodvik camp like ants, as if it were a delicious cake, but they didn't know it was a hard rock.

The camp wall was built with huge logs and stones. It was about 20 feet high and 6.5 feet wide, with parapets and arrow slits. The camp wall was piled with logs, stones, arrows, pots of feces water, and other defensive tools.

The sun seemed to have hidden somewhere, and the sky was gray.

In the Hrodvik camp, the 80 thousand Hrodvik soldiers included martial arts masters and strong men. Some had already married, and some were still young. There were fathers and sons, and brothers in arms.

They knew they would probably die there, but none of them flinched. They just clenched the swords, spears, halberds, and other weapons in their hands with expressionless faces.

They stared at these approaching enemies over the mountains. The atmosphere was deathly silent and extremely depressing.

Rankin Cahill, a soldier from the crossbow battalion, stood on the camp wall, tightly gripping the powerful crossbow in his hands. There was a hint of nervousness on his young face. There were so many Risunese, all over the mountains and plains, but they were devils. They even killed babies.

The Risunese's strange cries could be faintly heard. But the general hadn't given the order to shoot arrows yet.

Enoch, with a burly figure, held a single-horned lance, and his voice was loud. "Steady! Eight hundred paces. Hold on! Five hundred paces. Shoot!"

Countless arrows shot up into the air, traversed the sky, and then plummeted down like a violent storm. The parabolas brought up sprays of blood, accompanied by the continuous miserable screams from the Risunese.

Enoch shouted, "Four hundred steps. Shoot! Two hundred paces. Free shoot!"

Rankin secretly glanced at the young generals. They seemed to be about the same age as him. They were resolute, firm, and calm.

Rankin was already very familiar with the crossbow in his hands from daily drills. One iron arrow after another whistled out. Seeing those damned Risunese scream and fall, he felt delighted.

The number of Risunese corpses under the camp wall was increasing. The crossbowmen's arms began to feel stiff and numb, and the speed of shooting arrows was getting slower.
