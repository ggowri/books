Leo gestured, and several burly men wheeled out a glass display case.

The display case was made of reinforced steel, and the glass was bulletproof.

Inside the black velvet tray lay a string of yellow beads forming a bracelet, looking rather ordinary and unimpressive.

Leo spoke loudly, "This is the valuable bracelet retrieved from an ancient tomb, with a starting bid of eighty million dollars. Each increment must not be less than five hundred thousand dollars!"

Alexander's eyes lit up. With his energy perception, he sensed a similar aura from this bracelet as from the rare gems.

Could these yellow beads hold the same potential as a top-grade yellow gem for upgrading his energy sword?

Perfect!

Alexander clenched his fist in excitement; he had to win this bid.

"Eighty-one million dollars!"

Someone immediately raised their bid card.

"Eighty-two million dollars!"

Alexander didn't even hesitate, raising the bid by a full million dollars.

In auctions, it's not just about who has deeper pockets; it's about showing confidence. If you come across as determined, you can intimidate others into backing down.

Seeing Alexander join the bidding, several others hesitated.

It was clear he had deep pockets and wasn't about to give up easily.

"Eighty-three million dollars!"

Suddenly, a voice rang out from one of the VIP rooms.

The voice was calm and commanding—definitely someone influential from Tijuanea.

"Eighty-five million dollars!"

Alexander, unbothered, raised the bid by two million.

"Eighty-eight million dollars!"

The voice from the VIP room countered.

"One hundred million dollars!"

Alexander didn't flinch.

The crowd erupted, everyone staring at Alexander, curious about this young, wealthy bidder's background.

Not only had he spent big on a rare gem, but now he was also bidding one hundred million for a bracelet. What kind of deep pockets did he have?

Nate spat on the ground in frustration.

"Idiot. Just because you have money doesn't mean you should waste it!"

But Alexander stayed cool, a faint smile on his face, radiating confidence.

The room quieted again, all eyes on the showdown.

The VIP room remained silent.

After a moment, the door opened, and an elderly man with white hair stepped out, his face stern.

"Can you do me, Leonard Moon, a solid favor?"

The crowd gasped, whispering in excitement.

"It's the famous diviner, Leonard Moon. This bracelet must really be something special, maybe even magical!"

"Is that young guy out of his mind trying to go up against Master Moon?"

"Shh, keep it down! Leonard Moon isn't someone you want to mess with. If he curses you, you're in big trouble!"

"A curse? Yeah, let's not talk about this anymore..."

The room fell silent once again. Everyone looked at Leonard Moon with a mix of awe and fear while feeling a surge of sympathy for Alexander—how unfortunate to cross paths with a character like Moon.

After all, mystics and diviners were an enigma, often rumored to possess curses and dark powers that could harm others.

Leonard Moon saw that Alexander hadn't responded and assumed he was backing down, a satisfied smile forming on his lips.

"This bracelet is very important to me. If you're willing to let it go, I'll owe you a favor. I can even give you a free reading. What do you say?"

Clearly, Alexander raising the bid to one hundred million dollars had surpassed what Leonard was willing to spend.

But Alexander simply smiled.

"Sorry, Master Moon, but this bracelet is important to me too. Let's stick to the rules—may the highest bid win."

Leonard's face darkened, and he said nothing more.

"One hundred million dollars, going once, going twice... sold!"

"Congratulations to this gentleman for winning the valuable bracelet!"

On stage, Leo Jenkins was so excited his voice trembled slightly.

Honestly, they hadn't expected this item to sell for much.

They set the high starting price banking on its antiquity, hoping to snag an uninformed buyer.

But they never imagined it would reach a hundred million dollars—what a windfall!

Leonard Moon stormed back into his VIP room, clearly furious.

Alexander knew, though, that Leonard's eyes were fixed on him, filled with malice. He also noticed Leonard had taken out a deck of silver tarot cards.
