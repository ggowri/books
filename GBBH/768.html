The bodies of Woodrow and Kerwin quickly turned cold.

Taking down two ordinary sorcerers was as effortless as breathing for Alexander.

"Are you alright? Any discomfort?" Alexander asked, turning to Alyssa.

"No, I'm fine. But we should leave—there are still a lot of Mafia around."

Alyssa, still shaken, finally snapped out of her daze at Alexander's words.

She grabbed his hand, pulling him to hurry, but after being tied up for so long, her legs were numb and weak, and she stumbled forward.

Alexander was quick to catch her, pulling her into his arms.

Her soft, alluring body pressed against him, and he couldn't resist letting his hand slide over her chest, giving a gentle squeeze.

"Ah!"

Alyssa gasped, blushing. "Boss, there's danger here! Once we get out, you can touch me all you like."

Her bright, sparkling eyes gazed at him, filled with a mix of worry and admiration.

Before they could move, the sound of hurried footsteps echoed in the hallway outside—a crowd was clearly on its way.

"Alexander Hawthorne! Get out here! You're surrounded; there's no way to escape!" a voice boomed from outside, thick with menace.

"What now, Boss?"

Alyssa's face paled, realizing escape was nearly impossible.

"Don't worry about them. Just stay behind me. I'll handle this," Alexander said, taking her hand and striding forward with confidence.

Alyssa obediently followed, a mix of fear and reassurance in her heart as she watched his fearless stride.

In the hallway stood a dense crowd of dozens, fully armed. Some held guns, others brandished knives, blocking the exit completely.

A fierce, murderous aura filled the air—these were clearly hardened elites who had seen their share of bloodshed.

Even for a skilled fighter, the pressure of facing such a group of killers was undeniable.

Alyssa's face turned pale.

She was skilled herself and trusted Alexander, but the sheer number and ferocity of these opponents made her heart pound with anxiety.

"Don't be scared. They won't lay a finger on you," Alexander reassured her with a smile, giving her cheek a comforting squeeze before pulling her securely behind him.

He stepped forward alone, his broad back shielding Alyssa.

She looked at him, her heart swelling with admiration—this was the kind of man she had always dreamed of.

The leader of the group, a bald, muscular man, sneered as he glared at Alexander.

"Alexander, I didn't think you'd dare meddle in Mandale Town's affairs! You've got your hands in too many places. But this time, there's no escape. Surrender now, or it'll be worse for you."

Though he tried to sound intimidating, the leader's eyes betrayed a hint of fear.

They had just ambushed the Axe, killing over a hundred of their people and kidnapping Alyssa.

Yet Alexander had tracked them down so quickly, and his guards outside were already down. The sorcerers they'd left in the room hadn't even made a sound—a clear sign of their fate.

Beside the bald man stood a figure cloaked in black, his face dark with anger. His voice was icy as he demanded, "Did you kill my two companions?"

Alexander smirked coldly. "You came to Mandale Town to stir up trouble. Didn't think there would be consequences?"

Before he had finished speaking, Alexander attacked, vanishing in an instant.

He reappeared right in front of the cloaked figure, his fist flying toward the man's chest.

The sorcerer was his first target—eliminating him was the priority.

"You insolent fool, how dare you strike first?" the cloaked man roared, his body swelling as he unleashed a surge of power, hurling a punch to meet Alexander's blow.

Boom!

The impact of two fierce forces meeting resounded like a hammer against steel.

The cloaked figure staggered, unable to fully absorb Alexander's power, and Alexander's fist veered slightly, grazing the man's left arm.

The man screamed, blood pouring from the gaping wound on his arm, bone visible beneath torn flesh.

Alexander chuckled, "You're the first sorcerer who's managed to take a punch from me without losing a limb. Be proud."

The cloaked man's face twisted with rage, feeling humiliated.

"Go to hell!"

But Alexander didn't give him a chance to retaliate. He surged forward like lightning, his fist aimed squarely at the man's head.
