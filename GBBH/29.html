Ryan's frame quivered with rage.

He yelled, "Alexander, what gives you the right to judge us? A piece of trash like you at such an elite event?

"How dare you vie with me? I'm the newly appointed vice president. Ms. Hollister personally handed me the invitation. What right do you have to challenge me?"

"Who dares cause a scene here?"

An irate shout cut through the tension.

The Security Chief, Max Blake, marched over with his team, encircling the trio.

Jane brandished the invitation like a shield.

"We're invited! My husband, Ryan Hawkins, is about to become the Saint Group's vice president. This man is Alexander Hawthorne. He's nothing but a gatecrasher!"

Max's stare turned icy as it landed on Alexander.

"Sir, your invitation, please."

With serene confidence, Alexander replied, "I have none. A friend brought me in."

Max was stunned. It wasn't just anyone who could waltz into this exclusive event with guests in tow; it demanded a level of influence that was rare and revered.

He thought, "In the entire Saint Group, only three possessed such power. They were the chairman, Ms. Blackburn, and Ms. Hollister."

"Sir, might I inquire which of our executives you are acquainted with?"

Max's tone remained courteous.

Before Alexander could respond, Jane erupted into mocking laughter.

"How could he be a friend of the Saint Group's executives? That's hilarious. He's my ex, a jobless failure!"

Ryan's sneer was thick with disdain.

"This wretch is bottom-tier scum. He's here with some dark intent."

Alexander ignored them and lifted a glass of red wine from a nearby table, savoring a sip.

"My business is not yours to question."

His audacity stunned the onlookers.

Max's fury mounted.

It was a ball hosted by the new chairman. Max knew that any security lapse could spell disaster for him.

"Take this man to the isolation room for questioning," he ordered.

The guards advanced.

"Stop!"

A melodious command halted them.

Ava appeared as she furiously shielded Alexander.

Ryan's heart skipped with initial joy at seeing his dream woman, only to be consumed by jealousy.

Ava was breathtakingly gorgeous. Her icy demeanor made every other woman seem lackluster.

Jane paled in comparison to Ava. Here, at this ball where beauty was in abundance, Ava was unparalleled, who made even the stars above seem dim.

"Damn it! Alexander plays the gigolo. She must have brought him," Jane spat.

Her heart thrummed with a mix of anxiety and seething anger. The man she had once cast aside was now intimately close with a woman whose affluence and radiant beauty made her feel utterly eclipsed.

The sensation was akin to a child who discarded a toy, bored of its once cherished presence, only to watch in dismay as another child picked it up with eyes full of wonder, treating it like a newfound treasure.

Clutching Alexander's hand, Ava declared with pride, "Alexander is my chosen partner for the dance. I brought him. Any objections?"

Max nearly collapsed in fear.

The Saint Group's rules were unforgiving. To defy one's superiors was to court death itself, presented with a macabre choice: thirteen methods of execution.

Unaware of the gravity, Jane pointed at Ava and venomously cursed, "It's you. You bitch! You take my cast-off and parade him like treasure. Have you never seen a man in all your lives?"

A collective gasp swept through the crowd. At least half of the attendees were well-acquainted with Ava. No one had anticipated that anyone would have the audacity to publicly slight Ava.

Seeing an opportunity to redeem himself, Max struck Jane across the face.

"You're the bitch! Do you wish for death? Ms. Blackburn is the president of the Saint Group!"

Jane covered her cheeks, now aflame with both the sting of the slap and the heat of humiliation. She stood in disbelief.

"Ms. Blackburn? Which Ms. Blackburn?"

Max slapped her again.

Jane stumbled, and her complexion looked ghostly pale.

She thought, "How could Alexander be worthy of Ava, the president of the Saint Group?

This must be a nightmare."

In a moment of desperate disbelief, she pinched her own skin with a reluctant force. The sharp pain that followed was a cruel confirmation of reality.

The crowd's scornful gazes pierced her heart.

Ryan spent a while recovering from his stupor.

He forced a smile, extending his hand toward Ava with sycophantic charm.

"Ms. Blackburn, I'm Ryan Hawkins. I'm to be appointed vice president by the chairman. Let's foster a good working relationship."
