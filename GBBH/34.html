The air thickened with tension as Ava and Oscar faced off. Their confrontation cast a palpable chill over the room.

Sensing the brewing storm, Alexander attempted to diffuse the situation with a forced lightness in his voice.

"Ava, let's not dwell on unpleasantries. Let's sit over there," he said.

With a dismissive glance at Oscar, Ava allowed Alexander to lead her away.

Left standing, Oscar fell silent, but his aged eyes, now sharpened like flint, followed Alexander's retreating figure with a glint of unspoken challenge.

"Let's go and see. Wait until my grandson arrives in Loscity; Ava must dump you."

Amidst this, Alexander whispered to Ava, "Am I really your boyfriend? This feels like a dream."

With a playful snort, Ava replied, "You've just passed the initial test. How far we go depends entirely on you."

Alexander grinned.

"Well, you're my girlfriend for now. We'll figure out the rest as we go," he said.

Hugging her waist, he leaned in for a kiss on her inviting, crimson lips.

Just then, Billy clumsily timed his entrance with a glass of red wine.

"Ms. Blackburn, Mr. Hawthorne, might I toast to you?"

Ava glared at him. "Why are you here?"

Billy hastily offered an explanation.

"Ms. Blackburn, I was deceived by Ryan to challenge Mr. Hawthorne. I donated two million dollars to the Saint Charity Fund to be here tonight, barely securing an invite."

The mention of the sum made his heart wince visibly.

Alexander just smiled, harboring no despise toward Billy.

Clearly, Billy admired the Saint Group, thereby spending the money to expand his network by mingling with the guests at the ball.

Alexander raised his glass, clinking glasses with Billy.

"The past is behind us. Let's look to the future," he proposed.

Billy downed his wine in relief and joy.

"Mr. Hawthorne, you're a man of honor. I'm at your service," Billy declared.

Gazing at Billy, Alexander teased, "So if I call on you in the future, you won't decline?"

Billy was momentarily taken aback and nodded enthusiastically.

Billy thought, "The two million dollars was far from squandered. It helped me befriend Alexander, forging a connection with the Saint Group, a relationship cloaked in secrecy yet far more advantageous than depending on the Blood Gang."

He patted his chest and made a promise.

"Absolutely, Mr. Hawthorne. For you, I'll brave any storm."

"Okay. Go ahead. Leave me and Ms. Blackburn alone."

Alexander waved Billy off.

After apologizing, Billy walked away joyfully.

Ava's gaze was quizzical.

"You're considering him as an ally? Given that he establishes the Wolf Gang, he's capable. But he's also a ruthless snob. Be careful! "

Alexander chuckled.

"Thanks for the concern, honey. I'm not naive."

After a thoughtful pause, Ava turned serious and voiced her concerns.

"Because of me, you're now in the spotlight and at the very center of the storm.

"It's dangerous, Alexander. One misstep could be disastrous.

"So, if you want to ally with Billy, I have no objections. Still, there is cause for caution."

Alexander was silent for a while.

"Don't worry. Give me a year, and I'll give you the grandest wedding."

"Who said anything about marrying you? You're presumptuous!" Ava retorted, though her flushed cheeks betrayed her true feelings.

After a while, others began to drift over to greet Ava respectfully, but they only nodded at Alexander politely.

Those who approached Ava were influential figures from every corner of society. In fact, they regarded Alexander with barely veiled disdain, dismissing him as nothing more than a gigolo unworthy of their attention.

Alexander observed the dance floor, taking in the vibrant scene around him.

Hundreds of guests mingled. They were clustered into more than a dozen small groups, each defined by the weight of their family names and corporate empires.

It struck Alexander as enlightening; he hadn't anticipated that even the wealthy elite would be stratified in such a way.

Just as the atmosphere reached a fever pitch, a sudden crash shattered the revelry. The door swung open with a violent force.

Several security guards from the Saint Group were thrown in.

The room fell into an eerie silence. The laughter and chatter were extinguished in an instant.

Faces turned pale. Who would dare to stir trouble at the very heart of the Saint Group?
