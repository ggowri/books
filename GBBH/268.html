"What! The maintenance fee is this expensive? This is daylight robbery!"

Nick jumped up angrily.

Mia shouted angrily, "Why are you charging us so much? Over $300,000 a year? Why don't you just rob us instead?"

Charles let out a cold laugh.

"Are you playing dumb? The homeowners' association has standard fees. This villa is worth over 40 million dollars. Is this amount really too much?"

As he argued back, he looked at Alexander with confusion. How could someone who could afford such a mansion care about such a small fee?

Besides the maintenance fee, there are also property taxes, which cost hundreds of thousands annually.

Alexander remained calm, a meaningful smile appearing on his face.

"This isn't my concern. The money owed must be paid."

Nick kept yelling, "I don't care! This is robbery! I'm definitely not paying this!

"I'm not paying! What can you do about it? This is my house, and I'll live here. What can you do to stop me?"

Seeing that Alexander remained silent, Charles understood his stance and immediately toughened his tone.

"Mr. Portman, are you trying to stir up trouble? I'm not the one to be trifled with."

Mia adopted a shameless, hysterical stance.

"Who's afraid of you? This house is ours, and we make the rules! You're trying to rob us? Get out of my house!"

Charles was shaking with anger; he'd never seen such shameless people.

He made a call, and within minutes, seven or eight security guards surrounded Nick and Mia, looking aggressive.

"What are you doing? This is my house, private property! Who allowed you to come in?"

Nick tried to rage, but the security guards pinned him down on the table, making him immobile.

"Not paying, huh? Let's give him a beating first."

Charles ordered.

"Don't hit me! No... don't hit me! If you want the maintenance fee, go to Alexander. This is his house; he bought it..."

Nick, who bullied the weak but feared the strong, immediately backed down in fear.

Charles picked up the property documents, sneering.

"The name on these documents isn't Mr. Hawthorne's. It's Nick and Mia. Why are you trying to pin this on Mr. Hawthorne?

"No one has ever dared to default. Break a leg first, then seize the house!"

Charles threw the papers at Nick's face, looking as fierce as a man-eating tiger.

"Don't hit us! Please, Mom, help us! Say something and get Alexander to help us."

Nick and Mia begged desperately for mercy.

Lily felt conflicted; after all, they were her son and his wife.

She couldn't help but plead with Alexander, "Alexander, for my sake, forgive them this once."

Alexander sighed inwardly. Having such a son was indeed unfortunate for his aunt.

He grabbed Nick by the collar and lifted him up, coldly asking, "Is this house yours? Do you still want $20,000 a month?"

Nick shook his head repeatedly.

"The house isn't ours, and we no longer want the money. I was wrong, Alexander, please forgive us... I beg you..."

Alexander scoffed. "If you don't want a beating, then get lost. Don't let me see you again."

Nick initially wanted to plead with Lily to let him stay in the villa. When she turned away, he was disappointed.

"Mr. Hawthorne doesn't want to see you. If you don't leave now, I'll use force."

Charles grinned viciously and waved his hand, and the security guards prepared to act.

Nick and Mia fled in panic.

Charles then bowed respectfully to Alexander, apologizing, "Mr. Hawthorne, I apologize for my rudeness earlier."

"You did well, very satisfying. I'll cover all the fees from now on."

He then instructed Betty to transfer money to Charles.

After receiving the payment, Charles became even more courteous. He pulled out a business card from his pocket and handed it over with both hands.

"Mr. Hawthorne, according to our homeowners' association rules, whenever one of our owners encounters trouble, we'll help resolve it."

The homeowners' association in this wealthy neighborhood has a strong background and considerable influence.
