"Wait here," Alexander ordered, striding toward the dark passage. "I'll check for any threats inside."

"Watch yourself in there, Alexander," Quentin called after him.

Even though they knew Alexander was a total badass, he was still on edge. After all, this was the Sakra Society's secret base. Who knew what kind of nasty surprises they had stashed away in there?

Alexander just flashed a smile and waved off their concerns as he stepped inside.

The secret passage was pitch black, but that didn't slow Alexander down one bit. His eyes adjusted instantly, taking in every detail of the tunnel.

He hadn't gone more than a few yards before he'd already stopped three times to disable deadly traps.

One wrong move from any unlucky intruder would've triggered a barrage of gunfire or set off an explosion.

The tunnel spiraled down like a corkscrew, with Alexander following the stairs deeper and deeper underground.

After descending about 65 feet, he finally hit the bottom. A heavy-duty blast door made of reinforced steel blocked the way forward.

Motion sensors picked up his presence, and fluorescent lights flickered to life overhead, flooding the space with harsh light.

As he approached the door, a keypad lit up automatically, waiting for input.

Alexander ran through the code in his head before punching in each number carefully.

He stayed alert the whole time, ready for anything to go sideways.

A series of mechanical clicks echoed through the air as the steel door slowly slid open.

Alexander let out a breath he hadn't realized he'd been holding. Sure, he could've used his Lightsabers to force his way in if the code hadn't worked, but that might have triggered the self-destruct sequence.

Everything inside would've been destroyed, and the whole mission would've been for nothing.

Once the door was fully open, Alexander did a quick sweep of the room. When nothing looked out of place, he stepped inside.

The secret chamber was pretty small, maybe a dozen square meters, and practically empty except for a lit-up temperature-controlled cabinet.

Inside the cabinet sat a few vials filled with reddish liquid.

Alexander couldn't help but grin. These had to be Sakra Society's enhancement drugs. One of them might even be the pure Immortality Serum they'd been looking for.

If they could get these to their research team, they might finally crack the code on eliminating the serum's nasty side effects.

Alexander radioed Quentin to join him downstairs.

When Quentin made it to the chamber and spotted the vials, his face lit up like a kid on Christmas morning. This raid had definitely paid off.

"Alexander, you've really hit the jackpot here. If this really is pure Immortality Serum, we might be able to develop an antidote," Quentin said excitedly.

"Then even if Yinson creates more zombies or those living dead, we'll have a way to cure them—maybe even prevent it altogether." He was practically bouncing as he clapped Alexander on the shoulder.

While they were talking, Alexander spotted something interesting—a hidden safe tucked away next to the cabinet.

When they cracked it open, they found it packed with gold bars. There had to be at least a few dozen of them, worth a small fortune.

"Alexander," Quentin piped up, "why don't you keep these gold bars off the books? Consider it a bonus for helping us out."

Alexander picked up one of the bars with a slight smile, testing its weight. Each one felt like it was about 2.2 pounds of pure gold. A few dozen bars of gold would fetch a pretty penny, but money was the last thing he needed right now.

"Tell you what, Sir Thompson," Alexander said. "How about we trade these gold bars for some weapons and ammo to send to Treasure Island?"

Quentin hesitated, his expression turning serious. "Alexander... do you really think Yinson's 'Apocalypse Project' has a shot at succeeding?

"We'll definitely find a cure. Yinson's 'Apocalypse Project' is bound to fail."

Alexander didn't argue. Instead, he replied with a serious tone, "Sir Thompson, if we had more people like you in the top brass, Yinson would definitely go down.

"But you know what's been happening lately. More and more bigwigs are banking on Yinson's Immortality Serum. They're scared of death. They want to live longer, to cheat the clock.

"That's why I think Yinson's plans won't just fail. They're going to speed up."

Quentin couldn't argue with that. Sure, this raid had been greenlit by military command, but it had faced massive pushback. The officers pushing for this operation were just a small group. Most of the brass wanted nothing to do with it.
