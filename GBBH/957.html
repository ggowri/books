Lennox's fists were clenched as he shoved Ryder back.

"So what if the Vance family's involved? This is Loscity, not Kingsdon! As long as I'm in charge here, no one's touching this grave. You want to dig it up? You'll be out of a job before you can blink!"

He turned to the workers holding shovels, his voice rising.

"Get out of here, all of you. You really want to risk your livelihoods for this?"

The workers froze, realizing the gravity of the situation.

Lennox might still be the manager, and he meant it seriously.

The cemetery jobs paid well, and getting fired would be a devastating blow to both their income and their families.

Ryder, however, just smirked, unfazed.

"You're not going to last as manager for long, Lennox. A single call to Sir Vance, and forget about your job. He'll buy the whole cemetery if he wants. You think you can stop him?

"In two days, I'll have that grave dug up, and that manager's chair will be mine. Let's see who ends up getting kicked out."

With that, he turned on his heel, his men reluctantly following behind.

Alexander's gaze followed them, cold as ice.

Lennox had saved Ryder today. If he'd been a few seconds too late, Ryder wouldn't have walked away.

Lennox sighed, rubbing his temples, clearly conflicted.

"You all saw what just happened. If I were you, I'd think about moving the remains. The Vance family is serious. Once they set their sights on something, it's over.

"If they do buy this cemetery, there's not much I can do for you."

Lily's face tightened with worry. She nodded quickly.

"You're right, Mr. Wilder. Thank you for everything. We'll move the grave as soon as possible."

But Alexander wasn't having it. "This is my mother's grave. She's already laid to rest. No one, not even the Vance family, will move her."

Lennox, looking even more exasperated, shook his head.

"You don't get it, do you? The Vance family is one of the most powerful in Kingsdon. You're going up against people who can crush you without even trying.

"Please, take my advice. Move the grave while you still can. If they buy this place, you won't even have that option anymore."

Lennox sighed, turning to walk away, but Alexander's voice stopped him.

"Don't worry, Mr. Wilder. You'll still be in charge here."

Lennox didn't even look back, but the way he shook his head said it all. He wasn't convinced.

Ava, who had been quiet until now, whispered, "Mr. Wilder really is trying to help. He's a good person."

Lily's anxiety had reached a boiling point. She grabbed Alexander's hand, pleading.

"Alexander, maybe we should move the grave, just to be safe. If they start digging it up, it'll be too late."

Alexander's eyes burned with cold fury. "Anyone who touches my mother's grave won't live to see another day."

Lily, teary-eyed, looked up at him desperately.

"Alexander, you can't kill anyone. If you do, you'll end up in prison. The Vance family has been a major force in Loscity for years. Now, they've moved to Kingsdon and grown even more powerful. We can't fight them.

"This isn't about pride. If we wait too long, they'll dig up the grave. How can your mother rest in peace if that happens?"

Her voice broke as she wiped her tears away.

Alexander sighed heavily, his hand resting on the worn stone of the grave.

"Lily, don't worry. No one is touching my mom's grave. I promise you that."

His voice was low but firm, the kind of certainty that only someone who had made up their mind could have.

"Mom, don't worry. If anyone dares touch you, they'll regret it."

Lily, overwhelmed, just shook her head. "Why are you so stubborn?"

Alexander gave a faint smile. Despite the tension, he appreciated Lily's concern. She was always looking out for him.

Lily knew Alexander's stubbornness all too well.

Once he set his mind on something, nothing could change it. She knew pushing him further would only make things worse.

With a heavy sigh, she accepted it and stopped trying to argue.

"Fine, fine. We'll wait until the day after tomorrow. We'll bring more family members. Even if I have to fight them, I'll protect this grave!"

But Ava, still frowning, wasn't convinced. The situation was spiraling out of control. The next few days could bring a storm unlike anything they were prepared for.
