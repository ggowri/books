Alexander glanced around the casino floor, a confident smile playing on his lips. His sharp eyes quickly sized up the action, effortlessly discerning who was destined to win and who was set to lose.

Every alluring female dealer had her own arsenal of tricks—some relied on skillful sleight of hand, while others leaned on advanced technology hidden in plain sight.

It was no secret: most gambling was rigged. The house always ensured its bottom line. Occasionally, though, a true gambling prodigy would appear, winning big before slipping away. And then there were the legends—those rare, masterful gamblers capable of taking down an entire casino.

Casually, Alexander draped his arm over Norma's waist, his hand resting comfortably on her soft, curvaceous hips.

Norma's smile was tight, masking her discomfort. She needed Alexander's help and didn't dare let her irritation show.

Alexander chuckled inwardly. Norma was cunning, sly as a fox. But even the craftiest fox couldn't escape the hunter's trap.

Having pieced together bits of Norma's true intentions, Alexander let his guard down slightly, amused by the game they were both playing.

Just then, a man rushed over, his face flushed with urgency.

"Boss! That guy is back. He's on a winning streak again—already taken two hundred million dollars. And this time, it looks like he's not stopping. What should we do?"

The man hesitated, then added in a hushed voice, "Should we... take care of him?" He made a throat-slitting gesture.

Norma frowned deeply.

"A gambling master?" Alexander asked, raising an eyebrow with an intrigued smile.

Norma's expression shifted. Her face flushed momentarily, then returned to its usual composed demeanor. She let out a slow breath.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I apologize. Something urgent has come up that requires my attention..."

"No need to apologize," Alexander said smoothly, his tone laced with curiosity. "In fact, I'd like to see this gambling master in action myself."

Norma hesitated for only a moment before nodding. Together, they entered a private elevator and ascended to the casino's top-floor surveillance room.

The walls were lined with dozens of LCD screens, forming an expansive mosaic of live footage from every corner of the casino.

"In this casino, we have over a thousand surveillance cameras," Norma explained as they entered. "They provide round-the-clock, comprehensive monitoring of every guest and every inch of the property."

Alexander smirked slightly. If this opponent was truly a gambling master, no amount of surveillance would reveal their methods.

As Norma stepped into the room, everyone inside rose to their feet.

"Good evening, Boss!" they said in unison.

"Have your seats and get back to work," Norma said briskly, her commanding tone leaving no room for delay.

Norma led Alexander to the largest screen in the room, which displayed a live feed from one of the casino's VIP suites.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I might need your advice on how to handle this situation," she said, glancing at him.

The staff exchanged curious looks. Their boss rarely treated men with such deference. Who was this Alexander that he warranted such courtesy?

Norma gestured toward the screen, her expression growing serious.

"This is one of our VIP rooms," she explained. "To even qualify as a VIP, a guest must spend at least one million dollars. It's no exaggeration to say that each VIP here is worth hundreds of regular customers."

The screen showed a room so lavish it could rival the finest presidential suites in the world. In the center was a luxurious card table surrounded by seven men engaged in a high-stakes poker game. Each was flanked by a glamorous, provocatively dressed woman.

Alexander's gaze swept across the scene, lingering briefly on a few familiar faces—a famous actor, a chart-topping singer, and a prominent entrepreneur. He hadn't expected to see them here.

Norma pointed at the screen, her tone sharpening. "That white-haired man at the center is the one causing all the trouble."

Her playful, coquettish demeanor from earlier had vanished, replaced by a tiger-like ferocity.

Alexander leaned in, studying the man on the screen.

At that moment, the actor, singer, and entrepreneur threw their cards down in frustration. They rose from the table, their expressions sour as they stormed off, clearly having lost heavily.

Alexander's interest deepened.
