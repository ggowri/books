Alexander's mind drifted to the gentle visage of Frank, and with a wistful sigh, he picked up the phone.

Frank's voice quivered as he spoke, "Alexander, I've just learned that Jane coerced you into the divorce. I am truly sorry. This is a failing of our family."

Alexander offered a smile, uttering, "Sir Fairfax, that's all in the past. I'd rather not revisit it."

"Alexander, for Ashley's sake, could you perhaps remarry Jane?" Frank asked with a note of hope in his tone.

Alexander's response was immediate and resolute.

"There's no turning back for Jane and me, but I will care for Ashley well and ensure she lacks for nothing."

Frank fell silent for a moment before letting out a deep sigh.

"Alexander, my health is deteriorating. I fear my time is short. Could you come to see me one last time?"

Alexander pondered the request, aware that Frank's cerebral infarction and ongoing brain capillary bleeding and blockage left him with little time.

If the Force could be used to clear his blood vessels, perhaps Frank could live a few more years.

"Alright, I'll come to bid you farewell later."

After Alexander agreed, Frank audibly relaxed.

Half an hour later, Alexander arrived at the Fairfax Villa, a place that evoked both familiarity and disdain.

As he surveyed the villa, memories of the past five years flooded his mind, including the humiliations endured and the fleeting joys.

So much had transpired in just two days, but it felt like a lifetime had passed.

Forgetting all this and healing the trauma swiftly was impossible; only time could dull the pain.

Yet, thoughts of his adorable daughter and Ava's radiant smile filled Alexander with anticipation for the future.

With a somber expression, Alexander strode into the Fairfax Villa.

He intended to leave immediately after treating Frank, not lingering for even a moment.

The Fairfax family was in high spirits, drinking and laughing.

Praises for Andrew and fawning over Jane filled the air.

Frank sat on the sofa with a grim expression.

"Alexander, you're here! Come in and sit down!"

Frank stood up with unbridled joy as he caught sight of Alexander. His voice boomed with warmth as he greeted Alexander.

"Sir Fairfax, there's really no need for me to sit down," Alexander calmly replied.

The living room fell silent, and the atmosphere suddenly became tense.

"Alexander, what are you doing here? Weren't you in the hospital after that nasty hit from Billy?" Jane asked as she gaped at Alexander in surprise.

Frank interjected quickly, "Jane, I called Alexander to see me."

Jane scoffed coldly.

"Grandpa, he's the reason Ryan was hospitalized. I've divorced him. Why do you still want him here?"

Andrew chimed in angrily.

"Seize him! He's the one who harmed Ryan. He's not leaving until he reveals how to lift the curse!"

A group of people encircled Alexander menacingly to gain favor with Ryan.

Frank swiftly intervened, holding them back.

Andrew's anger boiled over, and he began to berate, "Dad, have you lost your mind? He's our enemy!"

Alexander's gaze was icy as he surveyed the scene.

"Sir Fairfax, I'll help unblock your blood vessels and then I'll take my leave," he stated calmly.

"Help my grandpa heal? You're just trying to curse him again, aren't you?"

Jane quickly pulled Frank away.

Frank was frantic, tears welling in his eyes. He grasped Jane's hand and pleaded, "Jane, Alexander is a good man. For Ashley's sake, could you remarry Alexander?"

"Remarriage? Impossible. Alexander, I truly despise you. You actually had my grandfather act as your matchmaker. How despicable!"

Jane laughed mockingly.

She waved the gold-embossed invitation card triumphantly.

"See this? It's the invitation sent by the Saint Group to my fiancé, who's just been appointed as their vice president.

"Alexander, I advise you to lift the curse immediately and beg for our forgiveness.

"For Ashley's sake, I'll give you one chance, or you'll die without a proper burial!"

Jane threatened menacingly.

Alexander couldn't help but feel a surge of amusement.

"Are you certain Ryan will become the vice president? Perhaps the chairman of the Saint Group is merely toying with you."
