Alexander quietly slipped out of Lynn's bedroom, glancing at the clock. It was already past eight.

He had heard Ava come home the previous night, right in the midst of his and Lynn's intense second round.

Lynn's moans had practically filled the entire house. Ava had certainly heard them.

Entering the kitchen, Alexander saw Ava standing with her back to him, busy preparing breakfast.

"Heh, my wife's making me breakfast. Just what I need—I'm actually pretty hungry."

Ava wore a seductive, silky nightgown, her long, fair legs exposed, with the gown barely covering her hips, though her figure was unmistakable.

Alexander felt a sudden rush of excitement.

He and Lynn spent a wonderful night and absorbed a lot of the Force left by James. He was in a good mood and wanted to make further progress.

"Good morning!"

He crept up behind her and wrapped his arms around her waist.

"Ah!"

Ava let out a soft yelp.

"Why sneaking up like that—you scared me!"

Alexander took a deep breath, inhaling her scent.

"Smells wonderful!"

"I'm cooking spaghetti; of course, it smells good."

"I meant you smell wonderful."

"Hey, quit it! I'm cooking..." she protested, blushing and trying to bat his hand away.

But Alexander's hands slid beneath her gown. Ava had clearly just woken up and hadn't bothered with a bra.

"Stop that! Didn't get enough last night? You're insatiable!"

Ava was speechless.

Alexander sensed a trace of jealousy in her tone.

"Silly, it's not the same when you're not there."

Touched, Ava reached up to wrap her arms around his neck, returning his kiss.

"Quit it; I'm making breakfast. Aren't we heading back to Loscity today? Ashley and Charlize both need us..."

He nuzzled her ear with a grin. "We're leaving tomorrow. Tonight, how about you both keep me company?"

"You...you are so perverted!"

She blushed deeply, wishing she could disappear.

Just then, footsteps echoed down the stairs, and Lynn came down, her cheeks still flushed from the night.

"Aha, my girl's back..."

Lynn's face glowed with a shy smile, her head lowered like a bashful schoolgirl.

"Mom, breakfast is almost ready," Ava replied, grinning as she noticed how much better her mother looked compared to just a day ago.

Lynn suddenly hugged her tightly, her eyes misty. "Thank you, Ava, for my birthday gift."

Ava rested her cheek on her mother's shoulder, her face radiating joy.

They held each other in silence, both savoring the moment without the need for words.

Suddenly, Alexander's phone rang, its shrill tone interrupting the tranquility.

He glanced at the screen—it was an unknown number.

Feeling a sense of foreboding, he hesitated but answered.

"Alexander? This is Rock Rossetti, Elder of the City of Sorcery," an old, raspy voice said on the other end.

Alexander raised an eyebrow, smirking slightly. "Well, Mr. Rossetti, what do you need?"

After a brief pause, Rock's voice hardened. "Alexander, I'm calling on behalf of the City of Sorcery. I want you to return the Ancestral Witch's Cane and the Spirit Bead Bracelet. They rightfully belong to us.

"If you surrender them willingly, I'll overlook your prior offenses—killing Guzman, Drake, and injuring Leonard."

Alexander's smirk grew wider.

"Since when is Leonard one of your people? I thought he was just a diviner?"

"Enough! Will you return them, or not?"

Rock's impatience was evident.

"You think defying the City of Sorcery will end well? Besides, the Ancestral Witch's Cane and Spirit Bead Bracelet are useless in your hands."

Alexander sneered, "I don't respond to threats. Once something's in my hands, no one's taking it back that easily.

"Others may fear your City of Sorcery, but I don't. If you were truly as powerful as you say, would I even be standing here?"

"You!" Rock spluttered, momentarily speechless.

A moment later, he began to chuckle darkly, "Alexander, don't get cocky. You've won a few battles and think you're untouchable. Here's a suggestion: go home and check on your daughter."
