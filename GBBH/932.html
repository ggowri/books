The armed helicopter loomed above, its firepower utterly overwhelming. Escape seemed impossible.

Yvonne's witchcraft was useless against the chopper; all she could do was brace for impact.

"The Mafia's really underestimating me? Just one measly attack helicopter?" Alexander's calm smile betrayed a quiet confidence.

Without a hint of panic, he crouched and picked up a few fist-sized stones from the roadside, testing their weight in his hands.

"Gulp." Yvonne swallowed hard, her eyes widening in disbelief.

Is he planning to fight a helicopter with rocks?

She glanced nervously at the chopper. Its undercarriage bristled with air-to-ground missiles, rockets, and heavy machine guns—the kind of arsenal capable of obliterating main battle tanks.

The convoy was nothing more than sitting ducks.

In the helicopter, a bald man sneered.

"Kill them. Now!" he barked at the pilot. "Alexander might be tough, but let's see him take on this bird!"

The Mafia had placed an enormous bounty on Alexander—enough to make him the top prize for any ambitious member. Whoever killed him would not only pocket millions of dollars but also secure a spot in the Mafia's upper echelon.

The pilot grinned, throttling up as the helicopter closed in on Alexander.

On his screen, the missiles locked onto the convoy.

All it would take was a single button press to unleash hell.

But then, something unexpected happened. Alexander swung his arm in a wide arc, hurling one of the stones toward the chopper.

The bald man burst out laughing. "Is he insane? He thinks a rock can stop us? What an idiot! Fire the missiles already and end this clown!"

The pilot shared in the amusement, his mind already counting the fortune he'd earn for taking down Alexander.

"Boom!"

A thunderous impact rattled the helicopter. It wobbled violently, its forward motion halting as it began to tilt erratically.

"What the hell was that? We're hit!" The pilot's voice was laced with disbelief.

"Don't just sit there! Shoot him! Fire the missiles!" the bald man roared, his smug grin replaced by panic.

As the pilot reached for the launch button, another rock—this one flung with uncanny precision—smashed through the windshield and struck him square in the face.

Blood splattered across the cockpit as the pilot's head all but disintegrated.

His lifeless body slumped over the controls, leaving the bald man drenched in gore and paralyzed with shock.

Before he could recover, a third stone, imbued with crushing force, punched through the helicopter's lower frame and obliterated the control console.

"No! I don't want to die!" the bald man screamed in terror, desperately clawing at the joystick in a futile attempt to regain control.

The helicopter spun wildly, its descent becoming an uncontrollable spiral.

With alarms blaring and the ground rushing up, the chopper plummeted like a drunken giant, finally crashing into a grove of trees.

An enormous explosion erupted, sending a fireball skyward and igniting the forest in a roaring inferno.

Yvonne and the others stared in stunned silence, their disbelief mirrored in their wide eyes. Alexander had just brought down a heavily armed attack helicopter with nothing but a handful of stones.

"Unbelievable... Truly remarkable..." Yvonne exhaled, her voice trembling with awe.

Her gaze softened, filled with adoration. "You really are incredible, Master..."

Alexander brushed off the praise with a dismissive grunt. "It's not over yet. Looks like the Mafia's got a bigger foothold in Tijuanea than I thought. Damn Timothy—what a useless idiot."

He cast his eyes toward the distant road, where another convoy of vehicles approached at high speed.

Timothy, the Mafia's local leader in Tijuanea, had long been under Alexander's control.

If the Mafia was mobilizing this kind of force without warning, it could only mean one thing—Timothy had betrayed him.
