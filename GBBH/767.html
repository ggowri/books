The two sorcerers exchanged glances, fear and dread evident in their eyes.

The silence outside confirmed that the guards had all been taken down.

Woodrow and Kerwin stood on high alert, facing Alexander as if confronted by an insurmountable mountain that might come crashing down at any moment.

Alexander gave Alyssa a quick look, relieved to find her unharmed.

Mandale Town was still in need of her, and if he was to maintain control there, Alyssa was indispensable.

"Boss, you... you came to rescue me?"

Alyssa's eyes filled with tears.

Ignoring Woodrow and Kerwin, Alexander walked straight toward Alyssa.

The two thugs retreated, beads of cold sweat forming on their foreheads, feeling a pressure unlike any they'd ever known.

"Now that I see you're alright, I can relax."

Alexander smiled and gently untied the ropes around Alyssa.

"Boss, how... how did you know I was here?" Alyssa asked, curiosity and confusion flickering in her gaze.

"I have my ways. Rest for a moment and watch me handle this," he said in a calm tone, as if Woodrow and Kerwin were no more than helpless chickens waiting for the slaughter.

"Thank you, Boss!"

Alyssa looked as though she might throw herself into his arms at any moment.

Her feelings for Alexander had started as admiration and fear, along with a desire to secure her own interests by staying close to him.

But now, it was different. His timely rescue had stirred something deep within her, an affection she could hardly put into words.

This powerful leader of the Axe gang was, at this moment, reduced to gazing dreamily at Alexander, her thoughts in a daze.

Alexander chuckled, understanding her reaction.

After all, she'd been on the brink of torture and despair, only to be saved at the very last moment.

Her growing attachment was only natural.

Woodrow felt deeply insulted as Alexander ignored him entirely, laughing and chatting with Alyssa instead.

He spat angrily, "Alexander, since you've walked right in, you might as well stay here with that bitch!"

Kerwin quickly chimed in, his eyes gleaming with greed, "Once we take him down, we'll be handsomely rewarded!"

Alexander's gaze turned cold as he strode toward them, his deep eyes swirling like whirlpools, as if ready to draw them in.

Woodrow instinctively took a step back, clutching a dark rod with a vicious grip.

"Stop! Don't come any closer! Take another step, and I'll finish you off!"

A sinister black mist began to rise from the rod, swirling around Woodrow's body, exuding a nauseating stench.

With his All-seeing Eyes, Alexander saw the mist for what it was—a swarm of tiny, hair-thin witchcraft insects, twisted and menacing, in countless numbers.

Yet Alexander did not slow his steps. He continued his approach, his eyes flashing with a fierce glint as he commanded, "Kneel down and await your end!"

The words held an imperial authority, like an emperor issuing orders to his subjects.

"I'll make you kneel, you bastard!"

Woodrow bellowed, swinging the rod and directing the black mist toward Alexander.

With a wave of his hand, Alexander conjured a blaze of white flames that instantly turned the mist to ash.

The Force-fueled flames had reached a temperature so intense that the witchcraft insects disintegrated in moments.

With a swift move, Alexander seized Woodrow's rod, snapping it in two.

A stream of black gas escaped, spilling onto Woodrow's face as the insects crawled rapidly into his eyes, nose, ears, and mouth.

"Ah!"

Woodrow let out a blood-curdling scream, collapsing to the ground, writhing in agony.

In seconds, his skin turned black, rotting as dark blood oozed out.

"Serves you right. A fitting end."

Alexander kicked Woodrow's corpse aside, turning his gaze on the terrified Kerwin.

Kerwin began to stammer, but Alexander moved with lightning speed, grabbing him by the head and twisting it sharply.

Crack!

A loud crack echoed as Kerwin's neck broke, his face twisted backward, his lifeless eyes staring wide as his body crumpled to the floor.
