The sheer power of the force was terrifying. It could not only change a person's size in seconds but even alter their appearance.

It was the stuff of legends, beyond anything anyone had ever seen or heard.

Alexander slipped through the grating with ease, entering the ventilation shaft.

Using his hands and feet to brace himself, he descended the steep walls with the agility of a giant gecko, moving swiftly and silently downward.

The further he went, the more shocked he became. Dozens of feet underground, and yet the shaft still seemed bottomless.

The walls were riddled with branching pipes leading to other levels, but Alexander didn't stop.

He was certain that the base's most vital research facility was buried at the very bottom.

He continued his descent without hesitation. When he passed three hundred feet, the shaft still stretched endlessly below him.

This base was so large it had hollowed out the mountain itself. The scale of the construction was almost incomprehensible.

Finally, after descending another few dozen feet, Alexander reached the base of the ventilation shaft.

At the bottom was a single narrow conduit. Keeping his movements precise and noiseless, Alexander slipped into it.

As he crawled through the passage, his mind quickly calculated.

He had passed at least forty branching pipes on his way down, meaning this underground facility had a minimum of forty levels, reaching depths close to six hundred feet. It was more than a base. It was an underground fortress.

A cold smirk curled Alexander's lips. Once he rescued Hazel, he'd make sure this entire place was reduced to rubble.

And if Sakra Society's leaders and their living dead experiments were here, all the better. He'd take them all out in one blow.

Moving with practiced ease, Alexander advanced, his x-ray vision scanning the surrounding walls and passages. Everything seemed normal so far.

He reached a vent cover, opened it silently, and dropped into a corridor. Without missing a beat, he straightened his posture and began walking confidently.

The lower levels were undoubtedly under heavy security. However, reaching this depth without detection meant no one would suspect him now.

As expected, Alexander encountered several uniformed staff along the way. They exchanged brief glances, but no one stopped him. They simply moved on.

The walls were lined with doors, many of which were marked with faded codes and numbers.

The design suggested the base had been here for decades, likely a remnant of World War II. Sakra Society must have revamped it, transforming it into what they now called Base One.

Eventually, Alexander stopped in front of a steel door guarded by two heavily armed sentries. Whatever lay beyond this door had to be of critical importance.

"ID," one of the guards demanded.

Alexander didn't bother answering. Before they could react, he struck both guards down in a blur of motion, leaving them unconscious on the ground.

Dragging their bodies into a shadowed corner, he stripped one of the guards of his uniform and slipped it on.

Now disguised, Alexander moved with even greater confidence. He pushed open the steel door and stepped through.

The corridor on the other side was wider and far more modern. The sleek design was proof of recent renovations by Sakra Society.

The guards here were different too, taller, more muscular, their presence radiating danger. These were elite fighters, not just regular sentries.

Yet no one questioned Alexander. This was the deepest level of the base, accessible only after multiple layers of identity verification, like passwords, fingerprints, and retinal scans.

The idea of someone infiltrating this far was unthinkable.

Calm and composed, Alexander strolled down the corridor, his x-ray vision scanning the rooms as he went.

It wasn't long before he discovered a massive hall spanning over a thousand square meters. The room was packed with strange machinery and rows of glass cylinders.

These cylinders were filled with a green liquid and contained unsettling contents, human organs, mutated specimens, and other grotesque forms.

At the center of the hall were several coffin-like glass tanks.

Each tank was hooked up to numerous monitors and instruments, and within the green liquid lay human bodies, perfectly preserved, lifelike, and horrifyingly intact.

Alexander's expression darkened. These weren't ordinary experiments. They were living dead.

Near one of the tanks, a group of scientists in white hazmat suits had gathered.

They were huddled around a woman who was speaking animatedly, her voice commanding their full attention.

Alexander recognized her immediately. It was Elaine.

Back in Loscity, during the plague outbreak, she had posed as an expert from Risun, publicly ridiculing Alexander and trying to seize his resources.
