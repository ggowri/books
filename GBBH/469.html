Kirk Harris' face turned a deep shade of red, clearly embarrassed.

"Ms. Blackburn, times are tough for me right now. I have to make every opportunity count. Please give the gemstone back, and I'll make some sort of compensation to Alexander."

"Compensation?" Alexander scoffed, not even pretending to give the gemstone back.

Harris stiffened, barking out, "Security! Get in here!"

"Mr. Harris, what do you need?"

A few burly security guards rushed in, looking aggressive as they surrounded Alexander.

Alexander didn't even flinch; he just gave a smirk.

"Mr. Harris, maybe you should be more worried about your health. Trouble's coming your way, and your life might be on the line."

"Don't try to scare me, Alexander! Treasures belong to those who deserve them. The gemstone is mine, and it's up to me if I want to sell it or not."

Feeling the backup behind him, Harris puffed up, growing bolder.

"Step aside." Alexander waved his hand casually.

Suddenly, the guards were thrown back as if hit by a truck, crashing hard to the floor.

Alexander simply dusted off his hands, as though swatting at some flies.

"Harris, you're about to have a heart attack. Get to a hospital if you want to live. If it wasn't for this gemstone, I wouldn't have bothered to warn you.

"And if you keep pushing me, you won't like what happens next."

A chilling aura radiated from Alexander, dropping the temperature around them significantly.

"W-what are you trying to do? There are cameras here..."

Harris felt like he had just been plunged into freezing water, his body trembling uncontrollably as he struggled to speak.

Alexander ignored him, taking Ava's hand and turning to leave.

Lynn shot Harris a scathing look before quickly following Alexander.

Harris' face twisted in anger, but he didn't dare make another move.

"Ridiculous! Since when do I have heart problems? He's just trying to curse me! Stealing my gemstone and cursing me—unbelievable."

"You'll regret this! Just wait!"

He pulled out his phone, ready to call for backup, completely dismissing Alexander's warning.

"Ahh!"

Suddenly, a sharp pain seized his chest. Moments later, his entire left side went numb, and he felt paralyzed.

Clutching his chest, Alexander's words echoed in his head. He tried to call for help, but no sound came out—it was too late for regrets.

"Mr. Harris, what are your orders?" a voice asked, sounding concerned.

"Help... my heart..."

With that, Harris collapsed heavily onto the floor.

Outside Dragonstone Gem Company, Lynn kept apologizing.

"Alex, I'm really sorry. I had no idea Kirk was like that. I guess you never really know people."

Alexander gave her an easy smile. "It's alright. I already called an ambulance for him. Consider it even for the gemstone."

Lynn eyed Alexander curiously.

"Alex, how exactly did you know this was a high-quality gemstone?"

"Maybe just luck..."

Alexander gave a mysterious smile, offering no further explanation.

Lynn rolled her eyes playfully. At that moment, she looked stunning—even Alexander, who had seen plenty of beautiful women, felt his heart skip a beat.

He couldn't help but think Ava was right—her mother really did have a charm that was hard to resist.

Feeling guilty about what had happened, Lynn insisted on helping Alexander search the market for more gems.

However, aside from the ruby Alexander had found, their efforts turned up nothing.

"Alex, there's nothing more we can do now. We'll have to wait for the auction—there'll definitely be some amazing pieces up for grabs." Lynn sighed.

"Thank you for your help, Ms. Blackburn. I really appreciate it."

Alexander meant it.

"It's getting late. Why don't we grab some food? Oh, and I can introduce you both to my fiancé."

Lynn blushed slightly as she spoke.

Ava's eyes widened in surprise. The last of her worries vanished—knowing her mom had found a stable partner was comforting.

The three of them headed to a restaurant in Loscity, where a distinguished middle-aged man was already waiting.

He wore a well-tailored suit, standing tall and proud. With his confident air and polished appearance, it was no wonder Lynn had fallen for him.
