Xavier struggled desperately, his voice cracking with fear.

"Please, don't do this! My mom is Julia, a well-known gem dealer in Loscity. I'm begging you... don't skin me alive..."

Panic had taken hold of him, and even Jennifer trembled at Evander's intimidating presence.

"My mom has a lot of money! She has tons of gems. If you let me go, I'll give each of you a gem!" Xavier shouted, his desperation spilling over.

"Shut up! Who needs your gems?" Evander barked, raising his taser threateningly. It looked like he was ready to bring it down on Xavier's face.

"Wait!"

A booming voice cut through the tension.

But Evander was already committed; he wasn't about to pull back.

Just as the taser was about to connect, Alexander appeared out of nowhere, his hand gripping Evander's wrist.

"Mr. Hawthorne?" Evander gasped, his eyes widening in shock.

Realizing the owner of this property had intervened, Evander quickly withdrew his hand, his bravado fading into respect and fear.

He could still feel the pain in his wrist as though the bones were cracking, a reminder of just how fast and powerful Alexander had been. Others might not have sensed the potency of Alexander's grip, but he was all too well aware of its ferocity.

If they were on a battlefield, Evander couldn't imagine how badly he would've fared against someone like Alexander.

"Mr. Hawthorne, my apologies," Evander said, his tone shifting from aggressive to deferential.

The other patrol members, who had just moments ago been ready to pounce on Xavier, now bowed their heads in respect.

Evander continued, "These two deliberately trespassed on your private property. I was just about to take them in for questioning.

"Please rest assured, our security at Grand Hyatt Estates is top-notch and impartial."

Alexander smiled, then turned his attention to Xavier.

At that moment, both Xavier and Jennifer were left in shock.

They had never imagined that the person they had looked down upon was actually the owner of the Majestic Manor.

Xavier's mind raced back to high school, recalling how he had bullied Alexander and even tried to provoke Alexander just moments ago.

His face turned ashen with dread. There was no doubt in his mind that Alexander would have his revenge.

"Ale... Alexander, I'm so sorry! I... I was wrong. Please, can you forgive me for the sake of our past as classmates?"

Though Xavier had a reputation for being arrogant, he was quick to backtrack when faced with the reality of his situation.

Alexander was someone who could afford a billion-dollar mansion; he was definitely not to be trifled with.

If he decided to hold someone accountable for this, skinning them alive would probably be the least of their worries.

Alexander regarded him with a bemused expression, clearly enjoying the sight of the once-mighty rich kid brought low.

Seeing Alexander's silence, Xavier began to cry.

"Alexander, I was blind. I was foolish... please!"

He slapped himself across the face twice, a gesture of self-recrimination that only added to the absurdity of the situation.

Finally, Alexander broke the silence. "Your mom is a gem dealer?"

Hope flickered in Xavier's heart, and he nodded vigorously.

"Yes! She's famous in Loscity! The Phoenix Gemstone Establishment is our family business. If you ever need gems, just say the word, and I'll make sure you're taken care of!"

After a moment of thought, Alexander waved his hand at Evander.

"Thanks for your efforts, but he was my classmate. He didn't do anything too terrible. Let's just let this slide."

Evander hesitated, clearly frustrated, but he knew better than to argue with the owner of the Majestic Manor.

"Understood, Mr. Hawthorne."

With that, Evander and the patrol team turned to leave, but not before shooting Xavier a final glare.

Once the patrol team had finally walked out of sight, Xavier let out a long sigh of relief.

His expression was a mix of gratitude and disbelief as he looked at Alexander. He couldn't believe that after ten years, the guy he used to bully had become into someone who could afford a mansion worth a billion dollars.

"Alexander, I can't thank you enough. If it weren't for you, I'd be in deep trouble right now. I was totally blind back at the entrance. Please don't take it to heart. Let me make it up to you—how about I treat you to a drink sometime?"

Xavier's attitude had changed completely. Gone was the arrogance; in its place was a desperate eagerness to appease.

Alexander just smiled and remained calm.

"It’s not a big deal. This mansion actually belongs to the Saint Group; I'm just here to keep an eye on things."

Alexander didn't want to flaunt his wealth or reveal the extent of his influence before Xavier.
