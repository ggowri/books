"We're leaving."

Alexander gave Maxine, who was still in a daze, a gentle pat on the back before leading her and her father out with confident strides.

As they stepped through the club's front door, Alexander suddenly paused, glanced over his shoulder, and gave Thomas a mysterious smile. With a snap of his fingers, he added, "Snap!"

Snap!

Thomas jumped, startled by the sound. When he saw that Alexander and his companions were truly leaving, he finally let out a sigh of relief.

But then, an intense, searing pain exploded in his body. It felt as if a thousand sharp blades were twisting inside him.

Before leaving, Alexander activated the Force hidden within Thomas' body.

Before Thomas could react, he collapsed to the floor, writhing in agony, blood gushing from his mouth.

Soon, it wasn't just from his mouth—blood began pouring from his eyes, nose, and ears, staining his face in a horrific, ghastly display.

Within mere minutes, before the ambulance could even arrive, Thomas convulsed violently and fell still, his body lifeless.

Meanwhile, Alexander was back in the car with Alvin and Maxine. As they pulled away from the club, Emma broke down in tears from the overwhelming relief.

Maxine, seeing her mother safe, let out a long sigh of relief.

Alvin, however, wore a grim expression. His fear was palpable—he was sure that the Nicholson family had drugged him, controlling him with no way out.

Emma, still trembling, asked, "What happened to Thomas?"

"Don't worry," Alexander replied coolly. "I've taken control of ninety percent of Silver Shield Security. It's mine now—no longer in the hands of the Nicholson family."

He pulled out two signed, unpaid transfer agreements and handed them to Alvin and Maxine to examine.

"What?"

The two stared at the documents, wide-eyed in disbelief.

They reread it several times, still stunned. The signatures of Thomas and his son were unmistakable.

The agreements confirmed that ninety percent of the company's shares had been transferred to Alexander, free of charge.

Maxine gasped, "Why would they do this? Why would they give up the shares so easily?"

Alexander smiled faintly.

"They didn't do it for any reason other than fear. They signed over their shares to save their lives. Unfortunately for Thomas, he chose to die. That's not on me."

His tone was flat, emotionless.

Alvin's face tightened, a flicker of panic flashing in his eyes. He had always been a straightforward, by-the-book kind of man, but the harshness in Alexander's words unsettled him.

Noticing Alvin's discomfort, Alexander gave him a reassuring smile and said, "I'm planning to appoint you as the General Manager of Silver Shield Security, to oversee the day-to-day operations. How does that sound?"

"Me? General Manager?"

Alvin's eyes widened in shock, but the next second, his excitement deflated, his shoulders sagging.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I...I'm still under the Nicholson family's drug control. I don't think I can handle such a responsibility."

Emma quickly interjected, "Why are you worried? Alexander already found the antidote. If you hadn't been captured, he would've cured you by now."

Alexander nodded, his voice calm. "That's right. The antidote's ready. As soon as we get back to the hotel, I'll help you fully break free from their control."

"Really? That's wonderful!"

Alvin's face lit up with relief, the weight of his worries lifting for the first time in what felt like forever.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I can't let you help us for free. I'm willing to transfer the All-purpose Bullet-resistant Jacket technology to you at no cost. Thank you for saving our family."

He squeezed his wife's hand, his expression full of gratitude.

Alexander couldn't help but feel a slight blush creeping onto his face, glancing at Emma, whose expression was unreadable.

He couldn't help but wonder—if Alvin knew about his and Emma's time together in the mountains, would he be as thankful? Would he even kill him for it?

As they arrived at the hotel, Alexander turned to the pair.

"You two talk it out. I'll go prepare the antidote."

"Alexander, you've been working non-stop. Don't you need to rest for a bit?" Maxine asked, concerned.

"I'm fine," he replied with a casual smile. "I'll be back soon."

With that, he headed for the kitchen, carrying the items he'd collected.

Before long, Alexander had turned the herbs into a paste, and the room filled with a calming, refreshing scent.

"Wow, it smells amazing!"

Maxine couldn't resist walking into the kitchen to see what was going on.

She had already showered and changed into a silky camisole nightgown, her Radiant skin glowing, her curves accentuated by the soft fabric.

The hem of the gown fluttered just above her knees, revealing her legs.

Alexander caught a glimpse of her, but quickly turned his attention back to the task at hand. He focused on packing the medicine into a small cup, mixing it with hot water.

Maxine's face flushed pink as she bit her lower lip and asked shyly, "Alexander... do you think I'm pretty?"
