Lynne led Alexander through several halls.

Through the bulletproof glass doors, he could see displays of exquisite jewelry, rare antiques, and even a few stunning models. These were clearly the prized possessions of the trading center.

At the end of the corridor, Lynne brought Alexander to a small, highly secure hall.

It was protected by two layers of bulletproof glass doors that could withstand sniper fire, and four armed guards were stationed nearby.

Surveillance cameras with infrared sensors monitored every angle, and hidden gun ports suggested automatic defenses inside.

"Mr. Hawthorne, behold the Red Umbrella Group's most groundbreaking invention of the century—the magical potential potion!" Lynne announced, gesturing toward a large glass cabinet.

The cabinet, made of transparent bulletproof glass, was surrounded by an electric fence. Inside were three small vials of red liquid, each flickering faintly under the lights.

Lynne's eyes shone with desire as she explained, "Anyone who drinks this potion can unlock their potential, enhancing physical strength, intelligence, and even lifespan. It can change your destiny."

Alexander stepped closer to the display, examining the vials. Lynne continued her enthusiastic pitch, her gaze never leaving the potions.

However, the price for each vial was a staggering one billion dollars, far beyond the reach of most. Even if Lynne could acquire one, she would likely sell it for a lifetime of luxury.

She had withheld one crucial detail from Alexander: the potion was far from perfect. Many who tried it became incapacitated shortly after initial success. The Red Umbrella Group had yet to fully understand these failures.

Out of over a hundred human trials, only a few were successful. Most users ended up with severe cognitive or physical impairments. The group found that only those with exceptional physical fitness or high intelligence fared well.

For instance, a world-class sprinter used the potion, breaking records repeatedly and living without issues for months. Similarly, a 90-year-old multinational chairman with a high IQ extended his life by half a year with no adverse effects.

The conclusion was clear: the potion's success depended on the user's attributes.

Alexander mused on the Red Umbrella Group's capabilities. They were indeed formidable, producing such an advanced product. More elusive and challenging than his rival, Guzman.

Despite the high cost, Alexander was intrigued. He considered purchasing a vial to study it further with his own methods. He wouldn't risk stealing it and alerting Guzman just yet.

"Mr. Hawthorne, are you interested in buying one?" Lynne asked eagerly.

"Has anyone else developed a similar drug?" Alexander inquired.

"Not yet. The Red Umbrella Group has military backing, which provides them with unique technology," Lynne explained.

Alexander thought for a moment. "I'm considering it, but you haven't mentioned any side effects."

Lynne's initial excitement turned to nervousness. She couldn't afford to deceive Alexander; anyone with a billion dollars to spend was not to be trifled with.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I was just about to explain... Most users do experience side effects, though some don't," she admitted cautiously.
