Gamblers from all corners of the world filled the casino, their spirits high.

Barry, too, was bursting with excitement.

His breathing grew heavy.

Several casino staff members in uniform took positions in each corner of the hall, their eyes discreetly trained on Barry.

High-definition cameras mounted on the ceiling also zoomed in on him.

"Felix, go exchange another 50 million dollars in chips," Barry urged, eager for more bets.

"Mr. Morris, you've already lost 100 million today. Betting more is just giving money to the casino. I really can't afford this," Felix pleaded.

Barry snapped, "Shut up! You're balking over a measly hundred million? You're the head of the Red Umbrella Group. Coughing up a few hundred million should be a walk in the park.

"If you don't want me to help you deal with Alexander, then forget it."

Beside them, Diego snorted, "Go! How dare you disobey our master?"

Felix had no choice but to exchange the chips.

At this rate, his fortune would drain in no time.

"Don't let me catch you slipping," he muttered under his breath.

Before the chips even arrived, Barry was already striding toward the roulette table.

His eyes were glued to the spinning wheel. Anyone could place a bet as the wheel spun.

The dealer was excited to see Barry approaching.

She smiled warmly and asked, "Sir, would you like to place a bet?"

"Of course, I would," Barry answered confidently.

Soon, the 50-million-dollar chips arrived.

Felix watched the towering pile with regret.

Barry was bound to lose.

Be it 50 million or five billion, Barry would lose it all.

But Felix needed him and Alexander to destroy each other, so he held his tongue.

The chips, arranged on a large tray, were stacked high, each silver chip worth a million dollars.

If it were gold, each would be worth ten million.

Barry quickly grabbed a one-million-dollar chip and threw it on number 18.

He then stared at the spinning roulette wheel.

The dealer, flashing a seductive smile, noted the hefty sum.

She nudged a specific tile under the table with her toe. The alarm in the surveillance room went off instantly.

The surveillance room on the top floor spanned thousands of square feet, an open space filled with rows upon rows of desks.

Each was equipped with LCD monitors, while an entire wall displayed a massive screen.

Hundreds of employees monitored the casino's thousand-plus high-definition cameras, keeping a close eye on every move within the building.

Barry, oblivious, was already under three-dimensional surveillance.

"Master, I didn't expect you'd take an interest in Barry," said a middle-aged man in a suit.

He was having a video call with Nancy.

His eyes were fixed on the screen showing Felix. "What would you like me to do next?"

Nancy smirked. "Drain them dry. Don't let them walk out of here easily."

The middle-aged man replied confidently, "Don't worry, Master. I'll make sure Barry gets so hooked he won't leave until he's lost his shirt."

Felix, unaware he was being monitored, kept a nervous watch on Barry's bets.

He would've never imagined himself feeling such anxiety over gambling chips.

This was all Alexander's fault.

Hatred seethed within him. He wished he could take out his half-brother right then and there.

As for Barry, he was no more than a naive amateur, gambling purely on luck.

He placed bet after bet, only to lose repeatedly.
