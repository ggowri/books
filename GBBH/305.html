They pretended not to notice, occasionally casting cold, piercing glares at Alexander.

Some were already reaching for their lower backs, clearly hiding weapons.

Schroder had brought three teams with him to Loscity. One was led by Rosen, another by Otis Stowe, and the last was for Schroder's personal protection.

With Betty and Charlize by Alexander's side, Otis assumed Alexander was there for a beach outing, completely surrounded and trapped.

What he didn't know was that Alexander's elite team was present, and besides William, all his core members had arrived.

Augusta, Marvin, and Logan hidden in the crowd, watched Otis' team closely.

Charlize sensed something was off. The hostile stares were unnerving.

But Alexander remained calm, exuding confidence that reassured Charlize.

Betty felt slightly anxious, knowing a fierce battle was inevitable.

Through his earpiece, Alexander instructed Augusta not to let anyone escape—swift action was essential.

A few thugs had already crept close, fingers twitching toward their holstered guns.

The bullets were loaded and ready to fire!

Alexander knew he couldn't wait any longer. It was time to strike first.

"Charlize, Betty, stay safe," he warned, then sprang up from his beach chair like a tiger pouncing on prey, launching himself toward the nearest foe.

His speed was blinding. The closest thug was still twenty feet away, yet Alexander appeared at his side like a specter.

"Damn it!" the man cursed as he drew his gun.

Whoosh!

Alexander's fist crashed into the man's face with tremendous force.

With a blood-curdling scream, the thug flew backward, colliding violently with two others behind him.

In an instant, Alexander vanished from sight.

When they finally spotted him again, he had seized another enemy by the throat, lifting him high and slamming him down hard.

Thud!

The man's body sank into the sand, unconscious.

Alexander unleashed his full power, demonstrating terrifying speed and strength.

Each strike cut through the air with a sharp whistle. Although Schroder's men were elite, they were no match for an enraged Warrior.

"Ah!"

Screams erupted as, in the blink of an eye, five men fell before Alexander's might.

Bang! Bang!

Finally, someone fired their weapon, but they couldn't keep up with Alexander's speed. The bullets flew wild, causing chaos among the beachgoers.

The two who shot were the unluckiest, quickly taken down by Alexander's wrath.

"This guy is a monster! A demon! Retreat!" Otis yelled, the first to turn and flee.

In moments, more men were falling.

As Otis ran a few paces, he suddenly felt a sharp pain in his throat—Alexander had him in a vice grip.

"Let me go...!" Otis gasped, struggling to pry Alexander's fingers away, but it was futile.

Alexander smirked, tightening his grip, and with a snap, Otis' neck broke, leaving him limp.

Like hauling trash, Alexander lifted Otis and charged into the enemy ranks, swinging him around like a windmill.

The whoosh of wind accompanied the continuous screams as he sent a whole crowd tumbling down.

"Everyone, get in there! Don't let them escape!"

Augusta was already coordinating the team into a curved formation.

Thirty specially trained subordinates lacked only real combat experience compared to true special forces members.

However, under the leadership of Alexander and Augusta, their morale soared, while the opposing team had already lost half their numbers and were terrified.

Dozens of them charged forward like fierce tigers, launching a relentless attack.

Meanwhile, the enemy was now reduced to half their original number, terrified and demoralized.

The remaining ones surrendered, hands over their heads, kneeling in defeat.

Blood stained the beach as a group writhed in agony, the scene resembling a hell on Earth.
