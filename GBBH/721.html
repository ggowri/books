Led by Oscar, Alexander entered the small courtyard outside.

"Oh!"

He suddenly caught a whiff of something... a faint but distinct herbal scent that felt refreshing and invigorating.

"This must be a rare medicinal herb that's over a century old," Alexander thought to himself as he followed Oscar toward a luxury RV.

Approaching the vehicle, Alexander immediately spotted a pot of green herbs on the coffee table.

The plant had jade-like leaves and pristine white flowers, releasing an enchanting fragrance that seemed to pierce straight to the soul.

It was the legendary "Heaven's Grass", a scarce medicinal plant from Native Loscity lore known for its remarkable detoxifying properties.

Several powerful presences, far more vital than ordinary people, had already locked onto Alexander.

Oscar quickly made introductions. "This is Mr. Hawthorne, who's here to treat Gideon."

The intense surveillance eased slightly but remained hidden in the shadows, keeping a constant watch on Alexander.

"Oscar!"

A frail, white-haired man wearing a surgical mask slowly emerged from the car.

"Gideon, this is Mr. Hawthorne I told you about. Let's get you started with the treatment right away," Oscar said, hurriedly helping him into a chair.

Alexander observed Gideon Patterson's pale complexion and listless expression. He clearly wasn't well.

"Hold up! We need to search him first!"

Two burly men suddenly appeared, moving to pat Alexander down.

"You think you can take me?"

Alexander let out an irritated scoff. Moving like lightning, he grabbed the man by the throat, lifted him clean off the ground, and slammed him down hard.

Before the other guy could even blink, Alexander had him by the neck, too, handling him as quickly as if he were a rag doll.

Alexander had held back. They were just dazed and winded from the impact, seeing stars.

"Freeze!"

Several burly men in black burst from the shadows, pulling guns on Alexander.

The tension in the air was thick enough to cut with a knife.

Gideon immediately roared, "What the hell are you doing? Have you lost your mind? I told you I don't need protection! Mr. Hawthorne is Oscar's guest! Why would he try to hurt me?

"Put those damn guns down and get the hell out of here!"

Gideon chewed them out viciously, tearing into the reckless guards.

The security team stared at Alexander with a mix of fear and awe. "Damn, this kid was something else. One move and he'd taken out our captain."

They were all ex-Special Forces, elite warriors who could each take on ten men.

Their captain was practically a legend in military circles, yet this young man had laid him out with a single move.

"Yes, Mr. Patterson!"

The guards picked up their fallen comrades and backed away, shooting bitter glares at Alexander.

Alexander couldn't be bothered with the guards. His piercing eyes scanned Gideon thoroughly, and through his All-seeing Eyes, he already knew what was going on, though he needed to confirm the details.

He said coolly, "You're infected with the plague. It started showing symptoms the day before yesterday morning, correct?"

Those simple words immediately got Gideon excited.

He hadn't even described his symptoms yet this man had figured everything out just by looking at him. It was incredible.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I'm so sorry about earlier. My guards are just being cautious. The Sakra Society from Risun has been trying to kidnap me, so they're on edge.

"Ah, Risun wanted me to research for them, but I refused. No matter how much money they offered...

"Those bastards, when they saw money wouldn't work, they tried to either kidnap me or kill me. This infection might even be their doing, setting me up behind the scenes."

Gideon broke into violent coughs as if his lungs were trying to escape his body. A thin line of blood trickled from the corner of his mouth.

"Take it easy. Let me examine you carefully."

Alexander stepped forward, using the Force to sense while scanning Gideon's body with his mystical vision.

After a moment, Alexander's brows furrowed.

"It's poison!

Could the City of Sorcery be behind this plague?"

The poison was incredibly potent. With current medical technology, there was no quick cure - it was terminal.

But for Alexander, who possessed the Ultimate Legacy, this wasn't a complex problem.

After all, this poison came from the wizard, who the Warrior had killed.
