Four people, including the white-haired man, sat around the poker table playing a showdown game.

Although the rules of showdown were simple, the stakes were high, making it a favorite among real experts.

"Zoom in on the screen and keep an eye on that old man!"

Norma ordered sharply.

The image on the LCD screen zoomed in, showing even the pores on the old man's face with remarkable clarity.

His subtle expressions couldn't escape the surveillance.

"Mr. Hawthorne, the three beside him are our casino's top gambling experts. Each of them ranks in the top thirty worldwide."

Although Norma's expression was serious, she wasn't too panicked. With three gambling masters at the table, they shouldn't lose too badly.

However, Alexander noticed that the three experts were in bad shape, with sweat covering their foreheads.

One was fidgeting, and another's hand trembled as he held his cards.

Alexander could tell immediately that they were nervous and bound to lose.

The white-haired old man facing the three experts looked relaxed and kept winning, with his stack of chips growing taller.

Just then, one of the gambling experts flipped over his hand and laughed triumphantly,

"Ha! You old man, I don't believe you can beat me this time. Four tens—I win!

"Showdown!"

He pushed all his chips into the center of the table.

The other two teammates looked relieved and quickly folded, putting all their hopes on the four tens.

"Fool!"

The old man smirked and calmly flipped over his hand.

"Four kings!"

He laughed arrogantly as he pulled the massive stack of chips toward him, making his pile even larger.

He won a few million dollars in that round, cleaning out the other side's chips worth tens of millions of dollars.

The three gambling experts gritted their teeth, wanting nothing more than to snatch the chips back.

"Damn it, we can't win..."

"It's over this time!"

The three muttered to themselves and collapsed into their chairs like deflated balloons.

In just one hour, the three had lost over two hundred million dollars combined.

Even though the casino was globally renowned and backed by the powerful Walsh family, losing over two hundred million dollars in an hour was unacceptable.

If the losses continued, the very future of the casino would be at stake.

Even though the Walsh family was worth billions, losing billions wouldn't be sustainable.

But Norma would likely lose control of the casino and even her position as head of the Dragon Gang, with Sean poised to take over completely.

Alexander saw through the entire situation.

"That old man wins every time; he's definitely cheating! Slow down the footage. I want proof to cut off those old hands of his!" Norma's voice dripped with menace.

Norma commanded the staff to play the video in ultra-slow motion to spot any flaw in the old man's cheating.

When the screen slowed down, everyone's eyes widened as they stared at it, searching for any sign of the old man cheating.

Alexander didn't even glance at the screen. His sharp eyes had already seen everything clearly.

How the old man swapped cards, his facial expressions, and every subtle move were all laid bare before Alexander.

Alexander just chuckled and stayed silent.

The old man was incredibly fast!

So fast that even with the video slowed to its minimum speed, there was no trace of cheating.

The conclusion was that the old man hadn't cheated and showed no suspicious behavior; he had won fair and square.

But everyone there was convinced he had to be cheating. There was no way luck alone could let him win every single time.

Norma's three gambling experts seemed like children up against an adult, unable to put up any resistance.

"This guy is definitely up to something. If even the surveillance can't catch him cheating, he must be a world-class gambler!

"He must be one of the top five on the global gambling leaderboard..."

Norma's expression turned grim, her mood sinking fast.
