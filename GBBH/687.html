Lucas was so terrified that he didn't dare meet Alexander's gaze, stumbling over his words in fear.

"I... I just... just..."

"Stop right there!"

A sudden shout cut through the tension.

Levi stepped forward; eyes blazing as he glared furiously at Alexander.

"You're not part of our organization. What gives you the right to lay a hand on one of our brothers? Are you looking to get yourself killed?"

With that one line, Levi had made Alexander an enemy of the entire organization.

Around a hundred skilled fighters stood at the ready, and no matter how strong Alexander might be, Levi was confident they could teach this arrogant young man a harsh lesson.

Alexander couldn't help but laugh at the absurdity. He had just been designated as the heir to the Godfather himself.

These people should have been his subordinates, yet here they were, challenging him again and again.

In a flash, Alexander appeared in front of Levi, grasping him by the throat and lifting him off the ground.

"What did you say? I'm looking for death? Who here is really asking for it?"

Levi's face turned ashen as he struggled for air, unable to respond.

Lucas, enraged, began to shout, "Everyone, get him! Take him down!"

Chaos erupted as the crowd, already holding a grudge against Alexander, surged forward with a battle cry.

In all, about ten fighters lunged for Alexander—half of them powered metahumans, the other half highly skilled martial artists.

They charged with such intensity that it looked as though Alexander would be buried under the onslaught of bodies.

Alexander scoffed. If these people weren't meant to be his own future subordinates, he would have crippled them on the spot.

As the dozen warriors encircled him, a faint smirk of disdain appeared on Alexander's lips.

Just as he was about to strike, a sudden, invisible mental force hit him.

Alexander hadn't anticipated that one of the metahumans in the crowd was capable of psychic attacks and had launched a mental assault on him.

Immediately, his mind felt frozen, and his body grew rigid, as though trapped in the grip of arctic ice.

"Kneel!"

A brutal voice echoed in Alexander's mind. The metahuman with the hypnotic power was formidable, already a level-two metahuman.

"Get out of my head!"

Alexander channeled his power, releasing it in an explosive surge.

The violent power surged like a raging storm, crashing toward the opponent.

In an instant, his mental strength was torn apart, and a scream erupted from the crowd as someone fell heavily to the ground.

Simultaneously, a dozen fighters who had nearly reached Alexander were blasted backward as though struck by a hurricane, landing meters away in a painful heap.

Their faces turned pale with shock. They had charged him together, confident in overwhelming Alexander, but had instead been effortlessly crushed.

It felt as though Alexander's strength bore down on them like a mountain, an unstoppable force that couldn't be challenged.

Alexander walked calmly toward Levi, Lucas, and the others, his gaze filled with scorn.

To Levi and Lucas, they were nothing more than ants on the ground, and Alexander was a towering giant.

"Kneel! And I might just spare your lives."

Alexander's words cracked through the air like thunder, and his fierce power crashed over them like tidal waves, obliterating any remaining defiance.

Levi and Lucas, closest to him, instantly crumbled, collapsing to their knees on the spot.

Around them, the weaker onlookers couldn't stop themselves from sinking to their knees.

"Defy me again, and I'll see you all dead!"

Alexander's voice was chilling, cold as ice.

Levi and Lucas screamed in agony, feeling as though their bodies were tearing apart, small cracks appearing on their skin through which crimson blood seeped.

Within moments, the two of them were soaked in blood, looking like figures drenched in red.

At that moment, not a single person among the hundred dared to aid Levi and Lucas. Their minds were filled only with the image of Alexander, radiating an unassailable authority and deadly intent.

A profound fear of Alexander grew within each of them. The weaker ones prostrated themselves on the ground while even the stronger fighters lowered their heads in dread.

They had never encountered anyone so terrifying, someone who made even Shadow and the Godfather himself seem less frightening by comparison.

"Son, a great battle lies ahead. Please, for my sake, spare these fools."

Just then, an urgent voice broke the silence.

The voice came from an elderly man with silver hair, seated in a wheelchair, being pushed forward by Shadow.

Alexander's body tensed, and he looked toward his father, his expression complicated.
