It was late at night when Alexander returned to his hotel room. Brittany, growing impatient, perked up at the sound of approaching footsteps.

"It's me!" Alexander called out.

Brittany opened the door with relief, throwing herself into his arms. "You're finally back. I was worried about you."

Seeing Alexander unharmed, she relaxed.

He noticed her fresh scent and the warmth of her embrace, his heart racing.

Wrapped in a simple white bath towel, Brittany appeared both shy and relieved.

Desire sparked in Alexander as Brittany leaned in to kiss him deeply, her soft lips filling him with sweetness.

The moment seemed to stretch on, as if time had stopped.

As Alexander gently laid Brittany on the bed, she suddenly hesitated, her face turning pale. "What's wrong?" he asked softly.

After a pause, Brittany replied, "I have a scar from a childhood burn. My ex-boyfriend left me because of it. Does it bother you?"

Alexander replied sincerely, "Not at all. Let me see it. Maybe I can help."

"Really?" Brittany asked, surprised and hopeful.

With trembling hands, she revealed the scar. Her body was beautiful, but the scar beneath her breast had always made her feel self-conscious.

Brittany blushed, whispering, "That's why I've never been with anyone."

Alexander, focused on her well-being, gently touched the scar. "Burn scars can be tough, but I know a way to help."

Brittany's eyes lit up with hope. "You really have a way?"

Alexander nodded, recalling a remedy from his studies. "Yes, I can help you."

He gathered the necessary herbs, and with Brittany's help, they were delivered quickly. Alexander prepared the ointment with care, blending the herbs and magic powder into a soothing blue balm.

"Is this really going to work?" Brittany asked, both eager and doubtful.

Alexander reassured her with a smile. "Trust me, this will help. It can reduce scars and improve skin."

Brittany felt a wave of relief and gratitude. "Thank you, Alexander. I believe in you."

She embraced him, filled with newfound confidence. Alexander, touched by her trust, gently reminded her, "Let's try the ointment first."

Brittany nodded, grateful for his support and the chance to finally heal.
