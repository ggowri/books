Using the Force to remove poison wasn't tricky for Alexander, but with so many infected people in Loscity, treating them one by one was impractical.

Then Alexander's eyes fell on the precious "Heaven's Grass", and an idea immediately struck him.

If he dried and ground this Heaven's Grass into powder, then combined it with the detoxification formula from the Ultimate Legacy, he could create an antidote powder.

By adding this powder to the water treatment plant's reservoir, he could distribute the cure to every household in the city.

Oscar broke the silence with an urgent question.

"Alexander, how's Gideon doing? Can you cure him?"

Gideon stared at Alexander hopefully, his eyes filled with anticipation.

"Don't worry, I can fix this," Alexander replied, confidently patting his chest.

Oscar let out a long sigh of relief, knowing Alexander always delivered on his promises.

Gideon felt both excited and skeptical.

He'd already seen the best private doctors and tried the latest medications, but nothing had worked.

So Gideon couldn't help but doubt Alexander's abilities.

He sighed quietly to himself, hoping Oscar's recommendation wasn't wrong. He still had too much research to complete and wasn't ready to die yet.

Alexander channeled the Force for healing, massaging, and pressing on Gideon's lungs.

Waves of the Force flowed into Gideon like an invisible tide, surrounding the poison and wreaking havoc within his body.

The others couldn't see it, but Alexander's All-seeing Eyes had a clear view.

Evil black tendrils of poison snaked through Gideon's system.

The Force, with pinpoint accuracy, wrapped around the poison, dissolving it bit by bit and drawing it out through his pores.

Oscar and the guards held their breath, eyes wide, not daring to make a sound.

"Mmm! Such a warm, comfortable feeling..."

Gideon couldn't help but sigh in wonder.

The sharp pain in his chest was easing up, replaced by an indescribable warmth flowing through his body, bringing incredible comfort.

His throat and lungs, raw from violent coughing, now felt wonderfully relaxed, with no trace of the irritating tickle that had triggered his coughing fits.

As Alexander's treatment deepened, Gideon felt a pleasant floating sensation throughout his body.

Soon, Gideon had drifted off to sleep, softly snoring.

The guards watched Alexander with bewildered expressions, their emotions mixed.

As Gideon's constant companions, they knew his illness had kept him awake and suffering for two straight nights.

Yet here was Alexander, who, with just a simple massage, had worked what seemed like a miracle, putting Gideon into a peaceful sleep.

The moment Alexander arrived, they sensed the intense aura of a killer around him that only came from someone who had taken many lives. That was why they immediately went to check things out.

They were impressed not only by Alexander's fighting skills but also by his miraculous medical abilities. They couldn't help but admire him.

Alexander had just finished the treatment, giving Gideon's chest a gentle tap.

"Ah!"

Gideon suddenly jolted awake from his deep sleep, amazed as he examined his body.

His clothes were soaked through with black sweat that gave off a foul stench. It was clearly something toxic that needed to get out.

Alexander smiled and said, "Mr. Patterson, I've expelled all the poison from your system. How are you feeling?"

Gideon excitedly moved around, cleared his throat, and realized the unbearable pain in his lungs was gone. His whole body felt lighter.

Suddenly, his expression changed to one of horror as he asked, "Are you saying the epidemic in Loscity was caused by poison?"

Alexander nodded. "The City of Sorcery must be behind this."

Oscar grimly agreed, "It's almost certainly the City of Sorcery, but why would they target Loscity?"

Alexander was at a loss for words. He didn't need to guess - the City of Sorcery was definitely targeting him, with Loscity becoming collateral damage.

Gideon slammed his palm down angrily.

"If it's not the City of Sorcery, then it must be the Sakra Society! Mr. Hawthorne, you can't let them get away with this. We need to end this epidemic quickly."
