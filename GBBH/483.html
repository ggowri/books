Ava tightly wrapped her arms around Alexander's neck, kissing him gently on the face, as if saying goodbye.

Alexander felt an inexplicable sadness wash over him. He asked, "Ava, is there something on your mind? Don't hide it from me. I promise I'll help you."

"No, really, there's nothing!" Ava quickly denied, wiping away a tear with a smile.

"Are you sure?"

"Absolutely! Come on, let's go buy a birthday cake for my mom."

She linked her arm with Alexander's and laughed as they headed outside, trying to act carefree.

But Alexander sensed something was off about Ava, though he couldn't quite put his finger on it.

As they approached the shopping district, they had just exited the parking lot when Leonard stepped out, blocking their path.

"Ms. Blackburn, what a pleasure! I didn't expect the president of the Saint Group to visit our Tijuanea incognito!"

He grinned at Ava while barely glancing at Alexander.

Ava frowned, "Mr. Moon, have you been spying on me?"

Leonard smirked.

"I'm not spying, just keeping tabs, mainly concerning that bracelet..."

As he spoke, he shot a sharp glare at Alexander.

"This Mr. Hawthorne must be the one you're financially supporting. He took it upon himself to spend one hundred million on that bracelet without your consent, I assume?"

Hearing this, Alexander couldn't help but chuckle.

Ava's expression turned serious. "Mr. Moon, you've got it all wrong. Alexander is not a man I keep; he's my boyfriend. He can buy whatever he wants without needing my permission. I should clarify that the bracelet means a lot to him, so it's not up for grabs."

Ava was quick to understand Leonard's intentions.

Known for his reputation in Tijuanea, Leonard was said to have accurate divination skills, which made Ava a bit uneasy.

Alexander had bought the bracelet with his own money, and Ava wouldn't agree to anything Leonard suggested.

Leonard, however, didn't seem upset. Instead, he smiled knowingly.

"Ms. Blackburn, I did come to discuss that bracelet, but I also have urgent news. You're in extreme danger right now."

"What? What danger?"

Ava's brows furrowed.

"Did you offend someone? You've been hit with a serious curse. If you don't act quickly to dispel it, you could be in grave danger within three days!"

Leonard spoke gravely, extending his hand towards Ava's forehead.

Ava froze, uncertain if Leonard was telling the truth or spinning a tale.

Alexander noticed a faint silver glow emanating from Leonard's palm. Using his All-seeing Eyes, he saw it was a silver tarot card.

Within that silver light, a hint of dark energy flickered.

Though Alexander was no expert in sorcery or divination, his mastery of the Force and All-seeing Eyes allowed him to see that Leonard was up to no good.

"Stop!" Alexander shouted.

Leonard flinched but didn't cease his approach, instead pressing on toward Ava.

The tarot card in his hand transformed from silver to black, revealing the Death card.

A grim skeleton knight sat astride a warhorse, brandishing a scythe and a battle flag.

If the Death card touched Ava, the dark energy would surely surge into her, threatening to control her and pressuring Alexander to hand over the bracelet—or worse, extort a fortune from him.

"You're asking for trouble!"

With a swift step, Alexander positioned himself in front of Ava, his palm meeting Leonard's.

"Kid, if you want to die, then let's get to it!"

Leonard sneered, and the dark energy from the Death card erupted forth.

This energy was the culmination of his secret curse techniques, and upon touching an ordinary person, it could mean death or worse—servitude to him.

Leonard's reputation in Tijuanea was built not only on his proficiency in divination but also on the control he wielded over those he cursed.
