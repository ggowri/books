The fuel tank was punctured, and the air immediately filled with the sharp stench of gasoline.

One stone skimmed the ground, sparks flying as it scraped against the asphalt.

In an instant, the gasoline caught fire, erupting in a massive explosion.

The blast hurled two of the cars through the air, turning them into blazing fireballs.

Some of the wounded, still alive, were engulfed in flames and writhed helplessly on the ground.

Others fired wildly in Alexander's direction, but their shots went wide, doing no damage.

Unhurried, Alexander strode toward the wreckage.

As he passed the survivors, he casually flicked stones at them. One by one, Tyler's men collapsed, their bodies crumpling in pools of blood.

The last survivor's head exploded under the impact of a stone.

The road was littered with corpses, blood flowing like a quiet stream toward the roadside.

Alexander sighed. It was a mercy to end their suffering now rather than let them burn alive.

He turned his gaze to Tyler, shaking his head in disdain.

Slowly, he began walking toward him, each step deliberate and calm, the stillness of his approach making Tyler tremble in fear.

"You bastard! You're a devil! You'll burn in hell!" Tyler's face was as pale as a sheet, his teeth gritted as he cursed.

He tried to escape, but his lower body was pinned under the wreckage. Even with his second-tier fire abilities, he was stuck.

Desperate, Tyler reached for his gun, the only option left to him. His hand was trembling as he aimed at Alexander and fired.

Two shots rang out, and the bullets, enhanced with fiery power, sped toward their target with deadly force.

Tyler believed that even if he didn't kill Alexander, the man would at least be forced to retreat, dazed.

But the moment the gunfire rang out, Alexander seemed to vanish.

Tyler's eyes darted frantically around, searching for any sign of him. Alexander had disappeared without a trace.

The bullets hit a nearby tree with explosive force, blowing it in half.

Then, a shadow flickered, moving so swiftly that Tyler's mind couldn't keep up. It felt as though dozens of Alexanders were closing in from every direction.

Tyler fired until the clip was empty, but still, he couldn't see where Alexander was. His hands fumbled to reload, panic setting in.

Suddenly, a sharp pain shot through his wrist. Looking up, he found Alexander standing right before him, gripping his wrist with terrifying strength.

Tyler screamed as his arm was twisted at an unnatural angle, rendering it useless.

"How... How did you find us?" Tyler stuttered, his voice filled with both terror and fury.

Under the glow of the flames, Alexander's face remained cold, a contemptuous smile on his lips. "Yinson sent you, didn't he?"

Alexander tore open Tyler's shirt, revealing Sakra Society's emblem tattooed over his left chest.

Tyler clenched his teeth, refusing to speak.

"Playing dumb?" Alexander sneered and twisted Tyler's arm again, making him cry out in pain.

"No! Please, don't kill me! Yinson sent me! I had no other choice... Please! I'll tell you everything!" With death staring him in the face, Tyler's defiance crumbled. Fear overtook him, and he began pleading for his life.

The more a man has enjoyed power and luxury, the harder it is for him to face the reality of his death.

Alexander scoffed and picked up the gun from the ground, pressing the barrel against Tyler's forehead.

"Tell me about your partners, and maybe I'll let you live. If you lie to me, I'll put a bullet through your skull."

Tyler's resolve crumbled under the threat. His words tumbled out in a panicked rush. "There's nothing left in Loscity... but in other cities... there are people using enhancement drugs to control key figures... That's all I know... Please, just don't kill me..."

Alexander's eyes narrowed dangerously. He hadn't expected Yinson to be so sneaky, spreading his influence in other cities.

Loscity was Ultimate Alliance's stronghold. They'd thwarted Yinson's plans here, but in other cities, the situation was far more complicated.
