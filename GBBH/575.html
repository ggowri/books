By the time Cole's team chased after them, the sports car had already disappeared into the dark night.

"Mr. Hawthorne, what should we do now?"

All eyes turned to Alexander with expressions of frustration. They'd underestimated the situation.

Alexander looked calm, his intense gaze piercing through the night.

"What should we do? They won't escape my grasp in the end!" he replied with unwavering confidence.

With his current power, he feared no one. So what if the Morris family were stronger than the Red Umbrella Group? They weren't invincible.

...

In the car, Felix gritted his teeth and said, "Why is Alexander here?! That young guy chasing you is Alexander."

Felix recognized Alexander instantly; he was the enemy Felix had been dying to confront. Even if Alexander were just ashes, he'd still recognize him.

"That guy's Alexander?"

Barry's face went pale; he'd once confidently agreed to help Felix go against Alexander. What a death wish!

"What about Diego?" Felix asked.

"Diego... if it weren't for him, I wouldn't have made it out... it was a trap!" Barry admitted, looking utterly defeated, a far cry from his former arrogance.

Barry was no longer the high-spirited man he used to be, much less proud. He looked like a terrified stray dog.

"Alexander's as crafty as they come. He's ruthless and cunning. He was probably onto us all along. Those guys, Finley and Jaylon, were his plants," Felix said, snapping him to reality.

"Damn Alexander!" Barry growled, clenching his fists.

"I swear, I'll make him wish he were dead."

Felix glanced over at him, a strange glint flickering in his eyes.

His grip on the steering wheel trembled slightly.

"Phew!"

Felix took a deep breath, tamping down his mixed emotions, and said calmly to Barry, "I'm pulling over to take a leak."

"Alright," Barry replied without suspicion.

Felix parked the car on the roadside and went into the bushes.

Barry walked over, cursing Alexander under his breath as he relieved himself.

At that moment, Felix positioned himself right behind Barry.

Something glinted in Felix's right hand as he leaned in close to Barry's ear with a sly grin.

"How long would it take to die if you got stung by a killer bee?" he whispered with chilling menace.

His tone dripped with lethal intent.

Barry jolted in shock, feeling a sharp pain in his side that nearly made him black out.

He turned in horror to see a black killer bee falling to the ground beside him.

"Damn you, Felix! How dare you betray me?" Barry yelled, trying to fight back.

But his vision blurred as a wave of weakness overtook him, draining his strength like the tide pulling back.

"You were destined to be my fuel, and Hell is waiting for you. Ha ha ha..." Felix laughed wildly, his voice thick with satisfaction.

He pulled out a red syringe, plunged it into his arm, and injected the entire dose.

Barry asked in terror, "W-What is that?"

Felix smirked and didn't hold back, explaining with a chuckle, "In a moment, you'll feel no pain. This is the latest extraction serum developed by the Red Umbrella Group. It refines a person's most essential qualities.

"Which means you're going to become my nourishment, helping me advance to a level-three metahuman.

"Plus, I'll gain your speed abilities. So I'll have speed, strength, fire, and freezing powers."

He went on, speaking with eager excitement.

Thud!

Barry collapsed to the ground like a felled tree.

His body started to dissolve rapidly, like ice melting in the summer sun, soon reduced to a few drops of red liquid dripping onto the leaves.

Felix eagerly cupped the leaves, raised them to his mouth, and swallowed the red liquid.

Instantly, a surge of power, like a searing warmth, flooded through his body.

Felix felt a snap in his mind, as if some restraint had been shattered.

Whoosh!

His figure vanished, only to reappear dozens of meters away.

"Ha ha ha! I've gained speed abilities and become a level-three metahuman!"

Felix's power had surged, and he was filled with uncontrollable excitement.
