A grotesque expression of rage twisted Felix's face.

He let out a blood-curdling roar and launched a barrage of attacks against Alexander.

Yet Alexander deflected each strike effortlessly, leaving a trail of wounds on Felix's body.

However, Felix dodged quickly enough that the Lightsabers only grazed his skin.

Alexander's expression turned grave as he noticed Felix's increasing speed and the sinister aura growing stronger around him.

Felix's face had become inhuman, his eyes now just protruding white orbs without pupils.

"So this is the power of demons? Pretty pathetic if you ask me!" Alexander taunted.

Felix responded with furious roars, barely able to stutter out words.

"You... must... die!"

As the dark power consumed him further, Felix was losing his ability to form coherent speech.

Just as Felix opened his mouth to speak again, a flash of white light appeared silently at his side.

"CRACK!"

Despite Felix's quick reflexes, Alexander's blade still caught his left arm, severing it completely.

The pale, shriveled arm fell to the ground without bleeding, writhing like a snake.

"I... will... kill... you!"

Felix howled in rage, seemingly immune to pain.

A mass of flesh began to writhe at the stump of his left arm. Tissue sprouted and rapidly grew, quickly taking the shape of a new limb.

Meanwhile, Felix's already pale and withered body deteriorated even further.

The dark power clearly corrupted his body. The more strength he gained, the more damage it caused him.

Though Felix was mighty, he wasn't in immediate danger of dying. It was just a question of whether he could kill Alexander before his body gave out ultimately.

Alexander gave a cold smirk.

"Keep this up and I won't even need to lift a finger. That dark power will drain you dry, and you'll die a meaningless death."

"You're... so... naive!"

Felix let out a twisted laugh.

Like a deranged psychopath, he wore a smug expression that Alexander found absurd.

Suddenly, Felix pulled out a dagger and plunged it deep into his chest. Blood gushed out like a fountain, dripping onto the ground and seeping into the magic circle.

"You're... finished... My master... is coming..."

Felix stuttered before collapsing to his knees, his body convulsing violently.

Alexander frowned at the bizarre scene before him.

He hadn't expected Felix to take his own life. Clearly, the dark power had taken control of him. He'd brought this on himself.

Alexander muttered to himself, "If even Felix got possessed, this evil force must be incredibly powerful."

Felix's blood covered the floor, and every last drop was absorbed into the ritual circle, turning him into the perfect sacrifice.

Having consumed enough power, the Demon Summoning Array began to pulse with a blood-red glow that grew increasingly blinding.

Alexander squinted involuntarily as the light became so intense it made his skin prickle.

Without hesitation, he bolted from the monitoring room and sprinted up the stairs toward the plaza above.

Being underground was too risky - any intense fighting could cause a cave-in.

Felix, practically a walking corpse now, had nothing to fear, but Alexander wasn't taking any chances.

Suddenly, the entire ground began to shake as if Treasure Island was hit by an earthquake, with massive energy threatening to burst from below.

A series of blinding red beams shot up from underground, piercing straight through to the sky.

In the plaza, when the light touched William, the blood mist surrounding him instantly vanished, melting away like ice under a scorching sun.

The swarm of vampire bats darkening the sky stopped following commands and scattered in panic.

"Oh no!" William shouted, quickly backing away.

Augusta's massive form was forced to shrink back to a standard size, her werewolf bloodline strength and speed vanishing without a trace.

"Felix must be making his move!"

Charlize and Rosalia's faces went ghost white with worry for Alexander.

Everyone instinctively backed away from the plaza, putting as much distance as they could between themselves and the impending confrontation.

They all felt the overwhelming power was beyond their ability to resist. Their only hope now rested with Alexander.
