Alexander paused for a moment, pushing aside the lingering frustration. A smile returned to his face as he glanced at the door.

Creek!

The study door slowly opened, and Charlize slipped inside, moving with the eagerness of a devoted follower. Without a word, she leaned into his embrace.

A shiver ran through her as Alexander's fingers lightly traced her skin.

A subtle sound echoed as Charlize began to unzip her leather jacket.

The black leather slid off, revealing her pale, alluring form, the fabric gliding away like peeling skin.

The next morning, just as dawn broke, the sharp ring of the doorbell jolted Alexander awake.

Rosalia's voice came from the other side of the door. "Mr. Hawthorne, Grace has arrived."

Ava, frowning and curling her lip in distaste, muttered, "She's here for you, isn't she? I don't like her. Go see what she wants."

With a nod, Alexander donned his robe and made his way downstairs.

"Is there something you need?"

"Alexander, I finally found you!"

Grace's face lit up with a smile as bright as a blooming flower, her cool, elegant demeanor softening. She stepped forward, looking almost relieved at his presence.

"What's so urgent that you came so early?"

Alexander's voice was calm, almost indifferent.

He wasn't thrilled to see Grace—not after the last time, when she'd tried to manipulate Ava into leaving him.

Her face paled at his cold reception, and she hesitated before speaking seriously.

"Alexander, my grandfather's condition has worsened. It's critical, and he's on the brink of death!"

"What? That's impossible!"

Alexander was shocked. He had already treated Gavin, and though he hadn't fully healed, his condition shouldn't have deteriorated so soon.

But seeing Grace's urgency and the fact that she had come to him so early made it clear that something was seriously wrong.

"I'll come with you," he said. "But I'll bet there's more to this than you're telling me."

Grace nodded eagerly, and her relief was palpable.

Minutes later, a sleek black military helicopter touched down on the lawn outside the mansion.

The blades kicked up dust as they made their way inside.

Ava stood by the window, watching the helicopter fade into the distance, her expression unreadable.

In the helicopter, Alexander gazed out at the passing scenery while his mind churned. He still couldn't figure out the mystery of the low-level energy cores needed for his Lightsabers upgrade. What exactly were they?

Grace, sensing his distraction and knowing the urgency of the situation, chose not to speak. They flew in silence, the tension between them palpable.

They soon arrived at a sprawling estate in the countryside—a mansion set in picturesque surroundings. The air was crisp, and the peaceful hum of nature contrasted sharply with the urgency of their mission.

Alexander, unreadable as ever, followed Grace through the gates and toward the mansion.

Grace glanced at him nervously, forcing a smile. "We're almost there, Alexander. Please, you have to help my grandfather."

Alexander nodded, his senses alert.

He could feel the presence of hidden guards all around them. The estate was heavily protected, which was no surprise given Gavin's status as one of Avalora's top figures.

Soon, they approached the grand entrance, where dozens of people had gathered.

Men and women, impeccably dressed, stood in anxious clusters, whispering among themselves.

It was clear they were all members of the Lawson family—wealthy, influential, and powerful, with several well-known faces among them.

The murmurs died down as they watched Grace lead Alexander through without any resistance.

"Wait a minute! Ms. Grace Lawson is personally guiding this young man? Who is he? Just like that, he gets to go in?"

"I've never seen him before. No idea who he is."

"That's Alexander—the one who supposedly cured Sir Lawson last time. But now Sir Lawson's condition is even worse. This time, he's definitely in for trouble."

The murmurs of the crowd reached Alexander's ears.

His expression remained unchanged, as if he hadn't heard a word. Calmly, he followed Grace across the hall and into a charming courtyard.

"Wait!"

Suddenly, several armed guards emerged from the shadows, blocking their path.

"What's going on? Step aside!" Grace snapped.

But Alexander, unphased, smiled slightly. "It's fine. Let them do their check."

He spread his arms out, completely calm as the guards moved in to inspect him.
