Three more days passed before the relentless rain finally stopped.

Alexander left Sally behind in the air-raid shelter and drove away.

Sally stood outside the heavy iron door, staring blankly as his car disappeared into the distance.

Alexander glanced at the rearview mirror and smiled faintly. He knew that as long as he could catch Yinson, he could put an end to this catastrophe.

He drove to the other side of Kylaville, heading toward the Dragon Gang's headquarters.

Then, he spotted something unusual.

In the distance, there was a massive underground entrance. Vehicles moved in and out freely, and strangely, there were no zombies or mutant beasts anywhere near it.

"Could this be Yinson's secret base?" he wondered.

Excitement surged through him. He parked his car at a safe distance and waited until nightfall before silently making his way toward the underground facility.

The perimeter was heavily guarded, with both open and hidden sentries and heavy weapon emplacements.

Armed soldiers patrolled back and forth. Moving like a shadow, Alexander found a ventilation duct, pried open the cover, and slipped inside.

*****

"Bastard! How dare you sleep here?"

A furious voice rang out, followed by the sharp crack of a slap.

A hand smacked hard across Alexander's face.

He jolted awake, staring in confusion at the stunning woman in front of him.

Her breathtaking features were twisted in anger. She wore nothing but a torn black silk nightgown, draped loosely over her body.

Her skin was marked with red welts and faint bruises.

There was clear evidence of someone's rough treatment.

Alexander blinked in bewilderment. "Who... are you?"

"Shameless scum!"

She was livid. Another slap landed on his face, leaving a matching print on his other cheek.

Now he was fully awake. But his mind was a complete blank. He had no idea what was happening.

Something felt terribly wrong.

"I forgot something... Something really important!"

He struggled to grasp the missing pieces. "I was the last Warrior, wasn't I? I infiltrated the underground base... So how did I end up here?"

Panic set in.

Then, another set of memories surged into his mind. He realized he was just a delivery guy. Also named Alexander. An hour ago, he had accepted an order and eagerly set off to complete it.

The order read: [Deliver one bottle of Lafite wine to Moon Bay Villas, Kylaville. Tip: 100 dollars.]

Moon Bay Villas was an exclusive neighborhood for the wealthy. It was no surprise that its residents drank expensive wine and tipped generously.

Right now, he needed the money desperately.

He was an intern at Healiva Hospital, drowning in student debt, so he had taken up a side job delivering food and goods.

He thought, "Did I develop a split personality?

Am I losing my mind? Was the Warrior... Ava... Godfather... all just a dream?"

Shock and doubt flooded his heart. The thought unsettled him deeply.

When he arrived at the villa, the front gate opened, and a young woman stepped out.

She wore a black leather miniskirt that showed off her long legs, her full chest straining against her tight top. Though her face was merely above average, her outfit pushed her attractiveness up a notch.

"What are you looking at? Never seen a woman before?"

She rolled her eyes at him, snatching the shopping bag out of his hands with an air of disdain.

Alexander felt a little embarrassed. He hadn't meant to stare, but he had instinctively taken an extra glance.

"Hmph! A mere delivery guy dares to look at me? Know your place."

Her voice dripped with contempt.

Annoyed, Alexander thought, "You dress like that because you want people to look."

But money was money. He still had a loan to pay off, and a hundred bucks was a hundred bucks. He let it slide.

Turning away, he strode toward his scooter, ready to leave.

Then he froze.

The woman had taken the shopping bag but didn't go inside. Instead, she pulled out a syringe and hastily inserted it into the bottle's cap.

Alexander's instincts screamed that something was off.

His impression of her sank even lower.

"Maris, is the wine here?"

A sweet, seductive voice called out from inside the house.

"Yes, it's here!"

Maris Becker answered hurriedly, tossing the syringe into a patch of grass before rushing inside and slamming the door shut.

Curiosity piqued, Alexander quickly walked over to the bushes and searched.

He soon found the syringe, still containing a few lingering drops of liquid.

Bringing it close to his nose, he sniffed. Then, his expression darkened instantly. He could tell the syringe contained a strong anesthetic.
