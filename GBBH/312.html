Alexander moved increasingly faster, carving a total of ninety-three Spellweave Patterns before he had to stop.

His Force had been fully depleted. He couldn't continue for the day.

Feeling an unprecedented weakness in his body, he was both helpless and relieved.

Although he hadn't managed to carve one hundred and eight patterns, he knew that after meditating, his Force would surely be restored.

Closing his eyes, he sat cross-legged and began to meditate.

Even with his eyes shut, it felt as if he possessed a mystical vision, allowing him to perceive everything around him without opening his eyes.

His heart beat slowly at a certain rhythm, resonating with the energies of the world.

A stream of mysterious energy was drawn into him, transforming into the Force and replenishing his earlier exhaustion...

He wasn't sure how long it had been, but eventually, Alexander stood up, feeling revitalized.

The night was pitch black. Glancing at the time revealed it was the early hours of the morning.

He chuckled softly, planning to find Ava for some intimate relaxation.

Suddenly, his expression changed, and his eyes narrowed.

A few unsavory characters were stealthily approaching in the darkness.

"Could it be that Quentin doesn't trust me and fears I might escape? No, they are Shadow Warriors—definitely members of the Red Umbrella Group..."

Alexander sneered to himself.

Through the curtains, he could see clearly, a woman accompanied by three men.

The woman wore a black combat outfit, a katana strapped to her back, leading the group.

Close behind her was a muscular man with a red bandana tied around his head. His bare arms were bandaged at the elbows and knees, his muscles bulging with power.

There were also two figures in gray robes, adorned with a necklace of white bones and wielding a bone staff—clearly, they were wizards.

With a cold smile, Alexander went directly to the living room, waiting for them to walk into his trap.

Soon, the front door was gently pushed open.

The group rushed in swiftly, and suddenly the bright lights flickered on, illuminating the living room in detail.

The four figures stumbled in from the darkness, momentarily blinded by the sudden brightness, their heads buzzing.

After a moment of confusion, they began to survey their surroundings.

When they noticed Alexander casually lounging on the sofa, they were stunned.

It was clear that he had anticipated their arrival. He didn't flee. He was waiting here for them. The group was momentarily at a loss—should they call this guy impressive or just plain reckless?

The woman at the front drew her katana, its blade gleaming sharply.

Her fitted black outfit accentuated her voluptuous figure, making her appearance strikingly alluring.

However, her face was obscured by a black mask, revealing only her large, expressive eyes.

While her features were hidden, her captivating physique and expressive eyes were mesmerizing.

The muscular man beside her licked his lips, gazing at Alexander like a tiger eyeing its prey, a fierce grin on his face.

The two wizards wore cold expressions, their gazes toward Alexander filled with cruelty and bloodlust.

"Alexander, you actually didn't run away. You've got quite a nerve!"

The muscular man glared at him, cracking his knuckles ominously.

Alexander relaxed in his seat, observing them silently.

Seeing his silence, the man assumed he had frightened him into shock and burst into arrogant laughter.

"Hahaha, I'm here to take him back, and no one should interfere with my glory!"

The woman and the two wizards remained silent, merely casting disapproving glances at the man.

The muscular man swaggered toward Alexander, his demeanor that of a hawk about to seize its prey, exuding an air of confidence.

"You're asking for trouble!"

A flash of danger glinted in Alexander's eyes.

"Who's asking for trouble?"

The man roared, lunging forward with outstretched hands.

His attack was as swift as a hawk diving from the sky, the force of his movement whipping Alexander's hair in the wind as he closed the distance in an instant.

Just as his opponent wore a victorious grin, Alexander sprang into action.

Without even rising from the sofa, he kicked out decisively.
