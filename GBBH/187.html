"It's you! What the hell are you doing here?" Lillian recognized Alexander immediately, her voice sharp with anger.

"Ian, this guy tricked the Fairfax family out of millions! You need to help me get our money back!"

She pouted, shaking Ian's arm like a spoiled child.

Ian smirked, patting her thigh casually.

"That so-called Ultimate Alliance of yours? I wiped the floor with them. Lucky for you, you weren't there, or you'd have been begging me for mercy."

Augusta couldn't hold back his laughter.

"You had over a hundred men, and we had just twenty. You lost dozens, and you couldn't even breach our front door. And you still have the nerve to brag?"

"Shut up!"

Ian slammed his hand down on the table, his face turning a deep shade of red, like a ripe tomato.

"I suggest you hand over the magic powder recipe if you want to walk out of here alive," he warned, his voice low and threatening.

In response, Alexander kicked the coffee table aside with a swift motion.

Drinks and snacks went flying, leaving Ian and Lillian in a mess.

"Ian, you hurt one of my buddies, and I always settle scores. Today, you're a dead man."

Alexander's voice dripped with icy menace.

Ian took a step back, momentarily startled, but then a cruel smile spread across his face.

"You've got some guts, but you're too stupid to know when you're in over your head."

With that, he clapped his hands three times.

Suddenly, the corridor erupted with the sound of heavy footsteps and angry shouts.

Augusta's expression shifted to alarm. "Oh no! We're ambushed!"

"Boss, we'll cover you! You need to get out of here!" Marvin shouted, urgency in his voice.

Ian laughed triumphantly.

"Now you want to run? Too late! I'm about to catch you all like fish in a barrel."

A mob of people surged into the room, filling the corridor with a sea of men—at least a hundred strong.

Each one brandished weapons: machetes, baseball bats, axes, and more.

"Alexander, now you see just how powerful I am? Hand over the magic powder recipe, and maybe I'll let you walk away," Ian said, reclining back in his chair with a smug grin.

Lillian, feeling emboldened, pointed a finger at Alexander.

"You scam artist, give us back our money! I warn you, Jane's hired a top lawyer. Even if you escape today, you'll end up in jail!"

"Get lost!" Alexander waved dismissively.

Lillian let out a yelp as she stumbled backward, crashing to the floor.

"Augusta, Marvin, you guard the entrance. I'll handle the rest," Alexander commanded as he strode toward Ian.

Though he was alone, a commanding presence radiated from him, causing Ian to take several steps back.

"Ewan, get in there! Take him down!" Ian barked, his voice laced with panic.

A massive man with a dragon tattoo on his neck stepped forward, blocking Alexander's path like a brick wall.

He cracked his knuckles, the sound echoing ominously.

"Alexander, I hear you're quite the freeloader. Why don't you lick my boots? Maybe I'll convince the boss to let you off the hook." Ewan Firth sneered, his eyes gleaming with malice.

He was Ian's top enforcer, a man known for taking on multiple opponents at once.

He had a particular taste for delicate-looking men—Alexander fit the bill perfectly.

Without missing a beat, Alexander replied, "Save your breath. You all come at me together; I'll take you down."

Ewan froze, his anger boiling over.

"Alexander, how dare you disrespect me? While I was making a name for myself in the Fraternity, you were still figuring out how to tie your shoes!"

Alexander beckoned with a casual flick of his fingers.

"If you're in such a hurry to die, then cut the crap and come take your beating!"

"You little pretty boy, you're asking for it!"

Ewan exploded in rage, his massive fist swinging like a sledgehammer straight for Alexander's temple.

The force of his punch created a gust of wind as it rocketed forward.

This blow was swift and powerful, akin to a cannonball targeting Alexander's temple.

If it connected, a concussion was the very least Ewan could expect.

Yet, Alexander stood motionless, almost as if frozen in shock, completely unresponsive to the impending danger.
