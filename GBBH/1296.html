Laurie was young, but she was already the head of the obstetrics and gynecology department at Healiva Hospital.

She earned hundreds of thousands of dollars a year, and rumors said she came from a wealthy family.

Yet, she always acted like she was infatuated with Alexander, an ordinary intern.

Something felt off, but Alexander couldn't quite put his finger on what.

"When you pass the exam and officially join the hospital, come to my department," Laurie said with a smile.

"What? Obstetrics and gynecology?"

Alexander looked reluctant. He had always felt that being a male doctor in that field was awkward...for both him and the patients.

"Hmph, and here I thought you'd be honored! Not just anyone gets the chance."

Laurie's voice was sweet and teasing.

Her fingers traced small circles on Alexander's shoulder. A shiver ran down his spine, like an electric current jolting through him.

She wasn't acting like a department head. She was flirting.

Alexander felt his self-control slipping.

"I should go. Enough messing around."

Laurie laughed and dashed back into her car, her eyes glinting with mischief.

The moment Alexander disappeared from her rearview mirror, a white-haired old man dressed in black materialized in the back seat. He was as silent as a ghost.

Frowning, he asked, "Ms. Garcia, is that boy really The Chosen One?"

Laurie's smile vanished.

"You think I'm wrong?" she said coldly.

The old man immediately lowered his head.

"Not at all!

"But Alexander is already in his twenties. He's just a nobody, an insignificant intern. He clearly hasn't received any Ultimate Legacy.

"He doesn't seem like he could ever become a Warrior. Honestly, I doubt he'll even make a great doctor."

"Shut up!"

Laurie cut him off before he could finish.

"Rudolf, who do you think you are? You're just a butler, and you dare lecture me?"

"I would never!" Rudolf said, "I only mean well, Ms. Garcia. Alexander doesn't deserve your attention."

Rudolf bowed lower, his tone more respectful.

Laurie sneered. "Oh? You wouldn't dare? Seems to me you're getting bolder."

"You are the heir of the Garcia family, Mr. Garcia's cherished daughter, and the future of the entire family! I'd risk my life to stop you from making a mistake. That boy is trash!"

Rudolf was visibly agitated.

Laurie's expression darkened.

"And you? You're just a servant. What right do you have to lecture me about the future of the Garcia family?

"My happiness is mine to decide. Even if he's a nobody, I can make him rise to greatness and shake the world!"

Rudolf had never seen Laurie so furious. He was terrified, but deep down, he still refused to accept it.

The Garcia family had been lying low in Kylaville for over twenty years. They weren't as powerful as they once were, but their influence remained strong.

Laurie only worked as a department head for fun.

To Rudolf, she was far above Alexander, who wasn't worthy of even standing in her shadow.

Laurie kept insisting Alexander possessed the Ultimate Legacy, but Rudolf was convinced she was lying.

Resentment boiled inside him. If he could, he would throw Alexander into the river and be done with it.

Laurie narrowed her eyes and spoke calmly. "I know you don't believe me. Let's make a bet. One day, Alexander will become someone you couldn't even dream of reaching."

Rudolf scoffed. "Unless his family wins a billion dollars-dollar lottery one day and buys their way into the Garcia family, he'll always be trash."

He decided he would bypass Laurie and report this straight to her father, Esteban Garcia.

Just then, Laurie muttered to herself, "Vincenzo is trying to eliminate Alexander to make room for his son, Drake."

"Tomorrow's entrance exam... Let's call it Alexander's first trial. I want to see how he handles it."

Alexander's mind was a complete mess. His thoughts bounced between Ava, the Ultimate Legacy, and a total blank.

He still couldn't make sense of what was happening.

Frustrated, he stopped overthinking and focused on meditation.

The trace of the Force inside him surged and roared like a rising tide. Something inside his mind was loosening. Something was fighting to break free.
