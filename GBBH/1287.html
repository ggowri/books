For the next few days, as Alexander drove, Sally rode ahead on her bicycle, wielding a machete to clear the way.

With her help, things became much easier for Alexander.

Under his guidance, Sally had learned to handle most mutant cats and dogs.

Against ordinary zombies, she could take down dozens on her own, as long as she didn't get surrounded.

Whenever they came across a grocery store or supermarket, Alexander would stop to explore.

Before long, their large off-road vehicle was packed to the brim with supplies.

Flour, butter, sausages, canned goods... The car was now stuffed with essentials.

Even Sally's bike had two bulging backpacks strapped to the back.

"It should be around here!"

Alexander pulled over and studied the map.

With his sharp senses and enhanced vision, he quickly spotted the entrance to an underground air-raid shelter.

It was hidden beneath thick bushes. There was a large iron gate, wide enough for a vehicle to pass through, measuring about 13 feet across.

A heavy iron lock hung on the door, rusted with age.

With a swift swing of his sword, Alexander sliced through the lock, and the lock snapped in two, dropping to the ground.

Sally rushed forward and pushed against the gate, but it didn't budge.

"Whoa! It's heavy!" she exclaimed.

She blinked, planted her feet, and used all her strength.

Slowly, the gate groaned and began to move. A gust of cool air rushed out as the door creaked open.

There was no foul smell, no dampness. The ventilation shafts were still working, which meant the place had plenty of oxygen.

Alexander drove the car inside.

After exploring for a while, they realized the air-raid shelter was enormous. Even after walking several hundred yards, they hadn't reached the end.

Some of the deeper tunnels had collapsed, blocking access to the lower levels.

The main chamber stretched hundreds of yards long, more than thirteen feet wide, and several yards high. Their voices echoed in the vast space.

They unloaded all the food from the vehicle and then set out again to raid a few nearby stores.

Before long, one corner of the air-raid shelter was piled high with supplies. There were over a hundred sacks of flour, each weighing twenty pounds.

That alone was enough to feed an adult for years.

On top of that, they had hundreds of pounds of sausages, hundreds of cans of food, plus a generous stash of chocolate, candy, and other snacks.

Alexander even found a generator, a lithium battery storage unit, and several barrels of diesel in the backyard of a grocery store.

After wiring everything up, they had working electric lights again.

Nearly a month had passed since the crisis started, and for the first time, Alexander had proper lighting.

Sally cheered, laughing and jumping like a child under the bright glow of the bulbs.

The air-raid shelter was like a natural refrigerator. Their food would last a long time.

Now, they needed a clean water source. They had collected plenty of bottled water, but for washing clothes and taking baths, they needed something more sustainable.

Alexander wasn't doing all of this for himself. He planned to leave Sally here.

With her current skills and this safe hideout, he believed she would be able to live well.

His own goal remained unchanged. He still wanted to find Yinson. He knew that old bastard was hiding somewhere, probably in an underground base in Kylaville or another secret location.

Alexander had to track him down. With the pure Immortality Serum, he could develop a vaccine and stop the spread of zombies, putting an end to this nightmare.

Suddenly, a deafening rumble of thunder echoed through the air-raid shelter.

"It's raining. Get some rest. I'll make us something to eat."

Sally beamed and sprinted toward their makeshift kitchen.

Alexander had gathered a full set of cooking utensils and built a simple stove out of stones. A fan, rigged with pipes leading outside, acted as a crude ventilation system.

Rain poured steadily outside.

Standing by the iron gate, Alexander watched as the heavy downpour blurred the world beyond.

Water streamed down the sloped entrance, forming a small, continuous creek.

His eyes lit up.

He grabbed a shovel and stepped outside. Sally, unsure of what he was up to, hurried after him.
