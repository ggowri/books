The club erupted with relief, the pallor on their faces giving way to a hint of color.

A group of men in black trench coats, flanking a bald, stern man, marched in. It was Kellen Hunter, the head of the Hunter family.

As soon as he saw Norma, his face twisted in anger.

His son, Matteo, had been left crippled after a run-in with her at the docks. The grudge had burned in him ever since.

Behind Kellen, four massive men dressed in traditional samurai garb stood, their weathered hands and tense postures revealing they were more than just show.

"Mr. Hunter! Help us!" the bartender cried out.

Before Kellen could respond, Alexander grabbed the man by the throat and lifted him effortlessly off the ground.

"Ah!"

The bartender struggled, desperately trying to pry Alexander's fingers loose, but his efforts were futile.

"Mr. Hunter... help..."

He weakly reached out toward Kellen, desperately pleading for help.

Kellen's face reddened with rage. "Let him go! Or I'll kill you!"

Alexander merely tightened his grip, snapping the bartender's neck with a sickening crack.

The bartender's body convulsed briefly before falling limp

With a flick of his wrist, Alexander tossed the lifeless man at Kellen's feet.

"Too much," Alexander said coldly. "You may intimidate others, but not me."

Kellen's face contorted with murderous intent.

He glanced around, his confidence bolstered by the sheer number of his men.

His fierce, predatory glares zeroed in on Alexander.

"Get him! Kill him!" Kellen barked.

The four Risun samurai charged forward, moving like predators closing in on their prey.

Alexander didn't flinch. He struck first.

His fist collided with the chest of the first samurai, the sound of bones shattering echoing in the room as the man was sent flying, his body slamming into the far wall.

Before the second and third could react, Alexander appeared between them, his hands snapping their heads together with a brutal crack.

Blood and brains splattered as their lifeless bodies hit the floor.

The last samurai froze in terror.

He turned to flee, but Alexander's kick sent him crashing into the wall with bone-crushing force, the room shaking with the impact.

In an instant, Kellen's elite warriors were nothing more than broken corpses, and the man who had once been so confident now stood shaking.

One of his subordinates whispered nervously, "Sir, should we fight?"

"Shut up!" Kellen snapped, slapping the man aside.

Then, turning to the rest of his men, he admitted, "We're done. This man is too strong. We can't take him."

As he turned to flee, Alexander's voice rang out coldly.

"Going somewhere? I didn't give you permission to leave."

Kellen turned back, his voice trembling.

"What do you want? I still have men. Are you really ready to die over this?"

Alexander's backhand sent Kellen stumbling, his head spinning.

His men stood frozen, unsure whether to jump in or back off.

Norma, watching the scene unfold, felt a deep admiration for Alexander. He had single-handedly put down Kellen and his entire crew.

"No one leaves," Alexander said firmly, his grip tightening on Kellen's collar.

"Take me to Base One."

Kellen hesitated before a twisted grin spread across his face.

"Fine, I'll take you there. But you're walking straight into your grave. Don't say I didn't warn you."

Base One was the most fortified and secretive stronghold of the Sakra Society.

Alexander was walking straight into their hands, and Kellen was thrilled about it.
