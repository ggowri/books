At the entrance of the Marriott Hotel, several black sedans were lined up, clearly waiting for someone.

In the middle car sat a stunning beauty with skin as tender as a peach, radiating maturity.

She stared at the hotel entrance, her delicate brows furrowed, her watery eyes filled with doubt and worry.

Beside her sat a heavily made-up, seductive woman, chattering away in complaints.

"Brittany, can that Alexander really cure your brother?"

"My uncle is a gray wizard from the City of Sorcery. Even he couldn't do it, so how could a guy from Loscity possibly succeed?"

A hint of displeasure flashed across Brittany's pretty face; her best friend was being too presumptuous.

At that moment, Alexander walked out of the hotel with large strides, scanning the area before heading straight towards them.

"It must be him!"

Brittany curiously observed Alexander, recalling what he looked like in the photos.

Beside her, her best friend wore a disdainful expression.

"He looks so young, hardly even an adult. How could he possibly treat your brother? Did your grandfather make a mistake..."

"Shut up!"

Brittany scolded. "Madison, even though you're my friend and the granddaughter of Mr. Johnson, you can't belittle my grandfather like that."

"Brittany, I'm sorry! I didn't mean it..."

Madison's expression changed; she couldn't afford to offend Quentin's eldest granddaughter.

Brittany got out of the car and greeted Alexander with a smile.

Alexander noticed her immediately; she was wearing a beautiful white dress, her figure sexy and captivating.

He couldn't help but think to himself, "So beautiful!"

"Hello, I'm Alexander. I thought Walter was here to pick me up, but instead, I see a beautiful lady."

"Hello, Mr. Hawthorne. I'm Brittany; Quentin is my grandfather."

After they introduced themselves, they shook hands.

Feeling the warmth of Alexander's hand, Brittany's face turned slightly red; she rarely had physical contact with men.

Madison scrutinized Alexander with a critical gaze.

"You're so young; can you really treat Brittany's brother?"

"I can't guarantee it now, but I'm confident."

Alexander sensed her hostility and was too lazy to say more, following Brittany to get into the car.

Madison disdainfully said, "I think you're just here to scam money, aren't you?"

Alexander glanced at Madison, a dismissive smile appearing on his face.

Through Force Sense, he understood that this woman was not clean.

"Miss, I was invited by Mr. Thompson. Questioning me is the same as questioning Mr. Thompson."

Alexander's words left Madison speechless.

However, Madison was still unwilling to back down and sneered,

"I'm just concerned. If you go and can't help, won't that delay the patient's treatment?"

Alexander rolled his eyes at her.

"You'd better mind your own business and manage yourself. With such a chaotic private life, your condition is already quite serious and will only get worse."

Brittany looked at Alexander in surprise; she didn't expect him to say such things.

Madison was shocked; Alexander was right and had seen through everything. She indeed had a hidden ailment from being too close to men.

But she wouldn't admit it and angrily retorted, "My private life is chaotic? You're slandering me!"

She was so furious that she wanted to slap Alexander, but suddenly, she felt a cramp in her lower abdomen, causing intense pain.

"Ouch..."

Madison crouched down, clutching her stomach, her body breaking out in cold sweat.

"Madison, are you really sick?"

Brittany was shocked.

Madison still stubbornly denied having any illness.

"Brittany, I'm just having menstrual cramps; this guy is talking nonsense..."

Alexander sneered and said, "That kind of ailment can't be cured and will have extremely serious consequences, even making it impossible for you to have children in the future."

Brittany felt that what Alexander said was harsh and hard to believe.

"Mr. Hawthorne, please stop defaming my friend. How could you diagnose her just by looking?"

But Alexander was full of confidence.

"Whether I slandered her or not, she knows it in her heart. It's her own fault that she has a chaotic private life and doesn't pay attention to safety measures. She deserves it."

Madison's heart was filled with fear because Alexander was absolutely right.

How did he know this about her on their first meeting?
