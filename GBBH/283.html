Orion saw Schroder leaving and hurried after him.

"General! You promised to support me as the head of the family. You can't just leave Loscity like this!" Orion called out.

Schroder only stopped after leaving the Hansen Villa, gritting his teeth as he spoke, "Who said we're leaving Loscity? I underestimated that kid. I thought I could suppress Alexander, but I didn't expect him to be this strong."

He patted Orion on the shoulder, letting out a sinister chuckle.

"Don't worry. Give me a few days to gather my men, and I'll make sure you become the head. Alexander won't have a place to die peacefully!"

Orion's excitement returned, and he expressed his gratitude repeatedly.

Meanwhile, Jane was absent-minded, her thoughts filled with Alexander's bold display that had sent Schroder packing.

"Cuff them all and take them away. No one is to be released without my orders," Orion commanded.

A dozen men in black immediately drew their guns, pointing the dark barrels at the crowd.

The Fairfax family was dumbfounded, unable to comprehend why Schroder was doing this.

Jane asked anxiously, "General, wh-what do you mean by this?"

Schroder grabbed her chin, sneering.

"You fool. Did you really think I liked you? You were just bait for Alexander!"

Jane's face turned pale, rendered speechless.

Andrew quickly pleaded, "General, you can't do this to us! We'll help you deal with Alexander, no need to lock us up..."

Before he could finish, a pair of shiny silver handcuffs snapped onto his wrists.

"General!"

Andrew panicked and rushed toward Schroder.

Schroder kicked him aside, knocking him to the ground. Andrew clutched his stomach in pain, groaning loudly.

The Fairfax family members trembled as they were driven away.

At that moment, Jane wished she could die. She had thought she had found a powerful ally, only to realize she was nothing more than bait for Alexander.

...

As soon as Schroder left, the Hansen family's banquet became lively again.

No one had expected Alexander to force a general to retreat, making everyone even keener to curry favor with him.

The room was full of wealthy and powerful individuals, all crowding around Alexander, flattering him while trying to gain some favor.

If Alexander agreed to lower the price of the magic powder just a bit, they could make a lot more profit.

But Alexander had no interest in these hypocrites. He either gave them cold looks or left them for Ava to deal with.

At this moment, Lara approached, whispering a few words to Ava, who nodded repeatedly.

Then, Lara linked her arm with Alexander's and led him inside.

"I've spoken to Ava. Please help my grandfather. If he recovers, I'll give up my position as head of the family."

Lara's beautiful face was filled with sadness.

Since Orion had gained the upper hand, most of the Hansen family members treated her with hostility, and Lara found the unfriendly gazes painful to bear.

"Alexander, sometimes I want to give it all up. I don't want to fight for the head position anymore..." Lara sighed deeply, clutching Alexander's arm tightly.

Alexander gently touched her cheek, comforting her, "Don't worry. I'll do everything I can to help you. The head of the Hansen family will be yours."

The two of them walked closely through a dimly lit corridor and stopped in front of an ornate door.

Alexander immediately sensed a few powerful presences hidden in the darkness, all focused on him.

No wonder the Hansen family was one of the four great families of Loscity; they had strong warriors hidden everywhere.

"Stop right there!"

A man appeared with two men in black, blocking Lara's way.

Lara quickly said, "Mateo, this is Alexander. I'm asking him to treat Grandpa."

Mateo looked at Alexander with a blank expression, sizing him up before asking, "Are you the Alexander who invented the magic powder?"

Alexander nodded. "Yes, that's me."

Mateo spoke in a cold tone, "Miss, Sir is currently meeting an important guest. It's not convenient right now. I suggest you don't go in."
