The hallway was bustling with activity, filled with doctors and nurses rushing past, their expressions cold and indifferent.

Billy approached the front desk nurse and, after a brief exchange, led Alexander to a room at the end of the corridor.

He pushed the door open, revealing a scruffy-looking man who turned to face them, his blue eyes filled with suspicion and wariness.

On the hospital bed lay a frail woman, her body emaciated and an oxygen mask covering her face, utterly still.

The only sign of life was the rhythmic beeping of the monitor beside her, indicating she was still hanging on.

Alexander recognized this man as Augusta.

He was a formidable presence, his muscular frame resembling a coiled spring, ready to unleash power at any moment.

Their eyes locked, and the air crackled with tension, an unspoken challenge passing between them.

Augusta was taken aback, thinking, "This guy is strong!"

Instinctively, he prepared for a fight, ready to unleash a storm of fury if Alexander made a wrong move.

That instinctive combat readiness was deeply ingrained in him.

Alexander sensed Augusta's tension and smiled slightly; it was clear he was a seasoned leader from the Phantom Squad.

In a one-on-one fight, Alexander would easily outmatch Augusta.

However, when it came to a broader skill set—marksmanship, driving, demolitions, sniping, diving—Augusta had the edge.

But with the Force at his disposal, Alexander knew that with just a bit of training, he could surpass Augusta in no time.

Billy quickly interjected, "Augusta, don't be tense. This is Alexander Hawthorne, the guy I told you about. He's here to help."

Augusta's posture stiffened, surprise washing over him.

He hadn't expected this young man to be Billy's boss.

Alexander approached the bedside, using the Force to assess the woman's condition.

After a moment, he sighed softly.

Unfortunately, her illness was too severe; even with the Force, he could only alleviate her suffering, not cure her completely.

"Can you heal her?" Augusta asked, a hint of skepticism in his voice.

Alexander replied with a gentle smile, "I can't cure her, but I can help ease her pain and improve her quality of life."

"Really? You're not pulling my leg, are you?" Augusta's excitement was palpable as he struggled to his feet, favoring his left leg.

"Why would I lie to you? You don't have anything I want," Alexander replied calmly, placing his hands on the woman's body and channeling the Force.

As he concentrated, beads of sweat began to form on his forehead.

The woman, who had been in a deep coma, slowly opened her eyes.

Her once-pale complexion began to show a hint of color, a flicker of life returning to her gaze.

She gazed at Alexander with an intense, unwavering look, her demeanor both delicate and sorrowful, exuding a unique, haunting grace.

"Darling, you're finally awake..." Augusta exclaimed, tears streaming down his face as he embraced his wife tightly.

"Have I been asleep for a long time? I'm so sorry for worrying you!"

Just then, a doctor entered, and upon witnessing the scene, he gasped in disbelief. "My God! You're awake! This is nothing short of a miracle!"

Several doctors and nurses rushed in, drawn by the commotion.

The hospital had issued multiple death warnings, expecting the woman wouldn't last another few days.

Yet here she was, inexplicably awake and speaking coherently—an astonishing turn of events.

Augusta realized he had encountered someone extraordinary. He thought, "No wonder this young man is Billy's boss; he is not just anyone."

With heartfelt sincerity, he turned to Alexander and pleaded, "Mr. Hawthorne, can you help my wife regain her health? I'm willing to pay any price!"

Alexander gave a wry smile and patted Augusta on the shoulder.

"It's not that I don't want to help. It's just that there's only so much I can do. All I can offer is to ease her suffering and help her leave with some dignity."

Augusta fell silent, the weight of the words settling heavily in the room.

The woman quickly called Augusta over and whispered something in his ear.

He nodded repeatedly, tears welling up in his eyes.

With a sharp salute, he turned to Alexander.

"Mr. Hawthorne, if you don't mind, I'm offering you my life in return."
