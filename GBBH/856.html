She suddenly realized the ambiguity in her words and blushed, leaning against Alexander's arm.

"Alexander, as you can see, Sean wants me dead. Will you help me fight him? I'm willing to pay any price, even if it means offering myself."

As she finished, Norma leaned into Alexander's ear, her breath warm, voice sultry like a purring cat.

Alexander could only smile. This woman was truly set on seducing him.

"Where's Sean?"

Norma was startled. "What? You're going to act now? We need a plan, to strategize, right?"

She pressed closer to Alexander, and the two walked slowly through the hallway, heading for her office to talk in detail.

Marvin and a few Dragon Gang bodyguards followed closely behind.

A few more bodyguards walked ahead of Alexander and Norma, keeping others at bay.

Suddenly, a seemingly average-looking man emerged from a group of gamblers passing by.

He kept his head slightly lowered, appearing as just another gambler, walking briskly toward Alexander and Norma without drawing any suspicion.

"Something's off!"

Marvin suddenly focused on the man, sensing an intense, murderous aura that seemed to strangle him, making every hair on his body stand on end.

It was the same terror as being in a sniper's crosshairs—inescapable.

Marvin was highly skilled now, his sensitivity to murderous intent far surpassing that of ordinary fighters.

But Alexander appeared oblivious, continuing forward without even glancing at the man.

Norma was even more unaware, still whispering softly into Alexander's ear.

The bodyguards ahead noticed the man walking briskly toward them and immediately grew angry.

"Idiot! Watch where you're going! How dare you block the boss' path."

"Move!"

The man suddenly roared, like a tiger descending a mountain.

The bodyguards flinched, their steps halting as they froze, faces turning pale.

What a formidable assassin!

Alexander smiled slightly, his cold gaze locking onto the man.

It was an ordinary face, one that wouldn't hint at his deadly nature if not for the exposed killing intent.

But Alexander had noticed him long ago; the man exuded a bloodthirsty aura deep down.

Ordinary people might never encounter such an aura in their lifetime, nor understand its true fear.

It was a terror unleashed through cold eyes and expressions, one that made people shudder.

The spring-like sparkle in Norma's eyes turned to sheer terror.

Just then, Norma's vision blurred as the man seemed to float toward them like a ghost.

It only looked like floating because he moved so quickly, leaving a trail of afterimages.

He was still several miles and a few bodyguards away from Alexander and Norma.

In the blink of an eye, the man darted past the bodyguards and appeared in front of Norma.

Marvin roared, "I know who you are! You're the Killer King! One of the top-ranked assassins, the slaughterer among mercenaries."

Marvin stepped forward, blocking the Killer King's path.

This was a top assassin, skilled in martial arts rather than firearms, making him far more difficult to handle.

"Heh!"

The Killer King sneered, stepping with an odd rhythm, nimbly evading Marvin and reaching Norma's side.

"Fool! Get back!"

Marvin yelled and swung a fierce hook behind him, the force whistling as it aimed for the Killer King's face.

The Killer King smirked, ducking low with an unnaturally awkward movement, dodging Marvin's strike.

At the same time, a small katana appeared in the Killer King's hand, which he drove mercilessly toward Norma's heart.

The clash lasted only a moment, with the Killer King gaining a slight advantage, as Marvin was more skilled in long-range assassination.

Norma was seconds away from being killed by the thrust of the blade!
