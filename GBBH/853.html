There were only four people in the VIP room, Alexander, Marvin standing behind him, Benjamin, and the beautiful dealer.

The first card was dealt to Benjamin.

He extended his right hand, gently covering the card without lifting it.

But Benjamin already knew what his card was.

A faint flush appeared on his previously tense face.

During the dealer's shuffle, he had memorized every card.

Every gambling expert possessed an extraordinary memory.

They used the tiniest imperfections on the cards to tell them apart.

Benjamin had been studying gambling since he was four; now, it had been sixty years.

He considered himself the king of the casino, capable of switching cards at lightning speed.

So skillful that even slow-motion surveillance footage couldn't catch his tricks.

Alexander, however, didn't touch his card; his X-ray vision allowed him to see it clearly without any effort.

As for Benjamin's prized card-switching technique, Alexander found it laughably slow.

Alexander smiled slightly. Too bad—even with Benjamin's skill, he stood no chance of winning.

When Alexander entered, he had already used Force Control on Benjamin, impacting his mental state without him even realizing it.

Whatever Benjamin saw or touched was just an illusion Alexander had implanted in his mind.

Benjamin was completely fooled by the illusion, a state worse than being blind or deaf.

Alexander was merely putting on a show, playing just to impress Norma. Winning was always within his grasp.

He was also watching Norma's reaction.

He wanted to see if she would be worth investing in.

Otherwise, Alexander wouldn't hesitate to take control of Sean and let him run the Dragon Gang.

"I'm all in!" Alexander declared confidently.

Alexander tilted his head, looking at Benjamin with an air of arrogance.

"Ha! You fool, do you even know how to play? You're going all in after just one card?" Benjamin laughed, clutching his stomach.

In the surveillance room, someone yelled angrily, "Boss, is this Alexander guy messing around? You gave him a billion in chips, and he's going all in right away?"

Even the other three gambling experts chimed in.

"Yeah, this bet is way too risky; he's bound to lose."

"Is he working with Benjamin? Why else would he go all in with a billion at the start?"

"Benjamin is the king of gambling; he'll definitely call!"

Norma couldn't help but frown.

Alexander's strategy was unexpected. Was he setting her up for failure?

"Benjamin has never lost, and we've already suffered heavy losses. We can't afford to lose again!"

"Boss, let me bring him back. Stop him from playing."

"This is planned; that guy has bad intentions..."

The chatter in the once-quiet surveillance room grew louder, turning it into a noisy marketplace.

"Shut up, all of you!" Norma shouted.

Norma's angry outburst silenced the room instantly.

She still believed Alexander had a reason for his actions and wasn't doing it on purpose.

"If anyone talks nonsense without proof again, I'll kill them myself." Norma's voice dripped with menace.

Her threat silenced everyone completely.

At the table, Alexander pushed the billion in chips to the center and looked at the camera.

"Add four billion more! Bring it now!"

The commanding tone left Benjamin stunned.

In no time, several staff members brought over a large case of chips, stacking it in the center of the table.

The neatly arranged, imposing stack of chips made an impressive sight.

"All in. Bet everything you have! Do you dare to call?" Alexander taunted Benjamin with a grin.

Alexander smiled provocatively at Benjamin.

Without a moment's hesitation, Benjamin pushed all his wealth into the pot—five billion dollars.

The ten-billion-dollar pot stunned the dealer.

She was a seasoned VIP dealer who had seen countless games but had never encountered stakes this high.
