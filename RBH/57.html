The scene at the dinner party was bustling with people, and Elliott was constantly socializing throughout. He skillfully raised and clinked glasses, showcasing his business elite demeanor to the fullest.

Time passed quickly, and gradually, the whole dinner finally approached its end, and the guests also left in pairs.

I rubbed my sore neck and slipped into the lounge to take off this gorgeous yet cumbersome dress, and changed into casual clothes.

Paige and Quinn invited me to have a drink after the show, and I agreed with them. After all, after dealing with such a night, I was very tired and really needed to relax.

I changed my clothes and returned to the venue. Most of the guests had already left, and Elliott was standing with his hands in his pockets, bidding farewell to a client.

I instinctively walked up to him, intending to explain something to him, but to my surprise, at that moment, I saw a waiter not far from him suddenly giving me a strange look.

He stared at Elliott's figure intently, then suddenly threw the tablecloth he was holding in his hand, and instantly had a shiny knife in his hand.

The lights in the venue were bright, and the knife gleamed with a cold light, stabbing straight towards Elliott's chest.

This sudden scene made my blood freeze instantly, and I hurriedly shouted, "Elliott, be careful!"

As soon as I finished speaking, at that very moment, a figure shot past me like an arrow.

The next second, I heard a girl's scream!

Everything happened so fast, within just a few seconds, so fast that there was no time to think.

When I came to my senses, several bodyguards had already swarmed and pinned the male waiter to the ground.

In Elliott's hands, he held a fallen girl, wearing a pristine white dress. Blood horrifyingly spread from her waist, and soon, on that pristine white hem, bloomed one after another dazzling and eerie flowers...

White dress, white shoes, ponytail, my brain couldn't help but go "boom" instantly!

It was Giselle!

I didn't expect that the figure that just ran past me was actually her!

I didn't expect that she would actually rush over and block the knife for Elliott!

Originally, even though I had set obstacles for their encounter, even though Elliott had been gradually changing his attitude towards me, the destined outcome of their meeting was still something I couldn't change.

They eventually met, and at this moment, Giselle, weakly lying in Elliott's arms, not only that, but she also became Elliott's lifesaver.

My feet went weak beneath me, cold sweat broke out in the palms of my hands, my head was buzzing, and I felt completely numb all over.

I watched helplessly as my husband picked up the injured Giselle, and then he roared excitedly.

"What are you standing there for! Hurry up, drive, and take the person to the hospital!"

Elliott's voice was so tense, he furrowed his brow and stared unblinkingly at Giselle, who he held tightly in his arms, with one hand firmly covering the wound on her body.

Blood oozed from the gaps in his large palm, appearing horrifyingly shocking. He lowered his head, seemingly comforting Giselle continuously.

And in his arms, Giselle, even though her face was filled with a painful expression, still managed to force a faint smile, her lips moved as if she was saying to him:

"I was fine, you didn't need to worry."

Their encounter, in the end, finally began.

This plot is far more profound and unforgettable than playing the piano.

Why wasn't I the one who just rushed over?

Why didn't I think of rushing over to block Elliott from getting stabbed like that?

My whole heart was plunged into chaos, and all my strength seemed to be instantly drained. I felt like I had become a wandering soul, just watching my husband, holding the girl he was about to fall in love with, go away until they completely disappeared from my sight.

A warm palm, at this moment, gently rested on my shoulder...
