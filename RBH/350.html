On this night, for the first time, we lay together in the same bed in my former room.

Elliott held me as tightly as he clutched a treasure that he had finally regained.

My bed was soft and surrounded by beautiful white curtains. Lily had kept these things that I adored as a girl.

Even my comforter still exuded a sweetly fruity fragrance.

I had no strength at all, but my mind remained active.

I asked Elliott, "Have you read every page of my diary?"

"Yeah, I have."

His hoarse and magnetic voice came pleasantly into my ears.

"You don't have to take some words in it seriously. I was too young at the time to understand what those words mean."

I vaguely remembered the many passionate love declarations within those pages, including promises of eternal love and vows to follow him to the ends of the earth, even if it meant I would become the lowly one.

Those were all the wishful thoughts of a girl blinded by infatuation.

The girl who penned those words was a distant memory. It was impossible for me to consider Elliott as my one and only, as I had before.

I wasn't even sure how I felt about him now. If I had no feelings for him at all, I wouldn't have been able to be so obsessed with him again and again.

But if I said I loved him, I always felt that I was no longer the same person I used to be. Therefore, whether I loved him or not was a bit vague to me now.

"I felt your sincere love, which made me touched. I didn't know before that you had done so many things for me silently. At that time, I mistook your love for childish infatuation."

Elliott smiled in my ear. He reached out to squeeze my waist lovingly and kissed my cheek.

"Why were you so disgusted with me before?"

Thinking of the past, I turned around. In the darkness, I blinked and looked at his delicate jaw.

He took a deep breath and then calmly gave me an answer.

"Because no man is willing to exchange his marriage for assistance to his family. I wasn't disgusted with you. I just couldn't accept this."

It was the first time I had heard Elliott explain this.

I finally figured out that he never thought I was bad. Instead, this marriage had wounded his pride.

"So you didn't hate me at that time, did you?"

I couldn't help but ask.

A chuckle rumbled in his chest, followed by a gentle kiss on my lips.

"That was true at first, but then you became too annoying. You always got angry with me, crossed the line, and always appeared in places where I didn't like your presence. At that time, you were not as sensible as you are now."

He was probably afraid that the truth would hurt me, so he kissed me for a while as soon as he finished speaking.

I thought, "I was indeed such a person during the first two years of my marriage to him. The way I expressed my love was too arbitrary. I was willful then.

Because I was eager to get his attention, I made him hate me further. He would rather hook up with other women outside than stay home with me."

Although I admitted that I did not do well, I still felt angry when I thought of the harm he had done to me at that time.

I pushed his hand away, pretending to be angry.

"No, I am and will be willful. Even if we are in a sweet love now, it doesn't mean anything. I must still be myself. If you can't accept it, you can continue to hate me. It's okay."
