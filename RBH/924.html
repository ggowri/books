"Stay in the bath a bit longer. I'll be outside having a smoke. Call me when you're done."

Whether it was hormones or the sweetness of the night, just a glimpse of his back made my heart flutter.

The bathroom finally became quiet.

My racing heart gradually calmed down.

Warm hands lifted me out of the water.

Our bodies pressed together, and a powerful electric current surged through me.

"I'm sorry for all you've been through."

This feeling of a shared bloodline could only be understood by parents.

Only by raising a child together could a man feel such deep, heartfelt guilt.

"Do you really mean that?"

I bit my lip, unable to hold back my tears.

I've been strong all this time, facing every storm alone. From pregnancy to childbirth, I never shed a tear or regretted my decisions.

But now, as Elliott spoke those words, I couldn't help but tremble. A flood of deep-seated grievances surged within me, overwhelming and unstoppable.
