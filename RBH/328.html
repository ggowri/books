My hand started trembling uncontrollably, and I couldn't even hold the glass steady, causing the wine to splash onto the table.

If Quinn hadn't grabbed my wrist tightly in time, I was afraid the glass would have fallen to the ground.

"Is it true? Are you really pregnant? Is it the child of the CEO of Bryant Group?"

"Oh, my goodness, I'm so excited. I feel like I'm going to cry!"

"Giselle, tell us the truth. Is the child you're carrying Elliott's? I'm so happy for you!"

They were clearly shocked by Giselle's words and began bombarding her with questions.

I couldn't take my eyes off Giselle, observing every subtle expression on her face.

She seemed genuinely ecstatic. With their compliments, her joy turned into pride. Giselle couldn't help but instinctively touch her abdomen...

Her joy of soon becoming a mother didn't seem fake.

And then, her following words hit me like a thunderbolt, leaving me unable to come to my senses for a long time.

"If I am really pregnant, then whose child could it be other than Elliott's?"

This sentence felt like a sharp knife stabbing directly into my heart.

My heart felt like it was torn open, bleeding profusely.

I thought, "Was Giselle implying that she is pregnant with Elliott's child?

But how could that be possible?

If Elliott really loves her, then why would he show such deep affection towards me?

Moreover, his parents and grandfather are so eager for a child. If he knows Giselle is pregnant, he will definitely divorce me immediately. Why would he deliberately delay the divorce with me?"

At this point, even Quinn fell into silence. His smiling face turned into a frowning one.

Quinn lowered his head to look at his phone, his fingers tapping the keyboard, seemingly chatting with someone. But I couldn't see the content on his phone screen.

The joyful atmosphere at their table continued.

Upon hearing the news of Giselle's pregnancy, a guy at their table loudly called the waiter to bring a bottle of champagne. They wanted to celebrate for Giselle.

In contrast, our table seemed so gloomy and desolate.

My heart was in turmoil.

I grabbed the glass on the table and poured myself another drink without hesitation.

"You shouldn't do that. This is craft beer. Drinking too much can make you drunk."

"Don't worry about it. I want to drink. Don't stop me. Let me drink."

A wave of endless bitterness surged in my heart. I pushed Quinn's hand away, poured another glass, and drank it all by myself.

"Madeline, don't do this! Things may not be as we think. Let's clarify the situation first, okay?"

Quinn saw that I was struggling to control my emotions and grabbed my wrist, taking the glass from my hand.

He just didn't want me to drink, but perhaps because we were struggling for the glass, Quinn accidentally spilled all the wine in the glass backwards.

The golden beer drew a parabola and all splashed onto the head of a guy at the next table.

"What's going on? Who spilled beer on my head?"

The guy immediately exclaimed and stood up, leaning over to look in our direction.

Quinn quickly turned around to apologize.

"I'm sorry, buddy. It was an accident. We didn't mean to."
