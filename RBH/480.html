I was lost in thought when Elliott suddenly stirred awake.

His hand reached out and landed on my knee, causing my heart to skip a beat. Hastily setting aside his phone, I quickly explained, "Barry called just now. He couldn't find you and was worried. Are you feeling any better? Marcus is preparing medicine for you. Let's see if it helps."

Elliott opened his eyes and felt the burning pain from the fever in his eyes.

He asked in a hoarse voice, "Where am I?"

"You're at Marcus' place," I replied promptly.

Struggling to turn his head, he surveyed his surroundings with a hint of dissatisfaction.

"Why did you bring me here?"

I thought, "I can't tell him that he has a highly contagious viral flu, so I can't take him to my place, right?"

I heard the sound from the kitchen and saw Marcus coming out with a bowl of medicine.

Quickly, I said to Elliott, "Marcus has effective cold medicine here. You're awake now, and he's ready with it."

Elliott struggled to sit up from the couch, but his body was too weak to do so. Seeing this, I moved over and used my arms to prop him up, letting him lean against me.

At that moment, Marcus brought a bowl of herbal soup in front of us.

"Mr. Bryant, you're awake just in time. This medicine is for you. Drink it while it's hot. Once you sweat, your fever will break."

Eyeing the dark concoction, Elliott instinctively frowned.

He grumbled in his hoarse voice, "I never drink this stuff."

I recalled Elliott's aversion to herbal remedies. Every time Laura arranged tonics for his health, he never even glanced at them, instructing Sean to dispose of them directly.

"You've been in the hospital for days with no improvement in your fever. This is our last resort. Be cooperative and drink it!"

I urged, spooning a dose towards his mouth.

In the past, I had been fearful of him, speaking to him in a gentle tone. Now, perhaps due to a lack of love, my tone had turned authoritative.

After my outburst, Elliott's body visibly trembled.

Seeing the spoon at his lips, he reluctantly opened his mouth.

Seizing the moment, I poured a spoonful of the bitter medicine into his mouth.

Forced to swallow, Elliott grimaced, muttering, "Bitter..."

"Bitter medicine cures illness. More!"

I didn't mince words, offering another spoonful.

Reluctantly, Elliott opened his mouth again. Despite the bitterness, he obediently swallowed.

Watching him grimace in pain while swallowing, I couldn't help but feel a sense of vindictive pleasure.

I thought, "Who told you to be so arrogant before?

Now, I won't be as kind to you."

With each spoonful, I poured the medicine down his throat.

Ill in bed, Elliott had transformed from a tiger to a sickly cat. His former dignity and aura were completely absent as he slumped in my arms, resembling a child in dire need of support.

Witnessing me feed him the entire bowl of medicine, Marcus smirked but refrained from commenting.
