After receiving my compliment, Rowan appeared somewhat bashful.

He quickly set down the lunch box and then said, "Madeline, you've been standing for a while. How about coming to my office for a bit? It's a bit messy with all the ongoing cases, but you're welcome to hang out."

Rowan's workplace was a bustling office, with everyone working together in a shared office space, leading to a clutter of various documents on the desks.

"No worries, we don't want to distract you from your work. I'll just wander around with my friend," I replied with a smile, gesturing for Rowan to attend to his duties.

Subsequently, Fiona and I strolled off in another direction.

Alexis' law firm was quite accommodating, with not only office spaces but also a staff gym and meeting rooms for client discussions, along with a small coffee bar for employees to relax.

I led Fiona into the coffee bar, and after each of us brewed a cup of coffee, we settled down in a corner.

The coffee bar's corner was adorned with numerous green plants. The sunlight streaming in from the floor-to-ceiling windows. Basking in the sun on such a day was particularly enjoyable.

Fiona chuckled and asked me, "How do you know that guy we just met?"

"He's my assistant's college classmate. I recommended him for the job here," I replied with a smile.

"I see, no wonder he looked at you with such gratitude," Fiona said, taking a sip of her coffee.

"Alexis mentioned he's been doing quite well," I said.

"Paige told me that one night, after a few drinks, she and Philip accidentally ended up sleeping in the same room."

"Hard to believe, right? Not only for me, but even Paige herself didn't anticipate it. She always saw Philip as a good friend, but that night, they ended up in the same bed, she completely bewildered when they woke up."
