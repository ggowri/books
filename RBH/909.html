A torrential downpour, accompanied by lightning and thunder, quickly cascaded from the sky like a waterfall.

I couldn't bother to answer my mom. I immediately ran to the window, pulled back the curtain, and saw Elliott still standing right there in the rain, holding the flowers.

What was he doing?

Why won't he leave in such heavy rain?

I anxiously banged on the window, trying to urge him to leave.

My mom walked over, still angry. She shut the curtain and said to me coldly:

"He can stand in the rain as long as he wants! He has nothing to do with us anymore. You swore you'd never see him again. Madeline, don't let his tricks got you moved. Remember, he didn't hesitate at all when he made you get a divorce!"

My mom has always been gentle and had never been this furious.

It was clear how much she now resented Elliott.

I understood that, though she didn't say it. Ever since Noah was born, she treasured him and felt even more sorrow for my past.

Moreover, she feared that the Bryant family might use any means necessary to fight for Noah if they discovered who his father was.

My dad also walked over, glanced deeply out the window, and then asked me:

"Madeline, does he know about Noah? Last time his mom came here and made a scene, and now he's here. Are they trying to take Noah from us?"

"Dad, just let him in first. He's already in poor health. Last time in Norch City, he was severely injured and unconscious for rescuing me. If he keeps standing in the rain and something happens to him, we won't be able to afford and explain to the Bryant family."

I looked at my dad and said seriously.

"It was Quinn who led the rescue. Don't let him fool you."

My mom quickly shook her head in denial when she heard what I said.

"I thought it was Quinn too, but later I found out that Elliott got there before Quinn and the rescue team.

He went there alone. When Quinn and the military arrived, Elliott had already been pushed down a slope by the kidnappers, so they didn't notice him."

I explained the situation. Hearing this, my dad was stunned and asked me, half-believing:

"Really? Madeline, are you sure about this?"

I nodded firmly:

"Yes. He was severely injured and unconscious because of me. He still has a blood clot in his brain that can't be removed, causing memory loss. He only remembers the best times we had together.

Dad, please let him in. I'm really afraid something might happen to him if he keeps standing out there."

I looked at my dad, my eyes pleading.

Outside, the storm raged on, with the sound of wind and rain howling like a beast, tearing at my heart.

I admit that I started to feel a bit of pity for Elliott.

Seeing him stand out there, my heart couldn’t help but ache.

However, I knew him. Once he made a decision, no one could change it.

"No, what if he comes in and sees Noah? Noah looks so much like him. If he sees Noah, he'll know it’s his son. What will we do then?"

My mom, despite her tough words, was soft-hearted. She wavered a bit but still hesitated to let him in because of Noah.

"Let Malina take Noah to the nursery and keep him there. Just let Elliott in to get out of the rain. Mom, I know you hate him now, but he's still Noah's father."

I said, looking at my mom.

A mother knows her daughter best. At that moment, my mom seemed to understand something from my eyes. She grabbed my shoulders and asked anxiously:

"Madeline, be honest with me. Are you having feelings for him again? You can't do that! You can't stumble over the same stone twice.

Besides, Quinn is way much better than him. He's nice and so is his family. His parents treat you well, and they get along well with us. Don't make the same mistake again."

"Mom, I'm not. I'm not thinking about that right now. I just think it's not right for him to stand out there."

I shook my head, trying hard to hide my true feelings.

Then my dad let out a heavy sigh:

"Let him come in first. The weather forecast says a typhoon might be coming tonight. If something happens to him at our place, we can't afford the consequences. The Bryant family might tear Madeline apart."

With my dad's words, my mom finally nodded, agreeing to let Elliott in.

The sound of wind and rain grew louder outside. I quickly opened the door with an umbrella.

Elliott was still standing there, completely soaked. The once beautiful bouquet in his hands had been battered by the storm, but he stubbornly held onto it.

The wind was too strong to hold the umbrella. I struggled to reach him, grabbed his cold fingers, and said:

"Elliott, come in. My parents agreed to let you in."

Elliott's dull eyes finally lit up. He looked at me and forced a difficult smile.

His fingers were ice-cold, and rain slapped his face. His features look even more striking with rain.

Seeing me getting wet too, he quickly shielded my head with his hand. Then, he followed me inside.

I immediately shut the door.

Reluctantly, my mom brought out a clean towel.

Ignoring my own wet clothes, I hurried to wipe the water off his face and head.

But he stubbornly shook his head, took the towel from me, and started drying me first.

Water had pooled on the floor, and Elliott was completely drenched. His lips were bloodless from the cold.

But he stood tall and straight, gently drying me with a tender look in his eyes that made my heart pounding.

I pressed his hand down:

"I'm ok. You're soaked. I'll get you some of my dad's clothes. Go take a hot shower and change."

"Okay, but I want to see your parents first."

After drying me off, Elliott scanned the room and finally fixed his gaze on my silent parents sitting on the sofa.

At that moment, he ignored his drenched state, holding the battered bouquet, and walked resolutely toward my parents.
