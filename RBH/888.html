Elliott wouldn't usually spill such intimate words in situations like this before.

In the past, he was always the silent type. He seldom spoke, leaving others to guess his intentions.

But now, he's openly flaunting his love for me, even going so far as to wear matching couple outfits identical to mine.

For a moment, their mouths hung open in surprise, and I could feel my cheeks flush with embarrassment.

"Mr. Bryant, who dares to steal your lady? Mr. Bryant, you must be joking."

"Yeah, Mr. Bryant, who would've thought you loved your wife so much."

"In that case, Mr. Bryant, how about you and your wife team up? That way, you won't have to worry about us stealing her away..."

Everyone's joking around.

"Alright, by the way, I can take a moment to appreciate my lady's skills in the game."

Elliott didn't refuse. He walked straight towards me in public, casually slinging his arm over my shoulder.

I was mortified, yet I didn't have the heart to reject him in front of the crowd.

And so, everyone swiftly reorganized the teams. Elliott and I ended up in the same group.

It seemed as if everyone was intentionally creating an opportunity for us to be alone. They took a few casual swings and hurried off to the next location, leaving just the two of us behind.

It's rare to see Elliott out of his suit and in casual wear.

Decked out in this white tracksuit, Elliott not only looked taller, but also fairer. The usually brooding him seemed more vibrant, leaving me somewhat dazed. Suddenly, I was reminded of the memory of him in his school uniform, riding his bicycle.

"Give it a shot, I've yet to see you play ball."

Catching my gaze, Elliott flashed a casual smile. He reached out, taking my hand, urging me to give it a try.

"I've never seen you play before, this is the first time."

I said nonchalantly.

"Yes, there's a lot we haven't been through together. But from now on, I don't want any more regrets between us."

Elliott spoke slowly, his voice tinged with regret.

I took my stance, gripping the club, eyeing the white ball. My hand began to swing in a steady rhythm, my heart pounding in my chest.

I didn't initially feel any pressure, but the way Elliott kept staring at me non-stop, it inexplicably made me a bit nervous.

Without thinking, I took a swing. Embarrassingly, I didn't even hit the ball. In stark contrast, Elliott had just sunk the ball in one shot.

I shot Elliott an awkward glance, hastily explaining:

"This is definitely not my usual standard. Let me give it another shot, just watch."

Elliott burst into laughter:

"Your swing just now wasn't quite up to par. Let me show you how it's done."

Elliott stood directly behind me, his hands reaching around to grasp my left and right hand respectively. Then, he used his body to guide mine, swaying together in unison.

Meanwhile, he was teaching and whispering some pointers and key notes to me.

His breath fanned against my earlobe.

His voice was a raspy yet gentle whisper, his hands seemingly magical. Under his guidance, I quickly found my rhythm, swinging out with a forceful stroke.

This stick is absolutely fantastic.

The ball effortlessly rolled into the hole, and immediately, Elliott broke into applause:

"Absolutely fantastic! You've got a real knack for this!"

"Is that so?"

I flashed a smile, then gestured for him to step back. I adjusted my stance, found the right angle, and then, I took a swing all by myself.
