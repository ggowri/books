Elliott clutched his mouth in feigned pain, yet a clear glint of amusement danced in his eyes.

Instead of getting angry at my harsh words, he stepped forward, taking hold of my hand:

"It's my fault. I just miss you too much. Seeing you, I can't help but want to kiss you. Hit me, hit me as you please."

He grabbed my hand and slammed it against his chest.

I was indeed angry, and I pounded on his chest a few times. Then I remembered that he had been injured there, and I quickly stopped.

Elliott didn't flinch, letting me pound on him. A faint, satisfied smile still lingered on his face. Seeing me stop, he asked me:

"Are you cooled off now? If you are, let's head home."

He finished speaking and smoothly hooked his arm around my waist, intending to lead me away.

He was already so unhinged just now. If I were to go back with him, who knows what he might do.

In a rush, I pushed him away, taking two steps back:

"Elliott, you head on home. I've got some business to take care of at the company."

Elliott swiveled around, giving me a puzzled look:

"What's the matter? Madeline, why have you turned so cold towards me now?"

He looked utterly innocent, completely baffled by my indifference.

Forget it. He's lost his memory. Right now, his mind is stuck in that sweetest time before our divorce. It's only natural he'd be surprised.

With that thought, I could only muster the patience to say in a nonchalant manner:

"All I want is to do my job well and manage the business of Glory Group. I really can't go back now. I'll have Barry take you home to rest."

"Alright, I'll have dinner ready. Just remember to come back and eat."

Elliott was still confused, but he didn't press me for answers.

"I might not be able to make it back. I've been staying at my mom's place lately, so you'll have to fend for yourself."

I was terrified he might actually wait for me to eat. So, I hastily said.

Elliot looked at me, a mix of helplessness and melancholy in his eyes. He raised his hand, pressing the corners of his brows. The moment he lowered his head, he looked like a picture of bottled-up grievances:

"Are you still upset with me for not having dinner with you before, Madeline? I promise you, I'll change. I won't treat you with the same coldness as before. Please, don't be so cold to me either, okay?"

Elliott's gaze was fixed on me, a boundless tenderness in his eyes that left me momentarily stunned.

Is he truly suffering from amnesia, or is he just trying to win me back?

I suddenly found myself a bit confused.

But no matter what he does, I won't be swayed again, easily falling into his tender trap.

"Elliott, I'm not brushing you off, I really do have things to handle. Besides, we both have our careers, there's no need to make this all melodramatic, don't you think?"

I rubbed my temples. I had softened my tone as much as possible to accommodate his condition. But to utter those intimate words, I just couldn't do it.

"Alright, I got it."

Elliott didn't persist. He just offered a faint smile, tinged with a hint of melancholy.

I hastily left the hospital, driving straight back home.

I've been by Elliot's side these past few days, haven't seen Noah in a while.

As soon as I stepped through the door, I made a beeline for Noah's nursery. I was eager to see if my little one had grown.

My dad and mom are gathered around Noah's crib, teasing him. They're laughing joyfully.
