"Isn't that Mr. Bryant's ex-wife? What's she doing here?"

"Yeah, they seemed like a perfect match. I wonder why they suddenly got divorced."

"Didn't you hear? I heard it was because of the relationship between Mr. Bryant and Giselle from the planning department. She hasn't been coming to work recently, right? I heard she's even pregnant and now living in the Bryant Villa."

"Really? How come I haven't heard anything about this? Why would Mr. Bryant be interested in her? I think his ex-wife is much prettier than her."

"Exactly. Have you seen the latest issue of Lotus Forum? It's the speech given by Mr. Bryant's ex-wife, she was absolutely brilliant, a role model for us young people to learn from."

As I made my way towards the elevator, the surrounding discussions filled my ears clearly.

I pretended not to hear anything and walked straight to the VIP elevator reserved for Elliott.

A security guard extended his hand to stop me, saying, "Madam, please hold on!"

I shot him a cold glare. "Who are you stopping? Do you know who I am?"

He was taken aback but insisted, "Madam, this is Mr. Bryant's private elevator. You can take another one."

"What if I say no? Call Barry now, tell him Madeline is here and demands to be picked up!" I arrogantly stated, staring him down.

Seeing that I had mentioned Barry's name directly, he hurriedly dialed Barry.

Soon enough, Barry descended into the elevator, sweating profusely, and approached me.

"Mrs. Bryant, you... How come you're here?"

"You're mistaken, it should be 'former Mrs. Bryant.' I'm here to speak with Elliott about something. Is he in?" I inquired directly.

Barry, still looking fearful, replied, "Mr. Bryant is a bit busy at the moment."

"Barry, we've known each other for so long, can you stop trying to deceive me with this act? I'm going up now, and if you dare stop me, give it a shot!" I challenged.

"I... I wouldn't dare!"

Barry couldn't even lift his head and promptly pressed the elevator button, gesturing gentlemanly for me to enter first.

I smirked disdainfully, walked into Elliott's designated elevator, pressed the button for the top floor, and arrived at Elliott's office door shortly after.

Pushing open the door to Elliott's office, I found him seated in a large boss chair. He was frowning, deeply engrossed in studying the documents on his desk.

Elliott looked up and was taken aback, asking in astonishment, "How come you're here?"

I shrugged off my coat and bag and sat directly in front of his desk.

Staring at him, I said, "Elliott, I never expected the Bryant family to play such a ruthless game of betrayal!"

My words caused a slight change in Elliott's expression.

A hint of confusion appeared on Elliott's face as he set aside the documents and calmly asked, "What do you mean by that?"
