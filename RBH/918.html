I instinctively froze.

But when I tried to look closely, the figure had vanished from sight.

The bar was crowded. Did I see it wrong?

Could I be hallucinating about someone in a foreign country?

He shouldn't know I'm in Korea.

I shook my head, mocking myself inwardly, then sat down in a corner with Fiona.

Paige was already deep in conversation with the guy who had approached her.

She never misses a chance for a romantic encounter, no matter where she is.

"Look at her, she's practically begging to be swept off her feet tonight."

Fiona shook her head, exasperated.

"That's her style. Ever since she broke up with Philip, she's stopped believing in love."

I couldn't help but laugh, holding my drink.

"Come on, let's have a drink."

Fiona picked up her glass without hesitation, clinking it with mine and smiling.

"Two beautiful ladies drinking alone? Let me join you. If I'm not mistaken, you must be from my hometown."

A trendy guy with silver hair suddenly sat next to us.

He spoke fluent English, making us feel a bit more at home in a place filled with Korean.

"You're right. Judging by your style, are you studying here?"

I looked up and saw a tall, handsome man with familiar features.

"You've got it right. What brings you two here? Are you also exchange students like me?"

The man sat down and asked us with a friendly smile.

Fiona and I graduated years ago. Being mistaken for students was a roundabout way of saying we look young.

Even Fiona couldn't help but laugh:

"You're quite the charmer. We're here for a vacation. Where are you from?"

"You both look so young like we're the same age. I'm from Hoswich city. My name is Orson Bryant. Nice to meet you."

He extended his hand to us with a friendly introduction.

Hoswich city? Last name Bryant?

I felt a shiver run down my spine.

No way.

Could he be related to Elliott? The moment I saw him, I felt a familiar resemblance. Now with his introduction, I realized he looks a bit like Elliott.

However, his fair skin and trendy style made him look different from Elliott, so I thought I was mistaken.

I glanced at Fiona, who was equally surprised.

Fiona shook his hand and casually asked:

"Oh? What a coincidence! We're also from Hoswich City. There aren't many Bryants there. Are you related to Mr. Bryant from Bryant Group?"

Orson smiled, a charming, youthful grin:

"Elliott is my cousin. Our dads are cousins."

We guessed right. This handsome guy was indeed related to Elliott.

Memories flooded my mind.

I remembered Elliott and his dad were only children, but Elliott's grandfather had several brothers, some in politics, some in business, all notable in Hoswich city.

So, this handsome guy must be the son of one of Elliott's uncles.

Unfortunately, despite my time with the Bryant family, I didn't interact much with their relatives, so I couldn't recall who they were.

"So, you're from the Bryant family. No wonder."

Fiona said meaningfully, then smiled at me.

I caught her drift. She thought it was quite a coincidence to meet Elliott's relative in Korea.

Who would have thought?

"What are your names? How long are you staying in Korea? I have free time and can show you around."

Unlike Elliott's aloofness, Orson was lively and eager to help.

"Sure, if you're free, why not?"

Fiona winked, clearly interested in Orson. In no time, they were sharing drinks.

Korean beer was tasty but filled you up.Seeing them engrossed in conversation, I excused myself to the restroom.

The bar's dim lighting made it hard to navigate through the crowd. Just as I neared the restroom, a drunk man blocked my way.

He looked sleazy, with a thick metal necklace and reeking of alcohol.

He grabbed my waist and started speaking in Korean.

My Korean was limited, but I could tell he was calling me "sister" and saying something vulgar.

"Let go of me! Or I'll call the police!"

I didn't expect this in a foreign country, and the language barrier made it worse. I struggled to speak Korean, unsure if my pronunciation was correct.

He clearly didn't understand me. He grinned lewdly and moved closer.

Then his hand slid towards my chest!

I felt a chill down my spine. As his hand approached, I instinctively bit his chubby arm!

In such moments, I had to save myself!

I bit hard, catching him off guard. He screamed in pain, my teeth leaving a red mark.

He turned vicious, cursing in Korean, and swung his hand towards my face!

"Stop!"

At that moment, a tall figure appeared beside me, grabbing the man's arm!
