I told him what had happened in Norch City and how Elliott got injured because of me.

Finally, I told him that he had amnesia because of a blood clot in his brain.

Elliott remained calm as he listened, as if hearing a story that didn't concern him. He began to wonder where the story was going to end:

"So, according to you, I've forgotten a lot of things, right? Madeline, before I lost my memory, what was our relationship like?"

The conversation had been easy until he asked this question. Suddenly, the atmosphere between the three of us grew heavy.

These days, I've been by his side. Though he was kept in the dark, I knew he was truly happy.

He cherished every moment with me.

But now, Quinn and I have to peel off the layers of truth, like peeling an onion, revealing the reality bit by bit.

This process is somehow cruel for someone with amnesia.

No one knew how he would react or what might happen once he heard the truth.

But since we've come this far, we have to continue.

I took a deep breath and gathered up some courage:

"Elliott, haven't you always wondered why my attitude towards you is so inconsistent? It's because, before your coma, we had already divorced, and it had been a long time. A lot of unpleasant things happened in between."

I told him the truth in one breath.

In an instant, the coffee cup Elliott had just picked up slipped from his hand and crashed to the floor with a sharp sound.

The coffee splashed onto his clothes and pants. The hot liquid burned him into a jolt, and he stood up abruptly.

His face was full of shock and disbelief.

He looked at me, his expression changing rapidly. The veins on his forehead were visible, and his fists were clenched tightly.

It was as if he had heard the most ridiculous joke. After a long pause, he slowly looked at me, his eyes filled with a mix of self-mocking sarcasm:

"So, what you're saying is, all this time, you weren't truly happy. You were just pretending to be a loving couple for me?"

It was like he had suddenly tasted something he really disliked. I knew that Elliott was struggling to absorb the truth.

But the truth is the truth.

"Dr. Jones said that only by doing so can your condition improve and your memory gradually return. We didn't mean to lie to you.

I thought that once your memory came back, you'd understand everything on your own, and we wouldn't need to explain so much.

But Quinn spilled it out this morning, and I know you. It’s impossible to keep it from you any longer."

I felt a pang of guilt, so I tried to soften my tone to prevent evoking any extreme emotions.

Quinn then took over:

"I spoke up because I knew this couldn't go on forever. I've searched a lot of domestic and international materials. Dr. Jones was lying.

In fact, even if Madeline continued to keep your company, your condition might not improve.

The blood clot is in your brain, and medication is unlikely to dissolve it. Surgery is too risky. If you remain amnesiac for life, it's unrealistic for Madeline to keep acting forever."

What?

Dr. Jones was lying to me?

It stunned me. I looked at him in disbelief:

"Quinn, are you serious? Dr. Jones said that if I cooperated and acted, he would recover in about three months. How can this be?"

Quinn nodded and gave me a bitter smile:

"If he didn't say so, how can he keep you staying by Elliott's side? I guess he said three months because he thought it would be enough for you two to rekindle your feelings.

Maybe you'd choose to take him back and keep this from him forever."

Quinn's words sent a shiver through me.

Indeed, at first, I resisted the idea of helping Elliott recover. But as time went, I found my resistance fading. Sometimes, I genuinely enjoyed being with him.

I never considered rekindling old feelings, but if this was all part of Dr. Jones' plan, who knows what might happen in time.

No one can completely control their feelings, and neither can I.

I had loved Elliott for many years. The traits that once drew me to him still hold a lethal attraction for me.

Once I fall for him again, I'm afraid I will be deeply involved, unable to pull myself out. After all, his care for me in recent days has been genuine.

"Amnesia... it's a convenient excuse. It erases the gap that once seemed insurmountable, wipes away unpleasant memories, and brings two people who should never have been together closer.

But not remembering doesn't mean those things never happened. Elliott, I once had doubts that you might be pretending to lost memory, but I choose to believe you.

But as I tell you this today, I hope you don't get too emotional or blame Madeline.

If you must find someone to take the fault, blame yourself for hurting her so deeply before.

As for the details, you can find out for yourself."

Quinn continued speaking slowly.

Elliott's eyes widened in shock. He stood in shock for a long time, then sat back down, ignoring the coffee stains on him.

"I didn't fake amnesia," he said, much calmer than I had feared. "I need some time to process what you've told me."

Seeing Elliott like this, I felt a pang of pity. I patted his shoulder and said softly,

"Hey, Barry knows everything. He's the assistant you trusted most. You can ask him for more."

"OK," he swallowed hard, glancing at me instinctively.

"So, is there anything else you should tell me?"

His eyes showed a whirlwind of emotions, as if he'd been hit really hard.

I couldn't bear to continue. I feared he wouldn't be able to handle it.

However, Quinn’s words made my heart skip a beat:

"And also, my relationship with Madeline is actually..."
