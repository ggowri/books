This must be my illusion, right?

I opened my eyes wide, trying to see more clearly, but perhaps because of the alcohol, my eyes couldn't focus at all. At this moment, Quinn went to the terrace.

He retrieved the cigarette case from his pocket, extracted a slender cigarette, and ignited it with a lighter, facing the night sky and leaving me a desolate figure.

There seems to be an inexplicable loneliness in this back view.

I staggered forward and asked softly behind him, "Quinn, why haven't I ever met your girlfriend for so long?"

He didn't look back but blew out a smoke ring. The light blue smoke curled up in the light, and he said, "I don't have a girlfriend. I've been single for a long time and got used to it."

"Isn't there any woman who attracts your attention?" I murmured. Feeling dizzy from the alcohol, I couldn't help but lean against the door frame.

He turned around and smiled playfully at me, "Yes, but the woman I like already has a crush on another man."

When he said this, there was clearly an inexplicable hint. Shocked, I instinctively felt that he seemed to mean something.

But, on second thought, how could this be possible?

Although Quinn cared about me very much, from beginning to end, I never felt that he had the slightest interest in me but more of a kind of sympathy from a confidant.

"What type of girls do you like?" I asked again.

However, he smiled and said nothing more. He just subconsciously pulled the coat aside and put it on me without any explanation.

"It's cold on the terrace. You drank a lot. The wind will make you drunk. Just wait inside for him to come. I'll finish this cigarette."

A gust of wind blew over at this moment, and it was indeed freezing. I shivered, turned around, and fell on the sofa due to the dizziness.

The potency of red wine proved formidable indeed. Once I fell, my mind momentarily drifted into a haze. Though I yearned to open my eyes, my eyelids weighed heavily. I succumbed to a deep slumber without realizing it.

I didn't know how long it took. Vaguely, I heard someone talking in the box.

Struggling to open my eyes, I glimpsed beneath the light a tall figure seated in my previous spot, exchanging clinks of glasses with Quinn using my wine glass. They engaged in conversation, their voices intermittently reaching my ears.

"Let's cut to the chase, Elliott. What do you think of Madeline?"

"She is my wife. Why do you ask this?"

"Do you really treat her as your wife now?"

"Yes."

"You're aware of the test results. And I know that your family has pushed you hard in the past two years. If you don't want to continue the marriage with her, at least be honest with me. I can't bear for Madeline to be hurt in any way."

"I will never divorce."

"If Madeleine is infertile, how will you explain that to your family?"

"There will be other ways. You don't need to worry about this matter. If you still consider me as a friend, please stay away from her."

...

They were obviously talking about me.

Although my eyelids were very heavy, and I felt listless, the room was so quiet that I could hear everything they said.

I thought that learning that I couldn't have babies, Elliott would definitely change his attitude towards me, perhaps even contemplating ending our relationship.

But to my surprise, I still felt the same firmness in his tone as before.
