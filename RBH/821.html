Despite my reluctance to share a room with Elliott, fate had other plans and bound us together for the night.

"Madeline..."

Elliott, who had been sleeping peacefully, suddenly furrowed his brow, letting out a soft cry.

In a flurry, I grasped his hand:

"Elliott, I'm here. Are you feeling any better?"

Elliott didn't open his eyes, yet, he held my hand tightly. Soon after, his furrowed brow relaxed. Before long, the sound of his steady breathing filled the air.

It seems, the sound he just made was likely sleep talking.

After a sleepless night, I was truly exhausted. I propped my chin up, gazing at Elliott, and before I knew it, I had dozed off on the edge of the bed.

By the time I regained consciousness, it was already the following morning.

The moment I opened my eyes, I found myself mysteriously relocated to a hospital bed. Not only was I tucked under a blanket, but also, draped over me was Elliott's suit jacket.

This little hospital is rather basic, hence the rooms lack air conditioning. Although the climate here is pleasant, it still gets a bit chilly during the wee hours and at dawn.

I saw Elliott, clad only in a thin shirt, perched on the couch across from the sickbed, his hand propping up his chin. I couldn't tell if he was asleep or not.

How did he end up giving me the bed, while he himself settled for the chair?

Could it be that he couldn't bear to see me sleeping hunched over after he woke up in the middle of the night?

I must have been utterly exhausted, for I didn't even stir when he lifted me onto the hospital bed.

"Elliott, why are you sitting over there? Get back in bed, pronto."

Realizing he was still a patient, I quickly got up, approached him, and gave his arm a gentle nudge.

Elliott jolted awake. A satisfied smirk played on his lips as he caught sight of me:

"I'm fine, had a good sleep last night. You awake? Feeling hungry? I've sent Barry out for breakfast. We can eat in a bit."

"Do you even realize you passed out last night? Why wouldn't you just stay in bed? Why did you insist on giving it up for me?"

I couldn't help but voice my complaints. Especially when I saw the faint dark circles around his eyes, my heart ached even more for him.

"I'm fine. Just sorry to have put you through this. Madeline, you still care for me. That's comforting."

Elliott's eyes gazed at me with a gentle warmth. His voice was unusually raspy, carrying a magical penetrating power that echoed within the confines of this small hospital bed.

I was taken aback. Just as I was about to defend myself, Barry had already pushed the door open and walked in.

He was holding a few cups of cola and some burgers in his hands:

"Mr. Bryant, this is about all I could find around here. I noticed this place was pretty popular, so I grabbed some burgers. Give them a try."

"Feeling hungry? Here, let me feed you."

Elliott took the burger and cola from Barry's hand, he picked up a burger and brought it to my lips first.

I hadn't eaten much last night, and after a night of hustle and bustle, I was famished. At this very moment, the rich aroma of the hamburger was particularly enticing, leading me to take an involuntary bite.

I never expected this burger to be so incredibly delicious. Not only was the patty thick and juicy, but the flavor was also out of this world. I couldn't help but exclaim:

"This burger is insanely delicious—"

This is nothing more than a commonplace compliment. Moreover, I'm not even praising anyone.

Yet, it was as if Elliott had suddenly received the highest honor, his face instantly filled with profound joy. And, in a rare moment, he flashed a brilliant white smile.

Despite being in his thirties, his smile was as radiant as a teenager's.

Before I could utter another word, he was already pushing the burger towards my mouth again. His voice was as soft as a whisper:

"Tastes good, huh? Dig in, have some more. If it's not enough, I'll send Barry out to get more—"
