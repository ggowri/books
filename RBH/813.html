Outside the yard, there's a vast expanse of fields. The herb seedlings have just been planted and haven't started to grow yet. It appears as nothing more than a flat expanse of brown earth.

A crescent moon hung high in the sky, casting a melancholic aura over the desolate fields. There were no inhabitants in sight. Yet, from this vantage point, I could faintly make out the twinkling lights at the foot of a mountain a kilometer away, suggesting the presence of a village.

Occasionally finding myself in such rural settings, with the chorus of frogs echoing across the fields, I find it rather pleasant.

Especially here, where the climate is incredibly pleasant. The wind brushing against my face is soothing, adding to my overall sense of contentment.

I was standing outside, taking in the fresh air, when suddenly, the roar of an engine filled my ears. Moments later, I spotted a large black SUV barreling towards me from a distance.

That's Quinn's ride.

Why on earth is he here too?

A surge of joy hit me in an instant, and without a second thought, I bolted in that direction.

The car came to a swift halt before me. In no time, Quinn hopped out from the driver's seat. He was clad in a dark denim jacket paired with grey trousers, exuding a casual vibe. A black earring adorned his right ear, his sleeves slightly rolled up. At some point, a tattoo had mysteriously appeared on his right arm.

"Quinn, what brings you here? You didn't come all this way just for me, did you?"

With a grin, I stepped forward and landed a punch on his arm.

He instantly seized my hand, chuckling as he draped it over his shoulder. Then, he swiftly looped his arm around my neck from behind, twirling me around in a full circle before finally setting me down.

He laughed heartily, saying:

"I heard you've arrived in Norch City. I was a bit concerned. As it happens, I have some business to attend to here myself, so I thought I'd come straight over. Conveniently, I can also check on how your new venture in Norch City is progressing."

"I see. And here I thought you came specifically to see me. Turns out you just dropped by because I was on your way."

Stumbling upon a friend in unfamiliar surroundings always brings an unexpected joy.

Being with Quinn never put any pressure on me. So, seeing him always brings me joy.

We exchanged a glance and shared a laugh. He then playfully pinched my nose.

"You're still teasing me. Alright, I admit it, I came all this way just to see you. And guess what? I brought you a bunch of delicious treats."

After Quinn finished speaking, he immediately swung the car door open, pulling out a large bag filled with all sorts of snacks and fruits. And the best part? They were all my favorites.

He's truly considerate. He silently keeps in mind all the food I love.

Upon laying my eyes on this massive bag of stuff, a wave of emotion washed over me in an instant:

"I'm heading back the day after tomorrow. Why are you packing so much?"

"Here, eat up. When are you heading back the day after tomorrow? I'll book the flights now. I'll go back with you."

Quinn chuckled, playfully nudging my nose before asking me.

We casually teased and joked with each other, a routine that had long become commonplace for us both.

However, as we huddled together, ready to check out the flight information, a chilling voice suddenly echoed from behind me:

"I've already booked Madeline's ticket. She's coming back to Hoswich City with me, so you don't have to worry about it."

This sudden voice jolted me back to reality in an instant.

It was only then that I realized Elliott was here too. In that moment, I hastily moved away from Quinn. Then, I turned back around, a bit awkwardly.

Elliott stood there, his expression unreadable. His eyes, cold and piercing, watched us, as if brewing a storm of complex emotions within...
