I couldn't help but recall the unexplained bouts of nausea I'd been having recently and how my tastes had grown increasingly bland. Coupled with the wild nights I'd spent with Elliott... The more I thought about it, the more panicked I felt inside.

"Could the universe really be playing such a massive joke on me at this crucial moment? What if I really was pregnant?

How would I face Elliott?" I couldn't help but think.

My mind was a mess, and I found myself unconsciously caressing my still-flat belly, torn between hoping I really was pregnant and fearing it was just a false alarm.

Seeing my turmoil, Quinn patted my shoulder and said softly, "Don't worry, what's meant to be will happen. Even if you are pregnant, we'll find a solution."

"I understand," I replied softly, not knowing what else to say.

"If you're pregnant, isn't that a good thing? Elliott has been hoping for a son. The Bryant family would be thrilled if you were," Fiona said.

Fiona didn't know about the recent developments. She thought Elliott and I were still blissfully together.

Indeed, Laura would have jumped for joy if she had heard I was pregnant before.

But now, everything has changed... If I really were carrying a child, there was no way I'd let the Bryant family have them!

With that thought, a wave of hatred surged within me. I clenched my fists without realizing it.

"The results should be ready. I'll check on my computer."

As time ticked by, Fiona calculated when the results would be ready and muttered to herself. Then she turned on her computer.

I held my breath, my eyes fixed on her without blinking, my heart jumping to my throat.

At first, Fiona looked relaxed, but a few seconds later, her expression turned serious.

Then, she looked up at me intently as if she had seen a miracle, and gradually, a smile spread across her face.

"How is it? What's the result?"

Quinn was clearly anxious. He couldn't help but ask.

Fiona nodded vigorously, her face lit up with joy. She immediately stood up and walked over to me, grabbing my hands. I could feel her hands trembling, and even her lips quivered as she spoke.

"Madeline, it's unbelievable! You're really pregnant! The blood test shows your HCG levels are over 4000! Congratulations! You're going to be a mom!"

Fiona looked at me, her eyes brimming with excited tears.

As my best friend, she knew all too well the struggles I had faced trying to conceive over the years and how much I longed for a baby.

My mind felt like it was struck by lightning. My mind went blank. I couldn't even say a word.

I blankly stared as Fiona wept with joy for me, yet my heart remained eerily calm, unsure of what to think.

I was pregnant.

I was going to be a mom.

I was having Elliott's baby.

I was plunged into a profound daze. For a moment, I couldn't decide whether to feel happy or sad, as if I had suddenly lost all sense.

"Fiona, are you sure there's no mistake?"

Quinn was just as shocked, his mouth agape. He hurried over to check with Fiona again.
