Hah, I used to think that as long as I showed respect and filial piety to her in this lifetime, maybe our relationship would improve.

But at this moment, I realized that Laura had always looked down on me from the bottom of her heart. Her previous kindness to me was just hope for my ability to bear children and continue the family line.

Holding my fiery face, I looked at Laura, then at Elliott, and couldn't help but bite my lip and let out a cold laugh.

"Mom, what are you doing?"

Elliott seemed to just realize that I was also in the hospital. After a brief shock, he rushed forward, shielding me behind him, and shouted at Laura.

Too late... At the moment everything happened, if he really cared about me, he should have rushed over, shouldn't he?

I forcefully shook off his hand, unable to bear such a huge humiliation anymore, turned around, and quickly ran outside...

How ironic everything was.

Not long ago, Elliott and I were a loving couple.

But in an instant, it seemed like a galaxy separated us, turning us into completely different people in two different worlds.

I rushed out of the gates of Bryant Hospital.

At this moment, a thunderstorm started outside, with lightning and thunder.

I didn't want to stay at this place for another moment, so without hesitation, I rushed into the rain.

I ran aimlessly in the rain, merciless raindrops hitting my face and body. As I ran, suddenly a big hand forcefully grabbed me.

Immediately, a suit covered my head. Before I could react, I was lifted waist-high by the other person.

In the downpour, I saw that it was Elliott holding me.

I instinctively started struggling. However, in the end, he held and pulled me, stuffing me into the car I had driven.

"Boom!"

With lightning, a loud thunderclap rang out, the sound immense.

I was soaked, shivering uncontrollably. Elliott was also thoroughly drenched.

"I know you're upset, but no matter how upset, don't play with your health."

He grabbed a blanket from the car, quickly drying my hair and face, then wrapped me in the blanket.

I looked at him, teeth chattering, the car's heating barely helping.

I knew it was more heart-chilling than body-chilling. I bit my lip, trembling as I asked him, "So, can you tell me the truth now?"

He naturally knew what I meant, looking at me with a hint of darkness in his eyes.

"I've been telling the truth. As for that photo and the origin of the child, I'll investigate. Give me some time."

"You're still lying to me, aren't you?"

I couldn't help but growl softly, my body trembling more.

Seeing me shivering, thinking I was cold, he immediately wrapped me in his arms. His tone was still firm.

"I'm not lying to you. Give me time to investigate thoroughly, Madeline."

Was that so?

Was he really not lying to me? Or was he liking his mother, saying one thing but meaning another?

I couldn't tell anymore, feeling like if this continued, my heart would be torn apart. I didn't know what to trust anymore.

I pushed him away forcefully. I didn't want to continue being immersed in the warmth he offered in such a confused state.

Enough, really enough... Why should I be led by him and Giselle like this?

I still had many more important things to do.

I deliberately distanced myself from Elliott. I looked at him, my gaze cold.

"Fine, I'll give you time to investigate. But until everything between you and Giselle is clear, don't touch me again, and don't look for me, okay?"

The sinking of the past few days had gradually lost me in his desires.

The rationality and self that I had painstakingly built before were almost destroyed by his madness.
