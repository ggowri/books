Toby drove me back to Glory Group.

He had been a quiet man. Breaking the silence, I asked casually, "Toby, I heard Mr. Bryant bought you a house?"

Toby's response was humble. "Yes. I initially declined due to the high price, but Mr. Bryant insisted on covering the cost upfront. I'm grateful."

His simplicity stood in stark contrast to Giselle's greed.

"There's no need for thanks. It was Mr. Bryant's idea. By the way, how's your sister lately?"

I probed, hoping for some insights.

Toby shrugged. "She recently broke up with a classmate and has been helping out at the Bryant Villa, looking after Mr. Cormac Bryant. Why do you ask?"

He looked at me with confusion.

His lack of connection with Giselle intrigued me.

Yet, as we boarded the bus with Elliott, Toby's gaze remained peculiar.

"Just making conversation," I replied.

Seeing that there were no more clues from his side, I dropped the topic.

Back at Glory Group, I mulled over our next moves.

Jade and my uncle, Bruno Sullivan, were currently in police custody, adamantly refusing to plead guilty despite the mounting evidence against them. This posed a significant challenge for Glory Group, especially considering the substantial funds they illicitly siphoned abroad.

Bringing them to justice was proving to be a complex task, and even with legal aid from Alexis, my prospects looked bleak. The best outcome I could hope for based on current evidence was a sentence of 1-2 years in prison for Jade and Bruno, with the possibility of probation.

Katrina Mason did some digging and found out the damage caused by Jade and Bruno's antics could be over 500 million dollars.

That was a gut punch for Glory Group, and it was a bitter pill to swallow to me knowing those two might just get away scot-free.

But at least I had managed to kick those troublemakers out of the Glory Group. Now, the most important thing was to patch up the mess they left behind.

In a strategic meeting with Adam, Isaac Norris, and Katrina, I outlined our plan moving forward.

Aside from our core machinery business, we would support investments in healthcare and entertainment while phasing out real estate and cutting automotive and finance ventures.

Adam would spearhead business development in the film and television sector, while Isaac would oversee real estate, finance, and automotive divisions.

As I concluded the meeting, I couldn't help but feel the weight of responsibility on my shoulders. Every decision carried immense implications, underscoring the complexities of leadership.

While I longed for my father's recovery and guidance, I understood that, for now, I must navigate these challenges alone.

Despite the uncertainties, I remained resolute in my determination to lead Glory Group forward, one step at a time.
