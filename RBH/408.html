The white Porsche Cayenne belonged to Elliott.

As soon as the car stopped, I checked the license plate through the surveillance feed and saw Elliott getting out.

The yard was dimly lit, and from the surveillance feed, I couldn't see where the figure had gone, but Elliott was heading straight towards my front door.

He was completely unaware, while the intruder, armed with a weapon, might ambush him at any moment...

The thought sent shivers down my spine, but a surge of courage I didn't know I had made me rush to the door, opening it without a second thought.

"Elliott, watch out!"

As the door swung open, I cried out in panic toward Elliott.

It was too late for words, but not for action. I spotted the intruder lurking not far behind Elliott with a weapon in hand. The intruder held the weapon up and approached Elliott slowly but my sudden shout startled him.

Realizing he was caught, the intruder swiftly darted out of my yard, disappearing into the night.

Elliott was visibly startled by my shout.

Before he could comprehend what happened, the intruder had vanished.

At this moment, my body was drenched in sweat despite the winter chill, and my mind was blank.

Afraid that the intruder might return or that he had other accomplices lurking around, I quickly pulled Elliott inside and slammed the door shut before gasping for breath.

Seeing my distressed state, Elliott's eyes widened with concern.

He approached, placing a comforting hand on my shoulder, his deep brown eyes reflecting worry and confusion.

"Are you okay? What just happened?"

I struggled to steady my breathing as I clutched my chest.

Seeing me soaked in sweat, Elliott quickly poured me a glass of warm water and guided me to the sofa.

After sipping the warm water, I gradually regained composure.

The living room window had a hole from the break-in, letting in the cold wind. Elliott felt the cold wind and glanced outside.

As he saw the obvious damage, realization dawned on him. He stood up from the sofa and raised his voice in alarm.

"Did that person do this? Who was it? What did they want?"

After finishing the water, my shaken emotions began to calm down. Trembling, I shook my head.

"I don't know. He kept ringing the doorbell, then started smashing the window when I didn't get the door. If you hadn't come in time, I might have... Right, why did you come over in the middle of the night?"

As I spoke, I realized something and asked Elliott about his unexpected arrival.

Elliott squatted down again, noticing my pallor. His eyes filled with tenderness as he gently cupped my face, his thumb tracing over my lips.

"It's quite a coincidence, really. Last night, I dreamt of you falling into a deep well. You were unable to climb out and screaming for help. It jolted me awake, and I couldn't shake off the worry, so I decided to come and check on you. I never imagined you were actually in danger..."

What? Was it really that much of a coincidence?

Elliott's revelation made me feel bewildered. I glanced at the ominous black hole, my heart dropping once again.

"The surveillance camera didn't capture his face, but my gut tells me he was a man. I don't know what he wanted, but it's clear he was after me."
