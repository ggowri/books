I clicked on that gossip news.

Comments are flooding in like a tidal wave, every second bringing a fresh batch. I can almost hear the sound of countless women's hearts shattering.

I glanced at those photos, presumably snapped by some tabloid paparazzi.

After all, Elliott was once so arrogant. How could he possibly be seen carrying a woman in a crowded mall?

This was, of course, front-page news for these journalists.

However, this meant that my face was clearly exposed. Even though the person filming was far away, my features were still distinctly visible.

Just like that, I couldn't keep a low profile anymore.

Reading this news gave me a headache. Without a second thought, I shot Paige a message:

"Paige, quick, get someone to scrub this news off the net! Now!"

Paige, however, sent a series of exaggerated emojis in response:

"First off, spill the beans. How on earth did you and Elliott patch things up? Weren't we supposed to ride the single wave together?"

With a sigh of frustration, I quickly typed back:

"I'll explain it to you in person when I get a chance. Now, quickly, find someone to take down this news."

A few seconds later, Paige shot back a reply:

Someone beat me to the punch; the news piece has already been taken down.

I hastily clicked on the link, only to find it had indeed been wiped out.

Seems like the PR team at Bryant Group did their magic, swiftly handling the news. However, a significant number of people have already caught wind of it.

I was wallowing in frustration when a message from Quinn popped up. There were no words, just a solitary, grey broken heart emoji.

This simple expression is giving me a hard time.

It seemed he must have seen the news too. For a moment, I was at a loss for words, resorting to sending a string of ellipses his way.

Quinn never responded to me again.

Not long after, I noticed he'd updated his social feed. It was a post featuring a pitch-black image, accompanied by a melancholic caption:

It's a movie for three, yet I'm stuck playing the nameless extra.

Seeing this post on my social feed, I felt an even deeper pang of sadness.

I stared at our chat window for a long time, trying to find some words to comfort him. Yet, in the end, I didn't send anything.

I confess, I'm filled with guilt.

The moment Elliott hoisted me up and we began to navigate through the mall, my happiness was anything but feigned.

Quinn must have noticed it too.

Sometimes, people walk guided by their heads, other times, they follow their hearts. When led by the head, there's a semblance of control. But when the heart takes the lead, it's an uncontrollable force, a surrender to the irresistible.

This weekend.

I was decked out in the white tracksuit Elliott had bought for me, heading to the golf course Grey had recommended.

The weather was splendid, the entire golf course was a lush expanse of green, offering a broad view that was absolutely refreshing and uplifting.

As soon as I arrived on the scene, before Grey could even introduce me, someone immediately recognized me. He pointed at me and said:

"Grey, is this lady the same one who was in the papers with Mr. Bryant yesterday? She looks familiar--"

Suddenly, all eyes were on me, sparking an immediate flurry of conversation.

"It does seem likely, Grey. You don't suppose the new president of Bryant Group is her, do you? As far as I recall, didn't she divorce Mr. Bryant? Are they planning on getting back together?"

"Mr. Bryant is quite aloof, isn't he? He rarely joins our gatherings. And to think he was spotted carrying a woman in the mall! He must be head over heels for her."

"Hi there, I'm Sofia Dewin from Power Group."

"Hey there, I'm Milter Lelitin from Spark Media."
