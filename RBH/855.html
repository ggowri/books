"Mr. Bryant, I've been searching high and low for you, and here you are, basking in the glow of romance."

Just as I was about to speak, a raspy female voice echoed through.

Realizing someone was approaching, I quickly extricated myself from Elliott's embrace. I turned to see Lisa Fox standing before us, clad in a sheer black mesh top and a form-fitting black skirt.

Her mesh top was sheer, revealing a bold red bra underneath. The striking contrast of red and black, coupled with her provocative attire, delivered a powerful visual punch.

Her makeup was quite bold today, with her lashes dark and long, lips a fiery red. She wore a black veil atop her head, obscuring half her face, lending her an air of mystery.

"Lisa, you're here too."

Elliott cast her a glance, responding with a nonchalant tone.

"I heard Mr. Bryant played the knight in shining armor, which apparently ruffled Diego's feathers. So, I came to see what the fuss was all about. But seeing Ms. Mayers here, it all makes sense now."

Lisa flashed a seductive smile. Her face, which once bore a resemblance to mine, was now heavily made up, significantly reducing our likeness.

This sudden realization hit me - her resemblance to me could be altered through the art of makeup.

"It was him who disrespected Madeline first, and we've just resolved the conflict. What's the matter? Is Mr. Rodríguez still in a bad mood?"

Elliott said, his voice icy.

"No, no. Diego just wanted me to lighten the mood a bit. Mr. Bryant, would you do us the honor of joining us for a few drinks in the room?"

Lisa asked with a laugh.

Without thinking, Elliott turned to me, asking:

"Do you want to go?"

I shook my head, stating bluntly, "I don't want to go, I don't want to interact with people like that."

"Alright, then I won't go either. If you're feeling tired, I can take you home right now."

To my surprise, Elliott wasn't going either. He took my hand, ready to lead me away.

The smile on Lisa's face vanished in an instant. She grabbed hold of Elliott's sleeve, saying:

"Mr. Bryant, think it through. Diego has a nasty temper. The fact that he's willing to share a drink with you is already him giving you a chance."

Elliott chuckled dismissively, remarking:

"In Hoswich City, I don't need anyone to give me a chance, except for my wife."

After speaking, Elliott disregarded Lisa and gently planted a kiss on my forehead.

His actions were clearly telling Lisa, he had no interest in any woman but her.

"Ms. Mayers sure has some luck, not only making a bunch of men fall head over heels for her, but also managing to secure their protection. I could really learn a thing or two from her skills and abilities."

Lisa shot me a glance filled with envy, but in a blink, she retracted it.

She knew the score between Elliott and me, so her words were dripping with sarcasm.

I chuckled, saying:

"Ms. Fox, when it comes to tactics and skills, you're truly the best."

I headed straight out into the open.

Elliott was held back by Lisa, who seemed to whisper a few more words to him.

I bid farewell to Dalton, leaving the private vineyard behind. Just as I reached the main gate, my mom suddenly rang me up.

I glanced at the time subconsciously, it was a little past two in the afternoon. Under normal circumstances, my mom rarely calls me during the day unless it's something urgent.

I hastily picked up the call:

"Mom, what's up?"

"Madeline, get back here quick! Laura Bryant has shown up at our place. She's knocking on the door right now!"
