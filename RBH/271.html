His reminder jogged my memory, and I recalled passing by a distinctive clothing store just now, where a variety of clothes caught my eye.

After hanging up the phone, I immediately turned back to that store. As soon as I entered, I spotted a black devil's dress, which was perfect for occasions like Halloween.

So, I bought the dress right away and also picked up a devil mask. Then, I headed straight to Quinn's club in Louis City.

Quinn's club was still in its soft opening phase, but even so, it was already bustling with activity. The interior décor was even more lavish than that of the Hoswich club.

Just as I parked the car, Quinn dashed out, dressed like a millennium-old vampire, with his face painted a ghastly white. Honestly, he startled me at first glance.

"Why did you dress up like this?" I thumped his chest in surprise.

"It's Halloween. I gotta be a little creative. How's my vampire look?" He winked, holding a juice bag made to look like blood.

Surprisingly, his charmingly wicked appearance fit the bill quite well.

"Looks good," I chuckled, playing along.

"Why are you still wearing your own clothes? Did you bring your costume? Let's get you changed." Quinn hurriedly urged me.

"I did. Find me a fitting room, and I'll change," I replied promptly.

So, Quinn dragged me to the upstairs fitting room. Once inside, I quickly changed into the mini black dress, adorned my head with two small red horns, and put on the devil mask before stepping out.

The black dress was one with straps and a puffy bottom that ended just above my knees. Its unique design accentuated my charming legs.

Quinn's eyes lit up at the sight of me, and then he grinned mischievously.

"Wow, you're quite wicked. Playing the little devil, huh?"

"Gotta embrace the darkness for Halloween, right? Let's go." I winked back, smiling.

Quinn offered his arm, indicating for me to link mine with his. Without hesitation, I did so, and together we set off for the costume party.

I thought this party would be just a small gathering, but as soon as the doors of the venue opened, the lively and eerie scene inside stunned me.

The entire venue was decked out in Halloween decorations, with pumpkin lanterns, spider webs, and skulls adorning the walls and ceilings. The men and women inside were already in a frenzy, dressed as ghosts, angels, or zombies, roaming the venue freely. The assortment of drinks and eerie music created a strangely frenetic atmosphere, engulfing everyone in a wild mood.

The last time I celebrated Halloween was back in college. Now, experiencing this atmosphere again after so many years, I suddenly felt a lot more relaxed.
