Giselle clearly had no idea who Quinn was. Otherwise, she would never have dared to confront him.

Quinn's face turned ashen in an instant. As a prominent figure in Hoswich, he had never been publicly humiliated like this before.

Without hesitation, Quinn snatched Giselle's phone from her hand and flung it out the door as if it were trash.

"What are you doing? That's my phone!"

Giselle's face went pale as she watched her beloved phone being tossed away. She instinctively moved to rush towards it, but Quinn grabbed her by the hair.

"You should consider yourself lucky that I don't hit women. Otherwise, you'd be in a much worse state tonight."

Quinn's words were delivered with a fierceness I had never seen before.

His usually gentle eyes now exuded coldness, transforming him into a ferocious beast.

Quinn's unprecedented fury took me aback. His expression startled me to the core.

Giselle's friends stood in shock, attempting to intervene, but Quinn's overpowering presence kept them at bay.

Giselle had never been reprimanded like this before. Her face was a mix of despair as she looked at Quinn, and tears welled up in her eyes.

"Let her go, stop!"

Just then, a chilling yet husky voice echoed from the entrance of the bar.

The moment I heard that voice, it was as if I was frozen in place by some spell.

It was Elliott.

I never expected him to show up here at this late hour.

Elliott picked up the shattered remains of Giselle's phone from the ground and walked towards us.

His gloomy and handsome face appeared even more menacing under the bar's lighting.

Approaching Quinn, Elliott wordlessly grabbed his arm, signaling him to release Giselle.

I thought, "It's evident he is trying to protect her.

Did Giselle call him here?

Giselle has only just messaged him, so how does he arrive so quickly? Is he nearby all along?

Did he bring Giselle to the gathering and wait for her outside?"

Various speculations flooded my mind, and my spirits plummeted into an abyss again.

"Elliott!"

Upon seeing him, Giselle rushed into his arms as if he were her savior.

In front of me, she shamelessly clung to Elliott, wrapping her arms tightly around his waist.

The sight of their intimacy felt like a dagger through my heart.

I stood there, trembling uncontrollably, barely able to keep my balance. Thankfully, Quinn suddenly reached out from behind and steadied me with his arm around my waist.

Giselle pointed an accusing finger at Quinn, her hand trembling as she spoke loudly. "Elliott, I was so scared just now. This guy was here with Madeline in the middle of the night, drinking. I just mentioned taking a photo to send to you, and he smashed my phone, threatening to hit me. Thank goodness you showed up in time."

Pretending to shiver, Giselle sought refuge in Elliott's embrace.

The attention of Giselle's group of friends was now entirely fixed on Elliott.

After all, figures like Elliott, who was prominent in the business world, were rarely seen in their circles.

"Step aside."

Elliott's tone was indifferent, devoid of any discernible emotion. However, his gaze slowly descended upon me.
