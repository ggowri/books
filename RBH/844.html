I practically lunged forward, positioning myself directly in front of Quinn.

Elliott's fist was coming straight for my face. However, when he realized I was standing in front of Quinn, his eyes widened in surprise and he quickly pulled back.

Even so, he still managed to land a hit on my face. The pain was so intense that I couldn't help but crouch down, clutching my cheek.

"Madeline! Are you alright?"

The two men nearly cried out in surprise at the same time.

No sooner had I crouched down than they both followed suit, their eyes on me, panic etched on their faces.

"Enough, Elliott, can you quit the drama? I just had dinner with Quinn, that's all. If I've committed a crime, call the cops, not make me watch you guys brawl, okay?"

Elliott's punch left my cheek throbbing with pain. I was seething with anger, unable to hold back a tirade against Elliott.

Elliott was rooted to the spot, dumbfounded.

He looked at me, a self-mocking smile playing on his lips. "Fooling around, huh? So, I shouldn't care, shouldn't ask?"

"We're just grabbing a bite to eat," I explained, looking up at him with a sense of resignation.

"Why did you keep it from me? And come on, there's no way it's just two people in that huge room, right?"

But Elliott is sharp, I can't fool him at all.

"I..."

I was suddenly at a loss for words.

"It's just an evening soiree I'm hosting, and I've asked Madeline to accompany me," Quinn explained on my behalf.

Elliott sneered, "Really? Quinn, you're throwing a party and you want Madeline to play hostess? Are there no other women in the world? Is that why you can't take your eyes off her?"

For a moment, Quinn was at a loss for words.

Elliott shot me a cold glance, then turned his gaze to Quinn. "You two owe me an explanation," he said. "Madeline, what exactly is going on between you and Quinn?"

"I..."

I hastily lifted my gaze, meeting his questioning eyes. In that moment, I felt an overwhelming urge to spill the beans, to stop this charade. However, considering Elliott's condition, I chose once again to remain silent.

"Lost for words?"

Elliott's hand rested on my shoulder, his repressed emotions threatening to break free. His eyes were a startling shade of red.

"Madeline! The child your mother was just holding, whose is it? Is it the one you gave birth to?"

Just then, Laura's piercing voice echoed from outside.

Next thing I knew, Laura burst through the door in a flurry of panic, storming towards me with a fierce determination.

I shot a panicked glance at Quinn, who seemed equally taken aback.

"Kid? What kid? Madeline, since when did you have a kid?" Elliott asked, baffled.

Seeing Laura charging towards me, I quickly yanked her aside, leaning in to whisper a threat into her ear, "If you don't want Elliott's brain clot to burst, putting his life in danger, then shut your mouth! He's amnesic now, don't mention anything about a child in front of him!"
