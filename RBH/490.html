Elliott and I had been married for over three years, and I had never been allowed to spend Christmas at Buttonwood Mansion.

This year, Elliott offered to take me to Buttonwood Mansion for Christmas.

But now, the person he brought had become Giselle.

She sat beside Cormac, holding his hand. She wore a red sweater, had a sweet smile, and seemed to have become the favorite of the family.

People always say that mothers gain more love from others because of their pregnancy.

I caressed my belly subconsciously, feeling a surge of bitterness in my heart. Quinn noticed something was wrong with me. He leaned over, took a look at my phone, and understood instantly.

He patted my shoulder and comforted me softly, "Don't think too much, just look forward. Christmas means to bring about a new beginning."

"Okay." I nodded in agreement.

Then, I uploaded the video of the fireworks I had just taken to Instagram.

"Yes, Christmas means to bring new beginnings. Let everything related to the Bryant family stay in the past," I whispered to myself.

I felt down for a while, but I quickly adjusted my mood and started playing with them again.

That night, Lily was delighted. She didn't want to sleep and was busy in the kitchen alone. After a while, she took out a small and exquisite stove from the kitchen and some fruits she had prepared.

"This small stove was bought by your father and me at the flea market a few days ago. It's just right for making mulled wine," she said.

Lily covered the small table next to us with a tablecloth and placed the small stove on it. Then, she put the fruit into the glass pot with wine and boiled it on the small stove.

The flame of the small stove warmed everyone's heart even more.

We drank the mulled wine and played cards until almost dawn. Then, we went to bed with some regrets as we had not played enough.

It was late. In addition to my family's habits, Quinn and Marcus stayed in the guest room of my house that night.

Early the following day, Lily cooked breakfast and woke us all up.

For Lily, she treated Marcus and Quinn as her children. Not only did she thoughtfully prepare toothbrushes and toothpaste for them, but she also gave each of them a Christmas present.

"Lily, you are the best! The last time I received a Christmas present was when I was 18 years old, and no one treated me as a child anymore!"

Quinn did not expect Lily to prepare a gift for him, so he excitedly gave her a big hug.

"You are luckier than me. I rarely received Christmas present when I was a child because my family lived in different places and rarely got together to celebrate Christmas. Lily, you are so kind! Merry Christmas, and thank you!"

Marcus also expressed his heartfelt gratitude.

"You are all Madeline's friends, so I treat you as my own children. You can always tell we whatever you want to eat. I has no other advantages, except that I like to cook!"

Lily looked at the two young men and loved them from the bottom of her heart.

"Then, I won't refuse. My mom rarely cooks, so if I want to eat home-cooked food in the future, I'll come over to eat!" Quinn laughed and said immediately.

"I live nearby. They say that living close can bring more benefits. Count me in for a good food from now on," Marcus also said.

It was Christmas, after all. Everyone's face was filled with joyful smiles involuntarily. Their smiles also infected me, so I joked, "You can't eat for free. I need to charge you."

Quinn smiled brightly, pointed at my belly, and said, "Don't worry, we won't eat and drink for free. After the baby in your belly is born, I will buy his milk powder and diapers."

"Then, I will be responsible for his toys and clothes," Marcus immediately said.

"Marcus, why do I find that every time I say something, you have to compete with me? Do you want to steal my godfather's position from me?"
