I thought I could say all of this because I no longer had feelings for him.

But for some reason, at the moment of speaking the last sentence, my heart suddenly felt like it was being pierced by a steel nail, tearing me apart with pain that spread throughout my body.

Yet, even so, I remained expressionless.

I didn't want Elliott to see any traces of my affection for him on my face again.

Elliott started coughing violently again. It seemed that he was truly infuriated by me because he coughed up a mouthful of blood directly.

The deep red splotches on the white tissue looked too shocking. I was taken aback, and at the same time, Elliott stood up once more.

"From now on, we're even, completely cut off... I never expected that after I developed genuine feelings for you, the blow you'd give me would be so devastating! Madeline, I've learned my lesson!"

Every word Elliott spoke was slow, and then he staggered away, clutching his chest.

Marcus stood at the staircase the entire time. Seeing Elliott walking unsteadily, he hurried over, trying to support him.

"Mr. Bryant, you're still weak, perhaps you should lie down and rest for a while."

"No need, I appreciate your help this time, I won't cause you anymore trouble!"

Elliott waved his hand, signaling to Marcus that he didn't need assistance. After thanking Marcus, he walked out unsteadily through the door.

I stared blankly at his departing figure, feeling as though the steel nail in my heart had pierced even deeper at that moment.

A sharp pain struck my chest, and instinctively, I held my hands against my heart. I thought I wouldn't feel any pain, but I realized I had severely overestimated myself.

Tears welled up in my eyes.

His departure this time was completely different from before.

I had a hunch that after tonight, our relationship was utterly over.

Could it not hurt?

This was the person I had loved for many years, someone I loved without hesitation.

This person had been with me through two lifetimes. In the previous one, he shattered me, and in this one, he nearly became my nightmare once again.

Was letting go easy?

For someone who truly loved and was deeply invested in another, cutting off that love was as painful as tearing apart the heart.

Only those who had truly loved could understand the taste of letting go, the kind of heart-wrenching, gut-wrenching taste.

The door slammed shut with a loud bang.

Elliott was gone.

It felt like something heavy had dropped in my heart, and I fell to the ground, feeling dizzy.

Out of the kindness that lingered from loving Elliott, I endured the discomfort and quickly sent Barry his location, asking Barry to come pick up Elliott.

I was afraid this fool would once again drag his sick body into the cold wind, harming his health.

After that, my phone slipped from my hand, and I collapsed weakly on the couch, feeling the world spinning around me, the ceiling becoming a blur.

Shortly after, I gradually lost consciousness. I could hear Marcus desperately calling my name, but no matter how hard I tried, I couldn't open my eyes.
