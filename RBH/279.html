The embrace, kisses, and caresses between a man and a woman spoke volumes beyond mere words.

Lost in our passionate kiss, we were oblivious to our surroundings until Elliott's phone interrupted us, snapping us back to reality.

"Where did you both disappear to? You haven't left already, have you?"

Quinn's cheerful voice sounded through the phone.

"Keep the festivities going. We've got some matters to attend to."

Elliott's response was unabashed over the phone, his eyes gleaming with desire, hinting at an unfulfilled need.

His insinuations instantly brought a blush to my cheeks, and I found myself melting into him.

"Paige and Philip seem to be on the verge of a brawl. Are you sure you don't want to come back?"

Quinn's relaxed voice sounded once more, and we could tell he was in a noisy environment.

Paige had just mentioned being on route to catch them in the act. Given Philip's temperament and Paige's fiery nature, a showdown was inevitable tonight.

Concerned that Paige might act impulsively, I signaled Elliott with a glance to go check on them.

Elliott grasped my meaning in an instant, his large hand squeezing my waist.

"Alright, we're on our way."

Elliott hung up, and with his arm around me, we made our way back to the party venue.

The corridor, dimly lit, seemed unlikely to be frequented. However, as we turned, a sudden flash caught our attention.

Then, a figure swiftly disappeared around the corner.

"Who's there?"

Elliott called out, releasing me and striding quickly toward the spot where the figure vanished.

I hurried to follow. Yet, the hallway remained quiet and deserted, devoid of any presence.

"I thought I saw a flash just now. Could someone be secretly taking pictures of us?"

I asked anxiously, still trembling from the encounter.

Tonight's party had drawn attendees from diverse backgrounds. Our earlier conspicuous presence and Elliott's melodic voice must have piqued someone's curiosity.

"That's not a big deal."

Seeing no sign of anyone else in the corridor, Elliott abandoned the pursuit.

"Are you really not bothered?"

I looked at him incredulously. After all, he had vehemently opposed any exposure of our relationship in the past.

"Of course not, we're now legally married."

With no reservations, Elliott wrapped his arm around me, heading confidently for the entrance to the party venue.

This time, neither of us wore masks.

It seemed as though he was prepared to announce to the world that I was his wife, filling my heart with warmth once more.

When we reentered the venue, it was already chaotic beyond words.

Everyone was gathered around, and even from a distance, I could hear Paige's heart-rending cries amidst the commotion.

Suddenly, hearing my friend in such distress left me breathless.

Rushing into the crowd, I found Paige standing with her hands on her hips, tearfully berating Philip, "Philip, you jerk! How could you do this? Did you deliberately ignore the report I sent you?"
