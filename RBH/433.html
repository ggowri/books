Enough!

Enough of all this.

I was tired of this evil ambiguity, and I couldn't bear to be entangled with Elliott endlessly, leading nowhere.

Knowing him as I do, as long as Giselle's child existed, he wouldn't go too far with her.

He shouldn't bother me for no reason.

My fist hit his face decisively like a cold arrow. Elliott must have felt my resolve as he stared intently into my eyes.

"What exactly do you want me to do? You want me to force her to abort the child? Life is innocent, Madeline! I can't do such a thing!"

His eyes were bloodshot, his voice deep and tinged with profound helplessness.

But from his words, I sensed his budding compassion for Giselle.

Good. This was what Giselle wanted.

Even if she couldn't have his heart, she could use the child to slowly gain his sympathy and love, gradually capturing his heart.

"Then stay away from me! Stop acting like a lovesick puppy around me, Elliott. I am not your tool for relieving your desire!"

I grabbed his collar, unable to hold back a growl.

Elliott stared at me in disbelief, still puzzled by my coldness toward him. Far from letting go, he cupped my face, and his eyes were affectionate.

"Relieve my desire? I can hardly believe such words would come from you! Weren't you happy with me before?"

It was happy before.

For a woman who had never known pleasure, Elliott's recent frenzy was something I couldn't resist.

But any joy involving another woman became worthless in an instant, utterly obliterated.

I was a woman who could not tolerate flaws in love. I could not accept a love triangle.

I tugged at the corner of my lips, a bitter smile forming.

"Do you think that kind of happiness will still exist after you have a child with Giselle? Elliott, if you were in my shoes, would you be happy?"

Elliott's lips moved, and he looked at me. His gaze shifted and he paused as if he harbored an unspeakable secret. I could sense his struggle to speak, yet he hesitated.

Finally, he confessed, "The matter of the child truly has nothing to do with me. It was all orchestrated by my grandparents and my parents. Should I be held responsible for that?"

Cormac, Laura, and Jasper designed this?

I couldn't understand the meaning of this sentence.

They planned a pregnancy for Giselle.

"What do you mean?" I asked coldly.

Elliott bowed his head and took a deep breath.

"Do you remember that time Grandpa forced us to go for a physical examination?" Elliott asked.

The physical examination?

Of course, I remembered. I couldn't possibly forget it.

The humiliation of that day was still vivid in my mind.

"I remember. But what does this have to do with the physical examination?" I asked, puzzled.
