I was thrilled to hear it, but I humbly responded:

"I just got lucky, made a few good investments, that's all."

Elliott flashed a subtle smile, his fingers gently grazing my cheek as he said:

"The value of Glory Group has skyrocketed, and it all happened under your leadership. Numbers don't lie, Madeline, there's no need for modesty. I realize now that I was the one holding you back. I owe you an apology."

He finally realized that I'm not some helpless little woman who revolves her entire world around him every day.

My mood involuntarily improved, saying:

"You once labeled me as a woman who only knew how to love."

"I apologize. I didn't know you well enough then," Elliott said, his laughter echoing in the air as he gently took my hand, examining it as if it were a piece of fine art. "I thought I was marrying a delicate damsel, but instead, I find myself wedded to a formidable businesswoman who commands the winds and waves of the corporate world."

Which woman doesn't enjoy a good compliment?

Especially when the praise comes from someone who once belittled you at every turn, but now genuinely appreciates you.

I chuckled.

"I'm far from being a strong, independent woman. Moreover, I've never aspired to be one."

"I know, I've let you down, haven't I? You would have rather been the perfect wife and mother for me, right?"

Elliott cradled my face, his eyes filled with remorse.

Indeed...

Indeed, that was the old me, with no grand ambitions, solely focused on taking care of the man I loved.

But now, ever since I've tasted the joy of success, I've become unstoppable, never wanting to revert back to my former self.

"That was then. Now, I aspire to be a woman who forsakes love, focusing solely on her career. When I achieve success, what will men amount to? Even the most powerful of them will be nothing more than ordinary in my eyes."

I teased on purpose.

"So, I'm feeling pretty anxious right now. To prevent you from 'abandoning love', I need to step up my game. I don't want you to leave me in the future without even saying goodbye."

Elliott chuckled lightly, affectionately pinching my nose.

He looked at me, his deep-set eyes brimming with a tender mix of passion and desire.

The proximity between us was too close for comfort. Suddenly, he couldn't help himself and swept me up, settling me onto his lap.

At the same time, his wristband wrapped around my waist.

I frantically struggled to get up, but he wouldn't let me.

"Kiss me, and I'll let you up. Otherwise, forget about getting back to work."

Elliott pouted like a child.

No matter how much I struggled, I was no match for him. In the end, I said in resignation:

"Elliott, I've got a ton of work to do."

The usually rational him, surprisingly started to play dirty:

"So what? I've got bigger fish to fry."

……

He pissed me off to the point of speechlessness, and in my anger, I gave his arm a hard pinch.

He grunted softly, yet he held onto my slender waist tightly, refusing to let go.

The standoff lasted for quite a while.

In the end, I had no choice but to compromise, planting a perfunctory kiss on his cheek.

With a hearty laugh, he finally let go of me. Just then, a crisp sound of something falling echoed from the doorway.

I jerked my head up in a panic, realizing the person who had just walked in was Quinn.
