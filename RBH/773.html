At my words, Jack instantly turned his gaze towards Laura. By now, Laura had caught on. However, she still harbored hostility towards me, her lips curling into a sneer:

"Your words sound sweet, but who knows if you truly mean to look after Elliott? Can you assure there's no ulterior motive towards Elliott?"

"This time, Elliott's injury is on me. I'm willing to take care of him, and I assure you, I have no ulterior motives. Of course, all of this is moot if you don't trust me enough. Otherwise, all my words are just empty promises."

I looked at Laura, my voice icy as I spoke.

"Mrs. Laura Bryant, you know, Mr. Bryant's health has significantly improved these past few days, all thanks to Ms. Mayers' constant companionship. I think, if Ms. Mayers is willing to take care of Mr. Bryant, it would be absolutely wonderful."

Barry stepped forward at just the right moment, addressing Laura with due respect.

Laura's eyes darted around quickly. She sized me up and down, still not quite believing me:

"Madeline, are you truly capable of taking good care of Elliott? I have my doubts."

I was at a loss for words.

I've called her 'mom' for years, and yet she dares to question my character. If it weren't for Elliott's current situation, I swear I'd never want to deal with her again in my lifetime.

"I trust Ms. Mayers' character. Plus, with surveillance in place, Mrs. Laura Bryant can rest easy."

Jack spoke a word to me.

Laura finally put her inner doubts to rest. Then, she whispered:

"What about the woman? Should we let her go?"

"Until Mr. Bryant regains consciousness, it's best not to let strangers of unknown identity take care of him. Barry, go tell that woman. Ms. Mayers, please come to my office."

Jack said.

Barry nodded and took off. I sensed that Jack had something to tell me, so I followed him into his office.

Just the two of us were in Jack's office. As soon as the door closed, Jack pushed his glasses up his nose with a serious expression:

"Ms. Mayers, it's just us here. What's your take on Mr. Bryant's recent reaction?"

"I'm not entirely sure, and I don't want to speculate. But I've heard whispers about some unique treatments in Norch City. What do you think, Dr. Jones?"

Jack nodded, his expression serious as he said:

"Yeah, I've heard the tales. Many moons ago, I was dispatched to a small hospital on the outskirts of Norch City. I treated some peculiar patients during my stint there. But that was a long time ago, and I've seldom heard of such cases since. Now, I fear that if Mr. Bryant has indeed been subjected to some sort of foul play, our modern medical prowess might not be sufficient to reverse it."

Jack's worries mirror my own. I had thought about confronting Lisa, but without concrete evidence, it's all just speculation. If she flatly denies it, I wouldn't be able to do anything about it.

"Dr. Jones, do you know any experts in this field, or could you possibly ask for their assistance?"

I ventured to ask.

"I don't know them. I was just a kid when I worked there. After I left, I gradually lost touch with the people from that place."

Jack shook his head in resignation.

"I've got a buddy, comes from a long line of medics, he's pretty chummy with the folks over at Norch City. I'll hit him up, see if he's got any bright ideas."

Suddenly, Marcus came to mind. So, right in front of Jack, I dialed Marcus' number.
