We had lived here for a long time, and the landlady was quite familiar with my families.

Suddenly seeing the man next to me, the landlady smiled and asked:

"Oh my, Madeline, is this your fiancé? Your mom has mentioned him before, saying he is tall, handsome, and very considerate. She’s right about this!"

My mom mentioned my boyfriend to the landlady?

That shouldn’t be. My mom never spoke so highly of Elliott, and we divorced a long time ago.

Could it be Quinn that my mom was talking about?

I felt something bad was coming, but Elliott seemed to enjoy the compliment.

He immediately put his arm around my shoulder in front of the landlady. Normally, he rarely initiated conversations, but this time he politely said to her:

"Thank you for, ma'am. We'll come by often for breakfast."

"Oh, wonderful! We're all neighbors, swing by often—"

The landlady was thrilled by Elliott's politeness, rubbing her hands together and smiling broadly.

Fearing that their conversation might reveal more inconsistencies, I quickly dragged Elliott away after buying breakfast.

"I didn't expect your mom to think so highly of me. Maybe they don't dislike me so much as you said. Are they testing me?"

Elliott was still basking in the landlady's praise as he walked and asked me.

I had no idea how to tell him because the truth was quite hurting. So, I chose to stay silent and kept walking.

The short walk of a few hundred yards felt like an eternity. Finally reaching the entrance of our neighborhood, I hastily asked Elliott to leave:

"Alright, we’re at the gate. You should go to work. I'll also go to work after bringing the breakfast to my parents."

"You won't invite me in? It's been a long time since I saw your parents."

Elliott looked at me, a bit hurt.

"It's still early, and they're resting. Noah had a fever last night, and everyone was up all night. Maybe next time, okay?"

I frowned and refused again.

Elliott seemed about to say something more, but just then, my mom's voice came from behind me:

"Madeline, why are you standing at the gate? Malina said you went out to buy breakfast. Buy an extra one, Quinn said he..."

I quickly turned around and stopped her with eyes when she mentioned "Quinn".

At that moment, my mom saw Elliott. She froze.

Next, I saw her expression change rapidly from friendly to furious...

"Elliott, go to work now!"

Realizing the situation was bad, I hurriedly pushed Elliott, signaling him to leave.

However, Elliott didn't move. Instead, he stared intently behind my mom, his eyes suddenly turning terrifyingly cold.
