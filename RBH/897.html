A rich aroma of herbal grass mingled with the scent of chicken wafted over.

"Oh, did I come at a bad time?"

Upon seeing her silhouette, I instantly knew who she was. I chuckled and teased in jest.

"You've arrived just in time. Jane has just finished making some Gastrodin Chicken Soup. You should have some too."

Marcus responded as naturally as if he and Jane were already lovers.

"She's at your place making soup this late, you two..."

I chuckled with a hint of ambiguity.

Shy Marcus instantly felt awkward, while Jane emerged with ease. Wiping her hands on her apron, she greeted me with a smile:

"Ms. Mayers, I just flew in this afternoon from Edulet City. I brought some fresh gastrodin to make a healing soup for Marcus. You've arrived just in time. Please, have a seat. I'll bring the soup over!"

"Alright, awesome! I'll just go with the flow and follow the master's plan then."

I nodded, saying with a smile.

Marcus hurried into the kitchen, assisting Jane with ladling the soup. I watched them, their synergy and affection for each other was unmistakable.

I didn't pry any further. After all, they both were incredibly discreet. If I were too blunt, it would just make them uncomfortable.

I plopped down and cut straight to the chase with Marcus:

"Marcus, I came to you today because I need a favor. Money is no object."

"Spill it. What's going on?"

Seeing me so serious and coming to him so late, Marcus knew something must be up. So, he immediately asked me.

"That Lisa, she's definitely into witchcraft. I need to protect myself, and I want you to find me an expert from Marcus's side. Find a way I can defend myself."

"Oh," Marcus paused, glancing at Jane, "One of Jane's pals hails from Norch City, home to a renowned specialist in this field. Jane, lend Madeline a hand, will you?"

"Ms. Mayers, ever since Marcus mentioned it to me last time, I've been brainstorming solutions for you. This time, I specifically asked my friend to have a doctor concoct a potion. If it's just a common spell, a spritz of this potion into the caster's eyes should break it instantly. I've brought the potion back with me, let me fetch it for you."

Jane dashed over to her backpack, rummaged through it, and handed me a potion.

This potion is housed in a dark bottle, its consistency thick and gooey. I'm clueless as to what it's made of, and it carries a peculiar scent.

However, I trusted Jane and Marcus. So, without further questions, I accepted the potion they offered. Then, I turned to Jane and asked:

"Alright, how much for the potion? I'll pay you, have the doctor brew a few more bottles. I'm afraid, she might harm others around me."
