When Elliott saw me standing side by side with Quinn, and Quinn's hand on my back, he couldn't contain his anger anymore.

"Did you hear that? Move aside! How dare you getting so close with Madeline while Elliott is right here!"

Giselle taunted from Elliott's embrace.

"I'm talking to you! Move!" Elliott's voice grew more sternly as if his patience had reached its limit. He forcefully pushed Giselle away and came straight toward me.

His towering presence seemed to dim the lights around us.

Elliott quickly walked toward me, his gaze sharply scanning me from head to toe.

Under the influence of alcohol, my cheeks started to burn, and I couldn't stand still.

It seemed that Quinn was worried that I might fall, so he never moved his hand from behind my back.

Seeing this, Elliott was clearly annoyed and yanked me into his arms.

"Quinn, how much did you let her drink?"

Elliott stared at Quinn, his tone cold as he interrogated.

Clearly, my flushed cheeks and slightly tipsy eyes had really upset him.

"Not much, just a couple of beers," Quinn responded calmly.

"Why did you let her drink? You know it's not good for her health!"

Elliott's frown deepened as he barked at Quinn.

His concern for my health would have touched me if I hadn't heard what Giselle had said.

So, now, I only found his words ironic.

"Whether I drink is not your concern. You better take care of the person behind you. She was just bragging to her friend about marrying you! I won't disturb you two tonight. Quinn, let's go."

I immediately broke free from Elliott's grip and quickly stepped back to keep my distance.

Staring at Elliott's face, a face I knew all too well, I felt my heart was broken, and I couldn't stand being here another second.

"We haven't cleared things up, and you just walk away?" Elliott's voice grew colder, and he firmly grabbed my arm when I turned to leave.

"Yes, there's a lot we haven't cleared up between us. But it's too much. What now? You want to explain here what is going on between you and Giselle?"

I suppressed the turmoil inside me and looked at him coldly.

"Just leave Madeline alone, Elliott. It's getting late. We can talk another time. Since we've bumped into each other, let me introduce you to my friends!"

Giselle moved closer to Elliott. She clung to his arm and deliberately called out in a sweet tone.

I could tell she wanted to prove to her friends that her relationship with Elliott was indeed close.

However, Elliott refused her. "Let go!"

He wrenched his hand away from Giselle again, his eyes never leaving mine as if Giselle didn't exist at all.

Another public rejection from Elliott left Giselle standing there with her face falling, awkwardly crossing her arms. Obviously, she hadn't seen it coming.

"Wait, I thought Giselle said the Bryant Group CEO is her boyfriend? That doesn't look like it."

"Yeah, he's totally ignoring Giselle, but seems worried about that other woman."

"Right? It's like he's married to her. What's really going on here?"
