Upon seeing my return, my mom immediately waved at me:

"Madeline, come quick, your son's just woken up. Look at him, with his round little eyes and his pouty lips. He's just too adorable!"

"My mom was speaking excitedly, her eyes constantly lingering on Noah," she said.

My dad, on the other hand, was even more dramatic. He rested his hands on the crib, his face a picture of rapture, as if he was admiring the most perfect piece of art in the world.

Watching their adoring expressions, I couldn't help but chuckle. I quickly made my way over, eager to catch a glimpse of Noah in his crib.

He was dressed in a soft blue onesie, a tiny baby cap perched on his head. His little hands were waving about, full of energy. His round, curious eyes darted around, taking in the world with an irresistible cuteness.

"Noah, let mom hold you. I've missed you so much."

My heart instantly melted at the sight of Noah before me. I reached out, lifting him from his crib.

When I felt his tiny, soft body snuggle against mine, the joy and bliss of motherhood washed over me more than ever.

I held him close, unable to resist stealing glance after glance. My mom and dad both gathered around, exchanging words of joy and excitement.

"His little pout, it's so much like Madeline when she was a kid. Madeline used to pout just like that."

"Yeah, his skin is just like Madeline's, milky white and absolutely stunning."

"His thick head of hair, it's truly a testament to our family's genes."

"I reckon Noah's gonna be a handful. Not even a month old and he's already bursting with energy. He's gonna be something else."

Perhaps it's a generational thing, but the more they looked, the more they fell in love.

The more my mom and dad discussed, the brighter their smiles became. The three of us huddled around Noah, as if we held all the happiness in the world.

Perhaps sensing the warmth of his mother's embrace, Noah squirmed in my arms for a bit before peacefully drifting off to sleep.

Mom told me to put Noah in the stroller, not to let him get used to falling asleep in arms.

We three settled Noah in, then tiptoed out of the room together.

It was as if my mom just remembered her precious little girl, and she knocked on my head in annoyance:

"Madeline, you're driving me nuts, always working and never resting. Listen to me, get back to bed and rest right now. I'll get you something to eat."

"Mom, I'm really hungry. I'm gonna go lie down now, thanks!"

I quickly responded with a chuckle.

"I swear, you're just as stubborn as your father. It must run in the family."

My mom looked at me, a mix of laughter and tears in her eyes, shook her head in exasperation, and quickly descended the stairs.

I no longer feel any discomfort in my body. On the contrary, I feel even lighter than before I was pregnant.

All of this, truly, is thanks to Marcus' meticulous care from the moment I became pregnant to the birth of our child. If we could mass produce the tonics he made for me, wouldn't that benefit more women going through childbirth?

I mulled over it as I slid back into bed. No sooner had I settled in than the phone began to ring.
