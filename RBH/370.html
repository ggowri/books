Quinn barged in without a hint of subtlety, tossing his belongings aside and shooting me a disdainful glance.

"Next time when you want to end your life, please give me a heads-up."

His words cut through the tension like a knife, yet strangely, his presence brought a sense of relief.

"Cut the sarcasm, Quinn. Madeline is not well, stop teasing her," Paige chided him.

Quinn asked, "What's the deal? I heard you were vomiting blood and had to be rescued. Is it that serious? If you want to be a successful career woman, you need to learn how to handle stress."

Quinn's words were blunt but effective, as always.

He sauntered over, casually pulling up a chair and sitting down, his mischievous smile still playing on his lips.

His grin and mischievous demeanor always managed to lift my spirits, and I found myself smiling in return.

I didn't hold back and smiled lightly.

"You're still able to crack a smile. I guess it's not as dire as I imagined. When Fiona called me and told me what happened, she made it sound like I'd never see you again."

Quinn said, reaching over to playfully tweak my nose.

He always spolied me like that, which caught me off guard.

Both he and Paige had rushed back overnight upon hearing the news of my condition, which touched me deeply.

Feeling the warmth of their concern, my tense and despondent mood began to lighten.

"Enough of that. Madeline is all fine now. I have no idea what Elliott did to trigger it."

Quinn's words lighten up Fiona's mood. Then she continued.

"I had a feeling it was him. No one else could get under her skin like that."

Quinn's expression softened momentarily as he looked at me, his worry obvious in his eyes.

But as soon as he realized I was watching him, he adopted his usual cynical demeanor.

"You two, go on now. You won't get anything out of her with both of you here. I'll handle this."

Quinn sat down casually as he ordered Paige and Fiona, being confident about how he knew about me.

"Are you sure about that? We've been Madeline's closest friends since childhood," Paige protested.

"Yeah, Quinn, don't flatter yourself" Fiona added, equally disgruntled with his dismissive attitude.

Quinn just raised an eyebrow.

"Sometimes I can do more things than you ladies. Off you go. And while you're at it, grab something for Madeline and me to eat. I haven't had a meal since last night, and I'm starving."

Quinn insisted and then waved his hand.

Paige and Fiona exchanged resigned glances before leaving the ward under the pretext of going shopping.

I took a look at Quinn, who wore a black leather coat with a V-neck undershirt, smoky gray trousers, and short Martin boots.

Despite the winter chill, his clothes seemed extraordinarily thin, accentuating his ruggedly handsome features.

I wondered where he had been all this time, only catching glimpses of his travels through Instagram posts from places like Juccester and Iwrinas.

His wandering lifestyle mirrored his usual aloof and unconventional manners.

"Don't look at me, I know I'm handsome."

Quinn quipped, catching me eyeing him. With a wink, he produced a lucky charm from his shoulder bag and handed it to me.

"I bring it for you from Juccester. Wear it on your wrist and give it a touch whenever you feel anxious. It'll help," he explained with a reassuring smile.
