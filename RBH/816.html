"What are you doing here all by yourself?"

I stepped forward, my tone still carrying a hint of indifference.

Elliott cast me a casual glance, his voice low and raspy:

"Why should I go in when there's no place for me in there?"

Quinn walked in and, as fate would have it, took the very same seat he had occupied before.

"Since when did you become so petty?"

I knew exactly what was bothering him. Yet, I had no intention of offering him any comfort.

It seems, only in this way, can I make myself feel a bit better.

Evidently, my words had struck a chord with Elliott. He shot me a glance, then chuckled softly under his breath. His expression was one of sheer resignation:

"Stingy? So, that's how you see me now."

I stepped forward, grabbing hold of his arm:

"Alright, you've been standing out here for too long, you'll catch a cold. Come in and grab a bite to eat."

The concern laced in my words seemed to soothe him. His tone instantly softened:

"So, you still care if I've eaten or not. I thought you couldn't even see me anymore."

"How could it be? I was just too engrossed in watching the commotion from the sidelines. I thought you were right behind me all along."

I feared he might misunderstand, so I decided to clarify.

Unexpectedly, he suddenly wrapped his arms around me from behind, burying his face in the crook of my neck. The faint scent of smoke mingled with his cologne, creating a unique aroma that filled my nostrils. His voice was a low, barely audible whisper:

"Madeline, it's been ages since we last met. I've truly missed you."

This is clearly a cheesy pick-up line.

Once upon a time, whenever he uttered such words, I would involuntarily melt into a puddle.

But now, hearing these words, I don't just feel indifferent. Instead, I'm actually annoyed.

"I've been a bit swamped lately, there's a lot going on at the company."

The words just tumbled out of my mouth without thinking.

In that fleeting moment, such familiar words stirred memories of times past.

Back when I loved him deeply, countless times I tried to bridge the gap between us in various ways, but all I ever got was this one sentence.

Turns out, this phrase was nothing more than an emotionless excuse, crafted carefully to avoid causing excessive heartache.

I used to naively fool myself into believing he was just too busy.

It wasn't until this moment that I realized, it wasn't that he was too busy. He simply didn't care about me back then, just like how I feel about him now.

Emotions, they're the hardest thing to fake.

The emotions within a person tend to reveal themselves, whether we like it or not. Trying to mask or play a role not only proves challenging, but it can also be downright exhausting.

"So... where am I staying tonight?"

His voice was still raspy, his breath fanning across my face. It was a simple sentence, yet it was laden with an ambiguous undertone.

The answer to this question, I had it figured out the moment he showed up.

"There's plenty of rooms here. I've had Jane set up an extra one. You and Barry bunk together. Quinn and Marcus share another. Sound good?"

I said nonchalantly.

In an instant, Elliott let go of me, his brows furrowing slightly:

"So, you're saying we're not living together?"

"The room's cramped, mate. I'm bunking with Jane. The place ain't much, so bear with us. We can't exactly have Barry kipping in the car now, can we?"

I had no choice but to patiently explain.

Truth be told, I didn't need to compete with Jane for the room at all. But, from the moment Elliott showed up, I've been deliberately avoiding any intimate actions with him.

"Ah."

As sharp as Elliott is, there's no way he wouldn't catch the undertone in my words.

He didn't persist any further. In that fleeting moment, I noticed his expression sinking deeper into despair.

He pulled out another cigarette from his pocket, lit it, and took a deep drag. His handsome face was etched with loneliness.
