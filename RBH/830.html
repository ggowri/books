I haven't seen the two of them for a while.

When I saw Philip again, I was taken aback. He had put on quite a bit of weight and had an air of desolation about him. His hair and clothes were no longer meticulously chosen, but rather, he seemed to have adopted a carefree approach to his appearance.

Contrarily, Alexis seemed to be more spirited than ever. His eyes, though not large, were full of life. He exuded a powerful aura of a lawyer, demanding respect and attention.

Philip and Alexis took their seats.

They first glanced at me, a hint of surprise in their eyes, then turned to Lisa, their surprise escalating instantly.

Barry had already filled them in on Elliott's previous injury and memory loss. So, they understood why I was sticking close to Elliott.

However, their gazes seemed foreign to Lisa.

Elliott introduced the trio to each other. Afterward, Alexis nodded and said, "Ms. Fox and Madeline could easily be mistaken for sisters. They look so alike."

"In this vast world, it's not uncommon to stumble upon someone who looks strikingly similar to another. Remember that news story a while back? About a waiter who was a dead ringer for that hotshot celebrity? But, even though they shared the same face, their auras were worlds apart." Lisa lifted her glass in a toast to Alexis, before continuing her tale.

I had gradually grown accustomed to the fact that Lisa and I looked alike. Hearing her words, I couldn't help but chuckle lightly, "The Ugly Duckling and the Swan may look alike, but there's always a difference. Who's the Ugly Duckling and who's the Swan, anyone with clear sight can tell at a glance."

I won't stay silent and endure anymore.

If anyone pisses me off, I won't hesitate to call them out, regardless of the situation or saving face. As for what others think or say about me, I couldn't care less.

All that matters is my own happiness.

My words instantly infuriated Lisa. She scoffed at me, asking, "So, according to Ms. Mayers, who's the ugly duckling and who's the swan between us?"

After Lisa uttered those words, the atmosphere in the room turned incredibly awkward.

For a moment, an awkward expression crossed the faces of the three gentlemen. Everyone could feel the tension between Lisa and me.

"The seafood here is pretty good, all netted up fresh. Give it a try." Elliott smoothly changed the subject.

Alexis popped a shrimp into her mouth, remarking, "Mmm, it's indeed fresh. Ladies, why don't you give it a try?"

I didn't move, yet, Elliott slid a piece of fish onto my plate. He murmured, "This fish is deep-sea caught, the flesh is incredibly tender, the best you can find on the market right now. Give it a try, Madeline."

"Alright."

I nodded, taking a bite. Indeed, the meat was incredibly fresh and tender.

Elliott placed a cooked shrimp onto Lisa's plate, "Ms. Fox, this shrimp is quite tasty. I know you don't get much fresh seafood where you're from, so you should give it a try. There's a particular way to eat it. Philip, would you show Ms. Fox how it's done?"

Elliott shot Philip a knowing glance, and in an instant, Philip caught on, immediately starting to demonstrate.

He jabbed his fork directly into the shrimp's belly, swiftly shedding its shell and revealing the freshest meat inside.

However, Lisa merely smirked, making no move. She flashed a small smile at me, "Ms. Mayers, you haven't answered my question yet."

The atmosphere, which had just been eased by Elliott, instantly stiffened once again.
