The Christmas prime time slot was a dream for many TV shows.

Did Elliott mean that he would consider rescheduling his airing time for me?

I looked at him incredulously. His words caught me off guard, and I shook my head in disbelief.

"After all the groundwork you've laid in secret, and with the airing date so close, would you really agree to reschedule? Elliott, I don't buy it."

Elliott, however, grabbed my shoulders, forcing me to meet his gaze. He looked at me, his deep eyes sincere.

"I'll admit I've had thoughts of competing with you before, but now, I've had a change of heart. While commercial interests are important, they pale in comparison to our relationship. I just want you to know that I'd do anything for you."

Elliott's sudden confession shook me to the core.

Despite years of seeing his aloofness and superiority, I had never witnessed this sincere and affectionate side of him.

He would do anything for me?

I admit I was moved by his words. However, in the next moment, I thought of the child in Giselle's belly.

In that instant, all the emotions in my heart turned to nothingness. I gave him a cold smile, pushing away his hands and keeping my distance from him once again.

"Is that so? If that's the case, then have Giselle give up having the child. Perhaps then, I could pretend like nothing happened and try to continue this relationship with you."

When I said those words, they were indeed filled with indifference and resentment.

And I distinctly noticed, when I uttered those words, Elliott's eyes widened slightly.

He probably hadn't expected me to say something so cruel. So, for the first time, he fell silent as his gaze dimmed.

"You didn't mean that, did you? It shows you still care about her. Elliott, you know better than I do what is really going on between you and Giselle!"

Through his reaction, my heart sank even further.

These days, despite his growing affection for me and his repeated assurances that there was nothing between him and Giselle, many things subtly hinted that their relationship was not simple.

I didn't confront him because I was tired. I didn't want to keep questioning him about one thing after another. I didn't want to become that crazy and hysterical woman again, entangled in the love world between him and Giselle.

"My grandpa and parents are convinced that she's pregnant with my child. If I insisted otherwise, my grandpa's illness might..."

Elliott pondered for a long time, and just as I turned away, he suddenly spoke slowly.

Once again, he brought up Cormac as an excuse. And I admit, this excuse was one I couldn't refute.

After all, it wasn't wrong for a terminally ill old man to want to see his great-grandchild before he passed away. The fault lay solely in my uncooperative body.

I didn't turn around, but just let out a cold laugh as I left the bathroom.

There were still clothes in the wardrobe that I hadn't taken with me back then. I pulled out a set, about to change when I suddenly smelled a familiar perfume scent from that outfit.

It wasn't a perfume I would use, but rather one that Giselle often wore.
