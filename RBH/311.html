Did I?

I couldn't help but feel bewildered for a moment.

Subconsciously, I ran through my thoughts, and indeed, it seemed to be true. It had been a long time since I had humbled myself, sending him messages or calling him like before.

Unconsciously, I had broken the habit of being overly dependent on him.

However, even though I realized this, I still didn't want to reach out to him out of stubbornness. Pushing his hand away, I whispered, "Your mother has pressured me to divorce you more than once. Even if I were to contact you, it wouldn't change anything. My current health condition cannot fulfill your family's desire for offspring. Our divorce is inevitable."

I couldn't help but recall Laura's repeated pressure and the shadow-like presence of Giselle. And thought about my inexplicable infertility, I felt agitated.

Although Elliott and I now had a substantial relationship, deep down, I still hadn't mustered an ounce of confidence in our relationship.

Many things were like hidden time bombs, readied to explode between us at any moment.

I dared not sink, commit, and believe too much in Elliott's sincerity. Because the pain I experienced in my previous life was truly unbearable.

Elliott looked puzzled.

I guessed Laura's repeated coercion was something she had never mentioned to him.

He reached out and grabbed my shoulder, looking at me incredulously.

"You mean, my mom has pressured you more than once? What did she say to you?"

"What she said, you can probably guess, right? It's not her fault either, after all, I couldn't do what mattered most to them."

My heart ached. Ever since I knew about this situation, it had been the biggest concern in my heart.

Despite seeking medical help in Louis City and taking medication on time every day, I wasn't sure if the doctor's prescription would be effective for a congenital condition.

If it weren't for not wanting Giselle to take over so easily, I would rather divorce Elliott earlier than endure this much torture.

"I've said it doesn't matter. You don't have to worry about my parents' thoughts. The most important thing is the feelings between us."

Elliott looked at me earnestly. Realizing I had suffered injustice from his mother, he embraced me without hesitation.

My head rested firmly against his warm chest. His strong heartbeat was so close to me. His embrace was so warm that it began to melt the facade of strength I had been maintaining.

If a woman could rely on a man, why bother to stay strong?

For a moment, I almost wanted to cry in his arms and tell him all the suffering I had endured in these days.

But my reason kept reminding me, Madeline, you must not.

Because once I relied on him again, it meant falling in love with him. And once in love, it implied enduring the heart-wrenching pain of the past all over again, day and night.

I was afraid of the pain. I would instead choose to stand alone in this world...

"I know you're a dutiful son, and I know how Cormac holds a place in your heart. Elliott, actually, you and I know better than anyone else, without children, we won't last long."

I pushed his hand away forcefully again. At the same time, I quickly stepped back a few steps and walked to the other side of my father's sickbed.

As I said this, I looked at Elliott, tears welling up in my eyes uncontrollably. But I didn't want him to see my vulnerability.

I quickly turned away and wiped the tears from my eyes. At that moment, my mom happened to push open the door and walked in.
