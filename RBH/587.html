His words sounded harsh, rendering me to retort with a cold smirk, "Your sudden price hike is simply pushing me to seek other partners, Elliott. Spare me the innocent act! Our marriage crumbled because you found a new partner first! As for this motor, it was your price increase that led me to consider severing ties! Little did I know you had such a knack for shifting blame back onto others!"

As I became agitated, Elliott unexpectedly lifted my chin.

His eyes fixated on my red lips, concealing emotions I couldn't decipher.

"I've explained countless times, the incident with Giselle's pregnancy, I truly was unaware at first. I find it hard to accept the accusation of betrayal. As for the price hike on the motors, I never gave such an order. As an upright CEO, I wouldn't concern myself with such trivial matters. Now that you stand in a position similar to mine, you should understand my predicament..."

"You're just making excuses for yourself!" I didn't want to hear his explanations. "Elliott, arguing about this is pointless. I'm here to inform you that our partnership ends here. I hope you cooperate with Glory Group's separation plan. From now on, we have no further ties!"

With those words spoken, I rose to leave.

However, Elliott gripped the chair's armrests tightly, trapping me in this small cage.

"I thought you had something important to discuss with me. Turns out, you're only here to say these things..."

Elliott looked at me, a hint of sadness in his gaze.

"At this point, what else is there to say between us?"

I gazed back at him with a touch of irony.

He asked, "You're dead set on being with Quinn, aren't you? No matter how hard I try, it's beyond salvaging, isn't it?"

Elliott suddenly cupped my face, a hint of reluctance in his eyes.

I felt communication between us becoming increasingly difficult. He always evaded my words, harping on about trivial matters.

I spoke coldly once more, "My future and who I'm with, the kind of life I lead, are my own affairs. Soon, we'll become strangers with no intersection in each other's lives, won't we?"

He looked at me, somewhat incredulous.

"Can you erase all those deep emotions from your diary, all the feelings you once had for me?"

I chuckled, realizing that the tumultuous feelings were long gone.

Love had faded, and reminiscing only made me see how naive I was. I dedicated years of my youth to an idealized man, only to end up like this.

"I have erased them, Elliott," I said coldly.

"Is that so? Then I'll leave a lasting mark on your life once again. I refuse to believe that anyone can replace me in your heart!"

I never expected his gaze to turn fierce. Without warning, he fiercely bit my lip.
