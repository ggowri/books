"Isn't it lively here? You go ahead and enjoy yourself; I've got some things to attend to first," Quinn said with a grin, leaning in close to my ear. As the host of this party, he clearly had tasks to handle. After leading me into the venue, he quickly disappeared into the crowd.

With my mask on, I wandered casually through the colorful scene. A waiter passed by, and I grabbed a glass of crimson-colored drink from his tray, curious about its taste. It was tangy and sweet, and I must say it was quite delicious.

At this moment, a girl dressed as an angel grabbed my arm and said, "Hey, gorgeous. You seem to be alone. Why don't you join us for a dance? We're short of one person."

She, too, wore a mask, so that I couldn't see her face, but her sweet voice sounded friendly. Since I didn't have any company anyway, I nodded readily and joined their group.

It was a lively group of five young and energetic girls, totaling six, including me. After a brief introduction to the rules, I quickly joined in the dance with them.

I took dancing lessons when I was a kid, so I had a solid foundation in dancing. Although I was a bit rusty at first, I quickly caught on to their rhythm. Dancing with them became increasingly exhilarating, to the point where our group suddenly found itself surrounded by several young men in eccentric robes.

These men, all dressed in identical black robes and masks, appeared tall and imposing. Judging by their attire, they seemed to be portraying wizards.

"Hey, our private booth is over there. Would you honor us with your presence and join us for a few drinks?" the leader of the men said to us. His voice sounded strangely familiar, almost like Philip's.

Was Philip also here? I couldn't help but wonder as I discreetly scrutinized the figure in the black robe.

However, the robes were too loose, and apart from their towering height, I couldn't discern any distinguishing features. So, I remained silent, quietly waiting to see how the other girls would respond.

The girl who had invited me to join them seemed to be quite playful herself, and seeing someone initiate a conversation, she smiled and said, "Sure, but we'll only drink the most expensive wine here. Can you afford that?"

"Of course, that's not a problem," the young man replied promptly, then made a gentlemanly gesture of invitation.

His tone, his mannerisms, and the swagger in his speech led me to believe that it was most likely Philip himself behind the mask.

He already had something going on with Paige, yet he continued to delay facing reality. Seeing him flirt with other girls now just made me mad.

So, I quietly followed the other girls and sat down with the boys at their booth.

Once seated, I discreetly took out my phone, snapped a picture of the leader in the black robe, and sent it to Quinn along with a message that read, "Is this Philip?"
