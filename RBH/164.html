I knew that she was lying.

If she really didn't like Elliott, she would never say things like there is a big gap between them and it's impossible for them to be together.

She subconsciously defended herself like this, proving that she must have thought so in her subconscious mind.

This further confirmed his own judgment, and he couldn't help but feel fortunate.

If I hadn't preemptively performed her script a few times, Elliott might have already fallen for her by now.

"What else did you guys talk about?"

I pretended to casually ask, hoping to gather more information from her.

She regained her composure.

She lowered her head and whispered, "He didn't say anything else. Elliott just said that he feels sorry for me. He said that I lost my father when I was young and had to rely on myself, unlike you. He also said that he wouldcompensate me twice as much in the future, and I can directly tell him what I need. But for now, I don't need anything. Madeline, as long as you and Elliott are happy, that's all that matters. If there's nothing else, then I'll leave first."

Giselle finished speaking and then stood up.

She had a different meaning in her words, her hidden thoughts were like thorns, piercing into my heart, causing my heart to ache as if it had been torn apart.

These are obviously words that Elliott would say, and she deliberately let me know, perhaps also deliberately wanting to make me sad.

However, she deliberately boasted with such an innocent tone, and I found it difficult to say anything.

After all, they both haven't made me aware of anything happening between them now, so I shouldn't argue with a little girl.

I laughed again.

I looked at the seemingly delicate girl in front of me, but every word and every expression seemed as if they had been carefully rehearsed. I understood even more why I had lost to her in my previous life.

The truly formidable enemy never boasts loudly, but rather, like her, hides in the shadows and strikes with precision when you least expect it, like a snake.

"Okay, then you go back first."

"Okay, sure. Madeline, take good care of yourself. I'll come to see you another day."

Giselle nodded and then turned around, walking out of my hospital room.

Giselle had just left when Elliott hurried back from the company.

He was wearing a silver-gray suit. As soon as he entered the room, I could smell that familiar hint of light perfume.

And this fragrance, overlapping with the lingering scent of Giselle's perfume in the air, I was instantly taken aback.

When Giselle was in the ward just now, I couldn't help but feel a sense of familiarity with the scent of her perfume. At this moment, I remembered that the scent of her perfume was surprisingly similar to the fragrance Elliott often used in his car.

Did Elliott give her perfume?

I felt confused, and Elliott clearly had no idea what I was thinking. Seeing me sitting on the hospital bed in a daze, he immediately approached and flicked my forehead with his finger.

He said, "What are you thinking about that you're so fascinated?"

"Did you just come over and meet Giselle?"

I said instinctively.

Elliott paused for a moment, then nodded.

"When I arrived at the door, she was standing there waiting for the bus, so I had Toby take her back. What did you all talk about?"

Elliott looked at me, probably thinking that my face was a bit pale. He felt a little sorry for me and gently rubbed my lower lip with the pad of his thumb. His actions were particularly gentle, and his eyes were full of compassion.

He really looked like the image of what a good husband should be.

I should have been moved, but why is it that I didn't feel anything in my heart, instead, I felt an unusually deep sadness?
