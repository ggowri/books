"So, you're saying that, according to you, Elliott...loves me?"

I looked at Quinn, as if finally confirming a fact I'd been too afraid to believe, and asked with difficulty.

"I reckon he's in love with you, Madeline. You ought to go see him."

Without a moment's hesitation, Quinn responded with certainty.

"Aren't you afraid that after I see him, I might... reconcile with him?"

I stared at Quinn, somewhat taken aback by his composure, yet realizing that this was the very quality that made Quinn so admirable and commendable.

"If you two can truly forget the past and reconcile, and if he genuinely loves you, then I will sincerely wish you well. Love is never about holding someone hostage, it's about wanting to be with them. Madeline, there's never been a moment where I've wanted to force you to stay by my side."

Quinn looked at me and laughed, a laugh so open and genuine. But I couldn't help but notice the sadness hidden within his smile.

He truly is that kind of exceptional man, so perfect it leaves no room for criticism. He would even go as far as to inconvenience himself.

"Reconciliation seems unlikely, I've said it before, I'm not one to look back. However, he got hurt because of me, I should at least go see him."

I chuckled, feeling even more indebted to Quinn at this moment.

I get it, this kind of debt isn't exactly the kind of love you'd find between a man and a woman. But I've long since considered Quinn one of the most important people in my life.

That night, after much hesitation, I finally clicked on the link Barry had sent me.

I never imagined that Elliott had signed up on a popular website, pouring out his innermost feelings and thoughts in words for the world to see.

All these words were penned down in the wee hours of the morning.

Clearly, Barry wasn't lying when he said he was battling insomnia.

"I never understood what love tasted like until now. It's like a virus, invading your mind, corroding your heart, making you constantly crave her presence. But I have to resist, because she's no longer mine."

"I used to despise men who drowned themselves in liquor. But now, because of her, I've fallen for the strongest spirits. Yet, I can no longer hold the one I love most in my arms as I drift off to sleep."

"She always thought I didn't care about her, but she'll never know, she was the first woman in my life, and so far, the only one."

"I've done all I could, I'm at a loss for how to win her back. Loving someone, it's really tough."

"She's becoming more and more exceptional, attracting more and more people around her. I'm becoming less and less significant in her heart, and there's nothing I can do about it."

"Her departure has made my once-prideful career seem insignificant. I spent the entire meeting today thinking about her. If this continues, I seriously wonder if I'm sick, or if she's implanted a chip in my brain."

"I find myself consumed by this insane urge to accept her and everything about her, even if the child she bears isn't mine. All I want is for her to return to my side. I think, I must be going mad."
