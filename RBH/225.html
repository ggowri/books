This song used to be one of my favorite songs. The emotions expressed in this song were very consistent with my mood in those years. It seemed to represent the deep love I once had for Elliott.

However, I just liked to listen to it in the past, but I had never sung it in front of anyone.

But tonight, when I sang out loud with the microphone, I realized I had accumulated too much sadness and needed to vent it.

Sitting alone at the bar and looking at the screen, I sang loudly into the microphone. Quinn sat beside me at some point.

He put his hand on my shoulder. When I finished singing, he patted me on the shoulder.

Then, he leaned over and whispered in my ear, "Are you alright? Why were you singing so sadly?"

I turned my head and looked at him. I wanted to smile, but for some reason, when I saw his face, my tears rolled down inexplicably.

I didn't say anything, but Quinn seemed to have understood. He leaned close to me again and said a word that almost made me collapse.

"Are you still unable to get over him?"

I didn't say anything.

Yet Quinn seemed to have read my mind and could always guess what I was thinking so easily.

I didn't answer, but he turned off the music.

He said, "Stop. Let's have more wine."

"Sure."

I nodded and stood up. I wiped away my tears and quickly rejoined them, pretending nothing had happened.

Quinn proposed, "Let's switch teams. Madeline and I will compete with the three of you. What do you think?"

Paige immediately looked at him with evil intentions.

She joked, "What are you doing? You want to be Madeline's knight?"

"Can't I?" Quinn blinked and didn't deny it.

"Of course, you can. But Madeline is unlucky tonight. Are you sure you want to be in a group with her?" Paige provoked with a smile.

"Sure. If we lose and she can't drink, I will drink."

Quinn patted his chest domineeringly.

"Okay. Let's get started!" Fiona said with a smile.

Quinn's actions inexplicably made me feel warm.

After all, I knew many people in this world. But there were very few people who could understand you even if I didn't say anything.

In the past, I was stupidly infatuated with Elliott. I had never paid attention to other men, so I had never had male friends.

But in this life, Quinn's kindness indeed gave me some strength invisibly.

I quickly sorted out my feelings and had fun with them again.

Quinn whispered in my ear and occasionally taught me the tricks of playing dice. Under his guidance, I no longer lost all the time.

I seemed to have regained my confidence in this game and suddenly became competitive.

Quinn and I worked together very well. Soon, the three of them couldn't drink anymore. In the end, they couldn't help but beg us for mercy.

Such pure venting was really delightful, and I became cheerful unconsciously. When I saw Paige couldn't drink anymore, I deliberately pinched her nose and poured the wine into her mouth, which made her chase me around in anger.

I couldn't help laughing. Inadvertently, I saw Quinn staring at me. The relief and encouragement in his eyes touched me, and I smiled at him.
