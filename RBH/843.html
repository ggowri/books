"It was a spur-of-the-moment decision. It's my work, not like I need to report to you or anything," I said, a hint of annoyance in my voice.

Elliott was pacing around the room, ready to head out to the hallway. I quickly grabbed his arm, "Shouldn't you be with your client? What are you doing wandering around here?"

"I just saw them out, Madeline. Did your client bring a baby? Why is there a baby bottle?"

Not only did Elliott stick around, but he also noticed a baby bottle on the table, recently emptied by my son.

That baby bottle? It was left behind by Lily in her rush.

"Ah, the client just had a newborn," I explained.

To prevent his endless questioning, I immediately grabbed his arm and yanked him out of the room. "Enough, no more questions. It's getting late, and you've had quite a bit to drink. You should head home and get some rest."

Elliott reeked of booze. As soon as I got close to him, I could smell the potent mix of alcohol and cologne on him.

"Alright then, I suppose you wouldn't dare to do anything shady behind my back."

Elliott lowered his gaze, scrutinizing my face with lingering doubt in his eyes.

Luckily, Barry was still waiting outside the room.

I quickly shouted at Barry, "Barry, what are you waiting for? Mr. Bryant's had too much to drink. Get him to the car, pronto!"

"Madeline, aren't you coming back with me?"

Though Elliott had quite a bit to drink, his mind remained sharp. At my words, he halted again, fixing me with his gaze.

"I..."

I was just about to explain when a text message popped up on my phone.

"I've already seen your folks off to their car, you alright? I'm waiting for you downstairs."

I glanced down at the text message just as Elliott leaned in closer.

He too saw the content of the text message, and he knew it was a message from Quinn.

Elliott suddenly blurted out, "So, you were just having dinner with Quinn, right?"

Elliott abruptly turned me around, interrogating me. His piercing gaze held a hint of sadness.

Before I could respond, he swiftly let go of me, turning on his heel and striding towards the stairs.

"Elliott, hold up!"

I hurried to catch up, but Elliott was fast. In the blink of an eye, he was already down the stairs.

By the time I caught up at the front gate, Elliott was already standing before Quinn.

Before I could even process what was happening, Elliott had already swung a punch at Quinn.

Watching as that heavy fist was about to land on Quinn, I yelled out in panic, then immediately sprinted over:

"Quinn, watch out!"
