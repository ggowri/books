"If my memory serves me right, this ward is under 24-hour surveillance, isn't it? Instead of spewing lies here, why don't you check the footage and see what really happened! Laura, I used to respect you as an elder, but now, all I feel is contempt. Because, you're not worth my respect at all!"

With a cold tone, I finished my words and let go of her collar.

If it wasn't for the fact that she's Noah's grandma, I'd really want to punch her. She's just so punchable.

"Mrs. Laura Bryant, it was I who invited Ms. Mayers here. You see, Mr. Bryant has been calling out her name in his sleep. The doctor even mentioned that Mr. Bryant's rapid recovery has a lot to do with Ms. Mayers' company. So, you've misunderstood Ms. Mayers."

At this moment, Barry walked in. In front of Laura, he stammered out a defense for me.

Laura probably never imagined that Barry, always quiet and obedient, would actually speak up in my defense.

Her mouth fell open in shock, words failing her. She probably felt that Barry's words had slapped her in the face, and she snapped back fiercely:

"Get out, who gave you the right to speak here?"

"Hold up, Barry, don't go just yet!"

No sooner had Laura's words faded than I hollered at Barry. Barry, initially frightened, was about to bolt. But my shout stopped him in his tracks. He turned around mechanically, like a robot, staring at me with a face full of fear, as if he'd been petrified.

"Pull up the surveillance footage from earlier, let Laura get a good look at it. And while you're at it, have the doctor check out what's going on with Elliott."

"Alright, I'm on it."

"Also, judging by Elliott's current state, he should be waking up soon. To avoid any misunderstandings, I won't be coming over anymore. I wouldn't want certain people thinking they've birthed a prince and everyone's after him. Little do they know, even princes can be rejected. I'm done with it. Goodbye!"

Though I was speaking to Barry, my words were truly meant for Laura.

After I finished speaking, it was clear as day that Laura was seething with anger.

I let out a cold chuckle, swiftly straightening out my clothes. I grabbed my trench coat from the hanger, slipping it on with ease, ready to leave Elliott's hospital room.

Before leaving, I cast a long, lingering glance towards the direction of the hospital bed.

But in a blink, I tucked away all my emotions and swiftly made my exit.

I didn't expect Laura to come chasing after me. She hollered from behind:

"Madeline, you haven't answered my question from earlier. Did you have the baby, or did you miscarry? Was it a boy or a girl?"

At this point, I didn't expect she'd still be hung up on my kid.

Her words left me feeling a mix of amusement and annoyance. I turned to face her, and memories of the past insults I had endured from her flooded back, leaving a stifling sensation in my chest:

"None of your damn business."

I responded to her, then turned on my heel, striding confidently towards the elevator.

Just then, out of the corner of my eye, I caught sight of the woman named Lisa.

She was less than ten meters away from me, leaning casually against the wall, dressed in a burgundy coat with a delicate belt loosely tied around her waist. One of her legs was hooked back against the wall, one hand in her pocket, a slender cigarette hanging from her lips. Her eyes were slightly squinted, her head tilted as she looked in my direction.

I was absolutely certain her gaze was fixed on me. Her stare was unabashed, radiating a potent hostility.

This woman truly lives up to her name, resembling a fox to a tee. The way her narrow eyes fixate on people, it's both mysterious and eerie.

A shiver ran down my spine, unbidden. Marcus's words from earlier drifted into my mind, uninvited.

This woman, she's not only versed in pharmacology, but also skilled in some unique treatment methods.
