"Is she going to get sick from anger? Shouldn't we go check on her?"

I watched as Laura was escorted away by a group of people, feeling somewhat guilty deep down.

"She'll be fine. There are doctors around, nothing to worry about," Quinn reassured me, pulling me without hesitation towards the exit of the yacht.

Two hours later, I hurried home to change clothes and quickly returned to the office at the Glory Group. Just as I sat down to start working, I received a call from Elliott, his tone filled with interrogation.

"Madeline, where were you last night?"

His tone irritated me.

"What do you mean? What does it matter to you where I was?"

Elliott's tone grew even more displeased. "Were you with Quinn last night?"

I thought, "Ha! He is just my ex-husband. What does it matter to him who I'm with? Why does he have the right to question me?"

"Yes, any problem with that? It's perfectly normal. We're already divorced, I can be with whomever I want," I replied coldly.

"Madeline, I can't believe you even attended the socialite party last night!"

Elliott's tone turned angrier.

I couldn't help but chuckle, "So what if I attended? Your mom was there too. Is it abnormal for me to go if she went? You're being overly broad in your oversight!"

Elliott raised his voice.

"How dare you mention my mom! Her blood pressure rose because of your antics, and now she's receiving treatment at Bryant Hospital! I never thought you'd stoop so low. You've truly disappointed me!"

I thought, "Me? Low?

Even if I did, it's nothing compared to their Bryant family's antics, right?"

I never expected Elliott to call and interrogate me so early. It made me furious.

"Her blood pressure rose, and so did mine! Elliott, I beg you and your mom, curb your curiosity about my life. Stop disturbing my present life. You have no right or qualification to interfere. We're divorced!"

I angrily spoke into the phone before hanging up, shaking with anger.

Since the divorce, I found myself increasingly disliking Elliott and his family. I felt more and more that all those years I spent with him were a waste of effort.

Adam pushed the door open and walked in.

Seeing me still fuming, he was startled and cautiously approached, asking, "Madeline, are you okay? Who made you angry so early?"

"Nothing, what do you need from me?"

I was still angry, so my tone came out a bit irritated as I glanced at Adam.

Adam shivered in fear, quickly placing a bag of snacks he had prepared for me on my desk.

"Eating sweets can improve your mood, Madeline. Have some cakes to calm down. Although there is something I need to report to you, if you're not in a good mood now, I can come back later?"

Adam was perspicacious. He wanted to slip away when he sensed my bad mood.

But his cautious and careful demeanor amused me.

Adam was always worried I'd go hungry, often preparing various snacks for me. Such thoughtful gestures were truly rare.

Pretending to be angry, I glared at him. "Alright, let's continue working. What's the matter?"
