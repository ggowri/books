I received a message at this time, sent by Quinn.

"Do you want to come out and play?"

I thought to myself, "Quinn doesn't even want to stay at home during these few days of Thanksgiving."

"No, I was at home enjoying the moon."

I rapidly tapped on the screen and replied to him.

"Watching the moon is so boring, let's go out and have some fun. Paige is also here."

Quinn quickly replied to me again and sent me a photo.

In the photo, he and Paige were singing with a group of people. Paige was dressed very sexy and was holding a bottle of beer, drinking.

I didn't go, you take care of Paige, make sure she is safe.

I saw Paige like this and felt a little worried, so I quickly replied.

Okay, don't worry.

Quinn replied to me quickly.

Elliott turned his head at that moment, just as I finished chatting with Quinn, but evidently, he had already seen.

He had a clearly displeased expression on his face.

"Do you two often chat?"

"Not often, occasionally," I said truthfully.

"I didn't want you to get too close to Quinn," he said calmly, suddenly.

Quinn and I, to be honest, didn't really get close. I felt like we were just ordinary friends, and I don't know why Elliott was so concerned about it.

"Let's still enjoy the moon quietly, this red velvet cake is good, try it."

The moonlight was beautiful tonight, and I didn't want to waste time on such annoying topics. So I said, and I casually put a piece of red velvet cake into my mouth.

My mom didn't deceive me, this red velvet cake was indeed delicious, and, it wasn't greasy at all, very sweet.

I was eating when Elliott suddenly asked, "Weren't you always wanting a child before? If you can stop arguing with me, we can start trying for a baby by the end of the year."

I almost choked, almost unable to swallow a bite of red velvet cake.

I quickly gulped down several mouthfuls of water, which finally suppressed the shock in my heart. I turned my head to look at him, feeling even more convinced that I couldn't understand him at all.

I thought to myself, "Did he really say to me so seriously that I want a child is just a dream?"

"Why did you suddenly change your mind and want to have a baby with me now?"

"But I have changed my mind now. I want to work hard and I don't want to think about having children for the time being."

I said to him seriously.

Elliott was feeling a bit unhappy. He turned his head to look at me, his sharp gaze filled with a hint of disbelief.

"What were you thinking now?"

"What did you think?"

"I said that we didn't need to make the relationship so tense. Maybe we could find a different way to get along well."

Elliott reiterated once again, and his tone sounded particularly sincere, as if he had truly pondered over it.

But I didn't think so. I suddenly sat up from the chair and looked at him, asking in confusion.

"What kind of way? As siblings? Well, that's not impossible either."

Elliott had just picked up a slice of watermelon when my words choked him, almost causing the entire piece of fruit to get stuck in his throat.
