Lisa gave a slight smile, feigning indifference as she said, "I see, it seems I've misjudged Ms. Mayers. I apologize to you, and I hope you won't hold a grudge, considering our striking resemblance."

After speaking, Lisa deliberately flashed her freshly manicured hand. Her nails, painted a vivid red, eerily resembled the blood-stained claws of a fox, sending an inexplicable shiver down my spine.

"Madeline, you and Lisa really are two peas in a pod. The first time I saw her, I almost mistook her for you. Apart from the voice, you two are like two sides of the same coin."

A smile finally graced Elliott's face. In front of Lisa, he wrapped his arm around my waist. He looked at me with eyes full of love.

"No, I beg to differ. Elliott, can't you tell the difference between Ms. Fox and me?" I asked, my voice icy.

I'm well aware that Lisa and I bear a striking resemblance to each other. However, I'm not exactly fond of this similarity. In fact, I find it somewhat offensive.

Perhaps it's because Lisa always gives me a peculiar vibe.

"Of course, I can tell the difference."

Elliott picked up on the annoyance in my tone, immediately responding. He then gently squeezed my waist, as if to soothe me.

I was about to speak, my brows furrowed, when Lisa beat me to it. "I share the same sentiment with Ms. Mayers. I once thought I was unique, never expecting that there would be someone in this world who looks like me."

Elliott declared, "To me, Madeline is one of a kind. I can't see any other woman but her in my eyes."

Elliott rarely made such emotional declarations in public.

And what's more, he said it right in front of a woman who clearly had feelings for him.

This shift, it caught me off guard. I couldn't help but glance up at him, a smile playing on my lips.

As I looked at Lisa, it was clear she was upset. She was staring at Elliott, her eyes brimming with hurt that even I couldn't help but feel a pang of sympathy.

Clearly, Lisa wasn't just skilled in medicine, she was also a master of seduction. No man could resist her captivating gaze.

However, Elliott's gaze finally landed on me.

"I've had a taste of Mr. Bryant's devotion today. I'm a bit peckish, I'll grab something to eat first."

Lisa quickly made up an excuse, then turned and hurried away.

It seemed as though she wiped her eyes with her hand as she turned away.

How long has she known Elliott?

Maybe I'm overthinking it.

Snapping back to reality, I found Elliott's hand suddenly shifting upwards, forcing my head onto his shoulder. He whispered softly, "Sleep a bit more. I'll wake you when it's time to board."

"Alright."

I wasn't really tired anymore, but to avoid further interaction with Elliott, I'd rather feign sleep to dodge the awkwardness.

However, there was an unmistakably familiar scent on Elliott's shoulder, broad and comforting enough that I found myself unconsciously drifting off to sleep against it.

After what felt like an eternity, it was finally time to board the plane.

Elliott roused me, taking my hand and leading me into the cabin. Once again, he had booked first class, and just like last time, we sat side by side.

And there was Lisa, sitting right behind us.
