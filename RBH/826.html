Elliott wasn't always this meticulous.

He seldom cares about what I love to eat, what I fancy.

And at this moment, his sudden thoughtfulness caught me off guard.

I was feeling a bit drowsy before, but now, sleep seems to elude me.

Feigning sleep proved to be a bit of a challenge, so I decided to just open my eyes and sit up straight. I asked, feigning confusion, "How did I fall asleep? How much longer until we reach Vespera Airport?"

"We're two hours out, Mrs. Bryant. You can catch some more sleep," came Barry's voice from the front seat.

"I'm awake now, Elliott. You should catch some sleep too."

I casually glanced over my shoulder at Elliott, our eyes meeting in a serendipitous moment.

He said with a grin, "Madeline, you've finally stopped calling me by my full name!"

Is that so?

Did I always call him by his full name before?

I hadn't even noticed that.

His words left me a bit dazed, but as I mulled it over, it seemed he was right. A subconscious smile tugged at my lips:

"Is it weird to use full names? Many couples do that."

"Something's off. You usually call me Elliott, sometimes even honey. Seems like I must've messed up somewhere recently, or haven't been doing well enough."

Elliott, however, took it seriously, beginning to reflect on his own mistakes.

"No, don't overthink it. You should get some rest, you didn't sleep well last night."

I didn't want to delve deeper into this topic with Elliott, so I simply urged on.

"Alright, got it."

Elliott obediently closed his eyes.

I didn't expect him to lean in, resting his head on my lap.

I wasn't sure if he was just too tired, or if my presence genuinely helped him sleep. But before long, I could hear the steady rhythm of Elliott's breathing.

Clearly, he had fallen asleep.

I instinctively glanced down at him. His eyes were closed, his lashes long. His skin was flawless. With Elliott's looks, he could easily become a superstar, even if he ventured into acting.

Once upon a time, I was utterly infatuated with his face.

Him sleeping with his head on my lap, it's something I've always yearned for.

Now that it's happened, I find myself not as thrilled as I thought I would be.

His handsome appearance no longer stirs my heart.

Even so, I could feel my thigh going numb with him using it as a pillow.

Time sure does drag on.

Finally, after two long hours, the moment the car pulled into the airport entrance, I couldn't help but let out a deep sigh of relief.

I nudged Elliott's arm, "Wake up, we're at the airport."

Elliott's eyes snapped open instantly. He rose from my lap, casting a glance out the window. His voice, raspy, murmured, "We're here already."

Barry swiftly parked the car in the basement.

After that, Elliott and I got out of the car.
