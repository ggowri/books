Marcus arrived at the Bryant family's place in no time, bringing along a few patches of medicine.

I had someone prepare the medicine as per Marcus's instructions. In front of Jack and me, Marcus administered the medicine to Elliott, and then, he began to give him injections.

Throughout the entire acupuncture process, Marcus was completely engrossed. Jack and I stood off to the side, not daring to make a sound, for fear of interrupting Marcus's treatment.

Marcus was drenched in sweat, his expression alternating between tension and relief. Finally, under his intricate needlework, we saw a few drops of blood, darker than ink, drip from Elliott's finger.

Meanwhile, a sigh of relief escaped Marcus, his face finally breaking into a smile:

"Luckily, they only messed around a bit. With some detox medication and the special treatment, it can be resolved. I'll be back. Once these bruises are cleared, Mr. Bryant should be waking up."

Marcus' words indirectly confirmed mine and Jack's suspicions about the woman. A look of relief washed over Jack's face:

"If that's the case, then it's fantastic! I was wondering why Mr. Bryant hadn't woken up yet. Turns out, someone's been messing around."

"Phew, good thing it was just a minor trick. Otherwise, even with my medical skills, it would've been tough to handle."

Marcus said with a laugh, his face visibly easing up.

For the next few days, I didn't go anywhere, not even home. I stayed glued to Elliott's side.

Once upon a time, my favorite thing was to watch Elliott lying quietly beside me, going nowhere.

On the sickbed, the face I once knew so well was mature and handsome, its skin a sickly pale. His eyebrows and neatly arranged eyelashes framed a pair of unusually elongated eyes. His originally sharp features had softened considerably due to his prolonged bed rest.

I once loved him. Every time I gazed at him, resting my cheek on my hand, I thought he was the epitome of beauty. No matter how much I looked at him, it never seemed enough. And the more I looked, the more I loved him, the more I felt I could never let go in this lifetime.

Though I no longer love him now, I have to admit, staring at his face for too long, those long-lost hormones that once fluttered in the depths of my heart, still have a kind of urge to break free.

"Elliott, could you quit playing the Sleeping Beauty? Do you really think you're that handsome? Let me tell you, looking at your face right now, I don't find it attractive at all. I'm even questioning my taste back then. What's so great about you? I used to like you so much, but now, the more I think about it, the less I understand."

Guarding a sick person, worry as you might, the boredom is simply unbearable.

I couldn't help but start to rant about him. After all, he was just lying there motionless, and I figured, no matter how much I ranted, it wouldn't hurt his pride.

Unexpectedly, the sickly man lying lifelessly on the hospital bed twitched his eyelids, followed by a slight movement of his lips. Before I could even react, his eyes snapped open, glaring at me with a face full of anger.
