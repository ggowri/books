"Go ahead, surpass me. It doesn't matter. To me, you're what matters most."

Elliott didn't give a damn. With a nonchalant smile, he surprisingly pulled up a business proposal on his phone and slid it across to me, saying:

"This is the blueprint for Bryant Group's next three years, the top-secret file of the company. Feel free to peruse it, Madeline, if you're interested."

Elliott's level of composure took me by surprise.

To convince me of his promise, he was even willing to share something as confidential as this with me.

I couldn't help but feel a bit moved.

Yet, I didn't even glance at his business proposal. Instead, I shut off his phone screen. I looked at him and said:

"Elliott, I can sense your complete trust in me right now. But honestly, I don't need to know all this. It's actually quite fun to be rivals with the Bryant Group. If I knew your next move in advance, even if I won, it would take all the fun out of it."

Elliott instantly grasped the meaning in my words.

Rather than him spilling the beans about Bryant Group's business plan, I'd prefer to be his business rival.

The gradual success in the business realm over the past few years has allowed me to savor the taste of accomplishment from my career.

I relish the sensation of Glory Group's steady ascent on the wealth charts. More than that, I yearn for the day when, armed with my own abilities and the strength of Glory Group, we topple Bryant Group and ascend to the pinnacle.

This feeling really gets my blood pumping.

Instead of knowing all the plans of the Bryant Group in advance, which is akin to having the answers to a test beforehand, it takes away the thrill of the game between us. It's no fun at all.

"So, my Madeline is an ambitious little lady after all. It's my fault for not understanding you enough before, leaving you without the chance to showcase your talents for so long."

Elliott gently caressed my face, tears welling up in his eyes.

From his words, I could clearly hear his regret for treating me so coldly in the past.

I froze, speechless for a good while, a surge of tears threatening to spill.

This is the "understanding" that my heart has longed for, yet always seemed just out of reach.

"Let's dig into the noodles first, they won't taste as good once they've cooled down."

I bowed my head, using the act of eating noodles as a cover for the turmoil within.

"Your spaghetti is still the best. I've been craving this taste for a long time."

After taking a bite, Elliott gave a thumbs-up in praise.

"Feel free to eat more if you find it tasty. You can't have food with strong flavors right now, so I've made the meat sauce a bit milder than usual."

My tone involuntarily softened considerably.

"The noodles you made are just like you, perfectly done. Almost forgot, I bought you a set of clothes, let me fetch it for you."

Elliott polished off a plate of noodles in no time. Wiping his mouth, he sprang up as if suddenly remembering something, and darted off to the cloakroom upstairs.

In no time, he descended the stairs, a gift bag in hand, and presented it to me.
