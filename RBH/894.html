There's a medical facility nestled within the golf course.

After Elliott swept me off my feet, a waiter noticed my injury. Without missing a beat, he directed Elliott to take me to the infirmary.

Lucy was a force to be reckoned with, and I was no match for her. The gash on my arm was deep. After the field medic had hastily bandaged it to stop the bleeding, he still recommended that I head to the hospital for a proper check-up.

Elliott paced back and forth in the medical office, seething with anger. Today's incident had him fuming. He barked at the golf course manager:

"Outrageous! She dares to harm others right here! Send her to the police station immediately! No one is allowed to plead for her!"

"Mr. Bryant, the police just took her away. However, she seemed a bit off-kilter. She was babbling nonsense the whole time."

The golf course manager said with a hint of fear.

Insane? Babbling nonsense?

I lay on the infirmary bed, my mind involuntarily replaying the twisted expression and eerie gaze of Lucy just before she lunged at me.

I've seen a similar expression on Elliott's face before, back when he was unconscious.

And Lucy, Lisa had brought her along specifically for today. The thought sent a shiver down my spine.

Just then, Lisa darted into the medical office.

The moment she stepped in, fear was etched all over her face. Innocently, she asked Elliott,

"Mr. Bryant, is Ms. Mayers okay? I didn't expect Lucy to be so impulsive as to do something like this. I brought her here, so I share the blame. I'm really sorry—"

She didn't even spare me a glance, her eyes were just sadly fixed on Elliott.

But it was me who got hurt, why is she apologizing to Elliott?

"Lisa, why on earth did you bring Lucy here? And why is she carrying a knife?"

Elliott glared at Lisa, his eyes still brimming with fury.

"I'm as clueless as you are, mate. I've bumped into her a few times at parties, we hit it off and became friends. We were supposed to be here for a friendly round of golf today. I didn't expect to run into you guys here. As for why she's packing a blade, I'm in the dark as much as you are. I know it's all on me. I shouldn't have brought her here. I'm sorry, Ms. Mayers, are you feeling any better?"

Lisa cast a pitiful glance at Elliott. Only after she had spoken did she shift her gaze from his face, flicking a quick look in my direction.

Our eyes met, and I could sense no apology in her gaze. She was merely pretending to be sorry in front of Elliott.

"Elliott, would you mind stepping out for a bit? I'd like to have a word with Ms. Fox alone."

I calmly regarded the woman before me.

If everything that had happened before could still be chalked up to coincidence, then there was no way she could hide her intentions this time, no matter what.

I'm absolutely certain she's targeting me.

At first, she was just targeting me in secret. But now, she's lost control, wanting others to hurt me.

If I let her carry on like this, I'll only find myself sinking deeper into a defensive position.

It's time to lay my cards on the table with her, to see what her true intentions really are.

"What? Ms. Mayers, what do you want to chat about?"

Lisa blinked at me, her eyes wide with fear.

"I've got something to tell you. Elliott, you need to leave."

I instantly became serious.

"Alright, I'll be just outside. Holler if you need me."
