Elliott looked at me, his eyes betraying a deep hurt at that moment.

Seizing his moment of shock, I pushed his arm away forcefully, trying to open the door to leave.

He quickly recovered and desperately tried to hold onto my hand. However, he was a step too late. I had already opened the door.

A man who had just come to use the restroom stood at the doorway, staring at us in shock, and exclaimed loudly.

I pulled my hair back from my forehead and walked out with a cold expression.

The pain in my chest still prickled at me. I leaned against the wall subconsciously as I was unsteady on my feet.

"Madeline... Madeline Bryant!"

Elliott followed me out and called out my name, trying to make me stay.

But I didn't want to turn back. Pretending to be strong, I left quickly without looking back.

Outside the restaurant, the night was profoundly deep. The entire sky was covered with clouds, making it hard to breathe.

I walked briskly to my car, got in, and slammed the door shut. That was when I couldn't hold back any longer and burst into tears.

It seemed that even if I could foresee and arrange many things in this life, I couldn't change fate.

Giselle still won, and I was forced to face the end of being cast out.

Clutching my chest, I gazed out the window at the raging wind. I was not ready to give up. I refused to let it end here!

Silently, I swore to myself that I would not let Giselle succeed and take everything away again. Not this time!

I leaned back in my seat, struggling to catch my breath.

For some reason, my stomach churned uncomfortably again, even though I had nothing left to throw up.

After a long about of retched, I finally felt a bit better.

Then, the storm broke. It was late, and while the hotel entrance had been bustling with rideshare drivers earlier, it was now deserted.

Trapped in my car, I reluctantly dialed Quinn's number.

Quinn was a night owl, so obviously, he wasn't asleep. He answered quickly.

"What's up? Calling me this late?" Quinn asked.

His tone was as casual as ever as if he was never troubled by anything.

"I've been drinking and can't drive back. There's no ride around. Can you come pick me up? I'm at Jade Restaurant," I said straightforwardly.

"OK, wait for me. I'll be there in ten minutes."

Quinn seemed always ready to help without a fuss. He didn't ask any further and agreed readily.

I hung up and sat waiting in the seat. Soon, I saw his large SUV charge through the rain, headlights blazing as he pulled up fiercely beside my car.

The rain continued to pour heavily. He quickly jumped out, umbrella in hand, and came over to get me.

I opened the door, and he immediately wrapped an arm around my shoulders, swiftly helping me into the passenger seat before hopping in and shutting the door.

"What's wrong? You look so pale, and your eyes are red. Have you been crying?"

Quinn immediately noticed something was off as soon as he got into the car.

He furrowed his brows and asked with concern, remarkably perceptive despite the dim lighting.
