In the end, I had no choice but to listen to him and put on his trench coat. Then, he buttoned it for me.

He couldn't help but say, "You look quite good in my trench coat."

"What?"

Hearing his compliment, I couldn't help but laugh. Honestly, I did feel a lot warmer after putting on his coat.

There was a nice smell on his coat, not like perfume, but it smelled very good. This smell was always unique to him, making people feel inexplicably relaxed.

While we walked side by side, Quinn asked, "I knew you and Elliott went hiking two days ago. You should be happy. Why are you so depressed?"

Obviously, he had seen the photos of Flora Moutain that Elliott had posted on Twitter. I was in Louis City, so even if he couldn't see my posts, he was clever and could guess Elliott had gone to Louis City and found me.

"I just feel that I am quite useless. I have decided to get over him, but as soon as he gets close to me, I find I can't do it."

I smiled helplessly. Unknowingly, I had treated Quinn as a friend with whom I could share my thoughts.

"Did you feel like you couldn't resist him when he was nice to you? This is normal. You're a hopeless romantic," Quinn said bluntly.

I was so angry that I punched him in the chest.

"What? No!"

"This is an undeniable fact. If he treated you with the same attitude as before, you might have gotten over him a long time ago. But recently, he has been caring about you. So, you waver, right?"

Quinn was like a sober bystander. H wasn't around me and Elliott all the time, yet it seemed like he knew clearly what happened between us.

I looked at him in disbelief and said, "How come you know everything? Quinn, I doubt you installed surveillance around me!"

"Does a person like me, a master of relationships, need to monitor you? "Quinn smiled, "I could tell what you are thinking from your tweets, expressions, and body language and where you are with Elliott."

"Do you think things can work out between Elliott and me?"

I suddenly stopped and looked at the crescent moon melancholy. At that moment, I didn't know why I would ask him such a question.

Quinn was stunned for a few seconds.

Then, he asked, "Do you want the truth or a lie?"

"Of course, I want the truth!"

Quinn suddenly sighed.

Then, he said meaningfully, "Don't you think some things are destined? You will not be happy if you force it."

Quinn didn't answer my question, but the meaning of his words was extremely obvious.

I was stunned, as if someone had spoken about what I was thinking. I turned around and looked at Quinn. At that moment, I felt an urge to cry.

Acutally, I had known the answer.

I didn't need others to tell me.

An inexplicable sadness suddenly emerged, and my tears rolled down instantly.

"Why are you crying again? You are so fragile."

Quinn panicked. He quickly took out a tissue, stepped forward, and wiped the tears from my face.

I cried suddenly, putting him at a loss, and we were very close unconsciously.

However, at this moment, a familiar and calm voice came from behind us.

"You are strolling down the street late at night. You must be in a good mood."
