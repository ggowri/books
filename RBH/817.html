"Let's head inside. It's pretty chilly out here, and there might be snakes lurking around."

I said to him once again.

"Uh-huh."

He gave a soft affirmation. His gaze locked onto me, then, extinguishing the cigarette in his hand, he took a step forward.

By the time we returned to the yard, everyone had already finished their drinks.

Jane and a few colleagues were busy tidying up the table, while Marcus and Quinn were nowhere to be found.

"What about the two of them?"

I couldn't help but ask, puzzled.

"Both of them got absolutely hammered. They went into the room together, I reckon they're probably asleep by now."

Jane said with a laugh, her face etched with kindness.

"Who won then?"

And so, I burst into laughter.

"It's a draw. Both of them are evenly matched. They managed to finish this high-proof bottle of liquor."

Jane swirled the whiskey in her hand, then said with a grin.

"Is there any booze left?"

Unexpectedly, Elliott, standing behind me, suddenly asked.

"Huh? Mr. Bryant, fancy a drink? We're out of the good stuff. But there's a bottle of wine left, a gift from the local villagers. If you're up for it, I can whip up a plate of crispy potato chips for you."

Jane immediately asked.

"Sure thing."

Surprisingly, the ever-picky Elliott actually agreed.

"It's late. How about you stop drinking?"

I spun around to face him. Concern for his well-being gnawed at me, prompting an instinctive question.

"I can't sleep without a drink. Don't worry about me. You should get some rest."

Elliott murmured, then promptly took a seat by the dining table.

Rather than Quinn's affability, he seemed to exude a chilling aura. As soon as he sat down, the other colleagues immediately stood up in restraint. They hastily retreated a few steps, then each found an excuse to skedaddle.

Jane was truly considerate. Before long, she whipped up a plate of crispy potato chips and served it up to Elliott.

Elliott, cradling a glass of wine and munching on Jane's homemade potato chips, was indulging himself under the cool moonlight, in the refreshing courtyard. His tall figure appeared profoundly lonely.

I watched for a while. For reasons unknown, a subtle pang of reluctance lingered in my heart.

I moved forward, slowly taking a seat next to him.

"Ease up on the drink, Elliott."

I couldn't help but whisper a reminder.

He looked at me, and then, out of the blue, he reached out and affectionately ruffled my hair.

"You know, I suffer from insomnia. Besides alcohol, you're the only cure I've got."

When Elliott spoke, his voice was as gentle as a whisper, his appearance pitiful in the extreme.

I was somewhat moved. In that moment, the deep affection in his eyes was so intense.

I could deeply sense from his gaze, he yearned for me, he cared for me so much.

Isn't this exactly what I've always yearned for?

I found myself staring at him, our gazes intertwining effortlessly. In that moment, it felt as if a hand had gently strummed the strings of my heart...

Just then, out of the blue, I heard Quinn's voice beside me:

"Madeline, I'm parched. Fetch me some water, will you?"
