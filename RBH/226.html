The five of us hung out until midnight. In the end, the four of us were fine. However, Fiona, who had been always reasonable, were drunk and couldn't stand up.

I didn't know whether Philip or Quinn had called Alexis. Just as we were about to leave, Alexis rushed to the club.

Alexis still wore their firm's exclusive lawyer uniform and was very elegant, but he looked a little tired. He said he had just settled an international economic dispute and got off the plane an hour ago.

Seeing Fiona lying on the sofa, Alexis felt distressed and punched Philip and Quinn.

"What's wrong with you? How could you let Fiona drink so much? Did you trick her because I wasn't around?"

"We were all willing to play games tonight. Fiona was unlucky and lost. It's not our fault."

Quinn took the punch with a smile and defended us.

"Fortunately, tomorrow is the weekend. Otherwise, how could she do the surgery tomorrow?"

Alexis blamed them, then stepped forward and brushed the hair away from Fiona's face.

He asked softly, "Fiona, are you okay? I'm Alexis. I'll take you home."

Fiona had been sleeping on the sofa for a while. Probably because she had heard Alexis's voice, she opened her eyes in surprise.

"I didn't think you would return tonight."

"I happened to catch the last flight of the day. Come on. I'll carry you back."

Alexis helped her up without saying anything more. We quickly stepped forward and put Fiona on Alexis's back.

"Alexis, I'll leave Fiona to you. You have to send her home safely," I said with worry.

However, I had known Alexis for so long and knew what kind of person he was. He was an upright lawyer and not a villain who took advantage of others. Therefore, I was not worried he would do something to Fiona.

"Don't worry."

Alexis smiled at me and left the club, carrying Fiona on his back.

Then, the four of us also left one after another. Paige said she hadn't had enough fun and wanted to go to the bar. Philip readily agreed to accompany her there.

I drank a lot and wasn't as playful as Paige. I said I wanted to go home, and Quinn said he would see me off. So, the four of us parted ways.

The club was near my neighborhood and about one mile from my home.

When I walked out of the club, a cool breeze blew in, which made me sober after just finishing my drink.

"How about we walk to your house and have a midnight walk?"

Quinn looked around. Although it was midnight, it was brightly lit in a big city like Hoswich. Moreover, there was a bright moon tonight.

"Sure," I readily agreed and walked forward.

I still wore the sportswear I wore when I went hiking with Elliott.

I didn't feel cold in this outfit during the day, but at midnight, when the wind blew, I couldn't help but shiver.

At this time, Quinn suddenly came forward, took off his trench coat, and put it on me.

"What are you doing? You gave me your coat. Aren't you feeling cold just wearing short sleeves?"

I was surprised. When I found Quinn only wore a black short-sleeved shirt, I immediately wanted to return the trench coat to him.

"I work out every day, and I'm strong. Just wear it."

Quinn said nothing more to me. He not only put his coat on for me but also motioned for me to fasten the buttons.
