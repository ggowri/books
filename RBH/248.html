The restaurant was indeed very close to the law firm, and we arrived in just seven or eight minutes. His law firm was housed in a small white building, standing out in the midst of old buildings. While the exterior seemed ordinary, stepping inside, I noticed the interior was meticulously designed.

Alexis gave Fiona and me a brief tour, revealing that this building was left by his grandfather, later converted into a law firm. He hadn't altered the exterior but renovated the interior to his liking.

Occupying the entire top floor, Alexis' office was particularly spacious.

Perhaps, like Fiona, he had a strong work ethic. His office resembled a home, not only grand in layout but also featuring a coffee shop, study, and living room. Alexis mentioned that he was sometimes too busy to go home, so he worked here, which was quite convenient.

We settled in at his coffee shop, and he quickly brewed us some coffee.

Fiona glanced around before giving me a signal.

"Why don't you two stays for coffee? Madeline and I will take a look around." Fiona suggested.

"Sure, feel free to explore," Alexis said with a smile.

Elliott nodded, indicating that I should accompany Fiona.

Fiona and I had been friends for many years, so when she gave me a look, I knew exactly what she was thinking. Fiona felt that discussing business with Alexis and Elliott was boring and preferred that we chatted alone.

So, I stood up, linked arms with Fiona, and strolled around Alexis's law firm together.

As soon as we left, Fiona immediately pinched my cheek.

"Madeline, what's going on between you and Elliott? I feel like your relationship has been strange lately."

After all, when I was injured some time ago, Fiona had arranged for my transfer to another hospital. She knew I was still upset with Elliott, so she found our current dynamic peculiar.

I smiled and whispered, "I don't know what's going on either. Fiona, what would you think if I told you I want to have a baby with him?"

"Are you out of your mind?" Fiona's voice immediately rose, but then she quickly lowered it. "What's going on? Weren't you adamant about getting a divorce not long ago? Why bring up having a baby now? Do you realize the lifelong commitment that having a child entails? Are you sure?"

"I don't know how to explain it to you, Fiona," I said with a wry smile.

After all, only Quinn knew the complexities between me and Giselle, and I wasn't sure how to explain it to Fiona.

Fiona frowned, "Do you have some ulterior motive? Is it because of a family alliance?"

"It's not just that. Fiona, let's talk about it another time. Since I ran into you today, could you give me some advice on preparing for a pregnancy?"

I changed the subject directly.

"It seems like you're really determined, but Madeline, you must think this through. Once you're pregnant, there's no turning back. If you're serious about having a baby, I can prescribe some medication for you. If you've been trying for a while without success, we can consider more targeted treatments."
