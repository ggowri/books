"Mom, let's hold off on suing the Bryant family for now. Dad just woke up, and we need to focus on his recovery first. I've got it under control," I reassured her, trying to keep things calm.

But Mom was fired up. "I was hopeful when I saw Elliott had a crush on you. But now, this stunt? With his parents backing him? Unbelievable! I'm telling the word about the real Bryants!"

Mom's anger was rare, and it showed how serious this situation was.

"Mom, let's focus on Dad for now. I was thinking of transferring him back to Fiona's place. What do you think?" I whispered as I patted my mother's shoulder gently.

At that moment, I just wanted to get far away from the Bryant family and all the drama.

Seeing the people of Laura, Jasper, Giselle, and Nicole was the last thing I wanted.

"Sure thing. Let's hit up Fiona and check if they've got an available bed. If they do, we'll arrange for himto be picked up ASAP," Mom promptly agreed.

"Okay."

As I was about to dial Fiona's number, I heard footsteps echoing down the corridor.

I glanced up and saw Elliott strolling down the corridor all serious-like.

He was alone, no one with him.

When Elliott spotted us in the hallway, he strolled over and asked, "Mrs. Mayers, Madeline, how's Mr. Mayers doing?"

My mom, usually friendly with him, wasn't having it this time.

She snorted, "Elliott, why the sudden politeness? Spare me."

Elliott shot her a look, sincere as ever.

"Mr. Mayers, today's mess is on me. I'll make sure nobody disturbs him again. I promise."

I kept quiet while my mom gave Elliott a cold reply, "No need for niceties. We've troubled you enough. We'll move Austin back to Fiona's to avoid causing any more fuss here."

Elliott seemed taken aback by the idea of us moving my dad.

He put a hand on my shoulder, saying, "Madeline, your dad's up now. The treatment's working. You sure about going back to Fiona's?"

His words mirrored my own doubts.

My dad hadn't shown much improvement for a while, but now he was awake.

Besides Giselle and her crew's little blunder, maybe it was also thanks to those doctor's plan.

Dad had just woken up, and his condition was still unstable. Staying put seemed wise, but I couldn't stand being pushed around here.

I gently shrugged off Elliott's hand and kept my cool, staying silent.

Elliott picked up on my vibe and grabbed my hand, leading me out. I was caught off guard.

"What are you doing?" I yelled.

"Let's have dinner and chat," he said.

Elliott had a tight hold on my wrist, refusing to let go despite my efforts to break free.

"Elliott, there's not much to talk about!" I protested, growing louder.

He shot me a look, then glanced at my mom.

"Some things are better said one-on-one."
