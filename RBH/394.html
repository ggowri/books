"Hey, Elliott, you're finally here!"

Giselle's voice perked up at the sound of Elliott's arrival. She dashed over to him, all sweetness and light.

I glanced up to see Elliott standing at the door, his face cool and collected as always.

But seeing him didn't exactly warm my heart, it actually made my stomach churn.

Elliott looked around the room, a bit surprised, before his eyes landed on me.

He made his way over and, right in front of everyone, reached out to give my shoulder a supportive squeeze, concern written all over his face.

"You okay? Sorry I'm late," he said.

But his words didn't do much for me. They just fueled my sarcasm.

I shrugged off his hand and shot back, "Well, you're not too late to witness your people causing chaos here."

I lifted my head, locking eyes with him with a cold stare.

His once-familiar face now seemed strangely unfamiliar to me.

My gut told me there was more to his connection with Giselle than met the eye.

That feeling gave me the courage to guard my heart and steer clear of any romantic illusions.

I wouldn't be dumb enough to put everything on the line for love, especially not with Elliott. It was time to be smart and focus on the future of the Glory Group.

No more foolish mistakes for me.

Elliott noticed my lack of reaction and seemed like he wanted to say something, but he turned to Giselle instead.

Giselle put on a whole show, rubbing her belly like she was trying to remind Elliott of their supposed baby.

She gave him this pleading look, biting her lip and saying with a fake tearful voice, "Elliott, please... don't be mad at me. I didn't mean for any of this to happen. I just came to visit Madeline's dad, but her mom freaked out when she saw me. It's all just a big misunderstanding, I swear."

It was like she did a complete 180 from her earlier proud self, now playing the innocent card.

I couldn't help but shake my head and chuckle. "Giselle, nice try, but let's not forget about the camera. Maybe it's time for a little reality check, huh? Let's see the CCTV footage and give Elliott the full picture."

As soon as I dropped that bomb, Giselle went pale as a ghost. Her eyes darted around nervously.

She was clearly caught off guard by the mention of CCTV camera.

Elliott caught on fast. He took out his phone and called up the director of Bryant Hospital, demanding they send over the footage from our room.

The director wasted no time, and in a flash, the footage was in Elliott's hands.

Elliott quickly played the video for everyone to see. And when he witnessed Giselle's earlier behavior, his expression darkened.

I could tell he was finally seeing Giselle's true colors.
