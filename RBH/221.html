"Elliott, now that Madeline objects, just forget it. It doesn't matter. My brother and I can earn money slowly. I believe that one day, we can buy a house on our own."

I didn't know when Giselle came down from upstairs. She changed her clothes and walked over with a faint smell of perfume on her body.

And the scent was the same as that in Elliott's car.

I turned my head to look at her, and my eyes fell on her neck. I didn't expect that she suddenly put on a pearl necklace.

Although the necklace was not as eye-catching as the one Laura had sent to me, it made me feel very familiar.

I suddenly remembered that there was a banquet where Laura once wore this pearl necklace. If I remember correctly, it was Elliott's gift for Laura after he took a trip abroad.

It seemed that Laura had given Giselle this pearl necklace as a gift.

Now, Giselle was wearing it on purpose just to show off in front of me.

It turned out that she didn't have a stomachache. She just wanted to go upstairs and show off to me.

"Why did you suddenly change into this dress? This is the birthday gift Elliott prepared for you, but your birthday hasn't come yet."

Nicole saw Giselle and couldn't help but exclaim.

I was shocked. I stood up and took a look at Giselle.

Only then did I realize that the dress she was wearing was just released this year by a famous brand. It was impossible for Giselle to afford it.

I wondered if it was the birthday present Elliott had given to her.

Elliott had never given me any birthday gifts, and he didn't even remember when my birthday was.

I felt furious and couldn't suppress my emotions anymore.

I glanced at Elliott and then watched Giselle and Nicole. I suddenly felt cold and dizzy. The house was too suffocating, and I couldn't stand steadily.

I turned around and ran out.

"Madeline, what's wrong with you?"

Elliott quickly caught up with me and yelled behind me.

I kept running forward, with tears flowing down my face.

I ran to the main road. When I was about to cross the road, Elliott hugged me from behind.

His tone was urgent, and he was panting heavily. "What are you doing? Are you running away again?"

If I didn't run away, should I have stayed there and let my love rival see me lose my composure?

If I didn't leave, did I have to let Elliott hurt me again and again in such an unintentional way?

I smiled bitterly. I didn't want to cry, but my tears still flowed down.

I took a deep breath and tried my best to adjust my breathing. At this moment, Elliott turned my body and made me face him.

Seeing my tears, he felt sorry for me and stretched out his hand to wipe them from my face.

He said softly, "I just treat her like a younger sister and pity her family. Madeline, don't be like this."
