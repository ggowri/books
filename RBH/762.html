When this sound reached my ears, it struck me as odd.

I instinctively thought of what Quinn had mentioned, that woman who was always by Elliott's side.

"Oh, could you let Barry know I'm on my way? He knows who I am. Thanks."

There's a slight curiosity stirring within me.

"Sure thing, Ms. Mayers, right?"

To my surprise, she knew who I was. And when she asked if I was Ms. Mayers, her voice noticeably heightened.

"Indeed, who might you be?"

My curiosity grew even more.

"See you in a bit, friend of Mr. Bryant."

After uttering those words, the woman promptly hung up the phone.

I was a bit taken aback, but didn't dwell on it. Upon reaching Bryant Hospital, I walked straight in.

Still in the process of recovery, I deliberately donned a long black coat, adding a houndstooth scarf around my neck for good measure. I topped it off with a beret and slipped on a pair of sunglasses. With a determined stride, I made my way towards Elliott's private hospital room.

The moment I stepped out of the elevator, a tall woman was striding in my direction.

However, she stepped into the elevator on the other side. Before I could get a good look at her, she had already disappeared inside.

The woman was clad in a silver trench coat, her bare feet slipped into high heels. Beneath the coat, I caught a glimpse of what seemed to be a deep green blouse. Her hair was styled into short, soft curls. Tall and slender, she moved with an air of authority that compelled passersby to steal a second glance.

Even between the same sex, there are times when attraction sparks.

I instinctively halted, casting a glance over my shoulder. To my surprise, I was immediately taken aback.

Even though the elevator doors closed swiftly, I could still clearly see the woman inside. Astonishingly, her face bore a striking resemblance to mine!

Could this woman possibly be the same one I encountered at that vacation hotel in Norch City last time?

If that's the case, why would she suddenly show up here?

I was left in a state of utter bewilderment, yearning to dig deeper. But the woman had already taken the elevator down, leaving me in the dust. And the worst part? I had no clue who she was.

"Mrs. Bryant, you've finally arrived. I've been waiting for your call for days."

I was still in a daze when Barry's voice echoed from behind me.

Without bothering to greet Barry, I immediately pointed towards the elevator and asked him:

"Barry, who was that woman who just stepped into the elevator?"

Barry paused for a moment, pushing up the glasses perched on the bridge of his nose:

"Mrs. Bryant, which woman?"

I quickly scanned the floor, my previous visits to Bryant Hospital had made me well aware that this level was off-limits to the public, reserved exclusively for the treatment of the Bryant family.

So, Barry must know who this woman is. And when he answered my question, he couldn't meet my gaze. This clearly indicates that he's trying to hide something from me.

I fixed my gaze on Barry, flashing him a cryptic smile, and took a couple of steps towards him.

Once again, I had Barry spooked like a deer caught in headlights. He hastily retreated two steps, nervously adjusting the glasses perched on his nose.

"Barry, you know how I am. If you don't spill it soon, I'm outta here—"

I narrowed my eyes, leaning in close to Barry, deliberately putting on a stern face to spook him.
