A noble lady directly exposed Laura's lie and made Laura's red face instantly turn pale.

I had to seize this perfect opportunity.

I acted like I had heard a great secret.

I held Laura's hand and asked in surprise, "Laura, you just introduced Giselle to them as Elliott's wife? How is this going? Don't tell me the child in her belly is Elliott's. Oh my gosh!"

I put my hands on my chest as if I was shocked.

Laura was dragged by me and couldn't leave. Her face turned even paler.

She could only deny it reluctantly, "No, they may have heard wrongly."

I hadn't divorced Elliott yet, so Laura didn't dare to tell the truth.

Hearing this, I suddenly felt that the grievances I had been bearing were finally vented.

"Really? What about her kid? Whose child is she pregnant with?"

The lady asked again.

I glanced at the lady subconsciously. She was wearing a white dress, a pearl necklace, and a Cartier watch.

I realized that I didn't know her, but I felt like she was a very kind and elegant lady for some reason, and she was also a little familiar to me.

She seemed to be deliberately speaking for me, which made me feel good about her. I met her eyes and felt that she was very friendly to me.

Being questioned, Laura was no longer arrogant. She stood listlessly among the crowd without saying a word. It was obvious that she hadn't found a chance to get out of her predicament.

Giselle was holding her belly, with tears filled in her eyes, because Laura denied her identity. I believe she must feel aggrieved now.

I noticed she was clenching her hands tightly, and tears falling from her face. Just when I thought she would do nothing, she suddenly choked and said, "Laura, I have been pregnant for so long, don't you dare to tell everyone the truth?"

As soon as Giselle said this, Laura shivered. Laura immediately raised her head and glanced sharply at Giselle.

Obviously, she was a very vain person, so of course she did not want Giselle to expose the scandal of the Bryant family here today.

After all, this was a grand gathering of the upper class in Hoswich. Today there were not only entrepreneurs in Hoswich who attended the party but their wives...

Compared to Laura who was aggrieved at this moment, I felt happy to watch this happen.

I pretended to be extremely surprised again and shouted, "Giselle, what are you talking about? What truth?"

As if she could not bear it anymore, Giselle suddenly became emotional.

She cried and shouted, "Madeline, it's enough! Stop pretending! You know everything! "
