Jimmy, come with me for a round of inspection, just in case, let's double-check."

Jimmy looked embarrassed.

"Take me there immediately, I won't feel at ease if you don't see me."

I didn't waste any more time with Jimmy and asked him to take me to the top of the greenhouse.

The greenhouses in this venue all adopt a grid structure. First, pillars are used to support the grid, and then panels are laid on top of the grid. This kind of structure is strong enough in the short term, and most convention centers also use this structure.

However, when we went up to the top to take a look, I discovered that in the corner of the hall, someone had piled up a heap of pumice stones on the roof.

When I saw that pile of pumice, I immediately understood where the danger of collapse lay.

This kind of pumice has many small holes on its surface, making it extremely absorbent. When it encounters water, it becomes heavier. And this heavy rain has been going on for so long, these pumice stones have already absorbed a lot of water, and their weight must be several times heavier than before. If they are not moved in time, once the weight exceeds the load-bearing capacity that the pillars and framework can support, it will inevitably cause the collapse of the pavilion.

Once the large-scale exhibition starts, there will definitely be countless people flooding in. If the pavilion collapses, the consequences will undoubtedly be devastating. Even a massive corporation like Bryant Group may not be able to bear such consequences.

No wonder Elliott was so good to Giselle in the previous life and even gave her such a nice apartment.

Considering the serious consequences, Elliott must have been very grateful to Giselle in his heart, right?

However, in this lifetime, I will not give Giselle such an opportunity again.

"Jimmy, is that piled up there perlite?"

I pointed at the pile of pumice stones and deliberately asked.

Jimmy nodded quickly, until now he hadn't realized the problem. He said, "Yes, they had nowhere to put them, so they temporarily piled them here and will take them away once the rain stops."
