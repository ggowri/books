I was extremely nervous, constantly shifting my gaze between Elliott and Giselle's faces.

At this moment, I felt sad and pathetic, silently waiting for the annual drama that was about to unfold.

I really had mixed feelings, and for a moment, I couldn't find the words to describe what kind of mood I was in.

At this moment, Elliott, prompted by Giselle's call, turned around hesitantly, his gaze scanning through the crowd, finally fixing on the delicate and slender figure of Giselle.

Giselle was Elliott's destiny. His gaze pierced through the crowd, and when it landed on Giselle, there was a hint of "a glance lasts forever" in his eyes.

They stared at each other from a distance like this, their gazes deeply captivated by each other. Giselle's eyes were sparkling, filled with the recklessness and innocence of someone who had just entered society.

In Elliott's eyes, besides confusion, there was clearly a faint and imperceptible glimmer.

"Yes?"

He responded softly, his voice deep like a cello, magnetic and pleasant to the ear, saying a word worthy of many words.

"If no one can be found as a substitute, I am willing to perform on stage. I have learned to play the piano, and I can play... fairly well."

Giselle was indeed brave. As a recent graduate intern, she volunteered herself in front of so many people.

However, she clearly did not consult with anyone before volunteering herself, which startled her immediate supervisor Amy. Amy quickly grabbed her wrist.

"Giselle, don't be silly. She is a heavyweight pianist in the country. How could you compare with her?"

Giselle, however, was full of confidence. Her eyes did not move away from Elliott when they intertwined. She exclaimed loudly.

"Although I couldn't compare to Carli, I had also been practicing piano for many years. With the situation being so urgent now, I am willing to give it a try."

Elliott had also been paying attention to this girl. I clearly saw that when Giselle said these words, there were clear traces of approval in his eyes.

He seemed to want to say something. In the case of the last life, he must have agreed to Giselle's request to perform, thus giving the girl a chance to make a splash.

But in this life, I had been secretly preparing for a long time. Just as he was about to speak, I interrupted him directly and said.

"I'll do the piano performance later. When I used to study piano before, I had received guidance from Carli for a period of time. She can be counted as my teacher. Since she can't attend, I'll perform in her place."

As soon as I said these words, all the backstage staff turned their gaze towards me.

And Elliott's gaze, too, shifted from Giselle to me in an instant.

He obviously didn't know that I could still play the piano, and his eyes were filled with doubt.

"Are you sure?"

I nodded and said, "Yes, there shouldn't be any problem."

"Absolutely no problem. Madeline was a piano prodigy when she was young and had previously won awards internationally. When she takes the stage, there will be no doubt about her performance!"

Just then, my best friend Paige appeared at the entrance of the backstage and she exclaimed loudly.

Paige's testimony reassured Elliott, and at this moment, the time for the performance was also approaching. Elliott immediately grabbed my hand and said in a deep voice.

"Alright, that's up to you."

I smiled slightly and said, "Don't worry. Let the host set the stage first."

Elliott made a decision, and everyone quickly went about their tasks, bustling around.

I took a deep breath, adjusted my breathing, stood behind the scenes, and prepared for the upcoming performance.
