I strolled through the garden, inhaling the floral scent, simultaneously absorbing the vibrant energy around me. My mood instantly brightened.

A flood of memories surged through my mind.

Suddenly, I'm reminded of that Mid-Autumn Festival years ago. Back then, Elliott and I were lying side by side right here, moon-gazing... Time really does fly. In the blink of an eye, we've been through so much together.

"This was all personally designed and maintained by Mr. Bryant in his spare time. He said you'd come back one day. I didn't expect, you really did."

Out of nowhere, Sean suddenly materialized behind me.

Hearing that familiar voice, I spun around in surprise. There stood Sean, not far away, clad in a slate-grey uniform. His face still wore that gentle smile, and in his hand, he held a pair of floral shears.

It seems like he makes it a point to tend to these plants every morning at the crack of dawn.

I thought these old friends would only remain in my memories. I believed we'd never have the chance to meet again.

And now, as Sean stands before me, I can't shake the feeling that nothing has changed at all. I find myself laughing, almost involuntarily:

"Sean, long time no see, you still look as robust as ever."

"Where have you been? Ever since you left, my hair has turned significantly grayer."

Sean just chuckled and shook his head. It was only then that I noticed his hair had indeed turned white. Suddenly, I felt a lump in my throat.

It's been quite a few years since I tied the knot with Elliott.

When I divorced Elliott, not a single member of the Bryant family spared a thought for the youth I'd wasted or the sacrifices I'd made for him. Only Sean genuinely cared about me.

Thinking back, it's truly a poignant and sigh-inducing memory.

"You've worked hard, Sean."

For a moment, I was at a loss for words. Looking at this garden, I couldn't help but utter a word of thanks.

"When Mr. Bryant has a spare moment, he often comes here to sit, sometimes even tending to the flowers himself, watering them and such. I can tell, you know, that Mr. Bryant misses you every day. Come back. You're both still young. Things will get better with time, they always do."

Sean stepped forward, speaking to me with a weighty seriousness.

I could see the sincerity in his earnest eyes. But we've been through so much together. How could these issues possibly be resolved just by me returning home?

The wound deep within my heart still throbs subtly, it's not healing as quickly as I'd hoped.

There are certain things I just don't want to bring up anymore. But that doesn't mean they're all in the past.

It's tough to piece a shattered mirror back together.

I forced a bitter smile. I looked at the elderly butler as if he were my own kin. My heart ached with a reluctance to part, yet I found myself at a loss for words. So, I chose silence.

"She had wanted to move in before, but Mr. Bryant chased her away. He said, this is your home with him. There won't be another mistress in this house."

It seemed as if Sean could read my thoughts. He let out a soft sigh, gave my shoulder a reassuring pat, and added.

Of course, I knew who he was referring to when he said "she"...
