The next day.

As I stirred from the haze of half-sleep, I found Elliott lying next to me, propped up on one elbow. He was gazing at me with a gentle intensity.

As he saw me open my eyes, he affectionately pinched my cheek with his hand. He gave me a slight smile:

"Do you recall what transpired last night?"

My eyes blinked involuntarily. Memories of last night flooded over me like a tidal wave. A blush instantly spread across my entire face.

I jolted upright from my bed in a rush. The moment I got up, my limbs still felt sore and heavy, but at least I could move:

"How did I end up here? How did I end up with you?"

"This is our home, where else should you be? What, you'd rather be at someone else's place?"

Elliott hoisted himself up beside me. He reached out, tucking my disheveled strands of hair behind my ear. His voice carried a hint of teasing.

"I..."

I initially wanted to tell him that we've been divorced for a while now, that this place is no longer my home. But then I remembered he's suffering from amnesia. So, I held my tongue and didn't utter those words.

"When I'm not by your side, don't drink so much. The world is a complex place. You never know what others might be up to."

Elliott sidled up to me. He kneaded my shoulders, then whispered gently into my ear.

"Uh-huh."

About last night, I've got a ton of questions myself. So, I didn't say much.

I was wide awake now. Elliott's affectionate gesture left me feeling a bit out of place. So, I gently pushed him away, silently pulled back the covers, and got out of bed.

The day had already broken.

Last night, the curtains were left undrawn, with only a sheer white veil covering the entire window. The morning light filtered softly through, casting a gentle glow. Subconsciously, I took in the familiar surroundings of the long-forgotten bridal suite.

Then, to my surprise, I found that a photo of Elliott and me had been deliberately enlarged and hung on the wall at some point.

Not only that, my old dressing table remains untouched. On it, my solo photo frame still stands. Even the bedding is the same four-piece set I personally picked out back in the day.

Seeing these familiar objects stirred something in my heart.

Should I call him devoted or indifferent? Sometimes, it's really hard to pin a man down.

A whirlwind of emotions swept over me. At that moment, Elliott stirred from his slumber. He wrapped his arms around my waist from behind, planting a gentle kiss on my cheek.

"I'm heading downstairs to whip up some breakfast for you. You can catch a few more winks, or, head up to the rooftop terrace for some fresh air. The flowers up there are in full bloom, quite a sight to behold."

The Rooftop Terrace?

There, I used to nurture a myriad of plants and flowers. Before the divorce, I specifically instructed Sean to take good care of them.

Hearing Elliott's words, a wave of nostalgia washed over me. So, after freshening up, I made a point to visit the rooftop.

When I pushed open the small door to the rooftop, the sight that greeted me was utterly astonishing.

I discovered that each plant I used to nurture was not only meticulously cared for, but the landscape on the terrace had been redesigned, becoming more aesthetically pleasing than before. Every corner here exudes a sense of freshness and refinement, akin to a SkyPark, uplifting my mood extraordinarily.

I've mentioned before, I've had this dream of planting grapes and wisteria here.

Now, Elliott had a wooden trellis specially made. Grapevines and wisteria flowers twined around the overhead trellis, hanging down in long tendrils. When the wind blew, the place seemed exceptionally lush and verdant.

I used to have a fondness for orchids. Now, I've set up a special rack dedicated to them. This rack is adorned with a variety of orchids, some of which have already bloomed, emitting an intoxicatingly subtle fragrance.

The entire garden was pristine, not a speck of dust in sight. From any angle, this patch of greenery seemed like it was plucked straight out of a fairy tale.
