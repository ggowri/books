Who could be knocking on my door this late?

Confused, I peeked through the peephole and saw Quinn standing outside with an umbrella.

Why is he here at this hour?

I froze, glancing instinctively at Elliott inside, unsure what to do.

If I let Quinn in, he might misunderstand seeing Elliott here.

But if I didn't let him in, I couldn't explain myself either.

I hesitated at the door, and my mom came out, having heard the doorbell.

Seeing my expression, she immediately knew who was outside and gave me a stern look.

"Look at the mess you've made!"

After scolding me, she opened the door.

Outside, Quinn's gentle voice floated in like a spring breeze:

"Auntie, it's raining and thundering. I know Madeline is scared of thunder, so I came to check on her. Is everyone okay?"

Quinn walked in with a big box of snacks, smiling.

"We're fine, but Elliott is here too. Just so you know, we didn't want to let him in, but the rain was too heavy. You young people sort it out yourselves."

My mom quickly distanced herself from the situation, explaining right away.

Quinn spotted Elliott at the dining table, enjoying his meal. His smile froze.

But he soon understood the situation.

"Auntie, can the three of us talk?"

Quinn's tone was calm and restrained, not impulsive.

"Sure, but I don't want any arguments here. Also, Quinn, regardless of what Madeline thinks, I don't support her getting back with Elliott."

My mom patted Quinn's shoulder, seemingly encouraging him.

Hearing my mom, I felt a bit helpless.

Outside, the rain continued, showing signs of a comeback.

Quinn brought my favorite lobster, which I rarely eat. As he opened the box, the familiar spicy aroma filled the room.

He took out disposable gloves, placing them in front of Elliott and me, then sat beside me, across from Elliott.

Elliott's eyes tensed up. He looked at Quinn, his lips moving:

"Quinn, there are plenty of seats. Why sit next to Madeline?"

His voice carried a hint of displeasure, though he didn't fully express it.

Quinn smiled slightly, put on the gloves, and started peeling the lobster. He skillfully placed the meat into my bowl.

His actions were smooth and natural.

This brought back memories of past years when Quinn would always sit beside me during lobster season, peeling the shells and placing the meat in my bowl.

He was always a gentleman, caring for me and others at the table.

Over time, I got used to it, thinking peeling shrimp was a man's job.

At that moment, seeing Quinn place the lobster in my bowl in front of Elliott, I realized only Quinn had ever treated me with such care.

Not even my dad, who loves me dearly, had done this for me.

Quinn didn't answer Elliott's question but showed him through his actions.

The night was long, and their eyes burned with hidden anger. Quinn said calmly:

"If you're here tonight, it means your memory is either fully back or you know everything."

"I know everything," Elliott replied bluntly.

"Good, let's talk. Madeline has decided to be with me. You and she were in love, but that's the past. I hope you understand."

Quinn's voice was deep, his hands still peeling the lobster, but his words were firm.

Elliott's face fell. He grabbed Quinn's hand holding the lobster, his voice turning cold:

"Wait, Madeline hasn't decided who to be with. At least, she hasn't told me directly."

"Really?" Quinn asked calmly, then turned to me, eyes full of certainty. "Madeline, will you tell him what you told me that day?"

I froze.

I wasn't mentally prepared for Quinn to ask me to clarify in front of Elliott.

But I had promised Quinn before. I said once Elliott's memory returned, I would be with Quinn.

Looking at the bowl filled with shrimp, I thought of all the subtle kindness Quinn had shown me over the years. My heart felt heavy.

In my past, I owed Elliott nothing, but I owed Quinn so much.

On a stormy night, he remembered my fear of thunder, knew I loved lobster, and peeled all the shrimp for me without tasting any himself. He always put me first. If I kept hurting him, I'd be going too far.

As for Elliott...

I admit, that his recent actions and words moved me. But I once swore I'd never be with Elliott again.

I should follow my heart. Wavering any longer would be unfair to Quinn.

Thinking of this, my resolve strengthened.

I nodded and softly said, "I remember saying that once Elliott's memory returned and my life was back to normal, we would be together."

"Madeline!"

Elliott gasped, standing up from his chair, and looking at me in disbelief, his eyes darkening.

I took a deep breath, mustered my courage, and looked him in the eye, saying calmly:

"Elliott, now that you know everything, I won't fear hurting you. We're even. For the rest of my life, I don't want to be entangled with you. I just want peace. I hope you understand."
