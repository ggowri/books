I raised my head and looked at Elliott with tears in my eyes. He still looked handsome against the neon lights on the street.

I asked, "If I don't allow you to get too close to her, can you stay away from her for me?"

In the past, I would never have asked such a question.

But I had to admit that, during this short period of time, the change in his attitude towards me gave me the illusion that he might start to care about me.

I wanted to get his preference, which I had never gotten in either my last life or this life.

However, Elliott's response dashed my hopes.

He said in a deep voice, "Madeline, can you please stop fooling around?"

It turned out that, in his eyes, my humble begging was just nonsense.

In front of me, he deliberately pretended to be indifferent to Giselle, but she must be very important to him, right?

Otherwise, how could he plan to give her a house, buy her a birthday gift, and care so much about her work and life?

I bit my lip but didn't feel the pain, as I was heartbroken.

I responded, "I don't want you to buy her a house or get close to her. Does this count as nonsense? Elliott, you have been so gentle to me these days, are you just pretending?"

I couldn't help but question him.

I knew that indifference was the best attitude towards a love rival, but I failed to be indifferent. I admitted that I started to care again.

Elliott frowned and looked at me in disappointment.

"Why do you think like that? You two are different. Madeline, you don't need to mind her existence."

I didn't need to mind her existence?

Giselle was his dream lover who could not be slandered by anyone else, so how could I not mind?

Nothing had happened yet, but he had already been so protective of her.

It was hard to imagine how much he would love her in the near future, but what about me?

I couldn't help but think about it. I suddenly felt that there seemed to be no need to say anything more.

I pushed his hand away, took a few steps back with a sneer, and wiped my tears. Everything that had happened tonight made me feel hopeless again.

I secretly laughed at myself.

Why did I go to Giselle's house with Elliott tonight, just to insult myself?

I initially thought the love between Elliott and I would stimulate Giselle and make her crazy, but I didn't expect that the person who was stimulated and finally went crazy was myself.

I had overestimated my position in Elliott's heart.

I also underestimated Giselle's position.

Seeing me lose my composure, she must be smiling proudly at this moment.

"I don't mind. How can I mind? I'm just a woman you've married as a decoration. I think too highly of myself. It's okay. You can go back and buy whatever you want for your dream lover. Buy her a house or clothes. Whatever it is," I sneered, pretending not to care about everything.

"What dream lover? Do you know what you are talking about, Madeline?"

He frowned and tried to step forward to hold my hand, but I bounced away immediately.

"Don't come over, Elliott! I don't need your false sympathy, nor your false love. From today on, stay away from me and don't come close to me, okay?"

I was really afraid.

I couldn't stand to be hurt by him again and again, and I couldn't bear to have my hopes dashed again and again.

"What's wrong with you?" Elliott asked helplessly. "Didn't we have a good relationship two days ago? Why are you like this again?"
