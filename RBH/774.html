On the phone, I relayed to him the recent happenings with Elliott.

After hearing this, Marcus fell into a thoughtful silence for a moment, then slowly said:

"If he's merely been tampered with in a minor way, that's easy to fix. I have a method to remove it. But if he's been hexed, that's a different story. However, in all my years, I've only heard rumors of such witchcraft, never seen it in real life. I'll make a trip and see if my method works."

"Alright, I'll be waiting for you."

I hung up the phone, then relayed his words to Jack.

Jack was out of options. After hearing about Marcus's background, he agreed to let Marcus help Elliott out.

After hashing it out with Jack, I made my exit from his office.

As for that woman who bears a striking resemblance to me, I have no intention of meeting her face to face. For reasons unknown, I instinctively feel a bit uneasy about her.

Unexpectedly, as I stepped out of the elevator, there she was, hands in her pockets, standing there. She was looking in my direction, a subtle and enigmatic expression on her face. It was clear as day, she was waiting for me.

I was utterly flabbergasted.

To be honest, standing face-to-face with someone who looks eerily similar to you, yet shares no blood or bond, is a truly bizarre experience.

Given that the other party had ill intentions, I had nothing to fear. I stepped forward, meeting her gaze head-on. I didn't speak, sensing that she seemed to have something to say to me.

"Hello, Ms. Mayers."

"Hello," I greeted with a slight nod.

"Since you've decided to stay and take care of Mr. Bryant, I'll be heading back to Norch City. But, I reckon we'll cross paths again."

She cocked her head slightly, giving me a sidelong glance, her voice cool and detached.

"Really? Why do you say that?"

I could tell there was more to her words than she let on. However, her calm departure took me by surprise.

"Because I believe, we are destined to cross paths."

She winked at me. There was an indescribable strangeness in her eyes.

"In what way are we connected? I don't quite understand."

I looked at her, asking coldly.

She flashed a smile, suddenly stepping up to my side, whispering softly:

"You'll understand in time, there's a long road ahead."

Then she swiftly stepped into the elevator, slamming the doors shut with urgency.

I spun around, my gaze landing in her direction. As the elevator doors were about to close, I distinctly felt her smile freeze on her face. The look she shot my way was laced with an unnameable resentment.

She gave me a sense of familiarity, as if we've known each other for ages. Yet, this was, in fact, our very first formal encounter.

I was utterly baffled, trying to figure out what on earth this woman wanted. Just then, Barry chimed in from behind me:

"I just spoke with Ms. Fox, thanking her for taking care of Elliott recently. She mentioned that she has to rush back to Norch City for some business."

"Barry, how long has Mr. Bryant known her?"

I inquired.

"It wasn't long ago. They met at the Entrepreneurs Summit in Norch City. She bore such a striking resemblance to you that Mr. Bryant asked for her contact information."

Barry spilled the beans.

I nodded, a strange feeling growing stronger within me. I had a vague sense that this woman was going to stir up some kind of storm in my life. But for now, she was like a fox lurking in the shadows, secretly watching me.

Her motives were unclear, her intentions were vague, and her actions were even more enigmatic.

So, I'm powerless. All I can do is remind myself to stay on guard.
