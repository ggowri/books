It wasn't until the evening, when I saw Elliott carrying Giselle and rushing onto the ambulance, that I suddenly woke up and realized I had unwittingly fallen into his trap, indulging in the dream he had woven.

"I can refrain from touching you, but I can't promise not to find you."

Elliott looked at me, lowering his voice.

"The Glory Group has a lot of things to handle, and my dad is eagerly waiting for me to take care of things. You have your own matters and people to take care of too."

I suppressed my inner emotions as much as possible, trying to remain rational.

I didn't want to become an unreasonable lunatic again, driven crazy by a man entangled in my life for two lifetimes. It wasn't worth it.

"Alright, you wait for me to investigate everything. But until I figure things out, you are not allowed to overthink or meddle in other things. And absolutely no mention of divorce, understood?"

Elliott laid out another condition. Obviously, he knew he was in the wrong, but he didn't want to give up on me easily.

After all, despite his repeated claims that there was nothing between him and Giselle, the child was a fact, and that photo was also a fact.

I lowered my head, pondering for a while before decisively saying, "Sorry, I can't agree to that."

It wasn't that I couldn't agree, but suddenly I felt that I didn't want to be controlled and constrained by Elliott anymore.

Why should Elliott get involved in such things and still demand this from me? It didn't make sense. I also didn't want to become his accessory, letting him dictate everything.

Elliott didn't expect my refusal. He wanted to say something more, but at that moment, his phone rang.

He took out his phone, glanced at it with a furrowed brow, but still answered it.

"Elliott, where did you go? Hurry back to the hospital! Giselle said she can't breathe properly now and wants to see you right away."

Once again, Laura's high-pitched voice came through the phone.

It seemed like Laura and Giselle had built a seamless wall between me and Elliott, desperately trying to keep us apart.

Elliott didn't say anything and hung up the phone. He looked at me, the guilt in his eyes sinking my heart to the bottom.

"Go, after all, she's still carrying your child."

I pretended to smile indifferently, but my heart ached as if stabbed by sharp knives.

Shouldn't the one really struggling to breathe be me?

"I'll go check, in case something happens, I'm afraid Grandpa..."

Elliott had obviously made up his mind. His explanation felt forced to me.

But I didn't say much. I nodded, gesturing for him to get out of the car.

He looked deeply into my eyes, instinctively cupping my face. But as he leaned in again to kiss me, I quickly avoided it.

With my dodge, he understood my intentions.

Elliott's lips moved as if to say something. But in the end, he said nothing, just jumped out of the car, braving the heavy rain, and quickly walked into Bryant Hospital.

His tall and sturdy figure gradually disappeared before me once again. This time, Elliott was heading towards another woman.

Thinking about this, my heart felt like it was being twisted and spasmed, causing my stomach to ache terribly.

I couldn't hold it back, quickly opened the car door, and vomited violently for a long time...
