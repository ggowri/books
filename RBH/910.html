"Austin, Lily, I'm sorry. I came here today to apologize."

Elliott stood before my parents, soaked and looking quite messy.

Yet, he stood straight as if he had all the courage and responsibility.

"Don't call us like that. We can't take it. Elliott, Madeline starts a new life now. You need to stop harassing her or us anymore."

My mom sat on the sofa, still stern, but I noticed her eyes lingering on the hem of Elliott's wet clothes.

He had stood in the rain for too long; water kept dripping from his clothes. My mom, always soft-hearted, must have felt uneasy seeing this.

But she didn't show it.

"I know my family and I have hurt Madeline badly in the past, causing much pain to her and you. I've talked to my dad, and he agrees that our family should make it up.

If you're willing, we can formally propose again and meet all your demands. We are even willing to hold another wedding. This is our sincere offer to Madeline and to you. I hope you can give me another chance, for the sake of our past."

Elliott's voice was sincere and firm, carrying a deep, magnetic tone.

Water still dripped from him, and his face grew paler, but he stood unwavering.

My dad's eyelid twitched, and he automatically glanced at my mom.

My mom was also surprised by Elliott's sincerity. For a moment, they exchanged looks, both showing a brief hesitation in their eyes.

But it was only for a moment.

My dad spoke in a deep voice:

"Let bygones be bygones, and our daughter would live a good life without the Bryant family. You speak nicely now, but what if Madeline goes back and ends up in the same nightmare again? How could we bear that?

Elliott, with your background, there must be many women eager to marry you and have your children. You don't need to stick to our Madeline."

"Yes, we won't let Madeline repeat her mistakes. Give up on this idea. Your act won't work on us."

My mom immediately added.

"Elliott, go take a hot shower and change your clothes."

The servant had already brought a new tracksuit of my dad's as I told. I took the clothes to Elliott and said to him.

Elliott still wanted to insist, but I was afraid he would catch a cold, so I dragged him into the bathroom, handed him the clothes, and closed the door.

"Mom, make him some ginger tea to warm up."

I said to my mom after coming out.

My mom glared at me fiercely:

"Make ginger tea? I think you're getting moved again. Madeline, this is a sugar-coated bullet. Don't fall for it easily."

Despite her tough words, my mom still went to the kitchen reluctantly, took ginger from the fridge, and started making the tea.

I walked to the living room, sat quietly next to my dad, and held his hand.

My dad patted the back of my hand and looked at me gently:

"Madeline, tell me, what are you thinking now?"

"Dad, I don't know. I'm lost."

I said honestly.

"Your mom and I think Quinn is definitely more suitable for you. His family is kind and polite, and Quinn is devoted to you and treats Noah as his own."

Clearly, my dad, like my mom, already saw Quinn as their future son-in-law.

My dad's words made my heart even more at a loss...

I knew Quinn deserved all the good things in the world. In terms of appearance, family background, and character, he was not inferior to Elliott in the slightest.

But from the beginning, my feelings for him were always more than friendship but less than love. I knew he was very, very good, but I found it hard to fall in love with him.

Everything he did moved me deeply, but there was always something missing.

My heart was easily affected by what Elliott said and done. But with Quinn, I always remained rational and found it hard to be impulsive.

Seeing my silence, my dad continued:

"Of course, if you insist on getting back together with Elliott, I have nothing to say. After all, he is Noah's father. You two being together would be better for Noah's future.

We love Noah very much and are willing to do anything for him, but there's no guarantee that when he grows up and learns about his background, he won't feel a sense of loss.

It’s best to raise a child in a healthy family, but only if Elliott can ensure he won't hurt you or Noah again."

My dad spoke calmly, his perspective far more rational and long-term than my mom's.

"Dad, let me think about it. This can't be rushed."

I said seriously.

At this moment, Elliott had finished changing and came out of the bathroom.

He had lost a lot of weight. He used to look much bigger than my dad, but now, except for the slightly short sleeves, my dad's tracksuit fit him quite well.

He dried his hair. The black hair fell messily across his forehead. Compared to his usual meticulous hairstyle, this look made him appear much younger.

My mom came out with a bowl of ginger soup, her tone still cold:

"Elliott, come over and drink this ginger soup."

"Thank you, Lily."

Elliott walked over and took the ginger soup from my mom's hands.

Even though he never liked ginger, this time he drank them all without even a frown.

The heavy rain outside had stopped by now.

The servants brought in the gifts he had brought, putting them by the entrance.

Luckily, the high-end packaging of the gifts kept them from getting soaked, so everything inside was still in perfect condition.

"Dad, Mom, I brought some premium health tonics for you. Also, a Rolex watch for Dad, a Hermès bag for Mom, and the latest LV bag for Madeline. Oh, and I got some educational toys and a few outfits for the baby..."

Elliott pointed to the pile of gifts and spoke to us.

His tone was casual, but the gifts he mentioned were worth tens of thousands, even hundreds of thousands.

The pile of gifts easily cost a few million, showing his extraordinary sincerity this time.

My dad and mom stood up in surprise at the sight of such expensive gifts.

My dad quickly said:

"We can't accept these. You should take them back to your parents."

"These are nothing. I just want you to know that I'm willing to do whatever it takes to win Madeline back. Just say the word, and I'll make it happen. Dad, I can't live without Madeline..."

Elliott looked at my dad, his sudden heartfelt confession leaving the old man stunned.
