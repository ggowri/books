I journeyed to Norch City with Adam by my side. Dalton, along with the core team of the crew, welcomed us.

We've reviewed the sample, participated in their discussion meetings, and offered suggestions from an investor's perspective.

In my past life, I've seen this movie. And, because it inspired me so much, I still remember which scenes were the most touching, which were the climaxes, and where the flaws lay.

So, when they showed me the sample, I gave my suggestions with pinpoint accuracy.

Dalton was taken aback. He hadn't expected that, despite being an amateur, my advice would be so seasoned, ruthless, and spot-on.

With a little nudge from me, Dalton immediately had his team re-edit the film, making necessary adjustments.

We observed and debated, over and over again. Time flew, and before we knew it, a week had passed.

After the discussion, I transferred another sum of money to them for promotional purposes, as per our contract agreement.

Afterwards, Adam and I took off together, planning to head home.

Just as we were about to leave the hotel, suddenly, Adam pointed ahead and asked:

"Madeline, is that guy Mr. Bryant?"

At his words, I immediately cast my gaze in the direction he pointed.

That silhouette was all too familiar to me. So, with just one glance, I knew, it had to be Elliott.

Elliott stood a short distance away, his back to me, one hand casually tucked in his pocket.

A young woman, clad in a white trench coat paired with a chiffon skirt, was chatting with him, her body angled towards him as she laughed merrily.

Because they were so far away, we couldn't make out a word of their conversation.

However, my gaze was firmly fixed on that woman, because she was none other than Lisa.

I've been swamped this week, with Elliott ringing me up every day. But each time, I'd just rush through a few words before hanging up.

He didn't mention to me that he too had come to Norch City.

And what's more, he's with Lisa, which really threw me for a loop.

"Madeline, should we go over and say hi?"

Seeing me frozen, Adam quietly asked me.

I shook my head:

"There's no need. We have other matters to attend to."

"Adam and I are planning a trip to Edulet City. Jane and Marcus are over there, overseeing the development of the medicinal herb base. I want to meet up with them and check on how things are progressing."

We immediately spun around and headed for the parking lot.

Sliding into the driver's seat, my hands took control of the steering wheel, guiding the car onto the open road. Yet, my mind was still wrestling with the scene I had just witnessed.

I found the car unusually heavy today, the steering wheel was damn heavy too.

As I was puzzling over what was happening, Adam whispered in my ear:

"Madeline, you didn't release the handbrake. You seem distracted. Maybe I should drive instead."

"Huh?" His words made me realize that I had indeed forgotten to release the handbrake. I was momentarily dumbfounded, then burst into laughter. "But you've been up all night, driving now would be fatigue driving. You should take a nap in the car, I'll drive until I'm tired and then we can switch."

I released the handbrake, forcing myself to focus. Then, I drove off.

The car hit the road, then zoomed off down the highway.

The weather was splendid that day, absolutely glorious. I was donning my sun-blocking shades, skillfully maneuvering the steering wheel.

Just when I was feeling pretty good, I suddenly sensed that the black sedan behind me seemed to be tailing me at a safe distance... and it had been doing so for quite some time...
