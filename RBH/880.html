Instinctively, I wanted to pull my hand back.

Elliott, however, wouldn't let go. He held my hand firmly against his chest.

I could feel the heat radiating from his chest, his palms equally as warm.

His gaze burned into me.

I simply couldn't bear such a gaze, so I had no choice but to dodge desperately.

Little did I know, he seemed to anticipate my evasion, deliberately holding my chin, forcing me to meet his gaze.

"Madeline, you used to always say that I didn't care about you, didn't you? What about now? Can you feel it?"

"Um, but..."

I initially wanted to say "but it's too late," yet considering his illness, I held my tongue.

"There are no buts, Madeline. I want you to know, you're the only woman who's ever truly touched my heart."

The only one, huh?

Has he forgotten about Giselle Cole? Has he forgotten how he hurt me because of Giselle, in the past life and this one?

I didn't respond to his words.

I found my heart surprisingly stirred by his words.

He pulled me into his embrace.

I carry a faint scent of gardenia, a fragrance he had specially concocted for my body wash.

He pressed himself close, taking a harsh sniff at the crook of my neck. His breathing instantly turned ragged.

He cradled my face, his handsome, compelling features leaning in, clearly intending to kiss me.

"Elliott, don't!"

In a flurry, I tried to stop him, simultaneously placing my hand before his lips.

He looked at me, a flicker of disappointment flashing quickly in his eyes, and said:

"After all this time, you're still not comfortable being affectionate with me?"

"No, I've been feeling really drained lately, can't seem to muster any enthusiasm. I think I'll head to the guest room to sleep, that way we can both get a good night's rest."

I feared that if things continued this way, disaster would strike. So, I immediately prepared to leave.

He didn't stop me.

His pride, evidently, was wounded amidst my repeated rejections.

The moment I swung the door open and stepped out, I heard his soft sigh.

I paused in my stride, but didn't linger. I dashed off to the guest room and shut the door behind me.

I dreamt that night.

I dreamt of the moment when I was taken away by those people back in Ruini.

In my dream, I was crying out in desperation. The sky was still thundering, with bolts of lightning slashing down. I saw a horde of menacing figures, brandishing their knives, intent on ending my life. I was retreating helplessly.

And just then, Elliott came roaring in on a dirt bike. He quickly helped me up, draping his jacket over my shoulders.

Afterward, he fiercely battled it out with that bunch.

In my dreams, I saw him being slashed again and again. I watched as the blood poured relentlessly from his wounds. Yet, he kept rising, battling those brutes, his body marred with countless scars.

In the end, he collapsed beside me, his body drenched in fresh blood. Blood was everywhere, flowing like a river.

"Elliott! Elliott!"

I was terrified, screaming his name as I jolted awake from my dream.

I was tightly enveloped in a pair of sturdy arms.

It was only then that I realized it was a dream, and by the time I woke up, I was drenched in cold sweat.
