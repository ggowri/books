Looking at him like that, I felt guilty and quickly grabbed the blanket, preparing to cover him.

To my surprise, he suddenly woke up. Seeing me awake, Quinn immediately smiled, "You're up?"

"Yeah, did you sleep like that last night? That must have been tiring," I remarked.

"That's nothing. Back when I used to camp in the wild, we didn't even have a couch, just had to curl up in the bushes for a while," Quinn said.

"In the wild? What were you doing out there?" I asked skeptically.

He hesitated for a moment, then quickly changed his tune.

"During previous outdoor camping trips, we occasionally had to sleep outside, experiencing a bit of a tough life."

"I didn't realize you not only liked off-roading but also enjoyed this kind of thing." I felt surprised.

He smiled, "I like trying out all sorts of new and adventurous things, so my parents always feel like I'm an untamable wild horse, impossible to rein in."

"You Woods family folks are quite unique, be it you, Carole, Auntie Grey, or even your parents... each with their own distinct style," I chuckled.

The recent interactions made me truly feel that every member of the Woods family had unique characteristics. However, they all shared a common trait of openness, displaying a high level of acceptance towards people and things.

"It seems like you have a soft spot for our Woods family," Quinn teased.

I caught the playful tone in his words and didn't hold back, playfully punching his chest.

"Alright, don't tease me. We both know it's just banter between us."

Quinn stood up, stretched lazily, and then affectionately ruffled my hair.

"Actually, I'm fine with it. With me, you have the freedom to choose. I just want you to be happy. Whatever role I play, I can meet your needs."

Quinn's words clearly conveyed that all his decisions were based on my happiness, with his own desires taking a back seat.

In one's lifetime, apart from one's parents, rarely did someone treat another so unconditionally.

I couldn't help but ask myself again, "What exceptional qualities do I possess that allow Quinn to treat me this way?"

"Don't dwell on it. Perhaps one day, you'll meet a girl who truly captivates you, and then you'll spend a lifetime together," I said solemnly.

"It won't happen again."

Yet, Quinn expressed it with such firmness and certainty, as if he had closed off all possibilities for his future, leaving no room for himself.

Staring at Quinn, I increasingly felt that a huge enigma lay hidden within him, an answer I had yet to uncover.

Elliott's words echoed in my mind. "Quinn isn't a good person."

I didn't understand why Elliott said that, and Quinn, although seemingly deeply affectionate towards me, always maintained a certain distance.

I thought, "The heart of a man is truly elusive."

Feeling perplexed, I decided not to dwell on it any longer. After all, I had many important things to attend to.
