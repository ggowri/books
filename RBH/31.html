Elliott was really ruthless and heartless. He actually abandoned me on a remote mountain road, disregarding the fact that I was a weak woman.

Was he really not afraid that something would happen to me here?

Or perhaps, in his eyes, my life was simply not worth mentioning at all?

My heart ached.

I subconsciously looked around. The pitch-black cedar trees were like one giant ghostly figure after another, making me, who was always afraid of the darkness, can't help but shiver.

I hurriedly took out my phone and turned on the flashlight, quickly checked the location, and found that I was still on that short section of mountain road.

But fortunately, I would be able to reach the main road if I walked another mile ahead.

However, it was already late, and it was impossible for me to walk back as the city was still several hundred miles away.

In order to avoid any unexpected situations, I quickly walked forward while immediately dialing Paige's phone number.

It was around 10 p.m. After Paige answered the phone, music blared over the phone.

It seems that he was at the nightclub again.

"Madeline, what happened?"

"Paige, I'll send you my location. Hurry and find someone to pick me up. Elliott left me stranded in the suburbs, and I don't have a car to get back."

"What? Elliott is damned. Send me your location quickly. I'll come over right now!"

When Paige heard it, she immediately exclaimed loudly.

After hanging up the phone, I immediately sent her my location. Then, guided by the faint light of the flashlight, I walked forward onto the main road.

It was so late that, apart from the occasional passing trucks, hardly any other cars could be seen on this road.

I had never walked alone on the road so late before. It was impossible for me not to be afraid.

A truck slowly stopped in front of me at that moment. The rough-looking truck driver stuck his head out and whistled at me, asking me where I was going and if I wanted him to give me a ride with a malicious look.

I was startled and instinctively started running forward. Luckily, just at that moment, a business car came towards me and stopped beside me.

"Is it Ms. Mayers?"

The window of the driver's cabin in the business car rolled down, and a driver who looked very unfamiliar asked me.

"Did Paige ask you to come over?"

I quickly looked inside, but I found that Paige was not in the car.

The car door opened then, and the driver said to me, "Ms. Mayers, please get in."

I think Paige might have drunk alcohol, so she delegated someone else to pick me up. It must be true since the driver knew I was "Ms. Mayers".

The truck was still parked there, and I felt a little scared. Without thinking too much, I immediately got into the business car.

The driver handed over a bottle of water. "Ms. Mayers, you must be thirsty, right? Drink some water first."

"Okay."

I sat in the car, and at this moment the driver had already closed the door and turned towards the direction of the city center.

After walking for so long at night, I was indeed thirsty. I took the water and drank a few sips in one gulp.

The driver was driving very fast. I felt a little dizzy, and it was at this moment that my phone rang, and Paige's anxious voice came through the phone.

"Madeline, where are you? We have already come over, and we are only 300 feet away from the location you just sent. But why haven't I seen you yet?"

"I've already got in the car. Wasn't the black business car sent by you?"

I asked with doubt. However, at that moment, I felt a sudden and inexplicable dizziness. I couldn't hold on and fell onto the back seat.

The driver turned his head and gave me a sinister look.

I realized that I got in the wrong car, but it was too late. Soon, a stronger dizziness came over me, and I fell into a coma.
