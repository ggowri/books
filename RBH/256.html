The incessant ringing of my phone only added to my distress.

I wasn't in the mood to talk to anyone, so I simply hung up and aimlessly wandered the bustling streets.

For some reason, despite my repeatedly rejecting Quinn's calls, he persisted.

Finally, I gave in to his persistence, answering the phone with an irritated tone, "Quinn, what do you want?"

"We haven't seen each other in so long, why are you being so cold? Are you at Hoswich? If you are, I'm picking you up, and we're having dinner together tonight."

Quinn's tone remained assertive, as if he had already decided I would accept his invitation.

"I'm not in the mood, I don't want to eat," I replied weakly, lacking energy.

Quick to pick up on my unusual demeanor, Quinn asked, "What's wrong? What happened? Where are you now? I'm coming to find you."

"It's okay, Quinn. You go about your business, I just need some time to myself," I replied, declining his offer.

I appreciated his concern but was not ready to face anyone at that moment.

The sudden inspection had thrown all my plans into disarray. My mind was a mess, and I just wanted to be alone, quietly thinking about what I should do next.

After hanging up again, I switched my phone to silent mode, tucked it into my pocket, and continued wandering the streets.

I thought, "Elliott still hasn't called me. I can imagine his disappointment upon receiving the test results.

I know his lack of affection towards me. Discovering my serious infertility issue will likely lead to a swift decision from his end.

It seems inevitable that divorce is our fate, regardless of my efforts to delay it and hinder his progress with Giselle.

He is probably consulting with Jasper and Laura to strategize their next steps. It won't be long before I'm pushed out." The mere thought of it made me tremble uncontrollably.

As the sky darkened, neon lights illuminated the city once more. Lost in my thoughts, I strolled aimlessly, only to find it suddenly drizzling.

Couples with umbrellas and parents with children crowded the streets, leaving me feeling like the loneliest and most helpless person in the city.

I stood in the rain, letting the water soak me. The rain intensified, but I had no desire to seek shelter. I just wanted to let the downpour wash over me.

In a moment of blankness, as I struggled to control my actions, a large black umbrella appeared above my head.

Then, a familiar voice roared in my ear.

"Are you out of your mind? Why are you being so immature? It's pouring, why aren't you seeking shelter indoors?"

Rainwater streamed down my face, blurring my vision. Before I could see clearly, a coat was draped over me.

Through the voice, I recognized Quinn.

I had no idea how he found me in the busy streets, but before I knew it, Quinn had forcefully ushered me into a car.

Inside the car, the heater was on, and Quinn grabbed a white towel, roughly wiping my face and head.

He then yanked off my coat and wrapped me in a blanket.

Opening my eyes, I saw him sitting in front of me, looking angry.

"You don't answer my calls, stand in the rain alone. Do you think you're still young? Do you realize how much effort I put in to find you?"
