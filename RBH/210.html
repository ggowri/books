I put my arms around Elliott's neck. He carried me into the bathroom and then put me down.

"I'll do some exercise on the balcony."

Elliott had the habit of exercising in the morning, which had never changed over the years.

I nodded and started brushing my teeth in a daze in front of the mirror, only to find that there was a smile on my face.

Obviously, Elliott's tenderness in these two days had melted me, and my sanity had long since disappeared.

I brushed my teeth and did simple skin care. When I came out, Elliott completed the last set of exercises. He walked in from the terrace and began to wash up.

I took a set of white sportswear from the suitcase and put it on, tied my hair into a ponytail, and then wore a black peaked cap.

Elliott finished washing up and walked out of the bathroom.

He put on a set of black sportswear. We looked like wearing a couple's outfit.

"Let's go."

I went downstairs with Elliott. He picked up the shoulder bag he had prepared a long time ago, took my hand, and walked out.

In Louis City, the morning air was fresh. We had a simple breakfast downstairs, and then Elliott drove toward Flora Mountain.

The weather was pretty good in autumn, and the melodious music in the car made me feel very relaxed.

We didn't talk much. After driving for nearly an hour, we arrived at the foot of Flora Mountain.

The weather was nice, with sunshine and breeze. It was really good to climb a mountain in this kind of weather.

Elliott carried the single-shoulder bag. We walked along the mountain road and soon began to climb up.

I didn't remember how long it had been since I climbed a mountain. The last time I climbed a mountain was in college.

I hadn't gone far before I gasped. Elliott had been exercising all year round. He looked relaxed and without gasping at all, which made me despise myself for being weak.

After a while, Elliott turned to ask me, "Are you okay? Can you continue walking?"

Seeing me blushing and gasping, he couldn't help but laugh.

"Well, I'm a little tired," I smiled.

"Come on. Give me your hand."

Elliott stretched out his hand towards me. I hesitated for a moment and held his hand.

His palm was warm, and he was strong. He pulled me and walked forward. With his help, I immediately felt much more relaxed.

I followed him all the way. He knew that I had no strength, so he was so considerate along the way.

We stood and gazed at the scenery or stopped to drink water before walking forward. Not long after, we were halfway up the mountain.

"I had climbed the Flora Mountain a few years ago. The most beautiful way to take pictures was from this angle. Stand over there, and I'll take a picture for you."

We were not in Hoswich. Without these identities and statuses, I felt Elliott was extraordinarily cordial, as if he was really my acquaintance who had been with me for many years.

Although my legs were a little weak and tired, such a trip made me feel particularly relaxed.

I listened to Elliott and stood over there. He raised his mobile phone and took a few photos of me, and then he sent them all to my phone.

This was the first time that he took photos of me. I looked at myself in these photos, and an inexplicable happiness welled up in my heart.

Elliott was very good at taking photos. The angles and compositions of these photos were pretty good. I looked good and full of energy in them.

"Do you want me to take a photo of you?" I asked him with a smile.

"Yeah."

Elliott rarely listened to others, but he readily agreed with me and stood there. I picked up my phone and took a few photos of him.
