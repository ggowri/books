The words were eerily similar to what Elliott said in the previous life when he tried to force me to divorce him, making it hard for me to believe that he didn't say them.

I thought, "So, even though he appeared so loving towards me in Louis City, could it be that he still despises me behind my back, just like in my previous life?

I thought my changes had influenced his attitude towards me, but was it all just my illusion?

Giselle is still babbling! I can see the smugness on her face!

What does she have to be proud of? She is just a snake lurking in the shadows, waiting for the opportunity to devour everything from others!"

I couldn't contain my anger anymore, and at that moment, without thinking, I slapped her face hard!

The crisp sound echoed in the hallway. Fueled by genuine hatred, I didn't hold back in the slightest, putting all my strength into that slap.

Her entire face shifted to one side from the force of my slap, and a bright red handprint appeared on her skin.

My entire body trembled with anger, and if murder wasn't a crime, if I had a knife in my hand at that moment, I might have stabbed it directly into her chest.

I admitted that I hated her intensely. The hatred from the previous life and this life had accumulated into a river of tears and blood.

"Madeline, what are you doing?"

A reprimanding voice suddenly rang out in the hallway.

Hearing the familiar voice, I raised my head in astonishment, only to see Elliott standing not far from us in a suit, with his trusted assistant Barry behind him.

"Elliott, Madeline suddenly hit me for no reason!"

Giselle instantly covered her face, crying out to Elliott with a hint of a sob in her voice.

My hand was still numb, and as I watched Elliott approach me and Giselle, I felt no fear, only despair.

"Why did you suddenly attack Giselle for no reason?"

Elliott looked at me with a cold and stern gaze, his protective stance towards Giselle mirroring his behavior from the previous life.

I felt even more despair, and I let out a bitter laugh.

"What's wrong with hitting her? Are you feeling sorry for her?"

Elliott looked at me in disbelief.

"You hit her and you're still so unapologetic? What did she do to provoke you?"

I looked at him with a cold smile, feeling a chill in my heart.

I suddenly found it ironic. The sweet two days in Louis City felt like a dream, and I even doubted if that was the real Elliott.

I really thought we could start over, but now reality had shown me once again how naive I was.

"Elliott, don't blame Madeline. I was in the wrong, I shouldn't have stayed up so late with you last night..."

Giselle pitifully tugged at Elliott's shirt hem, biting her lip and desperately holding back tears, looking truly heart-wrenching.

I stood still, my body stiff, looking at Elliott and Giselle with a cold smile the whole time.

I knew that the best way for me, in this situation, was to follow Giselle's example and act weak towards Elliott to have a chance of being with him in the long run.

But my pride wouldn't allow me to do that.

Elliott said, "You know what you did last night, you know it well! Madeline, you've gone too far!"

Elliott stood with his hands in his pockets, looking down at me with a stern gaze, which made me shiver inexplicably.

A gust of wind suddenly blew through the hallway, shaking my body and almost knocking me over. Giselle, in front of me, immediately hugged Elliott's arm.

She pouted and said, "Elliott, let it go. I don't blame Madeline, and you shouldn't be angry either."
