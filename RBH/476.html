I followed Marcus up to the second floor of his house. As he pushed open the door to one of the rooms, a strong herbal scent hit my nose.

He turned on the light. I glanced around and saw rows of cabinets, each divided into small compartments labeled with various herb names.

This didn't look like a living space but a pharmacy.

"Wow, so many herbs. Can you distinguish them all?" I exclaimed in surprise.

"Of course. Not only can I distinguish them, but I also know where each herb is stored. Herbs are no joke. if you make a mistake, the consequences can be serious," Marcus said with a smile.

He led me inside, grabbing herbs according to the prescription he had given me.

The dim light in the room shone on Marcus, making this usually rugged man appear gentle.

Marcus made me feel reliable and trustworthy.

I thought, "What was his ex-wife thinking. How could she choose to give up such a man." I couldn't help but be a little curious about Marcus' personal life.

However, I didn't ask and just quietly accompanied him to get the herbs. Then, as I watched him divide the herbs into small packets, I curiously asked him some questions about herbs.

Marcus was patient. He answered my questions one by one. Just as I was becoming deeply interested in herbs, my phone suddenly vibrated.

I took out my phone. To my surprise, the message was from Elliott.

He rarely initiates contact with me. This was unusual.

He asked if I was around.

I subconsciously checked the time, realizing it was almost eleven o'clock.

I had unknowingly spent so much time at Marcus' house.

Looking at the message, I frowned unconsciously. After thinking for a while, I decided not to reply to him and put my phone back in my pocket.

By this time, Marcus had already packaged the herbs.

He led me downstairs and said, "Taking these herbs can be troublesome. I have the equipment here. I'll prepare them for you. Just heat them up when you want to drink them."

"That would be wasting your time. It's not necessary," I quickly refused.

"For me, it's simple. I used to help my grandfather with herbs when I was a child. It's nothing. Don't worry about it," Marcus said.

After chatting for a few moments, I decided to head home. Although Marcus' house was close to mine, he was still concerned about me going back alone. So, Marcus insisted on walking me to the door.

So, we both put on our coats and walked out together, chatting and laughing.

On the way, we confirmed our collaboration on that medication. He promised to go to the Glory Group to sign the contract with me.

I was delighted all the way. I was so happy that, at one point, I couldn't help but bounce around like a little girl. Marcus quickly asked me to calm down, afraid that my excessive energy might harm the baby.

I increasingly realized that after leaving Elliott, I seemed to get along very easily with any person of the opposite sex.

When I was with Quinn, we could play around like buddies and share everything, like confidants. With Adam, we had a mutual understanding as boss and employee, and occasionally, I could feel the youthful vitality from him. And with Marcus, not only did I feel the joy of reuniting with an old friend, but more importantly, I could sense the joy of mutual appreciation.
