With a glare that practically screamed "get lost," Elliott cut off any more chatter with Giselle.

The doctor got the memo real quick and hustled Giselle out of there, no chitchat needed.

Nicole trailed after them, sobbing her heart out, her voice fading into the distance. Eventually, the distant echoes brought back some peace to the ward.

Elliott wasted no time in making tracks, probably worried Giselle and Nicole might stir up more trouble. They left together in a flash.

Once Giselle and Nicole were out of sight, Quinn shot a look to the people he brought along.

Everyone got the memo and high-tailed it out of the ward, slamming the door shut behind them.

What a scene! I bet it disturbed my dad's peaceful snooze big time.

He'd been knocked out for ages. Never thought he'd have to deal with all this mess while he was sick. Made me feel super guilty.

I approached his bed, grabbing his hand and leaning in close.

"Dad, sorry for all the drama. I'm moving you to another hospital now. Hang in there a little longer, okay?"

I was used to seeing him sleep so peacefully, and I didn't dare hope he'd wake up.

But when I glanced at him after saying those words, I saw tears welling up in the corners of his eyes.

At that moment, my whole body froze, like I was stuck in time.

My dad, shedding tears? I couldn't believe it.

Was this a sign he was about to wake up, feeling stuff and all?

I watched in disbelief, but then I felt his finger move.

It was subtle, but I felt it because I was holding his hand.

"Mom! Come quick, Dad's... he's crying!" I yelled, and my mother immediately rushed to the bedside.

Gripping the edge tight, she burst into tears when she saw Dad's tears, overwhelmed with joy.

Quinn rushed over too. "He's crying! I'll call the doctor!"

"Mom, Dad's fingers moved, and it feels like he's waking up," I said, gripping Mom's hand, tears welling up.

Sure enough, Dad's eyelids flickered, and his fingers twitched some more.

He was definitely on the brink of waking up.

I couldn't even put my feelings into words.

Quinn dashed off to get Dad's doctor, who asked us to step aside so he could check Dad out.

Before long, the doctor turned to me, grinning.

"Your dad's starting to respond. He's almost awake! We'll run some tests right away!"
