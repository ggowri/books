Elliott cast a pained glance my way, his gaze lingering on the blood seeping from my arm. Unable to resist, he leaned in, pressing a deep kiss to my forehead. Only then did he turn, leading the others out of the infirmary.

Elliott shut the door to the infirmary.

With her hands tucked into her pockets, Lisa stood before me, a peculiar smile playing on her lips. She stared at me, her eyes squinted in a way that made her look uncannily like a cunning fox.

That kind of gaze, it's unsettling. I can't quite put my finger on why.

"Ms. Fox, perhaps it's high time we had a chat, don't you think?"

I sat up, looking at her as I spoke calmly.

"Ms. Mayers, what exactly do you want to discuss with me? We... we don't seem to have much in common, aside from our looks."

Lisa skillfully steered the conversation elsewhere.

"Really? I'd say there's quite a connection between Ms. Fox and me, you seem to pop up wherever I go. If my memory serves me right, our first encounter was at that resort hotel in Onastin, remember?"

"Vacation hotel? Which one? I don't recall ever seeing Ms. Mayers in Onastin, at least not to my memory."

Lisa was still playing the innocent.

"Some things don't need to be spelled out. We're all smart here, tell me, Ms. Fox, what's your real game? Is it for Elliott? Or is it for me?"

I didn't want to keep going around in circles with her, so I went straight to the point.

Lisa deliberately glanced outside, then took a couple of steps towards me. She sat down in front of me, whispering:

"Ms. Mayers, I'm still not getting your point. I came to Hoswich City because I was drawn to the allure of the big city life. I just want to find my footing here in Hoswich. As for you, I'm really not interested--"

At this point, Lisa still had the audacity to try and deceive me.

I gave her a cool, detached smile.

"Really? Then I'd like to know, aren't you the woman who frequently visits the orphanage to see Giselle? From the surveillance footage the director showed me, wasn't that you I saw?"

I kept my gaze locked on her facial expressions.

Sure enough, the moment I mentioned Giselle, her gaze shifted rapidly. In that instant, a powerful instinct compelled her to blurt out:

"Madeline, have you been snooping around on me?"

Her sudden agitation was telling enough; there must be a significant connection between her and Giselle.

Otherwise, how could she possibly go to see Giselle's child? And there's no way she'd drop her act and get so worked up the moment I mention Giselle.

"I haven't been snooping on you. It's just that, you keep coming around me, over and over again. I have to figure out what you're up to, don't I?"

I stared at her, not wanting to miss the slightest shift in her emotions.

But what I hadn't anticipated was that this woman was even more adept at deception than I had imagined.

She was clearly emotional just a moment ago, but instantly, she simply offered a faint smile:

"Oh, you're talking about Jali Welfare Home? Yeah, I've made a few donations there. Those orphans, they tug at your heartstrings, don't they? But Giselle's kid? Can't say I know them."

She's definitely playing dumb about this!
