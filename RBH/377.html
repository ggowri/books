The next day.

Various tests showed that my body was fine. Elliott directly arranged for my discharge and brought me back to Serenity Villa.

Although Fiona and Paige were surprised by the sudden reconciliation between Elliott and me, they had gotten used to it, so they didn't say much.

Quinn, on the other hand, seemed understanding. He didn't say much and disappeared again.

It had been a long time since I returned to Serenity Villa. Sean, upon seeing me, was both shocked and delighted, immediately arranging for a large table of food, saying it was to welcome Elliott and me back.

I had planned to go to work, but Elliott, citing my health, asked me to rest at home for an extra day.

He also took a day off specifically to stay at home with me.

We had planned to visit Austin and Lily at Bryant Hospital after lunch, so Lily wouldn't worry since I hadn't appeared for several days.

However, what I didn't expect was that while Elliott and I were having lunch, we suddenly had two unexpected guests at home.

The dining room of the Serenity Villa was not connected to the living room but was set on the other side of the kitchen.

Therefore, people entering the living room couldn't immediately see the situation in the dining room.

Elliott and I were enjoying our meal when suddenly we heard voices in the living room.

"Mom, I think the color of this sofa doesn't match the curtains. When I move in, I'll change this sofa right away."

"Hmm, it doesn't really match. That painting looks dull, not bright enough. It should be replaced too."

"Right. Mom, Elliott likes the dishes you cook. When I move in, you should move in with us too, so I don't have to worry about you. What do you think?"

"Let's talk about that later. As long as you and Elliott are happy, where I live doesn't matter."

...

I immediately recognized the speakers. It was Giselle and her mother.

It seemed like they couldn't wait to occupy this place before I divorced Elliott. They thought I wasn't here and came to Serenity Villa for a visit.

They hadn't taken over yet, but they were already planning to change the decoration of this love nest of Elliott and me. Giselle's ambition was indeed not to be underestimated.

I put down my fork, my expression instantly turning serious.

Elliott also heard the voices in the living room, his expression darkened as he stood up to go out.

At this moment, Sean rushed into the living room in a fluster, carefully telling us, "I don't know who told them the password. They just came in suddenly and startled me too. Don't worry, I'll ask them to leave right away."
