Laura's gaze bore into me with a chilling intensity as she delivered her words.

Her hands tightly gripping Giselle's. The protective stance she took mirrored a scene from my past life.

I felt the icy chill of realization wash over me. Unbeknownst to me, tears streamed down my face.

My nails dug into my palms as I fought to maintain my composure.

The overwhelming flood of information left me reeling, unable to process the betrayal unfolding before me.

This house of Bryant family now felt much more cold and indifferent than ever.

I used to consider people standing here as my family, and they all treated me ruthlessly.

This was the big show Giselle said I would see tonight.

No wonder that time when I was at Alexi's law firm, Giselle told me that she would take Elliott away from me.

No wonder she provoked me again and again even Elliott didn't love her.

No wonder she lived in Bryant Villa so easily and let Laura accompany her to the hospital.

She held the most important leverage.

It dawned on me that Giselle had orchestrated a grand scheme, manipulating her way into Elliott's life and securing her position with a child—a child I had longed for across two lifetimes.

Compensation?

What good could money offer to salve the wounds of such profound humiliation?

"This whole family disgust me," I spat out bitterly, my words cutting through the tense silence like a knife.

With a steely resolve, I turned on my heels and made my escape from the Bryant Villa, a place that now felt more like hell than home.

I felt more humiliated than ever.

The realization of Elliott's deceit cut deeper than any physical pain, the weight of his betrayal suffocating me with every breath.

His adept performance, concealing his affair with Giselle and the child they shared, stung like a fresh wound.

He lied to me again and again. Played with my heart like a game.

How could he have feigned affection towards me while harboring such a devastating secret?

As I ran, the looming sycamore trees seemed to reach out menacingly, threatening to consume me whole.

Just as I felt myself teetering on the brink of despair, a strong hand seized me, pulling me into a comforting embrace.

Elliott's presence enveloped me, his touch grounding me in the midst of turmoil.

With his arms wrapped around me, Elliott's voice, tinged with urgency and sincerity, broke through the turmoil.

"Please, don't be angry," he pleaded, his breath warm against my ear. "Let me explain."
