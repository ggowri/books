I get what he's saying.

However, I didn't want to pursue this topic any further, whispering softly:

"I'm heading to the office first, got a morning meeting to attend."

"Alright, I'm pretty tied up today too. I'll have the driver take you there."

Elliott didn't whip up breakfast like he usually would, nor did he insist on personally dropping me off. It seemed that last night's events had left him feeling pretty down.

I drove myself to Glory Group.

Once in the office, I sank into the plush leather chair and started tackling the paperwork on my desk.

Halfway through, there was a knock at the door.

I lifted my gaze.

Caught sight of a towering, robust figure.

A faint smile graced his handsome, profound face, the previous desolation completely swept away. It was as if the awkwardness of last night had never happened.

His powerful aura seemed to dim the lights in my office.

I dropped the document, instinctively glanced at my watch, and asked, "What brings you here?"

I thought last night's incident would have him stewing for a while. But to my surprise, he actually showed up at Glory Group looking for me.

He held a thermos in his hand, speaking gently:

"Heard you coughing last night, so I had the chef whip up some chicken soup for you."

In the latter part of last night, I indeed found myself coughing.

However, it was just a few coughs, nothing I paid much attention to.

Unexpectedly, he actually heard it, and took it to heart!

The CEO of Bryant Group actually took time out of his busy schedule to personally deliver chicken soup to me?

In the past, this was something utterly inconceivable—

He's really as meticulous as ever now.

Suddenly, it felt as if we had swapped roles. I had become the one impervious to harm, while he had turned into the one desperately trying to please and win me over.

I took the thermos, saying:

"Just a few coughs and you heard it. Were you up all night?"

I popped open the lid of the thermos, instantly greeted by the savory aroma of chicken soup. The soup was still hot.

I suddenly felt a wave of blissful contentment.

"Do you think I can catch a wink?"

Elliott shot back a question at me. Then, picking up a spoon, he ladled some chicken soup from the thermos and brought it to my lips.

He insisted on feeding me, so I had no choice but to open my mouth.

The bird's nest soup was quickly finished, and Elliott pulled me down to sit on the sofa.

"Busy morning? You seemed swamped when I walked in—"

"I've been poring over these reports all morning, my eyes are about to cross."

"Really? Let me give you a massage."

Before I could react, his warm fingers had already begun to gently press around my eyes.

His technique was professional, massaging circles around my eyes. It was incredibly soothing.

Without realizing it, I found myself nestled in his arms, quietly savoring the moment.

At this moment, the café waitress gently knocked on my office door. Dressed in a sleek black professional suit, she carried a cup of coffee. Placing it on the table, she politely said:

"Hello, sir, here's your mocha coffee. Enjoy."

"Alright, thanks."

Elliott flashed her a grateful smile.

Seems like he grabbed a coffee at our new employee café when he came in.

Once the waiter had departed, Elliott spoke up:

"Glory Group is really taking off now. Never thought you'd have such a knack for business."

The tone was filled with admiration, a kind of recognition I had seldom received before.

Elliott rarely gives compliments in the business world, and he doesn't consider anyone as his equal.
