"What decision? Are you choosing to be with Quinn?"

Elliott's voice was low and hoarse.

I was silent for a long time, struggling, but finally, I nodded slightly.

My response hit Elliott like a ton of bricks. He staggered.

He could barely stand, holding onto the chair for support.

He looked at me, then at Quinn, his lips trembling before he painfully asked:

"Are you sure?"

"Madeline made up her mind long ago. If it weren't for your sudden amnesia and the doctor's orders, we would already be together."

Quinn stood up, putting his arm around my shoulder in front of Elliott, claiming his place.

My body trembled slightly.

For a moment, I wanted to pull away, but realizing it would hurt Quinn, I restrained myself.

Elliott's eyes were dark, his face pale under the dim light:

"Noah is my child. We just reunited. Can you really separate us?"

He clutched his chest, seemingly in great pain.

Mentioning Noah... my heart ached too.

Yes, if not for everything that happened, we would have been a happy family.

But now, too much has been broken. Trying to piece it back together is futile.

"When you decided to get engaged to Giselle, you ended your bond with Noah. Noah is my child. I chose to bring him into this world, and I intend to treat him as my own, with no ties to you or the Bryant family. I hope you understand."

I looked at Elliott calmly.

Since everything was out in the open, it was best to be clear to avoid future entanglements.

I hoped Elliott would understand.

But he gave a bitter smile:

"He has my blood, Madeline! How can I just give up? You're being too cruel!"

Elliott still clutched his chest, looking too pained to stand, sinking back into his chair.

The lobster on the table no longer smelled appetizing. The air felt cold, and the once warm family atmosphere was now icy.

I felt a chill, my whole body trembling.

Seeing Elliott like this, I felt awful and pained.

But my mind was clear, reminding me of the vows I made during sleepless nights after our divorce.

I might have been cruel then, but it didn't compare to the cruelty I faced from his family.

Actions had consequences. The pain he felt then was what I once endured.

"If you think Madeline is cruel, remember how you and your family treated her. Elliott, let go. Let me take care of Madeline and give her the happiness she deserves."

Quinn firmly held my cold hand, looking at Elliott and speaking calmly.

Quinn's words must have hit hard. Elliott clutched his chest, stood up, and stared at Quinn with a fierce look, suddenly saying:

"You give her happiness? Quinn, what do you have to offer her? You should know better than anyone if you can."

Quinn's hand holding mine trembled, his whole body shaking.

I glanced at Quinn, puzzled. I could sense some hidden meaning in Elliott's words, but I had no idea what he was implying.

"If I promised, I'll deliver. You don't need to worry about it!"

To prove his point, Quinn thumped his chest and declared loudly.

Elliott shot Quinn a cold look, then suddenly stepped forward and forcefully pushed Quinn's hand off mine.

He then grabbed my hand, cupped my face, and looked at me with deep affection:

"Madeline, please give me another chance not just for me, but for Noah. I believe you want to give Noah a happy, complete family. After all, he's still so young!"

As soon as Elliott finished, Quinn shoved him aside and grabbed my hand again, saying coldly:

"I can give Noah a happy, complete family. I can treat him as my own and give him a simple, joyful childhood. The stability I offer Madeline, you can't match. You'd never give up Bryant Group for her!"

I could feel that since I made my feelings clear, Quinn's attitude had become more assertive than ever.

He truly wanted to build a happy family with me and had already given everything he could.

Elliott fell silent.

Yes, no matter how much he loved me, he wouldn't give up Bryant Group, this vast business empire. And as long as he didn't, his schedule would always be packed...

A man focused on his career wouldn't have much time for his wife and child.

I had experienced the loneliness of waiting for him at home, both in my past and present life.

But Quinn was different. He had a substantial family fortune that allowed him to be carefree. He could choose not to inherit the family business and spend lots of time with me and Noah.

"What if I'm willing to give up Bryant Group? Madeline, would you come back to me?"

I thought Elliott would back off, but to my surprise, after a long pause, he asked me this serious question.

He'd give up Bryant Group?

How was that possible?

He had put unimaginable effort into building Bryant Group to its current state. He always prioritized his career above everything else.

I never expected that he would be willing to give up his proudest achievement for me...

Did he really need me that much? Love me that deeply?

I looked at him in disbelief. When I saw the passion in his eyes, an inexplicable instinct made me push Quinn's hand away.

"You're the sole heir of the Bryant family. Giving up isn't realistic..."

I couldn't help but entertain a fleeting fantasy but quickly dismissed it as impractical.

"I can hire a professional manager. I can make myself less busy. I'm willing to change everything for you. Madeline, whatever Quinn can do, I can too!"

Seeing my hesitation, Elliott stepped forward, promised in front of Quinn, and pulled me into his arms...
