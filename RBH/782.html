Seeing Elliott's name flash across my phone screen, I instinctively furrowed my brows but picked up nonetheless:

"Hello?"

"Why haven't you returned yet? I've been waiting for you all this while."

On the other end of the line, Elliott's tone was urgent yet tender.

"Like I told you, I'm staying at my folks' place for a while. I won't be coming over, you should get some sleep."

I said, with a patience I was barely holding onto.

Elliott fell silent for a few seconds, then his slightly magnetic voice echoed once more:

"I'm coming over, wait for me."

I didn't expect Elliott to be so relentless. I told him I wouldn't go, yet he insisted on coming to find me.

"Elliott, it's too late. Don't come over, I'm already asleep."

I hastily declined over the phone.

I wasn't sure if Elliott was angry or not. All I heard was the "beep beep" of a busy signal on the other end of the line. He'd hung up.

I glanced at the clock, it was already past ten in the evening.

I've been by Elliott's side these past few days, utterly exhausted. My time in the hospital has been a constant state of worry, sleep eluding me. Without a second thought, I hung up the phone and collapsed onto the bed, falling asleep instantly.

After what felt like an eternity, my phone rang again. It was Elliott, calling past one in the morning.

When Elliott woke up with amnesia, being so clingy was something I hadn't anticipated.

Jack had drilled it into my head a thousand times not to get on his nerves. So, I reluctantly picked up the phone, saying with a hint of annoyance:

"Elliott, you're calling again? Why aren't you asleep at this late hour?"

From the other end of the phone, his deep voice resonated, and boy, was he pissed:

"You lied to me."

I was baffled, unsure of what he meant: "What did I lie to you about?"

"Didn't you say you were staying at your parents' place? I'm right here, downstairs, and your place is empty. Your neighbors told me you moved out a while ago. Why didn't I know about this?"

Elliott's voice held a note of questioning, a hint of doubt. I realized, his memory was still stuck in a time when we were still married, when he thought we still lived in our old place.

He's just recovered from his illness, yet here he is, not sleeping at this late hour, but hanging around under my apartment. What on earth is Elliott up to?

I'm at a loss for words and helpless:

"Elliott, even though we're... we're married, you can't expect me to be by your side 24/7. I have my own personal freedom, don't I?"

Uttering the words "husband and wife" is truly a struggle for me. But right now, I have no other choice, I can only force myself to pretend.

"I yearn for sleep, but without you, it eludes me. Madeline, where are you now?"

Elliott's voice was soft, laced with a hint of weakness that stirred a sense of compassion within me.

But then it hit me. If I let him keep pestering me, treating me like his woman just like before, expecting me to be by his side every second, I fear I might lose my mind. Moreover, I'm genuinely afraid that if I keep playing this role, I might slip up.

"Elliott, I'm already tucked in. Please, no more messages tonight. Goodnight."

With a ruthless heart, I abruptly hung up the phone, then buried my head in the covers, ready to drift back into slumber.

But my sleep quality is truly worrisome. Elliott's call disrupted my slumber. I tossed and turned in bed for a long time, unable to drift off.

My phone rang again at two in the morning, it was a call from Barry by the sea.

"Mrs. Bryant, Mr. Bryant just got discharged from the hospital today, and he's already drinking. Dr. Jones strictly advised against any alcohol. But, I can't seem to stop him."
