"No, babe. Come on. I'll teach you."

Hearing Elliott's voice, I stopped reflexively. Paige heard it, too, and she suddenly became angry.

"Is Elliott fooling around with that actress again? He's married! This is unreasonable!"

Such a thing happened countless times in my previous life. Back then, I rushed in to get him like a shrew, only to be ridiculed by those women, despised by his friends, and even kicked out by security.

To Elliott, whatever I did was wrong.

I didn't understand it in the past. But now, I felt quite calm.

Paige wanted to rush in to teach Elliott a lesson, but I stopped her and shook my head.

I said, "Paige, let him be. He could find women, and I can also get young men. We just do our own thing. It's not worth being angry with him!"

After drinking, I talked more loudly. And with the hatred, I inevitably spoke with gritted teeth.

I was about to leave with Paige. Unexpectedly, someone behind me suddenly stopped me.

"Madeline, since when did you become so thoughtful? Elliott is there. Aren't you going to come in and meet us?"

I found the voice familiar. It was Quinn.

It was a small world. I wanted to leave quietly. However, he stopped me and opened the box's door.

At that moment, everyone in their box saw me. Soon, Elliott's cold voice sounded.

"Madeline, come in!"

He still had that emotionless and commanding tone.

I turned my head and rested my eyes on Elliott in the center of the box.

Everyone in the box was dressed casually except for Elliott, who was in a dark suit. He sat there with a solemn look. I could feel the pressure even from a distance.

I saw the woman beside him acting coquettishly. She was Sylvia Hart, a minor actress who played roles in several popular TV series. She was Elliott's type. They had been spending a lot of time together recently. Only I knew she was one of the women Elliott used as a cover-up during our six years of marriage.

The only woman he always loved was Giselle. But back then, I wasted too much energy on these women and made myself miserable. But I ignored Giselle.

"Anything I can help?"

I walked towards Elliott while he frowned again and mocked, "Madeline, who allowed you to drink?"

"Myself," I replied nonchalantly.

Elliott's scowl deepened, anger flaring.

He glared at me, his words icy. "How dare you dress so provocatively and drink at the club? Forgot you're married?"

He had the nerve to ask, parading around with another woman himself.

A sudden surge of bravery had me leaning in, arms resting on the couch beside him. Our faces, now mere inches apart.

Taken aback, Elliott recoiled. Our eyes locked. I could see every pore on his face.

I boldly breathed on him, flashing a bright smile. "And what about you? Drinking here with another woman, letting paparazzi track you. Ever consider you're married, too?"
