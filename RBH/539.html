It seemed that Quinn's position in his family was precarious, almost at the bottom.

Seeing Quinn with no say whatsoever, I found it amusing yet increasingly nerve-wracking.

Honestly, I was completely unprepared for the current situation.

I never imagined Quinn's parents could be so open-minded towards me, let alone readily accept everything.

But if one day they were to find out it was all a lie, how would they see me? How would they treat me?

I felt nervous and wished I could disappear immediately.

However, the Woods family was too hospitable. To entertain me, his parents even had the servants prepare a whole table full of dishes.

Moreover, Jessica kept serving me food as if afraid I wouldn't have enough to eat.

Thinking back to how I was treated coldly when I accompanied Elliott to the Bryant family, I felt conflicted.

After dinner, despite Quinn's and my objections, Jessica and Carole insisted on accompanying me to the hospital for a check-up.

We tried to refuse, but they persisted, leaving me no choice but to grit my teeth and let them accompany me to Fiona's hospital for the examination.

Fiona had finally returned from Tepit and was already back to work.

On the way, I briefly explained my current situation to them in our group chat.

When Paige and Fiona heard about Jessica and Carole's insistence on taking me for a check-up, they were both shocked, not knowing what to say in response to this miraculous turn of events.

But given the current situation, we could only go along with it.

Fiona was mentally prepared in advance, so when she saw Jessica and Carole accompanying me, she didn't say much but immediately prepared the relevant documents for me to undergo the examination.

It had been so long since I had an ultrasound. I had no idea what the condition of the baby in my belly was like now.

As I lay on the ultrasound bed, my heart was filled with unease and apprehension.

Jessica went in with me, and as the doctor placed the probe on my stomach, she couldn't help but get nervous, gripping my hand tightly, saying, "Madeline, don't worry. Everything will be fine."

The doctor manipulated the probe on my stomach for a while, then I heard him explaining some data to his assistant.

Although I didn't understand much of the data, I clearly heard the doctor say that the baby now had a fetal heart and an embryo bud, which meant that I had successfully passed the first hurdle of early pregnancy.

Upon hearing this news, I couldn't help but breathe a long sigh of relief.

The ultrasound was quickly done, and Jessica accompanied me to find Fiona with the report.

However, just as we were leaving the ultrasound room, I unexpectedly ran into Giselle again in the corridor...

Her belly was already quite big, with two maids supporting her on either side very carefully.

She also held a report in her hand, her face full of gloom, as if she had just suffered a blow, her lips colorless, her eyes dull...
