Rowan was still clad in his lawyer's uniform, clearly on his way to work, moving hastily. The pink takeout box in his hand suggested he was heading to deliver a meal to Giselle.

I hadn't expected that while Giselle was flirting with Elliott, she was also entertaining Rowan's advances.

Watching Rowan's humble and devoted demeanor, I couldn't help but realize I had underestimated Giselle's capabilities. She had a way of manipulating various individuals effortlessly.

Elliott, noticing my continued gaze out the window, skeptically asked, "What are you looking at?"

I pointed towards Rowan's figure.

"Isn't that Giselle's boyfriend? Seems like he's specifically going to the hospital to deliver food to her."

Elliott glanced out the window, momentarily stunned.

"How do you know that's Giselle's boyfriend? Are you sure you're not mistaking someone else?"

"How could I be mistaken? He's wearing the Alexis Law Firm uniform. Besides, I had dinner with him in Louis City once. There's no way I could be wrong," I asserted confidently.

"Why were you having dinner with Giselle's boyfriend?" Elliott asked, clearly puzzled.

"My assistant was his university classmate, so I helped him get a job. As a gesture of thanks, he treated me to dinner. Why, did you think I was deliberately trying to get close to her boyfriend? I'm not that bored," I replied.

"I didn't mean that, it just seems a bit odd," Elliott said, then asked meaningfully, "Giselle's boyfriend works at the Alexis Law Firm because of your referral?"

I nodded, not seeing a reason to hide my assistance to Rowan.

"Yes, his work capabilities are average."

Elliott remained silent, and when I turned to look again, Rowan's figure had disappeared from the street.

"The flavors of the dishes here are quite good. Try this piece of steak. It's cooked to perfection."

Elliott suddenly offered, spearing a piece of steak and placing it on my plate.

"Thank you."

I nodded, picking it up and taking a bite. The taste was indeed delightful, with a great texture, showcasing the chef's exceptional baking skills.

Elliott's expression softened, and then he suddenly said, "I know today's events were abrupt, but I hope you won't blame Grandpa. His only wish now is to have a great-grandchild. Also, during this hospital stay, I arranged for the doctors to conduct a comprehensive examination, revealing signs of his lung cancer relapse. The doctors said that at this rate, his life expectancy may not exceed two years."

Elliott rarely displayed his emotions, but as he spoke, his eyes unexpectedly welled up with tears.

Upon hearing this news, I was shocked and deeply distressed. Unable to contain my emotions, I blurted out, "Isn't there any other solution? With today's advanced technology, there should be other methods available."

Elliott shook his head.

"He underwent a similar surgery a few years ago, and the doctors then said that once the disease relapses, it becomes uncontrollable. So, to fulfill his wish, we need to hurry and start preparing for a grandchild. What do you think?"

Elliott once again brought up the request, and his reasoning made it impossible for me to refuse. After all, this was the dying wish of a man nearing the end of his life.

I suddenly set down my fork, feeling hesitant in the face of this new revelation.
