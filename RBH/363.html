Time slipped away unnoticed, and before I knew it, it was already five in the afternoon.

"Have you got everything done? Come downstairs, I'm waiting for you." Elliott's voice came through the phone.

"Okay, I'll be right there," I replied quickly.

I wrapped up my work and grabbed my bag before heading downstairs.

This time, Toby was behind the wheel, and Elliott was waiting for me in the car. As soon as I got in, Elliott took my cold hands and started rubbing them to warm them up.

"It's freezing out here. You should wear a coat next time," he said with concern.

My cold hands were quickly warmed by him.

"I'll be fine. It's just a short walk," I reassured him. "What would possibly your parents and your grandfather want to tell us?"

The thought of that I needed to face them at the Bryant Villa annoyed me.

"I don't know. We'll find out soon enough. It could be anything from a casual chat to discussing Christmas plans. My mother wants us to spend Christmas with my grandfather in Brila," Elliott explained gently.

The Bryant family had roots in Brila, and they often visited relatives there.

It seemed like a reasonable topic for discussion, but something about Laura's tone made me uneasy.

I decided to worry about it when I got there. At least, this time Elliott would be on my side to fight against his family.

We switched the topic, talking about the Christmas plans and some other stuff casually.

Elliott and I chatted for a while. Not long after, we arrived at the Bryant Villa.

As we approached the Bryant Villa, I felt a sense of apprehension creeping over me.

The atmosphere inside was tense, and there was none of the usual warmth that greeted us.

The entire Bryant Villa was soaked in a quiet and solemn gloom.

Cormac was sitting in his seat, wearing a serious expression.

Jasper and Laura were seated on the sofa. I could not tell their emotion.

The servants stood at attention, and the dining table was bare. Everyone stared at us in silence.

Elliott and I greeted them simultaneously as we entered.

"Please, have a seat. We have something important to discuss," Jasper said, motioning for us to sit down.

He pointed to the two chairs that had been placed in the living room early for us to sit down.

It felt like we were about to face a firing squad...

My heart started to race as I took in the scene before me. But Elliott's reassuring squeeze on my hand gave me some comfort.

He looked at me and gave me a reassuring look, then pulled out a chair for me.

"Just tell us what you want. I will take Madeline for dinner later," Elliott said, his tone tinged with impatience.

Despite the intimidating atmosphere, Elliott's confidence didn't waver.
