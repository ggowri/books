I had no idea what he wanted to talk to me about, but I couldn't resist his strength and found myself being dragged forward.

I wondered why he was seeking me out alone, especially since he had taken Giselle for a check-up, and Quinn had just gone to check on her. Why did Elliott suddenly want to talk to me?

With doubts lingering in my mind, I was pulled all the way from the hospital corridor to the back seat of his car.

He shut the car door. Before I could react, he pulled me into his arms, his tone unusually soft as he apologized, "I'm sorry for what happened today. It was entirely unexpected."

I thought he had something to say to me when he dragged me into the car. But now, was he trying to make me submit again with this approach?

A wave of inexplicable anger surged within me. I pushed him away with all my might and glared at him with bloodshot eyes.

"Elliott, mind your actions. It disgusts me."

At this moment, I resisted him intensely, even though he still looked like the same Elliott I used to know.

After everything that had happened, the trust I had painstakingly built up for him had returned to square one.

I found it hard to believe anything he said and didn't want any physical contact with him.

He must have felt the resistance emanating from me. His gaze held a hint of pain as he looked at me.

"I believed that we had developed enough of a bond during this time. Could this bond be so easily shattered by someone else?"

Bond?

I scoffed.

I chuckled coldly, deliberately retreating to the side of the car door, wanting to maintain as much safety distance as possible from him.

"You talk about having a bond with me after having a child with another woman. Don't you find that ridiculous? Is it because you think I can't bear children, so you want to force me to accept your child? Or are you planning to drive me away before they're born?"

I questioned coldly.

Elliott shifted forward slightly. He reached out and gently tucked my somewhat disheveled hair behind my ear, his eyes full of helplessness.

"The child indeed came unexpectedly. I have thought back repeatedly, and nothing happened between me and her at Dikio that night. I have no reason to deceive you," he insisted.

He desperately tried to prove his innocence, forcing me to look up at him. So I did, but with mockery written all over my gaze.

"Isn't it simple to prove that the child's not yours? You just took her for a paternity test. So, what were the results?"

I thought he would provide me with an answer honestly, but his eyes flickered for a moment, and then he lowered his head.

"I asked to speak with you alone to discuss this matter," he said.

His demeanor betrayed a sense of guilt.

My heart raced, and my voice turned sharp.

"Well? Have the results of the child's paternity test come back? Or did you never take her for examination in the first place?"
