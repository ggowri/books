The restaurant Elliott mentioned was near the hospital, not very spacious but exquisitely decorated in a vintage style. They served authentic local dishes from Hoswich, with the walls adorned with the history of the restaurant's development.

We chose a corner booth. As noon approached, the restaurant was bustling with many customers and was quickly filled up.

Elliott and I both lowered our heads to peruse the menu, silently selecting our desired dishes before informing the waiter.

After ordering, I gazed out the window. It was deep autumn, with golden leaves carpeting the street outside, creating a melancholic yet charming atmosphere.

Finally breaking the silence, Elliott spoke slowly. "How long will you be staying in Louis City?"

"At least half a month," I replied casually.

"Quinn's new store in Louis City is about to open. Avoid meeting him alone," Elliott warned, his tone revealing his concern.

Raising my head, I smiled meaningfully, "And what about you? Can you avoid meeting Giselle?"

Elliott's expression stiffened with a hint of displeasure.

"Giselle works at the Bryant Group, and she's currently spending every day with Grandpa. These two matters are not to be conflated."

It seemed Elliott intended to continue his relationship with Giselle but forbade me from meeting Quinn.

I smiled faintly and remarked, "Quinn's new store opening, and since I'm in Louis City, I definitely have to show support. In my view, these two matters are essentially the same."

Elliott's expression darkened further.

"You seem to enjoy arguing with me now. Giselle is so young, why do you overthink things?"

I retorted, "Quinn is your best friend, he's just been a bit more attentive to me as a friend. Why do you overthink it?"

We locked eyes, and despite my attempt to ease the tension between us, the mention of Giselle still irked me.

Frowning, Elliott stared at me, equally puzzled.

"I know Quinn, I understand his intentions towards you better than you do."

Crossing my arms, I looked at Quinn, feeling a sense of irony growing.

"I know Giselle too. You think she's young, but she's quite cunning. I understand her better than you do."

Elliott suddenly grew angry, his gaze cold as he glared at me.

"Madeline, I invited you to dinner not to argue with you."

And neither did I.

If it weren't for wanting to improve our relationship, I wouldn't have subjected myself to this dinner with him in such circumstances.

Realizing the futility of our argument, which only served to diminish our mutual regard, I suppressed my anger and looked out the window, refusing to say another word.

At that moment, the waiter brought our dishes. They showcased the renowned local Hoswich cuisine, which was prepared with authentic flavors and techniques.

Despite the delicious spread before me, I had lost my appetite.

Avoiding eye contact with Elliott, I gazed out the window, only to spot a very familiar figure hurrying towards the hospital with a takeout box in hand.

I thought, "Isn't that Rowan?"
