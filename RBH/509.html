I sat in the chair. I wanted to do something for Quinn, but I found I was too anxious when I arrived and had nothing prepared except for a few boxes of masks.

I didn't expect Quinn to be injured, so I didn't buy any fruits or nutritional supplements. At this moment, looking at his haggard and injured look, I couldn't help but shed tears.

When Quinn saw that I was silent and cried, he immediately panicked and took out a tissue to wipe my tears.

"Don't cry. I'm fine. It's not that serious. When I was talking to you on the phone, some gangsters were making trouble in the hospital, and I went up to try to stop them, but one of them cut my abdomen with a knife. It's a minor injury, and there is no internal damage. No crying."

Quinn comforted me.

"What happened to your head?" I asked.

"A few of them rushed up. I was outnumbered and was beaten. See, I'm still sober, so my head must be fine. Don't worry. I'll rest for two or three days, and then I'll be fine."

Quinn continued comforting me.

Typical Quinn. No matter how big the matter was, he always didn't take it seriously. He was obviously injured, but he acted like everything was fine.

Only then did I stop crying. I raised my head and looked at him with red eyes.

"Are you really fine? You didn't lie to me?"

"Yes. How about I get out of bed, walk around, and dance?"

Seeing that I didn't believe him, he moved to get out of bed. I was frightened and quickly pressed his arm.

"Quinn, stop messing around."

I looked very nervous, which made Quinn laugh. He looked at me meaningfully.

"I didn't expect you to feel so sad. If I had known, I should have let them give me more stabs to make myself more miserable. If so, would you faint from crying?"

Quinn was optimistic by nature and could make fun of anything.

I was speechless and glared at him.

"No more jokes. Do you have anything you want to eat? I'll buy it for you."

Quinn pointed to a bag of fruit on the table not far away and smiled, "Since you're so sincere, can you peel an apple for me?"

Without further ado, I walked over and took out an apple, washed it, and then carefully peeled it with a paring knife.

Quinn was also injured on his head, and it would be difficult for him to bite such a big apple. So, I cut it into pieces and put them in a plate. Then, I added some yogurt to the plate and brought it to his bed with a fork.

"Your hand is injured, too. Don't move, and I'll feed you."

I just wanted to do something for him and didn't give it much thought.

But I never expected that when I forked a piece of apple and handed it to Quinn's mouth, he gazed at me with tears in his eyes.
