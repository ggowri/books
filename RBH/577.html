Then, I heard a loud splash as someone jumped into the sea. Immediately, I felt a strong hand gripping my arm tightly.

"Madeline, hold on, it's me!"

A voice, incredibly familiar, whispered in my ear.

The actions, the sounds, everything felt like a replay of a past life. It seemed as though I had experienced this before, and now I was reliving it once again.

The icy seawater stung my eyes, making it impossible to open them. I tried several times to see the face of the person rescuing me, but no matter what, I couldn't make out the features.

I could only sense strongly that a great force was pulling me forward. After a while, the water around me finally turned into solid ground, as if I had been placed on a small boat.

The seawater on my face and body was quickly wiped dry by someone. I tried to open my eyes but found myself too weak to even muster the strength to do so.

Yet, my mind was unusually clear. I could feel the boat rocking, and more importantly, I could sense someone holding me tightly, calling my name, urging me to open my eyes.

In a daze, I heard him shouting, "Madeline, I will never let the accident of the past life happen again..."

This was the last sentence I faintly heard before losing consciousness. However, the roaring of the waves and the sea breeze were too loud for me to hear clearly.

When I woke up again, I found myself in a luxurious room, dressed in clean and dry clothes, with my hair also dry.

Everything felt like a dream. If not for the soreness in my body, I would have almost believed that none of it had happened.

I opened my eyes, propping myself up on the bed, trying to sit up.

Perhaps my voice disturbed the person waiting in the room, as I saw a tall figure rushing over, placing a pillow under my head after supporting my shoulder.

His voice was unusually gentle. "Madeline, you're finally awake. You scared me."

"Quinn?" I stared at the man in front of me, surprised, and asked, "How come it's you? Where am I now?"

He clearly knew what I was most concerned about, immediately reassuring me, "Still on the yacht, you fell into the sea, but luckily I arrived in time to save you. Don't worry, both you and the child are fine."

I thought, "The child is fine, that's a stroke of luck in misfortune."

Upon hearing his words, I quickly touched my abdomen, then asked him in surprise, "But how did you come to the yacht?"

Quinn said, "I saw Auntie Grey bring you here, which made me furious. Worried about you, I directly took a small boat to find you. I didn't expect to see you fall into the water as soon as I arrived, fortunately I made it in time. By the way, why did you fall into the water?"

At his question, I suddenly recalled what happened before I fell into the water.

In that instant, I became agitated.

I quickly asked him, "Where is Philip now?"
