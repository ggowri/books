That's how it goes in social settings. Once you've made a name for yourself, you become a topic of conversation, even if you don't utter a word.

These folks usually carry themselves with an air of arrogance. They wouldn't bother to mingle with the common crowd, yet now, they're all reaching out, eager to make my acquaintance.

I extended my hand, shaking each of theirs in turn, before Grey introduced me to the group. Then, the game was on.

Grey pulled me into her squad, and together we hopped into a buggy, heading towards the field.

She looked at me, her brows furrowed, and despite herself, she asked:

"Madeline, what's the deal with you and Elliott? Weren't you seeing Quinn?"

Not wanting to cause any more confusion, I explained to Grey about Elliott's injury and his current condition.

Upon hearing this, Grey had a sudden moment of clarity:

"So, you're just helping Elliott get better, right? That's good, just don't blur the lines, okay? Quinn is a one-woman man. Once he's chosen you, there's no turning back. If his heart gets broken, it's not something he can easily recover from."

I nodded, "Yeah, I got it."

Grey patted my shoulder, noticing my slight despondency. With a smile, he said to me:

"Alright, since you're here, just enjoy the game. These folks are the big shots of Hoswich City. Mingling with them will do you good."

"After speaking, Grey quickly pulled me along to join the squad,"

I seldom play golf, but I've learned it before, so I haven't forgotten how to swing. After a few simple tries, I found my groove.

Following Grey, we pressed on. By the time we reached the third opening, a sudden burst of applause and gasps echoed from the crowd.

From a distance, I spotted a tall figure in white, skillfully swinging his club under the sun.

The sun's warmth bathed him, dusting his slightly bronzed skin with a faint layer of gold. He wore a golf cap, obscuring his face from my view. Yet, even from his silhouette alone, I recognized him instantly.

It's Elliott.

Unexpectedly, he showed up too.

And believe it or not, he actually showed up wearing the same tracksuit as me.

Grey caught sight of it too. She looked at Elliott, then at me, surprise etched on her face:

"Madeline, what's going on? I invited Elliott before, he clearly said he couldn't make it, so how...?"

Grey instantly understood what was happening. A knot formed in her stomach as she quickly moved forward.

She was a master at socializing, even if she didn't particularly like someone, she never let it show. With a warm smile, she would say:

"Mr. Bryant, what brings you to the court today? We've invited you before, but you never showed up."

At that moment, Elliott took a swing. He looked dashing, utterly captivating as he played. His stroke was decisive, not a hint of hesitation.

The ball soared far into the distance, tracing a beautiful arc in the sky. A collective gasp echoed from the crowd:

"Nice shot! Mr. Bryant is so cool!"

Elliott gave a slight nod, his gaze fixed on me from a distance. His lips curled into a subtle smile, and in his profound eyes, there was a deep-seated affection.

"I'll always stick with my wife. Otherwise, if someone snatches her away, I'd be heartbroken for life."
