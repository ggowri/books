Sean finally reacted and immediately called for emergency help.

Elliott held Giselle. He initially intended to place her on the sofa, but Giselle held onto his collar tightly, like a struggling kitten on the brink of death, emanating a sense of helplessness and frailty.

Such a situation, surely no man's heart would remain unaffected, right?

I stood there, clasping my hands tightly, staring blankly as Elliott, who right before my eyes, tenderly cared for another woman who carrying his child.

Her complexion was so pale, her expression so moving, her body so delicate, and the crimson at the hem of her skirt so vivid.

In the face of life, his actions were understandable, yet why did my heart ache so much? It hurt so much that it made me almost breathless, almost unable to stand.

Giselle kept making fragile sounds, completely capturing Elliott's attention.

Elliott seemed flustered too. He probably didn't expect that a slight push from him would lead to such serious consequences.

At this critical moment, he didn't consider me, let alone what I might be thinking.

He kept looking down at Giselle, and seeing her in pain, he couldn't help but comfort her.

"The ambulance will be here soon, just hold on a little longer."

"Elliott, our child... Will our child be okay? You must save it, please save it."

Giselle grabbed his collar, as if holding onto a lifeline, her eyes filled with tears, so pitiful.

I saw Elliott's lips move. He pondered for a moment and finally said, "The child will be fine."

The child... their child.

That word was like a curse, swirling constantly in my mind.

My body staggered again. Thankfully, Sean noticed my distress, walked over, and supported me.

The sound of the ambulance could be heard at this moment.

Upon hearing it, without hesitation, Elliott picked up Giselle and rushed out like an arrow. Nicole also followed closely behind.

I felt dizzy, my mind blank. At this critical moment, I didn't know what to think, I just followed my instincts and went after them.

The ambulance didn't wait for me. It flew away with Elliott and Nicole.

My heart felt like it had been stabbed by countless knives, bloody and heavy. Yet despite this, I couldn't give up, I had to go and see what was happening.

I quickly grabbed a car from the garage, stepped on the gas, and sped out of Serenity Villa.

I chased after the ambulance, speeding all the way to Bryant Hospital.

I saw Giselle being carried on a stretcher, with Elliott escorting her all the way, and they quickly disappeared through the gates of Bryant Hospital.

By the time I parked and reached the emergency room's entrance, the operating room was already flashing red lights, with Elliott and Nicole sitting on either side of the resuscitation room.

Elliott looked utterly dejected. His hands and shirt still bore specks of blood, looking so distressing.

I instinctively wanted to approach. But at that moment, a sharp voice echoed in the corridor.

"How is Giselle? Is the child okay? Oh my god, how could this happen..."

It was Laura's voice.

She was wearing high heels, and her footsteps sounded rushed.

As she passed by me, she forcefully pushed me aside and rushed towards Elliott, asking in panic.

"Mom, how did you find out?"

Elliott stood up. Seeing Laura running over, he looked somewhat surprised.

"What have you done? Why did Giselle have such an accident? She's carrying the heir of our Bryant family! If anything happens, you'll see how I deal with you."
