I walked over to his bedside.

Laura hurriedly adjusted his bed and helped him sit up. Then, Jasper asked Laura to leave the room.

"Is there something I can't hear?"

Laura Bryant's tone was full of dissatisfaction.

"If I tell you to leave, you leave. I need to talk to Madeline alone."

Jasper's stern face left Laura no choice but to leave the room reluctantly, closing the door behind her.

When I was married to Elliott, Jasper often remained silent. On many events, even when present, he acted like a background figure.

Most of the time, it was Laura who made a fuss, or when Mr. Cormac Bryant was around, he took control of the conversation.

So, I seldom faced Jasper alone and had no idea what he wanted to talk today.

"How are you feeling?"

Out of courtesy, I asked.

"My health has been deteriorating over the past two years, but Dr. Jones has given me a treatment plan to see if I can recover. Madeline, I wanted to talk to you about you and Elliott."

Jasper Bryant spoke calmly, looking at me with a kind expression. It was the first time he addressed me so intimately as "Madeline."

"Elliott and I are over. What's there to talk about?"

I remained detached, but I could see his purpose for seeking me out.

"Elliott has lost his memory and forgotten many things. He talked to me a lot last night. He said he wants to leave the past behind and start anew with you.

If you're willing, the Bryant family can formally propose again, and your family can set the dowry. Would you consider accepting Elliott again with this offer?"

Jasper spoke slowly, and his words made me surprised.

He had always seemed extremely proud to me, even though he never spoke much. Many of Laura's actions were supported by him behind the scenes.

He must have thought I wasn't good enough for Elliott and that my family couldn't match the Bryant family's status.

I never expected him to lower himself so much now, offering to propose again.

I was surprised, I have to say, but I didn't feel moved. The past wounds couldn't be erased with a simple proposal.

"Sorry, I can't accept it, and neither can my parents. What’s done is done. You should know better than anyone how your wife forced me out of the Bryant family and how Mr. Cormac Bryant pushed for our divorce."

My tone was cold and calm.

Jasper sighed deeply:

"I know our family wronged you. My wife went too far back then. We were desperate because Cormac kept insisting on wanting a great-grandson, and you weren't getting pregnant.

We had no choice but to take drastic measures, and we were deceived by Giselle Cole, which led to your marriage falling apart.

I still feel sorry for it, especially seeing Elliott so lost after the divorce. My illness is caused by my inner pain."

Jasper Bryant bent a little for apology, coughed a while and continued:

"The Bryant family has few descendants. My father had only one son, and I only have Elliott. That’s why we place great importance on heirs. I hope you understand.

It was our fault back then. If you agree to be with Elliott again, our family is willing to provide a dowry and hold a grand wedding to bring you back with honor.

Also, I can transfer 20% of my shares to you as compensation from my wife and me."

I raised my eyebrows in disbelief.

A proposal?

A wedding?

20% of the shares?

This was no small gesture. Jasper was really giving an offer with great sincerity.

Bryant Group's 20% shares would make me the second-largest shareholder. Elliott, who controls Bryant Group, doesn't even own half of its shares.

20% of the shares is a huge fortune. With Bryant Group's current financial strength, the annual dividends alone would ensure a comfortable life for me.

"Aren't you worried that even if I remarry Elliott, things might not work out?"

I looked at Jasper, incredulous.

Jasper Bryant smiled slightly and looked at me sincerely:

"If Elliott loses you again after remarrying, that's his problem. I know the fault wasn't with Elliott; it was us who made mistakes. Since we caused the problem, we should fix it.

I only have one child, and I don't want him to be unhappy for life. Especially now, with the blood clot in his brain, I'm afraid if this continues, if any mishap happened to him, our wealth would become meaningless without him."

Jasper has a clearer logic than Laura.

He had a better sense of perspective than Laura Bryant does.

It's a pity he didn't have such foresight back then. If he did, Elliott and I wouldn't be where we are today.

But he's right. Back then, Elliott and I were so close. If they hadn't forced Giselle Cole to have a child, Elliott and I wouldn't have ended up divorced.

And if we hadn't divorced, they would already be enjoying a happy life with their grandchildren, instead of begging me to go back to Elliott now.

Everything has its reason.

If only they knew what mistake they were making.

"You're offering so much. Aren't you worried that I might be infertile and ruin the Bryant family's plans for an heir?"

I said, with a hint of sarcasm.

Jasper seemed to have anticipated my question. He replied calmly:

"Speaking of that, it’s only my wife to blame. She was convinced you were infertile based on a single report. Otherwise, your child would have already been part of the Bryant family.

If you remarry, I do hope you and Elliott can have more children. But if... you really can't, we are willing to accept your current son and raise him as the Bryant family's third-generation heir."

Jasper Bryant's words shocked me. I raised my eyebrows:

"That child isn't Elliott's biological son, and you don't mind?"
