I hastily escorted him out of the private room. Watching his flustered retreat, I couldn't help but smirk. It seemed that even those who were audacious were afraid of falling into traps one day.

Tonight, I showed Rickson those photos and videos on purpose as a favor to him.

I could have made them public, but there was no need to. I understood better than anyone that it was better to have an ally than an extra enemy.

Now, it was up to Rickson to make his next move.

I stood at the doorway of the private room, arms crossed, watching as Rickson's figure slowly disappeared.

For some reason, even though I tried hard to drink less tonight, it seemed like my tolerance for alcohol was waning. I had barely more than a glass of red wine, yet I felt the alcohol striking me, causing an intense discomfort in my stomach.

Realizing something was off, I quickly made my way to the restroom. I rushed, my head spinning, and suddenly, I bumped hard into someone's embrace.

"I'm sorry, I..."

I began to apologize, but then I caught a whiff of a familiar, light, masculine scent.

Suddenly, someone wrapped their arms around my shoulders, and I heard a familiar, concerned voice.

"It's you? What are you doing here?"

I looked up to see Elliott standing in front of me, clad in a sleek black suit.

He appeared to have been drinking. His breath carried the scent of alcohol, and a flush from the liquor slightly blurred his usually stern face. His typically cold eyes now held a touch of warmth.

I was taken aback.

It was a small world. I hadn't expected to run into him here.

But at the moment, I couldn't afford to chat, as a sharp discomfort in my stomach made me impatient.

Without time for explanations, I quickly pushed him away and dashed to the restroom sink, where I proceeded to vomit noisily into the basin.

After throwing up, my severely upset stomach finally felt a bit of relief.

Strangely, I wondered if it was a stomach problem or if my health was deteriorating. Not only had my appetite worsened, but I also frequently experienced acid reflux. Sometimes, even brushing my teeth in the morning triggered retching.

It seemed after this busy period, I really needed to get a thorough health check-up at the hospital.

A flicker of confusion passed through my mind. Just then, someone from behind handed me a bottle of mineral water and two tissues.

"Why did you drink so much? Who were you drinking with tonight?"

Elliott's voice rose again from behind me, his hand patting my back gently as he spoke.

I had left my suit in the private room. At the moment, I was only wearing a strapless long dress. His fingers felt ice cold on my bare skin, a touch too intimate.

I took the bottle of water, took a big gulp, and rinsed my mouth before wiping the corners of my lips with a tissue. Quickly, I stepped back to maintain a safe distance from him.
