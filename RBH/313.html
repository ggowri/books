Quinn seemed overly excited, so he rushed in without paying attention to what was happening in the hospital room and immediately exclaimed.

He waved a stack of documents, his expression unusually cheerful. It was evident that there had been a breakthrough in my father's accident case.

But in an instant, he noticed Elliott standing tall like a mountain in the hospital room, and his smile froze.

"Oh, Elliott, you're here," Quinn greeted immediately. However, perhaps sensing the heavy atmosphere in the room, his expression sobered, and he subconsciously shifted his gaze towards me.

"What's going on? What happened?" Elliott slowly turned around, his face filled with anger as he looked at me.

"So, you'd rather seek Quinn's help for everything than come to me, right?" Clearly, Quinn's earlier words had plunged Elliott into a more profound misunderstanding.

I was stunned for a moment, unsure of what to say. I could only explain, "Well, mainly because you're busier, and he's less occupied."

Quinn seemed visibly awkward. I knew he wasn't idle, but he had always treated my affairs as his own. "Yeah, I guess I'm less busy. You're busy with company matters every day and don't have time for these trivial things."

Quinn realized the discord between Elliott and me and quickly tried to explain.

However, Elliott gave Quinn a cold look. With his hands in his pockets, he seemed shrouded in a thick fog of disdain.

"In her eyes, anyone is more important than me, including you," Elliott spat out coldly before pushing Quinn aside abruptly. Then, he left the hospital room in giant strides without a moment's hesitation.

Quinn was caught off guard by Elliott's forceful push and stumbled for a moment. He instinctively grabbed the door frame to steady himself, looking at me with a mix of amusement and bewilderment. "Did I just walk into a minefield again? It's really unfair. I didn't do anything."

"Elliott always seems to be in such a bad mood nowadays," I shook my head, unable to help but comment.

However, as soon as I remembered Quinn's mention of new developments in the case, I couldn't afford to dwell on what Elliott might be thinking.

I quickly asked Quinn, "You mentioned there's been new progress in the case. What is it? Tell me now."

"Madeline, you should go after Elliott first and explain things to him properly. It's not good for you two to be at odds like this." My mom, seeing that I wasn't concerned about Elliott, became so anxious that she even stamped her foot, urging me.

"Mom, it's okay. I know where to draw the line between us." I shook my head and reassured her.

Without waiting for my mom to say anything more, I dragged Quinn out of the hospital room. We hurried to a corner of the corridor, and I immediately asked, "Is there any new lead regarding the driver?"
