Lily had witnessed the entire exchange between Elliott and me. She couldn't help but chuckle as she watched our playful banter and called out from behind.

"Madeline, be careful not to twist your ankle in those high heels. Don't rush!"

"Mom, it's okay. We're off."

I replied and hurried out the door.

Even though Elliott was still irritated, he patiently waited for me to get into the car before signaling Toby to drive off.

I sat beside Elliott, and he instinctively squeezed my hand tightly. At that moment, I noticed something peculiar. Toby, the usually stoic and focused driver, briefly glanced at us through the rearview mirror.

His seemed to be weirdly surprised. However, he only took a quick glance and then concentrated on driving.

Toby had been Elliott's driver for many years, known for his honesty and reserved nature. Despite being half-siblings with Giselle, he was different in character from her.

The fleeting glance he shot me was odd, but with Elliott present, I chose to let it go without question.

Soon, Toby pulled up at the Bryant Group.

Elliott exited the car first before offering me his hand to assist me.

What surprised me, however, was his subsequent gesture. For the first time, he extended his arm to invite me to take it.

Elliott typically shied away from public displays of affection.

I couldn't help but feel a little startled. Finally, I took his arm and accompanied him into Bryant Group.

While I had visited the Bryant Group on numerous occasions, it was the first time I did so arm-in-arm with Elliott.

Employees turned to stare at us, their faces reflecting surprise.

Greetings filled the air, accompanied by whispered discussions about us.

"Mr. Bryant brought his wife to work today? "

"Mrs. Bryant is so slender, tall, and stunning. She seems completely transformed!"

"Did you know that Mrs. Bryant is preparing to inherit her father's company? She's now a powerhouse in the business world, a perfect match for Mr. Bryant."

"They seem so affectionate. Despite what people say about Mr. Bryant not loving Mrs. Bryant, I think they're very much in love."

"Oh, the hearts of the girls in the company who adored Mr. Bryant will be broken now. The sweetness between Mr. and Mrs. Bryant is undeniable."

...

These comments, though subtle, reached my ears.

However, I paid no attention to them.

Together, Elliott and I made our way to his office. The scent of lilies filled the air as we stepped inside.

"Elliott, you're here."

I heard a sweet and familiar voice with a hint of excitement in the office.

Giselle, dressed in a white professional suit, emerged from Elliott's office.
