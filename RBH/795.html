After a while, I felt a corner of the blanket being lifted. Then, Elliott crawled into the cup.

He switched off the lights in the room. Only the small bedside lamp, which I had once scavenged from abroad, remained lit. The lamp cast a multicolored glow, bathing the room in a spectrum of five different hues. Suddenly, the atmosphere in the room took on an air of ambiguity.

Seems like he hasn't thrown away a single thing I bought in the past.

No wonder he's developed insomnia, spending night after night here, haunted by memories.

Inwardly, I was musing. Yet, my eyes remained tightly shut. My body was immobile, but my consciousness was gradually awakening. So, I had no choice but to feign sleep to dodge the awkwardness.

But tonight, I found myself somewhat grateful that he was the one who picked me up.

If it were Quinn caught in an unexpected situation, I wouldn't know how to face him afterwards. It would only leave me more torn between a rock and a hard place.

After all, Elliott and I were once married. He was, to this day, my first man.

So, at this moment, even though I'm embarrassed, I'm still pretty grateful.

Still, I can't help but wonder, how did he just happen to show up at Quinn's club tonight? Is this a coincidence?

I'm in the dark.

Elliott extended his arm. Just like before, he pulled me into a tight embrace.

Under the dim, hazy light, he turned my face towards him. I kept my eyes shut, too scared to move, but I could still feel his intense gaze studying my face.

After such a long time apart, the two of them found themselves lying together once again.

We lay together in our old room, clad in our old matching pajamas. Everything seemed as if it had never changed, yet, everything had.

My calm heart is experiencing some ripples now, a mix of complex emotions.

I kept my eyes shut, because I honestly didn't know how to face Elliott.

After a moment of staring at me, suddenly, I felt a chill on my forehead. He had gently kissed me there.

Next, I heard him murmur softly:

"Madeline, I'm in love with you."

My heart nearly stopped dead in its tracks because of these few words.

A wave of tingles washed over my entire body.

I was caught off guard. He chose this moment to confess his feelings for me. And in my memory, he had never before uttered these three words so clearly and directly.

He really thought I was asleep, didn't he?

After he uttered those words, he pulled me into his arms with a satisfied grin. Soon, I could hear the steady rhythm of his breathing.

His affection was so profound, yet so restrained. It made me question, for a moment, whether he had truly lost his memory.

I distinctly remember him being quite wild in the past. He had absolutely no control over his desires.

But tonight, his performance left me stunned and bewildered.

But regardless, the restrained Elliott was far more acceptable to me than his wild side. So, in this moment, as he held me, I felt no repulsion. Instead, I was enveloped in a long-lost sense of warmth.

After a solitary slumber on a frigid winter's night, the warmth and comfort she feels when she finally shares her bed with someone else is undeniable.

A familiar scent enveloped me, providing an extraordinary sense of security. Before long, a wave of intense drowsiness washed over me. And just like that, nestled in his embrace, I drifted off to sleep...
