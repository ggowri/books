After I finished speaking, I turned around deliberately. My words were a declaration, telling Elliott that I no longer would be the woman who blindly followed him and revolved around his every whim.

I would focus on myself and my tiny goals. Austin's illness and the precarious state of Glory Group demanded my independence.

I no longer wanted to be immersed in romance.

Elliott once again grasped my waist and forced me to face him.

"You mean, you just treat me as your sexual partner? I can't interfere with you in any way?"

I could sense the annoyance in his tone. After all, I had never spoken in such a tone before.

"You can understand it that way. We just take what we need."

I knew my words would sting him, but I was resolute to inform him of my change from a woman who blindly loved him to an independent woman, even if I still loved him.

I needed him to understand that I was no longer the same woman who was obsessed with him. I didn't want to be controlled by anyone.

"We get what we need? What are you thinking? We are a legitimate couple!"

Elliott was clearly unhappy with my words. His grip on my waist tightened.

I couldn't help shouting.

"Isn't this what you wanted? Didn't you say before that you hated me relying on you? So, I am different."

Elliott fell silent. I remembered his same yells at me in the past.

I thought, "Now that I became independent and less clingy, he should be pleased.

But why was he depressed? He suddenly stopped talking. Was he sad?"

I was tired of the repeated sex with him. At this moment, I barely kept sober.

When I nearly fell asleep, he suddenly whispered in my ear.

"A woman can't be too independent. She always needs to rely on a man."

I couldn't believe his words.

It was a typical double standard.

He used to hate me for being too clingy. He wished me to be independent and stay away from him.

Now, he disagreed that I should be independent.

It seemed that what men wanted changed with their moods.

I thought, "I was no longer the woman who revolved around you all day long."

I fell directly into sleep and ignored his words.

In the morning, Elliott planned to go to Bryant Group after breakfast. He spoke of a medical project that he was working on at the Bryant Group.

This project would greatly affect Bryant Group's position in the medical field in the next two years. Therefore, he attached great importance to the project.

When I heard that it was a medical project, my mind crazily worked.

With the knowledge from my past life, I knew that investing in the medical field was the key to saving Glory Group from its current crisis.
