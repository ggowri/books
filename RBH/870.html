Quinn was originally holding a takeout box in his hand.

He just happened to catch me kissing Elliott, and as a result, the takeout box in his hand dropped to the ground, scattering sushi all over the place.

I froze for a moment, instinctively breaking free from Elliott's embrace.

"Quinn..."

I let out a shout.

Quinn gave me an incredulous look, then glanced at Elliott. As if understanding something, he quickly turned and walked out.

Watching his dejected figure, an instinctual urge to chase after him surged within me.

But behind me, a strong hand gripped me. Elliott's voice, raspy, questioned:

"Madeline, you're after Quinn Woods? What's the deal between you two?"

Elliott's question snapped me back to reality.

Right now, to Elliott, I'm his wife. If I were to chase after him now, it would definitely shock him. And if he faints again, things would only get worse.

"Don't overthink our friendship, I'm just trying to spare him the heartache."

I turned to look at Elliott, speaking in a subdued tone.

Elliott finally let out a sigh of relief. He walked over, his hand cradling my head, gently pulling me into his embrace:

"If you're just friends, don't act too concerned. There should be boundaries in human interactions. If you blur those lines, he might get the wrong idea."

Elliott cast a glance at the sushi on the ground. He knew my preferences, understood that those were among my favorites, and naturally, he grasped Quinn's intentions.

"Alright, I got it. You can head back to work now! I have a meeting to attend shortly."

I was still in a whirl of confusion, wishing he would leave promptly.

"Kicking me out again, huh?! Now you understand why I didn't want you showing up at the office before, right?"

He chuckled, playfully pinching my nose, his face full of affection.

"I get it. But don't worry, I won't be as needy as I used to be."

I quipped with a grin.

"I wish you were still the person you used to be. Now, you're like the wind, slipping through my fingers no matter how hard I try to hold on."

After speaking, Elliott lingered, planting a reluctant kiss on my forehead.

Five minutes later, he strolled out of my office.

I watched him step into the elevator, then returned to my office.

Adam had already had all the sushi on the ground cleaned up. He looked at me, seemingly struggling to find the right words. Finally, he couldn't hold back and said:

"When Quinn left just now, he seemed pretty down. Madeline, are you really considering getting back together with Mr. Bryant?"

"This isn't your concern, focus on your own tasks. Have you prepared the materials for the meeting as I asked?"

I'm at a loss for how to explain it to him.

Adam stated:

"I'm all set, ready to start the meeting. I just feel sorry for you. Quinn is such a great guy, once you miss him, he's gone."

I shot him a glare, not responding to his words, but instead, gesturing with my eyes for him to leave.

I sat alone in my office chair, the image of Quinn's gaze and his utterly desolate silhouette haunting me. It felt as though my heart was being torn apart.

I pulled out my phone, tapping on Quinn's icon, intending to explain things to him, but in the end, I just gave up.

I'm at a loss for words.
