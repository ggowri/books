In a heartbeat, Quinn's gaze landed on Elliott. They both stared each other down fiercely.

Elliott sat there, a smirk playing on his face, as if he still held the upper hand.

He rose to his feet, casually draping an arm around my shoulders.

"Madeline, I'll take you back to the office."

Quinn was watching us from behind.

I suddenly felt a stiffness in my body. However, thinking of his illness, I couldn't resist too obviously.

So, I had no choice but to pretend I had an itch. As I scratched, I subtly moved a bit further away from him.

The three of us exited the restaurant together, then rode the elevator down to the first floor.

Suddenly, a tall figure caught my eye, striding towards me. She was clad in a black trench coat, radiating an intense aura.

It's Carole.

I paused, my gaze meeting hers. Without missing a beat, I exclaimed:

"Carole!"

"Ah, it's you guys. Just finished eating, huh? What a coincidence. I've heard good things about the newly opened SkyPark restaurant. I was thinking of taking my mom there to try it out."

Quinn stepped forward without hesitation. She was about to smile, but then, she caught sight of Elliott. In an instant, her smile faded.

She shot Elliott a meaningful glance. Then, she turned to her younger brother, her face etched with confusion.

She must be puzzled. How come I'm still hanging out with Elliott and Quinn, even after all these years since Elliott and I divorced?

"Sis, I was grabbing a bite with Madeline and we bumped into Elliott. Didn't you say you were bringing Mom along? Where is she?"

Quinn caught the confusion in his sister's eyes. He stepped forward, explaining calmly.

"Quinn, Madeline, what brings you two here? Have you eaten yet? If not, let's grab a bite together, come on—"

No sooner had Quinn finished speaking than his mom walked in through the front door.

Seeing me standing with Quinn, her tone was warm. She came forward and affectionately wrapped her arm around mine.

She didn't even notice Elliott, her eyes were all on me, her face filled with a warm, loving smile. She said to me:

"We've been wanting to visit you these past few weeks, but Quinn kept stopping me. He said you needed rest and we shouldn't disturb you. Just like that, a month has passed. How are you feeling now?"

The moment Quinn's mom laid eyes on me, there was an immediate sense of warmth and familiarity. Her care and concern kindled a comforting warmth within me.

However, realizing that Elliott was standing right there, I hastily cut her off:

"Auntie, I'm doing well, thank you for your concern. You and Carole go ahead and start dinner, we've already eaten. The food upstairs is indeed quite good."

"Alright, anyone up for seconds?"

Quinn's mom didn't sense anything amiss. She asked me again, her smile unwavering.

At that moment, a cough echoed from behind me. It was Elliott. He stepped forward, deliberately taking my hand in front of Quinn's mom and Carole. His eyes narrowed slightly:

"No need, Madeline just had a full meal. Auntie, thank you and Carole for your care towards Madeline."

Beneath Elliott's calm facade, it seemed as if a potent resentment was simmering.

His words left both Carole and her mother utterly astounded. As they looked at our intertwined hands, shock was written all over the faces of Quinn's mother and Carole...
