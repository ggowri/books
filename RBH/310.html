Elliott?

The sudden warmth made me tremble uncontrollably for a moment.

This week, he hardly contacted me at all. And I had been busy, never reaching out to him either.

Although we had moved past the initial stage of our relationship, it was as if there was still a barrier between us.

I still couldn't bring myself to depend on him or seek his help.

Subconsciously, I turned my head and saw Elliott's face, as handsome as a sculpture. The familiar scent of his cologne filled my nose.

Just as I turned my head, he leaned in, lifting my chin with one hand, and before I could react, he deeply kissed my lips...

There was no conversation between us. He didn't seem interested in hearing anything from me either.

His lips carried a faint hint of mint as they moved against mine. Then, his tongue slipped into my mouth, eagerly exploring and intertwining with mine.

He seemed to be urgently expressing his longing for me through his actions. But he left my mind momentarily blank as if short-circuited.

What was he doing?

This was in front of my dad's hospital bed!

Was he trying to show how much he missed me? But if that was the case, why didn't he call or message me all week?

I felt abrupt subconsciously. Even though I knew my dad was unconscious, I still felt uncomfortable and instinctively pushed him away.

Only then did he release me. I instinctively stood up, retreating two steps with a defensive stance, and unconsciously grasped the edge of the bedside table with both hands.

My face felt flushed, and he slightly disheveled my hair. I smoothed the stray strands behind my head and couldn't help but blurt out, "Elliott, what are you doing?"

"What am I doing?" He seemed puzzled by my question, his eyebrows furrowing. "Kissing my wife. Is that not allowed?"

"Are you... done?"

My heart continued to thump. For a moment, I didn't know what to say.

"It seems like, this past week, you haven't missed me,"

His eyes, deep and filled with disappointment, were fixed on me.

I lifted my head, meeting his eyes directly. I noticed the heavy weariness etched on his face. His eyes, usually bright and lively, were now tinged with redness.

What had he been doing this week?

Why did he look so tired?

I silently wondered. But outwardly, my demeanor remained indifferent.

"Sorry, I've been busy with Glory Group matters and taking care of my dad this week. I haven't had time to think about anything else."

My words clearly sparked strong displeasure in him. He immediately closed the distance between us, his gaze bearing down on me with intense pressure.

"So, in your eyes, I'm just something else?"

I felt a sense of helplessness.

Lately, it seemed like, no matter what I said, Elliott could find fault with it.

He used to never pay attention to what I said or did, but now, every word and action of mine seemed open to misinterpretation.

I struggled to find the right words.

"What I meant was, I've been really busy. My dad's been through several surgeries and still hasn't woken up. Glory Group has been chaotic. Plus, you haven't reached out to me either," I explained patiently.

But Elliott still frowned deeply, his hand lifting my chin, his gaze scrutinizing me.

"That's because I've been waiting for you to reach out to me. Haven't you noticed? You never initiate contact with me anymore."
