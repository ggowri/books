I was slightly tipsy, and this feeling of slight tipsiness made my blood warm all over my body, no longer like before in the confines of marriage, cold and oppressive.

Once upon a time, I had no desire to post on Instagram, but now I really want to capture my mood.

I aimed my phone at myself and took a blushing photo, then posted it directly on Instagram, along with the hot and spicy meal and the figures of the two fresh-faced boys across from me. Afterward, I added the lyrics of the song I heard on my way here. Nothing can stop your longing for freedom.

I did not slap Adam and Rowan's faces, but shortly after I posted it on Instagram, my long-dormant Instagram exploded again.

Paige once again insisted that I share my location, while Fiona relentlessly interrogated me about the identities of the two handsome guys across from us.

Quinn left a comment on my Instagram, saying that not inviting him to dinner was not a bad review. Surprisingly, Philip also left a comment, saying, "Your life is truly colorful."

I couldn't help but laugh as I watched this bunch of mischievous friends commenting so joyfully under my Instagram post.

And just when I was feeling particularly happy, Giselle suddenly sent me a private message.

Her first words were surprisingly accusatory: "Madeline, is that Rowan across from you? You actually had dinner with Rowan?"

These sharp words made my happy heart instantly unhappy, and I typed a few words: "Yes, what's wrong?"

She quickly replied to me, "Why did you have dinner with Rowan? Did you do it on purpose?"

I stared at the screen, the last few words in her speech made me even more unhappy. I replied, "I don't understand what you mean by 'intentional'."

Giselle quickly sent another message, and it was a series of texts: "Were you intentionally getting close to my ex-boyfriend because you were unhappy that Elliott took me to the hospital? Did you post this Instagram deliberately to show me, right?"

What a strong and assertive tone of declaring sovereignty.

I looked at this series of words and suddenly felt a kind of indescribable glare, and at this moment, Rowan's phone rang.

He looked at the screen, his expression clearly changed, and then he immediately walked to the side to answer the phone.

No need to say, it must have been Giselle who called.

Adam glanced at me and, until that moment, he still didn't understand what had happened. He thought I hadn't eaten enough and said to me, "Madeline, why are you staring? Eat a little more, this restaurant is authentic."

My heart was already burning hot, I had no desire to eat anymore. I shook my head, indicating that I was already full.

And at that moment, Rowan also returned, he sat down and looked at me with a troubled expression on his face. After a while, he stammered and asked me, "Madeline, did...did you know my girlfriend?"

"I can't say that I knew her well, we weren't very familiar." Of course, I was reluctant to admit it, after all, I didn't want to get to know a third party who had interfered in my marriage.

"Just now she called and said she saw you on Instagram, and she questioned me why I had dinner with you. I explained the reason, and she said she wanted to talk to you. Madeline, Giselle has a more aggressive temper, so if she says something she shouldn't, please don't stoop to her level." Rowan quickly explained, both his words and tone revealing his instinctive fear of Giselle.

It seems that Giselle had complete control over Rowan, otherwise, he wouldn't have been like a mouse seeing a cat when she called him.

Rowan had just finished speaking when, as expected, Giselle sent another message: "Rowan said you introduced him to his lawyer job, Madeline. I had no idea you knew my boyfriend so early. I'm truly surprised."
