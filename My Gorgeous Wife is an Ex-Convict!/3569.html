Jasper quickly said, "I'll help you throw it away!" and then picked up the empty milk tea cup and left quickly.

Roger's eyes flickered as he stared at her back, seemingly lost in thought.

Jasper walked quickly until she was sure she was out of Roger's sight, then carefully put on a disposable surgical glove and carefully pulled out the straw Roger had used from the milk tea cup, intending to put it in the storage bag she had prepared.

Just then, a voice suddenly sounded, "What are you doing?"

Jasper's hand shook and the straw fell to the ground.

The habit of dropping things on the ground is considered contaminated. Even if it is picked up again, it cannot be considered a good sample for extracting DNA!

Jasper felt a wave of regret in her heart. As she looked up, she met Roger's pretty face.

"I...I didn't do anything," she said, her voice revealing a sense of guilt.

"Is that so?" He approached, crouched down, and picked up the straw from the ground. "What were you planning to do with this straw?"

"Well...aren't we friends? So I wanted to collect some things from my friends," she finally managed to come up with this reason. But after saying it out loud, she herself felt that it was a lame excuse.

"I can't tell, you have this strange quirk of collecting straws, huh?" he said.

She sweated profusely, feeling like a pervert in his mouth.

"What about you? Why are you here? Shouldn't you be sitting on the rest area side?" Jasper asked.

"I just thought it was strange that there's a trash can on the rest area side, but you insist on coming here to throw it away. So I followed to take a look, and I didn't expect you to have such a hobby," he said.

She was speechless by his words.

He threw the straw in his hand into the trash can and said, "Alright, I've bought the book I was looking for. Let's go back."

Jasper bit her lip, feeling frustrated. It was her first attempt, but it ended in failure.

The two of them walked out of the bookstore, but Roger suddenly stopped in front of a shop window on their way back.

Jasper was surprised to see that the window displayed many dolls, including a three-story wooden dollhouse and various wooden furniture.

These things are usually played with by girls pretending to be homemakers.

Roger, on the other hand, was watching with great concentration, his gaze as if he was reminiscing about something.

"Are you interested in playing house?" she asked curiously.

After all, it's usually girls who are interested in it, and it's rare for boys to be interested, especially since Roger is already a college student.

He turned his head, his black eyes looking at her deeply, and in his gaze, there seemed to be a kind of reproach.
