.

 

Veronica got on the car and saw Grace sitting in the back seat.

"Auntie Grace," Veronica said.

"I heard you've been living alone in your old house lately?" Grace asked.

"Yes," she replied.

"Then let me take you there," Grace said, instructing the driver in the front seat.

The car slowly drove away from the school.

"Why do you want to leave home and live in the old house?" Grace asked.

"I feel it's quieter over there. Besides, I'm already grown up now. Even if I live alone, it's okay. And when my mom passed away, she left me a bank account with some money in it, so my life won't be a problem," Veronica said.

Grace looked at the girl in front of her and felt like she had changed again, as if she had grown up a lot overnight.

However, sometimes a person's sudden growth is often accompanied by hardships and pain.

At this thought, Grace felt even more heartbroken for the girl in front of her.

Arriving at Veronica's place, Grace looked around the old house. It was a 60-square-meter house with two bedrooms and a living room. It was not bad for one person to live in, but the decoration inside the house was over a decade old and quite outdated.

"Veronica, I came to see you today to tell you that even though Mason is not with you now, I am still your Auntie Grace. If you have any difficulties, don't hesitate to come to me," Grace said.

Veronica was stunned for a moment, looking at Grace in a daze.

Grace rubbed Veronica's head and said, "I am grateful for your protection of Mason in the past. I didn't expect him to go abroad for hypnosis, but now... Anyway, I hope you are doing well. If you need me to intervene in your relationship with your family, just let me know."

"No need," Veronica interrupted Grace, "I know that my father and stepmother have done many bad things under the name of the Reed family. They have caused a lot of damage to the reputation of the Reed family. So, the Reed family doesn't need to do anything for the Barlow family anymore. And now that I have moved out, I don't want to go back to that house."

Veronica paused and said seriously, "Auntie Grace, I also want to change my life and try to be independent."

Seeing the seriousness in Veronica's eyes, Grace sighed, "Okay, but you have to promise me that if you have any difficulties, you will come to me."

"Hmm," Veronica nodded.

The two chatted for a while longer, and just as Grace was about to leave, Veronica suddenly asked, "Auntie Grace, how is Mason doing now?"

Grace hesitated for a moment, then slowly replied, "Same as before, I rarely talk or converse with people. But he plans to go to a foreign school after graduating from high school."

"Really? Well, it's understandable since he has strong academic abilities. It's only natural for him to attend a prestigious foreign school," Veronica tried hard to put a smile on her face, but at this moment, her throat was bitterly tight.

Tap! Tap!

Tears slid down her cheeks, she didn't know when they started.

Pain spread from her chest to her whole body, silently telling her that the distance between her and him would only grow larger in the future!
