Chapter 3943

Did he not find her attractive since they didn't have sex?

"Why?" she asked, her words already one step ahead of her emotions.

"If you really want to sleep with me, then don't get so drunk. You don't think I would want to hold a woman who's shaking all over from being drunk, do you?" Mick said.

Nancy was speechless.

"And..." Mick paused, looking at her with a sharpness in his eyes. "Nancy, do you really like me?"

A chill ran down her spine for a moment. "Yes, I really do," she said, trying to suppress her panic and not avoid his gaze.

His lips curved slightly. "I hate being deceived, so during the time we spend together, I hope you won't deceive me. If you want something, just tell me directly, but don't lie to me. Because people who lie to me usually don't end up well."

Nancy's body trembled slightly, and she forced a smile. "I know, I won't lie to you."

...

Mick's warning echoed in her mind.

But there were some deceptions that had been there from the beginning, and they could only be carried through to the end.

But... was liking him really a lie? Although at first, she didn't have much of a feeling for Mick, at most she just thought he was the high and mighty Reed family's young master, someone who had nothing to do with her. And... his appearance, he just looked pleasing to the eye.

As long as she could really like Mick in the future, then it wouldn't be considered a lie.

And Mick was already a man who was easy to make a woman's heart flutter, it wasn't hard to like him!

Yes, it wasn't hard!

Nancy kept telling herself this in her heart.

Thanks to her persistence, Mick stopped coming to pick her up at the hotel where she worked. They would usually agree on a meeting place, and sometimes Mick would send a driver to pick her up.

At that time, Nancy would have the driver wait for her 500 meters away from the company.

The rumors about her in the hotel finally died down.

Some people would mock her behind her back, saying that rich people were just playing with her and wouldn't take her seriously. They would get tired of her in no time.

Nancy didn't pay any attention to this kind of ridicule.

It was the new receptionist, Irene, who took the initiative to befriend her and apologized for bumping into her before.

"I was in a bad mood that day, so I accidentally bumped into you and didn't apologize in time. I'm really sorry." Irene looked guilty and even gave Nancy a teddy bear as a gift. "This little gift is just a token of my apology."

"You don't have to give me a gift. It was just a bump, and you've already apologized. It's okay." Nancy politely declined.

"No, please accept the gift. It represents your acceptance of my apology. If you don't take it, I'll feel like you haven't forgiven me." Irene insisted and handed the teddy bear to Nancy.

"Okay then..." Nancy hesitated for a moment before accepting the gift.

"After work, let's have dinner and go shopping together. I haven't been in the company for long and haven't made any friends yet. I hope we can be friends in the future." Irene smiled and suggested.

"I'm afraid I can't today. I have something to do after work. Maybe another day." Nancy declined.
