Martin Weiss put down the phone, went to a secret door in the room, and pushed it open.

It was a square room about ten square feet.

However, there was a mannequin in the middle of the room that wore a white wedding dress, veil, shoes, and others. It was a complete set.

He had the wedding dress custom-made according to Kyla's size.

However, he had never shown Kyla the wedding dress, so she was unaware that a wedding dress like this even existed.

Photos of Kyla were hung on the walls around the room. There were photos of her smiling at the camera as well as those that were taken candidly.

The greatest difference was that the photos of her facing the camera were photos from when they were dating. At that time, her smile was bright and gentle as if she had nothing to worry about.

In the photos that were secretly taken after, worry seemed to cloud her eyes.

He should have emptied the room.

Since he no longer had feelings for Kyla, there was no need for him to keep the photos and wedding dress.

However, he was somewhat reluctant to do it as if he had some sort of nostalgia.

What was he nostalgic about?

"Kyla, I don't love you anymore," he murmured as if to remind himself of this reality.
