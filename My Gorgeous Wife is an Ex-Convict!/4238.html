Chapter 4236 Unexpected News

"Chandler, do you really love me?" Tina thought and asked.

"Hmm, I love you," he answered without hesitation.

"Then why can you easily let go? Can you easily decide to divorce me? Or do you think that divorcing me doesn't matter to you, that you'll only feel a little sad and then move on?" Tina asked, puzzled.

The more she understood this man's emotions, the more she felt like she couldn't understand them.

Chandler chuckled self-deprecatingly. How could it be easy?

How could it only be a little sad?

It was just that this time, he didn't want her to make her choice out of sympathy or a sense of responsibility towards him.

If the previous Tina made that choice because she loved him, then this time, he hoped for the same.

Once a person has experienced something good, they won't easily accept something slightly worse.

This was also his last bit of pride and self-esteem.

"I'll be fine. If you don't love me, but still stay bound to me by marriage, then you'll feel miserable, and I don't want to have a wife who doesn't love me. So if you still can't love me in the end, let's get a divorce," he said.

Tina frowned at Chandler, feeling that something was off, but she couldn't pinpoint it.

And so, Tina and Chandler spent their days together. It couldn't be denied that Chandler treated her really well and understood her well.

Even though she had lost her memory, her body's preferences hadn't changed.

What she liked to eat, drink, how she liked to dress, and what movies or songs she liked, Chandler knew them well.

The feeling of being with Chandler was comfortable and familiar...

Yes, it was familiar!

It was like a natural blending, a habit formed over a long time at a subconscious level.

For example, he would casually feed her fruits from the fruit bowl, and she would naturally eat them as if they were only natural.

This way of getting along also strengthened her attachment to him unconsciously.

Maybe falling in love with this kind of man really happened quickly.

Perhaps a year, or even half a year or a few months, was enough.

Tina thought this way.

After having a good meal with Chandler at a restaurant, Tina went to the restroom.

Her body was gradually recovering, and there was also good news from the hospital. In a few more days, Quinn would be able to be discharged.

Thinking of her daughter, Tina felt her heart surge with strong emotions. In her most difficult days, it was this child who accompanied her.

When she had always been trying to protect her child, it was also this child who supported her, allowing her to have some kind of belief in her heart even when she lost all her memories, without giving up on herself.

If it weren't for this child, she wouldn't have made up her mind to escape from the village.

And she wouldn't have been saved by Kit...

Kit...

When this name flashed through Tina's mind, a pain still struck her heart, just as intense as before.

"By the way, have you seen the news? It says Kit seems to be disabled."

"I saw a former idol star, who used to be so glamorous, but I never expected that he would become disabled after retiring. It's really sad."

The conversation between the two women reached Tina's ears.
