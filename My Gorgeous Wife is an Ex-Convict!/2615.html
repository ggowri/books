Grace said nothing, but her expression seemed to be agreeing tacitly.

The bitterness on his face thickened. "Yeah, I don't understand you. I never... understood you."

He did not understand her and missed out on her!

"Brian, stop your obsession with me. If you're willing to get over it, you’ll have a new beginning. I believe you’ll meet a woman truly worthy of your love," said Grace.

Grace said, "Yes, that woman won't be me and shouldn't be me. If you want what's best for me, you should get over me. I don't want Jay to misunderstand."

Getting over someone was not that easy! However... looking at the person brimming with happiness in front of him, what he said was... "Okay, I'll get over you."

If it would make her happy, he would do it.

Even if he was unwilling and was only acting, he would do it!

Looking at the faint smile on Brian's lips, Grace's heart ached. However, Brian then walked past her and got into a nearby car.

The car slowly drove out of her sight, and Grace gently looked down.

She could not give Brian any response because the person she loved had never been him.

He did not finish his words, but the meaning was clear.

Grace's face reddened. "Why did you suddenly..." As if recalling something, she suddenly said, "Do you know that I met Brian today?"

After all, he had assigned bodyguards to protect her in secret. She knew that.

"Yes," he muttered.

"Jay, Brian and I... only had a chat. Besides..."

"Besides what?"

"Maybe he'll get over me," Grace could not help saying when she recalled what Brian had said today.

Although Brian had said it indifferently, she seemed to feel how determined he was when he said it.
