Chapter 989

Even though she knew he was powerful and his ability was far better than hers, she still wanted to protect him with all her might.

If he was insecure, then she would give her everything to make him feel secure, to love him, and to let him understand how important he was to her.

However, what happened afterward let her understand that all the things she did for him were just a joke.

He never needed her to protect him.

She was just a pawn to him.

Grace smiled wryly before bending over to take off Jason's shoes. She planned to tuck him in before sleeping in the chair for the rest of the night.

As the bed was against one side of the wall and the sheets were against the wall, she needed to get over him to pull the sheets.

Grace had just pulled a corner of the sheets and was about to tuck Jason in when she noticed that he had somehow opened his eyes.

As he was drunk, those dark eyes seemed to glisten and exude endless seduction.

"Grace..." His thin lips parted, and her name escaped his mouth softly.

Grace immediately froze, and before she knew it, his hands were around her waist. He had pulled her into his arms.

Caught off guard, Grace lay on top of Jason.

Her instinct was to get up and free herself from his embrace.

However, his hands seemed to cling to her.

"Grace, don't... fall in love with Brian... alright?" His hoarse voice, mixed with the strong smell of alcohol, wafted through the room.

"I won't fall in love with Brian nor anyone. Are you happy with that answer? Let go, and I'll tuck you in," she said, wanting to get out of his arms.

"You... won't fall in love with anyone?" he repeated her words, and the next moment, he rolled over on top of Grace. "You won't love me either?"

Grace thought the pose was more ambiguous than before and... more difficult to break free from.

Half his weight was on her. The smell of liquor and his faint cologne rushed toward her nostrils.

"We’ve broken up. You said you don't want to love me anymore. Do you expect me to love you?" Grace only found his remark somewhat amusing.

He probably only said such words because he was drunk.

"You're drunk. Let me get up," she said.

"Are you sure... you don't love me anymore?" he muttered in a whisper. The intoxicated look in his eyes seemed to be gradually stained with pain.

Grace was stunned. She thought she was seeing it wrong. 'Pain? Why would he suffer when he's the one who broke up with me?'

"Yes, I'm sure," she answered him calmly.

Suddenly, the hurt in his eyes was so strong that Grace could no longer tell herself that she was seeing it wrong.

She could clearly see that the hurt in his eyes was so overwhelming, as if he had lost something that mattered to him most.
