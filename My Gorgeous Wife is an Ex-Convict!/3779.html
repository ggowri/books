Chapter 3777

When Veronica arrived at the door of her apartment and opened it, she was greeted by a soft light and the smell of food in the air. Then she heard a familiar voice say, "You're back."

It was just a simple sentence, but it made her feel like tears were welling up. And when she saw that figure, she suddenly felt like her heart was settling down.

That is... Mason, the person who has accompanied her for the longest time in her life.

"Hmm, I'm back," she raised her lips and smiled.

"The food is ready, wash your hands and we can eat," he said.

"Okay," she replied, put down her bag, washed her hands, and came to the dining table.

There were four dishes and a soup on the table, a bit more than what two people could eat, but it looked good!

Veronica took out her phone and started taking pictures.

"Why are you taking pictures?" Mason asked.

"This is the first meal you've cooked since we started dating. Of course, I have to take a picture to commemorate it," she said.

"If you like the taste later, I can cook for you whenever you want," Mason said.

Veronica was taken aback. So, he meant that he could cook for her anytime she wanted?

He knew that now, just like before, he would agree to any request she made.

She sat down and started to eat the food.

The taste of the food was better than she had expected, and as a result, she ate half a bowl more than usual.

"It seems that your cooking skills have really improved during your years abroad," Veronica said.

"Living alone, you always have to do something to pass the time," he said. If he didn't find something to distract himself, he would only think about those memories from the past.

Even if the unforgettable feeling has faded away, the memories of those experiences remain vivid in the mind, especially without the emotional attachment.

"It's true, living alone sometimes requires keeping oneself busy," Veronica murmured. Mason's words reminded her of her past.

When she first moved into this apartment alone, she used to overthink things and feel more pain and discomfort as a result.

Only by keeping herself busy could she forget about those things.

But what about Mason? Is he also doing it for the same reason?

Thinking about this, Veronica looked at the person in front of her and suddenly asked, "Don't you blame me?"

"Blame you for what?" he asked in return.

"Blame me for misunderstanding you, not trusting you back then, even though you saved me when I was kidnapped," she said.

"I blame you and resent you, that's why I chose to be hypnotized," Mason replied.

"Yes, I should be blamed and resented for misunderstanding and accusing you," she said.

"I blame and resent you not because of your misunderstanding and accusation," he said.

She looked surprised, "Not because of that?" Then why?

"I blame and resent you because if you can fall in love with someone, why can't that person be me? Why do you only see me as a little brother?" he said.
