Chapter 3582

 

She donated a butterfly-shaped hair clip, adorned with various precious gems, making it exquisite and beautiful.

This hair accessory has a starting bid of 300,000 US dollars.

William furrowed his brows slightly. "Don't you like this hair clip?" He remembered that this was a gift from Uncle Reed when she graduated from middle school, and she loved it at the time.

"I do like it, but I want to do something more meaningful with something I love," Jasper said. "To me, it's just a hair clip, but for some people, it can provide them with food and clothing."

He was a little surprised. He always felt her kindness unintentionally, just like when she chose music therapy as her future direction of study instead of pursuing piano as everyone had guessed before.

"Then how about I bid on it? Consider it my contribution to charity," William said.

"No need, you've already contributed to charity by bidding on the sheet music earlier. Let someone who truly likes this hair clip bid on it."

As soon as the auction began, many people started bidding on the hair clip. Of course, half of them were trying to please the Reed family and Jasper.

After all, if they could win Miss Reed's hair clip and use it to make friends with the Reed family, it would be a great thing.

When the bidding reached 800,000, even Jasper was surprised. After all, this hair clip was not like the sheet music, which could be a genuine article. It was just an ordinary item, not a limited edition. Even if someone else wanted to buy the same one, they could.

Just then, a voice suddenly rang out, "Two million!"

This price had more than doubled, and the scene fell silent as the voice sounded.

Jasper turned to look at the middle-aged man who had just spoken. He had a strange appearance that Jasper had never seen before.

"Do you know that person?" Jasper asked William beside him.

"I don't know him. He's probably not a celebrity from Emerald City," William said. After all, he knew almost all of the wealthy and famous people in Emerald City.

Jasper looked puzzled when she heard this. She wondered what the stranger's intention was in paying 2 million for her butterfly hair clip. Surely, a grown man wouldn't be so fond of a hair accessory.

With no one else competing, the item was sold for 2 million after the auctioneer called out three times.

After the charity auction ended, Jasper found out that the buyer had wired the 2 million directly to the organizers and took the hair clip with him.

"Why would someone spend so much money on a hair clip?" Jasper murmured.

"If that person has a real motive, we'll find out soon enough," William said, rubbing her head. "Don't overthink it. Stay flexible. Thinking too much will exhaust your brain cells."

Jasper smiled, "That's right."
