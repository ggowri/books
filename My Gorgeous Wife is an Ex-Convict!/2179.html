"Boo-hoo. Mommy, what should I do? I misunderstood William. I don't want him to hate me. I don't want to be unable to see him again. I... I miss him. I want to apologize to him! Boo-hoo..." said Jasper as she wept. Big teardrops kept falling from her face.

Grace gently hugged her daughter and comforted her gently. "Alright, stop crying. Stop crying. William isn't that petty. He'll forgive you if you apologize to him sincerely."

"Yes... Yes... I’ll apologize... I’ll apologize to him sincerely..." The little one cried and nodded her little head. She was a little out of breath.

"I'll take you to Deer City to visit William someday. Then you can apologize to him, okay?" said Grace.

Jasper answered, "Okay. When... When are you taking me? Mommy, I want to see William as soon as possible. I want to quickly apologize to him and make up with him!"

"I have to find out where exactly William is staying now and speak to William's daddy before we can go over. I'll arrange it as soon as possible," said Grace.

Just then, Mick Reed suddenly said, "I want to go too."

Grace paused. "You want to go too?"

"Since Tristas and I misunderstood him that day, we both have to apologize, of course," replied Mick Reed.

Grace thought about it and said, "Okay, we'll go together, then."

Grace told Jason about William being wronged and that she wanted to take their son and daughter to Deer City to see William.

"Now that we’ve found out the truth, we have to let William know about it even though he's no longer in Emerald City. Also, Jasper and Mick want to apologize to him. I think it's a good thing that they're apologetic, so I want to bring them there,” said Grace. It would be an unforgettable experience for the children.

The children would soon understand that what they saw was not necessarily true and would think more before making conclusions.

"If you want to go, I'll go with you," said Jason.

"You'll go with me?" She paused. "But your work..."

"If you can take a break from work and go to Deer City with the kids, why can't I?" He smiled. "Besides, I'll take care of the company. Don't worry."

Grace thought for a moment, nodded, and said, "Do you have the Barlow family's contact number? I want to call them and fix a date for a visit."

Jason smiled. "Leave it to me. I'll contact the Barlow family."

Once Grace went into the bathroom, Jason picked up his phone and dialed Philip Barlow's private number.

This number was also the number Philip Barlow had given to William.

When the call got through, Jason said, "It’s Jason."

"Young master Reed, why are you calling me out of the blue?" asked Philip Barlow, who was on the other end of the line. He had a certain fondness for Jason, who had taken care of his son for a while despite having bad blood with Lily. Otherwise, his son would have been in a worse situation when he found him.

"You picked up William without saying a word. I’m calling to ask for an explanation, of course," said Jason.

"Young master Reed, if you didn't want me to pick him up, I wouldn't have been able to pick him up, would I?" asked Philip Barlow.

Both men were sensible enough that some things could be left unsaid.
