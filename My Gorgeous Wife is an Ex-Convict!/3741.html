At the end of the date, Mason escorted Veronica back to her residence.

"Actually, it's okay if you don't come to the Stanford University festival. You don't have to force yourself to agree. I'll just find an excuse and tell Jasper that I have something else to do," Veronica said.

"I didn't force myself," Mason replied. "Or is it that you don't want to go to the Stanford University festival with me?"

"No, I... really want to," Veronica said, her face blushing slightly again.

After seeing Veronica back to her residence, Mason returned to the Reed family.

However, when he pushed open the door to his room, he saw Jasper sitting inside, obviously waiting for him to come back.

"What's up?" Mason asked.

"Mason, are you and Sister Veronica... dating?" Jasper asked.

Mason replied calmly, "We're not dating, we're just trying it out."

"Trying it out?" Jasper was surprised.

"Yes, trying it out. After all, I hypnotized her, and now I just want to try and see if this feeling can be rekindled," he said.

"If it is rekindled, will you be with Sister Veronica forever?" Jasper asked.

"I will date her, but... no one can guarantee that we will be together forever!" he said.

After all, Veronica had also said in his memory, "Mason, I will always be with you!"

"Mason, let's always be this good to each other!"

"Mason, let's always trust each other, okay?"

But in this world, some things are easy to say but so hard to do!

Jasper seemed like she wanted to say something, but hesitated.

"What's wrong? Do you want to say something?" asked Mason.

"What if in the future, you and Sister Veronica...will you hypnotize her again?" she asked.

"No, once was enough," he replied.

In the following days, Mason and Veronica met more often, and the Reed family's car would always be waiting at the school gate to pick her up.

Fortunately, Mason had bought a BMW, but the Reed family's car was naturally not cheap, costing around two million.

As for the various rumors about Veronica, they began to spread more and more wildly in school.

Some said she was doing compensated dating.

Some said she was being kept by a man.

Others said she was working at an agency, possibly trying to sell herself to become a star, since the entertainment industry is known for such things.

As for these rumors, Veronica didn't pay much attention to them. After all, since she was in elementary school, junior high, high school...because of living with Mason, she had already heard countless rumors.

She had even developed immunity to all kinds of gossip, treating them as nothing more than other people's nonsense.

On the other hand, Shirley, who had a good relationship with Veronica, was infuriated by these gossips.

"Veronica, aren't you going to clarify things? They're talking about you like that and you're not angry?" Shirley asked.
