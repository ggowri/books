Chapter 4258 She’s Worth it

Finally, at this moment, he released his tightly clenched hands, blood seeping from his palms.

He wanted to tell her how grateful he was that she was the one who could break his curse because she was so wonderful, and he loved her so much.

This love, even though it began with ulterior motives, eventually turned into genuine affection.

When he looked at her when she was a baby, when he watched her learn to speak, when he saw her call him Chandler, he knew his genuine affection was growing more and more. Later on, he gradually forgot about his original motives and only wanted her to grow up safe and sound.

If it were Tina hadn't lost her memories, she would have understood and believed his words because Tina had experienced those little bits of affection.

But the Tina of today, she had no memories of the past or feelings for Chandler, so she wouldn't believe it at all.

Chandler lowered his eyes, thinking about the last time when Tina found out about this secret before losing her memories.

But the Tina of that time, she loved him, so her reaction would be completely different.

He never expected that back then, Tina would take the initiative to want to marry him early, to want to have his child as soon as possible, to want to break the curse on him.

But now everything had changed.

Chandler looked at the portrait hanging on the wall.

The woman in the portrait had caused great pain to the Hunter family. And now he couldn’t help but wonder if it was also a curse that things turned out like that.

Tina left the Hunter family the next day, without even saying goodbye to Chandler.

When Chandler found out that Tina had left, his expression turned dim.

"What's going on? Wasn't that girl coming to see you? Why did she leave on her own?" old Mr. Hunter furrowed his brows, asking his grandson.

Chandler smiled wryly and replied, "Tina already knows."

"What?"

"She knows about my curse. She knows that she has to have my second child for me to break the curse," Chandler answered.

"Did you tell her?" the old Mr. Hunter stared at his grandson.

Chandler nodded calmly.

The old Mr. Hunter's face darkened, and he coldly snorted, "She knows everything, yet she’s still like this. I will go to the Stephenson family and ask what this means!"

"Grandfather, this is between me and Tina, you shouldn't interfere!" Chandler immediately spoke up.

"Do you think it’s only about the two of you?" the old Mr. Hunter snapped.

"I will save myself, grandfather, you can rest assured," Chandler looked straight at the old Mr. Hunter. "But you also know how important Tina is to me. I don't want anyone else meddling in our affairs, not even you, grandfather!"

Chandler’s resolute gaze left the old Mr. Hunter feeling helpless, reminding him of how his grandson spent the days when Tina was missing.

If Tina hadn't come back safely, Chandler might have gone insane, or he might have killed himself.

Letting out a sigh, the old Mr. Hunter said, "Alright then, I won't interfere in this matter, and I won't let your parents interfere either. But I hope Tina is truly worth it for you."

Chandler lowered his gaze and said softly, "She is worth it."
