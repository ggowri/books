"I really... um, just see him as a friend, definitely not thinking about marrying him," Jasper said.

"But I do hope she can marry me," a voice suddenly sounded coldly beside them.

Jasper suddenly looked embarrassed and looked to the side, only to see that Roger had appeared next to them at some point.

The little girl looked sad and asked, "Roger, do you... do you like this sister?"

"Yes, I like her," Roger walked up to the little girl and squatted down, saying, "But she only sees me as a friend."

Jasper looked embarrassed as the two, one big and one small, chatted away as if she wasn't there. However, she was the main topic of their conversation.

"I like Roger. Roger, will you wait for me to grow up and marry me? I will definitely grow up to be very beautiful, more beautiful than this big sister!" the little girl exclaimed.

Roger smiled slightly and replied, "I believe you will grow up to be very beautiful, but if liking someone can be changed so easily, then it cannot be called liking. I think when you grow up, you will meet a boy you like and who likes you back, and he will be better than me!"

The little girl listened, not fully understanding.

Jasper was surprised by Roger's words. He had always seemed cold and distant, not easy to approach.

Even though he had told her to become friends, Grace didn't feel like she could open up to him like true friends do.

It was as if Roger always gave her a sense of distance, as if there was a chasm between them that couldn't be easily crossed.

But now, as he spoke gently to the little girl, she suddenly felt that maybe he wasn't as difficult to approach as he seemed on the surface.

After the little girl ran off, Jasper said to Roger, "You don't have to use me as a shield. When a child says she wants to marry you, it's just because she likes you, not because she'll actually marry you when she grows up. Children's words aren't always true."

"Do you really think that children's words aren't always true?" he asked, standing up and staring straight at her with his deep black eyes.

Jasper was taken aback, his heart beating unusually fast. Unexplainably, he felt as though his gaze was silently condemning her.

But...what was he condemning her for? Just because of what she said earlier?

"Adults don't take children's words seriously, but children take them seriously. Sometimes, a single sentence can be engraved in their hearts for a lifetime," he said.

"Did you...have any words that were engraved in your heart when you were a child?" she couldn't help but ask.

"Yes," he answered, "I had words from someone that I kept deep in my heart, even if I wanted to forget, I couldn't."

In an instant, she had a feeling that the person in front of her was just like Lawrence! If...if he really was Lawrence...

"Also, what I said earlier wasn't just to use you as a shield. I really like you, and of course, I want you to marry me in the future."

She was dumbfounded, her cheeks suddenly turning red.

"Or maybe you like William?" he asked.

She thought of what William had said to her before, that he would wait for her to make a decision and give him an answer.

She knew she liked William, but she wasn't sure if it was the childhood crush kind of liking or if it was a more mature liking as a man.

She was unsure.
