But for him, he has no feelings for her in these years. All emotions related to her have been hypnotized.

Even now, he is willing to try to date her, and she should be grateful that she still has a chance to get close to him.

After spending about half an hour, Veronica finally found the books she wanted and then left the library with Mason.

"Do you have any place you want to go next?" Veronica asked.

He looked at her, "You can do whatever you want for the next date, I can satisfy you."

"..." Her heartbeat suddenly accelerated again. Come on, boy, does he know how suggestive his words are? Isn't he afraid that she will make some unreasonable demands?

"What, don't you have anything you want to do?" He looked at her dumbfoundedly and asked again.

"Ah, um... how about we grab a bite to eat first and then catch a movie or something?" she suggested, unsure of what else to do with him at the moment.

"Sure," he agreed.

Veronica found a mall with a movie theater. The fifth and sixth floors of the mall had restaurants, while the seventh floor had the theater.

"Do you have a restaurant in mind?" she asked.

"Not really," he replied.

"How about a spicy fish hotpot place?" she suggested.

"That's fine with me," he said.

So the two of them went to a spicy fish hotpot restaurant, and Veronica ordered the dishes. After ordering, she said, "I'm going to use the restroom."

The restroom was outside the restaurant, and there were several restrooms on the same floor. Veronica followed the mall's signs to the restroom, but suddenly heard someone calling out, "Veronica!"

When Veronica turned around, she saw that it was Daniel.

He was still wearing his delivery uniform and holding a food container. He must have come here to pick up an order!

"Mr. Cross, hello," Veronica smiled slightly.

"Are you here at the mall by yourself today?" Daniel asked.

"No, I'm here with my friend. I just came out to use the restroom," Veronica replied. "Well, Mr. Cross, you're busy, so I won't keep you any longer."

"Wait!" Daniel suddenly stopped Veronica in her tracks. Even though he knew he had to deliver the food quickly, he couldn't help but want to talk to her a little longer. "Is your phone number still the same as before?"

"Yes," she said, but she had already blocked his phone number, and she didn't tell him that.

"Can I contact you in the future?" He gathered his courage and asked.

"I think it's better not to. Everything between us ended in high school, and there's no need to keep in touch," Veronica refused.

"Why? If you don't hate me, maybe we can be friends again," Daniel said.

"But Mr. Cross, I don't want to be friends with you again," Veronica refused and tried to avoid Daniel.

Daniel suddenly grabbed her hand, "Veronica, we've met twice in a short time. Don't you think it's fate?"

Before Veronica could answer, another cold voice interrupted, "There was never any fate between you and her!"
