Chapter 2614

The domineering possessiveness was only because of her!

She was the only one who made him possessive of her. He did not allow anyone else to covet her.

His kiss almost drowned her in it. She managed to cling to a sliver of reason. "Jay... Don't... Don't go on. I'm not in the right condition to..."

"I know. I'm not going to do anything. Grace, I just want to kiss you and let you know how much I love you!" His magnetic voice was alluring. His breathing, eyes, and embrace was enchanting her.

She was drowning increasingly deeper...

...

He said… he would get over her. He said that earnestly.

It was because he wanted her to be happy.

Since it was her repeated wish, he would make every effort to fulfill her wish. Even if the fulfillment... was only an illusion, he wanted to make it happen!

The so-called illusion was that all the people around him thought he had moved on. Of course, she thought so too.

Moving on... Perhaps not moving on no longer held any meaning. Moving on was the best thing to do.

However, it meant nothing to him. Her existence had become some sort of spiritual support for him for so many years. If he moved on, what would he have left?

Brian drove aimlessly. When the car stopped, he found himself in front of the noodle shop where Grace used to work.

However, the noodle shop had become a small restaurant, and the name on the signboard outside had changed.

He had once asked Grace to be a substitute for the woman he was looking for at the noodle shop entrance. It was ridiculous that he had asked the real deal to become a substitute.

She told him that she would not become anyone's substitute. He kept on looking for substitutes, but they could never replace the real deal.

She was right about that. He was only repeating the same mistake by finding so many substitutes.

Even when he went abroad, he met Whitney Cooper who looked 70 percent similar to her.

However, the closer he got to these women, the more he felt that they were not her.

Brian parked his car in front of the small restaurant and walked in.

It was almost dinnertime, and the dining tables in the restaurant were occupied.

The owner enthusiastically walked forward, asking if he was willing to share a table with others.

"Okay, I'll share a table," Brian said indifferently.

Therefore, the man led him to a dining table where only one person was occupying it.

Brian sat opposite the person and randomly ordered a donburi.

He did not want to eat anything. He only wanted to come in and sit down.

"Huh. It's you." A clear female voice rang.

Brian looked up and saw a round apple-shaped face.
