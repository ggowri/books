Chapter 2970

After Valda and Brian sat down, the elderly Mrs. Hart turned to Valda and said, "You draw comics quite well. I personally admire girls who have a special skill. As the saying goes, if you have a skill, you won't have to worry about anything."

"Not at all, not at all. I just used to enjoy drawing before, and now I happen to be able to make a career out of it," said Valda.

"Pretty good," said Mrs. Hart, but then she changed the subject. "Is it true what Brian said, that you and he are back together now?"

Valda tensed up, knowing that the crucial point had arrived. "It's true."

"Because he took a stab for you?"

"No, it's because I love him," Valda said seriously.

It's strange to say, but normally if she said this in front of others, she might feel embarrassed. But at this moment, she is just being honest and saying everything clearly.

"Will you break up again in the future?" asked Mrs. Hart again.

Brian's body stiffened imperceptibly, and a hint of unease flickered in his eyes, as if he was afraid of her answer, which might be something he didn't want to hear.

Valda replied, "I won't break up with him unless I'm sure he doesn't love me anymore. Otherwise, it's a no-go!"

"Do you have any plans to marry my son?" asked Mrs. Hart.

Valda almost choked on her own saliva, this question... it's jumping too quickly, isn't it?

"You're not just planning to play around, are you?" The old Mrs. Hart furrowed her brow as she looked at Valda, who didn't answer right away.

"No, no, I... of course, I have plans to get married," Valda quickly clarified.

Mrs. Hart's brow relaxed, "When?"

"..." This...this is too direct. They have only met twice, including the time at the hospital. Is it normal for parents to ask such a question after meeting only twice?

"Mum, Valda and I have just started again. Don't you think it's too early to ask that?" Brian helped Valda out.

"Good morning? I feel like it's already too late. Look at all my sisters around me, who doesn't have grandchildren? And me...forget about grandchildren, I haven't even seen the shadow of a daughter-in-law!" grumbled Mrs. Hart.

Mr. Hart quickly comforted his wife while Brian said, "If Valda and I get married one day, it won't be for the sake of having children or continuing the family line. We'll only do it because she's the person I want to marry, and I'm the person she wants to marry."

"You--" Mrs. Hart suddenly found herself speechless.

Valda suddenly felt touched in her heart. She understood that Brian's words were meant to not put any pressure on her.

"Okay, then tomorrow night, let's have dinner together. It'll be a formal meeting!" said Mrs. Hart. "That shouldn't be a problem, right?"

Brian looked at Valda and asked, "Are you free tomorrow night?"

"I am, I am!" Valda quickly replied. Even if she wasn't free, she would make time for this.

"Okay, when you finish work tomorrow, I'll come to your studio to pick you up," he said.

"Okay," Valda agreed.

After finishing their discussion, Mr. and Mrs. Hart were about to leave. Before leaving, Mrs. Hart reminded Valda, "Valda, my son is a bit introverted. You have to work hard. I'm counting on you to give me grandchildren in the future."

Valda blushed at her words.
