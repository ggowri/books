Chapter 2736

"Then why did you..."

"I said it's up to me to decide when to divorce." With that said, he got up. "It's late. I'll have the driver take you back to the old residence."

Mrs. Weiss said, "Martin, as a mother, I only wish you safety. Even if you owe Kyla anything, you paid that debt long ago. Do you know I almost fainted when I found out you donated your liver to Kyla a second time? I was really afraid that you were going to die just like that and I would lose my son!"

Mrs. Weiss began sobbing.

After Mrs. Weiss left, Martin Weiss went to the study and opened the drawer. There was a divorce agreement in the drawer, and Kyla had signed her part.

In other words, he only had to hand it over to the lawyer after signing it. Then, he and Kyla would be officially divorced a day later.

However... he kept delaying signing it.

It was what he had wanted, but he had felt empty when Kyla gave the papers to him.

If he had ever owed her anything, he had already cleared that debt just as his mother had said.

Just then, there was a knock on the study door. Martin Weiss put the agreement back in the drawer and said indifferently, "Come in."

The door was pushed open, and Shana Nicholls walked in. She spoke gently to Martin Weiss, "Mr. Weiss, it's time to take your medicine."

Martin Weiss got up, took the pills Shana Nicholls handed him, and swallowed them with water. Then, he handed the glass back to Shana Nicholls.

Shana Nicholls's fingers seemed to inadvertently touch Martin Weiss's when she took the glass.

Martin Weiss glanced at her coldly, and Shana Nicholls suddenly panicked. The glass in her hand fell to the floor, and the water spilled all over the floor.

"Oh, I'm sorry. I didn't hold the glass properly." Shana Nicholls apologized quickly and bent down to pick up the broken glass on the floor.

She suddenly screamed. Her fingers were pricked by the broken glass, and the tips of her fingers oozed with red blood.

"Ouch!" she shouted softly. She looked at Martin Weiss with tears in her eyes, looking pitiful.

'Normally, men will express worry for me at such a time. That way, I can get into their arms...' Shana Nicholls cooked up a plan in mind.

However, Martin Weiss said nothing and just looked at her coldly.

His gaze made Shana Nicholls guilty. "M-Mr. Weiss..."

"Why aren't you clearing the mess yet?" Martin Weiss asked coldly.

Shana Nicholls endured the pain in her fingers as she gritted her teeth and continued to clean up the broken glass on the floor.

She got up when she was done. "I... I'll leave first."

"By the way, you can go back to the hospital starting tomorrow. I don't need you here anymore," Martin Weiss said suddenly.

"What?" Shana Nicholls was terrified. Kyla had finally left. She thought she would finally have a chance, but Martin Weiss's words made her drop her expectations instantly. "Mr. Weiss, w-what did I do wrong? Or did I upset you?"

"I'm well enough now that I don't need a nurse anymore. Of course, you should go back to the hospital," Martin Weiss said calmly.
