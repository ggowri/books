"When I donated blood the last time, I heard from the nurses that his disease requires him to undergo transfusion treatment with his immediate family's blood. That’s his only shot at survival, right? His blood type is the same as mine, so he'll recover if I can donate enough blood for his transfusion. Isn’t that right?" she asked.

George Gregory came back to his senses. "Yes, we do need you to help Simon with his transfusion so that he'll recover, but—"

"I'll help Simon with his transfusion. Even if he isn't my child, I'll do it if my blood can save someone else," she said.

After going through so many things, she realized the value of life and was more willing to help those in need.

With that said, she looked intently again at Simon who was still unconscious. Then, she turned to leave.

George Gregory grabbed Amber Mitchell's arm. "Do you think I said that to make you help Simon with his transfusion?"

"What else? Do you really want us to acknowledge each other? George Gregory, you can stop the act. Given the choice, I think you would rather Louise Mitchell be her mother than me," Amber Mitchell said mockingly.

George Gregory said, "I'm not acting, and I don't have to act in front of you! I... I really want to ask for your forgiveness."

His voice began to grow hoarse, while his dark eyes seemed to be tearing up. There was even a touch of inferiority in his proud expression.

"Amby, give me another chance, okay? Let's start over. I won't misunderstand and doubt you this time. I’ll be good to you. You’ll have a bright future ahead of you," George Gregory choked out the words.

Amber Mitchell could not help laughing. "A bright future? George Gregory, what future is there for you and me?"

"I'll make up for what you've suffered. I'll make you happy!" he said quickly.

However, Amber Mitchell only said, "You can never make up for what I've suffered. George Gregory, I'm Herman's wife now. Don't ever say such absurd things to me again!"

"Absurd?" He stared at her. "Who's being absurd now? Herman Moss's already dead. You have no legal support by marrying a dead man!"

"Why should I need legal support to marry? I'm marrying him because I love him!" Amber Mitchell raised her chin and said sternly.

"You love him? You love Herman Moss?"

"Yes, I love Herman, even if he's dead. Death doesn't mean the end of love." Instead, her love for Herman only began after his death.

If only... she had fallen in love with Herman earlier. Maybe many things would not have ended like this.

With that said, Amber Mitchell lifted her hand to break away from George Gregory's grip.

He stubbornly refused to let go, feeling that he might not be able to hold her ever again if he did.

"George Gregory, it's no use holding on like this. You know what? You only disgust me every time you touch me. I hope I never have to see you again!" she said.

Her words seemed to trigger him. He suddenly pulled her into his arms before lowering his head and kissing her hard on the lips.

The intense kiss seemed to want to prove something or bring her back in time.
