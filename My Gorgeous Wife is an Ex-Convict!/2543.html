Chapter 2541

Instead of driving away in the car he came in, he walked down the street in a daze. He did not even know where he was going.

It was as if the road ahead of him was dark, and perhaps it was the end of his life.

What had he done?

He wanted to forget about Grace but could not.

He wanted to keep her safe but could do nothing when she was in danger. He could only watch her put herself into a dangerous situation.

When she told him that she would give birth to the child at such a price just because it was her and Jason’s baby, his heart filled with endless sadness.

It was... the obvious answer, was it not? He had long understood that the person she loved could only be Jason.

To her, he was nothing more than a childhood friend who had gone through thick and thin with her. He was nothing more than a friend to her.

It had always been a one-sided crush.

He knew that, but why did he still feel so bad? It was almost as if he was about to suffocate.

Just then, there was suddenly the muffled sound of thunder in the sky.

People on the streets either took out umbrellas or rushed to a place not far away to seek shelter from the rain.

Only Brian continued to walk slowly as if nothing mattered.

Drip, drip!

Bean-sized raindrops began to fall from the sky, landing on his head, face, and body.

The raindrops became increasingly dense. It seemed to hurt when they hit the body.

However, he hoped that the rain could hurt him even more. Perhaps he would forget the pain in his heart if it hurt more.

Swoosh!

It started raining cats and dogs. Those without umbrellas scurried for shelter, but Brian continued to walk slowly in the rain.

'Where should I go?

'Where should I go without Grace in my life?

'How should I live my life?'

Just then, he stopped when an umbrella was held over his head.

He looked up blankly at the floral umbrella over his head that temporarily shielded him from the rain.

When he turned around, he saw an unfamiliar female face.

He only glanced at her faintly before continuing to walk forward.

To his surprise, the umbrella continued to follow him.

Brian turned around, and his dark phoenix eyes saw the woman following behind him.

She was a young woman with a round apple face. She had round deer-like eyes, long hair that was in a simple ponytail, a delicate nose, and lips of moderate thickness. She was wearing simple casual clothes.

In Brian's opinion, such a woman was ordinary. He had seen all sorts of women in the entertainment industry. Many beautiful women had even tried to get close to him.
