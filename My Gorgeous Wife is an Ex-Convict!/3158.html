At her words, Lina trembled in fear and said, "Oh, never mind."

Then Amber stood up, walked to the side and got herself a glass of champagne.

When she was drinking it, Kyla walked over to her and asked, "What's wrong?"

"Nothing, I just thought of Herman," Amber whispered.

Kyla was taken aback, then realized that Herman was also Amber's childhood sweetheart, but now they were worlds apart.

"Are you planning to stay single for the rest of your life?" Kyla asked.

"I think so," Amber replied. "I nearly died because I fell in love with someone before. I don’t wanna go through it again."

Kyla also sighed, knowing what Amber said made sense.

"What about George? Do you really have no feelings left for him?" Kyla asked.

Amber gave a bitter smile. "Well, hatred might be the only feeling I have for him. I hate myself for loving him back then."

Hearing this, Kyla knew that Amber would never ever get back together with George.

"Kyla, it's over between George and me. Gee... I envy what you have with Martin. You should cherish it," sighed Amber.

Upon hearing this, Kyla couldn't help but look up in Martin's direction, and their gazes met directly.

His gaze, in an instant, made her feel bewildered.

These days, they shared the same bed and lived under the same roof. On the surface, they looked to be a loving couple. But deep down, Kyla knew it wasn't such a case.

Martin still had his feelings for her in the past sealed under hypnosis. So, she had no idea what kind of feelings he had for her at the moment.

She wanted to ask him about it but gave up on the idea, for fear that it would only make her more troubled.
