"Why didn't you go back to the restaurant?" she asked, glancing around and breathing a sigh of relief when she didn't see Daniel's figure. He must have left.

"I was waiting for you to come with me. Are you looking for Daniel?" he asked.

"Ah? No, no!" She quickly waved her hand and took his hand, saying, "Let's go back to the restaurant. Maybe the food is already served."

When they returned to the restaurant, some of the dishes were already on the table.

As they ate, Veronica couldn't help but ask, "I went to the bathroom later. You and Mr. Cross...nothing happened, right?"

"What do you think would happen?" he asked in return.

"..." She thought to herself that nothing had happened.

"Do you care about Daniel?" he asked.

"No," she denied. "I just don't want to have anything to do with him anymore."

After all, their separation from Daniel was a bit of a painful memory.

"If you don't want to have anything to do with him, then you won't," he said firmly.

His resolute voice made her body tremble suddenly, as if as long as he said it, it would definitely come true.

"Don't hurt him!" she said. Although it was Daniel's father who embezzled public funds, Mason had taken advantage of it.

It can only be said that everyone was at fault for what happened back then!

"Are you worried about him? Afraid I'll hurt him?" His voice lowered a few degrees, filled with a cold feeling.

"I'm not worried about him, I just don't want him to experience anything bad because of me and feel guilty about it," Veronica said.

She didn't want to involve Daniel in the situation between her and Mason. To her, Daniel was "just an insignificant person."

Her words seemed to please him, as the low pressure around him slowly dissipated.

But just as Veronica was about to breathe a sigh of relief, Mason's next question made her hold her breath again.

"Daniel said today was only the second time you've met. Have you met him before?" he asked.

Although Veronica felt embarrassed, she still told Mason about her previous encounter with Daniel.

"It was just a chance encounter," she said.

"Do you really have no feelings for him anymore?" he asked, staring at her as if scrutinizing her.

"No, I don't," she replied, meeting his gaze without any evasion.

"Then do you have feelings for anyone else now?" he asked again.

Her face turned red involuntarily. My god, how could he ask such a question? Besides him, who else could she have feelings for now?

He calmly waited for her answer, while she had an urge to vomit blood.

"Can't you tell me?" he said, his voice somehow carrying a dangerous feeling.

No, she didn't want any more misunderstandings between them!

Veronica quickly said, "No, I can tell you. I have feelings for you now!"

Her face turned red as she blurted out the words in one breath. After finishing, she realized that people at nearby tables were looking at her.

Her face turned even redder!

Oh my god, she must have spoken too loudly. Everyone at the nearby tables probably heard her!

She quickly lowered her head and buried herself in her food, trying to ignore the stares from those around her.

"Really? That's great," his voice sounded low.
