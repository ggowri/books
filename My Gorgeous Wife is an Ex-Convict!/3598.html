Chapter 3596

After finishing her meal, Jasper got up to go to the restroom. When she returned, she noticed that William was no longer in his seat.

She looked around curiously and saw William outside the restaurant on the side of the road talking to the two elderly scavengers she had seen earlier. After a moment, William opened his wallet and gave all the money inside to the two elderly people.

The two elderly people took a while to accept the money, and William turned and walked away. The two elderly people seemed to shout something, but because of the glass window and the distance, Jasper couldn't hear what they were saying.

When William returned to the store, he said to Jasper, "Let's go, I've settled the bill."

"Oh," she replied, following him out of Deer Tower and getting into the car.

As the car headed towards the Barlow House, Jasper asked, "By the way, those two elderly scavengers you gave money to earlier seemed quite pitiful. I wonder if they have any children. They looked quite old, with gray hair, and they were rummaging through the garbage on such a cold day..."

Jasper's words were just casual conversation, but William's expression suddenly changed, with a hint of panic in his eyes. "You...you saw it?"

"Yeah, I saw it when I came back from the restroom," Jasper said, noticing that something was wrong with William's expression. "What's wrong? I'm not going to judge you for anything. I also think those two elderly scavengers are quite pitiful, and there's nothing wrong with giving them money."

"Is...is that so?" William murmured.

"But if you give them money, it's only a temporary solution. Why not help them contact the government relief center and see if they can arrange for a more secure life for the two elderly people?" Jasper suggested after some thought.

"No need!" William quickly said.

She looked at him strangely, "Why not?"

"They should have already applied for government relief, but sometimes, it's just a drop in the bucket," he said.

"How do you know they applied?" she asked, puzzled.
