"Oh, really?" Brian raised his eyebrows, clearly understanding. "Did you and Lina talk about hugging Kering when you meet him?"

"Well, when you meet an actor you usually only see on TV or in movies, especially one you like, you naturally want to get closer to them," Valda said.

"What about you? You seemed to like Kering before. You even watched his recent TV drama multiple times on your iPad, didn't you?"

"I really thought that drama was good, and Kering played his character well," Valda said matter-of-factly.

"What do you mean by 'played his character well'?" Brian leaned in, his voice becoming lower.

Valda suddenly realized something was off when she met Brian's intense gaze. "I...I was just speaking as a regular audience member."

"Oh, just a regular audience member..." he drawled, almost smiling. "So, as a regular audience member, are you planning to hug him too?"

"Of course not," she said. "I'm not even a fan of Kering."

"Don't you feel regretful?" he asked, moving his face closer to hers, his lips almost touching her cheek.

"Why would I regret not hugging a celebrity when I have such a handsome husband who I can't even hug enough?" Valda tried to move her head back, but his hand was holding onto the back of her head, preventing her from creating more distance.

He gave a slight smile and pecked her lips, "Since that's the case, why don't you hug me?"

Huh?!

She paused, "Now?"

"Yes, now," he said. "I just want my dear wife to hug me more, is that too much to ask?"

"Of course, it's not too much to say." She had a big belly and couldn't really knock him down. Holding him would feel like having a delicious food in front of her, but she could only look and touch, but not eat.

This feeling of helplessness is also very uncomfortable!

Valda opened her arms and hugged Brian.

Brian also opened his arms and hugged Valda, being careful not to hold her too tightly and avoid putting pressure on her belly.

Then he leaned in close to her ear and whispered, "It's really a test of self-control, isn't it? In the future, you'll have to make it up to me."

Valda understood immediately, because for her, it was also a test!

"Okay," she blushed and took the initiative to kiss him on the lips.

Although I can't actually taste it, hmm... I think I can still give it a try and have a little taste.

On the day of the premiere, Valda brought not only a group of close girlfriends but also Brian to attend.

In addition, Hadwin also showed up, which was a bit unexpected for Valda. However, Hadwin got his ticket from Brian.

Valda whispered to Lina, "Didn't you say your husband wasn't coming?"

"Yeah, we had agreed that he wouldn't come, but who knew he suddenly cancelled his previously scheduled work and decided to come here," Lina said with a frustrated expression.

"Didn't you say he wasn't interested in art films?" Valda asked.

Lina said helplessly, "He's not interested in art films, but he's afraid I'll be too interested."

Valda understood, "Are they afraid you are too enthusiastic about Kering?"

"Hehe." Lina chuckled awkwardly.

When the movie started, the women sat in a row with Valda and Lina on either side, while their respective husbands sat next to them.

Valda whispered to Brian, "Did you prepare a ticket for Hadwin too? Why didn't you say so beforehand?"

"I was going to say it that day, but then...I forgot because I helped you change clothes," Brian replied.

Valda's face turned red instantly, and she couldn't help but recall the scene from earlier. If it weren't for her stomach ache, they would have...

Stop, stop! Don't think about it anymore!

She reminded herself in her mind, then focused her attention and started watching the movie.
