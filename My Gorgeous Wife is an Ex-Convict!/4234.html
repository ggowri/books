Chapter 4232 She Only Likes What She Likes

The next day, Tina and Chandler went to the hospital's newborn ward.

The baby was still in the incubator, but compared to what Tina had seen before, it was much better, and it seemed to have grown a bit, no longer as fragile as before as if it could disappear at any moment.

"Have you thought about the baby's name?" Tina and Chandler entered the room after putting on sterile gowns.

When her hand touched her daughter's tiny hand, an indescribable feeling surged up.

This was her daughter, the little life she had fought so hard to protect.

"I've thought of some names, I've written them down, you can take a look later when we leave," Chandler said.

The nurse took the baby out of the incubator and let Tina try holding the baby.

Chandler seemed worried that Tina still had injuries and might not have the strength to hold the baby.

Tina said, "It's okay, I still have some strength left, I want to hold her."

The nurse carefully placed the baby in Tina's arms.

Tina held the baby, watching her daughter's little hands wave slowly, a smile appeared on her face.

"By the way, do you want to hold her?" Tina said.

Chandler seemed hesitant.

Tina felt a bit disappointed, "If you don't want to, then forget it."

"No!" he quickly denied, "I'm just not sure how to face her, I do want to hold her!"

So the little baby was handed over to Chandler's arms.

He carefully looked at the tiny baby in his arms.

Different from his previous impression, her skin was not as wrinkled as before, gradually becoming fuller, and faintly showing some resemblance to Tina.

This was his and Tina's daughter!

"I'm sorry, it's Dad's fault for not protecting you and not finding you and your mom earlier," Chandler whispered.

On his handsome face, there was an indescribable sense of guilt and self-reproach.

When visiting time was over, Tina and Chandler left the neonatal ward.

The little one would still have to stay here for some time until she reached the required weight before she could be discharged.

Tina turned to Chandler and said, "Let's go on a date next."

"A date?" Chandler was taken aback.

"Let's just consider it as starting to fall in love again. Otherwise, how can I fall in love with you all over again?" Tina said.

Instead of waiting for the memories to suddenly come back, it would be more practical to try and see if she could fall in love with him again.

He looked at her tenderly and said, "Alright, let's go on a date then."

"What did we do when we went on dates before?" she asked curiously.

"We would go shopping, eat whatever you wanted, or do whatever you wanted, like going to see a movie, an art exhibition, or a concert if there was something you wanted to see," Chandler said.

Tina frowned. "Why is it always what I want? What about you? When we go on dates, is there nothing that we do that you want to do too?"

"I'm a bit boring, I don't have any particular hobbies," Chandler said.

For him, being with her and seeing her happy was already a source of joy.

"Well, you should think about what you like too," Tina said.

These words made Chandler pause for a moment.

The old Tina had said something like this to him before.

"Chandler, you can't always just go along with what I like. I feel like you're indifferent to everything. I know you care about me, and even though this special care makes me happy, don't you think your life would be more interesting if you didn't always do that?"
