Chapter 2629

He stiffened. "I know I did a terrible thing back then. I've been wanting to apologize to you all these years."

Valda Theller said indifferently, "Okay, apologize."

He froze as if he was a little stunned that she was this straightforward.

"Didn't you say you want to apologize? Now that I'm here, aren't you going to apologize?" she asked.

Raymond Bowman took a deep breath, and his thin lips parted gently as he spat out two words, "I'm sorry."

"Okay, I heard you. Can you let go now?" Valda Theller asked without any expression on her face.

"Do you forgive me?" Raymond Bowman's eyes contained a rare look of eagerness.

"I've only acknowledged your apology. It doesn't mean I forgive you. I'm no saint. I'm not used to forgiving someone who used to hurt me, especially a man who hurt me to please another woman.”

"Valda, I..."

Valda Theller said, "Don't call me Valda! Raymond Bowman, whatever happened between us is over to me. I don't hate you, but it doesn't mean that I’ll forgive you for what you did. I only hope that we’ll never meet again or that we'll treat each other like strangers when we meet."

"Strangers..." Raymond Bowman laughed bitterly. "If I said I could start over with you—"

However, she interrupted him before he could finish, "There's no starting over. I want nothing more to do with you. Raymond Bowman, I have no feelings for you anymore. And to tell you the truth, I only think it's some other bet you've made at my expense when you talk about starting over!"

There was a pain in his eyes. "I won't hurt you again. I've been regretting what happened back then."

However, there was still a calm expression on her face as if whatever he said did not matter to her anymore.

Raymond Bowman could not help saying, "What about your so-called boyfriend? Do you think he's serious about dating you? That man looks rich and is good-looking. A man like him should have plenty of women around him. Maybe he's just fooling around with you."

Valda Theller said confidently, "He's serious about me! Whether a person is sincere or not has nothing to do with their looks and fortune but character. I trust my boyfriend's character, and I love him!"

Of course, the last part was a lie.

Raymond Bowman's pupils shrank suddenly. His five fingers that clenched Valda Theller's arm slowly loosened. "You... love him?"

Valda Theller said, "Yes, I love him very much. Raymond Bowman, I've heard your apology. I hope you'll leave me alone!"

With that said, she turned around and walked into her apartment block.

Raymond Bowman stared blankly at the figure until it disappeared from his sight. He gently lowered his eyes and looked down at the hand that had grabbed her arm earlier.

Her warmth seemed to remain in his palm, but she was already so cold to him.

She had once been warm to him, telling him, "Raymond Bowman, I like you very much. I think this is love. I think I’ll always love you. Even if I've become an old woman, I will still love you this much."

However, he had destroyed her feelings for him!
