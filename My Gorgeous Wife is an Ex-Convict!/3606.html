Chapter 3604

She ran all the way, stepping on each step, panting as she walked up.

Finally, a familiar figure caught her eye.

It was—William!

Jasper hurriedly ran up, intending to call out to him, but stopped abruptly when she saw his expression.

At this moment, from her perspective, she saw him from the side.

He stood there quietly, with his head half lowered, his gaze fixed on the tombstone, his face filled with an indescribable sadness and mockery.

She had never seen such an expression on his face before.

In an instant, she didn't know how to change her words, or what to say.

She just stared at him blankly, not knowing how much time had passed. It seemed like he could feel her gaze, as he slowly turned around and looked towards her.

Their gazes met.

"How did you come here?" There was a gentle smile on his face, as if they were meeting under normal circumstances.

In the past, she would have felt comforted by his smile.

But today, she felt a twinge of sadness.

"I... came to find you," she said, her nose tingling.

"I have never wanted to come here, never wanted to see this woman," William murmured. "I hate the mistakes she has made, I hate that her blood flows through me, I hate her even in death for never repenting. In the years we were together, she never loved me, I was probably just a tool she could use in the future."

Jasper listened and walked to William's side, reaching out and holding his hand!

As if this could give him strength.

Because he looked like a person exhausted to the extreme, as if he could collapse at any moment!

"But even so, I think I should be grateful to her. Regardless of her reasons, at least she gave birth to me, gave me life," William continued.

"William, you are you, and your mother is your mother. Regardless, the matters of the previous generation have already passed and have nothing to do with us!" Jasper said.

"Is it really unrelated?" he asked, looking at her with a bitter smile.

"Well, it's unrelated!" she replied firmly.

"You've also seen the post on the school forum, right?" he suddenly said. "I am the son of a criminal, so there is a high possibility that I will also commit crimes! Even my current friendship with you, getting close to you, it's all for a purpose, to avenge my mother!"

"I have seen that post, so I know better than anyone how nonsensical it is! Who says that the child of a criminal will definitely commit crimes? And who says that our friendship is solely for avenging your mother? If anyone dares to say that in front of me, I will definitely give them a piece of my mind!" Jasper angrily said.

He looked at her intently, as her face turned red with anger. "Do you really think I won't?" he asked.

"You won't, because you are William, the William I have known since childhood. I trust you, just as I trust myself!" she said. "So, no matter who maliciously slanders you, I won't believe it! I won't, and neither will my family!"

"Jasper..." he called out her name in a hoarse voice, suddenly reaching out and pulling her into his embrace, with his head resting on her shoulder.

It was as if, at this moment, only she could support his exhausted body.
