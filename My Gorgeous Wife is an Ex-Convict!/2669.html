However, what he said next made her freeze.

What he said was... "I probably won't have kids of my own, so I’ll probably never understand no matter how you explain it."

Valda Theller looked blankly at the person right in front of her. He looked down when he said this, and his long eyelashes concealed everything in his eyes. A blanket of solitude seemed to surround him, isolating him from his surroundings.

It was as if he would not let anyone get near him, nor could she get near him.

...

A week later, Valda Theller moved back to her place. She joined the film crew according to their original plan and began assisting the scriptwriter with the script.

However, she could feel many people's strange glances on her first day.

After all, even though the buzz of her news might have faded and it was quickly replaced by other eye-catching gossip news, everyone in the film crew knew about her past now.

However, what they knew was the distorted facts online, not the real truth.

"That's her. She bullied others when she was in university. It's satisfying to know that she was bullied back in the end!"

"Reading her comics, I thought she was amiable. I didn't expect her to be so mean!"

"The producer probably regrets choosing her comic. There are other comics better than hers."

Those surrounding people seemed to be whispering, but their volume was not low, and Valda Theller could easily hear them.

"Don't mind the gossip. Just think of them as bullsh*t!" Camille Strickland said as she reassured her best friend.

"Don't worry. I'm not a pampered princess who has never experienced anything in her life. It's just some gossip. I can stand them." Valda Theller smiled at her best friend to reassure her.

Valda Theller discussed the plot with the scriptwriter the entire morning.

After discussing for a while, the director called the scriptwriter away, so Valda Theller stayed alone in the room and waited.

Suddenly, the room door was pushed open, and a figure rushed in while panting.

Valda Theller looked up and frowned when she saw the intruder.

It was Raymond Bowman!

Knowing Raymond Bowman had invested in the film, she knew she might not be able to avoid meeting him. However, if possible, she did not want to meet him again.

"If you're looking for the scriptwriter, they're with the director," said Valda Theller.

Raymond Bowman approached her and said, "I'm here to see you. How have you been these days?"

"I'm fine," Valda Theller said with a blank expression.

"I'm sorry about the viral post. I had no idea that someone would expose the incident. I'll find out who's behind this and give you an explanation," said Raymond Bowman.
