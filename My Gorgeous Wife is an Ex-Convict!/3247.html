"Also, if you want to take a nap in my office next time, you can ask my secretary for a blanket to cover yourself. Otherwise, it's easy to catch a cold with the air conditioning on in the office," he added.

"Oh," she responded, glancing at the blanket covering her. It seemed that sometimes men could be more attentive than women, especially when it came to details.

"By the way, you..." She suddenly spoke up as if she had just remembered something, but after saying three words, she hesitated.

"What is it? Do you have something to say?" Martin asked.

Kyla bit her lip and asked, "Why did you put that photo on your desk?"

Even though she didn't specify which photo, he immediately understood.

Because there was only one photo on his desk!

"Because I really like it, but also regret it," he said.

She looked puzzled. She understood why he liked it, but why regret it?

"What do you regret?" she asked.

He suddenly turned the steering wheel and parked the car on the side of the road, turning to look at Kyla intently.

For a moment, there was silence in the car.

After a while, Martin murmured, "I regret that I lost your smile."

Kyla's heart suddenly shook, and her nose became sour.

Martin raised his hand and gently stroked her face, "I'm sorry, Kyla. If I hadn't done those stupid things back then, you wouldn't have lost that smile so early. I ruined your life, but I also ruined my own."

"Even if that smile is gone, there will still be new ones," she said.

"But it's not carefree anymore," he said. Later, even when she smiled, there always seemed to be layers of pressure, exhaustion, and worries underneath.

She could smile gently, elegantly, and peacefully, but she would never smile carefree again.

"People always grow up, and no one will always be carefree. Sometimes, the more experiences you have, the more you understand what to cherish," Kyla said.

"Yes," Martin murmured, "the more experiences you have, the more you know what to cherish."

So he cherishes every day now.

"Kyla, no matter what happens in the future, I won't leave you," he suddenly said.

She hesitated for a moment, "You--"

"So I hope you can be happier," he said.

She smiled slightly, "If you can really do what you say, then I will be happier."

"I will definitely do what I say." As he finished speaking, his lips landed on hers.

How could he bear to leave her? He couldn't bear it!

On the day of the wedding photo shoot, Kyla appeared in front of Martin in a white wedding dress with delicate makeup, and he felt a sense of confusion.

It was as if he saw her from years ago.

Back then, she had once said to him with a naive face that when they got married in the future, she would wear a beautiful white wedding dress and become the most beautiful bride.
