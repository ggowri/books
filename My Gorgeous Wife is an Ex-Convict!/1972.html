Chapter 1970

She asked tentatively, "Well... Do you still love me? But you're just too embarrassed to forgive me so easily, so... you hope I’ll continue pestering you a month later?"

Hadwin Stephenson glared at Lina. 'Does she have to talk about these things so frankly?'

Although... he was doing exactly what she said.

"Is it true?" She demanded to know.

He turned his back, only thinking that he would lose his restraint if she continued looking at him.

"Is it true or not?" She quickly walked around and continued to stare at his face, her hands pulling restlessly at one of his arms. "I didn't misunderstand, did I?"

He glared at her. The woman had always been able to drive him into a corner!

"... Yes!" He said gruffly and went straight back to his desk to look at the documents piled on it.

Lina's face was full of joy. 'He admitted it! It's really as I thought. He still has feelings for me, but I just hurt him too deeply before.'

Lina then approached Hadwin Stephenson's desk. "Hadwin, when are you going to forgive me? Two months? Three months? Or six months? Isn't six months a little long? The baby will be born by then.

"Hadwin, at least give me a time frame so I can prepare myself mentally.

"Why don't we have a forgiveness dinner on the day you forgive me? It'll serve as a day of commemoration to also remind us not to have any more misunderstandings."

She chattered as if she had come out of her shell.

Hadwin Stephenson was speechless. 'Forgiveness dinner... How did she come up with that? And she’s even trying to bargain with me on when I'm going to forgive her!'

"By the way, Hadwin, I love you. I love you so much!" Lina widened her round eyes and looked intently at Hadwin Stephenson as she confessed her feelings to him.

Hadwin Stephenson instantly blushed!

"Hey, Hadwin, your face looks a little red..." Lina blinked and put her hands on Hadwin Stephenson's cheeks.

He should take her hands off him. She was distracting him from his work.

"Hey, your ears are turning red too!" As if discovering something new, her hands began to creep up his ears again.

At that instant, Hadwin Stephenson felt blood rushing to his face. Even his ears turned red.

"You..." He glared at her. "Quit it," he said with a doting tone that even he could not describe.

Lina said with a smile, "I'm not messing around. I'm just happy. Hadwin, thank you for forgiving me."

Hadwin Stephenson pressed his lips together and said grudgingly, "I haven't forgiven you yet."

"But you'll forgive me sooner or later, won't you? Hadwin, I want to spend every day with you from now on!" Lina cupped her cheeks as she began to dream of a better future.

Hadwin Stephenson momentarily froze before saying, "Ask your parents if they’re free tonight. I haven't even invited them to dinner ever since they came."

Lina was delighted. She hurriedly answered on her parent's behalf, "They're free. They're free. I'll call my parents and tell them!" With that said, she began to call her parents.

Looking at Lina's reaction, Hadwin Stephenson could not help smiling. He found that the previous pressure in his chest was fading away.
