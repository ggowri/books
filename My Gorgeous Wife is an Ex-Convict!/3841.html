"I owe you, first because back then, I heard your piano playing and found you, and kept you by my side. To me, my words changed the fate of your family, so I often think, if I hadn't insisted on finding you, your family's life wouldn't have changed, and your mother might not have developed a gambling addiction."

Jasper murmured, confessing his guilt to Lawrence word by word, "Secondly, I didn't listen to your explanation and broke off our friendship. Thirdly, you called me at the most dangerous time, but I didn't answer. These twists of fate are my guilt towards you, but this guilt is only mine, the Reed family doesn't owe you, nor does your family, you better remember that!"

Lawrence stared at Jasper fiercely, her words were like digging up the bloody truth that had been buried under his revenge.

Of course, he also understood that the ones to blame for his parents' death were his mother's gambling addiction and his father's indulgence, and those loan sharks who charged high interest rates!

But...his parents were already dead, and those loan sharks who harmed his parents had been arrested and were receiving the punishment they deserved.

However, the anger and resentment in his heart still couldn't be quelled!

It was like needing a release valve to vent, in order to let out the anger that had been suppressed in his heart.

And the Reed family was his release valve, allowing him to vent the anger that had been squeezed in his heart.

"Since you also said that you owe me, how do you plan to make up for these three debts of guilt?" Lawrence suddenly asked.

"The guilt I owe you, I've made up for what I could over the years, and for what I can't make up for, I don't think I can do anything about it," Jasper said.

"No way to make it up? You don't even ask how to make it up, how can you say there's no way?" Lawrence sneered.

"Whether you want to take my life or leave me disabled to seek revenge, I won't agree," Jasper said.

"Aren't you afraid that I'll kill you right here, right now?" Lawrence said. "You know, this is a chemistry lab, everything inside is dangerous. It's easy to cause an explosion here."

"You won't," Jasper said firmly.

"What makes you so sure?" Lawrence asked coldly.

"If there really is an explosion here, neither of us can escape. Besides, if your goal is to kill me so easily, you had the chance before, didn't you?" Jasper said.

Lawrence pursed his thin lips. Yes, he had many chances before to kill her.

But strangely, he never had that thought.

The thought of letting her die never crossed his mind.

He thought of making the Reed family suffer, making them pay for his parents' death!

But he never thought of Jasper that way.
