"This is my chance to change my fate. I'd rather die in glory than live in shame," Osborne muttered as he entered the general store and collected what he needed.

A short while later, with determination on his face, he headed back to Caldwell Tower under the guise of gathering his things.

But as he reached the tower, a group of familiar faces blocked him from entering.

"Well look who's back! Why are you here?" Evelyn hissed as she pushed her way to the front of the crowd. "If you're after your belongings, you should know they've already been discarded. I handled that myself. Don't bother going in; you might steal something! Leave."

She glared at him as the other Caldwells gathered behind her.

Osborne's face darkened.

Evelyn never showed him kindness during his three years with the Caldwells.

She constantly insulted him and pushed Ophelia to divorce him. And now, apparently, she had discarded his possessions as if they were garbage.

The old Osborne might have endured the humiliation.

He sneered and shoved Evelyn aside, plowing through a sea of Caldwells and storming into the tower.

"Get out of my way!"

"You worthless fool!" Evelyn snapped. "How dare you push me? There's nothing for you here! Get lost, or you'll regret it."

She raced ahead of him and blocked his path.

Osborne smirked. "Nothing for me? I have rights to certain assets. Just give me a cool hundred million from the Caldwell family fortune, and I'll leave. Otherwise, I'm not going anywhere."

Osborne's high demand was intentional. He knew the Caldwells wouldn't agree. He aimed high to leave room for bargaining.

The Caldwell family was stunned. All at once, they burst into laughter.

"Hah! Has this loser gone mad?" one shouted.

"Looks like Ophelia's divorce scrambled his brain. He's lost his damn mind," another said, cackling.

Evelyn snapped. "Are you insane? Demanding a hundred million from the Caldwell family? Who do you think you are? I knew you were after my family's money. I should have ended you sooner!"

Osborne watched with quiet satisfaction as Evelyn's face contorted with rage.

"Guards!" she screamed.

In an instant, five bodyguards surrounded Osborne.

"Beat him senseless," Evelyn ordered, eyes blazing. "I'll take responsibility if he dies."

The bodyguards smiled coldly and descended on Osborne, swinging their fists wildly.

A cold glint flashed in Osborne's eyes.

For the past three years, whenever Evelyn was displeased with him, she'd have the bodyguards beat him without mercy.

Since Evelyn was Ophelia's stepmother, he had to endure it—and because of it, the Caldwell family thought him all the weaker.

If only they knew. If it weren't for my protecting Ophelia in secret, rival families would've taken her out long ago, leaving her no chance to inherit the Caldwell fortune.

Osborne wasn't going to take it anymore.

His left pupil split into two. The Eye of Satan had activated, triggered by his rage.

Everything around him slowed to a crawl.

Osborne moved swiftly, dodging a punch and landing an uppercut on a bodyguard's chin.

Bang!

Years of frustration came out in that punch.

The bodyguard's jaw shattered as he fell, sending blood and teeth skittering across the cold marble floor.

When another slow-motion punch came at Osborne, he dodged it with ease.

Bam! Bam! Bam!

Osborne, like a raging lion, defeated all five bodyguards in under ten seconds.

Silence fell over the scene. The Caldwell family stared on in shock and fear, jaws agape.

Looks like I won't need what I purchased from the general store—not yet, at least. Five bodyguards down in seconds. Who's the loser now?

"He's a monster!" one Caldwell cried.

"You, you..." Evelyn trembled, speechless.

Osborne's cold gaze fixed on her. He knew the bodyguards were just pawns. Evelyn was the real culprit.

With a steely expression, Osborne stepped closer to her.

Evelyn's eyes widened in terror. "You bastard, what are you going to do?"

"What am I going to do?" Osborne asked, a wry smile crawling across his face. "I'm going to collect what's mine."
