Osborne could feel the barrier to Class S within him, so tantalizingly fragile that it seemed like the lightest touch would shatter it, allowing him to transcend to Class S.

But as he began, Osborne quickly realized just how formidable this barrier truly was.

He attacked it relentlessly, but the barrier only quivered slightly. It appeared ready to yield at any moment yet remained frustratingly unyielding.

Fortunately, Osborne had a reservoir of immense power within him, allowing him to continue his relentless assault without restraint.

Having started, he had no intention of giving up.

What Osborne didn't know was that because his body was extraordinarily strong, his Class S barrier was also more formidable than others'.

Breaking through would be a tougher challenge. Few in this world manage to ascend to Class S—the path was reserved for those with unrivaled talent, insight, and perseverance.

The primary reason for this rarity was the sheer resilience of the Class S barrier.

This barrier was like an impassable chasm, a fortress wall that stopped over ninety percent of Class AAA individuals from ever advancing, no matter how desperately they tried until they eventually perished.

The fact that Osborne could even make the barrier tremble indicated that he was on the brink of a breakthrough. With sustained effort, he was certain to overcome it.

Osborne sensed this closeness and immersed himself in his task, oblivious to everything else around him.

Three days flew by.

On the vast, lonely island, Osborne sat cross-legged on the beach, continuously hitting against the Class S barrier.

Meanwhile, in Newport City, at the Augustine Estate, preparations were in full swing.

The estate, sprawling across nine hills, covered an expansive area—about three miles from north to south and over two miles from east to west, more like a small city than an estate.

It could easily accommodate hundreds of thousands of people.

Currently, the place was bustling with activity, even feeling a bit cramped.

Representatives from major Rosmandi powers had gathered there—a coalition of at least a hundred Ancient Families and fifteen major organizations—amassing nearly a million people.

The Augustine family couldn't possibly offer rooms for such a multitude. Many had to pitch tents wherever they could or simply sit on the ground.

Their expressions were grim; they all knew the Aberrants' army was on the move and would soon be upon them.

In recent days, the Aberrants had rampaged through Rosmandi. Those who refused to surrender were wiped out mercilessly.

The surviving factions had fled to the Augustine estate.

Yet even here, they were not entirely safe.

The power of the Aberrants was overwhelming. Could the Augustine family fend them off? If not, all these people would likely be annihilated by the Aberrants.

They knew it, plain and simple. Come tomorrow at the latest, the Aberrants' army would descend upon this place like a plague, ready to lock horns with the Augustine family in a final, decisive clash.

Such was the bloodthirsty and ruthless nature of the Aberrants—known all too well by these factions. Decades ago, the Aberrants' brutal rule over Rosmandi's underworld had become intolerable, prompting these powers to support Sebastian in expelling them from Rosmandi.

Now, the Aberrants had come back for revenge!

If the Aberrants won, none of them would be left alive.

"Let's hope we can take them down tomorrow!"

Many people were praying at that moment.

In the expansive conference room of the Augustine family, leaders from over a hundred Ancient Families and a dozen powerful factions had gathered, embroiled in a heated debate.

"Wesson, do you have any idea of our odds?"

"Decades ago, we helped Godfather Sebastian against the Aberrants. Now they're out for blood; you can't just sit back and do nothing. The Augustine family must protect us!"

"Where is Godfather Sebastian? He was able to drive Gerardo out of Rosmandi back then; and he can do it again, right?"

"Wesson, we need to see Godfather!"

"If he doesn't have a plan either, let's scatter and save ourselves! At least we can preserve our bloodlines!"

"Wesson..."

The voices made Wesson feel drained.

He couldn't tell them that former Godfather Sebastian was currently in critical condition, unconscious from his injuries. If he did, he feared the room would lose hope.

He furrowed his brow and spoke up, "Enough!"

Silence fell over the room.

"Listen up, everyone! Arguing is meaningless! We need to go back and prepare for battle. The Aberrants is coming for us, and besides fighting with everything we've got, what else can we do?

"Rosmandi is our home!

"If we can fight, we fight! If we can't, we'll still stand our ground! The Augustine family won't back down; if any of you want to run, then you're free to go!"
