Just a minute later, Ninety-third was back on his feet.

All his organs had completely healed, and a surge of vitality coursed through his body.

His bone marrow was working madly, replenishing the blood he'd lost, and color was rapidly returning to his face.

Osborne stood there, dumbfounded.

He thought, "Can you believe this? The Crystal of Life can actually pull off recovery from such grave injuries? If it could restore a crushed skull as well, wouldn't that be like having a lesser version of the Light of Redemption?

Imagine possessing the Crystal of Life—could that make one practically immortal?"

But deep down, he knew that was far from certain.

A smashed head would still mean certain death.

Even if Osborne could somehow use the Light of Redemption to revive someone whose skull had been shattered, they'd merely be recovering a body; the person's consciousness and memories would be lost forever—like an entirely different individual.

It was clear he wouldn't risk smashing Ninety-third's head just to test the limits of the Crystal of Life.

If it could mend a crushed heart, that was already more than enough for him.

As he felt the energy from the Crystal of Life, Osborne noted that a third of it had already been consumed.

He thought, "So, while it can bring someone back from a crushed heart, it takes much!

The amount of vitality in this Crystal is at least ten times that of Ninety-third, meaning it could theoretically revive him ten times! But to merely restore his heart, it costs a whopping twenty percent of the Crystal's vitality!

Still, that's more than enough!"

With satisfaction, he glanced at Ninety-third and tossed him the divine artifact dagger.

Osborne declared, "Ninety-third, your bravery truly impresses me!

"This top-grade divine artifact dagger is now yours as a reward!

In addition, I appoint you as the leader of the fifth squad of the Blood Demon Vanguard, overseeing twenty members. I trust you won't let me down."

Ninety-third stood there, shocked.

He clutched the top-grade divine artifact dagger tightly.

This was a top-grade dagger!

Most Class S warriors only have Ordinary divine artifacts, and only a few powerful Class S warriors could use Premium items.

But a top-grade divine artifact was reserved for the elite among elites!

And now, Osborne was giving it to him!

On top of that, he was made leader of the fifth squad!

At that moment, Ninety-third's excitement was overwhelming.

He quickly bowed to Osborne. "Mr. Augustine, you can count on me! I won't disappoint you!"

Meanwhile, the other ninety-nine members of the Blood Demon Vanguard watched with a mix of envy and awe.

Their eyes glimmered green as they looked at Ninety-third.

He had simply taken a couple of stabs to the heart—albeit with no risk to his life—and now he had claimed a top-grade divine artifact dagger and the title of squad leader!

If only they had known that all it took was a little courage to seize such a golden opportunity! They would've fought tooth and nail for a chance like that!

But it was too late.

The Blood Demon Vanguard was divided into five combat squads, each with its own leader responsible for twenty members. They initially planned to take a break before diving back into the competition for the remaining leader positions.

Now, with Ninety-third already secured as the leader of the fifth combat squad, they were left scrapping for the remaining four spots.

How could they not envy Ninety-third?

Osborne smiled and addressed the gathered warriors, "There's no need for envy. Each of you will soon possess a divine artifact! If you show enough strength, it's not impossible that each of you could have a top-grade divine artifact in the near future.

"Now, step forward and receive your Crystal of Life!

"Look, you've all seen what the Crystal of Life can do. This is your lifeline, so keep it close at all times during battle! I don't want any of you dying on my watch!"

With that, Osborne began distributing the crystals.

Apart from the hundred members of the Blood Demon Vanguard, every ordinary member of Blood Demon would receive a Crystal of Life.

Even those from the Draco Armed Services Branch, as well as all individuals within the Augustine family with a strength of Class A or above, would be granted one.

As for those below Class A, they'd be left out, as only those at Class A and above could absorb the vitality from the crystals.

The stronger the individual, the faster and more effectively they could absorb the healing energy, allowing for quicker recovery after injuries.

Before long, nearly all 10,000 Crystals of Life had been handed out.

Osborne kept a few hundred in reserve.

Although he had the Light of Redemption, which had superior healing capabilities, it required a significant cooldown period between uses.

Having the crystals on hand provided more safety.

Moreover, the storage area was filled with another 20,000 crystals ready for replenishment whenever necessary.

A thought struck him. "Now, I wonder if the Crystal of Life can help Grandpa?"

Without hesitation, he made his way to the room where Sebastian lay.

Sebastian had been severely injured and had slipped into a coma after exhausting himself in battle. His vitality was dwindling with each passing moment.

"Let's hope this works..." Osborne murmured as he gently placed a Crystal of Life on Sebastian's chest, activating its healing energy.
