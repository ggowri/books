The six miles he traveled felt eerily quiet, with the only noticeable change being the increasing intensity of the mental strength disturbance.

Osborne hadn't encountered a single Class S Dark Exotic Beast, which was a bit odd considering the sheer number he had faced earlier.

If anything stood out, it was the overwhelming density of the surrounding energy.

It felt at least twenty times more potent than what he experienced outside.

In this place, training would be incredibly efficient. But the mental strength disturbance was so intense that even top-tier Class S warriors would go mad in just a few minutes.

That was why few could train here—except for Osborne.

When he trained, he relied on Satan's Eye to absorb star power, so the density of the surrounding energy wasn't a major concern for him.

Still, he had no intention of using this place for training.

"If only we could eliminate this mental strength disturbance, this could become a training paradise! The Augustine family members could boost their cultivation speed by tenfold, even twenty-fold, compared to others! What a shame!"

Osborne mused as he pressed deeper into the cavern.

"What causes this mental strength disturbance? Is it a natural phenomenon, some kind of treasure, or the presence of a powerful entity? And why are there so many Dark Exotic Beasts here? Could it be that a Dark Deity truly exists down in these depths?"

After another six miles, Osborne's vigilance heightened.

"This tunnel should be leading toward the heart of the Lostridge Mountains. I've already covered 12 miles, so directly above me ought to be the Lostridge Mountains!

If I keep going for another 60 miles, I should reach the center of the mountains! And if I push another 200 miles beyond that, I'll break through the Lostridge Mountains and find myself beneath Newport City!"

A sense of gravity settled over him.

Despite the distance he'd traveled, there were no signs of the Dark Exotic Beasts. It was as if they had appeared out of thin air.

"This is strange," Osborne muttered to himself.

He continued onward, covering another 12 miles, yet still found no signs of life. The tunnel seemed devoid of any Dark Exotic Beasts.

"Did I take a wrong turn?" Osborne wondered.

There were three tunnels to choose from, and he had picked one at random. Now, after he had traveled over 24 miles without encountering anything unusual, so doubt crept in.

But the tunnel remained vast, and Osborne hadn't reached the end yet. Turning back now seemed unthinkable.

"I have to see this through! I won't be able to relax until I reach the end!"

With that resolve, Osborne quickened his pace. He had been cautious and worried about potential surprises, but after all this time without incident, he felt emboldened to push forward.

His speed increased.

With Satan's Eye guiding him, the winding, deep tunnel felt no different than the surface. He quickly covered another 60 miles.

"I must be at the center of the Lostridge Mountains now!" he murmured.

At that moment, Osborne sensed the energy around him had reached a staggering density—one hundred times that of the outside world. The energy felt almost liquid, swirling like mist.

Just breathing this air would enhance an ordinary person's strength, but the mental strength disturbance had also ramped up to a terrifying level.

In his mind, a protective light membrane enveloped his soul, gently pulsating in response to the chaotic mental strength disturbance surrounding him.

"The mental disturbances here are so severe that any top-tier Class S who dares enter would be instantly crippled! Going mad would be the least of their worries—their souls might shatter and disintegrate in an instant! Even an average god would struggle to withstand this!"

Osborne's expression turned grave.

If it weren't for the Soul Barrier that Isa had taught him, he would have turned back long ago.

And Isa was a disciple of the God of Night.

The Soul Barrier, without a doubt, was a soul defense technique that the God of Night had passed down to Isa. And since the God of Night was a Deity King, did anyone really expect his methods to be simple?

Even so, Osborne could feel the Soul Barrier starting to waver under the pressure. That alone spoke volumes about just how terrifying the mental disturbances around him were.

If they were to double or even triple in strength, even Osborne wouldn't be able to hold out much longer!

Suddenly, Osborne came to an abrupt halt.

"Light?!"

He looked utterly astonished.

In the deep, winding bowels of the earth, he was actually seeing a faint glow!

The light had a faint reddish hue!

"A reddish light? What's ahead? Could it be... a treasure?"

Despite his hesitation, Osborne decided to press on.

Osborne had come this far—over 60 miles—and had finally stumbled upon something unusual. There was no way he was turning back now; he needed to get to the bottom of this.

With his alertness heightened, he prepared himself to use his spatial power to escape at a moment's notice.

As Osborne moved forward, the red light grew more intense, transforming into a vivid, almost bloody hue, flowing like fresh blood.

Osborne felt his heart suddenly pounding in his chest, thumping wildly.
