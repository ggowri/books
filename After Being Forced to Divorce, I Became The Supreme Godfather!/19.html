Chapter 19 Boundless Love

"Who are you?" Alvin asked ingratiatingly.

The woman in red answered calmly, "My name is Helen White, and I am the maid of Ms. Rose Turner, the eldest daughter of the Turner family."

Everyone had heard about Rose Turner!

Her words caused a stir among the crowd.

"Is Ms. Turner here? She's the only diamond member of the Dragon Guild!"

"The Turner family is Yoland's only old-line family, unmatched in its influence. Ms. Turner, as their heir, is the most prestigious figure in Yoland!"

"Ms. Turner isn't just brilliant; she's breathtakingly beautiful. She's like a goddess!"

"If I could see Ms. Turner just once, my trip would be worthwhile!"

"Ms. White, did you say Ms. Turner is issuing a mission?"

The room fell silent as all eyes turned to Helen.

Helen nodded. "I have a painting here. Whoever can decipher it will earn the opportunity to serve Ms. Turner for three days. The reward is thirty million dollars."

To serve the eldest daughter of the Turner family was a dream opportunity for many.

It meant a chance to be close to Rose, to witness her elegance firsthand, and perhaps even earn her favor.

That favor could lead to access to the Turner family's resources and connections.

Even an ordinary person, backed by the Turner family's support, could quickly become one of Yoland's elite.

Not to mention, after three days of service, there was a bonus of thirty million dollars.

The offer was irresistible.

Even Anthony found himself tempted.

The others were utterly enthralled.

"Ms. White, please show us the painting!"

"I never imagined Ms. Turner was also an artist! Her work must be extraordinary!"

The crowd eagerly awaited a glimpse of Rose's creation.

Helen walked to the wall, carefully unwrapped a cloth-covered painting, and hung it up before pulling away the covering.

Every eye widened.

But instead of marveling at an intricate masterpiece, the crowd froze in confusion.

The painting wasn't what they had imagined. On the canvas, rough black lines crisscrossed aimlessly on a plain white background. It looked like a child's random scribbles.

Everyone thought, "What kind of painting is this?

How could it resemble a child's doodle?

Is this truly the work of the goddess-like Ms. Turner?"

"Ms. Turner has said she will come in person in one hour to hear the answers. Whoever solves the painting may leave with her."

"Good luck to you all," Helen added, then turned and left.

The crowd exchanged puzzled looks. In the awkward silence, Alvin suddenly clapped his hands and exclaimed, "As expected of Ms. Turner! She is unconventional. She is... painting with her soul!"

Anne chimed in enthusiastically, "Ms. Turner's art must carry profound meaning. We must reflect deeply!"

"Absolutely! Ms. Turner must be conveying an extraordinary message through this piece!"

"Quiet, everyone! Focus and contemplate!"

The crowd grew solemn, their eyes glued to the lines on the canvas as if hunting for hidden treasures within Rose's work.

Standing at the back, Anthony frowned.

"Painting with her soul? What nonsense!"

Anyone with eyes could see this was nothing more than a random mess. It wasn't even a proper painting, just scribbles.

He thought, "What is this goddess-like Ms. Turner trying to do?

Is she playing everyone for fools?

That doesn't seem right.

Feeling perplexed, Anthony instinctively activated his Devil Eye. A voice echoed in his mind, "Devil Eye activated. Analysis initiated."

As the Devil Eye unraveled the painting's mystery, Anthony's excitement surged.

He thought, "So that is it!"

He finally understood the message hidden in the painting.

And with thirty million dollars on the line, Anthony couldn't help but clench his fist in triumph.

The hour passed quickly, and all eyes turned to the hall's entrance.

Helen appeared, escorting a slender woman dressed in purple.

The woman wore a veil, concealing her face. Yet her graceful figure and striking presence were enough to leave everyone spellbound.

This was the charm of Rose Turner.

Before Rose arrived, Helen's beauty alone had captivated the room. But the moment Rose entered, even with her face hidden, Helen was instantly overshadowed.

"Greetings, Ms. Turner!"

"Nice to see you, Ms. Turner!"

"An honor to meet you, Ms. Turner!"

The crowd eagerly greeted her.

Rose responded with a gentle nod, acknowledging their enthusiasm.

Alvin, barely containing his excitement, stepped forward. "Ms. Turner, I have some unique insights into your painting..."

"Wait," Helen interrupted icily, cutting him off. She gestured toward two Dragon Guards standing nearby.

The guards immediately carried over a chair, placing it respectfully behind Rose.

Only Rose, as the Dragon Guild's diamond member, had the privilege of being attended to by Dragon Guards in this way.

Rose seated herself gracefully. Her voice, melodious and calm, broke the tension. "The rules of the Dragon Guild state that each member may only bring one companion. I have brought Helen, but my family is concerned for my safety and wishes for another person to accompany me. That's why I issued this Mission.

"Whoever deciphers my painting will earn the role.

"Now, let's begin. I'm listening."

"I'll go first!" Alvin shouted, terrified someone might seize the chance before him. "In my interpretation, Ms. Turner's painting embodies boundless love.

"Each seemingly random line represents a profound understanding of the curves of life. These interwoven lines symbolize the interconnectedness of all living beings. They remind us that we are a shared community of destiny, meant to support and cherish one another!"

"This painting is a testament to Ms. Turner's infinite love and compassion for the world!"

Life curves were woven into a shared destiny. Alvin's explanation was brilliant. It perfectly captured the boundless love of Rose, the noble heiress.

The crowd, feeling inferior, assumed Alvin had already won Rose's favor. They even felt the Blake family would grow stronger again under his lead.

Many people began to congratulate Alvin.
