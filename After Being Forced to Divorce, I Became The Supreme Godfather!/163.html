Chapter 162 You Certainly Have Guts

Arthur was stunned, while Anne couldn't hide her excitement.

She quickly asked, "Sir, are you saying that the Turner family has fallen too?"

For the past few days, Anthony had been hiding in the Turner family, and Anne was well aware of this.

So, when the messenger said Anthony was dead, her first thought was that the Turner family must have been destroyed for that to happen.

However, her question seemed to upset the messenger, who replied coldly, "You don't need to know the details."

"You only need to understand that following Archlord Iscairn will be the smartest decision of your life."

"The Evil Spirit Legion will ensure your family thrives for generations, rising to greatness."

"I apologize."

"I spoke out of turn..." Anne immediately bowed, her body stiff with fear.

"Please, rest assured. I swear to serve the Evil Spirit Legion and Archlord Iscairn with my life."

"If I betray, I'll accept any punishment..."

"Yes..."

"We swear to serve you..."

One by one, Anne, Alvin, and the others hurried to pledge their loyalty. They were all eager to prove their allegiance.

Arthur hesitated, still worried about Anthony. But after a moment of thought, he reluctantly joined in.

Seeing him fall in line, Anne smiled with relief and said, "Mr. Hastings, have you finally made up your mind to join Archlord Iscairn?"

Arthur froze, unsure of how to respond.

He hadn't intended to pledge allegiance, but now that the words were out, it seemed too late to backtrack.

If he refused in front of the messenger, he wouldn't make it out of Wolf Fang Valley alive.

He wasn't sure about the messenger's abilities, but the way things were going, the man didn't seem weak.

If a fight broke out, he wasn't confident in his chances.

Besides, Anthony had given strict instructions to gather information quietly, without causing any trouble.

"Yes." After a brief moment, Arthur made up his mind, bowing respectfully.

"I am willing to serve Archlord Iscairn with my life, without hesitation."

To his surprise, the messenger didn't seem particularly impressed. He glanced over at Arthur briefly and then turned away.

Seeing this, Anne grew anxious and quickly said, "Sir, Mr. Hastings is an exceptional B-level Metahuman."

"When Anthony caused chaos at the Smith family banquet, Mr. Hastings single-handedly defeated him."

"It was only after my persuasion that he agreed to join us and follow the Evil Spirit Legion."

Anne was eager to claim credit, hoping to make the most of her earlier efforts to win Arthur over.

"Oh?"

"A B-level Metahuman?"

The messenger finally turned his attention back to Arthur.

"Yes, sir." Arthur nodded, silently cursing Anne for being so talkative.

"Well done." The messenger studied Arthur for a long moment, then glanced at Anne with a satisfied look. "When we meet Archlord Iscairn, I will put in a good word for you."

Anne beamed, bowing deeply. "Thank you. It's the least I could do..."

Among the crowd, Alvin clenched his teeth in frustration. If things kept going this way, Anne might actually end up running Yoland.

At the thought, he was itching to speak out but dared not make a move. He didn't want to step out of line.

Before he could give it any more thought, the messenger waved his hand, signaling everyone to disperse.

"Alright, you may leave now."

"Wait..."

Suddenly, Arthur stepped forward, gathering his courage to ask, "Sir, if I may... what's with all the mining equipment?"

Anne froze, and everyone else was caught off guard.

No one had expected Arthur to be so bold.

After all, if the messenger hadn't mentioned it, it was likely because he didn't want to discuss it.

As expected, the messenger's expression darkened, and he sneered. "You certainly have guts!"

Anne gasped, hurrying to explain. "Sir, he... he didn't mean any disrespect..."

But before she could finish, the messenger's tone softened. "I like brave people."

Anne stood frozen, confused, glancing back and forth between the messenger and Arthur.

Seeing that, Arthur let out a quiet sigh of relief.

The reason he had dared to ask was that he suspected the Evil Spirit Legion's interest in Anne and the others must be part of a bigger scheme.

Now, they were buying up mining equipment in large quantities, which didn't make sense at all.

He had come to Wolf Fang Valley with Anne to help Anthony gather intelligence.

If all he brought back were a few scraps of information, he would look useless. But if he could figure out why the Evil Spirit Legion was buying the equipment, it would be a whole different story.

He was certain that Anthony's suspicions about the Evil Spirit Legion's plot had something to do with these mining tools. And those details were key.
