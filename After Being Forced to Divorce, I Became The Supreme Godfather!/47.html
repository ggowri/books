Chapter 46 The Real Murderer

Everyone was in a frenzy, desperate to catch Anthony.

Standing in the midst of the chaos, Anthony felt utterly helpless.

He had known the risks yet still insisted on coming. Now, there was no way to escape; he could only grit his teeth and get out of the car.

As he stepped out, the Turner family guards rushed forward, all of them loading their weapons, dozens of barrels pointing straight at him.

"Put your hands up. Hold your head and squat down."

"Follow orders immediately or we'll shoot!"

Faced with the guards' frantic threats, Anthony got worked up. He pulled out the invitation Rose had given him and shouted, "Look! I'm a VIP guest invited by Ms. Turner to her party.

"What are you doing right now? Is this how you treat your guests?"

His challenge left the guards at a loss.

To them, Anthony was nothing more than an unforgivable killer, a sworn enemy. When had he suddenly become a distinguished guest?

The Turner family's invitations were reserved for representatives of wealthy or noble families. How could Anthony, a nobody who had been kicked out of the Smith family, possibly receive one?

Could the invitation be fake?

Seeing the guards hesitate, Alvin quickly interjected, "His invitation has to be fake! He's a nobody with no power or influence. How could he ever qualify for an invitation from the Turner family?

"Don't let him fool you! His last assassination attempt failed; this time, he's just trying to escape using a counterfeit invitation to carry out another attack against Ms. Turner.

"Take him down!"

Anne also gritted her teeth and shouted, "I'm Anthony's ex-wife. I can testify that he's the murderer. Go get him!"

The other guests shared their disdain for Anthony, echoing shouts demanding the Turner Guards to take him out immediately.

The guards, irritated by the uproar, felt their tempers flare. A few of the less disciplined ones, eager to get rewards, couldn't resist pulling the trigger.

For Rose's sake, Anthony didn't want to fight the guards. Even though surrounded, he could easily take them all out if he unleashed his full power.

He called out again, "I'll say it again! I am a distinguished guest invited by Ms. Turner to her birthday party.

"Put down your guns and let me in. If Ms. Turner finds out, you won't be able to handle the fallout!"

Upon hearing Anthony mention Rose's name, the guards exchanged uncertain glances. Just then, a fierce howl echoed in the distance, followed by a shadow swooping down.

The leader barked, "Captain Stein is here!

"Form up!"

In an instant, the guards retracted their weapons and lined up neatly.

A muscular man in black landed from mid-air, causing the ground to tremble upon impact.

The onlookers were stunned.

The captain of the Turner Guards was indeed a powerhouse.

Anthony recognized him—it was Dariel Stein, the wolf metahuman who led the Turner Guards.

Dariel's expression was grim, and his sharp gaze was locked onto Anthony.

The two exchanged looks, each trying to gauge the other's intentions.

"You're Anthony. You..." Dariel finally spoke, his voice tight with barely restrained fury.

Anticipating his words, Anthony cut him off, shouting, "Dariel, as the captain of the Turner Guards, you conspired to assassinate Ms. Turner. What punishment do you deserve for that?"

This abrupt accusation not only shocked the onlookers but also left Dariel bewildered.

"What are you talking about?" he fumed, his face paling. "I'm loyal to the Turner family and to Ms. Turner. There's no way I would ever betray her!

"Don't make baseless accusations!"

Anthony smirked. "I have proof. You can't deny it."

Furious, Dariel clenched his teeth. "Bullshit! What proof do you have? Speak up!

"No matter what you say, I won't let you go today!"

Noticing Dariel's unease, Anthony pressed on, "First, as captain of the Turner Guards, you were supposed to pick Ms. Turner up from the Dragon Guild that day. I was with Ms. Turner and Helen at the time, and they had to take my car home because you didn't show up.

"Where were you? You deliberately delayed picking up Ms. Turner to create an opportunity for the real murderer. Are you still going to deny it?"

Dariel glared at him, his teeth grinding, but he struggled to respond.

All he could manage, with eyes blazing, was, "I was delayed because I received other orders at the last minute. I'm loyal; I would never betray Ms. Turner!

"Anthony, you murderer! Don't twist the truth!"

Anthony sneered, "Really? Then listen. My second piece of evidence is that I clearly protected Ms. Turner—I was her savior. But you claim I'm the killer. Why would you do that?

"Only the real murderer would be so eager to label me as the culprit to divert suspicion. Am I right?"
