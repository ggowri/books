Chapter 49 The Master of Close Combat

Sergio felt humiliated and furious. He leaped up and spat blood, shouting, "No way! Switch to live ammunition!"

His men, equally angry, immediately began loading live rounds.

"Enough!" Dariel bellowed, his face gloomy with anger. "Losers, don't you think you've embarrassed yourselves enough? You lost. Now get out!"

Although Sergio often disobeyed Dariel in private, he wouldn't dare challenge him openly due to Dariel's strength and standing. Glaring at Anthony, he ground his teeth and retreated.

From that moment on, Anthony became Sergio's sworn enemy. He vowed to find an opportunity to kill Anthony.

Anthony was indifferent to Sergio's hatred; he had already undergone his transformation, and people like Sergio were insignificant in his eyes.

The onlookers fell silent.

Anthony's power had silenced any lingering contempt.

Anne reluctantly spoke up, "Anthony, so you're a sharpshooter. You've been hiding your abilities for three years during our marriage. What are you really after?"

Alvin shouted, "Captain Stein, this guy must have some hidden agenda! Please investigate him thoroughly!"

Dariel's gaze intensified as he addressed Anthony seriously, "You've truly impressed me. Initially, I thought you were a space metahuman who could dodge bullets by manipulating space, but I see now that's not the case.

"You countered while being flanked on three sides, predicting the bullets' trajectories and using your agile movements. You really do deserve the title of sharpshooter."

Anthony smiled in response. "Thank you."

Dariel whispered, "But that alone won't cut it. Remember, your real opponent is me.

"Come on. If you can beat me, not only will I let you in, but I'll also fully support your investigation."

Dariel lowered his body slightly as he prepared for both defense and attack.

An overwhelming aura of menace radiated from him, making him appear vulnerable yet somehow untouchable.

It was clear he was a master of close combat.

This was what a werewolf was good at.

Right now, Dariel was fighting Anthony as a regular Warrior, having not yet transformed into his true werewolf form.

Anthony, for his part, didn't activate the Devil Eye. After consuming the mutant werewolf blood and essence extracted by the Seraph, his physical strength had reached Warrior level.

Moreover, last night, he had acquired a vast array of skills from Jane.

As he assessed the secrets of close combat used by the Royal Guards, the blood in his veins began to ignite with excitement.

"It's over for him. When it comes to close combat, Captain Stein is unbeatable in Yoland!"

"Anthony, give it up! Just because you can shoot doesn't mean you can take on Captain Stein. You're asking for trouble!"

Laughter erupted around him, disbelief evident in their voices.

None of them thought Anthony stood a chance against Dariel in hand-to-hand combat.

But Dariel's expression grew increasingly serious. As an expert, he recognized that while Anthony appeared relaxed, he was actually poised for both offense and defense—a level of skill only a true master could achieve.

Dariel hadn't expected to face a tough opponent today.

He couldn't believe such a powerful fighter existed in Yoland.

Initially, he planned to wait for Anthony to strike first, but the tension compelled him to act.

With a roar, he sprang into the air, his movements a blur. In an instant, he was above Anthony, his fist descending like a diamond hammer, creating a gust of wind as it crashed down.

His first move was a swift, lethal strike.

It seemed Anthony had anticipated this; he smoothly dodged to the side, narrowly avoiding Dariel's punch.

With a resounding boom, Dariel's fist smashed into the ground, creating a massive crater and sending sand and gravel flying everywhere. The spectators screamed and backed away.

"Captain Stein is awesome!"

"That's amazing!"

"This punch is invincible!"

"Anthony, is dodging all you can do? What kind of man are you? If you have any guts, fight Captain Stein head-on!"

The crowd erupted into wild shouts.

It felt like they had been transported back to an ancient arena.

Anthony pushed aside all distractions and focused on his battle with Dariel.

Fists clashed, feet struck out, and the intensity of their fight filled the air with a fierce wind and an atmosphere thick with murderous intent.

Soon, those who had looked down on Anthony fell silent.

The strength he displayed before them erased any doubt.

The mockery that he was just a powerless freeloader, that he relied on drugs to unleash a fleeting potential, and that he was merely boasting—all of it was proven wrong.

Everyone had to concede one undeniable truth: Anthony was not just a sharpshooter but also a master Warrior.

He was a top-tier fighter, taking on Dariel, the captain of the Turner family guards, without faltering.

"How is this possible? When did this loser become so powerful? No, I can't accept this," Anne murmured.

As she watched Anthony's commanding presence, she realized she had let go of a treasure. Her eyes misted with tears.

As she cast a glance at the shocked Alvin beside her, her regret deepened.

Anne silently vowed that if Anthony truly defeated Dariel, she would publicly apologize to him and ask for a second chance at their marriage.

She believed Anthony loved her deeply, and if she showed him kindness, he would surely come back to her.

With Anthony's support, Anne felt confident she could elevate the Smith family to one of the top families in Yoland in no time.

She imagined the admiration she would receive then, smiling mysteriously like a devoted wife gazing at her valiant husband on the battlefield.
