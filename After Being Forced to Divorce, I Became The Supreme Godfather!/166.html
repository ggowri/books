Chapter 165 No Turning Back

Carolyn's blood boiled as a familiar laugh echoed from the cliff above.

A rope dropped, and a man in black quickly descended.

Then, ropes appeared on both sides, each with a figure in black.

There were at least thirty men. Carolyn's eyes narrowed, and she instinctively stepped back, putting herself between Rose and the looming threat.

She wasn't scared, but she needed to stay sharp. A few steps back was the only way to maintain control.

But the edge of the cliff was right behind her, and her retreat was blocked by a railing.

"Vincent?" Rose froze, staring in disbelief.

"Why is it you? What's going on?"

At the front of the group stood none other than Vincent.

Rose, still naive and trusting, hadn't yet caught on to the danger.

Vincent laughed heartily, landing smoothly on the ground. "Rose, you're really too cute for your own good."

"What's going on? Well, I'm here to take you back. You believe me, don't you?"

Rose's face shifted from confusion to shock. "You..." she began, then faltered as the truth hit her. "Vincent, what are you planning to do?"

"My plan?" Vincent's grin vanished, replaced by a cold, dangerous smile. "I'm here to take back what's mine. And that means... killing you."

"Enough!" Carolyn's voice cut through the tension like a knife.

"Vincent, don't push it."

"Last time, I let you go because you're part of the Turner family. But don't think I'll be so lenient now."

"Cross me, and I'll use the family's laws to throw you out."

As she spoke, Carolyn was already scanning the situation, calculating her next move.

Carolyn wasn't fazed by the thirty or so men closing in.

She'd known Vincent's true nature for a long time, and nothing about his actions surprised her.

In the past, when Vincent and Rose had clashed, Carolyn, as the leader of the Turner family, had chosen to turn a blind eye.

She understood that some competition could be a valuable lesson for Rose. As long as the larger picture wasn't in danger, there was no need to step in too heavily.

Besides, Carolyn had always trusted that, as long as she was around, Vincent wouldn't be able to cause too much trouble.

That was until Rose was nearly killed in a werewolf assassination. Afterward, Anthony had gathered evidence that pointed directly at Vincent, enough to make it all but certain he was behind the attack.

And yet, Vincent had killed Calvin in front of her, silencing him before he could expose the truth.

Now, even though the pieces were falling into place, Carolyn still couldn't make a move without solid proof.

Despite everything, Vincent remained a member of the Turner family.

So it was a good choice to exile him to the Mines.

But she never thought he'd snap this fast, going full psycho and launching an outright rebellion.

Vincent's laughter rang out again, sharp and derisive. "Expel me from the family?"

"You think we are at home now?"

"Do you really think I'd fear you?"

"You know me well enough. I won't make a move unless I'm fully prepared."

As he spoke, more than thirty men closed in from all sides, trapping Carolyn and Rose.

Vincent stood tall among them, an air of complete confidence surrounding him.

"Vincent!" Carolyn's face grew cold, her tone firm as she called out to him.

"This is a point of no return. You really want to go down this path?"

"You still have a chance to stop this. If you back off now, I'll pretend none of this ever happened."

"Cut the act, Carolyn!" Vincent spat bitterly, his voice seething with fury.

"You've pushed me into this."

"You've spent your entire life protecting the Turner family, never marrying, and I actually respect that about you."

"But what about her?" He pointed accusingly at Rose.

"She hasn't even taken control yet, and she's already entangled with that shady Anthony."

"Why should our family's fate be tied to an outsider?"

"And the empire that the Turner family has built over generations. Why should it fall into the hands of someone like him?"

His eyes burned with loathing as they fixed on Rose, the hatred in them barely concealed.

"No," Carolyn replied, her voice unwavering.

"You're wrong. I never planned to hand over the Turner family to anyone."

"Rose is part of the family, and she is the most gifted individual the Turner family has seen in centuries."

"Ha! Talented, sure," Vincent sneered, cutting her off with contempt.

"But so what? You really think that soft-hearted Rose has what it takes to lead?"

"Handing the Turner family over to her is the same as handing it over to Anthony, isn't it?"
