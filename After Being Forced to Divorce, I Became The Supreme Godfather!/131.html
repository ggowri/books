Chapter 130 Best to Be Prepared

The arrival of the Evil Spirit Legion was all within Anthony's expectations!

"This..."

Anthony's attitude puzzled Carolyn and Rose, leaving everyone else bewildered.

He didn't seem worried at all; in fact, he appeared somewhat excited.

Had this guy really gone bonkers from excessive drug use?

Anne and the others weren't the only ones who believed Anthony's powers stemmed from drugs; some Turner family members shared the same sentiment.

Of course, this was mainly because Anthony himself had admitted to being a test subject at the Godfather Research Institute, so he had no one to blame but himself.

Instead of answering their questions, Anthony changed the subject and asked, "Aunt Carolyn, how much of the Turner family's security force has been restored?"

"About a third," Carolyn replied, letting go of her previous concerns when she saw Anthony shifting the topic.

She trusted that Anthony's confidence meant he had something up his sleeve.

If he didn't want to discuss it, she wouldn't press the matter.

The Turner family's security forces had taken a massive hit in the previous battle, losing two-thirds of their strength.

Such losses wouldn't be easy to recover from anytime soon.

Anthony wasn't surprised at all. "Here's an idea," he said. "Just to be safe, I think Aunt Carolyn should hire some outside help.

"There's this security company, Draconic Shield, that's been making a name for itself lately. They seem pretty capable. Might be worth a shot."

"Draconic Shield?"

Carolyn paused, then smiled. "Actually, I know a little about them. They're led by twelve people who call themselves the Zodiac.

"And each one is incredibly skilled. They've taken the city by storm. They're definitely the real deal."

"Exactly."

Anthony smiled with satisfaction. "No matter what, since the Evil Spirit Legion won't let things go, it's best to be prepared and hire some extra help."

"Alright," Carolyn agreed, then called out the door, "Dariel, come in."

Dariel strode in from outside, nodding respectfully to Anthony before turning to Carolyn with a bow. "Ms. Turner, what are your orders?"

"Get in touch with Draconic Shield. Hire enough of their people to patch up the holes in the Turner family's defenses."

"Yes, Ms. Carolyn Turner."

Dariel bowed and accepted the mission without unnecessary questions.

As head of security, he knew better than anyone the current state of the Turner family's defenses.

Anthony's actions weren't just about drumming up business for Draconic Shield. He was building their reputation.

"Hold up."

Just as Dariel was about to leave, Anthony called out to him.

After studying him for a moment, he asked, "You're about to break through, aren't you?"

"Huh?"

Dariel was clearly startled by the question and then nodded. "Yes, how did you know, Mr. Johnson?"

How did Anthony know?

The truth was, he didn't. He'd just happened to scan Dariel's power levels with Devil Eye on a whim, and it had alerted him to Dariel's imminent breakthrough.

Of course, Anthony wasn't about to reveal anything about Devil Eye. Instead, he kept them guessing and said, "Come find me after you're done with your tasks. I can help you with your breakthrough."

"Yes!"

"Thank you so much, Mr. Johnson!"

Anthony's words took Dariel entirely by surprise. He nodded respectfully, his eyes wide with a mix of disbelief and hope.

The scene stunned everyone present.

It was clear Dariel was on the verge of a breakthrough, but to claim he could help him achieve it...

That was something else entirely.

A wave of astonishment rippled through the group. Rose and Carolyn exchanged glances, surprise evident in their eyes.

Yet, unlike the others who were filled with doubt, they silently chose to believe Anthony.

They couldn't explain it, but they had a feeling that if Anthony said he could do it, he could. It was an almost unconditional trust.

Previously, Dariel's power level had been somewhere between Level A and Level B.

After his life-or-death battle with the Turner family, it made sense that Dariel would experience a breakthrough.

Still, being able to spot someone's impending breakthrough at a glance was hard to swallow.

Because of this, many members of the Turner family dropped their dismissive attitudes when they looked at Anthony again.

Since Anthony seemed so confident about the Smith family situation, Carolyn decided to hold her tongue.

Despite her concerns, she let the others leave first.

After Anthony and Rose were the only ones left, Carolyn still expressed her concerns for Anthony and the Turner family's determination to protect him.

However, Anthony once again declined their offer.

Then, he asked about the Mines.

Just as Rose had said, Carolyn didn't know what secrets the Mines held either.

However, Carolyn and Rose had different opinions about Vincent.

Or rather, Carolyn's view aligned with Anthony's.

Vincent couldn't be trusted!

Keeping Vincent at the Turner family Mines wasn't a wise move.

If the mines held no secrets, that would be one thing. But if they did, Vincent would stop at nothing to claim them for himself!

Therefore, Vincent's presence at the mines posed a potential threat to the Turner family.

Anthony was direct about it. "In that case, we should nip this problem in the bud.

"I'll make a trip to the mines in a few days."

Anthony knew he had to go to the Mines.

It would be ideal if someone from the Turner family could accompany him.

"Yeah."

Carolyn nodded in agreement. "We definitely need to check it out, but for now, we should focus on what the Smith family is up to.

"If the Evil Spirit Legion is dead set on targeting you, we can't let our guard down..."
