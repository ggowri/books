Chapter 154 Necros

At the Turner Manor, Rose couldn't ignore Anthony's words.

After a little while, once Anthony had left, she hesitated before deciding to call Carolyn. But just as she was about to dial, Carolyn's call came through first.

"Hello, Aunt Carolyn?"

"Rose, there's news about Vincent. The Mines have Leyline!"

"What?" Rose exclaimed, shocked.

"Leyline? Are you serious?"

As a member of the Turner family, Rose knew exactly what Leyline was, and how crucial they were for Warriors and Metahumans.

Even a small piece of Spirit Stone was incredibly valuable.

The thing was, Leyline was extremely rare these days.

"Yep!"

"Vincent sent the news, and from what it looks like, the scale of Leyline is huge!"

Not long ago, Carolyn had been worried about the situation at the mine, but then Vincent called her, practically bursting with excitement, confirming that they had discovered a massive Leyline.

Carolyn, unable to contain her excitement, spoke quickly, and Rose, equally thrilled, asked, "Aunt Carolyn, what do we do next?"

"Well, we head to the Mines, of course!"

"We need to go now. The sooner, the better!"

Carolyn's excitement was palpable. "I'll be back in half an hour. You get ready."

"Tell Sergio to pick a team and have them ready in half an hour so we can head out overnight."

Carolyn's enthusiasm was understandable.

The Turner family had been in decline for years, each generation weaker than the last.

As the head of the family, she was more anxious than anyone but felt helpless.

But now, the Turner family was about to possess a Leyline.

A Leyline alone could bring back the family's former glory or even surpass it.

"Got it!" Rose could hardly contain her joy. She was so thrilled that she almost hung up right away.

But then, she remembered Anthony's warning and quickly added, "Aunt Carolyn, wait."

"Anthony had to step out for something, but before he left, he told me to let you know that something doesn't feel right about the mine."

"He said we should wait for him to come back before doing anything rash."

There was a brief silence on the other end of the line before Carolyn responded, "Anthony is cautious, and he's only looking out for us."

"But he probably doesn't know about the Leyline yet."

"The reason he felt something was off is because he couldn't reach Vincent."

"But Vincent explained that he was unreachable because he took the lead and went into the mine himself to help with the excavation."

"It's precisely because of his involvement that they discovered the Leyline so quickly."

"Wait..." Rose hesitated, concerned.

"Aunt Carolyn, Vincent took the lead? I don't know if I believe that."

She knew Vincent too well.

Vincent liked to exaggerate and take credit, so hearing that he took the lead seemed far-fetched to her.

Carolyn chuckled, clearly understanding her doubts. "I know. He's not the type to take the lead."

"But whatever the case, he sent the data confirming the Leyline, and it's nearly certain that the mine has them."

"So don't worry. You go ahead and tell Sergio to prepare. We leave in half an hour."

"Oh, and keep this to ourselves for now. Don't tell anyone else."

"Got it. I'll get everything in order." Seeing how firm Carolyn was, Rose stopped overthinking it.

What Carolyn said made sense. Anthony's concern came from the fact that Vincent couldn't be reached.

But now that Vincent had reached out to confirm the Leyline discovery, it showed his loyalty to the family.

Any earlier worries were just unnecessary.

Half an hour later, Carolyn, along with Rose, Sergio, and more than thirty loyal guards, set off for the mine.

But halfway there, Rose still wasn't at ease. After quietly stepping away from Carolyn, she tried calling Anthony again.

The call didn't go through, so she decided not to dwell on it any longer.

Meanwhile, in the Virstino Mountains, Anthony moved with the speed of the wind, arriving at a hidden valley.

He suddenly stopped and turned around, shouting, "I know you're from the Evil Spirit Legion. Stop hiding."

"Show yourself!"

With a swift motion, Anthony flung a stone from his hand. The stone shot out like a bullet, slicing through the air with a sharp whoosh.

The stone flew about 30 feet before smashing through a thick tree, and moments later, a massive figure appeared from behind the tree.

The person was over 6.5 feet tall, with broad shoulders and a muscular build, dressed in night gear with a black belt at his waist.

It was none other than one of the Four Demon Lords, Necros.
