Chapter 187 Impossible

A crimson mist spread rapidly through the air, engulfing everything in mere seconds.

The blood-red fog carried an energy that sent chills down one's spine, radiating a presence that made even the bravest hearts quiver.

Its murderous energy felt like an ancient beast unleashed—wild, savage, and utterly terrifying.

Anthony, who had been charging forward, stopped dead in his tracks. "Blood Shadow? How the hell does he know that technique?"

His eyes widened in shock. But realization quickly dawned on him—Arthur had mentioned they were quadruplets. Of course, they would share the same abilities.

The memory of his defeat by Necros flashed through Anthony's mind. Blood Shadow had been the deciding factor in that battle.

Without it, Anthony was confident he could have killed Necros.

Even if Mordred was somewhat stronger than Necros, Anthony believed he still stood a chance if he went all out.

With no other options left, Anthony went all in for a desperate final attack!

He activated his Devil Eye and closed the distance using Whirlwind Step. In rapid succession, he unleashed a devastating combo—Super Fist, Thunder Claw, and Killing Kick—all enhanced by his Devil Eye. The relentless assault pushed Mordred to the brink.

For the finishing blow, Anthony planned to break through Mordred's defense with Dragonfang and end this once and for all.

Anthony was certain this combination would be too much for Mordred to handle.

And he was right—or so he thought.

But in all his calculations, Anthony had forgotten one crucial detail—Blood Shadow.

Before Anthony could react, a massive figure rose from the crimson mist.

Mordred's body grew rapidly, transforming in the blink of an eye into a thirty-foot monstrosity.

His face twisted into something inhuman—blue skin, protruding fangs, and features so grotesque they defied description.

Despite his hideous appearance, the creature radiated a sense of pure killing intent.

Anthony was charging forward with Dragonfang, committed to finishing Mordred off—but instead, Mordred's foot caught him square in the chest, sending him flying backward.

There was no way to dodge. Anthony had been moving too fast, carrying too much momentum forward to change direction.

Mordred's transformation happened in the blink of an eye.

The kick came fast, though it seemed more intended to push Anthony back than to cause real damage.

Lucky for him—if Mordred had put his full force behind it, that single blow could have taken Anthony out of the fight completely.

Anthony had already witnessed the terrifying power of Blood Shadow.

Its combination of raw strength and mystical properties made it practically invincible.

Even when he fought Necros, Anthony barely managed to hold his own using Dragon Avatar at full power.

Now, Mordred was clearly stronger than Necros had ever been, which meant his defensive capabilities would be even more formidable.

To make matters worse, Anthony couldn't maintain Dragon Avatar for much longer.

And even with it, he probably wouldn't stand a chance against Mordred.

This dark magic was beyond anything he'd expected. At his current level, Anthony was completely outmatched.

The gap in their powers was massive. Mordred was fighting at Level C Warrior, maybe even Metahuman level, while Anthony was stuck at Level B.

There was only one way he could hope to defeat Mordred. He needed to break through his limits once again.

His Devil Eye would have to evolve one more time. If he could upgrade it to version 3.0, the new powers it would grant might give him a fighting chance.

Anthony knew that with enough power to break through to Level C Warrior and Metahuman status, he could take down the seemingly invincible Mordred.

But there was a catch—breaking through wasn't simple.

Each time Anthony had reached a new level, it had been directly tied to the Chosen Path.

To advance now, he'd need to complete the project's third phase—finding the Leyline. The problem was that nobody knew its exact location.

He'd asked Arthur about it before, but Vincent had been careful to keep the Leyline's location a closely guarded secret.

The Virstino Mountains stretched for hundreds of miles, and even though Anthony knew the Leyline was somewhere in the Turner family mines within those mountains, finding it while being hunted by Mordred seemed like an impossible dream.

Anthony gasped in shock.

"Oh my God! Aunt Carolyn, what... what is that thing?" Rose's voice trembled as she stared in disbelief, her whole body shaking.

They watched in horror as a normal person transformed before their eyes into a thirty-foot monster. Rose had seen many strange things in her life, but nothing had prepared her for this.

Carolyn drew in a sharp breath, her face paling. "This... this has to be dark magic. Incredibly powerful dark magic."

"It looks like he's been enhanced with Metahuman abilities," she continued, shaking her head in disbelief. "But that doesn't make any sense. Mordred is clearly a Warrior. How could he possibly have merged Metahuman powers with dark magic? It should be impossible."

She was clearly baffled by this unprecedented combination of abilities, having never encountered anything like it before.

Yet, unlike Rose, who was trembling with fear, Carolyn maintained her composure.

After sharing her quick assessment, she clenched her fists and reached deep within herself, trying to tap into her remaining power.

The effects of the medicine were wearing off, and she had to find a way. Anthony needed her help.

Against a monster like this, even someone as strong as Anthony didn't stand a chance.

The sound of breaking bones echoed through the air as Mordred moved like a ghost, closing the distance in an instant. His fist crashed into Anthony, sending him flying, only to follow up with a brutal kick before Anthony could recover.

Anthony was completely outmatched. In Mordred's hands, he might as well have been a ragdoll—one moment thrown through the air like a discarded toy and the next slammed into the ground with bone-crushing force.

The sound of brutal impacts echoed through the air without pause.

Thank god for Anthony's Dragon Avatar. A normal human body would've been pulverized by now.

But even with the Dragon Avatar's protection, how much longer could he hold out? If he couldn't break free soon, death was inevitable.
