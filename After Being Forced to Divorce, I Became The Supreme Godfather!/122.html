Chapter 121 Thirteen Guardians

Rose exclaimed flushed, with a hint of reluctance.

Anthony could see that she was still unsatisfied. As a woman, she was more reserved after all.

Anthony flipped over and pounced on Rose.

As Rose's desire grew, Anthony secretly practiced the dual cultivation technique.

In the bedroom, there was an endless moan.

Rose's body radiated with a faint mist as if her superpower had been involuntarily triggered.

Could it be self-repair?

Anthony couldn't be sure.

What he did know was that their dual cultivation technique had noticeably enhanced his strength.

Afterward, they fell asleep, exhausted. While Rose slumbered deeply, Anthony sat cross-legged on the bed, practicing the Energy-absorbing Breathing.

The next morning, by the time Rose awoke, the sun was already high in the sky, and Anthony had long since left.

Early that morning, he'd received a call from Seraph. Arthur had arrived for his briefing.

Outside the grand hall of the Celestial Palace, Arthur stood in formation with a group of people.

Anthony approached with long strides, and Arthur hurried forward to greet him with reverence. "Mr. Johnson," Arthur said respectfully.

Anthony scanned the group. Counting them, his brows furrowed. "Only twelve people?"

Arthur nodded hastily.

"Yes, but rest assured, each one is worth ten ordinary experts.

"They all have unparalleled skills. Small in number, but their power is extraordinary."

"Is that so?"

Anthony's skeptical gaze swept over the group.

The twelve individuals varied greatly in appearance. Some were towering and muscular, while others, including a few women, seemed almost frail.

"Hold on, Mr. Johnson."

Sensing Anthony's doubt, Arthur didn't explain.

Instead, he turned to a burly man in the group and gestured. "Angus, show him."

"Yes, sir!"

The man named Angus stepped forward.

With a shake of his body, the muscles in his arms swelled to an unnatural size.

Then, with a thunderous punch, he struck the stone floor beneath them.

The floor, crafted from reinforced granite, was meant to be unyielding.

Boom!

Crack!

The ground shattered instantly, fractures spreading several feet outward from Angus' fist.

His massive hand was embedded deep within the stone.

Arthur seized the moment to explain. "Mr. Johnson, Angus is naturally gifted with immense strength and is a Level A physical metahuman.

"His punches carry the force of a thousand pounds!"

Anthony nodded in approval.

"Impressive. Who else here is a metahuman?"

"I am."

Before Arthur could answer, a hulking man with a wild mane and a thick beard stepped forward.

Broad-shouldered and towering, with a face covered in a thick beard and a mane of unkempt hair, he looked every bit like a wandering vagabond at first glance.

The man let out the roar of a lion that shook the air, his body radiating an animalistic energy.

He was a beast-type metahuman—a lion.

"Good. Anyone else?" Anthony remarked, hands in his pockets.

A slender woman stepped out next.

She waved her hands, and the once-calm courtyard was suddenly swept by a fierce gale.

The wind, carrying fine grains of sand, surged toward an ornamental tree, reducing its leaves and branches to fragments in an instant.

Anthony maintained a calm expression but was secretly delighted.

Arthur hadn't exaggerated—each of these individuals truly possessed exceptional abilities.

Of the twelve, five were metahumans, all Level A.

The rest were Warriors, each with their own unique talents.

Among them was a seemingly harmless woman, but Arthur explained that she was an expert in mechanical traps and concealed weapons, capable of killing without a trace.

Another was a quiet sharpshooter with a long rifle strapped to her back—an expert marksman who never missed her target.

After a thorough evaluation, Anthony was satisfied.

He released the group from the soul contract they'd been forced into by the Evil Spirit Legion and replaced it with his own pact.

Most of them had no real names, only nicknames or code names given by the Evil Spirit Legion.

Anthony didn't care for that.

"Since there are exactly twelve of you, from now on, you'll be named after the Zodiac.

"You are the founding members of the Draconic Shield and my most trusted team. Understood?"

"Yes, Mr. Johnson!

"Thank you for bestowing us with names!"

Freed from their previous soul contracts, the group visibly relaxed and expressed genuine respect for Anthony.

Arthur hesitated, then asked, "Mr. Johnson, what about me?"
