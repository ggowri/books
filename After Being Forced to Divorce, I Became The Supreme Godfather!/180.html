Chapter 179 Terrifying Power

The battle at Crimson Canyon erupted with a thunderous crash, shaking the entire area!

Their fight was absolute chaos. The sound of their blows echoed through the canyon, deafening even through the screen. Sand and rocks flew everywhere as they clashed.

The battle raged across every inch of terrain. They started on the road, then moved to the cliffs, down into the valley, and back to the highway again.

The once-smooth pavement was now completely destroyed, riddled with craters and debris.

"This Aaron's got some serious skills," Vincent remarked, arms crossed as he watched from the hidden chamber. He was clearly impressed with Aaron's performance.

By Vincent's calculations, it would take no more than thirty more exchanges to finish Anthony off.

Sure, Anthony's strength was terrifying in its own right, but neither Vincent nor Anne gave that fact much thought right now.

They only cared about one thing—Anthony was about to die, with no doubt about it.

Another explosive impact rang out as Aaron's fist slammed Anthony into the cliff face, sending clouds of dust billowing into the air.

The solid rock wall cracked and splintered from the force of the impact.

"Ugh!" Anthony's body shuddered as blood sprayed from his mouth, his face turning ghostly pale in an instant.

"Hah!" Vincent threw his head back with a maniacal laugh. "Are you watching this, Rose? Ms. Carolyn Turner?"

"Anthony's about to die, and trust me, it's gonna be slow and painful. I'll make you watch as we slice his head off, inch by inch."

"No! Anthony!" Rose burst into tears, gripping the iron chains until her knuckles turned white, veins popping on her hands as tears streamed down her face.

In the next cell, Carolyn couldn't bear to watch anymore. She squeezed her eyes shut, consumed by guilt that felt like it was eating her alive.

God, how she regretted everything!

If only she'd listened to Anthony's warnings!

The hatred burned inside her. Why hadn't she killed Vincent when she had the chance?

"What's this?" Vincent's voice turned ice cold. "Who said you could close your eyes?"

"Sergio, hold her down and force her eyes open!" he barked. "Make her watch Anthony die. Make her watch as we take his head!"

Vincent was losing it completely, high on his own bloodlust and twisted excitement.

"Yes... Mr. Vincent Turner." Sergio's voice wavered slightly, his hesitation obvious despite his attempt to hide it.

This was the lady of the Turner family, after all. She had run the Turner family for over a decade.

That kind of authority, built up over years, wasn't something to be disrespected.

But at this point, Sergio had crossed the line of no return. Steeling himself, he strode toward Carolyn.

Then, using the chains to his advantage, he roughly grabbed her head and forced her eyelids open, making her face the massive screen.

"Sergio! Vincent! You animals!" Carolyn spat through gritted teeth. "If you're real men, then just kill me and get it over with!"

For someone like Carolyn, this treatment was beyond humiliation. It was an absolute disgrace. Vincent's orders and Sergio's actions were nothing short of an insult to her dignity.

As head of the Turner family, she'd never faced such degradation in her life.

But there was nothing she could do. Not only was she severely injured, but Vincent had also injected her with a power-suppressing drug.

Even though she was a Level C Elemental Metahuman, the drug had reduced her to a state of complete helplessness.

Vincent threw his head back with a laugh, shrugging casually. "Don't blame me for being cruel. You brought this on yourself by being so damn stubborn!"

"All you had to do was hand over leadership of the Turner family to me, and none of this would've happened."

"What do you say, Ms. Carolyn Turner? Just name me as head of the family, and I promise you'll both walk free." Vincent leaned in closer with a smirk. "I'll even make sure you're taken care of in your golden years—the best retirement money can buy."

"Go to hell!" Carolyn spat in disgust. "You monster! Get this through your thick skull. I'd rather die than let you take control of the Turner family!"

"Without my blessing, you'll never be a legitimate head of the family. You'll always be nothing but a traitor to the Turner name—just a snake who turned against his own blood!"

Vincent's face twisted with rage as he struck Carolyn hard across the face. "You witch! Don't test me. I could kill you right here and now!"

"Then do it!" Carolyn shot back, her voice steady despite the stinging in her cheek. "Man up, go ahead and kill me!"

"Vincent, stop!" Rose screamed, horrified by the scene unfolding before her. "If you lay another finger on Aunt Carolyn, Anthony will make you pay for it. I swear to God!"

"Anthony?" Vincent's face twisted with rage, his eyes bulging as he growled, "Just wait! Once Anthony's dead, let's see who you've got left to save you!"

Still, despite his threats, Vincent couldn't bring himself to kill Carolyn, even after all this hesitation.

She was right. Without her blessing, he'd never be a legitimate head of the family, even if he took the position by force.

"You're finished, you worthless piece of trash!" Arthur's laughter boomed through the screen.

The footage showed Anthony backed against the cliff's edge, looking worse for wear—blood trickling from his mouth, his hair and clothes a mess.

Arthur advanced menacingly, but he quietly muttered under his breath, "I don't know their exact capabilities, but those four are quadruplets."

"From what I can tell, they're pretty much equal in strength. Vincent calls them the Four Demon Lords."

While pretending to be overwhelmed, Anthony's mind raced with this new information.

"The Four Demon Lords!" Anthony thought. So Necros, the one he'd faced last night, was just one of them?

Anthony couldn't shake off the memory of Necros' terrifying power. That fight had been way too close for comfort. And now, Arthur was telling him Vincent had four warriors of that caliber under his command?
