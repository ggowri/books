Chapter 119 The Charm of Maturity

The battle for the Turner family stretched from noon until nightfall.

As dusk descended, Anthony finished his bath and, with Rose by his side, made his way to the main hall.

After their union, Anthony's power had grown exponentially. His Devil Eye had also broken through its limits, granting him numerous new abilities.

However, the changes in him were not immediately apparent.

In contrast, Rose had made significant strides. Her healing superpower had reached Level B, and she had awakened a new elemental power: Water power.

Previously, she had used mist to channel her healing power, but now, she could fully manipulate water itself.

Her appearance had also undergone noticeable changes.

Her skin now resembled delicate white jade, smooth and flawless, glowing with a soft radiance.

After their union, her noble aura was now infused with a sense of maturity and grace.

In the main hall, Carolyn, already washed and waiting, greeted them with a smile when she saw them enter together.

She walked forward to meet them, her expression warm with approval.

Anthony was still somewhat of a stranger to Carolyn. They had only known each other for a day, and that day had been filled with life-or-death struggles for the Turner family.

In their darkest hour, it was Anthony, a complete stranger, who had stepped forward to save them, disregarding his own safety.

This debt of gratitude was not something Carolyn could forget.

Moreover, Rose had spoken of Anthony as her true soulmate, and their bond had deepened.

Rose's breakthrough in power was all the proof she needed.

"Mr. Johnson, please sit down," Carolyn said.

"Ms. Carolyn Turner, there's no need for such formality. Just call me Anthony," he replied.

Carolyn chuckled softly as she sat down, teasing. "Mr. Johnson, it's quite amusing that you still call me Ms. Carolyn Turner."

She glanced at Rose, who turned red and instinctively looked away.

In old families like the Turners, matters of romance and relationships were typically approached with strict standards.

However, in this case, Rose had made her own decision to sleep with Anthony.

Given the circumstances, Rose had had no choice, even though she knew Carolyn would not hold it against her. Yet, Carolyn's glance still made her feel shy. She hesitated for a moment before speaking. "Aunt Carolyn, I—"

"Ah," Carolyn waved her hand dismissively. "Silly girl, did you think I would fault you for this? In such a situation, you were free to make whatever choice you felt best, and it seems your decision was right."

With a reassuring smile, Carolyn added, "You don't need to explain anything. From now on, I'll consider Anthony a member of the Turner family."

Rose's expression softened with relief. "Thank you, Aunt Carolyn."

Anthony also relaxed, knowing this topic had been a source of anxiety for him.

The Turner family was old and prestigious, while he was still a relatively unknown figure.

He felt he wasn't quite worthy of Rose yet, but he knew that time would prove his worth.

He was genuinely worried that Carolyn might make things difficult for Rose because of this matter.

After a moment of small talk, Carolyn asked, "Anthony, did you defeat Mr. Foster?"

Earlier, when Anthony had allowed the werewolf army to escape, Carolyn had wanted to intervene but refrained, respecting Anthony's decision.

However, Mason was a formidable Level B werewolf metahuman, and if left alive, he would certainly return to cause trouble for the family.

Anthony had prepared for this question. He shook his head, saying, "Carolyn, I let Mason go."

Carolyn frowned but didn't respond immediately. She waited for him to continue.

"This is... a complicated matter to explain right now," Anthony said, pausing.

"But I can assure you that Mason will not trouble the Turner family again."

He chose not to explain the details of the Devil Eye or the situation with Arthur. There was simply no good way to explain it for now.

After a brief pause, Anthony shifted the topic. "But Mason told me that their attack on the Turner family was not because of Vincent or because of me.

"It was premeditated," he continued. "I believe there's more to this than we know.

"Carolyn, think about it—why would the Evil Spirit Legion go to such lengths to attack the Turner family? What could their motive possibly be?"

Carolyn hesitated, then spoke. "To be honest, I've considered this as well.

"At first, I thought it was because of Vincent, but upon reflection, the scale of the attack didn't seem like something that could have been a spur-of-the-moment decision.

"I suspected it was planned, but what could it be about? I really can't figure that out."

Carolyn didn't press further about Mason.

For her, the safety of the Turner family was all that mattered.

She trusted Anthony and didn't believe he would harm them.

The rumors that he had joined the Evil Spirit Legion?

Pure nonsense.

Anthony spent the afternoon thinking deeply. "We must get to the bottom of this," he said.

"Otherwise, the Evil Spirit Legion will definitely return, and we'll be caught unprepared."

"Sure." Carolyn's expression grew serious.

If they truly were to return, the Evil Spirit Legion would be even more ruthless than before.

At that moment, Rose spoke up. "Do you think... could it be about the drugs?"
