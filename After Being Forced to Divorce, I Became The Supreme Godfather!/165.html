Chapter 164 Ambush

Not long ago, Rose, Carolyn, Sergio, and a group of over thirty people headed toward the Virstino Mountains in a long convoy.

The Turner family's Mines were much closer to Yoland than Wolf Fang Valley, only about two to three hundred miles away.

The entire journey was along well-maintained roads, so the convoy was moving at a steady pace without slowing down.

By dusk, they'd traveled halfway.

Up ahead, the road wound around a cliff. One side had a sheer drop, while the other side had a steep rock face.

This dangerous-looking path was the only way to get to the Mines, as it had been built specifically by the Turner family for mining purposes.

Despite how it looked, it was actually very safe. Wide with four lanes and sturdy guardrails all along thirty miles.

They'd been driving this route for over ten years, so the Turner family was very familiar with it and never felt it was dangerous.

There were eight off-road vehicles in total. The first one was driven by the Turner family's personal guards, the second by Sergio, the vice-captain.

The third had Rose and Carolyn inside.

As they drove higher into the mountains, Rose started to feel uneasy. Her discomfort grew as they climbed the cliffs.

She discreetly pulled out her phone to try and reach Anthony, but there was no signal.

She frowned, puzzled. There had always been coverage here before.

Her unease didn't go unnoticed by Carolyn. "Rose, you okay? You don't look like yourself."

Rose blinked and quickly tried to brush it off. "Oh, it's nothing... Just tried to use my phone, but there's no signal."

"No signal?" Carolyn sounded doubtful.

She checked her own phone, and sure enough, there was no service. "That's weird. This place has signal coverage, and even the Mines have service..."

Before she could finish, Carolyn's eyes narrowed, scanning the road ahead.

The mountains stretched out in front of them, the green hills bathed in the glow of the setting sun, mist curling up from the valleys.

Everything looked calm on the surface, but Carolyn's gut told her something was wrong.

"Stop the car," she said suddenly, grabbing the radio.

Her instincts were never wrong. She'd led the Turner family for years, and the sense of fear she felt wasn't random.

But even after she gave the order, the convoy kept moving.

The sharp turn ahead was the only dangerous spot on the entire road.

As they neared it, the vehicles still hadn't slowed down. Carolyn's patience snapped. "Sergio, I said stop the damn car!"

There was a brief pause, then Sergio's voice came through. "Got it, Ms. Carolyn Turner."

Finally, the convoy slowed down just before the sharp bend.

"Sorry, Ms. Carolyn Turner, I got distracted for a moment."

"What's going on? Why are we stopping?"

Sergio had been with the Turner family for over ten years, and he was deeply loyal to them, especially to Carolyn, who valued him highly.

Because of that, he respected her, but their relationship felt more like equals than a typical boss-subordinate dynamic.

"I don't know..."

"Something just doesn't feel right. Send someone ahead to check."

Carolyn didn't mind the lapse.

They had driven this road countless times, so it wasn't surprising that Sergio was a little too relaxed.

"Got it, Ms. Carolyn Turner."

Sergio jumped into action and sent his vehicle ahead to investigate.

Although Carolyn had instructed him to send someone ahead to check, Sergio seemed to sense the need for extra caution and took the lead himself.

When she saw him take charge, Carolyn couldn't help but feel a sense of relief. She had complete trust in him.

But just as the vehicles rounded the bend, an earsplitting explosion rocked the air, the sound like a bomb going off.

Rose and Carolyn both jerked in their seats, eyes wide as they looked ahead. Dust and smoke filled the road.

Sergio's car had already turned the corner, and they couldn't see what was happening.

Carolyn grabbed the radio, her voice sharp and panicked. "Sergio, what's going on?!"

"Everyone, get out of the cars, now!"

"Protect Ms. Carolyn Turner!" Sergio's voice crackled urgently.

Before he could say anything more, the radio buzzed with static.

"Get out!

"Everyone, get out of the cars!"

Carolyn didn't hesitate and immediately ordered her people to evacuate.

Just as she was about to leap out, a flash of light came from the mountain cliff.

A grenade hurtled down toward them.

"Rose!" Carolyn shouted in alarm, grabbing Rose and yanking her closer. Without thinking, she kicked the door open and jumped out.

They landed hard on the ground just as the grenade hit the vehicle, engulfing it in flames.

The explosion's shockwave knocked Carolyn and Rose off their feet.

Inside the vehicle, there were four people. The others hadn't gotten out in time.

The entire car was consumed by fire, and a hot gust of air slammed into them.

Carolyn pulled Rose backward, eyes scanning the cliffside, fury and disbelief flooding her chest. "Who the hell dares to ambush us?!"
