Chapter 151 Biding His Time

Anne wasn't about to let someone as powerful as Arthur slip through her fingers.

But Arthur had no intention of getting involved.

He wanted to decline, but then a thought flashed across his mind. Changing tack, he said with a polite smile, "Maybe another time. I've got something to do right now."

Before Anne could press further, he turned and walked off in a hurry.

"Mr. Johnson! Bad news!"

"That person... she contacted Anne, and she said..."

The moment Arthur left the Smith Manor, he didn't waste a second. He immediately called Anthony to report what had happened, sparing no detail.

The scent of the letter was unmistakable.

It matched the person who had orchestrated the earlier attack on the Smith family, the very informant Anthony had been hunting for all this time.

"Well."

"That's quick," Anthony muttered, sounding slightly surprised.

He paused, then added thoughtfully, "This confirms Evil Spirit Legion is up to something big. They wouldn't be this desperate otherwise."

"Any idea what kind of strength we're dealing with?"

"No clue," Arthur admitted, shaking his head.

Then, after a moment's thought, he added, "But if I had to guess, it's no joke."

"At the very least, we're talking about a C-level Metahuman. Maybe even Level D."

"Alright."

Anthony frowned, tapping his fingers lightly on the desk. "Here's the plan. Find an excuse to join Anne on her trip to the Virstino Mountains."

"Scope things out, but don't make any moves. If that person really is there, we need to confirm their identity first."

After a pause, he added, "Actually, forget that. I'll come along in secret in three days. Better safe than sorry."

As cautious as Anthony was, he wasn't entirely convinced the person in question was a Level D Metahuman.

But when it came to situations like this, it was better to be overprepared than blindsided.

"Got it."

"By the way, Anne has been trying to recruit me into the Evil Spirit Legion."

"She brought it up again after receiving the letter. I've agreed to play along so she doesn't get suspicious."

"Smart," Anthony said with a nod. "Just gather intel for now. No heroics."

"I want to see what the Legion is really after. Sometimes you have to cast a wide net to catch the big fish."

"Yes, understood."

Anthony wasn't too surprised Evil Spirit Legion had pulled the Smith family into their levels.

What puzzled him was why they'd send Anne to the Virstino Mountains.

According to Seraph's earlier investigations, Evil Spirit Legion had already withdrawn from that area.

This only meant they were plotting something major, something worth the risk.

That was why Anthony decided to bide his time. If they acted too soon, they'd risk scaring off their target.

But ignoring the situation wasn't an option either. They needed to confirm the enemy's strength to prepare properly.

From what Arthur had gathered, the opponent was likely a C-level Metahuman, formidable enough to be taken seriously.

The gap between levels was no small thing.

For B-level Metahumans like Anthony and Arthur, their powers were limited to physical enhancements, boosting their strength and defenses.

As for C-level Metahumans, their abilities could manifest beyond their bodies. Take Carolyn, for example. As a C-level, she could summon massive swords out of thin air or stir up sandstorms with a flick of her wrist.

That kind of power wasn't something they could just brute-force through.

To prepare, Anthony got his hands on a batch of arcane tonic from Rose.

The Turner family was practically swimming in resources, and Rose wasn't stingy.

After handing over the arcane tonics, she wrapped her arms around him and teased, "Don't push yourself too hard, alright?"

"Aunt Carolyn is planning to visit the Mines in seven days. I need you in one piece."

"Don't worry, I'll be fine," Anthony replied.

He had to check out the Mines himself.

There was too much at stake with the Leyline and the Chosen Path.

If the third phase of the mission could be successfully completed, even without Devil Eye achieving another breakthrough, his power would be bound to skyrocket.

Plus, there was the Dragon Ring's reward to consider. With all of that, Anthony was confident he'd stand a chance against a C-level Metahuman.

But Rose didn't seem thrilled about his urgency. With a faint pout, she leaned in closer, her voice turning soft and alluring. "Can't you take a break?"

"You've been working nonstop, and I've missed you..."

As she spoke, she slid closer, wrapping herself around him, her breath warm against his skin.

Anthony chuckled, scooping her up effortlessly. Without another word, he carried her straight into the bedroom.
