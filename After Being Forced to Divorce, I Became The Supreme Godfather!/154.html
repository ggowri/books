Chapter 153 Virstino Mountains

In just three days, Anne, using the guise of the Evil Spirit Legion, went all out to recruit the families of Yoland.

Within such a short time, she managed to bring almost ninety percent of the families in Yoland under her control, making a huge splash.

As a result, the Smith family's status skyrocketed, and now, they were the most respected family in the entire city.

On the other hand, the Turner family, once an old powerhouse, had completely fallen off the radar.

But the Turner family didn't seem to care. For the past few days, there had been no word from the Mines.

Ever since Vincent went there, it was like he disappeared into thin air.

Carolyn sent someone to check, but there was still no update.

The more she thought about it, the more uneasy she felt, like something just didn't sit right.

She had originally planned to go herself, but with the Smith family stirring up trouble, she had her hands full with family matters and couldn't make the trip.

That afternoon, Anthony wrapped up his cultivation.

"Wait, no news at all?"

"The people we sent, they just... vanished?"

Anthony frowned when Rose filled him in. Something definitely felt off.

"But it's just Vincent. Does he have the guts to pull this off?"

"Trying to take control of the Turner family's Mines all by himself? With just a handful of guards?"

"Hold on a sec..."

"Wasn't it Vincent who secretly brought in those werewolves to attack me and Rose?"

"And didn't Arthur confirm that Vincent has ties with the Evil Spirit Legion?"

"The Turner family's mines are right in the Virstino Mountains... Could this be connected?"

As Anthony was piecing things together, his phone rang. It was Arthur, telling him that Anne and the heads of the other families had set off.

"Rose, the situation with the Mines doesn't feel right."

"I need to step out for a bit. When Carolyn gets back, tell her not to do anything stupid."

"No matter what happens, wait for me to come back. Don't make any moves without me, okay?"

Anthony gave a few final instructions before hurrying out.

The Mines could wait for now.

Even if Vincent was working with the Evil Spirit Legion, it wouldn't cause any big waves just yet.

But Arthur's informant was key.

If Anthony could catch this informant, he'd finally understand what the Evil Spirit Legion was up to.

Compared to that, Vincent's little schemes didn't matter much.

As for Anne, her group was a force to be reckoned with, at least three hundred people, made up of core family members and their security staff.

Out of all the security personnel, nearly seventy percent were members of Draconic Shield, around a hundred people.

Most of the families in Yoland had signed long-term contracts with Draconic Shield, though the number of people varied.

A convoy of over a hundred off-road vehicles sped through the winding mountain roads, their headlights bright and blinding.

From a distance, the convoy looked like a fiery dragon winding its way into the Virstino Mountains.

In the distant forests, Anthony moved like a sleek predator, keeping pace with the convoy as they traveled through the rugged terrain.

At the front of the convoy, a Draconic Shield's armored vehicle led the way, well-equipped and ready for anything.

The mountain path was tough, so the convoy wasn't moving very fast. It seemed like they had started a bit later than planned.

By the time they entered the Virstino Mountains, the sun had nearly set, and as they went further, the darkness took over, soon enveloping the entire landscape.

When they reached the mountain range, the convoy came to a halt. The path ahead was too treacherous to continue by vehicle, so everyone got out and started walking.

Inside the vehicles, there had been lighthearted chatter, full of excitement and anticipation for what was to come.

But as soon as they stepped out into the pitch-black wilderness, the mood shifted. The surroundings were eerie and desolate, with sharp rocks scattered all around, and the cold wind cut through the air.

These were the pampered children of aristocratic families, used to leisurely strolls through parks and scenic spots, not trekking through an uncharted mountain range at night.

Even Anne and Alvin were on edge, instinctively pulling closer together.

As the group grew increasingly uneasy, Arthur gave a commanding shout, and bright white lights suddenly illuminated the pitch-black sky.

"Relax, everyone."

"Draconic Shield's got everything covered. With these lights, it's practically daylight. You're safe with us."

He raised his voice again, encouraging them.

Since the showdown with the Smith family, Arthur's reputation had soared, and his strength was now widely respected.

His words had a calming effect on everyone, and they visibly relaxed.

With the path lit up, Arthur took the lead. "Let's go!"

Seeing Arthur so confident gave Anne the boost she needed. She waved her hand, signaling everyone to hurry. "We're already late. Don't keep Archlord Iscairn waiting. Move!"

"If anyone's scared, they can go back. I'm not about to let anyone embarrass us in front of Archlord Iscairn!"

Her words had the desired effect. This was their big chance, and no one was about to back out now.

The group quickly set off down the steep mountain path, heading deeper into the Virstino Mountains.

Anthony stayed close, moving silently on the outskirts, keeping an eye on the group.

But then, Anthony suddenly slowed his pace, instinctively turning to look behind him.

The night was pitch-black, and the surrounding forest seemed endless, silent, and abandoned.

"Devil Eye, can you scan the area?" Anthony asked, his instincts tingling with suspicion.

"I'm on it. Scanning now..."

"Completed. There's evidence of human activity 600 feet ahead."

Anthony's mind started racing. "This is still the outskirts of the Virstino Mountains. Could it be an outpost of the Evil Spirit Legion?"

"No... this is too far out for that."

"But if they've been watching me, then who are these people?"

"Did Anne hire someone? Or is this the Evil Spirit Legion?"

With these thoughts in mind, Anthony picked up the pace, but his path started to veer off, separating from the main group.
