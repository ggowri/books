Chapter 159 Give Up

A towering figure, built like a mountain, his strength and defense were no surprise.

But what shocked Anthony was how fast Necros was. He was just as quick as before, maybe even faster.

Anthony had thought that after the transformation, Necros would lose the ability to use his usual techniques.

But now, he was still pulling them off, and with more power than before.

Necros' strength made Anthony feel small, a sense of helplessness creeping in.

It wasn't that he was weak. Necros was just a fucking beast. His skills were on another level.

For a B-level Warrior, wielding the power at C level was beyond rank fighting.

Anthony could fight above his weight too, but only with his Devil Eye.

It was his trump card, his deadliest weapon.

"The way he's moving, there's no obvious weak spot."

"But when he used Blood Shadow Saber earlier, his internal energy dipped."

"Seems like maintaining his form and using those techniques costs him his essence."

The Devil Eye gave a quick, precise analysis.

Anthony smirked. No wonder Necros hadn't used the Blood Shadow Saber more. It was just too costly.

Essence was everything for the Warriors and Metahumans.

They could burn it for short bursts of strength, but it took forever to recover.

No one wasted it unless they had no choice.

"So, keeping up his defense costs him essence too?" Anthony asked.

"Yep."

Anthony straightened up, cracking his neck and brushing off the dust.

He grinned. This fight was about to get interesting.

Necros was charging at him like a raging bull, smashing through trees and rocks in his path. He was fast, and his power was terrifying.

But Anthony was ready. The second Necros got within range, Anthony whipped out the Inferno Gatling gun from his Dragon Ring.

A storm of bullets tore through the air, lighting up the dark forest. The sound was deafening.

"What the hell?"

Necros froze for a second, eyes wide in disbelief.

Ordinary guns wouldn't faze him, but the Inferno Gatling was a whole different monster.

The sheer volume of bullets was enough to make even Necros hesitate. He couldn't take that head-on.

He twisted his body, veering sideways to dodge the firestorm.

But Anthony was already one step ahead. He adjusted the gun's angle, cutting off Necros' escape.

Every bullet that hit shattered the ground, splintered trees, and sent rocks flying.

Necros kept running, but the bullets followed him like a relentless, fiery tide. He was fast, but not fast enough to outrun the Inferno Gatling.

With each burst, blood exploded from his body, but even that couldn't slow him down.

The Gatling's firepower was powerful, but it didn't break through his defenses. It tore his skin and spilled blood, but he kept pushing forward.

His defenses were insane.

Most B-level Warriors would have been shredded to pieces by now, but Necros just kept moving, his speed unaffected.

The constant barrage was burning through Anthony's funds at an alarming rate, each shot adding up fast.

But knowing Necros was the one really paying the price, draining his essence with every bullet, gave Anthony a sense of relief.

Necros snarled, finally losing his cool. "Where the hell did you get that gun?!"

Anthony didn't respond. Every time Necros slowed down, he was hit with another barrage of bullets.

They weren't lethal, but they hurt. And with the constant barrage, it wouldn't take long before Necros' defenses cracked.

Maintaining that defense was draining his essence. Even if Necros didn't fall right away, Anthony could outlast him.

Anthony laughed, the Inferno Gatling still roaring in his hands.

The world around him was being reduced to rubble. Trees were toppled. Rocks turned to dust, and the land itself seemed to tremble beneath the storm of bullets.

Necros was trapped. There was nowhere to run, nowhere to hide.

"Give up," Anthony shouted, aiming the gun directly at him. "Surrender, and I'll let you live."

Necros was strong, and his technique was impressive, which really intrigued Anthony.
