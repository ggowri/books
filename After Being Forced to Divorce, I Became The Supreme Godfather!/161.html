Chapter 160 Necros Escaped

Anthony could tell that Necros had a significant position in the Evil Spirit Legion, probably even higher than Arthur.

That meant Necros knew a lot more about the Legion, and that was exactly the information Anthony was after.

"Damn you!" Necros growled, spinning around with an expression full of fury and disdain. "A low-life like you thinks you can make me surrender? Keep dreaming!"

"Go to the hell!" With a savage roar, he wrenched a tree, thick as a man's waist, from the ground and hurled it at Anthony.

The tree flew through the dark sky, cutting a clean arc as it rocketed toward Anthony with incredible speed and power.

But with the Inferno Gatling in his hands, this was child's play.

Anthony unloaded a barrage of bullets, and the tree exploded into splinters, falling apart in midair like shattered glass.

However, just as the tree disintegrated, Anthony's instincts screamed. He couldn't see Necros anymore.

Before he could react, he heard the unmistakable sound of something large cutting through the air, a sharp, whistling whoosh from the side.

Without thinking, Anthony turned, and a massive fist flew straight at him.

There was no way to dodge it in time, not even with the Inferno Gatling in his hands. The punch was too fast.

With no choice, Anthony dove out of the way.

The fist missed by inches, the rush of wind from it almost knocking him off his feet.

His heart raced. Recovering quickly, Anthony lifted the Inferno Gatling again, unleashing another rapid-fire barrage.

"Fuck you!" Necros' roar was full of rage and frustration.

He stopped fighting, realizing he'd lost his edge. Without hesitation, he turned and sprinted toward the forest.

The punch he'd thrown had been a last-ditch effort, fueled by all his remaining strength.

Missing it meant he had no more tricks left.

If he didn't run now, Anthony would grind him down until there was nothing left.

As Necros started to flee, Anthony couldn't help but laugh. Gripping the Inferno Gatling, he chased after him.

He could feel Necros' power fading fast. His transformation wasn't going to last much longer.

Anthony wouldn't let him slip away.

As Anthony pursued Necros, he noticed his opponent gradually returning to his normal size, his speed slowing.

More importantly, his steps grew unsteady, as though his escape was no longer as effortless as before.

Anthony swiftly put away his Inferno Gatling and took off after him, moving like a shadow, closing the gap with incredible speed.

But just as he was about to catch up, Necros suddenly morphed into a shadow and vanished in the blink of an eye.

Anthony, almost upon him, was left frozen in surprise.

Necros was no ordinary foe. His abilities were unpredictable and powerful, always keeping Anthony on edge.

He had to admit, this was someone who truly wasn't to be underestimated.

What caught his attention, though, was the mention of Archlord Iscairn, which only deepened his curiosity.

The werewolf assassin, XN-601, had said he came from Southwest Camp in the Virstino Mountains.

Anthony remembered that even Arthur was tied to that base.

So, it seemed like the attack on the Turner family and everything that followed had been orchestrated from there.

That made Anthony wonder if Archlord Iscairn could be the leader of that Southwest Camp.

If so, he was getting closer than ever to uncovering the Evil Spirit Legion's plot.

Capturing Necros or tracking down the person behind him would be the key to exposing everything.

But now Necros had gotten away.

Still, the fact that someone of his caliber had been dispatched confirmed just how serious the Evil Spirit Legion's intentions were.

They wouldn't send someone that powerful unless the stakes were incredibly high.

Anthony couldn't help but wonder what exactly drew the Evil Spirit Legion to a small town like Yoland.

The Turner family's arcane tonics didn't seem like nearly enough to attract their attention.

His heart skipped a beat at the thought of the Leyline.

The Leyline was linked to the third task of the Chosen Path.

But so far, there was no concrete evidence that the Turner family's Mines had any.

If the Evil Spirit Legion was after the Leyline, everything he suspected could be true.

Frustrated and confused, Anthony took a deep breath and swallowed the blood that had gathered in his mouth from the intense battle.

The fight had taken a heavy toll on him. The effort it took to maintain his Dragon Avatar and wield the power of his Devil Eye had completely drained him.

But there was no time to waste. After catching his breath, he dialed Arthur's number only to find it didn't go through.

Anthony had already done his homework on Wolf Fang Valley's location, so without hesitation, he set off toward it.

Wolf Fang Valley was just one of many canyons in the Virstino Mountains, a dark and eerie place with towering trees and overgrown vines. The flowers were as tall as a man, and thick vines snaked up the cliffs.

In the distance, a cluster of lights quickly approached, growing brighter as they drew nearer to the valley.

On a massive stone in the heart of the valley, a Black Cloak stood with his hands behind his back, calmly watching the approaching lights.
