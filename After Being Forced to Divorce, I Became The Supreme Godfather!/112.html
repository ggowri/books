Chapter 111 True Intentions

Anthony's power had surged, and the Devil Eye had evolved.

Alongside this transformation, his mindset shifted as well.

He was no longer the destitute orphan wandering the streets or the live-in man under someone else's roof.

Now, he was a bona fide metahuman, a powerful Warrior, and a man destined to rise as the next Godfather!

If he couldn't even handle someone like Arthur, how could he dream of claiming that title?

Arthur's heart skipped a beat. He hesitated but finally said, "If... if you truly have a way to help me avenge my wife and daughter, then I, Arthur Hastings, will pledge my loyalty to you and follow your every command!

"But I must ask—what proof do you have?"

Arthur's voice carried anger—not toward Anthony but toward the Evil Spirit Legion and himself.

Beneath the anger simmered guilt.

He yearned for revenge, yet he knew deep down that such a goal was nothing more than a fool's dream.

"Good," Anthony said with a satisfied nod. "But first, I need you to tell me one thing.

"Why has the Evil Spirit Legion mobilized such a massive force this time? Their goal to destroy the Turner family cannot be as simple as targeting me or Vincent.

"Because the Evil Spirit Legion wouldn't mobilize such a massive force just because I killed a single werewolf.

"Nor would they recklessly launch an all-out assault on a centuries-old powerhouse simply over someone like Vincent."

Anthony had pondered this question endlessly, yet he couldn't piece it together.

"Heh..." Arthur let out a wry chuckle. "You're certainly different—sharp and wise beyond your years.

"Yes, this siege on the Turner family is not about you or Vincent.

"Vincent was merely the spark..."

Anthony waved his hand impatiently. "Enough with the nonsense. Get to the point. Speak."

"I don't know..." Arthur shrugged helplessly, his tone apologetic.

"Are you courting death?" Anthony's face darkened.

"No."

Arthur remained calm and shook his head. "I genuinely don't know. Before this, we had been stationed deep within the Virstino Mountains, lying low.

"Our orders come from our superiors. We are not allowed to contact them proactively, nor can we leave our designated positions without permission.

"We received this mission three days ago: infiltrate the assassins sent to kill you and seize Rose alive."

"Capture her alive?"

Anthony frowned, recalling that the werewolf assassins had indeed tried to capture Rose rather than kill her outright.

But why? Why capture Rose?

"Yes," Arthur confirmed. "The original mission was to take her alive.

"But when that mission failed, we were given new orders—to eliminate the Turner family.

"As for the exact reason, I truly don't know."

Anthony nodded thoughtfully.

His suspicions were confirmed.

The Evil Spirit Legion had clearly been planning this for some time. The siege on the Turner family wasn't a spur-of-the-moment decision; it was a meticulously crafted scheme.

But what could their true motive be?

Was it all about Rose?

No.

At first, their goal was to capture Rose alive. When that failed, the directive shifted to annihilating the Turner family entirely—Rose included.

In essence, they began with a plan to capture one person, only to pivot to wiping out the entire family.

But why?

Given Arthur's current state, he didn't seem to be lying. In fact, there was no reason for him to lie. It appeared he truly didn't know.

After a brief moment of thought, Anthony Johnson pressed further. "This superior you mentioned—can you find a way to contact them?"

Arthur shook his head helplessly, repeating. "We cannot contact our superiors proactively. That is a strict taboo in the Evil Spirit Legion."

Anthony frowned. "Then surely you've seen this person? You must at least know what they look like."

"I don't."

Arthur shook his head again. "They wore the same black robe as I do and always hid behind a demonic mask.

"Their voice was raspy, and they had a fiery temper. However..." He hesitated, frowning deeply as if trying to recall something.

Anthony's patience was wearing thin. "However, what?"

"I think it might be a woman..." Arthur muttered. "Even though the voice was low and rough, and the temper volatile, there was a distinct scent about them. Something unique. Strange.

"It's hard to describe, but once you smelled it, it stirred something inside you. A sort of... inexplicable restlessness."

As he spoke, Arthur seemed lost in thought, his face betraying a trace of lingering nostalgia, as if he were savoring the memory.

A woman?

Anthony wasn't particularly surprised by the revelation, but Arthur's expression made him frown.

"What's with you?" Anthony asked, his tone laced with irritation. "Even if it's a woman, you've never even seen her face. Is there really any reason for this dreamy look?"

"Huh?" Arthur blinked, startled out of his reverie.

He appeared momentarily disoriented, as though unaware he had zoned out.

Anthony didn't dwell on it. He didn't have the patience for such trivialities. Instead, he cut straight to the point. "If you want revenge, it won't be easy.

"First, you'll need to accomplish something for me. Do it well, and you might have your chance at vengeance.

"If you fail, not only will you never avenge your wife and daughter, but I'll personally end your life."
