Chapter 109 The Soul Contract

The enhanced Inferno Gatling was a force to be reckoned with, but it came at a hefty price.

At thirty thousand gold coins for the weapon and a staggering hundred gold coins per bullet, Anthony would have thought twice about purchasing such a powerful tool if the Turner family wasn't in dire straits.

He had just unleashed a barrage that cost him over ten thousand gold coins, but it was worth it to see the Devil Eye evolve, enhancing his overall strength.

Now, Anthony faced Mason without fear.

His decision to have a one-on-one hit with Mason was to force the truth about the Evil Spirit Legion's attack on the Turner family out of him.

Otherwise, no matter the cost, Anthony would have mowed down the werewolf army into oblivion.

Mason, trembling and coughing up blood, looked utterly terrified.

Seeing this, Anthony retracted his dragon power.

Mason caught his breath, but he remained on the ground, quivering in fear, not daring to make a single move.

The dragon power was indeed formidable.

For beast metahumans, it carried an innate aura of intimidation.

Anthony smiled down at Mason. "Mr. Foster, are you ready to answer my questions?"

"No... No..."

Mason looked up, his face a picture of terror, yet he stubbornly shook his head.

"Is that so? It seems you've lived too long."

Anthony's expression darkened as he summoned the dragon power once more.

"No! Please don't..."

Mason wailed, prostrating himself at Anthony's feet, begging for mercy.

Anthony had a thought, "He doesn't want to speak. He dares not. This fear seems to come from the depths of his soul... A soul contract?"

Anthony finally realized something.

To exert better control, the Evil Spirit Legion made every member sign a soul contract.

Once bound by the contract, they appeared normal but were utterly loyal to the Evil Spirit Legion, willing to give everything, even their lives, for the cause.

Understanding this, Anthony wasn't panicked. Instead, he laughed, "Look me in the eyes."

Mason swallowed hard, his face trembling as he looked up, revealing a countenance etched with terror.

A vicious scar marred his face, giving him a menacing appearance at first glance.

However, to Anthony's surprise, the man wasn't as old as he had expected.

He appeared to be in his thirties, exuding a strong, masculine aura.

Anthony was surprised.

He had anticipated Mason to be a middle-aged man with a repulsive face.

But he seemed like an ordinary person, not even resembling a villain.

Nevertheless, Anthony didn't dwell on it. He asked again, "What's your name?"

"Arthur Hastings..."

The intimidation of the bloodline left Mason no choice but to comply.

Even though his soul was ensnared, as long as Anthony's questions didn't pertain to the Evil Spirit Legion, he didn't seem to resist answering.

"How old are you?"

"Thirty-six."

"Why did you join the Evil Spirit Legion?"

This time, Mason, or rather Arthur, shuddered again. He stammered, "I... I don't know. I truly don't know..."

Anthony didn't waste any more time. He said again, "Look me in the eyes."

Without hesitation, Arthur lifted his gaze to meet Anthony's.

Anthony's eyes suddenly shimmered with a blue glow.

The Devil Eye had the power to peer into one's soul, cleanse the heart, and command the spirit.

The moment their eyes locked, Arthur's pupils contracted, and he stared blankly at Anthony.

His eyes showed a struggle, and so did his body. He trembled anew.

At that moment, Anthony's brow furrowed, his expression growing serious.

In a flash, his anger dissipated, replaced by surprise and undeniable pity.

As Anthony probed deeper, Arthur struggled even more violently.

It wasn't just his body. His soul and consciousness were at war.

He looked in agony, his brow furrowed, his face contorted.

Tears streamed down Arthur's face. He cried out to Anthony, "Kill... kill me. Please kill me..."

Anthony ignored him. With a flicker in his eyes, a blue ray shot into Arthur's pupils.

In an instant, Arthur stopped struggling.

At the same time, the anger in his eyes faded.

If you looked closely, Arthur's eyes seemed clearer.

It was because Anthony had cleansed his soul with the power of the Devil Eye.

In short, Anthony had broken his soul contract.

"Ah! Hannah! Mia!

"I deserve to die! I truly deserve to die!

"Hannah, Mia, wait for me! I'll be with you soon..."

Arthur shouted to the sky, sobbing uncontrollably.

Then, he picked up a sharp blade from the ground and slashed it across his own neck.
