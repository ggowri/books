Chapter 28 The Truth Unveiled

"Last call! Is there any other bidder? If not, these two will find their new owner!"

Jane, the auctioneer, called out thrice, her voice ringing clear across the room. Satisfied that no further bids were coming, she brought the gavel down with a sharp crack.

A ripple of applause swept through the venue.

The auction spotlight shone on two Eldergleam Fungus specimens, where one was authentic and the other a clever imitation. Anthony secured the counterfeit, labeled A, for a close 5.01 million dollars, whereas B, which captured the crowd's favor, was won by Alvin at an astonishing bid of 50 million dollars.

The staff members came forward, offering both Eldergleam Fungus specimens to Anthony. He grasped the one marked A, his face breaking into a self-satisfied smirk. "Thank you for your generous contribution, Ms. Turner. Please accept this as my gift to you."

Rose forced a smile, her eyes betraying a flicker of doubt. "Anthony, are you certain this one is real?"

Anthony nodded confidently. "Absolutely."

But Helen, her patience worn thin, burst out, "Are you blind? Even a child could see that this thing is a fake!

"Anthony, Ms. Turner just gave you 30 million dollars as a reward, and you waste it on this cheap imitation? That's disgraceful!

"And thanks to you, Ms. Turner lost her shot at the genuine article! What penalty do you think you've earned for that?"

Rose, despite her suspicions, couldn't bear to embarrass Anthony. She smiled weakly, silencing Helen. "It's alright, Helen. I like this one. Thank you, Anthony."

"My pleasure," Anthony replied smoothly. "This thousand-year-old treasure will surely benefit your health."

Rose, though convinced it was a sham, smiled and nodded, unable to bring herself to expose him.

Beside her, Helen seethed, eyes flashing daggers at Anthony. Her lips were pursed in a fierce pout.

From the other side of the room, Alvin let out a laugh. "Anthony, if the one you snagged for five million dollars is a thousand years old, then just how old do you reckon the one I got for fifty million is?

"There are two here, and only one's the real deal. You can't seriously believe yours is the authentic one, right?"

Anthony, unfazed, replied coolly, "Of course I do. Mine is the true thousand-year-old Eldergleam Fungus. Yours is a mix of hundred to three-hundred-year-old specimens. They may have some medicinal value, but they pale in comparison to mine. And as for price, it's nowhere near fifty million dollars."

Alvin scoffed. "Bullshit! Mine is the real deal!

"If you don't believe me, why not ask Ms. Smith, the owner of it, to reveal the truth?"

Anthony had walked right into the trap. The room buzzed with anticipation, everyone eager to see him proven wrong.

"Ms. Smith, please!" the crowd clamored.

"So, which one's the real deal?"

Anne rose, her eyes lingering on Anthony for a moment. For a fleeting second, she pitied him.

She whispered, "Am I being too harsh?"

But her heart quickly hardened. Anthony had weaseled his way in here with nothing but clever tricks. He was never meant to belong in her world.

She turned to Rose with earnest eyes and said, "Ms. Turner, my hope was for you to acquire the authentic thousand-year-old Eldergleam Fungus. That's why I reached out to Anthony last night."

"Really?" Rose's eyes locked onto Anthony with a mix of curiosity and suspicion. "Did Ms. Smith perhaps suggest to you that this one's the real Eldergleam Fungus?"

The night before, Anne had bared her soul, stripping off her clothes and kneeling before Anthony, urging him to believe that A was the genuine article. In this, Rose's suspicions had been correct.

Anthony, on the verge of nodding in confirmation, was cut off by Anne's cold, cutting words, "Wrong!" Anne sneered.

"Ms. Turner, I told Anthony repeatedly last night that A was a fake. I begged him to help you secure B. But instead, this despicable man chose to sabotage your chances, all to save a few miserable coins!

"He had the audacity to gift you a counterfeit Eldergleam Fungus!

"This counterfeit is toxic. Ingesting it would be detrimental to your health! Ms. Turner, it seems he is intentionally trying to poison you!

"I suggest that you investigate his true identity. Discover who sent him!"

Upon hearing this, Rose turned to Anthony with a look of sheer disbelief as her face drained of color.

Raised in a powerful family, she had witnessed the darkest corners of human greed and betrayal, where people killed their own kin for profit.

Anne's words were like a match to dry tinder, igniting fears that had long slumbered in the recesses of her mind.

Why did Anthony seem so in tune with her? He must have conducted an exhaustive investigation into her background beforehand!

His intention was clearly to get close to her, only to seize the right moment to harm her.

Her voice trembled as she confronted him. "Anthony, were you sent to harm me?"

Anthony, caught off guard by the turn of events, stammered awkwardly, "I..."

"Tell the truth!" Helen interjected sharply, her eyes flashing dangerously. "Or you'll face death!"

Anthony tried to defend himself. "I... I don't know what it will take for you to believe me. But this is the real one."

Alvin laughed, arguing, "Still trying to lie! Ms. Smith, the true owner of the Eldergleam Fungus, has already spoken. Are we to believe she doesn't know her own merchandise?

"Ms. Smith, I managed to secure the real one. Would you join me? Let's make the presentation of this ancient, thousand-year-old treasure to Ms. Turner.

"Ms. Turner, I wish to offer it to you as a gift, jointly from Ms. Smith and myself.

"As for Anthony, he is a disgraced loser, cast out by Ms. Smith. He embodies laziness and greed. Don't let his charm fool you. Keeping your distance would indeed be the wisest course of action."

Tears welled in Rose's eyes, her heart shattering into a thousand pieces.

She was starting to feel a creeping conviction that Anthony was being orchestrated by someone, perhaps with the intent to get close to her only to cause her harm.

But a part of her still clung to hope, refusing to let go.

Were the embraces and joyful moments they shared just yesterday nothing but a facade?

Never before had she felt such cheer and happiness, and in her heart, she had, quite on her own, crowned Anthony as her confidant.

Could it possibly be that all of this was just an illusion, a beautifully crafted lie?
