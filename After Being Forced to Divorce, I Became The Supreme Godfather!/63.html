Chapter 62 Welcoming Ms. Ronald

Calvin played his final card, demanding a face-to-face confrontation with the werewolf.

He knew this move would put him in an unassailable position. After all, there was no chance the werewolf would show up.

First, he believed that Anthony would never be able to capture the werewolf.

Second, even if the werewolf were captured, according to the rules of the Evil Spirit Legion and the werewolf's nature, they would never surrender.

Even if they failed and were caught, the werewolf would rather die than betray the organization.

This loyalty was precisely why the Evil Spirit Legion was so trusted by its clients.

Without the werewolf's testimony, no matter how much Anthony accused him or how suspicious Carolyn remained, Calvin was confident he'd walk away unscathed.

Hearing this, Carolyn frowned deeply, her gaze filled with unease as she looked at Anthony.

She was 80% certain Calvin was guilty, but without concrete evidence, she couldn't make a compelling case before so many onlookers.

Vincent, glaring menacingly at Anthony, growled, "If you're so sure, bring the werewolf here for a confrontation! If the werewolf identifies Calvin, I won't shield him. But if you fail, don't blame me for what happens next.

"I'll execute you on the spot for slander!"

Anthony checked the time, then smiled faintly. "Since you won't believe it until you see it, I'll settle this once and for all."

He pulled out his phone, made a call, and then leaned back with a relieved expression. "In ten minutes, the werewolf will appear.

"Meanwhile, I could use a drink. Rose, would you care to join me?"

Despite his calm demeanor, his smile betrayed exhaustion.

Anthony had been under immense pressure, gambling from the very beginning.

He bet that the mastermind was someone within the Turner family and, more critically, that Jane wouldn't fail in capturing the werewolf.

The phone call confirmed Jane's success, allowing Anthony to finally exhale.

The earlier battle with Dariel and the oppressive aura from Carolyn had drained him completely. Until the situation was resolved, he had forced himself to stay upright.

Now, with the tension easing, a wave of fatigue washed over him.

His legs felt heavy, and all he wanted was to collapse into a soft chair, or better yet, a bed, and drift into a deep sleep.

Seeing his sudden pallor, Rose was startled but quickly understood his intention from the look in his eyes.

"Of course," she said warmly. "You're my savior and a guest of honor. I'd be delighted to toast you."

Taking his hand, she led him toward the head table.

"Stop right there!"

"Anthony, what game are you playing? Explain yourself now, or I'll kill you where you stand!"

Vincent blocked their path, seething with fury.

Anthony chuckled. "Mr. Vincent Turner, I said the werewolf would arrive in ten minutes. Can't you even wait that long?"

"You're stalling for time!" Vincent spat. "And if you fail to produce the werewolf in ten minutes?"

"Then I'll deserve to die," Anthony replied coolly. "Consider this a parting drink for my journey to the afterlife. Does that satisfy you?"

Vincent gritted his teeth. "Fine! You said it yourself! Everyone here is a witness. If you can't deliver the werewolf in ten minutes, you die!"

Alvin stepped forward, shouting, "We all heard it! Mr. Vincent Turner, don't worry. We'll testify to this!"

Anne chimed in eagerly, "Exactly! Anthony, ten minutes from now, prepare to meet your end!"

The rest of the crowd also began shouting in unison.

In their view, not to mention ten minutes, even a day, or a year, Anthony would never be able to identify the werewolf killer, let alone directly accuse Calvin.

As for Anthony, he no longer cared. He sat down beside Rose, holding her hand.

Not only did Rose not let go of his hand, but she tightened her grip. At the same time, a pure energy flowed from her palm into Anthony's body.

In that instant, Anthony felt a jolt of mental clarity, as if someone suffering from altitude sickness had suddenly entered a forest oxygen bar or like a desert traveler who had just arrived at a freshwater lake. His dry, weary body and soul seemed to be bathed in a gentle spring breeze and soft rain.

Rose was using her healing superpower to replenish his strength.

The comfort was intoxicating, like a fine wine warming his spirit.

As time passed, Anthony's energy was rapidly recovering. Meanwhile, as the countdown approached ten minutes, Calvin and Vincent grew increasingly excited.

Vincent silently gathered power, ready to strike the moment ten minutes were up.

He wouldn't give Anthony a chance to defend himself.

"Time's up! Mr. Vincent Turner, act now!" Calvin shouted excitedly.

With a roar, Vincent lunged toward Anthony.

"Dragon Guild's Yoland branch director, Jane, has arrived with a grand gift to congratulate Ms. Turner!"

The announcement reverberated through Turner Manor, sending a shockwave through everyone's hearts.

Jane herself was here!

Though her status in Yoland didn't surpass Carolyn's, she represented the Dragon Guild, which was a core enterprise under the Godfather's domain.

Jane was someone from the Godfather's circle, and that alone was enough to overshadow Yoland, making everyone give her some respect.

Carolyn's eyebrows rose in surprise but quickly smoothed as she called out warmly, "Welcome, Ms. Ronald!"
