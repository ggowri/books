The bullet slammed into the Maybach's window with a sharp crack.

This particular Maybach, supplied by local authorities, wasn't bulletproof—just standard issue.

The glass shattered instantly, and the bullet came dangerously close to hitting Julian.

His face darkened as the gravity of the situation sunk in—someone had actually tried to assassinate him, in broad daylight, no less.

The bodyguard stood frozen for a moment, his usual confidence replaced with a look of alarm as it dawned on him that Marcus' warning wasn't just paranoia.

Marcus didn't waste a second. He reached into his Spatial Ring and pulled out a standard firearm, moving with a calm precision that belied the chaos.

Three rapid shots rang out, echoing through the street like a drumbeat.

The bullets found their marks, each one delivering a fatal headshot to the gunmen hidden in the buildings.

Two of the assailants, stationed close to the windows, toppled lifelessly through the shattered glass and hit the pavement with a sickening thud.

Gasps rippled through the bystanders on the street, their shock palpable.

Who in their right mind would attempt to assassinate a national leader? The sheer audacity of it was mind-boggling.

But what truly left them speechless was the young man sitting in the passenger seat of Julian's car. His shooting wasn't just skilled—it bordered on superhuman.

The car was moving at full speed, and the targets were more than 550 yards away.

Yet, without pausing to aim, Marcus had fired three consecutive shots, each one hitting its mark with a headshot. That was deadly accuracy.

Even the best marksmen in the world couldn't pull off something like that.

The bodyguard, a man who once prided himself on his sharpshooting, could only stare in disbelief.

He had always considered himself among the best, but watching Marcus in action shattered that illusion.

"Commander, someone just made an attempt on your life. We should turn back immediately and retreat to a secure location. This inspection needs to end here," the driver urged, his tone firm but laced with concern.

This driver wasn't just anyone—he had been with Julian for years and was one of his most trusted confidants.

His words carried weight, and Julian wasn't one to dismiss them lightly.

Julian's weathered eyes flickered toward Marcus for a brief moment before he made his decision. "No. We're not turning back. Keep driving. I want to see who has the nerve to try and kill me."

Julian wasn't just a leader—he was a seasoned war veteran. His courage and determination had been forged in the fire of battle, and this was no exception.

Part of him was driven by a desire to face the danger head-on, but another part wanted to test whether Marcus' warnings held water.

The driver hesitated, clearly uneasy, but before he could argue, Julian's voice cut through like steel.

"Do as I say. That's an order." His tone left no room for debate.

The driver clenched the wheel tightly, nodding reluctantly. Worry clouded his expression, but he followed Julian's command.

If Marcus was right, charging into a situation like this, with assassins reportedly waiting ahead, was a gamble that could cost them dearly.

Marcus glanced at Julian, a flicker of respect crossing his face.

Julian wasn't the man Marcus had expected—he was far more resolute, far braver.

The car sped past the intersection and approached the nursery, where more gunmen lay in wait.

But they didn't stand a chance. Marcus didn't even let them pull the trigger.

In a blur of motion, he fired again.

The shots were even faster this time, his precision sharper, his movements effortless.
