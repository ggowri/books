Selena's face lit up like she'd just won the lottery.

Chloe, on the other hand, looked like she was barely holding back her anger. So that was it. Miles had decided to shove this award into Selena's hands, no matter what.

Selena swayed her hips, already making her way toward the stage.

"I'm not accepting this!"

Before she could take another step, Chloe shot up from her seat, her voice ringing through the room.

Miles' gaze flicked toward her, his expression tightening. "The award was decided through public voting and the judges' scores. Selena had the highest total. What exactly are you complaining about?"

Chloe took a steady breath, her voice clear and sharp. "The public vote was open for everyone to see. I had 85.98 million votes. Selena was in second place with 35.89 million. That's not even close.

"From what I understand, this award is supposed to be based primarily on public votes, with the judges acting as a secondary factor. Even if you paid off every judge on the panel, the fact remains—I had twice as many votes as Selena. So tell me, Mr. Miles Donovan, how exactly did she end up winning?"

Chloe's words hit like a hammer—direct, undeniable.

A murmur ran through the crowd, growing louder as people nodded in agreement.

Especially Chloe's supporters, who wasted no time shouting their protests.

Miles' face darkened. Selena's smile twitched. Neither of them had expected Chloe to stand up and call them out in front of everyone.

Is she out of her mind?

Does she really think she can go up against Royal Entertainment and walk away unscathed?

Selena took a step forward, her eyes locking onto Chloe's with an icy smirk. "Oh, come on, Chloe. Don't be a sore loser. Public votes? Please. You think those numbers can't be manipulated? If the organizers say I won, then I won. Now sit down before you embarrass yourself."

Chloe didn't flinch. She met Selena's gaze, unimpressed. "Oh, give it a rest. We both know how you got that award.

"You didn't win it. You slept your way to it. And honestly? Bragging about an award you had to buy is just pathetic."

Chloe had never had the patience for women who played the casting couch game.

But Selena? She took it to another level—shamelessly clinging to Miles just to snatch a title she didn't earn. The thought of it made Chloe sick.

The second the words left her mouth, the room shifted. Miles and Selena both went rigid.

Everyone knew about Selena and Miles. But no one ever said it out loud. It was an open secret—something people accepted and ignored.

But no one dared to say it out loud. In this industry, it was just business as usual—an unspoken rule everyone pretended not to see.

But now? Chloe had just ripped the lid off it in the middle of the biggest event of the year.

"You bitch!" Selena snapped, her voice shaking with rage. "Say that again, I dare you!"

She spun toward Miles, her voice sharp with fury. "Mr. Donovan, this bitch is making a scene and ruining the ceremony. I say we have security beat her down and toss her out. Make sure she never works in Uswad's entertainment industry again!"

Miles' expression turned stone cold. He flicked a hand toward the security team.

At his signal, a wave of security guards moved in, nightsticks in hand. In seconds, Chloe and Marcus were surrounded.

Miles stepped forward, his tone low and dangerous. "Calling the award rigged is one thing. But throwing out baseless accusations? You just made this personal."

His eyes glinted with something mean. "And if Mr. Death weren't showing up tonight, trust me—I wouldn't be letting you off this easy. You'd be paying a much heavier price."
