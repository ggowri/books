"Mom, Christine, look! Isn't that Marcus driving? I heard his wife cheated on him and he got kicked out of the Winchester family. Looks like he's resorted to being a chauffeur!"

Louise and Christine instantly relaxed, a wave of smug satisfaction washing over them.

"Well!" Louise clucked her tongue. "A man without dignity will never amount to anything. He had a perfectly good life, but chose to be a live-in husband. What a disgrace to the Lewis family!" Samuel sighed.

Meanwhile, Marcus had parked the car in the courtyard.

Richard and his family stepped out.

The three sisters, who hadn't seen each other for almost half a year, exchanged pleasantries.

But to Marcus, they all wore masks, except for his adoptive mother, Melinda.

Their words, even their expressions, seemed fake.

Marcus couldn't understand why there was such a difference between blood sisters.

His adoptive mother, Melinda, was kind and down-to-earth.

Christine, although decent enough, was quite snobbish.

And Louise, the eldest, was the worst of all. Not only did she lack any sense of familial affection, but she constantly badmouthed Melinda behind her back.

If it were up to Marcus, he wouldn't have attended this wedding.

But Melinda, valuing family ties, insisted on coming.

"Melinda," Louise asked, her voice dripping with feigned concern, "where are you coming from? And how did you get a car?"

"We moved to Solomia a month ago for Nicole's college," Melinda explained with a smile. Seeing her father and two older sisters filled her with joy. "We couldn't get train tickets, so Marcus drove us back."

"You moved to Solomia? Did you buy a house?" Louise asked.

She had a competitive streak and couldn't stand the thought of others doing better than her, so she'd been grilling Melinda with questions since they arrived.

"Louise, you know Melinda's family situation!" Christine jumped in. "Solomia's housing prices are sky-high. There's no way they could afford to buy there. They must be renting."

"Makes sense," Louise replied, feeling a sense of relief.

Even Jessica's husband, who worked in construction, couldn't afford a decent place in Solomia.

The thought of Melinda's family owning a home there would have been unbearable.

"Aunt Melinda, Uncle Richard, Marcus, Nicole, come on in! The food's getting cold!" Jessica urged, inviting them into the house.

Just as everyone settled down, the roar of a powerful engine grew closer.

A Toyota Land Cruiser pulled into the courtyard, and a man with tanned skin and tattoos covering his arms stepped out.

Samuel was the first to react, rising to his feet with excitement. "Jessica, Paul's back! Hurry and go greet him!"

Jessica's face lit up with a happy smile as she heard her husband had arrived. She rushed out to meet him.

Paul Baker, with his buzz-cut and streetwise demeanor, was a well-known figure in Yolanda Village.

He had spent years working construction in Solomia and recently purchased a five-million-dollar house there to serve as his and Jessica's marital home.

It was a source of envy for many in the village.

"Honey, let me introduce you. This is Aunt Melinda, Uncle Richard, my cousin Marcus, and my cousin Nicole," Jessica said to Paul.

Paul gave Melinda's family a haughty once-over before quickly dismissing them.

He offered a casual greeting and sat down.

Jessica had already told him about her relatives.

They were just ordinary farmers, not worth his attention.

The only one who caught his eye was Nicole, who had just come of age.

Young girls were nice; she was undoubtedly fresh, much better than his wife Jessica!

"Melinda, look how successful Jessica is! She found such a good husband. Paul makes two to three million a year working construction in Solomia, and he even knows that wealthy big boss!

"My son, Henry, got a job in the city thanks to Paul. Five thousand dollars a month, with full benefits!

"Marcus is divorced, and being a driver for someone else isn't a real career. You should ask Louise if Paul can help him find a job.

"We're sisters! She'll definitely agree."

Christine turned to Melinda, seemingly struggling to find the right words to praise Paul.

"Melinda, I know your family has been struggling these past few years, but you never said a word to me about it. Now that Jessica has found a good husband, our family is on the rise, and we certainly won't forget about you."

Louise chimed in, also addressing Melinda, before shifting her attention to Marcus with a disapproving frown.

"Marcus, honestly, you have no sense of awareness! Your brother is here, and you don't even bother to greet him.

"Quick, pour Paul a glass of wine and apologize. If you get on his good side, maybe he'll hook you up with a decent job. Wouldn't that be better than chauffeuring people around?"
