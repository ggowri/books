Julian's gaze remained as cold as ice. "I may not listen to every accusation, but Marcus is different. I trust him completely—because he's the man who saved my life."

Those words landed like a bomb in the room, leaving Pablo, Arabella, and everyone else utterly speechless.

Around them, the crowd stood frozen, their wide eyes betraying a mixture of shock and awe.

"Father, are you telling me... he's the one who saved your life in Solomia?" Pablo's voice trembled as he asked.

Julian nodded, his eyes piercingly cold. "And now, you still dare to lie to me?"

Pablo felt as if the floor beneath him had crumbled.

For days, he had heard his father speaking about the young man who had saved him, his tone filled with awe and admiration.

Pablo had even believed that if this young man wished to enter the military, his father would personally ensure he rose to the highest ranks.

But never—never in his wildest dreams—did Pablo imagine that the young man in question was Marcus.

Arabella froze too, the realization hitting her like a cold slap.

She had heard Pablo recount tales of this mysterious hero who saved Julian, someone she had secretly admired for his bravery.

But now, standing before her, was the same man she had insulted, mocked, and threatened to have arrested.

Arabella's cheeks burned with shame, and for the first time in her career, she wished she could just vanish.

The rest of the onlookers stood rooted in place, their jaws slack.

Though Julian had kept the incident quiet, many of them had caught wind of the assassination attempt in Solomia, thanks to their high-rank background.

They had heard whispers of a fearless young man who had turned the tide.

But no one could have guessed that man was Marcus.

Grace Mitchell's chest tightened as she stared at Marcus, her mind struggling to piece it all together.

As the mayor's daughter, she had heard about the attempt on Julian's life.

Her father had been shaken by the news, relieved by the fact that someone saved Julian.

Grace had always wondered who this hero might be—someone capable of such incredible feats.

Never, not for a second, had she connected the dots to Marcus.

Now, she could hear her heart pounding.

"Father, I didn't know!" Pablo suddenly burst out, his voice cracking. "If I had known he was your savior, I never would have—please, don't be angry. I'll have Benjamin Mitchell released immediately!"

With everything Pablo knew about Julian, he could tell one thing for certain—his father was genuinely furious this time. If he didn't release Benjamin immediately, the consequences would be unimaginable!

Julian's expression didn't soften in the slightest. His voice thundered through the room. "Benjamin Mitchell served as Solomia's mayor with integrity, building it into a city recognized on the international stage. His contributions are monumental! And yet, you—my own son—dared to drag him to Kingston on baseless charges? How could I have raised such a disgrace?"

Julian's voice trembled with fury as his body shook with barely-contained anger.

As a man who had devoted his life to Uswad, Julian had hoped to spend his final years in peace.

But now, his legacy was tainted by his own son's betrayal.

Pablo's lips moved as if to explain, but Julian raised a hand, silencing him. "Don't waste your breath. You've falsified evidence and abused your authority for personal vendettas. Someone like you has no place in the OIG!"

Turning sharply to his assistant, Julian commanded, "Effective immediately, strip Pablo Ashford of his title as Director. Remove him from the Party and launch an investigation into his conduct!"

The silence that followed was deafening.

And a sheer shiver crept onto their spines.

No one dared to speak, the weight of Julian's words pressing down on the room like a leaden cloud.

Even the air seemed to grow heavier under Julian's imposing presence.

Pablo's knees buckled as he collapsed to the floor, his face pale and drenched in sweat.
