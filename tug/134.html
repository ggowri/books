"My name is Marcus Fitzgerald. Here's my contact info, but forget about the special reward," Marcus said as he handed Maxine a business card.

Maxine looked at him quizzically and replied, "Your skills are pretty impressive. I may have misjudged you earlier."

Marcus smiled, "Officer Burnett, I'm flattered. My skills are hardly comparable to yours."

Maxine rolled her eyes, realizing he was mocking her, and decided to stay silent.

The two made their way to the first-class cabin, which turned out to be a private room with just two seats and a bathroom—quite peaceful.

Marcus settled into his seat, closed his eyes, and quickly dozed off.

Maxine glanced at his profile, clearly tempted to ask him something, but seeing he was asleep, she held her tongue.

Two hours later, the plane touched down safely at Solomia Airport.

Due to the recent hijacking by mercenaries—who were ultimately killed—the airport had dispatched a large crew to greet the passengers and promote the heroic efforts of the flight crew.

Therefore, as Marcus and the others disembarked, they were swarmed by media personnel, cameras flashing everywhere, and reporters clamoring to interview the passengers.

Being a key player in the anti-hijacking endeavor, Maxine quickly drew media attention and found herself surrounded.

With her naturally aloof demeanor, she seemed to dislike the spotlight. She answered a few questions curtly before breaking through the throng and heading straight for the airport exit.

Her gaze scanned the area as if searching for someone.

Eventually, her eyes landed on a vehicle parked not too far away.

Diana was loading Marcus's luggage into the car and preparing to drive off.

Maxine took in the scene.

"Looks like this guy is a big deal. Maybe I really did misjudge him," she thought to herself as she hailed a taxi and left the airport.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, I heard your flight was hijacked," Diana casually remarked as she drove.

Her tone was relaxed, showing no hint of concern.

Diana was confident that with Marcus's prowess, the ordinary hijackers wouldn't pose any real threat to him.

"Yeah, they seemed to be mercenaries from the Black Hawk Legion," Marcus replied, still resting his eyes.

"The Black Hawk Legion? I see," Diana said, her expression shifting to one of relief.

As she drove, she explained, "On the surface, they seem like just another mercenary group, possibly a terrorist organization at worst.

"But there's a powerful force backing them. Along with their trained mercenaries, they've secretly developed orcs and cyborgs.

"Some of these have advanced to the levels of warriors, wizards, and metahumans.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, by participating in the anti-hijacking, you've put yourself on the Black Hawk Legion's blacklist.

"Once you're on that list, they'll stop at nothing to kill you. You need to be careful."

Marcus remained unfazed, keeping his eyes closed. "Well, I've killed them, so if they want revenge, just come to me."

Diana frowned, feeling that Marcus could sometimes be overly reckless.

Take that trip to Kingston a while back—it sounded thrilling but was incredibly dangerous.

Ricardo was clearly more powerful than Marcus, and one wrong move could have meant disaster.

Yet Marcus had gone without a second thought.

Though he'd returned unscathed, Diana's worry lingered.

Now he had made enemies with the Black Hawk Legion, but Marcus still seemed indifferent.

Diana couldn't quite understand his mindset. Was this simply his unwavering confidence in his abilities?
