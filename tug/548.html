Raymond was momentarily taken aback, but he quickly shook it off.

After all, the Axe Gang was one of the largest underground forces in Kingston, so it wasn't surprising that their rules had gotten out.

It was very possible that, as Edmund had suggested, Marcus was just trying to intimidate him.

"You still haven't answered my question." Marcus' voice rang out again.

Raymond's temper flared. "Who the hell do you think you are, questioning me?!" he roared. "Boys, take him down! Teach this guy a lesson!"

As he barked orders, the gang members began to close in on Marcus.

But just then, the sound of luxury cars pulling up echoed from the street. A convoy stopped quickly in front of the restaurant.

"I know that plate number. It must be the gang's leader!" someone in the crowd murmured.

At the mention of Blake, a wave of fear swept through the crowd. No one had expected Blake to show up in person.

Raymond, seeing this, immediately waved his hand to halt his men.

He walked forward, his face suddenly all smiles as he greeted Blake with deep respect. "Mr. Brooks!

"What brings you here?"

Blake, however, didn't even acknowledge his greeting. Instead, without warning, he slapped Raymond across the face.

The impact sent Raymond stumbling backward, crashing to the ground with a cry of pain.

The crowd froze, utterly shocked. No one had expected Blake to go straight for Raymond the moment he arrived.

Raymond scrambled to his feet, his face pale with shock. "Mr. Brooks, what did I do wrong? Why are you hitting me?"

Before he could finish, Blake kicked him hard in the stomach, sending him sprawling. "I'm hitting you because I want to! If I don't beat the hell out of you today, I won't be able to sleep at night!"

Blake wasn't done. He unleashed a flurry of punches and kicks on the helpless Raymond.

Raymond groaned in pain, pleading for mercy, but Blake didn't stop until he was clearly exhausted.

By now, Edmund and Tina were utterly stunned, their jaws hanging open.

Then, in front of everyone, Blake, who had just been violently attacking his subordinate, suddenly bowed to Marcus. "My apologies for the delay, Mr. Fitzgerald. Please don't be angry with me."

The words hit the crowd like a thunderclap. They were shaking, unable to process what they'd just heard.

Edmund and Tina were in complete disbelief. They never imagined that the gang leader, a figure so feared and powerful, would treat Marcus with such respect.

Raymond, his mind racing, suddenly realized why Marcus looked familiar. It hit him like a bolt of lightning.

A month ago, something huge had happened within the gang.

Though he wasn't there himself, he'd heard all the stories about how Marcus had taken control of the gang and reasserted his power.

How could he have not recognized someone of that caliber? And yet, he had fallen for Edmund's lies and almost made a move on Marcus.

Now, the realization stung so badly that he almost slapped himself.

Marcus, however, wasn't paying attention to the shock in the room. He turned to Blake and said casually, "I told you to properly vet the clients. How could you forget so quickly?"

Blake's face fell.

He swallowed hard, clearly shaken, and bowed his head. "Mr. Fitzgerald, it's my fault. I didn't supervise properly. This job wasn't approved by me. It was one of the branch leaders who sent him.

"I rushed here the moment I found out, but it was already too late. Mr. Fitzgerald, I'm so sorry for that!"

He stood up and walked back to Raymond, kicking him again with a sneer. "Someone, chop off this bastard's hands. This is what happens when you cross Mr. Fitzgerald!"

The Axe Gang members immediately rushed forward, ready to carry out the order.

Raymond's face drained of color. He could hardly believe the situation he was in.

Just as they were about to strike, Marcus raised a hand and pointed towards Edmund and Tina. "Forget him. Those two are the ones you need to deal with."

Edmund and Tina had frozen in place, terror written all over their faces.

Hearing Marcus' words made their expressions collapse.

They couldn't believe Marcus was so powerful.

Even Blake was bowing to him. They had no idea what kind of person they had offended.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, I was wrong! Please, I beg you. Forgive me this time. I'll do anything, anything at all!" Edmund cried, his voice desperate.

But Marcus didn't even glance at him. His eyes were cold and indifferent.

In the next instant, the Axe Gang members moved swiftly, grabbing Edmund and Tina and slamming them to the ground.
