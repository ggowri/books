The words froze everyone in their tracks, Darian, Ramona, Aniya, and even the nurse, Sophia, were all stunned.

Ramona's voice trembled as she asked, "Cal is okay? Really? Darian, let's go see him, quick!"

Darian snapped out of his shock and hurried to follow Ramona into the ICU.

Sophia and Aniya trailed behind them.

Inside, Cal was awake, his face flushed with color, looking lively and alert.

Kiera was feeding him fruit, and Cal was eating with gusto.

When he saw Darian and Ramona enter, he smiled widely and shouted, "Mom, Dad, you're here! I don't want to stay here anymore. I feel like I'm completely better now!"

Darian, Ramona, Aniya, and Sophia gathered around the bed.

They looked at Cal, completely stunned. His face was no longer pale or sickly—it was full of life. For a moment, none of them knew how to react, but then their faces lit up with pure joy.

"He's really better! This is unbelievable!" Ramona's voice quivered with emotion.

Before this, Ramona hadn't had any hope left.

When Darian had insisted on letting Marcus into the ICU, Ramona had been dismissive and even contemptuous.

At the time, her mind had been focused on leaving Darian, and she hadn't stopped him from bringing Marcus in.

She never could have imagined that Marcus had such extraordinary medical skills.

Cal's congenital heart disease had been severe, and even the top specialists from Kingston had given up.

Ramona was at a loss for how Marcus had cured her son.

Darian rubbed his eyes in disbelief, even questioning whether this was some kind of dream.

For years, he had drained his savings, worked himself to the bone, and still found himself buried in debt, all in an attempt to save his son.

He had no idea when the hardship would end, but as a father, he had gritted his teeth and kept going.

Never had he imagined that a chance car accident would lead to a miracle that saved his son's life.

"Did this kid really cure him?" Aniya's voice was full of disbelief.

Sophia, the nurse, had only recently joined the hospital and was unaware of Marcus' reputation at Solomia.

She, too, was skeptical.

She rushed out to call for the doctor.

Dr. Crawford, who had been on duty, seemed unbothered by the commotion. With a calm expression, he said, "What's the fuss about? I've already told you—if Mr. Fitzgerald is involved, Cal's condition is as good as cured. There's no need for any more tests. If the family agrees, they can take him home tomorrow."

Sophia stood frozen in shock.

She had worked as a nurse for several years, encountering many renowned doctors, but never had she seen one who could heal a congenital heart condition without surgery or medication.

Not fully believing Dr. Crawford's words, she rushed back to the ICU.

She brought in the heart monitoring equipment and conducted her own test on Cal.

The results left her stunned.

Cal, once plagued by a severe congenital heart condition, now had a heart that was healthier than most people's.

From the shape of his heart and the surrounding blood vessels, it was clear that, barring any accidents, he would never suffer from heart disease again.

This was nothing short of a miracle.

Sophia's gaze toward Marcus shifted. Filled with awe.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, thank you so much for saving my son's life. You're truly a lifesaver for our entire family!"

Darian rushed out of the room, dropping to his knees in front of Marcus. "Please, let me thank you properly." He tried to bow his head in gratitude.

Marcus quickly helped him up. "I told you I'd help your son. I always keep my word."

Ramona, too, expressed her heartfelt thanks to Marcus.

At that moment, Aniya walked out.

But rather than looking pleased or surprised, she still wore a dark expression. "Even if Cal's better, it doesn't change the fact that you're just a laborer. So, your marriage is still over."

Darian was jolted out of his moment of joy. He turned to Aniya, his voice urgent. "Aniya, I know I haven't made a lot of money over the years, but I've been working hard. Why can't you understand that?"

Aniya sneered. "Understand you? Who's understanding me? I'd love a son-in-law who can take care of me. But you—look at you! You can't even support Ramona, let alone take care of me. Director Rollins' son is the one with the good job—earning a million-dollar salary. If Ramona had married him, I'd be living the good life, too!"

"Aniya, I'll work harder. I'll do everything I can to make sure Ramona and you have a better life. Please, don't talk about divorce anymore."

Darian's plea was filled with desperation.

Now that Cal was better, he couldn't bear the thought of losing Ramona.

Aniya's eyes narrowed, her lips curling into a dismissive smile. "Work hard? What for? You really think this guy's going to make you a million-dollar salary?"
