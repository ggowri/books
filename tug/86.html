Walter trembled in fear, unsure of what to do next.

"If you don't speak up, I'll have no choice but to act!" Marcus said with a cold smirk, grabbing Walter's wrist, preparing to take action.

Walter cried out in desperation. "I'll do it, I'll do it myself!"

With trembling hands, he took the dagger, closing his eyes tightly before plunging it into his right leg.

"Ahhh..." Walter screamed in agony, the dagger sinking into his flesh, and blood poured out as his right leg's tendons were severed.

"Go on!" Marcus ordered, showing no mercy.

Walter was drenched in sweat, fully aware that if he didn't act fast, Marcus would be even more brutal.

With no choice left, he swung the dagger again, this time slashing his left leg, cutting through the tendons.

"Good, very good. You're being obedient. Keep going!" Marcus applauded Walter, as though praising a dog for its obedience.

The onlookers, unable to bear witnessing such cruelty, shut their eyes in horror.

Gritting his teeth, Walter raised the dagger again, cutting through the tendons in his left arm.

He was on the verge of passing out from the pain, but he held on, managing to say through clenched teeth, "Is this enough now?"

Marcus nodded in satisfaction. "I'll leave your right hand intact, so you can survive... for now. I hope you don't dare to provoke me again, or you'll regret it more painfully."

Walter, feeling as though he had been granted mercy, tried to leave, but just as he moved, the sound of sirens filled the air.

Several police cars drove into the courtyard, and a tall, middle-aged officer in a police uniform stepped out. His features bore a strong resemblance to Josie.

"Josie, I'm late," the officer said.

It was Josie's older brother, Allen Shelman, the captain of the East District Criminal Police Team in Kingston.

Josie quickly reassured him. "It's okay, Allen, Mr. Fitzgerald has already handled everything."

She then recounted the details of the incident to Allen, who listened intently.

Though known for his fair and just approach, hearing how Walter had bullied his sister stirred a deep sense of anger within him.

What's more, Walter's actions were particularly severe: leading an underground criminal group to cause trouble at a private company while armed with weapons.

Under the laws of Uswad, such actions were a serious crime.

Walter, clearly unaware of the gravity of the situation, saw the arrival of the police as his moment of salvation. A look of relief spread across his face, and he immediately shouted. "Officer! This man deliberately severed my tendons, causing grievous injury. Please, arrest him and bring him to justice!"

Allen had a cold smile. "The person who needs arresting is you. Take him into custody!"

Without hesitation, several police officers stepped forward and restrained Walter.

Walter's face turned pale with rage. "This man started the violence! Why are you arresting me?"

Allen calmly listed Walter's crimes, saying, "You were the one provoking trouble. This man was acting in self-defense. But your charge of provocation still stands, and that's why you're under arrest."

Walter's face twisted in despair, feeling as though his world was crashing down around him.

The Brown family of Kingston was well-known for their obsession with maintaining their family's reputation.

While they may have engaged in shady dealings behind closed doors, they were meticulous about keeping their dirty work hidden from the public eye.

To be associated with the criminal underworld so openly was a grave mistake, one that would cost Walter dearly.

Even if he were jailed and punished, the Browns would likely not come to his rescue.

"Josie, I'll take him now. You should thank Mr. Fitzgerald for his help," Allen said, casting a respectful glance at Marcus before leading the officers away.

Walter, still seething with fury, shouted. "So, you're siblings! This is personal vengeance! I'll sue you, I'll sue you both!"

As Walter's angry shouts faded, Josie sighed in relief and turned her gaze to Marcus, her admiration for him evident. "Mr. Fitzgerald, once again, you've helped me. I don't know how to thank you," she said, her voice softening with gratitude.

As she reflected on the course of events, Josie realized that everything had unfolded just as Marcus had predicted.

The way he orchestrated the situation was so precise that it was almost as if he had been pulling the strings from afar. It was a level of strategy and foresight that few could match—certainly not someone of Marcus's age.

Josie found herself growing more and more impressed by Marcus, her feelings for him only deepening.

"Mr. Fitzgerald," she ventured cautiously. "If I may ask, are you really just an ordinary executive at Golden Group?"
