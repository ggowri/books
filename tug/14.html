Falcon's muscles swelled with terrifying speed. Every fiber bulged, veins pulsing beneath his skin in a display of overwhelming power.

His punch cracked through the air like thunder, creating sonic booms as he launched himself at Marcus like a human missile.

Marcus didn't even blink. He met the attack head-on, their fists colliding with enough force to shake the earth.

Falcon stumbled backward, struggling to keep his footing.

Shaking out his numb arm, he grimaced. "Damn," he muttered in disbelief. "You're a warrior too!"

Warriors were categorized into nine distinct ranks. Though both of them stood at the threshold of Stage One, Marcus held the upper hand.

His mastery of the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire far exceeded Falcon's cultivation techniques, granting him an undeniable supremacy in raw strength.

"Enough talk," Marcus snarled. "Time to end this."

Without warning, he unleashed a devastating kick. Falcon barely managed to leap clear.

As he dodged, Falcon let out a roar. A fiery red light glowed brightly, crimson flames erupted from his right arm, the intense heat turning the air around them into an inferno.

The flames twisted and danced as Falcon launched a blazing punch, sending a massive fireball straight at Marcus.

Marcus' eyebrow twitched.

He can do fire attacks?

Hmm, interesting.

He's got some potential as a warrior.

But still nowhere near my level now.

With no time to waste, he dove sideways, avoiding the fireball by inches, then immediately counterattacked.

Drawing on the full power of the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire, his fist blazed with a terrifying aura of Numinous Energy.

Pure horror froze Falcon in place as the impending punch found him.

He flew through the air like a rag doll, hitting the ground with a sickening thud and coughing up blood.

Marcus stepped forward, planting his foot on Falcon's chest, pinning him down hard.

Leaning in, he demanded, "Who sent you here?"

After that punch, Falcon lay there broken, his whole body screaming in agony.

He stared up at Marcus in terror. "It was Anthony Wesley," he answered. "He ordered me to kill you. Please spare my life!"

"Anthony, huh?" Marcus echoed.

A sharp gleam flickered in his eyes. Without another word, he brought his foot down hard.

Falcon let out one last groan as his chest caved in. Then nothing.

The sky had darkened completely, but a faint glimmer of light flickered from Falcon's chest.

Marcus bent down and began to search.

His fingers brushed against something hard in Falcon's pocket. Pulling it out, he found a red crystal the size of an egg.

The moment he touched it, heat shot through his palm.

"Fire Elemental Power Crystal," Marcus muttered, recognition dawning on him.

The Timeless Warrior's Grimoire had described such crystals. This crystal held fire attribute power.

No wonder Falcon could have thrown those flame-powered punches earlier.

He's got such an impressive treasure.

But his cultivation was far too shallow that he couldn't take full advantage of it yet.

If he'd managed to merge the crystal's power with his body, he'd have been way more dangerous.

Well, it's mine now.

The fight is not that bad.

Marcus pocketed it. He'd planned to absorb its power later when he had time.

That would take his fighting skills to a whole new level. Plus, Fire Attribute Power would give him an edge if things ever went south.

Even though he had ascended to the ranks of warriors, he wasn't about to get cocky.

As the Godfather's heir, he knew his two unknown brothers wanted him dead.

To survive and claim the position of Godfather, he needed to be the strongest.

Falcon and his crew had wasted him some time, but he still had to catch up.

He jumped in his Ferrari and raced toward his next destination.

An hour later, he reached the villa.

When he opened the door, he saw a figure hunched over, sweeping the yard in the moonlight.

"Dad!" Marcus called out.
