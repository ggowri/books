Maxine, Shaun, and Aiton were all stunned.

They could hardly fathom that Marcus' casual punch had such overwhelming power.

The remaining wizard was terrified, attempting to flee.

But Marcus wasn't going to let him get away. With a swift motion, he turned and slammed his fist into the wizard's head.

The wizard hurriedly dodged, narrowly avoiding Marcus' punch. "The peak of Ancient Martial Stage Four? How is this possible? It's only been a month. How did you do it?"

Marcus didn't respond. Instead, he threw another punch, this time with even more lethal intent.

The wizard had no chance to resist. The punch landed with brutal force, striking his chest and sending him flying.

A bloodied hole appeared in his chest, and his internal organs were pulverized by the shockwave.

The pungent stench of blood filled the air, making the surroundings feel colder.

Shaun and Aiton were ghastly pale.

Aiton tried to flee.

But before he could move, Marcus' hand clamped down on his shoulder. "Do you think you can escape?"

Aiton broke into a cold sweat.

He looked at Marcus in horror.

Before this moment, he never imagined that Marcus' power could have grown to such an extent.

If he had known, he wouldn't have come.

"If you spare me, I'm willing to pay any price," Aiton begged.

Marcus sneered, "I'm not interested in your wealth. Die!"

He launched a punch directly at Aiton's head. The impact was so fierce that Aiton's skull exploded. Brain matter and fragments of bone splattered everywhere.

The scene sent Shaun into a complete stupor.

His legs turned to jelly, and before he could control himself, a hot, unpleasant warmth spread through his pants. The stench of urine quickly filled the air.

"Please, don't kill me," Shaun begged, trembling as he stumbled backward.

"Weren't you so arrogant earlier?" Marcus said, his voice dripping with disdain.

He took step after step toward Shaun.

Shaun had humiliated Maxine not long ago. There was no way Marcus was going to let him off.

"I was wrong. I won't do it again. Please, don't kill me. I don't want to die." Shaun dropped to his knees, letting go of his pride.

"To hell!" Marcus snorted.

He pulled out a pistol and aimed it at Shaun's forehead.

The gunshot echoed, and Shaun's body went limp before it collapsed to the ground.

Marcus put the gun away and turned to look at Maxine.

She stood frozen, shocked by the brutality of what she had just witnessed.

"What's wrong?" Marcus asked.

Maxine snapped back to reality. "I didn't expect you to be so strong."

Marcus chuckled, "It's just a few lowly creatures. Not worth mentioning."

"You destroyed eight cyborgs and two wizards. You're more than strong," she muttered.

Marcus didn't even seem human anymore.

"But you killed Shaun and Aiton. Their families are sure to come after you for revenge. You should be careful," she warned with concern.

Marcus laughed dismissively, "The fact that they attacked me means their families must have given them the go-ahead. Such families are already doomed.

"Within a month, I'll make sure they vanish from this world."

The air around him was chilling.

"Alright, enough about them. Tell me about the mission you mentioned earlier," Marcus said, shifting the topic.

Maxine took a deep breath. "Let's talk in the car. I don't want to see this bloody scene."

Marcus chuckled and used his power to incinerate the bodies.

In just a matter of seconds, those bodies and the eight cyborgs were reduced to ashes.

The sight once again left Maxine shaken.

She finally understood that Marcus wasn't an ordinary human being. His combat prowess was extraordinary.

Not only did he have a variety of lethal techniques at his disposal, but he could casually summon flames to obliterate evidence with ease.

His skill was so practiced that it was clear he had done similar things many times before.

This realization sent a chill down Maxine's spine.

The two of them got into the police jeep.

She started, "This mission is of great importance to the organization. Over a dozen departments are involved, and each has sent their most elite operatives. Our unit is just one of them.

"If we manage to capture the key figures of the Falcon, the upper-ups will give us major rewards."

Marcus' interest was piqued. "Rewards? Is it money?"

Maxine laughed, "Not so shallow. These kinds of honors aren't something money can buy."
