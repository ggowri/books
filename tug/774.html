A wave of overwhelming pressure emerged from the void, enveloping the three figures in its oppressive grasp.

Raphael immediately fell to his knees, his expression devout as he began to pray fervently toward the castle before them.

Marcus and Ada simply stood by, watching him in silence.

"What brings you here, humans?"

A voice echoed from the void, ethereal and ancient, its tone carrying the weight of countless ages. It resonated deeply, shaking them to their core.

Marcus responded calmly, "We've come to earn the right to pass through the space-time tunnel and to seek the Return Essence Crystal."

He spoke plainly, withholding nothing.

"To earn the right to pass through the space-time tunnel, you must conquer the first six of the nine levels ahead," the voice announced.

"If you can clear the first six levels, not only will you gain access to the space-time tunnel, but I will also reward you with three Return Essence Crystals."

The voice carried a tempting allure, its offer almost too good to resist.

Marcus' eyes lit up with a fervent glint. He asked, "I have a few questions. Will you answer them?"

"Of course," the voice replied without hesitation. "Ask away."

Marcus began, "If I clear the levels and gain the qualification to pass the space-time tunnel, but I don't want to venture into the world beyond the Morvalis Ice Wall right away, can this right be postponed?

"Also, three Return Essence Crystals won't suffice. Can we negotiate for more?"

Three crystals were barely enough for him, Ada, and Raphael.

But there were others by his side who were nearing the Ancient Martial Stage Nine.

With time, even more, would reach that stage, and Marcus knew he'd need more Return Essence Crystals to help them enter the Return Essence stage.

"The qualification is valid for life. As long as you live, you will automatically gain access to the space-time tunnel whenever you approach it," the voice explained.

"As for the crystals, three is the maximum reward for clearing the first six levels. If you want more, you'll have to conquer the higher levels.

"For every level beyond the sixth that you clear, your reward will double. For instance, if you conquer all nine levels, you'll earn 24 Return Essence Crystals."

The voice answered each of Marcus' questions methodically.

Hearing this, Marcus' eyes gleamed with determination. "Then I'll give it a try. I hope you'll keep your word!"

"I've guarded this place for millennia," the voice replied with a hint of disdain. "Do you think I'd bother lying to a mere human? You'd better think more about how to clear the first six levels first.

"If your strength is insufficient, regardless of any rewards I promise, you simply won't be able to attain it. You might even pay with your life. And then, all of this will be meaningless."

The voice carried an air of contempt, making it clear it didn't believe Marcus could succeed.

Marcus didn't argue. He knew his abilities would speak for themselves.

Turning to Raphael and Ada, he said, "Wait for me here. I'll face the trials alone."

Ada's face was etched with worry as she urged, "Be careful. If it's too dangerous, give up. Don't let pride get the better of you."

Her time with Marcus had taught her he was a man who refused to yield, even in the face of overwhelming odds.

She feared he might press on recklessly.

Just as the voice had warned, if that happened, Marcus could even lose his life.

"Don't worry."

Marcus gave her a reassuring glance.

As he spoke, the enormous castle doors creaked open on their own.

Golden light poured out, dazzling yet exuding a foreboding sense of danger.

Without hesitation, Marcus stepped inside.

Behind him, the doors closed slowly, sealing him in.

Almost immediately, his surroundings shifted.

The opulent castle faded away, replaced by a vast and desolate space.

It was an endless expanse of sky and earth, utterly barren.

The heavens above were pitch-black, devoid of stars, sun, or moon.

It was as though this strange world had been crafted solely for him.

Before long, a light flared in the distance ahead.

The glow intensified, taking the form of a human figure.

As the figure solidified, Marcus' eyes widened in shock.

He couldn't believe his eyes. It was him.

"You need not panic," the voice from earlier spoke again. "The trials of this castle are simple. To ascend to a higher world, you need only defeat yourself."

Marcus frowned.

He hadn't expected the trial to be like this.

Without warning, the other Marcus raised his hand, summoning the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire. In a flash, he unleashed a devastating handprint infused with overwhelming destructive energy.

This attack combined the powers of Timeless Numinous Energy, Thunderfire Power, and Rune Power swirling with lethal force.

The other Marcus' strength was identical to his own.

"Illusion," Marcus thought instantly.

He knew that this trial was, without a doubt, an illusion.

But this illusion was unnervingly real. It felt like he was experiencing it firsthand.

In the blink of an eye, the destructive Hand Strike was upon him.

Caught off guard, Marcus barely managed to summon a Fist Ray in defense. The impact sent him stumbling backward, blood churning in his chest.

"The peak of Ancient Martial Stage Nine," he muttered to himself. "This version of me is just as strong as I am. Defeating it won't be easy." Determined, Marcus threw himself into the fight.

The battle raged on, fiercer than he had anticipated. An hour passed with neither side gaining the upper hand.

His opponent was relentless, perfectly mirroring his every move.

Worse still, while Marcus grew fatigued, the illusion seemed inexhaustible, fighting with mechanical precision.

This left Marcus in a difficult position, retreating step by step as his opponent pushed him back.

Frustrated but undeterred, Marcus swallowed several energy-replenishing pills and pressed on.

Though draining, the battle revealed his weaknesses with startling clarity.

He realized the flaws in his combat techniques.

Suddenly, he understood the true meaning behind the idea that to defeat others, one must first defeat oneself.

The castle's challenges were more than mere obstacles. They were a crucible, designed to refine those who dared to face them.

His opponent might wield identical powers, but as an illusion, it lacked the ability to adapt and improve.

In contrast, Marcus' combat skills evolved with every passing moment. After more than an hour of fighting, Marcus identified his weaknesses and areas for improvement.

This battle had improved his ability, while his opponent remained unchanged.

Recognizing this, he adjusted his strategy, targeting the weaknesses in his mirrored self. Slowly but surely, the tide of battle began to turn in his favor.
