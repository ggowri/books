The hostess gasped, covering her mouth in shock.

She pointed at Marcus and said, "Manager! It's this gentleman!"

The hotel manager soon pushed through the crowd, waving off security.

He bowed to Marcus. "Honorable Supreme Card holder, you are one of our group's most distinguished guests. Please forgive my oversight and the staff's rudeness. This way, sir."

With that, he respectfully motioned for Marcus to enter.

Abigail and Felix stood frozen.

Is that really the so-called Supreme Card?

Isn't Marcus just a useless loser?

Where the hell did he get that valuable card?

Impossible!

Marcus walked past them without a glance.

"That worthless nobody has a Supreme Card?" Abigail stomped her foot.

"He must've stolen it!" Felix snarled, refusing to accept the truth.

Abigail nodded eagerly in agreement.

Just then, a fleet of cars pulled up. A Rolls-Royce Phantom stopped right in front of them.

A middle-aged man in a sleek suit stepped out of the car, exuding a powerful presence.

It was Anthony Wesley, one of the two leading figures of Golden Group's major factions.

He was Golden Group's heavyweight, controlling 25% of shares.

"Felix, why aren't you inside? The conference is starting," Anthony's eyes glinted beneath his silver temples.

He'd been planning to make Felix the next general manager, solidifying his control over the company's affairs.

"Got held up by some trouble," Felix said, his mood brightening at Anthony's appearance.

Finally, he felt he could reclaim some pride.

He quickly explained about the Supreme Card incident.

When he finished, Anthony's eyebrows lifted a bit. "Those haven't been issued in years."

The person beside him approached and whispered, "Sir, only you and Mr. Davis can authorize those. Could it be the new chairman?"

Anthony's gaze sharpened, a calculating look flashing across his eyes. "Issuing cards before taking office? Bold. Now I'm even more curious to meet this new chairman."

Abigail leaned close to Felix. "Mr. Yannis, if the new chairman is your old friend, why didn't he give you a Supreme Card too?"

Felix froze, struggling to come up with an explanation.

"Must be because I'm with Mr. Wesley," he muttered.

Abigail nodded, though suspicion nagged at her. She was already plotting how to build ties with both the chairman and Anthony.

Inside the Golden Hotel's banquet hall, Solomia's elite had gathered.

Dressed in a sharp suit, Marcus walked in, drawing all attention the moment he entered.

Some didn't bother hiding their sneers.

"Look who it is - the Winchesters' lap dog. Three years married and his wife wouldn't even let him touch her. He even donated a kidney to save her! A good-for-nothing. How dare he show up here?"

Although the Winchester family was a third-rate family in Solomia, everyone knew about their worthless son-in-law, Marcus.

No one wanted anything to do with him, leaving a wide berth around his table.

"If you don't mind, sir, you're welcome to join me," a soft voice offered.

A young woman in an elegant cream gown smiled warmly at him.

Seeing genuine kindness in her eyes, Marcus accepted.

She introduced herself as Michelle Smith, daughter of Golden Group's former president.

She had graduated from Briarwood University with a degree in economics and management.

Her father, Frank, had passed away in the power plays within the company due to conflicts of interest with the other two factions.

As a candidate for this selection, she was fighting to reclaim her father's legacy today.

Their conversation flowed naturally.

Michelle was graceful, well-spoken, and refreshingly unprejudiced about his past.

Marcus had a very good impression of her.

"Well, well, Marcus, you're really something. I just dumped you not long ago and you're already hooking up with others?"

Abigail's voice cut through the air as she strutted in with Felix and Anthony.

"Abigail, you better watch your mouth, or I'll make you regret it," Marcus warned, his cold stare making her stumble back.

"Soon Mr. Yannis will be the general manager of Golden Group. I'd like to see what you can possibly do!" Abigail snorted disdainfully.

With Anthony's support behind Felix, everyone assumed the general manager position was set for him.

"He'll never get that position," Marcus declared firmly. "Not while I'm here."
