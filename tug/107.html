The woman stared at Marcus as if he were some kind of freak of nature.

In her experience, both warriors and wizards typically focused solely on honing their specialized skills.

Individuals who possessed both warrior and wizard abilities were scarce, often existing only in legends.

She had never encountered such a person in reality.

When Marcus unleashed his Lightning, she felt her blood run cold.

In that instant, she knew this monster completely outmatched her.

In desperation, she pulled out a black orb and smashed it against the ground.

A thick cloud of smoke erupted, engulfing the entire area.

She tried to use this chance to make her escape.

But Marcus had seen right through her cheap trick, materializing right in front of her.

She nearly jumped out of her skin and tried to bolt, but Marcus's iron grip was already around her throat, lifting her off the ground!

"Please don't kill me! I'll do anything you want, just let me live!" she begged, pure terror flashing in her eyes.

Marcus couldn't care less about her pleas - he simply snapped her neck without a second thought!

After pocketing both Spatial Rings, he walked away from the scene without looking back.

After Marcus left, a graceful silhouette appeared on the cliffs overlooking Bayside Road.

Her face was veiled in gauze, her presence ethereal and elegant. Through the veil, her striking eyes gleamed as she mused, "Lucky I didn't make my move too soon. I clearly underestimated you!"

The woman vanished from the cliff as if she'd never been there at all.

She was the same mysterious woman who had appeared after Brandon's death.

She was from the Uswad Ancient Martial Art Sect, the Astral Serpent Order, and claimed to be Brandon's junior.

Surprisingly, she had been watching Marcus this whole time without him noticing her presence.

Back at Bayside Villa, Diana approached Marcus, her brow furrowing at the sight of blood on his clothes. "Mr. Fitzgerald, are you alright?"

Marcus shook his head. "The Browns made their move, but they still underestimated my abilities."

As he spoke, a deadly glint flashed in his deep-set eyes.

He knew it was time to strike back.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, be careful. My investigation shows that Mr. Roger Gellar has his eye on you now. I suspect the Browns' hasty attack is connected to him," Diana said with concern.

She knew better than anyone just how powerful Roger was.

Though Marcus's recent growth had exceeded her expectations, he was still no match for Roger.

However, it seemed that Roger didn't currently consider this younger brother a threat. Otherwise, Marcus alone wouldn't stand a chance against him!

Marcus's eyes flickered. He had suspected as much, and Diana's reminder had confirmed his suspicions.

"By the way, Mr. Fitzgerald," Diana asked, "where did you get that witchcraft book you're teaching them from?"

"It was a spoil of war," Marcus replied. "Why, are you interested in learning?"

Diana's sapphire blue eyes sparkled, and she gracefully raised her hand.

A blue flame erupted from her palm, its intense heat making Marcus's skin sting.

"Such powerful flames!" Marcus blurted out, his gaze shifting as he reassessed her.

"Diana, so you're a Wizard too!"

Diana smiled, "Mr. Fitzgerald, I'm far more than just a Wizard."

"Uh..."

Marcus broke into a cold sweat. This woman had been hiding some serious power.

He felt relieved he hadn't tried anything stupid with her earlier.

Judging by the flames she'd conjured, her witchcraft level had to be at least Ancient Martial Stage Three.

Even Marcus, as powerful as he was now, might not be a match for her.

Meanwhile, in the Kingston Brown family's secret chamber, a messenger rushed in. "Sir, both warriors you sent were killed by Marcus!"

"What?" Ricardo's eyes flashed with surprise.

Both warriors had reached the peak of Ancient Martial Arts Stage Two, making them incredibly formidable.

Together, they could even hold their own against someone who had just advanced to Level Three.

Yet even their combined strength hadn't been enough to stop Marcus from killing them both.

Such power was beyond Ricardo's wildest expectations.

"It seems I'll have to deal with this brat personally," Ricardo muttered.

A deadly glint flashed in Ricardo's eyes. He'd reached the peak of Level Three years ago and was just one step away from Level Four.

Taking out a youngster like Marcus should have been child's play.

The problem was that Marcus was as cunning as a fox. This failed assassination attempt with two elite warriors would definitely put him on high alert.

Ricardo knew that trying the same sneak attack approach wouldn't work anymore.

He racked his brain, trying to figure out how to bait Marcus into coming to him instead.
