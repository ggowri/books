The room fell silent.

Darian, in particular, looked utterly shocked, his face filled with disbelief. "Mr. Fitzgerald, are you saying you want to invest in me?"

"Yes," Marcus said with a slight smile. "I want to start a logistics company. You have experience in the logistics field, and you've proven to be responsible, hardworking, and willing to put in the effort. You fit exactly what I'm looking for."

Aniya scoffed, clearly dismissive. "How much are you really going to invest? A hundred thousand? Two hundred thousand? Oh, I know the courier station down the street is up for sale—only 150 thousand dollars. But that place doesn't even make much money."

She turned to Ramona and urged, "Ramona, just get that divorce with Darian over with. Jaxon's been fired, and I'll find you someone better!"

Marcus didn't react to Aniya's mocking. He simply continued, "Let's start with ten billion Uswadian coins. If you manage the business well and things go smoothly, I'll invest even more."

Boom.

His words hit like a bombshell, leaving Darian, Ramona, Kiera, and even Aniya frozen in disbelief.

"Ten billion Uswadian coins? You're really going to invest ten billion in Darian? Is this for real? Do you even have that kind of money?"

Aniya was completely flabbergasted.

Ten billion Uswadian coins. She'd never even seen that much money in her life.

Before, she'd thought Jaxon's six-figure salary was impressive.

If Marcus could actually invest ten billion Uswadian coins, Darian's salary would surpass Jaxon's by far.

Aniya could hardly believe it.

Ramona and Kiera shared the same skepticism.

After all, Darian and Marcus were barely acquaintances—no matter how rich Marcus might be, why would he invest such an amount in Darian?

Darian, too, thought he must have misheard. "Mr. Fitzgerald, are you serious? This isn't some kind of joke, is it?"

Marcus smiled. "Of course not. Just give me a bank account number."

Without thinking, Darian wrote down his bank account number and handed it over.

Marcus quickly accessed the app on his phone. As a platinum user, his transfer limits were far higher than ordinary people's.

In less than a second, ten billion Uswadian coins were transferred to Darian's account.

Darian stared at the successful transfer notification, frozen in shock. His whole body felt like stone.

"Ten billion Uswadian coins... is this really happening?"

Darian held the phone so tightly it almost slipped from his hands, his mind completely blank.

Ramona, Kiera, and Aniya were all staring at Darian's phone.

As they saw the overwhelming balance in his account, they too were utterly stunned.

Their gaze toward Marcus had completely shifted.

"I'm leaving the logistics company setup to you. I trust you'll do well," Marcus continued, his tone confident. "Once the company's up and running, you'll be the CEO."

"Your salary will have two parts, a fixed annual salary of ten million Uswadian coins, plus a 5% commission on the company's net profits if they exceed five billion Uswadian coins. How does that sound?"

Marcus asked, his eyes steady.

The commission-based salary structure was designed to motivate managers and foster mutual benefits for both parties. The proposal hit like another thunderclap.

"Ten million Uswadian coins, plus a 5% commission on profits? Mr. Fitzgerald, is this for real? Isn't that a bit too much?"

Darian couldn't believe what he was hearing. The entire situation felt surreal.

He pinched his leg hard to make sure he wasn't dreaming. The pain confirmed that it was real.

"It's not too much. If you successfully set up and manage the logistics company, your salary could increase even more down the line," Marcus replied with a knowing smile.

Marcus wasn't just investing to make a profit—he also wanted to secure better logistics services for his own business ventures.

With online shopping booming, if he didn't have his own logistics company, he'd be at the mercy of others for deliveries—and shipping costs were steep.

By having his own company, he could reduce costs and provide a better experience for consumers.

Marcus had been thinking about this for a while, but until now, he didn't know anyone in the logistics field.

Darian may have been just a delivery driver, but Marcus had noticed his solid work ethic, integrity, and determination.

These qualities were far more valuable than a fancy degree.

"Thank you, Mr. Fitzgerald, for believing in me. I promise I'll build this company and make it a success!"

Darian bowed deeply, the gratitude in his heart overwhelming.

Though he had only been a delivery driver, he had worked hard in the industry for five years. He knew the ins and outs of the business, and more importantly, he wasn't lacking in ambition.

He may not have had the formal education, but he wasn't ignorant—he'd just been forced to work in this job to support his family.
