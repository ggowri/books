At the sight, everyone's hearts pounded violently. No one had ever imagined—even in their wildest dreams—that Marcus' strength had reached such a terrifying level. Joel, in particular, furrowed his brows even deeper.

Joel knew all too well how powerful Axel was. Axel was an advanced fifth-generation cyborg—far beyond what even some of the strongest warriors, wizards, or metahumans could handle.

Yet now, Axel had lost an arm in front of Marcus with almost laughable ease. Joel couldn't help but feel a creeping sense of fear, wondering, "Just how powerful was Marcus, then?"

Joel wasn't the only one shaken. Cecilia, standing right beside Marcus, had an even clearer view of what had just happened. Her heart trembled uncontrollably.

Then, suddenly, it all clicked. She pondered, "That explained why Marcus had been so calm this entire time. With the kind of strength he just showed, he could've fought back when he was captured in Uswad. Hell, he could've even killed that ponytailed bastard right then and there!

So that means... Marcus really did let himself get caught on purpose—just like he said—to help me wipe out Uncle Joel and the rest of the rebels. To solidify my position as the rightful head of my family!"

As these thoughts sank in, Cecilia felt a wave of shock wash over her—but alongside it, a deep sense of gratitude.

She and Marcus had only met twice. If she were being honest, they were barely more than acquaintances. Sure, he had saved her life once.

But even in a situation like this, he had still chosen—without hesitation—to stand by her and help her. Something deep inside Cecilia softened at that thought.

Marcus swept his gaze over the two men in front of him before letting out a cold, mocking laugh. "I thought you two were going to keep hiding like cowards forever. Didn't expect you to be this eager to die. Fine, I'll grant your wish!"

As soon as Marcus said that, another wave of unease rippled through the crowd. A lot of people still believed that he had only managed to cut off Axel's arm because he'd caught him off guard.

They figured that if it had been a fair fight, there was no way Marcus could've taken him down so easily. Hell, some even wondered if Marcus could have won at all.

And now, he was up against two fifth-generation cyborgs—both even stronger than the last one. Yet he still had the audacity to make such a bold declaration? That was either arrogance or sheer stupidity.

Hearing Marcus' words, the two cyborgs had mocking smiles. One of them sneered, "You Uswad people always preach about humility, but I don't see a shred of it in you. If you want us dead, then come on—show us what you've got!"

As the fifth-generation cyborg spoke, the surface of his body morphed in an instant, sprouting multiple massive gun barrels. A split second later, a hail of oversized bullets tore through the air, all aimed straight at Marcus.

These bullets packed a punch far beyond anything available on the market. Their power was on par with armor-piercing rounds—meaning that even if Marcus had been encased in tank-grade plating, it still wouldn't have been enough to withstand the onslaught.

Gasps filled the banquet hall as panic erupted among the guests. People scrambled for cover, afraid of getting caught in the crossfire.

But Marcus? He just let out a sneer. Instead of retreating, he lunged forward. Shadow Step propelled him through the barrage, his figure flickering like a mirage as he weaved through the storm of bullets.
