Grace was Benjamin's only daughter.

She had just turned 20 this year, a sophomore at Kingston University. A cheerful, lively, and energetic girl, full of youthful vigor.

Before hitting the two-decade mark, she had lived a life of privilege, growing up with every opportunity at her fingertips.

As the mayor's daughter, not only was she a top student, but she was also strikingly beautiful.

Her future had once been blindingly bright.

But then, in the blink of an eye, everything changed. A sudden fainting spell during a routine physical education class. That was the first sign.

She had just been hit with the diagnosis: acute myeloid leukemia, and it was already in the final, ferocious stage.

The odds of beating this disease were slim to none, and now, in its vicious endgame, survival was pretty much off the table.

When Benjamin and his wife got the news, it was like the sky came crashing down on them.

They were dumbfounded. How could their daughter, with all her kindness and love, be struck down by such a cruel fate?

Benjamin was the mayor, but even he was powerless against the cold, unfeeling reality of disease.

Facing his daughter's grueling battle with severe leukemia, he scoured every renowned hospital in Uswad. He even took her to Acoton.

But still, there was no cure to be found.

Now, her life was sustained only by repeated blood transfusions.

Benjamin, buried in his responsibilities as mayor, could only rely on his wife, Lilian Mitchell, to care for their dying daughter in Kingston.

The thought of losing Grace tore at his soul.

The guilt was unbearable. He blamed himself for not spending more time with her when he could.

"God," he cried, "I have lived honorably. I've done nothing to deserve this. Why are you punishing me? Why my daughter?"

The once unshakable mayor was crumbling. It was hard to imagine that this broken man was the same powerful figure who commanded respect and authority.

"Grace, I won't let you die. I will find a way to save you," he vowed through tears. He made call after call, reaching out to every connection he had. But the answer was always the same: there was no point in treating her anymore.

At this point, with his daughter's condition, even the doctors said it wasn't worth the fight anymore.

"Mr. Fitzgerald can offer some surgical advice for Robert. Can he actually cure leukemia?"

In a flicker of hope, Benjamin thought of Marcus.

But Edward was an orthopedic surgeon, so Benjamin wasn't holding his breath for a miracle.

But now, with his daughter hanging by a thread, he was down to clutching at straws.

With that thought, he scrambled to get in touch with Marcus, only to realize he didn't even have the guy's contact info.

Benjamin had no choice but to visit Marcus in person.

Using his position as mayor, he tracked down Marcus' address and headed straight to Bayside Villa.

Marcus had practiced through the night. The Timeless Warrior's Grimoire was nearly at the third level, just one step away.

The sun was blazing down today, and no sooner had he reached the villa than there was a knock echoing through the door.

"Is Mr. Fitzgerald at home? I'm Benjamin Mitchell, mayor of Solomia. I've come to visit you!"

Benjamin's voice, laced with politeness, filtered through from outside.

Marcus raised an eyebrow, feeling surprised.

They had only met once, briefly, at the International Invitational. How had Benjamin found him?

Curiosity got the better of Marcus, and he swung the door open. There stood Benjamin, looking haggard and worn thin, with his secretary, Amelia Morgan, by his side.

"What brings Mayor Benjamin here?" Marcus asked.

Benjamin offered a bitter smile, "I came today to ask for your help, Mr. Fitzgerald. May I speak with you further?"

"Come in," Marcus said, gesturing toward the courtyard. They stepped inside, but instead of the main house, Marcus led them to a pavilion. "What's on your mind, Mayor Benjamin? You can speak freely now."

Though they weren't close, Marcus had a favorable impression of Benjamin.

If it had been someone he thought little of, he would've slammed the door right in their face.

"My daughter's got leukemia, and the docs back in Kingston have given her three days tops. I'm at the end of my rope here, Mr. Fitzgerald. Can you save my little girl?

"I know your expertise is in orthopedic surgery. It's a long shot for you to tackle leukemia, but I'm fresh out of plays here. I've got just one daughter, and I can't stand to watch her go this young.

"If you can just buy her some time, even a month, I'll owe you big time!"

Benjamin's voice cracked with excitement and pain. His heart clenched at the thought of losing his only child.

"Which hospital is she in? What type of leukemia are we talking about?" Marcus asked, remaining calm.

"She's at Kingston Hospital," Benjamin replied immediately. "Acute myeloid leukemia, the late stages."

Marcus lit a cigarette and crossed his legs. "Treatment isn't out of the question. But Kingston is quite far, and I've been buried in my own affairs lately. I'm not sure I can spare the time to travel there."

The moment Marcus said those words, a glimmer of hope ignited in Benjamin's chest. Standing beside Benjamin, Amelia gave him a subtle wink.
