Marcus smirked, his expression full of sarcasm.

Back at Grace's house, he had taught Randy a hard lesson.

Randy carried far more weight than someone like Heath, who was just a distant relative.

And yet, Heath dared to act arrogant in front of him.

Marcus' words made Heath's face darken instantly.

While he took pride in being part of the Deleon family, being called out as a side branch member was a sore spot.

The Deleon family hierarchy was notoriously rigid.

Figures like Randy and Oliver were the epitome of the family's elite.

Oliver, especially, was regarded as the family's shining star, with the patriarch himself grooming him to be the next head of the family.

But Oliver's heart was set on a military career, leaving little interest in family affairs.

Even so, neither Oliver nor Randy was someone Heath could compare himself to.

It wasn't an exaggeration to say that if Randy so much as sneezed, Heath wouldn't dare to call it an annoyance.

And yet, here in Solomia, Heath strutted around like he owned the place, wearing his Deleon name like a crown.

"Kid, you've got some nerve, insulting our young master like that. Today, you're going to learn your lesson!"

One of Heath's bodyguards stepped forward, pulling a pistol from his waistband and aiming it at Marcus.

Gustavo's face lit up with glee. His smugness returned, and he sneered.

"Oh, look who's in trouble now. I told you not to mess with Mr. Deleon, but you wouldn't listen. What now? Think you're tougher than a gun?"

To Gustavo, Heath pulling out a gun was the ultimate show of power.

He imagined Marcus trembling in fear any second.

"Kneel and apologize to Mr. Deleon, or I'll put a bullet in your head!" the bodyguard barked, his tone leaving no room for negotiation.

Kiera's face turned pale with worry.

She knew Marcus was strong, but against a man with a gun, even he wouldn't stand a chance.

She instinctively moved to shield Marcus, ready to take the consequences herself.

But Marcus simply gestured for her to stay calm.

"Do you believe," Marcus said with a cold smile, "that I could kill you before you even pull the trigger?"

His words were met with a chorus of laughter.

"You must be out of your damn mind!" the bodyguard spat. "You think you're faster than a bullet? What are you, Superman?"

He aimed the gun at Marcus' forehead, his tone filled with disdain.

But the laughter hadn't died down when Marcus moved.

In a flash, his figure blurred, leaving afterimages as he closed the distance in the blink of an eye.

The bodyguard's face twisted in shock.

He tried to pull the trigger, but Marcus struck his wrist with a lightning-fast blow.

The sound of bones shattering filled the air.

The gun clattered to the ground as the bodyguard screamed in pain. Before he could recover, Marcus' hand clamped around his throat, cutting off his cries.

The man's eyes widened in terror. He couldn't fathom how Marcus had moved so fast.

And he wasn't the only one. Gustavo, Heath, and the remaining bodyguards stared in stunned silence, paralyzed by fear.

They were all ordinary people, and Marcus' strength was beyond their imagination.

"L-let me go..." the bodyguard croaked, his face contorted in agony.

Marcus didn't respond. Instead, he tightened his grip.

Another sickening crunch echoed as Marcus crushed the man's throat.

His lifeless body crumpled to the ground, eyes frozen in disbelief at his sudden demise.

Gustavo and Heath gasped audibly, their faces pale with shock.

The other bodyguards hesitated only briefly before drawing their guns, ready to fire.

But Marcus moved faster than they could react.

Within moments, he was upon them, striking with brutal precision.

The sounds of gunfire never came. Instead, the room echoed with the sickening thuds of bodies hitting the ground.

In mere seconds, Marcus had incapacitated the remaining guards.

Some lay groaning with broken bones; others were dead, their lives snuffed out in an instant.

Everything happened so quickly. Though it seemed complicated, it happened in just a blink of the eye.

The seamless, deadly efficiency left Gustavo and Heath dumbfounded.

Even Kiera, who knew Marcus was strong, covered her mouth in shock.

"These are the bodyguards you hired?" Marcus asked mockingly.

As he spoke, he began walking toward Heath.

Sweat beaded on Heath's forehead. His voice trembled as he tried to muster a warning. "I'm a member of the Deleon family! If you lay a hand on me, they won't let you live!"

Marcus smirked coldly. "I don't care if you're from the Deleon family or anywhere else. If you mess with my people, even your so-called untouchable family will suffer."

Before Heath could react, Marcus delivered a brutal kick to his groin.
