"The Cole family, I won't forget this!" Marcus said, his eyes sharp with resolve.

Back then, he killed Jordon and his grandson because they repaid kindness with betrayal.

He had treated their illness and accepted payment—a fair and voluntary exchange.

But Jordon and his grandson grew greedy, plotting to kill Marcus and take back the payment.

People like that didn't deserve to live in this world.

Yet the Cole family ignored all this. All they cared about was that one of their own had been killed by a Uswadian man.

And they were determined to seek revenge for their family.

"Remember it all you want. In the afterlife, you'll have no way to avenge yourself against the Cole family, so why not say something useful instead?" the silver-haired woman sneered.

As she spoke, she raised her hand, summoning a fierce gust of wind around Marcus.

The wind swirled violently, unleashing an immensely terrifying wind-elemental force.

What's more, glints of cold light flashed within the whirlwind, like a deadly grinder, aiming to tear Marcus to pieces.

Wind metahuman abilities!

Marcus immediately understood that the silver-haired woman was a wind-element metahuman.

Moreover, her mastery of wind-elemental power was overwhelming.

An ordinary person would have no way to resist; they'd be shredded to pieces by the storm!

But Marcus wasn't ordinary. Faced with the terrifying whirlwind, he threw a single punch.

His punch carried the power of Thunderfire Power, with Timeless Numinous Energy swirling around it.

In an instant, his attack collided with the storm.

A deafening rumble erupted.

With just one punch, the whirlwind shattered into nothingness.

The sight made the silver-haired woman's expression falter slightly.

Marcus' punch was far beyond what anyone at Ancient Martial Stage Two could possibly achieve.

Before she could react, Marcus took a step forward, his figure splitting into countless afterimages.

In the blink of an eye, he was in front of the silver-haired woman.

The silver-haired woman was horrified, and even the metahuman with heterochromatic eyes nearby was shocked by Marcus' speed.

The man quickly unleashed a burst of flame, trying to push Marcus back and protect the silver-haired woman.

But Marcus had already reached Ancient Martial Stage Six, far surpassing the level of a fourth-tier metahuman.

With a casual wave of his hand, Marcus dissipated the fire attack entirely.

At the same time, he threw a punch at the silver-haired woman, whose face turned pale.

She quickly summoned another whirlwind to block him.

The sound of the impact roared through the air.

The whirlwind was utterly destroyed by Marcus' punch.

Marcus' fist, unstoppable, landed directly on her body.

The silver-haired woman let out a scream as her body was blasted backward like a cannonball.

She slammed into a nearby boulder with such force that it cracked and shattered.

Her body trembled as she coughed up blood, collapsing weakly onto the ground.

With one punch, Marcus instantly defeated a fourth-tier metahuman.

Even in death, the silver-haired woman's eyes remained wide open, her expression frozen in terror.

Perhaps, even at the end of her life, she couldn't comprehend the sheer strength Marcus possessed.

"You—you're not at Ancient Martial Stage Two!"

The heterochromatic metahuman suddenly realized this, retreating while launching several flames in an attempt to threaten Marcus.

Marcus didn't even bother dodging. He stepped forward, completely ignoring the attacks, and swung another punch at the man.

The metahuman's face was filled with panic. Even before the fist connected, he could feel the terrifying destructive force emanating from it!

"No!" he shouted desperately, but it was useless. Marcus' unstoppable punch smashed directly onto his head.

A muffled bang echoed through the air.

The metahuman's head exploded on the spot, his unique eyes shattering with it. He was beyond dead.

The remaining cyborgs were stunned. They instantly realized they were no match for Marcus and desperately tried to flee.

Marcus didn't give them a chance. Without moving, bolts of lightning descended from the sky.

Each lightning strike was as powerful as a fourth-tier Wizard, posing a deadly threat to the cyborgs.

The sound of crackling echoed endlessly as the lightning reduced the cyborgs to pieces.

Their damaged internal mechanisms ignited quickly, engulfed in flames until they burned to ash.

Marcus wasted no time. With a wave of his hand, flames erupted from his palm.

The fire enveloped the scattered remains on the ground, burning them to ashes.

After finishing, he returned to his Aston Martin.

Jade was still curled up in the car, trembling with fear.

"Are you okay?" Marcus asked.
