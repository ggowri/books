Marcus had no interest in the choices others made.

He had warned them earlier, but they refused to listen.

Now, paying with their lives was simply the price of their arrogance.

With Ada by his side, Marcus traversed the vast underwater expanse, arriving at an ancient city submerged in the ocean's depths.

Ada was utterly dumbfounded. Her stunning face was filled with disbelief.

Marcus led her to the central altar of the city, using it as a gateway to transport them to the Nuer Tribe.

The tribe's chieftain, Raphael, along with his deputy Elliott and other high-ranking members, was already waiting.

As soon as Marcus arrived, their faces lit up with reverence. They bowed deeply to him, their attitudes filled with utmost respect.

This scene left Ada in shock once again.

She quickly noticed that the people of the Nuer Tribe were distinctly different from ordinary humans.

She guessed they were likely the legendary guardians of this underwater world.

According to the tales, the Nuer Tribe had protected this oceanic realm for thousands of years and were its true rulers.

Marcus had only entered this underwater world three days ago.

Yet here was this mysterious tribe, bowing down to him as if he were a deity.

Ada could hardly wrap her mind around it.

She looked at Marcus with a mix of astonishment and awe.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, is this your friend? My, what a beautiful lady!"

A young woman stepped forward, grasping Ada's hand warmly.

"This is Princess Nadia of the Nuer Tribe," Marcus introduced, his tone calm and composed.

Learning that the woman before her was a princess, Ada was taken aback.

She quickly offered a polite greeting.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, your taste is impeccable. This young lady is truly stunning. I like her already!"

Another young woman, Susan, approached and joined Nadia in fawning over Ada.

Instinctively, Ada could sense that both Nadia and Susan shared a close connection with Marcus.

She was well-acquainted with Marcus' personality. His reputation as a charmer who left trails of admirers in his wake was no secret.

What surprised her, however, was that in just three days, Marcus had won over not one, but two of the most beautiful women in the Nuer Tribe.

Ada found this both shocking and impressive.

She realized that not only was Marcus incredibly powerful, but his charm with women was far more potent than she had imagined.

In just three days, he had won the affection of the two most beautiful girls in the Nuer Tribe.

One was a princess, while the other clearly held an extraordinary status as well.

This truly shocked Ada.

"Hello, ladies. You're both absolutely gorgeous," she replied with a polite smile, though her mind was racing.

While the surface interaction appeared harmonious, there was an unmistakable undercurrent of rivalry between the three women.

Though not overtly hostile, each was keenly aware of the others, sizing up their respective positions in Marcus' life.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, the space-time gate is ready to be opened. Shall we proceed now?" Raphael asked respectfully.

Marcus gave a slight nod. "Let's begin."

Raphael immediately relayed the order to the tribe.

"I will accompany Mr. Fitzgerald into the space-time gate. If anything happens to the tribe, use the amulet to contact me. I will return and shut the gate immediately," Raphael instructed.

Elliott and the other leaders nodded in agreement. "Rest assured. We will follow your instructions to the letter," Elliott replied solemnly.

With his concerns alleviated, Raphael led Marcus and Ada to the tribe's central area.

From a bird's-eye view, this was the very heart of the Nuer Tribe.

Standing tall in this space was a colossal statue.

It depicted a towering, muscular man wielding a trident, his presence exuding unparalleled strength.

The figure bore an uncanny resemblance to the sea god often depicted in ancient myths.

Marcus couldn't help but recall a movie he'd once seen in a cinema called The Sea God. The resemblance was striking.

Under the statue's shadow, Raphael's demeanor turned solemn. He offered a devout prayer.

Then he began to chant an arcane incantation, the words incomprehensible yet brimming with power.

As he chanted, mysterious forces converged, forming intricate patterns of light in the air.

These forces materialized in the void, forming amulets that covered the entire sky.

These glowing symbols gathered over the statue, illuminating it with a divine brilliance.

The statue's enormous form suddenly began to glow.

A towering beam of light descended from above, enveloping the statue in a golden aura.

In the radiance, the statue appeared even more majestic, an overwhelming sacredness emanating from it.

Even those without faith in the figure felt an almost irresistible urge to kneel before it.

"Open!" Raphael's voice rang out with authority. At his command, a massive golden gate slowly materialized beneath the statue.

The gate creaked open, releasing waves of powerful spatial energy.

The force was so intense that Marcus and the others had to brace themselves to avoid being sucked into the vortex.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, this is the space-time gate. It will remain open for three days. After that, it will close automatically," Raphael explained.

"Anyone entering must either pass through to another world or return to this one within the time limit. Failing to do so will trap them in the space-time tunnel forever, lost for all eternity.

"So during this journey, we must ensure we return to the Nuer Tribe within three days."

Marcus nodded slightly. Without hesitation, he took Ada's soft hand and led her toward the gate.

Ada's nerves were palpable.

She had heard countless legends about the space-time gate but had never witnessed it herself.

Standing before this otherworldly portal, she was filled with a mix of curiosity and dread.

She had no idea what awaited her on the other side or whether this journey would bring fortune or disaster.

Still, there was only one way to find out.

As they stepped closer, the spatial forces enveloped them completely.

In an instant, their vision darkened, and a wave of dizziness overtook them.

When they opened their eyes again, they found themselves in a seemingly endless tunnel of time and space.

The tunnel stretched infinitely in both directions, surrounded by an abyss of darkness.

Violent storms raged in distant pockets, tearing through the void with devastating force.

One misstep could lead to being swept away by the chaos, never to return.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, those are space-time storms. We must tread carefully. No matter how powerful one is, getting caught in those will mean certain doom," Raphael warned from behind.

Marcus gave a faint nod and pressed forward, unwavering.
