Led by the president of Sync Group, the other three group presidents collectively bowed to Marcus, their expressions full of respect.

They had previously been executives at their respective companies, but after Marcus acquired the four groups, he appointed them as presidents.

To them, Marcus was a benefactor who had recognized their talents.

As the saying goes, a great talent finds its perfect patron—that's exactly how they felt.

"No need for formalities," Marcus said, waving his hand.

Marcus gestured to the four presidents.

Only then did the four presidents dare to stand up.

"Do you know why I called you here?" Marcus asked, lighting a cigarette.

Marcus lit a cigarette as he spoke.

"We await your guidance, Death," the Sync Group president replied.

A man in his forties, he looked somewhat weathered but gave off an air of shrewd efficiency.

"I acquired your four companies because I saw potential in you.

"I want you to bring your companies to the number one spot in their respective industries in Uswad within a year.

"Whoever achieves this goal will be handsomely rewarded," Marcus said, glancing at them.

Marcus swept his gaze over them as he spoke.

The four exchanged glances before replying in unison, "We won't let you down. We'll do our best to complete the task!"

Marcus nodded with satisfaction and added, "Starting today, your base salaries will be doubled."

Hearing this, the four presidents immediately lit up with delight.

They were already earning millions annually, and doubling their salaries meant an additional million dollars or more each year.

The incentive was incredibly enticing for them.

As a result, their motivation skyrocketed upon hearing Marcus's decision.

"Thank you!" they said in unison.

The four presidents expressed their gratitude together.

Marcus's expression turned serious. "As long as you perform well, not only will your salaries double, but your year-end bonuses will also be doubled!

"But let me make one thing clear—since you chose this role, you must fulfill your duties and excel in your jobs.

"Don't even think about betrayal. If I find anyone leaking secrets, committing fraud, or taking company technology to another firm, the punishment will be severe!"

Marcus balanced rewards with warnings.

After all, he had only recently met the four and didn't know them well.

Handing over such large companies required setting clear expectations.

Hearing this, the four nodded vigorously.

Even though it was their first meeting with Marcus, his commanding aura made it clear that he was a decisive and ruthless leader.

They knew that crossing him would have dire consequences.

"Also, without my permission, you are not to reveal my identity to anyone. Do you understand?" Marcus continued.

Marcus used the alias "Death" for these acquisitions to avoid drawing unnecessary attention.

"Understood!" they replied in unison.

The four presidents collectively agreed.

After discussing a few more matters with them, Marcus left the presidential suite at the Hilton Hotel.

As soon as he got into a taxi, Marcus received a call from Josie.

"Honey, what are you up to? I want to go shopping this afternoon to get some essentials and a necklace," Josie said.

Ever since her relationship with Marcus began, Josie had noticeably become more cheerful.

She also seemed to enjoy life and dressing up more.

"A necklace? Which one?" Marcus asked.

In his memory, Josie wasn't particularly fond of wearing jewelry.

"A limited edition from Chaumet. I've liked it for a long time, but I've been too busy with work to get it.

"The store assistant just posted that one came in today. I want to go check it out. Honey, can you come with me?"

Josie's voice was soft and sweet, making it hard to imagine she was a billionaire entrepreneur.

Chaumet, a top luxury jewelry brand, reflected Josie's excellent taste.

"Sure. Where are you now?" Marcus asked.

Marcus had no reason to refuse. With no plans for the afternoon, a relaxing shopping trip with Josie sounded appealing.

"I'm at the office. Where are you? I'll drive to pick you up," Josie replied.

"No need. Just tell me which mall, and we'll meet there," Marcus replied.

Since Marcus was already in a taxi, he didn't want to trouble Josie.

"Vertex Plaza."

"Alright, see you there!"

"See you there!"
