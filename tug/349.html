Catherine stood there, her heart pounding like a drum, as if she were caught in a waking dream.

Just a little while ago, she had been losing sleep over job hunting.

Now, she was suddenly the General Manager of Sync Group's Solomia branch.

Watching Catherine, the woman she had always looked down on, take the position of General Manager, Gloria's face twisted with despair and disbelief.

Back in high school, Gloria had always thought of herself as the queen bee, the one everyone admired.

She never saw Catherine as anything but ordinary, someone easy to mock and overshadow.

Even years later, when they reconnected, Gloria still believed she was leagues ahead.

With her uncle holding a high-ranking position at Sync Group, Gloria was confident she could use his influence.

She thought she could easily push Catherine out and land herself a managerial job.

It was a move most people could only dream of pulling off, and she had been certain of her success.

But all of that fell apart because Catherine knew Marcus.

Gloria's uncle lost his job, got arrested, and Gloria's chance at Sync Group vanished in the blink of an eye.

And the woman she had mocked? Catherine had taken her uncle's seat at the table.

Gloria's thoughts spiraled as the humiliation and helplessness consumed her.

Around them, the crowd was equally stunned, struggling to process what had just happened.

Gloria had spent the entire time flaunting her "connections" and trying to intimidate Catherine.

But this? This was real influence—no contest.

Catherine didn't just get a foot in the door; she went straight to the top. Who could even compete with that?

And all of it was because of Marcus.

If anyone was the true game-changer, it was him.

Under the assistant branch manager's guidance, Marcus escorted Catherine into the Solomia headquarters.

The two of them took a tour of the facilities and offices.

As a newly established branch, everything was cutting-edge and designed for maximum efficiency.

It made Cedars Villas feel ancient in comparison.

Catherine's General Manager office was on the 88th floor, the very top of the skyscraper.

From the massive floor-to-ceiling windows, she had a panoramic view of Solomia's skyline.

Standing there, her pulse quickened with excitement and disbelief.

She never in her wildest dreams thought she'd rise to such heights.

"Mr. Fitzgerald... thank you," she murmured, her voice trembling with emotion.

Once the assistant manager and staff had left, only Catherine and Marcus remained in the office.

Catherine tilted her head up at Marcus, her eyes sparkling with admiration.

Time and again, Marcus had pulled off the impossible in front of her.

To Catherine, Marcus wasn't just a man—he was like a knight in shining armor.

Whenever she was backed into a corner, he showed up and turned the tide, every single time.

"Thank me? For what? This is all you. You earned it," Marcus said with a grin.

His gaze lingered on Catherine's face, her sweet and pure expression lighting up the room.

Catherine had a charm that was understated yet irresistible.

She wasn't icy like Maxine Burnett, commanding like Josie Shelman, or bold like Jane Winchester.

But her softness and sincerity struck a chord that Marcus couldn't ignore.

Just then, Catherine's phone rang, snapping her out of her thoughts—it was her mom, Laura.

"Mom! I got the job! I'm the General Manager now. Mr. Fitzgerald arranged everything for me!" Catherine blurted out, sharing her excitement with her mom.

"That's amazing news, sweetheart! Make sure you thank Marcus properly. He's done so much for us—don't forget, he's the one who saved my life!

"And tonight, you better bring Marcus home. I'll cook up something special for both of you!"

Laura's voice was filled with emotion.

Laura's gratitude for Marcus was immeasurable.

She also secretly hoped that her daughter's relationship with Marcus might grow into something more.

Laura had seen how Catherine looked at him, though her shy daughter would never admit it.

As a mother, she had been quietly thinking of ways to help bring the two closer together.

"Got it, Mom! Leave it to me!" Catherine said, patting her chest with determination.

After hanging up, she turned to Marcus with a hopeful expression. "Mr. Fitzgerald, my mom wants to cook for us tonight. I already said yes. Will you come over for dinner?"

Marcus blinked, about to politely decline, but Catherine grabbed his arm, her voice soft and pleading. "Mr. Fitzgerald, if you don't say no, I'm taking that as a yes. And if you don't come, I'll have no way to explain it to my mom."

Faced with her wide, earnest eyes, Marcus couldn't bring himself to say no. "Alright, I'll come," he finally agreed.

The two spent a bit more time going over operations at the branch.

By 3:00 PM, they left the building and headed to Catherine's apartment.

By 3:30, they arrived at her home, where Laura had already prepared a feast that filled the dining table.

Catherine's father, Philip, was also there. Since Marcus' last visit, Philip had given up gambling and cut back significantly on drinking.

This was a relief to both Catherine and Laura, a silver lining they both cherished.

An hour later, the dinner wrapped up in a cheerful and warm atmosphere, and Catherine walked Marcus to the door.
