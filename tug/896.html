Maxine could hear the contempt and disdain in Cody's voice.

She immediately frowned and said to Cody, "If you don't let my boyfriend come along, I'm not going either.

"And let me tell you something! My boyfriend isn't inferior to anyone, so what's this nonsense about lowering Special Advisor's standards?"

Marcus felt a warmth spread through his heart at Maxine's words.

Brenden had given Maxine direct orders to meet with the Special Advisor.

The fact that Maxine had resorted to begging Cody showed how crucial this assignment was.

Yet when Cody refused to let Marcus join them, Maxine didn't hesitate to back out, standing firmly by Marcus' side.

Moreover, when Cody took subtle jabs at Marcus, Maxine jumped to his defense without a second thought.

It demonstrated how much Marcus meant to her.

Cody's brow furrowed as he asked Maxine, "You're seriously giving up the chance to meet the Special Advisor because of him?

"This opportunity doesn't come around often! You better think this through carefully. How do you plan to explain this to Mr. Wilde?

"And I just can't wrap my head around this. What makes this guy so unique that you'd throw everything away for him?"

After Cody finished speaking, several people in military uniforms behind him cast puzzled looks at Maxine.

They were equally unable to comprehend her current behavior.

"This is my business, and you don't need to worry about it," Maxine said firmly. "I'll find a way to explain everything to Mr. Wilde myself."

Maxine turned to Marcus and said, "Marcus, if they don't show us respect, we might as well leave."

As she spoke, Maxine unexpectedly took Marcus' hand in hers.

It was worth noting that this was the first time she had ever initiated holding Marcus' hand.

Marcus felt entirely surprised by this unexpected gesture.

"Stop right there!" a voice commanded.

Before Marcus and Maxine could take their leave, Cody's ice-cold voice rang out from behind them once again.

Maxine furrowed her brows and turned around, asking, "Is there something else?"

Cody's eyes were fixed intently on Maxine's hand holding Marcus', and with growing coldness in his heart, he couldn't help but say, "You mentioned earlier that he defeated Eldoria's cyborg, so he must be pretty strong. I want to test his skills myself!"

Cody's declaration caught the attention of the uniformed soldiers beside him, their faces lighting up with excitement.

Everyone knew Cody had claimed the top spot in combat training at Acoton Military Academy.

He wasn't just physically imposing; he had been trained in lethal combat techniques since childhood.

This expertise was crucial in earning him Acoton's highest honors.

To their surprise, Cody openly challenged this young man before them.

Truthfully, none of them had believed Maxine when she claimed Marcus had defeated the Eldorian cyborg.

They had only kept quiet about their doubts to save her face.

Cody was throwing the gauntlet at this young man right before them.

Marcus would accept the challenge if he possessed the skills Maxine had bragged about.

If he backed down, it would prove Maxine had been lying all along.

Maxine frowned at Cody and said, "We've got business to take care of, so we'll have to pass on your little sparring invitation. Sorry about that."

With that, Maxine grabbed Marcus' hand and started heading toward her police car.

Before they could leave, Cody's massive frame blocked their path.

Standing at six-foot-five with muscles rippling under his clothes, he cut an intimidating figure.

Although Marcus was a respectable six feet tall, he seemed almost diminutive next to Cody's dominating presence.

"You're not going anywhere unless you beat me first!"
