Although Travis didn't consciously release any aura upon his arrival, most of the people around him could still sense the immense power emanating from him, a clear sign of his mastery at Stage Nine.

Even Liam and Ada were visibly taken aback.

Liam spoke up first, his voice respectful, "Mr. Sullivan, it's good to see you."

He gave a slight bow, his expression betraying a quiet sense of admiration.

In the world of Ancient Martial Art, the experts commanded respect, and Travis, being the first to reach Ancient Martial Stage Nine, was no exception.

Travis, however, seemed more interested in Ada. His gaze landed on her with a grin. "Ms. Wakefield, I didn't expect to see you here. What a coincidence!"

He then turned back to Liam, his tone shifting to something more inquisitive. "From the reports I've gotten, the space-time gate at Morvalis Ice Wall was supposed to open yesterday. If it was scheduled to stay open for three days, that means there's still a day left."

He raised an eyebrow, curious. "So, shouldn't you all be inside the gate, exploring its secrets? Why are you still up here on the surface waiting?"

Liam gave a small laugh. "Mr. Sullivan, you might not be aware, but the dangers within the space-time gate are far greater than we anticipated. None of us have entered to explore yet."

He gestured to Marcus and Ada. "Only Mr. Fitzgerald and Ms. Wakefield have just returned from their journey inside."

At the sound of Liam's voice, Travis' gaze shifted toward Marcus.

It was then that he noticed the young man standing next to Ada.

Travis immediately released his spiritual powers, probing Marcus' cultivation level, trying to discern his strength.

But after a moment, he couldn't get a clear read. The power radiating from Marcus remained elusive, impossible to penetrate.

The only thing Travis could confirm was that Marcus was in his early twenties—far too young to have lived through multiple lifetimes.

This realization sparked a sense of condescension in him.

In the world of Ancient Martial Art in Uswad, anyone who hadn't lived through samsara was typically considered to lack the experience necessary for serious achievement.

In Travis' view, strength was largely a matter of time—most people built their power through years of grinding, accumulating it slowly.

Few, if any, could reach the highest levels quickly.

"Tell me, young one," Travis spoke, his voice laced with a faint air of superiority. "What did you find inside the space-time gate?"

His eyes looked down at Marcus, his tone dripping with an unmistakable sense of disdain, and there was a commanding note to his words, as if expecting an answer without question.

Marcus raised an eyebrow, his expression unfazed. "I don't know you, so why should I answer your question? If you're so curious about the space-time gate, you can go in and find out for yourself."

It wasn't that Marcus refused to answer—it was that Travis had shown him no respect.

To come at him with such a condescending attitude, demanding answers like an arrogant superior, was enough to make anyone want to turn away.

And besides, the dangers of the Morvalis Ice Wall's space-time gate were well-known. Every person who had ventured inside did so knowing it was a gamble with their life, a dangerous opportunity that could be lost in the blink of an eye.

Why would anyone casually share their experiences from such a perilous journey with someone like Travis?

But before Marcus could finish speaking, Travis' expression darkened, his brow furrowing in irritation. He spoke coldly, "With my status, it's a privilege for you to be asked questions by me. You really don't know how lucky you are."

He gestured toward the group around them, his voice laced with arrogance. "Everyone here would consider it an honor to be in my circle."

Marcus gave a sharp, mocking laugh. "Sorry, I'm not interested in joining your circle."

Without another word, he turned to Ada. "Let's go."

Ada nodded quietly, and as Marcus reached for her hand, they prepared to leave the scene.
