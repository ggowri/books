Marcus was taken aback, wondering how Morgana had obtained his phone number.

So, he asked, "Sure, I've got nothing pressing at home anyway. But I'm really curious. How did you get my phone number? Did Nicole give it to you?"

Marcus couldn't think of anyone else besides Nicole.

Morgana replied, "No. I did ask Nicole for your phone number, but she said she wanted to protect your privacy and refused to tell me."

Upon hearing her words, Marcus inwardly commended Nicole.

She's matured and become far more considerate.

Protecting my privacy showed real growth.

"Then how did you get it?" Marcus persisted, his curiosity piqued.

"Don't you remember? When your sister started school, the guardian's contact number on file was yours. I'm her homeroom teacher," Morgana explained, a touch of pride in her voice.

Marcus was stunned. "Aren't you misusing your position?"

Morgana chuckled, "Well, I'm the homeroom teacher. With a meager salary, I've got to have some privileges, right?"

Marcus paused briefly before saying, "Alright, I'm just kidding. So, what would you like to eat?"

He'd already eaten.

Normally, he'd decline dinner invitations.

But Morgana was an exception.

When Nicole was on the verge of being carted off by Sebastien and his goons, Morgana had fearlessly stepped in and shielded her.

For that act of courage and kindness, Marcus felt compelled to have this meal with her.

Despite some flaws, her values were sound, and he admired her straightforward sense of right and wrong and her strong moral compass.

"Are you free? If so, come to my place. There's a pizzeria downstairs, and their food is really delicious," Morgana suggested.

Marcus agreed, "No problem. Where do you live? Send me the location."

Morgana asked, "How?"

"Via WhatsApp," Marcus replied.

Morgana complained, "I messaged you before, but you never replied."

Marcus felt a twinge of awkwardness.

He pulled over and retrieved his phone.

Then, he said to Morgana, "Send it now."

Shortly, a new message arrived.

Marcus opened it and saw that the sender's nickname was "Princess Morgana".

He knew it must be Morgana.

She sent a cute emoji, followed by the location.

Marcus checked the location and found that Morgana lived in an old part of town, in an aging residential area.

But it was well-equipped, bustling with activity.

The pizzeria, likely open for two or three decades, was a local institution.

Marcus drove to the pizzeria.

In less than 15 minutes, he arrived.

Upon entering, he spotted Morgana by the window, waving.

She'd changed into a light beige dress that complemented her beauty, making her stand out.

"I didn't expect you to be so quick," Morgana exclaimed, a smile spreading across her pretty face.

In casual wear, Morgana had shed the teacher's sternness, revealing a youthful, girlish charm.

Marcus thought it made sense. After all, she looked around 24 or 25.

She was still young, perhaps fresh out of university.

Yet, as a teacher, she had to project authority. Otherwise, students wouldn't listen.

"You're my sister's teacher. I had to hurry. Otherwise, what if you give her a hard time?" Marcus joked.

Morgana glared at Marcus and asked, "Do I seem that small-minded?

"By the way, what would you like to eat? Their pizza is delicious. The place isn't fancy, but you'll be hooked after one bite."

Morgana spoke highly of the pizzeria.

Marcus had already caught the pizza's rich aroma upon entering.

"You're familiar with this place. Order whatever you like. I'm sure I'll love it, too," Marcus said.

Upon hearing this, Morgana didn't hold back and immediately started scribbling on the menu.

Before long, a waiter brought out a lot of clean and appetizing food.

Morgana and Marcus began to dig in.

Over the meal, they chatted about many things. He learned about Nicole's school life.

Since starting university, she had matured, becoming a class leader with solid grades.

Her encounter with Sebastien at the pub was part of a pre-holiday class gathering. After dinner, they went there to have fun.

During the conversation, Marcus realized Morgana wasn't as serious as she'd seemed. She was lively and full of energy.

Clearly, her pretense was a professional requirement.

As a teacher, Morgana had to project an aura of authority.

But in her personal life, she was almost no different from an ordinary young woman of twenty-four or twenty-five.

"Ouch!"

Just after they finished eating, a pained expression crossed Morgana's face.

She clutched her stomach, her complexion turning pale.

"What's wrong?" Marcus asked.

Morgana waved her hand and replied, "It seems to be a stomach ache. I probably ate something too spicy just now."

Marcus quickly fetched a cup of warm water for her and asked her to drink it slowly.

However, her stomach ache didn't ease at all. Instead, it became more severe.

Marcus said, "You're in bad shape. I'll take you home right now."

They had planned to visit a coffee shop and sit for a while, but given Morgana's condition, they couldn't go anymore.

She nodded slightly and cast an apologetic glance at him.

Next, Marcus helped Morgana and entered the residential complex.

Luckily, the restaurant they had chosen was very close to her home. Otherwise, it would have been troublesome for her to get home.

Her place was on the sixth floor of a walk-up building, so going upstairs was very tiring.

She was now sweating profusely from the pain, showing symptoms of a stomach spasm.

In her current state, climbing the stairs was a daunting task.

Without asking for Morgana's permission, Marcus directly carried her on his back.

She let out a startled yelp and asked, "Marcus, what are you doing?"

Feeling the warmth from his back, she was embarrassed.

This was the first time she had been in such close contact with a strange man.

And it was no longer considered close contact, but zero-distance contact.

"Carrying you upstairs. What else?" Marcus replied.

Before Morgana could say anything more, he started climbing.

As she felt his body temperature, her heart raced, and a sense of warmth filled her.

Morgana had been working in this strange city for several years. Besides a few female friends, she had never received such care from a man.

Some men were only interested in her beauty and had ulterior motives.

But Morgana could tell that Marcus was different from them.

He genuinely wanted to help her.
