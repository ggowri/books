As the scene unfolded, Pablo, Arabella, and the surrounding crowd exchanged smirks of disbelief and mockery.

Arabella couldn't resist sneering, her tone dripping with sarcasm. "Look at this guy! He's really committing to the act. Honestly, his performance deserves an award—pretending to call someone important? Hilarious!"

She turned to Pablo, clearly amused. "Mr. Ashford, this is going to be a good show. Let's see how far he's willing to take this!"

Pablo leaned back, his expression smug. "Exactly. There's no need to rush. Let's see what kind of so-called 'big shot' he thinks he can pull in."

On the other end of the call, a strong and commanding voice answered almost immediately.

"This is Julian Ashford. Who's speaking?"

Marcus hadn't initially had Julian's number.

He'd only found it on the business card of the colonel who had handed him the commendation and medal a few days prior.

In the corner of the card, written in faint, almost imperceptible numbers, was Julian Ashford's personal line—a subtle but deliberate move.

For someone of Julian's rank and stature, such a number was undoubtedly classified and rarely shared.

That was why Julian had done this.

"Commander Ashford, this is Marcus Fitzgerald. We met recently in Solomia," Marcus began, his tone respectful yet direct.

Considering Julian's age—nearly eighty—Marcus addressed him with the utmost respect, not only as a senior official but as an elder.

It's the common courtesy.

There was a brief pause before Julian's voice warmed with recognition. "Ah, Marcus! What a pleasant surprise! What's the occasion for this call? Don't tell me you've finally decided to join Uswad's military?"

As a decorated war veteran and an influential figure, Julian had always valued capable individuals, especially those with strong combat skills.

He had personally trained numerous high-ranking officers for Uswad's military over the years.

After the assassination attempt in Solomia, Julian had been thoroughly impressed by Marcus' extraordinary skills.

Once he returned to Kingston, Julian couldn't get the thought of recruiting Marcus out of his mind.

With the talents Marcus displayed that day, a little guidance and mentoring would easily mold him into a formidable force within the military.

So when Julian received Marcus' call, he couldn't help but feel hopeful, thinking Marcus might finally agree to join Uswad's armed forces.

Marcus' voice remained steady. "Commander Ashford, I'm honored by the offer, but I haven't changed my mind about joining the military. That's not the reason for my call today."

Julian's tone grew serious, tinged with curiosity. "Oh? Then what's on your mind, Marcus? Go ahead, I'm listening."

Marcus didn't hesitate. "It's about Director Pablo Ashford."

Julian paused for a moment before responding, his voice firm. "Pablo? What about him? If he's done something wrong, rest assured, Marcus, I won't tolerate it. Even if he's my son, justice comes first."

Encouraged by Julian's words, Marcus began detailing everything.

He laid out how Pablo had fabricated evidence, abused his authority, and orchestrated the wrongful arrest of Benjamin.

"This... this son of mine has done such disgraceful things?" Julian's voice trembled, a mix of anger and disbelief seeping through.

Though Julian still held considerable power, his advancing years had led him to delegate much of his responsibilities, leaving him unaware of Pablo's covert actions.

Marcus explained that Benjamin's arrest had been carried out in secret, bypassing any oversight from Julian. It was a calculated, underhanded move designed to avoid detection.

"It's all true, Commander," Marcus affirmed. "I have evidence to back every word."

"Hold on for a moment, young man. I'll be there shortly," came Julian's steady, authoritative voice.

Marcus finally allowed himself to relax.

Julian was clearly a principled man, someone who wouldn't turn a blind eye to wrongdoing—even if it involved his own son.

Hanging up, Marcus returned to Grace's side.

Grace, however, looked anything but calm. Her hands trembled slightly as she held onto Marcus, her eyes betraying her growing unease.

She knew Pablo Ashford wasn't someone to be trifled with.

Even if Marcus had connections to the Ortega family, Pablo could still strike without hesitation.

It wasn't hard to understand why.

Political families wielded power that far surpassed that of business dynasties.

The Ortega family might have Battle God Oliver, a towering figure in Uswad's military, but that influence was limited to one arena.

The Ashford family, on the other hand, had Julian Ashford, a senior statesman, and several high-ranking officials in both political and military sectors.

The Ortega family might be richer than the Ashfords.

But in terms of power, the Ashfords left the Ortegas in the dust.

This thought made Grace's anxiety rise. She clutched Marcus' arm tightly, her face pale with worry.

"Well, who did you call? Where's this person you're supposedly bringing in?"

Pablo's sneer widened as Marcus stepped closer.

Arabella didn't miss her chance to pile on. "I think I overheard him pretending to call Commander Ashford. It's almost impressive how far he's willing to go. Does he really believe someone like him would know Commander Ashford?"

She laughed, shaking her head. "Honestly, Mr. Ashford, why waste time? Let's just arrest him already and put an end to this ridiculous charade."

Pablo kept his gaze fixed on Marcus, the amusement in his eyes making his intentions clear. "Let's humor him a little longer. I want to see what else he's got up his sleeve."

Around them, murmurs rippled through the crowd.

"This guy has no clue what he's doing. Doesn't he know Commander Ashford is Pablo's father? If he's going to fake a connection, at least pick someone unrelated!" someone whispered, shaking their heads.

"Seriously! He probably doesn't even realize the connection. People at his level wouldn't have access to that kind of information."

Laughter rippled through the group, their eyes filled with anticipation for the moment Marcus would be thoroughly humiliated.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, are you sure you know Commander Ashford?" Grace whispered, her voice barely above a tremble.

Marcus gave her a reassuring nod. "Yes, just give it a few more minutes. He'll be here, and your father's name will be cleared."

Grace's heart swelled with cautious hope, but doubt lingered.

Could Marcus truly know someone as powerful as Julian Ashford?

Ten minutes ticked by slowly, and still, nothing had changed in the room.

Arabella's smirk grew more pronounced as she turned to Pablo. "Mr. Ashford, it's been ten minutes. If something was going to happen, it would've happened by now. He's just bluffing, like I said. Let's arrest him already and be done with it."

Pablo checked the time, his expression darkening as he nodded in agreement. "Guards, take him away."

"Stop right there!"

A booming, commanding voice rang out from the entrance, freezing everyone in their tracks.
