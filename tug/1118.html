"Emotionless Gorge, located in the Kinton Mountains," Gertie replied.

"Emotionless Gorge? Your training place is actually called that? Are you emotionless now?" Marcus couldn't help but ask.

Gertie's cheeks flushed as she said, "I didn't name it, my aunt did. It has nothing to do with me. Stop asking, will you?"

Marcus nodded with a smile and said, "So, does that mean your aunt is the emotionless one?"

Gertie couldn't help but sigh and said, "My aunt... was once hurt by someone she loved, so she named this gorge Emotionless Gorge."

"She was hurt by that man and never got married. She hates men the most. What a pitiful woman."

For some reason, through Gertie's description, Marcus could picture what Gertie's aunt looked like.

He always felt that women hurt by love, though pitiful, were often eccentric, even to the point of being sharp-tongued.

Therefore, he really hoped he wouldn't encounter her this time.

After getting the exact location from Gertie, Marcus took her and used teleport.

In just a few seconds, the two arrived at the Emotionless Gorge deep in the Kinton Mountains.

The area was lush with vegetation, surrounded by towering mountains whose peaks were covered in snow year-round, resembling an isolated paradise.

"Why do you have the ability of teleport?"

Gertie looked at how she had traveled from Solomia to Emotionless Gorge in such a short time, her face full of shock, unable to believe her eyes.

"Don't ask. Even if I told you, you wouldn't understand," Marcus said casually, unwilling to reveal too many of his secrets to Gertie.

It wasn't that he didn't trust her, but the fewer people knew about such secrets, the better.

Seeing that Marcus was unwilling to tell, Gertie had no choice but to drop it.

Next, under Gertie's guidance, the two arrived outside an ancient courtyard.

This courtyard carried Uswad's ancient architectural style, giving one the feeling of being instantly transported back in time.

"Gertie, you're back!"

Before the two even entered the courtyard, a girl around seventeen or eighteen ran over to greet Gertie.

Noticing someone there, Gertie looked a bit flustered and said, "Yeah, I'm back."

This girl was from her clan and rarely appeared in Emotionless Gorge, so it was unexpected to meet her here today.

"Who is this?" The girl's gaze instantly fell on Marcus, asking with curiosity.

Emotionless Gorge had a rule that forbade men from entering.

That girl always saw Gertie as someone who had always strictly followed this rule.

Even though her aunt had already given Emotionless Gorge to Gertie, Gertie had never brought any man here. What Gertie had done today left her very puzzled.

"He is my friend. I got injured when I was outside, so he was here to help me heal," Gertie explained.

The girl nodded and then said, "I won't disturb you two. Be careful not to let anyone else find out, especially Ms. Minerva Leah."

The "Ms. Minerva Leah" mentioned was Gertie's aunt.

"Don't worry, I'll be careful..." Gertie replied somewhat awkwardly.

Clearly, she brought Marcus back to heal her injuries, but it felt like sneaking around, making her feel embarrassed in front of Marcus.

"Sorry about that," Gertie said to Marcus, a bit embarrassed.

Marcus raised an eyebrow and asked, "Isn't this your yard? Why so cautious?"

Gertie had no choice but to explain the rules of Emotionless Gorge to Marcus.

Marcus raised an eyebrow, finding it quite bizarre, and casually asked, "So if your aunt finds me, am I doomed?"

Gertie said stiffly, "Don't worry, if that happens, I'll protect you. You won't get hurt."

Next, the two entered a room in the courtyard.

The room wasn't large, but it was elegantly decorated, exuding a faint girlish fragrance.

"This is the room I usually stay in. No one comes here. It's very quiet. You can help me heal now," Gertie said to Marcus.

"Alright, sit cross-legged on the bed," Marcus nodded.

"This time, it's just for healing... Promise me, don't have any other thoughts."

Gertie seemed to think of something and couldn't help but remind Marcus.

Marcus raised an eyebrow, his gaze brazenly sweeping over Gertie, lingering a moment longer on her chest, and said, "Don't worry, I definitely won't have other thoughts, but I can't make sure that you can do the same."
