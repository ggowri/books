To Marcus, this matter was just a trivial interruption.

However, he felt that since the Hudson family took action against him, it was time to repay them in kind.

Upon arriving at the restaurant, Marcus immediately spotted Maxine sitting by the window.

Today, Maxine had shed her police uniform for a light-colored dress. Gone was the previous tough, independent look; instead, she exuded a more graceful, ladylike charm.

Marcus sat down and couldn't help but keep his eyes on her.

Maxine, feeling uneasy under his gaze, asked, "Why are you staring at me so much? Is there something on my face?"

Marcus shook his head. "No. I just found out you look different today. You are very beautiful tonight."

Maxine smiled, teasing him. "So, are you saying I wasn't beautiful before?"

Marcus chuckled. "You've always been beautiful. I just didn't dare compliment you before. I was afraid you'd think I was a creep."

Maxine rolled her eyes. The incident at Kingston Airport had indeed been awkward.

Soon, the table was filled with an array of exquisite dishes.

Maxine poured Marcus a glass of red wine and smiled, "Cheers to you for everything."

They clinked glasses and drank in one go.

Marcus asked, "How's Edward doing?"

At the mention of Edward, Maxine's eyes brightened again. "The surgery was a success. Professor Cooper wants to thank you properly, but you left before he could. I'm curious. Where did you learn your medical skills?"

Ever since Robert successfully performed Edward's surgery, Maxine had begun to regard Marcus with a new level of respect.

"I learned that from my elders," Marcus replied casually.

Maxine rolled her eyes, thinking he didn't want to tell her, so she didn't press further.

Marcus was becoming more and more mysterious to her.

The restaurant had an elegant, quiet ambiance, with soothing background music that created a relaxed atmosphere.

Maxine was noticeably more talkative than before. That night, she wasn't as cold as she had been in the past.

Marcus had always had a good impression of this young policewoman. He felt she was a bit aloof but had a strong sense of justice.

Just then, a group of men in suits entered the restaurant.

They all exuded a commanding aura, and at their head was an elderly man with graying hair. They walked over to Marcus and Maxine's table.

When Maxine saw the elderly man, a cold light flashed in her eyes. "What are you doing here? Why have you been following me?"

The elderly man bowed respectfully to Maxine. "Ms. Burnett, please don't take offense. The head of the family sent us to escort you home."

Maxine's face hardened. "Tell my father I'm not going back. You can leave now. Don't disturb my meal!"

The elderly man remained bowed, his expression exceedingly polite. "Ms. Burnett, this is Mr. Burnett's command. I can't disobey. Please, make it easier for us."

Maxine slammed the table and stood up. "What? Are you trying to drag me away by force?"

The elderly man answered, "Mr. Burnett said if you do not cooperate, we may have to use certain... measures."

There was a clear threat in his words.

Maxine shouted, "Get lost! Leave now, or I won't go easy on you!"

The elderly man straightened up and glanced at Marcus. "Ms. Burnett, are you refusing to go back with us because of this guy?"

Maxine's voice was cold. "It has nothing to do with him. I'll count to three. If you're not gone by then, you'll regret it!"

The elderly man laughed and looked Marcus over once more. "Ms. Burnett is not someone you can dream of. I advise you to stay away from her. The Burnett family is far beyond your reach."

Marcus raised an eyebrow and asked, "When did I ever say I wanted to reach the Burnett family?"

The elderly man sneered. "Don't play dumb with me. You're getting close to Ms. Burnett. Aren't you trying to latch on to the Burnett family?"

Maxine immediately interrupted, "Marcus is my friend. Show some respect, or I'll make you regret this!"

But the elderly man wasn't moved. He waved his hand, and the group of men behind him immediately advanced toward Maxine.

Maxine swiftly swept her leg and knocked two burly men to the ground.

The others rushed forward, all highly trained. Even though Maxine was skilled, she was no match for them.

Just as the men were about to overpower her, Marcus stepped in.

In just two punches, he took down two of the men, and the rest immediately turned their attention to him.
