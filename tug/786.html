As soon as Marcus and Ada stepped out, Liam and the others immediately gathered around them.

Liam wore a broad grin and said, "Welcome back, Mr. Fitzgerald, victorious as ever!"

Without missing a beat, Liam bowed deeply to Marcus.

After all, Liam was the leader of the Swords Sect, one of the three most powerful sects in the world of Ancient Martial Art in Uswad.

His status in that world was as towering as a skyscraper—undeniably commanding.

It was well known that during Marcus' absence, Liam had been putting on airs.

Many of the younger generation in the world of Ancient Martial Art had sought his guidance, but he'd brushed them off with an aloofness that made him seem almost untouchable.

He acted like a sage who had transcended the petty concerns of the world, clearly above everyone around him.

Yet now, seeing Marcus walk out, his demeanor shifted dramatically.

The obsequiousness he displayed in Marcus' presence was striking—he looked almost like a dog wagging its tail, eager to please.

The onlookers couldn't help but feel a mixture of awe and wonder.

It was a sharp reminder of the power of true strength.

"No need for such formalities, Mr. Sky," Marcus said, waving off the gesture.

Liam remained bent at the waist, his posture still one of submission, and asked, "If I may, Mr. Fitzgerald, how did your trip through the space-time gate go? Any issues?"

"It was... mostly smooth," Marcus replied, his tone nonchalant.

But for some reason, to Liam's trained eyes, there was something subtly different about Marcus.

Compared to before his descent into the underwater world, Marcus seemed even stronger now.

Though Marcus didn't exert any aura or presence, just standing there, casually at ease, he radiated a power that made Liam's chest tighten, an inexplicable unease washing over him.

He recognized the feeling—a primal fear deep in his soul, something only a person would feel when standing before someone far, far stronger than themselves.

"Mr. Sky, it's good to see you and all our fellow martial artists in good health!"

At that moment, a voice rang out from the distance, carrying through the air like a sharp whistle.

Many turned their gazes toward the source of the sound.

A man, appearing to be in his early thirties, floated effortlessly in midair, gliding toward them.

He descended swiftly, landing with ease near Marcus, Liam, and Ada.

Even before he spoke or made any move, the mere presence of this man radiated an undeniable pressure, a subtle force that weighed heavily on those nearby.

The crowd's reaction was immediate. Surprise flickered across many faces.

One person murmured, "Travis... Travis is here?"

"Yeah, I heard he was locked away in seclusion, trying to break into Ancient Martial Stage Nine. I didn't think he'd make it in time for this exploration of the Ice Wall's space-time gate, but here he is, right at the end!"

"Look at him... His aura. It seems he's already reached Stage Nine. That means his retreat was a complete success."

"Once he hits Stage Nine, Travis will be among the top experts in the world of Ancient Martial Art of Uswad."

"He's unstoppable. Despite being a lone wanderer with no sect or master, ever since Travis arrived in the world of Ancient Martial Art, he's been on a meteoric rise. At just 35 years old, he's already made it to Stage Nine."

"He's got serious potential—he could very well step into the Return Essence stage before long!"

As Travis Sullivan landed, the crowd buzzed with excitement and speculation.

Travis was a unique figure in the world of Ancient Martial Art.

Without a sect or a faction to back him, he was a rogue cultivator.

But his innate talent was so extraordinary that his progress seemed almost unnatural.

He'd surpassed the top disciples of most major sects, leaving them in his dust, his rise a testament to raw, untapped potential.

In the world of Ancient Martial Art, the only person to have broken through to Ancient Martial Stage Nine before Travis was Alonzo.

Alonzo was an ancient being who had lived through several cycles of samsara, making his breakthrough more a product of time than sheer talent.

Over decades, even centuries, he had chipped away at the barriers of cultivation, and finally, at long last, reached that elusive and coveted state.

But Travis was different.

He hadn't lived through multiple lifetimes. Yet, just like Alonzo, he had managed to reach Ancient Martial Stage Nine.

The comparison between their abilities spoke volumes about their respective innate talents.

Travis' potential, given enough time, was boundless.
