A low murmur of conversations rippled through the crowd.

Eventually, all eyes landed on the stunning woman standing beside Miles.

That woman was none other than Selena, Chloe's biggest competition.

Right now, Selena was practically draped over Miles, her posture soft, almost inviting.

She wasn't just standing with him—she was making a statement.

The moment someone pointed it out, the realization settled over the crowd like an unspoken agreement.

Yeah, that checked out.

They'd all been in the entertainment industry long enough to know how things really worked.

Chloe had the talent, the voice, the looks—hell, she outshone Selena in every category.

But Chloe played by the rules. No backroom deals and no powerful patrons pulled strings for her.

Selena, on the other hand, had Miles, VP of Royal Entertainment, in her corner. And that made all the difference.

If Miles had already decided she was getting that Best Female Singer award, then it was a done deal. Chloe could be the hottest star of the year, and it still wouldn't matter.

People sighed, shaking their heads.

It didn't sit right with them, but what could they do?

This was supposed to be Chloe's big moment—the night that took her from rising star to industry powerhouse.

If she won, her career would skyrocket. She'd be untouchable.

If she lost, well... who knew when another chance like this would come around?

Meanwhile, another figure had caught some attention—Marcus.

Walking beside Chloe, he looked about her age, with sharp features and an easy confidence.

That was enough to get people looking.

But in the next second, they lost interest.

He wasn't anyone they recognized, and judging by his simple but polished outfit, he was probably just her assistant.

Most people barely spared Marcus a glance before moving on.

Someone like Chloe always had a flock of assistants buzzing around her.

There was no reason to waste a second thought on just another one of them.

Chloe's entrance sent a ripple through the event, drawing plenty of eyes—including those of Miles and Selena.

The moment he spotted Chloe, attending this event safely, Miles' expression tightened.

He had sent people to "talk" to her, to make sure she knew her place.

But those guys? Not a word from them.

And yet, here Chloe was, walking in like nothing had happened. Something about this didn't sit right with Miles.

"Mr. Donovan... I'm getting that award, right?"

Selena's voice was light, almost playful, but there was an edge to it.

The way she leaned in, her breath warm against his ear, made it hard to say no.

"Relax." Miles gave her hand a squeeze, his grip firm. "I told you, no one's taking it from you. I sent a message to Chloe—she's smart enough to get the hint. And if not, well... I'll make sure she's got nowhere left to go."

A slow smile crept across his face as he traced small circles on the back of her hand.

At fifty-something, Miles had two major vices—money and women.

And Selena checked all the right boxes. Young, gorgeous, and just the right amount of dangerous.

Not to mention, she knew exactly how to keep him happy in bed.

Last night had been proof of that.

No way was he letting her go empty-handed.

"You spoil me, Mr. Donovan..." Selena whispered, eyes dancing with something sly. "Don't lock the door tonight, okay? We still have plenty to... discuss."

And there was a faint smile displaying on her face.

She didn't need to try hard to be irresistible—it came naturally.

Every glance, every touch was designed to pull a man right under her spell.
