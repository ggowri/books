Ophelia and Wesley shared a look of shock and dismay as they witnessed the unexpected scene unfolding before them.

How could Osborne be Clarissa’s savior?

Ophelia’s thoughts raced with disbelief and a tinge of jealousy.

Watching Clarissa, the aloof and unapproachable figure, now clinging to Osborne in tears, she felt a surge of bitterness.

Clarissa was the one she’d been trying so hard to impress.

Osborne, on the other hand, was the ex-husband she looked down upon, the failure for whom she initiated divorce.

They seemed worlds apart, yet here they were, embracing each other, with Clarissa taking the lead.

Suddenly, a cough interrupted the emotional moment, causing Clarissa to startle like a frightened rabbit, quickly pulling away from Osborne’s embrace.

Turning towards the source of the sound, she lowered her head, resembling a child caught in mischief.

A middle-aged woman scolded, her expression stern, catching everyone off guard, “Clarissa, you are being too impulsive.”

She carried a serious demeanor, a familiar figure often seen nearby, tending to her flowers with a watering can.

The trio had mistaken her for a gardener. But now, witnessing her scold Clarissa like a child left them all stunned.

“Aunt Donna, I’m sorry. I was just overwhelmed meeting my savior at last. I’ll be more composed from now on,” Clarissa addressed the middle-aged woman.

Donna nodded, her eyes shifting towards Osborne, a mix of scrutiny and wariness in her gaze.

At that moment, Osborne felt a surge of danger and a jolt from Satan’s eye, much like when he had first encountered Isa.

He realized that this middle-aged woman was the true force to reckon with.

Even Clarissa seemed respectful and somewhat fearful of her.

He pondered many thoughts but maintained a calm facade, meeting the middle-aged woman’s gaze steadily.

“Mr. Augustine, right? Thank you for saving our Clarissa fifteen years ago. I’m Donna Radcliffe, Clarissa’s aunt. The Radcliffe family never hesitates to repay a debt of gratitude.”

Donna’s words confirmed Osborne’s suspicions.

Her words left Ophelia and Wesley gazing enviously at Osborne, with Wesley’s jealousy reaching a boiling point, his eyes bloodshot with envy, and his face contorted.

Why wasn’t it me who saved Clarissa? he thought. If I had been her savior, the Radcliffe family would have granted me any wish I desired. My life would have soared to new heights.

Maybe I would even win Clarissa’s heart.

Wesley glared at Osborne as if his gaze could inflict harm.

Donna continued, “I don’t know what you want. Whatever the Radcliffe family can provide, we will fulfill. Money? Power? Or perhaps a beauty? Just name it, and we won’t owe you.”

Osborne raised an eyebrow, calmly stating, “You misunderstand. Though I saved Ms. Radcliffe, I never sought any repayment.”

Donna furrowed her brow.

“Do you truly want nothing, or do you want everything?”

After speaking, she glanced at Clarissa.

Instantly, a blush spread across Clarissa’s face as she understood the implication, feeling a sense of urgency. “Aunt Donna, how can you think like that?”

“Clarissa, I’m just worried about you. You’re still too young, too naive. I fear you might be deceived. You’re the eldest child of your parents, everything will be yours in the future. I worry that someone might deceive you, taking both you and the family fortune.”

Finishing her words, she cast a cautious look towards Osborne.

Osborne felt speechless. Donna was clearly worried that he might take advantage of being Clarissa’s savior to deceive her emotionally.

Marrying Clarissa would mean gaining everything from the Radcliffe family.

“Well, I’ll give you 100 million dollars, consider it repaying your favor from fifteen years ago. Of course, if you encounter trouble in the future or wish to start a business, you can come to me. I will represent the Radcliffe family and assist you as much as possible,” Donna said.

At this point, Wesley and Ophelia were even more envious, wishing they could replace Osborne.

However, Osborne remained calm. “Is this generosity genuine? There must be a catch, right?”

Donna was somewhat surprised by Osborne’s composure.

She nodded, saying, “If you have no ulterior motives, the cost is nothing to you. I just hope that after taking the money, you won’t come looking for Clarissa again.

“Sorry, I’m quite straightforward. I’m Clarissa’s aunt, and she has recently encountered some unfortunate events. I must ensure her absolute safety. I hope you understand.”

Clarissa couldn’t hold back, furrowing her brows and protesting loudly.

“Aunt Donna, Osborne is the savior I’ve been searching for fifteen years. Why shouldn’t he be allowed to see me? I’m an adult now, you have no right to control me.”

Donna’s expression turned serious at Clarissa’s words.
