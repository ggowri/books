"If it comes down to it, I can always have another."

Kurt's face turned a deep shade of red, but he dared not respond.

Trevor nodded slightly, his expression unreadable.

"I know your true self is nearby because I possess a treasure—one I obtained from the depths of a divine peak. It's called the Binding Twin Pearl."

"Thank goodness Trevor didn't bind Kurt with it. That would've been a disaster! After I knocked him out and left him in Hope Haven... if Trevor had teleported there, given his abilities, Hope Haven would have been doomed!"

The thought sent a chill down his spine.

He mused, "Looks like I can't afford to kidnap anyone lightly anymore!

Even if I do, I can't keep them close or stash them in Hope Haven. Who knows if they're linked to a super-strong warrior through the Binding Twin Pearl? If so... we'd be dead in the water!"

However, you're quite skilled at hiding your energy—so much so that even the Binding Twin Pearl can only confirm you're within 50 miles. I can't pinpoint your exact location!

"Otherwise, I wouldn't be going through all this trouble.

The Binding Twin Pearl could lock onto his true self through his avatars. Luckily, he had Eclipsing Mantra to mask his presence, throwing off the pearl's tracking ability.

If not for that, Trevor might have found him in an instant.

A heavy weight settled in Osborne's chest.

"The methods of a Supernal Deity King are beyond my imagination! I thought having Spatial Clones and interdimensional travel would let me stand toe-to-toe with one, even daring to 'extort' a Supernal Deity King. How naive of me!"

Ultimately, it all came down to strength. He realized just how weak he truly was.

"The antidote, huh? Of course, I, Theodore, always keep my word! But right now, I don't have a ready-made antidote. I'll need to whip one up on the spot, so just give me a moment!"

With that, he began to playact, pulling out a few herbs and casually rubbing them together, all the while stalling for time.

Trevor frowned, sensing something was off but saying nothing.

As the minutes ticked by, a growing unease settled over Trevor.

Osborne was taking too long; it was clear he was dragging his feet.

Something didn't sit right. He pressed his hand against Kurt's forehead, focusing intently.

Suddenly, Trevor's expression turned icy.

"Damn it! How dare you deceive me?"
