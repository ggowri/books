Chapter 1351 Press

The Transcendent Deity warrior spoke up, "Godfather, I assure you, all the warriors from the Hastings family are here. No one is hiding in the crowd.

"I don't know how the Hastings family offended you, but if they've done something wrong, I'll apologize on their behalf.

"Of course, you can deal with them as you see fit; we won't protect any of them."

He finished with a look of righteous determination.

Osborne shot him a scornful glance, his expression dripping with derision. The warrior felt a twinge of embarrassment but quickly regained his composure.

They all understood that Osborne's power was beyond their reach. If sacrificing nearly a hundred members of the Hastings family could quell his fury and save their own skins, they wouldn't hesitate to throw them under the bus.

"That's right! Those scoundrels from the Hastings family dared to cross Godfather; they deserve whatever's coming to them!"

"Godfather, you can punish them however you want."

"Hastings, get down and beg for mercy! Do you want to drag us all down with you?"

"Those Hastings fools..."

The crowd erupted in blame, eager to distance themselves from the Hastings family, hoping Osborne would focus all his wrath on them alone.

Osborne merely sneered.

The Pantheon League was made up of thirty-six Divine Families, and when they were thriving, they stood united, sharing both fortune and glory.

But when trouble struck, especially in the face of life-or-death crises, their true colors emerged. They would gladly step on each other to ensure their own survival.

At that moment, the crowd was united in their desire for the Hastings family to perish, believing it would appease Osborne's anger and grant them a reprieve.

The nearly hundred members of the Hastings family stood pale and terrified, trembling under the weight of accusations and scorn.

They wanted to shout back, to curse the ones who had offended Osborne, but they simply didn't know what exactly Osborne was here for.

All they could do was watch Osborne warily, waiting for his verdict as if standing before a judge. Anxiety hung thick in the air.

"Hmph!" Osborne let out a cold snort, uninterested in the internal strife of the Pantheon League.

His focus was singular: to find the ones who had tormented his parents and uncover the truth of their fate.

As he dismissed the crowd with a wave of his hand, silence descended once more, and no one dared to speak.

Finally, Osborne broke the tension. "Twenty-five years ago, the leader of the Hastings family, Reginald, ventured alone to Rosmandi's Skyefall to investigate the Crystal of Life reserves in a rare metal mine for the Pantheon League.

"On his way back, he encountered Theodore, the son of Sebastian, the Godfather of Rosmandi, along with his wife, Vivienne. Reginald abducted them and brought them back to the Hastings family, locking them in the dungeons."

Osborne's voice dripped with malice as he fixed his gaze on the Hastings family members.

"So, anyone from the Hastings family who knows about this, step forward! Don't even think about hiding the truth from me; I know more than you can imagine."

His icy words sent a ripple of panic through the Hastings family.

Some of them exchanged glances, confusion evident in some, while others were gripped by fear and anxiety, realizing Osborne's intent.

Their bodies began to tremble as the weight of the moment bore down on them.

A few Intermediate Deities from the Hastings family breathed a sigh of relief, knowing this incident didn't involve them.

Osborne's voice was as cold as ice. "I'm giving you ten seconds to step forward!

"If anyone knows anything but remains silent, I'll kill every last one of you and dig the answers out of your memories myself!

"If you're involved in this, now's your chance to come clean!"

At that moment, a few Intermediate Deities from the Hastings family spoke up, their voices urgent, "Don't let your mistakes drag the whole family down!

"This is something we can't hide anymore; the Godfather is here. We should cooperate and make this easier on ourselves.

"Just step forward," another urged.

Finally, trembling with fear, a dozen or so Junior Deities slowly emerged from the crowd of Hastings family members.

Looking at them, Osborne compared their appearance to the people Reginald remembered torturing his parents with and found that they were indeed the same individuals.

A flash of murderous intent flickered in Osborne's eyes, but he kept it in check.

"Let me be clear: Theodore and Vivienne are my parents! I'm searching for them.

"From what I learned from Reginald, my parents managed to escape into a black vortex. But Reginald didn't pursue them; he didn't know where that vortex was.

"So I ask you: who among you hunted my parents?

"Who knows the location of that black vortex?

"Tell me where it is! If my parents are still alive, I might just spare your lives!"

Hope sparked in the eyes of the dozen or so who stood before him.

"I know where the black vortex is! I can help! I want to make amends for my sins—I can lead you there!"
