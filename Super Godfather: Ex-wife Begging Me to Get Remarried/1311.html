Chapter 1310 Terrence's Shock

Osborne looked at Terrence, puzzled yet amused.

"You really want to see my Divine Flame? Honestly, it might be better for you if you didn't. Just facing the end like this could be a mercy for you."

Terrence frowned, confusion etched on his features.

"What do you mean?"

Osborne's smile grew wider. "Because just dying now would spare you some hurt feelings. If I show you my Divine Flame and beat you, I fear you might not take it well."

Terrence scoffed, saying, "Not take it well? What a joke! I'm Terrence! I've lived for over a thousand years—I've seen it all! Show me your Divine Flame already!"

Osborne shrugged. "Well, if that's how you feel, then alright! Watch closely."

With that, Divine Shadow and God of Star exchanged amused glances behind Osborne.

A knot of unease twisted in Terrence's gut.

Osborne then gathered his power, dispelling the Eclipsing Mantra, revealing two transparent flames hovering atop his head.

Terrence froze, disbelief flooding his mind. He rubbed his eyes vigorously, convinced he was hallucinating.

But no matter how many times he blinked, those two transparent Divine Flames stayed locked in place.

"Two Divine Flames?

"An Intermediate Deity?!

"You've got to be kidding me!

"You're telling me you're just an Intermediate Deity? A Waste Divine Body? Are you mocking me?"

Terrence's voice cracked, incredulity washing over him.

He could never have imagined that Osborne was merely an Intermediate Deity with a Waste Divine Body!

"This is impossible!"

The color drained from Terrence's face as he grasped the implications of everything Osborne had said.

Had Osborne remained on par with the Deschanel Clan's Transcendent Deities? That was acceptable—if they were a similar rank.

But to lose to someone significantly beneath him? The very thought made him feel ill.

Terrence nearly choked on his own shock.

Most damning was the fact that he had witnessed Osborne hold his ground against the Transcendent Deity warrior of his clan, proving more than a match with skills that had caused them plenty of headaches.

Terrence had genuinely thought Osborne was a Transcendent Deity himself.

Terrence's voice quivered with shock. "How can this be happening?

"You're an Intermediate Deity—specifically, an Intermediate Deity with a Waste Divine Body!

"How could you ever stand up to the likes of my Transcendent Deity warriors? This makes no sense!"

Osborne simply smiled, saying, "Why not? I'm standing right here, aren't I?"

"No way!" Terrence was nearly trembling. "Not even in the Age of Gods could an Intermediate Deity confront a Transcendent Deity directly, let alone defeat one!

"Even the most legendary Deity Kings, when at their Intermediate Deity stage, might take on Superior Deities, but never would they dare challenge Transcendent Deities!

"The gap between them is wider than the ocean! There's no way an Intermediate Deity could ever beat a Transcendent Deity!

"How could your talent possibly be stronger than that of a Deity King? It's inconceivable!"

Terrence was losing his composure.

Osborne chuckled. "Wait, so I'm that amazing? You're saying even the Deity Kings at the Intermediate Deity stage couldn't handle Transcendent Deities? I appreciate you sharing that; it boosts my confidence.

"But yes, I am indeed an Intermediate Deity! As for my Divine Body, it's not actually a Waste Divine Body—it's just transparent due to some special circumstances.

"In reality, my Divine Body might be around Level Seven. Perhaps even stronger..."

Terrence was left utterly speechless, staring blankly at Osborne.

Not only was Terrence stunned, but even Divine Shadow and God of Star behind Osborne were momentarily frozen in shock.

They stared at Osborne in disbelief.

They had sensed the strength of Osborne's Divine Body before, but because his Divine Flame was transparent, they couldn't determine the exact level of his Divine Body.

Now, hearing Osborne declare it himself, they realized it was equivalent to a Level Seven Divine Body.

It might even be stronger.

Exchanging incredulous glances, Divine Shadow and God of Star both gasped, their astonishment palpable.

Terrence, voice rising in disbelief, exclaimed, "Impossible! You're just an Intermediate Deity!

"How could you possess a Level Seven Divine Body? Historically, within the Intermediate Deity ranks, there have only been a handful of cases where someone even reached Level Six! And those individuals were exceedingly rare, ultimately rising to Deity King status!

"They were all the elites of the Deity Kings!

"How could you possibly have a Level Seven Divine Body while still at Intermediate Deity? This must be a mistake! You're trying to intimidate me!"

Osborne shrugged casually. "If my Divine Body weren't on par with Level Seven, how could I have defeated a Transcendent Deity warrior while still being an Intermediate Deity?"

Terrence was immediately at a loss for words as that argument struck a chord.

He could only think, "Right, if Osborne's Divine Body wasn't Level Seven, how could he have triumphed over a Transcendent Deity?

So, his Divine Body really is comparable to Level Seven... even stronger?

What kind of monster is this?"

At that moment, a wave of jealousy washed over Terrence. If he had a talent like Osborne's, he would have long since broken through to become a Deity King, reaching the pinnacle of warriors in the Age of Gods.

Osborne broke the silence with a grin. "Alright! So, can we kick things off now?"
