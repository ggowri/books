Chapter 987 You Haven't Earned the Right

As the two Class S assassins from the Eityreia Organization rushed in, it was already too late. They could only stand there in stunned disbelief as Osborne's foot connected forcefully with Scarlett's head, a sickening crunch echoing in the air.

Rage, hatred, and fear coursed through them.

Scarlett was the only child of Sin and Crimson.

They had been tasked with her protection, and now, witnessing her brutal death at Osborne's hands felt like a death sentence for them as well. Returning to the Eityreia Organization without her meant they'd face Sin and Crimson's wrath.

They didn't want to die.

The only chance of survival was to kill Osborne and avenge Scarlett.

"You're dead meat!"

In an instant, the two Class S assassins unleashed their attack, shattering Osborne's Spatial Domain effortlessly. After all, he was a Class AAA fighter, and his Spatial Domain was only at the pinnacle of Level Two—a solid defense against Scarlett but nowhere near enough for two true Class S assassins.

Yet Osborne remained unfazed.

The backlash from his collapsing Spatial Domain hit him hard, but he pushed it aside, grinning coldly at the two advancing threats.

He mused, "Much stronger than the Class S clones from the Aberrants! But still a bit weaker than Mr. Lambert. They're a troublesome duo but definitely not unbeatable!

First things first, can't let them sneak up on me!"

Without hesitation, he sprang into action.

Osborne summoned his Volt Hammer, and a terrifying bolt of lightning shot down from the heavens.

The two assassins reacted instantaneously, darting to either side, narrowly evading the thunderous strike that obliterated their previous location, leaving behind a charred crater.

Osborne didn't mind.

They weren't just any warriors; they were genuine Class S assassins, not the ragged clones he was used to facing. There was no way he would catch them that easily.

"Let's try again!" Osborne said, swinging the Volt Hammer once more.

Lightning crackled and arced toward the two figures, relentless in pursuit.

Although their movements were swift, it wasn't enough to completely escape.

Before long, each of them was struck, dark scorch marks appearing on their chests, skin splitting open.

Their expressions twisted in pain.

They were injured.

Osborne's Volt Hammer was no ordinary weapon; combined with the Thundergod Gloves, it rivaled top-grade divine artifacts, making it far from easy to withstand.

"Osborne, you bastard!" they shouted, their fury intensifying with their injuries.

In an instant, they vanished from sight, fading into stealth mode.

Osborne furrowed his brow.

The Stealth Art they used was similar to Scarlett's. He couldn't detect any spatial fluctuations, and even Satan's Eye wouldn't help here.

He remained calm. "Fine then, I'll just go with my old trick!

"Light Realm!"

With a pulsating hum, an enormous Light Realm enveloped an area of half a mile, illuminating everything and flushing out the two stealthy assassins.

Now revealed, they flanked him, standing only 150 feet away, clearly ready to mount a surprise attack.

Unfortunately for them, in Osborne's Realm, he was a god. As long as his Realm held, their plans for stealth were nothing more than a joke.

They realized this, too, but instead of retreating, they erupted with terrifying speed, closing the distance in the blink of an eye, daggers glinting in their hands.

"Die!"

"Kill him!"

Their voices blended in a hellish harmony as they coordinated perfectly, blocking all avenues of escape. Four daggers lunged toward his vital points—his heart, his throat—all aimed to end him.

At that moment, the true power of Class S warriors surged forth, fully unleashed and ready to bring destruction.

Osborne's martial art aura, formidable as it was, couldn't completely repel the vicious assault aimed at him. He understood that if he got hit, it wouldn't just be a mere scrape—it'd likely leave him seriously injured, if not dead.

A cold gravity settled over him as he felt the imminent danger.

"Fuck off!" Osborne bellowed, his voice echoing with fury.

Channeling the power within his Divine Bone, he thrust his right leg forward with a ferocious kick, sending the Class S assassin on his left crashing away, the sound of shattering bones ringing out like a sickening chorus.

But just as he dealt with one threat, the other moved in—striking hard and fast.

A divine artifact dagger sliced straight through Osborne's martial art aura, plunging deep into his flesh and aiming directly for his heart.

His Satan's Eye pulsed violently, screaming warnings of impending doom.

Yet, even in the face of such raw peril, Osborne remained composed.

With a sudden, powerful motion, he shot out his hand and seized the assassin's arm.

The sheer force of his grip bulged veins against the skin as he effectively halted the assassin's advance, the look of shock on the assassin's face unmistakable.

This was a Class S warrior caught helplessly in Osborne's grasp. No amount of effort could budge his arm a single inch, even with the dagger already piercing Osborne's flesh—barely half an inch away from his heart.

It wouldn't move any further!

"Get out of my way!" Osborne roared, his rage boiling over.

He unleashed another brutal kick, propelling the assassin backward with such force that the impact left the villain gasping for breath.

"Did you think you could kill me?

"You haven't earned that right yet!"
