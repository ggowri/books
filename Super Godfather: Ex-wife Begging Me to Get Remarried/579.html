Chapter 579 Heading to the Dungeon

"Can I say a few words to my family before we go?" Osborne asked coldly.

Matthew, slightly disappointed by Osborne's lack of resistance, shrugged.

"You've got three minutes."

Osborne walked over to Isaac.

He discreetly removed the Draco Star Wristlet from his wrist and handed it to Isaac.

"Isaac," Osborne said softly, "don't worry about me. As the heir apparent, they won't do anything too severe. They just want to make things uncomfortable for me.

"I'll be out soon. I'm more worried about what might happen to you all while I'm away."

"Take this. If something goes wrong, contact Serena and use this as a token to get her help. Even though she's not a direct family member, she has a lot of influence in the Augustine family."

Isaac took Draco Star Wristlet and nodded.

"Be careful. I don't know what the dungeon is like, but it seems like everyone in the Augustine family is afraid of it. Stay safe, and we'll be waiting for your return."

Osborne nodded and turned to Kendra. "Kendra, I know you're more capable than you let on. While I'm gone, make sure Zoe, Anastasia, and Yulissa are safe.

"You have to promise me they'll be okay!"

Kendra pouted with a frown. "Osborne, what are you talking about? I don't have any offensive abilities. How am I supposed to protect them?"

Osborne's gaze remained steady, clearly serious.

Kendra sighed.

"Alright, alright, I promise. If it comes to that, I'll get my brother to help. I'm not much on my own, but I'll do what I can."

Osborne relaxed, feeling reassured by her promise.

"Time's up!" Matthew called out sharply.

"Osborne, it's time to go to the dungeon."

Osborne gave him a brief, dismissive glance, then addressed the crowd watching them.

His eyes were cold as he took in their sneers and derision.

"While I'm away, anyone who dares to target the Augustine family in Dampston will become my enemy. Unless I'm dead, I will make sure to get my revenge!"

A fierce determination radiated from Osborne, making the crowd uneasy and their expressions tense.

"Ha! You're the one who should worry about getting out of the dungeon first."

"And who do you think you are, making threats like that? You think you can just come back and get revenge?"

"Laughable."

Some people scoffed.

Osborne took a deep breath but decided not to say more. He knew his threats wouldn't change the minds of those who truly wanted to harm him.

His words were meant to give him a reason to strike back once he was free.

"Lead the way," Osborne said, his voice as cold as ice.

Matthew gave a dismissive chuckle and turned to walk ahead. The crowd watched their retreating figures until they were out of sight.

"Everyone, head back. I'll handle him from here," Matthew called out once they left Mount Seven.

"Understood!" the onlookers replied, dispersing back to the main bloodline.

Matthew guided Osborne towards Challenge Peak, where the Augustine family's dungeon was located.

Osborne followed, deep in thought about why Matthew seemed to have it out for him.

At thirty-eight, Matthew was a powerful figure.

He'd become a metahuman master at twenty-two and was now also a martial master. His skills made him the second most formidable of the younger generation, only behind Vincent.

Rumor had it that he was nearing Class AAA and was significantly stronger than Owen.

Given his position, Osborne couldn't understand why Matthew would be targeting him.

"Is it something to do with Mr. Caleb Augustine's comments?" Osborne mused.

"You're wondering why I'm focusing on you, right?" Matthew's voice cut through his thoughts.

Osborne replied coolly, "Yes, I am. I don't remember doing anything to offend you."

Matthew shrugged nonchalantly. "Offend me? You're too naïve.

"From the moment you became the heir apparent, you became my competitor. Even if your strength doesn't impress me right now, I always think ahead. As competitors, it's only natural for me to try to outmaneuver you. Don't you think?"

Osborne was taken aback. "You're quite honest about your intentions."

Matthew smiled slightly. "Of course. There's no point in hiding my goals. I've learned from a young age that going all out against rivals is just part of the game. If you can retaliate effectively, I'll respect that and won't hold a grudge."

Osborne raised an eyebrow. "So you're saying I shouldn't hate you?"

Matthew laughed softly.

"Whether you hate me or not doesn't matter. I'm confident that, no matter how much you might despise me, you won't be able to change my path. I see potential in you. When I become the head of the Augustine family, I'll need someone like you to help strengthen the family."

Osborne's face hardened. "You're quite confident. But as far as I know, you're only the second strongest among the younger generation. What makes you think you can become the head of the Augustine family?"
