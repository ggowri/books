Chapter 1138 Anastasia and Zoe's Strength

"Anastasia, so you're skilled with Thunder Power?"

Anastasia nodded eagerly. "Yeah! Ever since I woke up, I felt this connection to lightning! Even Mr. Sebastian Augustine praised my control over it!"

Osborne smiled at her enthusiasm, asking, "Control? That's an understatement! Your Thunder Power is terrifying! How about we have a little competition?"

Anastasia's eyes lit up with interest. "A competition? How do we do that?"

Osborne explained, "Let's each unleash a lightning attack and see whose is stronger!"

"Sounds great! I'll go first!"

With a swift motion, she raised her hand, and suddenly, the sky darkened.

A fearsome bolt of purple lightning crackled in the distance, not striking down but dancing in the air like a massive serpent under her command.

"Your turn, Osborne!"

Osborne stood there, momentarily stunned. Watching Anastasia manipulate the lightning so easily, he realized he was already at a disadvantage.

The raw power of her lightning was explosive, and she could control it without making it explode—something Osborne knew he couldn't match.

But it was attack strength they were competing for!

"Thunder of Judgment!" Osborne shouted, summoning a powerful bolt of lightning that shot toward the one controlled by Anastasia.

The two lightning strikes collided with a deafening roar.

The sky lit up with a dazzling display of purple electricity, illuminating half the horizon before fading into darkness.

When the dust settled, only a faint flicker of lightning remained—the one Anastasia had conjured.

Osborne gasped, taken aback.

His strongest thunder attack, Thunder of Judgment, had been utterly shattered by Anastasia's control.

"So strong!" he exclaimed, genuine admiration in his voice.

Anastasia beamed at the compliment. "Osborne, you're powerful, too! You almost managed to break my lightning! Just so you know, Mr. Sebastian Augustine couldn't even do that without using Godfather's scepter!"

Osborne chuckled, feeling a surge of pride.

He hadn't even gone all out; his used the strength from three months ago before he fell into that deep slumber.

Now Osborne felt transformed, stronger than ever before, though he couldn't quite know just how much.

Still, Anastasia's prowess was impressive enough to leave him in awe.

She was definitely not weaker than Osborne had been three months ago.

Her attack power was even a notch above his.

Her defense, however, was still a bit lacking.

Three months ago, Osborne had been able to take down top-tier Class S warriors like Gerardo, Sin, Armstrong, and Reginald.

He gently ruffled Anastasia's hair, genuinely praising her, "My little Anastasia is amazing now!"

Her face lit up with pride at his words.

Meanwhile, Zoe stood a little way off, her expression dimming as she watched Osborne affectionately patted Anastasia's head.

A flicker of envy crossed her eyes; she wished she could be as powerful as Anastasia.

More than that, she longed for the same closeness with Osborne.

Osborne caught a glimpse of Zoe's longing expression from the corner of his eye.

Just as he was about to say something to her, a sudden noise pierced the air.

The sound of wings slicing through the wind echoed ominously.

A flock of enormous birds surged from the depths of the jungle below, heading straight toward them.

Osborne suddenly spoke up, his voice filled with anticipation, "Zoe, how about you take care of them? I want to see what you're capable of now!"

At his words, Zoe's face lit up with excitement, and she nodded vigorously.

"Okay!"

Facing the group of massive birds, each comparable to a Class B metahuman, Zoe softly uttered a single word, "Fall!"

A strange ripple passed through the air. The gigantic birds, which had been soaring with menace, suddenly lost their ability to fly. Their wings ceased to flap, their eyes glazed over, and they seemed to freeze mid-air, as if their thoughts had been abruptly halted.

And then, they all began to plummet.

One by one, the birds disappeared into the dense forest below, none of them ever getting to fly again.

Osborne watched in astonishment. "Is this some kind of sonic attack?"

Zoe shook her head. "It's more than just sound! To be honest, I can't fully explain it myself. But when I make a sound, my enemies pause, and their thoughts freezing, their senses disconnecting from the outside world.

"The stronger the enemy, the harder it is to affect them! For Class B enemies, a single word is enough to stop them in their tracks. If it's Class A, I'd need three words.

"If it's Class S, I'd probably have to sing a whole song!"

Her face flushed slightly as she finished, quickly adding, "But still, I'm not as awesome as Anastasia."

Osborne, however, was thoroughly impressed.

He ruffled Zoe's hair gently, praising her, "My Zoe, you're pretty amazing too!

"An attack like that? Even I would have a hard time resisting it! Plus, you can hit multiple targets at once."
