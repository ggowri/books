Chapter 887 Ninety-Five Wins

Osborne quickly racked up an impressive eighty consecutive wins.

Notably, Clarissa and Emma had also stepped into the ring. They currently ranked 300 and 299 in the Godson Rankings.

They naturally didn't intend to push Osborne too hard and soon "fell" to him.

With every match, Osborne surged ahead effortlessly.

In his eighty-fifth challenge, he bested Kate Winslet, who was ranked 230th.

By the ninetieth bout, he defeated Megan Robinson, who was in the 200th spot.

This brought his streak to a remarkable ninety wins.

Yet, this wasn't the end for Osborne.

Even though his opponents were the elite from their respective nations, ranked within the top two hundred, they simply couldn't hold him back.

After a series of intense battles, he finally reached ninety-five consecutive victories.

The crowd watching was left in awe.

If Osborne had struggled and exhausted himself for his ninety-five wins, perhaps they could have accepted it. But here he was, standing tall, still brimming with energy as if he hadn't broken a sweat.

It was as if Osborne hadn't spent any energy at all.

Excitement lit up the eyes of everyone from the Augustine family.

Ninety-five consecutive wins was the record set by Sebastian himself!

Over forty years ago, during his Honor Quest Ceremony, Sebastian had also achieved ninety-five victories but collapsed just short of the legendary one hundred wins.

It was a long-standing regret that haunted both him and the Augustine family.

The century mark was a significant milestone.

However, Sebastian's ninety-five wins remained the best in their family's history. The second-best was only a bit over sixty.

But now, Osborne had equaled that proud record.

His achievement was beyond anyone in the Augustine family's wildest expectations.

Even Wesson, who had thought the most Osborne could manage was fifty wins, was left speechless.

He knew Osborne was Sebastian's grandson and thought that fifty wins would sufficiently mark him worthy of the name.

But now? Fifty wins was nothing!

Osborne had matched Sebastian's mark, and with just one more win, he could surpass it and etch his name into the Augustine family's history anew.

He was even on the verge of a hundred victories!

Wesson's eyes glistened with tears as he looked at Osborne on the stage, who was still full of vitality.

"Sebastian, are you watching? I finally understand why you believed in him so much! He really can do it!"

The members of the Augustine family were filled with a whirlpool of excitement and anticipation, eager for Osborne to take one more victory and break Sebastian's record.

As they watched him, they couldn't help but feel they were witnessing the rise of a new legend.

Osborne was buzzing with exhilaration, too.

He could feel the weight of every challenge growing heavier, each opponent more formidable than the last, demanding more energy with every win.

But he had made it to ninety-five victories.

"Just one more win, and I'll surpass Grandfather," Osborne thought, clenching his jaw as he turned to face his opponent for the ninety-sixth battle.

A cloaked woman stepped onto the arena and slowly pulled back her hood, revealing a stunning face.

As she came into view, Osborne frowned, feeling an unpleasant scent radiating from her.

Osborne recognized her name immediately: Felicity.

She held the 128th spot in the Godson Rankings.

But beyond that, he knew nothing about her. No details were provided in the rankings.

Felicity smiled softly at him, "Well, isn't this a coincidence? I just happen to be your opponent for the ninety-sixth match! Maybe it's fate, or perhaps destiny itself!"

She finished speaking and looked Osborne up and down with a cold smirk.

Osborne frowned, perplexed. "Fate? What do you mean by that?"

With a calm smile, Felicity replied, "It seems you're not aware of my background! I didn't plan on revealing it, but since it's such a coincidence, it would be a shame to keep it from you.

"Remember this: my teacher is the King of the Aberrants! I'm his fourth direct disciple and his granddaughter!"

At her words, Osborne's expression hardened, his eyes glinting with cold fury.

The realization sent a chill through him.

He had never expected that his ninety-sixth opponent would be Gerardo's own flesh and blood.

Osborne thought, "The granddaughter of the King of the Aberrants? The fourth disciple?

How dare she come to Rosmandi?

How could she step foot on the Augustine Estate?

Doesn't she fear for her death?"

His eyes burned with murderous intent.

Felicity, however, seemed unfazed by his growing hostility. "Osborne, do you know who ultimately defeated Sebastian during his Honor Quest Ceremony over forty years ago?"
