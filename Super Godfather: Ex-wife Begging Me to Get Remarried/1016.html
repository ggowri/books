Chapter 1016 Thousands of Class S Clones

Two hours later, Osborne finally stopped.

Isa wasn’t joking; when she said he couldn't stop for even a second, she meant it.

But for Osborne, this level of exertion was a walk in the park.

As he looked down at Isa, panting beneath him, a wave of satisfaction washed over him.

"My man is getting better and better," Isa said, her voice playful as she wrapped her arms around his neck.

Her cheeks were flushed with the afterglow of the passion, and she planted a soft kiss on his lips before starting to get dressed.

"Isa, are you really leaving again?" Osborne asked, hesitation creeping into his voice.

He wanted her to stay to help him.

Now that he was the new Godfather, the Augustine family and the Draco Group both relied on him for protection.

With threats looming from the Aberrants, the Eityreia Organization, and the League of Divine Descent, the pressure was mounting.

Isa slipped on her clothes, glancing at him with calm determination.

"Yes, I have to go. I know what you want to say, but I can't stay to help you. First, I need to find the God of Time.

"Second, this is a test for you!

"You're my man, Osborne. If you can't handle the Aberrants, the Eityreia Organization, or the League of Divine Descent, maybe you should just give it all up and settle for being a little man by my side. I can protect you!

Osborne was taken aback.

He rubbed his nose, not keen on the idea of being a "little man" under Isa's protection.

Isa continued, "Right now, your opponents are just the Aberrants, the Eityreia Organization, and the League of Divine Descent. Sure, they're top-tier forces on Blue Planet, but in Celestara, they nothing!

"And don't forget about Lord Malevolence and Dark Deity. I wasn't joking; they're real, and they'll be our enemies soon enough.

"So I need you to step up. Take down the Aberrants, deal with the League of Divine Descent and the Eityreia Organization, and grow stronger!

"When you do, I'll share more truths about this world with you. I might even take you to Celestara to see what it's really like!"

With that, Isa finished dressing and stepped back to face him, pressing her cheek against his chest.

"Osborne, I need your help!

"I have all the patience in the world for you to grow, but this world doesn’t!

"You have to level up quickly. Or you might never see me again."

Her words hung heavy in the air, sending a chill down Osborne's spine.

He furrowed his brow, urgency in his voice as he asked,

"Isa, why might I never see you again?"

Isa shook her head, evading the question.

She offered a small smile and changed the subject.

"By the way, here's a free piece of intel.

"I've been tracking the energy fluctuations of the God of Time, and they've been concentrated near the Aberrants' stronghold. I've noticed they're mobilizing troops on a massive scale.

"Hundreds, even thousands of Class S divine clones have already entered Rosmandi! The Aberrants is gearing up for a full-scale war, and it's about to kick off!"

Isa's words left Osborne in shock, his mind racing as the implications sank in.

Osborne thought, "Divine clones?

They must be referring to the Aberrants' clones.

Isa actually saw thousands of Class S clones entering Rosmandi?

How is that even possible?

So many divine clones?

If it hadn't been for Isa, I would've assumed there are only a couple hundred Class S clones at most. Even that is a terrifying prospect.

But a thousand?

Gerardo has managed to produce a thousand Class S clones?

How on earth am I supposed to fight against that?"

A heavy weight settled in Osborne's chest as he felt the gravity of the situation. Just when he was about to ask Isa for more details, she flashed him a small smile.

"Don't ask me anything. Don't try to ask for my help. You have to handle this all on your own!

"Just hurry up and get stronger.

"That's the only way you can help me. Otherwise, if you can't find me again, don't cry like a little baby!"

Before he could respond, Isa's figure abruptly vanished from sight.

Osborne blinked, bewildered, unable to fathom how she had left.

There were no spatial fluctuations, no hint of power in the air—she had just disappeared in an instant.

"Whoa!" Osborne inhaled sharply, a chill running down his spine as he felt the depth of Isa's power—a strength that seemed boundless.

Osborne muttered under his breath, "Rely on myself, huh?

"You want me to grow quickly and come to your aid? Or I might not be able to see you again?

"I won't let that happen! I can't!"

His eyes burned with resolve.

"It's just the Aberrants, the Eityreia Organization, and the League of Divine Descent. I'll take care of them all by myself!"

But confronting the Aberrants was a difficult task. Nearly a thousand Class S clones, along with the formidable Gerardo, loomed ahead of him like a dark cloud.

The pressure was mounting; given their current strength, Osborne felt utterly outmatched.
