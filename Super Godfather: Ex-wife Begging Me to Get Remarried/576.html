Chapter 576 Death Sickle

Osborne's icy words sent a ripple of shock through the crowd.

No one had expected him to be so blunt, so fearlessly direct.

The onlookers stared in disbelief, and even Larry and the elite fighters from Ransford were momentarily stunned by his audacity.

It didn't take long for their surprise to turn into fury. Ransford's fighters glared at Osborne, their eyes blazing with anger.

Meanwhile, the people from Dampston felt a surge of pride. Watching Osborne standing there, unyielding and bold, filled them with a renewed sense of hope.

His defiant words had lifted a weight from their hearts, a weight they had carried for far too long.

For years, Dampston had been seen as the weakest among the nine Augustine family branches, dismissed and looked down upon by everyone else.

They had never been able to fight back because, frankly, they lacked the strength.

But now, they had Osborne.

In this moment, they felt that as long as Osborne stood with them, no one had the right to look down on them anymore.

"You're walking right into your grave, Osborne!" Larry snarled and his voice thick with rage.

Osborne didn't even flinch. "You've got five minutes. Get out of the Third Estate, or I'll make you."

"Five minutes? Don't waste my time," Larry shot back, his anger boiling over.

"If you're so eager to fight, let's do it now!

" You killed David. Let's finish this once and for all!"

A smirk played at the corner of Osborne's mouth.

"Fine by me!"

He waved his hand, signaling the Dampston members to step back a bit.

In response, Larry gestured for his fighters to advance.

Nearly thirty of them stepped forward, each exuding a powerful aura. Most of them had competed in Branch Bloodline Competition the day before, and they were all at Class A or above.

Leading them were the three elders of Ransford, including Larry, all of whom were at Class AA.

They looked at Osborne with a mixture of contempt and confidence.

This looked like a completely lopsided battle.

Osborne was alone, facing nearly thirty seasoned fighters.

Despite his victory over Sam during the tournament, almost no one believed he could stand against the combined might of Ransford.

As Osborne's icy words hung in the air, the atmosphere grew so tense it felt like the world had paused.

With a burst of energy, his martial art aura flared up, encasing him in a brilliant, protective light.

His gaze zeroed in on the three elders leading Ransford, Larry and his two companions, all seasoned fighters at Class AA.

Their expressions were calm, almost bored, as if they couldn't imagine Osborne posing any real threat.

But Osborne wasn't one to take chances. He knew that if he wanted to win, these three had to fall.

He'd been preparing for this moment.

Osborne reached into Dimensional Space and pulled out a massive, jet-black sickle.

Towering above even his tall frame, the sickle was nearly twice his height, its grotesque, fearsome design immediately capturing everyone's attention.

A sinister energy pulsed from the blade, chilling the air around it.

For the first time, Larry's confidence wavered. He frowned, his earlier arrogance beginning to crack.

Osborne's lips curled into a cold smile as he gripped Death Sickle tightly, his eyes never leaving the three elders.

"Let's settle this!"

With that, he swung the sickle in a wide, deadly arc toward them.

The very fabric of reality seemed to distort as the blade moved, sending an invisible, malevolent force surging toward the three elders.

They sensed the danger, but it was too late to dodge.

Desperation etched across their faces as they hastily unleashed their energy shields.

But it was no use. The sickle's power bypassed their defenses entirely, cutting straight to their souls.

Their screams echoed through the air as agony twisted their faces. The soul-searing pain from the sickle's attack left them trembling, their very spirits quivering under the assault.

Osborne's power had grown exponentially since he absorbed star power, making him an even more formidable force when wielding Death Sickle.

The Death sickle was powered by the wielder's soul.

The stronger the soul, the more devastating the sickle's impact.

In Osborne's hands, it was more powerful than it had ever been under its previous master, Chris.

While Chris had needed time to recover after each use of the sickle, Osborne was now strong enough to wield it in rapid succession.

His ability to continually draw on the star power made him the perfect wielder of this deadly weapon.

Without missing a beat, Osborne raised the sickle again.

His voice was cold and unyielding. "If you won't leave on your own, then I'll make sure you do."

With another swing, the sickle's soul-rending energy slashed through the three elders once more.

This was an attack they couldn't hope to resist. The only way to resist its devastating attacks was through formidable spiritual and mental fortitude.

Even Serena, who was stronger than them, had struggled to survive three hits from Chris' sickle.

But these three weren't nearly as strong, and Osborne was far deadlier than Chris had ever been.

The outcome was clear. Larry and the others were at their breaking point.

Their screams became more frantic, their faces turning ghostly pale as their defenses shattered. Their bodies shook uncontrollably, barely able to remain upright.

And then, with a final, despairing cry, Larry collapsed to his knees.
