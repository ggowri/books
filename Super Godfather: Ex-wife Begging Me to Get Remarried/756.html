Chapter 756 Rescue

Roger's eyes darkened with a lethal glint as he stared down the enraged Stuart.

"Seriously? You charge at me before I even accept the challenge? Breaking the rules like that? You've pretty much signed your own death warrant.

"Time to pay the price!"

With those words, a cold gleam flashed in Roger's eyes, and in a blink, he vanished. No one in the crowd could track his movements, not even those with enhanced sight.

It was as if he'd disappeared into thin air, leaving behind no trace, no presence.

Stuart was left standing there, his panic growing by the second.

He spun around, desperately trying to locate Roger, but it was useless.

"This isn't good!"

Vivian's face paled.

"Stuart, you're playing right into his hands! He's baiting you into breaking the rules so he can kill you!

"Get out of there!" she shouted, her voice filled with urgency.

A chilling voice whispered from behind Stuart.

"Too late!"

It was Roger, his figure ghostly and menacing.

But what truly sent chills down everyone's spine was the dagger he held.

It was a divine artifact, its blade glinting with deadly intent.

Unlike formal duels, the rules here were loose. Any weapon was fair game, even a divine artifact.

Earlier, Roger had been using an ancient magic weapon, but now he wielded something far more dangerous.

In an instant, the dagger sliced through the air, aimed directly at Stuart's throat.

Stuart, paralyzed with fear, couldn't move.

His energy shields crumbled like paper under the dagger's power, offering no protection at all.

Vivian watched from below, her heart pounding in terror as the blade neared Stuart's neck.

If he didn't move, he was as good as dead.

Stuart could feel the cold breath of death.

But his body wouldn't respond as he was frozen in place.

"No!"

Both Stuart and Vivian screamed in horror, but it was too late. The dagger was already descending.

Just as the dagger was about to slice through Stuart's neck, the air around him suddenly rippled and distorted.

"Space stretching!"

Osborne's calm voice cut through the tension.

In a flash, the space between Stuart and Roger stretched, pulling them apart.

The dagger that was mere inches from Stuart's throat suddenly found itself a good distance away, slashing nothing but thin air.

Seizing the moment, Stuart bolted off the platform, his back drenched in cold sweat.

Roger's expression darkened, his fury barely contained as he turned to glare at Osborne.

"Spatial power, huh? Impressive, but saving him was a mistake.

"He broke the rules, and I had every right to end him. No one can stop me! By stepping in, you're basically asking to take his place, right?"

Osborne raised an eyebrow.

He hadn't intended to start a fight. His actions were purely instinctive. As a member of the Lambert family, it was natural to protect one of his own.

Taking on Stuart's grudge wasn't something Osborne had planned on, though.

He spoke evenly, "He acted on impulse, out of anger. Let him apologize, and we can call it even. How about that?"

Roger responded with a cold, mocking laugh, "An apology? Like that matters to me. I want him dead for breaking the rules. If you're so eager to defend him, why don't you step up yourself?

"Don't think you can just walk away. The rules here are set by all the major powers. If you defy them, the Lambert family will face the wrath of everyone.

"And since you're from the Lambert family too, killing you works just as well for me!"

Roger's eyes gleamed with a dangerous edge.

Osborne's frown deepened.

It was clear now that Roger had been baiting Stuart from the start.

But why? Did he have some kind of grudge against the Lambert family?

The Lambert family had kept to themselves for centuries, avoiding external conflicts and staying out of the power struggles of the world. How could they have crossed someone like Roger?

Perplexed, Osborne glanced at Stuart and Vivian. Stuart was still trembling with fear. Turning to Vivian, he asked, "Vivian, do we have any history with this guy?"

Vivian looked equally baffled and shook her head.

"That's impossible. We've been in seclusion for centuries, barely interacting with the outside world. The only one who might've had any dealings with him is Rhett.

"He's the only one who's been active outside recently."
