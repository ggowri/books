Chapter 6: Confronting His Wife for the First Time

His eyes glowed a beastly red.

As the years of humiliation flashed through his mind, he got rougher, squeezing and spanking their behinds, going at them with all his might.

Ophelia and Evelyn's moans bounced off the walls.

After what seemed like hours, they both collapsed to the ground, breathless and dumbstruck.

They didn't process what had happened until moments later, when Ophelia seemed to come back to her senses. She stood, her legs still trembling.

Embarrassed and furious, she yelled, her voice echoing through the room.

"How dare you do this to us! I won't let you get away with it. You're dead!"

Three years of marriage, and she never once thought he could be this bold. This commanding. This powerful.

Evelyn stirred and tried to stand.

"Evelyn, are you okay?"

Ophelia looked at her. Her face was flushed, her eyes were wide and empty.

"Ophelia, I... I'm fine," Evelyn stuttered.

Hatred, anger, and an indescribable shame filled Ophelia's gaze as she glanced at Osborne.

She scoffed. "Osborne, you're lucky you married me. I'm the one who should have regrets. I'm the prize here. I can't believe I've been stuck with a loser like you for three years. Just thinking of it makes me sick! I'm not even asking you to make up for the time I wasted on you. I'm only asking you to leave with nothing. Be grateful!"

"Grateful? That's hilarious. What if I say no?"

He grabbed Evelyn by the neck, squeezing it tightly.

Ophelia's face darkened. "Osborne, it's just money you're after, right? I'll give you a hundred grand. Let my stepmother go now."

She said it as if she was shooing away a beggar.

"A hundred grand?"

Osborne glanced at Evelyn with a mocking smile.

"You don't care much about your stepmother, do you?"

"Then how much do you want?" Ophelia's anger flared.

"How about a billion dollars?"

"In your dreams!" Ophelia sneered. "Osborne, have you lost your goddamn mind? This is way out of the line! Who do you think you are? Do you really think you deserve a billion dollars?"

"Of course I deserve it!" Osborne shot back. "If it wasn't for me protecting you in secret, our rivals would have slaughtered you ages ago. You'd never have become the sole heiress to the Caldwell family fortune."

Ophelia froze for a moment, then burst into laughter.

"Ha! I'd be dead by now without your protection? Were you always this delusional?"

She continued. "I became the sole heiress because I'm capable of handling everything that comes with it. What does that have to do with you? You really have some nerve."

Osborne sneered, not bothering to argue further.

"Think whatever you want," he said nonchalantly. "But I'm getting my fair share of money. If you don't give it to me, I'll snap her neck."

With that, Osborne tightened his grip slightly, making Evelyn cry out in pain.

Ophelia glared at Osborne. "Guards!"

Her bodyguards burst into the room, drawing their guns from their holsters and aiming them directly at Osborne.

"I'm telling you one last time, let Evelyn go right now! Or I'll order them to shoot!"

"Do it if you dare!" Osborne hissed. "I have nothing to lose. If you shoot, I'll take her down with me!"

He tightened his grip around Evelyn's neck.

Evelyn cried and begged. "Ophelia, no! Please! Don't shoot!"

"Osborne, be serious. You think holding my stepmom hostage will get you the money you desire? How naive! You're not the only one with a bargaining chip!" Ophelia threatened.

She whipped out her phone and illuminated the screen.

"Take a closer look. Do you recognize her?"

On the screen was a frail girl around seventeen or eighteen years old.

She was lying quietly on a hospital bed, her face pale, looking like a sleeping fairy.

No…It couldn't be…

"What the hell is this?" Osborne demanded.

"Just keep watching," Ophelia said.

Suddenly, a dark gun barrel appeared, pressing against the girl's temple.

Osborne's mind raced as hot fury coursed through him.

"Anastasia! Ophelia, go to hell!" Osborne cursed, his face twisted with rage.

He couldn't believe Ophelia actually sent someone to the hospital to put a gun to his sick sister's head.

Anastasia was what mattered the most to him in the whole world. Osborne would do anything to keep her safe—even if it meant dying.

Ophelia couldn't help but smirk.

"You thought you'd kept your sweet little sister a secret all these years, didn't you? I told you, loser. I always win. Now, let Evelyn go, drop to your knees, and beg for forgiveness. Otherwise, Anastasia dies," Ophelia said, her eyes brimming with arrogance. "And don't even think of trying anything."

Osborne vibrated with anger, his bloodshot eyes consumed with rage.

"Let's do a countdown, shall we? Let go of Evelyn or I'll order them to shoot," Ophelia warned.

"Three!"

He looked around the room. There was no way out—at least twenty guns were trained on him. The person he loved the most, the one he'd vowed to protect at any cost, had a gun pressed against her head. He couldn't simply attack.

He was trapped.

"Two!"

Ophelia continued her countdown, her striking face growing colder with each passing second.

She raised her hand, ready to give the order to shoot at any moment.

"One—fire!"
