Chapter 907 They'll Talk

A long convoy of cars stretched around the club, sealing off every possible exit.

"This is it!" Alexander declared, leading the way with Norma close behind.

The two strode quickly toward the club's entrance.

The guards who were supposed to be on duty had already vanished, leaving the heavy doors shut tight.

Alexander's sharp gaze swept the area. "No doubt about it. Hazel's scent is here."

"Great!"

Norma's face lit up, excitement flickering in her eyes. But as her thoughts drifted to those risqué outfits and provocative toys hidden in her sister's closet, her cheeks burned.

And when she imagined her sister inside, tangled up with some man, her glance flicked to Alexander involuntarily, and an unfamiliar heat rose within her.

Before she could think too much, Alexander kicked out with a force that sent the club's heavy doors flying inward, crashing to the ground with a deafening thud.

Gunfire erupted.

Alexander tilted his head to the side, dodging the bullet with an almost casual grace, and bolted inside with lightning speed.

"Who the hell are you?!"

A scream tore through the air as Alexander sent one of the men flying with a single kick.

The Sakuragi family's men flooded in behind him, flipping on every light switch they could find. The club was bathed in harsh, glaring light, illuminating the chaos within.

A middle-aged man and several other burly figures lay sprawled on the floor. One of them was coughing up blood, while the shattered remains of an assault rifle lay nearby.

The moment their eyes landed on Norma, their expressions changed drastically.

Especially the bartender, he immediately dropped his gaze, avoiding eye contact.

Alexander caught the subtle shift in the man's demeanor and smirked coldly.

"So, where's Hazel?" Alexander demanded, his voice like a blade slicing through the tension in the air.

The room fell into a suffocating silence. The bartender kept his head low, and no one else dared to speak.

Norma clenched her fists, anger bubbling to the surface. "Not going to talk? Fine. Then none of you are walking out of here alive!"

The middle-aged man dragged himself to his feet, his face twisted with fury.

"Don't think your Dragon Gang can just bully whoever you want! Sakra Society isn't afraid of you!"

Norma's eyes burned with rage. This guy had the audacity to play the victim.

"Hand over Hazel right now, and we'll leave. Otherwise, you'll regret it."

If Alexander said Hazel was here, she believed him completely.

Alexander chuckled darkly. "They're not going to give her up. Let me handle this. I guarantee they'll talk."

"What do you think you're doing?" The middle-aged man's voice shook, though he tried to mask it with bluster. "Our men will be here any second! If you don't want to start an all-out war, leave now!"

Alexander's smirk deepened as he strode toward the man.

A wave of pressure seemed to radiate off him, so oppressive that the man stumbled back involuntarily.

"We're with Sakra Society! You've got a death wish if you lay a finger on me! If you so much as touch me, our people will..."

The man's tirade cut off as he swung a fist at Alexander, aiming straight for his face.

His punch had some weight behind it, looking like he wasn't just an amateur.

But to Alexander, it was almost laughable. The man's speed and strength were on par with a child's tantrum.

As Alexander stood perfectly still, the man's confidence surged. Seizing the moment, he put everything he had into his punch.

Just as the man's fist was about to land, Alexander moved. A single kick, precise and devastating, sent the man flying backward.

He hit the ground hard several meters away, blood spurting from his mouth as he lay there motionless.

The room froze. Everyone stared at Alexander, the brutal efficiency of his strike leaving them stunned and terrified.

Alexander turned his cold, cutting gaze on the bartender. "You can save yourself some pain and talk now."

The bartender's lips curled into a sneer. His hand darted to his back, pulling a gun.

"Die, you bastard!" he roared, aiming at Alexander and pulling the trigger.

The Sakra Society members burst into laughter, their smug satisfaction plain as they pictured Alexander dropping dead from a headshot.

But instead of a gunshot, there was only the hollow click of an empty chamber.

The laughter died instantly. Everyone's eyes fell on Alexander, who held up the gun's magazine in one hand.

Golden bullets spilled from it, clattering to the floor and rolling in all directions.

No one had even seen Alexander move. Somehow, he'd removed the magazine before anyone could react.
