Chapter 484 Destruction

Seeing Alexander prepare to confront him, Leonard felt not fear, but an exhilarating thrill.

He was determined to make Alexander suffer and beg for mercy.

Bang!

Their palms clashed forcefully.

The card in Leonard's hand erupted with dark energy, flooding into Alexander's palm.

With a triumphant smirk, Leonard retracted his hand, eagerly awaiting Alexander's pained reaction.

When the dark energy touched Alexander, he involuntarily shuddered.

In that instant, he sensed the despair, sadness, anger, and deep-seated hatred packed within that energy.

If he were an ordinary person, these negative emotions would have overwhelmed him, leaving him incapacitated on the ground.

But Alexander was no ordinary man. As those negative energies enveloped him, the Force surged within him.

His palm ignited with pale flames, fueled by the Force.

"The Fire can purify all evil!"

In an instant, the pale flames consumed the dark energy, leaving no trace behind.

Leonard let out a cry as his card shattered into countless fragments.

"Alex, are you okay?" Ava asked anxiously.

"Don't worry, I'm fine. This guy is the one in trouble," Alexander replied.

He turned to the astonished Leonard.

"You... you can wield fire... I see now, you must be a metahuman with flame abilities!"

Leonard looked at Alexander in fear.

Negative energies like witchcraft and curses were inherently suppressed by fire and lightning.

He never imagined that Alexander's Force was burning with a power that was as different from ordinary flames as the sun was from a mere spark.

"You destroyed my death card! I must repay this debt! Even if you are a metahuman, you can't withstand my reversed Hanged Man!"

Leonard produced a silver tarot card.

Whoosh!

The silver card spun rapidly and shot toward Alexander.

Leonard quickly followed up with additional gestures.

"Demon, Judgment, Chariot!"

He unleashed three more tarot cards alongside the previous Hanged Man, all shrouded in dark energy, targeting Alexander's vital points.

Yet, instead of flinching, Alexander stood firm, showing no intention of evasion.

Leonard's spirits soared; he thought Alexander was doomed.

But the next moment, a flash of white light erupted.

Slash! Slash!

In just two strikes, Alexander cleaved through the four tarot cards, turning them into eight halves that spiraled to the ground.

Leonard stood frozen, stammering, "What... what kind of monster are you?"

Alexander smirked.

"Hand over your cursed tarot cards. Tell me what you intended to do with the bracelet, and perhaps I'll let you live."

"You dare!" Leonard exclaimed, a mix of anger and fear flooding his face.

"What's the matter? Reluctant?"

Alexander's gaze was icy, the lightsaber flickering ominously, as if ready to sever Leonard's head.

Feeling the impending threat, Leonard was drenched in cold sweat.

He wanted to fight back but knew that even if he unleashed all his cards, Alexander could easily dismantle them.

"Damn it!"

Leonard sighed, realizing he was defeated.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I can't give you this tarot set. I'll compensate you with five million. Can we..."

"Shut up! Hand over the rest of the cards," Alexander interrupted, pointing the lightsaber at Leonard's throat.

The white blade illuminated Leonard's face, making him look even paler.

"Fine, I'll give them to you. Just don't kill me..."

Leonard trembled, retrieving the remaining cards.

Out of twenty-two cards, five had been destroyed, leaving seventeen.

With a flick of his lightsaber, Alexander sliced the remaining cards in half, igniting them in flames until they turned to ashes.

Leonard's heart sank, filled with regret. This tarot set was a genuine treasure, something he had worked tirelessly to acquire.

Now, with it destroyed, he would no longer have the power to control others or perform accurate readings.
