Chapter 947 A Slim Chance

A cruel smile spread across Jaxon's face. The poison on the needles was a secret concoction he had created himself.

No one other than him knew the antidote. Even if they were rushed to the hospital, it would be a death sentence.

Poison needles were one of the hardest to defend against. Most people wouldn't even see them coming.

Jaxon was confident. He knew Alexander and Cloe would be struck by the poison, and when that happened, they'd come crawling to him for the cure.

But to his shock, Alexander simply waved his hand. The poison needles hit an invisible wall of force and dropped to the ground as if they'd hit stone.

Jaxon froze, stunned.

Alexander gave a dismissive sneer.

"The Ghost is this pathetic? You two clowns thought you could run wild on Treasure Island?"

With another flick of his hand, a powerful surge of force filled the room. The poison needles, now lying on the ground, were sent flying back at even greater speed, faster than they came.

The sound of the needles slicing through the air was deafening, like tearing fabric.

Jaxon and Cynthia didn't even have time to blink. They were caught off guard by the sheer speed and power of Alexander's counterattack.

"Get down!"

Jaxon grabbed Cynthia and yanked her aside, but it was too late.

They dodged most of the needles, but a few still pierced them.

"Damn!" Jaxon gasped.

He fumbled through his jacket and pulled out a small vial, quickly swallowing a few pills. He handed some to Cynthia as well.

Alexander watched the scene with a cold, amused smile.

He wasn't interested in watching them die. He needed them alive, so he could get some answers about the Ghost.

Though both Jaxon and Cynthia had taken the antidote, the poison was potent.

They collapsed to the floor, their bodies shaking uncontrollably, gasping for breath.

Jaxon's glare was still burning with hatred, though now there was a flicker of fear in his eyes. He was defiant, but the situation was getting out of hand.

Alexander turned his head toward Cloe, who immediately understood.

She gave a quick nod and barked out the order, "The auction is over. Please settle your bids at the Brilliant Group later. Everyone else, leave now."

Within moments, a wave of fully armed guards poured in, surrounding the room with lethal intent.

Most of the guests, recognizing Cloe, quickly made their way out, eager to avoid any trouble.

Whether it was the Brilliant Group controlling Treasure Island or the infamous Ghost, neither was a force to be reckoned with. Staying out of their way was the only smart move.

The hall cleared out in minutes.

"Alexander, do you dare kill us? We're with the Ghost!"

Cynthia's voice was lower now, trembling with fear.

Alexander gave her a cold, mocking smile. "I actually like making new friends. How about you stay here and have a little chat about your organization?"

Cynthia's face turned ghostly pale. She raised her voice in a final threat.

"Alexander, do you have any idea what the Ghost really is? The higher-ups of the Ghost are the most powerful figures in the world!

"Do you understand what you're messing with? The Ghost represents the highest power. Gangs, wealthy families... they're nothing compared to us.

"You think you can imprison us?"

Alexander's smile only grew colder. "Quentin is just a low-level member of the Ghost. So what does that make you two? You're not even bottom-tier. You're just the Ghost's lapdogs, aren't you?

"So tell me, do you think the big shots at the top are going to care about you two little dogs?"

Cynthia's eyes widened in shock and panic.

Jaxon's fists were also clenched tightly.

"You really are clueless..."

Alexander chuckled, unhurried, as he strolled toward Cynthia. His calm, confident expression was like he was out for a walk.

"Don't worry. I promise you'll spill everything you know, and you'll die without feeling a thing."

"You!"

Cynthia felt a shiver run down her spine as Alexander's smile grew ever more sinister.

She realized that today, there was no other option but to fight for a slim chance of survival.
