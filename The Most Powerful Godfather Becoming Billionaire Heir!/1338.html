Chapter 1338 Keep Working Hard

Finn was soaring through the air when he suddenly felt a crushing pressure around his waist, causing the protective barrier surrounding him to shatter. With a dull thud, a violent force surged through him, breaking his meridians and bones.

Blood gushed from his mouth as he collapsed to the ground.

With a gaping wound at his waist, Finn writhed in agony, muttering, "I... I won't lose..."

Alexander looked on, his gaze steady yet laced with sarcasm. "Just die," he said slowly, his voice cold. "I'll make sure Julius joins you soon."

"You... you..." Finn's words faltered before he breathed his last.

After a moment of calm, Alexander carefully scanned the grove, ensuring there were no witnesses. He then turned his attention to the four bodies.

A grin spread across Alexander's face as he counted the money he'd acquired—over 10 million dollars. It was a substantial sum, a real windfall.

After all, Finn had been a level-three martial arts expert and the housekeeper of the Marshal's Mansion. Along with the cash, Alexander had also stumbled upon a manual for the powerful Smashing Palm technique, a small bronze plaque, and several vials.

He had no use for the skill himself, but he could pass the Smashing Palm Technique on to Logan.

The bronze plaque intrigued him; it measured 5.5 inches long and 1.5 inches wide, featuring an engraving on one side of a man and woman locked in an embrace.

The man wore a lewd expression, while the woman possessed a beautiful face and an appealing figure. On the reverse side, the words "Royal" and "Secret" were inscribed, but no matter how he examined it, its purpose eluded him.

Several small bottles bore labels like Life-Saving Pills and Corpse-Dissolving Powder.

Alexander felt satisfied.

He dug a hole to bury the assorted swords and daggers scattered on the ground before opening a bottle of Corpse-Dissolving Powder. He sprinkled a small amount onto Finn's bloodied chest.

A plume of green smoke erupted, and Finn's body began to corrode and dissolve, quickly transforming into a pool of black liquid.

After sealing the bottle tightly, Alexander dragged the remaining bodies over and tossed them into the dark water, watching as more green smoke billowed up.

At that moment, Alexander recognized the Corpse-Dissolving Powder as an essential tool for eliminating witnesses and erasing traces of his deeds.

Once he had disposed of the bodies and cleaned up the scene, he felt a twinge of concern for Logan.

Scanning the area for signs of life and finding none, he summoned the Force, employing his Spirit Step Evolution to leap towards Emerald Manor.

Fifteen minutes later, he arrived at Emerald Manor.

Logan was sprawled on the large bed, rubbing his sleepy eyes. "What brings you here in the middle of the night?" he asked.

Alexander playfully draped an arm over Logan's shoulders and smiled, "I missed you."

Logan grimaced. "I like women."

Alexander pulled out a martial arts manual from his pocket and tossed it onto Logan's belly.

Logan looked at it and asked, "Where did you get this?"

Alexander recounted the entire story. Logan, fuming, waved his arms. "How dare he impersonate me to write a letter? Does he have a death wish?"

"Logan, you need to practice martial arts," Alexander replied. "Be cautious when I'm not around at Emerald Manor."

"Don't worry, Alexander. I've become a level-one warrior and received the Spiritual Awakening. I'm doing fine," Logan reassured him.

"I have 10 million dollars. If you need money, just let me know," Alexander offered.

"I'm managing well here. Besides, I have my own funds. Keep the money for yourself. By the way, Alexander, I bought a small gold medal. It's quite exquisite. Do you want it?" Logan asked.

"No, keep it for yourself. While it looks nice, I'm not sure what the word 'Interception' on the back means," Alexander replied.

The two of them lay on the bed, chatting away. As the conversation continued, Logan drifted off.

Exhausted from the recent battle, Alexander soon drifted into a deep sleep.

In the morning, he was greeted warmly by everyone at Emerald Manor, which filled him with joy. Since arriving in Sahasia, this manor had become a place of deep connection for him.

Logan rubbed his nose and said with a hint of grievance, "Alexander, it's been months since I've seen you. Don't rush off. Let's hang out a bit."

After a brief pause, Alexander considered that he had just become a level-three warrior the day before and showed no signs of advancing to the fourth level yet. Perhaps he should take a break.

"Alright. Call Jalen. Let's go have some fun," Alexander said, raising his hands and laughing heartily.
