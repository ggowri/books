Chapter 520 Bearing the Insult

Joyce laughed softly alongside him.

"Felix, you're the chairman of the Red Umbrella Group, aren't you? How come you're so timid? Did Alexander scare you that badly?"

Felix's face turned stormy as he clenched his fists tightly.

He swore that one day, he would repay this humiliation to the entire Morris family a hundred times over.

But for now, he kept his composure, careful not to let Barry see his anger.

Instead, he smiled politely. "Mr. Morris, would you like to rest a bit before hitting the casino?"

Barry glanced at Felix with a smirk. "Sure, we'll rest a bit first."

At that moment, Felix took a couple of steps closer to Barry.

Suddenly, an icy surge of hostility enveloped him, like a cold snap.

"Hiss!"

Felix sucked in a sharp breath, his body tensing like a coiled spring, ready to snap.

Turning, he saw Diego glaring at him.

Diego sneered and said, "Mr. Vanderbilt, please don't get too close. Keep your distance. For the master's safety, I won't hesitate to take action."

Felix kept a stony face and stayed silent.

No matter how much he bore these insults, or curbed his temper, he still had his pride.

"What? You don't think I'm right?"

Diego's presence grew even more menacing, his gaze fixed harshly on Felix.

Felix shot him a fierce glare, their eyes clashing with an almost visible spark.

Barry, who was walking ahead, stopped and looked back at Felix with a mock smile.

"Mr. Vanderbilt, what are you doing? Diego's just concerned for my safety. Do you have a problem with that?"

Felix trembled with anger, his fury building.

He held back for a long moment before finally swallowing his rage.

"No worries, Mr. Morris. I just misunderstood. No problem at all."

Barry nodded in satisfaction.

Joyce shot Felix a scornful look, muttering under her breath.

"Glad you got it. If you're still feeling defiant, we can go for a round..."

Even this decorative little secretary looked down on him.

Felix's anger toward Barry and the others grew even stronger. He swore to himself that these clueless fools would pay for their arrogance.

Barry led the way, with Diego and Joyce flanking him on either side, while Felix trailed behind like a loyal lapdog.

Right next to the Kaylon Hotel was the Kaylon Casino, touted as the largest in town, offering every kind of leisure and entertainment.

As long as you had money, you could gamble here and be surrounded by beautiful women, with celebrities, models, and influencers often present.

These temptations for men came at a clear price, a straightforward money-for-pleasure deal.

Unsurprisingly, the Kaylon Casino became wildly popular soon after it opened.

Ordinary folks couldn't even get in; it offered everything, but at a steep cost.

Waves, beaches, sunshine, bikini-clad beauties, and a variety of gourmet foods—an absolute paradise for the wealthy.

At that moment, Barry entered his suite.

The top floor had several suites, the largest one spanning over 300 square yards.

Felix acted like a hotel concierge, personally opening the door to welcome Barry with a smile plastered on his face, as if he'd forgotten the earlier tension.

Joyce waved at Felix impatiently.

"Enough, stop fussing! You can go now. Make sure the hotel sends over the lunch menu so we can decide what to eat."

"Alright, I'll have them send the menu over shortly."

Felix nodded with a smile and turned to leave, showing no hint of anger as if he were born to serve them.

Barry watched Felix obediently leave the room like a servant and even call for the menu afterward.

From beginning to end, Felix was more polite and attentive than any hotel staff, keeping a charming smile the whole time.

When it was all over, the smile on Barry's face vanished, replaced by cold indifference.
