Chapter 172 Baiting

"Ladies and gentlemen, our first auction item today is a stunning diamond necklace, meticulously designed and crafted by the renowned jeweler, Jude Grant."

The large screen displayed the dazzling necklace, its alluring sparkle captivating everyone in the room.

"The opening bid is set at 560 thousand dollars, with a minimum increment of 10 thousand. Let’s begin!"

As the auctioneer's voice echoed, paddles shot up around the room, and in the end, the necklace sold for an impressive 700 thousand.

Alexander watched in disbelief. Spending 700 thousand on a necklace seemed utterly outrageous to most people.

The world had become starkly divided between the wealthy and the poor, with the rich indulging in their luxury while the less fortunate struggled to survive.

Next up was a piece of antique pottery from three centuries ago.

However, Alexander's interest waned as he eagerly waited for the coveted yellow gemstone.

Before he knew it, three items had already been sold, and one had gone unsold, yet the prized yellow gemstone remained elusive.

Noticing Alexander's growing impatience, Ava leaned in with a reassuring smile.

"Don't rush it; that yellow gemstone is worth at least tens of millions. They always save the best for last."

Alexander nodded, muttering, "I just hope I don't have to fight too many people for it. I want to grab it at a reasonable price."

Ava smiled. It was Alexander's first auction, and he was underestimating how competitive it could get.

Rare gemstones like that were highly sought after, and whether for collection or for a master jeweler to craft, they could significantly appreciate in value.

Thus, the exceptional yellow gemstone undeniably became the object of pursuit among the wealthy and powerful gathered in the room.

Just then, the auctioneer called for the next item: a meteorite the size of a basketball.

Surprised by this unexpected twist, Alexander focused intently. Using the Force, he sensed a high metal content within the meteorite.

"Ladies and gentlemen, we recently acquired this fascinating meteorite. The starting bid is five million, with a minimum increment of 50 thousand!" the auctioneer announced with a grin.

Without hesitation, Alexander raised his paddle. "Five point one million!"

His bid immediately drew attention from the crowd.

Whispers erupted as people speculated, "Does Alexander have a thing for meteorites?

Could there be a connection to the magic powder?"

"Five point two million!"

"Five point five million!"

"Six million..."

The price soared, all thanks to Alexander's bidding.

"Eight million!" he exclaimed, jumping the bid by two million, clearly determined to secure the meteorite.

With the backing of the Saint Group and the magic powder, Alexander felt confident that no one in Loscity could outbid him financially.

The room fell silent; after all, it was just a meteorite. While it had scientific value, the big players in the room weren't exactly researchers and had little interest in collecting such items.

The bidding frenzy was entirely driven by Alexander.

Just as it seemed the meteorite would fall into his hands, a cold, challenging voice broke through the silence.

"Eight point one million!"

The crowd turned to see David casually lounging with his legs crossed, a cigar pinched between his fingers, wearing a smug expression as he locked eyes with Alexander.

Ava leaned in with concern. "Did you cross David? What happened?"

Alexander scratched his head, reluctant to mention Mary's involvement.

He recalled how Mary had filled him in on the altercation with David after their last encounter.

"Nine million!" Alexander shot back, raising the stakes once more.

Gasps filled the room as the crowd buzzed with excitement.

"Can you believe this? This meteorite's max value is around seven million, and here it is hitting nine million. Is Alexander just throwing money around?"

"Seems like it. But the real story is the showdown between David and Alexander."

Many spectators were eager to see how this would end.

Although Alexander had the magic powder, there was an underlying tension in the room. While everyone outwardly acted as allies, deep down, many felt that David's power and status far surpassed Alexander’s.

David hesitated for a moment, his expression darkening as he raised his bidding paddle once again.

"Nine point one million!"

This price had already pushed him past his mental limit; to him, it was just a worthless chunk of rock. His bidding was more about baiting Alexander than genuine interest.

But for Alexander, that stone was priceless. The more he could gather, the better off he'd be in forging a Lightsaber.

Just as Alexander was about to increase his bid, he was caught off guard when Ava shot her paddle into the air.

"Ten million!"
