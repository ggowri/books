Chapter 718 Dismembered

Only after the three had walked far away did Ken finally let out a long breath, quickly wiping away the beads of sweat on his forehead.

He glared at Jane in annoyance. "This is all your fault. You nearly got me killed. Now don't even think about leaving Loscity."

Jane panicked. If they couldn't leave Loscity, she and Belinda would eventually get infected and meet a terrible end.

"Ken, please wait..."

Jane tried desperately to reason with him.

Ken violently shook off her hand and stormed away, cursing under his breath.

No one noticed the man in his thirties who started following him.

He wore an ugly mask and walked with a pronounced limp, carrying a machete with a sharkskin sheath in his right hand.

Bruno hobbled behind Ken, muttering to himself, "Men who bully women deserve to die..."

He kept whispering while stroking the machete.

He was so unremarkable that no one paid him any attention. Even Alexander hadn't noticed his presence.

"Alexander is truly evil, abandoning his ex-wife like that. Ava must have been deceived by him. Alexander deserves to die!

"Mom, don't worry. I'll fulfill your dying wish and restore your honor in the Hawkins family!"

A murderous glint flashed in Bruno's eyes.

He quickened his pace and bumped into Ken from behind.

At that moment, Ken heard an ice-cold voice whisper in his ear, "When you get to hell, remember the name Bruno Hawkins. In your next life, don't abuse women! Try being a decent person for once!"

Those were the last words Ken would ever hear.

In the next instant, his body froze, unable to take another step. Then came the excruciating pain, and everything went black...

Ken's body shattered like scattered building blocks. His dismembered remains spread across the ground as blood gushed everywhere.

"Oh God! Murder!"

The surrounding crowd erupted in terrified screams. No one had any idea what had just happened.

Bruno's face remained expressionless as he turned and walked in the direction Alexander had gone, his eyes gleaming with deadly intent.

"You're dead, Alexander. You don't deserve a perfect woman like Ava!"

By this time, Alexander had reached the parking lot and was about to get in his car.

He suddenly stopped and turned toward the airport.

"I sense a murderous intent. Someone's dead. You two head back first! Make sure Ava and Ashley are safe."

After instructing the women, Alexander rushed back to the airport entrance.

Even from hundreds of yards away, the smell of blood hit him like a wave.

Police tape was already up, with officers holding back the crowd that had gathered.

Alexander approached and found the dismembered corpse on the ground.

He recognized it immediately as Ken. "How had he ended up dead?"

Alexander scanned the area but saw no sign of Jane or Belinda.

"Alex!" A familiar, sweet voice called out.

It was Melin. The moment she spotted Alexander, she jogged over excitedly.

Her police uniform hugged her curves perfectly, drawing appreciative glances from the men around them.

"I just got transferred here from the hospital. Running into you must be fate!" she exclaimed.

Without hesitation, Melin threw herself into Alexander's arms and planted several quick kisses on him.

Alexander wrapped his arms around her waist, his hands resting on her hips as he chuckled, "Easy there, Ms. Ward. We're in public, remember?"

Melin's cheeks flushed as she reluctantly pulled away.

"What's the situation here?"

Alexander quickly changed the subject, gesturing to the body on the ground.

Melin's expression immediately turned serious.

She furrowed her brow and explained, "We're dealing with a skilled killer. The victim was dismembered in broad daylight, suggesting incredibly fast work. We're currently looking for potential witnesses."

Then Melin pulled Alexander aside, her voice filled with concern.

"Why did you come back? This epidemic is terrifying! They're about to lock down the city! And where's your mask? You could get infected."

She pulled out a mask and tried to put it on him, but he refused.

Alexander, protected by the Force, wasn't worried about any disease.

He looked around at the anxious, tense faces surrounding them and said firmly, "I grew up in Loscity. All my memories are here. I won't leave, and I won't let this place become a ghost town. I'll get to the bottom of this!"
