Chapter 461 Obsidian Core

Streams of the Force flowed from Alexander's hands into Charlize. Saving a life was urgent—now wasn't the time to worry about conserving energy.

Half an hour passed without Alexander even noticing...

Outside, everyone was getting antsy. Dr. Peter Sherwood, in particular, was ready to bust the door down.

Just then, the door opened, revealing Alexander's tired face.

With his current strength, he'd barely managed to stabilize Charlize's condition.

To fully heal her, he needed to use the Ultimate Legacy techniques and prepare a healing ointment known as Obsidian Balm.

During Warrior training, lightsaber burns were common—even with protective energy fields.

Obsidian Balm worked wonders for burns, but it required a rare black gemstone called Obsidian Core.

Dr. Sherwood practically barreled into the room first, unable to hold back.

The others followed right behind, crowding around Charlize's bed.

Charlize, now awake, looked startled. Her voice was weak as she spoke.

"Wh-what are you all doing?"

Dr. Neal and the rest were frozen, unable to believe what they were seeing.

"He really brought her back... it's like a miracle!"

Amazement spread among the crowd, and admiration for Alexander filled the room.

A moment ago, Dr. Sherwood had been sneering at Alexander—now, his face was bright red with shame.

"Thank you... thank you for saving me..."

Charlize's voice wavered, tears rolling down her cheeks.

Alexander spoke softly, reassuring her, "Don't worry. I'll make sure you recover completely—no scars, no side effects."

"Really?"

Charlize's eyes widened, hope brightening her once-dull gaze.

Alexander instructed the medical staff to take good care of her, then called Xavier Ulysses, asking if he had any Obsidian Core.

Xavier sighed over the line, "Alex, we've got plenty of regular black gemstones at Phoenix Gemstone Establishment, but Obsidian Core is incredibly rare. Still, don't worry—we'll search high and low."

After hanging up, Alexander frowned.

"This is tricky. Charlize's injuries are severe. Even though I've stabilized her, we can't drag this out. Without Obsidian Core, I can't fully heal her."

Ava hesitated like she wanted to say something but was holding back.

Alexander noticed and asked gently, "What is it? What's on your mind?"

Ava bit her lip before speaking, "My mom has a necklace made of Obsidian Core... but she's in Tijuanea, and I haven't talked to her in a long time."

Alexander could tell there was more to it—modern tech made keeping in touch easy, so distance alone wasn't the issue.

Ava took a deep breath, her expression conflicted, "For Charlize, I'll take you to Tijuanea to see her."

"Thank you. I'll pay whatever it takes to get it."

Alexander pulled Ava into his arms.

The next morning, Alexander and Ava left Loscity early, heading to Tijuanea.

Ashley stayed with Betty Rowe. Rosalia, Logan, and Marvin, along with dozens of their top men, handled security, making sure nothing—not even a fly—could get into the villa.

At the airport, a group of people waited respectfully—Timothy and Arthur were at the front.

Since Alexander's last punishment, which made Timothy experience pain worse than death, Timothy had turned on the Mafia and fully pledged loyalty to Alexander.

"Boss, welcome back to Tijuanea!"

Timothy kept his head down, rushing to grab their luggage like a hotel porter.

A trace of Alexander's Force always lingered inside him—a way for Alexander to keep control.

Whenever that energy acted up, Timothy would seize up, feeling like his bones were shattering. It was pure agony.

As much as Timothy wanted to kill Alexander, he didn't dare.

Alexander could crush him like a bug, and even if, by some miracle, Timothy got rid of this walking bane, who would take away the excruciating pain?

"Boss, I've already booked the best hotel—the presidential suite, of course."

Timothy flashed a grin, his gaze flicking toward Ava, who was at Alexander's side.
