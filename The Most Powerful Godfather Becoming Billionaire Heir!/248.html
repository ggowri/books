Chapter 248 The Scepter

Guzman drew a dagger and cut Cloe's wrist.

Crimson blood flowed out, dripping onto the ground.

Something eerie happened. The blood was absorbed as if by a sponge, leaving no trace on the floor but forming a line of blood that converged into the skull of the scepter.

The initially pale skull emitted a strange red glow. Everyone watched the scene, not daring to breathe.

Guzman stared blankly at the scene before him. Memories surged through his mind like a tidal wave.

Over twenty years ago, Guzman had just come of age when the Godfather found him and forcibly sent him to the battlefield.

"Please... I don't want to fight. I don't want to kill, nor be killed... I can't bear it!"

Guzman begged desperately but to no avail.

The man nicknamed "Reaper" left him with only one sentence.

"A man should act like a man. You are my eldest son. You are absolutely not allowed to be a coward!"

Guzman had no choice but to be forced into the military.

Over twenty years have passed, and the memories in Guzman's mind are still incredibly vivid.

Artillery raged, and flesh and blood flew. He watched his comrades fall one by one, only to be captured by a group of women.

Guzman, who had initially felt fortunate, soon realized they were a group of detestable female demons.

Guzman was still filled with fear to this day, unable to let go, and often woke up from nightmares.

These women belonged to a small tribe. Their tribe had been fighting for over a decade and had no adult males left.

They were in their prime, and their tribe needed to continue its bloodline. After discussing, these women decided to keep the rather handsome Guzman.

During the interrogation, Guzman suffered their torture and humiliation from those women.

Although Guzman tried his best to resist, facing more than a dozen strong women, he still could not escape.

In less than three days, he became much thinner and could not satisfy these ravenous women.

Guzman suffered for a month. He thought he would die at their hands until intense gunfire erupted outside the camp.

He finally found a chance to escape, but he had barely run a few hundred yards when he was caught by several women and taken to an off-road vehicle.

Guzman was bound and held in the vehicle all night.

Finally, they stopped at a weathered cave in the desert.

This vast cave had a rare water source, and the temperature was much lower than the scorching heat outside.

Upon entering the cave, they chained Guzman to a stone pillar with long iron chains.

The leading woman, named Adara, was beautiful in her twenties.

She grabbed Guzman by the collar and warned sternly, "Boy, if you dare to run away again, I will break your legs."

Guzman shook his head in terror.

That night, five women humiliated him again.

From that day on, the cave became Guzman's hell.

In a relaxed state, they became more and more brutal and inventive, making Guzman feel agonizing every day.

One day, the women discovered activity nearby and went out on alert, leaving Guzman alone.

He accidentally found a small arm-length, two-finger-thick black short stick under a round slab of rock.

In the middle of the short stick was a black circular protrusion. Guzman pressed it gently, and the stick suddenly extended at both ends, turning into a black scepter.

A large pale skull inexplicably appeared on top of the scepter.

How could such a large skull be hidden inside a small stick? Guzman was puzzled at the time.

The other end of the scepter was very sharp, and the scepter, taller than a person, could definitely be used as a spear.

Accidentally acquiring a decent cold weapon, Guzman thought about escaping again.

Due to the torture of those women, his lower abdomen would often bleed. Malnourished from hunger, he was in poor health.

If he stayed here for a long time, he would definitely be tortured to death.

Guzman did not act rashly. If he failed to escape again, these female demons might really break his legs.
