Chapter 1298 Catching the Thieves

"Alexander! Alexander, wake up! Don't scare me!"

Alexander struggled to open his eyes. Through the haze, he saw a boy about thirteen or fourteen years old staring at him with wide, anxious eyes. The boy had a round face, his small eyes filled with worry.

Alexander jolted awake. "Who are you?"

"It's me! We've turned into kids!"

Alexander studied the familiar chubby face. "You're... Logan?"

He glanced down at himself. His clothes were suddenly two sizes too big, hanging loosely over his frame. He pulled his hands out from his oversized sleeves.

The moment he saw them, he let out a startled yelp. "Are these even my hands?

"Why are they so small? They're soft and pale... These don't look like an adult's hands at all!"

Shaking his head, he tried to clear his thoughts and quickly got to his feet. This was beyond bizarre.

His clothes now fit him like a sack, but it wasn't the clothes that had changed. His body had shrunk. He looked like a teenager, no older than fifteen or sixteen.

Logan scratched his head nervously.

"Alexander, weren't we just at the movies? One minute we were watching, and then your helmet malfunctioned and zapped us. Next thing I know, I wake up here... and I'm a kid!"

Alexander frowned and sighed. "Something feels off. Logan, where are we? Why is it so dark?"

"See for yourself!" Logan nudged him and glanced around warily.

"What the hell..."

Alexander looked up. The night sky stretched above them, speckled with stars and a waning crescent moon. They stood in the middle of a farmland, surrounded by rows of watermelons.

The air was thick and humid. Even in short sleeves, sweat trickled down their backs. In the distance, flickering lights outlined what seemed to be a cluster of buildings, though the details were too blurry to make out. The tallest among them was a towering pagoda, its peak glowing faintly in shifting hues of seven colors.

"Alexander, what do we do?" Logan asked, looking dazed.

Alexander gritted his teeth. "I don't even know if we're still in Loscity!"

The watermelons at their feet weren't very big, but they were perfectly round, releasing a faint sweet scent. Their dark green rinds gleamed under the night sky as if coated in a thin layer of luminescence.

"Alexander, let's just eat one first. I'm dying of thirst!"

Logan, less concerned about their predicament, crouched down and started picking through the melons.

Since it was too dark to see clearly, he resorted to tapping them one by one. Just as he plucked one from the vine, a crisp jingling sound echoed in the air.

Within seconds, the glow of torches flared in the distance, and a group of people came charging toward them. "Thieves! Catch the melon thieves!" someone bellowed.

"Oh, crap! Are they talking about us?"

Logan rolled his eyes.

Alexander's brow twitched. "Stay calm. Let's see how this plays out."

A dozen figures soon surrounded them, some wielding wooden clubs, others gripping torches.

Leading the group was a young friar, wearing a yellow cap and a long red robe. Black leather boots covered his feet. His name was Donald Wachtell.

The rest of the men were dressed in simple, short-sleeved linen shirts, clearly laborers.

The friar had a broad, fleshy face and eyes that burned with menace. He jabbed a finger at them. "You little thieves! Finally caught you in the act!"

Seeing how fierce the friar looked, Logan instinctively hid behind Alexander.

Alexander, however, remained calm and asked in an even tone, "Sir, could you tell me where we are?"

The friar's eyes widened in outrage. "Still playing dumb? Tie them up!"

"Yes, Father Wachtell!"

The workers surged forward.

Logan tried to resist, but Alexander noticed a faint glow emanating from the friar's palm. His stomach clenched. He quickly grabbed Logan's arm. "Don't move!"

Alexander's mind raced. He thought, "Even if this is some elaborate trick, no magic show could actually shrink people... And that glow...this guy may be dangerous. We shouldn't act rashly."

Before they could do anything, the men tackled them to the ground. Their hands were yanked behind their backs, bound tightly with rough hemp ropes.

"Ow! Hey, not so tight!" Logan yelped.

Alexander glanced down at the rope biting into his shoulders, his eyes darkening. He shot a cold look at the friar.

A pockmarked worker leaned in close to the friar and muttered, "Father Wachtell, look at their strange clothes. And they're speaking some foreign tongue. These two must be runaway Risuneses slaves."
