Chapter 137 Appeasing

Jimbo let out a twisted grin. "Heh, Drake is my master, but I'm still a renowned gray wizard. Better spill it, kid, or I might get rough."

Alexander scoffed. "Gray wizard? Bring it on if you've got the guts!"

Jimbo's face twisted in anger, and his wand shook violently in his grip.

"You bastard! Go to hell!" With a fierce swing of his wand, Jimbo attacked.

A swarm of gray beetles burst out of his wand, clouding the air as they swarmed towards Alexander.

Each beetle was about the size of a soybean, covered in ugly, blotchy markings.

"Alexander, you're done for! One bite, and you'll be poisoned instantly. I'll make sure you beg for death. Let's see if you still have that big mouth..."

But before he could finish, Jimbo froze in shock, staring wide-eyed at Alexander.

"What... what's happening?"

The swarm of venomous beetles buzzed around Alexander but couldn't get closer than a meter.

Alexander surrounded himself with the Force, creating a tough, invisible barrier around him.

Poisonous bugs like these couldn't possibly break through a Force shield.

Alexander calmly started swatting the beetles away.

Since his hands were wrapped in the Force, he had no fear of the poison.

The beetles cracked and burst with every slap. Alexander swatted them down like they were nothing more than mosquitoes.

"Well, these venomous beetles aren't all that impressive, are they?" Alexander said, feigning surprise.

Jimbo was speechless, nearly choking with frustration.

He had gone to great lengths to breed these venomous beetles, each as deadly as a cobra with a single touch.

Yet, Alexander was swatting them dead without so much as a scratch.

Alexander continued his taunting. "Hey! Is this all a gray wizard can do? Maybe you should scuttle back to the City of Sorcery before you embarrass yourself."

"Damn you!" Jimbo was seething with fury.

Gritting his teeth, he snapped his wand and pulled out a small gray figurine the size of his thumb.

Biting his finger, he smeared blood over the figurine while muttering under his breath.

The figurine seemed to grin creepily at Alexander, exuding a strange aura.

Suddenly, Alexander felt a wave of dizziness, and his body weakened. The figurine's eerie smile grew even more twisted.

"So, this is sorcery?" Surprised, he quickly channeled the Force, immediately feeling reinvigorated.

Sorcery might tap into supernatural powers, but the Force is drawn from the essence of nature itself, pure and powerful.

Compared side-by-side, the Force is undoubtedly stronger and more refined.

"How...how are you still standing? Are you some kind of monster?" Jimbo's eyes widened in terror.

His sorcery figurine, a weapon crafted from his own heart's blood, could control others' minds and actions.

Every sorcerer had their unique trump card, and for Jimbo, it was this figurine. It was the key to his rise as a gray wizard.

But now, facing Alexander, he found his figurine utterly useless.

Normally, Alexander should have fallen into an unconscious stupor from the curse.

Yet Alexander looked unfazed, staring at the figurine with piercing intensity.

Jimbo felt a chill, beads of sweat forming on his forehead.

"Alexander... hey, this was just a misunderstanding, okay? I'll tell my master all about it and make sure we get along peacefully..." Jimbo forced a pitiful smile at Alexander.

Alexander couldn't help but laugh at how shameless Jimbo had become.

"A misunderstanding? Didn't you just say you'd make me wish for death?"

"Mr. Hawthorne, since that sorcery figurine didn't work, you must be a dark wizard, right? That bastard Sebastian hid your strength and status from us!"

Jimbo switched to a flattering tone, his earlier aggression nowhere to be seen, acting as if he'd always been mild-tempered.

"The City of Sorcery forbids wizards from harming each other. This is clearly just a misunderstanding. Can't we let bygones be bygones?"
