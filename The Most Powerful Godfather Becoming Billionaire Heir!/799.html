Chapter 799 Annihilation

"We... we have... over 30 people in Loscity..." The young man stammered, avoiding Alexander's gaze.

"What's your name? Why are you in Loscity instead of staying in Risun? Tell me everything you know. Maybe I'll let you die a quick death," Alexander said, pressing his foot onto the man's shoulder and looking down at him with a mocking smile.

He added, "If you lie, I have ways to make you wish for death."

The man's psychological defenses were already shattered. Under Alexander's threats, he could barely hold it together.

"I'll tell you everything. My name is Harvey Norgate. I'm Zora's personal assistant," he said.

Alexander chuckled, "Personal assistant? And what does that mean?"

Harvey, trembling, responded, "It means I help her with everything and spend nights with her."

Sizing up Harvey, Alexander thought the guy wasn't bad-looking. No wonder he'd been chosen as Zora's assistant.

"Where is Zora?"

"I'll tell you everything. She's rented a villa nearby, and she's guarded by eight bodyguards."

Harvey answered every question, and Alexander quickly had all the information he needed.

Alexander said casually, "Alright. Since you've been honest, I'll give you a quick end."

"No. Please don't kill me!" Harvey's face went pale as he struggled to escape.

"I despise garbage like you who sell out women," Alexander said.

His foot came down, and a surge of Force shattered Harvey's heart.

Harvey collapsed silently to the ground, lifeless.

"Boss, should we go get Zora right now?" Marvin asked, rolling up his sleeves in excitement.

Logan also looked eager.

He said, "These bastards dared to ambush us. We have to take them all out."

Everyone was eager to take revenge on Zora.

Alexander chuckled, "Marvin, Logan, you two come with me. The rest should clean up this place. As for these prisoners..."

His cold gaze swept over the remaining captives, who immediately pleaded for mercy.

Finally, Alexander waved his hand. "The Treasure Island needs laborers. Send them to work there for the rest of their lives."

The captives sighed in relief, spared from death but condemned to a lifetime of hard labor.

They were all devastated.

Alexander turned away, taking Marvin and Logan to leave the base.

An hour later, Marvin parked the car and looked back at Alexander.

He announced, "Boss, this should be the place."

Alexander glanced out of the window, spotting a villa in the distance, and nodded.

After Marvin parked the car, Alexander instructed them to guard the front and back entrances, ensuring no one could escape.

Alexander scanned the villa with his special vision. Sensing no immediate danger, he climbed over the fence and approached the front door with steady, measured steps.

If someone measured his strides, he would find each of them perfectly even, down to the millimeter.

His vision power illuminated the villa's interior.

It spanned around 200 square meters, simply but elegantly furnished.

After a moment, he pressed the doorbell.

Hurried footsteps approached from inside. Zora knew he had arrived.

"Mr. Hawthorne, what a surprise! I didn't expect you to find this place. Welcome!" She opened the door and greeted him warmly.

She was dressed in a silk nightgown that left little to the imagination, her ample curves swaying with each step.

But despite her warm exterior, Alexander noticed the fear and anxiety in her eyes.

"Sorry for the late visit, but 20 or so thugs tried causing trouble tonight, so I couldn't sleep. I thought I'd come to ask what's going on, Ms. Jared," Alexander replied with a courteous smile.

Zora's face, however, remained tense, her smile forced and uneasy.
