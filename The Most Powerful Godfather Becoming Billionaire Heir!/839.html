Chapter 839 Get on Your Knees

Keith was lying on the ground when he noticed a shadow cast over him. Someone was approaching.

"Do you want to rise above the crowd? Do you want the top ten actresses to bow to you?"

Alexander's voice broke the silence, his deep eyes glinting with an unusual light.

"Yes! That's my dream, and I'll make it come true!"

Keith nodded vigorously. He couldn't explain why, but this man, who appeared to be about the same age as him, exuded an irresistible charisma.

The sunlight streamed in from behind Alexander, making him look almost like a god descended to earth.

"If I help you achieve your dream, are you willing to submit to me?"

Alexander asked in an even tone.

Keith hesitated for a moment and then looked at Alexander with suspicion. "Who are you? Why would you want to help me?"

"You don't need to know why. Just kneel and swear your loyalty to me, and I'll help you fulfill your wish."

Alexander replied with an air of absolute confidence.

Keith was bewildered. He thought, "Who is this guy?

He seems kind of unreliable. Maybe he's a lunatic?

Or is he just bored and messing with me?"

Before he could react further, Marvin grabbed him by the collar and growled.

"Keith, do you recognize me? Are you stupid? When Sir tells you to kneel, you kneel! Call him Sir, and you'll benefit from it."

"Cousin? What are you doing here?"

Keith stared at Marvin in shock, unable to believe his eyes.

Marvin slapped him. "Cut the nonsense and get on your knees!"

He pressed Keith down until he was kneeling in front of Alexander.

Keith, now genuinely frightened, stammered.

"Why are you doing this? What's in it for you?"

"In it for me?"

Alexander smiled and said plainly.

"I only want your unwavering loyalty. You'll be my pawn in Azuran. Helping you rise above others is merely my gift to you.

"I'll ask you one last time. Are you willing?"

Alexander's penetrating gaze locked onto Keith, a strange light flashing in his deep eyes, causing Keith's mind to blur.

Keith was just an ordinary person. He couldn't possibly resist Alexander's psychic control using the Force.

"I swear my loyalty to you! I'll serve you, no matter the cost!"

Keith's voice trembled as he knelt straight before Alexander, bowing respectfully without daring to look up.

Alexander nodded in satisfaction.

"Good. From now on, your life is mine. Unless I want you dead, even death won't take you. And if you dare betray me, you won't have to worry about me. Your cousin will take care of you himself."

Marvin's cold glare and the murderous vibe he exuded made Keith shudder.

Keith turned pale with fright, cold sweat dripping down his back.

"Let's go. We're heading to Dragon Gang!"

Alexander commanded, turning to leave.

Keith remained frozen, muttering to himself, "Could he be a high-ranking leader in the Dragon Gang?"

"What are you spacing out for? Move!"

Marvin gave him a shove.

"Dragon Gang? Are we really going there? Does this mean... I might actually achieve my dream?"

Keith's excitement bubbled over as he quickly followed.

At this moment, he felt his heart filled with strength, joy, and excitement.

"Relax. I told you I'd help you achieve your dream, and I will."

Alexander said casually, even without turning his head. He knew what Keith was thinking.

"Thank you, Sir... Thank you..."

Keith's face flushed with excitement, his words stumbling as he struggled to contain his emotions.

He followed closely behind Alexander, watching his graceful figure, his confident stride, and his commanding presence, once again deeply amazed.

He thought, "No wonder even someone like my cousin is completely obedient to him."

Keith's heart was filled with awe and admiration.

Submission to strength was deeply ingrained in the Risun people's culture, and Keith was no exception.

What's more, Alexander had used the Force of psychic control on him.

"Is this the Dragon Gang's headquarters?"

Alexander stopped in front of a towering skyscraper over a hundred stories tall.

"Sir, ordinary people can't get in there. What should we do now?"

Keith asked cautiously.
