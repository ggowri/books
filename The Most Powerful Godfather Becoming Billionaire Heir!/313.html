Chapter 313 Impressive Swordsmanship!

With a simple kick, swift and powerful, it struck the muscular man's shin.

Crack!

The sharp sound of breaking bone echoed.

His leg snapped, the jagged white bone protruding out, and he fell hard to the ground.

He was in so much pain that he couldn't even scream. Just as he managed to catch his breath and prepare to shout, Alexander kicked his throat.

The horrifying sound of another fracture resounded, and the man lay motionless, head drooping.

The remaining group was utterly stunned. They had seen their companion charge forward like a tiger, gaining the upper hand, only to be knocked down by Alexander in an instant and then dealt a fatal blow.

A flicker of fear shone in the woman's eyes. Although she didn't like that man, she knew his power was on par with her own.

They were all battle-tested Shadow Warriors, ranked among the top ten in the entire Red Umbrella Group.

Yet, in a mere encounter, one had been killed.

Alexander's expression remained calm as if he had merely done something trivial.

He looked coldly at the woman. "You're a Shadow Warrior of the Red Umbrella Group?"

"You figure that out?"

The woman realized she couldn't hide it any longer, her palms and soles drenched in cold sweat, feeling her grip on the katana slipping.

"You and he are Shadow Warriors, and there are two gray wizards with you. Is the Red Umbrella Group linked to the City of Sorcery?"

Alexander scrutinized them, his gaze sharp.

An overwhelming pressure suddenly filled the space as if the air itself had thickened.

The woman couldn't help but swallow hard. His gaze was terrifying as if he could see through all her hidden secrets.

She had only felt such a chilling sensation with Felix Vanderbilt, the chairman of the Red Umbrella Group.

"Since you're here, let's not waste time. Attack," Alexander said casually.

At his words, the woman felt a jolt in her heart.

"Go!"

No longer hesitating, she shouted, slashing her katana toward Alexander's left shoulder.

The blade glimmered like snow, slicing through the air with a speed even faster than the muscular man's earlier strike.

But Alexander had already vanished from the couch as if he had never been there.

When he reappeared, the couch lay in two pieces, cushions shredded, feathers flying everywhere.

Impressive swordsmanship!

But Alexander was faster. The woman struck five times in rapid succession, creating a web of blades, yet not one touched Alexander.

The nearby furniture suffered greatly, being sliced to pieces.

With each swift cut, beads of sweat began to form on her forehead, and her strength began to falter.

Alexander stood with his hands behind his back, admiring the woman's stunning figure, a smirk appearing on his lips.

She was quite skilled, perhaps even better than Augusta.

Moreover, her figure was fantastic—a real siren. Alexander felt an urge to recruit her.

Having someone like her on his side would significantly boost their strength and be a pleasure to look at.

"If I'm not mistaken, you're not just any Shadow Warrior. You should rank high in the Red Umbrella Group."

The woman, panting to catch her breath, replied coldly, "The strength of the Red Umbrella Group is beyond your imagination. I'm just temporarily ahead; once the second-generation power elixir is in use, it will create warriors several times more powerful than me!"

Alexander frowned.

"Second-generation power elixir? Has the Red Umbrella Group resolved the side effects?"

The woman smirked. She spoke more to buy time to regain her strength.

But Alexander wasn't fooled. He had already seen through her plan.

"I suggest you surrender, or I'll have to get serious."

Alexander warned, his deep gaze flashing with an unsettling brilliance.

Unbeknownst to her, he had begun to exert the Force's control over her.

"What are you doing?"

The woman sensed something was off, her mind suddenly becoming hazy.

And Alexander's eyes grew brighter, seemingly penetrating her mind, refusing to let go.
