Chapter 615 No Chance for Revenge

Cedric asked, "What do you mean, Olivia? I've risked my neck for your father, given up a lot for you. You should trust me to take care of you. Surely, you're not relying on this guy, are you? Is he your boyfriend?"

With a sly glance at Alexander, Cedric clearly had something up his sleeve.

Olivia replied confidently, "Cedric, you don't need to meddle in my affairs.

"The exchange is ours, and the Mafia can't keep it. My boyfriend will help me reclaim it!"

Cedric sneered in response. "Ha! Your boyfriend's nothing. Thinking he can take on the Mafia? He's got a death wish."

He spat derisively and reached for Olivia's arm.

"Listen to me, stick with me, and you might just stay alive. Listen to this punk, and you're digging your own grave."

"What are you doing? Let go of me!"

Olivia struggled free and hid behind Alexander.

Alexander's eyes turned steely as he said coldly, "You have ten seconds to get lost."

Cedric laughed in disbelief, dismissing Alexander completely.

"Don't think I don't know your game. You only say you can handle the Mafia to get Olivia in bed. Be smart and walk away now, or I'll make you regret it."

Ignoring Cedric, Alexander began to count down, saying, "Ten, nine, eight, seven..."

Cedric was seething, his jaw twitching with irritation as Alexander showed no sign of backing down.

"You must be an idiot! What good is counting down ten seconds? You're nothing to me!"

Unfazed, Alexander kept saying, "Three more seconds, three, two..."

A spark of fury crossed Cedric's eyes.

"You're asking for it, moron!"

He hurled a punch at Alexander's face with all his might.

But with a casual swat, as if brushing away a fly, Alexander countered.

Cedric staggered, reeling from the blow, clutching his face in agony as it swelled up.

"I told you, make your exit in ten seconds," Alexander said before booting Cedric away with a swift kick.

The air around seemed to chill as Cedric writhed on the ground, clutching his stomach in pain that felt like it was tearing him apart.

Finally, he realized Alexander wasn't someone to cross.

"You'll regret this!" Cedric blustered weakly as he staggered to his feet.

Holding his aching body, he turned and fled, his retreat clumsy and humiliating.

Watching him go, Olivia felt a mix of triumph and concern.

"Will he come back for revenge?" she asked cautiously.

"Don't worry. That's not a concern anymore," Alexander reassured with a smile. A mere aunt like Cedric was hardly worth a second thought.

Witnessing Alexander's confidence, Olivia felt a pang of guilt.

"I'm sorry, this is all because of me," she said sheepishly.

"Olivia, you're with me now. What's yours is mine. No need to apologize," Alexander replied, pulling her close.

He added, "Let's head to the secret exchange, it's about time."

Together, they exited the hotel, making their way to Rose Market.

Meanwhile, Cedric, nursing his swollen face, hid in a corner and made a call.

"Some punk took a swing at me. Get some guys over here to teach him a lesson... Yeah, hurry."

Hanging up, he cursed under his breath, "That bastard will get what's coming to him."

Suddenly, the side of his face that had been hit began to itch fiercely.

The sensation spread quickly, an unbearable itch that snaked down to his neck, arms, and beyond.

"What the hell? Why is it itching so bad?" he murmured as he scratched frantically, but the irritation only intensified.

Within minutes, Cedric felt as though his skin was being gnawed on by countless ants.

His relentless scratching left angry welts across his skin, swollen and grotesque.
