Chapter 139 Must Save Her

It was a terrifying face, with wrinkled skin like old tree bark, covered in black scars and deep pits.

Two beady, triangular eyes glinted maliciously, their green pupils glaring fiercely at Lara, nearly scaring her unconscious.

Around Drake's scrawny, tree-like neck hung a necklace made of white bones.

The bones were of various shapes, with a pale hue mixed with yellowish-green scars, clearly indicating they weren't fake—they had to be from some beast or even a human.

A round, green orb dangled from the chain, emitting an eerie glow like a graveyard's ghost light, giving off an indescribable sinister energy.

"I don't want money. I want Alexander's magic powder formula—and his life!" Drake laughed with a mouthful of yellowed, decayed teeth, breathing out a putrid stench.

Lara shivered uncontrollably, stammering, "W-Why are you after Alexander? Why did you capture me?"

"Because you're his woman. Why else would you be so close with him in the bar?" Drake replied with a smug look.

Lara felt like crying—her luck couldn't be worse.

"You smell nice..." Drake leaned in close, breathing in deeply around her chest with a perverse expression.

Lara felt a wave of nausea, nearly throwing up on the spot.

Just as Drake reached out, aiming to grab her, his phone suddenly rang.

Seeing it was his disciple Jimbo calling, his eyes lit up, and he quickly picked up. "Did you catch Alexander?"

There was only silence on the other end.

Drake's eyes flashed with anger, and he snapped. "Jimbo, are you deaf?"

Alexander's icy voice then came through. "Drake, if you so much as touch a hair on her head, I'll make sure you suffer a fate worse than death."

Even through the phone, the cold menace in Alexander's tone took Drake by surprise.

Neither of them spoke for a moment, leaving only tense silence.

"Hahaha!" Drake suddenly burst into laughter. "Alexander, I'll admit you're skilled, but your woman's in my hands. Still feel like being cocky with me? I can bang her right now!"

Alexander frowned. He had just checked in with Ava, who was safe.

Just then, he heard Lara's voice calling for help over the phone.

"Alexander, please help me! Please, hurry!"

"Lara?" Alexander was stunned, realizing Drake had mistaken Lara for his woman. It must have happened right after he left the bar.

"Shut up, bitch!" A sharp slap echoed through the line—Lara had been struck.

After a pause, considering their alliance, Alexander decided to rescue Lara.

He spoke slowly and clearly into the phone, "Drake, your disciple Jimbo isn't dead. I have him here. Release Lara, and I'll release Jimbo. How about it?"

Drake scoffed. "Jimbo's worthless. He doesn't deserve to be my disciple. Kill him if you want. You think you can threaten me? I'll show your woman what I can do right now!"

Without waiting for Alexander's response, he hung up.

Alexander's expression turned serious. The deal involved ten percent of the Hansen family's shares—worth billions. Lara had to be saved.

Jimbo began crying in terror. "Don't kill me, please don't! I can track him down. He's a heartless monster. I'll help you. I'll take you to him, just don't kill me."

Alexander asked coldly, "Are you telling the truth?"

"Yes, yes! We wizards have our ways of contacting each other. Drake's my master, so we've exchanged 'aura signatures.' I can locate him as long as we're within range."

Jimbo began mumbling a chant, seeming to concentrate deeply.

He looked up after a moment, delighted. "Not far from here, just about five miles southeast!"

Alexander grabbed Jimbo and sprinted in the direction he indicated.

It was well past midnight, and the streets were deserted.

Despite carrying a man, Alexander ran swiftly, moving with the speed of a cyclist, dashing forward with ease.

Soon, he reached the edge of a forest, where the dense trees cast ghostly shadows in the darkness.
