Chapter 457 Nightmare

"Shadow, who'd have thought Alexander would turn Treasure Island around like this? Used to be chaotic as hell, but now it's practically a safe haven.

"Augustine's end wasn't a fluke. We may deal in the underworld, but we don't need to be enemies of the people. In fact, we should keep the peace. Guzman never saw that and ended up a target for everyone.

"Now, Alexander's got the Brilliant Group running like a well-oiled machine!"

The silver-haired old man nodded with a satisfied smile, clearly impressed.

Shadow stayed quiet for a moment, then asked, "Godfather, Mr. Anthony Hawthorne openly defied your orders and went after Miss Ashley. How do you want me to handle it?"

The old man's gaze shifted, snapping from weary to piercing in a heartbeat.

His frail frame seemed to swell with strength, turning him from a withered old man into a beast ready to strike.

Even Shadow, seasoned as he was, felt his heart race.

"Guess I'm getting old... not only is that snake SATAN coming for me, but my own son's stepping out of line," the old man sighed.

"Sure, Anthony let Ashley go, but that doesn't mean he shouldn't pay a price. I heard the Red Umbrella Group's top research project is being handled by some old-timer...

"If he's that old, he's just taking up space. And that research division of his has done nothing but stir up trouble. Let's have them both disappear. Consider it a little wake-up call for Anthony."

Shadow dropped to one knee.

"Understood, sir!"

.....

In the nightmare, Alexander was running for his life, clutching Ava as he stumbled through the darkness.

Thick fog surrounded them, drenching the air in an eerie, bone-chilling silence.

It felt like an endless maze of twisted forest, every path leading nowhere.

The monster thundered after them, plowing through even the thickest trees.

Alexander, breath ragged, tightened his grip on Ava and pushed forward with all he had.

"Huff... huff..."

They gasped for air, barely keeping ahead.

He clutched his broken lightsaber, not daring to glance back. He knew it meant certain death if he did.

"Thud, thud, thud!"

Their frantic footsteps pounded through the forest, mingling with their panicked breaths.

"Graaah!"

The monster's roar echoed from behind.

"Thud!"

Ava tripped, and Alexander barely caught her before she tumbled into a ravine.

"It's right behind us!"

Ava's scream sliced through the air.

"Boom! Boom!"

The ground trembled as a towering shadow emerged—hulking, with bristling black spines, and a twisted, hideous face topped with devilish horns.

Each step of its thick, muscular legs sent shockwaves through the ground.

Shockingly, a small figure stood perched on the monster's shoulder, her skirt whipping wildly in the wind.

When Alexander recognized her, he froze, his whole body locking up.

"Ashley! What are you doing here?"

Ashley stood on the monster's shoulder, her gaze icy, her eyes flashing with an unrecognizable fury as though she didn't know him at all.

"What... is this!?"

Panic twisted Alexander's voice, his heartbeat hammering in his chest.

Seeing his daughter like that made his legs feel like they were made of lead. He couldn't take another step.

"Boom!"

The monster took another massive step, sending the ground into violent tremors.

"Alex, we have to run! We'll be crushed if we don't move now!"

Ava tugged him desperately.

The colossal monster lunged at them, lifting one massive foot to stomp down with earth-shaking force.

Its huge, dark foot loomed over them like a small mountain, hurtling down with a deadly whistle.

"Ashley, it's me—your dad!"

Alexander shouted with everything he had, but the monster didn't hesitate.

"Help!"

Ava's terrified eyes filled with tears, her face twisting in fear.

The enormous foot was right above them, about to crush them both.

"Ashley, no!"

Alexander bellowed as he woke with a start.

He sprang to his feet and raced to his daughter's room next door.
