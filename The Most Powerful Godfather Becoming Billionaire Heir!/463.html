Chapter 463 Stirring up Trouble

Nate Sullivan's eyes went wide, his voice dripping with jealousy.

"Ava, you've got a boyfriend now?"

Ava smiled, leaning over to plant a kiss on Alexander's cheek.

"That's right. He's my boyfriend—Alexander."

Nate's face twisted, and his tone turned sarcastic.

"Alexander, huh? What makes you think you deserve to be Ava's boyfriend?"

Alexander shot him a disinterested glance. This guy was way too full of himself. That condescending tone really grated on his nerves.

But before Alexander could say anything, Ava's temper flared.

"Nate, what's that supposed to mean? Who do you think you are, belittling my boyfriend?"

Since Nate had it out for Alexander, Ava wasn't about to be nice to him.

Nate snorted, "Ava, I'm just saying this for your own good. This guy does not worthy of you. You deserve someone who's young, successful, and going places."

Alexander couldn't help but laugh, "Not worthy of her, huh? And you think you are? You're being ridiculous."

"Who are you calling ridiculous?"

Nate stepped up to Alexander, fists clenching, ready for a fight.

"Move it."

Alexander gave him a slight shove, and Nate stumbled back, nearly losing his balance.

"You just shoved me?"

Nate's face turned red with rage, and he aimed a vicious kick at Alexander's groin.

It was a dirty move—if it landed, Alexander would be seriously hurt.

Alexander's patience ran out. This guy wasn't just obnoxious—he was dangerous.

"Smack!"

The sound of a slap echoed through the gym.

Alexander's hand moved quicker than Nate could react, landing a sharp slap across his face.

Nate let out a yelp, falling to the ground as he clutched his now swollen cheek.

Ava shook her head, sighing.

"Nate, you're just like you were in college—always looking down on everyone. You've really got issues."

Alexander wrapped an arm around Ava, ready to leave, but Nate wasn't done yet. He stepped forward, blocking their way.

"What now? You want another beating?"

Alexander's tone was casual, almost amused.

Nate gritted his teeth. As a former college athlete and now a well-known fitness coach, he refused to believe Alexander had actually beaten him fair and square.

"Listen, Alexander. Unlike you, I'm not one to throw punches in front of a lady—it's not what a gentleman does.

"But if you've got the guts, let's see what you're made of. There are barbells right here. The loser leaves, and the winner gets to take Ava out. You in?"

Nate's eyes were filled with confidence as he glared at Alexander.

Alexander scoffed, "You're delusional. Why should I compete just because you say so? Ava's not some prize to be won."

Ava's face twisted in disgust as she shot Nate a cold glare.

Nate's expression darkened, anger bubbling beneath the surface.

"Son of bitch! If you beat me, I swear I'll never bother you again. But if you lose, you leave the hotel right now.

"And if you're too scared to compete, then you're nothing but a coward."

Alexander didn't see any point in wasting more time on Nate. He turned, ready to leave with Ava.

Suddenly, Ava grabbed his arm.

"He's gone too far, treating me like I'm some trophy. Compete with him, and make sure you win. Bet that he'll never show his face around us again."

Seeing Ava so worked up, clearly furious, Alexander decided to go along with it.

"Alright, I'll take him on. But how are you gonna thank me after I win? Maybe something like... being mine forever?"

"Ugh, you're impossible!"

Ava's face turned a bright shade of red at Alexander's teasing, and she leaned into his chest, giving him a playful shove.

At that moment, with Ava pressed up against him, Alexander couldn't help but let his hands wander, enjoying the moment.

Nate's eyes nearly popped out of his head, seeing Alexander touch Ava so casually and Ava enjoying it. He snapped.

"Cut it out! Alexander, how dare you act like that in public! It's too much."

Ava sneered at him, "Alexander's my boyfriend. If I want to be with him, that's none of your business. You and me? We're just classmates—nothing more."

Nate wasn't giving up, his voice rising in desperation.

"Ava, I chased you for three whole years, and I've got money! If you'd be with me, I'd give you 100 grand a month—how's that sound?"

Ava rolled her eyes. "100 grand dollars a month? Seriously, you think that's a lot?"

Nate completely misunderstood, his face lighting up with hope.

"Exactly! If you're with me, 100 grand dollars every month. That's over a million dollars a year!"
