Chapter 1290 Utterly Baffling

Alexander recognized the substance immediately. It was a surgical anesthetic so potent that just ten milliliters could knock out an elephant. Though he was only a medical intern, he had no doubt about his conclusion.

And that wasn't all. There was something else mixed in. The liquid gave off a strange, metallic tang.

He hesitated, gripping the syringe, thinking, "Should I warn the people inside or just pretend I haven't seen anything? Getting involved might only bring me trouble."

After a brief struggle, he made up his mind.

He turned back and pressed the doorbell hard.

The door swung open again, revealing an astonishingly beautiful woman. She gazed at him with a soft, gentle smile.

Alexander froze. She was breathtaking.

She was only slightly shorter than him, her almond-shaped eyes glistening with intelligence. Below her high-bridged nose was a delicate, rosy mouth.

But what really caught his attention was her figure. The sheer black dress she wore clung to every curve, revealing smooth, pale skin and a dangerously low neckline.

Then it hit him that this woman looked exactly like Ava.

Her smile faded as she furrowed her brows and crossed her arms over her chest. In a flat tone, she asked, "Can I help you?"

That cold, distant demeanor only made her more alluring.

Alexander still hadn't said a word. The woman's expression grew wary. She placed a hand near the call button by the door. "What do you want?"

This was an upscale neighborhood with tight security. If she pressed that button, guards would arrive within five minutes.

Snapping out of his daze, Alexander quickly held up the syringe. "My name is Alexander Hawthorne. I just saw your friend drug the wine. I thought you should know."

"Drugged?"

She frowned, studying him intently. "Are you sure? You saw it happen?"

"Silvia, who is it?"

A voice called out from inside.

The woman from earlier, Maris, appeared in the doorway. The moment she spotted Alexander and the syringe in his hand, her expression changed.

Silvia Hewitt, the stunning woman, caught the shift in Maris' demeanor. Her gaze darkened. "This delivery guy says he saw you spike the wine. Do you have anything to say?"

"What?! That's ridiculous!" Maris gasped, covering her mouth in shock. "Why would I ever do something like that?"

Alexander had to admit, her acting was impressive. She could have given professional actresses a run for their money.

"Silvia, this creep was ogling me earlier. He must want to do something to you!"

Maris hurriedly stepped in front of her friend as if to shield her. "You don't have to be afraid. I'll protect you."

Silvia visibly relaxed. She trusted Maris. After all, they'd been best friends for years.

Besides, the way Alexander had looked at her earlier had been unsettling, almost predatory.

She turned a sharp glare on Alexander. "I don't know what your game is, but if you don't leave right now, I'm calling security. Once I press this button, you'll be dragged off in seconds. You won't even have time to regret it."

Alexander refused to back down. He pointed at Maris. "It was her! If you don't believe me, just check the wine bottle. The cap will have a tiny puncture mark. That should prove I'm telling the truth."

Maris shot back instantly, "I didn't do anything! He's lying!"

Before Silvia could react, Maris grabbed the bottle off the table, popped the cork, and poured herself half a glass. Without hesitation, she took a long sip.

"There! Do I look drugged to you?" she sneered, raising a middle finger at Alexander.

He was stunned. He couldn't understand how she wasn't affected.

That anesthetic should have taken effect in under a minute.

Maris smirked, "Silvia, I am fine, right?

"Are you really going to trust some random delivery guy over our years of friendship?"

Silvia's eyes flashed with cold fury. Without another word, she pressed the button.

A deep male voice came through the intercom. "Ms. Hewitt, do you need assistance?"

Silvia turned back to Alexander, her tone icy. "I don't care who sent you or what your intentions are. If you don't leave now, you'll be dragged out of here."

Alexander hesitated, then suddenly asked, "Are you really called Silvia? Not Ava?"

The voice on the intercom grew tense. "Ms. Hewitt, are you in danger? I'll send someone immediately."

Alexander's face fell. He instinctively tried to summon the Force, only to find that his body was completely empty of it.

He thought, "Did I really just dream all of this?

Or have I truly lost my mind?"

"Fine. I'm leaving."

Conflicted and shaken, Alexander turned and walked away.
