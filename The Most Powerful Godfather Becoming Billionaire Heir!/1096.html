Chapter 1096 I'll Do Whatever You Say

"You're beautiful... very beautiful..."

Alexander replied calmly, without looking at her or saying anything more.

He knew Maxine had developed feelings for him, but just hours earlier, he had been with Emma in the mountains. He didn't want to get entangled with Maxine.

If he let the dark energy inside him grow any stronger, things could get out of hand.

But Maxine was clearly dissatisfied with his lack of attention. She was edging closer to him, her curves nearly pressing against him.

Alexander couldn't help but smile wryly. He knew he had to put an end to her expectations before things went any further.

Seeing his indifference, Maxine felt a pang of frustration.

She didn't have much experience with relationships, and in the past, any man she showed interest in had quickly fallen in line.

But Alexander remained unmoved, even cold toward her, leaving Maxine feeling rejected.

She muttered under her breath, "Alexander, if you think I'm beautiful, why don't you look at me more? You must be lying."

Alexander sighed inwardly. This girl was really smitten, but he wasn't interested in returning those feelings.

"Let's go. The medicine's ready. I'll treat your dad."

He quickly changed the subject, grabbing the cup and heading for the door.

Maxine could only follow behind, resigned.

In the living room, Alvin was anxiously waiting.

When he saw Alexander enter with a smile on his face, his expression immediately lit up with hope.

"Drink this, then I'll start the treatment."

Alexander handed Alvin the cup.

Without hesitation, Alvin drank the medicine in one go.

"Mr. Hawthorne, thank you for all your help. Whether this works or not, I'll never forget your kindness to our family, no matter what happens."

Alvin still looked a bit skeptical, unable to believe that he could truly be free from the control of the drug.

Alexander chuckled softly, not offering any explanation. He simply placed his hand on Alvin's forehead.

A surge of powerful Force poured into Alvin's body, like a tidal wave, pushing the toxins out through his pores.

Soon, dark sweat began to bead on Alvin's skin.

Emma and Maxine both smelled a pungent odor and quickly stepped back, covering their noses in shock, their eyes wide with horror as they watched Alvin.

"How does it feel? Do you feel better?" Alexander asked.

"Yes! It's amazing... it feels like I'm soaking in a hot spring. My whole body is warm, it's so comfortable."

Alvin's face was one of pure relief, a soft hum escaping him, as if he'd been pulled from the depths of hell into paradise.

Alexander smiled, "Don't get too excited. We still need a little more time to completely flush the toxins from your system."

"Yes, Mr. Hawthorne, don't worry. I'll follow your lead."

Alvin's confidence was clearly growing.

Soon, the dark sweat had stopped oozing from Alvin's skin, and the color of his perspiration returned to normal.

"Alright, you're all cleared of toxins, but your energy is still depleted. You'll need to rest."

Alexander instructed.

"Thank you, Mr. Hawthorne! Thank you so much!"

Alvin was incredibly grateful.

With a sudden movement, Alvin knelt before Alexander.

Tears streamed down his face, a testament to the depth of his sorrow. A man who rarely cried, he had finally reached his breaking point.

"Mr. Hawthorne, whatever you need, I'll do it. I'll take care of Silver Shield Security. I won't let you down."

Alvin's voice was filled with seriousness and sincerity.

Alexander nodded in satisfaction. "Tomorrow, we'll go take over Silver Shield Security. Then, I'll need you to make a prototype of the All-purpose Bullet-resistant Jacket for me to see."

Alvin nodded eagerly. "Silver Shield Security has samples. We'll go together tomorrow."

"Dad!"

Maxine rushed to hug her father tightly, a moment of warmth and unity shared between them.

Emma joined in, wrapping her arms around them, the feeling of family togetherness filling the room with sweetness.

But her gaze was fixed on Alexander, filled with something more complex.

Alexander felt a flicker of discomfort, like a thief caught in the act. He couldn't bear to meet their eyes.

In the dead of night, silence blanketed the house.

Alexander had returned to his room, showered, and was preparing to rest—there were still hours until dawn.

"Shh-shh-shh..."

Then, Alexander heard the familiar sound of footsteps approaching his door.
