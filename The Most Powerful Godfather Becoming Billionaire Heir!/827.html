Chapter 827 A True Performance

The crowd erupted in chatter.

"She is gorgeous. If she were really a secretary, she'd make a ton of money every month, right?"

"Secretary? Alexander's just bluffing. He's full of it!"

"Exactly! To find a woman that stunning and sexy for an act, she's probably just a call girl."

Not a single person believed Alexander.

Alexander scanned the room with his deep, penetrating gaze and sighed silently.

The kind-hearted classmates he once knew had changed, now shallow and materialistic, far removed from the innocent days of college.

Bobby sneered with a sarcastic grin, taunting Alexander. "How much do you even make? Why lie to us? You're the one who should be apologizing!"

Alice couldn't hold back anymore. With a sharp motion, she slapped Bobby across the face.

The sound echoed in the room. Bobby stood stunned, clutching his burning cheek, his eyes spinning.

"I am the secretary to the chairman of the Saint Group. If you don't apologize, you won't live to see tomorrow!"

Alice's words, full of icy determination, sent chills through everyone. The room fell silent.

The Saint Group was a household name in Loscity, and these small-timers knew better than to cross them.

Bobby's face turned pale as he stammered.

"Alexander, why... why didn't you make that clear earlier? So she's really the chairman's secretary? My bad, really, I... I shouldn't have run my mouth!

"Please, for Alexander's sake, don't take this to heart."

He added, slapping himself to show remorse.

Alice huffed, clearly unimpressed, before stepping back to Alexander's side after noticing his silence.

"Alexander, I'm so sorry. Please, have a seat. Both of you are honored guests."

Bobby said with an obsequious grin, eagerly inviting them to sit.

The tense atmosphere began to thaw, and everyone took their seats, though many continued to eye Alice with curiosity and doubt.

The question lingered in their minds why would a high-profile secretary from the Saint Group be so deferential to Alexander?

Was this really true?

Bobby, trying to break the ice, raised his glass.

"Let's toast to the success of this gathering!"

After the toast, Bobby slyly asked Alexander, "So, are you working for the Saint Group now?"

He was fishing for information, clearly skeptical of Alice's claims.

Alexander cast a cold glance at him. "I don't see why I should share my private affairs with you."

Bobby rolled his eyes, assuming Alexander was unemployed. His confidence surged.

Grinning slyly, he turned to Alice.

"So, Alexander, are you being kept by this lovely lady? You're a married man. How can you fool around like this?

"Why not join my company as a security guard? I'll even pay you five hundred dollars more each month. That's a fair deal! I own two companies, you know. Even my security guards earn three thousand dollars a month."

A few classmates chimed in, laughing, "Bobby, you're too generous. Cheers to you!"

"An extra five hundred dollars a month, that's six thousand dollars a year! Alexander, if you're out of work and relying on her, maybe working for Bobby would be a better move."

Their mocking tone and greedy glances directed at Alice were impossible to ignore.

They wondered why someone would as beautiful and alluring as her be with a so-called loser like Alexander.

The sycophantic classmates raised their glasses, flocking to Bobby and showering him with flattery, effectively sidelining Alexander.

They had clearly decided Alexander and Alice were lying, their attitudes flipping instantly.

One woman, emboldened by Bobby's wealth, batted her eyelashes at him and sneakily ran her hand up his leg under the table. He was too rich.

Alexander smirked, watching their antics like a crowd of clowns.

Alice's chest rose and fell in anger. She wanted to lash out at their foolishness but stayed silent, waiting for Alexander's cue.

But without Alexander's go-ahead, she held her tongue.

A gaudily dressed woman squeezed closer, leaning her chest against Bobby with a shamelessly fawning expression.

She turned to Alexander with mock indignation. "Why aren't you saying anything? It's so rude! Bobby is offering you such a good job and salary out of kindness. Don't be ungrateful.

"And your woman even slapped him! You two are putting on quite the act, pretending to be with the Saint Group. But we're not idiots. You can't fool us."
