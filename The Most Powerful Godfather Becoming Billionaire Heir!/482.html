Chapter 482 Depression

Whoosh!

The yellow bead was swiftly absorbed, causing the magical circle to radiate an even more brilliant light than before, with the Force fluctuations doubling in intensity.

Alexander inhaled sharply, excitement coursing through him.

It seemed these beads were even more effective than the top-tier gemstones.

Red, orange, yellow, green, indigo, blue and purple. He still needed the orange, green, indigo, and purple top-tier gemstones to complete his collection.

With eighteen beads remaining in the bracelet, he decided not to experiment further.

After integrating a yellow orb into the lightsaber core, he felt a subconscious connection between the bracelet and the Ancestral Witch's Cane.

The Ancestral Witch's Cane was acquired from Guzman and sought after by the City of Sorcery.

However, he had kept it well hidden and had never studied it in depth.

He had long established a conflict with the City of Sorcery, and sooner or later, they would come looking for him again.

While Alexander possessed the Ultimate Legacy and was uninterested in sorcery, he was curious about the relationship between the bracelet and the Ancestral Witch's Cane.

He planned to investigate it as soon as he returned to Loscity.

Just then, Lynn rushed downstairs in tears, bolting out the door without a backward glance.

Alexander was about to chase after her, but Ava stopped him.

"Let her be. She's going through a tough time," Ava said.

Her tone was heavy with concern.

He nodded and took a seat again.

Ava fell into his arms, tears welling in her eyes.

"What's wrong? Why are you crying?"

Seeing Ava so upset, Alexander thought she might be feeling hurt by something.

"I... I misunderstood my mom. All those years ago, everything she did was to support me, but I didn't understand. I ran away instead...

"Now, seeing her so heartbroken makes me really sad," Ava said, her eyes red.

Alexander sighed, "She'll be okay. Give it some time."

But Ava shook her head, tears streaming down her face. "I saw antidepressants on her nightstand. After such a blow, she might fall into a deeper depression."

"Depression?"

Alexander paused, realizing that while he could use the Force to heal physical ailments, mental health issues were beyond his reach.

Ava wiped her tears.

"Only the one who caused the pain can soothe it. A man caused this hurt, so it needs a man to mend it. But it can't be Victor; it needs to be someone reliable, someone she trusts.

"I think that's the most effective approach I can think of. I can't come up with anything else," Ava said, her tone suggesting something deeper, startling Alexander.

They fell silent for a moment.

Ava looked deeply into Alexander's eyes. "I need to ask you something, and you have to promise to answer honestly."

"Sure, go ahead."

For some reason, Alexander felt a surge of good fortune was about to come his way.

"How do you see my mom? Is she beautiful?"

"Uh! Why are you asking me this? What do you want me to say? She's my elder!"

Alexander was taken aback.

Ava glared at him.

"Don't think of her as an elder. Just answer me from a man's perspective—how does she look?"

Reluctantly, he replied, "Of course she's beautiful. She has a great figure, not at all less than yours."

"Then... if I weren't your girlfriend, would you date my mom?"

"What! What kind of nonsense is that? How can you ask me such a question?"

Alexander raised his voice, angry at the suggestion.

"Just answer me honestly!" Ava insisted firmly.

"I don't know what you're talking about, and I don't want to answer," he said, standing up to leave.

Ava quickly caught up to him, playfully clinging to him.

"You have to answer! Come on, please..."

"Alright, enough! If you weren't my girlfriend, maybe I would consider dating her. Are you satisfied now?"

"Very satisfied! Don't worry; I won't leave you. I was just asking out of curiosity."

Ava smiled and planted a quick kiss on his cheek.

"By the way, tomorrow night is my mom's birthday. She was planning to celebrate with Victor, but now the task falls to us."

"Okay, let's get ready!" Alexander agreed decisively.
