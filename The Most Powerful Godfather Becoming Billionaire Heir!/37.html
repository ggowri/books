Chapter 37 Who Dares to Move?

As Ava seemed on the verge of yielding, Billy's anxiety spiked.

If Joseph took him away, his fate was sealed.

Billy dropped to his knees and pleaded desperately, "Ms. Blackburn, please save me. From now on, I'll do anything you say."

Ava remained aloof, offering no response.

Desperate, Billy swiveled and turned to Alexander.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I'll serve you and be your man. Please, speak for me to Ms. Blackburn."

Max's voice cut through, cold and warning, "I suggest you know your place and stay out of this."

Alexander met his gaze with icy resolve.

"Your incapability doesn't mean mine. Billy wants to follow me; I'll protect him."

With those words, Alexander marched toward Joseph, leaving even Ava unable to hold him back.

Silence spread in the room. All eyes fixed on Alexander, whose purpose remained mysterious.

With a sneer, Joseph observed Alexander and challenged, "And who might you be, boy?"

"Are you the head of the Cripples?" Instead of answering, Alexander posed his question.

Joseph answered, "Yes. What do you want to do?"

"I'll do the Cripples a kindness. Kneel, apologize publicly, and you can leave with your crew."

Alexander's tone was so casual that it bordered on mundane.

But his words sparked an uproar among the onlookers. They stared at Alexander like he had lost his mind.

They all believed Alexander acted in such a buoyant fashion due to Ava.

Fury gnawed Joseph.

"You want me to kneel and apologize? Are you mad?

"You all witnessed it. I didn't want trouble with the Saint Group, but this fool has crossed the line. He must die."

Anxiety gripped Ava, who knew Alexander was skilled. But she also grasped the notion that Alexander was no match for Joseph.

She moved to intervene, only to be restrained by Max.

"Ms. Blackburn, it's dangerous!"

Whispers filled the room. Many faces lit with schadenfreude.

"Alexander is a fool, challenging the Cripples for Billy?"

"He must think Ava's protection makes him invincible. Doesn't he see the Saint Group's precarious position?"

"What an idiot!"

"If I were him, I'd ignore this nonsense and enjoy my time with Ms. Blackburn."

All eyes, filled with scorn, fixed on Alexander. They viewed him as a man marching to his doom.

"Move, fool!" Joseph roared.

He launched a thunderous punch at Alexander's face.

The air whistled with the force of the blow, quicker and mightier than before.

Alexander stood frozen, seemingly petrified.

Joseph smirked, certain his punch would shatter Alexander's jaw.

Just as panic seized Ava, Joseph's fist met nothing but air.

Alexander, who had been right there, now stood beside Joseph.

Joseph could feel the danger pulsating in the air.

His expression shifted to alarm. He attempted a swift retreat.

Alexander smiled and shadowed Joseph.

Feeling the prickling threat at his back, Joseph spun, throwing a desperate punch.

But as he raised his arm, Alexander caught it effortlessly.

Despite Joseph's fist being as large as a dinner plate, Alexander's grip was ironclad, unyielding. Joseph found himself restrained, unable to break free.

When Joseph subconsciously raised another hand, Alexander activated the Force and clenched his fist.

The sound of fracturing bones echoed like firecrackers.

Joseph screamed as his right hand was forcibly contorted into a grotesque claw. Every bone was shattered.

The pain from his hand seeped into his very being. His cries of agony filled the air as he collapsed, writhing in pain.

The members of the Cripples exclaimed in surprise and moved to advance, only to be halted.

As tensions neared a breaking point, Alexander stomped on Joseph's throat.

"If anyone dares to move, I will kill him right now!"

An aura of lethal intent radiated from Alexander, silencing the room.
