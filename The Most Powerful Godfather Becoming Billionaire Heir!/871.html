Chapter 871 Family Meeting

Keith was the last to wrap up the shoot.

Amira ran her tongue along her lips, a satisfied smile gracing her face.

Harold cheered, clearly pleased with the outcome.

"Great job! Absolutely fantastic!

"Keith, you've got a natural talent for this. Trust me, you're on your way to becoming the hottest male star at Dragon Film and Television Company!"

Harold clapped Keith on the shoulder, beaming with pride.

Keith said, bowing slightly in appreciation, "Thank you! Thank you so much!

"Ms. Barnes, you were wonderful as well."

"I didn't do much; it was you who worked hard," she replied, returning the gesture.

The group exchanged bows, their professionalism evident in every move.

*****

Meanwhile, Alexander sat in a conference room on the top floor of the building, flanked by Norma.

She occupied the main seat, looking sharp and commanding, shedding her usual seductive demeanor for a more authoritative presence.

Alexander, sitting beside her, remained unfazed by the dozens of shocked, confused, and angry gazes from the Walsh family executives.

Their stares didn't rattle him; he sat there calmly, smiling as if he were at a casual gathering.

The room was filled with members of the Walsh family, a mix of ages and ranks.

Those of higher status occupied the chairs around the table, while others stood nearby, casting furious glances at Alexander and occasionally checking in on Norma.

An elderly man, frail yet commanding, stood up and pointed an accusatory finger at her.

"Norma! You better come clean! Did you conspire with this guy to kill the patriarch?"

He was in his seventies, his body trembling, but his position in the family was undeniable.

Norma frowned, recognizing him as a senior member who often wielded his influence like a weapon.

Alexander sprang to his feet, his voice booming with authority. "Listen here, you old coot! Norma isn't just the leader of the Dragon Gang anymore; she's the patriarch of the Walsh family! Who do you think you are to speak to her like that?"

The old man sneered, his voice dripping with disdain. "Who the hell are you, you little punk? This is a Walsh family meeting! What right do you have to be here?

"You're just an outsider, sitting here like you own the place. What are you even doing here?"

Suddenly, a piercing scream sliced through the air.

In the blink of an eye, Alexander was in front of the old man, his grip like a vise around the elder's throat.

The old man's face turned a shade of purple as he gasped for air, stammering, "Help me... someone... Norma... say something..."

But Norma remained silent, her gaze cold and unyielding as she watched the scene unfold.

"Let him go!"

"Release him! Do you want to die?"

Chaos erupted in the room as several voices shouted in anger.

No one had ever dared to act out like this in a family meeting, especially not a stranger.

"Go to hell!"

A few hot-headed young men, used to getting their way, pulled out guns, aiming them at Alexander.

Suddenly, the sound of shattering glass echoed as the young men were hurled out of the window by Alexander, their screams fading into the distance.

Fortunately for them, they wouldn't feel any pain; their bodies were reduced to a bloody pulp upon impact.

The moment the glass shattered, they had snapped multiple bones, unable to withstand the force of the fall.

Before anyone could process what had just happened, several high-ranking members of the Walsh family lay dead on the floor.

A frigid wind howled through the open window, sending shivers down the spines of the dozens of family members present, even though it wasn't winter.

Beads of cold sweat trickled down their faces, their bodies stiff with dread.

Alexander's piercing gaze swept across the room like twin blades of steel, and wherever it landed, heads dipped in submission, avoiding eye contact.

These once-mighty higher-ups in the family, now enfeebled by luxury, cowered before Alexander like lambs in the presence of a tiger, too terrified to resist.

And then Norma rose, her hands firmly planted on the table, her voice tinged with a mix of excitement and defiance.

"By the Walsh family's code, the position of clan leader falls to me. Who among you dares to dispute it?"

Her eyes blazed with fervor that contrasted sharply with the room's chill.
