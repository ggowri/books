Chapter 511 Greed

Aside from Barry, the Red Umbrella Group had no other Level 3 metahumans, while Francis' personal bodyguard was a Level 3 metahuman.

Compared to the military, the Red Umbrella Group's power was definitely lacking.

Clang!

The door swung open, and the strikingly beautiful Juliet stormed in with a group of people in tow.

The group was heavily armed, with two Level 2 metahumans among them.

Dozens of people charged in, surrounding Felix and glaring at him with hostility.

It was no wonder the Kingsdon was such a critical zone; the number and strength of metahumans were staggering.

"Mr. Wallace, shall we finish him off?" Juliet asked, glaring at Felix.

Felix quickly apologized.

"Mr. Wallace, I was in the wrong. I'm willing to pay you five hundred million in compensation and sincerely apologize! I want to take down Alexander; he's our common enemy!" Felix pleaded.

Felix looked pitiful as he begged.

The mighty chairman of the Red Umbrella Group had now been reduced to pleading.

"It is all because of that scoundrel Alexander," Felix thought.

A deadly silence filled the room. Francis said nothing, and everyone stared menacingly at Felix.

Felix's back was drenched in sweat; he was as meek as a frightened chick.

After a tense minute or two, Francis' expression finally softened, and he spoke coldly. "What's your plan? If I send troops to protect your people, how exactly do you intend to deal with Alexander? I'm not one to be easily fooled, so don't try to scam me."

"I'd never deceive you!"

Felix exhaled deeply, wiping the sweat from his brow.

Forcing a smile, he asked, "Mr. Wallace, have you heard of the Morris Family Army?"

Hearing this, Francis' eyes narrowed with a hint of interest.

"You mean the Morris Family Army that dominates the northern wilderness?"

The Morris Family Army was renowned, filled with experts and deeply rooted strength across the wildlands.

Felix replied excitedly, "Exactly! Barry, a direct member of the Morris family, joined our Red Umbrella Group and, under my guidance, became a Level 3 metahuman.

"Barry's son, Raven, was killed by Alexander. Barry's determined to get revenge! I can bring the Morris family on board. Alexander won't stand a chance."

Francis sneered.

"The Morris family may be powerful, but they're not fools. Barry might want revenge on Alexander, but he's not the head of the family.

"Without substantial benefits, the Morris family won't step up on your behalf!"

Juliet and the others scoffed at Felix's plan.

The Morris family wielded such power that even the military showed them respect.

"Heh..."

Felix smiled smugly, unbothered by Francis and the others' mockery.

"I know the Morris family won't help me easily, but a hundred years ago, the Vanderbilt family saved their ancestors.

"If not for the Vanderbilt family, the Morris family would have been wiped out. That's why Barry joined the group to support me.

"I'm prepared to offer the Morris family a portion of the company's shares for their support. They surely won't refuse.

"And the magic powder is so popular. If we get the formula from Alexander, the profits will be astronomical!"

Felix spoke passionately, and even Francis' expression showed excitement.

He stared intently at Felix, his eyes brimming with unhidden greed.

"Felix, if you're willing to give up shares to get the Morris family's help, why should I send troops to protect your people?"

Felix's eye twitched, fury burning within him, though he kept his expression calm.

"Mr. Wallace, if you back me, I'll transfer five percent of Red Umbrella Group's shares to you personally."

"Five percent? At Red Umbrella Group's hundred-billion-dollar valuation, that's five billion, with a few hundred million in annual dividends..."

Francis swallowed hard, doing the math in his head.

After a while, he nodded slowly.

"Alright! Give me ten percent of the shares, and I'll agree to help you through this crisis."

Felix was so angry he almost slapped him.

Since the partnership with the military, they controlled sixty percent of Red Umbrella Group's shares, leaving him with only forty percent.

Francis was exploiting the situation, asking for ten percent upfront. How greedy could he get?
