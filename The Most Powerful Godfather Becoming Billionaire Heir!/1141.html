Chapter 1141 No Mercy

"Mr. Merrett..." Joaquin hesitated, confused.

Stephen immediately cut him off. "I'm in the mood to deal with these fools today. Let's make sure we do this right. I'll use their heads to honor Tyler and the others."

Stephen's eyes were ice cold as he glanced at Brock and his men.

Brock, usually brimming with confidence, suddenly felt a chill down his spine. His heart raced, and he stopped dead in his tracks.

Brianna, pressed close to him, gave him a little nudge and asked with a sweet, flirtatious voice, "What's wrong, baby? You look distracted."

"Nothing, let's go," Brock muttered, shaking it off.

He gave Brianna a playful squeeze on her chest, then signaled for his men to follow him inside, still trying to act unbothered.

Despite his reputation in Louvian and his solid fighting skills, Brock had no idea who he was up against.

Stephen was a monster, literally. As the top dog of Sakra Society and enhanced by enhancement drugs, he was on a whole different level, something close to a level-five metahuman.

Brock's cockiness would get him nowhere here.

"Shut the door. No one leaves," Stephen ordered, his voice low and threatening.

His gaze was like a predator's, cold and calculating. The twisted smile on his face would send shivers down anyone's spine.

The doors slammed shut.

In a heartbeat, screams echoed through the restaurant, desperate cries rising up.

Within less than a minute, the floor was covered in bodies, blood spilling everywhere, like something straight out of hell.

Brock, known as the "Undying Bird" in Louvian, and his four most loyal men had always been a formidable force.

They trained together relentlessly, perfecting a synchronized fighting style that made them almost unbeatable.

Brock himself was a force to be reckoned with. After five years of rigorous training at a dojo, he had become an expert.

His reputation was built on his fearlessness and raw fighting ability. He was known for never backing down, no matter the odds.

But now, in this small restaurant, Brock and his men found themselves trapped, powerless against an enemy far stronger than they had anticipated.

The five of them, plus Brianna, were the only ones still alive.

The others had been slaughtered, their bodies hacked to pieces and scattered around.

It was brutal, their dead eyes wide open, filled with terror and disbelief, forever frozen in their last moments.

Brock and his four subordinates were panting, their hands trembling as they gripped their machetes.

The fear was palpable. They no longer had any of their usual swagger. They were pale, sweat dripping, their weapons slipping from their hands.

Stephen, on the other hand, was all business. He moved like a blade, cold, sharp, and unstoppable.

His belt held two sabers, one of which was already drawn, slightly longer than the other.

The blade gleamed with a stark white sheen, streaked with fresh blood that stained the metal red. Intricate spiral patterns resembling chrysanthemums spiraled along the edge of the blade.

These delicate, swirling engravings were the mark of a master blade-forger, crafted only by the most skilled artisans through years of painstaking refinement.

Stephen's saber wasn't just any weapon. It was a true treasure, a weapon of rare and unmatched quality.

The sound of blood dripping from the edge of the blade was almost soothing, the only noise in the room besides the groans of the dying.

Brianna was huddled in a corner, clutching her head, too terrified to even look up.

Joaquin's men, however, watched from the sidelines, grinning, eager for the spectacle to unfold.

Stephen, calm and lethal, took a slow step forward, his saber held loosely in his hand. His eyes locked on Brock with a twisted smile, no mercy in sight.

Brock and his men slowly backed away, sweat soaking their shirts. The machetes in their hands felt like dead weight.

"You want to finish us off? It's just a small fight! You've already killed more than ten people, and now, you want to wipe us all out?" Brock growled, baring his teeth.

"You took my deposit! You promised me this place! You're going back on your word. What kind of business is this?"

Stephen's laugh was dark and mocking as he ran his tongue along the bloodstained edge of his saber, savoring the moment like a predator about to make the kill.

"Weren't you all tough a minute ago? Too scared now? Well, guess what? It's too late."

With a sudden, fluid motion, the blade was pointing straight at Brock, and the tension in the room was suffocating.

"You guys think you're worthy to feast on our food? Only we, the noblest people, deserve that privilege."

Stephen's disgust was clear, his eyes cold as he looked at Brock and his gang.
