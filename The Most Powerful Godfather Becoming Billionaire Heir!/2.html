Chapter 2 The Godfather's Heir

Alexander stood there like a deer in headlights, his brain short-circuiting.

"Hold up - what Godfather? Go home where? What are you talking about?"

At that moment, a beautiful woman walked toward him, her curvy body swaying with each step.

"Mr. Hawthorne," she purred respectfully, "the Godfather's waiting. You're one of three potential heirs. Please, come with me."

His heart nearly jumped out of his chest. This was way too much, way too fast.

The guards who'd been roughing him up were instantly thrown against the wall by those black-suit men, collapsing unconscious.

Alexander froze in shock, too frightened to say anything. He quickly followed the beautiful woman onto the helicopter.

Then a phone's ring cut through the tension like a knife.

The woman handed the phone to him. Shaking, Alexander answered the call.

"Welcome home, Alexander." The voice sounded ancient, powerful. "You'll be competing with your two brothers to become my successor. Only the winner inherits everything."

"Listen, you're now chairman of the Saint Group. You need to double its value to 200 billion in twelve months. Oh, and stay alive."

"If you fail, you'll lose your inheritance... or worse, death!"

Click. Dead air.

Alexander's brain went into overdrive.

What does this mean? A competition?

I'm now the chairman of the Saint Group - the company everyone kissed up to?

And he wants me to double its value to 200 billion? In a year? Hey, how is that even possible?!

Wait, did he say I have two brothers?

Death... So my brothers will try to kill me?

Damn, what the hell is this bullshit!

His head buzzed with confusion. All this information was too much to process.

"Mr. Hawthorne, get down!"

Suddenly, the beautiful woman lunged at him, knocking him to the floor. A shot cracked through the air.

A large man, who had just pulled out a pistol, fell to the ground with a thud. A huge hole appeared in his chest, his blood splattering everywhere.

His dying words came out in a wet gurgle, "Mr. Augustine Hawthorne says... trash like you... doesn't deserve... the Godfather's seat..."

Alexander went ghost-white, staring at the growing pool of red.

"I've got your back, Mr. Hawthorne. Don't worry!"

The woman helped him up, barking orders to clean up the mess.

That's when it hit him - he knew her face from TV. Alice Watts, the Saint Group chairman's secretary.

Alice was a total package - drop-dead gorgeous with killer curves, but don't let that fool you. She was a cold-blooded marksman with a mind sharp as a razor.

Thanks to her, the Saint Group was now at the top of Loscity's business world.

She sized up Alexander, underwhelmed. She hadn't expected the Godfather's illegitimate son to be such an ordinary man.

"Mr. Hawthorne, you're now Saint Group's chairman. Here's the five hundred grand for your daughter's medical bills." She popped open a briefcase, fresh bills still smelling of ink.

Alexander massaged his temples, muttering, "Everyone out. I need space... And keep my identity under wraps. Nobody needs to know who I really am."

Alice waved her hand, and everyone quietly exited. Then she headed to the roof.

Pulling out her phone, she video-called the Godfather.

Kneeling on one knee, she spoke respectfully, "Godfather, are there any other orders?"

The screen showed only the back of an old man in a leather chair. His silver hair and stooped shoulders screamed 'fragile,' but his presence could make the air freeze.

He coughed, not bothering to turn around. "Start the clock. He's got one year to prove himself."

Another cough. "Let's hope Augustine or Anthony don't kill him first. But that's our family's curse - only the strongest becomes Godfather. He's got my blood; he'll face the same trial."

"Understood, Godfather."

Alice frowned. Alexander was just some nobody from the streets, yet his brothers could run billion-dollar empires, command armies of loyal followers, and kill without blinking. He didn't seem to stand a chance!

But as the secretary to the chairman, her fate was tied to his now. If either brother won, she'd be dead meat for backing the wrong horse.

With a hint of doubt, Alice asked, "Godfather, something's odd."

"Mr. Hawthorne asked me to keep his identity a secret, and also his chairmanship. Why he wants to do that? "
