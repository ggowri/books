Chapter 584 Greet Your Wife's Mom

Clay's face felt hot, but he managed a forced smile and said, "Don't worry, I won't get upset. I'll find a way to help Mom get better."

"Good, you're such a good kid. I knew I made the right choice!" Luther beamed, giving Clay a hearty pat on the shoulder.

"Sorry for putting you through this, Clay," Richard added kindly.

At that moment, Sophie had a thought. She stepped up to Gill and said sweetly, "I just saw Nancy come back with her boyfriend. He's really handsome, way better looking than this guy."

"Really? So this piece of shit isn't my daughter's man? You all tricked me!" Gill laughed like a child, delighted.

Sophie nodded confidently. "She'll be here any minute."

"That's fantastic! I can't wait to see Nancy's husband. I hope Nancy has a bunch of kids; that would make me so happy!"

Gill clapped her hands excitedly.

The room fell silent, everyone casting awkward glances at Clay.

Even if Gill wasn't all there mentally, Clay still felt utterly humiliated today.

Luther was annoyed and snapped at Sophie, "What nonsense are you spouting? You know your aunt isn't in a good state of mind, and you're still saying these ridiculous things?"

Sophie pouted defiantly, "Dad, I'm just looking out for Nancy's future. She's beautiful and capable. If she marries this guy... that would be a total waste!"

She shot Clay a disdainful look, clearly unimpressed.

"Shut it! You've got some nerve talking back in front of everyone. Say that again, and I'll tear your mouth apart!" Luther's anger flared.

He favored his eldest daughter, Nancy, much more than his second daughter.

"Sophie, please stop! Don't make your dad angry! Just be quiet!" Clara quickly pulled her daughter aside.

"Hmph! You're restricting my freedom. My sister can marry whoever she wants. If I don't speak up now, you might force me to marry someone even uglier!"

Sophie continued to shout.

Luther was livid, rolling up his sleeves, ready to confront her.

"Enough!"

Richard's voice boomed, "Everyone shut up! It's my birthday! Are you all here just to argue and give me a heart attack?"

He slammed his hands on the table in frustration.

Every family has its troubles, and the Reynolds family was no different with so many issues at play.

He turned back to Clay with a smile. "Clay, don't take it personally. Everyone has their own opinions. But rest assured, we fully support your engagement with Nancy."

"Thank you, Grandpa Richard. I really appreciate it," Clay said, his spirits lifting.

Alexander would definitely not get the support of Nancy's family.

Richard nodded, satisfied with Clay's abilities and background.

Since Nancy was the eldest daughter and the boss of the Kaylon, even if she didn't like Clay, she had to make sacrifices for the family.

"Pfft! Ugly pig," Sophie muttered under her breath.

Women are usually quite perceptive, especially beautiful ones. Sophie could sense that Clay had ulterior motives.

His beady eyes kept wandering toward her chest, making her uncomfortable.

Just then, footsteps echoed as Nancy walked in, arm-in-arm with Alexander.

Clay's expression soured instantly.

"Wow! My daughter and her husband are back! Now this is my real daughter and her man!" Gill cheered, clapping her hands.

"Mom!" Nancy quickly hugged her mother.

At the same time, she elbowed Alexander, whispering, "Don't just stand there; call her 'Mom'!"

After a moment of hesitation, Alexander finally shyly said, "Mom..."

Suddenly, the atmosphere in the living room became tense.

Everyone looked at Alexander, then turned to Clay, as if a volcano was ready to erupt.

Clay wasn't the only one upset; even the old man and Luther had stern faces, glaring at Alexander.

"My daughter's man is so handsome, way better than that ugly pig! My daughter has great taste!" Gill's words only added fuel to the fire.
