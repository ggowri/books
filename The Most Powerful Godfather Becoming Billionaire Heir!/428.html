Chapter 428 Kidnapping Attempt

The sharp blade felt bone-chillingly cold as Andrew trembled like a frightened rabbit in the snow.

"Please... don't kill me..."

"It's Felix from the Red Umbrella Group. He... he signed a contract with us to have Ashley participate in their research project... it's just a research project!"

Andrew, fearing that Alexander wouldn't believe him, began to swear fervently.

Alexander's expression darkened as he pressed on. "What research project?"

"Genetics... a genetics research project... ah!"

Before Andrew could finish, Alexander kicked him hard in the stomach, causing him to curl up like a shrimp, convulsing on the floor.

"Where is Felix?"

Alexander pressed his foot against Andrew's chest.

Andrew's face turned pale as he stammered, "I don't know, I truly don't know! Alexander, you... you can't treat me like this... I was your father-in-law..."

Alexander delivered another kick to his gut.

"Ugh..."

This time, Andrew felt the pain surge through him like bile rising from his stomach.

"If it weren't for your blood relation to Ashley, you'd already be dead!"

Alexander's deep eyes glimmered with menace.

Andrew visibly relaxed.

"But while you can escape a death sentence, a living punishment is unavoidable. Since you sneaked in here, I'll treat you like a kidnapper."

Alexander gestured to Rosalia, signaling her to deal with Andrew.

With a smile, Rosalia drew her katana, holding it with both hands and mimicking a slashing motion.

Andrew nearly wet himself, pleading desperately. "No! Please don't kill me! I'm begging you... think of Ashley..."

Rosalia let out a tinkling laugh.

"Master said he won't kill you, so I won't. But to think you would kidnap your own granddaughter—you're even lower than an animal."

With that, the blade flashed twice.

Blood marks appeared on Andrew's wrists and ankles as the sharp edge bit into his flesh.

It wasn't until the blood began to seep out that Andrew truly felt the excruciating pain and opened his mouth to wail.

Rosalia stuffed a filthy sock into his mouth to silence him.

"Wah! Alex...ander, you... bastard..."

Andrew struggled and thrashed desperately.

The cuts Rosalia made had severed the tendons in his hands and feet, rendering him helpless.

Alexander waved his hand dismissively.

"Call the police, let them handle it."

With that, he headed straight for his study.

Now filled with the Force at its peak, Alexander was eager to try carving out one hundred and eight Spellweave Patterns.

"Yes, master..."

Rosalia watched Alexander disappear, running her tongue across her painted lips.

She'd had him right where she wanted him, until this old man showed up.

Furious, she turned her anger on Andrew, drawing a dagger and making a shallow cut on her own arm.

Blood trickled from the wound - just enough to make her point.

Andrew froze, watching the beautiful woman with growing dread.

"Breaking in, assault with a weapon... that's what, twenty or thirty years in prison? You'll be celebrating your retirement behind bars."

Her words made Andrew's blood boil.

.....

In the study, Alexander wasted no time. He took out a carving knife and pulled out a piece of exquisite gemstone to start carving.

To complete the magic circle, he had to carve out one hundred and eight Spellweave Patterns in one go.

Last time, he was just three Patterns away from success.

His knife danced across the stone, and in no time, dozens of intricate and profound Spellweave Patterns appeared on the gemstone.

Come on!

Alexander showed no signs of slackening, taking a deep breath as he quickened his pace.

In an instant, he had carved out one hundred Patterns.

The last eight Patterns consumed more and more of the Force, each becoming increasingly difficult.

"One hundred and one!"

"One hundred and two... three, four, five..."

In the blink of an eye, he returned to the spot where he had previously failed.

At this moment, Alexander felt a bit fatigued, but the Force still surged within him.

He channeled all his Force, and suddenly, his blood rushed like a tidal wave, propelling his spirit to an unprecedented peak.

His mind cleared, and all fatigue vanished in an instant.
