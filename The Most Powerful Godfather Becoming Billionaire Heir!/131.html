Chapter 131 The Plan

By around 10 a.m. the next day, Alexander finally left Ava's house, feeling completely refreshed.

Even though Ava's period wasn't over, the two had explored every way to be close except for that final step.

Having a beautiful woman pleasing him left Alexander feeling incredibly satisfied and full of pride.

When he arrived back at his Beverly Hills residence, he immediately noticed a familiar black sedan parked at the door.

Standing nearby were Andrew, Belinda, and Jane.

All three looked pale, with dark circles under their eyes, as if they hadn't slept at all.

"Alexander, sorry to bother you again!" As soon as Alexander entered, Andrew rushed over, nodding and bowing with unprecedented humility and respect.

"What are you doing here again?" Alexander's tone was ice cold.

"We're here to sincerely apologize. Please, for the five years you were with Jane and for Ashley's sake, can you help us? If not, the Fairfax family will go bankrupt."

Andrew broke down, tears and snot running down his face as he pleaded.

In a desperate attempt to raise the 40 million, he'd mortgaged nearly everything the Fairfax family owned, only to be swindled out of it all by Jackson.

If they couldn't pay by month's end, bankruptcy was inevitable.

Alexander ignored him and headed for the door.

Suddenly, Andrew and Belinda both took Alexander's arms and started crying.

"Alexander, we're sorry. We mistreated you. We were terrible people, blinded by prejudice. Please, forgive me."

Andrew pleaded while repeatedly slapping himself hard.

Belinda kept apologizing, her former arrogance nowhere in sight.

Jane stood there in silence, looking uncomfortable.

Andrew urged his daughter. "Jane, you're Ashley's mother—beg Alexander. He won't stand by and let Ashley's grandfather go bankrupt."

Jane hesitated, conflicted. After a long pause, she finally lowered her head deeply.

"Alexander, I'm sorry. Please, help us..."

She felt deeply humiliated and full of regret, but the looming threat of bankruptcy forced her to beg Alexander once more.

Andrew quickly added. "Alexander, could we at least get a basic distributor role for your magic powder? Just enough to keep us afloat."

Alexander looked at his fawning ex-father-in-law, then glanced at the tearful Jane, feeling both satisfaction and an inexplicable complexity.

The Fairfax family's downfall was the result of their own greed and lack of integrity.

If it weren't for Ashley, he would have slapped them even harder.

Alexander responded coldly, "Forget about being distributors. For Ashley's sake, I'll track down Jackson. He's not getting away with impersonating me and stealing money."

With that, Alexander stepped into his villa, slamming the door shut.

Andrew and Belinda dared not stop him and could only wail.

Jane's face turned from pale to red, unsure what to think.

Watching her from the window, Alexander thought Jane wasn't worth any sympathy.

She'd provoked him multiple times, and although he'd let it slide for Ashley's sake, he didn't forgive her entirely.

Alexander began planning his next moves.

He had Betty rename Wolf Gang to the Ultimate Alliance, not out of impulse, but to build a force he could fully control.

With the magic powder as a foundation, he now had some major tasks lined up.

First, have Betty expand the Ultimate Alliance and establish its headquarters in Loscity.

Second, gather materials to forge Lightsabers successfully.

The gemstone auction Oscar mentioned was only a week away, so he'd need to get enough money ready by then.

Third, capture Sebastian and make him tell where Guzman was.

As for finding Jackson, Alexander wasn't concerned.

Back at Astral Bistro, Alexander had left a Force mark on Jackson without him knowing.

He could still sense the general direction of the mark, indicating Jackson hadn't gone too far from Loscity and was hiding somewhere nearby.
