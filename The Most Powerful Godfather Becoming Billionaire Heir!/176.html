Chapter 176 Death

A few hundred yards away, a sleek Maybach parked by the roadside.

David, peering through binoculars, was entertained, laughing heartily.

"Oh, Alexander, you think you can take me on? Today's the day I get to watch you fall!"

Meanwhile, Alexander turned to the driver. "You keep Ms. Blackburn safe. I'll handle this."

"Are you sure you can take them on alone?" Ava asked, gripping his arm tightly.

Alexander replied, his gaze steady, "Don't worry.

"They may be numerous, but to me, they're just a herd of sheep. No matter how many there are, a lion isn't afraid of a flock."

With that, he bolted into the fray like a predator on the hunt.

His first targets were the gunmen wielding submachine guns.

He moved like a striking cheetah, the air filled with the sounds of gunfire, screams, and the sickening crunch of bones.

One by one, the gunmen fell, with a few managing to fire off wild shots before they met their end.

In the blink of an eye, Houtte's crew had lost over a dozen men.

But Houtte's forces were numerous, and soon Alexander found himself surrounded.

Blades and metal pipes rained down on him.

"Alexander, no matter how tough you think you are, I'm going to break every bone in your body today!" Houtte sneered with confidence.

A sudden explosion of noise erupted as a group of Houtte's men collapsed, seven or eight of them hitting the ground.

Houtte dove for cover, his heart racing. "Does he have grenades or something?"

His smile vanished, replaced by a look of sheer terror.

Alexander slammed his fist into the ground, creating a deep crater in the hard earth.

Debris flew everywhere, sending the surrounding men reeling in blood and chaos.

With two powerful punches, Alexander incapacitated over twenty of Houtte's men.

Each one that fell bore the marks of violence—bullet holes, shattered limbs, or worse.

In less than a minute of fighting, Houtte's fifty-plus men dwindled to just a handful.

The ground was soaked with blood, bodies sprawled in grotesque positions, and weapons littered the scene like a battlefield.

The horrific sight sent shockwaves through the remaining fighters, forcing them to retreat in fear.

Alexander, calm and collected, strode toward Houtte as if he were taking a leisurely stroll.

Suddenly, a thug circled around behind him, brandishing a machete and charging in.

But Alexander, as if sensing the attack, didn't even turn around. He pivoted and delivered a powerful kick, sending the attacker flying, then continued to approach Houtte, whose face went pale.

Every Cripple member who dared to intervene found themselves screaming as they crumpled at Alexander's feet.

Houtte's legs trembled uncontrollably. He had heard of Alexander's prowess but never imagined it would be this terrifying.

He had witnessed countless gang wars, but none compared to the sheer brutality unfolding before him today.

Alexander, unarmed, had already taken down more than forty of his men.

The few remaining were nothing more than frightened rabbits, either cowering or desperately trying to hide.

This experience was utterly mind-blowing for Houtte.

"Is this guy even human?!"

Ava watched in stunned silence, her mouth agape.

Her once-pale, exquisite face flushed. She was mesmerized by Alexander's prowess. He was simply stunning in his ferocity.

But for Houtte, the charming figure of Alexander was now the sign of death itself.

"Whoever kills him, I'll pay 10 million dollars!" Houtte shouted, his voice shaking.

When the reward was great, courage often followed.

Desperation fueled his men, who suddenly seemed to be on steroids, raising their weapons and charging at Alexander with reckless abandon.

Others scrambled to grab blood-soaked submachine guns, unleashing a hail of bullets in his direction.

With the power of the Force enhancing him, Alexander moved with such velocity that he became little more than a blur.

Not only did he evade bullets, but he glided through the chaos in the crowd like a wraith, leaving no one able to touch him. Despairing screams erupted around him, and the sounds of bodies hitting the ground rang out in the area.

David, watching the horrific scene through his binoculars, felt his entire body tremble in fear.

Suddenly, David caught a glimpse of Alexander turning and flashing a grin directly at him through the lens.

At first, David thought it might just be a trick of the light, but there was no mistaking it—Alexander was genuinely smiling at him, as if their eyes were locked across the distance.

"How could he see me from this far away? That's impossible!" Panic surged within him.

The binoculars slipped from David's trembling hands and clattered to the ground as he stumbled backward, racing toward his car.
