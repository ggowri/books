Chapter 945 Full of Confidence

"We're from the Ghost, not some small-time group like Guzman or Felix Vanderbilt. They wouldn't even qualify to join us!

"So if you don't want to end up dead, you'd better hand over everything we want."

Cloe's expression darkened—threatening Alexander here on Treasure Island, in the Brilliant Group's building? They clearly had no respect for him.

Just two Ghost operatives, thinking they could pull this off.

"You want me to hand over this, hand over that, but have you ever stopped to think if you can actually take it from me?"

Alexander's words almost made Cynthia explode with anger.

"Honestly, I have no idea what dying feels like, so I'd love it if you Ghost folks could actually make that happen. Think you can manage?"

"Alexander, you're way too arrogant!"

Cynthia's face twisted, her voice dripping with venom,

"The Ghost has unmatched power. Even someone like four-star General Quentin Thompson is just a low-level member to us.

"And you screwed up the Sakra Society's Apocalypse Project, stalling the immortality serum's development. Our leaders are furious!

"So, hand over everything you have to make up for it, and maybe the higher-ups will let you live. Otherwise, not just you, but everyone around you will pay the price."

Cynthia's threat made Alexander's rage flare.

Everything Alexander had done was for his loved ones, to keep them safe.

Cynthia's words were like twisting a knife in his heart.

Alexander put on a cold smile, his eyes filled with menace. "Heh, right now, I don't want to kill you. After the auction's over, I'll deal with you."

The middle-aged man beside Cynthia suddenly grinned. "I'll make you beg for mercy later."

His eyes were full of murderous intent, and his cruel smile was chilling.

Alexander sized him up, his All-seeing eyes seeing right through the man's secrets.

This guy was a master of sneak attacks with hidden weapons—his sleeves were rigged with devices filled with tiny black poison needles.

The poison smelled just like the powder Cynthia had tried to use on Alexander earlier—absolutely vicious stuff.

Cynthia let out a smug laugh,

"Alexander, you really think you're untouchable, but you're already poisoned, aren't you?

"I'm here for a few auction items myself. When it's all over, you'll see what the Ghost can do.

"You're just some nobody who got a bit of cash and power, thinking you can take on the Ghost? Keep dreaming!"

Alexander couldn't be bothered to respond—he just kicked back, crossing his legs, waiting for the auction to start.

Cloe leaned in close, whispering in his ear, her voice worried.

"Sir, are you really poisoned? Should I call someone?"

"No need, I'm fine."

Seeing Alexander so relaxed, with nothing seeming out of the ordinary, Cloe started to calm down.

But the name "the Ghost" still weighed heavily on her mind.

"Don't worry. I've got this. Leave it all to me, we'll be fine."

Alexander's confidence was unwavering.

"Alright..."

Cloe still felt uneasy. Those two were from the Ghost, and besides using poison, who knew what else they had up their sleeves?

But she trusted Alexander's abilities.

This auction organized by the Brilliant Group had some truly rare items.

Since Treasure Island was outside the law, it had treasures you wouldn't even find on the black market.

After Alexander's reforms, Treasure Island had become the most trustworthy black market in the world.

Many powerful figures wanted to buy and sell discreetly, so they turned to Treasure Island's auctions.

The commissions collected recently were astronomical—that was why the Ghost had set their sights on it.

Finally, the auction officially began.

The auctioneer stepped up, announcing the rules loudly before signaling the staff to present the first item.

"Ladies and gentlemen, our first lot today—five million tons of wheat from a major grain trader. Starting price—five billion dollars, with minimum increments of ten million dollars!"

As soon as the words left his mouth, the crowd erupted into chatter.

Even Alexander raised an eyebrow, surprised at how bold the auction had become.

Five million tons of wheat—that was more than some small countries produced in a year.
