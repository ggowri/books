Chapter 195 The Death

Although many of them held flashlights, the faint light was useless against the rainy night.

A flash of lightning illuminated the night sky, revealing the Federer family members with sinister and twisted expressions, like demons from hell.

Silently, Alexander had already approached them from the shadows.

A gleam of his knife flashed by, and the person’s throat was swiftly cut by Alexander.

At the same time, he quickly retreated into the darkness.

"Thud!"

A body fell heavily to the ground, splashing water.

Then another cold flash zipped by.

"Sizzle!"

A sharp blade pierced through the chest of a man in black; his heart was already stabbed through, blood gushing out.

Alexander moved like a predator, like a Death the night, with bodies dropping to the ground one after another.

In the blink of an eye, seven or eight members of the Federer family had fallen, each with a single fatal blow.

However, the vast majority of the Federer family members didn’t know about this because of the heavy rain.

Alexander, like a lurking demon in the shadows, took down at least one person with every strike, continuing to harvest lives.

Augusta and the others monitored the situation with night vision goggles, and upon seeing Alexander's rampage, they were filled with excitement.

Unfortunately, the torrential rain made sniping impossible.

Yet, watching Alexander eliminate enemies one after another made their blood boil with adrenaline.

"Boom!"

Thunder roared, accompanied by lightning, and Edward was the first to notice something amiss.

"Everyone be alert; there are enemies!"

Edward shouted in anger.

But it was too late; over twenty members of the Federer family had already been killed.

Their bodies lay in the muddy rainwater, with crimson blood flowing everywhere under the downpour.

The smell of blood mixed with the fresh air, creating a slightly acrid scent.

Alexander continued his slaughter, and Edward's warning only heightened his men's panic.

They were like headless flies, looking around in confusion, unable to catch sight of Alexander.

Each time Alexander appeared, an enemy fell, like a tiger among sheep, taking them down one by one.

More and more bodies collapsed into the rain, blood splattering everywhere.

Edward looked horrified; with his abilities, he could barely make out a ghostly shadow moving through the dark rain, killing everyone.

The enemy was like a farmer harvesting wheat, cutting down the Federer family members one after another.

"Who the hell is this? Who's killing our people?!"

Edward was both furious and shocked, feeling deeply helpless.

He prided himself on being the best fighter of the Federer family, yet in this moment, he felt powerless.

With each fallen brother, nearly half of the hundred were gone before they realized it.

If this continued, they would surely be wiped out; it was a matter of life and death.

"Come here, gather around me!"

Edward roared in anger.

As Edward shouted desperately, the remaining members snapped to attention, surrounding him.

They faced outward, backs to Edward, forming an unbreakable defense circle.

Without urging, they all widened their eyes, waiting for the cunning killer in the darkness to appear.

When Alexander eliminated another lagging member of the Federer family, they finally spotted him.

"There! Kill him!"

With a furious roar from Edward, dozens of people fired their guns, while others threw knives, axes, and various weapons at Alexander.

They didn't care if they might accidentally injure their own people; they were attacking Alexander wildly.

But Alexander had long vanished into the dark storm.

The Federer family was in a frenzy, knowing that if they didn't kill him, they would be in serious danger.

They rushed crazily toward the direction where Alexander was.

Flames flickered from the guns, and cold weapons glimmered as they flew through the air.

However, to Alexander, their speed of attack was as slow as snails.

Whether bullets or cold weapons, they were far away from him.

Yet, Alexander didn't stop at all, moving like a ghost and continuously killing members of the Federer family on the outside of the circle.

No matter how wildly the bullets flew or how the knives and axes were thrown, not even the edge of Alexander's clothes was touched; instead, several members of the Federer family were accidentally injured.
