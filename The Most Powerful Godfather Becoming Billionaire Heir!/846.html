Chapter 846 Quite the Presence

More than twenty bodyguards stepped out of the other Mercedes, all dressed in black suits and wearing sunglasses, exuding an intimidating presence.

They scanned the area before striding toward Alexander.

"Ah! The leader and the family head are both here!"

Shirley, who was hosting the interview, jumped in surprise.

After being hypnotized by Alexander, Shirley had stumbled out of the hall in a daze and only now regained full awareness.

Seeing Sean, the head of the Walsh family, and Norma, the leader of the Dragon Gang, both present, Shirley trembled in fear, unsure of what major event had occurred.

It was just an interview; there was no need to involve both the family head and the gang leader.

The Dragon Gang had always been under the Walsh family, with the head typically doubling as the leader.

But in this generation, things were different. Although Sean and Norma were cousins, their elders did not get along.

Both were equally powerful, so Sean became the head of the Walsh family, while Norma led the Dragon Gang.

The two maintained a delicate balance of power, often clashing.

"Tell them to clear out, get them all out of the way!"

Shirley waved her baton, directing her subordinates to drive the hundreds of job applicants away to avoid blocking the path.

"Who are those people? They look so imposing that even Shirley has to step aside for them."

"You must be clueless; that's Sean Walsh, the head of the Walsh family, and Norma Walsh, the leader of the Dragon Gang."

"What! It's those two powerhouses. What are they doing here?"

The scene erupted with chatter, excitement buzzing among both bystanders and applicants.

Figures of this caliber weren't people they could usually see up close.

Alexander stood still, watching as they approached, his sharp gaze fixed on Sean and Norma.

Sean's face was slightly pale, his eyes shadowed with gloom and occasionally flashing with menace. His thin lips hinted at a ruthless nature.

Norma was young and stunning, dressed in a red, high-slit cheongsam that revealed her fair, shapely legs, with the slit hinting at the top of her thigh.

"Norma, why did you invite Alexander here? He took Devil Island from us. Why bring him here?

"You made this decision without telling me first."

Sean's fierce gaze swept the area before settling on where Alexander stood.

"Ha, what's wrong with me inviting him? The Sakra Society has been pressuring the Dragon Gang. Can't I find an ally?"

Norma smiled at Alexander, her lovely face lighting up with a charming grin.

Sean stared at Alexander as well, while the hundreds of applicants had been pushed aside, leaving Alexander standing proudly in place.

"Step aside!"

Two oblivious men tried to shove Alexander aside but were sent flying by a slap from Marvin.

"Stop it, all of you! He is my guest, and you will show him respect," Norma commanded.

Norma ordered, swaying gracefully as she approached Alexander.

Sean and the elderly man followed behind, their faces expressionless.

Marvin kept his eyes on the old man, feeling his heartbeat quicken uncontrollably.

He leaned toward Alexander and whispered, "Boss, that guy seems formidable. I'm feeling a sense of danger; be careful."

Alexander simply gave a slight smile, his expression calm.

Soon, Norma and her group stood before Alexander.

Sean spoke first. "Mr. Hawthorne, hello. I didn't expect you to be so young!"

He carried himself with arrogance, eyes glancing sideways as if he didn't think much of Alexander.

Alexander responded with a slight smile, neither humble nor arrogant. "You're not old yourself. Apologies for the sudden visit."

Sean waved dismissively. "I didn't invite you; Norma did without my approval."

His tone was unfriendly, emphasizing that Norma hadn't sought his approval, as if asserting dominance over her.

Alexander ignored him and gestured to Marvin. "This is my bodyguard, Marvin."
