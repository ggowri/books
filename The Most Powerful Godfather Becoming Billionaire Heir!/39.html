Chapter 39 Assassination

Alexander bowed slightly, then scrutinized Oscar.

Since gaining The Force, Alexander could sense the strength of a person's life force.

Oscar's appeared vigorous and healthy on the surface, but his life force carried the scent of illness, like a candle flame about to be extinguished.

He advised, "Sir Stewart, you'd best get a full medical checkup. I'm afraid you might develop Parkinson's in the near future."

Oscar's face immediately darkened.

"Mr. Hawthorne, I admit I underestimated you before, but there's no need to deliberately curse me, is there?"

Alexander couldn't help but chuckle.

This old coot really didn't know what was good for him, assuming Alexander was as petty as he was.

"Sir Stewart, if you need help with treatment when the time comes, feel free to contact me."

Alexander smiled meaningfully.

"Bah! I'm as fit as a fiddle. My grandson is a doctor, for crying out loud. What makes you think I need your treatment?"

Oscar stormed off in a huff, waving his hand dismissively.

Ava quietly complained to Alexander, "Let bygones be bygones. Why did you have to provoke him?"

Alexander just chuckled, not explaining.

The couple strolled arm-in-arm out of the building, only to find themselves swarmed by a pack of reporters.

The Saint Group's gala was a who's who of bigwigs and Hollywood stars, and these vultures could smell a story a mile away.

Word had spread like wildfire that the Saint Group's CEO was going public about her boyfriend.

The moment Ava and Alexander appeared, the reporters pounced, cameras flashing, and questions flying like bullets from a machine gun.

"Ms. Blackburn, how did you and Mr. Hawthorne meet?"

"Mr. Hawthorne, some say you're just a gold digger. Care to comment?"

"Ms. Blackburn, Mr. Hawthorne, how about an exclusive interview?"

Ava frowned. These reporters were like pesky flies, utterly annoying.

Security guards quickly moved in to hold back the media frenzy.

Amidst the chaos, Alexander's eyes suddenly narrowed, his instincts kicking into high gear.

Danger!

His sharp gaze swept the area, instantly locking onto the threat.

A middle-aged man with blonde hair and blue eyes stood to the right. His crew cut and chiseled features exuded a deadly aura.

His thick neck and bulging muscles strained against his shirt, marking him as a formidable opponent.

Alexander remained impassive, seemingly oblivious to the man's presence. But The Force had already locked onto him.

The life force matched the previous sniper assassination attempt. It was undoubtedly the killer.

Alexander inwardly rejoiced. The prey had walked right into the trap.

"Target confirmed, Alexander!"

William was equally thrilled.

As one of the top thirty hitmen on the Dark Web, his last botched job had really pissed him off.

This time, William was dead set on nailing it.

Even though the target was now the boyfriend of Saint Group's CEO, he couldn't refuse the five million dollars.

William averted his gaze, careful not to make eye contact with Alexander.

The failed sniper attempt had taught him that his opponent was no ordinary mark. Like himself, Alexander was likely a seasoned veteran with an uncanny sense of danger.

His right hand held a voice recorder, feigning an interview. His left hand slowly crept into his waist pack, where a silenced pistol lay primed and ready.

William slowly turned his gun toward Alexander, pulling the trigger with savage intent.

Two muffled pops, barely audible over the clamor of surrounding reporters, went unnoticed by everyone except Alexander.

As expected of a top-notch assassin, meticulous planning and ruthless execution.

Everything was going according to plan, smoothly as silk.

William was already mentally patting himself on the back, feeling a surge of pride at taking down such a formidable target.

At less than six feet away with two shots fired, William couldn't fathom how Alexander might dodge this time.

Unless he wasn't human!

In the next instant, William froze in disbelief. Impossible!

The bullets had passed through nothing but a blur, missing their mark entirely.

Alexander, having anticipated this, was ready the moment William squeezed the trigger.

Tapping into The Force, he pushed off with his foot, and the world seemed to slow to a crawl.

Time itself appeared to stand still, everything moving in slow motion to Alexander's eyes, even the frozen expressions on everyone's faces.
