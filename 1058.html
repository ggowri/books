As Wesson finished speaking, a heavy silence fell over the crowd.

"Run?" they thought, but where would they even go?

They had spent centuries, even millennia, building their roots in Rosmandi.

If they fled, where could they possibly find refuge?

Escape to another country?

Those places had their own superpowers, and stepping into someone else's territory meant competing for resources. Would they really be worry-free?

It would only lead to more bloodshed.

Their situation wouldn't necessarily improve if they ran.

As for preserving their bloodlines?

The major Ancient Families and superpowers had already discreetly sent their promising younger generations away. That was a non-issue.

So, the only option left was to stand and fight the Aberrants head-on.

If they won, everything would be fine.

They would still be the Ancient Families and superpowers of Rosmandi. If they lost, then they could think about escaping.

Gradually, a fierce determination ignited in their eyes.

Wesson declared, sensing the shift in mood, "Looks like everyone's on the same page now!

"We're all in this together. If we don't want to end up like rootless animals, we have no choice but to face the Aberrants!

"Rest assured, the Augustine family will not retreat or beg for mercy! We'll fight the Aberrants to the bitter end! But let's be real; the Aberrants are much stronger now than they were decades ago.

"To be frank, the Augustine family alone can't hold them back! That's why we need your help! Together, we might just have a shot at driving the Aberrants out of Rosmandi once and for all!

"We can't let these brutal, bloodthirsty Aberrants take control!"

With Wesson's words, the fire in their hearts blazed even brighter.

"Mr. Wesson Augustine, just tell us what you need us to do! At this point, it's all or nothing!" someone shouted.

"Absolutely! Whatever you say, we're in!" another voice chimed in.

Wesson let out a sigh of relief.

He said, "Right now, we need to prepare for battle! The Aberrants is advancing fast, and we must gather everyone who can fight!

"All Class A and above fighters need to be ready! Everyone, count how many Class A and above fighters you can muster!

"When the time comes, act in unison!"

The crowd exchanged glances.

One person stepped forward first.

"Alright! We need to act as one; otherwise, the Aberrants will pick us off one by one! I'll start with my family. The Powell family from Newport City has 76 fighters who are Class A and above, including nine Class AA and one Class AAA."

"The Lambert family has 67 Class A and above fighters, with seven Class AA and two Class AAA."

"And the Paltrow family..."

The representatives of over a hundred Ancient Families and a dozen superpowers began to report their numbers.

Soon, the tallies were in.

Wesson's expression grew serious as he scanned the results.

"We've got 38 Class S fighters!

"520 Class AAA!

"Over 4,000 Class AA!

"And more than 30,000 Class A!"

All the warriors from the Ancient Families and superpowers were gathered here, a formidable force indeed!

But despite the impressive numbers, the mood was somber.

Wesson and the leaders of the various factions exchanged worried glances. The truth was glaring—they were still vastly outmatched.

From the information they had got, the Aberrants was estimated to have over 500 Class S clones.

"500," Wesson muttered under his breath, the weight of the situation pressing down on him.

Although the Class S clones were somewhat weaker than their genuine Class S counterparts, they were still a formidable force.

And what did they have on their side?

A mere 38 Class S fighters!

Including Class S members from the Augustine family and the Draco Group, they barely reached fifty.

The disparity in numbers was huge—ten times the difference!

Everyone understood that the outcome of the war depended on the fight between Class S warriors.

The atmosphere was thick with tension.

Wesson's calm voice cut through the unease.

"Don't panic, everyone!

"We hold the advantage of terrain! This is the Augustine family's territory, and we've fortified it for centuries. It won't be easy for anyone to breach our defenses!

"Plus, we have the Godfather's scepter on our side!

"This battle won't be easy, but if we act together, we'll have a fighting chance! Now, I need all Class A and above warriors to gather in the square!

"We need to strategize and set our defenses!"

At Wesson's words, the tension in the room eased slightly, though most still wore expressions of concern.

In no time, over 40,000 Class A and higher warriors had gathered in the Augustine Estate's square.

They were organized into four battle formations. Each consisted of over 10,000 members, including around 10 Class S fighters, more than 100 Class AAA, and over 1,000 Class AA warriors.

Each group was assigned to defend a specific direction.

Just as they were finalizing their preparations, the Aberrants' army arrived.
