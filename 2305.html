Malcolm barked, "Hand over all your tokens, now! And don't even think about making a run for it! You haven't got a snowball's chance in hell of getting away from us. And forget about registering those tokens now. Too late. We won't give you the chance.

"Those tokens, worth 100 thousand points, whether they were meant for someone else or not, now belong to Mr. Maurice!"

After speaking, Malcolm sized Clara up.

Not because he was checking her out—after all, their every move was being broadcast across Frost Peak City, visible to everyone.

Even if he had balls of steel, he wouldn't dare try anything here.

Plus, there were referees around.

Malcolm sized Clara up because she suddenly looked familiar.

"Hey, Mr. Maurice! This woman looks like Osborne's teammate! No wonder she rings a bell. Haha, she's one of Osborne's crew!"

Hearing this, Conrad took a good look at Clara, then let out a cold laugh. "So, you're one of that good-for-nothing Osborne's team members. So, these tokens were actually meant for Osborne? You haven't registered them because you haven't found him, right?

"Hahaha! That useless Osborne is long dead on the sixth layer. He probably kicked the bucket three days ago... And here you've been holding onto these tokens, waiting like a fool for him to come back so you could hand them over and help him climb the ranks.

"Hahaha, but in the end, it's me who's going to cash in!"

With that, Conrad waved his hand and ordered, "Enough screwing around. Beat her and grab all the tokens she's got!"

"I'll handle it!" Malcolm smirked. "This chick's only ranked two hundred and ninety-nine on the strength leaderboard. She's no match for us. I can take her down with one hand tied behind my back.

"You guys just hang back and watch."

With that, Malcolm gripped his Sacred Artifact and strode towards Clara. "Hey, bitch. You gonna hand them over willingly, or do I have to beat the crap out of you and take them?"

Taking a deep breath, Clara said, "If you want these tokens, you'll have to pry them from my cold, dead hands. You think I'm just going to hand them over? Dream on!"

As she spoke, a dark iron chain appeared in her hand.

Malcolm scoffed, eyeing the chain in Clara's hand with disdain.

"Hahaha, a quasi-Sacred Artifact? You don't even have a real Sacred Artifact and you're trying to act tough in front of me? That's hilarious! I'll take you down in one move!"

With that, Malcolm suddenly launched his attack.

He unleashed a powerful surge of Sacred Power, channeling it into his Sacred Artifact. A terrifying wave of energy instantly threatened to engulf Clara.

But Clara remained calm and fearless.

She took a deep breath and prepared to strike back.

The black chain in her hand shot towards Malcolm with lightning speed.

Before he could even react, he was completely ensnared.

Malcolm's face turned ashen. He immediately tried to break free, unleashing his Sacred Power.

But to his utter horror, he couldn't budge.

"How the hell is this happening?!" Malcolm's face darkened.

He was ranked eightieth on the strength leaderboard, while Clara was two hundred and ninety-nine. Their strength levels were worlds apart. He expected to crush her like a bug.

That was what Malcolm thought would happen.

But in this real-time showdown, Malcolm was shocked to find that Clara had managed to bind him with just a quasi-Sacred Artifact chain.

No matter how hard he struggled, he couldn't break free.

To everyone's surprise, Clara dodged his attack with astonishing speed. In the blink of an eye, she was dashing towards him, her palm crackling with powerful Sacred Power as she aimed a strike at his chest.

Malcolm's face drained of all color.

Trapped by the binding chains, he had nowhere to run.

If Clara's attack landed, he was toast. Even if he didn't die, he'd be seriously screwed.

In that desperate moment, all thoughts of pride went out the window.

Malcolm let out a panicked shout. "Help me! This woman's got some freaky tricks up her sleeve! Her quasi-Sacred Artifact chains have got me tied up good! I can't break free! Hurry up and save my ass!"

Malcolm's desperate cries left Conrad and the others utterly stunned.

None of them had expected Malcolm to be outmatched by Clara.

Conrad scoffed and said, "This woman's a slippery fish.

"Fine, I'll step in myself and take care of her. Can't afford any more surprises."

With that, Conrad vanished and reappeared in front of Malcolm. A haughty smirk appeared on his face as he raised his hand and met Clara's oncoming strike head-on.

The impact sent shockwaves through the air.

Conrad barely flinched, while Clara was sent flying. She spat out a mouthful of blood and crashed into the thick glacier like a rag doll, leaving a massive crater.

Blood trickled from the corner of her mouth as she glared at Conrad, her eyes filled with frustration.

Conrad was a beast, ranking fifteenth on the power list.

There was no way Clara could take him on, especially with her bare hands against his Sacred Artifact gloves.

The fact that she'd even managed to make Conrad stumble was a feat in itself.

But the gap in their strength was just too wide.

Clara coughed up another mouthful of blood, her eyes blazing with fury as she stared down Conrad and his gang.

"Grab all her tokens," Conrad commanded.

"You got it!"

Malcolm, finally freed from the chains thanks to Conrad, glared at Clara.

He was absolutely livid. If it weren't for Conrad's timely intervention, he would have been the one eating dirt.

That was a hard pill to swallow.

"Nice moves, bitch. Too bad they won't save you. Now, those tokens of yours are ours."

Malcolm waved his hand, and a shower of tokens flew out of Clara's body.

There were over a hundred, including seventy-two tokens from the fourth layer and two hundred and twenty-five from the third.

Converted into points, that was a whopping 945 hundred, almost 100 thousand!

These tokens were supposed to be for Osborne, but now they were Conrad's spoils of war.

Clara's eyes turned red with rage, but she was powerless to stop them.

She was no match for Conrad, let alone their whole team. Defeated, she could only watch helplessly as they claimed her hard-earned tokens.

Conrad was ecstatic.

He quickly pressed the tokens against his nameplate to register them.

His state duel points skyrocketed nearly 100 thousand, from 1.08 million to over 1.17 million.

His ranking on the points list jumped from ninth to eighth.

Conrad let out a triumphant "Hahaha!" and sneered.

"Blame it on being Osborne's teammate, woman. If you're not happy, come at me anytime.

"Let's roll, fellows!"

Then he arrogantly led his team away, leaving Clara seething with anger and helplessness.
