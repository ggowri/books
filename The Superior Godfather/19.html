Chapter 19 A Moment of Triumph

Anastasia's unexpected comment made Brian blink in surprise, but a wave of gratitude quickly followed.

The soft feeling of her touch on his arm only made him more drawn to her.

As Sophia noticed the beautiful woman standing beside Brian, her face soured instantly. The jealousy was hard to miss.

Ryan's eyes widened in surprise. His gaze locked onto Anastasia with an intensity that was almost burning.

She was stunning. Her beauty, figure, and presence easily overshadowed Sophia. This woman was in a whole different league.

Robert and Mary were equally stunned.

They couldn't understand why someone as beautiful as Anastasia would be hanging around Brian.

Anne's face darkened too, though there was a flicker of something else in her expression. She was clearly upset.

"Buying a house? Hahaha, good one!"

Sophia burst out laughing as if she'd just heard the best joke.

"Brian's a nobody, a poor loser. You seriously think he can buy a house here?

"Each villa here costs millions of dollars. Tell me, how could this failure afford one?"

Ryan chimed in, grinning mockingly. "Hey, beautiful, this guy just got divorced and walked away with nothing. He doesn't have a penny to his name. Don't let him trick you."

Robert and Mary nodded in agreement.

"Exactly! Don't believe him for a second. This guy's broke. There's no way he's buying anything."

"You're way too beautiful to fall for his tricks. Don't let him scam you for money or worse."

Anastasia's smile was almost teasing as she looked at Sophia. Her eyes carried an air of sympathy, almost as if she pitied her.

If only Sophia had treated Brian better. With his abilities and potential, he could've helped her rise to the top.

But instead, she had tossed him aside for someone like Ryan. It was laughable.

She leaned into Brian's side, her voice light and teasing. "I'd love for him to scam me for money and affection, but he's just not that type, you know?"

Before they could say anything else, Anastasia continued, "As for calling Brian a loser and a broke guy? You've got no idea what you're talking about. It's honestly embarrassing. His abilities are beyond what any of you could even begin to understand.

"You should be more worried about your own mess than trying to figure mine out. Right, babe?"

Brian couldn't help but feel touched by the way Anastasia was defending him. It was a rare feeling, and he appreciated it deeply.

He hesitated for a second before pulling her closer, wrapping his arm around her slim waist with a smile.

"You're right. They've got their hands full with their own problems, anyway. Their good times are coming to an end."

Sophia's face turned a deep shade of red, her anger boiling over.

She had always seen Brian as a tool, nothing more than a stepping stone.

But now, he was with someone else, and this woman was sticking up for him. That was more than she could handle.

Anne looked just as upset, though there was a hint of jealousy in her eyes.

Brian had been her first, after all.

Ryan clenched his fists, staring hard at Brian. The jealousy was eating him up.

He couldn't stand the fact that Brian had the attention of such a stunning woman.

Robert and Mary were seething with anger.

"You don't get it, do you? We're trying to help you here! You really think this guy..."

Before they could finish their sentence, Brian swiftly grabbed their hands and twisted their fingers with a quick snap.

"Don't you dare point those filthy fingers at my woman."

"Y-You... bastard!" Robert and Mary screamed in pain, clutching their hands, but they knew they were powerless. They couldn't do anything to him.

Just then, the sales intern and the manager walked over, both with big smiles on their faces. The intern handed over the property deed and villa keys with a respectful bow.

"Mr. Edwards, everything's all set. Congratulations! You're now the proud owner of Riverside Villa No. 9."
