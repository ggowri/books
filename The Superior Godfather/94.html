Chapter 94 Killing Dylan

"Mr... Mr. Edwards?"

When Raven saw Brian clearly, his heart dropped, and a cold sweat broke out across his forehead.

The memory of the last time Brian had beaten him senseless, followed by Zoe's punishment, flashed in his mind.

His body trembled uncontrollably from the sheer terror.

Before anyone could react, Raven collapsed to his knees in front of Brian, too scared to stand.

"Mr. Edwards... I didn't know it was you. I didn't mean to offend you. Please, I'm begging you. Punish me however you want."

"Mr. Edwards, we're sorry for the trouble," Raven's men stammered, their faces drained of color.

They had all witnessed what Brian was capable of at the bar.

Nobody dared to cross him, not even their leader.

"What... what's going on?

"Why are they apologizing to him?"

Karen stared in disbelief, her mouth hanging open.

She'd never imagined that Raven and his men would react like this.

Now, she was starting to believe that everything Brian had said about his power was true.

A flood of thoughts rushed through her mind as she looked at Brian, who suddenly seemed like a complete mystery to her.

Dylan was just as stunned. He stared at Raven in shock, unable to believe what he was seeing.

"Mr. Knox, why are you apologizing to this bastard? You're the..."

Before Dylan could finish, Raven slapped him hard across the face.

"Shut up and apologize to Mr. Edwards right now, or you'll regret it!"

"But..."

Dylan was still in shock, his hand on his stinging cheek, struggling to grasp what was happening.

He opened his mouth to argue, but Raven grabbed him and shoved him toward Brian.

"Mr. Edwards, please... we're sorry. We didn't mean any disrespect. Please forgive us..."

Brian sat down calmly in a chair, looking down at them with a cool, almost detached gaze.

"My friend owes him 20 million dollars. I'm here to pay it off. After I gave him 86 million dollars, he still had the nerve to ask my friend to entertain him.

"I'm letting you off the hook for Zoe's sake, but how you deal with this is up to you."

Raven's face went pale.

He couldn't believe what Dylan had done.

It was clear that Dylan had crossed the line, and there was no going back.

In a fit of rage, Raven grabbed Dylan by the collar and snarled, "Is everything Mr. Edwards said true? Did you really do that?"

Dylan hesitated, his face ashen, then reluctantly nodded. "Mr. Knox, I just..."

"Shut your damn mouth! If you want to die, don't drag me into it!"

With that, Raven's fist slammed into Dylan's face, knocking him off balance.

Blood poured from Dylan's mouth, and his face started to swell. He staggered back, confused.

"Mr. Knox, what the hell are you doing? This guy's just a..."

Before Dylan could finish his sentence, Raven drove a knife straight into his chest.

"You..."

Dylan's eyes widened in disbelief as he looked up at Raven, unable to comprehend what was happening.

Raven's expression was cold as he stabbed him again, finishing him off with a brutal final blow.

Raven's gaze swept over the room, and his voice dropped cold as ice.

"Listen carefully. Mr. Edwards is an honored guest of Shadow Fang. Anyone who dares show him disrespect will end up like this.

"Got it?"

"Got it!" the group responded quickly, fear evident in their voices.

Raven nervously stepped forward, offering a bank card with a forced smile.

"Mr. Edwards, I'm sorry for the inconvenience. My men made a mistake.

"Please accept this as a small token of my apology."

Brian didn't even glance at the card, his expression remaining unreadable.

Raven's smile faltered for a second before he quickly shifted his attention to Karen, holding the card out to her instead.

"Mrs. Edwards, I truly apologize for today's events. Please, accept this."

"This is for me?"

Karen blinked, completely taken aback.

The fierce, feared Raven was now acting like a servant in front of them.

And to her surprise, he thought she was Brian's wife.

Her cheeks turned pink, and she could feel her heart race as she processed everything that was happening.

"If you don't take it, I won't leave until you do," Raven insisted, his hand still holding the card out, his face desperate.

With a helpless sigh, Karen took the card. "Alright, I'll take it."

Brian stood up, his demeanor calm and emotionless as he turned to leave.

"Let's go."

Karen quickly followed, still trying to wrap her mind around the whirlwind of events.

"Take care, Mr. Edwards!" Raven said, his voice dripping with politeness as he quickly escorted them out.

As soon as they were out of sight, he exhaled deeply, his whole body relaxing.

He had just narrowly escaped a deadly situation.
