Chapter 163 The Fall of Wolfgang

A voice cut through the tension, sharp and commanding, startling everyone in the room.

Zoe strode in, her black trench coat billowing slightly with each step. Beneath it, a slinky camisole and sheer stockings hugged her figure, exuding a dangerous, magnetic allure. Raven trailed close behind, his own presence icy and unyielding as they descended into the chaos.

"Ms. Guzman?"

Wolfgang's eyes widened. Fear and disbelief flickered across his face, his voice quivering as he spoke.

"Apologize!"

Zoe's face was a mask of frost, her command booming with authority.

Wolfgang froze, utterly baffled. He had no idea why Zoe was demanding he grovel.

Still, his respect, no, his fear, of her ran deep. He bowed his head, swallowing hard.

"Ms. Guzman, I..."

He barely got the words out before Zoe's hand cracked across his face with a vicious slap.

"You brainless fool!"

The blow sent Wolfgang staggering, his cheek swelling instantly, a few teeth crumbling as blood trickled from his lips. Confusion swirled in his mind, heavier now than ever.

What's going on?

Why is Ms. Guzman so furious?

Raven watched from the sidelines, his eyes glinting with pity. He'd been through this wringer before and knew exactly how this played out.

Zoe had made it crystal clear long ago. Brian was to be treated with the same deference as herself. But Wolfgang, that arrogant idiot, had clearly ignored her orders.

Tucker, Lyle, and the others stood dumbfounded, their jaws slack. They couldn't wrap their heads around what was happening.

Why is Ms. Guzman siding with an outsider?

Then, in a moment that left them reeling, their eyes nearly popped out of their skulls.

Zoe, the infamous Crimson Demonic Ascendance, the terror of the underworld, strode straight to Brian and dropped to one knee before him.

"Mr. Edwards," she said, her voice low and laced with shame, "I've failed to discipline my people. I've let you down. Please, punish me as you see fit."

What... What the hell is going on?"

Wolfgang's mind imploded his thoughts into a chaotic whirlwind.

He'd never imagined Zoe, the invincible queen in his eyes, would kneel and apologize to anyone.

And to think, just moments ago, he'd been demanding Brian apologize to him, and worse, he'd dared to make a move on Brian's woman.

A bone-deep chill shot through Wolfgang, from the soles of his feet to the crown of his head. His entire body felt like it had been dunked in ice.

Lyle and Tucker were equally stunned, their minds struggling to process the scene.

They could hardly believe their eyes.

Zoe, the unchallenged empress of Riverwood's underworld, a woman whose reputation sent shivers down spines, was publicly humbling herself before this man.

Even when facing titans like the mayor, Zoe had never so much as blinked, let alone apologized.

Celeste, too, was floored, her exquisite face a canvas of shock and disbelief.

Her mind flashed back to the crisis at Trillion Group. Brian had swooped in with Zoe and her crew, resolving the mess with ruthless efficiency.

She'd assumed Brian and Zoe were just business associates, that Zoe's team was merely hired muscle.

But now? It was clear Zoe wasn't just working with Brian. She was working for him.

Has Brian been a player in the underworld all along?

Just how many secrets is this man hiding?

At that moment, Celeste's curiosity about Brian burned brighter than ever, a spark of fascination igniting in her chest.

Brian's gaze fell on Zoe, cold and devoid of warmth.

"This isn't the first time something like this has happened," he said, his voice flat but heavy with displeasure.

Zoe trembled slightly, a flicker of panic and fear darting through her eyes.

She knew she'd crossed a line this time, not just angering Brian but disappointing him.

She'd issued the directive after Raven's fiasco and made it loud and clear to everyone. And yet, here they were again.

Frustration and guilt churned in her gut, but she swallowed it down, taking a deep breath.

"Mr. Edwards," she said, her tone resolute, "please don't be upset. I promise you, I'll handle this today and give you a resolution you'll find satisfactory."

Brian's expression didn't soften. "Get up," he said simply.

Zoe rose, her gaze snapping to Wolfgang, her face as cold as a winter storm.

"Wolfgang," she said, her voice dripping with menace, "it seems you didn't take my last announcement seriously."

"Your... announcement?"

Wolfgang's face paled as realization hit him like a freight train. His eyes darted to Brian, wide with horror.

"He... He's the Mr. Edwards from your announcement?"

Zoe's eyes were glacial. Without a word, she pulled a dagger from her coat and tossed it at Wolfgang's feet, the blade clattering ominously on the floor.

"That's right," she said. "You know what to do next, don't you? Or do I need to spell it out?"

Wolfgang stared at the dagger, his face ashen, sweat beading on his brow.

"Ms. Guzman, I..."

He opened his mouth to plead, but Zoe's voice cut through like a whip.

"What's that? You want me to do it for you?"

"No... no, I would do it myself!"

Wolfgang knew Zoe's strength, her ruthlessness. Defying her was a death sentence.

Under the weight of everyone's stares, his trembling hands picked up the dagger. With a gritted-teeth grimace, he drove it into his own chest.

Blood was sprayed in a crimson arc.

Wolfgang's eyes bulged, filled with regret and a desperate clinging to life before he collapsed into a lifeless heap in a pool of his own blood.

He'd never dreamed that standing up for a friend would cost him everything.

The sight of Wolfgang's body sent a collective shudder through Lyle, Tucker, and the others. Fear gripped them, their souls practically fleeing their bodies.

In unison, they turned to Brian and Celeste, their voices quaking with desperation.

"Mr. Edwards, Ms. Hawthorne... We were wrong. We're so sorry."

"Mr. Edwards, please, give us a chance to make this right. We'll work with Trillion Group, no strings attached, whatever it takes!"
