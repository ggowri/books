Chapter 155 Lightning-Wielding Meta

Denzel's face went rigid the moment he heard it.

So much for loyalty in times of trouble.

He'd believed Thomas and the others would stick by him when things got rough. But the next thing he knew, they hit him with a cold slap of reality.

"You're pulling your investment?"

"It's not like we want to..." they looked helpless and frustrated. "But with how things are now, we just can't keep this partnership going."

We really were hoping to share in his success and enjoy the good life alongside him.

But he had to screw it all up at the worst possible time.

By now, Denzel's reputation was completely trashed.

Online, he was practically public enemy number one.

If they kept sticking with him, they would not just go down with him—they'd lose every cent they'd invested.

Pulling out wasn't about betrayal; it was survival.

Hearing that, Denzel felt a wave of resignation wash over him.

Still, he wasn't about to let go of that sweet deal with them just yet.

After a brief pause, he spoke up, "Look, I get it, I do. But it's not as bad as you think. Just give me a little more time—"

Before he could finish, Thomas cut him off, "We understand you're in a tough spot. But please, try to understand ours as well... Just give us our money back, alright?"

Another added, "Yeah, Mr. Denzel Vaughn, just return the money. Let's not make this ugly."

Seeing they had made up their mind, Denzel had no choice but to tell them the truth. "Guys, full disclosure—I already put that money into the stock market. Even if I wanted to give it back now, I couldn't."

At that, Thomas, Zac, Eric, and the rest turned pale.

They hadn't expected Denzel to gamble away their money without even telling them.

Glory Group's shares were in free fall, no bottom in sight.

Their money was as good as gone.

Might as well have lit that cash on fire.

Their expressions turned grim. They clenched their fists tight and fixed him with an angry glare.

One of them snapped, "How could you do this?"

Another barked, "I don't care how you do it, I just my money back. Today."

Under their glare, Denzel just shrugged and held his hands up in surrender. "Look, I want to give it back. But you see—my hands are tied.

"How about this—just go home. I'll reach out the second I can pay you back, alright?"

Thomas, Zac, and the others weren't buying it. Their expressions darkened.

They knew Denzel was just stalling. And waiting? God knows how long that would take.

So, they snapped back, "No way. We're not leaving without the money today."

Denzel had already been seething with anger, with nowhere to vent it. Seeing them push like this made his face turned utterly grim.

"Hey, don't push it. I'm trying to be nice here."

Thomas and the others tried to say more, but Denzel cut them off.

"Victor, show them out."

The moment he said that, they exchanged a look and then lunged at Denzel.

"Denzel, you heartless bastard! Give us our money back!"

He really couldn't blame them for losing it like that. They had poured everything they had into this—years of blood, sweat, and sacrifice.

Losing it all now meant they'd be dead broke, just like that.

Victor's face hardened the moment he saw what was going on. He took a step forward, placing himself in front of Denzel.

"Try touching Mr. Denzel Vaughn, and you'll regret it."

The very next second, a burst of crackling energy surged out of Victor's body. Thomas, Zac, Eric, and the others were slammed backwards. They coughed up blood as they hit the ground hard.

Seeing Victor surrounded by streaks of lightning, Thomas, Zac, and the others went pale. Their eyes widened with shock and fear.

"H-He's... a Meta?"

They hadn't expected Victor to be a Meta who wielded lightning elemental energy. None of them dared to make a move.

Denzel, meanwhile, stared them down. His expression was ice-cold and his gaze was filled with unmistakable intent to kill.

He spoke slowly and clearly, "I was going to let you live, figured you might still be useful. But clearly, I was wrong."

They stiffened, a wave of panic hit them all at once.

"Mr. Denzel Vaughn, we—"

They wanted to beg for mercy but Denzel didn't let them.

"Kill them all!"

At those words, their faces drained of color.

Before they turned to flee, Victor struck. In an instant, countless bolts of lightning surged from his body. They transformed into massive serpents of pure electricity that lunged forward and swallowed them whole.

A chorus of screams then erupted through the room. Within seconds, they were reduced to charred husks beyond recognition.

Bet they never saw that coming, even in their wildest nightmares.

"Toss them in the river. Feed the fish," Denzel said like it was nothing, not a trace of emotion in his eyes.

While they were hauling out the bodies, Denzel's phone rang.

His gaze darkened slightly when he saw Anastasia's name flash across the screen.

He pressed to answer.

"Denzel, are you okay? I saw what's going on online—"

Before she could finish, Denzel cut her off, "Oh, spare me the crocodile tears. Don't act like you give a damn. I know you're the one pulling the strings behind all this.

"You think you're the only one who knows those big shots overseas?"
