Chapter 81 Change Attitude

The Evans Villa was bustling with guests, the atmosphere vibrant and lively.

To dispel the embarrassment they had faced recently, Robert and Mary had gathered friends and family to announce the news: Ryan was set to meet the Master of the War Deities and was about to join their ranks.

Upon hearing this, everyone turned to Sophia with shock and disbelief.

Karen, a mature and charismatic woman, was particularly animated.

"Sophia, is what your parents said true? Ryan is being summoned by the Master of the War Deities and is about to join them?"

If Ryan truly became a member of the War Deities, it would effortlessly solve her 20 million dollars in high-interest debts.

As Sophia saw the astonished expressions on everyone's faces, a wave of pride washed over her.

She smiled and replied, "Aunt Karen, it's all true! Ryan has a cousin who is one of the Twelve Blades of the War Deities, the Unparalleled Blade, and he's the one who recommended Ryan to the Master."

With Sophia's confirmation, the crowd erupted in compliments.

"Oh my gosh, is that really the case? Sophia, your taste is impeccable!"

"Exactly! What does it matter that Brian became a partner at Trillion Group? He can't hold a candle to our Ryan!"

"Ryan truly is top among men. Sophia, your judgment is spot on; you've got to look out for us!"

"Right? Our family is counting on you to bring us honor and glory!"

As the flattery poured in, Sophia, Robert, and Mary basked in their moment of triumph.

They had faced a barrage of criticism when Brian became a partner at Trillion Group after Sophia's divorce.

Watching the adoration directed at Sophia, Anne felt a mix of emotions, a sense of unease creeping in.

If Ryan successfully joined the War Deities, it would spell disaster for Brian.

She could only hope he had followed her advice and gone to Polandia.

Just then, Sophia's phone rang.

"Sophia, answer it! It's probably Ryan calling with good news!" Mary urged, a smile on her face.

Nodding, Sophia pressed the button to answer.

"Hello, is this Ms. Sophia Evans, Ryan's fiancée? This is the Riverwood Police Department. I'm afraid Ryan has suffered a psychological breakdown and has jumped from a building... you'll need to come identify the body."

The voice on the other end struck Sophia like a bolt from the blue, leaving her frozen in shock.

"W-what? You... you said Ryan jumped?!"

"Yes, please come to the Beach Hotel to identify the body..."

The officer's words hit her like a freight train, and the weight of the news overwhelmed her. Her vision darkened, and she collapsed, the phone slipping from her grasp.

The sound of the officer's voice echoed from the ground, frantic and urgent. "Hello? Ms. Sophia Evans, can you hear me? Hello?"

"Sophia!"

The sudden turn of events sent Robert and Mary into a panic, rushing to support her as she swayed.

"Sophia, what's going on? What happened?"

As Sophia slowly regained her composure, she recounted the shocking news to the group.

"What did you say? Ryan suffered a breakdown and jumped down the building?"

Robert and Mary's faces turned ashen, as if struck by lightning.

Anne and Karen stood frozen, their expressions a mix of disbelief and horror.

The friends and family present were equally stunned, their faces reflecting shock and confusion.

Then, one of the guests changed his demeanor entirely, looking at Sophia and her family with disdain.

"Ugh, what a waste of time! I really thought Ryan was going to join the War Deities. Turns out he's just a flash in the pan!"

"Right? We bought all these gifts for nothing... might as well take them back for ourselves!"

"Exactly! It would be a waste to leave them with Sophia's family; they'll never recover from this!"

With that, they gathered their gifts and left the Evans Villa, leaving Sophia, Robert, and Mary dumbfounded.

Their faces shifted between shades of blush and pallor.

They were unable to comprehend the sheer superficiality of these people.

The blow was devastating, as if they had fallen from paradise into the depths of despair.

After a long, heavy silence, Mary finally found her voice. "How... how could this happen? Sophia, what exactly went wrong?

"Why would Ryan have a breakdown and kill himself?"

Sophia's voice was laced with bitterness as she spoke softly, her heart heavy, "I... I don't know!

"Let's go to the Beach Hotel and identify the body."

Before long, they arrived at the Beach Hotel, where the grim reality awaited them.

"Ryan, you bastard, get up!

"Ryan, you promised you'd love me forever! Come on, get up!"

As she looked at Ryan's lifeless form, Sophia couldn't hold back her tears any longer.

She broke down, sobbing uncontrollably.

They had even set a wedding date, planning to marry once he joined the War Deities.

But now, all of that was shattered.

Robert and Mary stood by, their faces pale, struggling to understand how everything had spiraled so drastically.

Though Anne and Karen had never been particularly close to Ryan, they wore expressions of complex emotions, caught in the gravity of the moment.

Meanwhile, upstairs, Brian stood by the window, coldly observing the heartbroken Sophia below, his expression devoid of any emotion.

Suddenly, Anne felt a strange sensation and glanced up toward the upper floor.

When she spotted Brian sitting there, she froze in disbelief.

She mused, "What's going on?

Why is Brian here?

Didn't he take the flight I booked for him to Polandia?

Or does Ryan's death somehow involve him?"

Her mind raced with confusion and suspicion.

Brian, sensing her gaze, offered a slight smile before rising and disappearing from her view.
