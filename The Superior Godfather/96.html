Chapter 96 Assassination

By evening, Anastasia arrived at Brian's villa.

She looked every bit the epitome of grace, dressed in a delicate light blue backless gown that made her tall, curvaceous frame look even more stunning.

The elegant fit of the dress enhanced her beauty, while the exposed skin along her back gave her an alluring, almost irresistible aura.

As soon as she stepped inside, the delicious aroma wafting from the kitchen hit her senses, making her stomach growl in anticipation.

"Wow, it smells amazing... What are you cooking today, Brian?"

"You'll find out soon enough..." he teased, before glancing at her with curiosity.

"By the way, where's Eliza? It's just you tonight?"

Anastasia set her LV bag down on the coffee table and gave him a playful smile.

"You're asking about Eliza? You've got your eye on her, don't you?"

Brian raised an eyebrow, maintaining his serious demeanor. "Well, Eliza's definitely beautiful, confident, and full of charm... But I think I prefer your type."

"Oh?" Anastasia leaned back on the couch, crossing her legs as she smiled at him. "And what kind of type do you think I am?"

Brian's gaze lingered for a moment, but he quickly regained his composure and replied, "To me, you're like a queen, elegant and stunning, decisive and bold, with a sharp mind and a deep, fascinating personality.

"You can come off as cold and unapproachable, but in reality, you're easy to talk to and incredibly understanding."

Anastasia's smile grew, and she leaned in slightly.

"I didn't realize you had such high praise for me. Are you sure you're not just flattering me to get on my good side?"

Brian quickly raised his hand, looking serious. "I swear, it's all true. No flattery here."

"Is that so?"

She raised an eyebrow. "Well, if you want to know my thoughts on you..."

Brian chuckled, a wry smile on his face.

"I'm a divorced man, so I'm guessing your opinion of me isn't exactly glowing."

Anastasia rested her elbow on her knee, cupping her chin with her hand, giving him an alluring look.

"I've met a lot of young, successful men. Compared to them, you're nothing extraordinary... Maybe even a little ordinary. But in my eyes, you're the most handsome and the one with the most potential.

"I believe in you, and I really hope one day we can be more than just friends. So... you better start working harder!"

She flashed him a playful wink, making it even harder for Brian to keep his composure.

That wink was irresistibly seductive.

Brian felt a rush of heat flood through him, his heart racing.

Without thinking, he responded, "Don't worry. I'll do my best."

Just as the moment hung in the air, a chilling voice suddenly pierced through the atmosphere.

"Kid, you're about to die, and you're still here flirting?"

The voice caught both Brian and Anastasia off guard.

As their eyes turned toward the source, two black-clad assassins dropped from the upstairs balcony.

They had been waiting in Brian's bedroom, planning to strike once he was resting.

But seeing him not only cooking dinner but also entertaining a guest was more than they could tolerate. So, they decided to reveal themselves.

Brian's eyes narrowed as he stared coldly at the two men. "Who are you?"

"We're here to take your life."

In a flash, the two assassins lunged forward, their knives aimed straight for Brian's throat.

They were so fast that they seemed like shadows, their daggers slicing through the air with deadly precision.

"Damn, they're fast!"

Brian's instincts kicked in. He swiftly sidestepped, evading their strikes, and in the same fluid motion, he planted his hands on the couch and delivered a powerful kick.

The two assassins barely managed to block, crossing their arms in front of them, but the force of his strike sent them flying backward.

Before they could regain their footing, Brian appeared in front of them like a blur. With one swift, forceful movement, he slammed both palms into their chests.

Blood sprayed out as they were sent flying across the room, crashing into the walls with a sickening thud.

The two men lay on the ground, pale and gasping, their faces filled with shock and fear.

The intelligence had made them believe Brian was a weakling, but now they realized just how wrong they were.

"The intel was wrong. Let's go!"

Without a second thought, they turned and leapt for the window, intending to escape.

"Think you can leave that easily?"

Brian's eyes flashed with cold determination. Like a predator on the hunt, he charged after them without hesitation.
