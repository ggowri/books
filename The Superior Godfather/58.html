Chapter 58 Desperate Karen

At six o'clock in the evening, in the Trillion Group headquarters conference room, the shareholders and executives, led by Thomas, wore grim expressions.

They had been waiting in the company for a whole day, and now it was already past the end of get off work time, but the chairman had not yet arrived.

Looking at Celeste, who hung up the phone, Thomas asked in a deep voice, "Ms. Hawthorne, what did the chairman say? When will he come?"

Celeste looked at Thomas and said calmly, "The chairman said to keep waiting. If you don't want to wait, you can withdraw your shares and resign."

She had anticipated Brian's intention to use this opportunity to assert his authority, so she had to support him unconditionally.

"What?"

The room fell silent as they exchanged incredulous looks.

Thomas' expression darkened further.

He had called this meeting to pressure the chairman for answers and to settle scores for Kevin.

As a result, Brian ignored them completely and did not take them seriously at all.

This made Thomas feel powerless and frustrated.

The mysterious chairman proved to be more challenging than expected.

As everyone prepared to leave, Celeste reminded them, "Of course, the chairman also said if any of you don't want to wait any longer, you can leave.

"But once you leave, you won't have to come back again."

The crowd's expressions darkened.

Thomas clenched his fists in anger and frustration.

He cursed the chairman inwardly, "Damn it!"

Inside the luxurious villa, the air was thick with the stench of alcohol, and beer bottles littered the floor.

Anne looked at Karen, who was drinking alone, her concern was evident.

She asked, "Karen, what's going on? Why are you drinking so much?"

Although Karen was ten years older than her and was her aunt, the two had a good relationship.

It was the first time she saw Karen, who had always been strong, drowning her sorrows in alcohol.

With bitterness etched on her beautiful face, Karen whispered, "I've been fired from the company."

"What? How is that possible?" Anne was stunned.

Karen had been a valued employee at Queen's Construction Group, earning nearly a million dollars a year.

If everything went well, she would be promoted to head of the human resources department next year and become a senior executive of the company.

But now, she was fired.

Karen's face twisted in anger and resentment.

She said, "It's all because of that jerk, Brian. If it weren't for him, I wouldn't have been fired."

Anne, shocked again, pressed for details. "What happened with Brian?"

Karen recounted the story, her frustration palpable. "I don't understand how Brian managed to charm Aria into treating him so well."

Then, a thought struck her. "Anne, I heard Sophia's new boyfriend is the CEO of the Carter Group. Tell her to help me get a job there."

Anne's expression darkened. "I'm afraid that won't work. The Carter Group is on the verge of bankruptcy."

"What? The Carter Group is going to go bankrupt?" Karen's eyes widened in disbelief.

"How is this possible? It is the leader in Riverwood's real estate and construction industry. I even heard that they are very likely to reach a cooperation with Trillion Group..."

Anne said helplessly, "It's a long story...but it's all because of Brian."

Karen asked, "Because of Brian?"

Anne nodded and recounted how Ryan and Brian had competed for the Trillion Group partnership.

She said, "Now, Brian is a partner and being hailed as a future star of Riverwood.

"Ryan and Sophia couldn't bear the blow and haven't returned home for a day."

Hearing that, Karen was frozen in shock.

She murmured, "How did Brian, that loser, become a partner of Trillion Group?"

At this moment, she finally understood why Aria treated Brian with such respect.

Suddenly, the villa door burst open.

Mikael Dunn entered with a dozen strong men in black.

They were professional debt collectors for loan shark companies.

"Ms. Jones, it's time to settle your debt," Mikael announced.
