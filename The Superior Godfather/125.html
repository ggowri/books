Chapter 125 The Temptation

When Brian returned to his upstairs bedroom and opened the closet, he found Anne curled inside, her eyes filled with hurt as she stared back at him.

The pain in her expression sent a wave of guilt through him. "Anne, I'm sorry..."

"Can I come out now?" she cut him off.

"Of course," Brian said, extending his hand.

But Anne ignored the gesture. She stepped out of the closet and began gathering her clothes in silence.

She could have accepted being asked to hide, but his threatening tone had crossed a line.

She'd always been clear about where she stood with Brian, never asking for more than he was willing to give.

Even without his warnings, she would never have revealed their relationship.

But his cold attitude had cut deep.

It confirmed her worst fears—that she meant nothing to him beyond physical release.

The realization was a blow to her dignity.

Sensing her distress, Brian moved to embrace her from behind. "Anne, I'm so sorry. I..."

"You've done nothing wrong," she said, her voice carefully controlled as she pulled away from his grasp. "I was the one who was shameless and kept bothering you. Don't worry—it won't happen again. You're free to pursue whoever you want openly," she said.

With that, she turned and walked away, her determination palpable.

Brian watched her go, his expression twisted with confusion.

How had things gone so wrong? Had he done anything wrong?

But there was no time to dwell on it. He pushed the thoughts aside and called Zoe about raising funds.

"Mr. Edwards," she reported, "all War Deities assets are under the Lord of Wealth's control. She's en route to Riverwood now. Sam and I can only manage two billion dollars on short notice. Would that be sufficient? We could try to gather more if needed."

After a moment's consideration, Brian replied, "That should do. Transfer it over."

*****

The next morning found Brian at Trillion Group, summoned by Celeste for a meeting with Damian.

He lounged in his chair in the chairman's office, languidly listening to Celeste's report while his gaze lingered appreciatively on her legs, elegantly showcased in black stockings with red trim.

He had to admit, Celeste cut a striking figure. Those legs, emphasized by her red skirt and the provocative black-and-red stockings, perfectly embodied sophisticated sensuality.

Catching his intense stare, Celeste frowned. "Are you even listening, or did you just come here to ogle my legs?"

Brian's eyes snapped to her face with feigned innocence. "I'm sorry, what was that? Something about wanting me to touch you?"

"No!" she cut him off sharply. "I said Thomas has orchestrated a mass absence of senior executives today."

Damian suppressed a smile.

"Even with the company in crisis, he still has the composure to flirt with Ms. Hawthorne. Truly the bearing of someone from a powerful family." he thought.

"Let them play their games," Brian said dismissively.

Then, his gaze drifted back to the red silk trim at Celeste's thigh.

Celeste's frustration was palpable.

Damian's admiration for Brian only grew. Such unshakeable confidence, even now!

He was about to speak when his phone rang.

Glancing at the screen, he said, "Mr. Edwards, it's Thomas."

"Take it," Brian directed. "Let's hear what he has to say."

Damian nodded slightly, answered it, and put Thomas on the speaker.

Thomas' smug voice filled the room. "Damian, have you considered my proposal from last night? What do you think?"

Damian seized his moment to prove his loyalty. "Thomas, I'm nothing like you. When I commit to something, I see it through. I've chosen to work with Mr. Edwards, and I won't betray that trust. If you're asking me to help you destroy Trillion Group, you can forget it!"

Thomas was completely confused by Damian's vehemence.

"Has he lost his mind? Thomas wondered. "Where does he get the nerve to speak to me like this?"

"Damian," he said, his voice turning to ice, "are you certain you want to keep backing that loser, Brian?"

The insult made Damian's anger boil. "A loser? Look in the mirror, Thomas. You and your whole family are the real losers here.

"You think being Denzel's lapdog makes you important? You're delusional.

"Taking on Mr. Edwards? You're just digging your own grave!"

He hung up before Thomas could respond.

Celeste stared at him in shock.

She barely recognized this version of the usually composed and diplomatic Damian. She half wondered if he'd been possessed.

Brian watched Damian with newfound interest, a slight smile playing on his lips.

"He's sharper than I thought," Brian mused. "With the right opportunity, he could go far."
