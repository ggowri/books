Chapter 18 An Unexpected Encounter

At 10 AM, Brian arrived at the Riverside Villas sales office.

Since he had no place to stay at the moment, Brian was planning to buy a villa.

He had invited Anastasia to join him in looking at the property.

As the most upscale river-view villa area in Riverwood, this location not only had beautiful surroundings and convenient transportation but was also considered top-notch in terms of geography.

Of course, the price was equally steep.

Even the smallest villa would cost over 15 million dollars.

Despite the hefty price, the sales office was still crowded with potential buyers.

The showroom was buzzing with activity, attracting elites from various industries.

Brian scanned the room, and his eyes immediately landed on Anastasia, sitting in the corner.

Today, she wore no makeup, her wavy hair cascading loosely over her shoulders, and a long white dress that revealed her perfect figure. Her simple yet elegant attire exuded a cold, refined beauty.

Sitting there like a solitary lily in full bloom, she caught everyone's attention.

Brian quickly walked over and greeted her.

"Rachael, sorry to keep you waiting."

"It's fine. I just got here," Anastasia responded with a smile.

She was pleased that Brian had invited her to join him in viewing the property.

After a bit of casual conversation, the two of them moved to the model display to choose a villa.

Given the overwhelming popularity of the property, the sales agents were all busy attending to their clients, leaving them largely ignored.

Finally, a sales consultant with an intern badge approached them.

With her enthusiastic introduction, Brian quickly set his sights on the most expensive villa, number 9.

"Rachael, what do you think of this one?" he asked.

Anastasia thought for a moment before replying, "The quality of this villa is excellent, the location is prime, and the river view is beautiful. It's definitely worth buying."

"Well then, we'll take this one. Full payment by card," Brian said decisively.

The intern was ecstatic when she heard Brian's response.

She had never expected such a wealthy and generous client.

"Please wait a moment, sir. I'll handle the paperwork right away."

While Brian waited for the paperwork to be processed, he spotted a few familiar faces in the crowd.

It was Ryan, Sophia, Anne, Robert, and Mary.

As soon as Brian saw them, Ryan and the others noticed him too.

Ryan's pupils constricted, and his face froze in shock.

"Damn it, how is this possible? I sent Raven, one of Shadow Fang's key members, to take care of him. How is he still alive?" Ryan muttered under his breath.

Sophia's face also turned pale.

Anne's expression darkened, her eyes filled with a complicated mix of emotions.

"Ghost... it's a ghost!" Robert and Mary screamed in fear.

They had assumed Brian must have been killed by the gang last night.

Their loud cries immediately drew the attention of everyone in the showroom. The five of them were mortified, wishing they could disappear on the spot.

Brian had burned down their villa, and now, they had come here to purchase a new one.

They never imagined they would run into him.

Brian smirked as he watched their stunned reactions. He walked over to them.

"What's the matter? Surprised to see me still alive?"

For a moment, they were all speechless.

They were indeed surprised.

After all, they had hired the gang to take care of Brian.

They never expected that Raven, one of Shadow Fang's most trusted men, would fail to eliminate him.

Sophia clung to Ryan's arm and whispered, "Darling, what's going on?"

Ryan thought for a moment before replying, "Most likely, Raven and the others didn't get the chance to act, so this guy managed to survive. I'll call them later to check."

Sophia, Robert, and the others all agreed with this explanation.

They were certain that if Raven had really made a move, Brian wouldn't have lived to see this day.

Ryan sneered, "Don't get cocky, you won't be happy for long."

Sophia couldn't resist mocking Brian. "What are you doing here? I see they're hiring janitors outside. Don't tell me you've come to apply for the job? You really are a pathetic, poor loser."

Once again, Sophia's taunts stung Brian deeply.

He was about to speak, but Anastasia walked over, linking her arm with his and looking at Sophia with a defiant gaze.

"Apply for a job? What are you talking about? My dear is here to buy a villa."
