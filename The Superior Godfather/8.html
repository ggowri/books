Chapter 8 Deal With Tyrone

"Please, don't hurt him. I'll drink with you," Anastasia interjected, her expression paling as she stood up.

While she wanted to test Brian's resolve, she didn't want him to suffer or get hurt for no reason.

Brian remained silent, focusing inward to calm the chaotic energy churning within him, waiting for the storm to pass before he could harness that power.

Once he was ready, handling these thugs would be a breeze.

"Ha! What a coward..." Tyrone scoffed, his contempt deepening as he saw Brian stay quiet.

He spat into Brian's drink, a cruel smirk spreading across his face.

Tyrone then reached out, intending to encircle Anastasia's enticing waist.

"Come on, beautiful. This weakling can't satisfy you. Join us for some fun..."

But just as his hand was about to graze her, a menacing glint flashed in Brian's eyes. He lunged forward, gripping Tyrone's wrist with a firm hold.

At that moment, the chaotic energy within him vanished completely.

"Fuck you..." Tyrone's eyes widened in shock as he sensed the sudden shift.

Before he could react, Brian twisted his wrist in a swift joint-lock maneuver.

"Ahh..." A sharp scream tore from Tyrone's lips as he felt his arm dislocate.

"You bastard! You'll pay for this..." Tyrone spat, his face contorted in pain.

He'd barely opened a move to fight back when Brian lunged for the wine bottle on the table and whacked it right across his head.

The bottle shattered against Brian, and blood flowed from Tyrone's gash, quickly soaking his shirt.

"Mr. Duffy!"

"Shit! You dare strike Mr. Duffy? You're asking for big trouble!"

Tyrone's henchmen looked on in shock, panic surging in their expressions.

They quickly drew their knives, lunging at Brian from either side.

With a swift sidestep, Brian dodged their attacks and swept his leg out, sending both thugs crashing into a nearby table, unconscious.

As Tyrone scrambled to recover and prepare for another strike, Brian seized him by the throat, lifting him off the ground.

Tyrone felt a wave of suffocating pressure envelop him, making him squirm, but his resistance was futile.

"I was so full of myself. I'll fix this mess. Please, give me a break..." Tyrone panted, with desperation seeping into his voice.

"Spare you?" Brian's eyes glimmered with cold determination as he picked up the glass Tyrone had just defiled.

"Chug this down, and I'll cut you loose. Deal?"

With the intensity of Brian's grip weighing on him, Tyrone nodded slowly, fear plain on his face. "I... I'll drink it."

Just as Tyrone was about to down the drink, Brian called out, stopping him, "Hold on a second..."

Confused, Tyrone watched as Brian spat a thick glob of phlegm into the glass.

"Now you can drink up!"

"You..." Tyrone's expression turned gloomy, but he was powerless against Brian's strength.

All he could do was swallow his pride and the disgusting drink, humiliation washing over him.

As the vile concoction settled in his stomach, nausea hit him hard.

"Ugh... You little punk, if you've got any balls, don't you dare run away! I ain't gonna let you off the hook today!"

Tyrone spat out a few curses and staggered toward the exit, stumbling out of the bar.

Onlookers were left in stunned silence, their jaws dropped at the unfolding scene.

Initially, they had thought Brian was just a coward for staying quiet.

Little did they know he was a formidable force.

Anastasia flashed a smile, her eyes now alight with even greater admiration for Brian.

It never crossed her mind that Brian, once drained of energy and reduced to a useless bloke, still had such moves up his sleeve.

Could his energy be making a comeback?

After dealing with Tyrone, Brian turned to Anastasia, concern etched on his face. "Ms. Vaughn, you good? You are not hurt, are you?"

Anastasia shook her head with a smile. "I'm fine... How about you?"

Brian just scoffed. "A bunch of low-lives like that? No way they could lay a finger on me."

Noticing that Brian and Anastasia weren't leaving after the ruckus, a well-meaning waiter scurried over to give them a friendly nudge.

"Hey, you'd better leave. See, Tyrone's sister's husband, Raven, is a heavyweight in the Shadow Fang, the biggest gang in town. He's probably gone to rally the troops. You gotta get outta here pronto, or you're toast!"

Anastasia wavered for a second, then looked up at Brian. "Mr. Edwards, the Shadow Fang is no joke. They're a force to be reckoned with. Maybe we should beat it?"

This was her litmus test for Brian.

If he decided to drag her out of there right then, it'd be game over.

She'd have no problem coming clean about who she was and calling off the engagement.

What Anastasia was after was a real man, a guy with guts who could stand up for her and make her feel secure, not a yellow-bellied coward who'd tuck tail and run.
