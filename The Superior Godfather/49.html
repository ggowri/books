Chapter 49 Eliza's Admiration

"Bring it on!"

Eliza's expression was cold as dark elemental energy coursed through her body. A terrifying and oppressive aura radiated from her.

The next moment, her right fist, enveloped in dark energy, smashed toward Brian with terrifying force.

The air crackled with a series of sonic booms as her fist cut through it.

"Not bad on strength, but your speed is way too slow!"

Brian smiled slightly, not even flinching. He took a half-step forward with his right foot and unleashed a punch, his fist howling with elemental energy.

Before Eliza's fist could land on Brian, his punch struck her chest.

The next second, blood sprayed out.

Eliza was sent flying backward.

"Did I actually lose?"

Just as she was about to hit the ground, Brian quickly appeared in front of her and caught her graceful form in his arms.

He spun around a few times before steadying himself.

"Ms. Gray, are you alright?"

Eliza felt herself enveloped in a warm and sturdy embrace, a strange sense of security washing over her.

Looking up at Brian's handsome and cold face, she felt a mix of emotions.

She hadn't expected to be defeated so quickly and completely.

If it hadn't been for the three moves Brian had let her have, she would have lost from the start.

She had trained in martial arts since childhood and cultivated dark elemental energy. Even her teacher had said she was incredibly talented and powerful.

She had never lost a fight before, yet she was defeated by Brian today.

Previously, she hadn't understood why Anastasia favored Brian so much, but now she did.

Brian really had potential.

If he could assist Anastasia, she might actually stand a chance in competing with the other heirs for the family leadership.

"Did he really defeat Eliza that easily?"

Anastasia, watching from a distance, was equally shocked by Brian's strength. Her admiration for him shone in her eyes.

She knew how formidable Eliza was. She had once single-handedly defeated over a hundred retired special forces soldiers.

Yet, she couldn't even withstand one of Brian's punches.

Brian had once again surprised Anastasia.

Anastasia thought, "Dad shouldn't have any more objections."

"Ms. Gray."

Brian's voice brought Eliza back to reality.

She broke free from his embrace and said, "I'm fine. Mr. Edwards, you're indeed very powerful. I lost."

As she spoke, Eliza couldn't help but cough violently, blood trickling from the corner of her mouth.

Clearly, she was seriously injured.

Brian looked at her apologetically. "Ms. Gray, I'm really sorry. I haven't fought in a long time and lost my sense of control. Let me treat your injuries as an apology."

"Can you heal wounds?" Eliza was surprised.

Anastasia walked over and said, "Brian's elemental energy has a strong healing ability. You have internal injuries. If you don't get treated promptly, you'll have lingering effects that could greatly impact your strength. Let him help you!"

Eliza couldn't bring herself to refuse. "Alright. Thank you, Mr. Edwards."

"Let's go to my place. It'll be more convenient."

Brian led Eliza to a room in his villa.

Looking at Eliza's black leather jacket, Brian said, "Ms. Gray, unzip your jacket so I can treat you."

Eliza hesitated for a moment and then unzipped her jacket, revealing a deep cleavage and her alluring breasts covered by a black bra.

"Is this okay?"

Brian hadn't expected Eliza to have such a great figure.

She was at least a 34C!

He quickly refocused, his gaze settling on the faint bruise on her chest, clearly caused by his punch.

"Sorry, Ms. Gray."

Without hesitation, Brian placed his palm on Eliza's chest.

The softness and elasticity he felt through his palm made his heart skip a beat.

Eliza's face changed, her eyes flashing with murderous intent.

She thought, "This jerk is using the excuse of healing to take advantage of me?"

Just as Eliza was about to snap, she felt a cool stream of energy flow from Brian's palm, soothing the pain in her body.

Her injuries began to heal, and she couldn't help but let out a soft, enticing moan.

Brian's heart raced at the sound, but he forced himself to stay calm and focused on healing Eliza.

Brian's expression was serious, and Eliza looked at him admiringly.

His eyes were clear and calm, devoid of any lust or malice.

Eliza's lips curved into a slight smile as she thought, "I guess I misunderstood him just now."
