Chapter 17 Reshaping the Workplace

"Ms. Hawthorne, is there something urgent you need to discuss with me?"

Brian lay back on the couch, eyeing Celeste with genuine interest, admiring her seductive, curvy figure.

Celeste and Zoe were completely different kinds of beauties.

Zoe was a tempting, alluring woman, full of sensuality and charm.

Celeste, on the other hand, was more of an elegant, refined woman, mature and sophisticated, with a kind of commanding allure.

Wearing a white blazer and skirt suit, Celeste exuded an aura of graceful sensuality that made her even more captivating. Few could resist her charm.

Sensing Brian's gaze, Celeste frowned slightly, her displeasure with him growing stronger.

She walked up to him and handed over the document she was holding.

"Mr. Edwards, the Trillion Group's investment conference is in two days.

"There are fifty-one companies shortlisted. I'm not sure which one we should choose for a partnership."

Brian casually took the document, tossed it aside, and asked, "Which company do you think we should work with?"

A flicker of intelligence appeared in Celeste's eyes. By now, she had figured out why Brian had asked her to establish his own company.

She realized he intended to use this opportunity to get back at Sophia and Ryan, those who had betrayed him.

She spoke in a calm voice, "Naturally, it should be the company you founded. But vice president Mr. Kevin Wright has already quietly convinced many executives to back the Carter Group.

"Right now, the company has already unofficially decided that the Carter Group will be our partner."

"Kevin?"

Brian furrowed his brow at the mention of the name.

Ryan had the audacity to announce the Carter Group as Trillion Group's official partner because his father was close friends with Kevin.

Anyone who supported Ryan was an enemy to Brian.

His voice turned cold. "What's Kevin like?"

Celeste's grip on the papers tightened, and anger flared in her eyes.

"Kevin is not just greedy and lustful but also corrupt and self-serving. He's constantly lining his pockets and has harassed several female employees. Recently, he impregnated one of them, and she ended up jumping off a building to her death..."

Brian's eyes narrowed, his voice ice-cold. "If he's such a scumbag, why haven't you fired him yet?"

A faint bitterness crossed Celeste's flawless face.

"Although I'm the CEO, Kevin has a complicated background. He's the vice president in charge of our marketing department. He also holds important company connections and has access to commercial secrets.

"Most importantly, his uncle, Thomas Lee, is the second-largest shareholder. With my authority, I can't just fire him.

"If I did, it would cause serious damage to the company.

"The fallout could range from internal unrest, with employees leaving in droves, to major shareholders pulling out, crashing our stock price..."

Brian's eyes gleamed with cold resolve. "So Kevin's a big deal. No wonder Ryan feels so confident. But keeping him around will only create endless problems and slow the company's progress. He has to go."

Celeste's expression changed.

She hadn't expected Brian to actually take action against Kevin.

Before she could say anything, Brian continued, "From what I know, Kevin is close with Ryan's father. The fact that he's pushing for the Carter Group means he's probably been bribed.

"I want you to have someone investigate him quietly. Gather evidence of his corruption. When the time comes, I want Ryan and Sophia to see them both thrown in jail and witness me becoming Trillion Group's official partner."

Celeste's face went pale, and she quickly protested, "Mr. Edwards, you can't let personal revenge ruin the company. If you go after Kevin, it will..."

Brian interrupted her firmly.

"I'll take full responsibility for the consequences. Just do what I've asked. Immediately.

"Also, make sure Kevin and Ryan don't find out about this. Understood?"

Celeste looked at him, her mind filled with helplessness. She knew she couldn't change his decision.

After all, Brian was the chairman of Trillion Group and the largest shareholder.

With a heavy heart, she said nothing more and left.

As she walked out of the room, she muttered to herself, "Hmph, this guy is out of control.

"I'll be watching to see how he handles things after taking down Kevin."
