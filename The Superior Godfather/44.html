Chapter 44 The Master of the War Deities

At ten-thirty in the evening, the Evans Villa was decked out in a riot of flowers and balloons. The dining table was laden with fresh blooms, a cake, and champagne.

The villa was brimming with festive cheer.

"Finally, it's all set," Robert, Mary, and Anne said with smiles on their faces. "Sophia and Ryan will be so surprised when they get back."

They had put a lot of thought into the celebration, wanting to make it special for Ryan and Sophia's success.

The doorbell rang, and they exchanged excited glances.

"It must be Sophia and Ryan," Robert said. "Let's get ready."

They grabbed confetti poppers and rushed to the door, ready to surprise the couple.

"Surprise!" they shouted in unison as the door swung open.

But the startled looks on Sophia and Ryan's faces quickly turned to irritation.

"Dad, Mom, what on earth are you doing?" Sophia snapped, clearly not in the mood for surprises.

"We just wanted to celebrate!" Mary, unaware of their upset, said with excitement undimmed. "Look at all this! We've got cake and champagne. Come on, let's have a party!"

"Tell us, Sophia," Anne urged. "How was the Trillion Group's event? Did Brian's face drop when you became partners?"

Sophia and Ryan exchanged a glance, and their dejection was palpable.

"Why are you guys looking like that?" Robert asked, finally picking up on the tension. "What happened?"

Ryan didn't say a word. He just turned and headed upstairs, his shoulders slumped.

Passing the dining room, he saw the table set with flowers, cake, and champagne. In a fit of rage, he swept everything off the table, smashing it to pieces.

He couldn't believe it. He'd lost the partnership to Brian, and with Ethan arrested and the Carter Group on the brink of collapse, he was at his wit's end.

Robert, Mary, and Anne stood there, stunned, unsure of how to react.

It wasn't until Ryan stormed upstairs that they could breathe again.

"Sophia, tell us what's going on," Mary pleaded, taking Sophia's hand. "Why is Ryan like this?"

Anne urged, "We are family. Just tell us what happened. Let's deal with it together."

"We're in trouble," Sophia said, her voice tinged with bitterness. "We didn't get the partnership. Brian did."

Robert, Anne, and Mary's jaws dropped in disbelief.

"What? That good-for-nothing Brian? Are you kidding me?"

"He's useless! How could Trillion Group choose him?"

"Didn't Kevin help Ryan? Aren't they friends? How could this happen?"

Sophia's face turned even more sour. "Kevin and Ethan have been arrested for corruption. We're completely ruined."

The news hit them like a ton of bricks.

They couldn't believe things had turned out like this.

"What do we do now?" Anne asked, her voice trembling.

Sophia was at a loss, her face pale.

With her and Ryan's scandal exposed, many of the Evans Group's partners were backing out, plunging the company into crisis.

And with Ethan behind bars and the Carter Group on the verge of collapse, she had lost her support completely.

She felt utterly helpless now.

"I'm going out for a bit," Anne said after a moment.

She needed to talk to Brian, to beg him for mercy and a chance for the Evans family.

Upstairs in his bedroom, Ryan's phone kept ringing, but he just stared at the ceiling, too numb to answer.

He had never expected that he would end up like this.

He'd tried everything to get Ethan out on bail, but nothing had worked.

He was about to switch off his phone and have a good rest when it rang again.

This time, he sat up, his eyes widening in surprise.

It was his cousin Sam Carter, calling from overseas.

Sam wasn't just a successful angel investor. He was also a powerful Meta, a former member of the War Deities.

If Ryan could get his help, maybe there was a way out of this mess.
