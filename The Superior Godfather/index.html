Chapter 1 Humiliate

Inside the luxurious Evans mansion, the party was in full swing, crystal chandeliers casting a golden glow over the champagne-soaked crowd.

It was a night to honor Amanda Evans on her eightieth birthday, and every family member had flocked to pay their respects and shower her with gifts.

"Grandma, check this out! These are some top-notch, century-old medicinal herbs. Cost me a million smackers! Here's to you living forever and drowning in good fortune!" Someone exclaimed.

"Grandma, see this emerald bracelet? Two million right here. May your special day be filled with all the good things you deserve, and may all your wishes come true! Happy birthday!" another chimed in.

Amanda surveyed the mountain of presents with a satisfied nod. The room buzzed with laughter and chatter, creating a heartwarming scene of family unity.

Seeing Amanda in a good mood, Brian Edwards - the family's deadbeat son-in-law - steeled himself and blurted out, "Grandma, there's this new bio-electrotherapy thing going around. Could I borrow you a million bucks? It might help fix my, uh, energy problems."

The room fell dead silent.

Everyone gawked at Brian like he'd just grown a second head.

No one could believe that on Amanda's big day, this loser not only showed up with no gift but also had the balls to ask for a loan.

Sophia Evans, Brian's wife, was seeing red. Her cheeks burned red with humiliation - she'd rather crawl under a rock and die.

Before Brian could say another word, she lashed out and slapped him across the face.

"Brian, you low-life scumbag! I'm done with you. Let's get divorced!"

Brian staggered back, covering his stinging cheek, with his eyes wide with disbelief.

Years ago, during a family mission, he'd met Sophia and thought he'd hit the jackpot.

To marry her, he didn't bat an eye before turning down the family's arranged match. And he even cut himself off from the family and kissed goodbye to his chance of becoming the next Godfather.

Behind the scenes, he'd poured money and connections into making Evans Group a local powerhouse. All for her.

He even took a rain of bullets to save her. And those wounds drained him of his energy, left him barely hanging on.

But none of that mattered. He was still head-over-heels, ready to do anything for Sophia without a single complaint, like a loyal servant.

All Brian wanted was to borrow a bit of cash to get his energy back while Amanda was in high spirits. But Sophia just exploded right there in front of everyone, slapped him hard, and demanded a divorce.

"Divorce? Sophia, you must be joking, right?" he asked.

Sophia glared at him, her eyes cold as ice. "Do I look like I'm kidding?"

Brian's jaw dropped.

Just as he was about to plead his case, Amanda cut him off. "Brian, it's time you knew the truth. Two years ago, I set Sophia up with a great guy. And they've been inseparable ever since!

"Ryan, come on out!"

In walked Ryan Carter, looking sharp in his designer clothes. He sauntered over and handed Amanda a bank card and a contract.

"Madam Evans, here's five million dollars and a contract worth a billion. Wishing you good health and a long life," Ryan grinned.

"Wonderful, just wonderful..." Amanda beamed and nudged Sophia into Ryan's arms.

"Ryan, I'll make sure Sophia dumps that good-for-nothing so you two can be together properly."

"Thanks, Madam Evans!" Ryan wrapped his arm around Sophia possessively, smirking at Brian.

Sophia proudly nestled into Ryan's embrace, looking like a lovesick puppy.

The crowd around them whooped and clapped, egging them on.

Brian stood there, feeling like the ground had just been pulled out from under him. His mind was a complete mess.

He couldn't wrap his head around how all his love and sacrifice had ended up in this disaster.

His eyes ablaze with blood, he fixed a death-stare on Sophia and let out a wild, hysterical roar.

"Sophia, you filthy bitch! What the hell did I ever do wrong?"

Sophia chuckled coldly. "Think of it as a little game we played... and now, game over."

Brian roared, "I cut ties with my family for you!

"I lost my energy, became a useless wreck just to save your sorry ass.

"I gave you my all, every last bit of me! And now you're telling me it was just a damned game?"

Brian's eyes bulged with blood vessels, howling like a rabid dog, tears of rage streaming down his face.

His whole fucking love story? Just one big cosmic joke.

"What's the big deal? You didn't actually think I'd be into a loser like you, did you?"

Sophia ran her manicured hand over Ryan's rugged jawline and looked at Brian like he was dirt.

She continued, "I, Sophia, go for guys like Mr. Carter—the cream of the crop, heirs to massive fortunes. Not some two-bit, dirt-poor slob like you. Got that through your thick skull?"

As she spoke, Sophia flicked a pre-made prenuptial divorce agreement at Brian like it was a piece of trash.

"Sign it and get the hell out. Don't ruin our fun here!"

The others quickly joined in. "Yeah, sign and hit the road!"

Ryan sneered at Brian, saying, "What? Need me to do it for you, punk?"

"You're going too far!" Brian couldn't hold back his boiling rage anymore. He lunged forward, ready to take these backstabbers down.

But in an instant, Ryan kicked him so hard he went flying through the air.

He crashed onto the hard floor, groaning in agony.

Ryan wasn't just the boss of the Carter Group; he was a battle-hardened ex-special forces dude.

Brian, who'd lost his energy and was beat up, didn't stand a chance.

As Brian tried to scramble up, Ryan planted his shoes right on Brian's chest.

"You're nothing but a piss-ant trying to go after my girl. Know your place. Stop embarrassing yourself!"

Sophia shot Brain a glare, full of disdain.

Then she locked her arms in Ryan's and said, "Honey, it's Grandma's birthday. Don't mess up the carpet..."

"No worries, I will take out this trash right now!"

Ryan let out an evil grin, then popped Brian's arm out of its socket and forced his hand to scribble on the agreement.

After that, Ryan grabbed him by the collar and threw him downstairs like a sack of garbage.

"Holy shit... Carter's a beast!"

"Man, once special forces, always special forces. What a badass!"

Downstairs, Brian somehow survived the fall.

He coughed like crazy, and blood spurted from his mouth and nose.

As cheers drifted down from upstairs, his blood boiled with rage and bitter injustice.

Revenge was all Brian could think about.

He'd make Sophia eat those words and reclaim every single thing he had lost.

With trembling hands, he fumbled for his phone and made one last call.

Then, he passed out cold.

A few minutes later, a fancy motorcade rolled up to the curb. With hundreds of Rolls-Royces, Mercedes-Maybachs, and Land Rovers, it was like a moving display of luxury—a true testament to opulence on wheels.
