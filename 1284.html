"Ultimately, Satan's Eye just doesn't cut it!

"Eamon's realm is two levels above mine, and the time power I gain from it loses its edge when I use it against him. I can only freeze his time for a second.

"I wonder how powerful Ophelia's time power truly is? Only she could unleash such a terrifying fighting style!

"The last time I faced her, I was utterly helpless! I can only imagine how strong she's become now," Osborne thought, his expression turning grave.

After all, Ophelia was the reincarnation of the God of Time. Now that she had recovered her past memories, she was essentially the real deal. Armed with the knowledge of a top-tier Deity King, her power was bound to skyrocket far beyond anything he could ever hope to match.

Osborne knew all too well how deeply Ophelia loathed him. It was only a matter of time before she would come for him again.

If Osborne wasn't a match for her, Ophelia would relish in humiliating him, crushing him underfoot and shattering his dignity.

That was something he simply couldn't accept.

Whether Ophelia was his ex-wife or the God of Time was irrelevant; Osborne would never allow her to trample on his pride again.

"Ophelia's past self was a top-tier Deity King warrior! She could go toe-to-toe with Lord Malevolence and alter the very fabric of time on Blue Planet!

"And here I am, feeling smug because I'm toying with a Transcendent Deity? Compared to a top-tier Deity King, this Transcendent Deity is nothing but an insect!

"If Ophelia regained her full strength, she could wipe out countless Transcendent Deity warriors with a mere breath! So, while I can mess around with a Transcendent Deity now, it's far too early to be feeling proud.

"I need to wrap this up quickly!"

With that realization, Osborne regained his composure.

He decided to change his approach. While using Temporal Stasis had given him the upper hand and ensured his safety, it wasn't the most efficient way to finish the fight.

Osborne abandoned the time power and shifted to his preferred combat style.

He unleashed his full power like a supernova blazing with unbridled intensity.

Osborne used Medusa's Gaze, a blinding light engulfing Eamon's form.

Despite being two levels above Osborne, Eamon found himself sluggish, the effects of Medusa's Gaze weighing heavily on him. Though he wasn't fully petrified, his movements were significantly hindered.

"Damn it!" Eamon growled, feeling as if his joints were filled with concrete.

Every step felt like a monumental effort.

But as he struggled, Osborne vanished from sight once more.

This time, it wasn't time power at play.

Eamon's eyes widened in realization. He instinctively attacked the empty space behind him, thinking Osborne would be there.

But his strike met only air.

Osborne had used spatial power this time, slipping into a parallel dimension through Phantom Steps.

In this alternate space, Osborne watched Eamon, whose face was a mask of wariness, a smirk creeping onto his face.

"It's time to end this!

"Right now, I'm an unseen assassin, weaving through the fabric of space!

"Transcendent Deity warrior?

"You're done!"

With a firm grip on the Goddess of Grain's scythe, Osborne felt an overwhelming surge of power coursing through his right hand. He erupted from the parallel dimension, reappearing directly behind Eamon.

In one swift motion, he swung the scythe down toward Eamon's neck.

The sheer force behind the blow was staggering; the scythe sliced through the air with a speed that left no room for reaction.

By the time Eamon sensed the danger, the razor-sharp blade had already struck, cleaving through his defenses like they were made of paper before biting into flesh.

Time seemed to freeze for Eamon as his body halted mid-air, with shock and confusion flooding his eyes, which were quickly replaced by sheer terror.

He slowly reached up, his fingers brushing against his neck, where a pristine surface had suddenly turned crimson.

As Eamon touched it, a thin line of blood appeared, rapidly oozing from the wound, a gruesome reminder of his impending doom.

A ring of blood began to encircle his neck, growing wider and deeper with every passing second.

"You... you..." Eamon stammered, trying to speak, but the mere act of opening his mouth widened the gash.

Blood gushed forth, spilling like a fountain, each heartbeat marking the end of his life.

Eamon's eyes were wide with fear and disbelief as he locked onto Osborne, but before he could utter another word, his head suddenly detached from his body.

He was dead.

The Goddess of Grain's scythe, wielded with Osborne's immense strength, had not only shattered Eamon's Ethereal Glow defenses but had also severed his Divine Body, ending the life of a Transcendent Deity warrior in a matter of moments.

Their battle had lasted less than ten breaths.
