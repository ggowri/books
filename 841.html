Osborne stared in shock at the scene before him.

In the vast space, a colossal mosquito stood, towering at least 16 feet high, with a wingspan stretching a daunting 33 feet.

Its wings were thin and translucent, covered in minute, scalelike patterns that shimmered like razor-sharp blades, displaying subtle shifts in color.

The mosquito's legs were long and slender, lined with rows of sharp, backward-facing barbs resembling serrated saw teeth.

Its head sported a pair of bright, intense eyes that were now fixed on Osborne with a chilling gaze. At the end of its long, slender antennae was a piercingly sharp proboscis shaped like a spear.

Osborne could feel an overwhelming sense of dread emanating from the creature.

He gasped involuntarily.

"A Dark Deity? It's actually a gigantic mosquito? And its proboscis looks like it's injured!"

His face grew serious as he quickly noticed Kendra and Yulissa lying unconscious on the ground. They appeared unharmed, breathing steadily. Osborne let out a sigh of relief.

Osborne turned his gaze back to the monstrous mosquito.

He had imagined many forms for the Dark Deity—three eyes, six arms, or an oversized head with long legs—but he never expected it to be a giant bloodsucker.

The grotesque legs, wings, and proboscis made his skin crawl.

Gripping his Rock Smasher and Volt Hammer tightly, Osborne tensed up.

The Dark Deity emitted a strange noise, incomprehensible to Osborne.

But then, its form began to shift, twisting and folding until it transformed into a stunning woman clad in black armor.

The armor barely covered her most essential parts, accentuating her voluptuous figure.

Her beautiful legs, prominent chest, and exquisite face were almost a mirror image of a more revealing Kendra.

Osborne stared, dumbfounded. "It transformed into Kendra! This is insane!"

The Dark Deity, now in human form, chuckled.

"Ah, another Earthling."

At first, the voice sounded odd, but it quickly normalized, with excitement flickering in her eyes.

"Another Earthling! Fantastic! I sense a tremendous amount of blood energy within you. Your body, your energy, and your blood are far stronger than those two combined!

"Once I consume you and absorb your energy and blood, I'll heal most of my injuries. Then that damned Blue Planet deity outside won't stand a chance!

"I'm going to slaughter all Earthlings, drain their blood, and restore my power!

"I'll exterminate all Blue Planet deities!"

With mounting excitement, she began to stride toward Osborne, her initial awkward gait smoothing into a confident walk, as if she'd always had a human body.

Osborne's face was a picture of shock.

"Indeed, it's the Dark Deity! It not only transforms into a human form but adapts so quickly!"

He took a deep breath to steady himself and said firmly, "So, you're the Dark Deity from beyond the stars? What's your name? Dark Deity?

She smirked. "Perhaps, but you can call me Mosley. Though you're about to die, so knowing my name won't do you any good. Just prepare to be my lunch."

As Mosley finished speaking, a chilling, killing intent and a terrifying surge of power erupted from her, radiating an immense and dangerous force.

"Psychic Blast!"

Osborne's expression turned grave. Even though Mosley's current strength was merely at the level of a Class S Supreme Overlord, she was still a genuine deity. He wasn't about to underestimate her.

Feeling the onslaught of Mosley's mental assault, he immediately activated his Soul Barrier.

This barrier, taught to him by Isa, was known for its extraordinary defensive capabilities against psychic attacks.

Just as Mosley's Psychic Blast reached him, it collided with the Soul Barrier, crashing into his mind.

Osborne felt the intensity of the Psychic Blast and couldn't help but grimace.

He thought, "This Psychic Blast is incredibly powerful!

If I hadn't activated the Soul Barrier and were just an ordinary Class AAA, I'd probably be knocked out instantly, my soul shattered by such an attack!"

Fortunately, Osborne had the Soul Barrier.

The powerful Psychic Blast was entirely blocked by the barrier, unable to affect his mental state or soul.

Mosley's face showed a hint of surprise.

"No wonder your energy and blood are so formidable. You've got some skill if you can withstand my psychic attack. Too bad you're still going to die!

"I can't wait to drain every drop of your blood!

"Come on, my little treasure."

With that, Mosley's form began to twist and shift once more.
