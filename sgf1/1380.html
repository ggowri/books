Chapter 1379 Encountering the Deathborn

Before long, Osborne found himself at the deepest part of the mine.

The path ahead ended abruptly, but that posed no challenge for him.

Despite his injuries, which had diminished his power to the point where even a Junior Deity could easily overpower him, Osborne still had the ability to carve out tunnels with ease.

Moreover, he possessed earth elemental power.

Although it wasn't his primary power, navigating through the soil was a breeze for him. As Osborne reached the end of the tunnel, he felt as if he had plunged into the water, his form vanishing into the depths of the mine.

"For me, mining is a walk in the park. With my powers and the combination of my Satan's Eye and earth elemental abilities, I can easily gather a mountain of Essence Crystals. But for others, it's a different story. They have to rely solely on their own strength to break through the rock. The stone here is incredibly tough; even a Junior Deity would struggle to make headway. Finding Essence Crystals really comes down to luck."

With that in mind, he ventured deeper into the mountain, sensing stronger energy fluctuations emanating from the depths.

Along the way, Osborne encountered numerous Essence Crystals.

This was, after all, a mining area that hadn't been fully exploited yet, and the number of crystals here was impressive. This time, he didn't hesitate; he collected every Essence Crystal he came across and stored them in his Dimensional Space.

His space elemental essence was severely depleted, and opening the Dimensional Space was pushing his limits.

Nevertheless, Osborne pressed on, moving deeper into the mine for several hundred miles.

By the end of his journey, he had amassed at least 30 thousand low-grade Essence Crystals, all neatly tucked away in his Dimensional Space.

"That's more than most human miners would gather in years. Yet, 30 thousand low-grade Essence Crystals won't do much for my injuries or the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity.

In fact, the Lotus won't even absorb low-grade energy! I need mid-grade or even top-grade Essence Crystals to help it recover. I can only hope there are some down here."

As he continued his descent, he couldn't shake the feeling that the heart of the mountain held something more powerful.

This mining area was massive; even with thousands of human miners from hundreds of villages working tirelessly, it would take centuries to fully excavate it.

The Essence Crystals he had gathered were merely a drop in the bucket compared to what lay hidden within the mountain's depths.

The rock was so solid that even his Satan's Eye could only penetrate a few hundred yards. Beyond that, he was blind to the crystals that might be waiting for him.

He pressed on, hoping to uncover mid-grade or top-grade Essence Crystals and see if they could aid in the recovery of the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity.

A single mid-grade Essence Crystal contained ten times the energy of a low-grade one and was ten times purer.

Just one could be exchanged for a hundred low-grade Essence Crystals.

As for a top-grade Essence Crystal, it was worth a hundred mid-grade Essence Crystals.

Unfortunately, these were exceedingly rare.

Osborne had traversed hundreds of miles within the mine, yet he still hadn't stumbled upon a single mid-grade Essence Crystal, let alone a top-grade one.

At this point, the number of low-grade Essence Crystals in his Dimensional Space had surpassed 50 thousand.

Osborne furrowed his brow, deep in thought. "Could it be that this mining area is completely devoid of any mid-grade or top-grade Essence Crystals?

Harry did mention that Deathborns sometimes roam these depths.

Yet here I am, making my way through without having spotted a single one. Have I just been lucky so far?"

Just then, he froze. As if his thoughts had summoned it, something strange caught his eye in the distance.

It was an entity, slick and without form, glowing a sinister blood-red.

It moved with an eerie grace, swimming effortlessly through the dark earth like a wraith. Osborne felt its presence keenly; it was honing in on him, zeroing in with alarming speed.

His eyes narrowed, tension coiling in his gut. "What a coincidence!

I mention Deathborn, and one shows up? How can it sense me from such a distance, traversing through all this rock? Can it smell my life force?"

Osborne's heart raced as the implications settled in.

The Deathborn had caught his scent, and he faced a choice: should he flee this place, or was it better to confront the creature and continue his quest for mid-grade and top-grade Essence Crystals?

Taking stock of the situation, he sensed the creature's energy. "This Deathborn doesn't seem too formidable.

The energy signature indicates it's just a Class S. Even in my weakened state, I should be able to handle it. Guess it's time to test its mettle!"

Resolved, Osborne tightened his grip on his power and moved toward the approaching creature, determined not to back down.

As he closed the distance, Osborne could see the Deathborn's feral nature clearly—its intelligence barely flickering, more beast than anything else.

It sensed Osborne's life force, its instinct kicking in as it lunged forward recklessly, uncaring of the gap in their strengths.
