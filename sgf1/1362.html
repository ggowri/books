Chapter 1361 The Appearance of Ophelia

Sebastian, Divine Shadow, and the others felt a surge of anticipation at Osborne's words.

"Osborne, be careful!" they warned him, quickly activating their keys to the Dimensional Microcosm and stepping inside for safety.

The situation in Celestara was uncertain, and entering it could bring unforeseen dangers. Their power levels were too low, so hiding within Osborne's Dimensional Microcosm seemed the safest option.

Once everyone had entered and confirmed that the Dimensional Microcosm was still protected by the Primordial Crimson Lotus of Eternity, Osborne took a deep breath.

"Celestara, here I come!" he declared, his voice filled with determination.

With that, Osborne unleashed the full force of his spatial abilities, breaking through the barrier of Celestara.

A low hum resonated as a dimensional portal opened, bridging Blue Planet and Celestara.

In an instant, Osborne felt a tremendous surge of chaotic energy pouring from Celestara. The portal pulled at him with an overwhelming force, and he let himself be drawn into it, surrendering to the current.

At that moment, even Osborne felt a twinge of unease.

He had heard countless legends about Celestara, but he still had no real grasp of what awaited him there.

"What is Celestara like after the war of doom?

"Is there still a place for humanity there?

"Has Lord Malevolence fully escaped and regained his strength? How powerful is the Dark Deity Legion in Celestara?

"Where are Isa and the others?

"Are my parents still alive?

"And what about Anastasia and Zoe's biological parents? Could one of them be from Celestara? Will I finally find them?"

As Osborne traversed the dimensional portal, his heart raced with a mix of excitement and anxiety. This journey was to an entirely foreign place, far beyond the simple act of traveling to another country; it felt like stepping into another world altogether.

It was almost like heading to another planet.

Time seemed to stretch at that moment.

The 140-thousand-mile-long dimensional portal felt endless, as if he could never reach the other side.

"Hmm?"

Suddenly, Osborne sensed something was off.

The speed of his passage through the portal should have been swift; even crossing two worlds, it shouldn't feel like this.

His body felt as if it were suspended in time, completely unable to move, and he wasn't getting any closer to Celestara.

Suddenly, a realization struck him.

"Something's wrong! Time is frozen here!"

Osborne's eyes widened in disbelief.

He quickly activated Satan's Eye, and while everything around him remained motionless, he found himself able to move.

Thanks to his own time power, Osborne could still act even in this frozen moment.

Then, as if drawn by an instinct, he turned sharply.

Osborne's eyes widened in shock as he recognized a familiar figure standing behind him.

It was Ophelia!

To his astonishment, she was right there, coldly observing him.

When she noticed Osborne moving within her Time Realm, her expression shifted to one of surprise, and she furrowed her brow.

"You can actually navigate through time in my Realm? Osborne, you're stronger than before," Ophelia murmured softly.

Osborne took a moment to steady himself, frowning as he replied, "Ophelia, when did you arrive? Have you been following me? What do you want?"

Ophelia gazed at Osborne's face, noticing the wariness in his eyes directed at her. She furrowed her brows, a flicker of anger igniting in the depths of her soul.

"Osborne. you think I'd ever hurt you?" she said icily.

"Wouldn't you?" Osborne shot back just as coldly.

Ophelia's frustration boiled over. "Osborne, if I wanted you dead, you'd already be gone! Your so-called strength might impress others, but to me, it's nothing!

"I could snuff you out without breaking a sweat.

"Even if Isa were to reappear, she couldn't stop me. You think I'd harm you? Why would you even think that?

"I've apologized to you! I've admitted my mistake!

"Why won't you forgive me?"

Ophelia's voice escalated into a frenzy, her face alternating between shades of red and white. Her erratic behavior struck Osborne as unsettling.

With her body trembling violently, her features contorted with an increasing savagery.

A lethal wave of murderous intent erupted.

The sheer intensity of it left Osborne frozen, as if plunged into an icy abyss.

Clearly, Ophelia's sanity was unraveling.

Clutching her head, she bellowed, "Why? Why won't you forgive me? I've admitted my fault, so why won't you forgive me?"
