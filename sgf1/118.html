Chapter 118 Forced Kiss

Isa's kiss was as fleeting as a dragonfly's touch. Before Osborne could even register what had happened, she had already pulled back, grinning mischievously at the flustered look on his face.

Osborne was taken aback, thinking, "What just happened?

Did she really just pull a sneak attack?

She gives me a peck and calls it a reward? The worst part is, I didn't even get a chance to savor it before it ended!"

Seeing Isa's teasing smile, Osborne decided to take action.

He stepped forward quickly, wrapping his arms around her slender waist. With a playful grin, he said, "A peck on the cheek as a reward? That's not nearly enough. I want more!"

Before Isa could react, he bent down and kissed her on the lips.

Osborne's bold move clearly took Isa aback. Her eyes widened in shock, and her body went rigid.

It wasn't until she felt Osborne's tongue trying to pry open her lips that she snapped out of her stupor.

Suddenly, Isa grabbed Osborne's arm and, with a swift motion, threw him across the room.

"Ow!" Osborne yelped as he flew through the air, feeling disoriented and seeing stars.

The force of Isa's throw left him utterly defenseless.

"Are you trying to kill me?" Osborne called out in disbelief.

Isa wiped her red lips with the back of her hand, glaring at him. "You cheeky bastard, how dare you kiss me? You're lucky I didn't break you into pieces."

"But you kissed me first!" Osborne protested, struggling to get up.

He knew Isa had shown restraint; otherwise, with her strength - evidenced by kicking Milo across the room and punching Pamela into oblivion - Isa could have seriously injured him.

Isa's strength was formidable, but Osborne had no regrets.

He thought, "I'm a man, and Isa had the nerve to kiss me. How could I not return the kiss?

Too bad I didn't get to taste her little tongue properly.

What a missed opportunity."

Isa, meanwhile, was fuming, her face flushed red all the way to her neck.

"Shut up!"

Osborne looked at her, puzzled.

He thought. "When I first met Isa, she was incredibly bold - flirting with me and making suggestive comments.

I thought she was pretty open-minded.

Now, after just one kiss, she's blushing like mad?"

Finding her reaction oddly endearing, Osborne couldn't help but chuckle. "If you feel cheated, I can let you kiss me back."

Isa's face turned an even deeper shade of red.

"You're such a brat. If you keep messing with me, I swear I'll make sure you'll never be a man again."

She gave him a threatening look, glancing pointedly at his lower half.

Feeling a shiver run down his spine, Osborne instinctively crossed his legs.

He laughed nervously. "Just kidding.

"It's getting late. You should go get some rest. I'll stay here and keep an eye on my sister."

"Do you have to be so bossy?" Isa replied.

She shot him another glare before running off.

Osborne watched her retreating figure, touching his lips as if reminiscing about the taste of her kiss.

After she was gone, he pulled out a cross necklace from his pocket and examined it closely.

This necklace had been taken from Paul.

Osborne was deeply intrigued by it, suspecting it might be the legendary artifact known as Cain's Blood Cross.

The powers it had displayed on Paul seemed strikingly similar to those of the Blood Cross.

For instance, it had transformed Paul into a Blood Clan member.

One of the Blood Cross' abilities was to elevate an ordinary person into a powerful Blood Clan member.

Moreover, Clarissa's ability to detect lies had no effect on Paul, possibly because of this very cross.

When Osborne first spied on Paul, Paul had been engaged in a video call with Davy. At that moment, the cross necklace around Paul's neck suddenly emitted a red glow, prompting Paul to immediately end the call.

At the time, Osborne hadn't given it much thought.

But now, reflecting on it, he suspected that the necklace might have been issuing a warning to Paul.

It seemed the cross had detected Osborne's surveillance.

Everything pointed to the necklace being linked to the ancient artifact known as the Blood Cross.

"Even if it's not the actual Blood Cross, it must be closely related. But why can't I detect anything unusual about it while it's in my possession?"

Osborne had been examining the necklace for a long time, but he hadn't found anything particularly special about it.

He murmured, "Could it be that it needs to be bonded to me?"

He then recalled that some special items required a particular ritual or connection to activate their powers.

This process was often referred to as "bonding".

Osborne had been unable to use the necklace because it likely hadn't accepted him as its master yet.

He thought, "What do I need to do? Could it be that the Blood Cross requires a blood bond?"

With this thought in mind, Osborne suddenly bit his finger, allowing a drop of blood to fall onto the cross necklace.

In an instant, something strange began to happen.
