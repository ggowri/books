Chapter 1026 Launching the Attack

As the vitality flowed into Sebastian's body, his complexion began to brighten, taking on a healthier hue.

But he didn’t wake up.

The truth was, Sebastian's problem wasn't just a lack of vitality; his martial art foundation had been severely compromised, causing a backlash that was eroding his body.

This wasn't merely a matter of vitality; it was a deep-seated injury that was consuming his very essence, eroding his spirit and soul.

Otherwise, Osborne would have already used the Light of Redemption to awaken him.

Osborne sighed heavily, thinking, "Looks like I'll have to wait until I break through to Class S before I can try using the Light of Redemption on him.

If that doesn't work, Grandpa might be in serious trouble."

His expression grew grim.

It’s urgent to reach Class S. The pressure of Sebastian's injury, the looming threat of the Aberrants, the assassins from the Eityreia Organization and the League of Divine Descent felt like a mountain pressing down on his shoulders.

Without breaking through to Class S, Osborne had no hope of overcoming these challenges.

He had felt Gerardo's incredible strength firsthand. After merging with the god's corpse, Gerardo could wield divine power.

Even with the Godfather's scepter, Osborne knew he wouldn't stand a chance against Gerardo.

Only by reaching Class S and combining that power with the scepter could he had some hope to contend with Gerardo.

And it wasn't just Gerardo he had to worry about.

The Eityreia Organization and the League of Divine Descent also had their own top-tier Class S members.

Names like Sin, Everett, and even Helios, the patriarch of the Thornberry family, who was known as the strongest on Blue Planet, loomed large in his mind.

After all, Sebastian had killed Helios' grandson, Rumen. The Thornberry family wouldn't let the Augustine family off the hook so easily.

Rumen was the vice-leader of the League of Divine Descent.

For the sake of reputation, the League of Divine Descent would surely avenge Rumen.

Thirty-six Divine Families might dispatch a number of Class S powerhouses, many of whom may be the top-tier Class S.

Osborne felt the pressure mounting.

As long as he remained stuck below Class S, he'd be unable to shake this burden. All the preparations he was making seemed futile without the top-tier combat power he so desperately needed.

Osborne longed to break through to Class S, but he had to wait.

Clarissa was still unconscious, and without her fate power to cleanse and strengthen his body, he was at a standstill.

Even with all his confidence, he knew it would take at least six months to make that leap on his own.

"Guess I'll just have to wait a bit longer," Osborne muttered to himself.

Stepping out of Mount Celestial Reach, Osborne wandered through the Augustine Estate, inspecting the defense system around and checking for any vulnerabilities.

Before he knew it, he found himself at Mount Seven.

Mount Seven was bustling with activity. In light of the Aberrants' threat, all members of the nine major branch bloodlines had relocated there for safety.

As he ascended to the peak, he arrived at the First Mansion, the residence of the Dampston branch bloodline.

Everyone in the Augustine family knew that Osborne was the former Godfather's grandson, and he had no blood ties to the Dampston branch bloodline.

By all rights, they should have been living in the Ninth Mansion, given their true strength.

Yet, here they were in the First Mansion.

The reason was simple.

Even though Osborne wasn't blood-related to the Dampston branch bloodline, his connection to them ran deep, perhaps even deeper than his ties to the main bloodline.

He often visited the Dampston branch bloodline to catch up with Isaac, Yulissa, Nathan, and others, offering some guidance on their training.

Because of this bond, no one dared to provoke the Dampston branch bloodline. Even members of the main bloodline treated the Dampston branch bloodline with respect; they understood the significance of Osborne's relationship with those from Dampston.

Now, once again, Osborne found himself back at the First Mansion.

"Osborne! You're here!"

Yulissa's eyes sparkled with delight as she rushed to greet him, wrapping her arms around one of his.

Despite everyone now knowing he wasn't her real brother, she still treated him just the same.

Osborne didn't correct her; it felt natural, and he enjoyed the affection.

As he stepped inside, Kendra, Nathan, Queenie, and a few others came over. They were the ones he felt closest to, and the ease of their interaction filled the room with warmth.

He smiled back at them, saying, "Nice to see you all!

"Look at you—everyone's finally broken through to Class A! But this is just the beginning. You have to keep pushing. Aim for Class AAA, okay? If you need resources, just let me know."

His words were met with looks of gratitude and appreciation from the group.

Just then, Osborne's Draco Star Wristlet vibrated violently, catching his attention.

He glanced at the screen, and his expression hardened.

The Aberrants had launched its attack.

Tension gripped him as he prepared to leave, but Kendra gasped suddenly, her face pale.

"What? My brother's in trouble?"
