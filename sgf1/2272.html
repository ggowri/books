Chapter 2236 Celestial Progeny-2

In the entire history of the Racial Convergence Planet, only a handful of these Celestial Progenies had ever emerged, each one a beacon of hope and aspiration for countless others.

Each of them eventually made a huge splash on the Racial Convergence Planet, becoming a living legend. In the end, they'd exhausted all the challenges on the planet and had no option but to set off for the heart of the cosmos in search of higher levels of cultivation.

Celestial Progenies were as rare as hen's teeth!

The last time one showed up on the Racial Convergence Planet was thousands of years ago. That Celestial Progeny had outshone every powerful figure on the planet in just a thousand years and then decided to head into the cosmic center.

Now, Amber was saying there was one right here?

Angela, Keeley, and Gwen were more than a bit skeptical.

Amber frowned and said, "You don't buy it?

"How else can you explain a whole bunch of Class-SS super-geniuses all grouped together? Celestial Progenies are the darlings of the universe; they just naturally attract other super geniuses to them!

"And the good fortune of a Celestial Progeny rubs off on those around them, making their companions stronger, more talented, and brimming with greater potential!

"So, I'm betting that one of them is definitely a Celestial Progeny! It's this Celestial Progeny who's influenced them all, leading to their Class SS scores!"

As Amber finished, the others stared wide-eyed, mouths agape, utterly astonished.

What she said made so much sense!

It was the only explanation for why so many Class SS super-geniuses had gathered in one place.

Their breaths quickened as they turned to Isa and Osborne.

They were the only two who hadn't yet tested their talents and potential on the Astral Testing Arch.

If there truly was a Class SSS Celestial Progeny among them, it had to be one of those two!

Their eyes flicked over Isa and Osborne's faces, brimming with excitement and anticipation, as if they were eyeing precious treasures.

This made Osborne and Isa feel a bit on edge.

They had no clue what Amber and the others had been chatting about, but the way the four were looking at them—like they were about to gobble them up—almost made them want to make a run for it.

"What's going on, seniors?" Osborne managed to ask.

The four of them exchanged looks, and finally, Angela took the lead, trying to rein in her excitement. "You two should step into the Astral Testing Arch for a check!"

She looked at them with eager expectation, but they didn't budge.

"I think I'll sit this one out," Osborne said, shaking his head.

He knew his situation like the back of his hand; with his talent, stepping into the Astral Testing Arch would probably land him a Class SSS score.

Class SSS was the label for Celestial Progenies.

In the whole cosmos, Celestial Progenies were as rare as rocking-horse droppings. Osborne didn't want to join the Fairy Moon Sect, didn't want to become a disciple of Angela and her fellow practitioners, and definitely didn't want to draw too much attention to himself.

Sometimes, showing off his talents and potential was just like flashing his cash—it could stir up all kinds of trouble.

So, Osborne was dead-set on avoiding the Astral Testing Arch.

Isa chimed in, shaking her head, "I don't wanna take the test either. And I sure as heck don't want to be anyone's disciple."

With that, she grabbed Osborne's hand. "Osborne, I've given it some thought, and I've decided to stick by your side! Someone's gotta look out for you! No matter how perilous the road ahead is, I'm not scared! I'll do whatever it takes to keep up with you."

After she finished speaking, Osborne fell quiet, mulling over her words.

Their refusal, though, really threw Angela and her fellow practitioners into a panic.

They were certain that either Isa or Osborne had to be the Celestial Progeny, but with both of them saying no to the test, how could they not be on pins and needles?
