Chapter 2408 Blood-Sacrificing Magic-1

Primordial said, "Why are you in such a hurry? The Astralwreck Dragon isn't that easy to hatch. If it were easy, the Magma Devourer would have hatched it long ago, and it wouldn't be your turn."

Staring at the Astralwreck Dragon Egg, Primordial seemed to be deep in thought.

His branch-like hand was touching his chin at the moment, looking somewhat comical.

However, Osborne did not disturb him.

Osborne knew that if it came to knowledge, he was no match for Primordial. If Primordial couldn't figure out a way to quickly hatch the Astralwreck Dragon, then there was no point in him trying.

Staring at the transparent coffin, Osborne felt a bit puzzled.

The surrounding mist was clearly released by the Astralwreck Dragon Egg. This mist was likely meant to protect the Astralwreck Dragon Egg. It was incredibly powerful, so much so that even a top-tier warrior at the level of Empyrean Sacredness couldn't break through it.

However, as soon as the transparent coffin appeared, the mist automatically opened a gap.

Osborne thought, "Could it be that this Astralwreck Dragon Egg doesn't belong to the Magma Devourer, but to the Wingless Angel in the transparent coffin?"

He felt that his guess was quite reliable.

A scene seemed to appear in Osborne's mind. On a certain day long ago, the Magma Devourer found a transparent coffin and an Astralwreck Dragon Egg within the core of a star.

To claim the Astralwreck Dragon Egg for itself, the Magma Devourer left the star it had always lived on and came to a small resource planet at the edge of the universe. It killed all the natives on the planet and took it over.

Using the planet's environment, the Magma Devourer wanted to hatch the Astralwreck Dragon Egg. It even planted a large number of Beast Soul Flowers in advance, preparing food for the Astralwreck Dragon after hatching.

However, after tens of thousands of years, the Magma Devourer still failed to hatch the Astralwreck Dragon Egg, and the warriors from the Racial Convergence Planet discovered this planet.

The Wingless Angel in the transparent coffin was the true owner of the Astralwreck Dragon Egg.

Osborne felt this guess was closer to the truth, but it didn’t matter.

"I have an idea," Primordial suddenly said. "I thought of a way to quickly hatch the Astralwreck Dragon Egg. However, it might be a bit risky. If we fail, this Astralwreck Dragon Egg might never hatch again."

"But this is the only way. Otherwise, this Astralwreck Dragon Egg won't hatch naturally even after tens of thousands of years, because the conditions here simply aren't suitable for hatching it."

Osborne curiously asked, "What’s the method?"

"Blood-Sacrificing Magic," Primordial said seriously. "Blood-Sacrificing Magic is an ancient ritual method. Initially, it used blood sacrifices to exchange for power from the cosmos. But later, it evolved into many variations, giving Blood-Sacrificing Magic numerous forms."

"I happen to know a variation of Blood-Sacrificing Magic. It should accelerate the growth of the Astralwreck Dragon Egg and make it hatch quickly. However, it will require you to consume a large amount of blood."

"Additionally, the Astralwreck Dragon Egg will also become a sacrifice for the Blood-Sacrificing Magic. If this method fails, the Astralwreck Dragon Egg will completely die and turn into a dead egg."

Osborne frowned, thinking, "Normally, the Astralwreck Dragon Egg might not naturally hatch for tens of thousands of years? Only Blood-Sacrificing Magic has a chance to hatch it quickly? But if the hatching fails, the Astralwreck Dragon Egg will become a dead egg."

He hesitated because the Astralwreck Dragon Egg was too precious. If he could hatch the Astralwreck Dragon, it would become a great ally for him. Therefore, Osborne didn’t want to take any risks.
