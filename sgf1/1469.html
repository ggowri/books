Chapter 1468 A Deity Overlord's Threat

Fabian leaped to his feet, his posture radiating menace as an oppressive wave of divine power swept over the auction hall.

A palpable tension filled the air, and the crowd fell into stunned silence, fear creeping into everyone's hearts—even Tiana felt a knot of anxiety tighten in her stomach.

Whereas Osborne, targeted by Fabian, now faced an even more formidable pressure.

But there stood Osborne, seemingly unaffected by the weight of Fabian's presence. His face remained calm, with not a hint of fear or panic. Instead, a smirk appeared on his face, one that carried a distinct air of mockery.

The situation left onlookers dumbfounded.

Fabian paused, taken aback. He had expected his threat to send Osborne scrambling for mercy, begging for forgiveness.

Yet here was Osborne, undeterred, even daring to taunt him with a look.

Fabian's voice dripped with disdain as he snapped, "You're not scared of death? You really think I won't turn you into a smear on the floor with a single swipe? You can't possibly withstand my power!"

Osborne chuckled. "Maybe you're right.

"But I have a strong hunch that you won't lay a finger on me—at least not here, not now. Care to know why?"

Fabian's curiosity piqued, and the rest of the crowd looked on with intrigue.

What made Osborne so confident? After all, everyone knew that Fabian had a notoriously short fuse; anyone who crossed him could expect swift retribution.

Osborne continued with serene confidence, "Because I understand that every Deity Overlord warrior, regardless of their race, can't be a fool or a lunatic. If you think he's a fool or mad, then either you're the fool, or it's just a ruse on his part.

"A Deity Overlord like you can't be either—so you're not foolish. You put on a mean face and act unreasonably, but we both know you only lash out at those who can't afford to take the hit.

"Otherwise, you wouldn't have made it this far!"

"I'm not afraid of you because I know you wouldn't dare provoke the slave market.

"You know the Demonic Dragon Clan and the Soaring Lion Clan back it up, right? So, you're not stupid enough to make a scene here! Even if you wanted to kill me, you'd wait until I'm out of the slave market first.

"Wouldn't you agree?"

As he finished, the room fell quiet. Fabian's expression soured, the color draining from his face as he felt an unsettling exposure.

A flash of realization ignited among the spectators; now they understood Osborne's leverage. He firmly believed that Fabian wouldn't risk taking action in the slave market, which gave him the guts to engage in a bidding war.

But despite this newfound clarity, many still believed his defiance was reckless.

After all, Fabian might hold back here, but once Osborne left, that wouldn't save him from the monstrous wrath waiting outside.

No one could save him then.

Fabian quickly came to that conclusion himself, his lips twisting into a cold smirk. "You think you're clever, don't you? Well, let me tell you something—you're dead wrong! You might think you've outsmarted me now, but you're still on my bad side.

"Mark my words: the moment you step out those doors, it'll be lights out for you!

"Better start planning your funeral!"

With that ominous threat hanging in the air, Fabian reined in his divine power and took his seat once more, tension fading as he shifted gears.

"Since I can't intimidate you, let's make it a fair contest. Even when it comes to cash, I, a Deity Overlord and head of a significant power, won't be bested by you! So I'll raise the bid to 31 million!"

He raised his paddle again, determination etched on his face.

Tiana exhaled in relief upon seeing Fabian regain his composure and shift back into the auction mindset.

Tiana cast a long, searching glance at Osborne but chose silence.

In her eyes, Osborne's bravado seemed reckless; standing firm against a Deity Overlord warrior might look bold, but underneath it all, it bordered on foolishness.

Unless he had some secret card up his sleeve and wasn't afraid of the consequences, he was playing a dangerous game.

She shook her head, a thought occurring to her. "I just hope he spends all the Essence Crystals in that Dimensional Bag before he leaves, or they might end up in Fabian's hands."

"Forty million!" Osborne declared, raising his bid again, his tone still casual as if he were tossing out a mere four thousand instead of 40 million.

The crowd stirred in excitement, their eyes lighting up with speculation. "How many Essence Crystals does this guy actually have?"

They were practically salivating, itching to leap at him, but this was the slave market—a place where even a Deity Overlord like Fabian treaded carefully. They could only glower at Osborne from a distance, their frustration simmering just beneath the surface.

Fabian, too, was seething.

He had his sights set on those three Celestial Deity-level female slaves and was willing to pay a hefty price. If not for Osborne's meddling, he could have snagged them for a mere 21 million.

By tonight, he would've had his fun.

But now, with Osborne in the mix, the bidding price had shot up to 40 million!

"Damn it! Just wait until you're out of here. I swear I'll make you pay!" Fabian thought, gritting his teeth.

He raised his paddle again.

"Let's see just how deep your pockets are. I'll raise it to forty-one 41 million!"

Osborne shot him a cold smile. "Oh, you'll see alright! Fifty million!"
