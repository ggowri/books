Chapter 1255 Dimensional Microcosm

Not every stone would melt in the lava. Within this river of molten rock, many such stones, with higher melting points than the surrounding magma, floated inconspicuously.

However, this particular stone was extraordinary, emanating a faint energy fluctuation.

This energy fluctuation was so weak that even Osborne, if not meticulously sensing, would have missed it.

He was very familiar with this type of energy, a spatial fluctuation.

In that instant, everything clicked.

"Dimensional Space!" he murmured to himself.

As a space elemental metahuman, Osborne was intimately acquainted with Dimensional Space. He himself possessed one, where he stored many of his belongings.

However, Osborne's was not advanced enough to house living beings.

Inhabitants entering his Dimensional Space would perish.

Yet Osborne knew that some advanced Dimensional Spaces were akin to real worlds, complete with mountains and rivers, air, and energy, functioning as a completely independent microcosm.

That was exceptionally rare.

"Who would've thought the Thornberry family had their hands on a Dimensional Microcosm? No wonder they vanished without a trace once they arrived here. Even my Satan's Eye couldn't pierce through that veil—they must have slipped into the Dimensional Microcosm!

This seemingly ordinary stone is the key to the Dimensional Microcosm! Concealing it in a lava river buried two miles deep underground? Talk about hiding in plain sight. If it weren't for my Satan's Eye and my relentless tracking of Helios, I wouldn't have sniffed it out either!"

Rapidly, Osborne weighed his options.

Now that he knew Helios had entered the Dimensional Microcosm, he hesitated on whether to follow. He analyzed the risks: if he entered this Dimensional Microcosm, would he be discovered, and would there be danger?

Ultimately, Osborne made his decision.

"I can't go in! Despite my spatial powers allowing me to enter without a key, such a Dimensional Microcosm is like a divine artifact and must have been refined by someone.

Unauthorized entry would surely alert them. Even with my spatial power, escape might be impossible.

Entering is like stepping into a trap—once caught, even my immortality would be useless.

My power isn't sufficient; this is too risky."

Yet, Osborne felt a pang of regret.

He knew the Dimensional Microcosm likely harbored secrets of the Pantheon League, including the fabled Mount of Gods.

The inability to see it firsthand gnawed at him.

"Ah, safety first. I'll come back when I'm stronger. Then, I'll claim the entire Dimensional Microcosm.

If the Augustine family could possess one, it would be much safer," Osborne mused, resolving to leave.

Today's discoveries were substantial enough. The priority now was to return and use his findings to enhance the Blood Demon's power. No more risks.

With a thought, Osborne left a faint trail of spatial energy on the Dimensional Microcosm's carrier.

This faint spatial energy clung to the Dimensional Microcosm's carrier, nearly invisible to anyone else.

After all, it naturally emitted a subtle spatial fluctuation.

Osborne could latch onto that delicate energy and lock in on the Dimensional Microcosm's location like a bloodhound.

Even if the Pantheon League moved it to a more elusive spot in the future, he could easily track it down using this subtle spatial trace.

A cold resolve flickered in Osborne's eyes.

"If it comes to war with the Pantheon League, I'll strike straight at their Dimensional Microcosm and wipe their stronghold off the map! Pantheon League, you'd better not mess with me!"

With that firm thought, Osborne stepped into the parallel space and quickly returned to the surface.

"After seeing what the Deschanel Clan is up to now, I should head back."

Before long, he spotted Joseph and the other warriors of the Deschanel Clan. They were flying silently above the ocean, speeding toward Rosmandi.

Eighteen of them, each with a gloomy expression, their eyes simmering with barely contained fury.

Finally, one warrior broke the silence. "Mr. Joseph Deschanel, are we really just going to let this slide?"

Joseph's face was a stormy sea of emotions. He sighed heavily, "What else can we do? Charge back in? With just us? Do you really think we can take down the Pantheon League?"

"But we lost eighteen of our own!"

"We only have one hundred Sanctuary Caskets available, and now we're down eighteen! We can't just let this go unchallenged; it's eating me alive inside."

"Exactly! We have to make them pay!"

"And let's be real—the Pantheon League is strong. They've got a wealth of warriors who've held on for so long. If we don't eliminate them now, it'll just be a matter of time before they become a serious threat."
