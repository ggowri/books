Chapter 1710 Timely Arrival

Hannah's face transformed, with a fierce determination replacing her fear.

Even though she had sent her location to Osborne, deep down, she really just hoped he wouldn't rush off to the Bowman family and endanger himself for her; she didn't truly believe he could save her.

But she wasn't about to wait for death to come knocking.

Hannah was a prodigy of the Gellar family, proud and unyielding. Even if the odds were stacked against her, fear wouldn't take hold.

She would fight back!

Meanwhile, once Osborne received Hannah's location, a wave of relief washed over him.

She was in a mining area near Wild Flame City, an area he knew well, the very place where he had first awoken, saved by Harry and his sister.

Harry and his sister had worked there as a miner long before Osborne showed up.

Osborne whispered, realization dawning, "So it's here!

"Divine Peak Nine base is millions of miles from Wild Flame City! Even with the fastest Supreme Malevolence Airship, it would take hours to get there! And a Deity King warrior flying at full speed would still need the same amount of time!

"Hannah's in a dire situation; she can't hold out for that long! Others might be too slow to save her, but not me."

Osborne's companions had been anxiously watching him.

"Osborne, what's going on?" Anastasia asked, with concern etching her features.

"My friend is in danger. She's surrounded by warriors from the Paramount Supreme Clan, the most powerful clan in Celestara. I have to get to her immediately!

"I don't have time to explain in detail; you all need to head back to Hope Haven! I'm going to move as fast as I can to save her before it's too late!

"Prepare yourselves for battle!"

The moment he finished, excitement and determination glimmered in their eyes.

They understood the significance of the Paramount Supreme Clan, and the thrill of confronting warriors from such a clan sparked a fire within them.

Without hesitation, they headed straight to Hope Haven, ready to face whatever was to come.

Osborne wasted no time and set off immediately.

His confidence in saving Hannah stemmed from his mastery of spatial power.

Osborne's space elemental essence had reached Level Seven, giving him an unprecedented ability to manipulate spatial energy. Every instance of interdimensional travel now allowed him to cover vast distances.

When he was at Divine Peak Nine, his power was suppressed, and he could only travel about 600 miles at a time. But outside the confines of that place, he could traverse with ease.

Each journey could cross 600 thousand miles in one go.

With Wild Flame City being less than six million miles away, Osborne knew he could reach Hannah with just a few well-timed travels.

Of course, the farther he traveled in one jump, the more divine power it consumed.

Crossing six million miles took a significant toll, but he didn't have time to worry about that. Osborne stepped into the dimensional portal, and a moment later, he found himself 600 thousand miles away.

Osborne repeated this process several times, each jump bringing him closer until he materialized deep within the mining area where Hannah had said she was hiding.

As he emerged from the tear in space, he couldn't shake the feeling that Hannah had not deceived him.

The air was charged with the fervor of battle; he hadn't even activated Satan's Eye yet, but he could already see a figure rushing toward him at breakneck speed.

"Hannah?" he murmured, unable to believe the sudden twist of fate.

At that instant, Hannah skidded to a halt, her eyes wide with disbelief as she saw Osborne standing there.

Hannah was stunned, too. She stood still as she rubbed her eyes. Certainly, she must be imagining things.

"Am I really hurt that badly? Is this a hallucination? Am I about to die, and is the last thing I see the person I want most to be with?" Hannah murmured, her voice barely above a whisper.

Osborne couldn't help but chuckle at her words. Yes, Hannah bore several wounds, but they weren't too severe. Just seeing her alive filled him with relief.

He had made it in time after all.

But then Hannah blinked and noticed his smile. She rubbed her eyes again, questioning her senses.

Osborne finally spoke, his tone reassuring, "Hannah, this isn't a hallucination! I told you I could save you! I'm here now!"

She exclaimed, her expression suddenly shifting to one of suspicion, "Impossible!

"Who are you really? Osborne is far away; it hasn't been long enough for him to reach me!

"You're trying to fool me by pretending to be him!"

Osborne rolled his eyes, about to retort, when his attention snapped to something behind Hannah.

His expression darkened.

Powerful figures rushed toward them, closing in fast.

Hannah's face went pale.

"Hannah, there's nowhere for you to run! You've hidden well! Everyone thought you were a natural-born cripple, a blind girl, but look at you! You are actually the Gellar family's greatest prodigy, managing to break through our encirclement! Too bad, it's still not enough to save you!"
