Chapter 974 Dark Deity?

Whether it was a house cat or a wild one, their lifespans typically ranged from a mere decade to two decades at most. Breeds that lived beyond thirty years were almost nonexistent.

Yet now, Emma was telling Osborne that the cat he was holding was at least three hundred years old.

Osborne's eyes widened in disbelief. "You've got to be kidding me."

Emma shook her head, her expression serious. "No joke. The higher-ups in the Augustine family all know about this. Divine Shadow isn't just any ordinary cat; it's incredibly powerful!"

"Powerful, huh? Just how powerful are we talking?" Osborne pressed, skeptical.

"Well, let's put it this way: I definitely can't take it in a fight, and neither can anyone else in the Augustine family! As for whether the former Godfather could take it on, I have no idea," Emma replied earnestly.

"Wait, what?!" Osborne couldn't believe his ears.

Emma was a Class AAA powerhouse, one of the elite in the world. If she said she couldn't beat a cat, then this cat must be something else entirely.

Osborne thought, "Is it some kind of monster?"

He glanced at Emma's serious face and realized she wasn't joking.

His gaze shifted back to Divine Shadow, who remained comfortably nestled in his arms, its bright eyes half-closed in a lazy demeanor.

It looked like any ordinary pet cat, but the thought that this creature could potentially be more powerful than him sent a shiver down his spine.

For a moment, Osborne considered tossing it aside out of sheer instinct, fearing it might swipe at him.

But he held back the impulse.

Osborne didn't sense any energy from Divine Shadow, nor did he feel any hostility.

It seemed the cat had no intention of harming him.

Still, curiosity gnawed at him. Osborne needed to see if Divine Shadow was truly as formidable as Emma claimed.

Osborne said, leaning down to look at the cat, "Divine Shadow, right?

"Everyone's saying you're something special. Why don't you show me? I still can't wrap my head around the idea that a cat could be so powerful."

Divine Shadow lifted its gaze briefly as if it understood Osborne's words but didn't make a move.

Feeling a bit embarrassed, Osborne glanced at Emma, who shrugged.

"Even the former Godfather couldn't command Divine Shadow. If it doesn't want to listen to you, there's not much I can do."

Osborne felt a surge of defiance. Without thinking, he flicked his arm, sending Divine Shadow flying through the air.

"Meow!"

In an instant, a sharp, clear meow echoed through the room. Divine Shadow halted mid-air, twisting its body around before extending a paw toward Osborne.

A terrifying claw-like force sliced through the air, narrowly missing Osborne's ear and severing a few strands of hair.

The claw then struck the wall with a resounding thud, leaving a deep gash on the sturdy surface.

Osborne stood frozen, utterly stunned.

The speed of the attack had left him completely off guard.

Finally, he believed Emma. This cat was no ordinary feline; it was a creature of immense power.

The claw strike hadn't been directed at him but was a warning shot. If it had landed on him, he would have been seriously injured, if not dead.

"Whoa!" Osborne gasped, his heart racing as he stared at Divine Shadow, who gracefully returned to his side, leaping back onto his arm.

The cat gave him a sidelong glance, almost as if to say, "Don't try that again."

Osborne was left speechless.

Emma, too, was wide-eyed in astonishment. She had only heard the legends about Divine Shadow but had never witnessed its power firsthand.

Now that she had seen it in action, she was equally shocked.

Osborne thought, "Is it a monster?

A deity?

Or maybe... a Dark Deity?!"

With that thought, a grave expression suddenly took over Osborne's face. His mind raced back to the dungeon where he had faced off against the Dark Deity, Mosley.

Mosley had been a Dark Deity, yet its true form was nothing more than a gigantic, blood-sucking mosquito.

If a mosquito could be a Dark Deity, then why not a cat?

The idea loomed large in his mind, but Osborne shook his head, unwilling to pursue that thought any further.

Deep down, he sensed that Divine Shadow meant him no harm.

After all, the cat had existed peacefully alongside the Augustine family for centuries, never causing trouble for anyone.

How could it possibly be a Dark Deity?

With that reassuring thought, he began to relax.

Osborne thought, "No matter what, Divine Shadow won't hurt me or the Augustine family! In fact, when the time comes, its formidable strength might even help us out.

But I can't pin all my hopes on a cat!

With Grandfather in a coma, the weight of the Augustine family's future rests on my shoulders.

I need to level up and break through to Class S!

Only then will I stand a chance against Gerardo, or the League of Divine Descent, or the Eityreia Organization!"

As this realization sank in, his expression turned serious once more.

"Emma, you need to go back and focus on your training! I have to make a trip down to the dungeon and check on Clarissa's situation."

Emma nodded obediently and turned to leave.

Osborne cradled Divine Shadow in his arms as he exited Mount Celestial Reach, making his way swiftly to the depths of the dungeon.
