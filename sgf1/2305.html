Chapter 2256 Yapping Cur-1

"Tyson! What do you think you're doing?!" Jamar shouted, his voice laced with frustration.

Anyone would be furious when faced with such an aggressive presence, especially one that seemed intent on provoking them.

"Ha! What does look like?" Tyson laughed, a booming sound that filled the air. "I'm just here to say hello and see if your Mystic Sirius Sect disciples have improved over the years!"

He didn't back down from Jamar's shout; if anything, he intensified his aura, a tidal wave of pressure crashing down on them.

Jamar, Bethan, and the others felt their faces pale as they struggled to hold their ground, channeling their own power just to remain upright. Anger and frustration were etched on their features.

Tyson sneered and said, "Looks like you haven't made any progress at all! You're all a bunch of trash. If you run into me on the city duel stage, just surrender right away! Otherwise, don't blame me for being ruthless. My real opponents are Leroy and Marreese from Azure Haven Academy. I don't want to waste my energy on you worthless losers.

"Got it?" he added, his expression dripping with arrogance, eyes locking onto Jamar and Bethan as if they were nothing more than insects beneath his feet.

"Tyson, you're out of line!" Jamar shot back, his anger boiling over.

Tyson scoffed. "Out of line? Maybe it's not me who's arrogant, but you lot who are just pathetic! If you've got a problem with that, why don't you try and do something about it right now?"

His gaze turned icy, and Jamar and Bethan felt a chill run down their spines.

The humiliation was palpable.

This wasn't just a challenge; it was an outright insult to the Mystic Sirius Sect.

But the weight of Tyson's terrifying energy left them speechless. They knew that if they met him on the duel stage, they'd stand no chance.

Frustration bubbled within them, but they felt utterly powerless.

In this world, there was a straightforward principle—the strong ruled. The powerful could look down on and mock the weak, and if one wanted to fight back, they had to have the strength to match.

As they exchanged looks, their eyes all turned to Osborne, filled with hope.

After all, he was the champion of the Mystic Sirius Sect.

Osborne wore the sect's champion medal on his chest with pride, a clear marker of his lofty status.

Everyone was hoping he'd step forward to take on Tyson and redeem their honor.

Osborne frowned slightly.

Without saying a word, he simply waved his hand nonchalantly. In an instant, Tyson's overbearing energy dissipated, and then he started walking back to his quarters.

He said in a laid-back tone, "Let's go! No point wasting our time on a yapping cur."

Osborne couldn't have cared less about Tyson's deliberate provocation. It was like walking down the street and getting barked at by a rabid dog. No one in their right mind would fight a rabid dog, right?

Any grudges could wait until the official start of the city duel. They'd sort things out in the ring then.

Osborne's nonchalance in the face of Tyson's blatant provocation came as a breath of fresh air to Jamar and Bethan, instantly boosting their morale.

But Tyson's expression soured.

He fixed his steely gaze on Osborne, his features hardening.

This guy has actually managed to shatter my oppressive pressure? Impressive.

Then Tyson's eyes landed on the champion medal on Osborne's chest, and a glimmer of interest flashed in them.

"Oh, so you're the big dark horse from the Mystic Sirius Sect—Osborne! Not bad, not bad! You're not caving in under my pressure. You've got some chops! I checked your file, and it says you're just an Ascension Eight Supernal Deity King. I could hardly believe it at first! How did someone like you manage to become the champion of the Mystic Sirius Sect? Are there no decent folks left in that sect?
