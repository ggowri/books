Chapter 984 Kill Them All

Standing atop the tallest building of the Lambert family's estate, Osborne weaponized his super energy pistol, methodically picking off Eityreia Organization assassins hiding within the two-mile radius.

No matter how well they concealed themselves or what cover they had, it made no difference in the face of the terrifying divine power bullets.

Once Osborne locked onto a target, the grim reaper's kiss awaited them, delivering a swift and silent end.

The power of those bullets shattered their brains in an instant, leaving no room for screams—just quiet, invisible deaths.

Twenty down.

Thirty down.

Osborne had transformed into a fearsome sniper lurking in the night, harvesting the lives of the Eityreia Organization's elite. Each of these individuals was a Class AAA assassin, a core member of their organization. For even Class S warriors, taking down one of them was no small feat, let alone wiping them all out.

Yet, in Osborne's sights, they seemed weak and no different from regular people.

Forty down!

As he fired again, a frown crossed Osborne's face the moment the bullet left the chamber.

He felt the power lagging. Simultaneously, the Divine Bone Crystal acting as the magazine on his super energy pistol suddenly shattered.

All the divine power within that crystal had been depleted.

"Damn it! That bullet didn't pack enough punch to take out a Class AAA!" Osborne muttered, eyebrows furrowing with alarm.

Quickly, he rummaged through his Dimensional Space for a new Divine Bone Crystal to replace the broken one.

Meanwhile, the divine power bullet he had just fired had already struck a hidden assassin a mile away. This assassin was lying low in the grass, waiting for the next order to attack, when the bullet found its mark, hitting him squarely in the head.

If the bullet had held sufficient power, the assassin would have met a swift death.

Unfortunately, this one merely left him gravely injured, allowing for a horrified scream to escape his lips.

"Ah!!"

The anguished cries tore through the night, causing the other Eityreia Organization assassins to freeze in shock as they scrambled to communicate, desperate to understand what had just happened.

"There's been an ambush!"

"Sniper!"

The wounded assassin's cries left his comrades in a state of disbelief.

An ambush they could comprehend, but a sniper?

What kind of sniper could pose a threat to Class AAA veterans like them?

Confusion coursed through their ranks, but they sprang into action, especially when they realized that many of their comrades were unresponsive.

Upon arriving at the location of their fallen allies, they were met with horror—comrades lay lifeless, bullet holes clean through their skulls, blood and brain matter splattered everywhere.

"There's an sniper!"

In an instant, every surviving Eityreia Organization assassin activated their Stealth Arts.

Osborne, having swapped his magazine, immediately took aim at the wounded assassin, determined to finish him off, but by then, the remaining nine assailants had all activated their Stealth Arts.

Though it was a simple application of spatial power, Osborne's reliance on Satan's Eye's X-ray vision wasn't enough to expose them.

"Ugh!" Osborne let out a frustrated huff, feeling a pang of disappointment.

"I was too careless! Got too caught up in the thrill of sniping and didn't check the energy left in the Divine Bone Crystal. I should have wiped out all fifty of those Eityreia Organization assassins, but now only forty-one are down!

"Guess there are still nine left to deal with?"

Osborne tucked away his super energy pistol with a smirk. "Well then, looks like it's time for a little fun with you guys. Just means I'll have to put in a bit more effort."

With that, he vanished from sight, his body blurring into nothingness.

While the nine remaining Eityreia Organization assassins had activated their stealth, Osborne knew they couldn't have strayed too far from their hiding spots.

In an instant, he reappeared at the location of one of the assassins.

A cold grin crept across his face. Three hundred yards to the left, in a patch of grass, he sensed a faint ripple of spatial disturbance.

"Your Stealth Art won't help you against me!" Osborne declared, gripping the Goddess of Grain's scythe tightly before vanishing once more.

When he reemerged, he stood near the assassin's hiding place, a seemingly empty expanse before him.

But Osborne knew better—one of Eityreia's assassins was hiding in plain sight, utterly focused on his surroundings, completely oblivious to the lethal presence behind him.

"Time's up!" Osborne announced as he swung the Goddess of Grain's scythe.

In a flash, he severed the assassin's head, blood spraying high into the air and splattering across the grass.

Before the body even crumpled to the ground, Osborne was gone, having sensed the second assassin cloaked in Stealth Art.

"Let's finish this!" Osborne growled, embodying the darkness as he struck down yet another assassin.

One by one, they fell like shadows at dawn, their heads collecting at his feet.

In a matter of moments, all nine Eityreia Organization assassins had been dispatched.

"Now, only Scarlett and those two Class S assassins remain!"
