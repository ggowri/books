Chapter 1483 Weakness

Darren's words sent a chill through everyone present.

Most of them, including Osborne, had never faced a Deity Overlord warrior and had only the faintest idea of just how terrifying their power could be.

In truth, many still thought that no matter how formidable a Deity Overlord warrior might be, surely a few hundred of them could stand up to the challenge.

But Darren had shattered that notion like glass.

Among everyone there, only Betty and her two sisters, who had reached the Celestial Deity level, would have a prayer of barely surviving a single strike from even the weakest Deity Overlord warrior. The rest of them? Just fodder, mere ants in the face of such overwhelming strength.

And this was only the weakest of the Deity Overlord warriors.

If a stronger one stepped in, even Betty and her sisters working together wouldn't stand a chance—they would be obliterated in an instant.

At that moment, even Betty and her sisters looked taken aback.

Osborne's brow furrowed deeply as a weight of understanding settled over him.

He realized he had underestimated the Deity Overlord warriors.

Having regained his full strength, he was confident he could easily overpower even the most elite Transcendent Deity warriors.

He had thought that with Betty and her sisters by his side, they might hold their own against a Deity Overlord like Fabian.

Perhaps they could even force him to retreat.

But now, it dawned on him that he had been mistaken. If Darren was being honest, even he would struggle against a Deity Overlord warrior, likely injured within a single blow; surviving a few hits would be a significant achievement.

At best, he might have a chance to escape.

"This just got complicated," Osborne muttered under his breath.

But he held no regrets about insulting Fabian, especially since Fabian's intentions to buy Betty and her sisters as breeding machines were unacceptable to him.

Osborne said, a serious tone creeping into his voice, "Mr. Pollitt, outside the slave market, there's a Deity Overlord warrior from the Wild Bull Clan named Fabian waiting for me. The moment I step outside, he's going to come after me. Is there any way we can handle this? I've heard he's just recently become a Deity Overlord, so his skills shouldn't be too advanced."

At these words, Darren's tense expression eased considerably.

"A Deity Overlord from the Wild Bull Clan? That's a relief! If it were a warrior from another race, we'd be in deep trouble—our current strength wouldn't stand a chance. But a Wild Bull Clan Deity Overlord gives us a fighting chance."

Osborne's eyes brightened. "Mr. Pollitt, what do you mean by that?"

Darren smiled. "Back in the West Zone, I fought alongside many warriors from the Wild Bull Clan, including several Deity Overlords. So, I know their strengths and weaknesses pretty well.

"The Wild Bull Clan excels in defense and raw power. Even among Deity Overlord warriors, their defenses are a real headache; it's incredibly tough to inflict serious damage on them. And when they charge at full force, not much can stand in their way.

"However, they do have noticeable weaknesses—speed and agility.

"Even a Deity Overlord from the Wild Bull Clan can seem clumsy compared to Celestial Deity warriors who specialize in speed.

"If we're going to face Fabian outside, our best bet is to use hit-and-run tactics against him. Especially in Wild Flame City, he wouldn't dare unleash widespread destruction—he'd be too concerned about damaging the surrounding buildings. He'll likely try to close in on us instead.

"But..."

Darren paused, his brow furrowing as he scanned the group, his expression turning serious.

"Fabian is still a Deity Overlord. No matter how slow he might be, I doubt any of us can match his speed, let alone outmaneuver him. It's a tough situation."

His words hung heavily in the air, and everyone's faces reflected their concern.

Betty exchanged glances with her sisters, her expression equally grave.

They couldn't help but sigh. After all, none of the three excelled in speed.

If they had, they wouldn't have ended up captured and put on display at the slave market.

Despite the Wild Bull Clan's slower pace, Fabian's status as a Deity Overlord meant his speed would still outstrip theirs.

"What do we do now?"

Betty and her sisters frowned deeply, brainstorming solutions.

If they couldn't neutralize the threat posed by Fabian, their newly formed Spark of Blaze wouldn't even be able to leave the slave market.

They could be snuffed out before they even got started!

And they couldn't hide in the slave market forever; it closed at midnight, leaving them just a few hours to figure something out.

Once the clock struck twelve, they'd have to face Fabian head-on.

"What's the plan?"

Everyone was deep in thought, including Osborne.

"Is speed really Fabian's weak point? Maybe we can work with that!"

He turned to Tiana, who was standing a short distance away. "Tiana, I need a private room!"
