Chapter 1400 Strike

At Patrick's command, a dozen Intermediate Deities lunged toward Harry and Amelia, their faces twisted into cruel grins. The siblings didn't even attempt to resist; they knew it would be futile.

Each of these Intermediate Deities hailed from the neighboring village, and Harry was all too aware of their strength.

Individually, any one of them posed a significant threat, and together, they were overwhelming.

There was no chance of escape, not even a glimmer of hope.

And even if they could run, where would they go?

With their limited abilities, getting out of this mining area would only lead to a gruesome death at the hands of Deathborns, leaving nothing.

At that moment, despair washed over them.

They understood all too well what awaited them. Even if they weren't directly responsible for Vance's death, the discovery of the vast number of Essence Crystals in their possession—including over a hundred mid-grade ones—would seal their fate.

Patrick would never let them go.

All they could do now was hope for a swift end, to suffer as little as possible.

Harry gritted his teeth, desperation lacing his voice. "Patrick, just kill us!

"Vance was out to get us, and it was Deathborn who finished him off! We can tell you where the high-grade vein is, just please spare us the torture and let us die quickly!"

Patrick's laughter echoed in the tense air.

"Harry, you're smart enough to know when to beg, but it won't save you! I have plenty of ways to find out where that high-grade vein is without your help. But you two? You killed my wife's brother, Vance.

"You won't get off easy! How could I explain that to her?"

With a swift motion, Patrick pulled the beautiful woman closer to him.

She looked up at him, her eyes shimmering with gratitude. "I knew you'd do the right thing, darling! These two scoundrels killed my brother. I want to make them suffer, to make their lives a living hell!"

"Of course, my love," Patrick replied, a cold smile creeping onto his face as he turned his gaze back to Harry and Amelia.

"Did you hear that? Lock them up and make sure they're thoroughly tortured! I want their screams to echo through this village for three days and nights! Let everyone know what happens to those who dare cross me!"

With that, his followers grinned wickedly, eager to drag Harry and Amelia into the depths of the stone hut for their torment.

The siblings' faces turned ashen, despair filling their eyes.

At this point, even death seemed a luxury they couldn't afford.

Just as they were about to be pulled away, a soft cough broke the heavy silence.

From behind the stone hut, a figure emerged slowly.

It was Osborne.

"Still someone here! You? Good, you didn't run away. You're one of their accomplices too! Grab him and make sure he suffers as well!"

Patrick sneered, unfazed by Osborne's appearance.

It was clear to anyone that Osborne was gravely injured—most tellingly, he lacked the Divine Flame above his head.

That meant he hadn't ascended to godhood yet.

He was not even a Junior Deity to them.

In Celestara, Junior Deities were considered the lowest of the low, and if Osborne wasn't even that, he was nothing more than an immature baby.

Who would see a baby as a threat?

As Patrick finished speaking, one of his Intermediate Deity subordinates approached Osborne with a menacing grin.

"Don't even think about struggling. If I squeeze too hard, I might just pop your head like a balloon!"

Harry and Amelia's faces turned white at the sight, their hearts sinking further into despair.

"Stop! Patrick, this has nothing to do with Osborne!

"He had no idea we killed Vance or anything about that high-grade vein! He's innocent, you can kill us instead, just let Osborne go," Amelia shouted loudly.

Patrick sneered, saying, "Innocent, you say? I'll believe it when I've interrogated him! Take him in, and make sure to torture him thoroughly."

"Got it!"

The Intermediate Deity chuckled as he reached for Osborne's shoulder, looking completely at ease.

But in that instant, Osborne struck.

He held an ordinary-looking scythe, which was none other than the Goddess of Grain's scythe. With a gentle swing, the scythe sliced through the air at a terrifying speed towards the Intermediate Deity's arm.

It was too fast!

The Intermediate Deity, already underestimating Osborne, didn't even see it coming. By the time he realized what was happening, it was too late to dodge.

In a flash, the sharp edge of the Goddess of Grain's scythe effortlessly severed his entire arm, blood spraying everywhere.

However, Osborne remained clean, protected by a transparent divine power barrier.

"Ahhh!"

A sharp, agonizing scream echoed through the air.

Despite losing an arm, the Intermediate Deity wasn't immediately killed. He glared at Osborne, his eyes burning with mad rage.

"You filthy bastard, you cut off my arm! I'm going to kill you!"

Enraged beyond measure, he threw a powerful punch directly at Osborne's head.

But in the next second, his own head was sent flying into the air.
