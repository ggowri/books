Chapter 2332 A Series of Battles-2

This time, the Sacred Power arrow effortlessly shattered Benson's defenses and pierced through his shoulder, sending him crashing back.

Benson sprayed blood on the ground, a look of bewilderment in his eyes as he lay there, staring blankly upwards.

He had lost!

After a grueling five minutes, he faced defeat—not just in terms of losing, but in the profound despair he'd felt during the confrontation.

It wasn't merely regret; it was a crushing sense of hopelessness.

At that moment, he realized this defeat felt more like a release than a despairing loss, far worse than his earlier defeat against Agatha.

"You're toast!" Osborne's voice rang out through the stillness.

Benson stirred a bit and slowly sat up. His eyes locked onto Osborne, a maelstrom of emotions swirling in his gaze.

After a long beat, he yanked off his chest badge and chucked it at Osborne.

"I'm beat! I throw in the towel!"

With a wince, he continued, "So Agatha was talking about you, huh? She tracked us down and took us out because she decided we weren't worth her time anymore, all thanks to you?"

Osborne arched an eyebrow. "Did Agatha really say that?"

Benson nodded with a wry smile on his face. "It's a real kick in the teeth. Before the state duel started, I thought I was untouchable, that I could take on anyone. And here I am, getting my butt kicked twice in a row... what a humiliation!"

One at the hands of Agatha and the other, a grueling contest against Osborne.

In Benson's eyes, Agatha still emerged as the stronger fighter. However, he had to admit that if anyone had a shot at challenging her, it might just be Osborne.

After all, even Horace had bitten the dust. Agatha's score was creeping close to 11 million. At this point, the only one who could still pose a threat to Agatha was Osborne.

He leaned forward, eyes blazing with determination. "Osborne, do me a favor. Help us take down Agatha!

"The state duel champion can't end up being a woman! We can't let a woman stand above all us men!"

Osborne couldn't help but laugh at that sentiment.

Meanwhile, Clara and Leroy shot him angry looks. "What's wrong with women? Why can't a woman win the championship? Your thinking is pretty outdated! Isn't it funny that you, a disciple of the Resurgent Throne Palace, are rooting for Osborne instead of Agatha winning?"

Benson merely sighed, not bothering to defend himself.

At that moment, a new figure emerged from the distance, bringing with him an aura of threat.

It was Broderick.

The moment he spotted Benson sprawled on the ground and Osborne clutching his chest badge, Broderick came to a sudden halt, his face etched with utter disbelief.

Broderick asked with a frown, "Benson, what in the world is going on here?"

Benson managed to pull himself up and shot a quick look at Broderick. "Look for yourself. I got clobbered. I lost to Osborne! You're just in the nick of time 'cause I've got a sneaking suspicion you're the next one up."

Broderick's eyes nearly popped out in shock. "You lost? One-on-one? Or was it a three-on-one situation?"

There had to be multiple opponents ganging up on Benson for him to lose; that was the only thing that added up. After all, Broderick reckoned he was a smidge stronger than Benson, but it was a close call.

He knew he couldn't bank on winning a one-on-one bout.

If Benson had been defeated by three opponents ganging up on him, Broderick thought he might have a shot at taking them on one by one and emerging victorious in the end.

But alas, Benson's next words blew his hopes sky-high.

"One-on-one!"

Broderick let out a deep breath and sized Osborne up. His expression was grim because he knew that Benson wouldn't pull the wool over his eyes on this matter.

So, Osborne's better than Benson?

It was a bitter pill to swallow, but instead of backing off like a coward, Broderick's fighting spirit went through the roof.

"Let's do this! Just because you took down Benson doesn't mean you can take me out! If you wanna prove yourself to me, you gotta fight me straight up!"

With that, Broderick charged forward, itching for a scrap.
