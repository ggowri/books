Chapter 7 The Real Ace Up His Sleeve

"Hold it! I'll let her go!"

Just as Ophelia finished counting down and was about to order the shooting, Osborne took a deep breath and pushed Evelyn away.

He stared at Ophelia with a crazy look in his eyes. "Ophelia, tell your men to get out of the hospital right now. Otherwise, if a single hair on my sister's head is harmed, I'll make sure all of you pay with your lives!"

Ophelia scoffed disdainfully at his words.

Seeing that Evelyn was free again, she sneered coldly, "Loser, you're in no position to bargain with me now. You're nothing but a sitting duck!"

With that, she waved her hand confidently. "Go up there and capture him alive! If he resists, you're allowed to shoot!"

A dozen bodyguards moved at her command. Grinning and sneering, they rushed up the stairs with their guns in hand and soon reached Osborne.

But at that moment, Osborne laughed.

Ophelia snapped, "What are you laughing at, loser?"

"I'm laughing at you for celebrating too soon!"

With that, in full view of everyone, Osborne abruptly flung open his coat.

"Ophelia, open your damn eyes and see what this is! If you've got the guts, order them to shoot! Come on!"

Hearing Osborne's roar, Ophelia remained calm at first, scoffing, "Loser, I told you, no matter what tricks you have up your sleeve, today you're..."

Suddenly, she stopped speaking. Her eyes widened in horror as she looked at Osborne's body.

Her eyes widened in horror as she looked at Osborne's body.

Not just her, everyone was in shock.

They stepped back in terror, as if they'd seen something truly terrifying.

Under his coat, Osborne was covered in rows of homemade explosives.

And in his right hand, he held a remote control, his finger already on the red button.

"Damn it!" Ophelia gasped, feeling a chill run down her spine.

Osborne saw her fear and sneered, "Ophelia, the high-grade explosives on me can turn my body into mincemeat and reduce this entire villa to rubble. None of you will make it out alive.

"I'm just an ordinary civilian. It's worth it to have you noble aristocrats accompany me to hell!"

Osborne's cold words made everyone gasp.

Ophelia shrieked, "Osborne, you're crazy! Where did you get so many high-grade explosives?"

In Rosmandi, guns were everywhere.

It was easy to get a pistol if one had money.

Even automatic rifles, though more strictly controlled, weren't a problem for the aristocratic families.

However, high-grade explosives were under extremely strict control.

Even a powerful family like the Caldwells had a hard time getting their hands on them.

So, Ophelia never imagined that Osborne would come to the Caldwell Estate strapped with explosives.

Osborne said coldly, "Ophelia, I told you, I didn't plan on leaving here alive when I came today. If I don't get the divorce compensation, we'll all go down together."

After saying that, Osborne suddenly took a few steps forward.

He walked up to a bodyguard who was still holding a gun. The guard didn't dare to shoot. As Osborne approached, cold sweat dripped down his face, and his hand holding the gun began to tremble.

Osborne sneered and snatched the gun from him.

The guard didn't dare to resist, afraid that any sudden move would make Osborne accidentally detonate the explosives.

At such a close distance, if the explosives went off, he wouldn't even have a body left.

He could only watch as Osborne took the gun and then smashed the butt of it hard against his eyebrow.

The guard let out a scream and fell to the ground, blood gushing from his head.

Osborne looked down at him and said coldly, "A few hours ago at the Caldwell Tower, it was you who smashed my head with the gun butt, right?

"How does it feel to be on the receiving end now?"

After saying that, Osborne suddenly stomped hard on the bodyguard's face, crushing his nose bridge and knocking him unconscious.

Then, he turned to look at the other bodyguards around.

These were Ophelia's bodyguards who had all laid hands on him earlier. They'd beaten his body and trampled on his dignity.

Now, there was nothing but terror in their eyes.

Without hesitation, Osborne brought the butt of the gun down on another bodyguard, knocking him to the ground, and then started punching and kicking.

And then the third.

The fourth.

The fifth.

In no time, the dozen or so bodyguards who had ganged up on Osborne a few hours ago were lying on the ground, their heads bloodied and faces covered in gore.

Ophelia could only watch helplessly.

As she stared at the crazy Osborne, a chill ran down her spine. It was as if she was seeing him for the very first time.

Was this really the good-for-nothing Osborne she used to know?

Today, he was a whole new person. He was wild, mighty, and dauntless!

"Enough!" Ophelia finally summoned the courage to push aside her fear and roared at the top of her voice.

"Osborne, I know you. You wouldn't go down with us. You've had your revenge. Call it off!"

Osborne wiped the blood off his pistol and said nonchalantly, "Are you really sure you know me?"
