Chapter 649 Angry Owen

"At that time, my points will instantly rise by over fifteen hundred!"

"According to the current ranking..."

Owen murmured, quickly scanning the scoreboard with a smile.

Vincent: minus 1620 points

Matthew: minus 1760 points

Emma: minus 1850 points

Osborne: minus 2020 points

Owen: minus 2060 points

Lance: minus 2960 points

All six participants on the scoreboard had negative points.

They could only defend the headquarters, as the Aberrants had breached all other properties, resulting in a deduction of at least 2,100 points.

The point gaps were due to the varying numbers of enemies each had defeated, but the difference was not significant.

Particularly, Vincent, Matthew, and Emma only needed to eliminate one or two Class AA enemies to improve their standings.

After looking at the standings, Owen felt smug.

"If all three sneak attack squads succeed, my points will increase by at least 1,500 points and I'll be number one on the standings!"

"Besides, leave the others far behind!"

"Even if it's temporary, being at the top will impress the godfather."

Owen was very expectant and excited.

He awaited updates from the squads, hoping to have his name listed at the top of the standings.

He felt that if Osborne could do it alone, he could definitely do better.

Time passed quickly, half an hour passed.

"The attack should begin! The Aberrants' defenses in the rare metal mining area are weak. The squads can finish in minutes!"

"My ranking in the standings will soon be at the top!"

Owen's excitement grew.

However, after several minutes, he still hadn't received good news. His points only increased by ten occasionally.

Seeing this, Osborne frowned.

"The slight increase means the squads have started attacking and taken down a few Class A enemies."

"But why haven't they resolved the battle and taken back the rare metal mining areas?"

"Don't be anxious. Maybe they will succeed soon."

Owen continued to wait.

But after another ten minutes, he still hadn't received any good news. He felt anxious, sensing something was wrong.

Finally, the three sneak attack squads reported back.

"Mr. Augustine, help!"

"The Aberrants have at least three Class AAs, three Class A+s, and nearly ten Class As guarding the mining area!"

"Our intelligence was wrong. We were ambushed."

"We've suffered heavy losses and can't hold out!"

"Help!"

A member of the sneak attack squads shouted in an anxious voice.

Owen's face turned pale. "How is that possible? You must be mistaken!"

He roared angrily, his cheeks turned extremely red.

But as more grim news arrived, he realized they were not wrong.

Each squad faced overwhelming odds, with at least three enemy Class AAs defending each area.

Under such circumstances, the sneak attack squads seemed to have walked into a trap. As soon as the battle broke out, they were immediately at an absolute disadvantage.

Although they could occasionally kill the enemy, their own losses were greater.

After about ten minutes of fighting, most of them were wiped out.

The few survivors sent brief messages before being overwhelmed.

Owen understood they were all dead.

"How is it possible?" he exclaimed, disoriented by the heavy losses.

There were six Class AA members in the three sneak attack squads, and the support forces behind him only had a total of more than twenty Class AA members.

The loss was too heavy.

Owen could hardly accept it.

Most importantly, he had dismissed warnings about the risks of the sneak attack, and now he had to bear the consequences alone.

He collapsed onto the sofa, while those around him fell silent, their faces grim.

The losses were catastrophic.

"Damn it!" Owen suddenly shouted, "This is Osborne's fault!"

"He gave me wrong information. The force of the Aberrants guarding the rare metal mining area is far stronger than what he said!"

"If it weren't for him, I wouldn't have taken this risk and lost so many experts!"

His face twisted with rage as he blamed Osborne for the operation's failure.
