Chapter 2125 Radiant Golden Ore-1

"Is it over?"

Alfreda approached Osborne, her gaze drawn to the Bloodwrought Bow gripped tightly in his hands, its eerie crimson glow captivating her. A sense of admiration mixed with envy washed over her.

She could feel an undeniable threat radiating from the bow.

Previously, although she had been defeated by Osborne, he had only managed to breach her Luminous Elven Wings' defenses when wielding the Dimensional Maw.

Now, however, Alfreda sensed a palpable danger emanating from a mere divine artifact placed in Osborne's hands.

"What kind of boost are we talking about?" Alfreda couldn't help but ask, her curiosity piqued.

With a subtle smile, Osborne replied, "Almost ten times as powerful!"

"What?!" Alfreda's jaw dropped in disbelief.

Twenty-eight pieces of Azure Ore had amplified the Bloodwrought Bow's power by tenfold!

Her envy flared, and a hint of frustration crept into her expression.

She pulled out her Arcane Staff of Nature, clenched her jaw, and said, "No way! I want Azure Ore too! My staff is an Apex divine artifact—its previous power was definitely greater than your Bloodwrought Bow. But now? I feel like your bow has surpassed it!"

"I've got to make my staff stronger!"

Osborne chuckled, "Perfect timing! I could use more Azure Ore myself. Let's head into the Corrosive Marsh and hunt for some Mud Golems to snag what we need!"

With that, he waved his hand, summoning a swarm of Spatial Clones.

Two hundred fifty-six clones appeared around him, standing ready for action.

"Spread out and search for Mud Golems! Bring back Azure Ore!"

After he finished speaking, two hundred and fifty-six Spatial Clones dispersed and headed in all directions.

Alfreda watched in awe, struck dumb by the spectacle.

"Spatial Clones? You have so many! Are you really sending them to fight Mud Golems? Can they handle it? Can they really collect Azure Ore?"

Osborne nodded confidently. "They should be alright."

"My Spatial Clones have around sixty to seventy percent of my attacking power, but their defenses aren't as strong. Mud Golems are slow, so they won't be much of a threat."

"They can definitely take down the Mud Golems and fetch us Azure Ore!"

Then, looking at Alfreda, Osborne added with a grin, "Since you're my little sidekick, I won't shortchange you! Whatever Azure Ore my clones gather, I'll give you ten percent!"

Alfreda's eyes widened.

She turned to him, an impish glint in her gaze as she hooked her arm around his. "Ten percent? Come on, that's hardly fair! How about we split it fifty-fifty?"

Osborne shot her an amused glare. "You want to split it equally when you're not lifting a finger? Not happening! Ten percent is already generous!"

Not bothering to acknowledge Alfreda's playful pestering, Osborne reached into his Dimensional Bag and handed it over to her.

He said, "My Spatial Clones also found some Element Gold Crystals in the Death Chasm! Given your situation, I'll toss you ten percent of those too."

"Next, I'm going to study that pale golden crystal. You go off and practice on your own!"

After saying that, Osborne ignored Alfreda.

"Now you're being stingy! Just ten percent for your little sidekick? That's not how a boss rolls!" Alfreda said as she accepted the Dimensional Bag, her lips forming a playful pout.

After Alfreda opened the bag, her expression quickly shifted from bemused to astonished.

"What the..."

Her eyes nearly popped out of her head as she gazed at the sheer volume of Element Gold Crystals packed inside the bag—all organized and shimmering brilliantly.

"Three hundred Element Gold Crystals?! And they're all the plant elemental kind I need?"
