Chapter 1220 Storming the Base

Alexander moved through the pitch-black forest like a ghost, leaving only brief screams and the dull thuds of falling bodies in his wake.

The darkness was so thick that other people might not even see their hand, but that didn't slow him down. He spotted each guard post long before they had a chance to spot him.

He came across a makeshift treehouse perched on an old oak tree that would've taken two people to wrap their arms around. A faint orange light flickered inside.

Without making a sound, Alexander appeared next to a black-clad guard who had dozed off at his post.

One quick tap to the forehead was all it took—the Force blast scrambled the guy's brain stem before he even hit the ground below, never knowing what hit him.

The night turned into a silent killing field. One by one, the sentries dropped, none of them living long enough to realize they were dead.

The metallic smell of blood drifted far on the night breeze.

Alexander had cleared out all the guards in front of them pretty quickly, but there was still no word from Jonah, who'd gone to check the left flank.

"What's the situation up ahead?" Quentin asked, his eyes hopeful as he looked at Alexander.

"All clear," Alexander replied with a cool smile. "There's nobody left breathing up there. We're good to move forward."

"Perfect! I knew I could count on you!" Quentin clapped his hands together, clearly pumped.

Ilona's eyes lit up as she stared at Alexander, practically hanging on his every move with that starry-eyed look of hers.

The team waited around for a while longer, but there was still no sign of Jonah.

"What's taking him so long? You think something went wrong?" Quentin was starting to get antsy.

Alexander didn't say a word. He just closed his eyes to rest.

He knew they'd be in for one hell of a fight once they got into Sakra Society's hideout, and he needed to stay sharp.

Finally, Jonah came jogging back from the left side, drenched in sweat.

He wasn't moving nearly as smoothly as before, breathing hard with his massive sword dripping blood onto the ground through its grooves.

He'd been wearing a pretty satisfied grin until he spotted Alexander—then his face fell. Clearly, he'd been hoping to beat Alexander back, but no such luck.

"Alright, people, let's roll!" Quentin threw his hand forward. "We're storming their base. Anyone who resists—take them out. No mercy."

The whole team sprang into action, making a beeline for the secret entrance.

The Sakra Society had picked a sweet spot for their hideout—tucked away in a cave halfway up the mountain.

"Ilona, Jonah, each of you take a squad and hit them from the sides," Quentin barked out orders. "We'll punch through the front. Time to box these bastards in."

Jonah gave a quick nod, and he and Ilona each took command of their squads before heading out.

"Everyone else, stay sharp and follow my lead. Let's move!" Quentin ordered as he led the remaining troops in a fierce charge toward the nearby base entrance.

The dead of night erupted into chaos as gunfire and battle cries shattered the silence.

The strike team's surprise attack caught the Sakra Society completely off guard.

Their advantage was overwhelming—fifty metahumans against a base staffed mostly with regular personnel. It wasn't even going to be a fair fight.

The timing couldn't have been better. Most of the base's staff were just stumbling out of their beds, still groggy and disoriented.

And with Alexander in the mix—that walking nightmare—no one stood a chance of lasting more than a few seconds against him.

Quentin's team stormed the facility, breaking into smaller combat units that swept through the maze of corridors. They systematically cleared each section, pushing steadily toward the heart of the base.

The team's silenced weapons made quick, quiet work of any resistance. Bodies of Sakra Society members hit the floor one after another, leaving trails of blood in their wake.

Screams echoed through the halls as panic spread like wildfire throughout the compound.

In less than ten minutes, the strike team had secured half the facility, leaving over a hundred casualties in their wake.

Their momentum only grew stronger. With captured personnel leading the way, they carved a path straight toward the command center.

Just then, blaring alarms ripped through the entire base.

"Who dares to attack us? They must have a death wish," a middle-aged man in a green uniform roared from the command center, his face twisted with rage.
