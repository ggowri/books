Chapter 1 Godfather

Champagne-soaked chatter filled the grand ballroom.

The cream of society had turned out in force to celebrate the engagement of Jane Fairfax - Alexander Hawthorne's wife - to Ryan Hawkins, heir to the Hawkins empire.

Alexander watched the scene unfold, white-knuckling their marriage certificate as rage boiled in his chest. Then he snapped.

"Jane!" His voice cut through the crowd. "You're my wife! What the hell is this engagement bullshit?"

The Fairfax clan soon descended on him like wolves, all shoves and snarls, trying to kick him out.

"Look who crashed the party - waving some paper, claiming he's Jane's husband," one sneered.

"Know your place, trash," another spat. "You? Marry Ms. Fairfax? In your dreams, loser!"

"How dare you say such nonsense? Fuck off!" someone else shouted.

Alexander had married into the Fairfax family years ago - no ceremony, no announcement. The social gap was too wide; they couldn't risk their precious reputation. They'd kept it secret all along.

Now with Ryan Hawkins in the picture, they saw their golden ticket. No way they'd let Alexander derail this gravy train.

They were set on ending this ridiculous marriage and kissing up to Ryan.

Alexander stood there shaking, that marriage certificate his only anchor.

Jane just watched, her face a mask of ice and disgust.

Thinking of their daughter fighting for life in the hospital, Alexander swallowed his pride.

"Jane, I get it - you're doing this for Ashley," he pleaded. "She needs that treatment money. But I'll find a way. You don't have to do this..."

"Ha! That's adorable!" Ryan's laugh cut like glass as he snatched the certificate and shredded it.

Then he pulled Jane close, smirking. "Here's the truth, buddy - Jane's been my girl for ages. Every. Single. Inch." His grin was pure venom.

Alexander felt the floor drop out from under him.

Fear flickered across Jane's face before ice claimed it again. "Just sign the divorce papers," she said flatly. "Don't flatter yourself, Alexander. Mr. Hawkins will handle Ashley's bills. You wouldn't want anything... unfortunate to happen to her, right?"

Alexander's world crumbled. In his eyes, nothing remained but raw despair.

He met Jane's gaze, jaw tight enough to crack. "Why are you doing this? Five years. I've been your family's damn dog for five years, jumping through every hoop. Whatever you wanted, I did it. And this is how you repay me?"

Jane smirked, face pure contempt. "Why? Because you're nothing. A nobody. How could you ever measure up to Mr. Hawkins?"

She pressed closer to Ryan's side. "He's the Hawkins heir. God, I must've been desperate when I married you. At least I wised up now."

The mockery in her eyes was like acid.

Alexander broke, tears cutting tracks down his face.

"Five years, Jane. Ashley's four now. I killed myself working to give you both everything."

His voice shattered. "Massaged your shoulders when you were tired. Cooked whatever you wanted, whenever. Starved myself for a month to buy that Chanel bag. And when you said you loved to travel—"

"Just shut up!" Jane snapped. "If you were so great, where's my better life? Huh?

"You got fired because you're worthless. Can't even pay for Ashley's surgery. Five years of chances, and what did you do? Jack shit!

"Once a loser, always a loser. You don't deserve someone like me."

She turned to Ryan, sugar-sweet. "Ryan's different. He's the sole heir to the Hawkins billions. And he's connected to the Saint Group."

Her smile turned razor-sharp. "You know the Saint Group, right? One word and the city bows. But," she laughed, "that's way above your pay grade.

"Go sign the papers. We'll cover Ashley's bills, and Ryan might even throw you a bone - career-wise. Don't waste your one shot at not being pathetic."

Ryan scoffed. "Why bother? Whether he agrees or not doesn't matter. He'll cave eventually." He waved lazily. "Someone! Get this trash out of my party."

Several security guards quickly grabbed Alexander by the arms and dragged him toward the exit.

He fought back hard until a fist found his gut. The world tilted sideways as pain exploded through his skull, his vision swimming in and out of focus.

Everything blurred into a nightmare carousel of sneering faces and vicious words, the Fairfax family's accusations hitting him like physical blows.

An hour later, Alexander limped back to the hospital, every step pure agony.

With Ashley needing critical care and Jane's betrayal still burning fresh, he stood outside Ashley's room, completely broken. His hand trembled at the door, but he couldn't bring himself to push it open. He couldn't bear to face the hope in her eyes...

Suddenly, the roar of helicopters shattered the silence.

Everyone's heads whipped up as a fleet of black choppers descended like birds of prey, casting ominous shadows across the hospital grounds. They touched down in perfect formation, their downdraft creating a mini-storm.

A small army of men in tactical black suits emerged, moving with lethal grace. They radiated pure danger - the kind that made civilians dive for cover without being told twice.

Alexander watched with bitter envy.

Some hotshot bigwig is throwing their weight around.

If I had that kind of juice...

Jane and Ryan would be fucking roaches under my boot—just waiting to get squashed.

To his surprise, those men marched forward and surrounded him, dropping to one knee in perfect sync.

"By order of the Godfather," they thundered as one, "we're here to take you home.

"Welcome back, Mr. Hawthorne!"
