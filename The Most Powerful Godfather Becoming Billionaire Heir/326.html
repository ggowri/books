Chapter 326 Refuse to Learn Your Lesson

Roman let out a deep sigh, a wry smile on his face.

"It's a shame. After all the hard work I put into my master's degree, finally landing a job at Tijuanea Bank... If I get fired, my parents would be heartbroken. And honestly, no other bank would even consider hiring me after that."

As he spoke, tears welled up in his eyes.

Alexander placed a comforting hand on his shoulder. "Don't worry, I'll do everything I can to help you."

"You helping me? Just don't gloat over my misfortune. I'm not counting on you for anything," Roman said, rolling his eyes, feeling like Alexander was mocking him on purpose.

Alexander turned to Janus, his expression shifting to one of cold determination. "Get lost. I'll make sure you don't last another minute at Tijuanea Bank."

His confidence left everyone stunned, their eyes wide with disbelief.

Only Madelyn seemed to believe in Alexander, her excitement bubbling over.

Janus looked taken aback for a moment before a sinister grin spread across his face.

"You think you can just hit me and walk away? I haven't settled the score yet. I'll make sure someone breaks your hands!"

Alexander's voice was icy. "Since you refuse to learn your lesson, I'll show you what real consequences feel like."

With that, he struck Janus squarely on the forehead.

It felt as if a thousand tiny needles pierced Janus' mind. The Force was working.

"Damn it, are you trying to hit me again?" Janus roared, rage boiling over as he swung his fist at Alexander.

"Ow!"

He suddenly clutched his head, shaking uncontrollably from the pain.

"Oh God, it hurts! My head is splitting open..."

Janus collapsed to the ground, cradling his head in his hands, howling in agony.

It felt like countless needles were digging into his brain, driving him to the brink of madness.

Moments later, Janus was drenched in cold sweat, the stench of urine filling the air as he lost control of his bladder.

"I'm sorry! Please... don't hurt me anymore..." Janus cried, his voice breaking with despair.

Alexander smiled, a glint of satisfaction in his eyes.

"Weren't you just saying you'd fire Roman and get someone to break my hands?"

"I was wrong! I was just talking nonsense! I swear I'll do everything to unfreeze the funds... please, just let me go!" Janus begged, his dignity shattered.

Alexander looked down at him, relishing the sight of Janus' pain and regret. It was a satisfying turn of events.

Madelyn and the others were wide-eyed, shocked at the strange power Alexander seemed to wield over Janus, who was now a quivering mess.

They couldn't help but feel a surge of joy at seeing such a scumbag getting his comeuppance.

Before long, Janus was convulsing on the ground, foam bubbling at his mouth, completely unconscious.

Only then did Alexander casually pat Janus on the head, the invisible Force in his mind temporarily subdued.

Janus suddenly felt a rush of clarity, a mix of joy and terror flooding through him.

"Wait... you... you can use magic? I'm sorry! I didn't know you were a wizard!"

The room fell silent.

Roman and Avery were speechless, and even Madelyn stared at Alexander in shock.

Seeing Alexander's unyielding expression, Janus broke out in a cold sweat, terrified of experiencing that hellish pain again.

"Please forgive me! I made a mistake... I'm not a person; I'm a jerk! I'm not human!"

As he berated himself, Janus slapped his own face hard, the sound echoing in the quiet room.

His cheeks quickly turned red and swollen.

"That's enough!" Alexander waved his hand, clearly annoyed.

He added, "I'll give you two days. If you don't unfreeze those funds, you can expect a living hell."

"Yes... yes! I promise I'll make it happen..." Janus stammered, desperate to avoid another round of torment.

The pain he just endured was worse than anything he could have imagined.

"This place is disgusting. Let's find somewhere else to eat," Alexander said, beckoning to Madelyn.

The three of them snapped out of their daze and hurried to follow him out of the room.

Avery and Roman were both in awe of Alexander's mysterious abilities, carefully keeping a step behind as he and Madelyn led the way.

"Mr. Dukes, is that you?"

Madelyn furrowed her brow; how did this guy know her number?

"Ms. Price, I've consulted with Mr. Myers, and I've managed to arrange a meeting. But Mr. Myers is very busy and doesn't like too many people around; can you come alone to discuss this?"

Owen's words made Madelyn feel elated.

Although Madelyn felt a bit worried, she believed a mere supervisor wouldn't dare to lie in Mr. Myers' name. It must be because Mr. Myers has a special status that only she could meet him.

Without further thought, Madelyn quickly tidied up and prepared to go to the appointment.

She also left a message for Avery, just in case.

Avery and Roman were both stunned by Alexander's magical skills. They carefully lagged behind Alexander and let him and Madelyn walk in front.
