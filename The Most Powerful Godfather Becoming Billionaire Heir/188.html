Chapter 188 Overwhelming

"Hahaha! I thought you were something special, but turns out you're just trash."

Ewan laughed triumphantly, fully expecting to knock Alexander flat on his back in the next moment.

But in a flash, Alexander simply smiled, unfazed.

"Ants are still ants, utterly foolish," he said, his voice calm and steady.

With a swift movement, he reached out and caught Ewan's fist in his hand like a lightning bolt striking.

To the onlookers, it seemed almost leisurely, but in reality, it was as fast as a flash of lightning.

"What the hell?"

Ewan's expression shifted from confidence to shock.

That was his strongest punch, one that could easily smash through a door, yet Alexander took it with one hand.

As Alexander tightened his grip, Ewan felt as if a massive iron band was crushing his hand.

The sound of cracking knuckles filled the air, and a sharp pain shot through him, unbearable and intense.

Unable to bear it any longer, Ewan let out a scream that echoed through the room like a tenor hitting a high note.

The members of the Fraternity stood frozen in disbelief, while Ian's heart raced with fear. He took several steps back.

Ewan was one of the top fighters in the Fraternity, and yet here he was, injured by Alexander in one swift move.

Judging by Ewan's twisted hand, it was clear: he was done for.

"Looks like you've got some skills; no wonder you're so cocky. But thinking you can run wild in the Fraternity with just your strength? That's naive.

"I've got over a hundred men outside. We can just bury you," Ian shouted, trying to maintain a facade of bravado.

Alexander shot Ian a cold smile, his demeanor sending chills down Ian's spine.

"This is just the beginning. You bring as many as you want; I'll take them all down."

With a powerful kick, Alexander sent Ewan flying across the room.

He crashed into the wall like a cannonball, hitting the ground and lying motionless.

The whole room shook from the impact, causing Lillian to scream in terror.

Ian rushed over to Ewan. He shouted in panic.

"Ewan! Are you okay? Please, say something!"

Ewan was Ian's most loyal helper, a key player in the Fraternity's hold on Loscity. To see him so easily destroyed by Alexander was a nightmare.

Blood streamed from Ewan's mouth as he struggled to speak, his breath ragged.

Realizing Ewan was on the brink of death, Ian roared, "You bastard, Alexander! You hurt my buddy; you'll pay for this!

"Everyone, get him! What are you waiting for?

"Take him down!"

Ian felt as though his dignity had been mercilessly trampled under Alexander's heel, and he knew he couldn't let this grudge go unpunished.

The dozen or so men in the room surged forward like a pack of wolves, howling as they charged at Alexander.

Although Alexander had displayed remarkable skills, his opponents were emboldened by their numbers. They figured that even if it came down to sheer number, they could overwhelm him.

But Alexander stood there. Suddenly, he spun around and left everyone dizzy.

In the chaos, the attackers couldn't even see him.

They crashed into him like waves breaking against rocks, collapsing to the ground with broken bones and cries of pain.

Inside, they swarmed Alexander, launching a coordinated attack, while outside, more and more people rushed in, eager to join the fray.

Augusta and Marvin held the entrance, fending off anyone who dared to step inside.

One by one, the attackers fell, rolling on the ground and howling in agony.

Alexander's fury at the Fraternity for injuring his men fueled his strength. He didn't hold back; those who survived would be left crippled for life.

The entrance was littered with bodies, thanks to Augusta and Marvin's relentless teamwork.

"How can so many people not take him down? Is he some kind of demon?"

Lillian's eyes were wide with fear.

Ian felt like he was standing on the edge of a cliff. Despite having overwhelming numbers, he was crushed by Alexander and his two allies.

The ones inside the room were incapacitated, and those outside couldn't break through.

What shocked Ian the most was Alexander's ruthless nature—something he had never expected.

The thought of what awaited him at Alexander's hands sent shivers down Ian's spine, leaving him drenched in cold sweat.

Alexander strode confidently up to Ian, his demeanor dripping with arrogance.

"Your turn now. Have you figured out how you want to die?"

Stunned, Ian fell silent, and an oppressive stillness enveloped the room.

Even those in the hallway hesitated to break in, staring wide-eyed at Alexander with a mix of shock and fear.
