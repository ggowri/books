Chapter 486 Candlelight Dinner

"You promised me! No backing out!"

Ava glared at Alexander.

Seeing her serious expression, Alexander realized she was genuinely committed to this plan.

Ava then threw herself into his arms, seeking comfort.

"Don't worry, I won't leave you. I just can't bear to see my mom suffer. She has endured so much for me, and I didn't appreciate her until now...

"She gave me this precious Obsidian Core just like that, and I don't want her to be sad anymore or encounter any bad guys again."

Alexander gazed at the ceiling, imagining how wonderful it would be if he could effortlessly bring joy to both mother and daughter.

The thought thrilled him.

"Let's do this: tomorrow night, we'll celebrate my mom's birthday. That's your best opportunity. Trust me, I'll help you!"

Ava hugged Alexander tightly, pushing him back onto the bed as she spoke.

The next evening, Lynn sat at home, feeling a bit restless.

It was her birthday, yet she felt no excitement.

She had planned to spend the day with Victor, but thoughts of him dulled her mood.

Ava mentioned going out to meet an important friend, and Lynn felt a pang of disappointment, thinking her daughter had forgotten her birthday.

"Mom, we haven't shopped together in ages. Let's go out!" Ava said cheerfully, linking arms with her mother.

Though Lynn wasn't in the mood, she forced a smile. "Oh, I don't feel like it. Aren't you meeting that important friend tonight?"

"It's still early! Come on, just this once..." Ava insisted, playfully tugging at her mother's arm.

"Alright, alright! I'll go with you."

Lynn sighed, following her daughter.

After they left, Alexander came down the stairs, looking sharp in a well-tailored suit.

He turned on the sound system, filling the villa with soft, pleasant music.

He laid a pristine white tablecloth over the dining table, set up candleholders, and lit the candles, dimming the lights for a cozy atmosphere.

He arranged vibrant roses in a vase and placed it on the table alongside gleaming wine glasses and polished silverware. The ambiance was becoming romantic.

Unbeknownst to him, Ava had discreetly added magic powder to the wine, knowing it would excite desires, especially when mixed with alcohol.

With plenty of time, Alexander began preparing dinner.

After years in the Fairfax family, he had honed his culinary skills, so crafting a lavish meal was a breeze.

Ava and Lynn spent two full hours shopping before dropping her mother off at the doorstep at home. Ava then made an excuse to leave, claiming she had to meet a friend.

Lynn sensed something odd about Ava today, but she couldn't pinpoint what it was.

As she looked up at the starry sky, she felt a wave of emotions wash over her.

Was fate really so cruel?

Losing her beloved husband in a car accident had forced her into difficult situations with men, leading to misunderstandings with her daughter.

After years of hard work, she had achieved some success and earned a decent living, yet her romantic life remained unfulfilled and flawed.

Then Victor appeared, charming her with sweet words, and they quickly began dating.

To her shock, he turned out to be a deceitful con artist!

What had she done to deserve such misfortune? Why did all these hardships fall upon her?

Tears welled in Lynn's eyes. She managed to hold back her emotions in front of Ava, but when alone, she couldn't contain herself.

Thinking Alexander was out with Ava, she opened the door, her eyes shimmering with tears.

She staggered to the door and opened it.

"Wow!" she exclaimed in surprise.

A warm, orange glow filled the room, accompanied by the delicious aroma of food.

The dining table was adorned with steaming dishes, and just then, Alexander emerged from the kitchen.
