Chapter 383 Discharge

Ava had dressed Ashley in a new dress with her hair in a bun, making her look adorable. Ashley's big eyes fixed on Alexander, her smile as sweet as a doll's.

The hospital director, along with a group of doctors and nurses, respectfully saw Ashley off.

The director, dealing with a high-ranking figure like Ava, dared not show any negligence.

Surrounded by everyone like a little star, Ashley was escorted out of the hospital, after which the director and his team finally took their leave with deep bows.

"Let's go celebrate at Aunt's house!" Alexander said.

Alexander smiled and casually looked into the rearview mirror.

The same van had been tailing them, and he smirked to himself.

Half an hour later, Alexander parked in Lily's driveway.

Lily had been waiting at the door. When she saw Ashley get out of the car, she ran up, laughing and crying, and hugged her tightly.

Before getting out, Alexander glanced back at the van parked far away. The family came first; he'd deal with them later.

Meanwhile, Belinda was at the Fairfax Villa.

Belinda asked anxiously, "Jane, today's Ashley's discharge day. Why didn't you go to pick her up?"

Andrew chimed in quickly, "Yes, if you go, you'll see Alexander. Try speaking kindly, maybe even plead a little. He might forgive you, for Ashley's sake.

"That guy has all the money now; he's at the peak of his power!"

Jane, hair disheveled and a dazed expression, seemed lost in thought.

Andrew, frustrated, nudged her, urging, "Jane, what are you thinking about? Change your clothes! Put on something sexy; you need to take advantage of this opportunity.

"We're bankrupt, and I can't show my face anywhere!"

Belinda kept urging, "Jane, if Alexander grants us a distribution license for his magic powder, we can rise up again."

Jane looked at her parents with a conflicted expression, letting out a bitter laugh.

"Do you really think Alexander would give us another chance? Haven't you had enough setbacks?"

Andrew gritted his teeth and said, "It's all because of that bastard Alexander! We're only begging him because we have no choice."

"Enough! I've already lost enough dignity. I refuse to go!"

Jane suddenly snapped.

Andrew said angrily, "What do you mean? Are you really going to ignore us, your parents, and the future of our family?"

Belinda dropped to her knees in front of Jane.

"Jane, at this point, you must go plead with Alexander. After all, you gave him a daughter."

Andrew knelt as well, pleading, "Dear, why do you think Alexander keeps going after us? Isn't it because he's not over you? Deep down, he still has feelings for you!

"If that's the case, why not use his feelings and Ashley? Have him give our family another chance, and everyone wins, right?"

Hearing this, Jane started to hesitate.

"Ha ha ha! The Fairfax family wants to rise again? It's easy!"

Suddenly, a burst of laughter echoed.

A middle-aged man with gold-rimmed glasses and a refined air strode over, followed by a large group of black-suited bodyguards.

"Who are you, and how did you get in?" Andrew asked, displeased.

A second later, he froze, his eyes widening.

"You! Aren't you the one from the news... the CEO of the Red Umbrella Group... Felix?"

Belinda and Jane were equally stunned, staring wide-eyed at Felix.

The Red Umbrella Group's financial power and connections far surpassed that of the Saint Group.

Everyone knew that the Red Umbrella Group had military ties, which granted it many privileges unmatched by any other corporation.

Felix adjusted his glasses and said with a smile, "I didn't expect Mr. Fairfax to recognize me right away. Impressive."

Andrew was instantly overjoyed.

"Mr. Vanderbilt, did you... did you just say you could help my family rise again? Is that true?"

Jane's expression shifted as this scene felt all too familiar.

Evan and Schroder had once said the same, only for it all to end in disgrace and disappointment for her.

Jane, her face pale, said, "Mr. Vanderbilt, I'm sorry, but I'm not interested in being your woman. Please leave."
