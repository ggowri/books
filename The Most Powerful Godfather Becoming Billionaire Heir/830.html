Chapter 830 Truly Deserve It

Everyone thought Alexander brought this upon himself. When Bobby's people arrived, he was bound to suffer.

Craig quickly stepped in to mediate.

"Come on, Bobby. We're all classmates. Let's not make this a big deal. I'll talk to Alexander and have him apologize. How about that?"

Bobby shoved Craig aside.

"Fatso, do you think I don't know how close you are with Alexander? Get out of my face, or I'll knock you out for a dollar!"

Craig hurriedly turned to Alexander.

"You'd better just apologize to Bobby. Avoid the trouble. We're all classmates, after all."

The other classmates, however, fueled the chaos.

"I think Alexander's lost it. He doesn't know when to back down. Mr. Murray was about to offer him a job, and now he goes and hits him?"

"Exactly! Who does he think he is? Someone needs to teach him a lesson."

"Alexander has to pay for this, so he learns his place."

The scene turned into an uproar.

But Alexander stayed calm, sitting there as if nothing had happened.

Alice, knowing Alexander well, saw the look on his face and immediately realized Bobby was in big trouble.

Bobby, seeing Alexander stay quiet, assumed he was scared and laughed smugly.

The door suddenly slammed open with a loud crash on the wall, startling Bobby.

More than ten burly men stormed in, their menacing presence filling the room.

Leading them was a man in his fifties, dressed in a black suit and tie, a cigarette dangling from his lips as he casually blew out a puff of smoke.

"Holy crap! That's Shawn Bailey from the Iron Cobras!" someone exclaimed. "I can't believe Bobby knows him. Alexander's doomed now."

"Yeah, the Iron Cobras are one of the top gangs in Loscity. Alexander's lucky if he only loses a hand today."

"He should kneel and apologize now if he knows what's good for him."

Everyone was whispering, astonished that Bobby had connections with Shawn Bailey.

Seeing their fearful expressions, Bobby became even more smug, though it pulled on his swollen cheek, making him wince.

"Ahh..."

He groaned, covering his face. Alexander's slap had really done a number on him.

He felt grateful for knowing Shawn, as it was Shawn's assistance that had enabled him to make a fortune.

Shawn barked, "Bobby, who the hell dared to hit you? Tell me, and I'll make sure they pay."

Bobby pointed viciously at Alexander.

"It's him! Shawn, break his hand for me, and I'll give you one hundred thousand dollars."

Shawn turned to look at Alexander, his eyes narrowing. Then his gaze shifted to Alice, and his expression froze in disbelief, eyes widened.

Alexander stood up, his calm demeanor unshaken, and walked over to Shawn.

"Iron Cobras, Shawn, you should know who I am, right?"

Alexander smiled faintly.

Before Shawn could respond, Bobby interrupted.

"Know you? Alexander, who the hell do you think you are? Why would Shawn know you?"

He jabbed a finger into Alexander's chest. "Scared now, aren't you? Kneel and apologize!"

Shawn suddenly shouted, "You're the one who deserves to die!"

Bobby grinned smugly. "Hear that, Alexander? Shawn said you deserve to die!"

To everyone's shock, Shawn swung his fist and punched Bobby square in the face, sending him flying.

"Ahhh!"

The room fell silent. Everyone was stunned, wondering what was going on.

They didn't understand Shawn was supposed to be Bobby's backup. Why had he just hit him?

As the crowd looked on in shock, Alexander raised his hand and slapped Shawn. Hard.

Shawn didn't move. He didn't even flinch. He stood tall, letting Alexander hit him.

Alexander slapped him again, this time on the other cheek, leaving Shawn's cheeks swollen on both sides.

But the head of the Iron Cobras still stood tall and proud, his face not showing anger but rather a mix of fear and ingratiation.
