Chapter 40 No Escape

Alexander yanked Ava close, dodging two bullets like it was nothing. In a flash, everything went back to normal.

How the hell did he pull that off! William stared at Alexander, looking like he'd seen a ghost in broad daylight.

Regret hit him hard. No amount of cash, not even ten million bucks, was worth messing with this... freak.

Without thinking twice, William turned tail and ran. As he bolted, he frantically mashed buttons on a remote.

Boom!

A car in the parking lot went up in flames.

The fireball shot into the sky, tossing nearby cars around like toys.

Shrapnel and shockwaves tore through the air, sending reporters flying like ragdolls.

Screams pierced the chaos. Some stumbled about with bloodied faces, others ran blindly in panic, while a few crawled desperately across the ground.

Ava's face drained of color. In a heartbeat, Alexander pulled her tight against him, shielding her from the mayhem.

When the powerful shockwave slammed into him, Alexander channeled The Force to create a resilient energy barrier around his body.

Though he staggered back a few steps, he remained completely unharmed.

"Ava, stay put here. I'm going after that assassin!"

Alexander pressed Ava behind a flower bed, then took off in pursuit of the fleeing William.

"Think you can get away?"

Alexander kept William locked in his Force-enhanced senses. Within a hundred yards, there was nowhere to hide.

This guy was indeed a formidable killer! Not only were his plans meticulous and vicious, but he'd also planted bombs to cover his escape.

Alexander let The Force flow through his entire body, instantly becoming light as a feather and fleet of foot.

He deftly weaved through the traffic and crowds, rapidly closing in on William.

Soon, Alexander spotted William's silhouette darting towards a park on the street corner.

The park was densely vegetated with an artificial lake. It was perfect for hiding or making a quick getaway.

Alexander swiftly scooped up a rock and hurled it at William's retreating form with all his might.

Propelled by The Force, the fist-sized stone whistled through the air like a cannonball, slamming into William's back with brutal force.

"Ah!"

William let out an agonized scream as he crashed hard onto the grass.

William tried to get up, but a searing pain shot through his back, slamming him to the ground once more.

His bones broke!

William's fierce glare was filled with terror and disbelief.

He was wearing the latest model of body armor, designed to stop bullets and knives, yet a mere rock had shattered his bones.

Damn! Alexander must be some kind of demon! His strength was inhuman!

Thank God for the armor, or that rock would've killed him outright.

William took a deep breath, fighting through the excruciating pain tearing through his back, and stumbled to his feet, staggering towards the forest.

He had to run!

Run like hell!

Only by escaping that terrifying monster did he stand a chance of survival.

William cursed himself bitterly. Five million dollars was a lot, but it wasn't worth squat compared to his life.

Alexander was somewhat surprised. This hitman was no joke.

He'd taken Alexander's full-force blow, one that had shattered the rock itself, yet he wasn't down for the count and could still run.

"Do you think you can get away?"

Alexander quickened his pace, a cold smirk playing on his lips.

In the park, near the lake, hidden in a pitch-black grove.

William tumbled onto the sidewalk, his back a bloody mess, crimson droplets splattering the ground.

He gasped for air, his face as white as a sheet.

"Were you nowhere left to run? Spill the beans on who hired you, and I might just let you live."

Alexander appeared before him, cool as a cucumber, not even breaking a sweat.

William stared at Alexander, terror etched across his face.

This guy was downright terrifying, especially those eyes that seemed to see right through him.

"You... you dodged bullets... What are you, some kind of superhuman? "

William stuttered, his words tripping over themselves.

For the first time in his life, someone had filled him with bone-chilling fear.

"Cut the crap. Who's pulling your strings?"

Alexander closed in, his graceful steps reminiscent of a regal emperor.

William's face contorted as he felt the total weight of Alexander's overwhelming presence. His whole body shook uncontrollably.

"Not talking? You got a death wish?"

Alexander's tone grew increasingly ruthless.
