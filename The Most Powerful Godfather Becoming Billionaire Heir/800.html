Chapter 800 Consequences

If possible, Zora would have loved to shoot Alexander on the spot. But with more than half of her men already lost in Loscity, she no longer dared to act so recklessly.

"Heh, Ms. Jared, aren't you going to invite me in?" Alexander smiled harmlessly.

Zora took a deep breath, forcing herself to calm down.

She replied, "Welcome, Mr. Hawthorne. Please come in."

The two of them entered the villa together, smiling as if they were old friends who hadn't seen each other in years.

Once Alexander sat down on the sofa, eight men in black suddenly rushed in.

They surrounded Alexander, glaring at him like wolves ready to pounce.

"Ms. Jared, what is the meaning of this?" Alexander asked.

Only when seeing Alexander surrounded did Zora let out a sigh of relief and allow herself to smile.

She replied, "Mr. Hawthorne, let's be direct. What did you do to my men?"

Her tone turned cold, as Alexander was trapped, and there was no need to hold back.

Alexander suddenly burst into laughter as if he had just heard the funniest joke in the world.

"Ms. Jared, do you think I'm stupid enough to walk into your den alone? Are you planning to kill me now?" he smirked, glancing at those men.

Chills crept up their spines, and they instinctively took two steps back.

Zora was oblivious to their reactions. She chuckled darkly, "Alexander, you're far too careless. You must have thought I was alone, but unlike you, I'm a very cautious person.

"No matter what you've done to my men, you're in my hands now. The advantage is mine, so you'd better behave."

She slapped the table hard.

Alexander asked, still smiling, "And what do you want?"

Growing smug, Zora crossed her legs, revealing almost her entire thigh and even a bit more.

Clearly, she was wearing something scandalous or perhaps nothing at all.

"Alexander, you're not as fearsome as the rumors say. Ha. Now, we can have a nice little chat. If you can't meet my demands, the consequences will be severe." She threatened, pleased with herself.

"Then, go ahead. What do you want to talk about?" Alexander mirrored her, crossing his legs with a smile that turned slightly cold.

"I came to Loscity with a simple goal. Hand over the formula for the magic powder to the Dragon Gang, and we'll ally with you to take on the Sakura Society.

"This way, you won't have to worry about your safety. Trading a formula for your life seems more than fair."

Dropping her pretense, she let her greedy expression show.

With Alexander under control, she no longer needed to act.

Alexander chuckled, "Ms. Jared, I should thank you. I had no idea that the Dragon Gang was here to ally with me."

Zora lifted her head proudly.

She said, "Of course. The Dragon Gang and the Sakura Society are sworn enemies. You're quite lucky to have caught our attention.

"So? Will you hand over the formula?"

Alexander gave her a contemptuous look. "Do you think these few men could do anything to me?"

"What? Are you still defiant?" Zora slapped the table again. "Alexander, you'll either hand over the formula today or die here."

She exuded confidence, issuing her death threats as if everything was under her control.

Alexander sprang to his feet, and the eight men immediately blocked his path.

The air immediately grew tense.

Yet Zora failed to notice those guards' pale faces and their trembling.

Sneering, she mocked him, "Alexander, do you think you can take on all eight of them and turn the tables?"
