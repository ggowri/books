Chapter 1134 Boosting the Morale

Carson's eyes tracked a shadow rolling down the hillside, tumbling wildly before crashing to the ground.

"Another one down!" he shouted, a grim smile spreading across his face.

The rest of the team whooped their nerves now replaced by pure adrenaline.

"Prepare yourselves. We're hitting back!" he ordered.

The remaining soldiers gripped their rifles, their bodies taut with readiness. They were waiting for the signal to charge.

Less than a minute passed before another gunshot split the silence, followed by a blood-curdling scream.

"Ah!" A second shadow came rolling down the hill, crashing into the dirt below.

Carson squinted, catching a flash of fire from the trees. It was the muzzle flare from the enemy's gun.

Gunfire erupted from the woods, the sound of bullets tearing through the night. The glow of gun flashes pierced the darkness like lightning strikes.

Then, an earth-shaking explosion rocked the hillside. A massive fireball erupted from the trees, sending debris flying like hail in a storm.

The team fell into a tense silence, the crackling flames the only sound.

William emerged from the trees, rifle in hand, his figure framed by the smoke. He waved over to Carson.

"Carson, bring your team up, but stay alert!" William's voice crackled through the radio.

With a surge of excitement, the men sprang into motion, climbing the slope with renewed urgency.

Marvin led his squad in as well, quickly arriving at the body of one of the fallen ambushers.

William was already searching through the man's gear.

The soldier was a beast of a man, his ID tag marked with symbols from Risun.

William's single bullet had blown the man's head apart.

The front of his face was still recognizable, but the back was nothing but a bloody mess.

Marvin quickly scanned the surroundings through his night-vision goggles, making sure no enemies were lurking. After confirming the area was clear, he moved to inspect another body.

The enemy wore high-tech combat gear. They were light, tough, and breathable, equivalent to Silver Shield Security's finest.

And their weapons were top-of-the-line military hardware, like assault rifles, rocket launchers, and sniper rifles. Whoever had arranged this attack had a serious network behind them.

Marvin's lips twisted in disgust. "Sakra Society... So, it's Yinson's people, huh? Didn't think that old bastard would have the guts to pull a stunt like this."

William sneered, his face twisted with amusement, "They're making a grave mistake. Whoever they send, I'll drop them. All of them."

Marvin narrowed his eyes, a dangerous gleam in them. "It's not just Sakra Society we need to worry about. These weapons didn't just appear out of thin air. Someone in Loscity is in bed with Yinson. That's how they're getting this kind of firepower."

William chuckled, his grin icy, "Then, we'll find out who. And when we do... we'll wipe them off the map."

The two of them gestured for their men to load the bodies into the vehicles.

"Thanks for saving us, William," Carson said, offering a respectful salute.

William patted his shoulder, sighing, "Don't mention it... but it's a damn shame we lost some good men."

Carson's voice was tight with anger. "Who the hell are they? Who wants us dead?"

"Yeah, ambushing us like this on a highway? They're playing with fire!"

"We'll make them pay!"

The whole group was boiling with rage now, ready to tear into anyone who dared oppose them.

Marvin's voice rang out, cutting through the noise. "Quiet!"

The men immediately fell silent, the tension in the air thick enough to cut with a knife.

Marvin's eyes were hard as steel as he looked at each man. "Someone wants to take down Ultimate Alliance. They don't want us to have peace. So, are we going to let them get away with it?"

"No!"

"Exactly! Anyone who dares cross us, we'll take them down!"

The men shouted in unison, their voices a solid wall of defiance.

As elite members of Ultimate Alliance, they were no strangers to danger. Their skills and dedication earned them not just a hefty salary but also unparalleled benefits and compensation for their families.

The idea of abandoning all that to scrape by in hardship was unthinkable.

"Good! We're not cowards. They have guns, but so do we. Ambush us? Big mistake. They all ended up dead anyway!" Marvin lifted his assault rifle high with a defiant gesture that was a symbol of their unyielding resolve.

The team also raised their weapons, cheering as one. Their morale was higher than ever.

"Boss will make sure the families of our fallen brothers are well taken care of. No one will be left behind!" Marvin added his words like fire, pushing the team into a frenzy.

William cast a strange look at Marvin. Usually, the guy came across as rough and carefree, but in this moment, he knew exactly how to lead.
