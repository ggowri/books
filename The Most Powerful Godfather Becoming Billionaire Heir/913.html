Chapter 913 Evil Plan

Alexander sneered coldly. He hadn't expected to see that shameless woman here. And not only was Elaine present, but she seemed to be running the show.

Elaine's dazzling smile lit up her delicate features as she addressed the room with unrestrained enthusiasm.

Focusing his force, Alexander tuned into their conversation. The voices in the room came through crystal clear.

One of the researchers practically beamed as he spoke.

"Dr. Bell, this is a breakthrough! With our latest progress, the launch date for the Apocalypse Project can be moved up again!"

Elaine nodded, her satisfaction evident.

"Fantastic. Eddie is already gathering forces on the outskirts of Loscity. He has a zombie army of over a hundred, ready to strike at any moment!

Her voice brimmed with excitement as she continued. "Once Eddie makes his move, we'll collect even more data. That will bring us closer to unlocking true immortality."

Someone in the room let out a derisive laugh.

"If we can destroy Loscity in the process, even better. Let them see who they're messing with!

"We are destined to rule the world. We are the most superior race, and soon, all of humanity will kneel before us!

"And we," Elaine declared, her voice rising to a crescendo, "will live forever!"

She raised her arms dramatically, like a charismatic leader rallying her troops. Her fervor was contagious, and the researchers cheered wildly in response.

Hidden in the shadows, Alexander clenched his fists. These people were deranged.

His suspicions had been correct. Sakra Society was planning to unleash chaos in Loscity. A bioweapon attack wasn't just on the table. It was imminent.

Alexander's mind raced. If he could take out their research team, the Apocalypse Project would be dead in the water.

From there, he'd hunt down every living dead they'd created and snuff out this nightmare before it truly began.

A researcher interrupted his thoughts. "Dr. Bell, should we test the new immortality serum? Let's see if it works as expected."

Elaine showed a self-satisfied smile.

"I've already conducted the tests, and this new immortality serum behaves very differently from the earlier versions.

"Previously, anyone injected would immediately transform into one of the living dead. But this time, our monitoring revealed some fascinating anomalies.

"Look here!"

She gestured toward a screen nearby, her excitement spilling over.

"The subject's brainwaves are heightened, incredibly active. There's even a hint, just a hint of consciousness."

Gasps filled the room, followed by an eruption of applause.

Elaine, however, raised a hand to temper their enthusiasm. "Unfortunately, the subject eventually lost that spark of awareness. It reverted to a typical mindless undead state.

"But this anomaly proves we're on the right track. Give us three more months, and we'll have a perfected immortality serum.

"This will be the most groundbreaking discovery in human history.

"And all of us, each and every one of you, will go down in the annals of greatness!"

The researchers cheered, their faces alight with zeal. Satisfied with their energy, Elaine clasped her hands together.

"You've worked hard. Keep at it. I have other pressing matters to attend to. But soon, very soon, the Apocalypse Project will come to fruition.

"We, the Risun people, are destined to reign supreme. The world will bow before us!"

The researchers roared their agreement, thrilled at the thought of restoring their country's former glory.

"Leave it to us, Dr. Bell!"

With a final wave, Elaine strutted out of the room, her movements oozing confidence and pride.

From his hiding spot, Alexander's gaze turned icy. His rage simmered beneath the surface, threatening to boil over.

"These maniacs!" he thought, grinding his teeth.

"Human experiments, immortality serums, world domination? They're out of their minds."

Alexander's eyes sharpened, his fury simmering beneath a calculated calm. He knew he couldn't let their twisted plans come to fruition.

The urge to take Elaine down right then and there was almost unbearable, but he restrained himself and stared at those researchers.

Just then, a burly man with a smug grin stood up.

"I'll grab a fresh subject for our next test."

Alexander's eyes narrowed as he silently trailed the man.

Now wasn't the time to act impulsively. He needed to find Hazel first. Only then could he dismantle their operation.
