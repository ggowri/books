Chapter 398 At a Loss

Wyatt, leader of The Blood Gang, was one of the gang's strongest fighters.

Alexander's sharp gaze fell on Wyatt, mildly surprised; Wyatt was indeed a skilled fighter.

To be exact, Wyatt's abilities likely surpassed even those of Joseph and Houtte, putting him among the best Alexander had encountered.

However, Alexander was already an Intermediate Warrior, putting Wyatt far below him and not even in the same league.

"Interesting, didn't expect to come across someone with this skill level," Alexander said.

Alexander flashed a slight smile.

Mary became increasingly nervous at the sight.

"It's over, we're really in trouble...now what?" she murmured.

She knew Alexander could fight, but Wyatt had both the numbers and was a true combat champion.

Mary was at a loss, watching Alexander with a mix of worry and disbelief.

She saw him calmly sipping his drink and even taking a bite of a snack.

Of all times, how could he still have the nerve to eat and drink?

Mary was anxious, almost wanting to jump up.

"Wyatt's right here, and you're eating? Are you insane?" she whispered.

Alexander replied nonchalantly, "Well, it's your fault; I haven't even had breakfast yet."

Mary wasn't the only one speechless; everyone else was too.

Eugene began whining to Wyatt.

"Dad, Alexander just killed Lawrence using some dirty trick, then said he'd teach me a lesson and that The Blood Gang was nothing..."

Eugene embellished his complaint as he glanced over at Alexander, who just kept eating and drinking without paying them any mind.

Wyatt's expression darkened as he listened to his son's account.

Lawrence was a representative of the Mafia Godfather, and he'd died right there in The Blood Gang's meeting. How was Wyatt supposed to explain this to Satan?

Alexander kept eating and drinking as he arrived. It was obvious he didn't take Wyatt seriously. If word of this got out, it would be an utter humiliation.

Furious, Wyatt ordered, leaving no room for negotiation, "Alexander, I'll give you a chance! Kneel down and bow three times, hand over the magic powder formula, and I might consider letting you go."

The tension spiked instantly as every eye turned to Alexander.

Alexander, though the target of all the attention, continued eating and drinking as if nothing had happened.

"Damn it, Alexander's really arrogant; that's our gang leader he's ignoring!" someone murmured.

"Did Alexander lose his mind? He's just eating," said another.

"Maybe he thinks he's done for and wants one last meal," someone speculated.

The crowd buzzed with discussion.

"Showing off in front of me, huh?" Wyatt huffed, displeased.

He waved to the four men in black beside him.

"Make him kneel!"

"Yes, boss!"

The four men closed in on Alexander, their expressions vicious, like hyenas about to attack prey.

Mary quickly called out, "Watch out!"

The lead man swung a brutal punch at Alexander's face, and if it hit, his nose would surely break.

Alexander finally put down his glass and caught the man's fist in his hand.

"A mere lackey dares to strike at me?" he said coldly.

His icy voice seemed to carry a hypnotic power, echoing in everyone's ears, leaving their minds reeling.

In an instant, the other three reached Alexander, one of them pulling out a machete while another brandished a dagger, both swinging at him forcefully.

Alexander unleashed his Force and tossed them aside with a flick.

The man in black went flying sideways, slamming into his companions like a bowling ball and sending them crashing, screaming in pain.

With just one move, Wyatt's four followers were down for the count.

Everyone was stunned as every one of Alexander's moves delivered an intense shock.

Mary was nearly trembling with excitement, her chest heaving intensely, catching Alexander's eye for a moment.

Seeing Alexander's gaze linger on her chest, Mary blushed, feeling a rush of warmth.

Watching Alexander exchange glances with Mary, Wyatt felt utterly humiliated, and his fury rose even higher!
