Chapter 185 The Lair

Marvin quickly chimed in, "Boss, if you take us with you, we won't slow you down, I promise."

A warm feeling washed over Alexander; it was good to know he had such loyal buddies by his side.

"Alright, we'll go together. The rest of you listen to Betty's orders. William will stay here to keep watch and prevent any surprises."

Just then, Logan's eyelids fluttered, and his fingers twitched slightly.

Before long, he opened his eyes, and the first face he saw was Alexander's.

"Boss, I thought I was a goner... but I'm still here... thank you for saving me..."

A faint smile broke across Logan's pale face.

The room erupted in excitement, and Alexander couldn't help but beam back at Logan.

But then Logan's expression darkened, a fierce glint flashing in his eyes.

"Boss, I didn't let them take the magic powder. I swear I'll make them pay for this!"

"Easy there, Logan. You're hurt and need to rest. The Fraternity's mess is my responsibility now. Just focus on getting better."

His gaze was filled with resolve; vengeance could only be cleansed with the blood of their enemies.

"Thanks, Boss. Just be careful out there," Logan said, managing a smile.

He thought to himself, "Messing with the Ultimate Alliance? They're asking for trouble. Those fools from the Fraternity don't know what they're up against."

After chatting for a bit, Logan's exhaustion caught up with him, and he drifted back into a deep sleep.

Alexander instructed the others to keep the room secure before stepping out of the hospital ward.

"Boss, I've gathered all the intel on the Fraternity. I'm sending it over now," Augusta said, pulling up a document on his device.

"Great, let's take a look."

Alexander opened the file and quickly scanned the contents.

He knew that even a lion used all its strength to catch a rabbit; he couldn't afford to underestimate his opponents. Understanding the enemy was half the battle, especially since the Fraternity was a major player in Loscity.

Their leader, Ian Oldman, was regarded as a formidable figure in many circles.

Alexander was determined to strike hard and uproot the Fraternity, leaving no chance for retaliation.

The document spanned dozens of pages, but Alexander's sharp mind absorbed the information effortlessly.

He was surprised at how quickly he could recall everything, the key points flashing vividly in his mind.

As his power grew, Alexander's abilities as a Warrior expanded, including an almost photographic memory. He was brimming with anticipation for the future.

"Boss, is there something wrong with the intel? You finished that pretty fast," Augusta asked, a hint of confusion in his voice.

"Yeah, all good. We'll hit the Fraternity's hideout tonight at the Magic Bar and take them down along with Ian," Alexander replied decisively.

"Let's go in and wipe them out completely!"

Augusta and Marvin were practically buzzing with anticipation.

...

As the clock struck ten, the Magic Bar was buzzing with activity, the peak of its nightlife.

People streamed in and out, a mix of men and women, the atmosphere thick with excitement and a haze of smoke.

The pounding music rattled eardrums, overwhelming anyone not accustomed to the chaos.

Colorful laser lights danced across the room, illuminating the crowd as they waved glow sticks, lost in the rhythm of the night.

Alexander noticed that nearly everyone had a blue drink in hand, which was undoubtedly spiked with magic powder.

The magic powder undeniably provided an intense rush.

The three of them found a vacant table, ordered a few bottles, and settled in to observe their surroundings.

The Ultimate Alliance had mobilized fifty members, who were discreetly surrounding both entrances of the bar, waiting for Alexander's signal to close in and trap the Fraternity members inside.

Augusta leaned in, his expression serious. "Boss, even though Ian comes here regularly, do you think he might skip out tonight after they just attacked our headquarters?"

Alexander gave a composed smile. "If Ian truly respected us, he wouldn't have dared to storm our headquarters."

Marvin fumed, saying, "We must dismantle the Fraternity! Let everyone in Loscity understand that touching our magic powder comes at a steep price—their lives!"

"Take it easy, there's still plenty of time," Alexander assured, confident in his prediction.

He mused, "Once a habit is ingrained, it's hard to break. Ian will come.

The Fraternity disregards both me and the Ultimate Alliance so blatantly.

If they didn't, they wouldn't have dared to amass over a hundred goons for such a grand assault."
