Chapter 189 Surrender

Ian gritted his teeth, forcing himself to steady his nerves.

"You're strong, I'll give you that. But this is the era of technology. No matter how tough you are, you can’t escape a bullet."

Before the words even left his mouth, Ian pulled out his gun and fired.

Ian shot twice, and with only about 6 feet separating them, he couldn't believe Alexander would survive this.

Augusta and Marvin felt a chill run down their spines. They hadn't expected Ian to have a gun, let alone to use it in a blink of eye.

In such close a distance, no one could dodge a bullet. After all, this wasn't some action movie.

A smug grin spread across Ian's face as he pulled the trigger.

But in an instant, Alexander moved like a ghost, effortlessly dodging the bullets and seizing Ian's wrist in a vice-like grip.

With a sickening crack, Ian's gun clattered to the ground as he screamed in agony.

"No way! How did you do that?" he gasped, clutching his hand in sharp pain.

Alexander yanked and dragged him like a rag doll toward the door.

"From now on, there will be no Fraternity in Loscity!"

"Please! Don't kill me... I give up, I surrender! I'll do anything, just don't kill me!" Ian begged in terror.

The Fraternity members in the hallway stood frozen, looking at their boss—who had always been aggressive—now crying like a child. It was a shocking sight.

"You want to surrender?" Alexander asked, a teasing smile on his face.

"Yes, yes! I'll surrender! Whatever you want, I'll do it! Just don't kill me, please!"

Ian sounded like a scared child caught doing something wrong.

Lillian, unconvinced by Alexander’s power, shouted, "Ian, don't be afraid of him!

"We're all here; he wouldn't dare do anything to you! He can’t afford to be sent to jail!"

Ian’s anger flared and shouted at her,

"Shut up, you foolish woman!"

But Lillian continued, "I'm calling the cops! I'll..."

"Shut up, or I'll have someone take you out!" Ian bellowed.

Lillian flinched.

Turning back to Alexander, Ian pleaded, "Please, don't kill me! I'll do whatever you want.

"I have men, money, territory—I'm useful to you!"

Alexander paused, contemplating. If Ian was willing to cooperate, he could use the Force to control him.

This would allow him to secretly absorb the entire Fraternity, benefiting his larger plans.

Augusta interjected, "Boss, he's definitely trying to play us. He's just stalling for time. We should take him out."

Marvin glared at Ian, ready to pounce at Alexander's command.

But instead, Alexander picked up Ian's gun, pointing the dark muzzle at Ian's forehead with an amused expression.

"Now, relax and swear to serve me faithfully from now on. You won't live if you betray me!"

Cold sweat dripped down Ian's forehead as he nodded frantically.

"Okay, okay! I swear! I'll do it right now!"

He took a few deep breaths, trying to calm himself down.

With a gentle pat on Ian's shoulder, Alexander subtly infused the Force into Ian's mind.

"I, Ian, in front of everyone here, swear to serve you faithfully..." Ian began earnestly.

As he spoke, the Force surged within his brain, subtly shifting his mental state.

Ian had no resistance in mind, and with Alexander's recent substantial improvements in strength, the manipulation went well.

When Ian began his oath, he was initially dismissive, just as Augusta had predicted, only wanting to get through this ordeal and then find a way to exact his revenge on Alexander.

By the time the oath was complete, however, Ian's mind was completely under the control of the Force.

He looked at Alexander with great sincerity. "Boss, I'm sorry for everything. From today on, I'll atone for my mistakes!"

Augusta and Marvin were stunned, exchanging bewildered glances. "He's serious?" they wondered.

The Fraternity members were also confused. They didn’t understand how did Alexander became their own boss in a instant.

Yet, some of them couldn't suppress their excitement, for the generous benefits promised by the Ultimate Alliance had long been the subject of their envy.
