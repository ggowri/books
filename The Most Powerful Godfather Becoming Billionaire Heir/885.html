Chapter 885 Brothers

Marvin and Shadow both froze, their eyes locked on the darkened area ahead.

Out of nowhere, two hulking men appeared, looking as savage as barbarians.

Their similar square faces were etched with cold, murderous intent.

The two men stood still, glaring menacingly at Alexander.

Using the Force-enhanced vision, Alexander could clearly see the thick black aura surrounding the men—pure negative energy.

Such beings, fueled by negative energy, usually possessed supernatural powers.

Alexander said calmly, "Leave these two to me. You go ahead—I'll catch up later."

Marvin and Shadow were strong, but against such unusual foes, they might only be a liability.

"Got it!"

Without hesitation, Marvin and Shadow pressed forward.

They knew those two men were trouble and didn't want to hold their leader back.

The older of the two men sneered wickedly, "It's a good thing we got here quickly! If we'd waited for the captain, these fools would've already broken through.

"I've heard Alexander is strong, but how strong? Are you going, or should I, or do we both go at once?"

"Don't underestimate him. They say even the sorcerers from the City of Sorcery have fallen to him."

The younger brother smirked sinisterly, his expression like that of a venomous snake.

"Hah! Sorcerers are nothing compared to the ghost arts of Risun!"

"Alexander, ending up in my hands is just bad luck for you," Cyrus growled.

Black energy surged around Cyrus as he stomped on the ground with immense force.

Boom!

The ground shook violently, as if an explosion had gone off.

Countless shards of stone, coated in eerie black energy, shot out in all directions.

The sound of destruction echoed as nearby cars were riddled with holes, none left intact.

Dozens of these shards sped toward Alexander, faster than bullets.

Yet Alexander didn't flinch. An invisible wall of energy stopped every shard, sending them clattering uselessly to the ground.

Suddenly, a sharp gust of wind whooshed through the air.

Cyrus had ripped a lamppost out of the ground and swung it toward Alexander.

His strength was terrifying.

Bang!

With a deafening crash, the lamppost missed its mark as Alexander suddenly vanished.

When Alexander reappeared, he was behind Cyrus, holding a gleaming kukri dagger.

The blade flashed!

It struck Cyrus' chest, but no blood emerged.

The dagger was caught by Cyrus' muscles, failing to penetrate his bones or organs.

Black energy surged across Cyrus' chest, his muscles and bones harder than steel.

Even though Alexander had swung casually, the fact that he couldn't cut through Cyrus' body showed how powerful he was.

"Damn you! How dare you injure me—I'm the heir to the ghost arts!" Cyrus roared.

Cyrus bellowed in rage but retreated quickly, a glimmer of fear flashing in his eyes.

Alexander hadn't managed to cut him open, but Cyrus was still thoroughly shaken.

"Heh, running pretty fast there," Alexander said with a cold smile.

With a smirk of disdain, Alexander prepared to chase him down and finish him off.

"Stop right there!"

Edmund, the younger brother, saw the situation turning against them and immediately started moving his hands rapidly.

Dozens of black throwing knives shot toward Alexander, tearing through the air with an ear-piercing whine.

Alexander vanished again as the knives buried themselves into a concrete wall, turning it into a honeycomb-like structure that wobbled precariously.

Cyrus and Edmund exchanged uneasy looks, regretting for the first time that they had taken the Mafia's lucrative contract.

This Alexander was not someone to mess with!

Edmund shouted in alarm, "Watch out!"

But it was too late. Alexander suddenly reappeared behind Cyrus.

He delivered a powerful punch to Cyrus' lower back.

The force of the blow sent Cyrus' massive body flying like a cannonball, slamming into a wall and bringing it down in a cloud of dust and debris.
