Chapter 28 The Ball

Alexander returned to immerse himself in meditation to enhance the Force.

As the hours ticked by, the evening dance drew nearer.

The entire upper echelon of Loscity buzzed with excitement, each member preparing to attend the ball.

First, everyone was eager to witness if Ryan would indeed become the vice president.

Second, they all sought an opportunity to meet and ingratiate the enigmatic new chairman.

Alexander's phone alarm sounded.

He glanced at the time, noting the ball would commence in an hour, and he immediately felt a surge of energy.

He showered, donned the attire Ava had chosen for him, and prepared to depart.

Anne had the car ready and kept waiting outside.

Once Alexander was settled, Anne promptly handed him an exquisite wooden box.

"Mr. Hawthorne, this is a custom-made mask for you," she said.

Alexander opened the box to reveal an intricate silver mask. Its surface was smooth and emanated a soft glow.

He placed it on, instantly exuding an air of mystery that left Anne in awe.

"Let's go!" Alexander commanded.

Anne's car raced towards the Saint Tower.

The Saint Group occupied the top floor of the 108-story Saint Tower.

As evening descended, the parking lot teemed with luxury vehicles.

Men and women in dazzling attire formed a long queue at the entrance.

Security for the ball was tight; no one without an invitation was granted entry.

"Alright, I'll go in alone," Alexander declared.

Stowing the mask away, he exited the car and approached the building.

Ava stood by the steps, resplendent in her finest attire.

Her beautiful face and alluring figure, accentuated by her evening gown, radiated an aura of nobility and elegance akin to a goddess.

Alexander was momentarily spellbound.

"It's not our first meeting. Why the astonishment?"

Ava blushed, playfully rolling her eyes at him.

A smile graced Alexander's face. "Sorry, I'm a bit late."

Ava admired his suit, clearly pleased.

"You look quite dashing. You should dress up more often and not always so casually," she remarked.

"Ms. Blackburn, I am honored to be your dance partner tonight," Alexander said.

He extended his arm with gentlemanly grace.

Ava smiled, taking his arm, and together they walked towards the Saint Tower.

With their striking appearances, they drew the eyes of countless admirers.

Those familiar with Ava were shocked, speculating about Alexander's identity and assuming he must be the scion of a prominent family.

Otherwise, with Ava's status as the president of the Saint Group and her aloof nature, she would never show interest in an ordinary man.

Faced with the curious and eager gazes of the crowd, Ava remained aloof, distancing herself from the crowd.

It was Alexander's first time attending such a grand ball. His first time under the scrutiny of so many complex stares made him feel a twinge of nervousness.

"Don't worry. Just relax.

"From today on, everyone here will vie for your favor; you're at the pinnacle."

Ava whispered reassuringly.

Alexander couldn't help but chuckle. "So, am I officially a loverboy?"

"Judging by your current appearance, it's too soon for you to think of living off women!"

Ava playfully pinched his waist.

Once they entered the venue, Ava offered Alexander a few words of advice before seeking out Anne.

To this moment, Ava remained unaware if the new chairman would grace the ball with his presence, a fact that irked her.

Alexander spotted Ryan, seated in a wheelchair, being pushed in arrogantly by Jane.

Despite his inability to walk, Ryan was impeccably dressed and carried an air of pride.

As Alexander looked their way, they also noticed him. They looked shocked.

"What are you doing here? Who let you in?" Ryan demanded, his voice laced with anger.

He had yet to seek revenge for his broken legs, and here was Alexander, brazenly appearing before him.

"Security! Security! You must have sneaked in. You definitely don't have an invitation. How did this happen?"

Jane called out imperiously as if she were the hostess of the ball.

Alexander sneered in response, "If even you two can gain entry, why wouldn't I be allowed?"
