Osborne caught every word of Bradley's taunt, a flicker of realization igniting in his mind.

He thought, "Number Six Elixir? Made from the blood of a Vampire deity? The King of the Aberrants got access to a Vampire god's corpse, so he's cloning Dukes of Blood Clan. That means the Number Six Elixir was extracted from the blood of that very corpse!"

His expression turned serious as these thoughts raced through him.

This theory seemed spot on. Blood Cross must have sensed the divine blood within the elixir, which was why it had reacted so suddenly.

But this had ended up working in Osborne's favor.

With Blood Cross absorbing all the Vampire god's blood from the Number Six Elixir, the elixir's power had diminished significantly, leaving Bradley's strength back at square one.

Faced with a weakened opponent, Osborne felt a rush of confidence.

He scoffed, saying, "Relying on some potion for strength? That's just plain unreliable! Now that your little drug has worn off, how do you plan to keep up with me?"

With that, Osborne charged forward.

Bradley's expression shifted from arrogant confidence to shock. Without the effects of the elixir, not only had his power faded back to normal, but his speed had taken a hit as well.

There was no dodging Osborne's attack.

All he could do was raise his arms in a desperate attempt to block.

Osborne's overwhelming force shattered Bradley's defenses, exploding within him.

This time, without the divine power shielding his insides, Bradley took a brutal hit, blood spilling from his mouth.

With a single strike, Osborne had seriously injured him.

Yet, Bradley was far from finished. Even with the effects of the elixir gone, he was tough enough to keep fighting. Shifting his strategy, he decided not to engage in a brawl anymore.

Instead, Bradley resorted to every trick in the book to try to entangle Osborne.

"Ha! You think you can drain my energy? In your dreams! You're the one who's going down!" Osborne roared, unleashing the full force of his strength into Bradley's frame.

In an instant, he sent Bradley flying across the arena.

As Osborne charged at him, Bradley gritted his teeth and summoned every ounce of strength to propel himself away from the ring.

He knew he had lost.

But he also realized that Osborne wouldn't let him off easily; Osborne would relish tormenting and humiliating him. Faced with that fate, Bradley thought it wiser to concede defeat and leave the arena on his own terms.

Osborne halted his advance and smirked at Bradley. "Nice escape!

"But weren't you shouting about revenge for Felicity? Weren't you going to torture and humiliate me? And yet here you are, fleeing the arena like a scared dog?

"The third disciple of the King of the Aberrants?

"It's laughable!"

Osborne's disdainful comments ignited fury in Bradley, but he couldn't muster a retort, as it was undeniable that he had lost.

All he could manage was a cold huff. "Osborne, enjoy your little victory while it lasts! You've burned through most of your energy. You're barely at thirty percent of your peak power!

"Even if you do win against me, Marlon will wipe the floor with you.

"The moment this Honor Quest Ceremony wraps up, you're finished! I'll find my opportunity to humiliate you, and you'll wish you had never been born."

With that, Bradley turned on his heel and made his exit.

Osborne snorted but didn't pursue.

His own Honor Quest Ceremony wasn't over yet.

He still had one last challenger—Marlon.

As he turned his gaze towards Marlon, Osborne's expression grew grave.

Bradley had been right; he was only at thirty percent of his full strength now.

He thought to himself, "I've still got a good ten minutes before I can use Light of Redemption again. Can I really take on Marlon in this state?"

Down below, the crowd watched Osborne, who was utterly worn out, scarcely believing he could pull off any miracles to defeat Marlon.

With a cold smirk, Marlon sauntered onto the arena; his gaze locked onto Osborne.

"Who would've thought you'd actually make it this far? If you manage to take me down, you'll clear the Honor Quest Ceremony, becoming the first from Rosmandi and the fourth in the world to do so. You'd be a legend!

"But standing in your way is me, Marlon!

"Right now, you're like a fly trapped on a glass window—so close to the light, yet utterly lost. Even if you were at your peak, you wouldn't stand a chance against me. And let's not even talk about the fact that you're limping along at a measly thirty percent of your strength."

Marlon's disdain was palpable as he scrutinized Osborne.

"Honestly, in this state, why bother struggling? It'd be smarter to just admit defeat."

Osborne replied with a cold smile, "Defeat? You think you're worthy of that? Even at thirty percent, I can take you down! Just don't go crying like your sister did when I knocked her around!"

At the mention of his sister, Marlon's face contorted with rage.

The memory of Osborne humiliating her on the arena floor flooded back, igniting a firestorm of fury within him.

Marlon's eyes blazed with a murderous intent that could cut through steel.

"Osborne, you want to die? Fine, I'll be the one to grant you that wish!"

With a fierce determination, Marlon declared, "Now, let me show you just how terrifying the strongest of the younger generation can be!"

With that, he lunged forward, ready to unleash his power.
