Chapter 61

Charles’s eyes widened as soon as he spotted the yellow sapphire stone. Though blue sapphires were known to be the most expensive, yellow sapphires followed closely..

He turned to Natasha and asked, “If this stone contains yellow sapphire gems, how much do you think it might be worth?”

Natasha eyed him suspiciously and smiled. “At least three hundred million dollars,” she said. “But yellow sapphires are rare. I don’t even think they exist in this region.”

But Charles was certain the stone he was holding contained a sapphire gem. He picked it up and said firmly, “I choose this one.”

Natasha took it from him and examined it closely. Then she whispered, “Look, I wouldn’t advise you to take this kind of stone. I’ve been in this business for years, and I can tell this one’s empty.”

The stone was rough, its surface uneven with irregular patterns. Stones like this usually contained nothing, so Natasha was genuinely concerned.

Charles said confidently, “Yeah, I’ll take it.”

He was stubbornly fixated on it. Seeing she couldn’t convince him, Natasha decided to let him have his way. They walked back to the others in the outer room.

When Norman, Derek, and the others saw the stone in his hand and could identify stones, they immediately burst into laughter.

Norman frowned. “What is this? Wait—don’t tell me you plan to strike ruby or sapphire with that stone. It’s obvious you can’t even identify a real gem.”

Derek burst out laughing. “You are the stupidest man alive! That stone contains nothing!”

Charles shot him a glare. “Plenty of talk. Are you still in on the bet? What’s your wager?”

Norman folded his arms, smirking. “l will bet twenty million dollars this stone contains absolutely nothing. I’m certain about it.”

Derek jumped in eagerly. “Count me in. I will stake my own two million.”

Charles smirked. “You sure, Derek? Because I’m pretty sure that’s the last two million you have got.”

Derek snapped, “Shut up! I have got more. This is just what I have on me right now, and I’m staking it.”

Charles nodded slowly. “Alright then.”

Norman glanced at him with mock amusement. “What about you? What are you putting up for the bet? Have you even paid for the stone yet?”

Charles gulped, he stepped more forward. “No. I haven’t paid… and I don’t have any money with me to stake.”

They all burst out laughing, mocking him. “I knew it! He is just a poor man, All he know is bluffing!” someone jeered.

Derek chimed in, “I told you all, he is the most useless man I have ever seen, why do you even come here in the first place if you know you don’t have any money”.

Norman also broke-shame him, “Pretending to be cool won’t take you anywhere, you're supposed to be licking feet of people like us, that's how you are going to be somebody!”.

Charles just stayed mute and didn’t talk, as everyone was abusing him.

Finally, Norman leaned forward. “So… how are you going to bet when you can’t even pay?”

Charles straightened and said, with a strange calm, “My fingers. I will bet my fingers.”

Everyone froze. Gasps filled the room. “Is he crazy?!” someone shouted. “Betting fingers on a stone that probably contains nothing?!”

Natasha leaned in, alarmed. “Uhm… are you serious? I will give you the stone for free and even lend you two million for the bet!”

Charles shook his head. “Thanks, Ms. Natasha, but I don’t take money from a woman.” He said it with a confident grin, A bit of conscience bit him, because he had begged Lizypie for money before. But now he just wanted to look cool.

He repeated firmly, “My fingers.”

Norman groaned, spreading his hands. “Are you sure? I hope everyone is hearing this—I don’t want to hear that someone’s being bullied!”

Derek grinned wickedly. “With your fingers gone, I’m sure you won’t be attracting Ms. Serena again!”

Norman smirked, He thought about it, if Charles lost his fingers, he would have a big advantage of getting Serene. 

But Charles remained calm, certain he was right about the stone. “I am sure. My fingers,” he said again.

Then, with a flourish, Norman dropped his card into Natasha’s hand. “Withdraw twenty million dollars if I lose,” he said, his confidence absolute. He didn’t even doubt his own victory.

The betting was set. Natasha stepped forward and carefully brought out the stone cutter, the cold metal gleaming under the light. Everyone leaned in, eyes fixed on Charles, waiting eagerly.

Many were already certain this was the moment he would lose his fingers. 

 Derek leaned back, smirking. “It’s over for you, Charles,” he said, his voice dripping with mockery.

Charles didn’t flinch. He placed the stone firmly on the cutter, his hands steady. Natasha adjusted the machine, and the tension in the room thickened.

With a sharp inhale, she began the cut. The blade descended slowly, slicing through the rough surface. Everyone’s breath caught, hearts racing.

Just then, the blade hit something.

Natasha froze. There was something inside the stone. She immediately stopped the machine and grabbed a scraper, careful not to damage it as they usually would with all stones.

Everyone leaned in, their anticipation turning into disbelief. Now it was clear: this stone contained a gem. But many assumed it would be something cheap, maybe a $100,000 one at most.

As Natasha carefully scraped away the surface, a soft yellow light began to shimmer from within the stone, cutting across the room and striking everyone’s eyes. They squinted, shielding themselves instinctively.

Finally, Natasha lifted the gem out. Its shape was flawless, its figure unique, like a molten gold frozen mid-flow, glowing with its own inner light.”

She couldn’t hide her astonishment.

“It’s… it’s a yellow sapphire!” she exclaimed, her voice echoing across the room.
