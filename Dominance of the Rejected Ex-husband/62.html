Chapter 62

Natasha was still in a daze as she brought the stone out for everyone to see, and they were all shocked as well.

“What the heck… that’s a yellow sapphire!”

“That’s even more expensive than a blue ruby. How the hell did he get it right?!”

“Am I dreaming? This is really a sapphire!”

Derek and Norman stood frozen, too shocked even to speak. Derek had to step closer to examine it properly and sure enough, it was a yellow sapphire.

“How… how is this possible…?” Derek stammered, utterly confused. Most rough stones were empty or low grade; finding a sapphire or a ruby was almost unheard of. Yet Charles had been so confident he had bet his fingers. How could he be that sure?

Norman rose, gulping. He sniffed and stared at the gem; its glow and brilliance were undeniable. He couldn’t deny it. “How did you get it? This is impossible! What is happening here?!” he yelled.

Charles remained calm. “You lost the bet. I don’t have anything to explain to you.”

Norman slammed the table. “No! Absolutely not! Natasha, you and him planned this—you already knew what was inside and used it to scam us!”

Natasha shook her head. “I brought the stone out for everyone to see. You can see it’s rough, flawless, with no signs of cutting. How could I possibly know what’s inside? And this stone is rare, how would I even get it right?”

“Who knows?” Derek barked. “Maybe you cut a lot of stones one by one, until you found the sapphire. That’s why Charles was so sure, maybe you rigged it!”

Natasha glared at him, disgust twisting her face. “Are you making any sense to yourself?”

The time it would take to cut all the stones—it would be impossible to do it in just the few minutes they spent in the store section. 

Practically, Stone store owners didn’t bother checking the stones themselves. They bought most of them in expensive wholesale, and buyers expected reputable, flawless stones with no signs of prior cuts.

 If a store owner cut every stone looking for hidden gems and most of it came up empty, they could lose a fortune since stones that contain rubies or sapphires are extremely rare. 

Many people had tried it before and ended up ruined. It was safer to sell the stones as-is for a couple million and let buyers gamble on what was inside

Charles repeated, steady, “Well then—I won. I will take my reward.” He turned to Natasha.

Natasha slid Derek’s envelope across the table; the flap rustled, bills shifting inside. Derek lunged for it, panic stretching his face. “No—give me my money back! That’s all I have! Please!” His voice broke.

“You made the bet,” Charles said flatly. “You lost and must pay the price. So shut up.”

Derek’s hands trembled. “Please—”

Natasha didn’t listen to him and gave it to Charles.

 Without hesitation. She brought out a portable card terminal, took Norman’s card, and was about to swipe it. Norman’s face drained.

“Natasha, you can’t do that—” he began. “Return the card. Don’t do anything stupid.”

“You told me to withdraw twenty million if you lost,” she said, eyes hard. “You said it in front of everyone.”

Norman scrambled for an excuse. “I—I was bluffing. Come on, just give me the card back.”

Charles smiled, faint and dangerous. “Were you bluffing when you said you would cut off my fingers? You sounded sincere.”

Norman pressed his palms to the table, desperate. “Please, Natasha—don’t take it. If you return my money, I will forget everything you did today. I will make business smooth with you again. Please, just give it back, don’t remove anything.”

Natasha paused, this was a chance for her to redeem herself. Charles watched her, waiting what her decision would be. “No. This is a bet. You made the promise. I’m withdrawing twenty million.”

She swiped the card.

The terminal beeped, Tooon!, and the amount wiped away from Norman’s account instantly.

“No—!” Norman shouted. The twenty million had been money his father gave him to hand to a business partner. Though he was heir to the Consortium, Norman’s father had stopped giving him large sums of money.

Norman squandered money, lived lavishly, and had been warned once more that a mistake would cost him his inheritance. If he failed to deliver this transfer, his father would be furious and possibly remove him as heir.

“Natasha, please return my money now! I swear, if you fail to— I will make you regret this. I will smash your shop. I will ruin you,” Norman spat.

Natasha didn’t flinch. “Stop being pathetic. Embrace your loss.” Something in Charles’s calm had seeped into her, and gave her a lot of confidence.

Norman grew angrier.

He turned to his two big guards. “Get my money back. Now. Beat them. Crush them. Bring my money back.”

The two men stood like statues. They didn’t answer because they couldn’t move—Charles had already put them out of commission a little while before. One still clutched his groin, the other was holding his leg. 

“Are you two useless?” Norman barked. “I said go and collect my money back or I will sack you both!” But they still don't move.

This made him lunged at Natasha himself. Charles caught his wrist in a hurry and twisted it backwards.

“My hand, my hand!”

Norman howled, trying to let go but Charles was so strong, then suddenly he let him go and this made him fall hard

Natasha then threw the card at him. It hit his chest.

He held his wrist in pain and started swearing. “You dare touch me? I swear I will have my revenge. You will regret crossing me.”

He picked up his card.

  “Will you leave, or should I call the guard to throw you out?” Natasha told him

Norman frothed, sputtering curses as everyone began filming with their phones. “Do you know who I am? You will pay for this! I swear!”

Derek looked around, and could see everyone's eyes on them. It was so humiliating and embarrassing, and he leaned toward Norman “We should just leave for now. We will get back at them later.”

Even though Derek and Norman weren't friends before, the humiliating moments have made them one.

Norman pointed a shaking finger towards Charles and Natasha. “I will get back at you two, you will both go on your knees and apologize for what you both did!”

He barked as Derek dragged him out.
