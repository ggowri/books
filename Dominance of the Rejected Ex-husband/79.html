Chapter 79

All heads turned to see Ms. Janet walking toward them.

Ms. Janet was Mr. Vasilios’ daughter — the same woman whose Charles had surprised his fiancée with a portrait of his father back then.

As soon as she approached, everyone bowed to her.

“Ms. Janet, welcome,” the guard greeted respectfully.

Janet wore a frown. She had been observing everything and had witnessed it all.

Her voice was sharp as she demanded, “Hey, did you just try to beat him? You raised your hand to hit someone, for what reason?”

Norman chuckled and quickly chimed in, “Oh, Ms. Janet, they just wanted to teach him a lesson. He came here to see Mr. Vasilios without any appointment. And instead of explaining himself, he was being stubborn, even insulting the security guards.” He sprinkled his lies smoothly.

Janet’s frown deepened. She had seen everything and knew Norman was lying. She also respected Charles since the engagement party — she still remembered how he had impressed her.

Even though she knew the truth, she turned to the guard. “Can you tell me what happened?”

The head security guard immediately replied, “Yes, ma’am. He was so arrogant and wouldn’t take simple instructions. He tried to force his way in, so I had to teach him a lesson.”

Janet smiled faintly. “Really?”

“Yeah, he’s just a useless, foolish somebody. Please have him dragged out,” Norman added smugly. He didn’t know the history between them and kept bad-mouthing Charles.

Then he leaned toward Charles and whispered, “Finally, you’ve humiliated yourself. You should’ve left when you had the chance.”

Janet turned to the second security guard, who was standing idly by. “Tell me — what really happened here?”

Norman and the first guard shot the second one a dangerous glare. He immediately felt pressured, their eyes burning into him. He didn’t want to lie, but fear held him back.

Janet stepped closer. “You better talk now. I want everything and don’t you dare lie!”

He exhaled shakily and finally spoke. “Ma’am… Mr. Norman here was claiming that this man was just a random nobody and that we shouldn’t let him in. Dylan was only following his orders and tried to beat him up.”

Dylan was the bad guard name.

“So he wasn’t trying to force his way in or being arrogant?”

“No, ma’am. I’d even say Mr. Norman was the arrogant one,” the guard confessed fully.

Janet smirked, satisfied, That was exactly what she had seen. She had only wanted to hear it directly.

The first guard suddenly blurted out, “No, ma’am, he’s lying—”

SMACK!

Janet’s hand struck across his face. He held his cheek, stunned.

“Shut your mouth and stop lying! I saw everything. You’re following orders from someone who has no authority in this company. You’re a disgrace to security officers. In fact, people like you could let robbers in, just because you follow anyone who gives orders!”

Norman gulped. The guard trembled.

“I repeat — you are a disgrace! And for what you did, you’re fired!” Janet’s voice rang through the room.

Everyone went silent.

Mr. Vasilios had always made it clear: Janet’s words carried the same authority as his. Whatever she said was final.

The guard fell to his knees, pleading.

“No, please, ma’am! Don’t fire me! This job it’s the best-paying one I’ve ever seen. I won’t survive without it. Please, I beg you!”

Janet’s expression didn’t soften.

“Next time,” she said coldly, “you will learn to take your work seriously. In whatever job you get next,”

She raised a finger toward the exit.

“Now get out of here before I call the inner guards on you.”

“Oh no… no, please!” he cried again, his voice cracking, but her stare made him freeze. He knew it was over. But he kept pleading.

Janet turned to the second guard—the honest one.

“You,” she said, her tone shifting. “You displayed courage. You didn’t fear them, and you followed protocol. You didn’t let greed or pressure overcome you.”

The guard blinked, stunned.

“For that,” she continued, “you will be promoted to the inner guards. That means a higher salary and some extra benefits."

The man’s jaw dropped. “What! Really? Wow… thank you, ma’am! I—I’m just grateful. Thank you so much!”

His joy burst out uncontrollably; he couldn’t stop smiling.

The fired guard, standing by the door, froze in regret. His eyes filled with tears as the weight of his loss sank in.

Norman just stood there, speechless. He’d watched everything right in front of him. Once again, Charles had somehow turned the situation around—defeating him without lifting a hand.

Janet finally faced Norman.

“Mr. Norman,” she said firmly, “I respect you. I know how influential you are, and I value that. But I can see you’re in bad standing with Mr. Charles. That doesn’t give you the right to impose your will on the guards or twist company protocol.”

Her tone hardened.

“For that, please leave for now. Come back another day when you’ve calmed down.”

Norman’s face tightened.

Norman stiffened, his jaw twitching.

“No,” he said quickly, shaking his head. “I have to see your father, Ms. Vasilios. I need to discuss something important with him.”

Janet’s eyes narrowed.

“Then you shouldn’t have caused trouble in front of this house,” she replied sharply. “If you truly needed to see him, you should’ve acted with respect.”

Norman’s pride cracked. He lowered his tone.

“I’m sorry,” he said. “Please… forgive me.”

Janet crossed her arms. “Mr. Norman, I’m not the one you should be apologizing to.”

He blinked. “W-what?”

She pointed toward Charles, her gaze firm.

“It’s him. You insulted him, humiliated him, and almost had him beaten. Bow, and apologize. Do that and I will let you in.”

The air went still.

Norman’s throat bobbed as he swallowed.
