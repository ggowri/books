Chapter 165

They barely made it back to the hotel.

Charles stumbled through the corridor, he leaned heavily on Lizzypie and Charles as they dragged him. Every step sent a sharp throb through his bones. His vision swam and the carpet blurred beneath his feet. He was really beaten mercilessly. 

Lizzypie kicked the door open and they dragged him inside.

The moment the door shut, his legs gave out.

They hauled him into the bedroom and laid him flat on the bed. Charles groaned low. Pain pulsed everywhere, deep and heavy, like his bones had been struck with iron.

Lizzypie did not waste time. She pulled off his coat, then his shirt.

Purple bruises spread across his chest and ribs. Some were already dark. Others were swelling fast.

Sapphire sucked in a breath.

Her hands trembled as she stared at him. “I caused this,” she said, her voice cracking. “I caused all of this.”

Tears slid down her cheeks and dropped onto the sheets.

Lizzypie shot her a sharp look. “Do you want to cry,” she said, “or assist me here.”

Sapphire sniffled hard. She wiped her face with her sleeve and nodded, keeping her mouth shut as she moved closer.

Charles shifted and let out a strained groan. His bones ached in a way he had never felt before. 

The assassin’s punches had gone straight through him, like cannon fire wrapped in flesh.

Lizzypie pushed his pants down enough to check his legs, her hands quick and precise. More bruises. One knee is already swollen.

“Tch,” she muttered.

She straightened and turned away, moving to the corner of the room. She opened a small case Sapphire had not seen before and pulled out several wrapped bundles of herbs, dried roots, leaves and powdered bark.

Sapphire watched silently as Lizzypie worked. She moved in practiced motions, lighting a small burner, crushing herbs with a stone, measuring without hesitation. It was like she had done this a hundred times before.

Sapphire frowned through her tears. How did she even have all of this here?

The smell of bitter herbs filled the room as Lizzypie brewed the mixture. She poured it into a cup and brought it back to the bed.

Charles was barely conscious now, eyes half open, breath shallow.

Lizzypie lifted his head and pressed the cup to his lips. “Drink,” she said firmly.

He swallowed with difficulty.

The moment the tonic went down, warmth spread through his body. The pain dulled, not gone, but pushed far away.

Within seconds, he slipped into a deep sleep.

Lizzypie watched him closely, fingers on his wrist, eyes sharp. After a long moment, she nodded.

“He will be okay, ” she said. “He just needs rest.”

Sapphire let out a shaky breath and slumped into the chair beside the bed. She stayed close, watching Charles’s chest rise and fall.

Lizzypie wiped her hands and stood. “I have somewhere else to be.”

Sapphire looked up quickly. “You are leaving?”

“I won’t be long,” Lizzypie said. “But he will be out for a while, call me if anything strange happens.”

Sapphire nodded. “Okay. I will look after him.”

She hesitated, then bowed her head slightly. “Thank you. And… I’m sorry. For everything.”

Lizzypie paused at the door and glanced back. Her expression softened just a little.

“Don’t blame yourself,” she said. “Charles facing Mr. Damien has nothing to do with you.”

Sapphire frowned. “He followed back to Winchester which caused all this”

“He has his own mission,” Lizzypie cut in. “He wanted to kill Mr. Damien even before he knew you, that's the reason he was in Winchester in the first place ”

Sapphire looked lost, confusion mixing with guilt.

“He will explain when he wakes up,” Lizzypie said.

She opened the door. “Take care of him.”

With that, she left.

The room fell quiet.

Sapphire sat beside the bed, eyes fixed on Charles’s bruised face, listening to his steady breathing, waiting for him to wake.

Hours passed.

Sapphire had fallen asleep sitting beside the bed, her head resting near Charles’s arm. Her eyes snapped open when he groaned.

“Ughh…”

She jolted upright. “Gentle, gentle,” she said quickly, her hands hovering. “Are you okay?”

Charles pushed himself up. Too easily.

Sapphire froze. She stared as he swung his legs off the bed and stood like nothing had happened.  No pain in his face.

She reached for his chest, then his arms, then his ribs. Her fingers traced where the bruises had been. Where the swelling had risen and there was nothing.

Her breath caught. “They are gone."

“Wow,” she whispered. “How was Lizzypie able to do this? You were screaming of pain some moment ago”

Charles scoffed and replied. “She has ancient medical knowledge,” Charles said. “Stuff that works like magic”

Sapphire’s eyes softened. Relief flooded her face and before she could stop herself, she leaned forward and hugged him.

“I’m so sorry,” she said quietly.

Her breast pressed against his and Charles stiffened for half a second, then relaxed. He placed a hand on her back.

“It’s okay,” he said.

She pulled away quickly, embarrassed, then hesitated as questions rushed back into her eyes.

She looked at him closely. “What was all that?”

Charles raised an eyebrow.

“When you were fighting,” she continued, “I could hardly even see you. Both of you were moving so fast. It didn’t feel human. It was naturally impossible to do that.”

She swallowed. “And that blue thing that came out of his hand. That glowing power.”

Her voice dropped. “Was that magic?”

Charles studied her face for a moment. The fear was still there, mixed with awe. Charles exhaled and contemplated what to tell the Sapphire.

Sapphire added, “Also, why are you trying to kill Mr. Damien,  lizzypie told me you had your own mission!”.

Charles nodded. Since lizzypie has already given her some heads up, there was no reason to hide things from her. So he decided to tell her everything. 

Everything,  including his power and how it tied to finding a virgin.
