Chapter 15

Charles arrived at the location within thirty minutes, his heart pounding harder with each passing second. He prayed silently that Tracy hadn’t gone through with her madness.
The parking lot looked eerily calm. No smoke. No screaming crowd. No chaos. Only dim streetlights stretching shadows across rows of parked cars.
He narrowed his eyes, dragging his suitcase behind him, when he spotted a lone figure walking toward him. It was Zoe.
“Where is she?” Charles demanded, his voice clipped.
Zoe exhaled heavily, a strange flicker in her eyes. “Since you are here, I will leave you two.”
“What?” Charles frowned, bewildered. But before he could question further, Zoe turned her back and walked away without another word.
Charles stood frozen for a beat, suspicion crawling up his spine. Something didn’t add up. Why would Zoe just leave if Tracy was in danger?
Jaw clenched, he moved quickly toward the silver car Zoe had pointed out.
Through the tinted glass, he caught sight of her. Tracy. Sitting calmly in the driver’s seat, perfectly composed.
Not a drop of alcohol in sight.
Charles rapped his knuckles against the glass, anger surging. “Hey! What the hell are you doing, Tracy?”
Slowly, the door opened. Tracy stepped out with a calm smile,  almost triumphant.
“Finally,” she said softly, her eyes glinting in the pale light. “You came for me.”
Charles froze, his pulse hammering in his ears. She wasn’t drunk.
It hit him in a wave of bitter realization.
He had been played and deceived. Zoe had been acting. And Tracy had staged all of it, just to drag him here.
Charles’ fists clenched at his sides. “Unbelievable,” he muttered under his breath. “I should have seen this. There’s no way a big-shot CEO would pull something so reckless. And yet… here I am.”
Tracy tilted her head, her smile never faltering.
Charles’ eyes darkened as he stepped closer, his voice cold and sharp. “So, what’s all this, Tracy? Another one of your childish games?”
“You know faking an emergency is illegal, right?” he added.
Tracy only smiled, tilting her head with an air of playful assurance. “My emergency is that I wanted to see you.”
She watched him, fully expecting a look of gratitude or delight. Instead, he simply scoffed.  “Well, it looks like you are more than fine. I will be leaving now.” He turned to walk away.
“Wait,” she called out quickly. Her tone shifted, almost trembling. “I’m truly drunk… won’t you help me out?”
Before he could answer, she yanked out a half-empty bottle of liquor from the car and gulped it down recklessly.
Charles lunged forward, snatching it from her. “What the hell are you doing, Tracy?!” His voice cracked with anger. “Where’s your driver? You are the CEO of a company… when will you stop acting so damn recklessly?”
She leaned against the car door, smirking faintly. “You will be the one driving. You’re my husband.”
Charles’ jaw tightened. “Not again. No, Tracy. We are divorcing in two days.”
She stepped closer, her perfume wafting sharply into his nose. “That’s exactly what I’m saying. I’m still your wife till then.”
“You are so shameless,” Charles snapped. He turned on his heel. “I’m done here.”
Her voice lashed out behind him. “You know… I have men lining up for me. I can call any of them right now to come pick me up.”
He stopped, fists curling.
“Look,” she went on, pulling out her phone, scrolling quickly before shoving it toward him. “See these?”
Text after text lit the screen, messages from Derek. Begging to come get her. Calling her pen names. Babe, sweetheart and so on.
Charles’ stomach twisted. Was she trying to rub it in his face? Trying to make him jealous? Proving that he’d never be able to live without her? Because it wasn’t working.
“I don’t care, Tracy,” he said flatly. “You can be with whoever the hell you want.”
But then her expression shifted. Her smile fell. “Look… I know you are confused because of what you see in the video. But Francis was never mine.”
Charles stared at her, waiting for what she would say.
“I’m not his mother,” she said firmly, almost pleading. “That day, I said what I said only so Francis wouldn’t be left out with his classmates. He’s just a child, for God’s sake.”
He let out a cold laugh. “Unbelievable. All your stories you share seem personal. And I don’t even care if you’re the mother or not.”
“Don’t you get it?” she pressed, eyes glistening now. “I’m explaining things. I want to avoid this divorce… for you. How will you even live without me?”
“None of your business,” Charles said, his tone sharp as glass. “And I don’t care what explanation you spin. Nothing you say will erase what I saw in that hotel room. You even ordered condoms.”
Tracy’s voice cracked as she barked, “I told you, that was for a project! Why are you so gullible?”
Charles scoffed, shaking his head. She was talking in circles, saying sorry without apologizing, using pride as a shield. 
Even if she fell to her knees and begged, it wouldn’t matter anymore. Tracy had shown him where he stood in her life, beneath her pride, beneath her lies.
And yet, she wouldn’t let him go.
“I will introduce you to Francis properly next time, so you can both make things up and live together,” she said suddenly.
Charles scoffed. Tracy really wanted him to bind himself to the sin of his ex-lover.
Her audacity…she didn’t even regard Charles. The scene dissolved into something raw and messy.
“I have already made up my mind about the divorce,” Charles said, voice like ice. He turned and began walking away.
But before he could take three steps, her hand shot out, gripping his tightly.
“Charles. My patience is wearing thin here. I have been tolerating your…can't you see? You kept pushing and pushing. Don’t you see I value our marriage and didn’t want to let it break? Yet you don’t care! Look, if you leave tonight, it's over for us..finally over!”.
