The man turned, face twisting in surprise.

It was Derek.

Derek was just as stunned to see that it was Charles who had stopped him. He felt exposed, caught in the act of flirting with another woman.

The lady immediately spoke up, her voice sharp and angry. “Hell no! You were harassing me. You even tried to touch me!”

Charles glared at him. “It’s none of your business.”

Derek scoffed. “Obviously, you are clueless. This place isn’t for someone like you.”

Before Charles could respond, the lady stepped between them, straightening her posture. Her tone shifted to calm professionalism.

“Welcome to The Light Store. I’m Natasha, the owner. Please, how can I help you?”

Charles blinked in surprise. The woman Derek had just been harassing was the owner of the entire store.

Damn, that was something. That meant she was worth millions, in fact, hundreds of millions even. This business wasn’t child’s play. Natasha wasn’t just beautiful; she was powerful and rich. No wonder Derek had been drooling over her.

“Oh, you are the owner here,” Charles said. “I would like to do stone trading.”

Even though he had no money, he said it with quiet confidence.

Derek let out a mocking laugh. “You? Doing stone trading?” He folded his arms, still smirking. “You think this is some kind of game? What do you even know about stones?”

Charles’s jaw tightened. “It’s none of your business, Derek.”

Derek sneered. “You’ve been fooling around with Ms. Serena, haven’t you? Using her money for your little games. And now you want to gamble it away?” He chuckled darkly. “You will lose every damn cent.”

Before Charles could respond, Natasha spoke, her voice calm but firm. “Enough.” She turned to Charles, her eyes softening. “Have you ever done a blind stone cut before?”

Charles hesitated. “No.”

“Then I’d advise you not to,” she said. “Normally, I don’t care if people who know nothing about these stones want to throw their money away—but you seem decent. You helped me deal with this filthy man,” she added, casting a glare at Derek. “So take my advice. Don’t get into it.”

Charles gave a faint smile. He appreciated the warning, but a quiet confidence stirred inside him.

“Don’t worry,” he said calmly. “I know what I’m doing.”

Just as he finished, a gruff voice bellowed from behind him.

“Do you really know what you’re doing, dumb man?!”

They all turned. It was Norman, flanked by three hard-looking men.

heir of the Consortium Enterprises.
