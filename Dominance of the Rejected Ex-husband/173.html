Chapter 173

---
Reaper and Zander stood in shock, eyes wide at the glowing aura swirling around Razor's fists. This was their first time seeing real magic. Even their men froze, mouths open.
“That’s… magic,” Reaper muttered, voice trembling. “He uses magic. No wonder he can kill so many at once. He’s going to kill the boss too!”
Meanwhile, the fight began.
Razor charged first. He thrust his fist forward, and the aura tore through the air like a sharp blade.
Charles reacted on instinct. He twisted aside, just inches away as the aurora attack slammed into a parked car behind him.
BOOM!!!
The explosion was violent. The car burst into ashes in seconds. The shockwave ripped through the circle of men, forcing them to stagger back.
A low murmur spread through the crowd.
Razor smiled. Then, in a blink, he vanished, not literally, just too fast to track. One moment he was ten steps away; the next, he was already inside Charles’s guard.
Charles activated his Flash Power and lunged forward. His fists glowed, as his vision sharpened with X-ray sight locking onto Razor’s muscles and joints.
He aimed a punch at a weak point, he threw the punch but missed.
Razor slipped past every strike like smoke. Each blow hit only air. Charles felt him brush past his shoulder, his back, his side. Razor was impossibly fast.
SMACK!!!
Razor’s glowing fist smashed into Charles’s ribs. Charles flew backward, body crashing into the ground. He slid several meters before stopping. Concrete cracked beneath him.
Razor walked toward him slowly, full of confidence.
“You are even weaker than before,” he said calmly. “Stand up. Let us finish this.”
Charles coughed blood and pushed himself up. His legs shook, but he straightened his back. He closed his eyes.
[SOUL SIGHT activated]
As always, he glimpsed the future to predict Razor’s moves. But in that split second, Razor was already on him. A powerful kick sent Charles spinning through the air. A heavy punch followed, clean and brutal.
Another blow slammed into his chest, hurling him across the ground again.
Crack. 
Charles' ribs shifted inside him.
Reaper shouted in panic. Zander grabbed his arm.
“Someone needs to call RAIN,” Zander whispered urgently. “Or the Godfather. Now.”
Soul Sight ended. Charles rolled to his feet and snapped his eyes open.
This time, he moved first. Razor stepped in exactly as predicted. Charles ducked under the punch and drove his fist deep into Razor’s ribs.
Flash Power surged. Punch after punch landed, fast, precise, hitting every opening he had foreseen.
Razor staggered.
Charles pressed the attack. A hook. An elbow. A knee. Every strike connected cleanly.
“Yes! Master is winning!” Reaper grinned.
But it didn’t last. The five minutes of prediction ran out. Everything returned to normal.
Razor recovered and took control again.
His smile widened as he closed in. His fists glowed brighter.
He grabbed Charles by the collar and yanked him up. A knee smashed into Charles’s gut—hard.
Charles’s mouth flew open. Blood sprayed out as all air left his lungs.
Another punch crashed into his jaw, snapping his head sideways. Teeth cracked. More blood poured down his chin.
Razor didn’t stop.
Left hook to the face, Charles’s cheek split wide open.
Right uppercut to the ribs, his bones cracked louder this time.
Charles tried to raise his arms, but they fell limp. Razor flattened his nose with a final punch. Blood exploded across the ground.
Charles dropped to his knees. His body shook. Blood poured heavily from his mouth, nose, and swollen eyes. His shirt was soaked red. He couldn’t stand. Could barely breathe.
Razor turned to his men, arms spread wide, laughing.
“Look at this weak, delusional man!” he shouted. “He actually thought he could beat me—the almighty Razor, the number one assassin in this city!”
His men cheered wildly. “Master Razor! Finish him! Kill him now!”
Reaper and Zander stood frozen with their own men, faces pale with fear.
Reaper whispered, voice shaking, “We should run… After he finishes the boss, he will come for us next.”
Zander gripped his arm tighter. “You think we can run? It’s too late. Our fate is sealed. We’re dead men.”
Razor turned back to Charles, still kneeling, head hanging low.
He kicked Charles hard in the chest. Charles fell backward, coughing up more blood.
Razor stepped on Charles’s hand and ground his boot down. Bones snapped.
“YAAAA!” Charles groaned in agony.
“Pathetic,” Razor said, bending close. “When you get to hell, help me greet my grandma… and the two thousand others I’ve killed.”
He straightened up, ready to deliver the final blow.
At that moment, Charles slowly raised his head. Blood covered his face, but he smirked.
Perfect. This was the moment.
He closed his eyes deeply. 
[Hellfire Beam activated]
His eyes opened slowly, first normal, then glowing darker and darker red.
Razor frowned. “Huh? What’s that?”
Charles’s smirk grew wider.
“Something… that will make you BURNNNN!”
Instantly, a thick, blazing red laser beam shot straight from Charles’s eyes.
Razor reacted fast—he threw up both hands. Aura formed a glowing shield. The beam slammed into it. Sparks flew. The ground shook.
But Charles roared, “UGGGG!” and poured in more power. The beam grew wider, hotter, stronger.
The shield cracked.
Razor’s eyes widened in panic. He knew he couldn’t hold it. Energy draining fast, he tried to flash away—but he was too slow now. Charles had weakened him on purpose.
Too late.
The hellfire beam shattered the shield and struck Razor dead center in the chest.
“YAAAAA!” Razor screamed, voice raw with pain and terror.
The beam burned clean through. Blood exploded outward in a bright red spray—splashing the ground, the walls, his own men.
Skin and flesh melted away in seconds. A massive, smoking hole opened right through his chest—you could see straight through it.
Razor’s body flew backward, arms limp.
THUD.
He hit the ground hard, like dead weight. Eyes wide open. Lifeless.
Dead.
Silence fell over everything.
Only the sickening smell of burned flesh filled the air.
