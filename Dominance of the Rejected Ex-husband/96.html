Chapter 96

The garage fell into a dead silence, thick enough to choke on. Lizzypie’s crossed arms and narrowed eyes said it all—she knew Charles never made a move without proof.

Tracy, however, was boiling over. "What is this nonsense? I came here to buy stones, I was given stones, and now you're claiming they're empty?" she spat, her voice trembling with rage.

"Every last one of them," Charles stated, his voice cold and flat. "There isn't a single gem in this entire shipment."

A smug staff member stepped forward, folding his arms. "Who do you think you are, spouting nonsense like that? If you're not here to do business, get out!"

"No!" Tracy shrieked, jabbing a finger at Charles. "I don't know him! He's insane – just trying to ruin me! He can't stomach the thought of my success!"

Charles let out a short, harsh laugh. "For God's sake, Tracy, use your head for once. If you sell these, you won't just lose your money—you'll destroy your reputation. When your customers crack these open and find nothing, they'll never trust you again."

"Shut up! Just shut up, Charles!" she screamed, her face turning crimson. "Stop projecting your miserable failures onto me! You cling to women to get anything done, and now you're trying to drag me down with you out of spite! You divorced me over a stupid misunderstanding, and now instead of apologizing, you're trying to destroy my life!"

Lizzypie rolled her eyes. "It's always about you, isn't it? Even when he's trying to save your neck."

"Stay out of this, you whore! You're in this together! How could he possibly know they're empty without cutting them open? He can't! It's all lies!" Tracy yelled, her voice cracking.

That's when Wilson, the CEO, decided to step in, his voice slick and threatening. "Sir, I won't tolerate you slandering our business. Our stones are legitimate."

Charles turned his icy gaze on him. "I don't care. I'm not buying. Give me my money back. She can do what she wants." He jerked his head toward Tracy.

Wilson smirked, shoving his hands in his pockets. "I'm afraid you don't understand how this works. A deal is a deal. The money is ours."

A deep frown etched itself on Charles's face. "That's not a problem. I'm voiding the deal. Give. Me. My. Money."

"Don't make this difficult," Wilson sighed, the fake patience in his voice wearing thin. "Take your stones and leave."

A flicker of doubt finally crossed Tracy's mind. Any reputable company had a return policy. Charles hadn't even touched his shipment. Why was Wilson refusing?

Crack!

The sound echoed through the garage. Charles had snatched one of Tracy's stones and smashed it on the concrete floor. Nothing.

Wilson scoffed. "You expect every rock to have a gem? You clearly know nothing about this business."

"I know exactly what I'm doing," Charles said calmly. "I'll pay for every one I break." He picked up another.

Crack. Empty.

Crack. Empty.

He smashed ten stones in rapid succession. All empty. Tracy felt the blood drain from her face. He was right.

As he reached for the eleventh, Wilson's voice cut through the air. "Stop. Now. Get out."

"I said I'd pay for the broken ones. You have nothing to worry about." The truth was now undeniable. Ten duds in a row.

Suddenly, Wilson's demeanor shifted. The slick businessman vanished, replaced by a cold, cruel smirk. "Fine. You want the truth? They're all fake. Every last one. And there's not a damn thing you can do about it. Pack up this garbage and resell it. Maybe you'll get pennies back."

Tracy gasped, her hand flying to her mouth. Ten million dollars. Gone.

"Just give us our money, and we'll go," Charles said, his voice dangerously low.

Wilson laughed. "Go to hell. We only sell the real stuff to people who matter. We scam people like you for a living. And you can't touch us. Because we're the Titans."

On cue, his men pulled out guns, the metallic clicks echoing in the space. Muzzles pointed at them from every direction.

Lizzypie and Charles stood perfectly still, but Tracy began to shake uncontrollably.

"Pick up your worthless rocks and get out," Wilson snarled. "Or I'll have my men paint the walls with you."

Tracy, sobbing, started frantically waving at her truck driver to load the stones. But Charles didn't move a muscle.

He took a step forward. "You think this is how you do business? Let me make something clear. I'm not leaving without my money or the real stones I paid for."

"You think this is a game?" Wilson sneered.

BANG! BANG!

Two shots rang out, hitting the ceiling. Plaster dust rained down.

"The next ones go in your skulls. Get out," Wilson threatened.

"Charles!" Tracy wailed, tears streaming down her face. "Let's just go! We'll call the police! They're going to kill you!"

Charles acted as if he hadn't heard her.

"I'll leave you here! I swear I will! You're going to get yourself killed, and I won't care! Please, let's just go together!" she begged, her voice raw with terror.

Wilson chuckled. "Seems the little lady cares about you. You should listen to her."

Charles finally looked at Tracy. "Go. Don't worry. I'll get your money back."

"Are you insane?" she cried, her face a mess of tears and confusion. "Look around! They have guns! You don't have to prove anything to me! I know... I know you still love me, okay? I get it now! Please, don't throw your life away for this!"

Charles simply nodded. There she was again, making it all about her, about a love that had died long ago. Lizzypie couldn't hold back a derisive snort.

"Charles, please... don't be so childish, I don't want you to die," Tracy whispered, her whole body trembling.

Charles looked at her, she looked terrified and genuine. She was crying seriously.

With that, Charles did the unthinkable.
