Chapter 136

Mr. Jacob’s face was drained of color.

He was an old man. Raphael was his only son. The only heir of the Trisian bloodline. He had poured everything into that boy. Power. Influence and Connections. Even fragments of his own cultivation to push him higher. So he didn't have any idea Raphael did such a deal.

“You fool…” Mr. Jacob whispered under his breath as Raphael lay trembling on the stretcher. “How dare you do such a thing behind my back.”

His jaw tightened as he turned to Mr. Belly.

“You can’t do this,” he said, voice shaking. “You know what your sponsorship means to us.”

The Belly Family’s wealth was the sole pillar holding the Trisian family upright. For years they had relied on that steady river of money. They never built their own enterprise. Never secured independent influence. They lived off support and prestige.

If the funding stopped, collapse would not be immediate. But it would be inevitable.

Mr. Belly’s expression did not change.

“We had a deal with Raphael,” he said calmly. “A legal bind one. He was supposed to inform you. If he lost, which he did” Mr. Belly continued coldly, “our sponsorship ends. Permanently. And he will never approach my daughter again.”

.

Then Mr. Jacob snapped.

“No!” he roared, cane slamming against the arena floor. “We won’t accept this!”.

Mr. Belly turned away from him and just walked away. While Charles and others follow.

They were about to get out of the arena when a slow clap echoed from behind.

“Well,” a voice said smoothly, “you are more powerful than I thought.”

Everyone turned.

Standing at the edge of the arena floor was Lord Hierem. Beside him stood his son, Jeremy, fists clenched so tightly his knuckles were white. He thought Raphael would defeat Charles.

Charles looked at them without expression.

Lord Hierem smiled faintly.

“I like your talent and cultivator gift,” he said. “And I want to reward it generously. Work for me. Two hundred million dollars per month.”

Five hundred million!!!

Jeremy’s eyes twitched.

The ladies were surprised to hear this. Even Charles. Lord Hierem must be a big deal for him to say this.

Charles scoffed lightly.

“Thanks,” he said. “But I’m not taking that.”

The air shifted. Lord Hierem’s smile thinned.

“You should think carefully,” he said quietly. “If you do not stand with me, then for some reason… we may become enemies.”

His gaze sharpened.

“And trust me. No matter how powerful you are, you can never defeat us.”

Charles understood. The Kyoul Stone which they had recently acquired. They must want to do something bad with it, and they were little afraid to have him as their enemy. But with the kyoul stone, they were still on edge.

Charles smiled faintly.

“Raphael said something similar,” he replied calmly. “Before I burned him.”

Jeremy’s breathing grew uneven. Lord Hierem’s jaw flexed.

Charles turned and walked away without another word. Lizzypie and the others followed close behind.

Behind him, Lord Hierem’s composure cracked.

Jeremy angrily said, “Father. I told you, he won’t join us . But there is nothing he can do. He can never stop us, we have the kyoul stone!”.

Lord Hierem then nodded in agreement.

××××

Outside the arena, which looked perfect that normal people would never know what happened.

Mr. Belly stopped walking and turned to Charles. His usually composed face softened.

“Thank you,” he said sincerely. “Since the day we met you, our lives have changed. Today… you did more than win a fight.”

Charles gave a small smile. “It was nothing.”

“It wasn’t,” Mr. Belly replied firmly. “We were finally able to cut ties with the Trisian family because you defeated Raphael. Without that loss, the contract would never have ended cleanly.”

Charles shrugged lightly. “Well, I’m glad it worked out.”

Mr. Belly nodded and glanced at Gabriella.

“I will be heading back now. There are matters I must settle immediately. Gabriella, we still have to stay with you, hope you don't mind.”

Charles smiled and nodded, “That’s fine. Its okay”

Mr. Belly cleared his throat.

“Ah… one more thing.”

His eyes shifted toward Lizzypie.

“I saw the potion you gave Charles,” he said carefully. “His wounds healed instantly. That was no ordinary medicine. Are you… a potion maker?”

Lizzypie scoffed lightly and crossed her arms.

“Potion maker?” she said with mild amusement. “That’s too basic. I’m more of a divine doctor. I studied it seriously… I just don’t take it seriously.”

Mr. Belly’s breathing subtly changed.

He stepped forward slightly.

“Then… is there something you can do for my daughter’s legs?”

Gabriella’s expression tightened. “Father… you know it isn’t possible. We have tried everything. It’s okay. You have already done more than enough.”

But Mr. Belly shook his head immediately.

“No,” he said, voice heavy. “I will not give up.”

He looked at Lizzypie again, this time not as a wealthy patriarch, but as a desperate father.

“Please,” he said quietly. “If there is even the smallest chance… Can you help me?”

Lizzypie’s expression softened slightly. She sighed.

“I can’t guarantee anything,” she said honestly. “Leg conditions from birth are complicated. I would need to examine her and see if there is a way.”

Mr. Belly stepped closer.

“If there is a chance, I will take it.”

Lizzypie studied him for a long moment, then nodded once.

“I will try my best.”

“I will be forever grateful,” he said.

“I swear… if you succeed… I am willing to give you half of my wealth!!!.”

But what she didn't know was, it was all a lie, and a very first step of Collins, scamming , destroying her and planning to take every dime from her.
