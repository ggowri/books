Chapter 235

Raphael smirked as the hundred copies raised their blazing fireballs toward Charles. Heat washed over the arena in rolling waves.

Mr. Jacob smiled from his seat, arms folded. He was certain it was over. His son was going to roast him.

“Tap out or you burn!” voices shouted from the crowd.

“Tap out!”

“End it!”

Charles did nothing. He stayed on one knee, breathing slow, eyes half-lidded.

The real Raphael stepped forward and laughed. “You heard them. Tap out. Or you die.”

Charles lifted his head and smirked. “Perfect.”

Raphael frowned. “What?”

This was what Charles had been waiting for. To identify the real Raphael, talking him down and others would go down with him.

Raphael scoffed and thought he was bluffing, then he raised the fireball, and his replicates do the same thing.

“Dieeees”

Just as they were about to launch their fireball.

Charles closed his eyes fully.

[Moment Lock activated!!!]

Every Raphael froze mid-motion, their Fireballs hung in the air like burning suns, unmoving. Raphaels just stand there, not even moving an inch. Just like a statue. Same with his others.

The radius only captured him, so the audience could clearly see what was going on.

The arena erupted.

“What the hell is happening?”

“Did everything just stop?”

“He froze them!”

“What kind of power is that?!”

Lizzypie scoffed, folding her arms as she leaned slightly forward. “I knew he had something up his sleeve,” she muttered, a small smirk tugging at her lips.

Her eyes sparkled with excitement and anticipation, clearly enjoying the chaos.

Charles stood slowly. His boots clicked softly on the arena floor as he walked deliberately toward the real Raphael, moving inside the radius of the Moment Lock.

[Fire Beam activated!]

A white-hot light flared from Charles’ eyes. The beam shot straight at Raphael’s tight, scorching through the air with a hiss.

The moment it hit, the heat burned through the clone swarm, instantly vanishing all the replicated Raphaels.

The real Raphael, frozen mid-motion, hadn’t even realized what hit him. The beam cut a perfect hole through his tight, smoke curling from the burn.

The audience gasped.

“What the hell is he doing?!” someone shouted.

“He is burning him!”

Charles didn’t pause. He adjusted his aim, focused, and fired again at Raphael’s other tight.

Zrrrrr!

The second beam hit, scorching, burning, cutting another hole. Smoke curled into the air, heat radiating out in waves. The arena erupted in noise—shock, fear, disbelief.

Mr. Jacob, sitting high in the seats, leaped to his feet, his cane clattering against the floor. “What are you doing to my son?!” he bellowed, voice shaking with rage and fear.

The moment timer on the Moment Lock hit its limit. Time snapped back into motion.

The frozen Raphael blinked, his muscles twitching as life returned to his body. He staggered slightly, his eyes widening as they locked on Charles.

“What… what happened?!” he stammered, shock and fury mixing in his voice.

Charles smirked, cool and collected, his aura steady. “This is what you get for underestimating me,” he said, calm but deadly.

Then Raphael’s pain hit fully. He glanced down and saw the two holes in his tight. Blood seeped through, dripping onto the floor. His legs shook, the heat from the burns still scorching him.

“What the… fuck! What is this! What did you do to me?!” he screamed, voice cracking, anger and agony mixing into one.

The arena was in chaos. Spectators shouted, some in awe, others in panic. Lizzypie’s smirk widened as she leaned back, clearly enjoying the spectacle.

Gabriella covered her mouth, eyes wide. Sapphire crossed her arms, impressed. Even Natasha whistled softly, a mix of admiration and disbelief on her face.

Raphael stared at his legs, eyes wide with horror. Blood kept pouring down, staining the arena floor.

“I… I can never walk again,” he choked out. “Oh fuck no… no… no!”

He writhed in pain, clutching his thighs, his body shaking.

Charles walked toward him slowly. Calm. Steady.

“So…” Charles said, his voice low. “Are you tapping out, or should I finish you?”

Raphael looked up at him, eyes wet, face pale. He tried to glare, but all that came out was a weak whimper.

Charles took another step closer. “Well, since you aren’t tapping out. Let’s finish this.”

Panic exploded across Raphael’s face.

“I TAP OUT!” he screamed suddenly. “I surrender! Get me out of here before this demon kills me!!!”

The announcer quickly stood up, voice shaking but loud.

“Raphael has tapped out! The fight is over! Raphael loses! Charles is the winner!”

The arena erupted.

“Yaaaaa!!!”

Lizzypie and the others screamed in joy. Natasha threw her fist in the air. Sapphire smiled proudly. Gabriella clapped excitedly, her eyes shining.

Raphael didn’t even realize when tears started running down his face. Public defeat. In front of everyone. His image, his pride, his reputation. It was all shattered.

Medics rushed in and lifted him onto a stretcher. His old father, Mr. Jacob, walked beside him, face pale and stunned.

Meanwhile, Lizzypie and the others rushed into the arena toward Charles.

“Are you okay?” Sapphire asked quickly.

“You did it!” Natasha grinned.

Gabriella beamed. “Wow… you are awesome.”

Charles gave a small tired smile. He was still bleeding. Bruised. One of his ribs was cracked. His breathing was heavy.

Lizzypie reached into her sleeve and brought out a small vial filled with clear liquid.

“Drink this,” she said softly.

Charles didn’t hesitate. He knew exactly what it was. A wound-clearing potion, carefully refined to repair internal and external injuries fast.

He uncorked it and drank.

Warmth spread through his body instantly. The cuts on his skin began to close before everyone’s eyes. The bleeding stopped. His bruises faded. Inside, the cracked bone snapped back into place with a soft shift. His breathing steadied. Within moments, he stood straight again, fully restored.

The crowd gasped.

Mr. Belly saw it clearly. His eyes narrowed slightly. A potion that strong…

If she could heal wounds like that, then maybe… maybe she could cure his daughter’s legs.

But he said nothing for now. Instead, he stepped forward and placed a hand on Charles’ shoulder.

“Well done,” he said with a firm nod. “You fought well.”

Due to this defeat, the mood of the arena shifted completely. The young cultivators who once mocked Charles now approached him with respect.

“That was incredible,” one said.

“I’m impressed,” another added.

“What’s the origin of your power?” someone asked.

“Which bloodline are you from?” another questioned curiously.

Charles simply smiled calmly. He didn’t answer.

At that moment, Raphael was being carried past them on the stretcher. His face was pale. His eyes were empty. Mr. Jacob walked beside him, still shaken.

“Stop,” Mr. Belly said firmly.

The medics paused.

Mr. Belly stepped closer, looking down at Raphael.

“Since you lost,” he said coldly, “remember our deal. You will never disturb my daughter again. And from today, the Belly Family will stop sponsoring you.”

Mr. Jacob froze.

“What?!” he shouted. “What the hell are you saying?!”
