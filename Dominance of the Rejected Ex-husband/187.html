Chapter 187

 

In a minute, the lobby shifted.

Boots scraped against marble. As the uniformed guards closed in from every direction. Batons hung heavy in their hands, black and unforgiving. In seconds, Charles and Serena were boxed in.

Jude spat on the floor.

“Don’t even try to beg,” he snarled. “I’m going to break your legs no matter what you say.”

Charles didn’t flinch. He looked at the circle of guards and let out a short scoff.

“Who told you we are going to apologize?”

Jude’s lips curled into a cruel smile. “Good. Then this will be easy.”

He lifted his hand and pointed sharply.

“Security. Beat them. Cripple them.”

The guards hesitated. No one moved.

A few exchanged uneasy glances. Batons tightened in their grips, but their feet stayed planted because this wasn’t right. They knew it. 

Jude noticed them.

His face twisted with rage. “What are you waiting for?” he barked. “Anyone who refuses will be sacked. Consider your job gone. Kiss it goodbye.”

With that, the hesitation cracked. One guard took a step forward. Then another. The circle tightened.

Serena’s nails dug into Charles’ sleeve. Her breath came fast now, shallow and sharp. “Charles…” she whispered. “This is bad.”

Just as the first guard raised his baton—

“Sir!”

The shout cut through the lobby like a blade.

Everyone froze as heads turned toward the reception desk. It was the receptionist,  who stood there, hands clenched at her sides.

“Please stop.”

Jude slowly turned.

His stare was deadly. “What did you just shout at me, Quincy?”

The room went silent.

Quincy, the receptionist swallowed hard. Her shoulders stiffened, then she lowered her head slightly. 

“I didn’t mean it like that, sir,” she said quickly. “All I’m saying is… this isn’t right. We shouldn’t beat people. If there’s an issue, we should detain them and call the police. That’s the proper way.”

Jude’s jaw tightened. If the police were too involved, he would be the one to be blamed. 

“Just shut up,” he snapped. “You have no say in this. I’m the COO. I can do whatever I want.”

Quincy lifted her head again. Her hands were shaking now, but she didn’t step back.

“They came here with a proposal, sir,” she said. “We should at least let them present it. What do you think the boss will do if she hears about this?”

Jude’s eyes flickered.

For a brief moment, doubt slipped through the cracks of his fury. The CEO’s face flashed in his mind. 

Linda noticed immediately.

She shot Quincy a venomous glare. “You are just a receptionist,” she snapped. “Know your place.”

She whispered, “will you let this dirty bitch talk down on you!”.

Linda’s words pushed Jude over the edge completely.

He spun toward the reception desk, his face dark with fury. “Quincy ” he snapped, voice sharp and trembling, “say one more word and I will sack you immediately.”

Then he turned back to the guards, spreading his arms in rage. “What are you waiting for? Do your fucking job.”

He sneered and added coldly, “And don’t fool yourselves. The CEO is on a two-week trip. I’m the only one who can contact her directly. Everything I decide here is final and no one can change that.”

He pointed straight at Charles. “So beat him up like I ordered.”

The guards had no choice now. They moved in.

Collins’ grin stretched wider as he watched, arms crossed, already savoring the outcome.

Just as one guard lifted his baton—

“Step aside.”

The voice rang through the lobby, firm and commanding, cutting straight through the tension.

Everyone froze.

Heads turned toward the entrance as the guards instinctively moved apart, a path opening without anyone quite realizing why.

“Ms. Sapphire has arrived!!!.”

The words landed heavy in the air, and in that instant, the entire lobby went silent.

Jude froze.

The color drained from his face as the words sank in, his confidence collapsing in an instant. He had personally followed Sapphire to the airport yesterday.

He watched her board. That was the only reason he had acted so almighty today. She was supposed to be gone for two full weeks.

Why would she be back already?

He swallowed hard.

Then she came into view.

Sapphire stepped into the lobby dressed in a pristine white suit, tailored to perfection, elegant without effort. 

Every line of her carried quiet authority, every step radiating power. Her beauty wasn’t loud, but it was undeniable, refined, commanding. 

This was the richest woman in the nation, the kind whose presence is overwhelming.

Behind her, a line of security men in black suits moved in formation, sharp-eyed and disciplined.

Jude reacted on instinct.

He bowed deeply, almost folding in half.

Around the lobby, employees snapped into motion. Heads lowered. 

Even the guards surrounding Charles and Serena stiffened and stepped back.

Jude slowly raised his head, forcing a smile that barely held together.

“Ms. Sapphire,” he said carefully. “Why are you back so soon? You said two weeks. Did something happen.”

Sapphire smiled faintly as she adjusted her cufflinks. “Yes,” she replied calmly. “Something came up and I had to take care of it.”

Her gaze drifted across the lobby then, taking in the guards, the tense faces, Linda on the floor, the frozen expressions of Tracy and Collins.

Her eyes briefly passed over Charles.

She didn’t react like he recognized him. 

Then she looked back at Jude.

“What’s going on here,” she asked lightly. “It seems my company is already on fire after just one day away.”

Jude’s throat tightened.

He swallowed again, his mind racing as lies began forming in his head.
