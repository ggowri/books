Chapter 879: Tickling

"Life goes by in a flash. Time flies! Look at you, you've been fond of me for so many years. There aren't many decades in a lifetime, just watch how I fulfill my promises."

Elliott gave a nonchalant smile, the very picture of a man determined to play the perfect husband to the hilt.

His transformation was truly remarkable.

It's unclear whether this change is genuinely from his heart, or simply a result of his current amnesia.

So, even though he's being his true self now, I keep reminding myself in my heart, this is just a part of the show.

"Alright! I'm a bit tired, I'll head upstairs to freshen up."

I responded to his words with a nonchalant air, then made up an excuse, ready to head upstairs.

Elliott forced me to down a few more sushi rolls before he finally let me off the hook. He told me to head upstairs while he stayed behind to clean up.

As I ascended the stairs, I noticed that the painting at the top of the second floor had been replaced.

Previously, Elliott had a classic Western painting hanging up. Now, he's had our picture turned into a piece of art and hung it in its place.

This group photo was snapped during that time we went hiking.

Looking at this group photo, and thinking about everything that happened afterwards, I can't help but feel a wave of nostalgia.

If our relationship had stayed as beautiful as it was in the beginning, without all the twists and turns that followed, I guess the life I'm living now with Elliott would be what I've always dreamed of, right?

I paused for a moment, taking it all in, before heading back to my bedroom.

By the time I finished my shower, Elliott had already returned to the bedroom, reeking of kitchen fumes.

This man, who used to shoo me off to the guest room at the slightest hint of cooking oil smell, has indeed changed a lot.

As soon as he entered the room, he disdainfully stripped off all his clothes, quickly revealing his tall, sexy figure clad only in a pair of underwear.

Clad in my bathrobe, I stood frozen, staring at the scene before me. In a panic, I quickly lowered my gaze, seeking escape.

Elliott, however, didn't seem to care. Perhaps he knew I was shy, but he deliberately walked up to me, casually hooked his finger under my chin, and teased,

"After all these years of marriage, how can you still blush? I've got an itch on my back, would you mind scratching it for me?"

It was then that I realized my face was flushed.

"Alright, where does it itch?"

As we drew close, a certain part of him began to swell reflexively. For a moment, I was unsure whether to look down or up, so I hastily asked him to turn around.

"Scratch my shoulder blade, would you?"

"Alright. Here? Or here... here?"

I began to scratch him. But no matter where I scratched, he said it wasn't the right spot.

I was so engrossed in tickling him that I didn't realize my hand had gradually moved from his back to his chest.

I was puzzling over why his ticklish spot was so elusive when he suddenly seized my hand, thrusting it onto his chest, declaring:

"Those places aren't right. The itchiest place is my heart. I panic now whenever I can't see you. Even though I know you don't need me that much, I can't help but want to do something for you. Do you think I'm less of a man now?"

He looked at me intently, unable to resist sharing the thoughts swirling within him.

I could sense his sincerity now, unlike before when it was all just manipulations and schemes.

He... he's truly smitten, isn't he?
