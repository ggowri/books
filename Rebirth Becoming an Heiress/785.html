Chapter 785: Reminiscence

"Yes. It was from that day on, I realized you were nothing like the image I had of you. I found myself gradually drawn to you, increasingly wanting to know more about you. That's when I discovered, you're a woman of mystery, an enigma I can't quite decipher."

Elliott drawled, his voice laced with a hint of intoxication.

His voice, tender and intimate, was like a lover's whisper, suddenly piercing my heart, gently stirring something within.

I didn't see it coming, him suddenly spilling all this to me. Tonight, he was eager to share, not even waiting for my questions before he softly continued:

"The first time you took my breath away was when I read your diary. Before that, I knew you had feelings for me, but I had no idea how deep they ran. You documented every encounter we had, every look I gave. Yet, I had no recollection of those moments. Do you know it took me two full days to read through your diary? And after I finished, I couldn't sleep for two nights. I was moved, yes, but more than that, I felt guilt. I regretted not cherishing a woman who spent her entire youth loving me. Madeline, I'm sorry. For all the years of your youth I missed, I plan to spend the rest of my life making it up to you."

Elliott, when he's had one too many, sure does become unusually chatty.

I furrowed my brows at him. The depth of emotion in his eyes was so genuine, it left me stunned.

Why would he suddenly say such things?

Was he provoked by alcohol, or had he been bottling it up for too long?

I'm at a loss. I don't know what to say, so I just dumbly utter, "Oh."

Elliott still held me. I struggled, but he wouldn't let me up, murmuring again:

"Madeline, I can't pinpoint the exact moment I fell for you. All I know is, every time you talk about parting ways, it hurts like hell. I don't want to part from you, I want to spend the rest of my life with you."

As Elliott spoke, his voice gradually softened, his breathing growing heavier.

Glancing up, I found him dozing off mid-sentence. Moreover, his head had unconsciously nestled into the crook of my neck.

I was momentarily stunned. His words stirred a flurry in my otherwise tranquil heart.

I've stopped reminiscing about the many things that happened between him and me.

But his words, they stirred up memories within me. I found myself recalling a myriad of details from the past.

I lowered my gaze to his sleeping face. For a fleeting moment, a pang of reluctance tugged at my heartstrings.

As someone who has been in unrequited love for years, I know all too well the lonely taste of a relationship where only one person is moving, one person is pursuing, and one person is desperately trying.

I know, it's like Elliott and I have switched places now.

Once upon a time, I was the one chasing after him. Now, he's the one clinging to our shared memories, refusing to let go.

I posed a question to myself in my mind:

Is there still a chance for Elliott and me?

The question had barely formed in my mind when, in the next second, I heard a resounding answer deep within me.
