Chapter 82 Disappointed Again

"Madeline, were you deliberately trying to annoy me?"

He finally spat out the melon and glanced at me.

"No. Didn't you say before that we could never be together?"

I shrugged my shoulders and put on an innocent look.

"I believe that I have now lowered my posture."

His voice became low again. He lay down, opened his eyes, and silently gazed at the moon in the sky, appearing particularly indifferent.

I thought to myself, "Is this what they call lowering oneself?"

To be honest, I didn't feel it.

If in the previous life I had not witnessed with my own eyes the kind of madness he had for Giselle, perhaps, if he has reached this point now, I would truly fall in love with him again.

However, I have witnessed firsthand his craziness for love in the past, and compared to what he is doing for me now, I don't feel moved.

In other words, he used to not see me as a person before, but now he finally sees me as half a person.

"Red velvet cake was really delicious. Won't you give it a try? When I was a child, my favorite thing was sitting on the rooftop with my parents, eating red velvet cake and admiring the moon."

I shifted the topic again, I really didn't want to ruin the harmonious atmosphere tonight.

The wind tonight was particularly comfortable, everything was just right, and what's more, today was Thanksgiving.

Elliott's face became even more gloomy, and he stood up and walked to the front railing, the wind carrying his low voice over.

Grandfather's health was getting worse and worse. He hoped that before he passed away, we could have a child.

Upon hearing his words, I also felt a bit sad.

Elliott's grandfather, who was already ninety years old, had been living in his hometown since his retirement, with someone specifically responsible for taking care of him.

I only saw him on the day he got married to Elliott. He was in high spirits at that time, wearing a black suit, and he seemed very happy to see me and Elliott getting married.

I heard that Elliott was raised by his grandparents, and he had a deep bond with his grandfather and grandmother. Unfortunately, his grandmother passed away due to illness before I married him.

I thought to myself, "No wonder he suddenly treated me so well, it turns out it was because of his grandfather."

I was confused before, but now I finally understand.

I used to wonder how Elliott suddenly changed his attitude towards me. It's unrealistic for someone who didn't like you from the beginning to suddenly change.

The small flame that was burning in my heart was instantly extinguished by a splash of cold water.

And just then, Elliott's phone, which was placed aside, rang. I glanced at it and saw that it was a text message from Giselle. I was instantly extremely disappointed.

The text message only displayed a small portion, but from this small snippet of text, I already have a general idea.

"Mr. Bryant, thank you for the gift you gave me. I was very happy..."

I thought to myself, "Elliott really cares about her."

We have been together for so long, and he has never given me any gifts. But he has already carefully prepared a gift for Giselle, even though they have only known each other for a few days.

I was very frustrated, and in an instant, I suddenly felt that the moonlight had also become desolate.

"Hasn't Giselle already been discharged from the hospital?"

I casually asked, once again avoiding the topic of Elliott.

"Well, she just left the hospital yesterday."

Elliott turned to look at me. At that moment, he was bathed in moonlight, appearing somewhat holy. His every gesture and movement still resembled that of a high and mighty prince, making one unable to resist the urge to prostrate oneself at his feet.

"Oh, she just seemed to have sent you a message. Don't you take a look?"

I raised my eyebrows at him, indicating him to look at the phone.
