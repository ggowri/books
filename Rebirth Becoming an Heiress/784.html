Chapter 784: His Innermost Thoughts

Inside the room.

Beer bottles littered the floor. Under the dim light, Elliott slouched in the middle of a dark green sofa, a bottle of beer in his hand. His black shirt was carelessly unbuttoned. The flickering light cast shadows on his face, making him appear even more disheveled and icy.

Upon seeing me, Barry instantly relaxed. He quickly leaned in and whispered:

"Mr. Bryant has already downed over ten bottles of booze. Neither Mr. Woods nor I could talk him out of it."

I gave a nod, waved my hand, signaling him to leave. Then, I made my way towards Elliott.

Elliott seemed oblivious to my presence. He lifted his beer to his lips, ready to take a swig, but I snatched the bottle from his grasp. I furrowed my brows, my voice icy as I spoke:

"Enough, Elliott, no more drinking! What the hell are you trying to do?"

Elliott scowled, his first glance at me was filled with palpable anger.

The moment he realized it was me, the anger in his eyes dissipated instantly. He grabbed my hand in a swift motion.

Before I knew it, I had tumbled right into his arms. The strong scent of alcohol hit me instantly:

"Honey, you're here. I knew it, you couldn't bear to leave me behind."

I yearned to rise, but his hand was already wrapped around my waist. "Elliott, don't," I pleaded.

Evidently, Elliott was drunk. He buried his head in the crook of my neck.

"Don't move, it's been a while since I've held you. I just want to hold you tight."

Elliott's head was heavy. I tried to push it away, but no matter how hard I tried, I couldn't. I was frustrated:

"Elliott, quit fooling around. Let go of me, I'll take you home!"

It was as if Elliott hadn't heard my words. Suddenly, he cupped my face, gazing at me with such intense affection:

"Madeline, do you know when I fell for you?"

His eyes were hazy, as if lost in a sea of pleasant memories. His deep, affectionate demeanor left me momentarily stunned.

I'm here tonight purely out of humanitarian spirit, intending to take him back home.

But I didn't expect him to suddenly hit me with this question.

He never mentioned when he started falling for me. Even in our most passionate moments, he never uttered the word 'love'.

Now, he's conveniently forgotten, daring to utter such words.

His words instantly piqued my curiosity, prompting me to ask:

"When?"

Elliott sat up and I thought he was going to let me go. But instead, he pulled me into his embrace, forcing me to press tightly against his chest.

The long-lost heartbeat echoed powerfully in my ears, instantly stirring up memories that were once buried deep within my mind.

This level of intimacy left me at a loss. Just as I was about to push away, he softly said:

"The first time I fell for you was the night you played the piano. Before that, I never knew how beautiful you could look, sitting at the white piano, your delicate hands dancing over the keys."

I'm somewhat touched.

His words instantly yanked my thoughts back to that time.

Of course, he wouldn't know. That piano performance was my all-out effort to impress him after living a second life. That's why I played so earnestly.

That day, I was determined to show him that I wasn't as lousy as he thought I was.

"Oh, so you're just smitten, huh?"

I arched an eyebrow, questioning.
