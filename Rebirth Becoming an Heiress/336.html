Chapter 336 He Is So Good

That night, Elliott was a man possessed.

And he drove me mad as well.

By the end, I couldn't even feel the cold anymore. I was exhausted and thirsty, feeling like my bones were coming apart.

My clothes were all torn to shreds, and they were no longer in use.

He wrapped me in his overcoat and carried me to the car. Then, he laid me across the back seat and turned on the heater.

I was too spent from his relentless passion to even fight him.

There I lay, aching all over, and yet...there was an indescribable sense of satisfaction.

It was shameful, really.

I had never experienced such madness before.

My lips were parched, desperate for water.

Elliott found a bottle of water in the car and handed it to me. I was so thirsty that I instinctively reached out to grab it. But to my surprise, he pulled his hand back and took a big gulp himself.

I glared at him, so furious at his selfishness that I wanted to scream. But my throat was too dry, and the words wouldn't come.

I thought he wanted the water all to himself, but then, with a sly grin, he suddenly leaned in and kissed me.

I was forced to open my mouth, and he started passing the water to me, sip by sip.

If we weren't already married, I'd have died of embarrassment right there.

But a wave of warmth washed over me as we grew intimate.

When he laid the seat back, the car seemed to expand. He kept feeding me water until I pushed him away, showing I'd had enough. Only then did he lie down beside me.

He reached out his arm to pull us close, and we lay side by side in the car. Exhausted, we cuddled in silence for a while before Elliott finally spoke.

"Stop making me angry. Stop doing things that upset me."

He didn't mention Quinn again, but I knew he meant he didn't want me getting too close to Quinn.

"Then stop seeing Giselle," I challenged.

"Okay," he said, his tone serious.

I didn't expect him to agree, so I instinctively turned and leaned on him, "Really? Then make her leave Bryant Group. Tell her to stay out of your life, how about that?"

"Fine, but you also have to promise me you won't see Quinn without my approval. Whatever happens, you should think of me first."

Elliott actually agreed, which surprised me.

"And you always have to answer my calls."

My heart, which felt incredibly insecure, finally found a sliver of satisfaction. His chest was still bare, his broad muscles undeniably sexy and tempting. I couldn't help but draw circles on his chest.

"Deal. I'll set a unique ringtone for your calls."

He grabbed my hand suddenly and then gently kissed me on the lips.

I thought that tonight would be like the old times when we would end up fighting over a misunderstanding.

But now, I finally see where I stand in his heart...

It was funny how those impossible contradictions had found a way to work themselves out.

I quietly nestled in Elliott's arms, and just then, he suddenly opened the sunroof of the car and muttered, "Look, stars."

Earlier, I'd been too angry to notice how beautiful this place was.

It wasn't until he opened the skylight that I realized how beautiful the stars above were.

"Wow, this is amazing..." I couldn't help but exclaim.

He pulled me closer into his arms again, and I heard his rare hearty laughter.

"Sometimes, when I can't sleep, I drive here alone. I found that this place is quiet late at night. That's why I wanted you to see this beautiful view."
