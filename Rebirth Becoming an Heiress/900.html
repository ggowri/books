Chapter 900: Collision Course

I froze, rooted to the spot, staring at Elliott in disbelief.

I blurted out subconsciously:

"How come you're here?"

I feared that his knowledge of Noah's existence would cast a shadow of doubt over everything, so I never disclosed to him my parents' current whereabouts.

During the period I was assisting with his treatment, every time I headed home, I'd cautiously check if there were any vehicles tailing me.

But I never expected Elliott to find this place. And judging by his appearance, he'd been waiting at the entrance of this neighborhood for quite some time.

He looked just as worn out as I did, his eyes bloodshot. He stared at me and asked point-blank:

"Last night, I heard a child's cry over the phone. Madeline, since when did you guys have a kid?"

Sure enough, he heard me. And he was suspicious of me.

With a smile, I instinctively spun a lie:

"Oh, that's my relative's kid. He's been staying at my place for a bit recently."

"Really? Which relative?"

He started asking again.

"You don't know him, I have a lot of distant relatives."

I wanted to fob him off with some lame excuse, but he wasn't having any of it. He gripped my shoulders, looking straight into my eyes:

"Madeline, your folks live in here, right? Why won't you let me meet them now?"

He started asking that question again.

And the question I dread the most is the one he might ask.

I stared at him, feeling a wave of irritation. I began to explain:

"Elliott, it's not that I don't want to, it's just that my parents aren't keen on meeting you. You know you haven't been the best to me in the past, so they're a bit put off by you. Plus, they had a long night yesterday and they're already exhausted. It's not a good time for you to come over."

"Same excuse, again..." Elliott looked at me, his gaze complex as he said, "Madeline, it seems like you're keeping a lot of things from me."

"No, I'm off to buy some bread for my parents. Elliott, you should head to the office early. I'll be heading to Glory Group later, there's a ton of stuff waiting for me to handle today."

I was at a loss for words, unable to explain to him. So, I made up an excuse on the spot. Then, I turned on my heel, intending to continue my journey towards the bakery.

However, Elliott suddenly grabbed my hand, wrapping his arm around my waist from behind. He pulled me close into his embrace, whispering softly into my ear:

"Madeline, I've laid all my cards on the table, so I expect the same from you, alright?"

There was a hint of humble pleading in Elliott's tone that caught me off guard.

"Mhm."

I gave a soft nod, instinctively pulling away from his hand wrapped around my waist. After a moment's hesitation, I strode towards the bakery.

The bakery was bustling in the early morning, with various types of bread wafting a rich aroma.

"Madam, I'd like some bread..."

I'm a regular here, pretty chummy with the bakery owner.

"How much is the total? I'll pick up the tab!"

Just as I was about to pay, Elliott suddenly appeared behind me. His deep voice stood out in the crowd. The bakery owner immediately looked up, giving him a thorough once-over.

Clad in a casual black ensemble, Elliott's towering figure stood out in the crowd, his aura even more so. His commanding presence was impossible to ignore, leaving those around him in awe.
