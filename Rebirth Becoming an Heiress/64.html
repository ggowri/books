Chapter 64 Don't Be Afraid

I kept my head down. My emotions remained inexplicably low. If he had said that before, I could have confidently told him, "No, you're wrong. I don't care about him at all."

But now, I find myself unable to refute it. Because I know better than anyone else.

When Giselle and Elliott met, my emotions were truly in complete disarray...

"Is he still in the hospital?"

I remained silent for a long time. Eventually, I couldn't help but ask.

"I didn't know. How about I accompany you to the hospital again?"

Quinn asked me tentatively.

Do I have to go again?

I also asked myself in my heart.

What difference does it make whether I go or not? Even if I went and saw them together, what difference would it make?

My whole heart was caught in a dilemma. And Quinn, clearly seeing through my thoughts, pulled me aside and said:

"Let's go. I'll accompany you for another visit."

I originally wanted to refuse. But I don't know why, in the end, I still got into his car with Quinn.

We once again arrived at the entrance of that hospital ward.

Through the small window at the door of the ward, I saw Giselle wearing a patient gown. She was still half lying in bed. And the tall figure by her bedside was clearly Elliott.

Wasn't he always busy with work?

In theory, at this point in time, he should have already been working at Bryant Group.

But now, he has given up all his work for her. He willingly stays by Giselle's side.

They seemed to be chatting again. Perhaps, they were talking about something funny. Because I saw Giselle on the hospital bed unable to hold back her laughter, covering her mouth. And Elliott, he had his back turned to me. I couldn't see his expression. But his entire posture was clearly relaxed and natural.

The two of them really hit it off at first sight.

Mingming, they had just met yesterday. Are they already getting along so well?

And this kind of relaxed and natural sweetness is something that I had tried so hard to achieve in my marriage, but couldn't force it.

I clearly didn't care anymore. But why is my heart still hurting like this?

I covered my chest. For a moment, I didn't even have the courage to push open the door of the ward. So, I turned around and wanted to escape.

But Quinn stopped me.

"What happened to you? Are you afraid to go in?"

"Do you think I had the need to go in?"

I glanced at Quinn. I smiled weakly at him.

"You are not like the carefree you during this period of time. Didn't you keep saying that you don't care about him anymore? Now, a young girl who has just entered society has defeated you?"

Quinn raised an eyebrow and said.

"How is that possible? Of course, I didn't care about him anymore. I was too lazy to bother about who he liked!"

When I felt Quinn's provocation, I instantly retorted. I quickly snapped back.

"That's right. Be brave. I thought you had turned back into the timid coward you used to be."

Quinn laughed immediately.

Afterward, he leaned close to my ear and suddenly whispered something to me.

"Madeline, don't be afraid. Return the pain given to you by others a thousandfold, a hundredfold!"

Quinn's sudden words hit me like a powerful stimulant, injecting fiercely into my body.

I quickly woke up from that state of inertia and despondency.

Yeah, what am I doing? Why until now, am I still plagued by doubts?

I had long stopped caring about Elliott. Even now, if there is a Giselle by his side, so what?

Why was I sad? Why was I sad because of those two people in my previous life who caused me so much pain?

I shouldn't have let my emotions be affected by these two jerks at all. On the contrary, I should have done everything in my power to return all the pain I have endured in the past to them.

My mood was turned upside down as if by force. In an instant, the gloom that Giselle brought to me disappeared completely.

I looked at Quinn. On his handsome face, there was a full encouragement. He gestured for me to go in and face everything.
