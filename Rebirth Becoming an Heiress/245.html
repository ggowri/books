Chapter 245 Decision

"Why are you so silent? What are you really thinking?"

Elliott furrowed his brow, looking at me intently.

I raised my gaze to meet his, unable to hold back my curiosity. "Does Cormac know about his condition?"

Elliott shook his head.

"We're keeping it from him. If he finds out, it will only burden him psychologically and potentially accelerate the spread of the cancer."

I thought, "Judging from the awkwardness of my current relationship with Elliott. There is no way I can conceive a child with him within the next two years.

However, refusing him now will make me appear callous unless I choose to divorce him immediately.

Giselle is still coveting Elliott. If I divorce him, she will surely swoop in to marry him, leading me down a path I have experienced in a previous life.

Since Elliott has proposed the idea of preparing for a child, I can give it a try."

Slowly, I spoke up. "I can agree to preparing for a child. However, we need to set some ground rules. Even if I have a child, I have my own career and life plans. I don't want my plans disrupted because of a child."

Elliott visibly relaxed upon hearing my acceptance. "That's fine. I can hire a nanny to handle the household chores."

He didn't insist on me becoming a full-time homemaker, which suited me perfectly.

"I'm quite busy at the moment. Can we start after I finish filming this series and return from Louis City?" I asked, giving myself a buffer period.

"Sure, no problem. Have some tomato soup to boost your health. Since we're planning for a child, we should approach this scientifically. After dinner, let's consult Doctor Louis in detail on the necessary preparations. We can also take some essential medications to ensure we have a healthy and smart baby next year."

Elliott suggested enthusiastically, his eyes filled with anticipation.

I was surprised, thinking, "Is he really looking forward to having a healthy, smart baby with me?

I've never had such thoughts.

Perhaps he is just considering Cormac's health, and I happen to be his wife, making me the chosen one to bear a child.

Nevertheless, if having a child with Elliott means closing the possibility of him being with Giselle, I am willing to take the risk.

After all, I will turn 28 soon, an age where childbirth is becoming more pressing."

"No need to consult Doctor Louis. Have you forgotten that Fiona is a doctor?" I chuckled.

It seemed more fitting to ask Fiona, an expert in the field, rather than a less familiar doctor.

"You're right, I forgot about that. Let's ask Fiona and get all the necessary medications ready in advance," Elliott smiled unconsciously, his tone lightening up.

He immediately served me a bowl of tomato soup and continued to fill my plate with food.

As I watched him being so attentive and eager to become a father, my tense heart was gradually lightened by his infectious enthusiasm.
