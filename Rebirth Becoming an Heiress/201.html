Chapter 201 Enjoying the Beautiful Scenery

I wondered, "Has Elliott gone mad? Did he really come just to keep me company?"

I felt as if my brain had exploded. Such a thing had seemed impossible in the past. But now, it was almost unbelievable to me.

"I can set aside my own affairs. After all, it's rare for me to take a break. In all these years, this is the first time," Elliott said as he looked into my eyes sincerely. At that moment, his eyes seemed to electrify, capturing my attention completely.

I had to admit, it was indeed difficult to refuse an invitation from such a handsome and perfect man.

I actually already had everything planned out for the afternoon, but when I looked at Elliott's face, I just couldn't say no.

I then instinctively said, "Well... Okay then."

Elliott smiled with satisfaction upon hearing my response.

When he smiled, it was as if an ancient glacier had suddenly melted.

His features were relaxed, looking like nature reviving in spring, and I found myself mesmerized by him.

With that, my infatuation with Elliott was reignited.

It seemed that when he unleashed his charm before me, I still couldn't resist him.

Realizing this, I felt indescribably frustrated. But by then, Elliott had already taken my hand and led me out of the conference room.

"Adam, I have something to attend to this afternoon. I'll let you handle the rest," he said.

With that, Elliott took me away from the film set amidst Adam's once again shocked expression.

This time, Elliott really was on vacation. He didn't even bring Toby along but drove his rarely used white Porsche to Louis City to find me.

I got into Elliott's car, and he quickly turned the car around, driving us away from the film set.

He had a habit of driving with one hand on the steering wheel.

As Elliott gripped the wheel, the watch on his wrist sparkled dazzlingly in the sunlight, making him look exceptionally dashing.

Sitting in the car, I couldn't help but steal a few glances at him. For some reason, my heart, which had been calm for so long, suddenly fluttered.

I felt like Elliott was unconsciously exuding his masculine charm today. Even the fragrance in his car seemed to carry a hint of romantic scent.

It was clear he had come prepared. With that realization, I immediately became more guarded.

I kept telling myself, "Hang in there, Madeline. Don't be conquered by his charm. You have to be rational, absolutely rational."

Watching Elliott drive straight ahead, I asked, "Where are you taking me?"

"You'll know once we get there," he replied.

Elliott didn't tell me, but soon, he turned the steering wheel and headed toward the heart of Louis City.

Since arriving in Louis City, I had mostly been wandering between the film set and the hotel, and I hadn't had the chance to admire the beauty of this ancient city.

Now, I finally got to see the city's ancient buildings and beautiful urban landscape from the car.

Elliott then drove along the street for about seven minutes before parking in front of a glass-fronted skyscraper.

He turned to look at me and signaled for me to get out. But I had no idea why he had brought me here.

I then cluelessly followed Elliott out of the car. Afterward, he immediately took my hand and led me into the building.

Feeling a bit nervous, I couldn't help but ask again, "What exactly are we doing here?"

Elliott just continued to smile and didn't respond. After taking me into the elevator, he pressed the button for the top floor of the building.
