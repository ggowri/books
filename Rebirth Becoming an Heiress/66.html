Chapter 66 Why Don't You Go Die?

"Nothing happened between me and Quinn. Don't falsely accuse me of anything."

I instinctively waved my hand and continued to speak casually.

I found that, compared to my own sadness and anger, I would rather see Elliott angry and concerned.

The pleasure of revenge made me particularly happy.

"You, in the future, stay away from Quinn. Don't forget, you and I are still husband and wife!"

Elliott angrily pinched my chin.

"And what about you? Can you stay away from those wild women?"

I coldly retorted. At the same time, I closely watched Elliott. I wanted to see his reaction.

"You were simply being unreasonable!"

He coldly uttered a few words. In his tone, there was contempt towards me.

Was I being unreasonable?

In our conversation in the previous life, this sentence also appeared frequently.

Perhaps in his eyes, no matter how much I changed, no matter what I became, I was just a woman who was being unreasonable and not worth his attention.

I couldn't help but laugh coldly. Then, I pushed away his hand that was holding my chin.

"Actually, I don't want to be an unreasonable woman either. Elliott, how about from today onwards, we go our separate ways? You can have any woman you want, I promise not to interfere. But what I do and who I am with, please don't meddle, okay?"

Elliott looked at me coldly. He seemed to be watching a joke.

"Madeline, how could you, as a married woman, say such things? You want to have an open marriage with me? I'm sorry, but I'm not that open-minded!"

Elliott could really say these words.

Can a married man do what he does openly and honestly?

Since we got married until now, has he ever regarded me as his wife?

He had always been high and mighty, doing whatever he pleased. He spent time with whomever he chose. Rumors about him spread with whomever he was seen. And I, am I supposed to remain faithful and keep to an empty house just for him?

"Since you can't do an open marriage, we might as well get a divorce. Elliott, I'm serious about filing for divorce. I hope you'll consider it."

I said coldly.

"Madeline, you were definitely trying to provoke my limits, weren't you?"

Elliott instantly became furious. His voice was deep, like a devil from the depths of hell.

But I am no longer afraid of him getting angry like before. I smiled indifferently. Because I didn't want to show any concern for him in front of him.

"If you can wholeheartedly focus on me, I can also do the same for you. If you can't, then I can't either. Elliott, loyalty in marriage requires both parties to be able to do so, doesn't it?"

I looked at him and said word by word.

However, what I didn't expect was that he suddenly laughed sarcastically.

"Madeline, are you now talking to me about marriage equality? Are you even worthy?"

His words, indeed, made people feel sad.

He carried himself with the same high posture as before. It was as if I was born to submit to him, to obey him. No matter how he treated me, I had to accept it.

But why?

In my previous life, I was too humble in front of him. I waited for him for too long in the same place. But in this life, I will not allow myself to be like that again. I want him to watch me, step by step, leaving his control.

I looked at him. I thought, at that moment, the smile on my face must have been more dazzlingly eerie than a poppy flower.

"Elliott, why am I not deserving? In terms of family background, education, appearance, and everything we possess, I am not much inferior to you. Why can't I demand equality? Why? Just because I have loved you for so many years, does that mean I deserve to be stepped on, trampled, and humiliated by you? Elliott, why don't you go die?"
