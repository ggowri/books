Chapter 862: She Pleads for Forgiveness in a Hushed Tone

Apologies?

Didn't she just put on a whole spectacle at my doorstep yesterday? And now she's apologizing to me?

If it weren't for Elliott's illness, I would've exposed her true colors right then and there.

"I'm just here to celebrate Elliott's birthday today, there's no need for you to apologize to me."

I set down my fork, speaking with an icy indifference.

Laura glanced at me, a hint of guilt in her eyes. Subconsciously, she looked towards Jasper, then abruptly grabbed my hand, saying:

"Madeline, Jasper and I spent the entire night talking yesterday. I've come to realize that I've wronged you in the past, and I've done things I shouldn't have. Given how much Elliott adores you, and considering he's our only son, we should treat you with the respect you deserve. I promise you, I won't treat you the way I did before. I'll regard you as my own daughter. I hope you can put the past behind us and forgive me, will you?"

Laura's words came across as deeply touching.

I was just about to lay into her with some sarcasm.

Elliott set down his fork, glanced at Laura, and said:

"Mom, what's gotten into you today? Such a sudden change of heart? If you'd felt this way earlier, Madeline and I wouldn't have had to go through so much turmoil. We might even have had a child calling you 'grandma' by now. That would have put grandpa at ease."

Elliott's still grappling with amnesia, I reckon they haven't broken the news about his grandpa's passing to him yet.

Laura awkwardly muttered:

"Yeah, yeah, I admit it. I was wrong before. Your dad gave me a good talking to last night. I went too far with some things. I get it now. Madeline's so young, how could I just decide she can't have kids? Medicine's so advanced these days. I truly regret it!"

At this point, I finally got it.

Her sudden change of attitude was due to regret.

Perhaps it was because I didn't agree to let her see Noah yesterday, and she saw how warmly I treated the Woods family, that she took some time to reflect on her actions when she got home.

If it weren't for her past actions being way out of line, I wouldn't have kept her from seeing Noah.

In the end, Noah is Elliott's child, a fact that is undeniable no matter what.

Had she said these things to me before the whole Giselle incident, perhaps I could have forgiven her.

But now, it's all too late.

Hearing her words of regret, not a ripple stirred within me. I responded coldly:

"All that's in the past now. If every harm in the world could be mended with a simple 'I'm sorry', wouldn't that mean murderers could get off scot-free?"

Laura was initially filled with anticipation, but at those words, her face instantly turned sour.

She looked at me, disbelief etched on her face, her hand reaching out to grasp mine. But I pulled away, instinctively shifting my body a bit to put some distance between us.

Jasper hadn't uttered a word, but I could feel his eyes, always watching us.

He's always been a man of few words. Through my marriage and divorce with Elliott, through it all, he's just been a bystander, never voicing his opinions.

However, after all these years, I've come to know them quite well.

He's a bit wary of Laura. And a lot of things, even though he doesn't say anything in front of us, he's definitely discussed them with Laura behind the scenes.

In the past, it was always Laura who took the spotlight, with Cormac controlling the situation at home. Thus, at times, he felt like an invisible man.

He coughed lightly, finally breaking the silence:

"Of course, we're not just offering a verbal apology, we admit our mistake. To express our regret, Laura and I are willing to give you five percent of our shares in the Bryant Group each."

Ten percent stake in the Bryant Group?
