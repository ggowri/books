Chapter 548 He Is Drunk

Elliott had arranged to meet Alexis for drinks.

From the video, it was evident that Elliott had already consumed a fair amount of alcohol. Alexis reached out to stop him. "Elliott, don't drink anymore. You'll get drunk like this."

Elliott brushed off his hand. "You don't need to worry about me."

"Why shouldn't I worry? If you get drunk, I'll have to help you back," Alexis said.

"No need for your help. Trevor and my bodyguard are here," Elliott replied.

Bottle after bottle, Elliott seemed unreachable. In the video, his expression was solemn as he continued pouring drink after drink down his throat.

It was rare to see Elliott lose control like this, and it was unclear what had caused it.

Alexis asked him again, "Why are you drinking so much when everything's fine? If you can't let go, why not talk to her instead of drowning your sorrows here?"

Elliott lowered his gaze and muttered, "She... She divorced me and is carrying someone else's child. Alexis, she's so heartless."

Elliott's lips tightened into a straight line, and intense emotions were buried deep in his eyes.

The video froze on his face, showing a desolate expression that looked heartbroken, as if he had been deeply wounded.

Following this, Alexis sent a text message.

Alexis: [Madeline, he truly loves you. I've never seen him like this before. If it's a misunderstanding, it's better to clear it up. Keeping it bottled up isn't good for either of you.]

Reading Alexis's message, I felt a surge of complex emotions.

Our situation had evolved beyond mere misunderstandings and couldn't be resolved with just a few words.

It seemed destined that Elliott and I were not meant to be. He had his dream lover, and I had my own path to walk.

After some thought, I replied to Alexis calmly: [Alexis, it's impossible between him and me. Take care of him and make sure he doesn't drink too much.]

Alexis didn't respond further.

I knew that for someone as composed and sober-minded as him, even a few emotional lines were rare.

Moreover, those who understood our situation knew that Elliott and I had a complicated history.

Glancing at my phone, I realized it was almost midnight. Elliott was still out drinking. Shouldn't he be at home with the pregnant Giselle?

As I pondered, my phone rang suddenly.

It was from an unfamiliar number. Who would be calling me at this hour?

Although puzzled, I answered the call. To my surprise, the voice on the other end sounded very familiar.

"Madeline, long time no see," the person said.

"Who is this?" I asked, astonished.

"You forgot my voice so soon? Your memory is really bad. It's me, Sylvia," she said.

Her laughter echoed through the phone, accompanied by noisy background music.

Sylvia? It had been a long, long time since I heard her voice.

Since being completely ousted by Elliott, I heard she had taken on a suspense-themed TV drama and gone off to the deep mountains to train with the crew. After that, I rarely heard any news about her, not even in the news.

"It's you. What's up? Why are you calling me?" I asked, surprised.
