Chapter 513 Awkward Moments

"I can support you. You don't have to be shy. Just think of me as a nurse from this hospital," I said, stepping forward and immediately putting Quinn's arm over my shoulder.

Quinn's body clearly hadn't recovered as well as he claimed. After a few steps together, I noticed sweat on his forehead.

"Is your wound hurting a lot? Can you manage to walk?" I couldn't help but ask worriedly.

"I'm fine. It's just a little pain."

Quinn pretended to be nonchalant, but each step he took seemed heavy. However, he tried not to shift his weight onto me to lessen the pressure on my shoulder.

Seeing him endure the pain made my heart ache even more. I supported him step by step until we reached the entrance of the men's restroom.

"Wait for me at the door. I'll go in by myself."

Quinn gestured for me to stop. He tried to move his hand from my shoulder and attempted to walk into the restroom.

But as soon as he took a step, he nearly collapsed from the pain, swaying dangerously to one side.

I couldn't help but cry out in alarm, rushing to support him again.

At the same time, I instinctively looked around, hoping to find a man to assist him, but to my surprise, there wasn't a soul in sight.

"Why is this floor completely empty?" I asked suspiciously.

"This floor of the hospital isn't open to the public. Dr. Muller arranged for me to be here so I can rest quietly," Quinn explained.

It suddenly clicked for me. No wonder the fifth floor was so quiet.

"I... I can't hold it anymore," Quinn blurted out, his face flushing red.

I immediately understood what he meant, and my face instantly turned red.

He tried to push me away again, but I was worried he might fall if I let go, so I firmly held onto him.

"I'll help you to the urinal. Go ahead. I won't look," I murmured with a red face.

"I don't know why, but I felt like the atmosphere between Quinn and me had suddenly become extremely awkward," I said inwardly.

"Um... this doesn't seem quite right." Even Quinn, who usually joked around, became reserved in this situation.

"It's okay. It's an emergency. Don't worry about it. Hurry, let's go!" I urged him, seeing his face turn red with embarrassment, knowing he must be at his limit. I then helped him to the urinal.

Quinn began to undo his pants, but just then, even his pants seemed to be against us. The belt was tied into a knot that he struggled to undo.

"Damn it, this hospital even has crappy patient clothes..." Quinn muttered in frustration.

I had already averted my gaze, but hearing him, I had to turn back with a red face.

Seeing that he couldn't undo the knot on his pants with just one hand, I blushed and said, "Don't move. Let me help you."
