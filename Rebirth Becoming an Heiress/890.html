Chapter 890: Rivals in Love are Everywhere

Grey and I were in the middle of a conversation when a burst of laughter echoed through the air.

This laugh was unique, a woman's, not crisp but rather husky. It stood out distinctly in a crowd.

I immediately cast my gaze over.

It's Lisa, she's back again. And what's eerie is that she's wearing a white tracksuit that's eerily similar to mine. However, hers is a knockoff, because there's only one set of this outfit, and it's mine.

If all our previous encounters were mere coincidences, then this time, I'm certain she's blatantly provoking me.

My gaze suddenly sharpens.

Grey gave my shoulder a pat, saying with a meaningful tone:

"I swear, I didn't invite this guy."

"I know."

I nodded, heading in that direction.

Lisa, accompanied by a pretty companion, both donned in golf caps and white lace gloves, were fully equipped and ready to go.

It seems they're here to practice their golf swings, not to compete in a match.

She's now quite the talk of the town in Hoswich City's social scene.

The few men accompanying us had them both encircled, engaged in a lively conversation.

As I made my way over, I happened to catch the raspy sound of Lisa's voice:

"You guys flatter me too much. I'm just from a small family, how could I possibly compare to Mrs. Bryant? I'm just a few years younger than her, but she's far more beautiful than I am. I know my place."

"Ms. Fox, you're being too modest. Your family is the most prominent pharmaceutical dynasty in Onastin, and you're the only daughter. I've heard your father dotes on you immensely. Next time we have a friendly match, you should join us."

"My ball skills are subpar. I can't join you in the game like Mrs. Bryant. But please, stop saying I look like her. I've always been a bit of a maverick. I'm one of a kind, I don't imitate anyone, haha..."

Lisa clearly knew I was nearby, yet she still provoked me. It seems, I must respond.

I pressed my lips into a smirk, stepping forward and positioning myself smack dab in the middle of them.

"Ms. Fox, you and I are birds of a feather. I detest being imitated or followed just as much as you do. There's a saying, 'Imitators are nothing but jesters.' Look at those celebrity impersonators, no matter how hard they try, they can never outshine the real stars. On this, we are in complete agreement!"

I said it with a smile.

However, the bosses present could all sense the aggressive undertone in my words.

Lisa's mental fortitude was impressive, she said without batting an eye:

"Indeed, great minds think alike, don't they? But who would've thought? You and I, miles apart, yet strikingly similar. It's a magical twist of fate, really. Ms. Mayers, if you don't mind, I'd love to become good friends with you. What do you say?"

She knew exactly what these men liked to hear.

Men hate nitpicking the most. Her words make her seem quite generous.

I let out a cold chuckle, never thought she could be so shameless.

"If we vibe well, we'll naturally become good friends. Ms. Fox, if you're up for it, there's more fun to be had this afternoon. Stick around and join us."

Of course, I wouldn't be fooled by her. If I agreed, it would only give her more legitimate opportunities to approach me in the future.
