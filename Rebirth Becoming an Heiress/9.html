Chapter 9 I'm Over Him

I scoffed and looked defiantly at Elliott. "You play around, why can't I? We're a match, aren't we?"

"Shut up!" He said angrily.

"Just leave me be. If you're not up for a divorce, fine. But we're doing our own thing from now," I said coolly.

It was the first time I stood up to him, in front of everyone.

It felt liberating. He couldn't touch my mood anymore. I felt free and relieved.

Men were so selfish. They could mess around and stay out late, but a wife was supposed to just stay home and listen to him, even without winning his love.

"First, you drug me, then you talk divorce, and now you accuse me of cheating?"

Elliott sneered with overconfidence, thinking he had me figured out. He still thought I was just messing with him.

"Think what you want," I said.

I was done talking to Elliott and instinctively tried to pull away from him.

However, he held my waist tightly, making me feel suffocated.

"Go home! Now!"

With a glare, Elliott gave another order. He was overbearing, which could make people tremble with fear.

If it were in the past, I would have been horrified and apologized.

But now, it didn't work on me at all. I smiled indifferently and touched his chest.

"You want me to go home? Okay. But you have to call Sylvia and ask her never to show up again."

I didn't think Elliott would do that. I said that just to challenge him.

Unexpectedly, as soon as I finished speaking, he picked me up.

Before I could react, he carried me on his shoulders. Then, he walked out quickly with anger in front of the others!

Elliott took me straight to his limo.

This was my first time in it after six years of marriage.

He roughly pushed me into the back seat, glaring at me fiercely. I'd never seen him look so dark.

I tried to sit up, but he grabbed my neck and pinned me against the window, nearly crushing it.

"Madeline, remember you're Mrs. Bryant, my wife!"

It was absurd. I never thought Elliott would play the 'husband' card.

He hadn't acted like a husband for years.

Now, just because I did something for myself, he wanted to trap me with this title.

How ironic!

I struggled to pry his hand open, and he finally let go. I caught my breath and glared at him.

"Elliott, when have you ever treated me like your wife?"

He shot back, "Didn't you always want to marry into the Bryants? You should appreciate it instead of flirting with others!"

He looked down on me, a look I once adored but now found revolting. My love for him was dead; he just disgusted me.

"Our marriage is just for show. It means nothing to me," I told him coldly.

I thought he might pick up on my detachment, but he was more arrogant than I expected. He grabbed my chin and touched my lips.

"You're asking for sex? Behave, and maybe I'll consider it," he said coldly as if he was doing me a favor.

It was demoralizing. I didn't care for him anymore; my heart was steady.

"No thanks. I can find someone else. Anyone I want, whenever I want!"

I shoved his hand off and casually tucked my hair behind my ear, my collar slipping slightly.

It was dark in the car, exposing my collarbone and smooth skin to Elliott.

Catching him staring with desire, I knew exactly what he was thinking.

He clearly wanted sex, his eyes filled with lust!

"Madeline, you are..."

Elliott was irritated by me. He was about to curse when his phone rang.

As soon as he saw the caller ID, he frowned but took the call. I was close enough to overhear. It was Sylvia's assistant.

"Mr. Bryant, Ms. Hart cut her wrists. She's getting a blood transfusion now."

"Which hospital? Tell her to see a doctor if there's an issue!" Elliott sounded annoyed.

"Mr. Bryant, please, she wants to see you tonight or she'll kill herself. What should I do?"

"Fine, I'm on my way."

Despite his impatience, Elliott cared enough about Sylvia to go. Before, I would have fought with him over this, trying to stop him from seeing other women. But now, not anymore.

Leaning back, I smiled and said, "So, your lover is threatening suicide? Mr. Bryant, you really have a way with women."

"Don't mock me," he snapped, glaring at me.

"Fine, go see her. I'll leave now and won't bother you," I said.

Feeling like I couldn't breathe, I got up to go.

But then, Elliott grabbed my T-shirt.

"Trying to see them again? No way. Stay here; I'll have Toby drive you back!"

"Elliott, Paige is still with Quinn. I'm worried!"

I wanted to leave, especially since Paige had an accident before, and I feared it might happen again.

"Shut up! You're not going anywhere tonight. Back to Serenity Villa!" Elliott commanded, then stormed off with a stern look.

Toby Santos, Elliott's longtime driver, locked the doors and drove off to Serenity Villa as soon as Elliott left. The doors were tightly shut; no matter how much I tried, I couldn't open them.

I knew better than to keep struggling, so I sat back and thought about Paige, feeling uneasy.

Then, I decided to give her a call. Her phone rang twice before someone picked up.

To my surprise, it was a man's voice, one that sounded familiar.

"She's fallen asleep. Are you home yet?"
