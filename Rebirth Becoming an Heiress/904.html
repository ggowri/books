Chapter 904 Torn Apart

"We're just friends! Right, Quinn?"

Before Quinn could finish, I blurted out, cutting him off.

Quinn was stunned. He looked at me in shock, a complex emotion flashing through his eyes. His lips moved, but in the end, he didn't say anything more.

I didn't know what happened me at that moment.

I knew what Quinn was trying to tell him.

But I realized I was deeply afraid. Afraid the truth would be too much for Elliott. Afraid that if his emotions became unstable, something bad might happen.

Afraid that once certain words were spoken, there would be no turning back.

"Well, at least there’s some comfort."

Elliott looked at me, his eyes filled deeply with a heartbreaking sense of helplessness.

He then said:

"So, as you said, we are divorced now, right?"

He asked me again, tentatively.

I nodded, lowering my head to avoid his gaze.

He stared at me with dark, intense eyes:

"So, no matter how happy we seemed recently, you were just acting, right?"

I paused, then slowly raised my head to look at him:

"Not entirely. There were moments when I was genuinely happy."

Elliott gave a faint smile, one filled with indescribable emotions. He slowly stood up:

"Give me some time. I'll talk to Dr. Jones and Barry to find out what really happened.

But if you ask me how I feel after knowing all this, my only feeling is, Madeline, I sincerely hope you are the one who will never leave me, no matter what happens."

Elliott finished with a slight sigh. Then, he slowly turned around, ready to leave.

My heart felt a sharp pain, like a bee sting.

I watched his back, took a deep breath, but my heart ached terribly for no reason.

Elliott opened the door, his steps unsteady, his tall figure radiating deep sorrow.

I moved my lips and instinctively stood up, feeling an urge to chase after him.

But Quinn grabbed me.

I just watched Elliott drag himself away.

The room quickly fell silent again.

"Madeline, I..."

Quinn tried to say something, but I turned around, a surge of instinctive anger filling my chest. I couldn't help but yell at him:

"Are you happy now? You've told him everything, is that what you want?"

My emotions became restless for no reason.

I knew Quinn was the most wronged and tolerant one in all this, but at that moment, I couldn't stop myself from lashing out at him.

I didn't know what was going on with me. I felt like I was being dragged into an abyss of emotions, not even understanding what I was doing or thinking.

My mind was a chaotic mess, indescribably chaotic.

Quinn was stunned by my anger. He looked at me, his eyes filled with disbelief:

"Madeline, are you blaming me? Are you blaming me for telling him the truth, or for ruining your chance to get back together?"

His last words were spoken so softly, as if hidden with immense grievance, making my heart skip a beat.

"I had my plan. I knew what I was doing. But now, you've told everything ahead of time, and my plan is completely ruined.

I don't know what to do now, or if Elliott's condition would ever improve. Everything is destroyed, do you understand?"

"No!" Quinn’s tone was firmer than ever. He held my shoulders and looked into my eyes.

"I already told you that your plan won't work. Dr. Jones' three-month timeline was just an excuse.

Even if you continued pretending with Elliott, he wouldn't regain his memory in three months. Don't you see?

If you keep this up, you'll only hurt yourself. Madeline, I'm trying to help you move on!"

"I don't need your help! I know what I'm doing!"

I pushed Quinn’s hands away, snapping at him.

"You don't understand. If you keep going like this, you'll fall for him again. Do you really want to repeat the same mistake?"

Quinn shouted at me helplessly, his eyes filled with heartache.

I knew he was looking out for me, but the indescribable emotion in my chest made me refuse to acknowledge his words:

"That's none of your business!"

Quinn was stunned.

"So, I've been meddling too much, right?"

Quinn, who had never raised his voice at me, suddenly spoke louder. He looked at me, his eyes clearly showing anger:

"It seems I've been the one deceiving myself all along. I'm the one who forced myself between you and Elliott. I'm the one who ruined your relationship, and now I'm the one tearing apart your dream, forcing you back to cold reality.

It's all my fault. And I deserved it.

You two love each other, and I'm the third wheel. Madeline, I’m done! If this is how you feel, let's end it here."

Quinn's emotions became more intense than ever. His sudden outburst startled me.

I realized I had been a bit unreasonable. I quickly tried to grab his sleeve:

"Quinn, I didn't mean that. Don't be mad, I..."

But he pushed my hand away. He looked at me, his eyes full of disappointment:

"I'm not angry, just disappointed. It turns out no matter what I do or how well I treat you, I'll never measure up to Elliott. As long as he beckons, you would fall for him at once.

Madeline, this time, I decide to let you go. If you want to get back with him, go ahead. I won't stop you. But I hope you think it through and consider if he's really worth it."

Quinn looked at me and slowly finished speaking. Then, he turned around angrily and started to leave.

"Quinn, I'm sorry, I didn't mean it that way..."

Seeing him like this, I hurried forward to explain again. But Quinn walked so fast, without saying another word to me. Soon, he disappeared from my sight.
