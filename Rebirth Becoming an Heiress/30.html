Chapter 30 Capricious

"You only care about the reputation of the Bryant family. It's fine. I can be the one to initiate the divorce, as long as you convert those two billion dollars into shares for me!"

Austin had mentioned this idea when Elliott and I got married two years ago. He wanted me to get some shares at the Bryant Group.

Unfortunately, I naively believed that once we got married, our possessions would be shared and there would be no need to fight over fame and fortune.

Looking back now, I realize that my past self was really naive.

That was twenty billion dollars. This love was too expensive.

What is even more tragic is that I devoted so much money and the best years of my youth, but in Elliott's eyes, I was nothing.

"It was impossible."

Elliott's response was very straightforward, and I wasn't surprised at all.

"You care so much about Sylvia, why don't you just divorce me and be with her? Perhaps, in the future, you will meet your true love. Why should I hinder your path to love?"

Thinking about Giselle, who would appear soon, I couldn't help but kindly remind Elliott.

Elliott lowered his head, his gaze deep and still elusive, as he said in a faint voice.

"You don't need to worry about this. I am accompanying you to your parents' house now. Tonight, you had to move back to Serenity Villa."

He didn't want to sleep with me again, and I didn't know why he insisted on having me move back to Serenity Villa.

I was speechless. The more he acted like this, the less I wanted to listen to him.

"I wouldn't move back, don't force me, Elliott."

"I'll say it one last time. You moved back to Serenity Villa. Madeline, you better not challenge my bottom line!"

He frowned, clearly he had already reached the edge of anger.

The driver, Toby, had already started driving. The vehicle was speeding along the winding mountain road, and the eerie atmosphere inside the car made me angry as well.

In the past, I had already sworn that I would never let Elliott manipulate me again.

Everything I did had to be based on my willingness.

I would no longer fear his tyranny, and I would no longer care about his thoughts!

"I wouldn't go back, even if you tied me up and forced me to go back, I would still go to my parents' house!"

I also reiterated my bottom line and my tone was even more resolute than his.

Elliott gave me a cold glance. I couldn't describe his expression.

In short, his gaze was terrifying and icy, as if all patience had been completely worn away, sending chills down one's spine.

He suddenly yelled at Toby, "Toby, stop the car!"

Toby was startled, and the entire body of the car shook for a moment. However, he immediately reacted and applied the brakes, bringing the car to a halt.

"The thing I hated the most in my life was a woman who acted willfully."

Elliott coldly dropped a sentence and, without waiting for me to say anything else, directly grabbed my collar and threw me out of the carriage.

I fell to the ground in a sorry state. The stone by the roadside hurt me terribly.

The biting cold wind echoed mournfully in the valley. Despite it being midsummer, the nighttime temperature in the mountains remained cool.

I couldn't help but shiver as I sat up from the ground, and Elliott's car had already driven away.
