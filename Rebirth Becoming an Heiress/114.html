Chapter 114 Elliott Is Not a Man

The thunder continued to rumble, and I, like a trembling little lamb, huddled helplessly in his arms, afraid to move.

Elliott gently comforted me. Sensing my fear, he suddenly lightly touched my forehead.

When I was a child, I was afraid of thunder. My mother would also hold me in her arms and comfort me softly.

At this moment, in Elliott's arms, I felt a sense of security like never before.

The thunder continued, but it seemed to be getting quieter. His warm embrace made me feel drowsy. Soon, I, who was extremely tired, fell asleep.

When I woke up again, it was already the next morning.

I discovered that the new blanket I brought had fallen on the ground, while I was covering myself with Elliott's blanket. There were still traces of him lying next to me. However, he had already gotten up.

Did I sleep in Elliott's arms all night last night?

Was he so good? Was he willing to patiently comfort me?

I rubbed my eyes, sat up from the bed, and felt moved.

Upon reflection, I began to doubt if Elliott was even a man. I lay in his arms, wearing a thin nightgown, and to my surprise, he didn't do anything to me. Instead, he held me and fell asleep.

Did my figure not make him desire me to such an extent?

Do adult men not easily have physiological reactions?

Doesn't he see any charm in me at all?

I couldn't help but lose confidence, and a sense of frustration arose. At the same time, I quickly comforted myself.

It's not that I lacked charm, but Elliott wasn't a man.

Yes, it must be like this.

I gritted my teeth and got out of bed. I was so angry that I almost knocked over a bottle of toner in the bathroom.

I didn't forget what I had to do today. After getting ready, I quickly put on makeup. Then, I searched for a long white dress with floral patterns from the wardrobe. I casually tied my hair up in a bun and sprayed it with hairspray before heading downstairs.

In my memory, Laura liked my gentle dressing style, she thought it made me look virtuous.

So, this floral dress was bought by me in the past specifically to cater to Laura's preferences. However, I haven't worn this dress yet because I have had fewer opportunities to meet Elliott's parents.

I walked down the stairs and Elliott was already waiting for me downstairs.

He wore a casual outfit, a navy blue turtleneck sweater, and light gray pants. His hair was meticulously groomed. He sat there, looking like an international supermodel.

Seeing me, Elliott said, "Let's have some breakfast first. Then, we'll leave."

He sat in front of the laptop, furrowing his brows, possibly dealing with official matters.

On the dining table, Sean had prepared a glass of fresh milk, a slice of bread, and a serving of salad for me.

I nodded and walked over to finish my breakfast. Afterward, I went out with Elliott.

The Bryant Villa was located in the old town area of Hoswich, in the former busiest district of Hoswich. Its surroundings were filled with ancient buildings.

Elliott's parents were nostalgic. Despite having multiple properties in Hoswich, they still preferred to live in the Bryant Villa. Because of its old age, every time I stepped into that place, it felt like stepping into a castle.
