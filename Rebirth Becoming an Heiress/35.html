Chapter 35 Compromise

"It was. You went too far. Don't take advantage of your family's influence to bully a woman like this!"

"This is too much. Elliott, if you ever treat Madeline like this again, we will have to force her to divorce you no matter what she thinks!"

My two friends became even more emotional when they saw Elliott give in.

"Since Elliott came over, he must have realized that he was wrong. Why don't we leave them alone for a while?"

Quinn made a suggestion slightly.

Paige and Fiona glanced at each other and then looked at me, waiting for me to decide.

I actually had nothing to talk about with Elliott. Being left on the mountain road by him tonight made me very sad.

I remained silent. Unexpectedly, Elliott spoke.

"I bring night snacks for her. Leave us alone. I have something to say to her."

Elliott raised the takeout box in his hand. Only then did I notice that he had brought something with him.

"I'm also hungry. Let's go eat something."

Quinn said casually. He shook his head, gesturing for Paige and Fiona to leave the ward with him.

"Madeline, take a rest. If you feel uncomfortable, feel free to call me. I'll be nearby."

Fiona was still a little worried about me and repeatedly reminded me. Then, they left the ward.

Elliott closed the door of the ward and walked towards me.

He opened the takeout box and a delicious smell hit my nose. I was very familiar with this smell. It was the sandwich from a restaurant that I really liked. That restaurant was not far from Serenity Villa.

If my memory served me right, this was the first time Elliott had bought me night snacks. Moreover, he knew what I liked, which was beyond my expectations.

"Eat some. It must have been a tough tonight."

He spoke in a calm tone, and it was impossible to tell whether he felt guilty or not. He handed me the sandwich.

I actually didn't have much appetite. When I saw him and remembered how he left me alone on the mountain road, I still felt angry. So, I turned my head away.

"I'm good. I'm not hungry."

"I didn't expect such a thing to happen."

He saw that I wouldn't eat it, so he put the sandwich aside. His flat tone sounded like he was justifying himself.

"Like you care. I never really meant much to you."

When I said this, my heart still ached. Perhaps this was the aftermath of being hurt too many times.

"I can let you do that script."

He finally compromised, sounding like he was using that script to redeem himself.

It was ridiculous. I had already signed the contract for that drama. His opinion didn't matter.

I didn't look at him but just snorted coldly. Then, I turned my head away, not wanting to look at him anymore.

Unexpectedly, he reached out and forcefully pulled my body towards him, forcing me to turn my head and meet his gaze.

"I thought about it. Actually, things should have been easier between us."

He looked into my eyes, and surprisingly, there was a hint of sincerity in his gaze. Was he trying to shake hands and make peace with me?

His eyes were like black holes with mysterious power. When we locked eyes, my heart skipped a beat.
