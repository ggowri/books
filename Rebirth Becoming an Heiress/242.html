Chapter 242 Preference

However, Cormac shook his head.

"You've done the check-up, that's enough. Elliott mentioned you're busy now, so why don't both of you go attend to your own matters? Giselle can keep me company here."

Cormac suggested, his eyes reflecting a fondness for Giselle that he had never shown towards me.

Curious, I tentatively asked Cormac, "Grandpa, it seems like you and Giselle get along well."

"I get bored in the countryside, and Giselle often takes time out to keep me company. She's thoughtful and filial, unlike Elliott, who used to spend time with me as a child but seems to have forgotten me as he grew up," Cormac complained. He was smiling, not blaming Elliott.

"I've been busy these past few years, it wasn't intentional neglect towards you, just..." Elliott tried to explain, feeling helpless.

"I understand, a man must prioritize his career. I don't blame you. Giselle is like my own granddaughter, always bringing me treats," Cormac said.

Then he looked at Giselle affectionately, surprising me with the depth of his feelings.

I thought, "For me, Cormac's strong affection for Giselle is not good news.

After all, Cormac's influence and authority within the Bryant family are undeniable. Even Elliott has mentioned wanting to have a child with me for Cormac's sake.

If Cormac were to think that Giselle is right for Elliott, it would be terrible. However, no matter what, I will never allow Giselle to easily take away everything from me and win the love of everyone in the Bryant family like in the previous life."

"Elliott, go about your business. I've taken a week off from the company to spend time with Mr. Cormac Bryant. You can't fire me for that," Giselle said to Elliott.

Her coy demeanor was proof that she didn't take me seriously.

"Alright, don't worry, I won't fire you," Elliott replied calmly.

He then stood up and glanced at me before speaking, "Madeline, leave Giselle here with Grandpa. Let's head back."

With Cormac's request for Giselle to stay and Giselle taking a week off for him, I knew that staying wouldn't win Cormac's favor.

Giselle had already succeeded in pleasing Cormac. I had to come up with other plans.

Nodding, I bid farewell to Cormac, then turned and left the ward with Elliott.

As we exited, I walked ahead, ignoring him completely.

I felt ashamed about the check-up and irritated by Elliott's defense of Giselle.

To my surprise, Elliott caught up and gently held my arm, asking, "Madeline, where are you going?"

"If Grandpa is fine, I plan to head back to Louis City. I've already delayed a lot of work these past few days," I replied.

Sensing my anger, Elliott suggested. "It's almost noon, you must be hungry. There's a classic restaurant nearby, how about I take you there for a meal?"

Glancing at my phone, I realized it was almost noon, and I hadn't eaten much for breakfast, feeling a pang of hunger.

In Elliott's gentle gaze, I sensed a mix of reconciliation and appeasement.

Deciding not to dwell on the issue further, I relented.

"Alright, let's go."

I nodded and agreed to join him for lunch.
