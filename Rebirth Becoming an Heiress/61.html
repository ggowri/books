Chapter 61 Bitterness

Quinn blinked at me. Then, he picked up one of the glasses of champagne and handed it to me.

My mood tonight, indeed had an indescribable... bitterness.

So, I picked up the glass and without hesitation, I tilted my head back and drank it all in one gulp.

This champagne is truly a rare find from the auction. Its taste is indeed good. It drinks sweetly. Its flavor is delicious and authentic.

After I finished a glass, I poured another one. Immediately, I drank it all in one go. When I was about to pour the third glass, Quinn quickly stopped me.

"Drink slowly. Although this wine is sweet, it is easy to get drunk."

"Quinn, were you reluctant to let me have a drink?"

I glanced at him. I knew very well that he didn't mean it, but I still deliberately said it.

Quinn instantly let go of my hand. He pointed to the rows of expensive wines in the cabinet behind him, his face full of smiles.

"Madeline, I don't have much else here, but the most I have is alcohol. Tonight, as long as you want to drink, you can drink as much as you like. I'll accompany you."

Quinn, on the other hand, was also generous.

He didn't say much anymore. He just accompanied me, drinking glass after glass of alcohol.

Gradually, I became a little drunk. This floating feeling intoxicated me. No wonder many people like to drink when they are in a bad mood. It turns out that alcohol really can temporarily make people forget about their unhappiness and depression.

Quinn, as the club owner, lived up to his reputation. He was very skilled at playing drinking games.

He taught me how to play a drinking game. Afterward, he brought out dice to play a points game with me. He tried his best to make me laugh heartily. Unknowingly, I drank a lot of alcohol. I was completely drunk.

"Quinn, what did you say Giselle had going for her? Why did Elliott like her so much?"

"Madeline, who did you say Elliott liked?"

"Quinn, I am clearly not inferior to her in any way. Why does Elliott never pay attention to me?"

"Quinn, you said in this world, is it possible that there are some loves that I am destined to never have?"

"Quinn, do you think Elliott would love Giselle to the extent of being willing to sacrifice his life, just like before? Would he love her to the point of completely disregarding everything else?"

***

I gradually drank too much. Even though I was asking Quinn, I seemed more like muttering to myself. I no longer knew what I was saying.

When people are feeling upset, tears seem to come out of nowhere. I clearly told myself not to be sad for Elliott anymore. However, my tears couldn't help but surge out.

All the painful memories from the previous life, like fragments, cruelly inserted into my heart. I was in so much pain that my heart felt like it was bleeding. I was almost suffocating.

"Madeline, what do you mean by that? How did you know what Elliott was like before?"

Quinn supported my shoulders. He loudly asked, puzzled.

I really wanted to answer him. But at that moment, my brain was foggy. I was swaying all over. And in front of me, Quinn suddenly had several more faces. But I couldn't see any of them clearly.

"Quinn, I wanted to go home."

I stumbled towards the door.

"You were already drunk. Come, I'll carry you."

Quinn's warm large hand grabbed my wrist. Immediately, he crouched in front of me. Soon, I felt like I was swinging on a swing, swaying back and forth on his back.

My stomach was very uncomfortable. I felt an indescribable discomfort. I had no strength at all. I just let him carry me forward.

I heard my phone ringing continuously.

But when I picked up my phone and saw the name "Elliott," I didn't want to answer the call at all.

I forcefully threw my phone away.

The phone fell onto the floor and made a loud "thud" sound.
