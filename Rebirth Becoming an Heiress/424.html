Chapter 424 Past Lives

"Has Catage already been hit by this virus?"

Upon hearing Quinn's words, I immediately tensed up.

In my memory from the previous life, this virus began to spread widely during Christmas. Due to people returning home, it quickly spread from individual provinces to the entire country.

"I wonder how far Elliott and Marcus got in their last collaboration. Now, Marcus is my neighbor. I must make good use of this opportunity to expedite collaboration with Marcus."

I said, "We already have our own production workshop. Do I still need to stock up? I've already had our workers work overtime to produce masks."

I winked mischievously at Quinn. At that moment, he seemed to understand something and smiled, "It seems like you and I both have foresight. Madeline, do you think we were enemies in our past lives?"

Quinn's casually spoken words made my heart skip a beat.

"Past lives. Why would he suddenly bring that up? And especially while we were just discussing the virus and masks."

I looked at him, stunned, unable to speak. Seeing me frozen, Quinn mischievously snatched my hat off.

"I was just teasing you. Look at you taking things so seriously."

With that, he deliberately tousled my hair, pushed the cart, and ran off.

"Quinn, damn it! Give it back to me!"

I immediately chased after him, cursing as I ran.

Being with Quinn, I found that I could really let my guard down completely, almost like turning back into a carefree kid.

We goofed around in the supermarket for a while. Finally, we managed to gather all the groceries my mother wanted us to pick up, and then we headed back to my place together.

I helped my mother cook while Quinn sat in the living room with my father, watching the news and chatting about current events. Before long, my mother had prepared a feast.

Just as the food was ready, the doorbell rang.

I hurried over to answer it, only to find Marcus standing there, holding two bottles of wine and smiling at me.

"You said to come over for food, so here I am, shamelessly showing up. It's been so many years since I've had Lily's cooking. I've been missing it. I hope I'm not intruding?" he said.

"Of course not. You're right on time. We were just about to eat," I smiled, quickly ushering Marcus inside.

Years had passed. My parents were pleasantly surprised to see Marcus and hurriedly invited him to sit down.

My father couldn't have alcohol. To everyone's surprise, my mother joined Quinn and Marcus that afternoon for a drink.

We gathered together at the end of the year, and the atmosphere was particularly relaxed and harmonious, making this meal feel meaningful.

Because it was our first day in the new house, I took a few photos and posted them on Instagram without thinking much about it.

I didn't capture Quinn and Marcus in the photos, just the delicious food.

What I didn't expect was that after posting the photos on Instagram, Elliott called me within less than a minute.
