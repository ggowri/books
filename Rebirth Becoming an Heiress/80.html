Chapter 80 Accompany Me to Appreciate the Moon

I didn't expect Elliott to accompany me to the rooftop to admire the moon tonight.

I thought to myself, "In the past two years, I invited him every year, but back then, he would just sneer at it and never wanted to accompany me."

"The moon is really rising from the east now..."

I thought to myself and said, "Okay, good idea."

So, after Toby dropped us off at Serenity Villa, we went straight to the top floor.

Serenity Villa was a modernly designed small villa that the Bryant family specifically prepared for our wedding. The villa was conveniently located on the halfway up Bunera Hill. According to Sean, there was a river in front of it and Bunera Hill behind it, making it a place with breathtaking scenery.

When we first got married, I was full of anticipation for a happy life with Elliott. From the first day we moved in here, I created a small garden on the rooftop, planting flowers and a lawn. I also set up a swing, along with a unique small round table and two reclining chairs.

I thought that when I was not busy, Elliott could also lie with me and enjoy the beautiful scenery around Serenity Villa, overlooking the entire cityscape of Hoswich.

However, I was filled with joy as I arranged everything, but Elliott never once thought about stepping onto the rooftop.

In his eyes, everything I did was like a joke, and he didn't like it at all.

This made me very frustrated, and later I never went to the top floor again. I knew what the top floor had become now.

Elliott and I pushed open the glass door on the top floor. I had thought that it would be overgrown with weeds up there, but to my surprise, it was well-maintained and organized. Moreover, the flowers I had planted before were all in bloom. The entire rooftop looked like a miniature sky garden, incredibly beautiful.

A few days ago, I came up here on a whim and found that it was beautifully decorated. Sean told me that you usually take care of it.

Elliott stood behind me, his words revealing a hint of approval.

I haven't been here for almost a year.

It seems that Sean, who is attentive, often comes up to take care of these plants, which is why this place can maintain such a state.

"Since we are going to appreciate the moon, let's prepare some red velvet cake and a dried fruit platter, and make a fruit platter as well."

I turned my head and said to Elliott.

"Okay."

He readily agreed.

So, I immediately went downstairs and rummaged through the cupboard to find the pine nuts, cashews, and raisins that I had bought before. I put them on a beautiful plate and took out the cantaloupe, cherry tomatoes, and grapes from the refrigerator. I washed them separately and put them on the fruit plate. Then, I assembled the cake that I had brought from my parents' house and the red velvet cake that Laura had sent over. Sean and I carried it together to the top floor.

We placed these things on the small round table, and at that time, Elliott was already sitting on the lounge chair, leisurely playing with his phone.

Sean and I placed the small round table in between two recliners. After completing all of this, I quietly gave Sean a thumbs up, and he responded with a warm smile before heading downstairs.

I lay down on another lounge chair, lifted my head, and looked at the silent sky.

The moonlight was exceptionally beautiful, with the moon resembling a huge silver plate, hanging high in the sky. A cloud covered half of the moon like a thin veil, making the whole Hoswich appear as if it was wearing a silver coat.

The gentle breeze accompanied by the fragrance of flowers blew slowly, and this tranquil scene made me feel as if time had stopped.

Neither Elliott nor I spoke. We lay on the rocking chair like that, as if neither of us wanted to break the rare tranquility.
