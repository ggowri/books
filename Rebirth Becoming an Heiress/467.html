Chapter 467 I Want to See You

There were basically no secrets between us, so she had known about my pregnancy for a while.

"Being a single mom is still better than being looked down upon in a family. Besides, the Bryant family has really gone too far. You know, Giselle has been flaunting her pregnancy everywhere."

Fiona said again, somewhat irritably.

"Just because that woman is flaunting everywhere, now that Madeline is pregnant, she can't lose to her even more. Moreover, Elliott's heart is still with Madeline now, so I think she still has the upper hand."

Paige protested, feeling indignant.

"Stop arguing, you two. I've already made up my mind to get divorced."

At this point, I had slowly detached myself from the emotions of a moment ago. After calming down, I reiterated my determination.

Upon hearing my words, Fiona immediately raised her hands in support.

"Madeline, it's great that you've made up your mind! I don't want to see you wavering for this man anymore. A truly reliable man wouldn't keep his partner in such a state of uncertainty!"

Paige couldn't help but sigh again.

"But, Madeline, you need to think it through. Once you're divorced, you'll have to live alone with the child. That will be very difficult."

"I'm not afraid."

I said calmly, feeling more determined.

However, just then, my phone suddenly rang.

I picked up the phone, only to be surprised to find that it was Elliott calling.

Looking at the name on the phone screen, my once firm heart suddenly became confused again.

After hesitating for a while, I still answered the call. And the moment I picked up the phone, I heard Elliott's extremely hoarse and low voice.

"Madeline, I'm sick..."

I gasped for breath, my heart tightening, but I involuntarily responded coldly, "If you're sick, go see a doctor. I can't cure you."

"Where are you? I want to see you."

His hoarse voice came again, accompanied by a few violent coughs. It sounded like he was really sick.

I clenched my lips tightly. Every cough of his made my heart tremble. Despite that, I deliberately maintained a distance.

"You should focus on getting better."

"I want to see you, Madeline."

He repeated this sentence again, with an unprecedented urgency.

With the video Paige had just shown me, I could imagine what he looked like now lying in the hospital bed calling me.

I felt soft-hearted again, but just then, Fiona snatched my phone directly. Without waiting for me to say anything, she immediately hung up the call.

"Stop hesitating. Since you've decided to move forward, Madeline, stay firm."

Fiona said, afraid that I would become entangled again, and made the decision for me directly.

"Okay."

I nodded and grabbed the coffee on the table, which had cooled down, and drank it in one gulp. It was only then that I realized how bitter the coffee was.

...

We chatted in the coffee shop for a long time before we finished.

I drove home.

My mom called to say she was making roasted sandwiches for dinner and asked me to come home early to help. I happily agreed.

But as soon as I parked the car at the door, I saw two people getting out of a black commercial vehicle parked at my door.

I was stunned for a moment.

I realized that these two people, one wearing a patient gown with an IV drip on his hand, was Elliott, and the other was Barry, who was holding the IV bag high for him...

Even in his condition, he came to wait for me at my doorstep?

Was he crazy?

I felt very confused and just stood there in a daze...
