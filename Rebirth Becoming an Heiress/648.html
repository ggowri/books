Chapter 648 Deliberately Provoked Him

My phone screen was very bright. Elliott must have seen the word "Quinn" displayed on it.

I picked up the phone and pretended to be affectionate as I said, "Hello, darling, what's up? Why are you calling me so late?"

I knew Elliott was very upset. But it didn't matter, so I made him even more upset.

Elliott used to receive calls from other women in front of me, and even intentionally flirted with other women.

Now, it was my turn to provoke him.

Quinn was very surprised.

"Darling? I know you're happy, but do you have to be this happy? Is everything done?"

"Yup. Where shall I celebrate?" I smiled, with a playful tone.

Elliott was still standing by the window, motionless. I glanced up and saw that his expression was very serious.

"Sure, you can come to my club. Do you want me to pick you up?"

Quinn's tone also involuntarily rose.

"No need, I'll drive there. Just wait for me over there, see you later, love you!"

I pretended to be coquettish on the phone and then hung up.

Outside the car, Elliott stood like a statue. However, I noticed that his fists were clenched tightly, as if he was holding back a rage that had nowhere to be released.

"So, are you going to meet Quinn now?"

Elliott's voice became cold and increasingly low and hoarse.

"Yeah, we were getting married soon, you should have wished us well."

I deliberately provoked Elliott by saying things he didn't want to hear.

He was indeed provoked, and then he bent down to stick his head in. Suddenly, he reached out and grabbed my chin, revealing a fierce expression.

"Wish? How can you say such words? Do you really not care about my feelings at all?"

I had expressed it so clearly, but I didn't expect Elliott to still be so persistent in wanting to verify.

It was hard for people to accept that the person who loved them didn't love them anymore.

I reluctantly untangled his hand and glanced at him.

"Elliott, I have answered this question too many times in the past, and this is the last time. Listen carefully. I didn't care about you."

I spoke decisively and didn't care about his feelings at all.

This also made me feel a sense of indifference, which was surprisingly refreshing.

In the past, I did so much for him. I put in so much effort, yet Elliott hurt me with the sharpest words, tearing apart all of my emotions.

Back then, he had the same indifferent attitude as I do now, right?

It felt so good when I didn't care about him.

Elliott was shocked.

He bent over, staring at my face, his sharp gaze like that of a cheetah, as if he wanted to tear me apart at any moment, making me instantly feel a little scared.

I instinctively tilted my head backward.

To be honest, I was afraid that Elliott would suddenly attack me and force me like before... Luckily, he didn't do that.

Elliott suddenly laughed, as if he had figured out a lot of things and moved on. He self-mockingly said, "It seems that I was too arrogant and sentimental. Madeline, I understand now."

He suddenly got out.

Before I could react, Elliott had already turned around and quickly walked towards the direction of the hotel.

I watched his tall figure until it disappeared.
