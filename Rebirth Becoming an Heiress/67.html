Chapter 67 She Just Loved You

This speech, as if it came from the depths of my soul, was a protest and a cry.

I clearly wanted to laugh. I never wanted to show even a hint of vulnerability in front of him. However, at the moment when I finished shouting, I suddenly realized that tears were streaming down my face.

Just as I shouted out those words, all the grievances I have endured over the years, all the anger I have suppressed within me, were released little by little.

I clenched my hands tightly and bit my lip. Tears streamed down my face as I gazed at the man standing before me with a proud posture. My heart, riddled with countless wounds, felt as if it was bleeding.

Those abscesses that had accumulated in my heart in the past, at this moment, all burst open. In an instant, pain spread throughout my whole body.

Elliott froze.

He looked at me thoughtfully. Then, he instinctively approached me. He reached out his hand, wanting to wipe away the tears on my face.

However, I instinctively stepped back like a wounded animal facing a hunter. I shook my head.

"Don't come over. I don't need your pity or sympathy. Elliott, I used to love you. That was my own business. Now, I don't love you anymore. That's also my own business. In the past, I was the one who kept this marriage together. Now, I will be the one to end it. I don't regret that I once loved you. But listen carefully, from today onwards, I am no longer the person I used to be. I will not love you even a bit! Elliott, my love, is dead. Do you understand?"

My tears flowed like a raging flood. I shouted while stepping back.

I knew that at that time, I was probably seen as a lunatic in the eyes of others. Countless patients or their family members who came and went probably thought that I had escaped from a mental hospital.

Only I myself knew what I was doing.

My legs were getting weak. My body was trembling incessantly. My tears were falling down like broken beads. I let out all the last suppressed emotions from my heart.

And at the same time, I also found redemption in such cries.

Elliott was clearly shocked. He probably never expected me to shout these words at him.

He stared at me blankly. When I saw a flicker of pain on his face, I almost thought it was my imagination.

He saw my vulnerability and sadness. So, he instinctively walked up to me. He reached out his hand again, wanting to support me.

However, I quickly waved my hand. I desperately stepped back. I desperately tried to keep my distance from him. At the same time, I desperately tried to calm down my excited emotions.

I wiped away the tears from my face. Afterward, I looked at him solemnly and reiterated,

"Elliott, I am seriously proposing a divorce to you, please consider it."

Our marriage no longer needed to linger on in agony.

At this moment, I was willing to let go. I only wanted to get divorced from him as soon as possible. I wanted to regain a new life.

When he looked at me, his lips moved. He wanted to say something, but in the end, he didn't say it. He just stood there, silently watching me.

Perhaps it was our arguing that startled Giselle. Somehow, Giselle and Quinn found themselves standing at the door of the hospital room.

When Quinn saw that I seemed unable to stand steady, he couldn't hold back any longer. He rushed over, bypassing Elliott, and immediately grabbed me for support.

"Elliott, have you ever thought that these past few years, which you have disregarded, were the most beautiful youth for a woman? She only loved you, but she didn't commit any crime. You shouldn't treat her so harshly!"

Quinn looked at Elliott and said these words, one by one. Afterward, he turned around and firmly grabbed my arm. He firmly said, "Let's go."

"Madeline, we left!"
