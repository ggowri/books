Chapter 321 Injured

In the midst of the scuffle, some one took a nasty bite at Elliott's arm when he wasn't looking.

That man definitely bit hard. Now, Elliott's arm has a bright red bite mark around it, and blood is oozing out.

"I'll grab the first aid kit and clean you up." I said, rushing to get our kit from the house.

I pulled out the iodine and cotton swabs, ready to disinfect, but he stopped me.

"You haven't answered my question," he kept wondering why I always turned to Quinn first whenever something happened.

But as I looked at him, I didn't know how to respond.

I called Quinn because I consider him my most trusted friend, that's the only reason.

But Elliott seemed to have the wrong idea about me and Quinn.

"Was it because you were with him when I called?" I asked, puzzled why he showed up before Quinn did.

But then he suddenly lifted my chin. As he looked at me, there was a hint of sadness in his eyes. "That doesn't matter. What's important is I want to know, where am I in your heart now?"

Where was him in my heart?

This question was really hard for me to answer for a while.

If he had asked me this before, I would have told him without hesitation that I loved him and that he was the love of my life.

But now, I couldn't say those words anymore. But one thing I was sure of, he was still important to me. And so far, I hadn't fallen in love with anyone else but him.

"Family, maybe?" I finally said after pondering.

After three years of marriage, we were family.

As for whether he was my lover, I was not sure right now. Because I didn't know what I meant to him, could he end up falling for someone else like he did with Giselle in our past life and abandoned me again?

My response made Elliott's head hang low.

"Family..." he muttered softly, followed by a self-deprecating laugh.

I had never seen Elliott like this before. The cold smirk on his face seemed especially desolate under the cold, white light of my home.

"Let me disinfect it for you first." I said, gripping his arm and attempting once more to apply iodine.

However, Elliott brushed my hand away. He stood up straight, his voice cold as he said, "No need, I'm leaving."

"But your arm is still bleeding, Elliott," I instinctively exclaimed.

He just glanced at me coldly and said, "I'm bleeding inside too, not just my arm."

With that said, he left without another word.

I stared at his retreating figure, the weight of his words lingering heavily on my heart.
