Chapter 902 Confrontation

I glanced behind my mom.

To my shock, I saw Quinn Woods, dressed in black sportswear, standing right behind her.

He must have heard about Noah's fever and came over early in the morning.

In that moment, my mind went blank.

"Elliott Bryant, who gave you the nerve to come for my Madeline! Have you forgotten what you did to her?"

Seeing Elliott, my mom's emotions surged. She pointed at him and couldn't help but scold him harshly in public.

Her loud voice immediately drew the attention of many passersby.

Seeing this, I hurried to my mom's side and whispered:

"Mom, calm down. It's not good to cause a scene. People are watching. I'll explain everything to you later. Come home first."

As I spoke, I dropped tons of hints to my mom with my eyes.

After years together, my mom and I had known each other very well. She saw that I had something I couldn't say openly, so she suppressed her anger, glared fiercely at Elliott, and then at me:

"I'll go back, but you better explain everything later! Quinn, let's go!"

After shouting, my mom turned and walked away, not forgetting to call Quinn to follow her.

Elliott still stood there, not moving an inch. But through his terrifying gaze, I could imagine the turmoil raging inside him.

Quinn was also surprised. He looked at me, then at my mom, planning to say something but perhaps fearing it would deteriorate Elliott's condition.

In the end, he turned away silently.

Just as he turned, Elliott suddenly made a move.

He swiftly stepped in front of Quinn and grabbed his collar:

"Didn’t you owe me an explanation for this?"

Quinn looked at him, surprisingly calm:

"I have nothing to explain. You should ask your doctor what's going on, not me."

"What do you mean, Quinn Woods?"

Elliott, suffering from amnesia, didn't understand what Quinn was referring to.

I hurried to interrupt:

"Quinn, stop! Go home with my mom. I'll talk to Elliott."

Quinn looked at me, and at that moment, his eyes were filled with disappointment and loneliness that I had never seen.

As if he had already made up his mind, he took a deep breath and calmly said:

"Why don't we have a talk? If it keeps going like this, I'll go crazy. Madeline, no matter how much his amnesia had gone, I think we need to tell him the truth."

"What truth? What amnesia? Quinn Woods, why are you bringing these up?"

I got emotional, never expecting Quinn would choose to reveal everything now.

Elliott's treatment wasn't done. Dr. Jones said his condition was improving, and with a bit more time, he could fully recover.

Revealing everything now could harm him deeper. If the blood clot in his brain dislodged and endangered his life or turned him into a vegetable, how could I ever live with that for the rest of my life?

I tried hard to stop Quinn. But days of tolerance perhaps had pushed his emotions to the edge.

He would respect my decisions in usual, but this time, he insisted on his own.

"Madeline, he's an adult. He just has temporary amnesia. I believe once he knows everything, he'll make his own judgment and won't lose control of his emotions."

Quinn said slowly. Then he pulled Elliott's hand off his sleeve and looked him straight in the eye:

"Let's go. Find a place to talk about your condition, and the relationship between you, me, and Madeline. How about that?"

"What amnesia? What condition? Quinn Woods, what are you talking about?"

Elliott looked completely confused.

"I'll explain everything to you later. Or, if you don’t mind, we can talk here."

Quinn said in a serious tone.

"Quinn, you..."

I saw that Quinn was determined to lay everything out for Elliott. For a moment, I didn't know what to say to stop him.

Quinn walked up to me, placed his hands on my shoulders, and looked at me with firmly:

"Madeline, acting in the show is too exhausting for everyone. Most importantly, it's too hard on you. Since we've all run into each other today, let's just lay it all out."

Quinn had put everything on the table. I knew that even if I wanted to keep hiding the truth, Elliott wouldn't believe me anymore. He would definitely find the truth.

"Alright, let's go to that nearby café."

I nodded reluctantly. Then, I walked over to my mom, handed her the breakfast I had bought, and signaled her to go back.

My mom didn't say anything more. She felt that something was off, so she grabbed the breakfast and stormed back into home.

The three of us headed to the nearby café.

As if fate was playing a trick, the café was named "Encounter," the same name as the flower shop Giselle Cole used to run.

The three of us sat down face to face.

Elliott quickly took the seat next to me, while Quinn sat across from us.

We each ordered a cup of coffee.

I looked down at my phone and sent a message to Dr. Jones to have an ambulance wait outside the café, just in case something happened to Elliott.

"Say what you need to say. Start now."

After a long silence, Elliott pursed his lips and glanced at Quinn, starting the conversation calmly.

"Alright, let's start with the incident that caused your unconsciousness. We've all been hiding things from you, but I think with your sharpness, you must have known something was off, right?"

Quinn said calmly.

"I did find it strange. Many things didn't match my memories—work, relationships, even Madeline's attitude towards me. So, what's really going on?"

Elliott remained calm, clearly prepared to find out the truth, everything.

Quinn and I exchanged a glance. Then, I took a deep breath and said to Elliott:

"It all started like this..."
