Chapter 79 Change of Mind?

Elliott looked at me helplessly.

"What purpose could I have? Madeline, when did you become like this? Weren't you the one who used to long for me to spend more time with you?"

"Before was before, now is now. I believe we made it very clear that day at the hospital."

I didn't like him bothering me like this. I thought our relationship had already ended.

I thought to myself, "On that day, I expressed myself loudly and clearly enough for Elliott to hear, so why is he deliberately saying these ambiguous words to me now?"

Elliott leaned back in the car seat. He let out a slight sigh and pinched his nose with his hand, appearing somewhat tired.

He said, "My mom called and said you prepared gifts for them today, I thought maybe you had changed your mind."

Did I change my mind?

I didn't know what his words meant.

I said lightly, "I didn't mean anything else. I just feel that we are still a couple after all, your parents have been nice to me, and it's a holiday today, so I should give them a little gift."

As soon as I finished speaking, Laura suddenly called me.

She, like Elliott, rarely called me. In previous years, even when Elliott didn't bring me back to meet them for various reasons, they never called me proactively.

Seeing the phone number, I was slightly taken aback and quickly answered the call.

"Laura, Happy Thanksgiving! Do you need something from me?"

"Madeline, thank you for the Mid-Autumn Festival gift you gave me. I really liked it. Elliott said he went with you to your parents' house, so please say hello to them for me."

Laura's tone today was particularly polite, with laughter interspersed in her words, perhaps due to the effect of the gift.

"I haven't usually given you anything, so don't mention it. Alright, I will convey it to my parents."

"Your dad also said that the taste of this cigar is good, thank you. I just had someone deliver a box of handmade red velvet cakes to you and Elliott. Remember to have them for dinner tonight."

Laura smiled on the other end of the phone and I quickly responded with a "thank you". After exchanging a few pleasantries, we hung up the phone.

I didn't expect that two ordinary gifts could make Laura so happy.

I had never given them gifts in the past. On one hand, I felt that they had everything and they might not like the gifts I gave. On the other hand, I always felt that they treated me poorly at that time, so I didn't want to give gifts to them.

But now I gradually understand that as long as I work hard, some relationships can actually be slowly brought closer, it's just that I didn't understand it in the past.

"Today is Thanksgiving, actually, we didn't have to be so serious, isn't it nice to celebrate the festival happily?"

At this moment, Elliott suddenly said lightly.

"Why did you want to do it like this?"

I turned my head to look at him and noticed that he seemed a bit tired lately, his deep eyes becoming even more gloomy.

The colorful lights of the street outside shone on his face, and the contours of his profile became even more perfect in the moonlight. I couldn't help but marvel at his face, and his figure, which were indeed impeccable and full of attraction.

"Hey, don't compromise again just because of his good looks..." I almost couldn't resist, but luckily, my rationality timely reminded me in my mind.

"Our rooftop terrace at home was the perfect spot for moon gazing."

Elliott turned his head and looked at me, murmuring. His voice was gentle, making it hard for me to refuse.
