Chapter 829: A Trio's Journey

I refuse to believe this is a coincidence.

My gut tells me, Lisa must have found out about Elliott's flight details first. That's why she booked the same flight, even managing to get a seat right behind him.

She just didn't expect me to show up, throwing all her plans into disarray.

She's really into Elliott, isn't she?

By the time we rolled into Hoswich City, it was already seven in the evening.

Just touched down and as I switched on my phone, a message from Quinn popped up: "I'm waiting for you at the airport exit, ready to take you home."

Instinctively, I wanted to bolt from the plane, but inadvertently, my gaze met Elliott's.

His eyes were filled with scrutiny, it was clear he had seen who the message was from and what it contained.

He was quite miffed, "This kid, he's getting more and more disrespectful."

"Don't be so harsh, he meant well," I instinctively defended Quinn.

He had bought a ticket for a flight that was scheduled earlier than mine, and by now, he must have been waiting outside the airport for nearly an hour. I couldn't bear to turn him down.

However, Elliott had already seen the message. Knowing his character, he wouldn't let me meet Quinn alone.

Elliott chuckled, "In that case, we really can't let his kindness go to waste. Since Barry hasn't made it to Hoswich city yet, I'll ride with you in his car."

Elliott was gearing up for another demonstration.

But if Quinn and I haven't clarified our feelings yet, I think it's okay. However, having said what I did to Quinn, pretending to be in love with Elliott in front of him seems a bit cruel to Quinn.

"Let's hail a cab, shall we?" I suggested nonchalantly.

"Ride with me," Lisa, standing behind us, offered. "My driver's outside, and we've got plenty of room in the van."

"I've been meaning to ask Ms. Fox out for dinner. What do you think, Madeline?" Elliott sought my opinion.

"Sure," I agreed. I figured more time with Lisa wouldn't hurt, and it would give me a chance to dig a little deeper into who she really is.

I shot Quinn a message, letting him know a ride was coming for me and he should head back first.

Afterwards, Elliott, Lisa, and I collected our luggage and strolled out through the VIP exit.

Elliott kept his arm around my waist the whole time, or held my hand, his gaze never leaving me.

His constant gaze was making me uncomfortable, yet, every time I caught a glimpse of Lisa's crestfallen expression, I couldn't help but feel a smug satisfaction.

We hopped into Lisa's business van, zooming all the way into the city.

Elliott invited us to the most renowned seafood restaurant downtown, even securing a private room for us.

A lavish seafood feast was swiftly served up, covering the table in abundance.

Elliott had a fine bottle of red wine delivered on purpose. Lisa and I sat on either side of him.

Luckily, he was aware of the awkwardness of the situation, so he called over Philip and Alexis.
