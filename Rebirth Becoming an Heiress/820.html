Chapter 820: Taking Him to the Hospital in the Dead of Night

"Elliott!"

This sudden scene scared the hell out of me.

The moment I saw Elliott hit the ground, I immediately dropped to my knees, frantically tugging at his clothes.

"Madeline, I... I was drunk, I didn't mean to. I genuinely thought he was faking it..."

Witnessing this scene, a somewhat sobered Quinn clumsily crouched down beside me, stuttering out an explanation.

"Enough talk! Now, get Barry over here, pronto! And rush Elliott to the nearest hospital!"

I could see something was off with Elliott, so I quickly yelled out in alarm.

It was then that it hit Quinn. He quickly sprang to his feet, bolting towards the door while calling out Barry's name loudly.

"The nearest hospital is about 30 kilometers from here. Mrs. Bryant, please step aside. Let me check on Elliott first. I have some basic medical knowledge."

Jane crouched down at this moment. First, she flipped Elliott's eyelids upwards, then she felt his breath. After that, she started pressing against Elliott's chest.

At this point, Barry had already rushed in, all flustered.

Seeing Elliott collapsed on the ground, Barry frantically pulled out a bottle of medicine from his bag and thrust it into my hands. He was so nervous, he was stuttering:

"Mrs. Bryant, use... use this. Dr. Jones instructed that if Mr. Bryant ever falls into a coma, he should take this medicine as quickly as possible."

Upon hearing Barry's words, I quickly instructed Jane to halt her actions and immediately fetch a bowl of water.

Next, we pried open Elliott's mouth and shoved the pill in, following it up with a swig of water. We watched as he swallowed it down.

"Barry, given his condition, does he need to go to the hospital?"

Watching Elliott swallow the pill, my frantic nerves finally settled. Then, I found myself asking again.

"I just got off the phone with Dr. Jones. He said as long as Elliott's breathing is steady, he should just lay low and rest. Take his meds. No need for a hospital visit."

Barry chimed in again.

We're on the outskirts of Edulet City, a place remote and unfamiliar to us. Despite Dr. Jones' reassurances, I can't help but feel uneasy.

What if something unexpected happens to Elliott in the middle of the night? What would be the best course of action then?

So, after discussing with Barry and Jane, we decided to drive Elliott to the hospital together.

Quinn was already three sheets to the wind at this point, but when he heard us talking about heading to the hospital, he insisted on tagging along.

However, after some persuasion on my part, he finally retreated to his room for some rest.

Barry fired up the car, taking me, Jane, and Elliott on a ride to the nearby hospital.

Jane was familiar with the surroundings. Under her guidance, we quickly drove to the hospital, checked into the emergency room, and briefed the doctor on Elliott's condition.

This is nothing more than a small hospital.

It was the dead of night, and the hospital was running on a skeleton crew. Upon hearing the situation, the doctor simply arranged for an available room, suggesting that Elliott stay for observation for a while.

Elliott, unconscious in the hospital, was somehow more reassuring than at home.

The three of us maneuvered Elliott into the vacant hospital room. Afterward, I had Barry take Jane home.

After all, those two drunkards in the Little White Building needed someone to look after them at night. I felt more at ease once Jane returned.

Alone in the hospital room, I found myself sighing softly as I watched Elliott lying still on the sickbed.

I thought I was in for a peaceful night's sleep, but little did I know, a series of unexpected events were about to unfold.
