Chapter 240 Feeling Sorry for Me

I hesitated for a moment, then answered the phone, "Hello, what's up?"

Elliott's voice sounded unusually calm as he said, "Are you busy? If not, come to the hospital. Grandpa says he needs to talk to us."

I thought, "Grandpa wants to see us? Didn't I just visit Grandpa with Mom?" But since Grandpa has never reached out to me before, there must be a reason this time."

"Alright, I'll come right away," I quickly replied.

After told my mom, I drove back to the hospital.

Pushing open the door to Cormac's room, I was surprised to find Elliott, Giselle, and Nicole inside.

Perhaps due to my recent slap on Giselle's face, as I entered, the looks from Giselle and Nicole were particularly fierce, especially Nicole.

Her usually friendly face now appeared gloomy and almost terrifying.

Spotting me, Elliott motioned for me to join him.

With a smile, he informed Cormac, "Grandpa, Madeline is here."

"Grandpa, I passed by a famous beverage shop just now and brought you an original milk and a chocolate milk. Which flavor do you prefer?"

I raised the cups of milk I had bought from the shop and approached Cormac, smiling.

"Is it sweet? I don't like it too sweet," Cormac asked, trying to sit up.

Elliott quickly placed a large pillow behind him.

"It's slightly sweet. I asked them not to put in too much sugar. Would you like to try it?" I replied, opening the lid.

"Sure." Cormac nodded.

I placed a tissue under Cormac's chin and fed him the milk slowly.

I had always been distant with Cormac before, never thinking of doing such things.

However, after learning about Giselle's efforts to please Elliott's family, I decided to change my attitude and get along well with every member of the Bryant family.

"Madeline, I called you and Elliott here today to tell you that you two need to hurry up. I'm getting older, and all I wish for now is to see your children born in my lifetime," Cormac said with a smile after finishing the milk.

I was prepared for Cormac's urging for us to have kids, so I smiled and replied, "Alright, Grandpa, Elliott and I will do our best."

"Sometimes effort alone isn't enough. Today, I've arranged for you both to see Doctor Louis, the most authoritative expert in this hospital. You need to have a check-up to see if both of you are fit for having a child. Doctor Louis is hard to book, so you must agree to this request," Cormac said.

I hadn't expected Cormac's reason for calling us to the hospital to be this, especially in front of Giselle and Nicole.

An elderly person asking for such tests undoubtedly implied that he believed we had some health issues due to our long time without children.

Feeling extremely embarrassed, I wanted to disappear at that moment.
