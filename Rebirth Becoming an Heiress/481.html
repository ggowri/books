Chapter 481 Recovery

After Elliott had finished the medicine, Marcus advised, "Lift his head to prevent reflux and vomiting, which could be uncomfortable for him. If the medicine works, he should start sweating soon."

Following his instructions, I grabbed another pillow and placed it under Elliott's head.

Just as I was about to leave, Elliott suddenly grasped my hand and hoarsely pleaded, "Don't go..."

"I'm not leaving. I'll be sitting right across from you." I gestured to the sofa opposite, reassuring him softly.

Only after hearing my words did Elliott release my hand. He was visibly exhausted as he closed his eyes and drifted back into a slumber.

As Marcus predicted, soon enough, beads of sweat began forming on Elliott's forehead, followed by a profusion on his face.

It seemed the medicine was truly effective.

As long as Elliott continued sweating, his high fever would gradually subside.

I felt a surge of joy witnessing this progress.

"Marcus, he's sweating. Looks like the medicine is working wonders!" I exclaimed.

"Indeed, his constitution is strong. Targeted treatment is key. Once the fever subsides, he should be in the clear!" Marcus replied, relieved and smiling.

"That's fantastic! We don't need to test on anyone else. We can confidently produce this medicine in bulk. It could benefit many patients!" I expressed my delight.

"If Mr. Bryant knew we used him as a guinea pig, I wonder if he'd blame us when he wakes up," Marcus said.

Watching Elliott's complexion gradually regain color, and his lips regain their rosy hue, I couldn't help but speak up.

"This is actually his good fortune. He's lucky to have met you tonight. Otherwise, he would have suffered from this virus for much longer."

That night, Marcus and I barely slept as we tended to Elliott.

Once he sweated profusely, his clothes were drenched. Marcus fetched a set of casual clothes, and together, we wiped his body and changed his attire.

After rechecking Elliott's temperature, Marcus confirmed his fever had subsided. Clearly more comfortable, Elliott finally drifted into a peaceful sleep.

Exhausted from the night's events, I initially sat by his side but eventually succumbed to sleep.

Upon waking up, I found myself nestled in Elliott's embrace. As I stirred, he immediately opened his eyes.

"Are you awake?"

Elliott's voice was still hoarse but noticeably improved from the previous night.

Startled, I quickly extricated myself from his embrace and rubbed my eyes, asking, "How did I fall asleep? Are you feeling better?"

"I'm much better. My body doesn't feel as heavy anymore," Elliott replied before sitting up effortlessly on the sofa.

He appeared rejuvenated, no longer the sickly figure from before.

Glancing around and then down at his clothes, Elliott seemed about to speak.

At this moment, Marcus entered the room with a tray from the kitchen.

"Mr. Bryant, you seem to be doing much better. Perfect timing, I've made some cereal. Both you and Madeline should eat something."
