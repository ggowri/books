Chapter 536 To Meet His Parents

"What's up? You woke me up so early. I'm half asleep." I grabbed the phone, still groggy from sleep.

"My parents would like to meet you and have invited you over to our villa for lunch. I'll pick you up." Quinn's direct statement hit me like a bombshell, instantly clearing my mind and jolting me out of bed.

"What are you talking about? Y-Your parents want to meet me? For what? Are they going to negotiate with me? Quinn, don't mess around!" I said.

"It's fine. It's not as scary as you think. My parents knew about it and wanted to meet you." Quinn's tone was extraordinarily gentle compared to my agitated tone.

"But we are obviously not a couple. There's no need for me to meet your parents," I said to the phone after calming down a little.

"Don't be too nervous. Take it as a normal dinner and a visit to a friend. My parents are very nice. Don't worry. They won't give you a hard time," Quinn said with a smile on the other end of the phone.

Even though he made it simple, I still felt something strange.

​It all happened so fast that I was not even mentally prepared for it.​

"It's better to put it off. Tell your parents that I've been tied up with things lately. Once being free, I'll treat them to dinner, okay?" After a moment's reflection, I still did not think it very proper, and politely declined.​

"But my parents went shopping early this morning, and the kitchen is bustling with activity now. If you don't come, how am I going to explain it to them?" he said.

Unexpectedly, Quinn mentioned that his parents had already started preparing the lunch. At that moment, I was compelled to say yes.

"But it's really inappropriate. We are well aware of what's going on between us, aren't we?" I said.

I was all of a sudden torn up inside.

"I said it was okay. Madeline, you don't have to be psychologically pressured. I'll arrange everything. Rest assured. I'll never let you suffer any embarrassment," Quinn reassured again and again.

Hearing Quinn's words and reminding myself of how good he had always been to me, I could only steel myself to agree.

After I hung up, my drowsiness dissipated, prompting me to rise from bed and meticulously choose an outfit from my closet. After much deliberation, I settled on a white satin dress paired with a fox-collar tweed jacket, complemented by flat-pointed shoes and flesh-toned stockings.

Adorned in this manner, I stood in front of the mirror, quietly scrutinizing my reflection. Then, I curled my hair with a curling iron, put on light makeup, and wore some relatively simple jewelry before walking downstairs with my bag.

Observing my choice of attire for the day, which leaned towards elegance rather than smart wear, Lily inquired, "Madeline, what's the occasion for dressing up like this?"

"Mom, I won't have lunch at home but will meet a friend," I said.

To avoid worrying Lily, I opted not to disclose the truth and instead told a white lie before swiftly departing.

Quinn came quickly. By the time I walked out the door, his car was already waiting quietly at the curb.

It was likely his first time seeing me dressed so elegantly. From a distance, he immediately got out of the car, approached me, and sized me up before smiling, "Hey, today, your outfit is quite formal. You put in some effort."

"Considering it's lunch with your parents, I can't afford to look sloppy. But Quinn, I'm really kind of overwhelmed," I said.

​It was all so sudden that I was still a little apprehensive.​

"It's okay. Trust me. My parents are known to be good-natured." Quinn reached over and took me by the shoulders. Without further ado, he opened the car door and gentlemanly let me sit in the car.
