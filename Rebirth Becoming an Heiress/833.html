Chapter 833: The Necklace

Elliott chuckled lightly, looking at me with a hint of helplessness. "Madeline, we're husband and wife. The way you're talking, it's as if I'm some sort of lascivious villain in your eyes."

His voice was full of grievance, and I couldn't help but laugh: "Well, you are."

Now, when I'm alone with Elliott, I'm always on guard. I've hardly ever laughed in his presence.

Suddenly, I let out a laugh, and Elliott joined in.

"Madeline, it's been a while since I've seen you smile. You look so beautiful when you do. And with this, you'd look even more stunning."

After speaking, Elliott suddenly produced a deep blue, square-shaped jewelry box.

"What is this?" I blurted out, my surprise evident.

Meanwhile, my heart started to pound uncontrollably. There was a subtle anticipation building within me.

No woman can resist a surprise. So, upon seeing this box, I can't help but feel a sense of anticipation, wondering what could possibly be inside.

I was somewhat taken aback by my own reaction.

I should be feeling disdain, yet for some reason, that's not what I'm feeling at this moment.

Elliott flipped open the jewelry box, revealing a necklace that shimmered brilliantly within. The pendant, shaped like a begonia flower, was unlike anything I'd seen in stores, yet it was incredibly beautiful.

"In your diary, you mentioned your fondness for begonias. On the day you were born, the begonias in your family's garden bloomed unexpectedly. So, I had this necklace custom-made for you, with your name engraved on the back. This one-of-a-kind piece was designed by the renowned French jeweler, Char."

Elliott chuckled lightly.

Next, he pulled out a necklace from the box, placing it before my eyes.

The carriage was dimly lit, yet even in the subdued light, the necklace still shimmered with a subtle glow. The pendant, in particular, was of an exceptional shape and quality, exquisitely crafted. It was clear at a glance that it was a product of a designer's meticulous work.

I instinctively glanced at the back of the pendant, finding the name "Madeline" etched into it. It was indeed custom-made for me.

I've heard of the renowned French jeweler, Char. He's quite the big deal. Your average Joe can't just hire him. The cost for a custom design from him is through the roof.

Elliott, for my sake, was willing to go all out.

For a moment, I was overwhelmed with excitement. My usually calm heart was suddenly unable to find its peace.

"May I put it on for you?"

Elliott caught on to my liking. Subsequently, he unclasped the necklace, wrapped his hand around the back of my neck, and adorned me with it.

Elliott's fingers were a touch too cold, sending an involuntary shiver down my spine as they brushed against my skin.

The air in the car was stagnant and somewhat stifling. At this point, Barry cranked up the air conditioning.

The sudden chill in the air cooled my fevered mind in an instant.

Like snapping back from a dream, my sanity returned in an instant.

What's happening to me?

All Elliott did was stick up for me at the dinner table and gift me a necklace.

I was actually moved?

Just like that, all the past pains were forgotten?

Am I about to fall into Elliott's trap again?

I cursed myself thoroughly in my mind.

Just as Elliott was about to clasp the necklace around my neck, I yanked it off in an instant, saying coldly:

"Such a precious gift, how could I possibly accept it? I can't take it!"
