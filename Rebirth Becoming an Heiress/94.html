Chapter 94 Dinner

"Can I call you Madeline?"

Giselle smiled, like an innocent little white rabbit.

I thought, "Once she found out that I married Elliott, she started getting closer to me. What a cunning woman!"

I put on a fake smile.

"Okay. I didn't mind what you called me."

Giselle explained, "You and Elliott were very much in love. I'm sorry. I didn't know you were in Elliott's office."

I had actually forgotten about this little incident, but Giselle reminded me of that awkward scene.

I said, "It's okay. Elliott and I have been married for a long time. It's inevitable for couples to have some intimate moments. It's the same when you're in a relationship, right?"

"I heard before that your relationship was not good. It seems that those media were spreading rumors."

Giselle kept asking questions about my relationship with Elliott.

I didn't know what Giselle wanted to do unless she wanted to find out the truth from me.

"The media likes to spread rumors. Elliott and I don't need to pay attention to these rumors, right?"

I pretended to be friendly and repeatedly emphasized that Elliott and I were in love in order to make Giselle give up coveting Elliott.

"Yes. Elliott was really great. Madeline, you were lucky to be able to marry him."

Giselle did not hide her envy.

I actually really wanted to tell Giselle that she didn't have to envy me because she could quickly replace me and take away everything from me.

I politely smiled but remained silent because I didn't want to discuss this boring topic anymore. In doing so, Giselle had enough room for imagination.

The elevator finally reached the first floor. I had never realized that the time spent riding the elevator could be so long.

I took a deep breath and immediately walked out. Giselle, who was right behind me, noticed that her phone was ringing.

I heard Giselle answering a phone call behind me.

"Rowan, what did you say? You stayed in Hoswich and joined March as a legal assistant? Oh my goodness! Is it true? You previously said no law firm would hire you!"

It was Rowan.

Quinn successfully convinced Rowan to stay in Hoswich. Rowan even managed to become an assistant at Alexis' law firm.

Rowan should have said something because I immediately heard Giselle yell.

"Did your dad agree to you buying a house in Hoswich? Great! Shall we go and see the house now? Come to my flower shop and pick me up, and we can go together!"

Giselle was very happy.

Giselle didn't even say goodbye to me as she bounced past me with her phone in hand. She swiftly left the Bryant Group.

I thought, "Rowan not only found a job, but his dad also promised to buy him a house in Hoswich." This is the best news for Giselle, who has always wanted to settle down in Hoswich.

Giselle was too naive. Clearly, Rowan's message reignited her hope and made her abandon her determination to break up with Rowan.

Great! We are one step closer to breaking up Giselle and Elliott.

Seeing Giselle run far away, I inexplicably felt extremely happy and sang all the way to my car.

I was just about to drive when Elliott suddenly called me.

After I answered the phone, Elliott suddenly asked, "Did you leave?"

"I was still downstairs. What's wrong?"

"I have never had dinner with you outside. Let's have dinner together."

Elliott suddenly issued an invitation.
