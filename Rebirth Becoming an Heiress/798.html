Chapter 798: The Breakfast He Made Himself

"She's passed away. Let's not dredge up the past. Sean, take good care of yourself, alright?"

I responded nonchalantly. I had no desire to utter that name again.

"Wow, you've lost a lot of weight, Ms. Mayers. I heard you were expecting... Where's the baby?"

Sean cast a glance at my belly, then cautiously asked.

From pregnancy to childbirth, I didn't really gain much weight. Plus, after giving birth, I maintained a regular diet and didn't fancy greasy food, so overall, I didn't put on much weight. In fact, I'm a bit lighter compared to before I got pregnant.

When Sean suddenly brought up the subject of children, I was at a loss for words.

Just then, a message from Elliott popped up, saying he'd whipped up breakfast and I could head downstairs to grab a bite.

I didn't respond to Sean's words, instead, I shifted the topic. I filled Sean in on Elliott's current situation, urging him to take extra care of Elliott.

Moreover, I was concerned that Sean might misconstrue my intentions, thinking I wanted to reconcile with Elliott. So, I made it clear to him that my current communication with Elliott was solely for the purpose of aiding his medical treatment.

After explaining it to him, I headed downstairs.

Downstairs, Elliott was busy setting a surprisingly lavish breakfast on the table. I took a quick peek and found an array of treats - pastries, purple yams, perfectly fried sunny-side-up eggs, and two glasses of milk.

The fried egg he cooked this time looked really appetizing. Compared to before, he has truly made a lot of progress.

It seems that after we parted ways, he must have been cooking for himself quite a bit.

"Take a seat and dig in."

Seeing me descend, Elliott flashed me a warm smile.

I nodded, settling myself at the dining table. I took a sip of the milk, finding it at just the right temperature... When did he start paying such close attention to these little details?

We sat down, face to face. I could feel his gaze on me, so intense that it made me eat with a certain restraint.

I pinched off a piece of pastry and gave it a taste, then devoured a poached egg, and finished off the milk in my cup. Then, I stood up and said to him:

"Thanks for the breakfast, I'm heading out for work now."

Elliott rose to his feet in an instant, his slender fingers naturally crossing over his chest. He lifted his gaze and asked:

"Will you be coming home tonight? If you are, I'll come back early to prepare dinner for you."

I was taken aback for a moment.

Between making breakfast and cooking dinner, it's as if Elliott has become a whole new person, isn't it?

I shook my head, "I'll be heading to my folks' for dinner tonight. My mom's got my meal all planned out. So, I won't be coming back."

"Oh," he said, a hint of disappointment clear in his voice, "It's been a while since I've seen your folks. How about I bring some gifts and pay them a visit tonight?"

I didn't see it coming, him making such a demand.

Isn't this a tough one for me?

If my folks found out I'm back with Elliott, they'd probably break my legs.

However, he's currently suffering from amnesia. For a moment, I was at a loss for words, unsure of how to respond. So, I found myself dodging the question, saying:

"Let's do it another time. Tonight, I want to have a chat with my parents about the Glory Group. It's not convenient for us to talk about it with you around."

"Alright then... I'll swing by your place tomorrow morning and we can head to work together."

I thought Elliott would have given up by now. Surprisingly, he's been pretty persistent.

I couldn't help but slightly furrow my brows, saying:

"No need, I have my own car. It's more convenient for me to drive to and from work."

"Madeline, why do I get the feeling that you're resisting being with me? You weren't like this before. Did I do something wrong?"

My repeated rejections left Elliott puzzled, a hint of hurt lacing his tone.
