She thought as she ran, "With my current power, halting time for three minutes is the best I can do.

"Within those three minutes, I must get Osborne out of here. That should be enough!"

Ophelia moved with incredible speed. The entire base of Aberrants was immobilized, its inhabitants frozen like statues, their time arrested.

Their thoughts, too, were suspended.

Only an attack triggering a reflex or a power vastly exceeding Ophelia's could break the temporal halt. Or they would never break the halt or even realize it.

Only Ophelia could move freely, slowing the flow of time around her to accelerate her own speed.

Her speed was 100 yards per second, but the slowed time doubled her speed.

This ability to manipulate time flow, sounding easier than time freezing but actually more challenging, was a skill she mastered only after breaking into the Class AAA tier.

Time freezing, in contrast, was the easiest for her.

Slowing time by half was already her limit, a secret even Gerardo didn’t know.

Ophelia actually had six minutes for rescue, though even that was a tight squeeze.

"Faster! I need to go faster!"

Ophelia pushed her limits, swiftly reaching the Human Slave cultivation room, where everyone stood still, unaware of her approach.

Inside, she found Osborne in a massive cultivation tank. Her gaze icy as she assessed him.

" 93% enslavement? Good, I'm just in time. If it weren't for me, you'd be Gerardo's slave!"

Without wasting more words, she opened the tank, lifting Osborne out.

She quickly took him away.

A tall figure materialized in the void after she left.

It was Isa, whose face showed shock and disbelief.

She thought, "It was her all along?

Why her?

I should have known that!"

Her expressions fluctuated, torn and hesitant, her eyebrows knit tight before she sighed, "Perhaps, it's fate."

Isa's face regained its usual calm as she said, "It's surprising, Ophelia, that you'd save Osborne. It's quite risky for you now. Why are you doing this, for love or hate?"

"But why the rush? His enslavement was only at 93%. Wait until 99%; you're wasting the remaining energy.

"It's a good opportunity for him to improve."

Dismissing her thoughts, Isa reached out, causing the tank's pale red solution to boil.

The remaining 25% of its energy was extracted by Isa, turning the liquid transparent.

She looked at the hundreds of other tanks. "Well, you've saved Osborne and spared me the trouble of showing up.

Let me do you a favor in return. Such enslaving methods are too wicked to exist."

With a surge of energy, Isa shattered all hundreds of cultivation tanks.

Isa waved her hand, and all the people in the shattered tanks vanished without a trace.

The next moment, Isa herself disappeared as well.

The dozen or so doctors in white coats and the Human Slave guards guarded at the entrance remained frozen in place, so was the time.

Meanwhile, Ophelia raced toward the exit, dragging Osborne along with her.

Getting him out of the cultivation tank was just the first step; she needed to get him to a safe place, far from Gerardo's reach.

If Gerardo discovered Osborne was missing, he would undoubtedly hunt Osborne down, and that would lead to Osborne being captured again.

"Come on! Come on!" Ophelia urged, pushing her speed to the limit.

In just two minutes, she burst out of the Aberrants' base and arrived a cave half a mile away, shoving Osborne inside.

"Hmph! This is how far I can help you!

"This cave is well hidden; they shouldn't find you here. You better not let Gerardo catch you again because I'll be coming for you soon!

"I will let you know that I, Ophelia, am more than worthy of you!

"It's you who is not good enough for me!"

Ophelia fixed her gaze on Osborne for a moment and left.

She had to get back quickly; there was no room for error. Gerardo could never find out what she had done.
