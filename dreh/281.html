Chapter 282

Morning light spilled through the thin curtains and stretched across the couch.

Charles opened his eyes slowly.

He groaned, sat up and ran a hand over his face. He stood and walked toward the bed.

Empty.

He was surprised to see that the girls were gone. He checked them everywhere but could see them.

Maybe they left early to avoid awkward goodbyes. He did not overthink it.

He showered, dressed in a clean shirt and dark trousers.

He stepped out of the room and headed downstairs. He left the lodge.

In one dim corner of the bar across the street were the two girls.

They were putting on a mischievous smile.

Collins leaned forward across the table. “Were you able to get the pictures?”

One of the girls pulled out her phone and slid it toward him.

“Yes.”

He picked it up and scrolled.

Image after image.

Carefully angled shots. Charles asleep on the couch. What the picture contained was able to destroy Charles' life.

If this got spread, Charles would never recover from it.

Collins’ lips curled into a slow grin. “Wow. You are better than I thought.”

He handed the phone back. “I will give you a bonus.”

The girls' smiles widened.

In the hotel, Charles stepped out of the building and walked toward the hotel parking lot.

Just as he was about to step inside, a sharp voice cut through the air.

“CHARLES.”

He stopped.

Slowly, he turned.

Tracy stood a few steps behind him.

She was dressed as boldly as ever, confident posture, heels clicking lightly against the pavement.

Charles frowned slightly. “What are you doing here?”

She crossed her arms. “Long time. I have been coming here for weeks. Your botches would not even say a word to me.”

He sighed. “Do not call them that. I have warned you.”

She rolled her eyes. “Okay, okay. I know you will always defend them.”

“What do you want, Tracy?” he asked, voice firm.

She stepped closer, lowering her tone. “Relax. I am not here to fight.”

He said nothing.

“My company anniversary is in two days,” she continued. “It is a big one. Investors. Press. Influential people.”

“And?” Charles asked flatly.

“And I want you there. As a man of honour. Special guest.”

He studied her carefully.

Charles scoffed lightly. “Man of honour? You actually mean that?”

Tracy nodded without hesitation. “Yes. I want you there.”

He looked at her like she had just told a joke. “Why would I be there? And why exactly do you want to use me as a man of honour?”

She shifted slightly, her voice softening. “Because you deserve it. We both built that company together and… I just want to thank you.”

Charles frowned. Appreciate him?

When they were married, she never used a single day to acknowledge him. Not once. Every achievement had been hers alone. Every success had her name stamped on it. Now, after divorce, she suddenly wanted to “appreciate” him?

He let out a short laugh. “Well, thank you. I don’t want it.”

Tracy blinked, caught off guard. “I… I just feel like I should acknowledge you. Do you even know what being a man of honour will open for you? The connections. The exposure.”

Charles shrugged. “I don’t care.”

Her eyes hardened. “Do you want to be with those bitches for the rest of your life? Don’t you even think of elevating yourself?”

His expression turned cold instantly. “How is that your business? You already have your fiancé. I never interfered. Go and be with him.”

Tracy smirked slightly. “I know you are jealous of Collins. That’s why you’re mentioning him. Unlike you, he’s a man with value.”

Charles scoffed again. “Value?”

She lifted her chin proudly. “Yes”.

“That’s why we’re leaving the country soon??” Charles intentionally said that to see if Tracy knew about Collins leaving the country

She raised her brow slightly. “Leaving the country?”

She scoffed, “Let me burst your brain. Collins just secured a partnership with a very wealthy man in Dubai. Akeem. The biggest man there. Once this deal goes through, he’ll be insanely rich. So rich you won’t even be able to compare.”

She leaned closer, her voice filled with excitement. “Even Serena, who threw him out, will be on her knees begging him back. He’ll become the richest man in this city.”

Charles stared at her quietly, watching how passionately she hyped Collins up.

But something didn’t add up.

If Collins was about to hit a jackpot and become the biggest man in the city… Why did Charles overhear him arranging a boat to secretly leave the country next week?

He didn’t say it out loud. He simply scoffed, shaking his head slightly. Something was off.

Charles tilted his head slightly. “Really? become the biggest man? Akeem from Dubai?”

Tracy’s eyes lit up immediately. “Yes. In fact, I have FaceTimed him. I searched for him online too. He’s huge in Dubai right now. Very influential. Very rich.”

Charles nodded slowly, keeping his expression neutral. “Well… I’m happy for you both.”

She narrowed her eyes. “I know you are not. Because you can never achieve what he did by following those bitches around.”

Charles’ jaw tightened instantly. “Hey. Watch your mouth.”

She folded her arms, but didn’t respond.

Charles let out a breath, clearly losing patience. “Look, I don’t have time for this nonsense. If you want me at your anniversary, fine. I’ll come.”

Her eyes brightened slightly.

“But,” he added firmly, “I don’t want your man of honour title. Keep it for your fiancé.”

Her expression stiffened.

“I will attend as a guest. Nothing more,” he finished.

Without waiting for her reply, he turned around and walked inside the hotel.

Tracy stood there for a few seconds, heels planted on the pavement, watching his back as he left.

She sighed. She just felt bad for Charles, because to her it was like he was living a fake and useless life. That the ladies were using him.
