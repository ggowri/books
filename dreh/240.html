Chapter 239

Charles woke late in the night.

The room was dim, moonlight slipping through the curtains and casting a pale glow across the bed.

 Natasha and Sapphire were still asleep beside him, their breathing slow and even. As they curled around him.

He carefully slid out from between them and stood up. His muscles stretched as he raised both arms above his head, rolling his shoulders and savoring the quiet. A small smile touched his lips. 

He looked back at the bed. They were still fast asleep.

Gently, he pulled the blanket up over their bodies, tucking it around them so they wouldn’t catch cold.

 For a second, he just stood there, watching them, then shook his head lightly with a soft chuckle.

After dressing, he stepped out of the room and walked toward the sitting room.

The lights were on.

Lizzypie and Gabriella were there.

The moment they saw him, both of them smiled.

Lizzypie leaned back in her chair, folding her arms. “I bet you got your rest very well.”

Charles chuckled in embarrassment as he rubbed the back of his neck. “Uhm…”

Even Gabriella added with a small teasing smile, “Sapphire’s voice was echoing through the house. I couldn’t believe it.”

Lizzypie added, “You didn't even care if we are hearing”.

Charles coughed lightly and quickly sat down on the chair opposite them. “Let’s… change the topic.”

Lizzypie smirked but didn’t press further.

Charles leaned forward, his tone turning serious. “Did you find the diagnosis? What happened to Gabriella’s leg?”

The playful air vanished instantly.

Lizzypie’s expression hardened. “Yeah. I did.”

Charles straightened.

“This isn’t natural,” she continued. “Someone poisoned Gabriella. With a Yasin worm. One of the most dangerous microscopic parasites that specifically attacks nerve clusters in the lower limbs. It paralyzes the legs slowly and permanently if not treated early.”

Silence filled the room.

Charles frowned deeply. “What? But Mr. Belly said she was born like that.”

Lizzypie shook her head. “Obviously not. The traces in her system show long-term parasitic damage. Someone infected her right from infancy. Possibly days after birth.”

Gabriella’s smile faded. Her fingers tightened over the fabric of her dress. “All my life… I thought I was born like this.” Her voice trembled. “How could someone be so cruel?”

A tear slid down her cheek.

Charles’ jaw tightened. “To do that to a baby…” He shook his head slowly. “That’s beyond cruel.”

Lizzypie nodded. “Whoever did this had pure hatred for you. Intentional. Personal. From the very beginning.”

Gabriella’s eyes streamed with tears now.

Charles’ expression darkened further. His mind connected the pieces. “And it didn’t stop there. The fire. The car attacked. Must be done by the same person!”.

Charles added quietly, “Your father might have known who this person is. That’s probably why he asked you to stay here.”

Gabriella’s composure broke completely. She started crying harder, shoulders shaking. “How could someone do this to me? What did I ever do?”

Charles felt a tight pull in his chest but kept his voice steady. “So… is there treatment?”

Lizzypie inhaled, then nodded slightly. “Good news first. I know what to mix to make a good concoction that will kill the worm and the leg will heal gradually .”

Charles leaned forward immediately. “And the bad news?”

“We will need a Red Moon Herb,” she said. “It’s extremely rare. You can’t find it anywhere in this city.”

Charles frowned. “Really? So where can it be seen?”

Lizzypie hesitated. “I can’t say for certain. But there’s a family… the Quill family. They live in Texas. They specialize in ancient medicine. That’s how they built their wealth. They collect rare herbs. If anyone might have the Red Moon Herb, it’s them.”

Charles’ eyes sharpened. “If we find this herb, will Gabriella walk?”

“Certainly,” Lizzypie replied. “The issue is, I don’t even know for sure if the Quill family has it. And even if they do, the Red Moon is rare. They won’t just hand it over.”

Charles sat back, thinking for a moment, then said firmly, “We never know.”

He looked at both of them, determination settling in his expression.

“We are meeting the Quill family tomorrow.”

Gabriella listened to them, but she could barely process the words. She just kept crying softly, repeating how grateful she was.

“If… if you manage to cure me,” she said between tears, “I will forever be grateful. I swear.”

Charles walked over to her without hesitation and pulled her into a firm hug.

“We will,” he said quietly. “I bet you that.”

She held onto him tightly.

After a moment, Charles straightened and looked at Lizzypie. “You and I will go there tomorrow.”

××××

The next morning, Charles and Lizzypie were already prepared. Their bags were packed and waiting by the door.

Gabriella and the other girls stood in the room, seeing them off.

Charles looked at Sapphire and Natasha. “Take care of Gabriella. And inform me immediately if anything happens.”

They nodded.

“Yes,” Natasha said.

“Don’t worry,” Sapphire added. “We will handle everything here.”

Then both of them stepped forward and kissed Charles lightly on the cheek.

“Call us if you need anything,” Natasha said.

They made their way out shortly after.

The flight took just two hours to Texas.

While they were on their way, Charles turned slightly toward Lizzypie. “This Quill family… Are they cultivators too?”

Lizzypie shook her head. “Nope. But they believe such people exist. They focus strictly on ancient medicine. And it works. That’s why they became so rich. They are one of the wealthiest families in Texas.”

She paused briefly. “But they don’t use magic.”

Charles nodded slowly in understanding.

They arrived in Texas just before noon.

The air felt different the moment they stepped out of the airport. Wider skies. Bigger roads. Everything felt louder and larger.

Before heading to meet the Quill family, they decided to rest at a hotel nearby.

As they made their way down the street toward the hotel entrance, something magnificent happened.

Vroom. Vroom. Vroooom.

A convoy of cars rushed in from both sides of the road. Not just two or three. Nearly twenty of them. Maybe more. Sleek black vehicles moving in perfect coordination.

They slowed, then formed a wide circle.

At the center stood a lady.

Charles stopped walking.

He didn’t mean to stare, but he couldn’t help it.

She looked Latina. Slim, but undeniably sexy. Long black hair flowed down her back like silk. She wore a white dress that hugged her shape perfectly. Her breast was modest in size, but round and firm, noticeable even beneath the soft fabric.

Before she could even react, the doors of the cars swung open at the same time.

Men dressed in black suits stepped out swiftly, moving with rehearsed precision.

Then something unexpected happened.

They began unloading roses.

Bundles and bundles of roses. Red ones.

Dozens became hundreds. Hundreds became thousands.

They spread them carefully across the ground in front of her, layering them so thick it began to look like a red carpet made entirely of petals.

It had to be close to three thousand roses.

The scent filled the air instantly.

People nearby stopped to watch.

The lady stood frozen in the center of it all, her white dress glowing against the sea of red beneath her feet.

It was extravagant. Dramatic and Beautiful.

Charles blinked slowly. “Wow, so beautiful”.

Just that, it will take a dark turn soon
