Chapter 257

.

Mr. Orlean started sweating and trembling violently.

If he withdrew now, he would lose the twenty-nine billion dollars he had just bid. Twenty-nine billion. And that Charles had toyed with him by adding only one hundred million at a time. It was deliberate. 

He knew he was doomed.

The auctioneer cleared his throat, trying to steady the charged air. “So, Mr. Orlean… your bid? Are you continuing?”

Orlean gulped. His throat felt dry as sand. He slowly turned to Charles.

Charles was smirking.

Murmurs spread through the hall like wildfire.

“What the hell is happening?”

“Is Mr. Orlean backing down?”

“He spent so much money just to retreat over a plant?”

Cameras zoomed in. Reporters leaned forward. The richest man in the city looked like he had just seen a ghost.

Mr. Orlean swallowed again, his voice cracking. “Please… can I opt out?”

The words detonated in the hall.

“What?!”

“Back down?”

“No way!”

The auctioneer blinked in disbelief but quickly recovered. “Yes, sir. You may withdraw. However, as stated, the full amount of your last bid will be transferred to the opposing party.”

Orlean’s head spun.

Twenty-nine billion dollars. Gone. For nothing.

His legs felt weak. The weight of it crushed his chest. He realized he had gambled a fortune on something he would never possess.

Slowly, unsteadily, he stepped down from his VIP platform and walked toward Charles.

Every eye followed him.

Whispers rose in waves.

“Where is he going?”

Unexpectedly. Orlean dropped to his knees.

The sound of fabric hitting polished marble echoed louder than any bid.

Gasps filled the hall.

“The richest man in the city is kneeling…”

“Is this real?”

Orlean’s pride shattered in front of hundreds of witnesses. His shoulders shook. “Please,” he said hoarsely. “Forgive me. I was foolish. You can have the herb. Please… can I just have my money back? Please.”

His voice trembled like a man begging for his life.

The crowd stood frozen. Everyone was recording it and the humiliation was written all over his face. 

Charles leaned forward slightly so only Orlean could hear him.

His smile never reached his eyes.

“You made me spend money on something I should have gotten for free,” he whispered calmly. “Losing your twenty-nine billion is nothing compared to that. And if you dare expose my identity…”

He paused.

“I will roast your entire life.”

Orlean gulped hard, his eyes glossy, on the verge of tears. He dare not offend Godfather's offspring. 

The auctioneer, still stunned, asked again, “Mr. Orlean… are you continuing the bid?”

Orlean slowly turned his head toward him.

His lips trembled.

He shook his head.

“No,” he said weakly. “I’m out.”

The hall erupted.

“He lost!”

“Mr. Orlean lost to that young man!”

“Who is he?!”

“Why would Orlean kneel like that?”

The auctioneer raised his gavel with shaky hands and slammed it down.

“Sold! The winner is… our young man!”

He did not even know Charles’s name.

The hall remained in shock. No applause. No cheers. Only stunned silence.

Except for two people.

Lizzypie and Caroline clapped enthusiastically.

Charles stood up calmly and pulled out a sleek black card.

The nearest reporters gasped.

“He has a black card?”

“What the hell…”

Even the auctioneer’s eyes widened for a split second before he straightened himself.

Charles handed the card over without a word.

The machine was brought forward. The process began. Staff moved quickly, their hands almost trembling as they handled the transaction. Seconds later, the confirmation came through.

The auctioneer lifted his microphone.

“Ladies and gentlemen… The Red Moon Herb has officially been sold. Final price… twenty-nine point one billion dollars!”

The hall stirred again in disbelief.

Charles gave a faint smile.

Then the auctioneer slowly turned toward Mr. Orlean.

“Sir… it is now your turn.”

Every eye shifted.

Orlean stood stiffly, face pale. He knew he could not argue. The rules had been clear. He had lost.

His fingers shook as he requested Charles’ account details. The information was provided. The transfer process began. 

Minutes later, the confirmation alert appeared.

Twenty-nine billion dollars Transferred successfull

.

Charles glanced at his phone and smiled slightly. “Well received.”

He stepped closer to Orlean.

“Nice doing business with you, Mr. Orlean.”

Orlean did not utter a word. His pride had already been stripped away.

Charles took the secured case containing the Red Moon Herb. Without another glance, he turned and walked out of the hall.

Whispers followed him like a shadow.

“For his account to receive that amount so easily…He’s insanely rich.”

“Who exactly is he?”

Outside,  Charles exhaled the atmosphere.

With the herb finally in his possession, it was time to return home.

Carline stepped forward, her expression sincere. “I’m very grateful. You saved me from Ivan and the Diamond Group’s uncouth behavior. I’m sorry for dragging you into all this.”

Charles scoffed lightly. “It’s nothing.”

He had not expected the herb to come easily anyway.

Carline hesitated, then quickly asked for his contact. “I will  definitely call you.”

Charles gave it without hesitation.

With a final grateful look, she bid them farewell.

Even though she knew Charles was no ordinary man, he never revealed who he truly was. She was curious, but she did not ask. As she watched him leave, something settled in her heart.

She had fallen for him.

And she vowed to see him again.

Meanwhile, inside the car on their way home, Lizzypie glanced sideways at Charles.

“How did Mr. Orlean suddenly find out who you are? That’s the main reason he dropped out.”

Charles looked ahead calmly. “His guard told him.”

She frowned. “How would the guard know?”

Charles’ lips curved slightly. “I ordered Yachino to inform him.”

Lizzypie blinked. “So you planned it?”

“I wanted him to keep bidding,” Charles replied. “After he spent enough, I let him know. I know he will opt out as soon as he knows who i am”

Lizzypie nodded slowly. “That’s cold.”

Then she smirked. “Hmm. Caroline… Don't you think she’s sexy?”

Charles turned his head slightly. “Yeah. She is.”

Lizzypie gave him a knowing smile. “Another virgin for your catalogue.”

Charles didn’t deny it.
