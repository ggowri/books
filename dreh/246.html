Chapter 245

Ivan saw the tension rising and quickly took advantage of it.

“Yeah, yeah,” he said loudly. “I am also suspecting that. He just showed up out of nowhere and paid fifty million dollars? That does not make sense. This is exactly how Caroline would go so far just to reject me.”

Caroline’s face flushed with anger.

“No. Absolutely no!” she snapped. “I did not do anything like that. It is all lies. Go and check the company accounts. Every single transaction is recorded. Nothing is missing.”

She looked directly at her father. “I would never misuse company funds.”

Charles then spoke calmly.

“I used my own money,” he said. “I paid it to stop him from forcing her into something close to sexual abuse.”

The room went cold.

Mrs. Quill’s eyes widened slightly. “Sexual abuse?” she repeated sharply. “Young man, you are taking things too far with that accusation.”

Mr. Quill raised his hand.

“That is enough,” he said firmly. “We will know the truth soon. The finance department will check whether company money was moved or not.”

He then fixed his eyes on Charles.

“But listen carefully, gentleman. I do not care who you are. I do not care whether you paid the money or not. You can never marry my daughter.”

Mrs. Quill added coldly, “Do you know who Ivan’s family is connected to? The Diamond Bank. The richest and most powerful financial family in this city. You cannot even stand at their level. So do not give yourself false hope. Leave.”

Caroline stepped forward angrily.

“I do not care what you are saying!” she said. “You cannot force me to marry someone I do not love. I love him. I will date him, not Ivan.”

Mr. Quill slammed his palm on the table.

“Why are you being so stupid and selfish?” he demanded. “You know very well that only the Diamond Bank family can help us deal with the Yachina Gang!”

Ivan smiled slowly.

“Yes, Caroline,” he said softly. “You keep forgetting about the Yachina Gang. If you refuse to marry me, my family will not speak to them on your behalf. And they will continue disturbing your family business… until you are completely ruined.”

The room grew tense.

The Yachina Gang was no small group. They were vast and deeply rooted across Texas. They controlled underground networks, illegal trade routes, and protection rackets. Many powerful businessmen had suffered under their pressure. They constantly demanded money and interfered with business expansions.

The Quill family had been one of their targets for years. Every new project faced threats. Every expansion was blocked.

Only one family the Yachina Gang never touched, the Diamond Bank family. For some reason, they respected them.

Desperate, the Quills had once approached the Diamond Bank for help. The condition was simple.

Caroline had to marry Ivan. 

Caroline’s anger finally exploded.

“So all you care about is this family business?” she shouted. “Because of the Yachina Gang, you want to push me to marry someone as horrible as Ivan?”

She pointed at him.

“He even had a rape case before!”

The room erupted into loud murmurs.

Mrs. Quill looked shocked. Vivian frowned. Even Mr. Quill’s expression changed slightly.

Ivan shot to his feet, furious.

“I cannot continue listening to this nonsense!” he barked. “If this is how you treat me, I will tell my father not to help your family anymore!”

That statement immediately made the room tense.

Charles, who had been quiet, finally spoke.

“What about this,” he said calmly. “I will handle the settlement with the Yachina Gang myself. If I do that, will you stop forcing Caroline to marry this despicable person?”

For a second, there was silence.

Then—The entire room burst into laughter.

Even Ivan laughed loudly.

“Did you hear him?”

“He wants to deal with the Yachina Gang?”

“Does he even know who they are?”

Ivan wiped imaginary tears from his eyes.

“You?” he mocked. “You think an ordinary person like you can convince the Yachina Gang? Do not be a clown.”

Charles looked at him steadily.

“Who told you I am an ordinary person?”

Mr. Quill shook his head impatiently.

“Stop bluffing and stop making me laugh,” he said coldly. “If you provoke the Yachina Gang, they can kill you. Do you understand that?”

Charles did not flinch.

“Well,” he replied calmly, “you can never know.”

He glanced around the table.

“You have nothing to lose. If I go there and fail, and they kill me, you lose nothing.”

The room went quiet again.

“But if I succeed,” Charles continued, “then it is a win for you. No marriage deal. No dependence on the Diamond Bank. And no more threats from the Yachina Gang.”

Mr. Quill looked around the table as low murmurs spread among the family members.

They whispered to one another, unsure whether to treat Charles as a fool or a madman.

After a moment, Mr. Quill raised his hand and the room quieted.

He fixed his sharp gaze on Charles.

“Are you absolutely sure about what you are saying?” he asked slowly.

Caroline quickly stepped forward.

“What are you doing?” she demanded in a low but urgent voice. “The Yachina Gang kill mercilessly. They are extremely dangerous. This is not something you can just volunteer for.”

Charles looked at her calmly.

“I am confident I can get the deal,” he said.

Ivan laughed harshly.

“I am so fucking sure he cannot do anything,” he said loudly. “I swear it. Do not waste your time and hope on this liar and bluffing bastard.”

Several relatives nodded in agreement.

Charles turned his eyes to Ivan.

“Why don’t we make a bet,” he said evenly.

Ivan stopped laughing.

“A bet?” he repeated.

“Yes,” Charles continued. “If I successfully settle things with the Yachina Gang and stop them from disturbing the Quill family…”

He paused slightly.

“You will stop disturbing Caroline forever.”
