Chapter 188

 

Seeing Jude remain silent, Sapphire’s gaze sharpened slightly.

“I asked you,” she said again, calm but firmer this time. “What is going on here?”

Jude jolted, then immediately pointed at Charles and Serena.

“It’s these two,” he said quickly. “They are disturbing the peace of everyone here. Causing trouble. They came with some proposal I already told them we weren’t interested in, but they refused to leave.”

Sapphire tilted her head slightly. “Really.”

Collins stepped forward at once, eager. “If I may,” he said smoothly. “They almost broke Ms. Linda’s leg just now. That’s the reason Mr. Jude wanted to teach them a lesson.”

Sapphire’s eyes shifted to Serena and Charles. Her gaze was steady, unreadable.

“Is that really what happened?”

Serena’s heart pounded, but she forced herself to speak. “No,” she said. “He didn’t even listen to our proposal before rejecting us and ordering us to leave. He—”

“Stop lying,” Jude snapped, cutting her off. “You are just disappointed I didn’t accept your proposal. You refused to leave after that.”

Sapphire turned slowly toward the reception desk. “Quincy,” she said calmly. “Is that what really happened?”

Before Quincy could open her mouth, Jude spoke again, fast and sharp. “She wasn’t here at the beginning of everything. I’m telling you the truth. These two people are crazy. We should call the police on them immediately.”

Collins nodded eagerly. “That’s the best option,” he added. “This man here is a troublemaker.”

Sapphire’s eyes drifted back to Charles.

For a split second, she almost smiled at him. 

Charles met her gaze and gave a subtle nod to pretend he didn't know him. 

She masked it instantly.

Then Serena spoke again, her voice steady now, edged with quiet anger.

 “So you are saying the problem is just the two of us, right.”

Jude nodded quickly. “Yes. Yes, exactly.”

Collins grinned, victory already written across his face. “Also, he didn't deserve to be here. He is mentally deranged saying he knows you!”. 

Sapphire inhaled slowly, then exhaled as she stared at Charles. 

“You deserve a slap for what you did,” she said coolly.

Linda’s face lit up with satisfaction as she chimed in, “Yes, madam. Slap some sense into him right now.”

Sapphire raised her hand.

The sound echoed sharply through the lobby.

Smack.

The slap rang out across the company floor, silencing everything.

Everyone froze.

Jaws dropped across the lobby, not because of the slap itself, but because of who received it. 

For a split second, most of them had thought Sapphire was manipulated enough to strike Charles.

Instead, Jude was the one holding his face. She had turned swiftly and delivered the slap to him. 

His hand trembled as it pressed against his cheek, eyes wide in disbelief. “Ma… why…” he stammered, voice breaking.

Sapphire’s expression was cold now. 

“Say one more lie,” she said evenly, “and I will slap you again.”

Jude went silent.

She took a step forward, her presence crushing.

 “Do you really think I didn’t know what was happening here? I saw everything”

Collins, still stunned, blurted out to defend jude,  “Ma, this lowlife, this ragman, he is the one who started everything. He—”

“Enough,” Sapphire cut in sharply. “Shut up.”

Her eyes flicked to him, sharp and unforgiving. “You should not even attempt to lie”

She straightened and continued calmly, “From the beginning, I saw everything. I also sent one of my guards here earlier to secretly record what was going on. I received everything in real time.”

She reached into her pocket and pulled out her phone.

The screen lit up.

She turned it around.

The video played clearly, capturing Jude’s threats, Linda’s manipulation, Collins’ smug provocations, the guards being ordered to beat Charles and Serena, every word sharp and undeniable.

Linda’s mouth opened, then closed. Collins’ grin vanished completely. Tracy silently looked away.

Jude’s hand dropped slowly from his cheek. His lips moved, but no sound came out.

Sapphire lowered the phone and looked straight at him.

“Because you wanted to please your stupid fiancée,” she said calmly, “you went against company protocol, abused your authority, threatened violence inside my company, and nearly destroyed my reputation.”

She took one final step closer.

“You don’t deserve this position,” she said flatly. “You ought to be sacked.”

Thud!

Jude’s knees hit the marble hard.

“What—no—please,” he blurted out, panic ripping through his voice as he grabbed Sapphire's trouser leg.

 “Boss, don’t do this to me. Please. I was unemployed for four years before I finally got this job. This is the best-paying company in the city. I can’t lose this. Please.”

Sapphire looked down at him, her expression unmoved.

 “Maybe you should have thought about that,” she added brutally,  “before listening to your stupid fiancée and taking her side.”

Linda exploded.

“Hey, hang it right there,” she shouted, stepping forward. “Don’t you dare call me stupid. Just because you are some CEO doesn’t mean you can talk to me anyhow—”

She didn’t finish.

Smack.

The sound cracked through the lobby as Jude slapped her hard across the face.

“Shut the fuck up,” he yelled, eyes wild. “Do you want to completely ruin my life?”

Linda staggered back, stunned, her hand flying to her cheek. “Baby—”

“Don’t you dare baby me,” Jude snapped. “You pushed me into this. I didn’t even want to do it. I’m about to get sacked and you’re still talking back to my boss.”

Linda stood there shaking, cheek burning, anger and disbelief swirling in her eyes.

Sapphire watched the scene quietly, then spoke again, her voice cutting through the noise.

“Do you even know who you offended,” she asked calmly. “Do you know who he is?”

The room went still.

Employees leaned forward.

Everyone’s eyes slowly shifted to Charles, curiosity and disbelief written on their faces.

Did she really know him?

And who exactly was he to her?.

They thought in their mind, because this was the first time they saw sapphire angry. Totally outburst.

At that moment, Sapphire knew she had slipped.
