Chapter 277

Charles swallowed hard, his pulse hammering in his ears. He hadn’t expected this to be easy, but… not this hard. 

The mountain’s face loomed larger now, its jagged eyes gleaming as it spoke again in a low, hollow voice.

“Tick… tock… tick… tick…” it whispered in an eerie, almost horror-like cadence. The sound echoed against the gray mist, vibrating through Charles’s chest.

He pressed his palms together instinctively, his mind spinning. Think. Focus. Think.

“I… was here before you were born. I heal all wounds, yet cause them too. Something you spend but never own…” Charles muttered under his breath. The words repeated in his head, a maddening rhythm: I heal, yet cause… spend, never own…

He wasn’t a riddle or brain-twister person. His strength was in fighting, in reflexes, in sensing energy, not solving abstract puzzles. His brow furrowed, jaw tightening. Ten seconds…

The hollow, jagged eyes seemed to pierce him as the mountain’s voice whispered again, slow and deliberate. “Tick… tock… tick… tick… ten… nine… eight…”

Charles gulped. He knew the mountain wasn’t joking. One wrong answer, and the black arrow would pierce his chest.

Money?

The word escaped his mouth instinctively. “Money!” he shouted. His voice echoed across the gray waters, stiff and uncertain. “It… it’s money! Because—because we meet it in life, we spend it, and yet it’s never really ours…”

The mountain’s jagged mouth opened wider. A shrill crack tore through the air. “Wrong!”

A black arrow shot through the mist in a blur. Charles felt it pierce his chest. Pain exploded across his ribs, sharp and suffocating. 

He spat blood, staggering backward, clutching the wound. His knees hit the gray sand, scraping them badly. He groaned as his body trembled under the pain, bile rising in his throat.

The mountain’s voice rumbled again, cold and unyielding. “Will you back down… or use your second trial?”

Charles grit his teeth, forcing himself upright. His ribs burned, but he refused to retreat. “I… I won’t back down!” he spat. 

He thought about it for a long time, then snapped his eyes. “I know it…!”.

“Life… life itself… we met it here, and will also continue if we die!”

“Wrong!” the mountain snapped again. Another black arrow pierced his chest, tearing through muscle and bone. Blood spattered the ground, and Charles gasped, staggering backward. 

His vision blurred, the pain surging with every heartbeat. Already, he could feel his strength fading. Climbing the mountain like this… would be impossible if he didn’t succeed soon.

The jagged face leaned closer. “Third trial. Lose… and you die. You may leave and return tomorrow. The trial will start from three again.”

Charles clenched his fists, tasting blood in his mouth. He nodded slowly, forcing himself to focus. No. He wasn’t giving up. 

“No… no,” he muttered. “I… I will give the last try…” His lips pressed tight, teeth gritted. “Yeah… yeah… fuck… yeah…”

He recited the riddle again, louder this time, slow enough for each word to sink into his mind:

“I was here before you were born. I will remain long after you are gone. I heal all wounds, yet cause them too. I am something you spend, but never own. I can be wasted, saved, or lost, yet no one can touch me or see me pass. Kings and beggars bow to me equally. What am I?”

Tick… tock… tick… tick… The sound echoed around him, as if counting down his remaining time.

Charles closed his eyes. He pictured the words in his mind, one by one, repeating them softly under his breath: You spend but never own… Heal all wounds, yet cause them… Remain after I’m gone…

Six… five… four… three…

Then it hit him. What triggered him was the countdown itself!.

“Time!” he shouted, eyes snapping open. “The answer… it’s Time! Because it’s here before we are, it stays after we are gone. It heals… it hurts… it can be spent, saved, or lost. Everyone bows to it. Time!”

He exhaled, voice echoing as the word left him, forcing logic and reason into the maddening rhythm of the mountain’s tick-tock.

The rocky eyes stared at him, motionless, then slowly, the jagged mouth twisted into what could only be described as a smile.

“No… you lose!” it said in a grating voice.

Charles frowned. “What?”

The mountain’s jagged face creaked, like he was ready to shot another arrow.

“Wait wait”, Chadles stated. 

“It’s a prank,” a hollow, echoing voice said faintly. “You got it right!!!”.

“Good luck on your climb.”

The mountain’s shape melted completely into the gray stone of the peak, leaving Charles blinking in disbelief. 

Charles exhaled, finally allowing himself a small, shaky smile. He had survived the trial. The climb… was only beginning, but he had cleared the first, deadliest obstacle. That was what he thought.

Charles wiped sweat from his brow and reached for the nearest rough patch of rock. He pressed his fingers into every groove, every crack, trying to find solid grips. Slowly, carefully, he began climbing.

He looked up. The peak still seemed impossibly high, shrouded in mist. By the time he reached the top, it would take at least three hours. His left chest throbbed sharply where the black arrows had pierced him. Every breath was a struggle, but he knew he couldn’t stop now.

If he climbed down, he’d have to start all over tomorrow. Face another riddle. Spend more time in this dangerous place, weak and injured, surrounded by monsters. That wasn’t an option.

He focused. “No… can’t stop now,” he muttered.

Activating his Flash Movement, Charles dashed upward along the mountain’s edge. For a few minutes, it worked perfectly. His legs moved faster than before, his body barely touching the rock, and he covered almost half the remaining distance to the summit.

Then — Snap!

Pain exploded in his chest. The wound from the second black arrow flared violently, and he stumbled, nearly losing his grip.

“Fuck! Fuck!” he shouted, clutching a jagged patch of rock with trembling hands.

The sharp pain burned like fire, coursing through his ribs with every heartbeat. He pressed himself against the stone, trying to steady his breath. Sweat dripped down his face, mingling with the blood from his chest.

He stared up, the summit still far above him. can he really do this or he would die here.
