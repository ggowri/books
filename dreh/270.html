Chapter 271

The young man smiled faintly and bowed in return, his movements calm and confident, as if he already belonged there.

Gabriella frowned deeply, confusion tightening her expression as she looked from him to Mr. Timothy. “What the heck is going on here?”

Mr. Timothy adjusted his suit and gestured toward the man. “We were just talking about you.”

“I don’t understand,” Gabriella said sharply. “Who are you, please?”

Mr. Timothy turned fully toward her, his tone measured and deliberate. “Another direct heir. This is Xavier. Mr. Belly’s son.”

The words landed like a sudden explosion.

Gabriella scoffed in disbelief. “Direct heir? I don’t understand anything you’re saying. What direct heir?”

Mr. Timothy nodded slowly, as though expecting her reaction. “Let me explain properly. This is Xavier, who is also Mr. Belly’s son.”

Gabriella stared at him, her brows knitting together. “That’s impossible. How does that even make sense?”

Without replying immediately, Mr. Timothy reached into a leather folder resting on the table. He pulled out several documents and placed them carefully on the polished surface.

“These,” he said, sliding them toward her, “are DNA test results confirming Xavier’s identity. He is biologically Mr. Belly’s son. In fact, he was born before you.”

The room fell silent.

Gabriella rushed forward almost instinctively, snatching the file from the table. Her hands trembled as she flipped through the pages. Charts. Signatures. Laboratory seals. Official confirmations. Every line she read tightened the knot in her chest.

Her breathing grew uneven.

Her father… had another child.

A son. And he never told her.

Her vision blurred, she didn't believe her father could do something like this. 

“How…” she whispered, barely audible. “How is this possible?”

Mr. Timothy clasped his hands behind his back. “Since Mr. Belly did not leave an official will naming a successor, Synx tradition applies. The position goes to the eldest direct heir. Xavier, being older, holds the rightful claim to leadership.”

Gabriella’s head snapped up, fury replacing shock. “This is bullshit!” she shouted, her voice echoing across the boardroom. “I can’t accept this. I won’t! You fabricated this!”

Several board members exchanged glances, but one of the senior directors leaned forward calmly. “You are free to conduct your own independent test,” he said. “The board will fully cooperate.”

Gabriella’s legs weakened beneath her. The file slipped slightly in her grip as the weight of everything pressed down on her at once. Betrayal. Confusion. Loss. The ground felt unsteady, and she staggered backward, nearly falling.

Before she could lose balance completely, Xavier stepped forward.

He moved smoothly.

“Careful,” he said softly.

Gabriella steadied herself, breathing hard, refusing to look weak in front of the room.

Xavier then offered a small smile. “Hello, sister. Finally meeting you.”

He extended his hand toward her for a handshake.

Gabriella didn’t take Xavier’s hand. She let it hover there for a moment, then slowly withdrew, her eyes narrowing as they swept across every board member in the room. 

“How did you even know?” she demanded, her voice sharp. “My father… he would have at least told me!”

Mr. Timothy’s expression remained calm, almost apologetic. “He kept it a secret from you. He didn’t want to hurt you,” he said gently.

Gabriella’s chest heaved with anger, every word from him stoking the fire inside her. She turned her gaze toward Xavier, who now faced the board leaders confidently. “Thank you, everyone, for believing in me,” he said, his tone steady. “I promise I will deliver.”

Before anyone could respond, the boardroom door swung open.

 All eyes turned, and even Gabriella froze. Charles stepped in, moving with his usual calm, authoritative presence. Gasps echoed through the room.

Ganrikled whispered under his breath, “Charles… what are you doing here?”

Mr. Timothy’s frown deepened. “I know you, Charles, and how you helped Gabriella, but that does not give you the right to barge into a board meeting,” he said firmly.

Others muttered softly, “Yes… that nonsense again.”

Mr. Timothy waved them aside, raising his voice. “We are having an important meeting. Please step aside.”

Charles, smiling faintly, took a step forward. “Important meeting? It involves Gabriella. It concerns me,” he said, voice calm but carrying weight. Then, before anyone could react further, he reached out and hugged Gabriella tightly.

Gabriella gasped, her eyes wide. Shock and disbelief clashed with warmth. Her heart raced at the unexpected gesture.

Mr. Timothy scoffed, shaking his head. “Regardless, you shouldn’t just barge in,” he said, though his tone carried a hint of exasperation rather than anger.

Charles pulled back slightly, still holding Gabriella’s hands. “We are making decisions about the CEO role,” he said, voice steady. “Xavier is the rightful heir and we are giving it to him.”

Gabriella’s eyes darted between Charles and Xavier. “I… I didn’t even see him before. My father never mentioned him. And now a DNA test? How do I know it’s not fake?” she asked, her voice trembling with a mixture of disbelief and anger.

Mr. Timothy yelled, “it's original!".

Charles nodded knowingly. “I know it's original”. 

Gabriel stared at Charles, muttering, “It’s original? You are testifying to it… how did you know?”

Charles smiled calmly. “Because Mr. Belly told me himself.”

The room went silent. Gasps rippled through everyone.

“Really?” Gabriella’s voice was a mix of shock and confusion. “He told you… but didn’t tell her?”

Mr. Timothy frowned, folding his arms. “So, if you knew all this… why did you barge in here and cause a scene?”

Charles’s smile widened slightly. “Because he also told me another surprising thing.”

Gabriella’s eyes narrowed. “Another… thing?” she asked sharply, her voice tight.

Mr. Timothy folded his hand and scoffed, “What would Mr. Belly tell you that he didn't tell us?”.

Charles stepped forward,  “He told me about how you… and his supposed son right here plan to kill Gabriella”.

“What!”. 

“what the heck is he saying!”.

Everyone gasped and started murmuring.

Mr. Timothy scoffed and said, “What the heck are you saying! How dare you accuse me of something horrendous!”.
