Chapter 217

Charles arrived at Natasha’s store and slowed when he saw them.

Six men stood outside, spread casually across the front like they owned the street. Black suits and hard eyes.

As Charles stepped closer, one of them noticed him. A second later, they all did. Without a word, they parted, creating a clear path to the door.

He walked in.

Inside the shop, Natasha sat behind the counter, stiff, her hands clasped together. In front of her sat a young man with slick, jelly-packed hair and an expensive jacket that screamed arrogance. He lounged on the chair like the place belonged to him.

The young man turned as the door closed.

He looked Charles over, slow and deliberate, then smiled.

“So,” he said lightly, “you must be the one who beat up my men.”

Charles didn’t stop walking. He moved until he stood right in front of him.

“If you keep disturbing her,” Charles said calmly, “I will do the same to you.”

For a second, the room was silent.

Then the young man burst out laughing.

He leaned back, clapping once. “You really think you can fight me?”

Charles scoffed. “I have fought monsters. I don’t give a fuck who you are.”

The smile didn’t leave the man’s face. He nodded, amused. “You are not the only one who’s fought a monster.”

Charles’ eyes hardened. “What do you want?”

The man spread his hands slightly. “I think you already know. I’m here to buy this place.”

Natasha stiffened.

“I’m willing to raise the price,” he continued casually. “One point two billion.”

Natasha’s eyes widened before she could stop herself. For a split second, shock flashed across her face. That amount was insane.

Charles' frowned upon hearing this. It only made his jaw tighten. It only made him more curious as to why they were willing to go this far.

He looked at Raphael. “We’re not interested.”

Raphael tilted his head. “You don’t have to be difficult. You should accept us while we’re being generous.”

Charles stepped closer, invading his space. His voice dropped. “Or what?”

Raphael leaned forward slightly, his confidence untouched. “Or we will keep coming here.”

He smiled, calm and certain.

“And we will later destroy everything and get it forcefully.”

Charles scoffed. “You must think very highly of yourself. But like I said, if you dare disturb her again, I will whoop your ass.”

Raphael chuckled and nodded. “I have heard about you. I was told you even killed Blade. If that’s what’s giving you confidence, then let me correct you. Blade was my lowest junior in cultivation.”

Charles stepped forward again, refusing to retreat. “I don’t care what you are. We are not selling this store. That’s the end of it.”

Raphael rose slowly from his chair. He glanced around the shop as if inspecting a toy, then looked back at Charles with clear amusement.

“I like your bravery,” he said. “And the way you are not afraid of the Trisian family. Most people would already be pissing themselves.”

He took a step closer. Then something unexpected happened.

Raphael’s body shuddered, and in the next instant, another Raphael stepped out of him, as if peeling away from the same body.

Charles’ throat tightened. He instinctively stepped back half a pace.

Natasha covered her mouth. “How… how is that possible?”

Before anyone could process it, the Raphaels split again. And again. And again.

One became two. Two became four. Four became six. Then ten.

They surrounded Charles in a perfect circle, each one identical, each one smiling.

Charles stood still, muscles tense, eyes moving rapidly. He could no longer tell which one was real.

So this was it. Peak-level power.

One of the Raphaels stepped forward. “Why don’t you join us?” he asked calmly. “Work for the Trisian family. We will pay you well.”

Charles exhaled slowly.

“I don’t want your money,” he said. “Or any shit that comes with it. Just leave this store. We’re not selling, and nothing will change that.”

The smiles faded.

The Raphaels began multiplying again, their presence pressing down like weight in the air.

One of them leaned in slightly.

“Do you know,” he said, “that right now, right here, I can kill you?”

Charles' shoulders relaxed, but his eyes sharpened. He scanned the circle slowly, counting distances, angles, exits. He was ready to take them.

Then one of them spoke.

“Why don’t we settle this with one-on-one combat?”

Charles’ gaze snapped to him. “What’s that supposed to mean?”

The Raphaels shifted, and the one closest to him stepped forward. His tone was calm, almost playful.

“You said you would whoop my ass,” Raphael said. “So let’s make it fair. One on one. If I win, you sell this shop. No arguments.”

Charles’ jaw tightened.

“And if I win?” he asked.

Raphael smiled. “Then I will never disturb you or this place again.”

Charles frowned.

It didn’t sit right.

Raphael had just demonstrated power far beyond anything he’d faced recently.

Splitting into ten bodies like it was nothing. On top of that, the bet was lopsided. If Charles lost, Natasha lost everything. If Raphael lost, he only walked away.

Raphael saw the hesitation and laughed softly.

“What’s wrong?” he asked. “Are you chickening out now? Don’t tell me you’re afraid.”

Natasha turned sharply. “Charles—”

He raised a hand slightly, stopping her.

Charles exhaled. He didn’t like being cornered. He liked it even less when someone thought fear would make him bend.

He lifted his head and looked Raphael straight in the eyes.

“Yeah,” he said evenly. “Why don’t we do that. One on one combat.”
