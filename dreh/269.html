Chapter 270

At that moment, the remaining Trisian gangs immediately dropped their weapons and surrendered. Their voices trembled as they cried out, “All hail Godfather! All hail Godfather!”.

They all bowed. 

Charles stepped forward cautiously. His legs still weak from the poison, he bowed deeply in front of Godfather Vincent. “I’m sorry… and thank you for saving us,” he murmured, his voice heavy with gratitude.

Vincent nodded slowly, eyes calm but piercing. “I knew you could never defeat the Trisian family by yourself. I have been monitoring them. That’s why I arrived when I did—quickly and in time.”

Charles straightened slightly, still bowing.

Vincent continued, his voice low and instructive. “The reason you were so badly affected by the poison powder is because you’re not strong enough yet. Your Yin and Yang haven’t fully awakened. To fix that, you must meet Mr. Jay as soon as possible. Only then can you upgrade further.”

Charles nodded.

The soldiers and mercenaries around them began cleaning up the warehouse, disposing of the bodies, repairing damage, and securing the place.

By the next day, news of the deaths of Mr. Jacob and Raphael spread like wildfire. Whispers and rumors claimed that Godfather himself had executed them both. No one knew that Charles had been involved in the battle—his name remained hidden.

With the Trisian family empire now leaderless, Godfather Vincent formally annexed it. The entire empire—businesses, territories, and loyal soldiers—was absorbed under his command. The cultivator world buzzed with astonishment and fear. Godfather’s power had just grown immeasurably, and even the most hardened masters trembled at his name.

As a reward for Charles’ accomplished mission, Godfather gave Charles the Trisian family. He became their leader and could use them whenever he wanted. Godfather also announced this. But no one knew who the grandson was, except a few of the Trisian family members who were present during the fight. Godfather had already given them a big warning not to expose him.

The next few days, after Mr. Belly’s funeral, Gabriella became the leader of the Synx family. Everyone was shocked to see that Gabriella could walk properly.

But during the day of her inauguration, when everyone was in the boardroom, Gabriella—still mourning her father—stood and began her speech.

“Good morning, everyone. I want to begin by acknowledging a personal loss that has deeply affected me—my father passed away recently. His guidance, love, and unwavering belief in me shaped who I am today, and I carry his lessons with me every day. I stand here now, grateful for your support, and I hope we can work together with mutual respect, trust, and collaboration. Together, I believe we can achieve remarkable things, overcome challenges, and honor the values my father instilled in me. Thank you for being part of this journey.”

Something strange happened. Instead of clapping or welcoming her, the board members just stared at her, whispering among themselves. Gabriella, noticing the pattern from where her father had died, raised her voice.

“If anyone here has anything to say, this is the time now.”

Just then, Mr. Timothy, one of the board leaders and also Mr. Belly’s brother, spoke up.

“Actually,” he said, “the board and every one of us have discussed, and we deem that you aren’t worthy to be the leader of Synx World.”

Gabriella frowned. “Uhn… what the heck is that supposed to mean?”

Mr. Timothy continued, “You are young and inexperienced to handle this role.”

Gabriella scoffed, her anger rising. “Well, that’s the reason I have all of you—to teach me?”

Mr. Timothy replied, “We have a lot to do, and pending your experience, you won’t be able to rule us. I and the other board leaders have discussed this.”

Gabriella slammed her hand lightly on the podium. “Well, I am not listening to all your bullshit!”

Mr. Timothy raised his hands, trying to maintain control. “Do not abuse us, Gabriella. We arrived here only to do what’s best for this company. Everything we have said, we have said with the company’s future in mind.”

Gabriella’s eyes flashed with anger. “What are you even saying? My father owned this company! You don’t get to have any say in this. I automatically inherit everything. This is mine!”

“That’s exactly why we’re speaking,” Mr. Timothy said calmly but firmly. “Your lack of experience is a real concern. The board has always supported your father because he had the wisdom and skill to lead. You… do not yet. If we all step back, if the board refuses to back you, the company will falter. There is no way you could rule it on your own. This is the board’s unanimous decision.”

Gabriella felt her chest tighten. Shock. Confusion. The board had always obeyed her father unquestioningly. They had never dared oppose him, never revealed their hidden calculations. Why now?

“So,” she spat, her voice sharp, “if I back down, who will rule this company then? I am the only child. The direct heir. Or are you suggesting one of you will take over?”

Mr. Timothy’s expression remained calm. “No. We cannot. It goes against Synx tradition. Only the direct heir can rule. That’s why you are the rightful leader. But…” he paused, choosing his words carefully, “we need someone experienced enough to guide the company”.

Gabriella’s frown deepened. “You just said only the direct heir rules! Who the hell are you talking about aside from me?”

Before Mr. Timothy could answer, the boardroom door opened slowly. All eyes turned. 

A man stepped inside. He was tall, broad-shouldered, and carried himself with an aura of quiet authority. His hair curled naturally, thick and black, framing a sharp face with piercing eyes. 

Gabriella’s brow furrowed in suspicion. “Who is this?” she demanded.

Mr. Timothy straightened, then walked toward the man. He paused, bowed slightly, and uttered with respect, “Welcome, Young Master.”
