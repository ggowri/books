Chapter 279

Charles exhaled slowly, the cold wind brushing against his face. “I’m… really grateful,” he said quietly. “Is there anything I can do to repay you?”

Aries hummed softly, her voice echoing around the summit like a gentle bell. “Actually… there is. But not now. It will be later.”

Charles frowned slightly. “Later? Then how will you be able to contact me?”

A small laugh followed. “I will find a way.”

He nodded, feeling strangely reassured by her confidence. “Still… Thank you. Truly. If only I could see you in person.”

Her tone softened, almost shy. “I’m working on that soon.”

Charles let out a small breath of amusement. “I will  look forward to it.”

“You should start your meditation now,” Aries said gently.

Charles nodded and slowly lowered himself onto the cold stone. He crossed his legs carefully, straightened his back, and rested his hands on his knees. 

The wind howled around the peak, yet a strange calm surrounded him. He closed his eyes deeply, shutting out the world, focusing only on his breathing.

Inhale.

Exhale.

Energy slowly gathered around him, faint currents brushing against his skin like invisible waves.

“Goodbye,” Aries whispered softly. “We will meet soon.”

Her presence faded, and Charles felt himself drifting, sinking into a deep and silent state. His thoughts dissolved. The mountain, the pain, the climb all vanished as his consciousness floated in darkness.

Then—

“Charles… Charles, are you okay?”

His eyes snapped open.

He found himself sitting inside Mr. Jay’s chamber. The familiar walls surrounded him, and Mr. Jay stood directly in front of him, staring with wide eyes.

Charles blinked several times, adjusting to reality. He inhaled deeply and nodded. “I’m… okay.”

Mr. Jay leaned closer, disbelief written all over his face. “What the heck happened? Minutes ago you were covered in wounds. Then suddenly everything healed. You even lost your bracelet… and you still came back.”

Charles rubbed the back of his neck calmly.

Mr. Jay’s eyes widened further. “Don’t tell me… were you able to attain the Yang Peak?”

Charles nodded. “Yes.”

“What the fuck?!” Mr. Jay blurted. “You did that within one trial? How the hell? How are you able to do it?”

Charles shrugged lightly. “Well… I just did.”

Mr. Jay stared at him, unsettled. Even geniuses failed multiple times before succeeding. Doing it in one trial was beyond absurd.

“That’s… crazy,” he muttered under his breath.

Charles stood up as if nothing had happened. “So… is my Yin–Yang opened now? Can I continue with my upgrading?”

Mr. Jay slowly nodded, still processing everything. “Yeah. In fact… you can go home today.”

A satisfied smile appeared on Charles’s face.

Mr. Jay frowned slightly. “At least give me a hint. What exactly did you do?”

Charles only smiled and walked toward the door without answering.

After he left, Mr. Jay remained silent for a moment before picking up his phone. He dialed a number.

“Godfather,” he said once the call connected. “Charles has concluded his training. He attained Yin–Yang. He’s now in Class Four.”

There was silence on the other end before a shocked voice responded. “Really?”

“Yeah,” Mr. Jay replied. “One trial. One freaking trial. You tried twenty times.”

Godfather exhaled slowly. “With his power… he can now take them down.”

“He can,” Mr. Jay agreed, then hesitated. “But… if he learns about his father… I hope he won’t back out.”

“That’s why I introduced this mission,” Godfather said calmly. “He won’t know until he finishes it.”

Mr. Jay sighed. “I just hope everything works out well.”

“It will,” Godfather replied.

Charles stepped out of the temple grounds as the evening wind brushed softly against his face. The stone gates slowly closed behind him, marking the end of his training. He was now known as the fastest person to ever open Yin and Yang in a single day. Even thinking about it still felt unreal.

He turned back briefly and bowed respectfully toward Mr. Jay.

Mr. Jay crossed his arms and smiled faintly. “Now you can go crush them.”

Charles gave a small nod and walked away without another word. Deep inside, he still couldn’t believe everything had happened so quickly. 

He hadn’t expected to finish this in five days, let alone one. His body felt lighter, stronger, calmer. A quiet satisfaction settled in his chest.

All thanks to Aries.

The thought made him smile slightly as he entered his car and began the drive back to the city. 

The road stretched endlessly ahead, lights flickering on as night slowly took over. By the time he arrived, exhaustion finally caught up with him. 

Before heading back to the hotel, he decided to stop for a drink.

The bar area was dimly lit, filled with soft music and low conversations. Charles sat at the counter, ordered a drink, and sipped slowly, letting the cold liquid calm his nerves. 

Just then, murmured voices from the person beside him caught his attention.

“Hey, prepare the boat,” the man said quietly into his phone. “I’m moving out of this country next week. Be fast. I’ll pay you three times your money… okay, thanks. Thanks so much.”

The call ended, and the man stood up.

Charles barely cared at first. Conversations like that were common in places like this. He casually turned his head, only intending to glance around the room.

Then he froze.

His eyes narrowed as recognition struck him instantly.

Collins.

Charles slowly stood, watching carefully as the man walked toward the exit. A faint frown formed on his face. It was truly Collins. 

Collins was leaving the country?

He folded his arms slightly, thoughts running through his mind. If Collins was leaving… then what about Tracy? Was she leaving too?

He paused, then shook his head lightly.

Not my business.

With a quiet sigh, Charles finished the rest of his drink in one go and placed the glass back on the counter. Whatever Collins was planning had nothing to do with him right now.

He decided to lodge and have a good sleep before he continued his journey.
