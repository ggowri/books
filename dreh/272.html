Chapter 273

Gabriella watched them, stunned, before a small relieved smile formed on her lips.

The meeting concluded quickly. Gabriella thanked everyone sincerely and asked for their continued support. One by one, the board members left the room until only she and Charles remained.

Silence settled between them.

Gabriella turned toward him, her eyes filled with emotion and admiration.

“I don’t even know how to thank you enough,” she said softly.

Charles shook his head lightly. “It’s nothing,” he said. “I’m only fulfilling the promise I made to Mr. Belly.”

Gabriella exhaled slowly, the tension finally leaving her shoulders. “So… Father had a son and never told me,” she murmured. “I still feel bad he was arrested too.”

Charles nodded gently. “Mr. Belly was shocked when he found out as well. Xavier was born even before you, but he never knew about him until Mr. Timothy discovered the truth. Your father has a long relationship with a woman that birth Xavier”

Gabriella looked down, listening quietly.

“Xavier was already living like a thug,” Charles continued. “Rebellious. Reckless. Mr. Belly didn’t want him anywhere near Synx, but didn't abandon him either.  so he gave him twenty billion dollars to start a life of his own.”

Gabriella’s eyes widened slightly.

“And you know what?” Charles added. “Xavier wasted all of it in four months. Completely gone. That was when Mr. Belly became afraid. If he brought him into Synx, he might destroy everything.”

He paused before continuing.

“He started suspecting Xavier and Mr. Timothy’s relationship, so he secretly recorded them. That’s how he got the video. By the time the fire incident happened, he already knew who was behind it. He didn’t hide Xavier from you to hurt you. He was trying to protect you.”

Gabriella sighed deeply, emotions swirling inside her. Then she stepped forward and hugged Charles tightly.

“I’m so grateful,” she whispered. “My father trusted you a lot.”

Charles lowered his gaze. “I’m sorry I couldn’t protect him till the end.”

Gabriella pulled back slightly and shook her head. “It’s okay. Don’t blame yourself.”

For a moment, neither of them spoke.

Then Charles said quietly, “I won’t be around for the next few days.”

Gabriella frowned. “What? Why?”

“I need to upgrade my inner power,” he replied seriously. “That was why we lost to Mr. Jacob… why I couldn’t save Mr. Belly. If only I had balanced my Yin and Yang earlier…” he muttered. “I’m going to do that now.”

Gabriella nodded slowly. “Alright. But don’t blame yourself too much for my father’s death.”

After a brief silence filled with unspoken feelings, Gabriella leaned forward and kissed him gently before stepping back.

“Call me if anything happens,” she said softly.

Charles gave a small nod before turning to leave.

××××

That evening, Charles had already packed his load and was making his way out.

“Or should I follow you? Me too,” Sapphire said quickly.

“Me too,” Natasha added.

Charles smiled gently and shook his head. “This is a personal journey. I have to face it alone.”

He stepped closer and hugged each of them one after another.

“I will be back soon,” he said quietly.

It was obvious they were going to miss him.

Sapphire tried to hide it, but her eyes followed every movement he made. Natasha folded her arms tightly, clearly unwilling to let him go so easily.

Charles smiled reassuringly. “Call me if anything happens. I’ll be back in a jiffy.”

They nodded reluctantly.

With one last look at them, he turned and left.

Inside the car, as he drove along the quiet highway, Charles picked up his phone and dialed Serena’s number.

She answered almost immediately. “Charles? Where are you, i have been expecting you to check this document together”

“I will be leaving for about a week or two,” he said calmly. “There’s something I need to handle.”

Serena went silent for a second. “Where are you going? Is everything okay?”

“I’m fine,” Charles replied.

Her voice softened, carrying worry. “You are not… leaving forever, right?”, she was scared. 

Charles frowned slightly. “No. I will come back.”

A small breath escaped her. “Please come back. I can’t do without you.”

“I will,” he assured her gently. “And when I return, we will go through the documents together like I promised.”

“Okay…” she said, still uneasy. “But where are you going?”

Charles hesitated.

Serena was the only one who still didn’t know about the cultivator side of his life.

“Just… a compulsory trip,” he said finally.

“Be careful,” Serena whispered.

“I will.”

The call ended.

It took over four hours of driving before Charles finally arrived at Mr. Jay’s Temple, hidden deep within the mountains. The air there felt different, heavier, filled with an invisible pressure.

Several disciples were already waiting for him at the entrance.

They bowed slightly before guiding him inside.

As they led him through long stone corridors, Charles felt a strange calm settle in his chest.

Soon, they arrived at a quiet chamber.

Mr. Jay stood inside, leaning on his staff. The old man smiled the moment Charles entered.

“Welcome,” he said. “Here comes Frank’s son.”

Charles paused.

Frank?

That… was his father’s name.

He silently took note of it. Perhaps during this training, he would finally learn more about the man he barely knew.

Without another word, Mr. Jay suddenly lifted the stick in his hand and pointed it directly at Charles’s stomach.

A sharp force shot forward.

Pain exploded through Charles’s body.

He stiffened instinctively, expecting blood to spill like before—but this time, nothing came out.

Mr. Jay smiled and said, “You have passed the first stage. You are really ready to open your Yin Yang, but beware. It won't be easy, and you can also die in the process.”
