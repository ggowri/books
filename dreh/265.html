Chapter 266

Mr. Jacob looked toward the guard. “Is she alone?”

“She came with a driver,” the man replied. “She’s on her wheelchair.”

Raphael’s face twisted with rage. “I swear I will kill her. She made that bastard embarrass me.”

Mr. Belly roared, struggling against the ropes. “My daughter has nothing to do with this. You were the one who proposed the fight and lost. If you dare touch my daughter, I swear you will die.”

Mr. Jacob walked over calmly and pressed his stick against Mr. Belly’s chest. “Shhh. You are not in any position to talk. Do you understand? So shut up.”

He turned to the guard. “Let her enter. Make sure you’re careful and look around. That girl might bring that bastard with her.”

The guard hurried out.

Minutes later he returned, pushing Gabriella in.

She rolled into the open space, the sound of the wheelchair echoing across the concrete floor. The moment she saw her father tied up, her composure broke.

“Father!” she cried, wheeling quickly toward him. “Are you okay?”

Mr. Belly’s eyes softened despite the tension. “Gabriella. What the heck? You shouldn’t have come.”

She sniffed. “I couldn’t just stay and do nothing.”

Mr. Jacob raised a hand. “Enough of this family reunion.”

He stepped forward and suddenly pressed a gun against Mr. Belly’s head.

Gabriella screamed. “Let him go! What are you doing?”

Mr. Jacob’s voice was cold. “I’m planning on doing that… if you give me the file of ownership of Snyx World.”

Gabriella’s fingers tightened around the folder in her lap.

“Don’t act tough,” Mr. Jacob warned, pushing the gun harder against Mr. Belly’s temple. “Unless you want your father dead.”

Her hands trembled as she lifted the file. “This is the thing you want,” she said through clenched teeth. “Now let my father go.”

Mr. Jacob took it, flipped through the pages briefly, then nodded.

All this time Raphael stared at her with raw hatred, as if he could strangle her with his eyes.

Then suddenly Mr. Jacob turned and pressed the gun against Gabriella’s head.

“Now,” he said, “make a signature transferring Snyx World to the Trisian family.”

Mr. Belly scoffed angrily. “Are you crazy? What the hell are you demanding? You want my entire fortune?”

“Yes,” Mr. Jacob replied calmly. “And I am going to take it.”

“Even if it’s signed, it’s under coercion,” Mr. Belly shot back. “It won’t stand. I can revoke it.”

Mr. Jacob shrugged. “Just sign and leave the rest to us. Or else I’ll drop a bullet in her head.”

Mr. Belly’s voice broke with fury. “How can you be so cruel? I gave you everything you ever wanted.”

“Then you shouldn’t have acted smart and stopped,” Mr. Jacob snapped. “Now you will pay the price.”

He threw the file down in front of Mr. Belly. A guard forced a pen into his bound hand.

“Now,” Mr. Jacob said coldly. “Sign it.”

Gabriella burst into tears. “Father, no. No. Don’t sign anything. Don’t sign that. This is your legacy.”

Mr. Jacob’s voice cut through sharply. “Your legacy or your daughter. Choose one. Now sign.”

He leaned closer. “We will also record a verbal agreement. But sign first.”

“No!” Gabriella cried again.

But Mr. Belly’s expression had already changed. He was a father before he was a businessman.

He looked at her and spoke firmly. “Okay. I will do it. Just don’t touch my daughter.”

Gabriella shook her head violently. “No, Father. Please.”

Mr. Belly ignored her pleas. His jaw tightened. “Do it,” he told Mr. Jacob. “Untie my hand properly so I can sign.”

The guards loosened one arm just enough for him to grip the pen.

He had already made up his mind.

His legacy meant nothing compared to his daughter’s life.

Just as he was about to press the pen down—

Thud.

One body dropped from the upper level of the unfinished building.

Everyone froze.

Then…

Thud. Thud. Thud.

Three more men hit the concrete floor.

Mr. Jacob’s head snapped upward.

Then it came again—

Thud. Thud. Thud. Thud.

Like dominoes falling.

Within seconds, dozens of guards were collapsing across the floor.

Panic rippled through the armed men.

Mr. Jacob roared, “Who the fuck is that?”

At that moment…

Swrrrrrrrr.

A blur of movement flashed through the open space like wind slicing through steel.

In the next second, the blur materialized behind Raphael’s wheelchair.

It was Charles.

He had used his flash movement ability to take down the outer guards and breach the building in seconds.

Now he stood behind Raphael, a gun pressed firmly against the back of his head.

Mr. Jacob stared in disbelief.

“Hey, old man,” Charles said calmly.

Raphael sneered despite the gun at his skull. “You bastard. You turned me into this and still dare show your face here.”

Charles’ voice was ice. “Release Mr. Belly and Gabriella. Now. Or I do worse.”

Mr. Jacob slowly smiled. “I’m impressed. You are very good.”

“Yeah. Thanks,” Charles replied casually. “Now release them this instant.”

Mr. Jacob’s smile faded. “Why should I be afraid? Kill my son and I will kill Gabriella here.”

Charles tilted his head slightly. “Is that what you want? Why don’t we test it?”

Mr. Jacob hesitated.

Gabriella suddenly shouted, “Yes, Charles. I don’t care if I die as long as that bastard dies with me.”

She said it with terrifying sincerity.

Charles nodded slightly. “You hear her.”

He pressed the gun harder into Raphael’s head. “Untie Mr. Belly. Or I will kill him.”

The remaining guards quickly pointed their guns at Charles from every direction.

Mr. Jacob glanced around, calculating. “Fine. I will untie him. But do you really think you can leave here alive with over four hundred of my men?”

“Shut up and do what I told you,” Charles snapped.

Mr. Jacob turned and walked toward Mr. Belly.

As he began untying him, Charles kept his focus on the old man.

And that was his mistake.

Raphael’s eyes flickered with sudden movement.

Hidden inside the side of his wheelchair was a compact powder. 

Before Charles could react—

Swwwww.

Raphael blew it. 

A sharp burst of compressed toxin sprayed backward into Charles’ face. Instantly,  Charles couldn't see anything again. 

Gabriella screamed. “Charles!”

Charles staggered.

“What did you—”

He didn’t even finish the sentence.

His grip loosened as the gun slipped from his hand.

Thud.

Charles collapsed flat onto the concrete floor.
