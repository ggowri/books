Chapter 243

Lizzypie walked forward slowly.

She looked calm, confident, and extremely beautiful. Her heels clicked against the pavement with a steady rhythm. The crowd parted for her without anyone asking them to move.

Ivan saw her and gulped.

“B-beauty… how can I help you?” he asked quickly, forcing a weak smile.

Lizzypie scoffed lightly.

“I am here with my boss,” she said, tilting her head slightly toward Charles.

Ivan blinked rapidly. “What? He is your boss? How could he—”

“Enough talking,” Lizzypie cut him off sharply. “Enter your account details properly.”

Her tone carried authority.

Ivan did not dare argue.

With trembling fingers, he carefully typed his bank details again and showed her the screen.

Lizzypie took out her phone.

She pressed a few buttons calmly.

Everyone watched in silence.

A few seconds later—

Ding.

Ivan’s phone vibrated.

He looked down and his eyes widened.

“F-fifty million dollars… credited successfully.”

His voice shook.

He slowly raised his head and stared at Charles in disbelief.

Charles had destroyed the car completely. Not only did he pay forty million. He added ten million extra.

The crowd exploded into murmurs.

“Who is this man?”

“He smashed a luxury car and still paid more than its worth!”

“Is he some hidden billionaire?”

Ivan looked at the wrecked car again, then back at Charles.

His mind could not process it.

Charles spoke calmly.

“Now. Take your men and leave immediately.”

His eyes turned cold.

“If you disturb her again, I will not show mercy next time. I will break your bones.”

He took one step forward.

“Scram!!!.”

Ivan felt a chill down his spine.

Instantly, he signaled his injured men. Those who could walk began dragging the others away. Within moments, they were retreating as fast as they could.

But before getting into the vehicle, Ivan turned back.

“You disgraced me today,” he said bitterly. “I don’t know how you got that much money. But you messed with the wrong person. You will suffer for it.”

Charles only scoffed at the empty threat.

Ivan finally left.

The crowd slowly scattered, still whispering about what they had witnessed.

Caroline stood still for a few seconds, then walked toward Charles slowly.

She swallowed.

“Thank you so much,” she said sincerely. “I am really grateful for your intervention.”

Charles shook his head slightly. “It is nothing.”

Caroline looked at him carefully.

“I am shocked,” she admitted softly. “You really spent fifty million just to save me.”

Charles gave a small shrug. “It is really nothing.”

That answer made her even more curious.

After a short pause, she lowered her eyes slightly.

“I am sorry,” she said gently. “For calling you my boyfriend like that without asking.”

Charles looked at her and nodded slightly.

“I understand,” he said calmly.

Caroline studied his face for a moment. The confidence, the calm voice, the strange power. Nothing about him felt ordinary.

“Please,” she asked softly, “who are you?”

Charles only smiled faintly.

“And I would like to repay your money,” she continued. “Even though it is a lot, I will find a way.”

Charles shook his head.

“You do not have to repay the money,” he said.

Caroline frowned slightly. “Then what do you want?”

Charles’ eyes sharpened just a little.

“You can repay us with something else.”

“What?” she asked carefully.

“The Red Moon Herb.”

Caroline froze.

Her expression changed immediately.

“Let us find somewhere quiet to talk,” she said.

Earlier, when Ivan began his dramatic proposal, Lizzypie had recognized Caroline instantly. She had quietly informed Charles that Caroline was the heiress of the Quill enterprises where  the rare Red Moon Herb lives.

But it was not the only reason.

They would not have stood by and watched her be forced into something she did not want.

The three of them walked down the street and found a quiet café nearby. It was calm inside, with soft music playing in the background.

They chose a corner table.

Lizzypie ordered three cups of coffee. The waiter left shortly after serving them.

For a few seconds, none of them spoke.

Caroline took a slow breath.

“So,” she began, looking at Charles directly, “you helped me because of the Red Moon Herb?”

Charles shook his head.

“Actually, no,” he said simply.

Caroline blinked.

“We helped you because you were in need of help,” he continued calmly. “We would not have allowed that situation. We would have asked about the herb anyway”.

Caroline studied him carefully..

She leaned back slightly in her chair.

“So,” she said slowly, “what exactly do you want with the Red Moon Herb?”.

Charles nodded and spoke plainly.

“We need the Red Moon Herb to treat someone who is crippled,” he said. “It is part of a medical formula. Without it, the medicine cannot be completed. And without the medicine, that person may never walk again.”

Caroline listened carefully.

Charles continued, “We are not using it for business or profit. It is to save someone.”

Caroline leaned back in her chair and sighed softly.

“I understand,” she said after a moment. “And I am very grateful that you helped me today.”

She paused before speaking again.

“Honestly, I cannot say for sure that we have the Red Moon Herb specifically. But my family manages many rare herbs. It is possible that it is among them.”

Charles nodded once. “we might be lucky”

Caroline’s expression then turned slightly complicated.

“But,” she added, “I cannot get my hands on it directly.”

“You will have to speak to my father,” she said. “He controls everything related to our herb storage and distribution.” 

Her expression changed. 

Lizzypie raised a brow slightly. “Is that a problem?”

Caroline gave a small, uneasy smile.

“A very big problem!”.
