Chapter 218

Natasha muttered Charles’s name under her breath.

“Charles.”

Raphael’s many bodies shuddered, then flowed back into one, merging seamlessly like it was nothing. He rolled his shoulders and smiled.

“You said it yourself,” he said lightly. “You better not back out.”

Charles met his gaze without blinking. “Why would I?”

Raphael’s smirk deepened. “Good. But a fight like this shouldn’t happen in a small shop. It deserves witnesses.”

Charles frowned. “What are you talking about?”

“The Cultivator Arena,” Raphael said. His tone shifted, pride slipping through. “We’ll make it a public match. Everyone will witness how I whoop your ass and humiliate you.”

Charles kept his composure. He didn’t know what the Cultivator Arena was, but the way Raphael said it made one thing clear. It would be a public fight.

He didn’t know if he could take Raphael head-on. The man had split into ten bodies like it was nothing. That kind of power wasn’t normal.

Still, Charles refused to back down.

He shrugged. “Fine. However you want it.”

Raphael’s eyes gleamed. “Good. I’ll handle the preparations and the announcement. If you don’t show up, I win by default.”

“And the store becomes mine,” Raphael added smoothly.

Charles nodded once. “Deal.”

With that, Raphael turned and walked out, his men falling into place behind him. He wore a wide grin.

The moment the door shut, Natasha rushed toward Charles.

“What the hell was that?” she demanded. “Why would you agree to something like that? Did you see what he did? He multiplied himself like it was nothing. How are you supposed to beat someone like that?”

Charles exhaled and leaned against the counter. “You’re worrying too much. I’ll defeat him.”

She stared at him. “He seems powerful.”

He smiled, calm and almost casual. “I always win.”

Natasha didn’t look convinced. She sighed and rubbed her temples.

“Still… something’s off. They raised the price like it meant nothing. One point two billion doesn’t just happen.”

Charles’s smile faded slightly. “Yeah. That’s what bothers me too.”

He straightened and looked toward the door Raphael had exited through.

“We need to find out why they want this store so badly,” he said. “Before the fight.”

Natasha nodded in agreement, and Charles immediately called Zander and Reaper to start checking the store for anything out of the ordinary.

Whatever Raphael was chasing here was something big. He had to find it first.

After that, he bid Natasha farewell with a peck and returned home.

When he arrived, he told Lizzie everything that had happened.

Lizzie lost her cool instantly. “Are you fucking serious right now? Do you know who Raphael is? He’s the heir of the Tristain family. You can never defeat him. And you agreed to the Cultivator Arena?”

Charles shrugged. “Then what was I supposed to do? Chicken out? You know I can’t do that. And tell me, what exactly is the Cultivator Arena?”

Lizzie blew out a frustrated breath. “So you agreed without knowing. It’s a stage set aside for battles between cultivators. Super-powered people like you. It’s public. Almost every major cultivator will be watching.”

Charles nodded. “I thought as much. It would be a huge embarrassment if I lose.”

Lizzie sighed. “This just adds to the problems you already have on the ground.”

Charles frowned slightly. “What problem on the ground?”

Lizzie didn’t answer immediately. She reached into her tablet, tapped twice, then pulled up a photo and handed it to him.

Charles glanced at it.

A very pretty girl stared back at him. Round face. Soft features. The kind of beauty that looked unreal, like she had stepped out of a cartoon.

He nodded once. “She’s beautiful. How is she a problem?”

Lizzie took a slow breath. “That’s your next mission from Godfather.”

Charles raised an eyebrow. “Her?”

“Yes.”

He looked at the photo again. “What am I supposed to do? Kill her? Why would I hurt such a beautiful soul?”

“No. That’s not the mission,” Lizzie said quickly. “The mission is to separate her from her fiancé.”

Charles paused. “Just separate them?”

“That’s all. Break the engagement.”

He leaned back, relaxed. “That’s easy. Godfather must have his reasons. This isn’t a problem at all.”

Lizzie added quietly, “Do you know who her fiancé is?”

Charles looked up. “Who?”

“Raphael of the Tristain family.”

The room went silent.

Charles’s eyes widened slightly. “The same Raphael I met today?”

Lizzie nodded. “Yeah. That one.”

Charles scoffed. “Breaking their engagement will piss him off completely.”

Lizzie shrugged. “Maybe you two were bound to fight anyway. I don’t know the Godfather's reasons.”

Charles sighed. “Every mission has a purpose. If Godfather assigned it, then there’s something to it.”

Lizzie studied him for a moment. “If you want to beat Raphael, you need to seriously upgrade your power.”

Charles said nothing.

“The technique he used today on you,” she continued, “is called Heavenfold Manifestation.”

Charles’s eyes narrowed.

“When he multiplies, each body has the same strength as the original. He controls all of them at once. Fighting them is like fighting dozens of Raphaels at the same level.”

She shook her head. “I don’t see how you win.”

Charles closed his eyes briefly, then nodded.

“I know,” he said quietly. He opened them again, his gaze steady. “But I’m not backing down. I would rather die in that arena.”

Lizzie sighed. “This will be dangerous.” She paused and added. “Godfather also wants you to meet the lady tomorrow. Before twelve p.m and talk to her.”

Charles frowned. “Tomorrow?”

“Yes. You have to meet her.”

He shook his head slightly. “What if I don’t?”

Lizzie met his eyes. “Then the mission is considered a failure. The inheritance will be null. You will lose everything.”
