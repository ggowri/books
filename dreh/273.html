Chapter 274

Two weeks had passed since Charles began the Yin–Yang process.

Most days were spent in deep meditation, sitting motionless for hours while guiding energy through his body. Other times the training turned brutal. He stood on his toes for hours while balancing a wooden stick across his shoulders, enduring pain without moving. Sometimes Mr. Jay struck him without warning just to test his stability. Every exercise pushed his body and mind to their limits.

Charles progressed far faster than Mr. Jay had expected.

Within only four days, he had already broken through multiple stages that normally took disciples months or even years. Now only the final stage remained.

Inside the quiet chamber, incense smoke drifted slowly through the air.

Mr. Jay watched him carefully and nodded. “You surprised me,” he said honestly. “I cannot deny it. You are extremely talented.”

Charles bowed respectfully. “Thank you, Master Jay.”

The old man tapped his staff lightly against the floor. “Listen carefully. There are classes among cultivators. From Class One to Class Five. You are currently in Class Three.”

Charles listened silently.

“Class Four is called the Grandmaster Stage,” Mr. Jay continued. “It is a realm that cannot be reached unless Yin and Yang are fully opened. That is why you are here.”

Charles nodded. “I understand.”

“I know you have fought many opponents and won,” Mr. Jay said calmly. “But at your current level, you can never defeat a Class Four cultivator… talk less of Class Five. The difference is absolute.”

Charles’s eyes hardened slightly. He had already felt that gap when facing Mr. Jacob.

“That is why opening your Yin and Yang is necessary,” Mr. Jay finished.

Charles bowed again. “Then I will complete it.”

Mr. Jay’s expression grew serious. “Many never reach the Grandmaster stage. The process is difficult, and many die attempting it. In this entire city, there are at most ten people between Class Four and Class Five.”

The weight of those words settled heavily in the room.

“So,” Mr. Jay asked quietly, “are you ready to step into that world?”

Charles lifted his head without hesitation. “Yes, Master Jay.”

The old man nodded slowly. “Good. This is the final stage. Once you complete it, your Yin and Yang will open.”

Charles sat cross-legged on the floor. “What must I do?”

Mr. Jay tilted his head slightly. “Have you ever performed a mind journey?”

Charles frowned, then nodded. “Yes. Once.”

“Good,” Mr. Jay said.

Charles’s brows tightened.

“You will journey into the Yin Realm,” Mr. Jay continued. “And there, you will find a mountain known as Yang Mountain. You must climb to its peak. Only then will your Yin and Yang awaken.”

Charles blinked. “That’s all? Just climbing a mountain?”

Mr. Jay chuckled softly. “If it were simple, everyone would become a Grandmaster.”

He stepped closer, his voice lowering.

“The Yin Realm reflects your fears, regrets, and weaknesses. Many lose themselves before even reaching the mountain. Others fall while climbing. Some never return at all.”

Charles absorbed every word, then nodded firmly. He wasn't even fretting. “I’m ready, Master.”

Mr. Jay looked directly into his eyes.

“There is something you must understand first,” he said gravely. “You only have a seventy percent chance of surviving.”

Charles listened quietly, then gave a small smile. “Master Jay, you shouldn’t worry. I already decided before coming here. That’s why I came. I’m ready.”

The old man studied his face for a long moment before nodding. From his sleeve, he brought out a simple silver bracelet and handed it to Charles.

“Wear this.”

Charles slipped it onto his wrist and noticed a small circular button embedded into the metal. “What is it?”

“A rescue device,” Master Jay explained. “If you reach a point where you cannot continue, press the button. Your consciousness will immediately return to this world. We will stabilize you… and you can attempt the trial again.”

Charles raised a brow slightly.

Mr. Jay added calmly, “I attempted this process ten times before I succeeded. So do not hesitate to press it. Survival is more important than pride.”

Charles nodded in understanding.

Then Master Jay’s expression turned serious again. “But listen carefully. This is the most important warning.”

He pointed at the bracelet.

“Never lose it.”

Charles frowned.

“If you lose that bracelet inside the Yin Realm,” Mr. Jay said slowly, “you will have no way to escape. The only way back will be to reach the peak of Yang Mountain.”

Charles processed the words and asked quietly, “So if I lose this… I can only return by reaching the summit?”

“Yes.”

“And if I lose it… and fail to reach the peak?”

Mr. Jay met his eyes without hesitation. “Then you will die.”

Silence filled the chamber.

 Charles also asked why don't he go with two bracelets. But the Realm has its rules and regulations. One can't even enter with toe bracelets in his hand. 

Charles looked down at the bracelet for a moment, then nodded calmly. “Understood. If things get dangerous, I will press it and try again.”

“Good,” Master Jay said. “Like I told you, I failed ten times before getting it right. Even your Godfather required twenty attempts.”

Charles exhaled lightly. “Alright. Anything else? I’m ready now, Master Jay.”

The old man smiled faintly. 

Soon, preparations for the ritual began.

Charles sat at the center of the chamber in a meditative position while symbols were drawn around him using ash and incense powder. Candles flickered, their flames bending as spiritual energy gathered in the air.

Master Jay moved slowly around him, chanting ancient words under his breath while tapping his staff against the ground at steady intervals.

The pressure in the room grew heavier.

Charles closed his eyes.

Energy surged.

A sudden force rushed into his mind, and his breathing stopped for a split second before his body went completely still.

Master Jay released a long sigh. “It begins,” he murmured. “I wonder how many trials he will need… even the Godfather struggled.”

He sat nearby, closing his own eyes as he monitored Charles’s spiritual state carefully.

Meanwhile—Charles gasped sharply.

His eyes snapped open.

At first, there was nothing. Only in the endless darkness, there was a big black pitch. 

Then slowly… light took over. 

Charles found himself standing on an island surrounded by silent gray waters stretching endlessly in every direction. The sky above swirled with dim silver clouds, neither day nor night.

A cold wind brushed past him.

He looked around carefully, alert. “So… this is the Yin–Yang Realm. It's beautiful ”

Suddenly—

Grrrrrrr.

A deep growl echoed behind him.
