Chapter 268

Mr. Jacob pressed the barrel of the gun against Lizzypie’s forehead.

She didn’t scream. 

She just stared at him coldly, her jaw tight, eyes steady.

“NO!” Charles roared, his voice cracking. “Don’t you fucking touch her!”

Mr. Jacob tilted his head. “Or what?”

Charles’ anger broke into desperation. “Please… just let her go. You can have me. Kill me instead. Just let her go.”

Raphael’s eyes narrowed slowly as he studied Charles’ reaction. Then a slow, cruel smile crept across his face.

“Oh…” he murmured. “So she’s important.”

He turned to his father. “Father, please. Let me kill this one.”

Mr. Jacob smirked. “Sure, son. Go ahead.”

He handed the gun to Raphael, who wheeled himself closer to Lizzypie.

“I’m going to make you feel the pain I felt when you embarrassed me,” Raphael sneered.

Charles spat back, “We had a fight. You lost. How is that my fucking problem?”

Raphael’s smile widened. “You are in a losing fight now. So watch carefully. Watch how your lover’s brains scatter across this floor.”

He wheeled behind Lizzypie and pressed the gun against the side of her head.

Charles shook his head frantically, tears burning in his eyes. “Please… don’t do this. Please. I will do whatever the fuck you want”

Lizzypie slowly closed her eyes.

“Bye,” Raphael whispered, his finger tightening on the trigger.

Just at that moment.

Swwwwrrrrrrr.

A strange sound sliced through the air.

Everyone froze.

It sounded like something spinning at impossible speed.

Then…

Thud.

A guard near the entrance suddenly split in half. For a split second, nobody understood what happened.

Swerrrr—

Another guard was cut clean through the torso.

Thud!

Bodies began dropping.

In seconds, chaos exploded.

The invisible force tore through the room like a living blade. Heads separated from shoulders. Torsos split at the waist. There was blood everywhere. 

Ten seconds.

Almost a hundred of Mr. Jacob’s men hit the floor.

Silence followed—except for the wet sounds of bodies collapsing.

One thing was clear.

Whoever—or whatever—had entered…was a monster. A very powerful one. 

Raphael’s confident smirk vanished. He looked around wildly.

“What the hell is that?!” someone shouted.

Swrrrrrrrr—

The sound came again.

And this time it passed directly through Raphael’s arm—the one holding the gun.

For a heartbeat, nothing happened.

Before he could process whatever was going on. 

His hand slid off at the wrist.

The gun dropped to the floor with a metallic clatter.

A second later, blood burst from the severed limb.

Raphael screamed. His scream tore through the warehouse.

“YAAAAA! My hand! My fucking hand is gone!”

Blood sprayed wildly from the severed wrist, splattering across the concrete and nearby guards. He thrashed in his wheelchair, shock and agony twisting his face as the stump poured red onto the floor.

“It’s gone! It’s gone!” he howled, staring at the spot where his hand had been seconds ago.

Mr. Jacob staggered backward, his cane nearly slipping from his grip. His entire body trembled.

“Who the fuck is that?!” he shouted, voice cracking for the first time.

Even Charles didn’t know.

He strained his eyes, trying to see through the chaos, heart pounding.

With that, a thunderous voice echoed through the building.

“All hail Godfather!!!”

Another voice followed.

“The Godfather is here!!!”

Charles lifted his head sharply.

No way. Mr. Vincent?

Mr. Jacob’s face was drained of color. He instinctively stepped back.

Thud!

Thud!!

Thud!!!

Heavy boots struck the ground in perfect rhythm.

From the shattered entrance, rows of mercenary soldiers marched in. They were dressed in black tactical armor, rifles held firmly, faces cold and disciplined. They lined up with mechanical precision.

Crash!!!

One of the warehouse walls exploded inward.

Through the dust and falling debris rolled an armored tank, its metal surface scarred and battle-worn. It stopped at the center behind the first line of soldiers.

In front of the line up men, a man was leading them.

Charles’ eyes widened.

Rain. 

He immediately recognized him at the front, leading the formation, posture straight and commanding.

More men. They kept pouring in and pouring in.

For nearly three minutes, soldiers flooded the warehouse until the entire place was filled wall to wall with armed mercenaries.

If someone counted, there would be at least a thousand.

Rain raised his fist and shouted, “All hail Godfather!”

In perfect unison—Every single mercenary dropped to one knee.

“All hail Godfather!!!”

The sound shook the warehouse like thunder.

The top hatch of the armored tank slowly opened.

Two elite soldiers climbed out first, scanning the area, thier weapons ready. Then they turned and extended their hands respectfully inward.

A figure emerged.

They stepped aside and assisted him down with careful precision, as if handling royalty.

Mr. Vincent.

He wore a long black coat over a tailored suit, gloves on his hands,  boots polished and heavy. His silver-streaked hair was combed back neatly. His face carried calm authority, no rush, no panic, just absolute control.

His eyes were sharp, cold and dangerous.

The air itself seemed to shift around him.

“All hail Godfather!” the army roared again.

Mr. Jacob swallowed hard.

Vincent began walking forward slowly, soldiers parting like the sea before him.

He stopped a few meters from Mr. Jacob.

“Long time,” Vincent said calmly.

Mr. Jacob stared at him. “Vincent… how dare you come here with troops? This is an act of war against the Trisian family. Can you handle the consequences?”

Godfather Vincent smiled faintly.

“I won’t repeat myself,” he said softly. “Order your men to surrender now. Release them immediately.” pointing at Charles and others. 

Mr. Jacob said nothing.

In Vincent’s hand was a strange weapon shaped like an S—the center a grip, both ends extending into curved twin blades. It hummed faintly.

Without another word, Vincent flicked his wrist. The blade spun.

Swrrrrrrrr.

It vanished from his hand and started running around like before. 

A split second later….

Cut!!!  

Raphael’s second hand dropped to the floor.

For a heartbeat, there was silence.

Then Raphael’s scream returned—louder than before.

“Oh Fuck no!”.
