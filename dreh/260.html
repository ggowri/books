Chapter 262

Everyone frowned at once.

Allison’s brows pulled together sharply. “What’s that supposed to mean? I didn’t call for you.”

Jimmy remained calm. “Actually, Mr. Charles informed me of the situation. I’m here to clear a few things up.”

All eyes locked on him.

“Speak, what the hell are you here to clear ” Mr. Gerald demanded.

Jimmy adjusted his glasses. “Mr. Moretti knew that Serena was not his biological daughter.”

“What?” The word burst from several mouths at once.

“My father knew!”.

“That's crazy!”.

Serena’s tear-stained face lifted slowly. “Is… is that true?”

Jimmy nodded. “Yes. He knew.”

The air felt thinner.

“I don’t know every private detail,” Jimmy continued carefully. “But from what I understand, during that period he suspected your mother had an affair. He ordered a DNA test quietly. When the result came back negative, he was devastated.”

Serena’s fingers tightened around Charles’ sleeve.

“But,” Jimmy added, “he later said the fault was his. He chose to forgive your mother. And he chose you.”

Allison’s shock twisted into anger. “That changes nothing! Even if he forgave her, she isn’t legally his daughter. She has no right to be CEO.”

Jimmy’s lips curved slightly. “Oh, but she is legally his daughter.”

Silence fell again.

“After discovering the truth,” Jimmy said, pulling a document from his briefcase, “Mr. Moretti filed for legal adoption. He completed all registration processes. Serena Moretti is his lawful daughter under the law.”

He handed the papers forward.

Allison snatched them and scanned the pages frantically. Her face drained of color.

“This… this can’t be real.”

Mr. Gerald grabbed the document next, reading quickly before scoffing. “This is nonsense. Why would my brother do such a thing?”

Charles stepped forward, his voice steady. “Because Mr. Moretti had vision. He valued capability over blood. He saw Serena’s potential and refused to lose her to your greed.”

Jimmy nodded. “The adoption was finalized years ago. It is airtight. Legally and publicly, Serena Moretti is his daughter. Nothing about today’s DNA result changes her status, her inheritance, or her authority as CEO.”

Serena stared at the document with trembling eyes.

Allison shook her head violently. “No. I won’t accept this. She manipulated everything!”

Allison’s chest heaved violently.

“I’m sure all of you are in this together,” she snapped, pointing accusingly at Charles and Jimmy. “You manipulated this entire mess. You fabricated some messed up document to protect her.”

Jimmy did not flinch. “The document is original. We can take it to court if you like. There is also a certified copy at the magistrate court where Mr. Moretti personally signed and filed it. You are free to verify.”

Allison looked like she might explode. The veins along her neck stood out as anger flooded her face. She had walked in certain victory. Certain that today would end Serena’s reign and place the company in her hands.

But this…

She refused to accept it.

“There’s something off here,” she said through clenched teeth. “And I swear I’m not backing down. In fact, I’ll call Collins. I’m sure you all know exactly how he’ll react to this.”

Charles let out a short, dry scoff. “Oh yes. I’m terrified.”

His tone turned sharper. “Call him. I’m right here.”

Mr. Gerald folded his arms. “Every time we clash with you and this lawyer, things magically favor Serena. It’s suspicious.”

Jimmy responded evenly. “All I am presenting is what Mr. Moretti instructed me to execute while he was alive. His decisions. His signature. His will.”

Allison gave a cold laugh. “I don’t care. If the law won’t remove her, the public will.”

Her gaze cut straight to Serena.

“I will go to every major outlet. I will tell them how you are an illegitimate child who manipulated your way into this family.  I will expose everything. With enough public pressure, you’ll step down yourself.”

A murmur spread across the room.

Serena swallowed hard.

She understood the danger in that threat. Public outrage could destroy reputations faster than any legal battle. The shareholders would panic. The board would fracture. Even if she kept her title, the pressure might crush her sanity.

For a moment, doubt flickered in her eyes.

Then Charles’ phone buzzed.

The sharp vibration cut through the tension.

He glanced down at the screen.

A message from Zander.

“Boss, we checked the room. We found a hidden camera. The person who killed Mr. Moretti was captured.”

Charles’ expression darkened.

Without looking up, he typed two words.

[Send it]

Charles had given Zander a private assignment days ago — find out who killed Mr. Moretti.

With Serena’s knowledge, they had already searched Mr. Moretti’s bedroom carefully. They knew he was a cautious man. Who always planted cam inside his room. And just as Charles suspected, they found it — a hidden camera installed discreetly inside the room.

Now, the footage had arrived.

A few seconds later, the video file dropped into Charles’ phone.

The room was still arguing when he opened it.

He watched.

Inside the recording, the bedroom appeared quiet. Then the door opened.

The killer walked in.

Mr. Moretti was seated near his bed. At first, they spoke calmly. Their voices were low, almost gentle. But gradually, the conversation grew heated. The killer’s tone sharpened. Mr. Moretti stood up.

The argument escalated.

The killer began shouting.

Mr. Moretti pointed firmly toward the door, clearly ordering the person to leave.

Then everything turned.

In one sudden motion, the killer reached behind, pulled out a knife, and lunged forward.

The blade flashed.

It sliced across Mr. Moretti’s throat.

Blood sprayed across the floor as he collapsed.

Charles froze.

His grip tightened around the phone.

Because the face captured clearly by the camera was not a stranger.

It was Allison.
