Chapter 292

.

Inside, the hall was breathtaking.

A massive crystal chandelier hung from the center of the ceiling, its thousands of sparkling pieces reflecting golden light across the polished marble floor. Smaller chandeliers surrounded it in perfect symmetry, creating a sea of glittering lights overhead. The ceiling itself was decorated with elegant patterns and soft gold trims that gave the room a royal atmosphere.

Outside the hall was just as impressive.

Luxury cars filled the parking area. Security guards stood at attention near the entrance, welcoming important guests while photographers snapped pictures of wealthy businessmen and elegant socialites arriving in expensive suits and gowns.

The atmosphere was lively.

Near the entrance, Tracy stood outside in a stunning evening dress, occasionally glancing toward the road.

She seemed distracted.

Collins, who stood beside her, noticed.

“Who are you looking for?” he asked curiously.

Tracy quickly looked away.

“No one,” she said with a scoff.

Collins smirked.

“Charles?”

Tracy rolled her eyes.

“Of course not.”

Collins chuckled. He knew Tracy still loved Charles. He didn't care. After today, Tracy would sign the company to him. He would vanish with over 200 million dollars. 

“He would never come,” he said dismissively. “He doesn’t have what it takes to show up in a setting like this.”

Just then—

A loud engine roared in the distance.

Everyone near the entrance turned their heads.

A red Lamborghini slowly pulled into the driveway.

The sleek exotic car glowed under the lights like a moving piece of art. Its low body, sharp edges, and polished surface instantly drew attention from every direction.

People began whispering.

“Who is that?”

“That car costs a fortune.”

The Lamborghini stopped smoothly in front of the hall.

For a moment the door stayed closed.

Then—The door lifted upward.

The first person stepped out.

Lizzypie.

She wore an elegant black dress that hugged her figure perfectly, her confident stride immediately commanding attention.

Then Natasha stepped out.

Her red gown flowed gracefully as she stood upright, her beauty striking enough to make several people freeze where they stood.

Next came Sapphire.

Her ocean-blue dress shimmered under the lights, her calm elegance making her look almost unreal.

Finally Gabriella stepped out.

Her white dress highlighted every curve effortlessly, giving her an angelic but seductive presence.

The entire entrance area fell silent.

People stared.

Some even forgot to breathe.

Each of them was unbelievably beautiful.

Together they looked like goddesses stepping out of a dream.

Whispers exploded instantly.

“What the hell…”

“Who are they?”

“They are insanely hot.”

“They look like celebrities.”

The atmosphere outside the hall suddenly turned electric.

Then the Lamborghini door opened again.

This time, Serena stepped out.

She wore a long silver evening gown that shimmered softly under the lights. The dress wrapped around her figure elegantly before flowing down to the floor like liquid light. Her hair rested neatly over her shoulders, and the delicate diamond earrings she wore sparkled every time she moved.

The moment she stepped out, several people gasped again.

“Another one?”

“She’s stunning.”

Before the whispers could settle, another figure stepped out of the car.

Charles.

He wore a perfectly fitted dark suit that highlighted his tall frame and calm presence. The moment he stepped beside Serena, he naturally took her hand.

The two of them together looked breathtaking.

Charles and Serena began walking forward first.

Behind them, Lizzypie, Natasha, Sapphire, and Gabriella followed.

The formation alone made the entire entrance freeze.

The whispers exploded.

“What?!”

“Who is that man?”

“The ladies are following him.”

“Damn… how is he related to them?”

“Those are the city’s goddesses!”

Some guests stepped aside instinctively as the group approached the entrance like a moving wave of beauty and confidence.

They walked calmly until they reached the front door of DeRoyal Hall.

That was when Tracy stepped forward and blocked their path.

Her eyes burned as she looked at Charles.

“What is all this?” she demanded sharply.

Charles sighed.

“What what?”

Tracy gestured angrily.

“This parade!” she snapped. “This is my company anniversary. Why would you bring all of them here?”

Charles looked at her calmly.

“You invited them all yourself, didn’t you?”

Tracy’s face tightened.

“Yes, but that doesn’t mean you should all come together like this,” she said bitterly. “You’re parading yourselves and stealing my spotlight. Everyone is looking at you!”

Serena smiled slightly.

“Well,” she said coolly, “maybe we’re just hotter than you.”

Tracy’s face turned red with anger.

Before she could respond, Collins stepped forward.

“Sister,” he said, looking directly at Serena.

His voice carried a mix of disappointment and accusation.

“Father left us a legacy, and yet you sent me away from home and now you parade yourself as some kind of sex object for this womanizer.”

He shook his head.

“I’m really disappointed.”

Serena didn’t even flinch.

“I don’t care what you think,” she replied calmly. “I love it this way. And the company has been doing much better without you.”

She smiled politely.

“Thanks for the concern though.”

Tracy scoffed loudly.

“Keep bragging,” she said coldly. “It’s only a matter of time before Collins becomes bigger than you.”

She glanced at Charles and the others.

“In fact, bigger than every one of you.”

Charles scoffed quietly.

He knew exactly why Tracy was saying that.

The so-called Akeem from Dubai story.

But after Lizzypie had investigated, they discovered something interesting. There really was a wealthy Akeem in Dubai, but the man had absolutely no idea who Collins was.

Not even a single connection.

Which meant Collins had been lying the entire time. Charles didn’t bother exposing him now though.

Instead he simply looked at Tracy calmly.

“Let us in, Tracy.”

Tracy didn't want to cause any commotion on her day, she warned. “You better be all in your best behavior today” 

Then she moved out of the way.
