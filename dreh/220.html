Chapter 220

Charles followed the maid’s gaze.

He saw  the window on the upper floor, flames pouring out violently, black smoke rolling nonstop. The fire there was serious. Way worse than the rest.

Hearing her words, several guards rushed toward the entrance at once.

People panicked and started shouting. 

Everyone was being evacuated successfully. Everyone except her.

Wailing broke out.

“She’s not even on her wheelchair!” someone cried. “Poor Miss Gabriella!”

A man turned sharply toward the maid. “Weren’t you supposed to be with her? Why wasn’t she on her wheelchair?”

The maid broke down completely. “She went to the toilet,” she sobbed. “She sent me to get something for her. I didn’t know this would happen.”

Charles’ eyes narrowed. Wheelchair. So she couldn’t walk.

That explained everything. Why she had no public appearances.

The guards who had rushed in suddenly stumbled back out, coughing hard.

“We can’t enter!” one of them shouted. “The fire is everywhere. It’s too intense!”

Flames surged higher through the windows.

A well-dressed man, clearly part of the family, yelled in panic. “Then go in again! Save my niece!”

The guards shook their heads. “We can’t just rush in. We have to wait for the firefighters. Call an emergency now!”

Another family member snapped back, “She will be dead before they arrive! Do something!”

The guards hesitated, clearly lost.

Charles looked up again. The flames were climbing fast.

His jaw tightened.

He remembered his past as firefighters. The way fires spread and they could never enter.

She wouldn’t last.

Without another word, Charles stepped forward.

A hand suddenly grabbed his arm.

“Who the fuck are you?” one of the guards barked. “Where do you think you’re going?”

Charles yanked his hand free. “To save her. Obviously.”

“She will die from smoke before the fire reaches her if we don’t act now.”

The well-dressed man stepped closer, eyes sharp. “You still haven’t answered the question. Who are you, and what do you think you are doing?”

“Saving her, we don't have time for this talk” Charles replied flatly.

One of the guards snapped, “How? The fire is everywhere. We already told you that. There’s nothing you can do.”

Another added quickly, “And we don’t even know you. Who’s to say you are not the one responsible for this fire?”

Charles exhaled slowly. Talking to them was pointless and would only waste time. 

His eyes hardened.

[Eyes of Satan activated]

[Flash Mode activated]

In the next instant, he vanished.

The guards froze.

“What the fuck—?” one of them shouted. “Where did he go?!”

Before anyone could react, Charles was already upstairs.

Fire raged everywhere. Flames crawled across the walls and ceiling, smoke thick enough to choke. Heat pressed in from all sides. The guards were not exaggerating at all. 

Then he heard it.

A weak, panicked voice cutting through the roar of the fire.

“Save me… I’m here… please… somebody save me.”

Charles turned onto the sound. It was a female voice. That had to be Gabriella.

Charles looked toward the room entrance. Fire swallowed the doorway completely. Flames rolled and snapped like a living thing.

[Flash Mode activated]

He rushed straight through the fire. The heat brushed past him.

Inside, it was worse. The room was burning like hell.

“Save me… I’m here…”

The voice was clearer now. The sound came from the toilet.

The fire hadn’t reached it yet. That was the only reason she was still alive.

Charles slammed the door open.

Thud!!!.

Then, he saw her. A round, beautiful face, pale with fear. Smoke clung to her hair. Her eyes widened the moment she saw him.

She screamed. “Who are you?!”

“Calm down,” Charles said quickly. “I’m here to save you.”

She shook her head in panic. “I don’t know you. Are you here to kidnap me? Please don’t. My father will give you whatever you want. Please spare me.”

Charles smiled faintly. “No time for that.”

He didn’t wait for permission.

He wrapped an arm around her and lifted her up, carrying her in his arms in the Princess style. 

Then he turned but stopped in his tracks because there was no exit.

Fire blocked every path.

And with her in his arms, his flashstep wouldn’t be fast enough anymore.

Meanwhile, outside, the panic hadn’t stopped.

People shouted. Guards ran back and forth. No one knew what was happening inside.

Then a black car screeched to a stop at the gate.

Mr. Belly jumped out.

“Where is my daughter?” he roared. “Where the fuck is my daughter?!”

The head of the guards rushed to him. “Sir, we tried to get in, but the fire is everywhere. She—”

Smack!!!.

Mr. Belly slapped him hard across the face.

“What the fuck are you saying?” he shouted. “You left my daughter there?”

He pointed at the building. “Go in now! If anything happens to my daughter, I will kill all of you!”

The guards exchanged looks.

“Why are you still standing there?” Mr. Belly screamed. “Enter right now!”

They hesitated, fear written all over their faces. Some took reluctant steps forward, then stopped again.

Just then, one of the maids narrowed her eyes, staring at the entrance.

Her voice trembled. “Wait…”

Everyone turned.

“I think… someone is coming out.”
