Chapter 280

-

Charles headed toward the stairs that led to the upper floor to the bar and made his way to his room

As soon as he climbed up the stairs. 

Collins walked back inside.

He had forgotten his lighter. His eyes scanned the room casually.

Then he froze.

That back and that posture. He immediately recognized it. 

His heart skipped.

Charles?

No… it couldn’t be.

Charles was already halfway up the stairs.

Collins stood there, stunned, his mouth slightly open. Why is he here? Did he follow me? Did he hear the call?

A thin layer of sweat formed on his forehead.

He turned sharply and walked to the bartender.

“The man who just left,” Collins asked quietly, trying to sound normal. “The one in black. Has he been here long?”

The bartender shrugged. “Yeah. Came in earlier. Been sitting right there.”

Collins swallowed.

“Did he hear… anything. Like maybe he heard what I was saying?” he asked carefully.

The bartender frowned. “It’s a bar. People hear things. You sat closer to him im sure he heard you well”

Collins forced a smile and walked away.

Damn it.

If Charles heard about him leaving the country… everything would collapse. The money. 

He had planned it perfectly.

Just a few more days and he would vanish with everything. Tracy's 200 million dollars.

Now this.

Collins stepped outside and paced near the parking lot, his jaw tight.

No. No. He couldn’t let this ruin him.

He pulled out his phone and dialed a number.

“Madam Sharon,” he said once the call connected. “I need your help.”

A lazy voice answered, amused. “Help? From me? What would that be”

“I don’t care. I need something arranged. Fast.”

“What kind of arrangement?” she asked smoothly.

Collins glanced toward the hotel entrance.

“I need two girls. Minor. Maybe 17 or 16. But they should look older to make it look… compromising. .”

There was a pause.

“well, I can do that, but it will cost you”. 

He gulped and said, “Fuck yeah, I will do whatever you want!”.

Madam Sharon arrived less than an hour later.

Collins was waiting in a dim corner of the parking lot when a black SUV pulled in. The engine went quiet. The door opened.

Two under age girls stepped out first. Both dressed boldly. One chewing gum with loud, lazy snaps. The other scrolling through her phone, clearly impatient.

Madam Sharon followed behind them, heels clicking against the concrete.

She looked at Collins from head to toe and smiled faintly. “Here they are. I didn’t know you had such… unique taste.”

Collins glanced at the women briefly and shook his head. “This isn’t for me.”

The gum-chewing one raised a brow. “Then what are we doing here?”

“I want to use them,” Collins said flatly. “To set someone up.”

Madam Sharon’s smile faded into understanding. “Ohhh. I see.” She folded her arms. “Then your price just increased.”

“Fine,” Collins replied immediately. “I don’t care.”

The second girl sighed. “Be quick. I have other customers.”

Collins stepped closer and lowered his voice. He then explained what he wanted to do to the girls. Even though he knew the girls were in illegal under age sex trafficking, horrible Collins wanted to use them.

××××

Upstairs, Charles had already showered and collapsed onto the bed. His body felt heavy from the trial. His muscles finally relaxed.

He was seconds from sleep when—

“Help me! Help! Someone help!”

His eyes opened instantly.

He frowned. Another scream followed. Louder. Panicked.

They were screaming so loud that he couldn't sleep. 

He recognized fear when he heard it.

Charles exhaled slowly. He was exhausted. and didn’t want trouble. But the screaming didn’t stop.

So he stood up and decided to check what was going on.

Outside his room, down the hallway, he saw a large man gripping two women by their wrists. They struggled violently.

“Let us go!” one shouted.

The man cursed and dragged them toward the stairwell.

Charles stepped forward.

“Hey.”

The man turned. “It’s none of your business.”

Charles’s eyes sharpened. “It became my business when you started rough handling them.”

The man scoffed. “You trying to be a hero?”

Charles didn’t answer.

“Fine,” the man growled. “Come prove it.”

He charged at Charles very fast motion. 

Heavy steps shook the hallway.

Charles shifted slightly to the side at the last second. The man’s fist swung past his face.

Charles grabbed his wrist mid-motion.

Twisted.

A sharp crack echoed.

The man screamed.

“YAAAA, the fuck!”

Charles didn’t hesitate. He stepped in and drove his elbow into the man’s ribs. Once. Twice. Precise. 

The man swung wildly with his other hand.

Charles ducked and delivered a short punch straight to his throat.

The man staggered.

Charles caught his hand again.

One finger and then bend it to the other side. 

Snap.

Second finger.

Snap.

The screams turned raw.

“Don’t,” the man begged, collapsing to his knees. “Please forgive me. Let me go!  I won't touch them again”

Charles shoved him backward. “Leave. Right now”

The man scrambled away, clutching his broken hand, disappearing down the stairs.

Silence returned to the hallway.

Charles looked at the two women.

“Are you okay?”

They stared at him, wide-eyed.

Downstairs, in the shadows, Collins clenched his jaw.

This was going as he wanted.
