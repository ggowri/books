Chapter 263

Charles frowned as he rewatched the video, his eyes narrowing, his jaw tightening with every second that replayed.

Serena noticed the shift immediately. “What is it?” she asked softly.

He didn’t answer. He just stared at the screen.

Her heart began to pound. Slowly, she stepped closer, pressing against his arm. “Charles… what are you looking at?”

Still no response.She leaned in and saw the screen.

The world seemed to tilt.

Her breath caught in her throat as the footage played again.  The argument and how the killer slit their father's throat. 

Serena’s fingers trembled.

Across the room, Allison frowned at them and scoffed. “What now? What the heck is wrong with you? Why are you looking at me like that?”

Serena’s lips parted but no sound came out at first. Then barely above a whisper, she said, “How could you…”

Allison’s brows furrowed. “How could I? what?”

Without warning, she snatched the phone from Charles’ hand.

Charles didn’t stop her.

The video was still playing.

Everyone saw it.

The room fell into a suffocating silence as the footage showed Allison lunging forward, the blade flashing under the light, Mr. Moretti collapsing.

Gasps erupted.

Someone cursed under their breath.

Mr. Gerald staggered back a step.

Allison’s face drained of color for half a second. Then she straightened. “What the heck is that? Why are you trying to frame me?” she snapped. 

“That video is fake.”

“Fake?” Charles said coldly. “How about we hand it to a forensic expert right now and have them run a full authentication?”

Allison gulped.

Serena stared at her, tears streaming freely now. “How could you, Allison? How could you kill Father?”

“I didn’t!” Allison screamed. “I don’t know what that is!”

Murmurs filled the room.

“That video looks real.”

“It doesn’t look edited.”

“I swear it’s real.”

Serena’s voice broke but her anger pushed through. “Stop lying, for God’s sake! You killed Father. What did he ever do to you? How could you?”

Allison’s composure finally cracked. Her face twisted with rage, years of resentment spilling out at once.

She screamed back, her voice shaking with fury.

“Because he deserved to fucking die.”

The words hit the room like a gunshot. For a second no one even breathed.

Allison had just confessed.

Serena stared at her, her lips trembling. “Allison…”

Allison lifted her chin, defiant. “Yeah. I fucking killed him. And what’s your business? None. Because he wasn’t even your father.”

A collective gasp rippled through the family.

Mr. Gerald exploded. “But he was my fucking brother!”

Another relative shouted, “How could you be so horrible, Allison?”

“She has to go to prison for this!”

Allison scoffed loudly. “Oh, shut up, all of you. I know you’re happy I did it. You all hated him. Because he only favored Serena. He disrespected everyone and favored her.”

Her eyes burned as she continued. “I went to him to ask for just twenty percent of his assets. Just twenty. But he refused. He insisted on giving everything to Serena.”

She pointed violently at Serena. “Then I found out you are a bastard overall. I confronted him with it. He knew. He always knew. And he still wouldn’t change anything. Even knowing you weren’t his daughter, he still wanted to give you everything.”

Her voice cracked with fury.

“So yeah. I snapped. And I killed him.”

Silence swallowed the room whole.

Serena slowly covered her mouth as if trying to hold herself together, but her knees gave way and she sank to the floor, sobbing.

The weight of the confession hung heavy in the air.

Nearly two hours passed in stunned quiet, broken only by low murmurs and distant phone calls as some family members contacted authorities.

One by one, they began to stand and drift away, whispering among themselves.

Serena remained curled in the corner of the room, her shoulders shaking.

Charles finally walked over and crouched beside her. “Are you okay?”

She let out a hollow laugh through tears. “No. I’m not. How do you expect me to handle all this? This is unbelievable. First I find out I’m a bastard. Then my sister killed Father because of it.”

Charles’ voice softened but stayed firm. “None of this is your fault. Remember that.”

She didn’t look at him.

“The family is full of entitlement,” he continued quietly. “They would do anything for power. Your father knew that. That’s why he trusted you. He wanted you to fix what they would destroy.”

At the mention of your father, Serena stiffened.

She didn’t even know how to respond to that anymore after learning he wasn’t her biological father.

Charles looked at Serena carefully. “So what do we do about Allison? We hand her over to the police?”

Serena wiped her face and slowly shook her head. “No.”

Charles frowned. “No?”

“That will create another public disaster,” she said quietly. “A daughter kills her father? The media will tear the company apart. Shareholders will panic. The board will fracture. It won’t solve anything. Another branch of the family will use it to start a war.”

Charles studied her. “So how do you want to handle it?”

Serena inhaled deeply, then pushed herself to her feet. Her legs were still weak but her voice steadied as she turned to face Allison.

“I can’t believe you could do that.”

Allison gave a hollow laugh. “I did it anyway. Call the police. I’m ready to serve my time.”

Serena stared at her for a long moment. There was anger in her eyes, but also exhaustion. “I don’t want to drag this any further,” she said at last. “This is what we will do.”

Everyone looked up.

“You will choose,” Serena continued. “Either you take forty percent of the family assets and leave. Completely. You will renounce the Moretti name. You will have no claim, no seat, no voice in this family or company ever again.”

Shock swept across the remaining relatives.

“Forty percent?” someone whispered.

“She killed him!”

Mr. Gerald stepped forward angrily. “Are you insane? She murdered my brother!”

Serena’s voice did not rise, but it cut through the noise. “And if this explodes publicly, it will destroy everything he built. His legacy will become a scandal. Is that what you want?”

Everyone went mute. Serena said, “I'm ready to give you this, on one condition”.
