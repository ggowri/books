Chapter 266

The next morning Charles woke with a low groan, stretching as sunlight filtered into the room. He felt surprisingly refreshed.

He turned his head. The space beside him was empty. Gabriella was already gone.

At that moment, his phone rang.

Serena.

He picked up. “Hello.”

Her voice came through slightly concerned. She asked about how he had left abruptly the previous day and whether everything was okay.

“Perfectly okay,” Charles replied calmly. “Everything is fine.”

There was a small pause before he asked, “What about the other document?”

“I got it,” Serena said. “But just like Allison, I don’t fully understand it. There are pictures attached… and some technical reports.”

“Bring it when we meet,” Charles said. “We will go through it properly.”

“Okay,” she replied softly. Then her tone shifted. “I don’t even know how to thank you enough. You are always there for me.”

“It’s nothing,” Charles answered simply.

After the call ended, he bathed and dressed.

When he stepped out, he saw Gabriella sitting with the others in the living area. Natasha, Sapphire, and Lizzypie were in casual clothes, but Gabriella was fully dressed, looking bright and excited.

“I want to meet my father today,” Gabriella said immediately when she saw him. “I want to surprise him. Show him I can walk. He will be over the moon. I swear, I can’t wait.”

Charles smiled. “Okay. I will accompany you.”

Lizzypie grinned mischievously. “Hope you both enjoyed your last night,” she teased.

Charles scoffed and gave her a look. She loved putting people in awkward positions. The others chuckled lightly.

Before anyone could continue, Gabriella’s phone buzzed.

She glanced at it casually. “Talking of the devil,” she muttered as she opened the message.

Her expression changed instantly.

The color drained from her face.

Charles noticed. “What’s… wrong?”

Her fingers trembled. “My… my dad has been captured.”

“What?!”

Everyone rushed toward her.

On the screen was a message from Raphael, the heir of the Trisian family—the same family Charles had defeated before.

Attached was an image.

Mr. Belly tied to a chair. A gun pressed against his head.

Below it was a text.

If you don’t want your father to die, bring the company file of ownership. I will send you the location. Meet us there.

Almost immediately after the message, another notification came in. It was the location. 

Gabriella’s hands began to shake as she stared at the screen. Her breathing turned uneven.

“No… no…” she whispered, her voice breaking. “They captured my father. They’re going to kill him.”

Tears welled in her eyes and she began to whimper helplessly.

Sapphire’s expression hardened with anger. “How could the Trisian family do this? Charles won the bet. They promised they wouldn’t disturb Mr. Belly or Gabriella again. What is all this?”

“They’re going to kill him,” Gabriella cried softly. “Oh no… what should I do?”

Charles stepped forward and held her shoulders firmly. “Chill. Chill. Don’t panic.”

She looked up at him, eyes filled with fear.

“Nothing is going to happen to your father,” he said steadily. “You’re going to do exactly what they asked.”

Gabriella blinked. “What?”

“You will take the ownership file and give it to them,” Charles continued calmly. “We will be behind you. Don’t worry. Me and Lizzypie will handle everything else.”

Gabriella sniffled. “I just keep dragging you into trouble. You have never had a rest because of me.”

Charles shook his head. “This isn’t even about you. Godfather gave me a mission to finish the Trisian family. Maybe this is the time.”

Lizzypie nodded firmly beside him. “We end it today.”

With that, the three of them began preparing to leave.

Charles turned to Gabriella. “Stay on the wheelchair.”

“They still think you can’t walk. Let’s keep it that way. We will catch them off guard.”

She nodded weakly.

As they headed toward the door, Natasha and Sapphire followed them outside.

“Call us if you need any help,” Natasha said seriously.

“We will,” Charles replied.

And with that, they drove off toward the location.

Meanwhile, inside an unfinished industrial building owned quietly by the Trisian family

Mr. Belly was tied to a steel chair in the center of the open floor. Thick ropes bound his arms and chest so tightly the veins in his neck bulged with strain.

In front of him stood Mr. Jacob of the Trisian family, leaning on his polished walking stick.

Beside him sat Raphael in a wheelchair, his expression dark and impatient.

Around them were nearly four hundred armed men forming a wide circle. Guns hung from their shoulders. 

Mr. Belly glared coldly at Mr. Jacob. “I swear you will regret this. Untie me right now, old man.”

Mr. Jacob tapped his stick against the concrete floor. “Untie you? We can do that… if you agree to continue sponsoring the Trisian family.”

Mr. Belly sneered. “We already made that clear. We are no longer supporting you. You duped me. You didn’t help my daughter yet you kept collecting my money. And Charles won the bet. You promised not to disturb us again.”

Mr. Jacob’s jaw tightened. “That was because of my idiot son. And your daughter can never walk again. How is that my fault? I didn’t promise anything personally. We’ve been in partnership for thirty years and you suddenly walk away? No. That is not how this works.”

Mr. Belly barked a harsh laugh. “Thirty years. Thirty fucking years and you built nothing. How is that my fault? You just spent and expected us to fund you forever. I am no longer sponsoring you whether you like it or not.”

Mr. Jacob’s expression darkened. “We’ll see if that remains your decision when your cripple daughter arrives.”

Mr. Belly’s eyes burned with fury. “Whatever you are planning, I repeat, you will regret it.”

At that moment, one of the armed men hurried inside, slightly out of breath.

“Miss Gabriella is here.”
