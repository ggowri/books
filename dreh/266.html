Chapter 267

Charles groaned, his head thick and heavy, fog clouding his thoughts. At first, everything was a blur, the shapes and shadows of the room moving strangely before his vision slowly sharpened.

“Charles? Charles, are you okay?”

The voice cut through the haze. Gabriella.

He blinked rapidly, trying to focus, and realized he was tied to a steel chair. Bound tightly, his arms locked at his sides, chest pressed against the cold metal.

In front of him, Gabriella sat in her wheelchair, wide-eyed but alert. To his surprise, Lizzypie was also tied to another chair nearby, struggling against the ropes. Mr. Belly, still bound from earlier, was seated not far off.

“Are you awake?” Mr. Belly whispered, his voice low, eyes darting around.

Raphael’s wheelchair rolled smoothly in front of Charles. 

“What the fuck did you do to me?” Charles croaked, trying to strain against the bindings, but his limbs felt sluggish, as though heavy clamps held them down.

Mr. Jacob stepped forward, cane tapping lightly against the concrete. His smile was cold, measured. “Do you know what we, the Trisian family, are known for?”

 “Poison… and making dangerous chemicals?” Raphael chimed in.

Mr. Jacob’s smile widened. “Exactly. What you just inhaled will leave you incapacitated for about four hours. You will be able to watch… but you won’t be able to stop anything that’s about to happen.”

Charles felt the lethargy hit him fully. His strength drained, his attempts to move weak and shallow.

Mr. Belly’s voice cut through the tension. “I’ve signed it. Everything you demanded. The file. It’s done. Please… let us go now.”

He had already signed. The ownership of Synx World was in Trisian hands.

Mr. Jacob chuckled darkly, tilting his head. “We should let you go? That way, you will come back for us later.”

He stepped closer, eyes cold. “But we’re not doing that. No. We’re going to kill every person in this room. Every last one of you.”

Mr. Belly yelled, his voice cracking with disbelief, “Why are you doing this? I gave you what you wanted! I signed everything!”

Mr. Jacob didn’t even hesitate. “Because you are not the type to back down once you walk out of this room. You will regroup. You will retaliate. So we better eliminate the problem now.”

Charles tried again to force his body to move. He focused every ounce of strength into his wrists, his shoulders—anything. 

But it was useless. His muscles felt disconnected from his will. He could only watch.

“Please,” Gabriella begged, her voice trembling. “Just let us go.”

Mr. Jacob slowly lifted his gun and pointed it directly at Mr. Belly’s head. The room fell into a suffocating silence.

“We will start here,” he said calmly, eyes flicking toward Charles. “So you can watch, hero.”

Mr. Belly didn’t flinch. He straightened as much as the ropes allowed and said quietly, “If you want to kill me, go ahead. But please… let the others go.”

Mr. Jacob laughed. “Oh, I am doing that.”

The click of the gun being cocked echoed sharply.

“No, Father!” Gabriella screamed.

In one sudden movement, she pushed herself up from her wheelchair.

Everyone froze.

She stepped forward Standing on her own two feet.

She rushed in front of Mr. Belly, spreading her arms wide to shield him.

“Please don’t kill my father!”

Raphael’s eyes widened. “What the—”

Mr. Belly stared in shock. “Gabriella…? Gabriella… you can walk?”

Tears streamed down her face as she nodded. “Yes, Father. I can. I was treated… they helped me. I wanted to surprise you when everything was over.”

Mr. Belly’s eyes filled with tears, pride and disbelief washing over his fear. “You’re… you are walking. My daughter is walking…” His voice broke. “I’m the happiest man alive right now. Even if I die, I can die in peace.”

Even Mr. Jacob looked genuinely stunned. The Trisian family had spent years trying to find a cure for her condition and failed. Yet somehow, she had recovered.

“Impressive,” Mr. Jacob muttered. “Very impressive. Who cured you?”

Gabriella ignored the question. She dropped to her knees in front of him.

“Please,” she begged, bowing her head. “Let my father go. We’ll give you whatever you want. The company, the assets, anything. Just stop this.”

Raphael’s face darkened. “Your father humiliated me in front of the entire world. He let them laugh at me. He destroyed my name. He deserves to die.” Raphael just blaming everybody

Gabriella turned toward him, still kneeling. “If you’re angry, take it out on me. Don’t hurt him. Please.”

Mr. Jacob motioned to the guards to drag Gabriella away.

They grabbed her arms and pulled her back as she struggled wildly.

“Let me go! Don’t do anything! Please, don’t hurt my father! No—!” she screamed, kicking and twisting, but the guards held her tight.

Mr. Jacob didn’t even look at her.

He raised the gun and pointed it straight at Mr. Belly’s forehead.

“Any last words?” he asked casually.

Mr. Belly turned his head toward Gabriella. His eyes softened despite the ropes digging into his wrists.

“You are the most wonderful thing that has ever happened to me,” he said gently.

Then he faced Mr. Jacob.

“Fuck you.”

Mr. Jacob smirked.

He pulled the trigger.

Boom!

The shot exploded through the room. The bullet tore through Mr. Belly’s head, exiting the other side. Blood sprayed across the concrete floor and nearby crates as his body jerked once before going limp.

His head dropped forward.

Silence followed for half a second—

“Nooooooo!” Charles roared, his voice cracking with helpless rage. He fought against the restraints with everything he had, but his body refused to respond.

Gabriella’s scream was even louder.

“Yaaaa! No! Father! Father! No!!!”

She tried to throw herself toward him, but the guards restrained her, gripping her arms as she sobbed uncontrollably, her knees nearly buckling beneath her.

Mr. Jacob calmly wiped the blood spatter from his cheek with a handkerchief.

“Well,” he said coldly, glancing at his watch, “I don’t have much time. Let’s move on to the next person”

He nodded toward Lizzypie.

The guards dragged her chair forward.

Lizzypie’s face had gone pale, her breathing shallow as she stared at Mr. Belly’s lifeless body on the floor.

Mr. Jacob walked slowly toward her, gun still in hand.
