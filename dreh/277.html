Chapter 278

Mr. Jay’s eyes were fixed on the floating image of Charles’s body, there was blood all over him.

“Shit… he can’t make it,” Mr. Jay muttered, voice tight with worry. “He can’t.”

One of his students stepped forward nervously. “Master… can’t we call him back?”

Mr. Jay shook his head slowly. “He’s in the Yin–Yang Realm. Once he’s there, we can never just pull him out.” His eyes narrowed, scanning the energy ripples surrounding the mountain.

 “The only thing I could do is enter the realm myself… but I doubt I could add much. He already lost his bracelet, and even if I brought another, it wouldn’t work here.”

He exhaled slowly, leaning on his staff. “We just… have to hope he survives this.”

Meanwhile, in the Yin–Yang Realm, Charles continued his climb. Every movement tore at his chest, every breath stabbed through his ribs, but he forced himself upward.

“I… I will reach the peak. I can do it,” he muttered, gritting his teeth. He tried to motivate himself, forcing the pain in his body aside. His fingers clawed into every crack, every rough edge of stone, and his legs pushed against the mountain with all the strength he had left.

He was almost there. He could see the peak stretching above him, he pushed harder, channeling every ounce of energy into climbing.

Suddenly, a rustling hiss echoed from the mountain.

Snrrrrrr!

Snakes burst from the crevices along the rock face, their glistening scales flashing in the dim light. One lunged at his hand, biting deep. Pain exploded in his palm, sharp and searing.

“Fuck!” Charles yelped, jerking his hand back and searching frantically for another grip. But the venom was already moving through his veins, numbing and burning, spreading like wildfire.

He clenched his jaw, forcing himself to keep going. He moved faster, trying to outrun the poison, to reach the summit before it slowed him too much.

Snrrr! Another snake struck. Then another. Each bite sent waves of agony through his arms and shoulders, his grip weakening with every strike.

The mountain was alive with them. Venomous, relentless. His hands throbbed, his vision blurred, and for a moment, he couldn’t hold on. His fingers slipped from the stone.

He gasped, trying to catch another edge, but the grip was gone. Gravity pulled him downward, and he started to fall.

“No… no, no, no!” he shouted, reaching blindly for anything to save himself. Air rushed past his face as he plummeted, the ground coming closer. He knew if he hit it, he wouldn’t survive — he would crumble into the rocks, crushed.

His mind raced, desperate. What can I do? There’s nothing to grab… nothing!

The venom burned through his veins, making his body heavy, his reflexes sluggish. He swung his arms, searching for a hold, for a jutting rock, for anything. But there was nothing.

Charles’s eyes were tightly shut as he fell, expecting a sudden, crushing impact. This is it… he thought. This is the end.

But nothing happened. No thud, no pain, nothing.

Blinking, he opened his eyes and froze. He was hanging in mid-air, suspended as if gravity itself had vanished. “Uhn… what the fuck?” he muttered, shock spreading through him. He hadn’t done anything to stop himself. He wasn’t controlling it—it just happened.

The wind whistled past him as he floated upward slowly, his body moving effortlessly, guided by some unseen force. He didn’t even attempt to climb the mountain. Instead, he drifted, each second bringing him closer to the summit.

Finally, he gently touched the peak, his feet resting on solid stone. He gasped, his chest rising and falling rapidly. 

Almost immediately, he began feeling the pain in his chest ease. The venom from the snake bites retreated from his veins, and the agony from the black arrows dulled, leaving him stunned. His body felt lighter, cleaner, restored.

He frowned, looking around. The wind whipped hard, tugging at his hair and robes. “What the fuck just happened to me?” he muttered. “I nearly fell down…”

A sharp, feminine voice screamed suddenly, echoing across the summit. “Hey, long pal!”

Charles whipped around, frowning. Did I just hear that?

“Yes, you did,” the voice said again, playful yet familiar.

His brow furrowed. He had heard that voice somewhere before. Then it clicked. “Wait… Aries? ARIES FROM THE HOLE REALM?” he shouted.

The voice laughed. “Yaaa! You remember me!”

“Yeah… I remember you,” Charles said, still scanning the air. “How did you know I’m here?”

“Well,” she replied, her tone softening, “I can sense your presence in any realm. I store it in my memory.”

Charles raised an eyebrow. “I must be very important for you to remember that.”

Aries giggled. “So important… I think I’m in love with you.”

Charles let out a low mutter, shaking his head. “Ugh… that’s a joke, right?”

“How can a spirit love a human?” she said teasingly.

Charles nodded slowly. “I thought as much.”

“You saved me from dying today,” he said after a pause, voice quiet. “Phew.”

“Yeah, I did,” Aries replied, relief in her tone. “You would have split into two if I didn’t intervene.”

Charles exhaled, still catching his breath. “You even healed me… I can’t repay you much for this.”

Aries laughed softly. “You gave your button to someone else. I don’t even know what to call you.”

Charles scoffed, shaking his head. “Stupid.”

“You must be here to open your Yin and Yang,” Aries said, her voice warm and encouraging.

He nodded, gazing at the swirling clouds around the peak.

“Well,” she continued, “you are now at the summit. All you need to do is meditate, focus your energy… and you will open your Yin and Yang, then return to your world.”

Charles exhaled slowly, if not for Aries, all this won't be possible.
