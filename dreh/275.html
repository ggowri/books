Chapter 276

Quincy’s eyes went wide, and he let out a sharp laugh, half-shocked, half-relieved. “You… you want to give me your button?”

Charles nodded calmly. “Yes. You can use it.”

Quincy shook his head and scoffed, still staring at him. “Technically… I can use it, but… that’s basically like you committing suicide. Look at you — that monster already hit you hard. You can’t even fight it off properly now. You would definitely need it. What are you going to do?”

Charles glanced down at his own chest, blood still wet on his shirt. The man was right. But Quincy wouldn’t survive long in this realm without a way home. He sighed, thinking quickly.

“I… don’t worry,” Charles said finally. “I already know the answers to that riddle. And… I have another way to get home if I need it.” he lied. 

Quincy’s frown deepened. “Are you for real? If not, I’m telling you… you give me this, you die. I am not expecting it from you.”

Charles shook his head. “Don’t worry about me. Worry about yourself.”

He slowly offered the bracelet. Quincy’s eyes widened, disbelief and relief mixing in his expression. “I don’t believe it… there’s someone as humane as you? I swear I’ll never forget this. What’s your name? Where do you live?”

Charles shrugged, a small smirk on his lips. “I didn’t do it to expect any rewards.”

Quincy stared at him, then carefully took the bracelet and fastened it onto his wrist. Without hesitation, he hugged Charles tightly, almost crushing him. “Thanks so much! You know what? I swear I will return to this place one day… to make sure you’re safe.”

Charles shook his head gently. “You shouldn’t. It’s dangerous.”

Quincy ignored him. “Thanks, Charles!”

With that, he pressed the small button on the bracelet. In a flash of light, he vanished.

Charles exhaled slowly, a small smile tugging at his lips. Somehow, he felt… happy. He had saved someone. That was worth the pain.

Glancing down at his own wounds, still bleeding but not too severe, he flexed his fingers and stood. Time to climb the mountain.

Meanwhile, in the human world, Mr. Jay’s eyes widened in shock as he saw the bracelet missing. “What the heck… he lost the bracelet? How… how is he going to return?” His voice shook. “He can’t even finish on his first trial! Oh God… no!”

There was no way to slip another bracelet to him. It wouldn’t work. Mr. Jay’s hands trembled as he realized the danger.

Back in the Yin–Yang Realm, Charles moved quickly, heading toward the mountain. Its peak stretched impossibly high, shrouded in illusion. The smooth gray surface glimmered faintly, with only tiny edges breaking its uniformity — a clear signal of how treacherous the climb would be.

He looked around. No monsters. Nothing. Just the gray mist and the looming mountain.

He placed his foot carefully on the first small ledge, testing the surface. “Uhnnn…”

Then, without warning, something surged from the mountain — a strike, faster than he could see, like a massive fist punching outward.

Charles’s body was slammed backward violently. Air left his lungs. His hands scraped against the mountain’s smooth edge as he tumbled down. He hit the ground hard, dust and small rocks flying.

Pain shot through his side. His chest heaved. His legs shook.

He clenched his teeth and pushed himself upright. 

Charles wiped the sweat from his brow, frowning as he muttered under his breath, “What is that…”

He looked down at the mountain, a rock was now stretching impossibly high, like it was growing then it stopped. It was like 5 feet high. Though, it was a rock,  yet, unmistakably resembling a face. Two deep hollows formed eyes, a sharp ridge made a nose, and a wide crack shaped a mouth. The natural stone glimmered faintly under the silver mist, giving it a life-like aura.

Charles stepped back instinctively. His heart pounded.

Then a deep, rumbling voice echoed from the rock itself.

“I am the Yang Mountain Monster,” it said. “I guard this holy place. A place for only the intelligent and wise to ascend. For you to climb this mountain, you must first answer my riddles.”

Charles swallowed hard. His throat felt dry. This… this was exactly what Quincy had warned him about.

“Go ahead,” Charles said, trying to steady his voice.

The stone mouth curled slightly, though it was rigid and unmoving.

“If you fail,” it continued, “a black arrow will be shot into your heart. You have three chances. Use them all without backing down… and the three arrows will kill you.”

Charles felt a chill.

“You… have a choice to walk away at any time,” the mountain added, voice echoing like thunder through stone.

Charles exhaled slowly, checking his body for injuries. His ribs still ached from the previous fight, his legs were scraped and sore, but he could move.

“I… hope I get this in the first trial,” he muttered to himself. Then, louder, “Go ahead.”

The mountain’s eyes seemed to glint. “Very well. This test will measure your intelligence. Only those who prove themselves may climb.”

Charles nodded, his hand flexing slightly, ready to steady himself.

The rocky mouth opened slowly. “Now… here go your riddles…”

[I was here before you were born,

I will remain long after you are gone.

I heal all wounds, yet cause them too,

I'm something you spend, but never own.

I can be wasted, saved, or lost,

yet no one can touch me or see me pass.

Kings and beggars bow to me equally —

What am I?].

Charles frowned and gulped down hard. This was hard as he had thought. 

The mountain said, “You have a minute interval for each answer you will provide. If you fail, it would be counted as lost. And the black arrow will be shot”.
