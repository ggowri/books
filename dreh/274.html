Chapter 275

Charles turned slowly toward the sound, his eyes narrowing as the growl echoed again across the gray waters. 

He exhaled once and steadied himself.

“Eyes of Satan… activated.”

A faint crimson glow flickered deep within his pupils. The world sharpened instantly. He waited. Looking everywhere at once. 

The bushes near the shoreline shook violently.

Someone burst out from inside, stumbling and nearly falling into the shallow water. It was a man, thin and pale, his clothes torn, his face covered in sweat and dirt. His eyes were wide with pure terror.

The moment he saw Charles, he screamed.

“Run! Run! It’s coming! It’s coming!”

Charles frowned, confused. “What—”

Grrrrrrrrr!

The ground trembled.

The bushes exploded outward.

The creature that emerged. It looked like a big lion. It was enormous, nearly twice the height of a man, its body twisted like melted flesh forced into shape. Black veins crawled across gray skin that pulsed as if alive. Its arms were too long, dragging against the ground with clawed fingers that carved deep lines into the soil.

Its mouth stretched unnaturally wide, splitting its face almost in half. Rows of jagged teeth rotated slowly inside like grinding blades. Thick saliva dripped and sizzled when it touched the ground.

Its eyes… were empty holes burning with dull red light.

The monster lunged.The man screamed and ran past Charles without looking back.

Charles clicked his tongue and immediately sprinted forward too. He used his power which made him run faster. past the man. 

“Yaaa! No help! No help!” the man cried as he ran wildly.

Charles glanced back. The monster moved terrifyingly fast, its limbs bending at impossible angles as it crawled and leaped at the same time.

The man had tripped and the monster was already over him. 

The monster’s claw slammed down beside him, blocking his escape. The creature lowered its head, mouth opening wide enough to swallow him whole.

The man shrieked, voice cracking. “Help! Help! Please!”

Charles stopped running.

For a brief moment, he considered leaving.  He still has his own mission. 

The man screamed again — raw, and desperate.

Charles sighed.

“Tch… ”

He turned and dashed back.

The monster snapped its jaws downward—

—but Charles slid in at the last second, grabbing the man by the collar and dragging him backward across the ground.

The creature’s teeth slammed shut inches away, creating a thunderous crack.

“Move!” Charles shouted.

The monster roared, furious its prey had escaped. One massive claw swung toward them.

Charles pushed the man aside and raised his arm.

Boom!

The impact threw Charles backward several meters. Pain shot through his bones as he rolled across the dirt before forcing himself back onto his feet.

“he is so fast…” he muttered.

The monster charged again.

Charles stepped forward instead of retreating.

He twisted his body and drove a punch into its side.

Thud!

It felt like striking solid iron. The creature barely moved before its tail whipped around and smashed into Charles’s ribs.

Crack.

Air left his lungs as he was sent flying into the sand.

The man watched in horror. “We are going to die!”

“Stop shouting,” Charles growled as he stood again, wiping blood from his lip.The monster leaped, claws descending.

Charles activated his vision fully. Time seemed to slow. He saw every muscle movement before the attack landed.

He sidestepped.The claws slammed into the ground.

Charles grabbed its arm and spun, using its own momentum to flip the massive body sideways. The creature crashed into the water, sending gray waves exploding outward.

“Now!” Charles shouted without thinking.

The man hesitated but grabbed a broken branch nearby and struck the monster’s head repeatedly.

The creature roared and rose again, enraged. Its claws tore across the man’s chest before Charles could react.

Blood sprayed.

The man collapsed, screaming.

Rage flickered in Charles’s eyes.

The monster lunged again, mouth wide.

Charles stepped directly toward.

He ducked under its jaws, drove his shoulder into its torso, and unleashed a rapid series of strikes — ribs, throat, jaw, heart.

Each blow landed heavier than the last.

The creature staggered.

Charles gathered his energy and slammed both palms forward.

Boom!

A shockwave burst outward, sending the monster crashing flat onto its back. The ground cracked beneath its weight.

It struggled to rise.

[Hell fire beam activated].

Charles activated this and sent it directly to the monster leg.

“Yaaaaaa”, the monster shrieked in pain. and instantly,  his leg burned and he dropped on the floor. 

It was already weakened and found it hard to stand up. 

The man slowly pushed himself to his feet, breathing hard as he stared at the fallen creature. His eyes moved back to Charles, filled with disbelief.

“Wow… you are strong, you defeat it!” he said, shaking his head.

Charles held his side where the earlier strike had landed. Blood stained his clothes, but he simply nodded once, calm and quiet.

The man spat toward the monster. “Good. Finally. Let me finish it. This bastard has kept me trapped here for four months.”

Charles frowned immediately. “Four months… here?”

Before he could ask more, the man picked up a sharp piece of broken stone and began walking toward the monster.

Charles stepped forward and grabbed his arm.

“Hey. Leave it.”

The man froze and turned sharply. “Uh… the hell? Are you serious? That thing will heal itself and kill us!”

Charles looked at the creature. Its twisted body was already moving slightly. The cracked skull slowly pulled itself back together, gray flesh knitting like melting wax reversing time.

He remembered the words of Aries, the spirit of the Hole Realm.

The monsters are not evil. They are inhabitants. To them, humans are the intruders.

Charles released a slow breath. “It was defending its territory,” he said calmly. “We are the trespassers here. Let it be.”

The man stared at him like he had lost his mind. “Are you crazy? That thing has hunted me for months! Months! You want me to just let it be, also if he regenerate it will attack us back”

Behind them, the monster’s fingers twitched. A low growl vibrated weakly from its chest as regeneration continued.
