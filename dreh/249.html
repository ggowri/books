Chapter 248

Boots struck the floor in a slow, deliberate rhythm as the man stepped fully into the room.

He looked striking, sharp features with a calm, unreadable face. His hair was dark and neatly combed back, his suit perfectly tailored. There was something about him that felt foreign and disciplined at the same time. He was an American-Japanese. 

The moment the thugs saw him, everything changed.

They lowered their guns and stepped back.

Nero himself dropped to his knees despite the pain in his shattered fingers.

“All hail Lord Yachina!” he shouted.

The others followed instantly, bowing their heads. “All hail Lord Yachina!”

Lord Yachina walked forward without hurry.

He was the true head of the Yachina gang. Nero was only second in command, running operations because Yachina was rarely around. In a whole year, he might appear three times. And whenever he appeared, something big followed.

That alone made Nero’s stomach tighten.

What could Lord Yachina be doing here today?

Even Ivan bowed his head quickly.

Only Charles remained standing, watching, trying to understand the shift in power.

Lord Yachina stopped in the center of the room. His voice was deep and controlled.

“What is happening here?”

Nero scrambled forward on his knees. “Boss! I didn’t know you were coming today. I would have prepared—”

Yachina’s eyes hardened. “All I asked you is what is happening here.”

Nero swallowed. “This bastard dared us. He said the Yachina gang is nothing. He claimed he would force us to sign a stupid deal and came here to challenge us. I was about to teach him a lesson.”

Lord Yachina turned slowly toward Charles.

Then something unexpected happened.

He bowed slightly to Charles. 

The room gasped.

“Sir,” Lord Yachina said calmly. “Did you really say that?”

Even Charles looked surprised at being addressed that way. He shook his head.

“No. That man,” he said, pointing at Ivan, “lied. And he,” his eyes shifted to Nero, “attempted to assault Ms. Caroline.”

Yachina’s face changed. The calm vanished. His gaze snapped toward Nero.

“Is that true?”

Nero panicked. “He’s lying, my lord! He provoked us first. He even broke my fingers!”

Yachina’s expression darkened further. “He broke your fingers?”

Nero quickly lifted his twisted hand. “Yes, lord. He broke them and said there’s nothing anyone can do.”

Yachina turned back to Charles.

“Why did you break his fingers? How could you!” he asked, yelling very loudly.

Nero allowed himself a small smile, thinking his boss was taking his side.

Yachina’s voice sharpened.

“You should have sliced them off!!!”

The smile vanished from Nero’s face.

Before anyone could react, Lord Yachina moved. The katana at his side flashed free in one smooth motion.

Srrrrrr

A silver arc cut through the air.

Nero screamed.

His remaining fingers dropped to the floor.

Nero let out a shrieking yell.

“Yaaaaa! My hand! Oh no!”

Blood poured from the clean slice, thick and bright, spilling onto the floor in heavy drops. He clutched what was left of his hand, trembling, knees shaking beneath him.

Gasps filled the room. The thugs stared in horror. Even Charles felt a flicker of shock at the speed and precision of it.

Yachina did not even glance at the blood.

“Make another sound,” he said calmly, “and I will slit your throat.”

Nero bit down on his scream. His body shook violently as he tried to silence himself. Soft whimpers slipped through his clenched teeth while the blood continued to gush between his fingers.

Yachina’s gaze swept across the room.

“Do you know who you just challenged?” he asked coldly. “Do you know him? Do you understand that he can wipe out the entire Yachina Gang if he wishes?”

A ripple of disbelief moved through the men. They exchanged stunned looks.

Charles frowned.

Does he know who I am?

Does he know I’m the Godfather’s grandson?

How would he?

He glanced at Lizzypie. She looked back at him and gave a small, confused shrug.

Nero’s voice cracked through his pain. “Who is he, boss? I don’t know!”

“Shut up,” Yachina snapped. “I always told you to call me when matters like this arise. Instead you overstep your boundary and offend someone far beyond your level.”

“Boss, I swear I didn’t know he was close to you—”

“If you utter another word,” Yachina said quietly, “I will kill you.”

Nero went silent instantly, choking back his sobs.

Yachina turned back to Charles and bowed deeply.

“I sincerely apologize for my uncultured subordinates,” he said. “We are not like this. They act without discipline when I am not present. Please forgive this disgrace.”

He bowed even lower.

Caroline stared in disbelief.

Lord Yachina, the most feared figure in New York, the man even powerful families avoided crossing, was bowing.

To Charles.

Her eyes slowly shifted to him.

Who are you?

If Yachina bows… then this man must stand above the city itself.

Charles gave a small nod. He chose not to ask how Yachina knew him. Not here. Not now.

“Thank you,” Charles said calmly. “I understand.”

Yachina straightened.

“How may I assist you?” he asked respectfully.

Charles cleared his throat.

“Actually… we came to discuss a deal.”

Yachina’s eyes settled on him. “What kind of deal?”

Charles gestured toward Caroline.

She stepped forward carefully, still shaken but steady enough to speak.

“Lord Yachina… the Yachina Gang has been pressuring the Quill family. It’s unusual compared to how you treat others. Nero has been collecting monthly dues from us. Every month.”

Caroline continued, “We understand paying for protection once in a while. Or when we’re undertaking a major project. That’s normal. But this is constant. The Quill family is overwhelmed. We came to ask for a fair arrangement. We want assurance that we won’t be disturbed like this again.”

Yachina’s expression darkened.

“Monthly dues?” he repeated slowly.

Caroline nodded. “Yes.”

Yachina turned his head toward Nero.

“Is that true?” His voice was low but dangerous. “Land dues for a specific project, I understand. But monthly collections? Who authorized that?”

Nero looked up from the floor, pale and sweating. His lips trembled.

“I asked you a question,” Yachina thundered. “Who ordered you to do that?”

Nero flinched at the shout.

“The Diamond Group!” he blurted out. “Ivan and the Diamond Group told us to do it!… they gave us incentives to keep the pressure on the Quill family!”.
