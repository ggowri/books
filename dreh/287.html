Chapter 288

Johnson stood there stunned.

The way Zander had changed in an instant shook him more than anything else. Just moments ago the man had been standing respectfully beside him like an obedient employee. Now he was defending Charles like his life depended on it.

That meant only one thing.

Whoever Charles was… he was far more important.

Johnson gulped.

Then another thought struck him.

Zander had said something. He said whatever Johnson paid him was peanut money compared to what Charles paid him.

Johnson felt a cold sensation crawl down his spine.

He was paying Zander one hundred thousand dollars every month.

If that was peanut money…Then how much was Charles paying him?

Johnson swallowed hard and tried to regain his composure.

“I don’t even understand whatever is going on here,” he snapped loudly. “But I swear I will take my revenge.”

Just then—

A weak voice came from behind the crowd.

“It’s you… it’s you…”

Everyone turned toward the voice.

An old man slowly stepped forward, still wrapped in the thick shirt Charles had given him earlier.

Charles immediately recognized him.

“The old man…”

Charles rushed toward him.

“Are you okay?”

The old man nodded weakly and pointed a shaking finger toward Johnson.

“He… he is the one who pushed me into the ocean,” the old man said. “I only asked him for money. Just a small change… but he pushed me. He didn’t even care if I died.”

The crowd gasped.

Charles frowned deeply.

He already expected Johnson to be arrogant.

But this?This was worse.

The old man continued, his voice trembling.

“And I heard you calling him a ragged man…”

He shook his head.

“No. He isn’t ragged. He took pity on me because I was freezing. He exchanged clothes with me.”

The old man lifted the shirt slightly.

“I’m the ragged one here.”

Johnson frowned in confusion and shock.

Whispers began spreading again through the crowd.

“Oh…”

“Now it makes sense.”

“So he gave the old man his clothes?”

“That means he must be someone important.”

Just then Natasha returned, walking quickly through the crowd.

She held a neatly folded shirt in her hands.

It was clearly expensive.

High-end fabric and Elegant design.

She walked straight to Charles and handed it to him.

“Babe,” she said softly, “change into this.”

Everyone watched curiously.

Charles smiled slightly and took the shirt.

Without hesitation he removed the ragged one and slipped into the new shirt right there in front of everyone.

The transformation was immediate.

The moment the shirt settled on his shoulders, his entire presence changed.

He looked sharp and handsome.

No longer like an ordinary man standing on the beach.

The crowd stared in shock.

“Wow…”

“He’s actually handsome.”

“He doesn’t look ordinary at all.”

Johnson felt his throat go dry.

His girlfriend quietly lowered her head, hiding her face in embarrassment.

Just then someone in the crowd shouted.

“Here! I got it on video!”

Everyone turned.

A young man stepped forward holding up his phone.

“What did you get?” Johnson muttered uneasily.

“The incident,” the young man said. “I think your girlfriend claiming he touched her ass is a lie.”

The crowd leaned in.

“I accidentally recorded it while filming myself earlier.”

He walked into the middle of the group and tapped his screen.

“Here.”

The video started playing.

The footage clearly showed the moment the woman bumped into Charles, knocking the drinks from his hands. It showed her losing balance and Charles catching her before she fell.

His hand never touched her ass.

It only held her back to stop her from hitting the ground.

The video ended.

A wave of murmurs exploded across the beach.

“So it was all lies!”

“What the hell!”

“This is pathetic.”

“Typical Mayor family behavior.”

“Disgusting.”

Johnson slowly turned his head and stared at his girlfriend with burning anger.

Charles looked at Johnson calmly, the faintest trace of mockery in his eyes.

“Well,” he said, gesturing lightly toward the phone that had just finished playing the video, “you can now see how useless and dishonest your girlfriend is. You shouldn’t have trusted her blindly.”

Johnson’s face burned.

Everywhere he looked people were staring at him with disgust. Some shook their heads. Others whispered openly about how pathetic the situation was.

For the first time since the confrontation began, Johnson wished he could disappear.

He clenched his jaw and turned away.

“I’m leaving,” he muttered.

Without waiting for a response, he began walking away through the sand.

But Charles had already anticipated it.

He gave Zander a small signal.

Zander moved instantly.

In two quick steps he blocked Johnson’s path and grabbed his shoulder firmly.

“Where do you think you’re going?” Zander said coldly.

Johnson jerked his arm back angrily.

“What the heck are you doing?” he snapped. “Fine, I made a mistake. I have seen it now. I’m not arguing anymore. I’m going home.”

Charles scoffed.

“How convenient,” he said slowly.

Johnson froze.

Charles stepped forward, his gaze sharp now.

“After you accused me blindly… after you tried to have my legs broken… after you pushed an old man into the ocean…”

He paused. “You think you can just walk away?”

Johnson gulped.

Then Charles pointed calmly at the sand in front of him.

“You will kneel here,” he said. “And apologize.”

He gestured toward the old man.

“To him.”

Then toward the four women beside him.

“To my ladies you all called sluts.”

Finally his eyes landed on Johnson again.

“You and your shameless girlfriend will both apologize.”

Johnson’s eyes widened in disbelief.

“Me?” he shouted. “Kneel for you?”

He shook his head furiously.

“That’s impossible! I would rather die.”
