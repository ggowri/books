Chapter 221

Everyone turned toward the entrance as they saw the silhouette of someone really walking out.

“Yeah… yeah, someone’s coming out.”

The crowd went quiet.

Smoke poured out first, followed by  Charles.

In his arms was Gabriella. Unharmed, with no single scratch. 

There were wet towels wrapped around both of them, pressed against their faces and shoulders. The fire raged behind them, but they walked straight through it.

For a second, no one spoke.

Shock froze everyone in place.

The head guard stared in disbelief. “What…? He actually went in and saved her?”

Another guard whispered, “But the fire was everywhere… how did he do it”

Mr. Belly didn’t wait for another word.

 He shouted. “Gabriella!”

He rushed forward.

Charles lowered her gently and set her on the ground. She was alive. Breathing safely.

Mr. Belly rushed to her side. “Gabriella, are you okay? Are you hurt?”

She reached out weakly. “Father… I’m fine.”

She turned her head slightly and looked at Charles. “That man… Did you hire him? He saved me.”

Mr. Belly finally looked up at Charles properly.

Before he could say anything, sirens wailed loudly as the firefighters arrived.

Everyone scattered as they rushed in, dragging hoses, smashing windows, moving fast to put out the fire.

An ambulance pulled up immediately.

“Help needed here,” Mr. Belly said firmly. “She needs treatment.”

“I’m really okay,” Gabriella tried to say.

He didn’t even listen.

She was lifted onto a stretcher and wheeled into the ambulance. Mr. Belly followed closely.

Charles stood where he was.

Just before getting in, Mr. Belly turned back and looked at him. “Please,” he said. “Follow us.”

Charles hesitated for half a second, then nodded and got in.

As the vehicle drove off, people murmured quietly.

“Who the hell is he?”

---

At the hospital, about twenty minutes later, Gabriella was stabilized and moved to a private room.

She wasn’t badly injured, but Mr. Belly refused to leave her side until the doctor reassured him.

“She just needs rest,” the doctor said. “She’ll be fine.”

Only then did Mr. Belly's ease.

He turned to Charles and bowed slightly. “Thank you for saving my daughter. Those guards were useless.”

Gabriella smiled weakly. “Father, I think he’s a Cultivator also. He used his power to take me out. He even walked through fire.”

Charles stiffened slightly. They knew about Cultivator?. 

Mr. Belly looked impressed. “Oh? Really. Then please, if you don’t mind… who are you?”

Charles shook his head. “Just a passerby. I saw what happened and couldn’t leave.”

Mr. Belly nodded slowly.

Just then—The door burst open.

“Where is Gabriella?! Where is my fiancée?!”

The voice was sharp and furious as Raphael stormed into the room. 

The moment he saw her, his expression cracked.

“Gabriella.”

He rushed to her side.

She grabbed his hand tightly, tears streaming. “Baby… I nearly died. I thought I would never see you again.”

Raphael’s voice softened completely. “I’m sorry I wasn’t there. If anything had happened to you… I’d never forgive myself.”

Charles watched silently. This was not the Raphael he knew. 

Gabriella turned and pointed weakly. “You should thank him. He saved me.”

Raphael straightened and followed her gaze. Then his eyes landed on Charles.

He squinted and immediately recognized him. “Him?”

“Yes,” Gabriella said. “He saved me.”

Raphael stepped forward and grabbed Charles by the collar.

“What the fuck are you doing with my fiancée?”

Mr. Belly and Gabriella froze in shock.

“What are you doing?” Mr. Belly demanded.

Charles frowned. “What are you even saying? I saved her life.”

“Fuck you,” Raphael snapped. “Bullshit. Why were you there at the exact moment she needed help?”

Charles scoffed. “What, I can’t save someone because of coincidence?”

Raphael’s face darkened. He drew his fist back and tried to punch him.

“Raphael!” Mr. Belly shouted. “Please don’t misunderstand. He’s our savior. He saved my daughter’s life!”

Gabriella nodded quickly. “Yes. He’s just a passerby.”

Raphael laughed coldly. “Oh, he isn’t.”

He turned fully toward Charles. “I know this motherfucker.”

Then his voice dropped. “Whatever you are doing, you better stop.”

Mr. Belly tried to speak again. “Raphael—”

“Shut up,” Raphael barked as Mr. Belly recoiled

The room fell silent.

“Who knows,” Raphael continued, “maybe he’s the one who set the explosion and staged all this just to play hero.”

Charles stared at him. “Why would I do that?”

“I don’t fucking care,” Raphael snapped. “I don’t want to know. We already fixed our fighting date, you shouldn't do any dirty trick ”

He shoved Charles back and released him. “Get the fuck out. Now.”

Charles straightened his clothes calmly.

“Out. Now,” Raphael repeated. “Or we will have the fight here.”

Charles glanced at Gabriella.

She was watching him, confused, scared and still shaken.

He didn’t want violence here. Not in front of her as he still needed to get close to her.

So he stayed calm.

“I only saved your fiancée when you couldn’t,” Charles said evenly. “If this is how you want to react, fine.”

He turned toward the door.

“I will leave.”

And without another word, Charles walked out.
