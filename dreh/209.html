Chapter 209

 

Charles gasped as he opened his eyes and his body nearly collapsed forward.

Natasha and Lizzy rushed in at the same time and caught him before he hit the ground, each grabbing one of his arms and holding him up.

“Are you okay?” Natasha screamed. “Charles! Is everything alright?!”

The entire hall was frozen.

Lord Hierem stared in disbelief. “Did he just… survive the Hole Realm?” he muttered. “Impossible.”

One of the elders leaned forward, eyes locked on Charles’s hand. “And that diamond… is that the Kyoul Stone?”

Charles was still breathing hard, chest heaving like he had run for miles. Sweat soaked his face. For a moment he couldn’t speak. Then his breathing slowly steadied.

Lizzy asked again, softer this time, “Charles… are you okay?”

He nodded weakly. “Yeah… yeah.”

Natasha pulled back slightly to look at his body. “You need treatment, you’re blee—”

She stopped mid-sentence.

The blood was gone.

She stared at his stomach, then touched it, checking again. The wound had completely closed. The torn flesh was smooth, healed like it had never been cut.

Natasha’s eyes widened. “Wow… you’ve been healed!”

Charles let out a small scoff and muttered under his breath, “Thanks, Aries,” knowing exactly who was responsible.

Lizzy frowned. “Aries? Who’s that?”

“Long story,” Charles replied quietly.

Just then Lord Hierem stepped forward and shouted, “Did you truly enter the Hole Realm and return… or have you been deceiving us this entire time?”

Charles straightened slowly, standing on his own feet now, and smiled faintly.

Jeremy stared at him in shock before snapping, “Yeah! He deceived us! Maybe he entered a different realm!”

Another elder stepped forward and said, “The Hole Realm is impossible for a man to enter and return from. You’re deceiving us.”

Charles lifted his hand calmly and showed them the diamond. “Isn’t this the Kyoul Stone you wanted?”

The room fell silent as all eyes locked onto it.

Jeremy shook his head violently. “No. I don’t believe this. And none of you should either. I swear this is all lies. We should never have allowed him to go!”

Even Lord Hierem showed hesitation. Doubt flickered in his eyes before he turned and said, “Chief priest. Check it.”

An elder stepped forward slowly. Charles didn’t release the stone, but the moment the chief priest came close, he stopped. His body stiffened. He could feel the energy radiating from it without touching it.

He stepped back immediately and nodded.

“Yes. It is.”

A wave of murmurs filled the hall.

“He must be something else…”

“Who the hell is he…”

Lord Hierem swallowed. Fear crept into his chest. For a man to enter that realm and return alive meant power beyond normal understanding. But he forced his expression back into control.

“Well,” he said stiffly, “hand it over, as promised.”

Charles hesitated.

Aries’s warning echoed in his head. The stone was dangerous in the wrong hands. The Hierem clan was definitely dangerous. He didn't want to let it go.

Lord Hierem’s voice hardened. “Remember. We had an agreement.”

Charles gulped.

He had already promised. If he failed to honor it, Lizzy’s Divine Oath would become binding again. 

Slowly, he stretched his hand out.

Lord Hierem stepped forward immediately and took the stone from him.

As soon as Lord Hierem collected the stone, His eyes widened slightly as he felt the energy flowing from the Kyoul Stone, rich and overwhelming. Then a slow grin spread across his face.

“With this,” he said quietly, almost reverently, “we will rule the cultivator world.”

Charles stared at him without expression, but inside his mind he already made a vow to take it back. 

He turned to Lizzy. “Well,” he said calmly, “she’s coming with me now. We are leaving.”

Lord Hierem nodded without hesitation. “Of course. You Can”

Jeremy snapped.

“Father!” he shouted. “You can’t just let her leave! You promised she would marry me!”

“That was only if she entered the realm,” Lord Hierem replied coldly.

“Still!” Jeremy barked. “You can’t just let her go!”

Lizzy scoffed loudly. “You must have had some wild fantasy in your head thinking you would fuck me,” she said. “You are a weak, pathetic virgin hiding under your father’s wings, and you will stay that way.”

Jeremy’s face twisted with rage. He stepped forward. 

Charles stepped forward too.

Lord Hierem shouted, panic breaking through his voice, “Jeremy! Back off right now!”.

He was already scared of Charles. 

Jeremy froze, trembling with anger and humiliation.

Lizzy spat at his feet. “Let’s go,” she said.

She turned, and Charles and Natasha followed. The hall stayed silent as they walked out, every eye on them until the doors closed behind them.

One elder finally spoke. “Who the hell is he?”

Lord Hierem stared at the entrance, jaw tight. “We are going to find out,” he said quietly. “Because I do not want that man as an enemy.”
