Chapter 186

 

The COO stared at them for a long second, his brows drawn together.

“Them?” he asked flatly.

“Yes,” Tracy said immediately, stepping forward. “They are extremely arrogant and they came here just to cause trouble. They were here because of the deal, but we’ve already closed it. I honestly don’t understand why they still had the nerve to come inside.”

Linda crossed her arms and tilted her head, her smile cold. Then she turned to the COO beside her and softened instantly.

“Jude, baby,” she said sweetly. “I really hate it when someone looks down on my friends. Please just send them out real quick.”

Jude hesitated.

Company protocol flickered in his mind. Visitors were supposed to be heard. Complaints were meant to be verified before any action. He cleared his throat.

“Uh… babe,” he said carefully. “Let’s at least hear what they have to say.”

Linda’s expression hardened for half a second.

“No, baby,” she said, shaking her head. “Don’t listen at all. I don’t want you listening to them. They disrespected my friend and I can’t tolerate that.”

She stepped closer and pressed a quick kiss to his cheek.

“Do this for me, babe.”

That was all it took.

The last trace of hesitation drained from Jude’s eyes, just like it always did when Linda asked him for something. In fact, due to his foolishness, he had done many illegal things in the company. 

 He straightened, his chest puffing out with borrowed authority.

Then he walked toward Charles and Serena.

Serena instinctively moved closer and slipped behind Charles, her fingers gripping the back of his jacket. 

Jude stopped in front of them and looked at Charles from up to down like he was some trash. 

“How can I help you?”

Charles lifted his chin slightly, his voice calm and steady.

“We are here to seek a partnership,” he said. “Your company is currently open to proposals, so we came to present ours.”

Jude’s eyes narrowed.

“Well,” he replied coldly, “I have heard you are from the Moretti family. But we have already decided to sign through Mr. Collins. That decision is final. Leave Now.”

Behind him, Collins and Tracy exchanged grins, their satisfaction visible. 

“So you won’t even hear our presentation?” Charles asked. “Not even what we have to offer, despite company protocol?”

Jude exhaled sharply, irritation flashing across his face.

“I said I’m not interested,” he snapped. “It doesn’t matter what you say. Save us all the trouble and leave right now.”

Serena tugged at Charles’ sleeve, her voice low and urgent. But he shook her off without looking at her.

“We are not leaving,” Charles said evenly, “until someone actually looks at what we came here to present.”

That was enough.

Jude’s face darkened completely.

“You are really annoying,” he said, his voice rising. “I already hate you.”

He jabbed a finger toward Charles. “Listen, Mr. Man. I’m the COO here. The CEO isn’t around and I run this company. What I say is final. Leave now, before I call security. If I do, you will regret what happens next.”

Charles scoffed, a faint smile tugging at his lips.

“Funny you mention regret,” he said. “Because if you don’t fix this biased behavior, it won’t be mine. It will be yours”

The words hung in the air. 

Serena swallowed hard, then finally stepped forward, her voice trembling but clear.

“At least,” she said, “give us a chance.”

Collins stepped forward quickly, seizing the moment.

“Sir, this is exactly what I was saying,” he said, shaking his head. “They are here to cause trouble. If you don’t embarrass them now, they won’t stop.”

Linda immediately grabbed Jude’s arm, her nails digging in as she leaned into him. She shot Charles and Serena with a look filled with disdain.

“Are you two really that dumb?” she snapped. “He’s the one who listens to proposals and he already told you he won’t take whatever nonsense you brought. Use your common sense and leave.”

Charles let out a quiet scoff.

“And who exactly are you to think you even have a say in this?”

Linda’s eyes widened in disbelief. “Me? Who am I?”

Before she could continue, Jude cut in sharply, pointing at Charles.

“Yeah, you,” he barked. “This doesn’t concern you. Stop acting like a bee”

He stopped himself, then exploded. “Hey! Don’t you dare abuse my fiancée. This is exactly what will make you regret everything.”

Linda was already seething. She stepped forward, her face flushed with anger.

“You just called me a bee?” she demanded. “Say that again. Say it once more and I swear I will slap you.”

Charles didn’t even blink.

“Yeah,” he said coolly. “You are just buzzing into something that doesn’t concern you. Like a bee.”

That was it.

Linda screamed and raised her hand, swinging it toward Charles’ face.

But it never landed.

Serena moved faster than anyone expected.

She stepped in front of Charles and caught Linda’s wrist midair, then shoved her back hard.

“What do you think you’re doing?” Serena snapped, her voice shaking with fury.

Linda stumbled back a step, heels scraping against the polished floor, shock flashing across her face as the lobby fell dead silent.

Linda let out a sharp, exaggerated cry and collapsed to the floor.

“Ahhh—my leg!” she screamed, clutching her calf. “My leg! They broke my leg!”

The lobby erupted.

Jude rushed to her side in a panic, dropping to his knees. “Babe, baby, what’s wrong?” he asked frantically. “What happened to your leg?”

Linda pointed at Serena, her face twisted in pain and fury. “That crazy girl,” she sobbed. “She broke my leg.”

Jude snapped

He stood up slowly, his face flushed red, his eyes wild. He pointed straight at Charles and Serena, his hand shaking with rage.

“You…” he growled. “You both have trespassed now. I swear, I will make sure your legs are broken too.”

He spun around and roared, his voice echoing through the vast lobby.

“Security!”

“Security!” he shouted!.

Two men hurried in from the side corridor, stopping in front of him.

Jude jabbed a finger toward Charles and Serena. “What are you standing there for?” he barked. “Go and call all the others. I want everyone. Thirty of you if possible. Bring your weapons.”

The guards stiffened.

“These two,” Jude continued furiously, “just broke my fiancée’s leg. I want you to do the same to them.”

“Yes, sir,” one of the guards replied quickly. “We’ll be back now.”

They turned and rushed off.

Serena’s face was drained of color. Her breathing turned shallow as panic clawed up her chest. 

She leaned closer to Charles and whispered, her voice trembling, “Oh no… we should have left.”

Collins chuckled darkly at her.

“You are a really hot one, sister,” he sneered mockingly. “I knew this bastard would land you in serious trouble one day.”
