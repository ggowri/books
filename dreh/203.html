Chapter 203

 

Everyone froze immediately and turned.

An old man stood there, wrapped in a long dark robe and heavy cloak, his presence alone pressing down on the entire courtyard like an unseen weight. 

His hair was white, his back straight, and was gazing at them like he was a lion. 

Every single guard dropped to one knee.

“Lord Hierem!”

The shout rose as one, filled with fear and reverence. So this was him. The true leader of the Hierem clan.

At that moment, another figure stepped out from behind him.

Charles’s breath caught.

It was Lizzy.

“Lizzy!” Charles shouted instinctively, his body moving forward without thinking.

He didn’t get far.

Men poured out from every direction, from left to right corridors, inner gates, surrounding him in seconds. 

There were too many to count, easily over three hundred, forming a tight ring around him, their presence heavy and unnatural. Charles could feel it instantly. Every one of them carried power.

He stopped andgulped.

Across the courtyard, Lizzy stared at him in disbelief, her eyes wide as shock washed over her face. “Charles!” she shouted back, her voice trembling.

Lord Hierem’s gaze shifted to the ground where Jeremy knelt, battered and humiliated. Rage flickered across his face.

“How dare you,” he thundered, his voice echoing. “You caused a ruckus and fought when you knew you could not win. You keep disgracing me.”

He stepped closer, his tone cold. “You are not worthy of being the heir of this clan.”

Jeremy’s face burned with shame as his head dropped.

Then Lord Hierem turned to Charles.

“And you,” he said sharply. “What do you want? Who the hell are you.”

Charles met his gaze without backing down. “Maybe I should be asking you people,” he snapped, “why are you hoarding my friend.”

A man immediately stepped forward. “Watch your mouth. If you dare speak such foul words against Lord Hierem again, we will not hesitate to kill you.”

Charles scoffed and repeated. “Fuck him.”

He didn’t even flinch.

Several men shifted forward, killing intent flaring, but Lord Hierem raised a single hand and they stopped instantly.

He studied Charles for a moment, then spoke calmly. “He looks like nothing more than a loyal friend who is completely clueless.”

He turned fully to Charles. “Who told you we are hoarding her. She is here of her own accord.”

Charles frowned and turned toward Lizzy. “Really?”

Lizzy swallowed hard, then let out a slow sigh.

Lord Hierem nodded. “I should not let your disrespect go scot-free,” he said, “but it seems ignorance is your only crime.”

He waved his hand lightly. “I will leave you two to talk.”

Then his gaze snapped back to Jeremy. “You. Come with me. Now!!!.”

Lord Hierem turned and walked away.

Jeremy muttered under his breath, “Fuck,” knowing exactly what awaited him.

As Lord Hierem left, the men followed, peeling away one after another, until the courtyard slowly emptied.

After everyone left, the courtyard felt strangely empty, the tension still hanging in the air as Lizzy stood there staring at Charles

 Then she walked to him. 

At the same moment, Natasha hurried closer to them.

Charles didn’t move. He just stared at Lizzy in disbelief, his chest tight, anger and relief tangled together as he finally spoke.

“Why the fuck would you come all the way here without telling me?”

Lizzy slowed to a stop in front of him and sighed, brushing her hair back with shaking fingers. “Why are you even here?” she asked quietly.

Charles scoffed, the sound sharp. “Really? That’s the question?”

She looked away for a second, then said, “I left you a letter.”

Charles let out a bitter laugh. “A letter? You really think that cuts it? You didn’t tell me anything. You didn’t tell me where you were going. You just left a fucking letter. Do you know how worried I was?”

Lizzy bit her lip, her eyes dropping as she took in the fury in his face. She understood Charles's frustration.

 To Charles, she wasn’t just a friend. She was family. The closest person to him. 

She breathed out slowly. “I’m sorry,” she said. “I just… I had to be here.”

Natasha frowned slightly and stepped closer. “Had to be here?” she asked carefully. “I hope everything is alright.”

Charles turned his eyes back to Lizzy, his expression hardening again. “Yeah,” he added, “what’s that supposed to mean?”.

Lizzy nodded slowly, her fingers tightening together as if bracing herself, then she said, “I had to complete a mind-journeying ritual for Jeremy, and after that, I will have to marry him and stay here forever.”

Both Charles and Natasha frowned instantly, the explanation landing heavy and wrong.

Natasha let out a short scoff before she could stop herself. “Is that even real? A ritual?”

Her head spun as she spoke. Everything has been overwhelming for her. She just witnessed Charles turn to Flash, dark aura  and clans stuff, and now she was being told about mind-journeying rituals like it was casual conversation. 

Charles stared at Lizzy, disbelief hardening into something sharper. “Are you teasing me right now?”

Lizzy shook her head quickly. “I’m being real,” she said. “I had to do it.”

Her voice dropped. “And after that, I had to marry Jeremy.”

Charles’s jaw tightened.

“I’m sorry,” Lizzy continued quietly. “Nothing could be done about it.”

Charles scoffed,  as frustration bled into his voice. “If this isn’t a joke, I don’t even know what to call it. Stop this nonsense and let’s leave here. Now.”

“Charles,” Lizzy said softly, shaking her head. “I can’t.”

She took a breath, steadying herself. “I made a pledge to the Hierem clan. I agreed to help them retrieve the Kyoul Stone from the Hole Realm.”

 “The Hole Realm?, what's  that”

Lizzy nodded. “The most dangerous realm. A place where people don’t usually come back from.”

Charles frowned as he listened to her.

“It’s a realm only women are allowed to enter,” Lizzy continued, her voice low. “And even then, survival is almost impossible.”

She looked at him, and added. “If I am able to survive there,” she said, “I will also have to marry Jeremy.”
