Chapter 264

.

Allison found this unbelievable—that Serena was willing to forgive her just like that. She gulped, trying to process it.

“I… I don’t want the assets by dropping my family name,” Allison said slowly. “But I want a position in the company.”

Serena tilted her head. “As what?”

“All right… at least as a sales manager,” Allison replied.

Serena nodded. “Okay. Sure. That’s done.”

A stunned silence filled the room. Everyone’s mouths hung open, their opinions of Serena shifting instantly. The woman who had just endured betrayal, and lies had shown mercy and decisiveness all at once. Many of them, in that moment, began to see her differently.

Allison cleared her throat. “So… what are the conditions you want?”

Serena’s eyes were steady. “First, I will make sure this incident ends in this room. No one shall talk about it. Whoever does will be removed from the family and branded as a liar.”

She paused, her gaze fixing on Allison. “Now my condition is… how did you know I wasn’t my father’s daughter?”

Allison gulped, her bravado faltering. “I… I was in Father’s room one day. I wanted to steal some money. I had been monitoring him and knew his safe password. When I opened it, I found the money and… some documents in one folder. I didn’t understand many of the documents, but I could see the DNA part. That’s where I saw it.”

Charles frowned. “And the rest of the documents?”

“They are  with me at home,” Allison admitted. “I didn’t fully understand them. They seemed classified… internal. Inside, you could know who her real father was.”

Serena’s eyes widened slightly. She swallowed, her mind racing. Everyone else in the room was visibly shocked by this revelation.

“Okay,” Serena said finally, her voice firm. “You will give me the folder when we get home. And so… listen all. Like I said, whatever happens stays in this room. You understand?”

Everyone nodded quickly, the weight of her words settling over them.

In that instant, Serena earned their respect.

After everything was settled, the family slowly dispersed. One by one they left the estate, quiet and thoughtful, the earlier chaos replaced with heavy reflection.

Charles drove Serena home.

The city lights blurred past the window as silence filled the car.

“You handled that very well,” Charles said finally. “I’m impressed.”

Serena nodded faintly. “I just hope there won’t be any problem after this.”

“You showed them how merciful you are,” Charles replied. “I’m sure they will accept you now.”

She leaned back against the seat. “This is just too much. I wonder what I’ll find in that document.”

Before Charles could respond, his phone buzzed sharply.

He glanced at the screen.

A message from Lizzypie.

This is about Gabriella. Something has gone so fucking wrong. You have to come now. I think we killed her.

Charles froze. “What?”

Serena turned quickly. “What’s that? Is something wrong?”

He swallowed. “I have to go. Call me if anything happens. Okay?”

He didn’t even wait for Serena to talk before stepping out and hurrying down the alley. He flagged down a taxi and made his way to the hotel. 

The ride felt endless.

When he arrived, he rushed inside and barged into the living room.

There stood Natasha, Lizzypie, and Sapphire.

“What’s wrong?” Charles demanded. “What happened to Gabriella? How did you do it?”

Lizzypie sniffled. “I mixed the concoction herb and gave it to her. She drank it and within minutes she started convulsing. I was shocked and… and I couldn’t even process what was happening before she dropped and stopped breathing.”

Charles ran a hand through his hair. “What the fuck.”

Sapphire and Natasha stood there silently, faces pale.

“Where is she?” Charles demanded.

“In your room,” Lizzypie whispered. “I dragged her there.”

Without another word, Charles rushed down the hallway and pushed the door open.

“Surppprrrrrrise!”

Gabriella stood in the middle of the room, upright, her hands spread wide and a huge grin on her face.

Charles stopped cold.

Behind him, Lizzypie and the others burst into laughter.

“Yayy! It’s a prank!” Lizzypie cheered. “The herb worked perfectly. Gabriella can now walk!”

Charles stared at Gabriella standing firmly on her feet.

For a long second, he couldn’t breathe.

Then relief flooded him.

He stepped forward and pulled her into a tight hug, holding her tightly. 

Charles held her shoulders and looked at her again as if to be certain she was real.

“I’m so glad you’re okay,” he uttered softly. “So very glad.”

Gabriella smiled warmly. “Yeah. I’m surprised too. It was their idea to prank you.” She tilted her head. “Are you okay?”

Charles slowly withdrew his hands but kept his eyes on her, watching her stand there steadily on her own feet.

“I’m perfectly okay,” he said. “And you? You’re not feeling any pain? This is your first time standing like this… and you are this stable?”

Gabriella shifted her weight slightly, testing herself. “No pain,” she said, almost in awe. “It feels… natural.”

Lizzypie beamed proudly. “Well, that’s the power and magic of the Red Moon Herb.”

“It retrained the nerves,” Lizzypie explained further excitedly. “The other ingredients fought off the worm completely. The Red Moon activated the dormant signals and taught her legs what to do again. That’s why she adjusted so fast.”

“Wow”, Charles said.

“And,” Lizzypie added dramatically, “she won’t just walk. She will be able to run. Probably faster than the average person.”

Charles let out a breath of disbelief and smiled. “Wow. That’s… that’s incredible.”

Gabriella laughed softly, flexing her toes against the floor as if enjoying the sensation.

Sapphire crossed her arms with a teasing grin. “Soooo… we will  leave you two to process this miracle.”

Before Charles could respond, the girls began sneaking out.

The door closed behind them with a soft click.

Silence filled the room.

Charles turned back to Gabriella.

She was still standing there. On her own. Steady. Smiling.
