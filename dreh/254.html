Chapter 256

The announcer stepped forward, microphone in hand, his voice booming across the grand hall.

“Ladies and gentlemen… The reason we are here will commence shortly. As you all know, we have a bidding war for the Red Moon Herb between Mr. Orlean, the richest man in this city, and this young man here.”

A ripple of murmurs swept through the crowd.

“And this,” the announcer continued dramatically, “is a winner-takes-all arrangement. The victor will claim not only the Red Moon Herb… but also the full amount of the losing bid.”

The hall erupted in hushed excitement.

“This is going to be crazy.”

“The auction house is making history today.”

The announcer lifted his hand. “We will begin on the count of three.”

He paused.

“One.”

The entire hall fell silent. Even the faint clicking of cameras seemed to fade.

“Two.”

Across the aisle, Mr. Orlean leaned back slightly in his VIP seat, a composed smile resting on his lips as he glanced toward the reporters and flashing lenses. 

He wanted this spectacle. He wanted every headline tomorrow to carry his name beside the humiliation of the arrogant young man who dared challenge him.

“Three!”

Mr. Orlean’s bidding paddle went up instantly.

“Three hundred million dollars.”

Gasps broke out across the hall.

“What the hell? For a herb?”

“He’s insane.”

“The young man should just back out now.”

Charles didn’t react to the noise. He didn’t blink. A faint smile curved his lips as he lifted his paddle.

“Five hundred million dollars.”

The reaction was louder this time.

“Does he even have that kind of money?”

“Who is he?”

Mr. Orlean turned his head slowly, eyes narrowing. His voice carried across the aisle. “If you refuse to pay your bidding money, understand this clearly… you will pay with your kidney.”

A tense ripple passed through the audience.

Charles met his gaze evenly. “Will you bid… or talk?”

A few people chuckled nervously.

Mr. Orlean’s jaw tightened. He raised his paddle again.

“One billion dollars.”

The number slammed into the room like a bomb.

“This is insane.”

“It’s only the opening!”

Charles didn’t hesitate.

“Three billion.”

The hall exploded in gasps.

Even the announcer froze for a split second before regaining composure. Cameras zoomed in. Reporters leaned forward.

“Three billion dollars!” he repeated loudly.

Mr. Orlean stared at Charles, searching his face for doubt, for fear, for even the slightest crack. He found none.

“I know you don’t have the money,” Orlean said coldly.

Charles shrugged faintly. “Yeah. You know??. Will you try me?”

uncertainty flickered through Orlean’s eyes. Was this bluff? Was this madness? Or was this young man far deeper than he appeared?

He could not afford to lose face. He didn't want to take the risk.

His paddle rose again.

“Ten billion dollars.”

The number dropped like thunder.

The entire auction hall fell into stunned silence.

A breathless pause hung in the air after the ten-billion declaration.

Charles lifted his paddle again without the slightest tremor. “Twenty billion dollars.”

The announcer nearly choked. “Did you just say… twenty billion dollars?”

Charles’s voice was calm and steady. “20 billion.”

The hall exploded.

“This young man is playing Mr. Orlean!”

“He doesn’t have that kind of money.”

“He’s bluffing. He has to be.”

Across the aisle, Mr. Orlean smirked, leaning back in his seat. “You’re lying.”

Charles didn’t even spare him a full glance. “Just shut up and bid.”

A sharp intake of breath rippled through the audience.

Mr. Orlean’s eyes hardened. He raised his paddle with deliberate slowness. “25 billion dollars.”

Gasps broke out again.

“There’s no way he’ll go higher than that.”

“What is even in that herb?”

“Are they insane?”

Without hesitation, Charles said, “25.3 billion dollars.”

The crowd reacted differently this time. Instead of shock, there was confusion.

“Only three hundred million more?”

“Is he backing down?”

“I told you. His money is running out.”

“I’m sure he’s putting in his entire life savings… maybe even borrowed money.”

Mr. Orlean laughed, loud and confident. “Alright then. 29 billion dollars.”

He raised the paddle high, almost triumphantly, making sure the cameras captured the moment. He expected Charles to finally break.

 Twenty-nine billion was more than he had intended to spend. If he actually had to pay it outright, it would damage both him and the institution he controlled, the bank. Still… if Charles lost, he would collect 25.3 billion from him. That would cover the excess and silence any internal complaints. 

He was delighted Charles proposed this type of bidding. 

It was worth crushing this arrogant boy publicly.

Charles responded almost lazily. “29.1 billion dollars.”

The reaction this time was mixed with disbelief.

“What is he doing?”

“Why add only one hundred million?”

“Is this some kind of mockery?”

Mr. Orlean’s composure cracked. He shot to his feet. “Are you playing with me?”

Charles finally turned fully toward him, a faint smile touching his lips.

At that moment, one of Mr. Orlean’s guards hurried forward and bent down, whispering urgently into his ear.

Instantly, Mr. Orlean’s face changed.

Mr. Orlean grabbed the guard’s arm tightly. “Really? Do you mean that?”

The guard swallowed and nodded. “Yes, sir. It’s confirmed.”

A thin layer of sweat formed instantly on Orlean’s forehead.

What he had just heard echoed in his mind like a death sentence. Charles… was the grandson of the Godfather.

The almighty Godfather.

Orlean’s breathing grew shallow. He had heard the stories for years. The underground king. The silent ruler behind markets, shipping  oil contracts, private security networks.

Rumors have it that his net worth  exceeded seven hundred billion dollars. That's just personal wealth. Talk less of connections. 

If this stretched any further, Charles would not just outbid him. He would crush him effortlessly. Twenty-nine billion would be nothing more than pocket change in that family.
