Chapter 232

Charles’s head whipped back just in time to see the van barreling toward them at insane speed. Instinct took over.

His hands clenched the wheel, muscles coiling. He swerved sharply to the left, tires screaming against the asphalt.

The van clipped the edge of the road, fishtailing, and Charles slammed the brakes hard. Smoke hissed from the tires as the car came to a jerking stop.

The van didn’t slow—it skidded past, scraping the curb, and then disappeared into the distance.

Gabriella clutched her chest, eyes wide, still trembling. “Oh my… Charles!”

Charles exhaled slowly, still gripping the wheel tightly. “Are you okay?” he asked, his voice tense.

She nodded frantically, still shaking. “Y-yes… I’m fine…”

He frowned, gaze narrowing. “Who… who would send that? Was it meant for me, or…?” His eyes flicked to her, realization dawning. “No. It has to be you. Your father was right. Someone is after your life. But… don’t worry. You will be safe.”

Gabriella just nodded.

The rest of the drive back to the hotel was quiet. Gabriella didn’t speak; her hands trembled slightly in her lap, her mind replaying the near collision.

Charles’s eyes stayed on the road, tense and alert.

When they arrived, Charles carefully opened the car door. “Here, let me help you,” he said, sliding his hands under her arms and gently placing her into the wheelchair.

Gabriella’s legs felt weak, her heart still racing.

He wheeled her through the hotel lobby and stopped in front of the door.

Charles knocked lightly. The door opened, revealing Sapphire. Her face lit up with excitement. “You are back!!!” she exclaimed.

Her eyes fell on Gabriella in the wheelchair, and she froze for a brief moment, processing the scene. Then, stepping aside quickly, she said, “Oh…, come in,” clearing the way for them.

Charles pushed Gabriella inside.

Lizzypie’s eyes widened as she noticed Gabriella in the room. “Oh!” she said, surprise clear on her face.

She gave Charles a questioning look, and he explained, “Mr. Belly asked me to protect her. She’s being constantly targeted.” He explained everything to them.

“Ohh,” Lizzypie said, her expression softening, then smiled warmly at Gabriella. “Welcome to our abode!”

Gabriella, seeing Charles living with two other ladies, felt her thoughts about marriage momentarily dissolve. She smiled back, a little shy but genuine. “Nice to meet you, I’m Gabriella.”

Lizzypie introduced herself. “I’m Lizzypie, Charles’ friend”

“I'm sapphire, also Charles’ friend” Sapphire also introduced.

Gabriella smiled and nodded.

Charles then spoke, calm and firm. “You will be living in my room for the time being.” Gabriella gulped, a mix of surprise and nervousness washing over her.

Lizzypie nodded and pulled out an invitation, handing it to Charles. “You have to see this,” she said.

The invitation was about the fight between Charles and Raphael which had been made official and published to the Cultivators people.

“This means every cultivator will be there to watch the fight,” Lizzypie explained. “If Raphael beats you, it will be a huge embarrassment.”

Charles scoffed, undeterred. “He’s really going to such lengths for this,” he muttered.

Lizzypie added, “Raphael is tagged a Class 2 cultivator, one of the top powers. Almost every young cultivator fears him. If you defeat him, you will take that position. If you win this, it will be so huge”

Charles’ jaw tightened. His determination rose sharply. “I’m winning tomorrow,” he said firmly.

He then wheeled Gabriella into his room, helping her unpack everything, showing her every corner with care.

Gabriella watched him, her heart softening. “That’s why you have so many girls here… you are so charismatic!” she said, amazed.

Charles just scoffed lightly, brushing it off.

Once everything was settled for Gabriella, Charles picked up one of his gemstones.

His mind focused—he would use it to upgrade the limit of his Moment Lock stage so that he could edge Raphael more.

Charles, as usual, stepped out onto the balcony. The night air was cool against his skin.

He placed the gemstone in front of him and began his meditation, focusing all his energy, willing the stone to release its power into him.

But almost immediately, a voice rang inside his mind: [Upgrading impossible — Host body is weak and can’t take any more aura energy].

Guck guck.

Charles coughed violently, blood spraying into the air. His eyes snapped open, sharp and alert.

He wiped the blood from his mouth, frustration flashing across his face. He had hit his limit with his current energy.

So this is what Mr. Noh was talking about, he thought grimly.

To truly upgrade, he would need Mr. Noh’s training—but it was already too late.

Tomorrow, he had to face Raphael with only the power he possessed now.

Just then, his phone buzzed. He picked it up. “Hello?”

Natasha’s voice came through, bright and excited. “I think I might have found what Raphael is looking for! I found a very small black stone among the others. I looked carefully—and it shines so brightly under the light!”

Charles frowned. “Really?”

“Yeah!” Natasha said. “And I have already found three.”

Charles’ eyes narrowed. “Three?”

“Yes,” she replied. “Whatever this stone is, it’s worth a fortune.”

He took a deep breath. “Natasha, the fight with Raphael has been shifted to tomorrow. After the fight, I will come check it out.”

Natasha’s tone shifted to worry. “Really? Will you be okay? You know what! I will come tomorrow, so we can go together.”

Charles nodded calmly. “Okay. Just make sure to hide the stone well.”

After they hung up, Charles exhaled slowly, leaning back against the balcony railing. The night was silent. He let out a long sigh and braced himself, waiting for the next day to arrive.
