Chapter 272

Charles’s voice cut through the chaos, calm yet unshakable.

“This isn’t something horrendous,” he said firmly. “It’s a fact.”

Mr. Timothy’s face reddened instantly. “You better know what you are doing!” he screamed, pointing a trembling finger at Charles. “You better shut up right now or I will have you arrested!”

Xavier let out a cold laugh and stepped forward slightly. “Mr. Man, I know you are disappointed that your lover doesn’t get to be CEO,” he said mockingly, “but that doesn’t mean you get to accuse people with such ridiculous nonsense.”

The board leaders erupted at once.

“This is absurd!”

“Someone call security!”

“Throw him out!”

“He’s spewing nonsense!”

Charles only smiled.

Without another word, he reached into his pocket and brought out his phone. The murmuring continued until he tapped the screen and raised the device so everyone could see.

A video began to play.

The room slowly fell silent.

On the screen was Mr. Timothy, seated across from Xavier in what looked like a private lounge. Their voices were clear as Charles turned up the volume.

Mr. Timothy’s voice came first.

“Don’t worry. After Belly dies, you will be next in line. The next heir, not Gabriella.”

A wave of gasps spread across the boardroom.

In the video, Xavier looked uneasy. “But how is that possible? I’m… I’m just an illegitimate son. Mr. Belly will never put me in his will.”

Mr. Timothy leaned back calmly. “I will make sure you get it by all means. Mr. Belly will die soon, even before he writes his will.”

The shock in the room deepened.

Video-Xavier frowned. “That makes it worse. Because his wealth will automatically go to Gabriella… that bitch.”

Several board members exchanged horrified looks.

Mr. Timothy in the video nodded slowly. “Hmm… you are right.”

Xavier leaned closer. “I want you to do something for me.”

Mr. Timothy asked, “What’s that?”

Xavier’s voice lowered.

“Kill Gabriella for me.”

The boardroom exploded with gasps.

“What the heck—!”

“Is this real?!”

On the video, Mr. Timothy looked visibly shocked. “No, Xavier, we can’t do that. You are Mr. Belly’s son. You will still be entitled to some wealth. In fact, we can still fight for the CEO position, but killing Gabriella is too far.”

Xavier’s expression hardened. “It’s impossible to get anything with her in the way. We have to eliminate her. If you make that possible, I will give you twenty-five percent of Father’s wealth.”

Mr. Timothy gulped.

Xavier continued, his voice filled with resentment. “I know you hate your brother because he never treated you the way he treated Gabriella. Even when he found me as his son, he just gave me something small and expected me to accept it. That’s all because of Gabriella. We kill her… and everything becomes ours.”

The video continued.

On the screen, Mr. Timothy sat silently, his fingers tapping against the table as he thought deeply. 

“Please do this,” Xavier urged. “We will have a lot of money.”

Mr. Timothy hesitated, then sighed heavily. “Shit… Belly has always been stubborn, and that Gabriella is truly a brat. She doesn’t respect anyone. It will make things hard if she assumes the position.” He paused, his expression darkening. “Fine… we kill her, as you said.”

The boardroom erupted.

“What the hell?!”

“He agreed?!”

“That’s insane!”

Shock spread like wildfire across every face present.

Mr. Timothy, watching the video play in real time, felt the color drain from his face. His lips trembled before he suddenly scoffed loudly.

“Uhh… what the fuck is that?” he barked. “Where did you get that video? I’m sure it’s AI! Completely fake!”

Xavier quickly added, panic creeping into his voice, “That’s absurd! Don’t believe this nonsense!”

Charles scoffed softly.

“Mr. Belly sent this to me himself,” he said calmly. “The day the house was on fire and Gabriella nearly died. Mr. Belly already knew who was behind it and how you were trying to kill her. That was why he placed her under my protection.”

Murmurs filled the room again.

Charles continued, his tone turning colder. “And even after that, you tried again. The truck driver you hired to run us over? Newsflash. He’s been caught. He’s confessing right now… and he mentioned your name.”

Mr. Timothy gulped hard, sweat forming across his forehead.

“No… no,” he muttered weakly. “He’s just making things up because he doesn’t want Gabriella to become CEO.”

Charles stepped forward. “I already submitted this evidence to the police and ran full authenticity tests.”

Right at that moment—

BANG!

The boardroom doors flew open.

Four uniformed police officers strode inside.

A wave of whispers spread instantly.

“He must be telling the truth…”

“Oh no…”

The officers walked straight toward Mr. Timothy.

One officer spoke firmly, “Mr. Timothy, you are under arrest for attempted murder and multiple related offenses.”

Before he could react, cold handcuffs snapped around his wrists.

“No! Nooo!” Mr. Timothy shouted desperately. “It’s all lies! All lies!”

The officers then turned toward Xavier.

“You are under arrest for conspiracy and solicitation of attempted murder.”

Another pair of handcuffs slammed onto Xavier’s wrists.

Xavier’s face twisted with rage as he glared at Charles. “Fuck you! Fuck you! I swear I will kill you if I return! Mr. Belly is my father! I’m entitled too—!”

His voice faded as the police dragged both men out of the room, their shouts echoing down the hallway until the doors closed and silence swallowed everything.

The boardroom fell completely still.

No one spoke.

A heavy silence lingered for several long moments before Charles finally broke it.

“I know all of you were deceived by Timothy,” he said calmly, scanning the room. “But Mr. Belly would have been disappointed by how quickly you sided against Gabriella.”

The board members lowered their heads uneasily.

Charles continued, voice steady. “Mr. Belly kept records of each of your… darker secrets. He entrusted them to me as leverage if any of you failed to support Gabriella the way you once supported him. His final wish was simple. Support her without conflict.”

The next-in-line board leader, Mr. Parish, immediately stood. “Yes… we will give our full and endless support to Miss Gabriella.”

Others quickly followed.

“Yes, Miss Gabriella.”

“You have our loyalty.”

“We stand with you.”
