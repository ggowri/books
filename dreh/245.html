Chapter 244

The drive to the Quill building was quiet.

Soon, the towering headquarters of Quill Enterprises came into view. It was one of the largest holdings in Texas, a glass giant that reflected the evening sun like a blade. 

The structure stood tall and imposing, surrounded by tight security and polished marble steps that led to massive revolving doors.

Caroline slowed her steps as they approached.

“My father is a very strict man,” she said quietly. “He is stubborn. He does not part with rare plants easily. Especially something like the Red Moon Herb.”

She hesitated before adding, “And Ivan is close to him. He has always wanted Ivan to marry me.”

Lizzypie’s eyes narrowed slightly

Caroline exhaled. “After what happened today…he was humiliated publicly… my father will not be pleased.”

Charles remained calm. “We will handle it.”

They stepped inside.

The lobby was grand, polished floors gleaming under crystal lights. Staff members greeted Caroline respectfully the moment they saw her.

“Miss Quill.”

A few bodyguards moved instinctively to block Charles and Lizzypie, but Caroline raised her hand.

“They are with me. Let them pass.”

The guards immediately stepped aside.

As they walked toward the private elevator, Caroline lowered her voice again. “I am almost certain he will refuse. So you should try your best to convince him.”

Charles and Lizzypie nodded once.

The elevator doors opened to the top floor.

They walked down a long hallway lined with dark wood panels and family portraits. 

At the end stood a pair of heavy double doors.

Caroline pushed them open.

Inside, a long polished table dominated the room.

It looked like they had been waiting for her.

The Quill family was having a meeting.

At the head of the table sat Mr. Quill, his posture straight and commanding. Beside him sat Mrs. Quill, elegant and composed. Caroline’s sister and brother were seated further down.

And sitting across from them—Ivan.

His face was covered in bandages. One arm was in a sling. Bruises peeked from beneath the wrappings.

The moment Charles stepped in, Ivan froze.

His eyes widened.

Then he shot up from his chair, fury exploding across his face.

“That’s him!” he shouted, pointing violently at Charles. “That fuxking— he is the one that beat me up and destroyed my car!”

Ivan’s chest heaved with anger.

“What the heck are they doing here?!” he barked, glaring at Caroline and then at Charles.

Mr. Quill, a man in his mid-sixties who looked remarkably healthy for his age, slowly lifted his head. His white mustache twitched slightly as his sharp eyes settled on his daughter. His voice was calm, but it carried a chilling authority.

“Caroline,” he said coldly. “Is that true?”

Caroline swallowed. “Father… yes, but they are—”

“You brought someone who beat your future husband into this house?” Mr. Quill cut her off sharply. “You brought them here?”

Caroline sighed, frustration flashing across her face. “Father, he is not my future husband. I can never marry him. Please stop giving him false hope.”

“Shut up!” Mr. Quill’s palm struck the table lightly, yet the sound echoed in the room. “Do you even know what you are saying? It is him you are marrying, whether you like it or not.”

Caroline’s hands clenched.

“Father, I don’t like this,” she said through her teeth. “There is no way I can live forever with someone like him.”

She paused, then added firmly, “Besides, I have someone else.”

The room went silent.

Caroline walked straight to Charles and stood beside him.

“He is my boyfriend.”

Mrs. Quill narrowed her eyes slightly. “Who is he?” she asked. “Is he rich?”

Charles immediately understood the kind of person Mrs. Quill was.

Caroline lifted her chin confidently. “Of course. He saved me from Ivan when he was using the public to pressure me into a proposal and even tried to abduct me.”

Gasps rippled across the table.

“He destroyed Ivan’s forty-million-dollar car,” she continued clearly. “And he paid fifty million instantly. He even added ten million extra.”

The room erupted into murmurs.

“Is that true?”

“What?”

“He destroyed a car and paid for it… with an additional ten million?”

“If he can do that, he must be strikingly rich.”

The whispers grew louder as everyone stared at Charles in disbelief.

The murmuring slowly died down as all eyes shifted to Mr. Quill.

He gulped once.

Then he leaned forward, fingers interlocked on the table.

“Is that true?” he asked, staring directly at Charles.

Before Charles could speak, Ivan quickly cut in.

“Yes,” he said stiffly. “He destroyed my car. It is only normal that he paid for it.”

Mr. Quill’s eyes narrowed.

“But he paid back fifty million,” he said slowly. “That is not compensation. That is… giving money away.”

Several family members nodded in agreement.

Then Vivian, Caroline’s older sister, crossed her arms and spoke in a cool voice.

“Who knows?” she said lightly. “He might have planned this with Caroline.”

The room quieted again.

Vivian continued, “Maybe Caroline sent him the money beforehand. Then he used it to impress everyone publicly. I am sure this is a setup so we would accept him.”

A few relatives exchanged looks.

“That might be true.”

“Yes. That makes more sense.”

“There is no way someone would just waste fifty million dollars.”

The whispers grew louder.

Mr. Quill’s face darkened. He slowly turned toward his daughter.

“Is that true?” he demanded. “I just made you the company manager last week, and you are already using company funds recklessly?”

The accusation hit hard.

Caroline was not just any businesswoman. She was a professional doctor and surgeon, exceptionally skilled and respected. Over the years, she had won numerous national awards and received several international recognitions for her medical expertise. Her precision in surgery and dedication to research had brought prestige to the Quill name.

That was why Mr. Quill trusted her.

That was why he confidently appointed her as the company manager.

But Vivian had never accepted it.

Being the older sister, she believed the position should have been hers. Since that day, she had been quietly waiting for an opportunity to discredit Caroline.

And now, she seized it.

Caroline stared at her sister in disbelief.

“There is no way I would do that!” she said firmly. “I would never misuse company money. Never.”
