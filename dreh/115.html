Chapter 115

Charles didn’t know whether to scold Lizzypie for doing what he told her to stop… or praise her. The outcome was far bigger than anything he expected. 
With this, his dream of becoming an entrepreneur,  a real businessman  suddenly felt possible again. He had abandoned that dream long ago.
 But now he wanted it back. Even without becoming the Godfather, wealth was already coming his way.
Eventually, he thanked Lizzypie.
Lizzypie folded her arms. “I’m sorry I went ahead and exposed Tracy, but I had to. Mr. Vincent has been pressuring me a lot about it.”
Charles nodded. “Is okay, I understand”.
They smiled at each other.
Then Lizzypie checked her phone, and her mood changed instantly.
“Who’s that from?” Charles asked.
She scoffed and hid her expression. “Nothing.”
Charles frowned. “You are hiding something from me. You aren’t telling me. What is it, Lizzypie?”
She said, “Just personal stuff. None of your business,” and she stormed off to her room. 
Charles exhaled sharply. He really wanted to know what she was keeping from him.
Just then, his phone rang. Serena.
He cleared his throat before picking up.
Serena excitedly said from over the phone. “I found the hideout of the thugs. Please come so we can go together.”
Charles blinked.
“Really? Okay, I’m on my way.”
He rushed into the bathroom, took the fastest shower of his life, got dressed in a clean fit, and headed straight out.
He arrived at the Moretti building in a cab.
When he reached the front steps, he saw Serena and Fred, her personal bodyguard, standing near a row of black SUVs. The morning air was tense.
They exchanged a subtle greeting.
Serena stepped toward him, her expression cold and focused.
“I did some tracking and investigation,” she said. “We managed to find where the thugs live.”
“I want you to follow me,” Serena continued. “I will need your fighting skill.”
Charles nodded without hesitation, then looked around. There was only a small convoy, one car filled with guards, maybe ten men.
He frowned.
“Aren’t we calling the police?”
Serena scoffed lightly.
“No. I’m not handing them to the police. I’m going there to kill them after I torture them for information about who sent them.”
Charles didn’t react, just nodded.
He examined the guards again, counting silently.
“So… is this all?” he asked. “Hope we won’t be outnumbered. These men aren’t up to twenty.”
Serena gave him a look.
“You are underestimating our guards. Many of them are retired soldiers. And I already sent men to surround the area ahead of time. The thugs aren’t many either, maybe twenty at most. We will take them by surprise. It’s fine.”
Charles nodded slowly.
“Alright.”
Moments later, Fred and the guards entered one car, while Serena and Charles got into another
.
Charles sat in the driver’s seat and started the engine.
For a while, the ride was silent, only the hum of the car and the sound of the road under the tires. Then Serena finally spoke.
“About last night…”
Charles’ jaw tightened.
“Oh… that. I’m sorry. I didn’t mean to reject you—”
“It’s okay,” Serena cut in. “It won’t be happening again. And… I’m sorry for making such an inappropriate advance.”
Charles wanted to say no, it wasn’t inappropriate, but Serena didn’t let him. She was so embarrassed by Charles’ rejection.
“And also,” she continued calmly, “my father is giving me the company officially next week. So… after that week, we can break up.”
Charles stared at the road.
“Oh.”
He just nodded, without responding further.
After some minutes, they arrived at a large uncompleted building and parked some distance away. It was old and the whole place looked abandoned and perfect for criminals to hide.
They stepped out of the car.
Fred approached them quickly, speaking in a low, controlled voice.
“I already studied the entire building. We will sneak in from the east side, climb through the back corridor, and then ambush the thugs in their main hideout room.”
Serena nodded sharply.
She grabbed a bulletproof vest from one of the guards and slipped it on, tightening the straps. Fred handed her a gun, and she checked it with practiced hands.
Charles reached for a weapon too, but refused the bulletproof vest.
Serena turned to him immediately.
“What are you doing? Put it on.”
Charles shook his head.
“It’s too heavy. I don’t need it.”
Serena stared at him, partly annoyed, partly concerned, but didn’t argue more.
He grabbed a small pistol instead.
Fred motioned them forward, leading the group in a silent line along the shadowed side of the building. 
They entered, with Fred signalling when to stop. All of them with their guns prepared. They took a staircase. When they reached the landing, they stopped.
A flickering light glowed under a door ahead.
They could hear muffled talking.  That was the thugs main hideout. They took sideways,  and stopped just beside the door.
Fred lifted his hand, raised his finger. Signaling for countdown to burst in. He mouthed silently
1… 2… 3…
They burst in.
“DON’T MOVE OR I’LL BLOW YOUR HEAD OFF!” Fred roared.
The thugs inside froze instantly, shocked by the sudden ambush.
They had no time to react. Serena and all her guards were already surrounding them, guns pointed from every angle.
The thugs stared wide-eyed, They were trapped.
Some of the thugs were armed, gripping their guns like they wanted to fight back. Fred pointed his weapon at them sharply and barked:
“Drop your guns! If any of you act funny, you’ll be dead even before pulling the trigger.”
They looked at each, clearly not wanting to obey. Then a voice chimed in. 
“Drop it.”
A man stepped forward from the middle of the Gang. The thugs obeyed instantly. They dropped their guns one by one.
Serena’s eyes narrowed.
She recognized him.
Charles did too.
He was one of the three gunmen who attacked them last night.
Serena stepped forward, and pointed a gun at him.
“Where are the other two?”
The leader grinned, “Nice meeting you again,” he said.
“Shut up your mouth and answer my question,” Serena snapped. “Or I will blow your head off right here. Where are the other two?”
The thug leader’s smirk widened.
And right then, Charles felt something wasn’t right.
