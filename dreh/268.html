Chapter 269

Raphael stared at the bloody stumps where his hands had been.

Then his body collapsed forward from the wheelchair, dropping to his knees, armless, trembling violently.

“No… no… Father…” he sobbed, tears mixing with sweat and blood. “My hands… my hands…i got no hands again, I'm done for…”

“Raphael!” Mr. Jacob shouted, panic completely replacing his earlier arrogance.

But the fear in his eyes had already spread.

He raised his shaking hand and signaled his remaining men.

“Drop your weapons! Drop them now!”

One by one, rifles and pistols clattered onto the concrete.

Some of his men immediately rushed to untie Charles, Lizzypie, and the others.

As soon as Gabriella was freed, she ran—stumbling but determined—to her father’s lifeless body. She dropped beside him, pulling him into her arms, sobbing uncontrollably.

“Father… please wake up… please…”

Charles, still weak, slowly stood after the ropes were removed. His legs almost gave out, but he forced himself forward.

He walked toward Mr. Vincent. Then bowed.

“I’m sorry,” he said quietly.

Mr. Vincent scoffed lightly. “I expected this, actually.”

He reached into his coat and pulled out a small vial, handing it to Charles.

“Drink.”

Without hesitation, Charles swallowed it.

Instantly, it felt like something snapped inside his body. The fog in his head cleared. Strength surged back into his muscles. The strange paralysis evaporated.

He flexed his fingers. The weakness was gone.

“Are you okay?” Lizzypie asked, rushing to him.

Charles nodded. “Perfectly okay now.”

Behind them, Mr. Jacob finally found his voice again.

“Vincent… this is none of your business. Why interfere? Why involve yourself?”

Mr. Vincent turned slowly toward him.

“You still don’t understand?” he said calmly.

The Trisian family had once been untouchable—wealthy, powerful, feared. There was even a time they didn’t fear the Godfather.

But times had changed.

Now Mr. Vincent had government backing. Political protection. Military contracts. His influence stretched beyond the underground. That alone swallowed whatever legacy the Trisian family once had.

Mr. Vincent’s voice turned colder.

“Do you know who he is?” He gestured toward Charles. “He is my grandson.”

Mr. Jacob’s face went blank. “W–what?”

“Grandson?” Raphael whispered weakly from the floor.

“I… I thought he was just an orphan… that’s what we were told…”

Realization crashed down on Mr. Jacob.

His cane fell from his hand.Despite his age and pride, he dropped to his knees.

“Please forgive me, Vincent. I didn’t know. I had no idea he was your blood. Please… forgive me.”

Mr. Vincent’s eyes hardened.

“If I hadn’t come in time, you would have killed him.”

“We didn’t know!” Mr. Jacob pleaded desperately. “If we had known—”

“That’s exactly the problem,” Vincent interrupted. “The Trisian family has terrorized this land for years. Abusing power. Destroying lives. Is high time to face your Karma”He gave a small nod to Rain.

Rain stepped forward and pointed his gun directly at Raphael’s head.

Mr. Jacob’s composure shattered.

“Please! Don’t kill my son! He is my only legacy! Please!”

Mr. Vincent’s expression didn’t change.

“By the time I’m done with you,” he said coldly, “you won’t have any legacy left.”

Raphael looked up, pale and shaking.

“Dad… please… don't let them kill me”

Boom!

The gunshot echoed brutally. Raphael’s body jerked and fell still.

Blood splattered across the floor.

“Nooooo! My son! Noooo!” Mr. Jacob screamed, crawling toward the body, tears streaming down his face.

The same man who had just executed Mr. Belly in front of his daughter…was now crying over his own son. What a big hypocrisy. 

He cried so bitterly and no one felt sympathy.

Mr. Vincent looked down at the broken old man kneeling before him.

“Now let me tell you how this will go,” he said calmly. “I will kill you. Then I will collect your empire and turn it into mine.”

Mr. Jacob’s grief twisted into fury. He slowly lifted his head, eyes burning.

“I won’t let you do that,” he growled. “It’s my legacy!”

With sudden force, he slammed his cane against the ground.

Crack!

The cane split apart and extended, transforming into a long spear. The metal shaft shimmered, glowing faintly with a strange aura that pulsed along its length.

The remaining Trisian men gasped.

Mr. Jacob’s breathing grew heavier. “I never wanted to use this power. It drains what little life I have left. But now… I don’t care. You will die for what you did!”

Rain immediately stepped forward.

Charles moved too.

But Mr. Vincent lifted a hand slightly to stop them. They did. 

Mr. Jacob lunged.

The spear shot forward, its glowing edge slicing through the air with deadly speed, light trailing behind it.

But Mr. Vincent didn’t move.

Not even a step. At the very last second, he casually raised two fingers and tapped the spear’s shaft.

Tap.

The glowing aura flickered. Then it died instantly.

The light vanished like a candle blown out.

The spear reverted back into an ordinary wooden cane mid-thrust.

Mr. Jacob stumbled forward in disbelief.

“What… what the—”

Mr. Vincent brushed dust off his sleeve. “Is that all you have got? I truly had higher expectations of you.”

Mr. Jacob’s hands trembled as he stared at the now harmless cane.

Impossible!!!. How could someone be so powerful!.

Mr. Vincent walked toward him slowly.

Each step echoed.

Mr. Jacob began backing away. “Wait… wait… please. I’m sorry. I—”

Mr. Vincent moved faster than his age suggested. In a blink, he was behind Mr. Jacob.

The S-shaped twin blade appeared in his hand again.

He grabbed the old man by the collar, yanking his head back.

“Too late.”

A single clean motion.

Shhk.

The blade slid across Mr. Jacob’s throat.

For a split second, nothing happened.

Then,  blood sprayed outward.

Mr. Jacob’s eyes widened, staring into nothingness as dark red poured down his chest. His mouth opened, but no words came out.

He stood there, frozen, as life drained from him.

Then his body collapsed to the floor.

Silence.

Just like that…The Trisian family crumbled.

The remaining Trisian gang members looked at the fallen patriarch… then at Raphael’s body… then at the thousand armed soldiers surrounding them.

One by one—

They dropped to their knees.

“All hail Godfather!”

“All hail Godfather!”

“Please forgive us! Spare us!”

Their voices overlapped in fear.

Mr. Vincent wiped the blade clean and let it rest at his side.

The era of the Trisian family ended on that warehouse floor.
