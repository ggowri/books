Chapter 289

Charles scoffed.

“Would you rather die?” he repeated calmly.

“Maybe I should give you that.”

Johnson’s eyes widened.

“Don’t you know who I am?” he shouted angrily. “It’s me. The mayor’s son!”

His voice trembled with fury and humiliation.

“I swear, if you touch me… if you dare do anything to me… my father will destroy every single one of you!”

Charles didn’t look impressed.

In fact he looked bored.

“I’m looking forward to that,” he said flatly.

Then he pointed again to the sand.

“So kneel. Or I will make you.”

Johnson’s pride exploded.

“No!” he yelled. “I won’t!”

The next moment happened so fast that many people barely saw it.

Charles moved.

In a blink he stepped forward and delivered a sharp kick upward.

CRACK!

His foot struck Johnson under the chin.

The sound of bone snapping echoed sharply.

Johnson’s head snapped back violently as he screamed.

“AAAAAH!”

His shin had cracked painfully from the impact, and the sudden shock sent him collapsing down onto his knees in the sand.

Tears burst from his eyes instantly.

“You—fuck you!” he cried.

Before he could say anything else—

BAM!

Zander’s fist smashed into his face.

Blood sprayed from Johnson’s nose as he fell sideways into the sand.

“Don’t call him that,” Zander growled coldly.

He grabbed Johnson by the collar and yanked him upright again.

“Say it again and I’ll break your nose completely.”

Johnson’s bravado shattered.

Seeing the situation completely reversed, the arrogance drained from his face and panic replaced it.

He started crying openly.

His girlfriend, who had been standing behind him in shock, suddenly dropped to her knees first.

“I’m sorry!” she blurted out desperately.

Her voice trembled as she bowed her head repeatedly.

“I was wrong! I lied! Please forgive me. I shouldn’t have said those things. I shouldn’t have accused you.”

She cried harder as the humiliation sank in.

Johnson looked around.

Hundreds of eyes were watching. With phones were recorded.

People were whispering and laughing.

The embarrassment burned through him like fire.

He had never imagined kneeling publicly like this.

Especially not for someone he had mocked minutes earlier.

But the fear in his chest was stronger than his pride now.

He slowly lowered his head.

“I… I apologize,” he muttered.

Charles frowned slightly.

“Louder.”

The single word echoed.

Johnson clenched his teeth as laughter began spreading through the crowd.

“I said I’m sorry!” he shouted, his voice cracking with humiliation.

More laughter rippled across the beach.

Charles watched him quietly for a moment before speaking again.

“Good,” he said.

Then he added calmly,

“I will be waiting for your father.”

Johnson’s face twisted with shame.

Without saying another word he staggered to his feet and limped away across the sand, tears still running down his face.

His girlfriend quickly stood up as well.

But instead of following him closely like before, she walked in a completely different direction, pretending like she had never known him at all.

After Johnson and his girlfriend disappeared into the distance, the tension on the beach slowly dissolved. The crowd began to scatter, though many still whispered about what they had just witnessed.

Zander turned toward Charles immediately.

He bowed his head deeply.

“Boss… I’m sorry,” he said.

Charles looked at him calmly.

“Why are you even doing this part-time thug job?” he asked. “If that had been someone else earlier, you would have definitely beaten him without thinking.”

Zander gulped.He couldn’t deny that.

Charles sighed lightly.

“I will double the salary I already give you,” he said. “From now on, I don’t want you working as a thug for anyone else. You work only for me.”

For a moment Zander just stared.

Then his face exploded with joy.

“Thank you, boss!” he shouted happily. “Thank you, sir!”

The other men behind him bowed respectfully as well.

Charles waved a hand.

“You can all leave.”

They nodded immediately and began walking away, following Zander as they disappeared from the beach.

The old man slowly stepped forward again.

His eyes were filled with gratitude.

“Young man,” he said softly, “I am so grateful. You gave me justice today. My heart is satisfied now.”

Charles smiled.

“Wait here,” he said.

Before the old man could respond, Charles jogged off toward where their bags were placed.

A moment later he returned holding a sleek black card.

He placed it gently into the old man’s trembling hands.

“This is an unlimited card,” Charles said. “Get whatever you need with it. When you’re done, return it to me at the Elite Hotel.”

The old man froze.

His eyes widened as he looked down at the card.

“T-this… this is…”

People nearby gasped when they saw it clearly.

“That’s a black card.”

“Wait… that’s an unlimited black card.”

“What the hell?”

“Who is this man? Giving black card randomly”

Whispers erupted again through the beach crowd. The old man’s hands trembled.

“No… no… I can’t accept something like this,” he said quickly, trying to give it back.

Charles gently pushed his hand away.

“Take it,” he insisted.

The old man hesitated.

“But I will ll return it,” he promised nervously.

Charles nodded.

“Spend as far as you need.”

Tears filled the old man’s eyes.

“I am so grateful,” he said again and again.

Charles simply smiled.

“You can go now. Just return the card to me at the Elite Hotel.”

The old man walked away slowly, but every few steps he turned back to look at Charles again with deep admiration in his eyes.

Natasha nudged Charles playfully.

“You’re always so cool,” she teased. “That’s the reason I’m crazy in love with you.”

Sapphire laughed softly.

“Same here. You always find the perfect way to resolve things.”

Lizzypie grinned.

“That stupid Johnson thought you were some ragged nobody,” she said. “Did you see his face when he realized who you actually were?”

Everyone burst into laughter.

With the tension completely gone, they returned to their spot on the beach.

Soon the group was laughing, playing, and enjoying the ocean again like nothing had happened.
