Chapter 293

.

Charles didn’t say anything more. He simply walked past her with Serena beside him, while Lizzypie, Natasha, Sapphire, and Gabriella followed behind. The group entered DeRoyal Hall together.

Inside, the atmosphere shifted instantly.

People began whispering the moment they noticed them.

Many guests immediately recognized Sapphire and Natasha. Both were already well-known public figures in the city, and seeing them arrive together like this naturally drew attention.

“Is that Sapphire?”

“And Natasha too.”

“What are they doing here together?”

“Who is that man with them?”

Eyes followed Charles as he walked calmly through the hall.

Meanwhile Serena glanced quietly at Sapphire walking beside Charles. She knew Sapphire very well. In fact, Sapphire was the same person she had once approached for a business partnership, and surprisingly the agreement had gone through smoothly without much resistance.

Now seeing Sapphire walking comfortably beside Charles made something click in Serena’s mind.

So that was it.

Charles had been behind that connection.

Serena realized something at that moment.

Charles wasn’t what he appeared to be.

There was clearly much more about him than he ever revealed.

Still, she didn’t ask anything.

Not tonight.

The party atmosphere gradually settled again as soft music floated through the hall. Waiters moved around carrying trays of drinks while guests mingled and laughed. A few performers later stepped onto the stage and entertained the crowd with elegant dance and music.

Businessmen connected with potential partners. Investors exchanged cards. Laughter and conversation filled the hall.

Everything was going smoothly.

Until Collins decided to speak.

He raised his glass slightly and spoke loudly enough for many nearby guests to hear.

“You know something interesting,” he said with a smile. “Every one of us here either runs a company or helps run one.”

He turned his eyes toward Charles.

“Except our man here.”

A few people chuckled nervously.

Collins continued with a mocking grin.

“Charles, maybe you can tell us your secret. Perhaps you’re doing something we don’t know about.”

The room grew quieter.

Many eyes turned toward Charles.

Whispers spread.

“What?”

“He doesn’t run anything?”

“Then who is he?”

“How did he even get invited here?”

Charles simply scoffed and ignored the question.

Tracy didn’t like the direction things were going.

She stepped forward.

“Actually,” she said firmly, “he’s my best friend. I invited him here.”

Someone in the crowd asked curiously, “He’s your ex-husband, right?”

The murmuring grew louder.

“Ex-husband?”

“He doesn’t own anything and still comes to his ex-wife’s anniversary?”

“That’s shameless.”

Tracy raised her voice slightly.

“Yes, he is my ex-husband,” she admitted. “But everything I have achieved today, including this company, would not exist without him.”

The crowd quieted again.

“We built the foundation together,” Tracy continued. “He always gave me ideas and guidance. I invited him here as a man of honor.”

Charles looked at her, surprised.

He hadn’t expected that.

Tracy had always been proud and stubborn. But lately, she seemed to be changing, even if her ego still remained.

Some people nodded respectfully at her explanation.

But Collins clearly didn’t like it.

He laughed lightly.

“Well,” he said sarcastically, “my fiancée must be very forgiving.”

He looked at Tracy.

“I heard he did a lot of wrong things to you.”

“Collins,” Tracy warned quietly.

“Let me talk,” Collins said.

Then he looked at the crowd again.

“Did you know Tracy even gave him a large amount of money after their divorce?”

People leaned in curiously.

“And do you know what he used that money for?”

He pointed openly at Serena and the other women.

“To gather women like them.”

The crowd erupted in whispers.

“That’s ridiculous.”

“So that’s what happened?”

Collins continued confidently.

“That’s why he doesn’t have any company to his name.”

He leaned closer to Charles.

“Or do you?”

Charles only stared at him calmly.

Inside, he knew exactly what Collins was trying to do.

He had suspected for a while that Collins planned to scam Tracy and run away with her money. Charles had kept quiet because he didn’t want to interfere before.

But now Collins was pushing too far.

Charles smiled faintly and decided to reveal things.

“You must be very proud of yourself,” he said.

Collins raised an eyebrow.

Charles continued calmly.

“Akeem of Dubai is willing to give you a partnership contract.”

He tilted his head slightly.

“How I wish I were like you.”

Two or three men in the crowd suddenly froze. They knew who Akeem of  Dubai was.

“What?”

One of them gasped.

“Did he just say Akeem of Dubai?”

Another whispered in shock.

“Akeem… one of the biggest businessmen in Dubai?”

Eyes immediately returned to Collins.

“If that’s true,” someone murmured, “then Collins must have landed a massive deal.”

Collins gulped,  he knew Charles might have known something.  He just gulped and put on a forced smile.
