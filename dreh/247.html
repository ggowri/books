Chapter 246

Ivan scoffed loudly.

“I am so sure you cannot pull it off,” he said with open contempt. “What is this bet even about?”

Charles did not look away.

“If you are that sure,” he replied calmly, “then let us make it official.”

Ivan hesitated for only a second before lifting his chin.

“Fine. Yes. I bet. If you actually get the deal with the Yachina Gang…” He smirked. “I will never disturb Caroline again.”

Charles smiled faintly and nodded.

“Good. Then we have your word.”

“Yeah,” Ivan said confidently. “We do.”

Charles turned slowly to face Mr. Quill.

“There is something I want as well,” he said.

Mr. Quill frowned. “And what would that be?”

“The Red Moon Herb.”

A visible shift passed across Mr. Quill’s face.

“Hmm,” he muttered. “I do have one. Only one. It is worth over two hundred million if I decide to sell it. Even with that price, I refused to sell because it is extremely rare. You cannot find it twice.”

Charles spoke steadily. “We need it to cure someone. Please help us”

Mr. Quill folded his arms and thought for a moment.

“If you truly settle the Yachina Gang issue,” he said slowly, “you will be saving this family far more than two hundred million. In that case… I will give you the herb.”

Charles let out a quiet breath and nodded.

“Thank you.”

The silence that followed lasted only a few seconds before it broke into scattered laughter across the room.

“He really thinks he is coming back alive.”

“Someone should prepare a coffin in advance.”

“The Yachina Gang do not negotiate with nobodies.”

“He just signed his own death certificate.”

“This is a suicide mission.”

Charles stood there without reacting. His face remained calm, unreadable.

Caroline was so shocked as well.

Mr. Quill said sharply, “So get to it right now.”

With that, the meeting ended.

Lizzypie and Charles walked out, with Caroline following them. She sneered, “What the heck is that? I don’t understand you. The Yachina Gang fear no one, I swear.”

Then Ivan also appeared, grinning. “I love your bravery,” he said, his voice dripping with malice. “Sad you will die, because I will go and meet the Yachina Gang myself and push them to kill you. I will tell them how you are ridiculing them.”

Charles scoffed, calm but deadly. “I swear you will regret it.”

Ivan laughed hysterically. “Okay, let’s see who will regret it,” he said, and dashed off.

Caroline let out a frustrated sigh. “This is getting harder.”

Charles glanced at her and said calmly, “Don't worry. Nothing will happen.”

Still uneasy, Caroline insisted on following them as they made their way out.

Charles, Lizzypie, and Caroline finally arrived at the Yachina Gang hideout.

 From the outside, it was clear who owned the place—they didn’t even bother hiding it. 

Axe symbols were painted across the walls and etched into the gates, an unmissable mark of their territory. Lizzypie muttered under her breath, “I hope they don’t kill us before we even get a chance to explain ourselves.”

Charles let out a quiet sigh, steadying himself as they approached the entrance. Just as they were about to step in, a massive man covered in tattoos blocked the way.

“Where the fuck are you going?” he demanded, his voice low and threatening.

Caroline stepped forward confidently. “They’re with me.”

The man’s eyes flicked over her, and with a subtle nod, he stepped aside. Everyone know Caroline in the city. 

They entered, and the interior hit them like a punch. Smoke thickened the air, mingling with the sharp tang of sweat and sex. 

Music throbbed through the walls, and the low murmur of dangerous laughter filled the space. 

Caroline led them deeper, finally stopping at a heavy wooden door. She knocked firmly, and a burly man opened it. Recognition flashed in his eyes, and without hesitation, he let them in.

Inside, the room was a different world. Around fifty men, all heavily tattooed and armed, were sprawled across chairs and sofas. 

At the far end, a man lounged on a sofa, smoking lazily. Beside him, a woman, naked, played with him provocatively.

Caroline’s face tightened at the obscene display. And next to the man sat Ivan.

The moment Ivan’s eyes fell on Charles, he shouted, “Nero! This is the fucking man that looks down on the Yachina Gang!”

The man on the sofa, Nero, signaled the woman to leave. She slipped out silently, leaving him standing. Smoke curled from his cigarette as he rose and stepped toward Charles, puffing smoke in his face.

“Really,” Nero said, voice low and deadly, “you fucking say that?”.

Charles kept his gaze steady, unflinching. “I never said that,” he said calmly, his voice measured.

Ivan jumped up, pointing at Charles with venom. “No! He’s lying! He said that! He’s literally here to tell you that you shouldn’t disturb the Quill family. We told him how dangerous you are, and he said—you will not fucking do anything!”

Nero’s eyes narrowed, the smoke from his cigarette curling around his face like a warning. 

His voice was sharp, dangerous. “Really? Who the fuck are you that you think you can waltz in here and we will give you what you want?”

Charles exhaled slowly, as if weary of the tension, and said, “Well… let’s have a proper talk. Who knows, you might see sense in it.”

Nero let out a low, gravelly growl. “You are really daring, you know that? Me… have a proper talk with me? Who the hell do you think you are? Tell me—who are you?”

Ivan’s grin twisted, almost gleeful. “You shouldn’t even allow him to talk at all. You should just cripple him, beat him mercilessly, then throw him out!”

Nero turned fully toward Charles now, his rage barely contained. “Listen to what he said! We will do that if you don’t get out right now!”

Charles didn’t move. He didn’t blink. He just stared at Nero.
