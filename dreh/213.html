Chapter 213

 

Everyone reacted to what Serena said as they turned at her. 

Mr. Gerald’s face darkened. “Serena,” he said sharply, “what did you just say?”

Serena scoffed. “I said, are you being a clown right now?”

Mr. Gerald slammed his palm on the table. “Watch your mouth. I am the elder of this family.”

Serena laughed coldly. “Elder my ass. And who appointed you for that role?” Her voice rose, controlled but cutting. 

“First, you rushed my father’s body into burial while the investigation was still ongoing. Then you let Collins—who was disowned—walk back into this house like nothing happened. And now you are handing him the CEO position. If that isn’t clown behavior, I don’t know what is.”

“Watch your mouth, Serena,” Mr. Gerald snapped.

 “Your father is gone. We can’t halt the family’s affairs. As the next elder, it is my right to take charge. And Collins is the first son. It makes sense for him to be CEO.”

Allison scoffed loudly. “Oh, so now you care?” she cut in. “Since the day Father died, you haven't shed a single tear. You didn’t even act like you cared. Now that the CEO position is on the table, suddenly you are all righteous?”

Serena turned to her, eyes blazing. “And you don’t think we have a bigger issue right now?”

She swept her gaze across the room, meeting every face.

“Our father was murdered under this roof,” she said firmly. 

“The killer might be sitting right here among us. And instead of finding out who did it,  we are busy dividing power.”

Mr. Gerald waved a dismissive hand. “That’s what the police are for. They will find the truth sooner or later. Our focus should be on moving this family’s affairs forward.”

Serena nodded slowly, disbelief written all over her face. “I can’t believe this.”

Collins finally spoke, his voice calm but edged with entitlement. “We are all affected by Father’s death. As a righteous son, it’s only right that I return home and do my part. Why do you have a problem with that?”

Serena swallowed, then lifted her head, her voice steady as she finally said what she had been holding back. 

“If we are talking about moving this family forward, then I should be the CEO. And the head of the family.”

The room stirred.

“My father already appointed me,” Serena continued. “He made it clear.”

Whispers broke out immediately.

“Oh, so that’s what she cares about.”

“She’s unbelievable.”

“How does she think she can rule over us?”

Mr. Gerald scoffed. “That was before my brother died. Things have changed.”

Serena’s eyes hardened. “How have things changed?”

She looked around the room, one face after another. “I can’t help but feel one of you killed Father for this nonsense.”

The murmurs grew louder.

“What is she saying?”

“Is she accusing us now?”

Allison folded her arms and said sharply, “If we are going down that road, then you are the prime suspect. That’s why you are angry. Things didn’t go your way.”

Collins nodded in agreement. “Exactly. Father announces her as CEO, and suddenly he’s dead. Maybe she thought killing him would secure her position so she could rule however she wanted.”

The room went quiet, eyes slowly turning back to Serena as the accusation settled in the air.

Serena gulped and slowly nodded, her hands clenched at her sides not knowing what to say.

Mr. Gerald straightened and spoke firmly. “Let’s not throw accusations around. My decision is final. I will be the head of the family, and Collins will be the CEO of the family business.”

Then a voice resonated clearly through it.

“Well… no. You won’t.”

Everyone turned as Charles stepped into the grand room.

Murmurs erupted instantly.

“What the fuck is he doing!”.

“That trash doesn't have any say in this family”.

Mr. Gerald scoffed. “Who do you think you are? You are not part of this family. You have no say here. Shut up.”

Collins sneered. “You think you can just barge in and speak nonsense? This is a Moretti house meeting. Exclusively for the Moretti family.”

Charles didn’t flinch. “Well, I won’t shut up,” he said calmly. “Not when it involves my fiancée.”

“She is supposed to be the CEO,” Charles continued evenly, “as Mr. Moretti stated before he died.”

Mr. Gerald slammed his hand on the table. “He is dead. An announcement means nothing unless it was documented. And it wasn’t.”

Charles scoffed. “Who told you it wasn’t?”

He reached into his coat, pulled out a folded document, and held it up.

“Here it is,” he said. “Mr. Moretti’s signed document appointing Serena as the CEO and the head of the family.”

Everyone tensed up.

Even Serena froze, her eyes locked on the document in Charles’s hand.

Mr. Gerald’s face tightened as Charles calmly stepped forward and spread the file on the table in front of him.

Collins shot to his feet, fury flashing across his face. “What the hell do you think you’re doing?” he roared. “Fabricating documents?”

He pointed at Charles aggressively. “Get out now before we have you arrested!”
