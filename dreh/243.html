Chapter 242

Ivan frowned and stepped closer, eyes narrowing. “Are you sure of what you are saying? What exactly are we putting to test?”

Charles met his gaze evenly. “That I can do more than all this. This isn’t something to be proud of.”

Ivan scoffed and pointed toward the Mercedes-Benz S-Class. “That car alone cost forty million dollars. Forty million. Money you can never hold in your lifetime.”

Charles slowly walked toward the car.

“This car?” he said calmly. “You mean this?”

Before anyone could react—

Boom.

His fist drove straight into the hood.

A deafening metallic crack split the air. The bonnet caved inward unnaturally. A violent hiss followed as smoke burst out from beneath it, spilling into the air.

The crowd erupted.

“What?!”

“What the hell did he just do?”

“Did he just damage the engine with one punch?!”

Ivan staggered forward in disbelief. “What the hell did you do to my car?!”

Charles didn’t answer.

He stepped to the front windshield.

Boom.

His fist struck again.

Shrrrrr—

The glass shattered instantly, fragments crashing onto the ground in a glittering rain.

Ivan looked like he might faint. “My car! My forty-million-dollar car! Are you insane?!”

Charles’ expression didn’t change.

He moved again.

Smash.

The side mirror snapped off.

Smash.

The door dented inward beyond repair.

Smash. Smash. Smash.

Each blow echoed across the street. Metal crumpled like paper. The pristine luxury vehicle transformed into twisted wreckage in seconds. Smoke continued pouring out as alarms screamed uselessly.

People backed away in fear.

“That car can’t be repaired anymore!”

“He destroyed it!”

Ivan’s face turned pale, then red. Rage and disbelief collided in his eyes. The car had been bought to impress Caroline, to force a yes in front of cameras. He had planned to retrieve it later once the publicity worked in his favor.

But now—It was ruined.

Charles stepped back at last, flexing his fingers once.

The Mercedes-Benz no longer looked like a luxury vehicle. It looked like scrap metal waiting to be recycled.

Silence fell over the crowd.

Charles glanced at Ivan and spoke calmly,

“So, this is your Forty million!!!.”

Ivan snapped.

“What the fuck have you done!” he shouted, his voice cracking with rage. “You just signed your death!”

Charles only smiled faintly.

Ivan turned sharply to his men. There were many of them, nearly forty, all dressed in black, strong and trained.

“Kill him,” Ivan barked. “Cripple him first. I will sell his kidney and use it to recover what he destroyed!”

The men did not hesitate.

They rushed forward together.

The first three reached Charles at the same time, fists swinging toward his face and ribs. Charles stepped slightly to the side and avoided the first punch easily.

 He grabbed the attacker’s wrist and twisted it hard. A sharp crack sounded as the man screamed and dropped to his knees. 

Without pausing, Charles drove his elbow into the second man’s jaw. The impact lifted the man off his feet before he crashed onto the ground.

The third tried to tackle him from behind, but Charles shifted his weight, grabbed the man’s shoulder, and flipped him over his hip. The man slammed onto the pavement flat on his back, the air forced out of his lungs.

More men charged in.

One pulled out a baton and swung it down toward Charles’ head. Charles caught the baton mid-air with one hand, squeezed it until it bent slightly, then kicked the man straight in the chest. The man flew backward and collided into two others behind him.

Five attacked together from different angles. Charles moved smoothly, like he could see every motion before it happened. He ducked under a punch, drove his fist into someone’s stomach, then spun and kicked another across the face. 

Teeth flew. Blood sprayed. Another man tried to grab his arm, but Charles slammed his forehead into the man’s nose and broke it instantly.

The crowd screamed and scattered further away.

Two men came at him with knives. Charles’ expression hardened. He stepped forward instead of back. He caught one wrist, twisted it until the knife fell, then used the same hand to punch the man so hard he collapsed unconscious.

 The second stabbed forward wildly, but Charles shifted slightly and drove his knee into the man’s ribs. A loud crack followed as the man dropped, clutching his side.

Within seconds, bodies were already covering the ground.

But they kept coming.

Charles grabbed one man by the collar and used him like a shield when another tried to punch him. The punch landed on his own teammate instead. Charles then shoved the shielded man into three others, knocking them all down.

Someone jumped at him from behind. Charles sensed it and spun, catching the attacker mid-air by the throat. He lifted him briefly and threw him onto the hood of another car, denting it deeply.

The fight did not even last five full minutes.

One by one, the men fell.

Some groaned in pain. Some could not move. Others crawled away in fear.

When it was over, all forty of Ivan’s men were on the ground.

Charles stood in the middle of them, breathing steady, his clothes barely disturbed.

The entire street was silent.

Ivan stared in horror.

Charles slowly turned his head toward him.

“Now,” he said calmly, “who is selling whose kidney?”.

Caroline stood frozen for a moment, her heart racing. She had watched everything unfold in front of her.

Forty men.Forty grown men rushing at one person. And he handled them without panic, without struggle, without even looking tired.

Ivan, on the other hand, was no longer angry.

He was terrified.

As Charles began walking toward him slowly, step by step, Ivan stumbled backward.

“Yo! Somebody call the police!” he shouted, voice shaking. “Call the police!”

No one moved.

Most people were too stunned to react.

Charles stopped right in front of him.

“Now,” he said calmly, “I will be the one to cripple you.”

Ivan’s legs gave out instantly.

He dropped to his knees.

“Please,” he begged, his arrogance completely gone. “Please don’t injure me. I’m sorry. I’m sorry.”

Charles looked down at him without emotion.

“Good. You apologized,” he said. “I will leave you because of that.”

Ivan nodded quickly. “Yes. Yes. Thank you.”

Charles leaned slightly closer.

“But next time,” he continued in a quiet voice, “if you dare disturb my woman here again, I will really sell your organ.”

Ivan swallowed hard.

“And that will be your two scrotum.” Charles added.

Ivan’s face went pale. He nodded rapidly. “Okay. Okay. I understand. I swear i won't disturb her again”

Charles straightened.

“Also,” he added calmly, “as for your car.”

Ivan’s eyes widened slightly.

“Give me your account details.”

The crowd murmured again.

Ivan blinked in confusion. “My… account details?”

“Yes,” Charles replied. “You said the car cost forty million. I destroyed it. So I will pay you.”

Everyone froze.

Caroline looked at Charles in surprise.

Ivan stared at him, trying to understand whether this was another insult.

“You… you are going to pay?” Ivan asked carefully. Because, Charles just damaged it, which made it not useful for anybody. Yet he still wants to pay 40 million. That's like giving money out.

Charles nodded once. “Yes, to know your 40 million is nothing”

The street was completely silent now.

Ivan hesitated, then quickly pulled out his phone with shaking hands.

He opened his banking app and showed the account number. Ivan read the details out loud.

That was when Lizzypie came closer.
