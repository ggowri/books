Chapter 251

With that, everyone rushed out toward the storage house. It was just inside the Quill compound, near the gate, and by the time they arrived, panic was already written across every face. 

The sight that met them was chaos. The doors were smashed open, shards of wood scattered across the floor. Guards were lying groaning on the ground, bruised and battered.

The storage house had been used for plants and herbs, including the rare Red Moon Herb. Mr. Quill’s eyes narrowed as he muttered under his breath, “What the heck is happening here?” Then, without hesitation, he rushed inside.

Inside, it seemed almost… untouched. The shelves were intact, the jars still in place, all except one. 

The jar labeled Red Moon Herb—the one they had guarded so carefully—was empty. Someone had come directly for it.

Mr. Quill spun around, voice sharp, “What the hell happened here?”

One of the guards slowly lifted his head, blood trickling down his temple. “Sir… actually, Mr. Ivan came here… with a car. Since we are familiar with him, we let him in. But then some men dashed out from his vehicle… threatened us with guns… beat us up… and went straight into the storage house.”

Mr. Quill’s eyes widened. “Really? Ivan did this?”

The guards nodded.

Caroline’s face paled. “He must be crazy… how could he rob us?”

Mr. Quill’s jaw tightened. “He only took the Red Moon Herb.”

“What?!” Lizzypie exclaimed. 

“Yes,” Mr. Quill said grimly.

Charles scowled, shaking his head. “Why would he do that?”

Caroline chimed in, realization dawning. “For revenge, of course. He knew you wanted the Red Moon Herb, so he came to steal it… just to spite you.”

Charles scoffed softly. 

Mr. Quill’s anger flared visibly. “This is complete nonsense! And daring… I will go and deal with him myself. What the hell is this madness?”

Caroline hesitated. “Or… we should call Yachina. He should deal with Ivan. Can you contact him?”

All eyes turned to Charles. He slipped his hand into his pocket, the weight of decision evident in his movements. After a tense pause, he spoke, calm but firm. “No. Don’t. I will handle this myself.”

Caroline’s jaw dropped slightly. “What do you mean, you will handle it?”

“I will meet with Ivan and the Diamond Group personally,” Charles said. “I will collect the Red Moon Herb from him.”

Caroline’s voice rose in alarm. “Why? They could kill you! They are angry right now!”

Charles scoffed, unfazed. “I don’t want things to get messy. Since he took the Red Moon Herb, I’m certain he will  give it back—on his terms, of course—but he will give it back.”

Mr. Quill hesitated, running a hand over his face. “If you insist… fine. If you get the Red Moon Herb, it’s yours. Just… be careful.”

Charles nodded. “I appreciate it.”

With that, they made their way toward the Diamond Group headquarters. Caroline stayed close, guiding the way, heart pounding as the tension built with every step. 

The building loomed large, sleek, and imposing. Inside, they expected Ivan—or perhaps his father, Mr. Orlean—to be waiting.

As they approached the entrance, two massive bodyguards appeared, each armed with electric long batons, their expressions deadly serious.

“Hey! Stay back!” one of them barked, stepping forward.

“What the hell do you want?” the other growled, his eyes sharp.

Caroline faltered. “We… we just need to speak with—”

The guard cut her off, his tone icy. “You are the ones who messed with Mr. Ivan. And you still dare show your faces here? You want to die?”

Charles took a step forward, his voice calm but filled with steel. “Your boss has something of mine. I’m here to collect it.”

The bodyguards scoffed, exchanging a look of disbelief. “Who do you think you are? You think because you know Mr. Yachina, we’ll be scared? The Diamond Group has its own power and domain. We’re ready to go to war with the Yachina Gang.”

Charles’s lips curled into a faint smirk. “Well, I’m not here for war. I’m here for the Red Moon Herb.”

One of the guards lunged, trying to grab him by the shoulder, but in six seconds, Charles had both men on the ground, incapacitated. Precision and control made it look effortless. He was about to throw a strong  punch at them when a voice cut through the tension.

“You are a fucking daring one, as I was told.”

Charles raised his head and saw a man stepping forward—mid-50s, grey-streaked hair, glasses perched on his nose. Beside him was Ivan, visibly shaken.

It clicked immediately—this was Mr. Orlean.  As they walked closer, Mr. Orlean’s gaze fixed on Charles.

“You must be Charles,” he said smoothly. “Pleasure to finally meet you.”

Charles’s expression remained guarded. “Well, I’m not pleased. Give me the Red Moon Herb while I’m being nice.”

Orlean arched an eyebrow. “Or what? What are you going to do? Call the Yachina Gang?”

Charles’s eyes flicked to Ivan, who flinched slightly at the mention. “Maybe that… or maybe I make your son crippled.”

Ivan froze, unable to speak, dread washing over him.

Orlean chuckled, a low, calculating sound. “I admire your bravery. But here’s the thing—the Red Moon Herb is truly with me. You want it? You will have to earn it.”

Charles frowned. “I don’t understand. Why is this about earning it? I’m here to collect what belongs to me.”

Orlean’s lips curved in a thin smile. “Not like that. Today, you mocked my son in public, damaged his car, made him pay fifty million dollars in compensation… showing off your wealth to humiliate him. You do that to demonstrate your power.”

Charles’s brow furrowed. “Yeah, I did that. I wanted to show him his place. He has nothing. What does that have to do with the Red Moon Herb?”

Orlean’s voice rose, filled with controlled intensity. “I am here to do the same—to show you your place. To show you that you have nothing!”

Charles stared, unflinching. “And how does this, any of this justify keeping the Red Moon Herb from me?”
