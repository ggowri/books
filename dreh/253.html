Chapter 252

Mr. Orlean adjusted his glasses and said calmly, “Well, I have given the herb to an auction house. Which means the Red Moon Herb is currently on bid.”

Charles’s expression darkened. “Why would you do that?”

Orlean clasped his hands behind his back. “Simple. We will bid for it together. The highest bidder will get the Red Moon Herb.”

Charles let out a cold laugh. “You stole a herb from me, and now you want me to bid for it?”

Orlean’s smile thinned. “You destroyed my son’s car publicly and paid him back just to flaunt your wealth and disgrace him. Why are you scared now?”

Caroline stepped forward, unable to stay silent. “This is nonsense! You committed theft. That’s a crime. You should be arrested. And now you want him to waste money bidding for something he originally owned? It makes no sense.”

Lizzypie folded her arms firmly. “No. We’re not accepting that. You will give us the herb.”

Mr. Orlean’s tone dropped, calm but edged with warning. “Look… there are two ways to do this. Either we handle this peacefully through bidding… or you try to take it from me by force.” He paused, his eyes turning cold. “The second option will be very bloody.”

Charles’s gaze sharpened. “Are you threatening us right now?”

Orlean shrugged slightly. “If it seems so.”

Silence fell.

Charles stared at him without blinking.

If he chose force, he could take them down. He knew it. He could beat Orlean and his men and still retrieve the herb. But that would drag things out. It would create noise. Attention. Rumors would spread about him again in this new city . He didn’t want that.

After a long pause, Charles spoke evenly. “Okay. We will do the bidding.”

“Charles!” Lizzypie protested. “That would be a waste of money!”

Caroline shook her head. “Yeah, I can’t accept this.”

Charles didn’t look at them. “I know.” Then he turned back to Orlean. “But this is the game. For whatever amount we bid, whoever loses will still hand over the amount they bid to the winner.”

Mr. Orlean’s brows lifted slightly. “You mean… if you bid a certain amount and lose, you will still give me that money?”

“Yes,” Charles replied calmly. “And the same applies to you.”

A slow smirk spread across Orlean’s face. “Wow. You really know how to spice things up.”

Charles’s tone was flat. “So what are you saying?”

Orlean chuckled softly. “You really want to embarrass yourself? I own the biggest bank in this city. There’s no way you will outbid me. You’ll just lose your money.”

Charles met his gaze steadily. “Drop the talk. Are you in or not?”

Orlean’s smile widened. “Why not? I’m fully in.”

Mr. Orlean adjusted his cufflinks and said coolly, “Then let’s meet at the City Hall Auction in an hour.”

Charles nodded once. “Sure.”

With that, Mr. Orlean turned and walked away, Ivan trailing behind him. Ivan’s face was flushed with embarrassment.

As soon as they were out of earshot, Caroline spun around to face Charles. “Who the heck are you? Do you just make crazy deals with the biggest bank owner in the city like it’s nothing? There’s no way you can outbid him!”

Charles simply adjusted his sleeves. “Sure, I can.”

Lizzypie remained silent, her eyes fixed on Charles. Unlike Caroline, she didn’t panic immediately. She knew Charles had money—serious money. 

Mr. Orlean owned a bank, yes, but most of that capital belonged to the institution, to shareholders and corporate structures. Charles, on the other hand, had personal liquidity. A lot of it.

Still… This was not a small gamble.

She folded her arms quietly but said nothing.

Caroline exhaled sharply. “This is ridiculous. Completely ridiculous.”

Without waiting for further argument, she led them toward the City Hall Auction House. The drive there was filled with her complaints.

“This makes no sense.”

“You’re literally paying for something that was stolen from you.”

“He’s baiting you.”

….

Charles sat calmly, staring out the window, unmoved.

When they arrived at the grand auction building, the atmosphere was already buzzing. Expensive cars lined the entrance. Security was tight. The event had clearly attracted the elite of the city.

As soon as Charles stepped out of the car, whispers began spreading like wildfire.

“That’s him.”

“He’s the one bidding against Mr. Orlean.”

Another voice chimed in, louder this time. “He must be stupid. I heard they made it winner takes all—including the losing bid money!”

A small crowd began gathering near the entrance, some pretending to scroll through their phones while clearly recording.

“This is going to be insane.”

“But come on, we all know who’s winning,” someone scoffed.

.

They walked straight ahead, composed, unbothered by the murmurs. 

Inside, the auction hall was magnificent—crystal chandeliers, polished marble floors, and rows of velvet seating.

 At the front, a raised stage displayed a glass case. Inside it, illuminated under white light, was the Red Moon Herb.

Across the hall, Mr. Orlean was already seated in the front VIP section. 

When Orlean spotted Charles entering, a slow, confident smile spread across his face.

He gestured casually toward the empty VIP seats opposite him.

The challenge was clear.

Charles walked forward and took his seat directly across from Mr. Orlean.
