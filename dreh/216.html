Chapter 216

The room stayed quiet for a moment after Collins left.

Then Serena spoke.

“Everything we do from this point forward,” she said evenly, “will not interfere with my father’s investigation. It will continue until the killer is found. We will capture him.”

No one responded.

“Well,” Serena added, “this meeting is over.”

Slowly, everyone stood somberly.

This was not what they had expected when the meeting was called. The power had completely shifted.

Mr. Gerald was the first to storm out, his face tight with frustration. One by one, the others followed, leaving the room empty.

Mr. Valentino stepped closer to Serena.

“The board will be pleased to have you as CEO,” he said. “I always knew you were capable.”

Serena nodded. “Thank you. I appreciate that.”

She also thanked Jimmy for his assistance . After a brief conversation, both men left the room.

Only Serena and Charles remained.

Serena stood there for a moment, then walked toward him and wrapped her arms around him.

“I’m so grateful,” she said quietly. “I don’t even know what to say.”

She pulled back slightly. “How did you get the document?”

Charles took out his phone and handed it to her.

On the screen was a message sent the night Mr. Moretti died.

[The document is in the toilet roof. Protect Serena.]

Serena stared at it, reading it again.

“My father sent this to you?” she asked.

Charles nodded. “Yes.”

Serena swallowed. “That means… he knew he was about to be killed.”

Charles nodded again. Blaming himself “If only I had seen the message sooner, I would have suspected something.”

Serena frowned. “If he knew, why didn’t he call me? Or shout for help? He knew basic self-defense. He didn’t even fight back.”

Charles hesitated, then said, “That would only make sense if the killer was someone close to him. Someone he was emotionally attached to. Someone he didn’t want to fight.”

Serena cursed under her breath.

Because that pointed to only one person.

“Collins,” she said quietly. “That bastard really killed my father.”

Charles shook his head slightly. “Most likely. But we can’t say anything for sure yet.”

Serena’s eyes filled.

“The Moretti family was once one of the most influential families,” she said. “Collins destroyed everything because of the CEO position he was never capable of holding.”

She wiped her face and straightened.

“I will do anything,” Serena said firmly, “to have him arrested and exposed.”

Charles looked at her seriously. “You need to be careful. He threatened to kill you. I’m sure he isn’t bluffing.”

Serena scoffed. “That’s nothing. I’m waiting for him.”

Charles shook his head slightly. This was the cold Serena he had first met.

She was about to speak when Charles’s phone vibrated.

He checked the screen. It was Natasha.

He answered. “Natasha?”

Her voice came through panicked. “They’re back.”

Charles frowned. “Who?”

“The men from the other day,” she said quickly. “The Trisian gang or whatever they’re called. And the young master—Raphael—is here.”

Charles raised his brow. “I will be there right now!!!”

“Be quick,” Natasha said. “He’s also waiting for you.”

“Okay,” Charles replied. “On my way.”

He hung up.

The Trisian gang—the ones who had been insisting on buying Natasha’s store for one billion—were back.

He knew this wouldn’t be easy. Lizzy had already warned him about them.

Serena noticed his expression. “What is it?” she asked.

“I have to go somewhere,” Charles said. “Will you be okay by yourself?”

Serena didn’t want him to leave. Not because she was scared, but because she wanted him around. She didn’t say it loud though.

She smiled instead. “Of course.”

“Call me if anything happens,” Charles said.

“I will,” Serena replied.

Charles nodded, said his goodbye, and rushed out of the room.

Meanwhile, inside a moving car, Collins slammed his fist against the seat.

“Fuck!” he shouted. “I didn’t ask you to kill my father. I said cripple him. Just cripple him. Why did you go that far?”

The thug beside him, a man with tinted hair, turned sharply. “Yo, what the fuck are you saying? We didn’t even carry out the operation.”

Collins froze. “What?”

“We never met your father,” the thug said. “We were still mapping the best way to approach him. You told us to wait. What are

you talking about?”

Collins’ throat went dry.

“You… you didn’t see him at all?”

“No,” the thug replied. “Not even close. We haven't done anything yet ”

Collins swallowed hard.

So it wasn’t the gang he sent that killed his father.

His hands clenched slowly.

“I knew it,” he muttered. “I fucking knew it.”

His eyes darkened as he leaned back in the seat.

“That fucker Serena did it.”
