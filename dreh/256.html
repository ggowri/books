Chapter 258

When they arrived home, Sapphire, Natasha, and Gabriella rushed forward the moment the doors opened. Their faces were bright with anticipation.

After welcoming them properly and settling everyone down with glasses of coffee, Natasha leaned forward eagerly. “So? How did it go? Did you see the herb?”

Charles nodded once. “Yeah.”

Gabriella’s eyes lit up instantly. “Wow… really? I’m so grateful.”

Sapphire smiled and added, “That’s great. So good.”

Lizzypie crossed her legs and began narrating everything in detail. From the bidding war to Orlean’s trembling voice. From the kneeling to the twenty-nine billion transfer. She did not leave out a single humiliating second.

Natasha grinned proudly. “I know Charles. He will always come out on top.”

Sapphire laughed. “Why would he even think he could outbid the Godfather’s grandson?”

They all laughed lightly at that.

Gabriella lowered her head slightly. “I’m sorry you all went through that stress because of me.”

Charles waved it off. “It’s nothing.”

Lizzypie chimed in with a mischievous tone. “He should be the grateful one. He managed to bag another virgin lady in the process.”

“Ohhhh…”

They all chorused teasingly, nudging him with knowing smiles.

Charles simply adjusted his sleeve and switched the subject. “What’s next?”

Lizzypie straightened. “Now I will mix the herb and prepare the concoction. It should be ready by tomorrow. Once she drinks it, if it works, the effect will show within an hour.”

Charles nodded slowly. “I really hope it turns out positive.”

Gabriella’s voice trembled. “I hope so.”

They all smiled and  pulled her into a hug.

“Don’t worry,” Sapphire said softly. “It will work.”

“You will walk,” Natasha added firmly.

At that exact moment, Charles’ phone buzzed.

He glanced down.

A message from Serena.

[Something shocking just happened. Allison is here claiming something absurd. Please come right now!!!]

His expression darkened instantly.

Lizzypie noticed. “What happened?”

He slipped the phone back into his pocket. “I have to go to Serena. She needs help.”

Natasha folded her arms slightly. “She always calls you when she’s in trouble.” like she was jealous.

Sapphire gave a small knowing smile. “Like I said, her relationship with you is different from ours.”

Charles’ tone remained calm. “She just needs help. I will be back.”

Lizzypie nodded. “Okay. Call us if you need us. I will  start mixing the herb now.”

He gave a short nod in response.

Without another word, Charles turned and strode out of the house.

Charles drove like a storm cutting through the city and arrived at the Moretti Building in record time.

He stepped out and walked straight inside without waiting to be announced.

The atmosphere in the main hall was suffocating. The entire Moretti family was gathered.

Serena stood in the middle of them, surrounded.

Allison stood in front like a prosecutor delivering a verdict. The moment Charles entered, Serena saw him.

“Charles!”

She broke away from the circle and ran to him.

He caught her instantly and pulled her into a tight embrace. She buried her face in his chest and began crying.

His jaw tightened.

He slowly lifted his gaze toward the family.

“What the hell is going on here?” his voice was cold. “What did you people do to her?”

Allison folded her arms. “That bastard doesn’t belong in this family. Talk less of being the CEO.”

Mr. Gerald stepped forward. The same Gerald Serena already sent away..

“Yes,” he sneered. “She’s a bastard child. A big one. We should have known from the beginning. Especially when she started associating herself with someone as low as you.”

Charles’ eyes darkened.

Bastard?

Why are they calling her that?

He looked down at Serena. She was shaking.

Then he raised his head again. “What exactly are you all saying?”

Another family member scoffed. “You tell us. I’m sure she already told you everything. Good thing we ran our own investigation before she scammed this entire family.”

Charles’ patience snapped.

“So what the fuck is it?!” he roared.

Allison pulled out a document and waved it in the air.

“I conducted a DNA test. Between Serena and my father.”

She dropped the bomb.

“It came out zero percent.”

The hall fell into a tense silence before erupting again.

“That means she is not Father’s daughter.”

“She lied to all of us!”

“She’s a fraud!”

Charles frowned deeply. “What nonsense are you people saying? Serena isn’t Mr. Moretti’s daughter?”

Mr. Gerald nodded viciously. “Exactly. We conducted the DNA test. It came out negative.”

Voices overlapped.

“She’s a bastard!”

“How did she manage to scam us for this long?”

Charles slowly looked around at every single one of them.

“All of you sound insane.”

His voice was no longer loud. It was dangerous.

“Everyone knows Serena is Mr. Moretti’s daughter. In fact, she was his favorite. He loved her more than anyone here.”

He took a step forward.

“She is the last born. Every single one of you was present when she was born. You watched her grow up in this house.”

His eyes locked onto Allison.

“So what the hell are you saying?”

The hall went quiet after Charles’ words.

Several of them gulped.

For a brief moment, no one spoke.

Then Allison lifted her chin stubbornly as she repeated. “So? What do you say about this then? The result came out negative.”

Charles let out a cold scoff. “What do you expect me to say? What am I supposed to do with a piece of paper? Results can be fabricated.”

Allison’s eyes flared. “No. It isn’t fabricated!”

Her voice rose but it did not shake.

“I did it myself. I took Serena’s toothbrush. I took Father’s toothbrush too. I personally submitted them for the DNA analysis.”

She stepped forward. “I’m telling the truth. I swear.”

There was sincerity in her eyes. No visible hesitation.

Mr. Gerald added quickly, “We didn’t just rely on one test. We repeated it four times before accepting it.”

Another relative chimed in, “There’s an official doctor’s report as well.”

Charles’ expression hardened. “Bring it.”

A file was handed to him.

He released Serena gently and took the documents.

The room held its breath as he began flipping through the pages.

Laboratory headers, Sample IDs, Genetic markers, Probability index.

His eyes moved carefully, line by line.
