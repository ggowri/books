Chapter 261

Everyone immediately rushed into the ward.

The lead doctor stood in the center, the DNA chart trembling slightly in his hand. The second doctor leaned over his shoulder, carefully cross-checking every figure on the report one last time.

Allison pushed forward on her toes. “So what does it say?” Her lips curled. “She’s definitely a fraud, right?”

The doctor inhaled slowly.

“After careful analysis of all genetic markers and repeated verification…”

The room went still.

“The paternity probability between Subject A and Subject B is 0.1%.”

There was a total silence across the room. 

“They share no biological relationship. Mr. Moretti is not Ms. Serena’s biological father.”

The words dropped like a hammer.

Serena’s knees buckled instantly.

She stumbled, nearly hitting the floor, but Charles caught her just in time and pulled her against him.

Allison leaned forward sharply. “You’re sure. Right? You’re absolutely sure?”

The doctor nodded firmly. “One hundred percent sure.”

Charles’ eyes snapped to the second supervising doctor. “What is he saying?”

The man adjusted his glasses and answered calmly. “The result is negative. Everything was conducted properly and transparently. There was no interference. He is telling the truth”

Allison burst into loud laughter.

“I knew it! I fucking knew it!” She pointed at Serena. “You are a fraud. A bastard. You scammed this entire family!”

Mr. Gerald shook his head in exaggerated disbelief. “Wow. I’m so shocked. All this time, you weren’t even a Moretti. Yet you were flaunting the name. Taking advantage of our legacy.”

Allison’s voice grew sharper. “And you had the audacity to want to be CEO? To rule over us? Thank God I sniffed this out before you swallowed everything!”

The others joined in.

“So shameless.”

“No wonder she clung to Father.”

“She must have planned it from childhood.”

“A stray picked from the street.”

“A parasite.”

“Living off our blood.”

They were seriously mocking her in the most painful away. 

Someone even laughed and said, “Maybe her mother didn’t even know who the real father was.”

Another added, “Throw her out. She has no right to stand here.”

Serena’s cries grew louder.

Her fingers dug into Charles’ shirt as if the world was collapsing beneath her.

“I… I don’t understand…” she choked. “How …. How could this be”

Charles stood frozen for a brief moment.

Because it didn’t make sense.

Mr. Moretti had adored Serena. Favored her openly. Trusted her with the company. 

Allison stepped closer, her voice dripping with venom. “Pack your things. You don’t belong here anymore.”

Serena could barely breathe through her sobbing. Her entire identity had just been ripped apart in front of everyone.

Allison folded her arms and lifted her chin.

“Well, we don’t have to explain anything to you,” she said coldly. “You should step down as CEO immediately and announce to the world the fraud you are.”

Mr. Gerald nodded in agreement. “A bastard cannot lead the Moretti family.”

Serena stood there trembling, tears pouring down her face. She tried to speak but nothing came out. Her entire world had cracked open in a single day.

Charles finally stepped forward.

“That changes nothing,” he said firmly. “Mr. Moretti wanted her to lead the company. That was his decision. And that stands. She has no reason to step down.”

The room exploded into laughter.

Allison clapped mockingly. “Did you hear him? She’s a fraud. She deceived Father. Legally, every property under the Moretti name will be withdrawn from her.”

Serena whimpered softly. “But… if Father isn’t my biological father…”

She looked directly at Allison, desperate.

“You were there when Mom was pregnant, right?”

Allison answered stiffly, “Yes. I was.”

“Then the DNA makes no sense,” Serena continued through tears. “Which means… which means Mom must have cheated on Father… and gave birth to me…”

Her voice broke completely.

Allison’s face twisted in anger. “Don’t you dare call Mom a cheater!”

She stepped closer, eyes blazing. “Maybe Mom lost the pregnancy. Maybe she miscarried. And then they replaced the baby with you. Maybe you are some gutter child they picked up out of pity.”

The cruelty of it sliced through the room.

Serena looked like she had been physically struck.

She covered her mouth as another wave of sobbing overtook her.

Charles’ voice hardened. “We can’t jump to wild conclusions. If you want proof, then we test whether you and Serena are half-sisters. That would determine if you share the same mother.”

Allison recoiled. “God forbid! I will never associate myself with this… this nothing. This dirty, stinky person. I’m sure she came from an orphanage.”

Charles did not flinch. “Even if she did, Mr. Moretti acknowledged her as his daughter. Raised her. Loved her. That alone makes her a Moretti. This DNA result doesn’t automatically give you the right to strip her of everything.”

Mr. Gerald scoffed. “Really? And what do you think will happen when we make this public? When the world finds out she’s not a Moretti by blood? The backlash alone will force her to withdraw.”

Serena kept crying.

The position of CEO was no longer her biggest fear.

The real terror was deeper.

If Mr. Moretti was not her father… then who was?

And if the pregnancy had been false… was her mother even her mother?

Her identity felt erased.

For a moment, she wished both of them would rise from the grave and tell her the truth.

Meanwhile, Charles’ mind was racing.

No one had ever doubted Serena’s parentage. Not once. Mr. Moretti had adored her openly. Protected her fiercely. 

So why would Allison suddenly conduct a DNA test?

Why was she so confident?

There had to be more to this.

Did Mr. Moretti know?

Allison pointed toward the exit. “From here, go home and pack your luggage. You no longer belong in this family.”

At that exact moment, the door opened.

A man in a tailored suit stepped inside.

Attorney Jimmy.

Mr. Moretti’s personal lawyer.

Serena’s heart skipped. She whispered urgently, “Charles… what is he doing here?”

Charles answered calmly, “Relax. I called him.”

Allison frowned deeply. “What are you doing here?”

Mr. Gerald sneered. “You must have heard about this fraud of a child. We want her out of the family.”

Lawyer Jimmy adjusted his glasses and said evenly,

“Actually, that’s the reason I’m here.”
