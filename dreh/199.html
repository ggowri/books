Chapter 199

 

Charles stood there in surprise. He decided to call her as he dialed her number. He pressed the phone to his ear only to be met with the automated voicemail.

His brows knitted. That had never happened before. Lizzy’s phone was never switched off.

“Really?” he muttered, trying again, but it also went straight to voicemail.

He lowered the phone and walked back to the room, scanning everywhere. Just then his eyes noticed a folded white paper partially hidden under the jug on the table.

Wondering what it could be, he walked over, lifted the jug, and picked up the paper. The moment he unfolded it, he recognized the handwriting. It was obviously Lizzy’s.

[I should have told you this earlier.  

I have somewhere to be, and I’ll be gone for a long time.  

Please don’t look for me.  

I’m sorry for not telling you sooner.]

Charles frowned.

That was it. No explanation, no reason, not even where she was going.  

It didn’t make sense to him.

“She could have just texted me,” he muttered. “Why write a letter?”

He deduced that the only reason she hadn’t called him was because if she had told him, he would have stopped her.

His jaw clenched. Recently, Lizzy had been distant and always uneasy, as if she were trying to hide something.

He called her again — same response.

With that, Charles knew he had to do something. He had to look for Lizzy.

His mind went straight to Natasha. Lately, Lizzy had been much closer to her. He pulled out his phone and dialed Natasha’s number.

She picked up on the second ring. “Charles? Is everything okay?”

“No,” he said bluntly. “Lizzy’s gone. She left a note saying she’s leaving for a long time and not to look for her. Her phone’s off. Do you know anything?”

There was a pause.

“What? No… I had no idea. She came by yesterday just to say hi. She was… strange. Way more friendly than usual.”

“Exactly,” Charles muttered. “More like a goodbye. I think she’s headed somewhere dangerous. I have to find her.”

“Where could she even be?” Natasha asked, her voice softening with worry. “I will do my best to locate her.”

“Call me the second you find anything,” Charles said.

“Okay… I sure will.”

He hung up without another word and rushed back outside.

The evening air was already cool and heavy. He slid into the car, started the engine, and sped toward Mr. Noh’s place.

By the time he arrived, it was late.

He knocked hard on the door. After a long moment, one of the students opened it, squinting at him.

“I need to see Mr. Noh,” Charles demanded.

The student studied him for a second, then nodded and led him inside, maneuvering past a few curious faces in the hallway until they reached Mr. Noh’s study.

Mr. Noh looked up from his desk, surprise flickering across his face. “Huh? What brings you here so late?”

“She’s gone,” Charles said without preamble. “Lizzy left. Disappeared. Left me a note saying she will be gone a long time and not to look for her. She didn’t say where. Do you know anything? Where could she have gone?”

Mr. Noh exhaled slowly, setting his pen down. He looked genuinely troubled. “No, I don’t.”

Charles frowned, stepping closer. “You seem closer to her than almost anyone. You even call her your daughter. She would have told you something… anything.”

Mr. Noh leaned back in his chair, rubbing his temple. “Yes, she’s close to me. But she didn’t tell me a thing. Not a word about leaving.”

Charles grew more anxious. She hadn’t even told Mr. Noh where she was going.

“Okay, thanks,” he said, but before he left, he scribbled his number on a scrap of paper and handed it over. “Call me if you remember anything—anything at all.”

With that, he left.

Now, with nowhere else to turn, only one person might certainly know where she’d gone: Godfather.

According to Lizzy, she had grown up with the Godfather. She also worked for him. So  Godfather would definitely know where she was. Who knows—he might even be the one who gave her this long, dangerous mission.

Charles called Rain to set up a meeting with Mr. Vincent.

“I don’t think that’s possible,” Rain said. “He made it clear not to contact him until you finish your missions.”

“Look, this is urgent. It’s about Lizzy—she’s gone, nowhere to be found. She left, and I knew Godfather would know where she went.”

Rain insisted, “All the same, Godfather forbade me from taking any requests from you.”

Charles was furious. He didn’t care about the mission anymore—he couldn’t afford to lose Lizzy.

He barked back at Rain, “You know what? Tell him I’m not interested in the mission anymore. I will  drop it. Just set up the meeting.”

Rain sounded shocking. “What the fuck! You are going to drop it after everything you went through… just because of a woman?”

“Lizzy is not just a woman—she’s my woman. Even these missions wouldn’t have been possible without her. I can’t afford to lose her. I will do anything. So tell me—where is Godfather?”

Rain sighed and swallowed hard. “You know I can’t just let you throw everything away. Look… Godfather actually tried to make Lizzy stay after she told him she was leaving. But she insisted. And Godfather can’t force her to stay.”

Charles frowned, demanding, “She told Godfather she was leaving? Did she tell him where she was going?”

“No,” Rain replied. “She didn’t tell him anything. Just that she was leaving. But Godfather couldn’t just sit and do nothing, so we traced her. She’s currently in Ashtown… and most surprisingly, with the Hierem clan.”

That only confused Charles more. “Ashtown? Who are the Hierem clan?”

“They are like the Godfather here in New York, but in Ashtown. They dominate the area. I’ve heard how cruel they are—and they use magic also. They are  cultivators.”

Charles froze at the words. “So… what the hell is Lizzy doing there?”
