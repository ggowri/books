Chapter 287

Charles stayed calm. Completely calm. He didn't even fret at all.

He stared at Johnson like the name he just mentioned meant absolutely nothing.

Johnson had expected something else. Fear. Panic. At the very least some nervous reaction when people started whispering that he was the mayor’s son.

But Charles didn’t even blink.

Johnson frowned.

“Did you hear what they just said?” he demanded. “I’m the mayor’s son.”

Charles casually lifted a finger and dug it into his ear as if trying to clean something out.

“Yeah,” he said lazily. “I heard you.”

He lowered his hand and looked straight at Johnson.

“So what exactly am I supposed to do about it? Start shaking?”

He smirked slightly. “Come on.”

Johnson’s face turned red.

“I have given you your chance,” he said coldly. “After this, don’t you dare beg me.”

He pointed at Charles with a threatening finger.

“By the time I’m done with you, even your sluts won’t be able to save you. In fact, I might just take them for myself.”

Charles chuckled softly.

“Keep dreaming.”

Just then—

THUD.

THUD.

THUD.

Heavy footsteps began approaching from the far side of the beach.

People turned their heads.

A group of men were marching in.

Then another group and another.

They moved with discipline, boots pressing into the sand as they spread out across the area. One by one they formed a line behind Johnson.

Thirty men. They were big and built.

All wearing black. The crowd gasped.

“Bodyguards…”

“He called his bodyguards!”

“It’s over for that guy.”

Several people shook their heads in pity.

“Why didn’t he just apologize?”

“He’s finished.”

The leader of the men stepped forward confidently.

He was tall, muscular, and carried himself with authority. He walked directly to Johnson and bowed his head slightly.

“Young Master,” he said respectfully. “How can I help you?”

Johnson smirked proudly, clearly enjoying the moment.

“Yeah,” he said. “There’s someone here I want you to deal with.”

He pointed straight at Charles.

“I want you to break his leg. That bastard touched my girlfriend inappropriately.”

The leader’s eyes hardened immediately.

“Who dares do such a thing?” he said angrily. “I swear we will do more than break his leg.”

Johnson nodded eagerly.

“Him. That one right there.”

Then he added coldly,

“And those sluts with him? Beat them too. Beat them until they come to their senses and kneel before me to apologize.”

His girlfriend crossed her arms proudly.

“Yeah. Do that.”

The leader nodded once.

“It will be done.”

With that, he turned toward Charles.

His boots pressed heavily into the sand as he walked forward.

“So it’s you who dared—”

He stopped mid-sentence as his eyes landed fully on Charles.

For a second his brain didn’t process what he was seeing.

Then his eyes widened. Shock.

Pure shock.

“What the—”

His mouth fell open.

“Boss?!”

His voice came out almost like a shout.

Because the man leading the bodyguards was none other than Zander.

And the moment he recognized Charles, the blood drained from his face. Zander was completely terrified.

“Boss… Boss…”

Zander kept repeating the word like he couldn’t believe his own eyes.

The entire situation had flipped so fast it left everyone stunned.

Charles simply scoffed.

“It’s you, Zander,” he said calmly. “What are you doing here?”

Zander gulped nervously.

“Actually… this is my part-time job,” he admitted.

Charles nodded slowly as if the explanation made perfect sense.

Before anyone could react further, Zander immediately bowed deeply.

“Sorry, boss.”

The beach fell silent.

Johnson stared in disbelief.

His mind struggled to process what he was seeing. The bodyguard he had hired, the man who led thirty trained fighters, was bowing to a ragged man standing barefoot in the sand.

A ragged man.

“W-what…” Johnson muttered.

Zander straightened slightly and suddenly shouted at the men behind him.

“What the heck are you all doing standing there?!”

The bodyguards stiffened.

“Bow to boss right now!”

Since their leader was already bowing, none of them dared question it.

One after another—They knelt.

Thirty large men dropped to their knees in the sand before Charles.

The crowd erupted with shocked murmurs.

“What the fuck…”

“The bodyguards are kneeling.”

“They’re calling that ragged guy boss.”

“What is going on here?”

“Do they know each other?”

Johnson’s face twisted with confusion and anger.

“What the heck is happening here?!” he shouted.

His voice cracked through the crowd.

“How could you bow to him?! What’s going on?!”

He pointed furiously at Charles.

“You! I’m paying you people and you dare kneel to someone like him?”

His anger kept rising.

“Someone dirty like that?!”

Zander slowly stood up.

His calm expression was gone now. His face had turned cold as he looked directly at Johnson.

“Listen carefully, young master,” Zander said firmly.

He gestured toward Charles.

“This man is my boss.”

The words dropped like a stone into water.

“And whatever you are paying me,” Zander continued, “is nothing but peanut money compared to what my boss pays.”

Johnson blinked rapidly.

“Him? Him?” he stammered.

He pointed again at Charles, still wearing the old ragged shirt.

“This ragged man?”

That was the wrong thing to say.

Zander’s eyes immediately sharpened.

A dangerous aura surrounded him as he stepped closer to Johnson.

“Call him a ragged man again,” Zander said slowly, his voice dropping to a threatening whisper, “and I will twist your neck.”
