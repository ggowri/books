Chapter 249

Caroline’s eyes widened. “What? The Diamond Group?”

Nero winced through the pain. “Yeah! It’s them!”

Her mind raced.

The Diamond Bank Group had always acted close to the Quill family. Supportive. Cooperative. They had even offered to help the Quill family from The Yachina Gang trouble in exchange for a partnership. And also on one condition. 

Ivan must marry her.

That was why Mr. Quill had been pushing so hard for the marriage. 

And all this time…They were the ones creating the pressure.

Mr. Yachina slowly turned toward Ivan.

“Come here.”

Ivan didn’t move.

Yachina’s voice exploded across the room. “I said come here!”

Ivan stumbled forward, trembling.

Yachina stared down at him. “Is what he said true?”

Ivan’s lips quivered. He gave a small nod.

“Speak properly,” Yachina ordered. “Declare it.”

“Yes!” Ivan burst out. “Yes, it’s true! We ordered Nero to pressure the Quill family. We needed leverage so Caroline would marry me and our families could merge businesses!”

A heavy silence followed.

Yachina’s eyes grew colder.

“So you used the Yachina Gang as your personal tool.”

Ivan dropped to his knees. “Please forgive me. I was wrong. Just let me go. Please—”

He didn’t finish.

Smack.

Yachina’s fist drove straight into his nose.

A sharp crack echoed. Blood poured instantly as Ivan screamed and clutched his face.

“My nose! My nose!”

“Quiet,” Yachina said flatly. “It’s not over.”

Ivan whimpered, blood dripping through his fingers.

“Kneel properly,” Yachina ordered. “In front of them. And apologize. Now.”

Ivan looked at Charles in disbelief.

Just hours ago he had mocked him, confident the Yachina Gang would destroy him.

Now the boss himself was forcing him to kneel before him.

“Do it,” Yachina warned. “Or I will remove your feet.”

Ivan dropped fully to his knees, bowing despite the blood still flowing from his broken nose.

“I’m sorry,” he choked out. “I’m so sorry for what we did. Please forgive us.”

Caroline stared at him with disgust.

“You are horrible,” she said coldly. “You pretended to care about our family while you were the one strangling us from behind.”

Ivan crawled forward and grabbed Caroline’s leg.

“Please forgive me,” he cried. “Just let me leave here. I’m sorry. I’m truly sorry.”

Caroline looked down at him with cold restraint.

“Well,” she said slowly, “before I accept that apology, you will refund every single dollar the Quill family paid to the Yachina Gang because of your pressure.”

Ivan gulped hard.

Then he nodded frantically. “Yes. Yes. I will refund everything. Every cent. I swead”

Yachina’s voice cut in, calm but deadly. “Remember what you just promised. If you fail… I will personally visit you.”

Ivan’s face drained of color. “Yes. I understand.”

“Now get out,” Yachina said flatly. “Right now.”

Ivan didn’t wait for a second command. He scrambled to his feet, nearly tripping over himself as he rushed out of the building, blood still dripping from his nose.

Silence settled again.

Yachina turned to Charles and Caroline and bowed once more.

“I apologize for the inconvenience caused by my subordinates. We will also provide compensation for the distress inflicted upon the Quill family.”

He straightened slightly.

“I will draft a formal agreement immediately. From today onward, the Yachina Gang will never collect protection fees, land fees, or project dues from the Quill family. Ever.”

Caroline blinked. “Really?”

“Yes,” Yachina replied. “It will be written and sealed.”

Within minutes, his men brought forward documents. Signatures were placed. Stamps pressed.

Then another case was carried in.

A heavy black suitcase.

It was placed in front of them and opened. Stacks of cash filled it.

“One hundred million dollars,” Yachina said evenly. “As compensation.”

Caroline’s breath caught.

“In cash?” she whispered.

Yachina nodded.

The signed agreement was handed over along with the suitcase.

Caroline felt overwhelmed. “I’m… I’m grateful, Lord Yachina. Thank you. I am speechless right now”

“If you require anything else,” he replied respectfully, “contact us.”

With that, Charles, Caroline, and Lizzypie made their way out.

When Caroline and Lizzypie reached the car, Charles paused.

“I need a moment,” he said quietly. “Wait for me.”

They nodded. Charles turned and walked back inside to meet Yachina. 

Yachina was still there.

The moment he saw Charles return, he bowed again.

Charles studied him carefully.

“For you to act like this,” Charles said calmly, “you must know who I am.”

Yachina lifted his head slightly.

“Yes,” he replied. “I know.”

A brief pause then he added. 

“You are the Godfather’s grandson.”

Charles held Yachina’s gaze.

“How did you know that?” he asked quietly.

Yachina answered without hesitation. “I am close to the Godfather. Everything I have today… my empire, my influence… it was built with his guidance. He supported me from the beginning. Even now, he still backs me. He is my superior.”

Charles listened carefully.

“I met you once,” Yachina continued. “The very first day you met him. I was there, standing behind him.”

“Oh,” Charles murmured.

Then his eyes sharpened. “You know about my mission too.”

“Yes, my lord,” Yachina replied respectfully. “That is why I did not expose you.”

He paused, then added, “You are as brave as your father.”

Charles stiffened slightly. “You knew my father as well?”

Yachina gave a small nod. “Everyone in our circle knew him. He was respected.”

Silence lingered for a moment.

“Can you tell me anything?” Charles asked. “About him. About how he died.”

Yachina’s expression became unreadable.

“You will know soon,” he said calmly. “For now, continue your missions. The truth reveals itself step by step.”

Charles understood. Pressing further would lead nowhere. Everyone he had met has refused to tell him anything about his father. 

He inclined his head. “Thank you.”

Yachina bowed once more.

Charles turned and left.

Outside, Caroline was waiting beside the car. The suitcase rested safely in the trunk.

She looked at him curiously. “How do you know him? He respects you too much. Why?”

Charles smiled lightly.

“He owed me,” he said smoothly. “I once saved his life. He’s just returning the favor.”

Caroline studied him for a second, then nodded. It sounded believable enough.

“Well,” she said with relief, “at least we got the deal. And Ivan’s exposed. I’m so happy.”
