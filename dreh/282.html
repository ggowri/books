Chapter 283

Charles entered the room and closed the door behind him.

Before he could take another step, the ladies rushed toward him.

“Yay! You’re back!”

Charles smiled and nodded. “Yeah. I’m back.”

Lizzypie tilted her head, eyes shining. “So soon? You already opened your Yin Yang?”

He nodded again. “Yeah.”

“That fast?” Natasha asked, genuinely surprised.

Sapphire stepped closer, studying him with admiration. “You are truly a strong man.”

Their excitement filled the room. The heavy tension from outside disappeared from his mind. Here, everything felt lighter.

Natasha insisted on cooking for him, moving quickly to the kitchen area while the others surrounded him with questions. They talked over each other, laughing, teasing, asking how it felt, what changed, whether he noticed anything different.

Charles answered calmly, enjoying the energy around him.

To celebrate and enjoy the day, they decided to go to the beach.

“Let’s not waste this kind of achievement indoors,” Sapphire said.

They all agreed immediately.

As they prepared to head out, Lizzypie suggested they call Gabriella.

They called her, and she arrived shortly after.

The moment Gabriella walked in and saw Charles, her face lit up just like the others.

“You did it?” she asked eagerly.

Charles nodded. “I attained it.”

She gave a playful whine. “Wow. So now you can have sex without issue?”

The others burst into laughter, brushing off the comment with playful teasing.

They gathered their things and made their way toward the beach.

They arrived at the beach shortly after.

The afternoon sun stretched wide across the sky, casting gold over the endless blue water. Waves rolled in gently, foaming at the edges before retreating again. Music drifted from a nearby beach bar. Laughter mixed with the cries of seagulls. The air smelled of salt, sunscreen and grilled seafood.

Each of the ladies had already planned her bikini.

They carried light bags and disappeared briefly into the changing rooms near the shore. Charles remained by the sand, slipping off his shoes and exhaling slowly as the ocean breeze brushed against his face.

He looked around.

Couples walked hand in hand. Friends splashed in the water. A few influencers posed dramatically near the shoreline, chasing the perfect angle.

Charles sighed.

A memory flashed through his mind.

How many times had he asked Tracy to come to the beach with him? How many times had she rejected the idea?

“It’s too public,” she would say.

“Too common.”

Or worse, “I don’t want people seeing us like that.”

She never wanted to be seen casually with him. Never wanted to look ordinary beside him.

Now he stood here. And he wasn’t alone. In fact, He was with four baddies.

The ladies stepped out one after the other.

Charles gulped.

Lizzypie wore a sleek black bikini that contrasted beautifully against her glowing skin. Natasha chose a bright red two-piece that made her look bold and fiery. Sapphire’s ocean-blue bikini almost blended with the waves behind her. Gabriella wore white, simple but elegant, highlighting every curve effortlessly.

They all had perfect bodies. Smooth skin. Confident posture. Natural glow under the sunlight.

For a moment, Charles forgot to breathe.

People around them began to stare openly.

“What the fuck… who are these goddesses?”

“I’m sure they are big women. Look at their skin.”

“How I wish I could bag one of them.”

Whispers moved like wind through the crowd.

Some men removed their sunglasses just to look properly. Some women stared too, half impressed, half threatened.

The ladies simply smiled it off.

They walked with grace, unbothered, their confidence louder than the noise around them.

Charles felt something shift inside him.

After settling at a good spot close enough to the water but far from the heavy crowd, Charles suddenly remembered something.

“We need drinks,” he said.

“We can all go,” Natasha suggested.

He shook his head immediately. “You’re already dressed in bikinis. Just relax. I’ll be quick.”

They exchanged smiles.

“Okay,” Gabriella said. “We will stay right here.”

Charles nodded and headed toward the small beach bar a short distance away.

As he walked, he felt lighter than he had in weeks.

But a few steps from the bar, his pace slowed.

Near a rocky edge not far from the water stood a man.

He was shaking violently, like someone who had just been pulled out of the sea. His clothes were torn and clung awkwardly to his thin frame. His hair was grey and messy. His lips trembled from the cold.

He looked old. Fragile. Almost like a beggar.

Charles frowned.

At first, he thought of ignoring it.

This was a beach. Strange people were common.

But the man kept shaking.

His shoulders trembled uncontrollably.

Charles felt pity rise in his chest.

He hesitated for only a second before walking closer.

“Sir,” he said gently, studying the man’s condition. “Are you okay?”.

The old man lifted his head slowly.

“I am fine,” he said, though his teeth were chattering.

Charles frowned. “You don’t look fine. What happened?”

The man hesitated at first, as if debating whether to speak. His hands kept trembling. His lips were pale.

“I… I have a condition,” he said finally. “It’s called Raynaud’s Phenomenon. Whenever I am exposed to cold water or strong wind, my body reacts badly. I get extremely cold. I start shaking like this. Someone pushed me inside the ocean”

Charles glanced at the ocean behind them, then back at the man’s soaked and torn clothes.

“And someone pushed you into the water?” Charles asked, disbelief clear in his tone.

The old man nodded weakly. “I only asked him for money. Just a small change. I had nothing to eat since yesterday. He got angry. Said I was disturbing him.” His voice cracked. “Then he pushed me.”

Charles’ jaw tightened.

How could someone be that cruel?

The beach was full of laughter and music just a few meters away, yet here stood a helpless old man shaking like he might collapse at any second.

“You just begged?” Charles asked again, making sure he heard right.

“Yes,” the man whispered. “I didn’t touch him. I didn’t insult him. I just asked. No body want to employ me because I'm old”

Pity swelled in Charles’ chest.

He looked around.

Charles sighed deeply.

“Come,” he said.

He guided the man away from the direct wind and closer to a small wall that blocked some of the breeze. The man’s body continued to tremble.

Without overthinking it, Charles began unbuttoning his thick shirt.

“Sir, what are you doing?” the old man asked weakly.

“You need this more than I do.”

Charles removed his shirt completely and handed it to him. He then took off the inner layer he had on and helped the man put it over his shoulders. The fabric was dry and warm from his body heat.

The old man looked stunned.

“No, no, I can’t take this.”

“You can,” Charles said firmly.

He then gently pulled off the old man’s soaked, torn top and replaced it with his own dry one. The difference was immediate. The man clutched the thick shirt tightly around himself as if afraid someone would take it away.

Charles was now left wearing the old, damp rag the man had on.
