Chapter 240

Charles turned to Lizzypie, eyes still fixed on the scene. “Wow… this is beautiful. What could be happening to make something this grand?”

Almost as if they heard him—

Puff puff. 

A loud puff burst into the air.

Tubes hidden around the convoy exploded softly, scattering colorful ribbons and glittering streamers that floated down like rain over the roses.

Then—

Boom! Boom!! Boom!!!

A firework shot into the sky.

It exploded in brilliant gold light, forming glowing letters above them:

Happy Birthday Caroline !!!

Gasps spread through the crowd.

More fireworks followed, bursting one after another in red, silver, and violet. The display continued for nearly five full minutes, lighting up the Texas sky in dazzling patterns.

People around them started cheering.

“Wow!”

“So beautiful!”

“I love this!”

“She’s so lucky!”

The lady—Caroline —covered her mouth in shock, her eyes shimmering as the colors reflected against her white dress.

Then one of the car doors opened slowly.

A man stepped out.

He wore a perfectly tailored grey suit that fit him like it had been stitched onto his body. The fabric was smooth and expensive, the cut sharp and clean. 

His white shirt underneath was crisp, collar firm, paired with a slim dark tie that rested flawlessly against his chest.

 Polished black shoes reflected the light from the fireworks. Even his hair was styled with precision, swept back neatly, not a strand out of place.

He moved with calm confidence, each step deliberate.

He adjusted the cuff of his sleeve lightly before walking toward Caroline  across the carpet of roses.

Everyone suddenly started screaming.

“Wow! That’s Ivan!”

“The heir of Diamond Bank!”

“Ms. Caroline  is so lucky!”

The murmurs spread like wildfire through the crowd.

Ivan stopped a few steps in front of her.

With slow confidence, he lowered himself onto one knee right there on the thick carpet of roses. The fireworks were still fading in the sky as he reached into his suit pocket and pulled out a small velvet box.

He opened it.

Inside sat a dazzling diamond ring that caught every remaining spark of light in the air. It shimmered intensely, the stone large, perfectly cut, mounted on a sleek platinum band.

Gasps erupted.

Ivan lifted his chin slightly and spoke clearly, his voice smooth and controlled.

“This is a three-million-dollar ring. First edition.”

The crowd went wild again.

“I want to use it to propose… and to wish you a happy birthday at the same time.” He held the ring up slightly. “Caroline … will you date me?”

The crowd exploded.

“Mmm!”

“Wow!”

“Say yes! Say yes! Say yes!”

Even Charles and Lizzypie found themselves smiling, curious, waiting for her answer.

Caroline , however, looked completely stunned.

Her lips parted slightly as she stared at him, then at the ring, then at the sea of roses beneath her feet.

“Ivan…” she murmured softly, almost overwhelmed. “What are you doing? What is all this?”. 

Ivan smiled, still on one knee. “To propose to you, of course.”

He snapped his fingers lightly.

Right on cue, a sleek black Mercedes-Benz S-Class rolled forward from the convoy and stopped just beside the roses. The engine purred softly before going silent.

A driver stepped out, walked around the car, and presented a key with both hands.

“This is also for you, Miss Caroline .”

The crowd gasped even louder.

“This is getting more perfect!”

“Oh my God, I want this in my life!”

“Who does this anymore?”

Murmurs spread everywhere. Phones were up. Cameras flashing. Fireworks smoke still hanging faintly in the air.

Ivan’s smile widened with quiet confidence.

Caroline  stared at the car, then at the ring, then back at him.

“Ivan,” she said again, her voice lower this time. “What is all this? What are you doing?”

“Proposing to you, of course,” he replied smoothly.

She leaned slightly closer and whispered, her tone tense. “Look… I don’t want to embarrass you. Just pack this up and leave.”

Ivan blinked, still smiling but more stiffly. He lowered his voice. “What are you saying, Caroline ? This is public. I just gave you a car and a three-million-dollar ring. Isn’t that enough?”

Caroline ’s expression hardened.

“Ivan, whatever you are doing, I don’t care. I’ve told you countless times—I don’t like you. I can’t date you. Yet you still do all this?”

His jaw tightened. “I spent fifty million dollars overall for this. Please. Just accept me.”

She shook her head slowly. “That’s your problem. You think money can buy love. It can’t. I don’t love you. There’s nothing you can do about it. Fifty million can’t buy it.”

The crowd’s excitement began to shift into confusion.

“Wait… what’s happening?”

“Is Ms. Caroline  rejecting him?”

“She better not. After all this?”

“No man can pull this off. Mr. Ivan is a real man.”

Cameras zoomed in from every angle, capturing every second.

Ivan forced a smile, though the tension in his eyes betrayed him. “Can’t you just say yes? We can talk about it later. Everyone is watching.”

Caroline  lifted her chin.

“You caused this yourself,” she said firmly. “I made it clear I would never date you. Don’t use the public to pressure me. This is on you.”

Ivan’s smile finally cracked.

“What else do you want me to do?” he demanded, still kneeling. “I love you!”

Caroline ’s voice stayed firm. “But I don’t. You’re arrogant. You think money can buy love.”

A murmur rippled through the crowd.

She didn’t stop.

“Just last month, I caught you with a prostitute.” Her eyes locked on his. “I know who you are. And I can’t be pressured. Just stand up… and go.”

The atmosphere shifted completely.

Ivan’s jaw flexed. “At least collect the gifts.”

“Cameras are everywhere,” Caroline  replied sharply. “If I collect them and later say we are not dating, it will be on me. I don’t care.”

His voice dropped, colder now. “Don’t do this to me. Or else.. ”

“Or else what?” Caroline  shot back immediately. “This is exactly the problem with you. So aggressive. So entitled.”

People were whispering openly now.

“Is she really rejecting him?”

“After all this?”

“This is bad…”

Caroline  turned toward the crowd, lifting her voice so everyone could hear.

“No. I won’t date you. I don’t accept your proposal.”

A loud collective gasp swept through the street.

Charles folded his arms slowly, watching the drama unfold.

“Wow,” he muttered under his breath. “Interesting.”
