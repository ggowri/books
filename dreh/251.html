Chapter 250

.

They returned to the Quill Company headquarters just before evening.

The entire family was already gathered in the main conference room, tension thick in the air. Mr. Quill stood at the head of the table, arms folded. Mrs. Quill sat beside him, while Vivian paced near the window.

The moment Caroline and Charles walked in, all eyes fell on the briefcase and the thick file in Caroline’s hand.

Mr. Quill frowned. “What is that?”

Caroline walked forward calmly and placed the file on the table.

“Well,” she said, “we managed to get the deal you wanted. The Yachina Gang will never disturb the Quill family again. I mean never.”

Silence.

Mr. Quill blinked. “What do you mean never? As in… never ever again?”

“Yes,” Caroline said firmly. “Never again.”

“How is that even possible?” Mrs. Quill asked in disbelief.

Caroline took a breath. “That’s not all. Charles was able to make the Yachina Gang refund us one hundred million dollars for the unfair money they’ve taken from us.”

“What the hell?!” Mr. Quill exploded.

Mrs. Quill stared. “Are you sure you’re telling the truth?”

Vivian scoffed loudly. “This is ridiculous. Either she’s lying or the Yachina Gang is deceiving her. There’s no way they would do that.”

Caroline calmly opened the briefcase.

Stacks of cash filled it.

The room went silent.

“In fact,” Caroline continued, “Lord Yachina himself agreed to all of this. We signed a formal agreement.”

She handed the document to her father.

Mr. Quill adjusted his glasses and began reading. His expression slowly shifted from skepticism to shock. He reread sections twice, checking the seal and signature carefully.

“This…” he muttered. “This is real.”

Mrs. Quill leaned closer. “Let me see.”

Caroline added, “There’s something else you need to know. The Diamond Bank Group was behind the pressure from the Yachina Gang.”

“What?!” several voices shouted at once.

Murmurs filled the room.

Caroline explained everything — how Ivan and the Diamond Group paid Nero to pressure them monthly, how they created financial strain so the Quill family would feel forced into a partnership, and how Ivan intended to secure that partnership through marriage.

The room erupted in disbelief.

Vivian slammed her hand on the table. “This is nonsense! She’s saying this because she doesn’t want to marry Ivan!”

Caroline calmly pulled out her phone.

“I recorded his confession.”

She pressed play.

Ivan’s trembling voice filled the room, admitting everything.

When it ended, silence returned — heavier this time.

Mr. Quill slowly lowered himself into his chair. “I can’t believe this…”

Mrs. Quill looked shaken. “They were manipulating us the entire time.”

Mr. Quill then looked at Charles. “How did you manage this? How did you get Lord Yachina on your side?”

Caroline answered before Charles could. “It turns out Charles knows him. He once saved Yachina’s life. Yachina felt he owed him.”

Murmurs spread again.

“Ohh… that explains his confidence.”

“So that’s why he wasn’t afraid.”

Mr. Quill studied Charles differently now.

“I can’t believe Ivan and the Diamond Group would go this far,” he said quietly.

Caroline crossed her arms. “Well… if not for Charles here. You would have pushed me to the Diamond family trap.”

Caroline folded her arms and looked directly at her father.

“Don’t you think you owe him an apology?” she said, pointing at Charles.

The room grew quiet.

Mr. Quill looked overwhelmed. Everything had happened too fast. The money. The confession. The agreement. It felt unreal. 

“I can't believe this, i sense you want to scare me!”. 

He claimed, at that moment, his phone rang. 

He frowned at the unknown number but answered anyway.

“Hello?”

The moment he heard the voice on the other end, his eyes widened.

It was Lord Yachina.

Mr. Quill straightened instantly. “L–Lord Yachina?”

The entire room froze.

“Yes,” Yachina’s calm voice echoed faintly through the speaker. “I am calling to personally apologize for the misconduct of my subordinates toward the Quill family. It was unacceptable.”

Mr. Quill’s mouth went dry. “You… you’re apologizing?”

“I am,” Yachina replied. “And I assure you it will never happen again. Perhaps I will visit sometime for a drink, to formally express my regret.”

Mr. Quill looked like he had just seen a ghost. “Of course. Of course, you are welcome anytime.”

They exchanged brief formalities before ending the call.

The moment he lowered the phone, he burst into stunned laughter.

“Wow… this is impressive. This is unbelievable.”

Now it was real. Yachina himself had called.

Mr. Quill turned toward Charles with new eyes.

“In fact,” he said excitedly, “Caroline… you can marry him if you want. You’re free to marry him! He’s more useful than I imagined. He made Yachina call me personally and even offer to visit. I sense there is more to him.”

Caroline scoffed under her breath. That was not happening.

Charles gave a faint smile and stepped forward.

“Well,” he said calmly, “I’ve done my part. I won the bet.”

Mr. Quill blinked. “The Red Moon Herb… right?”

Vivian quickly interrupted. “Father, don’t tell me you’re actually going to give him the herb.”

Robert, Caroline’s older brother who had remained mostly neutral, spoke up. “The Red Moon Herb is one of a kind. It’s extremely rare. We can’t just hand it over.”

Caroline looked at them in disbelief. “Why are you all like this? We made a bet. He fulfilled his part. Now it’s your turn.”

Mr. Quill rubbed his chin thoughtfully.

“He secured one hundred million dollars for us. And if Lord Yachina visits and we take a photo together, post it publicly…” His eyes lit up. “Investors will flood in. Our status will skyrocket.”

Caroline nodded firmly. “Exactly.”

Mr. Quill exhaled slowly.

“Alright,” he said at last. “We will honor the agreement. You shall have the Red Moon Herb. It’s stored in the storage house.”

Relief flickered across Charles’s face, subtle but real.

Just then, the conference room door burst open.

One of the security guards rushed in, breathless.

“Sir!” he shouted. “The storage house has been attacked!”

The room froze.

“What?” Mr. Quill barked.

“They broke through the outer gate. Our men are trying to hold them off!”
