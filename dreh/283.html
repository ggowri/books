Chapter 284

Charles stood there a moment longer, watching the old man clutch the clothes like they were something priceless.

The way his trembling fingers pressed the fabric against his chest made it clear this wasn’t just about warmth. It was a relief.

The man’s eyes glistened as he whispered a broken thank you, and Charles felt a tight weight settle in his chest because he understood that look too well.

“It’s nothing,” Charles said quietly

He adjusted the damp rag now hanging loosely on his own body. It smelled of salt and old sweat, and the rough fabric scratched lightly against his skin, but he ignored the discomfort.

He picked up the drinks he had bought and told the old man he would be back soon. He didn’t want to judge him.

It would be easy for someone else to stand there and ask why he was begging or what he had wasted his youth doing, but life was not that simple.

Everyone had a story. Some people were born into opportunity and stability while others were born into survival. As an orphan, Charles understood that better than most. The privilege to grow into something “respectable” was not evenly distributed. Some people had to fight for basics. Some lost the fight.

His plan was simple. Deliver the drinks to the ladies, reassure them, then return and help the old man properly.

He had barely taken several steps when someone crashed into him from the side. The impact knocked the drinks clean out of his hands and they flew forward, spilling across the sand in a splash of melting ice and colored liquid.

The woman who collided with him lost her balance at the same time and tipped forward dangerously. Without thinking, Charles dropped any thought of saving the drinks and reached for her instead, catching her firmly around the waist and supporting her weight before she could fall.

“Are you okay?” he asked immediately, steadying her.

Instead of relief, her face hardened. She jerked herself out of his hold like his touch burned.

“What the fuck are you doing?” she snapped loudly.

Charles blinked in confusion. “What?”

“Why are you touching my ass?”

The accusation stunned him. “Your ass? No. You’re mistaken. You bumped into me.”

Her eyes scanned him from head to toe and paused at the ragged shirt hanging off his shoulders. Her expression changed instantly, disgust replacing surprise.

“Oh my God,” she scoffed. “You filthy old rag man. How dare you touch me with that dirty hand?”

The shift was obvious. It wasn’t about what happened. It was about how he looked. In her mind, a man dressed like this should never touch him.

“Well,” Charles replied evenly, refusing to match her aggression, “I didn’t touch you like that. You ran into me and made me lose my drinks.”

She let out a sharp laugh. “Please. You planned this. How convenient that the drinks fall and your hands land on my body. You think I’m stupid?”

Charles studied her for a second and realized there was no point arguing with someone who had already written her own version of the story. He wasn’t going to beg for understanding.

“Believe whatever you want,” he said calmly and turned to walk away.

“Hey,” she barked behind him, anger rising in her voice. “Why are you going?”

“To get another drink, of course,” Charles replied without even turning back. “The ones you knocked out of my hand.”

Her eyes widened in dramatic disbelief. “Ohhh, so now you want to pin it on me? You’re not just a sicko, you are shameless too. You used the opportunity to grab my ass and now you are pretending you’re the victim?”

Charles didn’t answer her again. He simply adjusted the rough fabric on his shoulders and started walking. The smell of the rag probably offended her more than his silence did.

But she wasn’t done.

She stepped in front of him again, blocking his path with deliberate hostility. “You can’t just walk away.”

Charles exhaled slowly, patience thinning. “What the heck do you want?”

“I want you to admit what you did,” she said loudly, making sure nearby people could hear her. “And bow down and apologize.”

That made Charles laugh.

It burst out of him before he could stop it.

“In your dreams,” he said, shaking his head. “There is no way I’m apologizing for something I didn’t do.”

Her face darkened instantly. “You keep denying it. You keep lying.”

Around them, people had started to gather. Beachgoers slowed their steps. A few men folded their arms, watching. Some women whispered behind their sunglasses.

“That old man grabbed her.”

“I saw him holding her.”

“This is why they shouldn’t allow cows and goats on this beach.”

Charles heard it. They didn't know the root and already judge

He didn’t react.

He just looked at her, calm but firm. “If you like, keep crying like a child. I won’t admit to something that never happened.”

That did it.

Her expression twisted with rage. “Me? You’re calling me a child?”

She raised her hand to slap him.

The movement was quick, dramatic.

But Charles was quicker.

He caught her wrist midair.

Her hand felt small and fragile in his grip. He didn’t squeeze hard. He didn’t need to. He simply held it there, stopping the slap like it was nothing.

For a split second, her confidence cracked.

“Let go of me!” she screamed.

Charles released her wrist and pushed her hand away.

The force wasn’t violent, but it was enough to throw her balance off. She stumbled backward, heels digging into the sand, arms flailing as her body tipped.

She was about to hit the ground when a man rushed in and caught her from behind, steadying her before she fell.

She clung to him instantly.

And then the tears came.

“Hubby!” she cried out dramatically, pointing at Charles with a shaking finger. “This bastard twisted my wrist. He grabbed my ass, refused to admit it, and then tried to run away.”
