The thought sent a cold chill down her spine. Her gaze darted nervously to Marcus, hoping against hope he wouldn't target her.

But as time passed, she realized how misplaced her fears were.

Marcus didn't even glance in her direction. It was as though she didn't exist in his world.

His indifference hit harder than any confrontation ever could, leaving her questioning her significance for the first time in years.

Was she worth nothing to him?

One of Julian's aides promptly stepped forward, efficiently formalizing Marcus' appointment as Special Military Advisor and granting him the rank of Major Commander.

The speed and precision with which Julian's team worked were astonishing.

Tasks that usually involved layers of bureaucracy were handled with remarkable ease.

This left Marcus with little doubt—Julian had come prepared.

It was obvious the old Commander had set his sights on recruiting Marcus well before arriving today.

And just as Marcus suspected, Julian had indeed planned every detail meticulously to bring him on board.

That was where all the preparation came from.

"By the way, Commander Ashford, when will Benjamin Mitchell be released?" Marcus asked, his voice calm but edged with concern.

This was the issue that weighed on his mind the most.

As long as Benjamin was locked up, Grace would continue to worry herself sick.

Julian chuckled, a glint of reassurance in his eyes. "Don't worry, Marcus. As long as I'm around, everything's in good hands.

"You see, since Benjamin's a city official, there are a few formalities to take care of when it comes to his release. It'll take no more than three days. I'll personally arrange a plane to get him back to Solomia and reinstate him as mayor. How does that sound?"

Julian smiled, his tone full of confidence.

Marcus still looked uneasy, his brow furrowing slightly. "You really think it'll be done in three days?"

Julian gave a hearty laugh. "I can guarantee it. I might be getting up there in years, but I'm still good for this. Trust me, Marcus.

"And don't worry about Benjamin. For the next three days, he won't be suffering. He'll have the same conditions as on the outside—just no freedom for a while."

Hearing Julian's assurance, Marcus finally relaxed.

Grace nodded in agreement, feeling a weight lift off her shoulders.

As long as her father was safe, a few extra days didn't matter.

The fact that Benjamin would regain his position was an unexpected but welcome surprise for Grace.

With the promise made, Marcus and Grace exited the OIG's building.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, I can't thank you enough! Without you, my dad would never have been out this quickly. He might have spent his whole life behind bars!"

The moment they stepped outside, Grace's face lit up, her voice filled with genuine admiration.

Her eyes sparkled with gratitude, unable to hide her awe.

Before this, they had been facing off against Julian Ashford's son, Pablo.

Despite Marcus' assurances, Grace had remained skeptical.

But now, seeing the way Julian Ashford treated Marcus, Grace realized how wrong she'd been.

One phone call from Marcus, and Julian had removed his own son from his position.

Then, he appointed Marcus as a Special Military Advisor for Uswad and even granted him the rank of Major Commander.

It all felt like something out of a dream.

She couldn't tell if she was awake or not.

"You're my woman. Helping you get your father back was the least I could do. No need to be so formal with me anymore," Marcus said with a teasing smile, his eyes tracing the soft, innocent lines of Grace's face.

More than that, he had noticed her progress—she had advanced to the Ancient Martial Stage Two.

In just over a month, she had made a huge leap from the first level to the second.

Marcus hadn't helped her directly at all during this time.

It was all her own hard work.

While her progress wasn't on his level, it was still impressive enough to put most in the Ancient Martial Art world to shame.

Grace's cheeks flushed with embarrassment, a shy smile tugging at the corner of her lips. "Mr. Fitzgerald, do you really see me as your woman?"
