"Yvonne, please don't scare me! Yvonne, you have to hang on, you can't give up now!"

Charles gripped Yvonne's hand tightly, his face twisted in a mixture of rage and helplessness.

Emily stood next to him, her eyes swollen from crying.

"This curse is too evil... If Marcus doesn't return soon, she's really going to die," Diana said, her voice tinged with despair.

Emily's face went pale at the words, nearly collapsing.

Charles looked as though he might explode with frustration.

Just as Yvonne seemed on the verge of losing her life to the blood curse, the doorbell rang.

Charles and Emily rushed to the door, opening it together.

There, standing in a blood-streaked shirt, was Marcus.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, you're back! Yvonne's going to be okay, this is incredible!"

Charles' face lit up with an ecstatic joy he couldn't hide.

Marcus walked briskly to Yvonne's side.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, did you succeed?" Diana asked with an eager, almost anxious look.

Marcus nodded.

Without wasting any time, he began circulating his energy, carefully feeding the blood energy he had taken from Eden Child into Yvonne's body.

As the energy flowed in, Yvonne's complexion began to improve, her breath becoming more regular.

Her vital signs surged back to life, slowly but surely.

As the blood energy poured into her, the curse that had wrapped itself around her began to crack and crumble.

Finally, with a silent shudder, the curse collapsed entirely and vanished.

"Phew..." Marcus let out a deep breath, his tense muscles relaxing for the first time.

Though Yvonne hadn't regained consciousness yet, with the curse gone, Marcus knew she was out of danger.

The reason she hadn't woken was simply because the curse had drained so much of her life force.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, you've saved her! She's going to be okay! I can't thank you enough!"

Charles' face beamed with gratitude and joy as he looked at his daughter's recovering form.

He immediately bowed deeply, but Marcus stopped him, holding up a hand. "No need for that, Mr. Miller. This was my responsibility. I couldn't just sit back and watch Yvonne suffer, especially since we're good friends," Marcus said with a faint smile.

Charles nodded, and Emily, eyes filled with emotion, looked at Marcus. "How long until Yvonne wakes up?" she asked, her voice full of hope.

"She'll be awake within an hour. After a couple of days of rest, she'll be as good as new," Marcus replied, his tone reassuring.

Diana pulled Marcus aside, her eyes sharp with curiosity. "What level was the Curse Weaver you killed?"

Marcus paused, then answered with a casual tone, "A third-stage Curse Weaver."

He wasn't lying—he simply preferred to keep his real abilities under wraps, not wanting to show off too much.

"I bet it was Mr. Roger Gellar who sent him," Diana said, a glint of suspicion in her eyes.

"You're right," Marcus answered, his expression darkening.

After today's events, it was clear that his second brother's intentions had become far more dangerous.

The Curse Weaver had been one of the strongest enemies Marcus had ever faced, even stronger than Asher Dupont, the Grand Elder of the Astral Serpent Order.

If not for his variety of life-saving techniques, Marcus wasn't sure he would have made it through Eden Child's attack.

It made him realize just how critical his own power was.

Even though his growth had been rapid, his second brother's hired assassins were catching up fast.

It was clear that Marcus would need to accelerate his own training if he hoped to stay ahead.

An hour later, Yvonne stirred, her eyelids fluttering open.

The first thing she saw was Marcus' familiar face, looking as handsome as ever.

"Marcus... What happened? I had the longest nightmare... There was this bloodthirsty demon chasing me... I thought I was going to die..."

Yvonne's voice was faint, barely more than a whisper.

"Yvonne, it's okay now. Mr. Fitzgerald saved you," Charles said, his voice full of relief and gratitude.

Diana briefly explained the situation to Yvonne, though she left out the details about the blood curse.

Yvonne, being an ordinary person, wouldn't be able to understand the concept of a blood curse. It was better for her mental state if she never found out.

"Marcus, thank you... Thank you for saving me," Yvonne said, her eyes shimmering with gratitude.

Marcus looked uncomfortable, quickly shaking his head. "There's no need for thanks, Yvonne. This was partly my fault."

He took out a healing pill from his Spatial Ring and helped Yvonne swallow it.

Almost immediately, Yvonne's strength began to return, the pill doing its job.

In moments, she was nearly back to normal, though still a bit weaker than usual.

"Just a couple more days, and you'll be back on your feet," Marcus assured her.

Once he was sure Yvonne was stable, Marcus took a deep breath, his mission complete. He made his way out of the Miller house, heading back to Bayside Villa.

After the battle, Marcus had sustained a few injuries, and he knew he needed time to heal.

So, he spent the night refining the energy he had absorbed, converting it into his own power, and preparing for the next challenge.
