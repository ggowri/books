Chapter 126 The Notorious Troublemaker

Julius scoffed, "A pharmaceutical company worth ten billion dollars can't hold a candle to my uncle's business."

His gaze shifted back to Josie, and he added coldly, "Even if you're the president of BioFusion Pharmaceuticals, you won't be able to stop me from taking down this loser!"

With that, Julius dialed Stephen's number.

Sync Group had recently been acquired by Death, and their internal operations were in need of a major overhaul.

Stephen had been swamped with work lately.

He never had high hopes for Julius and had only been providing him with material support.

When he saw it was Julius calling, he barely caught what he said over the noise and simply directed him to contact his secretary instead.

Julius didn't care. To him, Stephen's secretary was just as good as Stephen himself, wielding considerable authority.

Either one would easily handle Marcus.

"Ms. Shelman, it might be best to leave now," the service assistant cautioned Josie in a hushed tone. "Julius is a notorious troublemaker. He's likely to bring in some backup soon."

Julius's notoriety was well-known throughout Kingston; everyone had heard of him.

Before Josie could respond, Marcus interjected, "Are you Stephen's nephew?"

Julius's expression shifted to one of arrogance. "Since you know my uncle, you still dare to act tough? If you cut off the foot you used to kick me, I might let you go. Otherwise, you're in for a rough time!"

Abigail smirked, "A loser like you thinks you can talk back to Mr. Julius Andrade just because you're with Josie?

"Her company is nothing compared to Mr. Andrade's uncle's empire. You're in big trouble!"

At that moment, Abigail felt a surge of satisfaction and pride.

Ever since getting involved with Felix, she'd felt overshadowed by Marcus. Even Felix was likely to be killed by him.

Now, with a wealthy guy like Julius on her side, she finally felt empowered.

Marcus shot Abigail a sarcastic look and said, "I can't believe you're still this clueless after all this time."

Furious, Abigail retorted, "You may think you're hot stuff now, but when Mr. Julius Andrade's people arrive, I'll cut out your tongue and leave you speechless for life!"

About ten minutes later, a man with slicked-back hair and glasses strode into the lobby, accompanied by a dozen muscular bodyguards.

Julius's face lit up, and he rushed over to greet him. "Jake, you finally made it! Get rid of this loser right now!"

Jake's gaze zeroed in on Marcus. Without a moment's hesitation, he barked orders to his men, "He hit Mr. Julius Andrade. Take him down—hard!"

The bodyguards descended on Marcus, ready to pounce.

Tension filled the air; everyone knew that with Stephen's secretary in the mix, Marcus was likely facing a grim fate.

"Don't you dare," Marcus said, remaining seated and crossing his legs.

To everyone's surprise, the bodyguards hesitated, clearly intimidated.

Then, Marcus pulled out his phone and called Stephen.

Earlier, in the presidential suite of the Hilton Hotel, Stephen had been nothing but courteous, always quick to respond to Marcus's words.

Marcus was a little disappointed that he had such an arrogant nephew.

Soon, Stephen's respectful voice came through, "My lord, what can I do for you?"

"You have ten minutes to come meet me at the Chaumet counter in Vertex Plaza," Marcus said before hanging up.

Stephen immediately set aside his work and instructed his driver to rush to Vertex Plaza.

As they drove, doubts began to creep into his mind. He recalled that Julius had asked him about sending someone to Vertex Plaza not long ago.

Was this all just a coincidence?
