After Josie left, Marcus retrieved Johnson's Spatial Ring.

With a drop of blood, he performed the ritual to bind the ring to himself, making it his own.

Immersing his consciousness into the Spatial Ring, Marcus meticulously examined its contents, transferring everything of value to his own Spatial Ring.

Among Johnson's belongings were millions of dollars in cash and several enchanted gemstones.

According to magical tomes, enchanted gemstones contained latent magical energy.

By reciting specific incantations, one could unleash this energy in battle.

However, the stones were single-use, discharging their power in one burst before becoming useless.

As such, they were often reserved for life-threatening situations.

During his fight with Johnson, Marcus had been too swift for him to activate these stones, ultimately claiming them for himself.

The Spatial Ring also contained two spell manuals: Fireball and Lightning—abilities Marcus had seen Johnson use.

Marcus was ecstatic. Up until now, his magical tomes were merely introductory guides for novice wizards, lacking any advanced spells.

With these manuals, he could finally begin learning and mastering the powerful techniques of Fireball and Lightning.

Among the items, a tattered, yellowed booklet caught Marcus's attention.

Flipping through it, he realized it was Johnson's personal journal.

The entries revealed Johnson's affiliation with a Weston Magic Guild, where he had worked as a freelance wizard—essentially a mercenary.

Through the guild's network, he had been hired by the Brown family in Kingston for long-term service.

Besides fulfilling his obligations to the Browns, the journal mentioned two names that struck a chord with Marcus: his estranged brothers.

It became clear that both his brothers had dealings with the Weston Magic Guild, with the second brother even hiring Johnson on occasion.

As Marcus encountered more people and delved deeper into the world, he began piecing together fragments of his brothers' lives, learning more about their connections and motives.

He put the items away and began practicing the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire.

After an intense night of training, enhanced by rare magical herbs, Marcus successfully elevated his cultivation to the second peak of Ancient Martial Stage Two, significantly increasing his combat prowess.

The next morning, Marcus hailed a cab and returned to BioFusion Pharmaceuticals.

It seemed Josie had been waiting for him at the entrance.

The moment she saw Marcus's striking figure appear in her line of sight, her face lit up with a radiant smile. She hurried to greet him. "Good morning, Mr. Fitzgerald!" she said warmly.

Marcus nodded in acknowledgment. Guided by Josie, he toured the BioFusion Pharmaceuticals' production line.

The facility was entirely modernized, featuring cutting-edge equipment and highly trained staff, meeting the highest international standards.

Marcus found it hard to believe that Josie had achieved all this in just five years.

He made a mental note to seek her advice on business management.

During the tour, Marcus finalized the details for his order of ten production lines. The two of them then returned to Josie's office.

Thanks to Marcus's treatment the previous day, Josie looked radiant, with no sign of her prior ailments.

On the contrary, she appeared invigorated, exuding a newfound femininity.

"Ms. Shelman, the representatives from AquaVital Pharmaceuticals in Kingston are here. They're insisting on meeting you," Josie's secretary said as she knocked and entered the office.

Without hesitation, Josie replied, "Tell them I'm hosting an esteemed guest. They'll have to wait a moment."

The girl nodded and left.

"You certainly are busy, Ms. Shelman," Marcus remarked. In just the two days he'd been here, BioFusion Pharmaceuticals had been bustling with visitors eager to negotiate deals.

Before Josie could respond, a rough voice echoed from the corridor. "I don't care! I want to see Ms. Shelman now! If she doesn't come out, I'll barge into her office myself!"

"Mr. Wollaston," the secretary pleaded, "Ms. Shelman is meeting with a guest. Please wait in the reception room for just a moment!"
