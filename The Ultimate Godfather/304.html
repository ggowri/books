Chapter 303 Dylan Dixon

In recent years, Builder Market's performance has been declining year by year due to the impact of online shopping.

According to last year's financial report, Builder Market had been barely surviving, relying on subsidies from the group.

"Is your chairman's surname Dixon?" Marcus asked.

He thought to himself, "I vaguely saw this name on the Builder Market page of the financial report Michelle handed to me.

But I only remembered the surname, not the full name."

"Yes, the chairman's name is Dylan Dixon. Do you know him, Marcus?" Allan asked.

"Don't worry, Dylan won't be able to fire you." Marcus reassured Allan.

Allan froze for a moment, exchanging a glance with Lisa, unsure of what Marcus meant by this statement.

Marcus then took out his phone and made a call.

On the other end of the line, Michelle's voice came through. "Mr. Fitzgerald, is there something you need?"

Marcus got straight to the point, "I'm at Solomia Hospital, in the cardiovascular department. Have Dylan Dixon from Builder Market come to see me. I'm giving him five minutes!"

With that, Marcus hung up the phone.

Allan and Lisa could hardly believe his words.

They thought, Marcus just told the chairman to come here within five minutes.

How is that possible?

Marcus is skilled in medicine and has a vast network, but how could he possibly know Dylan?

Dylan is a billionaire tycoon!

And more importantly, Marcus just gave him a five-minute ultimatum.

It was clearly an order from a superior to a subordinate.

Even if Marcus really knew Dylan, how could Marcus command him like that?"

Lisa seemed unable to hold back anymore and asked Marcus, "Marcus, the Dylan you're talking about is the chairman of Builder Market, right?"

"Yes, I just called. Dylan should be here in five minutes."

Marcus replied as if it was the most ordinary thing in the world.

"Marcus, I could believe that you know the chairman, but telling him to come here in five minutes? Is that even possible?"

Lisa still couldn't wrap her head around it.

To them, Dylan was like a top-tier tycoon, a billionaire.

How could someone like Dylan just show up at Marcus' beck and call?

"Well, I really hope the chairman does come, but..."

Allan sighed, but his words trailed off.

It was hard for him to believe Marcus' words.

Marcus didn't explain. Instead, he remained quiet and waited.

"Isn't that Dylan, the chairman of Builder Market? Why is he here at the hospital? Is he sick?"

"Look at how he's walking so briskly. Does that look like someone here for a check-up? I think he's here to see someone."

"Yeah, he definitely doesn't look sick. He must have other business here."

Just then, voices were heard outside the room.

People automatically made way.

A bald man, about 5 feet 5 inches tall but weighing close to 200 pounds, walked quickly toward them, leading a group of sharply dressed bodyguards.

The bald man exuded a commanding presence, though not tall.

Soon, he arrived at the room where Marcus and the others were waiting.

"Mr... Mr. Dixon, you're here!"

Allan was stunned when he saw Dylan, his expression full of disbelief.

Lisa was also in shock.

Dylan completely ignored Allan, walking straight up to Marcus. After bowing, he said, "Mr. Fitzgerald, you asked me to arrive within five minutes. It's 4 minutes and 30 seconds now. I'm not late. Please give me your instructions, Mr. Fitzgerald!"

Dylan just finished, and Allan and Lisa felt as though they had been struck by lightning.

They looked at Marcus in complete disbelief.

They never imagined that the phone call Marcus made earlier was actually real!

"Marcus told Dylan to come within five minutes, and Dylan did it in just 4 minutes and 30 seconds.

It has shown just how vital Marcus' words were to Dylan," They thought.

Marcus remained seated in his chair, looking at Dylan. "Did you approve of firing Allan?"

Dylan Dixon froze for a moment, instinctively glancing at Allan not far away.

His mind quickly began to process Marcus' words.

Dylan was no stranger to the ways of the world.

In an instant, he understood Marcus' meaning and said, "Mr. Fitzgerald, there must have been some misunderstanding. Aaron has been reporting Allan to me, and I believe his slander. That's why I agreed to fire him. It has nothing to do with me!"
