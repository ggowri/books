As the head of the Uswad First Family, Jasper was, of course, an exceptionally wise individual.

Although Oliver's words were meant to undermine others and deflate their spirit, Jasper didn't immediately explode in anger.

Instead, he calmly turned to Oliver and asked, "Oliver, do you know this person?"

Oliver took a deep breath and explained in detail the events from that day when Solomia had captured Auber, the leader of the Black Hawk Legion, as well as Josiah's actions.

A serious expression appeared on Jasper's face. "So, you're saying that the person who killed Xander and his family is Mr. Death?"

Oliver nodded with quiet certainty. "He's young, yet possesses immense power. There's only one person who fits that description."

Jasper paced back and forth in the room, before finally pausing and taking a deep breath. "I trust your judgment. We'll proceed as you suggest."

Though Oliver was not his son, he was the chosen heir to the family leadership, selected by his father.

Jasper couldn't dismiss Oliver's opinions lightly.

Once Jasper had agreed with his assessment, Oliver left the room, satisfied.

Despite his strong suspicion that Marcus was the killer, Oliver felt no hatred toward him.

It was simple—he understood Xander's family too well. Xander's family had always been reckless, their personalities abrasive.

With Marcus' low-key, calm nature, it was clear that Xander's family had provoked him first.

In the end, their deaths were merely a consequence of their own actions.

*****

Marcus, however, remained unaware of the reactions following Xander's family's deaths.

He had returned to Bayside Villa, where he relaxed in comfort, taking a long, soothing bath before sinking into the couch with a cigarette in hand.

Nearby, Pearl, ever attentive, massaged Marcus' shoulders. It was a rare moment when he wasn't focused on training.

Meanwhile, in the heart of Uswad's Ancient Martial Arts sects, things were much more tense. The Astral Serpent Order, one of the three major factions, was in turmoil.

The Soul Lamps of two prominent elders had shattered, a sign of grave news. Even Phoenix, the normally composed and cautious leader of the Order, could no longer stay still.

Determined to get to the bottom of it, Phoenix decided to personally investigate the deaths, and if necessary, take action to avenge the fallen Asher and the two elders.

The next morning, Marcus set out early to visit Serpent's Coil and check on the progress of Jalen and the others' training.

However, as his car neared a bustling shopping plaza, the noise of a crowd caught his attention.

Marcus instinctively pulled over, stepping out and standing on a flower bed to observe the scene.

To his surprise, a fighting ring had been set up in the center, and inside, a Crankhurst man was engaged in combat with a woman dressed in modern martial arts gear.

Under normal circumstances, such an arena was typically rented by local fighting clubs for promotional purposes.

Anyone could register to compete, and while it was meant for entertainment, the fighters were supposed to wear protective gear.

But here, neither the Crankhurstian man nor the woman wore any protection.

Their battle was intense, brutal, and clearly not staged.

"Go Shannon! Take him down! Show that Crankhurstian what Uswad's made of!"

The crowd cheered with fervor.

From their reaction, it was clear that the Crankhurstian in the ring had earned their disdain.

Marcus squinted, his gaze sharpening as he recognized the man in the ring.

It was Easton, a member of the Griffin family from Crankhurst, who had once come to Benjamin's house with his father Terry, his uncle Zane, and his sister Charlotte.

Back then, Easton had tried to challenge Marcus in an attempt to showcase Crankhurst's might but had been easily defeated, leaving in disgrace.

It seemed their paths had crossed once again.

"Ha! Uswad's weakling, you're no match for me. Why don't you just admit defeat now?" Easton sneered, fully in control of the fight.

Shannon Jarvis, the woman in the ring, had nothing left but defense; she struggled to keep up.

"Stop talking and show me what you've got!" Shannon clenched her jaw, fists flying toward Easton's face.

Easton scoffed, grabbing her wrist with ease, swinging her violently to the side. Shannon staggered backward, barely managing to keep from falling out of the ring.

"Ha ha ha! Is this all Uswad has to offer? A woman fighting for their honor? What a joke!" Easton's arrogance knew no bounds, and despite the furious glares from the crowd, he only grew bolder.

Shannon, enraged, lunged at him, delivering a powerful kick aimed at his chest.

Easton didn't even flinch. With a cruel grin, he took the full force of her kick, and with a forceful push, Shannon was sent sprawling to the ground, her body crashing heavily. She had no strength left to rise.

The crowd's anger erupted in a wave of frustrated shouts, but no one dared to challenge Easton directly.

He had already won over ten matches that day, and no one was capable of defeating him.
