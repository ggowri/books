Overhearing snippets of chatter from curious onlookers, Marcus pieced together the story—Julian Ashford was in town, visiting Solomia.

As a senior government official—a heavyweight in national politics—Julian's visit naturally called for tight security.

Moments later, a convoy of sleek, black Maybachs cruised down the street, with flashing police cars clearing the way. The atmosphere was electric.

Drivers pulled over, some whipping out their phones to snap photos or record videos, eager to capture the grandeur of Julian Ashford's impressive motorcade.

"Huh?"

Marcus barely gave it a second thought and turned to leave.

But just as he was about to step away, a faint yet unmistakable wave of deadly intent prickled at his senses, coming from the sealed-off road.

Instantly, Marcus extended his spiritual powers, covering the entire area like an invisible web.

Every detail of the road came into sharp focus.

His gaze locked onto figures hiding inside buildings along the road—civilians by appearance, but armed to the teeth.

Despite their attempts to blend in, Marcus caught them all.

Among them, several seemed to be coordinating via walkie-talkies, lying in wait for Julian's motorcade.

This was a textbook assassination plot, no question about it.

Marcus didn't know who was behind this, but as a patriot, there was no way he'd let someone take out a national leader on his watch.

He quickly parked his car and moved toward the road.

"Hey, this area's off-limits! Turn around and head back now!"

Before he could get any closer, two SWAT officers stepped in front of him, arms crossed and firm.

Marcus glanced at the officers, then at the road. Time was running out.

Without hesitation, he activated his teleportation ability, disappearing before the officers could even blink.

Both SWAT officers froze, their jaws dropping. One rubbed his eyes as if trying to wake himself up, while the other muttered, "Did you see that? Or am I losing it?"

The stunned crowd nearby shared their disbelief, and some even managed to record the surreal moment on their phones.

Marcus reappeared in the blink of an eye—now sitting calmly in the passenger seat of Julian's Maybach L5.

The driver yelped in shock, jerking the wheel slightly, while Julian's eyebrows shot up in surprise.

The bodyguard didn't hesitate, whipping out his gun and aiming directly at Marcus.

Before the bodyguard could squeeze the trigger, an invisible force pinned him to his seat, his arms frozen mid-motion.

"This is the Commander's vehicle! Who the hell are you?" the bodyguard growled through gritted teeth, his face turning red as he struggled against the pressure.

Marcus gave a casual shrug. "Relax, I'm not here to hurt anyone. Someone's planning to assassinate Commander Ashford, and I'm here to stop them."

By now, Marcus had pieced together the situation. The attackers were almost certainly mercenaries hired by some overseas faction.

Julian's usually calm demeanor cracked slightly, his weathered eyes narrowing with intrigue.

The bodyguard, still straining against the invisible force, scoffed, "Assassinate? You're out of your mind. We've already done a full security sweep and set up multiple checkpoints. No way anyone could get close enough to pull that off."

Ignoring the bodyguard's skepticism, Marcus turned to the driver. "Keep driving. Oh, and don't freak out when I start firing, alright?"

His spiritual powers had already locked onto the would-be assassins, tracking their every move with pinpoint precision.

"Alright, listen up. At the first intersection, there's a yellow building with three gunmen inside. After that, there's a nursery—third floor, elevator shaft—two snipers. Then, you've got a bank headquarters up ahead. Four more guys there, and they've got grenades ready to blow this car to pieces..."

Marcus rattled off the positions of the attackers like he was reading from a map, his tone calm and matter-of-fact.

The bodyguard's eyes narrowed, suspicion warring with shock.

How could this guy, who hadn't even been down the road, know all of this?

Even Julian, known for his stoic composure, couldn't help but glance sideways at Marcus, his expression briefly betraying curiosity.

Yet, no response came from him.

"Bang!"

Then, as the Maybach rolled up to the first intersection, a sharp crack split the air—a gunshot.
