"Are you feeling better, Mr. Fitzgerald?" Diana's concerned voice came from the doorway.

Marcus opened his room door and stepped out, looking radiant with eighty percent recovery.

"I'm fine," he replied.

Diana let out a long breath, relief washing over her.

She had been anxious during the three days of his recovery, struggling to eat or sleep.

Seeing him well again brought a bright smile to her stunning face.

She said, "I'm glad to hear that."

"How is the gun remodeling going? Can you show me?" Marcus asked, eager to see the progress, as it was crucial for his plans to build a force.

Diana nodded, and they made their way to the villa's backyard, where dozens of guns were neatly arranged on a large table, each emitting energy fluctuations.

Upon closer inspection, Marcus noticed energy crystals the size of nails embedded in the guns, each colored differently to represent various attributes.

He picked up a gun and pulled the trigger.

Swoosh!

A bullet shot out, radiating destructive energy, directly falling on a boulder not far away.

Boom!

With the sound of a crack, the boulder was directly beaten apart.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, please don't shoot again. It might disturb the neighbors," Diana hurriedly cautioned.

This was the villa area, and the noise just now had been quite loud.

Marcus scratched his head in embarrassment.

The firearm was indeed more powerful than the ones Diana had remodeled before, likely due to the higher-grade energy crystals embedded in them.

And the energy crystals were more than twice as small as before.

"I'm impressed. Keep up the good work," Marcus said with a laugh.

"Remodeling isn't an issue, but I need you to bring me some more guns. Also, these energy crystals aren't permanent. After a period of use, they'll be exhausted, and it is necessary to replace them," Diana explained, adjusting her hair.

Marcus raised an eyebrow. "I will try to get a batch of guns. How long do they last?"

"They should last about three months during a large-scale battle," Diana replied casually.

Then Marcus had nothing to worry about.

He didn't intend to launch any large-scale battles.

He was primarily focused on strengthening his forces for deterrence.

Even if there was a conflict, it should remain local.

Compared to large-scale battles, this was merely a minor issue.

Therefore, the energy crystals reserved for these guns should last over a year.

If he went to southeast Uswad for crystals next time, it would be for new gun upgrades.

As for replacing the second batch of energy crystals, there was no need to worry for now.

With that in mind, he called Maxine. "Hello, Officer Burnett!"

"What's up? Why the formal title?" Maxine's cold voice responded.

"Oh, no, Baby Maxine, is that better?" Marcus quickly corrected himself.

"Please drop the 'baby'!" Maxine replied with a hint of fluster in her tone. No one had ever called her that, which made her heartbeat.

"What? I'm your fiancé recognized by the Burnett family. Can't I call you that? Are you falling for someone else?" Marcus teased, enjoying her reaction.

The unsmiling appearance of Maxine could stimulate Marcus' desire to conquer.

"Enough with the flirting. If you called for a reason, just say it," Maxine said, trying to sound impatient but unable to hide her amusement.

"I need a batch of guns. Do you have access?" Marcus asked.

Maxine frowned. "I don't have that kind of access."

"Aren't you a police officer? How can you not get guns?" Marcus pressed.

"Just because I'm a police officer doesn't mean I'm selling arms! What do you think I am?" Maxine retorted, rolling her eyes.

"Seriously, what do you need guns for? Planning a rebellion?" she added, half-joking.

"I'm a good citizen! I wouldn't rebel," Marcus laughed.

"Guns are usually sold on the black market or smuggled in," Maxine explained.

"The black market guns are outdated and limited in power. Do you know how to smuggle them in?" Marcus asked.

If it was the kind of gun in the black market, he could acquire plenty from the Serpent's Coil.
