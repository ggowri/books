As if to show off his financial power, Atlas didn't wait for Marcus to respond. He took out a card from his crocodile leather bag and tossed it toward Marcus with a look of arrogance.

"Looking at you, I'm guessing you're probably just a driver for some company. This is probably your salary for ten years. I'd say it's a good deal. Hope you can appreciate this opportunity!"

Seeing this, Payne and Edwin were both shocked.

Only after hearing Payne's words did Edwin reluctantly put the card on the ground, coldly saying to Marcus, "Hmph, consider it a favor!"

"He's just a driver. How could he be compared to Atlas?

"Yeah, Natalie, don't make excuses for this guy. I really don't understand what's so good about him that you're defending him.

"Kid, just take this one million dollars and get lost. There won't be a second chance. How dare you compete with me for a woman? You're not even close!" Atlas said arrogantly to Marcus.

"Don't worry, Cora. This is nothing to me. When Natalie and I get together, you'll definitely benefit, too!"

Atlas, being a clever man, understood how to navigate relationships and show gratitude where needed.

"Are you the sales manager under Hector?"

Despite Atlas' arrogance, Marcus remained unfazed, his expression still calm as he asked.

Atlas' eyes narrowed in surprise. "You actually know Mr. Olson? Are you perhaps a driver for our company?"

"Yes, I did drive the car from the company, but have you considered that the company is actually mine?" Marcus said.

As soon as he said this, everyone froze for a moment, their faces filled with disbelief.

While they could admit that Marcus' words were technically possible, they found it impossible to believe that Marcus owned his own company.
