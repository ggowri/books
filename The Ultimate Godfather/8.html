Abigail and Felix shared a knowing look before bursting into laughter.

"Who do you think you are? Flashing that Supreme Card around like you own the place. I'm betting that card is stolen," Felix taunted with a sneer.

"Look at the screen! With Mr. Wesley here, the general manager position is mine."

He pointed to the vote tally displayed prominently, his confidence overflowing.

"What if the chairman doesn't want you to be the general manager?" Marcus asked, his tone cold and mocking.

Abigail scoffed immediately. "Stop dreaming, Marcus. You're nothing but a loser. What connection could you possibly have to the new chairman?"

The room erupted in mocking laughter.

Marcus straightened his tie and said, "I don't need connections. I am the new chairman."

His quiet words silenced the room.

All eyes turned to him, filled with disbelief and scorn.

Abigail and Felix doubled over laughing.

"You? The new chairman? Have you lost your mind? You'd better go to the hospital for a check-up now." Felix wiped tears from his eyes, his tone dripping with mockery.

"Mr. Yannis, this guy must be delusional after getting hit too hard. This is hilarious!" Abigail cackled.

Whispers and jeers filled the room.

Even Michelle looked uncomfortable, unable to believe Marcus' claim despite not sharing others' contempt for him.

Anthony puffed on his cigar, watching Marcus with an amused smirk. His disdain was unmistakable.

"Mr. Wesley," Felix bowed slightly, "this man's impersonating the chairman. Should we teach him a lesson? How about breaking a few bones?"

Anthony waved his hand casually, signaling his approval.

With Anthony's consent, Felix grew even more brazen. He immediately summoned the enforcers from Golden Group.

A bunch of men dressed in black, armed with baseball bats, surrounded Marcus, ready to attack.

"Wait! He's a Supreme Card holder anyway- you can't do this to him!" Michelle jumped between them, unwilling to watch Marcus being hurt.

With a smack, Felix slapped Michelle hard across the face. Her delicate cheek instantly turned red and swollen.

"You stupid bitch! How dare you defend this fraud? Still take yourself as the daughter of the general manager?"

With that, he raised his hand for another strike.

But before he could touch her, an iron grip caught his wrist.

"That's enough!"

Marcus' glare bore into Felix, his voice icy.

With a firm squeeze, Felix yelped, cursing through clenched teeth. "Let go of me, you worthless piece of shit! Let go, or I'll..."

Before he could finish, a sharp "crack" echoed through the room. Marcus had broken his wrist.

Felix screamed in agony, his face twisting in pain.

"You bastard, you'll pay for this! You're dead!"

Seeing his struggles, Marcus landed several brutal slaps on him. His teeth cracked, blood spraying from his nose and mouth.

The next moment, Marcus grabbed him and hurled him across the room.

Felix crashed into a group of men in black suits, sending them sprawling like bowling pins.

The crowd watched in stunned silence.

No one could believe this supposed nobody would dare beat Felix to a pulp right in front of Anthony. They thought he had to be insane.

"Kill him! Kill the bastard!" Felix screamed through bloody lips.

A dozen men charged at Marcus, baseball bats raised.

But Marcus didn't flinch. His Timeless Warrior's Grimoire might not have been fully mastered, but his body had evolved far beyond normal human limits.

These thugs were nothing to him and their attacks seemed to crawl through molasses.

With a few precise strikes, Marcus left them all groaning on the floor.

Dead silence fell over the room.

The weak nobody they'd mocked had just demolished a dozen armed men without breaking a sweat.

Michelle's hand flew to her mouth, eyes wide with shock.

Only Anthony remained unfazed, casually puffing his cigar. When the whispers died down, he stood and gave a slow clap.

"Impressive," he smirked. "You've got some skills after all."

"But don't think you can cause trouble in Golden Group and walk away. Impersonating our chairman? Attacking my men? You'll pay for that today."

He waved lazily.

His men soon stepped forward, pistols drawn. Cold steel aimed at Marcus's heart.
