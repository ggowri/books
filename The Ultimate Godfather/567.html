The high-ranking members of the Axe Gang were struck with sheer terror, a suffocating pressure washing over them in an instant.

The next moment, Derek's massive hand gripped tightly, ready to claim his opponent's life.

"Whoosh!"

But just then, a beam of destructive light shot down from the distance.

Derek's instincts screamed in warning, the threat approaching fast.

He swiftly tossed the Axe Gang member aside, his body darting to the side just in time to avoid the annihilating beam.

Everyone turned their gaze toward the direction of the blast, eyes widening as they saw Marcus walking slowly toward them.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, you've finally arrived..."

Blake and Baron couldn't hide their relief, their faces lighting up with a mix of hope and awe.

Marcus—he was their last hope.

If Marcus couldn't handle Derek, it would mean the end for the Axe Gang.

Marcus nodded at Blake and Baron before turning his gaze to Luther and Derek.

"Who gave you the courage to kill my subordinate?" he asked coldly.

Luther froze for a second before sneering. "What are you? A nobody daring to act all high and mighty in front of an elder of the Celestial Arc Order? You must be asking for death!"

Marcus' eyes narrowed slightly, and he turned to Luther, his voice calm but dangerous. "Did you say he's from the Celestial Arc Order?"

Luther's face took on a smug, arrogant expression. "That's right. This is Derek, an elder of the Celestial Arc Order. He could wipe the floor with scum like you. I suggest you kneel and beg for mercy, or maybe he'll spare you, seeing as you have some strength. But don't get your hopes up."

Before Luther could finish his taunt, the air cracked with the sound of thunder.

In a blink, a bolt of lightning descended from the sky, striking him down without mercy.

Luther didn't even have time to scream before the bolt exploded on impact, his body disintegrating in an instant.

Flesh and bone shattered, raining down in pieces, and the stench of blood filled the air.

The crowd froze, their hearts pounding.

No one expected Marcus to strike so decisively, especially when they had just learned that Luther was standing next to a Celestial Arc Order elder.

And yet Marcus had killed him without hesitation. The speed and power of that lightning were terrifying.

Even though Luther was just an ordinary person, the fact that he was backed by Derek didn't matter. He was obliterated without a second thought.

Blake and Baron's expressions shifted, a flicker of hope igniting within them.

Given the power Marcus had just displayed, maybe he really could stand toe to toe with Derek.

Derek's cold eyes swept over Marcus, his voice dripping with disdain. "So, you're a Level 5 Wizard? Well then, I guess I'll finally see what all the fuss is about."

He had mistaken Marcus for a Level 5 Wizard.

Marcus didn't bother to correct him. He simply watched as Derek took a step forward, the full force of his Ancient Martial Art power unleashing with a deafening roar.

The aura of a Stage Five Master flooded the area, and in the blink of an eye, a crushing palm strike was launched toward Marcus.

The palm carried with it an overwhelming, almost impossible force—a deadly blend of martial arts and mystical techniques. In a heartbeat, the space in front of Marcus was completely sealed off, the air vibrating with raw energy.

"This palm strike is insane. Let's see how this guy plans to survive it," muttered one of the Celestial Arc Order's experts.

Though Derek was only at the Stage Five of Ancient Martial Art, he had been entrenched in that realm for years, his strength honed to a razor's edge.

His experience gave him the power to challenge opponents far beyond his level.

Taking down a young Level 5 Wizard should be a walk in the park.

By the looks of the palm strike, Derek was clearly planning to end this quickly.

"Kid, you're so young and already looking for death... What a shame," Derek sneered as he released the devastating attack.

His voice dripped with mockery, as if he were certain that this one strike would seal Marcus' fate.

Marcus, however, didn't flinch. Instead, he let out a cold laugh. "You really think that palm of yours is strong?"

With that, he threw a single punch.

The air shook violently as the shockwave of destruction spread in all directions, a low, thunderous roar ripping through the sky. The ground trembled beneath them.

Derek's mocking grin quickly faded.

The moment their attacks collided, he felt an irresistible force crash into him, like a sledgehammer slamming into his body.

The next instant, there was an explosive crack, and Derek's body was sent hurtling through the air. Like a bullet, he slammed into a nearby wall, shattering it on impact.

The entire structure collapsed into ruins, burying him in a pile of debris.

Blood poured from his mouth as he lay in the wreckage, his eyes wide with disbelief.

"How... how is this possible?" he gasped weakly, his voice barely a whisper. "You... you're actually... an Ancient Martial Art Stage Six expert..."

With those final, incomprehensible words, Derek's body went limp, his life snuffed out in an instant.

He had been so certain that Marcus was just an Ancient Martial Art at Stage Six. In the end, he couldn't have been more wrong.
