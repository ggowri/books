Chapter 300 We Are Family

"Marcus, how is Nick?"

Richard asked with a worried expression.

"I've cleared the blocked blood vessels. Nick should be fine now," Marcus replied.

He just finished. Richard, Melinda, Aria, Lisa, and Allan were all frozen in place, their expressions showing disbelief.

"Marcus, you've only been in the ward for 10 minutes, and Nick's illness is already cured? Are you kidding me?"

Richard asked, hardly believing Marcus' words.

"Yeah, Marcus, this is a life-and-death matter. You can't joke about something like this."

Melinda also reminded him of that.

She worried that Marcus, afraid that Nick's family would look down on him, might be lying.

"You can go in and see for yourselves. Nick should be awake by now."

Marcus said with a smile.

The group exchanged glances and hurried into the ward.

Just as Marcus had said, Nick had indeed woken up.

His complexion was rosy, and his breathing was perfectly steady.

Nick surprisingly sat up in bed and tried to get out of it while seeing his family enter the room.

"Dad, you're awake!"

Lisa rushed over to stop him, afraid that Nick might relapse if he wasn't careful.

"Lisa, I'm fine now. Don't stop me. I feel no dizziness no shortness of breath. I'm full of energy now. Let me take a walk!"

Nick said, getting out of bed and walking around.

His posture was upright, his breathing strong and steady, and he no longer looked frail like before.

Aria, Lisa, Allan, and the others were all stunned by seeing this, their eyes filled with disbelief.

They all knew Nick's condition well.

Even when his life wasn't in danger,

Nick could barely walk a few steps without panting, let alone move around like he was now, full of energy.

The way Nick looked now seemed no different from someone who hadn't been sick at all.

"Lisa, go ask Allan which doctor he got for me. I need to thank him properly. It feels like my illness is completely gone. I feel so much younger than before!"

Nick said to Lisa with excitement.

Nick's symptoms had improved, and he was overjoyed.

He was even thinking about writing a thank-you letter to the doctor who treated him.

"Nick, it wasn't the doctor. It was Marcus who saved you. If you want to thank someone, thank Marcus!"

Lisa said, tears welling up in her eyes.

Nick froze at her words. "Marcus? Really, Marcus?"

Aria nodded. "Yes, it was Marcus who saved you. The hospital had already declared you dead earlier. If it weren't for Marcus, we'd be planning your funeral right now."

Nick immediately walked over to Marcus after hearing this.

His expression was filled with emotion, and he said, "Marcus, thank you so much! You're my lifesaver!

"I can't believe I didn't believe in you before. I was so wrong!"

Nick tried to bow to Marcus after saying this, but Marcus stopped him. "Nick, don't say that. I'm your nephew. I must help you. We're family."

"Although we had some misunderstandings before, I can tell that Nick's family is generally good people.

They might be a bit narrow-minded, but they aren't bad at heart.

They are completely different from my aunt, Alice.

Her family is both cruel and snobbish, and they don't want their relatives to have a better life than they do.

They even insulted Melinda in public, which is why I stepped in to teach them a lesson.

But a relative like Nick, I'm more than happy to help," Marcus thought.

"Marcus, I've said some harsh things to you before. I hope you don't take it to heart. I owe you an apology."

Lisa walked over, and she looked regret.

Thinking back on the things she said to Marcus, Lisa felt deeply guilty.

"Even though Marcus might be a bit of a show-off as a son-in-law, he really is a good person. Despite everything I said, he still saved my dad," she thought, filled with gratitude.

"Lisa, I already told Nick that we're family. There's no need to be sorry."

Marcus waved his hand.

"Lisa isn't a bad person. She just didn't believe I could save Nick.

But those are not important." Marcus didn't take it personally.

"That's right. Marcus is right. We're family. Nick, Aria, Lisa, don't be so formal with Marcus anymore!"

Richard spoke up at this point.

"Allan, do we have any good, available stalls at the wholesale market? I want to rent one for Marcus. He's married a rich lady, and while his life might be more comfortable, he'll definitely face some hardships. I want him to stand on his own feet so no one will look down on him!"

At that moment, Lisa suddenly spoke up.
