Leia noticed the marks on Josie's neck and kept asking questions.

It was then that Josie realized her skin was covered with the marks Marcus had left during their wild night.

"It wasn't Marcus bullying me. I wasn't feeling well, so he was helping treat me," Josie said, coming up with another little white lie.

Josie once again told a harmless lie.

But as soon as she said it, she regretted it.

She could feel Marcus's peculiar gaze on her.

At that moment, Marcus could barely hold back his laughter.

Treating her?

This woman really knew how to make up stories.

Then again, it did feel like he was treating her last night.

Treating her loneliness!

After breakfast, Marcus and Josie took Leia to preschool together.

Perhaps afraid of being seen, Josie covered herself up tightly.

Some of the other mothers at the preschool looked concerned, assuming Josie was sick.

Embarrassed, Josie wished she could disappear into a hole, all while glaring furiously at Marcus.

"Marcus, it's all your fault. Why were you so wild last night? How am I supposed to show my face now?" she complained as they got into the car.

Although Josie's tone was full of complaints, her voice carried an unmistakable happiness.

The unforgettable memories of last night replayed in Josie's mind, leaving her longing for more.

"You were even crazier than me! Look at my body—it's no better than yours. And isn't this exactly what you wanted? You kept begging me to go faster and not stop!" Marcus teased.

Marcus said with a grin.

Josie quickly covered his mouth. "Stop talking! Don't you have any shame?" she exclaimed.

"What's there to be embarrassed about? We've already done everything that can be done. What good does being shy do now?" Marcus replied, shaking his head.

Over the next few days, Marcus didn't return to Solomia but stayed at Josie's house.

By midday the next day, news broke of Ricardo Brown, the head of the Brown family, passing away.

The news swept through all of Kingston, becoming the city's top headline of the day!

The news shocked countless people, with many speculating about Ricardo's cause of death.

After all, Ricardo was the head of one of Uswad's top families and a formidable warrior.

His influence and resources were immeasurable.

For someone like that to die so suddenly was unbelievable!

This was akin to a super-guided bomb for Kingston's political and business landscape.

By that afternoon, the ripple effects of Ricardo's death began to unfold.

All of the Brown family's business stocks in Kingston plummeted, and operations came to a halt.

Kingston's major families all rushed to claim a piece of the pie, frantically pooling their funds to acquire the Brown family's assets.

For a time, a frenzy of buying ensued.

With the death of one man, an era of a great family's dominance came to an end.

Amidst the chaos, a mysterious figure known only as "Death" invested 350 billion dollars to swiftly seize several of the Brown family's core industries.

Entering at the lowest point of the stock crash, the moves were not only lightning-fast but remarkably calculated, completing acquisitions before the other Kingston families could react.

Many began speculating about the true identity of Death.

After acquiring the companies, Death immediately implemented a series of actions.

The first step was replacing the legal representatives, removing the Browns and appointing senior executives from within the companies.

However, everyone understood that this Death figure must have an extraordinary identity but was unwilling or unable to reveal themselves.

Thus, the companies were entrusted to others for management, but the real power behind them remained Death.

For 350 billion dollars, Death had acquired four companies, all of which were renowned emerging high-tech enterprises in Uswad.

These companies were Sync Group, Rising Dragon Group, Pioneer Group, and Eastcomm Group.

Although these companies' current business volumes were not on par with Kingston's established enterprises, they held immense potential.

With proper management and favorable winds, they could rise to prominence rapidly.

That day, Marcus left Sunshine Estates and went to the presidential suite at the top floor of Kingston's Hilton Hotel.

He sat in the executive chair, overlooking the bustling city below.

A moment later, the doorbell rang.

Four figures walked into the room.

The four individuals—three men and one woman—were the newly appointed CEOs of Sync Group, Rising Dragon Group, Pioneer Group, and Eastcomm Group.

"We pay our respects to Lord Death!" they said in unison.
