Chapter 479 Can You Hold Me?

Marcus looked at Jade with concern.

Jade suddenly snapped out of her daze, her body trembling violently. "I... I'm fine... I'm just really scared. Can you hold me?"

It was clear she was truly terrified. Under normal circumstances, she would never have said something like that.

Marcus nodded and pulled Jade gently into his arms.

A faint, sweet scent reached him, and Marcus couldn't help but take a deep breath.

Jade didn't care about any of that now. Wrapped in Marcus' embrace, she finally felt a sense of security.

"It's over. You don't need to be scared anymore. I took care of them," Marcus said, patting her shoulder to comfort her.

Jade instinctively glanced toward the car window and noticed traces of fire burning not far away.

"Did you... kill all of them?" Jade asked, disbelief in her voice.

She stared at Marcus in shock.

In her mind, Marcus was just an exceptional doctor—she had never imagined him as someone capable of killing.

"Yes," Marcus replied calmly.

Jade's gaze shifted, now filled with a hint of fear.

"You're not going to kill me, are you?" she asked suddenly.

Marcus chuckled and said, "Why would I kill you?"

"I'm sorry, I didn't mean it that way. I'm just really scared," Jade apologized, guilt in her voice.

"Why are you so scared of everything?" Marcus asked.

"Because I grew up without a mom. My dad worked at the hospital and often had night shifts, so I was always alone at home," Jade explained.

"In Solariae, it rained a lot. Thunderstorms scared me when I was home alone, and even as I grew up, I stayed afraid of things.

"I'd imagine all kinds of scary things, and even though I knew it was just in my head, I couldn't stop myself," Jade spoke softly.

Marcus finally understood—it was childhood trauma that left Jade with her fears.

Coming from a single-parent family, she really was a pitiful girl.

Hearing her story, Marcus held her tighter, feeling an inexplicable urge to protect her.

"Don't worry. As long as I'm here, no one will hurt you," Marcus said gently.

Jade nodded slightly. Resting against Marcus' broad chest, she could feel the steady rhythm of his heartbeat.

For her, it was a comforting sense of security.

"Mm..."

Just then, a look of pain crossed Jade's face. She held her lower abdomen with her hand, looking uncomfortable.

"What's wrong?" Marcus asked.

Jade frowned, trying to endure the pain as she said, "When I get too scared, my lower abdomen starts hurting—it's probably related to my cramps...

"It's fine. I'll manage for a bit," she said, forcing herself through the discomfort.

Jade was already in significant pain but tried her best not to let Marcus see her suffering.

Marcus quickly activated his X-ray vision, focusing on Jade's lower abdomen.

He discovered that her reproductive organs had blocked meridians and poor blood flow.

A chilling energy lingered inside, continuously affecting her organs. This was the root cause of her cramps.

The intense fright had amplified the cold energy, making Jade experience the pain of her cramps prematurely whenever she was scared.

"If you're okay with it, I can completely cure your cramps," Marcus offered.

Marcus hadn't intended to bring it up, but seeing how much pain Jade was in, he couldn't hold back.

He had previously cured Josie's cramps with great success.

Josie no longer suffered from cramps and looked much healthier now.

Holding Jade, Marcus could feel the cold energy radiating from her body.

If the cold energy wasn't removed, Jade's cramps would only worsen.

The buildup of cold energy also affected her emotions, exacerbating her timidity.

It was a vicious cycle.

"You can really cure my cramps?" Jade asked, disbelief evident in her tone.

She looked at Marcus skeptically.

"You already know I've treated late-stage cancer. What's a little cramp compared to that?" Marcus replied casually.

"What kind of person are you? How do you have such incredible abilities?" Jade asked, tilting her head curiously.

"You'll find out over time," Marcus said with a smile.

"Okay, I'll trust you this time. But don't you dare take advantage of me," Jade warned.

As a doctor, she had a pretty good idea of what the treatment for cramps might involve.

That's why she made sure to warn Marcus in advance.

If she suspected him of crossing any lines, she'd stop the treatment immediately.

Marcus gave her a slight nod.

Then, he carefully reached out and began to lower her pants bit by bit.

Although Jade had mentally prepared herself, the moment Marcus touched a sensitive area, she couldn't help but tense up.

Her body stiffened, and for a moment, she felt an urge to resist.
