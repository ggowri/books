Chapter 357 Oscar Cromwell Starts to Panic

The Solomia Special Police Academy Tactical Unit operated independently, outside Benjamin's direct authority.

So when Brenden Wilde arrived with his team, it completely blindsided Ryder.

"Ryder, what do we do now?"

Oscar, surrounded by hundreds of heavily armed special forces, was starting to lose his nerve.

He had never seen such an overwhelming display of firepower.

With so many guns aimed at him, the thought of one accidental trigger pull sent a shiver down his spine.

"Relax. They won't dare touch me," Ryder replied, forcing a confident smirk.

But deep down, unease crept into his thoughts. Benjamin Mitchell wasn't acting on a whim—this was a calculated move.

"Drop your weapons immediately, or we will take action!"

A commanding voice boomed from a sniper team stationed on a nearby factory rooftop, their rifles trained on the officers below.

The criminal investigation officers exchanged tense glances, their faces pale. Slowly, one by one, they lowered their weapons.

Compared to the Solomia Special Police Academy Tactical Unit, the city's criminal investigation team was vastly outmatched—not just in authority, but also in individual combat skills.

Their equipment was inferior by a wide margin, leaving no room for comparison.

Besides, they were simply following Ryder Cromwell's orders to make an arrest. There was no way they'd risk their lives in a fight they had no chance of winning.

With the situation fully under control, Benjamin and Brenden made their way toward Marcus.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, please forgive our delay. We should have arrived sooner."

Standing in front of everyone, the two high-ranking officials greeted Marcus with an air of deference that stunned the crowd.

A political powerhouse and a military leader were speaking to Marcus with visible respect.

The scene was nothing short of surreal.

Ryder and Oscar looked on, their disbelief written across their faces.

Especially Oscar, who never imagined Marcus held this kind of sway.

"Thank you both for stepping in," Marcus replied calmly, offering a small nod.

At that moment, Theodore Drake, the city's chief prosecutor, stepped forward to greet Marcus with equal reverence.

Watching from a distance, Michelle Smith was dumbfounded.

Since when did Marcus have such powerful allies?

After a brief exchange, Benjamin turned toward Ryder, his expression hardening. "Ryder Cromwell, do you have any idea what you've done?" Benjamin's voice cut through the air like a blade.

Ryder sneered, his composure unwavering. "Benjamin, don't think pulling in the Special Police Academy will change anything. If you want to accuse me, show me your evidence!"

Benjamin's smirk deepened as he signaled to two officials from the Department of Justice.

He had been waiting for Ryder to demand proof.

The officials retrieved several documents from their briefcases and handed them to Ryder.

"These files contain evidence of your corruption—dereliction of duty, embezzlement, bribery. Your time as Deputy Mayor is over," Benjamin said, his tone firm and unyielding.

Ryder's expression darkened as he clenched his teeth in fury. "Benjamin, you've been lying in wait all these years, gathering dirt on me. You're a snake."

But then he let out a bitter chuckle. "Still, my advice? Shred those files while you still can. You won't like what happens if you don't."

Benjamin's eyes narrowed. He knew Ryder's confidence came from his father-in-law, a senior official with substantial influence.

But Benjamin had anticipated this from the very beginning.

Ryder Cromwell was a threat that needed to be neutralized. Without action, Benjamin's position as mayor would always be undercut.

"Ryder Cromwell, your crimes are undeniable. No amount of connections will save you now. Arrest him and let the provincial authorities handle the case!" Benjamin commanded decisively.

Knowing Ryder's rank exceeded the city's jurisdiction, Benjamin had no choice but to escalate the case.

Brenden gestured to his team, who swiftly moved in to apprehend Ryder and Oscar.

"Benjamin, you'll regret this! You've just signed your own political death warrant!" Ryder shouted as he struggled against the officers.

But against the highly trained special forces, his resistance was futile. Cold steel cuffs snapped around his wrists as he was hauled into a waiting police vehicle.

Oscar, meanwhile, stood frozen, his face pale and blank with despair.

He never expected his greed to spiral into a crisis of this magnitude.

And he realized Benjamin wasn't bluffing—he was ready to burn every bridge if that's what it took.

As the saying goes, "When the city burns, the fish in the moat suffer."

Even if Ryder's powerful connections saved him, Oscar knew his own career was finished.
