Watching as Nash's calf continued to bleed profusely, Kenia trembled all over in fright.

She never expected that Rodger would actually fire a gun at his own son.

Moreover, Rodger had aimed his shot directly at Nash's leg. It was a wound that might very well impair his mobility for the rest of his life.

That single fact revealed how terrified Rodger truly was. He feared that Nash's reckless act might drag down the entire Holden family.

Marcus merely glanced at Nash with a cool indifference and said nothing.

Rodger wiped the sweat from his forehead and walked straight up to Marcus. He performed a crisp military salute and said respectfully, "I am Rodger Holden. Mr. Fitzgerald, good evening."

Marcus shot Rodger a brief look and asked flatly, "You are Nash's father?"

Rodger nodded and replied, "Yes, my son was far too arrogant and was so stupid to offend you. Mr. Fitzgerald, please spare him. I am prepared to accept any punishment."

But Marcus said, "You are also a senior officer. I cannot punish you. It is only your son who has clearly overstepped."

Still wiping the sweat from his brow, Rodger hurriedly nodded. "Yes, Mr. Fitzgerald, you are right. I have spoiled this child since he was little, which is why he has become so overbearing. If you will forgive him, I promise to discipline him properly so that he never misbehaves again."

Marcus replied coolly, "Your son's nature isn't all bad. He merely fell under the bad influence of a woman of poor character."

Hearing that, Rodger immediately realized who was truly at fault. His gaze turned icy as it fell upon Kenia.

Then he told Marcus, "I will follow your advice, Mr. Fitzgerald. From now on, I will personally supervise who my son dates. I will not let him fool around any longer!"

Seeing Rodger's sincere tone, Marcus decided not to make a further issue of it. He waved his hand dismissively and said, "Let's forget it. Take him away, or he may become a cripple."

Marcus hadn't expected Rodger to actually shoot his son.

Rodger was clearly desperate to gain Marcus' forgiveness, using everything at his disposal to do so.

Since Rodger, a military officer, had apologized so earnestly, Marcus was willing to show mercy.

Grateful for the reprieve, Rodger quickly expressed his thanks. At that moment, all traces of his usual commanding presence had vanished, replaced by sheer deference and groveling subservience.

Without delay, he ordered his men to lift Nash into the car and rush him to the hospital.

Afterward, Rodger bid farewell to Marcus and left with his entourage.

Everyone present felt a chill in their hearts at the sight.

Although they had long admired Marcus and knew that his position as Special Advisor carried considerable power, they never imagined that a military officer like Rodger would appear so abject in his presence.

They were astonished that Rodger, in his effort to save his son, would go so far as to shoot him.

They all knew that Nash was Rodger's only son.

It was obvious to everyone that Rodger was at his wit's end. He had resorted to this desperate measure in hopes that Marcus would show him mercy.

Rodger's fear of Marcus was palpable, and the incident only served to elevate Marcus' reputation further among the onlookers.

As Nash was taken away by his father, Kenia's face crumbled completely.

Nash was rescued by Rodger, but Kenia was left behind.

For a moment, Kenia had no idea what to do.

The mere thought of facing a military tribunal filled her with dread.

So overcome by panic, she lost control and wet herself, the sharp odor of urine wafting through the air.
