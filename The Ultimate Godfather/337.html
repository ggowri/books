Maxine couldn't comprehend it. The handgun she had just used was the latest model, capable of firing larger-caliber bullets.

Its power was no less than that of the newest submachine guns from Acoton.

Yet, even so, her shots against the cyborgs felt like mere tickles to them.

It was completely ineffective.

But Marcus' weapon was different. Two shots were enough to destroy a cyborg.

It was overwhelmingly powerful.

Shaun and Aiton were dumbfounded.

They could never have imagined that the cyborgs they had prided themselves on were so pitifully weak against Marcus.

With just a few casual shots, he had annihilated them. The destructive power of his weapon was absurd.

"If you have any more tricks, you'd better use them now. You won't get another chance," he taunted.

Despair painted Shaun's and Aiton's faces. They had no cards left to play.

"Useless fools, step aside. I'll take him on."

A sharp voice suddenly came from the distant horizon.

Moments later, two figures emerged from the forest nearby, their movements swift and precise.

They radiated immense magical power.

Removing their hats, they coldly looked at Marcus.

Marcus could feel that they were both wizards at level four.

Their magical power was solid and intense, clearly honed over years of practice. They were not far from breaking into level five.

"Ha-ha! I knew Mr. Roger Gellar wouldn't just sit by," Shaun laughed.

The arrival of the two wizards meant his life was saved and Marcus' death was imminent.

Aiton was equally excited. He recognized the two wizards.

They were associates of Roger and very powerful.

Either of them could easily destroy his eight cyborgs.

Together, they were invincible, even against Marcus' weapon.

Maxine panicked, not expecting such formidable foes.

Though she didn't know much about wizards, she could tell the two were far from ordinary.

"Are you here to die?" Marcus remained unflustered.

He glanced at the two wizards and stowed his weapon back into his storage ring.

"You're as arrogant as the rumors suggest," one of the wizards sneered. "Don't think that killing a few nobodies gives you the right to challenge us. Facing us, you're doomed."

Their investigation had shown Marcus to be at the Ancient Martial Stage Three.

Even if he could fight above his level, he couldn't beat them.

Marcus had no interest in exchanging words.

A bolt of lightning erupted from his hand, splitting into two as it shot toward their heads.

The two wizards frowned, not expecting Marcus to strike first.

They swiftly dodged to the side, narrowly avoiding the lightning.

With a roar, they retaliated, unleashing powerful fireballs that streaked toward Marcus.

The air sizzled as the temperature soared, the fire seemingly capable of melting everything in its path.

"Boy, Mr. Roger Gellar send us two powerful wizards to kill you. You could die with honor," one of them smirked.

He channeled his magic to its peak, aiming to obliterate Marcus in a single strike.

"Is that all you've got?" Marcus chuckled disdainfully, making no move to dodge.

Instead, he threw a punch.

It seemed like an ordinary punch, but within it roared the Thunderfire Power and the Timeless Numinous Energy.

The fusion of these forces brought the punch's power to an unprecedented peak.

A deafening explosion resounded.

Marcus' punch shattered the incoming fireballs instantly.

Instead of retreating, he used the Shadow Step to advance.

His figure blurred, turning into countless shadows.

In an instant, he appeared before one of the wizards, his fist blazing with terrifying energy.

The wizard was taken aback and hastily conjured a defensive spell.

In the next moment, an even more terrifying shockwave rang out.

Marcus' punch tore through the wizard's defense and slammed into his body.

The wizard screamed as his body was flung back like a cannonball, blood gushing from his mouth.

By the time he hit the ground, his internal organs had been pulverized. He was dead.
