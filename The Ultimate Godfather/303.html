Allan made a gesture for everyone to be quiet and answered the phone.

"Hello, Mr. Dawson, I'm out handling something. Is there something you need?"

A deep voice from a middle-aged man came through the other end of the line.

"Are you out handling things during working hours? Allan, do you even care about your job? How many times have I told you that your work attitude needs to be serious? The competition for your position is fierce. Since you're not listening, you don't have to come to work tomorrow!"

Allan's expression darkened, and he hurriedly explained, "Mr. Dawson, please listen. I asked for leave this morning, and to make sure it didn't affect my work, I finished everything for today, last Saturday. Have you forgotten?"

Although both of Allan's parents worked for the government, he had never relied on them. Allan had worked hard on his own to rise to his position.

Allan thought, "Years of hard work weren't easy, yet Mr. Dawson was now threatening to fire me for something trivial."

"I don't care. Your attitude towards work has been an issue lately. I've already reported this to the chairman, Mr. Dixon. Come in tomorrow to collect your salary, and then you can leave.

"Also, your wife's stall is being taken back too. You two can get lost!"

Aaron didn't want to listen to Allan's explanation and hung up the phone.

Allan's expression collapsed utterly. He felt like the sky had fallen, almost fainting from the shock.

"Allan, what happened? Why do you look so pale?"

Lisa noticed something was wrong and quickly asked Allan.

"I... I've been fired. Aaron also said he's taking back your stall, too!"

Allan said, his expression was full of misery.

"What?"

Lisa felt dizzy as well.

This news was like a bolt from the blue for both of them!

"Allan, you've been doing such a good job. Why would Mr. Dawson fire you? Since you've taken over, the wholesale market has been running smoothly. I'm sure Mr. Dixon has noticed that."

Lisa said with frustration, standing up for Allan.

"He's only firing me because he's got Mr. Dixon's approval. Otherwise, Aaron doesn't have the authority to do this."

Allan's face was grim.

Nick and Aria, as well as Richard and Melinda, had more or less understood what had happened, and their faces darkened as well after hearing their conversation.

"I don't understand why Mr. Dixon always sides with Aaron," Lisa said.

"If Allan gets fired, we're finished.

My stall will be taken back, too. We'll have no way of making money.

We still have nearly ten thousand in mortgage payments every month. I don't know what we're going to do," Lisa thought to herself.

"Aaron not only has a ten percent stake in the wholesale market, but he also gave his daughter to Mr. Dixon, that old pervert. Of course, Mr. Dixon favors him.

"If it weren't for this, the position of general manager would have been mine long ago!

"He's scheming to fire me because he has a nephew who recently left a foreign company's clothing business. Now he's eyeing my position as vice manager at the wholesale market, preparing to take over!" Allan growled.

After hearing Allan's words, Lisa's expression became even worse.

Lisa thought, "So Aaron is holding onto the general manager position through favors.

He's trying to push his nephew into the position, greedy and insatiable."

"Allan, what should we do? Do we just let Aaron get away with it?"

Lisa said, her face filled with dissatisfaction.

At this moment, she felt that the world was terribly unfair.

She also felt a deep sense of helplessness.

"Aaron has the chairman backing him. I really can't do anything about it. It looks like I'll just go collect my pay and leave tomorrow."

Allan sighed, his expression firming up with resolve.

"Lisa, don't worry. Even if I leave this company, I'll apply to other companies and work hard to support you and take care of our parents!"

Allan really had a sense of responsibility.

And it was clear that he genuinely cared for Lisa, and this care extended to Nick and Aria as well.

"What's the name of your wholesale market?"

At that moment, Marcus' voice came through.

Everyone froze for a moment.

Allan replied, "Builder Market."

Marcus raised an eyebrow, thinking to himself, "Builder Market... That must be something from the 1980s."

It was established when Golden Group was first founded.

It once contributed to a third of Golden Group's overall revenue.

As Golden Group diversified and expanded its business,

Builder Market gradually faded from their focus.

Even so, Builder Market remained a pillar of Golden Group.
