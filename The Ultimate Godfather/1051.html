Chapter 951 Callum Finch

Stanley didn't waste a second. He tapped into the magic manual he'd been training with, a faint shimmer of power rippling around him.

He funneled it straight into his fist, juicing it up until it felt like a whole new beast.

With a guttural roar, he swung hard, unleashing a blazing punch straight at Marcus.

The guy was built like a tank—broad shoulders, ripped from head to toe.

He'd boxed for years before this; even without the magic, most punks couldn't get within arm's reach of him.

Now, with that mystical edge boosting his swing, it hit like a pro fighter's—damn near Warrior-level.

No regular one could take that kind of heat.

That's why Stanley didn't give Marcus a second thought.

"See that, punk?" he barked mid-swing, cranking the power even higher, aiming to end it in one shot. "This is what I can do with a flick of my wrist. Let's see you try—"

Marcus just smirked, cool as ice. He didn't dodge, didn't flinch—just hauled off and slapped Stanley clean across the face.

Crack. The sound cut through the air.

Marcus' hand blew right past Stanley's glowing fist like it was nothing, landing square on his cheek.

Stanley let out a howl, his bulk flying sideways.

He spun through the air a good few times before crashing down hard, his face already swelling purple, blood and broken teeth spilling out. He looked like roadkill.

Everett and Chloe stood there, mouths hanging open. They couldn't believe Marcus had taken Stanley apart that easy—like swatting a fly.

One smack and the guy were a mess. Marcus was on another level.

"What, this your big move?" Marcus said, his voice dripping with mockery as he eyed Stanley's sprawled-out wreck.

Stanley clutched his busted face, rolling on the ground, groans turning into full-on wails.

It took him a solid minute to stagger back up, glaring at Marcus through the pain. "You've got some nerve, hitting me like that. Who the hell are you?"

Even as cocky as he was, Stanley knew now—Marcus wasn't just some nobody. No regular guy could pull that off.

"The one running Serpent's Coil," Marcus said, flat and matter-of-fact.

Stanley blinked, then barked out a laugh, all scorn. "Oh, you're full of it now, huh?

"You know who's really in charge of Serpent's Coil? Mr. Fitzgerald's a legend—you think you can name-drop him like that? You're digging your own grave, punk!"

"Mr. Fitzgerald?" Chloe caught the name from a few feet away, her mind spinning.

Same last name as Marcus. No way it's that much of a coincidence.

Stanley didn't notice her muttering. He yanked a dagger from his belt and lunged, the blade flashing toward Marcus' chest with lethal intent.

Marcus didn't even blink—just slapped him again, this time on the other cheek.

The hit ignored the knife, sending Stanley spinning back to the pavement with an even louder scream, clutching his face as he writhed.

He dragged himself up, eyes blazing. "You're dead, punk! I'm getting our boss—see how you like it when he tears you apart!"

With that, he stumbled off, propped up by his goons.

Everett saw the tide turning and bolted after them.

Chloe's stomach sank. This was spiraling fast. "Marcus, they're going for Serpent's Coil's top dog. Maybe we should lay low?

"I've heard they've taken over Solomia's underground scene lately—biggest crew around.

"If their boss shows up with his heavy hitters, what if something goes wrong?"

She hadn't signed up for this.

It started as a family spat—now it was Marcus versus Serpent's Coil.

If he got hurt because of her, she'd never forgive herself.

"Relax," Marcus said, unfazed. "I've been waiting for their boss. I want to know who's letting them run wild with drugs."

Chloe's brows shot up.

"Wait—don't tell me you're actually the head of Serpent's Coil."

She'd thought he was joking before.

Serpent's Coil was Solomia's top underworld outfit—Marcus had never even hinted at a connection.

"Have I ever lied to you?" he said with a sly grin.

Chloe's eyes flickered, but she stayed quiet.

He was dead-set on facing them down.

Another fight was coming, and all she could do was hope he'd come out on top.

Not long after, Stanley and Everett rolled back in, with a pack of Serpent's Coil's muscle in tow.

Leading them was a lean guy in his early thirties—short, but built like he lived in the gym, every inch of him screaming pro athlete. Callum Finch, the boss Stanley had been yapping about.

"Who's the dumbass that roughed you up?" Callum's voice carried weight, his strut oozing confidence as he glanced at Stanley.

Stanley's face was a swollen mess—barely recognizable after Marcus' double whammy.

He jabbed a finger at Marcus. "That's the guy, Callum. Don't let him off easy, or Serpent's Coil's gonna be a laughingstock!"

Callum turned toward Marcus, ready to unload a string of curses—then froze mid-breath.

His eyes locked on Marcus' face, widening in disbelief.

"Callum, this clown's been running his mouth, saying he's Serpent's Coil's big shot," Stanley pressed, annoyed at the delay. "If we've got a boss like him, we're screwed. Take him out already!"

Smack! Callum's hand cracked across Stanley's face before he could finish.

The hit was brutal, piling onto Stanley's already wrecked mug.

He crumpled, howling, and stared up in shock. "Callum, why me? I didn't screw up—it's that guy you should be hitting!"

But Callum's fury only spiked.

He stormed over and laid into Stanley—fists and boots flying, each blow landing like a sledgehammer.

In seconds, Stanley was a bruised heap, squealing like a stuck pig. "Callum, talk to me! There's gotta be a mix-up—don't do this!"
