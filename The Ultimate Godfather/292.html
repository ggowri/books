One of the soul lamps belonged to Asher and caused Phoenix's expression to darken and he rose from his futon.

The shattering of the soul lamps signified the deaths of the three individuals.

"How could this happen? Asher had reached Ancient Martial Stage Five. Who could have killed him?" Phoenix exclaimed.

Given Asher's strength, few in the world of Ancient Martial Art in Uswad could pose a threat, aside from ancient masters beyond the Foundation Establishment level.

He thought, "Can it have been an external force, a Wizard family, or a powerful metahuman?"

Phoenix was filled with questions.

He recalled that Asher had left the Astral Serpent Order the previous day to seek revenge for his son, Truman, and targeted only a junior at the Ancient Martial Stage Four.

Even Asher's two companions should have easily handled the situation.

Therefore, Phoenix thought Marcus could not threaten Asher.

The only explanation for his death would be a formidable force backing Marcus.

This realization deepened Phoenix's concerns.

A young man living in the mundane world may be less than twenty-five, and his cultivation had already reached Ancient Martial Stage Four.

With such a cultivation, even in the world of Ancient Martial Art, he should be a genius.

Such a talent must be supported by a powerful figure.

When Asher tried to go to the mundane world to avenge Truman, Phoenix opposed him. He was afraid of the consequences of angering Marcus' mysterious backing.

Now, with Asher dead, the murderer was likely tied to that very power.

At the thought of these, Phoenix's expression darkened.

The grim thoughts overshadowed his earlier joy.

He instructed his subordinates, "Investigate Asher's death in the mundane world, but do not disturb Marcus to avoid alarming him."

"Yes, Mr. Morris!" an Astral Serpent Order expert responded.

Asher's death quickly spread throughout the Astral Serpent Order.

This news hit Truman, whose cultivation had already been abolished, like a thunderbolt.

Without cultivation, he was struggling.

Now that his father was dead, he had no backer in the Astral Serpent Order, and his survival would be challenging.

The world of Ancient Martial Art was extremely ruthless, where strength was everything.

People without strength were doomed to be eliminated.

Little did he know that his father's death was indirectly due to him.

If he hadn't clashed with Marcus in the Southern Market, Asher wouldn't have sought revenge, leading to his own demise.

Cause and effect were inescapable, shaping the fates of both Asher and Truman.

Meanwhile, Marcus was unaware of these developments as he returned to the Bayside Villa.

Diana emerged from the villa, startled to see him covered in blood.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, did you encounter a strong enemy?" she asked.

Marcus nodded gently and didn't say much.

As time went on, he felt that his injury was getting heavier and heavier.

He hurried to his room, swallowed several healing pills, and began practicing the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire to mend his wounds.

Asher deserved to be an expert in the peak of Ancient Martial Stage Five, and his attack was too harmful.

Using internal vision during his recovery, Marcus discovered that his injuries had affected his organs and major meridians.

Previously, he had masked the injuries by devouring energy from Asher, but they resurfaced with time.

Marcus was extremely weak. If he hadn't quickly taken a dozen healing pills, he would have fainted.

As the pills took effect, he breathed a sigh of relief and managed to use the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire.

Plumes of Numinous Energy, combined with the medicine, spread throughout his body, gradually repairing his injuries.

This process was slow. Marcus spent three days in the room, doing almost nothing and focusing entirely on his healing.

After that, he recovered eighty percent of his injuries, while the remaining twenty percent would require more time to mend.
