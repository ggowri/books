Chapter 579 Penthouse Dreams and Doubts

Rodney let out a dry chuckle, his skepticism clear as day.

Gina turned her sharp gaze toward Marcus, sizing him up with thinly veiled contempt. "Catherine, so you've got yourself a boyfriend now? And you didn't tell me? I could've helped vet him for you! Guys like him? They're a dime a dozen these days—full of empty promises. Are you seriously trying to tell me he got you a ten-million-dollar job? Come on, Catherine."

Her words carried a heavy dose of sarcasm, practically daring Marcus to prove her wrong.

To Gina, it was laughable—her younger cousin spinning fairy tales to make her life sound more glamorous than it was.

"Don't worry about it, Gina," Catherine replied, her tone soft but resolute. "Marcus treats me wonderfully. In fact, this house is his gift to me."

She smiled warmly, unfazed by Gina's jabs.

Rodney and Gina exchanged a quick look, their shared amusement evident in their eyes.

"So," Gina asked, her tone as sharp as her gaze, "which house did you pick?"

Rodney had chosen Dragon Crest One for Gina specifically because of its proximity to the Sync Group's Solomia office.

He'd already paid for the smallest unit available—a modest 200-square-meter apartment.

The location wasn't ideal, and the floor left much to be desired, but at 15 million dollars, it was still a significant expense for their family.

If not for Gina's relentless persuasion, Rodney wouldn't have spent more than five million dollars.

Catherine instinctively pointed to the penthouse, perched like a crown jewel in the heart of the community. "That one. Marcus says it's the best unit in the complex. You can see nearly all of Solomia from there. My parents have spent their lives in a cramped factory apartment. I want them to finally enjoy some comfort."

Her words hung in the air, drawing stunned silence before Rodney and Gina both broke into laughter.

"Catherine," Gina said, wiping an imaginary tear, "I agree—it's a fantastic unit. But have you seen the price tag? I just asked. That penthouse is 30 million dollars.

"Dad's buying me the smallest place here, and even that's 15 million dollars.

"Be real, Catherine. Your family's situation? We all know it. Who in your house has 30 million dollars lying around?"

Rodney joined in, shaking his head. "You're too much like your father. All dreams, no reality. When I started my business, I wanted to bring him on board, but no—he was too busy drinking and gambling. Now look at him—pushing fifty and still spinning his wheels. And your poor mother's the one paying the price.

"Catherine don't end up like him. Be realistic. If Gina settles in at Sync Group, maybe she can get you a job there. It might not pay as much as hers, but at least it's stable."

Catherine smiled again, seemingly unbothered by their remarks. "You're right—I don't have that kind of money. Marcus is the one paying for it."

Rodney and Gina's laughter stopped abruptly, replaced by matching expressions of disbelief.

"Him?" Rodney asked, pointing at Marcus. "Where's he getting that kind of cash?"

Marcus, calm as ever, pulled a sleek card from his pocket and handed it to the sales associate. "Please process the payment," he said with a polite nod.

"Please wait a second, sir."

The associate smiled professionally and walked off toward the payment desk.

Moments later, the clear chime of a successful transaction echoed through the room.

Rodney and Gina's jaws dropped in unison, their disbelief palpable.

Thirty million dollars—paid in full.

Turns out, this young man wasn't as ordinary as he seemed at first glance.

But Gina's sharp eyes zeroed in on his card, and a knowing smirk tugged at her lips. "That's a credit card," she thought.

"So, the 30 million dollars? Probably maxed out on credit," she concluded, her tone internally dripping with amusement.

"Gina, who'd have guessed this guy had such deep pockets?" Rodney said, throwing her a glance filled with mock surprise.

"Dad," she said quietly, "that's a credit card. The 30 million dollars? It's borrowed money."

Still, a small flicker of intrigue lit up Gina's mind. What kind of guy walks around with a credit card limit that high?

What neither of them realized was that Marcus' card wasn't a credit card at all.

It was a Supreme Card issued by a state-owned bank, reserved exclusively for accounts with deposits exceeding 100 billion dollars.

Marcus had it because of over 100 billion dollars in his account.

The card's design resembled a credit card, leading to Gina's misjudgment.

"A credit card, I see. Kids these days are living like kings on borrowed money. Sure, it feels great now, but let's see how he feels when the bill comes due."

Rodney sighed dramatically, shaking his head in faux pity.
