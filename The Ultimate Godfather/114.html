Chapter 113 Goddaughter

David trembled, her voice shaking as she said, "M-Mr. Fitzgerald... I had no idea you held such an esteemed status. Please forgive me..."

This preschool was previously part of an early education company managed by Noah.

Technically, it also belonged to the Godfather's network.

If Marcus could get Noah removed with just a word, how could she possibly fare any better?

"If you're so loyal to Noah, then you can join him!" Marcus sneered.

David's face was filled with despair. She was about to beg further when one of the aides interrupted, "Didn't you hear Mr. Fitzgerald? You're fired too. Get lost!"

David looked dazed.

He had worked at this preschool for many years and enjoyed excellent benefits.

If he was fired, it was impossible to find another position with the same perks elsewhere.

At that moment, his heart filled with regret.

He cursed his own shortsightedness. If he hadn't been so biased and sided with Noah against Marcus, he wouldn't have ended up like this.

"Good... Serves him right to be fired!"

The crowd erupted in cheers.

Most of the cheering came from the children's parents.

Many of them had experienced unfair treatment from David.

However, because the preschool's teaching quality was exceptional, most parents had endured it in silence.

But that didn't mean they didn't resent him.

Now, seeing David fired, their pent-up frustrations were finally released.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, is there anything else you need? If so, we're here to serve you," one of the aides asked.

The lead aide directed the question to Marcus.

Marcus waved with a smile. "That's all."

The aides exchanged a few pleasantries with Marcus before leaving.

"Marcus is amazing..." Leia cheered, her little fists clenched in excitement.

Marcus picked Leia up in one swoop and turned to Josie, who was still in shock. "Ms. Shelman, we can leave now."

Josie nodded stiffly, her mind involuntarily speculating about Marcus's true identity.

They got into Josie's Mercedes and soon arrived at Sunshine Estates.

Josie smiled and said, "To welcome you properly, Mr. Fitzgerald, I'll cook dinner myself tonight."

Marcus didn't refuse. He didn't know many people in Kingston, and having dinner with Josie sounded like a good idea.

Besides, it had been a while since they'd last seen each other.

They bought a variety of groceries at the local fresh market, and once they got home, Josie started cooking.

Marcus kept Leia company.

He had never been fond of kids, but he had a soft spot for Leia.

She wasn't spoiled like most kids her age and was much more mature.

Leia seemed to adore Marcus too, and they had a great time together.

In less than an hour, Josie had prepared a table full of delicious dishes.

"Mom, I want Marcus to be my dad..." Leia said with childlike innocence.

Josie's cheeks flushed. "Leia, what nonsense are you saying? Marcus can't be your dad!"

"No, no, I want Marcus to be my dad..." Leia protested, her eyes welling with tears.

Seeing her mom's stern reaction, Leia's eyes turned red, and she started crying.

Marcus quickly stepped forward to console her. "Leia, don't cry. How about I be your dad?"

Leia instantly stopped crying, her face lighting up with a pure, childlike smile. "Okay! Marcus is my dad now! I finally have a dad!"

Watching Marcus and Leia interact so naturally, Josie felt a little dazed. At that moment, she wished time could stop and stay frozen here forever.

Marcus didn't just provide her with an overwhelming sense of security—he gave her a feeling of home. Josie suddenly realized she might not be able to let this man go...

"Ms. Shelman, Leia will be my goddaughter from now on. You don't mind, do you?" Marcus asked.

Snapping out of her thoughts, Josie replied, "Of course, I don't mind. Having someone like Mr. Fitzgerald as Leia's godfather is a blessing!"

The three of them enjoyed a warm and cheerful dinner together.

Leia fell asleep soon after.

Marcus approached Josie and asked, "Ms. Shelman, have you been feeling unwell lately?"

Josie's cheeks turned crimson as a certain memory crossed her mind. "No, not at all. Since your last treatment, I've felt amazing. Not only is my pain gone, but even my skin has improved so much...

"And also..."

She trailed off halfway, struggling to continue.

"What else?" Marcus pressed, curious.

Marcus asked, intrigued.

"It's just that... during my ovulation period, I feel particularly impulsive, like I want to do things I wouldn't even have dared to imagine before..." Josie finally blurted out, summoning her courage.
