Aquila ignored Marcus' attack once again, closing in on him with crushing force.

"Die!"

Aquila roared, unleashing another blast of black flames from his mouth, this time laced with a horrifying, corrosive liquid.

In an instant, the flames enveloped Marcus' body.

This attack was deadly. Even someone at the peak of Ancient Martial Art Stage Seven would struggle to resist it.

But what made it even more lethal wasn't just the flames. It was the liquid mixed within them—poisonous and corrosive.

For an expert in Ancient Martial Art, this was a death sentence.

"Celestial Reversal!"

Marcus didn't bother trying to fight back.

He released the Celestial Reversal technique, and in an instant, the terrifying black flames and corrosive liquid were reflected back toward Aquila.

Though Aquila was still agile, his massive frame and larger target made him vulnerable.

The rebound attack caught him off guard, and his body was immediately consumed by the black flames.

The deadly liquid poured over him, spreading across his body like a nightmare.

"Ahh..."

Aquila let out a tortured scream.

The combination of the corrosive liquid and the black flames burned away large chunks of his flesh.

His skin sizzled, and green blood oozed from the open wounds, sending a foul, rancid stench into the air.

Marcus' eyes sharpened, and the Heavenly Circle Diagram flared to life.

Lines of intricate runes formed in his palm, cascading down and enveloping Aquila's body.

The formation was powerful and highly restrictive, quickly binding Aquila in place.

No matter how hard he struggled, he couldn't break free. He could only watch helplessly as the corrosive liquid devoured his body.

"You insignificant worm, you should have died long ago!"

Aquila howled, his body cracking and snapping as if ready to explode.

The force of his fury twisted the lines of the Heavenly Circle Diagram.

With a deafening explosion, the formation shattered, and Aquila launched himself toward Marcus in a berserk attack.

One massive claw swiped at him, slashing through the air.

Marcus countered with a fist ray, but the impact forced him to stagger back.

In the same moment, Aquila opened his enormous jaws and lunged at Marcus with terrifying speed, aiming to swallow him whole.

Marcus dodged with a quick sidestep, narrowly avoiding the attack.

Then, before Aquila could react, Marcus' hand shot out, seizing Aquila's wrist. Devouring Power surged from his palm, erupting into Aquila's body.

Aquila's face contorted with shock and pain as he tried to break free, but he felt his energy draining rapidly, as though a dam had broken inside him.

His vision blurred, his strength faded. In a matter of moments, his frantic struggles ceased, and his once-burning rage turned into empty, glassy-eyed despair.

The Devouring Power intensified, and Aquila's massive muscles began to shrink, his bones cracking back into their original shape.

Within moments, his hulking form returned to its normal state. His body collapsed to the ground, twitching violently as his life force drained away.

"You've got to be kidding me. I've devoured every ounce of energy in your body, and you're still alive?"

Marcus stared at Aquila's twitching form on the ground, disbelief flickering across his face.

Until now, anyone who had been hit by his Devouring Power had turned into nothing more than a dried-out husk—no exceptions.

Yet this Aquila... somehow, wasn't dead.

Mutants were truly terrifying.

"How... How could you possibly drain my energy? What the hell are you?" Aquila's voice trembled with disbelief.

As a mutant, he had always believed his energy control was beyond anything ordinary, something grotesque.

But after Marcus had drained him, he felt like a child playing with fire.

"You don't deserve to know," Marcus sneered coldly. Then, with a sharp edge to his voice, he demanded, "Where is Josie?"

Aquila, shaking, managed to croak, "You've slaughtered everyone here. Your woman might be the only one left alive. Why are you still asking me?"

Marcus' brow furrowed. A burst of flame erupted from him, engulfing Aquila's body.

A howl of agony ripped through the air, and in moments, Aquila was nothing but ash.

Without a second glance, Marcus reached out with his mind, sweeping the entire Black Hawk Legion headquarters with his spiritual senses.

It didn't take long for him to pinpoint Josie's location.

Shadow Step activated. Marcus moved like a blur, heading straight for her.

Meanwhile, Josie remained bound to a pillar in the courtyard, her stunning face etched with worry.

The constant explosions from outside told her that Marcus was undoubtedly engaged in a deadly battle.

"Josie, are you okay?"

Suddenly, a familiar voice reached her ears. Her eyes flickered with hope, and she quickly looked up.

The sight of Marcus walking toward her, step by step, sent a rush of disbelief through her. She could barely believe her eyes.

"Marcus... is it really you?"

Josie's voice trembled, the weight of her fear making it hard to trust her senses. She thought she must be hallucinating.

"It's me," Marcus said simply, nodding as multiple beams of destructive energy shot out, slicing through the ropes that bound her.

Relief flooded through him when he saw she was still alive. Without thinking, he pulled her into his arms, his heart swelling with emotion.

"Marcus... I was so scared... so scared I'd never see you again. Now that you're here... I'm so happy..."

Tears streamed down Josie's face as she sobbed, overwhelmed by the sheer relief of being reunited with him.
