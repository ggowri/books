The night was alive in the Mountaintop Villa District, where an all-night party raged in a luxurious manor.

By the pool, Anthony lounged shirtless on a massive couch, rocking the beach boy look. Two blondes were draped over him.

His hands roamed their chests freely, making all kinds of dirty noises.

It was a far cry from the composed, powerful figure he cut during the day.

"That Marcus has caught the Godfather's attention. Mr. Roger Gellar wants him gone!"

Laird Sable, decked out in a light suit and shades, downed his wine before dropping this bomb on Anthony.

Anthony shoved the girls aside, a look of surprise crossing his face. "What? That loser Marcus is connected to the Godfather?"

Laird nodded. "Mr. Gellar's digging shows Marcus is probably the Godfather's bastard son. If he lives, he'll challenge Mr. Gellar's inheritance. He needs to die."

Anthony smirked. "Don't worry about it. I've already sent Falcon to kill him before Mr. Gellar even gave the command. By my plans, he should be dead now."

Laird nodded approvingly. "That's the best. I'll tell Mr. Gellar about your initiative. I'm sure he'll reward you handsomely."

Anthony hastily brushed a hand through his hair. "Thank you very much, sir!" he exclaimed.

Clearly pleased with himself, he pulled the two beauties back into his arms, his hands wandering to their sweet spots.

But just as the party was heating up again, an icy voice cut in. "You won't be getting any reward."

Anthony and Laird sobered up instantly. They turned to find the source.

When they laid eyes on Marcus, shock rippled through them.

"You... you're not dead?" Anthony stared in disbelief, rubbing his eyes to erase the scene before him.

Falcon is a trained warrior, and his skills far surpass those of ordinary men.

Most of my bodyguards couldn't beat him, even in groups.

No way should Marcus have survived his hit!

How the hell is he still breathing?

He will ruin all my plans!

Marcus walked toward him. "You thought a few killers could take me out? Dream on."

Anthony trembled at his words. Each of Marcus' steps felt like a death knell.

"Somebody! Kill him, now!" Anthony screamed, panic lacing his voice.

A pack of armed guards rushed forward, ready to take action.

But Marcus didn't even slow down. He kept moving like a man on a mission.

The guards who attacked dropped in seconds, their blood splashing across the white marble pool deck. The air grew thick with the metallic scent of death.

"What do you want?"

Facing Marcus' deadly presence, Anthony's earlier bravado melted into pure fear.

Marcus slid into the seat next to him. Without a word, he pulled out a contract and slammed it down. "Sign it."

Anthony's eyes went wide as he read the document. It was an equity transfer contract.

"You want my 25% equity? Hell no!" he yelled, jerking back.

Marcus' patience was wearing thin. He grabbed Anthony's wrist and pinned it to the table.

Before Anthony could move, Marcus picked up a wine bottle and brought it crashing down.

CRACK!

Anthony's hand was smashed into a bloody pulp. The pain was unbearable, his scream piercing the night.

Marcus leaned in close, his voice a deadly whisper. "Want to sign now?"
