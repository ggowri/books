Chapter 166 The Surgery Was a Success

Everyone was shocked, thinking, "What is going on?

Has Professor Cooper just lost control?

Why did he bow to an outsider and act so humble?

This is simply unbelievable!"

The young doctor thought Robert had lost his mind. He couldn't help but mutter to him, "Professor Cooper, this diagram looks like a complete mess. It has nothing to do with medical practice. Did you make a mistake?"

Everyone turned to Robert, puzzled.

Brenden, especially, was worried that Robert might have really lost his mind or was seeing things, which could lead to a disastrous surgery outcome.

After all, if that happened, Edward would be done for!

"I told you to shut up before, didn't I? Why are you speaking now?"

However, the young doctor's words seemed to have triggered Robert completely.

His face darkened with cold fury, "Tomorrow, you'll be on outpatient duty. Don't follow me anymore. I don't need a student like you!"

Hearing that, the young doctor felt the ground spinning beneath him. He nearly collapsed.

He had only gotten this opportunity to study under Robert through connections and the last of his parents' savings.

Now, all that was lost because of his careless comment!

He couldn't understand what had happened to Robert and why he was so obsessed with this messy drawing.

"What are you all looking at? If anyone dares to speak out again, you'll end up like him!"

Robert swept his gaze over the other young doctors.

Marcus' diagram had opened the door to a whole new world for Robert!

After looking at it, problems that had plagued him for years were suddenly solved!

To Robert, this seemingly messy diagram was more precious than gold.

It was worth more than any high-level medical theory out there!

"Mr. Fitzgerald, can we begin the surgery now?"

Robert forced himself to calm his excitement and turned to Marcus, speaking as a subordinate would to his superior.

"Yes, we can begin."

Marcus nodded casually.

Only after receiving Marcus' approval did Robert dare to prepare for the surgery.

This scene left Benjamin, Brenden, and Maxine stunned.

They couldn't quite process what was going on. They couldn't figure out what was happening between them.

All they could do was wait and watch, their minds in a haze.

"The surgery will take about four hours. I'll be on my way now."

Marcus said his goodbyes to Benjamin, Brenden, and Maxine as he left the clinic.

He had more important things to do and wasn't about to wait around.

He believed with his guidance and Robert's years of clinical experience, Edward's recovery was almost certain.

"Alright, then I'll walk you out."

Maxine nodded but wasn't overly insistent.

"No need. I can go on my own."

Marcus shook his head and left without another word.

Watching his retreating figure, Brenden turned to Maxine, asking, "Maxine, do you think Mr. Fitzgerald really knows medicine?"

Maxine shook her head. "He never mentioned it before, and I'm not sure. But seeing how Professor Cooper reacted, maybe he does know a thing or two..."

Despite her words, Maxine's heart still felt uneasy.

After all, Edward was her instructor, and she didn't want him to be crippled for the rest of his life.

Brenden sighed, not holding out much hope.

Believing that the young man, in his twenties, had easily defeated a half-mechanical person was already a stretch for him. However, it was even more difficult for him to accept that the same young man had knowledge of medicine.

Brenden had mentally prepared himself for the surgery to fail.

As Marcus had predicted, the surgery lasted a full four hours.

Benjamin hadn't left, still waiting outside.

During that time, Brenden sighed frequently, filled with guilt for Edward.

Maxine was clearly tense as well.

When the surgery door finally opened, the two of them rushed forward.

Brenden spoke first, his voice tinged with worry, "Professor Cooper, how did it go? You don't have to feel guilty; you did your best. Even if Edward has to spend the rest of his life in a wheelchair, our academy will compensate him well so that he'll have no worries."

At those words, Maxine's hopes gradually faded. She could feel the flicker of hope inside her extinguishing.

"What guilt? Brenden, what are you talking about? The surgery was a success!"

Robert pulled off his mask, clearly bewildered by Brenden's strange attitude.

With Marcus' insightful guidance, he had never thought the surgery would fail.

But Robert's words didn't bring much relief to them.

Brenden seemed to be in disbelief, shaking his head, "Professor Cooper, I already prepared myself for the surgery to fail. Please, just tell me the truth. No need to comfort me with lies!"
