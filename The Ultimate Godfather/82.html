Chapter 81 Place an Order

The young secretary stopped a chubby, balding man outside the office.

This man was Walter Wollaston, the Deputy general manager of AquaVital Pharmaceuticals in Kingston.

AquaVital Pharmaceuticals, based in Kingston, was part of the influential Wollaston family and ranked among the top three pharmaceutical companies in the city.

It was a true industry leader.

Backed by the influential Brown family, AquaVital Pharmaceuticals had seen its profits soar in recent years, its sales skyrocketing as it aggressively expanded its operations.

The company had recently acquired land near Kingston's border to construct its third regional branch.

"What kind of so-called 'distinguished guest' could possibly be more important than AquaVital Pharmaceuticals?" Walter sneered arrogantly. "Do you know that a single order from us could keep BioFusion Pharmaceuticals busy for half a year?"

The secretary flustered, tried to explain. "The guest arrived earlier, and according to protocol, Ms. Shelman must attend to them first. Please wait patiently, Mr. Wollaston."

Walter, clearly having lost his temper, slapped the secretary across the face.

Her cheek flushed red, swelling almost instantly, as tears welled up in her eyes. "Mr. Wollaston, why did you hit me?" she cried.

"Why?" Walter growled, his voice full of disdain. "Because you're in my way! Your boss has no respect for AquaVital. The last time I came, she was busy with another client. Now, again, she's making me wait! This is nothing short of deliberate disrespect!"

As he finished, Walter shoved the secretary aside and stormed towards Josie's office.

Inside, Josie, hearing the commotion in the hallway, furrowed her brow. Turning to Marcus, she said, "Mr. Fitzgerald, let me handle this situation. Please wait a moment."

Not wanting to disturb Marcus, Josie left to intercept Walter.

"Well, Ms. Shelman, you certainly make it difficult for someone like me to see you," Walter sneered as she appeared.

His eyes roved over her with undisguised greed, lingering on her poised figure.

As one of the most prominent young businesswomen in Kingston, Josie was often the target of unwanted attention, and Walter had long harbored less-than-professional intentions towards her.

Feeling disgusted by his lecherous gaze, Josie maintained a polite but distant smile. "Mr. Wollaston, I was in the middle of assisting a client. If there's been any oversight, I hope you can understand."

"Oh? And what kind of client could possibly rival AquaVital Pharmaceuticals?" Walter retorted.

He moved as if to barge into Josie's office, but she quickly stepped in front of him.

"We at BioFusion Pharmaceuticals prioritize client confidentiality," she said firmly. "Please respect that, Mr. Wollaston."

But Walter only scoffed, shoving past her to push the door open.

Inside, seeing a young man seated calmly, Walter's face twisted with contempt. "This is the 'distinguished guest' you mentioned? This kid?"

Josie replied calmly, "This is Marcus Fitzgerald, a senior executive from Golden Group in Solomia."

Walter's eyes narrowed with a mix of disdain and calculation. "Golden Group may be somewhat notable in Solomia, but in Kingston, they're nothing. And he's just an executive? How could he possibly compare to me?"

Josie's dislike for Walter deepened. Not only was the man arrogant and unreasonable, but his lack of professionalism was appalling.

Josie couldn't understand why AquaVital Pharmaceuticals would hire someone like Walter. "Mr. Wollaston," she said coldly, "at BioFusion Pharmaceuticals, we treat all clients equally. I hope you can understand that."

"Equal treatment?" Walter laughed derisively. "Ms. Shelman, let's not pretend. AquaVital Pharmaceuticals is in urgent need of expansion. I'm here to place an order for ten top-of-the-line production lines. I expect them delivered in one month!"

Josie frowned slightly. "Mr. Wollaston, I've just signed a contract with Mr. Fitzgerald for ten production lines as well. Our workers are already working overtime to meet the three-month timeline we agreed upon. Your order will have to wait."

Walter's face darkened. "With the business AquaVital brings you, shouldn't we be prioritized? I suggest you delay other orders and focus on ours first!"

Josie shook her head firmly. "That's not possible. Business must be conducted on a first-come, first-served basis. I signed Mr. Fitzgerald's contract first, and I will honor it."

Walter scowled. "So, you're saying you're not giving us any special consideration?"

"I'm afraid not," Josie replied apologetically.

Turning his glare to Marcus, Walter sneered. "Listen, kid. Push your order back and let ours take priority. Do that, and I'll make it worth your while."

Marcus raised an eyebrow, feigning interest. "And how much are you offering?"

Walter, thinking he'd found a weak spot, smirked. "Ten million. How's that? A company exec like you probably earns two or three million a year. Ten million is more than enough for you to live comfortably for years. Any smart man would take it."

Walter was not only the vice president of AquaVital Pharmaceuticals, but his mother's maiden name was Brown, making her a distant relative of the prominent Brown family from Kingston.

Additionally, Walter held shares in AquaVital Pharmaceuticals.

With his influence, he could easily allocate ten million to Marcus.

In Walter's eyes, Marcus would surely be tempted by the offer of such money.

Josie glanced at Marcus, her heart sinking slightly.

Could he really be tempted by money?

If so, why had he refused the previous offer of 2.5 million?

"Too little," Marcus said flatly.

Walter's eyes gleamed. "Name your price. Twenty million, then. But I won't go any higher. Opportunities like this don't come twice."

Marcus smiled faintly and held up five fingers.
