Heath let out a blood-curdling scream, collapsing to the ground and writhing in agony.

The groin, packed with sensitive nerves, was one of the most painful places to be struck.

It was even more painful than giving birth.

The searing pain was so intense that Heath nearly passed out.

His first thought was that he was finished.

Such a devastating blow meant he would never function as a man again.

No more women, no chance of fathering children.

For a man, this was a punishment worse than death.

Marcus had said earlier that nobody could leave intact if they dared provoke him.

In Heath's case, Marcus didn't show any mercy and destroyed his most important part.

Gustavo and the remaining bodyguards instinctively clutched their groins, horrified by what they had just witnessed.

No one expected Marcus to actually kick Heath in such a brutal way.

"You bastard!" Heath growled through clenched teeth, his voice trembling with pain.

"How dare you kick me there! I swear, I'll destroy you!"

Marcus ignored his threats and walked over to Kiera, his tone calm. "Where do you want to go? I'll take you there."

Kiera, still reeling from what she had just witnessed, was stunned.

She hadn't expected Marcus to strike so ruthlessly.

Most importantly, he had harmed Heath, a member of Kingston's Deleon family.

This wasn't just a fight. Marcus had crossed paths with the most powerful family in Uswad, and all because of her.

If the Deleon family learned about this, they wouldn't let it slip.

"Why are you standing there? Let's go," Marcus reminded her gently.

Snapping out of her daze, Kiera quickly followed Marcus.

As the two walked away, Gustavo finally dared to move, rushing over to help Heath to his feet.

Heath had already fainted from the pain, so Gustavo and the surviving bodyguards hurried to get him to the hospital.

In the Aston Martin, Marcus glanced at Kiera and asked, "You haven't told me where you want to go."

Still shaken, Kiera answered quietly, "Home. Take me to Peaceful Apartments."

Living alone due to her job, Kiera rented a modest apartment in an older complex.

While not dilapidated, the complex was old and offered cheap rent, just 1.5 thousand dollars a month for a one-bedroom unit.

It was the affordability that had drawn her there.

Marcus adjusted the car's direction and began driving toward Peaceful Apartments.

Along the way, the two chatted casually, and Marcus learned about Kiera's relationship with Gustavo.

They had been childhood friends, growing up together.

But while Kiera went to college, Gustavo fell into the life of a petty thug.

Despite this, their shared history had created a bond, and Gustavo's relentless pursuit eventually convinced Kiera to date him.

Gustavo had promised to turn his life around and find a proper job.

But when Kiera refused to let him cross physical boundaries, his resentment grew.

That resentment had led him to offer Kiera to Heath, hoping to curry favor and gain benefits.

Had Marcus not intervened, Kiera's fate would have been sealed.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, thank you so much for what you did today!"

Kiera's wide eyes sparkled with gratitude.

Marcus waved it off. "It was nothing. Don't mention it."

Kiera's face softened, though a trace of guilt lingered. "Because of me, you've made an enemy of Heath. I'm so sorry.

"Please, be careful. Heath won't let this go."

"Don't worry," Marcus said with a chuckle.

"The Deleon family won't do anything to me."

His confidence seemed unshakable, but Kiera couldn't help feeling concerned.

The Deleon family was the most powerful in Uswad.

No matter how formidable Marcus was, the idea of him going against such a force seemed impossible.

Their conversation ended as Marcus pulled up to Peaceful Apartments.

He intended to drop Kiera off and leave, but she insisted on cooking him a meal to show her gratitude.

Unable to refuse her sincerity, Marcus followed Kiera upstairs.

The Peaceful Apartments building had been constructed over 20 years ago and showed its age.

The facilities were outdated, and Kiera's one-bedroom unit was dimly lit, facing away from the sun.

Despite this, the apartment was spotlessly clean, a testament to her care.

As Marcus entered, his gaze accidentally landed on Kiera's open bedroom door.

Hanging from a drying rack were several pieces of women's lingerie.

Kiera's cheeks turned crimson. She quickly gathered the items, stammering an apology. "I'm sorry, Mr. Fitzgerald. I didn't expect to have a guest, so things are a bit... unprepared."

Marcus chuckled, his tone easygoing. "It's normal, isn't it? Everyone wears these. It just shows you have a life, that's all."

As he spoke, his eyes briefly swept over Kiera.

Then, he noticed something. Though she was slender, her figure was surprisingly curvaceous.
