Marcus had just parked his car when a sleek white Rolls-Royce Cullinan rolled up and stopped right across from him.

Out stepped two women and a man.

The man was tall, sharply dressed in a suit, exuding an undeniable charisma.

It was Jenson, Marcus and Chad's classmate from high school.

By his side was a striking woman, tall with a graceful figure, her features exquisite.

She wore an elegant evening gown, sparkling with jewels, but it wasn't over the top.

The whole look was impeccably refined, stylish, and unmistakably expensive, radiating a quiet sense of wealth that caught everyone's attention.

When Chad saw her, his gaze froze, and his body began to shake.

The woman was none other than his ex-wife, Vivian Eleanor.

"Chad, calm down," Marcus said, slapping Chad on the shoulder.

Taking a deep breath, Chad tried to steady himself.

The other woman, Natalie, was also dressed in an evening gown, looking just as stunning.

While her gown wasn't as expensive as Vivian's, her beauty more than made up for it.

"Well, well, look who it is!" Jenson said, strolling over confidently, hands in his pockets, the sound of his leather shoes clicking against the pavement.

"Chad, we met again. It's been three years, and you're still the same. Can't even bother to clean yourself up for a big event like this? You look like a mess. Seriously disappointing."

Vivian's eyes turned to Chad, arms crossed, her face twisted in disdain.

"We don't have any connection anymore. Just keep to yourself and stop judging me," Chad said coldly, his heart a tangled mess of resentment and bitterness as he looked at the woman who once meant everything to him.

"Isn't she right, Chad?" Jenson chimed in with a smug smile. "You don't even look like someone who's had a proper education. You look so pathetic. No wonder Vivian left you for me."

"You..." Chad's fists clenched, a mixture of anger and shame bubbling up. He opened his mouth to retort, but seeing how smug Jenson looked, he felt suddenly small, the words catching in his throat.

"What? You still have a problem with us? I must have been blind choosing you. I wasted my best years on a man like you," Vivian said, her voice dripping with contempt as she looked at him, unashamed.

"You're Chad's ex-wife, right?" Marcus suddenly spoke up.

Vivian turned her gaze to him, sizing him up before nodding. "Yes, that's me. And you are?"

Natalie quickly introduced, "This is Marcus Fitzgerald, our high school classmate. He and Chad were close back then."

"Oh, you're the one Chad always talked about," Vivian said, her tone now a little more curious.

"So, you're hanging out with someone like Chad? Guess that doesn't say much about you.

"Anyway, what do you want?"

Marcus sneered, his eyes sharp. "Nothing much. I just can't wrap my head around it. How does a woman like you, ungrateful and, frankly, cheap, have the nerve to lecture Chad? How shameless can you be?"

Vivian's face turned crimson, her jaw tightening.

She hadn't expected Marcus to speak so bluntly, especially in front of Jenson.

It was clear he had no fear of Jenson either.

"Marcus, are you out of your mind? Badmouthing my wife in front of me? You think I won't do anything about it?" Jenson's face hardened, his voice cold as ice.

His gaze then shifted to the sleek Aston Martin behind Marcus, eyeing the car. "Is that yours?"

Marcus nodded indifferently. "What's it to you?"

"I never thought the poorest kid from school would be driving a few million-dollar car now. No wonder you're acting so cocky," Jenson said, sizing up Marcus with a knowing glance, though his expression quickly returned to its usual arrogance.

At that moment, Natalie quickly told Jenson about how Marcus had spent 650 million buying all the jewelry from their store.

Jenson chuckled darkly. "So, looks like this poor guy is rich now. But you don't really think spending 650 million on jewelry is such a big deal, do you? Well, you're mistaken. To me, 650 million is nothing. Just pocket change. But for someone like you? That might be all you have."
