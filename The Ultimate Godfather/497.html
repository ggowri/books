Marcus froze for a moment, clearly not recognizing Clayton. "You are...?"

Clayton, unfazed, maintained his respectful demeanor. "I'm Clayton Prescott, the owner of Le Vieux Château. Solomia's mayor, Benjamin Mitchell, is my sister's husband."

"I see," Marcus replied, now understanding.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, since you are here, today's meal is on the house!"

With a wave of his hand, Clayton turned to the staff and issued his command.

The waiters immediately nodded and acknowledged the order.

Kyle, Penny, and Jeremy all felt a jolt of surprise.

Le Vieux Château was a renowned restaurant in Solomia, primarily catering to the city's elite.

Even government officials often hosted their guests here.

Due to its high demand, the restaurant never offered discounts to anyone.

But now, Clayton had just made the rare decision to waive the bill for Marcus.

Though there were only a few of them, their meal consisted of expensive dishes that easily totaled over one hundred thousand dollars.

This made it clear just how highly Clayton regarded Marcus.

"Well, since you insist, I'll accept it," Marcus said coolly.

He had decided to repay this favor to Benjamin at a later time.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, if you need anything, just let me know. Whatever I can do for you, I will do without hesitation," Clayton said, bowing slightly, his face full of respect.

Marcus nodded casually.

Clayton bid Marcus farewell, leaving the room without acknowledging Kyle, Penny, or Jeremy at all.

Kyle, Penny, and Jeremy couldn't help but feel a sting of disappointment.

Especially Kyle.

He had made a point of greeting Clayton earlier, only for Clayton to ignore him completely.

The sharp contrast left Kyle feeling utterly embarrassed.

Jeremy, on the other hand, was consumed by despair. But that despair quickly turned to deep hatred.

"We should go," Marcus said, not bothering to acknowledge Kyle, Penny, or Jeremy. He turned to Shannon.

Shannon nodded obediently, standing up and linking her arm with Marcus'.

After exchanging a brief word with Kyle and Penny, she prepared to leave the private room with Marcus.

However, just as they turned to go, Jeremy suddenly grabbed a steel fork from the table and lunged toward Marcus' temple!

"You ruined me, so now I'll make sure you die!"

Jeremy's voice was frantic, his face twisted with fury.

Kyle and Penny both gasped, shocked by Jeremy's sudden, reckless aggression.

Before Jeremy's fork could reach its target, Marcus swiftly spun around and grabbed Jeremy's wrist with a vice-like grip.

Jeremy's face turned pale with shock. He hadn't expected Marcus' reflexes to be so fast.

He struggled fiercely to free himself, but it was futile.

Marcus' hand was like iron, and he felt an overwhelming, unyielding force.

"You've got some nerve. Let go of me, and I'll kill you!"

Jeremy's eyes were bloodshot as he raised his leg, aiming a vicious kick at Marcus.

Marcus' brow furrowed. Without hesitation, he twisted Jeremy's wrist.

There was a sickening crack as Jeremy's arm snapped!

Jeremy screamed in agony, his face turning ghostly pale.

Marcus followed up with a kick that sent Jeremy flying across the room, slamming him into the wall of the private room.

"Do you really think you can lay a hand on me? You don't deserve it!"

Marcus sneered as he looked down at Jeremy.

Jeremy, drenched in sweat from the pain, didn't dare move, too terrified even to make a sound. He could only watch as Marcus led Shannon out of the room.

Once outside Le Vieux Château, Shannon gazed up at Marcus, her eyes filled with even more admiration than before.

If, earlier, she had admired Marcus purely for his strength when he defeated Easton, now her admiration was all-encompassing.

She admired him in every way.

She hadn't expected that Marcus was not only strong but also wealthy, with connections far beyond anything she could have imagined.

This realization stirred a new feeling in Shannon.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, I really owe you one today. With your impressive display, I doubt my uncle and aunt will ever try to set me up with anyone again," she said.

Shannon could tell that Kyle and Penny were just as shocked by Marcus' power as she was.

As long as she didn't announce a breakup with him, not only would her uncle and aunt likely give up on matchmaking her, but others would, too.

Thinking about this, Shannon felt even more grateful to Marcus. At least for the next few years, she wouldn't have to deal with annoying matchmaking attempts.

"As I promised, I'll do my best to help you," Marcus said with a smile.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, you're too kind..."

Shannon beamed back at him, squeezing his arm tighter, her grip almost possessive.

She had even pressed her body closer, feeling the warmth of Marcus' arm against her chest.

Marcus could feel her soft form against him.

"Are you heading back to school? I can take you there," he asked.

The thought of parting from him left Shannon feeling a pang of disappointment deep inside.
