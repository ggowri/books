Marcus instructed the taxi driver to turn around and head toward Vertex Plaza.

He soon spotted Josie, elegantly dressed as always.

Recently, under Marcus's influence, Josie's aura had transformed entirely.

Her complexion glowed, and she exuded an increasing sense of femininity.

Linking her arm with Marcus's, Josie first bought some skincare products and then headed straight to the Chaumet counter.

As a top global jewelry brand, Chaumet's store was exceptionally upscale.

Entry required a reservation, and even with one, customers still had to queue at the door.

With Josie's status and wealth, she could have easily skipped the process, but she remained humble and waited for her turn.

As they stepped inside, Marcus caught sight of a familiar figure.

Abigail, Marcus's ex-wife, was at the Chaumet store!

Beside Abigail was a man dressed head-to-toe in trendy designer wear, sporting gold-rimmed glasses.

Previously, through Natalia, Marcus learned that Abigail's family had gone bankrupt due to Felix using her name to secure loans and cash out.

To pay off the debt, Abigail resorted to high-interest loans but only sank further into financial ruin.

She was ultimately sold to a nightclub by an underworld boss and forced to work as a hostess.

Yet, in just over a month, Abigail had escaped the nightclub and resurfaced in Kingston.

"Honey, do you know her?" Josie asked, noticing Marcus's reaction.

Josie seemed to sense something unusual about Marcus's expression.

Marcus gave a faint smile. "I used to."

"Oh."

Josie, perceptive as ever, could see there was more to the story from Marcus's expression.

She didn't press further and instead led Marcus to a limited-edition diamond necklace on display.

The necklace, showcased on a dedicated stand, held the central spot in the store.

Under the spotlight, it radiated brilliance and elegance.

Many were drawn to the necklace but backed away when they saw its hefty price tag.

The necklace, priced at 9.5 million dollars, far exceeded Chaumet's average product range.

Even among the elite clientele, few could afford it.

Abigail hadn't noticed Marcus and Josie. Her hands were full of shopping bags, most containing high-end luxury items.

Just at Chaumet, she had bought three pieces of jewelry worth over a million dollars.

It was clear the man in trendy clothes beside her was quite wealthy.

"Miss, are you interested in this limited-edition diamond necklace?" a sales associate asked Josie.

Josie, captivated by the necklace, nodded. "Yes."

The associate smiled professionally. "This necklace costs 9.5 million dollars. If you'd like to purchase it, we offer installment plans, even with zero down payment..."

To the associate, Josie didn't look like someone who could afford such a necklace.

After all, she wasn't wearing any particularly valuable items—not even jewelry.

Josie smiled politely. "No need for installments. I'll pay in full."

The associate was taken aback.

She hadn't expected such an expensive necklace to sell so easily.

It meant a nice commission for her.

Seeing Josie's decision, Marcus handed the associate an international bank card.

The associate hesitated briefly, unfamiliar with the card, but said nothing. "Please wait a moment, sir."

She took the card and processed the transaction swiftly.

Josie smiled in satisfaction and said, "Thank you, honey."

Marcus gave a nonchalant shrug.

Josie was a billionaire businesswoman, so a 9.5-million-dollar necklace wasn't particularly extravagant for her.

Abigail noticed Marcus by chance.

She froze momentarily, then she had a knowing smile.

She turned to the man beside her. "Julius, let's go over there and take a look!"

The man in trendy clothes nodded with a smile, clearly doting on Abigail.

"Marcus, we meet again!" Abigail greeted him first.

Marcus gave Abigail a brief glance and said, "What do you want to say?"
