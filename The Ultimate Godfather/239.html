Chapter 238 Interception!

"Honey, dinner's ready."

Josie, wearing an apron, accentuated her slender waist, making it impossible to ignore her curves.

"Thank you, Ms. Shelman," Marcus replied as he sat down to enjoy dinner with Josie.

They spent the evening together, relishing their private time, both physically and mentally relaxed.

The next day, Marcus prepared to leave for Solomia.

Early in the morning, he received a call from Maxine.

Coincidentally, she was also heading back today and would pick him up to go to the airport.

Marcus sent her his location, and less than half an hour later, the Burnett family's business car pulled up outside Sunshine Estates.

Josie seemed to sense something, and with great tact, she didn't go to see Marcus off.

She peeked from the window, watching as Maxine got out of the car to greet Marcus. A complex expression crossed her face.

"You've been staying here these past few days?"

Maxine asked, looking at the unassuming Sunshine Estates.

"Yes." Marcus nodded casually.

Maxine gave him a curious glance. "I didn't expect you to live in such a modest place. Could it be you're hiding a mistress here?"

"Uh..."

A few beads of sweat appeared on Marcus' forehead, and he felt an uncomfortable chill down his spine.

"You're not saying anything, so I must've guessed right," Maxine said, scrutinizing him with a sharp gaze.

At that moment, she seemed to return to her cold, untouchable demeanor, exuding a strength that rivaled any man's.

"You don't admit that I'm your man, but why are you so concerned about what I say?"

Marcus smirked lightly.

"You..."

Maxine bit her teeth in frustration.

The car soon merged onto the expressway to the airport.

But just then, Marcus sensed three powerful presences approaching from afar, sweeping toward them.

The car was quickly enveloped by an overwhelming aura.

The energy was terrifying, indicating that their owner was at least at Ancient Martial Art Stage Three.

The most formidable of them all had reached Ancient Martial Art Stage Four!

Marcus' eyes sharpened, instantly recognizing the danger.

"Driver, pull over!"

Marcus instructed, his voice calm but urgent.

The Burnett family's driver knew Marcus' status and immediately complied.

"What's going on?"

Maxine asked, confused.

"Wait here. I'm going to take care of a few people."

Marcus said with a nonchalant tone.

As he spoke, he already exited the car.

"Take care of a few people?!"

Maxine raised an eyebrow. "Is there anyone who would talk about killing so casually? This guy's arrogance knows no bounds!"

But Maxine knew that Marcus had the right to be arrogant.

Marcus walked to an open area, surrounded by tall weeds, with no one else in sight.

"Come out," he called into the empty air.

Moments later, three figures appeared, their powerful auras sweeping across the area.

Two of them were Wizards, both at Level Three, surrounded by a strong magical energy.

The leader, an elderly man, possessed Ancient Martial Art Stage Four cultivation!

At this level, he was far stronger than Marcus had been before.

If Marcus hadn't been training hard these past few days, breaking through to Stage Four himself, he wouldn't have stood a chance against them.

"You're quite the smart one to come here and wait for death," the elderly man said with a sneer.

He had originally thought Marcus would run the moment they ambushed him, considering the news that Marcus' cultivation was still around Stage Three.

But with two Wizards at Level Three and an Ancient Martial Art Stage Four expert, they could easily destroy him.

So, if Marcus didn't flee, he was either foolish or brave.

"I don't understand. Where does your confidence come from, thinking you can kill me?"

Marcus' eyes glinted with cold amusement as he addressed the elder.

The elder paused for a moment, then chuckled again, "I may not be the strongest, but I'm more than enough to deal with someone like you. Could it be I'm wrong?"

Marcus smirked again. "Was it the second son of the godfather who sent you here?"

"You're smart," the elder said with a cruel grin, revealing his nearly toothless mouth.

"Then you can die," Marcus' expression hardened, and his gaze turned sharp.

His hands, which had been resting behind him, suddenly shot forward.

Two intense fireballs erupted from his palms, filled with searing heat.

The temperature around them skyrocketed as the fireballs hurtled toward the two Wizards.

The two Wizards' eyes widened in surprise, realizing Marcus had mastered Wizard-level power.
