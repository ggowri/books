The gunmen fell where they stood, their lives snuffed out in an instant.

Julian, the bodyguard, and the driver exchanged looks of stunned disbelief.

They couldn't fathom how Marcus had predicted the exact locations of the assassins—or how he had taken them out so quickly.

His shooting was unmatched, but his ability to anticipate the enemy's every move bordered on the supernatural.

And this was only the beginning.

The road ahead stretched for 1.2 miles, dotted with ambush points every couple hundred yards.

The assassins had blended into the crowd, using civilians as cover to deceive the security teams.

They had planned to deliver a single, decisive strike against Julian.

But none of them could have predicted the presence of Marcus, the man riding shotgun with skills that defied belief.

Marcus' spiritual senses spread across the entire street, mapping every movement like a live radar.

Those criminals didn't have a prayer of slipping through his grasp.

For the next three minutes, the Maybach convoy glided smoothly down the street, the pace calm and deliberate.

To Marcus, this was starting to feel like a high-stakes level in a first-person shooter.

Each time they passed a hidden ambush, he fired with unerring precision, dismantling the threat before it even had a chance to unfold.

The gunmen didn't even get to lift their weapons before Marcus took them out, one by one.

Julian and his bodyguard-driver duo were slack-jawed, their disbelief growing with every flawless shot.

Three minutes later, the convoy cleared the danger zone, unscathed.

Julian sat in stunned silence, struggling to find words for what he had just witnessed.

"Well, that's that. All the gunmen are down. You just need to ID them," Marcus said casually, slipping his gun back into his Spatial Ring.

He reached for the door, ready to exit.

"Hold on, kid," Julian called after him.

"What is it, Commander?" Marcus turned back, his tone even.

He hadn't intervened for accolades or rewards—it was simply the right thing to do as a Uswadian.

"You've saved my life today. I don't even know your name. May I ask who you are?" Julian's eyes lingered on Marcus, a glint of genuine admiration in his otherwise weathered gaze.

"Saved your life? Nah, let's not make a big deal of it. Name's Marcus Fitzgerald. Take care, Commander," Marcus replied with a half-smile and a quick wave.

With that, he stepped out of the car and walked off without looking back, his stride confident and unhurried.

Julian's gaze followed Marcus as he disappeared into the distance, a rare expression of gratitude softening his normally stoic features.

He turned to his bodyguard and said firmly, "Find out everything you can about Marcus Fitzgerald. I want to honor him properly."

As one of the nation's top leaders, Julian knew his survival wasn't just a personal matter—it was a matter of national pride.

If foreign operatives had succeeded in assassinating him on home soil, it wouldn't just be his name tarnished—it would've been an embarrassment for the entire country of Uswad.

Marcus' intervention had spared not only Julian's life but also a potential international humiliation.

A hero like that deserved recognition. After all, if the nation didn't reward its defenders, who would step forward the next time danger struck?

The bodyguard gave a sharp nod, his expression serious.

It was the first time he had seen Julian speak so highly of someone.

He had a feeling Marcus' life was about to take a dramatic turn for the better.

Marcus, blissfully unaware of Julian's plans, made his way back to Bayside Villa.

The next morning, every major news outlet exploded with headlines about Julian Ashford's visit to Solomia and the foreign-backed assassination attempt.

The stories focused on a mysterious young hero, recounting his incredible sharpshooting skills and his ability to take out ambushers hiding among civilians.

Words like "marksman," "instinctive genius," and "unsung savior" filled the articles, painting the picture of a larger-than-life figure.

Curiously, the reports kept the hero's identity under wraps. No photos, no name—just vague descriptions of his actions.

Marcus smirked as he read the articles, appreciating the effort to protect his privacy. With a satisfied nod, he closed the news page.

Another alert popped up moments later, announcing the formation of a special task force to investigate the assassination attempt on Julian.
