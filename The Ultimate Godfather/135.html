Chapter 134 Positive Outcome

When Marcus returned to Bayside Villa, he found Natalia packing her belongings.

Curious, he asked her what she was up to and learned that she was preparing to return to Kingston for university.

"Natalia, when do you plan on leaving?" Marcus asked.

"Tomorrow. Marcus, will you miss me?" Natalia asked, her eyes sparkling with affection.

"Of course," Marcus replied with a smile and a nod.

"You're just saying that!" she pouted, looking adorably cute.

"You've been so busy lately, you hardly have time for me."

Scratching his head, Marcus quickly tried to soothe her.

He knew he had been swamped and hadn't seen Natalia often.

But there was just so much on his plate—he couldn't be in two places at once.

"By the way, Natalia, I ran into Abigail in Kingston," he mentioned, recalling his encounter.

After a brief pause, Natalia asked, "How is she? Is she doing okay?"

After all, they were sisters, so Natalia still cared about Abigail.

Marcus recounted his meeting with Abigail at Vertex Plaza in detail.

Natalia sighed. "After everything that happened with Felix, she still hasn't learned her lesson? She's still dreaming of marrying into money. I just don't understand.

"But she is my sister, so I can only hope she finds a good guy soon."

Marcus remained silent.

Natalia was kind-hearted; despite Abigail's past treatment of her, she still held concern for her sister.

The next day, Marcus drove Natalia to the airport before calling Michelle to get the latest updates on Golden Group's business.

Michelle reported that TreatMed Pharmaceuticals had completely collapsed following Jared's death.

Although their partnership had ended, Golden Group's other ventures were thriving and setting sales records.

Overall, their business was improving, with turnover up by one-third and projected net profits reaching 10 billion dollars this year.

After that, Marcus visited the construction site of the pharmaceutical company for an inspection.

Thanks to the hard work of Solomia Construction, everything was progressing smoothly.

Several factory building frames were already up, and they were on track to finish in less than two weeks.

This advance meant that the overall timeline could be pushed forward by two and a half months, which was fantastic news for Marcus.

After all, he had personally planned and overseen the construction of this factory, giving him a special attachment to it.

Once he left the construction site, Marcus went to check on the Serpent's Coil members who were practicing their magic.

Jalen, after mastering the Fireball and Lightning techniques, had been training tirelessly, even stepping back from the gang's battles to focus on his skills.

His dedication paid off—he had become proficient enough to easily take down ordinary fighters with a casual Fireball spell.

Jalen's rapid progress exceeded Marcus's expectations, showcasing a talent for magic that was far beyond what he had anticipated.

Of course, Jalen's hard work was crucial. All in all, Marcus was quite satisfied with Jalen's training results.

Marcus also identified several promising talents among the members practicing the magic classics.

These individuals displayed exceptional understanding and aptitude, mastering all the techniques in the books.

He formed them into a small team for individualized training.

With nine members, each possessing extraordinary skills, Marcus instructed Jalen to teach them the Fireball and Lightning techniques for intensive practice.

This way, they could quickly become true wizards.

Meanwhile, Edgar, the current head of the Serpent's Coil, didn't seem to have much aptitude for magic, which made him feel somewhat inferior.

Observing his former subordinates surpassing him, he likely felt the pressure.

Marcus recognized this concern. Edgar had spent years in the underground world and was shrewd in business.

Marcus had no intention of asking someone else to replace him.

To stabilize Edgar, Marcus gifted him a martial arts manual he had gotten from Ricardo's Spatial Ring, encouraging him to hone his martial skills.

"Thank you, Mr. Fitzgerald!" Edgar exclaimed, tears of gratitude in his eyes.

It reassured him that Marcus hadn't given up on him, sparking a renewed sense of hope as he committed to training day and night.

Overall, the members of the Serpent's Coil were surpassing Marcus's expectations.

If everything continued on this path, within six months, the Serpent's Coil could dominate the Solomia underground world and become an unstoppable force.
