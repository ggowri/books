"Well, since you're already here, come in," Marcus said, pulling his thoughts back to the present and looking at Ada.

Ada frowned and shot him an annoyed glare. "Why do I feel like you're not exactly thrilled about this?"

Marcus sighed, feeling a twinge of helplessness.

"Women," he thought, "are truly a puzzle."

Not too long ago, when he'd forced himself on Ada, she'd regarded him as a mortal enemy, vowing to tear him apart piece by piece.

Now, after his exhausting return from the space-time gate, all he wanted was a good night's sleep.

A moment's hesitation on his part, and she was offended again.

He thought to himself, "She didn't even want to be near me before.

So why is she upset now?"

"I asked you a question. Are you willing or not? If not, I'll just find some random place outside to spend the night," Ada said, her voice crisp with irritation as Marcus remained silent.

"I never said I wasn't willing. Just come in," Marcus replied hastily.

He knew Ada could be quite stubborn at times.

If he didn't appear welcoming enough, she really might go off to sleep elsewhere.

And no matter what, she was his woman. There was no way he'd let her spend the night outside.

Hearing his response, Ada finally stepped inside.

But once she was in, she simply stood there, unmoving.

"Aren't you here to sleep? Why not get on the bed?" Marcus asked.

He wasn't trying to take advantage of her. He was genuinely curious.

After all, sleeping usually involved getting into bed.

Ada gave him another sharp glare. "Turn around. I'm going to undress and take a bath. Do you know how filthy I feel after all those fights with those beasts in the space-time tunnel?"

Marcus raised an eyebrow and replied casually, "I've already seen everything there is to see. What's left to hide?"

Ada's cheeks flushed crimson. She glared at him again, this time with even more ferocity. "Never bring up such embarrassing things again," she snapped.

With that, she walked straight into the bathroom. Moments later, the sound of running water echoed through the room.

Marcus chuckled quietly to himself, thinking that Ada might just have a touch of obsessive cleanliness.

As if to confirm his suspicions, she spent over an hour in the bathroom.

By the time she emerged, Marcus was already on the verge of dozing off.

When she finally stepped out, she was wrapped in a thick bathrobe, her elegant figure concealed beneath its folds.

Yet despite the modest covering, Marcus' sharp eyes couldn't help but notice her long, graceful legs.

Freshly washed, Ada looked more radiant than ever, her beauty exuding an air of ethereal charm.

"Are you planning to stand there all night? Why don't you get on the bed already?" Marcus asked, noticing her hesitance.

Ada glanced around the room and bit her lip. "The people of the Nuer Tribe are unbelievably stingy. They couldn't even arrange a room with a sofa for you!"

It dawned on Marcus then that she'd been hoping he'd offer to sleep on the sofa. He silently cursed her ruthless streak.

"Well, there's nothing we can do about that now. Let's just make do with the bed for one night. Tomorrow, we'll both head home. It's not like we're in a position to complain about their hospitality," Marcus said matter-of-factly.

Ada begrudgingly admitted that he had a point.

She cautiously approached the bed and lay down, keeping her bathrobe on and looking visibly tense.

Marcus saw it and reached out to the belt of her bathrobe.

Ada's hand shot out to grab him, her expression sharp with alarm. "What are you doing?" she demanded.

"It's uncomfortable to sleep in a bathrobe. We've had a long day. You should get some proper rest," Marcus said with a faint smile, gently tugging at the belt.

He was genuinely trying to help. Anyone would agree that sleeping in a bathrobe wasn't ideal.

But Ada glared at him yet again. "There's no need. I'll make do for one night. Sleeping in a bathrobe is just fine," she said firmly, pushing his hand away.

Despite the fact that they'd already been intimate twice, this was the first time they were sharing a bed.

Ada couldn't help feeling a little nervous.

Seeing her insistence and feeling his own weariness catching up to him, Marcus decided not to press the issue.

He turned over and said, "Alright, do as you like. I'm going to sleep now."

And with that, he left her alone.

Ada blinked in surprise, her beautiful eyes narrowing slightly. She hadn't expected him to give in so easily.

"Doesn't he feel the slightest temptation with someone like me lying next to him?" she wondered, a strange sense of disappointment flickering in her chest.

But she quickly brushed the thought aside, reminding herself that she was only here for a place to sleep. There was no point in overthinking things.

She told herself to focus on resting. She was just as exhausted as Marcus.

After coming to terms with it, Ada settled down, ready to sleep.

Yet, as the silence stretched on, Ada found herself unable to fall asleep.

She tossed and turned, her mind spinning with thoughts.

"Could he really be angry with me?

Or is he just the kind of man who loses interest after getting what he wants?"

The more she dwelled on it, the harder it became to quiet her racing thoughts.

Finally, after nearly thirty minutes of restless fidgeting, Ada couldn't take it anymore.

She reached out and tapped Marcus lightly on the back. "Hey, are you asleep?" she asked softly.

Marcus feigned a groggy tone. "I've been asleep for ages. What is it?"

Ada's cheeks puffed with frustration. "How can you be so dense? There's someone else in your bed, and you're able to fall asleep just like that?"

Marcus rubbed his eyes and replied, "Well, you're not talking to me, and you're not doing anything else either. What else am I supposed to do?"

Ada caught the implication in his words and immediately felt her blood boil.

Marcus turned back over and closed his eyes, letting out a faint snore.

This infuriated Ada even more. She gave him a hard shove and said, "Don't sleep! Talk to me for a bit."

"Fine," Marcus replied lazily. "What do you want to talk about?"

Ada hesitated for a moment, then said, "You gave me a Return Essence Crystal today, but my cultivation is only at Ancient Martial Stage Seven. I still have a long way to go before I can use it. Who knows how many years it'll take."

Marcus replied casually, "With your current level, it's not hard to advance quickly. The only question is whether you're willing to."

Ada's eyes lit up with interest. "Do you have a method? I'm willing to try."

Marcus glanced at her with a mischievous smirk. "It's the same method we used the last time you broke through. If you're up for it, I can help you reach Stage Eight tonight."

Ada's cheeks flushed bright red. She glared at him, torn between shame and indignation.
