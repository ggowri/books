And that's when he spotted her. Maximilian Lockwood, the husband of the raging woman in front of her, had his eyes on her from the moment she walked in.

The man was a sleazebag, the kind who threw his money around and expected women to fall at his feet.

But tonight's karaoke bar wasn't that kind of place—there were no working girls to keep him entertained.

Three drinks in, he was restless. Bored. And his gaze kept drifting back to Kiera.

That's when he sent someone over to fetch her—offering to pay for her company.

Kiera couldn't believe what was happening—she felt like she was stuck in some twisted nightmare. She immediately turned him down.

But Maximilian wasn't about to take no for an answer. Without hesitation, he barged into her room, grabbing her by the arm and pulling her towards his private booth.

Kiera fought back with all her strength, and her friends tried to talk Maximilian down, but it was no use.

His mind clouded by lust, Maximilian simply ignored their protests, dragging Kiera into the booth.

Maximilian was the executive vice president of SiliconWave Technologies, one of the largest corporations in Kingston.

Originally from Solomia, he was in Kingston for business, heading up his company's team for a deal.

SiliconWave Technologies was worth hundreds of billions of dollars, and its stock had been climbing ever since it was acquired by Death.

As a result, Maximilian was well-known and respected, not only in Kingston but also in Solomia.

He had connections with all sorts of influential people in Solomia, including the owner of this bar, who, despite witnessing the situation, didn't dare interfere with Maximilian's actions.

Just as Maximilian was about to assault Kiera, he sent two security guards to stand by the door, making sure no one would interrupt him.

Kiera's friends were at a loss.

They even tried to call the police, but the lines were strangely busy, and no one answered.

It was obvious that Maximilian had probably arranged things with the police beforehand.

This man was untouchable—his reach extended far beyond what anyone could imagine.

To make things worse, when his wife arrived and saw Kiera, she immediately assumed the worst.

Without even asking questions, she blamed Kiera for seducing her husband.

The way she twisted the truth was beyond belief, and the scene unfolded just as Marcus had witnessed.

After listening to Kiera's story, Marcus' eyes narrowed, a cold glint flickering in them.

To put it simply, Kiera came to the karaoke bar with her friends.

Maximilian invited her to join him for drinks, but she refused.

He didn't take no for an answer, dragging her into his booth and trying to make unwanted advances.

When his wife found out, instead of confronting her husband, she shifted all the blame onto Kiera, accusing her of trying to seduce him.

It was disgusting how easily the woman had turned the whole thing around on Kiera.

If Marcus hadn't stepped in, Kiera would have been completely screwed.

"Mr. Fitzgerald... You need to understand. This man is the executive vice president of SiliconWave Technologies. He has major connections in Kingston and Solomia. Please, don't get involved. I don't want to bring trouble your way."

Kiera's voice trembled as she spoke, her eyes red with the threat of tears. She couldn't bear the thought of Marcus getting caught up in this mess because of her.

In her mind, Marcus was powerful, but not enough to take on someone like Maximilian or his massive corporation.

She feared that if things got ugly, Marcus wouldn't stand a chance.

"Don't worry, I'm not going to let this slide. I'll make sure you get the justice you deserve," Marcus said firmly, his gaze steady and reassuring.

At that moment, Fiona Lockwood, Maximilian's wife, who had been silently observing them, took in Marcus with a cold, assessing look. A sneer appeared on her face. "Who the hell are you? Some sort of knight in shining armor? Let me give you a piece of advice: stay out of this, or you'll end up just like this little slut—miserable and alone!"
