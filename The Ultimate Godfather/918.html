Marcus didn't even spare the cyborgs a glance. He casually unleashed two blasts of Fist Ray, and with a deafening boom, the shockwaves tore through the air.

The three fifth-generation cyborgs he had been so proud of turned out to be nothing more than scrap metal before Marcus. With just a flick of his wrist, Marcus had annihilated them.

And as for the intricate trap Joel had meticulously set up? It hadn't even slowed Marcus down. At this moment, Joel had played all his cards—there was nothing left for him to fall back on.

Marcus paid Joel no mind. He reached out and clamped a hand around Joel's throat, lifting him clean off the ground with sheer brute force. Then, turning his gaze toward Cecilia, Marcus asked, "I've got him for you, Cecilia. What do you want to do with him?"

Before she could say a word, Rhett's trembling voice cut through the silence from not far away. "He may have committed unspeakable crimes, but no matter what, he's still my blood. I can't bear to see him die... Let him spend the rest of his life rotting in the dungeons!"

Marcus gave a slight nod in response. Without hesitation, two men from the McHale family stepped forward and swiftly bound Joel, tying him up tightly with thick ropes.

As they restrained him, Joel thrashed violently, his voice ringing with desperation. "What the hell do you think you're doing? Let go of me right now! I am the head of this family! He was on the verge of losing his mind, unable to accept that the power he had clung to so desperately was slipping through his fingers.

Meanwhile, Tobias, Luna, and Albert stood rooted to the spot, shivering in fear. None of them had expected Marcus to be this terrifyingly strong. Luna and Albert, in particular, felt a deep, gut-wrenching fear settle in. The more they thought about how they had acted so smugly in front of Marcus earlier, the more their stomachs twisted with regret. A bone-chilling dread seeped into their veins, making their limbs go numb.

Albert's eyes darted around as he quickly hatched a plan. If he stayed, he was doomed. Moving as discreetly as possible, he edged toward the banquet hall's exit, step by step, trying to slip away unnoticed into the night.

To be honest, Marcus had never really taken Albert seriously. But the man was like a persistent fly, constantly buzzing around, refusing to go away. And now, he had even involved himself in the McHale Pharmaceuticals Group's internal power struggle. That alone sealed his fate.

Albert's face turned deathly pale as he begged for mercy, "I... I was wrong... I swear, I know I was wrong! Marcus, please, just let me go..." He even attempted to bolt from the banquet hall when Marcus wasn't paying attention.
