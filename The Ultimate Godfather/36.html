In an abandoned factory on the eastern outskirts of the city, Richard and his family were held captive, bound to stone pillars by Felix.

"Mr. Davis, Marcus is a well-known dutiful son. He'll definitely come for them," Felix said, bowing respectfully to Brandon.

Brandon nodded and grinned. "Good. After I kill Marcus, I'll make you the general manager of Golden Group."

Jackson, fearing he'd be left out, hastily stepped forward. "Mr. Davis, don't forget about me."

Jackson knew Cedars Villas better than anyone.

It was his knowledge that had helped Felix successfully capture Richard and his family.

"Don't worry, there's plenty in it for you."

Brandon made a promise.

"Thank you, Mr. Davis!"

Jackson bowed deeply.

A wave of relief washed over him at the thought of Marcus's impending demise.

Melinda, tears streaming down her face, pleaded, "My son has been a good boy his whole life. He won't defy you. If you're going to kill someone, kill me and let him go!"

In her heart, Marcus's life meant more than her own.

Richard nodded vigorously in agreement. "As long as you let my children go, you can tear me into pieces!"

Felix wore a cruel smile. "You think you're worth that much? If it weren't for luring Marcus here, I wouldn't waste my time on you old fools!"

He motioned to the thugs beside him. "Beat them until they shut up. Their whining is annoying."

Several thugs complied, raining blows on Richard and Melinda.

Within moments, Richard and Melinda lost consciousness.

Their daughter, Nicole, had avoided this nightmare by being away at college.

Before making his move, Brandon had conducted a thorough investigation of Marcus.

Knowing that Marcus was likely a warrior, Brandon had left nothing to chance, ensuring that a tight net of death awaited Marcus at the factory.

Marcus drove his Ferrari to the gates of the abandoned factory.

Two armed guards spotted him immediately and raised their guns without a word.

But Marcus was faster. He whipped out a submachine gun and fired with deadly precision. The silencer made a soft puffing sound as the two men fell, riddled with bullets.

He quickly grabbed their guns and found two grenades on their belts, which he also confiscated.

He moved swiftly into the factory.

Before arriving, Marcus had already grasped a clear understanding of the situation at hand.

He was aware that the adversary was baiting him into a trap, undoubtedly having made ample preparations.

This abandoned factory was nothing less than a meticulously laid dragnet of traps!

Despite being a warrior at the primordial ascension stage of Stage One, Marcus knew better than to underestimate his enemies.

He kept his focus sharp, using every piece of cover the factory offered, advancing cautiously.

"Someone's coming! Take him out!"

The security was tighter than Marcus had anticipated.

Before he could even enter, he was spotted.

As the voice rang out, gunfire erupted from several directions, with bullets immediately targeting him.

Marcus didn't flinch. Dodging bullets was second nature, and with the cover of the factory, he was nearly untouchable.

His submachine gun sang in his hands, dispatching his enemies silently.

In less than two seconds, the silencers whispered death, and several shooters lay dead.

Marcus advanced swiftly, and more armed men emerged, unleashing a hail of bullets as dense as a downpour!

Marcus darted between covers, using the terrain to shield himself from the relentless hail of bullets.

He pulled a grenade from his belt and lobbed it into a cluster of enemies.

Boom!

The explosion sent bodies flying, reducing them to nothing more than a bloody pulp.

Simultaneously, he rolled and maneuvered his body to another strategic vantage point for shooting.

Three men armed with heavy machine guns stood before him, ready to open fire.

Marcus squeezed the trigger, and the submachine gun's bullets tore through the chests of several adversaries!

Grabbing one of the heavy machine guns, Marcus unleashed a torrent of fire at another group of attackers.

There were at least a dozen armed men, their weapons raised, poised for retaliation.

But the heavy machine gun was a weapon of devastation. Its high-caliber rounds tore through their defenses as if they were made of paper. The enemy's cover was shredded in an instant.

For more than ten minutes, the battle raged. The factory yard was soon littered with fallen enemies, and the air reeked of gunpowder.

Throughout this onslaught, Marcus killed his foes with ruthless efficiency, culminating when he killed a sniper with a flying knife through the neck.

Every single ambusher lying in wait within the abandoned factory yard was taken down by Marcus alone!

He stood at the center. But just as he was about to breach the main building, the sound of clapping shattered the silence.

Brandon, dressed in a black windbreaker, emerged from the shadows, flanked by his men.

Marcus raised the heavy machine gun and aimed it squarely at Brandon.

"Impressive," Brandon said calmly. "You actually managed to get through my dragnet. Most men wouldn't have made it past the first wave."

Marcus glanced at Felix and Jackson. In an instant, the pieces fell into place.

His voice was cold. "Where are my parents? What have you done with them?"

Brandon sneered, "No need to rush. We can play a little game first. Then, if you're still standing, maybe I'll tell you where they are."
