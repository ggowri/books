Chapter 5 The Supreme Card

The Golden Hotel was packed with people attending the general manager selection conference.

"Golden Group's really top-tier. Look at all these big shots from Solomia!"

Abigail stared at the lineup of luxury cars, buzzing with excitement.

Without Felix's invite, she'd never have gotten into something this fancy.

"Everyone's here to kiss up to the new chairman, someone said he'd come," Felix replied casually.

Abigail's eyes sparkled. "Golden Group's new chairman? He must be someone incredible!"

"What, want to meet him?" Felix smirked.

"Obviously!" she gushed. "Who in Solomia wouldn't?"

"Well," Felix puffed up importantly, "he's actually an old friend of mine. Once I'm general manager, I'll introduce you."

Felix bragged shamelessly, confident as if he could bluff his way past Abigail's expectations later.

"Oh, Mr. Yannis, you're amazing!" Abigail squealed, kissing his cheek.

A connection to the Golden Group's chairman would definitely put the Winchester family on top.

Dumping that useless Marcus and getting close to Felix was the wisest choice I'd ever made.

That loser can get out of my life forever!

Little did she know, the chairman she was so desperate to meet was Marcus - her husband of three years whom she'd written off as worthless.

Abigail clung to Felix's arm, beaming as they approached the entrance with their invitations.

Then a familiar figure appeared, walking toward them.

"Mar... Marcus? Is that... is he a ghost?" Abigail stammered, her face pale with fear.

Felix, more composed, studied the figure before sneering. "Well, It's really that loser. Can't believe he's still alive. How could he even survive that?"

After her shock wore off, Abigail's eyes turned to ice.

"Marcus, this is the Golden Group's general manager selection conference. What's trash like you doing here? Get lost before you embarrass my family!"

Marcus shot them a disgusted look. "If you two are allowed in, why can't I be?"

He had bigger things to deal with today. These shameless scumbags weren't worth his time.

"I'm the deputy general manager of Golden Group, I belong here," Felix interjected smugly. "But you? A worthless nobody. How dare you show up here and talk to me like that?"

"I'll count to three," Abigail snapped, "and you'd better leave. Or else it gets messy."

The disdain on their faces couldn't have been clearer.

Marcus just ignored them, pulling out a black card and handing it to the hostess.

The hostess smiled and said politely, "I'm sorry, sir. You'll need an invitation."

Seeing this, Felix and Abigail burst into mocking laughter.

"Look at this loser trying to crash the day!" Abigail sneered. "Did you really think that fake card would work?"

"This is Golden Group's Supreme Card," Marcus said coolly. "I don't blame you for not knowing. After all, It's a bit above your pay grade."

Felix was momentarily stunned, and then he snorted. "Supreme Card? Never heard of it. What a good joke."

Abigail hung on Felix's arm quickly, soothing him. "Don't waste your time on him, Mr. Yannis. He's just jealous of us and wants to make a scene."

She turned to Marcus with a smirk. "Get lost, loser. That worthless card won't help you here. Leave before security throws you out!"

"Oh, and FYI," she bragged, "Mr. Yannis isn't just getting the general manager position today - he's also besties with the new chairman. He's introducing us later. That's someone you'll never be important enough to meet."

"The new chairman's his friend? Are you sure about that?" Marcus's lips twitched with amusement.

He didn't recall ever being Felix's friend.

"Of course!" Abigail lifted her chin. "Mr. Yannis knows everyone who matters. Once I meet the chairman, my family's set for life. By then, you'll be nothing but dirt beneath my shoes!"

"Face it - we're in different leagues now. Stop trying to win me back."

Marcus barely held back his laughter.

Abigail, how did I never notice you were this stupid before?

But you're right about one thing - we are indeed in different leagues now.

Because you're not even fit to polish my shoes!

Can't wait to see your face when you find out I'm the chairman you're so desperate to meet.

"Security!" Felix barked. "Get this trash out of here!"

Guards with batons rushed forward as Abigail watched eagerly, ready for the show.

"WAIT!"

Before the guards could move, the hotel manager hurried over, looking panicked.

"The group just informed us a distinguished guest with a Supreme Card would be attending. May I ask who it is?"
