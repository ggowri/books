The commotion immediately caught the attention of several onlookers, most of whom were there on official business with the OIG.

In such a solemn, high-pressure environment, no one dared to speak loudly, let alone argue.

Even when faced with the arrogant attitudes of staff, most people chose to endure the humiliation in silence.

When Grace fearlessly stood up to Arabella Vandenberg, a staff member here, the crowd couldn't help but feel a mix of satisfaction and concern.

"Oh, so that's how it is?" Arabella sneered, her tone dripping with sarcasm. "You're just here to cause trouble, aren't you? Fine, if that's what you want, don't blame me for being harsh."

She clapped her hands mockingly, as if applauding their audacity.

Turning to the nearby security guards, she barked, "Security! Take these two troublemakers out of here. No need to be gentle—escort them straight to the police station."

The guards nodded and began moving toward Grace and Marcus, ready to carry out her orders.

"What's going on here?"

A sharp, authoritative voice cut through the tension, drawing everyone's attention to the entrance.

Heads turned to see a group of middle-aged men in formal jackets walking toward the scene.

Leading them was none other than Pablo Ashford, the Director of the OIG.

Behind him were other high-ranking officials, their presence instantly commanding respect.

The crowd parted instinctively, making way for the group.

Arabella's haughty demeanor vanished in an instant, replaced by a look of deference. "Mr. Ashford, these two have been causing a scene, demanding to see you. I was about to have security remove them."

Pablo's brow furrowed as he scanned Grace and Marcus.

His gaze lingered on Grace for a moment before he spoke, "You're Benjamin Mitchell's daughter, aren't you?"

His eyes narrowed slightly, and a faint, mocking smirk tugged at the corners of his face.

"Yes, I am," Grace said firmly. "And I'd like to know why you've arrested my father. The charges you've brought against him don't add up."

Pablo adjusted his tie, his tone calm but laced with superiority. "The charges against your father were based on a thorough investigation by our special task force. Do you honestly think I, as the director of OIG, would fabricate something like this?"

Pablo's history with Ryder ran deep.

Ryder had saved his life once, and ever since, Pablo had treated Ryder with unwavering respect, almost like a blood relative.

Benjamin, however, had been the one to send Ryder to prison, condemning him to a life sentence.

That act had festered into a deep resentment within Pablo.

He had even approached his father, Julian, asking him to use his influence to secure Ryder's release.

But Julian, ever the principled man, had flatly refused.

Julian, a former soldier and an incorruptible leader, valued integrity above all else.

Even though Ryder was his son-in-law, he refused to bend the rules for him.

Pablo, unable to accept this outcome, decided to take matters into his own hands. Fueled by a desire for revenge, he fabricated evidence of bribery, embezzlement, and other crimes to frame Benjamin.

Pablo's goal was simple—to make Benjamin suffer the same fate as Ryder—spending the rest of his life behind bars.

Only then would Pablo feel as though he had avenged Ryder and balanced the scales.

At least Pablo had found a way to avenge Ryder.
