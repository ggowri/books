Chapter 583 Turning the Tide

Not long after, under the astonished gazes of everyone, Rodney suddenly woke up.

His eyes scanned the surroundings in confusion, and he muttered, "Am I... dead?"

"Uncle Rodney, you're not dead. Marcus saved you." Catherine quickly explained.

Rodney's gaze turned to Marcus, and he suddenly looked at him with a grateful expression. "Young man, did you really save me?"

Gina, still in disbelief, had witnessed Marcus' life-saving actions firsthand.

She nodded towards her father, her voice soft. "Dad, it really was Marcus who saved you. We were wrong about him..."

A wave of shame washed over Gina as she thought back to the harsh things she had said to Marcus earlier. She felt mortified, wishing she could disappear into the floor.

Seeing her father come back to life under Marcus' hands, Gina's heart filled with gratitude.

"Young man, I can't thank you enough. You're like a second father to me!" Rodney said, squeezing Marcus' hand tightly, his voice thick with emotion.

Marcus waved his hand nonchalantly. "No need for thanks. If you want to thank anyone, thank Catherine."

Truth be told, Marcus had no particular interest in Rodney or his family. If it weren't for Catherine, he would have just walked away.

"Catherine, before... I was wrong about you. I shouldn't have doubted you. Please don't be upset. I owe you an apology," Rodney said, his eyes focused on Catherine as he moved to bow deeply.

Catherine immediately stopped him, saying, "Uncle Rodney, please don't. We're family. I couldn't just stand by and do nothing."

Gina looked at Catherine with an apologetic expression. "Catherine, I was too hasty before. I was just so worried about Dad, I said things I shouldn't have. I'm really sorry to both of you."

Without another word, Gina gave a deep bow to both Marcus and Catherine.

"How is this possible? How could Rodney's heart have stopped, and now he's suddenly alive again? This is... a miracle!"

At this point, Berg finally snapped out of his shock.

He stepped forward to Marcus, and his face had shifted from disbelief to quiet respect. "I... I was out of line earlier. I hope you won't hold it against me."

Marcus simply waved him off. "We're just passing strangers. No need for all this. You're a doctor, and you didn't do anything wrong."

With that, Marcus turned his attention elsewhere, clearly uninterested in further conversation.

Berg had hoped to use this opportunity to form some kind of connection with Marcus.

After all, a healer with this kind of skill was rare—worldwide.

But seeing Marcus' cold, indifferent attitude, Berg swallowed his words.

He realized it was his earlier mocking remarks that had put him in this position.

He'd lost the chance to align himself with such a Master Healer.

"Gina, Catherine and Marcus are my saviors. Tomorrow, when you start work, please check with the Sync Group's Solomia branch and see if they have any suitable openings for Catherine. If not, I'll have her join our wellness company." Rodney said, turning to Gina.

Gina nodded confidently. "Dad, don't worry. This time, I won't let Catherine be left without a job. I'll make sure she's with me and help her grow. I'll even make sure she earns a six-figure salary, at least. That way, their family will have a good life."

"Gina, really, you don't have to. I already have a job lined up. Marcus got me a position with an eight-figure salary. And we're probably going to be colleagues too!" Catherine laughed lightly.

Gina froze for a moment, then her face lit up. "Wait, you've already joined the Sync Group's Solomia branch? That's amazing! Now I can really take care of you!

"Let's not get carried away with the eight-figure talk. I'm your cousin, so it's fine to share with me, but if you tell others, they'll think you're exaggerating."

Catherine didn't go into detail, but soon she would be starting work after her break, and they would probably cross paths at the office.

Marcus' Timeless Numinous Energy had such powerful healing effects that, as his abilities grew, the results became even more incredible.

Rodney's aorta had been completely blocked, but after Marcus' treatment, his body had fully recovered.

There was no need for further hospitalization—he was as good as new.

After Marcus and Catherine completed the paperwork for her new house, they left the sales office.

Since Dragon Crest One was a move-in-ready property, Marcus immediately arranged for a design company to start the renovations that same day.

The design would be ready in three days, and construction could begin on the fourth day.

The process would take about two months, and after a six-month formaldehyde release period, she could move in after about eight months.

During this time, Catherine would have to stay in the staff housing building temporarily.

Marcus helped Catherine drive her new Mini Cooper back to her building. By the time they arrived, it was already past 7 p.m.
