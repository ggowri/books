Chapter 564 Contraceptive Pills

Marcus worked his way through a series of positions, each more intense than the last. Finally, with a wild, storm-like thrust, he erupted deep inside Josie.

They both reached their peaks simultaneously, their bodies convulsing in a shared climax.

Josie was utterly spent, her breath coming in ragged gasps as she lay trembling on the bed.

Marcus rolled off her and sat on the couch, lighting a cigarette and exhaling a cloud of smoke.

After a moment, Josie caught her breath. Sitting up, she glared at him as she surveyed the messy aftermath.

"You did it again," she snapped. "You promised me you'd be careful. Are you trying to get me pregnant?"

Marcus rubbed his forehead, a look of frustration crossing his face.

He had made that promise, but lately he couldn't seem to help himself.

Every time he finished, he'd feel guilty and swear it wouldn't happen again.

But then, the next time, he'd do it all over.

Men, he thought. We're all just slaves to our biology, hardwired to procreate.

"I'll be more careful next time, I promise," Marcus vowed, his eyes locked on Josie.

Josie let out a soft chuckle, her rosy cheeks blooming like a flower. "Don't worry, honey. I was just teasing. I've been on the pill to prevent any surprises."

Marcus felt a wave of relief wash over him, but also a pang of concern. "I know birth control pills are supposed to be safe, but Josie, I don't want you taking any unnecessary risks."

Josie tilted her head up, her eyes meeting his. "It's okay, really. I've heard they're pretty safe and can even regulate your cycle."

Marcus nodded, hoping she was right.

It was definitely better than the constant worry of an accidental pregnancy.

"Are you feeling alright now?" he asked gently.

Josie nodded vigorously. "I'm good, all better. Whenever I'm feeling down, just do that to me. It's like a magic cure."

"You're unbelievable," Marcus muttered, tapping her lightly on the forehead.

Josie winked at him, her boldness growing with each passing day.

As they lay together, Marcus began to feel himself stirring again.

He pulled her closer, and soon they were lost in the heat of the moment once more.

It was another hour and a half before they finally collapsed, sated and content.

As Josie drifted into a deep sleep, Marcus finally entered his cultivation mode.

The fight with Aquila, the Black Hawk Legion leader, had left its mark on him.

His injuries weren't minor, and on top of that, absorbing Aquila's energy required intense refinement.

Marcus tapped into the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire, first healing his wounds until they were as good as new, then starting the long process of refining the energy he'd consumed.

The entire process took about five hours. By the time it was over, the energy had fully stabilized and been fully integrated into his system.

Aquila, being a mutant, had shown incredible strength in their fight, but much of that power came from genetic modifications and various performance-enhancing drugs.

In reality, his base energy was relatively weak, comparable to someone at the Fourth Stage of Ancient Martial Arts.

The energy Marcus had absorbed was not only potent but also carried a dangerous toxicity, similar to the corrosive fluid Aquila had spit out during their battle.

It seemed to be the very essence of that corrosive liquid.

While the refining process was difficult and the benefits minimal, Marcus still made a breakthrough, his cultivation had reached the threshold of the Seventh Stage of Ancient Martial Arts.

Reflecting on the battle, Marcus couldn't shake the lingering feeling of unease.

Even now, with his power at the Seventh Stage, he knew he couldn't afford to act recklessly.

There were too many powerful figures hidden in the world.

Take Aquila, for instance—on the surface, he was just the leader of the Black Hawk Legion.

His individual power seemed to be only on par with someone at the Fourth Stage of Ancient Martial Arts. But with his mutation, his strength had skyrocketed in a short period of time.

And the Black Hawk Legion? Just a mercenary group. If he were up against someone from a larger, more dangerous organization, Marcus knew their strength would be far more terrifying.

So, despite his progress, Marcus couldn't afford to become complacent. He needed to grow stronger, faster, if he wanted to survive and keep ahead of the many threats that loomed.

The friction with his two older brothers was also escalating.

He knew they wouldn't back down easily in the battle for Ryan's inheritance.

They'd do whatever it took to eliminate him, and if he didn't keep his guard up, they'd find a way to take him out.

The only way to stay safe was to grow stronger—much stronger.

Just as Marcus was lost in thought, his phone buzzed with a new message.

It was from Blake, the leader of the Axe Gang.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, are you in Kingston? The Axe Gang is under attack by Dragon Tiger Hall. We're in serious danger. We need your help..."
