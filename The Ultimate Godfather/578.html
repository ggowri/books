Chapter 577 A Penthouse and New Beginnings

After spending another night at Lila's place, Marcus hopped on a flight back to Solomia the next morning.

His stay in Kingston had dragged on far too long, and he had a promise to keep. Before leaving for Kingston, he had told Catherine he'd help her buy a house.

But it had been over ten days, and he'd already missed their agreed date.

As soon as his plane landed, Marcus pulled out his phone and dialed Catherine's number.

Catherine, enjoying a rare day off at home, practically lit up when she heard his voice.

Diana had offered to pick him up from the airport, but Marcus declined and took a cab straight to Catherine's neighborhood instead.

When he arrived at the gate of her apartment complex, his eyes immediately picked her out of the crowd.

At the entrance, a variety of street vendors had set up shop, adding to the lively atmosphere.

She wore a light pink dress, and even amidst the hustle and bustle, she stood out effortlessly.

Catherine's natural charm and gentle demeanor made her a magnet for attention.

Even with just a dab of makeup, she looked stunning.

"Catherine, did you miss me?" Marcus teased, sneaking up behind her and giving her shoulder a playful tap.

Catherine jumped with a small yelp, spinning around, her eyes wide.

"Marcus! That was quick!" she exclaimed, her voice bubbling with joy. "I'm so happy you're here!"

When she saw Marcus, a radiant smile spread across her face.

She bit her lip nervously and added, "I've been here for a while now. Maybe we don't need to rush into buying a house. I mean, I heard Solomia's real estate prices have shot up again."

Her tone was soft, almost hesitant as if she didn't want to burden him with the expense.

Marcus chuckled, shaking his head. "Absolutely not. This place isn't safe, and it's definitely not up to your standards. You're the general manager of Sync Group's Solomia branch now. You deserve better than this."

Catherine's cheeks turned a soft shade of pink. "Marcus, if it weren't for you, I wouldn't even have this job. I owe it all to you."

Her gratitude was heartfelt. Marcus had not only saved her mother's life but also given her the career of a lifetime.

"You're my woman," Marcus said with an easy grin. "It's my job to take care of you. No arguments—we're house shopping today."

He added with a wink, "And about commuting? Forget the subway. You need a car, and I'll take care of that too."

Catherine hesitated. "I've had my license for years, but honestly, I've barely driven. I'm scared I'll mess it up."

Marcus laughed. "Driving's not rocket science. You'll pick it up. Let's start with a small car to get you comfortable. Once you're a pro, we'll upgrade."

Catherine nodded slowly, trusting him, though a little unsure of herself.

They hailed a cab and headed to a Mini dealership, where Marcus bought her a brand-new Mini JCW for 350,000 dollars.

The car was less than 12 feet long—small, sleek, and oh-so-cute, yet it packed a punch that could rival some serious performance machines.

It was the ultimate wolf in sheep's clothing, a playful charmer with a wild side.

But what truly sealed the deal was its design—inside and out, every detail was crafted to perfection, blending elegance with fun.

Honestly, who could say no to a car this irresistibly adorable? It was practically made to steal hearts at first glance.

Catherine fell in love with it almost immediately. In less than an hour, the paperwork was signed, and Catherine was behind the wheel of her very first car.

They drove straight to Dragon Crest One, a luxury residential complex near Sync Group's Solomia office.

The community was a cut above the rest, boasting stunning river-view apartments and top-tier amenities.

Thanks to its prime location and excellent connectivity, the community offered top-notch amenities and services. With an average price exceeding 80,000 dollars per square meter and a minimum unit size of over 200 square meters, it was the very definition of a luxury residence.

Marcus knew that Dragon Crest One was a government-backed development under Solomia's Finance Bureau. The future residents would mostly be high-ranking officials, making it an exclusive and elite community.

In terms of safety, it was undoubtedly in a league of its own.

Marcus parked in the underground garage, where security guards directed them to a reserved spot with precision.

The sales office, sleek and modern, welcomed them with open arms.

A professional sales associate quickly stepped up to assist.

After an in-depth walkthrough of the available options, Marcus' eyes settled on the jewel of the complex.

A 380-square-meter penthouse, perched at the heart of the community, offered sweeping views of the city skyline.

The price tag was a cool thirty million dollars. And it was worth every cent.

Just as Marcus was about to seal the deal, a man and a young woman approached from across the room.

The man, short and wiry, looked to be in his early fifties.

The young woman, however, bore an uncanny resemblance to Catherine.

"Catherine? Is that really you?" the woman called out, her voice filled with surprise. "What are you doing here? Did you start working here?"

Catherine turned toward the voice, her brows lifting in recognition as her gaze landed on the approaching pair.
