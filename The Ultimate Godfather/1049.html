Chapter 949 A Deep Chill

"How'd you not watch your step?"

Marcus asked Chloe, his voice low but teasing.

Chloe wanted to crawl into a hole and die. Right then, she was fully wrapped in Marcus' arms, one of his big hands resting on her stomach, the other shamelessly cupping half her chest.

Her heart slammed against her ribs like a drumline gone rogue. She bolted upright, brushed her hair back into place, and blurted, "How'd the floor get so slick suddenly? I didn't smash into you, did I?"

Marcus grinned, shaking his head. "I'm good." Inside, though, he was smirking to himself.

Keep tumbling like that, and I'll get a handful of the other side next time.

Thanks to Chloe's little spill, Marcus had scored a feel of her perky, standout curves.

Just as he'd figured—full, generous, and way more than her slim frame suggested.

It was a wild contrast, like with Gertie, another girl he'd known. Both women were rail-thin everywhere else but stacked up top.

Chloe knew exactly where Marcus' hand had landed. Her cheeks flared redder than a traffic light, and she couldn't even look him in the eye anymore. "Good thing you're okay," she mumbled, scrambling for an out. "I'll, uh, grab you another coffee."

She started to bolt, but Marcus snagged her wrist and tugged her onto the couch beside him. "Forget it. Stay here with me. I already had a cup earlier."

His warm grip sent a jolt through her. Chloe's pulse raced, her breath hitching.

She tried to wiggle her hand free, but Marcus held on tight, leaving her no choice but to sit there, trapped in his hold.

"Your hands are freezing," he said, frowning a little. Her fingers felt like ice cubes, a faint chill seeping into his palm.

Chloe ducked her head, her face still flushed. "Girls get cold hands sometimes. Didn't you know that?"

"Sure, I've heard that," Marcus said, "but yours are next-level chilly."

She shrugged, voice quiet. "Guess it's from when my mom was pregnant with me. We didn't have much—barely kept the heat on, and food was scarce. Left me with a weak constitution and cold hands ever since."

"Huh," Marcus said, nodding. Touching her, he could sense it—a deep, stubborn chill running through her core. "You know, I might actually have a fix for that."

Chloe's brows shot up. "What, you're a doctor now?"

"Something like that," he said with a half-smile. "With how cold you run, does it hit you hard when your period rolls around?"

Her face went scarlet. The question was blunt and borderline invasive, and it threw her off.

But then she figured he was diagnosing her—like a patient, not a person. "Yeah," she admitted. "It's brutal. Feels like I'm dying every time—vomiting, passing out, the works. It's screwed up my job more than once. I've seen tons of doctors, but nothing's fixed it."

Marcus nodded, unfazed. "Let me take a quick look."

Before she could say yes or no, his hand was on her stomach again, pressing gently through her shirt.

Chloe flinched, her whole body tingling as heat bloomed under his touch.

Her cheeks burned, but she told herself he was checking her out—medically, that is.

She sat there, tense as a coiled spring, while a warm current flowed from his palm into her.

It spread through her abdomen, a weird mix of electric and soothing that made her squirm.

A soft hum slipped from her lips before she could stop it.

Her eyes fluttered shut as the heat sank deeper, easing something she hadn't even realized was knotted up inside her.

It lasted maybe two minutes before Marcus pulled his hand back.

He'd already clocked her issue: killer cramps from that icy chill in her system, bad enough to mess with her whole body.

"I can clear that cold out for you," he said casually. "Fix the cramps and whatever else it's stirring up there."

Chloe blinked, still dazed from the sensation. He was talking about using his Timeless Warrior's Grimoire, his voice steady as if he were offering to fix a busted phone instead of rewriting her whole damn system.

She didn't get a chance to ask more before a loud banging rattled the door.

"You, asshole, get out here! I brought backup—you're done for!"

It was Chloe's brother, Everett Sinclair, hollering like a lunatic.

"My brother's here," Chloe said, her face tightening. "He's probably got his sketchy crew with him. Stay put—I'll shoo them off."

"No." Marcus said, standing. "He keeps coming back. He'll just keep hassling you. Let's settle this now."

He swung the door open. Outside stood a handful of rough-looking guys, Everett front and center.

His face was swollen from a punch Marcus had landed earlier, giving him a lopsided, almost comical glare.

"Well, look at you, tough guy," Everett snarled. "Big balls stepping out here to die."

He jabbed a thumb at the guy next to him. "Meet Stanley—from Serpent's Coil outta Solomia. You messed with me, and now you're gonna pay."

Marcus raised an eyebrow, sizing up the group. "All of you with Serpent's Coil?"

He could tell at a glance—they were junkies, every one of them, including Everett.

Serpent's Coil was an underground outfit Marcus knew well; he'd taken it over a while back and laid down strict rules: no drugs, no dealing.

If these clowns were from Serpent's Coil, something was rotten in the ranks.

"Yeah, we're from Serpent's Coil," Stanley said, smirking. He was a big dude, broad-shouldered and cocky, looking less strung-out than the rest. "What, scared now?"

Chloe's stomach dropped. Serpent's Coil wasn't just some street gang—it was the top dog in Solomia's underworld.

Over the past year, it'd clawed its way to the top, pretty much owning the whole damn scene.

It wasn't just its size—it had heavy hitters, too.

Rumor had it half its crew were Wizards, with guys like Jalen, a master Wizard, and Edgar, a hardcore Warrior, leading the pack.

That kind of combo was rare as hell across Uswad's city underbellies.

And their gear? Top-notch.

They had laser guns that carved through rival gangs like butter—unstoppable, people said.

Marcus wasn't some lightweight; she'd watched him tear it up at that awards gala, his sniper team covering his back like a hawk squad.

Still, stacked against the Serpent's Coil's firepower, magic, and muscle, she couldn't shake the feeling he might be outmatched.

"Stanley, chill," she cut in, stepping forward. "This is just a dumb spat between me and Everett. Marcus isn't part of it."
