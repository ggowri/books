"Marcus, I'll explain to Gina. Please, just go ahead and treat my uncle. I can't stand watching him suffer like this," Catherine said, panic clear in her voice.

Marcus nodded, about to reach for Rodney's arm, when a loud voice cut through the tension.

"A guy with no medical qualifications, and he's talking like he's the expert? Get your filthy hands off him!"

People who had gathered around after Rodney collapsed turned to look towards the entrance of the sales office.

A group of doctors in white coats strode towards them, led by a man in his fifties.

He wore glasses, exuding an air of authority and confidence.

This was Berg Nell, the renowned head of cardiology at Solomia Second Hospital, the very doctor Gina had mentioned.

"Dr. Nell, thank you for coming! Please, you have to save my dad, he's hanging by a thread!"

Seeing Berg approach, Gina rushed to him, visibly relieved. Her voice quivered with emotion.

"Dr. Nell's here in person! Looks like these two really have some serious clout!"

"No kidding! Dr. Nell's one of the top cardiologists in Solomia. It's a miracle he's here—he's usually too busy at the clinic to make house calls."

The crowd murmured in admiration. It was clear they held Gina in high regard now that Berg Nell had arrived.

"Don't worry, sweetheart. With me here, your dad's in good hands," Berg said with complete confidence as he got to work.

Rodney had been Berg's patient for years. He knew the man's condition like the back of his hand, and had helped him pull through more than once.

Gina breathed a sigh of relief.

As Berg had said, her father had faced several life-threatening crises over the years, and it was always Berg who had helped him pull through.

At least she knew Berg had a handle on her father's condition.

Berg wasted no time, pulling out his stethoscope and beginning his examination of Rodney.

"His aorta's completely blocked. We need to act now, or we'll lose him," Marcus said, his voice firm with urgency.

Using his X-ray vision, Marcus could see the full extent of Rodney's condition. He wasn't going to let this man die if he could help it.

Since Rodney was Catherine's uncle, Marcus felt it was important to speak up. He would've ignored anyone else.

"Shut your mouth!"

Gina snapped, her eyes narrowing dangerously. "What are you going on about? Don't you dare interfere with Dr. Nell's treatment! If my dad dies because of you, I won't forgive you!"

"Who the hell is this kid?" Berg raised an eyebrow, clearly unimpressed. "Is he out of his mind, telling me how to do my job?"

Gina shot Marcus a scornful look and turned back to Berg, her voice dripping with sarcasm. "He's just my cousin's boyfriend. Some clueless guy who thinks he's a doctor. He even said he could treat my dad. Just ignore him, Dr. Nell, and focus on saving my father!"

Berg gave a short nod, not even sparing Marcus a second glance, and continued his examination of Rodney.

"Gina, seriously, you should listen to Marcus," Catherine urged, still desperate. "He really knows his stuff. We're running out of time, I'm worried about Uncle Rodney."

"Catherine, stop being so naive. Do you really think he knows anything about medicine?" Gina scoffed. "If I'd known you were dating someone like him, I would've put my foot down. He's a fraud. You need to be careful."

Catherine gave a long, helpless sigh. She was about to explain Marcus when suddenly, Rodney gasped for air.

His eyes widened, his face turning an awful shade of purple, his breathing erratic and shallow.

"Dad, what's happening? Please, don't do this to me!" Gina cried out, her voice breaking.

Seeing this, Gina's worry intensified. She rushed to Berg, her voice frantic. "Dr. Nell, please, you have to save him. I beg you!"

Berg's face grew more serious, but he still exuded confidence. "Don't worry. I know this man's condition inside and out. We've got this."

As he spoke, Dr. Nell grabbed more medical gear, preparing to take action.

But despite his efforts, Rodney's condition continued to worsen.

His skin darkened further, his pupils dilating.

"Dr. Nell, his pulse is barely there, it's slowing down," one of the nurses said, her voice filled with panic.

Berg frowned. "Get the adrenaline, quick. This is the protocol. I saved him like this before."

The nurse hurriedly grabbed the syringe, preparing to inject the adrenaline.

"Adrenaline is just going to speed up his death," Marcus said, his voice calm but firm.
