Chapter 130 Hijacking

The long-haired girl glared at Marcus with a look of disgust, clearly not pleased to run into him again.

Marcus set his luggage down and replied, "I didn't expect to see you again either. What a coincidence.

"But I really didn't mean any harm earlier—please don't call me a pervert."

She shot him a withering look and retorted, "You think you can just act like that and not face the consequences? What kind of man are you?"

Marcus felt a mix of anger and amusement. "The police cleared me of any wrongdoing. Are you seriously going to judge me based on just your word?"

The long-haired girl cursed under her breath and waved the stewardess over. "Can you please change my seat?"

The stewardess approached with a polite smile. "I'm sorry, Miss, but the seats on this flight are fixed, and we can't accommodate a change."

"Then I want to upgrade to first class," the long-haired girl demanded.

The stewardess's smile remained apologetic. "Unfortunately, first class is fully booked, and we can't offer you an upgrade. I hope you understand, Miss."

Fuming, the girl sat down with a look of frustration, having nowhere to direct her anger.

Marcus stayed silent, leaning back in his seat with his eyes closed, waiting for the plane to take off.

Sitting next to Marcus made the long-haired girl feel increasingly uncomfortable.

After a while, she couldn't contain herself any longer. She pulled out a rope from her backpack, separating the two seats, and said, "Don't cross the line, or there will be consequences."

Amused, Marcus wondered why she hated him so much.

Did she really just have a problem with all men?

The plane took off quickly.

Everyone switched their phones to airplane mode.

To avoid boredom, Marcus planned to sleep all the way to Solomia.

Meanwhile, the long-haired girl pulled out a book and began reading intently.

It was then that Marcus noticed her beauty.

Despite her bad temper, she was tall, had delicate features, and smooth skin.

She looked to be only a year or two older than Natalia, probably a college student.

He then noticed that the book she was reading was about fighting techniques, detailing professional combat methods, some of which were even lethal techniques that were banned in the ring.

This sparked Marcus's curiosity about her identity.

It was intriguing to see such a young and attractive girl studying fighting skills.

As if sensing his gaze, she shot him a glare and asked, "Why are you peeking at me? What do you think you understand?"

Marcus smiled and replied, "A lot of the moves in that book lack depth. I practice some fighting skills myself. If you're interested, we could exchange tips."

He wasn't bragging; as a Level Three warrior, he could easily spot flaws in common fighting techniques.

The girl threw him a look of disdain. "Stop bragging. You know fighting techniques?

"I've seen plenty of guys like you trying to pick me up. Just a warning: don't peek at me again, or I'll call the police."

She clearly didn't believe that Marcus had any real fighting experience; in her eyes, he was just another creep trying to hit on her.

Marcus sighed, feeling his good intentions go to waste.

He decided to shut up and closed his eyes, focusing on getting some sleep.

Suddenly, a cold voice rang out from somewhere on the plane, "Everyone raise your hands and don't move! I will shoot anyone who does!"

Marcus's eyes flew open as he saw several men standing up, pulling guns from their bags and threatening the terrified passengers.

Screams filled the cabin, and chaos erupted as many fell into panic.

The stewardesses turned pale and scrambled into a corner, trembling.

In an instant, Marcus understood they were experiencing a hijacking.

He had only seen such scenes in movies and never thought he would face one in real life.

But it seemed the hijackers had picked the wrong flight—they encountered Marcus.

He was ready to deal with them when the long-haired girl yanked him down. "Don't move! Do you want to get killed? These hijackers are serious—they've planned this and have guns. They could shoot you at any moment."

Marcus glanced at her, surprised. "We're going to die anyway, so why not give it a shot?"
