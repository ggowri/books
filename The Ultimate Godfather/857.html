Under the immense pressure, the soldiers gradually felt their breaths becoming constricted.

Their bodies trembled, and many were forced to crawl on the ground. Their eyes were filled with fear.

Some even bled from their ears and noses, dying on the spot.

This scene left Nolan and the two Swords Sect warriors completely stunned.

They never expected that Marcus possessed such power.

"How is this possible? How could you have such incredible strength?"

Nolan, sitting in his wheelchair, was filled with astonishment.

Before he could finish speaking, Marcus had already stepped forward. Like a ghost, he appeared before Nolan in the blink of an eye.

Nolan instinctively threw a punch, trying to push Marcus back.

But Marcus grasped Nolan's wrist instead.

Nolan struggled with all his might but could not break free.

With a casual twist, the sound of bones snapping echoed. Nolan's wrist was broken.

He let out a piercing scream and turned to the two Swords Sect warriors for help, saying, "Save me!"

Before the two warriors could act, Marcus had already snapped Nolan's neck.

Nolan's eyes widened, and his body slumped helplessly in the wheelchair, lifeless.

Up until the moment of his death, his eyes remained open.

"You dared to kill a disciple of the Swords Sect. Are you going to become an enemy of the Swords Sect?" One of the warriors asked.

He realized that Marcus' strength was not as simple as it appeared.

"Even your sect leader, Liam Sky, would not dare to speak to me in such a manner. You truly have some nerve," Marcus said with a cold smile.

The reason he did not immediately kill the two was entirely out of respect for Liam.

During the incident at Morvalis' space-time gate, Liam had treated him with considerable respect.

Thus, Marcus did not want to kill members of the Swords Sect.

After hearing Marcus' words, the two warriors wore mocking expressions.

One of them scoffed, "What a joke! I admit I underestimated your power, but you don't think your strength surpasses our sect leader's, do you? You're just dreaming."

The other one sneered, "Stop wasting time talking to him. Let's join forces and eliminate him."

Although Marcus had showcased impressive strength, the two still believed that if they worked together, they had enough power to take him down.

However, before they could act, a majestic voice resounded in the air.

The voice was transmitted through sound waves, meaning the speaker might be extremely far away, even thousands of miles away.

Yet, to the two warriors, it sounded as if it was right next to them.

"Stop it."

Importantly, only Marcus and the two Swords Sect warriors could hear this voice. Others could not.

Upon hearing the familiar voice, the two warriors were stunned and then showed respectful expressions. They looked up at the sky and asked, "Mr. Sky, is that you?"

"Who else could it be?" Liam responded through telepathy, his voice carrying a chilly intent.

"Mr. Sky, what do you want us to do?" The two asked respectfully.

"You two idiots. If I had not intervened in time, you would have caused a great disaster."

Liam's voice contained authority, resonating in the minds of the two warriors.

Confused, they asked Liam, "Mr. Sky, we do not know what trouble we have caused. Please tell us."

Liam replied, "You dared to disrespect Mr. Fitzgerald. You deserve to die a thousand deaths. Hurry and apologize to Mr. Fitzgerald."

The two warriors were taken aback and involuntarily looked at Marcus.

One of them asked Liam, "Mr. Sky, who is Mr. Fitzgerald? Could it be..."

They had begun to suspect but didn't dare confirm it.

"You two fools. Is there anyone besides Mr. Fitzgerald there?

"You have offended him today. If Mr. Fitzgerald holds a grudge, not only will you suffer, but the Swords Sect could also be implicated. Even I am powerless before Mr. Fitzgerald. With a wave of his hand, he could annihilate me.

"Apologize to Mr. Fitzgerald immediately. If you cannot obtain his forgiveness, I will ensure your demise even from a distance of a thousand miles," Liam said through gritted teeth.

The Swords Sect already had some minor friction with Marcus.

To quell these frictions, Liam had to lower his tone and appease Marcus to calm his anger.

He had just established a good relationship with Marcus, and now these two had offended him again.

This made Liam feel an impulse to crush those two.

Upon hearing Liam's words, the two warriors fell into despair, their eyes filled with disbelief.

They never imagined that Marcus' bravado was actually true.

In their eyes, the normally imposing Liam showed such respect towards Marcus, which they found unbelievable.

Liam was a powerful figure and his strength was in Ancient Martial Stage Eight.

In their minds, Liam was almost like a deity.

The normally imposing Liam was showing such respect to someone in his twenties.

This completely shattered their perception.

The two began to slap their own faces and apologized, "Mr. Fitzgerald, we were wrong. We were ignorant. We hope you won't hold it against us and let us go."

Marcus ignored the ignorant fools.

Liam's voice came through again. "Mr. Fitzgerald, it is my fault for not managing my subordinates well. Please do not take it out on them. If there is an opportunity, I will personally apologize to you."

Marcus replied coolly, "No need. Manage your disciples well. Next time I won't hold back." With that, Marcus turned and left.

Seeing Marcus leave, the two felt as if a weight had been lifted and quickly left.

Although Marcus had told Melina to wait in the villa, she was very curious about what was happening outside.

She pulled back the curtains and witnessed everything occurring outside.

She too was shocked by Marcus' display of power.

Faced with hundreds of armed warriors, Marcus still effortlessly killed Gabriel and Nolan.

Such dominance far exceeded Melina's imagination.

After Marcus returned to the villa, he called Lydia, asking her to send people to handle the aftermath.

As Marcus' personal assistant, Lydia was very skilled at such tasks.

Soon, she arrived with the Special Advisor Department, using the military's powerful influence to suppress all news.
