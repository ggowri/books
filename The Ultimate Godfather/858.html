The next day, news of Gabriel and Nolan's deaths spread like wildfire across Kingston, shaking the entire city to its core.

The Watson family plunged into chaos. Gabriel's brothers wasted no time making their moves, each vying for control of the family empire.

Meanwhile, the stock value of Watson-owned enterprises took a nosedive, none more so than SiliconWave Technologies, the family's flagship company. Its shares plummeted below 50 Uswadian coins—a historic low since its inception.

SiliconWave Technologies wasn't just any corporation. It was one of Uswad's top semiconductor manufacturers, boasting cutting-edge technology that could rival even the global superpowers. At its peak, the company's stock had soared to 500 Uswadian coins per share. Now, it was worth less than a tenth of that.

Despite the tempting price, few dared to step in. The Watson family's internal power struggle made the situation too volatile. Investors feared that pouring money into the company would be like stepping into quicksand—impossible to escape. As a result, Watson-owned assets became untouchable and deemed too risky for acquisition.

The infighting escalated rapidly. That very night, a violent clash broke out within the family, resulting in heavy casualties as factions battled for dominance. Then, when SiliconWave Technologies' stock hit rock bottom at 35 Uswadian coins per share, an enigmatic figure known as Death made his move.

With a staggering 350 billion Uswadian coins, he executed a full takeover of SiliconWave Technologies, making himself its sole owner.

For those familiar with Uswad's business scene, Death was no stranger. He had previously acquired Sync Group and Eastcomm Group—two rising industry giants owned by Kingston's Brown family—along with Bayside Petroleum Company in Solomia and Uswad's largest entertainment conglomerate, Royal Entertainment.

Under his control, these companies had not only rebounded but flourished, their market values multiplying several times over. Each of his investments had been nothing short of genius, earning him the title of a legend in the Uswad business world.

Death was now synonymous with unparalleled investment acumen, a name revered by all in the financial sphere. And now, in less than a month, he had struck again—securing SiliconWave Technologies for 350 billion Uswadian coins and officially entering the semiconductor industry. But he wasn't done yet.

On the same day, he shelled out another 150 billion Uswadian coins to acquire MedicaGenix, a major subsidiary of the Watson family, claiming full ownership. With a total of 500 billion Uswadian coins spent in two swift moves, Death had once again reshaped Kingston's business landscape.

His latest acquisitions dominated headlines across Uswad, sparking heated discussions everywhere. Adding SiliconWave Technologies and MedicaGenix to his empire only strengthened Marcus' grip on Kingston's economy, further expanding his already formidable business empire. And all of this? Orchestrated remotely from Melina's villa.

From a distance, Marcus could see her locked in a tense exchange with a tall man wearing glasses.

Their argument was intense, with the man clearly overpowering her in both tone and presence. He spoke loudly, and forcefully, and even reached out to grab her arm multiple times. Jade yanked her arm away repeatedly, but the man refused to back down.
