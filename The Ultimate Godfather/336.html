Marcus raised an eyebrow, glancing at Shaun and Aiton. "Just you lot? And you think you can kill me?"

Shaun sneered, "I might not be a match for you, but Mr. Hudson has brought his 4th-generation cyborgs. No matter how strong you are, there's no way you can handle eight of them."

Shaun's impression of Marcus was still stuck on the events of Matthew's 80th birthday.

Back then, Marcus was only at the Ancient Martial Stage Three, far from being a match for these advanced cyborgs.

But Marcus' progress had been astonishingly rapid.

In less than a month, he had advanced from Ancient Martial Stage Three to the peak of the Ancient Martial Stage Four.

He had also mastered lethal techniques such as the Devouring Power and Shadow Step.

His strength had more than doubled, and dealing with these half-human, half-machine monstrosities was no longer a challenge.

"Mr. Whitman, don't waste time on him. Watch how I slay him," Aiton said.

He issued a command to the cyborgs.

The eight cyborgs let out a unified roar and charged toward Marcus.

Maxine immediately drew her pistol and fired at the two leading cyborgs.

The gunfire rang out in rapid succession.

Shockingly, the cyborgs didn't dodge or flinch.

They marched forward step by step, the bullets ricocheting harmlessly off their bodies.

Maxine was shocked. She had never imagined that these so-called cyborgs could be immune to bullets.

"Marcus, what should we do?" Maxine asked.

She wasn't sure if Marcus could handle these monsters.

"Step aside. I'll take care of them," Marcus replied, gesturing for her to retreat.

Once Maxine moved to a safe distance, Marcus retrieved a compact submachine gun from his storage ring.

The weapon was embedded with a blue energy crystal.

It radiated a powerful energy wave that filled the place.

This gun had been modified by Diana, and it was the best among the weapons she had provided.

Marcus carried it wherever he went.

Today was the perfect opportunity to test its power.

"Marcus, these monsters are immune to ordinary weapons. Your gun won't work," Maxine shouted.

She was worried he might underestimate the enemies.

"Just watch," Marcus said, giving her a reassuring glance.

One of the cyborgs lunged toward him.

Enhanced by 4th-generation mechanical augmentations, its punch carried the force of ten thousand pounds.

But before the cyborg's fist could land, Marcus pulled the trigger.

A beam of destructive light shot out from the submachine gun.

It contained terrifying destructive force.

The light beam struck the cyborg, sending it crashing to the ground.

The explosive energy blasted a bowl-sized crater into its chest.

Its internal machinery was exposed and began to smoke.

Without hesitation, Marcus fired again.

Another beam pierced the cyborg's chest, destroying its mechanical core.

In seconds, flames erupted, engulfing the cyborg's body. Within moments, it was reduced to a charred husk.

"What?" Shaun and Aiton were stunned.

"What kind of gun is that? How can it be so powerful?" Aiton muttered in disbelief.

His 4th-generation cyborgs were as strong as practitioners at Ancient Martial Stage Three.

Together, they were comparable to an expert at the peak of Ancient Martial Stage Four.

That level of strength was more than enough to kill Marcus.

But he hadn't expected Marcus to wield such a devastating weapon.

The gun was clearly of Acoton origin. It was one of their latest submachine gun models.

Yet, it didn't fire bullets but beams.

"You're not qualified to know," Marcus laughed, pulling the trigger again and targeting the second cyborg.

Thanks to the high-grade energy crystal embedded in the weapon, its power was unmatched.

Each shot carried the strength of an expert at Ancient Martial Stage Three. Even the advanced defenses of these cyborgs couldn't withstand the relentless barrage.

Explosions echoed continuously.

The cyborgs were hit and struck down.

In less than two minutes, all eight cyborgs were reduced to scrap metal.

Maxine was astounded by the scene.
