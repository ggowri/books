The air was pierced by two terrifying shockwaves.

The bodies of the two 5th-generation cyborgs exploded into nothingness, torn apart without even a shred of resistance.

Their proud defense, more than three times stronger than the 4th-generation cyborgs, was utterly useless against Marcus' fist ray—the devastating force of his fist.

For the next several minutes, Marcus unleashed a barrage of destructive attacks.

The 5th-generation cyborgs could only block, unable to even think about fighting back.

In less than ten minutes, every last one of them had been annihilated.

The sight of this slaughter left the Black Hawk Legion's top leaders pale with shock. They had never anticipated Marcus' strength to be so overwhelming.

"Your turn," Marcus sneered, his voice dripping with disdain.

He took a single step forward, and in an instant, Shadow Step activated.

His form blurred, scattering into countless afterimages, and within a blink, he appeared before the two highest-ranking members of the Black Hawk Legion.

They froze in terror, panic rising in their chests as they desperately tried to flee.

But they were no match for Marcus.

With a flick of his wrist, two Firebolts shot out, engulfing the two men in searing flames.

The air filled with their agonized screams, but the fire consumed them quickly.

Less than a minute later, all that remained was ash and smoke.

Two more high-ranking members tried to make a break for it.

Marcus drew a compact submachine gun and fired two deadly energy beams.

The two men's heads exploded in violent bursts, their lives snuffed out in an instant.

The tension in the air was suffocating.

Without missing a beat, Marcus shifted his stance, his foot barely touching the ground before he was right in front of another leader.

His fist slammed into the man's chest with a deafening crack.

The force of the blow ripped a massive hole through his torso, and the man crumpled to the ground, dead before he hit the floor.

In a flash, every top leader of the Black Hawk Legion—except for Aquila—lay dead at his feet.

Marcus' cold, piercing gaze locked onto Aquila. "Where is Josie?"

His voice was lethal, his mind laser-focused on one thing—Josie's safety.

Aquila's lips curled into a sinister grin, and he sneered. "You want to know where your girl is? Beat me first."

His expression twisted into something dark and eerie.

In the next instant, Aquila pulled several vials from his pocket, each filled with a different colored serum.

Without hesitation, he jammed the needles into his abdomen, injecting the strange concoctions into his body.

Marcus watched in disbelief as Aquila's body began to change before his eyes.

His muscles swelled and his bones shifted, cracking and popping as his form grew unnaturally larger.

His body expanded at a terrifying pace, his veins bulging and his muscles distorting to their limits.

And then, his head... it morphed into something grotesque—a monstrous dragon-like visage.

His jaw unhinged into a massive, gaping maw, revealing rows of razor-sharp teeth that gleamed with an unsettling hunger. It was like staring into the face of a nightmare.

A mutant.

Marcus finally pieced it together—Aquila wasn't just a normal human.

He was a mutant, and from the looks of it, a highly advanced one.

The changes in his physique weren't just genetic enhancement; they were the result of some twisted experiment. Marcus could see the telltale signs of dragon DNA and other altered genes coursing through him.

"You pathetic fool," Aquila sneered, his voice dripping with venom. "Let's see how long you can last in front of me!"

Without another word, Aquila roared, his voice shaking the very air. Then, he lunged at Marcus, his gargantuan form moving with terrifying speed.

Despite the sheer size of his body—over nine feet tall—he moved with the swiftness of a predator.

One massive, clawed hand shot toward Marcus, radiating an aura of dragon-like ferocity.

The claws glowed with a blood-red light, tearing through the air as if they were cutting space itself.

Marcus didn't flinch. His Timeless Warrior's Grimoire flared to life, his Thunderfire Power crackling around him as he unleashed a devastating punch, meeting Aquila's attack head-on.

The collision was deafening. The shockwave tore through the air, sending waves of destructive force rippling outward.

Marcus was driven back several steps, his arm going numb from the impact.

Aquila's lips curled into a mocking smile. "I've been augmented with countless cutting-edge research projects," he taunted. "To become the ultimate dragon mutant, I've gone through hell. And now, I'm going to carve you up."

As he spoke, Aquila threw back his head and opened his massive mouth wide, releasing a jet of pitch-black flames.

The fire blazed toward Marcus, scorching the air with an overwhelming heat.

As it spread, it gave off a nauseating stench—an acrid, corrosive vapor that seemed to eat away at everything in its path.

Marcus' face tightened with concern. He hadn't expected Aquila's strength to be this terrifying.

In a flash, he drew his golden scimitar, slashing it through the air with precision. The blade sliced through the space between them, sending out sharp, deadly arcs of energy.

Each strike was perfectly aimed, but Aquila barely reacted.

The energy slashes hit his body with a sound like metal on metal—loud, but ultimately futile. His skin, mutated and reinforced by the strange serums, didn't even flinch.

Aquila continued his relentless advance, undeterred, and Marcus' attacks seemed to do nothing more than tickle him.

Desperate, Marcus unleashed a barrage of Lightning and Firebolts, each strike aimed at exploiting some weakness. At the same time, he activated his X-ray Vision, scanning Aquila's mutated body for any sign of vulnerability.

But Aquila remained unfazed, shrugging off the blows with terrifying ease.
