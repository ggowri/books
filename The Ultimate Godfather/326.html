Marcus nodded.

He got out of the car alongside Tony, walking toward the port.

As they moved, Tony dialed a number on his phone.

After a brief conversation, he led Marcus to a specific dock.

Several men approached them. Marcus could see pistols tucked into their belts.

Tony spoke to the leader, a man missing one finger on his hand, "Jack, Mr. Fitzgerald wants to buy Acoton's latest model of submachine guns. How many can you get here today?"

The man with nine fingers sized Marcus up and asked, "Where are you from? Care to introduce yourself?"

He said and offered Marcus a business card.

Marcus took it without answering.

Tony quickly smoothed things over. "Mr. Fitzgerald is from Solomia. He's also a warrior. Please give us a favor, Jack. Maybe offer a discount?"

Jack said with a smile, "There are plenty of buyers here. Being a warrior doesn't mean much. I've seen all kinds of people. Look over there. The man is a warrior, too. Whoever offers the highest price gets the goods!"

Marcus followed Jack's gaze and saw a man with long flowing hair standing on a nearby abandoned ship.

Judging by that man's energy, he was indeed a warrior, and his strength had reached Ancient Martial Stage Two. In the secular world, he was already a master.

Apparently overhearing the conversation, the man leaped down from the ship and walked toward them.

He sized Marcus up and said to Jack, "I don't like bidding against others. I prefer to solve things with strength. If he can defeat me, the entire shipment of guns is his."

As he spoke, the long-haired man's energy flared, and his gaze burned with fighting spirit.

Without warning, he unleashed a powerful palm strike, its energy so strong that Tony and the others were forced to stumble backward.

Marcus, however, didn't dodge or flinch. He simply watched the long-haired man approach.

With a loud sound, the force of the strike descended on Marcus.

But it stopped just one millimeter from Marcus' body, blocked by a faint protective barrier.

The long-haired man's eyes widened in disbelief.

"You? You dare call yourself my opponent?"

Marcus sneered and pointed a finger, and a burst of Numinous Energy silently shot out from his fingertip, piercing through the man's throat.

The long-haired man clutched his neck, his eyes filled with unwillingness.

His body collapsed to the ground. Before his death, he spoke out his final words, "You dare kill me? The Dragon Hall will not let you go!"

With that, his body fell still.

"Tsk..."

The chilling scene made Tony and Jack gasp audibly, their spines tingling with fear.

They had never expected Marcus' strength to be so terrifying.

In their eyes, the long-haired man was already an elite among elites.

But he couldn't even withstand a single move from Marcus. His throat had been pierced, and he died instantly.

They felt a cold sweat running down his back.

Tony, in particular, silently thanked himself for having been wise enough to submit earlier.

If he had offended Marcus, he would undoubtedly have met the same fate.

Jack's attitude shifted immediately. No longer acting arrogant, he turned to Marcus with a respectful tone. "Mr. Fitzgerald, I'll personally see to it that the next shipment of guns belongs to you!"

Marcus simply nodded in acknowledgment without a word.

At 2 AM, a ship carrying Acoton's latest model submachine guns docked at the port.

Under Jack's guidance, Marcus boarded the vessel and inspected the cargo. It was indeed the latest high-end shipment.

These submachine guns were the same as the ones used by Diana, except she only had a dozen of them, whereas this ship carried thousands.

It was enough to arm a small battalion.

Smuggled goods' price was naturally higher than the factory rate.

In the end, Marcus purchased the entire shipment for one hundred billion dollars.

Marcus couldn't take thousands of guns with him.

Jack promised to arrange for the weapons to be delivered to Solomia.

Satisfied, Marcus left the port with Tony.

With the arms problem resolved, Marcus returned to his hotel. It was already 4 AM. He collapsed onto the bed and drifted off into a deep sleep.

The next thing he knew, it was 9 AM, and Marcus was woken up by a loud knocking on the door.
