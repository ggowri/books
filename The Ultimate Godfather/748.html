Marcus couldn't care less about what the people at Divine Mount thought.

He had already brought Ada to the Morvalis Ice Wall region.

They landed on a massive ice floe floating on the ocean surface, spanning hundreds of yards in diameter like a mobile island drifting on the waves.

Before they loomed the colossal Ice Wall, hundreds of meters high, stretching endlessly to the horizon.

It stood like an impenetrable barrier, completely isolating their world from whatever lay beyond.

To ordinary people, the sky above the Ice Wall appeared normal. Still, Marcus could faintly detect an almost imperceptible constraining force.

This force seemed beyond anything a regular warrior could control, belonging to an entirely different dimension.

Marcus attempted to probe the barrier with his spiritual senses.

His mind exploded like a thunderclap when his spiritual powers touched the constraining force, nearly knocking him off his feet.

His heart raced wildly, cold sweat breaking out on his forehead as he barely managed to stand.

"This constraining force is overwhelming! Far beyond what any warrior like us could handle. No wonder humans have lived on Earth for millions of years without many venturing beyond the Morvalis Ice Wall!"

Marcus thought to himself, his eyes trying to peer beyond the barrier.

The Ice Wall proved vastly more extensive than he had imagined.

His mortal eyes couldn't penetrate beyond the Ice Wall. The massive structure loomed before him like ancient armor, completely sealing off the outside world.

Where sky met earth, everything faded into an endless white expanse with no visible end.

"How... how did we suddenly get here?"

Ada finally snapped out of her shock.

She vaguely remembered standing at the peak of Divine Mount just moments ago. They'd somehow traveled to the Morvalis Ice Wall region in the blink of an eye.

It felt surreal that she had to double-check to ensure that this wasn't just a dream, but everything around her seemed very real.

"I brought you here," Marcus replied coolly, his eyes still scanning the surroundings.

"But... but how did you do it?"

Ada was too stunned to take in their surroundings, still trying to process what had just happened.

Marcus' abilities had shattered entirely her understanding of what was possible.

The distance between Divine Mount's peak and the Morvalis Ice Wall region was over 6200 miles.

Even humanity's fastest technology, intercontinental cruise missiles, would take about fifteen minutes to cover that distance.

While some mighty warriors had mastered flight techniques, reaching this area would still take several hours or even days.

Marcus had transported her to the Morvalis Ice Wall region in just one second.

To Ada, this was nothing short of a miracle.

"Don't ask questions you shouldn't ask. Understand?"

Marcus replied dismissively.

As his cultivation progressed, his teleport abilities grew exponentially more potent.

He could now move freely to any location on Earth he knew of and even transport another person along with him.

The energy cost had also decreased significantly compared to before.

At this moment, using only thirty percent of his energy, he had successfully transported himself and Ada to the Morvalis Ice Wall region.

Ada's beautiful eyes sparkled, her heart trembling with amazement.

She watched Marcus sit cross-legged on the floating ice, channeling the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire to restore his depleted Numinous Energy.

It was Ada's first time witnessing Marcus in cultivation.

His cultivation manuals seemed quite different from that of ordinary warriors.

The Numinous Energy radiating from his body was utterly distinct from that of everyday warriors.

It was more refined and integrated better with his body, strengthening his bones and nourishing every organ and cell.

Ada had experienced this miraculous Numinous Energy when Marcus removed her Blood Curse.

Witnessing Marcus' cultivation firsthand, Ada was increasingly amazed by the miraculous power of the Timeless Numinous Energy.

In just fifteen minutes, Marcus had fully restored the damaged Numinous Energy within his body.

As he slowly concluded his practice and stood up, he had returned to his peak condition, his eyes radiating with a sharp gleam.

"Liam and others haven't arrived yet. What should we do now?" Ada asked Marcus.

Marcus' gaze fell upon the Morvalis Ice Wall, his pupils flashing with determination. "I want to scout the space-time gate area first."

Ada responded, "I've heard the Morvalis Ice Wall's space-time gate is located in the deep sea near the ice-strengthened zone. It could be dangerous for you to go alone."

A look of resolve flashed across Marcus' eyes. "This is a risk I'll have to face sooner or later. Might as well be now."

Seeing Marcus' determination, Ada took a pearl from her Spatial Ring and handed it to him.

The pearl was about two inches in diameter, a deep sapphire blue that seemed to glow from within. It was utterly transparent and radiated blue light.

"This is the Aqua Sphere. It will protect you from the ocean's pressure and let you breathe underwater normally," Ada explained.

Marcus pocketed the Aqua Sphere. "Thanks. Be careful while you're here. If you run into trouble, break this amulet, and I'll know you need me."

He handed Ada a yellowed amulet, then walked to the edge of the ice floe and jumped straight in.

When his body hit the water, he felt the bone-chilling cold.

However, the sensation lasted only a second before an invisible force pushed it away.

Marcus instinctively looked down to see the Aqua Sphere glowing in his pocket.

The light quickly enveloped his body, creating a barrier between him and the seawater.

Just as Ada had said, under the Aqua Sphere's influence, the water no longer affected his body.

Not only could he breathe normally, but he couldn't even feel the usual sensation of being submerged.

Except for being able to swim freely through the water, it felt exactly like being on land.

Marcus thought, "This Aqua Sphere is truly remarkable."

His body gradually descended toward the deep ocean zone.

The waters teemed with various marine life.

Due to the remoteness of the Morvalis region, many of these fish species were entirely new to Marcus.

As Marcus dove deeper, numerous fish gathered around him, seemingly curious about their unexpected visitor.

Soon, Marcus reached a depth of about one thousand meters.

It was the limit of sunlight penetration, with nothing but pitch darkness below, like staring into an abyss.

At this thousand-meter depth, the water pressure increased dramatically.

Without the Aqua Sphere's protection, no ordinary person could withstand such terrifying pressure.

As Marcus continued his descent, he extended his spiritual powers, attempting to probe the deeper regions.

However, that area seemed sealed by some constraining force, completely blocking his investigation into his spiritual powers.
