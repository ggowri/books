Chapter 768 The Death of Nathan

Under the astonished gaze of the crowd, Marcus struck back against Alonzo's Hand Strike with a seemingly effortless Fist Ray.

At first glance, the punch appeared light, as if it carried no power at all.

Yet, that gentle-looking Fist Ray intercepted Alonzo's destructive Hand Strike with precision.

A resounding boom echoed as the two forces collided.

Alonzo's lips curled into a sneer. He couldn't believe Marcus dared to challenge him.

With a surge of energy, Alonzo poured more destructive force into his attack, determined to annihilate Marcus completely.

But his arrogance didn't last long. In moments, Alonzo felt an overwhelming power emanating from Marcus' Fist Ray. It was a force that seemed capable of obliterating everything in its path.

That power engulfed his Hand Strike entirely.

Then came another deafening boom. In the face of Marcus' deceptively simple punch, Alonzo's Hand Strike shattered into dust.

Marcus' Fist Ray continued unabated, cutting through the void and slamming directly into Alonzo's chest.

Alonzo let out a blood-curdling scream.

His body shot through the air like a cannonball, crashing into a towering iceberg with devastating force.

The impact triggered a thunderous crack. The entire iceberg crumbled, collapsing into icy rubble.

Alonzo spat blood uncontrollably, his body trembling as he slid to the ground.

His chest had caved in, and all traces of his former arrogance were gone.

His face now showed only fear and disbelief.

Pointing a shaking finger at Marcus, Alonzo stammered, "This... this can't be! How are you this powerful?

"I'm an Ancient Martial Stage Nine expert! How could I lose to you? How is this possible?"

With those words, Alonzo's body gave out entirely. He slumped lifelessly to the ground, his breath extinguished.

The air fell silent. Everyone stood frozen, staring in shock at the scene before them.

Not a single person could believe what they had just witnessed.

No one could have anticipated that Marcus would be able to defeat an Ancient Martial Stage Nine expert with a single blow.

The onlookers were stunned, their hearts trembling with fear as they looked at Marcus in utter horror.

Even Ada was left stunned.

She had always known Marcus was powerful, someone who could easily dispatch opponents like Nathan.

But when she heard that Alonzo had reached Ancient Martial Stage Nine, even her faith in Marcus' abilities had wavered.

After all, an expert at Stage Nine was considered invincible in their world.

Alonzo's breakthrough had cemented his status as the strongest in Uswad, the world of Ancient Martial Art. But now, Marcus had shattered that perception with one casual punch.

However, Marcus paid no mind to the shocked stares.

He glanced at the lifeless Alonzo, then casually took his Spatial Ring.

He walked over to Ada, gently caressing her swollen cheek in front of everyone. "Does it hurt?" he asked.

Ada's body trembled slightly.

In the past, she would have pulled away without hesitation.

After all, she had always maintained a pure and proud image in front of the experts in the world of Ancient Martial Art.

Being touched like this in front of everyone clashed with her usual persona.

But now, all she did was tremble before lifting her face to meet Marcus' gaze. "It doesn't hurt," she said softly.

Marcus channeled a thread of Timeless Numinous Energy into her cheek. The redness and swelling vanished instantly, leaving her skin flawless once more.

His gaze shifted to Nathan, who was standing a short distance away, utterly petrified.

Nathan had been completely broken by fear.

Until moments ago, he believed that with Alonzo backing him, he could act as he pleased in Uswad.

He had thought that if Marcus dared to show himself, Alonzo would kill him without a second thought.

But what he never imagined was that the seemingly invincible Alonzo would fall so easily before Marcus.

Alonzo hadn't even been able to take a single punch before being killed on the spot.

This shattered Nathan's entire worldview. Every belief he had crumbled under that single blow.

Now his face was pale, filled with despair and terror.

Marcus approached him with a cold expression. "You dared to lay a hand on my woman. Do you think I'd let you live? Killing you is the only way to appease my anger."

Without another word, Marcus raised his hand. A beam of destructive light burst forth from his fingertips.

It streaked through the air, striking Nathan squarely on the forehead.

With a sickening explosion, Nathan's head burst into a cloud of blood and mist.

His body collapsed to the ground.

The crowd trembled once more.

No one could have imagined that the arrogant Nathan, who had been so full of himself, would meet such a tragic end.

"He said I'm his woman. Did he mean it?" Ada thought, her heart racing.

Watching the powerful figure before her, Ada bit her lip, feeling a surge of admiration for Marcus. He was so handsome, so commanding, and so reassuring. As she thought about it, her cheeks flushed slightly.

Marcus had no idea what Ada was thinking. He scanned the remaining experts from Uswad with a calm, piercing gaze. "The space-time gate is about to open," he said. "But with your current abilities, entering it will almost certainly lead to your death.

"Ada and I will be exploring the space-time gate together. If anyone wishes to follow, I won't stop you. But don't expect me to save you if you're in danger. Make your decision wisely."

With that, Marcus turned back to Ada. Taking her hand gently, he asked, "Shall we explore the space-time gate together?"

Ada didn't resist. Instead, she tightened her grip on his hand and nodded eagerly. "I'm willing."

In an instant, Marcus and Ada disappeared into the vast ocean.

The remaining experts in Uswad exchanged uncertain looks.

Some chose to stay behind, giving up the chance to explore the space-time gate. Others, driven by ambition or desperation, dove into the ocean, chasing after Marcus and Ada.

Liam, who had lived a samsara, had worked his way up to become the leader of the Swords Sect through his own wisdom.

Based on the immense power Marcus had just demonstrated, Liam believed Marcus' words to be highly credible.

He knew that in Marcus' eyes, they posed no threat to him, and there was no reason for him to deceive them.

So, Liam decided to stay where he was and observe the results of Marcus and the others entering the space-time gate.

He planned to gather experience so that the next time the space-time gate opened, he would be prepared to venture inside.

Some of the others, eager to explore the space-time gate, were already making their move.

They dove into the ocean, heading toward the deep-sea rift.

However, with their current cultivation, they couldn't even reach the bottom before succumbing to the pressure.

And those who managed to press on met their ends at the hands of countless unseen dangers.
