"Catherine, you look stunning today," Marcus couldn't help but compliment.

Catherine blushed slightly. "I was worried the recruiters wouldn't be impressed, so I decided to change up my style a bit."

"Maybe this is the look you were always meant to have," Marcus teased with a smile.

After lunch, they headed to the nearby CBD business district.

This area was filled with towering skyscrapers, the financial hub of Solomia, home to the headquarters of many listed companies and multinational corporations.

The two of them visited several companies and got a general sense of the job opportunities and salary ranges in the area.

For a prestigious graduate like Catherine, the typical salary was around 10,000 to 15,000 dollars per month.

Given her experience as a sales office manager, Catherine was also qualified for managerial positions, where the salary could range from 20,000 to 30,000 dollars.

These positions often came with benefits like insurance, year-end bonuses, paid vacation—things that made them very appealing.

For Catherine, this was more than enough. She simply wanted a stable job, one that wouldn't leave her facing the risk of unemployment like her previous role, where once the real estate project sold out, her job could end too.

"Marcus, look over there! Sync Group is hiring for their Solomia branch!"

Catherine pointed toward a building at the corner of the street, where colorful flags waved in the breeze and workers were bustling around, setting up for interviews.

A large banner hung across the building, reading: Sync Group: Uswad's Leading Emerging Industry Brand—Solomia Branch is Now Hiring!

Marcus raised an eyebrow. Not long ago, Stephen, the president of Sync Group, had briefed him about the company's plans to expand into Solomia and invest billions to establish a local branch.

He had approved the project, and it seemed to be progressing smoothly.

The location for the branch had already been secured, and now they were in the hiring phase.

"Marcus, Sync Group is a leader in Kingston's emerging industries. Recently, after being acquired by someone called Death, their performance skyrocketed, and their market value has nearly tripled!"

Catherine continued, her voice filled with excitement. "I can't believe a company like Sync Group is opening a branch here in Solomia. It looks like they're almost done with the hiring process. I should've come earlier!"

Catherine's eyes lit up with anticipation as she grabbed Marcus' arm and led him toward the recruitment area.

Sync Group, one of the hottest emerging companies in recent months, was already a well-known name in Solomia.

Many talented individuals had flocked to the area, eager to showcase their skills and join the company.

Sync Group's compensation packages were among the best in the CBD business district.

Even entry-level employees earned over 15,000 Uswad dollars a month, and managerial positions like the ones Catherine was eyeing could easily reach 30,000 or more.

Despite the scorching sun and temperatures above 30°C, a long line of applicants stretched out in front of the building.

Recognizing that Catherine needed to prove her own worth, Marcus didn't get involved. The two of them stood in line for almost an hour before it was finally their turn.

Just then, a staff member from Sync Group appeared, escorting a tall, graceful young woman toward the front of the line.

"Ms. Hope, you can join the line here. You'll be up next," the staff member said, guiding the woman into position.

The people behind them, some visibly frustrated, held their tongues.

Many had seen this before—a clear case of someone with connections skipping the queue.

Those without money or power knew their protests would be futile and only risked upsetting the interviewers, so most chose to remain silent and endure.

The woman, "Ms. Hope," turned to glance at the long line behind her, her expression a mix of smug satisfaction.

Since Catherine was ahead of her in line, Marcus didn't say anything either.

"Catherine, is that you?"

The woman's voice suddenly rang out, a note of surprise in her tone as she recognized Catherine.

Catherine turned around, equally startled. "Gloria? What a coincidence! It's been, what, six or seven years since we last saw each other?"

She smiled warmly, genuinely happy to see an old face.

The woman who had cut the line was none other than Gloria, Catherine's high school classmate.

Back then, Catherine's family had been struggling, and she was often overlooked.

Gloria, on the other hand, had been different. Her parents were successful business owners, and she had a well-off background.

Gloria was stunningly beautiful, always fashionable, with a dominant personality and a knack for talking her way into things.

By her second year, she had become the president of the student council, and her classmates had grown wary of her.

During high school, Catherine and Gloria had never been close. In fact, Catherine had often been bullied by Gloria and a few of the other girls.

After graduation, the two had lost contact, and now, years later, here they were, bumping into each other at Sync Group's hiring event.

"It's been a while, hasn't it?" Gloria said with a bright smile.

Her gaze flickered over to Marcus, and she asked, almost absentmindedly, "Catherine, who's this? Your boyfriend?"
