The sheer force sent Yasser crashing into a nearby building, which crumbled upon impact.

His eyes widened in sheer terror and despair as he coughed up blood in torrents.

With a single punch, Marcus had shattered his insides, organs, bones, and cells alike.

Brett's heart wrenched with anguish at the sight. His roar of fury echoed through the air. "Yasser! You killed my son! I will make you pay for this!"

As he spoke, Brett unleashed a torrent of destructive energy, attempting to crush Marcus beneath his power.

But Marcus didn't even flinch.

With a swift motion, his hand clamped down on Brett's throat. A sickening snap rang out as Brett's neck was broken.

Brett's eyes bulged in shock and desperation.

Never in his wildest dreams had he imagined dying like this, nor did he expect his son to meet the same fate.

At that moment, the reality hit him. His death was inevitable, and the entire Tucker family would likely be wiped out in the aftermath.

A wave of regret flooded over Brett. It was too late to change anything.

He cursed himself for ever urging Yasser to challenge Marcus in Solomia.

If he hadn't, perhaps none of this would have happened.

But now, it was too late.

He was dead, and the rest of his family would follow.

Marcus casually discarded Brett's lifeless body and collected the Dragon's Wrath Formation in Spatial Ring.

He could tell the Dragon's Wrath Formation had tremendous potential, but Brett had barely unlocked any of its power.

Marcus planned to study it later, figuring it could be useful in future battles.

Seeing that both Brett and Yasser were dead, the remaining Tucker family members were paralyzed with fear.

They lost the will to fight back and scrambled in all directions, desperate to escape Marcus' grasp.

But it was already too late for them.

Marcus flicked his hand, summoning a devastating burst of Thunderfire Power from the heavens.

It rained down in a blazing inferno, engulfing the entire Tucker Estate in an instant.

Within seconds, the once-proud Tucker family was obliterated, trapped in a storm of lightning and fire.

Screams and cries filled the air as the firestorm tore through them.

Less than thirty minutes later, not a single Tucker family member was left standing.

What had once been one of the most respected families in Uswad's Ancient Martial Art world had been wiped off the map.

One wrong decision had sealed their fate, erasing their centuries-old legacy.

Marcus stood at the center of the devastation, his presence chilling and final, as if death itself had taken form.

Despite the brutal battles he had just fought, he stood untouched, his body pristine, unmarked by a single injury.

Gertie, standing a distance away, stared in disbelief, her face pale with shock.

She had thought Marcus was walking to his death when he entered the dungeon.

She hadn't taken his threats seriously, especially the one about destroying the Tucker family.

But now, she realized that everything he said was true.

This was Marcus. When he spoke, he meant it. And he would never back down from fulfilling his promises.

Marcus' voice broke the heavy silence. "Let's go."

Gertie snapped back to reality, her body trembling slightly.

She looked at him, her eyes filled with a flicker of fear.

How could she not be scared of someone like him, a man who had effortlessly wiped out the entire Tucker family? Such power was something even the bravest would hesitate to challenge.

Before she could process her thoughts, Marcus took her hand and swept her off her feet, carrying her away at a swift pace.

Only after they had disappeared did the shadows around the Tucker Estate begin to stir.

Figures emerged, all from deep within the Kinton Mountains, members of other Ancient Martial Art families.
