"Back off!"

But before Darren Crane, Leader of Team Tiger of Dragon Tiger Hall, could get anywhere near Lila, Marcus' voice cut through the air like a blade.

Darren paused, his face twisting into a sneer. "Who the hell do you think you are, brat? You got a death wish messing with me? You know who I am?"

As he spoke, Darren reached out, his hand aiming to grab Marcus by the collar.

"Whack!"

Before Darren could even touch him, Marcus smacked him across the face with enough force to echo in the room.

Darren hit the ground like a sack of bricks, his head spinning. One side of his face ballooned up, glowing red like a warning sign.

Liz, Jacqueline, and Abraham stared, slack-jawed.

None of them had expected Marcus actually to hit Darren. It was like walking up to a lion and slapping its face.

"You little bastard!" Darren snarled, clutching his cheek as he wobbled to his feet. "You're dead meat! I'll gut you myself!"

He snatched a knife from one of his lackeys, the blade flashing in the dim light as he took a step forward.

Another one of Darren's lackeys kept staring at Marcus.

Noticing something was off, he suddenly stepped up, waving his hands in alarm. "Wait, boss! Hold up!" he stammered, stepping between Darren and Marcus.

Darren glared at him, his eyes narrowing to slits. "What's your deal, huh? You better have a good reason for stopping me!"

The lackey's voice quivered as he pointed toward Marcus. "Boss, I think I've seen him before. You might wanna think twice before you do anything."

Darren barked, his patience wearing thin. "So what if you've seen him? You see a lot of people every day! You think that means I can't handle him? Move it!

"Get outta my way, or you'll be next!"

The lackey's face turned pale as he sputtered, "I swear, boss, he looks just like that guy the Axe Gang brought in yesterday—the one who took out our general! Maybe we shouldn't start something head-on with him!"

The lackey had been there to gang up on Axe Gang, watching the chaos unfold as Marcus tore through Dragon Tiger Hall like a storm.

Even though he had been far away during the fight, Marcus' face had been seared into his memory—it wasn't the kind you could forget.

Seeing Marcus here today, the lackey couldn't believe his eyes at first.

He stared hard, squinting as if his life depended on it, until he was absolutely sure.

"Smack!"

Darren didn't wait to hear more. His palm cracked across the lackey's face with a sickening slap.

Blood sprayed from the man's nose as he stumbled, a couple of teeth scattering on the floor.

"Quit spouting garbage! This country bumpkin? No way he's that guy!" Darren roared. "Now, move, or I'll finish what I started!"

He had been defending their headquarters, not in the mission the day before.

His fury was boiling over, his temper blinding him to reason.

As the leader of Team Tiger, he wasn't used to being humiliated.

Being slapped in front of everyone, especially with Lila watching, was a wound to his pride he couldn't ignore.

The lackey looked ready to cry but kept his mouth shut, stepping aside under Darren's glare.

"Alright, boys, show this punk what happens when you cross me!" Darren barked, his tone venomous.

Darren's men grinned wickedly, pulling out weapons as they surrounded Marcus.

Lila's worry was clear as day on her face.

She took a step toward Liz, ready to beg for help.

But Marcus raised a hand, stopping her. "Relax. They're nothing," he said, his tone casual, almost bored.

One of the lackeys lunged, his weapon slicing through the air.

Marcus' face hardened. A single bone-crunching punch sent the man sprawling.

The lackey crumpled like wet cardboard, groaning as he hit the floor.

The others charged, their weapons glinting as they aimed for Marcus with ruthless precision.

They attacked like rabid dogs, swinging wildly and without a care for consequences.

A normal person would have no way of surviving this.

Liz squeezed her eyes shut, unable to watch what she thought would be a massacre.

She had warned Lila to ask Marcus not to provoke Darren, but he hadn't listened.

Marcus didn't just talk back to Darren—he had the audacity to slap him square in the face.

"Now," she thought grimly, "he was reaping the consequences."

But Marcus didn't flinch at the lackeys. He moved like a panther, swift and unrelenting.

The room filled with the sound of fists meeting flesh and the groans of men hitting the ground.

Marcus' punches were merciless, each one dropping a lackey like clockwork.

In less than a minute, the floor was littered with groaning bodies.

The fight had been clean, efficient, and utterly one-sided.

Everyone in the room stared in stunned silence, unable to believe their eyes. How could Marcus, such a seemingly ordinary guy, be this capable of combat?

Even Darren's bravado faltered as he studied Marcus, his eyes narrowing with a mix of caution and disbelief.
