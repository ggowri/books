As he spoke, Marcus wrapped a towel around Pearl's body.

Pearl's beautiful eyes sparkled with disappointment. "Am I ugly, sir? Don't you like me?"

As she said this, tears began to well up in her eyes.

"No, you are very beautiful." Marcus shook his head.

"Then why don't you want me? I am still a virgin," Pearl continued to press. Her father had given her a strict order to offer herself to Marcus. Pearl dared not disobey.

"This is our first meeting. You don't have to do this."

Although Marcus enjoyed beauty, he did not like to obtain it through coercion.

He could see that Pearl was forced into this.

Marcus did not want to force others to sacrifice themselves for him.

"But, this is my father's command," Pearl said with some worry.

Cooke was the king of the entire Red River Mines. His commands were obeyed by all.

Even as Cooke's daughter, Pearl had an innate fear of him.

"Tell Cooke that this is my idea. If he dares to trouble you, let him come and see me," Marcus said.

Pearl looked deeply at Marcus.

On the way here, Pearl had thought that the man her father wanted her to serve would be an old man.

But she did not expect Marcus not only to be handsome but also different from other men.

The men in the Red River Mines were all covetous of her beauty.

If she agreed, those men would surely rush at her like hungry wolves.

Yet, Marcus had refused her offer of herself.

This made Pearl grateful but also made her doubt her own beauty.

Since Marcus had refused so resolutely, Pearl had no choice but to leave the cabin.

Marcus had not expected Cooke to take such a step, sacrificing his own daughter to show his loyalty.

However, this action made Marcus feel somewhat disgusted.

That night, he did not rest but continued to practice Shadow Step.

By the next morning, Marcus could already take more than ten steps.

And these simple steps were enough to deal with most opponents' attacks.

Moreover, once Marcus advanced to the next level, his speed would increase by more than 20%.

The next day, before Marcus even woke up, Pearl came to attend to his needs.

Although she was Cooke's daughter, Pearl worked efficiently, unlike a pampered young lady.

Marcus did not refuse Pearl's service. After breakfast, he got into Cooke's off-road vehicle.

The mountain road was rugged and muddy, and only special off-road vehicles could barely pass through.

They drove through the rainforest for several hours and finally arrived at the foot of a mountain range.

In front of the mountain range was a cliff as if cut by a knife, reaching up to the sky, with clouds and mist above, giving a mysterious and ethereal feeling.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, we have arrived," Cooke said to Marcus.

Marcus released his consciousness, enveloping the area.

He found that an invisible force confined this area, and nothing from the outside could penetrate it.

Marcus guessed that a powerful array master must have set up a shielding array here, blocking all contact between this area and the outside world.

Soon, two elders emerged from the dense forest nearby. Judging from their aura, they were also warriors.

They scrutinized Marcus and Cooke with serious gazes.

"Gentlemen, we wish to enter the Southern Market. Please allow us," Cooke said, taking off his hat in a bow.

"I remember you came to the Southern Market more than ten years ago. You should know the rules here. Those below Ancient Martial Stage Two are not allowed to enter.

"You brought another youngster here this time. Are you mocking us?" One of the elders said coldly.

He could see that Cooke's cultivation had not improved in over a decade, still at Stage One. The reason he had allowed Cooke to enter the Southern Market back then was because Cooke's companion was a powerful figure.

"Leave, or don't blame us for being rough," the other elder said coldly, paying no attention to Marcus beside Cooke.

Since Marcus looked to be in his early twenties, the elders assumed he was Cooke's subordinate.

However, as soon as the words left the elders' mouths, Marcus released his aura.

A supreme pressure descended from the sky, pressing down on the two elders.

The elders' expressions changed drastically, and their backs were bent under the pressure.
