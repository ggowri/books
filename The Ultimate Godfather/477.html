Chapter 476 Freya's Despair

Freya's face darkened; she hadn't expected Marcus to press her so relentlessly.

"What bet? I never agreed to any bet! Stop making things up!" she snapped.

Marcus chuckled coldly and casually explained the bet between him and Freya to Carter.

Carter's expression turned icy. "Freya, we don't keep parasites in this hospital. Are you resigning, or should I fire you?" he asked bluntly.

Freya felt dizzy and hurriedly said, "Mr. Lake, I trained in Acoton! Firing me would be a huge loss to the hospital. You should think this through!"

Carter sneered, his face full of disdain. "You're threatening me with that? Tell me, could you have completed that surgery just now?"

Freya was speechless. Even with her overseas credentials, she knew she couldn't have handled that operation.

"If you can't complete it, what's the point of your training in Acoton? The goal of training is to improve skills, not to earn an empty title!

"Since you won't resign, I'll formally fire you now!"

Freya looked utterly desperate.

She was a doctor who had trained in Acoton.

But Solomia Hospital had excellent benefits, and her family was based in Solomia.

Losing this job might force her to leave her hometown.

Moreover, other hospitals might not offer the same benefits.

More importantly, she enjoyed her unchecked freedom here, but at another hospital, she'd likely have to start as a junior doctor.

But it was too late now. Carter was firing her, and there was no way to change his mind. She could only accept her fate.

This left Freya full of regret.

She regretted going against Jade. If she hadn't teamed up with Jayden to ostracize her, she wouldn't have ended up in this situation!

"Mr. Fitzgerald, do you have any further instructions? If so, just say the word, and I'll take care of everything," Carter asked Marcus.

Carter looked at Marcus as he spoke.

This was the first time Marcus had come to him for help, so Carter wanted to handle it flawlessly.

A master healer like Marcus was someone he, even as a director, had to curry favor with.

After Marcus cured a late-stage cancer patient at their hospital, Solomia Hospital's reputation had grown significantly.

They had even risen two places in some professional rankings.

These tangible benefits were all thanks to Marcus.

Marcus waved a hand dismissively and said, "I just hope no one else bullies my friends again."

Jade felt a surge of gratitude. She hadn't expected Marcus to be looking out for her all this time.

Carter readily agreed, and Jade was promoted to department head.

The doctors and nurses who had mocked her before now stood frozen in fear, their hearts full of anxiety.

If she took over, they were likely in trouble!

It was the ultimate turn of events—what goes around comes around!

Life's tides shift; don't underestimate anyone.

Everyone learned an important lesson—never bully anyone—you never know what they might become.

"Marcus, thank you," Jade said.

She turned her face up to Marcus, her eyes brimming with gratitude.

It was the first time she had smiled at Marcus.

It was also Marcus' first time seeing her smile. He hadn't expected her smile to be so beautiful.

She usually wore a serious expression, so very few people had probably ever seen her smile.

"It's nothing. We're friends, aren't we?" Marcus said casually.

He waved his hand nonchalantly.

Part of the reason he helped Jade was that he couldn't stand Jayden and Freya's shallow politics.

Another reason was out of respect for Robert.

More importantly, because he had killed Jordon, Jade had lost the opportunity to train in Acoton.

That was why she had been reassigned to Solomia Hospital.

He bore some indirect responsibility for her being ostracized.

"Do you really consider me a friend?" Jade asked, her eyes sparkling.

"What else? A girlfriend?" Marcus teased.

Jade's cheeks turned red, and she shot him a fierce glare. "Don't joke around. You've got so many women around you—why would you be interested in me?"

"And what if I really was?" Marcus asked.

Jade remained silent.

Jade's cheeks flushed even redder, and her heart started racing for reasons she couldn't explain.

Since Jade had just completed a major surgery and was exhausted, she was able to clock out early.

Jade didn't own property in Solomia, so she lived in the hospital's dormitory.

The dormitory was several miles from the inpatient wing.

Marcus started his Aston Martin, ready to drive Jade back to the dormitory.

"By the way, why did you transfer to Solomia Hospital?" Marcus asked as they drove.

"I requested the transfer. Working under my dad in his department meant he handled everything, and I could never grow on my own," Jade explained.
