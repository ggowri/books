The identity of the mysterious savior, however, remained unknown, leaving the medical community buzzing with speculation.

A sudden humming filled his mind, accompanied by a rush of unfamiliar memories.

Each breakthrough in the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire not only enhanced his strength but also granted him a unique ability.

When he reached Stage One, he gained X-ray vision, which allowed him to see through obstacles and illusions.

This time, the ability was called Celestial Reversal.

The concept reminded Marcus of a technique he'd read about in an old novel, where a character named Murdoch wielded a similar skill.

The potential of this ability thrilled him.

In combat, the element of surprise could turn the tide of a fight.

If he encountered an attack he couldn't endure, Celestial Reversal could deflect it and potentially incapacitate his opponent.

However, as Marcus absorbed the intricacies of the technique, he noted its limitations. Activating Celestial Reversal consumed vast amounts of Numinous Energy and placed significant strain on the user's body.

This made the ability more suited as a trump card for life-threatening situations or to counterattack someone stronger than himself rather than a regular tactic.

To test its capabilities, Marcus experimented with the ability and it worked exactly as the memory indicated—he could deflect any object.

However, it was also true that Celestial Reversal consumed a significant amount of Numinous Energy. Even simply deflecting a tennis ball took a certain amount of his Numinous Energy.

Reflecting a more formidable attack would undoubtedly come at a severe cost to his vitality.

Aside from mastering this ability, Marcus also devoted the two days to honing the Fireball and Lightning spells.

Using the Magic Codex he had, he progressed to an elementary level of proficiency.

By now, Marcus had mastered the Fireball and Lightning to a degree where they possessed real power, no longer just party tricks.

A single bolt of lightning could shatter a one-meter-wide stone.

Such power was enough to pose a serious threat to someone at the Stage One of Ancient Martial Arts.

Marcus was confident that with continued practice, these two techniques would become his second trump card, aside from the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire.
