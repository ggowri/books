Chapter 347 Appointment of General Manager

"Honey!"

The middle-aged female interviewer rushed forward to prop Leif Leonard up, her voice sharp with panic.

She snapped at the security guards, "Security! What are you waiting for? That hillbilly just attacked someone! Arrest him and call the cops, now!"

The head of security smirked, clearly unimpressed.

He had been watching the scene unfold from the start and had seen enough to know the real story.

"It was Leif Leonard who swung first with a weapon," the security captain said, crossing his arms. "That gentleman over there? Just self-defense. Boys, take Leonard down and hand him over to the cops!"

The guards wasted no time. They surged forward, quickly restraining Leif with practiced efficiency.

Leif struggled against their grip, his voice cracking with rage. "I'm the branch manager of Sync Group's Solomia office! Do you even know who I am? You're all fired! None of you will have jobs tomorrow!"

The guards exchanged glances, their expressions dripping with sarcasm. One even spat at Leif's face, smirking. "News flash, buddy—the one who got fired is you. Still think you're the boss?"

"Alright, guys," one of the guards said with a laugh. "Let's get this maniac to the station. Assault with a weapon? That's an easy few years behind bars."

They hauled Leif off, his protests fading as he was shoved into the waiting vehicle.

"It's all over. It's completely over. My husband's been arrested! What am I supposed to do now?!"

The middle-aged woman crumpled to the floor, her sobs echoing through the room.

Gloria stood frozen, her face pale as she stared at Marcus in stunned disbelief.

The man she had ridiculed as a nobody, a mere bumpkin, had dismantled her carefully cultivated network with a single phone call.

If she had known this was possible, she would never have dared cross Marcus or Catherine.

But it was too late for regrets now. Far too late.

Her aunt's words echoed in her mind: everything was ruined.

Her uncle had been fired and arrested. Her hopes of joining Sync Group? Crushed.

Everyone else in the room was just as stunned, their thoughts spinning.

What was supposed to be a routine recruitment event for Sync Group's Solomia branch had spiraled into utter chaos.

And all because Gloria had tried to use her so-called connections to put pressure on Catherine.

The result? Her uncle, Leif Leonard, had lost everything.

The absurdity of it all felt surreal, like something out of a bad movie, but there it was, happening in real life.

Right then, Marcus' phone buzzed, cutting through the heavy silence.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, the tasks you assigned have been completed. Sync Group's new CEO is in place. Is there anything else you need?"

Troy's calm voice carried over the line.

This guy had always worked behind the curtain, but in truth, he was someone Marcus had deliberately placed to keep tabs on Stephen.

Ever since the blow-up between Marcus and Stephen's nephew, Troy had been on the scene, quietly watching and reporting back.

The reason was straightforward: Marcus didn't trust Stephen as far as he could throw him.

He had been biding his time, waiting for the right person to step in and take over.

But after Stephen's latest blunder, Marcus had finally hit his limit. Enough was enough—it was time to cut him loose.

"Send a skilled operations person to Solomia as the assistant branch manager," Marcus instructed. "I'll send over the name for the general manager shortly. Make sure everything is formalized."

"Understood, Mr. Fitzgerald," Troy replied without hesitation.

Minutes later, the assistant branch manager and a group of senior staff emerged from the headquarters building.

They walked directly to Marcus, bowing in respect before turning toward Catherine Wilson. In unison, they bent forward in deference. "Greetings, General Manager Wilson!" they announced.

Catherine's eyes widened, and she waved her hands in protest. "You've got the wrong person! I'm just here for a job interview. I'm not your manager!"

The assistant branch manager chuckled warmly. "We just received orders from HQ. As of now, you are officially the General Manager of Sync Group's Solomia branch!"

The announcement sent a ripple of shock through the room.

No one could believe their ears.

Catherine, who had just been excluded from the interview process thanks to Gloria's meddling, was now running the branch.

It was surreal—in just thirty minutes, the tables had turned so dramatically that it felt like a fever dream.

Catherine herself was in disbelief, teetering between shock and awe.

She glanced at Marcus, her eyes silently pleading for guidance.

Marcus smiled at her, his tone calm and reassuring. "You're honest, hardworking, and capable—exactly what the job needs. There's no reason to hesitate."

"But Mr. Fitzgerald," Catherine stammered, "I don't know anything about Sync Group's operations. How am I supposed to do this?"

"Don't stress," Marcus replied smoothly. "I've sent someone from Kingston who knows the ropes. They'll help you settle in. Just focus on doing your best. I'm counting on you to succeed."
