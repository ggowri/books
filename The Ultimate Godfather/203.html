"Dad, that man came to the Burnett Villa without our permission. We should throw him out immediately!" Malcolm said to Matthew.

"Exactly. The Burnett family is one of the four major families in Kingston. No one, especially someone like him, should be allowed in here. Maxine has gone too far; she needs to be properly disciplined."

Ruth immediately chimed in, adding fuel to the fire.

Matthew, however, raised a hand to silence them.

"How could this nobody somehow manage to catch Maxine's eye? He doesn't seem like someone of status. No way he could bring a gift as valuable as Shaun's. Even a gift on par with the others in the room seems beyond him. Sir Burnett's move is absolutely brilliant."

The guests whispered among themselves, casting looks of disdain toward Marcus.

Maxine's face flushed with embarrassment. Her impulsive kiss had dragged Marcus into this mess, and now, her grandfather's unreasonable demand made her regret everything.

"Grandfather Marcus came in such a hurry that he didn't have time to prepare a gift. I'll cover for him and provide one in his place."

Ruth echoed similar sentiments.

Arnold remained silent, but his expression was one of amusement as if he was just watching the drama unfold.

The three of them even thought it would be a good opportunity to give Maxine a lesson, as she was simply too headstrong.

Matthew's voice was laced with mockery as he aimed to humiliate Marcus in front of everyone.

Then, Marcus stepped forward and looked Matthew in the eye. "First, as a younger generation, I wish you a happy 80th birthday, Sir Burnett. As Maxine said, I came in such a rush that I didn't have time to prepare a gift for you, but that doesn't mean I came empty-handed."

With that, Marcus reached into his Spatial Ring and pulled out a small vial containing a medicinal pill.
