Chapter 651 Poor Chad

When Jenson spoke, Natalie's face instantly betrayed her shock.

Just as she had suspected, Jenson was far wealthier than Marcus.

Unfortunately, Jenson still seemed to treat Vivian well, leaving little room for her to step in.

Vivian, meanwhile, was brimming with pride. She folded her arms and sneered at Chad. "Chad, do you really think bringing a rich friend here is enough to save face?

"Let me tell you, your friend's wealth is nothing compared to my husband's. And even if he's rich, that money isn't yours. What are you showing off for?"

She smirked arrogantly, her posture oozing confidence.

Jenson chimed in with a mocking tone, his expression dripping with smugness. "Exactly. He's just a delivery driver. How much perspective can he possibly have? His world is as small as his paycheck. There's no way he could understand the life of the wealthy like us."

Chad clenched his fists tightly, anger boiling within him with nowhere to go.

"Jenson, Natalie, Marcus, Chad. Is that you guys?"

Just then, a few figures approached from the parking lot. It was some of their high school classmates.

The dim lighting made them hesitant to confirm who was who.

Natalie quickly waved. "Yeah, it's us!"

"It's been so long! You all look just the same. I heard from the group chat a lot of people are already here. Let's head up so we're not the last ones," one of them said, smiling.

As he spoke, his gaze lingered on Jenson, a hint of envy flashing in his eyes.

Jenson's wealth was practically legendary among their old classmates. Everyone envied him.

"Vivian, let's not waste any more time with the delivery boy. Come on, I'll take you upstairs," Jenson said, holding out his arm.

Vivian happily took it, and the two of them strolled toward the grand entrance of Golden Hotel.

Natalie glanced at Marcus and decided to follow.

"Wow, look at Jenson's wife. She's stunning! That dress is so classy. He's got great taste."

"Careful what you say. I heard she used to be Chad's wife. The three of them were college classmates. Chad married her first, but then she left him for Jenson."

"No way. Poor Chad... That's brutal."

"Yeah, it sucks. That's what happens when a guy doesn't have money. He can't even keep his own wife. Now he has to watch her cling to someone else's arm, looking so happy."

As Jenson and Vivian disappeared into the hotel, a group of male classmates whispered among themselves.

Chad's face darkened, his fury boiling over.

"Come on, Chad. Let's head up too," Marcus said, his voice calm but with a hint of steel. He could see the storm brewing in Chad's eyes.

"No, Marcus. Let's just leave. If I go up there, all I'll see is those two rubbing their happiness in my face. I can't stand it!"

"Chad, you have to keep your cool. Let them gloat while they can. The higher they fly, the harder they'll fall later."

Marcus' voice was steady, but his resolve was unwavering.

Today, he was determined to make this shameless couple taste humiliation.

After a moment of silence, Chad nodded, and the two of them headed into the luxurious Golden Hotel.

The lobby was breathtaking, glittering with gold and radiating opulence.

Golden Hotel was a crown jewel of Golden Group, famously the most extravagant spot in the city.

Many of their classmates, who had come from modest backgrounds, were in awe of the grandeur, their eyes wide with amazement.

For most, opportunities to visit such a high-end establishment were rare.

In one of the expansive private dining rooms, the classmates gathered one by one until everyone had arrived.

Jenson poured himself a glass of wine and stood up with a confident smile. "I'm the organizer of today's reunion. And, not to brag, but I think I can safely say I'm the most successful among us now. So tonight, everything's on me! Order whatever you like. Don't hold back!"

He downed the wine in one go, his demeanor full of swagger.

Vivian's eyes sparkled with admiration.

She felt more certain than ever that leaving Chad for Jenson had been the right choice.

Her husband had built a business empire worth billions in just a few years.

Compared to Chad, who was nothing but a useless loser, Jenson was in a league of his own.

Jenson made the rounds, greeting classmates one by one.

Years of business dealings had clearly sharpened his social skills and his drinking tolerance.

When he reached Chad, Jenson took a deliberate step back, his nose wrinkling in mock disgust. "What's that smell?" he asked loudly.

A classmate nearby looked puzzled. "What smell? I don't smell anything."

Jenson smirked. "It's the stench of poverty. Can't you smell it?"

The room burst into laughter as everyone caught on to his insult.
