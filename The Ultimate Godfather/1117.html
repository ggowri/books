The girls immediately showed expressions of surprise and joy.

Marcus destroyed all the cages, and the girls were finally free.

The moment they walked out of the cave, they burst into tears, overwhelmed with the feeling of surviving such an ordeal.

"Thank you, thank you for saving us... You're our savior!"

A girl knelt on the ground, and kept thanking Gertie.

Gertie quickly stepped forward to help the girl up. "Please don't. The one who saved you is Marcus. I just came along with him, I didn't really do much."

The girl immediately turned to Marcus with grateful eyes and said, "Thank you, thank you so much. I don't even know how to repay you."

As soon as she said this, the other girls also turned grateful eyes toward Marcus.

Some girls expressed their willingness to become servants for Marcus.

Some even said they were willing to repay his kindness with their own bodies.

Marcus waved his hand and said to the girls, "It's nothing, not worth mentioning. You don't need to thank me. I just hope you can do one thing."

The girls immediately nodded in agreement. One girl asked, "Just tell us what you need us to do, and we'll make it happen!"

Marcus said, "After being rescued, you will surely become the focus of society, and many media outlets may rush to interview you. I just hope you won't reveal my personal information to anyone."

"Remember, anyone, including your parents and close friends."

The girls immediately nodded in agreement and promised not to reveal it to anyone.

Gertie look at Marcus.

If it were someone else, they would surely be eager to receive praise and recognition for such a good deed.

But Marcus was different from ordinary people.

He actually asked these girls to keep it a secret, not wanting others to know his identity.

This shows how humble Marcus is.

Being capable yet humble is the quality a successful person should have.

This made Gertie admire Marcus even more.

Because there were too many girls, Marcus couldn't take them all out of the wilderness at once.

So he contacted Serpent's Coil to send a convoy of dozens of off-road vehicles to the wilderness to pick them up.

Hundreds of girls were taken away by the convoy.

After everything was settled, Marcus finally felt at ease.

At this moment, the sky gradually darkened.

Due to her previous injuries, Gertie couldn't help but cough, with a trace of blood seeping from the corner of her mouth.

"You are not recovered yet, let me help you first," Marcus said.

At this moment, Gertie's face was somewhat pale as she said, "We can do that later. I'm worried those Ophidians might come for revenge. What we should do now is leave here quickly."

Marcus nodded, thinking it made sense.

He didn't care if the Ophidians came for revenge.

He was afraid of being interrupted while healing Gertie.

Judging by Gertie's injuries, the healing process might take some time.

If interrupted at a critical moment, it could affect the healing process and leave Gertie with permanent complications.

Thinking of this, Marcus reluctantly agreed and asked Gertie, "So, where do you think we should go?"

Gertie's eyes flickered; she initially wanted to say hotel but felt it might not be appropriate.

Other places seemed unsafe, so she said, "Let's go to my family's training ground. It's empty, and the Rune set up by our ancestors will keep anyone from disturbing us."

Marcus immediately nodded. "Where is your family's training ground?"

Gertie said, "Deep in the Kinton Mountains."

"Be more specific," Marcus continued to ask. His instant teleportation worked like map navigation, requiring an exact spot to arrive instantly.
