Chapter 657 In Awe

The words hit the crowd like a thunderclap, leaving everyone stunned.

A stunned silence followed as disbelief spread across the faces of the crowd. Every single person stood frozen, unable to process what had just been said.

Marcus had always been the target of ridicule and dismissal from his classmates.

Yet, just a few short years after graduation, the once-ignored kid had somehow become someone powerful enough to decide the fate of the chairman of Sync Group.

To everyone in the room, it felt like a deep-water bomb had exploded beneath their feet, shaking them to the core.

Jenson and Vivian were utterly dumbfounded. They had never in a million years imagined that Marcus had such a prestigious status.

Natalie shared their disbelief.

Her first encounter with Marcus since high school had been at the custom jewelry store.

Back then, she thought he couldn't even afford Tears of the Angel and had snickered at him.

She treated Joan like her top-tier customer, while ignoring Marcus completely.

Then, in a moment of frustration, Marcus had dropped 650 million to buy every piece of jewelry in the store.

At that time, Natalie still thought that amount was probably the limit of Marcus' wealth.

But now, this reunion was completely upending her understanding of everything.

Jenson had always prided himself on being the most successful in the class, constantly mocking Marcus and Chad.

Even Natalie had believed that in front of Jenson, Marcus didn't even stand a chance.

But now, she realized that in front of Marcus, Jenson wasn't even worth mentioning.

With just a few words, Marcus had effortlessly stripped away the one thing Jenson had built his fortune on.

And just like that, he'd promoted Chad, who had been barely scraping by as a food delivery driver, into the role of General Manager of Sync Group's East Sales Division.

A figure like that was standing before them, and yet they hadn't even realized it.

To make things worse, most of them had been standing by Jenson's side, mocking and belittling Marcus all along.

Now, looking back, Natalie felt an overwhelming urge to vanish.

She regretted looking down on Marcus so much that she wished she could crawl into a hole.

If, back when he bought the jewelry, she had treated him with sincerity, their relationship would have turned out completely different.

What was more, Marcus had pursued her all through school.

Even though she had rejected him back then, when they'd run into each other again just a few days ago, Natalie had been confident that she could win his heart back.

But now, it felt like it was too late for any of that.

Natalie felt like she'd lost the most precious thing in the world, overcome with a desire to bang her head against the wall in regret.

Chad was also frozen in shock. Grinning awkwardly, he said to Marcus, "Man, I had no idea you were this powerful! I'm seriously in awe!"

He didn't even know how to praise Marcus properly, so he just gave him a thumbs-up.

"Not a big deal."

Marcus gave Chad a playful slap on the shoulder and asked, "Do you think you can manage Sync Group's sales division?"

Chad hesitated, looking uneasy. "Marcus, you know I know nothing about sales. Managing such a big company? Honestly, I'm not confident at all."

Marcus' gaze shifted to Silas, and he calmly ordered, "Contact the headquarters immediately. Get them to send a few of their best salespeople and managers from Solomia to assist Chad with the work."

Silas immediately nodded. "Understood, Mr. Fitzgerald!"

Without missing a beat, Silas stepped out of the room to make the call.

A wave of realization swept over the group.

It was no longer a matter of qualifications or experience. It was about power.

It didn't matter whether Chad was actually qualified for the role. Marcus had made up his mind, and that was that.

To the rest of them, it felt like a punch to the gut. The idea that someone like Chad, who had once been struggling to get by as a delivery driver, could now be in charge of such a massive company was mind-blowing.

In that moment, a few of them started to regret not trying harder to connect with Marcus when they had the chance.

If they'd known who he would become, they could've been in his circle.

They might have been able to change their futures with just a single word from him.

And now, looking back, they realized that if they'd only backed Marcus at the dinner instead of siding with Jenson, things might have turned out so differently.

But it was too late. The opportunities had been there, right in front of them, and they hadn't taken them.

"Chad, still have any doubts?" Marcus asked.

Chad shook his head, still trying to wrap his mind around it all. "Nope, none. But... I've got to ask. What's the salary looking like for this gig?"
