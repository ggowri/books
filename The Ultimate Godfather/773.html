Chapter 770 The Space-Time Tunnel

This place was far different from what Marcus had imagined a space-time tunnel would look like.

The vast emptiness and the endless expanse inspired a bone-deep fear, a terror that seemed to claw at the very essence of one's soul.

A sharp, piercing screech suddenly echoed through the darkness.

Moments later, a swarm of beasts with black wings surged toward them.

These creatures looked vaguely like vampire bats, but their wings resembled those of birds, and their heads were eerily reptilian.

Their gaping mouths revealed inch-long razor-sharp teeth, and their bodies emanated dark spatial forces.

"Be careful, Mr. Fitzgerald. These creatures survive on spatial forces. If they bite you, even once, the spatial forces within the tunnel will start tearing at your body.

"If the wounds are too severe, the spatial forces will rip you apart completely!" Raphael warned.

Marcus felt a flicker of shock.

He hadn't expected these seemingly insignificant beasts to possess such deadly power.

He understood all too well how terrifying spatial forces could be.

He had experienced it firsthand when he entered the space-time gate, the sensation of his body being torn by invisible giants as if he could be shredded at any moment.

The screeching intensified, and the beasts lunged toward them.

Though the trio was far stronger than ordinary people, the suddenness of the attack threw them into temporary disarray.

Marcus swiftly summoned a Firebolt. The fiery energy surged forward, enveloping the beasts in front of him.

A few of the creatures let out agonized cries, struggling for mere moments before they were reduced to ashes.

But Ada wasn't as fortunate. A beast sank its teeth into her shoulder.

In an instant, an overwhelming spatial force descended upon her, tearing violently at her body.

Marcus' brow furrowed as he unleashed a series of Firebolts and bolts of lightning, forcing the attacking beasts to retreat.

He quickly channeled a surge of Timeless Numinous Energy into Ada, driving out the invading spatial force.

This intervention prevented her body from being shredded, but her shoulder was left bleeding profusely, with a long, gruesome gash that looked alarmingly severe.

"These creatures are terrifying," Marcus thought grimly, bracing himself for the next wave.

He moved Ada behind him.

The Heavenly Circle Diagram began to activate in his mind, and his body became surrounded by a shimmering light of runes.

The intricate patterns of the runes flowed into his palms.

With a sweeping gesture, he unleashed the rune patterns into the void. They morphed into a massive totemic symbol, encompassing the entire area around them.

The beasts grew even bolder, launching a ferocious assault against the group.

As Marcus muttered an incantation, the rune totem roared to life, channeling devastating energy into the void.

The runes retaliated with relentless force, cutting down the creatures one by one.

The air was filled with the grotesque sound of beasts being torn apart. Black blood splattered everywhere, releasing a foul, corrosive stench.

In less than two minutes, Marcus had wiped out every last one of them.

Raphael stared at him in stunned disbelief.

He hadn't anticipated that Marcus would not only possess extraordinary combat skills and healing abilities but also command the profound power of runes.

In Raphael's eyes, Marcus seemed capable of anything.

"How are you feeling?" Marcus asked Ada.

Ada winced, her face pale. "It hurts..."

Marcus glanced at her mangled shoulder and felt a pang of guilt.

He swiftly activated the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire and pressed his palm against her shoulder.

A stream of Timeless Numinous Energy flowed into her body.

The energy's healing properties were remarkable, and since Marcus had reached the peak of the Ancient Martial Stage Nine, its effects were even more pronounced.

Under its influence, Ada's wound began to close rapidly.

In less than ten minutes, her shoulder had completely healed, leaving her skin unblemished.

Both Raphael and Ada watched in awe, their disbelief plain on their faces.

Though they had known Marcus' medical skills were astonishing, seeing it firsthand was an entirely different experience.

"Mr. Fitzgerald, you're incredible!" Raphael exclaimed.

Marcus waved off the compliment and resumed walking forward.

Ada followed closely behind.

For reasons she couldn't explain, Ada felt an inexplicable sense of safety whenever Marcus was by her side, no matter how perilous the situation.

The deeper they ventured into the tunnel, the more oppressive it became.

There was no end in sight.

The surroundings were pitch-black, like the depths of a cosmic black hole, as if ready to devour everything.

For the next hour, Marcus, Raphael, and Ada pressed on through the endless void.

During this time, they faced numerous attacks from the tunnel's peculiar beasts.

The creatures were unlike anything from the normal world, their forms bizarre and their methods of attack even stranger.

Although the beasts weren't exceedingly powerful, they still caused trouble for the group.

If not for Marcus' overwhelming strength and battle-hardened instincts, the journey would have been far more dangerous.

Within the span of an hour, they had slain countless creatures.

But the beasts weren't the only threats.

The tunnel was rife with hidden perils, from sudden spatial storms to abrupt collapses in the tunnel itself.

In this place, a single mistake could mean death.

Marcus kept his spiritual senses on full alert, maintaining vigilance at all times.

Even so, the trio couldn't avoid minor injuries along the way.

Thanks to the Timeless Warrior's Grimoire and its potent healing abilities, they managed to treat their wounds as they advanced.

"What lies at the end of this space-time tunnel?" Marcus mused aloud after smashing a beast's head with a single punch.

Raphael replied, "This tunnel leads to the world beyond the Morvalis Ice Wall. Only by overcoming its countless trials can we reach the other side."

Marcus' gaze hardened, his determination etched onto his face.

He led Raphael and Ada onward.

Eventually, they entered a dense, foggy region shrouded in layers of mist.

The mist concealed countless dangers. Strange, powerful creatures emerged one after another.

And as they delved deeper into the fog, the beasts grew stronger.

Marcus drew his golden scimitar, carving a bloody path through the treacherous terrain.

Finally, after three grueling hours, they broke free from the suffocating mist.

Ahead of them stood a towering fortress.

The structure, with its nine imposing levels, radiated an ethereal golden light. It loomed before them, majestic and unyielding, exuding an overwhelming sense of grandeur.
