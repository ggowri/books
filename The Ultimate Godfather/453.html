Chapter 452 A Gynecological Condition

The first time Marcus met Kiera, his attention hadn't been on her, so he hadn't noticed certain details.

Now, however, his gaze lingered briefly before Kiera, sensing his eyes on her, flushed a deep red. Trying to diffuse the awkwardness, she said, "Mr. Fitzgerald, please wait here. I'll go prepare dinner for you."

She slipped off her jacket, revealing an elegant figure as she tied on an apron.

There was something comforting and wholesome about her as she smiled lightly and walked into the kitchen to start cooking.

Marcus wandered casually around the living room, taking in the space.

From the decor, it was clear Kiera was someone who loved life and was very disciplined.

On a chalkboard near the door, she had a detailed to-do list with neatly drawn checkmarks under completed tasks.

Her organization and self-discipline were remarkable, something rarely seen in everyday life.

Beyond that, Kiera seemed quite frugal. Many items in her home appeared to be repurposed from old materials, creatively transformed into practical and decorative objects.

It was both money-saving and practical.

In today's materialistic society, such ingenuity was rare.

Most people either lacked the skills or thought it was beneath them.

As Marcus' gaze fell on the dining table, he noticed several medications.

From their labels, it was clear they were for treating gynecological conditions, including uterine fibroids, ovarian cysts, and vaginitis.

Given Kiera's pale complexion and her frail, undernourished appearance, Marcus deduced that her condition was likely severe, at least serious enough to require constant medication.

Soon, Kiera emerged from the kitchen, carefully carrying four dishes, which she placed on the table.

Noticing the medications, she quickly gathered them up, embarrassment flashing across her face.

"I'm afraid the food isn't very good, Mr. Fitzgerald," she said with a sweet smile.

She knew it was an honor for someone as formidable as Marcus to come to her place.

Marcus sampled one of the dishes and gave her a thumbs-up. "This is wonderful. It tastes like home."

In today's world, it was rare to find a young woman who could cook, let alone prepare dishes this flavorful and well-presented.

After a moment, Marcus asked casually, "By the way, I noticed the medication. Are you dealing with a gynecological condition?"

Kiera froze, her cheeks turning crimson.

Discussing such matters with a man was deeply embarrassing.

She regretted not hiding the medicine earlier.

"Yes," she admitted softly. "But it's not too serious."

In fact, her illness had been torturing her for days.

In truth, Kiera had been struggling recently. Her lower abdomen often ached, and her chronic anemia left her feeling dizzy and fatigued.

"Take care of yourself," Marcus said gently.

He had already noticed her symptoms but chose not to press further.

After dinner, as Kiera began clearing the table, a sudden wave of dizziness hit her.

She stumbled, nearly collapsing, but Marcus caught her just in time.

"Kiera, are you okay?" Marcus asked, his tone concerned.

Kiera rubbed her temples, forcing a smile. "I'm fine. It's probably just from the stress earlier with Gustavo and his friends."

She tried to get back to work, but Marcus held her back.

"Your anemia has worsened, and the stress caused your blood to flow abnormally, leading to your dizziness. You need to rest. No more chores."

Kiera chuckled weakly. "Don't worry, Mr. Fitzgerald. I've felt like this before. I just need a moment to recover."

"This time is different," Marcus said firmly. "Your uterine fibroids have grown severe enough to compress your cervix. If left untreated, it could lead to serious consequences, even permanent damage."

Kiera stared at him in shock.

She hadn't expected him to know so much just by observing her. "What should I do? Does it require surgery?"

Though she was frugal, Kiera always took her health seriously.

Watching her nephew suffer from a congenital heart condition had made her even more cautious.

She didn't want to endure unnecessary pain or burden her family.

Hearing that her condition was this severe left her feeling panicked.

"Surgery carries risks and potential side effects. I wouldn't recommend it," Marcus said, shaking his head.

"Then what should I do?" Kiera asked, her voice trembling.

"I can treat your condition. The only question is whether you're willing to trust me and cooperate," Marcus replied calmly.

His Timeless Numinous Energy had cured advanced cancer and leukemia before. A gynecological condition like this was trivial by comparison.

"Really?" Kiera asked, her eyes widening in surprise.

She quickly remembered how Marcus had cured her nephew's heart condition in less than half an hour and felt a wave of relief.

"What do I need to do?" she asked, her tone now filled with hope.
