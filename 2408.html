Primordial said, "Why are you in such a hurry? The Astralwreck Dragon isn't that easy to hatch. If it were easy, the Magma Devourer would have hatched it long ago, and it wouldn't be your turn."

Staring at the Astralwreck Dragon Egg, Primordial seemed to be deep in thought.

His branch-like hand was touching his chin at the moment, looking somewhat comical.

However, Osborne did not disturb him.

Osborne knew that if it came to knowledge, he was no match for Primordial. If Primordial couldn't figure out a way to quickly hatch the Astralwreck Dragon, then there was no point in him trying.

Staring at the transparent coffin, Osborne felt a bit puzzled.

The surrounding mist was clearly released by the Astralwreck Dragon Egg. This mist was likely meant to protect the Astralwreck Dragon Egg. It was incredibly powerful, so much so that even a top-tier warrior at the level of Empyrean Sacredness couldn't break through it.

However, as soon as the transparent coffin appeared, the mist automatically opened a gap.

Osborne thought, "Could it be that this Astralwreck Dragon Egg doesn't belong to the Magma Devourer, but to the Wingless Angel in the transparent coffin?"

He felt that his guess was quite reliable.

A scene seemed to appear in Osborne's mind. On a certain day long ago, the Magma Devourer found a transparent coffin and an Astralwreck Dragon Egg within the core of a star.

To claim the Astralwreck Dragon Egg for itself, the Magma Devourer left the star it had always lived on and came to a small resource planet at the edge of the universe. It killed all the natives on the planet and took it over.

Using the planet's environment, the Magma Devourer wanted to hatch the Astralwreck Dragon Egg. It even planted a large number of Beast Soul Flowers in advance, preparing food for the Astralwreck Dragon after hatching.

However, after tens of thousands of years, the Magma Devourer still failed to hatch the Astralwreck Dragon Egg, and the warriors from the Racial Convergence Planet discovered this planet.

The Wingless Angel in the transparent coffin was the true owner of the Astralwreck Dragon Egg.

Osborne felt this guess was closer to the truth, but it didn’t matter.

"I have an idea," Primordial suddenly said. "I thought of a way to quickly hatch the Astralwreck Dragon Egg. However, it might be a bit risky. If we fail, this Astralwreck Dragon Egg might never hatch again."

"But this is the only way. Otherwise, this Astralwreck Dragon Egg won't hatch naturally even after tens of thousands of years, because the conditions here simply aren't suitable for hatching it."

Osborne curiously asked, "What’s the method?"

"Blood-Sacrificing Magic," Primordial said seriously. "Blood-Sacrificing Magic is an ancient ritual method. Initially, it used blood sacrifices to exchange for power from the cosmos. But later, it evolved into many variations, giving Blood-Sacrificing Magic numerous forms."

"I happen to know a variation of Blood-Sacrificing Magic. It should accelerate the growth of the Astralwreck Dragon Egg and make it hatch quickly. However, it will require you to consume a large amount of blood."

"Additionally, the Astralwreck Dragon Egg will also become a sacrifice for the Blood-Sacrificing Magic. If this method fails, the Astralwreck Dragon Egg will completely die and turn into a dead egg."

Osborne frowned, thinking, "Normally, the Astralwreck Dragon Egg might not naturally hatch for tens of thousands of years? Only Blood-Sacrificing Magic has a chance to hatch it quickly? But if the hatching fails, the Astralwreck Dragon Egg will become a dead egg."

He hesitated because the Astralwreck Dragon Egg was too precious. If he could hatch the Astralwreck Dragon, it would become a great ally for him. Therefore, Osborne didn’t want to take any risks.

But Osborne couldn’t wait tens of thousands of years for it to hatch.

Who knows what things will be like in tens of thousands of years? Maybe by then, he’ll already be dead.

By then, even if the Astralwreck Dragon Egg hatches, what’s the point?

Osborne took a deep breath and asked, "Primordial, how confident are you?"

Primordial replied, "I’m about fifty percent confident, at most no more than sixty percent."

"Fifty to sixty percent?" Osborne said seriously, "Then let's begin. It's worth a try. Whether I can hatch it depends on its luck."

Primordial nodded and said, "Alright, I'll teach you Blood-Sacrificing Magic now."

Soon, Osborne's mind was filled with some new information.

After quickly grasping the information, Osborne had already mastered Blood-Sacrificing Magic. "So that's how it is."

After simulating it a few times in his mind, Osborne confirmed he had completely mastered Blood-Sacrificing Magic. He looked at the Astralwreck Dragon Egg and the transparent coffin not far away.

"I need to put this transparent coffin away first to avoid it interfering with my use of Blood-Sacrificing Magic," Osborne thought, then once again stored the transparent coffin into the Dimensional Space.

This time, the transparent coffin didn't come out on its own again.

Osborne took a deep breath and got ready to begin.

At this moment, Primordial said, "You need to be careful. Astralwreck Dragons are incredibly rare. If you miss this one, it will be very hard for us to find another."

Osborne rolled his eyes. "Shut up. Of course, I know to be careful. Your words do nothing but add pressure on me."

Primordial laughed.

Osborne ignored Primordial because he could feel that Primordial was also nervous and knew that Primordial said that to help ease his pressure.

This was entirely because the Astralwreck Dragon Egg was just too precious.

It’s a terrifyingly fierce beast that, once fully grown, can easily tear apart a star or even destroy a Stellar Region. If Osborne could successfully hatch it and raise it as a pet, it would be a tremendous help to him.

Even in Osborne's upcoming revenge plan, the Astralwreck Dragon could provide him with immense support.

Primordial was nervous, and Osborne was nervous too.

But after hearing Primordial's words, Osborne found himself much calmer.

"Then I'll begin." Osborne took out the Warfare Scythe and slashed at his wrist.

Osborne's body had already reached the mid Sacredness Level, just a step away from advanced Sacredness Level. His skin was incredibly tough, and even an ordinary Sacred Artifact couldn't easily cut through it.

However, the Warfare Scythe was sharp enough. A huge gash appeared on Osborne's wrist, and blood instantly spurted out.

But the blood didn’t fall to the ground; instead, Osborne controlled it like a small stream, directing it all onto the Astralwreck Dragon Egg.

In an instant, Osborne lost a significant amount of blood. The 33-feet-tall Astralwreck Dragon Egg was completely covered in Osborne's blood.

"Blood-Sacrificing Magic!" Following the Primordial teachings, Osborne instantly performed the Blood-Sacrificing Magic.

The next second, the blood on the Astralwreck Dragon Egg seemed to come alive, writhing continuously before quickly merging into the eggshell, causing the entire shell to emit a blood-red glow.

Seeing this, Osborne was thrilled because if the Astralwreck Dragon Egg's shell absorbed his blood, it meant the first step of the Blood-Sacrificing Magic had succeeded.

The second step was to use the Astralwreck Dragon Egg, which had absorbed his blood, as a sacrifice for the ritual.

During the ritual, the Astralwreck Dragon Egg would grow faster. If it could hatch before the ritual was completed, Osborne could immediately end the ritual.

Forcibly ending the ritual would cost him something. However, if the Astralwreck Dragon Egg could hatch, everything would be worth it to him.

But if the Astralwreck Dragon Egg still couldn't hatch after the ritual was completed, Osborne would have no choice but to finish the ritual.

By then, Osborne would gain many benefits, but the Astralwreck Dragon Egg would end up as a dead egg.

At this moment, Osborne nervously stared at the Astralwreck Dragon Egg, hoping it would successfully hatch.
