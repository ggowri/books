Diana also saw the news about Queenie's successful redemption and her increase in followers, but she wasn't surprised at all.

The development of events in this life differed from the previous one.

It was because she had disrupted Queenie's climb up to Rita.

Queenie then turned to Jace, but that put her at a disadvantage.

Jace and Rita weren't in the same league.

Jace was a nouveau riche, while the Farley family had a century-old legacy, with Rita coming from an established family and being very thoughtful in her actions.

Jace couldn't compete. In less than six months, the Foliot family would surely fall into Queenie's hands.

Queenie reached her current status, all because Steven had gradually nurtured her rise.

Diana wrapped her arms around herself. She had only herself. When she felt sad, she could only hug herself for comfort.

She couldn't drag her grandfather and brother into this filthy mess.

The Azure Group was her ultimate fallback.

Diana lay on her desk, feeling miserable.

"What are you doing?" Diana was lost in her thoughts and hadn't noticed someone entering.

She muttered, "I'm thinking about how to get a divorce."

"Diana, do you really want a divorce that badly?" a cold voice sounded, making Diana sit up straight abruptly.

Seeing it was Steven, standing tall with a deep, cold gaze, Diana scoffed. "Steven, aren't you the one who wants the divorce? Doesn't it feel hypocritical to question me like this?"

"We won't divorce. I'll wait for you to calm down. Once you're over it, you'll come back," Steven said coolly, his voice devoid of warmth.

Diana finally asked, "How did you get in here?"

Steven sat on the nearby sofa, elegantly crossing his long legs. "Luna let me in. I told her I was here to discuss the divorce, so she didn't stop me."

The bastard knew how to use people to his advantage.

Diana thought for a moment. "Isn't that exactly how scumbags operate? Use everything to their advantage."

As Diana was thinking, her phone on the desk rang.

She picked it up and saw it was Alexander.

"Alexander," she answered.

As soon as she called him "Alexander," Steven's face darkened.

Since she left, there were new men around her.

Each one came from a better family background and was more handsome than the last.

"Diana, I just had someone check. Jace offered Frank some projects as a peace offering. He's got a few promising artists under his wing, and Frank managed to secure those investments, keeping the company afloat."

Diana was a bit surprised that Jace would go to such lengths for Queenie.

"Alexander, don't worry. I'm fine. You don't need to be concerned about me."

"Alright! Just letting you know. If you need any help, call me," Alexander replied.

"Okay, if I need anything, I won't hesitate to ask. Thank you!" Diana said, smiling as she ended the call.

Steven frowned and asked, "Diana, you'd rather seek help from other men than from me?"

Diana laughed bitterly at his words. "Steven, don't you feel ashamed? Have you ever helped me once in all these years I was with you?"
