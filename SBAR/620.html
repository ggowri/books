After fastening her seat belt, Diana gazed at Luna and said, "Luna, let's go home."

Sensing her low mood, Luna asked, "Diana, are you still upset after the whole day?"

Diana actually didn't feel unhappy. Jeff had already met his retribution. What was meant to happen had already happened. She was living a good life. She explained, "No. Let's go home. I'm not upset. But Hubery still ignores me."

Luna replied, "Well!"

She was about to drive away when Diana's phone suddenly rang.

She picked it up and saw that it was Cecil.

"Cecil," she smiled.

"Diana, you're back. I want to invite you to dinner. I know you're off work," Cecil chuckled.

Diana glanced out the car window and spotted his car parked opposite them.

He beamed brightly and waved. "Diana, I'm here."

Diana said, "Sure. Send me the address, and I'll ask Luna to take me there."

Cecil replied, "Okay!"

Diana hung up the phone and soon received a message from him.

She sent the address to Luna.

Luna drove over. Cecil showed up just in time. Diana was dejected, and she would feel better if he kept her company.

The restaurant he chose was quite a distance away, near the radio and television building. It took them forty minutes to reach their destination due to the rush-hour traffic jam.​

After getting out of the car, Diana put on a black coat with a black business suit underneath, looking very capable.​

She went to the door in the snow and wind and saw Cecil already there waiting for her.​

He immediately opened the windproof curtain for her.

"Diana, there's a traffic jam. Are you hungry?" he asked with a grin.

Cecil was dressed very maturely in black trousers, a black shirt, and a black woolen coat, which added to his calm and steady appearance.​

It was the first time Diana had seen him appear like a domineering president.

"Cecil, you are different today."

Cecil's ears blushed instantly. He was somewhat embarrassed. To make himself more mature, he wore clothes he did not normally wear.

He didn't like suits, but casual clothes.

"Diana, do you like me dressed like this?"

Diana chuckled and shook her head honestly. "The outfit is very mature, but I prefer your previous dressing style."

In the workplace, there were not so many requirements now.

Except for very important banquets, everyone dressed casually.

Cecil's charming eyes were bright and tender. "I just wanted to dress myself up a little more maturely, but unexpectedly, you didn't like me in this."

Diana was stunned that he had changed his dressing style for her, but she didn't think much about it. "Maturity doesn't depend on clothes. Let's go. Where are we going?"

Cecil replied, "It's here."

His voice was too low for Diana to hear.​

He trotted after her and said with a smile, "Diana, let's go to the sixth floor. There is a casserole restaurant. I've made a reservation in advance, and now it's just the right time."

Diana said, "Okay."

Still, she liked his attractive, youthful appearance.​

They took the elevator to the sixth floor. The environment was great, with many restaurant waiters handing out flyers at the door.​

The atmosphere was lively. Diana was very fond of such places. It was nosy, but it made her feel like she was living a real life.
