No matter how long she slept, Diana was still tired.

"So sleepy."

She muttered to herself, yawned, and looked at her tearful, blurry-eyed reflection in the mirror, feeling a sense of pitiful charm.

Steven couldn't sleep himself, and he wouldn't let Diana sleep either.

So outrageous!

She and Steven really hadn't been to an auction together.

Diana also wanted to give it a try.

She freshened up, and after stepping out, saw that Steven had already picked out the clothes she would wear later.

She had already applied light makeup, tied her hair up, exuding a youthful vibe.

Steven liked her energetic look. He chose a pink dress for her, which suited her hairstyle perfectly.

Steven handed her the clothes and said, "Diana, wear this tonight. It's a bit chilly in the evening, so this long-sleeved outfit will be better."

Diana saw his gentle demeanor and couldn't help but smile.

"Alright! I'll listen to you." Diana felt she had no resolve.

But she was truly happy.

It felt as if her long-held wish had come true. That joy radiated from the depths of her heart.

Diana smiled, took the dress, and went to change.

The pink long dress made her look pure and beautiful.

Steven knew she would look stunning in that dress.

He walked over, took Diana's hand, and gently said, "Let's go, my dear."

Diana no longer felt sleepy at this moment.

Steven's gentle words sometimes made her feel like dreaming.

The boy who suddenly entered her world in her dreams is now her husband.

But there weren't many beautiful memories between them.

It wasn't until she sat in the passenger seat and Steven started the car to leave the garage that Diana came back to her senses, looking at Steven driving.

He looked gentle, different from his usual cold expression.

He really changed.

Steven felt her gaze and smiled, "Diana, I like the way you look at me, so focused."

Diana quickly looked away.

"I didn't." Diana denied it, not expecting him to notice her gaze.

Steven looked at her evasive gaze and smiled.

Outside the car window, the sun was about to set.

Diana looked at the sunset. It was beautiful.

She looked out the window and said, "I loved you so much back then. Wherever you were, I would always pay attention."

"Steven, can I never escape you?"

Diana still doesn't dare to gamble with her true feelings.

Steven domineeringly said, "Diana, as long as you understand."

Diana was speechless and gave Steven a glare.

Steven just smiled. He had never been this happy in days.

"Diana, don't be mad. If you're upset in the future, just hit me until you feel better."

Diana smiled, "I'm not a shrew."

Steven frowned and said, "I mean if you're upset, you can hit me or scold me until you feel better."

Diana couldn't understand why he would think that way.

"Steven, I'm not an unreasonable person. When I was sad or upset before, I would go back to the place we share and sort out my emotions. I never brought my bad mood to you."

Steven said what he did earlier precisely because he knew her personality.

He would rather she express her emotions and vent her anger at him than force herself to smile at him.

Steven said, "I know, but we are husband and wife. I hope you can share any troubles with me."

"In the past, our relationship wasn't good, and I didn't even have a chance to explain. Now that we're together, you can talk to me about everything in the future."

"Diana, don't you always say that life is full of conflicts and troubles?"

"That year we lived together, I truly felt happiness. But at that time, I was living in darkness and didn't dare to drag you, who lived in the sunlight, into the abyss."

Diana was slightly stunned, finally realizing that this was what he had been thinking.

But at that time, she was willing to fall into the abyss with him.

Diana just hadn't expected that he didn't want to pull her into the abyss.

She loved him, but it had to be Steven's willingness. She blamed everything on Steven.

The goodness she forced upon Steven might not have been what he wanted.

Diana looked at Steven with a bitter smile and said, "Steven, turns out neither of us knows how to love someone."

They didn't know each other's thoughts without having an open-hearted conversation.

She only knew her own unbearable pain in love but forgot Steven's situation and what he felt inside. Her love, he didn't dare to accept!

Hearing Diana's words, Steven had already parked the car at the entrance of Capitole Hotel.

He held Diana's hand, smiled at her, and said, "Diana, we will learn how to love each other."

Diana smiled, "Let's go, I'm hungry."

Steven saw that she was in a much better mood, and he smiled too.

Under the sunset, they walked into Capitole Hotel side by side.

They had dinner and then went to the auction.

Diana hadn't been to the auction house for a long time.

She always does charity in a practical way.

This auction featured Faelandir's top-tier sapphire necklace, named Stellar Love.

Diana wasn't really interested in it; she just hadn't been to the auction house in a long time and wanted to take a look.

She already had plenty of jewelry.

"Diana."

Diana turned around and saw it was Queenie.

Queenie's face was pale. She walked hunched over, as if she had lost a lot of weight.

Diana was surprised that Queenie still came to the auction house in such a state.

Diana then remembered to ask Steven, "Who set us up last night?"

Steven glanced at Queenie indifferently and said, "It was her! But we have no evidence."

Queenie rarely leaves evidence when she does things.

Diana looked at Queenie and smiled, "Queenie, thank you for helping us last night. We became a real couple."

Queenie's face turned pale. She thought Alexander was useless.

She prepared everything for him, but he just stood by and did nothing.

Queenie sneered and said, "Diana, it's embarrassing that you only slept together after three years of marriage."
