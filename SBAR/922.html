Queenie was in unbearable pain. Everything she had worked for turned into nothing but an empty dream, and in the end, everything ended up belonging to Diana.

What was even more infuriating was that Diana was better than her.

Why was Diana so exceptional?"

Diana, who had been retrieved from the countryside like a wild child, had somehow managed to study abroad all these years. Where had it gone wrong?

All her hopes and dreams had been carefully planned and executed with all her might. Yet, at this moment, everything had crumbled into nothingness.

But that wasn't enough for her.

Not nearly enough.

Her dream was to become a businesswoman admired by everyone.

Her current achievements were far from sufficient to shock everyone.

She wanted to leave a legacy for generations to admire.

But George? He didn't have Steven's abilities.

Steven would have had control of the situation.

But George, this useless man, could accomplish nothing without her and Andrew preparing for him.

Queenie was struck across the face and glared at him with hatred. She had endured so much hardship over the years for this man.

By now, she would have had endless wealth.

And with Steven's help, she could effortlessly become a successful businesswoman.

If it is, then my failure is my own doing."

Diana had never responded to her provocations.

Looking back, Diana was the most resilient one. Meanwhile, Queenie had grown complacent over small successes.

Queenie lowered her head in defeat. She couldn't accept it.

She had been too arrogant, always looking down on Diana.

Diana had once said, "Never underestimate any opponent."

During the Gale incident, Diana had spoken those words, and Queenie hadn't understood. Now she did.

Back then, Diana had only been focused on treating Steven's eyes, so she hadn't reacted.

Diana's patience made her label Diana as useless.

But today, Diana's stunning performance had left her completely in awe.

Queenie's mind went blank as she asked George, "George, what do we do now?"
