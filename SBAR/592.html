"Queenie, of course, I love you. You're my woman. How could I not care about you? Didn't I give you the drug? Just use it to keep him under control. Once you slip it to him, he won't be able to do much. You're smart, and I know you understand what I mean. You're mine, so I won't allow you to get all cozy with him."

Queenie's eyes glistened with tears. She wasn't even sure why she was crying. It felt like he was just saying these things to comfort her.

"I understand. I'll find an opportunity to act," she replied, her voice low and heavy.

"Good. With our people working behind the scenes, that plot of land you bought for the Farley family has attracted a lot of investors. Once this project is completed, we'll be one step closer to our goal.

"As the Gellar family gets stronger, it'll be good for us, too. Queenie, everything I'm doing is for our future. I know your dreams, and I promise I'll help you achieve them."

Queenie felt her heart soften at his gentle words. She still loved him. "Alright, I understand. Don't worry, everything will go according to plan."

"Good. I love you, Queenie," he said, his voice tender and doting.

Hearing that, Queenie felt a sudden warmth. "I know. Thank you for loving me. I'm really happy."

She ended the call with a smile and headed to the restaurant. She called Mario to join her for dinner.

When Steven arrived at the office, he had just settled into his chair when his phone buzzed.

He picked it up to find a text message from an unknown number.

After reading the message, anger surged through him.

"Queenie, you're even more vicious than I thought," he muttered to himself.

It seemed that, once again, he would be failing Diana.

Taking a deep breath, he called Evan into his office.

Evan came in carrying Steven's dinner. "Mr. Gellar, your dinner."

"Alright," Steven said, motioning for him to set it down. He handed Evan his phone, showing him the text.

Evan read it and was stunned. "Oh my God, Mr. Gellar. Is Queenie planning to target you, too?

"What should we do next? Can we trust this information? Who sent it?"

Steven opened the meal box but found he had no appetite. "I have no idea who sent it. But I plan to play along and see when she makes her move. You must keep this news between us."

Evan looked at him sympathetically. He couldn't believe there was no one who truly cared for Steven.

He used to have a pretty good impression of Queenie, and he never expected her to do something like this.

Steven took a bite of his dinner but barely tasted it. "After Paula's company went bankrupt, did you find out who acquired it?" he asked.

Evan replied, "The buyer is named Felicity Aniston, but she's just a front. She's not the real owner; she's the acting CEO of the company."

"But we heard that name when we were in Woodston. Cecil got caught up in a scheme by that woman there. She's probably working with Queenie. That's what I heard from Ms. Bishop."

"Ms. Bishop..."

"Call her Mrs. Gellar. We're married now. Why are you still calling her Ms. Bishop?" Steven interrupted, his voice sharp with anger.

Evan hesitated, then said, "Mr. Gellar, I used to call her Mrs. Gellar. But you once said she was just a country girl and didn't deserve that title."

Steven froze.

Regret washed over him.
