"What are you daydreaming about? Come on, eat up," Steven said suddenly, pulling her out of her thoughts.

Diana held up the lobster in her hand. "I'm eating, see?"

She loved seafood, while Steven typically ate lighter meals.

After two years of a bland diet, indulging in some delicious food lately felt like a breath of fresh air.

After just one lobster, she was already feeling satisfied.

The freshness was incredible, and a smile spread across her face as she savored every bite.

Steven watched her enjoy the meal, a small smile playing on his lips.

He knew how easily she could be pleased.

They ate in comfortable silence, both absorbed in their food.

Once dinner was over, Diana felt a warmth blossoming in her chest, filling her with happiness.

Standing up, she said, "Thanks for dinner. I'm full."

Politeness was important to her, and she always made it a point to express her gratitude.

"Glad to hear it," Steven replied, a sense of contentment lingering from the evening. Just then, his phone buzzed on the table.

He glanced at the screen and saw it was Evan calling.

"Yes?"

"Mr. Gellar, our report didn't work. Queenie's company managed to weather the storm. The evidence we provided doesn't match their data, and the system shows no signs of tax evasion. Now, they're threatening to investigate who framed them."

Steven's brow furrowed. It seemed like Queenie had some powerful allies.

He had known her long enough to realize she had some tricks up her sleeve.

He had underestimated her.

"Find out who's backing Queenie," he instructed before hanging up.

He glanced at Diana, aware she had more insight into the situation.

When she heard him mention investigating Queenie, her eyes immediately locked onto his.

"I asked Evan to report on Queenie's company with solid evidence, but they managed to deflect it. It looks like there's a big player behind them," Steven explained.

Diana's eyes widened in surprise. Something about Steven felt different today, and her guard went up.

Noticing her unease, Steven raised an eyebrow. "Why are you so suspicious? I haven't done anything to you. I told you I'd help you with Queenie..."

"Hold on there, Mr. Gellar. Fewer words and more action, please. I know how you work. You say one thing and do another. I've had enough of your tricks to trust you now."

"Diana," he said, a hint of frustration in his voice.

She didn't respond but instead called for someone to come clean the room.

Once the cleaner left, she headed to her bedroom to call Luna.

Steven followed her, but just as he reached for the door, he caught her on the phone.

"Luna, I need you to look into the company managed by Felicity under Queenie's name. Check if they are involved in tax evasion.

"And check on Queenie's computer skills. She's talented, but she shouldn't be able to handle the tax issues on her own. Find out if she recently hired any top-notch hacker from a school to work for her. And make it quick."
