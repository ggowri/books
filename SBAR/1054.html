Luna saw everything and said, "Diana, he already has wounds and is weak. He lied to you. You two aren’t even divorced yet, and you’re talking about dating another man right in front of him. No wonder he passed out."

Diana heard Luna's voice and asked in surprise, "When did you get here?"

Luna looked a bit surprised, "Diana, you still love him. I've been standing at the door for a while. I came up to call you both for dinner, but when I saw you talking, I didn’t interrupt."

Diana didn’t believe Luna and said, "Luna, I can even talk to him about finding a boyfriend. I don’t love him anymore."

Luna knew Diana all too well. "Diana, you only say that to spite Mr. Gellar. When you mentioned Queenie, your tone was full of jealousy. Forget about Alexander. Just stay with Mr. Gellar already."

"Impossible. You’re underestimating me..."

Luna said, "You should hurry up and wake Mr. Gellar. Could he be in danger?"

Steven was Diana's first love. She loved him deeply.

Diana looked down at him. He had actually fainted from sheer emotion.

Diana looked at Luna, puzzled. "Luna, do you think he fainted because he cares about me and got upset by what I said?"

Luna glanced at her silently, thinking Diana was emotionally clueless.

But back then, Diana had actually pursued Steven for years.

Luna said, "How did you even realize you liked Mr. Gellar? I still remember the professor at the international medical center kept inviting you to study abroad. You turned him down, you wouldn’t leave Mr. Gellar. How could you not notice that Mr. Gellar cared about you?"

Diana sighed helplessly.

She thought for a moment and said, "That professor looked at me differently. He seemed composed, and refined, something strange in the way he looked at me."

Luna chuckled and teased, "Because he liked you. He probably thought someone as talented as you shouldn’t stop here. If you went abroad, you’d achieve so much more."

Diana scoffed, "Luna, he thought I was dumb. He saw me wearing designer clothes, driving a fancy car, and wanted me to invest in him. He had a girlfriend—a really pretty one. I’ve seen her. No way I’d follow someone like that abroad."

"I had a good school, better professors than him, and in the end, I became a professor myself. I’d never stay connected with someone like that."

"That professor believed women were emotional fools, easy to manipulate. Later, he ruined his reputation for dating too many girlfriends."

Luna didn’t pay attention to that professor’s situation.

Diana laid Steven flat, "The year I met Steven and fell for him, because I was tired of studying. I wanted a break. Meeting Steven and letting go of my dreams wasn’t really about him—it was because I’d been pushing myself too hard."

"These three years, I’ll just treat it as giving myself a break."

Luna knew Diana had been exhausted since she was a kid. Luna felt the same, but all the knowledge she learned had faded away.

But Diana picked up everything so quickly.

The first time she touched a computer and keyboard, she mastered it on the very first day.

Back then, Diana had a huge appetite.

Luna and Nolan used to think Diana got smarter from eating so much. Later, they realized she was so intensely focused that she burned energy fast.

Looking back now, Luna thought they were so naive back then.

Suddenly, Luna remembered what happened online and felt uneasy.

Watching Diana take care of Steven, she stayed silent and quietly walked away.

Diana gave Steven a pill.

A few minutes later, he slowly opened his eyes.

Seeing Diana's worried gaze, he asked, confused, "Diana, what happened to me?"

Diana didn’t know how to respond. He had fainted because he got too emotional.

She hesitated and said, "You passed out."

Steven was surprised, unsure why he had passed out.

Hearing Diana say she wanted to date another man, he felt a cold chill run through his body, and then everything went black.

Now he realized it was Diana's words that made him pass out.

Steven grabbed Diana's hand tightly, "Diana, you're not allowed to date other men."

Diana stared at him, stunned.

Did my words really make him pass out? How can he be this fragile?

She said, "I was just talking casually. I meant in the future, not now. Right now, you've tricked me again, and I haven't even gotten mad at you yet. And now you're telling me I can't even talk to other men."

Diana shot him an angry glare and said, "Other women get married and live happily ever after. But marrying you? I almost lost my life."

She truly didn’t want to have anything to do with Steven anymore.

Steven knew she had endured a lot. He said sincerely, "Diana, just give me some time. Once I sort everything out, I’ll make you happy."

His eyes were red, his voice hoarse, and he stared at her intently.

Diana had never seen Steven this gentle before, and it left her a little flustered.

She said, "Stop. Just take care of yourself. Any idea about your leg and eyesight? I found the herbs myself, nearly died. Please, don’t get hurt again. I know Evan secretly gave you medicine, but I switched it with something even helpful. Your body is slowly healing."

Diana smiled proudly. "Steven, I’ve saved you so many times. If I didn’t love you, you’d be dead by now."
