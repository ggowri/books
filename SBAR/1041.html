Seeing Diana's call, Steven got excited.

"Diana," he exclaimed.

Evan watched Steven’s excited expression.

In the past, whenever Diana called, Steven had been cold and dismissive.

But now? He looked as happy as a teenage boy getting his first crush’s call.

Evan shook his head slightly, "Men only learn to cherish what they’ve lost."

"Andrew won't take a commercial flight back. His suitcase probably contains liquids, so he can't pass security. He'll take a private jet. Keep an eye on him."

Steven frowned and then replied, "Alright, Diana. You've looked into the Mysterium Organization too, right? What do you think they're up to?"

He wanted to hear what the Mysterium Organization looked like through Diana's eyes.

She said, "They focus on biotechnology. It's been around for years, but all we know is just surface-level information.

Whatever Andrew's carrying, it's either dangerous, shocking, or something that could turn things around for George."

She had always been wary of Andrew's revenge. Now that he's back, so is the danger.

Steven said seriously, "Diana, let's meet tomorrow. We need to talk."

Diana refused. "No. Queenie and George think you're dead, but Andrew is sharper and more suspicious than they are. Let's see what Andrew brought back first."

"The moment he's back, he definitely wants to take over the Gellar Group. Your biotech company isn't ready yet. Let's see what he's planning."

"Tell me his updates."

Steven replied gently, "Alright! As soon as he arrives, I'll let you know right away. Take care of yourself."

Diana said, "Alright! I'm hanging up now."

Steven hesitated for a moment, "Goodbye."

Diana hung up first. Steven stared at his phone for a long time.

She hadn't called him in a long time.

Back then, Steven used to get annoyed when Diana texted him, reminding him to eat on time or take his medicine. He found it irritating.

As an adult, he didn’t think he needed her constantly nagging him about meals.

But in his life, only she cared for him like that.

Steven was in a good mood.

He glanced at Evan, "Evan, I'm hungry. Get me something to eat. Make sure there's plenty of beef ribs. I feel like having beef ribs tonight."

Evan quickly put his phone down and smiled. "Sure thing, Mr. Gellar. You're finally feeling hungry."

After he went out, he grabbed something delicious to eat. When he came back, he asked Steven if he was hungry, but Steven replied that he wasn’t.

Steven had just taken a call from Diana. And then, suddenly, he was hungry.

Sure enough, only Diana could change him.

Evan prepared him something light. Steven was in a good mood, so he ate a lot for dinner.

Queenie went with Dylan to the hospital to get his wound dressed.

His finger had been broken. By the time he finished surgery, it was already early morning.

The long wait left Queenie feeling annoyed.

She hadn’t managed to teach Diana a lesson. Instead, she was stuck awake until the early hours.

Dylan’s finger was all bandaged up. He stared at his immobile finger, seething with anger.

He was determined to get revenge for tonight.

Diana had everything now, yet she still fought with Queenie. She even snatched the entire Gellar Group from Steven's hands.

When Dylan heard the news abroad, he found it unbelievable.

Steven was highly capable, and now he was helping Queenie.

Dylan looked at Queenie and said, "Queenie, it's late. Let me take you home to rest. Whatever it is, we can talk tomorrow."

She laughed and said, "Dylan, we don’t even have a home anymore. Paula, Eric, and Gale moved into a shabby little apartment. She’s probably afraid we’ll take her inheritance, so now she’s pretending to be poor while secretly handing the company over to Eric."

"I’m wondering if she plans to give the company to Eric. After all, among the four of you brothers, he’s the most capable."

It was obvious that Queenie’s words were meant to sow discord, but Dylan didn’t notice.

His face grew darker. "Why would be Eric? I’m part of the Barnes family too. I still have inheritance rights. Even if you’re illegitimate, you still have a claim. Paula’s gone too far. I’m going back to confront her."

Queenie smiled. "Dylan, you’re back now, but they didn’t even call you. And you came to see me. I think they probably believe I pulled you over. You’ll definitely get scolded from them. They might not even let you in."

"What? Not let me in?" Dylan let out a bitter laugh.

The company belongs to the four of us brothers.

"Why does Eric get to run it alone?"

"If Mom really made that decision, I’ll make a scene for sure."

He sneered, "Queenie, don’t worry. I’ll take back everything."

"Let’s go, take you home first. Where are you staying now?"

The next second, Dylan heard George’s voice. "No need. I’ll pick Queenie up. You should go back to Paula and your brothers."

Dylan saw it was ‘Steven’ and smiled. "Steven, since you’re here, I can head back with peace of mind. Wait for my good news."

Queenie pretended to look worried. "Dylan, if you can’t convince them, maybe fake joining them. Find out the location of Eric’s company, take it back from them. We may be dutiful children, but we have to reclaim our things."

Dylan’s expression was firm. "Don’t worry. If Paula refuses, I’ll take the company back."

Why can Eric run the company, but I can’t?

George said coldly, "Then you better head back. It’s getting late. If there’s anything, talk tomorrow."

Dylan said his goodbyes and took a cab straight home.

An hour and a half later, he got out of the cab. Staring at the rundown neighborhood, he felt disbelief.

Is Paula pretending to be poor, living in a place like this?

She was the rich daughter of the Barnes family. She had always been privileged. How could she possibly get used to such place?

Really broke, or fake it?

Dylan felt completely lost.

Looking at the message Eric had sent him, he walked through the gate of the complex. There wasn’t even a guard at the entrance.

In his twenty-something years of life, it was his first time in a place like this.

Dylan let out a bitter laugh.

Once inside, he found the apartment building. He was stunned—it was an old walk-up.

Dylan was used to living in villas.

By the time he climbed to the fifth floor, he angrily pounded on the door.
