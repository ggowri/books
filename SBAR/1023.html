Cecil heard the soft chime of a bell, and suddenly, it was as if something had latched onto his mind, tugging at his thoughts like invisible strings.

His lips moved before his brain caught up. "You... you're my woman. Come with me, come with me. Tonight, I'm gonna rock your world."

That cocky, over-the-top arrogance? Yeah, that was pure Cecil—when he was himself.

The whole room went still.

Diana's stomach dropped.

What in the actual hell?

Cecil had been keeping a low profile for over a year now—no women, no scandals. So, where was this "woman" coming from?

There was something seriously off about this.

The two bodyguards gripping Cecil hesitated, clearly thrown off by his words.

One of them stepped up, his voice low but firm. "Mr. Farley, you might want to think about what you're saying. We've been with you all night. This is the first time you've seen this woman. She's not yours. You haven't had a girlfriend for over a year. You've only been around Ms. Bishop and Ms. Laurier—no one else. We don't know this woman. She drugged you. You need to wake up."

"Tch!" Lillian scoffed, folding her arms. "And who exactly gave you the right to comment on Mr. Farley's personal life?"

Diana's brows pinched together. "Cecil, you say she's yours? Then tell me—what's her name?"

The sound of Diana's voice seemed to cut through the haze. It was familiar, grounding—like an anchor in a storm.

He gasped, like a drowning man finally breaking the surface. "Diana, help me! A woman is trying to set me up. It—it smells so sweet, I can't think straight."

Lillian's eyes flickered. She gave her wrist another subtle shake.

The bell's chime rang out again, crisp and clear, curling through the air like a whispered command.

"Mr. Farley, it's me, Lillian Everhart. Don't you remember? You forced me. You tried to make me do things I didn't want to. Upstairs—you grabbed my hand, wouldn't let me go. I barely managed to escape. Have you already forgotten what you did?"

Before Cecil could react, Diana's voice cut through. "Ms. Everhart, are you seriously trying to seduce a man who's clearly out of it?"

Lillian turned, her gaze as sharp as a blade. "I'm not seducing him. I'm just reminding him."

Diana leaned back, her expression unreadable, gaze sweeping over the men in the room.

"Why don't we ask the gentlemen here? The way you were talking, the way you moved—tell me that wasn't meant to reel a man in."

A beat of silence.

The men in the crowd shifted, some casting awkward glances at their partners, others avoiding eye contact altogether.

But the truth hung thick in the air.

Then, a woman in the back snorted. "Seduction? Please. Look at her, all desperate and theatrical. If you ask me, this whole thing is a setup. Cecil probably wasn't interested, and she decided to spin it into a whole 'poor me' act. Women like her? They know exactly what they're doing."

Diana exhaled, relieved someone was willing to call it like it was.

The Farley name carried power, wealth, and status. People envied it.

And in situations like this? If someone saw a chance to take Cecil down, they'd jump at it.

Lillian's lips curled, her tone dripping with venom. "Lady, maybe if you kept a better handle on your man, you wouldn't need to blame other women for 'tempting' him. Ever considered that? Maybe instead of whining, you should ask yourself why he's looking elsewhere."

The woman's face darkened, her jaw tightening.

"Oh, honey, I know I'm not twenty anymore. But let's see how you look when you hit my age. You think being young gives you the right to be a little viper? That youth makes you untouchable?

"And honestly? Women like you don't last. You either wise up or you burn out—fast."

Lillian rolled her eyes, unimpressed. Great. Now, she had to deal with a woman going through a midlife crisis. Waste of time.

Things were so close to going her way.

She wasn't about to waste breath on nobodies. She turned back to Cecil. "Mr. Farley, you just admitted it. You wanted to hurt me. Now, in front of everyone, do you dare say it again?"

One step closer. One inch at a time.

All she needed was for him to say it. One confirmation and his reputation would never recover.

She moved just slightly—her wrist flicking again, just enough for the bell to chime. Diana's eyes narrowed. She had been watching every movement, every word.

This was the third time Lillian rang that damn bell. Why did Cecil only seem panicked when Diana spoke, but completely docile when she did?

What the hell was going on?

Diana watched as Cecil's gaze turned vacant, his eyes losing focus.

A sinking feeling crept into her chest—was he being controlled?

The thought had barely formed when Cecil suddenly shouted, "That's right. I want you."

Diana's fingers clenched around the armrest of her wheelchair.

The words landed like a bomb.

The crowd collectively held its breath.

And then, Queenie smirked. Diana might be smart, but she'd never be as ruthless as her.

Diana pressed her lips together, her mind racing. Where the hell was Luna?

Why wasn't she back yet?

Lillian's voice wobbled, just enough to sound pitiful. "Did you all hear that? He admitted it! He admitted that he wanted to humiliate me! But what did I do to deserve this?

"Just because someone has money and power, does that mean women like us don't matter?"

"They say Cecil likes playing games with women, but if this is what he does in public, imagine what he's like in private." A voice came out of the crowd.

"How many women has he done this to? Women too scared to speak out, because the Farley family is too powerful?" another chimed in. "But this woman—she's different. She got away. And she's standing up for herself."

"You don't have to be afraid, sweetheart. We won't let him get away with this."

A middle-aged man stepped forward, voice ringing loud over the murmurs.

Lillian turned to him, dipping her head, her expression pure gratitude. "Thank you, sir. I'll never forget your kindness."

Her face was soft, innocent. But her eyes—sharp, calculating, unwavering.

Diana's grip tightened around her wheelchair. Her gaze was ice-cold.

Cecil's bodyguards darted frantic looks in her direction.

Diana didn't need to hear them beg—she knew they were in deep trouble. And so was Cecil.

She turned back to Lillian. Something was off with this woman.

Why the hell was Cecil following her lead so easily?

She was lost in thought when Lillian suddenly turned to her. "Ms. Bishop, now that Mr. Farley has admitted it, do you have anything else to say?"

Diana let out a thoughtful "hmm" and tilted her head. "Well, since you asked... yeah, I do."

Lillian's eyes flickered. "What?"
