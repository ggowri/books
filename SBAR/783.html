Another companion stepped forward, a look of regret on his face.

The boy smiled, "It's alright; fate will bring us together again."

Steven dragged Diana back to his room.

Althea and Paige were surprised to see someone like Steven in a place meant for young people.

Althea wondered, "How did Steven end up here?"

Paige pointed to the Gellar Group logo above the elevator.

Althea quickly understood. "This is Steven's territory."

Paige nodded. "That must be it."

Althea thought, "Of course it's his territory!"

"Is Diana in danger? Steven looks really angry."

Althea was worried.

Concern creased Paige's brow. Diana often faced bullying from Steven.

Noticing Diana's phone was with her, Althea felt even more anxious.

"Paige, we need to check on her. If Steven hurts Diana, we can help. Her phone is with me, so if she needs to call for help, she won't be able to."

Paige felt the urgency and replied, "Okay, let's go find her!"

As the elevator doors opened, they rushed toward Diana's room.

Paige tapped rapidly on the door, but there was no response.

Althea felt like crying, tears welling in her eyes. "What if she's somewhere else? What if she hasn't come back?"

They had been too hurried to notice where Steven might have taken Diana.

Paige leaned against the door, trying to reassure Althea. "It's okay, it's okay. Steven wouldn't dare hurt her. In the last two times I saw him, he seemed to care about Diana."

Althea scoffed, "His affection is worthless now."

Diana found herself in Steven's room.

She struggled against his iron grip, wincing as he squeezed her wrist. "Steven, why did you bring me here? Let me go; my hand hurts."

Steven looked at her with a smile that didn't quite reach his eyes, his anger simmering beneath the surface.

"Diana, I can't believe you dance so well."

He had watched her dance the whole time, mesmerized. Since she left, every time he saw her, she was more captivating than ever.

Seeing her connect so perfectly with that boy had ignited a spark of jealousy in him.

She was an excellent driver, a skilled healer, a talented designer, and now an amazing dancer—what else had she hidden from him?

Was she really just a country girl?

No. The last time he met Leopold, the man had seemed ordinary, but his wise eyes spoke of a lifetime of experience—definitely not an average person.

Diana couldn't help but laugh at his frustration. How was it that he could be upset about her dancing with someone else while he sought out other women?

"You never pay attention to my life or what I do every day. Of course, you wouldn't know that I can dance! I don't just do street dance; I can also do jazz and Latin..."

She counted on her fingers but lost interest, feeling deflated as she plopped onto the couch.

Her voice lowered. "Steven, we agreed this morning—one year and then we'll divorce, each living our own lives. How can you interrupt my chance to find a cute guy? Do you know what my grandfather's dream is? To surround me with a hundred handsome men, each ready to serve me every day."
