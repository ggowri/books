Steven watched her puzzled expression. She had just woken up and was still groggy.

She had just yawned, her sleepy eyes so adorable that he couldn't help but want to kiss her.

Steven reached out to wipe the tear from the corner of her eye and said gently, "Sweetheart, I didn't do anything to you. Why are you crying?"

Diana froze, looking at his smiling face.

He was getting more and more skilled at saying sweet, tender words.

Diana smiled and slowly sat up straight.

After a good sleep, she felt completely refreshed and stretched lazily.

Steven steadied her. She asked, "Steven, since when did you learn to joke around?"

To her, Steven was as cold as an emotionless robot, hardly ever joking.

Steven smiled, "Everything's settled. No more threats, no more danger. My mood's changed, so I can finally say what I want."

Diana froze, thinking, "Just how oppressed has he been, that he couldn't even speak freely?"

She had been a pampered child growing up. Except for the past few years, she had always basked in the love of her brother and grandfather.

She had never been through what he had. It was only after Helen told her about his childhood that Diana felt such sympathy for him, and love.

Diana wanted to give him hope, to help him live a better life. Even if he was always cold and distant, she still greeted him with a smile.

Back then, her thoughts were simple—she just wanted him to stay alive.

Soon, the boat docked. Steven drove off the ship.

Diana glanced around and said, "Isn't this the island you bought for Queenie? Why did you bring me here? Are you trying to show me how much you adore her?"

Diana mocked him on purpose, but when she turned, she caught the sadness in Steven's eyes.

She knew perfectly well that he bought this island because of her.

Diana instantly regretted what she had just said and fell silent.

When they were together, the past was something they could never bring up.

Whenever the past was mentioned, she would always break down, always end up mocking Steven.

The pain, the humiliation she once endured—Diana found it hard to forget.

This time, she was going to live for herself. She wouldn't let them hurt her again.

Steven stayed silent. He knew she couldn't let go of the past, but he was determined to make her feel his love.

The car quickly pulled up in front of the seaside villa.

Steven unlocked his phone, using the remote app to open the villa's gate, then drove straight into the garage.

The March wind was still chilly, especially on the island. The coconut trees swayed and rustled in the breeze.

Every now and then, they could hear the sound of coconuts hitting the ground.

The wind was really strong.

Diana suddenly asked curiously, "Why are we at the beach this time of year? It's freezing!"

Steven said, "The villa has heating. We'll be warm once we're inside. Didn't you say you wanted barbecue and beer? I had everything prepared. We can eat soon."

Diana was surprised to see him respond to her so normally.

She thought, "Could it be that now everything's out in the open, his mindset has changed, and he's acting like a normal person again?"

"The impact of the environment is really scary. But this version of Steven is pretty nice."

Diana opened the car door and stepped out. The wind was strong. She quickly ran toward the villa.

Steven got out of the car and followed right behind her. He quickly opened the villa door and turned on the heat. The room gradually started to warm up.

Steven knew Diana was always cold.

He pulled her to sit on the couch, grabbed the thin blanket nearby, and draped it over her shoulders.

"Thanks!" Diana said with a smile.

Steven looked at her tenderly and said, "Let's go to the restaurant, have some barbecue, and drink beer."

Diana looked at him and said, "I don't think you really want a drink right now. You just want to talk to me."

Neither of them liked drinking.

If they ended up sleeping together after drinking, she would be the only one regretting it.

It wasn't until she arrived at the Marsh Villa that she realized how dark some people's minds could be—all thanks to Paula and her crew.

Steven pulled Diana into the kitchen to grab something to eat. He really did want to talk to her—and seriously win her over.

Given the chance, he would make sure to be with the one he loved.

Diana looked at his smiling face. This side of Steven—gentle and refined—completely made her heart skip a beat.

His face was her absolute favorite.

Alexander was undeniably handsome, but the way Steven made her feel was completely different.

Cecil and Joseph were good-looking too, but in her heart, Steven was still the most handsome.

On the table in the restaurant sat a grill pan and marinated meat.

Diana spotted her favorite shrimp and suddenly felt a little hungry.

She figured grilling here would leave the villa filled with smoke.

It was freezing outside, and she didn't feel like sitting out there for a barbecue.

Diana said, "Steven, help me out. Forget the barbecue. I'll cook something else."

Steven backed her up. "Alright!"

Diana glanced around and spotted an apron on the chair.

She walked over, picked up the apron, tied it on, and checked the ingredients. There was fish, shrimp, crab, along with cabbage and tomatoes.

She decided to make vegetable soup, seafood, and a salad.

Diana finished cooking in just half an hour.

She even made pasta for Steven.

They sat across from each other. Steven pulled out a bottle of red wine, poured two glasses, and handed one to Diana.

She served him some vegetable soup and told him to start with that.

Steven knew Diana had this habit when it came to cooking.

She loved having soup.

Vegetable soup, chicken soup—she loved them both.

Steven picked up the vegetable soup and took a sip. Her influence had made him love soup too.

After finishing the soup, they both felt warm inside.

They didn't say a word, quietly eating their food.

Steven cracked the crab for Diana, letting her focus on eating.

After finishing the shrimp and crab, Diana felt full.

She put down her utensils and went to wash her hands.

Steven followed her to wash his hands too.

When they returned, Steven grabbed a bottle of red wine and led Diana to the room upstairs.

Diana went to freshen up, while Steven sat in the bedroom upstairs, waiting for her.

He glanced at the crashing waves outside the window, then shifted his gaze toward the bathroom.

After a while, he got up and headed to take a shower too.

Half an hour later, they emerged from the bathroom into the room.

Diana picked up the glass of red wine, handed it to Steven, and smiled. "Cheers."
