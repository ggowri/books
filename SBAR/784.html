At those words, Steven inhaled sharply.

One was not enough, two were not enough—she wanted a hundred.

"Diana..."

He breathed heavily, his frustration mounting.

Diana discreetly glanced at the furious Steven, who was usually calm and composed around her. Even when angry, he spoke to her in a cold, distant tone.

She had rarely seen him this upset.

"I truly don't understand you," she said, observing his darkened features. "You told me not to cling to you, so I didn't. I followed your wishes and divorced you. Now you suddenly don't want the divorce? I've never met someone so fickle."

Steven's eyes flickered, caught off guard by her question. He wanted to admit he had misjudged Queenie and that he had loved the wrong person.

He didn't understand love and thought that being nice to Queenie was love.

It was Diana's leaving that made him understand what love was.

Diana couldn't just walk away, leaving him ensnared in confusion.

"You're silent again," she pressed. "What does my life plan have to do with you? Why are you so angry?"

Diana was angry too; he had ruined her good mood yesterday, and now today.

It felt so unfair!

"Also, I'm just out to unwind and spend time with friends. Why do you always have to show up?"

She was clearly annoyed, deeply frustrated.

"So, if I didn't appear, you'd be happy mingling with those guys?" Steven's gaze bore into her, serious and intense.

Diana thought there was nothing wrong with it.

Meeting like-minded people could lead to exchanges and learning. Why did he see it as something sinister?

"I'm not as pathetic as you think! Did I ever interfere when you were with Queenie? You even let her send you those photos to annoy me—did I ever scold you for it?

"Because you don't love me, I didn't tell you about those photos or Queenie's taunts. You two have become increasingly outrageous, and I had to bring it into the open."

After speaking, she turned her back on him, deciding that the best punishment was to ignore him.

Steven sighed helplessly. He had explained the photos with Queenie countless times, but she refused to believe him.

He had never been intimate with her!

Feeling helpless, he stated firmly, "I've already answered that question. I've never touched her!"

Steven felt that he was wronged.

But Diana didn't trust his words; to her, Steven was simply a scoundrel who denied responsibility.

"Ugh!" she sighed deeply. "Steven, this is exhausting. Aren't you tired of living like this? Poor Queenie, how did she end up with such a terrible man like you?"

Steven was stunned.

He clenched his fists, unsure of how to respond.

While she was tired of love, his feelings were just beginning.

He pulled her to her feet. Startled by his roughness, Diana looked up at him, wide-eyed. "What are you doing?"

Seeing the fear in her eyes, he suddenly felt playful.

"What am I doing?"

His voice was low and teasing, dripping with ambiguity.

"Diana, you're my wife." His intense gaze held hers, commanding and assertive.

Diana's eyes flashed with panic. "S-So what?"

"So, you've never sleep with me. How about tonight?"
