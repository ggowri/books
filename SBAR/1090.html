Diana waved her hand and said, "Before George showed up, I had no idea there was a man who looked exactly like you. I always thought it was you who caused the death of me and the baby in my past life."

"I was reborn on Queenie's birthday party. She framed me for kidnapping her. Even when I showed evidence, none of you believed me. From that moment on, I swore to myself I wouldn't get tangled with any of you again."

"Later, I found out about George. The moment he spoke to me for the first time, I knew it was him, not you, who pushed me down the stairs in my past life."

"Coincidentally, Queenie remembered some things recently. She came to me and told me about your past life. After I died, you died too, though I don't know how you died."

"Queenie started carrying out her plan to make sure Paula lost everything."

"She staged a kidnapping, taking both me and herself. Paula was frantic. My four brothers only saved Queenie. Their ending was tragic. By then, two of them were already crippled. I told them it was all Queenie's plan, but none of them believed me."

"When the kidnappers demanded they could only save one person, they chose Queenie. I was tortured—my hands and feet crippled, my face disfigured. In the end, I was pushed down the stairs."

"After I died, I appeared around them as a ghost and saw all of their endings. Maybe it was because I hated you so much, I didn't realize George wasn't you."

"In the end, George killed himself in front of my grave. Queenie got everything she wanted and became a business queen."

"This time, a lot has changed. The day I came back, I warned Paula that Queenie was plotting against her. The supplements she was taking were laced with drugs that could trigger psychosis."

"Because in the last life, Queenie succeeded. Jeff and Queenie's biological mother lived happily and even managed to take over your company..."

Diana told Steven everything.

She thought everything that happened was pretty surreal, but Olivia said life itself is surreal.

Diana didn't know why she came back, but since she did, she decided to live her life fully. She wouldn't compromise herself for others anymore.

There weren't many people in this world worth her sacrificing herself for.

A woman who doesn't rely on or cling to others is the strongest of all.

Diana finished her sentence and downed the glass of red wine in one go.

She felt herself relax.

Coming back to this world, her relationship with her mom was still strained.

Family, to her, was a luxury she could only dream of.

As Diana sighed, she was suddenly pulled into Steven's arms.

She could hear his quick, uneven breaths.

"I'm sorry, Diana," he said, his voice trembling. "I failed to protect you and the baby."

It turned out they really did have a child once.

Diana patted his back gently, a faint smile on her lips. "I'm just telling you the truth," she said softly. "My thoughts are all over the place too. Whether it was a dream or it really happened doesn't matter anymore. The truth I needed... I already knew it the moment I was pushed down the stairs."

Because she had once loved Steven, she hated him that much more.

It was because she had given her love so completely that the trampled love turned into such intense hatred.

But now, Diana had let it go. The hatred had exhausted her.

She decided to surrender to God's plan. A life with regrets is the only normal one.

The feeling of struggling in despair was just too painful.

Even after living through two lifetimes, Diana still felt that heart-wrenching pain.

Steven looked at her, his voice trembling. "Diana, do you really forgive me?"

Steven stared into her eyes. They were beautiful, carrying a faint smile, like winter sunlight, warm enough to chase away the shadows in his heart.

Diana smiled. "Of course. Holding on only hurts me. You didn't kill me or the child. I can forgive you."

"Before, you hurt me because you didn't love me. But that wasn't your fault. It was my own stubbornness, staying by your side, that caused me all that pain."

"That night when I left the party, I had already figured it out."

"Not just that, I also saved Cecil's family."

Looking back now, Diana realized even eyes could be deceiving.

In her past life, many things she saw weren't the truth.

Diana said, "As a soul, I was tormented by hatred. I stayed far away from you—no, I mean George. I was trapped near him, and it was unbearable."

"If I don't let it go, I'll never move forward. Letting go of that hatred is the best redemption for myself."

Now Diana finally understood that only she could save herself.

Her death brought unbearable pain to those around her.

She said, "Steven, you know what? Death is contagious. After I was gone, my grandpa and Tobias were killed by Queenie. She secretly raised a group of assassins. Tobias was killed by one of them."

"I still have so many things to deal with right now."

Diana felt deeply restless. Not finding those people left her constantly uneasy.

Tears rolled down her cheeks before she even realized it.

She was a deeply emotional person, the kind who cried at movies all the time.

Steven leaned down, kissed the tears on her face, and said softly, "Diana, leave everything to me from now on."

Diana felt his tenderness, caught the scent of wine, and froze for a moment. She wondered if she was drunk too.

Feeling his kiss, she pulled back slightly, putting some distance between them. It brought her back to her senses.

She said, "Steven, don't you dare mess with me."

Steven chuckled. Watching her cry, he found her both adorable and heartbreaking. "Kissing you counts as messing with you?"

Diana said, "Of course it does. Without my permission, you're not allowed to touch me."

She had a low tolerance for alcohol, and now her eyes were hazy, slightly tipsy.

Steven smiled gently and said, "You say you've forgiven me, but you haven't in your heart. You're my wife. I have the right to kiss you."
