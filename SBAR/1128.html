Steven's eyes snapped open, landing on Diana's flushed cheeks. Hearing her faint complaint, he quickly sat up and reached out to feel her forehead.

Her forehead was burning hot. He murmured softly, "Diana, you're sick. We need to get you to the hospital right away."

Diana felt miserable. She shook her head weakly and whispered, "Take me home. I have medicine and needles there. I can give myself a shot. The hospital's too uncomfortable."

She didn't hate hospitals, but it was just a minor cold. She didn't see the need to go.

Steven said, "Alright! Let's go home."

Steven quickly got out of bed, slipped on his shoes, then helped her with hers. Scooping her up in his arms, he carried her home.

In the car.

Diana was drowsy, her eyes shut tight. The fever left her feeling miserable. Occasionally, she pressed her dry, chapped lips together in discomfort.

Steven drove fast, weaving through traffic to get home as quickly as possible.

By the time they got home, Diana had fallen into a deep sleep.

On the way, Steven had already instructed Evan to arrange for a doctor to come and give Diana an IV.

In all their years together, this was the first time Steven had seen Diana seriously ill. She was always healthy, and even a minor cold would pass with just a bit of medicine.

But this time, she was seriously ill.

After the IV, the doctor examined Diana thoroughly and found no other issues. It was just a cold and fever; she'd be fine after the treatment.

Steven stayed by Diana's side the entire time. It took four IV bottles before her fever finally broke.

She woke up, her forehead damp with sweat. Her hair clung to her skin, and Steven had never seen Diana look so fragile before.

He held her hand tightly, his heart aching. "Diana, are you feeling uncomfortable anywhere else? The doctor's downstairs. I can call them up right now."

Diana blinked, her voice hoarse. "No need. My whole body feels awful. Steven, I'm sick. I can't even remember the last time I needed an IV—it's been years."

Diana's throat felt sore, and her lips were dry. Steven gently used a cotton swab to moisten her lips, easing her discomfort.

Diana slowly sat up. This time, she truly felt miserable.

Steven placed a pillow behind her, making sure she could lean back more comfortably.

Steven gently brushed his hand across her forehead. Her fever had finally subsided.

At this moment, her face was pale and cold to the touch.

After the illness, she looked even thinner.

"Diana, are you hungry?" Steven asked softly.

Diana nodded. "Yes, I'm hungry. I want something delicious."

Steven smiled. "Alright, I'll have Luna prepare something for you."

Steven picked up his phone and texted Luna, asking her to whip up something delicious and bring it up for Diana.

Luna had everything ready—steak, salad, and Steven's favorite mushroom soup.

He had been by Diana's side all evening and skipped dinner himself.

Diana no longer felt dizzy and could walk on her own. She decided to freshen up.

She took a deep breath, instantly feeling much lighter.

She looked at Steven. "Go have dinner first. I'll join you in a bit."

Steven noticed her awkwardness and quietly left the bathroom.

Diana let out a sigh of relief. Steven was starting to feel a bit clingy.

She closed the bathroom door and took a quick, hot shower.

Half an hour later, she dried her hair and finally felt completely refreshed.

Feeling weak, she dragged herself to get something to eat.

Steven wasn't eating; he was waiting for her.

He sat on the couch, dressed in pristine white loungewear, exuding an air of elegance.

His skin was flawless, his features strikingly refined. He looked just like she remembered!

Diana sighed. Seeing Steven in white, it was impossible not to steal glances at him.

He was just so irresistibly handsome.

Diana sat down beside him, openly letting her gaze roam over Steven.

Steven felt a bit embarrassed under her stare, but he didn't look away. Instead, he gazed at her intently. "Diana, what are you looking at? Is there something on my face?"

He had just been to the bathroom. He only came out after washing his face. Could there really be something on his face?

Steven reached up and touched his face, but felt nothing there.

Watching his reaction, Diana flashed a sly smile. "You're really handsome. Have I ever told you what I thought the first time I saw you?"

In Diana's mind, she replayed that picturesque moment from years ago. Every time she thought of it, a smile would unknowingly grace her lips.

Steven remembered that smile. It was gentle, warm, and radiant. Back then, the subtle melancholy in his eyes had transformed into deep affection.

He hadn't forgotten how she once said she loved seeing him in a white shirt.

He said softly, "Diana, I remember."

Diana smiled, her voice slightly husky. To Steven, her laughter was enchanting, full of life and allure.

Steven pulled her into his arms, holding her tightly with heartfelt emotion.

He lowered his head and kissed the top of her hair. "Diana, I've admired you for so long," he said tenderly.

Diana snapped back to her senses, quickly averting her gaze. He was just too handsome.

Once again, she found herself irresistibly drawn to Steven.

Diana stepped out of his embrace, her cheeks flushed. She pointed to the dishes on the table. "Let's eat."

Watching her avoid him, Steven felt a twinge of disappointment, but not for long. As long as he tried, he knew he could win her back again.

His Diana was pure and kind-hearted.

Steven handed her a glass of warm water. "Diana, drink this first."

Diana's throat was already parched. She took the glass, tilted her head back, and drank half of it in one go.

Steven took the glass from her and slipped on a pair of disposable gloves. Then, he tore the chicken into small pieces and fed them to Diana.

The roasted chicken was tender and flavorful. Diana ate happily, savoring every bite.

"This is delicious, so tender. Luna's cooking just keeps getting better."

Diana was starving. After she finished a drumstick, Steven stopped her from eating more. He set his phone aside on the other end.

Diana looked at him, puzzled. "I'm not full yet."

Steven knew she wasn't full. He picked up the bowl of porridge and fed her. "You're sick, you can't eat too much. Have some oatmeal porridge instead."

Diana blinked but kept her mouth shut. She still wanted more roasted chicken.

"Just one more chicken wing, and I promise I'll stop. Then I'll drink the oatmeal porridge like a good girl."

Seeing the hopeful look in her eyes, Steven couldn't bring himself to say no.

In the end, he tore off another chicken wing and fed it to her.

After finishing another chicken wing, Diana felt nearly full. A few sips of oatmeal porridge later, she finally regained some strength.

Only then did Steven calmly start eating his dinner.

Diana watched him and smiled. "Steven, after all these years of knowing you, this is the first time you've ever taken care of me."

Steven replied, "It was always you taking care of me before."

Diana pointed at him. "Just eat your dinner already."

Diana's emotions were a tangled mess. Once you bow your head and forgive, do you forever lose your dignity? Was she doing the right thing?

Steven finished his meal. Noticing Diana lost in thought, he texted Luna, asking her to come upstairs and clear the table.

Luna came upstairs, said nothing, and quickly cleared the table before leaving.

Diana was still lost in thought.

Steven sat down beside her. Gently tucking a strand of hair behind her ear, he asked softly, "Diana, what's on your mind?"
