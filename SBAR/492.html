Courtney began, "Diana, you—"

"Enough," Wyatt growled, standing off to the side.

Then, he turned to look at Diana, and she met his gaze. He wore an impeccably tailored suit, and though well past fifty, he still exuded a mature charm that most women found irresistible. But in his eyes, there was a calculating glint, a touch of malice that was hard to miss.

"Ms. Bishop," Wyatt said. "My wife was raised in high society. She's used to being treated with the utmost respect and can sometimes be a little...proud. Please, don't take it personally."

"Your son's arrogant behavior was trending this morning. Your company's shares are dropping as we speak—none of which concerns me."

Sorry, but there's no way I'd agree to any kind of private settlement!" Her voice shook with emotion, and her eyes blazed with anger.

"So, if anyone's got a messy private life, it's definitely you."

Diana figured if Courtney were going to sling mud at her, she'd just double it right back. Before coming here, she had already looked into Courtney's affairs. It turned out that she had been keeping several younger men, though Wyatt had no idea.

Courtney's face darkened, and she glared at Diana, furious. "What are you talking about? Diana, are you seriously trying to smear my name? Where's your proof?"

Watching Courtney unravel, losing her composed, classy façade, Diana chuckled and replied, "If you didn't have proof, why throw dirt on me then? Funny thing is, I actually do have evidence. I just listed the locations, didn't I? So, unless you've got something to show that proves I'm the one 'getting around,' go ahead and bring it out."
