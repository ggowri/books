She deserved it!

In the end, Diana was still outsmarted by Leopold and Edward.

Though she adored Steven, being manipulated left her utterly miserable.

So this was how Diana ended up by his side—because she loved him!

And there's something I need to tell you—I've liked you for a long time. I've wanted to chase you for even longer. Honestly, I've probably loved you just a few months less than you've loved me."

Every single day she was away, he missed her desperately.

He had mentioned the idea of having a child before.

Steven saw she wasn't angry and kept smiling. "Diana, the moment I met you, I fell in love with you."

But after everything in our past life, can we truly find happiness in this one?

Time has blurred so much, but the pain still lingers.

"For the rest of my life, I just want to cherish myself!"

Leopold, tears streaming down his face, said, "Diana may have been cold to you before, but she didn't hurt you as much as Paula did."

Back then, it was for Paula's love that Diana recklessly went to the Marsh Villa.

Leopold would never allow Diana to suffer or be bullied by the Marsh family.

Steven joined in bullying Diana too. If it weren't for Edward, Leopold would have taught Steven a harsh lesson.

But at that time, his family was dealing with some issues of their own.

Leopold had no choice but to return home to handle family matters, leaving Diana to live like that.

Now, Diana is both her father's weakness and his armor.

But Diana had already repaid Paula's kindness.

Helen said, "Diana, sometimes leaving and hurting are what teach us how to truly love someone. Whether you forgive Steven or not, it's your choice. We'll always support you."

Diana turned to Jenny and asked, "So, what secret were you planning to tell me when you asked to meet on January 8th?"

This had been bothering her all along.

"That man was your dad's sworn enemy. For the sake of his research, he didn't even spare your father."

"Your father never married, and you're his only daughter. Taking you back means facing challenges, not living in luxury."
