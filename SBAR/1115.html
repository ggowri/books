Diana thought for a moment and decided not to ask. If she was tired enough, she could sleep by the side of the road.

She loved the spring sun. On windless days, she'd turn her back to the sun, find a spot to lean against, and take the sweetest nap.

She also loved napping on a small boat, gently rocking back and forth. It was so relaxing.

She said, "Thank you."

"Diana, I'm your husband. You don't need to thank me," he said, staring at her with a gentle smile.

Diana thought for a moment and said, "But you've never made me feel like I have a husband."

"Back then, I had a family, a husband, but it always felt like I had nothing. I had to face being framed and all your accusations alone."

"Grandma and Althea would comfort me, but other than that, no one cared about how I felt. Back then, I didn't even dare to reach out to Grandpa. When I left, he warned me not to call him unless I was coming home."

After saying it, she felt the words sounded a bit off, especially the first sentence. It made her seem like a woman with an insatiable desire.

Steven walked in with a smile and leaned close to her ear. "Darling, give me a chance now, and I'll make sure you feel it. I'm sorry I didn't make you feel like you had a husband before. From now on, I'll make sure you know how wonderful it is to have one. And... everything before, that was my fault."

Diana's face turned red instantly, feeling like she had just walked straight into her own trap.

She wiped the sweat off her forehead and thought, "Why did I say something like that? No, speaking my mind isn't shameful."

Diana quickly composed herself. "Move aside, I need to start getting ready."

Steven kissed her cheek and left the bathroom with a smile.

The first rule of dating is to flirt occasionally, make them blush and their heart race—then their heartbeat will follow yours.

He had never been in a relationship. Evan said being nice to Queenie was dating, but he didn't think so. Love was about caring for the other person's feelings, making them feel truly happy—that was real love.

Steven went back to his room, put on his shoes, and headed downstairs to freshen up. Soon, he reached the kitchen and saw Luna and Evan making breakfast.

The two stood very close. Ever since they got together, they didn't care who was around—they were always glued to each other.

"Evan looks excited. Did they sleep together last night?" he wondered.

Steven walked up behind them and suddenly said, "Good morning!"

Evan had just kissed Luna on the cheek when Steven's sudden voice startled him. He quickly pulled away, his face turning bright red.

Luna blushed and kept washing the vegetables. Evan flashed Steven a bright smile.

Steven thought, men are so different when they're in love. Now, Evan's attention was constantly on Luna.

Steven walked over and poured himself a glass of water.

Luna said, "Morning, Steven. I heard Diana forgave you. That's really good news. There's some coffee over there, go help yourself."

Steven chuckled, "Luna, you're so thoughtful. When you two get married, I'll gift you a new house. If there's a villa nearby you like, just let me know."

Luna's eyes widened in shock. She hadn't expected a cup of coffee to earn her a multi-million-dollar villa.

"Steven, we heard you and Diana made up. Congrats. We made coffee for you too," two voices chimed in from behind.

Steven turned around to see Nick and Barry staring at him expectantly. Clearly, they were tempted by the idea of a new house too.

Steven raised an eyebrow, feeling pleased to earn their approval. They were Diana's closest friends, after all. "Alright, when you two get married, I'll get you a house too."

Nick chuckled. Barry, on the other hand, dashed over excitedly. "Steven, I'm not a fan of the villas around here. The maintenance fees are insane—enough to raise two kids! I want a big apartment instead. Get me a big apartment!"

He would absolutely accept a house from Steven with all his heart.

Steven laughed, "Alright! Once you pick an apartment, call Evan. He'll take care of it."

Evan was speechless. A simple blessing had earned such a hefty reward.

At that moment, Diana let out a cold snort. Luna and the other two immediately straightened up, looking at her respectfully and saying in unison, "Good morning, Diana."

The three of them smiled, unable to hide their excitement.

Diana was furious, pointing at the three of them. Steven's actions would only fuel their greed.

"You three sold me out for an apartment."

Actually, even if Steven didn't give them a new apartment, she had already prepared homes for them. They grew up together, and if she was happy, she naturally wanted them to be happy too.

The three exchanged awkward glances, a bit embarrassed. After all, wishing her happiness in exchange for multimillion-dollar apartments was a steal.

Luna smiled and said, "Diana, didn't you forgive Steven already?"

Diana raised an eyebrow, staring at Steven with a smirk. "Who said I forgave him? I'm just giving him a chance."

In her past life, if it weren't for Steven causing her and her child's death, she could have endured everything else.

Diana said, "But since Steven wants to give you the apartments, just take them. He's loaded."

Luna laughed. Nick and Barry looked pleased too. "Alright, Diana."

Diana asked, "Is breakfast ready? After breakfast, I'm heading to the office today."

Steven was back. She needed to go to the office to hand over her work and pass the company matters to him. Many people wanted Steven's CEO position, but she didn't. She just wanted a break.

Luna said excitedly, "Diana, you and Steven wait a bit. Breakfast will be ready soon."

Steven turned around to pour some coffee and poured another cup for Diana. He handed it to her. "Diana, have some coffee first."

Diana took it, drank half, and said, "Today we'll go to the office to hand over the work. After that, the company is yours."

Steven said, "Alright! I'll handle the company. Nothing else changes."

Nothing else changes meant he wouldn't take back everything he had given her before.

Diana got interested, smiled, and asked, "Steven, aren't you afraid I'll fall for someone else if you don't take your assets back?"

Steven gave a confident smile. "Diana, I'm not afraid. If you really fall for someone else, it just means I'm not a good husband and failed to satisfy you in some way."

Diana thought, "Well, at least he's self-aware. But... in some ways? Is he hinting at something?"
