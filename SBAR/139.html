After listening, Evan seemed to time it perfectly, sending him a voice message.

"Mr. Gellar, look at the girl you've spoiled since childhood. You spent billions on her, and she doesn't even know what you like to eat, haha..."

Hearing Evan's gloating laughter, Steven knew he was being teased.

He tiredly rubbed his forehead and stood up to get some water.

Steven had insomnia that night, and the next day, he didn't look too well.

During the morning meeting, everyone in the conference room spoke carefully. They were afraid of saying something wrong and being kicked out.

When it was Emma's turn, she presented the new winter collection.

Seeing Emma made him think of Diana.

After listening to Emma's team's suggestions, Steven said, "Ms. Milton, last year's winter collection generated two billion in sales, and your designs are well-known. This year, let's push for 2.5 billion. I'll meet your requests, but you need to hit the targets."

After hearing that, Emma was full of confidence. She smiled warmly. "Thank you, Mr. Gellar, I won't let you down."

Steven didn't want to be disappointed either, knowing the competitor had already signed with the Powell Group.

Partnering with the Powell Group, Diana's designs were solid, and Nolan's company would likely surpass last year's revenue by half.

"Meeting adjourned." His tall figure stood up, and he walked out.

Emma was in a good mood, watching Steven's back as he left, feeling like she was getting closer to him.

She was certain she could make Steven see her in a better light.

Steven and Evan returned to the office.

Queenie had already arrived, wearing a pink dress with her long hair down. It was a look Steven used to like.

She was holding a thermos. When she saw Steven, she smiled sweetly.

"Steven, I came to see you. I made some pumpkin soup for you."

Evan almost laughed when he heard that. She was really going for it.

He quietly slipped away, not wanting to stick around for the drama.

Queenie, smiling, reached to hold Steven's arm.

Steven subtly moved away.

Queenie froze for a moment, then asked in a hurt tone, "Steven, how can you ignore me just because Diana said a few things?"

Queenie never missed a chance to undermine Diana. She was insightful and knew how to play to men's emotions.

She knew how to compete with Diana for Steven's attention.

It was a risky move. If she won, she could live like a queen. If she lost, she'd only lose Steven, but the result would still be what she wanted.

Steven sat on the sofa under the soft light, which cast a lazy and indifferent glow over him. He rested his chin on his hand and asked casually, "Queenie, do you really think it's just a few words? Diana is my wife. I've given you the privileges she should have, what more do you want? Hmm?"

His voice had a seductive lilt, making it impossible to ignore.

Queenie coldly laughed inside. Of course, she wasn't satisfied. Everything Steven had should've been hers, so why should he keep it all for himself?

"Steven, I never wanted to compete with Diana. I just want you back by my side. You were going to marry me, remember?" Queenie lowered her head, tears silently falling.

Steven's gaze darkened. Before he could respond, Evan suddenly burst in.

Evan, slightly out of breath, said, "Mr. Gellar, bad news. Harry's mom brought reporters and is causing trouble for Ms. Bishop across the street."
