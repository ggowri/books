But she didn't ask anything further and continued to soothe the baby.

Steven watched her tender demeanor, a faint smile gracing his lips. There was something charmingly innocent about her, untouched by the complexities of romance.

Just then, Diana's phone buzzed. She glanced at it and saw that the cake she ordered had arrived.

Standing up, she said, "Mr. Gellar, you can go ahead. I have something to take care of."

Hearing her formal address felt strange to him.

When she used his name, it didn't bother him, but hearing her be so distant made him frown.

"Where are you taking the baby?" he asked as he rose.

"I ordered a small cake and I want to pick it up. It'll be here any minute, so you can go do your thing."

The cake could only be delivered to the lobby, not the top floor.

"Wait here. I'll get it for you," Steven offered.

"No, no, no, it's fine. I'll get it myself," Diana insisted, grabbing her bag and hugging Harlan as she headed out.

He felt a surge of frustration. "Diana, why do you insist on opposing me like this?"

As she reached the door, she turned back, giving him a bemused look. "You're really impossible. I'm not bothering you. I just don't want your help."

He scoffed, "But I'm your husband."

She retorted coldly, "You didn't act like my husband when I needed you. Now that I don't need you, you want to play that role again? It's too late for that."

With that, she turned and walked out, leaving him feeling helpless and a bit heartbroken.

Steven thought back to the times he hadn't trusted Diana, and a wave of sadness washed over him.

It hurt to realize that the person he cared for didn't believe in him.

He followed her downstairs in silence, pressing the elevator button for her without a word.

Diana assumed he was going down for work and didn't question it.

She had her period and was craving sweets, so she'd ordered takeout.

Like many girls, she had those days each month when all she wanted was dessert.

But when they reached the first floor, Steven took the delivery bag from the delivery guy without hesitation.

Diana looked at him, puzzled. "Aren't you supposed to be working?"

Steven smiled lightly, "Didn't I just say I finished my work? I just want to spend time with you. It's not easy to carry the baby, so I followed you to get the takeout. Are you getting it now?"

Realization dawned on her, and her cheeks flushed. "Sorry, I misunderstood."

As a chilly breeze swept through the lobby, Diana instinctively turned to head back inside, concerned about the baby getting cold.

While waiting for the elevator, she suddenly spotted a familiar figure and instinctively pulled Steven into a nearby corner.

"What's wrong? Who did you see?" he asked, his voice low.

"Shh! Just be quiet," she whispered urgently, her eyes focused ahead.

At the elevator entrance stood two familiar faces.

They were Amelie and her lover, Mark.
