Diana lightly punched his firm chest, warning him, "Steven, I haven't admitted you're my husband yet. Don't push your luck. Stay away from me."

She finished speaking and put some distance between them.

She didn't hate him anymore, but she wasn't ready to make up with him so soon either.

Diana had hated Steven for so long, suffering alone all that time.

She wanted him to go through the same pain she had endured.

But soon, Diana regretted it. She couldn't bear to see the man she loved suffer with her.

So much time had passed, and the truth had reignited her love for Steven.

Diana thought love was something she just couldn't control.

The misunderstanding between them was resolved. She thought of that melancholic boy from the summer years ago.

That summer, when their eyes met, it felt like time had stopped.

The boy's deep eyes seemed to hold stories, secrets he could never tell.

Steven had touched Diana's heart just like that.

It was later that she risked everything to save him from that fire.

When she woke up in the hospital, Leopold was by her side, but her mind was on Steven.

Diana had hopelessly fallen in love with Steven.

That summer, her love was vivid and burning.

Steven looked at her, lost in thought, and gently asked, "Diana, what's on your mind?"

Diana replied, "I'm thinking about you."

Steven froze for a moment, a look of surprise crossing his face.

She said she was thinking about him!

"Diana..."

Diana stopped his hand mid-reach and said, "Relax. I was just thinking about the day we met. If I hadn't met you back then, maybe things wouldn't be like this now."

Steven smiled. "Diana, do you believe this is God's plan? No matter how much you run, no matter how hard you fight, we were always meant to meet in the end."

Diana glanced at him and fell into deep thought.

After a long pause, she replied softly, "Maybe."

Steven moved closer to Diana, catching the faint, delicate scent on her.

He wouldn't let them become mere passersby in each other's lives. He was determined to be with her forever.

Steven said, "Diana, give me a chance. You used to chase me, now it's my turn to chase you."

Diana huffed, "No. I was too young, too naive back then. I did love you, deeply, but that was in the past."

Diana held back from speaking too harshly, afraid she might regret it later.

Steven just looked at her quietly.

Diana suddenly pointed to the glass of wine nearby and said, "Steven, I've had whiskey, I've had beer, but I can't stop missing you. Do you know what that feels like?"

Steven took her hand. After drinking, her hand felt even softer.

He said, "Diana, I won't leave you again. You've loved me in two lifetimes, so why can't you give me one more chance now?"

Diana shook her head. "But my heart is already dead."

Steven smiled and said gently, "Diana, I'll make you fall in love with me."

Steven's gentle demeanor was undeniably captivating.

Tears clung to Diana's lashes, making her look heartbreakingly vulnerable.

She said, "Steven, I told you, my heart is dead."

Steven held her hand tightly, his fingers softly tracing her palm.

It tickled, and Diana felt a little shy.

She looked at Steven. To him, her lips were like tempting roses.

She did look incredibly alluring right now.

Steven suddenly leaned down and kissed her.

Diana let out a sound of protest, but Steven was hooked. One hand cradled the back of her head as he kissed her fiercely.

After what felt like forever, Steven reluctantly let her go.

Diana gasped for air, glaring at him angrily.

Steven smirked and asked, "Diana, tired?"

Diana thought he might kiss her again, and to her surprise, she even found herself hoping for it.

She seemed a little unwilling to let it end.

Steven suddenly asked her, "Diana, do you like this feeling a lot?"

He instinctively licked his lips, poured more wine with a sly smile, and handed her a glass.

He said, "Diana, have another drink. Let's go to bed."

A lot had happened today, and he was tired too.

Diana took the glass and downed the wine in one gulp.

"This wine is really good." The weather was still cold, and the wine's cool temperature made it taste even better.

After finishing the wine, Diana felt dizzy, her strength slipping away.

Her skin flushed a soft red.

She said, "Steven, is this wine really strong? Why do I feel so dizzy?"

Diana handed the glass back to him. She couldn't drink anymore. She was drunk.

She shook her head hard, but it only made her feel dizzier.

Steven set the glass on the nearby table and helped her lie down.

Diana was drunk and feeling a bit uncomfortable.

She grabbed Steven's hand, looking at his handsome face, and said, "Steven, I really loved you. But why did you have to hurt me like that?"

She slowly closed her eyes, tears streaming down her face.

Maybe Diana was truly drunk. Today, they had cleared everything up. The deep love in her heart surged like a tidal wave.

She wanted to yell at Steven, to tear into him, but she just couldn't.

She couldn't stop herself from saying she loved him, and it made her feel so pathetic.

But during those years taking care of Steven, Diana had done things far more humiliating than this.

Diana, curled up on the bed, looked both pitiful and irresistibly alluring.

Steven couldn't help himself. He scooped her up and held her close against his chest.

Steven looked down at her. The air between them grew even more romantic.

He said, "Diana, I know you're hurting. But from now on, I won't let you cry again."

Steven gently wiped the tears from her face.

Diana's feelings of grievance and emotion swelled, and she couldn't hold back anymore.

She was clearly drunk, too weak to stand, yet she still wanted to curse someone out.

Diana said, "Steven, you jerk."

Steven listened to her soft voice, and it made his heart ache.

She didn't even use a harsh tone to insult him.

He whispered in her ear, "Diana, I am a jerk. You can say whatever you want to me."
