Diana sensed that hearing all this was enough for now.

She watched as Lily trembled with grief, her voice choked with emotion.

Gently, Diana pushed her forward, and they moved away from the two women talking nearby, who were completely unaware of their presence.

Once they reached the underground parking lot and settled into the car, Lily finally let go, crying openly.

Neither Diana nor Luna attempted to comfort her. Sometimes, a good cry was the best way to heal.

Jean had been well-educated, yet she showed no sign of mourning her mother's departure.

Her resentment towards being controlled by Lily had created a cold distance between them.

Diana understood how fragile that bond was.

She recognized that Lily had devoted her life to raising her children, only to see it undermined by Amelie's influence.

The more Lily tried to be strict, the more Jean sought refuge in Amelie's gentleness.

After a few minutes, Lily's sobs quieted, but her throat felt tight, as if a weight was pressing down. She sat silently, allowing the pain to wash over her, her mind replaying the scene from the mall.

As Diana drove them back to the hospital, the heavy burden on Lily's heart began to ease.

Once they reached the ward, she turned to Diana and offered a grateful smile. "Diana, thank you for bringing me here. Now I understand my daughter's true feelings. I have no regrets about how I raised her.

"From now on, I will focus on loving myself."

Diana smiled in return. "Mrs. Foliot, when you learn to love yourself, you've already conquered the world."

Lily regarded her with a tender gaze, knowing they had both faced similar struggles.

"Diana, I see that your mother regrets her actions. Could you consider forgiving her?" She recalled her two encounters with Paula, each filled with remorse.

Diana chuckled softly. "Mrs. Foliot, I've been hurt just like you. Can you forgive your daughter?"

Lily shook her head firmly. "I will never forgive her. She's an adult now and made her own choices. Let her deal with the consequences."

At that moment, Lily realized that Diana would never forgive Paula.

Diana smiled but remained silent, encouraging her to rest and prepare for what lay ahead.

Lily then called her parents, assuring them of her safety, and instructed her father's lawyer to start the divorce process.

She had collected some evidence and was ready to act once Amelie made her move.

After calming Lily, Diana and Luna returned to Steven's villa.

To make things easier for work, Diana arranged for Luna to stay in the small courtyard behind the house, next door to Evan.

When she got back inside, she went to prepare dinner but noticed Steven was still out.

She hadn't told him she was home, and he had no idea.

Changing into comfortable clothes, she headed downstairs to cook.

Just as she entered the kitchen, her phone buzzed with a message from Steven.

[I'll be home for dinner tonight.]

Surprised he knew she was back, Diana continued preparing the meal.

Jean was planning to make her move in the morning, and Diana was curious about how the situation would unfold.
