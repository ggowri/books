"I didn't do it," Queenie insisted, continuing to deny the accusation.

At that moment, Steven approached, and Queenie's hope was rekindled. Steven wouldn't just stand by and let this happen to her.

"Steven, I really didn't do anything wrong. Please help me; I'm scared," she pleaded, looking fragile and on the verge of collapse.

Steven's deep gaze met Cecil's. "Mr. Farley, Queenie didn't do anything wrong. If you let her go, I'll offer you the Skyline Project."

Cecil regarded Steven suspiciously, suspecting he was in cahoots with Queenie for the sake of cooperating with the Farley family.

"Fine. But Steven, Queenie—if it's found that the accident involving Grandma's bodyguard is linked to either of you, I'll make sure you regret it."

Cecil turned to Rita. "I'll take you home to rest."

Rita's sharp gaze fixed on Queenie. "Queenie, I may not have solid evidence, but you were caught on camera throwing something into the pool after you split up with Steven. Next time, you won't be so lucky."

Then she addressed her grandson. "Cecil, Diana is an excellent and kind person. You should spend more time with her. I spent just an hour with her, and I can tell she's genuine and down-to-earth."

Cecil, recalling her skill, felt a twinge of envy and smiled slightly. "Ow... it hurts." Diana really hadn't held back.

Rita looked at him with concern. "Is your hand okay?"

Cecil shook his hand. "I'm fine. It just hurts a bit."

Steven frowned, puzzled by Diana's apparent medical knowledge.

Could she have treated injuries before?

But he dismissed the thought, reminding himself that Helen had found the doctor. Still, her bone-setting skills nagged at him.

Feeling frustrated, Steven turned to Queenie and said, "You should go home and rest. I have something to take care of."

Queenie quickly grabbed his hand, looking at him pitifully. "Steven, aren't you going to stay with me? I'm feeling really down right now."

Without responding, Steven walked away, frustrated. He pondered Rita's words: after entering the banquet hall, Queenie had left briefly but returned quickly.

In his eyes, Queenie was gentle and kind; she wouldn't do such a thing. It must have been Diana badmouthing Queenie to the old lady again.

This thought eased his mind a bit. Diana always seemed to be causing trouble.

 

He recalled Diana's earlier accusation by the elevator—that Queenie had set her up. How could that be? He had witnessed Queenie's kindness firsthand.

Watching Steven's retreating figure, Queenie cried angrily. He had never left her like this before.

Paula supported her, saying, "Let's go, Queenie, let's go home."

Queenie nodded, feeling wronged. "Okay."

The banquet continued, with guests enjoying themselves, and the Farley family overseeing the event.

After getting into the car, Steven asked Evan, "Can you check where Diana is?"

Evan pointed to a restaurant across the street, smiling as he explained, "Ms. Bishop is on a date over there. I saw her when I went to buy dinner earlier. The man she's with looks very handsome. It seems like Ms. Bishop is enjoying their conversation."

Steven opened the car door and headed toward the restaurant.

Inside, elegant and soothing music played.

Diana was happily chatting with the man across from her, Alexander. He was young and successful, having returned to the country after working abroad for years.

She knew he would become one of the top billionaires in the coming years.

In her previous life, she had met him at a banquet upon his return. In this life, thanks to her grandfather's introduction, she met him earlier.

Diana could hardly believe she was on a blind date with Alexander, the tycoon she had admired most in her past life. They were having a great conversation, sharing their experiences in Raventia.

Steven walked in, saw them, and was about to approach but was held back.
