Steven froze for a moment. It hit him—he had never taken Diana to a banquet before.

What had he been thinking back then? He wanted her to stay low-key, to avoid drawing anyone's attention.

As long as Charlie didn't notice her, she would be safe.

Steven smiled, taking her hand gently.

"Back then, I had my reasons. I wanted you to stay under the radar, to keep Charlie from realizing how I felt about you. By treating Queenie well, I kept him in check, stopped him from sabotaging the company.

"I'm sorry, Diana. At that time, I couldn't afford to have feelings for you. With Queenie, it was all an act, but sometimes..."

"Sometimes, though, pretending can feel real. You did have feelings for her, didn't you?"

Diana pulled her hand back, wrapping her arms around her knees.

She thought about it but couldn't let go of the past. Every time it crossed her mind, the pain resurfaced.

"I'm not going. I hate those parties. You go," Diana said stubbornly. She stared blankly at the marble tabletop, her blurred reflection staring back. She was clearly unhappy.

Steven pressed his lips together slightly. Every time the past was mentioned, her mood soured, and she just couldn't let it go.

"Fine! I'm not going either. Let Evan handle it."

He just wanted to stay by her side.

Diana looked at him and smiled. "Steven, why aren't you going? It's a business networking event. You might get plenty of opportunities there. You can't just skip it. Go ahead. If you need a date, find one."

"I'm not in a good mood today. I can't go with you. If I'm unhappy, it won't be good for you either."

Diana gave a faint smile. She was breathtakingly beautiful.

But it was a beauty tinged with sorrow.

Steven's heart clenched painfully. Seeing her bittersweet smile, thinking of all the hardships and injustices she had endured, it tore at him.

Even without growing up in the Marsh family, she was still a proud and stunning heiress. If she wanted, countless men would gladly offer her their hearts.

But in this girl's heart, there had always been only him.

He had let down her unwavering devotion.

"It's fine. Without you by my side, what's the point of making all that money? There's no one to help me spend it."

Diana caught the hint of sulkiness in his words and couldn't help but chuckle.

"What do you mean no one spends your money? I'll do it! And if I can't use it all, we'll donate the rest to an orphanage."

"Every year, I buy clothes and school supplies for kids, just so they can feel loved too."

"No matter how much love they feel, every time I buy something for the kids, it makes me happy."

Steven said, "Alright! Every winter, we'll do charity together. We'll personally shop for everything and deliver it to the orphanage ourselves."

Diana nodded, "Mm-hmm!"

She took a deep breath. The ache in her heart eased significantly.

"I'm sorry, Steven. I never meant to bring you down. But every time the past comes up, I just can't let it go."

She hated this side of herself too.

She wanted to let go of the past and live her life. But every time it resurfaced, she couldn't help dragging others into her emotions.

Steven suddenly pulled her up, placing her on his lap.

Their position was far too intimate, making Diana a little nervous.

Since she got sick, Steven always held her while they slept. She'd grown used to it, though they hadn't taken things any further.

Diana pressed her hands against his shoulders, her eyes full of caution. "What are you doing?"

Steven chuckled, a mix of anger and amusement in his voice. "Diana, if I really wanted to do something to you, do you think you could get away?"

Diana was speechless.

She quietly stared at his handsome face. She really did love that face.

"Don't you dare!" Diana warned him.

Steven smirked and pinched the soft flesh at her waist.

Diana stiffened, her back straight as she glared at him. "You... you stop that!"

Instead of stopping, Steven leaned in and planted a kiss on her cheek.

In a husky voice, he said, "Diana, do you really think I have no desires? Do you know why I've kept my distance? Because once I touch you, I'm afraid I'll lose control. I've been holding back, trying not to hurt you."

"You underestimate your allure. You have no idea how hard it is to hold back when I'm holding you at night—soft, warm, and intoxicatingly sweet. Baby, I'm waiting for your forgiveness. Only when you truly forgive me, when our souls align, can we find real happiness together."

Diana was stunned.

She had always thought it was her lack of charm, assuming Steven had no interest in her that way.

If Steven hadn't admitted today that he desired her, she would've kept doubting herself.

If a man truly likes a woman, his first thought is always desire—everything else comes after.

Diana lowered her head, resting her forehead against his. She was in a great mood.

"Steven, I'm so happy. Tonight, I'll go to the party with you. Let's drive Queenie mad."

Steven was speechless.

He frowned slightly but ultimately said nothing.

Her jealousy was a good thing.

"Alright! Then I'll take you to buy a dress."

Diana stood up and said, "Let's go. I could use some fresh air anyway."

Seeing her mood improve, Steven couldn't help but smile.

Diana went upstairs to change her clothes.

After she came down, Steven took her to a luxury mall to shop for a dress.

Steven's company owned many high-end brands, but today he wanted to show her some international luxury labels.

An hour later, the two arrived at the mall.

Steven held Diana's hand as he led her toward the store he had in mind.

It was a high-end luxury mall—quiet, spacious, and dripping with opulence.

When they reached the boutique, Steven turned to her.

"Diana, the gowns here are really nice. See anything you like?"

Diana recognized the brand immediately. She gave a mysterious little smile.

"Steven, don't you know whose name this store operates under?"

Steven paused for a second, slightly caught off guard.

His tall frame cast a shadow over her petite figure as he smiled.

"Is it part of Azure Group?"

Diana looked proud.

"Nope. It's actually a fashion brand I co-founded with an overseas partner. I do the sketches, and they handle the production in their country. And just like that, a luxury brand was born."

She glanced around with a sparkle in her eyes.

"Most of the clothes here are my designs. But if you want something truly unique, go check out the boutique across the aisle. That designer's a good friend of mine. His work is just as stunning."

Without hesitation, Steven took her hand and pulled her toward the other store.

Diana picked out a champagne-colored gown.

The shade flattered her fair skin, making her look effortlessly radiant.

Just as she was about to say something, a woman's voice suddenly rang out behind them.

"I want the dress she's holding. Wrap it up for me."
