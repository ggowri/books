Steven gazed deeply at Diana's proud expression.

Honestly, she’s so simple and easy to please, yet she stubbornly loves me no matter what.

"I’m so lucky to meet you, you are so perfect."

"Diana, I know you love me. I’ve always remembered your kindness. Diana, I’ve always remembered how good you’ve been to me, but back then, I didn’t have the courage to love you. If I cared about you, I’d only end up hurting you..."

Diana knew exactly what Steven meant, because she was right in the middle of it all. Yet, out of all the ways, he chose the stupidest one.

She thought he was an idiot, but why should she pay the price?

"Steven, you shouldn’t have helped them bully me. You had so many other ways..."

"No. This was the only way to keep you alive," Steven cut Diana off, picking up his phone. He opened a video and handed it to her.

In the video, Queenie was talking to Andrew.

Queenie smirked and said, "Andrew, Steven completely trusts me now. When I threw a tantrum and begged him to go against Diana with me, he didn’t even believe Diana—he only believed me."

Andrew frowned deeply. "No. Steven is too shrewd, and Diana is stunning. He’s bound to have feelings for her. You have to marry Steven."

Queenie's eyes gleamed with malice. "If he falls for Diana, she might just end up dead. I have plenty of ways to get rid of her."

Steven turned off the video. "Diana, only if you leave will they let their guard down, so..."

As for what came next, Steven didn’t need to say it—Diana already understood. That was the meaning behind those dozen pages of the divorce agreement.

Diana quietly stared at Steven's handsome profile. He was a master at acting, fooling her and everyone else.

Her gaze darkened. "Steven, you might be able to let go of the past, but I can't."

Steven gripped her hand tightly. "Diana, if nothing changed and you still wanted to leave, I wouldn’t stop you. But now, it’s different. It’s all set, just need the right time. Once comes, George and Queenie will get exactly what they deserve."

After speaking, Steven smiled—a smile as bright and warm as the spring sun.

Diana found herself momentarily lost in his smile.

Steven softly pleaded, "Diana, please give me one more chance."

Diana stared at him in silence. She had seen how good he was to other women, endured so much hurt—she couldn’t bring herself to agree so easily.

She slowly shook her head. "Not the time to talk about love. As for feelings... let’s just leave it to fate."

Steven's eyes dimmed with disappointment.

Diana changed the subject. "Get up. Come downstairs for breakfast."

Looking into her bright eyes, Steven suddenly said, "Diana, I want to stay here for a while. My injuries haven’t healed yet, and my situation is... complicated. I can’t go to a hospital. If Queenie finds out I’m here, we’ll both be in danger."

That was indeed one of the reasons he came back to her.

Diana recalled what Luna had told her, "He's frail, and he faints when his emotions get too intense." Her heart softened despite herself.

She thought to herself with a bitter smile, "My kindness is my own downfall. I totally deserve this."

"Fine. But you better heal fast and get back to your company. I don’t want to spend all day stuck in an office, looking like some uptight workaholic at my age."

Diana pointed to her face, especially the corners of her eyes. "Look at me! Staring at a computer all day, I’m getting wrinkles here already."

Steven couldn’t help but laugh at her adorable expression. He said gently, "Alright. I’ll stay and help you with the company."

Diana thought his suggestion wasn’t bad. "Great! Start with breakfast. There’s an international meeting later, you can join me. We have to turn things around."

"If you hadn’t dragged me into this, you wouldn’t be part of my plan. I was going to wait for the person behind Queenie to surface, take them all down in one go."

"But then you suddenly got into trouble, so I had to wait for you to deal with it first."

"When you got into trouble, that’s when I learned some of the truth."

Steven knew she’d been through a lot lately. "Diana, I’ll stay and help you. And I’ll get better soon, I promise."

Diana nodded. "Alright. I’ll get you some clothes now. You’ll stay next door, in the same room as before."

Steven's eyes looked a bit disappointed. "Alright."

Diana brought him a set of comfortable loungewear and told him to change before freshening up.

Steven stepped into the walk-in closet to change.

Then, he caught a glimpse of himself in the mirror—thinner, sharper eyes. He no longer looked lifeless, his gaze was filled with anticipation.

Steven smiled faintly. Surrounded by the traces of Diana’s life, the tenderness in his eyes was almost overflowing.

He thought he would live in despair forever, but now, he found hope again.

Steven straightened his shirt and headed downstairs with Diana.

Seeing him come out of the room, Diana said, "Come on. Let’s have breakfast."

Steven nodded softly. "Alright."

Diana sat in her wheelchair, and Steven walked over to push her. His legs ached too, but somehow, being with her made the pain feel lighter.

As they stepped outside, Diana pointed at the wheelchair. "Get on. It’s automatic. If you don’t want crippled, stop acting cool. Sitting in the wheelchair will be more comfortable, and your legs will heal faster."

Steven looked down at her, feeling like she cared about him. He said, "Diana, I’m fine. I can handle it."

Diana turned to him. "Steven, so hard to follow the doctor? Your fracture is displaced, it hurts when you walk. If you keep putting pressure on it, how can you heal? Just stay here. I’ll prepare your meds and give you massages. You’ll recover in no time."

Steven glanced at her legs and asked, "What about you?"

Diana replied, "I’m almost fine. I’m not like you guys, pretending you’re okay when it obviously hurts."

As soon as Steven heard her words, he immediately sat in the wheelchair.

He wanted to recover quickly, deal with the current mess, and make things right with her again.

The two of them went downstairs together, just as Nick walked in through the door. Seeing them both in wheelchairs, Nick froze for a moment and said, "Diana, what’s he doing here?"

Diana smiled and explained, "Nick, this is Steven's house. He’s staying here for now. Just be careful not to slip, okay?"

Nick shot a glance at Steven, then turned to Diana and said, "Diana, check the news online. Dylan’s gone completely insane!"
