"Steven, you've never felt grateful or remorseful toward me. Just like the Marsh family, you looked down on me because I came from the countryside, with nothing to offer. You thought I should be grateful for everything you gave me and never compete with Queenie.

"But what you wanted to give me wasn't necessarily what I wanted. Why should I be grateful for your charity? From the very start, you've treated me with contempt.

"I never needed your pity, nor did I care to compete with Queenie."

Steven's heart felt as if it were being sliced with a dull knife, driving him nearly mad with pain.

After Steven left, Diana asked Luna to drive her to the Gellar Manor.

The Gellar Manor was located in Capitole's most exclusive neighborhood.

She used to visit Helen here frequently on weekends.

Diana bought some pastries Helen liked and went in alone.

The manor was peaceful, with fresh air and an elegant, luxurious design.

"Diana, you're here," Helen said. She wore a white dress, her demeanor still graceful.

Born into a noble family, even at seventy, she maintained an elegant presence.

Diana approached, set the pastries down, and hugged Helen.

"Grandma, I've missed you," Diana said.

Helen gently patted her back and smiled. "Silly girl, if you missed me, why didn't you come sooner?"

Diana looked at the kind face of Helen. She had been beautiful in her youth, and even now, her natural grace remained unmatched.

"Grandma, you know about me and Steven," Diana said with a bitter smile.

Helen took her hand, sat her down, and patted it gently. "Diana, Steven doesn't understand love. I know you've suffered. Give him time. He'll figure things out. Queenie's manipulative ways won't hold up, and eventually, he'll see it."

Diana shook her head. "Grandma, you were young once too. When you love someone, you don't count the costs. Love comes with blinders; it makes you overlook their flaws.

"Steven and Queenie were childhood friends; they share many good memories. I know you care for me, Grandma, and you won't force me to stay."

Diana knew Helen well enough to speak her mind.

Helen patted her hand with a look of regret. "It's my fault for insisting. It's that boy who's unlucky."

Diana smiled. "Grandma, meeting Steven was like a burst of color in my dull life. I'm actually grateful to him."

She was grateful for the push that gave her a chance to start over.

Helen understood her suffering. "The Marsh family is full of ambitious wolves who can't distinguish right from wrong. You've endured a lot over the years, Diana.

"I know you've made up your mind. I asked you here today to thank you for saving Steven's life back then. Without you, he wouldn't be here."

Diana replied with a smile, "Grandma, everyone has their fate. It was meant to be that I crossed paths with him."

"But I'm still grateful to you, Diana. You were with him during his darkest days. He's grown up now, but if he can't appreciate you, there's nothing more I can do. I just hope your life is peaceful and fulfilling from now on."

Having lived through it, Helen knew how easy it was to fall and how hard self-control and success were to achieve.

Back then, her grandson had already begun to spiral into self-destruction. Such a mindset could be terrifying.

In a single month, a person could change completely and be driven into hell.

Diana nodded with a smile. "Grandma, I loved Steven for four years, but he never loved me back. There's no fate between us. Loving him once dragged me into the dirt, but now I want to rise high. I won't betray myself anymore or waste my life.

"From now on, I'll smile happily in a future without Steven."

Helen smiled brightly at this. "Diana, you're destined for greatness. No matter how much you love someone, never lower yourself to the dust for them. I told you this before, and now you've finally realized it."
