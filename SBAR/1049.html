Alexander looked at Luna, "Luna, let me do it."

Luna smiled and let Alexander push Diana into the office.

Luna secretly hoped that the gentle Alexander would end up with Diana.

She poured coffee for them and left the office.

Diana braced herself on the wheelchair and moved to the sofa, settling in.

Alexander watched her determined demeanor, feeling a bit helpless. He didn’t even have a chance to help her.

Alexander handed her a glass of water.

Diana had talked a lot in the meeting room earlier. If it weren’t for Dalton, she would’ve been truly exhausted. She drank half of it.

Two hours of meetings finally over.

Hate spending the whole day stuck at the office.

She hated days like this, which was why she had handed the company over to Nolan.

But now, Diana was working even harder than Nolan every single day.

Alexander sat down, "Diana, talk about the medical assistance system. I want these products on the market in six months. I can handle this without involving Mr. Turner."

Diana looked a bit surprised. "Alexander, these products were supposed to launch next month. But now we have a better option, so we decided on three months. Why delay it to six?"

Alexander said, "Diana, this is a letter Sir Bishop asked me to bring to you. Open it and take a look."

Diana was puzzled, unsure why Leopold had given Alexander a letter.

Leopold must really trust Alexander!

Diana pulled out a piece of white stationery from the envelope.

The letter read: [Diana, I know the medical assistance system is your passion, but you're drawing too much attention right now. Wait a little longer, launch these products in six months. Trust me.]

Diana looked at Alexander and asked, "Alexander, when you saw my grandfather, did he say anything else to you?"

Alexander replied, "He said something might happen soon, and since you're the CEO, a lot of people are watching you. He suggested launching the new products in six months."

"And you're doing great in the fashion industry. Sir Bishop meant he hopes you'll focus on that first."

"I'll go with you to talk to Mr. Turner about this. The other shareholders will benefit a lot from the fashion industry, they won't oppose delaying the system launch."

"Diana, your designs already caused a sensation. If the medical system launches now, the Gellar Group might come under attack. I know you care about the patients, but we need to think about more."

Diana realized that Leopold must have sensed danger. "Alexander, I understand what you mean now."

Alexander smiled as he opened the cake he brought, speaking softly, "Diana, try this cake."

Diana looked at the chocolate cake he handed over and smiled brightly.

"Thank you." Diana took a bite of the cake. It was delicious.

Maybe it was the sweetness of the cake, but her mood lifted instantly.

This, however, put Steven in a foul mood. Diana rarely smiled that brightly in front of him.

Evan stood nearby, watching the video on Steven's phone. Diana and Alexander looked perfect together.

Seeing the anger and jealousy on Steven's face, Evan felt no sympathy for him.

Steven had no right to be angry. He let Diana go first.

Steven muttered, "I pushed Diana away. Do I even have the right to be angry? Or jealous?"

Evan said, "Mr. Gellar, you're absolutely right. This sudden affection? I don't get it."

Steven snapped, "Evan, how dare you talk to me like that."

Back then, he simply didn’t know how to love Diana.

He was accustomed to loneliness, locking away his kind heart and keeping everyone at a distance.

To others, Steven was arrogant, dismissive, and ruthlessly cold-hearted.

He was numb. For any feeling, except for his grandma and grandpa.

Other than completing the tasks Edward assigned and keeping himself alive, Steven felt everything else was utterly meaningless.

It wasn’t until Steven’s car accident, when he lost his sight and heard Diana’s joyful voice, that he finally felt alive.

He handed her over a dozen divorce papers because Queenie was back, and Diana's life was in danger.

It was Steven who made Diana leave. But when she truly left his world, he regretted it.

Evan boldly said, "Mr. Gellar, if you still love Mrs. Gellar, take out Queenie and George already. Let’s get back. Otherwise, Luna’s gonna be gone too."

Evan was desperate, determined to keep Luna from leaving.

Steven looked at his anxious expression and joked, "Relax, Luna’s waiting for you. Aren’t you secretly texting her every day and even sneaking around asking about her lingerie size?"

Evan stared at Steven in shock, completely caught off guard that Steven had noticed his actions.

Evan said, "Mr. Gellar, that’s too much. How could you secretly keep tabs on my life?"

Steven sighed, "It’s just we living here. Sometimes you get so excited, I can see your phone screen without even trying."

Evan was speechless.

Steven asked, "Does buying lingerie for a woman have some special meaning?"

Evan shook his head, "I never thought that. Just want to buy all kinds of clothes for my girl. I love her."

Steven froze in surprise.

Evan was surprised too.

Steven, with all his schemes and arrogance on the surface, was actually caught off guard by those words.

Steven asked again, "Is this what truly loving someone looks like?"

He wanted to buy Diana beautiful clothes too, wanted to give her all the best things in the world.

Evan nodded, then shook his head. "Mr. Gellar, honestly, I don’t really know what love is either. But I know, I love Luna. I’ll make her the happiest woman. After working for you all these years, I have the money and the means to do it."

Steven sat on the couch, watching Diana happily finish her second slice of cake.

It hit him then—he didn’t really know how to love her.
