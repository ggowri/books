Paula was overwhelmed, looking at Marie. "Marie, thank you! Thank you so much!"

While Gale watched from outside, Althea observed from upstairs. She knew her daughter still had feelings for Gale.

When he was younger, Gale was truly a great guy. But over time, he changed.

Everyone gathered in the living room.

No one said a word.

Diana broke the silence, "Marie, maybe we should head back for now? We can come back another day to celebrate."

She simply couldn't stand it any longer.

She knew she could never forgive Gale.

Marie said, "Diana, they're your mom and older brother. You can stay and hear them out too."

Diana shook her head, refusing. Just seeing them unsettled her. "Their family drama has nothing to do with me."

Althea could forgive anyone, but Diana? She could never forgive Gale.

Gale once hired thugs to ruin her life.

She had a biological brother and mother, but she didn't want them anymore.

Gale lowered his head in shame, too cowardly to ask for Diana's forgiveness.

Eric kept his head down, silent. For Queenie, he had once tormented Diana mercilessly, doing every cruel thing imaginable.

Only Paula felt a pain so deep it stole her breath away.

Althea hesitated, her voice trembling, "Diana, I..."

Diana cut her off with a smile. "Althea, as long as you're happy, don't worry about anyone else's opinion, least of all mine. Whatever you decide, we'll always be best friends."

Althea felt a pang of sadness too. Suddenly, she no longer wanted to forgive Gale.

But Gale before he turned twenty... he really wasn't that bad.

Back then, Gale was so warm and kind.

If she fell, he'd panic and carry her on his back all the way to the hospital.

If it rained and she didn't have an umbrella, he'd take off his jacket to shield her from the rain.

Back then, Gale truly liked her.

Paige also said, "Althea, you two talk. Let's catch up tomorrow."

Eric clung to his last shred of hope. "Paige, Frank is still alive."

Paige chuckled, "I know. But it has nothing to do with me. When I loved him, he wouldn't even spare me a glance. He never respected me."

"Now I've met a man who truly loves me. I've realized love isn't supposed to be like that."

"When I was willing to give him everything, all he gave me in return were harsh, hurtful words."

She is not Althea.

Althea and Gale are neighbors.

Althea had loved Gale her entire life.

Steven drove Diana out for dinner.

Truthfully, she had no intention of forgiving Steven.

She wanted to leave this place alone and travel the world.

But she couldn't let go of Leopold.

"Steven, sometimes I just want to leave everything behind, move to a new city. Meet new friends, find a new love."

Steven's grip on the steering wheel tightened slightly. She clearly didn't want to forgive him. How could he not feel it?

He stayed silent, driving quietly.

Diana closed her eyes, frustration bubbling inside her.

Forty minutes later, Steven pulled up in front of the restaurant.

As he parked the car, Diana opened her eyes.

Steven took her hand and asked, "Are you still feeling unwell?"

Diana let out a bitter laugh. "Unwell? That was my birth mother, my own brother. They used the cruelest words imaginable to tear me down."

No matter what he was thinking back then, the damage he caused her was already done.

Steven felt a pang in his chest as he watched her force a smile. "Alright!"

If he had known about George earlier, he would never have let her endure so much pain.

Everything was his fault. If only he had chosen to live back then, their child would probably be walking by now.

Seeing Althea's son today, he deeply regretted everything he had done before.

When the child called Gale "Daddy," his emotions were... complicated.

Diana playfully winked at him. "Steven, I'm not upset anymore. My moods come and go quickly. I apologize for what I said earlier!"

Diana grinned, "How about you treat me to a fancy dinner tonight?"

Steven pointed to the restaurant in front of them. "They make amazing crab dishes here."

Diana frowned, looking troubled. "But peeling shrimp is such a hassle."

Steven smiled gently. "I'll peel them for you. Let's go."

Diana suddenly asked, "Steven, have you ever peeled shrimp for Queenie?"

Diana instantly regretted it. This self-inflicted torment only served to remind her of the pain she had endured in the past.

Steven shook his head. "Never."

Everyone thought he treated Queenie well, but that wasn't the truth.

Diana asked again, "Have you ever carried her on your back?"

Steven shook his head. "No."

Diana pressed further, "Have you ever kissed her?"

Steven chuckled and shook his head. "Never. I can't stand her. The closest we've been is standing side by side. Sometimes she throws herself into my arms. Diana, how could I ever do anything more intimate with her?"

Only then did Diana feel at ease. She cared about this. Queenie was utterly repulsive.

"Alright!" Diana's smile was radiant. "Come on, let's go have dinner."

Diana hooked her arm around Steven's. As she glanced up, she saw Queenie standing across from her, her face dark and stormy.
