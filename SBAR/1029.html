She died once and realized that love was not as important as herself. Her own well-being was the most important thing.

He missed her madly. It felt so comfortable to sleep with her in his arms. Without her, he couldn't sleep well.

Diana recognized him at first sight, which made him very happy. Luna and Cecil didn't recognize him.

She loved him. And he knew he could always count on her.

Diana knew it was him at first sight.

That night, he kissed her forcefully, so he didn't pay attention to the car on the opposite side.

Her bright eyes and beautiful smile resembled the stars in the sky, luminous and stunning.

This was her. Her smile was pure and clean, and she faced everything with composure. She no longer cared about anyone.

Always.

This was the least she could do for him since she once loved him.

She was such a great girl, yet he still lost her.

She had let go of the past. She would not immerse herself in the pain of the past, but it did not mean that she would forget them.

Steven was almost driven crazy by her eagerness to draw a line between them.

He suddenly pulled Diana's hand and said in a low and domineering voice, "Diana, can you give me another chance to love you?"
