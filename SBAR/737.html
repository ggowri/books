"Let's make it three days from now!" Zion suggested after a moment's thought. "I'll send someone to pick you up to the company."

Queenie could barely contain her excitement. "Perfect!"

"Come on, I'll take you home!" Zion offered.

Queenie had been waiting for precisely these words. With Zion personally escorting her, no one could doubt her innocence now!

Diana watched from a distance as Queenie got into Zion's car, her smile growing wider.

"Go ahead, sweetheart. Hell's waiting for you."

Diana was practically gloating.

"How do you know she's the one headed for hell?"

The soft voice came from behind her, quickly swallowed by the cold wind.

Diana spun around to face Steven. He stood there in his black coat, his handsome features looking even more striking than usual.

His voice had been slightly husky, seeming to linger in her ears.

Diana's heart fluttered as she quickly stepped back. "What... what are you doing here? Shouldn't you be off comforting your lover?"

"Why does this man keep popping up around me out of nowhere?" she thought.

"Diana, she's not my lover! You are!" Steven's gaze turned cold.

"Oh please, you're all cut from the same cloth. Just stay out of my way, and I'll stay out of yours."

In this life, she'd decided to mind her own business, only speaking up occasionally. And she was living well because of it!

Respecting the fate of others could bring good fortune to oneself.

Diana walked toward her car without another word.

Steven watched her retreating figure with helpless frustration.

In an instant, news broke online about Queenie being signed by a top-tier agent.

Steven's profile had been caught on camera, too.

This only fueled Queenie's rising popularity.

Back at the company, Diana saw Queenie trending on every major search engine.

And she couldn't help but laugh - laugh with pure joy.

"Haha..." She could barely contain her excitement, feeling like setting off firecrackers in celebration.

Luna stood in the doorway, giving her a skeptical look. "Diana, she's getting more famous by the day, and you're still laughing?"

Diana looked at the young girl's frustrated face and explained with a smile, "Luna, why so impatient? The real show hasn't even begun. The higher she climbs now, the harder she'll fall later."

"I just don't get you. If it were me, I'd have had her thrown in jail by now."

"Throwing her in prison would be too easy on her."

Diana raised an eyebrow and shot Luna a knowing look. "No, darling, prison would be far too merciful. Sure, she's riding high right now! That's just her luck. But what's coming for her will be so much worse.

"She needs to taste every bit of suffering I went through. That's only fair."

Diana thought, "Lock her up? Three meals a day served to her, making her life nice and easy?

No, out here, I can make her jump out of her skin every day. What's coming for her will make her break down a little more each day."

Luna shook her head slightly, baffled by this twisted logic.

"Diana, would you prefer coffee or hot milk?"

"Hot milk, please."

As she sat in her swivel chair, an unknown number flashed on her phone.

"Hello!"

"Hello, Ms. Bishop. We're calling from the Fashion Association. The design competition you registered for begins in two weeks. Please ensure you're prepared."

Diana froze. She knew nothing about it! "What? I never signed up for any competition!"
