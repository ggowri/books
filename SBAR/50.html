Diana heard her phone ring. She sniffled, wiped her tears, and answered with a smile.

"Tobias," she said, her voice tinged with emotion.

Tobias's voice was filled with concern. "What happened? Who hurt you? Why are you crying?"

In that moment, Diana just wanted to be comforted by her brother.

"I'm just feeling down," she admitted. "How did my life become such a mess? I have a heartless family and fell for a terrible man. I want to leave this place. The air feels so tainted, especially seeing Steven and Queenie together. It’s suffocating.

"He’s not worthy of being called human. They need to face the consequences of their lies. Remember when Steven was injured, and Queenie ran off with someone else abroad? I spent a fortune and a year of my life helping him heal. And how did he repay me? With betrayal and lies. Tobias, how did I let him into my life?"

Tobias asked gently, "Diana, where are you right now?"

Diana's tears had stopped, and she managed a smile. "I just needed to vent. I'm okay now. I have you all," she said, feeling a bit of relief.

"Silly girl," Tobias replied warmly. "We’re your family. No matter what happens, always tell us. Don’t waste your tears on those people. Believe in yourself; you’ve always been amazing. You’ll shine for yourself and for others. Stop crying, okay? I’ll be back tonight. Tell me what you want for dinner."

Diana felt warmth spread through her heart. She thought for a moment. "Bring me a roasted chicken, and get some shrimp for Olivia. She’s staying with us for a while."

Hearing her calmer voice, Tobias sighed in relief. "Alright! I’ll get whatever you want. Rest up and take care. I’ll be home around 6 PM. Don’t let Steven upset you; he’s not worth it."

"Okay! He’s not worth my tears. I understand," Diana chuckled and hung up.

Talking to Tobias had lifted her spirits. She used a tissue to wipe her tears and clean her sticky eyelashes. After a deep breath, she felt much more at ease.

She stood up and headed downstairs, hoping by the time she reached the bottom, her face would show no signs of tears so Olivia wouldn’t worry.

She didn’t notice Steven standing on the staircase, stunned by what he had overheard.

It was true—Diana had saved him back then.
