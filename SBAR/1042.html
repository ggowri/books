Dylan banged on the door hard.

Eric hadn’t slept, worrying about Dylan all night.

The sudden loud knock made him irritated.

Who could it be?

Eric's face darkened.

He got up slowly, slipped on his slippers, and turned on the light.

The pounding continued.

He quickened his pace, walked over, and opened the door. Outside was the security door. Through it, he saw Dylan.

He growled, "It's the middle of the night. What are you fucking doing? Gonna wake the neighbors? Have you forgotten your manners?"

Eric felt a surge of anger.

Seeing him, Dylan thought about what Queenie had said—Eric was the heir, while they could only get dividends.

Dylan would never accept this arrangement.

There was no way he’d let Eric control the entire company.

Dylan rattled the security door a few times, his eyes dark and cold as he stared at Eric. "Eric, Paula’s loaded, but living in a place like this? Acting a show?"

Hearing that, Eric frowned slightly. "Dylan, what’s that supposed to mean?"

Dylan let out a cold laugh, his eyes fixed on Eric. "Eric, why don’t you open the door first? Let me in, and then we’ll talk."

I’m going to settle this. Paula’s fortune belongs to me too.

Eric said, "You can come in..."

"Eric," Dylan suddenly shouted, "We haven’t divided the family yet. This is our home, not just yours. What are you trying to threaten me with?"

"Dylan, what have you become?" Eric was shocked and furious. He couldn’t help but snap at Dylan, glaring at him intensely.

They hadn’t seen each other in over a year, and Dylan had grown increasingly irritable.

Eric didn’t say anything else and opened the door.

Noticing Dylan’s injured finger, Eric froze for a moment. He asked, "What happened to your finger? How did you get hurt?"

The moment it was mentioned, Dylan got furious.

He sneered, "Simple. Diana's boy toy’s doing."

Eric had been so angry earlier that he’d completely forgotten about what happened tonight.

"He’s Diana’s bodyguard, not a boy toy. Don’t you feel ashamed saying that? Aren’t you a man too?"

Dylan let out a cold snort and fell silent.

He and Eric walked back to the living room.

Hearing their voices, Paula got up as well.

She was wearing pink pajamas with a shawl draped over her shoulders. She looked exhausted. Ever since the trouble at home, her sleep had been restless.

Even though the rooms were small, she felt oddly at ease.

Seeing Dylan, she frowned, "It’s one thing to come back in the middle of the night, but why did you bang on the door so loud?"

"If you wake the neighbors, they’ll definitely think you’re rude."

Dylan walked over, and snapped back, "This is all your fault. You have money, but you don’t live in a big house. You chose to live here."

"If we lived in a standalone villa, we could do whatever we wanted without worrying about bothering the neighbors. You made life hard for yourself."

Dylan's arrogant attitude made Paula realize that Queenie had been driving a wedge between him and their relationship.

Paula looked at him coldly and asked, "I have money? Then where is it?"

"Stop pretending. Queenie told me everything. Everything you did was to make sure Dad didn’t take a single penny. Now he had a stroke and stuck in bed, no way could he take anything. Plus, you’re already divorced. Disgraceful for your behavior."

"He loved you for over twenty years. So what if he had an affair? These days, every man has a mistress."

"Queenie has inheritance rights too. What gives you the right to take all the assets?"

Paula was furious at Dylan's words.

She stared at him in disbelief.

How did he grow up like this?

She yelled, "Dylan, if Queenie said that, then let her come talk to me herself.

"Let me tell you why. I supported Jeff. When we got married, he had nothing. He lived a life of luxury for over twenty years, but it wasn’t enough for him. He even tried to kill me."

"You think I shouldn’t fight back and just die, is that it?"

Paula stared coldly at Dylan. His personality was the most like Jeff's. Just as ruthless and cruel.

He shot back quickly, "Paula, it’s been decades. There’s nothing that can’t be forgiven. Look how happy we were as a family before."

"Even if he had a mistress, you could’ve just pretended not to know."

"So, when you get married someday, if your wife has another man, you won’t care either?" Eric asked.

Dylan said irritably, "That’s different. Women should be loyal to their husbands."

Eric sneered, "Wow, double standards much? You can’t even hold yourself to that, so what gives you the right to judge?"

Dylan's face darkened, "Women and men are different. If a man has a mistress, it’s a status symbol. I mean, only wealthy men can afford mistresses. The kind who can buy them limited-edition bags and luxury cars—that’s what makes a man impressive."

"If a man can buy his mistress a mansion, that’s the real mark of his status."

Eric was speechless. He was furious.

Dylan was just like Jeff, maybe even worse.

Paula closed her eyes in anger. She blamed herself for this—she hadn’t raised Dylan right.

Her voice was trembling. "Dylan, if you like Queenie so much, go live with her. Didn’t you say you wanted to split? We don’t have much, just this house. But it’s under my name. But guess what? I’m not dead yet. You can split it after I’m gone."

Dylan was furious. Paula really is just like Queenie said—unwilling to split the inheritance with me.

Frank was missing, and Gale was crippled. Now, only Eric could handle everything.

Paula has decided to give the company to Eric.

And me? I’ve got nothing.

This can't be happening. I'm her son too. She has to give me what's mine.

We are destined to compete one day.

Dylan stood up, his eyes cold as he looked at Paula. "You really be this heartless to me?"

She knew his purpose wasn’t simple.

Frowning, she asked, "How have I been heartless to you? Not enough basic necessities?"
