After Steven made his point clear, he stood up and walked away.

His cold demeanor left Queenie utterly stunned.

This was not the attitude she expected from him.

Queenie shot up from her seat, her eyes narrowing as she called after his retreating figure. "Steven, you shouldn't treat me like this. I saved your life!"

Steven turned around, his gaze icy as he stared at her. He recalled that he had just mentioned this very thing a mere ten minutes ago.

He replied, his voice steady and unyielding, "Queenie, I've been more than generous with you. I've given you five million dollars every month. Do the math—how much have I given you over the years? Don't push your luck."

With that, Steven turned away, not sparing her another glance.

He refused to indulge Diana's grievances by allowing Queenie to thrive.

Steven planned to rattle Queenie's secret backers, showing them just how ineffectual she was, and goad them into coming out into the open to engage him.

Queenie, seething with indignation, rushed after him, only to be intercepted by Evan.

He flashed a polite smile and said, "Ms. Marsh, Mr. Gellar has an important meeting. Please, stay here."

"Evan, you have no right to stop me! Move aside!" Queenie spat, her anger boiling over.

She thought to herself, "How dare Steven reject me?

Even if I don't love him, he should still be willing to give everything for me!

If he keeps this up, I'll just go after his biggest rival.

I've been waiting for the right moment to hypnotize him. But he doesn't dine with me anymore.

Just because I've been pregnant with another man's child doesn't mean I'm not desirable!

In this day and age, who really waits for someone?

It's the twenty-first century, and Steven still clings to those outdated values.

No wonder he turned out like this, raised by that old fossil."

"Evan, you go tell Steven that I'm furious about his attitude today. If he doesn't apologize, I will never forgive him."

She had used this tactic before, and it always worked.

After dealing with whatever kept him busy, he would come running to her.

This way, she could continue to torment Diana, who always looked so heartbroken and frustrated.

It brought her immense satisfaction.

Evan couldn't help but feel speechless, thinking, "What kind of person has the audacity to say such things?

She used to be able to talk like this because Mr. Gellar spoiled her, but now that he doesn't love her anymore, she still dares to say this?

She's got some nerve!

And after her recent miscarriage, doesn't she ever feel exhausted?"

But to avoid further entanglement with Queenie, he reluctantly agreed.

"Ms. Marsh, I'll pass your message along to Mr. Gellar."

Evan hurried off.

Tears of frustration welled in Queenie's eyes.

"Steven, if you don't love me, I swear I'll ruin you."

She watched as the doors of Steven's private elevator closed before she finally left.

Evan stepped into the elevator and relayed her message to Steven. "Mr. Gellar, Ms. Marsh said that if you don't apologize, she won't forgive you for today's incident."

Steven frowned, his tone icy. "Is she out of her mind?"

Evan shook his head, bewildered. "How would I know if she's lost it?"

Steven fell silent as the elevator reached the top floor.

He strode out with purpose. Once in his office, he commanded, "Keep a close watch on Queenie's every move. She's bound to make a lot of moves soon. Her phone doesn't hold any valuable clues, but she must have another one. Felicity is our best lead right now."
