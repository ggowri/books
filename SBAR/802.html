"What's going on with you?" Queenie said, a look of disbelief on her face as she regarded Amelie. "Can you please keep it down a bit? If this gets out, it'll be so embarrassing for me! My movie is about to hit the theaters, and I just signed with Excellence Entertainment. You and Dad can't mess this up for me. You can't divorce now, at least not yet!

"Just wait a bit. If you rush to be Mark's wife, people are going to blame you for his wife's death. Lily's parents are still around, and they come from a respectable family. If they get even a whiff of something between you two, it'll be a nightmare for both of you.

"Mom, have you lost your mind? You're not thinking straight!" Queenie was already on edge, her anger flashing in her eyes.

Amelie felt the weight of those words. The thought of a divorce made her uneasy.

If she went through with it, she would be left with nothing.

After all, Mark might not even value her once he had her.

She had nothing to her name, and even if Lily had some skills and a strong family, she still ended up dead.

Amelie wasn't naive. She wanted to marry Mark, but she also knew how reckless and arrogant he was. He wouldn't appreciate her after he got her. He had plenty of other women in his life, many of whom were younger and prettier.

"Queenie, I see what you're getting at. I won't be divorcing your father. As for Mark, I know exactly who he is. Once I'm him, the novelty will wear off. The more elusive I am, the more he'll crave me. If I keep him on his toes, it'll work to my advantage. I slept with him today and secured us a business deal. Tomorrow afternoon, I'll take you to meet our partners."

At the mention of profit, Queenie's face lit up.

"Mom, you really are the best!" she said, her eyes sparkling with mischief

As long as her mother stood by her, she would always be good to her.

"Thanks, Mom! If everything goes well tomorrow, I'll get you a designer bag you love," she promised.

Amelie yearned for a life of luxury. Designer clothes and high-end handbags were her greatest dreams.

She laughed and patted Queenie's hand, feeling a sense of relief. "It's settled then. I'll text you at four tomorrow. You meet the partners at the agreed spot. For now, I'll head home and charm your dad. I need to smooth things over first."

"Sounds good! Mom, Dad has put up with so much for you. When you get home, pick up some of his favorite food, and maybe grab a couple of nice outfits. He'll forgive you, especially since he has his flings."

That last comment resonated with Amelie, giving her a newfound confidence.

In her eyes, Jeff was simply a man who cared too much about money.

"Don't worry. Men who are all about money are the easiest to handle. It's the emotional ones that are tricky; once they fall in love, they become impossible to sway," she remarked, walking out.

As Queenie sank into the sofa, she felt a wave of nostalgia wash over her. Suddenly, she found herself thinking of Paula, missing her unexpectedly.

When she was ill, Paula would skip work to take care of her, cooking and buying her the best clothes and shoes every season.

But now, her mother seemed completely absorbed in men, hardly paying attention to her.

Queenie sighed, a bitter smile tugging at her lips. Was this what she deserved for abandoning Paula?
