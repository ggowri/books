And yet, at his core, he was still the same selfish bastard.

Queenie's jaw tensed. She had expected Diana to be rattled, even just a little. But instead, all she got was detached indifference.

Across the room, guests were watching, but no one dared to step closer.

And partly for her parents, too.

Stingy bastard.

Good thing she didn't care anymore.

It wasn't that she was slow. But Diana acting smug while rolling around in a wheelchair? That was rich.

That idiot really had no sense of self-preservation.

Terror flickered in her wide eyes as she looked at Cecil, frozen in place.

Diana clenched her jaw. Perfect. Just perfect.

She had just joked about not becoming the one getting laughed at tonight. And now? Here they were.

This was bad. This wasn't just some behind-the-scenes scandal. This was public.

"Well, well, Mr. Farley, what exactly are you trying to do here?" Yanson sneered, his voice dripping with sarcasm. "Thinking of making a move on a girl in front of an entire crowd? Hate to break it to you, but that's not happening—not while we're all standing here."

Cecil really was a piece of work—going after a woman in public like this?

Cecil's mind was a mess. His skin felt like it was on fire, heat crawling under his flesh.

Then, through the haze, Cecil's eyes locked onto the girl. Something inside him snapped. He raised a shaky hand and pointed straight at her, his voice rough with frustration. "You. Get over here. Now."
