Emma sneered, "No need, my deal with you is over. I hope you can keep your promise."

She went upstairs to pack up her belongings. She couldn't stay here any longer.

She joined the company in a high-profile manner but left with embarrassment.

Queenie stopped her and said, "Emma, I told you I could arrange a position for you. You can continue to be a designer."

"No need." Emma smiled bitterly, "Queenie, I indeed have a crush on Steven, but giving up my dreams and future for him will only destroy my life. The deal is over. I'm leaving now."

She stepped back indifferently and turned into the elevator.

Queenie gazed at her back with a frown.

She mocked, "Emma, except for me and my company, no one wants you now."

She was waiting for Emma to beg her.

Her real purpose in this move was to make Diana utterly disappointed in the Marsh family and deeply resentful of Gale.

It worked.

However, Queenie was displeased that Diana got close to Steven again.

She headed for the coffee shop across the street, where they were sitting face to face.​

Diana's resistance to Steven was evident.

He stared at her. Noticing that she was not as angry as before, he said in a deep voice, "Diana, I had no idea that the design drawings were yours. A few days ago, Emma showed them to me. I was very satisfied, so I prepared to launch the new products.

"It was my negligence. All the profits will be yours."

"No. I have nothing to do with you. You don't have to give me all the profits. Just give me as much as I deserve. I won't ask for even a penny more. After all, you once said that I had no right to use your money, and I never used it..."

"Diana, I said those in anger..."

Steven couldn't continue speaking because he had really made those statements.

"I'm sorry. I didn't understand much about love at that time. Queenie saved my life and grew up with me. I trusted and doted on her completely, and she was always gentle and kind in front of me.

"That's why I said those words."

Steven's voice was low, and his regret was obvious.

Diana's heart couldn't help but ache.

She couldn't describe how she felt at that moment.

How could their relationship of just a few years compare to their friendship since childhood?

"Steven, when did Queenie save you?"

Last time, Diana wanted to ask this question.

But Queenie chipped in.

Steven replied, "It was..."

"Steven, I brought you breakfast. Eat some now," Queenie suddenly interrupted him loudly.

Diana gazed at her thoughtfully.

Could it be that Queenie took the credit for saving Steven from the fire?

That day, she was also nearby.

The fire came out of nowhere.

Queenie opened the lunchbox and poured out the pumpkin soup.

Diana knew at a glance that it was not tasty. Steven did not enjoy the pumpkin soup like this, and it was not a pleasant color.​

He fixed his cold eyes on Queenie. "Queenie, can't you see that I am discussing cooperation?"

She smiled mischievously, "Steven, Diana and we are all family..."

"Don't flatter yourself. We are not family. Don't disgust me."

Queenie was embarrassed.

At the sight of Evan, Diana took the contract from him and scanned through it. After confirming that there was nothing wrong with it, she signed her name and left.

Queenie stared at her back and chuckled, "Steven, Diana is becoming more and more elegant. Your father has been urging you to get a divorce. Steven, when are you going to complete the procedures?"
