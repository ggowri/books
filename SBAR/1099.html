Steven gazed at her delicate profile and shared his feelings, "Diana, it feels like I'm holding the whole world in my arms. I'm so incredibly happy!"

This was his truest feeling.

His overwhelming desire to be with Diana made it hard for him to hold himself back.

Diana froze, unmoving, her eyes brimming with tears.

Her soft hand gently brushed against the back of his hand.

The involuntary motion surprised even herself.

It was an unconscious gesture from Diana.

Steven felt her soft touch and smiled.

Standing by the window, Diana could clearly see the tenderness in his eyes and his smile.

At this moment, every emotion Steven showed was genuine.

Diana's heart was racing.

She couldn't imagine this scene happening to them, or that expression on Steven's face.

Before knowing the whole truth, Diana had always felt they were never meant to be together.

She couldn't imagine such a tender moment would ever happen to them.

Tears slipped from Diana's eyes before she even realized it.

She didn't know what was wrong with her, but she felt like crying again.

Steven watched her tears fall and panicked. "Diana, did I hurt you? Why are you crying?"

Diana shook her head. "I don't know. I just feel like crying."

A lot had happened recently. The truth had left her completely shaken.

Honestly, Diana still couldn't figure out her own thoughts.

Ever since she found out Steven wasn't the one who caused her death in her past life, her heart had been wavering.

He would stay by Diana's side, using his love to help her forget the pain of the past.

He said, "Diana, it probably won't rain. Let me take you out for a walk. The scenery on the island is amazing. Let's go pick some coconuts. I remember your coconut seafood dish was incredible."

She said, "Let's go. I don't want to stay home all day either."

Staying home only made her overthink.

Steven said, "Alright!"

Steven stopped and said, "Diana, wait here. I'll go back and grab a coat for you."

It was easy for them to buy seafood.

The sea breeze drifted in, carrying a faint chill.

Diana faced the sea breeze, her hair tousled by the wind, brushing against Steven's face. He caught a faint, fresh scent.

Steven stayed close to her. He loved the subtle fragrance she carried—it always helped him sleep peacefully at night.

He said, "Diana, let's go over there. There's a white sand beach. It's beautiful."

Diana glanced at the white sand beach in the distance and said, "Sure! The island's all natural beauty. Must cost a lot to maintain every year, right?"

After his eyesight got better, he started disappearing for months at a time.

Steven shook his head. "I would never bring her to the island. Back then, I just wanted Charlie to think I really adored Queenie."

"I was planning to wait until I found my mom before making a move."

She should have asked Steven back then.

Those photos haunted her, constantly gnawing at her.

Especially after Diana got pregnant and Steven kept her confined in the villa, she truly felt like she was suffocating in there.

She often had those impulses back then.

She felt like the dumbest person alive, completely played by Queenie. It was unbelievable, yet it had all happened, undeniably real.

Diana said, "Of course, I was in so much pain back then."

Diana couldn't continue.

Steven held her tightly, his voice filled with pain. "Diana, it won't happen again."

"From now on, you can ask me anything. I'll always tell you exactly what I'm thinking."

His gentle voice couldn't soothe Diana's wounded heart this time.

She leaned against Steven, tears streaming down her face.

Suddenly, she thought of Jenny. It felt like she had overlooked something.

Diana said, "By the way, how did you handle things with Jenny? From what I remember, she gave so much for you. Even after she lost her mind, she'd still smile at me and tell me to stay by your side."

"I still remember the day you sent her away. She kept looking at me, crying. Did you two make some kind of deal back then?"
