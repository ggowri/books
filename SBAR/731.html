Amelie was barely home anymore, coming and going at all hours, having completely forgotten she had a husband.

Now, all he could count on were Queenie and his son.

"Yes, I still have my son. I can't let anything happen to me. I must wait for him to return. Once he turns things around, I'll become the chairman."

Jeff finally gave in. "Fine! I'll be your stepping stone. But I want one million dollars this month. Don't forget, Queenie! I've got proof that you've been using me all along!"

Jeff smirked smugly. "Queenie, I'm sure you don't want this to turn ugly between us. All I want is money. I couldn't care less about anything else you're up to."

Queenie sneered, "I'll have someone transfer you a million dollars right away. You'll get the same amount every year, as long as you keep playing your role as my father and don't cause any trouble."

Jeff chuckled, "Well, well! One million dollars a year is enough for my retirement. My daughter sure knows how to take care of her old man."

Queenie hung up the phone without a word.

A cold glint flickered in her eyes. Finally, she was getting closer to her goal.

"But what about the baby?"

Her eyes darkened with malice. "Damn you, Levi. I'll make you pay dearly for this. I need to find a way to get rid of this pregnancy.

This baby can't be born. No one can ever know about it."

Queenie gleefully picked up her phone, pleased to see everyone trashing Diana online.

But her joy was short-lived as Mario came rushing in, panic written all over his face.

Queenie frowned, "What's wrong, Mario?"

Mario hesitated, and before he could speak, the police walked in.

Queenie froze at the sight of them. "You..."

"Ms. Queenie Marsh, we've received a report that you're involved in deliberately defaming Ms. Diana Bishop. We need you to come with us to the station for questioning."

"What?" Queenie was stunned. She had been about to call Diana just to rub it in, but Diana had beaten her to the punch by calling the cops.

Queenie shook her head defensively. "I... I didn't... I didn't frame her. She's the one who ruined my reputation."

The lead officer, a handsome man with refined features, showed her the warrant. "Miss, during your press conference just now, you repeatedly accused Diana of defamation and slander. She has filed a police report, and we need you to come with us for an investigation.

"If she really did defame you and damage your reputation, you should have pursued legal channels to protect yourself, not resort to these tactics."

Queenie was livid. "Have you all lost your minds? Who cares how I handle this? Do you know who I am? I'm Steven's fiancee! You dare take me to the police station?"

"I'm sorry, but regardless of who you are, we have to follow proper procedure," the officer said sternly.

"You'll need to come with us for questioning."

Queenie noticed how the officer remained unmoved, utterly unfazed by Steven's presence.

She was starting to panic. She had anticipated various scenarios and prepared different strategies for a long-term PR battle with Diana, but she never expected Diana to counter-attack so directly and brutally.

Of course, she had no evidence to back up her claims.

"Wait, this is all a misunderstanding. I'll call Diana right now."
