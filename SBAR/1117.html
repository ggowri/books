Alexander's cold eyes fixed on Steven's hand resting on Diana's waist.

Things were different now. In the past, Steven wouldn't have dared, and Diana would never have let him get this close.

Alexander's gaze lingered on Diana as he said, "Diana, you..."

He stopped himself. Now wasn't the time to say more.

He raised the gift box in his hand and said gently, "I brought you a gift from my trip. This diamond bracelet—I'm sure you'll love it."

Diana glanced at the gift box but didn't reach for it.

She smiled and said, "Alexander, I can't accept such an expensive gift."

The last diamond necklace she accepted, worth $500 million, was still locked away in her safe.

Diana once thought about finding someone to rely on, but now she felt having Steven was enough.

Alexander firmly pressed the gift box into her hands, saying, "This represents my feelings. Please, take it."

It was a gift he had carefully chosen.

Diana reluctantly accepted it, planning to return it another day.

She smiled and said, "Thank you, Alexander."

Alexander gazed at her radiant smile and chuckled bitterly, "I'll wait for your divorce."

He shot a defiant glance at Steven before turning and walking away.

Diana stood there, puzzled, sensing that Alexander might have ulterior motives.

"You care about him that much?" Steven's voice suddenly cut through, dripping with jealousy.

Diana snapped back to reality, a wave of tension washing over her.

Looking into his intense eyes, she couldn't help but smile.

Diana's smile only made Steven more agitated.

He leaned in, pressing his forehead against hers, and whispered, "Darling, don't smile at anyone else like that."

His words dripped with a lingering tenderness.

Diana felt her heartbeat quicken.

Just then, someone chimed in, "Keep it professional. This is the office entrance—save the romance for home."

Leopold was teasing them.

It was then Diana noticed Leopold, Tobias, Julius, and Olivia had all arrived.

"Grandpa! What brings you all here?" she exclaimed in surprise and delight.

Steven nodded politely to everyone in greeting.

Leopold warned him sharply, "If you ever hurt Diana again, you'll answer to me."

"Sir Bishop, I promise I'll never hurt her or make her cry again," Steven vowed solemnly.

"Let's hope you keep your promise. If it weren't for Edward, I'd never have agreed to this marriage," Leopold said dismissively.

He said, "Back then, watching you so stubborn, Edward begged me to step in. I had no choice but to go to your office myself and tell you—it wasn't Queenie who saved you, it was Diana."

Diana gasped in surprise, "Grandpa, why didn't I know about this?"

She never expected Leopold had kept so much from her.

Leopold said, "It was all Edward's idea. When you wanted a divorce and Steven treated you poorly, I was ready to bring you home. But Edward begged me on his knees to help you two. So, I went to Steven's office and told him it was you, not Queenie, who risked everything to save him from the fire."

"Thinking about the pain you went through back then just breaks my heart."

"I would never let Queenie take credit for what you did."

Diana's eyes widened in shock.

She had always thought Leopold loved her the most, but in her darkest moments, he had sided with the Gellar family.

She turned away angrily.

Tobias chuckled and said, "Diana, don't be mad. Let's arrange a proper talk between our families soon."

"Mr. Tobias Bishop is right. How about we have dinner together tonight?" Edward suddenly appeared and suggested.

"Sir Gellar! You're awake!" Diana exclaimed in surprise and delight.

"Diana, it's all thanks to your medicine. I only trust yours," Edward said warmly.

Diana let out a relieved smile.

Steven took her hand and said to Leopold, "Thank you, Sir Bishop."

He bowed deeply. The moment he learned Diana had saved him, it gave him purpose. He quickened his pace, determined to win her back as soon as possible.

Leopold sighed, "I hope I made the right choice. Seeing you both so happy puts my mind at ease."

"Edward's right. Let's have a proper talk tonight."

"Alright," Steven agreed.

Edward smiled with relief. A good outcome was worth the wait.

If they had failed, it would've been cruel of God to both of them. But this is the best outcome.

"You take care of things here. We'll wait for you at the Gellar Manor," Leopold said.

One by one, everyone left.

Diana looked utterly confused.

"Do you think they're hiding a lot from us?" she asked Steven.

Steven shook his head, unsure. Over the years, he had lived like an empty shell, incapable of loving anyone.

But over the past year, he finally found himself again, understanding his own heart. He cherished this year—because the woman he loved was still by his side.

Steven said, "Diana, it was my grandfather who insisted I marry you. Looking back, it seems there are still many secrets between them that we don't know."

"We'll find out tonight," Diana said.

"Alright, let's go," Steven nodded.

Just as they were about to leave, someone called out to her. "Diana."

The familiar voice made Diana freeze in her tracks.

Turning around to see who it was, Diana frowned and said coldly, "Mrs. Paula Marsh, is there something you need?"
