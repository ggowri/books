"If it’s false, once it hits the internet, it could seriously affect Ms. Marsh, damage your reputation, and impact small companies like ours."

Dylan despised Diana.

The morning sunlight streamed in brightly. Steven opened his eyes, taking in the familiar room.

He felt at ease. He had slept well last night, without any dreams.

Suddenly, he realized something.

Looking down at Diana still asleep in his arms, he let out a helpless smile. She slept like a child.

Last night, she had rolled into his arms again on her own.

Steven might have improper thoughts about her, but he would never take advantage of her in a situation like this.

Diana slept well and woke up early. She opened her eyes to see Steven's smiling gaze.

She froze for a moment, staring blankly at Steven for a while before realizing she was in his arms.

She let out a startled scream and quickly pulled away from Steven's arms.

Diana had no idea how she ended up rolling into Steven's arms.

Diana felt like these days were taking her back to the time when she used to take care of Steven.

Back then, Steven had injured his leg. After his surgery, she got him ridiculously expensive medicine and spent most of her days massaging his leg.

At first, Steven resisted it. He always felt like his life was over, drowning in pain every single day.

He pushed everyone away. Helen hired several caregivers for him, but he drove them all off.

Then Diana stepped in to take care of him herself. Being a doctor, it didn’t feel like much of a hassle for her.

At first, Steven couldn’t see, but his hearing was sharp. The moment he heard the door open, he’d lose it, yelling at her, "Get out!"

Diana didn’t leave. At that moment, she just thought Steven was pitiful—there wasn’t a single person who truly cared about him.

She encouraged him, telling him that with surgery and consistent medication, his leg could heal.

Seeing hope, he stopped getting angry, and she was able to get close to him, massaging him every day.

It wasn’t until Steven gradually regained feeling in his leg that he finally calmed down.

But Diana felt exhausted.

In the scorching summer, she massaged his leg, sweat dripping from her forehead.

She cared for him tirelessly, rain or shine, until he finally recovered.

But then Queenie came back. The peaceful time was shattered.

At that time, Diana was truly confident.

But the Diana who had faced death wasn’t confident anymore.

At this moment, to her, safety and love couldn’t coexist.

Diana sat across from him, smiling. "I want to travel. And if I meet someone who makes my heart race, I’d like to fall in love too."

Diana didn’t care at all. "We’ll get divorced eventually. And you’ll meet a girl you love too."

"I’ve seen you at your lowest. We can only be strangers."

"I’ll find someone genuine someday, someone who truly cares about me."

He had hurt her too deeply, that’s why she had thoughts like this.

Steven looked at her intently and said, "There’s still a year left until our agreement. During this year, you can’t betray me, and I’ll do my best to fall in love with me again."

His voice was almost trembling.

She had truly let go.

Steven felt a sharp pain in his chest. Her saying something like this wasn’t unexpected, but it still hurt.

Steven's face turned pale. She had let go because he wasn’t the one who pushed her back then.

If he had been the one, with her personality, she would’ve made sure he paid—maybe even with his life.

Steven felt like he was plunging into an abyss.

A massive wave of grief swallowed him whole, fierce and unrelenting.

He suddenly collapsed into Diana's arms.

She jumped in shock, staring at Steven's pale face in her arms. Gently patting his cheek, she said, "Steven, what's wrong? Wake up, please."

Diana checked him over. Steven had fainted from sheer exhaustion.

She looked at Steven and murmured, "Steven, did my words really shake you up this much?"
