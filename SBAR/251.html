In her dream, the attacker slowly sliced her face with a knife, then dripped a highly corrosive, stinging solution into the wounds, causing her to faint over and over from the pain.

Even in the dream, the pain was so intense that her entire body convulsed.

"Hah..." Diana gasped as she jolted awake, the sunlight streaming through the window. She grabbed her phone and quickly called Olivia.

"Diana, get some more rest. I'm almost at the airport," Olivia said.

"I'm sorry, Olivia. I overslept and couldn't see you off. I'm really sorry!" Diana said apologetically.

Diana felt guilty. The recurring nightmare had kept her trapped in sleep, making her miss the chance to see Olivia off.

"Diana, don't worry about me. Tobias brought me to the airport. I'll be back soon," Olivia reassured her.

Hearing that Tobias had taken Olivia to the airport made Diana happy. "Alright, Olivia. Stay safe abroad, and call me if you need anything."

Olivia cheerfully replied, "Of course, I'll call you if I need anything. You're my only friend, Diana. The future will get better, so don't be too sad."

Diana realized that Olivia must have heard her crying the night before.

Diana smiled and said, "Alright!" After chatting a bit more, she hung up.

Diana didn't rest any further. She got up, freshened up, and drove out. She needed to see Althea.

Diana found Althea on a small island in the south.

By the time she arrived, the sunset was beautiful.

The island's scenery was serene, making Diana feel at ease.

She and her car took a ferry to the island, then drove for another 20 minutes to Althea's seaside villa.

Althea came from a wealthy family, but she worked hard on her own.

While her parents and brother ran the family business, she worked at a relevant company, gaining experience.

There was a tourism project on the island, which was why she chose to live there.

After becoming pregnant, she no longer wanted to see the Marsh family, so her family moved away.

When Diana saw her, Althea had just finished a meeting, now nearly eight months pregnant.

She had become much fuller in figure, but her gentle smile remained as pure as ever.

Althea was planning to take a walk on the island and have dinner afterward.

The moment she saw Diana, Althea happily walked over, her eyes turning red. "Diana, is it really you? Am I seeing things?" she exclaimed.

It had been a full nine months since they last met.

After returning to the Marsh Villa, Diana had the closest bond with Althea. No matter how much Queenie tried to provoke her, Althea always believed in Diana.

Because Althea believed in her, Gale disliked Althea, always suspecting that she wasn't a good person either.

It was obvious that he was blind and foolish, yet he tried to act all high and mighty.

Diana walked over, her eyes red. Though it had only been nine months apart, to Diana, it felt like a century.

In a low voice, she said, "Althea, you're not mistaken. It's me."
