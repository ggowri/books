"I've done more than enough for Queenie. I won't marry her.

What's more, if anyone dares to interfere with my marriage to Diana, don't blame me for being unkind."

Stunned, Queenie forced out a sentence after a long time. "Are you going back on your word?"

Steven raised his sharp eyebrows, his ocean-like gaze showing no emotion. "I didn't. You left first. Diana wouldn't have married me if not for your father..."

"But you didn't say no in the first place." Queenie interrupted sorrowfully, her eyes teary.

Jeff went panicked. He knew well why Steven married Diana back then.

Even if Queenie had stayed, she couldn't have married Steven.

Steven's grandpa only wanted Diana to be Steven's wife, otherwise, he wouldn't have let Steven take over the company.

But now as the CEO of the Gellar Group, he was capable to confront his grandfather.

If he wanted to marry Queenie, no one could stop him.

However, he felt something was off.

There must be something off with Steven today given his calmness after he knew Diana had poisoned his mother.

Jeff couldn't look through him.

As perceptive as always, Queenie had noticed Steven's change.

She angrily stood up and ran upstairs crying.

Paula pretended to be worried. "Queenie, don't be too upset. Steven's love for you is unwavering. He won't marry anyone but you."

Queenie ignored her and quickly went upstairs.

Steven stared at Paula. She acted very strange tonight.

He then left the Marsh Villa in silence.

A table full of carefully prepared dishes remained untouched.

Jeff furrowed his brows at Paula who was lost in thought. "Honey, don't be angry. Steven will come to his senses. Eat something first."

"You go upstairs to comfort Queenie."

Jeff pressed his lips. He wasn't good at comforting people.

But seeing Paula's compelling gaze, he stood up and went upstairs to check on his daughter.

Paula picked up a lobster and cracked it open. This meal was never meant for the two of them. It was prepared for herself.

She ate heartily, and then found an excuse to leave for the company.

She glanced upstairs before leaving, a mysterious smile flashing across her mouth.

...

Royal Island, a five-star hotel.

Diana and her friends stayed on the island last night. She set an alarm in case she would be late for the divorce.

She sat up as soon as the alarm went off.

"Ah!" She let out a scream seeing a dark figure by the bed.

Fearing she might be hallucinating, Diana pinched the back of her hand hard. Immediately, a drop of blood appeared on her smooth skin.

Her voice trembled as she saw the blood and felt the pain. "It hurts. I'm not dreaming."

Steven was speechless.

Diana looked at Steven, terrified.
