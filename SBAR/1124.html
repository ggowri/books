Steven turned to Diana beside him and asked, "Diana, what's your take on this?"

Diana chuckled softly, glancing at Yanson. "Yanson, Owen warned you, but you didn't listen. You've always been stubborn, refusing to take advice from others."

"Shouldn't adults face the consequences of their own actions?"

She had tried to advise Yanson, but he refused to listen.

Diana had no intention of helping someone who ignored well-meaning advice.

Yanson looked at her, hesitating to speak, regret written all over his face.

What was I thinking back then? Why didn't I trust Diana and Owen?

Owen clearly warned me that Queenie might target my company.

Back then, he didn't believe it. He thought Queenie would never hurt him—after all, he had helped her so many times.

But this morning, his company's secrets were leaked, causing massive losses.

Steven stood silently nearby, saying nothing.

They stood frozen in the hallway, the tension thick and suffocating.

Only Yanson looked at Steven and Diana with hopeful eyes.

Steven suddenly spoke, "Diana, maybe we can use this incident to uncover Queenie's hidden forces."

Diana thought of Sandra, then Leopold's offer to take her back to see her father.

Diana glanced at Yanson and changed her mind. He was at fault, but she could benefit from this.

Diana's expression darkened. "Let's go," she said coldly. "We're checking out your company."

Yanson stared at Diana in shock, then relief washed over him. She was willing to help.

"Diana, you..."

Diana shot him a cold glance, cutting him off. "I'm not helping you," she said flatly. "I'm helping myself."

Yanson looked down, his voice tinged with disappointment. "I'm sorry, Diana. But thank you anyway. I'll take you to my company right now."

Steven called Evan, telling him to bring the car over.

The three of them got in the car and headed straight to Yanson's company.

Yanson led them to his office, pointing at the computer. "Steven, my company's computers went black this morning and still aren't fixed. The bank called, saying a payment was deposited into my account earlier, but it's not showing up."

"The bank investigated and found that the money had been transferred overseas."

"One of my long-term overseas projects had its base price leaked, and the deal was stolen. I'm on the verge of bankruptcy."

Steven knew his way around computers but wasn't an expert. One glance, and he could tell this was a newly developed virus.

Diana stood by for a few minutes, then sat at the computer, her fingers flying across the keyboard.

Yanson watched in shock as the woman he once dismissed as a nobody turned out to be a computer whiz.

In that moment, Yanson learned a hard lesson.

The woman he looked down on, Diana, was his savior, while the Queenie he had flattered was the real devil.

A few minutes later, Diana stopped typing and called Joseph.

Joseph's voice was filled with excitement. "Diana."

Diana chuckled. "Joseph, remember the USB drive I gave you? There's a link starting with 'F' on it. Send it to my phone—I need it."

Joseph replied, "Got it. I'll send it to you right away."

Diana ended the call and asked Yanson for a new USB drive. Once she received the link from Joseph, she loaded it onto the drive, plugged it into her computer, and entered another set of data.

Minutes later, the screen filled with blue text, strange symbols flickering across. Even Steven couldn't make sense of it.

But Diana remained calm, her eyes fixed on the screen as she occasionally typed swiftly on the keyboard.

At that moment, Yanson no longer dared to underestimate Diana—she truly had skills!

He felt like a complete fool.

If Diana lacked the ability, Steven would never entrust her with his life's work.

Yanson realized he shouldn't have been prejudiced against Diana.

Ten minutes later, Diana turned to Yanson and said, "I've located the hacker targeting your company. Send someone there immediately, but make sure not to alert them."

Yanson was about to agree when Diana stood up and said, "Forget it. I don't trust you to handle this. I'll go myself."

Yanson froze, overwhelmed with shame.

He had consulted every computer engineer in the company, and they all said it was impossible to solve.

Yet Diana had solved the problem in just a matter of minutes.

Yanson asked, "Diana, I want to help. What can I do?"

Diana thought for a moment and said, "Just call the cops."

Yanson felt even more ashamed.

Steven told Yanson to come along with them.

On the way, Yanson called the police.

Diana sent the directions to Evan in advance, and he drove fast.

After cracking the password, Diana didn't alert the hacker. Instead, she wanted to find out who they were.

In her past life, Queenie used Joseph to develop a game project and made a fortune.

In this life, Joseph's skills were applied to the more impactful field of medicine. He excelled greatly.

Diana checked the time. They had ten minutes left to arrive.

Seeing her anxious, Steven took her hand and reassured her softly, "Diana, don't worry. They won't get away."

Diana looked at him and said, "I'm wondering, after Queenie lost Joseph, who did she turn to? We haven't figured that out yet."

Queenie had hidden herself well. Diana had been too busy to dig into it before. But now that Leopold was back and Steven's issues were resolved, she finally had time to handle it.

Yanson listened to Diana's words and suddenly realized he knew nothing about Queenie—and even less about Diana.

"Steven," Yanson said, sitting in the passenger seat.

Steven asked casually, "What is it?"

Yanson muttered, "I had no idea Queenie was hiding so much. She always played the innocent victim in front of us. Am I really that blind?"

He deeply regretted ever trusting Queenie.

Steven glanced at his guilty expression and said coolly, "Isn't she exactly your type? Getting played by her—do you feel wronged?"
