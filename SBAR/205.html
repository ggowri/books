"Damn Diana, she's so infuriating!"

"Hmph! You ruined my plan tonight, so you'd better think about how to explain this to Paula," Queenie snapped as she stormed off. She still needed to appease Jace.

Jeff was speechless.

"But what should I do? I can't go back now!" he thought anxiously.

Hiding in the shadows, Steven saw Diana and Cecil exit. He quietly followed them.

"Diana, we won tonight. Queenie's plan failed. What do you think she'll do next?" Cecil asked.

Cecil turned his head, gazing at Diana's captivating beauty, the bright light reflected in her eyes.

She was breathtakingly beautiful, and he was handsome. They were a perfect match!

Diana looked into the distant with a faint smile. "Next, she won't come after me for a while. She'll be busy pleasing Mr. Foliot and Steven. We'll have a short break, but you won't. You need to find the mole in your company."

Cecil's eyes turned icy. "You still haven't found out who it is? Not even a suspect?"

Diana stopped and looked at him, her tone serious. "Cecil, stop hesitating. You have a suspect, but you're afraid to confirm it. Don't gamble on fate, and don't gamble on people's hearts. You can't read hearts, and you can't afford to lose on fate.

Cecil shook his head, looking serious. "What does it mean?"

"It means that Queenie has someone highly skilled in medicine to help her."

This wasn't just a guess. In her previous life, she had sensed it but never found out who that person was.

Cecil was speechless.

"How does this woman have such skills?" he wondered aloud.

"Because she has Steven. Steven turned her into a viper, using his resources to do whatever she wants," Diana said bitterly.

Diana's eyes were full of regret. "I was blind to have fallen for someone like Steven," she thought.

She still remembered just how vicious Steven had been in her previous life.

Seeing the distant look in Diana's eyes, Cecil's heart suddenly ached. "Diana, will that viper turn on Steven one day?"

Diana smiled. "Yes, but you have to stay alive to see that day."

She gazed deeply at Cecil's strikingly handsome face and smiled. "Spending time with you, I think you're pretty decent. Here are a few names. Look into them. I don't want to see you die."

Diana shoved a list into his hand and then walked away.

Diana was gradually warming up to Cecil. This time, he had helped her, and she intended to repay him.

It seemed Paula had discovered the whereabouts of Queenie's birth mother. Tonight had been a success, and a gleam flashed in Diana's eyes. Next up was Gale.
