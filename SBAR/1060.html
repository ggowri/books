Diana looked at him and noticed his eyes suddenly light up. She knew Steven was serious at this moment.

"Of course! I mean it," she said, her tone encouraging. "You need to pull yourself together now and beat them all. We'll figure out the rest later."

After saying that, she even gave him a little thumbs-up gesture.

Steven couldn't help but smile. To him, she looked both beautiful and adorable in that moment.

He nodded gently, his gaze filled with affection. "Diana, I will."

After a moment of silence, he asked, almost sulking, "Diana, do we really have to sleep in separate rooms?"

Diana shot him a sharp glare. "Steven, you're pushing your luck! Legally, we're married, but we don't have a real marriage, and we've kept our distance in private. You better not cross any lines."

Steven rubbed his face, his tone filled with helplessness. "Diana, ever since the car accident, I've had trouble sleeping. But last night, lying next to you, I slept so well. I can only make better decisions when I'm well-rested."

Diana felt speechless. Does he see me as a sleeping pill?

And a free one at that.

How dare he make such a request?

"When I truly loved him, he didn’t want me. And now he’s starting to act all affectionate? I just feel so uncomfortable."

Her smile was bright, almost deliberate. She had no intention of being his sleep aid, let alone comforting him to sleep.

A year ago, it was Steven who couldn’t sleep because of her.

"It’s fine. Even if you’ve been an insomniac for ten years with depression, I can fix that. I have a special incense. Light it before bed, and I guarantee you’ll fall asleep in ten minutes, dreamless. Plus, you’ll wake up feeling refreshed the next day."

Noticing the dark circles under his eyes, Diana figured he really hadn’t been sleeping well.

No wonder he’d made such an irrational decision earlier.

But since his mind wasn’t clear, she could forgive him this time.

Steven lowered his head, feeling dejected. To him, no sleep aid could compare to the comfort of holding her as he drifted off.

"Alright," he forced a smile. After all, he still had a year, so he wasn’t giving up.

Diana glanced at the time and said, "Head upstairs. The meeting starts in ten minutes."

Steven replied gently, "Alright."

The two took the electric wheelchair upstairs.

Watching the scene, Diana couldn’t help but find it amusing. "Well, I never thought I’d end up using a wheelchair one day."

It was Steven’s second time in a wheelchair.

He glanced at his leg.

During the accident, he turned to shield Diana, which left his left leg the most injured. But now, he was much better.

Though there was still a sharp pain when he walked, he’d fully recover in three months.

"I dragged you into this."

Diana smiled. "I already told you, no need to apologize. Even if that night hadn’t happened, they would’ve come for me sooner or later. But you really should focus more when driving. Stuck in a wheelchair isn’t exactly fun."

Steven noticed she was always smiling. She was undeniably optimistic, even when bullied by the Marsh family, she still faced it all with a smile.

However, he knew deep down she was hurting.

"Alright," he replied guiltily, silently vowing to make it up to her in the future.

At Paula's house, Gale, Eric, and she sat in the living room, the atmosphere heavy.

Paula had been in low spirits ever since she saw the news that morning.

Seeing Paula silent, Gale softly comforted her, "Don’t be too upset. Dylan won’t grow up unless he learns the hard way. Once he struggles out there, he’ll figure things out."

Long lectures were useless on Dylan, just like when they tried to reason with him before.

Paula had clearly said it wasn’t Diana’s fault, and Diana even explained how Queenie was out to get him, but he refused to believe it no matter what.

Who would’ve thought the little sister they spoiled since childhood could be this terrifying?

Paula couldn’t deny it. Every time she thought about it, it felt like her heart was being torn apart.

She couldn’t accept that her son could be that kind of person.

March was Diana's birthday, and Paula had planned to make it up to her.

Since Diana came back, Paula had thrown Queenie a birthday party worth millions of dollars, but she had never cared about Diana’s birthday.

But sadly, Diana didn’t need it anymore.

The only thing Paula could do now was to bring Queenie to justice.

Thinking of Diana’s bright smile and the careful way she used to call her "Mom," Paula now felt nothing but heartache.

It was all her own doing, but she couldn’t accept being deceived. In the end, she even lost her own daughter.

Paula looked at Gale and said, "Gale, Diana’s birthday is in just over a month. Every year, we’ve focused all our attention on Queenie. This year, I want to throw a birthday party for Diana."

Paula said coldly, "Whatever he’s planning, he’s definitely going to make things hard for Diana. You two better get him in jail. He can’t keep being Queenie’s pawn."

She fixed her gaze on Eric, her tone firm. "Eric, call him now and take him to your company. He won’t steal anything today, but he’ll definitely try at some point. When he does, you’d better send him straight to jail."
