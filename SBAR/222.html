Steven gazed at Queenie coldly. "Queenie, are you threatening me?"

She smiled sweetly and winked at him. "Steven, how dare I threaten you. We are a perfect match."

She was really cute.

But in Steven's eyes, she was pretentious!

His favorable impression of her was shattered. He could see through her stupid trick at a glance.

"So, ever since your return, you have been looking for opportunities to frame Diana and sow discords between her and me, right?" Steven asked calmly.

Queenie was so angry at his words that she laughed. She reminded him seriously, "Steven, I don't understand what you are talking about. You always want to divorce Diana because you are not firm enough, and you love me.

"Why did you blame me?"

All men desired to have a wife and a lover!

If Steven had always been steadfast, how could he not have believed in Diana but in her alone?

If the Marsh family had loved Diana enough, how could they have bullied Diana with her?

In short, their affection for her was not that intense.

Steven narrowed his deep eyes and leaned back on the chair casually. "You're right. I am not firm enough."

Queenie beamed with joy.

Although Steven's words were ambiguous, she thought that he had finally realized that he always loved her.

"Steven, I knew you could figure it out. We grew up together. I stayed by your side when you were in the most difficult times." Queenie reminded him all the time that she was his savior.

Steven pondered and discovered that it was Diana who stayed by his side when he was in the most difficult time.

"You are amazing. Go back now. I have to return to the company."

His tone was indifferent.

Queenie pointed to the pumpkin soup she had brought. "Steven, I brought the pumpkin soup for you. Eat some."

Steven gazed at the soup. Its color was very terrible. He said with disgust, "No. The smell suggests that the taste is not what I want.

"Queenie, if you want to marry me, be as sincere as Diana. The pumpkin soup was cooked by the servants. Have you ever been sincere?"

Steven left after he finished speaking.

Queenie glared at his back. In the past, whenever she said something was delicious, even if it was not, he would still say it was delicious to save her face.

Now, he compared her with Diana.

How could Diana, a country bumpkin, be compared with an elegant lady like her?

Diana was overshadowed by her.

Diana had been gone for five months, but Steven was still unwilling to divorce.

Why?

Did he really believe Diana's story?

Did he still remember how good she had been to him, so he couldn't adapt to life without her?

After all, she had taken good care of him in the past two years.

Diana depended on him, and he couldn't live without her consideration.

Therefore, he felt uncomfortable and refused to divorce.

But it didn't matter. Queenie was determined to help him adapt to her kindness quickly.

She must capture his heart. She would let Diana know that she had easy access to things that Diana would never dare to dream of.​

Besides, she would snatch away whatever Diana liked and cared about.
