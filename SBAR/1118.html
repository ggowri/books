Paula's eyes brimmed with tears at how Diana addressed her. Diana wouldn't even call her "Mom" anymore.

Back when Diana was willing to call Paula "Mom," Paula never once acknowledged it and constantly criticized her for being uncouth.

Now that Paula was facing her own punishment, she finally understood how much pain Diana had endured back then.

Paula let out a bitter laugh. This was the punishment she deserved, but it was excruciating. "Diana, I just wanted to see you. Knowing you're happy with Steven puts my mind at ease."

Diana stared at her intently. Paula's words meant nothing now—back then, she truly hadn't wanted Diana. "Mrs. Paula Marsh, if there's nothing else, I'll be on my way."

Diana turned and walked toward the company entrance.

Steven didn't spare Paula a single glance and walked away without hesitation.

Paula watched their retreating figures, tears streaming uncontrollably down her face.

She had come today seeking forgiveness, but when she saw Leopold, something about him felt familiar—like she'd seen him somewhere before.

Paula couldn't shake the feeling that Leopold genuinely cared for Diana.

She wondered, puzzled, "Was it really just a whim of Jeff's to switch the two children back then?"

Feeling uneasy, Paula called her assistant to pick her up and headed straight to the care facility where Jeff was staying.

Since becoming paralyzed, Jeff had been living in a constant state of misery, wishing for an end.

His hair was nearly all gone, and his mind was barely lucid.

But when he saw the radiant Paula, he grew excited, struggling to sit up. "Paula."

Paula looked at Jeff's pitiful state and let out a cold laugh. He deserved every bit of this.

Seeing him so pathetic only filled her heart with satisfaction.

Jeff was clearly struggling with this life.

His hair was almost completely gone, and what little remained had turned white.

He was the picture of utter misery.

Paula demanded, "Jeff, when you swapped Diana and Queenie back then, was it your choice or did someone put you up to it?"

Seeing Leopold earlier suddenly reminded Paula of Diana's biological father.

Even after all these years, that man's striking features were still etched vividly in her memory.

Jeff shook his head blankly. "Paula... I don't remember. I was wrong... Please, take me home. Living here is worse than death."

He truly regretted it. This place was pure hell, and he couldn't bear to stay another moment.

Paula said coldly, "Jeff, your memory isn't that bad. You couldn't possibly forget something as big as swapping babies. Tell me the truth about what happened back then, and I'll get you a better room."

Jeff was stuck in the worst room of the nursing home. Queenie, trying to save money, had dumped him there to fend for himself. Yet, he stubbornly clung to life.

Of course, Jeff wanted a better room. Even a small improvement would make a world of difference.

He deeply regretted it now.

If he hadn't betrayed Paula back then, he'd be living a free and comfortable life, with Paula buying him new designer suits every season.

But now...

Jeff's eyes brimmed with tears.

"Paula, I... I really don't remember," he stammered.

He truly couldn't recall the details from back then.

All he remembered was deliberately swapping the two daughters, giving Queenie a life of luxury and wealth.

He had no other significant memories.

Jeff shook his head in agony, drooling slightly.

"Paula, I really can't remember. For the sake of our marriage, please help me."

Paula felt a wave of disgust, her heart sinking with disappointment.

Coming here had been a complete waste.

"If you don't know, then pretend I was never here. Want a better hospital room? Go ask Queenie," Paula snapped before walking away without looking back.

Behind her, only Jeff's regretful sobs remained.

Stepping out of the care facility, Paula stood by the roadside, gazing at the blooming tulips across the street.

But a burning question lingered in her heart. She needed to know—who was Diana's real father?

Paula took a deep breath, got into the car waiting for her, and drove away.

The moment Paula left, Queenie received news that Paula had visited Jeff.

At that moment, Queenie was in her office, celebrating the acquisition of the King Group with Mario.

It was a surprise gift from Mario, and Queenie couldn't be happier.

They were tangled in a heated embrace, passion filling the office.

Queenie leaned against Mario's chest, her expression darkening as she read the message from the care facility.

"Does Jeff still have any value to us? Why would Paula go see him?"

Mario swirled his glass of red wine, downed the rest in one gulp, and murmured, "Probably something else."

Queenie let it go and turned her gaze to Mario. Seeing his satisfied expression, she couldn't help but ask, "Has Steven been investigating our company?"

With Steven's level of caution, he's bound to dig into who acquired the King Group.

Mario let out a cold laugh, his tone dripping with arrogance.

"Queenie, Steven's been swamped with meetings since he got back. He doesn't have time for this.

"Besides, a small company like the King Group isn't even on his radar. He wouldn't bother investigating. Our people say the couple's been really busy lately. But here's some good news—Alexander went to see Diana today."

"Alexander has feelings for Diana."

Jealousy burned fiercely in Queenie's heart.

She thought bitterly, "Even Alexander has a thing for Diana. She's ridiculously lucky."

Her good mood vanished instantly. Queenie snapped, "Is Dylan's friend all set up?"

She was determined to destroy Diana completely. There was no way she'd let Diana be happier than her.

Mario thought of those greedy fools and sneered. "Once they take care of Diana, we'll send them in to keep Dylan company."

"I've already given them instructions, but they just can't seem to find the right opportunity."

Queenie stood up and said, "Let's go. You're coming with me."

Mario asked, "Where to?"

Queenie flashed a wicked smile. "You'll find out soon enough."

Mario didn't press further and followed her out...
