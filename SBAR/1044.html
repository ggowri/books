Dylan, stripped of all worth, felt a surge of disbelief.

He stared at Paula, his eyes wide open.

In her eyes, he was nothing.

Dylan let out a bitter laugh.

How did I never notice how much Paula despised me before?

Since he came back, his world turned upside down. He was struggling to accept it.

He was too angry tonight, his words earlier were a bit extreme. But he felt Paula had gone way too far.

"Paula, I never wanted you dead. But let's be clear, rich people's wars aren't for the faint-hearted."

"Such war left Dad with half his face paralyzed! Now he drools when he begs for mercy. He lost, that's the truth. If you can't accept that, go win it back for him."

Paula's voice was calm.

"I already said, whatever you want to do with Queenie, go ahead."

"But don't expect me to recognize illegitimate children as heirs. Everything I have came from my parents. Why should I share it with outsiders and support your dad's mistress for life?"

Paula's tone was firm.

Dylan was stunned. She was throwing his own words back at him. In this battle of the wealthy, Jeff and Queenie had lost.

He felt helpless. "Queenie was just a baby. She was switched, but that wasn’t her fault. You raised her for over twenty years. Why you treat her like that?"

Really confusing! Everyone makes mistakes.

How could she tear our family apart?

Paula shot Dylan a disdainful glance, wanting to kick him out the door.

"And Diana? Was she not a baby too? Or is your father's mistress's child more deserving of compassion than my own daughter? Your bastard father messed such drama today!"

"I know you have your thoughts, and I don’t blame you. But no more words. Sign the disownment agreement, and go."

Paula suddenly felt exhausted, but she had to keep going. She hadn’t seen Diana live a happy life yet, so she wouldn’t let anything happen to herself.

Just as Dylan was about to say something, Eric had already picked up the disownment agreement.

He handed. "Sign it."

Dylan froze for a moment, staring at Eric. "Eric, are you serious?"

Eric let out a cold laugh. "Do I look like I'm joking?"

Dylan scoffed. "Fine, I'll sign. Paula, you abandoned me. Don't regret it."

Paula sneered. "Not a chance. You even wished me to die."

Paula locked eyes with Dylan, her voice like ice. "Remember this day. You'd choose that murderous bitch over your own mother's love."

Dylan's proud facade cracked. He was caught in a dilemma. He knew Paula wouldn’t be that ruthless. Even if he signed the agreement, it wouldn’t change the fact that he was still her son.

He'd come to defend Queenie, believing Paula would soften. But the signed severance papers in his hand proved how wrong he'd been.

Scrawling his signature, he stood abruptly. "Congratulations, Paula. You just lost a son."

Paula just laughed. "Good. See me on the street? Walk the other way."

She stood up and went back, without a trace of reluctance.

Dylan's face turned pale.

Shit. This wasn't supposed to happen.

Eric snatched the document. "Sir, please leave."

His cold demeanor infuriated Dylan. "Fuck you, Eric! This is still my house! Paula will eventually agree to let Queenie and me back."

"Get out!" Eric shoved him toward the door, not giving him much chance to speak.

Then he slammed the door shut without hesitation.

Dylan rubbed his nose.

2 AM and homeless. Perfect.

He could only call Queenie tomorrow.

Dylan turned and walked down the stairs, a faint sadness lingering in his chest. Paula really didn’t want him anymore.

Eric knocked on Paula's door.

Hearing the knock, Paula opened the door.

"You okay?" Eric asked, concern written all over his face.

Paula smiled and shook her head. "Eric, I’m fine. But Pack our things. We're moving to Verve Residences tomorrow. Not convenient here for Gale."

Eric blinked. "But the money—"

Paula smirked. "Honey, we never needed to play poor. That was just bait for Queenie's little games. Now? I hold all the cards. Not afraid of her causing trouble again."

Paula’s plans were executed flawlessly.

She had even anticipated the unexpected twist with Dylan.

Eric chuckled, "Yeah, this place is small but cozy. Just those damn stairs for Gale..."

Paula glanced around, her expression shifting. "We keep this place then. Staff can maintain it for when we want to visit." She turned sharply. "The new place is ready. We move at dawn."

"I'm done with Dylan," she said flatly. "Changing you three nearly broke me. If it weren’t to keep you alive, I would’ve killed Queenie. The mess I’d leave behind would only drag you all into despair."

Almost over now, she thought.

Eric felt guilty, but he knew he would help Paula manage the company from now on.

"I'm sorry. We made you worry."

Eric gave a bitter smile, blaming himself for realizing it too late.

Paula narrowed her eyes and smiled. "Eric, it's okay. As long as you all grow up, that's all I need. Now go to bed, don't overthink it."

Eric finally left.

Paula closed the door. She looked out at the misty night, her eyes filled with tears.

My daughter...my boys...all casualties of my blindness.

A tear slid down her cheek. She closed her eyes, letting the sorrow wash over her.

Dylan spent the night at the hotel. Early the next morning, he was jolted awake by the pain in his finger.

Then he glanced at the time. Just past 8 a.m from his phone. He was exhausted, and his finger throbbed with pain.

His face was pale. He picked up his phone and called Queenie.

She was woken up by the call and answered reluctantly. "Jesus, Dylan...it's so early. What’s up?"
