Olivia frowned. "Diana, before you came back, I didn't check your fortune, but I did a reading for your love life. Everything pointed to things being great. So why are you suddenly getting a divorce? I just don't get it."

Diana wanted to say that maybe even Olivia could get it wrong sometimes.

But deep down, she knew Olivia wasn't the type to make mistakes. With a calm smile, she replied, "Olivia, maybe it's just not the right time yet."

Olivia narrowed her eyes, clearly not convinced, but chose to keep her thoughts to herself.

*****

The next morning, Olivia drove Diana to the Courthouse.

When they arrived, Olivia parked the car and retrieved a wheelchair, helping Diana into it before pushing her toward the entrance.

"Wait," Diana said suddenly. "Let's double-check everything."

She meticulously went through the documents. "Alright, all here. The divorce agreement is with Steven. Since we don't have any property disputes, it should be pretty simple."

Olivia shook her head, exasperated. "Diana, you've been married to him for years. You didn't take a single penny from him, even paid for his treatments out of your own pocket, and now you're just walking away? Seriously?"

Diana smiled faintly and turned to her friend. "Olivia, I don't want anything from him. I just want a clean break. No more ties and entanglements. He lives his life, and I live mine. That's it."

Olivia sighed, throwing her hands up. "Fine, fine. You're a self-made queen, after all. Let's go get that freedom you're so set on.

"Diana, you're a star. Once you shake off all this mess, you're going to shine brighter than ever."

But Diana didn't laugh. For some reason, her smile just wouldn't come.

Not far away, a man sat in a wheelchair, bundled up in layers. His eyes stayed locked on her back, a storm of pain swirling in them.

He spoke in a low voice, "Is everything ready?"

The man beside him nodded. "Everything's in place."

*****

Inside the hall, Diana was caught off guard.

Steven was standing. His forehead and chest were bandaged, but he was on his feet.

Beside him, Queenie clung to his arm, her movements overly intimate.

Diana's brows knitted together as unease crept in.

"Olivia, I can't even stand. How is he walking? I'm pretty sure his injuries were worse than mine."

Olivia scoffed. "Please, he only fractured his arm. The rest? Barely a scratch."

Diana's frown deepened. "That doesn't add up. I'm a doctor. I know what I saw. He passed out on the spot. There's no way his injuries were less severe than mine."

As Diana tried to make sense of it, Steven's icy voice sliced through the air. "Diana, you're late. Do you even know what punctuality means?"

That familiar yet distant tone sent a chill down her spine.

In her mind, another voice from her past echoed, booming like thunder.

"Diana, just go die!"

The voice she heard today was identical.

Her eyes widened in shock as she stared at Steven, unease prickling at her senses.

Something was terribly wrong.

Back then, when Steven had pushed her, his voice and eyes had felt just as cold and unfamiliar as they did now.

Queenie smirked, her tone dripping with sarcasm. "Diana, don't tell me you're having second thoughts about divorcing Steven?"

Before Diana could respond, Steven's voice rang out, sharp with anger. "Diana, you're signing those papers today whether you want to or not. The only woman I care about is Queenie."

He wrapped his good arm around Queenie, pulling her close as if to drive the point home.

The sight stung. Diana felt a sharp ache in her chest as tears pricked her eyes.

Yet, despite her emotions, her doctor's instincts kicked in. Something didn't add up. Steven's left arm had been the one injured, but it was his right arm now wrapped in a cast.

She distinctly remembered feeling the wound on his left hand.

But everything else about him, his expressions, his movements, even his voice felt so familiar, except for the subtle accent that wasn't quite right.

She dropped her gaze, avoiding his cold, piercing stare. Her voice was calm, almost detached. "Fine by me. Let's get this over with."

She turned to Olivia. "Olivia, take me to the counter."

Queenie visibly relaxed, a triumphant smile spreading across her face. With the divorce finalized, Diana would no longer have any claim or leverage, and Queenie could finally shed the label of mistress.

The paperwork was processed quickly. Within minutes, Diana and Steven both held their divorce certificates.

Diana stared at the document in her hand, a weight lifting off her shoulders.

"Steven," Queenie beamed as she glanced at Steven's certificate. "Now I can officially be your fiancée."

Steven smiled warmly and planted a soft kiss on her forehead, a public display of their new beginning.

Just then, both Diana and Steven's phones buzzed incessantly, notification after notification lighting up their screens.

Curious, Diana unlocked her phone. Her eyes widened as she read the first message.

It was a transfer of forty billion dollars.

She blinked in disbelief, scrolling through more messages.

[All assets under the Gellar group, including the headquarters and 1999 subsidiaries nationwide, have been transferred to Diana.]

[99 luxury villas in Capitole, 60 shopping centers, office buildings, and every asset under Steven's name in Luspus Hill, Westwood, Luspus, and Banngrad are now owned by Diana.]

Her jaw dropped. "Am I dreaming? Did I just win the lottery?"

Standing behind her, Olivia leaned over to read the messages.

Her eyes nearly popped out of her head. "Diana, this is insane! Steven must've cared about you way more than he let on. He gave you everything!"

Diana turned to Steven, her head spinning.

Across the room, Steven's face was pale, his body trembling. Queenie, on the other hand, looked like she might pass out.

A memory seemed to hit Queenie, her expression twisting as she recalled the agreement.

Meanwhile, Diana's shock gave way to a smirk. In an instant, she went from being free of her marriage to becoming one of the wealthiest women alive.

Steven's voice thundered across the room. "Diana, what the hell did you do? How is everything under my name now yours?"

Diana's eyes gleamed with amusement as she met his furious glare.

She'd thought the contract was a joke, something Helen made up to tease her.

But now, it was all too real.

Instead of donating his wealth elsewhere, Helen had handed it to her.

The sheer magnitude of it was overwhelming.

This was more money than she could ever spend in a lifetime.

Turning to Olivia, Diana managed a laugh despite her disbelief.

"Olivia, pinch me. Is this real? How does something like this even happen?"

But her initial excitement was quickly replaced by a gnawing unease. This wasn't a gift but a ticking time bomb.

She could already imagine the chaos Andrew's illegitimate children would unleash. They wouldn't stop until they tore her apart for this inheritance.

Steven's face darkened, his voice cold and menacing. "Diana, answer me! What did you do to get your hands on my assets?"
