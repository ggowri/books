Diana stared angrily at his feigned innocence. "Stop pretending. The photos you took are identical to the new products released by the Gellar Group," she said.

Diana pulled up the Gellar Group's latest products again and showed them to Gale. "Look at what you've done. You tore up my designs. Even though I know they stole my drafts, there's nothing I can do now but watch them make money from my work.

"I've already cut ties with your family, yet you keep setting me up again and again!

"Even stealing my designs... How dare you!"

Silent tears streamed down Diana's face; she felt truly heartbroken.

How could Diana have been born into such a family?

After being reborn, why did she still have to come from that woman's womb?

Seeing her cry made Gale uncomfortable as well. "Diana, no matter what you say, I didn't do it," he insisted.

Luna shouted angrily, "There's video evidence here. How can you still say you didn't do it?"

"Ms. Bishop, let's call the police," Luna suggested.

Diana nodded. "Alright!" she agreed.

"Diana, you can't set me up like this. I'm about to compete, and I must win this time. And I already told you, I didn't do it," Gale protested.

"Then explain to me who you sent those photos to," Diana demanded, pointing at the video of him taking the photos.

Gale knew Diana wouldn't believe him no matter how he explained. "Diana, I told you, I didn't do it. I took the photos, but I didn't send them to anyone. I don't even know who deleted them from my phone," he insisted.

"Shut up. Do you think I'd believe a word you say? You're born wicked, just like your vile father," Diana sneered. "Just wait. He'll personally send you to hell."

With only a few days to go, Diana could wait. Gale, born rotten and shameless, wouldn't meet a good end.

Speaking of his father, Gale's anger flared. "Diana, was it you who set up my father last night?" he accused.

"Do you have any proof?" Diana asked. "If I'd framed your father, would I still be standing here? Your family would've thrown me in jail by now.

"Has Queenie's nonsense completely filled your brain? You believe everything she says?" Diana mocked.

"Diana, you..." Gale started.

Luna interrupted, "Ms. Bishop, should we call the police?"

Gale was nervous; he couldn't afford to be arrested now.

Diana stopped Luna from making the call. She sneered, "No need. There's no need to call the police. The wicked will meet their match. Harry will find a way to get revenge as long as he's out.

"A man like him, who twists the truth, should be left to be dealt with by someone like that."

Gale bit his lip. "That incident was your fault..." he muttered.

"Your people did it. I'm the victim here, and you blame me? Gale, you're always this shameless?" Diana snapped.

Luna, fuming, asked, "Ms. Bishop, are we just going to let this go?"

Diana took a deep breath, wiped her tears, and looked at Gale with a strange smile. "Of course, we're not letting this slide. Let him compete first, then hand the evidence to the police afterward."

Gale quickly tried to explain, "Diana, don't frame me. I really didn't give your designs to anyone."

Diana didn't even look at him, walking away with Luna.

Gale was furious, hands on his hips, watching Diana leave. He felt conflicted. Was Diana really scheming against him now?

But strangely, who deleted the design photos he took?
