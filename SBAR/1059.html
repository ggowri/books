"I won't get into trouble, and I don't need you to do anything for me. Taking down Dylan and sending him to jail is a piece of cake for me."

No one can understand my will to survive.

Not just to live—I want to live brilliantly, to shine.

If I’m not ruthless, the enemy will keep pushing, trampling over my boundaries step by step.

If I make it through this year, what about next year?

I still want to play cards and laugh with my family and friends like I did this year. Even if I lose, I’d still feel happy."

She would never forget that she once died on Christmas Eve. While others were celebrating with family, fireworks lighting up the night sky, she lay disfigured in the freezing snow with the unborn child she never got to meet.

Steven clenched his fists, saying nothing.

The air froze instantly, heavy and suffocating.

Luna, Evan, and the others felt the tension between the two. They quickly found excuses and left one by one.

Only the two were left in the living room.

Diana casually picked up her coffee cup and took a sip, soothing her throat. She was exhausted from working tirelessly for the company every single day.

Even though she wasn’t going to the office today, Dylan was bound to throw a fit.

Maybe she could take this chance to dig up some dirt on him.

With Dylan's reckless nature, he must have been spending lavishly before Paula went bankrupt.

Now that Paula's declared bankruptcy, his allowance is bound to be cut.

His life can't be as extravagant as before, those fair-weather friends are bound to ditch him. To save face, he's definitely going to do something shady.

If time could prove Steven's sincerity, maybe she’d let go of her grudge and reconcile with him.

Love is an unpredictable journey—either filled with thorns or smooth sailing, full of sweetness and stability.

In the previous life, Steven didn’t personally harm Diana or her child. He even protected them.

Queenie admitted herself, and Diana had experienced it firsthand, so there was no doubt about it.

Now, Diana felt as calm as still water, an unprecedented sense of ease washing over her.

She had already won effortlessly.
