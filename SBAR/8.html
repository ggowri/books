Diana's feet were killing her, and she started sinking.

Rita Farley, the old lady, caught her breath. Something had stabbed her foot, freaking her out and making her leg cramp up. She was in deep water and nearly went under.

She watched Diana going down and got worried. "Hey, you okay? Come on up!"

Diana tried to chill out.

Fighting through the pain in her feet, she slowly made it to the surface.

Rita spotted blood in the water, then saw it on herself. Her eyes went wide, realizing someone had messed with the pool.

"Miss." Rita wanted to help Diana, but she couldn't move.

Diana felt something was wrong. She tried to climb up but finally fell powerlessly beside Rita. She felt dizzy, and it seemed that the stuff in the water was anesthetic.

"Madam, there's something in the water. It seems to make people lose their strength."

Diana was soaked, and her feet were bleeding.

The wound on the soles of her feet felt horrible.

Rita saw that Diana's wounds were more severe than the ones on her feet, and she also felt that her whole body was gradually losing strength.

"It's okay. My bodyguard will be here soon. Thank you for saving me. If it weren't for you, I would have been dead today." Rita’s voice was weak.

Diana wanted to speak but gradually lost consciousness.

"Madam, are you okay?" Rita's bodyguard came running over in panic.

She pointed at Diana and said, "Come on. Send her to the hospital. She is seriously injured."

The bodyguard was worried. "Madam, I'll take you there first."

"Cut the crap. She saved me. Send her to the hospital now." Rita's tone left no room for doubt.

The bodyguard did not dare to delay and carried Diana to the hospital.

Watched them walk away, Rita fainted.

Just then, Queenie, who had arrived, did not see the previous scene and quickly ran to Rita.

Looking at the sleeping old lady, she quickly jumped into the water. After she was soaked, she came ashore near Rita, and shouted around, "Help! Help! Someone got drowned!"

Meanwhile, Rita's grandson came to pick up his grandma. Hearing the cry for help, he quickened his pace.

"Grandma! Grandma!" Cecil Farley was prettier than most women, but not in a weird way. Tall and slim, with a laid-back vibe.

Queenie's eyes widened as she looked at the handsome man. Compared with a cold man like Steven, such a man seemed more ideal.

"Sir, it's better to take her to the hospital." She said.

Cecil glanced at Queenie and said gratefully, "Thank you for saving my grandma. This is my business card. Please contact me tomorrow."

Queenie took the business card and looked at Rita, pretending to be worried. "Sir, I just want to help. You should take her to the hospital now."

"Thank you. Miss, remember to contact me."

Cecil looked at the blood all over the ground, his eyes deepening slightly. Someone had tampered with the pool and wanted to kill his grandma.

He carried Rita and left, with the bodyguards leading the way.

Queenie looked at the business card and showed a strange and triumphant smile. She had just accompanied Steven to the banquet and wasted some time.

But the result was what she wanted.

"Diana, just wait. I will trample you under my feet!" she thought, feeling smug.

Diana didn't wake up until the next day.

Tobias sat beside her. His handsome face eased as he noticed she was awake.

Tobias couldn't help scolding, "Silly girl, if you don't wake up, your name will be engraved on the monument!"

Diana asked in surprise, "Tobias, you mean I almost died?"
