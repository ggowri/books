Diana dropped all pretenses and smiled broadly as she said, "Then I'll call you Alexander."

"That has a nice ring to it," Alexander said softly, staring at her stunning visage, "but one day, I hope you'll call me something even closer."

Diana squinted. A smile flickered in her clear eyes. She had grappled with this dilemma on several occasions, ultimately choosing simplicity by deciding to address Alexander simply as "Alexander."

"Diana, let's go. I've made reservations for the day; now's the perfect time," Alexander said, moving forward to the car. He opened the door with grace, ushering Diana inside.

Luna was arranged by Alexander to ride in the car behind, leaving Diana and Alexander alone in the front car.

As their car pulled away, Steven, sitting in a nearby vehicle, wore a somber expression. The departure of Diana and Alexander left him haunted by the memory of her smile.

She had never smiled like that in his presence.

Once, during the half year when he was confined to a wheelchair, she had offered him such smiles and encouragement. Perhaps, due to his different state of mind at the time, he had failed to appreciate her vibrant and luminous smile.

He clenched his hands tightly on his knees.

His eyes remained fixed on their car until it vanished from sight.

Evan sensed the oppressive atmosphere in the car and dared not speak.

After a long silence, he asked, "Mr. Gellar, shall we head home?"

Steven finally snapped out of his reverie, hoarsely replying, "Follow them."

"Huh?" Evan murmured, his mind already drifting towards the sweet sleep at home.

Even men needed their rest to stay youthful.

Steven frowned, his voice gruff, "I asked you to follow them."

Evan whispered under his breath, "Before, when Ms. Bishop was at home, you refused to come back even though she prepared dinner for you every day. Why are you chasing after Ms. Bishop now?"

Though Evan muttered quietly, Steven caught every word, so his mood darkened.

He leaned back, lips pressed tightly together, but inside, a surge of anger threatened to erupt.
