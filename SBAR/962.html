As layers of mystery peeled away, her identity seemed unreachable.

Steven stopped talking and continued to watch Diana eat.

He was in a good mood today. Queenie and George were hospitalized, meaning she could have a peaceful Christmas.

At Paula's house, only she, Gale, and Eric were celebrating Christmas together. It was the quietest celebration they ever had.

In previous years, she would start preparing Christmas gifts for Queenie and the whole family.

She would spend millions of dollars on custom dresses for Queenie without batting an eye.

She would only give a few thousand dollars to dismiss Diana.

Paula looked out at the snowy scene. On Christmas day, it continued to snow heavily, making the outside world a bleak gray, which mirrored her somber mood.

Only the fireworks shooting into the sky, with their dazzling colors in the night, gave her a sense of the festive spirit.

She thought Diana was spending Christmas alone too.

Steven was with Queenie now, the ending she had always wished for.

A man like Steven, no matter how wealthy, wasn't good enough for Diana.

She was determined to avenge Diana.

Soon, Queenie would be sent to hell.

Jeff would soon face retribution.

Gale, sitting in his wheelchair, saw her staring out the window in a daze and felt a deep sorrow for her. "Mom, are you thinking about Diana?"

Paula showed no joy for Christmas. "Yes, in previous years, none of us cared about her. Every year, she would give us gifts, but we despised them, often discarding them in front of her.

Paula sighed deeply, "It's my fault for not raising you properly, to the point where you all wanted to eliminate her. If she hadn't been capable, she would have been destroyed by you all."

Gale and Eric felt immense guilt.

They had opposed Diana for Queenie as if under a spell.

Looking back now, they realized how wrong they had been.

"Mom, Diana is getting better now. As long as she's happy, we'll work hard to earn her forgiveness."

She truly regretted it. She shouldn't have looked down on Diana.

Paula covered her face and wept.

Each firework blossomed like a flower, or twinkled like a star, illuminating the night.

Paula stood by the window, her face tear-streaked.

The fireworks shot straight into the sky, coloring the city sky with brilliant hues.

With each crackle of the fireworks, her sobs grew louder.

"Diana, I'm sorry. I was wrong. You longed for my love, but I wouldn't even look at you. Diana, it's my fault," she cried out in anguish.

Her sorrow was drowned in the noise of fireworks.

Eric brought water to her, and when he opened the door, he saw her weeping in pain. His hand tightened around the cup.

He too was deeply sorrowful. They all regretted their actions. Silently, he withdrew, stood on the balcony, and pulled out his phone to call Diana.
