She smiled and said, "Alexander, you look very handsome today."

Alexander glanced at his casual wear and smiled back, his smile bright as the moon in the sky. His ears turned slightly red. Being complimented by a girl felt different.

He was nothing like Steven. Steven was too brooding. He'd say a few words when in a good mood, but on bad days, he wouldn't say a thing—let alone smile. Sometimes, she wondered if Steven ever smiled.

Most of the time, it was her making him laugh, while he'd look at her coldly, lacking any romantic interest.

People should smile more to attract better luck.

Feeling a bit bashful from Diana's compliment, Alexander looked into her clear eyes and radiant face, smiling back at her. "You look beautiful too, Diana!"

Diana blushed. She had that kind of personality—unable to handle compliments. When moved, she would cry freely.

"Let's head inside quickly. It's getting colder," she urged.

Alexander led her in. "Yes, in this cold weather, you were just diving the other day! Did you catch a cold?"

Diana shook her head. "No, I didn't get sick after taking medicine when I got home. Just a bit tired."

Alexander nodded, understanding she was worn out. "I remember fishing on the sea last year and caught a huge fish. The drag was terrifying! I have no idea how you manage to catch those tuna and pull them in."

Diana explained with a laugh. "I wouldn't attempt to catch a fish that big. I'm afraid they'd drag me away!

"I have my ways to catch them! Growing up by the sea, I know the terrain is complex and that there are many dangers, so I don't mess with the really big fish.

"But I do catch the smaller ones. I love abalone! Once it warms up next year, I'll go harvest some."

Alexander grinned and asked, "Can I join you then? I recently started learning to dive. I want to experience the beauty of the ocean."

Diana nodded happily. "Sure! I'll introduce you to my older brothers. My three brothers all love diving too, so we can go together."

He was going to be a super big shot in the future, and all his projects were profitable, so she wanted him to know her brothers.

Connections were important, and her brothers were even more so.

Alexander smiled back at her bright expression. "Okay! My grandfather often mentions your grandfather's three grandsons, all of whom are exceptional. I'd love to meet them!"

Diana was thrilled and planned to introduce Alexander to her brothers when Tobias returned.

In this life, her brothers wouldn't end up as tragically as they had before.

But she still didn't dare to let Queenie know. That woman inexplicably hated her and everyone around her.

The two of them ordered their favorite dishes and enjoyed a cheerful meal while chatting.

Not far away, Steven watched Diana's radiant smile. Ever since Queenie had returned, he hadn't seen her smile like that again.

He clenched his fists tightly, fixating on Diana‘s bright smile. "Diana, I will never let you go!"
