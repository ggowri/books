Steven raised an eyebrow, narrowed his eyes, and said coldly, "Mr. Powell, Diana is still my wife."

Diana turned to Alexander and said, "Alexander, you can go back. I'll talk to him. Don't worry about me. I'll come back to you."

She wanted to talk about divorce.

Alexander turned and looked at her, saying, "Diana, I'll be waiting for you in the car."

With that, Alexander strode away.

Steven reached out to pull Diana, but Diana swiftly dodged him.

He was left standing there, his hands empty. "Come back with me."

Diana questioned, "Steven, what are you playing? You're the one who asked me to leave."

"I thought you were just messing around. It's been over six months. Enough is enough. Come back with me," Steven insisted.

Diana found his reasoning absurd. She gazed at him; under the light, his face gleamed, exuding breathtaking handsomeness. Any woman would be drawn to him, yet Diana now found him repulsive.

She retorted, "Why do you always think I'm just messing around? You were the one fooling around with Queenie every day. I messed around because I wanted you to come home and take medicine.

"I've done everything to bring you back. But what have you done? Nothing. Why should I leave and return whenever you please?"

"Steven, mark my words. I will never be with you again. I would rather die than be with a jerk like you," Diana declared firmly.

Steven was taken aback by the intensity of her words. He realized he had been wrong and deceived by Queenie.

He never truly loved Diana, so he never bothered to consider her feelings.

But over the two years of marriage, she had become an integral part of his life, a good wife.

However, Queenie's return brought out Diana's jealousy and confrontational nature, testing his patience.

As he reflected on his actions, Steven recognized his own foolishness.

A dangerous glint flashed in his eyes as he spoke in a low voice, "You would rather die than be with me?"

Diana had been tired of dealing with him.

"Yes," she affirmed, her tone unwavering.

Steven focused on her, his expression unreadable. "That is not what I want. Once I settle my affairs, I will bring you back. Until then, you must maintain distance from other men, especially Alexander. His force is overseas, but mine can easily outmatch his."

Diana was stunned. She recalled Steven's formidable overseas force that he had established at 18 years old, overshadowing Alexander's.

"Are you threatening me?" she asked, her face turning red with anger.

Steven smirked, finding her fiery look somewhat endearing. "Diana, in this world, as long as I want, no one can escape my grasp, including you.

"Except for when necessary for collaboration, avoid contact with him, and remember that your grandfather and Tobias are your weaknesses."

His smile held a wicked edge, and Diana couldn't decipher the emotions in his narrowed eyes.

Diana glared at him. She had been careless, revealing Tobias and Leopold in his presence.

Leopold and her three brothers were extremely private people and rarely seen in public.

"Could Steven really find out the whereabouts of my brothers?" she wondered.
