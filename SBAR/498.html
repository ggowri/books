Steven asked coldly, "Who told you I'm Queenie's fiancé?"

"Is that how the rumors outside portray him and Queenie?"

Wyatt laughed. "Mr. Gellar, everyone in Capitole knows you're engaged to Queenie. You take Ms. Marsh to every event, and the two of you make a perfect couple. Congratulations in advance, and send me an invitation when you get married."

Steven frowned and changed the subject. "By the way, Mr. Farley, what deal were you discussing earlier?"

Remembering the matter at hand, Wyatt's expression turned serious. "Mr. Gellar, could you do me a favor? I'll give you the land in Westwood if you can convince Diana to spare my son. The Westwood property will be yours."

Steven gazed at him thoughtfully, his eyes darkening. The Westwood land?

Wyatt was willing to go to great lengths for his son, even offering the valuable Westwood land in exchange.

Steven thought it might be worth considering.

"I'll try to talk to her," Steven replied indifferently.

Wyatt gave a meaningful smile, knowing that the Westwood land was something Steven had always wanted.

Men are willing to sacrifice women for profit.

But he wasn't aware of everything; his son had assaulted a police officer just now.

As Steven got into the car, Evan asked, "Mr. Gellar, are you going to make Ms. Bishop forgive Samuel just to get Westwood land?"

Steven gave Evan a cold look. Evan seemed overly concerned about Diana.

"You know how important the Westwood land is to the company."

Evan fell silent, realizing that, to Steven, nothing was more important than the company's growth.

He then asked, "Mr. Gellar, are we heading to the office?"

Steven looked out the window, his gaze unreadable. "Yes."

Steven's phone vibrated; it was Queenie calling.

He pressed his lips together, a flash of cold anger in his eyes, and answered the call with composed grace.

"Hello!" he said, his voice low.

Hearing his tone, Queenie felt a slight chill.

She had been filming on the island for a few days, and Steven hadn't called her even once, making her uneasy.

"Steven, I'm back today. Can we have lunch together?" Queenie asked tentatively.

Steven glanced out the window and agreed. "Sure. Once you've booked the restaurant, text me. I have a meeting at the office."

Steven ended the call after that.

Evan glanced at Steven through the rearview mirror and sighed inwardly. So it was true that one's first love was the hardest to forget.

At noon, Diana arrived at the office, where Luna was waiting for her at the entrance.

"Diana, your injury hasn't healed yet. Why did you come to the office?" Luna asked worriedly.

Diana shook her head and smiled. "I'm fine. I have some matters to attend to. Paige brought ten models back to the company, and I came to check on them and give them a few days off."

Luna noticed Diana's immobile arm, still badly injured, and that she had driven herself.

Luna said, "Diana, Queenie came back today."

Diana thought about Samuel's situation. Queenie must be getting desperate. Diana was fine, but Samuel was facing jail time, so how could Queenie not be anxious?

Having lost the gamble, she was beginning to panic.

She had believed Samuel could kill her, but even he hadn't expected her to survive.

Diana sneered. "They'll be in touch with Wyatt and Courtney soon. Keep an eye on them."
