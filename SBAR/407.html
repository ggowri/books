Cecil laughed in anger, "How could I not be afraid? Your dream lover is a venomous snake. You two are truly a perfect match. I wish you both eternal happiness and a long life together, but please, don't harm any more innocent people."

Steven was furious. "How ..."

Cecil stuck out his tongue at him and turned to take the lobster to the boat.

Steven frowned; it turned out that Diana's danger last night was indeed because of Queenie.

He thought, "Why does she have to kill Diana? Just because she wants to marry me?"

Steven jumped into the water and swam over.

Diana spat out a mouthful of seawater.

Suddenly, she saw a magnified handsome face.

"Oh, you scared me!" Diana, seeing Steven appear suddenly, quickly retreated.

Diana, seeing his arm wrapped in gauze, frowned and said, "Steven, what are you doing? Your arm is still injured. What were you thinking of jumping down here?"

He had no idea of how to take care of himself.

Steven smiled. "You still care about me?"

Diana laughed in exasperation, "Steven, I've gone to great lengths to save you so many times. You nearly died in a fire, and then almost got hit by a car. I was almost eaten by a pack of wolves too."

Steven raised his eyebrows and said, "I'll give you money."

Diana smiled brightly and charmingly. "Alright! This fish costs ten thousand dollars."

Steven smiled and said, "Deal."

Diana was speechless.

She thought Steven had a masochistic tendency.
