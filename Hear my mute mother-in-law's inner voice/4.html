Chapter 4

The hospital corridor reeked of harsh disinfectant, with noisy voices and hurried footsteps blending into chaos.

I pushed through the crowded hallway, heading straight for Patricia's room.

Outside her door, nurse Diana Jones paced back and forth with a stern expression.

She swept her sharp gaze over passing visitors, then suddenly locked onto me like she'd spotted prey.

"You can't go in. Just give me the photo album and I'll handle it," Diana said coldly, reaching out for the album.

I stepped aside and replied calmly, "Patricia specifically asked me to deliver this to her personally. I should take it in myself."

Diana's face immediately darkened as she raised her voice: "Patricia's about to go into surgery. She needs absolute quiet right now and can't be disturbed by anyone."

Other patients' family members nearby turned to look with curious eyes.

I smirked inwardly: "Just as I expected."

"Fine. You'd better look carefully and make sure you don't give it to the wrong person."

I handed the album directly to her.

Diana glanced down quickly, then gripped the album tightly, as if clutching some piece of incriminating evidence.

"Everyone come look! This woman abuses elderly people and even had photos printed to deliberately upset the patient!

We all have parents, and we'll all grow old someday. How could you be so cruel as to beat her up like this?"

The onlookers gradually gathered closer, their faces full of curiosity and shock.

Diana felt this wasn't enough, so she held the album high for everyone to see clearly.

"The woman abusing the elderly in these photos is this vicious person right here!

Look, this shows her true nature!"

She shouted while flipping open the album.

The crowd immediately erupted in gasps of surprise.

I felt secretly pleased but put on an innocent expression as I looked toward the album.

"What are you talking about? This is clearly the family photo album Patricia asked me to bring. It's full of old pictures of her and her husband."

Hearing my words, the anger on Diana's face instantly vanished, replaced by complete bewilderment.

She pulled the album back in disbelief, examining it page by page, unable to accept what she was seeing.

The album contained no evidence of me abusing Patricia at all—just sweet photos of her and her husband from their younger days, along with some warm childhood pictures of her and Thomas.

In the photos, Patricia smiled brilliantly, her eyes radiating happiness.

"How is this possible?" Diana stammered. Her hands began to tremble slightly, clearly caught off guard by what she was seeing.

"Weren't you just saying I abused her? Where's your evidence?" I asked with a cold laugh, my tone dripping with sarcasm.

Diana's face flushed red as she continued flipping through the album with shaking hands, seemingly still trying to find something to prove her earlier accusations.

But there was nothing in the album that could prove my guilt.

"What I saw wasn't this!"

Diana's voice grew quieter and quieter.

The onlookers began whispering among themselves, expressing doubt about Diana.

"You must be mistaken. This woman seems very kind," said an elderly lady.

"That's right. The album is full of heartwarming old photos. Which one shows evidence of elder abuse?" an old man nearby chimed in.

Fine beads of sweat appeared on Diana's forehead, her lips trembling slightly, unsure how to explain herself.
