Chapter 8

Diana collapsed on the ground, her eyes filled with madness and unwillingness, like a dying beast struggling in its final moments.

"You've all been deceived by her! She's the vicious woman who abuses the elderly, and all these photos are fake—fabricated to frame us!" she shrieked hysterically, her voice particularly grating and ridiculous amid everyone's accusations.

I let out a cold laugh, my eyes full of contempt.

"Stop making excuses. You're too stubborn."

I gathered the scattered evidence and walked up to her again, viciously throwing a photo in her face—it was an intimate shot of her and Thomas together at a hotel.

"What? This can't be..."

A flash of panic crossed Diana's eyes as she instinctively tried to cover the photo, only to find her hands wouldn't obey her.

"And there's this!" I threw another document at her feet.

"Don't think I don't know what you're after. Your son is seriously ill and needs a lot of money, so you set your sights on my insurance payout? You wanted to stage my accidental fall from the building, then live off my insurance money after I died? This is your comeuppance. Not only did you have an affair with a married man and destroy my family, but you also tried to frame me."

At this sight, the onlookers immediately burst into gasps of shock.

"She's gone too far! How could she do such a thing?"

"She's completely lost her conscience for her own selfish desires!"

Everyone's accusations surged toward Diana like a tide, leaving her nowhere to escape.

Looking at this evidence, Thomas swayed unsteadily, as if all his strength had been drained from him. His eyes filled with despair and regret as he staggered toward me.

"Isabella, I'm so sorry. I was wrong. I was confused and made this mistake. I don't love her—I was just seduced by her. For the sake of our years of marriage, please forgive me."

Tears streamed down his face, his voice filled with pleading.

Looking at this pitiful man, I felt deeply conflicted. The love I once had for him had long been worn away by all his betrayals and hurt, leaving only endless disappointment and pain.

"Thomas, how shameful! From the moment you cheated on me with her, we should have been over." I said coldly, my voice extremely detached.

Seeing that I remained unmoved, Thomas grew even more frantic. He stepped forward and grabbed my sleeve, crying and pleading: "Isabella, I swear to God I don't love her. I was only with her because she gave me a son. You know I've always wanted a child, but you haven't gotten pregnant after all these years. I... I was just confused for a moment."

Tears streamed down his face, no longer maintaining his usual composure.

Looking at him in this state, I felt nothing but disgust.
