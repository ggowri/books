Osborne's brow furrowed deeply, frustration etched across his face.

"The Pantheon League is utterly useless! I've already taken out five Intermediate Deities from the Deschanel Clan, and they still can't hold their own!

Do they really need me to eliminate two Superior Deities for them?"

As he contemplated the unfolding disaster, Osborne realized he couldn't sit by and watch the Pantheon League get wiped out.

If the Deschanel Clan triumphed, their first order of business would undoubtedly be to come after the Augustine family.

But the power of the Deschanel Clan was overwhelming.

Despite having only thirty-six deities, they boasted a staggering six Superior Deities, whose sheer might tipped the scales in their favor.

At any given moment, each of those Superior Deities was fending off multiple Intermediate Deities from the Pantheon League, along with several Junior Deities, and they were emerging victorious.

Bodies of defeated Pantheon League Junior Deities piled up around them.

If this continued, the nearly two hundred Junior and Intermediate Deities of the Pantheon League would be decimated without question.

"I can't let this one-sided slaughter carry on!" Osborne's expression grew grim.

He needed to take a risk.

Watching the performance of the Deschanel Clan's Superior Deities, he assessed his own abilities. Engaging them directly was a tall order; given his current strength, it would be difficult to gain an advantage, let alone kill one outright.

However, there might still be a chance through stealth.

Especially since each Superior Deity was occupied, battling several Intermediate and Junior Deities at once—a perfect opportunity for him to strike and take one out.

"However, getting a guaranteed kill is unlikely," Osborne mused, weighing his options.

Though he detested the idea of the League of Divine Descent meeting its end, he wouldn't mind seeing a few more of their warriors bite the dust.

He waited patiently for the right moment.

"The Deschanel Clan's Superior Deities have robust defenses. Right now, I can take down an Intermediate Deity in a flash with a sneak attack, but taking out a Superior Deity? That won't be easy!

After all, I'm still just a Junior Deity! My best bet is to severely wound one and then book it—no second chances, or I could be in a world of trouble.

A serious injury would be enough.

If I can land a solid hit, the Pantheon League needs to handle the rest. After all I've done for them, if they can't finish off a Superior Deity themselves, then they deserve to be eradicated."

Determined, Osborne picked his target.

He locked onto a particularly imposing Superior Deity from the Deschanel Clan, who was ruthlessly dispatching six of the Pantheon League's Intermediate Deities and fifteen Junior Deities around him, leaving a trail of corpses.

This deity's powerful attacks were fearsome, but Osborne noticed a weakness in their defenses—this one was the least protected among the six he had observed.

"Time to make my move."

As the Deschanel Clan Superior Deity unleashed a fierce attack that swiftly took down a Pantheon League Junior Deity, Osborne seized his chance. He appeared suddenly behind the deity, a divine artifact dagger gleaming in hand, and struck with all his might.

The sound of metal slicing through the air rang out.

Although this Superior Deity of the Deschanel Clan had the weakest defenses among all the Superior Deities present, when Osborne's attack struck his barrier, Osborne still felt a formidable impediment that prevented him from driving his full force through.

The Superior Deity was formidable, and while Osborne managed to penetrate their defenses, he could feel a massive drain of energy. The dagger pierced the Superior Deity's body deeply, but it fell just short of finding the heart.

"I can't kill him!"

But rather than feeling despair, Osborne seized the moment. With unwavering determination, he triggered the dark power coursing through the blade lodged in the Deschanel Clan Superior Deity's form.

The explosion erupted from within the Superior Deity, a cataclysmic force that tore through his Divine Body, creating a gaping wound that sent a wave of destruction through his vital organs.

"Ah!"

The cry of anguish echoed through the battlefield. Despite the debilitating attack, the Superior Deity's vitality surged, fierce and unyielding, but anger flashed in his eyes. He spun around, unleashing a desperate counterattack.

But it was in vain.

Osborne, having delivered his strike, bolted from the scene without a moment's hesitation, vanishing as quickly as he appeared.

"Get him!"

The warriors from the Pantheon League seized the opportunity. Previously suppressed, they lunged at the wounded Superior Deity like a pack of wolves on a wounded prey.

The scent of blood sparked their fury, and now that their adversary was reeling from a serious injury, they couldn't resist the urge to attack.

The luckless Superior Deity, weakened and staggering, struggled to fend them off, but the onslaught was relentless. His body began to suffer multiple wounds under the coordinated assault; each cut was a testament to his rapidly deteriorating condition.

"Help me!" he bellowed, desperation leaking into his voice.

Nearby, other deities from the Deschanel Clan exchanged alarmed glances, their expressions shifting from shock to horror as they surged toward their beleaguered comrade.

Among them was another Superior Deity, ready to intervene.

Yet, Osborne wasn't about to let that happen.
