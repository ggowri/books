Someone scoffed loudly, drawing attention from the crowd.

"Dark horse? Ha! What dark horse could possibly emerge? The strength of Vadim, Jamar, and Bethan clearly puts them in a league of their own! After all, they've all faced countless super talents in the state duel ten years ago. Even if a dark horse did show up, just making it into the top ten would be an achievement. Challenging those three? That's just wishful thinking! I honestly don't believe anyone can pull that off."

Many around him nodded in agreement.

"Vadim, Jamar, and Bethan are indeed the strongest disciples in Mystic Sirius Sect! Sure, everyone else is at Ascension Ten, but their power is on a whole different level. It's going to be tough for anyone to take them on," a second voice added.

"But outside of those three, the competition for the top ten is going to be fierce! Peak Four and Five have some impressive talents showing up, and even Peak Six and Eight have contenders who could make a run for it," someone else said, excitement creeping into his tone.

"Ha! That just leaves Peak Seven and Peak Nine with no hope whatsoever! I mean, come on, there's no way their disciples can even dream of making it into the top ten. They'll be lucky to crack the top twenty!"

"I heard the core disciples from Peak Seven are total cowards. They're too chicken-hearted to enter the tournament. They're terrified of getting humiliated! They couldn't even manage to fill all thirty tournament slots, so they had to scrounge up some inner disciples to pad out the roster."

"Inner disciples? Are they just here to embarrass themselves? Without an Ascension Ten cultivation level, competing in the Sect Tournament is just a joke," someone else said.

The crowd was buzzing with gossip.

Their words made the faces of the Peak Seven participants turn sour.

Just then, Osborne and Maddox finally reached the arena. Eyeing the bustling scene, Osborne gave a faint smile and sauntered over to where the Peak Seven participants were standing.

He whipped out his Kingdom Duel Token. In an instant, all the Peak Seven participants swiveled their heads towards Osborne, their eyes brimming with bewilderment.

Not a single one of them had a clue who Osborne was.

"Are you an inner disciple?" one of them asked, eyebrow raised.

Osborne nodded. "Yeah."

Without saying a word more, he made a beeline for a quiet nook and plopped down.

Then he shut his eyes and went into a meditative state. His nonchalant act only rubbed the nearby disciple the wrong way.

"Humph! An inner disciple thinks he can just brush us core disciples off? What a lack of respect! Being so full of himself is just asking for a beating!"

The disciple snorted and stomped over to Osborne, itching for a confrontation.

Another disciple jumped in, blocking the angry one's path.

"Whoa, hold your horses! He's a face I don't recognize. He must be new around here. Right now, the whole place is making fun of us from Peak Seven. If we start fighting among ourselves, they'll have a field day.

"And don't forget, Ms. Olsen and the Elders are watching! If you start a ruckus, you'll just make a fool of yourself, and Ms. Olsen won't cut you any slack!"

The angry disciple's face changed as he took in these words.

He shot Osborne a dirty look. "You got lucky today. I'm letting you off the hook for now, but you better come crawling back and beg for my forgiveness later! Otherwise, you'll be sorry!"

His words echoed, but Osborne didn't seem to bat an eye like he couldn't even hear them.

In fact, he had heard every word but chose to ignore the provocation.

Looking for a fight, are you?

If that guy still has the balls to come after me after the Sect Tournament, I might just respect him for his audacity.

Osborne's thoughts drifted back to the conversations around him.

Vadim from Peak One?

Jamar from Peak Three?

Bethan from Peak Two?

Those three are the cream of the crop among all the core disciples in Mystic Sirius Sect! They took part in the city duel in Tempest Fortress City ten years ago and made it into the top ten! It's a pity they couldn't make a name for themselves in the state duel in Zephyr State.

Osborne finally cracked open his eyes.

He followed the crowd's line of sight and caught a glimpse of those three big-shot figures.

Not too shabby! They're seriously strong, and their presence is off the charts!

With that, he shut his eyes again and settled in to wait for the official start of the Sect Tournament.

Before long, whispers started to spread through the crowd.

"Greetings, Mr. Wilkens!"

"Greetings, Mr. Wilkens!"

The head of Mystic Sirius Sect, Lenny, made his grand entrance. He was a middle-aged man. He tossed out a few words of encouragement and then declared the tournament officially open.

Osborne watched as Lenny sauntered closer to Angela and started chatting her up like there was no tomorrow.

It was a real contrast. While Lenny was all grins, Angela remained as cold as ice, only responding after a long, drawn-out pause.

Osborne raised an eyebrow.

Is the sect master of Mystic Sirius Sect trying to sweet-talk Angela?

But he quickly shrugged off that thought as the tournament took the spotlight.

The first thing on the agenda was the draw.

All 280 participants would be randomly divided into twenty groups of fourteen.

There, they'd dive into a wild free-for-all. No teaming up allowed—just straight one-on-one showdowns.

The last person left standing in each group would seize the top spot, and the twenty group winners would move on to the final rounds.

Next up were the one-on-one matches, where each contestant would go head-to-head in nineteen battles.

The ten with the best overall records would get the golden ticket to represent Mystic Sirius Sect in the upcoming city duel at Tempest Fortress City.

The rules were simple yet ruthless, and Osborne was all for it.

The draw wrapped up in a flash, and Osborne peeked at the wooden token in his hand.

Number five!

He'd landed in the fifth group.

Osborne couldn't be bothered to check who else was in his group; it was all the same to him. No matter who his opponents were, he was dead set on being the last one standing.

However, his couldn't-care-less attitude didn't stop others from taking notice.

The other participants from Peak Seven were scoping out the place, nervously trying to figure out who they'd be grouped with.

That was when the earlier critic, the one who'd called Osborne rude, caught sight of his token.

A mischievous glint sparked in his eyes.

"Ha! What a coincidence! You're in Group Five too! Looks like your luck has just run out by being in the same group as me! I was gonna teach you a lesson after the tournament, but now I don't have to wait!

"I'll be the first one to square off against you in the arena! Even if I don't make it out of the group stage, at least I can brag about beating you. That ain't a total wash, right?"

He shot Osborne a menacing smirk.

Osborne turned to face him, looking calm on the surface, but a hint of contempt flashed in his eyes.

"I'd advise you not to pick a fight with me. If you stay out of my way, you might last a bit longer in this tournament. But if you don't, you're gonna end up kicking yourself!"

With that, Osborne took a look at the other guy's Kingdom Duel Token and finally caught his name.

Areli Seyfried!

Areli's face contorted with fury at Osborne's words. "You're way too full of yourself for an inner disciple! You think you can talk to me like that? Well, fine! I was just gonna give you a taste of my power, but now I'll make sure you're laid up in bed for days after this!"

His eyes were filled with a menacing glint.

Osborne just let out a chuckle. "We'll just have to wait and see."

With that, he turned his back on Areli and headed straight for Arena Five.

There were ten arenas in all, all set to kick off the group battles at the same time.

Osborne was raring to go; his fight was about to start!
