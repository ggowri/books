Clap, clap, clap!

"You're remarkable! I've heard that in the East Zone, the Mystic Feather Clan is the only race that has never targeted or looked down on the Human Community. God of Light didn't dwell on the matter.

She turned to Osborne and said, "Even if you manage to win the Ascension One Supernal Deity King tournament, it's only three matches. Winning three would already be a huge morale boost for the Human Alliance, but it's still far from the five or six victories you promised.

"It's true, we're all only Ascension One Supernal Deity Kings. But as far as I know, the rules allow us to compete in the Ascension Two tournament, right?"

"You're saying... you want to compete in the Ascension Two tournament?" she asked, shocked.

After speaking, she furrowed her brow.

"To be honest, I don't think this is a good idea. Even though I know you all have the ability to challenge levels above your own and your strength is comparable to Ascension Two Supernal Deity Kings, you might even have a good chance of winning.

Osborne shook his head calmly. "I think you misunderstood, God of Light. We're not just planning to enter the Ascension Two tournament. We're entering the Ascension Three and Ascension Four tournaments as well!

"And we're more than 90% confident we can win!"

"That's impossible!"

God of Light shot to her feet, frowning. "Osborne, as much as I hate to say this, I think you're being overly confident! Even if you are a prodigy—able to challenge Ascension Two Kings while still at Ascension One—that kind of talent is already once-in-a-century!

"If you could manage to fight against Ascension Three Kings, you'd be on par with the Seven Great Deity Kings of the Human Community. I have no doubt your future achievements would surpass mine!

"But Ascension Four...

God of Light's face was deeply serious.

This time, she wasn't accusing Osborne of arrogance or overconfidence—she was genuinely trying to dissuade him.

Osborne smiled faintly, "You're right, God of Light. Normally, an Ascension One Supernal Deity King would never stand a chance against an Ascension Four King—the gap is just too wide. Even I don't have absolute confidence...

"You're serious?"

Osborne nodded. "No amount of explanation can compare to a single real fight, don't you think?"

God of Light's expression shifted unpredictably.

Finally, she gritted her teeth and said, "Fine! Since you're so insistent, I'll grant your request. This will let you experience the power of Ascension Four Kings firsthand—and hopefully put an end to this reckless idea of yours!"

"Understood!"

After speaking, they turned their attention to Osborne and his group.

Osborne sized them up as well. They were all Radiant Guardians, each one an Ascension Four Supernal Deity King, radiating an overwhelming presence.

"Osborne, these eight participated in the Ascension Four tournament two days ago... and they all lost. They didn't even come close to winning.

"If you can't even handle them..."

God of Light didn't finish her sentence, but the meaning was clear.

If Osborne couldn't even beat these eight, then he had no business competing in the Ascension Four tournament—it simply wasn't realistic.

But Osborne simply smiled and nodded. "Don't worry. If we can't handle them, I'll accept it and give up on the Ascension Four tournament.
